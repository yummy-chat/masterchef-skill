# Shrimp and Celery Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Fresh Shrimp, to taste (mashed into a paste with the back of a knife)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (finely minced)
🥕 Carrot, to taste (finely chopped and blanched)
🌿 Celery, to taste (finely chopped and blanched)
🌽 Cornstarch, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌶️ White Pepper, to taste
🍬 Granulated Sugar, to taste
💧 Ginger-Scallion Water, a little
🍶 Steamed Fish Soy Sauce, 2 tablespoons
💧 Water, 2 tablespoons
🧄 Minced Garlic, to taste
🌶️ Small Red Chili Pepper, to taste (finely chopped)
🧅 Scallion, to taste (finely chopped)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife (for mashing shrimp with the back of the knife)
🪵 Cutting Board
🥣 Glass Bowl (for mixing shrimp paste)
🍳 Non-Stick Frying Pan (for pan-frying the pancakes)
🥄 Spoon (for scooping shrimp balls and pressing them into shape)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring the sauce)
🍶 Small Bowl (for mixing the sauce)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the fresh shrimp on a cutting board and repeatedly pound them with the back of a knife into a fine paste.
   **Key Point**: Using the back of the knife, not the blade, preserves the shrimp's fiber and elasticity.
2. Add minced ginger, salt, white pepper, granulated sugar, and a little ginger-scallion water to the shrimp paste. Stir and slap the mixture continuously in one direction with your hand until it becomes noticeably sticky.
   **Note**: The stirring direction must remain consistent to avoid breaking down the gel formation.
3. Add the blanched chopped carrot, chopped celery, and cornstarch. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Blanching the vegetable pieces beforehand removes excess moisture and raw flavor, preventing the pancakes from releasing water.
4. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped small red chili pepper, chopped scallion, 2 tablespoons of steamed fish soy sauce, and 2 tablespoons of water. Stir until well mixed and set aside.
5. Heat a small amount of oil in a non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Scoop the shrimp mixture into balls and place them in the pan. Gently press each ball into a pancake shape using the back of a spoon that has been dipped in water.
   **Key Point**: Dipping the back of the spoon in water prevents sticking. Fry over medium heat, cooking each side for about 40 seconds until both sides are golden brown and set.
6. Evenly pour the prepared sauce over the shrimp pancakes in the pan. Quickly cover with a lid.
   **Key Point**: Cover immediately after pouring the sauce. Use the steam to "braise" the pancakes, allowing them to absorb the flavor quickly and the sauce to thicken.
   **Time**: About 30–60 seconds, adjust according to the amount of sauce and heat level.
7. Turn off the heat. Transfer the shrimp pancakes to a serving plate and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat. Pairing it with celery and carrots supplements dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for those on a weight loss diet or managing calorie intake for fitness. It is recommended to use minimal oil during frying to avoid excess calories.
