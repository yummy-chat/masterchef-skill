# Mo's Chicken Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 1 free-range chicken (cut into small pieces, thoroughly cleaned of internal organs)  
🌿 25g fingerleaf gourd root  
🍂 5g smilax glabra  
🌰 10g poria cocos  
🍊 5g dried tangerine peel  
🫘 25g soybeans  
🌴 5 red dates (pitted and halved)  
🫚 1 large piece of ginger

🧄 65g kencur  
🧄 2 cloves of garlic  
🧂适量 salt  
🥜适量 peanut oil (heated)  
🥢适量 seafood soy sauce  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Stainless steel soup pot  
🔪 Chopping board, chef's knife  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Dipping sauce bowl  
🔥 Portable stove, gas stove  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the herbs**: Rinse smilax glabra, poria cocos, fingerleaf gourd root, dried tangerine peel, soybeans, and pitted red dates. Soak in clean water for 1–2 hours.  
**Note**: Red dates must be pitted and halved. The quality of the herbs directly affects the flavor of the broth.

2. **Prepare the chicken**: Cut the free-range chicken into bite-sized pieces, thoroughly clean any internal organs, and rinse well.
3. **Make the kencur dipping sauce**: Finely chop the kencur, mix with minced garlic and a pinch of salt. Heat peanut oil until smoking, then pour it over the kencur and garlic mixture to release the aroma. Add seafood soy sauce and chopped scallions, and stir well. If you like heat, add chopped bird's eye chilies.
4. **Simmer the herbal broth**: Place the soaked herbs and ginger in the soup pot, add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 40 minutes until the herbal fragrance is fully released.
5. **Poach the chicken**: Keep the broth at a rolling boil, add the chicken pieces, and cook over high heat until the chicken is just cooked through. Remove immediately to avoid overcooking and toughening the meat.
6. **Serve and continue cooking**: First, enjoy a bowl of the clear broth, then dip the chicken pieces generously in the kencur sauce. Feel free to add chicken gizzards, liver, or roe sacs to the pot to cook—cook the roe sacs just until they burst. The remaining broth can be simmered further and will become richer in flavor over time.

## Health Advice  
This chicken pot features nourishing herbs and tender chicken, making it especially suitable for seasons with high humidity. Fingerleaf gourd root and smilax glabra help dispel dampness and strengthen the spleen. Removing the chicken as soon as it is cooked ensures a tender texture and prevents overcooking.
