# Pirate-Style Braised Beef Ribs

Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Instructions  
1. Cut the beef short ribs into large pieces roughly the size of the serving plate's diameter.  
2. Place the meat pieces in cold water and blanch. After the water boils, skim off the foam, remove the meat, and rinse with warm water.  
3. Bring fresh water to a boil in the wok, add the blanched meat, ginger slices, and black peppercorns. Boil uncovered, then skim off any remaining foam.  
4. Cover the pressure cooker, bring to pressure, and cook for 20 minutes. Allow the pressure to release naturally.  
**Key point**: Do not add salt, to prevent the meat from becoming tough. The meat should be easily pierced with chopsticks but not fall apart.  
5. Remove the meat pieces to a tray, arrange them neatly, press flat with a heavy weight, and refrigerate or freeze until firm and set.  
6. Remove the set meat and slice into even, thick pieces.  
7. Heat oil in a wok, add scallion sections, julienned ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns. Sauté until fragrant, then turn off the heat and add black peppercorns.  
8. Turn the heat back to low, add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce, and stir-fry until aromatic.  
9. Pour in enough of the reserved cooking liquid from the meat, bring to a boil, then season with salt.  
10. Cook for 3–5 minutes, then use a slotted spoon to remove all solid seasonings.  
11. Turn off the heat, arrange the meat slices flat in the liquid, then turn the heat to medium and simmer slowly until the meat is heated through and tender. Gently shake the pan to prevent sticking.  
12. When the liquid has reduced by half, add the first portion of water starch. After the liquid reduces further, add the second portion of water starch to create a glossy sauce.  
13. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in the garlic sprout or garlic stem sections and gently mix. Do not stir repeatedly to avoid breaking the meat.  

## Health Recommendations  
This beef short rib dish is rich in fat; it is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables to balance the richness. Those with high blood pressure or high cholesterol may eat in moderation but should control portion sizes.
