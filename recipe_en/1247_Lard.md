# Lard
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork fatback 2 pounds (without skin)
💧 Water 200 milliliters

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon, strainer
🥣 Heat-resistant container (such as a baking pan or ceramic bowl)

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork fatback, first cut it into strips, then dice into small cubes.
   **Key Point**: Dicing into small cubes accelerates the rendering of fat; the skin must be removed, otherwise it may cause oil splattering during rendering.
2. Place the diced fatback into the wok, add 200 milliliters of water, and bring to a boil over high heat until the meat cubes turn white.
   **Key Point**: Adding water prevents the fatback from directly contacting the hot pan and burning, which would affect the final color and flavor of the lard.
3. After the water boils, reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly, stirring occasionally to ensure even heating of the meat cubes.
   **Status Check**: After about 5 minutes, the water will begin to decrease; after about 10 minutes, the water will mostly evaporate, and oil will start to render; after about 15 to 20 minutes, the lard will become a clear liquid, with the amount of oil gradually increasing and the fatback cubes shrinking.
4. Continue simmering over low heat for about 30 minutes until the fatback particles become shriveled and yellow, then immediately remove them as cracklings.
   **Key Point**: The cracklings must be promptly removed once they are shriveled and yellow to avoid overcooking, which would create a burnt flavor and affect the lard's taste.
5. Remove the wok from the heat and strain the rendered lard through a strainer into a heat-resistant container.
   **Note**: Be sure to use a heat-resistant container such as a baking pan or ceramic bowl; do not use ordinary glass containers to prevent the hot oil from causing the container to shatter.
6. Allow the strained lard to cool at room temperature, or refrigerate until fully solidified, resulting in a snowy white appearance.

## Health Advice
Lard is rich in saturated fats. Consuming it in moderation can quickly replenish energy, but individuals with high blood lipids should exercise caution. It is recommended to pair lard with vegetables and whole grains for a balanced diet, avoiding long-term excessive intake.
