# Hot and Sour Fern Root Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🥬 Ingredients
- Fern root noodles
- Small chili peppers (optional)
- Garlic (optional)
- Scallions (optional)

🧂 Seasonings
- Chili oil
- Soy sauce
- Black vinegar
- Salt
- Sugar

🍳 Utensils
- A deep pot

📝 Steps
1. Fill the pot with water to about three-fifths of its depth and bring to a boil over high heat.
2. Once the water is boiling, add the fern root noodles, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook for about 8 minutes. Drain and set aside.
3. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, black vinegar, and chili oil in a ratio of 3:2:2. Taste with a chopstick. If too salty, add 2-5 grams of salt; if lacking umami, add 2 grams of sugar and stir until fully dissolved.
4. Prepare another serving bowl and pour in the prepared sauce.
5. Rinse the cooked fern root noodles under cold water, then add them to the sauce bowl and mix thoroughly.
6. Finely chop the scallions, garlic, and small chili peppers separately, and sprinkle them over the mixed noodles. Serve immediately.

Note: This dish is spicy. If you cannot handle heat, reduce the amount of chili oil and omit the small chili peppers. Adjust sugar and salt to personal taste. It is recommended to serve in a bowl for easier eating.

## Health Tips
Fern root noodles are rich in dietary fiber and minerals, making them suitable for those who need to control calorie intake or promote intestinal motility. However, the high proportion of chili oil and vinegar in the seasoning means that those with excessive stomach acid should consume in moderation.
