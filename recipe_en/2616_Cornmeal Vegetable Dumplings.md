# Cornmeal Vegetable Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Spinach, appropriate amount (blanched, squeezed dry, and chopped)  
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (grated into shreds)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount (diced)  
🍄🟫 Wood ear mushrooms, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🥚 Eggs, appropriate amount (pre-cooked and chopped)  
🍤 Dried shrimp, appropriate amount  
🌽 Cornmeal, appropriate amount (for the outer coating, applied in two layers)  
🌽 Cornstarch, 1 tablespoon (added to the filling for binding)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (basic seasoning)  
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount (to enhance flavor)  
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount (for aroma)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl (for mixing the filling)  
🥣 Large plate or bowl (to hold cornmeal for coating)  
♨️ Steamer  
🧻 Steamer cloth or steaming mat (to line the steamer basket and prevent sticking)  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the vegetables**: Blanch the spinach, squeeze it dry, and chop it. Grate the carrot into fine shreds. Dice the shiitake mushrooms and chop the wood ear mushrooms into small pieces. Place everything into a large bowl.  
   **Key point**: The vegetables must be finely processed to make shaping easier later.  
2. **Add protein ingredients**: Add the pre-cooked and chopped eggs, along with the dried shrimp.  
3. **Season and mix**: Add salt, MSG, sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon of cornstarch. Use your hands to thoroughly mix and combine all ingredients and seasonings.  
   **Key point**: Cornstarch enhances the stickiness of the filling, preventing it from falling apart during steaming.  
4. **Form the dumplings**: Take an appropriate amount of filling and squeeze it tightly in your hands to form firm, uniform-sized balls.  
   **Key point**: The filling must be tightly compressed, or it may fall apart during coating and steaming.  
5. **First coating**: Place the vegetable balls into a plate filled with cornmeal. Gently roll them around to coat the surface evenly with a layer of cornmeal.  
6. **Second coating**: Take a coated ball in your hands, **squeeze it tightly**, then roll it in the cornmeal again to apply a second layer.  
   **Key point**: Squeezing helps form a complete, secure outer coating; repeating the coating process ensures a more even crust.  
7. **Steam**: Line the steamer basket with a steamer cloth and arrange the dumplings in it. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the basket in, and steam over high heat for 10 minutes, until the outer skin becomes translucent and golden yellow.  
8. **Serve and enjoy**: Serve immediately, or dip in chili oil or other sauces for enhanced flavor.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is nutritionally balanced, with a good combination of vegetables, mushrooms, and protein. The cornmeal provides dietary fiber, making it suitable for light meals or during weight loss. Be sure to compress the filling tightly, or it may fall apart after steaming.
