# Spicy Braised Duck Parts
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Duck necks, as needed  
🦆 Duck gizzards, as needed  
🦆 Duck feet, as needed  
🦆 Duck wings, as needed  
🦆 Duck heads, as needed  
🦆 Duck collarbones, as needed  
🦆 Duck intestines, as needed  
🦆 Duck tongues, as needed  
🍄 Black fungus, as needed  
🫘 Dried tofu, as needed  
🫘 Bean curd sticks, as needed  
🥒 Lotus root slices, as needed  
🍢 Various fish balls and fish tofu, as needed  
🌶️ Braising spice pack (contains dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, etc.; adjust spiciness to taste)  
🍯 Soy sauce, as needed  
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫕 Large stainless steel soup pot  
🥘 Glass pot (or another pot)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the spicy braising broth**: Place dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and other spices into a pot. Add water, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes to fully release the spicy flavor.  
   **Key point**: Simmering on low heat allows the spices to fully infuse; avoid high heat to prevent rapid evaporation.  

2. **Add duck parts in batches based on cooking time**: Maintain medium-low heat throughout to keep the broth gently simmering.  
   - First, add duck necks and duck gizzards, braise for about 25 minutes.  
   - Then, add duck feet, duck wings, duck heads, and collarbones, continue braising for about 15 minutes.  
   - Next, add duck intestines, braise for about 5 minutes.  
   - Finally, add duck tongues, braise for about 1 minute, then turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Gently turn the ingredients during braising; avoid vigorous stirring to prevent breaking them. Duck heads tend to float, so use a slotted spoon to hold them under the liquid.  

3. **Soak after braising**: After turning off the heat, let all duck parts soak in the broth for 1 hour to fully absorb the flavors. Do not remove immediately.  

4. **Remove duck parts**: In order from least to most fragile, first remove the duck intestines from the top, then gently scoop out the remaining duck parts along the edge of the pot using a slotted spoon. Drain and let cool naturally.  

5. **Braise meat and vegetables separately**: Transfer an appropriate amount of broth from the main pot to another pot. Add black fungus, dried tofu, bean curd sticks, lotus root slices, and other vegetables. Cook until done, then remove and let cool. After removing the vegetables, add fish balls and fish tofu. Cook until done, then remove and let cool.  
   **Note**: The broth used for braising vegetables and fish balls should be discarded; do not return it to the main braising broth.  

6. **Strain and save the master stock**: Remove the spice residues from the main braising pot. Strain the remaining broth through a fine mesh strainer into a clean container. Let cool, then refrigerate to store as "master stock." When using it next time, reduce the amount of seasonings accordingly.  

## Health Advice  
This spicy braised duck dish is rich in protein and collagen, making it suitable for meat lovers who can tolerate spiciness. Note that it has a high sodium content; pair it with light vegetables to balance salt intake. People with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
