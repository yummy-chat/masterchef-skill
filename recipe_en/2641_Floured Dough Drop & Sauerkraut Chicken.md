# Floured Dough Drop & Sauerkraut Chicken  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Free-range chicken, half (cut into pieces)  
🥬 Sauerkraut, to taste (cut into pieces)  
🌶️ Pickled ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers, to taste (cut into short sections)  
🌶️ Wild pepper, to taste (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (crushed)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small pinch  
🌾 Flour, about 1 bowl  
🌾 Cornstarch, about half a small bowl  
🥚 Egg, 1 piece  
💧 Water, to taste (for dough and simmering)  
🐷 Lard, half a tablespoon  
🌻 Vegetable oil, one tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (to be added to the batter)  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🧂 White pepper, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chopping board, chef's knife  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel large bowl (for mixing dough, holding ingredients)  
🥢 Wok spatula (for mixing batter, dropping dough pieces)  
🍽️ Flat plate (for holding batter, convenient for dropping)  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the free-range chicken into pieces. Do not blanch; stir-fry directly in the wok to preserve the original flavor.  
2. Cut the sauerkraut into pieces, slice the pickled ginger, cut the pickled chili peppers into short sections, chop the wild pepper, and crush the garlic. Mix all the prepared ingredients together and set aside in a bowl.  
3. In a large bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, and a pinch of salt. Crack in the egg. Gradually add water while stirring in one direction until you have a relatively thin batter that can form a thin stream when lifted. Pour the batter into a flat plate and set aside.  
4. Heat the wok, add lard and vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken pieces. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until all moisture has evaporated and the oil in the wok turns from cloudy to clear.  
**Key Point**: Thoroughly drying the chicken is crucial for removing gaminess and enhancing flavor. Fry patiently until the oil becomes clear.  

5. Add the mixed sauerkraut, pickled ginger, pickled chili peppers, wild pepper, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns. Continue stir-frying, ensuring the sauerkraut is cooked thoroughly until it releases a lactic acid aroma and begins to bubble.  
**Key Point**: The sauerkraut must be stir-fried thoroughly. Only when its lactic acid scent is fully released will the broth become rich and flavorful.  

6. Add enough water to the wok. After it comes to a boil, season with MSG, chicken bouillon, and white pepper. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer slowly to fully infuse the chicken with flavor.  
7. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Using a wok spatula or chopsticks, scrape the batter from the flat plate into the boiling water along the edge of the pot. Once the dough pieces float to the surface and turn slightly yellow, they are set and cooked. Remove them with a slotted spoon and drain.  
**Key Point**: Scrape the batter into the water quickly to prevent sticking. Once they float, cook them only briefly before removing; overcooking will make them mushy.  

8. Add the cooked dough pieces to the pot with the sauerkraut chicken. Simmer over low heat for a short while to let them absorb the broth. Before serving, you can add some pre-stir-fried green and red chili pepper segments for color and aroma. Mix well, then plate the dish.  
**Note**: Do not stir the dough pieces immediately after adding them to the pot. Wait until they float and set before gently pushing them to prevent them from breaking apart. Maintain low heat throughout to keep the broth rich and allow the dough pieces to absorb flavor without becoming too soft.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and contains probiotics from the fermented sauerkraut, which can help stimulate appetite and aid digestion. However, pickled peppers and lard are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair this dish with light vegetables for balance. The dough pieces should be cooked until they float and then combined into the broth, avoiding prolonged cooking to prevent them from becoming mushy and to retain their chewy texture. Be mindful of portion sizes. Suitable for those needing to supplement protein and promote digestion.
