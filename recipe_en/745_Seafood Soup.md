# Seafood Soup
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Onion, about 1 (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic, 5-6 cloves (finely chopped)
🍅 Tomatoes, about 2 (finely chopped)
🦐 Shrimp, generous amount (remove heads, keep whole shrimp bodies)
🦪 Mussels, 10-15 (frozen mussels can be used)
🐚 Clams, 15-20 (fresh clams)
🍷 Dry white wine, about 300 ml (can substitute with cooking wine or water)
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil, as needed (more oil for stir-frying shrimp heads, add a little more later)
🧂 Salt, to taste (final seasoning)
🌶️ Black pepper, to taste (final seasoning)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍲 Deep pot
🔪 Knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions
1. Finely chop the onion, garlic, and tomatoes separately.
   *Note: Since a long simmer is required, the chopping does not need to be precise.*

2. Pour a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil into a deep pot. Add the shrimp heads and stir-fry over medium heat until they change color and release a red shrimp oil.
   - Once done, remove the shrimp heads, keeping the shrimp oil in the pot.

3. Using the shrimp oil in the pot, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté over medium-low heat until the onion turns translucent.

4. Add the reserved whole shrimp and quickly stir-fry until they change color.

5. Add the chopped tomatoes.

6. Add the mussels, then pour in about 300 ml of dry white wine. The liquid should partially cover the ingredients.

7. Cover the pot and simmer over medium heat for about 7-8 minutes.
   - If you have fresh rosemary or thyme, you can add it at this stage.

8. Add enough water to slightly cover the ingredients in the pot, then add the fresh clams.

9. Cover the pot and turn the heat to high. Cook for about 3 minutes, until the clams are fully open.

10. Finally, season with salt and black pepper. Adjust the saltiness according to personal taste.

## Health Tips
This seafood soup is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, making it suitable for those who need nutritional supplementation or a low-fat diet. However, individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt used.
