# Tender Flatbread (Jin Bing)  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 300g all-purpose flour (for dough)  
🧂 3g salt  
💧 60g boiling water  
💧 135g cold water  
🌾 20g all-purpose flour (for oil paste)  
🫒 40g cooking oil (for oil paste, recommend neutral oils like cooked oil, corn oil, or sunflower oil)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🪵 Wooden lid  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🍽️ Plate  
🧴 Oil spray bottle  
🪚 Cutting board  
🖌️ Small brush (or use hands as alternative)  
🪯 Rolling pin  
🍳 Electric griddle or non-stick pan  
🧺 Woven basket  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place all-purpose flour and salt in a bowl, mix evenly.  
2. Gradually pour in freshly boiled water while stirring with chopsticks to scald part of the flour.  
3. Gradually add cold water, stirring into flocculent pieces.  
4. Wearing disposable gloves, knead the flour pieces into a dough until no dry flour remains in the bowl; a rough surface is fine.  
5. Cover with a lid and rest for 15 minutes. During this time, knead the dough every 5 minutes (about 10 times each), repeating 3 times until the dough becomes smooth.  
6. After the final kneading, cover and let rest for another 5 minutes.  
7. Remove the dough, roll it into a long strip, and evenly divide into 5 portions.  
8. Lightly spray oil on the bottom of a plate. Round each dough piece and place in the plate, then spray oil evenly on the surface of each piece, ensuring the edges are coated.  
9. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid, and rest for at least 1 hour.  
   **Key point**: Do not shorten the resting time, otherwise the dough will shrink and be difficult to roll out. You can prepare this the night before and refrigerate, using it the next morning.  
10. Make the oil paste: In a small bowl, mix 20g all-purpose flour with 40g cooking oil, stirring into a smooth thin paste.  
11. Lightly spray oil on the cutting board and spread evenly. Take one rested dough piece with the oiled side facing up, shape it into a square by hand, and press flat.  
12. Use the heel of your palm to push the dough outward into a thin rectangle about the size of two palms.  
13. Spread a layer of oil paste evenly over the surface, leaving no edges.  
14. Fold like a blanket: fold the left side over the right 1/3, gently press to release air; then fold the right side over, and press again. Spread oil paste over the folded surface.  
15. Fold the top down 1/3, then bring the bottom up, forming a small square. Cover all prepared dough pieces with plastic wrap and rest for 15 minutes.  
16. Fully preheat the electric griddle or non-stick pan before rolling out the dough.  
17. Spread a little remaining oil paste on the cutting board, take one rested dough piece, and press flat with your hand.  
18. Use a rolling pin to roll diagonally in one direction, then switch to the opposite diagonal, rolling into a thin flatbread about the size of four palms (do not over-roll).  
19. Lightly spray a thin layer of oil on the preheated pan. Use the rolling pin to lift the rolled dough and place it into the pan, then lightly spray oil on the surface to prevent drying.  
20. Cook over medium heat. The dough will begin to change color and bubble within seconds after placing it in the pan. After about 20+ seconds, lift a corner to check the bottom; flip immediately when golden brown spots appear.  
21. After flipping, cook for a few more seconds until the bread puffs up fully and is cooked through. Total cooking time should not exceed 1 minute.  
22. Place the cooked tender flatbread into a woven basket. Serve immediately while warm for the best texture, ideal for wrapping dishes like shredded potatoes or spicy shredded pork.  

## Health Advice  
This tender flatbread achieves its soft and chewy texture through scalded dough and extended resting. Calories mainly come from flour and oil paste, making it a good match with vegetables and lean meat for balanced nutrition. It is recommended to consume in moderation to avoid excessive oil intake.
