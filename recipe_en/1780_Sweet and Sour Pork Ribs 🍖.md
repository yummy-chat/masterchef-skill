# Sweet and Sour Pork Ribs 🍖
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Style
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Dish

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs (center-cut or front ribs) 500g
🧅 Scallion, white part, about a palm's length
🫚 Ginger, young ginger about a palm's length (5-6 slices) or old ginger half a palm's length (3-4 slices)
🍑 Preserved plums 3-4 pieces (optional)
🍶 Cooking wine 1 tbsp (for blanching) + 1 tbsp (for sauce)
🧴 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp
🍬 White sugar or rock sugar 3 tbsp
🍋 Vinegar 3 tbsp (for sauce) + 1 tbsp (before serving)
🧴 Dark soy sauce about 1/2 tbsp (optional, for color)
🧂 Salt to taste
🤍 White sesame seeds to taste

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Soup pot
🍳 Wok or frying pan
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🧻 Kitchen paper (optional)

## Instructions
### Preparation
**Ingredient Prep**
- Have the butcher cut the ribs into pieces. Soak in cold water for 20–30 minutes to remove blood.
- Cut the scallion white into sections; score once lengthwise to release flavor. Slice the ginger.
- If using preserved plums, soak them in a suitable amount of water to make preserved plum water; set aside.
**Make the Sauce**
- Mix 1 tbsp cooking wine, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 3 tbsp sugar, and 3 tbsp vinegar in a bowl, stirring until combined. Sauce ratio mnemonic: cooking wine 1, soy sauce 2, sugar and vinegar 3.
### Cooking
1. **Blanching**
   - Place the ribs in a pot with cold water. Add scallion, ginger, and cooking wine to remove odor. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Skim off any foam, then remove the ribs. Rinse with hot water or pat dry with kitchen paper to prevent oil splatter during frying.
   **Key point**: Blanching starting with cold water fully removes blood and reduces any gamey taste.

2. **Frying the Ribs**
   - Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the drained ribs and stir-fry until the surface is slightly charred and golden, creating a caramelized layer. Add ginger slices during frying for extra aroma.
   **Key point**: The ribs must develop a caramelized surface for deeper flavor and to avoid greasiness during simmering.

3. **Adding Sauce and Braising**
   - Pour the prepared sauce into the wok. Add enough boiling water to submerge the ribs. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover and simmer for about 45 minutes, until the ribs are tender and the meat easily separates from the bone.
   **Key point**: Slow simmering over low heat makes the meat fork-tender. Preserved plums can add flavor complexity.

4. **Reducing the Sauce and Seasoning**
   - Increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Stir continuously for about 3–5 minutes, until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ribs. Add dark soy sauce if needed for deeper color. Stir constantly to prevent sticking. Just before serving, drizzle in 1 tbsp of vinegar to enhance the tangy aroma. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt if necessary.
   **Key point**: Adding vinegar at the end boosts the sourness; do not add it too early.

5. **Plating and Garnish**
   - Transfer to a serving plate and sprinkle with white sesame seeds.

## Health Tips
Sweet and sour pork ribs are high in sugar and oil, making them suitable for occasional enjoyment. Those on a diet or with high blood sugar should eat in moderation. Adding preserved plums during braising can enhance flavor but requires reducing the sugar accordingly.
