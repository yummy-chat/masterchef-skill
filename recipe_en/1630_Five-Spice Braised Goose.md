# Five-Spice Braised Goose
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦢 Whole Magang goose (freshly slaughtered and cleaned)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (cut into chunks)
🧅 Red shallots, to taste (cut into chunks)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (whole cloves)
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (for final garnish)

## Seasonings
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (for rubbing on the goose and seasoning during braising)
🍶 Rice wine, to taste (sprinkled along the wok edge to remove odor and enhance aroma)
🍂 Five-spice powder, to taste
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, to taste
⭐ Star anise, to taste
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste
🥮 Fermented red bean curd (nam yu), to taste
🫘 Chu hou paste, to taste
🍬 Rock sugar, to taste
🍾 Eight-treasure sweet vinegar (not dark soy sauce, rich in aroma and oil)
💧 Water, to taste (for braising, enough to reach halfway up the goose)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪓 Thick round wooden chopping board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Chinese wok
🎋 Bamboo mat (placed at the bottom of the wok to prevent sticking)
🥢 Long chopsticks
🥄 Large soup spoon
🥣 Strainer
🍽 Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process the Goose**: Clean the whole goose thoroughly. Cut at the joints of the goose legs to remove the feet. Locate the gland at the goose's tail, press it out using the back of a knife to remove it, being careful not to cut the skin.
**Key Points**: Cut accurately at the joints; the gland must be removed to avoid affecting the flavor.

2. **Marinate the Goose**: Place the whole goose in a basin, pour in an appropriate amount of light soy sauce, and rub it evenly over the inside and outside of the goose with your hands.
**Key Points**: Rub evenly to infuse a base flavor and to achieve a golden-brown skin when frying.

3. **Prepare Spices and Ingredients**: Cut the ginger into chunks, the red shallots into chunks, and peel the garlic. Set aside the fermented red bean curd, chu hou paste, rock sugar, five-spice powder, dried tangerine peel, star anise, bay leaves, and eight-treasure sweet vinegar.
4. **Fry the Goose**: Heat the wok, add a generous amount of oil. Once hot, add the marinated whole goose. Continuously ladle hot oil over the goose to fry the skin until golden brown. Turn the goose over and fry the other side the same way.
**Heat**: Medium-high heat
   **Doneness Check**: Goose skin showing an even golden-brown color
   **Key Points**: Follow the "hot wok, cold oil" principle; baste the goose with hot oil for even coloring and aroma.

5. **Stir-fry the Sauce Base**: Remove the fried goose from the wok. Leave some oil in the wok, add the ginger, shallots, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the fermented red bean curd, chu hou paste, dried tangerine peel, star anise, bay leaves, rock sugar, and five-spice powder. Stir-fry evenly until aromatic.
**Heat**: Medium heat
   **Doneness Check**: The aromatics and sauce ingredients emit a rich fragrance.

6. **Braise**: Return the fried goose to the wok with the stir-fried sauce. Sprinkle rice wine along the wok edge, using the high heat to release the aroma. Then add light soy sauce, eight-treasure sweet vinegar, and enough water to reach halfway up the goose. Place the bamboo mat at the bottom of the wok, and set the goose on it. Ladle the sauce from the wok over the goose repeatedly and frequently. Add the previously removed goose feet and wings. Cover with a lid and braise over medium-low heat.
**Heat and Time**: Medium-low heat for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it rest covered for another 5 minutes
   **Doneness Check**: A chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part of the goose meat, indicating it is fully cooked
   **Key Points**: Use high heat when adding the rice wine to create "wok hei"; place the bamboo mat to prevent sticking; the "braising" (碌) process requires patience, with repeated basting to ensure even flavor and color; strictly control braising and resting times for tender, flavorful meat that is not dry.

7. **Chop and Plate**: Remove the braised goose and let it cool slightly. Chop off the head, neck, feet, and wings. Using Cantonese "pi" (splitting) and "zhan" (chopping) techniques, cut the goose body into even pieces. Chop the neck, feet, and wings separately. Arrange the chopped goose pieces on a plate in their original shape, pour the remaining thick sauce from the wok over them evenly, and garnish with cilantro.
**Key Points**: Chop neatly and attractively; pour the sauce evenly.

## Health Advice
This Five-Spice Braised Goose is rich in protein and iron, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost. Note that the fermented red bean curd, chu hou paste, and sweet vinegar contain relatively high levels of salt and sugar. Those with high blood pressure or who are controlling sugar intake should consume in moderation.
