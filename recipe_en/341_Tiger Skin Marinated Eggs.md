# Tiger Skin Marinated Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings

🥃 Light soy sauce 3 tablespoons  
🥃 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥘 Pot  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  
🔪 Knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍲 Glass lid (for simmering)  

## Steps  
1. Place eggs in cold water, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 8 minutes until fully cooked.  
   **Note**: Must start with cold water; ensure sufficient cooking time.  

2. Remove the cooked eggs, submerge in cold water to cool, peel off the shells, and pat dry. Use a knife to make several shallow cuts on the egg surface.  
   **Key point**: Cold water helps with easy peeling; cuts allow the marinade to penetrate better.  

3. Heat the wok with slightly more oil, add the eggs, and fry over low heat until the skin turns golden and wrinkled (tiger skin texture). Remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: Keep heat low throughout to avoid burning.  

4. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add sliced garlic, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and cinnamon, and stir-fry until fragrant.  

5. Add the fried tiger skin eggs, pour in one can of beer, and bring to a boil over high heat.  

6. Add cola, rock sugar, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce in sequence, then sprinkle in the peanuts. Stir well.  

7. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.  

8. Uncover, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the eggs and peanuts.  
   **Note**: Stir constantly during reduction to prevent sticking.  

9. Transfer to a plate. Optionally, cut the marinated eggs in half and drizzle with some thick sauce before serving.  

## Health Advice  
Eggs are rich in high-quality protein and lecithin, while peanuts provide unsaturated fatty acids. However, the marinated preparation is relatively high in calories, so portion control is advised. Individuals with high blood pressure should reduce salt intake.
