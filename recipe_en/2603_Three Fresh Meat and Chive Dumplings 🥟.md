# Three Fresh Meat and Chive Dumplings 🥟

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons (spring chives are best)

## Ingredients and Seasonings 🛒
🥩 Ground pork belly 250g (with some fat, not lean only)  
🦐 Shrimp 75g (cut into chunks, keep some texture)  
🥬 Chinese chives 125g (finely chopped)  
🌾 High-gluten flour 280g (or dumpling flour)  
🥔 Potato starch 12g (added to dough, also use as dusting flour to prevent sticking)  
🧅 Onion about 10g (sliced, for infused oil)  
🫚 Scallion about 10g (cut into segments, for infused oil)  
🌿 Cilantro roots about 5g (crushed, for infused oil)  
🫚 Ginger about 3g (sliced, for infused oil)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 1 pinch (for infused oil)  
🫒 Cooking oil 40g (about 4 tbsp, for infusing)  
🧂 Salt 3g (1 level teaspoon)  
🧂 Baking soda 0.5g (a pinch, optional, makes meat filling more tender and water-absorbent)  
💧 Cool boiled water 100g (add to meat filling in batches)  
🍬 Sugar 3g (1 level teaspoon)  
🧂 MSG 1.5g (½ teaspoon)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g (1.5g for meat filling, about 0.5g for shrimp marinade)  
🥄 Light soy sauce about 15g (1 heaping tablespoon, plus more for dipping sauce)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce about 5g (⅓ tablespoon)  
🫒 Sesame oil about 6g (a few drops for shrimp marinade, a few drops for chive treatment)  
🌶️ Chili oil to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🍶 Black vinegar to taste (for dipping sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment 🍳
🥘 Small saucepan (for infusing oil)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (for kneading dough, mixing filling, tossing chives)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing spices and Sichuan peppercorns, adding and retrieving dumplings)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring chives, checking dumpling doneness)  
📄 Paper towels (for drying shrimp)  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board (for prepping ingredients)  
🥖 Rolling pin (for rolling dumpling wrappers)  
🍳 Wok or pot (for boiling dumplings)

## Cooking Steps 👩‍🍳
1. **Make infused oil**
   Pour cooking oil into a small saucepan. Add sliced onion, scallion segments, crushed cilantro roots, and ginger slices. Heat over high heat until bubbles appear, then reduce to medium-low. Fry slowly until the aromatics turn golden brown, then remove them. Turn off the heat. Immerse a slotted spoon containing Sichuan peppercorns into the hot oil, using residual heat to fry them until they turn deep purple. Remove the peppercorns and strain the oil. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Remove aromatics as soon as they are golden to avoid bitterness from burning. Fry Sichuan peppercorns using residual heat after turning off the stove; stop once they turn deep purple.

2. **Prepare meat filling**
   Add salt and baking soda to the ground pork belly. Mix well, then repeatedly slap and throw the mixture for 30-40 seconds until it becomes sticky and forms strands. Prepare 100g of cool boiled water. Add to the meat filling in 5-6 batches. After each addition, stir vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed and the filling becomes sticky and stringy again. Add sugar, MSG, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Mix well and stir in one direction until the filling is firm and cohesive. Finally, add 2 tablespoons of the infused oil in batches, stirring until fully incorporated each time.
   **Key point**: Slap the meat filling until it becomes sticky. Add water gradually in small batches, ensuring each addition is fully absorbed before adding more. Stir consistently in one direction throughout.

3. **Prepare shrimp**
   Rinse the shrimp clean and pat them completely dry with paper towels. Cut each shrimp in half or into three pieces depending on size, ensuring each dumpling gets a piece. Add a little chicken bouillon and a few drops of sesame oil. Toss to coat evenly, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate until ready to use.
   **Key point**: Shrimp must be thoroughly dried, or they will affect the filling’s texture. Keep them chunky; do not chop too finely.

4. **Knead and rest the dough**
   In a bowl, combine high-gluten flour, potato starch, and a pinch of salt. Mix well. Slowly add 150g of cool water, stirring into a flaky mass, then knead into a dough. Knead until the dough, hands, and bowl are all clean. Cover and let rest for 15 minutes. During this time, knead the dough every 5 minutes, 3 times total, until it becomes smooth and supple. After the final kneading, cover and let rest for a while longer.
   **Key point**: Do not make the dough too soft initially. Kneading multiple times during the resting period yields smoother, more elastic wrappers.

5. **Prepare chives**
   Wash and finely chop the chives. Place in a bowl. Add 2 tablespoons of the infused oil and a few drops of sesame oil. Gently stir with chopsticks to coat the cut surfaces of the chives evenly with oil.
   **Key point**: Oiling the chives prevents them from releasing water when mixed with the other filling ingredients.

6. **Combine the filling**
   Add the marinated shrimp and oiled chives to the bowl with the prepared meat filling. Gently mix everything together by hand.
   **Key point**: Mix lightly and just until combined. Avoid overmixing to prevent the chives from releasing water.

7. **Roll wrappers and assemble dumplings**
   Roll the rested dough into a long log. Cut into small pieces, each about 10g. Dust with potato starch to prevent sticking. Flatten each piece, then roll into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges, nearly translucent at the rim. Take one wrapper, place a portion of the filling in the center depression, and add a shrimp piece. For beginners, first pinch the edges together, then use the crook of your thumb and index finger to squeeze the dumpling into a plump, belly-shaped form.
   **Key point**: Wrappers should be thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Fill generously. The sealing method just needs to be tight to prevent leakage.

8. **Cook the dumplings**
   Fill a pot with plenty of water and bring to a rolling boil. Add the dumplings. Immediately after adding, gently push them along the bottom of the pot with the back of a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. Do not cover the pot. After the water returns to a boil, reduce heat to medium-low and let the dumplings boil gently for 2-3 minutes. Do not add cold water. The dumplings are done when they all float to the surface, the wrappers look puffed up, and they spring back when pressed.
   **Key point**: Use plenty of water. Gently stir immediately after adding dumplings. Boil with the lid off. Doneness is indicated by puffed wrappers that spring back.

9. **Prepare dipping sauce**
   In a small bowl, combine light soy sauce, chili oil, and black vinegar to taste. Stir to mix. Garlic is optional for chive-filled dumplings.

## Health Tips 💚
Three Fresh Meat and Chive Dumplings provide quality protein and dietary fiber, making them suitable for a warming springtime meal. Chives are believed to support Yang energy, and shrimp add calcium. However, the pork belly is relatively high in fat, so pairing these dumplings with a vegetable soup can help balance the calorie intake. When using the dipping sauce, moderate the amount of vinegar to avoid stimulating excessive stomach acid.
