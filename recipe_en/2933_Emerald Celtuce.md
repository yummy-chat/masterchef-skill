# Emerald Celtuce
Category: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Season Meals

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 One celtuce (peeled, strings removed, and cut into shreds)
🫑 About half a yellow bell pepper (cut into shreds)
🌶️ About half a red bell pepper (cut into shreds)
🧄 4-5 cloves of garlic (sliced)
🧂 Salt: two tablespoons for pickling, a pinch for the sauce
🍬 White sugar: two tablespoons for pickling, a pinch for the sauce
🍶 White vinegar: an appropriate amount for pickling, a splash before removing from heat
🧂 Chicken bouillon: a pinch
🌾 Cornstarch: an appropriate amount
🫒 Vegetable oil: an appropriate amount
🧈 Chicken fat or lard: one tablespoon

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Vegetable peeler
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup spoon
🔥 Stove

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the celtuce and remove the strings. First cut diagonally into sections, then cut into diamond-shaped slices, and finally cut into uniform shreds. **Note:** The shreds must be consistent in length and thickness to ensure even doneness and texture.
2. Remove the seeds from the yellow and red bell peppers. Cut them into shreds of similar thickness to the celtuce shreds.
3. Place the celtuce and bell pepper shreds in a bowl. Add two tablespoons of salt, two tablespoons of sugar, and a splash of white vinegar. Mix well with your hands, press down firmly, and let sit for about 8 minutes. **Key point:** The salt and sugar draw out water from the vegetables; this is a crucial step for maintaining a crunchy texture.
4. Once the vegetables have released water, rinse them several times with fresh water. Then, squeeze them firmly to remove as much water as possible. **Key point:** They must be thoroughly rinsed and squeezed dry to prevent the finished dish from being too salty.
5. In a small bowl, mix a pinch each of salt, chicken bouillon, and sugar with an appropriate amount of cornstarch. Add a small amount of water and stir to form a thin slurry. **Note:** The slurry should be lightly salted and thin in consistency.
6. Heat the wok over high heat. Add the vegetable oil and one tablespoon of chicken fat. Once the oil is hot, add the garlic slices and sauté over medium heat until fragrant.
7. Add the prepared vegetable shreds to the wok. Immediately turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until they are just cooked through and aromatic. Do not overcook.
8. Pour the prepared slurry along the edge of the wok. Immediately turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the slurry becomes translucent and evenly coats the ingredients.
9. Just before removing from the heat, add a splash of white vinegar along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly a few more times, then plate and serve. **Key point:** Adding vinegar at the end enhances the sour aroma and "wok hei," significantly elevating the flavor.

## Health Advice
Celtuce is rich in dietary fiber and potassium, low in calories, and high in water content, making it suitable for weight management or individuals with high blood pressure. The pickling and dewatering process creates a crunchier texture, but thorough rinsing is needed to remove excess salt and avoid excessive sodium intake.
