# Traditional Wooden Press Rapeseed Oil
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🟫 Rapeseed Approximately 100 jin per batch (core ingredient)  
* (Seasonings: None) *  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🌬️ Winnowing Machine (Winnowing Basket)  
🍳 Large Iron Wok  
🪨 Stone Mill  
♨️ Steamer / Large Pot  
🌾 Straw Rings  
🪵 Wooden Press (Press Trough)  
🔩 Wooden Wedges  
⚖️ Suspended Stone Hammer  

## Production Steps  
1. **Impurity Removal**: Pour the rapeseed into the winnowing machine, using wind to remove empty shells and impurities.  
   **Key Point**: The operation must be continuous without stopping; if sweat drips in or foreign matter mixes in, the already-screened rapeseed must be completely discarded and restarted.  
2. **Stir-frying Seeds**: Quickly and evenly stir-fry the rapeseed in a large iron wok until the color deepens and a rich, smoky aroma is released.  
   **Key Point**: The speed of stir-frying directly determines the oil quality; even a slight delay will burn the seeds.  
3. **Grinding into Powder**: Use a stone mill to grind the hot stir-fried rapeseed into a fine powder.  
   **Key Point**: Emphasizes “hot grinding,” racing against time, usually done in the early morning to ensure quality.  
4. **Steaming the Powder**: Place the rapeseed powder into a steamer and steam at high temperature; observe where steam emerges to ensure it is fully steamed through.  
   **Key Point**: Must be thoroughly steamed to soften the oil and facilitate oil extraction.  
5. **Wrapping the Cake**: While hot, pour the steamed powder into a round mold lined with straw, wrap tightly with straw, and trample it firm.  
   **Key Point**: The wrapping must be tight and sturdy; the straw serves both to guide oil and provide support.  
6. **Loading the Cake**: Vertically and tightly arrange the finished oil cakes into the wooden press trough.  
   **Key Point**: Must be packed tightly to prevent the cakes from collapsing or pressure from dispersing.  
7. **Pressing Oil**: The operator pulls the suspended giant stone hammer, using momentum to repeatedly strike the wooden wedges behind the oil cakes.  
   **Key Point**: Requires coordinated body exertion with the stone hammer’s gravity; typically 2,000 to 3,000 strikes are needed; extremely dangerous and physically demanding, demanding full concentration and focus during operation.  
8. As the striking continues, golden liquid oil flows from the gaps in the wooden press; collect it accordingly.  

## Health Advice  
The traditional pressing process preserves the natural flavor of rapeseed oil, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to use no more than 25 grams of oil per day, suitable for middle-aged and elderly people who value traditional flavors. Be careful to prevent burns and slipping during operation; beginners should never imitate the “stone hammer striking” step.
