# Oil Dip
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (crushed and minced)  
🍋 Small kumquat, 1 piece (juice extracted, seeds removed using a strainer)  
🍋 Lemon juice, to taste (optional, enhances freshness)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, 1 piece (chopped, optional)  
🫒 Sesame oil, a small amount  
🫗 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife  
🥣 Strainer  
🥃 Glass bowl  
🥄 Mixing spoon  
🔥 Pot and stove (for blanching seafood)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for picking up ingredients)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Chop the cilantro, and crush the garlic with the knife blade before mincing it into small pieces.  
**Note**: The garlic does not need to be minced too finely; retain some graininess.  

2. Cut the small kumquat in half, squeeze the juice into the bowl using a strainer to remove the seeds; add an appropriate amount of lemon juice if desired.  
3. If you like spice, cut the bird's eye chili into small rings or fine pieces and set aside.  
4. In a bowl containing the chopped cilantro, minced garlic, and juice, first add a small amount of sesame oil, then add an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce.  
**Key Point**: Add the sesame oil first, then the steamed fish soy sauce, to facilitate flavor blending.  

5. Add the chopped bird's eye chili, and use a spoon to stir all ingredients thoroughly until well combined.  
**Consistency Check**: The sauce should be a dark brown color, with visible green cilantro, white garlic, red chili, and floating yellow oil droplets.  

6. For serving: Blanch the seafood until it turns red and is fully cooked, then use chopsticks to pick it up and dip it into the sauce.  

## Health Advice  
This dipping sauce is rich in antioxidants from garlic and lemon juice, with low calories and high flavor, making it suitable for weight management. Note that the sodium content in sesame oil and steamed fish soy sauce is relatively high; individuals with high blood pressure should moderate the dipping frequency.
