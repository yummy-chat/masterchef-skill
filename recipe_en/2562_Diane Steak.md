# Diane Steak
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 1 ribeye steak (about 4cm thick)
🍄 Mushrooms, to taste (sliced, plus whole ones for garnish)
🧅 Red onion, to taste (minced)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)
🫛 Green peas, to taste
🌿 Parsley, to taste (chopped)
🧂 Salt, to taste
⚫ Black pepper, to taste (for marinating and garnish)
🫒 Olive oil, to taste (for marinating)
🌻 Rapeseed oil, to taste (for pan-searing the steak)
🧈 Butter, 1 piece
🥃 Cognac or whiskey, to taste
🥛 Heavy cream, to taste (added in two batches)
🟡 Yellow mustard, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🍽️ Plate
🍳 Frying pan
🌡️ Food thermometer
🛡️ Aluminum foil
🥢 Tongs
🥣 Strainer

## Cooking Steps
1. Pat the steak dry with kitchen paper towels in advance. Evenly sprinkle salt and black pepper on both sides, drizzle with olive oil, and gently massage until fully absorbed.
2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add salt, blanch the green peas until they float, then drain and set aside.
3. Add rapeseed oil to a frying pan, heat over high heat until the oil smokes, then place the steak in the pan.
   **Note**: The oil temperature must be high enough to quickly form a crust.
4. Sear one side until deep brown, then flip. Once both sides have a caramelized crust, remove the steak and wrap it in aluminum foil to keep warm.
5. Without washing the pan, add minced onion, minced garlic, sliced mushrooms, and butter, and sauté until fragrant.
6. Pour in the cognac or whiskey, scraping the bottom of the pan with a spatula to release the browned bits and incorporate them into the sauce.
7. Add whole mushrooms to cook until done, season with a little salt, then remove and set aside for plating garnish.
8. Pour half of the heavy cream into the pan sauce, stir to combine. Then add the yellow mustard and mix thoroughly.
9. Add the remaining heavy cream and a little water, season with salt and black pepper, and cook until the sauce slightly thickens.
   **Key**: Adding the heavy cream in two batches helps prevent the sauce from separating.
10. In another pan over medium-low heat, reheat the steak wrapped in aluminum foil until the center reaches 55–56°C (medium-rare), with pink flesh.
11. Remove the steak, let it rest for a moment, then slice. On the plate, arrange the green peas and whole mushrooms, drizzle with the sauce, and sprinkle with a little crushed black pepper and chopped parsley.

## Health Tips
This Diane Steak is relatively high in protein and calories, making it suitable for muscle-building periods or as an occasional indulgence. However, it is a high-fat, high-calorie dish, so people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should be mindful of portion sizes. The cognac sauce is key to the flavor, so it is recommended to pair it with the steak.
