# Braised Chinese Cabbage with Vermicelli

Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Steps
1. Soak the vermicelli in clean water in advance until fully softened, then drain and set aside.
   **Note**: Vermicelli must be soaked beforehand, otherwise it will be difficult to cook through during braising and will absorb too much broth, affecting the texture.
2. Mix the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and thirteen-spice powder together in a bowl to form a sauce mixture, and set aside.
   **Note**: Preparing the sauce in advance prevents scrambling during cooking and ensures even seasoning.
3. Place the wok over heat, pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil and heat it. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes until the meat is fully browned and the fat has rendered out.
   **Note**: Thoroughly rendering the fat from the pork belly reduces greasiness and makes the dish more aromatic.
4. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, minced garlic, dried red chili peppers, and star anise. Continue stir-frying over medium heat for about 1 minute until the aromatics release a rich fragrance.
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of chili peppers according to personal preference; be careful not to burn the aromatics while stir-frying.
5. Add the Chinese cabbage sections and stir-fry over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes until the cabbage is just cooked through, the leaves become soft, and the stems turn translucent.
   **Note**: Do not overcook the cabbage; it should be just cooked through to avoid becoming too mushy during subsequent braising.
6. Pour in the prepared sauce mixture and stir-fry over medium heat for about 1 minute, ensuring all ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce.
   **Note**: Stir-fry thoroughly to ensure every piece of ingredient absorbs the savory flavor of the sauce.
7. Add an appropriate amount of hot water and place the soaked vermicelli on top, gently spreading it flat.
   **Key**: Use hot water instead of cold to avoid drastically lowering the wok's temperature, which would affect the braising process; pre-soaking the vermicelli shortens the cooking time.
8. Add the salt and MSG, gently stir to combine, allowing the seasonings to dissolve into the broth.
   **Note**: Add salt and MSG in small amounts first, then adjust, to avoid oversalting.
9. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to medium, and braise for about 10 minutes, minimizing the number of times you open the lid during this period.
   **Key**: The braising time must be sufficient for the vermicelli to fully absorb the broth and become soft and springy, but avoid over-braising, which can make the vermicelli mushy and cause it to break apart.
10. Remove the lid, increase the heat to high, and stir-fry while reducing the sauce for about 2-3 minutes until the broth thickens and evenly coats the ingredients. Then turn off the heat and serve.
    **Note**: Stir constantly while reducing the sauce to prevent burning; the desired thickness of the broth can be adjusted to personal preference, and the reduction time can be shortened if you prefer more broth.

## Health Advice
Rendering the fat from the pork belly before braising reduces greasiness, making this dish suitable for warming up in autumn and winter; pre-soaking the vermicelli prevents it from absorbing too much broth. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure should consume in small amounts.
