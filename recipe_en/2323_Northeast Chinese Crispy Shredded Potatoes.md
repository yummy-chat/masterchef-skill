# Northeast Chinese Crispy Shredded Potatoes
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, about 500g (two)
🧂 Potato starch, about 6g (half a spoon)
🧂 Salt, about 3g (1 level teaspoon)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, about 3g (1 teaspoon)
🫒 Cooking oil, twice the amount used for stir-frying
🧂 Cumin powder, to taste
🧂 BBQ seasoning, to taste
🌿 Scallions, chopped, to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (or iron wok)
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Grater
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍽️ Plate (for assisting with flipping)
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for chopping scallions)

## Instructions
1. Peel and wash the potatoes, then thoroughly pat dry the surface with a paper towel.
**Note**: The potatoes must be dried, otherwise it will affect subsequent steps.
2. Use a grater to shred the potatoes into thin strips, preferably using the fine shredding side.
3. Add about 6g potato starch, 3g salt, and 3g chicken bouillon to the shredded potatoes.
**Note**: Do not rinse the shredded potatoes; do not stir at this point to prevent the salt from drawing out too much water prematurely.
4. Heat the pan over high heat, pour in twice the amount of oil used for stir-frying, enough to cover the bottom; if using an iron wok, fully season the pan: spray oil, heat until smoking, pour out the hot oil, then add fresh oil.
**Key**: The pan and oil temperature must reach about 200°C; you should feel obvious heat when holding your hand above the pan.
5. Right before adding to the pan, toss the shredded potatoes with the seasonings until well mixed, then spread them evenly into the hot oiled pan. Do not pour in any liquid remaining at the bottom of the bowl.
**Doneness check**: When the oil is hot enough, you will hear a distinct sizzling sound.
6. Use a spatula to spread the shredded potatoes evenly, shaping them into a round disc, and round off the edges.
**Key**: Maintain the highest heat and fry for 1 minute to fully set the potato cake.
7. Reduce to medium heat and continue frying for about 4 minutes, using the spatula to adjust the shape as needed.
**Note**: When the edges turn golden brown and crispy, you can lift the edge with a spatula to check the browning on the bottom; if browning too quickly, adjust the heat down slightly.
8. Carefully flip the cake and continue frying the other side over medium heat for 4 minutes.
**Tip**: If you are not skilled at flipping with a toss, use a plate—slide the cake onto the plate, then slide it back into the pan.
9. Once both sides are golden and crispy, sprinkle with cumin powder, BBQ seasoning, and chopped scallions, then remove from the pan.

## Health Advice
This dish uses potatoes as a carbohydrate source and is high in calories after frying. It is suitable for those in a muscle-building phase or for heavy laborers; those in a fat-loss phase should consume in moderation. Be careful to control oil temperature to avoid excessive acrylamide formation. Best enjoyed hot, as the texture deteriorates when cooled.
