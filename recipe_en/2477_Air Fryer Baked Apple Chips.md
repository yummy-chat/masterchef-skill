# Air Fryer Baked Apple Chips
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍎 Apples, as needed  
🧂 Table salt, as needed (for soaking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Peeler, knife, cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowls (2)  
♨️ Air fryer  
🍲 Steamer (with lid)

## Steps
1. Wash the apples, peel off the skin, cut in half and remove the core, then slice into thick pieces. If the apples are large, further cut them into uniform chunks.  
2. Place the apple slices in clean water, add an appropriate amount of table salt, and soak for 5–10 minutes. Remove and drain well.  
   **Note**: Adding salt prevents oxidation and discoloration and enhances sweetness.  
3. Arrange the apple slices flat in the air fryer. Set to 180°C and bake for 20 minutes, until the surface is dehydrated and dry, fruit sugars are released, and the aroma is rich.  
4. Remove the apple slices and place them in a steamer. Steam over high heat for 15 minutes, until the apples are fully softened and full of moisture.  
5. Place the steamed apple slices back into the air fryer. Set to 130°C and bake for 30 minutes, until the color deepens significantly and the surface appears caramelized.  
6. Remove and place in the steamer for a second time. Steam over high heat for 10 minutes, until the moisture is noticeably reduced.  
7. Finally, return the apple slices to the air fryer. Set to 120°C and bake for 20 minutes to complete dehydration.  
   **Note**: If using a conventional air fryer, flip the slices halfway through to ensure even heating.  
8. Remove and let cool slightly before eating. It is recommended to let them sit until the next day for better flavor.

## Health Tips
These air fryer apple chips use a salt-soaking method to prevent oxidation and a three-bake, three-steam process, retaining the natural sweetness and dietary fiber of the apples. They are a low-fat, healthy snack suitable for those managing sugar intake. Be mindful of portion size, as the concentration process also concentrates the sugar and calories.
