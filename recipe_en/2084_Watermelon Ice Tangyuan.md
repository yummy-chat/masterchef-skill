# Watermelon Ice Tangyuan
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍉 Watermelon (take the flesh to extract juice, hollow out the rind to use as a container)  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour (knead into a dough)  
🍚 Sago (pre-cooked and set aside)  
🧊 White jelly powder (for making watermelon jelly)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🔪 Large knife (to cut the watermelon)  
🥄 Masher (to mash out the juice)  
🥅 Strainer (to filter the juice)  
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing and kneading)  
🪓 Cutting board (for rolling and cutting dough pieces)  
🔪 Small knife (to cut dough pieces and jelly)  
🍲 Cooking pot (to cook tangyuan and heat juice)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (to scoop out tangyuan)  
🧊 Ice water bowl (to cool tangyuan)  
🍳 Non-stick pan (to heat watermelon juice for jelly)  
📦 Rectangular mold/preservation box (for setting the jelly)  
🍉 Hollowed-out watermelon rind bowl (for final serving)  

## Steps  
1. **Extract watermelon juice**: Cut the watermelon open, take some of the flesh and cut it into chunks. Use a masher to extract the juice, then strain it through a strainer to remove the pulp, obtaining pure watermelon juice.  
   **Note**: Make sure to strain thoroughly, leaving no pulp in the juice.  
2. **Knead the dough**: Gradually pour the filtered watermelon juice into the glutinous rice flour a little at a time, kneading as you go, until a smooth, non-sticky pink dough forms.  
   **Key point**: Adjust the amount of juice based on the dough's consistency; avoid adding too much at once.  
3. **Shape the tangyuan pieces**: On a cutting board, roll the dough into a long strip, then cut it into evenly sized small segments. A little dry flour can be sprinkled to prevent sticking.  
4. **Cook the tangyuan**: Bring water in a pot to a full boil, then add all the dough pieces. Once all the tangyuan float to the surface, continue cooking for another 5 minutes.  
   **Judgment criteria**: They are cooked when they float.  
   **Key point**: Remove immediately after cooking and soak in ice water to make the tangyuan more chewy.  
5. **Make the watermelon jelly**: Mix the white jelly powder with the reserved watermelon juice, stir well, and pour into a non-stick pan to heat.  
   **Note**: Turn off the heat as soon as the liquid comes to a slight boil; avoid prolonged boiling. Pour the hot liquid into a rectangular mold, let it cool until fully set, unmold, and cut into large pieces.  
6. **Assemble**: In the hollowed-out watermelon bowl, layer in the chilled watermelon tangyuan, watermelon jelly pieces, and pre-cooked sago in order.  
   **Note**: All ingredients should be pre-cooled or chilled to ensure a cold, refreshing texture.  
7. Serve immediately and enjoy this pink, sweet, chewy, and smooth summer cold dessert.  

## Health Advice  
This watermelon ice tangyuan uses fruit juice to knead the dough, with sweetness coming from the watermelon itself, making it lighter and more refreshing than traditional sweet soups, perfect for cooling down in hot summer. Glutinous rice products are hard to digest, so consumption should be moderate. The watermelon rind used as a container is for decoration only and is not edible.
