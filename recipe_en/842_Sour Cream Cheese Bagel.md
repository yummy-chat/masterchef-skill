# Sour Cream Cheese Bagel
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 300g
🥯 Dry yeast 3g
💧 Water 160g (adjustable as needed)
🥛 Sour cream 30g (excess will make the dough wetter, but will not affect the texture)
🧂 Salt 5g
🍬 Sugar 10g
🧀 Cheese powder to taste (for surface decoration, adds savory flavor)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🤖 Stand mixer
🍲 Pot
🥖 Rolling pin
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍞 Baking sheet
📄 Parchment paper
🔥 Oven
🧻 Plastic wrap
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Weigh all ingredients. Prepare bread flour, dry yeast, water, sour cream, salt, and sugar according to the recipe. Set aside cheese powder separately.
   **Note**: Water amount can be slightly adjusted; if sour cream is excessive, the dough will be wetter, but the final texture will remain soft and the overall flavor will not be affected.
2. Place all ingredients except cheese powder into the stand mixer. Knead for about 8 minutes. The dough will be slightly wet. No need to knead until windowpane stage.
   **Key point**: Control the dough temperature after mixing. Avoid over-kneading when the environment is too warm to prevent the dough temperature from rising and affecting subsequent fermentation.
3. Remove the dough and shape it into a ball. Divide evenly into 8 pieces. Round each piece, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for about 10 minutes to relax the gluten for easier shaping.
4. Take a rested dough piece and roll it out with a rolling pin. Roll it into a long strip, then join the ends to form a bagel ring. Place on a cutting board lined with parchment paper.
   **Note**: The dough will be slightly sticky due to high hydration, but the seams will be less likely to burst during boiling after shaping.
5. Place the shaped bagels in a warm spot to ferment for about 40 minutes, until they spring back slowly when lightly pressed on the side with a finger.
6. Bring a pot of water to a boil over high heat. Lower each bagel together with the parchment paper into the boiling water. Boil each side, then remove with a slotted spoon and transfer to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
   **Observation**: Due to the high hydration dough, the surface will develop noticeable wrinkles after boiling; some will smooth out during baking as the bagels expand.
7. Evenly sprinkle an ample amount of cheese powder over the boiled bagels. The amount can be adjusted to personal preference.
8. Preheat the oven to 210°C. Place the baking sheet on the middle rack and bake for 20 minutes.
9. After baking, transfer the bagels to a cooling rack to cool. Store in a sealed plastic bag and keep in the freezer.

## Health Advice
Sour cream cheese bagels have a moderate carbohydrate content, suitable as a breakfast or post-workout energy boost. Be mindful of the amount of cheese powder to avoid excess sodium intake. The high hydration dough results in a softer texture, but careful handling is needed during fermentation and boiling steps.
