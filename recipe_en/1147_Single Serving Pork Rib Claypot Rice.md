# Single Serving Pork Rib Claypot Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount (no need to pre-soak the rice)
🥩 Pork spare ribs (front ribs), about 200g (flat bones, tender meat)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced into thin strips)
🥦 Broccoli, appropriate amount (cut into small florets)
🌽 Corn kernels, appropriate amount (frozen)
🥕 Diced carrots, appropriate amount (frozen)
🥚 Egg, 1 piece
🫘 Fermented black beans (douchi), appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating and sauce)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
⚪️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
💧 Boiling water, appropriate amount
🛢️ Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for cooking rice and marinating)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottomed pot (with lid)
🥣 Small milk pot (for marinating)
🥛 Glass cup
📡 Microwave
🥄 Wooden spoon
🥣 Small bowl

## Steps
1. Place the ribs in a small milk pot, add fermented black beans, ginger strips, cornstarch, a little cooking oil, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, and white pepper powder. Mix well and set aside to marinate.
**Key Point**: Choose front ribs; the flat bones are easier to flavor and the meat is more tender.

2. In a small bowl, mix light soy sauce, a pinch of sugar, and an appropriate amount of boiling water. Stir to combine and set aside as a pouring sauce.
**Key Point**: The ratio of soy sauce to boiled water is simple; no complicated preparation needed.

3. Cut the broccoli into small florets and place them in a glass cup. Add a little water and microwave for about 3 minutes until cooked. Remove and drain.
4. Add rice and water to the flat-bottomed pot, drizzle in a little cooking oil, cover with the lid, and bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat.
5. When the surface of the rice appears mostly dry with visible "rice eyes" (about 80% cooked), evenly spread the marinated ribs over the rice. Cover and continue to simmer over low heat.
**Key Point**: Ribs take longer to cook than beef or cured meats, so add them earlier to ensure they are fully cooked.

6. After simmering for a while, crack the egg into an empty space on the side of the pot. Add the blanched broccoli, corn kernels, and diced carrots. Cover and continue to simmer.
7. When you hear a crackling sound from the pot and the egg is mostly set, open the lid and pour in the prepared sauce. Immediately turn the heat to high to quickly reduce the liquid.
**Key Point**: Turning to high heat after adding the sauce is the key to forming crispy scorched rice; this step needs to be done quickly.

8. Turn off the heat. Use a spoon to lift the crispy rice crust from the bottom of the pot. Serve the entire pot as is.

## Health Tips
This claypot rice is rich in protein, and the combination of ribs and rice provides long-lasting satiety, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. The addition of broccoli and corn kernels increases dietary fiber. However, the ribs are marinated with oil and oyster sauce, making the dish relatively high in calories. For weight loss, consider reducing the amount of ribs or substituting chicken breast.
