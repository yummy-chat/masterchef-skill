# Dongpo Pork (Braised Pork Belly)
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Available for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Skin-on pork belly 1000g (about 2 jin, choose pork belly with firm skin and tightly connected fat and lean layers)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into long sections, enough to cover the bottom of the pot)
🧄 Garlic with skin 30g (4 cloves, do not peel)
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced)
🧅 Leek 50g (cut into large long sections)
🫘 Red dates 4
🍂 Dried hawthorn slices 6
🍬 Rock sugar 85g (50g for caramelizing, 35g for braising; rock sugar preferred, granulated sugar can substitute)
💧 Water, as needed (30g for caramelizing, additional boiling water for braising)
🍶 Yellow wine 90g (about 9 tablespoons, choose a high-quality brand such as Lao Heng He or Gu Yue Long Shan)
🧂 Light soy sauce 70g (about 7 tablespoons)
🧂 Dark soy sauce about 20g (10g initially, add more after 1 hour of braising depending on color)
🧂 Salt 3g (about 1 teaspoon)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 1g (a pinch)
🌿 Cinnamon stick 0.5g (about the size of a thumbnail)
⭐ Star anise 2g (2 pieces)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch or hot iron pan (for scorching the pork skin)
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🥘 Clay pot
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale and measuring spoons
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Treat the pork skin**: Use a blowtorch to scorch the pork skin until black, or place it skin-side down in a hot dry pan until charred. Immediately soak in warm water for a while, then scrape and wash clean.
    **Key point**: Scorching effectively removes any gamey smell.

2.  **Blanch and set shape**: Place the pork in a pot of cold water with the skin facing up. When the water is about to boil, turn the pork over. Skim off any foam. Reduce heat to the lowest setting, cover, and cook for about 10 minutes, until the pork is 40% cooked and fully set.
    **Key point**: Starting in cold water allows blood to seep out gradually.

3.  **Cut into pieces**: Remove the pork and let it cool until it's no longer hot to the touch. Pat dry with kitchen paper towels. Cut into cubes of about 4×4 cm.
    **Key point**: Uniform cubes ensure even cooking.

4.  **Line the pot bottom**: Cover the bottom of the clay pot with a generous layer of scallion sections. This prevents sticking and adds scallion aroma.
5.  **Pan-fry the pork**: Over medium heat, place the pork cubes meat-side down, frying each side until lightly golden. Briefly fry the skin side last. Avoid high heat and prolonged frying to prevent the meat from becoming tough. Reserve the rendered pork fat in the pan for later use.
6.  **Arrange the pork**: Place the fried pork cubes skin-side down in neat rows on top of the scallions in the clay pot.
7.  **Fry the aromatics**: Using the reserved pork fat in the pan, add ginger slices, leek sections, garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Fry until fragrant. Turn off the heat and pour the aromatics and oil over the pork in the clay pot.
8.  **Caramelize sugar**: In a clean wok, combine 50g of rock sugar and 30g of water. Melt over low heat, then reduce to the lowest setting. Stir constantly. When the syrup changes from large bubbles to dense small bubbles, and then turns a date-red color with large bubbles forming and popping, immediately remove from the heat. Continue stirring off the heat until the bubbles subside. Add half a bowl of cold water to the caramel. Return to medium heat and stir to dissolve any solidified sugar, creating caramel water.
    **Key point**: The water method with cold water prevents splattering. The caramel color should be between date-red and golden. Control using residual heat after removing from the stovetop.

9.  **Season and braise**: Pour the caramel water into the clay pot. Add yellow wine, light soy sauce, 10g of dark soy sauce, 35g of rock sugar, red dates, and dried hawthorn slices. Pour in enough boiling water to submerge the meat cubes completely, plus a little extra. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Keep the lid slightly ajar until the boil is stable to prevent spillover. Once stable, cover tightly, reduce to the lowest heat, and braise for 1 hour.
10. **First adjustment**: After 1 hour, turn the meat cubes over. Add the salt. Check the color and add more dark soy sauce if needed.
11. **Continue braising**: Cover and braise over the lowest heat for another 1.5 hours, until the meat is extremely tender. If the liquid reduces significantly during this time, add boiling water to prevent scorching.
12. **Remove solids**: After braising is complete, use a slotted spoon to remove all the spices, scallions, ginger, and other solid seasonings to prevent them from dissolving into the sauce and affecting the texture.
13. **Reduce the sauce**: Strain the braising liquid into a wok or frying pan. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and reduce until it thickens slightly and coats a spoon (a "natural glaze"). Turn off the heat.
    **Note**: The meat is very tender by now. Move it carefully during sauce reduction to prevent it from breaking apart or the sauce from burning.

14. **Plate and sauce**: Arrange the meat cubes on a serving plate. You may trim the bottom of each cube so it stands upright. Pour the reduced sauce evenly over the meat. Any trimmed pieces of meat can be eaten as well.

## Health Advice
Dongpo Pork is rich in high-quality protein and energy, making it suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure when consumed in moderation. However, it is also high in saturated fat and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and whole grains to balance the nutrition.
