## Instructions
1.  **Prepare the fish:** Scale the bighead carp, remove the gills and innards. Separate the head and bones from the flesh. Reserve the bones for making soup and the head for another use.
2.  **Cut the fish:** First cut the fish flesh into long strips, then cut into evenly sized cubes. If the fish is large, remove the腥筋*.
3.  **Marinate the fish:** Rinse the fish cubes several times in clean water to remove any blood. Drain well. Add salt, chicken bouillon, ground white pepper, light soy sauce, a generous amount of cooking wine, and 1 whole egg. Mix thoroughly. Add one bowl of cornstarch and continue mixing until each chunk is evenly coated. Let marinate for 20 minutes.
    **Note:** The mixing must be thorough to ensure every chunk is well-coated. This is the key to a crispy exterior and tender interior.
4.  **Prepare vegetables and aromatics:** Cut the konjac into cubes similar in size to the fish chunks. Slice the pickled radish, then shred. Chop the pickled ginger, pickled chili, and pickled cowpea. Cut the scallion and celery into sections. Slice half the garlic, mince the other half. Slice or mince the ginger. Set aside.
5.  **Fry the fish:** Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot (approx. 350°F/175°C). Gently add the marinated fish chunks one by one and fry over medium-low heat until golden and crisp.
    **Key point:** Do not stir immediately after adding the fish. Wait until the surface is set, then gently move them to prevent the batter from falling off or sticking. Fry until the chunks float to the surface and are a light golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
6.  **Prepare the base seasoning:** In a separate wok, heat some oil. Add the sliced ginger and garlic, and Pixian broad bean paste. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the oil turns red. Add the chopped pickled ginger, pickled chili, shredded pickled radish, green and red Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili segments. Continue stir-frying over medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes until the sour, spicy, and numbing aromas are fully released.
7.  **Braise to infuse flavor:** Pour in the entire bottle of beer. Bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt, chicken bouillon, ground white pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Add the konjac cubes first and cook for about 5 minutes to absorb the sauce. Gently add the fried fish chunks. Lightly push them with a spoon to submerge. Reduce the heat to medium-low and braise for about 10 minutes.
    **Note:** Konjac is resistant to overcooking, so it goes into the pot first. The fish is added later. Do not braise for too long, or the fish will break apart.
8.  **Finish and enhance aroma:** Just before removing from the heat, add a dash of vinegar and Sichuan pepper oil. Sprinkle the celery sections, scallion sections, and a generous amount of minced garlic. Stir until the vegetables are just cooked. Serve immediately.
    **Note:** The minced garlic is added last to preserve its strong, aromatic flavor, which enhances the overall complexity of the dish.

## Health Advice
Fish Chunks is a boldly flavored sour and spicy dish. The combination of konjac and fish flesh can increase satiety, making it suitable for sharing. It is recommended to control the intake of rice, as the sauce is calorie-dense. Use a moderate amount of cornstarch for marinating to avoid the fish chunks having a pasty texture.
