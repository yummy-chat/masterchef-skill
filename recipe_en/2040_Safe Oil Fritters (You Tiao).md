# Safe Oil Fritters (You Tiao)
Category: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 2 jin (about 1000 grams)  
🧂 Aluminum-free baking powder 5 grams  
🧂 Salt 12 grams  
🍚 Dry yeast 10 grams  
🍬 White sugar 12 grams  
💧 Warm water 640 grams (about 40°C, not hot to the touch)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Measuring spoons  
🔪 Cutting board, rolling pin, chef's knife  
🥢 Long bamboo chopsticks  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon or stainless steel oil drain rack  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Pour the flour, aluminum-free baking powder, and salt into a large bowl. Stir thoroughly to mix evenly.  
   **Note**: Baking powder and salt must be fully combined to prevent uneven expansion due to local concentration.  
2. In a measuring cup, add warm water, dry yeast, and white sugar. Stir until the yeast and sugar are mostly dissolved.  
   **Key point**: The water temperature should be comfortable to the touch (not hot), as excessive heat will kill the yeast; adding sugar accelerates yeast activation.  
3. Gradually pour the yeast water into the dry mixture in batches, stirring as you go, to form a soft, moist dough. Use the "pressing" method: lightly dip your hand in water to prevent sticking, then quickly and firmly press down on the dough with your fist or the heel of your palm. Avoid kneading; the goal is to press the water and ingredients evenly into the dough.  
4. Perform "three presses and three rests": Press for 2-3 minutes until the dough is initially smooth, then let it rest for 10 minutes. Press again for 1-2 minutes until the dough is smooth, fine, and elastic. If not sufficiently smooth after one cycle, repeat the process.  
   **Key point**: Pressing promotes gluten formation, while resting relaxes the gluten. Alternating these steps is fundamental for fluffy and soft fritters.  
5. Form the dough into a thick, long strip. Brush the surface with oil, wrap tightly with plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.  
   **Purpose**: Brushing with oil and sealing prevents the surface from drying out while fully relaxing the gluten, making it easier to shape later.  
6. On a cutting board, press and stretch the rested dough into an evenly thick rectangular sheet. Cut it into small pieces 2-3 fingers wide. Apply a water line on every other piece, then stack another piece on top. Use the back of a knife to press firmly along the water line to bond them together. Take a prepared piece, gently stretch it by holding both ends to a length suitable for your wok, and trim off any uneven ends.  
   **Note**: Stretch evenly to avoid uneven thickness; trimming the ends ensures a more uniform shape for the fried fritters.  
7. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat it to 60-70% of its capacity. Carefully place the prepared pieces into the oil. Once they float to the surface, immediately use long chopsticks to flip and move them continuously.  
   **Key point**: The oil temperature must be high enough for the pieces to expand quickly and float; frequent flipping ensures the fritters remain straight, puff up fully, and brown evenly.  
8. Fry until the fritters are golden brown all over, have fully expanded, and are set. Use a slotted spoon to remove them and drain the oil.  

## Health Advice  
Safe oil fritters eliminate the risk of aluminum, but they are still high in calories. It is recommended to consume them with soy milk or vegetables, limiting the portion to 2-3 pieces per serving. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood lipids should eat in moderation.
