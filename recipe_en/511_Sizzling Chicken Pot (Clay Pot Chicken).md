# Sizzling Chicken Pot (Clay Pot Chicken)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken (bone-in, cut into bite-sized pieces, about two bites each)  
🧄 Garlic (1 whole head, peeled)  
🫚 Ginger (about half a piece, sliced)  
🧅 Shallots/Red onions (a few, peeled)  
🌿 Sand ginger (to taste, cut into large pieces)  
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers (to taste, cut into chunks)  
🍃 Cilantro (to taste, cut into sections)  
🥜 Chu hou paste (about 1 tablespoon)  
🥜 Sesame paste (about 1 teaspoon)  
🥜 Peanut butter (about 1 teaspoon)  
🦪 Oyster sauce (about 1 tablespoon)  
🍬 Sugar (about 1 teaspoon)  
🌶️ White pepper (about 1/2 teaspoon)  
🌾 Cornstarch (about 1 tablespoon)  
🐔 Chicken fat (about 1 tablespoon, divided for marinating and sautéing)  
🍶 Shaojiu/High-proof liquor (to taste, divided for marinating and cooking)  

## Equipment & Tools  
🍲 Clay pot  
🔥 Stove/Gas range  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Large bowl  
🧻 Paper towels  
⏲️ Timer  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces (about two bites each). Use paper towels to repeatedly press and thoroughly dry the surface. This step is key to achieving a dry, fragrant result without being watery.
2. Place the dried chicken pieces in a large bowl. Add chu hou paste, sesame paste, peanut butter, oyster sauce, sugar, white pepper, cornstarch, and a portion of the chicken fat. Mix thoroughly by hand, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated with the sauce. Finally, drizzle in a small amount of shaojiu and mix well.
   **Note**: After adding the shaojiu, cook within 5 minutes, or the chicken may spoil.

3. Heat the clay pot over high heat. Add the remaining chicken fat. When the oil is hot and just begins to smoke, add the ginger slices and whole garlic cloves. Stir-fry over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant.
4. Arrange the marinated chicken pieces flat in the pot, ensuring direct contact with the hot surface. At this point, a loud sizzling sound (“啫啫”) should be heard. Maintain high heat and pan-fry.
5. Cover the clay pot with its lid. Reduce the heat to medium-low and braise. Time for about 4 minutes total.
6. At around 2 minutes and 30 seconds of braising, drizzle a circle of shaojiu along the edge of the hot lid. The alcohol will evaporate from the high heat, possibly igniting to enhance wok hei and aroma.
7. Remove the lid. Add the shallot chunks and sand ginger pieces. Stir with chopsticks. The sauce in the pot should be reduced, with no excess liquid visible.
8. Finally, add the green and red bell pepper chunks and cilantro sections. Quickly toss using residual heat until just cooked. Serve immediately from the pot.

## Health Tips  
This dish uses a clay pot over high heat for a quick braise, resulting in dry, fragrant chicken without greasiness. However, the chu hou paste, sesame paste, and peanut butter are relatively high in sodium. Those with high blood pressure should minimize consuming the sauce. It is ideal for those who enjoy rich, savory flavors and wok hei, and pairs perfectly with a bowl of steamed rice.
