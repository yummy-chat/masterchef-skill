# Home-Style Sauce-Braised Lamb Spine
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lamb spine (lamb backbone) about 3 jin (about 12 pieces)
🧅 Scallion 1 section (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (sliced)
🧂 Vinegar appropriate amount (for blanching)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers a few
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns a pinch
🪷 Angelica dahurica appropriate amount
🌿 Galangal appropriate amount
⚪ White peppercorns appropriate amount
🫘 Soybean paste appropriate amount (one of the mixed sauces)
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount (one of the mixed sauces)
🍲 Chu hou paste a little (one of the mixed sauces, key flavor)
💧 Mineral water appropriate amount (to cover the ingredients)

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Stainless steel soup pot/cooking pot
🍳 Wok
🍚 Rice cooker or pressure cooker (pressure cooker recommended)
🥄 Spoon, chopsticks, slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🥣 Bowls, plates, strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the lamb spine in water for 6 hours to fully draw out the blood, changing the water midway.
2. Prepare angelica dahurica, galangal, white peppercorns, dried chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns; mix soybean paste, oyster sauce, and a little chu hou paste into a combined sauce.
3. Place the lamb spine in a pot of cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, add an appropriate amount of vinegar, and continuously skim off the foam until the broth becomes clear.
**Note**: Must start with cold water to fully force out the blood foam; vinegar evaporates with boiling water to remove gamey and fishy odors.
4. Remove the lamb spine and rinse thoroughly with hot water to clean off any foam and impurities on the surface, ensuring the bones and meat are clean.
5. Place the lamb spine into a rice cooker or pressure cooker, add mineral water to cover the ingredients, and put in all the spices.
**Key point**: If using a pressure cooker, cook for 30 minutes until the meat falls off the bone.
6. Pour oil into a wok, add scallion sections and ginger slices before the oil heats up, and sauté over low heat until golden brown and fragrant.
**Note**: The oil temperature must be low to avoid burning the scallions and ginger. Then pour in the combined sauce, stir-fry briefly until the sauce aroma releases, again taking care not to burn it.
7. Add mineral water to the stir-fried sauce and bring to a boil, then strain the sauce through a strainer into the pot with the partially cooked lamb spine, removing the residue to keep the broth clean.
8. Continue to simmer over low heat for 20 minutes, allowing the lamb spine to fully absorb the complex flavors of the sauce, then serve.

## Health Advice
This sauce-braised lamb spine is rich in protein and calcium, but has high purine and sodium content. It is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables. People with high uric acid or high blood pressure should control their portion size. The lamb spine braised until the meat falls off the bone has a rich sauce aroma, making it suitable for warming the body and supplementing yang in cold weather.
