# Braised Lion's Head Meatballs
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork belly or pork shoulder 750g (separate fat and lean, recommended ratio 30% fat to 70% lean)  
🥬 Water chestnuts 130g (peeled, about 7 pieces)  
🧅 Scallion 10g (for scallion-ginger water) + about 30g (for braising)  
🫚 Ginger 10g (for scallion-ginger water) + about 20g (for braising)  
🥚 Egg 1 piece  
🥬 Baby bok choy about 10 pieces  

💧 Water 50g (for scallion-ginger water)  
💧 Water 30g (filtered and added to meat filling)  
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.5g  
🍬 White sugar 4g  
🧂 Salt 4g (for meat filling)  
🌽 Cornstarch 20g (about two heaping tablespoons)  
💧 Water as needed (for blanching greens)  
🧂 Salt about 3g (for blanching greens)  
🍃 Bay leaves 2 pieces  
⭐ Star anise 1 piece (about 2g)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine or huadiao wine 30g (about three tablespoons)  
🍯 Light soy sauce 50g (about five tablespoons)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (about one full tablespoon)  
🍬 Rock sugar 15g (or white sugar 1.5 tablespoons)  
🧂 Salt 5g (for braising)  
🥔 Potato starch 10g (for thickening)  
💧 Water 50g (mixed with starch to make a slurry)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Meat grinder  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥣 Small bowl  
🧴 Strainer  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥄 Spoon  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍲 Stockpot  
🍽️ Plate  
🧴 Fine mesh strainer  
🍲 Small pot (Japanese milk pot)  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Selecting meat and grinding**
   Separate fat and lean meat. Grind fat into small cubes, grind lean meat into a grainy paste, chop water chestnuts into pomegranate seed-sized pieces. Process separately, do not mix while grinding.
2. **Making scallion-ginger water**
   Flatten 10g scallion segments and 10g ginger slices, add 50g water and squeeze vigorously to extract juice, strain and reserve about 30g of scallion-ginger water.
3. **Preparing meat filling**
   - **Stage one**: Add white pepper powder, white sugar, and salt to the fat and lean meat mixture. Mix evenly first, then repeatedly press firmly using a "kneading" technique until the filling becomes sticky, cohesive, and forms strings.
   - **Stage two**: Add the egg, break it up first, then quickly stir in one direction until the egg liquid is fully absorbed and the filling is sticky and resistant.
   - **Stage three**: Add cornstarch and 30g scallion-ginger water, mix well, then continue stirring vigorously in the same direction until the filling becomes extremely sticky again.
   - **Stage four**: Add the chopped water chestnuts, mix evenly.
**Note**: The order of adding ingredients must not be reversed; after each addition of liquid or powder, stir thoroughly until cohesive. This is key for the final product to hold its shape without falling apart and to have a springy texture.

4. **Forming the lion's head meatball bases**
   Divide the filling into four equal portions. Take one portion and gently toss it between your hands about ten times to release air, then shape it into a smooth, round large meatball using your thumb and index finger.
**Note**: Tossing makes the meatball firm, but the motion should be gentle.

5. **Deep-frying to set shape**
   Pour enough oil into a wok, heat to 60% hot (about 180°C/350°F). Gently add the meatballs and fry over high heat. Do not stir for the first minute after adding. Once the bottom is set and color changes, flip them. Fry for about another minute. Once the surface is set and color changes, remove. Total frying time is about 2 minutes, only for setting the shape.
**Note**: Oil temperature must be high. Do not move them after adding to prevent them from falling apart.

6. **Braising in a clay pot**
   Place the fried meatballs in a clay pot. Add the remaining scallion segments, ginger slices, bay leaves, star anise, then add Shaoxing wine, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, rock sugar, and salt. Add enough hot water at once. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer slowly.
**Note**: Must add enough hot water at once. Try not to add water midway. The heat must be very low.

7. **Midway maintenance**
   After 1 hour of simmering, open the lid, carefully turn the meatballs over, and check the liquid level. If significantly reduced, add boiling water until the meatballs are 2/3 submerged. Continue simmering on the lowest heat for another 1 hour.
**Note**: The water added must be boiling water.

8. **Preparing the side greens**
   In a separate pot, bring water to a boil with about 3g salt. Blanch the baby bok choy for about 30 seconds, remove, rinse with cold water, squeeze dry, and arrange around the serving plate.
9. **Plating**
   After 2 hours total braising time, turn off the heat. Use a spoon to carefully lift out the meatballs. Place them in the center of the plate surrounded by the bok choy.
10. **Making and pouring the sauce**
    Take about 1/3 of the braising liquid, strain through a fine mesh strainer to remove solids into a clean pot, and bring to a boil. Mix potato starch with 50g water to make a slurry. Add the slurry to the pot in small batches while stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy. Turn off the heat. Evenly pour the sauce over the meatballs and greens.
**Note**: Stir constantly while thickening to prevent lumps. The ideal consistency is thick enough to coat a spoon but flows smoothly.

## Health Advice
Braised Lion's Head Meatballs are rich in high-quality protein and fat, suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or needing to gain weight, but those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should consume sparingly. Water chestnuts help cut the richness, but the deep-frying step adds saturated fat; an air fryer can be used to reduce calories.
