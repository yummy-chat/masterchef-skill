# Torn Cabbage
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Cabbage 2 heads (use only the leaves, remove outer leaves and hard stems)
🥩 Pork belly适量 (half fat and half lean, sliced)
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (crushed)
🌶️ Dried red chili peppers 1 small handful
🫒 Cooking oil适量
🍶 Cooking wine适量
🧂 Salt适量
🍬 Sugar适量
🦪 Oyster sauce a little
🧂 Soy sauce适量
🍇 Fragrant vinegar适量 (added before removing from heat)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the cabbage: Remove the outer dirty leaves, tear the leaves by hand, and discard the hard stems in the middle.
2. Prepare the auxiliary ingredients: Slice the pork belly thinly, crush the garlic, and cut the dried red chili peppers into segments or leave them whole.
3. Dry-fry the cabbage: Heat the wok without oil, add the cabbage leaves, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until they soften, darken in color, and are about 70% cooked. Remove and set aside.
   **Key**: Dry-frying removes moisture from the cabbage and enhances its aroma. In professional kitchens, the cabbage is usually not washed to avoid residual water affecting the flavor.
4. Stir-fry the pork belly: Add oil to the wok, put in the pork belly slices, drizzle in cooking wine, and stir-fry over low heat until the edges of the meat curl slightly and turn golden brown, and the lard is rendered.
   **Key**: Be patient in rendering the lard to make the dish more fragrant.
5. Infuse the aromatics: Add the crushed garlic and dried red chili peppers, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Be careful not to burn them, as this would impart a bitter taste.
6. Mix and season: Turn the heat to high, add the dry-fried cabbage and stir-fry quickly. Add salt, sugar, and oyster sauce. Drizzle soy sauce along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry to combine the seasonings.
   **Key**: Drizzle the soy sauce along the edge of the wok to use the wok's heat to enhance the sauce's aroma.
7. Add vinegar and finish: Over high heat, drizzle fragrant vinegar along the edge of the wok. Quickly stir-fry until you smell the vinegar's aroma, then immediately remove from the heat.
   **Key**: Vinegar is the soul of the dish; it must be added last. The high heat releases the vinegar's fragrance, and you should quickly remove it from the heat to preserve the flavor.

## Health Advice
This torn cabbage dish is stir-fried with lard rendered from pork belly, providing both the vitamins of the vegetable and an appropriate amount of high-quality fat. It is suitable as a daily side dish with rice. However, the amount of pork belly should be controlled, and people with high blood pressure should reduce the addition of salt and soy sauce.
