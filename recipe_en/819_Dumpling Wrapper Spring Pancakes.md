# Dumpling Wrapper Spring Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 Dumpling wrappers, as needed (leftover dumpling wrappers)
🥬 Chinese chives, as needed (cut into inch-long sections, separate stems and leaves)
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed
🍜 Glass noodles, as needed
🥚 3 eggs (beaten with a pinch of salt)
🧅 Scallion, small amount (shredded)
🫚 Ginger, small amount (sliced)
🌾 Flour, small amount (for anti-stick use)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪓 Cutting board
🔪 Knife
🖌️ Oil brush
🫓 Rolling pin
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Take one dumpling wrapper, evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on its surface, then stack another dumpling wrapper on top, brush with oil as well, and continue stacking in turn to form a pile. Using a rolling pin, press from the center outward to roll it into a large, thin round pancake.
   **Key point**: Be sure to brush oil between each layer to prevent sticking during steaming; the thinner you roll it, the better, as it will become translucent after steaming.
2. Fill the steamer with water and bring to a boil. Place the rolled-out thin pancake inside, cover, and steam over high heat for about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Make sure the water is boiling and steam is rising before adding the pancake.
3. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them, adding a pinch of salt and stirring well. Heat the wok over high heat, pour in oil, then add the egg mixture and spread it into a thin omelet. Remove and set aside.
4. Pour oil into the wok, add the ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the bean sprouts and pre-soaked glass noodles, and stir-fry quickly over high heat. Season with salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper powder. Then add the chive stems and stir-fry a few times. Finally, add the chive leaves and scallion shreds, sprinkle in chicken bouillon, and stir-fry quickly until the chive leaves become soft, then immediately remove from the wok.
   **Note**: Stir-fry over high heat the entire time; do not cook the chive leaves for too long—they are done as soon as they soften.
5. Take one steamed thin pancake, lay a piece of egg pancake on it, place an appropriate amount of the stir-fried mixed vegetables on top, roll it up tightly, and enjoy.

## Health Advice
These dumpling wrapper spring pancakes make use of leftover dumpling wrappers, are rich in vegetable fiber and protein, and are suitable for cleansing the intestines and reducing fat in spring. When filling the pancakes, it is recommended to use less oil and salt. The combination of chives and bean sprouts aids digestion. Be mindful of portion sizes.
