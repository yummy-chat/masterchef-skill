# Braised Pork Ribs with Tomato
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork ribs 2 jin (approx. 1 kg)  
🍅 Tomatoes 4  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (cut into shreds)  
🌶️ Dried chilies a few  
🍃 Bay leaves 2  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (for garnish)  
🧂 Cooking wine 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1/2 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1/2 tablespoon  
🧂 Five-spice powder, to taste  
🫙 Chu hou paste 2 tablespoons  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🍲 Rice cooker  
🥄 Wooden spoon/chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Soak the pork ribs in clean water for 30 minutes to fully remove blood.
   **Note**: Soaking effectively removes gamey smell and makes the meat cleaner.
2. Peel the tomatoes: Score a cross on the top, blanch with boiling water, then peel off the skin, and cut into rolling-cut pieces.
   **Note**: Scoring and blanching are key for quick peeling; rolling-cut pieces are convenient for stewing to release flavor.
3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, mix cooking wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, five-spice powder, chu hou paste, and white sugar. Stir until well combined.
   **Note**: Mixing the sauce in advance ensures a balance of savory and sauce flavors.
4. Cut the ginger into shreds. Drain the soaked ribs, add the ginger shreds and the sauce, mix well, and marinate for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: The marinating time must be sufficient to allow the ribs to fully absorb the flavors.
5. Spread the tomato pieces evenly on the bottom of the rice cooker inner pot.
   **Note**: The tomatoes at the bottom prevent sticking, and when heated, they naturally release juice, forming a rich broth base.
6. Pour the marinated ribs along with the sauce and ginger shreds into the pot, arranging them on top of the tomatoes.
7. Add the bay leaves and dried chilies.
8. Close the rice cooker lid, press the "Cook" button, and cook for about 45 minutes.
   **Note**: This uses the cooking program for waterless stewing; after the time is up, the ribs should be tender and the broth should be thick.
9. Open the lid, serve, and sprinkle with scallions for garnish.

## Health Advice  
This braised pork ribs with tomato dish is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, suitable for muscle building, calcium supplementation, and antioxidant benefits. However, the chu hou paste has a fairly high sugar and salt content, so it is recommended to control the intake of the broth. The waterless stewing in a rice cooker locks in nutrients, especially suitable for busy cooks.
