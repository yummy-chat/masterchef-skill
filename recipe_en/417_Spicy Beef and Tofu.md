# Spicy Beef and Tofu
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Dried chilies, large amount (for making coarse chili paste)
🧄 Ginger, to taste
🧄 Garlic, to taste
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)
🥩 Steak, about 1 whole piece (cut into cubes)
🧈 Silken tofu, 1 box
🌿 Cilantro, a handful (chopped)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (for making ground Sichuan pepper)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), small amount
🌶️ Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste), to taste
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for marinating beef)
🧂 Cornstarch, to taste (mixed with water to make slurry, used in batches)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🫗 Colander
🍲 Stockpot
🫙 Immersion blender (with blending cup)
🍳 Frying pan
🥘 Dutch oven
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🧂 Mortar and pestle
🫖 Electric kettle

## Cooking Steps
1.  Wash dried chilies, place in a stockpot with water, and boil until completely soft, about half an hour. Drain well.
**Key point**: Chilies must be boiled until fully softened.
2.  Place the softened chilies together with ginger chunks and garlic cloves into a blender. Blend into a coarse chili paste.
**Key point**: Keep some texture; do not over-blend into a fine puree.
3.  Heat cooking oil in a Dutch oven. Add the chili paste and stir-fry over low heat, stirring constantly, until the moisture reduces and red oil separates. Splash in a small amount of baijiu, stir to combine, then turn off the heat. This is the homemade coarse chili paste. Set aside the amount needed for this dish; store the remainder in a sealed container in the refrigerator.
**Key point**: Stir-fry patiently; adding baijiu before finishing helps extend shelf life; the storage container must be clean and dry.
4.  Toast Sichuan peppercorns in a frying pan over low heat until fragrant. Transfer to a mortar and grind into ground Sichuan pepper. Set aside.
5.  Cut the steak into cubes, add a pinch of salt, and mix well to marinate briefly. Cut the silken tofu into blocks directly from the box, handling gently to prevent breakage. Chop the cilantro.
6.  Clean the Dutch oven. Heat oil over medium-high heat, then stir-fry the beef cubes until fully browned and cooked through.
7.  Add one large spoonful of homemade coarse chili paste, one spoonful of Pixian doubanjiang, and scallion sections. Stir-fry until red oil releases and a rich aroma develops.
8.  Gently add the tofu blocks. Carefully stir a little, then pour in boiling water until it roughly reaches the same level as the ingredients. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the tofu to absorb flavor.
9.  Add the cornstarch slurry in 2-3 batches, gently pushing the ingredients after each addition. Continue until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy.
**Key point**: Adding the slurry in batches allows better control over the thickness.
10. Before turning off the heat, sprinkle in the chopped cilantro and a generous amount of homemade ground Sichuan pepper. Gently mix and remove from heat.
**Key point**: Add the ground Sichuan pepper at the very end to preserve its numbing aroma.

## Health Tips
This spicy beef and tofu dish is rich in protein and calcium, making it suitable for those needing to supplement iron or warm up during cold seasons. Chilies and Sichuan peppercorns can be harsh on the stomach; those with digestive discomfort should consume in moderation.
