# Simplified Caramelized Sugar  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍬 Rock Sugar (recommended)  
🍬 Granulated Sugar  
🍬 Caster Sugar  
🧂 Cooking Oil  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Stirring Tool  

## Steps  
1. Pour 100ml of water into the wok and bring to a boil.  
2. Immediately add 100ml of cooking oil, ensuring the interval is as short as possible, and heat over high or medium-high heat.  
3. Add the rock sugar.  
4. Reduce the heat to medium.  
5. Stir continuously until the syrup turns a brownish-red color, then reduce to low heat.  
6. Continue stirring until the syrup thins and changes from a reddish-brown tea color to a dark red, with fine bubbles appearing.  
7. The fine bubbles gradually disappear, replaced by larger bubbles. When large bubbles first appear, the caramelization is complete.  
8. Quickly proceed to the next step: either directly add 400ml of boiling water to stop the cooking, or add scallions, ginger, garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, and other seasonings for stir-frying.  

**Key Notes**:  
- Do not let the heat become too high throughout the caramelizing process; excessive heat will make it bitter, while insufficient heat will make it too sweet.  
- Stir continuously to prevent the sugar from burning and sticking to the wok.  
- Act quickly when large bubbles appear, and have the subsequent ingredients ready in advance.  
- Induction cooktops may not reach sufficient heat, which can affect the result.  

## Health Advice  
Caramelized sugar is the soul of braised dishes, but sugar caramelizes rapidly at high temperatures. Be mindful of heat control to avoid a bitter taste. Suitable for those who want to enhance the color and aroma of their dishes, but people with diabetes or those controlling sugar intake should consume sparingly.
