# Chicken Hotpot with Five-Leaf Fig Root and Herbs
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🐓 Chicken, appropriate amount (fresh live chicken recommended, choose one with thick and sturdy legs)
🌿 Five-leaf fig root (Wuzhimaotao) 20g
🌰 Lily bulb 6g
🍂 Dried tangerine peel (Guipi) 2g
🔴 Goji berries 10g
🔴 Red dates 10g
🍒 Longan meat 5g
🥜 Dried figs 10g
🍀 Astragalus root (Huangqi) 8g
🌱 Codonopsis root (Dangshen) 8g
🥔 Chinese yam 10g
🫚 Ginger 20g (for the broth) + 100g (for the dipping sauce, peeled and grated)
🧅 Scallion white 20g
💧 Water 2500ml

**Dipping Sauce Seasoning**
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning the broth)
🧅 Scallion 20g (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic 10g (finely chopped)
🌶️ Fresh red chili (Xiaomila) 10g (sliced into rings)
🧂 Chicken powder 3g
🥢 Seafood soy sauce 30g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Clay pot
🔥 Portable gas stove
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Soup ladle, slotted spoon
🍽️ Bowls and plates
🫗 Pot lid

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare herbs and dipping sauce**: Weigh all the herbs and set aside. Finely chop the scallion. Peel and grate 100g ginger into a paste. Finely chop the garlic. Slice the fresh red chili into rings. In a bowl, combine the ginger paste, minced garlic, chili rings, and chopped scallion. Add 3g chicken powder and 30g seafood soy sauce, and mix well to form the dipping sauce.
2. **Cook the herbal broth**: Pour 2500ml water into the clay pot. Add all the weighed herbs, 20g ginger, and 20g scallion white. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and continue boiling for 5 minutes to fully release the herbal aroma.
3. **Submerge the chicken**: Add the chicken pieces into the boiling broth. Once the broth returns to a boil, immediately skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Then turn off the heat, cover with a lid, and let it steep for about 10 minutes.
- **Key to perfection**: The chicken is perfectly cooked when the meat near the bone still shows a slight trace of pink, achieving a tender and succulent texture.

4. **Season and serve**: Remove the lid, season with salt to taste, and serve the broth and chicken together. Enjoy with the prepared dipping sauce.
- **Note**: Only salt is needed for the broth; the herbal aroma and chicken freshness are already rich enough.

## Health Tips
This chicken hotpot with five-leaf fig root and herbs combines nourishing herbal properties with fresh ingredients. It is suitable for people with qi and blood deficiency or weak spleen and stomach throughout the year. For the best tenderness, choose fresh live chicken. The ginger paste in the dipping sauce helps neutralize the cold nature of the dish, but those with high blood pressure should control their salt intake.
