# Braised Pork Belly (Bazi Pork)
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork belly 1000g (lower belly, skin on, cut into thick slices slightly wider than chopsticks)
🥚 Eggs 6 (pre-boiled and peeled)
🌶️ Thin-skinned green chili peppers 250g (can substitute with screw peppers, wash and dry, flatten with knife blade, keep stems intact)
🧅 Scallion 25g (cut into segments)
🫚 Ginger 20g (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 20g (about 4 cloves, flattened)
🍂 Bay leaves 2 pieces
🍂 Star anise 2 pieces
🍂 Sichuan peppercorns about 10 grains
🍂 Baizhi (Angelica dahurica) 1 small piece (optional, about fingernail-sized)
🍂 White cardamom 3 grains (optional)
🍬 Rock sugar 15g (about two lumps)
🧂 Light soy sauce 30g (about 3 tablespoons)
🧴 Dark soy sauce 10g (about 1 heaping tablespoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (about 1 heaping tablespoon)
💧 Boiling water as needed (to cover all ingredients)
🧂 Salt 2-3g (for final seasoning)
🧂 MSG 3g (for final seasoning, adjust to taste)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch (to scorch pork skin for deodorizing)
🍳 Wok/iron pan (for frying ingredients)
🫕 Clay pot (for braising)
🔪 Chef's knife
🥄 Spatula
🔗 Food tongs
🧻 Kitchen paper
🥄 Spoons (for adding seasonings and skimming fat)
🪵 Wooden lid (to prevent oil splatter)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the pork belly**: Use a blowtorch to scorch the pork skin until blackened. Soak in water for about 2 minutes, then scrape clean with a knife. Pat dry with kitchen paper. Cut into thick slices slightly wider than chopsticks.
**Key point**: Scorching the skin effectively removes odor; drying thoroughly prevents oil splatter during frying; do not remove the skin.

2. **Prepare side ingredients**: Boil and peel the eggs in advance; wash and dry the green chili peppers, gently flatten them with the flat side of a knife, keeping stems intact.

3. **Fry the pork belly**: Lightly coat the wok with a thin layer of oil. Heat over high heat, then add the pork belly slices. Reduce to medium-low heat and fry slowly until both sides are slightly golden. The lean parts should not become hard. Remove and set aside in a clay pot, leaving the rendered pork fat in the wok.
**Note**: Avoid dry-frying without oil, as this will make the meat tough.

4. **Make tiger-skin eggs**: Using the pork fat in the wok, add the boiled eggs over the lowest heat. When the sizzling sound diminishes, flip the eggs. Turn to medium-low heat and fry until the surface forms a golden tiger skin pattern. Set aside.
**Key point**: Watch out for splattering; adjust heat from very low to medium-low gradually.

5. **Fry the chili peppers**: Add the flattened chili peppers to the wok over the lowest heat. When the sizzling sound reduces, turn to medium-low heat and fry until the peppers soften and show a tiger skin pattern on the surface. Set aside.
**Key point**: The peppers must be completely dried of surface moisture.

6. **Braise the pork belly**: Place the fried pork belly in the clay pot. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic, bay leaves, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, baizhi, white cardamom, and rock sugar. Pour in light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Add boiling water to cover all ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover and reduce to the lowest heat. Braise for 1 hour.

7. **Add the eggs**: After 1 hour, add the fried tiger-skin eggs. Cover and continue braising over the lowest heat for 40 minutes.

8. **Skim fat and add chili peppers**: After braising, remove the spices from the pot. Use a spoon to skim off the surface fat. Add the fried chili peppers, cover, and braise for another 15 minutes.
**Key point**: Skimming off the fat is crucial for making the fatty meat non-greasy.

9. **Final seasoning**: Open the lid. Adjust saltiness by adding salt and MSG to taste. Gently stir to combine, then turn off the heat.
**Note**: The broth already has some saltiness from the sauces, so add salt sparingly.

## Health Advice
Bazi Pork is high in fat. It is recommended to pair with coarse grains and vegetables, and to control the serving size. Individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of the amounts of light soy sauce and salt used.
