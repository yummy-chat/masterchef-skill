# Steamed Chicken with Dried Mushrooms
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs, about 3-4 pieces (boneless, skin can be kept)  
🍄 Dried mushrooms, about 10-15 pieces (fully soaked and cleaned)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🍶 Yellow wine, to taste  
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste (added in batches)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
⚪ White pepper powder, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🌽 Cornstarch, to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef’s knife  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Strainer/skimmer  
🥄 Soup spoon  

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the bones from the chicken thighs, discard any small bone fragments, keep or discard the skin as desired, then cut into pieces about 2-3 cm.
**Note**: Debone thoroughly; keeping the skin adds aroma and a smoother texture.
2. Place the chicken pieces in a bowl. Add yellow wine, light soy sauce, a little dark soy sauce, salt, white pepper powder, sugar, and cornstarch in sequence. Mix thoroughly by hand, then marinate for 15-20 minutes.
**Note**: Use only a small amount of cornstarch to lightly coat the chicken; mix until the chicken becomes sticky and fully absorbs the marinade.
3. Soak the dried mushrooms in water until fully rehydrated. Remove and place in a bowl. Add one spoonful of cornstarch and a pinch of salt, rub and wash repeatedly, then rinse clean. Repeat 1-2 times.
**Key**: The starch and salt effectively absorb sand trapped in the mushroom folds. Clean thoroughly.
4. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the cleaned mushrooms and blanch for about 1 minute. Remove, drain, and slice into thick pieces.
**Note**: Do not blanch for too long to maintain the tender texture of the mushrooms.
5. Add the sliced mushrooms to the marinated chicken. Drizzle in sesame oil and gently mix.
6. Spread the chicken and mushroom mixture evenly in a deep dish. Do not pile too thickly.
**Note**: The dish should have some depth to hold the juices released during steaming.
7. Fill the steamer with water and bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Place the dish inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 20 minutes.
**Key**: Ensure the water is fully boiling before placing the dish in. High heat quickly cooks the dish, sealing in the juices.
8. Remove and serve. Garnish with chopped scallions as desired.

## Health Tips
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. Dried mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for those aiming to lose fat or build muscle. Steaming preserves nutrients as much as possible. However, control the amount of dark soy sauce and salt to avoid excessive sodium intake.
