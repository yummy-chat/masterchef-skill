# Compound Scallion Oil
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Small scallions 160g (tied into bundles)
🧅 Large leek 50g (cut into sections)
🧅 Onion 30g (shredded)
🌿 Cilantro 15g (arranged in bunches)
🫚 Ginger 20g (sliced)
🥕 Radish slices 20g (for absorbing impurities)
🫘 Cumin 2g
🍃 Bay leaf 1g
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn 1g
🫒 Soybean oil/salad oil 500g (use clear oil)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥣 Porcelain bowl (with lid)
🥄 Strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. Tie the small scallions into bundles, cut the large leek into sections, shred the onion, slice the ginger, slice the radish, arrange the cilantro in bunches, and place the cumin, bay leaf, and Sichuan peppercorn on separate plates for later use.
2. Pour 500g of clear soybean oil or salad oil into a wok. Keep the oil at room temperature and do not turn on the heat yet.
3. Place all prepared fresh ingredients and spices into the cold oil in the wok. Turn the heat to the lowest setting and heat slowly.
   **Key**: The ingredients must be added to cold oil. The slow temperature increase allows the flavors to fully release and prevents the outside from burning while the inside remains raw.
4. Gently stir with a wooden spatula to ensure the oil temperature evenly affects the ingredients.
   **Heat and state judgment**: Maintain the lowest heat throughout; dense small bubbles will appear in the oil, the ingredients will gradually turn yellow, and the aroma will intensify.
5. Once the ingredients are fried until dry, golden brown, and their interiors turn white, immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key**: Time the turn-off correctly, as the residual heat will continue to infuse the ingredients after removing from the heat, preventing them from burning and becoming bitter.
6. Pour the scallion oil along with the ingredients into a porcelain bowl. Immediately cover tightly with a lid and let it steep until naturally cooled.
   **Key**: Covering and steeping allows the flavors to fully dissolve into the oil while preventing the ingredients from burning due to high heat.
7. After completely cooling, remove the lid. Use a strainer to filter out the solid residue, yielding a clear compound scallion oil final product.
   **Key**: Filter only after cooling to achieve a clearer oil.

## Health Advice
Compound scallion oil is rich in antioxidants from various spices, making it suitable for daily use to enhance freshness and aroma. However, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to limit each use to one tablespoon. Slow frying over the lowest heat preserves more flavor and reduces the formation of harmful substances.
