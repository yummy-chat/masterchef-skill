# Dumpling Skin Eggplant Meat Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥟 Dumpling skins, as needed (use 5 pieces stacked together)  
🍆 Eggplant, as needed (peeled, sliced into 0.5 cm thick pieces, then diced)  
🥩 Ground meat, as needed (fat-to-lean ratio as preferred)  
🧅 Scallions, chopped, as needed  
🧄 Ginger, minced, as needed  
🫑 Green pepper, diced, as needed  
🥕 Carrot, diced, as needed  
🌾 Flour, 7 grams  
💧 Water, 100 grams (for making batter)  
🫗 Cooking oil, as needed (used in steps for dipping skins, mixing filling, preparing batter, and pan-frying)  
🧂 Salt, 3 grams (for salting eggplant)  
🧂 Soy sauce, as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed  
🌿 Five-spice powder, as needed  
🧂 MSG, a pinch  
🧂 White pepper, as needed  
🌰 Sesame oil, as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🪚 Silicone pastry mat  
🥣 Stainless steel small bowl  
🪖 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer (with parchment paper)  
🔪 Cutting board and chef’s knife  
🍳 Black iron pan or non-stick frying pan  
🍽️ Ceramic plate (for flipping and serving)  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps
1. Make Thin Pancakes
   - Dip each dumpling skin individually into an oil bowl to coat, then stack 5 pieces together. Gently press with your palm.
   - Use a rolling pin to roll the stack into a large thin pancake about the thickness of a coin.
   - Place on a steamer tray lined with parchment paper. Once water boils and steam rises, steam for 8 minutes. Remove and let cool, then peel apart the layers one by one into single thin pancakes.
   **Note**: Ensure thorough oil coating and apply even pressure while rolling for uniform thickness.

2. Prepare the Eggplant
   - Cut the peeled eggplant into 0.5 cm thick slices, then into even small cubes.
   - Add 3 grams of salt, mix well, let sit for 3 minutes, then squeeze out excess moisture.
   **Key**: The eggplant cubes must be thoroughly squeezed dry, or the filling will release water and affect texture.

3. Make the Filling
   - In the squeezed eggplant cubes, add ground meat, chopped scallions, minced ginger, diced green pepper, and diced carrot.
   - First, drizzle in about 5 grams of cooking oil and mix well to lock in vegetable moisture.
   - Then add soy sauce, oyster sauce, five-spice powder, MSG, and white pepper. Finally, drizzle in sesame oil and mix until well combined.

4. Prepare the Batter
   - In a small bowl, combine 7 grams of flour, 100 grams of water, and 3 grams of oil. Stir until smooth with no lumps to form a “net crust batter.”

5. Wrap the Rolls
   - Take one thin pancake, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold the bottom edge over the filling and press firmly, then fold in the left and right sides, and roll tightly from bottom to top into a roll. Repeat until all pancakes and filling are used.

6. Pan-Fry
   - Heat a frying pan over high heat, add oil, and arrange the rolls neatly in the pan.
   - Stir the batter again until smooth, then pour it evenly into the pan, just enough to cover the bottom of the rolls.
   - Immediately cover the pan with a lid, reduce heat to the lowest setting, and cook for about 2 minutes.
   **Key**: Use the lowest heat throughout to prevent the bottom from burning.

7. Serve
   - Once the liquid has evaporated and a golden, crispy lace crust has formed on the bottom, with edges lifting, turn off the heat.
   - Invert a plate over the rolls in the pan, press down on the plate base, and quickly flip the pan so the rolls are transferred intact onto the plate.
   **Key**: The flipping motion must be decisive, using the plate to complete the transfer in one smooth motion.

## Health Suggestions  
Eggplant is rich in fiber, ground meat provides protein, and transforming dumpling skins into thin pancakes reduces carbohydrate density—but the soul of this dish is the oily, crispy crust. Pair it with a plate of cold cucumber salad to balance the calories. Ideal for fitness enthusiasts who want to "disguise" vegetables as meat; be mindful of controlling the amount of cooking oil to avoid excessive calories.
