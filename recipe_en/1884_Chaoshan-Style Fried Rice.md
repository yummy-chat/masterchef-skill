## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Universal Base**
   - Heat oil in a wok over high heat, stir-fry the sliced onion until fragrant, then add the beef cubes and stir-fry until they change color.
- Reduce to low heat, add doubanjiang, huangdoujiang, and chopped chili sauce, stir well, then remove and set aside.
**Note**: The base is the foundation of the fried rice's seasoning; ensure you stir-fry the sauces over low heat to avoid burning.

2. **Prepare the Special Sauce**
   - In another wok, heat oil and stir-fry the minced garlic and shallots until fragrant.
- Add huangdoujiang, an appropriate amount of chopped chili sauce, white sugar, and flavor powder, and stir well to create a compound sauce, then remove and set aside.
**Key**: The sugar helps balance the saltiness of the sauces and enhances the umami.

3. **Stir-Fry the Rice**
   - Heat oil in a wok, scramble the eggs, then add the diced ham sausage and cabbage pieces, stir-frying until fragrant.
- Add the prepared base and special sauce, and stir well with the other ingredients.
- Add the rice, using the back of a spatula to break up clumps, and stir-fry quickly over high heat.
- Season with flavor powder and light soy sauce, add a small amount of dark soy sauce for color, and continue tossing until each grain of rice is distinct.
- Before serving, sprinkle with chopped green onions, add one tablespoon of butter, and stir-fry over high heat until it "sears" and releases its aroma, creating wok hei (the breath of the wok).
**Note**: The butter is the finishing touch; it must be cooked over high heat to release its fragrance, ensuring the rice is dry and aromatic.

## Health Advice
This fried rice has a rich mixture of sauces. Beef and eggs provide high-quality protein, but doubanjiang and huangdoujiang have high salt content. It is recommended to serve with a light vegetable soup. Suitable for those with high physical energy demands or needing an energy boost. Hypertensive patients should control their portion size.
