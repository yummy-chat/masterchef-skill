# Spicy Cabbage Kimchi
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Napa cabbage, about 5 lbs
🥕 White radish, half (julienned)
🧅 Onion, half (minced)
🧄 Garlic, 2 heads (minced)
🫚 Scallions, as needed (julienned)
🌶️ Fine chili powder, half a bowl
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes, as needed
🦐 Dried shrimp, 3 tbsp
🍚 Plain porridge or glutinous rice paste, 1 large bowl
🍬 Sugar, 3 tbsp
🧂 MSG, 2 tbsp
🧂 Salt, 1 tbsp (final seasoning)
🥤 Sprite, as needed
🧂 Sea salt or fine salt, as needed (for salting)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel basin
📦 Plastic food container (at least 2)
🌀 Food processor or blender
🥣 Glass bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the cabbage**: Cut off the root of the napa cabbage, cut it in half lengthwise, then tear it by hand into quarters. Rinse thoroughly and drain. Set aside the green outer leaves for later use.
2. **Prepare the ingredients**: Peel and finely julienne the white radish. Mince the onion and garlic in a food processor. Cut the scallions into thin strips.
3. **Salt the cabbage**: Place the cabbage into a container. Evenly sprinkle sea salt between each leaf layer, coating both the inside and outside. Press down firmly, cover with the lid, and let it salt overnight until the cabbage has completely softened and released a large amount of water.
   - **Key point**: The cabbage must be fully salted, or the final texture will be poor.
4. **Make the seasoning paste**:
   - Add the minced onion and garlic to the julienned radish. Add the fine chili powder and coarse chili flakes, and mix well by hand.
   - Add the scallion strips and plain porridge, and continue mixing well.
   - Add the dried shrimp, sugar, MSG, and 1 tbsp of salt. Mix thoroughly.
   - Finally, add an appropriate amount of Sprite, and stir into a thick paste.
   - **Note**: The paste should not be too thin; adjust saltiness and sweetness to taste if needed. Fermentation relies mainly on the porridge paste.
5. **Rinse the salted cabbage**: Discard the salted water. Rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold running water 2 to 3 times. Then squeeze each cabbage leaf firmly to remove as much water as possible.
   - **Key point**: Be sure to squeeze out all the water, otherwise the final product will be too salty and fermentation will be affected.
6. **Apply the paste and pack**: Wearing disposable gloves, evenly spread the seasoning paste onto each cabbage leaf. Neatly stack the coated cabbage leaves into a clean, airtight container. Cover the surface with the reserved green outer leaves, press down firmly, and seal the lid.
7. **Fermentation**: Let it naturally ferment at room temperature for 2 days, then transfer to the refrigerator. The optimal fermentation period is 7 to 15 days. Once opened, it can be eaten when a sour, aromatic smell is present.
   - **Key point**: Use a well-sealed container for proper fermentation and storage.

## Health Advice
Spicy cabbage kimchi is rich in dietary fiber and fermented probiotics, which help stimulate the appetite and aid digestion. However, its salt and sugar content is relatively high, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to use it as a side dish to replace some stir-fried vegetables, rather than eating it as a snack in large quantities.
