# Tofu Toast
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Silken tofu, about 100g (added to the dough in batches)  
🌾 Bread flour, as needed  
🍚 Tangzhong (tangzhong starter), as needed (prepared the night before)  
🍞 Yeast, as needed  
🍬 Sugar, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🧈 Butter, as needed (softened at room temperature, added after the dough reaches the expansion stage)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Stand mixer  
🫗 Glass square fermentation box  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔪 Dough scraper/bench scraper  
📦 Square or rippled square toast pan  
🔥 Oven (with proofing function)  
🔪 Serrated bread knife  
🧊 Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the tangzhong in advance**: Make the tangzhong the night before. Let it cool, then seal and refrigerate for later use.
2. **Knead the dough**: Add bread flour, yeast, sugar, salt, tangzhong, and most of the silken tofu to the stand mixer bowl. Start mixing on low speed until the ingredients come together, then increase to medium speed and knead until the dough is smooth and has developed some gluten.
3. **Add butter**: Add the softened butter and continue kneading until the dough reaches the full development stage, meaning it can be stretched into a thin, smooth, and even membrane.
4. **First proofing**: Round the dough, place it in the fermentation box, and cover. Let it proof in a warm place until it doubles in volume.
5. **Divide and rest**: Remove the dough, gently press to release gas, and divide it evenly into 4 portions. Shape each portion into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for about 15 minutes.
6. **Shape and place in pan**: Gently press each rested dough ball to release gas again, shape into rounds, and place them seam-side down, side by side, into the toast pan.
7. **Final proofing**: Place the pan in the oven, activate the proofing function, and let it rise until the dough reaches about 80% of the pan's height.
8. **Baking**: Preheat the oven. Bake at 190°C (top and bottom heat) for 20 minutes, then reduce to 180°C and bake for another 8 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the toast rest in the residual oven heat for 5–8 minutes. Actual temperature and time may need adjustment based on your oven and pan.
9. **Cooling**: Immediately after baking, tap the pan to release steam, unmold the toast, and place it on a cooling rack to cool completely to prevent collapsing.

## Health Tips  
This tofu toast uses silken tofu to replace part of the liquid, increasing plant protein and calcium content, making it suitable for those controlling oil intake or building muscle. Note that tofu has a high moisture content, so it should be added gradually during kneading to prevent the dough from becoming too sticky to shape. Be sure to cool the toast completely before slicing to avoid collapsing.
