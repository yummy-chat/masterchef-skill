# Crispy Roasted Pork Belly
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Whole piece of pork belly with skin (choose one with distinct layers and intact skin)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (sliced)
🧅 Scallion 1 section
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 1 small handful
🧂 Salt 2 small handfuls (for blanching) + 2 pinches (for marinating)
🌿 Five-spice powder to taste
🧂 Coarse salt enough to cover the pork skin (must use coarse salt)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🔥 Oven (needs to be preheated)
🔪 Knife
🔨 Meat tenderizer (or fork)
📦 Aluminum foil
🍽️ Wire rack/grill rack
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🥘 Baking sheet

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Trim the pork belly, scrape the skin clean of any impurities. Blanch in cold water, skim off the foam, then add ginger slices, scallion sections, Sichuan peppercorns, and 2 small handfuls of salt.
2. Once the water comes to a boil, turn off the heat, cover, and let it sit for about 1 hour to cook the meat through and infuse flavor.
3. Remove the pork and pat it completely dry with kitchen paper towels.
4. Use a meat tenderizer to poke dense, even holes across the pork skin, deep enough to penetrate the skin layer.
5. Flip the pork over, make several cuts about 2cm deep into the lean side. Sprinkle with 2 pinches of salt and an appropriate amount of five-spice powder, rub in evenly.
6. Flip again so the skin faces up, and wipe the skin surface dry.
### Roasting Phase
1. Wrap the bottom and sides of the pork tightly with aluminum foil to form a foil container slightly taller than the meat, leaving only the skin exposed.
2. Spread a thick layer of coarse salt over the skin surface, completely covering it.
**Key**: The salt layer must be thick enough, otherwise the skin may burn easily and the moisture won't be drawn out properly.
3. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Place the pork on a wire rack and put it in the middle rack. Roast for 1 hour, until the meat is fully cooked.
4. Remove from the oven, carefully remove the foil, discard the solidified salt crust, and wipe the skin clean with kitchen paper towels.
5. Return the pork to the oven, increase the temperature to above 220°C, and turn on the convection fan. Roast for another 30 minutes, until the skin has fully puffed up into a golden, crispy "sesame crust."
6. Once out of the oven, let the meat rest for 30 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute and the skin to cool and become crispier.
7. Cut into pieces and plate. Serve with white sugar or yellow mustard.

## Health Advice
Crispy Roasted Pork Belly is high in fat. It is recommended to serve with plenty of leafy green vegetables and control portion sizes to avoid excess calories. Suitable for those who enjoy a crispy texture, but people with high blood lipids or those on a diet should consume sparingly.
