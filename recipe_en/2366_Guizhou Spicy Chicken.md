# Guizhou Spicy Chicken
Cuisine: Guizhou cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: All seasons 🌍

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Free-range corn-fed chicken 🐓
🥔 Potatoes
🧄 Garlic
🍃 Bay leaves
Ginger
🌱 Garlic sprouts
🍺 Beer
🍚 Fermented rice (jiuzao)
🟤 Dark soy sauce
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns or numbing peppercorns
🌶️ Ci Ba spicy chili (a blend of Huaxi Dangwu chili, Zunyi bullet chili, strip chili, and Dafang wrinkled chili, ground)
🫘 Broad bean paste (Doubanjiang)
🛢️ Cooking oil (rapeseed oil)

## Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Add oil to the wok until it reaches about half the height of the wok. Heat the oil, then add the potatoes cut into strips. Fry until the surface turns golden, then remove and set aside.
2. Heat the oil further until it feels hot to the touch (when you hold your hand above it). Add the cut chicken pieces, along with sliced ginger and Sichuan peppercorns. Fry until the oil changes from cloudy to clear, then remove and set aside.
3. Remove one-third of the oil from the wok, leaving the remaining oil to heat.
4. Add the Ci Ba spicy chili, broad bean paste, and sliced ginger. Stir-fry until the oil turns red, then add the fried chicken pieces and stir-fry evenly.
**Note**: Be mindful of the heat during stir-frying, as the Ci Ba spicy chili can easily burn; keep stirring continuously.

5. Once the chicken pieces are evenly colored, add dark soy sauce. Pour in beer until it covers the chicken pieces, add bay leaves, cover the wok, and braise for 10 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom.
**Key point**: Ensure the amount of beer fully covers the chicken pieces to allow proper braising and flavor absorption.

6. Add the fried potato strips and whole garlic cloves, then braise for another 20 minutes.
**Key point**: Add the potatoes and garlic later to prevent them from becoming overly soft and losing their texture.

7. Finally, add the fermented rice and stir-fry evenly. Add the cut garlic sprouts, stir briefly, then remove from the wok and serve.

## Health Advice
Guizhou Spicy Chicken primarily features chicken and potatoes, making it rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for those needing an energy boost. Be mindful of the amount of cooking oil used, as the frying process increases the calorie content. This dish is spicy and robust, so individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation.
