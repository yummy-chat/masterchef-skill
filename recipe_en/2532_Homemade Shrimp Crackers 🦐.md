# Homemade Shrimp Crackers 🦐
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦐 Fresh shrimp 400g (approximately 190g after peeling)
🥔 Potato starch 420g (can substitute with tapioca starch; adjust amount based on shrimp moisture)
💧 Water 150ml
🫚 Ginger 3 slices
🧂 Sugar 2 tablespoons
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Blender/Food processor
🥣 Glass bowl
🪓 Cutting board and knife
🥘 Steamer
🔗 Metal drying rack
🍳 Small saucepan
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Instructions
1. Remove the heads and shells from the fresh shrimp, devein, wash, and drain. Yield approximately 190g of clean shrimp meat.
   **Key point**: The shrimp must be thoroughly cleaned, with no residual impurities.

2. Place the clean shrimp meat, water, sugar, salt, and ginger slices into the blender. Blend until a fine shrimp paste forms. If the mixture is too thick, pause stirring and continue.
   **Key point**: Blend until completely smooth with no lumps to ensure an even texture.

3. Transfer the shrimp paste to a glass bowl. Gradually add the potato starch, mixing well after each addition, and knead into a slightly firm and smooth dough. The amount of starch can be adjusted flexibly based on the shrimp's moisture content—the dough should hold together and be slightly firm.

4. Knead the dough on a work surface until smooth, roll it into a long log, and divide into 6 equal portions. Bring water in a steamer to a boil, line it with a steaming cloth, place the shrimp dough pieces on it, and steam over medium heat for 1 hour until fully cooked through.
   **Key point**: Ensure sufficient steaming time for the center to be thoroughly cooked.

5. Remove the steamed shrimp cake and place it on a ventilated drying rack to air-dry for approximately 12 hours, until the surface is dry enough for slicing, while the interior remains slightly soft. Avoid over-drying until it becomes too hard, as this will make it difficult to slice thinly.

6. Slice the dried shrimp cake into the thinnest possible pieces. Spread evenly on the drying rack and dry again until the shrimp slices become hard and dry, making a crisp sound when handled. The thinner the slices, the better the puffing effect when fried. Alternatively, use an oven's dehydrating function to bake for about 2 hours as a substitute for air-drying.
   **Key point**: The thickness of the slices directly affects puffing—be sure to slice very thinly.

7. Pour enough oil into a pot and heat over high heat until about 80% hot. Reduce to low heat, add the shrimp slices, and fry quickly at high temperature until they puff up and float immediately. Remove and drain the oil promptly to prevent burning. The oil temperature must be high enough; otherwise, the shrimp slices will not puff fully.
   **Note**: Oil temperature is crucial—if too low, the shrimp slices will not puff.

8. Store any unfried dried shrimp slices in a sealed bag in the refrigerator. Take out and fry as needed.

## Health Advice
This homemade shrimp cracker is made with fresh shrimp and potato starch, rich in protein, and can be enjoyed as an occasional snack. However, since it is deep-fried, it is high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Individuals with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
