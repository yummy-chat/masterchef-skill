# Multigrain Vegetable Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Spinach 500g (can be substituted with other small greens, trim roots and wash)  
🥕 Carrot 130g (half a carrot, peeled and grated into thin shreds)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms 100g (about 4 large ones, remove stems and wash)  
🥚 Eggs 2  
🍤 Dried small shrimp 15g  
🌽 Cornmeal 80g (fine cornmeal, about 8 level teaspoons)  
🧅 Scallion 20g (one stalk, chopped)  
🥔 Potato starch 15g (to add stickiness to the filling, can substitute with sweet potato starch)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for greasing the pan and stir-frying)  
🧂 Baking soda about 3g (to keep spinach vibrant green when blanching)  

🧂 Salt about 3g (one level teaspoon)  
🍬 Sugar about 5g (one heaping teaspoon)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon/MSG about 3-5g (one heaping teaspoon)  
🦪 Oyster sauce about 10g (half a tablespoon)  
🫒 Sesame oil about 10g (one tablespoon)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or iron pan  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🧇 Grater  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍽️ Steamer basket (bamboo)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale (optional)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Trim the roots and any wilted leaves from the spinach, wash and drain thoroughly. Peel the carrot and grate into fine shreds. Wash the shiitake mushrooms and remove the stems.  
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk with chopsticks until evenly combined.  
3. Heat the wok over high heat, spray in a thin layer of oil. Once the oil starts to smoke, immediately turn off the heat, add cool oil, then slowly pour in the beaten eggs while continuously stirring with chopsticks. After all the egg is in the wok, turn the heat back to high and keep stirring until the eggs break into fine, uniform crumbles.  
4. Add the dried shrimp and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant. Turn off the heat, remove from the wok, and let cool.  
5. In the same wok, bring cold water to a boil. First add the shiitake mushrooms, cook over medium-low heat for 5 minutes, then remove. Turn the heat to high and bring the water back to a boil. Add the carrot shreds and blanch for about 40 seconds, then remove. Add the baking soda, then first blanch the spinach stems, followed by the leaves. Blanch until soft and bright green, then immediately remove.  
6. Immediately rinse the blanched shiitake mushrooms, carrot shreds, and spinach under cold running water. Ensure the spinach is completely cool.  
7. Squeeze the excess water from the shiitake mushrooms, then cut into small dice. Squeeze the carrot shreds dry and roughly chop a few times. Squeeze the spinach into a tight ball, but with a little moisture still remaining when squeezed by hand, then cut into large pieces.  
8. Place the processed spinach, carrots, and shiitake mushrooms into a large bowl. Add the chopped scallion and the cooled egg and dried shrimp mixture. Then add salt, sugar, chicken bouillon, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Mix thoroughly by hand. Then add the potato starch and mix again until evenly combined.  
9. Take portions of the filling and roll them into tight balls, each about 50g.  
10. Prepare a plate of cornmeal. Roll each filling ball in the dry cornmeal, then gently press with your hands to make the flour stick and become moist. Repeat the rolling and pressing process a total of three times. On the final coating, keep a thin layer of dry flour on the surface and roll into a neat ball.  
11. Place the dumplings in a cold steamer basket. Turn the heat to high. Once the water boils and steam rises, maintain high heat and steam for 10 minutes. Immediately turn off the heat and remove the dumplings directly from the steamer.  

## Health Suggestions  
These multigrain vegetable dumplings are rich in dietary fiber and various vitamins. Spinach provides iron, and carrots support eye health. The steaming method is low in oil and healthy, making them suitable for those on a weight-loss or blood sugar control diet. Note that after blanching, the vegetables must be squeezed dry; a filling that is too wet will fall apart. Adding starch helps increase stickiness.
