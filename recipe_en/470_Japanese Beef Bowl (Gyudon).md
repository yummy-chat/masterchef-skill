# Japanese Beef Bowl (Gyudon)
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Sliced beef (hotpot beef or original cut)  
🧅 Onion (white-skinned onion)  
🌱 Scallion (chopped)  
🫘 White sesame seeds  
🍶 Mirin (can be substituted with cooking wine)  
🫗 Soy sauce  
🦪 Oyster sauce  
🍬 Sugar  
🌑 Dark soy sauce (optional)  
💧 Water (or dashi)  
🍷 Sake (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan  
🍳 Wok  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥣 Small bowl  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the outer skin and core of the onion, then cut into crescent-shaped thin slices. Wash the scallion and cut into 0.5 cm long scallion greens. Heat the pan, add white sesame seeds, shake the pan to stir-fry until lightly yellow, then remove and set aside. Blanch the sliced beef in boiling water for 1 minute, then remove and drain.
   **Key Points**: If using high-quality sliced beef, you can skip blanching and directly cook it; stir-fry white sesame seeds until lightly yellow to avoid bitterness.

2. In a small bowl, combine 40 g mirin, 30 g soy sauce, 20 g oyster sauce, 5 g sugar, and 5 g dark soy sauce, and stir into a seasoning sauce. You can add 15 g sake to enhance the flavor. It is recommended to use an electronic scale for precise measurement.
   **Key Points**: Sake is optional; mirin itself has deodorizing properties.

3. Heat oil in a pan, add the onion slices, and stir-fry over high heat until translucent. Reduce to low heat, add 250 g water or dashi, then bring to a boil over high heat and maintain for 3 minutes.

4. Add the blanched beef slices, pour in the prepared seasoning sauce, and stir constantly while cooking for 10 minutes to prevent sticking. After cooking, turn off the heat.
   **Key Points**: Cooking for 10 minutes ensures the beef is fully cooked; taste the soup for saltiness, and if not salty enough, add 1 g of soy sauce each time.

5. Serve the beef over the rice, drizzle with the sauce from the pan, and sprinkle with scallion greens and toasted white sesame seeds.
   **Key Points**: For leftover refrigerated portions, add 50 ml of water to the pan and reheat while stirring, or microwave on high for 2–3 minutes.

## Health Advice
The beef bowl is rich in high-quality protein and easily digestible fats, paired with onions to provide dietary fiber. It is suitable as a work meal or a carbohydrate supplement after exercise. Note that the sugar content in the sauce should be controlled; sugar-sensitive individuals should reduce the amount of sugar or use a sugar substitute.
