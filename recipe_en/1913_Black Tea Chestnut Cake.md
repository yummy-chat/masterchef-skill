# Black Tea Chestnut Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌰 Chestnut kernels 250g (It is recommended to directly purchase peeled and pitted chestnut kernels)  
🍵 Black tea bag 1 pack  
💧 Water appropriate amount (enough to cover the chestnuts)  
🥛 Milk or light cream 40g (about 1 block)  
🧈 Butter or corn oil 30g (about 1 block)  
🍬 Sugar 16g (2 bottle caps, can be omitted if avoiding sugar)  
🍼 Milk powder or condensed milk appropriate amount (optional, to enhance milky flavor)  
🌸 Dried osmanthus flowers appropriate amount (optional, for surface decoration)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🛍️ Ziplock bag  
🪯 Rolling pin  
🌀 Blender or food processor (for machine version)  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
📐 Square mold (or other preferred molds)

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Stage**  
1. Prepare chestnuts: It is recommended to directly use peeled and pitted chestnut kernels. If handling yourself, first remove the outer shell, blanch in boiling water to remove the inner skin, then remove the core.

**Handmade Version**  
2. Place chestnut kernels and the black tea bag together in the rice cooker inner pot. Add enough water to cover the chestnuts, then start the "Cook" program. If there is residual water after the program ends, repeat the "Cook" program until the water is fully absorbed.  
   **Key point**: The goal is to obtain cooked chestnuts with a black tea aroma and no excess moisture.  
3. While hot, use the back of a slotted spoon to mash the cooked chestnuts into a paste. Manual mashing helps retain some granular texture.  
   **Note**: Mash while hot for easier processing; handle gently to avoid damaging the rice cooker inner pot coating.  
4. Add milk or light cream, butter or corn oil, and sugar to the chestnut paste. If you prefer a stronger milky flavor, you can additionally add milk powder or condensed milk.  
   **Key point**: Seasoning can be adjusted according to personal preference.  
5. Press the rice cooker's "Cook" button again, and occasionally stir the chestnut paste with a rice paddle to ensure even heating. Continue stirring for about 5 minutes.  
   **Consistency check**: Stir until the chestnut paste clumps together and no longer sticks to the spatula.  
   **Key point**: The rice cooker heats slowly, so there is no need to stir continuously; turning it over at intervals is sufficient.  
6. Place the stir-fried chestnut paste into a ziplock bag, and use a rolling pin to roll it into a thin sheet approximately 1cm thick.  
   **Key point**: Rolling it inside the bag makes the texture smoother and the shape more uniform.

**Machine Version**  
7. Place chestnut kernels, milk or light cream, butter or corn oil, and a suitable amount of water into the blender. Blend until a smooth, thin paste forms.  
   **Note**: The chestnut paste will be quite thin at this stage.  
8. Pour the blended thin chestnut paste into a non-stick pan, add an appropriate amount of sugar, and stir-fry over heat.  
   **Consistency check**: Stir-fry until the chestnut paste clumps together and no longer sticks to the silicone spatula.  
   **Key point**: The paste made by the machine contains more water, so it must be thoroughly dried by stir-frying.  
9. Place the stir-fried chestnut paste into a bowl, cover it tightly with plastic wrap touching the surface, and allow it to cool. The texture will become firmer after cooling.  
   **Note**: Covering the surface tightly prevents it from drying out.  
10. Divide the cooled chestnut paste into portions, roll them into balls, and press them into shape using your preferred mold.  
    **Note**: The cooled chestnut paste will not stick to your hands, making it easy to press into molds.

## Health Advice
This chestnut cake is rich in dietary fiber and the antioxidant components of black tea, making it suitable for autumn nourishment. However, pay attention to the amount of butter and sugar added. For those on a weight-loss diet, it is advisable to reduce the sugar or omit it. Hand-mashing retains a granular texture for a more natural mouthfeel, but those with weak spleen and stomach should not consume too much.
