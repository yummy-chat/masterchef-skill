# Crispy Fried Pork Strips
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🧂 **Seasonings**
- Salt
- Thirteen Spices
- Ground White Pepper
- MSG
- Chicken Bouillon
- Coarsely Ground Sichuan Peppercorns
- Whole Sichuan Peppercorns
- Light Soy Sauce
- Cooking Wine

🥩 **Ingredients**
- Skinless Pork Belly or Loin (choose fat-to-lean ratio as preferred)
- Eggs
- All-Purpose Flour
- Sweet Potato Starch
- Old Ginger
- Scallions
- Vegetable Oil

🍳 **Utensils**
- Wok or Deep Pot
- Chopsticks
- Slotted Spoon or Spatula
- Plastic Wrap
- Refrigerator

### Preparation Steps

**1. Make Ginger-Scallion Water**
- Julienne the old ginger; keep the scallions whole.
- Place the ginger julienne and whole scallions into a bowl. Add 15g cooking wine and 80g water. Squeeze and rub the mixture with your hand for 5 minutes, until the ginger and scallion flavors are fully infused into the water. Set aside.
**2. Prepare the Pork**
- Use skinless pork. Wash and cut into strips about 8-10 cm long and 1.5 cm thick.
- Add 4g salt, 2g thirteen spices, 3g ground white pepper, 5g MSG, 5g chicken bouillon, 3g coarsely ground Sichuan peppercorns, 3g whole Sichuan peppercorns, and 8g light soy sauce. Pour in the prepared ginger-scallion water. Mix thoroughly and knead for 10 minutes until the strips have absorbed all the liquid and the surface becomes tacky.
- Cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
**3. Coat the Pork**
- Add 30g all-purpose flour and 120g sweet potato starch to the marinated pork. Add the egg whites from 2 eggs. Mix and knead for 15 minutes. Ensure the sweet potato starch is completely dissolved with no dry lumps, otherwise they may spatter during frying.
**4. First Fry for Setting**
- Pour vegetable oil into the wok to a depth of at least 3 cm.
- Heat over high heat to 150°C, then reduce to low heat to maintain the temperature.
- Using chopsticks, pick up a coated pork strip, gently straighten it, and carefully slide it into the oil one by one.
- Fry for 3-5 minutes until the surface is lightly golden and feels slightly firm when touched with a spatula. Remove and drain on paper towels. If unsure about timing, extend frying slightly to avoid raw-tasting meat. Beginners should add strips one by one to prevent sticking.
**5. Second Fry for Finish**
- Increase oil temperature to 180°C. Add the first-fried strips and fry again until golden brown. Remove and drain.
- If making a large batch, you can stop after the first fry and freeze the strips. When ready to serve, directly fry from frozen. The texture will remain nearly as good.

## Health Tips
Crispy Fried Pork Strips are rich in protein and help replenish energy, but they are a deep-fried food with relatively high calories. Pairing with a vegetable salad is recommended to balance nutrition. Pay attention to oil intake; keep the oil temperature not too high during the second fry to minimize the formation of harmful substances.
