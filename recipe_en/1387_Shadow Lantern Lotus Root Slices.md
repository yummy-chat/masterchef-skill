# Shadow Lantern Lotus Root Slices
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Crisp lotus root - appropriate amount (peeled, sliced extremely thin to be translucent)
🌱 Cooked white sesame seeds - appropriate amount
🧂 White vinegar - about 2 tablespoons (for soaking the lotus root slices in water)
🍬 Rock sugar - 80 grams
💧 Hot water - half a bowl (used to rinse the caramelized sugar, must be hot)
🍶 Aromatic vinegar - 30 grams
🌶️ Chili oil - appropriate amount (add according to taste)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking the lotus root slices)
🥢 Bamboo strainer (for removing the lotus root slices)
🧻 Kitchen paper towels (for drying the lotus root slices)
🍳 Wok, stove
🥄 Skimmer / slotted spoon (for lifting and separating the lotus root slices when deep-frying)
🥄 Soup spoon (for stir-frying the sugar syrup and scooping the sauce)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing the lotus root slices)
🍽️ Plate and plastic wrap (for arrangement and decoration)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Peel the lotus root and cut it into extremely thin slices, about the thickness of copperplate paper so they are translucent. Soak the lotus root slices in clean water mixed with about 2 tablespoons of white vinegar for 10 minutes. Remove them and thoroughly dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels.
2.  Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat it to about 30-40% of its maximum temperature. Unfold the lotus root slices one by one and add them to the oil. Gently separate them with the skimmer and fry over medium-low heat for about 1 minute, until the slices are set and slightly yellow. Remove them.
**Key Point**: The oil temperature should not be too high, to prevent the outside from burning before the inside is cooked; the lotus root slices must be unfolded to avoid sticking together.

3.  Increase the oil temperature to about 40-50% of its maximum. Add the set lotus root slices back in and re-fry over medium heat for about 30 seconds, until they turn golden and crispy. Stir quickly during this time to ensure even heating and prevent overlapping. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key Point**: Re-frying forces out excess oil, making the slices crispier; keep stirring throughout to prevent them from stacking and turning black.

4.  Clean the wok, add 80 grams of rock sugar, and stir-fry slowly over low heat until it melts and turns an amber color with small bubbles. Immediately add half a bowl of **hot water** and quickly cover the wok to prevent splashing. Uncover, add 30 grams of aromatic vinegar, and continue to boil until about 70-80% of the water has evaporated and the sugar syrup becomes thick and sticky.
**Key Point**: After the caramelized sugar changes color, add hot water immediately. You must use hot water and be careful to avoid splashes. Control the heat while simmering the syrup to prevent burning.

5.  Add an appropriate amount of chili oil and cooked white sesame seeds to the thick sugar syrup. Stir well to form the sauce. Pour the sauce into a stainless steel bowl and let it cool to about 40-50°C (104-122°F). Then, add the fried lotus root slices and gently toss them with chopsticks to coat evenly.
**Key Point**: The sauce must be cooled before mixing, otherwise the high temperature will soften the crispy lotus root slices. The mixing action should be gentle to prevent the slices from breaking.

6.  Quickly stack the mixed lotus root slices on a plate for serving. You can place a small object wrapped in plastic wrap under the plate as a decoration and to prevent oil from seeping through. Serve immediately to maintain crispness.

## Health Advice
These Shadow Lantern Lotus Root Slices are as thin as cicada wings, crispy and delicious. They are suitable as a snack or appetizer. However, since they are deep-fried and paired with a sugar syrup, the calorie content is relatively high. It is recommended to control the portion size. Lotus root is rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion, and when paired with vinegar and chili oil, it can help stimulate the appetite and cut through grease.
