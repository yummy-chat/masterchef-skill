# Spicy Tofu Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🧊 Dry yeast 3g
🍬 Granulated sugar 3g (for kneading dough, providing nutrients for yeast)
💧 Water 150g
🐷 Lard/vegetable oil 9g (add to dough to make the wrapper more moist and white)
🫘 Firm tofu 400g (firm tofu is preferred as it holds shape well and has a strong bean flavor)
🍜 Sweet potato vermicelli 100g (dry)
🧅 Scallions 100g
🧂 Salt, to taste (for blanching tofu) + 5g (for filling)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a small handful (for blanching tofu to remove odor; boil for 2-3 minutes then remove)
🍬 Sugar 3g (for enhancing flavor in filling)
🥃 Soy sauce 20g (provides savory flavor and color)
🥃 Dark soy sauce 5g (for color)
🥃 Oyster sauce 20g (for umami)
🫒 Rapeseed oil 100g (for frying scallion oil)
🌶️ Chili powder 20g (recommend using a fragrant but not too spicy variety; add after turning off heat to infuse with residual heat)
🌿 Ground Sichuan peppercorn 5g (add after turning off heat to infuse with residual heat)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading dough and mixing filling)
🍳 Wok/soup pot (for blanching tofu, soaking vermicelli, and frying scallion oil)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board (for cutting tofu, vermicelli, and scallions)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing blanched tofu, vermicelli, and Sichuan peppercorns)
📦 Plastic wrap (for covering dough during proofing, preventing dough pieces from drying out)
🥖 Rolling pin (for rolling out bun wrappers)
♨️ Steamer, steaming basket (for steaming buns)
🧤 Disposable gloves (for mixing filling by hand)

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Knead the dough:** Mix all-purpose flour, dry yeast, and granulated sugar. Add water and stir into a flaky mixture, then add lard. Knead into a rough dough.
    **Note:** This dough has a relatively low water content, similar to mantou dough, to ensure the bun wrapper holds its shape well. No baking powder is used, aiming for a chewy wrapper texture.
2.  **Rest and knead the dough:** Cover the rough dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 10 minutes. Then take it out and knead again until the surface is smooth.
    **Key:** After resting, the gluten relaxes, making it easier to knead until smooth.
3.  **Proof the dough:** Cover the smooth dough with plastic wrap and leave it in a warm place until it doubles in volume, about 1–1.5 hours. The interior should have a honeycomb-like structure.
4.  **Prepare the tofu:** Cut the tofu into small cubes. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add Sichuan peppercorns and salt, and boil for 2–3 minutes to release flavor. Remove the peppercorns, add the tofu cubes, and blanch until the water returns to a boil.
    **Purpose:** Effectively removes the beany taste, gives the tofu a base flavor, and makes it more tender and less likely to break apart.
5.  **Prepare the vermicelli:** In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the dry vermicelli, turn off the heat, cover, and let it soak for 20 minutes until soft. Drain and cut into short segments.
    **Note:** The vermicelli does not need prolonged boiling; simply soak it in hot water until it reaches the desired softness.
6.  **Basic seasoning for filling:** Combine the drained, blanched tofu cubes with the cut vermicelli segments. Add salt, sugar, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Mix well.
    **Key:** The tofu may release more water after sitting; drain any excess liquid to keep the filling dry.
7.  **Make the scallion oil:** Heat the rapeseed oil in a pot. Add the chopped scallions and sauté over medium-low heat until they become soft, slightly yellow, and fragrant, but not browned.
8.  **Make the chili oil and combine:** Once the scallion oil is ready, turn off the heat. While the oil is still hot, add the chili powder and ground Sichuan peppercorn, stirring quickly to infuse and release their fragrance and red oil. Pour the finished scallion-chili oil entirely over the tofu and vermicelli filling. Mix thoroughly by hand.
    **Note:** Adding chili powder after turning off the heat prevents it from burning. The hot oil dramatically enhances the filling's aroma.
9.  **Divide the dough:** Take out the proofed dough and roll it into a long log. Divide it into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 15 minutes.
10. **Roll out the wrappers:** Take one dough ball, coat both sides with dry flour, and roll it into a round disc about 14–15 cm in diameter. The edges should be thin, and the center slightly thicker.
11. **Fill and shape:** Take one wrapper and place about 90g of filling in the center. Pleat and seal the bun using your thumb to gently press the filling inward while wrapping.
    **Key:** A generous amount of filling, pressed in firmly, helps the bun stand tall and plump.
12. **Second proofing:** Place the shaped buns on a steamer basket lined with parchment paper. Cover and let them proof for 15–20 minutes, until they have visibly increased in size and feel light to the touch.
13. **Steam:** Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Place the steamer basket on top. Once steam is rising, reduce the heat to medium-low and steam for 8 minutes. Turn off the heat and let them rest covered for 2 minutes before opening the lid.
    **Key:** Start steaming with boiling water. After steam appears, cook over medium-low heat for 8 minutes. The resting period after turning off the heat prevents the buns from collapsing. Adjust timing slightly based on the number and size of buns.

## Health Tips
These spicy tofu buns are rich in plant-based protein and dietary fiber, making them a great option for vegetarians or those trying to lose weight. However, tofu tends to release water, so be sure to drain it thoroughly before preparing the filling.
