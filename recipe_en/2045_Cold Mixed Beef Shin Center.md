# Cold Mixed Beef Shin Center
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank (beef shin center cut), appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, 2 (for tying into knots)
🫚 Ginger, about 5 slices
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, about 2 (sliced into rings)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (julienned)
🫑 Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (julienned)
🫑 Green bell pepper, appropriate amount (julienned)
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (stems finely chopped for the sauce, leaves reserved for mixing)
🍋 Small limes, appropriate amount (for juicing)
🧂 Hua Diao wine, 1 bottle cap
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, about 20 pieces
🍃 Bay leaves, 3-4 pieces
🧂 Salt, 2 grams
🍬 Sugar, 3 grams
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Vinegar, appropriate amount
🫒 Sichuan peppercorn oil, appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili oil, appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🍽️ Stainless steel tray
🍲 Glass pot
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife

## Steps
1. Cut the beef shank into large chunks, soak in clean water for 15 minutes, thoroughly removing blood until the meat looks clean.
   **Key point**: Soaking effectively removes blood, resulting in a clear and bright final color.

2. Prepare the braising aromatics: ginger slices, scallion knots, Sichuan peppercorns, and bay leaves.

3. Prepare the sauce ingredients: minced garlic, finely chopped cilantro stems, bird's eye chili rings. Mix with salt and sugar, stir quickly to release their juices.

4. Prepare the vegetables for mixing: julienne the onion, red and green bell peppers, and set aside the cilantro leaves.

5. Place a bamboo mat at the bottom of the glass pot. Add the beef shank chunks and pour in enough water at once, covering the meat by at least two centimeters. Bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Key point**: Water must be added in one go; avoid adding water midway, as it affects the clarity of the broth.

6. After boiling, skim off any foam from the surface to ensure the broth is clear.

7. Add the Hua Diao wine, ginger slices, scallion knots, Sichuan peppercorns, and bay leaves. Reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer for two and a half hours.
   **Key point**: Maintain a gentle simmer throughout; slow cooking makes the meat tender without drying out.

8. Insert a chopstick into the beef shank; if it goes in without resistance and the meat naturally falls apart when lifted, it is fully cooked and tender.

9. Remove the beef shank and let it cool completely and naturally. The meat will tighten and the jelly will set, making it easier to slice thinly.

10. Prepare the sauce: squeeze the small lime juice into the mixture of minced garlic, cilantro stems, bird's eye chili, salt, and sugar. Stir well. Then add light soy sauce, vinegar, Sichuan peppercorn oil, and chili oil. Mix thoroughly.
    **Key point**: Lime juice provides a fruity sourness, which is the core flavor; chili oil adds color and depth.

11. Slice the completely cooled beef shank into thin pieces, revealing the clear marbling of the beef shin center.

12. Place the beef slices, julienned onion, bell peppers, and cilantro leaves into a large bowl. Pour the sauce over and toss well to combine.

## Health Tips
Beef shank is rich in high-quality protein and iron, suitable for fitness muscle building or blood replenishment. Using lime juice in the sauce replaces some of the vinegar, offering a refreshing tanginess. However, chili oil and Sichuan peppercorn oil are relatively high in calories, so reduce the amount as desired. Skimming the foam during braising and cooling the meat completely before slicing ensures the slices remain intact and distinct.
