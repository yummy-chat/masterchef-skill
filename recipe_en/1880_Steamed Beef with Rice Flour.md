# Steamed Beef with Rice Flour
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin 500g (clear grain, tender texture)
🍚 Rice 1/3 cup
🍚 Glutinous rice 1/3 cup
🧄 Garlic 7 cloves (for dipping sauce)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (for garnish)
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 tbsp
🧀 Fermented bean curd 2 pieces (mashed)
🫚 Ginger paste 1 tsp
🍬 Brown sugar 1 tsp
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1/4 tsp (for marinade) + 1/4 tsp (for dipping sauce)
🌫️ White pepper powder 1/4 tsp
🧂 Five-spice powder 1/8 tsp
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1.5 tbsp
🧪 Baking soda 1/4 tsp
🌿 Spices: star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, 1-2 each (for toasting with rice flour)
🧂 Salt 1/4 tsp (for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Chili powder 1.5 tbsp (for dipping sauce)
🌰 Sesame seeds 1 tbsp (for dipping sauce)
🫒 Cooking oil 2 tbsp (for coating and sealing in moisture) + 1/3 cup (for dipping sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp (for dipping sauce)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls, measuring cup
🍳 Wok
🥡 Blender/food processor
♨️ Steamer, steaming rack
🥣 Steaming bowl (with lid or coverable with plate)
🥢 Chopsticks or hands (for mixing ingredients)

## Instructions
1. Cut the beef tenderloin into thick slices. Soak in clean water for 10 minutes, then rinse until blood water is expelled, and drain well.
2. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, combine doubanjiang, mashed fermented bean curd, ginger paste, brown sugar, Sichuan peppercorn powder, white pepper powder, five-spice powder, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir into a smooth sauce.
3. Make the steaming rice flour: In a dry wok over low heat, toast the rice, glutinous rice, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, and cloves until the grains turn golden and the spices become fragrant. Remove large spice pieces. Transfer the toasted rice to a blender and pulse into a coarse powder with some granules remaining.
4. Marinate the beef: Add baking soda to the drained beef and mix well. Pour in the prepared marinade sauce, mix thoroughly, and let marinate for 1 hour.
5. Gradually add the steaming rice flour to the marinated beef, mixing well after each addition to coat each slice evenly. Add about 100–120g of water in batches, mixing and moistening as you go. Finally, drizzle in cooking oil and mix well to seal in moisture.
6. Transfer the mixed beef into a steaming bowl, press down lightly, and cover the bowl with a plate to prevent condensation from dripping in. Place in a steamer, bring water to a boil, then reduce to low heat and steam for 55 minutes.
7. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, chili powder, sesame seeds, salt, and Sichuan peppercorn powder. Heat the cooking oil until smoking, then quickly pour it over the mixture to release the aroma. Stir well, then add sesame oil to create a garlic-chili oil dipping sauce.
8. After steaming, remove the lid and take out the bowl. Sprinkle with chopped scallions. Serve with the dipping sauce for enhanced flavor.

## Health Tips
Steamed beef with rice flour uses rice and glutinous rice instead of high-oil cooking methods, providing rich protein with relatively low fat content, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those watching calorie intake. The beef becomes tender and easy to digest. Pairing with the chili oil dipping sauce can stimulate appetite and warm the body. However, doubanjiang and fermented bean curd are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of dipping sauce used.
