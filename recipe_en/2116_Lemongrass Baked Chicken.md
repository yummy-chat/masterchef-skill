# Lemongrass Baked Chicken
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐓 Whole chicken 1 (about 2 jin, choose a Guangdong chicken with small head, small feet, small body, and no jaundice)
🌿 Lemongrass appropriate amount (cut into sections and minced, for layering and stuffing)
🧄 Sand ginger appropriate amount (granular, for stuffing and layering)
🧅 Dried shallots appropriate amount (sliced, for layering)
🫚 Turmeric appropriate amount (in pieces, for layering)
🍋 Lemon peel appropriate amount (sliced, for stuffing and layering)
🍃 Lemon leaves appropriate amount (for stuffing)
🌶️ Thai chili peppers appropriate amount (optional, added when turning over)
🧄 Minced garlic appropriate amount (for stuffing)
🐔 Chicken fat trimmings appropriate amount (for rendering chicken oil)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount
🧂 Salt-baked chicken powder appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🫒 Chicken oil appropriate amount (rendered from chicken fat trimmings)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥘 Deep, tall pot (must be able to cover the whole chicken)
🔥 Portable stove
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🪣 Strainer
🍽️ Plate
🥣 Small bowl

## Steps
1. **Selecting chicken & pre-treatment**: Choose a Guangdong chicken around 2 jin, ensure it has no jaundice, and thoroughly remove the lungs from the chicken's inner cavity.
   **Key point**: The lungs must be cleaned out completely; otherwise, they will be difficult to cook through during the baking process, affecting texture and safety.
2. **Marinating**:
   - Evenly rub salt and sugar over the chicken's body and inner cavity.
   - Rub with salt-baked chicken powder, then rub with light soy sauce.
   - Stuff the chicken's inner cavity with sand ginger granules, minced garlic, minced lemongrass, lemon peel, and lemon leaves, packing tightly.
   - Refrigerate and marinate for 2 hours to allow the spices and seasonings to fully penetrate.
3. **Rendering chicken oil**: Place a frying pan over low heat, add the chicken fat trimmings, and slowly render out liquid chicken oil.
   **Key point**: Rendering the oil in advance prevents the chicken from sticking to the pan during frying and adds a natural aroma.
4. **Frying the chicken**: Add chicken oil to the frying pan, place the marinated whole chicken in the pan, and fry over low heat until both sides are golden and shiny.
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
5. **Layering and sautéing aromatics**: Switch to a deep, tall pot. Heat it over low heat, then add lemongrass sections, sliced dried shallots, sand ginger granules, turmeric pieces, and lemon peel in order. Sauté until fragrant, then pour in the rendered chicken oil and stir-fry.
   **Key point**: A tall pot is necessary to cover the whole chicken; do not add water at any point.
6. **Baking the chicken**:
   - Place the fried chicken on top of the aromatics, and sprinkle minced lemongrass over the surface.
   - Cover with the lid and bake over low heat for 15 minutes, then turn the chicken over. At this point, Thai chili peppers can be added.
   - Continue baking covered over low heat for another 10 minutes, for a total of 25 minutes.
   **Key point**: Turning the chicken halfway ensures even heating; Thai chili peppers are an optional ingredient.
7. **Checking doneness**: Insert chopsticks into the thickest part of the chicken thigh. If the juices run clear with no blood, it is cooked through.
8. **Adding aroma & finishing**: Just before turning off the heat, drizzle a small amount of cooking wine along the edge of the pot, then ignite the wine to release its aroma.
9. **Making the sauce**:
   - After removing the chicken from the pot, add light soy sauce to the aromatics left in the pot and stir well.
   - If the liquid is too dry, add a little water and simmer briefly to infuse the flavors of the aromatics.
   - Strain the mixture through a strainer to remove the aromatics residue, yielding a pure sauce.
   **Key point**: Straining gives the sauce a fine, smooth texture.
10. **Plating**: Evenly drizzle the sauce over the baked chicken and serve.

## Health Advice
This lemongrass baked chicken is rich in high-quality protein, and lemongrass has antioxidant properties, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those in recovery. However, the salt-baked chicken powder and light soy sauce are high in sodium, so those with hypertension should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with a light vegetable salad to balance the overall nutrition.
