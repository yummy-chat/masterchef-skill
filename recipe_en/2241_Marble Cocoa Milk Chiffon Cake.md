# Marble Cocoa Milk Chiffon Cake  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 Milk 20g (for cocoa paste)  
🥛 Milk 24g (for egg yolk batter)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 9g  
🥚 Eggs 3 (about 150-180g, room temperature, separate egg whites and yolks)  
🫒 Corn oil 30g  
🌾 Cake flour 35g (sifted)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 40g  
🍋 Lemon juice a few drops  
🧂 Salt a pinch (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Mixing bowl (stainless steel, grease-free and water-free)  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🍴 Spatula  
🔍 Sifter  
🍰 6-inch anodized removable bottom cake pan  
🔥 Oven  
🌬️ Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the cocoa paste**: Mix 20g milk with 9g cocoa powder. Use a spatula to press and stir until smooth and lump-free, achieving a chocolate sauce consistency. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature.  
   **Note**: If lumps form, crush them with the spatula.  

2. **Make the egg yolk batter**: Separate 3 room-temperature eggs into yolks and whites. Place the egg whites in a clean bowl and freeze until ice crystals form around the edges. To the egg yolks, add 30g corn oil and 24g milk. Whisk in one direction with a hand whisk until emulsified and the oil is fully incorporated. Sift in 35g cake flour. First, gently press to disperse the flour, then stir in a "Z" motion until smooth and ribbon-like, with a continuous dripping consistency.  
   **Note**: Room-temperature egg yolks emulsify better; avoid over-mixing to prevent gluten development.  

3. **Whip the meringue**: Remove the frozen egg whites. Add a few drops of lemon juice and a pinch of salt. Beat at high speed with an electric mixer until frothy and coarse. Add 40g granulated sugar all at once. Mix at low speed to combine, then increase to medium-high speed until the foam is fine. Finally, reduce to medium-low speed until the meringue is glossy, elastic, and forms small bends or stiff peaks when the whisk is lifted.  
   **Note**: Egg whites must be frozen; turn off the mixer to check consistency, stirring a few times manually before testing; if the meringue becomes coarse after resting, re-whip at low speed briefly.  

4. **Combine the batters**: Add one large scoop of meringue to the egg yolk batter and mix quickly until combined. Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and fold rapidly until uniform.  
   **Note**: In hot weather, fold quickly to prevent deflating.  

5. **Create the marble pattern**: Take about 50g of the mixed plain batter and add it to the cocoa paste. Mix well. Pour the cocoa batter back into the plain batter bowl, spreading it as evenly as possible over the surface. Use a spatula to cut vertically into the center of the batter and fold. Repeat twice. On the third fold, scrape the batter from the sides back into the center. Do not overmix.  
   **Note**: Fold only twice to maintain clear marbling; overmixing will create a uniform color.  

6. **Pour into pan and bake**: Pour the batter from a slight height into the 6-inch pan. Gently tap the pan a few times to release large air bubbles. Bake in a preheated oven at 140°C (284°F) top heat and 155°C (311°F) bottom heat for 55 minutes. After about 10-15 minutes, when the surface has set, quickly use a thin knife to make a cross cut on top, then immediately close the oven door and continue baking.  
   **Note**: Adjust preheating temperature and time based on your oven; the cross cut prevents the cake from cracking on the sides.  

7. **Cool and unmold**: Once baked, immediately drop the pan from a slight height onto a surface. Quickly invert it onto a cooling rack and let cool completely before unmolding.  
   **Note**: Dropping and inverting are key to preventing shrinkage and collapse.  

## Health Tips  
This marble chiffon cake uses cocoa powder instead of high-calorie chocolate, making it suitable for weight management. However, it contains a moderate amount of sugar, so it is best enjoyed with unsweetened tea or coffee. For those with high blood sugar, replace granulated sugar with a sugar substitute, but note that this may affect meringue consistency.
