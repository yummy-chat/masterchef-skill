# Seafood Spaghetti
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍝 Spaghetti, about 200g  
🦐 Shrimp, to taste (peeled and deveined)  
🐚 Clams, about 200g  
🧅 Onion, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🌿 Celery leaves, to taste (for garnish and aroma, can substitute thyme)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🫒 Olive oil, to taste  
🧈 Butter, one piece (about 10-15g, used in portions)  
🥛 Milk, to taste (about 100ml)  
🌶️ Black pepper, to taste (freshly ground preferred)  
🫚 Ginger slices, a few (for removing fishy odor when cooking seafood broth)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥘 Stockpot  
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon  
🥢 Cooking chopsticks  
🔪 Cutting board (for prepping ingredients)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add salt and a little olive oil, then cook the spaghetti until al dente. Drain and rinse immediately under cold water, then drain again and toss with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
   **Note**: The pasta must be fully cooked and then rinsed with cold water to maintain a firm, non-sticky texture.

2. In another stockpot, bring water and ginger slices to a boil. Add the clams and cook until all shells have opened, then immediately turn off the heat. Strain and reserve the broth. Remove the clam meat and set aside.
   **Key**: The seafood broth is the crucial source of umami for the entire dish.

3. Clean the shrimp and remove the vein. Finely chop the onion and mince the garlic.

4. Heat the frying pan, add olive oil, then add the minced garlic and chopped onion. Sauté over medium-low heat until the onion is translucent and the garlic is golden.

5. Add the shrimp and cook over medium heat until they turn color. Add one piece of butter and let it melt.
   **Note**: Butter adds rich flavor.

6. Pour the milk into the pan and bring to a gentle boil. Then add the reserved seafood broth from step 2. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Cook until the sauce turns white and begins to bubble.

7. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce, along with the clam meat. Gently toss to combine. Keep the heat at medium-low and let the spaghetti absorb the sauce. Simmer until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the noodles.
   **Key**: Allow it to simmer briefly to ensure the noodles are fully infused with flavor.

8. Finally, add another small piece of butter for sheen, sprinkle with chopped celery leaves, and toss to combine before serving.

## Health Advice  
The seafood broth is the soul of this dish's flavor, but use less salt when cooking the clams to control sodium intake. Both shrimp and clams provide high-quality protein, making this dish suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those managing blood sugar levels.
