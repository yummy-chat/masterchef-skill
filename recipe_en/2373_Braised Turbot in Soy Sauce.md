# Braised Turbot in Soy Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 turbot (cleaned, with slits cut on both sides)  
🧅 1 scallion (cut diagonally into thick sections)  
🫚 1 piece of ginger (sliced)  
🌶️ Green pickled peppers, to taste (seeded and shredded)  
🌶️ Red pickled peppers, to taste (seeded and shredded)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (sprinkle on the pan bottom before frying the fish, for seasoning)  
🥄 Light soy sauce or steamed fish soy sauce, to taste  
🥄 A splash of dark soy sauce (for coloring)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (with lid)  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🥄 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Heat the wok over high heat until hot, add cooking oil to coat the pan walls, and evenly sprinkle a layer of salt on the pan bottom. Place the turbot in the pan and fry one side over medium-high heat until set. Carefully flip and continue frying until both sides are golden brown.  
   **Key point**: Sprinkling salt on the pan bottom prevents the fish skin from sticking and tearing.  
2. Add the scallion sections and ginger slices next to the fish, and fry until fragrant.  
3. Add an appropriate amount of hot water. Season with salt, light soy sauce or steamed fish soy sauce, and a splash of dark soy sauce for color. Cover the wok tightly and braise over medium-high heat for about 5 minutes.  
4. Transfer the fish to a serving plate. Bring the sauce in the wok to a boil, add the shredded green and red peppers, blanch until the color changes, then pour the sauce and peppers over the fish. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This braised turbot is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for those needing to boost brain function and strengthen the body, especially children and the elderly. When frying the fish, use less oil and salt, avoid making the sauce too salty, and pair with vegetables for a more balanced nutrition.
