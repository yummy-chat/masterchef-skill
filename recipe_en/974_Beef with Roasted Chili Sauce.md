# Beef with Roasted Chili Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Green finger chili peppers 1680g  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers 50g (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic 250g (finely chopped)  
🐂 Beef 1200g (minced)  
🫒 Cooking oil 500g  
🧂 Salt 40g  
🍬 White sugar 20g  
🍶 Cooking wine 20g  
🥄 Light soy sauce 30g  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 10g  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil 5g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔥 Oven (with convection fan function)  
🔪 Food processor/Meat grinder  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🫙 Airtight jar  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the stems from the green finger chili peppers, spread them on a baking tray, and roast in the oven at a temperature above 200°C until the skin changes color and shrivels, achieving a "roasted chili" state.  
2. Allow the roasted chili peppers to cool until they are no longer hot to the touch, then process them in a food processor until finely chopped. Set aside.  
3. Cut the beef into small pieces and mince it in a food processor or meat grinder.  
4. Pour 500g of cooking oil into a non-stick pan and heat it. Add the chopped bird's eye chili peppers and garlic, stir-frying until a rich aroma is released.  
5. Add the minced beef and continue stir-frying until the beef changes color and separates into loose pieces.  
6. Add the roasted chili pepper mixture, then add salt, white sugar, cooking wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and Sichuan pepper oil. Stir-fry until everything is evenly mixed.  
7. Continue to stir-fry over medium-low heat until the moisture is fully evaporated, resulting in a thick sauce with clear, bright oil.  
**Key point**: Make sure to thoroughly evaporate the moisture to concentrate the flavors and extend the shelf life.

8. Turn off the heat and let the sauce cool slightly, then transfer it into an airtight jar that has been pre-washed with boiling water and thoroughly dried. Seal the jar tightly for storage.

## Health Advice  
This beef with roasted chili sauce is rich in protein and capsaicin, which can stimulate the appetite and refresh the mind. However, its oil and sodium content is relatively high. It is recommended to use only one spoonful per serving with staples and not to consume it directly as a snack. Suitable for those with poor appetite or needing a flavor boost, but individuals with sensitive stomachs or those needing to control blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
