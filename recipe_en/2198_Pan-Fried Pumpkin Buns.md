# Pan-Fried Pumpkin Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🎃 Pumpkin 350g (peeled and mashed)
🍞 High-gluten flour 600-650g
🥛 Whole milk 250ml
🍄 Yeast 5g
🍬 White sugar 70g (for dough)
🌽 Corn oil 20g
🍚 Dry flour (as needed, to prevent sticking)
🌰 White sesame seeds (as needed)
🔴 Goji berries (as needed)
🍬 White sugar (as needed, sprinkled on the pan bottom to form caramel)
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, used multiple times: brushing the pan, brushing the dough, preventing sticking)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Blender/food processor
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍞 Cutting board
🔪 Kitchen knife
🥢 Rolling pin
🖌️ Oil brush
♨️ Electric griddle
🫖 Lid
🍳 Silicone spatula
⚱️ Blue-and-white porcelain bowl (for second fermentation)

## Steps
1. Peel the pumpkin, cut into thin slices, place in the blender, add the whole milk, and blend into a fine pumpkin puree.
2. Add 5g yeast and 70g white sugar to the pumpkin puree, and stir until well combined.
3. Gradually add the high-gluten flour, stirring with chopsticks until it forms a flaky texture. Add 20g corn oil, then knead by hand into a soft dough.
   **Note**: The dough must be kneaded until soft for the final product to be fluffy and tender.
4. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot to ferment until doubled in size, with a honeycomb-like structure inside.
   **Summer note**: High room temperature speeds up fermentation; about 30 minutes is sufficient. Monitor closely to avoid over-fermentation.
5. Sprinkle dry flour on the cutting board, take out the dough, and knead to release gas. Divide the dough into several large portions. Roll each portion into a long strip, brush the surface with a layer of cooking oil, then cut into evenly-sized bun-shaped pieces.
6. Take a large bowl, brush the inner wall with oil to prevent sticking, place the bun pieces inside, brush the surface with another layer of oil, cover with plastic wrap or a lid, and perform a second fermentation for about 25 minutes, until the pieces have noticeably increased in size and feel light to the touch.
7. Preheat the electric griddle to medium heat, brush the bottom with oil, and evenly sprinkle white sesame seeds and a layer of white sugar.
   **Key**: The sugar and sesame seeds on the pan bottom are essential for forming a caramelized crispy base; do not skip.
8. Arrange the fermented bun pieces in the electric griddle, cover with the lid, and turn to low heat to steam for about 5 minutes, until the pieces have further expanded.
9. Open the lid to check the bottom color. Use a silicone spatula to flip the pieces. Optionally sprinkle a few goji berries for decoration, then cover again and continue to simmer on low heat for about 5 minutes.
   **Doneness check**: Press a piece with the spatula; if it springs back quickly, it is cooked through. If it springs back slowly or feels sticky, extend the cooking time and reduce the heat appropriately to avoid a burnt exterior and raw interior.
10. Remove from the pan and serve in a bowl. The pan-fried pumpkin buns, soft with a caramelized sesame crispy base, are ready.

## Health Advice
This pan-fried pumpkin bun is rich in pumpkin’s beta-carotene and dietary fiber. Kneading the dough with milk provides more comprehensive nutrition, making it suitable as breakfast or a snack. Pay attention to controlling the amount of white sugar; it is recommended to reduce the caramel part for diabetics or those on a weight-loss diet.
