# Microwave Cold Noodles (Liangpi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Wheat starch 125g
🌾 All-purpose flour 30g
🧂 Salt, a pinch
💧 Water, approximately 225ml
🥒 Shredded cucumber, as needed
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed
🧄 Garlic water, as needed
🌿 Chopped cilantro, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, as needed
🌶️ Chili oil, as needed
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Microwave oven
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍽️ Heat-resistant flat round plate
🖌️ Brush
🥄 Spoon
✂️ Scissors

## Preparation Steps
1. In a large bowl, mix the wheat starch, all-purpose flour, and salt evenly. Slowly add the water while stirring continuously until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.
2. Take a flat round plate and evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the bottom to prevent sticking.
3. Scoop two spoonfuls of batter into the plate, tilt and swirl to spread the batter evenly to a thickness of about 2mm.
4. Place the plate in the microwave and heat on medium-high power for 1 minute and 30 seconds. Remove once the cold noodles are fully set and appear transparent or semi-transparent.
5. Immediately brush a layer of oil on the surface of the cold noodles to prevent drying and sticking.
6. Allow the cold noodles to cool until no longer hot to the touch, then carefully peel them off completely.
7. Roll up the cold noodles, cut them into strips with scissors, and place them in a bowl for later use.
8. Add shredded cucumber, bean sprouts, garlic water, light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, chili oil, chopped cilantro, and sesame oil in order. Mix thoroughly and serve.

## Health Tips
This cold noodle recipe uses a microwave instead of traditional steaming, making it more convenient. The higher proportion of wheat starch makes it suitable for those who want to control calorie intake while satisfying cravings. When preparing, ensure the batter is spread evenly, otherwise uneven thickness will affect the texture.
