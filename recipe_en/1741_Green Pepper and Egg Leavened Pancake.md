# Green Pepper and Egg Leavened Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April), Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
🥛 Room temperature water 450g
🍬 Granulated sugar 15g
🧂 Salt 3g
🫙 Yeast 5g
🫑 Green peppers, as needed (diced)
🌶️ Red peppers/bell peppers, as needed (diced)
🥫 Luncheon meat/ham sausage, as needed (diced)
🥚 Eggs, 3 pieces
🧂 Salt, about 2 pinches (for seasoning the egg mixture)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, about 1 pinch (to enhance flavor)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing and fermenting)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring the batter)
🫙 Cut open plastic water bottle (homemade measuring cup)
🔪 Knife, cutting board (for chopping ingredients)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for cooking pancakes)
🪩 Red silicone spatula (for flipping and removing)
🧤 Disposable gloves (for handling sticky dough, needs to be oiled)

## Preparation Steps
1. Combine the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, salt, and yeast in a bowl. Gradually add the room temperature water, stirring continuously in one direction with chopsticks until the dry ingredients are fully incorporated with the water, forming a thick, rough batter. Stirring time is about 3-4 minutes.
   **Note**: No need to knead by hand; this batter has a very high water content and a soft, sticky texture.
2. Cover the bowl and let it rest until the batter has doubled in volume. When lifted with chopsticks, the interior should show an even honeycomb structure.
   **Key Point**: If the room temperature is low, you can prepare the batter the night before and refrigerate it for fermentation; it will be ready to use the next morning.
3. Dice the green peppers, red peppers, and luncheon meat into small cubes.
4. Place the prepared vegetables and meat into a bowl, crack in the eggs, add the salt and chicken bouillon, and stir into a uniform egg and vegetable mixture. Set aside.
5. Brush a layer of oil in the frying pan, heat over high heat, then turn to the lowest heat. Wear disposable gloves and apply a generous amount of cooking oil to the glove surface to prevent sticking.
   **Key Point**: Maintain the lowest heat throughout the cooking process; high heat can easily cause the outside to burn while the inside remains raw.
6. Take a piece of the fermented batter and place it in the pan. Gently press it flat into a round pancake shape with the back of your hand.
   **Key Point**: The batter is extremely soft and sticky. Ensure that your hands and the bottom of the pan have plenty of oil. If using a regular non-stick pan, add more cooking oil to the pan bottom.
7. Using a spoon, scoop up the prepared egg and vegetable mixture and spread it evenly over the surface of the pancake. Some of the egg mixture may run off, which is normal; it can be shaped once it has set.
8. Cover the pan and cook for about 1 minute, until the egg mixture is mostly set and the bottom of the pancake is firm, then flip.
9. After flipping, cover the pan again and cook for about half a minute.
10. Use an alternating cooking method: cook the side with the egg mixture for 30 seconds, then the plain side for 60 seconds – consider this one cycle. Repeat for about 4 cycles, until both sides of the pancake are golden brown, puffy, and cooked through.
    **Key Point**: Flipping multiple times ensures the inside is cooked through without burning the outside.

## Health Advice
The green pepper and egg leavened pancake offers balanced nutrition, suitable as a breakfast or lunch staple: the flour provides carbohydrates, the eggs and luncheon meat supply protein, and the green and red peppers add vitamin C. Note that luncheon meat is high in sodium; it is recommended to serve this with a light vegetable soup. People with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of luncheon meat used.
