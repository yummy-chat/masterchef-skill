# State Banquet Lamb Pilaf  
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb hind leg (choose a leaner cut)  
🧈 Lamb tail fat (about 1/3 to 1/4 the amount of lamb, for richness and moisture)  
🥕 Carrots (cut into thick strips)  
🧅 Purple onion (cut into thick strips)  
🍚 Rice (soak for half an hour to an hour in advance, then drain)  
🍇 Raisins (适量, added at the end)  
🫒 Cooking oil (适量)  
🍶 Yellow wine (to remove gamey smell and enhance aroma)  
🧂 Salt  
🌶️ White pepper (to remove gamey smell and add spice)  
🍅 Tomato sauce  
🥫 Tomato paste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Rice cooker  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the lamb and lamb tail fat into cubes of about 1.5 cm. Cut the carrots and purple onion into thick strips, matching the size of the meat cubes.  
2. Wash the rice and soak it for half an hour to an hour, then drain and set aside.  
3. Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat. Stir-fry the onion strips until translucent and slightly charred, releasing a rich aroma.  
4. Add the carrot strips and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until slightly softened.  
5. Add the lamb cubes and lamb tail fat cubes. Turn to medium-high heat and stir-fry until the lamb changes color and the lamb tail fat partially melts.  
6. Pour yellow wine along the edge of the wok. Add salt and white pepper. Stir-fry evenly to remove the gamey smell and season.  
7. Add tomato sauce and tomato paste. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 1 minute, ensuring all ingredients are coated in a glossy red sauce and the aromas are well combined.  
8. Pour the stir-fried base, along with the juices and fat, into the rice cooker inner pot. Spread the soaked rice on top. Add water until it just covers the surface of the rice.  
**Key Note**: The water amount is critical. Too much water will make the rice mushy, while too little will result in undercooked rice. Do not stir the rice; simply spread it evenly.  
9. Cover the lid and start the normal rice cooking program until the rice cooker switches to keep-warm mode.  
10. Open the lid and evenly sprinkle in the raisins. Cover the lid again and let it sit for a few minutes using the residual heat.  
11. Open the lid and thoroughly mix from bottom to top with a spoon, allowing the rice, meat, vegetables, and fat to blend evenly.  
12. Serve and enjoy. The rice is oily and glossy with a red tint, offering a savory and slightly tangy flavor with the sweetness of dried fruit.  

## Health Advice  
This pilaf uses lamb tail fat for richness, making it high in fat. It is recommended to serve with a refreshing salad to balance the greasiness. Suitable for those needing an energy boost, but individuals with high blood lipids should control their portion size.
