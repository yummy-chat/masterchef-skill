# Pig Trotters Aspic
Cuisine Type: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐷 Pig trotters, about 1-2 pieces (cleaned and split)
🍶 White liquor, as needed (for blanching to remove gamey smell)
🍾 Soy sauce, as needed (to adjust to a light reddish-brown color)
🧂 Salt, as needed (to season during the second boiling)
🪵 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🌸 Cumin, as needed
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🌰 Black cardamom, 1 piece (cracked)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking and cleaning trotters)
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Wok (for blanching)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing and skimming)
♨️ Pressure cooker (for pressure cooking until tender and falling off the bone)
🎒 Spice bag (for holding spices)
🥣 Large bowl (for holding the liquid to solidify)

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the pig trotters in clean water to remove blood and impurities. Remove and split them lengthwise from the middle into two halves.
2. Add cold water to a pot, place in the trotters, drizzle with white liquor, and bring to a boil. After boiling, continue cooking for 3-5 minutes to thoroughly remove blood foam and impurities, then remove the trotters.
   **Note**: Starting with cold water helps draw out blood; white liquor effectively removes the gamey smell.
3. Place the trotters in warm water and scrub them carefully, especially cleaning the fat from between the bones. Remove and drain.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to wash with warm water; this is crucial for a firm, non-soggy aspic.
4. Place the cinnamon stick, cumin, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and cracked black cardamom into a spice bag, tie it securely, and rinse the outside with water.
5. Put the trotters in a pressure cooker. Add enough water to cover them, add soy sauce to achieve a light reddish-brown color, and add the spice bag. Cover. After the pressure comes up, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 30 minutes, until the trotters are tender and falling off the bone.
6. Turn off the heat, allow the pressure to release naturally, then open the lid. While hot, remove the trotters, discard all bones, and keep only the skin and tendons.
7. Cut the skin and tendons into small pieces and return them to the original broth in the pressure cooker. Bring the broth to a boil, then season with salt.
8. Use a fine-mesh slotted spoon to carefully skim off all surface fat and foam until the broth is clear.
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly skimming oil and foam ensures the final aspic is clear and free of impurities.
9. Reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly until the broth becomes noticeably thick and gelatinous, allowing the collagen to fully dissolve.
10. Pour the simmered broth along with the skin and tendon pieces into a large bowl. Allow it to cool and set at room temperature, resting overnight until firm, bouncy to the touch, and not sticky.
    **Note**: Generally, no refrigeration is needed; it will set at room temperature. If the ambient temperature is high, light refrigeration can be used.
11. Once set, slice the aspic and serve with a dipping sauce made from mashed garlic, vinegar, light soy sauce, etc.

## Health Advice
This pig trotters aspic is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity. However, it is high in fat, so individuals with high cholesterol or those on a weight loss diet should control their intake.
