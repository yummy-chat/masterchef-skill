# Spicy Pork Liver  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐷 Pork liver, about 2 pieces (one large chunk)  
🧄 Ginger, 1 small piece (crushed and diced)  
🧄 Garlic, 4-5 cloves (crushed and diced)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, 1 large handful (cut into segments)  
🌿 Green Sichuan peppercorns, 1 small handful  
🫒 Cooking oil, enough (generous amount for deep frying)  
🌰 White sesame seeds, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste (used in two separate times)  
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste  
🥔 Dry starch, to taste (for coating)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil, a little  
🍶 Vinegar, a little (for the wok edge)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🪣 Large basin (for washing, soaking, and marinating)  
🥣 Bowl (for holding aromatics)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Large wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rinse the pork liver thoroughly, first cut into strips, then dice into uniform cube-shaped pieces.  
2. Place the diced liver into a large basin, add clean water, and repeatedly wash to remove blood, then squeeze out excess water.  
3. Add salt and white pepper powder to the squeezed liver, mix thoroughly by hand to marinate.  
4. Add dry starch, continue to mix evenly so that each piece of liver is thinly coated to seal in moisture.  
5. Crush the ginger and garlic, then dice; cut the dried chilies into segments. Set aside.  
6. Pour a generous amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat until it reaches 70% hot.  
7. Scatter the coated liver pieces into the oil, let them sit for a few seconds to set, then gently loosen with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking.  
8. Deep-fry over high heat until set, then reduce to medium-low heat, continue to fry for 4-5 minutes until golden brown on the surface, fully cooked inside, and crispy.  
9. Remove the liver pieces and drain the oil.  
10. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, stir-fry the ginger and garlic over medium heat until fragrant.  
11. Add the green Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili segments, stir-fry to release their spicy aroma, being careful not to burn them.  
12. Pour in the fried liver pieces, stir-fry evenly to coat with the aromatics.  
13. Reduce to the lowest heat, slowly stir-fry to allow the spicy and numbing flavors to penetrate the liver.  
14. Add salt and chicken bouillon to season, sprinkle in white sesame seeds.  
15. Drizzle with Sichuan pepper oil, splash vinegar along the edge of the wok, quickly stir-fry evenly, and immediately remove from the heat.  
**Key Point**: The vinegar splashed along the wok edge enhances aroma and flavor; stir-fry quickly to release fragrance using residual heat, but avoid overcooking the liver.  

## Health Advice  
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, suitable for those with anemia or eye strain. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it sparingly. Deep frying increases calorie content, so it is recommended to balance with vegetables.
