# Braised Duck Gizzard in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck gizzards, about 500g (washed and scored in a chrysanthemum pattern)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections and minced)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)
🌶️ Pickled wild peppers, to taste (whole or halved)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, a little (minced for garnish)
🧅 Small scallions, a little (minced for garnish)
🍠 Sweet potato starch, to taste (for making slurry)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥡 Light soy sauce, to taste
🌿 Ground white pepper, to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for marinating and sautéing)
💧 Water, a small amount (for mixing sweet potato starch slurry)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🫖 Clay pot
🥄 Soup spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the duck gizzards**: Wash the duck gizzards. For easier cutting, place them in the freezer until slightly firm. Then, without cutting all the way through, score them in a crosshatch pattern to create a chrysanthemum shape.
- **Key point**: Cutting when slightly frozen results in clearer, more attractive scoring.
2. **Marinate**: Place the scored duck gizzards in a bowl. Add cooking wine, salt, light soy sauce, white pepper, and oyster sauce. Wearing gloves, mix well and let marinate until flavorful.
3. **Coat with slurry**: Mix the sweet potato starch with a small amount of water to form a thick slurry. Pour over the marinated duck gizzards and toss to coat evenly. Finally, add a spoonful of cooking oil and mix again.
- **Key point**: Must use sweet potato starch; no need for egg whites. The oil prevents sticking and adds sheen.
4. **Sauté aromatics**: Pour oil into the clay pot. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic slices, and pickled wild peppers. Sauté over medium heat until fragrant.
5. **Braising**: Spread the coated duck gizzards evenly over the aromatics. Cover with the lid, reduce the heat to low, and braise for about 12 minutes.
- **Key point**: Do not add any water throughout the process. Cook using the natural moisture of the ingredients and the heat retention of the clay pot. Braise for about 12 minutes, or until the gizzards curl up and are cooked through.
6. **Garnish and serve**: Remove the lid. Sprinkle with minced scallions and red bell pepper. Let the residual heat release the aromas before serving.

## Health Advice
Duck gizzard is a high-protein, low-fat ingredient. Paired with ginger, garlic, and pickled wild peppers, it helps dispel cold and warm the stomach, making it suitable for those with good digestive function. Note that duck gizzard is relatively high in cholesterol; it is recommended to limit intake to 10–15 pieces per serving.
