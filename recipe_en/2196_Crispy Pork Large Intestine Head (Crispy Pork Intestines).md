# Crispy Pork Large Intestine Head (Crispy Pork Intestines)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork large intestine head, about 500g (use only the thickest section)  
🧂 Salt, about 20g (for cleaning + seasoning the braising liquid)  
🌾 Cornstarch, 1 tablespoon (for cleaning)  
🫚 Ginger, 3-4 slices  
🧅 Scallions, 2-3 stalks  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, 2-3 pieces  
🌿 Bay leaves, 3-4 leaves  
⭐ Star anise, 5 pieces  
🍶 Rice wine, as needed  
🌶️ Hainan peppercorns (whole black peppercorns), 5-10g  
🤍 White vinegar, 100g (for the crispy coating liquid)  
❤️ Red vinegar (Zhejiang vinegar), 100g (for the crispy coating liquid)  
🍯 Maltose, 20g (for the crispy coating liquid)  
🥚 Egg whites, 2 (for the crispy coating liquid)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Long chopsticks / skewers  
🪝 Meat hook  
🌀 Electric fan  
🥄 Slotted spoon / strainer  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Turn the large intestine inside out and gently peel off excess fat. Turn it right side out again, add salt and cornstarch, rub thoroughly, then rinse repeatedly under running water until clean.  
**Key point**: After cleaning, some of the intestine’s natural flavor should remain; washing it too cleanly makes it "boring."

2. Heat the wok with cold oil, add ginger slices and scallion sections, stir-fry until fragrant, drizzle in rice wine, then add enough clean water.
3. Add cinnamon, bay leaves, star anise, and peppercorns. Once the water boils, add the cleaned large intestine and season with salt.
**Key point**: The water must completely cover the intestine; otherwise, any exposed parts will not absorb flavor.

4. Braise over medium heat for about 45 minutes, until a long chopstick can be easily inserted into the intestine. Remove immediately and rinse under cold water to tighten the meat, making it more crisp and firm.
5. Prepare the crispy coating liquid: Mix together white vinegar, red vinegar, maltose, and egg whites until well combined.
**Key point**: Maltose has lower sweetness and is less prone to burning at high temperatures than honey, making it more suitable.

6. Place the cooled intestine into the crispy coating liquid, rub thoroughly by hand to ensure the entire surface is completely moistened.
7. Hang the intestine using a meat hook or bamboo skewer, place it in a ventilated area, and blow-dry with an electric fan for 1.5-2 hours, until the surface is completely dry and no longer sticky to the touch.
8. Frying is done in two steps:
   - First fry: Heat a generous amount of oil to about 100°C. Add the intestine and deep-fry over medium-low heat for about 1 minute, turning constantly, until set. Remove.
   - Second fry: Raise the oil temperature to about 160°C. Return the intestine and fry for about 30 seconds, until the surface is golden and crispy, you can hear a crackling sound, and the color turns a deep jujube red.
**Key point**: Oil temperature is crucial. The first fry at low temperature cooks the inside through; the second fry at high temperature crisps the skin.

9. Remove and drain excess oil. Cut into sections and plate. If you hear a crisp, crunchy sound when cutting, it is a success.

## Health Advice  
Crispy large intestine head is rich in protein, but it also contains high amounts of fat and cholesterol. It is recommended to eat it with plenty of vegetables. This dish is suitable only as an occasional treat and should not be a daily staple. Those with weak digestion or high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
