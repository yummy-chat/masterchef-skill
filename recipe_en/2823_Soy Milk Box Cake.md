# Soy Milk Box Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Soybeans 50g (soaked overnight in advance)
💧 Water 630g (600g for making soy milk, 30g for mixing egg yolk batter)
🌾 Cake Flour 160g (30g for soy milk cream, 130g for cake body)
🥚 Eggs 3 egg yolks (about 60g) + 6 whole eggs (about 240g)
🍬 White Sugar 130g
🧈 Butter 30g (can substitute with equal amount of vegetable oil)
🟤 Cooked Soybean Flour appropriate amount (for decoration)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🫙 Blender/Food Processor
🥄 Fine Mesh Strainer/Filter
🍳 Pot
🥣 Whisk (manual + electric)
🫓 Electric Griddle or Non-stick Pan
📦 Glass or Ceramic Container
🎨 Piping Bag or Ziplock Bag

## Instructions

### Preparation Stage
1. Soak soybeans in water overnight in advance. After soaking, rinse them clean.
2. Separate the egg yolks and egg whites from 3 eggs, and set the yolks aside.
3. Prepare 6 whole eggs in a clean, oil-free and water-free bowl and set aside.

### Making the Soy Milk Cream
1. Pour the soaked soybeans and 600g of water into the blender, and blend until a smooth raw soy milk forms.
2. Strain the raw soy milk through a fine mesh strainer, squeezing the soybean pulp as dry as possible, and retain the strained soy milk liquid.
3. Pour the strained soy milk into a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 5 minutes to ensure it is fully cooked. Turn off the heat and set aside.
   **Note**: Soy milk must be fully cooked, otherwise it will have a beany flavor and may cause discomfort.
4. In a bowl, mix 30g of cake flour, 3 egg yolks, and 30g of water until you have a smooth, lump-free batter.
5. Keep 200g of the cooked soy milk in the pot. Heat it over the lowest heat to keep it warm.
   **Note**: The soy milk must not boil to prevent lumps from forming when added to the batter.
6. Slowly pour the batter into the warm soy milk while quickly and continuously whisking with a whisk.
   **Key Point**: The whisking must be fast and continuous to prevent the batter from cooking into lumps.
7. Continue whisking until the mixture becomes smooth and thick, like a thick yogurt consistency. Then turn off the heat.
8. While still hot, add 30g of butter and stir until fully incorporated.
   **Explanation**: The butter adds smoothness and prevents the soy milk cream from drying out as it cools.
9. Transfer the finished soy milk cream into a piping bag or a Ziplock bag and refrigerate until needed.

### Making the Cake Body
1. Add 130g of white sugar to the 6 whole eggs. Use an electric mixer to beat on high speed.
2. Beat until the egg mixture turns pale, increases in volume to about 3 times, and has clear lines.
3. Sift 130g of cake flour into the mixture and beat with the mixer for about 10 seconds until just combined.
   **Note**: Do not overmix to avoid developing gluten which will make the cake texture tough.
4. Brush a thin layer of oil on an electric griddle or a non-stick pan. Pour in half of the batter and spread it flat.
5. Cover with the lid and cook over medium heat for about 6 minutes, until the bottom is golden brown and the surface is set.
6. Flip and cook for another 2 minutes until the other side is browned. Remove to get the first cake sheet.
7. Repeat steps 4-6 to cook the second cake sheet.

### Assembly
1. Once the cake sheets have cooled, use the bottom of your container to press down and cut them into pieces that fit the container, trimming off the edges.
2. Place one cake sheet at the bottom of the container.
3. Take the refrigerated soy milk cream piping bag, snip a small hole, and pipe an even layer of soy milk cream onto the cake sheet.
4. Place the second cake sheet on top, and pipe another layer of soy milk cream.
5. Pipe the remaining soy milk cream onto the surface. You can use a piping tip to create small dots or patterns.
6. Using a fine mesh strainer, generously sift a thick layer of cooked soybean flour evenly over the surface.

## Health Advice
This Soy Milk Box Cake uses soy milk to replace cream, reducing saturated fat. However, its sugar and carbohydrate content remains relatively high. It is recommended to consume it in moderation as a dessert, and it pairs best with unsweetened tea. The soy milk cream must be fully cooked to avoid any beany flavor or digestive discomfort.
