# Little Cat Potato Cake 🐱
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 1 large and 1 small potato (the large one is enough; the small one is for comparison)  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, as needed (to adjust the moisture of the mashed potatoes)  
🥓 Ham sausage, as needed (chopped and mixed into the mashed potatoes)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (to season the mashed potatoes)  
🍙 Seaweed, as needed (for decoration)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Peeler  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
✂️ Scissors  
🔬 Tweezers (to assist in placing the seaweed)

## Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and steam them whole until fully cooked.  
   **Note**: Steaming whole preserves the original flavor and prevents excess moisture.
2. Mash the cooked potatoes into a puree, then add glutinous rice flour, chopped ham sausage, and salt. Mix until a dough forms.
3. Take an appropriate amount of the potato mixture, shape it into a ball by hand, then form it into a kitten-shaped patty.  
   **Note**: Glutinous rice flour absorbs excess moisture, making it easier to shape.
4. Pour a suitable amount of oil into a non-stick pan and heat it. Place the kitten-shaped raw potato cake into the pan and fry over medium-low heat until one side is golden brown and set. Flip and continue frying until both sides are golden brown. Remove from the pan.
5. Use scissors to cut the seaweed into shapes for the kitten’s eyes, nose, ears, and whiskers. Use tweezers to attach them onto the fried potato cake.

## Health Suggestions
Potatoes and glutinous rice flour provide complex carbohydrates for a steady release of energy. Ham sausage adds protein, making this suitable as a main dish or a post-workout snack. When frying, use medium-low heat and minimal oil to control calorie intake.
