# New-Style Yu Xiang Rou Si (Fish-Fragrant Shredded Pork)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork (lean meat) – appropriate amount (cut into thin shreds)
🥔 Potato – appropriate amount (cut into thin shreds)
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (minced)
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (minced)
🌿 Cilantro – appropriate amount (minced, for garnish)
🧂 Salt – a pinch (for marinating pork shreds)
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount (for marinating pork shreds and cooking)
🌽 Cornstarch – a pinch (for marinating pork shreds)
🫒 Cooking oil – a small amount (for marinating pork shreds) + a large amount (for frying potato shreds and sliding pork shreds) + a small amount (for stir-frying the topping)
🧂 Light soy sauce – one tablespoon (for preparing the sauce)
🫗 Aged vinegar – two tablespoons (for preparing the sauce)
🍬 White sugar – two level tablespoons (for preparing the sauce)
💧 Water – two tablespoons (for preparing the sauce)
🌶️ Pixian chili bean paste – appropriate amount (to stir-fry until red oil emerges)
🍶 Huadiao wine – appropriate amount (to remove odor and enhance fragrance)
💧 Water starch – appropriate amount (to thicken into a light gluey sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
♨️ Gas stove
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Wooden or metal spoon (for scooping seasonings and stirring)
🥢 Chopsticks (for mixing pork shreds evenly and separating them)
🍽️ Plate (for serving)

## Cooking Steps

### Preparation Stage
1. **Prepare the potato shreds**: Cut the potato into thin shreds, rinse thoroughly with clean water repeatedly to remove surface starch, then drain well.
**Note**: Surface starch must be washed off completely to prevent sticking during frying.

2. **Marinate the pork shreds**: Add a pinch of salt, cooking wine, and cornstarch to the pork shreds. Mix until sticky, then drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil and mix well.
**Note**: Adding cooking oil prevents the pork shreds from sticking together during sliding.

3. **Prepare the sauce mixture**: In a bowl, combine one tablespoon of light soy sauce, two tablespoons of aged vinegar, two level tablespoons of white sugar, and two tablespoons of water. Stir until well mixed and set aside.
**Note**: Preparing the sauce mixture in advance avoids rushing during cooking.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Fry the potato shreds**: Pour a sufficient amount of cooking oil into the wok. When the oil is hot, add the potato shreds. Fry over medium-low heat throughout until the moisture is gone, the shreds turn golden yellow, and they become crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.
**Heat and time**: Medium-low heat, about 3–4 minutes.
   **Doneness check**: The potato shreds are golden yellow in color and crispy in texture.
   **Note**: The potato shreds must be completely dried of moisture to achieve a crispy texture.

2. **Slide the pork shreds**: After frying the potato shreds, reduce the oil temperature to about 20% heat. Add the marinated pork shreds and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Once the shreds change from red to white, immediately remove and drain excess oil.
**Heat and time**: Low oil temperature, short time.
   **Doneness check**: The color of the pork shreds changes from red to white.
   **Note**: Slide the pork at low oil temperature and remove as soon as the color changes to ensure tenderness.

3. **Stir-fry the topping**: Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. First, add minced garlic and ginger and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the Pixian chili bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until red oil is released. Drizzle in a small amount of Huadiao wine, then pour in the prepared sauce mixture. Bring to a boil, then add the slid pork shreds.
**Heat**: Stir-fry the seasonings over low heat, cook the sauce over medium heat.
   **Note**: Stir-fry the chili bean paste over low heat to release red oil without burning the wok.

4. **Thicken and plate**: After adding the pork shreds, drizzle in a small amount of water starch to thicken into a light gluey sauce. Stir-fry evenly, then pour over the fried potato shreds arranged as a base on the plate.
**Note**: A light gluey sauce helps the sauce better coat the pork shreds.

5. **Garnish the dish**: Sprinkle a small amount of fried potato shreds and minced cilantro on top.
**Note**: The potato shreds on top add a crispy texture and visual layering.

## Health Advice
This dish replaces traditional lettuce with fried potato shreds, enhancing the crispy texture but also increasing the calorie content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. Suitable for those who enjoy sweet-and-sour flavors and crispy textures; be mindful of controlling oil intake.
