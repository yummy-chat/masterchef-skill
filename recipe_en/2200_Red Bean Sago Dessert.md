# Red Bean Sago Dessert
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red beans 150g (rinsed in advance)
🥄 Small sago appropriate amount (pre-cooked and rinsed in cold water, chewy and translucent)
🍬 Yellow rock sugar about 50g

## Cookware and Equipment
🫖 Clay pot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥢 Strainer

## Cooking Steps
1. Add enough water to a deep pot, put in the small sago, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 10 minutes. Turn off the heat, cover, and let it sit for 10 minutes. If the center of the sago still has a white core, bring to a boil again and cook for 5 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it sit for 5 minutes. Repeat until completely translucent with no white core. Remove the sago, rinse with cold water, and drain for later use.
**Key point**: Combining boiling and steaming makes the sago chewy and translucent; rinsing with cold water prevents sticking and maintains texture.

2. Place the red beans and enough water in a clay pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 50 minutes, until the skins of the red beans crack open.
**Note**: Red beans are larger in size and need a longer cooking time than mung beans to become soft.

3. Add the yellow rock sugar, stir until the sugar dissolves, and use a strainer to remove the floating red bean skins from the surface.
4. Add the sago prepared in step 1, continue cooking over low heat for 10 minutes to allow the sago to absorb the sweetness.
**Key point**: The sago is already cooked, so a short cooking time is sufficient; avoid prolonged heating to prevent it from becoming too soft.

5. Turn off the heat, serve in bowls, and enjoy hot or let it cool and refrigerate for a chilled version.

## Health Tips
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron, which help with blood enrichment and promote intestinal motility, making them suitable for cooling and relieving heat in summer. However, yellow rock sugar has a high sugar content, so those with high blood sugar should control their portion size.
