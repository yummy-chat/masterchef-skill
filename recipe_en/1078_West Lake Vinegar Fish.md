# West Lake Vinegar Fish
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Freshwater sea bass 1
🫚 Ginger, as needed (some sliced for boiling, some finely minced into ginger paste)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (scallion segments, used to deodorize the fish broth)
🌿 White pepper powder, a pinch
🍶 Huadiao wine, a splash
🧂 Salt 2g
🍬 Sugar 10g
🥫 Soy sauce 30ml
🍎 Vinegar 50ml
🌾 Water starch, as needed

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🧻 Kitchen paper or towel
🍳 Pot
🥢 Slotted spoon or chopsticks
🕸️ Fine-mesh strainer
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Soup spoon

## Cooking Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Pat the sea bass dry. With one hand pressing down on the fish head, insert the knife from the tail and push flat toward the head, splitting the fish in half. You will get one “male side” with the backbone and one “female side” without the backbone.
   **Note**: This is the first cut. Seven cuts are made in total to ensure the fish cooks evenly and quickly.
2. Make five diagonal slashes on the “male side.” On the thickest part of the inner flesh of the “female side,” make one cut from head to tail without cutting through the skin.
3. Finely mince the ginger into extremely fine pieces, the finer the better.

**Cooking Stage**
1. **Low-temperature poaching of the fish**: Add water to a pot, along with scallion segments, ginger slices, a pinch of white pepper powder, and Huadiao wine. Keep the heat at the lowest setting so the water temperature stays around 90°C, barely simmering.
   - First, add the “male side” and cook for about 1 minute, then add the “female side” and continue cooking for about 3 minutes. Total cooking time is approximately 5 minutes.
   - Doneness test: When a chopstick can easily pierce through the thickest part behind the head of the “male side,” the fish is cooked.
   **Key point**: Do not use any oil. Use the low-temperature “immersion” technique to preserve the fish’s original flavor. Because the “male side” and “female side” have different thicknesses, they must be added to the pot at different times.
2. **Preparing the fish broth**: Gently remove the fish and drain. Turn the heat to high and bring the fish broth in the pot to a boil. Use a fine-mesh strainer to remove any solids, reserving about 200ml of clear broth.
   **Note**: Boiling on high heat helps remove some of the fishy smell.
3. **Making the sauce**: Pour the 200ml of reserved fish broth into a pot. Add 2g salt, 10g sugar, 30ml soy sauce, and 50ml vinegar. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add an appropriate amount of water starch to thicken, stirring until the sauce becomes glossy and viscous.
   - Turn off the heat, then add the minced ginger and stir to combine.
   **Key point**: The sugar is only used to balance the sourness of the vinegar; the sauce should not taste sweet. The minced ginger must be added after the sauce has thickened and the heat is off to preserve its aroma.
4. **Plating and saucing**: Place the cooked and drained fish on a serving plate. Evenly pour the ginger sauce over the fish, ensuring the entire fish is coated.

## Health Advice
This West Lake Vinegar Fish is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for those watching their weight or preferring a light diet. Those with excessive stomach acid should consume the vinegar in moderation. The low-temperature poaching method keeps the fish tender and smooth, but be sure the fish is fully cooked to avoid any risk of parasites.
