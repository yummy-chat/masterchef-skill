# Indian Flatbread
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Durum flour (hard wheat flour) 380g (can be substituted with regular whole wheat flour)  
🧂 Salt 4g  
💧 Boiling water 310g  
💧 Room temperature water 45g  
🌾 All-purpose flour (or dry flour) as needed (for dusting to prevent sticking)  
🧈 Salted butter as needed (for brushing after cooking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks or fork  
✋ Clean kitchen towel  
🍳 Cast iron skillet (or regular skillet)  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔩 Metal tongs (with mesh grip)  
🔥 Metal cooling rack (placed on gas stove for direct flame roasting)  
🫙 Sealed container  
🍽️ Plastic wrap or lid

## Instructions  
1. **Mix Dry Ingredients**: Place durum flour and salt into a bowl, mixing evenly.  
2. **Scald the Dough**: Pour boiling water into the flour in a circular motion, stirring simultaneously until flaky.  
3. **Knead the Dough**: Let cool slightly, then knead into a dough by hand. Gradually add room temperature water and knead until the dough is smooth and soft.  
   **Note**: Use about 45g of room temperature water, adjusting based on the dough’s condition to avoid adding too much at once; the dough should be moderately soft.  
4. **Rest the Dough**: Cover with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 1 hour to fully relax the gluten.  
5. **Divide the Dough**: Sprinkle dry flour on the work surface, divide the dough into about 10 equal pieces, roll each into a ball, and cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying.  
6. **Shape the Dough**: Take one dough ball, roll it in dry flour, and flatten it. Use your fingers to thin the edges evenly.  
7. **Roll the Flatbread**: Use a rolling pin to roll the dough ball into a thin, round flatbread, employing a technique of “push with the left hand, pull back with the right” to naturally rotate the bread.  
8. **Cook the Flatbread**:  
   - Preheat the cast iron skillet over medium-low heat, then place the flatbread in it.  
   - After about 30 seconds to 1 minute, when white spots appear on the surface, flip it.  
   - Gently press and rotate the flatbread with a clean towel to ensure even heating until obvious bubbles form.  
   - When the bubbles enlarge and the flatbread is about to puff up, use metal tongs to lift it, flip it, and place it directly over the gas stove’s open flame for roasting.  
   **Key**: The high heat from the open flame allows the flatbread to puff up instantly, creating a hollow center. The total cooking time per flatbread is about 1–2 minutes.  
9. **Cook Another Method**:  
   - Preheat the skillet over medium heat, rolling the flatbread slightly larger.  
   - Follow the same cooking technique as above, finally pressing the flatbread in the skillet to help it puff up fully, using slightly higher heat than the open flame method.  
10. **Post-Cooking Treatment**: Once the flatbread puffs up, remove it immediately and evenly brush the surface with salted butter.  
11. **Keep Warm and Store**: Stack the buttered flatbreads, wrap them in a clean cloth, and place them in a sealed container. This keeps the flatbreads soft even after cooling.  
    **Note**: The steam softens the flatbread, and sealing prevents moisture loss; uncooked dough can be refrigerated, and flatbreads can be directly cooked when needed.

## Health Advice  
Durum flour combined with the scalded dough technique makes the flatbread easier to digest, making it suitable as a staple for those with sensitive stomachs or fitness enthusiasts. Control the amount of butter applied to avoid excessive calorie intake.
