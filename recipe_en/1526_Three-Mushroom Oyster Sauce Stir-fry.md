# Three-Mushroom Oyster Sauce Stir-fry
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 Ingredients
* 🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms 2 pieces
* 🍄 Beech mushrooms 30 g
* 🍄 White beech mushrooms 30 g
* 🌶️ Small chili pepper 1 piece
* 🫑 Sweet bell pepper 0.5 piece
* 🥦 Broccoli 100 g
* 🧅 Scallion 0.5 piece
* 🫚 Ginger (minced) 1 clove

🧂 Seasonings
* 🛢️ Cooking oil 10 ml
* 🧂 Salt 5 g
* 🍶 Cooking wine 2 ml
* 🌾 Cornstarch 10 g
* 🥄 Light soy sauce 10 ml
* 🧂 Chicken bouillon 3 g
* 🦪 Oyster sauce 5 ml
* 💧 Boiling water 350 ml

## Kitchenware
🍳 Pot
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board
🔪 Knife

## Cooking Steps
1. Trim the root ends of beech mushrooms and white beech mushrooms, then separate them into individual pieces.
2. Slice the shiitake mushrooms to a thickness of 0.5–1 cm per slice; thicker slices provide a chewier texture.
3. In a small bowl, combine cornstarch with 50 ml of water, stir until smooth, and set aside as slurry.
4. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the broccoli, blanch for 3 minutes, then drain and arrange at the bottom of a serving bowl.
5. In a fresh pot of boiling water, add 5 g of salt, then blanch the beech mushrooms, white beech mushrooms, and shiitake mushrooms for 1 minute.
6. Drain the mushrooms thoroughly.
7. Heat a clean wok over medium heat, add oil. When small bubbles appear on the oil's surface, add minced ginger, small chili pepper, and sweet bell pepper, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
8. Add the blanched three mushrooms, then sequentially add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken bouillon. Stir-fry evenly, then pour in the prepared cornstarch slurry.
9. Thicken over medium heat. When the sauce becomes glossy and thick, add cooking wine, sprinkle scallions, stir-fry evenly, then remove from heat.
10. Transfer the stir-fried mushrooms into the bowl lined with blanched broccoli. Garnish with the broccoli and serve.

## Health Advice
The combination of three types of mushrooms with broccoli provides low-fat, high-fiber nutrition, suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or for vegetarians. Oyster sauce and light soy sauce already contain significant salt, so you may reduce the additional salt as needed.
