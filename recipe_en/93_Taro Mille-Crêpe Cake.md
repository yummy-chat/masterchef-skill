# Taro Mille-Crêpe Cake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🥚 2 eggs
🍚 About 60g cake flour
🥛 About 270g milk (divided for batter and taro filling)
🧈 About 60g butter (about 25g for batter, about 35g for filling)
🍠 About 300g taro
🟣 About 100g purple sweet potato
🍬 About 60g white sugar (about 30g for batter, about 30g for filling)
🥫 About 30g condensed milk

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔥 Induction cooktop or gas stove
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon
🫗 Strainer or sieve
🍽️ Plate
🔪 Knife, cutting board
♨️ Steamer or steamer oven
🛍️ Ziplock bag or plastic wrap
🎂 Piping bag (for decoration)

## Instructions
### Prepare the Batter
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add about 30g white sugar and about 60g cake flour. Mix until no dry flour remains.
2. Add an appropriate amount of milk and continue mixing until smooth.
3. Melt about 25g butter over hot water, then add it to the batter and mix well. If butter is unavailable, corn oil can be used as a substitute, though the milky aroma will be slightly diminished.
4. Strain the batter through a sieve once to remove lumps, then let it rest for a moment.

### Prepare the Taro Filling
1. Cut the taro and purple sweet potato into small chunks. Steam until fully cooked. Adding extra purple sweet potato will deepen the color.
2. Once cooked, transfer to a bowl. Add about 30g white sugar and about 30g condensed milk. Add an appropriate amount of milk depending on the moisture level.
3. Place the mixture into a ziplock bag. Knead repeatedly with your hands until smooth and evenly pasty.

### Cook the Crêpes
1. Preheat a non-stick pan over the lowest heat. The pan should feel warm when your hand is held above it.
2. Ladle a scoop of batter into the pan. Quickly swirl the pan to spread the batter into a thin, even circle. Pour any excess batter back into the bowl. Stir the batter well again before each ladle.
   **Key Point**: If the batter sizzles and sets immediately upon entering the pan, the pan is too hot; remove it from the heat to cool slightly. If the batter cannot coat the pan and just runs, the pan is not hot enough.
3. Cook until the surface of the crêpe is covered with small bubbles, indicating it is done. Use a toothpick to scrape off any excess batter around the edges for a cleaner, rounder crêpe.
4. Remove the cooked crêpe and lay it flat to cool. After each crêpe, reheat the pan for about 5 seconds before cooking the next one.
5. Repeat the process, stacking the finished crêpes neatly.

### Assemble and Set
1. Line a bowl of suitable size with a layer of plastic wrap.
2. Place one crêpe in the bowl. Spread an even layer of taro filling on top.
3. Cover with another crêpe. Gently press down so the filling spreads evenly.
4. Repeat the process of layering crêpe and taro filling until the bowl is filled.
5. Seal the top with plastic wrap. Freeze for 30 minutes to set.
6. Once set, remove from the freezer. Invert onto a plate to unmold.
7. Optional: Reserve one perfect crêpe to cover the top of the cake. Press it down gently to adhere. Decorate with whipped cream or taro cream flowers using a piping bag.

## Health Advice
Taro and purple sweet potato are rich in dietary fiber, which can help increase satiety and aid digestion, making this a suitable occasional treat. However, note the higher amounts of white sugar and butter used. Those monitoring blood sugar or on a weight-loss plan should control portion sizes.
