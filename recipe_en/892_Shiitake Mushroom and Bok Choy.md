# Shiitake Mushroom and Bok Choy
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps
1.  Slice fresh shiitake mushrooms into thick pieces. Cut large bok choy in half lengthwise. Set aside.
2.  Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add a small amount of salt and cooking oil. Once boiling, add bok choy. Bring to a rolling boil again and immediately remove.
    **Note**: Adding oil and salt helps maintain the bok choy's vibrant green color.
3.  Using the same boiling water, add the shiitake mushroom slices. Cook for 2-3 minutes until softened. Drain and set aside.
4.  Heat a small amount of oil in a clean wok. Quickly stir-fry the blanched bok choy for a few seconds. Remove and arrange neatly on a serving plate.
5.  Add a small amount of lard to the wok. Sauté minced garlic until fragrant. Add the blanched mushrooms and stir-fry until well combined.
6.  Pour light soy sauce and a small amount of cooking wine along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry over high heat until aromatic. Add oyster sauce and stir-fry to combine.
7.  Add a small amount of water. Season with salt, sugar, and chicken bouillon. Add a little dark soy sauce for color. Simmer over medium heat until the sauce thickens slightly.
8.  Once the sauce has reduced, pour in the cornstarch slurry to create a thin glaze. Add a few drops of sesame oil and stir to combine.
9.  Pour the cooked mushrooms and sauce over the arranged bok choy on the plate. Serve.

## Health Advice
This dish uses lard to sauté garlic and oyster sauce for glazing, which enhances flavor. However, lard and oyster sauce contain significant fat and sodium; consume in moderation during weight loss. Adding salt and oil when blanching bok choy preserves its green color, but control sugar and salt amounts in the glaze. Suitable as an occasional treat or to encourage children to eat vegetables.
