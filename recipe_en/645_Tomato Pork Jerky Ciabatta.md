# Tomato Pork Jerky Ciabatta
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour, as needed  
💧 Water, as needed  
🫙 Yeast, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🍅 Tomato paste, a small amount (added during kneading)  
🥓 Pork jerky, as needed (diced)  
🍅 Oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, as needed (halved or whole, with herbs)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🫗 Square glass container (with lid)  
🔔 Dough scraper (plastic)  
🔥 Oven  
📋 Baking tray  
📄 Parchment paper  
🍳 Cast iron pot (for generating steam)  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the dough**: Mix bread flour, water, yeast, and salt, then add a small amount of tomato paste. Stir into a dough. It is recommended to do this the night before for thorough hydration.  
   **Key point**: The dough has a high hydration level. When handling, dust your hands or tools with flour or water to prevent sticking.  
2. **Fold the dough**: Use multiple folding techniques to strengthen the gluten. Fold gently to avoid over-kneading.  
3. **Add the fillings**: During the folding process, evenly scatter the diced pork jerky and oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes.  
4. **Overnight fermentation**: Cover the container and let the dough ferment at room temperature or in the refrigerator overnight until it has significantly expanded and is filled with large air bubbles.  
5. **Shape**: Invert the fermented dough onto a floured work surface. Use a dough scraper to gently divide it into several portions. Dust the surface with additional flour to prevent sticking.  
   **Key point**: After dividing, try to keep the dough’s original shape as much as possible. Avoid overworking it.  
6. **Preheat the oven**: Preheat the oven to a high temperature, and place the empty cast iron pot on the bottom rack to preheat as well.  
7. **Bake**: Place the shaped dough onto a parchment-lined baking tray. Transfer to the middle rack of the oven. Quickly pour about one cup of hot water into the preheated cast iron pot on the bottom rack to generate steam, then immediately close the oven door.  
   **Key point**: Steam is essential for forming a crispy crust.  
8. **Check for doneness**: Bake at high heat until the crust is golden brown, cracked, and the interior is fully cooked. Remove from the oven.  

## Health Advice  
This is a high-carb, high-protein creative bread, suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. Since pork jerky already contains salt and sugar, be sure to reduce the amount of salt added during kneading. A sticky dough with high hydration is normal; there is no need to worry.
