# Maoxuewang (Sichuan Blood Tofu and Tripe Hot Pot)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🩸 Duck blood 500g (mixed with 1000g water and 10g salt to make blood tofu)
🐮 Beef tripe (毛肚) appropriate amount (cut into large pieces)
🥫 Canned luncheon meat appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🥓 Braised pork intestines appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🥩 Pork aorta (黄喉) appropriate amount (cut into comb shape)
🐍 Eel appropriate amount (cut into sections, marinate in advance)
🌱 Soybean sprouts appropriate amount (blanched and used as a base)
🧄 Garlic scapes (青蒜苗) appropriate amount (cut into sections)

🧂 Salt appropriate amount (10g for coagulating blood, a little for blanching bean sprouts)
🍶 Huadiao wine appropriate amount (for marinating eel, blanching ingredients, and seasoning the broth)
🧂 Soy sauce appropriate amount (for marinating eel and seasoning the broth)
🌶️ White pepper powder appropriate amount (for marinating eel)
🧄 Minced garlic appropriate amount (for stir-frying bean sprouts and final oil splashing)
🌶️ Dried chili segments appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base and oil splashing)
🧅🧄 Scallion, ginger, garlic appropriate amount (for stir-frying the broth base)
🌶️ Erjingtiao (Sichuan chili) appropriate amount (soaked in liquor)
🫑 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base and oil splashing)
🌶️ Knife-cut chili (刀口辣椒) appropriate amount
🥘 Pixian bean paste appropriate amount
🧱 Hot pot base (火锅底料) 1 small piece
🫒 Aromatic, traditionally-extracted rapeseed oil appropriate amount
🧈 Beef tallow appropriate amount
♨️ Hot water appropriate amount (for blanching vegetables)
🍲 Beef bone broth appropriate amount (for the soup base)
🫗 Hot oil appropriate amount (for the final pour)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large wok
🍲 Small saucepan (for soaking duck blood)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl/basin
🔥 Stove
🥄 Hot oil ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make blood tofu**: Mix 500g duck blood, 1000g water, and 10g salt. Stir and let it sit undisturbed until it solidifies into blood tofu.
**Key point**: Must be freshly made and used immediately, following the "perfect ratio of water to salt for blood."

2. **Poach duck blood**: Bring water in a pot to 95°C. Gently slide in the cut blood tofu pieces. Keep the heat low and maintain 95°C, poaching for 15-20 minutes until the duck blood is fully cooked with no honeycomb holes inside. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Use low heat throughout; do not boil vigorously, or the texture will become tough and porous.

3. **Prepare ingredients**: Cut beef tripe into large pieces. Cut pork aorta into comb shapes. Cut eel into sections, marinate with Huadiao wine, soy sauce, and white pepper to remove gaminess. Cut luncheon meat and braised pork intestines into pieces.
4. **Stir-fry bean sprouts for the base**: Heat oil in a wok, stir-fry garlic scapes and dried chili segments until fragrant. Add minced garlic and soybean sprouts, stir-fry with a pinch of salt until just cooked. Transfer to the bottom of a serving basin.
5. **Quick-fry eel**: Add more oil to the wok. Quickly stir-fry the marinated eel over high heat until just cooked. Remove and set aside.
6. **Blanch meat ingredients**: Use hot water to quickly blanch the braised pork intestines, luncheon meat, and pork aorta in sequence. For the beef tripe, blanch using the "seven up, eight down" method to remove gaminess quickly. Drain all.
7. **Cook the red soup base**: Heat the wok, add rapeseed oil and beef tallow. Add scallion, ginger, garlic, liquor-soaked Erjingtiao chilies, Pixian bean paste, knife-cut chili, Sichuan peppercorns, and hot pot base. Stir-fry over low heat until the chilies darken and the oil turns a bright red. Splash in Huadiao wine and soy sauce, then pour in the beef bone broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for about 3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
**Note**: In the video, a "mystery ingredient" is added to balance the spiciness, but its name is not disclosed. This can be omitted, or you can add a little sugar or vinegar to taste to balance the flavors.

8. **Cook the main ingredients**: Add the poached duck blood, blanched tripe, luncheon meat, intestines, pork aorta, and quick-fried eel into the red soup. Cook over medium heat for 3 minutes until flavorful. Finally, add the garlic scapes and blanch briefly.
9. **Plate and pour hot oil**: Pour all the ingredients and soup into the basin over the bean sprouts. Sprinkle with minced garlic, dried chili segments, and Sichuan peppercorns. In a separate pot, heat oil until smoking, then pour it evenly over the surface to release the aroma.

## Health Advice
Sichuan-style Maoxuewang is rich in protein and relatively low in fat. Duck blood and beef tripe provide iron and collagen. However, the red oil broth is high in sodium and fat. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for those managing blood sugar; individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake.
