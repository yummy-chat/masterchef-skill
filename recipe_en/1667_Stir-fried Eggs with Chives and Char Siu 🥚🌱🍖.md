# Stir-fried Eggs with Chives and Char Siu 🥚🌱🍖
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings 🥚🌱🍖🧂🍬🌶️🫒
🥚 Eggs 500g (approximately 6-7 eggs)
🌱 Chives (Yellow Chives) as needed (washed and cut into sections)
🍖 Char Siu (BBQ Pork) as needed (pre-sliced)
🍚 Rice as needed (for serving)
🧂 Salt 4.5g (based on 500g eggs)
🍬 Sugar 1.5g
🌶️ White pepper 0.6g
🫒 Cooking oil 9ml (added into egg mixture) + as needed (for stir-frying chives and greasing the pan)

## Cookware and Equipment 🍳🥣🥢🔪🕸️
🍳 Wok
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Spatula
🕸️ Strainer (slotted spoon)

## Cooking Steps
1. ⚡ Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add salt, sugar, and white pepper. Beat thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture forms a continuous stream when lifted.
**Key Point**: The egg mixture must be beaten thoroughly to ensure even seasoning and avoid uneven saltiness.
2. ⚡ Add 9ml of cooking oil to the egg mixture. Stir again to fully incorporate the oil with the eggs.
**Key Point**: The oil helps make the scrambled eggs more tender and smooth. This step should be done after seasoning.
3. ⚡ Wash the chives, cut into sections, and drain well.
4. ⚡ Heat the wok over high heat. Add an appropriate amount of oil. Add the chive sections and stir-fry quickly over high heat for about 30 seconds, until they are just cooked but still crisp. Immediately remove and drain excess liquid using a strainer.
**Note**: Do not overcook the chives, or they will release water, become soft, and lose their crunchy texture.
5. ⚡ Reheat the wok over high heat. Add an appropriate amount of oil to coat the wok evenly.
6. ⚡ Stir the seasoned egg mixture again, then pour it into the hot wok along the edge.
7. ⚡ Immediately stir the egg mixture rapidly in a counterclockwise direction with a spatula, maintaining medium-high heat throughout. This will create layers of tender, smooth egg curds.
**Key Point**: Stir the egg mixture again just before adding it to the wok to prevent the seasonings from settling. Stirring counterclockwise helps form smooth, layered curds.
8. ⚡ When the center of the egg mixture is still runny and most of it has set, quickly add the pre-stir-fried chives. Toss and stir-fry rapidly for a few seconds, then remove from the wok immediately.
**Key Point**: The entire combining process must be extremely quick to prevent the scrambled eggs from becoming overcooked and tough.
9. ⚡ Serve the stir-fried eggs and chives over rice. Arrange sliced char siu on the side. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips ⚕️
This dish is rich in protein. Chives aid digestion, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. Char siu has its own sweet and salty flavor; the salt amount has been controlled, so no additional seasoning is needed. Eggs are most tender when cooked to a runny, creamy consistency, but pregnant women and the elderly should ensure they are fully cooked before eating.
