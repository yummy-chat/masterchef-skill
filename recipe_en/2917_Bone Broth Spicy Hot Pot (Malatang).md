# Bone Broth Spicy Hot Pot (Malatang)  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 Beef fat 100g  
🫒 Rapeseed oil 500g  
🌶️ Dried chili segments 40g (softened in water to make mashed chili)  
🫘 Bean paste (doubanjiang) 120g  
🫘 Fermented black beans (douchi) 20g  
🍬 Rock sugar 5g (ground into powder)  
🌿 Cilantro 10g  
🫚 Ginger 15g  
🧅 Onion slices 50g  
🧅 Scallions 20g  
🫑 Green Sichuan peppercorns 20g  
🌶️ Red Sichuan peppercorns 10g  
🍲 Bone broth spice powder 8g (containing star anise, bay leaves, etc.)  
🍚 Fermented glutinous rice (laozao) 20g  
🦴 Beef bones (as needed, for making broth)  
🥬🍢 Assorted side dishes (vegetables, tofu products, meatballs, etc.)  
🍶 Baijiu (as needed, for soaking Sichuan peppercorns and spice powder)  
🧂 Salt (to taste)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon (to taste)  
🧂 White pepper (to taste)  
🥜 Sesame paste (to taste)  
🧄 Minced garlic (to taste)  
🍶 A little aromatic vinegar  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Soup pot  
🥄 Large soup ladle  
🥄 Strainer  
🫕 Cloth spice bag  
🥣 Large bowl  

## Instructions  
1. **Pre-treat spices**: Mix the green Sichuan peppercorns, red Sichuan peppercorns, and spice powder. Soak with baijiu until moistened to prevent burning during stir-frying.  
2. **Prepare the bone broth**: Put beef bones in a pot with enough water. Simmer until the broth turns rich and milky white. Remove the bones and residue, reserving the broth.  
3. **Prepare the seasoned oil**: In a wok, add beef fat and rapeseed oil. Once melted, add scallion segments, ginger slices, onion slices, and cilantro. Fry over low heat until dry and golden, then remove the solids. The resulting oil is the seasoned oil.  
4. **Stir-fry the base seasoning**:  
   - Lower the oil temperature to about 20-30% heat. Add bean paste and fermented black beans. Stir-fry slowly over low heat until the paste is aromatic and the oil turns a bright red.  
   - Add the mashed chili and stir-fry to loosen. Then add the ground rock sugar to balance the spiciness.  
   - Stir-fry until most of the moisture is gone from the wok. Add the moistened Sichuan peppercorns and spice powder. Continue stir-frying slowly over low heat until the alcohol aroma dissipates and the numbing-spicy fragrance emerges.  
   - Turn off the heat. While still hot, mix in the fermented glutinous rice. Stir evenly and remove from the wok. **Key point**: Let the prepared base seasoning rest and sweat for a while to allow natural fermentation, which enhances the flavor.  
5. **Prepare the soup base**:  
   - In another soup pot, pour in the bone broth. Place the base seasoning in a cloth bag, tie it tightly, and immerse it in the broth.  
   - Simmer over low heat to infuse the flavors into the soup, keeping the soup clear and free of solids.  
   - Once the flavors have melded, season with salt, chicken bouillon, and white pepper. Keep warm over low heat.  
6. **Cook the side dishes**:  
   - Add ingredients in order of cooking time; you can cook each batch separately using the same soup base.  
   - First, add hearty items like tofu products, meatballs, and root vegetables. Then add leafy greens.  
   - Remove the cooked ingredients from the pot.  
7. **Prepare the bowl base and serve**:  
   - In a serving bowl, place sesame paste, minced garlic, and a little aromatic vinegar.  
   - Pour in the cooked ingredients and piping hot soup. The heat will help blend the seasonings. Sprinkle with cilantro and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Bone broth spicy hot pot offers high-quality calcium and dietary fiber from the rich beef bone broth and a variety of vegetables. However, the soup base is high in fat, so it is recommended to prioritize vegetables and tofu products and consume less of the broth itself. Suitable for those who want to warm up and are not concerned about calorie intake. Those with weaker digestion may reduce the spiciness.
