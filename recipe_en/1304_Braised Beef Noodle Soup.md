# Braised Beef Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐂 Beef brisket or sinewy beef 4 jin (cut into thick slices against the grain)
💧 Cold water approx. 16 jin (for blanching; also need enough boiling water for stewing)
🍜 Noodles appropriate amount
🥬 Greens appropriate amount (e.g., spinach)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (cut into rolling knife pieces, prepare extra)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
⚫ Black pepper appropriate amount (recommend generous use)
🫘 Pixian chili bean paste 1 large tablespoon (or Sichuan-style spicy seasoning)
🍶 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add sparingly at first, adjust later during stewing)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Large basin (for soaking beef to remove blood)
🍲 Large pot (for blanching)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok or frying pan
♨️ Pressure cooker (optional)
🍜 Noodle pot
🍚 Bowls, soup ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. After cutting the beef, place it in a large basin and soak in cold water for at least 2 hours to fully draw out the blood.
   **Key point**: Choose beef brisket or a sinewy cut, slice thickly against the grain; avoid cutting along the grain.
2. Cut ginger into rolling knife pieces and set aside; prepare the Sichuan peppercorns and black pepper.
3. Fill a large pot with plenty of cold water, add the soaked beef, bring to a boil over high heat, and continuously stir with a slotted spoon.
   **Key point**: Add a splash of *baijiu* (white liquor) to remove odor; turn off the heat as soon as foam rises and the water is about to boil, to prevent excessive fat loss; remove the beef, rinse thoroughly with warm water, and drain.
4. Heat the wok thoroughly, add oil, and swirl to coat twice to prevent the beef from sticking during stir-frying.
5. Keep the heat at medium-low. First, stir-fry the ginger pieces until the edges turn slightly yellow and fragrant; add Sichuan peppercorns and stir until aromatic; then add Pixian chili bean paste and stir-fry until red oil is released.
6. Add the drained beef and stir-fry evenly. Sprinkle in black pepper, drizzle in light and dark soy sauces, and add a small amount of salt. Stir well after each addition. Continue stir-frying until only oil remains at the bottom of the wok, with no visible liquid.
7. Pour in enough boiling water, bring to a rolling boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and simmer slowly. Simmer in a regular pot for about 2 hours, or in a pressure cooker for 25–30 minutes after reaching pressure, then allow natural pressure release; stew until the beef is tender. Adjust salt as needed during cooking.
   **Key point**: Keep the liquid at a gentle simmer; skim off any foam if necessary at this stage.
8. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil, cook the noodles and greens until done, drain well, and do not rinse with cold water.
9. Place the noodles in a bowl, sprinkle with chopped scallions and cilantro, then ladle the braised beef and hot broth over the top. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Braised beef noodle soup features tender beef and a rich, flavorful broth. However, the combination of 4 jin of beef and 16 jin of water results in a high-calorie dish. It is suitable for those with high physical exertion or those needing to warm up. It is recommended to pair it with greens for balanced nutrition and to consume the broth sparingly to control salt intake.
