# Classic Three-Filling Green Rice Balls
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌿 Fresh mugwort leaves, as needed (first-growth tender shoots)  
🍚 Water-milled glutinous rice flour, 1000 grams  
🌾 Wheat starch, 150 grams  
🍬 White sugar (for the skin), 50 grams  
🫒 Cooking oil (for the skin), 60 milliliters  
🫘 Red beans, 500 grams  
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks, 16 pieces  
🥓 Pork floss, 100 grams  
🍘 Sesame and seaweed crumbs, 80 grams  
🥜 Roasted black and white sesame seeds, 200 grams  
🥜 Crushed peanuts, as needed (optional)  
🧂 Baking soda, 1 teaspoon  
🍬 White sugar (for red bean filling), 100 grams  
🥗 Mayonnaise, 2 tablespoons  
🍬 White sugar (for black sesame filling), 50 grams  
🐖 Lard, 2 tablespoons  
🫒 Cooking oil (for brushing the surface), as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍲 Stockpot  
🌀 Food processor/blender  
♨️ Electric pressure cooker  
🔥 Oven/baking tray  
🥄 Spatula  
♨️ Steamer  
🧺 Bamboo steamer basket  
🖌️ Brush  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
📦 Plastic wrap  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the mugwort**: Wash fresh mugwort leaves. Add baking soda to boiling water, then blanch the leaves for 3-5 minutes until softened. Remove and immediately rinse with cold water, then squeeze out excess water.
**Key point**: Baking soda must be added to maintain a vibrant green color; rinsing with cold water removes bitterness.

2. **Make mugwort puree**: Place the squeezed mugwort into a food processor. Gradually add 300-500 milliliters of water and blend into a smooth puree. Set aside 1000 grams for use.
3. **Make red bean filling**: Cook pre-soaked red beans in an electric pressure cooker until soft and tender. While hot, add white sugar and crush with a spoon, leaving some texture. Let cool, then refrigerate until needed.
4. **Make salted egg yolk and pork floss filling**: Roast salted duck egg yolks until oil appears. Crush them, then mix well with pork floss, sesame and seaweed crumbs, and mayonnaise.
5. **Make black sesame filling**: Grind the roasted black and white sesame seeds in a food processor. Optionally add crushed peanuts, then mix in white sugar and lard until the mixture can be formed into balls.
6. **Knead the dough**: Combine glutinous rice flour, wheat starch, white sugar for the skin, cooking oil, and 1000 grams of mugwort puree. Knead into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: To increase elasticity, you can pre-cook a small piece of dough and knead it into the main dough.

7. **Assemble**: Take a small piece of dough and shape it into a bowl. Add the filling, then pinch the edges closed and roll into a ball. Different fillings can be marked simply for identification.
8. **Steam**: Line the steamer basket with parchment paper and arrange the green rice balls. Steam over boiling water over medium heat for 10 minutes.
**Key point**: The water must be boiling before placing the rice balls in the steamer.

9. **Finish after steaming**: Once the green rice balls are cooked, brush the surface with cooking oil while hot to prevent drying and sticking.
10. **Store**: After the green rice balls have cooled slightly, wrap each one individually in plastic wrap to prevent them from drying out and becoming hard.

## Health Advice  
This green rice ball dish contains glutinous rice flour, three types of sweet fillings, and oil, making it relatively high in calories. It is recommended to eat only 1-2 pieces per serving, paired with green tea to aid digestion. Mugwort is rich in dietary fiber and chlorophyll and has certain antioxidant properties. However, those with weak digestive function should consume it in moderation.
