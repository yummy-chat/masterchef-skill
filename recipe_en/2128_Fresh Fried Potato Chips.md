# Fresh Fried Potato Chips
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 White potatoes, 10 jin (low starch content, more crispy)  
🫘 Soybean oil, 20 jin (for frying)  
🧂 Salt, 20g  
🍬 Powdered sugar, 20g  
🧅 Brewed soy sauce powder, 2g  
🥒 Cucumber powder, 12g  
🧂 Flavor powder (MSG), 18g  
🧂 Disodium 5'-ribonucleotides (I+G), 1g  
🌿 Coriander seed powder, 0.5g  
**Note**: The above seasoning mix is for cucumber flavor. Recipes and ratios for other flavors (original, spicy, barbecue, tomato) are shown in the video; the mixing method is the same. For every 150g of chips, use 8~10g of seasoning (about 4g for original flavor).

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥒 Mandoline slicer  
🪣 Large basins (multiple, for washing, draining, and mixing)  
♨️ Commercial electric deep fryer with temperature control  
🥄 Slotted spoon / skimmer  
🧂 Small glass bowl (for mixing seasoning)  
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for final mixing)  

## Steps  
1. Wash and peel the white potatoes, then slice them into uniform rounds 1.5 to 2 mm thick using a slicer.  
   **Key point**: Ensure even thickness; too thick causes puffing and lack of crispness, too thin leads to breakage.  
2. Place the potato slices in clean water, rinse and change the water 3 to 4 times until the water runs completely clear.  
   **Key point**: Thoroughly wash off surface starch; this is essential for crispness.  
3. Remove the potato slices and thoroughly drain surface water. Do not blanch; set aside directly.  
4. Pour 20 jin of soybean oil into the electric fryer and set the temperature to stabilize at 150°C.  
5. While the oil is heating, pour all the cucumber seasoning powder into a small bowl and mix evenly.  
6. When the oil reaches 150°C, add the drained potato slices, then gently shake and continuously stir with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking.  
   **State**: The potato slices will release dense bubbles upon entering the oil.  
7. Fry at 150°C until the slices are slightly firm, then reduce to low heat to lower and stabilize the oil temperature at 130°C.  
   **Key point**: Reducing heat helps control temperature later and prevents burning.  
8. Continue frying until golden brown, bubbles become small, and chips are fully crispy, then turn off the heat. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.  
9. While still hot, transfer the chips to a stainless steel basin, sprinkle the pre-mixed seasoning in proportion, and quickly toss to coat evenly, ensuring every chip is covered with seasoning powder.  
   **Note**: Toss while hot for better seasoning adherence.

## Health Advice  
This fried chip recipe uses a large amount of oil. It is recommended to make small batches and control the amount of salt and MSG. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood pressure or on a weight loss diet should consume with caution.
