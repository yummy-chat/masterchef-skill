# Pearl Pork Chop
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 One piece of pork loin (remove tendons, cut into slices about 1 cm thick)
🍞 White bread slices, as needed (remove crusts, cut into even small cubes)
🥚 7 eggs (egg whites only)
🧂 Cornstarch, as needed
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed
🧂 Salt, a small amount (sprinkle in two batches)
🌿 Ground pepper, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Round wooden cutting board
🍽️ Flat plates (two, for holding cornstarch and bread cubes)
🥣 Bowl (for whisking egg whites and marinating meat slices)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove tendons from the pork loin and cut into slices about 10 cm long, 5 cm wide, and 1 cm thick, ensuring even thickness.
2. Lightly score a crosshatch pattern on both sides of each slice to tenderize the meat and allow flavors to penetrate.
3. Cut off the hard crusts from the white bread slices and cut them into even small cubes. Set aside.
4. Add cooking wine, a little salt, and ground pepper to the meat slices. Mix well with your hands, then sprinkle a little more salt and mix again. Marinate to infuse flavor.
   **Note**: Add salt in batches and mix well to ensure an even base flavor.
5. Place the egg whites from 7 eggs into a bowl and whisk evenly with chopsticks. Do not whip to stiff peaks.
6. Pour cornstarch onto one flat plate and place the bread cubes on the other. Prepare for a three-step coating process: cornstarch, egg white, bread cubes.
7. First, evenly coat each marinated meat slice with a thin layer of cornstarch. Then dip it into the egg white to coat. Finally, place it onto the bread cubes and gently press with your hands to ensure bread cubes stick well on both sides.
   **Note**: Press slightly when coating with bread cubes to ensure they adhere firmly.
8. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 160°C (320°F). Add the breaded pork chops and fry over medium heat for about 2.5 minutes, until the surface is golden brown, the chops float, and the inside is cooked through.
   **Key point**: Control the oil temperature properly; if too low, the chops will absorb oil; if too high, the outside will burn while the inside remains raw. After frying, remove and drain the oil. No need to re-fry.

## Health Tips
Pearl pork chops are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making them suitable for those who need to supplement protein and energy, such as teenagers or manual laborers. Add salt in batches during marinating to avoid excessive saltiness; control the oil temperature during frying to prevent excessive oil absorption.
