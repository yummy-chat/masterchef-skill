# Shangzhi Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with skin (boneless)
🌿 Shangzhi (wild fern)
🧅 Scallions
🫚 Ginger
⭐ Star anise
🥚 Egg crepe
🍯 Honey
🍶 Vinegar
🍾 Cooking wine
🧂 MSG
🥡 Soy sauce
🧂 Salt
🍗 Chicken broth
🛢️ Sesame oil
🧈 Lard

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥘 Stockpot
🍳 Wok
🍲 Steamer
🪓 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Serving plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Scrape and clean the pork belly with skin. Boil in a stockpot until six-tenths done. Remove and, while still hot, evenly brush the skin with a mixture of honey and vinegar.
**Key Point**: Brushing while hot helps the skin achieve better color, resulting in a bright red hue after frying.

2. Heat lard in a wok over high heat until it reaches 80% hot. Place the pork belly skin-side down and fry until golden red. Remove immediately and soak in the original pork broth until softened. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into pieces three inches long and two-tenths of an inch thick. Arrange the slices skin-side down neatly in a steaming bowl.
**Note**: The oil temperature for frying must be high enough to achieve a golden red skin without burning; sufficient soaking time is needed to soften the meat for easy slicing.

3. Cut 5 grams of scallions into 2.4 cm sections, and another 5 grams into 2.4 cm diagonal slices. Peel and wash the ginger; slice 1.5 grams and mince 5 grams. Cut the egg crepe into isosceles triangles with 2.4 cm sides.
**Note**: The shapes of scallions and ginger should be uniform for easy removal after steaming.

4. Boil the shangzhi in a pot of water until soft. Remove, discard old stems and impurities, wash thoroughly, and cut into 3 cm sections. Place in a bowl, add soy sauce, salt, and lard, mix well, and spread over the meat slices. In a small bowl, combine chicken broth, soy sauce, salt, and cooking wine, stir, and pour into the steaming bowl. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, and star anise. Steam over high heat for about 30 minutes, then reduce to low heat and continue steaming for about 1.5 hours until the meat is tender. Remove, discard the ginger, scallions, and star anise. Strain the reserved liquid, and invert the meat onto a serving plate.
**Key Point**: Sufficient steaming time is crucial to melt the fat in the pork belly, resulting in a soft and sticky texture; placing the meat skin-side down in the bowl ensures a complete presentation when inverted.

5. In a wok, add chicken broth and the strained liquid. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add minced ginger, scallion slices, and MSG, stir well. Add the egg crepe pieces, drizzle with sesame oil, and pour the mixture over the meat on the serving plate.
**Key Point**: The final sauce should be poured quickly while boiling hot to release the aroma.

## Notes
- Shangzhi is a specialty of Shaanxi. If unavailable, other fern vegetables can be used as a substitute.
- Control the oil temperature during frying to ensure the skin turns golden red without burning.
- Ensure sufficient steaming time for the meat to become tender and flavorful.
- When arranging in the bowl, always place the meat skin-side down to maintain a complete shape when inverted.

## Health Advice
This Shangzhi Braised Pork Belly, after frying and prolonged steaming, has its fat largely melted, resulting in a soft and non-greasy texture. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost when consumed in moderation. However, due to its high calorie and sodium content, individuals with hypertension or those managing their weight should consume it sparingly and pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition.
