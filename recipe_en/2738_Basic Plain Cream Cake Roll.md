# Basic Plain Cream Cake Roll
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 6 Eggs
🍬 60g Caster Sugar (for meringue)
🍬 Caster Sugar, as needed (for filling, approximately 8% of cream weight)
🥛 45g Milk (warmed to 60℃)
🌽 50g Corn Oil
🌾 70g Cake Flour
🥄 12g Corn Starch
🍶 120-150g Heavy Cream
🍋 A few drops of Lemon Juice
🌿 A little Vanilla Extract
🧂 A pinch of Salt

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🌀 Electric Hand Mixer
🥄 Hand Whisk
🔪 Silicone Spatula
🍜 Sifter
🍰 28×28cm Square Baking Pan
🔥 Oven
🗑 Dough Scraper
📏 Steel Ruler
📄 Baking Parchment Paper

## Steps
1. **Preparations**
   - Sift together the cake flour and corn starch once to remove lumps.
   - Separate the eggs. Place the egg whites in a clean, grease-free bowl. Discard any watery parts and freeze until ice crystals form on the surface. Set the egg yolks aside.
   - Preheat the oven in advance.

2. **Egg Yolk Batter**
   - Add 50g of corn oil to the egg yolks and whisk with a hand whisk until well combined.
   - Add 45g of warm milk and whisk until combined.
   - Pour in the sifted dry ingredients. Mix using a zigzag or cutting motion until smooth, lump-free, and the batter forms a flowing, ribbon-like consistency.
   - Optional: Add a little vanilla extract and mix well. Set aside.

3. **Meringue**
   - Take the frozen egg whites with ice crystals and add a few drops of lemon juice.
   - Beat on low speed with an electric mixer until large bubbles form, then add all 60g of caster sugar at once.
   - Continue beating on low speed, moving the mixer in a vertical circular motion, until the meringue is glossy and smooth, and when the whisk is lifted, it forms a flexible, large hook shape.
   - Avoid beating on high speed to prevent a coarse texture.

4. **Combining the Batter**
   - Add one-third of the meringue to the egg yolk batter and fold quickly until just combined.
   - Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue. Use a silicone spatula to cut into the batter from the top right of the bowl, then scoop from the bottom and fold, while turning the bowl. Fold until the color is uniform and no streaks of meringue remain.
   - Work quickly and gently to minimize deflating.

5. **Pouring and Baking**
   - Pour the cake batter into the 28×28cm baking pan. Spread it evenly and smooth the surface with a scraper. Gently tap the pan to release large air bubbles.
   - Bake in the preheated oven on the middle rack.
     - Convection oven: 135℃ for about 22 minutes, then switch to 190℃ for about 3 minutes until the surface colors.
     - Conventional oven: 150℃ for about 20 minutes, then switch to 190℃ for about 3 minutes until the surface colors.
   - Remove from the oven as soon as the desired color is achieved.

6. **Post-Baking Handling**
   - Immediately after removing from the oven, tap the pan firmly. Use a dough scraper to loosen the edges of the cake from the pan.
   - Invert the cake onto a cooling rack or clean parchment paper. Peel off the bottom parchment paper.
   - Allow the cake to cool completely, then trim the edges of the cake sheet to prevent cracking when rolling.

7. **Filling and Rolling**
   - Whip the heavy cream with sugar until stiff peaks form.
   - Place the cooled cake sheet on a new piece of parchment paper with the browned side facing down and the textured (skin) side facing up.
   - Spread the whipped cream onto the cake, applying a thicker layer on the side near you and a thinner layer on the far side, creating a slope.
   - Using the parchment paper and a steel ruler, roll the cake sheet up, keeping the paper tight against the cake.
   - Once rolled, wrap it tightly in the paper and refrigerate to set for at least 2 hours.

8. **Slicing and Serving**
   - Remove the set cake roll from the refrigerator. Slice with a hot knife, placing a small piece of parchment paper between slices to prevent sticking.
   - Optionally, dust the surface with a little milk powder or powdered sugar for aroma and decoration.

## Health Advice
This cake roll is rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable as an energy supplement after exercise or a teatime snack. Pay attention to the amount of cream used, and it is advisable to eat no more than two slices at a time to avoid excessive calorie intake.
