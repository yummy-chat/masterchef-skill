# Shredded Pork with Green Peppers and Ham Sausage
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork tenderloin, appropriate amount (shredded)
🫑 Green pepper, appropriate amount (shredded)
🌭 Ham sausage, appropriate amount (sliced)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon (for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for sauce)
🥄 Dark soy sauce, a little (for marinating to add color) + half a tablespoon (for sauce)
🧂 Ground white pepper, appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🛢️ Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for sealing the marinade and frying)
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a tablespoon

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥄 Spoon
🥣 Small bowl (for sauce)

## Steps
1. **Marinate the meat**: Place the shredded pork tenderloin in a bowl. Add 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, a little dark soy sauce, appropriate amount of ground white pepper and cornstarch. Mix thoroughly with your hands. Then drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix again. Let it sit and marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Key points**: Mix thoroughly until the meat shreds are coated with a starchy slurry. Sealing with oil helps lock in moisture, making the meat more tender and preventing it from sticking in the pan.
2. **Prepare the sauce**: In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, half a tablespoon of dark soy sauce, and half a tablespoon of oyster sauce. Stir well and set aside.
3. **Stir-fry the meat**: Heat oil in the wok until warm. Add the marinated meat. Stir-fry quickly over high heat. As soon as the meat changes color completely, immediately remove it from the wok to avoid overcooking.
4. **Stir-fry the vegetables**: Heat oil in the same wok. Add the shredded green pepper and sliced ham sausage. Stir-fry over high heat until the peppers are just cooked and fragrant.
5. **Combine and season**: Return the cooked meat to the wok. Pour the prepared sauce from step 2 along the edge of the wok. Keep the heat high and stir-fry quickly so all ingredients are evenly coated in the sauce.
6. **Serve**: Once everything is well mixed, turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein, but ham sausage and soy sauce have high sodium content. It is recommended that people with high blood pressure reduce the amount of ham sausage or use low-sodium soy sauce. Green peppers are rich in vitamin C, and pairing them with lean meat helps with iron absorption, making this a good choice for a quick, everyday meal.
