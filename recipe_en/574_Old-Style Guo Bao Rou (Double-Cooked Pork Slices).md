# Old-Style Guo Bao Rou (Double-Cooked Pork Slices)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin, appropriate amount (cut into thick slices)
💧 Water, appropriate amount (for rinsing blood from the meat)
🌾 Potato starch, appropriate amount (for coating and batter)
🫒 Soybean oil, appropriate amount (some mixed into the starch batter, most for deep-frying)
🧄 Garlic cloves, sliced, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, julienned, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, julienned, appropriate amount
🥕 Carrots, julienned, appropriate amount
🥚 Egg whites, appropriate amount (for tenderizing the meat)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch
🧅💧 Ginger-scallion water, appropriate amount (to remove gamey flavor and enhance aroma)
🍬 White sugar, 4 tablespoons
🍾 Vinegar, 5 tablespoons
🍶 Cooking wine, a small amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or deep-fry pot
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions
1.  Cut the pork loin into thick slices. Place them in clean water and repeatedly knead and rinse until the water turns cloudy and the slices turn white. Drain and pat dry.
2.  Season the pork slices with egg white, salt, white pepper, and ginger-scallion water. Mix well and let marinate for a short while.
**Key Point**: The egg white makes the meat tender and juicy; the ginger-scallion water removes gamey flavors and enhances aroma.

3.  In a small bowl, prepare the sweet and sour sauce by mixing 4 tablespoons of white sugar with 5 tablespoons of vinegar. Set aside.
**Key Point**: The sugar-to-vinegar ratio of 4:5 is the core of the authentic old-style sweet and sour flavor.

4.  Slice the garlic. Julienne the ginger, scallions, and carrots. Place them together with the cooking wine into a small bowl and set aside.
5.  Coat each marinated pork slice evenly with potato starch. Drizzle in a small amount of soybean oil and gently mix until well combined.
**Key Point**: Must use potato starch. Adding soybean oil is the secret to keeping the coating crispy for a long time.

6.  Pour enough soybean oil into the wok and heat until it reaches 80% hot. Add the pork slices one by one to set the shape. Fry for no more than 30 seconds. Once the slices float and bubbles appear, remove immediately.
7.  Return all the pre-fried pork slices to the 80% hot oil. Fry for about 20 seconds to cook the inside thoroughly.
8.  Maintain high oil temperature and fry the slices a third time for about 10 seconds to force out excess oil and make the coating crispier. Remove and drain.
9.  Leave a small amount of oil in the wok and heat it. Add the julienned ginger, scallions, and sliced garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant. Quickly add the fried pork slices.
10. Pour the prepared sweet and sour sauce along the edge of the wok. Turn the heat to high and quickly toss the wok to stir-fry, allowing the sauce to atomize in the high heat and evenly coat the pork slices. When a large amount of steam rises and the dish appears glossy, remove from the heat immediately.
**Key Point**: Keep the heat high and stir-fry quickly throughout the "pour sauce" step to prevent the pork slices from becoming soft.

## Health Tips
This Guo Bao Rou is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but it is relatively high in calories due to repeated deep-frying at high temperatures. It is suitable as an occasional treat. It is recommended to pair it with a light stir-fried vegetable to balance the meal. Pork loin is rich in protein, but diabetic patients should be mindful of the sugar content in the sweet and sour sauce and may reduce the proportion of white sugar.
