# Zero-Rise Spicy Eggplant Filling Buns
Cuisine: Common Home-Cooked Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 High-gluten or all-purpose flour 1000g
💧 Warm water 550g
🍆 Eggplant appropriate amount (diced)
🫑 Green pepper appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🌶️ Red pepper appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (minced)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🫗 Yeast 8g
🧂 Glucono delta-lactone 10g
🧂 Baking soda 6g
🌶️ Fine chili powder appropriate amount
🫒 Hot oil about one tablespoon
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar a pinch
🍲 Thirteen-spice powder appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍶 Small bowl
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪓 Cutting board
🫓 Rolling pin
🥟 Steamer basket
🍳 Steamer pot

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the fermentation liquid**: Add yeast, glucono delta-lactone, and baking soda to 550g of warm water, and stir until completely dissolved.
2. **Knead the dough**: Gradually pour the mixed liquid into the flour, stirring as you go, and knead into a smooth dough. Once smooth, use immediately without fermentation. This is the key to "zero rise," relying on the reaction of baking soda and glucono delta-lactone with heat to produce gas.
3. **Prepare the filling**: Place the diced eggplant, green and red pepper rings, and minced scallion and ginger into a bowl. Sprinkle fine chili powder on top, drizzle with a tablespoon of hot oil to release aroma, and quickly mix until the vegetables are coated with oil. Then add salt, chicken bouillon, a pinch of sugar, and thirteen-spice powder, and mix evenly. Mixing with hot oil first seals in the moisture of the vegetables, preventing water release after adding salt.
4. **Form the wrappers and fill**: Take a piece of dough, roll it into a long strip, and cut it into evenly sized pieces. Flatten each piece and roll it into a wrapper that is thick in the center and thin at the edges. Fill with the filling, pleat and seal the edges. Work directly without resting the dough, to prevent the wrappers from fermenting prematurely.
5. **Steam**: Bring water to a boil in the steamer pot. Line the steamer basket with a damp cloth, place the raw buns inside, and cover with the lid. Once steam rises, steam over high heat for 12 minutes. Be sure to place the buns in the steamer only after the water is boiling, and maintain high heat, so that the glucono delta-lactone and baking soda react quickly, making the buns fluffy.
6. **Remove from steamer**: After 12 minutes, turn off the heat and remove the lid directly. The buns should be white, puffy, and well-risen.

## Health Tips
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber, and combined with vitamin C from green and red peppers, it can help balance the greasiness. However, since the filling is flavored with hot oil, it is relatively high in calories and suitable as an occasional "cheat meal." People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt, and those with diabetes should control their serving size.
