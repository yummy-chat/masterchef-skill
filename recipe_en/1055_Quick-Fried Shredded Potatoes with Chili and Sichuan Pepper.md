# Quick-Fried Shredded Potatoes with Chili and Sichuan Pepper
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato, about 400g (1 piece)
🧄 Garlic, 2 cloves (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste
🫒 Green Sichuan peppercorns, a few
🧅 Spring onions, a few (cut into sections)
🧂 Table salt, 1.5g
🍬 White sugar, a pinch
🍶 Light soy sauce, 3g
🍎 White vinegar, a little (for blanching)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Wok spatula
🥅 Strainer
🔥 Gas stove

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the potato, first slice it thinly, then cut into evenly thick shreds.
   **Key Point**: When cutting the potato, first slice off a small piece to create a flat base for stability. The shreds should not be too thin, otherwise they won't hold up during quick frying.
2. Place the shredded potatoes in clean water and rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear.
   **Key Point**: The starch must be thoroughly washed off; this is key to ensuring a crispy texture.
3. Slice the garlic into thin pieces, cut the dried chili peppers into sections, prepare the green Sichuan peppercorns, and cut the spring onions into sections.
4. Bring a pot of water to a boil over high heat, add a little white vinegar, then add the shredded potatoes. Blanch over medium heat for about 20 seconds.
   **Key Point**: Adding vinegar before blanching helps maintain a crisp texture; remove the shreds as soon as they turn slightly translucent and drain well, avoiding prolonged blanching.
5. Heat the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil, and heat until it reaches about 60% hot (180°C/350°F).
6. Add the dried chili peppers, sliced garlic, and green Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
   **Key Point**: Add the aromatics when the oil is at 60% heat to prevent the chili peppers from burning.
7. Once the aromatics are fragrant, turn the heat to high, add the blanched shredded potatoes, and stir-fry quickly for about 15 seconds until just cooked.
8. Add the table salt and a pinch of white sugar, then drizzle the light soy sauce along the edge of the wok.
   **Key Point**: Pour the soy sauce along the wok edge to use the high heat to bring out its aroma.
9. Add the spring onion sections, turn to medium heat, and stir-fry quickly until evenly mixed. Immediately remove from the wok and plate.
   **Key Point**: Do not stir-fry the shredded potatoes for too long, or they will become soft and lose their appearance.

## Health Advice
This quick-fried shredded potato dish is low in fat and crisp, suitable for those looking to control calorie intake or increase dietary fiber. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt.
