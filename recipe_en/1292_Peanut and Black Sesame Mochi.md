# Peanut and Black Sesame Mochi  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Peanuts 150g (with red skin)  
⚫️ Black sesame seeds 30g  
🍬 White sugar 60g (30g for filling, 30g for batter)  
🥛 Whole milk 250ml  
🫒 Cooking oil 25g  
🌽 Cornstarch 40g  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 200g  
🥥 Shredded coconut or cooked glutinous rice flour, as needed (for coating)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan/wok  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls (multiple)  
🥚 Whisk  
🍽️ Plate (flat)  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Place the peanuts in a cold pan. Stir over high heat continuously until the peanut skins can be easily rubbed off. Remove from heat and let cool.  
   **Note**: The skins should be easy to rub off when done.  
2. Toast the black sesame seeds in a pan until fragrant, then remove.  
3. Let the peanuts cool, then rub off the red skins and blow away any residue. Place on a cutting board and crush with a rolling pin.  
   **Key point**: Do not crush too finely; leaving some texture improves the mouthfeel.  
4. Combine the crushed peanuts, black sesame seeds, and 30g of white sugar in a bowl. Mix well to form the filling. Set aside.  
5. Prepare a small bowl with an appropriate amount of cold boiled water and set aside.  
6. Make the batter: In a bowl, combine the whole milk, 30g of white sugar, and cooking oil. Whisk until well blended.  
7. Add the cornstarch and mix well. Then add the glutinous rice flour and continue stirring until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.  
   **Note**: Adding cornstarch reduces stickiness and gives the finished product a more translucent appearance.  
8. Pour the batter into a flat plate and spread evenly. Place in a steamer over boiling water, cover, and steam over medium heat for 20 minutes.  
9. Remove the steamed dough and place it on a cutting board. Beat it repeatedly with a rolling pin until it becomes very elastic and can be stretched into long strands.  
10. Dip your hands in cold boiled water to prevent sticking. Roll the dough into a long log and divide it evenly into 8 portions.  
11. Take one portion, flatten it, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, and wrap it using the technique for making buns. Shape into a ball.  
    **Note**: Keep your hands moist with cold boiled water throughout to prevent the dough from sticking.  
12. Roll the finished mochi in shredded coconut or cooked glutinous rice flour to coat evenly.  

## Health Tips  
Mochi is rich in carbohydrates, providing a quick source of energy, but it also contains high amounts of sugar and fat. It is best enjoyed after exercise or as an occasional snack. Peanuts and sesame seeds offer high-quality fats and vitamin E, but portion control is important to avoid excessive calorie intake.
