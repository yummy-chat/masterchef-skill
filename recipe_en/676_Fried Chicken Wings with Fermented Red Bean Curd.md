# Fried Chicken Wings with Fermented Red Bean Curd
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken whole wings, about 16 pieces (can be substituted with chicken mid-wings)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (smashed)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (smashed)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (smashed)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, appropriate amount (smashed)
🫘 White sesame seeds, one tablespoon
🧂 White pepper powder, two grams
🧂 Chicken powder, two tablespoons
🍬 White sugar, one small tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce, two tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce, one tablespoon
🍶 High-proof liquor (or cooking wine, rice wine), two tablespoons
🥘 Fermented red bean curd sauce, three tablespoons
🫒 Tea oil (or peanut oil, salad oil, olive oil), 50 grams
🌾 Flour, 300 grams
🍺 Beer, 750 milliliters

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫙 Sealed bag
🥢 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Steps
1. Make a few cuts on the thicker parts of the chicken whole wings to allow the marinade to penetrate.
2. Place the smashed scallion, ginger, garlic, and bird's eye chili in a sealed bag. Add white pepper powder, chicken powder, white sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, high-proof liquor, fermented red bean curd sauce, and tea oil.
3. Put the chicken wings into the sealed bag, expel the air, seal the bag, and rub with your hands to evenly coat the wings with the marinade.
4. Refrigerate and marinate overnight to ensure thorough flavor absorption.
5. In a bowl, mix the flour, beer, and white sesame seeds, stirring into a thick, moderately runny batter.
6. Remove the marinated chicken wings and thoroughly pat dry the surface with kitchen paper towels to remove excess fermented red bean curd sauce and moisture.
**Note**: If too much of the fermented red bean curd sauce remains on the surface, it may turn dark during frying, affecting the final color.
7. Dip the chicken wings into the batter, coating them evenly with a thin layer. Avoid applying too thick a batter.
8. Pour oil into a wok and heat to 350°F (180°C). Add the chicken wings and fry over low heat until done. Fry whole wings for about 12 minutes, or mid-wings for about 8 minutes, until the coating is golden and crispy. Remove and drain on a plate.

## Health Advice
This dish of fried chicken wings with fermented red bean curd is rich in protein, but the frying process makes it high in calories. It is suitable for occasional consumption, and pairing it with vegetables is recommended to balance nutrition. While the fermented red bean curd adds flavor, it is also high in sodium, so people with hypertension should consume it in moderation.
