# Sour Cabbage with Pork Slices
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Baby cabbage (cabbage) 1 piece
🥩 Lean meat 1 small piece (sliced against the grain)
🥔 Potato starch appropriate amount (a bit more)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (some sliced, some minced)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (some in sections, some shredded)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (some sliced, some minced)
🌶️ Red chili peppers 2-3 pieces (some shredded, some for oil splashing)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, dried Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (soaked in water beforehand)
🧂 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 MSG 1 tablespoon
🧂 Sugar appropriate amount
🧂 Chili powder appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon, soup ladle
🥣 Bowls (for holding meat slices, marinating)
🍽️ Large bowl (for finished dish)
🥘 Small pot (for heating oil)

## Steps
1.  Slice the lean meat thinly against the grain. Add 1 tablespoon of salt and an appropriate amount of potato starch. Mix for about 1 minute until evenly coated and sticky. Then drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil and toss to separate.
**Key point**: Use slightly more starch. Mix until the meat surface becomes sticky, which helps lock in moisture.

2.  In a bowl, combine minced garlic, minced ginger, shredded red chili pepper, and chopped white scallion parts. Pour in one spoonful of boiling oil to release the fragrance. Then immediately add steamed fish soy sauce, a little sugar, and aged vinegar. Stir to combine into a poaching sauce. Set aside.
**Note**: The oil temperature must be high enough to fully release the aroma of the spices.

3.  Pour oil into a wok. Over medium-low heat, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and 1 tablespoon of doubanjiang. Stir-fry until the oil turns red.
4.  Add the cabbage stems and turn the heat to high. Stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in boiling water until the cabbage is just covered. Add salt, 1 tablespoon of MSG, and an appropriate amount of sugar to season. Bring to a boil and cook for 1 minute.
5.  Use a slotted spoon to remove the cabbage and spread it at the bottom of a large serving bowl.
6.  Keep the soup in the wok boiling. Slide the marinated meat slices in one by one. Boil for about 2 minutes until the meat changes color and the starch has set.
7.  Pour the meat slices and the soup into the large bowl over the cabbage.
8.  Sprinkle minced garlic, scallion sections, shredded red chili peppers, and chili powder over the meat slices.
9.  Heat oil in a separate small pot until it smokes. Add the pre-soaked dried chili peppers and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Fry until fragrant and slightly charred. Quickly pour the oil and spices over the seasonings in the bowl to release the aroma. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish uses lean meat paired with cabbage, making it low in fat and high in protein, suitable for those looking to reduce fat and control oil intake. Marinating the meat with potato starch locks in moisture and reduces oil absorption, but be mindful of the calories from the doubanjiang and the oil splash. Cabbage is rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion.
