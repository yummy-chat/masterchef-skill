# Tomato Hot Pot Base
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 Tomato About 2 (requires pre-freezing treatment)
🥫 Tomato paste To taste
🌿 Bay leaves To taste
🧅 Scallion and ginger To taste
🧂 Salt To taste
🍬 Sugar To taste
🦪 Oyster sauce To taste
🫒 Vegetable oil To taste (for stir-frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
⏲️ Timer
🫙 Sealable bag
🔥 Gas stove

## Steps
1. Take out the pre-frozen tomatoes, place them in a glass bowl, and pour hot water to soak. Once the skin naturally lifts, peel off the skin.
   **Note**: Soaking frozen tomatoes in hot water quickly removes the skin, and the juice is less likely to be lost when cutting.
2. Cut the peeled frozen tomatoes into evenly sized small pieces.
   **Key point**: When cutting frozen tomatoes, less juice is lost, making it easier to release the pulp during subsequent stir-frying.
3. Heat the non-stick pan on the stove, pour in an appropriate amount of vegetable oil, add bay leaves, scallion, and ginger, and stir-fry until fragrant.
4. Add the frozen tomato pieces and tomato paste, and stir-fry evenly.
   **Heat and state**: About 1 minute is enough; the frozen tomatoes release pulp very quickly. Stir-fry until completely thickened into a paste.
5. Add salt, sugar, and oyster sauce to the pan, and stir-fry evenly to season.
   **Key point**: Seasoning should be simple; no complex spices are needed.
6. Reduce the heat to low and continue simmering until red oil appears on the surface of the base.
   **State assessment**: The process is complete when a shiny red oil layer forms on the surface.
7. After cooling the simmered base, divide it into sealable bags and store them in the freezer.
   **When serving**: Take out the base and mix it with water to create the tomato hot pot broth.

## Health Advice
Tomatoes are rich in vitamin C and lycopene, suitable for people aiming to lose weight or protect their eyes. It is recommended to use less oil and salt, and fully utilize the natural sweet and sour flavor of tomatoes to reduce reliance on seasonings.
