# Shrimp and Sea Cucumber Mapo Tofu  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 Silken tofu 1 box (cut into 2 cm cubes)  
🦐 Shrimp about 100 g (cut into large cubes)  
🦪 Ready-to-eat sea cucumber 2 pieces (cut into large cubes)  
🥩 Ground meat as needed  
🧅 Scallion white as needed (minced)  
🧄 Ginger as needed (minced)  
🧄 Garlic as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Broad bean paste as needed  
🫘 Fermented black beans a pinch  
🧂 Salt as needed  
🍬 Sugar about 5 g (used twice, once for blanching to remove fishiness, once for seasoning)  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper about 8 g (part for seasoning, part for sprinkling on top)  
🧂 Ground white pepper about 3 g  
🧂 Light soy sauce about 10 g  
🫕 Chili oil as needed (drizzled on top before serving)  
🌿 Garlic sprouts/green garlic about 10 g (minced for garnish)  
🧴 Cornstarch slurry as needed (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥘 Strainer/skimmer  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥣 Bowls, plates  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add a pinch of salt and sugar, then blanch the tofu cubes, shrimp cubes, and sea cucumber cubes together for about 1 minute. Drain well.  
**Key point**: Blanching removes the beany taste of tofu and partially cooks the shrimp and sea cucumber. The sugar helps eliminate fishy odors.  

2. Heat oil in another wok over medium heat. Add the ground meat and stir-fry until it separates and becomes fragrant. Add the broad bean paste and fry until red oil is released, then add the minced scallion, ginger, garlic, and fermented black beans. Stir-fry until aromatic.  
**Note**: The broad bean paste must be fully fried to release enough red oil; this ensures the dish has a bright red color.  

3. Add an appropriate amount of water. Bring to a boil, then season with sugar, ground white pepper, ground Sichuan pepper, and light soy sauce.  

4. First add the blanched sea cucumber and shrimp cubes, then gently add the tofu cubes. Simmer over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes, allowing the ingredients to fully absorb the flavors.  
**Key point**: After adding the tofu, minimize stirring. Gently move the wok by shaking it to prevent the tofu from breaking apart.  

5. Add the cornstarch slurry in batches. Gently push the mixture with the bottom of the spatula to evenly coat the ingredients. Cook until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, then transfer to a serving plate.  

6. Evenly sprinkle the remaining ground Sichuan pepper on top, garnish with minced garlic sprouts, and finally drizzle with hot chili oil to release the aroma. Serve immediately.  
**Key point**: The Sichuan pepper and chili oil must be added at the very end to preserve the numbing sensation and spicy aroma.  

## Health Tips  
This dish combines ingredients from both sea and land. Tofu provides high-quality plant protein, while shrimp and sea cucumber add low-fat animal protein, making it suitable for those needing a high-protein, low-fat diet. Note that broad bean paste and fermented black beans are high in salt; people with high blood pressure should limit consumption of the sauce.
