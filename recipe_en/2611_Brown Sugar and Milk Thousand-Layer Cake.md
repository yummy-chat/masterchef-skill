# Brown Sugar and Milk Thousand-Layer Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Water Chestnut Flour 200g (divided into two portions of 100g each)  
🍬 Brown Sugar 120g  
🥛 Whole Milk 280g  
💧 Cold Water 280g  
🍚 White Sugar 30g (optional, to enhance sweetness)  
🫒 Cooking Oil as needed (to grease the mold)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍲 Small Saucepan  
♨️ Steamer  
🍽️ Heat-Resistant Mold (glass or metal)  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🪥 Strainer  
🥢 Chopsticks or Whisk  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. Add cold water and brown sugar to the saucepan, stirring continuously until the brown sugar is completely dissolved and the mixture boils. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
2. Add water chestnut flour to the cooled brown sugar water, stirring thoroughly until no lumps remain. Strain the mixture through a strainer.
3. In another bowl, mix the whole milk with water chestnut flour, stirring until well combined. Add white sugar according to preference, then strain the mixture through a strainer as well.
4. Bring the water in the steamer to a boil over high heat. Grease the inner surface of the mold with a layer of cooking oil to prevent sticking.
5. Reduce the heat to medium, pour an appropriate amount of the brown sugar batter into the mold, spreading it to a thickness of about 0.5 cm. Cover with a lid and steam for approximately 5 minutes, until the surface is completely set.
   **Note**: Stir the corresponding batter well before adding each layer. If the lid tends to drip water, cover the mold with a plate or plastic wrap to prevent water droplets from affecting the surface smoothness.
6. Continue pouring the batter in alternating layers of brown sugar batter and milk batter, steaming each layer for about 5 minutes, until the final layer. The top layer must be made with brown sugar batter for a better color appearance.
7. After steaming, remove the cake and let it cool completely. Run a small knife along the inner wall of the mold, then invert it to unmold.
8. Cut into pieces or slices and serve.

## Health Advice
This brown sugar and milk thousand-layer cake uses water chestnut flour as a substitute for high-calorie starches, and the steaming process involves minimal oil with no deep frying, making it suitable for those looking to control calorie intake. Note that while brown sugar contains iron, it is also high in sugar. During weight loss, it is advisable to cut it into smaller portions for consumption.
