# Laba Congee (Eight Treasures Porridge)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice
🍚 Glutinous Rice
🥜 Peanuts
🫘 Red Beans
🫘 Red Dates
🌾 Coix Seed
🖤 Black Rice
🌾 Millet
🌰 Lotus Seeds
🫘 Mung Beans
🫘 Soybeans
🫘 Peas
🫘 Kidney Beans
🍇 Longan
🌰 Chestnuts
🥜 Walnuts
🍇 Raisins
🧂 Rock Sugar
🧂 White Sugar

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Porridge Pot
🔥 Pressure Cooker
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥣 Small Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the mung beans, red beans, peanuts, soybeans, peas, and kidney beans. Place them in a glass bowl and add fresh water until it reaches 3/4 of the bowl's height. Soak for over 8 hours.
   **Key Point**: Insufficient soaking time makes beans difficult to cook until soft; extend time as necessary.

2. Wash the rice, glutinous rice, coix seed, black rice, millet, and lotus seeds. Place them in another glass bowl and add fresh water until it reaches 3/4 of the bowl's height. Soak for 3 hours.
   **Key Point**: Grains require a shorter soaking time than beans to ensure they finish cooking at the same time.

3. Discard the soaking water from step 1. Pour the beans into the porridge pot and add 1 liter of drinking water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately cover and reduce heat to low. Cook for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: After boiling, promptly reduce heat and stir occasionally to prevent overflow or scorching.

4. Discard the soaking water from step 2. Pour the grains into the porridge pot, cover, and continue cooking over low heat for 60 minutes.
   **Note**: Stir gently every 15 minutes during cooking to ensure even heating; if the water level drops below the ingredients, add hot water promptly.

5. Wash the red dates, longan, chestnuts, walnuts, and raisins. Remove pits from the red dates and slice them. Add these along with the rock sugar to the pot, cover, and continue cooking over low heat for another 60 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Add dried fruits and rock sugar at the end so their sweetness fully integrates into the porridge while retaining the fruit texture.

6. Once the porridge is thick and the beans and grains are completely soft, turn off the heat. Ladle into small bowls for serving.
   **Key Point**: If using a pressure cooker, steps 3 through 5 can be combined under pressure, reducing total cooking time to about 30 minutes. Follow the equipment instructions.

## Health Advice
Laba congee is rich in dietary fiber and various vitamins, making it ideal for warming the body in winter. However, diabetics should control the amount of rock sugar used.
