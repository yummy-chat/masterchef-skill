# Home-Style Stir-Fried Cured Pork
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 1600g (3 strips, washed)  
🌿 Star anise 3 pieces  
🌿 Bay leaves 3 pieces  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 10g  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 2g  
🫑 Green bell pepper to taste (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Scallion to taste (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (minced)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (minced)  
🍬 White sugar 30g  
🍾 Dark soy sauce 40g  
🍾 Light soy sauce 100g  
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor) 40g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stockpot  
✂️ Scissors  
🪢 String  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🪣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  

## Steps

**Curing and Air-Drying**
1. Place the pork belly in the stockpot, and add star anise, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, white sugar, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and baijiu.
   **Key point**: The seasoning ratios must be accurate; the baijiu removes odor and acts as a preservative.
2. Rub and massage repeatedly with your hands to ensure every part of the pork belly is evenly coated with the seasonings.
3. Cover the pot and refrigerate for 3 days to marinate, turning the meat once during this time to ensure even flavor absorption.
4. Remove the marinated meat. Use scissors to poke a hole at one end of the meat, thread the string through, and tie it tightly.
5. Hang in a cool, well-ventilated place to air-dry for about 15 days.
   **Texture check**: The meat becomes hard and dry, with no visible moisture on the surface, and turns a deep red color.

**Steaming and Stir-Frying**
6. Take the air-dried cured pork and wash it multiple times in warm water to remove surface dust and impurities.
7. Place the cured pork in a steamer. Steam over high heat until boiling, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes.
   **Texture check**: The cured pork becomes soft when done.
   **Key point**: Steaming softens the cured pork, making it easier to slice and allowing it to absorb flavors better during stir-frying.
8. Remove the steamed cured pork and slice it thinly.
9. Heat the wok over high heat, add oil, and stir-fry the chopped scallion, minced ginger, and minced garlic until fragrant.
10. Add the sliced cured pork. Stir-fry over medium heat until the fat renders and the aroma of the cured pork is fully released.
11. Add the green bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry until well combined and the peppers are just cooked through. Remove from heat and serve.

## Health Tips  
Cured pork is flavorful but high in salt. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables rich in vitamin C, such as green bell peppers, and to avoid adding extra salt. Those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. Steaming for 15 minutes softens the meat and reduces some nitrites. Washing thoroughly before cooking helps reduce salt content.
