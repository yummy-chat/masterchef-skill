# Honey Oat Bread
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 405g (75g for starter, 330g for main dough)
💧 Water 268g (100g for starter, 168g for main dough)
🍯 Honey 11g
🧂 Granulated sugar 7g
🧂 Salt 6g
🧈 Unsalted butter 24g
🍞 Fresh yeast 9g (or dry yeast 3g, with 3g/1g for starter, 6g/2g for main dough)
🌾 Cooked rolled oats 65g (25g for starter, about 40g for surface decoration)
🐟 Canned tuna in oil 100g (drained)
🌽 Sweet corn kernels, as needed
🧅 Onion, as needed (sliced)
🍅 Tomato, as needed (sliced)
🥒 Pickled cucumber, as needed (sliced)
🧀 Cheese, as needed (grated)
🥄 Mayonnaise, as needed

## Tools and Equipment
⚖️ Digital scale
🥛 Measuring cup
🔪 Dough scraper and bench scraper
🍞 Rolling pin
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer
⏱️ Timer
🫙 Plastic wrap
💨 Spray bottle or damp cloth
✂️ Scissors
🔥 Oven (convection or conventional)
📄 Baking sheet
🌫️ Cooling rack
🪚 Serrated knife
📜 Parchment paper
🧀 Grater

## Instructions
### Honey Oat Starter
1.  Mix water, honey, and fresh yeast until well combined.
2.  Add the cooked rolled oats and let soak until fully hydrated.
3.  Add bread flour and stir into a smooth batter with no dry flour.
4.  Ferment at room temperature for about 1 hour, until doubled in volume and honeycombed inside.

### Main Dough and Kneading
1.  Mix bread flour and granulated sugar in a bowl, making a well in the center.
2.  Pour in water, fresh yeast, and all of the fermented starter. First mix the liquids, then incorporate the flour, cutting until no dry flour remains.
3.  Add softened butter and salt. Continue to cut and knead until the butter is fully absorbed.
4.  Continue kneading and slapping the dough, monitoring its temperature. If it exceeds 26°C, wrap it in plastic wrap and freeze for 5 minutes to cool, then resume kneading.
5.  Knead until the surface is smooth and a thin, unbreakable membrane can be stretched.

### First Fermentation
Shape the dough into a round, smooth side down. Ferment at room temperature for 60 minutes, until doubled in volume.
### Dividing, Rounding, and Bench Resting
1.  Press the dough to release gas and divide it into 3 equal portions, about 240g each.
2.  Keep the smooth surface, tuck the rough sides inside, and round the dough, gently patting out large air bubbles.
3.  Bench rest for 25 minutes.

### Shaping and Final Fermentation
1.  Take a rested dough piece. Flatten it and roll it into a thin sheet, narrower at the top and wider at the bottom.
2.  Flip it over so the smooth side faces down. Roll it up from the top, pressing and sealing with your fingers as you go. Roll it out to about 30cm long.
3.  Spray or moisten the surface of the shaped dough. Evenly coat with the cooked rolled oats. Use scissors to make 5 lengthwise cuts.
4.  Place on a baking sheet and ferment at room temperature until doubled in volume.

### Baking
-   Convection oven: Bake at 200°C for 11 minutes
-   Conventional oven: Bake at 220°C for 14 minutes
Immediately after baking, transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely. Once cooled to hand temperature, it can be wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and frozen.

### Sandwich Assembly and Reheating
1.  Drain the tuna and mix with corn kernels and mayonnaise.
2.  Slice the bread horizontally or vertically. Fill with the tuna salad, onion slices, tomato slices, pickled cucumber slices, and grated cheese.
3.  Place in an oven preheated to 200°C and bake for 5 minutes, until the bread is crisp and the filling is warm.
4.  Wrap in parchment paper, slice lengthwise, and serve.

## Health Advice
Honey oat bread is rich in dietary fiber and quality carbohydrates, making it suitable as an energy boost after exercise. Tuna and cheese provide protein and calcium. Note that mayonnaise is relatively high in calories; it is recommended to consume in moderation.
