# Spicy Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken wings (drumettes) 500g
🥚 Whole eggs 2 (for extra crispiness, use only egg yolks + water)
🌾 Tapioca starch 100-110g (for batter)
🍚 Cake flour 120g (for dry coating)
🌽 Cornstarch 60g (for dry coating)
🧂 Salt (for marinating) 6g
🌶️ Chili powder (for marinating) 3g (mildly spicy, increase to 6g for more heat)
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder (for marinating) 2g
🧂 Five-spice powder (for marinating) 1g (do not use too much to avoid a medicinal taste)
🧂 Black pepper (for marinating) 2g
🧄 Garlic powder (for marinating) 2g
🧂 Salt (for coating) 4g
🌶️ Chili powder (for coating) 3g
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder (for coating) 3g
🧄 Garlic powder (for coating) 3g
🧂 Black pepper (for coating) 3g
🌶️ Chili powder (for dusting) to taste
🧂 Black pepper (for dusting) to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating and making batter)
🍽️ Square baking sheet or shallow dish (for mixing dry coating)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for precise measurement of seasonings)
🍳 Deep frying pot (for first fry)
🍳 Stainless steel pot (for second fry)
🥢 Tongs (for placing and removing chicken pieces)
🧻 Paper towels (for draining excess oil)

## Instructions
1. Prepare the chicken wings: Use the tip of a knife to poke several small holes in the surface of each wing to allow the marinade to penetrate.
2. Marinate: Place the chicken wings in a bowl. Add the salt, Sichuan peppercorn powder, five-spice powder, black pepper, chili powder, and garlic powder for marinating. Rub and mix thoroughly. Let rest for 30 minutes.
3. Make the batter: Gradually add the tapioca starch and whole eggs to the marinated chicken wings. Stir until the mixture forms a thick, paste-like consistency that evenly coats the chicken. If too dry, add 5-10g of water; if too wet, add a bit more starch.
4. Prepare the dry coating: In a square dish, combine the cake flour, cornstarch, and the chili powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper for coating. Mix well.
5. Coat the chicken: Place the battered chicken wings into the dry coating. Use your hands to cover and press firmly so the powder adheres well. Gently shake off any excess dry mixture.
6. First fry: Heat the oil to 140°C. Add the chicken pieces and fry for 3-4 minutes, until they float, the coating is set, and they are fully cooked through. Remove from the oil.
   **Note**: Do not add too many chicken pieces at once to avoid a sudden drop in oil temperature.
7. Second fry: Increase the oil temperature to 180°C. Add the first-fried chicken pieces and fry for about 30 seconds, until the surface turns golden brown and the coating is crispy. Remove.
   **Note**: The second fry is the key step for achieving a crispy coating. Do not fry for too long.
8. Remove and final seasoning: Place the twice-fried chicken pieces on paper towels to drain excess oil. Before serving, sprinkle with chili powder and black pepper to taste.

## Health Advice
This spicy fried chicken is high in calories and sodium. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad or pickled cucumbers to balance the greasiness. Suitable for occasional indulgence. People with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should control their portion size.
