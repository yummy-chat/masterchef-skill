# Healthy Fried Dough Sticks (You Tiao)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 1000g (2 jin) all-purpose flour
🍞 10g yeast
🧪 5g baking soda
🧂 10g salt
🍬 8g granulated sugar
🥚 2 eggs
🫒 30g cooking oil (for the dough)
💧 500g water
🫒 Sufficient vegetable oil (for deep frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍽️ Small dish (for holding pre-measured dry ingredients)
🥢 Chopsticks
🫖 Plastic wrap
Rolling pin (rolling stick)
🔪 Kitchen knife
🍳 Large iron wok
🥢 Long chopsticks (for turning the fried dough sticks while frying)

## Cooking Steps
1. Add all-purpose flour, yeast, salt, sugar, and baking soda to a stainless steel bowl. Mix with your hands until evenly combined.
2. Crack in two eggs and stir until the egg liquid is fully incorporated with the dry ingredients. Add 30g of cooking oil and continue to mix evenly.
3. Gradually add 500g of water in batches, stirring as you go, until the flour becomes flocculent with no dry flour remaining.
4. Use a "pressing and folding" technique to press the dough into a ball. Avoid excessive kneading to prevent the dough from developing too much gluten, which would hinder its ability to expand.
5. Brush a thin layer of cooking oil over the surface of the dough. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap.
6. **Proof the dough**: Proof at room temperature – about 1 hour in warm weather, about 2 hours in cool weather. Alternatively, you can proof it overnight in the refrigerator for use the next day. The dough is ready when it has softened and its extensibility has noticeably improved.
7. Dust a work surface with dry flour to prevent sticking. Remove the proofed dough and gently stretch and press it into a long strip.
8. Use a rolling pin to roll the dough into a rectangular sheet of even thickness.
9. Use a knife to cut the dough sheet into even strips.
10. Take two strips for one portion. Use chopsticks dipped in water to draw a wet line down the center of one strip, then place the second strip on top. Press down firmly in the center with the chopsticks to make the two strips stick together securely.
11. Hold both ends of the combined dough strips and gently and evenly stretch them to a suitable length.
12. Pour sufficient vegetable oil into a large wok and heat to 160°C (320°F / 6 out of 10 heat).
13. Gently place the stretched dough strips into the hot oil. Once they float to the surface, continuously turn them with long chopsticks to ensure even heating. The dough sticks will gradually expand and turn golden brown. Once they have set in shape, remove them from the oil and drain.

## Health Advice
Using yeast instead of traditional alum reduces aluminum intake. However, the deep-frying process makes this dish high in calories. It is recommended to pair the dough sticks with soy milk or vegetable salad, and to limit consumption to 2-3 sticks per serving. This dish is suitable for occasional indulgence. People with high blood pressure should be mindful of the added salt.
