# Ready-to-Wash Cold Noodles (Liang Pi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥣 High-gluten flour 200g
🥣 Wheat starch 90g
💧 Cold water 500g (added to batter in batches)
🧂 Salt 3g (added to batter for gluten strength)
🧂 Edible alkali 0.5g (dissolved in 10g hot water)
🧄 Garlic 20g (mixed with 100g water for garlic water)
🥜 Peanuts about 50g
🥒 Cucumber appropriate amount (shredded)
🌱 Bean sprouts appropriate amount (blanched and cooled)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫘 Sesame paste 30–40g (thinned with water)
🌶️ Chili oil appropriate amount

### Compound Seasoning Liquid Ingredients
🍶 Aged vinegar 40g
💧 Water 225g
🌿 Cumin 1.5g
⭐ Star anise 0.5g (2 cloves)
🌳 Cinnamon 0.5g
🍃 Bay leaves 0.5g (2 leaves)
🌸 Sichuan pepper 0.5g (about 20 grains)
🥜 White cardamom 0.5g (1 piece, split open)
🌰 Amomum tsao-ko 0.5g (1 piece, cracked open)
🍂 Grass fruit 0.5g (half, seeds removed, use only the peel)
🧂 Salt 10g (added to seasoning liquid while hot)
🍬 White sugar 10g (added while hot)
🥄 Light soy sauce 10g (added while hot)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 5g (added while hot)
🧂 MSG 4g (added while hot)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick baking tray (liang pi pan)
🥘 Large pot or wok (for boiling water)
🪣 Cold water basin
🥄 Whisk or hand (for stirring batter)
🫗 Strainer (for filtering seasoning liquid)
🍲 Small pot (for simmering seasoning liquid)
♨️ Air fryer (for roasting peanuts)
🔗 Tongs or steaming clips
🔪 Bamboo skewer (for loosening liang pi edges)
🖌️ Brush (for oiling)
🥤 Blender or food processor (for garlic water)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 225g water, 40g aged vinegar, and all spices in a small pot. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
**Note**: Turn off the heat immediately after boiling; do not boil for long, or the spices will become bitter.

2. Strain out the spice residues using a strainer. While hot, add 10g salt, 10g white sugar, 10g light soy sauce, 5g chicken bouillon, and 4g MSG. Stir well and let cool completely, then set aside.
3. Place 30–40g sesame paste in a bowl. Gradually add water in small amounts, stirring in one direction each time, until it reaches a consistency slightly thinner than yogurt, able to flow in a steady stream.
4. Blend 20g garlic and 100g water in a blender until a milky white liquid forms. Set aside.
5. Lightly spray the peanuts with a thin layer of oil, place in the air fryer, and roast at 180℃ for 6 minutes. Remove and let cool completely.
6. Shred the cucumber, cut the cilantro into sections, blanch the bean sprouts, cool them in cold water, and squeeze out excess water. Set aside.
7. In a bowl, combine 200g high-gluten flour, 90g wheat starch, and 3g salt. First, add half of the cold water. Stir by hand in one direction until a very thick batter forms. Continue stirring for 1 minute to fully develop the gluten.
8. Add the remaining cold water in three batches, stirring in the same direction after each addition until the water is fully absorbed. The final batter should be a flowable liquid with some thickness.
9. Dissolve 0.5g edible alkali in 10g hot water, pour it into the batter, and stir well. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes. In hot southern regions, it is recommended to refrigerate the batter during resting to prevent fermentation and sourness.
10. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, and prepare a basin of cold water nearby. Lightly spray a thin layer of oil onto the non-stick baking tray and spread it evenly.
11. Stir the batter well before each scoop. Ladle one scoop of batter into the tray, and tilt the tray to spread it evenly across the bottom.
12. Place the tray into the boiling water, cover the pot tightly, and maintain the highest heat throughout.
**Key**: The lid must be sealed tightly, and the pot should have good sealing. This is crucial to prevent the liang pi from wrinkling or cracking.

13. Steam for about 2–3 minutes, until the liang pi becomes entirely translucent and large bubbles appear. Quickly remove and immediately place it into the cold water basin to cool.
14. Once cooled, brush a thin layer of oil onto the surface of the liang pi to prevent sticking. Use a bamboo skewer to loosen the edges, then carefully peel off the entire sheet.
**Suggestion**: Using two liang pi pans in rotation improves efficiency. If the water in the pot evaporates too much, replenish with boiling water and bring it back to a boil promptly.

15. Cut the liang pi into wide strips, place them in a large bowl, and layer on the bean sprouts, shredded cucumber, cilantro, and roasted peanuts.
16. Drizzle with the prepared sesame paste, garlic water, compound seasoning liquid, and an appropriate amount of chili oil.
17. Toss thoroughly by hand or with chopsticks until every strip of liang pi is evenly coated with the sauce. Serve immediately.

## Health Advisory
Liang pi is primarily high in carbohydrates and low in protein, making it suitable for occasional indulgence in summer. Pairing it with eggs or lean meat can help balance nutrition. However, the seasoning liquid contains significant amounts of salt and sugar, so those with high blood pressure or needing to control sugar intake should consume it in moderation.
