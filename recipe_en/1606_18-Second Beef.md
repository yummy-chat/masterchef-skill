# 18-Second Beef
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐄 Beef about 1 jin (slice against the grain into thin strips)
🍊 Orange 1 (use only the peel, cut into thin strips)
🌶️ Millet peppers to taste (cut into small sections)
🌿 Cilantro to taste (cut into sections)
🧂 Baking soda 1 tsp (for marinating beef)
🧂 Salt a pinch (for marinating beef)
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste (for marinating beef)
🧂 Dark soy sauce to taste (for marinating beef)
🐔 Chicken powder to taste (for marinating beef)
🥚 Egg white 1 (for marinating beef)
🥔 Cornstarch to taste (for marinating beef)
🚰 Water a small amount (to incorporate into beef)
🧂 Salt a pinch (for blanching orange peel water)
🍯 Honey to taste (to mix with orange peel strips)
🍶 Yellow wine a small amount (for sauce)
🍾 Light soy sauce to taste (for sauce)
🍬 Sugar a small amount (for sauce)
🐔 Chicken powder a small amount (for sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil a small amount (for sauce)
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil a small amount (for sauce)
🫒 Rapeseed oil 5 tbsp (for cooking)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪚 Cutting board
🔪 Chef’s knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Small bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. Cut the beef against the grain into thin strips. Cut orange peel into thin strips, add a pinch of salt, mix well, squeeze out excess water, then mix in honey. Cut millet peppers into small sections and cilantro into sections.
2. To the beef strips, add in order: baking soda, salt, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, chicken powder, and mix well. Gradually add a small amount of water, stirring in one direction until fully absorbed. Then add egg white and cornstarch, continue mixing until the beef becomes firm, then refrigerate to rest.
3. In a small bowl, combine yellow wine, light soy sauce, sugar, chicken powder, sesame oil, and Sichuan pepper oil to make the sauce; set aside.
4. Heat rapeseed oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Add the beef strips, gently push and separate them with a spatula. Once the beef changes color, immediately remove and drain oil.
   **Key**: Oil temperature should not be too high; add beef gently to prevent the coating from falling off.
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add millet pepper sections, the blanched beef strips, and honey-mixed orange peel strips. Immediately pour in the sauce. Stir-fry over high heat quickly; total time should be about 18 seconds.
   **Note**: Start timing from when the sauce is poured in; the process must be done in one fluid motion.
6. Just before removing from heat, add cilantro sections, stir-fry twice, then immediately turn off the heat and plate.

## Health Tips
Beef is high in protein and low in fat; orange peel helps cut the grease and adds aroma, making it suitable for muscle building. Baking soda tenderizes the meat, but it is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should reduce it appropriately. Cooking heat must be high; removing from heat in 18 seconds locks in the juices.
