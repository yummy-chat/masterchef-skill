# Coconut-Simmered Chicken
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 whole chicken (choose a large, high-quality, local chicken)  
🥥 About 5 old coconuts (sweet coconut water, tender meat, rich coconut aroma)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (for sauce; mince and sauté until fragrant)  
🌶️ Chili peppers (such as bird's eye chili), to taste (for sauce; slice into rings)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (for sauce; chop finely)  
🥣 Soy sauce, to taste (for sauce; mix well)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (optional; add to soup to adjust seasoning before serving)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board (for processing coconut and chicken)  
🍲 Glass pot or soup pot (for boiling coconut water and simmering chicken)  
🍳 Wok (for making the sauce; heat oil and sauté minced garlic)  
🥣 Bowl, spoon (for preparing and serving the sauce)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring and retrieving ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1. Use a knife to pry open the top of the old coconut. Pour the coconut water into a bowl and set aside. Remove the coconut meat and cut into strips or slices.  
   **Note**: Choose old coconuts for richer flavor and sweeter coconut water.
2. Cut the cleaned chicken into large pieces, about ten pieces per chicken.  
   **Key**: Cutting into large pieces helps lock in the chicken juices, making the meat more tender.
3. In a bowl, place the sliced chili rings and chopped cilantro. In a separate wok, heat oil and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant. Pour the hot oil and garlic over the chili and cilantro to release their aroma. Finally, add soy sauce and mix well to make the dipping sauce.
4. Pour the coconut water into the soup pot and add the coconut meat strips. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes, until the coconut aroma is fully released.
5. Keep the soup at a gentle simmer. Place the chicken pieces into the pot one by one with the skin side up. Once the soup returns to a boil, immediately turn the heat to the lowest setting and let it steep for 12 minutes.  
   **Key**: Throughout the cooking process, keep the soup at a "barely boiling" state and avoid vigorous boiling at high heat to ensure the chicken remains tender. Do not cover the pot during simmering. After cooking, do not skim off the chicken fat from the surface to preserve the soup's flavor and aroma.
6. Once the steeping time is up, use chopsticks to lift the chicken pieces out and arrange them on a serving plate. The coconut chicken soup left in the pot is ready to drink. Serve the chicken with the prepared dipping sauce.

## Health Tips
This Coconut-Simmered Chicken uses sweet coconut water to gently cook the chicken, preserving high-quality protein and natural electrolytes. It is suitable for those needing mild nourishment or following a low-oil diet. It is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables to balance dietary fiber. However, note that the soup naturally contains fat, so individuals with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should limit their intake.
