# Soup-filled Yellow Croaker 🐟
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 1 large yellow croaker (about 1.6 jin, 43-45 cm long, whole deboned with intact skin)
🎋 1 bamboo shoot (for making a deboning bamboo knife)
🦀 8 crabs (extract crab meat and roe for filling)
🐷 About 400 g pork skin (for making pork skin jelly)
🐔 Half an old hen (for making stock)
🦴 About 350 g pork leg bone (for making stock)
🦴 About 350 g pork spine bone (for making stock)
🧅 About 35 g scallion (for removing fishy smell, making stock, and filling the fish belly for shaping)
🫚 About 15 g ginger (for removing fishy smell in stock)
🥕 Some carrot (for making fish bone stock)
🌿 Some celery (for making fish bone stock)
🧅 Some onion (for making fish bone stock)
🍇 Some white wine (to add aroma to fish bone stock)
🌽 Some corn starch / tempura flour (for battering the fish)
🥤 130-140 g sparkling water (for mixing batter)
🍤 Some shrimp paste (to seal the fish belly opening)
🍡 Some pearl fish balls (for filling to enhance texture)
🧂 Some salt
🌶️ Some black pepper

## Cookware & Equipment
🪶 Bamboo knife (special deboning tool)
🫕 Multiple soup pots
🍳 Wok
🥄 Skimmer, soup ladle
🕳️ Large skimmer
📦 Plastic wrap
🫕 Large deep pot (diameter ≥ 55 cm)
🔪 Cutting board, knives
✂️ Scissors
📦 Airtight container
🥣 Strainer

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. **Make the bamboo knife**: Select fresh bamboo with a thickness similar to an arm. Carve it into a bamboo knife about 50 cm long and elastic.
2. **Debone the whole fish**:
   - Remove the gills and innards. Insert the bamboo knife through the fish’s mouth, and gently separate the flesh from the spine along the backbone.
   - Break the tail, cut the spine, and carefully pull out the entire bone, ensuring the fish skin remains intact.
   - Fill the fish belly with scallion for shaping, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate.
3. **Prepare the soup jelly**:
   - Blanch the pork skin, scrape off any fat, and cut into strips. Add ginger, scallion, and 3-4 times the water. Bring to a boil, skim off foam, and simmer for 2 hours. Strain to get pork skin jelly liquid.
   - Blanch the old hen, pork leg bone, and pork spine bone. Add ginger, scallion, and enough water. Bring to a boil, skim foam, and simmer for 2.5 hours to get pork and chicken stock.
   - Sauté the removed fish bones with carrot, celery, and onion. Add white wine and water, cook for about 25 minutes to get fish bone stock.
   - Combine the pork skin jelly liquid, pork and chicken stock, and fish bone stock. Skim off any foam and fat. Add the prepared crab meat and roe, salt, and black pepper. Mix well, pour into a container, and refrigerate overnight until set into jelly.
4. **Prepare the filling**: Cut the chilled crab meat and roe soup jelly into small cubes. Prepare the pearl fish balls and shrimp paste.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Stuff and seal**: Remove the chilled fish and discard the scallion. Fill the fish belly with the soup jelly cubes and pearl fish balls. Apply shrimp paste over the opening to seal tightly.
   **Note**: The seal must be secure to prevent leakage during frying.
2. **Batter and deep-fry**:
   - Mix tempura flour with sparkling water to make a batter. Coat the whole fish evenly with the batter.
   - Pour enough oil into the large deep pot and heat to 140°C.
   - Gently lower the fish into the oil, ensuring it is fully submerged.
   - Continuously ladle hot oil over the top to ensure even cooking on both sides. Maintain the oil temperature and fry for about 7 minutes, until the fish is golden and crispy, and a chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part without any blood seeping out.
   **Key**: The pot diameter must be larger than the fish’s length to avoid uneven frying or cracking.
3. **Remove**: Carefully lift the fish out with a large skimmer, drain excess oil, and plate. The finished dish has a crispy exterior, tender fish meat, and a rich liquid filling inside.

## Health Advice
Soup-filled yellow croaker features stock jelly and crab meat stuffed into a deboned fish, which after frying becomes crispy outside and tender inside with ample liquid. For best texture, enjoy immediately while hot. It is suitable for banquets, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
