# Korean Street Sandwich
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 1/4 cabbage (finely shredded, half for the egg pancake, half for raw filling)  
🥕 1 carrot (finely grated)  
🥚 3 eggs  
🍞 3 slices of bread (2 for toasting, 1 used as-is)  
🥬 Some raw cabbage shreds (mixed with mayonnaise for filling)  
🧂 Two pinches of salt  
🫙 Some mayonnaise (used twice: in egg mixture and with raw cabbage)  
🫒 A small amount of sesame oil (optional)  
🧈 1 small piece of butter  
🫒 A small amount of cooking oil  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🧇 Grater  
🥣 Bowl  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or tongs  
🌯 Plastic wrap  

## Instructions  
1. Finely shred the cabbage into very thin strips, and peel and grate the carrot into fine strips.  
   **Note**: When cutting the cabbage, curl your left hand into a claw shape and keep your knuckles against the knife blade, cutting vertically. When grating the carrot, use a fork to hold the end to avoid injuring your hand.  
2. Place half the shredded cabbage and all the grated carrot into a bowl. Add the eggs, salt, some mayonnaise, and sesame oil. Mix well and let it rest for 5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: After resting, the vegetables will release water. If the egg mixture becomes too thin, add more vegetable shreds. If too dry, add another egg. The mayonnaise can be a bit more generous for extra sweetness and richness.  
3. Mix the other half of the raw shredded cabbage with mayonnaise and set aside as filling.  
4. Add a small amount of oil to a non-stick pan, heat over medium heat, then pour in the vegetable and egg mixture. Use a spatula to bring the edges inward and press the surface flat, shaping it into a square about the size of a slice of bread.  
   **Heat and technique**: Once shaped, reduce the heat to low. Fry for about 1.5 minutes until the bottom is golden and set, then flip and fry for another 1.5–2 minutes. Gently press the egg pancake repeatedly during cooking to make it firmer and drier.  
5. Clean the pan, add butter, and melt over low heat until bubbling. Place the bread slices in the pan and fry one side until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside. Fry 2 slices in total, about 30–60 seconds per side. **Must use low heat to prevent burning**. For a fully crispy texture, toast both sides.  
6. Lay a piece of plastic wrap on the counter. Layer in this order: one slice of toasted bread, the vegetable egg pancake, the untoasted soft bread slice, the mixed raw cabbage shreds, and finally the other slice of toasted bread.  
   **Key point**: The layering order determines the texture. The soft bread in the middle provides a tender transition, while the raw cabbage adds freshness and moisture.  
7. Tightly wrap the sandwich with plastic wrap, twist both ends shut, flip so the sealed side faces down, and let it sit for a moment to set.  
8. Cut the sandwich in half with one clean slice. The cross-section will show distinct layers, crispy on the outside and tender inside.  

## Health Advice  
Cabbage and carrots are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, while eggs provide high-quality protein. This sandwich is nutritionally balanced and suitable as a quick breakfast or light meal. Pay attention to the amount of mayonnaise and butter used to avoid excess calories. For those with high blood lipids, low-fat mayonnaise can be used as a substitute.
