# Michelin-style Palmier
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose or bread flour, as needed
🧈 Unsalted butter, as needed (some mixed into dough, the rest for butter block)
🍬 Granulated sugar, as needed (added to dough)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
💧 Cold water, as needed
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed
🍬 Granulated sugar, plenty of (for sprinkling after lamination, coating during shaping, and dusting midway through baking)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
👨‍🍳 Rolling pin (regular and thick)
📐 Acrylic board (or other hard, flat tool)
⚙️ Dough sheeter (professional equipment, can be substituted with manual method)
🔥 Convection or regular oven
🍳 Baking sheet
📄 Parchment paper or silicone mat
🪨 Pie weights (or another baking sheet for weighing down)
🔪 Bench scraper or kitchen knife
📏 Ruler
🧹 Scraper or pastry brush
🥘 Small saucepan or bowl

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**: Combine flour, sugar, and a small amount of butter in a mixing bowl. Mix until uniform. Separately, combine water, salt, and heavy cream. Gradually add the liquid mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring as you go, and knead into a smooth dough.
   **Key point**: Add the liquid in batches, stirring constantly, to ensure even incorporation.

2. **Chill the dough**: Divide the dough in half, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the gluten to relax fully.

3. **Make the butter block**: Take the unsalted butter and repeatedly pound and roll it with a rolling pin into a thin square shape, until its pliability and firmness match that of the chilled dough.
   **Key point**: Pound the butter until it reaches a workable temperature, avoiding melting.

4. **Encase the butter**: Remove the rested dough and roll it out to a size that can completely enclose the butter block. Place the butter in the center, then seal it tightly with the dough, pinching all seams closed.
   **Note**: The seams must be tightly sealed to prevent butter leakage during lamination.

5. **Laminate**: Roll out the butter-encased dough and perform a letter fold (tri-fold). Then chill thoroughly. Repeat this process, performing a total of 5 tri-folds.
   **Key point**: Before each roll-out, ensure the dough and butter have the same firmness. After each fold, chill thoroughly to prevent butter from blending into the dough or the gluten from contracting.

6. **Final shaping**: After the last lamination, roll the dough into an even sheet about 3 mm thick. Generously sprinkle the surface with sugar. Fold both sides toward the center, then fold in half to form a square log. Cut into slices about 1.5 cm thick. Coat the cut sides with sugar and gently shape the pieces with your hands to make them round and plump.
   **Note**: Use enough sugar for sprinkling. Cut slices to an even thickness. Handle gently during shaping.

7. **First bake**: Place the shaped pieces on a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for about 12 minutes, until the interior has puffed and the exterior is slightly soft.

8. **Mid-bake shaping and weighing**: Remove the baking sheet from the oven. Use a chopstick or similar tool to gently nudge the palmiers into a rounder shape. Dust the surface with powdered sugar. Then press another baking sheet weighted with pie weights on top.
   **Purpose**: To achieve a flat surface and caramelized layer on the finished product.

9. **Continue baking**: Return to the oven and bake for about 15 more minutes, until both sides are caramelized to a golden brown. Remove from oven and let cool.

## Health Advice
This palmier recipe has a relatively high calorie density. It is suitable for occasional enjoyment as an afternoon tea treat. Be mindful of your total sugar and fat intake for the meal. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened tea or black coffee to balance the sweetness. Those with blood sugar sensitivities should avoid overconsumption.
