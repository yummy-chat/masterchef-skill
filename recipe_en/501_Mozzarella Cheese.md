# Mozzarella Cheese
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Year Round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 3000g fresh whole milk  
🧂 150g white vinegar  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🫗 Cheesecloth bag  
🧤 Disposable food gloves  
🥣 Glass bowl  
📦 Airtight container  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🥄 Spatula  

## Instructions  
1. Pour the fresh whole milk into a non-stick pan, and slowly heat over low heat to about 50°C.  
   **Note**: Be sure to use fresh milk; boxed milk will cause the curds to crumble and prevent a stretchy texture. Do not let the milk boil.  
2. Turn off the heat, then pour in 150g of white vinegar. Gently stir a few times with a spatula to combine the vinegar with the milk. Let it sit for 3–5 minutes until large, white, fluffy curds form and separate clearly from the pale yellow whey.  
   **Key point**: Add the vinegar only after turning off the heat, and stir gently to avoid breaking up the curds.  
3. Pour the curds and whey into a cheesecloth bag. Place the bag opening over a glass bowl, and use your hands to press and squeeze the bag, extracting as much whey as possible, until the curds inside form a firm, solid mass with little to no liquid seeping out.  
   **Key point**: The drier the curds, the better the subsequent stretching.  
4. Pour the squeezed-out whey back into the non-stick pan and heat over low heat to about 70°C.  
5. Place the squeezed curd mass into the hot whey and scald for 10–15 seconds. Remove, then repeatedly squeeze, fold, and pull with your hands. Return the curds to the whey for another 10–15 seconds, remove, and continue pulling and folding. Repeat this process 10–15 times, until the surface of the curds becomes smooth, stretchy into long strands without breaking easily.  
   **Key point**: Repeated scalding and pulling/folding is the core technique for achieving mozzarella’s stretchy texture. Work patiently, and do not scald for too long each time to prevent the curds from firming up.  
6. Shape the stretched mozzarella into a ball. Place it in an airtight container or wrap tightly with plastic wrap, then freeze for about 2–3 hours until completely firm and ready to use.  
   **Key point**: Seal and freeze to prevent odor absorption. Freezing until firm makes it easier to shred or use directly in cooking.  

## Health Tips  
Homemade mozzarella cheese is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for those needing calcium supplementation, but it is relatively high in calories, so consume in moderation. Always use fresh milk to ensure a stretchy texture, and be careful with temperature control during preparation to prevent the curds from becoming tough.
