# Universal Red Bean Paste Filling
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red beans 400g
💧 Water 1200g (approximately 3 times the weight of the red beans, for boiling)
🧂 White sugar 100g
🍯 Brown sugar 100g (for color and aroma)
🧈 Butter 20g (optional, enhances flavor and helps bind)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
⚡ Electric pressure cooker
🕸️ Coarse mesh strainer
🧶 Clean kitchen cotton cloth
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Glass bowl
🎞️ Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. Soak the red beans in water overnight until fully hydrated and expanded.
2. Drain the soaked red beans and place them in the electric pressure cooker. Add approximately 1200g of water.
   **Key point**: Do not use too little water, or the beans may not cook thoroughly; slightly more water is fine, as it will be filtered out later.
3. Select the "Beans" or "Pressure Cook Beans" mode and set the time to about 50 minutes, cooking until the red beans are extremely soft.
4. Pour the cooked soft red beans into a coarse mesh strainer set over a bowl. Use a spatula or spoon to press the beans, rinsing with a small amount of boiling water to allow the bean paste to pass through the strainer while the skins remain.
   **Key point**: This is the "washing bean paste" skin-removal process, which significantly improves the fineness of the final product. Continue rinsing until only a large amount of bean skins remain in the strainer.
5. Pour the filtered bean paste liquid into a bowl lined with a clean kitchen cotton cloth.
6. Briefly stir the contents in the cloth with a spatula to help drain the water, then gather the cloth opening, lift it, and squeeze firmly to remove most of the water.
   **Key point**: Work quickly. Squeeze until the bean paste no longer sticks to the cloth, forms a ball, and can be easily separated from the cloth.
7. Transfer the squeezed dry bean paste ball into a non-stick pan. Add white sugar and brown sugar, and heat over low heat. Add butter at this step if desired.
8. Continuously stir the bean paste with a spatula to mix it evenly with the sugar and evaporate moisture, taking about ten minutes.
   **Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent scorching on the bottom. Cook until the bean paste is dry and forms a firm peak that stands upright when lifted with the spatula without softening.
9. Place the cooked bean paste into a clean glass bowl. While still hot, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the bean paste, covering it tightly.
   **Key point**: Covering the surface directly prevents the paste from drying out and forming a skin. It can be refrigerated for 3-5 days.

## Health Advice
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron, and are known for their blood-nourishing and swelling-reducing effects. However, the sugar content is relatively high, so it is recommended to consume in moderation per meal, especially suitable after exercise or during periods of high physical exertion. Ensure adequate soaking and cooking time to avoid undercooked beans, which can affect digestion.
