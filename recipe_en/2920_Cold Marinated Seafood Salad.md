# Cold Marinated Seafood Salad  
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Live shrimp, as needed (cook until curled)  
🐚 Live abalone / small abalone, as needed (remove innards while alive, then clean)  
🐚 Clams / scallops / surf clams, as needed  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (for vegetable broth)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for broth and deodorizing seafood)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (for vegetable broth)  
🥬 Celery, as needed (for vegetable broth)  
🥕 Carrot, as needed (for vegetable broth)  
🌶️ Green chili pepper, as needed (for vegetable broth)  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for broth and garnish)  
🌿 Fresh Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (for numbing aroma)  
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili, as needed (for garnish and heat)  
🥄 Seafood soy sauce, 100g  
🥄 Spicy fresh sauce, 40g  
🥄 Mala fresh sauce, 40g  
🧂 MSG, 20g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, 20g  
🍬 White sugar, 80g  
🥄 Oyster sauce, 100g  
🍯 Wasabi (green horseradish), 10g  
🫒 Sichuan peppercorn oil, about 15-20g (1.5 tablespoons)  
🥄 Prepared seafood marinade, 300g  
🍶 Laofangu rice wine, as needed (for deodorizing seafood)  
🫚 Ginger chunks, as needed (for deodorizing and enhancing seafood)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥘 Stainless steel bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Ladle  
🔪 Chef’s knife  

## Preparation Steps  
### Making Vegetable Broth  
1. Add water to a wok, then add scallion, ginger, onion, celery, carrot, green chili, cilantro, and other vegetables. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer to infuse the broth with the vegetables’ fresh sweetness.  
**Key point**: Extract the fresh sweetness from vegetables into the water to serve as one of the marinade bases.  
2. After simmering, remove the vegetable solids. Quickly pour the vegetable broth into a bowl with ice cubes to cool it down. Set aside.  
### Preparing Seafood  
3. Clean live abalone: insert a knife tip from the side, tilt the knife upright, and rotate along the inside of the shell to remove the innards. Rinse briefly with water.  
**Key point**: Abalone must be handled while alive. Be precise to ensure all innards are removed.  

### Blanching Seafood  
4. In a separate pot, add water with ginger chunks and rice wine to remove fishy odors. Heat to about 80°C (176°F), then add the shrimp and small abalone first, as they are more heat-resistant.  
5. Once the shrimp turn pink and curl, add the cleaned abalone, clams, scallops, and all remaining seafood.  
**Heat control**: Maintain water at around 80°C; add quick-cooking seafood last and blanch just briefly—do not overcook.  
   **Doneness check**: Shrimp fully red and curled; all shellfish open.  
   **Key point**: Add ingredients in batches to ensure even doneness.  
6. Remove blanched seafood and let cool naturally—do not rinse with raw water.  
### Preparing the Marinade  
7. In a large bowl, combine seafood soy sauce, spicy fresh sauce, mala fresh sauce, MSG, chicken bouillon powder, and white sugar in the specified proportions. Stir until sugar and MSG dissolve.  
8. Add oyster sauce, wasabi, and Sichuan peppercorn oil. Mix thoroughly.  
9. Finally, pour in the prepared seafood marinade and the cooled vegetable broth. Mix until well combined.  
**Doneness check**: Marinade should be bright red, with a savory-slightly sweet flavor, plus numbing, spicy, and wasabi notes.  
   **Key point**: All seasonings must be fully blended.  

### Assembly & Marination  
10. Arrange the cooled seafood neatly on a plate. Sprinkle with fresh Sichuan peppercorns, cilantro segments, and bird’s eye chili rings.  
11. Pour the prepared marinade evenly over the seafood until most ingredients are submerged.  
**Key point**: After pouring the marinade, refrigerate the dish for 5-6 hours to allow the seafood to fully absorb the flavors, achieving the best taste.  

## Health Tips  
This cold marinated seafood salad is low in fat and high in protein, rich in zinc and selenium—ideal for summer to stimulate appetite and supplement nutrition. Ensure all seafood is thoroughly blanched. Those with sensitive stomachs should limit consumption of chilled, marinated shellfish.
