# Yangzhou Fried Rice
Cuisine: Jiangsu cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Cold rice (preferably dried leftover rice from the previous day)
🥚 Eggs
🦐 Frozen peeled prawns
🥫 Canned luncheon meat
🟢 Green peas
🥕 Carrot
🌽 Corn kernels (optional)
🧅 Scallions
🛢️ Oil
🧂 Salt

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Bowl
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥘 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the carrot into 0.2 cm cubes and set aside.
2. Cut the luncheon meat into 0.2 cm cubes and set aside.
3. Separate the white parts and green parts of the scallions, cut each into 0.25–0.5 cm lengths, and set aside separately.
4. Crack the eggs into a bowl, whisk into a uniform egg mixture, and set aside.
5. Boil the carrot cubes, green peas, and corn kernels in boiling water until cooked, then remove and drain. Reserve the vegetable cooking water.
6. Boil the prawns in boiling water until cooked, then remove and drain. Discard the prawn cooking water.
7. Using the hot wok double-oil method: heat the wok, swirl a small amount of oil to coat the wok and then pour it out. Add 20–30 ml of cold oil and heat to 70% hot. Slowly pour the egg mixture in a thin stream, let it set until the egg solidifies, then immediately remove and set aside.
8. Add the luncheon meat cubes, green peas, carrot cubes, corn kernels, and prawns to the wok. Stir-fry over high heat for 1–2 minutes, then remove and set aside.
9. Clean the wok and wipe it dry.
10. Add 10 ml of oil to the wok, heat until hot, then add the white parts of the scallions and stir-fry until fragrant.
11. Reduce the heat to low, add the cold rice, quickly break it apart with the back of the spatula and stir-fry, ensuring each grain of rice is evenly coated with oil and separate.
12. Pour in the previously stir-fried egg pieces, continue to break them apart, and mix the egg thoroughly with the rice.
13. Turn the heat to high, add all the stir-fried toppings, and quickly stir-fry for 1–2 minutes.
14. Season with 12–15 g of salt, and stir-fry evenly.
15. Sprinkle in the green parts of the scallions, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant.
16. Turn off the heat, plate, and serve.

## Health Advice
Yangzhou fried rice is an excellent combination of carbohydrates and protein, suitable for consumption after exercise or when a quick energy boost is needed. Be mindful of the amount of luncheon meat and salt used; those with high blood pressure can reduce the salt or opt for a low-sodium version.
