# Shaanxi Cold Skin Noodles (Liangpi)
**Cuisine**: Shaanxi Cuisine  
**Difficulty Level**: ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation**: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 350g (darker color)  
🌾 Wheat starch 150g (ratio of 5:2 with all-purpose flour)  
💧 Water 800-900g (added in batches, adjust based on flour absorption)  
🥒 Cucumber, to taste (shredded, for final garnish)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for greasing the liangpi tray to prevent sticking)  
🧂 Vinegar water 1 spoonful (large serving spoon in the video; see previous recipe for preparation)  
🧄 Garlic water 1 spoonful  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 spoonful  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🪣 Large mixing bowl  
🥘 Liangpi tray (flat-bottomed metal round pan)  
🍲 Large pot with lid (lid should be large and heavy to create pressure)  
🖌️ Brush  
🥄 Soup spoon  
💧 Basin of cold water  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Place all-purpose flour and wheat starch in a large bowl, mixing by hand until well combined.
2. Gradually add 800-900g of water in batches, stirring thoroughly after each addition until no lumps remain.  
   **Key point**: Water must be added gradually; stir continuously until the batter is smooth, with a consistency similar to cooking oil, flowing in a continuous stream when lifted.
3. Prepare vinegar water, garlic water, and chili oil in advance, and shred the cucumber for later use.  

**Cooking Stage**
1. Dip a brush in cooking oil and evenly coat the inside of the liangpi tray with a thin layer of oil.
2. Ladle one spoonful of batter into the tray, then shake it horizontally to spread the batter evenly across the bottom.
3. Place the tray into the boiling water in the large pot, rotating it clockwise once to set the bottom evenly with heat.
4. Quickly lift the tray out of the water, shake it horizontally again to further spread the batter evenly.
5. Place the tray back steadily into the boiling water, covering with a large, heavy lid.  
   **Key point**: Maintain high heat with ample steam; steam for about 2 minutes, until large bubbles form on the surface of the liangpi, indicating it is cooked.
6. Remove the tray from the pot and immediately immerse it in a basin of cold water to cool.  
   **Key point**: After fully cooling, gently peel the entire sheet of liangpi from the edge; the finished product should be translucent, chewy, and unbroken.
7. Fold the liangpi and cut it into wide strips with a knife.
8. Place the cut liangpi into a bowl, add 1 spoonful of vinegar water, 1 spoonful of garlic water, and 1 spoonful of chili oil, mixing thoroughly so each strip is coated in the glossy red seasoning.
9. Serve in a bowl, sprinkle with white sesame seeds, and garnish with shredded cucumber.

## Health Tips
This liangpi is made by mixing wheat starch into a batter, resulting in a translucent and chewy texture ideal for summer appetizers. It is recommended to include plenty of shredded cucumber to boost dietary fiber. However, the vinegar water and chili oil are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure may reduce their amounts accordingly.
