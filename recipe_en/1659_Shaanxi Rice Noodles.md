# Shaanxi Rice Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 500g long-grain rice (soaked for 6 hours in advance)  
🌾 50g all-purpose flour  
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (julienned)  
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed (blanched)  

### Chili Oil Ingredients  
🫒 500g rapeseed oil  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for frying aromatics)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed (for frying aromatics)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (for frying aromatics)  
🤍 20g white sesame seeds  
🌶️ 75g chili flakes  
🟤 25g special spice powder  
🍇 A splash of aromatic vinegar (for aroma activation)  
🍶 A splash of baijiu (for aroma activation)  

### Vinegar Water Ingredients  
🍇 500g aromatic vinegar  
🧄 Ginger, minced, as needed  
🟤 625g spiced water  

### Garlic Water Ingredients  
🧄 100g garlic cloves (mashed into paste)  
💧 1000g cooled boiled water  

### Final Seasonings  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🍇 Vinegar water, as needed (prepared)  
🧄 Garlic water, as needed (prepared)  
🌶️ Chili oil, as needed (prepared)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Large pot (stockpot)  
🍳 Wok  
🌀 Precision blender  
🥘 Steamer with matching steaming trays  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin  
🥣 Strainer/skimmer  
🔪 Cutting board and large knife  
🥄 Seasoning bowls and spoons  
🖌️ Oil brush (for greasing steaming trays)  

## Instructions  
1. Soak the long-grain rice in clean water for 6 hours in advance, until the grains are fully plumped.  
2. **Make the chili oil**: Pour rapeseed oil into a wok and heat until it reaches 70% hot. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and onion chunks. Fry until charred yellow, then remove and discard. Once the oil temperature drops to about 180°C, add white sesame seeds, special spice powder, and chili flakes in that order, stirring to combine. Immediately drizzle in a splash of aromatic vinegar and baijiu to release the aroma, then let it cool and rest. This is the chili oil.  
**Key point**: Control the oil temperature at around 60% hot. The "aroma activation" step with vinegar and baijiu intensifies the fragrance.  

3. **Make the vinegar water**: Heat a little oil in a wok, add minced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in aromatic vinegar and bring to a boil. Add the spiced water, bring to a boil again, then strain to remove the ginger. The resulting liquid is the vinegar water.  
**Key point**: The vinegar water must be boiled and strained to ensure a pure flavor without residue.  

4. **Make the garlic water**: Mash the garlic cloves into a fine paste, add cooled boiled water, and stir well. Let it sit briefly; this is the garlic water.  
5. **Blend and scald the batter**: Drain the soaked long-grain rice, place it in a blender, add an appropriate amount of clean water and the flour, and blend into a fine rice batter. Pour the batter into a large basin, then slowly pour in boiling water while stirring vigorously, until the batter becomes a thick, sticky paste.  
**Key point**: Scalding the batter with boiling water is crucial for chewy rice noodles; stir quickly to prevent lumps.  

6. **Steam the rice noodles**: Lightly brush the inner surface of a steaming tray with cooking oil. Ladle in an appropriate amount of the scalded batter, and tilt the tray to spread it evenly. Once the steamer is producing steam, place the tray inside, cover, and steam over high heat for about 3 minutes, until large bubbles appear on the surface. Remove the tray.  
7. **Cut and assemble**: Let the steaming tray cool slightly, then use a large knife to cut the rice sheet into wide strips. Place the rice noodles in a bowl first, then top with cucumber julienne and blanched bean sprouts.  
8. **Season**: Add salt, MSG, vinegar water, and garlic water in that order. Finally, drizzle generously with chili oil. Mix thoroughly and serve immediately.  
**Key point**: Chili oil is the soul of this dish; the proportions of vinegar water and garlic water can be adjusted to personal taste.  

## Health Advice  
This Shaanxi Rice Noodles dish uses long-grain rice with a scalding technique, resulting in chewy and smooth noodles. It is ideal for stimulating the appetite in summer or as a light meal. The homemade chili oil and vinegar water offer unique flavors, but be mindful of the salt and MSG amounts; those with high blood pressure can reduce the quantity.
