# Christmas Stamped Cookies
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Unsalted butter 100g
🍬 Powdered sugar 60g (contains about 1/3 cornstarch)
🥚 Whole egg liquid 36g (room temperature, beaten and weighed in advance)
🌾 Cake flour 215g (protein content ≤ 9%)
🧂 Salt 1 small pinch

## Tools & Equipment
🔥 Oven
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍴 Spatula/Scraper
🪚 Rolling pin
🌀 Electric/Hand whisk
🍴 Fork/Toothpick
🎨 Royal icing bag/Piping bag
🔔 Icing spatula/Small offset spatula
🖌️ Brush
🍪 Cookie cutters

## Instructions
1. Weigh 100g unsalted butter, 60g powdered sugar, 36g whole egg liquid, and 215g cake flour.
   **Key Point**: Cake flour protein content must be ≤ 9%, weigh precisely.
2. Soften the butter at room temperature; if the room is cold in winter, assist by softening over warm water.
   **Key Point**: Soften until a finger can easily press a dent, smooth and creamy, do not melt to a liquid state.
3. Place the softened butter in a mixing bowl, add 1 small pinch of salt and powdered sugar, and beat with a whisk until smooth and uniform.
   **Key Point**: Do not over-cream, avoid incorporating too much air.
4. Add room-temperature whole egg liquid in two batches, mixing thoroughly after each addition to prevent separation.
   **Key Point**: Egg liquid temperature must be close to the butter's; must be added in batches.
5. Sift in the cake flour, mix with a spatula by cutting or mix with your hands until it just comes together into a dough.
   **Key Point**: Avoid over-mixing, prevent gluten development, which would make the cookies hard and the surface uneven.
6. Place the dough between two sheets of parchment paper, and roll it out with a rolling pin to an even thickness of about 5–7 mm.
   **Key Point**: Using parchment paper keeps the surface smooth; roll evenly from the center outward, maintaining a flat surface without cracks.
7. Refrigerate the rolled-out dough until firm, then remove and use cookie cutters to stamp shapes. Dip the cutter in a small amount of flour, tap off excess, then stamp.
   **Key Point**: Refrigerate the dough until it is no longer sticky and firm enough before stamping; gently brush or blow off any excess flour on the cookie surface.
8. Arrange the cookie cutouts evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving space between each.
9. Place in the preheated oven at 170–175°C (top and bottom heat), middle rack, and bake for about 12–15 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: Bake until the edges are lightly colored; adjust time based on your oven and cookie thickness.
   **Key Point**: Avoid opening the oven door frequently at the beginning of baking.
10. Let the cookies rest on the baking sheet for 5 minutes after taking them out of the oven, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
    **Key Point**: Freshly baked cookies are soft; they become crispy only after cooling completely. They are fragile when warm.
11. Once the cookies are completely cool, decorate with royal icing of different consistencies.
    **Key Point**: After decorating, place in a well-ventilated area, or dry in the oven at a low temperature of 50°C, or use a dehydrator, until the icing is completely hard.

## Health Advice
These Christmas cookies are not low in sugar, suitable for occasional enjoyment as holiday treats, and should not be consumed in excess. The large amount of cake flour has low protein content, ideal for achieving a crispy texture, but pay attention to baking time to avoid hardness.
