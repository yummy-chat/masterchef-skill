## Preparation Steps
1.  Slaughter and clean the longsnout catfish. Scald the fish body with warm water and scrape off the white mucus on the surface to thoroughly remove the fishy smell. Then, fillet the fish and cut the bones into sections.
2.  Place the fish fillets and bones in a bowl. Add salt, ground white pepper, scallion sections, shredded ginger, and cooking wine. Mix well and marinate to remove any fishy odor.
3.  Crush the ginger and then dice it. Crush the garlic cloves. Cut the scallions into rings. Dice the pickled young ginger and pickled radish. Finely chop the pickled chili. Cut the garlic sprouts and scallions into sections. Peel the purple-skinned single-clove garlic and set aside.
4.  Place the wok over high heat and heat it up. Pour in cooking oil. When the oil is hot, add the Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then, add the minced ginger, garlic, scallion rings, diced pickled young ginger, diced pickled radish, and finely chopped pickled chili. Stir-fry until the aroma is released. Finally, add 1 tablespoon of doubanjiang and stir-fry until the oil turns a bright red color.
5.  Add the fish bones and head to the wok and stir-fry briefly. Add an appropriate amount of water, then add the peeled single-clove garlic. Bring to a boil over high heat.
6.  After the soup comes to a boil, season with salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, and ground white pepper.
7.  Slide the marinated fish fillets one by one into the boiling soup. Turn the heat to high and cook for about 5 minutes, until the fish fillets are fully cooked.
8.  Add the garlic sprout sections. Drizzle in the Sichuan pepper oil, light soy sauce, and chili essence. Gently push to combine. Add the water starch in two batches to thicken the sauce, adjusting to a suitable consistency.
9.  Transfer the entire wok of fish into a large soup bowl. Sprinkle the top with minced garlic, dried chili pepper sections, and scallion sections. In a separate wok, heat a spoonful of oil until it is smoking hot. Pour the hot oil over the minced garlic and dried chili peppers to release their fragrance. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Longsnout catfish is low in fat and high in protein. Combined with fermented pickled peppers and single-clove garlic, it aids digestion and boosts immunity. It is suitable for people with high internal dampness or poor appetite. Note that pickled vegetables and doubanjiang are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume the soup sparingly.
