# Green Pepper Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## 🥬 Ingredients
🌾 Flour 500g
🫙 Yeast 3-5g
🍬 Sugar 5-8g (for kneading dough)
💧 Warm water as needed (water temperature should feel neither hot nor cold when tested with a finger for three seconds)
🫑 Green peppers, as needed (julienned then diced)
🌭 Starch sausage, as needed (diced)

## 🧂 Seasonings
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for filling)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (for filling)
🌿 Cumin powder, as needed (adjust to taste)
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed (adjust to taste)
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (added last to the filling for aroma and to seal in moisture)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying)

## 🍳 Kitchen Tools and Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🪓 Rolling pin
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Non-stick pan/frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, yeast, and sugar. Gradually add warm water while stirring, until a flaky dough forms.
   **Key point**: Sugar provides nutrients for the yeast to help fermentation. Water temperature should feel neither hot nor cold when tested with a finger for three seconds.
2. Knead the dough repeatedly by hand until the surface is smooth, achieving a clean bowl, clean hands, and smooth dough.
3. Quick fermentation: Bring 8 bowls of water to a boil in a pot. Turn off the heat, add 3 bowls of cold water. Cover the bowl of dough with a lid or plastic wrap, place it in the steamer, and let it ferment in the warm, humid steam environment until it doubles in volume.
4. Wash the green peppers. First julienne them, then dice into small pieces. Dice the starch sausage into small pieces.
5. Place the diced green peppers and starch sausage into a bowl. Add salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, cumin powder, and chili powder. Finally, drizzle with sesame oil. Mix thoroughly and set aside.
   **Key point**: Adjust the amount of cumin powder and chili powder according to personal taste. Add sesame oil last to enhance flavor and seal in moisture.
6. Remove the fermented dough. Knead it on a cutting board to expel air, then divide it into two equal portions. Take one portion and roll it into a large square sheet with a rolling pin, dusting with enough dry flour to prevent sticking.
7. Spread the green pepper filling evenly over the dough sheet, leaving the top and bottom edges empty while covering the left and right sides fully. Then fold it like a blanket from one end to form a rectangle. Cut into long strips about 5–6 cm wide.
   **Key point**: Spread the filling evenly, and fold as neatly as possible.
8. **Method one**: Place the dough pieces into the steamer, leaving space between them. Once the steamer is steaming, steam over medium heat for about 10 minutes. Turn off the heat and let sit for 5 minutes, until the pieces have expanded and spring back when pressed.
   **Method two**: Heat a non-stick pan over high heat for 30 seconds, then reduce to the lowest heat. Pour in a small amount of cooking oil and swirl to coat the bottom. Heat for another 30 seconds. Place the dough pieces in the pan, keep the heat on the lowest setting, cover with a lid, and pan-fry for 3 minutes. Flip them, add a little more oil if the pan looks dry, cover again, and pan-fry for another 3 minutes. Turn off the heat and remove the lid.
   **Key point**: Always use the lowest heat during pan-frying to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains raw.

## Health Advice
Green pepper pancake contains starch sausage. It is recommended to pair it with leafy greens or eggs to balance protein and vitamins. Suitable for those who love pasta and quick breakfasts. When pan-frying, use the lowest heat and slow cooking to avoid the outside burning while the inside stays raw.
