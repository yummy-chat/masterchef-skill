# Thousand-Layer Scallion Hand-Pulled Pancake
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
🧂 Salt 5g (for kneading the dough)
🌶️ Sichuan pepper salt 3g
🫒 Corn oil 15g (for kneading the dough, plus extra for making oil pastry and frying)
💧 Warm water 250-260ml
🧅 Chopped scallions (as needed, chopped, for making oil pastry)
⚪️ White sesame seeds (as needed)
🥚 Egg 1 (can add more according to preference)
🌶️ Chili sauce (as needed, for application after frying; type depends on personal taste)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for kneading and resting dough)
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board
🫗 Small bowl (for mixing oil pastry)
🔩 Metal work surface (for rolling and shaping)
🥖 Rolling pin
🍳 Non-stick frying pan or electric griddle
🥄 Silicone spatula
🧻 Parchment paper (for separating raw pancakes, convenient for freezer storage)

## Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: Add 5g salt, 3g Sichuan pepper salt, and 15g corn oil to 500g all-purpose flour. Gradually add 250-260ml warm water, stir, and knead into a smooth dough.
   **Key point**: Adjust water amount based on the flour's absorbency; the dough must be kneaded until the surface is smooth.
2. **Rest the dough**: Place the kneaded dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for 30-40 minutes.
   **Key point**: Do not knead again after resting; the dough will have the best elasticity at this point.
3. **Make the thin oil pastry**: In a small bowl, place the chopped scallions, one spoonful of flour, and an appropriate amount of white sesame seeds. Pour in heated cooking oil and mix well.
   **Key point**: Pouring hot oil over the ingredients fully releases the aroma of scallions and sesame.
4. **Divide the dough**: Move the rested dough to the work surface. Shape it directly into a long strip. Use a knife to cut it into several evenly sized pieces.
5. **Shape and apply oil pastry**: Take one dough piece. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a thin, long strip. Evenly brush a layer of thin oil pastry over the surface.
   **Key point**: The strip should not be rolled too thin.
6. **Roll and cut**: Starting from one end of the strip, slowly roll it up to form a cylindrical roll. Use a knife to cut the roll in half lengthwise, exposing the inner layers.
   **Key point**: Cutting in half increases the number of layers in the finished product.
7. **Coil the pancake**: Stack the two halves of the cut roll with the cut sides facing up. Hold both ends with your hands, gently twist them like twisting a rope, then coil the twisted strip from both ends towards the center. Pinch the ends to seal and press into a round pancake base.
   **Key point**: The techniques of cutting, stacking, twisting, and coiling create rich layers.
8. **Roll out the pancake**: Use a rolling pin to gently roll out the coiled pancake base into a round pancake of moderate thickness.
   **Key point**: Apply gentle force to prevent the layers from sticking together.
9. **Fry the pancake**: Preheat a frying pan or electric griddle. Optionally brush a little oil or use no oil. Place the pancake base in the pan. Fry over medium-low heat.
   **Heat judgment**: Flip when the bottom is set and has light brown spots.
10. **Add egg and seasoning**: After both sides of the pancake are golden brown, crack an egg onto the pancake and spread it evenly. Once the egg is set, flip the pancake to cook the other side. Finally, brush with chili sauce or other sauces according to preference.
    **Key point**: Adding an egg enriches the texture. Uncooked raw pancakes can be separated with parchment paper and stored in the freezer for quick breakfasts.

## Health Advice
Hand-pulled pancakes are rich in carbohydrates. It is recommended to eat them with vegetables and protein to help maintain stable blood sugar levels. The fully rested dough is easier to digest, making this dish suitable for busy people who need a quick energy boost.
