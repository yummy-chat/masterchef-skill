# Lamb Offal Soup
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lamb offal (lamb tripe, lamb intestines, lamb liver, lamb lungs, etc., mixed)  
💧 Boiling water  
💧 Water  
🫒 Cooking oil  
🧂 Salt  
🌶️ Ground white pepper  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Soup pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🫖 Clay pot  
🥣 Strainer  
🥣 Bowl  

## Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the lamb offal and blanch. Once boiling, skim off the foam thoroughly, then remove and drain well.  
**Key point**: Blanching removes the gamey smell and is the foundation for a clear and clean-flavored soup. Be sure to skim off all the foam.  

2. Add a little cooking oil to the pot. Add the drained blanched lamb offal and stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture evaporates and the surface turns slightly yellow.  
**Key point**: Make sure to fry thoroughly until the moisture is completely gone. Keep stirring until no visible steam escapes.  

3. Pour boiling water into the pot with the stir-fried lamb offal. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
**Key point**: Boiling water must be used; the soup will immediately turn a milky white.  

4. After boiling, thoroughly skim off any foam from the surface of the soup.  
**Key point**: Foam is one of the sources of the gamey smell; it must be skimmed clean.  

5. Reduce the heat to medium, cover the pot, and simmer for about 10 minutes.  

6. Use a slotted spoon to remove all the lamb offal from the pot. Set the soup aside separately.  
**Key point**: This prepares for the "second stir-fry," which makes the soup richer.  

7. In another pot, return the removed lamb offal and stir-fry again over medium heat until the moisture evaporates.  
**Key point**: Again, stir-fry until the moisture is gone. This is the core step to enhance the soup's color concentration and flavor richness.  

8. Pour the reserved original soup back into the pot with the twice-stir-fried lamb offal. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
9. Once boiling again, skim off any small amount of foam that may appear on the surface.  

10. Reduce the heat to medium and continue simmering for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  

11. Season with salt and a little ground white pepper to taste. Stir well, then turn off the heat.  
12. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with chopped scallions and cilantro before serving.  

## Health Tips
Lamb offal soup is rich in protein, iron, and zinc, making it suitable for winter nourishment. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Blanching and the second stir-fry effectively reduce the gamey smell and enhance the aroma. Be mindful of the salt amount when seasoning.
