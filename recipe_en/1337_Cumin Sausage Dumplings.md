# Cumin Sausage Dumplings

Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the sausage into uniform small cubes; beat the eggs in a bowl until well blended; prepare the chopped scallion, diced bell peppers, chopped spring onion, and both types of cumin.
2. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, pour in the beaten eggs. When the edges begin to set and the center is still semi-liquid, **immediately turn off the heat**. Use the residual heat to gently push and scramble the eggs into soft, tender curds. Remove from the wok and set aside.
   **Note**: Turning off the heat and using residual heat allows the eggs to reach a "just-cooked" state, resulting in an extremely tender texture.
3. Leave a thin layer of oil in the wok. Add the diced sausage and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the surface is lightly browned and fragrant.
4. Add the chopped scallion and cumin seeds. Reduce the heat to low or turn off the heat, and gently toss to combine, being careful not to burn the spices.
5. Remove from heat, immediately add the ground cumin, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, and matsutake seasoning. Use the residual heat to mix well.
   **Note**: Adding seasonings off the heat prevents scorching and fully preserves their aroma.
6. Add the scrambled eggs back to the wok and mix thoroughly with the sausage and seasonings.
7. Once the filling has cooled down, add the diced bell peppers and chopped spring onion. Drizzle with sesame oil and mix to seal in moisture and ensure a crisp texture.
8. Take a dumpling wrapper, place a portion of filling in the center, and seal the edges firmly to form a dumpling.
9. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the dumplings and gently stir with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. Cook until all dumplings float to the surface and the wrappers become translucent. Drain well.

## Health Advice
This dish wraps a filling of cumin-scented sausage and tender eggs in dumpling skins, providing protein and vitamin C from the bell peppers. It is suitable as a quick main meal or late-night snack. Note that sausage is relatively high in sodium; those sensitive to blood pressure are advised to serve these dumplings with a light vegetable soup.
