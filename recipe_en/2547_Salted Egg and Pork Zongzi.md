# Salted Egg and Pork Zongzi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥡 Glutinous rice 3 jin (soak in water for 2 hours, drain and set aside)
🥩 Pork belly appropriate amount (cut into small pieces, can substitute with lean meat)
🥚 Salted duck eggs appropriate amount (use the firm, translucent yolks)
🌿 Bamboo leaves appropriate amount (dried bamboo leaves recommended for beginners)
🧂 Salt one spoon (for seasoning glutinous rice) + appropriate amount (for marinating pork)
🥢 Light soy sauce 2-3 spoons (for seasoning glutinous rice) + appropriate amount (for marinating pork)
🍯 Dark soy sauce a little (for coloring glutinous rice and pork)
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount (for marinating pork)
🍶 Baijiu a little (for removing pork odor) + a little (for spraying on egg yolks)
🍬 MSG or white sugar optional, a little (for enhancing flavor when marinating pork)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🪣 Large basin (for soaking glutinous rice)
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating pork, handling egg yolks)
🪣 Water basin
🪥 Brush
✂️ Scissors
🥄 Spoon
🧵 Cotton string
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the glutinous rice in clean water for 2 hours until fully hydrated, then drain and set aside.
2. Add one spoon of salt and 2-3 spoons of light soy sauce to the drained glutinous rice, optionally add a little dark soy sauce for color, and mix thoroughly.
3. Cut the pork belly into small pieces about 2 cm square and place in a bowl.
4. First, add a little baijiu or ginger-scallion water and mix to remove the odor. Then add salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Optionally add a little MSG or white sugar to enhance flavor according to personal preference. Mix well and marinate for at least 4 hours.
5. Crack open the salted duck eggs, remove the firm and translucent yolks, rinse with clean water to remove any remaining egg white film, drain, and evenly spray with a small amount of baijiu. Set aside.
6. Wash each bamboo leaf thoroughly with a brush, and trim the ends with scissors. Dried bamboo leaves are recommended for beginners as they are moderately pliable and less likely to tear.
7. Take two bamboo leaves, place them tip to tip in opposite directions. Fold the middle section toward yourself to form a funnel shape, ensuring the bottom is tight and seamless. Fill with ingredients in the order: "glutinous rice → salted egg yolk + marinated pork → glutinous rice". Finally, add a top layer of glutinous rice and press firmly.
8. Cover the opening by folding the top bamboo leaf over, pinch the sides to seal, and fold the excess into a corner shape. Wrap cotton string around the zongzi several times, tying securely to ensure no rice leaks out during cooking.
9. Arrange the finished zongzi neatly in the pressure cooker inner pot. Add enough clean water to completely submerge the zongzi. Select the "Beans/Tendons" function and pressure cook for 2 hours, then let it rest with residual heat for 1 hour.
   **Key point**: The water must fully cover the zongzi, and sufficient cooking time is essential for a soft and sticky texture.
10. After cooking, remove the zongzi and let them cool slightly before unwrapping the bamboo leaves and enjoying.

## Health Advice
Salted egg and pork zongzi are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to eat one at a time and pair it with unsweetened tea to cut the grease. Glutinous rice is sticky and difficult to digest; the elderly and those with weaker digestive functions should chew slowly. Pork belly and salted egg yolks are high in cholesterol, so moderate intake is advised.
