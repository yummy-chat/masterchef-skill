# Fish Soup with Shredded Fish
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🐟 1 large silver carp (about 13 jin / 6.5 kg)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, as needed (pre-boiled and shredded)
🍄 Small Northeast black fungus, as needed (pre-soaked and shredded)
🥬 Yellow chives, as needed (cut into sections)
🥒 Luffa (sponge gourd), as needed (peeled and shredded)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (partially cut into pieces, partially shredded)
🍊 1 small piece dried tangerine peel (shredded)

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating fish pieces and seasoning the soup)
🌾 Starch, as needed (dry starch for marinating, water starch for thickening)
🍶 Rice wine, about 1–2 liang (50–100 ml)
🍗 Chicken bouillon, a little
🍬 Sugar, as needed
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch
🫗 Sesame oil, a little (optional)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Frying pan
🥘 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Fine mesh strainer / filter
🫕 Clay pot (sand pot)

## Cooking Steps
1. Bleed the silver carp, scale it, remove the innards, and wash it clean. Split the fish head in half, remove the gills; cut the fish body into pieces.  
**Note**: Fish scales, gills, and innards must be thoroughly removed to reduce any fishy smell.

2. Rub the fish head and body pieces with salt and dry starch, let them sit for a while, then rinse clean with water.  
**Key point**: Salt and starch effectively remove the muddy smell from the fish skin.

3. Prepare all the shredded shiitake mushrooms, black fungus, yellow chive sections, shredded luffa, ginger pieces, shredded ginger, and shredded tangerine peel in advance.
4. Heat oil in a frying pan, add the fish head and fish pieces, fry until both sides are golden brown and show a dark brown crust, then remove.  
**Purpose**: To enhance aroma and eliminate fishiness.

5. Heat oil in a wok, sizzle the ginger pieces until fragrant, then pour off the excess oil. Add the fried fish pieces, splash in the rice wine, and cook over high heat to let the alcohol evaporate. Pour in enough boiling water, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, cover, and cook for 10 minutes. Remove the fish head, continue to boil the remaining fish bones and meat over high heat for another 20 minutes, until the soup turns milky white.  
**Key point**: Only boiling water should be added, and keep the heat high to allow full emulsification and achieve a white soup.

6. Strain the fish soup through a fine mesh strainer, retaining only the liquid; discard the bones and meat.
7. Let the cooked fish head cool, then use chopsticks to carefully remove all bones and impurities, yielding pure fish meat.  
**Key point**: Thorough deboning is the core step of “shredded fish soup.”

8. Pour the strained fish soup into a clay pot and add the shredded tangerine peel. Heat a little oil in a frying pan, add the shredded fish meat and shredded ginger, lightly fry over medium-low heat, then transfer everything, including the ginger, into the clay pot with the fish soup.  
**Key point**: Pan-frying again helps remove any remaining fishy smell from the fish head.

9. Add the shredded shiitake mushrooms and black fungus to the clay pot. Season with salt, sugar, and a little chicken bouillon. After it comes to a boil, drizzle in water starch to thicken, stirring continuously until the soup becomes smooth.
10. Once it boils again, add the shredded luffa and yellow chive sections, sprinkle in a pinch of white pepper, and add a drop of sesame oil if desired. Stir well and serve immediately.  
**Note**: Add the quick-cooking vegetables last to keep them tender and crisp.

## Health Advice
This fish soup with shredded fish is a classic Cantonese skill-demanding dish. The soup is milky white and smooth, suitable for people with weak spleen and stomach or those needing high-quality protein. Ensure all fish bones are thoroughly removed; people with gout should limit consumption of rich broths.
