# Cold Tossed Beef Tripe
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐂 Beef Tripe (Honeycomb Tripe) 1 piece (about 1.2 jin)
🧅 Onion 1/4 piece (shredded)
🌿 Cilantro A small bunch (chopped)
🍄 Black Fungus A small handful (pre-cooked, cut into wide strips)
🧄 Garlic To taste (minced)
🌶️ Small Chili Peppers To taste (sliced into rings)
🧅 Scallions To taste (tied into a knot, for braising)
🫚 Ginger A few slices (for braising)
🌶️ Dried Chili Segments About 5-6 pieces
🟤 Sichuan Peppercorns A small handful
🫒 Cooking Oil About 3-4 tablespoons (for oil splashing)
🧂 Salt To taste (3 tablespoons for braising, about 1/2 teaspoon for mixing)
🍬 White Sugar To taste (1 tablespoon for braising, about 1 teaspoon for mixing)
🧂 MSG (Monosodium Glutamate) To taste
🧂 Light Soy Sauce About 1 tablespoon
🍶 Aged Vinegar About 1 tablespoon
🫒 Sesame Oil About 1 teaspoon
🌶️ Spicy Chili Oil (Homemade) 1 large tablespoon
🍶 Yellow Wine (Cooking Wine) To taste (for braising)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure Cooker
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl (for mixing)
🍶 Bowls, Spoons

## Preparation Steps
1.  Thoroughly scrub the beef tripe with flour to remove slime and odor, then cut into large pieces.
2.  Place the tripe in a pot with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then remove and rinse clean.
3.  Add fresh water to the pot, then add the tripe, scallion knot, ginger slices, 3 tablespoons of salt, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and an appropriate amount of yellow wine. Cover with the pressure cooker lid, bring to high pressure, then reduce heat to low and cook for 15 minutes. After cooking, let the tripe cool naturally in the braising liquid to fully absorb flavor and become easier to slice.
4.  Once cooled, slice the tripe diagonally into wide, thin pieces, maintaining an even thickness.
5.  Place the dried chili segments and Sichuan peppercorns in a small bowl. Heat oil in a wok until it smokes, then promptly pour the hot oil over the spices in the bowl to release a fragrant, charred aroma. Set aside to cool.
6.  Put the sliced tripe into a stainless steel bowl. First, add the prepared chili oil, then add the shredded onion, minced garlic, sliced small chili peppers, chopped cilantro, and black fungus strips. Add an appropriate amount of salt, sugar, MSG, light soy sauce, aged vinegar, and sesame oil. Finally, drizzle with the homemade spicy chili oil. Mix everything thoroughly and evenly. Transfer to a serving plate.
   **Key Points**: Honeycomb tripe has many cavities, making it better at absorbing sauce than other cuts. Cooling naturally in the braising liquid prevents the tripe from shrinking and allows for thinner, more attractive slices. The oil for the chili and Sichuan peppercorn mixture must be at a high temperature to instantly bring out their scorched fragrance. Adding the seasoned oil first during mixing helps lock in the flavors; adding the spicy chili oil last gives the final dish a bright, glossy red appearance.

## Health Advice
Beef tripe is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for individuals needing to replenish blood or who enjoy a chewy texture. However, those with high blood pressure should control their intake of the salty braising liquid. Adding more vinegar and garlic when preparing this cold dish can help kill bacteria and stimulate the appetite.
