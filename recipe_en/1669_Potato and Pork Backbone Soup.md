# Potato and Pork Backbone Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Ingredients
🥩 Pork backbone 1500g (cut into pieces, not too small)
🥬 Dried Chinese cabbage 120g (needs to be soaked overnight in advance, or substitute with 400g fresh bok choy)
🥔 Potatoes about 500g (two medium-sized, cut in half or into three pieces)
🧅 Scallion about 110g (100g cut into large diagonal sections for stewing, 10g sliced for final garnish)
🧅 Onion 50g (about 1/6, cut into thick strips)
🧄 Garlic 25g (15g sliced for stewing, 10g minced for marinating dried cabbage)
🫚 Ginger 7g (4 small pieces, sliced)
🌿 Licorice 1g (about the size of a thumbnail, do not use too much)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers as needed (about 5-6)
🍄 Enoki mushrooms 50g (trim roots, tear into small clusters)
🥬 Crown daisy 50g (wash, cut into two sections)

### Seasonings
🌿 Bay leaf 1 piece (for blanching the backbone)
🍶 Cooking wine 20g (about 2 tablespoons)
🫘 Korean soybean paste (doenjang) 60g (30g for first stew of backbone, 30g for marinating dried cabbage)
🌶️ Korean chili paste (gochujang) 20g (about half a tablespoon)
🧂 White pepper powder 1g
🍃 Perilla seed powder 10g (for marinating dried cabbage, also prepare enough for final sprinkling, do not omit)
🌶️ Chili flakes 10-20g (for marinating dried cabbage)
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 15g (for final seasoning)
🌶️ Fine chili powder 10g (for final seasoning)
🧂 Soy sauce 10g (about 1 tablespoon)
🧂 Beef bouillon powder 15g (not recommended to omit)
🧂 MSG 3g (about 1 teaspoon)
🧂 Salt as needed (add after tasting in the final seasoning stage)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🥘 Glass deep stew pot (for blanching and first stew)
🍲 Stainless steel shallow pot (for second stew and serving)
🥣 Strainer (for filtering broth)
🪣 Stainless steel basin/large bowl (for soaking dried cabbage, mixing marinated cabbage, and holding raw backbone)
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥄 Spoons (soup spoon, teaspoon)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place backbone pieces in cold water in a pot, add 1 bay leaf, and heat on the stove.
   **Note**: Backbone pieces should not be cut too small; if the meat is fresh, no additional deodorizing ingredients are needed when blanching.
2. After the water comes to a boil, turn off the heat, remove the backbone, and rinse clean with warm water. Set aside.
   **Note**: Keep the heat high throughout the blanching process, turning the bones occasionally to ensure even heating.
3. Cut scallion into large diagonal sections, slice onion into thick strips, slice garlic, slice ginger, and prepare licorice and dried chili peppers.
   **Note**: The amount of scallion must be sufficient; do not use too much licorice, or it will taste overly medicinal.
4. Soak dried cabbage in water overnight in advance. If using fresh bok choy, blanch in salted water for 1 minute, then squeeze out the water.
5. Trim the tough roots from the soaked dried cabbage, rinse repeatedly to remove sand and dirt, then squeeze out the water.
6. Mix the squeezed-dried cabbage with 30g Korean soybean paste, 10g perilla seed powder, 10-20g chili flakes, 10g soy sauce, and 10g minced garlic. Mix evenly with your hands and set aside.
   **Key point**: Perilla seed powder is a soul ingredient and cannot be omitted.
7. Peel potatoes and cut into two or three pieces; trim enoki mushrooms, tear into small clusters; wash and cut crown daisy into sections; slice scallion into thin pieces. Set aside.
8. Place the cleaned backbone into a glass deep stew pot, add about 3000ml water, add all the stewing ingredients, cooking wine, 30g Korean soybean paste, Korean chili paste, and white pepper powder.
9. Stir all ingredients well, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover the pot, and stew for 1 hour.
10. Turn off the heat. Take the stainless steel shallow pot, spread the marinated dried cabbage on the bottom, arrange potato pieces in a circle along the edge, then arrange the stewed backbone pieces.
11. Pour the backbone stew broth through a strainer into the shallow pot, straining out the solids. Press the solids to extract as much broth as possible.
12. Add coarse chili flakes, fine chili powder, beef bouillon powder, and MSG to the shallow pot.
    **Note**: The amount of chili powder can be adjusted according to taste; beef bouillon powder is not recommended to be omitted.
13. Place the shallow pot back on the stove, bring to a boil over high heat and stir to mix the seasonings. After boiling, reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and stew for 25 minutes.
14. After the time is up, gently turn the dried cabbage to the surface of the broth. Taste and add salt as needed.
    **Note**: If eating all in one meal, add enough salt at once; if eating while it simmers, add less salt to prevent it from becoming too salty as it cooks.
15. Arrange crown daisy and enoki mushrooms on the surface of the soup, sprinkle with scallion slices, and finally sprinkle generously with perilla seed powder.
    **Note**: Fresh vegetables cook quickly; if not using as a hotpot, they can be eaten after blanching in the soup for 1-2 minutes.

## Health Advice
This potato and pork backbone soup is rich in protein and calcium, especially suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter. Pay attention to controlling the amount of potatoes and seasonings to avoid excessive calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the saltiness and spiciness. People with high blood pressure can reduce the salt as appropriate.
