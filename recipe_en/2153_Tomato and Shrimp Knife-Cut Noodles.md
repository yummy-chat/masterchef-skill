# Tomato and Shrimp Knife-Cut Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Sea shrimp, about 12 pieces (8 for shrimp meat, 4 reserved with heads and shells)
🍅 Large tomato, 1 piece (seeded and diced into 1 cm cubes)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes, a handful (halved)
🍜 Dried knife-cut noodles, 1 serving (about 100-150g)
🥫 Tomato paste/ketchup, 1 teaspoon
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌶️ Chili powder, a little
🧂 Black pepper, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🦐 Shrimp oil, 1 teaspoon (can substitute with rendered oil from frying shrimp)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Stainless steel frying pan/wok
🥘 Soup pot
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer/sieve
🧻 Paper towels
🥢 Kitchen tongs
⏲️ Timer

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the heads and shells from the sea shrimp. Make a shallow cut along the back of the shrimp meat to devein. Pat the shrimp heads, shells, and meat dry separately with paper towels.
   **Key Point**: Handling separately and drying thoroughly prevents oil splatter and promotes browning.
2. Seed the large tomato and dice into 1 cm cubes. Halve the cherry tomatoes.
3. Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Place the shrimp meat in the pan and fry one side until golden red. Flip and fry until both sides change color and the center is translucent and slightly soft. Remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Do not overcook to maintain the tenderness of the shrimp.
4. Leave the base oil in the pan. Add the dried shrimp heads and shells. Stir-fry over medium heat until they change color and red oil is released. Add the tomato paste and stir until fragrant. Then add all the diced tomatoes and stir-fry until aromatic.
5. Pour in enough water to submerge the shrimp heads. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for 20 minutes, until the broth turns a rich orange-red color.
   **Key Point**: Do not simmer seafood soup for too long to preserve its sweetness. Scum does not need to be removed.
6. Strain the broth using a strainer. Firmly press the shrimp heads and shells to extract all the essence.
7. Return the strained shrimp broth to the pan. Simmer over medium heat until reduced to about one-quarter of its original volume, forming a thick sauce.
8. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add salt. Cook the dried knife-cut noodles for about 2 minutes. Drain and directly add them to the pot with the thick sauce.
9. Add the fried shrimp meat, salt, black pepper, and a little chili powder to the sauce pot. Quickly toss and stir for about 1 minute, ensuring the sauce evenly coats the noodles.
10. Plate the noodles and drizzle with shrimp oil to enhance flavor and add shine.

## Health Advice
This Tomato and Shrimp Knife-Cut Noodles dish is rich in high-quality protein from the shrimp and lycopene from the tomatoes, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle and meet antioxidant needs. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp meat. Although the broth is sweet, it is relatively high in salt; people with high blood pressure should consume less of the broth.
