# Plum Spareribs
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork spareribs 1000g (soak in water for 30 minutes to remove blood, then pat dry)  
🍑 Sweet preserved plums 40-45g (about 13-15 pieces, choose ones with moderate acidity and not too salty or sweet)  
⭐ Star anise 1 (large) or 1.5 (small)  
🫚 Ginger 7g (cut into 4 slices)  
🍬 Rock sugar (or white sugar) 30g  
🍶 White vinegar (5% acidity) 40g (used in two parts: 20g for the sauce, 20g drizzled along the pan edge before serving)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g  
🥢 Red fermented bean curd juice 10g  
🍅 Tomato ketchup 30g  
🧴 Dark soy sauce approx. 7g (2/3 spoon)  
🍾 Yellow wine 10g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)  
🍲 Wooden lid  
🥄 Long-handled spoon or spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the spareribs**: Soak the pork spareribs in clean water for 30 minutes to remove blood. Drain and discard the water, then pat the surface dry with kitchen paper towels. Place a paper towel at the bottom of the bowl for later use.  
   **Note**: Drying the surface of the spareribs effectively reduces oil splatter during stir-frying. The paper towel at the bottom prevents water accumulation, ensuring a good searing effect.  

2. **Prepare spices and preserved plums**: Set aside the star anise and ginger slices; take out the preserved plums for later use.  
   **Note**: Do not use too much ginger, as its flavor may overpower the sweet and sour taste. Add the preserved plums later during braising—adding them too early may cause them to disintegrate.  

3. **Mix the sauce**: In a small bowl, combine rock sugar, 20g white vinegar, light soy sauce, red fermented bean curd juice, tomato ketchup, and dark soy sauce. Stir until the rock sugar dissolves.  
   **Note**: There is no need to caramelize sugar separately. The red fermented bean curd juice enhances color and reduces greasiness, while tomato ketchup adds depth to the sweet and sour flavor without an overt tomato taste.  

4. **Stir-fry the spareribs**: Pour an amount of oil typical for stir-frying into an iron wok. Heat the oil, then add the ginger slices. Fry until the edges turn golden and the wok begins to smoke. Place the spareribs along the edge of the wok and stir-fry over maximum heat for about 5 minutes, until the spareribs are slightly browned and the oil in the wok turns from cloudy to clear.  
   **Note**: Ensure the wok is hot enough to prevent sticking. Stir-fry until the oil is clear to effectively remove any gamey smell and enhance aroma.  

5. **Deglaze with yellow wine**: Pour the yellow wine along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly until the alcohol evaporates completely.  
   **Note**: Use the high heat at the wok’s edge to release the wine’s fragrance.  

6. **Add spices and sauce**: Add the star anise, then pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly to coat the spareribs evenly with the sauce.  
   **Note**: Work rapidly to prevent the sauce from burning due to high heat.  

7. **Braise the spareribs**: Add about 700ml of boiling water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover with a wooden lid and reduce to the lowest heat. Braise for 40 minutes.  

8. **Add preserved plums for a second braise**: After 40 minutes, remove the star anise and ginger slices. Add the preserved plums, bring to a boil over high heat, then cover again and braise over the lowest heat for another 20 minutes.  
   **Note**: Adding the preserved plums at this stage allows their aroma to fully release without overcooking them.  

9. **Reduce the sauce and serve**: Increase to high heat to reduce the sauce. Once the sauce has thickened to your liking, drizzle the remaining 20g of white vinegar along the edge of the wok, stir quickly to combine, then turn off the heat and plate.  
   **Note**: Adding vinegar along the wok edge just before serving instantly elevates the sweet and sour flavor profile.  

## Health Tips  
These plum spareribs are sweet and sour, stimulating the appetite—ideal for when you have no desire to eat. However, the sweet and sour sauce is relatively high in sugar, so it’s best to pair with light vegetables to balance the meal. Spareribs are also high in fat; those with high blood lipids can remove the skin before braising.
