# Stir-fried Pork Face Meat with Mushrooms and Onion over Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐷 Pork face meat, about 250g
🧅 Onion, 1 piece (julienned)
🍄 Fresh mushrooms, about 300g (sliced)
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, 2 slices
🧄 Garlic, 3 cloves (crushed)
🍶 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🥢 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍚 Rice cooker
🍽️ Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1.  Slice the pork face meat into thin pieces, place them in a stainless steel bowl. Add ginger, garlic, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and cornstarch. Mix well, then drizzle in cooking oil and toss to coat, allowing the meat to marinate and absorb the flavors.
   **Key Point**: Slice the meat as thinly as possible for a crisper and more tender texture; adding oil at the end helps seal in the meat juices, so no additional oil is needed in the pan during stir-frying.
2.  Julienne the onion and slice the fresh mushrooms, then rinse and drain thoroughly. Use a generous amount of onion; it becomes sweet and tender after braising and is key to enhancing the flavor.
3.  Place the non-stick pan on a cold stove without lighting the heat. First, spread the marinated meat slices evenly over the pan bottom, then layer the julienned onion and sliced mushrooms on top.
   **Key Point**: The order for layering ingredients in a cold pan is meat first, then vegetables. This prevents burning and allows the vegetables' released moisture to braise the meat slices.
4.  Cover the pan with a lid and heat over medium heat. Observe the liquid released from the vegetables; if there is little liquid, adjust the heat slightly higher. Braise for about 10–15 minutes until the meat slices are fully cooked through, and the onion and mushrooms have softened.
   **Note**: Avoid using leafy greens; vegetables like onion and mushrooms that can withstand braising are best for this method.
5.  Remove the lid, and use a wooden spatula to stir-fry the ingredients evenly. Check for doneness; if not fully cooked, stir-fry briefly until the liquid reduces, then turn off the heat.
6.  Scoop the steamed rice into a bowl, and top with the stir-fried pork face meat, mushrooms, and onion to form a rice bowl.

## Health Advice
Pork face meat is rich in collagen, while mushrooms provide dietary fiber. Paired with onion, this dish helps boost immunity and is suitable for those with high physical exertion. Note that pork face meat is relatively high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance calorie intake.
