# Thick Cut Fried Yogurt
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥭 Mango 2 (about 180g flesh, some diced for filling and decoration)
🥛 Yogurt 300g (mixed with mango to make a puree)
🥛 Whipping cream 150g (separated from a total of 450g)
🍬 White sugar 40g (whipped together with whipping cream)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🔪 Blender or food processor
🥄 Electric mixer
🥄 Spatula
🧊 Square mold (lined with parchment paper)
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥣 Large bowl

## Instructions
1. **Make mango yogurt puree**
   Place about 180g of mango flesh in a blender, add 300g yogurt, and blend until smooth with no lumps.

2. **Whip the cream**
   Add 40g white sugar to 450g whipping cream, and whip with an electric mixer until it reaches 80% stiffness.
   **Key point**: Avoid whipping to stiff peaks, as this may make subsequent mixing difficult or result in a hard final texture.

3. **Combine the base**
   Take 150g of the whipped cream and add it to the mango yogurt puree. Fold gently with a spatula until the color and texture are completely uniform.
   **Note**: Use a folding motion rather than stirring to prevent deflation.

4. **Mold and layer**
   In the parchment-lined square mold, pour half of the mixed mango yogurt puree.
   Evenly spread a layer of diced mango as a filling.
   Pour the remaining half of the mango yogurt puree, smooth the surface, and sprinkle an appropriate amount of diced mango on top for decoration.

5. **Freeze to set**
   Place the mold in the freezer for at least 5 hours, until completely frozen solid.

6. **Final processing**
   Remove the frozen yogurt block, cut into evenly sized squares, and serve.
   **Texture**: Smooth and creamy, rich in milky flavor, with a pleasant icy slush feel without being too hard. The mango flavor is sweet and tangy with a refreshing aroma, and the diced filling adds layers of texture.

## Health Recommendations
This thick cut fried yogurt combines mango and yogurt with whipping cream, rich in calcium and probiotics, making it ideal for summer refreshment. Note that the sugar and cream are not low in calories, so portion control is recommended. For those with sensitive digestion or during weight loss, reduce the proportion of whipping cream.
