# Spicy Beef Tripe Hotpot
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫁 Fresh beef tripe – about 1 whole (main ingredient, needs to be split and cleaned)
🐂 Beef – appropriate amount (sliced into large pieces)
🧅 Scallions, ginger – appropriate amount (for stir-frying the seasoning)
🫘 Broad bean paste (doubanjiang) – appropriate amount
🧱 Hotpot base – a few chunks
🌶️ Dried chilies – appropriate amount
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns – appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves – a few pieces
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount (for marinating beef and seasoning the broth)
🧂 Chicken bouillon – appropriate amount (for marinating beef and enhancing the broth)
🧂 White pepper – appropriate amount (for marinating beef to remove gameyness and enhance flavor)
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes – appropriate amount (for marinating beef)
💧 Scallion-ginger water – appropriate amount (to tenderize and remove gameyness)
🫒 Sesame oil – appropriate amount (for dipping sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥣 Metal bowl
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🚿 Handheld hose/water faucet
🔥 Portable hotpot stove
🥢 Chopsticks, bowls

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Prepare the tripe**: Rinse the surface of the fresh beef tripe clean, split it open, and remove any internal debris. Then unfold each piece of tripe and rinse repeatedly under running water, turning them like pages of a book, until no impurities remain.
2. **Marinate the spicy beef**: Cut the beef into large slices and place in a bowl. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and scallion-ginger water in sequence, mixing well. Finally, sprinkle with coarse chili flakes and rub gently. Set aside to marinate and absorb the flavors.
3. **Stir-fry the hotpot base**: Heat oil in a wok to 60% hot, add scallion segments, ginger slices, and broad bean paste, and stir-fry until red oil releases. Then add bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chilies, and hotpot base, stir-frying until fragrant.
**Key point**: All spices must be fully stir-fried in oil to release their complex aromas.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Simmer the broth**: Add enough water to the stir-fried base. Bring to a boil over high heat, then season with salt and chicken bouillon, stirring to combine.
**Key point**: The flavors meld better once the broth is fully boiled.
2. **Quick-boil the tripe**: Place the prepared large pieces of tripe into the boiling hotpot broth. Quickly blanch for about 15–20 seconds, using a "seven up, eight down" technique of repeatedly lifting and lowering. Remove immediately when the tripe slightly curls and becomes crispy.
**Note**: Tripe must never be cooked for too long, or it will become tough and lose its tender-crisp texture.
3. **Cook other ingredients**: Add the marinated spicy beef, luncheon meat, baby octopus, and vegetables one by one or together. Beef slices are done when they change color and should not be overcooked either.
4. **Prepare the dipping sauce**: Serve the cooked items with a dipping sauce of sesame oil and minced garlic to enhance the flavor.

## Health Advice
Beef tripe is rich in protein and low in fat, making it great for gatherings, but the high oil and salt content in the hotpot base may raise blood pressure. It is recommended to balance the meal with vegetables and replace half of the sesame oil with a vinegar-based dipping sauce to reduce calorie intake.
