# Braised Pork Ribs with Taro

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Pork ribs – appropriate amount (about 30 yuan portion, cut into small pieces)  
🥔 Lipu taro – appropriate amount (peeled and cut into small pieces)  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount (for cleaning, marinating, and seasoning the taro)  
🌾 Cornstarch – appropriate amount (for cleaning and marinating to coat)  
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount (scallion-ginger cooking wine or regular cooking wine)  
🍯 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar – appropriate amount  
🌶️ White pepper powder – 1 spoon  
🫒 Peanut oil – appropriate amount (for sealing in moisture at the end)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon – appropriate amount  
🐖 Lard – 1 spoon  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🔥 Steamer  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the pork ribs into small pieces, place them in a bowl, add 1 spoon of salt, 2 spoons of cornstarch, and an appropriate amount of water. Knead and scrub for 5–10 minutes until the water becomes clear, then rinse clean and drain well.  
**Note**: Thorough kneading and scrubbing helps completely remove blood and reduces any gamey smell.

2. Add an appropriate amount of scallion-ginger cooking wine, light soy sauce, salt, white sugar, 2 spoons of cornstarch, and 1 spoon of white pepper powder to the cleaned ribs. Mix well, then drizzle with peanut oil to seal in moisture. Marinate for 20 minutes.  
**Note**: Ensure enough marinating time; add peanut oil last to lock in flavor and moisture.

3. Cut the taro into small pieces. Add salt, chicken bouillon, and 1 spoon of lard, then mix well.  
**Note**: Adding lard makes the texture richer and softer.

4. Spread the seasoned taro evenly on the bottom of a plate, arrange the marinated ribs on top, and pour over the reserved marinade liquid.

5. Bring water in a steamer to a boil, then place the dish inside. Steam over high heat for 30 minutes, until the ribs are fully cooked and the taro is tender.

6. After removing from the steamer, sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve.

## Health Tips  
The combination of pork ribs and taro steamed together uses low-oil and low-salt cooking, providing both protein and dietary fiber. This dish is suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing to control calorie intake. Thoroughly scrubbing the ribs beforehand to remove blood can reduce purine content; those with high uric acid should consume in moderation.
