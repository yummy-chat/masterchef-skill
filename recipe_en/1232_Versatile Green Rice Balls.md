# Versatile Green Rice Balls  
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌿 Mugwort (or cudweed, spinach) appropriate amount (to make about 1200g of mugwort puree)  
🍚 Water-ground glutinous rice flour 750g  
🌽 Cornstarch 100g  
🍬 White sugar 160g  
🫒 Corn oil (or butter) 50-80ml  
🧂 Baking soda (or edible alkali) a pinch (for blanching to fix color)  
💧 Water appropriate amount (for making puree and blanching)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan (essential tool)  
🫕 Pot (for blanching)  
🥛 Blender/food processor  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Whisk/spatula  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🍞 Chopping board  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🛍️ Plastic wrap/bag  

## Steps  
1. Wash the mugwort. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add a pinch of baking soda, blanch the mugwort until soft, then immediately transfer to cold water. Squeeze out excess water and shape into small balls.  
**Note**: Adding baking soda during blanching helps maintain a bright green color; squeezed mugwort balls can be frozen for storage.  

2. Place two mugwort balls into a blender, add an appropriate amount of water, and blend until smooth to create a fine mugwort puree. No need to strain.  
3. Add white sugar and cornstarch to the mugwort puree, stir until the sugar is completely dissolved and no lumps remain. Then add the water-ground glutinous rice flour, stir until there is no dry flour left. Finally, add corn oil and continue stirring into a smooth, even green batter.  
4. Pour the batter into a non-stick pan. Cook over the lowest heat, stirring and pressing continuously with a spatula. Keep the heat low throughout. Stir-fry until the moisture evaporates, the batter gradually solidifies into a dough, the color becomes a uniform bright green, and the dough is fully cooked and flexible.  
**Key point**: Constant stirring during the cooking process prevents sticking and burning; the cooked dough should have a consistent color inside and out.  

5. Remove the cooked dough onto a chopping board. Let it cool completely until it is no longer hot to the touch. Use a dough scraper to divide it into evenly sized small portions.  
6. Shape each small portion into a round green rice ball by using the tiger’s mouth technique, or process further as needed—for example, coat with soybean flour and flatten, or wrap with fillings. This dough is soft and chewy, and will not harden easily even after cooling.  

## Health Tips  
Mugwort is rich in dietary fiber. Combined with glutinous rice flour, these green rice balls aid digestion, but they are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to eat 1-2 at a time, suitable as a small snack during spring outings. Pay attention to controlling the amount of sugar and oil; those with high blood sugar should consume with caution.
