# Handmade Spicy Strips (Latiao)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Dried gluten noodles (Niu Jin Mian) 250g
🫒 Rapeseed oil, approx. 4 jin (for making seasoned oil)
🧅 Onion, as needed (for oil frying)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for oil frying)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (for oil frying)
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed (for oil frying)
🌿 White cardamom, as needed (for oil frying)
🌱 Lemongrass, as needed (for oil frying)
🌶️ Fine chili powder 60g
🌶️ Coarse chili powder 50g
🧂 Salt 40g
🍬 White sugar 40g
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 40g
🧂 MSG 30g
🫘 White sesame seeds 60g
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), as needed
🧂 Homemade spice powder (red Sichuan peppercorns 25g, green Sichuan peppercorns 20g, fennel seeds 6g, cumin 6g, cinnamon 2g, star anise 2g, one cardamom, ground into powder)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack)
⚙️ Grinder (blender)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the dried gluten noodles in clean water for 30 seconds, then remove and drain.
**Note**: Soak briefly, only to soften the surface.
2. Drizzle a small amount of cooking oil over the drained noodles and mix well, ensuring each strand is lightly coated.
**Purpose**: To prevent sticking during steaming.
3. Spread the oiled noodles evenly on the steaming rack. Steam over high heat for 3 minutes. Remove immediately once softened.
**Key**: Do not steam too long to avoid the noodles clumping together into a mush.
4. Combine the red Sichuan peppercorns, green Sichuan peppercorns, fennel seeds, cumin, cinnamon, star anise, and cardamom. Grind into a fine powder using a grinder. Seal and set aside.
5. In a large bowl, combine fine chili powder, coarse chili powder, salt, white sugar, chicken bouillon powder, white sesame seeds, MSG, and the prepared spice powder. Stir thoroughly.
6. Drizzle a suitable amount of baijiu into the dry spice mixture, stirring continuously until evenly moistened.
**Purpose**: Prevents scorching when hot oil is added later, and enhances aroma.
7. Pour the rapeseed oil into a wok. Add the onion, ginger, garlic, bay leaves, white cardamom, and lemongrass. Fry over low heat slowly.
**Stage indicator**: Turn off the heat when the added ingredients turn yellow and dry. Remove and discard all solid residues.
8. Allow the oil temperature to drop until it is 50-60% hot. Pour the hot oil all at once into the baijiu-moistened dry spice mixture. Stir quickly and thoroughly to release the numbing, spicy aroma.
**Note**: Do not let the oil be too hot, otherwise the chili powder will burn and taste bitter.
9. After the chili oil has completely cooled, pour it along with all the sediment over the steamed noodles. Use chopsticks to toss and mix repeatedly, ensuring every strand is evenly coated in the chili oil.
10. Let the mixed spicy strips sit for a while to absorb the flavors before serving.

## Health Advice
Although spicy strips are delicious, the amount of rapeseed oil used is relatively large. It is recommended to control the portion size per serving, suitable for occasional indulgence. The gluten noodles provide carbohydrates, and combined with high-oil, high-salt seasoning, individuals with high blood pressure should consume with caution.
