# Stir-Fried Glutinous Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak dried shiitake mushrooms, conpoy, and dried shrimp in water separately. After rehydration, reserve the soaking water for later use. Soak raw glutinous rice for 1 hour in advance. Dice preserved pork belly, preserved sausage, dried shiitake mushrooms, and carrot. Shred conpoy into thin threads. Chop scallions into scallion flowers.
2. Heat the wok over high heat, then melt lard in it. Add dried shrimp, diced preserved pork belly, and diced preserved sausage in sequence, stir-frying over medium heat until fragrant.
**Key point**: Stir-fry dried shrimp first until aromatic, then add the cured meats to release their aroma through the fat; cook until the cured meats turn translucent.

3. Add diced dried shiitake mushrooms and shredded conpoy, stir-frying until fragrant. Pour in the drained glutinous rice and stir-fry evenly.
4. Drizzle in the shiitake mushroom soaking water, stir to combine. Cook over medium heat, cover the wok with a lid, and braise for 3 minutes, until the steam on the inside of the lid gradually dissipates.
5. Uncover, stir-fry the rice, then drizzle in the dried shrimp soaking water and mix evenly. Poke holes in the rice with the wok spatula, reduce heat to medium-low, cover the lid again, and braise for another 3 minutes.
6. Uncover, stir-fry and break up the rice, then drizzle in the conpoy steaming liquid and mix evenly. Add diced carrot and green peas, cover with the lid again, and continue to braise.
7. Increase heat to high, drizzle Shaoxing wine along the side of the wok, add salt, white pepper powder, and a little light soy sauce. Stir-fry quickly until the rice has dried out, becoming fluffy with distinct grains.
**Key point**: The final step is to "dry out the moisture" by stir-frying, allowing the rice to absorb the seasonings and develop a dry, fragrant texture.

8. Sprinkle in a large handful of chopped scallions and stir-fry evenly. Finally, add pine nuts and stir-fry to combine. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This stir-fried glutinous rice is rich in cured meats and seafood, making it high in calories and suitable for replenishing energy after physical labor. Glutinous rice is difficult to digest, so those with weak gastrointestinal function should consume it in moderation.
