# Stir-Fried Pork with Green Peppers and Long Beans
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Green peppers, about 3-4 (cut into segments)
🫘 Long beans, appropriate amount (cut into segments)
🥩 Pork, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount
🧂 Soy sauce/light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Plate
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the green peppers into segments, cut the long beans into segments, slice the pork, and mince the ginger, garlic, and scallions. Set aside.
2. Heat the pan over heat, pour in cooking oil, add the green pepper segments and long bean segments, and stir-fry until they are cooked through and bright green.
3. Drizzle in soy sauce/light soy sauce, and stir-fry evenly to season.
4. Add the pork slices, stir-fry together with the vegetables until the meat slices change color and are fully cooked.
5. Season with salt, stir-fry evenly, and immediately remove from the heat and plate.

## Health Tips
Stir-fried pork with green peppers and long beans is rich in vitamin C and high-quality protein, making it suitable for office workers who frequently use their eyes and those needing to supplement iron. Ensure the long beans are thoroughly cooked to avoid saponin poisoning. It is recommended to use lean loin meat for the pork slices to reduce fat intake.
