# Tomato Egg Fried Rice with Instant Noodles  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 Tomato: about 1 (score the top, blanch, peel, and cut into small pieces)  
🥚 Eggs: about 2 (beat thoroughly until well combined)  
🍚 Rice: about 1 large bowl (preferably leftover rice or rice with less moisture)  
🥓 Bacon: appropriate amount (sliced or diced)  
🍤 Shrimp: appropriate amount (cleaned)  
🌽 Corn kernels: appropriate amount  
🫛 Peas: appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallions: appropriate amount (for garnish and aroma)  
🍜 Instant noodles: about 1 pack (cooked in advance and drained)  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (added when stir-frying the tomato to help release juice)  
🧴 Light soy sauce: appropriate amount (added during final stir-fry for umami and color)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board or knife  
🍲 Pot (for blanching the tomato and cooking the noodles)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Score the top of the tomato, blanch it in boiling water until the skin curls, remove and rinse with cold water, peel off the skin, and cut into small pieces.  
   **Key Point**: Blanching makes the tomato skin easy to peel.  
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them in one direction until the mixture is uniform and frothy.  
   **Key Point**: The egg mixture should be well beaten to ensure fluffy egg pieces when cooked.  
3. Dice the shrimp and bacon; blanch the corn kernels and peas. Set aside.  
4. Cook the instant noodles in boiling water until just done, then drain.  
   **Key Point**: Adjust the cooking time according to the package instructions; avoid overcooking to prevent mushiness during stir-frying.  
5. Add an appropriate amount of oil to the wok, heat over high heat until about 80% hot, pour in the beaten eggs, and wait until they quickly expand and set. Use a spatula to break them into pieces, then remove and set aside.  
   **State Check**: The eggs should rapidly expand and set.  
6. Leave some oil in the wok, add the tomato pieces over medium heat, and stir-fry until soft and juicy. Add a pinch of salt to help release the juice quickly, then cook into a thick sauce.  
   **Key Point**: Adding salt helps the tomato release its juice faster.  
7. Add the rice to the tomato sauce, break it apart with the spatula, and stir-fry until evenly coated.  
8. Gradually add the cooked eggs, diced shrimp, diced bacon, corn kernels, and peas, continuing to stir-fry until all ingredients are well combined with the rice.  
9. Drizzle in the light soy sauce, stir-fry evenly to enhance color and aroma.  
10. Add the drained instant noodles, using chopsticks and a spatula to toss and mix until the noodles are evenly coated with the sauce.  
11. Finally, sprinkle in the scallions, stir-fry quickly, and remove from the heat.  
    **Key Point**: The finished dish should be moist and glossy, with the sauce evenly coating everything and no excess liquid.  

## Health Tips  
This tomato egg fried rice with instant noodles is rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, those with high blood sugar or on a weight-loss diet should control the portion size. The tomato provides vitamin C and lycopene, while the eggs and shrimp enhance the nutritional value; the instant noodles and bacon are high in sodium, so it is recommended to reduce the amount of light soy sauce.
