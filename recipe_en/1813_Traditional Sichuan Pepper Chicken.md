# Traditional Sichuan Pepper Chicken  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Half a chicken (about half a whole chicken)  
🧅 Green scallion parts, to taste (use only the green parts, mix with Sichuan pepper in a 4:1 ratio)  
🌶️ Red Sichuan pepper, to taste (some soaked and minced into a paste, some used for cooking the chicken)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (smashed, for cooking the chicken)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste (cut into sections, for cooking the chicken)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for cooking the chicken and making the Sichuan pepper paste)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch (for cooking the chicken)  
🫒 Cooking oil, 1 tablespoon (hot oil, for drizzling over the Sichuan pepper paste)  
🍬 Sugar, a tiny pinch  
🧂 MSG, a pinch  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a splash  
💧 Reserved chicken cooking broth, to taste (for mixing the Sichuan pepper sauce)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥘 Large iron wok  
🪓 Round thick wooden cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl  
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl/regular bowl  
🥄 Strainer/soup ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. Clean the half chicken thoroughly, keeping the skin intact, and drain well. Set aside.  
2. **Make the Sichuan pepper paste**:  
   - Soak the red Sichuan pepper in boiling water until soft, drain, and crush with a knife.  
   - Use only the green parts of the scallion, chop them finely, and mix with the crushed Sichuan pepper in roughly a 4:1 ratio.  
   - Sprinkle in a little salt and repeatedly chop the mixture with a knife until very fine and paste-like.  
     **Note**: The finer you chop, the better the aromas of the pepper and scallion are released.  
   - Transfer the minced Sichuan pepper paste to a small bowl. Heat one tablespoon of cooking oil until it just starts to smoke, then quickly pour it over the paste and stir rapidly with chopsticks.  
     **Note**: Stir immediately after adding the oil, and promptly place the bowl in cold water or the refrigerator to cool, preserving the bright green color.  
3. **Prepare the Sichuan pepper sauce**: In a large bowl, add an appropriate amount of reserved chicken cooking broth, along with salt, MSG, a tiny pinch of sugar, and a splash of light soy sauce. Stir until fully dissolved. Then add the cooled Sichuan pepper paste and mix well. Alternatively, you can combine after the chicken is cooked.  
4. **Cook the chicken**:  
   - Bring water to a boil in a wok. Using a ladle, repeatedly pour the boiling water over the chicken skin, or repeatedly dip the chicken into and out of the boiling water, then immediately plunge it into cold water. Repeat this blanching process 2–3 times.  
     **Note**: Blanching firms up the skin, making it more chewy after cooking and less likely to break.  
   - Add smashed ginger, scallion sections, a small handful of red Sichuan pepper, an appropriate amount of salt, and a pinch of chicken bouillon to the water used for cooking the chicken.  
   - Place the blanched chicken into the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for about 30 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest for another 10 minutes.  
   - Remove the cooked chicken and let it cool in cold chicken broth or cold boiled water. This makes the skin crisp and the meat firm.  
5. **Mix and serve**: Chop or slice the cooled chicken into pieces and arrange on a plate. Evenly pour the prepared Sichuan pepper sauce over the chicken, mixing thoroughly or letting it soak in.  
   **Note**: Traditional Sichuan pepper chicken does not include chili oil. The focus is on the fresh, numbing aroma of Sichuan pepper and scallion.  
6. Serve immediately after plating. The finished dish features a fresh, numbing Sichuan pepper fragrance, rich scallion aroma, chewy skin, and tender meat.  

## Health Tips  
Sichuan pepper chicken uses chicken as the main protein source, paired with Sichuan pepper and scallion greens, which can aid digestion and stimulate appetite, making it suitable for enjoyment all year round. Be mindful to control the amounts of salt and chicken bouillon; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce seasonings. Cooling the cooked chicken in cold liquid can help lower fat absorption.
