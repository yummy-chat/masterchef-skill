## Steps
1.  Remove the eggs from the refrigerator and let them come to room temperature. The eggshell surface has tiny pores, so clean them by scrubbing with salt and water.
2.  Fill a basin with water, add salt, place the eggs in, and thoroughly rub the shells with your hands, using the friction of the salt grains to remove surface dirt.
3.  Bring water to a gentle boil in a pot, add the eggs, and reduce to low heat to simmer. Precisely control the cooking time to 6 minutes and 30 seconds, gently stirring the eggs with chopsticks during cooking for even heating.
4.  Once the time is up, turn off the heat, immediately remove the eggs, and immerse them in ice water. The ice water bath helps the shells peel off easily and maintains the soft yolks inside.
5.  Peel the eggs, drain them, add dark soy sauce, and mix well to evenly coat the egg surfaces.
6.  Pour enough cooking oil into a pot and heat until it reaches 80% hot (approximately 180°C / 350°F). Quickly fry the colored eggs in the oil. The total time from entering the oil to removing should be about 15 seconds. Fry until the skin wrinkles and forms a tiger-skin pattern, then remove and drain the oil. **Key Point**: Do not fry for more than 6 seconds, or the eggs may burst.
7.  Leave a small amount of oil in the pot. Over low heat, add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic cloves, onion chunks, star anise, and cinnamon stick, and stir-fry quickly until fragrant.
8.  Add light soy sauce, pour in the cola, bring to a boil over high heat, then season with salt and white pepper powder. Continue to simmer to allow the spices to fully release their flavors.
9.  Add the fried tiger-skin eggs to the pot and toss to coat evenly with the sauce.
10. Once the pot comes to a boil, drizzle in the water starch to thicken the sauce. Gently push and stir slowly to make the sauce thick and evenly coat the egg surfaces. **Key Point**: The action must be gentle to avoid damaging the soft yolk shape.

## Health Advice
Eggs are a high-quality protein source. For soft yolks, ensure the eggs are fresh and free from bacteria. Cola has a high sugar content, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables to balance blood sugar. This dish is suitable for occasional indulgence and not recommended as a daily high-protein meal.
