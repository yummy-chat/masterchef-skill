# Honey Peach Cake 🍯  
**Cuisine:** Sichuan  
**Cooking Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Rice flour, as needed (recommended: sticky rice flour or glutinous rice flour)  
🍯 Crystallized honey, about one jar (serves as binder; adjust amount based on flour consistency)  
🥜 Mixed dried fruit/nuts, as needed (used as filling inside the cake)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🥢 Bamboo chopsticks  
🔍 Sieve  
🥘 Steamer + steaming basket  
🧵 Cheesecloth  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🔥 Gas stove / induction cooktop  

## Instructions  
1. Let the crystallized honey sit until it naturally liquefies into a fluid state. **Do not heat it.**  
   **Note:** Heating will cause moisture loss in the honey, making it too sticky. When mixed with rice flour, it will clump and form hard lumps that cannot be worked with.  
2. Place the rice flour in a stainless steel bowl. Slowly pour in the liquefied honey, then rub and mix repeatedly with both hands until the flour reaches a loose, sand-like texture with no large clumps.  
   **Key point:** The amount of honey must be moderate. Too little, and the cake won't hold together; too much, and the flour will clump. The correct consistency should be like fluffy, light sand that crumbles easily when pinched, similar to kinetic sand.  
3. Line the steamer basket with a damp cheesecloth, pour in the mixed flour, gently press the surface flat, and place it in the steamer.  
   **Note:** The cheesecloth should be pre-moistened to prevent sticking. Steaming time and heat should be adjusted based on actual conditions, until the cake is fully set and firm.  
4. Once steamed, remove and let the cake cool completely until fully set. Slice into thick pieces with a knife.  
   **Key point:** If the slices fall apart, it means there isn't enough honey—increase the ratio. The cake must be completely cooled before slicing for easier shaping.  

## Health Tips  
Using crystallized honey instead of white sugar provides a milder sweetness and helps avoid blood sugar spikes from refined sugar, making it suitable for those managing sugar intake in small amounts. Steaming is healthier than baking, but nuts are still calorie-dense, so it's recommended to enjoy just 1–2 slices at a time as a treat.
