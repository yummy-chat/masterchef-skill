# Mushroom and Bok Choy Baozi 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Baby bok choy, about 1000g (weight before trimming roots)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, 230g (washed, trimmed, and finely diced)
🌾 All-purpose flour, 250g
🍞 Yeast, 2.5g
🧂 Baking powder, 2g (aluminum-free, optional)
🍬 Sugar, 5g (for the dough)
💧 Warm water, 130g (around body temperature)
🐖 Lard, 20g (for the dough, can substitute with vegetable oil)
🧂 Baking soda, 3g (for blanching, to keep bok choy vibrant green)
🫒 Sesame oil, 30g (about 3 tablespoons, for stir-frying mushrooms)
🐖 Lard, about 20g (1 tablespoon, for stir-frying mushrooms, optional)
🧂 Light soy sauce, 10g (1 tablespoon)
🍬 Sugar, 10g (for the filling, to enhance freshness)
🧂 MSG, 3g
🌶️ White pepper powder, about 0.2g (do not use too much)
🧂 Salt, 5g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading, proofing, and mixing filling)
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for stir-frying diced mushrooms)
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife (for chopping bok choy and mushrooms)
🥖 Rolling pin (for rolling out baozi wrappers)
🥟 Bamboo steamer (for proofing and steaming baozi)
🍲 Stockpot or large pot (for blanching bok choy)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for measuring ingredients)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: In a bowl, combine the flour, yeast, baking powder, and 5g sugar. Mix well. Gradually add the warm water while stirring until a shaggy dough forms. Add the 20g lard and knead into a smooth dough. If the dough seems dry, dip the back of your hand in water and knead it into the dough repeatedly until the surface is smooth. Note that the water temperature should be close to body temperature; do not let it get too hot.
   **Key point**: Water temperature must be near body temperature; too hot will deactivate the yeast.

2. **Proof the dough**: Place the dough in the bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place until it doubles in volume.

3. **Prepare the bok choy**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add the baking soda. Once boiling, add the whole bok choy. If using baby bok choy hearts, blanch the stems first. Blanch for about 2 minutes, then immediately remove and plunge into plenty of cold water until completely cooled. Drain and squeeze out most of the water. Chop into pieces about the size of a fingernail. Wrap in cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel and squeeze firmly until no more water can be extracted.
   **Key point**: Blanching time must be strictly controlled. Thoroughly cooling and squeezing out all water is key to preventing the baozi bottoms from collapsing.

4. **Prepare the mushrooms**: In a wok or pan, heat the sesame oil and 20g lard over low heat until just warm. Add the diced mushrooms and stir-fry over high heat until softened. Drizzle in the light soy sauce and continue stir-frying for about 1 minute until the mushrooms have shrunk and look glossy. Turn off the heat, remove, and spread out on a plate to cool, using chopsticks to separate. The mushrooms must be fully cooked and dry, and cooled to room temperature before using.
   **Key point**: Mushrooms must be thoroughly stir-fried to remove moisture and cooled completely to prevent the filling from becoming watery.

5. **Mix the filling**: Combine the cooled mushroom dice with the squeezed, chopped bok choy. Add 10g sugar, MSG, white pepper powder, and salt. Mix well with your hands. Divide the filling into 8 equal portions and set aside.

6. **Deflate and portion the dough**: On a lightly floured surface, take the proofed dough and knead it repeatedly to deflate. Roll it into a long log, fold it in half, and knead again until smooth. Divide into 8 equal pieces, each about 50g. Dust the cut sides of each piece with flour, then roll each into a ball and flatten into a disc.

7. **Roll out wrappers and wrap**: Roll each dough piece into a circular wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place one portion of filling in the center of the wrapper and press it down firmly. Using your thumb and forefinger, pleat the edges while rotating the wrapper. Finally, pinch the pleats together to close tightly, or leave a small opening if desired.

8. **Second proofing**: Place the wrapped baozi in a bamboo steamer lined with a damp cloth, leaving at least two fingers' width between each baozi. Fill the steamer pot with water and heat over high heat until small bubbles appear at the bottom, then turn off the heat. Place the steamer basket over the warm water, cover, and let the baozi proof for about 15 minutes, until they have noticeably increased in size and feel light, and when pressed gently with a finger, the indentation slowly springs back.
   **Key point**: The second proofing must be sufficient; the baozi should visibly expand and the indentation from pressing should recover slowly.

9. **Steam**: After the second proofing, turn the heat to high and bring the water to a full boil. Once steam is rising, reduce to medium-high heat and steam for 12 minutes. Immediately turn off the heat after steaming, but do **not** open the lid. Let the baozi rest in the steamer for 2 minutes before uncovering, to prevent the wrappers from shrinking due to a sudden temperature drop.
   **Key point**: Resting for 2 minutes after steaming prevents the baozi wrappers from shrinking.

## Health Advice
This mushroom and bok choy baozi is a golden combination of vegetables and fungi. It is high in fiber and low in calories, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or looking to increase their vegetable intake. Note that the bok choy must be thoroughly squeezed dry; otherwise, the bottoms of the baozi may collapse.
