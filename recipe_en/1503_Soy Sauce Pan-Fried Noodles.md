# Soy Sauce Pan-Fried Noodles
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Cooked noodles (Cantonese-style fried noodles) – appropriate amount (steamed noodles, store-bought, soaked in hot water)
🥚 Egg – about 1 piece (made into an egg sheet and cut into shreds)
🌱 Bean sprouts – appropriate amount
🧅 Onion – appropriate amount (cut into shreds)
🥬 Chinese chives – appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌾 Cooked white sesame seeds – appropriate amount (for garnish)
🥜 Peanut oil – appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce – about 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Ground white pepper – about 1 teaspoon
🧴 Dark soy sauce – a little (for color)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Plate
🔥 Portable gas stove

## Steps
1.  Place the cooked noodles in a bowl, pour in hot water to soak for about 3 minutes, loosen the noodles with chopsticks until they are separated, then drain and set aside.
2.  Beat the egg until smooth. Heat the wok, add a little peanut oil, pour in the egg liquid and swirl the wok to form an egg sheet. Cook over medium-low heat until set and bubbly, then remove from heat and take it out. Fold the egg sheet and cut into thin shreds, then set aside.
3.  Heat the wok, add a small amount of peanut oil, spread the drained noodles flat in the wok, and flatten them with chopsticks. Pan-fry over medium-low heat until the bottom is fragrant and golden, then flip and continue frying the other side until both sides are golden brown, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
4.  Turn the heat to low. In a small bowl, mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and ground white pepper to make the seasoning sauce. Pour it over the noodles and toss evenly.
5.  Add bean sprouts and onion shreds, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry for about 2 minutes. Drizzle in a little dark soy sauce to adjust the color until it is evenly red and glossy.
6.  Add Chinese chive sections and stir-fry for about 15 seconds, then add the egg shreds and gently mix everything together.
7.  Plate the noodles and sprinkle with cooked white sesame seeds.

## Health Advice
This soy sauce pan-fried noodles dish uses pan-frying instead of deep-frying, with bean sprouts and Chinese chives adding dietary fiber, making it suitable as a lunch staple. Limit the amount of dark soy sauce; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of light soy sauce and oyster sauce as needed.
