# Prawn and Lemongrass Clay Pot Shrimp

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 5 lbs giant freshwater prawns (trim whiskers, butterfly the back, keep head attached to body)  
🌿 Lemongrass, to taste (sliced)  
🧅 Shallots, to taste  
🧄 Garlic cloves, to taste  
🥬 Chinese chive flowers, to taste (cut into sections)  
🫑 Bell peppers, to taste (red and yellow, sliced)  
🦐 Shrimp paste, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (to mix into shrimp paste sauce)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for saltwater soak)  
🫒 Oil, to taste  
🍶 Rice wine, to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫕 Clay pot  
✂️ Scissors  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the whiskers off the giant freshwater prawns and cut an opening along the back, keeping the head attached to the body.
2. Add a pinch of sugar and a small amount of water to the shrimp paste, stir until it becomes a flowing liquid, and set aside.  
**Note**: Shrimp paste is quite salty, so it must be diluted in advance. Do not add additional salt or other seasonings during cooking.
3. Add salt to clean water and stir to dissolve. Soak the prepared prawns for about 5 minutes, then remove and thoroughly pat dry the surface with kitchen paper towels.  
**Key**: The saltwater soak makes the prawn meat firmer and sweeter; thoroughly drying is essential for the principle that "the drier, the more fragrant."
4. Heat the clay pot, then pour in cold oil. Once the oil is hot, add the garlic cloves and lemongrass slices. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the aromatics turn golden and release their full fragrance.
5. Add the thoroughly dried prawns and stir-fry quickly over high heat until the shells start turning red, allowing the prawns to absorb the lemongrass flavor while the aromatics are at their peak aroma.
6. Pour the first splash of rice wine along the edge of the clay pot. It will ignite instantly, creating the distinctive wok hei and smoky aroma unique to "clay pot cooking."
7. Pour in the prepared shrimp paste sauce, add the bell pepper slices, and stir-fry evenly.
8. Cover the clay pot with its lid, then pour another splash of rice wine around the edge of the lid. Let it steam for about 20 seconds to fuse the flavors and lock in the juices.
9. Remove the lid, sprinkle in the Chinese chive flower sections, gently turn them over, and cook for about 45 seconds to keep them crunchy and almost raw. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Giant freshwater prawns are high in protein and low in fat. Combined with the fermented flavor of lemongrass and shrimp paste, this dish helps stimulate appetite and is suitable for those needing protein supplementation. Note that shrimp paste is high in sodium; people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount or balance it with vegetables.
