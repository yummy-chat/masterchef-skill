# Suiyuan-Style Stir-Fried Clams with Pork Belly
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥓 Pork belly, as needed (skin removed, sliced thinly)
🐚 Fresh clams, as needed (blanched, meat removed, with a little clam broth reserved)
🌿 Ginger slices, as needed
🧅 Scallion, as needed (cut into sections)

🍶 Huangjiu (Chinese rice wine), as needed
🧂 Soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🔪 Cutting board

## Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork belly and slice it thinly. Blanch fresh clams in boiling water, remove from the water, take out the meat, and reserve a little of the clam broth for later use.
2. Place the wok over heat, add cooking oil, and heat to about 30% hot. Add the sliced pork belly and stir-fry over medium heat.
**Key Point**: The pork belly must be fully rendered, stir-fried until the edges are slightly charred and the fat is released, achieving a state where it is fatty but not greasy and lean but not dry.
3. Add ginger slices to the fried pork belly, then immediately drizzle in Huangjiu to remove any gamey smell and enhance the aroma. Add a little soy sauce for color and flavor, and stir-fry evenly.
4. Add the blanched clam meat, along with a little of the reserved clam broth, and stir-fry together.
5. Season with a pinch of salt to taste.
6. Just before removing from the wok, add the scallion sections, quickly stir-fry a few times to add color and aroma, then turn off the heat and serve.

## Health Tips
The combination of clams and pork belly provides both zinc and protein, making it suitable for those seeking to enhance umami flavor. However, individuals with high blood lipids should monitor their intake of pork belly fat. Note that clams have a natural savory flavor, so salt should be added sparingly to avoid excessive saltiness.
