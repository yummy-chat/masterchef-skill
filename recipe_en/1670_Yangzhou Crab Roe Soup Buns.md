# Yangzhou Crab Roe Soup Buns
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork back skin (for making aspic, must be washed, scraped, and pinched clean)  
🍖 Pork leg bone (for aspic broth base)  
🐔 Aged hen (for aspic broth base)  
🦐 Shrimp roe (as a substitute for MSG to enhance umami)  
🦀 Crab roe (including crab oil, about 150g, paired with 500g aspic)  
🌾 High-gluten flour 500g  
🥚 Egg whites (about 1-2 eggs, added to the water for kneading)  
🧂 Salt 3g (for kneading dough)  
🧂 Alkali water about 3g (for kneading dough, to improve color and elasticity)  
🐖 Lard (for frying crab roe)  
🫘 Soybean oil (for frying crab roe, to achieve a golden color)  
🍶 Soy sauce适量 (for seasoning the aspic broth)  
🌿 Ground white pepper适量 (added to filling just before use)  
🧅 Ginger-scallion juice适量 (for frying crab roe to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)  
🍶 Cooking wine适量 (for removing fishy smell from aspic broth)

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Make aspic**: Thoroughly wash, scrape, and pinch the pork back skin to remove any remaining hairs, then blanch and drain. Cook the processed pork skin, leg bones, and aged hen together until the skin and meat are tender. Strain the broth, season with soy sauce, shrimp roe, scallions, ginger, and cooking wine.  
**Note**: The pork skin must be clean to ensure texture and remove fishy smell.  

2. **Set and process aspic**: Let the seasoned broth cool naturally until set, then grind it into a fine paste using a meat grinder.  
**Note**: The aspic must be fully set before use.  

3. **Prepare crab roe filling**: In a pot, heat soybean oil and lard, add ginger-scallion juice and crab roe, and fry together. Let cool and set aside.  
**Key**: Soybean oil helps achieve a golden color for the crab roe.  

4. **Mix filling**: Combine the ground aspic and cooled crab roe filling in a ratio of approximately 500g:150g, mixing just before use. Add ground white pepper to taste and mix well.  
**Key**: The filling must be evenly mixed and prepared just before use to maintain the aspic's form.  

5. **Knead dough**: Place high-gluten flour in a basin. Mix egg whites, salt, alkali water, and cold water together, then pour into the flour and knead into a dough.  
**Key**: Use cold water; egg whites increase the dough's elasticity, while alkali water improves color and texture.  

6. **Knead and rest dough**: Knead the dough repeatedly until smooth, with the basin clean, the dough glossy, and hands clean. Let rest for about 20 minutes.  
**Key**: Thorough kneading develops the gluten network; resting relaxes the dough.  

7. **Roll dough**: Use a pasta roller to repeatedly roll the rested dough into a smooth, even, and elastic sheet.  
**Key**: Rolling makes the dough skin finer and more uniform.  

8. **Divide dough**: Roll the dough sheet into a long strip and cut into pieces weighing about 23-24g each.  
**Key**: Weight should be as precise as possible.  

9. **Roll wrappers**: Flatten each piece with your palm, then use a single rolling pin to roll into a round wrapper about 15-18cm in diameter.  
**Key**: The wrapper should be "thin on the edges, thick in the center" to ensure the seal cooks quickly while supporting the large amount of soup.  

10. **Assemble soup buns**: Hold the wrapper in your left hand and fill with about 125g of filling. Using your right hand, pinch the edge of the wrapper while lifting and pleating, then seal tightly.  
**Key**: The filling should be generous, with about 28 pleats. The technique involves lifting, pinching, and folding; the seal must be tight.  

11. **Steam**: Place the assembled soup buns seam-side up into a bamboo steamer lined with pine needles. Steam over high heat once the water is boiling and steam is rising. Steam each batch individually.  
**Indication**: The wrapper becomes translucent, and the soup inside is boiling. The exact steaming time for home cooking is not provided in the video; adjust based on actual conditions.  

12. **Serving**: Serve the soup bun on a high-rimmed plate. Tilt it at a 45-degree angle, first bite a small "window", slowly sip the soup, then eat the wrapper and filling.  

## Health Advice  
These crab roe soup buns use aspic to lock in the essence of the broth, are rich in high-quality protein and calcium, but the crab roe is high in cholesterol. It is recommended to enjoy only 1-2 pieces at a time. Those with high blood lipids, blood pressure, or blood sugar should consume sparingly. Pair with vinegar and shredded ginger to cut the richness and aid digestion.
