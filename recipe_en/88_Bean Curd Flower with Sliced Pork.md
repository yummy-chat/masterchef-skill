# Bean Curd Flower with Sliced Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork (pork shoulder) 1 piece, cut into slightly thin large slices
🍮 Bean curd flower (tofu pudding) 2 lumps
🧅 Scallions, to taste, cut into sections, some for garnish
🫚 Ginger, to taste, sliced or crushed, for marinating and stir-frying
🧄 Garlic, to taste, minced, for stir-frying and garnish
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste, cut into sections
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
🌶️ Knife-cut chili, to taste, for stir-frying and garnish
🌶️ Fine chili powder, to taste, for stir-frying
🥬 Pickled mustard tuber granules, to taste, for garnish
🧂 Salt, to taste, added in two batches (marinating and seasoning)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste, added in two batches
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste, added in two batches
🫘 Broad bean paste (Doubanjiang), to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🌾 Starch, to taste (for thickening and coating)
💧 Ginger-scallion water, to taste (add to the pork slices in small amounts)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🥄 Strainer spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Large bowl/basin

## Steps
1.  Cut the pork into slightly thin large slices, ensuring clean cuts and even thickness.
2.  Add salt, chicken bouillon, and white pepper powder to the pork slices. Gradually add ginger-scallion water, and mix vigorously in one direction until the surface of the meat becomes sticky and the liquid is fully absorbed.
**Key Point**: Add water in small amounts to allow the meat to fully absorb the liquid, resulting in a more tender texture.
3.  Sprinkle a handful of starch into the pork slices and mix well to coat, sealing in the moisture.
4.  Let it marinate for 10 minutes.
5.  Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Once the oil is hot, add scallion sections, ginger slices, minced garlic, dried Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili pepper sections, knife-cut chili, and fine chili powder. Stir-fry over medium heat until the spicy and numbing aroma is released.
6.  Add broad bean paste and continue stir-frying until red oil is released and the color turns bright red.
7.  Add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then season with salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, and white pepper powder to establish the spicy, numbing, and savory base flavor.
8.  Once the soup is boiling, add the bean curd flower and cook over medium heat for several minutes to allow it to fully absorb the broth.
**Key Point**: The bean curd flower should be cooked for a longer time to absorb as much broth as possible.
9.  Using a strainer spoon, remove the bean curd flower and spread it at the bottom of a large bowl as a base.
10. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting, keeping the soup surface barely simmering. Gently add the marinated pork slices one by one into the wok, avoiding them sticking together.
**Key Point**: The lowest heat prevents the coating from being washed off the slices, keeping them intact and tender. Once the slices are set and change color, gently push them apart with the back of a spatula.
11. Once all the pork slices have changed color and are cooked, add a starch slurry to thicken the soup slightly, so it clings to the meat slices.
12. Pour the pork slices and the broth into the large bowl containing the bean curd flower.
13. Sprinkle minced garlic, knife-cut chili, and pickled mustard tuber granules evenly over the pork slices. Pour a ladle of hot oil heated to a high temperature over them to release the complex aromas.
**Key Point**: Splashing with hot oil instantly elevates the aromatic layers.
14. Finally, sprinkle with scallion sections and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein but also has a significant calorie content, making it suitable for muscle-building purposes or moderate consumption. For those with cardiovascular sensitivities, it is recommended to reduce the amount of salt and oil used.
