# Low Sugar & Oil Whole Wheat Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Year Round  

## Ingredients  
🌾 Whole wheat flour 150g  
💧 Cold water 150g  
🌾 Bread flour 150g  
🥚 Whole egg 1  
🍄 Active dry yeast 3g  
💧 Ice water 30g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 20g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🍬 Brown sugar 10g  
🥛 Milk powder 16g  

## Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔌 Stand mixer  
🍴 Dough scraper  
🌡️ Thermometer  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  
📦 250g square bread loaf pan  
🔆 Proofing box  
🔥 Oven  
🍽️ Cooling rack  
🔪 Serrated bread knife  

## Instructions  
1. Mix 150g whole wheat flour with 150g cold water in a 1:1 ratio until combined. Cover with plastic wrap and let sit at room temperature for at least 2 hours, or refrigerate overnight, to soften the bran.  
2. Place the soaked whole wheat mixture, 150g bread flour, 5g salt, 10g brown sugar, 16g milk powder, and the whole egg into the stand mixer bowl. Do not add the ice water yet.  
3. Mix on low speed (setting 2-3) for 3 minutes, while gradually adding the 30g ice water in small portions, until the dough comes together, moist but not sticky.  
4. Increase to high speed and knead for about 5 minutes until the dough is partially developed. Add 3g active dry yeast at this point.  
5. Add 20g softened unsalted butter. Knead on low speed (setting 3) for 5 minutes until incorporated, then switch to high speed for 5-6 minutes until the dough can be stretched into a thick, elastic membrane with slightly jagged edges when torn. The final dough temperature should be around 24°C.  
6. Cover the dough in the bowl with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 5 minutes.  
7. Remove the dough, slap it against the counter a few times, then shape it into a ball. Cover with plastic wrap and let ferment at room temperature until doubled in size. Test by pressing a floured finger into the dough; the indent should remain without springing back or collapsing.  
8. Turn the dough out, gently press to release gas, and divide into 2 equal portions. Shape each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.  
9. Dust one dough portion lightly with flour, then gently press it into a round disc, deflating large air bubbles. Using the folding method, shape the disc into a rectangle, fold both sides toward the center, and press the seams. Flip over and roll from one end into a tight cylinder, with the seam facing down.  
10. Place both dough cylinders into the 250g loaf pan. Proof in a proofing box at around 35°C/80% humidity until the dough rises to 90% full, about 1 hour.  
11. Preheat the oven to 210°C for the top heat and 190°C for the bottom heat. Cover the pan with its lid, place on the middle-lower rack, and bake for 25-35 minutes. Since this low-sugar low-oil recipe browns slowly, check the color after 25 minutes and extend baking time if still too pale.  
12. Immediately after baking, remove the pan from the oven and tap it lightly on the counter. Turn out the bread onto a cooling rack and let cool completely.  
13. Slice with a serrated knife and enjoy. The bread is soft and tender, with visible shredding, a pronounced milky flavor, and low sweetness.  

## Health Note  
Whole wheat flour paired with brown sugar provides dietary fiber and lower sweetness, making this bread suitable as breakfast or a fitness snack for those managing sugar intake. Although the butter amount is low, the milk powder contains fat, so it should not be consumed in large quantities at once during weight loss.
