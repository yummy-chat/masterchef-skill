# Cheese-Baked Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 White steamed buns 2 (medium size)
🫛 Green peas appropriate amount (already blanched or thawed)
🥕 Carrot dices appropriate amount
🍖 Ham dices appropriate amount
🥚 Egg 1 (can use whole egg or just the yolk)
🧀 Mozzarella cheese shreds appropriate amount (about 1/4 cup per bun)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Small knife
🥄 Spoon
🔥 Oven
📜 Parchment paper (or baking paper)

## Instructions
1. Cut around the top third of the steamed bun, then carefully scoop out the inner bun core to form a bowl-shaped container with a wall thickness of about 1 to 2 centimeters. Be careful not to cut through the bottom. The removed bun core can be used for other purposes.
2. In the hollowed-out bun bottom, layer in the green peas, carrot dices, and ham dices in order. Do not overfill the filling; leave space for the egg and cheese.
3. Crack the egg on top of the filling. The egg liquid should take up about one-third of the remaining space in the bun. If using a whole egg, you can crack it on the edge of the bowl first and then pour it in. The yolk can remain intact or be scrambled.
4. Evenly spread a layer of mozzarella cheese shreds on top, completely covering the filling and the egg.
5. Line the oven rack with parchment paper, place the filled buns on top, and put them into a preheated oven. The recommended temperature is 180 to 200°C. Bake until the cheese melts, the surface turns golden brown with some charred spots, and the edges of the buns become slightly firm. Watch closely during baking to avoid burning.

## Health Tips
This dish uses steamed buns as bowls, saving you from washing dishes, but note that carbohydrates and cheese are high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a portion of stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable as a snack to satisfy cravings. For people with high blood lipids, the amount of cheese should be controlled.
