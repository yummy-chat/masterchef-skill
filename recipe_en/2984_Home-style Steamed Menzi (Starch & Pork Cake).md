# Home-style Steamed Menzi (Starch & Pork Cake)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍠 Sweet potato starch 500g (must be pure sweet potato starch, otherwise not chewy enough)
🥩 Pork belly about 200g
🧅 Scallion 1 section (for boiling meat) + 2-3 stalks (minced)
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices (for boiling meat) + 1 small piece (minced)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about 1 small pinch
💧 Warm water 350g (about 30°C)
💧 Reserved meat broth 850g
🧂 Dark soy sauce about half a spoon (for color)
🧂 Light soy sauce about half a spoon
🧂 Salt 15g
🧂 MSG about 1 spoon
🍬 Sugar a pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste
🫒 Sesame oil to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large stainless steel mixing bowl
🍳 Large wok (frying pan)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫗 Measuring cup
🪣 Small stainless steel basins (molds) ×3
🫙 Oil spray bottle
🔪 Chef knife and cutting board
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon
♨️ Steamer

## Preparation Steps
1. **Dilute the starch**: Place sweet potato starch in a large bowl, add 350g warm water, and knead with your hands until no lumps remain.
2. **Boil the meat broth**: Add enough cold water to a pot, add pork belly, scallion section, ginger slices, and Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 20 minutes, until chopsticks can easily pierce through the meat. Remove the pork belly and reserve the broth.
3. **Season the starch slurry**: Add dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, salt, MSG, sugar, and oyster sauce to the diluted starch water, and stir until well combined.
4. **Prepare the garnishes**: Finely dice the cooked pork belly; finely mince the scallion and ginger.
5. **Combine solid ingredients**: Add the diced meat, minced scallion, and minced ginger to the seasoned starch slurry and stir to combine.
6. **Prepare the hot broth**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the scallion, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns from the reserved meat broth. Accurately measure 850g of the broth into a clean pot and bring to a rolling boil over high heat.
7. **Combine and stir**: Pour the boiling broth all at once into the starch slurry. Immediately stir quickly and vigorously with a spoon, continuing until the starch mixture becomes very thick, visibly stringy, and about 80% cooked. This step directly determines the chewiness of the menzi.
8. **Add sesame oil and fill molds**: Stir in an appropriate amount of sesame oil. Lightly spray the inside of small stainless steel basins with oil to prevent sticking. Fill each basin about 80% full with the starch mixture.
9. **Steam**: Place the basins on a steamer rack, cover with a lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and steam for 40 minutes.
10. **Cool and unmold**: Remove and let cool completely. Invert to unmold and slice into thick pieces. The finished product can be served with braised cabbage or pan-fried. It has a chewy, smooth texture and rich meaty flavor.

## Health Advice
This steamed menzi is rich in starch and a small amount of animal protein. It is relatively high in calories, making it suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or for winter nourishment. However, people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Be mindful of controlling the amount of salt and MSG to avoid excessive sodium intake.
