# Steamed Pork Ribs with Preserved Mustard Greens
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs, appropriate amount (preferably spare ribs)
🥬 Preserved mustard greens, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (some sliced for soaking, some minced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Dried chilies, appropriate amount
🫘 Fermented black beans, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, a small amount (chopped, for final garnish)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount
🥡 Corn starch, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating ribs)
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🥢 Spatula or spoon
🧊 Stainless steel container with lid (for portioning and freezing)
♨️ Steamer or steaming oven
🧤 Heat-resistant gloves

## Steps
1. **Prepare preserved mustard greens**: Soak the preserved mustard greens in water until soft, then squeeze out the excess water and set aside.
2. **Marinate the ribs**: Cut the pork ribs into small pieces and soak them in water with ginger slices, changing the water several times until the blood is released. Drain the water, place them in a bowl, add ginger slices, minced garlic, dried chilies, fermented black beans, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and corn starch, and mix thoroughly. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix well. Let sit to marinate.
   **Key point**: Adding oil helps seal in the meat juices, resulting in a smoother and more tender texture after steaming.
3. **Stir-fry the preserved mustard greens**: Heat a wok over medium-low heat with a little oil, add the squeezed preserved mustard greens, and stir-fry until fragrant. Remove and set aside.
4. **Portion for storage**: Take a stainless steel container with a lid, lay the marinated ribs evenly on the bottom, then spread the stir-fried preserved mustard greens evenly on top. Seal tightly.
   **Note**: After portioning, you can store the container directly in the freezer for later use.
5. **Steam**: When ready to eat, there is no need to thaw. Place the container directly into a steamer or a steaming pot with boiling water, and steam over high heat for about 30 minutes, until the ribs are fully cooked and the flavors have melded.
   **Note**: Adjust the steaming time according to the total amount of ingredients. The ribs are done when a chopstick can easily pierce through them.
6. **Garnish and serve**: After steaming, wear heat-resistant gloves to remove the container, invert it onto a plate, and sprinkle with chopped scallions for aroma. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Steamed pork ribs with preserved mustard greens are savory and rich, with the ribs tender and flavorful, making them an excellent side dish for meals. Since preserved mustard greens naturally have a high salt content, reduce the amount of salt and light soy sauce when marinating. People with high blood pressure should consume this dish in moderation.
