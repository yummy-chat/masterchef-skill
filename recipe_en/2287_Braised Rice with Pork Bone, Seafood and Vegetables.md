# Braised Rice with Pork Bone, Seafood and Vegetables
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 1 pork marrow bone
🦐 1 large shrimp
🥬 About 1 small handful of flowering bok choy (chopped)
🥬 About 1/4 head of napa cabbage (torn into pieces)
🍄 1 shiitake mushroom (stem removed)
🧅 About 1 stalk of scallion/leek (pounded and cut into sections)
⭐ 1 star anise
🥚 1 egg
🍚 About one bowl of leftover rice
🫗 Soy sauce or seasoning sauce, to taste (referred to as "beautifying spray" in the video)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker (with inner pot)
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board

## Instructions
1. Take the inner pot of the rice cooker, and spread the leftover rice evenly on the bottom.
2. Pound the scallion and cut into sections, chop the bok choy, tear the napa cabbage into pieces, and remove the stem from the shiitake mushroom.
3. Place all prepared vegetables, star anise, and the pork marrow bone into the inner pot, and mix well with the rice.
4. Crack a raw egg on top of the ingredients.
5. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, select the rice cooking or braising mode, and start cooking.
6. Once cooking is complete, open the lid, drizzle or spray with soy sauce, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish features a pork bone broth base combined with seafood and vegetables, providing balanced nutrition. It is suitable for babies over 8 months old who are gradually being introduced to a variety of foods. Be sure to remove bones and shrimp shells, and season lightly to reduce the burden on the baby's kidneys.
