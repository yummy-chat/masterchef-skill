# Stir-Fried Shrimp with Chinese Cabbage
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine (Shandong Cuisine)
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Large shrimp 300-350g (black tiger shrimp or common prawns, remove the sharp rostrum, legs, butterfly the back to devein, peel leaving the tail fin on)
🥬 Chinese cabbage leaves 180g (tear into large pieces by hand)
🌶️ Green chili pepper 15g (sliced diagonally into rings)
🌶️ Red chili pepper 10g (sliced diagonally into rings)
🧅 Scallion 10g (chopped)
🧄 Garlic 10g (sliced)
🫚 Ginger 5g (minced)
🌿 Cilantro stems 10g (cut into small sections)
⭐ Star anise 1 small piece (use half if large)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about a dozen (around 10 pieces)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🍶 Shaoxing wine or rice wine 10g
🥄 Light soy sauce 15g
🍬 White sugar 3g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g
🧂 MSG 3g
🧂 Salt 2g (adjust to taste)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok (or frying pan)
🥘 Small pot (for blanching)
✂️ Scissors
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Spoon (for pressing shrimp heads and stirring)
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing aromatics)
🥣 Large bowl (for holding raw shrimp)
🍽️ Plates (for holding blanched shrimp and finished dish)
🪵 Wooden lid (to prevent oil splatter)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Trim the shrimp: cut off the sharp rostrum, eyes, antennae, and legs. Remove the sand sac (the dark vein near the head). Butterfly the back by cutting along the shell from the first joint. Devein, peel off the shell, and keep the tail shell intact.
2.  Take the Chinese cabbage leaves and tear them into large pieces by hand. Slice the green and red chili peppers, scallion, ginger, and garlic as prepared.
3.  Bring water in a small pot to a gentle boil. Blanch the shrimp until they just turn slightly pink, then immediately remove and drain. If using live shrimp, this step can be skipped.
4.  Add a little oil to the wok. Over high heat, quickly stir-fry the cabbage leaves, pressing and turning them until they are all wilted and softened. Remove from the wok. Slightly charred edges on some leaves are desirable.
5.  Add a normal amount of oil for stir-frying to the wok. Add the star anise and Sichuan peppercorns. Fry over low heat until they change color, then remove and discard. Note: Do not use too many Sichuan peppercorns, and avoid burning them, as this will make the dish bitter.
6.  Add the prepared shrimp to the wok and immediately cover with the wooden lid to prevent oil splatter. Once the crackling sound subsides, remove the lid. Spread the shrimp out and fry until the bottom side is set. Use a spoon to firmly press down on each shrimp head to extract the shrimp roe and fat, coloring the oil a rich red. This is the key step for developing deep umami flavor.
7.  Once the shrimp are curled, pour the Shaoxing wine along the edge of the wok. Add the scallion, ginger, garlic, and chili peppers. Stir-fry over high heat to combine.
8.  Add the stir-fried cabbage leaves back to the wok and toss to combine. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add the light soy sauce, white sugar, chicken bouillon, MSG, and salt. Stir-fry everything together evenly.
9.  Sprinkle in the cilantro stems, turn the heat back to high, and quickly stir-fry for a few more seconds. Remove from the wok and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
The combination of shrimp and Chinese cabbage provides both protein and dietary fiber. The natural umami from the shrimp roe pressed out of the heads makes this dish deliciously satisfying, making it suitable for those on a diet. Be sure to clean the digestive tract of the shrimp thoroughly. Cover the wok when stir-frying the shrimp to prevent oil splatter and ensure safe operation.
