# Charred Meat Strips
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin About 450g (9 liang, trim off sinew, cut into strips about 1 cm thick and finger-width)
🥔 Potato starch Half the weight of the meat (about 225g, soak in cold water in advance until settled, drain off the clear water on top and use the wet starch)
🧅 Scallion One small section (finely shredded)
🫚 Ginger One small piece (peeled and finely shredded)
🧄 Garlic A few cloves (sliced or crushed into mince)
🌿 Cilantro To taste (use only the stems, cut into sections)
🧂 Salt 2g for marinating meat; 4-5g for sauce (adjust to taste)
🌶 White pepper 1g
🥄 Light soy sauce About one hot pot spoon base
🍬 White granulated sugar For sweet and sour flavor: slightly less than a level spoon; for non-sweet and sour flavor: about 1/5 of a hot pot spoon
🍶 9% rice vinegar About 1 level spoon (hot pot spoon, only for sweet and sour flavor)
💧 Cold water 1-2 spoons for sauce; small amount for adjusting batter
🫗 Cooking oil As needed (for frying, enough to submerge the meat strips)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (for soaking starch, marinating meat, mixing batter, making sauce)
🥄 Slotted spoon (large)
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🌡 Oil thermometer (recommended)
🥄 Spoon (for measuring seasonings and drizzling sauce)

## Preparation Steps
1. Add cold water to the potato starch until submerged, let it settle without stirring. Once fully soaked, pour off the clear water on top, retaining the wet starch at the bottom for later use.
   **Key point**: Thorough settling removes excess water, making the batter thicker and stickier.

2. Finely shred the scallion and ginger, slice or crush the garlic into mince, and cut the cilantro stems into sections.
   **Key point**: Finely cutting the aromatics helps release flavor.

3. Trim the sinew from the pork tenderloin and cut into strips about 1 cm thick and uniform finger-width. Mix in 2g salt and 1g white pepper. If the meat is dry, add a little cold water and mix until absorbed. Let it marinate in advance for flavor.
   **Key point**: Let it rest briefly after mixing to ensure the seasoning penetrates.

4. In a bowl, mix light soy sauce, salt, sugar, vinegar, and 1-2 spoons of cold water until combined. Then add a small spoon of the soaked wet starch and stir until cloudy. Stir again just before use.
   **Key point**: Adding the wet starch last prevents it from settling.

5. Add the drained wet starch to the marinated meat strips. If too dry, add cold water a little at a time, mixing until some strips are fully coated with white batter while some red meat is still visible.
   **Key point**: The batter should not be too thick; exposed red helps achieve a charred red color after the second fry.

6. Heat oil in the wok to about 200°C. Add the meat strips one by one, frying over medium-high heat until they float, the shells are firm, and the color is light yellow. Remove.
   **Key point**: Fry strips individually to prevent sticking.

7. Place the fried meat strips on a slotted spoon and let them rest for 5-10 minutes until the shells soften due to moisture escaping from inside.
   **Key point**: Softening is a necessary step for a crispy second fry.

8. Reheat the oil to about 200°C. Add the softened meat strips and fry over high heat for about 2-3 minutes until the shells are golden red and crispy, the exposed areas turn jujube red, and they make a crisp sound when tapped. Remove.
   **Key point**: Quick frying over high heat gives color fast without absorbing excess oil.

9. Lay the shredded scallion, ginger, garlic slices, and cilantro stems over the fried meat strips. Heat the wok until it is smoking hot, add a little oil to coat the surface, then pour it out. Keep the heat high. With one hand, pour the meat strips and aromatics into the wok, and with the other hand, drizzle the sauce in a circular motion along the hot wok edge. Quickly stir-fry until the sauce boils, thickens, and evenly coats the meat strips. Immediately remove from the wok.
   **Key point**: Work quickly, using the wok's intense heat to instantly boil and coat the sauce.

## Health Advice
This charred meat strip dish is rich in high-quality protein, suitable for those needing energy replenishment, such as physically active individuals. However, it is high in calories due to frying, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for a balanced meal. Moderate the use of sugar and salt to avoid high fat and sodium intake.
