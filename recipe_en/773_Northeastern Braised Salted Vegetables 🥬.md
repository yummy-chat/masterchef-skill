# Northeastern Braised Salted Vegetables 🥬
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 2 lbs long beans (cut into long segments)
🥔 1 lb small potatoes (unpeeled, thoroughly dried after washing)
🌿 4 stalks cilantro (roots intact, washed)
🌶️ 2 snake peppers (stems removed)
🥓 Pork belly, sliced (适量)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🧅 3 scallion sections (smashed or cut into segments)
🧄 4 garlic cloves (smashed)
🫚 3 slices ginger
🍂 2 bay leaves
⭐ 1 star anise
🌿 1 piece cinnamon (thumb nail size)
🧂 2 small bowls light soy sauce
🧂 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
🍬 1 level tbsp white sugar

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Iron wok (frying pan)
🪵 Wooden lid
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🥢 Chopsticks or spatula

## Steps
1. Cut the long beans into long segments, wash the cilantro thoroughly with roots attached, remove the stems from the snake peppers, wash the small potatoes and dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels, and slice the pork belly for later use.
2. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat over high heat until 70% hot. Add the small potatoes and fry until the skin is wrinkled and golden brown, like a tiger skin. Then remove and set aside.
**Note**: The potatoes must be thoroughly dried to prevent oil splattering when added to the wok.
3. Leave some base oil in the wok. Add the scallion, ginger, garlic, bay leaves, star anise, and cinnamon. Reduce to low heat and sauté slowly until the spices release their fragrance. Remove and set aside.
4. Add the sliced pork belly, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry until the fat renders and the surface of the meat becomes slightly charred.
5. Immediately after stir-frying the meat, turn the heat to the lowest setting. Add 2 small bowls of light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, and 1 level tbsp white sugar. Mix well.
**Key**: Add the seasonings after reducing the heat to prevent liquid splashing and burning.
6. Layer the long beans on top of the meat and sauce mixture. Then pour in the fried small potatoes. Cover the wok with the lid.
7. Keep the heat at the very lowest setting. Remember, do not add any water. The salt from the soy sauce will draw out the natural moisture from the vegetables. First, braise for 20 minutes.
8. After uncovering, flip the beans at the bottom to the top. Add the cilantro (with roots) and the snake peppers. Cover again and continue braising over the lowest heat for another 20 minutes.
9. Uncover, mix everything evenly, and turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Leave a small amount of sauce remaining. Serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is relatively high in salt. It is recommended to serve with mild foods such as plain congee or multigrain rice. Individuals with high blood pressure should control their portions. The pork belly and potatoes provide ample carbohydrates and protein, but the fat content is high, making it suitable for moderate consumption when physical exertion is significant.
