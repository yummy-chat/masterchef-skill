# Chrysanthemum Greens Salad with Peanuts
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥜 Peanuts 70g  
🥬 Chrysanthemum greens 250g (about two small bunches)  
🧅 Onion 100g (about half a small onion)  
🥕 Carrot 40g  
🌶️ Red bell pepper 40g (half a pepper, choose thick-fleshed variety)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (chopped or crushed)  
🌶️ Dried chili segments, to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp (about 10g)  
🫒 Cooking oil 4-5 tbsp (about 40g)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 3.5 tbsp (about 35g)  
🍬 White sugar 2 level tbsp (about 20g)  
🍶 Aged vinegar 3.5 tbsp (about 35g)  
🧂 MSG 1 tsp (about 3g)  
🧂 Salt 1/2 tsp (about 1.5g, adjust to taste, add sparingly)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Air fryer  
🍳 Small pot (saucepan)  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoon / teaspoon  
🥣 Stainless steel seasoning bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Lightly spray a thin layer of oil onto the peanuts, place them in the air fryer, and roast at 180°C for 6 minutes. Remove and set aside to cool.  
2. Trim the root end of the chrysanthemum greens, wash thoroughly, and drain well. Cut into about three sections and place in a large bowl.  
3. Shred the onion, carrot, and red bell pepper. If the red bell pepper has thick flesh, slice it thinly first before shredding, and remove the inner ribs. Add all the shredded items to the bowl with the chrysanthemum greens.  
4. Prepare the dressing: In a bowl, combine the minced garlic and dried chili segments, then mix in the sesame oil. Heat the cooking oil in a small pot until it begins to smoke, then wait until the smoke subsides slightly, and quickly pour the hot oil over the garlic and chili mixture to release the aroma.  
5. Add the light soy sauce, white sugar, aged vinegar, and MSG, and stir well. Taste and add salt as needed.  
6. Pour the dressing over the vegetables, add the cooled peanuts, put on gloves, and gently toss from the bottom to combine evenly. Avoid pressing or squeezing the ingredients.  
7. Transfer to a serving plate, and pour any remaining dressing from the bottom of the bowl over the top.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, making it suitable for cooling and reducing internal heat in summer. Peanuts provide high-quality protein but are relatively high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Chrysanthemum greens are cooling in nature; those with a weak spleen or stomach should consume in moderation or balance with ginger and garlic.
