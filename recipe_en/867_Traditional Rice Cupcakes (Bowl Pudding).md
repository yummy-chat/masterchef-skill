# Traditional Rice Cupcakes (Bowl Pudding)

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Rice (Silk rice or oil-sticky rice) 250g  
💧 Water 250g (for soaking and grinding rice)  
💧 Boiling water 400g (for making syrup)  
🍬 White sugar 60g  
🫘 Red beans/mung beans appropriate amount (pre-cooked)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
⚖️ Electronic scale  
🔪 Measuring cup  
🥢 Stirring stick  
🌀 Blender (or stone mill)  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🍳 Stainless steel pot  
🔥 Stove  
🎋 Bamboo steamer  
🫖 Small ceramic bowls (bowl molds)  
🗜️ Tongs  
📦 Rectangular tray  

## Steps  
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water overnight to allow full absorption.
2. Drain the soaked rice and place it in a blender. Add 250g of water and blend until smooth to form a rice paste.
3. Strain the rice paste through a fine mesh strainer to remove any residue, resulting in a smooth raw rice paste. Set aside.
4. In a pot, bring 400g of boiling water to a boil, add 60g of white sugar, and stir until the sugar is fully dissolved. Turn off the heat.  
**Key point**: The sugar water must be brought to a boil and the sugar must be completely dissolved.

5. Combine the hot syrup: While the sugar water is still hot, slowly pour half of it into the raw rice paste while stirring quickly to form a thick cooked paste. Immediately pour this cooked paste back into the remaining raw rice paste and continue stirring until evenly combined.  
**Key point**: This step, known as "touching the paste," is crucial to prevent layering or hard cores after steaming. Properly mixing the hot and cold pastes stabilizes the starch structure.

6. Place the small ceramic bowls in the bamboo steamer, cover, and preheat them together with the steamer.
7. Open the steamer, stir the prepared batter again, and fill each preheated bowl to 80% full. Add pre-cooked red beans or mung beans to the bottom or top of each bowl.
8. Cover the steamer and steam over low heat for 15 to 20 minutes, until the batter sets, the surface is smooth, and a toothpick inserted comes out clean.  
**Note**: Use low heat throughout to prevent bubbles on the surface or undercooking; a bamboo steamer absorbs steam, preventing condensation from dripping and ruining the appearance.

9. Remove the bowls immediately after steaming and place them on a tray to cool completely to room temperature.  
**Key point**: The pudding must be completely cooled to achieve a chewy, Q-texture. It is soft and sticky when hot and should not be eaten warm.

10. Once cooled, use two bamboo skewers to gently run around the inside edge of the bowl, then skewer and unmold the pudding. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice  
Bowl pudding is primarily made from rice, making it low in oil and fat, suitable as a light tea snack or treat. However, it has a relatively high sugar content, so those monitoring sugar intake should consume it in moderation. Its chewy texture comes from the starch retrogradation upon cooling; it will stick to teeth when hot and must be fully cooled before eating.
