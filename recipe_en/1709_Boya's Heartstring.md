# Boya's Heartstring  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌱 Jasmine Snow Buds (or seven-infused jasmine green tea) 30g (to brew 1000ml tea broth)  
🥛 K-cream (concentrated cream) 40g (can substitute with 30g whipping cream + 10g condensed milk)  
🥛 Fresh milk 100ml (pasteurized chilled milk recommended)  
🧊 Ice cubes as needed (350g for cooling tea broth; add up to 600ml line during preparation)  
🍯 Bamboo cane rock sugar syrup 35g  

## Tools & Equipment  
🫖 Teapot (for steeping tea leaves)  
🥄 Measuring cup  
🔗 Strainer (for filtering tea broth)  
🔬 Cocktail shaker (for mixing and shaking)  
🥃 Glass (for final serving)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place 30g jasmine snow bud tea leaves in a teapot, pour in 1000ml of 80°C hot water, cover, and steep for 7 minutes.  
   **Note**: Use 80°C hot water to fully release the tea aroma and avoid bitterness; strictly control the steeping time to 7 minutes.  

2. Strain the tea broth into a container using a strainer, immediately add 350g ice cubes, and stir to rapidly cool the tea broth.  
   **Key point**: Strain promptly to prevent over-extraction; rapid cooling locks in the tea aroma and prevents flavor loss.  

3. In a cocktail shaker, add 40g K-cream, 100ml fresh milk, about 250ml of the cooled jasmine tea broth, and 35g bamboo cane rock sugar syrup.  

4. Add ice cubes up to the 600ml mark, close the lid tightly, and shake vigorously until the mixture turns an even milky white.  
   **Key point**: Shake thoroughly to blend all flavors; if no cocktail shaker is available, a stirring rod can be used as a substitute.  

5. Pour the shaken milk tea into a glass filled with ice cubes and serve.  
   **Note**: Adding extra ice to the glass maintains an icy, refreshing texture; dilution level will vary over time.  

## Health Advice  
This Boya's Heartstring features a refreshing jasmine aroma and silky milk flavor, making it ideal for an afternoon pick-me-up or to cut through summer greasiness. Note that the syrup amount can be halved to avoid excess sugar; those with lactose intolerance can substitute with plant-based milk.
