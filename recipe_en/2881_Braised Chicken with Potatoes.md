# Braised Chicken with Potatoes
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken 1500g (about 1300g after cleaning, cut into pieces, pat dry with kitchen paper)  
🥔 Potatoes 730g (about 2, peeled and cut into large chunks)  
🧄 Garlic 15g (about 4 cloves, smashed)  
🫚 Ginger 20g (about 4 slices, sliced)  
🧅 Scallion 20g (about one section, cut into sections)  
⭐ Star anise 2 (use 1.5 if very large)  
🍃 Bay leaves 2  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste (adjust according to preference)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for greasing the pan and stir-frying)  
🧂 Chinese allspice/five-spice powder about 1 level teaspoon (1.5g)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 30g (about 3 tablespoons)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 10g (about 1 tablespoon)  
🥄 Oyster sauce 20g (about 1 heaping tablespoon)  
🍬 Sugar 6g (about 2 teaspoons)  
💧 Boiling/hot water enough to cover the ingredients (must use hot water)  
🧂 Salt about 1g (half a teaspoon, add to taste before removing from heat)  
🧂 MSG 3g (about 1 teaspoon)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok (round-bottomed)  
🥄 Spatula (for stir-frying, scooping oil, and skimming foam)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for gently turning during the sauce reduction stage)  
🥣 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam)  
🪵 Wooden lid (for simmering)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat the iron wok over high heat until smoking. Add enough cooking oil and swirl to coat the wok walls. Heat until the oil smokes, then pour out the excess oil.  
**Note**: This is a key step to prevent the chicken from sticking to the wok.  
2. Keep the heat on high. Add ginger slices first and fry until the edges are golden brown. Then add the chicken pieces along the side of the wok. Do not stir initially; let them sear for 20–30 seconds to set.  
3. Continue stir-frying the chicken over high heat, occasionally turning and flattening the pieces to sear them. This should take about **7 minutes** in total, until the chicken skin is tightened and slightly charred, and the oil in the wok becomes clear.  
**Note**: The chicken must be stir-fried thoroughly to remove any gamey smell and develop a dry, fragrant flavor.  
4. Add the scallion sections, smashed garlic, star anise, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry quickly until fragrant. Add 1.5g of Chinese allspice or five-spice powder and stir until aromatic.  
5. Add the potato chunks and continue stir-frying for 2–3 minutes to allow the potatoes to absorb the spices.  
6. Pour in the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar along the side of the wok. Stir evenly.  
7. Add enough **boiling or hot water** to cover the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then use a slotted spoon to skim off any foam.  
8. Cover with the lid, reduce the heat to **medium-low**, and simmer for **30 minutes**.  
**Note**: If using a hen over one year old, add more water first and braise the chicken alone for 40 minutes before adding the potatoes.  
9. After 30 minutes, remove all the spices except the dried chili peppers. Turn the heat to **high** to reduce the sauce.  
**Note**: At this point, the potatoes are very soft. Use **chopsticks** to gently turn them while the sauce reduces to prevent sticking and breaking the potato pieces.  
10. When the sauce has thickened to the desired consistency, add salt and MSG to taste. Stir evenly, then turn off the heat and serve.  
**Note**: Since light soy sauce and oyster sauce already contain salt, add salt sparingly to avoid oversalting. Garnish with cilantro if desired.  

## Health Advice
This dish provides a balanced combination of protein and starch. Chicken is low in fat and high in protein, while potatoes are rich in potassium and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose weight. Be mindful of the salt content (light soy sauce and oyster sauce are salty), and those with high blood pressure can reduce the added salt.
