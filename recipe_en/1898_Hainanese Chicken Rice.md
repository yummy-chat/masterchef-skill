# Hainanese Chicken Rice  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Wenchang Chicken 1 whole  
🍚 Silk-thread rice or Thai jasmine rice 500g (soak for 1 hour in advance)  
🌿 Pandan leaves as needed (tie into knots or snap into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic as needed (lightly crushed with skin on)  
🍋 Small green limes 2-3 (for dipping sauce, to enhance freshness)  
🧈 Chicken fat as needed (yellow chicken fat, for stir-frying rice)  
🍯 Sweet soy sauce as needed (dipping sauce)  
🫚 Ginger-garlic paste as needed (dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili sauce as needed (Southeast Asian-style dipping sauce)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (to mix into rice after cooking)  
🍲 Chicken broth 400g (from poaching the chicken, for cooking rice)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🫕 Large pot (for poaching whole chicken)  
🫖 Clay pot/casserole (for cooking Hainanese chicken rice)  
🔥 Small gas stove  
🥄 Soup ladle (for scooping chicken broth)  
🥣 Bowl (for preparing dipping sauces)  
🧻 Towel (to protect hands from heat, for rotating the pot)  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak for about 1 hour, allowing the grains to absorb water fully, resulting in fluffier rice after cooking.  
2. Prepare the aromatics: tie pandan leaves into knots or snap them into pieces, lightly crush garlic with the skin on; prepare the dipping sauces.  
3. **Poach the chicken**: Bring water in a large pot to a boil. Holding the chicken by the head, submerge it into the boiling water and lift it out again. Repeat this three times to release the cold air from the cavity. Then fully submerge the chicken in the water, maintaining a gentle simmer (shrimp eye water). **Do not cover the pot, and do not use high heat** throughout the entire 25-minute poaching process.  
   **Note**: Covering the pot may cause the chicken skin to overcook or the meat to become tough.  
4. **Cold bath**: Remove the poached chicken and immediately plunge it into cold or ice water, allowing the skin to become smooth and the meat to tighten.  
5. **Stir-fry rice and cook**: Heat the clay pot over medium heat. Add chicken fat and sauté the crushed garlic with skin until fragrant. Add the soaked rice and stir until evenly coated with oil. Add the pandan leaves and pour in the reserved chicken poaching broth.  
6. Cover the pot and bring to a boil over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until the liquid is mostly absorbed. Then reduce to low heat.  
7. **Rotate the pot**: Hold the lid with a wet towel and continuously rotate the pot to change the heat exposure on different sides, for about 20 minutes.  
   **Purpose**: To ensure even heating of the rice, prevent burning the bottom, and create a crispy rice crust.  
8. Once the rice is cooked, uncover, remove the garlic cloves and pandan leaves. Sprinkle in a pinch of salt while hot, and fluff the rice with a spoon.  
9. Chop the chicken into serving pieces and arrange on a plate. Serve with sweet soy sauce or a dipping sauce of ginger-garlic paste mixed with small green lime juice and chili sauce.  

## Health Advice  
Hainanese chicken rice uses chicken fat to stir-fry the rice and poaching broth to cook it, offering balanced nutrition but relatively high fat content. It is recommended for occasional consumption. The chicken skin is smooth and tender due to the cold bath step; those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should remove the skin before eating, and avoid the crispy rice crust.
