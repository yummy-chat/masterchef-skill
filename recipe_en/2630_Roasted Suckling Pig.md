# Roasted Suckling Pig
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 1 suckling pig (about 4 weeks old, cleaned and gutted)
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌿 Thirteen Spices, to taste
🌿 Five Spice Powder, to taste
🧴 White vinegar, to taste
🧴 Zhejiang vinegar, to taste
🍶 Erguotou (Chinese liquor), to taste
🥄 Baking soda, a small amount
🍯 Maltose, about 10g (melted)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for brushing the skin)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪓 Round chopping board
🔪 Chef's knife
🛠️ Wire cutters
🔱 Steel fork
🪵 Wooden frame
🧹 Wire
🍲 Large iron pot
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🔥 Hanging oven
🪝 Hook
🍖 Open grill
🖌️ Brush
🪬 Aluminum foil

## Preparation Steps
1.  Split the suckling pig open from the belly. Remove the brain, neck bones, neck lymph nodes, and shoulder blades from the front legs. Score the meat on the ribs and thicker areas to separate the skin from the meat.
**Key Point**: Removing specific parts reduces gaminess and ensures good shape; scoring helps with flavor absorption and even heating.

2.  Use wire to tightly bind the pig's front legs crossed together.
**Key Point**: Prevents the legs from spreading out and burning during roasting.

3.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Hold the pig and submerge it completely for about 10 seconds to tighten the skin. Remove immediately and rinse with cold water.
**Key Point**: The blanching time should be short, just to tighten the skin and reduce gaminess; rinsing with cold water prevents residual heat from overcooking the meat and rendering out fat.

4.  Mix sugar, salt, Thirteen Spices, and Five Spice Powder to make a dry rub. Apply the rub evenly to the inside cavity of the pig. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to marinate.
**Key Point**: The rub must be applied evenly, and the marinating time must be sufficient for the flavor to penetrate.

5.  Insert a long steel fork from the pig's hind legs, through the body, and out to the head. Place a wooden frame inside the pig's belly to prop it up. Use wire to securely tie the front and back legs to the steel fork.
**Key Point**: Ensure the pig is completely fixed so it does not slide or deform during roasting.

6.  Mix white vinegar, Zhejiang vinegar, Erguotou, a small amount of baking soda, and melted maltose to make the crispy skin water. Use a brush to apply the crispy skin water evenly and thoroughly over the pig's skin.
**Key Point**: The crispy skin water must be applied evenly; this is crucial for achieving a uniform, bright red color after roasting.

7.  Hang the secured pig in a preheated hanging oven at about 60°C (140°F) using a hook. Cover about half of the oven lid and roast at low temperature for about 1 hour.
**Key Point**: The temperature should not be too high; the main goal is to cook the inside and dry the skin. Do not cover the lid completely to prevent steaming.

8.  Remove the cooked pig from the oven. Wrap the ears and tail tip with aluminum foil. Roast the skin over an open fire grill while holding the steel fork. Start roasting from the tail and gradually move towards the head. During this process, brush the skin with cooking oil to ensure even heating and a shinier color. Roast until the skin color changes from light yellow to a bright, uniform deep ruby red and the skin becomes crispy.
**Key Point**: Wrapping the ears and tail prevents them from burning; brushing oil makes the skin glossier.

9.  Once the pig skin is evenly colored, bright red, and crispy, it is ready to be removed from the fire. Serve with white sugar for dipping.

## Health Advice
This roasted suckling pig is high in protein and fat. It is suitable for sharing with family, but do not consume more than 3-5 pieces per serving. Pair with cold mixed vegetables to balance the greasiness. The key to crispy skin lies in low-temperature drying; it is recommended to pat the pig skin dry with a kitchen paper towel before roasting to prevent blistering.
