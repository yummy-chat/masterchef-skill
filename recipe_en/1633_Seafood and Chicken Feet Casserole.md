# Seafood and Chicken Feet Casserole
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦶 Chicken Feet: appropriate amount (fried until tiger-skin textured, pressure-cooked until soft)  
🦐 Shrimp: appropriate amount  
🐚 Abalone: appropriate amount  
🦑 Squid: appropriate amount (cut into pieces or scored)  
🐚 Clams: appropriate amount (essential, provides umami flavor)  
🧄 Garlic: appropriate amount (whole cloves or slightly crushed)  
🌿 Galangal (Sand Ginger): appropriate amount (core flavor of this dish)  
🌶️ Bird’s Eye Chili: 2–3 pieces  
🌿 Perilla (or Cilantro): appropriate amount (added just before serving)  
🧅 Scallion: a little (for pressure-cooking chicken feet)  
🫚 Ginger: a little (for pressure-cooking chicken feet)  
🌿 Bay Leaves, Star Anise, Dried Galangal: a little each (for pressure-cooking chicken feet)  
🫘 Red Yeast Rice: a little (for coloring)  
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce: appropriate amount (used twice)  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (used twice)  
🍶 Rice Wine: appropriate amount (to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🦪 Oyster Sauce: appropriate amount (to enhance umami)  
🍬 White Sugar: appropriate amount (for seasoning)  
🍯 Light Soy Sauce: appropriate amount (poured along the side of the wok)  
🧂 White Pepper Powder: appropriate amount (to enhance aroma)  
🌊 Cornstarch Slurry: a small amount (to thicken slightly before serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure Cooker  
🥄 Strainer or Slotted Spoon  
✂️ Kitchen Scissors  
🔪 Cutting Board and Knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare Chicken Feet**: Heat oil to about 180°C and fry the chicken feet until the skin turns golden and they float. High heat quickly solidifies and dehydrates the collagen in the skin, creating a tiger-skin texture, which is key for absorbing the broth. Transfer the fried chicken feet to a pressure cooker. Add bay leaves, star anise, galangal, scallion, ginger, hot water, red yeast rice, dark soy sauce, and a little salt. Once the pressure cooker reaches full pressure, cook for 2–3 minutes until the chicken feet are fully infused with broth and plump. Remove and trim the toenails with scissors.  
2. **Prepare Seafood**: Devein the shrimp. Clean the abalone and squid. Score the squid or cut into pieces. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add rice wine, then quickly blanch the squid and abalone. Remove and set aside. Clean the clams thoroughly.  
3. **Aromatics**: Heat oil in a wok. Add the garlic cloves and sliced galangal. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant, then remove the aromatics, leaving the infused oil in the wok.  
4. **Sear Shrimp**: In the reserved oil, sear the shrimp until they change color and release their red oil. Remove and set aside.  
5. **Prepare the Broth Base**: Return the fried garlic and galangal to the wok. Add the clams, pour rice wine along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry quickly until the clams open. Add an appropriate amount of water and bring to a boil.  
6. **Combine and Season**: Add the bird’s eye chilies. Return the pressure-cooked chicken feet, blanched squid and abalone, and seared shrimp to the wok. Season with oyster sauce, salt, and white sugar. Pour light soy sauce along the edge of the wok and sprinkle with white pepper. Cook over medium heat for 1–2 minutes, allowing the chicken feet to fully absorb the seafood umami.  
7. **Thicken the Sauce**: Drizzle in a small amount of cornstarch slurry to lightly thicken the sauce, allowing it to coat the ingredients evenly.  
8. **Finish and Serve**: Just before turning off the heat, adjust the color with a little more dark soy sauce if needed. Add the perilla leaves and toss everything together evenly.  

## Health Tips  
This Seafood and Chicken Feet Casserole is rich in collagen, but chicken feet are also relatively high in fat. Pair with vegetables for balanced nutrition. Those with seafood allergies should exercise caution. Individuals with high blood pressure should monitor their salt intake.
