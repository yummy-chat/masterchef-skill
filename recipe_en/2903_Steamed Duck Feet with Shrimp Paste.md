# Steamed Duck Feet with Shrimp Paste  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Duck feet, appropriate amount (pre-deboned)  
🦐 Fresh shrimp, appropriate amount (for making shrimp paste)  
🥓 Fatty pork, appropriate amount (cut into small dices)  
🥬 Choy sum, appropriate amount (as a side vegetable)  
🍖 Ham, appropriate amount (diced, for garnish)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (leaves only, for garnish)  
🥚 Eggs, appropriate amount (egg white only, for brushing the surface)  
🧅 Ginger, appropriate amount (for removing odor)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for removing odor)  
🍶 Rice wine, appropriate amount (for removing odor)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (season in stages)  
🧂 Sugar, appropriate amount (for seasoning and enhancing freshness)  
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch (for aroma)  
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for binding shrimp paste, dusting duck feet, and thickening the sauce)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (core seasoning for the sauce)  
🧂 Chicken powder, a pinch (for enhancing freshness)  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, 2 drops (for coloring the sauce)  
🧈 Lard oil or cooking oil, appropriate amount (for shine and finish)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for blanching choy sum)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Strainer  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Debone the duck feet**: Remove the bones from the duck feet while keeping the shape intact.  
2. **Blanch duck feet to remove odor**: Add water to a pot, then add ginger, scallions, rice wine, and salt. Add the deboned duck feet and cook for 3 minutes. Remove and drain thoroughly.  
**Key point**: Blanching for exactly 3 minutes removes odor without making the duck feet tough; draining thoroughly ensures the shrimp paste adheres well during stuffing.  

3. **Make the shrimp paste**: Pound fresh shrimp to break them up, then chop into a fine paste. Add salt, sugar, white pepper, and cornstarch. Stir in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky and elastic. Add the diced fatty pork and mix evenly.  
**Key point**: Pounding and chopping releases the shrimp's natural gelatin, and stirring until elastic is essential for a bouncy texture; the fatty pork adds moisture and richness, preventing the shrimp paste from being dry.  

4. **Dust the duck feet**: Lightly dust the concave side of each deboned duck foot with a thin layer of cornstarch.  
**Key point**: The cornstarch acts as a binder, preventing the shrimp paste from detaching during steaming.  

5. **Stuff and shape**: Stuff an appropriate amount of shrimp paste into each duck foot, shaping it into a dome (higher in the center, lower at the edges). Brush the surface with egg white, sprinkle with diced ham, and place a cilantro leaf on top for garnish.  
**Key point**: The shape should be neat; the egg white gives a shiny surface after steaming; the ham and cilantro enhance aroma and appearance.  

6. **Prepare the choy sum**: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add a pinch of sugar, salt, and a few drops of oil. Blanch the choy sum until just cooked, then remove and drain.  
**Key point**: Seasoning the blanching water keeps the choy sum bright green and crisp-tender, no further cooking is needed.  

7. **Steam the duck feet**: Place the stuffed duck feet in a steamer and steam at high heat for 3 minutes.  
**Doneness check**: The shrimp paste should be fully cooked and opaque, while the duck feet remain tender and springy. Do not over-steam, or the shrimp paste will become tough and the duck feet will lose their bounce.  

8. **Make the oyster sauce glaze**: Add water to a wok, then season with oyster sauce, sugar, chicken powder, and a pinch of white pepper. When the liquid is just simmering, thicken with a cornstarch slurry. Add 2 drops of dark soy sauce for color, and finish by drizzling in some lard oil or cooking oil for shine, then mix well.  
**Key point**: Thicken the sauce when it is just simmering for a smoother texture; use only a small amount of dark soy sauce to avoid darkening the dish; the finishing oil enhances the sauce's gloss and silkiness.  

9. **Plate and pour the sauce**: Arrange the blanched choy sum on a plate as a base, place the steamed duck feet on top, and evenly drizzle the oyster sauce glaze over them.  
**Key point**: Plating should be aesthetically pleasing, showing respect for the dish; the glaze should fully coat the shrimp paste for better flavor and visual appeal.  

## Health Advice  
This dish features deboned duck feet paired with high-protein shrimp paste. Steaming keeps it light and low in fat, making it suitable for those who enjoy texture without greasiness. Ensure the shrimp paste is well-kneaded for a bouncy texture, and strictly control the steaming time to 3 minutes to avoid overcooking.
