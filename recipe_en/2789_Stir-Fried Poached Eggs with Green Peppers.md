# Stir-Fried Poached Eggs with Green Peppers
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 6  
🌶️ Erjingtiao Chili Peppers 6 (if not spicy, substitute with large green peppers)  
🌶️ Large Red Pepper about half a small one (for color)  
🧅 Scallion 1  
🧄 Garlic 3 cloves  
🧂 Salt 1 teaspoon (about 2g)  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance freshness)  
🧂 Chicken Bouillon a pinch  
🧴 Light Soy Sauce 5g (drizzle along the side of the wok)  
🫒 Bright Oil a pinch (to add shine)  
🫒 Cooking Oil (base oil) as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥄 Wok Spatula  
🥢 Slotted Spoon  
🔪 Cutting Board and Chef's Knife  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Remove the stems from the Erjingtiao chili peppers and cut into small pieces. Cut the large red pepper into small pieces. Split the scallion and cut into small pieces. Crush the garlic and mince into garlic paste.
2. Place the wok over high heat until very hot. Add base oil to coat the wok thoroughly, then pour out the hot oil, leaving a small amount of base oil in the wok.  
   **Note**: The wok must be very hot and well-oiled to prevent the eggs from sticking.
3. Reduce to low heat. Crack in the eggs one by one. Fry until both sides are golden brown and the edges are charred and firm. Keep the heat low throughout; ensure no raw water is in the wok.
4. Remove the fried eggs and set aside.
5. Leave base oil in the wok. Heat over high heat. Add all the chopped aromatics and stir-fry until the green peppers are cooked through and show a tiger-skin pattern on the surface.
6. Reduce to low heat. Add salt, sugar, and chicken bouillon to season. Drizzle light soy sauce and bright oil along the side of the wok.
7. Add the fried eggs back to the wok. Immediately turn off the heat. Use residual heat to quickly stir-fry and mix evenly, allowing the flavors to meld. Then remove from the wok.  
   **Key**: Stir-frying with the heat off prevents the chili fragrance from being lost excessively under high temperature.

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in protein and vitamin C, suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, fried eggs absorb a lot of oil, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance calories. Adjust the amount of chili according to personal tolerance; those with sensitive stomachs should reduce the amount of Erjingtiao peppers.
