# Flourless Potato Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 potato (peeled)  
🥚 1 egg  
🥬 Some lettuce  
🍜 100g cooked fire chicken noodles  
🌭 1-2 cooked starch sausages (pre-fried)  
🍗 1 piece of fried chicken cutlet (pre-cooked)  
🫒 Some cooking oil  
🖤 Some black sesame seeds  
🌶️ Some fire chicken noodle sauce  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔪 Vegetable peeler  
🥄 Spatula or food tongs  
🫗 Oil spray bottle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Preheat a frying pan over medium heat and spray a small amount of cooking oil to evenly coat the bottom with a thin layer of oil.  
2. Use a vegetable peeler to slice the potato directly into thin slices, evenly covering the pan bottom to form a complete pancake base.  
   **Key point**: The thin slices are more uniform; they become translucent when heated and naturally stick together.  
3. Crack an egg over the arranged potato slices and gently spread the egg liquid with a spatula to evenly cover the potato slices.  
4. When the egg liquid is half-set, evenly sprinkle black sesame seeds over the surface.  
   **Note**: This is black sesame, not nuts.  
5. Gently shake the pan to confirm the bottom is fully set and golden brown, then flip the entire potato and egg pancake.  
   **Key point**: Use a spatula or a toss to flip, keeping the pancake intact and avoiding breakage.  
6. Evenly spread fire chicken noodle sauce over the flipped potato side.  
7. On the sauced surface, layer lettuce, cooked fire chicken noodles, cooked starch sausages, and cooked fried chicken cutlet in order.  
   **Key point**: The fillings can be flexibly replaced according to personal preference.  
8. Turn off the heat, fold or roll up the potato pancake to wrap all the fillings, then remove from the pan.  
   **Key point**: Wear disposable gloves during operation for easy shaping and hygiene.  

## Health Advice  
This potato pancake has a balanced ratio of carbohydrates and protein, making it suitable for quick energy replenishment after exercise. Note that the starch sausages and fried chicken cutlet are high in calories, so avoid overconsumption.
