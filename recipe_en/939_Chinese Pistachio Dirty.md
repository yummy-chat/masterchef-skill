# Chinese Pistachio Dirty
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
☕ Double shot of espresso  
🥜 Pistachio paste, as needed  
🌰 Chopped pistachios, as needed  
🥛 Ice-brewed milk, as needed  
🍯 Condensed milk, as needed  

## Tools & Equipment
☕ Coffee machine (including grinder and tamper)  
🥣 Plate  
🥛 Coffee cup (pre-chilled in freezer)  
🥄 Stirring spoon  

## Steps
1. Place the coffee cup in the freezer to chill. On a plate, separately put the pistachio paste and chopped pistachios.  
   **Key Point**: A frozen cup enhances the frosting effect, making it easier for the rim to hold the paste and nut pieces.  
2. Remove the chilled cup, dip the rim first into the pistachio paste, then into the chopped pistachios, forming a ring of nut coating around the cup.  
3. Mix the ice-brewed milk with an appropriate amount of pistachio paste and condensed milk, stir until well combined, and pour into the cup.  
4. Adjust the coffee machine settings: slightly increase the water temperature and extend the pre-infusion time. Tamp the coffee grounds firmly, then extract the double shot of espresso directly into the cup.  
   **Key Point**: Allowing the espresso to fall directly into the cup creates a natural layered dirty effect; higher water temperature and longer pre-infusion yield richer crema and clearer layers.  
5. Once the espresso slowly settles into the green milk, forming distinct layers, it is ready to serve.  

## Health Advice
Pistachio paste combined with ice-brewed milk provides high-quality fats, protein, and antioxidants from nuts, making it suitable for those who need a quick energy boost but dislike black coffee. However, condensed milk is high in sugar; for sugar-controlled diets, reduce the amount by half or substitute with a sugar alternative.
