# Iced Tangyuan

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, 200g
💧 Boiling water, 120ml (for dough)
🍬 Brown sugar, 60g
💧 Water, 80ml (for syrup)
🍶 Fermented glutinous rice (jiuniang), as needed
🫘 Candied red beans, as needed
🍎 Dried hawthorn, as needed
🍇 Raisins, as needed
🫐 Wolfberries (goji berries), as needed (soaked in advance)
🧊 Ice cubes, as needed

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🍲 Small stainless steel milk pot
🔥 Induction cooker
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Regular spoon
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥛 Measuring cup
📦 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead and Rest Dough**: Place glutinous rice flour in a bowl. Gradually add boiling water while stirring with chopsticks until flaky. Then knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 15 minutes.
**Key Point**: Must use boiling water, otherwise the dough lacks stickiness; do not skip resting.
2. **Prepare Brown Sugar Syrup**: Add brown sugar and 80ml water to a small pot. Heat over low flame and stir continuously until the sugar is fully dissolved into a syrup. Remove from heat and let cool completely.
**Key Point**: Stir continuously to prevent burning; must cool before use.
3. **Shape Tangyuan**: Roll the rested dough into a long log. Cut into evenly sized small pieces. Cover unused pieces with plastic wrap to prevent drying. Roll each piece into a small ball between your palms.
4. **Cook Tangyuan**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the tangyuan and cook over medium heat. Gently push them with the back of a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. When all tangyuan float to the surface and have visibly enlarged, they are cooked.
5. **Ice Water Bath**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the tangyuan. Immediately immerse them in prepared ice water. Soak for a few minutes, then drain.
**Key Point**: This step gives the tangyuan a chewy, bouncy texture.
6. **Prepare Ice Crumbs**: Chop or scrape ice cubes into fine or coarse ice crumbs.
7. **Assemble and Serve**: In a bowl, place the ice-bathed tangyuan and ice crumbs. Layer on the fermented glutinous rice, candied red beans, dried hawthorn, raisins, and soaked wolfberries. Finally, drizzle the chilled brown sugar syrup on top.

## Health Advice
This iced tangyuan is refreshing and helps beat the heat, but both glutinous rice and brown sugar are high-glycemic ingredients. People with high blood sugar or those needing to control sugar intake should eat sparingly, and even others should consume in moderation. The candied red beans and raisins in the toppings are high in sugar; feel free to reduce them and add more dried hawthorn to cut through the richness and stimulate the appetite.
