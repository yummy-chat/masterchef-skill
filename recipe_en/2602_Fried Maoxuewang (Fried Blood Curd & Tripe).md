# Fried Maoxuewang (Fried Blood Curd & Tripe)

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Fried chicken 2 pieces (whole chicken legs)  
🦆 Duck blood curd appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🥩 Luncheon meat appropriate amount (sliced)  
🐄 White tripod appropriate amount  
🐄 Black tripod appropriate amount  
🐄 Yellow throat (aorta) appropriate amount  
🥠 Fried bean curd rolls appropriate amount  
🥬 Lettuce appropriate amount  
🥔 Potato 1 piece (sliced)  
🌱 Bean sprouts appropriate amount  
🥬 Tianjin preserved vegetable appropriate amount  
🟡 Golden chickpeas (chickpeas) appropriate amount  

🌶️ Erjingtiao chili powder 3 tbsp  
🌶️ Chaotianjiao chili powder 6 tbsp  
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount  
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount (for chili oil, stir-frying base, and oil splashing)  
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor) a small amount  
🤍 White sesame seeds to taste  
🍲 Hot pot base about 3 tbsp (chopped)  
Pixian chili bean paste about 3 tbsp (red oil variety)  
🧅 Ginger, scallion, garlic appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder a pinch  
🧂 Salt a pinch  
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments 1 handful  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Portable stove  
🥄 Strainer  
🧂 Seasoning bottle  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Make chili oil**: Place Erjingtiao chili powder and Chaotianjiao chili powder in a stainless steel bowl. Moisten with a small amount of sesame oil. Heat rapeseed oil until it begins to smoke lightly, then pour it into the chili powder in three batches, stirring well after each pour. When the oil temperature drops to warm, add a small amount of baijiu to enhance aroma and lower the temperature, then mix in white sesame seeds to taste.  
**Note**: Pour the oil in three stages, following the principle of "first for fragrance, second for flavor, third for color."  

2. **Prepare duck blood curd**: Cut duck blood curd into pieces, blanch in boiling water for about one minute, drain and set aside to remove any off-flavors.  
3. **Cook the soup base**:  
   - Heat the wok and pour in rapeseed oil.  
   - Once the oil is hot, reduce to low heat and sauté ginger, scallion, and garlic until fragrant.  
   - Add the chopped hot pot base and Pixian chili bean paste, stir-fry until the red oil releases and the aroma is strong.  
   - Pour in enough boiling water, add a pinch of chicken bouillon powder and salt to season.  
   - Turn to high heat and bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 5–6 minutes to let the spices meld.  
   - Use a strainer to remove the solid residue from the soup, yielding a clear base broth.  
4. **Cook the side ingredients**:  
   - Place the blanched duck blood curd and potato slices into the base broth. Cook for 5–6 minutes.  
   - Add Tianjin preserved vegetable, cook for about 3 more minutes.  
   - Gradually add luncheon meat, fried bean curd rolls, yellow throat, white tripod, and black tripod. For tripe varieties, blanch for about 30–40 seconds only.  
   - Use a strainer to remove all the cooked side ingredients.  
5. **Assemble**: Place the two pieces of fried chicken in the center of a large bowl. Arrange the cooked side ingredients around it, then pour all the red broth from the wok over the contents, ensuring the liquid covers most of the ingredients.  
6. **Splash oil for fragrance**: In a separate pan, heat rapeseed oil until hot. Sprinkle dried chili pepper segments and golden chickpeas over the surface of the ingredients in the bowl. Pour the hot oil over the dried chilies to release their fragrance. Optionally, drizzle with homemade chili oil to taste.  

## Health Advice
This Fried Maoxuewang combines the crispiness of fried chicken with the smooth texture of tripe, but it is high in calories. It is suitable for occasional consumption or as an energy replenishment after intense exercise. Ensure that the duck blood curd and tripe are fully cooked to avoid digestive discomfort.
