# Teriyaki Chicken Thigh Rice
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧂 Seasonings  
- Cooking wine  
- Light soy sauce  
- Honey (optional)  
- Black pepper flakes  
- Black pepper powder  
- Salt  
- Dark soy sauce  
- Cooking oil  
- Water  

🥬 Ingredients  
- Chicken thighs  
- Broccoli  
- Carrot  
- Rice  
- Garlic  
- Ginger  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Kitchenware  
- Cutting board  
- Knife  
- Bowl  
- Pot  

## Cooking Steps
1. Debone the chicken thighs: Place the chicken skin-side down on the cutting board. Use the tip of the knife to cut along the bone edge to separate the meat. Use your fingers or the back of the knife to push away the membrane, then twist and pull out the bone. Repeat for all chicken thighs.  
2. Marinate the deboned chicken thighs with black pepper powder, black pepper flakes, salt, and ginger slices for 5 minutes.  
3. In a bowl, mix cooking wine, light soy sauce, honey, dark soy sauce, and water, stirring evenly to make the sauce.  
4. Blanch the broccoli and carrots in boiling water for 1–2 minutes until cooked but still crisp. Remove and set aside.  
5. Heat 15 ml of cooking oil in a pot over high heat, then add garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.  
6. Add the chicken thighs and pan-fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown.  
7. Pour in the prepared sauce, cover the pot, and simmer over medium-low heat for 5–10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce thickens and bubbles.  
8. Slice the chicken thighs and arrange them on top of the rice along with the vegetables. Drizzle the sauce from the pot over the dish.  

## Health Suggestions
Teriyaki chicken thigh rice is rich in protein and vegetables, making it suitable for muscle building and fat loss. However, the sauce contains sugar and sodium, so people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of honey and soy sauce. It is recommended to pair it with brown rice or increase the vegetable proportion to balance carbohydrate intake.
