# Tomato Egg Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork – about 200g (lean meat preferred, minced)
🍅 Tomato – 2 (cut into chunks)
🥚 Eggs – 3 (beaten into egg wash)
🫑 Green peppers – 3 (some cut into rings for garnish)
🧅 Scallions – to taste (chopped into scallion flowers and segments)
🧄 Garlic – 2 cloves (minced)
🫚 Ginger – 1 small piece (sliced)
🧂 Salt – to taste
🧴 Light soy sauce – to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce – to taste
🫒 Cooking oil – to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Small milk pot (with handle, for frying egg wrappers)
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls, plates
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon
🥄 Spatula
♨️ Wok or frying pan

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the meat filling**: Mince the pork into fine paste. Add scallion flowers, salt, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Mix well by hand, stirring in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and firm.
2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Cut tomatoes into chunks, slice some green peppers into rings, slice ginger, mince garlic, chop scallions into flowers and segments, and beat the eggs thoroughly into egg wash.
3. **Fry the egg wrappers**: Heat a small milk pot over low heat. Add a little cooking oil and coat the bottom. Pour in one ladle of egg wash, gently swirl the pot to form a thin round sheet.
   - **Key point**: Use just enough oil and egg wash to cover the bottom; keep the heat low to prevent the egg sheet from hardening too quickly.
4. **Wrap the dumplings**: When the bottom of the egg sheet is set but the surface is still runny, place a small portion of meat filling on one side. Use chopsticks to fold the other side over the filling, gently pressing the edges to seal.
   - **Key point**: The folding must be done before the egg wash sets completely, or it won’t seal; too high heat will make the egg sheet brittle and prone to cracking.
5. **Cook the dumplings**: Continue frying the wrapped dumplings in the pot, turning them until both sides are golden brown and the filling is fully cooked. Set aside.
6. **Make the tomato sauce**: Heat oil in a wok or frying pan. Sauté minced garlic and ginger slices until fragrant. Add tomato chunks and salt. Use a spatula to press and stir continuously until the tomatoes soften and break down into a thick sauce.
   - **Heat**: Medium heat; **Texture**: Tomatoes fully softened, sauce thick and rich.
7. **Simmer the dumplings**: Add some water to the tomato sauce and bring to a boil. Gently place the fried dumplings into the sauce. Season with light soy sauce and oyster sauce. Reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for a few minutes to let the dumplings absorb the flavors.
8. **Reduce the sauce and plate**: Add green pepper rings. Increase heat to high and reduce the sauce until thick. Sprinkle with scallion segments. Remove from heat and serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Tomato egg dumplings feature egg wrappers filled with meat and served with a tomato sauce. They are rich in protein and lycopene, making them ideal for energy replenishment and for children to stimulate appetite. Keep the heat low when frying egg wrappers to prevent them from hardening. Adding a little water to the meat filling can make it more tender and smooth.
