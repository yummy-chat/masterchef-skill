# Homemade Konjac Snack (Moyu Shuang)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍥 Ready-made konjac 1000g (500g white, 500g black)
🫘 White sesame seeds, as needed
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed
🍳 Hot oil, as needed
🧴 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons
🧴 Oyster sauce 2 tablespoons
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tablespoon
🍬 Granulated sugar 1 tablespoon
🍶 Fragrant vinegar 1 tablespoon
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder 1 teaspoon
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3 teaspoons
🧂 Salt 1 teaspoon
🍶 White vinegar, as needed (for soaking)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large clear glass bowl (for soaking)
🍲 Stainless steel pot (for blanching)
🥣 Heat-resistant glass bowl (for making sauce)
🥄 Soup spoon, teaspoon (for measuring)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)

## Steps
1. Place the ready-made konjac pieces into a bowl, add water and enough white vinegar, and soak for 1 hour.
   **Key point**: This step effectively removes the alkaline taste from the konjac and improves its texture.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the soaked konjac, and boil over high heat for 2 minutes. Remove and immediately plunge into water or ice water to cool, then drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Blanching and cooling makes the konjac Q弹 (chewy and springy) while further removing the alkaline taste. The konjac must be fully drained, otherwise the sauce will not coat well and the flavor concentration will be affected.
3. In a bowl, combine white sesame seeds and chili powder. Pour in hot oil that has been heated until smoking, and stir well to release the aroma.
   **Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the fragrance.
4. To the bowl of chili oil, add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, chili oil, sugar, fragrant vinegar, thirteen-spice powder, chicken bouillon, and salt. Stir well to make the sauce.
   **Key point**: This batch size is for 1000g of konjac at one time. One tablespoon refers to the ladle used for daily soup consumption.
5. Place the drained konjac into the prepared sauce and mix thoroughly with chopsticks until well coated. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Konjac is low in calories and high in dietary fiber, making it an excellent snack for satisfying cravings during weight loss. However, the sauce contains relatively high amounts of oil, sugar, and salt. It is recommended to mix in moderation to avoid turning a healthy ingredient into a high-calorie burden. This dish is suitable for those who love spicy food but need to control calorie intake. Homemade allows you to adjust the seasoning amount, making it more manageable than store-bought packaged konjac snacks.
