# Jiangxi Spicy Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken feet, appropriate amount (already blanched)  
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, appropriate amount (sliced into rings)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (added before removing from heat)  

🧅 Scallion and ginger, appropriate amount (for stewing)  
🌶️ Dried chili, appropriate amount  
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount  
🍂 Bay leaves, appropriate amount  
🌰 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount  
🥄 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 teaspoon  
🍬 White sugar, 1 teaspoon  
💧 Hot water, appropriate amount (for stewing)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for stir-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Strainer or slotted spoon  
🫖 Lid  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the blanched chicken feet into the wok. Add scallion and ginger, dried chili, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, and white sugar. Pour in an appropriate amount of hot water.  
   **Note**: Add all spices and seasonings at once.  
2. Cover with the lid and stew over medium-low heat for 40 minutes, until the chicken feet are tender and soft.  
3. Use a slotted spoon to remove the stewed chicken feet. Then, strain out the solid residue from the broth, reserving the liquid for later use.  
4. In a separate pan, pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and bird's eye chili rings, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Add the reserved chicken feet and stir-fry evenly, ensuring the chicken feet are well combined with the garlic and bird's eye chili.  
6. Pour in the reserved broth and increase the heat to reduce the sauce.  
   **Note**: Stir continuously during reduction to prevent burning, until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken feet.  
7. Add the cilantro, quickly stir-fry until evenly mixed, then remove from heat.  
   **Key**: Do not overcook the cilantro; mix it in briefly.  

## Health Advice  
This Jiangxi spicy chicken feet dish is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those seeking skin beauty benefits who also enjoy spicy food. However, individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt and light soy sauce. Chicken feet are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair them with vegetables to balance the meal.
