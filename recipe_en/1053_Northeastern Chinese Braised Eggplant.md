# Northeastern Chinese Braised Eggplant
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant 500g (about 3 pieces, remove stem and peel, cut into rolling knife chunks)
🫑 Green bell pepper 40g (about 1 piece, remove stem and seeds, cut into diamond-shaped pieces)
🥕 Carrot 60g (peeled, cut into diamond-shaped slices)
🧄 Garlic 25g (about 5 cloves, finely minced)
🌾 Cornstarch about 7g (divided into two portions: one for coating eggplant, one about 2/3 flat spoon added to sauce)
🍶 Light soy sauce 40g (about 4 tablespoons, reduce to 3 if too salty)
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g (about half a tablespoon)
🍇 Aged vinegar 5g (about half a tablespoon)
🍬 White sugar 10g (about 1 flat tablespoon)
🧂 MSG 3g (about 1 teaspoon)
💧 Water about 100g (for sauce, about 1/3 of a small bowl)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for frying, enough to submerge eggplant)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl (for soaking and coating eggplant)
⚖️ Kitchen scale, small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🥢 Wooden chopsticks, kitchen thermometer (for measuring oil temperature)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫗 Stainless steel bowl, strainer (for storing frying oil)

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the eggplant and cut into rolling knife chunks. Soak in water for 5 minutes, then drain and thoroughly pat dry the surface with paper towels.
**Key point**: Soaking prevents oxidation and helps with coating; drying thoroughly prevents the coating from clumping.

2. Cut the green bell pepper and carrot into diamond-shaped pieces. Smash the garlic cloves and finely mince.
3. In a sauce bowl, sequentially add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, aged vinegar, sugar, MSG, cornstarch, and water. Stir well and set aside.
**Key point**: Stir the sauce again before use to prevent the starch from settling.

4. In a bowl, add cornstarch to the eggplant in batches, tossing to coat evenly each time, until the surface is dry and the pieces do not stick together, forming a thin, tight coating with no visible dry starch or moisture.
**Texture check**: The eggplant pieces should appear matte, feel dry to the touch, and have a firm coating.

5. Heat oil in a wok to about 180°C (356°F). Add the eggplant chunks and fry over maximum heat for about 3.5 to 4 minutes, until the exterior hardens and turns slightly golden. Remove.
**Key point**: Do not stir the eggplant immediately after adding to the oil. Wait about 20 seconds for the exterior to harden, then gently separate with chopsticks.

6. Raise the oil temperature to about 210°C (410°F). Return the eggplant to the wok and fry for a second time for 20 to 30 seconds, until the surface shows distinct golden brown spots. Remove and drain oil.
**Key point**: The second frying should be brief, mainly for coloring and adding crispness.

7. Using the hot oil in the wok, place the carrot and green bell pepper slices in a slotted spoon and submerge in the oil for about 10 seconds. Quickly remove.
**Key point**: The blanching time is extremely short, just to maintain bright color and crisp texture.

8. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Turn off the heat and use residual heat to sauté the minced garlic until fragrant. Pour in the stirred sauce, turn the heat to high, and bring to a boil while stirring constantly until the sauce thickens, becomes translucent, and coats the spoon.

9. Quickly add the fried eggplant and vegetables. Stir-fry rapidly for 10 to 15 seconds to evenly coat the ingredients with the sauce, then immediately remove from heat and plate.
**Key point**: The final stir-fry must be quick to maintain the crispy exterior of the eggplant.

## Health Note
This braised eggplant dish is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C, making it a good appetizer when your appetite is low. However, due to frying, the eggplant absorbs a lot of oil and is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup to balance the meal.
