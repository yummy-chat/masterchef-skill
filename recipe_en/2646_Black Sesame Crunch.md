# Black Sesame Crunch

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October), Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Black sesame seeds 190g (rinsed, then dry-roasted until fragrant)
🥜 Nuts (peanuts + walnuts) 120g (roasted, peanuts skinned)
💖 Pitted red dates 100g
🍒 Goji berries 40g
🍇 Dried cranberries 20g
🍯 Maltose 150g (main binder)
🍬 Brown sugar 50g (adds caramel flavor; can substitute with white sugar or rock sugar)
💧 Warm water 80g

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottom pan (for dry-toasting ingredients and boiling syrup)
🫗 Plastic water bottle (cut open to use as a measuring cup)
🥄 Silicone spatula or spoon (for stirring and mixing)
📦 Square baking pan (lined with parchment paper, used as a shaping mold)
🔨 Hammer plus a book (for pressing the mixture firmly)
🔪 Knife (for cutting into pieces)

## Instructions
**Preparation Phase**
1. Cut the plastic water bottle at the ridged section to use as a measuring cup.
2. Measure 1 bottle of rinsed black sesame seeds, pour into the flat-bottom pan, and stir over low heat continuously until fully cooked, fragrant, and you hear popping sounds. Remove and set aside.
3. Measure 1 bottle of mixed nuts, dry-roast them in the pan, then remove the peanut skins.
4. Weigh the pitted red dates, goji berries, dried cranberries, and other dried fruits as needed, and set aside.
5. In the empty bottle, measure successively 3 bottles of maltose, 3 bottles of warm water, and 2 bottles of brown sugar, and set aside. The warm water can also be used to rinse any remaining maltose from the bottle.

**Cooking Phase**
1. Pour the measured maltose, warm water, and brown sugar into the flat-bottom pan. Heat over low flame, stirring continuously to prevent scorching.
2. Boil until the syrup thickens and, when lifted with a spatula, forms a wide, thick sheet. Immediately turn off the heat.
3. Quickly add the roasted black sesame seeds, nuts, and all the dried fruits into the syrup pan. Use the spatula to stir rapidly and evenly, ensuring every piece is coated with syrup.
4. Immediately pour the mixture into the parchment-lined baking pan. Spread flat with the spatula. Cover with another layer of parchment paper, then press firmly with a heavy object (e.g., a book) to create a level surface and compact interior.
5. Once the crunch is completely cooled and hardened, unmold and cut into uniform strips or pieces with a knife.
   **Note**: Cool at room temperature in winter; if the environment is too cold, keep it warm to prevent the syrup from setting too quickly, which would make shaping difficult.

## Health Advice
Black sesame crunch is rich in calcium and dietary fiber, suitable for those needing calcium and an energy boost. However, it is high in sugar and calories, so it is recommended to eat only 1–2 small pieces per serving to avoid overconsumption. The red dates and nuts provide natural sweetness and healthy fats, offering a satisfying chew.
