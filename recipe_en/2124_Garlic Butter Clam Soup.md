# Garlic Butter Clam Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐚 Clams, as needed  
🧅 Ginger, as needed (peeled and julienned)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (finely minced in large quantity)  
🌿 Scallions, as needed (chopped)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (can substitute with bird’s eye chili)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🍶 White liquor, a small amount (optional, to enhance flavor)  
🥛 Light cream, half a cup (can substitute with milk)  
🌿 White pepper powder, as needed (optional)  
🧈 Butter, as needed (optional)  
🧀 Cheese, as needed (optional)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan or soup pot  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥛 Measuring cup  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Soak the clams in salted water for about 30 minutes to allow them to fully expel sand.  
2. Peel and julienne the ginger, finely mince a generous amount of garlic, chop the scallions, and cut the dried chilies into sections. Set aside a small amount of scallion greens for garnish.  
3. Heat the pan over high heat and pour in a small amount of base oil.  
4. Add all the clams to the pan, then layer on the ginger julienne, minced garlic, dried chili sections, and a small amount of chopped scallions.  
   **Note**: Do not stir during this step; simply arrange the seasonings on top of the ingredients to simplify the process.  
5. Add water to the pan until it just covers all ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once the broth is bubbling and the clam shells gradually open, continue cooking for about 2 minutes after the clams open to ensure they are fully cooked. The entire process takes about 7–10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: After the clams open, continue cooking for about 2 minutes to ensure they are fully cooked.  
6. Stir the broth with a soup ladle and discard any clams that have not opened. Add salt to taste and cooking wine to remove any fishy smell. Optionally, add a small cup of white liquor to enhance the flavor.  
   **Note**: Be sure to remove unopened clams to avoid discomfort after consumption.  
7. Pour half a cup of light cream into the pan and stir well until the broth turns milky white. Then turn off the heat and serve.  
   **Remarks**: If you prefer a lighter taste, milk can be used instead of light cream.  

## Health Advice  
This soup is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. The combination of garlic and ginger helps warm the stomach and dispel cold, making it suitable for those with weak constitutions or who tend to feel cold. However, note that cream and cheese are high in calories; it is recommended to control portions. For those with high blood lipids, skim milk can be used as a substitute.
