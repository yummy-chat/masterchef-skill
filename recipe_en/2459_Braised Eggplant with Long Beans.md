# Braised Eggplant with Long Beans
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 Eggplant: appropriate amount (cut into strips)  
🌱 Long beans: appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧄 Minced garlic: appropriate amount (used in two batches)  
🥡 Cornstarch: appropriate amount (for coating eggplant and preparing sauce)  
🧂 Light soy sauce: 2 tbsp  
🧂 Dark soy sauce: 1 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce: 1 tbsp  
🧂 Salt: a pinch  
🧂 Chicken bouillon: a pinch  
🍬 Sugar: a pinch  
💧 Water: half a bowl  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Cut the eggplant into strips, add an appropriate amount of cornstarch, and mix well by hand so that each strip is evenly coated with a thin layer of cornstarch.
2.  Wash the long beans and cut them into sections. Mince the garlic and set aside.
3.  Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, 1 tbsp of cornstarch, and half a bowl of water. Stir until well combined.
4.  Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a pan over high heat. Add the eggplant strips and fry for about 2 minutes until the surface turns golden brown. Remove and set aside.
5.  Reheat oil in the same pan. Add the long bean sections, reduce the heat to medium-low, and fry for about 5 minutes until the surface becomes wrinkled and they are fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
6.  In another pan, heat oil over high heat. Add half of the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
7.  Add the fried eggplant strips and long bean sections. Pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce.
8.  Sprinkle the remaining minced garlic over the dish. Stir-fry rapidly for a few seconds, then remove from heat and plate.

## Health Tips  
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber, and long beans provide protein. However, the frying step increases calories, so it is recommended to pan-fry with minimal oil. Suitable for those who need to control blood sugar and blood lipids. Note that long beans must be fully cooked to prevent poisoning.
