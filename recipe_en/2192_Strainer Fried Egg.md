# Strainer Fried Egg
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 1 egg  
💧 Water (enough to create steam)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Metal strainer (medium density, capable of draining thin egg whites)  
🥣 Bowl (to catch drained thin egg whites)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🔪 Spatula  

## Instructions
1. Place the metal strainer over a bowl and set aside. Place the non-stick frying pan on the gas stove and preheat over the lowest heat.  
2. Crack the egg into the strainer and let it sit briefly, allowing the thin egg whites to drip naturally into the bowl. The strainer should retain the intact thick egg whites and yolk.  
3. Gently pour the remaining egg from the strainer into the center of the pan, keeping the yolk as intact as possible.  
4. If there are noticeable bubbles on the surface of the yolk, gently poke them with a toothpick to prevent expansion and puffing during frying.  
5. Use a soup spoon to pour a small amount of water along the edge of the pan, then immediately cover with a lid to steam and fry the egg.  
6. Once the edges of the egg white are mostly set, gently lift the bottom and edges with a spatula to prevent sticking.  
7. When the egg white is fully set and opaque white, while the yolk remains runny, turn off the heat. Carefully transfer to a plate and serve.  

## Health Tips
Removing thin egg whites with a strainer reduces fat and calories, making it suitable for those managing fat intake. Steaming and frying preserves the runny yolk, which is rich in vitamins and lecithin. Be sure to use low heat throughout to avoid overcooking the yolk.
