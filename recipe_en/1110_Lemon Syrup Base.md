# Lemon Syrup Base
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍬 White sugar 200g  
🍬 Rock sugar 200g (white or yellow rock sugar both acceptable)  
💧 Water 400g  
🍋 Lemon 1 slice (green lemon or yellow lemon, seeds removed)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍲 Soup pot  
🔥 Induction cooker  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🥅 Strainer  
🥣 Glass bowl (clean and dry)  
⚖️ Electronic scale

## Preparation Steps
1. Place white sugar, rock sugar, and water together in the soup pot; slice the lemon or squeeze the juice, making sure to remove all lemon seeds.
   **Note**: Lemon seeds must be completely removed, otherwise the syrup will become bitter.
2. Heat over high heat, stirring with a silicone spatula until the sugar is completely dissolved and the liquid becomes clear with no granules.
3. Stop stirring immediately once the liquid comes to a boil.
   **Key point**: Continuing to stir after boiling will cause the sugar syrup to crystallize and turn sandy.
4. Add the seedless lemon slices or squeezed lemon juice.
5. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 10 minutes, maintaining a gentle boil.
   **Note**: Avoid high heat to prevent the syrup from boiling over or becoming bitter.
6. Turn off the heat. At this point, the syrup will be watery in consistency, which is normal; it will naturally thicken as it cools.
7. Strain the cooked syrup through a strainer into a clean, dry glass bowl, seal, and store in the refrigerator.
   **Note**: The container must be free of water and oil; it can be refrigerated for about one week.

## Health Advice
This syrup is a high-sugar beverage base and can cause significant blood sugar fluctuations after consumption. It is only suitable for occasional use as a drink accent. Diabetic patients and those controlling sugar intake should avoid consumption.
