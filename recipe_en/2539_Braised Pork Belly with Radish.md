# Braised Pork Belly with Radish
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1 strip of pork belly (well-marbled with distinct layers, cut into pieces)  
🥬 1 green radish (peeled and cut into diagonal chunks)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
⭐ 2 star anise  
🌿 Codonopsis pilosula, to taste (optional)  
🧂 2 tablespoons salt  
🥄 1/2 bowl light soy sauce  
🥄 A splash of dark soy sauce  
🍶 Rice wine, to taste  
🍬 2 tablespoons sugar  
💧 Water, to taste (enough to submerge ingredients)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat a dry wok, add a moderate amount of oil, then add the pork belly pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface turns golden and the lard is rendered.
   **Key point**: The pork belly must be stir-fried thoroughly to render the lard, enhancing the aroma.
2. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, star anise, dried chili peppers, and codonopsis pilosula. Stir-fry until fragrant.
3. Pour in 1/2 bowl of light soy sauce and a splash of dark soy sauce for color. Add a moderate amount of rice wine to remove any gamey smell. Add water until the ingredients are submerged.
4. Add 2 tablespoons of salt and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Cover and braise over medium heat for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: The sugar is used to remove any gamey smell, cut through the richness, and add a subtle sweetness.
5. After 30 minutes, add the radish chunks. Continue to braise over medium heat for another 15 minutes, until the sauce thickens and the radish and pork belly are tender and fully flavored. Then remove from heat.
   **Key point**: Adding the radish later prevents overcooking and loss of texture. It should absorb the meat juices fully and become soft and tender.

## Health Advice  
Braised pork belly with radish is rich in protein and dietary fiber, but it has a high saturated fat content. It is suitable for those with high physical exertion or for nourishing the body in winter. People with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation; the rendered lard can be partially skimmed off during braising. Pairing it with radish aids digestion and cuts through the richness.
