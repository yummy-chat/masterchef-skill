# Salmon Cream Mushroom Soup
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Salmon fillet, to taste (cut into large chunks)
🍄 White mushrooms, to taste (sliced or halved)
🧅 1 leek (sliced into rings or minced)
🥕 Carrots, to taste (sliced)
🥛 150 ml heavy cream
🥛 150 ml milk
🍗 Chicken broth or water, to taste
🌾 2 tablespoons flour
🌿 Fresh dill, to taste (chopped, added at the end)
🧂 Salt, to taste (for marinating and seasoning)
⚫ Black pepper, to taste (for marinating and seasoning)
🧈 1 piece of butter (added when sautéing mushrooms)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Cast iron pot (good seal, used with induction cooktop for precise temperature control)
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥢 Tongs
🥄 Wooden spoon
🥛 Measuring cup
🔥 Induction cooktop

## Cooking Steps
1.  Marinate the salmon fillet with a little salt and black pepper for 20 minutes. After marinating, thoroughly pat dry any surface moisture with a paper towel, then cut into large chunks.
2.  Pour a little oil into the cast iron pot and preheat thoroughly until it just begins to smoke. Add the salmon chunks and pan-fry over medium heat until one side is golden brown and sizzling, then flip. Only sear the surface; it does not need to be fully cooked through. Remove and set aside.
3.  Using the residual oil in the pot, directly add the leek and carrot. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant. Then add the mushrooms and a piece of butter, and continue to stir-fry.
   **Note**: The oil and fond left in the pot are key to the soup's flavor; do not wash the pot.
4.  Add 2 tablespoons of flour to the sautéed vegetables and stir-fry thoroughly to remove the raw flour taste. Then pour in enough chicken broth or water, along with 150 ml heavy cream and 150 ml milk. Stir well.
   **Key Point**: Ensure the flour is cooked through to avoid a raw flour taste in the final dish.
5.  Once the soup comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes. Utilize the good seal of the cast iron pot and the precise temperature control of the induction cooktop to prevent scorching.
6.  After simmering, season the soup with salt and black pepper. Return the previously seared salmon chunks to the soup and cook briefly until the soup thickens.
   **Key Point**: Add the salmon at the very end to prevent it from becoming overcooked; the thickened soup coats the ingredients better.
7.  Just before serving, sprinkle in the chopped fresh dill and stir well. Dill pairs excellently with salmon, greatly enhancing the soup's flavor profile.

## Health Tips
Salmon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids. Combined with mushrooms and carrots, this soup is suitable for supplementing nutrition and boosting immunity during winter. Pay attention to controlling the amount of heavy cream to avoid excessive calories; add the salmon at the end to keep it tender and flaky.
