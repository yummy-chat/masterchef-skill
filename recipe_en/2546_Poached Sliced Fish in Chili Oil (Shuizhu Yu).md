# Poached Sliced Fish in Chili Oil (Shuizhu Yu)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (use fillets, reserve head and bones)
🌱 Bean sprouts appropriate amount
🥬 Celery appropriate amount
🥒 Cucumber appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic sprouts appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🥚 Egg 1 (use only egg white)
🧅 Chopped scallion appropriate amount (for garnish)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 White pepper powder appropriate amount
🍶 Yellow rice wine appropriate amount
🧂 Cornstarch about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Salad oil appropriate amount
🧂 Pixian chili bean paste appropriate amount
🧂 Fermented black beans appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili powder appropriate amount
🧂 Soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🧂 MSG appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount
🧂 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍲 Large soup bowl
🍳 Small wok or metal ladle (for heating oil)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Slaughter the grass carp, scrape off the slime, remove the blood and black membrane inside the belly, debone and take the two fillets, remove the rib bones, and slice the fish into large pieces using an angled knife. Reserve the head and bones for other uses.
2.  Add an ample amount of salt to the fish slices, rub and mix vigorously with your hands until sticky, then rinse with clean water three times until the water runs clear, and drain thoroughly.
    **Key Point**: The osmotic pressure of the salt thoroughly removes the surface slime and fishy smell.
3.  Coat the fish slices: Add salt, white pepper powder, yellow rice wine, and one egg white, mix well. Add one tablespoon of cornstarch and mix evenly. Finally, drizzle in an appropriate amount of salad oil and mix to seal the coating.
4.  Prepare the base vegetables: bean sprouts, celery sections, cucumber slices. Cut scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic sprout sections for stir-frying. Also cut dried chili pepper sections and chopped scallion for garnish.
5.  Heat oil in the wok, quickly stir-fry the bean sprouts, celery sections, and cucumber slices. Add a little salt and stir-fry until just cooked. Use a slotted spoon to remove and spread them at the bottom of the serving bowl.
6.  Heat more oil in the wok, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic sprout sections, stir-fry until fragrant. Add Pixian chili bean paste and stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant. Then add fermented black beans and chili powder, stir-fry until aromatic.
7.  Splash in yellow rice wine along the edge of the wok, add enough boiling water, bring to a boil over high heat, and let it simmer to extract the flavors.
8.  Use a slotted spoon to remove the solid residues from the soup. Season the soup with soy sauce, chicken bouillon, MSG, and white pepper powder. Return the previously stir-fried base vegetables to the soup and cook briefly to absorb the flavors, then remove them again and place them at the bottom of the bowl.
9.  Reduce the heat to low, keeping the soup at a near-boiling state. Gently add the coated fish slices one by one into the pot.
    **Key Point**: Do not stir the fish slices after adding them to prevent the coating from coming off and the soup from becoming cloudy.
10. Keep the heat low and cook until the fish slices are set, then turn to medium heat and bring the soup to a boil. Gently push the fish slices with the back of a ladle to prevent sticking. Cook until the fish slices turn white and curl, indicating they are done.
11. Pour the cooked fish slices and soup into the large bowl with the vegetables at the bottom. Sprinkle dried chili pepper sections and Sichuan peppercorns on top.
12. Heat oil in another wok until it smokes, then quickly pour the hot oil over the dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns to release their aroma. Finally, garnish with chopped scallion and serve.

## Health Advice
This Poached Sliced Fish in Chili Oil is rich in high protein and low in fat, making it suitable for muscle gain or weight loss. However, it has relatively high oil and salt content, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or brown rice for a balanced diet. Those with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs should control the spiciness. Using less oil when coating the fish slices makes for a healthier version.
