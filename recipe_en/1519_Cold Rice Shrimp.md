# Cold Rice Shrimp
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April - August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice flour 50g  
🌽 Corn flour 30g  
🥣 Pea starch 150g  
💧 Water (for slurry) 300ml  
💧 Water (for cooking) 1200ml  
🧊 Ice water (for shaping the cold rice shrimp) as needed  
🍯 Brown sugar as needed  
🍬 Rock sugar as needed  
💧 Water (for syrup) a little  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🪵 Rolling pin (or wooden stick)  
🧇 Stainless steel steamer rack or slotted spoon  
🪣 Large basin  

## Preparation Steps
1. Take a clean pot, add brown sugar lumps, rock sugar, and a little water. Simmer over medium-low heat, stirring constantly. Turn off the heat when the syrup forms dense small bubbles. Pour it out and let it cool for later use.  
2. In a large bowl, sequentially add rice flour, corn flour, pea starch, and 300ml water. Stir thoroughly until smooth and free of lumps.  
3. Pour 1200ml water into a pot and heat until just before boiling. Add the prepared slurry, then immediately reduce to medium-low heat. Use a rolling pin or wooden stick to stir quickly and continuously in one direction to prevent sticking. Cook until the slurry thickens, clings to the stick, and large bubbles form, then turn off the heat.  
4. Prepare ice water in a large basin and set a steamer rack or slotted spoon over it. While hot, scoop the cooked slurry and let it drip through the holes into the ice water, instantly solidifying into "small shrimp" shapes.  
5. Once the cold rice shrimp are fully set, remove them and drain the water. Place them in a bowl, pour the cooled brown sugar syrup over them, and optionally sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds.  

## Health Advice
This cold rice shrimp uses rice flour, corn flour, and pea starch as ingredients, providing carbohydrates and dietary fiber. It helps cool and relieve summer heat, making it suitable for hot weather. However, individuals with diabetes should control the amount of brown sugar used.
