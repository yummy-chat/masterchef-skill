# Charcoal-Grilled Sturgeon
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Sturgeon 1 (several catties, farmed)  
🍋 Lemon 1 (sliced)  
🍎 Apple 1 (sliced)  
🧅 Onion 1 (shredded, part for marinating, part for the base)  
🌿 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections or shreds)  
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (part for marinating, part for garnish)  
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (crushed)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed)  
🥬 Bean sprouts appropriate amount (for the base)  
🧈 Tofu skin appropriate amount (for the base)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount  
🧂 MSG appropriate amount  
🌶️ Ground white pepper appropriate amount  
🍶 Huangjiu (Chinese rice wine) appropriate amount  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn appropriate amount (part for marinating, part for stir-frying the base)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (part for marinating, part for stir-frying the base)  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Soy sauce appropriate amount  
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base)  
🌶️ Spicy chili sauce appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base)  
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large basin (for washing and marinating)  
🔗 Double-layer wire mesh grilling rack and wire  
♨️ Grill (charcoal)  
🍳 Wok, long-handled spatula  
🍽️ Large stainless steel tray  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Clean and gut the sturgeon, scrape off the scales on both sides of the backbone. Cut along the backbone to split the fish body into two connected halves, and chop through the head so the fish body lies flat.
2.  Slice the lemon and apple, shred the onion, and crush the ginger and garlic. Place the prepared fish in a large basin and add the lemon slices, apple slices, shredded onion, scallion sections, cilantro, crushed ginger and garlic. Then add salt, chicken bouillon, MSG, ground white pepper, huangjiu, Sichuan peppercorn, dried chili peppers, oyster sauce, and soy sauce. Rub the mixture thoroughly and evenly all over the fish. Marinate for 3 hours.
**Note**: Ensure the mixture is applied evenly so the fish is fully flavored.

3.  Spread shredded onion, tofu skin, and bean sprouts on the bottom of a large stainless steel tray to serve as the base vegetables.
4.  Light the charcoal. Place the marinated sturgeon into the double-layer wire mesh grilling rack, secure it with wire, and grill over low charcoal heat for about 1 hour, until the fish is cooked through and the surface is fragrant and charred.
**Key Point**: Must grill slowly over low heat to prevent burning the outside while leaving the inside raw; turn the fish over periodically during grilling for even heating.

5.  Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Stir-fry scallion, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add doubanjiang and stir-fry until the oil turns red. Then add spicy chili sauce, pickled chili peppers, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry until the aroma is rich.
6.  Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok. Season with salt, chicken bouillon, MSG, and ground white pepper. Simmer over medium heat for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to fully meld, creating a spicy base sauce.
7.  Remove the grilled sturgeon and place it flat on the tray with the prepared base vegetables. While still hot, evenly pour the boiling hot spicy base sauce along with the red oil over the fish and vegetables. Finally, garnish with cilantro and serve.
**Note**: The sauce must be poured while piping hot to maximize the release of its aroma.

## Health Advice  
This charcoal-grilled sturgeon is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, making it suitable for those needing to support brain function and joint health. However, the marinating and base sauce contain high sodium levels, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Grilling with a base of fruits and vegetables increases dietary fiber, helping to balance the greasiness. Ensure to grill slowly over low heat to guarantee the fish is fully cooked.
