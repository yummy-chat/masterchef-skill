# Pan-Fried Oysters with Eggs
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 5 eggs (approx.)
🦪 Oysters (appropriate amount, rinsed and drained in advance)
🧅 Chopped green onions (appropriate amount)
🧂 A pinch of white pepper
🧂 A splash of soy sauce
🧂 Salt to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🥢 Chopsticks
🍴 Spatula (slotted type)
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1.  Crack the eggs into a large bowl. Add the chopped green onions, white pepper, soy sauce, and salt. Whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until evenly combined, creating the seasoned egg mixture.
2.  Place the wok over high heat until hot. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil and swirl to coat the sides. Pour out the hot oil, completing the "lubricating the wok" step to prevent sticking.
3.  Add a small amount of cold oil to the wok. Pour in about half of the seasoned egg mixture. Quickly stir-fry with a spatula until the eggs are mostly set but still tender and silky (half-cooked). Transfer this partially cooked egg back into the bowl with the remaining raw egg mixture. This is called "raw-cooked egg" (生熟蛋).
4.  Add the pre-washed and drained oysters to the bowl. Quickly and evenly mix them together with the half-cooked egg and the raw egg mixture. Note: Oysters release moisture easily; work immediately after mixing to prevent the egg mixture from thinning.
5.  Add another drizzle of cooking oil to the same wok. Pour in all the combined mixture. Use chopsticks to help spread the oysters and egg mixture evenly. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook until the bottom begins to set.
6.  Once the bottom is set and the egg cake can slide freely in the wok, drizzle a little oil along the edge, then flip it. Flipping method: Either toss the wok to flip the omelette, or cover the wok with a large plate, quickly invert to release the omelette onto the plate, then slide it back into the wok to cook the other side.
7.  Continue cooking the other side until both sides are golden brown and the edges are lightly crispy. Remove from the wok and serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Oysters are an excellent source of zinc. Pairing them with eggs enhances protein absorption, making this dish ideal for those looking to boost immunity and vitality. Ensure the oysters are thoroughly drained to prevent the egg mixture from becoming watery and affecting the texture.
