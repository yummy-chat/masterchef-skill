# Twice-Cooked Pulling Potatoes
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine (Shandong Cuisine)
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 2
🍬 White Sugar 120g
🌰 Sesame Seeds 5g
🫒 Cooking Oil 300ml
💧 Water 100ml

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Large Bowl
🌾 Cornstarch 30g

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into evenly sized chunks.
2. Place the potato chunks in the dry cornstarch and toss thoroughly to coat the surface evenly.
3. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat to 170°C / 340°F. Add the coated potato chunks. Reduce the heat to low and fry slowly for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until a chopstick can easily pierce through, indicating they are cooked through.
**Key Point**: Do not fry the potatoes for too long; overcooking will make them difficult to shape when pulling the sugar, affecting the appearance.

4. Remove the fried potatoes, drain the oil, and place them in a large bowl for later use.
5. Add water and white sugar to the wok. Cook over low heat, slowly stirring in one direction, until the sugar is completely dissolved and the syrup turns amber.
**Key Point**: Use sufficient sugar to create a thick syrup coating on the potato surface, which is essential for the pulling effect.

6. Return the fried potato chunks to the wok. Quickly stir-fry for about 30 seconds to coat the potatoes evenly with the syrup, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.
**Key Point**: Stir-fry quickly when returning the potatoes to the wok to prevent the syrup from cooling and solidifying.

7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve hot.

## Health Advice
Potatoes are rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C. However, due to the frying and sugar coating, this dish is high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy occasionally as a snack, and those managing sugar intake should consume sparingly.
