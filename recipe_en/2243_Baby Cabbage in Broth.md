# Baby Cabbage in Broth
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## 🥬 Essential Ingredients
- Baby cabbage
- Century egg
- Luncheon meat
- Scallion
- Ginger
- Garlic
- Enoki mushrooms

## 🧂 Seasonings
- Salt
- Sugar
- Oyster sauce
- MSG
- Cornstarch

## 🍳 Cookware
- Wok
- Pot
- Plate

## 📝 Preparation Steps
1. Wash the baby cabbage and cut it lengthwise into sections.
2. Cut scallion into small pieces, slice garlic and ginger, about 3g, 10g, and 10g respectively.
3. Dice the century egg and cut the luncheon meat into cubes about 1 cm in size.
4. Wash and separate the enoki mushrooms.
5. Bring water to a boil in a pot, blanch the baby cabbage for 10 seconds, then remove and drain.
6. Heat the wok, add a small amount of oil to coat the surface, then pour it out, leaving just a thin layer of base oil.
7. Reduce to low heat, add scallion, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.
8. Add about 300 grams of water (enough to just cover the baby cabbage), then add the baby cabbage, enoki mushrooms, and luncheon meat.
9. Season with oyster sauce, sugar, salt, and MSG. Bring to a boil over high heat.
10. After cooking for 3 minutes, first remove the baby cabbage and arrange it on a plate. Sprinkle the diced century egg over the baby cabbage, then pour the broth from the pot on top.

⚠️ **Notes**
- The blanching time for baby cabbage should not exceed 10 seconds to maintain a crisp texture.
- Century egg can be added or omitted based on personal preference.
- Enoki mushrooms are optional and can be included or excluded according to taste.
- The amount of water should just cover the baby cabbage; adjust seasoning to achieve a balanced saltiness.

## Health Advice
This dish is based on baby cabbage, paired with century egg and luncheon meat, offering a balanced combination of vegetables and meat. The unique flavor of century egg enhances the freshness. It is suitable for those looking to try vegetables in a different way, but note that century egg and luncheon meat are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation.
