# Cloud Cupcakes
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 4 eggs (separate yolks from whites, whites must be free of oil and water)  
🌾 60g cake flour (sifted)  
🍬 30g granulated sugar (for whipping egg whites)  
🍬 10g granulated sugar (for making egg yolk batter)  
🌽 35g corn oil  
🥛 40g milk  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥣 2 mixing bowls  
🥄 Manual whisk  
🍴 Spatula  
🔥 Oven  
🧁 Baking tray and cupcake liners  
🎂 Piping bag  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🛡️ Sealing machine (for airtight storage)  

## Instructions  
1. Separate the egg whites and yolks into two clean, oil-free, and water-free bowls. Ensure no yolk or grease gets into the egg whites; refrigerating the eggs makes separation easier.  
2. Add 30g granulated sugar to the egg whites. Beat with an electric hand mixer on high speed until lines appear, then switch to medium-low speed and beat until stiff peaks form.  
3. Add 10g granulated sugar to the egg yolks. Beat with an electric hand mixer until the color lightens, volume increases, and the mixture becomes thick, with ribbons that do not disappear immediately when drizzled.  
4. Add 35g corn oil and 40g milk to the egg yolk mixture. Stir with a manual whisk until fully emulsified.  
5. Sift 60g cake flour into the egg yolk mixture. Stir with a manual whisk until no dry flour remains and the batter is smooth, thick, and forms ribbons when drizzled.  
6. Take about 1/3 of the meringue and add it to the egg yolk batter. Quickly fold together using a spatula.  
7. Pour the mixed batter back into the remaining meringue. Gently and quickly fold using a cutting and turning motion to avoid deflating the batter.  
8. Transfer the cake batter into a piping bag. Pipe into cupcake liners until about 80% full. Gently tap the tray to level the surface.  
9. Preheat the oven. Bake at 110°C for 40 minutes, then increase the temperature to 130°C and bake for about 20 minutes until the tops turn golden brown. Starting at a low temperature and then increasing prevents cracking and shrinking.  
10. Remove the baking tray and gently tap out the hot air. Place the cupcakes upright on a wire rack to cool. Once cooled, store in an airtight bag. After resting overnight, the texture becomes softer as the oils redistribute.  

## Health Advice  
These cloud cupcakes are primarily carbohydrate-based with moderate fat, making them suitable as an afternoon tea treat. However, those managing blood sugar levels should consume in moderation. Ensure the bowl for whipping egg whites is completely free of oil and water, or the cake will fail.
