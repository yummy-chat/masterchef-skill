# Lemon Iced Black Tea
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍋 Lemon: About 4 (scrubbed with salt, sliced, and seeds removed)  
🍂 Black tea: About half a tael (Jin Jun Mei or regular black tea)  
💧 Water: 1.5 liters  
🧂 Salt: 2 tablespoons (for scrubbing lemons)  
🍬 White sugar: 15 grams (for stir-frying tea)  
🧂 Salt: A pinch (to sprinkle on lemon slices)  
🧊 Rock sugar: 50 grams (adjust sweetness to taste)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🍲 Glass pot (or heat-resistant pot)  
🥄 Red silicone spatula  
🥄 Metal slotted spoon  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
📦 Plastic wrap  
❄️ Refrigerator  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Use 2 tablespoons of salt to scrub the lemon peels, then rinse clean with water.  
   **Key Point**: Use the friction of salt to remove dirt and impurities from the surface.  
2. Slice the lemons into rounds about 3 mm thick, and carefully remove all seeds.  
   **Key Point**: Seeds must be removed, otherwise the tea will become bitter.  
3. Sprinkle a pinch of salt on each lemon slice and let them marinate briefly.  
   **Key Point**: This step enhances freshness and sweetness.  
4. Place the glass pot over low heat, add the black tea leaves and 15 grams of white sugar, and stir-fry continuously until the sugar melts, the tea leaves become moist, and a caramel-like aroma is released.  
   **Doneness Check**: Sugar granules disappear, and the room fills with caramel fragrance.  
5. Quickly pour 1.5 liters of water into the pot, turn the heat to high, bring to a boil, then immediately turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Adding water produces a large amount of steam—be careful to avoid burns.  
6. After turning off the heat, add 50 grams of rock sugar and stir until completely dissolved. Adjust sweetness to taste.  
7. Use a fine mesh strainer to completely remove the tea leaves, leaving only the clear tea liquid.  
   **Key Point**: Do not steep the tea leaves for too long, otherwise the tea flavor will become too strong and bitter.  
8. Let the tea liquid cool until it is no longer hot to the touch.  
   **Key Point**: High temperature will destroy the fresh aroma of the lemon.  
9. Place the salted lemon slices into the cooled tea liquid and gently mix well.  
10. Seal the container with plastic wrap and refrigerate until fully chilled and infused.  
    **Key Point**: Refrigeration allows the lemon flavor to meld with the tea liquid, resulting in a better taste.  

## Health Tips  
Lemons are rich in vitamin C but are highly acidic; those with excessive stomach acid should drink in moderation. Adding sugar when stir-frying the tea enhances the aroma; those controlling sugar intake may reduce the amount or use sugar substitutes. Chilling improves the flavor, but the drink should not be stored for more than two days to avoid bitterness.
