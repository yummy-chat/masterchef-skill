# Hong Kong-Style Curry Beef Brisket Casserole 🥘
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket 500g (cut into chunks, can substitute with beef shank or topside)
🧅 Onion half (about 100-150g, shredded)
🍅 Tomato 1 (about 100-150g, cut into small pieces)
🥔 Potato half (about 100-150g, cut into chunks)
🫚 Ginger 3-5 slices (cut into large slices)
🧅 Scallions适量 (cut into sections, for blanching)
⭐ Star anise half (optional)
🍃 Bay leaf 1 (optional)
🥚 Eggs适量 (for making scrambled eggs, to serve with rice)
🥬 Baby bok choy适量 (blanched, to serve with rice)
🍶 Shaoxing wine/Huadiao wine/cooking wine适量 (for blanching + 1 tbsp for stir-frying)
🫘 Zhuhou sauce 1 tbsp
🥜 Satay sauce 1 tbsp
🥜 Peanut butter ½ tbsp
🥡 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🥡 Dark soy sauce ½ tbsp (optional, for color)
🍬 Rock sugar 10g (or 2 tsp white sugar)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Non-stick pan/wok
♨️ Pressure cooker/electric pressure cooker
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥡 Airtight container (for portioning)

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef brisket into pieces about 3-4 cm square. Add cold water to a pot, put in the beef brisket, scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Heat over high heat until boiling, skim off the foam, remove the beef, and rinse with hot water. Drain and set aside.
**Key point**: Blanching removes blood and gamey smell. Avoid rinsing with cold water to prevent the meat from contracting.

2. Prepare the side ingredients: shred the onion and separate the layers, cut the tomato into small pieces, cut the potato into chunks, and slice the ginger into large pieces. Beat the eggs and set aside. Wash the baby bok choy and set aside.
3. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the pot. Heat over medium heat, add the beef brisket pieces, and pan-fry until the surface is golden brown. If you are sensitive to gamey smells, you can drizzle a tablespoon of cooking wine along the side of the pot.
**Key point**: Using a non-stick pan can reduce the amount of oil needed, as the beef brisket will release its own fat.

4. Add the ginger slices and shredded onion, stir-fry until the onion softens and becomes fragrant.
5. Add the tomato pieces, then add the zhuhou sauce, satay sauce, peanut butter, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry quickly until well combined and the sauce is fragrant.
6. Add the star anise, bay leaf, and rock sugar. Pour in hot water until it reaches the level of the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Simmer in a regular pot for 1.5-2 hours; or in a pressure cooker for 17-20 minutes after it comes to pressure.
7. After stewing, remove the ginger slices, bay leaf, and other solids. Add the potato chunks, bring to a boil again, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 15 minutes, until the potatoes are soft and flavorful.
8. Place a bowl of rice as the base, top with scrambled eggs and blanched baby bok choy, and generously ladle the curry beef brisket over the top. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Beef brisket is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for nourishing the blood and Qi. It is recommended to pair it with potatoes and greens to increase dietary fiber. Diabetics or those on a weight loss plan should control their rice intake. The gravy is rich and has a high salt and sauce content, so people with high blood pressure should limit their consumption of the broth.
