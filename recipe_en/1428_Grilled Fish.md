# Grilled Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 **Ingredients**
- Grass carp (about 3 jin / 1.5 kg)
- Tofu skin sheets (qianzhang)
- Mung bean sprouts
- Onion
- Celery, cut into segments
- Roasted peanuts
- Cilantro (optional)

🧂 **Seasonings**
- Cooking wine
- Ground white pepper
- Salt
- Hot pot base (adjust to taste)
- Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste)
- Sugar
- Light soy sauce
- Scallions
- Garlic
- Cinnamon stick
- Star anise
- Bay leaves
- Sichuan green peppercorns
- Dried chili segments
- Lantern chilies
- White sesame seeds
- Cumin powder

🍳 **Cookware**
- Oven or wok
- Baking sheet
- Brush
- Wok

## Steps
1. Split the grass carp from the back and make several cuts along the spine on both sides of the fish, being careful not to cut through the belly skin. Pour hot water over the fish to wash off the surface slime, or scrub thoroughly under running water with a brush until no stickiness remains.
**Key point**: Avoid cutting the belly skin to prevent the fish from falling apart when frying or grilling.

2. Place the fish in a container, add cooking wine, 10 g white pepper, and 5 g salt, rub evenly, and marinate for 20 minutes to let the flavors infuse.
3. Cut half a scallion into chunks, halve the garlic cloves lengthwise, and place them together with star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick in a container for later use.
4. Cut the dried chili segments in half and keep them together with the lantern chilies in one container.
5. Cut the celery into small segments.
6. Blanch the mung bean sprouts.
7. Blanch the tofu skin sheets, then cut them into shreds.
8. Cut the onion into shreds.
9. If using an oven, brush the baking sheet with a thin layer of oil, place the fish skin-side down, grill until both sides are golden, then sprinkle with cumin powder. If no oven is available, heat the wok with oil, sprinkle 2 g salt along the edge, add the grass carp, do not move it immediately, wait until one side is set before flipping, fry until both sides are golden, then sprinkle with cumin powder, remove, and set aside on a plate.
**Key point**: Wait for one side to set before flipping to prevent the fish skin from breaking.

10. Pour 20 ml cooking oil into the wok, heat to about 180°C, add the scallion chunks, garlic cloves, star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves, and stir-fry until fragrant.
11. Add half a packet of hot pot base and 15–20 g doubanjiang, stir-fry over low heat until the red oil releases.
12. Add 5 g sugar, 10 g salt, and 5 ml light soy sauce, then add enough water to just cover the ingredients and bring to a boil.
**Key point**: The hot pot base and doubanjiang already contain salt, so adjust total salt carefully.

13. Add the celery segments, blanched mung bean sprouts, and shredded tofu skin sheets in order, blanch briefly, then spread the onion shreds on top, and place the grilled fish on top of everything.
14. Sprinkle the dried chili segments, lantern chilies, and Sichuan green peppercorns over the fish.
15. Heat oil in another pot until it smokes, then pour it sizzling over the just-added chilies and peppercorns to release their aroma.
16. Finally, sprinkle with roasted peanuts, chopped scallions, white sesame seeds, and cilantro.
17. Let the whole pot continue to cook for 5–6 minutes until the sauce thickens, then serve directly from the pot.

## Health Tips
Fish provides high-quality protein, pairing it with bean sprouts and tofu skin sheets adds dietary fiber, making it suitable for moderate consumption during weight loss. However, the hot pot base and doubanjiang are high in salt and oil, so it is recommended to reduce dipping sauce usage and avoid drinking the broth as much as possible to keep sodium intake in check.
