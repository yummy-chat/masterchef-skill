# Steamed Tofu with Squid
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦑 Squid 3 pieces (gutted, cartilage removed, washed and cut into rings)
🧈 Silken tofu 1 piece (about 300-400 grams, sliced into 1 cm thick pieces)
🧂 Salt to taste (a pinch)
🧅 Scallions 2 stalks (half cut into long sections, half thinly shredded)
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices (half sliced, half thinly shredded)
🌶️ Red bell pepper to taste (thinly shredded)
🍾 Steamed fish soy sauce 2-3 tablespoons (or light soy sauce)
🫒 Cooking oil 1-2 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍽️ Steaming plate
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack)
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1.  Arrange tofu slices evenly on the bottom of the plate, sprinkle with a little salt for flavor, then lay the squid rings flat on top of the tofu.
2.  Place scallion sections and ginger slices on the surface of the squid to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma.
3.  Fill the steamer with water and bring to a boil over high heat. Once the water is boiling and steaming, place the plate in the steamer and steam over high heat for 5 minutes.
    **Note**: Putting it in after the water boils shortens steaming time and locks in tenderness; steaming for too long makes the squid tough and the tofu lose water.
4.  Remove the steaming plate, immediately discard the used scallion sections and ginger slices, and evenly pour the steamed fish soy sauce along the edge of the plate.
5.  Pile the shredded scallions, ginger, and red bell pepper in the center of the dish.
6.  Heat oil in a separate pan until about 60% hot, then quickly pour the hot oil over the "three shreds" until you hear a sizzling sound.
    **Key**: The oil must be hot enough, and the oil should be poured evenly over the three shreds to instantly release the aroma.

## Health Tips
Tofu and squid form a high-quality protein combination. Steaming for 5 minutes locks in tenderness while retaining the soft texture of the tofu, making it suitable for those on a low-fat, high-protein weight management diet. Note that salting the tofu first prevents excess water from diluting the flavor after steaming.
