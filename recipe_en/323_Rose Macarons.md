# Rose Macarons
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌰 Almond flour 47.5g (sifted)  
🍬 Powdered sugar 42.5g (mixed and sifted with almond flour)  
🥚 Egg whites 40g (for whipping)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 37.5g (added to egg whites in batches)  
🎨 Pink food coloring, as needed (paste or gel preferred)  
🧀 Softened cream cheese 50g (for filling)  
🥛 Milk powder 5g (added to filling)  
🥛 Whipping cream 25g (added to filling)  
🌹 Rose jam 15g (added to filling)  
🌹 Edible dried rose petals, as needed (for decorating the sides)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥣 Mixing bowl (stainless steel/glass)  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🧁 Flour sifter  
👝 Piping bag and round piping tip  
🔥 Oven  
📜 Baking sheet with parchment paper/silicone mat  
📦 Airtight container  

## Instructions  
1. Take the cream cheese out in advance to soften. Mix almond flour with powdered sugar and sift 1–2 times to ensure the dry ingredients are fine and free of lumps.  
2. Place egg whites in a mixing bowl. Use an electric hand mixer to whip them, gradually adding the granulated sugar in batches. Whip until stiff peaks form, adding pink food coloring during this stage to tint the mixture.  
   - **Check for doneness**: The meringue should be glossy, stable, and at stiff peaks.  
   - **Key Point**: Adding the coloring while whipping helps monitor the batter’s consistency later.  
3. Add the sifted dry ingredients all at once to the meringue. Use a spatula to fold and mix gently and quickly, using a cutting and folding motion until the batter is smooth, glossy, and flows in a continuous ribbon from the spatula with a pattern that doesn’t immediately disappear.  
   - **Key Point**: Avoid overmixing, as this can deflate the batter, affecting the formation of the "feet."  
4. Transfer the batter to a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Pipe evenly sized, round mounds vertically onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone mat.  
5. Let the baking sheet sit at room temperature to dry until the surface is no longer sticky to the touch and forms a thin shell.  
   - **Check for doneness**: The surface should be dry and non-sticky.  
   - **Key Point**: Drying thoroughly is essential; otherwise, the macarons may crack or lack feet during baking.  
6. Preheat the oven. Place the baking sheet on the middle rack and bake at 145°C (293°F) for about 16 minutes.  
   - **Heat control**: Adjust time and temperature slightly based on your own oven.  
7. Make the rose cream cheese filling: Place the softened cream cheese, milk powder, whipping cream, and rose jam in a mixing bowl. Use an electric hand mixer to beat until smooth and well combined.  
   - **Key Point**: All ingredients can be added and whipped together at once.  
8. Once the baked macaron shells have cooled completely, pair them by similar size. Pipe an appropriate amount of filling onto one shell, top with another shell, and press gently. Then, roll the edges in a plate of edible dried rose petals to coat.  
9. Place the assembled macarons in an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.  
   - **Key Point**: This "maturation" step in the fridge allows the texture to transition from crisp on the outside and soft inside to uniformly moist and cohesive, with enhanced flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This recipe for rose macarons is based primarily on almond flour and egg whites, but it is high in sugar. Enjoy it occasionally as an afternoon tea treat, and avoid overindulgence. Pair with unsweetened tea or coffee to balance the sweetness. Diabetics or those controlling sugar intake should approach with caution.
