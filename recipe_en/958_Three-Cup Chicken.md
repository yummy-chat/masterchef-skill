# Three-Cup Chicken
Cuisine: Taiwan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs 2 (choose fresh thighs with bright red color and undried skin, cut into large pieces)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (cut into thick rolling knife pieces)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (trim ends, cut in half)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into long sections, substitute for Thai basil)
🌿 Thai basil (or garlic sprouts) appropriate amount (best added when removing from heat)
🥄 White pepper powder a pinch
🧂 Salt 2 grams
🍶 Yellow rice wine a small amount (for marinating) + about 1 cup (for braising, **must use yellow rice wine, not cooking wine**)
🥄 Light soy sauce about half a tablespoon (for marinating) + about 1 cup (for braising)
🥄 Dark soy sauce about half a tablespoon (for marinating)
🌾 Cornstarch 1 teaspoon
🫗 Sesame oil (small mill sesame oil) about 1 cup (for braising) + a small amount (for sealing oil in marinade)
🍬 White sugar or rock sugar appropriate amount (optional, add to light soy sauce to neutralize saltiness and enhance freshness)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Slotted spoon, chopsticks
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍲 Glass lid

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse chicken thighs, then chop into bite-sized pieces.
**Key point**: Pieces should be large to better lock in moisture and fat.

2. Rinse chicken pieces repeatedly with clean water to remove blood, then drain and thoroughly pat dry all moisture on the surface and in crevices with kitchen paper towels.
**Key point**: **Moisture must be completely dried**; this is key to successful raw stir-frying, preventing liquid release, and maintaining tenderness.

3. To the dried chicken pieces, add white pepper powder, 2 grams salt, a small amount of yellow rice wine, half a tablespoon light soy sauce, and half a tablespoon dark soy sauce. Mix well. Add 1 teaspoon cornstarch and mix well. Finally, drizzle in a small amount of sesame oil and mix well. Let marinate.
**Note**: Do not add too much liquid such as yellow rice wine and light soy sauce to avoid releasing water during frying; starch and sealing oil help lock in moisture and prevent sticking.

4. Cut ginger into thick rolling knife pieces. Trim garlic ends and cut in half. Cut scallions into long sections.
**Note**: None.

5. Pour sesame oil into a cold pan. Heat over medium-low heat. First, add ginger pieces and stir-fry until edges curl and color. Then add garlic cloves and stir-fry until golden and fragrant. Remove the aromatic ginger, garlic, and seasoned oil and set aside.
**Key point**: Ginger is more resistant to stir-frying than garlic, so add them in order; the seasoned oil is the core of the flavor.

6. Heat the non-stick pan over high heat until it is visibly smoking. Then **turn off the heat**, quickly add the marinated chicken pieces, using residual heat to prevent sticking. Once all chicken is in, turn the heat back to **medium**. Pan-fry until both sides are golden and crispy.
**Key point**: Pan-fry slowly over medium heat to avoid burning the outside while raw inside; let one side set before flipping, do not stir frequently.

7. After the chicken is pan-fried, return the reserved ginger, garlic, and seasoned oil. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the pan and stir-fry until an aromatic soy sauce smell emerges. Then pour in enough yellow rice wine, cover with the lid, and braise over medium heat for 3–5 minutes.
**Note**: The order of seasoning is seasoned oil → light soy sauce → yellow rice wine; the sauce contains collagen, so watch during cooking to prevent scorching.

8. Braise until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken. Add scallion sections and quickly stir-fry evenly, then remove from heat and serve.
**Key point**: Add the green ingredients when the sauce is mostly reduced, using residual heat to cook them just through, preserving color and aroma.

## Health Advice
Three-cup chicken is slowly braised with sesame oil, rice wine, and soy sauce, providing high-quality protein and iron. However, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables and control rice portions. Suitable for those needing to replenish energy; those with high blood lipids should consume sparingly.
