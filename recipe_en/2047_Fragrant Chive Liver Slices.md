# Fragrant Chive Liver Slices
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season dish

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork liver 1 piece (about 250-300g, fresh, sliced into willow-leaf thin slices)
🥬 Chinese chives 1 bunch (chive white cut into short segments, chive leaves chopped into small pieces)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🥒 Green asparagus lettuce appropriate amount (sliced or cut into strips, as base vegetable)
🌱 Bean sprouts appropriate amount (soybean sprouts, as base vegetable)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (smashed, for removing fishy smell and making sauce)
🌿 Cilantro 3-4 stalks (for making sauce, then removed)
🧅 Scallion/spring onion appropriate amount (for initial marination and making sauce)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (added in batches)
🌫️ White pepper powder appropriate amount (added in batches)
🍺 Beer about 2-3 tablespoons (for initial marination to remove fishy smell, can be substituted with cooking wine or high-proof liquor)
🟤 Dark soy sauce a little (for marination, adding color and flavor)
🌾 Sweet potato starch appropriate amount (dry starch, for coating)
🫒 Cooked rapeseed oil about 1 tablespoon for sealing oil + about 1 tablespoon for drizzling (must use thoroughly cooked rapeseed oil)
🧂 MSG appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🐟 Steamed fish soy sauce half tablespoon
🍬 Sugar a little (to enhance freshness and balance flavors)
🌶️ Chili seasoning sauce a little (optional)
💧 Water starch appropriate amount (for thickening)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🍳 Large wok/frying pan
🥄 Wok spatula/soup ladle
🥢 Chopsticks
🔥 Induction cooker/gas stove

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. Slice the top of the pork liver diagonally into willow-leaf-shaped thin slices.
**Key Point**: The liver should be sliced thinly, but not too thin to avoid becoming tough.

2. Place the sliced liver in a bowl. Add smashed ginger, scallion segments, salt, white pepper powder, and beer. Mix well and marinate for 10 minutes. Then remove the ginger and scallion. Rinse repeatedly under running water until the blood is washed away. Finally, squeeze out the water forcefully.
**Key Point**: This step is crucial for removing the gamey smell of the pork liver. Rinsing and squeezing out the water makes the liver cleaner and fresher. This method also applies to other animal innards.

3. To the squeezed-dry liver, add salt, white pepper powder, and a little dark soy sauce again. Mix well. Add sweet potato starch and stir in one direction until the starch is completely dissolved and evenly coats the liver. Finally, drizzle in one tablespoon of cooked rapeseed oil and mix well.
**Key Point**: Use dry starch; stir vigorously until the coating adheres well; the oil seal locks in moisture and makes the liver shine when cooked.

4. Cut chive white into short segments, chive leaves into small pieces; slice bird's eye chili into rings; slice or cut green asparagus lettuce; wash bean sprouts.
**Key Point**: Handle chive white and leaves separately. The leaf part is sprinkled raw on the final dish.

5. Boil water in a pot. Add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, and a little cooked rapeseed oil for seasoning. Once boiling, add the asparagus lettuce slices and bean sprouts. Blanch for about 1 minute until just cooked through. Drain and spread them at the bottom of a serving bowl.
**Key Point**: Adding oil and seasoning during blanching gives the base vegetables flavor and keeps them vibrant green. Do not blanch too long; keep them crisp.

### Cooking Stage
1. Bring water in a pot to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Break up the marinated liver slices again and drop them into the water one by one, spreading them out. Do not stir immediately after adding. Wait until the starch coating sets, then gently separate them with chopsticks. Cook until the liver is fully变色 (changed color), floats, and firms up. Then drain. Maintain a "gentle simmer" throughout, do not let it boil vigorously. This takes about 1-2 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: The liver is fully变色, holds its shape, and floats on the surface.
   **Key Point**: This is the critical step for tender liver. High water temperature or vigorous boiling will cause the coating to fall off and the liver to become tough.

2. In another wok, heat oil. Add ginger slices and scallion segments, stir-fry until fragrant. Add about half a ladle of water. Then add steamed fish soy sauce, MSG, chicken bouillon, a little sugar, and optionally a little chili seasoning sauce. Add cilantro stalks. Cook until the aromas are released. Then remove and discard all the solid seasonings (ginger, scallion, cilantro). Keep the broth boiling. Drizzle in water starch to thicken the broth until it reaches a "honey-like" consistency.
   **Doneness Check**: The thickened broth should coat the back of a spoon.
   **Key Point**: Removing the solid seasonings ensures a clear broth. Thickening is key for the flavor to cling to the liver slices.

3. Turn off the heat. Add the poached liver slices into the thickened sauce. Toss well to coat each slice evenly. Pour the coated liver slices and the sauce over the vegetable base in the serving bowl.
4. Evenly sprinkle the chopped chive leaves and bird's eye chili rings over the liver. Heat one tablespoon of rapeseed oil until smoking hot. Carefully pour the hot oil over the chives and chilies.
**Key Point**: The hot oil instantly releases the fragrance of the chives and chilies. This is the source of the "fragrant chive" aroma.

## Health Tips
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, suitable for people with anemia or eye strain, but it is high in cholesterol; those with high blood lipids or blood pressure should consume in moderation. The water-poaching method effectively reduces oil usage. Pairing with chives and bean sprouts adds dietary fiber, making it healthy and refreshing. The final hot oil sizzling over the chives releases a strong aroma; it is advisable to turn on the range hood while cooking.
