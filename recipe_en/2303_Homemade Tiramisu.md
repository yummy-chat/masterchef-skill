# Homemade Tiramisu
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 3 eggs (use 2 if large)  
🫒 100 grams cooking oil  
🍬 120 grams white sugar (for the crust batter)  
🌾 380 grams flour (low-gluten or all-purpose)  
🌽 30 grams cornstarch  
🥛 350 grams whipping cream  
🍬 50 grams white sugar (for whipping cream)  
🍪 Crushed biscuits, as needed (made by crisping crust trimmings and rolling them into crumbs)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🪥 Toothpick  
🍳 Frying pan or electric griddle  
🧁 Electric mixer  
🍴 Spatula  
🍽️ Plate  
📦 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. **Prepare the crust batter:** Mix the eggs, cooking oil, and 120 grams of white sugar until evenly combined. Add the flour and cornstarch, stir until no dry flour remains, and knead into a smooth dough.
2. **Divide and roll out:** Roll the dough into a long strip and divide into 9 portions. Cover with a damp cloth to prevent drying. Dust the cutting board with flour, take one portion, and roll it into a large thin sheet. Place a round plate on top, trace around it with a toothpick, and remove the excess edges to get a round crust. Poke holes evenly across the crust with a toothpick.
3. **Cook the crusts:** Preheat a frying pan or electric griddle over medium-low heat without oil. Cook each crust for about 3 minutes per side until golden and crispy. Gather all trimmings, reroll them thinly, and cook until golden and crispy as well. Then roll them into coarse biscuit crumbs.
4. **Whip the cream:** Add 50 grams of white sugar to the whipping cream. Use an electric mixer on high speed until firm peaks form, and the cream shows clear lines.
5. **Assemble the cake:** Place one crust as the base, spread a layer of cream, and sprinkle with biscuit crumbs. Top with another crust and press gently. Repeat until all 9 crusts are used. Evenly spread cream over the top and sides.
6. **Decorate and set:** Sprinkle biscuit crumbs evenly over the top and sides. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight or freeze for about 2 hours to allow the crusts to absorb moisture and become soft in texture.
7. **Slice and serve:** The layers are distinct, soft, and fragrant.

## Health Advice
This recipe uses a large amount of sugar and cream to mimic the flavor of tiramisu, substituting homemade crusts for ladyfingers. Suitable for occasional indulgence. Consider reducing the sugar or pairing with black coffee to balance sweetness. Those with high blood sugar should limit consumption.
