# Risen Dough Juicy Meat Pie
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 400g
🥩 Ground pork 500g (with some fat, can substitute with beef)
🧅 Scallion 150g (chopped into scallion pieces)
💧 Warm water 320g (about 80% of flour weight, around 30°C)
🫙 Yeast 4g
🍬 White sugar 4g (for dough) + 1 teaspoon (for filling)
🧂 Baking powder 4g (optional, for extra fluffiness)
🧂 Salt 2g (for dough) + 2/3 teaspoon (for filling)
🫚 Ginger mince 10g (can substitute with ginger powder)
🧂 MSG 1 teaspoon (optional)
🍾 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon
🧴 Dark soy sauce 1/2 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce 1/2 tablespoon
🌿 Chinese thirteen-spice or five-spice powder 1/2 teaspoon
🫒 Cooking oil 20g (for filling) + as needed (for pan-frying)
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🫙 Large glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Measuring board
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🔪 Kitchen knife
♨️ Electric griddle or frying pan
🥄 Silicone spatula or spatula
🫗 Oil spray bottle
📦 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
**Prepare the Dough**
Mix flour, salt, yeast, white sugar, and baking powder evenly. Slowly pour in warm water while stirring with chopsticks. The dough will be extremely wet and sticky; do not knead with hands. Stir until no dry flour lumps remain, then use wet hands to smooth the dough surface. Seal and place in a warm spot to rise.

**Rise the Dough**
Let rise at room temperature (around 30°C) for 3-4 hours, until the dough expands to about 4 times its original size, with a honeycomb-like structure on the surface and inside. After rising, do not stir or degas; preserve the internal air pockets.

**Prepare the Meat Filling**
Mix ground pork, ginger mince, salt, white sugar, MSG, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and thirteen-spice powder until well combined. Add cooking oil and mix again to lock in moisture. The filling should not be too salty; no need to add water.

**Prepare the Scallions**
Chop scallions into pieces and mix with sesame oil. Keep scallions separate from the meat filling; combine only just before wrapping to prevent the scallions from developing off-flavors prematurely.

**Shape and Divide**
Spread a thick layer of dry flour on the cutting board. Transfer the risen dough to the board and dust the surface with more dry flour. Turn the dough repeatedly to coat it completely with flour until no longer sticky. Gently pinch and stretch the dough into an evenly thick long log. Use a knife to mark 8-10 equal portions, then tear off dough pieces of uniform size by hand.

**Combine the Filling**
Just before wrapping, pour the scallions into the meat filling and mix well, then press the filling flat in the bowl.

**Wrap the Filling**
Coat both hands with dry flour. Gently flatten a dough piece. Pinch only the edges to make them thinner, keeping the center thick, forming a wrapper with a thick center and thin edges. Add an appropriate amount of filling. Use a baozi-wrapping technique: stretch as you pinch, lift the edges, pinch them together, and gather toward the center. Seal the opening tightly, place it seam-side down, and gently press into a round patty. Cover the assembled patties with plastic wrap to prevent drying.

**Pan-Fry**
Preheat the pan until your hand feels a noticeable heat when held about 5 cm above the surface, then add a generous amount of cooking oil. Remove a patty, shake off excess flour from the bottom, and place it into the hot oil. Press it directly in the pan with your palm into a thin pancake about palm-sized. Lightly spray or brush the surface of all patties with oil. Cover with a lid. Pan-fry over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the patties puff up and the bottom is golden brown. Flip them over. Cover and continue pan-frying for another 2-3 minutes, until both sides are golden and the patties spring back quickly when pressed. Ensure the pan is hot enough and there is sufficient oil.

## Health Advice
This risen dough juicy meat pie is high in carbohydrates and protein, suitable for those with high physical exertion, but it is calorie-dense. For weight loss, pair with a vegetable salad. Control salt intake; those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of light soy sauce and oyster sauce.
