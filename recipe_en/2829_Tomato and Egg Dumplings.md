# Tomato and Egg Dumplings
Category: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 Tomatoes: About 2 (1 roasted over open flame, peeled, and used for sauce; 1 peeled and diced raw)
🥚 Eggs: About 5
🥟 Dumpling wrappers: As needed
🧅 Scallion: Half a stalk (cut into sections, for scallion oil)
🧄 Garlic: About 5 cloves (minced, for stir-frying in oil)
🧅 Spring onion: As needed (chopped, mixed raw into filling)
🫒 Peanut oil: As needed
🧂 Salt: As needed
🍶 Light soy sauce: A small amount
🍬 White sugar: A pinch
🌽 Cornstarch: As needed (mixed with water into a slurry)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍴 Fork (for holding tomato when roasting over open flame)
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Frying pan or wok
🥣 Glass bowl
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the tomato**: Secure a whole tomato with a fork and roast it over the open flame of a gas stove, turning it evenly, until the skin is largely charred and blistered, and the flesh separates from the skin.
2. **Peel and tear**: Immediately immerse the roasted tomato in cold water. Once cooled, peel off the charred skin. Tear the flesh into irregular small pieces and set aside for the sauce.
3. **Prepare raw ingredients**: Peel the other tomato and dice it into small pieces. Chop the spring onion into small pieces. Set both aside separately.
4. **Beat the eggs**: Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk quickly with chopsticks until uniform.
5. **Scramble the eggs**: Heat oil in a pan. Add the scallion sections and fry until translucent and fragrant, then remove. Pour in the beaten eggs and stir-fry over high heat, stirring quickly with chopsticks, until the eggs are tender and jelly-like in texture. Remove immediately.
   **Key point**: The eggs must remain tender and not be overcooked.
6. **Make the tomato sauce**: Leave a small amount of oil in the pan. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the torn tomato pieces and stir-fry quickly. When the tomatoes soften and release juice, reaching a semi-sandy state, add an appropriate amount of salt, a small pinch of sugar, and a little light soy sauce. Stir well. Drizzle in the cornstarch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens.
7. **Combine and cool**: Pour the cooked tomato sauce into the scrambled eggs and mix well. Spread the filling out on a plate or wooden cutting board and let it cool completely.
   **Note**: The filling must be fully cooled, otherwise it will cause the dumpling wrappers to break or become soggy when wrapping.
8. **Add raw ingredients**: Sprinkle the diced tomato and a generous amount of chopped spring onion over the fully cooled cooked filling. Gently mix to form the final filling.
9. **Wrap the dumplings**: Take a dumpling wrapper, add the filling, and pinch pleats using the "open a window first, then drink the soup" wrapping method.
10. **Boil the dumplings**: Bring water to a boil, add the dumplings, and cook until done. Remove and serve. The finished dumplings have smooth wrappers, tender fillings, and are full of juice.

## Health Advice
This dish uses a technique combining roasting with a raw and cooked dual filling method, preserving the tomato flavor. The eggs are tender, and the filling is full of juice, making it suitable for those seeking low oil and good flavor. Note that the filling must be fully cooled before wrapping, otherwise the dumpling wrappers may break. When scrambling the eggs, do not use overly high heat to maintain the tender, jelly-like texture.
