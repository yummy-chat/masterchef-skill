# Strange Flavor Soybeans
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Recommended Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Soybeans, about 500g (soaked in cold water overnight)
🥚 Egg, 1 piece
🌽 Cornstarch, 1 tablespoon
🧄 Garlic, 3 cloves (cracked whole)
🫗 White sesame seeds, 5g
🌶️ Chili powder, 5g
🧂 Salt, 3g
✨ Thirteen spices powder, 5g
🌿 Cumin powder, 5g
🍬 White sugar, 1.5 tablespoons
💧 Water, 1 tablespoon
🛢️ Cooking oil, a small amount (to prevent sticking when caramelizing sugar)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Ladle or spatula
🧲 Stainless steel strainer
🍳 Wok or frying pan

## Instructions
1. Drain the soaked soybeans, crack in one egg, and mix thoroughly so each soybean is coated with egg wash.
   - Key point: Soybeans must be soaked in cold water until fully expanded; allow enough time.
2. Add one tablespoon of cornstarch and mix by hand until the surface of the soybeans is evenly coated with starch.
3. Place the coated soybeans into a strainer and gently shake off excess starch.
   - Key point: Removing excess starch prevents the frying oil from getting cloudy.
4. In a small bowl, combine 3g salt, 5g thirteen spices powder, 5g cumin powder, 5g white sesame seeds, and 5g chili powder to make the strange flavor seasoning. Set aside.
5. Heat oil in a wok to about 170°C (340°F), then add the prepared soybeans and three cracked garlic cloves.
   - Key point: Use medium heat throughout; do not use low heat, or the starch coating may fall off.
6. Stir the soybeans continuously with a spatula to ensure even heating and prevent sticking. Fry over medium heat for about 7–8 minutes, until all soybeans float and the garlic cloves turn golden brown. Remove and drain the oil.
7. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, then add 1 tablespoon of water, 1.5 tablespoons of sugar, and a little cooking oil. Caramelize over medium heat.
   - Key point: Adding a little oil prevents sticking; watch the syrup's state—first large bubbles appear, then they turn into dense fish-eye bubbles. When the color turns slightly yellow, turn off the heat immediately to avoid burning.
8. After turning off the heat, quickly add the fried soybeans and stir rapidly so each soybean is evenly coated with syrup.
9. Immediately pour in the prepared strange flavor seasoning and continue stirring quickly until the seasoning is evenly attached and the fragrance emerges, with soybeans remaining separate and distinct.
10. Transfer to a plate. Allow to cool completely for a crispier texture.

## Health Advice
This strange flavor soybean dish is rich in protein and dietary fiber, suitable as a snack or accompaniment to drinks. However, due to frying and the syrup, it is relatively high in calories. People with high blood lipids or those needing to control sugar intake are advised to consume in moderation. Pay attention to heat control when caramelizing the sugar to prevent the syrup from turning bitter.
