# Tomahawk Steak
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 1 kg tomahawk steak (approximately 5 cm thick)  
🧄 2 heads of garlic  
🌿 2 sprigs of rosemary  
🍄🟫 Approximately 1 black truffle (chopped)  
🫗 120 ml broth (beef bone broth or chicken broth both acceptable)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (evenly coat both sides and edges of the steak)  
⚫ Black pepper, to taste (sprinkle together with salt)  
🫒 Olive oil, as needed (added when frying aromatics)  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed  
🧈 1 small piece of butter  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
📄 Kitchen paper  
🔥 Oven (for low-temperature roasting at 120°C)  
🌡️ Probe thermometer  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🪜 Wire rack  
🔩 Aluminum foil  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Small saucepan  

## Steps  
1. Fully thaw the tomahawk steak, then use kitchen paper to blot dry all surface moisture, ensuring the surface is completely dry.  
2. Evenly sprinkle a generous amount of salt and black pepper on both sides and the edges of the steak. Let it rest for 30 minutes to allow the seasoning to fully penetrate.  
3. Place the steak on a wire rack, insert the probe thermometer from the side center, ensuring the probe is located in the center of the thickest part.  
4. Preheat the oven to 120°C. Place the steak inside, set the thermometer's target temperature to 52°C, and roast for about 35 minutes. Remove once the center temperature reaches 52°C.  
5. Heat a frying pan over high heat without oil. First, stand the steak up to sear the fat on the edges, then lay the steak flat. Sear each side for 30–40 seconds until a uniform deep brown crust forms, then remove. Only sear to add color and aroma, avoid overheating.  
6. Leave the pan with rendered fat, add a little olive oil, reduce heat to low, and add the crushed garlic and rosemary to fry until fragrant. Place the garlic and rosemary on top of the steak and lightly rub the surface, then immediately wrap the steak completely in aluminum foil. Let it rest to allow the juices to redistribute.  
7. Using the same pan, sauté minced garlic over low heat until fragrant. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil. Add heavy cream and the chopped black truffle, then cook until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and black pepper to taste, then turn off the heat. Add a small piece of butter and stir until melted, using residual heat to brighten and enhance flavor.  
8. Remove the aluminum foil, place the rested steak on a cutting board, cut off the bone, and slice the meat into even pieces. Drizzle with the black truffle sauce and serve. When sliced, the meat should be uniformly pink and tender, with a thin outer crust.  

## Health Advice  
This tomahawk steak is high in protein and minimally processed, making it a quality choice for fitness or muscle-building enthusiasts. However, the 1 kg portion combined with the black truffle cream sauce is not low in calories. It is recommended to pair with generous amounts of vegetables to balance satiety, or share with friends. When searing, use high heat quickly to avoid overheating the interior and preserve the tender pink texture.
