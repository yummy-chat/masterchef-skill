# Homemade Spicy Strips (Latiao)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Gluten noodles 300g (main body of spicy strips)  
🧂 Salt 1 tbsp  
🍬 Sugar 2 tbsp  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1 tbsp  
🧂 White pepper powder 1 tbsp  
🌱 Cumin seeds 2 tbsp  
🌿 Cumin powder 1 tbsp  
🌶 Chili flakes 3 tbsp (adjust to taste)  
🧄 Minced garlic 2 tbsp  
🌾 White sesame seeds 2 tbsp  
🥄 Light soy sauce 3 tbsp  
🫒 Cooking oil approx. 250g (for frying spiced oil)  
🌿 Star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, scallion segments appropriate amounts (for flavoring the oil)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for coating gluten noodles to prevent sticking)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Heat-resistant bowl (for mixing seasonings and chili oil)  
🍳 Small saucepan (for frying spiced oil)  
🥄 Strainer (for removing spice solids)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring and turning)  
🥄 Measuring spoons (for measuring dry ingredients)  
♨️ Steamer (for steaming gluten noodles)  
🍽 Plate (for serving)  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the chili oil seasoning**
    In a heat-resistant bowl, add salt, sugar, Sichuan peppercorn powder, white pepper powder, cumin seeds, cumin powder, chili flakes, minced garlic, and white sesame seeds. Then pour in light soy sauce and stir well with chopsticks. Set aside.
   **Note**: All dry ingredients and light soy sauce must be mixed in advance so that the flavors are fully released when hot oil is poured over later.

2. **Prepare gluten noodles and prevent sticking**
    Place gluten noodles in a bowl, add cold water, and rub and rinse for about 1 minute until the outer layer softens slightly. Drain well. Then evenly brush a layer of cooking oil over the noodles and rub thoroughly with your hands to ensure each strand is coated with an oil film.
   **Key point**: Coating with oil is essential to prevent the noodles from sticking together during steaming.

3. **Fry the spiced oil**
    Pour about 250g of cooking oil into a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat, then add star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and scallion segments. Fry until the spices turn yellow and release their aroma, then turn off the heat. Use a strainer to remove and discard all spice solids.
   **Indicator**: The spices should turn yellow; be careful not to burn them.

4. **Pour hot oil to release aroma**
    Slowly pour the hot oil in batches into the seasoning bowl, stirring continuously with chopsticks to ensure even heating. Mix into a slightly runny chili oil and set aside.
   **Key point**: Use a heat-resistant bowl, add the hot oil in portions, and keep stirring to prevent the seasonings from burning.

5. **Steam the gluten noodles**
    Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Spread the oil-coated gluten noodles evenly in the steamer basket, cover, and steam over medium heat for 3 minutes. Open the lid and flip the noodles every 1 minute during steaming to prevent sticking. Steam until the noodles have no hard core inside.
   **Key point**: Do not over-steam, or the texture will lose its chewiness.

6. **Mix while hot to form spicy strips**
    Immediately transfer the steamed gluten noodles into the bowl of chili oil. Use chopsticks to lift and mix from the bottom up until all noodles are evenly coated with red oil and seasoning, resembling store-bought spicy strips in appearance.
   **Key point**: Mixing while hot helps the flavors penetrate better, resulting in a spicy, chewy texture.

## Health Suggestions
For homemade spicy strips, it is recommended to use gluten noodles with a reduced-oil version of spiced oil, maintaining chewiness while lowering calorie content. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy flavors and wish to reduce additive intake. Be mindful to control the amount of chili and salt to avoid irritating the stomach.
