# Seaweed and Egg Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs  
🌿 Dried seaweed (nori)  
🧅 Scallions, chopped  
🦐 Shrimp (optional)  
🦐 Dried shrimp (optional)  
🧂 Salt  
🫗 Cooking oil  
🫒 Sesame oil  
🧂 Cornstarch (optional)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Soup pot  

## Instructions
1. Soak the dried seaweed in clean water for 15 minutes, then drain and set aside.
2. Pour 1.5 liters of water into the pot, add 5 ml of oil and 2 g of salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the seaweed.
3. Once the seaweed has boiled, continue cooking for 3 minutes. Slowly pour the beaten eggs into the pot, keep the heat low, and let the egg flowers float to the surface for about 30 seconds.
   **Key point**: Pour the egg liquid slowly into the center of the boiling water, cover the pot with a lid, turn off the heat, and let it sit for half a minute to make the egg flowers more tender.
4. Sprinkle in the chopped scallions, turn to low heat, and cook for 20 seconds.
5. Turn off the heat, drizzle in a few drops of sesame oil, and optionally add a pinch of dried shrimp to enhance the flavor. Then serve.
   **Key point**: If you prefer a thicker texture, mix in 2 g of cornstarch with a little water to create a slurry before adding the seaweed.

## Health Tips
Seaweed and egg drop soup is low in fat and high in protein, rich in iodine and dietary fiber. It is suitable for those managing their weight or supplementing iodine, but individuals with hyperthyroidism should limit their seaweed intake. It is recommended to replace salt with dried shrimp for seasoning to reduce sodium consumption.
