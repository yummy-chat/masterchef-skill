# Crispy French Fries 🍟  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (cut into thick strips)  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed  
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon  
🌿 Bay leaves, a few  
🪵 Cinnamon, a small piece  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Boiling pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Deep fryer / wok  
📦 Stainless steel tray  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the potatoes into thick fries. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the fries, salt, bay leaves, and cinnamon. Blanch until the edges of the fries turn slightly translucent, then remove immediately.  
   **Note**: Do not blanch for too long, or the fries will become sticky during frying.  
2. Quickly transfer the blanched fries into cold water to cool. Then thoroughly pat dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels.  
   **Note**: All moisture must be absorbed; otherwise, the coating will clump and affect the texture.  
3. Place the fries in a tray, sprinkle with cornstarch, and toss until each fry is evenly coated with a thin layer of powder.  
   **Note**: Only a light coating is needed; do not overdo it.  
4. Pour enough oil into the pot and heat over high heat. Add the coated fries and keep the heat on high throughout. Fry until the fries are distinct from each other and lightly golden, then remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Key point**: High heat sets the fries quickly, preventing them from sticking together.  
5. Increase the oil temperature and return the first-fried fries for a second fry. Fry until golden and the outer shell is crispy, then quickly remove.  
   **Key point**: The second fry is essential for achieving a crispy crust; the oil should be hot enough, and the frying time short.  

## Health Tips  
Adding cinnamon and bay leaves during blanching gives the fries a unique aroma, but the blanching time should be short to avoid stickiness during frying. This dish is high in carbohydrates and calories, suitable for occasional indulgence. Diabetics should monitor their intake. Pair with a vegetable salad to balance the oil.
