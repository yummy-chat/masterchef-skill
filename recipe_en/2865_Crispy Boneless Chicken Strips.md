# Crispy Boneless Chicken Strips  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken breast 230-250g (remove membranes and fat, cut into strips along the grain)  
🌿 Ginger 10g (crushed)  
🌱 Scallion 10g (crushed)  
🧄 Garlic 5g (crushed)  
💧 Water 70g (for infused water, take 50-60g after straining)  
🌽 Cornstarch 10g  
💧 Water 13g (for cornstarch slurry)  
🍞 Breadcrumbs适量 (coarse, homemade style, blitz in food processor to fine)  
🧂 Baking soda 1g  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g  
🍬 Sugar 3g  
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.3g  
🌿 Thirteen-spice (or five-spice powder) 0.3g  
🧄 Garlic powder 1g (optional)  
🧅 Onion powder 1g (optional)  
🍛 Curry powder 1g (optional)  
🫒 Cooking oil适量 (for frying)  
🌶️ Ground cumin适量 (sprinkle while hot)  
🌶️ Chili powder适量 (sprinkle while hot)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (multiple)  
🧫 Bowl  
🕸️ Strainer  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🧊 Meat grinder/food processor  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Wooden chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Use scissors to trim off the fat and white membranes from the edges of the chicken breast. Lay the chicken breast flat and evenly slice into 3 pieces with a knife, then cut into strips about the thickness of a pinky finger, following the grain.  
   **Key Point**: Removing membranes ensures tender meat; cutting along the grain prevents shrinkage during frying.  

2. Place the crushed ginger, scallion, and garlic in a bowl, add 70g of water, and squeeze and knead vigorously until the water becomes cloudy. Strain out the solids with a strainer to obtain about 50-60g of infused water.  
   **Note**: Squeeze thoroughly to extract the aroma from ginger, scallion, and garlic; use after straining.  

3. Add baking soda, salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, white pepper powder, and thirteen-spice to the infused water. Optionally add garlic powder, onion powder, and curry powder. Stir until all seasonings are fully dissolved.  
   **Note**: Seasonings must be completely dissolved to ensure even flavor during marination.  

4. Mix cornstarch with 13g of water, stir into a thick slurry, and set aside.  
   **Key Point**: The slurry should be thick for easy coating later.  

5. Pour coarse breadcrumbs into a food processor and blitz into a fine powder with small granules. Transfer to a plate and set aside.  
   **Note**: Finer breadcrumbs adhere better, and the granules add extra crunch.  

6. Place the chicken strips in a bowl, pour in the seasoned infused water, toss to coat evenly, then stir continuously in one direction for about 2 minutes until the liquid is fully absorbed and the bottom of the bowl feels slightly sticky. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.  
   **Key Point**: Thorough stirring helps the liquid penetrate the meat; refrigerating makes the meat more tender and flavorful.  

7. Remove the marinated chicken strips, pour in the cornstarch slurry, toss to coat evenly, and stir a few more times in one direction so each strip is covered with a thin batter.  
   **Note**: Coat evenly; a thin batter locks in moisture, resulting in crispy outside and tender inside after frying.  

8. Take one battered chicken strip, place it in the plate of breadcrumbs, and roll it back and forth with your hand to coat thoroughly. Lift it up and gently shake off excess crumbs. Repeat for each strip one by one. **Do not dump all chicken strips into the breadcrumbs at once**, as this may cause clumping and loss of coating.  
   **Key Point**: Coating one by one ensures even coverage on each strip and prevents sticking.  

9. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat over high heat until it reaches 50% hot. Test by inserting a wooden chopstick—dense bubbles should quickly form around it. Reduce to medium-low heat. Gently add the breaded chicken strips one by one into the oil. Let them set for about ten seconds before gently stirring with chopsticks to separate. Fry over medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes, turning occasionally with chopsticks, until golden brown and floating. Remove and drain.  
   **Key Point**: Maintain oil at 50% heat and fry over medium-low heat for a crispy exterior and tender interior; do not disturb immediately after adding to prevent coating loss.  

10. While hot, evenly sprinkle ground cumin and chili powder over the fried chicken strips and serve immediately.  
    **Note**: Sprinkling while hot helps the spices adhere better, enhancing flavor.  

## Health Tips  
Chicken breast is low in fat and high in protein. Although frying increases calorie content, moderate consumption is fine. Marinating with baking soda and infused water tenderizes the meat, making it a good option for satisfying cravings while staying fit. Be careful with oil temperature to avoid excessive oil absorption; sprinkling cumin and chili powder is healthier than dipping in sauce.
