# Lin Xue Braised Pork Belly (Hong Shao Rou)
Cuisine: Shanghainese (Benbang) Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Black-haired pig skin-on pork belly, appropriate amount (choose three-layered belly with alternating fat and lean meat and thick skin)
🥢 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into sections, added before serving)
🌶️ Small chili peppers, appropriate amount (added before serving for aroma)
⭐ Star anise, a small amount (used during blanching and stir-frying)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧂 Rock sugar, appropriate amount (small pieces for caramelizing, large pieces for braising to add sweetness)
🍶 Rice wine, appropriate amount (for blanching to remove odor and stir-frying to enhance aroma)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (added after stir-frying meat for color to enhance umami)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring, giving the meat a deep sauce color)
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount (for caramelizing sugar)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (used throughout: blanching, caramelizing sugar, stir-frying meat, braising, and reducing sauce)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing blanched pork belly and draining)
🥢 Chopsticks (for turning ingredients and checking doneness)
🥣 Glass bowl (for holding blanched pork belly)
🔧 Spatula (for stir-frying ingredients and stirring caramelized sugar)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Selection and Cutting**
   Select skin-on pork belly, preferably with alternating fat and lean layers and thick skin. Cut the pork belly into uniformly large pieces to ensure a satisfying texture and prevent crumbling during braising.
2. **Blanching to Remove Odor**
   Add warm water to the wok, place the pork belly pieces in, add star anise and a small amount of rice wine, heat until boiling. Cook until the meat pieces are fully cooked, springy to the touch with no blood water seeping out. Remove and drain, about 5-8 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Using warm water prevents the meat from shrinking and becoming tough; draining thoroughly prevents oil splattering during subsequent stir-frying.
3. **Caramelizing Sugar**
   Pour a small amount of cooking oil into the wok, add small pieces of rock sugar, stir-fry over low heat until the sugar is fully melted, turns burnt brown, and emits fine bubbles. Turn off the heat and let the residual heat cause the sugar liquid to continue bubbling, about 2-3 minutes.
   **Note**: Avoid overcooking the caramelized sugar, or it will become bitter. The burnt brown color gives a unique caramelized flavor.
4. **Stir-Frying Meat for Color**
   Turn the heat to high, add the drained pork belly pieces, add star anise and ginger slices. Stir-fry quickly until each piece is evenly coated with caramelized sugar and the surface turns deep reddish-brown, about 2-3 minutes.
   **Key Point**: High-heat quick stir-frying allows the caramelized sugar to fully adhere while releasing the meat's aroma.
5. **Seasoning and Braising**
   Drizzle rice wine along the edge of the wok. Add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce in sequence. Pour in hot water until the meat pieces are submerged. Add large pieces of rock sugar, cover the wok, reduce heat to low, and braise for about 30 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to use hot water. Cold water will cause the meat to tighten and become tough. The water amount must cover the ingredients for thorough flavor infusion. Large rock sugar pieces melt slowly during braising, providing a sweet, mellow taste.
6. **Reducing Sauce and Finishing**
   After braising, turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly to prevent burning. When the sauce is thick and can cling to the meat surface, add scallion sections and small chili peppers, stir-fry until fragrant. Arrange the braised pork belly on a plate and pour the thick sauce from the wok over it, about 3-5 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Keep stirring during sauce reduction. Adding scallions and chili peppers enhances the complex aroma; pouring the sauce over the meat makes the finished dish glossy and appealing.

## Health Recommendations
Although braised pork belly is delicious, pork belly has high fat content. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables or coarse grains to balance calories and nutrition. It is especially suitable for replenishing energy in autumn and winter. People with high blood lipids should consume in small amounts, control portion sizes, and avoid mixing the sauce with rice.
