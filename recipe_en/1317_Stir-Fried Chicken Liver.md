# Stir-Fried Chicken Liver
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ★★★
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## 🥬 Ingredients
- 🐔 Raw chicken livers 5 (about 800g)
- 🌱 Garlic sprouts about 200g
- 🧅 Scallions 150g
- 🫚 Ginger 120g

## 🧂 Seasonings
- 🍶 Cooking wine 30ml
- 🧂 Table salt 5g
- 🧂 Chicken bouillon 5g
- 🌿 Five-spice powder 5g
- 🌶️ Ground pepper 5g
- 🍢 BBQ seasoning or cumin powder 10g (optional)
- 🌾 Sesame seeds 5g (optional)

## 🍳 Cookware
- 🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
- 🥘 Wok
- 🥄 Spatula
- 🥣 Slotted spoon
- 🧊 Cold water or ice water

## 📝 Instructions
1. Clean the chicken livers thoroughly and drain well.
2. Wash the garlic sprouts and cut into sections; set aside.
3. Wash the scallions. Cut 100g into sections and 50g into slices; set aside.
4. Wash the ginger. Slice 70g and dice 50g; set aside.
5. **Blanching**
   - Place chicken livers in a pot, add cold water until fully submerged, then add 100g scallion sections, 70g ginger slices, and 30ml cooking wine.
   - Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Skim off any foam and continue cooking for 5–10 minutes.
   - Turn off the heat. Remove the chicken livers and immediately immerse them in cold water (or ice water). Clean and slice them; set aside.
6. **Stir-Frying**
   - Heat the wok over medium heat, add 30ml cooking oil, and wait until the oil is hot (about 10 seconds).
   - Add 50g scallion slices and 50g ginger dice; stir-fry until fragrant.
   - Add the sliced chicken livers and stir-fry until the surface turns slightly golden.
   - Add the garlic sprout sections and stir-fry for 30 seconds until just cooked; reduce to low heat.
   - Add 5g table salt, 5g chicken bouillon, 5g five-spice powder, 5g ground pepper, and 10g BBQ seasoning or cumin powder (optional).
   - Increase to high heat and quickly stir-fry for 30 seconds to prevent sticking. Turn off the heat.
   - Transfer to a plate. Optionally, sprinkle 5g sesame seeds on top for garnish.

⚠️ **Notes**
- Keep an eye on the water level during blanching to prevent overflow.
- After blanching, it is recommended to cool the chicken livers in cold or ice water for a better texture.

## Health Advice
Chicken liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, making it suitable for people with anemia or eye strain. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Pairing with garlic sprouts and five-spice powder helps reduce the gamey taste and enhances flavor. For a healthier version, use less oil and salt.
