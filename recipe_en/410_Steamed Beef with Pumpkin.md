# Steamed Beef with Pumpkin  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef appropriate amount (sliced thinly)  
🎃 Pumpkin about half (cut into thick slices to cover the bottom of the plate)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Chef’s knife/scissors  
🍽️ Steamer (with steaming rack)  
🥄 Chopsticks  
💨 Oil spray bottle  

## Steps  
1. Slice the beef into thin pieces of even thickness for quick cooking and a tender texture.  
2. Add minced ginger, minced garlic, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, and sugar to the beef slices, and mix thoroughly.  
3. Add cornstarch and mix again to evenly coat the surface of the beef.  
4. Finally, drizzle in an appropriate amount of cooking oil and lightly toss to lock in moisture, preventing the meat from becoming tough during steaming.  
5. Cut the pumpkin into thick slices and spread them evenly on the bottom of a plate. Place the plate in the steamer with cold water, cover, and steam for about 2 minutes to slightly soften the pumpkin.  
6. Spread the marinated beef slices evenly over the pumpkin surface, avoiding stacking to ensure even heating.  
7. Cover the steamer and continue steaming for 5 minutes.  
8. Turn off the heat, remove, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Beef paired with pumpkin is steamed for a low-oil, healthy preparation. Pumpkin is rich in carotene, while beef provides high-quality protein, making it suitable for those needing energy without excess grease. Ensure beef is sliced thinly and spread out during steaming to avoid overlapping, which can cause the meat to become tough.
