# Braised Beef Brisket with Tomatoes
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the beef ribs and cut into large pieces. Choose parts with some fat; cut the pieces slightly larger than the desired final size, as they will shrink during braising.
2. Chop the onion, and cut the ginger and garlic into small pieces or slices.
3. Open the cans of tomato paste and crushed tomatoes and set aside.
4. Pour olive oil into the braising pot, heat over high heat until the pot is fully hot, then add the beef ribs and quickly sear until evenly browned on all sides. Do not blanch the beef; this step uses the Maillard reaction to remove odors and enhance flavor.
5. Remove the seared beef and set aside. Use the oil left in the pot for the next steps; do not wash the pot.
6. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger to the pot. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.
7. Add the pure tomato paste and stir-fry thoroughly to release its aroma.
8. Pour in the entire can of crushed tomatoes, then return the seared beef to the pot. If desired, add a small amount of red wine to enhance flavor.
9. Add enough water to submerge all ingredients. Season with salt, black pepper, sugar, bay leaves, and dried chili segments to create a base flavor and balance the acidity.
10. Bring to a boil over high heat, then skim off any foam from the surface to keep the broth clear.
11. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is tender and the broth thickens.
12. Remove the lid. Based on the thickness and taste of the broth, you can increase the heat to reduce the liquid and adjust the seasoning with additional salt and black pepper as needed. Once the desired consistency and flavor are achieved, remove from heat and serve.

## Health Tips
Beef is rich in iron and protein, and the vitamin C in tomatoes can enhance iron absorption, making this dish suitable for those in need of iron supplementation. It is recommended to serve with mixed grain rice to reduce fat intake, and use less sugar during cooking to control calories.
