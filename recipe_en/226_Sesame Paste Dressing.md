# Sesame Paste Dressing
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 Pure sesame paste, appropriate amount (3:1 ratio with peanut butter)
🥜 Pure peanut butter, appropriate amount (omit if using blended sesame paste that already contains peanut butter)
🫗 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🌿 Sesame oil, appropriate amount
🍶 Yellow wine, appropriate amount
💧 Water, about 500g (for making seasoned water)
🌰 Cooked soybean oil, about 500g (for making seasoned oil)
🍃 Water seasoning spices (per 500g water)
 - Cumin, 1g
 - Cinnamon, 1g
 - Star anise, 1g
 - Sichuan peppercorn, 1g
 - Long pepper, 1g
 - Bay leaf, 1g
 - Clove, 2 pieces
 - Amomum villosum, 1 piece
🍂 Oil seasoning spices
 - Star anise, 2g
 - Cinnamon, 2g
 - Cumin, 5g
 - Bay leaf, 1g
 - Cardamom, 2 pieces
 - Sichuan peppercorn, 3g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Bowls (at least two)
🥄 Spoon (for stirring)
🥢 Strainer (slotted spoon)
🍳 Pot
🍽️ Metal tray or bowl (for holding dry spices)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place pure sesame paste in a bowl, and add pure peanut butter in a 3:1 ratio.
2. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and yellow wine in sequence, stirring until well combined. Divide the prepared paste into two equal portions, one for water seasoning and one for oil seasoning.

3. **Make seasoned water**: Pour 500g of water into a pot, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Add all water seasoning spices and cook for about 2 minutes until the aroma is fully released. Turn off the heat, strain out the seasoned water with a strainer. The spent spices can be reused once.
   - **Key point**: The seasoned water must be completely cooled before use; otherwise, hot seasoned water will cause the sesame paste to release oil and fail to emulsify.

4. **Make seasoned oil**: Lightly moisten the oil seasoning spices with water; pour about 500g of cooled cooked soybean oil into the pot, add the moistened spices directly, and fry slowly over low heat.
   - **Key point**: Moistening the spices helps release aroma and prevents burning; the oil must be started cold, and the heat must remain low throughout.
   - Fry until the spices darken in color and the oil is richly aromatic. Pour both the oil and spices into a bowl, cover, and let it steep until completely cooled. Then strain out the spice residue, retaining only the seasoned oil.

5. **Water-seasoned sesame paste**: Add the completely cooled seasoned water to one portion of the prepared paste in small batches. Each time, add a small amount and stir rapidly in one direction with a spoon until the paste fully absorbs the liquid and becomes thick again before adding the next batch.
   - **Note**: Do not pour all the seasoned water in at once, as it may clump and be difficult to mix evenly.
   - Repeat this process until the paste is smooth, fine, free of lumps, and forms a flowing thread when lifted with the spoon.

6. **Oil-seasoned sesame paste**: Add the completely cooled seasoned oil to the other portion of the prepared paste in the same way, stirring in one direction as you add.
   - Stir until the paste is smooth, glossy, and also reaches a flowing, thread-like consistency.

7. **Final product and serving suggestions**: The water-seasoned sesame paste is lighter in color and has a refreshing taste, making it ideal for dishes like hot pot, spicy hot pot, and braised vegetables. The oil-seasoned sesame paste is darker and more aromatic, better suited for cold dishes such as cold noodles, cold jelly noodles, and cold rice noodles.

## Health Tips
Combining sesame paste with peanut butter reduces the bitterness of pure sesame while promoting absorption of calcium and protein. The prepared dressing is high in calories; during weight loss, it is recommended to use one spoonful per serving and avoid excessive consumption.
