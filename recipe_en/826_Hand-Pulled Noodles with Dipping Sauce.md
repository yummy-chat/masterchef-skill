# Hand-Pulled Noodles with Dipping Sauce
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour, about 600g
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon (added to flour)
💧 Water, 300ml (for kneading dough)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (generous amount, for sealing dough pieces)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (cut into sections)
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed
🧅 Chopped green onions, as needed
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder, as needed (optional)
🍶 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍶 Aged vinegar, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Cutting board and knife
📦 Plastic wrap or damp cloth
🥘 Metal tray or baking sheet
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Instructions
1. **Knead the dough**: Put flour in a bowl, add 1 teaspoon of salt. Slowly pour in water while stirring with chopsticks until it forms flaky pieces, then knead with your hands into a dough.  
   **Note**: It is fine if the dough is not smooth after the first knead. Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 15 minutes, then knead again—it will become smooth quickly.

2. **First resting and shaping**: Place the smooth dough on a cutting board, roll it into a long log, and cut into evenly sized dough pieces.  
   **Note**: Each piece can be pulled into one bowl of noodles; let the cut pieces rest for another 15 minutes.

3. **Second resting**: Slightly roll each dough piece into a longer shape, and arrange them neatly in an oiled tray. Pour a generous amount of cooking oil over the dough pieces, making sure to coat the gaps as well. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for at least 40 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Use enough oil to prevent sticking; sufficient resting is crucial for pulling the noodles thin and long.

4. **Prepare the dipping sauce**: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped green onions, and chili flakes. Pour a spoonful of hot oil over them to release the aroma. Once the oil cools slightly, add light soy sauce and aged vinegar, and stir well.  
   **Key point**: Hot oil maximally releases the fragrance of the spices.

5. **Pull the noodles**: Take one rested dough piece, flatten it, and use a rolling pin to thin out both ends while keeping the center thicker. Hold both ends with your hands, gently stretch while slapping and shaking the dough against the cutting board, until it forms a long, evenly thick noodle strip.  
   **Key point**: If the dough stretches easily without breaking, it has rested enough; the thicker center provides better chewiness; pull gently and evenly.

6. **Cook the noodles**: Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil, add the pulled noodles, and quickly separate them with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Cook over high heat until all noodles float and change color, indicating they are done.  
   **Note**: Ensure the water is boiling before adding noodles; they cook quickly and should not be boiled too long.

7. **Combine and serve**: Once the noodles are cooked, add cilantro pieces, blanch briefly, then remove with a slotted spoon. Serve in a bowl, either with the hot cooking water for a warm dish, or rinse in cold water for a cold dish.  
   **Note**: Choose the method according to the season—hot soup in winter, cold water rinse in summer for a chewier texture.

8. **Eating**: Pick up the cooked noodles with chopsticks, dip them into the prepared dipping sauce bowl, stir to coat, and enjoy. This is the standard way to eat "dipping noodles."

## Health Tips
This hand-pulled noodle dish uses oil to seal the dough for a chewy texture. It is high in carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise or as a main meal. It is recommended to pair it with a serving of stir-fried vegetables or cold cucumber salad to balance dietary fiber and vitamins. The hot oil in the dipping sauce releases the aroma of the spices, but control the amount of oil and salt used; those with hypertension can reduce the chili flakes and light soy sauce.
