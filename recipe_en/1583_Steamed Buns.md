# Steamed Buns  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g (2 jin)  
🍄 Dry yeast 10g  
🍬 White sugar 5g (to aid fermentation)  
💧 Warm water 460g (feels warm to the touch, not hot)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍲 Steamer pot  
🥟 Steamer basket  
🧻 Cloth or silicone mat for steaming (non-stick)  
🫙 Plastic wrap (for sealing)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Add dry yeast and white sugar to warm water, stir well, and let sit for a moment to activate the yeast.  
   **Note**: Must use warm water to dissolve; adding directly to flour will affect fermentation.  
2. Gradually pour the activated yeast water into the flour, stirring as you go, and knead into a smooth dough.  
3. Place the dough back in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place until it expands to more than double in size, with a honeycomb-like interior.  
4. Remove the fermented dough, and on a surface dusted with dry flour, repeatedly knead and press firmly to release gas.  
   **Key**: Must knead until the dough is firm, smooth, and free of large air pockets, otherwise the buns will have a rough texture and easily shrink.  
5. Roll the dough into a long log and cut into even pieces, then shape each piece into a smooth, round bun blank.  
6. Place the bun blanks in a steamer basket lined with cloth, leaving gaps, cover with a lid, and perform a second proofing.  
   **Judgment standard**: Blanks visibly increase in size, and feel light and springy to the touch.  
7. Add enough water to the steamer pot and bring to a boil. Place the proofed steamer basket over the boiling water, cover tightly, and steam over high heat for 12 minutes once steam rises.  
   **Note**: If second proofing is insufficient or the weather is cold, you can start with cold water, allowing the rising water temperature to assist with proofing.  
8. Immediately turn off the heat after the time is up—no need to let it sit—open the lid and remove the buns directly to avoid condensation dripping and causing a wrinkled skin.  

## Health Tips  
Steamed buns are a great staple food, providing carbohydrates for energy, and they are easier to digest after fermentation. Pay attention to reducing sugar usage; diabetics can omit it. Let the buns cool before eating to lower the blood sugar response.
