# Shrimp, Zucchini, and Egg Dumplings  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp, appropriate amount (naturally thawed)  
🥕 Carrot, about 1 small piece (cut into thin strips)  
🥒 Zucchini, about 1 small piece (shredded into thin strips)  
🥚 Eggs, appropriate amount (pre-scrambled and crumbled)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (divided into two parts: ginger slices for cleaning shrimp, minced ginger for filling)  
🧂 Salt, about 4 tablespoons (for marinating shrimp), about 1 tablespoon (for marinating zucchini), a pinch (for blanching and boiling dumplings)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, one small spoonful  
🥄 Light soy sauce, 1/3 spoonful  
🌿 White pepper powder, a pinch  
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar, a small amount  
🤍 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount (for stir-frying carrot)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl/container (for marinating, cleaning, and mixing filling)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🧀 Grater  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍲 Cooking pot  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Marinate and clean the shrimp**: Place the naturally thawed shrimp into a bowl, add about 4 tablespoons of salt, and gently knead with your hands for about 1 minute until the shrimp release a little water. Then rinse repeatedly with plenty of water, adding ginger slices and a pinch of salt, several times. Remove and place on a clean towel to pat completely dry.  
   - **Key point**: The heavy salt marinade helps draw out impurities, and multiple rinses are crucial for removing the fishy smell.  

2. **Prepare dual-texture shrimp filling**: Take half of the shrimp and repeatedly pound them with the back of a knife until a fine shrimp paste forms. Cut the other half into small pieces to retain a chunky texture. Then mix the shrimp paste and shrimp pieces together evenly.  
   - **Key point**: The shrimp paste provides binding, while the shrimp pieces offer a bouncy, chewy bite.  

3. **Process the zucchini shreds**: Shred the zucchini into fine strips, add about 1 tablespoon of salt, and mix well to marinate. Once water is released, squeeze thoroughly dry. Rinse briefly with clean water once, then squeeze dry again.  
   - **Key point**: Be sure to squeeze out the moisture completely to prevent the filling from becoming watery later.  

4. **Stir-fry the carrot strips**: Add a small amount of cooking oil to a pan, add the carrot strips, and stir-fry over medium heat until softened and the color deepens. Remove and let cool.  
   - **Key point**: Stir-frying makes the carrot sweeter and helps the body absorb fat-soluble vitamins.  

5. **Season the shrimp filling and build texture**: Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, white pepper powder, sesame oil, white sugar, and white sesame seeds to the mixed shrimp paste and shrimp pieces. Stir continuously in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and elastic.  
   - **Key point**: Stirring consistently in one direction makes the filling more springy.  

6. **Combine all filling ingredients**: Add minced ginger, the squeezed-dry zucchini shreds, stir-fried carrot strips, crumbled eggs, and chopped scallions to the well-stirred shrimp filling. Mix gently until just combined.  
   - **Key point**: Add the vegetables and eggs last, and mix gently to prevent water release and avoid a mushy filling.  

7. **Wrap the dumplings**: Divide the dough into portions, roll out into dumpling wrappers with a thicker center and thinner edges. Fill with an appropriate amount of filling, then pinch the edges tightly to seal.  

8. **Boil the dumplings**: Bring a pot of water to a full boil, add a pinch of salt, then drop in the dumplings. Gently push with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. When the water returns to a boil, add a small amount of cold water once. Boil until the dumplings are all puffed up and floating on the surface, then remove.  
   - **Key point**: Add dumplings to boiling water, add salt to prevent sticking, and add cold water to ensure even cooking of the wrapper and filling.  

## Health Advice  
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with zucchini and carrot, it adds dietary fiber, making it suitable for those on a weight loss or blood sugar control diet. Note that the salt used for marinating shrimp is relatively high; individuals with high blood pressure may consider reducing the salt amount.
