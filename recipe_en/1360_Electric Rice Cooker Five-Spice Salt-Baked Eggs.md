# Electric Rice Cooker Five-Spice Salt-Baked Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 6 eggs (need to be cleaned)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for coating eggs and heat conduction)  
🍾 Soy sauce, as needed (for brushing onto eggs to add color and aroma)  
🌿 Five-spice powder, as needed (for sprinkling on eggshells to enhance flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍚 Electric rice cooker  
🧻 1 facial towel (to line the bottom of the pot)  
💧 Water, as needed (to wet the facial towel)  
🍽️ 1 plate (to hold the salt)  
🥄 1 spoon (for cracking the eggshells)  
🖌️ 1 brush (for applying soy sauce)  

## Steps  
1. Lay the facial towel flat on the bottom of the electric rice cooker inner pot, and pour in enough water to completely soak it.  
   **Note**: The lining must be thoroughly wet.  
2. Roll the cleaned eggs in the plate of salt so that the surface is evenly coated with a layer of salt.  
   **Key Point**: The salt crust allows the eggs to heat more evenly and imparts a basic salty flavor.  
3. Place the salt-coated eggs on the wet facial towel and close the lid.  
4. Select the "Wood-Fire Rice" function on the electric rice cooker and steam for the first time until the program automatically ends.  
5. Open the lid and use the back of a spoon to crack the shell of each egg.  
   **Key Point**: The eggshells must be broken; this is a prerequisite for subsequent flavor infusion.  
6. Evenly brush a layer of soy sauce over the cracked eggshells, then sprinkle a layer of five-spice powder on top.  
7. Close the lid again and select the "Wood-Fire Rice" function for a second steaming session to allow the seasoning to penetrate.  
8. After the second steaming session is complete, remove the eggs and they are ready to eat. The finished egg whites and yolks are fully set, with a strong five-spice flavor and a savory, firm texture.  

## Health Advice  
Five-spice salt-baked eggs are low in fat and high in protein, making them suitable as a snack for weight loss or fitness enthusiasts, but consuming 1–2 eggs per day is healthier. Note that the salt content is relatively high, so people with high blood pressure should control their intake.
