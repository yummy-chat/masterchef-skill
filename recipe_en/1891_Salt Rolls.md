# Salt Rolls
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥖 Bread flour 600g
🍬 Caster sugar 24g
🧂 Salt 6g
💧 Ice water 360ml
🍞 Dry yeast 6g
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (chilled and thinly sliced, for kneading)
🧂 Salted butter approx. 5-8g per piece (for rolling)
🌿 Seaweed powder approx. 6g (for making seaweed-flavored dough)
🤍 White sesame seeds as needed (for decorating the bottom of seaweed-flavored rolls)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Stand mixer
⚖️ Digital scale
🥛 Measuring cup
🥄 Measuring spoon
🔪 Dough scraper
🥘 Baking tray
🧊 Cooling rack
🌡️ Proofing box
🔥 Oven
🧽 Silicone mat or kneading mat
🪚 Rolling pin
⏱️ Timer

## Steps
1. Add ice cubes to water to create ice water. Cut salted butter into strips, each about 5-8g, and refrigerate. If making seaweed-flavored dough, mix the seaweed powder with an equal amount of water to dilute.
2. Pour bread flour, caster sugar, and salt into the stand mixer. Add most of the ice water and mix on low speed until a rough dough forms.
3. Add dry yeast and the reserved small amount of ice water. Continue mixing until the yeast is fully incorporated and no granules remain.
4. Add the chilled, thinly sliced unsalted butter. Mix until the dough is smooth and can be pulled into a thick membrane with jagged edges.
5. Remove the dough and divide it into two equal portions: keep one as plain dough. Return the other to the mixer, add the diluted seaweed powder, and mix on low speed until evenly incorporated to make seaweed-flavored dough.
6. Shape each dough into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
7. Divide each dough into 8 equal pieces, preserving the smooth surface. For each piece, press out, fold, and shape into a smooth rectangle. With the smooth side facing down, roll into a cylinder, then taper one end to form a water-drop shape.
8. Place the shaped water-drop dough pieces on a baking tray, cover, and refrigerate for 30 minutes to relax.
9. Remove the dough. Gently elongate by hand, then use a rolling pin to roll from the thick end toward the pointed end, stretching the tail as you go, to form a thin, even inverted triangle approximately 55cm long. Place one strip of salted butter at the wide end. Optionally, brush a little softened butter on the sides of the dough.
   - **Key**: Keep the dough relaxed to avoid shrinkage; apply even pressure while rolling to maintain uniform thickness.
10. Loosely roll up from the butter end, without stretching, and place seam-side down.
    - **Condition**: The finished roll should have clear spiral lines.
11. For seaweed-flavored rolls, dip the bottom of the rolled dough in water, then coat generously with white sesame seeds.
12. Place in a proofing box at 30-35°C for final proofing until significantly puffed up.
    - **Note**: Proofing temperature must not exceed 35°C, or the butter inside will melt.
13. Preheat the oven. For a home oven, set top heat to 230°C and bottom heat to 200°C. For a convection oven, set to 210°C. Bake for approximately 12-15 minutes until the surface is golden brown.
    - **Key**: Bake at high heat for a short time to prevent the bread from drying out.
14. Once baked, transfer to a cooling rack to cool.

## Health Tips
These salt rolls contain a relatively high amount of butter and refined flour, making them calorie-dense. They are suitable for active young people or as an occasional energy boost. Be careful not to let the proofing temperature exceed 35°C, or the butter will melt prematurely, affecting the layered texture.
