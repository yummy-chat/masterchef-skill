Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish

Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Cooking Steps  
1. Chop the pork ribs into pieces and wash them clean. Soak the dried bamboo fungus in advance. Peel the Chinese yam and cut it into 3–4 cm long sections.  
   **Note**: It is recommended to wear disposable gloves when handling Chinese yam to prevent skin irritation from the mucus; use scissors to cut the bamboo fungus into small sections to maintain their shape.  
2. Place the prepared pork ribs and Chinese yam sections into the inner pot of the electric hot pot. Add enough water to submerge all the ingredients.  
3. Cover the lid and start the stewing function. Cook until the ribs become tender and the soup turns milky white.  
   **Key**: Try not to add water midway during stewing. If the water level becomes too low, replenish with hot water. The bamboo fungus should be added shortly before the soup is done, then simmer for a few more minutes.  
4. Before serving, taste the soup with a spoon. Add salt according to your preference, stir well, and then ladle the soup into bowls.  
   **Key**: Adding salt at the end allows better control of the saltiness and preserves the freshness of the soup.  

## Health Tips  
This soup is rich in collagen and dietary fiber, suitable for those who need calcium supplementation or enhanced immunity. However, pork ribs are high in fat; skimming off the floating oil before drinking makes it healthier. The combination of Chinese yam and bamboo fungus increases satiety, so the amount of ribs can be reduced during weight loss.
