# Taro Balls with Grass Jelly
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🎃 Pumpkin, to taste (peeled and cut into pieces, used for making yellow taro balls)
🐟 Taro, to taste (peeled and cut into pieces, used for making taro-flavored taro balls)
🍠 Purple sweet potato, to taste (peeled and cut into pieces, used for making purple taro balls)
🌾 Tapioca starch, to taste (mashed ingredient: tapioca starch ≈ 1:0.7)
💧 Water, to taste (to adjust dough consistency)
🍮 Grass jelly powder, to taste (for brewing)
🥛 Milk, to taste (to serve with grass jelly and taro balls)
🍯 Sugar substitute syrup, to taste (added when kneading the taro ball dough; other sugar can be used as a substitute)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🍳 Steamer, stove
🥘 Cooking pot, stove
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Spatula or knife
📦 Plastic storage bag
🥛 Measuring cup (glass, for soaking taro balls in ice water)
🥢 Food tongs
🍽️ Bowl, spoon

## Instructions
1. Peel the pumpkin, taro, and purple sweet potato. Cut them into thick slices or chunks.
**Note**: Cut them thinner to ensure they steam more easily.

2. Place the cut root vegetables into a steamer and steam until soft.
**Note**: They are done when a chopstick can easily pierce through them.

3. Mash the steamed taro or purple sweet potato into a puree. Gradually add tapioca starch to the bowl while kneading.
**Note**: The recommended ratio is mashed ingredient: tapioca starch ≈ 1:0.7. If the dough is too dry, add water as needed.

4. Take about one-third of the kneaded dough and cook it in boiling water until it floats and becomes translucent.
**Key point**: Remove the cooked portion and return it to the remaining raw dough. Knead them together. This increases the dough's elasticity and prevents the taro balls from cracking.

5. Repeat the same process with the pumpkin puree to obtain three colored doughs.
**Note**: Pumpkin has a higher water content, so adjust the amount of starch added based on the actual consistency.

6. Roll each of the three colored doughs into long strips.
7. Use a spatula or knife to cut the long strips into small, evenly sized pieces. These are the raw taro balls.
**Note**: Dust them with some tapioca starch to prevent sticking.

8. Place the cut taro balls into a plastic storage bag. Seal and store in the freezer for later use.
9. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the raw taro balls. Cook until they all float to the surface, then cook for an additional 1–2 minutes.
10. Remove the cooked taro balls and immediately soak them in prepared ice water.
**Key point**: Soaking in ice water gives the taro balls a chewier and more elastic texture.

11. Place the brewed and set grass jelly into a bowl. Add the drained taro balls. Pour in an appropriate amount of milk.

## Health Tips
This taro ball and grass jelly dessert is rich in dietary fiber and antioxidants, making it suitable as a summer refreshing snack. However, note that tapioca starch can cause a rapid rise in blood sugar. It is recommended to pair it with milk and a sugar substitute to help control blood sugar fluctuations.
