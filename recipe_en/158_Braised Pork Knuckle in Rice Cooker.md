# Braised Pork Knuckle in Rice Cooker
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 1 pork knuckle (front knuckle is leaner, back knuckle is fattier, choose according to preference)
🧅 3-4 scallions
🫚 1 large piece of ginger (smashed)
🍊 A small amount of dried tangerine peel (optional)
🧂 About 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce (soup spoon)
🧂 About half to 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce (combined with light soy sauce to make one full tablespoon)
🍬 About half a tablespoon of rock sugar
🍶 About 3 tablespoons of yellow rice wine (or cooking wine)
🧂 2-3 grams of salt (optional, add as needed after the stew comes to a boil)

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Rice cooker (or electric rice cooker)
🍳 Soup pot (for blanching)
🔪 Knife or scraper
🥄 Soup spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Burn the skin to remove gamey smell**: Use a torch to burn the skin side of the pork knuckle until black. The blacker the burn, the better the gamey smell removal.
2. **Scrape clean**: Soak the burned knuckle in warm water for 10 minutes, then gently scrape off the charred layer with a knife until the skin shows a clean yellow color. This step removes about 90% of the off-flavors.
3. **Blanch to set shape**: Place the cleaned knuckle in a pot of cold water, add enough water to cover, bring to a boil over high heat, and continue cooking until the outer layer is fully cooked to remove any odors from the surface blood. Since the knuckle is large, it does not need to be fully blanched through.
4. **Layer the base ingredients**: Thickly layer scallion sections, smashed ginger pieces, and a little dried tangerine peel at the bottom of the rice cooker inner pot. This prevents sticking and adds fragrance.
5. **Place the knuckle**: Put the blanched knuckle on top of the scallions and ginger.
6. **Add seasonings**: Drizzle in the mixed soy sauce of light and dark soy sauces, add rock sugar, then pour in about 3 tablespoons of yellow rice wine.
7. **Add water**: Pour enough **boiling water** into the pot, making sure the water level **completely submerges the knuckle**.
   **Key point**: The pork skin must never be exposed above the water surface during stewing, or it will become hard and tough to chew; ensure the entire skin remains submerged in the liquid until fully tender.
8. **Stew**: Close the rice cooker lid, select the rice cooking or stewing function, and heat for 2 hours.
9. **Season**: Open the lid and taste. If the flavor is too light, add 2-3 grams of salt.
10. **Let marinate**: After 2 hours, turn off the heat, let the knuckle cool naturally in the pot, and marinate overnight to thoroughly absorb the flavors and reach a soft, tender state.
11. **Reheat before serving**: Before eating, reheat the rice cooker, remove the cooked scallions and ginger, heat the knuckle thoroughly, and then serve.

## Health Tips
Pork knuckle is rich in collagen, suitable for those needing collagen supplementation and skin nourishment, but it has high fat content, so consumption should be controlled. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance the richness, and avoid overeating it at dinner.
