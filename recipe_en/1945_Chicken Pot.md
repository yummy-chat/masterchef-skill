# Chicken Pot

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh meat: appropriate amount (cut into pieces, pre-marinated)  
🥬 Green vegetables: appropriate amount (choose your preferred variety)  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms: appropriate amount  
🥚 Quail eggs: appropriate amount  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms: appropriate amount  
🍜 Instant noodles: 1 pack  
🧂 Light soy sauce: 4 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce: 1 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce: 1 tablespoon  
🫘 Sweet bean paste: 1 large tablespoon  
🌶️ White pepper powder: 1 teaspoon  
🌶️ Pixian broad bean paste: appropriate amount  
🍲 Hot pot base: appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount  
💧 Hot water: appropriate amount  
🧄 Scallion, ginger, garlic: appropriate amount  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spoon  
🍽️ Pot lid  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the chicken thigh meat into small pieces, place them in a bowl, add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sweet bean paste, and white pepper powder. Mix well and marinate for 20 minutes.  
   **Note**: Ensure each piece of chicken is evenly coated with the sauce, and the marinating time is sufficient.  
2. Wash and prepare the green vegetables, enoki mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, and other side ingredients in advance. Add quick-cooking greens last.  
3. Heat the wok, then pour in cold oil. Add the marinated chicken pieces and stir-fry until they are completely changed in color. Remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: Do not stir-fry for too long to avoid the chicken becoming tough and dry.  
4. In another wok, heat oil and add scallion, ginger, garlic, Pixian broad bean paste, and hot pot base. Stir-fry over low heat for about 1–2 minutes until red oil is released and the aroma is rich.  
   **Note**: Must use low heat to prevent burning, which can create a bitter taste.  
5. Add the previously stir-fried chicken pieces, mix well, then add hot water. Cover with a lid and simmer over medium-low heat for 20 minutes until the soup is boiling and the chicken is fully flavored.  
   **Note**: Add boiling water instead of cold water to prevent the chicken from shrinking and becoming tough upon contact with cold liquid.  
6. Add the prepared green vegetables, enoki mushrooms, quail eggs, shiitake mushrooms, and other side ingredients. Continue to simmer over medium-low heat for 5 minutes, then add salt to taste.  
   **Note**: Add quick-cooking side ingredients last to prevent them from becoming mushy.  
7. Add one block of instant noodles, cover with the lid, and continue to simmer over medium-low heat for 5 minutes until the noodle block softens and fully absorbs the soup.  
   **Note**: Add the instant noodles last to avoid them becoming overcooked and mushy if added too early.  
8. Serve immediately. You can mix the thick soup and ingredients with rice.  

## Health Advice  
This dish contains protein, vegetables, and carbohydrates, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during muscle-building or fat-loss periods. However, the hot pot base and instant noodles result in high sodium and calorie content, so it is recommended to consume less of the soup.
