# Clear Dumplings  
Cuisine Category: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 500g all-purpose or high-gluten flour  
🌽 30g cornstarch or potato starch (to enhance transparency)  
🥬 Celery, to taste (diced)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste (diced)  
🍄 Wood ear mushroom, to taste (finely chopped)  
🍖 Ham sausage, to taste (diced)  
🥚 Eggs, to taste (scrambled and finely chopped)  
🧂 3g salt (added to the flour)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for filling seasoning)  
🫗 1 tablespoon seasoned oil  
🍗 Chicken bouillon powder, to taste  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, to taste  
💧 220-230ml warm water  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🪓 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place 500g all-purpose flour, 3g salt, and 30g cornstarch into a bowl, and mix thoroughly.  
   **Key Point**: The starch is the core ingredient for making the dumpling skin transparent.  
2. Gradually add 220–230ml warm water, stirring as you go until the mixture becomes flaky, then knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Key Point**: Warm water slightly gelatinizes the starch, making the dumpling skin more transparent after cooking.  
3. Seal the dough and let it rest for 15 minutes to fully relax the gluten and enhance elasticity.  
4. Prepare the filling: Dice the celery, carrot, wood ear mushroom, and ham sausage. Scramble the eggs and chop finely. Mix all ingredients in a bowl.  
   **Key Point**: First, add one tablespoon of seasoned oil and mix thoroughly to seal the cut surfaces of the vegetables, preventing water release during subsequent seasoning that could cause the skin to break.  
5. Add salt, chicken bouillon powder, thirteen-spice powder, and other seasonings to the oil-sealed filling, and stir evenly.  
   **Key Point**: Season simply to highlight the natural flavors of the ingredients.  
6. Roll the rested dough into a long strip, divide into equal-sized portions, and use a rolling pin to flatten each into a thin round wrapper.  
   **Key Point**: The wrapper should be rolled slightly thin; otherwise, the final transparency will be insufficient.  
7. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center of each wrapper, then pinch and press the edges together using the tiger’s mouth of both hands to form the dumplings.  
8. Bring water in a pot to a rolling boil, add the dumplings, and cook until they all float and the skin turns semi-transparent, then remove.  
   **Doneness Check**: The fully cooked dumpling skin changes from opaque to transparent, allowing the color of the filling to be faintly visible.  

## Health Tips  
Clear dumplings, made with starch to achieve a translucent skin, feature a primarily vegetable-based filling that is light and low in fat, making them suitable for weight-loss diets. Be mindful to control the amount of salt used to prevent water retention.
