# Sandpot Pepper Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken thighs 4 pieces (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Onions 3 pieces (cut into pieces)  
🫚 Ginger 2 stalks (cut into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic 2 heads (cut into pieces)  
🧂 White pepper powder 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Black pepper powder 1 tablespoon  
🍬 White sugar a pinch  
🌶️ Dried chili segments to taste  
🧅 Scallion whites to taste  
🫒 Hot oil 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Light soy sauce 4-5 tablespoons  
🍶 Rice wine or sake drizzle a circle  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1/2 tablespoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫕 Sandpot  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Measuring spoons  
🥣 Bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the chicken thighs into pieces and soak in clean water for half an hour to remove blood and gamey smell. Drain and set aside.  
2. Cut the onion, ginger, and garlic into pieces. Place white pepper powder, black pepper powder, a pinch of sugar, dried chili segments, and scallion whites in a bowl. Drizzle with oil heated until smoking to release the aroma. Then add 4-5 tablespoons of light soy sauce and mix well to make the pepper sauce.  
3. Heat the sandpot with oil, add the onion pieces, ginger pieces, and garlic pieces. Stir-fry until the edges are slightly golden and the aroma is released.  
4. Spread the drained chicken pieces evenly over the base ingredients. Drizzle a circle of rice wine or sake along the edge of the pot, cover, and cook for 5 minutes.  
5. Remove the lid, pour in the prepared pepper sauce, and spread evenly over the chicken surface. Cover and continue to cook for 30 minutes.  
   **Note**: If the liquid increases during cooking, open the lid and turn the chicken pieces so they are evenly coated with the sauce.  
6. In the last 10 minutes, add 1/2 tablespoon of dark soy sauce for color, and continue cooking until the chicken is infused with flavor and the sauce has thickened.  
7. Turn off the heat, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This pepper chicken is rich in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those needing to warm the body and stomach. Pepper is pungent and hot in nature, which can easily cause internal heat. Those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
