# French Butter Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour, as needed  
🥚 Whole egg, about 1 (use whole egg liquid for brushing)  
🥚 Egg yolk, 1  
🥛 Milk, as needed  
🧈 Butter, as needed (softened, added in batches)  
🍬 White sugar, as needed  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍞 Stand mixer (with paddle attachment)  
🥄 Spatula  
Rolling pin  
🍞 Pound cake pan (without holes, note removal)  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🖌️ Brush  
🔥 Oven  
🔪 Bread knife  

## Instructions  
1. Add bread flour, yeast, white sugar, salt, whole egg liquid, egg yolk, and milk in order to the stand mixer bowl.  
   **Note**: Different flours have varying water absorption; adjust the milk amount as needed to achieve a soft, moderately firm dough.  
2. Mix on low speed until combined, then increase to medium-high speed and knead until the dough begins to develop gluten.  
3. Add the softened butter in batches, mixing on low speed after each addition until incorporated, then increase to medium-high speed until the dough is smooth, elastic, and can be pulled into a thin film.  
   **Key**: Butter must be added in batches; do not add all at once, as this will hinder gluten development. This is crucial for the success of the butter toast.  
4. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it undergo the first fermentation.  
   **Doneness Check**: Ferment until the dough has doubled in volume, about 1 hour.  
5. Remove the fermented dough, gently deflate, divide, round, rest, then roll out and shape.  
   **Note**: This recipe uses a pound cake pan. The pan is non-stick without holes; you can brush a thin layer of butter or line with parchment paper to facilitate removal.  
6. Place the shaped dough into the pan and let it undergo the second fermentation.  
   **Doneness Check**: Ferment until the dough reaches 80–90% full of the pan.  
7. Brush the surface of the fermented dough evenly with whole egg liquid.  
8. Bake in a preheated oven, adjusting temperature and time based on your oven’s characteristics, until the surface is deep golden brown and the inside is fully cooked.  
9. Remove from the pan immediately after baking and cool completely on a wire rack.  
   **Note**: Let it cool completely before slicing with a bread knife; otherwise, the internal structure may stick, affecting texture and appearance.  

## Health Advice  
This butter toast is high in carbs and fat, suitable for replenishing energy after exercise, but it should not be consumed regularly as a daily breakfast to avoid increasing waist circumference.
