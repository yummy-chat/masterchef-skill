# Earl Grey Swiss Roll 🍰
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Year Dining

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Whipping Cream 100g (for cream base) + 230g (for filling)
🍂 Earl Grey Tea Bags 2g × 4 bags (for cream base, milk tea, and cake batter)
🥛 Milk 50g (20g of tea-infused milk used after boiling)
🥚 Egg Whites 170g (frozen until edges have a thin layer of ice)
🥚 Egg Yolks 85g
🥛 Sour Cream 70g (key to cake softness; try not to substitute)
🌽 Corn Oil 50g
🌾 Cake Flour 70g
🍬 White Sugar 75g (caster sugar can be substituted)
🧂 Sea Salt 1.5g (or table salt 0.8g)
🍋 Lemon Juice A few drops (can substitute with white vinegar)
🍬 Powdered Sugar 18g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥘 Heavy-bottomed Pot
🔌 Electric Hand Mixer
🪢 28×28cm Baking Tray
📒 Parchment Paper / Silicone Mat
🧴 100-Mesh Sieve
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🍶 Glass Bowl
🪅 Rolling Pin
📏 Bench Scraper / Spatula
🌡️ Food Thermometer

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. **Make Earl Grey Cream Base**
   Add 100g whipping cream to a heavy-bottomed pot. Open 1 tea bag and pour the leaves in. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then remove from heat and cover with a lid for 5 minutes. Pour out to cool, cover with plastic wrap, and freeze for 2 hours or refrigerate overnight.
   **Note**: Steeping allows the tea flavor to fully infuse the cream; thorough cooling helps with stable whipping later.

2. **Make Earl Grey Milk**
   Add 50g milk to a heavy-bottomed pot. Open 1 tea bag and pour the leaves in. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat and cover for 5 minutes. Pour out to cool, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 2 hours or prepare the night before. Strain before use, reserving 20g of the tea-infused milk.
   **Note**: Straining removes tea leaves for a smooth cake batter texture.

### Cake Batter Preparation
1. **Make Egg Yolk Mixture**
   - In a small bowl, combine 20g tea-infused milk, 70g sour cream, and 50g corn oil. Whisk with a hand whisk until fully emulsified.
   - Pour the emulsion into 85g egg yolks and mix well.
   - Sift in 70g cake flour and the leaves from 1 tea bag. Mix using a zigzag stir method until combined. Pass the entire mixture through a sieve once, then add the leaves from another tea bag and mix well.
   - Cover with plastic wrap and keep warm over 50°C hot water.
   **Note**: Sour cream is essential for cake softness; try not to substitute. The zigzag stir prevents gluten development; straining yields a finer batter.

2. **Whip Egg Whites**
   - Remove 170g egg whites (frozen until ice forms on edges). Add a few drops of lemon juice and 1.5g sea salt.
   - Whip on high speed with an electric mixer until large bubbles form. Add 1/3 of the white sugar. Continue whipping on high until fine foam appears; add another 1/3 sugar. Whip until soft peaks form, then add remaining sugar. Switch to low speed and whip until the whisk forms a small hook when lifted.
   **Note**: Frozen egg whites whip into a finer, more stable foam. Adding sugar in stages strengthens the meringue. Final low-speed mixing removes large bubbles and prevents over-whipping, which can cause cracking when rolling.

3. **Fold Meringue into Yolk Mixture**
   - Add 1/3 of the meringue to the yolk mixture. Cut and fold with a spatula, then mix using the chiffon folding method for about 20 strokes, leaving a marbled texture.
   - Pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue. Continue cutting and folding for about 10 more strokes. Total mixing should not exceed 30 strokes.
   **Note**: Do not stir in circles to avoid deflating; the marbled look helps keep the cake light.

4. **Bake the Cake Sheet**
   - Pour the batter from a height into a 28×28cm tray lined with parchment paper. Spread evenly with a spatula and tap the tray twice to release air bubbles.
   - Bake in the middle rack at 190°C for 10 minutes, then reduce to 150°C for 20 minutes.
   **Note**: Pouring from a height helps eliminate small bubbles; initial high heat sets the shape, then lower heat prevents shrinkage and cracks. Tap the tray and slide the cake out of the tray immediately after baking.

### Assembly & Finishing
1. **Whip Earl Grey Cream**
   - Strain the chilled Earl Grey cream base through a 100-mesh sieve. Add 230g whipping cream and 18g powdered sugar. Whip with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.
   **Note**: Straining removes tea leaf bits for a smooth texture; whipping over ice water helps stabilize the cream.

2. **Assemble the Roll**
   - Let the cake cool to hand temperature. Invert it onto a large piece of parchment paper, peel off the baking liner, then invert again onto another piece of parchment for rolling.
   - Pile the cream in a mound on the front third of the cake. Spread the remaining cream thinly over the rest.
   - Use a fixed object to brace the front edge. With the help of a rolling pin, roll the cake tightly. Tighten the roll with a bench scraper. Refrigerate for 1–2 hours before slicing.
   **Note**: Roll with the textured side facing down; the cream mound helps create a tight roll. Chilling before slicing yields clean cuts.

## Health Tips
This Earl Grey Swiss Roll offers a unique flavor pairing of sour cream and tea, but the whipping cream and powdered sugar contribute to higher calorie content. Enjoy as an afternoon treat in moderation, paired with unsweetened tea to balance the sweetness. The cake sheet is soft and delicately tea-scented, ideal for those who enjoy light, creamy desserts. Be careful not to over-whip the egg whites to prevent cracks during rolling.
