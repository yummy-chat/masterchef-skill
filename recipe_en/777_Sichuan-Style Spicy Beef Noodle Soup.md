# Sichuan-Style Spicy Beef Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐂 Fresh beef brisket 2000g (un-injected with water, cut into pieces)
🍜 Alkaline noodles 300g
🥬 Water spinach appropriate amount (can be substituted with preferred vegetables)
🫚 Ginger 1 large piece (cut into thick slices)
🧅 Scallions 2 stalks (cut into sections)
⭐ Star anise 3 pieces
🌿 Cinnamon stick 2 small pieces
⭐ Sand ginger 2 pieces
🌸 Cloves a few pieces (do not use too much, to avoid bitterness)
🌱 Cumin seeds 1 small handful
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching) + 30g (for stir-frying)
🍬 Caramel color appropriate amount (for coloring, can substitute with dark soy sauce)
🥜 Sesame paste 10g (can substitute with 2 pieces of fermented bean curd)
🫒 Base oil appropriate amount
💧 Water appropriate amount (for blanching, simmering, and cooking noodles)
🍲 Clear broth 350g (can substitute with water)
🥄 Soy sauce a little
🌶️ Sichuan spicy hotpot base 30g (can substitute with chili flakes or Sichuan peppercorns)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Round cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Chinese wok (iron wok)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🔥 Gas stove
🍜 Bowl

## Steps
1.  Cut the beef brisket into 4 large pieces, place in a bowl, add enough water to soak for 60 minutes to fully remove blood, changing the water 1–2 times during this process.
**Note**: Must use un-injected beef brisket, ensure sufficient soaking time.

2.  Slice ginger into thick slices, cut scallions into sections; place star anise, cinnamon stick, sand ginger, cloves, and cumin seeds in a bowl and set aside.
**Note**: Do not use too many cloves, otherwise the broth will become bitter.

3.  Add water to a pot, put in the soaked beef brisket, bring to a boil over high heat, then add cooking wine, ginger slices, and scallion sections. Blanch for 10 minutes, skimming off any foam. Remove the beef brisket and rinse clean with water, discard the ginger and scallions, then cut the brisket into evenly sized small cubes.
**Note**: If using frozen beef brisket, ensure it is cooked through thoroughly. After blanching, be sure to wash off any foam from the surface.

4.  Add base oil to the wok. When the oil reaches 50% heat, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and the spices. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add the beef brisket cubes and stir-fry over medium-low heat for 5 minutes, until the brisket is dry and the aroma is released. Add caramel color and stir-fry to color the meat. Pour 30g of cooking wine along the edge of the wok and stir well. Finally, add sesame paste and mix well.
**Note**: If caramel color is unavailable, substitute with dark soy sauce. Ensure the beef brisket is thoroughly stir-fried to dry out and release its fragrance.

5.  Add enough water to the wok to cover the beef brisket, bring to a boil over high heat. After 10 minutes, remove the ginger and scallion sections. Reduce to low heat and continue simmering for 30 minutes. Once done, remove the beef brisket and broth and set aside.
**Note**: Ginger and scallions should not be simmered for too long, otherwise the broth will become sour.

6.  In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the alkaline noodles and cook until just done, then quickly remove and place into a bowl.
**Note**: Alkaline noodles should not be boiled for too long, otherwise they will lose their chewy texture.

7.  Heat a little oil in a wok over medium heat. When the oil reaches 40% heat, add the broken-up hotpot base. Stir-fry over low heat until completely melted.
**Note**: Must keep the heat low to prevent the base from burning and turning bitter.

8.  Add the simmered beef brisket and stir-fry evenly. Pour in 350g of clear broth, bring to a boil over high heat, and add a little soy sauce to season.
9.  Add the water spinach to the soup base and blanch until just cooked. Remove and place on top of the noodles. Then ladle the beef brisket and broth into the bowl.
**Note**: Blanch the vegetables just until done to maintain a crisp and tender texture.

## Health Advice
Beef brisket is rich in protein and iron, while alkaline noodles provide carbohydrates. However, the Sichuan spicy hotpot base and the beef brisket itself have a relatively high fat content, so it is recommended to control the portion size. This dish is suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or needing to ward off cold. At the same time, be mindful of reducing oil and sodium intake. Pairing with water spinach can help supplement dietary fiber.
