# Coral Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Grass carp, one fish (about 1.5 kg)  
🌽 Cornstarch, one large bowl (mixed with potato starch)  
🥔 Potato starch, one large bowl (mix with cornstarch in a ratio of about 3:7)  
🧂 Table salt, about one small teaspoon (for marinating) + a pinch (for the sauce)  
🍶 Yellow rice wine,适量 (for marinating, can substitute with cooking wine)  
🥫 Tomato ketchup, 300 grams  
🍶 White vinegar, 40 grams  
🍬 Granulated sugar, 120 grams  
💧 Water,适量 (to dilute the sauce)  
🥣 Water starch, a small amount added in batches (for thickening)  
🫧 Clarified oil, a little (for gloss)  
🌿 Cilantro, a small amount (for garnish)  
🧅 Shredded scallion, a small amount (for garnish)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Cleaver, small pointed knife, slicing knife (for fish processing: chopping bones, descaling, slicing and shredding meat)  
🪵 Large round wooden cutting board  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and marinating the fish)  
🧻 Pure white kitchen towel (to pat dry the fish surface)  
🥘 Rectangular stainless steel tray (for marinating and coating)  
🍳 Large wok (for deep frying)  
🥄 Wok spatula / ladle (for scooping oil, stirring sauce)  
🥢 Strainer / slotted spoon (for removing fried fish)  
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl (for mixing marinade and sauce)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring seasonings)  
🍽️ White long platter (for final plating)  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Stun the grass carp by hitting its head, then make a horizontal cut at the neck to sever the nerve. Scrape off the scales, gut the fish removing internal organs and gills, wash thoroughly, and drain.
    **Note**: Cutting the neck prevents the fish from jumping; scales and innards must be completely removed.

2.  Fillet the fish along the backbone, removing the two skin-on fillets. Reserve the head and bones for other uses.
    **Note**: When removing the fillets, the knife should slide down against the ribs.

3.  Place the fish fillet skin-side down on the cutting board. Using a slicing knife at a 20–25 degree angle, make cuts 1 cm apart down to, but not through, the skin. Complete the first angled slicing.
    **Note**: Fish slices should be about 3 mm thick, do not go thinner than 3 mm or they will break easily. Cut down to the skin without cutting through it.

4.  Turn the angled-cut fish fillet over so the flesh side faces up. Starting the knife from the heel (the part closest to the handle), push forward to cut the flesh into thin shreds, completing the second cutting step.
    **Note**: The heel of the knife should hit the cutting board first. The knife tip must push against the skin while cutting for a beautiful finished "coral" shape.

5.  Gently rinse the cut fish in clean water to remove blood and loose meat scraps. Remove and wrap in a clean kitchen towel, gently pressing to absorb excess moisture.
    **Note**: Rinsing removes fishy odors and debris; drying thoroughly helps the marinade penetrate.

6.  In a bowl, mix one small teaspoon of salt with an appropriate amount of yellow rice wine until dissolved. Evenly drizzle this mixture over the dried fish fillet and marinate for two minutes.
    **Note**: Do not add black pepper, as it will create a burnt/bitter taste in the finished dish.

7.  In a bowl, successively add 300 grams of tomato ketchup, 40 grams of white vinegar, and 120 grams of granulated sugar. Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved, then mix in a pinch of salt.
    **Note**: Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved.

8.  In a large bowl, combine one large bowl of cornstarch with one large bowl of potato starch in an approximate ratio of 3:7. Mix well and set aside.
    **Note**: Potato starch helps keep the fish meat crispy for a longer time.

9.  Place the marinated fish fillet into the mixed starch, ensuring every single "coral shred" and the skin surface are evenly coated with the starch mixture.
    **Note**: Coating must be thorough; don't miss any parts. Ensure even coverage.

10. Heat the large wok and add a generous amount of oil (about 5 kg). Heat the oil to 160°C (6/10 heat). Holding the fish fillet by both ends, gently shake off excess starch and carefully lower it into the hot oil. Hold it by both ends to fry until slightly set, then release it completely. Keep the heat low for the first two minutes, then increase to medium heat for about another minute, allowing the oil temperature to rise by about 15°C. Total frying time is about three minutes, until the fish is golden brown and set. Remove and drain on a rack or plate.
    **Note**: Oil temperature control is crucial. Lower the fish into the oil holding it by both ends to set the shape. Drain after frying.

11. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Pour in the prepared tomato sauce mixture. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then add some water to dilute. Once boiling again, remove the wok from the heat. Add the water starch in several small batches, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens to the desired consistency. Return the wok to the heat and bring the sauce to a boil.
    **Note**: When thickening, remove the wok from the heat. Add water starch in small batches to avoid lumps or the sauce becoming too thick. The sauce should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.

12. Once the sauce is boiling, drizzle in the clarified oil in two parts. Quickly and evenly pour the hot sauce over the plated fried coral fish. Garnish with cilantro or shredded scallion. Serve immediately.
    **Note**: The sauce must be poured on while hot to release the aroma and keep the fish crispy.

## Health Advice  
This dish uses grass carp, which is high in protein and low in fat, paired with a tomato-based sauce that helps stimulate the appetite. Because it employs deep-frying and a generous amount of sweet and sour sauce, the overall calorie content is relatively high. It is recommended to control portion sizes and pair it with stir-fried vegetables to balance the nutrition. Suitable for festive gatherings, but those on a weight loss or blood sugar management plan should consume it in moderation.
