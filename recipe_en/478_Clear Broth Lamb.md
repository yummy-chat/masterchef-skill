# Clear Broth Lamb
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb spine 1 portion (cut into large pieces)  
🥩 Skin-on lamb (belly meat) 1 portion (cut into large pieces)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 1 piece  
🫘 Red dates several pieces  
🌿 Chinese angelica several slices  
🎋 Sugarcane 1 section (cut into segments and flattened)  
🌰 Water chestnuts several (flattened)  
🥕 Carrot 1 (cut into rolling knife pieces)  
🌿 Amomum villosum 1 cluster (about 5-6 pieces)  
🍋 Lemon leaves 2 leaves (torn)  
🫚 Ginger slices 3-4 slices  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating lamb bones and final seasoning)  
🍶 Rice wine appropriate amount (for blanching to remove gamey smell)  
🫘 Fermented bean curd 2 pieces (for dipping sauce)  
🥜 Peanut oil appropriate amount (to dissolve fermented bean curd and adjust dipping sauce)  
🧄 Raw garlic about 3-4 cloves (minced)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers 3-4 (cut into rings)  
🌊 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Wok  
🫕 Large stainless steel soup pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon, soup ladle  
🥣 Bowls, small dishes  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare Main Ingredients**: Cut the lamb spine and skin-on lamb into large pieces. Burn the lamb skin over fire until slightly charred to remove any off-flavors and enhance aroma.  
**Note**: The meat pieces should be large to prevent excessive shrinkage during stewing.  
2. **Marinate Lamb Bones**: Sprinkle an appropriate amount of salt over the lamb bones, mix well and marinate to draw out excess fat and blood.  
3. **Blanch**: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add ginger slices and rice wine. After the water boils, first add the lamb bones, cook until foam overflows, then remove. Then add the lamb pieces, cook until the color changes, then remove.  
**Key**: Blanching separately removes impurities more thoroughly and reduces the gamey smell.  
4. **Rinse**: Thoroughly rinse the blanched lamb bones and lamb pieces under running water, drain and set aside.  
5. **Prepare Accompaniments**: Cut the sugarcane into segments and flatten, flatten the water chestnuts, cut the carrot into rolling knife pieces, tear the lemon leaves.  
6. **Make Soup Base**: Put the rinsed lamb bones and lamb pieces into the soup pot, add enough clean water, then add dried tangerine peel, red dates, Chinese angelica, sugarcane, water chestnuts, carrot, Amomum villosum and lemon leaves. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
**Key**: Add enough water at once to avoid adding water midway, which could affect the soup's color.  
7. **Slow Simmer to Extract Flavor**: After the soup boils, turn to low heat and slowly simmer for about 1 hour, until the soup gradually turns white and the aroma of bone and meat is released.  
8. **Add Lamb and Continue Simmering**: After 1 hour, use a slotted spoon to remove most of the lamb bones and spices. Add the previously blanched lamb pieces, continue to simmer over medium-low heat for about 45 minutes.  
**Note**: Adjust the time according to the desired texture. 45 minutes yields a tender and springy texture. If you prefer a softer texture, you can turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 15 minutes.  
9. **Make Dipping Sauces**:  
   - Fermented Bean Curd and Lemon Leaf Sauce: Put 2 pieces of fermented bean curd into a small bowl, add a small amount of peanut oil to dissolve, add finely shredded lemon leaves and mix well.  
- Garlic and Chili Sauce: Put minced raw garlic and small chili pepper rings into a bowl, add an appropriate amount of peanut oil and light soy sauce, mix well.  
10. **Season and Finish**: After the soup is done, add an appropriate amount of salt to taste according to your preference. The soup should be milky white and richly aromatic. Serve with the dipping sauces for an enhanced flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This clear broth lamb, using sugarcane and water chestnuts to counteract the richness of the lamb, is a nourishing winter delicacy that is less likely to cause internal heat, especially suitable for those with cold hands and feet. Lamb is rich in protein, but the skin-on part has a relatively high fat content, so floating oil intake should be controlled. Thorough blanching to remove the gamey smell is key to the soup's clear and sweet flavor.
