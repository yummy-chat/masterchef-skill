# Dried Apricots
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍑 Apricots 4 jin (about 2 kg, choose ones that are not too soft, as overly soft ones may easily turn mushy when cooked)
🍬 White sugar 400 grams

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🍲 Stainless steel pot
🥢 Chopsticks
♨️ Baking sheet, parchment paper
🔥 Oven (optional, for drying)

## Steps
1. Wash the apricots, cut them in half and remove the pits, then cut each half into four wedges.
2. Place the apricot flesh in a large bowl, add the white sugar, and mix well, ensuring each piece of fruit is coated with sugar.
3. Cover with plastic wrap and seal to marinate for at least 5 hours, until the sugar has completely dissolved and the apricots have released their juice.
4. Pour the marinated apricots along with the sugar syrup into a pot. No additional water is needed.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat. Keep the boil for 1 minute, then remove from heat.
6. Cover the pot and let the apricots soak in the sugar syrup overnight.
7. After soaking, use chopsticks to pick out the apricot pieces and arrange them neatly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or on a ventilated drying rack. If sun-drying, cover with a mosquito net.
8. Drying process:
   - Method 1: Dry under direct sunlight.
   - Method 2: Set the oven to the dehydrate function. Bake at 70°C for about 10 hours, until the apricot flesh has lost moisture, shrunk, and reached a chewy, dry consistency.

## Health Tips
Dried apricots are rich in dietary fiber and vitamin A, which aid digestion and support eye health. However, they are high in sugar, so it is recommended to consume only 3–4 pieces per serving. Diabetic patients should consume with caution. Homemade versions without additives are healthier than store-bought ones, but portion control is still important.
