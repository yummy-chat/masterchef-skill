# Steamed Chicken in Its Own Juices (Sauna Chicken)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Chicken 1/2 (deboned and sliced, choose tender chicken)
🍄 Cordyceps Flowers (Cordyceps militaris) About 300g
🍈 Winter Melon About 300g (peeled and cut into thick slices)
🥬 Pea Shoots Appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions Appropriate amount (chopped)
🦴 Chicken Carcass (for making broth)
🧂 Light Soy Sauce About 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster Sauce About 1 tablespoon
🌿 White Pepper Powder A pinch
🥔 Cornstarch A pinch
🫚 Ginger Slices 3-4 slices
🧂 Salt Appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking Oil Appropriate amount

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🫕 Clay Pot / Casserole
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🥄 Measuring Spoons

## Cooking Instructions
### Preparation
- Debone the chicken and slice the meat; wash the chicken carcass separately.
- Peel the winter melon and cut into 0.5 cm thick slices. Wash and drain the cordyceps flowers and pea shoots. Chop the scallions.

### Marinating the Chicken
1. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper powder, cornstarch, and ginger slices to the chicken slices. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes.
   **Note**: Cornstarch helps lock in moisture, making the chicken more tender.

### Making Chicken Broth
2. Place the chicken carcass in a stockpot, add ginger slices and enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.
3. Once the broth is ready, add the pea shoots and blanch until just cooked. Season with salt, then turn off the heat.
   **Key Point**: Add the pea shoots at the very end to maintain their tender texture.

### Steaming the Sauna Chicken
4. Lightly brush the bottom of a clay pot with oil. Arrange the winter melon slices neatly in a single layer.
5. Spread a layer of cordyceps flowers over the winter melon slices.
6. Evenly distribute the marinated chicken slices on top of the cordyceps flowers.
7. Cover with a lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and cook for 12 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The steam released by the winter melon and cordyceps flowers will “sauna” (steam) the chicken until cooked through. Timing 12 minutes ensures it is fully cooked.
8. Once the time is up, turn off the heat. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve the pot directly at the table.

## Health Suggestions
Cordyceps flowers and winter melon provide a low-fat, high-fiber steaming liquid. The chicken is steamed with minimal oil, making it suitable for weight loss periods or for those needing a high-protein, low-burden diet. Be careful not to use too much cornstarch when marinating the chicken, as it can affect the texture.
