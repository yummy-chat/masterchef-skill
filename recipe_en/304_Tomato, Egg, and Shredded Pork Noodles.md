## Preparation Steps  
1. **Marinate the shredded pork**: Shred the pork tenderloin and mix with 0.5g salt, 5ml soy sauce, 10ml cooking wine, a small amount of water, and half a tablespoon of cornstarch. If using slightly fatty front leg meat, cornstarch can be omitted.  
*Note: Marinating makes lean meat more tender; for meat with a mix of fat and lean, the process can be simplified.*  
2. **Prepare the green peppers**: Cut the green peppers in half, remove the membranes and seeds, and shred them finely to reduce spiciness.  
*Note: The membranes are very spicy, so be sure to remove them thoroughly.*  
3. **Handle the tomatoes**: Blanch them in hot water, peel, and cut into small pieces.  
*Note: Peeling makes stewing smoother and allows the tomatoes to break down more easily.*  
4. **Prepare the eggs**: Crack the eggs into a bowl, add about 3-4g rice vinegar, and whisk until well combined.  
*Note: Rice vinegar removes the eggy smell and makes the scrambled eggs fluffier.*  
5. **Prepare the aromatics**: Mince the ginger, slice or mince the garlic, and cut the scallions into both chopped scallions and eyebrow-shaped scallions.  
6. **Scramble the eggs**: Add half a tablespoon of oil to the wok. When the oil is hot, pour in the egg mixture and stir-fry over medium heat until set into tender yellow pieces. Remove and set aside.  
*Note: Use a small amount of oil to avoid overcooking.*  
7. **Cook the tomato and egg sauce**: Add more oil to the wok, sauté the minced ginger and garlic until fragrant, then add the tomato pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until they break down. Add about 5g salt, Maggi soy sauce and dark soy sauce, and a small amount of water. Simmer, then add a pinch of sugar. When the tomatoes release their juice, stir in the scrambled eggs and mix well. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and set aside.  
*Note: Cook the tomatoes thoroughly for a rich sauce; use sugar sparingly to balance flavors.*  
8. **Stir-fry the green pepper and shredded pork**: Heat the wok with cold oil, add the marinated shredded pork, and quickly separate with chopsticks. Stir-fry over medium heat until it changes color. Add the shredded green peppers, ginger, garlic, mixed soy sauce, salt, sugar, and about 1.5 tablespoons of rice vinegar. Stir-fry until the peppers soften slightly. Before removing from heat, add the eyebrow-shaped scallions. Set aside.  
*Note: Use cold oil in a hot wok to prevent sticking; cook the peppers just until slightly tender to retain crunch.*  
9. **Cook the noodles**: Bring a generous amount of water to a boil. Add the noodles and stir to separate. After the water returns to a boil, add a small amount of cold water. Boil again, and repeat once more. When the noodles are cooked through with no hard core, drain them. Rinse egg noodles in warm water to keep them from cooling down.  
*Note: Adding cold water makes the noodles more chewy; rinsing in warm water maintains temperature.*  
10. **Assemble the bowl**: Place the noodles in a bowl. First, ladle the tomato and egg sauce over the noodles, then add the green pepper and shredded pork sauce. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and mix well before serving.  
*Note: Combining two sauces enhances flavor and provides balanced nutrition.*  

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in protein. The vitamin C in tomatoes pairs well with the vitamins in green peppers, making it ideal for post-exercise energy replenishment. The shredded pork is marinated with minimal oil, and removing the membranes and seeds of the green peppers reduces spiciness. Those with a sensitive stomach should consume in moderation.
