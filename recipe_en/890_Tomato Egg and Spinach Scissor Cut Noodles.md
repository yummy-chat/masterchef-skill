# Tomato Egg and Spinach Scissor Cut Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast, about 100g  
🧅 Purple onion, about 1/4 piece  
🥔 Potato, 1 small (about half after peeling)  
🥬 Spinach, a handful (about 50g after blanching)  
🍅 Tomato, 2 medium-sized  
🥚 Egg, 2 pieces  
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (adjust according to dough consistency)  
🧂 Soy sauce (or light soy sauce), a pinch  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🧄 Garlic, 2 cloves (minced)  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🌀 Food processor  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥣 Regular pot (for blanching and boiling noodles)  
🥄 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place chicken breast, purple onion, and potato into a food processor. Add a pinch of soy sauce and salt, and blend until a smooth meat paste forms. Remove and shape into small pieces convenient for eating.  
2. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a non-stick frying pan. Over medium-low heat, pan-fry the meat paste pieces until golden brown and set on both sides. Remove and set aside.  
3. Using the remaining oil in the pan, pour in the beaten eggs and spread into a thin omelet. Remove and set aside.  
4. Bring another pot of water to a boil. Briefly blanch whole tomatoes and spinach. Remove, peel and chop the tomatoes, and squeeze out excess water from the spinach.  
5. Place the blanched spinach, torn omelet pieces, and potato into a food processor. Blend into a fine green paste. Mix this green paste with flour and knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.  
6. Heat oil in another pot. Over low heat, sauté minced garlic until fragrant. Add the tomato pieces and a pinch of sugar. Switch to medium heat, pressing and stirring with a spatula until the tomatoes soften and release juice, forming a rich broth base.  
7. Keep the broth boiling. Hold the dough in one hand and use kitchen scissors with the other to quickly cut small strips directly into the pot. Gently stir and cook until the noodles float and are fully cooked.  
8. Add the pan-fried meat pieces into the pot, mixing well with the noodles and broth. Cook briefly, then remove and serve.  

## Health Tips
This dish replaces regular noodles with a spinach dough, increasing dietary fiber and iron content. The tomato broth provides lycopene, making it suitable for a low-fat, high-protein diet. The chicken breast and potato paste, along with the omelet, offer dual protein sources. However, be mindful not to use too much oil when frying the meat pieces.
