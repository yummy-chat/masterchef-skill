# Drunken Peanut
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts (red-skinned) approx. 500g
🧄 Garlic 5-6 cloves
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns to taste
🌱 Numbing peppercorns (má jiāo) to taste
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🧂 Salt 1 part
🍬 White sugar 3 parts
🌿 Cumin powder to taste
🌶️ Chili powder to taste
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor) as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Gas stove
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Wok spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the peanuts with skins, immediately turn off the heat, cover, and let them steep until naturally cooled.
   **Key point**: After steeping and cooling, the peanut skins are easier to peel.
2. Remove the cooled peanuts, gently rub off the red skins by hand to obtain white peanut kernels.
   **Key point**: After boiling and cooling, the skins come off very easily.
3. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok, add the peeled peanut kernels and a few garlic cloves. Fry slowly over low heat for about 7–8 minutes.
   **Key point**: Start with cold oil; use the garlic cloves to check doneness. Stir constantly with a spatula to prevent the bottom from overheating and burning. When the garlic cloves turn golden brown and the peanut skins show fine "tiger stripes," remove and drain the oil.
4. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add Sichuan peppercorns, numbing peppercorns, and dried chili segments. Stir-fry slowly over low heat until fragrant.
5. Add the fried peanuts and stir well. Add salt and white sugar, continue to stir-fry until the sugar and salt are fully melted and combined.
6. Add cumin powder and chili powder, switch to the lowest heat, and stir-fry to fully blend the spices.
7. Turn off the heat, pour a small amount of baijiu along the edge of the wok, and quickly stir-fry using the residual heat.
   **Key point**: Adding baijiu after turning off the heat enhances aroma, makes the peanuts crispier, prevents them from softening, and extends shelf life.
8. Remove from the wok and serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Peanuts are rich in high-quality protein and healthy fats, but they are high in calories after frying, so it is recommended to consume in small amounts. If sensitive to spiciness, reduce the amount of dried chili peppers. The antioxidant components in numbing peppercorns are beneficial to cardiovascular health.
