# Grapefruit Bavarian Cream
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍊 Grapefruit 3 (use about 20g of peel, use the flesh separately)
🍮 Gelatin sheets 2 pieces
🥛 Milk 400g
🥚 Egg yolks 4
🥛 Heavy cream 250g
🍬 Sugar 40g (for egg yolk mixture) + 15g (for heavy cream)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Small saucepan
🌀 Electric mixer
🥣 Glass or stainless steel bowl
🥄 Spatula
🧽 Strainer
📦 Square mold or bowl
❄️ Refrigerator

## Steps
1. Soak gelatin sheets in cold water for 5-10 minutes until completely softened and translucent.
2. Finely shred the grapefruit peel, take about 20g and set aside.
3. Mix egg yolks with 40g sugar, beat with an electric mixer until the mixture turns pale and thickens to a consistency like thick yogurt.
4. Put milk and shredded grapefruit peel into a small saucepan, bring to a boil and immediately turn off the heat.
5. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture in small batches while stirring quickly with a spatula to prevent the yolks from curdling.
6. Strain the mixture through a strainer back into the saucepan to remove the grapefruit peel residue.
7. Heat over low heat while stirring continuously until the liquid noticeably thickens and coats the spatula, then turn off the heat.
8. Add the softened gelatin sheets, stir until completely melted.
9. Whip the heavy cream with 15g sugar using an electric mixer until soft peaks form.
10. After the egg-milk mixture has cooled slightly, pour it into the whipped cream and fold until evenly combined.
11. Pour the mixture into the mold, refrigerate for at least 2 hours until fully set into a pudding-like consistency.

## Health Advice
Grapefruit Bavarian Cream uses the peel for fragrance and the flesh for flavor. Note that softened gelatin should be drained thoroughly, otherwise it may affect the setting effect. Suitable for cooling off in summer, but the calorie content is not low. It is recommended to enjoy with green tea.
