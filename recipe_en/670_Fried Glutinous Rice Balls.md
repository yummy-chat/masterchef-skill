# Fried Glutinous Rice Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍡 Frozen glutinous rice balls, about 30 pieces  
🥚 Egg, 1 piece  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
💧 Water, about half a bowl (enough to reach 1/3 of the height of the glutinous rice balls)  
🍶 White vinegar, two drops  
🫘 Black sesame seeds, as needed  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍽️ Plate  
🥘 Transparent lid  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Take out the frozen glutinous rice balls and place them on a plate for later use.  
2. Crack the egg into a bowl, add two drops of white vinegar, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is well combined.  
   - **Key**: The white vinegar helps remove the eggy smell.  
3. Place all the glutinous rice balls in a cold wok, drizzle a circle of cooking oil along the edge of the wok, and gently shake the wok to ensure the oil evenly coats the bottom.  
4. Add half a bowl of water, with the water level reaching about 1/3 of the height of the glutinous rice balls.  
5. Cover with the lid and cook over the lowest heat for about 5 minutes, until the bottom of the glutinous rice balls turns golden brown.  
   - **Note**: Keep the heat on the lowest setting throughout to prevent burning.  
6. Take a plate, evenly spread a layer of cooking oil on it, then invert the plate over the glutinous rice balls in the wok.  
7. Hold the bottom of the plate with one hand and the wok handle with the other, quickly flip the wok so that the glutinous rice ball cake comes out intact onto the plate.  
8. Place the uncooked side of the glutinous rice ball cake facing down, slide it back into the wok from the plate, and continue frying over low heat until the other side is also golden brown.  
9. Before removing from the wok, evenly sprinkle black sesame seeds over the surface of the glutinous rice ball cake, turn off the heat, and use the plate to help transfer it out.  
   - **Result**: Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, sweet and delicious.  

## Health Advice  
Fried glutinous rice balls are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair them with unsweetened tea to help cut the richness. They are suitable as an occasional treat rather than a regular part of your daily diet. Diabetic patients and those controlling sugar intake should consume with caution.
