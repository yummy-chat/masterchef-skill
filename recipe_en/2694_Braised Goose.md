# Braised Goose  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 1 Lion’s Head Goose (about 20 lbs, old goose)  
🫚 Proper amount of Galangal/Thai Ginger (smashed)  
🌿 Proper amount of Lemongrass  
🐟 Proper amount of Dried Sole (dried flatfish)  
🍖 Proper amount of Jinhua Ham  
🦴 Proper amount of Pork Ribs  
🦆 1 Old Duck  
🧅 Proper amount of Shallots  
🧄 Proper amount of Garlic  
🫚 Proper amount of Ginger  
🧅 Proper amount of Scallions  
🌿 Proper amount of Cilantro  
🦐 Proper amount of Dried Shrimp  
🌰 Spice Pouch (contains: Cinnamon, Dried Chili, Bay Leaves, Black Cardamom, Sichuan Peppercorn, Fennel Seeds, Star Anise, Pepper, Dried Lemongrass, Nutmeg, Cloves, White Cardamom, Sand Ginger, Round Cardamom, Pinctada Chemical, Red Yeast Rice)  
🍾 250g Dark Soy Sauce (used only for first-time braising)  
🐟 750g Fish Sauce  
🥃 500g Erguotou (or Baijiu)  
🍬 500g Rock Sugar  
🧂 150g Salt  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure Cooker  
🪣 Large Soup Pot/Soup Bucket  
🔥 Grill Rack + Gas Stove  
🥄 Fine Mesh Strainer  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🪝 Hooks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Except for the red yeast rice, place all dry spices into a wok and dry-fry over low heat until a medicinal fragrance releases, then put them into a soup bag.  
2. Smash the galangal, and cut the shallots, ginger, cilantro, and scallions into large pieces or smash them flat.  
3. Sear the dried sole and Jinhua ham over an open flame; chop the pork ribs and old duck into pieces, blanch them, and rinse clean.  
4. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok, add the shallots, ginger, cilantro, and scallions, and fry slowly over low heat until the edges are slightly charred. Pour out the oil and solids together for later use.  
5. Prepare the stock:  
   - Bring water to a boil in a large soup pot, add the blanched pork ribs, old duck, seared dried sole, Jinhua ham, and dried shrimp. Bring to a high boil, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer.  
   - In a pressure cooker, add the galangal, spice pouch, the fried vegetable oil and solids, and water. Once pressurized, cook on high heat for 30 minutes, then reduce to low heat for another 30 minutes.  
   - Remove the solids from the pressure cooker, then pour the spice broth into the main stock and mix. Skim off any foam.  
   (大汤煲烧滚水，放入飞过水嘅排骨、老鸭、烤过嘅大地鱼、火腿、海米，猛火烧滚转中慢火煲。  
   高压煲放入南姜、香料包、炸好嘅蔬菜油同料，加水，上汽之后猛气压30分钟，转慢气压30分钟。  
   捞起高压煲入面嘅汤渣，将香料汤倒落主高汤度混合，撇走浮沫。)  

6. Bring the mixed stock to a boil, then add dark soy sauce, fish sauce, rock sugar, salt, and baijiu. Stir well.  
   **Note**: Dark soy sauce is added only this one time and will not be used again.  
   (混合汤底烧滚，加老抽、鱼露、冰糖、盐、白酒，搅匀。  
   **注意**：老抽只系第一次加，之后唔再用。)  

7. Break down the Lion’s Head Goose: cut off the goose feet and goose wings, and cut off the goose head along with part of the breast.  
   **Note**: Fresh goose meat does not need to be blanched.  
   (狮头鹅斩件，切落鹅掌、鹅翼，鹅头连胸切落。  
   **注意**：新鲜鹅肉唔使飞水。)  

8. Place the various goose parts into the boiling braising liquid. Use a fine mesh strainer to press them down so they are fully submerged. Braise on high heat for 40 minutes, then reduce to low heat for another 20 minutes.  
9. Remove the goose meat and hang it in a ventilated area to rest for at least 6 hours or overnight.  
   **Key**: This step allows the goose meat to contract and the flavors to fully penetrate; do not skip it.  
   (捞起鹅肉，挂起晾干6个钟以上或者过夜。  
   **关键**：呢一步令鹅肉收缩、味道渗入，唔可以跳过。)  

10. Return the rested goose meat to the boiling braising liquid and braise over medium-low heat for about 90 minutes.  
11. Once braised, remove the goose meat and **let it cool completely** before cutting it into pieces and plating.  
    **Note**: Cutting while hot will cause the meat to shrink again, affecting texture and appearance.  
    (卤好之后捞起鹅肉，**一定要完全摊冻**先至斩件上碟。  
    **注意**：热切会令鹅肉二次收缩，影响口感同外形。)  

## Health Advice  
Braised goose is rich in high-quality protein, but it also contains high levels of fat and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation. The braising liquid is used in two stages, and dark soy sauce is only added the first time, which helps control sodium intake.
