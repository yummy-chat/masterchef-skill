# Shrimp and Beef Stir-Fried Noodles
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef (tender cuts like beef tenderloin) appropriate amount, sliced  
🦐 Shrimp appropriate amount  
🍜 Noodles appropriate amount (cooked in advance and rinsed with cold water)  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount, sliced or cut into pieces  
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers appropriate amount, sliced or cut into pieces  
🧂 Light soy sauce 3 tablespoons (for the sauce, also use a small amount for marinating)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon (for the sauce, also use a small amount for marinating)  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount (for marinating the beef)  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for marinating the beef)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for marinating the beef and stir-frying)  
🌿 Black pepper 1 teaspoon  
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Bowls (for marinating beef, holding shrimp, and mixing sauce)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring and presentation)  

## Instructions
**Preparation Stage**
1. Place the beef slices in a bowl. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch. Mix well, then drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix evenly. Marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Coating with oil locks in the juices, resulting in more tender beef.
2. Slice the onion and green and red chili peppers. Set aside. Mix 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce, 1 teaspoon of black pepper, and 1 teaspoon of white sugar into a sauce. Stir until well combined.

**Cooking Stage**
1. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a wok until it reaches 70% hot. Add the marinated beef and stir-fry over high heat until it changes color. Immediately remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Remove the beef as soon as it changes color to avoid overcooking.
2. Heat oil in another wok. Add the shrimp and stir-fry until they change color. Then add the onion and green and red chili peppers. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until the vegetables are just cooked.
3. Return the beef to the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry over high heat quickly until the ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce.
4. Add the cooked noodles (which have been rinsed with cold water). Stir-fry over high heat, ensuring the noodles are fully mixed with the ingredients and sauce.
   **Note**: The noodles are already cooked; only thorough heating and even coating are needed.

## Health Suggestions
Beef and shrimp provide high-quality dual protein, suitable for muscle building and fat loss; noodles absorb sauce easily, so control the amount of light and dark soy sauce to prevent excessive saltiness. It is recommended to let the sauce penetrate on medium-low heat first, then switch to high heat for stir-frying. Overall, control portion sizes to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake.
