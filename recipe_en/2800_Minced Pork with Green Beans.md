# Minced Pork with Green Beans
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Green beans, appropriate amount (snap beans or long beans, cut into 0.5 cm small dices)  
🥩 Pork belly, about 200-300 grams (cut into small dices or minced)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers (xiaomila), 3-4 pieces (cut into rings, added just before finishing)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped for stir-frying)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (minced)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, appropriate amount (for stir-frying)  
🫘 Fermented soybean paste (huangdoujiang), a small amount  
🍶 Hua Tiao wine, appropriate amount  
🍯 Soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount  
🌾 Water starch, appropriate amount (starch mixed with water)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Wok spatula or spoon  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing water starch)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the green beans, trim off both ends, and cut into 0.5 cm small dices. Cut the pork belly into small dices. Mince the scallions, ginger, and garlic. Cut the red chili peppers into rings.
2. Heat the wok until hot, then add cold oil. Heat the oil over high heat until it rises in temperature. Add the minced pork and stir-fry until the meat turns white. Do not overcook.
3. Add the diced green beans. Keep stir-frying over high heat until the bean surface develops a tiger-skin wrinkled texture and the beans shrink and become fully cooked.
   **Key Point**: It is essential to achieve the tiger-skin texture; otherwise, the beans will taste raw and tough, and undercooked beans may pose a food safety risk.
4. Add the minced scallions, ginger, garlic, and dried chili segments. Quickly stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Add a small amount of fermented soybean paste and stir-fry evenly until the aroma releases.
6. Pour in the Hua Tiao wine along the edge of the wok. Then add soy sauce, white sugar, sesame oil, oyster sauce, and five-spice powder. Stir-fry evenly until a small amount of sauce forms in the wok.
7. When the sauce is nearly reduced, pour in the water starch to thicken. Quickly stir-fry so the starch coating evenly covers all ingredients.
8. Just before turning off the heat, sprinkle in the red chili pepper rings. Toss to combine and serve.
   **Tip**: Adding the red chili peppers last preserves their bright color and fresh, spicy flavor.

## Health Advice
Minced pork with green beans is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for controlled portions during weight loss. Ensure the green beans are fully cooked with a tiger-skin texture for safety, to avoid any discomfort from undercooking.
