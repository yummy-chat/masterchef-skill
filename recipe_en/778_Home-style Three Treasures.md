# Home-style Three Treasures

Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 potato (peeled, cut into rolling chunks)  
🍆 3 eggplants (peel depending on age, cut into rolling chunks)  
🫑 1 green bell pepper (seeded, cut into rolling chunks)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped into green onion pieces)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (minced)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (used twice: first for stir-frying, second for raw garlic added before serving)  
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (for coating the eggplant)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🧂 MSG, a pinch  
🍬 Sugar, as needed  
🍯 Light soy sauce, half a tablespoon  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, one-third tablespoon  
💧 Cold water, equal amount to the mixture of light soy sauce and dark soy sauce  
🥣 Water starch, two tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a dash  
🍇 White vinegar, a dash (for stir-frying)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for stir-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥒 Peeler (optional, for eggplant)  
🍳 Wok (needs to accommodate deep-frying)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the potato**  
   Peel the potato and cut into rolling chunks, not too thick. Immediately soak the chunks in cold water to remove excess starch and prevent oxidation discoloration.  
2. **Prepare the eggplant**  
   Wash the eggplant. If older, peel it; if younger, leave the skin on. Also cut into rolling chunks and set aside.  
3. **Prepare the green bell pepper**  
   Seed the green bell pepper and cut into rolling chunks, set aside.  
4. **Make the sauce mixture**  
   In a bowl, add salt, a pinch of MSG, sugar, half a tablespoon of light soy sauce, one-third tablespoon of dark soy sauce, then add an equal amount of cold water to the sauce mixture, two tablespoons of water starch, and finally a dash of oyster sauce. Stir thoroughly until well combined and set aside.  
*Key point: The sauce mixture is the core of the three treasures' flavor; prepare it in advance to avoid scrambling during cooking.*  

5. **Deep-fry the potato**  
   Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 170°C (340°F). Add the drained potato chunks. At high heat, the potato surface will quickly form a crispy crust, then reduce to medium heat and fry until the potatoes float to the surface, indicating they are cooked. Remove and drain oil.  
*Key point: High heat for setting, medium heat for thorough cooking, ensuring a crispy exterior and tender interior while not absorbing too much oil.*  

6. **Coat the eggplant**  
   Sprinkle a little of the water used to soak the potatoes over the eggplant chunks. Gradually add cornstarch in 4–5 batches, tossing thoroughly each time to evenly coat the surface with a thin, uniform starch layer. Just before frying, add another handful of dry cornstarch and shake off any excess.  
*Key point: The "water-starch alternating method" ensures even coating; the final dry cornstarch is crucial for the eggplant to be crispy and not absorb oil.*  

7. **Deep-fry the eggplant**  
   In another wok, add enough oil and heat to 170°C (340°F). Add the coated eggplant chunks and maintain high heat throughout. Fry until the shell hardens, sets, and turns golden brown. Remove and drain oil.  
*Key point: Do not stir immediately after adding the eggplant; wait until the starch is fully set before gently breaking them apart to prevent the coating from falling off.*  

8. **Re-fry at high heat and blanch the green bell pepper**  
   Raise the oil temperature to 190°C (375°F). Return the fried potato and eggplant to the wok for a second fry to expel excess oil and make the shell crispier. Just before finishing, place the green bell pepper chunks in a slotted spoon and dip into the hot oil for a few seconds to blanch, then remove all ingredients together and drain oil.  
*Key point: Blanching the green bell pepper last ensures it is cooked through while retaining a crisp texture and bright green color.*  

9. **Stir-fry to finish**  
   Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add some garlic and scallion pieces and stir-fry until fragrant. Splash a dash of white vinegar along the edge of the wok to enhance aroma. Pour in the prepared sauce mixture, turn to high heat, and cook until the sauce thickens and bubbles. Add the fried potato, eggplant, and green bell pepper, stirring quickly. Finally, sprinkle the remaining raw garlic, remove from heat, and toss well before serving.  
*Key point: The sauce must be thickened before adding the ingredients, otherwise the coating won't adhere; "garlic added in two batches" means the final raw garlic is key for a rich garlic aroma and wok hei.*  

## Health Tips  
The potato undergoes two rounds of deep-frying to expel excess oil, and the eggplant is coated and fried quickly at high heat to minimize oil absorption. Paired with green bell pepper for vitamin C, this dish is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet in moderation. Be mindful of the sugar and salt in the sauce mixture; those with high blood pressure or managing sugar intake can reduce the amount of sugar and dark soy sauce as needed.
