# Steamed Free-Range Chicken
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 young chicken (about 1200g, preferably free-range)
🫁 Chicken innards (optional, such as heart and gizzards, scrubbed clean with flour and salt)
🫚 Several slices of ginger (sliced)
🧂 About 4 teaspoons of salt (for marinating)
🫒 Appropriate amount of rapeseed oil
🥓 Appropriate amount of lard

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for washing and marinating)
🍽️ Plate (for holding the chicken during steaming)

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the whole chicken: Remove the chicken breast and set aside for another use. Chop the remaining parts into pieces, with the drumstick pieces slightly larger.
   **Note**: Chicken breast becomes dry after steaming; it is better to freeze it separately or use it for stir-frying.
2. Wash chicken pieces and innards: Rinse the chicken pieces thoroughly, focusing on removing blood clots near the ribs, and drain well. Wash the innards repeatedly with flour and salt, then rinse clean.
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly removing blood clots is crucial for eliminating any gamey smell.
3. Marinate: Place the drained chicken pieces and innards in a bowl. Add ginger slices and salt. Rub and mix vigorously until the chicken surface feels sticky. Marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Mix well to ensure the salt penetrates fully, enhancing the base flavor.
4. Stir-fry: Heat a wok or frying pan over high heat. Add rapeseed oil and lard. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken. Sear for about 1 minute over high heat, then stir-fry for a total of 2 to 3 minutes, until the chicken slightly shrinks and the surface turns lightly golden.
   **Purpose**: Stir-frying releases a richer meat aroma, resulting in better flavor after steaming.
5. Arrange for steaming: Transfer the stir-fried chicken and the rendered chicken oil into a plate. Add water to the bottom of the pressure cooker, place the plate inside to steam on a rack (ensuring the plate does not touch the water), and cover tightly with the lid.
   **Note**: Must steam indirectly; do not submerge the chicken in water.
6. Steam: Once the pressure cooker reaches high pressure, reduce to medium-low heat and begin timing. Adjust time based on the chicken's age. For a young chicken under one year, pressure cook for 10 minutes.
   **Time Reference**: If using a regular steamer, steam for about 30 minutes.
7. Serve: Release the pressure, open the lid, and remove the plate. Skim off the surface oil and serve. The broth will be clear and delicious, with tender, savory chicken.

## Health Advice
Steamed free-range chicken is rich in high-quality protein with controllable fat content, making it suitable for fitness and fat-control diets. Including chicken innards can supplement iron. Be mindful of oil and salt amounts during marinating; those with high blood pressure should reduce salt intake.
