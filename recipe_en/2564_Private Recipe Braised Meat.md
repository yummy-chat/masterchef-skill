# Private Recipe Braised Meat
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
- 🐷 Pork hock, as needed
- 🐔 Chicken feet, as needed
- 🍈 Luo Han Guo (monk fruit) (needs to be cracked open)
- 🍊 Chenpi (dried tangerine peel)
- 🌰 Star anise
- 🌿 Cinnamon bark
- 🍃 Bay leaves
- 🌱 Cumin seeds
- 🧅 Galangal
- 🫘 Black cardamom (needs to be cracked open and seeds removed to prevent bitterness)
- 🥜 Nutmeg (needs to be cracked open and seeds removed to prevent bitterness)
- 🥥 Fragrant fruit (needs to be cracked open)
- 🍃 Lotus leaf (needs to be soaked in warm water until soft)
- 🌸 Cloves (three cloves, use sparingly, do not add more)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
- 🔪 Knife (for cutting open spices to "crack")
- 🪵 Cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Use a knife to cut open the black cardamom and nutmeg, and thoroughly remove the inner seeds to prevent the braised meat from becoming bitter.
2. Crack open the fragrant fruit and Luo Han Guo in the same way to facilitate flavor release during braising.
3. Soak the dried lotus leaf in warm water until it softens.
4. Accurately control the amount of cloves; only add three cloves per batch of braising spices.
5. Put all the pre-processed spices together with the pork hock and chicken feet into a pot. Add an appropriate amount of water, along with seasonings such as soy sauce, salt, and sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and braise until the ingredients are thoroughly cooked and infused with flavor.

## Health Advice
This braised meat is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those who need to supplement collagen. However, it is a high-fat, high-purine food; people with hypertension, hyperlipidemia, hyperglycemia, or gout should be mindful of their intake. Luo Han Guo and chenpi impart a refreshing fruity sweetness that can partially offset the greasiness, but it should not be consumed in excess.
