# Stir-Fried Beef with Yellow Chili

Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef knuckle (top round) 300g (slice against the grain into 2-3mm thin pieces)
🥚 Egg ½ (beaten)
🌿 Cilantro 300g (separate stems and leaves; cut stems into sections, reserve leaves for lining the plate)
🌶️ Beauty peppers 300g (sliced into rings)
🧅 Shallots 300g (julienned)
🌶️ Pickled wild peppers 100g (with pickling liquid reserved)
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies 200g (sliced into rings)
🫚 Ginger 200g (minced)
🧄 Garlic cloves 10-15 (smashed)

**For Marinating the Beef**:
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce ½ tbsp
🧂 Dark soy sauce ½ tbsp (small spoon)
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp
🧂 White pepper a pinch
🥄 Cornstarch 1 tbsp
🧂 Chicken powder 1 tsp
🫒 Oil 1 tbsp (added last)

**For the Sauce**:
💧 Pickled wild pepper liquid 2 tbsp
🧂 Chicken bouillon 1 tbsp
🧂 White pepper a pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
🧂 Light soy sauce 1½ tbsp
🧂 Dark soy sauce ½ tbsp (small spoon)
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp

**For Stir-Frying**:
🫒 Rapeseed oil as needed

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Glass bowls (for marinating and sauce)
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Wok spatula
🔥 Stove

## Steps
1. **Slice and Marinate the Beef**
   Slice the beef knuckle against the grain into thin pieces, about 2-3mm thick. Add half of the beaten egg, chicken powder, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, cooking wine, white pepper, and cornstarch. Use your hands to mix and massage vigorously until the beef becomes sticky and is evenly coated. Finally, drizzle in 1 tbsp of oil and mix well to lock in moisture and prevent sticking.
   *Note*: Slicing against the grain is essential to break down the muscle fibers; this is key to tender beef. The mixing must be firm and even.

2. **Prepare Aromatics and Sauce**
   Separate the cilantro stems and leaves. Cut the stems into sections and set aside the leaves for lining the plate. Slice the beauty peppers and bird's eye chilies into rings. Julienne the shallots, mince the ginger, and smash the garlic. In a bowl, combine the pickled wild pepper liquid, chicken bouillon, white pepper, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and cooking wine. Stir until well mixed.
   *Key Point*: The pickled wild pepper liquid is the soul of the flavor; do not omit it. Prepare all aromatics in advance to ensure a smooth and fast stir-fry.

3. **Heat Wok and Flash-Fry the Beef**
   Heat the wok until it starts to smoke, then add enough rapeseed oil. When the oil reaches about 60-70% hot (just starting to shimmer), add the marinated beef. Keep the heat on high and quickly separate the slices with a spatula. Stir-fry until the surface changes color and the beef is just cooked through. Then, push the beef to one side of the wok.
   *Note*: The wok must be very hot, the oil temperature high, and the action quick. If cooked too long, the beef will release water and become tough.

4. **Stir-Fry the Aromatics**
   Using the oil left in the wok, first add the smashed garlic and julienned shallots, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add the bird's eye chilies, pickled wild peppers, and beauty pepper rings, and stir-fry briefly. Finally, push the beef back into the center and stir-fry everything together evenly.
   *Note*: Adding aromatics in stages helps release a more complex and layered fragrance.

5. **Add the Sauce and Finish**
   Pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat to coat the beef and aromatics evenly with the sauce. Finally, add the cilantro stems and stir-fry a few times until they are just wilted. Turn off the heat immediately. Line a serving plate with the cilantro leaves and plate the stir-fried beef on top.
   *Key Point*: Maintain high heat throughout for a continuous and swift cooking process. Add the cilantro stems at the very end to keep them vibrant green and crisp-tender.

## Health Advice
This stir-fried beef dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for muscle building and weight loss. However, the marinade and sauce are relatively high in sodium. Those with high blood pressure should consider reducing the salt intake and eating it with less rice. Cilantro is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber.
