# Stir-Fried Poached Eggs with Peppers
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Wrinkled peppers 5-6 pieces (flattened and cut into sections)  
🥚 Eggs 5  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed or sliced)  
🫘 Fermented black beans appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (used twice)  
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount  
🌿 White pepper a small amount (before serving)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥢 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Small bowl  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Take the wrinkled peppers, flatten them with the knife blade, then cut into sections; crush or slice the garlic.  
2. In a small bowl, mix oyster sauce, chicken bouillon, cooking wine, salt, and light soy sauce evenly to make a sauce; set aside.  
3. Crack the eggs into a bowl, keeping the yolks intact without whisking.  
4. Place the wok over heat, add an appropriate amount of rapeseed oil and heat until hot. Swirl the oil to coat the wok, then pour in the whole bowl of eggs. Fry over medium heat until the bottom is golden and set, then flip and continue frying until both sides are golden, forming a complete poached egg patty.  
   **Key point**: Flip only after one side is golden and set to avoid frequent turning. Remove and set aside once done.  
5. Without adding oil, heat the wok and add the pepper sections. Dry-fry over medium-low heat until the skin wrinkles and charred spots appear.  
   **Key point**: Dry-frying first enhances the pepper's aroma.  
6. Add a small amount of rapeseed oil and a pinch of salt to the peppers, stir-fry evenly, then add the garlic slices and fermented black beans, stir-frying until fragrant.  
7. Use a spatula to break the fried egg patty into large pieces, return it to the wok with the peppers, and stir-fry together. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry quickly to combine.  
8. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in a small amount of white pepper, and mix evenly before serving.  
   **Key point**: White pepper is the finishing touch; be sure to add it at the very end.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein, and the peppers provide vitamin C, making it suitable to pair with staple foods. Control the amount of oil when frying the eggs. Fermented black beans and oyster sauce are high in sodium; those with high blood pressure may reduce the sauce amount as needed.
