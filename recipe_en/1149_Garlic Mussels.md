# Garlic Mussels  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦪 Mussels 2 jin (cleaned)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (use white part, minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper a little (minced)  
🥜 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for sizzling garlic)  
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🌿 White pepper powder appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar a little  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok (with lid)  
🔥 Simple brick stove  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)  
🥄 Chopsticks or spoon  
🍽️ Serving plate  

## Steps  
1. Place the cleaned mussels directly into a cold wok without adding any water.  
**Key Point**: Use the mussels’ own moisture to steam them, preserving the original flavor.  

2. Cover with a lid and cook over medium heat for about 3 minutes, until all mussels have opened, indicating they are cooked.  
3. Remove the mussels, use scissors to cut off the beard (byssus) from each piece of mussel meat.  
4. Prepare the garlic sauce: In a bowl, add a generous amount of minced garlic, minced scallion white, and minced red bell pepper. Pour in one spoonful of hot oil to sizzle and release the aroma, then add light soy sauce, white pepper powder, and a little sugar. Stir well.  
5. Arrange the debearded mussels on a plate, and evenly drizzle the prepared garlic sauce over each piece of mussel meat.  
6. Serve and enjoy—savory with a hint of sweetness and rich garlic aroma.  

## Health Tips  
Mussels are high in protein and low in fat, rich in zinc and selenium, making them suitable for those needing to boost immunity, but people with gout should eat them in moderation. Steaming without water preserves the original flavor; the garlic sauce must be sizzled with hot oil to release its aroma.
