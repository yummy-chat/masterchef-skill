# Rice Daifuku 🍡
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Leftover rice, as needed
🍪 Crushed Oreo cookies, as needed
🍦 Cream, as needed (for filling)
🍬 White sugar, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker
🥣 Small pot
🔨 Rolling pin (used as a mashing tool)
👨‍🍳 Silicone kneading mat
🛍️ Plastic wrap
🥣 Small bowl
🍽️ Metal divided serving plate

## Steps
1. Place the rice in a small pot, add white sugar, and repeatedly mash with a rolling pin until the rice is fully combined, sticky in texture, and can be pulled into strings.
   **Key point**: Mash with even force and patience; this is the foundation for a soft and chewy final product.
2. Transfer the mashed rice dough to a silicone mat and divide into evenly sized small portions.
3. Place one portion on plastic wrap and press it into a round flat disc using a rolling pin or the palm of your hand; the plastic wrap prevents sticking.
4. Place the rice skin into a small bowl, squeeze an appropriate amount of cream into the center, and sprinkle with crushed Oreo cookies.
   **Tip**: Use a moderate amount of filling to avoid leakage during wrapping.
5. Gather the edges of the rice skin towards the center, pinch the seal tightly like wrapping a bun, ensuring the seal is secure.
6. Place the finished product upside down on a plate; no further cooking is required, and the shape can be adjusted freely.

## Health Tips
These small sweets made from leftover rice are low in oil and salt, making them suitable for satisfying cravings. Note that the cream and Oreo cookies are relatively high in calories, so portion control is advised.
