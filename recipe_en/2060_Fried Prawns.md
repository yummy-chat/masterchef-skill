# Fried Prawns  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Instructions  
1. Remove the heads and veins from the fresh prawns, then rinse thoroughly under clean water.  
2. Place the prawns on kitchen paper and press firmly to absorb and dry all surface moisture.  
   **Key point**: The moisture must be completely dried, otherwise the seasoning will not penetrate well, and the batter may detach during frying.  
3. Add cooking wine, a pinch of salt, white pepper, and black pepper to the prawns. Mix well and let sit briefly to remove fishy odor and enhance flavor.  
4. First, add the egg white and mix evenly. Then gradually add the potato starch, stirring until the prawns are evenly coated with a thick batter. Finally, drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil and mix again until the batter reaches a thick, yogurt-like consistency.  
5. Place the wok on the stove and add enough cooking oil. Heat the oil to about 150°C. Add the prawns one by one and fry over medium-low heat for about 3 minutes, until the batter is set, golden yellow, and the surface is crispy.  
   **Note**:  
   - When frying at home with less oil, pay extra attention to controlling the oil temperature to avoid the exterior burning while the interior remains undercooked.  
   - When using potato starch, do not let the oil temperature get too high, as this starch is less heat-resistant than cornstarch.  
   - If the prawns stick together in the wok, gently separate them with a slotted spoon.  
   - Keeping the heat low throughout the frying process is key to achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.  
6. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried prawns and place them on kitchen paper to absorb excess oil. Then transfer to a serving plate.  

## Health Advice  
These fried prawns are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but the battered frying method is not low in calories. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad or lemon juice to cut the richness. Suitable as a party snack; those with high blood lipids should enjoy in moderation.
