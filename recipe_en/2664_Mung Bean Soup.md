# Mung Bean Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Mung beans – appropriate amount (choose dry mung beans with uniform size, plump shape, and bright green color; soak in clean water for 4 hours in advance)  
🌿 Rue herb – about 1 liang (use fresh leaves, washed clean)  
🟤 Dried kelp – appropriate amount (must use salted dried kelp pieces; wash, rehydrate, then cut into thin strips; **do not use** pre-tied kelp knots)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel strips – appropriate amount (Cantonese aged tangerine peel, can neutralize the cold nature of the beans)  
🧊 Rock sugar – appropriate amount (3–5 qian per jin of water, adjust proportionally based on water volume)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Glass pot or clay pot (to prevent oxidation and keep the soup clear)  
🔥 Stove  
✂️ Scissors (for cutting rue leaves)  
🥣 Bowls, spoon

## Steps
1. Pick out any yellowed or whitened grains from the dry mung beans. Wash with clean water, then soak for 4 hours until expanded. Wash and rehydrate the dried kelp pieces, then cut into thin strips. Wash the dried tangerine peel strips and rue leaves separately and set aside.
2. Pour enough clean water into a glass pot or clay pot. Bring to a boil, then add half of the soaked mung beans, all the tangerine peel strips, and all the kelp strips. Once the water boils again, reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for 45 minutes.
3. After 45 minutes, add the washed rue leaves and the remaining half of the mung beans. Continue simmering over medium-low heat for 15 to 20 minutes, until the later-added mung beans just begin to bloom and release their starch, maintaining a creamy and grainy texture.
4. Add rock sugar, stir until fully dissolved, then continue simmering for another 15 to 17 minutes. Note: After adding rock sugar, it must be boiled for sufficient time; otherwise, the cold nature of the mung beans will not be fully neutralized, which may cause gastrointestinal discomfort after consumption.
5. Turn off the heat and ladle into bowls. The finished soup is clear with a slight reddish tint, the mung beans are creamy and grainy, carrying the aroma of rue herb, a hint of sea flavor, and the fragrance of beans – a mellow and balanced taste.

## Health Advice
This mung bean soup, paired with tangerine peel and kelp, helps to neutralize the cold nature of mung beans. It is suitable for summer cooling, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation. Ensure the rock sugar is boiled for sufficient time; otherwise, it may cause bloating.
