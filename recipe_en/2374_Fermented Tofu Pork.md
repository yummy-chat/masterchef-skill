# Fermented Tofu Pork  
Cuisine: Henan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 3.5 jin (about 1.75 kg, choose streaky with alternating fat and lean)  
🧅 Scallions As needed (scallion segments for boiling meat, scallion mince for the seasoning sauce)  
🫚 Ginger As needed (ginger slices for boiling meat, ginger mince for the seasoning sauce)  
🧄 Garlic A large amount (about one large bowl of minced garlic)  
🧈 Red fermented tofu (red fang furu) One bottle (about three bowls' worth)  
🍬 White sugar 3 hot pot spoons  
🧂 MSG 3 small spoons  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3 small spoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce As needed  
🍶 Cooking wine As needed (for boiling meat and preparing the sauce)  
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns A small handful  
⭐ Star anise 3 pieces (broken apart)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Chopping board  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥄 Wok spoon/soup ladle  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Bowls (several)  
🍽️ Plate  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Wash the pork belly and cut into large pieces about a palm's width. Place in cold water in a pot, add scallion segments, ginger slices, and cooking wine.  
**Note**: Blanch in cold water to thoroughly remove blood and gamey smell.  

2. Once the water boils, skim off the foam. Reduce to low heat, cover, and cook for 30 minutes until the meat is about 70-80% done. Remove and trim into square blocks, discarding excess edges.  
3. Prepare the fermented tofu sauce: Crush the fermented tofu in a bowl until no large lumps remain. Add white sugar, MSG, chicken bouillon, minced scallion, minced ginger, a large amount of minced garlic, dark soy sauce, cooking wine, broken star anise, and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Mix well.  
4. Slice the cooked meat into large slices about the thickness of three one-yuan coins stacked. Place into the bowl with the fermented tofu sauce and use your hands to mix thoroughly, ensuring every slice is coated with the sauce.  
5. Take a bowl, first place two slices of meat at the bottom. Then, arrange the marinated meat slices skin-side up, overlapping one by one in your hand to form a fan shape. Place them skin-side up into the bowl, forming an arched shape. Pour any remaining meat pieces and the fermented tofu sauce over the top.  
6. Place the bowl in a steamer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover and steam for 1.5 hours until the meat is tender and soft.  
**Key Point**: The steaming time must be sufficient to allow the fat from the pork belly to fully render, achieving a melt-in-the-mouth texture.  

7. Remove the steaming bowl. Quickly invert it onto a plate, carefully lift the bowl, and it is ready.  
**Note**: Inverting must be steady and fast to prevent the meat slices from falling apart or the sauce from spilling.  

## Health Advice  
Although this fermented tofu pork is rich and fatty, the steaming process renders out some of the fat. The probiotics produced during the fermentation of the tofu (though deactivated by high heat) also contribute a unique flavor. It is fine to enjoy occasionally. Suitable for those needing energy; those with high blood lipids are advised to only have a small taste.
