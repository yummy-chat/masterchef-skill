# 🥚 Steamed Egg Custard
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs
🧂 Salt
🛢️ Sesame oil (or sesame oil)
🥢 Light soy sauce
🥢 Premium soy sauce
🍶 White vinegar
🍶 Cooking wine
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Whisking tool
🪄 Strainer
🍲 Steamer
🛡️ Lid
🛡️ Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk until the egg mixture is even.
2. Add 3g of salt.
3. Optionally add 2ml of white vinegar or cooking wine to remove the eggy smell.
4. Add purified water at 70°C, 1 to 1.5 times the volume of the egg mixture, and stir well.
5. Strain the egg mixture to remove foam, ensuring a smoother surface for the finished dish.
6. Pour 50ml of water into the steamer, bring to a boil, then place the bowl with the egg mixture inside.
7. The steaming bowl must be covered with a lid, an inverted concave iron plate, or wrapped with plastic wrap to prevent steam droplets from falling in and causing a honeycomb texture in the custard.
8. Steam over medium heat for 10 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it sit for 5 minutes; or steam over high heat for 5 minutes.
9. Gently shake the bowl. If the custard is jelly-like with no obvious liquid movement, it is fully cooked and ready to be removed from the steamer.
10. After removing from the steamer, drizzle with sesame oil and light soy sauce for seasoning.

## Health Tips
Steamed egg custard has a high protein absorption rate, making it suitable for the elderly, children, and those with weak digestive systems. Be sure to cover the bowl during steaming to prevent water droplets from entering, and control the water volume to 1-1.5 times the egg mixture for the best results.
