## Cooking Steps
1.  Soak the pork ribs and rice in clean water separately. Soak the ribs for about 20 minutes to remove blood, and soak the rice in advance to allow it to hydrate.
    **Note**: If the pork has a strong smell, you can blanch it with scallions, ginger, and cooking wine before using.
2.  Drain the soaked ribs, add cooking wine and a pinch of salt, mix well, and marinate for a while.
3.  Slice the ginger. Peel and cut the onion into pieces. Peel the taro and cut into thick slices or large chunks. Cut the carrot into pieces. Chop the scallions. Slice the Chinese sausage.
    **Note**: Cut the taro into relatively large chunks to preserve its soft and sticky texture.
4.  Pat the marinated ribs dry with kitchen paper to prevent oil splattering when adding them to the wok.
5.  Heat the wok, add cooking oil, and heat over medium heat. Add the dried ribs and fry until both sides are golden brown.
    **Note**: Fry until a deep golden brown to enhance flavor through the Maillard reaction.
6.  Reduce the heat to low, add the onion, scallions, and ginger slices, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add bay leaves, star anise, and cinnamon stick, and stir-fry together.
    **Note**: Use a small amount of cinnamon stick to avoid bitterness.
7.  Turn the heat to the lowest setting. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, cooking wine, sugar, salt, and chicken bouillon. Stir-fry evenly.
    **Note**: Operate on the lowest heat throughout this step to prevent the seasonings from burning.
8.  Add approximately 450 ml of boiling water. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover the wok, and simmer for 10 minutes.
    **Note**: You must add boiling water; adding cold water will make the rib meat tough.
9.  After simmering, pour all the ingredients in the wok along with the liquid into the inner pot of the rice cooker. Remove and discard the ginger slices, bay leaves, star anise, and other spices.
10. Add the root vegetables and the soaked rice in order. Then cover the surface entirely with the sliced Chinese sausage. Finally, drizzle in one tablespoon of sesame oil.
    **Note**: For a drier texture, you can remove some of the liquid before adding the rice. The sausage should cover the surface.
11. Close the rice cooker lid. Press the "Steam Rice" or standard cook setting and cook until the program finishes.
12. Open the lid. Use a rice paddle to thoroughly mix the rice, sausage, vegetables, and the ribs at the bottom.
    **Note**: For enhanced flavor, you can optionally add blanched leafy greens and a fried egg, then close the lid and let it steam for another 10 minutes before mixing.

## Health Advice
This braised rice with pork ribs and Chinese sausage is rich in fat and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost (e.g., manual laborers, individuals aiming for muscle gain). It is recommended to serve it with a portion of stir-fried vegetables to help cut the richness. Chinese sausage and pork ribs are relatively high in salt content; those with high blood pressure or on a weight-loss diet should consume it in moderation.
