# Chinese Yam and Black Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Chinese yam About 1 piece (peeled, cut into rolling chunks)
🐔 Black chicken 1 whole (chopped into four large pieces)
🫚 Ginger 2-3 slices
🍄 Dried Cordyceps flowers A small handful
🍄🟫 Dried shiitake mushrooms 3-4 pieces
🍒 Goji berries About 10 grams
🧂 Salt To taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍲 Clay pot
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Plate/bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the Chinese yam and cut into rolling chunks for later use.
   **Note**: The mucilage of Chinese yam may irritate the skin; it is recommended to wear gloves while handling.
2. Rinse the black chicken clean and chop into four large pieces; slice the ginger; rinse the dried Cordyceps flowers, dried shiitake mushrooms, and goji berries separately with clean water and set aside.
3. Place the black chicken pieces, Chinese yam chunks, ginger slices, dried Cordyceps flowers, and dried shiitake mushrooms together in a clay pot. Add enough water to completely submerge all ingredients.
4. Bring to a boil over heat, then carefully skim off the foam with a spoon.
   **Key point**: The foam must be thoroughly removed, otherwise the soup will be cloudy and have a fishy smell.
5. After skimming off the foam, cover the pot with a lid, reduce to the lowest heat, and simmer slowly for about 2 hours.
   **State check**: Maintain a state where the soup is barely simmering with a gentle "bubbling" sound, avoiding high heat which would make the soup cloudy and the meat tough.
6. Once done, add salt to taste, then add the goji berries. Cook for a short while longer, then turn off the heat and serve.
   **Key point**: Salt must be added just before removing from heat; adding salt too early will make the black chicken meat tough.

## Health Advice
Chinese yam strengthens the spleen, black chicken replenishes Qi, and Cordyceps flowers combined with goji berries help boost immunity. This soup is suitable for autumn consumption and is suitable for all ages. However, those with high uric acid levels or those needing to control their weight should drink less soup and eat more meat. When handling Chinese yam, remember to wear gloves to avoid skin irritation from the mucilage.
