# Lavender Syrup
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌸 Fresh lavender 1 bunch (only the buds can be used to reduce grassy taste and impurities)  
💧 Water, as needed (enough to submerge the lavender)  
🍋 Lemon juice, as needed (for adjusting and setting color)  
🍬 White granulated sugar, about 1:1 (relative to the volume of the filtered liquid)

## Cookware & Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Snow pot (hammered pot)  
🥄 Strainer  
🍶 Glass measuring cup  
🍯 Mason jar (glass jar)

## Instructions
1. Trim the roots of the lavender with kitchen scissors and wash in clean water.  
   **Key point**: It is recommended to pick only the purple buds to avoid the grassy taste from stems and to facilitate cleaning; keeping the stems is also possible, but extra care is needed for subsequent filtration.
2. Place the prepared lavender in a pot, add water, and heat to a boil. At this point, the liquid will appear dark green or brown.
3. Squeeze an appropriate amount of fresh lemon juice into the pot. The liquid will immediately undergo a color change reaction, turning purple-red.
4. Use a strainer to remove the lavender stems and impurities, retaining only the purple-red liquid.
5. Pour the filtered liquid back into the pot, add white granulated sugar at about a 1:1 ratio, and heat while stirring until the sugar is fully dissolved and the liquid reaches the desired consistency.
6. Pour the finished syrup into a clean glass jar and seal tightly for storage.

## Health Advice
This lavender syrup is rich in natural floral aroma and can be used to enhance drinks or desserts, making it suitable for those who enjoy creative cuisine. However, it is high in sugar, so those controlling sugar intake should use it in moderation. Note that the lemon juice is key for adjusting the color; insufficient amounts will result in a loss of the dreamy purple hue.
