# Oven-Baked Spicy Sichuan Pork Skin
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Raw pork skin, about 2 lbs
🫚 Sliced ginger, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🌶️ Chili powder, to taste
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍲 Large pot
🔪 Chef knife, cutting board
🕸️ Baking rack/drying rack
♨️ Oven

## Instructions
1. Rinse raw pork skin under clean water and set aside.
2. Place cold water, pork skin, sliced ginger, and cooking wine in a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and continue cooking for about 10 minutes, until the pork skin is fully cooked and firm.
**Key point**: Ginger and cooking wine are used to remove any gamey smell; adjust based on the intensity of the pork skin's odor.
3. Remove the pork skin. While still hot, use a knife to scrape off the excess white fat layer from the inner side.
**Key point**: Be sure to remove all the fat completely; this is the key to a non-greasy final result.
4. Place the cleaned pork skin in a well-ventilated area to dry completely, or hang it up by threading, until the skin is completely hard and dry, making a crisp sound when tapped.
**Note**: Drying time varies; check for the right texture rather than relying on a fixed time.
5. Preheat the oven to 200°C (approx. 390°F). Lay the dried, hard pork skin flat on a baking rack and place it in the middle oven rack. Bake for about 10 minutes.
**Texture check**: During baking, the pork skin will puff up from a flat state to a round, golden, and crispy state. Remove once it reaches this stage.
   **Alternative**: An air fryer can achieve a similar effect, yielding a good puff and low oiliness.
6. Remove the baked pork skin, cut into strips, and place in a bowl. Add chili powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, salt, and sugar. Toss evenly. Serve directly as a spicy snack.
7. Soak the baked pork skin in water until soft, then stir-fry with vegetables such as green peppers for a savory dish.

## Health Advice
Pork skin is rich in collagen but also high in calories. It is recommended to consume a small handful per serving and pair with vegetables for a more balanced meal. Suitable for those who enjoy a crispy texture and are not prone to excessive internal heat. Those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
