# Clear-Braised Beef
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef brisket or short ribs, as needed (preferably cuts with sinew and fat, cut into 3-4 cm chunks)  
🥬 White radish, as needed (optional side vegetable)  
🧅 Scallions / garlic sprouts, as needed (for aroma when serving)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, one small piece (optional)  
🌶️ Black and white peppercorns, one small handful  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), a little (optional for blanching)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (add to dipping sauce or soup when eating; do not add during simmering)  
🍯 Light soy sauce, to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🫒 Green Sichuan pepper oil, to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds, to taste (for dipping sauce)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔥 Wok  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🚿 Sink  
🥣 Bowls, plates  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Soak the beef in cold water for 2–3 hours or more, changing the water several times, until most of the blood has leached out.  
**Key point**: This is crucial for a clear broth.

2. Add plenty of water to the wok, place the beef in cold water, and heat over high flame. Just before the water comes to a boil, optionally add a little baijiu. After boiling, use a slotted spoon to continuously skim off the foam and stir the beef. Once most of the foam is removed, turn off the heat immediately and remove the beef.  
**Key point**: Use plenty of water to allow the temperature to rise slowly, forcing out the blood foam fully; turn off the heat as soon as it boils to prevent excessive loss of beef flavor and fat.

3. Remove the beef and rinse thoroughly under warm or hot water until the surface foam is gone and the water runs clear.  
**Key point**: Use warm/hot water for rinsing to prevent the beef fibers from contracting due to cold.

4. Place the cleaned beef into a clay pot, add enough boiling water, then add fried ginger slices, raw smashed ginger chunks, black and white peppercorns, and dried tangerine peel. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer gently, keeping the surface barely bubbling.  
**Key point**: Do not add salt throughout; simmer slowly over low heat, avoiding continuous vigorous boiling, until the beef can be easily pierced through with chopsticks.

5. Cut the white radish into chunks. In a separate pot, add the radish to cold water, bring to a boil, and cook for another 2–3 minutes. Remove and rinse under cold water. Once the beef is done simmering, add the radish and cook until tender.  
6. Before serving, prepare the dipping sauce: in a bowl, combine scallions/garlic sprouts and toasted sesame seeds. Drizzle in green Sichuan pepper oil, chili oil, and light soy sauce, then ladle in a spoonful of the beef broth and stir well.  
7. Once the beef is fork-tender, sprinkle with scallions/garlic sprouts and serve with the dipping sauce.

## Health Tips  
Clear-braised beef is low in fat and high in protein. Pairing it with white radish aids digestion, making it ideal for warming up in autumn/winter or for fitness and weight-loss diets. Note that no salt is added during simmering; control the amount of soy sauce and oil in the dipping sauce to avoid excess sodium and calories.
