# Homestyle Street Steak
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef (hind leg meat) Appropriate amount (cut into thick slices against the grain)  
🧅 Onion Appropriate amount (cut into strips)  
🫚 Ginger Appropriate amount (sliced or minced)  
🥦 Broccoli Appropriate amount (blanched until cooked)  
🍝 Spaghetti Appropriate amount (pre-cooked and drained)  
🥚 Egg 1 piece  
🥝 Kiwi 1 piece (peeled and mashed into a paste, used for tenderizing meat)  
🧂 Salt 10g  
🤍 White pepper powder 15g  
🍾 Light soy sauce 15g  
🌶️ Black pepper powder 5g (used in two portions)  
🫒 Cooking oil 15g (for marinating) + appropriate amount (for frying)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g  
🧈 Butter 15g  
🍬 White sugar 10g  
🌽 Cornstarch 10g  
💧 Water 20g (mixed with cornstarch to make a cornstarch slurry)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Electric frying pan / skillet  
🥢 Tongs  
🥄 Measuring cup / bowl  
🫙 Sealed ziplock bag  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef into thick slices against the grain and place them in a glass bowl.
   **Key point**: Cutting against the grain severs the muscle fibers, making the texture more tender.
2. Add onion strips, ginger slices, salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, half of the black pepper powder, cooking oil, and kiwi mash. Mix thoroughly by hand.
   **Key point**: Kiwi mash is a natural meat tenderizer that softens the meat.
3. Seal the mixed beef and refrigerate for approximately 30 minutes to marinate.
   **Note**: Do not marinate for too long, otherwise the meat will become mushy.
4. Prepare the sauce: In a pot, add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, butter, white sugar, and the remaining black pepper powder. Heat over low heat until melted and combined. Pour in the cornstarch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat and set aside.
5. Pre-cook the spaghetti until done and drain. Blanch the broccoli until cooked.
6. Heat oil in a frying pan. Place the marinated beef slices in the pan and fry over medium-low heat for about 5 minutes, turning multiple times during the process. Fry until both sides are browned and the inside is fully cooked.
   **Key point**: Regular beef must be cooked through to ensure food safety.
7. Crack an egg into an empty space in the pan. Sprinkle a little onion strips on top and fry using residual heat until cooked.
8. Add the pre-cooked spaghetti to the pan. Pour in the prepared sauce and quickly toss everything together so the noodles are evenly coated.
   **Note**: Work quickly to prevent sticking.
9. Remove the steak and cut it into small pieces on a cutting board. Plate together with the spaghetti, fried egg, and broccoli. Finally, drizzle a little more sauce on top.

## Health Advice
Using kiwi mash as a meat tenderizer is a highlight of this steak recipe, but do not marinate for more than half an hour, otherwise the texture will become mushy. The combination of broccoli and spaghetti is well-balanced, making this dish suitable for recreating a Western-style meal at home. Note that the beef must be cooked through to ensure food safety.
