# Peach Syrup
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍑 Peach 2 pieces (slightly firm, not overly soft)  
🍄 Dried white fungus 3 pieces (pre-soaked)  
🌿 Perilla leaves a small handful  
🍉 Mixed fruit cubes as needed (e.g., watermelon cubes, blueberries, optional)  
🍋 Lemon half (juiced)  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar 10 ml  
🍬 Rock sugar to taste (can substitute with white sugar)  
🧊 Ice water as needed (for cooling peaches)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Glass soup pot  
🥄 Metal spoon/slotted spoon  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🫗 Fine mesh strainer  
🔪 Cutting board, fruit knife  
🧴 Measuring cup/small glass bowl  
🧊 Refrigerator  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Wash and peel the peaches, set aside the peels. Pre-soak the dried white fungus in clean water. Wash and drain the perilla leaves.  
2. Place the soaked white fungus in a soup pot, add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer until the gelatinous texture is released.  
3. In another pot, bring water to a boil. Add the whole peaches and cook for about 5–8 minutes until par-cooked. Lightly press the peaches with a finger—once slightly soft, remove and immediately submerge in ice water to cool.  
4. Continue to simmer the white fungus soup over low heat for 45 minutes, until the broth becomes thick and gelatinous.  
5. Add the perilla leaves and reserved peach peels to the white fungus soup. Simmer for a short while to allow the broth to absorb the peach aroma and turn pink.  
6. Strain the broth using a fine mesh strainer to remove the white fungus, perilla leaves, and peach peels. If you prefer a textured consistency, skip this step.  
7. Add rock sugar to the strained syrup and stir until completely dissolved.  
8. When the syrup cools to 40–50°C, squeeze in half a lemon's juice and add 10 ml of apple cider vinegar. Stir well—the syrup will turn a more pinkish-purple color.  
9. Place the cooled peaches into the seasoned syrup, cover the container, and refrigerate for about 2 hours to allow the peaches to fully absorb the flavors.  
10. Remove the peaches and plate them, optionally garnishing with fresh fruit cubes. Drizzle the chilled syrup over the top and enjoy.  

## Health Advice  
Peach syrup is rich in Vitamin C and dietary fiber, making it ideal for cooling off in summer. However, the amount of rock sugar should be controlled to reduce sugar intake. It tastes best when served chilled. Elderly individuals and children should be mindful of the temperature to avoid irritating the stomach.
