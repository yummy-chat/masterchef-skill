# Milk Song Gao (Steamed Milk Cake)
Cuisine: Common Home-style
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 White sugar 100g
🥛 Milk 300ml (room temperature)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🥔 Tapioca starch 200g
🍄 Dry yeast 5g
🔬 Baking powder 5g
🫒 Cooking oil 20g (odorless vegetable oil, added to batter for degassing)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for greasing the mold and surface to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🫙 Glass bowl
🍴 Spatula
🥄 Hand whisk
🖌️ Brush
🍽️ Stainless steel flat tray (used as mold)
♨️ Steamer
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board

## Steps
1. Add 100g white sugar to 200ml boiling water, stir until completely dissolved to make sugar water.
2. Pour room temperature milk and dry yeast into the sugar water, stir well.
   **Note**: Milk temperature should not be too high, to avoid killing the yeast.
3. In another bowl, mix all-purpose flour, tapioca starch, and baking powder evenly.
4. Pour the milk-yeast mixture into the mixed dry ingredients, stir with a whisk until there is no dry flour and the batter is smooth and thick.
   **Consistency check**: When lifting the whisk, the batter should flow down in a ribbon-like stream.
5. Cover the batter with plastic wrap and let it ferment in a warm place until it doubles in volume and the surface is covered with bubbles.
   **Key point**: Fermentation time depends on the ambient temperature.
6. Add 20g cooking oil to the fermented batter, quickly stir with chopsticks for 2 to 3 minutes to thoroughly release the gas.
   **Key point**: Thorough degassing results in a fine texture in the cake.
7. Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the inner wall of the stainless steel flat tray, pour in the degassed batter, and gently tap the tray a few times to release large air bubbles.
   **Key point**: Greasing the tray is essential for easy unmolding.
8. Place it again in a warm place for a second fermentation until the batter rises to 80-90% full in the mold.
9. Add water to the steamer and bring to a boil, place the batter in, steam over high heat for 30 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before opening the lid.
   **Key point**: Resting prevents the cake from shrinking when exposed to cold air.
10. Remove and brush the surface with a layer of cooked cooking oil, let it cool slightly, then gently pry the edges with your hands to help unmold, cut into pieces and serve.
    **Key point**: Brushing the surface with oil prevents dryness and hardening, and facilitates unmolding.

## Health Advice
Milk Song Gao is rich in carbohydrates, suitable as breakfast or afternoon tea, but has a high sugar content, so people controlling sugar intake should consume it in moderation. Fermented flour foods are easy to digest, and pairing with milk can supplement protein.
