# Stir-Fried Cat Ear Noodles
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 All-purpose flour, as needed
💧 Water, as needed
🥩 Lean meat, as needed (shredded, marinated in advance)
🥚 Eggs, as needed
🍅 Tomato, as needed (cut into chunks)
🥔 Potato, as needed (peeled, shredded, and soaked in water)
🫑 Green pepper, as needed (shredded)
🥨 Small fried dough twists, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for kneading dough and seasoning)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed
🌿 Ground white pepper, two shakes
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🍶 Fragrant vinegar (aged vinegar), as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading dough)
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🥖 Rolling pin
🪝 Metal strainer (or patterned cutting board)
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Bowl/Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Knead and rest dough: In a bowl, combine flour with a pinch of salt. Gradually add water in batches, stirring with chopsticks until flaky. Then knead into a smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let rest for about 15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Add water in batches, knead until the bowl, dough, and hands are clean. Adequate resting makes the dough soft and easy to work with.
2. Prepare ingredients: Shred the lean meat. Cut the tomato into chunks. Peel and shred the potato, then soak in water. Shred the green pepper. Beat the eggs and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Soaking shredded potatoes in water removes excess starch, keeping them crispy.
3. Mix the sauce: In a small bowl, add salt, sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground white pepper, and sesame oil. Stir well.
   **Key Point**: Pre-mixing the sauce allows even seasoning and saves time when added to the wok.
4. Shape cat ear noodles: Knead the rested dough again. Roll it out into a sheet about 1 cm thick. Sprinkle with dry flour to prevent sticking. First cut into strips, then into small pieces. Using your thumb, press and roll each piece against a metal strainer or patterned cutting board to form curled, textured cat ear shapes.
   **Key Point**: Apply even pressure with your thumb to create naturally curled and clearly textured cat ears that easily hold the sauce.
5. Cook cat ear noodles: Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the cat ears and gently stir with a spoon to prevent sticking. After it returns to a boil, add a small amount of cold water. Repeat this process three times, until the cat ears float and have noticeably expanded to about 90% done. Remove immediately, rinse under cold water, and drain.
   **Key Point**: Adding cold water ensures even cooking inside and out. Rinsing with cold water enhances a chewy texture and prevents sticking.
6. Stir-fry base ingredients: Heat the wok, then add cold oil. Sauté minced garlic and dried chili peppers until fragrant. Add the marinated shredded meat and stir-fry until fully cooked. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir until aromatic. Add the tomato chunks and cook until they release juice. Add the scrambled eggs and drained shredded potatoes, stirring well. Add a small amount of hot water and bring to a boil. Taste and add more salt if needed.
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry over high heat. Add the shredded potatoes later to keep them crunchy.
7. Combine and reduce sauce: Add the cooked cat ears. Stir-fry over high heat, ensuring they are evenly coated with the sauce. Continue stirring until the sauce thickens. Then add the shredded green pepper and small fried dough twists. Quickly stir-fry for a few seconds.
   **Key Point**: Add the green pepper and fried dough twists last to keep the pepper crisp and the twists crunchy.
8. Serve: Drizzle fragrant vinegar along the edge of the wok. Quickly toss everything together, then immediately plate.
   **Key Point**: Adding vinegar just before serving maximizes its aroma and tanginess, enhancing the wok hei (breath of the wok).

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced mix of carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables, making it suitable for teenagers or office workers needing an energy boost. However, rinsing the cat ears in cold water may improve their chewy texture but can be hard on sensitive stomachs. Those with digestive issues should consume in moderation to avoid cold or hot stimulation.
