# Chicken Thigh and Egg Sandwich
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh fillet 1 piece (pre-marinated, can be pan-fried directly from frozen)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🥬 Lettuce appropriate amount (washed and drained)  
🍞 Sandwich bread 2-3 slices  
🥛 White sauce appropriate amount (mayonnaise or cream cheese)  
🟡 Yellow mustard appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥢 Food tongs  
🫗 Lid  
🔪 Cutting board  
🥄 Spatula  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Serrated knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Heat an appropriate amount of cooking oil in a pan over medium-low heat, place the chicken thigh fillet in the pan, and cover with a lid to create a slightly pressurized environment for faster cooking.  
   **Key point**: The chicken fillet does not need to be thawed in advance; adjust the cooking time based on thickness to ensure it is fully cooked through.  
2. When the chicken fillet is almost done, crack the egg into an empty spot in the pan, and cook over residual or low heat until the egg white is set; the yolk can remain runny or be fully cooked.  
3. Spread white sauce evenly on both sides of the bread slices. Layer lettuce, the cooked chicken fillet, squeeze yellow mustard on top, then place the fried egg, and cover with another slice of bread.  
4. Wrap the sandwich tightly with plastic wrap, then cut it in half with a serrated knife.  
   **Note**: Wrapping it tightly ensures clean cuts and prevents the filling from spilling out.  

## Health Advice  
Chicken thighs and eggs provide high-quality animal protein. The white sauce adds flavor, and the lettuce supplies dietary fiber. However, be cautious with the amount of yellow mustard, as too much can make the taste excessively spicy and pungent. This sandwich is suitable for fitness enthusiasts and office workers as a simple meal, but total calorie intake should be monitored.
