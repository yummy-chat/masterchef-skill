# Green Vegetable and Mushroom Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Green vegetables (such as Shanghai bok choy) - appropriate amount (core ingredient, must remain bright green)
🍄 Mushrooms - appropriate amount (side ingredient, diced after blanching)
🫘 Baking soda - appropriate amount (added during blanching to prevent the vegetables from turning yellow)
🫒 Cooking oil - appropriate amount (add a small amount during blanching; add more when mixing the filling, five-spice oil recommended, for color protection and moisture prevention)
🧂 Salt - appropriate amount (for seasoning the filling)
🍬 White sugar - appropriate amount (for blending and enhancing freshness in the filling)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Cooking pot
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Kitchen knife
🥟 Steamer basket
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the green vegetables. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add a small amount of cooking oil and an appropriate amount of baking soda. Once the water is boiling, add the vegetables and blanch until just cooked, then quickly remove them.
   **Note**: Baking soda neutralizes oxalic acid and prevents the leaves from turning yellow; oil helps protect the color. Blanching time should be just until cooked, not too long.
2. Immediately transfer the blanched vegetables into ice water to cool, using the temperature difference to lock in the green color.
3. Remove the cooled vegetables, squeeze out the water thoroughly, and then chop finely.
   **Note**: Be sure to squeeze dry to prevent the filling from releasing water later.
4. Blanch the mushrooms, then cut into small dices.
5. Place the chopped vegetables and mushroom dices into a large bowl. First, add cooking oil and mix well, ensuring every leaf piece is coated with oil to form a protective layer.
   **Key point**: Oil before salt prevents the vegetables from releasing water after adding salt, and makes the filling moist and glossy.
6. Add an appropriate amount of salt and white sugar, mix again, and the filling is ready. Season simply to highlight the fresh flavors of the vegetables and mushrooms.
7. Wrap an appropriate amount of filling with prepared bun wrappers, pinching into a bun shape.
8. Place the buns into a steamer basket lined with a cloth or brushed with oil, and allow them to proof.
9. Bring water to a boil in a pot, place the steamer basket on top, cover tightly, and steam over high heat. Once steam rises, time for **8 minutes**. Remove the lid and take out the buns immediately when done.

## Health Advice
The combination of green vegetables and mushrooms is low in fat and high in fiber, suitable for weight loss or blood pressure control. Adding baking soda and oil during blanching helps preserve the color, but the water must be squeezed out to prevent the buns from collapsing. The technique of adding oil before salt is key to keeping the filling dry.
