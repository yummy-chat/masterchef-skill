# Extra Rich Chocolate Sourdough Bread  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g (50g for starter + 250g for main dough)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 25g (mix with flour in advance)  
🧪 Fresh yeast 0.7g (or dry yeast 0.3g)  
💧 Warm water 278g (40g for starter + 238g for main dough, water temperature ≤40°C)  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🌰 Chopped walnuts 40g  
🍫 Dark chocolate chunks 75g (pure cocoa butter dark chocolate)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl/measuring cup  
🥚 Whisk  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
📄 Plastic wrap  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🔪 Bench scraper  
🔪 Scoring knife (or sharp blade)  
🪨 Baking stone  
🍽️ Drip tray/baking sheet  
♨️ Oven  
📜 Parchment paper  
⏱️ Timer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the Polish starter**: Dissolve yeast in warm water, add 50g all-purpose flour and stir into a paste. Ferment until volume triples, surface is puffed and full of bubbles. Can be refrigerated overnight for later use.  
2. **Mix main dough and autolyse**: Dissolve 5g salt in 238g warm water. Pre-mix 250g all-purpose flour with 25g cocoa powder, then pour into salted water. Use a spatula and hands to briefly mix until no dry flour remains—no need to knead. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 20 minutes to allow flour to fully absorb water.  
3. **Add starter and combine**: Add the fermented Polish starter to the rested dough. Dip hands in water and use pinching, slapping, and folding motions to combine the two doughs until uniform in color and smooth on the surface.  
4. **Bulk fermentation and fold to strengthen gluten**: Cover dough with plastic wrap, rest at room temperature for 40 minutes, then perform one coil fold: dip hands in water, lift and fold the dough from all four sides toward the center until surface is smooth. Rest another 40 minutes, then perform a second fold. The dough should now be able to stretch into a thinner membrane.  
5. **Add inclusions and continue fermentation**: Spread chopped walnuts and dark chocolate chunks over the dough surface. Use folding motions to evenly incorporate. Rest again for 40 minutes until small bubbles appear on the surface, then perform a final fold if needed.  
6. **Cold fermentation**: Transfer dough to a clean container, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for approximately 8 hours.  
7. **Return to temperature, divide, and shape**: The next day, remove dough and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes. Generously dust the work surface and dough surface with flour to prevent sticking. Turn out the dough, divide into 3 equal portions, gently round into pre-shapes, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 30 minutes. After resting, flatten each piece with your palm, roll from top to bottom, and shape into a plump oval. Preserve internal gases—do not roll too tightly.  
8. **Final proof**: Place shaped doughs on floured parchment paper, separate them with thick paper strips, cover with plastic wrap, and proof at room temperature for about 60 minutes, until dough is visibly puffed and feels lighter.  
9. **Preheat and score**: Place baking stone inside the oven and preheat to 230°C. Quickly score the surface of the proofed doughs with slashes.  
10. **Bake**: Place a drip tray at the bottom of the oven and add two ice cubes to create steam. Transfer the doughs with parchment paper into the oven, immediately cover the tops with another sheet of parchment paper. Bake at 230°C for about 25 minutes, until the bottoms are well browned, and the scores have visibly opened during the initial stage.  
11. **Finish**: Remove and cool. Enjoy the extra rich chocolate sourdough with a crispy crust and a soft, moist interior.  

## Health Suggestions  
This extra rich chocolate sourdough is high in fiber and low in oil and sugar, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during fitness or weight loss periods. However, the chocolate chunks and walnuts are calorie-dense, so it’s recommended to consume no more than one-third per serving. Pairing with black coffee can enhance antioxidant absorption. Cold fermentation makes it easier to digest.
