## Steps
1.  Choose green-shell crayfish; avoid using red-shell ones.
2.  Use a stiff brush to thoroughly scrub the belly, head, and back of the crayfish; grasp the middle tail fin, twist, and pull out the intestine.
3.  Mix the prepared crayfish with high-proof liquor and a handful of salt, then marinate for 10 minutes.
4.  Pre-soak dried Sichuan pepper and dried chili peppers in warm water until soft; cut the cucumber into strips and set aside.
5.  Pour sufficient rapeseed oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot. Add the crayfish; on a home stove, fry for about 30 seconds until the shells turn bright red and the meat is fully cooked. Remove with a slotted spoon.
6.  Leave a thin layer of oil in the wok, reduce heat to the lowest setting. First, fry scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Then add hot pot base and red oil doubanjiang, stir-frying on low heat until melted and aromatic. Finally, add the soaked Sichuan pepper and dried chili peppers, stirring evenly.
7.  Pour in the beer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Add sugar, chicken bouillon, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and five-spice powder. Add the fried crayfish, then supplement with enough hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, cover with a lid, and simmer on low heat for 10 minutes.
8.  Turn off the heat and let the crayfish soak in the broth for 30 minutes.
9.  Turn the heat to high, add the cucumber strips, and bring the broth to a boil again. Reduce the sauce slightly, then remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Tips
Spicy crayfish paired with cucumber strips can help refresh and cut through the grease. However, the seasoning is high in oil and salt, so intake should be controlled. Those with high blood lipids or gout are advised to consume only a small amount.
