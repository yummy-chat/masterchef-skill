# Tofu Braised with Chinese Cabbage
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Tofu – 1 block (firm or silken, cut into triangular pieces)
🥬 Chinese cabbage – about half a head (remove the root, use large leaves)
🍜 Wide vermicelli – about 100g (dry, use directly without soaking)
🥚 Eggs – 2 (beat with a pinch of salt)
🫘 Soybean paste – 1 tablespoon
🧂 Light soy sauce – 2 tablespoons
🍬 Sugar – 1 tablespoon
🍶 Vinegar – to taste
🍾 Cooking wine – to taste
🫗 Dark soy sauce – to taste
🧂 Salt – a little (add a pinch when beating eggs, then add a bit more in the sauce)
💧 Water – as needed (for mixing the sauce and during braising)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder – a pinch
🌻 Cooking oil – as needed
🧅 Scallions – as needed (sprinkle after cooking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowl, 🥢 chopsticks
🍳 Nonstick pan (also used as braising pot)
🫖 Lid

## Cooking Steps
1.  Cut the tofu into triangular pieces. Remove the root of the Chinese cabbage and use the large leaves. Beat the eggs with a pinch of salt and set aside. Prepare the wide vermicelli.
2.  In a bowl, combine soybean paste, light soy sauce, sugar, vinegar, cooking wine, dark soy sauce, salt, and water. Stir well to make the sauce.
3.  Heat the pan, add cold oil, then place the tofu pieces in. First, pour in half of the beaten eggs. Fry over medium-low heat until the bottom is crispy and golden. Flip the tofu, pour in the remaining eggs, gently spread them, and fry until both sides are golden. Remove and set aside.
4.  Without washing the pan, spread a layer of wide vermicelli on the bottom, then a layer of Chinese cabbage leaves. Place the fried tofu and egg pieces on top.
**Nonstick tip**: If the pan tends to stick, use a layered arrangement: “cabbage – vermicelli – cabbage – tofu.”

5.  Pour in the prepared sauce, add a little more water, and sprinkle in the thirteen-spice powder.
6.  Cover with the lid, reduce the heat to the lowest, and braise for 8 minutes.
7.  Remove the lid, turn off the heat. Transfer the dish to a plate and sprinkle with chopped scallions.

## Health Advice
This dish provides high-quality protein from tofu and eggs, dietary fiber from Chinese cabbage, and increased satiety from wide vermicelli. It is suitable for those on a light diet or managing their weight. Note that soybean paste and light soy sauce already contain salt; for a low-sodium diet, use less salt.
