# Korean-Style Seafood Soft Tofu Soup
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Zucchini 80g (diced)
🧅 Onion 80g (about 1/4, diced)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms 60g (2 pieces, sliced)
🧅 Green onion 25g (chopped into scallions)
🐚 Clams 8 pieces (cleaned)
🦪 Mussels 6 pieces (cleaned)
🦐 Large shrimp 2 pieces (cleaned)
🫘 Silken tofu 1 box
🥚 Egg 1 piece
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers 10g each (sliced into rings)

🫒 Sesame oil as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce 20g (2 tablespoons)
🌶️ Coarse chili powder 10g (1 tablespoon)
🌶️ Fine chili powder 10g (1 tablespoon)
💧 Water 600g (about 2 bowls)
🧂 White pepper powder 0.5g (a pinch)
🧂 Salt 3g (1 level teaspoon)
🧂 Beef bouillon powder 3g (1 heaping teaspoon)
🧂 MSG 3g (1 teaspoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g (half a tablespoon)
🧄 Minced garlic 15g (3 cloves)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Small clay pot
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer/soup ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Dice the zucchini and onion into small cubes, slice the shiitake mushrooms, chop the green onion into scallions, slice the green and red chili peppers into rings, and mince the garlic. Clean the clams, mussels, and large shrimp, and drain the water. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Preheat ingredients carefully to ensure uniform size.

2. Pour sesame oil into the clay pot and heat it. Add all the vegetable cubes, scallions, and sliced shiitake mushrooms. Stir-fry over high heat until the vegetables soften and release water.
   **Key point**: Sesame oil is the core flavor of the soup base; make sure to stir-fry thoroughly.

3. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add the light soy sauce, coarse chili powder, and fine chili powder. Continue stir-frying until the chili releases fragrance, being careful not to burn it.
   **Key point**: Slow stir-frying over low heat allows the chili to develop a toasty aroma, which is a key step for enhancing the soup base flavor.

4. Pour in the water and add all the seafood. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil. Then cover with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Leave a gap to prevent overflow; simmer over low heat to release the seafood’s umami.

5. Add the white pepper powder, salt, beef bouillon powder, MSG, oyster sauce, and minced garlic sequentially. Stir to combine.

6. Use a spoon to scoop the silken tofu into large chunks, and gently place them into the soup. Bring to a boil again over high heat, then cover and simmer over low heat for 5 minutes to allow the tofu to absorb the flavor.
   **Key point**: Minimize stirring after adding the tofu to prevent it from breaking apart.

7. Sprinkle in the green and red chili pepper rings. Crack a raw egg into the center of the soup. Turn the heat to high and cook for 1 minute.
   **Key point**: Do not overcook the egg; let the residual heat of the clay pot keep the yolk runny.

8. Turn off the heat and serve directly in the clay pot. When eating, stir the runny egg yolk into the soup.

## Health Advice
This Korean-style seafood soft tofu soup is rich in high-quality protein and minerals. The combination of seafood and tofu is suitable for individuals needing calcium supplementation or a low-fat diet. It is recommended to use less salt and MSG, letting the natural umami of the seafood provide flavor. Gout patients should control their seafood intake.
