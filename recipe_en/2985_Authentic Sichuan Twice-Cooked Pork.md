# Authentic Sichuan Twice-Cooked Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Skin-on pork belly (hind leg meat), as needed  
🧄 Garlic sprouts, as needed (flattened and cut into diagonal sections, separate white parts from green leaves)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (crushed for boiling, minced for stir-frying)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for deodorizing during boiling)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, a few (for boiling)  
🫘 Broad bean paste, about one tablespoon  
🍯 Sweet bean sauce, a little  
🧴 Soy sauce, a little  
🍶 Vinegar, a little (splashed along the edge of the wok)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for final seasoning)  
🍬 White sugar, a little (to enhance freshness)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔥 Fire tongs (with hooks, for holding the meat to singe the skin)  
🔫 Kitchen torch (to assist in singeing the pork skin)  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking and cooling the pork)  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Singe the skin**: Use fire tongs to hold the whole piece of skin-on pork and singe the skin over an open flame until the surface is charred black.  
**Key point**: Singeing until black thoroughly removes any remaining bristles and eliminates the pork skin’s unpleasant smell.  
2. **Scrape and wash**: Place the singed pork in clean water, scrape off the charred parts with a knife until the skin reveals a clean yellow color.  
**Key point**: Scrape thoroughly to ensure the skin is clean.  
3. **Boil**: Put the prepared pork into cold water, add crushed ginger, scallion segments, and a few Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium-low heat and boil for about 40 minutes.  
**Doneness check**: The pork is cooked when a chopstick can be easily inserted without blood seeping out.  
   **Key point**: Immediately remove the cooked pork and cool it in clean water.  
4. **Slice**: Slice the cooled pork into thin pieces about 2.5 mm thick.  
**Key point**: Slices should be uniform in thickness; too thick and they won't form the "oil lamp nest" shape, too thin and they may break during stir-frying.  
5. **Prepare ingredients**: Flatten the garlic sprouts and cut them diagonally into sections, separating the white parts from the green leaves. Mince the ginger and chopped pickled chilies.  
6. **Render the meat**: Heat the wok, add a little rapeseed oil to coat it, then add the pork slices and stir-fry slowly over medium-low heat.  
**Doneness check**: Fry until the fatty parts release oil and become translucent, and the slices curl into an “oil lamp nest” shape.  
   **Key point**: Slow stir-fry over medium-low heat to fully render excess fat, making the dish rich but not greasy.  
7. **Season**: Push the meat slices to one side of the wok, use the remaining oil to stir-fry the minced ginger and chopped pickled chilies until fragrant. Add the broad bean paste and fry until it releases red oil and aroma, then add a little sweet bean sauce and stir-fry evenly.  
**Key point**: Fry the broad bean paste and sweet bean sauce over low heat to avoid burning.  
8. **Aromatize**: Splash a little soy sauce and vinegar along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry quickly to combine.  
9. **Add vegetables**: First, add the white parts of the garlic sprouts and stir-fry briefly. Then add the green leaves and stir-fry quickly until just cooked.  
**Key point**: Add the green garlic sprout leaves last to keep them vibrant and fragrant.  
10. **Serve**: Add a little salt and white sugar to taste, stir-fry evenly, then serve.  
**Key point**: Add salt last, adjusting according to taste, as the broad bean paste and soy sauce already provide saltiness.  

## Health Advice  
The secret to twice-cooked pork being rich but not greasy lies in the "oil lamp nest" rendering method, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost or a satisfying treat. People with high blood lipids are advised to consume in moderation and pair with light vegetables for a more balanced meal.
