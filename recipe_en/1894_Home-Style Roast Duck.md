# Home-Style Roast Duck  
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 1 whole duck  
🧅 1 bunch of green onions (some cut into sections for marinating, some thinly sliced for serving)  
🫚 A few slices of ginger  
🧅 1/2 onion (sliced)  
🥟 7 spring pancake wrappers (pre-made)  

🧂 Salt to taste  
🧂 Chinese thirteen-spice powder to taste  
🧂 Five-spice powder to taste  
🧂 Ground bay leaves to taste  
🧂 Ground lemongrass to taste  
🍶 White vinegar to taste (for crispy skin water)  
🫒 Cooking oil to taste (for crispy skin water)  
🍬 White sugar to taste (for crispy skin water)  
🍯 Maltose (or honey) to taste (for crispy skin water)  
🍯 Sweet bean paste to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🧄 Minced garlic to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste (for dipping sauce)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🪝 Hooks or string  
🪣 Air pump (or straw)  
🖌️ Brush  
🔥 Oven  
🍗 Roasting rack  
♨️ Steamer  
🔪 Knife  

## Steps  
1. Clean the whole duck thoroughly. Place it in a wok, adding green onion sections, ginger slices, and sliced onion.  
2. Add salt, thirteen-spice powder, five-spice powder, ground bay leaves, ground lemongrass, and other spice powders. Pour in an appropriate amount of cooking wine or water. Marinate until the duck meat is fully infused with flavor.  
**Key point**: The marinating time must be sufficient to ensure the duck meat is fully flavored.  

3. Remove the marinated duck. Insert the air pump nozzle under the skin at the duck's neck and inflate until the duck body is plump and the skin is fully separated from the meat.  
4. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Repeatedly scald the duck's skin with the boiling water to tighten it quickly.  
**Key point**: The scalding must be even, covering every part of the duck.  

5. In a pan, mix white sugar, white vinegar, cooking oil, maltose, and a small amount of water. Boil until the mixture thickens into a crispy skin water.  
**Key point**: The ratio of sugar to vinegar affects the color and crispiness; it must be boiled until thick.  

6. Hang the scalded duck. Use a spoon to evenly and repeatedly pour the crispy skin water over the duck's entire surface until it is fully coated.  
**Key point**: Apply the crispy skin water evenly and multiple times to ensure every part is covered.  

7. Hang the duck with the crispy skin water applied in a well-ventilated area to allow the skin to dry completely.  
**Key point**: This is a crucial step for achieving crispy skin after roasting; ensure the skin is fully dry.  

8. Wrap the duck legs and wing tips with aluminum foil to prevent burning.  
9. Preheat the oven. Place the duck on a roasting rack and put it in the oven to roast.  
**Key point**: Adjust the roasting temperature and time based on your oven. Monitor the coloring during the process to avoid burning.  

10. While roasting, mix sweet bean paste, minced garlic, and light soy sauce to make the dipping sauce.  
11. Steam the pre-made spring pancake wrappers in a steamer until hot. Set aside.  
12. Once the duck is roasted, remove it and let it cool slightly. Use a knife to chop or slice the duck meat.  
**Doneness indicator**: The skin should be deep reddish-brown, with tender and juicy meat.  

13. To serve, place duck meat and shredded green onions on a spring pancake wrapper, spread with dipping sauce, roll it up, and enjoy.  

## Health Advice  
This is a high-protein dish, but the sugar and oil content in the crispy skin water is relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables when wrapping in pancakes and to reduce the proportion of duck skin consumed directly. Ideal for sharing at gatherings; those with high blood lipids should only eat in moderation.
