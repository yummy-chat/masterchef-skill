# Honorable Fragrant Pork Rib Rice 🍚
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🥩 Pork ribs: 1 strip (chopped into small pieces)
🥔 Potato: 1 (cut into large chunks)
🥕 Carrot: half (diced small)

## Cooking Steps 🍳
1. Slice scallions and julienne ginger; cut potato into large chunks and dice carrot into small pieces.
2. Place pork ribs in cold water in a pot, add scallion slices, ginger, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off foam, then remove ribs and drain well.
3. In another pot, add a dash of cooking oil. Over low heat, add rock sugar and stir continuously until completely melted into a reddish-brown caramel.
4. Add the blanched ribs and stir-fry quickly to coat evenly with caramel. Immediately add scallion slices and ginger, stir-frying for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Pour in enough boiling water to just submerge the ribs. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and fermented bean curd sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: Must use boiling water to prevent the meat from tightening.
6. Remove the lid, add potato chunks and carrot dices, stir well, then turn off the heat.
7. Place the rinsed rice into the inner pot of the rice cooker. Pour the ribs, vegetables, and all the cooking liquid from the wok on top. The liquid should just submerge the ingredients; if insufficient, add a little more boiling water.
8. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, select the normal rice cooking program, and let it finish automatically.
9. After opening the lid, use a spoon to loosen and mix the rice, ribs, and vegetables well. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and cooked sesame seeds.

## Health Advice 💪
This one-pot rice dish with ribs, potato, and carrots offers a balanced mix of carbs, protein, and vegetables, making it both nutritious and convenient. It’s recommended to control the amount of ribs to reduce fat and sodium intake. Potato can partially replace the staple rice, so reduce the rice portion accordingly. Suitable for those seeking a convenient yet nutritious meal.
