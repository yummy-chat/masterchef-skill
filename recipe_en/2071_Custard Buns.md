# Custard Buns
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks 4 pieces (steamed in advance)
🥚 Eggs 4 pieces (take yolks, steamed in advance)
🌾 All-purpose flour 150 g
🌾 Cake flour 150 g
🥛 Milk powder 60 g
🌽 Cornstarch 50 g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 60 g (for filling) + 5 g (for dough)
🧈 Unsalted butter 90 g (softened in advance)
🍞 Dry yeast 3 g
🧂 Aluminum-free baking powder 1 g
🥥 Coconut milk 151 g (can be substituted with 145 g milk + 6 g vegetable oil)
💧 Water 15 g (to dissolve yeast)
🍶 Rice wine or liquor appropriate amount (to remove fishy smell from salted egg yolks)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔌 Electric steamer (with fermentation function)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Spatula
🪚 Rolling pin
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🪒 Dough scraper
🧻 Plastic wrap or damp cloth
🧤 Disposable gloves
📄 Steamer paper
🥛 Measuring cup

## Steps
### Prepare Custard Filling
1. Drizzle a little rice wine or liquor over the salted duck egg yolks, place them in the steamer along with the whole eggs, and steam for 15 minutes until fully cooked.
**Key Point**: The yolks must be fully cooked; this is key to the sandy texture.

2. Remove the eggshells, take only the cooked egg yolks, and place them in a bowl with the salted duck egg yolks. Add 60 g of fine granulated sugar, then crush and mix together with your hands.
3. Add milk powder and cornstarch one at a time, mix into a loose "sandy" texture. If it forms a ball, the yolks may not be fully cooked, or they are too small.
4. Add the softened butter, knead into a smooth ball. The prepared filling will be on the softer side; it can be chilled until slightly firmer before wrapping. Scratch it loose slightly before wrapping.
### Prepare Dough
1. Add 3 g of dry yeast to 15 g of warm water, stir until dissolved.
2. Pour in all-purpose flour, cake flour, 5 g of fine granulated sugar, and baking powder, stir briefly.
3. Add coconut milk in batches, first stir into a flaky texture, then knead into a dough with no dry flour. Coconut milk contains its own fat, resulting in a whiter and shinier dough with a coconut fragrance.
4. Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 20 minutes. Take it out, divide and knead until the surface is smooth, form back into a ball, and cover with a damp cloth to rest for another 20 minutes. Do not knead again after the second proof.
### Shaping & Filling
1. Take out the dough, flatten it, and roll it into a rectangular sheet about 30×25 cm. Fold into three layers, then roll out again to the same size. This step replaces using a dough sheeter to release air, resulting in a finer texture for the buns.
2. Spray a little water on the second rolled-out sheet to prevent drying. Roll it tightly from one end into a long log, pinch the seam to seal, and roll it longer.
3. Divide into 16 pieces of about 28 g each. Tuck the corners of each piece downwards, flatten, and form into a ball. Cover with a damp cloth to prevent drying.
4. Take the first piece and roll it into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place about 15 g of custard filling in the center. Use the tiger's mouth (area between thumb and index finger) to push the wrapper upwards to seal tightly. Place it seam-side down on a steamer paper.
### Fermentation & Steaming
1. Place the raw buns in the steamer and ferment using the yogurt fermentation mode for about 30 minutes, until they are slightly larger in size and feel lighter when held in hand. Do not let them double in size, or they may collapse after steaming.
2. Start steaming directly. Once steam rises, steam for about 12–15 minutes. The steamer used in the video (Beide brand) can steam quickly and open without collapsing. For regular steamers, it is recommended to let them sit for 2–3 minutes after steaming before opening the lid.
3. After taking them out, the dough will be snowy white and fluffy, the filling golden, sandy, and soft.

## Health Advice
Custard buns are a high-sugar, high-fat pastry. It is recommended to eat 1–2 buns per serving and pair with unsweetened tea to cut the richness. Salted egg yolks are rich in lecithin but high in cholesterol; people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume them sparingly.
