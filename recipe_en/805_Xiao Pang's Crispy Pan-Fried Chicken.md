# Xiao Pang's Crispy Pan-Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole chicken (boneless, about 18 yuan)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), as needed  
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed  
🍖 Barbecue seasoning, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, a pinch (to be added just before removing from heat)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating)  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🧻 Aluminum foil  
🍶 Small bowl or clay pot (to be used as a weight)  

## Instructions  
1. Debone the whole chicken: Remove the breastbone along the ribs, make cuts at the joints of both chicken legs, and spread the chicken flat from the legs, laying it out evenly.  
2. Place the prepared chicken in a stainless steel bowl, add scallion sections, ginger slices, salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Sichuan peppercorns, baijiu, and chili flakes. Mix well with your hands, then marinate for at least 8 hours, preferably overnight.  
3. Heat a non-stick pan without oil, and place the marinated chicken skin-side down in the pan.  
4. Cover the chicken with a sheet of aluminum foil, then press a small bowl filled with a heavy object on top to ensure the chicken is in close contact with the pan bottom. Keep the heat on low.  
5. Pan-fry over low heat for about 15 minutes. Lift the foil to check; when the chicken skin is caramelized, remove the weight and foil, then flip the chicken.  
6. Evenly sprinkle barbecue seasoning on the cooked side, flip the chicken, and sprinkle the same amount of seasoning on the other side. Continue to cook for another 7–8 minutes.  
7. Just before turning off the heat, sprinkle a pinch of white sugar on top, letting it melt with the residual heat to enhance gloss and flavor. Then remove from the pan and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish involves deboning, marinating, and pan-frying with a weight, which both locks in the juices and makes the skin crispy. It is suitable for fitness enthusiasts looking to supplement protein during muscle gain. However, note that the barbecue seasoning and soy sauce are high in sodium; those sensitive to blood pressure should use less seasoning.
