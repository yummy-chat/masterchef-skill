# Summer Lemon Tart 🍋
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍪 Cookies 120g
🧈 Butter approx. 170g
🍋 Lemon 2 pieces
🍬 White sugar 50g
🥚 Egg 1 piece (1 additional egg yolk optional)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🛍️ Transparent plastic bag
🍾 Bottle
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Small knife
🧴 Plastic wrap
🧽 Strainer or squeezing tool

## Steps
1. Prepare the cookie crust: Place the cookies in a plastic bag and crush them into fine powder using a bottle. Melt about 80g of butter over hot water until liquid, then mix thoroughly into the cookie crumbs until the mixture can be pressed into a ball without falling apart. Line a bowl with plastic wrap, fill in the cookie crumbs, and press firmly against the bottom and sides of the bowl to form a tart shell with even thickness. Set aside.
   **Note**: Press evenly all around to ensure uniform thickness, otherwise it may break easily.

2. Prepare lemon zest and lemon juice: Scrub the lemon skins with salt and rinse clean. Use a small knife to gently shave off the yellow skin, then chop finely and set aside. Cut the lemons in half and squeeze out about 50g of lemon juice.

3. Make the lemon curd: In a bowl, mix together the lemon juice, white sugar, and whole egg, stirring well. Place the bowl over a large bowl of hot water to heat indirectly, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spatula without easily dripping off, reaching a thick puree or yogurt-like consistency.
   **Note**: If the hot water temperature is insufficient, replace it with fresh hot water; stir constantly to prevent the egg from curdling.

4. Finish the curd: Remove from heat and let the bowl cool slightly to 50–60°C. Add the remaining 90g of butter cut into small pieces, stirring until fully melted and smooth using the residual heat. Then add the lemon zest and mix well. The curd is ready.

5. Assemble and chill: Pour the lemon curd into the cookie crust. Gently tap to level the surface. Let rest in a cool place for 3 hours, or refrigerate for 1 hour, until the filling reaches a semi-set, creamy consistency.

6. Decorate and serve: Remove the set lemon tart from the mold. Dust the surface with powdered sugar and decorate with lemon slices. Cut into pieces and enjoy.

## Health Advice
Lemons are rich in vitamin C, but the butter and sugar content is relatively high. It is recommended to enjoy this as a weekend dessert, eating a small piece each time. Chilled, the texture is even better. Best shared with friends to avoid consuming too much at once.
