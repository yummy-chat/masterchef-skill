# Broccoli Steamed Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥦 Broccoli 1 head (cut into small florets)  
🥚 Eggs 5  
🌾 Flour as needed (for washing, not added to the dish)  
🌽 Starch as needed (for setting)  
💧 Water as needed (for washing, blanching and cooling, and mixing starch water)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for blanching and greasing the mold)  
🧂 Salt as needed (used in batches)  
🍶 Vinegar as needed (to remove eggy smell)  
🧄 Garlic sauce as needed (dipping sauce, preparation method not mentioned)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowls  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok, slotted spoon  
♨️ Steamer, steaming rack  
📦 Square mold (baking pan)  
🫗 Spray bottle  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the broccoli into small florets, place in a bowl, add salt and flour, pour in water, and scrub with your hands. Rinse again with clean water and drain, squeezing out excess moisture.
   **Note**: Washing with salt and flour effectively removes impurities and insect eggs; squeeze out as much water as possible after washing.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add one spoonful of salt and a little cooking oil. Add the cleaned broccoli and blanch for about 1 minute until bright green and just cooked. Immediately remove and plunge into cold water to cool quickly, then remove and squeeze out excess water.
   **Key Point**: Adding oil and salt during blanching preserves color and basic seasoning; cooling quickly locks in the bright green color and crisp texture.
3. Crack the 5 eggs into a bowl, add one spoonful of salt and an appropriate amount of vinegar, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Vinegar effectively removes the eggy smell.
4. Add the blanched and drained broccoli to the egg mixture, add an appropriate amount of starch, and stir until the broccoli and egg mixture are fully combined.
   **Key Point**: Starch helps the steamed cake set more easily and results in a firmer texture.
5. Evenly spray cooking oil on the inner walls of the square mold to prevent sticking.
6. Pour the broccoli-egg mixture into the mold, place it on the steaming rack, invert a plate over the mold, and cover with the lid. Bring to a full boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and steam for about 8 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Inverting a plate prevents condensed steam from the lid from dripping onto the surface and ruining its smoothness.
7. After steaming, turn off the heat, remove the mold, let it cool slightly, then invert the cake onto a cutting board. Cut into thick slices, arrange on a plate, and place a small dish of garlic sauce in the center.

## Health Tips  
Broccoli steamed egg cake is high in protein and low in carbs, suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or with poor teeth. Steaming uses less oil and is healthier. It is recommended to serve with garlic sauce for flavor and antibacterial benefits. Note that the broccoli must be well-drained, or the cake may fall apart easily.
