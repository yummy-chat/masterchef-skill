# Old-Style Sauerkraut Hotpot
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Cooked Pork Belly 6 slices (thinly sliced)  
🐑 Sliced Lamb Meat appropriate amount  
🦐 Dried Shrimp appropriate amount (soaked in advance, divided into two portions for use)  
🥬 Sauerkraut 1 bowl/appropriate amount (thinly shredded, rinsed and squeezed dry)  
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (part minced for dipping sauce, part cut into thick slices for the pot base)  
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (part chopped as scallions for dipping sauce, part crushed and cut into sections for the pot base)  
🌿 Cilantro 1 bunch (chopped, for dipping sauce)  
🥜 Sesame Paste 2 tablespoons  
🥜 Peanut Butter 1 tablespoon (optional)  
🫘 Fermented Tofu 1 piece + fermented tofu liquid (mashed into a paste in advance)  
🌱 Chive Flower Sauce appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame Oil appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili Oil appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar a pinch  
🧂 Salt a pinch (needed for both dipping sauce and broth)  
🧂 MSG a pinch (for seasoning the broth)  
💧 Water appropriate amount (to dilute sesame paste and for the broth base)  
⚪ White Pepper Powder a pinch (optional, can be added before serving)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🪔 Copper Hotpot  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🪓 Cutting Board  
🥄 Soup Ladle  
🥣 Bowls/Plates  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Slice the cooked pork belly into thin pieces and arrange on a plate; prepare the sliced lamb meat and set aside on a plate.  
2. Mince the ginger, chop the scallions into small pieces, and chop the cilantro, placing them into separate small bowls for later use.  
3. Take scallion sections, crush with a knife, and cut into small segments; cut ginger into thick slices as supplementary ingredients for the pot base.  
4. Shred the sauerkraut into thin strips about 2 millimeters thick, rinse with cold water, squeeze dry into bundles, and set aside on a plate.  
5. Take one piece of fermented tofu, mash it into a paste in a small bowl with a spoon, mix evenly with the fermented tofu liquid, and set aside.  
6. Prepare the sesame paste dipping sauce:  
   - Place 2 tablespoons of sesame paste in a bowl.  
   - Add a pinch of sugar and a pinch of salt.  
   - Gradually add warm water below 40°C, stirring continuously while adding, until it reaches a suitable consistency.  
   - Add chive flower sauce, the prepared fermented tofu paste, sesame oil, and chili oil, and mix thoroughly.  
7. Start the broth: Add water to the copper hotpot, then add the soaked dried shrimp, crushed scallion sections, and ginger slices.  
8. Layer the squeezed-dry shredded sauerkraut into the pot.  
**Note**: After the broth comes to a boil, continue to cook for about 5 minutes to allow the umami of the dried shrimp and the aroma of the sauerkraut to fully meld.  
9. Season the broth: Once the pot is boiling, add an appropriate amount of salt and a little MSG; if desired, add a small amount of white pepper powder.  
10. Cook and eat: When the broth is ready, first add the cooked pork belly slices, then add the sliced lamb meat according to personal preference to cook until done, and enjoy with the dipping sauce.  

## Health Advice  
The combination of sauerkraut and seafood can reduce greasiness, making this dish suitable for warming up in cold weather. However, pork belly and lamb are high in fat, so it is recommended to pair with vegetables for balanced nutrition. People with high blood pressure should use less salt in the dipping sauce.
