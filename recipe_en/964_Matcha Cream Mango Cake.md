# Matcha Cream Mango Cake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs (separate egg whites and yolks)
🌾 Cake flour
🫒 Cooking oil
🥛 Milk
🍋 Lemon (squeeze juice)
🍬 White sugar (use in batches)
🥛 Heavy cream
🍵 Matcha powder
🥭 Mango (peeled and diced)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
⚖️ Digital scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (grease-free and water-free)
🥄 Manual whisk/hand whisk
🔌 Electric hand mixer
🔥 Oven
🍰 6-inch round removable-bottom cake pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
🎂 Turntable for frosting
🔪 Offset spatula (long handle, small handle)
👝 Piping bag
🎨 Piping tips (Mont Blanc tip, round tip)
🔍 Strainer/sieve

## Steps
1. Separate egg whites and yolks. The container for egg whites must be grease-free and water-free.
2. Make the egg yolk batter: Mix egg yolks, cooking oil, and milk evenly. Sift in the cake flour. Using a manual whisk, mix in a "Z" or "I" shape motion until smooth and lump-free.
   **Note**: Do not overmix to avoid developing the gluten.
3. Add a few drops of lemon juice to the egg whites. Add the white sugar in three batches. Use an electric hand mixer to beat until stiff peaks form—when lifting the beater, the meringue should form short, upright peaks.
4. Add one-third of the meringue to the egg yolk batter. Quickly fold it in using a silicone spatula until combined. Then, pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and continue folding until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Use a folding motion to prevent deflating the meringue.
5. Pour the cake batter from a height into a 6-inch round removable-bottom cake pan with its bottom wrapped in aluminum foil. Gently tap the pan to release large air bubbles. Bake in a preheated oven. Adjust baking temperature and time as needed. Bake until the cake rises to its highest point and then slightly sinks back, the surface turns golden brown, and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean with no wet batter.
6. Immediately after baking, invert the cake pan onto two bowls to cool completely before unmolding to prevent collapsing.
7. Add white sugar to the heavy cream and whip until it reaches 80-90% stiffness. Take a portion of the whipped cream, add matcha powder, and mix until evenly combined to create matcha cream.
8. Once the cake base is completely cooled, unmold and slice it into layers. Assemble by repeating the order: one layer of cake, one layer of cream, one layer of mango cubes.
9. After assembly, apply a crumb coat and then the final coat of frosting: Use a Mont Blanc piping tip to pipe lines for side decoration. Use a turntable and offset spatulas for the frosting process.
   **Note**: Prepare a sufficient amount of matcha cream; otherwise, the color might be uneven, or there may not be enough to cover the cake.
10. Finally, use plain cream and yellow cream to pipe simple dot decorations on the top edge of the cake. Sift a little matcha powder over the top.

## Health Advice
This cake is rich in vitamin C from the mango and antioxidants from the matcha. However, it is high in cream and sugar content. It is recommended as a dessert for special occasions, with a small slice per serving. Diabetic patients and those on a weight loss diet should consume it with caution.
