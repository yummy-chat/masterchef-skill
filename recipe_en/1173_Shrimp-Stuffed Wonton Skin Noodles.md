# Shrimp-Stuffed Wonton Skin Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Wonton wrappers, as needed (pre-cut square)  
🦐 Shrimp paste, as needed  
🌿 Dried seaweed, a small amount  
🦐 Dried shrimp, a small amount  
🧅 Chopped green onions, as needed  
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon  
🧂 MSG, 1 teaspoon  
🥄 Soy sauce, 4 tablespoons (2 tablespoons for broth base, 2 tablespoons for noodle sauce)  
🫒 Sesame oil, 2 tablespoons (1 tablespoon for broth base, 1 tablespoon for noodle sauce)  
🍶 Black vinegar, 2 tablespoons  
🌶️ Chili oil, 1 tablespoon  
🤍 Sesame seeds, a small amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Take one wonton wrapper and place an appropriate amount of shrimp paste in the center. Fold the wrapper in half, then gently stretch and pinch the edges to seal, ensuring the shrimp paste is fully enclosed to prevent leakage during cooking.  
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the prepared shrimp-stuffed wonton skins and cook over high heat for 3 minutes, until they float to the surface and the wrappers turn translucent. Remove from the pot.  
   **Note**: For a cold mixed version, immediately rinse the cooked skins under cold water after removing to enhance a smooth and chewy texture.  
3. To make a soup version: Place dried seaweed, dried shrimp, and chopped green onions in a bowl. Add salt, MSG, 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Ladle in an appropriate amount of hot noodle-cooking water and stir to combine. Then add the cooked shrimp-stuffed wonton skins.  
   **Key point**: Using boiling-hot noodle water to mix the ingredients fully releases the aroma of the dry ingredients and seasonings.  
4. To make a cold mixed version: In another bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, black vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sesame oil, chili oil, chopped green onions, dried shrimp, and sesame seeds. Stir well to form a sauce. Add the cold-rinsed noodles to the bowl and toss thoroughly to combine.  

## Health Advice  
Shrimp-stuffed wonton skin noodles use wonton wrappers encasing shrimp paste, offering a low-fat, high-protein option suitable for weight loss diets. It is recommended to reduce the amounts of MSG and chili oil. Patients with high blood pressure should limit salt intake. Pairing with dried seaweed and dried shrimp can supplement calcium and enhance umami.
