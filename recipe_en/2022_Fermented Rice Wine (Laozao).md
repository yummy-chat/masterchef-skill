# Fermented Rice Wine (Laozao)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 500g glutinous rice
🧂 3g sweet wine starter (jiuqu)
🫘 Dried red dates, to taste
🍒 Goji berries, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste (add according to preference when making soup)
💧 About 250g cooled boiled water (for mixing) + additional as needed (for storage)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking rice, mixing, and fermentation)
🍲 Steamer or steaming basket
🥄 Spoon (must be completely free of oil and water)
🍸 Glass bowl (for serving the finished product)
🛡️ Plastic wrap, cloth, lid (for sealing the fermentation basin)
🫙 Sealed jar (for storage)

## Steps
1. Soak the glutinous rice in warm water for 3 hours, until it crushes easily between fingers. Rinse clean and drain thoroughly.
2. Place the rice in a steamer with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 40 minutes, until the rice grains are translucent and fully cooked.
3. Transfer the cooked glutinous rice to a basin that has been rinsed with boiling water and is completely free of oil and water. Using a similarly clean, oil-free spoon, stir and cool the rice to below 30°C. Gradually add about 250g of cooled boiled water, mixing with your hands to break the rice into individual grains. Add 3g of sweet wine starter and mix thoroughly, ensuring every grain is coated.
4. Press the mixed rice down firmly and evenly, and make a well in the center. Seal the basin with plastic wrap, cover with a cloth and lid, ensuring it is airtight. Place in an environment at around 30°C and ferment for about 2 days, until a rich wine aroma is produced, sweet wine liquid seeps into the well, and the taste is sweet.
5. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add dried red dates, two spoonfuls of fermented rice wine, a small amount of goji berries, and white sugar to taste. Stir well and bring to a boil again, then serve.
6. To stop fermentation for leftover fermented rice wine, add an appropriate amount of cooled boiled water, transfer to a sealed jar, and refrigerate. It can be stored for up to a year.

## Health Advice
Fermented rice wine is rich in probiotics and B vitamins, making it suitable for people with indigestion or insufficient qi and blood. However, glutinous rice can cause a rapid rise in blood sugar, so those with diabetes should consume it in small amounts. During fermentation, ensure all containers and utensils are free of oil and water to prevent mold growth.
