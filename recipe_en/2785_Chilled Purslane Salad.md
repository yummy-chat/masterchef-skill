# Chilled Purslane Salad
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Purslane: about 1 bunch (fresh, with impurities removed)  
🧄 Garlic: minced, to taste  
🧅 Scallions: chopped, to taste  
🧂 Salt: to taste (used twice, for blanching and seasoning)  
🥄 Light soy sauce: to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil: to taste  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Small soup spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a spoonful of salt, then blanch the purslane for 30 seconds.  
   **Note**: Adding salt helps lock in color and enhance the base flavor; do not blanch too long—remove the purslane when it softens but remains crisp and tender, avoiding mushiness.  
2. Remove the blanched purslane and immediately plunge it into cold water to cool.  
   **Note**: Quick cooling locks in the crisp texture and vibrant green color.  
3. Place the cooled purslane on a cutting board, trim off the tough roots, cut into bite-sized pieces, and transfer to a glass bowl.  
   **Note**: Removing tough roots improves texture; cutting into pieces makes mixing and eating easier.  
4. Add salt, minced garlic, light soy sauce, and sesame oil to the bowl, mixing thoroughly with chopsticks.  
   **Note**: Adjust seasoning amounts to personal taste; mix well to ensure ingredients are evenly coated.  
5. Add the chopped scallions, then gently mix again with chopsticks.  
   **Note**: Add scallions last to avoid over-mixing, which can diminish their fresh aroma.  
6. Plate the seasoned purslane; garnish with red chili shreds or cilantro to enhance presentation.  

## Health Tips
Purslane is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants, helping to lower blood lipids and clear heat-dampness, making it especially suitable for summer. Blanching and then cooling in cold water removes sourness and astringency while preserving crispness; however, those with a weak spleen and cold stomach should use less sesame oil and add ginger or garlic to balance the cold nature.
