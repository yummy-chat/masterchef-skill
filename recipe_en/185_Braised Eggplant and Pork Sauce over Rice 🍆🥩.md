# Braised Eggplant and Pork Sauce over Rice 🍆🥩
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍆 Long eggplant, appropriate amount (tear into small pieces by hand)  
🥩 Pork belly, 100g (cut into small cubes)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)  
🧅 White part of scallion, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🌿 Chopped scallions, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for marinating the eggplant)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🫘 Fermented soybean paste, 1 tablespoon  
🟤 Dark soy sauce, a small amount  
🍬 Rock sugar, 4 pieces  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick skillet  
🥄 Spatula or ladle  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  

## Instructions
1. Tear the long eggplant into small pieces by hand, mix well with salt, marinate for 15 minutes, then squeeze out excess water and set aside.  
   **Key point** : Hand-tearing the eggplant makes it easier to absorb flavors than cutting with a knife, and it is essential to squeeze out excess moisture.  
2. Finely chop the onion, cut the pork belly into small cubes, and mince the garlic.  
3. Place the pork belly cubes into the skillet without any oil, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until they change color and render a generous amount of lard.  
4. Using the rendered lard in the skillet, add the chopped onion, white scallion parts, and minced garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the prepared eggplant and stir-fry in the oil for about 1 minute.  
5. Add 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon fermented soybean paste, and a small amount of dark soy sauce, and stir-fry evenly. Pour in a small amount of water and add the 4 pieces of rock sugar.  
6. Cover the skillet, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the eggplant to fully absorb the sauce and become soft and tender.  
7. Remove the lid, increase the heat to high, and stir-fry constantly until the sauce thickens and the eggplant becomes mushy. Sprinkle in the chopped scallions and small chili pepper pieces, stir-fry quickly to combine, then remove from heat.  
8. Pour the prepared eggplant and pork sauce over the rice, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice  
This braised eggplant and pork sauce over rice is rich in dietary fiber and protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, due to the pork belly and rock sugar, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to control the portion of rice. Note that salt is already used during the eggplant marinating step, so reduce the salt in subsequent seasonings as needed to avoid excessive sodium intake.
