# Red Bean Paste
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red beans 500g (need to be soaked overnight in advance)
🍬 Rock sugar 150g
💧 Water appropriate amount (for soaking, steaming, and blending)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for soaking red beans)
🍚 Rice cooker (for steaming red beans)
🌀 Blender (for blending red beans into paste; alternatively, a sieve and spoon can be used for manual straining, but it is time-consuming)
🍳 Wok (flat-bottomed or non-stick pan both work)
🪵 Wooden or silicone spatula (for stirring)
🥣 Glass bowl (for storing the finished product)

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the red beans clean, place them in a bowl, and add enough water to soak overnight.  
   **Key point**: Thorough soaking allows the red beans to absorb water and expand, making them easier to soften during subsequent steaming.

2. Rinse the soaked red beans again, drain them, and pour them into the rice cooker inner pot. Add water and start the cooking or steaming program.  
   **Doneness check**: Steam until the beans can be easily crushed with your fingers, achieving a completely soft and mushy state.

3. Pour the steamed red beans into the blender and blend into a fine paste. If the paste is too dry to blend smoothly, add water in small increments until it blends easily.

4. Transfer all the blended red bean paste into a wok, add rock sugar, and stir well.

5. Cook over low heat, stirring continuously with a spatula. The entire process takes about 25–30 minutes.  
   - Early stage: The paste is relatively thin and lighter in color.  
   - Middle stage: Water evaporates, the paste thickens, and the color deepens.  
   - Later stage: The paste noticeably dries out and begins to clump together.  
   - Done: The paste firmly sticks to the spatula without falling off.  
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low and stir constantly throughout to prevent scorching and ensure even heating and moisture evaporation.

## Health Suggestions
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron, making them suitable for people with anemia or those needing detoxification. However, the sugar content is high, so consider reducing the rock sugar by half or replacing it with a sugar substitute. During cooking, maintain low heat and stir continuously to prevent scorching.
