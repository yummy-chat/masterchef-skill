# Pocket Tofu
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 Brine tofu (firm tofu, mashed into a paste)  
🐟 Catfish (use only the meat, scrape into a paste; can substitute with silver carp)  
🥚 Eggs (egg whites only)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms (sliced)  
🥬 Bok choy/greens (for garnish)  
🔴 Goji berries (for garnish)  
🧅 Scallions (for ginger-scallion water and broth)  
🫚 Ginger (for ginger-scallion water and broth)  
🧂 Salt  
🌶️ White pepper  
🌾 Potato starch (cornstarch)  
🧪 Baking soda (one small spoonful)  
🧈 Lard (for the filling, deep-frying, and making broth)  
🧪 Edible alkali (for boiling)  
💧 Ginger-scallion water (water infused with sliced ginger and scallions)  
🧂 MSG  
🧂 Chicken bouillon  
🍬 Sugar (a pinch, for final seasoning)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife (for pressing tofu and scraping fish paste)  
🪣 Large basin (for rinsing and mixing filling)  
🧶 Cheesecloth/gauze (for squeezing tofu dry)  
🫗 Fine-mesh strainer/sieve (for straining tofu paste)  
🥄 Rice paddle (for pressing tofu paste)  
🍳 Wok/large iron pot (for deep-frying, making broth, and braising)  
🥄 Slotted spoon/spider (for removing tofu puffs)  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoon  
🪚 Cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Press the tofu into a paste, rinse it in clean water to remove the brine flavor, then squeeze dry using cheesecloth. Press it through a fine-mesh strainer to achieve a smooth texture.  
**Note**: Squeeze dry thoroughly and strain to ensure the tofu paste is fine and free of lumps.

2. Skin and debone the fish, scrape the meat along the grain to make a paste, and mix it with the tofu paste in a ratio of approximately 7:3 (tofu to fish).  
3. While stirring, gradually add ginger-scallion water in batches. Season with salt, white pepper, egg whites, a little potato starch, and baking soda. Stir vigorously in one direction until the mixture is elastic and sticky, then fold in lard.  
**Key point**: Lard enhances the aroma and fluffiness; thorough stirring is essential to develop the binding texture.

4. Shape the filling into olive-shaped balls.  
5. Pour lard into a wok and fry in three stages:  
   - First fry at medium-low oil temperature until set, then remove;  
   - Second fry at a slightly higher temperature until fully cooked;  
   - Third fry at high temperature until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.  
6. In another pot, bring water to a boil and add a spoonful of edible alkali. Boil the fried tofu puffs for about 1 minute, then transfer them to clean boiling water to soak. Change the water 3–5 times until no alkali taste remains.  
**Note**: The alkali must be thoroughly rinsed away to achieve the soft, pouch-like texture.

7. In a wok, heat lard and fry the fish bones until lightly golden. Add ginger slices and scallion segments, then pour in boiling water. Boil vigorously for 2–3 minutes until the broth turns milky white. Strain out the solids.  
8. Add sliced shiitake mushrooms to the broth, along with MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Add the tofu puffs and simmer gently until they are fully infused with flavor.  
9. Just before serving, blanch the bok choy in the broth until tender, then garnish with goji berries.  

## Health Suggestions  
This Pocket Tofu is oily, soft, and tender, making it suitable for elderly individuals or those recovering who require a high-protein, low-salt diet. Be sure to rinse the tofu puffs thoroughly after the alkali boil to remove any alkaline taste.
