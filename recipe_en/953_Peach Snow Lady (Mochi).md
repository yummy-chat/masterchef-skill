# Peach Snow Lady (Mochi)

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 240 ml whole milk  
🍬 50 g granulated sugar  
🌾 130 g glutinous rice flour  
🌽 40 g cornstarch  
🧈 20 g butter (or corn oil)  
🔴 1 drop red food coloring  
🍑 approximately 1 tablespoon peach jam  
🥛 an appropriate amount of heavy cream  
🌾 an appropriate amount of cooked glutinous rice flour (for dusting to prevent sticking)  
🌿 an appropriate amount of mint leaves (for decoration)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 glass bowl  
🥄 hand whisk  
🧴 strainer  
🍳 steamer (bamboo steamer)  
🔪 cutting board, scraper  
🥢 rolling pin  
🥣 small bowl (used as a mold for wrapping the filling)  
📦 piping bag  
🥢 chopsticks  
🧁 paper liner  
❄️ refrigerator (for freezing and setting)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Prepare the batter: In a glass bowl, pour in 240 ml of whole milk and add 50 g of granulated sugar, stirring until fully dissolved. Then add 130 g of glutinous rice flour and 40 g of cornstarch, and whisk until smooth and free of lumps. Pass the batter through a strainer once to make the texture finer.
2.  Steam the batter: Once the water in the steamer is boiling, place the strained batter into the steamer and steam over medium heat for 35 minutes, until the batter is fully solidified into a dough. Remove from the steamer.
3.  Knead in the butter: While the dough is still hot, add 20 g of butter. On a cutting board, repeatedly knead the dough using pushing, pulling, and stretching motions until it becomes smooth and can be stretched into a thin film. If the dough sticks to the cutting board, use a scraper to assist.
4.  Color the dough: Drop 1 drop of red food coloring onto the kneaded dough. Through repeated pulling, stretching, and folding, knead the dough until it displays an even pink color.
5.  Divide and roll out the wrappers: Sprinkle cooked glutinous rice flour on the cutting board to prevent sticking. Roll the pink dough into a thick long log and divide it into 10 portions. Take one portion, press it flat, and roll it into a thin round sheet approximately 8 cm in diameter.
6.  Wrap the filling: Place the rolled-out wrapper into a small bowl. Use a piping bag to pipe a layer of heavy cream into the bowl, add a spoonful of peach jam, and then pipe another layer of heavy cream on top to cover the jam. Gather the edges of the wrapper towards the center and pinch them tightly to seal, removing any excess dough. Ensure the seal is tight and does not leak.
7.  Freeze to set: Place the wrapped Snow Lady in the refrigerator and freeze for 1 hour to allow it to set.
8.  Shape and decorate: Remove the frozen Snow Lady from the refrigerator and place it upside down in a paper liner. Use chopsticks to gently press a natural-looking indentation on the side of the round ball, imitating the shape of a peach. Insert a mint leaf next to the indentation as a decoration.

## Health Advice
This peach Snow Lady has a relatively high sugar and glutinous rice flour content, making it suitable as an occasional dessert. Diabetic individuals or those controlling their sugar intake should limit consumption. The heavy cream and jam are not low in calories; it is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the palate.
