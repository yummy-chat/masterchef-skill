## Cooking Steps
1. Wash and julienne the green bell pepper, carrot, and onion respectively. Slice the pork into thin pieces.
2. Add cooking wine and light soy sauce to the pork slices, mix well, and marinate for 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Pork has natural sweetness that blends well with the spiciness of the curry.
3. Heat the frying pan, add the marinated pork slices, and pan-fry until the surface turns caramelized.
4. Increase the heat to high, add the julienned vegetables, and stir-fry quickly until the vegetables soften and the surfaces turn slightly golden.
   **Key point**: High-heat stir-frying allows the ingredients to fully develop color, resulting in a richer flavor.
5. Keep the heat on high, add one curry block,适量 light soy sauce, and a pinch of chili powder. Stir-fry until the curry block melts and is evenly distributed.
   **Note**: Any brand of curry block can be used.
6. Pour in one can of milk, stir well, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes, until the sauce reaches a slightly thick consistency.
   **Key point**: Do not add water throughout the process; use only milk as the simmering liquid, making the sauce smoother and richer.
7. Bring water to a boil in another pot, cook the noodles until done, then remove and place them in a bowl.
   **Key point**: Since the curry sauce is rich, firmer noodles are recommended for better texture.
8. Generously pour the simmered milk curry sauce over the noodles, sprinkle with scallions, and add chili oil according to personal preference. Mix well and serve.

## Health Tips
Milk curry paired with vegetables and lean meat provides a balanced mix of protein and vitamins, making it suitable for quickly replenishing energy after work or study. It is recommended to choose firmer noodles to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake from too much sauce. The milk helps buffer the spiciness of the curry, making it enjoyable even for those with sensitive stomachs in moderation.
