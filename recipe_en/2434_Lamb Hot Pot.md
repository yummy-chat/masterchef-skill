# Lamb Hot Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Skin-on lamb (lamb ribs) about 1.5-2 kg (cut into large pieces)  
🥒 White radish 1 piece (cut into large chunks)  
🥬 Napa cabbage about 2-3 leaves (separate stems and leaves)  
🍄 Oyster mushrooms/button mushrooms appropriate amount (torn apart)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped)  
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic chives appropriate amount (minced)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount  
🍾 Yellow wine appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt 5 teaspoons  
🌿 Angelica dahurica 1 slice  
🌿 White peppercorns about 10-15 grains  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🫕 Small copper pot/hot pot basin  
🔥 Alcohol stove  
🥄 Stainless steel soup ladle  

## Steps  
1. Chop the skin-on lamb ribs into large pieces, rinse, then place in cold water and bring to a boil over high heat to blanch.  
   **Key point**: Do not add ginger or scallions during blanching; skim off the foam once the water boils, then remove the lamb and drain.  

2. Cut the white radish into large chunks, add to the water used for blanching the lamb, and cook for about 3 minutes, then remove.  
   **Key point**: Boiling the radish in the blanching water effectively removes its raw taste.  

3. Pour rapeseed oil into the wok, add ginger slices, and fry over medium heat until fragrant. Add the blanched lamb and stir-fry over medium heat for about 3-5 minutes, until the lamb skin is golden brown and the lamb fat is fully rendered.  
   **Key point**: Thorough stir-frying is key to a rich, thick, and aromatic soup.  

4. Splash in the yellow wine, quickly stir-fry a few times, using the alcohol vapor to eliminate the gamey smell.  

5. Transfer the stir-fried lamb to a pressure cooker, add enough boiling water to submerge the meat, add the angelica dahurica and white peppercorns, and cover with the lid.  
   **Key point**: Must add boiling water to help emulsify the soup; do not add salt at this step.  

6. Heat over high heat until the steam valve releases steam, then immediately reduce to low heat and pressure-cook for 25 minutes, then let the pressure release naturally.  

7. After opening the lid, the soup should be thick and white. Add the prepared white radish chunks, season with salt, and cook uncovered over medium heat for another 5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Radish cooks quickly and does not need prolonged simmering.  

8. In a separate pan, add a little oil, stir-fry the cabbage stems and torn mushrooms until soft and they release water, then line the bottom of a small copper pot.  
   **Key point**: Stir-frying the base vegetables adds depth of flavor and prevents sticking.  

9. Layer the cooked lamb and radish on top, ladle in the lamb broth, and sprinkle with chopped scallions, garlic chives, and cilantro. Serve at the table and light the alcohol stove to keep it warm.  
   **Serving suggestion**: Eat the lamb and radish first, then you can blanch napa cabbage leaves and other vegetables in the pot.  

## Health Advice  
Lamb is rich in high-quality protein and iron, and pairing it with radish and mushrooms can help warm the body and boost energy, making it suitable for those with cold hands and feet in winter. People with high blood pressure should use less salt, and those with gout should limit the amount of broth consumed.
