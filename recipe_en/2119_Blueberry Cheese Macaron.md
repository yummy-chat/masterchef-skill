# Blueberry Cheese Macaron  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 3 pieces  
🧀 Cream cheese 180g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (for light cheesecake)  
🥛 Milk 100g  
🌾 Cake flour 10g  
🌽 Cornstarch 20g  
🍬 Granulated sugar 60g (for light cheesecake)  
🫐 Frozen blueberries 100g  
🍯 Yellow rock sugar 20g  
🥚 Egg yolk 24g (for buttercream)  
💧 Water 7.5g (for buttercream)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 22g (for buttercream)  
🧈 Room temperature butter 90g (for buttercream)  
🌰 Almond flour 95g  
🍚 Powdered sugar 85g  
🥚 Egg whites 80g (for macaron shells, use fresh egg whites)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 75g (for macaron shells)  
🍋 Lemon juice a pinch  
🌸 Vanilla extract a pinch (optional)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Electric hand mixer  
🥢 Silicone spatula  
🧂 Sifter  
🍳 Saucepan  
🥣 Square baking dish/baking pan  
📥 Baking tray (with hot water)  
🔥 Oven  
🌀 Piping bag  
🌡️ Infrared thermometer  
🔖 Macaron silicone mat (optional)  

## Steps  
1. Place cream cheese, unsalted butter, and milk in a bowl, and heat over a water bath until smooth and melted. Add egg yolks and mix well. Sift in cake flour and cornstarch, stir thoroughly until no lumps remain, then strain the entire mixture once more to ensure a smooth batter.  
2. Add a pinch of vanilla extract to the egg whites. Gradually add 60g of granulated sugar and beat with an electric hand mixer to **soft peaks** — when lifting the beater, the peaks form a gentle curl. Do not over-whip to stiff peaks, or the top may crack during baking.  
3. Take 1/3 of the meringue and fold it into the egg yolk mixture using a folding motion. Then pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue and continue folding until well combined. Be careful to fold in a circular motion to avoid deflating the batter.  
4. Pour the batter into a square baking dish lined with parchment paper, place it in a baking tray with hot water, and bake using the water bath method. Preheat the oven in advance. Bake at 150℃ for about 30 minutes, then reduce to 110℃ for about 60 minutes. If the oven has a convection setting, clip the baking dish to prevent direct hot air from hitting the top.  
5. Place the frozen blueberries (no need to thaw) directly into a saucepan with yellow rock sugar and a pinch of lemon juice. Cook over medium-low heat until the liquid evaporates and the sauce becomes thick.  
6. Place 22g of granulated sugar and 7.5g of water in a saucepan, and boil until the sugar syrup reaches about 110℃. Meanwhile, beat the egg yolks at high speed until the color lightens and the volume increases. Slowly pour the hot syrup into the yolks while continuing to beat until the mixture cools to room temperature. Add softened butter in batches, beating at high speed until fluffy and smooth. Transfer to a piping bag and **do not refrigerate**, or it will become too hard to pipe.  
7. Mix almond flour and powdered sugar together, then sift to ensure a fine texture. An extra 0.5–1g can be added to compensate for sifting loss.  
8. Gradually add 75g of granulated sugar to the egg whites, beating at high speed until **stiff peaks** — the meringue is firm, and when lifting the beater, the peaks stand upright.  
9. Pour the sifted dry ingredients into the meringue. Use a silicone spatula to fold and press the mixture until the batter is smooth and flows in a continuous ribbon when lifted. Be careful not to overmix (batter becomes too thin) or undermix (too thick).  
10. Transfer the batter to a piping bag and pipe even round circles onto a baking tray lined with a silicone mat. Tap the tray a few times gently and use a toothpick to pop any large surface bubbles.  
11. Let rest at room temperature until the surface forms a soft, non-sticky shell. This step is crucial for forming the "feet." Insufficient drying time may result in less pronounced feet.  
12. Preheat the oven and bake at 145℃ for about 16 minutes. The shells will first puff up, then settle, forming the feet.  
13. Take two cooled macaron shells as a pair. Pipe a ring of buttercream on each. Place a piece of the cut light cheesecake in the center of one, then add a small amount of blueberry sauce. Top with the other shell. After assembly, **refrigerate** before serving for a better texture.  

## Health Advice  
Blueberry cheese macarons are high in sugar, so diabetics should avoid them. Healthy individuals can enjoy them occasionally as a dessert, but portion control is important. Although this dessert is highly appealing to the palate, it is essential to balance consumption with self-discipline.
