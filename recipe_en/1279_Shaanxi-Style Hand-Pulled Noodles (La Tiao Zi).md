# Shaanxi-Style Hand-Pulled Noodles (La Tiao Zi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 500g (1 lb) All-Purpose Flour (regular all-purpose flour)  
💧 230g Room-Temperature Water (adjust according to flour’s moisture level)  
🧂 3g Salt (mix with flour to enhance gluten strength)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🫗 Large Mixing Bowl  
🔪 Cutting Board  
🛡️ Plastic Wrap  
🖌️ Brush  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: Mix flour with salt evenly. Add room-temperature water in batches, stirring until it becomes flaky, then knead into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: The dough should be slightly softer than hand-rolled noodles and slightly firmer than leavened dough, ensuring it won’t break easily during pulling.

2. **Initial resting**: Press the dough into a ball in the bowl, cover the lid, and let it rest briefly to relax.

3. **Knead the dough again**: Take out the dough and place it on the cutting board. Knead vigorously and repeatedly until the dough’s surface is smooth and the texture is firm.
**Key point**: Knead thoroughly until smooth; only then can the gluten fully develop, preventing breakage during pulling.

4. **Shape and oil**: Roll the dough into a long log, brush the surface with oil, and cut it into evenly sized dough pieces. Roll each piece into a thin strip, about the thickness of chopsticks, with even length.
**Key point**: The dough pieces and rolled strips must be uniform in size to ensure the pulled noodles have consistent thickness.

5. **Arrange and rest**: Place the thin strips neatly on the cutting board, brush the surface again with oil, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 40 minutes.
**Key point**: Adequate resting relaxes the gluten, improving extensibility for easy stretching.

6. **Separate and take**: Take out three strips, gently separate any sticking points.

7. **Pull the noodles**:
   - Pinch both ends of a strip, gently stretch outward to lengthen initially.
   - Slap the noodle so the middle part hits the cutting board, then fold it in half, hook the middle with your right middle finger, and slap outward again.
   - Hook one end of the noodle onto your left pinky finger, continue slapping the middle part with your right hand; then hook onto your ring finger, and then index finger, slapping and twisting slightly each time after hooking.
   - Stretch until the noodle is sufficiently long and evenly thick, then pinch off the thick end pieces held in your hands.
**Key point**: Support both ends with your hands throughout the stretching; slapping makes the noodle more even. The hooking sequence is pinky → ring finger → index finger.

## Health Suggestions  
The chewy texture of hand-pulled noodles depends on sufficient resting time (about 40 minutes); it’s recommended to plan ahead. The dough is relatively firm, making it suitable for those with good digestion. Control your strength during pulling to avoid breaking the noodles. Thorough kneading builds enough gluten toughness, resulting in a springy and elastic texture.
