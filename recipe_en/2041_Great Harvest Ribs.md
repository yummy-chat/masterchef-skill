# Great Harvest Ribs
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Seasonal dish

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs, to taste (pre-blanched)
🧅 Purple onion, 1 (cut into chunks)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices
🥔 Potatoes, to taste (cut into chunks)
🌽 Corn, to taste (cut into sections)
🌭 Sausage, to taste (optional, cut into sections)
🌶️ Small red chili peppers, green chili peppers, a little (for garnish)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (used in two batches)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a little (optional)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (Chinese iron wok)
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Wooden spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Heat cooking oil in a wok, add ginger slices and purple onion chunks, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the pre-blanched pork ribs and stir-fry until the surface is lightly browned.
2. Add enough water to the wok and bring to a boil over high heat.
3. Add potato chunks, corn sections, and sausage sections. Season with an appropriate amount of light soy sauce, salt, and chicken bouillon for the first seasoning. Reduce heat to medium and simmer.
**Note**: This is the first seasoning, intended to infuse the ingredients with underlying flavor; additional seasoning will follow.
4. When the potatoes are nearly cooked and the ribs are tender, drizzle in dark soy sauce, cooking wine, and the remaining light soy sauce. Toss evenly to color all the ingredients.
5. Continue to simmer over medium-low heat to reduce the sauce, allowing the flavors to fully infuse the ingredients.
6. Plate the dish and garnish with small red chili pepper rings and green chili pepper rings before serving.

## Health Tips
This dish features tender ribs and a variety of vegetables, making it suitable for supplementing protein and dietary fiber. However, potatoes and sausage are high in carbohydrates and fats, so it is recommended to pair with light leafy greens for balanced nutrition. When simmering, use less salt and chicken bouillon, relying on light soy sauce for flavor to control sodium intake.
