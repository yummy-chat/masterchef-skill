# Homemade Sichuan Pepper Salt  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns: 2 parts (remove stems and seeds)  
🌿 Cumin seeds: 1 part  
🌾 Roasted sesame seeds: 1 part (if using raw sesame seeds, add them to the pan together with the cumin seeds)  
🧂 Salt: 2 parts  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Red silicone spatula (or a wok spatula)  
🪵 Square wooden tray or large plate (for quick cooling)  
🗡️ Cutting board  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥛 High-speed blender (optional, as an alternative to rolling)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Prepare the ingredients in the ratio of "2 parts Sichuan peppercorns, 1 part cumin seeds, 1 part roasted sesame seeds, 2 parts salt." Pick out the stems and seeds from the Sichuan peppercorns to ensure a smooth texture in the final product.  
2. Place the wok over medium-low heat, add the Sichuan peppercorns, and stir continuously with the spatula. Fry for about 10 minutes until the peppercorns turn a deep brown color and feel dry when pinched.  
   - **Note**: Stir continuously to prevent burning; take this opportunity to remove any remaining stems or seeds.  
3. Add the cumin seeds, keeping the heat on medium-low, and stir-fry for about 10 minutes, until the cumin seeds change from light green to a grassy yellow color.  
4. Add the roasted sesame seeds, and continue to stir-fry over medium-low heat for about 5 minutes, until a single Sichuan peppercorn can be easily crushed into powder between your fingers.  
   - **Note**: If using raw sesame seeds, add them together with the cumin seeds in Step 3.  
5. Quickly pour all the fried spices onto the wooden tray or large plate, spread them out flat to cool rapidly, preventing residual heat from scorching them.  
6. Turn off the heat, add the salt to the wok, using the pan's residual warmth. Gently stir with the spatula for a moment to evenly warm the salt and release its fragrance, then spread it flat on the bottom of the pan and let it sit.  
   - **Key Point**: Use only the residual heat; do not fry the salt until it changes color.  
7. Once the cooled spices are no longer hot to the touch, transfer them onto the cutting board and use a rolling pin to crush them into a fine powder. Alternatively, grind them in a high-speed blender.  
8. Pour the ground spice powder back into the wok containing the warm salt, and stir with the spatula until well combined, achieving a uniform brownish powder.  
9. Store in a clean, dry, airtight container for convenient use.  

## Health Advice  
Homemade Sichuan pepper salt is low in oil and calories, but its sodium content is not low. It is recommended to use sparingly when cooking. People with high blood pressure should control their intake. The finer the grind, the more aromatic the blend. Sealed away from moisture, it can be stored for several months.
