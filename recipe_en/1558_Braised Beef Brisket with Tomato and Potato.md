# Braised Beef Brisket with Tomato and Potato
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket 1 jin (500g) (cut into small pieces)
🍅 Tomatoes 2 (medium-sized, cut into small pieces)
🥔 Potatoes 2 (medium-sized, peeled and cut into large chunks)
🧅 Onion about half (diced)
🫚 Ginger about 4-5 slices (divided into two portions: 3 slices for blanching, the rest for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallions about 3 (use the white part, cut into sections; if using large scallions, use about one palm-length)
🧄 Garlic about 5-6 cloves (smash flat and mince)
⭐ Star anise 1-2 pieces
🌿 Bay leaves 2 pieces
🍅 Tomato paste 2 tablespoons (preferably pure tomato paste with only tomatoes in the ingredient list)
🥄 Light soy sauce 3 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
🍬 Granulated sugar 1 small pinch (to enhance freshness and balance acidity)
🌶️ White pepper powder 2 small pinches
🍬 Rock sugar a handful (for caramelizing; can substitute with granulated sugar)
🍶 Cooking wine or Shaoxing wine适量 (for blanching)
🧂 Salt适量 (add to taste before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🫕 Braising pot (e.g., enameled cast iron pot)
🍳 Wok or frying pan (e.g., non-stick pan)
🪣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking beef brisket)
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥒 Vegetable peeler
🥄 Spatula or ladle

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Cut beef brisket into small pieces, place in a bowl, and soak in cold water for half an hour to draw out blood. If the smell is strong, replace the water and soak again.
   **Key point**: Soaking to remove blood is the first step in reducing gamey odor.
2. Wash the tomatoes. Optionally, score a cross on the bottom, blanch in boiling water to peel, then cut into small pieces. Peel potatoes and cut into rolling chunks. Dice the onion. Slice the ginger and divide into two portions. Cut scallions into sections. Smash and mince the garlic.
3. In a bowl, pre-mix 2 tablespoons tomato paste, 3 tablespoons light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 small pinch granulated sugar, and 2 small pinches white pepper powder.

### Cooking Phase
1. Add cold water to a pot, put in the beef brisket pieces, 3 slices of ginger, scallion sections, and a suitable amount of cooking wine. Bring to a boil over medium heat. Once boiling, skim off any foam thoroughly, remove the beef, rinse with warm water, and drain well.
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps draw out blood; the foam consists mainly of blood, so skim it thoroughly.
2. In a separate wok or frying pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add ginger slices, minced garlic, and scallion sections, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add diced onion and tomato pieces, and stir-fry over medium heat until the tomatoes soften and release juice. Pour in the pre-mixed sauce, stir well until the sauce thickens. Transfer the mixture to the braising pot and set aside.
3. Clean and dry the wok or pan. Add a small amount of water or oil, add rock sugar, and heat over medium heat. Once the sugar melts and turns into a light yellow liquid, stir continuously until it reaches a caramel color, then immediately turn off the heat, allowing residual heat to deepen the color to a dark reddish-brown. After turning off the heat, add the drained beef brisket and stir to coat in caramel. Then, turn the heat back on.
   **Key point**: The caramel color changes quickly; watch closely to avoid burning.
4. After the beef is colored, add star anise and bay leaves, and stir a few times. Transfer the beef to the braising pot with the tomato base. Add enough boiling water to submerge the beef. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour.
   **Key point**: Always add boiling water; cold water will make the meat tough and difficult to soften. Stir every 10 minutes to prevent sticking.
5. After the braising time, uncover the pot, add the potato chunks, cover again, and simmer over low heat for about 30 minutes, until the potatoes can be easily pierced with a chopstick and the beef is fork-tender.
6. Uncover the pot, increase to high heat, and reduce the sauce until thickened. Taste and adjust salt or sugar as desired.
   **Key point**: Adjust the sauce thickness and seasoning to your preference.

## Health Notes
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene. Potatoes provide carbohydrates, making it suitable for those needing energy replenishment. Beef brisket has a relatively high fat content, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance calories. Ensure sufficient braising time to achieve tender, easily digestible meat.
