# Red Bean Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 500g all-purpose or bread flour
🥚 2 eggs
🍬 2 tablespoons white sugar (adds sweetness to the flour and promotes fermentation)
🧂 5g dry yeast
💧 200ml warm water (to dissolve the yeast)
🫒 50ml vegetable oil (for kneading, makes the dough softer)
🫘 Red bean paste filling, as needed
🧈 Breadcrumbs, as needed (yellow breadcrumbs, for coating the raw buns)
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for pan-frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥛 Measuring cup
🥢 Chopsticks
🪵 Wooden cutting board (for kneading and dividing dough)
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍽️ Tray (for holding breadcrumbs)
🍳 Electric griddle
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Activate the yeast**: Place the 5g of dry yeast into 200ml of warm water and stir until completely dissolved. Let it sit for 3–5 minutes until bubbles form on the surface.
2.  **Knead the dough**: Pour the 500g of flour into a bowl, add the 2 eggs, and add the 2 tablespoons of white sugar. Add the yeast water to the flour in batches, stirring with chopsticks until it forms a flaky texture. Add the 50ml of vegetable oil and knead into a smooth, slightly soft dough.
    **Key Point**: Adding the liquid in batches makes it easier to adjust the consistency; the dough should be kneaded until it is soft but not sticky.
3.  **First fermentation**: Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place for about 40 minutes.
    **Check Point**: The dough should double in volume. When you poke a hole with a finger dusted with flour, it should not spring back. The inside should have a uniform honeycomb texture.
4.  **Knead to release air and divide**: Coat the cutting board with a layer of vegetable oil to prevent sticking. Take out the dough, knead it briefly to release air, and roll it into a long log. Divide the log into evenly sized dough pieces.
5.  **Fill and shape**: Roll each dough piece into a ball, then flatten it. Roll it out into a wrapper that is thin at the edges and thicker in the center. Place an appropriate amount of red bean paste filling in the center. Pinch the edges tightly to seal the bun, then place it seam-side down and gently flatten it into a round patty.
6.  **Coat with breadcrumbs and second proofing**: Place the raw bun on a tray containing breadcrumbs and coat it evenly. Arrange all the coated buns on the cutting board, cover with plastic wrap, and let them proof for a second time for about 20 minutes.
    **Check Point**: The buns should noticeably increase in size and feel light to the touch.
7.  **Pan-fry**: Preheat the electric griddle on low heat and pour in an appropriate amount of vegetable oil. Place the proofed buns on the griddle, keeping the heat on low the entire time. Flip the buns after about 1 minute and continue frying until the other side is golden brown.
    **Key Point**: It is crucial to use low heat throughout to prevent the crust from burning while the inside remains uncooked.
8.  **Serve**: Fry until both sides are golden brown and the buns have noticeably puffed up. Press the side with chopsticks; if it springs back quickly, the buns are ready.
    **Finished Product Features**: The outside is slightly crispy, the inside is fluffy and soft, and the red bean paste filling is sweet and smooth.

## Health Suggestion
As a staple food, red bean paste is rich in dietary fiber and plant protein, making these buns suitable for people who need to increase satiety. However, the pan-frying process adds extra oil, so it is recommended to serve alongside light vegetables to balance the calories. Diabetic patients should be mindful of their consumption amount.
