# Smashed Chili and Century Egg

Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Green chili peppers, about 7  
🥚 Century eggs, about 2 (peeled and cut into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic, about 3 cloves (minced)  
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍶 Aged vinegar, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥣 Stainless steel mortar/bowl  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Wooden rolling pin/pestle  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Place a non-stick pan over heat without oil. Add the green chili peppers and pan-fry over low heat until the skin wrinkles and develops charred black spots.  
**Note**: Dry-fry the entire time; do not add oil.  

2. Remove the pan-fried peppers and let cool. Peel off some of the charred skin.  
3. Place the prepared peppers, century egg pieces, and minced garlic into a mortar. Add light soy sauce, aged vinegar, salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, and sesame oil in order.  
4. Use a pestle or rolling pin to thoroughly smash and mix until a coarse paste forms.  
5. Plate and serve. It can be enjoyed with rice, noodles, or stuffed into steamed buns for a rich flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, making it ideal for stimulating the appetite and cutting through grease. However, century eggs are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with multigrain rice to balance high-carbohydrate staples.
