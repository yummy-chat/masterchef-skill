# Zhajiang Noodles (Fried Sauce Noodles)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 300g (diced, separating lean and fatty parts; choose fattier portion)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms 50g (diced; can be omitted if not preferred)
🧅 Scallion 100g (use the white part, chopped into scallion pieces; use plenty)
🧄 Garlic 30g (about 5 cloves, minced)
🫚 Ginger 5g (sliced into two thin pieces)
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces
🌿 Celery, diced as needed (for topping)
🌱 Bean sprouts as needed (for topping)
🥕 Carrot, julienned as needed (for topping)
🥒 Cucumber, julienned as needed (for topping)
🍜 Noodles as needed

🫘 Dry yellow soybean paste 30g
🍯 Sweet bean paste 40g
🫘 Soybean paste 40g
💧 Water 20g (to dilute the pastes)
🍶 Aged vinegar 10g (to soften the saltiness of the paste)
🍬 Sugar 5g (to soften the saltiness of the paste)
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g
🍾 Dark soy sauce 10g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 5g
🌽 Cornstarch 3g (about a pinch)
💧 Water 100g (for cornstarch slurry)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (use a bit more, for frying the sauce)
🫗 Sesame oil ½ tablespoon
🧂 Salt (not used; the pastes are salty enough)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board and knife
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔥 Gas or electric stove
🍲 Stockpot (for boiling noodles and blanching toppings)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Separate the fatty and lean parts of the pork belly, cut into small dice, and place separately. Dice the shiitake mushrooms. For the scallion, use the white part, split thick sections, and chop into scallion pieces. Crush the garlic and mince it. Slice the ginger into two thin pieces.
**Key Point**: Fatty and lean pork dices must be separated, as they are added to the pan at different times.

2.  In a bowl, combine the dry yellow soybean paste, sweet bean paste, and soybean paste. Add two tablespoons of water and stir to dilute. Then add aged vinegar, sugar, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and chicken bouillon. Stir until well combined.
**Key Point**: The aged vinegar and sugar are used to neutralize the saltiness of the pastes, making the salty flavor milder; they are not meant to provide a sour or sweet taste.

3.  In a bowl, add a pinch of cornstarch. Add about 100g of water and stir until well combined. Set aside.
4.  Pour a generous amount of oil into the pan. Add the ginger slices and two star anise to the cold oil. Fry over medium heat until the ginger is dried and the aroma is rich. Remove and discard using a slotted spoon.
5.  Turn the heat to high. First, add the fatty pork dices and fry until they turn golden and shrink in volume. Then, add the lean pork dices and stir-fry until they are separated and have changed color.
6.  Add the minced garlic and half of the scallion pieces. Stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the diced shiitake mushrooms and stir-fry until they soften.
**Key Point**: Reserve half of the scallion pieces; they are added at the very end.

7.  Pour in the prepared mixed sauce. Keep the heat on high and stir-fry until the sauce aroma develops, and dense small bubbles appear on the surface.
8.  Pour in the cornstarch slurry and stir to combine evenly. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, cover the pan with a lid, and simmer for about 10 minutes.
**Key Point**: Stir occasionally during simmering to prevent sticking to the bottom. The sauce's aroma will transition from a dry, salty smell to a rich, meaty fragrance.

9.  After 10 minutes, add the remaining half of the scallion pieces and half a tablespoon of sesame oil. Stir immediately to combine, then turn off the heat right away.
**Key Point**: If the sesame oil is added too early, its flavor will be lost. Once mixed, turn off the heat immediately.

10. Cover the pan with the lid and let it sit for 5 minutes using residual heat, allowing the scallion and sauce aromas to fully meld.
11. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Cook the noodles until done. Blanch the diced celery and bean sprouts in the noodle water. Place the noodles in a bowl and top with the blanched vegetables, julienned carrot, cucumber, etc.
12. Spoon the cooked meat sauce over the noodles, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice
Zhajiang noodles are relatively high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables (such as cucumber julienne and bean sprouts) to reduce the overall dietary burden. Suitable for consumption after significant physical exertion, but those with high blood pressure or managing their weight should be mindful of portion sizes.
