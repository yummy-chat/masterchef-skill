# Emerald Consommé 🥒
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🐔 Chicken carcass – appropriate amount (finely minced, for quickly extracting a fresh broth)  
🥒 Cucumber – about 6 (peeled, seeded, cut into thin small slices or cubes)  
🐔 Chicken breast – appropriate amount (beaten into a fine puree)  
🌿 Scallion and ginger – a small dish (to remove fishiness and enhance aroma)  
🧂 Salt – about 4 small wooden spoons  
🍬 Sugar – about 2 small wooden spoons  
🌶️ White pepper – appropriate amount (for removing fishiness from the chicken puree and seasoning the clear broth)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine – about 20 grams (one liang)  
🥄 Water starch – appropriate amount (for thickening)  

## Cookware & Equipment 🍳
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥅 Fine-mesh strainer  
🔪 Cutting board & chef's knife  
🌀 Grinder/food processor  
🥣 Bowls  

## Preparation Steps 📋
1. Finely mince the chicken carcass.  
**Key point**: The finer the mince, the faster the umami flavor is released.

2. Peel the cucumber, cut it lengthwise, remove all seeds, and slice into thin small pieces or small cubes.  
**Key point**: Knife skills must be precise to ensure a fresh aroma and appealing appearance; use a generous amount.

3. Beat the chicken breast into a fine paste, add white pepper, Shaoxing wine, and an appropriate amount of water, stirring until it forms a thick, corn-porridge-like consistency.  
**Key point**: This is crucial for the second clarification step to make the soup clear.

4. Bring water in a wok to a boil, add the minced chicken bones, and cook rapidly over high heat for about 10 minutes until the broth turns white. Add scallion, ginger, salt, sugar, white pepper, and Shaoxing wine to season.  
**Key point**: Boil for about 10 minutes to quickly obtain a flavorful base broth.

5. Gently pour the prepared chicken puree into the barely simmering broth, then turn the heat to the lowest setting; do not stir.  
**Key point**: Utilize the principle of protein suspension and adhesion to allow the chicken puree to coagulate into a "meat cake" that floats and absorbs impurities from the broth.

6. Once the "meat cake" has fully coagulated and floated to the surface, turn off the heat. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer, discarding all solids, and retain only the clear broth.
7. Return the strained clear broth to a boil, add all the cucumber cubes, and stir rapidly over high heat to prevent oxidation and discoloration.
8. When the broth comes to a rolling boil, drizzle in the water starch to thicken. Remove from heat and serve immediately once the broth thickens.  
**Key point**: After adding the cucumber, work quickly to maintain its crunch and vibrant green color.

## Health Tips 💚
Cucumbers are rich in water and vitamin C, offering a refreshing and palate-cleansing quality ideal for summer heat relief. However, the thickening slightly increases calorie content, so those managing their weight should consume in moderation.
