# Braised Pork Trotters with Soybeans
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐷 Pork trotters, about 2 lbs (cut into chunks, preferably front trotters)
🫘 Dried soybeans, about 150g (soak overnight in advance)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (soaked in water in advance)
⭐ Star anise, to taste (soaked in water in advance)
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste (soaked in water in advance)
🌿 Cinnamon stick, to taste (soaked in water in advance)
🫚 Ginger, about 2 slices (for blanching and braising)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)
🍬 Rock sugar, to taste (for caramelizing)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🫘 Soybean paste, 1 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tbsp
🧂 Light soy sauce, 3 tbsp
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp
🧂 Salt, to taste
🐔 Chicken powder, 1 tsp
🍺 Beer, 1 whole can (about 330ml)
💧 Water, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🍲 Clay pot or Dutch oven
🥄 Ladle or spatula
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Bowls or plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the pork trotters into chunks. Soak the dried soybeans in cold water overnight in advance. Soak the dried chili peppers, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and other spices in clean water in advance and set aside.
2. Add cold water to a pot, place the pork trotter chunks, ginger slices, and cooking wine in it. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and continue boiling for about 5 minutes. Remove and rinse thoroughly with clean water.
   **Note**: Must start with cold water for better deodorizing effect.
3. Heat oil in a wok over low heat, add rock sugar, and stir-fry until it turns a deep brown, caramel color with tiny bubbles. Immediately add the pork trotter chunks and stir-fry quickly to coat each piece evenly with the caramel.
   **Key point**: Add the trotters immediately when the sugar starts to bubble slightly to prevent it from burning and turning bitter.
4. Add the soaked spices, ginger slices, and scallion sections, stir-fry until fragrant. Then add soybean paste, cooking wine, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, and chicken powder, and stir-fry evenly.
5. Pour in a whole can of beer and enough hot water. Transfer all ingredients to a clay pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: The beer helps tenderize the meat and enhance the flavor.
6. After 30 minutes, remove the lid, discard the ginger slices, scallion sections, and spice solids to clarify the broth. Add the soaked soybeans and gently stir to combine.
   **Note**: Add the soybeans at this stage to prevent them from overcooking and becoming mushy too early.
7. Cover again, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and continue simmering for another 30 minutes, until the trotters are tender and sticky, the soybeans are flavorful, and the broth has thickened.

## Health Advice
Braised pork trotters with soybeans are rich in collagen and plant protein, suitable for those needing collagen supplementation or skin nourishment. However, the dish is high in calories, so consumption should be moderate. People with gout should only have a small taste.
