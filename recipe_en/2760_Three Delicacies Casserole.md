# Three Delicacies Casserole

Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork front leg meat 400g (for meatballs)  
🦴 Pork backbone 3 pieces (for broth)  
🥬 Yellow heart cabbage 1 head  
🍄 Oyster mushrooms, as needed  
🥚 Eggs 2 (for egg skin)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms 8-10 (soaked and chopped)  
🌰 Water chestnuts 5-6 (crushed and minced)  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms 1 small bowl (soaked)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for scallion-ginger water and garnish)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed (for scallion-ginger water and broth)  
🌿 Cilantro 1 small bunch  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste  
🥫 Light soy sauce, a little  
💧 Scallion-ginger water about 80-100ml (crush scallions and ginger, soak in water)  
🫒 Sesame oil, a little  
🐖 Lard, as needed (for stir-frying vegetables)

## Cookware & Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Soup pot  
🫕 Clay pot or casserole  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🔥 Alcohol burner

## Steps
1.  Rinse the pork backbone clean, place it in a pressure cooker with ginger slices and enough water. After it comes to pressure, cook for 20 minutes to make bone broth.
2.  Beat the eggs into a liquid. Brush the wok with a thin layer of oil, pour in the egg liquid to spread into a thin egg skin. Remove, let cool, then roll it up and cut into shreds for later use.
3.  Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms and wood ear mushrooms in advance. Cut the shiitake mushrooms into strips then chop; crush the water chestnuts then mince finely; wash the yellow heart cabbage, tear the oyster mushrooms into small florets.
4.  Place the ground pork front leg meat in a bowl. Add salt, white pepper powder, and light soy sauce. Add the scallion-ginger water in two batches, stirring in one direction until the water is fully absorbed, and the meat filling becomes sticky and firm.
    **Note**: The scallion-ginger water must be added in batches with thorough stirring, allowing the meat filling to fully "absorb water" and release gelatin.
5.  Add the chopped shiitake mushrooms and water chestnuts to the meat filling. Mix well, then repeatedly slap the mixture against the bowl a dozen times to increase its chewiness.
6.  Heat lard in the wok until hot. Add the yellow heart cabbage and oyster mushrooms, stir-fry over high heat until wilted, then transfer them to the casserole dish to form the bottom layer.
    **Key Point**: Stir-frying before boiling enhances the savory flavor more than boiling directly.
7.  Pour the prepared bone broth along with the pork backbone into the wok and bring to a boil. Use your hand to squeeze the meat filling into meatballs and drop them into the broth. Cook for about 6-7 minutes until the meatballs float and are cooked through.
8.  Season the soup with salt, a little white pepper powder, and sesame oil. Then pour it into the casserole dish containing the vegetables.
9.  Top the surface with wood ear mushrooms and shredded egg skin, and finally garnish with chopped scallions and cilantro.
10. Place the casserole on an alcohol burner, light the burner, and serve while keeping it warm over heat.
    **Key Point**: Once served, keep it on a low, continuous heat to maintain the soup's temperature and savory flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish combines meat and vegetables, with a rich, flavorful broth made from pork bone stock. Water chestnuts and shiitake mushrooms are added to the meatballs to enhance texture, making it suitable for warming and nourishing the body in winter. Be sure to stir the meat filling in one direction until it becomes firm for a springy texture. Gout patients should consume the soup in moderation, as the bone broth has a high purine content.
