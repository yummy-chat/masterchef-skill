# Homestyle Supreme Pizza
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 200g
🌾 Cake flour 50g
🧈 Butter 20g (for dough)
🍅 Tomatoes 2 (for pizza sauce)
🧅 Onion 1 (for pizza sauce)
🥓 Sausage/ham appropriate amount (sliced)
🥓 Bacon appropriate amount (sliced)
🫑 Green bell pepper appropriate amount (julienned)
🌶️ Red bell pepper appropriate amount (julienned)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes appropriate amount (halved)
🌽 Sweet corn kernels appropriate amount
🧀 Shredded mozzarella cheese appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🥫 Ketchup 150g
🍬 White sugar 20g (for pizza sauce)
🧂 Soy sauce 1 tbsp
🧂 Salt 2 tsp (for pizza sauce)
⚫ Black pepper appropriate amount
🌿 Dried oregano appropriate amount
🫙 Yeast 3g
🍬 White sugar 10g (for dough)
🧂 Salt 1g (for dough)
💧 Cold water 130ml

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Mixing bowl or large bowl
🔪 Rolling pin
🍕 10-inch pizza pan
🖌️ Brush
🍴 Fork
🔥 Oven
🥅 Wire rack
🍴 Pizza cutter or regular knife

## Preparation Steps
1. Make pizza sauce: Finely chop onion and tomatoes separately. Heat oil in a frying pan, sauté chopped onion until fragrant and lightly browned, then add diced tomatoes and cook until soft and liquid has evaporated. Add ketchup, white sugar, freshly ground black pepper, dried oregano, soy sauce, and salt. Stir well and simmer until thick.
   **Key Point**: Tomatoes must be cooked until all liquid evaporates; seasonings can be adjusted to taste.
2. Make dough: Combine bread flour, cake flour, white sugar, yeast, and salt. Add cold water and stir into a shaggy mass. Add butter and knead into a soft dough. Transfer to a work surface and continue kneading for about 5 minutes until smooth. Divide the dough into two portions. Take one portion and roll it into a circle with a rolling pin.
   **Key Point**: Knead the dough until smooth; portions can be adjusted based on pan size.
3. Prepare toppings: While the dough is resting, julienne green and red bell peppers, halve cherry tomatoes, and prepare sliced sausage, sliced bacon, corn kernels, and shredded mozzarella cheese.
4. Proof the dough base: Place the rolled-out dough into a buttered pizza pan. Press and spread it to cover the pan, making the edges slightly thicker. Use a fork to prick holes evenly across the dough. Let it rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Proof until the dough springs back quickly when pressed at the edge.
5. Assemble the pizza: Brush the proofed dough base with butter, then spread the homemade pizza sauce evenly. Layer in order: a thin layer of shredded cheese, julienned green and red peppers, onion strips, sweet corn kernels, another layer of shredded cheese, bacon slices, ham slices, and cherry tomatoes. Sprinkle with black pepper and dried oregano, then top generously with two layers of shredded mozzarella cheese.
   **Note**: Distribute toppings evenly; use ample cheese.
6. Bake: Preheat the oven to 210°C (410°F) with top and bottom heat. Place the assembled pizza on the middle rack and bake for about 20 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: Bake until the crust is golden brown, the cheese is melted and develops browned spots.
7. Remove and slice: Take the baked pizza out of the oven. Cut into slices using a pizza cutter or regular knife. Serve hot.

## Health Tips
Homemade pizza allows you to control the amount of cheese and sauce, making it a good choice for family gatherings to reduce the high oil and salt content of takeout. Note that the crust is still calorie-dense; pairing with vegetables can increase satiety.
