# Spicy Cream Noodles  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧅 1 onion (purple onion, sliced)  
🧄 3 garlic cloves (minced)  
🍄 5 button mushrooms (sliced)  
🍜 1 serving of noodles (fresh noodles, instant noodles, hand-pulled noodles, pasta, etc. are all fine)  
🍥 Small rice cakes, as desired (optional ingredient; add to cold water if using)  
🧈 1 knob of butter (for frying the base)  
🥄 2 tbsp soy sauce  
🥄 1 tbsp oyster sauce  
🌶️ 1 tbsp Korean chili paste  
🍅 2 tbsp tomato paste (Xinjiang tomato paste recommended)  
🍬 1 large pinch of sugar  
🧂 1 large pinch of chicken bouillon powder  
💧 About 500 ml water (400–600 ml is fine)  
🥛 3–4 tbsp heavy cream  
🌶️ 1 whole packet of spicy chicken noodle sauce (can be adjusted or omitted based on taste)  
🌶️ Fresh bird's eye chilies, as desired (chopped, optional)  
🌿 Dried garlic and parsley flakes, as desired  
🧀 1 slice of cheese (optional)  
🧂 Salt, as desired (add at the end based on preference)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan (or any pot)  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🔥 Gas stove (or induction cooktop)  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥘 Pot lid  

## Steps  
### Preparation Stage  
1. **Prepare the onion**: Peel off the dry outer layer and any tough old skin, cut off the root end, and rinse under running water.  
   **Key point**: Removing an extra layer helps eliminate dry skin; cutting off the root reduces irritation while chopping.  
2. **Prepare the mushrooms**: Rinse under running water, focusing on the gills under the cap.  
   **Key point**: Mushrooms are relatively clean; a quick rinse is sufficient.  
3. **Slice and chop**: Cut the onion into thin slices, flatten the garlic cloves and mince them, slice the mushrooms into even thin slices.  
   **Key point**: When cutting the onion, curl your fingers like a "cat's paw" and use your knuckles to guide the knife to avoid cuts; you can hold a sip of water at the back of your tongue to reduce eye irritation.  

### Cooking Stage  
1. **Fry the base**: Place the butter in a frying pan over low heat until melted and bubbling. Add the minced garlic and stir until fragrant, then add the onion slices. Turn the heat to medium and stir-fry until the onion softens.  
   **Key point**: Butter burns easily; be sure to melt it over low heat.  
2. **Add vegetables**: Once the onion is soft, add the mushroom slices. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until the mushrooms soften and release moisture.  
3. **Season**: Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, Korean chili paste, tomato paste, sugar, and chicken bouillon powder one by one, stirring quickly to coat the vegetables evenly.  
   **Key point**: Keep the heat on the lowest setting when adding seasonings to prevent burning.  
4. **Add water and boil**: Pour in about 500 ml of water, turn the heat to high, cover the pot, and bring the broth to a boil.  
   **Key point**: If adding rice cakes, put them in the cold water at this step and let them boil together with the broth.  
5. **Cook the noodles**: Once the water boils, add one serving of noodles, use chopsticks to separate them, and cook according to the noodle type until reaching the desired doneness.  
   **Key point**: If using pasta or other hard-to-cook noodles, pre-cook them separately until 80% done before adding to the pot.  
6. **Add cream and spiciness**: When the noodles are nearly done, reduce the heat to medium. Add 3–4 tablespoons of heavy cream and stir well. Then squeeze in the whole packet of spicy chicken noodle sauce, add chopped bird's eye chilies if desired, and stir again to combine.  
   **Key point**: Do not add too much heavy cream; it’s meant to enhance flavor. The spicy chicken noodle sauce and bird's eye chilies are extra sources of heat; omit if you dislike spiciness.  
7. **Serve**: Turn off the heat. Taste and add salt if needed. Sprinkle dried garlic and parsley flakes on top. Add a slice of cheese if desired, letting the residual heat melt it.  
   **Key point**: Cheese and parsley flakes are optional garnishes.  

## Health Notes  
This spicy cream noodles dish combines vegetables and creamy richness, with a spicy kick that stimulates the appetite while offering relatively balanced nutrition. It is suitable for occasional consumption. Pair it with a light side of cold mixed vegetables or fruit to round out the meal. Note that this dish is relatively high in calories and sodium. Those with sensitive stomachs or those managing fat intake should enjoy it in moderation.
