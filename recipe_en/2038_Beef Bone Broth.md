# Beef Bone Broth
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦴 5 grass-fed beef thigh bones (better quality yields more flavorful broth)
🧂 1 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns
🌿 1 tbsp cumin seeds
🧂 2 tsp salt
💧 Water, as needed (add up to the maximum capacity of the pressure cooker)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔥 Oven with convection fan function
🥘 Baking tray
🍳 Pressure cooker
🥄 Metal spatula
🗑️ Strainer
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🍶 Small saucepan (for clarifying beef tallow)
🍱 Airtight container or graduated measuring cup
🫙 Glass jar (for storing beef tallow)
🛍️ Sealable bags (for freezing broth cubes)
🌡️ Infrared thermometer

## Instructions
1. Arrange the beef bones in a single layer on a baking tray. Place in an un-preheated oven. Set the temperature to 205°C (400°F), turn on the convection fan, and roast for 60–75 minutes, until the bone surfaces are golden brown, a significant amount of beef tallow has rendered into the tray, and there are browned, caramelized residues.

2. Remove the baking tray from the oven. Strain the liquid tallow and pour it into a glass jar for storage. Use a metal spatula to scrape up all the browned residues from the tray; set them aside.

3. Place one of the roasted beef bones into the pressure cooker. Add the maximum amount of water. Add the Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, salt, and the scraped browned residues.

4. Close the lid. Once the cooker reaches pressure, reduce the heat to low and cook for 2 hours.

5. Open the lid. Use a soup ladle to skim the thick layer of tallow from the surface of the broth. Pour this skimmed tallow into the small saucepan; set it aside.

6. Remove the bones from the broth. Break them open and extract the meat and marrow. Mash the marrow.

7. Return all the extracted meat, bones, and mashed marrow to the broth in the pressure cooker.

8. Close the lid again. Once the cooker reaches pressure, reduce the heat to low and cook for 1 more hour.

9. Open the lid again. Skim any remaining surface fat. Remove all meat and bones from the broth.

10. Strain the remaining broth through a fine-mesh strainer into an airtight container or measuring cup. Cover it and allow it to cool slightly, then refrigerate until it solidifies into a jelly-like consistency.

11. Cut the solidified beef bone broth into blocks sized for a single use. Place the blocks into a sealable bag, press out as much air as possible, and freeze.

12. Heat the tallow mixture from the small saucepan over high heat until it boils and all water has evaporated. The oil will become clear and its temperature will rise above 100°C (212°F). Pour the hot, clarified tallow into a glass jar and seal it tightly.

## Health Tips
Beef bone broth is rich in collagen and minerals, which may support joint health and recovery after surgery. It is recommended to enjoy it with vegetables for balanced nutrition. Individuals with high blood pressure should monitor their salt intake.
