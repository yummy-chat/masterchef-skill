# Small Hanging Pear Soup
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍐 Crown pear/Snow pear 2 (peeled and cut into pieces, retain the pear skin)
🌰 Water chestnuts 4 (peeled and sliced)
🎋 Sugarcane 1 small section (cut into small segments)
🫘 Red dates 8 (pitted)
🍄 Dried white fungus 25g (soaked in advance, tear into fine pieces)
🍇 Goji berries 10g
💧 Pure water 5L
🍬 Rock sugar 50g
🍯 Brown sugar 20g

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl/container (for soaking white fungus)
🍲 Transparent pot (recommended for visible cooking, used for simmering)
🍚 Rice cooker (optional alternative device)
🥄 Soup ladle
☕ Small bowl (for serving the finished dish)

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the dried white fungus in cold water until fully rehydrated, then tear it into the smallest possible pieces by hand.
**Key point**: The finer you tear it, the easier it is to release its gelatin.

2. Peel the pears, reserve the peels for later use; core the pear flesh and cut it into pieces. Peel and slice the water chestnuts, pit the red dates, and cut the sugarcane into small segments.
3. Add 5 liters of pure water to the pot, then add the white fungus, pear peels, pear pieces, sugarcane, water chestnuts, and red dates in order. Bring to a boil over high heat.
**Key point**: Do not add sugar at this stage, as it will affect the fungus releasing gelatin.

4. After the soup boils, reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly for 3 to 4 hours. If too much water evaporates, you can add a small amount of hot water midway. Simmer until the soup becomes thick and exhibits a noticeable gelatinous texture. Alternatively, you can use the soup-cooking function of a rice cooker instead of simmering over an open flame.
**Note**: If adding water midway, use hot water to avoid a sudden drop in temperature that could affect gelatin release.

5. After simmering for about 4 hours, add the goji berries, rock sugar, and brown sugar, and stir well.
6. Continue simmering over low heat for 20 minutes, until the sugar is completely dissolved and the soup is well blended.
7. Ladle into small bowls and serve hot.

## Health Advice
Small Hanging Pear Soup is rich in gelatin and vitamins, making it especially suitable for moisturizing the lungs and nourishing the skin during the dry autumn season. The degree to which the white fungus is torn affects the amount of gelatin released; it is recommended to tear it as finely as possible. Diabetic patients need to control sugar intake. If you find open-flame simmering too troublesome, you can use the rice cooker's soup-cooking function as an alternative.
