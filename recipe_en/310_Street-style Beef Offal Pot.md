# Street-style Beef Offal Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef offal appropriate amount (includes beef intestines, beef lung, beef stomach, beef heart, beef tripe, etc., thoroughly cleaned in advance)
🧅 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🌿 Celery appropriate amount (cut into sections, used for garnish when serving)
🍶 High-proof baijiu about one liang (for deodorizing during blanching)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for sautéing ginger slices)
🥫 Braising sauce (or chu hou paste) appropriate amount (for stir-frying)
🍚 Rice wine appropriate amount (to enhance aroma)
🧂 Soy sauce appropriate amount (for adding aroma during stir-frying)
💧 Water appropriate amount (for braising, enough to submerge the offal)
🍬 Rock sugar one small piece (to enhance freshness)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🍯 Dark soy sauce a little (for coloring)
🌶️ Peppercorns a little
🧂 Spice bag 1 (contains cinnamon, star anise, dried tangerine peel, dried chili, licorice, fennel, and black cardamom)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok (large iron wok)
🥄 Strainer/spatula
♨️ Pressure cooker
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and holding)
🥘 Clay pot or large bowl (for serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Thoroughly wash the beef offal and cut into pieces of appropriate size; slice the ginger, cut the celery into sections, and put all the spices into a cloth bag to make a spice bag.
2. Add cold water to the wok, put in all the beef offal, and heat over fire.
**Key point**: Must start with cold water so that the unpleasant odors are gradually released as the water temperature rises.
3. When the water is about to boil, pour about one liang of high-proof baijiu along the edge of the wok. Blanch for about 2–3 minutes. Once the offal has set and more foam appears, remove and rinse clean with water.
4. Put the rinsed beef offal into the pressure cooker, add an appropriate amount of water, cover the lid, bring to high pressure over high heat, and cook for about 2 minutes.
5. Remove the cooked offal and cut into small, bite-sized pieces.
6. Heat the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil, and sauté the ginger slices over low heat until fragrant.
7. Add the cut beef offal and turn the heat to high, stir-frying.
8. Add an appropriate amount of braising sauce and rice wine, and continue stir-frying over high heat to release the aroma of the sauce and wine.
9. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of soy sauce and stir-fry evenly.
**Note**: The soy sauce here is used to enhance aroma, distinct from the dark soy sauce used later for coloring.
10. Add hot water, then put in one small piece of rock sugar, an appropriate amount of salt, a little dark soy sauce, and a little peppercorns.
11. Once the soup comes to a boil, skim off any foam, and add the spice bag.
12. Cover the wok, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise until the offal is tender and flavorful and the broth is rich.
**Key point**: The cooking time depends on the tenderness of the offal and personal preference; if there is more broth, it can be drunk, or if a thicker consistency is desired, you can reduce the sauce or thicken it with a slurry.
13. Transfer to a clay pot or large bowl and sprinkle with celery sections before serving.

## Health Advice
Beef offal is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for people with high physical exertion. However, it is high in cholesterol and purines, so gout patients should consume it with caution. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables to balance nutrition and avoid overburdening the digestive system.
