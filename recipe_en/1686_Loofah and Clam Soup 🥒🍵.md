# Loofah and Clam Soup 🥒🍵
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Loofah (to taste; recommended: Hunan white jade loofah, cut into long strips or slices)
🐚 Clams (to taste; washed in advance, soaked in salted water to expel sand)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes (to taste; halved; can substitute with sliced tomatoes)
🫚 Old ginger (to taste; peeled and cut into ginger shreds)
🫒 Vegetable oil (to taste; for greasing the pan)
🧂 Salt (to taste; used in stages: for soaking clams, softening loofah when stir-frying, and seasoning after serving)
🌶️ White pepper (to taste; added after serving)
🍶 Fujian rice wine or cooking wine (one small cup; drizzled along the pan edge before serving)
🫗 Sesame oil (a little; optional; drizzled on the surface after serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Stainless steel pot
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥒 Vegetable peeler
🔥 Stove
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the loofah and cut into long strips or slices. Halve the cherry tomatoes. Peel the old ginger, first slice it, then cut into ginger shreds. Wash the clams and soak in salted water to let them expel sand.
2. Heat the stainless steel pot over medium-high heat, pour in the base oil, and fully grease the pot for about 3 minutes until a non-stick surface forms on the walls.
   **Note**: Greasing is key to preventing sticking; using a stainless steel pot better handles shellfish ingredients.
3. Add the loofah, stir-fry over medium-high heat, and add a pinch of salt to help it soften. Stir-fry until the loofah surface evenly releases water.
4. Add the cherry tomatoes, gently stir-fry, keeping their shape intact as much as possible.
5. Add about half a pot of water to the pot and put in the ginger shreds.
6. After the water boils, add the clams, maintain medium-high heat, cover the pot, and cook for about 3 minutes, until all clams have opened. Immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key**: Do not cook too long to avoid the clam meat becoming tough.
7. Before serving, drizzle one small cup of Fujian rice wine along the pan edge to remove any fishy smell and enhance aroma.
8. Ladle the soup into a bowl. Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Optionally, drizzle a little sesame oil to enhance fragrance.

## Health Tips
Loofah and clam soup is low in fat and high in protein. Loofah is rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for summertime cooling and weight control. Clams are zinc-rich and easy to digest, ideal for the elderly and children. However, those with gout should consume the soup sparingly.
