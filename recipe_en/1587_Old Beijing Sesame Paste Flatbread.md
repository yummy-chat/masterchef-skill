# Old Beijing Sesame Paste Flatbread
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g  
🧂 Baking soda 6g  
💧 Water 600g (for kneading dough)  
🥜 Sesame paste, as needed (for filling)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed  
🌿 Cumin seeds, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🌱 Raw sesame seeds, as needed (for coating)  
🫘 Soy sauce, as needed  
🌾 Flour, as needed (for making "fake sesame paste")  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount (for brushing dough and pan-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🫗 Large bowl  
🌀 Rolling pin  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🖌️ Brush  
🍞 Dough board  
🍳 Electric griddle  
🔥 Oven  
🥘 Baking sheet  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: Combine all-purpose flour and baking soda in a large bowl. Gradually add water while stirring until the mixture becomes flaky. Then, use a slapping and folding technique to knead until the dough is smooth and no longer sticks to your hands.  
2. **Rest the dough**: Brush a layer of cooking oil over the surface of the dough, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest sufficiently to fully relax the gluten.  
3. **Make spice powder**: Roast the Sichuan peppercorns and cumin seeds in an oven or a dry pan until they deepen in color and release a fragrant aroma. Remove and grind into a fine powder.  
**Note**: The spices must be thoroughly roasted to eliminate the numbing sensation, retain the aroma, and remove excess moisture.  
4. **Prepare the sesame paste filling**: Add the roasted spice powder and salt to the sesame paste, and stir until it forms a smooth, even paste.  
5. **Prepare the "fake sesame paste"**: Mix soy sauce, a small amount of flour, and a little water to form a paste with a color and consistency similar to sesame paste. This will be used later to increase adhesion when coating with sesame seeds.  
6. **Roll out the dough**: Take the rested dough and roll it into a long triangular sheet that is wider at one end, narrower at the other, and slightly thicker in the middle. Roll from the wide end toward the narrow end, stretching the dough as thin and long as possible to increase the number of layers.  
7. **Spread the filling and roll**: Evenly spread a thick layer of the sesame paste filling over the dough sheet. Then, roll it from the wide end toward the narrow end, gently stretching the dough as you roll.  
**Note**: The filling must be spread evenly and generously; stretching the dough during rolling will create more layers.  
8. **Second resting**: Cover the rolled-out long strip with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15 minutes to facilitate portioning and shaping later.  
9. **Portion and shape**: Tear the long strip into evenly sized pieces. Take one piece, fold the cut ends inward, pinch them together, and gather the dough towards the center to form a peach-like shape with a pointed top. This encases the filling and creates internal layers.  
10. **Coat with sesame seeds**: Brush the smooth side of the flatbread blank with the "fake sesame paste," then immediately press it upside down into a plate of raw sesame seeds. Ensure it is evenly coated; press lightly to make the seeds stick until no dough is visible when turned over.  
11. **Pan-fry**: Preheat the electric griddle and brush with a little oil. Place the flatbread blank with the sesame-coated side down. First, fry the sesame side until golden-brown "smile" spots appear, then flip and fry the other side until both sides are golden.  
12. **Bake**: Place the pan-fried flatbreads into an oven preheated to 200°C and bake for about 7–8 minutes to make the outer skin crispier.  
13. **Serve and enjoy**: The flatbreads have a crispy outer skin and a soft interior, with a rich, layered aroma of sesame paste, Sichuan peppercorns, and cumin. They can achieve over 20 layers.  

## Health Advice
Sesame paste flatbreads are rich in layers but high in calories. It is recommended to serve them with plain congee or vegetables for breakfast to balance the carbohydrate load. They are suitable for people needing energy replenishment, such as manual laborers or students. For those on a weight-loss plan, portion control is necessary.
