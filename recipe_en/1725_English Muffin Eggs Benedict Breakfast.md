# English Muffin Eggs Benedict Breakfast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 English muffin dough, appropriate amount (completed overnight cold fermentation)
🎨 Beetroot puree, a little (for coloring the hollandaise sauce)
🥔 Small potatoes, appropriate amount (halved)
🥓 Bacon, 3 strips
🥚 Eggs, 2-3 (1 for hollandaise sauce, 2 for poached eggs)
🥬 Endive, appropriate amount
🍊 Orange, half (cut into pieces)
🍋 Lemon juice, a little
🧂 Salt, a little
💧 Water, a little (to assist in whisking the hollandaise sauce)
🧈 Butter, about 60 grams per egg yolk
🌽 Corn flour, appropriate amount (to prevent sticking)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
⚫ Black kneading mat
⭕ Round metal mold
🔥 Oven
🍲 Stainless steel basin/bowl
🥄 Hand whisk
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥘 Soup pot
☕ Moka pot
🥄 Kettle/soup ladle
🔪 Dinner knife/fork
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1.  Take out the English muffin dough that has completed overnight cold fermentation. Sprinkle corn flour on the kneading mat to prevent sticking.
   **Note**: The dough is extremely sticky and soft, semi-fluid. When handling, add more corn flour to prevent sticking.

2.  Use a round metal mold to press and cut the dough into rounds. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

3.  Transfer the shaped dough to the oven. Place a bowl of hot water on the lower rack for the second fermentation, about 40 minutes.

4.  Make the hollandaise sauce: Mix egg yolk, lemon juice, salt, and a little water in a bowl. Place over a hot water bath and whisk continuously with a hand whisk until thick and pale.
   **Key point**: Heating over a water bath keeps the egg yolk mixture stable, preventing it from overheating and curdling.

5.  Add the beetroot puree to the whisked egg yolk mixture. Whisk until evenly combined and turns pink.

6.  Slowly pour the melted butter into the pink egg yolk mixture while whisking at high speed until fully emulsified, forming a pink hollandaise sauce.
   **Key point**: The ratio is about 60 grams of butter per egg yolk.

7.  Wash the small potatoes and cut them in half.

8.  Place a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat. Fry the bacon until crispy and the fat is rendered. Remove and keep warm.
   **Judgment standard**: The bacon edges curl, color deepens, and texture becomes crispy.

9.  Using the remaining bacon fat, place the fermented muffins in the pan. Fry over low heat until golden brown on both sides and cooked through.

10. Bring water in a soup pot to a gentle simmer. Use a ladle to gently slide the egg into the water. Cook for about 3 minutes to the desired doneness, then remove.
    **Key point**: The water temperature must be at a gentle simmer. Too hot and the egg white will disperse.

11. Using the remaining bacon fat, optionally add a small amount of water. Pan-fry the halved small potatoes until the surface is golden and the inside is soft and cooked.

12. Assemble: Horizontally split the cooked muffin. Layer with endive, fried bacon, and poached egg. Finally, drizzle with the pink hollandaise sauce.

13. Place the endive, fried potatoes, and orange pieces on one side of the plate to complete the plating.

## Health Advice
This breakfast is rich in protein, but the hollandaise sauce has a high butter content. It is recommended to serve with oranges and endive to cut the richness. Best enjoyed slowly on a weekend. Poached eggs with a runny yolk are most tempting.
