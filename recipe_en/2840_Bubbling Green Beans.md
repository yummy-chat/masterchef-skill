# Bubbling Green Beans  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫛 Oil green beans (or broad beans) as needed, washed and drained  
🐖 Lard as needed  
🥩 Pork belly as needed, cut into thick slices  
🧅 Scallions as needed, cut into sections  
🫚 Ginger as needed, sliced  
⭐ Star anise 1 piece  
🧪 Baking soda as needed (key for bubbling)  
🧂 Salt as needed  
🥄 Soy sauce (not recommended, as it will deflate the bubbles)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula or wooden spatula  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Slotted spoon (for plating)  

## Steps  
1. Remove the ends and strings from the green beans, wash and drain thoroughly. Cut pork belly into thick slices, scallions into sections, and slice the ginger. Set aside.  
2. Heat the wok, add lard and melt. Once the oil is hot, add the green beans and stir-fry over medium-low heat, slowly cooking until the bean skins wrinkle and the beans release their own moisture.  
3. When the beans change color, add an appropriate amount of baking soda to the wok. The oil will begin to react with the moisture released by the beans and the baking soda, causing a saponification reaction that quickly produces fine foam.  
**Note**: Do not add too much baking soda at once; add it in small batches and observe the bubbling effect.  
4. After the foam rises, add the pork belly slices, scallion sections, ginger slices, and star anise. Stir-fry together until the meat is cooked through.  
**Key**: Throughout the process, **do not add vegetable oil or soy sauce**, as these will quickly eliminate the foam produced by the saponification reaction. If you aim for the traditional "bubbling" effect, avoid these entirely.  
5. Depending on the liquid in the wok, if braising is needed, add a small amount of hot water, cover the wok, and simmer over low heat until the beans are tender and fully cooked.  
**Note**: Do not use too high heat, as excessive temperature will inhibit the foaming effect of the saponification reaction.  
6. Before serving, season with an appropriate amount of salt according to taste, gently mix, and plate. The beans should be glossy and vibrant green, with a layer of fine oil bubbles on the surface.  

## Health Advice  
Bubbling green beans utilize the saponification reaction between lard and baking soda to create a unique foam, making it an appetizing dish for summer. However, pork belly is high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with coarse grain rice for balance. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive fat and sodium intake.
