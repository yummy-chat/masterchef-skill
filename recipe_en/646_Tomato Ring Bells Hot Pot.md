# Tomato Ring Bells Hot Pot
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍅 2 tomatoes (frozen, then blanched with hot water to peel, diced into small pieces)  
🍤 200g shrimp meat (for making shrimp paste)  
🥚 1 egg (added to shrimp paste)  
🫘 1 sheet dried tofu skin (soaked until soft, then rolled)  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms, to taste (side vegetable)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (for stir-frying base and garnish)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (for stir-frying base)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (for stir-frying base)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (shrimp paste seasoning)  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (shrimp paste seasoning)  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste (shrimp paste seasoning)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste (for soup base)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste (for soup base)  
🧈 About 2 small pieces of butter (animal fat butter, not margarine)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Clay pot  
🍳 Wok  
🍳 Oil pan  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Food processor  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place tomatoes in the freezer for about one hour, then remove and blanch with hot water to separate the skin from the flesh. Easily peel off the skin and cut into small dice.  
   **Key Point**: Freezing followed by hot water blanching helps separate the skin from the flesh, making it easier to sauté into a sauce later.  
2. Put the shrimp meat into a food processor, add the egg, and blend into a smooth, flowing shrimp paste. Add salt, sugar, and white pepper, then continue blending until the shrimp paste becomes sticky and elastic.  
   **Key Point**: The paste must be thoroughly blended until it forms a firm consistency for a bouncy texture.  
3. Soak the dried tofu skin in clean water until it softens and regains flexibility. Lay the softened tofu skin flat, spread the shrimp paste evenly over it, roll it into a long strip, and cut into bite-sized segments.  
   **Key Point**: The tofu skin must be fully soaked; otherwise, it will crack when rolled.  
4. Pour enough oil into a pan and heat to 60–70% hot. Fry the tofu skin roll segments until they quickly puff up and turn golden and crispy on the surface. Remove and set aside—these are the ring bells.  
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature must be high enough to instantly expand and set the shape.  
5. Heat the clay pot, add butter, and stir-fry scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add the diced tomatoes and stir-fry until they break down into a sauce and the mixture becomes thick and paste-like.  
   **Key Point**: Use animal fat butter for better aroma enhancement.  
6. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and an appropriate amount of water to the pot. Stir well and bring to a boil. Lay the fried ring bells and enoki mushrooms on top of the soup.  
7. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for five minutes to allow the ring bells to fully absorb the broth.  
   **Key Point**: Simmering on low heat is crucial for making the ring bells soft and flavorful.  
8. Uncover, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene, paired with high-quality protein from shrimp paste and tofu skin, offering balanced nutrition and an appetite-stimulating effect in summer. Note that the oil temperature must be high enough when frying the ring bells to achieve a crispy exterior and tender interior, but control the portion size to avoid excess calories.
