# Celery, Egg, and Dried Shrimp Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 1 stalk of celery (use the whole stalk)
🥚 4 eggs
🦐 1 pack of dried shrimp (about 15-20 grams)
🌾 About 1 flat spoon of all-purpose flour (about 30 grams, adjust slightly based on batter consistency)
🌾 About 2 small spoons of cornstarch (about 10 grams, mix with a little water to make a cornstarch slurry)
🧅 About 1 small handful of chopped scallions (about 15 grams)
🍶 2-3 drops of white vinegar (for removing fishy smell)
🧂 A pinch of salt
🧂 A pinch of five-spice powder
🧂 A pinch of ginger powder
🧂 A pinch of MSG (optional)
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for greasing the pan)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for blanching celery)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing celery)
🥣 Bowl of cold water (for cooling celery)
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for chopping celery)
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl (for mixing filling)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring egg mixture and batter)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for frying pancakes)
🥄 Soup spoon (for scooping batter)

## Instructions
1. Wash the whole stalk of celery, break it in half, and blanch in boiling water for about 20 seconds. Remove immediately and immerse in a bowl of cold water to cool. Then, squeeze out excess water.
   **Note**: Do not blanch for too long, as this helps remove pesticide residue and reduce bitterness while minimizing nutrient loss.
2. Place the squeezed celery on a cutting board and chop it into fine pieces. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
3. In a small bowl, crack 4 eggs, add 2-3 drops of white vinegar, and pour in the prepared cornstarch slurry. Whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Key Point**: White vinegar helps remove the fishy smell, and the cornstarch slurry makes the egg texture smoother after frying.
4. Pour the beaten egg mixture into the large bowl containing the chopped celery.
5. Add the dried shrimp, salt, five-spice powder, MSG, chopped scallions, ginger powder, and one flat spoon of all-purpose flour.
6. Stir all ingredients and seasonings together with chopsticks until evenly combined, forming a thick batter. Finally, add an appropriate amount of sesame oil and mix well again.
   **Note**: Sesame oil enhances aroma and helps prevent sticking during frying.
7. Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and brush a thin layer of cooking oil. Use a soup spoon to scoop the batter into the pan, shaping it into small round pancakes, leaving space between each.
8. Cook over low heat. Once the bottom is set and golden brown, flip the pancakes and continue cooking over low heat until the other side is also golden brown and the pancakes are fully cooked through. Each side takes about 1-2 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Cook over low heat throughout to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains raw.
9. Remove the cooked celery pancakes from the pan and serve on a plate.

## Health Suggestions
This dish is high in protein and low in calories. Celery is rich in dietary fiber, and dried shrimp provide calcium. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or managing blood pressure, blood sugar, and blood lipids. Since dried shrimp are naturally salty, use less salt to avoid excessive sodium intake. It is best to serve hot, as the celery will release water when cooled, affecting the texture.
