# Flash-Fried Treasure Shrimp  
Cuisine: Common Home-Cooked Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Large shrimp, about one plate  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (to coat the shrimp meat)  
🧂 Salt, 2 grams  
🍬 White sugar, 30 grams  
🍶 Rice vinegar, 1 tablespoon  
🍶 White vinegar, 2 tablespoons  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar, 3 tablespoons  
🍶 Cooking wine, 1 tablespoon  
🍯 Honey, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce, half a tablespoon  
💧 Cold water, 2 tablespoons  
🍋 Lime, as needed (to squeeze juice)  
🧄 Ginger and garlic, as needed (minced for stir-frying)  
🫒 Cooking oil, enough (for frying shrimp)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
✂️ Scissors  
🪥 Toothpick  
🧻 Kitchen paper  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Wok (frying pan)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Prepare the shrimp: Insert a toothpick into the second joint of the shrimp's body, lift out the digestive tract, and keep the body intact.  
2. Cut off the shrimp's antennae, squeeze out the sand sac inside the head, and rinse thoroughly with clean water.  
3. Place the shrimp belly-up and slice along the midline from the antennae to the shell, creating a split belly.  
4. After cleaning, use kitchen paper to thoroughly dry the surface of the shrimp, then evenly coat the cut areas of the shrimp meat with a layer of cornstarch.  
5. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add salt, white sugar, rice vinegar, white vinegar, apple cider vinegar, cooking wine, honey, light soy sauce, and cold water in order, stir to combine, and finally squeeze in lime juice and mix well. Set aside.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to around 180°C (350°F). Fry the shrimp one by one until the shell is crispy and the meat is tender, with a golden color. Remove and drain excess oil.  
2. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over low heat, stir-fry the minced ginger and garlic until fragrant.  
3. Pour in the prepared sauce and bring to a boil. No need to thicken with starch. Continue cooking until the sugar is fully dissolved, the sauce becomes thick, and even fish-eye bubbles appear.  
4. Quickly add the fried shrimp and stir-fry rapidly until each shrimp is evenly coated with the sauce. Immediately turn off the heat and plate.  

## Health Tips  
This shrimp dish is seasoned with various vinegars and honey, offering a sweet and sour flavor that whets the appetite. However, the sugar content is as high as 30 grams, so it is recommended to pair it with whole-grain staples. Suitable for those needing calcium and protein supplements, but note the high calorie content from frying, so portion control is advised.
