# Blueberry Egg Tarts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (sliced, steamed, and mashed into a puree)
🥚 Eggs – 2
🥛 Milk – appropriate amount
🌾 All-purpose flour – appropriate amount (adjust based on carrot puree moisture)
🫐 Blueberries – appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar – appropriate amount
🧂 Salt – a small pinch (sprinkled on carrot slices while steaming)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Steamer
🥣 Bowl
🧁 Foil egg tart molds
♨️ Air fryer
🥄 Spoon

## Steps
1. Cut carrots into thick slices, evenly sprinkle a small amount of salt on the surface, and steam in a steamer until completely soft. Remove and mash into a smooth carrot puree.
   **Key point**: Adding salt enhances the carrot's base flavor. Steaming time depends on thickness; ensure no hard core remains in the center.
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk. Add milk and stir thoroughly until fully combined to make the egg tart liquid.
3. Gradually add all-purpose flour and sugar to the carrot puree, kneading into a non-sticky dough. Adjust the flour amount flexibly based on the moisture of the carrot puree, ensuring the dough is moldable.
4. Take an appropriate amount of carrot dough and place it into a foil mold. Use the back of a spoon along with your fingers to gently press the dough, evenly covering the bottom and sides of the mold to form a cup-shaped tart crust.
   **Key point**: The crust should be uniform in thickness, with no holes at the bottom, otherwise, the egg liquid will leak out.
5. Pour the egg tart liquid into the crust, filling it to about 70–80% full to prevent overflow during baking.
6. Evenly place a few fresh blueberries on the surface of the egg tart liquid.
7. Place the egg tarts in the air fryer basket, leaving space between each. Bake at 160–180°C for about 20 minutes, until the egg liquid is fully set and the surface and edges are caramelized brown. Adjust baking time according to the power of your device.
8. Remove and let cool slightly. Enjoy while warm. The crust has a soft carrot cake-like texture, the filling is silky, and the blueberries burst with fruity acidity when heated.

## Health Advice
The carrot crust is rich in dietary fiber, while the eggs and milk provide high-quality protein. This dessert is suitable for occasional enjoyment. Be mindful of the sugar amount to reduce calorie intake.
