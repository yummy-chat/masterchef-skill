# Dirty Bun
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 260g bread flour
🍫 12g cocoa powder (for dough) + appropriate amount (for surface decoration)
🍬 30g white sugar
🧂 5g salt
🍞 5g dry yeast
🥚 2 eggs (approx. 70g)
💧 100ml cold water
🧈 145g unsalted butter (25g kneaded into dough, 120g for lamination)
🍫 Appropriate amount of chocolate chunks (for filling, approx. 10g each)
🥛 50g whole milk (for chocolate sauce)
🍫 80g chocolate chunks (for sauce)

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥖 Rolling pin
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Dough scraper
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🍳 Baking tray & parchment paper
🥘 Small saucepan
🔥 Induction cooker
🥅 Small sieve
🥄 Spoon
🔥 Oven

## Steps
1. Place bread flour, cocoa powder, white sugar, salt, and dry yeast into a large bowl. Mix well.
   **Note**: Ensure dry ingredients are thoroughly combined.
2. Add eggs and whisk. Gradually pour in cold water, stir into fluff, then knead into a non-sticky dough.
3. Transfer dough to the board. Use the "rubbing method" to knead for about 3 minutes until a thick membrane can be pulled.
4. Flatten the dough. Add 25g softened butter. Continue kneading for about 10 minutes until butter is fully absorbed, dough is smooth, and a thin, resilient windowpane membrane can be pulled.
   **Note**: Windowpane membrane is key for successful lamination; scrape dough stuck to the board with a scraper and continue kneading.
5. Shape the dough into a square. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and freeze for 30 minutes to cool and relax the gluten while inhibiting premature yeast fermentation.
6. Wrap 120g butter in plastic wrap. Roll into an evenly thick rectangular sheet. Freeze for 20 minutes to set.
7. Remove frozen dough. Sprinkle a little flour on the board. Roll dough into a rectangle about twice the size of the butter sheet. Place the frozen butter sheet in the center. Wrap it completely with the dough, pinching all seams tightly.
   **Note**: Wrapping must be tight to prevent butter from leaking out during rolling.
8. Roll the butter-wrapped dough into a large thin rectangle. Fold both sides toward the center like folding a blanket. Wrap with plastic wrap and freeze for 15 minutes.
9. Remove dough. Roll and fold again. Wrap and freeze for 15 minutes.
10. Remove dough again. Perform the third roll and fold. Three folds in total.
    **Note**: Freeze to relax after each fold to prevent butter from melting and gluten from tightening, ensuring clear layers without merging.
11. Roll the final folded dough into a large thin rectangle. Trim uneven edges. Cut into 5 equal-width long strips.
12. Place two chocolate chunks at one end of each strip. Roll up from the chocolate end, seam side down, to form a croissant shape.
13. Place the buns spaced apart on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Let ferment in a warm, humid environment for about 1 hour until doubled in size to 1.5 times the original volume.
    **Note**: Low-temperature fermentation is critical; high temperature will cause butter to melt and leak out.
14. Without preheating the oven, place the fermented buns inside. Set to top and bottom heat at 180°C. Bake for about 20 minutes until cooked through and surface is nicely colored.
15. Remove buns and cool completely on a wire rack.
16. Make chocolate sauce: Add milk and 80g chocolate chunks to a small saucepan. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly, until chocolate is fully melted and mixture is thick and smooth. Remove from heat.
17. Use a spoon to spread chocolate sauce on top of each bun. Finally, use a sieve to evenly sift a layer of cocoa powder on top.
    **Note**: Buns must be completely cooled before coating; otherwise, hot chocolate sauce will flow excessively, ruining the appearance.

## Health Advice
This dirty bun is delicious but high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with black coffee to cut the richness and control the portion. Suitable for occasional indulgence, but mindful consumption is advised.
