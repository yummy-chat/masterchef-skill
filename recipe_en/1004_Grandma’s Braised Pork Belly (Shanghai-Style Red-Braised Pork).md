# Grandma’s Braised Pork Belly (Shanghai-Style Red-Braised Pork)
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (with skin, distinct layers)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for blanching and stir-frying)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for blanching and stir-frying)
🍶 Yellow wine, appropriate amount (added once during blanching, once before braising)
🍺 Beer, appropriate amount (enhances flavor, can replace part of the water)
🍎 Apple cider vinegar, appropriate amount (to tenderize the meat)
🥫 Light soy sauce/soy sauce, appropriate amount (provides salty umami flavor and base color)
🥫 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring)
🍬 Rock sugar, proportion of about 0.8:1 relative to the amount of soy sauce (added after skimming oil, to brighten and sweeten)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok (stir-fry pan)
🥄 Wok spatula
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🥛 Measuring cup (for temporarily holding the broth, to facilitate oil skimming)
🔥 Stove
🥢 Slotted spoon/tongs

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the whole piece of pork belly in a pot with cold water. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and yellow wine. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then reduce to low heat and cook for 30 minutes until fully tender.
**Note**: Must be started in cold water to help draw out blood; the pork must be completely cooled before cutting into pieces.

2. Once cooled, cut the pork belly into uniform cubes of about 3 cm per side.

3. Heat the iron wok, add a small amount of oil, and sauté the scallion segments and ginger slices until fragrant. Add the pork cubes and stir-fry over low heat until the surface turns slightly golden and fat renders out.
**Key point**: Shanghai-style red-braised pork does not caramelize sugar; this step provides the coloration.

4. Pour apple cider vinegar along the side of the wok and stir-fry until the vinegar aroma dissipates. Add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce, stir-frying until the meat is colored and the soy sauce forms large bubbles. Then pour in yellow wine and beer, enough to submerge the meat.

5. Cover with a lid, reduce to low heat, and braise for 35 minutes until the meat is tender and flavorful.
6. After braising, remove the meat pieces. Pour the broth into a measuring cup and let it sit until the fat clearly separates into a top layer. Skim off the surface oil.
**Note**: This step is key to reducing greasiness.

7. Return the skimmed broth to the wok, add the rock sugar and meat pieces. Increase to high heat and quickly reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens and forms large bubbles, toss the wok a few times so the glaze evenly and glossy coats the meat. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Pork belly is high in fat. It is recommended to serve with green leafy vegetables to cut the richness and balance nutrition. Those with high blood lipids or on a weight-loss diet should control their portion size.
