# Sichuan Salted Steamed Pork Belly
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (with skin, alternating fat and lean)
🥬 Crushed mustard greens
🧅 Scallions, 2-3 stalks
🫚 Ginger, 1 small piece
🌶️ Pickled chilies, 3-5 pieces
🍬 Brown sugar, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Light soy sauce, 5 tablespoons
🧂 Donggu Yipinxian soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🍶 Huangjiu (rice wine), enough for blanching + 2 tablespoons for the sauce
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🍶 Fermented glutinous rice juice, enough for coating the skin

## Tools and Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing the sauce)
♨️ Steamer
🧻 Plastic wrap
🥣 Ceramic steaming bowl
🍽️ Ceramic plate

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the sauce**: Mix together 1 tablespoon brown sugar, 5 tablespoons light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons Donggu Yipinxian soy sauce, 2 tablespoons huangjiu, and 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce until well combined.
**Key point**: This sauce provides the base flavor of salty, savory, and slightly sweet.

2. **Prepare the mustard greens**: Rinse the crushed mustard greens under clean water to remove excess salt. Drain and dry-roast in a dry wok over medium heat until the moisture evaporates. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Dry-roasting removes raw and bitter flavors while enhancing the aroma.

3. **Blanch and boil**: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the whole piece of pork belly, scallions, ginger slices, and some huangjiu. Cook over medium heat until a chopstick can easily pierce through the meat.
**Note**: Starting with cold water helps remove the gamey smell and skims off blood foam.

4. **Color the skin**: Remove the pork belly. While still hot, evenly coat the skin side with fermented glutinous rice juice.
**Key point**: The sugar and fermentation agents in the juice create a bright, reddish color upon high-heat frying.

5. **Fry to set shape**: Heat enough oil in a wok until it reaches 60-70% hot. Place the pork belly skin-side down and immediately cover with a lid to prevent splatter. Fry until the skin turns deep red with wrinkles.
**Note**: Covering the lid is a safety measure. The wrinkled skin, or “tiger skin,” is key to a soft, tender texture.

6. **Slice and soak**: Let the fried meat cool, then cut into thick slices about 5-8 mm thick. Soak the slices in the prepared sauce to infuse flavor and color.
**Key point**: Soaking ensures even flavor and color distribution throughout the slices.

7. **Arrange in a bowl and steam**: Place the meat slices skin-side down neatly in a steaming bowl. Spread the dry-roasted mustard greens on top. Pour the leftover marinade over everything, then add pickled chilies, scallion sections, and ginger slices.
**Key point**: Placing skin-side down means the skin will face upward after unmolding. Seal the bowl tightly with plastic wrap.

8. **Steam**: Once the steamer produces steam, place the bowl inside. Start on high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and steam for about 90 minutes.
**Key point**: Long steaming allows fat to render and be absorbed by the mustard greens, resulting in meat that is fatty but not greasy and lean but not dry.

9. **Unmold and drizzle sauce**: After steaming, remove the plastic wrap and discard the scallions and ginger. Pour the accumulated juices into a wok and reduce over high heat. Invert the steaming bowl onto a plate, lift the bowl, then drizzle the reduced sauce over the top.
**Key point**: The final drizzle enhances the shine and richness of the dish.

## Health Advice
This salted steamed pork belly is rich in high-quality protein and collagen, but also high in fat. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables. It is suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or for winter nourishment. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of seasonings. The 90-minute steaming process makes the pork belly fatty but not greasy.
