# Crispy Sugar Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 All-purpose flour 200g (for scalded dough)
🍞 All-purpose flour 15g (for oil paste)
🌾 Cooked flour appropriate amount (about 75g, for filling, must be dry-fried over low heat in advance and sifted)
💧 Water 340g (for scalded dough)
💧 Water 20g (for oil paste)
🐖 Lard 15g
🍬 White sugar 20g (added to water for scalded dough)
🍬 White sugar or powdered sugar about 10g per piece (for runny filling)
🍬 White sugar 60g (for non-runny filling, mix with cooked flour at a 4:1 ratio)
🍬 Brown sugar 50g
🌰 White sesame seeds 25g
🥜 Crushed peanuts 25g
🫒 Rapeseed oil 50g (for filling)
🫒 Frying oil appropriate amount (rapeseed oil or vegetable oil)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Dough scraper
🫙 Plastic wrap
🥄 Spatula/stirring spoon
🧁 Cup-shaped mold (to assist in pressing dough)
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🌡️ Infrared thermometer

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour 340g of water into a wok, add 20g of white sugar, and bring to a boil over high heat until the sugar dissolves, then reduce to low heat. Add 200g of all-purpose flour in three batches, stirring quickly and continuously each time until a uniform batter forms without dry flour. Turn off the heat, cover, and let it rest for 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Stirring must be quick and continuous to ensure the flour is fully scalded and to avoid large lumps.
2. Turn out the scalded dough and quickly cut it into small pieces with a dough scraper to allow it to cool.
   **Note**: Thorough cooling prevents the dough from sticking to your hands during subsequent steps and results in a better texture.
3. Prepare the oil paste: In a bowl, mix 15g all-purpose flour, 15g lard, and 20g water, stirring into a smooth paste.
4. Choose one or more fillings based on preference:
   - **Runny white sugar filling**: Use white sugar or powdered sugar directly.
   - **Non-runny white sugar filling**: Mix white sugar and cooked flour at a ratio of about 4:1 until uniform.
   - **Brown sugar filling**: Mix 50g brown sugar, 50g white sugar, 25g white sesame seeds, 25g crushed peanuts, 75g cooked flour, and 50g rapeseed oil, stirring until it can be formed into a ball.
   **Note**: The cooked flour must be dry-fried over low heat until slightly yellow and then sifted.
5. Roughly knead the cooled dough pieces together, add the oil paste, and continue kneading and pressing into a ball. Wrap the dough completely in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 1 hour.
   **State**: The rested dough should feel slightly sticky to the touch and have a slight puffiness. The longer the resting time, the crispier and fluffier the final product.
6. Divide the rested dough into portions of about 50g each and roll them into balls. Dip your right thumb in a little cooking oil, press it into the center of each ball while rotating, forming a bowl-like hollow. Fill with about 10g of filling, slowly gather the edges using the tiger's mouth technique, pinch off any excess dough, and seal the opening tightly, smoothing it out.
   **Note**: Dip your fingers in oil to prevent sticking; do not overfill to avoid bursting during frying; ensure the seal is tight.
7. Press the filled dough into a flat round cake shape, and use your fingers to press a few ridges on the smooth side. You can also prick a few small holes on both sides with a toothpick.
8. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 160°C, then reduce to low heat. Place the cakes ridge-side down, gently sliding them in along the edge of the wok, and immediately stir gently with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Maintain an oil temperature between 150-160°C, frying until the cakes float and set.
   **Note**: Do not turn the cakes immediately after placing them in the oil; wait until they are slightly set before gently moving them.
9. Once the cakes float, increase the heat to high and raise the oil temperature to 180°C, while constantly flipping the cakes with chopsticks until both sides are golden brown and puffed up.
10. Remove the fried cakes with a slotted spoon, drain off excess oil at the edge of the wok or on a wire rack, and enjoy while hot.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in sugar and oil, suitable for occasional indulgence and not recommended as a breakfast item. People with diabetes or those on a weight loss regimen should exercise restraint. After consumption, it is advisable to increase physical activity to offset the calories.
