# Pepper and Radish Braised Beef Brisket Soup  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the whole beef brisket in a pot with cold water, blanch until blood foam rises, then remove and rinse the surface thoroughly with hot water.  
2. Put white peppercorns in a clean wok, dry-fry over medium-low heat until the grains jump in the pot, remove and let cool completely, then crush them. Place the crushed peppercorns, star anise, and cumin seeds into a soup bag, and tie the bag tightly.  
3. Peel the white radish and cut into large rolling chunks; lightly smash the garlic cloves and cut the onion into thick rings; set aside.  
4. Pour oil into the wok, add the garlic cloves and onion rings, fry over medium-low heat until the surface turns slightly yellow and fragrant, then remove and set aside.  
5. Put the blanched beef brisket into a clay pot, add cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then add the fried garlic and onion, drizzle in the yellow wine, reduce to medium-low heat, and simmer until the beef brisket can be easily pierced with chopsticks. Remove the beef brisket. Discard the cooked onion, garlic, and other solid residue from the pot.  
6. Let the cooked beef brisket cool slightly, then cut it into large, bite-sized pieces.  
7. Return the cut beef brisket pieces and raw white radish chunks to the beef brisket clear broth, and also put the spice bag back into the broth.  
8. Add salt, fish sauce, and one small piece of rock sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover the pot, and simmer for about half an hour until the radish is completely tender and infused with flavor.  
9. Turn off the heat, serve, and garnish with a few goji berries for decoration.  

## Health Advice  
This dish features tender beef brisket and radish fully soaked in the broth, rich in protein and low in fat, ideal for warming the body and replenishing energy in winter. It is recommended that individuals with high uric acid levels consume the broth in small amounts and pair it with vegetables to balance nutrition.
