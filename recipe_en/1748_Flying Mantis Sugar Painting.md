# Flying Mantis Sugar Painting
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍬 White granulated sugar, as needed (core ingredient, boiled into syrup)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Pot (for boiling sugar)  
🥄 Metal spoon (for scooping syrup and drawing)  
🪟 Square glass plate (greased and used as a drawing base)  
🔪 Kitchen knife (for lifting the set sugar painting)  
🥢 Bamboo skewer (attached to the bottom as a handle)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Evenly spread a layer of oil on the surface of the square glass plate, ensuring complete coverage to prevent the syrup from sticking after it sets.
2. Heat the pot empty until hot, then add the white granulated sugar. Heat until it is completely melted and reaches an amber-colored, flowing syrup state.  
   **Note**: Control the heat while boiling the sugar to avoid burning, but the specific heat level and duration should be adjusted based on actual conditions.
3. Use a metal spoon to scoop up an appropriate amount of syrup. Draw the mantis shape smoothly on the greased glass plate, outlining details such as the head and body curves, eyes, limbs, and wings in sequence to ensure a vivid design.
4. Let it sit until the syrup has completely set into an amber-colored, translucent solid.
5. Carefully lift the mantis sugar painting from the glass plate with a kitchen knife, using a gentle touch to avoid breakage.
6. Attach a bamboo skewer to the bottom of the sugar painting as a handle. It is now complete.

## Health Advice  
This Flying Mantis sugar painting is made purely from sugar, with a concentrated calorie content but no fat. It is suitable for occasional consumption as a decorative dessert in small amounts. Pay attention to blood sugar levels and dental health. When making it, focus on controlling the heat to avoid burning the syrup, which would affect the taste.
