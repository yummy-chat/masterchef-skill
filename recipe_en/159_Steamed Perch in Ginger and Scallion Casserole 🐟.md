# Steamed Perch in Ginger and Scallion Casserole 🐟

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 1 perch (cleaned and cut into pieces)  
🧅 About half an onion (cut into thick slices)  
🧄 1 clove garlic (minced)  
🧈 A small piece of butter  
🫚 A suitable amount of ginger (cut into thick slices)  
🧅 A suitable amount of scallions (cut into white and green sections, kept separate)  
🥚 1 egg (for marinating)  
🧂 A suitable amount of salt (for marinating and seasoning)  
🍬 A suitable amount of sugar (for marinating and seasoning)  
🍶 A splash of huadiao wine (for marinating)  
🌾 A suitable amount of starch (for coating)  
🦪 A splash of oyster sauce (for seasoning)  
🍶 A suitable amount of rice wine (for adding aroma)  
🥄 A few drops of soy sauce (drizzle before serving)  
💧 A little cornstarch slurry (for a light glaze)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Large chef's knife  
🪵 Thick wooden cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl or stainless steel basin  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍲 Clay pot  

## Directions
1. Cut off the head of the cleaned perch, split it in half, then cut into long strip pieces.  
**Note**: Perch has few bones, making it suitable for the elderly and children.

2. Slice the ginger thickly; cut the scallions into long sections, separating the white and green parts; cut the onion into thick slices; mince the garlic.
3. Add a suitable amount of salt, sugar, a splash of huadiao wine, one egg, and a suitable amount of starch to the fish pieces. Mix well and marinate for a while.  
**Key point**: Coating with starch helps lock in the moisture of the fish, making the texture tender.

4. Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok to 180°C. Fry the fish pieces until the surface is golden and crispy, then remove and drain the oil.  
**Note**: High oil temperature ensures quick setting. Do not stir vigorously before the surface sets to avoid breaking the fish. To save oil, you can pan-fry instead.

5. Leave a little oil in the wok. First, stir-fry the ginger slices until the edges curl and the aroma is strong. Then add the minced garlic and butter, stir-frying until fragrant.  
**Key point**: The ginger slices must be stir-fried sufficiently; otherwise, the final dish will lack flavor.

6. Add an appropriate amount of water. Season with salt, sugar, a splash of oyster sauce, and rice wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then drizzle in a little cornstarch slurry to create a light glaze.  
**Key point**: The sauce must be prepared in advance before adding the fish to prevent overcooking and breaking the fish pieces.

7. Add the white parts of the scallions and the fried fish pieces. Quickly and gently toss to coat the fish evenly with the sauce.  
**Note**: Be gentle to avoid breaking the fish pieces.

8. Line the bottom of a preheated clay pot with onion slices. Pour in the stir-fried fish pieces, drizzle with a few drops of soy sauce, and toss to combine. Serve immediately.  
**Key point**: Keeping the clay pot heated draws out the onion aroma and retains warmth.

## Health Suggestions
Perch is low in fat and high in protein. Prepared with ginger and scallion in a casserole, it helps warm the spleen and stomach, making it suitable for the elderly and children for nutritional supplementation. Frying the fish at a high oil temperature for quick surface setting minimizes oil absorption, so portion control is advised.
