# Handmade Beef Meatballs
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lean beef  About 500g (after removing sinew)
🧊 Ice cubes  150g
💧 Ice water  100ml
🌾 Sweet potato starch  2 tbsp
🧂 Table salt  2 tbsp
🧪 Baking soda  A pinch
🧄 Onion, ginger, and garlic powder  2 tbsp (can substitute with fresh onion, ginger, and garlic)
🧄 Garlic oil  1 tbsp
🥜 Satay sauce  1 tbsp
🥓 Small amount of pork fat or beef fat  Optional (adds juiciness)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥩 Meat grinder
🍲 Large pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Spoon
🍶 Measuring cup

## Preparation Steps
1.  Remove the white sinew from the beef and cut it into small pieces suitable for the meat grinder.
2.  Place ice cubes, ice water, sweet potato starch, and onion, ginger, and garlic powder in a measuring cup. Stir well and set aside.
3.  Put the beef pieces, salt, and baking soda into the meat grinder. Pour in the prepared ice and starch water. Use an intermittent grinding method, meaning "grind for a while, stop for a while," to prevent the machine from overheating. If the machine stops, refrigerate the meat along with the container to cool it down, then continue. Grind until the meat paste is extremely fine, sticky, and firm.
**Key Point**: Keep everything cold throughout the process to avoid the meat temperature rising and affecting the elasticity.
4.  Add garlic oil and satay sauce to the ground meat paste. If using pork fat or beef fat, add it as well. Mix thoroughly with a spoon.
5.  Add water to a pot and heat to about 50–60°C. Turn to the lowest heat and keep the water from boiling. Use your hand to squeeze smooth meatballs from the gap between your thumb and index finger, and use a spoon to scoop them off and place them into the pot.
**Key Point**: Maintain a "broth nurturing" state throughout. The water temperature must not come to a boil. The meatballs need to set at a low temperature.
6.  Cook until all the meatballs float to the surface. Press them gently with your hand; when they feel springy, remove them immediately.
7.  Bring the original broth (used to cook the meatballs) back to a boil. You can blanch ingredients like pork fat, pork slices, pork heart slices, or pork liver slices in sequence. Then, briefly blanch celery bits, ginger shreds, scallions, and leafy greens. Season with salt and white pepper powder. Drizzle a spoonful of garlic oil to enhance the aroma. Serve with the beef meatballs and rice noodles.

## Health Advice
These beef meatballs are high in protein and low in fat, making them suitable for fitness, muscle building, or weight loss diets. However, note that baking soda tenderizes the meat but also slightly increases the sodium content. People with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt.
