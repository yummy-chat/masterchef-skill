# Double Chocolate Pull-Apart Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
🍫 Cocoa powder 5g  
🍬 Caster sugar 30g  
💧 Water 130g (for dough) + 7g (for dissolving yeast)  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 27g  
🍞 Dry yeast 3g (needs to be dissolved in 7g water)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 20g (added after softening)  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍫 Dark chocolate 75g (for filling) + 25g (for dough)  
🥛 Whipping cream 75g  
🌰 Crushed almonds, as needed (for decoration)  
🧂 Icing sugar, as needed (for decoration after baking)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔥 Gas stove/Electric ceramic stove  
🍳 Snow pot (for double boiling)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Glass bowl (for double boiling chocolate and cream)  
🥣 Wooden bowl (for mixing dry ingredients)  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🧊 Plastic wrap  
🍞 450g loaf pan  
🔥 Oven (with proofing function)  
🧊 Cooling rack  
💦 Spray bottle  
🔍 Sieve  

## Instructions
**Making the Chocolate Filling**:
1. Place 75g dark chocolate and 75g whipping cream together in a glass bowl. Boil water in a pot, then remove from heat. Place the bowl over the hot water to double boil, stirring continuously until completely melted and smooth.
   **Key Point**: Remove the water from heat before double boiling to avoid the chocolate separating from the oil due to sustained high heat.
2. Set aside 25g of the chocolate mixture for the dough.
3. Cover the remaining chocolate mixture with plastic wrap directly on the surface, and refrigerate until it thickens to a spreadable, non-runny consistency.

**Making the Dough**:
1. In a wooden bowl, mix the bread flour, cocoa powder, and caster sugar evenly.
2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients, then pour in 130g water, the whole egg liquid, and the reserved 25g chocolate mixture.
   **Key Point**: Making a well prevents the liquid from spilling to the edges of the bowl, making it easier to mix.
3. Use a spatula to combine the ingredients until no dry flour remains and the mixture is shaggy. Then knead by hand into a rough, dry-flour-free dough.
4. Lightly coat the dough surface with a little water or oil to prevent a skin from forming. Cover with a damp cloth and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
5. Remove the chilled dough. Dissolve the dry yeast in 7g water to create a yeast liquid. Spread the dough flat, evenly apply the yeast liquid, and repeatedly knead and fold until fully absorbed.
6. Slap the dough on the counter for 3-5 minutes until the surface is smooth and soft, and you can pull a thick, extensible membrane that tears easily.
7. Spread the dough flat, sprinkle with salt, and add the softened butter. Wrap the butter with the dough, then knead, squeeze, and tear until fully incorporated. The dough will become wet, sticky, and messy at this stage, which is normal; be patient and continue kneading until it becomes smooth again.
8. Slap the dough on the counter again for 3-5 minutes until the surface is very smooth and you can pull a thin, strong windowpane membrane with smooth edges when torn.
9. Round the dough into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let it proof at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until noticeably increased in volume.

**Shaping & Baking**:
1. Roll the proofed dough into a rectangle about 40cm x 20cm. Flip it over so the smooth side is facing down. Spread the chilled chocolate filling evenly, leaving a small border on both sides.
2. Roll it up from the top, fairly tightly, and pinch the seam to seal. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 10 minutes to firm up the dough, making it easier to cut.
3. Remove the dough log, dust the surface with a little flour, and roll it out to a length of about 50cm. Using a dough scraper, cut it lengthwise down the middle, leaving the top end uncut to form two strands.
4. With the cut sides facing up, braid the two strands together by crossing them over each other. Pinch the bottom end to seal.
5. Place the braided dough, cut side down, into the loaf pan. Let it proof in a warm, humid place until it reaches about 80-85% of the pan's height.
6. Spray the surface of the proofed dough with water and sprinkle with crushed almonds. Preheat the oven and bake at 150°C (top heat) / 210°C (bottom heat) for 35 minutes. This temperature is suitable for a mountain-shaped loaf.
7. Immediately after baking, gently tap the pan to release steam, and turn the loaf out onto a cooling rack. After cooling for about 10 minutes, dust the top with icing sugar.
