# Pickled Cabbage and Stinky Tofu
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Firm tofu 850g (cut into dice-sized pieces)  
🥬 Cabbage (green cabbage) 500g (cut into thick shreds)  
🧄 Garlic 6 cloves (for the pickled cabbage, crushed)  
🧄 Garlic 4 cloves (for the sauce, crushed)  
🧀 Stinky tofu 2 pieces (use only the tofu blocks, discard the liquid)  
🧂 Salt approx. 8g (2 level teaspoons and a half)  
🍬 White sugar approx. 90g (9 tablespoons)  
🍶 White vinegar approx. 130g (13 tablespoons)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon approx. 3g (1 teaspoon)  
🤎 Light soy sauce approx. 30g (3 tablespoons)  
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 30g (for oil pouring) + 10g (for the sauce)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 10g (for oil pouring) + 10g (for the sauce)  
🫘 White sesame seeds 10g (for oil pouring) + 10g (for the sauce)  
🌿 Cumin powder 5g (half a tablespoon)  
🧂 Barbecue seasoning 20g (2 tablespoons)  
🥜 Sesame paste 15g (1 tablespoon)  
💧 Water approx. 20g (2-3 tablespoons)  
🥜 Cooking oil as needed (for pouring oil and spraying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Air fryer  
🍳 Small pot (with a long handle, for heating oil)  
🫗 Oil spray bottle  
🥣 Large glass bowl (for mixing sauce and tofu)  
🪣 Stainless steel square basin (for mixing the pickled cabbage)  
🔪 Chef's knife + 🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🥄 Metal spoon (for measuring seasonings)  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥢 Chopsticks  
📦 Glass storage container (for storing the pickled cabbage)  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the firm tofu into evenly sized dice-shaped pieces. Place them in the air fryer basket and spray a uniform layer of oil over the surface using the oil spray bottle.
**Key Point**: The tofu pieces should be uniform in size, and the oil should be sprayed evenly to ensure consistent heating.

2. Cut the cabbage into thick shreds and place them in the stainless steel square basin. Add salt, white sugar, white vinegar, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, and the 6 crushed garlic cloves. Wearing gloves, mix and knead until the cabbage shreds become soft and release water.
**Key Point**: Cut the cabbage into thick shreds to maintain a crunchy texture. The seasoning ratio is intensely sour and sweet; knead thoroughly to infuse the flavors.

3. In a bowl, mix together the coarse chili flakes, fine chili powder, and white sesame seeds. Heat oil in a small pot. After turning off the heat, wait until the smoke changes from thick to thin, then pour the hot oil over the chili mixture, stirring continuously while pouring.
**Key Point**: Pour the oil after turning off the heat and when the smoke has thinned; the oil temperature should be hot enough to release the aroma of the chilies without burning them.

4. Immediately pour the prepared chili oil into the bowl of pickled cabbage while it is still hot. Mix again evenly. The pickled cabbage is now ready.
**Key Point**: Pour the chili oil while it is still hot so the fragrance permeates the pickled cabbage fully.

5. Preheat the air fryer to 180°C. Place the tofu inside and fry for 15 minutes. Remove and use chopsticks to turn each piece of tofu. Spray oil evenly again, then return to the air fryer and continue frying at 180°C for another 15 minutes.
**Key Point**: Turning the pieces and adding more oil halfway through is a necessary step for achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.

6. In a large glass bowl, combine the white sesame seeds, cumin powder, coarse chili flakes, fine chili powder, barbecue seasoning, and the 4 crushed garlic cloves designated for the sauce. Pour a small amount of hot oil over the mixture to release the aroma, then stir well.
**Key Point**: Use only a small amount of hot oil, just enough to release the fragrance.

7. Add the sesame paste and water, stirring into a paste. Then add the two pieces of stinky tofu, crush them, and mix thoroughly to form the core sauce.
**Key Point**: The liquid from the stinky tofu is very salty and must be discarded. The sesame paste needs to be diluted with water to create a smooth coating sauce.

8. Pour the fried tofu pieces into the bowl with the sauce. Wearing gloves, toss to coat each piece of tofu evenly with the sauce.
**Key Point**: Ensure every piece of tofu is uniformly coated with the sauce.

9. Add an appropriate amount of the pickled cabbage to the tofu, then toss again to combine evenly, allowing the sweet and sour juice from the pickled cabbage to blend with the sauce. The final mixture should have a good, balanced consistency – not too wet, not too dry.
**Key Point**: Mix in stages: first coat the tofu with the sauce, then mix in the pickled cabbage. This prevents the dish from becoming watery and ensures the flavor is rich and concentrated.

## Health Advice
This dish of pickled cabbage and stinky tofu is high in salt and sugar. It is advisable to pair it with unsweetened tea or plain water. People with high blood pressure should only consume it in small amounts. Using an air fryer is healthier than traditional deep-frying, but attention should still be paid to controlling the amount of oil used.
