# Korean-Style Spicy Sauce Crab
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦀️ Blue Crab  
🧅 Onion  
🧄 Garlic  
🌶️ Chili Pepper  
🫑 Green Chili Pepper  
🧴 Korean Soy Sauce  
🫚 Ginger  
🍶 Rice Wine  
🧂 MSG  
⚫ Ground Black Pepper  
⚪ Toasted Sesame Seeds  
🍯 Corn Syrup (White Malt Syrup)  
🌶️ Fine Chili Powder  
🌶️ Coarse Chili Powder  
🍬 Sugar  
🫗 Sesame Oil  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
🔥 Stove  
🥄 Strainer or Skimmer  
🔪 Knife, Cutting Board  
✂️ Scissors  
🪥 Toothbrush  
🧤 Disposable Gloves  
🥄 Spoon  

## Steps
1.  Thoroughly scrub the live blue crabs with a toothbrush under running water. Lift the carapace, remove the gills and mouth. Use the back of a knife or scissors to crack the hard shell at the base of the legs, and cut the body in half. The roe or tomalley can be scooped out and set aside.
   **Key Point**: This dish is raw marinated; the crabs must be extremely fresh, and hygiene during handling is critical. Cracking the hard shell helps with flavor absorption.
2.  Place the prepared crab body pieces, legs, and roe into a large bowl.
3.  Cut the onion into chunks, slice some of the garlic, cut the chili pepper into segments, and slice the green chili pepper. Set aside.
4.  Pour the soy sauce into a pot, add ginger slices and garlic slices. Simmer over low heat, then turn off the heat. Use a strainer to remove and discard the ginger and garlic. Allow the soy sauce to cool completely to room temperature.
   **Key Point**: The soy sauce needs to be simmered slowly to extract flavor and must be completely cooled before further mixing to avoid affecting the taste and texture of other seasonings.
5.  In a clean large bowl, pour in the cooled cooked soy sauce. Add rice wine, minced garlic, MSG, ground black pepper, toasted sesame seeds, and corn syrup. Stir to combine.
6.  Gradually add the fine chili powder and coarse chili powder, stirring continuously, until a uniform thick paste forms. Add sugar and sesame oil, mixing thoroughly until the sugar is completely dissolved and the sauce is bright red and sticky.
   **Key Point**: Adding the chili powder in batches makes it easier to control the color and spiciness. The amount of sugar is relatively high; adjust to personal taste as needed.
7.  Pour the prepared spicy sauce into the bowl with the crabs. Add the onion chunks, garlic slices, and chili peppers. Wearing gloves, gently but thoroughly toss to coat each piece of crab evenly with the sauce. Finally, add the green chili pepper slices and mix lightly.
   **Key Point**: Be careful of sharp crab shells while mixing.
8.  Place the mixed crab and sauce into an airtight container. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 3 to 4 hours before serving.
   **Key Point**: Do not marinate for too long, or the crabs will lose too much moisture and the texture will be affected. This is raw food; it is recommended to consume promptly after marinating and not to store overnight.

## Health Advice
Sea crab is rich in high-quality protein and zinc. The fermented components in Korean chili paste aid digestion, making this dish suitable for stimulating appetite when needed. Note that this dish is high in sugar and sodium; those with hypertension or monitoring sugar intake should consume in moderation. Always use the freshest crab for raw preparations.
