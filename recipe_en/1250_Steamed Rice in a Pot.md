# Steamed Rice in a Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Northern Rice  
💧 Water  
🧂 None  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Thick-bottomed Pot  
🔒 Tight-fitting Lid  

## Steps
1. Rinse the rice with clean water 2–3 times until the water runs clear, then drain.
   **Key Point**: Rub the rice gently while rinsing to avoid breaking the grains; do not rinse too many times to prevent nutrient loss.
2. Place the rinsed rice and water into the thick-bottomed pot at a ratio of 1∶2.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring the bottom gently with a spatula to prevent the rice from sticking.
   **Note**: Stir while boiling, but do so gently to avoid the rice water spilling over.
4. Quickly cover with the tight-fitting lid, reduce the heat to low, and continue cooking for 10–15 minutes. Do not open the lid during this time.
   **Key Point**: Steam circulates inside the pot during low-heat cooking; opening the lid will cause a sudden temperature drop, affecting the texture of the rice.
5. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes to allow the rice to cook evenly.
   **Key Point**: Do not skip the resting time, as it allows excess moisture to be fully absorbed by the rice grains, resulting in a better texture.

## Health Advice  
Rice is a high-quality source of carbohydrates, suitable for people who need a quick energy boost. Paying attention to the rice-to-water ratio and heat control when steaming rice in a pot can prevent undercooked or burnt rice, making it easier to digest for the elderly and children.
