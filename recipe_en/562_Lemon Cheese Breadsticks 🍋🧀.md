# Lemon Cheese Breadsticks 🍋🧀  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year-round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥖 Bread flour 520g  
🥛 Milk 374g  
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (yolks only, to make the bread soft)  
🍬 Caster sugar 173g (62g for dough, 111g for filling)  
🍄 Instant dry yeast (high-sugar tolerant) 5g  
🧂 Salt 8g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 52g (softened in advance)  
🧀 Cream cheese 450g (softened over hot water)  
🍶 Whipping cream 192g  
🍋 Lemon 1 (use zest from half a lemon and 12g juice)  
🥥 Desiccated coconut as needed (choose fine desiccated coconut for decoration)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Mixing bowl (large)  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🌡️ Proofing box (or a warm environment, 28°C / 38°C, 75% humidity)  
🍳 Baking tray  
🔥 Oven (home convection or fan-forced oven)  
🌀 Electric hand mixer  
🧊 Cooling rack (wire rack)  
🔪 Bread knife  

## Instructions  
1. Mix bread flour, caster sugar, and instant dry yeast evenly. Make a well on the work surface, add milk and egg yolks. Gradually push the dry flour into the liquid with a dough scraper and mix until a dough forms.  
   **Key point**: A rough dough at the start is normal; using only egg yolks makes the bread softer.  
2. Let the dough rest for about 25 minutes. Ensure the ingredients are thoroughly combined before resting. Resting improves gluten extensibility, making it easier to develop a thin membrane.  
3. After resting, knead the dough by slapping, folding, and cutting. Spray a little water to prevent the surface from drying out. Knead until the surface is smooth and you can pull a thicker but easily torn membrane.  
4. Cut open the dough and flatten it. Wrap in softened unsalted butter and salt. Continue kneading by cutting, slapping, and folding.  
   **Note**: When butter is first added, the dough becomes greasy and loose; use more cutting motions to speed up incorporation.  
5. Continue kneading until the butter is fully absorbed, the dough surface becomes smooth again, and you can pull a large, thin, translucent, and tear-resistant membrane.  
   **Note**: If you cannot achieve the ideal state in one go, let the dough rest for 5–10 minutes before resuming kneading.  
6. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in the proofing box, and let it ferment at around 28°C for about 30 minutes, until it has visibly increased in size.  
7. Remove the dough, gently press to release gas, divide it evenly into about 18 pieces, and roll each into a ball. Work quickly to avoid uneven fermentation among the pieces.  
8. Flatten each small dough ball directly with your hand, shape it into a short thick log about 11 cm long, and arrange them evenly on the baking tray.  
9. Place in the proofing box for final fermentation, about 50 minutes, until the dough expands to 1.5–2 times its original size. Dip your finger in flour and gently press the dough; if the indentation does not spring back immediately, fermentation is complete.  
10. Preheat the oven in advance. For a home convection oven: top heat 160°C, bottom heat 190°C, bake for about 20 minutes; for a HiBake T60S fan-forced oven: 165°C, bake for about 15 minutes. It is recommended not to brush with egg wash for a thinner crust after baking. Remove from the baking tray immediately after baking and cool completely on a wire rack. It is best to leave it overnight before proceeding.  
11. Prepare the lemon cheese filling: Soften cream cheese, whipping cream, and caster sugar over hot water. Beat with an electric hand mixer until silky. Add lemon zest and lemon juice, continue beating until smooth, glossy, and paste-like.  
12. Assembly: Cut a slit lengthwise down the middle of each cooled breadstick. Fill generously with lemon cheese filling. Lightly spread a thin layer of filling around the breadstick, then roll it in desiccated coconut to coat evenly. Wrap the bottom with a semicircle of baking paper and store in an airtight container.  

## Health Advice  
These lemon cheese breadsticks are rich in protein and calcium, making them suitable for breakfast or afternoon tea. However, they are high in sugar and fat, so moderate consumption is recommended. Pair with black coffee or green tea to cut through the richness.
