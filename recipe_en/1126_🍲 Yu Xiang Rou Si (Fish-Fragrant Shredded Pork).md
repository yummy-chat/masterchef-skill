# 🍲 Yu Xiang Rou Si (Fish-Fragrant Shredded Pork)
Cuisine: 🍛 Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: 🌍 All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin 200g  
🥕 Carrot 100g  
🫑 Green bell pepper 100g  
🍄 Dried wood ear mushroom 5g  

🧂 Light soy sauce 10ml  
🍶 Cooking wine 5ml  
🥚 Egg white 1  
🌾 Cornstarch 10g  
🍶 Vinegar 15ml  
🍬 White sugar 10g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🌶️ Doubanjiang (Chili bean paste) 15g  
💧 Water 40ml (shared for marinade and sauce)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or pan  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce)  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  

## Instructions
1. Prepare the marinade: Mix 5ml light soy sauce, 5ml cooking wine, 5g cornstarch, 20ml water, and 1 egg white. Stir well.
2. Prepare the sauce: Mix 5ml light soy sauce, 15ml vinegar, 10g white sugar, 1g salt, 5g cornstarch, and 20ml water. Stir well.
3. Marinate the pork tenderloin with the marinade for 15–30 minutes. Mix well to ensure the meat is fully coated.
4. Soak the dried wood ear mushrooms in water for 4 hours in advance. Wash and cut into small pieces.
5. Wash the green bell pepper, remove the stem, and shred into strips. Wash and shred the carrot, blanch quickly in boiling water, then set aside.
6. Finely mince the ginger and garlic. Cut the green onion into 5mm segments.
7. Heat the wok, add 15ml oil. Add the marinated pork, quickly stir-fry and separate the pieces until the meat turns white. Remove and set aside.
8. Reheat the wok, add 5ml oil. Add all the green onion, ginger, garlic, and doubanjiang. Stir-fry until fragrant.
9. Add the shredded carrot and stir-fry for 20 seconds. Then add the green bell pepper and wood ear mushrooms. Continue stir-frying for 2 minutes.
10. Add the cooked pork back to the wok. Stir-fry quickly to combine, ensuring it does not exceed 20 seconds.
11. Pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly to coat all ingredients evenly, ensuring it does not exceed 15 seconds.
12. Turn off the heat. Transfer to a plate and serve.

## Health Tips
This dish uses pork tenderloin to provide high-quality protein, while carrot and wood ear mushrooms add dietary fiber. However, the sugar and doubanjiang are high in sodium, so it is advisable to control the amount of rice served. Suitable for those needing to supplement iron or build muscle.
