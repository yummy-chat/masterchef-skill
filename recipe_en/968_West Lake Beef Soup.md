# West Lake Beef Soup  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef, 4 liang (finely minced)  
🍄 Straw mushrooms, to taste (cut into small pieces similar in size to the beef)  
🥚 Egg whites, to taste (beaten)  
🌿 Cilantro/Chinese celery, to taste (finely chopped)  
💧 Water, 2.5 jin (can substitute with chicken broth)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for marinating and seasoning)  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch  
🌾 Cornstarch (bean starch), a pinch (for marinating and thickening)  
🫒 Cooking oil, a pinch (for coating the meat and stir-frying)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon/chicken powder, a pinch (to enhance the broth flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok (non-stick pan)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔥 Stove  

## Instructions  
1. Finely mince the beef. Add a pinch of salt, sugar, white pepper, and cornstarch, and mix well. Finally, drizzle a little cooking oil over the surface to seal it, and let it rest for 15 minutes.  
**Key**: The oil seal prevents oxidation and locks in moisture, making the beef more tender; resting allows the muscle fibers to relax and absorb moisture.  

2. Cut the straw mushrooms into small pieces; beat the egg whites; finely chop the cilantro or Chinese celery and set aside.  
3. Heat the wok over high heat, add a little cooking oil, stir-fry the straw mushroom pieces, then add the marinated beef and stir-fry together until the beef is about 40-50% cooked, then stop.  
**Key**: Stir-fry until half-cooked to release the meat's aroma; it does not need to be fully cooked, and no wine is needed.  

4. Pour the water into the wok and bring to a boil over high heat.  
5. After the soup boils, add a pinch of chicken bouillon and salt to season, skim off any foam, and cook for about 2 minutes before reducing the heat to low.  
**Key**: Reducing to low heat keeps the surface calm for the subsequent thickening, ensuring a smooth slurry.  

6. Gradually drizzle in the cornstarch slurry while gently stirring the soup with a ladle until it thickens.  
**Key**: Be meticulous to avoid lumps in the starch.  

7. Slowly drizzle the beaten egg whites into the gently simmering soup, and gently stir to form white egg flower patterns.  
**Key**: The heat should not be too high, to avoid scattering the egg whites and ruining the "West Lake" aesthetic.  

8. Sprinkle the chopped cilantro/Chinese celery on top for garnish, then turn off the heat and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Beef and straw mushrooms provide high-quality protein and dietary fiber, while the egg flower makes the soup smooth and easy to digest, making it suitable for those with weak digestive systems or those looking to build muscle. Control the amount of cornstarch used for thickening to avoid excessive calories.
