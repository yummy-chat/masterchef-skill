# Hunan Chopped Chili Sauce
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌶️ 500g green chili peppers  
🌶️ 500g beauty peppers  
🌶️ 500g bird's eye chilies  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🫚 Ginger, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🧂 50g salt  
🍗 4 teaspoons chicken bouillon powder  
🍬 15g sugar  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), as needed (for aroma and preservation)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🌀 Blender or food processor  
🍳 Wok or pan  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🫙 Airtight glass jar  
📦 Plastic wrap (as backup)  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the chilies under clean water and place them in a well-ventilated area to fully air-dry the surface moisture.  
**Key Point**: Moisture must be completely dried, or the finished product may spoil and turn sour.  

2. Finely mince the scallions, ginger, and garlic by hand, or pulse them in a blender until fine, ensuring a delicate texture for maximum aroma.  
3. Cut the dried chilies into small sections, then pulse them briefly in a blender, retaining some coarse texture.  
**Note**: Do not over-process; a coarse texture enhances the crunchy mouthfeel of the finished product.  

4. Place the processed chilies in a large bowl. Add 50g salt, 4 teaspoons chicken bouillon powder, 15g sugar, and a suitable amount of baijiu. Mix thoroughly.  
**Key Point**: Those sensitive to spiciness may wear gloves during handling. Use baijiu sparingly, mainly to enhance aroma and prevent spoilage.  

5. Heat oil in a wok or pan. When it reaches 50% heat, add the minced scallions, ginger, and garlic. Fry over low heat until fragrant, then turn off the heat.  
**Note**: Control the oil temperature at 50% heat, fry slowly over low heat throughout, and stop once the scallions, ginger, and garlic release their aroma.  

6. Allow the fried scallion-ginger-garlic oil to cool completely before pouring it into the seasoned chili mixture. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks to combine.  
7. Transfer the chopped chili sauce into a clean, dry, and oil-free airtight jar. Seal the lid tightly and store in a cool place or refrigerate.  
**Suggestion**: Consume within 1–2 months for the best flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This chopped chili sauce, made with three types of chilies, is rich in vitamin C and capsaicin. It can stimulate appetite and accelerate metabolism, making it suitable for those with cold constitution or poor appetite. However, it should be used with caution by individuals with gastric ulcers or hemorrhoids. During preparation, ensure moisture is fully dried to prevent spoilage. Due to its high salt content, it is recommended to limit each serving to within 10–15g.
