# Chicken Soup with Fish Maw  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐠 Dried fish maw approx. 30g  
🐓 Free-range chicken one approx. 900g  
🎃 Pumpkin 100g  
🫚 Ginger a small piece  
🍶 Rice wine 50ml  
⚪ White peppercorns one tablespoon  
🧂 Salt to taste  
💧 Purified water as needed (for soaking fish maw, do not use tap water)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🫙 Sealable bag  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🍳 Wok (iron pan)  
🥘 Stewing pot  
🌀 Blender  
📡 Microwave  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Soaking fish maw**  
   - Place dried fish maw in a sealable bag, pour in purified water, seal, and soak overnight.  
   - The next day, bring a pot of water to a boil, add fish maw and cook for about 5 minutes. When the edges turn white and curl, add a few slices of ginger to remove the fishy smell.  
   - Turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let the fish maw cool naturally in the hot water.  
   - Remove the fish maw, replace with fresh purified water, soak in the refrigerator overnight, and it is ready to use.  
**Key point**: The entire process takes about two nights; use only purified water throughout; after boiling, let it cool before refrigerating to ensure it is fully rehydrated and not hard.  

2. **Stir-frying chicken**  
   - Add oil to a wok, put in chicken pieces and ginger slices, and stir-fry over medium-high heat.  
   - When the chicken surface turns an appealing golden brown, add one tablespoon of white peppercorns and stir-fry until fragrant.  
   - Pour 50ml of rice wine along the edge of the wok, quickly toss, allowing the wine vapor to remove any fishy smell.  
3. **Simmering the base broth**  
   - Transfer the stir-fried chicken to a stewing pot, add enough water, and simmer over low heat for 1–2 hours until the chicken is tender and the broth is rich.  
4. **Adding fish maw**  
   - Cut the soaked fish maw into pieces, add to the stewing pot, and continue simmering over low heat for 20–30 minutes, allowing the fish maw to absorb the broth and become soft and sticky.  
5. **Making golden soup**  
   - Cut pumpkin into chunks, soften in the microwave, then place in a blender with just enough water to cover the pumpkin, and blend into a very fine pumpkin puree.  
   - Pour the pumpkin puree into the stewing pot and gently stir to combine.  
**Key point**: This is the “trick” to achieving a golden and thick soup; pumpkin adds color and thickness, but do not use too much or it will overpower the dish.  

6. **Final seasoning**  
   - Before removing from heat, add salt to taste, stir well, and serve. No need to add MSG or chicken bouillon.  

## Health Tips  
This chicken soup with fish maw is rich in collagen and high-quality protein, suitable for those seeking beauty, nourishment, or post-surgery recovery. Be sure to use purified water for soaking the fish maw to avoid affecting texture and nutrient release; the broth is rich, but the proportion of pumpkin should not be too large to avoid overpowering the chicken flavor.
