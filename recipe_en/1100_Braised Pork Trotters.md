# Braised Pork Trotters
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 Dark soy sauce  
🧂 Light soy sauce  
🧂 Cooking wine  
🧂 Rock sugar  
🧂 Salt  
🧂 Cooking oil  
🍖 Pork trotters  
🍖 Bay leaves  
🍖 Ginger  
🍖 Scallions  
🍖 Cinnamon stick  
🍖 Star anise  

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Wok  
🔪 Pressure cooker  
🔪 Spatula  
🔪 Pot for cold water  

## Steps
1. Place the chopped pork trotters in a pot of cold water, add 20 ml of cooking wine, scallion segments, and ginger slices. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, and continue blanching for 15 minutes. Remove, rinse thoroughly, and drain well.
   **Key point**: Blanching in cold water helps fully remove blood and any gamey smell.

2. Heat the wok, pour in 30 ml of cooking oil, add 7–8 pieces of rock sugar, and reduce to low heat. Constantly crush and stir the sugar with a spatula until it turns an amber color, about 2 minutes.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low to prevent the sugar from burning and turning bitter.

3. Once the caramel is ready, immediately add the blanched pork trotters. Keep the heat low and stir-fry until the trotters are evenly coated with the caramel and both sides are slightly browned.

4. Add 2 bay leaves, 1 cinnamon stick, 4 star anise, 3 slices of ginger, 20 ml of light soy sauce, 20 ml of dark soy sauce, 10 ml of cooking wine, and 8 g of salt. Increase to medium heat and stir-fry for 1 minute to fully blend the spices and seasonings.

5. Pour in enough boiling water to completely submerge the trotters. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn off the heat.

6. Transfer all ingredients and the liquid to a pressure cooker, seal the lid, and cook for 15 minutes once pressurized. If you do not have a pressure cooker, return the ingredients to a regular pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 1 hour until tender.

7. After the pressure cooker has naturally released its steam, pour the trotters and liquid back into the wok. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Taste at this point; if it is too bland, add 2–3 g more salt.

8. Reduce the sauce over high heat for about 30 seconds until it thickens, then turn off the heat and serve. Any remaining sauce can be saved to mix with rice for an excellent flavor.

## Health Advice
Braised pork trotters are rich in collagen, making them suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity, but they are high in calories, so portion control is recommended. People with high blood pressure or high cholesterol should eat in moderation to avoid excessive fat and sodium intake.
