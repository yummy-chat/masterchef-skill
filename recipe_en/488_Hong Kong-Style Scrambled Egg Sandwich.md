# Hong Kong-Style Scrambled Egg Sandwich
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Chilled eggs, several (about 2-3 per serving)
🥛 Evaporated milk, 1 tablespoon per egg (recommend Black & White brand)
🐂 Beef, as needed (fresh beef diced, or canned corned beef)
🍞 Sliced white bread, 2 slices (whole wheat bread optional, recommend unmolded loaf with crust left on)
🧈 Butter, as needed (for scrambling eggs and spreading on bread)
🍖 Ham/luncheon meat, as needed (basic ingredient)
🍄 White mushrooms, as needed (upgraded ingredient)
🧄 Garlic, a little (for sautéing)
🧅 Shallots, a little (optional)

🧂 Salt, a little (for seasoning)
🥛 Evaporated milk (already listed in ingredients)
🫗 Mayonnaise, as needed (for spreading on bread)
🫑 Black pepper, as needed
🫘 Sa cha sauce, 1 tablespoon (for marinating beef)
🫒 Sesame oil, a little (for marinating beef)
🍬 Sugar, a little (for marinating beef)
🥜 Peanut butter, as needed (optional, for spreading on bread)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan/wok
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula/cooking spatula
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥢 Toasting tongs/fork

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare Ingredients**
   - Dice fresh beef; if using canned corned beef, scoop it out directly.
   - Crack eggs into a bowl, add evaporated milk and a pinch of salt, and whisk thoroughly.
   - For the mushroom version, slice white mushrooms.
2. **Marinate Beef**
   - Add sugar, salt, sa cha sauce, and sesame oil to the diced beef. Mix vigorously and marinate for 10 minutes.
3. **Cook Fillings**
   - Heat oil in a pan, sauté garlic until fragrant.
   - Mushroom version: Add white mushrooms, stir-fry until soft, season with salt and black pepper, then set aside.
   - Beef version: Add the marinated beef, keep the heat high, stir-fry until the surface is browned and fragrant, then set aside.
4. **Cook Scrambled Eggs**
   - **Layered Tender Texture**: Heat butter in a pan until melted, pour in the egg mixture. Wait until the bottom is slightly set, then use a spatula to gently push the eggs from the edges toward the center. Repeat this pushing motion until the eggs are mostly set.
   - **Creamy Silky Texture**: After pouring in the egg mixture, stir quickly. As soon as the eggs begin to set slightly, immediately turn off the heat. Keep stirring using residual heat until the eggs become a paste-like consistency.
   - **Egg and Meat Fusion Method**: Mix the cooked beef or mushrooms into the egg mixture, then pour into a hot oiled pan. Cook until about 70% done, then immediately turn off the heat to maintain a silky, tender texture.
5. **Toast Bread**
   - Place the bread slices in a pan and toast until both sides are golden and crispy.
   - For "crustless" sandwiches, trim the hard crusts off the bread edges before toasting.
6. **Assemble Sandwich**
   - Spread a layer of butter on the inner side of one bread slice.
   - Add ham slices/luncheon meat, or the cooked mushroom scrambled eggs/beef scrambled eggs, and sprinkle with a little black pepper.
   - Cover with the other bread slice, press lightly, and cut diagonally.
> **Key Notes**:
> - Eggs must be chilled for a firmer texture when cooked.
> - Do not use too much evaporated milk, or the egg mixture will be too thin to set properly.
> - Control the heat carefully when scrambling eggs; the pushing or stirring motion must be quick to avoid overcooking and drying out.
> - Marinate the beef, then stir-fry quickly over high heat for a caramelized surface and tender interior.
> - Toasting time depends on pan temperature and bread color; avoid burning.

## Health Tips
This Hong Kong-style scrambled egg sandwich is rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it an ideal breakfast to fuel your day. However, be mindful of the combined calories from butter and mayonnaise; controlling portions is healthier. The beef version provides iron, suitable for busy office workers, but canned corned beef has high sodium content, so pairing it with unsweetened tea is recommended for balance.
