# Cauliflower and Tomato Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥦 Cauliflower, appropriate amount (finely chopped, as a rice substitute)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🍅 Tomatoes, 2
🍶 Ketchup, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Chili sauce, 1 tablespoon
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🌶️🧂 Ground pepper, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Non-stick skillet or wok
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1.  Finely chop the cauliflower into rice-like pieces. Place in a bowl, add the oyster sauce and chili sauce, stir to combine, and let it marinate for a short while.
   **Key Point**: The cauliflower does not need to be chopped too finely. Allow it to sit for a moment after mixing with the seasonings to absorb the flavors.
2.  Heat a skillet or wok over medium heat. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Crack the eggs into the pan. Once the bottoms are set, use chopsticks to quickly scramble them into small pieces.
   **Key Point**: There's no need to aim for a soft and silky texture. A slightly crispy bottom adds a better texture.
3.  Pour the marinated cauliflower into the pan and stir-fry together with the scrambled eggs until evenly combined.
4.  Squeeze an appropriate amount of ketchup into the pan. Use the spatula to press down on the tomatoes, mashing them slightly, then stir-fry everything together to coat the cauliflower evenly with the sauce.
5.  Cover the pan with a lid, reduce the heat to medium, and simmer for 5 minutes, until the cauliflower is cooked through but still retains a tender-crisp texture.
6.  Remove the lid, increase the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly to reduce the sauce and intensify the flavors.
7.  Turn off the heat and plate the dish. Garnish with ground pepper and chopped scallions.

## Health Advice
This cauliflower and tomato fried rice replaces rice with cauliflower, making it low in carbohydrates and high in fiber, suitable for those on a weight loss diet or managing blood sugar levels. Tomatoes and eggs provide vitamin C and high-quality protein. However, oyster sauce and chili sauce are high in sodium; those sensitive to blood pressure should reduce the amount used.
