# Oil-Exploded Freshwater Prawns
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Freshwater prawns (about 1 jin; if unavailable, substitute with regular river shrimp or white shrimp)
🫚 Ginger (a large handful, cut into thin shreds)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections)
🧴 Dark soy sauce with rock sugar (appropriate amount)
🍬 Rock sugar (a large amount)
🍶 Yellow rice wine (a large amount)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)
🧂 MSG (appropriate amount)
🍾 Cooking wine (a small amount)
🌿 White pepper (a small amount)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Soup spoon
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer

## Preparation Steps
1. Use scissors to cut off the two long claws of the prawns, leaving some of the whiskers. Wash and drain thoroughly.
**Note**: These prawns generally do not require deveining.

2. Pour a sufficient amount of oil into the wok and heat until it reaches 80% hot. Add the prepared prawns and fry quickly for about 30–40 seconds, until the shells turn red and crispy and the meat is just cooked. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key**: The oil temperature must be high, add the prawns quickly, and fry for a short time to make the shells crispy and lock in the freshness of the meat.

3. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Heat over medium heat, add a large amount of ginger shreds, and stir-fry until fragrant.
**Key**: Ginger is the main source of flavor; use a large amount and stir-fry thoroughly to release the aroma.

4. Reduce the heat to low. Add dark soy sauce with rock sugar, a large amount of rock sugar, a large amount of yellow rice wine, salt, and MSG. Simmer over low heat until the sauce boils and the rock sugar is completely melted.
**Key**: Simmer over low heat to allow the flavors of the seasonings to meld and eliminate the raw soy sauce taste; rock sugar enhances the brightness of the finished dish.

5. Turn the heat to high, add the fried prawns to the sauce, and quickly stir-fry evenly to coat the prawns thoroughly with the sauce.
6. Sprinkle in the scallion sections and stir-fry quickly a few times before plating.
**Note**: If the color is insufficient, you may add a little more dark soy sauce before removing from the wok.

## Health Advice
This oil-exploded freshwater prawn dish is high in oil and sugar. It is suitable for occasional indulgence and should not be eaten frequently, especially for those with high blood sugar or blood lipid levels; portion control is recommended. Prawn meat is rich in high-quality protein and zinc, and paired with ginger shreds, it can warm the stomach and dispel cold. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables.
