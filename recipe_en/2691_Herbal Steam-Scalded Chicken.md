# Herbal Steam-Scalded Chicken  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Half a free-range chicken (cut into small pieces)  
🍂 Angelica root (Danggui), to taste  
🍒 Red dates (jujubes), to taste  
🍇 Goji berries, to taste  
🌿 Solomon's seal (Yuzhu), to taste  
🌱 Glehnia root (Shashen), to taste  
🌾 Codonopsis root (Dangshen), to taste  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel (Chenpi), one small piece  
🪨 River pebbles, to taste (preheated in advance)  
🧅 Scallions (spring onions), to taste (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a small amount  
🍶 Yellow rice wine (Huangjiu), slightly more than usual  
🥡 Dark soy sauce, a small amount  
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste  
🥜 Peanut oil, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥅 Strainer  
🔥 Oven  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🫕 Clay pot (sand pot)  
🥣 Large bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place angelica root, red dates, goji berries, Solomon's seal, glehnia root, codonopsis root, and dried tangerine peel in a stockpot. Add cold water and bring to a boil. Continue boiling until the herbal flavors are fully extracted. Strain the liquid through a strainer into a bowl, discarding the solids.  
**Note**: Start with cold water to thoroughly extract the herbal benefits.  

2. Arrange the river pebbles on a baking sheet. Place in an oven preheated to 250°C (482°F) and roast for 30 minutes until scorching hot. Remove and set aside.  
**Key**: The pebbles must be fully heated to ensure proper steaming effect later.  

3. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with salt, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, yellow rice wine, dark soy sauce, and cornstarch. Mix well with your hands. Add the scallion sections and ginger slices, and mix again. Finally, drizzle in peanut oil and mix to seal in moisture.  
**Key**: The chicken pieces should not be too large; use slightly more yellow rice wine. Adding peanut oil at the end prevents sticking.  

4. Place a heated clay pot on the counter. Quickly pour the hot river pebbles into the pot, then evenly spread the marinated chicken pieces over the pebbles.  
**Note**: Work quickly to prevent the pebbles from cooling, which would affect the steaming process.  

5. Immediately pour the hot herbal broth over the chicken. Cover the pot quickly with its lid, using the rising steam to cook the chicken thoroughly.  
**Note**: The entire process must be done rapidly to avoid heat loss. The pebbles are extremely hot—be cautious of burns.  

6. Let the chicken steam for a short while. Once the steam subsides and the chicken is fully cooked, remove the lid and serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This herbal steam-scalded chicken uses high-temperature river pebbles for cooking, combined with nourishing herbs like angelica root and glehnia root. It is ideal for those with insufficient blood and energy (Qi and blood deficiency) or for nourishing during autumn and winter. Ensure the chicken pieces are cut small, use extra yellow rice wine to remove any gamey odor and enhance flavor, and work quickly during steaming to trap the steam.
