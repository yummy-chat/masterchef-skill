# Chestnut Filling
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌰 Chestnuts 2 jin (washed and cut open)
🍬 White Sugar 150 grams
🛢️ Cooking Oil 80 grams
💧 Water Approximately 500 milliliters (adjustable based on chestnut puree consistency)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🍚 Rice cooker
🥣 Glass bowl
🫗 Strainer (sieve)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the chestnuts. Use a knife to make a deep horizontal cut across the middle of each chestnut, cutting through the outer skin and slightly into the flesh. Exercise caution during this step.
2. Place the chestnuts cut-side up in a single layer in the rice cooker inner pot. Add enough water to just cover the bottom of the chestnuts. Close the lid and select the "Steam Rice" function.
3. Once the rice cooker switches to keep-warm mode and the water inside has evaporated, remove the chestnuts. While they are still hot, peel off the outer shell and inner skin.
   **Key Point**: Use a spoon or break open to extract the kernel. Peeling while hot makes the process easier.
4. Press the chestnut kernels through a strainer to create a fine chestnut puree.
   **Note**: This step is time-consuming and requires patience. Alternatively, you can use a blender with a small amount of water or milk to puree directly.
5. Add approximately 500 milliliters of water, white sugar, and cooking oil to a wok. Heat and stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.
6. Add the prepared chestnut puree. Reduce the heat to medium-low and stir continuously with a spatula to prevent sticking to the bottom.
7. Continue stirring until the water has evaporated and the chestnut puree becomes thick and clumps together, no longer sticking to the wok or spatula.
   **Tip**: If the mixture seems too watery initially, extend the stirring time. Avoid adding too much water at once. If it becomes too dry, you can add a small amount of water to adjust.
8. Transfer the finished chestnut filling to a container. Allow it to cool before use. The result is a smooth, sandy, and sweet filling, ideal for use in pastries.

## Health Advice
Chestnut filling is rich in dietary fiber and complex carbohydrates, making it a natural energy source suitable for autumn nourishment or as a low-fat dessert filling. However, it contains significant amounts of sugar and oil, so those controlling sugar intake or losing weight should consume only half the recommended portion.
