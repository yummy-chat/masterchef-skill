# Taro Ball with Fresh Milk Mochi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥛 Milk 200g  
🌿 Tapioca Starch 20g  
🍬 White Sugar 5g  
🍠 Taro Balls About 1 bowl (pre-cooked and drained)  
🍯 Brown Sugar Water To taste  
🍪 Oreo Crumbs To taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Microwave  
🥣 Heat-resistant bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍜 Strainer

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour the milk, tapioca starch, and white sugar into a bowl. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until no dry powder remains.  
   **Key point**: Stir well to prevent the starch from settling and forming lumps.
2. Place the prepared milk mochi mixture into the microwave and heat until it sets into a thick, stretchy paste.  
   **Key point**: During or after heating, take it out and stir to ensure even heat distribution.
3. Layer the pre-cooked and drained taro balls at the bottom of a bowl, then drizzle with an appropriate amount of brown sugar water.
4. Scoop a large spoonful of the heated milk mochi and place it on top of the taro balls.
5. Sprinkle an appropriate amount of Oreo crumbs on top for decoration.
6. The finished dish is rich in color, well-layered, with a soft, chewy, and springy texture, and obvious stretchiness. Paired with crispy Oreo crumbs, the flavor is even better.

## Health Tips
This dessert offers a good balance of calcium and carbohydrates, making it suitable as an afternoon snack. However, individuals with diabetes or those controlling sugar intake should be mindful of the amount of brown sugar water added. Although the mochi has a great stretchy effect when hot, it is best enjoyed warm, as the texture will harden after cooling.
