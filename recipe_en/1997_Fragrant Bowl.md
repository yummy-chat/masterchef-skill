# Fragrant Bowl

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs: 5 (3 per 500g of meat, about 5 total)  
🥩 Pork front shoulder: 850g (fat-to-lean ratio 30:70)  
🧅 Ginger: to taste (finely minced, generous amount)  
🌱 Scallions (white parts): to taste (finely chopped, generous amount)  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms: to taste (pre-soaked)  
🧈 Tofu: to taste (sliced)  
🌱 Bean sprouts: to taste  
🍗 Crispy pork: to taste (for layering at the bottom)  
🥔 Taro: to taste (sliced)  
🍠 Sweet potato starch: 75g (**must use**)  
🥔 Potato starch: 1 tablespoon (for egg skin)  
🐷 Pork fatback: 1 small piece (for greasing the pan)  

🧂 Salt: to taste  
🧂 MSG: to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon: to taste  
🌿 White pepper: to taste  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper: to taste  
🍂 Five-spice powder (optional): to taste  
🥣 Clear broth (or stock): to taste (for the second steaming)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok (Chinese wok)  
🥄 Fine mesh strainer  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🪥 Toothpicks  
♨️ Steamer or large steaming pot  
🥣 Large soup bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
### Making the Egg Skin  
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat until well mixed. Take 1 tablespoon of potato starch and dissolve it in a small amount of water. Let it settle, then pour off the clear water on top. Add the wet starch paste to the beaten eggs and stir well. Strain the egg mixture through a fine mesh strainer for a smoother texture.  
**Key point**: Straining the egg mixture through a fine mesh strainer yields a finer texture.  

2. Heat the iron wok until the back of your hand feels obvious heat near the bottom. Use the pork fatback to evenly coat the wok with a thin layer of oil.  
3. Ladle one spoonful of the egg mixture into the wok. Quickly tilt and rotate the wok to spread it into a thin, rectangular egg skin. Use the residual heat of the wok to let the egg mixture set naturally, without turning on the heat.  
4. Once the bottom is set, carefully flip the egg skin and lightly cook the other side briefly. Remove it. Three eggs should yield about two egg skins.  
### Preparing the Meat Filling  
1. Remove the skin from the pork front shoulder and slice thinly.  
2. Place the meat slices in a bowl. Add a generous amount of minced ginger and chopped scallions, then add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, ground Sichuan pepper, and optionally a little five-spice powder.  
3. Beat in the eggs and add sweet potato starch. Stir vigorously in one direction, kneading and mixing until the filling becomes very thick, stringy, and sticky.  
### Rolling and Shaping  
1. Brush the side of the egg skin that was against the wok with a thin layer of egg mixture.  
2. Evenly spread the meat filling over the egg skin, shaping it into a rectangular strip as wide as the skin.  
3. Starting from one end, roll the egg skin tightly inward, pressing firmly as you go to ensure the filling is fully wrapped. Place the seam on the bottom. Use a toothpick to poke small holes in the rolled egg roll to allow steam to escape, preventing the skin from separating from the filling during steaming.  
**Key point**: Poking holes to release steam prevents the egg skin from separating from the filling.  

### First Steaming  
1. Place the raw fragrant bowl rolls in a steamer or steaming pot. Steam over high heat for about 40 minutes. It is now edible, but the traditional method requires the following steps for arranging in a bowl.  
### Arranging in a Bowl and Second Steaming  
1. Let the steamed fragrant bowl rolls cool completely, then slice into pieces about 0.5 cm thick.  
2. Line the bottom of a large bowl with pre-soaked wood ear mushrooms, sliced tofu, bean sprouts, crispy pork, and sliced taro as base ingredients.  
3. Neatly arrange the sliced fragrant bowl pieces over the base ingredients in a petal pattern.  
4. Pour clear broth into the bowl until it is level with or slightly below the ingredients. Place the bowl back in the steamer and steam for another 30 minutes, allowing the fragrant bowl to fully absorb the broth and the base ingredients to become flavorful.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses ginger, scallions, and starch to bind the meat filling tightly, offering rich protein and carbohydrates. It is suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or needing a nutritional boost. Note that sweet potato starch is a key ingredient and cannot be substituted, as it affects the rolling and shaping results.
