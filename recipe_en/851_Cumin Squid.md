# Cumin Squid
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Steps  
1.  Blanch the squid in advance and set aside; cut the green pepper and onion into strips; mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, chicken bouillon, and sugar evenly to make the sauce.  
   **Note**: The sauce must be mixed evenly in advance.  
2.  Place the wok over heat, pour in cooking oil and heat until 70% hot, then add minced ginger, minced garlic, dried chili pepper sections, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
   **Key**: Use hot oil to fully bloom the aromatics, allowing their fragrance to release.  
3.  Add the blanched squid rolls, green pepper strips, onion strips, and the prepared sauce. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly for about 2 minutes.  
   **Key**: Stir-fry over high heat to ensure the ingredients are well-flavored and the texture remains tender and crisp.  
4.  Sprinkle in chili flakes, cumin powder, and white sesame seeds. Stir-fry rapidly until evenly coated, then immediately remove from heat and plate.  
   **Note**: Add dry spices last to avoid prolonged high-heat cooking that would diminish their flavor.  

## Health Advice  
Squid is rich in high protein and low in fat. Pairing it with green pepper and onion adds dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss diets. However, the sauce has a relatively high salt content; those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of dark soy sauce and oyster sauce.
