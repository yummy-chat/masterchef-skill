# Braised Noodles with Tomato and Fried Egg
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 2 tomatoes (1 cut into large chunks for cooking, 1 diced and added at the end)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (beaten, for making fried egg)  
🍜 Dried noodles, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, minced, as needed (used twice: for stir-frying the base and for finishing flavor)  
🧅 Scallions, chopped, as needed (sprinkled before serving)  
🫒 Peanut oil, as needed (for frying the egg and stir-frying the base)  
🧂 Salt, a small amount (to help the tomatoes release juice)  
🥢 Light soy sauce, as needed (for color and flavor)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Small pot (for deep-frying the egg)  
🥘 Wok (for stir-frying the base and braising the noodles)  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowl or plate (for holding the cut tomatoes and beaten egg)  

## Steps  
1. Prepare the tomatoes: Cut one tomato into large chunks and dice the other, setting them aside separately.  
   **Key point**: The two cutting methods provide different textures—the large chunks soften and release juice during cooking, while the diced tomato added at the end enhances freshness and layering.  
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat thoroughly to form an egg mixture, then set aside.  
3. Pour enough peanut oil into a small pot and heat until it reaches 70% hot. Quickly pour the beaten egg from a height into the oil, frying until it rapidly puffs up and turns golden.  
   **Key point**: Deep oil, high heat, and pouring from a height are key to making the egg fluffy.  
4. Flip the egg and continue frying until both sides are golden brown and slightly charred. Remove and drain the oil to make the fried egg.  
5. In a separate wok, add an appropriate amount of peanut oil and stir-fry half the minced garlic until fragrant.  
6. Add the large chunks of tomato and a small amount of salt, stir-frying until the tomatoes soften and release juice, forming a thick paste.  
   **Note**: Adding salt helps the tomatoes release juice more quickly.  
7. Add light soy sauce for color and flavor, then pour in an appropriate amount of water.  
8. Lay the dried noodles flat on top of the sauce, then place the whole fried egg on top of the noodles.  
9. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and braise for about 6 minutes until the noodles fully absorb the sauce and are cooked through.  
10. Remove the lid and sprinkle with the diced tomato, remaining minced garlic, and chopped scallions.  
11. Toss the noodles, fried egg, and all toppings together until evenly combined, allowing the flavors to fully meld before serving.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in lycopene and protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. The fried egg absorbs a lot of oil, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the calorie intake. Patients with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of light soy sauce.
