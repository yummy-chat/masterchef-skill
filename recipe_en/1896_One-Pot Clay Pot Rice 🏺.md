# One-Pot Clay Pot Rice 🏺

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice – about one small jarful
🍃 West Lake Longjing tea leaves – a few leaves
🥩 Cantonese sausage – 1 piece (placed in whole)
🥓 Luncheon meat – 1 piece (sliced)
🥚 Egg – 1 (raw egg cracked directly in)
🥬 Baby cabbage – 2–3 leaves (torn into large pieces)
🍖 Chicken sausage/corn ribs – as desired
🍶 Rice seasoning sauce – to taste (added after cooking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🏺 Clay pot
♨️ Electric pressure cooker
🥢 Chopsticks
💧 Water tap

## Steps
1.  Place an appropriate amount of rice into the clay pot, rinse it clean with water, and drain.
2.  Add water to the clay pot until it roughly covers the rice.  
    **Note**: Adjust the water amount based on your preferred rice firmness.
3.  Put a few West Lake Longjing tea leaves into the rice water.  
    **Key point**: The tea leaves add a special aroma to the rice; do not use too much.
4.  Layer the Cantonese sausage, meats, and luncheon meat slices over the rice water in order.  
    **Key point**: The meats do not need to be pre-cooked; add them directly to the pot raw.
5.  Crack a raw egg into the center of the ingredients.  
    **Key point**: The egg will naturally set during the cooking process.
6.  Tear the baby cabbage into large pieces and place them on top of all ingredients.  
    **Note**: Add the vegetables last so they are cooked by the steam.
7.  Place the entire clay pot into the electric pressure cooker's inner pot and close the pressure cooker lid.  
    **Note**: Ensure the clay pot fits inside the pressure cooker.
8.  Start the "Quick Cook" mode on the electric pressure cooker.  
    **Key point**: Use the preset program; the cooking time is automatically controlled by the appliance.
9.  After the program ends, manually turn the pressure release valve to vent steam. Do not open the lid until venting is complete.
10. Carefully open the lid and take out the clay pot.
11. Add an appropriate amount of rice seasoning sauce based on your taste.
12. Use chopsticks to thoroughly mix all the ingredients with the rice in the pot. At this point, the rice grains are distinct, the vegetables have softened, the egg is set, and the dish carries the aroma of tea and meat.

## Health Suggestion
This dish is relatively high in sodium due to the sausage, luncheon meat, and rice seasoning sauce. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or soup to balance. Individuals with high blood pressure should moderate their intake. Tea-leaf rice can help cut through the greasiness, but do not use too many leaves to avoid a bitter taste in the rice.
