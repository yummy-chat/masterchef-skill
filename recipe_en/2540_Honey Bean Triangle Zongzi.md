# Honey Bean Triangle Zongzi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice 1 kg
🫘 Red beans 300 g
💧 Water as needed (for soaking, cooking beans, and boiling zongzi)
🌿 Zongzi leaves as needed (trim hard stems)
🧵 Cotton string as needed
🍬 Brown sugar 300 g
🧂 Baking soda 1/8 tsp

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for soaking rice and beans)
🍳 Stockpot (for cooking red beans, stir-frying filling, and boiling zongzi)
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Spoon (for scooping filling)
🪣 Strainer/colander
🍽️ Plate (for pressing while cooking)
🪨 Clean stone or heavy object (for pressing with plate)
✂️ Scissors (for trimming leaves and cutting string)

## Steps
1. Soak the glutinous rice and red beans separately in clean water overnight. Drain the water the next day.
2. Place the soaked red beans in a pot, add just enough water to cover the beans, and stir in the baking soda. Place a plate on the pot opening, weigh it down with a heavy object, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and cook for about 1 hour and 20 minutes, until the beans are soft and tender, the skins remain intact, and they can be easily crushed with fingers.
3. Drain the cooked red beans, return them to the pot, add the brown sugar, and heat over the lowest heat for about 20 minutes, keeping the pot at a gentle simmer without stirring. Once the sugar water reduces and the beans become dry, glossy, and fluffy, remove from heat and let cool. Set aside.
4. Trim the hard stems from the zongzi leaves. With the smooth side facing up, fold the leaf into a cone shape. First, fill the tip with half a spoonful of glutinous rice, then add one spoonful of rice and press flat. Add two spoonfuls of honey bean filling, and finally top with one spoonful of glutinous rice, pressing flat. Fold the leaf tip downward to cover the filling, forming a triangular cone.
5. Wrap the cotton string around the wide end of the zongzi, wrapping the second loop over the starting end and the third loop over the second. Tie a knot to secure, and trim any excess leaf.
6. Bring a pot of water to a boil, then add the zongzi, making sure the water fully covers them. Place a plate on the pot opening, weigh it down with a heavy object, bring to a boil again, then reduce to the lowest heat and cook for 3 hours, until the water becomes clear and the zongzi are soft and sticky.
7. Remove the zongzi, drain the water, peel off the leaves, and enjoy.

## Health Advice
This honey bean triangle zongzi is primarily made with glutinous rice and honey beans, rich in carbohydrates and dietary fiber, making it suitable for replenishing energy after summer activities. The combination of beans and glutinous rice helps stabilize blood sugar, but diabetics should control portion sizes.
