# Gold Ingot Flower Bun 🥟  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🎃 Pumpkin 250g (peeled and seeded, then mashed into puree)  
🌾 Flour 400g  
🍞 Yeast 1 small packet (approx. 3-5g)  
🐖 Lard about 30g  
🔴 Red dough small amount (for making the "福" character decoration)  
🍬 White sugar to taste (to promote fermentation)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍲 Steamer  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🪒 Scraper  
🟫 Silicone kneading mat  
🎞️ Plastic wrap/damp towel  
💦 Spray bottle  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel and seed the pumpkin, cut into thick slices, and steam over medium heat for 15 minutes until soft. Mash into puree while hot, then let cool until it is no longer hot to the touch.  
2. Add white sugar and yeast to the pumpkin puree, and mix well.  
3. Gradually add flour, stirring into a flaky texture, then knead into a initially smooth dough. Add lard and continue kneading until the lard is fully absorbed and the dough is smooth and soft.  
4. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let it ferment in a warm place until doubled in size.  
5. Punch down the fermented dough to release air, take half and roll into a long strip, then cut into pieces about 40g each.  
6. Roll each piece into a ball, then shape into a long oval that is slightly thinner at both ends and thicker in the middle. Flatten both ends, fold over, and pinch the sides together from the bottom upward to form a gold ingot shape.  
7. Spray a small amount of water on top of the gold ingot, and attach a red "福" character decoration. Place the shaped buns in the steamer basket and let them proof for a second time for 15-20 minutes until they feel light.  
8. Once the steamer is emitting steam, place the shaped buns in, and steam over high heat for 12 minutes. Turn off the heat and let them sit covered for 2 minutes before opening the lid.  

## Health Advice  
The combination of pumpkin and flour provides carbohydrates and vitamin A, making it suitable as a festive staple, especially for those needing eye care. Diabetic patients should note the amount of white sugar added.
