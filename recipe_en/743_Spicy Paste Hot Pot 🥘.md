# Spicy Paste Hot Pot 🥘  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌶️ Goat Horn Dried Chilies 3 portions (pounded into powder)  
🌶️ Bullethead Dried Chilies 0.5 portions (pounded into powder)  
🌶️ Line Dried Chilies 1 portion (pounded into powder)  
🥩 Beef Rolls to taste  
🥢 Bean Curd Skin to taste  
🍡 Assorted Balls (shrimp balls, fish balls, etc.) to taste  
🥬 Leafy Greens (such as bok choy) to taste  
🐔 Old Hen 1 whole (pre-brewed into plain chicken broth)  
🍅 Tomato Paste to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌿 Sichuan Pepper Powder to taste  
🌿 Spice Powder (angelica, star anise, cumin, cinnamon, white cardamom, dry-roasted and ground) to taste  
🌿 Cumin (dry-roasted, half ground, half left whole) to taste  
🌰 Toasted White Sesame Seeds to taste  
🌾 Cornstarch to taste  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🪨 Stone Mortar  
🔍 Sieve  
🥣 Bowl  
🍲 Copper Pot (or Clay Pot)  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prepping Stage  
1. Place the Goat Horn, Bullethead, and Line chilies in the stone mortar and pound into powder.  
   **Key Point**: The mix of three chilies provides a complex, spicy flavor for the broth.  
2. Sift the chili powder with a sieve to separate coarse particles from fine powder; set both aside.  
3. In a bowl, combine 1 portion of fine chili powder, 2 portions of coarse chili flakes, Sichuan pepper powder, spice powder, cumin, sesame seeds, and cornstarch. Mix thoroughly to form the "Spicy Paste Mix" dry powder.  
   **Key Point**: Cumin must be pre-roasted; half ground for aroma, half left whole for a chewy texture.  
4. Pre-brew the old hen to make plain chicken broth; set aside.  

### Cooking Stage  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add tomato paste and stir well. Then add the prepared spicy paste dry powder, stirring quickly to fully dissolve and prevent clumps.  
2. Bring to a rolling boil, stirring constantly with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. Cook until the broth becomes thick, red, and glossy.  
3. Pour in the pre-brewed chicken broth and continue boiling for 3–5 minutes, allowing the broth to thicken further and cling better to ingredients.  
4. Add a small amount of salt to taste.  
5. Place beef rolls, bean curd skin, assorted balls, leafy greens, and other ingredients into the bubbling broth to cook and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This spicy paste hot pot uses chili powder and chicken broth to create a thick, coating soup base. It is rich in protein and capsaicin, making it ideal for warming the body in autumn and winter, as well as for those who crave intense, spicy flavors. Pair with leafy greens to balance the heat. Those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation to avoid excessive irritation.
