# Braised Pork Belly with Oil Beans
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Preparation Steps
1. Scrape and clean the skin of the pork belly, then slice it thinly. The pork must have skin; hold the meat firmly and slice along the grain.
**Key Point**: The pork should have skin; slice along the grain while holding the meat firmly.

2. Cut the scallion into larger sections and the ginger into larger slices. The scallion and ginger pieces should not be too small to avoid dissolving during prolonged braising.
**Key Point**: The scallion and ginger pieces should not be too small to prevent dissolving during long braising.

3. Remove the ends and tough strings from both sides of the oil beans, wash them clean, and drain for about 10 minutes. Ensure they are thoroughly drained to avoid affecting the stir-frying when added to the wok.
**Key Point**: The beans must be thoroughly drained to avoid affecting the stir-frying result.

4. Heat the iron wok, add oil, and once the oil is hot, add the pork belly slices. Stir-fry over medium heat until the slices change color and some fat renders out. A hot wok and hot oil prevent the meat slices from sticking and speed up the color change.
**Key Point**: A hot wok and hot oil prevent the meat slices from sticking and speed up the color change.

5. Add the scallion and ginger, stir-fry quickly until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.
**Note**: Avoid burning.

6. Splash a little Sichuan peppercorn water along the edge of the wok, stir-fry a few times, then add the seafood soy sauce. The action should be quick so the liquid vaporizes instantly on the hot wok wall, fully releasing the aroma.
**Key Point**: The action should be quick to allow the liquid to vaporize instantly on the hot wok wall and release the aroma.

7. Add the drained oil beans, stir-fry continuously over medium heat for about 2 minutes, until the beans change from light green to dark green and appear slightly glossy on the surface. This step removes the raw taste of the beans and reduces some moisture, making the braised beans more aromatic.
**Key Point**: Stir-frying removes the raw taste of the beans and reduces moisture, resulting in a more aromatic finished dish.

8. Add an适量 amount of salt, stir-fry evenly to season the beans early.
9. Add water until it reaches the same level as the ingredients, splash in a little more Sichuan peppercorn water, cover the wok, and braise over medium heat for about 15 minutes. Braise until the beans are completely tender and can be easily cut through with a spoon, and the liquid has reduced and thickened. Adjust the braising time based on the tenderness of the beans, ensuring they are fully cooked.
**Key Point**: Braise until the beans are tender and the liquid is thickened; adjust the time based on the tenderness of the beans, ensuring they are fully cooked.

10. Remove the lid, increase the heat to reduce the liquid until it is mostly evaporated, leaving only an oily glaze coating the ingredients. Turn off the heat, add MSG, and gently stir with a spoon to distribute evenly. When reducing the liquid, stir gently to avoid breaking the beans; MSG must be added only after turning off the heat.
**Note**: Stir gently when reducing the liquid to avoid breaking the beans; add MSG only after turning off the heat.

## Health Advice
Braised pork belly with oil beans is rich in protein and dietary fiber, suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter. However, the pork belly is high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with brown rice to balance calorie intake. Ensure the beans are fully cooked to avoid poisoning from saponins.
