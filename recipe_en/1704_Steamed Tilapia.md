# Steamed Tilapia  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Cooking Steps  
1. Clean the tilapia thoroughly. Make several diagonal cuts on both sides of the fish, creating a crisscross pattern. Ensure the cuts are even in depth for better marination and even steaming.  
2. Rub the fish inside and out with scallion and ginger cooking wine and salt. Place it in a bowl and marinate for ten minutes to remove any fishy smell and enhance flavor.  
3. Stack the scallion greens, red chili shreds, and spring onion whites together. Cut them into fine shreds and soak in cold water until they curl naturally, making them more visually appealing.  
4. Bring water in the steamer to a boil until steam rises. Place the marinated fish on a steaming plate and steam over high heat for eight minutes, until the fish’s eyes protrude and the flesh turns white.  
**Note**: Make sure the water is boiling before placing the fish in the steamer. Adjust the steaming time based on the size of the fish, avoiding overcooking.  
5. Remove the steaming plate and carefully discard all the liquid from the plate.  
6. Evenly drizzle soy sauce for steamed fish over the fish.  
7. Retrieve the soaked three-shred mixture, drain well, and heap it generously on top of the fish.  
8. In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil until it just begins to smoke. Quickly and evenly pour the hot oil over the three-shred mixture. Listen for a sizzling sound and enjoy the aroma of scallions.  

## Health Advice  
Tilapia is a high-quality source of protein, low in fat and rich in unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or managing blood lipids. Steaming preserves the fish’s tenderness; discarding the steaming liquid reduces fishy flavors, resulting in a cleaner taste.
