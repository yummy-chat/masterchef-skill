# Cheese
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Whole milk 1800 ml  
🍋 Lemon 2 (about 80 grams of juice)  
🧂 Salt 2 grams  
🧴 White vinegar 80 grams (can substitute for lemon juice)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Pot  
🕸️ Strainer  
🧽 Cheesecloth (and bowl)  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
📦 Airtight container (such as a glass food storage box)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Juice the lemons and strain to obtain about 80 grams of pure juice; if using white vinegar as a substitute, set aside 80 grams.
2. Pour the whole milk into a pot and heat over medium heat until steam gently rises from the surface and the pot wall feels hot to the touch, then turn off the heat. Do not let it boil.
3. Slowly pour in the lemon juice, stirring continuously for about 1 minute, until the protein fully coagulates into flocculent curds.
4. Line a bowl with cheesecloth, pour in the mixture, gather the four corners of the cheesecloth, and firmly squeeze out the whey.  
   **Key**: The drier you squeeze it, the thicker the finished cheese will be; the curds remaining in the cheesecloth are the cheese base.
5. Place the squeezed-dry curds into a large bowl, add the salt, and beat with an electric hand mixer on medium speed. If the mixture is too dry and hard, add a small amount of the strained whey, and beat until smooth, glossy, and creamy.
6. Transfer the cheese into an airtight container, smooth the surface, seal, and refrigerate. Consume within 3 to 4 days.

## Health Suggestions
Homemade cheese is high in calcium and low in sugar, making it suitable for those controlling sugar intake or supplementing calcium. However, it loses a significant amount of whey protein, so it is recommended to pair it with yogurt or eggs to supplement amino acids. When squeezing the curds dry, control the pressure to avoid making the final product too dry or grainy.
