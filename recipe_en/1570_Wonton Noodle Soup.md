# Wonton Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥟 Wontons, about 8 pieces
🍡 Beef balls, 2 pieces
🥬 Lettuce, to taste
🫔 Dried seaweed, to taste
🦐 Dried shrimp, to taste
🥚 Egg, 1 piece
🥄 Vegetable oil, a splash
🧂 Ground dried fish powder, about 1 tablespoon
🧂 Ground white pepper, to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥘 Soup pot
🥣 Small wok
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks or tongs
🍲 Noodle bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Add a splash of oil to a small wok, put in the dried seaweed and dried shrimp, stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, then transfer to a noodle bowl.
**Key point**: Stir-frying fully releases the aroma of the seaweed and dried shrimp.
2. Add the ground dried fish powder and an appropriate amount of ground white pepper to the bowl, mixing to create a Cantonese-style soup base.
3. Bring water to a boil in a soup pot. Add the wontons and beef balls, and cook until they all float to the surface and are fully cooked.
4. Add the lettuce and blanch until tender. Pour everything, including the broth, into the prepared bowl with the seasonings.
**Key point**: Using the original cooking broth to dissolve the seasonings at the bottom of the bowl enhances the flavor harmony.
5. Heat a little oil in a frying pan and fry a sunny-side-up egg. Place it on top of the noodle soup to finish.
**Note**: The egg can be fried to single-side or double-side doneness according to preference.

## Health Advice
This wonton noodle soup contains eggs, vegetables, and seafood, offering balanced nutrition. It is especially suitable for a meal that needs to quickly replenish energy without being too greasy. It is recommended to use less ground dried fish powder and dried shrimp, as they are high in sodium. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soup consumed.
