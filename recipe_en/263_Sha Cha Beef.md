# Sha Cha Beef
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐂 Beef shank 600g (cut into small pieces)
🧅 Ginger slices 15g
🧅 Scallion segments 1 stalk
🧄 Minced garlic 2 cloves
⭐ Star anise 1 piece
🍶 Five-spice powder 1/2 tsp
🥜 Sha cha sauce 2 tbsp
🍶 Yellow wine 1 tbsp
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🧂 Salt to taste
🍬 Sugar 1/2 tsp
💧 Water as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Knife
🫖 Measuring spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut beef shank into small pieces, blanch in cold water. After water boils, skim off foam, drain well.
2. Heat oil in wok, add beef pieces and stir-fry until surface changes color and releases aroma.
3. Add ginger slices, scallion segments, star anise, continue stir-frying until fragrance intensifies.
4. Add five-spice powder and sha cha sauce, quickly stir-fry to coat beef evenly with sauce.
5. Drizzle in yellow wine and light soy sauce, add salt and sugar, then pour in enough water, bring to a boil over high heat.
6. Reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 1 to 2 hours.
**Note**: You may open the lid once to stir and check water level; if broth reduces too quickly, replenish with hot water as needed.
7. Simmer until beef is tender, and broth thickens naturally. Remove from heat.
**Key Point**: Beef shank is low in fat; after prolonged braising, it becomes soft and sticky without drying out. The savory saltiness of sha cha sauce has infused into the beef, no need for extra thickening.

## Health Advice
Beef shank is low in fat and high in protein, braised with sha cha sauce until tender and easy to digest, suitable for muscle-building and weight-loss diets. Watch salt intake, as the sauce itself is high in sodium.
