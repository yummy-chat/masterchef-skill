# Braised Pork Ribs with Beans and Steamed Bread Lid
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork spare ribs (clean ribs) 1000g
🥓 Pork belly 300g (thinly sliced)
🫛 Green beans 1000g
🥔 Potatoes 500g (peeled and cut into rolling chunks)
🌽 Sweet corn segments 400g
🌾 High-gluten flour 500g
🧂 Baking powder 5g
🫙 Yeast 5g
🍬 White sugar 10g (for proofing the dough)
🧅 Spring onion 30g (cut into sections, for blanching and stir-frying)
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced, for blanching and stir-frying)
⭐ Star anise 40g
🍶 Cooking wine 20g
🧴 Dark soy sauce 20g
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder 1g
🧂 Salt 10g
🫘 Farm-style soybean paste 50g
🦪 Oyster sauce 30g
🧴 Light soy sauce 50g
🧂 MSG 10g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 8g
🫒 Vegetable oil 50g
🚰 Warm water 250g (for kneading dough)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🪵 Wooden lid
🥣 Large bowl
🥣 Glass bowl
🪢 Rolling pin
🪟 Plastic wrap
🥣 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the ribs into small pieces. Place in cold water in a pot, add enough water, cooking wine, and some spring onion and ginger. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove and rinse clean with cold water.
**Note**: Starting with cold water helps to fully remove any gamey odors.
2. In a separate pot, add the blanched ribs, hot water, dark soy sauce, thirteen-spice powder, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and braise for about 20 minutes, until the ribs are half-cooked. Reserve the cooking liquid.
3. Make the bread lid: Mix the flour with baking powder. Dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water, pour into the flour, and knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 20 minutes. After resting, punch down the dough, roll it out into a round slightly larger than the pot’s opening, wrap it around the rolling pin, and let it rise for a second time for 20 minutes.
4. Pour vegetable oil into the iron wok. Add the sliced pork belly and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the slices turn golden and crispy, forming cracklings.
**Note**: Fully rendering the pork fat is key to the dish’s rich flavor.
5. Add the spring onion sections, ginger slices, and star anise. Stir-fry until fragrant, then add the green beans. Continue stir-frying over medium-low heat for at least 5 minutes, until the bean skins change color and become wrinkled.
**Note**: Stir-frying removes the raw taste, making the beans more flavorful and tender.
6. Add the farm-style soybean paste, oyster sauce, and light soy sauce. Stir-fry until the sauce is aromatic.
7. Pour in the partially cooked ribs and their reserved liquid from step 2. Stir to combine. Cover the wok and braise over medium heat for 10 minutes.
8. Remove the lid. Add MSG and chicken bouillon. Taste and adjust seasoning.
9. Arrange the corn segments along the edge of the wok. Then evenly spread the potato chunks on top, gently pressing them into the liquid.
10. Gently lay the proofed bread lid over the ingredients, tucking the edges against the sides of the wok to form a seal. Cover with the lid and simmer over low heat for 20 minutes.
11. When the time is up, remove the lid. Cut an opening in the center of the bread lid, take out the entire bread lid, and place it on a plate.
12. Leave the remaining ingredients and liquid in the wok. Increase the heat and stir-fry to reduce the liquid until thickened. Serve together with the bread lid.

## Health Advice
This dish is nutritionally balanced: the ribs provide quality protein and calcium, while the green beans and potatoes are rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for replenishing energy in winter. It is recommended to moderate the amount of pork belly, and those with high blood lipids can reduce the fat intake. The bread lid, having absorbed the flavorful liquid, is relatively high in calories, so it’s best paired with light vegetables.
