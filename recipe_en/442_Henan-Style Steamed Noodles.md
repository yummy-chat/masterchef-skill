# Henan-Style Steamed Noodles
Cuisine: Henan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Fresh-cut noodles, as needed
🥩 Pork slices, as needed (lean or pork belly)
🫘 Green beans, as needed (washed and trimmed)
🌱 Soybean sprouts, as needed (washed)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)
🧄 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (whole)
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (whole)
⭐ Star anise, as needed (whole)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (used in two batches)
🧴 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, 20 ml
🦪 Oyster sauce, 20 g
🧂 Salt, 2 g
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 3 g
🧂 Thirteen-spice blend, 3 g
💧 Water, as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🔗 Lid
🥢 Chopsticks or tongs (for mixing and tossing noodles)

## Instructions
1. Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place fresh-cut noodles in the steamer basket, drizzle with a suitable amount of cooking oil, and mix evenly with your hands. Cover and steam over high heat for 20 minutes.
**Note**: Drizzling with oil and mixing evenly effectively prevents the noodles from sticking together after steaming.

2. Heat the wok over high heat, add cooking oil, and stir-fry the pork slices over medium-high heat until they turn white and are cooked through.
3. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and dried chilies. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
4. Drizzle in a suitable amount of dark soy sauce and toss to evenly coat the meat for color.
5. Add the green beans and soybean sprouts. Stir-fry over medium-high heat for about 1 minute.
6. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, and thirteen-spice blend in order. Stir-fry quickly to combine evenly.
7. Add water until it is roughly level with the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 3 minutes to infuse the flavor.
**Key**: The broth forms the base for the noodles to absorb flavor; do not use too little water.

8. Pour the steamed noodles into the wok. Quickly toss and mix until each strand is evenly coated with broth and oil.
9. Return the mixed noodles to the steamer. Steam over high heat for 5 minutes.
**Key Point**: The second steaming makes the noodles thoroughly soft and flavorful. Do not skip.

10. Remove and mix again. Plate and serve.

## Health Suggestions
This steamed noodle dish combines meat and vegetables. The fresh-cut noodles become soft and flavorful after the second steaming, making it suitable for those needing carbohydrates and vegetable intake. It is recommended to control the amount of pork belly; for those with high blood pressure or during weight loss, choose lean meat instead and avoid overly salty broth.
