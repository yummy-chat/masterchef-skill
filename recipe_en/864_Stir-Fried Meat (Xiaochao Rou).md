# Stir-Fried Meat (Xiaochao Rou)
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin, half a strip (cut crosswise into thick slices, about the thickness of two coins)
🥓 Pork belly, about 100g (freeze until firm, then slice extremely thin, about 1mm)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili peppers, 150g (cut into rolling knife pieces)
🌶️ Small red chili peppers, a few (sliced; omit if you don't want heat)
🧄 Garlic sprouts, a few stalks (smash and cut into sections, separate roots from leaves)
🫚 Ginger, 10g (smash and chop finely)
🧄 Garlic, 10g (smash and chop finely)
🧂 Edible salt, 2g (for marinating) + 1g (for cooking)
🌿 White pepper powder, a pinch
🥚 Egg white, 1/3
🍯 Dark soy sauce, a little
🌾 Cornstarch, a little
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for sliding the meat)
🧂 MSG, 1g
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1g
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce, 2g
🥄 Light soy sauce, 2g (drizzle along the edge of the wok)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪓 Wooden cutting board, chef's knife
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok (frying pan)
🥄 Wok spatula
🥅 Oil strainer (slotted spoon)
🔥 Gas stove

## Steps
### Preparation
1. Wash the pork tenderloin, squeeze out excess water, cut crosswise into slices about the thickness of two coins, and place in a bowl.
2. Add 2g salt, a pinch white pepper powder, 1/3 egg white, and a little dark soy sauce. Mix until the seasonings are absorbed.
3. Add a little cornstarch and some vegetable oil, and mix well to coat and prevent sticking.
4. Remove the skin from the frozen pork belly and slice it very thin, about 1mm.
5. Cut the Erjingtiao peppers into rolling knife pieces, slice the small red chili peppers, smash the garlic sprouts and cut into sections, and smash the ginger and garlic into fine pieces.

### Cooking
1. Heat the wok until smoking. Remove from heat, add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, and slide the marinated tenderloin slices into the oil, stirring to separate and cook until the color changes. Drain the oil and set aside.
2. Clean the wok, heat it, and coat it with a thin layer of oil. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry over low heat for about 1 minute, until they become translucent with slightly curled edges, rendering out the pork fat.
3. Add the minced ginger and garlic, Erjingtiao peppers, and small red chili peppers. Stir-fry for about 40 seconds, releasing the aromatic, spicy "wok hei."
4. Add the pre-cooked tenderloin slices. Increase the heat to medium-high and stir-fry quickly to combine the flavors of the meat and peppers.
5. Drizzle the light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Then add 1g salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, sugar, and oyster sauce. Stir-fry until evenly combined.
6. First, add the root sections of the garlic sprouts and stir-fry a few times. Then add the leafy sections of the garlic sprouts. Stir-fry quickly until just cooked through but still bright green.
7. Remove from heat and serve immediately on a plate.

## Health Advice
This dish uses a combination of pork tenderloin and pork belly, balancing tenderness with a rich, crispy texture. However, it is relatively high in fat and chili peppers. It is recommended to pair it with light steamed dishes or multigrain rice. Be mindful of salt intake; individuals with high blood pressure should consume it with caution.
