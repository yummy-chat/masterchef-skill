# Seasonal Spring Bamboo Shoot Rice & Greens

Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🎍 2 spring bamboo shoots (peeled, cut into pieces, flattened, and blanched)  
🥬 200g clover (can substitute with shepherd's purse)  
🥓 Appropriate amount of southern-style cured meat/salted pork (diced)  
🫘 Appropriate amount of fava beans (shelled)  
🍚 3 cups of rice  
🥄 1 tablespoon of lard  

🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍯 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste  
💧 Water, as needed  

## Kitchen Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥘 Stockpot/small saucepan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥢 Wooden spoon  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the bamboo shoots**: Make a slit in each bamboo shoot with a knife, peel off the outer shell, and trim away the tough root end. Flatten the shoot with the side of the knife blade, then cut into irregular small pieces.
**Note**: Irregular pieces absorb flavor more easily.
2. **Prepare the cured meat**: Dice the southern-style cured meat.
3. **Blanch**:
   - Spring bamboo shoots: Bring water to a boil, add salt, then blanch the bamboo shoot pieces to remove oxalic acid.
   - Cured meat: Place in cold water, add a drizzle of cooking wine, and bring to a boil to remove impurities and some salt.
4. **Prepare the clover**: Rinse the clover thoroughly.
5. **Stir-fry the mix**: Heat rapeseed oil in a wok, add the blanched cured meat and bamboo shoot pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the fat renders and becomes fragrant. Add the fava beans, then season with salt, light soy sauce, and a pinch of sugar. Stir-fry quickly to combine.
**Key point**: Use rapeseed oil to enhance aroma; season mainly to bring out umami.
6. **Mix and cook the rice**: Pour the stir-fried mixture along with any juices into the rice cooker over the rinsed rice. Spread it evenly. Use slightly less water than you normally would for rice, then start the rice cooking program.
**Note**: The clover will release water when added later, so use less water now.
7. **Fluff the rice**: Once the rice is cooked, use a wooden spoon to thoroughly mix the stir-fried ingredients into the rice while it is still hot.
8. **Flash-fry the clover**: In a separate wok, heat oil over high heat. Add the clover and immediately pour in a pre-mixed sauce. Stir-fry vigorously for about 30 seconds, then remove from heat.
**Key point**: Work quickly to keep the clover tender and bright.
9. **Final mix and steam**: Pour the freshly stir-fried clover into the rice mixture. Add one tablespoon of lard. Mix everything thoroughly once again. Cover the rice cooker and let it steam with residual heat for about 1 minute to allow the flavors to meld.
**Note**: Lard is the finishing touch and should not be omitted.

## Health Suggestions  
Spring bamboo shoot rice and greens is rich in dietary fiber and high-quality protein, making it suitable for a spring wellness diet. However, control the amount of cured meat and lard; those with high blood pressure should eat it sparingly. Quick-frying the clover helps retain vitamins, and slightly firmer rice aids digestion.
