# Huangpu Scrambled Eggs
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Whole eggs 5  
🌾 Starch water appropriate amount (starch and water mixture)  
🧂 White pepper appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt a little  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Whisk  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🍴 Spatula (silicone spatula)  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Crack 5 whole eggs into a glass bowl. Add white pepper, sesame oil, chicken bouillon powder, cooking wine, starch water, and a pinch of salt.  
2. Whisk all ingredients together until the egg mixture has fine bubbles on the surface. Be careful not to overmix, or the texture will become fluffy like a soufflé.  
3. Pour cooking oil into the non-stick pan and add a piece of butter. Heat until the butter melts and oil just begins to bubble. Oil temperature is crucial; if too high, the eggs will brown too quickly, turning into "fried eggs" instead of "scrambled eggs."  
4. Pour the egg mixture into the pan all at once. Do not stir immediately; let the bottom set for a few seconds until the edges turn white.  
5. Gently push the cooked egg from the edges toward the center using the spatula, allowing the uncooked egg liquid to flow to the empty space at the pan bottom. Move gently to form large, flaky curds; do not break them into small pieces.  
6. Repeat the "push and fold" process until most of the egg is set but still moist and silky.  
7. Immediately slide the scrambled eggs onto a plate; the residual heat will finish cooking to the ideal tenderness. Do not overcook to prevent the eggs from turning tough.  

## Health Advice  
This Huangpu scrambled egg dish is rich in high-quality protein, making it ideal for muscle building or as a breakfast energy boost. However, egg yolks contain relatively high cholesterol; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Control the oil temperature while cooking to maintain a silky texture.
