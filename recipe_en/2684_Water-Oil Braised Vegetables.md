# Water-Oil Braised Vegetables
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Leafy greens 300g~500g  
🧂 Cooking oil 2ml  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🧂 Oyster sauce 3ml (optional)  

## Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Pot  
🥘 Lid  
🥣 Serving dish  

## Instructions
1. Pick and wash the leafy greens thoroughly, then drain and set aside.
2. Pour 150ml of water into the pot and bring to a boil over high heat. The water level should be slightly lower than the height of the vegetables.
3. Add 3g of salt and stir until dissolved.
4. Add 3ml of oyster sauce to enhance freshness, and stir well.
5. Drizzle in 2ml of cooking oil so it floats on the surface.
6. Add the prepared vegetables and stir quickly with a spatula to coat each leaf evenly with the seasonings and oil.
7. Cover with the lid and braise over medium heat for about 1 minute.
8. Turn off the heat, remove the vegetables with a slotted spoon or chopsticks, and plate them.

⚠️ **Notes**
* Do not braise for too long, or the vegetables will become overly soft and lose their crisp texture.
* If unsure about saltiness, add a small amount of salt first, then taste before adding more before serving.
* Substituting water with chicken or bone broth can enhance the dish’s flavor.

## Health Tips
This water-oil braised vegetable dish uses minimal oil and a short cooking time to maximize the retention of dietary fiber and vitamins, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing light meals. Be mindful of the oyster sauce amount to avoid excessive sodium intake.
