# Purslane and Egg Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Purslane tender tips, appropriate amount (use only tender tips, discard the tough parts)
🥚 Eggs, 4
🫑 Green pepper, appropriate amount (diced)
🌶️ Red pepper, appropriate amount (diced)
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🍶 White vinegar, a few drops
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🌫️ Ground white pepper, a pinch
💧 Starch water, 2 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫗 Steamer
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Stainless steel basin or bowl
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Plate
🚿 Oil spray bottle

## Cooking Steps
1. **Process the purslane**: Wash and pick the purslane tender tips. Place them in the steamer basket, spray the surface with cooking oil, cover, and bring to a boil over high heat. Then reduce to low heat and steam for 2 minutes. Remove and immediately plunge into cold water to cool. Take out, squeeze dry, finely chop, and set aside.
   **Note**: Only select tender tips to ensure texture; spraying oil before steaming enhances the color; plunging into cold water after steaming helps lock in freshness; be sure to squeeze dry for better flavor absorption later.
2. **Prepare the egg mixture**: Crack 4 eggs into a bowl, add a few drops of white vinegar, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Note**: White vinegar removes the eggy smell and makes the scrambled eggs fluffier and softer.
3. **Mix the filling**: Place the chopped purslane in a bowl. Add salt, sugar, and ground white pepper. Then add the diced green pepper, diced red pepper, pour in the prepared egg mixture, and finally add the starch water. Mix well.
   **Note**: Starch water gives the eggs a smoother, more tender texture.
4. **Fry the minced garlic until fragrant**: Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a wok and heat it up. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry over low heat until the aroma is released.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning the garlic.
5. **Cook the egg pancake**: Pour the mixed egg mixture into the wok. Once the bottom is set, use a spoon to push the set parts toward the center, allowing the uncooked egg liquid to flow to the bottom. Gradually cook until set and shape into a round pancake.
   **Note**: Use the lowest heat throughout to prevent burning.
6. **Flip and cook the other side**: Once one side is set, place a plate over the egg pancake. Hold the plate and flip the wok to invert the pancake. Then slide the pancake back into the wok to cook the other side. Gently press the pancake with a spoon for even heating. Cook until both sides are golden brown and the interior is fully cooked.
7. **Serve**: Once the pancake is cooked, cut it into small pieces with a spatula, and plate it.

## Health Advice
Purslane is rich in Omega-3 and vitamins. Paired with eggs, it provides high-quality protein, making it suitable for people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, high cholesterol, and those needing eye care. Note that purslane is cooling in nature; people with a weak or cold spleen and stomach should not consume too much.
