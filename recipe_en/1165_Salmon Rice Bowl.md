# Salmon Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes 2 (steamed and peeled)  
🥚 Egg 1 (steamed)  
🥬 Lettuce to taste (chopped)  
🐟 Salmon about 2 packs (part diced, part sliced)  
🥭 Mango 1 (half diced, half sliced)  
🥑 Avocado 1 (half diced, half sliced)  
🥒 Cucumber to taste (sliced)  
🍚 Rice to taste  
🫒 Olive oil to taste  
🥛 Milk a small amount  
🥣 Salad dressing to taste (for mixing salad and final garnish)  
🥢 Soy sauce a little (optional for dipping)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker (for steaming rice, potatoes, and egg)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing salad and mashed potatoes)  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  

## Steps  
1. Wash the potatoes and place them along with the egg on the steaming rack of the rice cooker. Steam them together with the rice until cooked.  
   **Key point**: Use the rice cooker to steam the main dish and side dishes simultaneously. The potatoes need to be soft enough to be easily mashed with a spoon.  
2. Remove the potatoes, peel them, and place them in a glass bowl. Mash them with a spoon. Add the cooked egg, an appropriate amount of olive oil, a small amount of milk, and chopped lettuce. Mix well and set aside.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amounts of olive oil and milk based on the dryness of the mashed potatoes to achieve a smooth and not dry texture.  
3. Dice the salmon, mango, and avocado separately. Mix with salad dressing to create the salmon salad.  
4. Thinly slice the remaining mango, avocado, and salmon. Slice the cucumber. These will be used for plating the rice bowl.  
5. Fill a bowl with hot rice. Arrange the mango slices, avocado slices, salmon slices, and cucumber slices neatly on top of the rice. Then add the prepared salmon salad. Finally, squeeze an appropriate amount of salad dressing on top as a garnish.  
6. Optionally, prepare a small dish of soy sauce for dipping the raw fish slices.  

## Health Advice  
Salmon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids. Paired with avocado and mango, it provides high-quality fats and vitamins, making it an ideal energy meal for brain workers and fitness enthusiasts. Pay attention to the amount of salad dressing used to avoid excess calories. Those with sensitive stomachs can lightly pan-fry the salmon before eating.
