# Crispy Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 1 large piece of firm tofu (about 400g, cut into 1 cm cubes)  
🌾 120g flour (20g for batter + 100g for dry coating)  
🥔 About 20g potato starch (for batter)  
🥔 About 100g sweet potato starch (for dry coating)  
🧂 1 teaspoon baking powder (about 2g)  
🧂 Appropriate amount of table salt (for soaking tofu)  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dry chili powder (for final sprinkling, can substitute with pepper-salt powder)  
🧅 Appropriate amount of chopped green onions (for final sprinkling)  
💧 1 bowl of water (for batter)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking tofu and mixing batter)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for draining and scooping tofu)  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Stove  
🥘 Metal tray (for mixing dry powder)  
🍜 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the firm tofu in half horizontally, then cut into 1 cm cubes. Place in a stainless steel bowl.
   **Note**: Tofu pieces should not be too large, otherwise they will not fry through easily.
2. Add an appropriate amount of salt to the tofu cubes, then pour in boiling water to soak for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Soaking in boiling water removes the beany flavor and makes the tofu structure firmer and less likely to break.
3. Prepare the batter: In another bowl, add baking powder, flour, and potato starch. Pour in 1 bowl of water. Stir with chopsticks until a smooth, thin batter forms with no dry lumps.
4. Remove the soaked tofu and drain well. Place it in the prepared batter and continue soaking for 5 minutes.
   **Note**: This step coats the tofu surface with a thin layer of batter, which helps with the subsequent coating and frying.
5. Prepare the dry powder: In a metal tray, mix sweet potato starch and flour in a 1:1 ratio.
6. Remove the batter-soaked tofu cubes and place them in the mixed dry powder. Gently toss to evenly coat each piece, then shake off excess powder.
   **Note**: Sweet potato starch absorbs surface moisture to create a crispy crust, while the flour forms a fine batter layer.
7. Heat a wok with plenty of oil until the oil temperature reaches 180°C (350°F).
   **Note**: The oil must reach at least 180°C; lower temperatures will cause the tofu to stick together when added.
8. Temporarily remove the wok from the heat, and add the coated tofu cubes one by one to the oil.
   **Note**: Removing from heat prevents the oil temperature from rising too rapidly, which could lead to a burnt exterior and raw interior.
9. Return the wok to low-medium heat and fry for about 6 minutes, until the tofu floats and turns light golden.
   **Note**: After about 2 minutes, remove the tofu. Let the oil temperature rise back to 180°C.
10. Return the removed tofu to the hot oil for a second fry. Repeat the "remove - reheat - fry again" process a total of 3 times.
    **Note**: The purpose of repeated frying is to dry out the surface moisture, achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior, with an internal temperature above 100°C.
11. Place the tofu in a slotted spoon and shake. A crisp, clattering sound indicates it is perfectly crunchy. Remove and plate.
12. Sprinkle with an appropriate amount of dry chili powder and chopped green onions.

## Health Tips
This crispy tofu is crunchy on the outside and tender on the inside, rich in plant protein and calcium. It is a great source of high-quality protein for those on a weight-loss diet or vegetarians. However, it is high in calories after deep-frying, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balanced nutrition. Control the oil temperature during the repeated frying process to avoid excessive oil absorption.
