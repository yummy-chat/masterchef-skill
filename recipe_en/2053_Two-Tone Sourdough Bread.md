# Two-Tone Sourdough Bread
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥖 Bread flour, as needed
💧 Water from boiled beetroot, as needed
🍞 Yeast, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍽️ Kneading surface
🧺 Proofing basket
🔪 Scoring knife
🍲 Dutch oven
🔥 Oven

## Preparation Steps
1. Divide the main dough into two portions: replace part of the liquid in one portion with beetroot water and knead into a pink dough; keep the other portion in its original color and knead into a white dough.
   **Key point**: Before the last two folds, gently mix and fold the two-colored doughs together to create a natural pattern, avoiding over-kneading that could cause the colors to fully blend.
2. Place the combined two-tone dough into the proofing basket, cover, and cold-ferment overnight in the refrigerator.
3. Preheat the oven together with the Dutch oven in advance. Turn the cold-fermented dough out onto parchment paper and score patterns on the surface with a scoring knife.
4. Place the dough with parchment paper into the preheated Dutch oven, cover with the lid, and bake in the oven.
   **Note**: Bake until the crust turns a deep golden brown.
5. Once baked, remove and let cool before slicing. Natural beetroot pigment darkens when exposed to high heat; the two-tone pattern inside the bread may appear light brown, which is normal.

## Health Tips
Beetroot water gives this sourdough bread a natural color, making it suitable for those needing iron supplements or seeking natural pigments. Cold fermentation enhances flavor, but be mindful of salt quantity to avoid affecting fermentation activity.
