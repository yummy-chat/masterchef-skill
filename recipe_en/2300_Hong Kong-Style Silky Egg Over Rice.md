# Hong Kong-Style Silky Egg Over Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Dish

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 1 jin (about 6-7 pieces)  
🥬 Chives (yellow chives) about half a jin (shredded)  
🍖 Char siu (barbecued pork) appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧂 Salt 1 and a half qian (about 4.5g, for 1 jin of eggs)  
🍬 Sugar 5 fen (about 1.5g)  
🌶️ White pepper powder 2 fen (about 0.6g)  
🫒 Cooking oil 3 qian (about 9g, to mix into the egg mixture)  
🐖 Lard appropriate amount (for greasing the pan)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for stir-frying chives)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks/Whisk  
🔪 Spatula  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍽️ Plate/Bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash and shred the yellow chives, slice the char siu, and set aside.
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add salt, sugar, and white pepper powder, then whisk thoroughly until well combined.  
**Note**: Do not add oil at this stage.
3. Add 3 qian of cooking oil to the egg mixture and stir again until the oil is fully incorporated.  
**Key point**: This step is essential for achieving silky eggs.
4. Heat the wok with an appropriate amount of oil, add the shredded chives, and stir-fry over high heat for about 30 seconds until half cooked. Remove and set aside.
5. Heat the wok thoroughly, then use lard to grease the entire inner surface to prevent sticking.
6. Stir the egg mixture again just before pouring it into the hot wok.
7. When the bottom of the egg mixture starts to set, use medium-high heat and gently push the eggs from the outside inward in a counterclockwise motion with the spatula, until they are about 70% cooked.  
**Note**: Work quickly to avoid overheating and making the eggs tough.
8. Add the stir-fried chives and quickly toss everything together using the residual heat.
9. Immediately serve over rice, and arrange the sliced char siu on the side.

## Health Advice
This dish features 6-7 eggs as the main ingredient, providing high-quality protein; the yellow chives and char siu add flavor and supply iron. It is suitable for growing teenagers and those needing muscle gain. Although the lard used for greasing the pan enhances flavor, individuals with high blood lipids can substitute it with vegetable oil.
