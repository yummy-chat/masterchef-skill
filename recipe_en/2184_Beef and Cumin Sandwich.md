# Beef and Cumin Sandwich
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank 1.4 kg (with some fat)
🥩 Beef brisket 0.9 kg (with some fat)
🫚 Ginger 80 g (sliced)
🧂 Salt 75 g (for curing) + about 16 g (added gradually during braising)
🌶 Dried chili peppers 5 g
🍬 White sugar 35 g (for caramelizing)
🫘 Light soy sauce 30 g
🫘 Dark soy sauce about 15 g (added later for color adjustment)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 5 g (for curing) + 5 g (ground for spice bag)
🍂 Star anise 3 (ground)
🌳 Cinnamon sticks 2 pieces (ground)
🍃 Bay leaves 3 (ground)
🌸 Cloves 6 (ground)
🌰 Cardamom 1 (ground)
🌾 Cumin seeds 8 g (ground)
💧 Water 1.25 kg (for curing brine) + enough boiling water (for braising)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl/container (for curing)
🔗 Tongs
🥄 Spoon
🥘 Deep pot/braising pot
🌀 Grinder (for grinding spices)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the beef**: Cut the beef shank and brisket into large chunks and rinse clean with water.
2. **Cure the beef**: Dissolve 75 g salt and 5 g Sichuan peppercorns in 1.25 kg hot water to make a 6% brine. Let cool, then add the beef, ensuring it is fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
- **Key point**: Curing with brine helps draw out blood, infuses flavor, and tenderizes the meat.
3. **Prepare spice powder**: Grind star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cumin seeds, and the remaining 5 g Sichuan peppercorns into a fine powder using a grinder.
- **Key point**: Grinding the spices prevents biting into whole pieces and allows flavors to release more easily.
4. **Blanch**: Remove the cured beef and place it in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook until scum rises. Remove the beef and rinse off any scum in the hot water from the same pot. Drain and set aside.
- **Key point**: Do not rinse with cold water, as the temperature shock will cause the meat to tighten and become tough.
5. **Caramelize the sugar**: Heat oil in the pot and add ginger slices and 35 g white sugar. Stir over high heat until the sugar melts, then reduce to low heat and continue heating until the sugar-oil turns a clear, deep reddish-brown and forms dense small bubbles.
- **Key point**: Caramelizing gives the meat a “bright red” color; control the heat carefully. When it becomes a deep reddish-brown, immediately proceed to the next step, as overheating will cause bitterness.
6. **Stir-fry the beef and spices**: Quickly add the blanched beef chunks and stir-fry over medium-high heat to coat each piece evenly with the caramel. Then add the ground spice powder and continue stir-frying until fragrant.
7. **Braise**: Add enough boiling water, then season with 30 g light soy sauce, 8 g salt, and 5 g dried chili peppers. Bring to a boil over high heat. Taste and adjust flavor with additional salt and dark soy sauce. Reduce heat to low, cover, and braise for 2 hours and 15 minutes.
- **Key point**: Using boiling water maintains the meat’s temperature and prevents shrinkage; slow simmering over low heat is essential for tenderness.
8. **Finish**: When the beef is “tender with softened tendons” and can be easily pierced with chopsticks, remove it. Let cool slightly, then chop the meat into fine pieces using a knife. Mix in a few spoonfuls of the braising liquid for moisture and flavor. Stuff the chopped meat into toasted flatbreads and serve.

## Health Advice
Beef and cumin sandwich is rich in protein and iron from the shank and brisket, making it suitable for those with high physical energy demands or needing blood nourishment. Be mindful of the salt content, as both curing and braising contribute significant sodium; those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount used.
