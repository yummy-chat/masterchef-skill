# Water-Fried Chive Shrimp with Toon Sprouts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January - April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp (peeled and deveined), an appropriate amount (large shrimp balls recommended)  
🥚 Eggs, 10 pieces  
🌱 Toon sprouts (香椿苗), an appropriate amount  
🍒 Goji berries, an appropriate amount (soaked, for garnish)  
🍶 Yellow wine (Huangjiu), an appropriate amount (for deodorizing shrimp while blanching)  
🧂 Salt, an appropriate amount (one spoonful added to egg mixture, another amount added to cooking water for seasoning)  
🍇 White vinegar, a small amount (added to the egg mixture to remove eggy smell)  
🫒 Sesame oil, a few drops (drizzled after turning off the heat to enhance aroma)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowl (or stainless steel basin)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Spatula  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  

## Preparation Steps
1. Slit the back of each shrimp without cutting through, making them easier to cook and visually appealing.
2. Fill the wok with water and heat to 80–90°C. Pour in yellow wine, add the slit shrimp, and blanch over medium heat for about 15 seconds. Immediately remove with a slotted spoon once the shrimp curl and slightly change color.  
   **Key point**: Blanch briefly to prevent the shrimp from becoming tough.
3. Gently pat the shrimp dry with kitchen paper towels.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the shrimp are thoroughly dried; otherwise, it will affect the integration of shrimp with the egg mixture and the final texture.
4. In a bowl, crack 10 eggs. Add one spoonful of salt and a small amount of white vinegar. Whisk vigorously until the egg mixture is even.
5. Add the dried shrimp to the egg mixture and mix well.
6. In a separate wok, add an equal amount of water to the egg mixture. Bring to a full boil over high heat.  
   **Key point**: The ratio of egg to water is 1:1, which is crucial for success.
7. Once the water is boiling, add an appropriate amount of salt. Keep the heat on high and slowly pour the shrimp-egg mixture into the boiling water.  
   **Key point**: The water must be boiling when the egg mixture is added to allow the protein to coagulate quickly, resulting in a tender texture.
8. Immediately use a spatula to gently but continuously push from the bottom of the wok in one direction until the egg coagulates into flocculent or lumpy pieces.  
   **Note**: The action must be gentle and continuous to prevent sticking and burning, and to make the eggs fluffy and even.
9. Once most of the egg is set with a little liquid remaining, sprinkle in the toon sprouts and toss gently a few times, allowing residual heat to wilt them slightly.
10. Turn off the heat immediately, drizzle in a few drops of sesame oil, and stir briefly to combine. Plate and serve.  
    **Key point**: Adding sesame oil after turning off the heat maximally preserves its aroma, removing any off-flavors and enhancing fragrance.
11. After plating, garnish the surface with the soaked goji berries.

## Health Advice  
Shrimp is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, and when paired with the seasonal nutrition of toon sprouts, it is suitable for boosting immunity in spring. Eggs are nutritious but high in cholesterol; it is recommended to limit daily intake to one egg. This recipe uses 10 eggs, suitable for sharing among multiple people, so be mindful of controlling individual serving sizes.
