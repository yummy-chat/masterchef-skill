# Rose Flower White Bean Paste Cookies
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Low-sugar White Bean Paste 500g
🥚 Egg Yolks 2
🥜 Almond Flour Approximately 2 measuring cups (sift before adding)
🍓 Fruit and Vegetable Powder/Food Coloring As needed (such as raspberry powder, strawberry powder, pumpkin powder, etc., for coloring)
🍵 Matcha Powder As needed (for green leaves)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing Bowl
🥄 Silicone Spatula
🔍 Flour Sifter
🛍️ Plastic Wrap
👝 Piping Bag
🌸 Rose Piping Tip (such as Bingcheng sd806 or sd847)
📄 Parchment Paper
✂️ Scissors
📦 Sealable Bag
🔥 Sealing Machine
♨️ Oven or Air Fryer

## Steps
1. Place the low-sugar white bean paste into a mixing bowl, add the egg yolks, sift in the almond flour, and stir with a silicone spatula until completely uniform.
   **Note**: The almond flour must be sifted to prevent particles from clogging the piping tip; after mixing, immediately cover the surface tightly with plastic wrap to prevent it from drying out and forming a crust.
2. According to the desired colors, divide the base batter into several portions, add fruit and vegetable powder or matcha powder respectively and mix well. The green batter is used for making leaves.
3. Cut the parchment paper into small squares. Fit the piping bag with the rose piping tip. Cut a V-shaped opening in the piping bag for the green batter. Fill the respective piping bags with the corresponding batters and expel any air from the tip.
4. Piping the rose: Pipe a small amount of bean paste onto the center of the parchment paper to secure it; hold the piping tip with the pointed end facing up and the wide end facing down, pipe a small cone and swirl it to form the center of the flower; around the center, pipe the first layer of 3 petals. For each subsequent layer, start piping from about 1/3 of the previous petal, with the petals gradually extending upward and outward. The angle of the outer petals can be slightly tilted downward; after finishing, you can gently pinch the edges of the petals to make them pointed.
   **Key Points**: The consistency of the batter should be moderate; the overlapping layers and angles of the petals determine the final shape.
5. Use the green batter to pipe a leaf on each side at the base of the rose.
6. Arrange the finished cookies on a baking sheet. Oven: Dry at 110℃ for about 30 minutes; Air Fryer: Bake at 110℃ for 20 minutes, flip over and bake for another 5 minutes, until the moisture is removed and the cookies are firm and set.
   **Note**: Bake at a low temperature throughout to preserve the color and shape.
7. After completely cooling, place into a sealable bag and seal with a sealing machine. In a dry, room temperature environment, sealed they can be stored for about one month; if unsealed and placed directly in a box, they can be stored at room temperature for about three days.

## Health Advice
These rose cookies use white bean paste instead of butter, making them low in sugar and fat, suitable for those needing to control their calorie intake. Note that almond flour must be sifted, and low-temperature baking will help maintain the color and shape.
