# Two-Tone Pudding
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍬 Red jelly sheets, as needed  
🍬 Yellow jelly sheets, as needed  
🍎 Pomegranate, as needed  
🍊 Orange, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Non-stick pan  
🥄 Spoon  
🥤 Empty beverage bottle  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the red jelly sheets and yellow jelly sheets in two separate heat-resistant bowls.  
2. Bring water to a boil in a non-stick pan, then reduce to medium-low heat. Place the heat-resistant bowls in the boiling water to heat indirectly (double boiler method), while stirring continuously with a spoon until the jelly sheets are completely melted and form a smooth liquid.  
   **Key point**: Indirect heating prevents direct burning; continuous stirring ensures no undissolved particles, resulting in a finer texture.  
3. Carefully pour the melted pink liquid and yellow liquid into a homemade mold.  
4. Place the mold in the refrigerator to chill until the liquid has fully set into a pudding-like consistency.  
5. Remove the set two-tone pudding and decorate the surface with pomegranate seeds and orange segments.  
   **Key point**: Decoration enhances visual appeal and flavor complexity.

## Health Advice  
Two-tone pudding mainly consists of jelly sheets and fruit, with a high sugar content. It is suitable as an occasional dessert, and portion control is necessary to avoid blood sugar fluctuations. Children and diabetics should consume it with caution; it is recommended to pair with unsweetened yogurt to balance the sweetness.
