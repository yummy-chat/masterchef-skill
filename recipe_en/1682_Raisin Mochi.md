# Raisin Mochi
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍇 Raisins,适量 (soaked in boiling water until soft, drained and chopped)
🍠 Tapioca starch, 100g (do not pack down)
🌾 Flour, 25g (any type of flour works)
🥛 Milk, 120g (can be substituted with water)
🧈 Oil, 30g (butter or odorless vegetable oil)
🧂 Salt, 1g (about a small pinch)
🍬 White sugar, 24g (optional)
🥚 Egg,适量 (adjust amount based on batter consistency)
🫘 Black sesame seeds,适量

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Saucepan or small pot
🥄 Spatula or silicone spatula
🖐️ Piping bag
🔥 Oven or air fryer
📝 Baking tray (with parchment paper)
🔪 Knife
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Instructions
1. **Prepare raisins**: Soak raisins in boiling water for about 8 minutes until softened. Remove and pat dry with kitchen paper towels. Chop finely and set aside.
   - **Key point**: Rehydrating the dried fruit prevents burning during baking; ensure it is thoroughly drained to avoid affecting the batter consistency.
2. **Prepare dry ingredients**: Weigh out 100g tapioca starch and 25g flour separately. Set aside. Do not pack down the tapioca starch.
3. **Prepare roux**: Place flour, milk, oil, salt, and sugar into the pot. Stir until well combined. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens into a paste, resembling glue.
   - **Key point**: Mix thoroughly before heating to prevent lumps; stir constantly to avoid scorching the bottom.
4. **Combine tapioca starch**: Remove from heat and immediately add all the tapioca starch. Use a spatula to quickly stir until a smooth, dry dough forms.
   - **Key point**: Use the residual heat of the roux to gelatinize the tapioca starch, creating stickiness. If the dough is too cool to mix evenly, heat over the lowest heat briefly while stirring to help it combine.
5. **Add egg mixture**: Let the dough cool until it is warm to the touch (not hot). Gradually add beaten egg in portions, mixing thoroughly after each addition, until the batter slowly drips from the spatula and forms an inverted triangle shape when lifted.
   - **Key point**: The amount of egg is not fixed; adjust based on the final batter consistency. The dough must cool before adding egg, or it will cook the egg into curds.
6. **Add mix-ins**: Fold the chopped raisins and black sesame seeds into the batter until evenly distributed.
7. **Shape**: Transfer the batter to a piping bag. Snip a small opening. On a baking tray lined with parchment paper, pipe out small, even mounds vertically, trying to make them round.
8. **Bake**:
   - **Oven method**: Place in a preheated oven at 190°C (375°F) on the middle rack. Bake for about 12 minutes, until the surface is lightly golden and the bottom is browned.
   - **Air fryer method**: Place in the air fryer basket. Bake at 160°C (320°F) for about 13 minutes. Once the surface is colored, flip and bake for another 3 minutes.
   - **Key point**: Oven and air fryer heat varies; adjust temperature and time as needed.

## Health Tips
These raisin mochi, made with tapioca starch and a roux technique, have a chewy texture and are great for an occasional treat. However, they are relatively high in calories and carbohydrates. During weight loss, it is recommended to eat only 3-4 pieces per serving. Raisins and black sesame seeds can provide iron and calcium, but be mindful of the sugar and oil intake.
