# Braised Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 **Ingredients**  
- 🍗 Fresh chicken wings 500g (about 8-10 pieces, preferably middle sections)  
- 🫚 Ginger slices 4-5 slices (about 10g, slice thickness about 2mm)  
- 🧅 Scallion segments 2-3 pieces (about 15g, each segment about 3cm long)  
- 🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (about 15g)  
- 🍂 Bay leaf 1 piece  
- ⭐ Star anise 1 piece  
- 🌶️ Dried chili peppers 2 pieces (optional, about 5g)  
- 🍬 Rock sugar 15g (in pieces)  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- 🍶 Yellow wine or cooking wine 35ml (yellow wine offers a richer flavor)  
- 🥄 Light soy sauce 15ml  
- 🥄 Dark soy sauce 5ml  
- 🧂 Salt 3g  
- 🍬 White sugar 2g  
- 🌫️ Ground white pepper 1g (white pepper recommended)  
- 🫒 Cooking oil 10-15ml  
- 🌱 White sesame seeds (toasted) 3g  
- 🌿 Chopped scallions 5g  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- 🔪 Knife  
- 🍲 Stockpot  
- 🍳 Wok or frying pan  
- 🥢 Chopsticks  
- 🥄 Spatula  

📝 **Cooking Steps**  
1. Clean the chicken wings thoroughly. Make three slits on the thickest part of each wing and one small stab on the back side.  
2. If the chicken wings are not fresh, soak them in ginger water for 10-20 minutes after scoring.  
3. Prepare ginger slices (3-4 slices), scallion segments (2-3 pieces); lightly crush the garlic cloves with the flat side of a knife; set aside bay leaf, star anise, and dried chili peppers.  
4. In a pot, place the chicken wings in cold water. Add 20ml yellow wine (or cooking wine) and 2 ginger slices. Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling for 2-3 minutes. Skim off any foam.  
5. Remove the chicken wings and rinse twice with warm water to completely remove any residue. Drain well.  
6. Heat another pot or wok over high heat, then pour in 10-15ml cooking oil.  
7. When the oil reaches about 50% heat, add the rock sugar. Cook over high heat until fully melted, then reduce to low heat and continue stirring until the sugar turns a jujube-red color. Quickly add the chicken wings and stir-fry vigorously for 1-2 minutes, ensuring each wing is evenly coated with the caramel color.  
8. Add the bay leaf, star anise, dried chili peppers (optional), remaining ginger slices, scallion segments, and the crushed garlic. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until the aromatics are fully released.  
9. Pour 15ml yellow wine (or cooking wine) and 15ml light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly for 10 seconds.  
10. Add warm water (about 50-60℃), just enough to barely cover the chicken wings.  
11. Add 5ml dark soy sauce, 3g salt, 2g white sugar, and 1g ground white pepper. Gently stir with a spatula to combine.  
12. Cover with a lid and braise over medium heat for 7-8 minutes.  
13. Remove the lid. Use chopsticks to pick out and discard the bay leaf, star anise, ginger slices, scallion segments, and garlic.  
14. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry continuously until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy (about 1-2 minutes).  
15. Turn off the heat. Transfer the chicken wings to a serving plate. Drizzle the remaining thick sauce from the wok over the top.  
16. Sprinkle with toasted white sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Serve.  

## Health Advice
Braised chicken wings are rich in protein and carbohydrates. The addition of ginger, scallion, and garlic aids digestion, making it suitable for moderate consumption by fitness enthusiasts. It is advisable to control the amount of rock sugar and soy sauce used; those with high blood pressure should reduce salt intake.
