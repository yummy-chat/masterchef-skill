# Soft Flour Pancake 🥞
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g (for soft dough)
🍚 Yeast 10g
🧂 Baking powder 5g
💧 Water 800ml
🫒 Cooking oil 30ml (for mixing dough)
🥜 Roasted peanuts, as needed
🫘 Roasted sesame seeds, as needed
🍬 Brown sugar, as needed (for filling)
🌾 Flour (for filling), a small amount
🍬 White sugar 20g (for mixing dough)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (to prevent sticking on surface)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🟫 Square mixing bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula
⚙️ Food processor
⭕ Round pancake presser
🍳 Electric frying pan or non-stick pan
📦 Food storage container with lid
🧤 Disposable gloves
🎞️ Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix flour, yeast, baking powder, and white sugar. Add cooking oil and water. Stir with a silicone spatula until a wet, soft dough forms with no dry flour left.
   **Key point**: The dough has a high water content and does not require hand kneading.
2. Spread a suitable amount of cooking oil evenly over the dough surface. Flatten the dough, cover with a lid, and let it rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Resting helps gluten develop naturally.
3. Grind the roasted peanuts and roasted sesame seeds into a powder using a food processor. Mix with brown sugar and a small amount of flour until evenly combined to form a brown sugar filling.
   **Note**: Can also be substituted with meat filling or vegetable filling.
4. After resting, lightly arrange the dough, cover with the lid, and let it ferment at room temperature until doubled in size.
   **Doneness check**: The fermented dough has visibly expanded, shows a honeycomb structure inside, and feels very soft to the touch.
5. Take out a portion of the fermented dough. Wearing gloves lightly greased with oil to prevent sticking, take a small piece of dough, flatten it in your palm, wrap in a suitable amount of filling, pinch the edges tightly to seal, and shape into a ball.
   **Key point**: The dough is very soft; applying oil easily prevents sticking.
6. Preheat the frying pan in advance and pour in a suitable amount of oil. Place the raw dough ball in the pan, fry briefly, then use the pancake presser to flatten it into a round pancake. Fry until one side is golden brown, then flip. Continue pressing the other side flat with the presser until both sides are golden brown.
   **Doneness check**: The pancake is fully cooked when the center puffs up.
   **Key point**: Use slightly more oil; the presser helps achieve an even pancake shape. Adjust the frying heat as needed.

## Health Suggestions
Soft flour pancakes have a high water content and are easier to digest after fermentation, making them suitable for people with weaker digestive systems in moderate amounts. However, the brown sugar filling and frying process increase the calorie content. For those on a weight-loss plan, it is recommended to fry with less oil and reduce the amount of filling used.
