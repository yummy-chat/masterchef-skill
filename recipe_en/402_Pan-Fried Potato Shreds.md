# Pan-Fried Potato Shreds
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 1 potato (medium-sized, peeled)
🥚 1 egg (yolk only)
🌾 A small amount of flour
🧂 A pinch of salt
🧂 A pinch of MSG

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🍽️ Plate
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Slice the peeled potato into thin slices, then cut into even, thin shreds.
**Note**: The potato shreds should be as even and long as possible.

2. Place the cut potato shreds directly into a large bowl. Do not rinse with water, as retaining the starch helps with coating and pan-frying for better shape.
3. Add a pinch of salt and MSG, then add the egg yolk. Mix evenly by hand, ensuring each potato shred is coated with the yolk and seasonings.
4. Add a small amount of flour and mix again by hand, until a thin, sticky layer forms on the surface of the potato shreds.
**Note**: Use less flour rather than more to avoid affecting the texture.

5. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the pan and heat it. When the oil is hot, add the seasoned potato shreds. Use low heat and immediately flatten and spread the shreds evenly with your hands, shaping them into a round patty of uniform thickness.
6. Keep frying over low heat until the bottom is set. When you can shake the pan and the patty moves as a whole, and the bottom is golden brown.
7. Flip the patty: Professionals can use a large flipping technique; at home, carefully flip it using a plate or spatula. The motion should be decisive to keep the patty intact.
8. Continue frying over low heat after flipping until the other side is golden brown and crispy, with the entire patty showing a beautiful golden color.
9. Once done, slide the entire potato shred patty onto a plate.

## Health Tips
Potatoes are rich in potassium and dietary fiber, making them ideal for replenishing energy in a fast-paced lifestyle. Using only egg yolk and frying with less oil over low heat makes this dish lighter than traditional deep-frying. However, those with weak spleen or stomach should avoid overeating to prevent bloating.
