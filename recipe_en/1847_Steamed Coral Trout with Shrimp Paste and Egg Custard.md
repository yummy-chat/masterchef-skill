# Steamed Coral Trout with Shrimp Paste and Egg Custard  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 coral trout (deboned, flesh reserved, bones and head kept)  
🍤 Approximately 150g shrimp (minced to make shrimp paste)  
🥚 3 eggs  
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallions (for scallion-ginger juice and garnish)  
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (for scallion-ginger juice)  
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt  
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper  
🥔 1/2 tablespoon potato starch (about 15g)  
🥚 1 egg white  
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil (for greasing fish bones and final drizzling)  
💧 Appropriate amount of warm water (approximately 1:1 ratio with the egg mixture)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Several bowls  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Deboned the coral trout, slice the flesh into butterfly cutlets, and soak in clean water until the fish pieces turn translucent.  
**Key point**: The fish pieces should remain connected without breaking, to facilitate wrapping the filling later.  
2. Squeeze the water out of the fish pieces, then add salt, white pepper, scallion-ginger juice, and half a tablespoon of potato starch. Mix thoroughly until evenly coated.  
3. Flatten the shrimp with a knife and mince into a paste. Add salt, white pepper, egg white, potato starch, and scallion-ginger juice. Mix well and repeatedly slap the mixture against the bowl until it becomes firm and elastic, making the shrimp paste.  
**Key point**: Slapping makes the shrimp paste more elastic and sticky.  
4. Beat the eggs, add salt and warm water, and whisk until well combined.  
5. Arrange the fish head and bones on a plate for presentation, sprinkle with a little salt and brush with oil. Fill the shrimp paste into the fish slices, roll them into fish rolls, and arrange neatly around the fish bones.  
6. Once the steamer water is boiling, place the plate inside and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.  
7. Remove from the steamer, slowly pour in the egg mixture, then switch to medium-low heat and continue steaming for 5 minutes, until the egg mixture sets into a smooth custard.  
**Key point**: Use medium-low heat to prevent the custard from overcooking and forming a porous texture.  
8. After removing from the steamer, sprinkle with chopped scallions and minced red bell pepper. Heat cooking oil until hot, then pour it over to release the aromas.  

## Health Tips  
Coral trout and shrimp paste are rich in high-quality protein. Paired with steamed egg custard, they are easy to digest and absorb, making them suitable for fitness recovery or post-surgery nourishment. Note that the marinating time for the fish slices should not be too long, to avoid affecting the flavor.
