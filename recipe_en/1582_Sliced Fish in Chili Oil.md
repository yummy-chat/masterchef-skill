# Sliced Fish in Chili Oil
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 1 black fish (about 1.6 jin, choose a variety with few bones and tender flesh)
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed
🫘 Tofu skin, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🥬 Leaf lettuce, as needed
🍄 Enoki mushrooms, as needed (yellow variety)
🧂 2 spoons salt (for marinating fish) + 2 spoons (for cooking soup)
🧅 Scallion and ginger water, about 3/10 of the fish's weight (add in two batches, made by soaking crushed scallions and ginger in water)
🌽 Cornstarch, a small amount (for marinating fish)
🌶️ 1 spoon Doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
🌶️ 1 spoon chili powder
🍲 1 small piece hot pot base (optional)
🍶 Shaoxing wine, as needed
🥢 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍚 White pepper powder, as needed
🧂 MSG, as needed
🧅 Chopped scallions, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (a mix of rapeseed oil and lard is better)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowls/basins
🔥 Induction cooker or gas stove

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the black fish**: Blanch the fish with boiling water and scrape off the surface mucus. Cut off the head, fillet the two sides along the backbone, remove the rib bones, then slice the fillets into thin pieces against the grain.
**Key point**: Scraping off the mucus prevents slipping; slice the fillets as thin as possible.
2. **Prepare the ingredients**: Chop the fish bones and head into small pieces, wash and drain. Rinse the fish slices and squeeze them dry. Cut the tofu skin into strips, slice the leaf lettuce diagonally, and wash the bean sprouts and enoki mushrooms.
**Key point**: The fish slices must be squeezed dry, otherwise, they cannot be properly coated.
3. **Marinate the fish slices**: Add salt to the fish slices. Add scallion and ginger water in two batches, stirring each time until the fish absorbs the liquid and becomes sticky. Finally, mix in a small amount of cornstarch.
**Key point**: Adding water in batches and stirring is key to tender fish; add cornstarch last to seal in the moisture.
4. **Fry the fish bones**: Heat the wok and add oil. Once hot, add ginger slices and fish bones. Fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown, then set aside.
**Key point**: The wok and oil must be hot enough to prevent sticking; frying the bones is key to a rich soup.
5. **Prepare the soup base**: Add fresh oil to the wok. Sauté scallion segments, ginger slices, and garlic cloves until fragrant. Add Doubanjiang and chili powder, stir-frying over low heat until the oil turns red and fragrant.
**Key point**: Stir-fry slowly over low heat to prevent the paste from burning.
6. **Cook the fish soup**: Return the fried fish bones to the wok. Splash in Shaoxing wine and light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, stir-fry a few times. Add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt, white pepper powder, and MSG. Reduce to medium heat and cook for about 5 minutes, until the soup thickens.
**Key point**: Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat to develop a rich broth.
7. **Cook the vegetables**: Add all the prepared vegetables to the soup. Cook over high heat for about 2 minutes until just tender.
8. **Prepare the serving bowl**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the vegetables and fish bones from the soup, and spread them at the bottom of a large bowl.
9. **Cook the fish slices**: Bring the soup in the wok back to a boil. Add the fish slices one by one. Do not stir immediately after adding; gently shake the wok to prevent sticking. Cook until all the fish slices have changed color, then turn off the heat.
**Key point**: Do not stir the fish slices vigorously after adding them, as this can cause the coating to come off and the fish to break.
10. **Assemble and aromatize**: Pour the fish slices and soup into the bowl over the vegetables. Top with chopped scallions, minced garlic, dried chili segments, Sichuan peppercorns, and white sesame seeds. In a separate wok, heat oil until smoking, then pour it over the toppings to release their aroma.
**Key point**: The final step of pouring hot oil is the finishing touch, fully releasing the flavor of the spices.

## Health Advice
Sliced fish in chili oil is spicy, fragrant, and numbing. The fish is rich in high-quality protein, and when paired with bean sprouts, tofu skin, and other vegetables, it offers a balanced nutritional profile. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy food while managing calorie intake. However, individuals with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs should consume with caution to avoid irritation.
