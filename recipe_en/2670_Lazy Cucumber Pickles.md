# Lazy Cucumber Pickles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Cucumber 1000g (wash and dry, remove seeds and cut into strips)  
🫑 Thin-skinned green peppers about 2 (pat flat and slice diagonally into strips)  
🌿 Cilantro 2 (remove leaves, keep roots, cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic 80g (slice)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers 50g (cut into rings)  
🍬 White sugar 100g  
🥄 Light soy sauce (or Donggu Yipinxian) 12 tablespoons  
🍯 Black vinegar 11 tablespoons  
🍯 Dark soy sauce 2 tablespoons  
🧂 MSG 1 teaspoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥄 Spoon  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash the cucumbers and dry the surface thoroughly. Cut off both ends, split each cucumber lengthwise in half, then cut each half into four long strips.  
   **Key point**: Use the knife to remove the soft seed core from each strip. This step is crucial for ensuring the finished pickles remain crunchy.  
2. Cut the seedless cucumber strips into bite-sized pieces and place them in a bowl. Add all of the white sugar.  
   **Note**: When mixing, do so gently by lifting from the bottom of the bowl upward, to avoid breaking the cucumber pieces. Mix until the sugar is mostly dissolved and the cucumber surfaces release water.  
3. Add the prepared chili pepper strips, cilantro sections, sliced garlic, and small chili pepper rings to the bowl.  
4. Add the light soy sauce, black vinegar, dark soy sauce, and MSG in order. Put on gloves and thoroughly mix all ingredients and seasonings by hand.  
5. Cover and move to the refrigerator to marinate overnight.  
   **Key point**: During marination, the cucumbers will continue to release water, increasing the amount of liquid. This pickled vegetable is not very salty. It is recommended to consume the prepared batch within one week, as it does not keep well for long.

## Health Advice
This cucumber pickle is pickled with sugar and vinegar, offering a crunchy and appetizing texture. However, it is high in sugar. It is recommended to halve the white sugar or use a sugar substitute. Suitable for stimulating the appetite in summer, but those managing sugar intake should consume in small amounts. Removing the cucumber core makes it more tender and crunchy. Paired with peppers and garlic, it is rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making it a relatively healthy side dish.
