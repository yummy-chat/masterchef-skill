# Old Shanghai Borscht
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef brisket or beef ribs, about 1 lb (cut into large chunks)  
🧅 Purple onion, half (cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot, 1 (cut into small pieces)  
🥔 Potato, moderate amount (cut into chunks larger than the carrot)  
🍅 Tomato, 2 (cut into chunks)  
🥬 Cabbage, moderate amount (cut into chunks)  
🥓 Shanghai red sausage, moderate amount (sliced)  
🫚 Ginger, a few slices  
🌿 Bay leaves, 1–2 pieces  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
⚫ Black pepper, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (can be slightly more)  
🥫 Tomato paste, 2 tablespoons  
🧈 Butter, 10g  
🌾 Flour, 10g  
🍾 Worcestershire sauce, a small amount (added just before serving)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Cast iron pot  
🔥 Induction cooktop  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Stockpot/wok  
🥄 Spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Pour a small amount of cooking oil into the pot. Heat over high heat until the oil is hot. Add the beef chunks and sear until the surface is browned and fragrant.  
**Note**: Choose beef brisket or beef ribs with a balance of lean and fat. Fresh beef does not need to be blanched; searing directly helps remove any gamey odor and enhances flavor.  
2. Pour enough boiling water into the pot. Add ginger slices and bay leaves. Bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam and excess oil from the surface. Cover with a lid, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is tender.  
**Note**: Using a cast iron pot with good sealing reduces liquid loss.  
3. In a separate pot, pour a small amount of cooking oil. Over high heat, first stir-fry the onion pieces until fragrant. Then add the carrot pieces and potato pieces and stir-fry. Finally, add the tomato pieces and 2 tablespoons of tomato paste. Cook until the tomatoes release their juice and all ingredients are evenly fragrant.  
4. Pour the cooked beef along with the broth into the pot with the stir-fried vegetables. Add salt, black pepper, and sugar to taste. Stir well, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes.  
5. Add the cabbage pieces and sliced red sausage. Continue simmering for about 10 minutes until all vegetables are soft.  
6. Taste and adjust seasoning, then drizzle in a small amount of Worcestershire sauce as desired. In a separate small pot, cook 10g of butter and 10g of flour together to form a butter roux. Pour the roux into the soup and stir well to thicken, making the broth rich, smooth, and slightly creamy. Serve once done.  

## Health Advice  
This borscht is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber. The beef provides iron, while the vegetables supplement vitamin C, making it suitable for warming the body in winter. Those with sensitive stomachs should note that the red sausage is relatively high in salt, and the butter and flour used for thickening can be reduced as needed.
