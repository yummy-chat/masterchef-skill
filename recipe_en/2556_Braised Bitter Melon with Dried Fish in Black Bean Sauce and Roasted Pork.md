# Braised Bitter Melon with Dried Fish in Black Bean Sauce and Roasted Pork

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Bitter Melon (Thunder God variety) 1 piece (halved lengthwise, remove seeds and white pith, slice into 5mm thick crescent-shaped pieces)  
🥩 Roasted Pork appropriate amount (sliced)  
🐟 Canned Dried Fish in Black Bean Sauce half a can (including the black beans)  
🧄 Garlic 5-6 cloves (crushed and minced)  
🌶️ Green and Red Bell Peppers a small amount (sliced into strips for color)  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount (one spoonful for blanching, adjust for seasoning)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (one spoonful for blanching, reduce for seasoning as dried fish in black bean sauce and roasted pork are salty)  
🦪 Oyster Sauce about half a tablespoon  
🐖 Lard generous amount (for stir-frying the base aromatics)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥄 Spoon  
🔥 Gas Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Blanch the Bitter Melon**: Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add one spoonful of sugar and one spoonful of salt. Add the bitter melon slices and blanch for 1-2 minutes. Remove and drain well.  
- Key Point: Blanching removes bitterness and keeps the bitter melon vibrant green; thoroughly removing the white pith inside further reduces the bitter taste.  

2. **Sauté the Base Aromatics**: Heat the wok over high heat, then add a generous amount of lard. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry over low heat until golden and fragrant. Turn to medium heat, add the sliced roasted pork, and pan-fry until the surface is slightly charred, rendering out excess fat. Then add the canned dried fish in black bean sauce and stir-fry together until aromatic.  
- Note: The roasted pork and dried fish in black bean sauce must be fully stir-fried to infuse their flavors into the oil.  

3. **Braise and Stir-Fry to Combine**: Add the blanched bitter melon slices and quickly stir-fry until evenly mixed. Add about half a small bowl of water, reduce the heat to low, cover, and braise for 1-2 minutes, allowing the bitter melon to fully absorb the savory flavors of the dried fish in black bean sauce and the richness of the roasted pork.  
4. **Season and Reduce the Sauce**: Season with sugar, salt, and oyster sauce. Stir-fry until evenly mixed, then turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Just before serving, add the green and red bell pepper strips. Stir-fry over high heat for about 30 seconds until just cooked through. Transfer to a serving plate.  

## Health Tips  
This dish pairs bitter melon with dried fish in black bean sauce and roasted pork, making it a refreshing and appetite-stimulating choice for summer. However, the combination of roasted pork and lard results in high calorie content. It is recommended that individuals with high blood pressure consume it in moderation. Adding sugar and salt during blanching helps reduce bitterness and preserve the vibrant green color while retaining a suitable bitter taste for cooling effects.
