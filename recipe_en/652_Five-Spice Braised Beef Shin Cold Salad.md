# Five-Spice Braised Beef Shin Cold Salad
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef Shin (beef tendon) 2 pieces
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (shredded)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili appropriate amount (diced)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (finely chop the roots)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced, for blanching)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections, for blanching)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching)
🌱 Five-spice powder 2 tablespoons
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon
🍬 Sugar 1 tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce 4 tablespoons (for the five-spice sauce) + appropriate amount (for mixing the beef)
🥄 Dark soy sauce 2 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
🫘 Soybean paste 2 tablespoons
🍯 Braising liquid appropriate amount (original broth from braising the beef)
🌶️ Chili oil appropriate amount (can be substituted with chili sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok/Soup pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍚 Rice cooker
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥢 Tongs

## Instructions
1.  Place the beef shin in a pot of cold water, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 10 minutes. Remove and rinse thoroughly under clean water.
    **Key point**: Starting in cold water helps draw out blood and gamey flavors; rinsing removes surface impurities.

2.  In a bowl, combine five-spice powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, salt, sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and soybean paste. Mix well to form a five-spice sauce.
    **Key point**: The ratio of seasonings directly affects the flavor; mix according to the proportions.

3.  Place shredded onion, diced bird's eye chili, minced garlic, and finely chopped cilantro roots into a bowl and set aside.

4.  Put the blanched and rinsed beef shin into the rice cooker's inner pot. Pour the five-spice sauce over it, then add enough water until it reaches the same level as the beef.
    **Key point**: Do not use too much water; the level should be even with the beef.

5.  Cover the lid and start the "Cook Rice" mode, simmering for about 40 minutes.

6.  After cooking, if not serving immediately, let the beef soak in the braising liquid for 30 minutes to enhance the flavor. Then remove the beef and let it cool completely.
    **Key point**: Soaking enhances flavor; cooling completely is essential for later shaping.

7.  Place the cooled beef in the refrigerator until it is fully chilled and firm.
    **Key point**: It must be chilled until "rock hard" so it does not fall apart when sliced.

8.  Remove the firm beef and slice it thinly.

9.  Place the sliced beef into the bowl with the prepared aromatics. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of braising liquid, light soy sauce, and chili oil. Mix well by hand.
    **Key point**: Chili oil can be substituted with chili sauce according to taste.

10. After mixing well, transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Tips
Beef shin is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for nourishing blood and energy. However, the braising liquid is relatively high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Slicing it and pairing it with onions and cilantro adds dietary fiber and aids digestion.
