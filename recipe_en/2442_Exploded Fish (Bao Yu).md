# Exploded Fish (Bao Yu)
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (about 2 jin or 1 kg)
🧂 Salt 5g (for marinating fish)
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor) 10ml
🫚 Ginger slices 20g
🧅 Scallion 20g (cut into sections)
🌿 Spring onion 30g
⭐ Star anise 15g
🌳 Cinnamon bark 10g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 3g
🍷 Shaoxing wine 50ml
🥫 Soy sauce 30ml
🌿 Cilantro 20g
🍃 Bay leaves 5 pieces
🌰 Cloves 2g
💧 Water 1000ml
🍬 Rock sugar 50g
🧂 Salt 5g (for boiling the sauce)
🍎 Aged vinegar 10ml

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🧻 Kitchen paper
🥣 Glass bowl
🕳️ Fine-mesh strainer
🍜 Bowl

## Steps
1. **Prepare the grass carp**: Scale the fish from tail to head. Cut open the belly from the vent and remove the internal organs and gills. Rinse thoroughly to remove any blood. Cut off the head and tail. Slice the fish from the back into pieces about 1 cm thick. Rinse again and pat completely dry with kitchen paper.
   **Note**: The fish pieces must be thoroughly dried to prevent oil splatter during frying.

2. **Marinate the fish pieces**: Add 5g salt and 10ml baijiu to the fish pieces. Mix well and set aside.

3. **Prepare the sauce**: Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. First, stir-fry the ginger slices until the edges turn slightly yellow. Add the scallion sections and spring onion, and continue stir-frying until the scallions are charred yellow and the spring onions turn dark green. Add star anise and cinnamon bark, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add Sichuan peppercorns and stir-fry until aromatic. Pour in the Shaoxing wine and soy sauce along the side of the wok. Then add cilantro, bay leaves, cloves, and 1000ml water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add 5g salt and 50g rock sugar. Once boiling, immediately turn off the heat and add 10ml aged vinegar. Strain out the spice solids using a fine-mesh strainer and let the sauce cool.
   **Key point**: The aged vinegar must be added after turning off the heat to prevent the sourness from evaporating at high temperature.

4. **First fry**: Heat oil in the wok to 50-60% hot (approx. 160-180°C). Add the fish pieces one by one. Do not stir for about 1 minute to allow them to set. Then gently push to prevent sticking. Fry over medium heat for about 10 minutes until the surface is golden brown and fully cooked. Remove with a slotted spoon.

5. **Second fry for extra crispiness**: Increase the oil temperature to 60% hot (approx. 180°C). Add the fried fish pieces back and fry for about 1 minute until the surface is even crispier and darker in color. Remove immediately.
   **Note**: Do not over-fry during the second fry to avoid burning. This recipe does not use any starch coating to fully dry out the moisture in the fish.

6. **Soak for flavor**: Immediately immerse the piping hot fried fish pieces into the cooled sauce. You should hear a "sizzling" sound. Soak for about ten minutes to allow the fish to fully absorb the sauce, achieving an exterior that is crispy while the interior remains tender, with a savory-sweet, juicy burst.
   **Key point**: The fish must be soaked in cold sauce while still hot. The temperature difference is crucial for creating the dual texture of crispiness and juicy burst.

## Health Advice
Bao Yu is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for those needing brain nourishment or muscle gain. However, due to the frying process, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables for a balanced diet. Control the amount of salt and rock sugar used. People with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
