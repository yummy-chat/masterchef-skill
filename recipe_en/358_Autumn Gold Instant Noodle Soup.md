# Autumn Gold Instant Noodle Soup  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍜 Instant noodle cake 1 pack (use only the noodle cake, discard seasoning packets)  
🥩 Ground pork appropriate amount  
🥓 Sliced fatty beef (snow beef) a few slices (add to taste)  
🥬 Chives appropriate amount  
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten)  
🍅 Tomato about half (diced)  
🧈 Lard about 1 tbsp  
🌶️ Chili flakes to taste  
🍶 White liquor a splash  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount  
🧂 Fermented soybean paste 1 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🧂 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🌿 White pepper about 3 shakes  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tbsp  
💧 Boiling water (or chicken broth) appropriate amount  

## Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Clay pot (or regular pot)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Bowl  

## Steps  
1.  Take the instant noodle cake; discard the seasoning packets.  
2.  Place the clay pot on the stove, add one tablespoon of lard, and heat over medium heat until fully melted.  
3.  Add the ground pork, quickly break it apart with chopsticks, and stir-fry until completely changed color.  
**Note**: The pork is done when it changes from pink to grayish white.  
4.  Splash a small amount of white liquor along the edge of the pot, and stir-fry to remove any gamey smell.  
5.  Add an appropriate amount of chili flakes, turn the heat to low, and stir-fry evenly until a light toasted aroma is released.  
**Key**: Do not use high heat; let the chili flakes develop a “burnt fragrance”.  
6.  Pour in enough boiling water to submerge the noodle cake that will be added later.  
7.  Add the diced tomato, one tablespoon of fermented soybean paste, one tablespoon of oyster sauce, and two tablespoons of light soy sauce.  
8.  Turn to medium-low heat, keep the broth at a gentle simmer, and cook for about 2 minutes until the broth thickens slightly.  
9.  Add the chive segments and a few slices of fatty beef.  
10. Drizzle in the beaten egg. Once the egg begins to set slightly, gently stir.  
11. Immediately after adding the noodle cake, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let it steam for 5 minutes.  
**Key**: Be sure to turn off the heat and steam—do not cook on the stove—so the noodles become just tender and fully absorb the broth.  
12. Open the lid, use chopsticks to thoroughly mix the noodles and soup.  
13. Sprinkle with about 3 shakes of white pepper, drizzle with one tablespoon of chili oil, and serve.  

## Health Notes  
This dish is made with lard, fatty beef, and various sauces. The protein and vegetable components are decent, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to control portions and pair with light vegetables. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood lipids or on a weight-loss diet should choose carefully.
