# Homestyle Cold Dish
Cuisine: Common home cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍜 Vermicelli – appropriate amount (pre-soaked in warm water until translucent with no hard core, drained)  
🥒 Cucumber – appropriate amount (washed and cut into thin strips)  
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (washed and cut into thin strips)  
🍞 Dried tofu – appropriate amount (cut into thin strips)  
🥬 Napa cabbage – appropriate amount (washed and cut into thin strips)  
🌿 Cilantro – appropriate amount (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili – appropriate amount (cut into segments)  
🌶️ Chili flakes – appropriate amount (for oil infusion to enhance aroma)  
🫙 Hot oil – appropriate amount (heated to 70–80% hot, until slightly smoking)  
🧴 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount (for seasoning and color)  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount (for seasoning)  
🧂 MSG – appropriate amount (for umami)  
🍬 Sugar – appropriate amount (for umami and balancing acidity)  
🧴 White vinegar – appropriate amount (for acidity and cutting richness)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🪣 Large mixing bowl (for mixing and tossing all ingredients)  
🥄 Spoon (for adding seasonings and pouring hot oil)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for auxiliary tossing and picking)  
🧤 Disposable gloves (for hand mixing)  

## Instructions  
1. Place the drained, softened vermicelli into a large bowl. Add the cucumber strips, carrot strips, dried tofu strips, and napa cabbage strips in order.  
2. Pile the chopped cilantro, minced garlic, chili flakes, and dried chili segments together on top of the ingredients.  
   **Note**: Pile the chili flakes etc. together on the surface so the hot oil can concentrate to release their aroma.  
3. Heat oil in a pan until it begins to smoke slightly. Pour the hot oil directly over the chili flakes and dried chili segments. You should hear a sizzling sound and smell the fragrance.  
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature must be high enough to fully release the aroma of the chili and garlic.  
4. Immediately add the light soy sauce, salt, MSG, sugar, and white vinegar around the area where the hot oil was poured, allowing the residual heat to blend the flavors.  
5. Put on disposable gloves. Use your hands to thoroughly toss all ingredients and seasonings from the bottom up until the ingredients are evenly coated with the red oil and seasoning mixture.  
   **Key Point**: Using your hands to toss helps the vermicelli and vegetable strips absorb the dressing more fully without breaking easily.  
6. Plate and serve. The finished dish is sour and spicy, savory and refreshing, and cuts through richness.  

## Health Tips  
This is a refreshing, richness-cutting cold dish high in dietary fiber, suitable for eating alongside greasy main dishes or during weight loss. Vermicelli and vegetable strips are rich in dietary fiber, promoting intestinal motility; carrots and dried tofu already have certain nutritional value, so MSG can be reduced as desired. It is recommended to use less oil and salt. Diabetic patients should be cautious with sugar.
