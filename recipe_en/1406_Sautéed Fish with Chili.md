# Sautéed Fish with Chili

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 carp (about 3-4 jin / 1.5-2 kg)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (wrinkled chilies, Erjingtiao; add Qixing chilies or bird's eye chilies to adjust spiciness)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🥜 Crushed roasted peanuts, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🧂 White pepper powder, as needed  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🥫 Broad bean paste (doubanjiang), as needed  
🧂 Monosodium glutamate (MSG), as needed  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, as needed  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🥡 Soy sauce, as needed  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Large basin  

## Cooking Steps  
1.  Kill and clean the carp: remove scales and internal organs, rinse thoroughly. Score the back on both sides with a crisscross pattern, and cut a fork pattern near the tail.  
*Scoring helps with flavor absorption and shape retention during frying.*  
2.  Place the fish in a basin. Add bruised ginger and scallion pieces, salt, white pepper powder, and cooking wine. Rub the ginger and scallions to release juice, then evenly massage the mixture over the fish inside and out. Let it marinate for a while.  
*Massaging helps the fish fully absorb the marinade for deodorizing and flavoring.*  
3.  Heat a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the dried chilies and fry over low heat until they turn dark red (like jujube color). Remove and let cool. Press the chilies with the knife blade to crush them, then chop finely to make knife-edge chili.  
*The chilies should be fried until the color deepens but not burned; chopping them finely releases the fragrant numbing-spicy aroma better.*  
4.  Smash the ginger and garlic, then chop into fine mince. Set aside.  
5.  Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot (about 180°C). Hold the fish by the tail and first immerse the head into the oil. Once there is no violent reaction, slide the whole fish in along the edge of the wok. Fry over medium-high heat until the fish skin is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.  
*Frying the head first prevents violent oil splatter; the fish should be fried until slightly overdone for a crispy exterior and tender interior.*  
6.  Leave a small amount of the oil used for frying the chilies in the wok. Add the broad bean paste and stir-fry until fragrant and crispy. Then add the minced ginger and garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add an appropriate amount of water. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon powder, white pepper powder, a pinch of sugar, and soy sauce. Add scallion segments and bring to a boil to make a homestyle sauce.  
7.  Place the fried fish into the sauce. Reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly. While simmering, continuously spoon the sauce over the fish. Carefully flip the fish once halfway through. When the sauce is almost reduced, sprinkle in some of the knife-edge chili. Continue to simmer for a while so the chili flavor infuses.  
*Basting and flipping ensure even flavor on both sides of the fish.*  
8.  When the sauce is nearly fully reduced, insert chopsticks into the fish at a spot about one finger-width behind the gill. If you can easily lift a whole piece of flesh without the fish falling apart, carefully remove the fish and plate it.  
*This is a key technique to determine if the fish is fully cooked while keeping its shape intact.*  
9.  Thicken the remaining sauce in the wok over low heat. Add Sichuan pepper oil and pour the sauce evenly over the plated fish.  
10. Sprinkle the remaining knife-edge chili, toasted sesame seeds, and crushed peanuts over the fish. Finally, garnish with chopped scallions.  
*The final sprinkling of knife-edge chili is not heated; it releases fragrance when moistened by the hot sauce and is also excellent for mixing with rice.*  

## Health Advice  
This sautéed fish dish is rich in protein and relatively low in fat. However, the use of deep-frying and broad bean paste increases its calorie content. It is suitable for fitness enthusiasts as an occasional indulgence, but those with poor digestion should consume it with caution. When frying the fish, be mindful of the oil temperature to avoid excessive oil absorption.
