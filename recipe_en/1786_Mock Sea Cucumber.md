# Mock Sea Cucumber  
**Cuisine:** Other  
**Cooking Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍄 Black fungus – appropriate amount (soaked and softened; should be dark and thick)  
🦐 Dried shrimp – appropriate amount (soaked and finely chopped)  
🧈 Firm tofu – appropriate amount (mashed into a paste)  
🥚 Egg – 1  
🌾 Flour – appropriate amount  
🌾 Starch – appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallions – 2–3 (white part only, cut into sections)  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (enough to submerge the scallion sections)  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount  
🧂 Soy sauce – appropriate amount (used twice: for seasoning the filling and for the sauce)  
🍯 Oyster sauce – appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar – 2 tablespoons  
🫒 Scallion oil – appropriate amount (obtained from frying scallion sections; used for cooking and finishing)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍲 Steamer / steamer basket  
🍽️ Plate  

## Instructions  
1. Finely chop the soaked black fungus, finely dice the soaked dried shrimp, and mash the firm tofu into a paste.  
2. Place the chopped black fungus, diced shrimp, and tofu paste in a large bowl. Add salt, soy sauce, egg, flour, and starch. Mix until the mixture is sticky and can be formed into a ball.  
3. Take an appropriate amount of the filling and roll it into a long strip in your palm. Use chopsticks to press vertical lines on the surface to mimic the appearance of sea cucumber. Arrange on a plate.  
4. Place in a steamer. Steam over high heat until steam rises, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes. Remove and let cool, then cut into bite-sized pieces. Make several shallow cuts on each scallion section.  
5. Pour cold oil into the wok, add the scallion sections, and fry over medium-low heat until golden brown. Remove and set aside, reserving the scallion oil. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add oyster sauce and stir-fry until fragrant. Add soy sauce, cooking wine, and sugar, stirring until the mixture comes to a boil.  
6. Add the fried scallion sections and the mock sea cucumber pieces. Cook over medium-low heat, gently stirring, until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.  
   **Note**: Do not cook for too long, as the mock sea cucumber may become too soft and lose its shape.  
7. Before turning off the heat, drizzle in a small amount of scallion oil to enhance aroma and shine. Transfer to a plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This "Mock Sea Cucumber" dish uses black fungus and tofu to replicate the texture of sea cucumber. It is low in calories and rich in dietary fiber and plant protein, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or vegetarians. However, dried shrimp is high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should control the amount of soy sauce used. Note that after steaming, the cooking time should be kept short to prevent the mock sea cucumber from falling apart.
