# Braised Beef

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef short ribs 2 jin (recommended, with layers of fat and lean meat and connective tissue for good texture)  
🧅 Large pieces of scallion and ginger, to taste  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 piece  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🌰 Black cardamom 2 pieces  
🍃 Bay leaves 2 pieces  
🌶️ Dried chilies 4–5 pieces  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 30 pieces  
🍂 Hawthorn slices 4 pieces  
🌱 Angelica dahurica slices 4 pieces  
🍶 Huadiao wine 1 bottle cap (for blanching) + 1 handful (for caramelizing sugar)  
🍬 Rock sugar, to taste  
🍾 Light soy sauce 1 handful (for stir-frying) + 3 tablespoons (for dipping sauce)  
💧 Hot water, to taste (enough to submerge ingredients by about 3 cm)  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (added later during braising) + 2 grams (for dipping sauce)  
🍭 Sugar 1 gram (for dipping sauce)  
🍎 Vinegar 3 tablespoons (for dipping sauce)  
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn oil 1 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)  
🧄 Minced garlic 1 portion (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Bird’s eye chilies 1 portion (for dipping sauce)  
🧅 Scallions 1 portion (for dipping sauce)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🫕 Clay pot  
🎋 Small bamboo mat (lined at the bottom of the clay pot to prevent sticking)  
🔥 Gas stove (adjustable to the lowest flame)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking beef and preparing dipping sauce)

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Soaking and blanching**: Soak the beef short ribs in clean water for 10 minutes to draw out blood. Remove and place in a pot of cold water. Add scallion, ginger, and one bottle cap of Huadiao wine. Bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam, then reduce to medium heat and continue boiling for 3–5 minutes to fully release blood. Remove the beef and place it in a clay pot lined with a small bamboo mat.  
   **Key point**: Thorough soaking and blanching remove any gamey smell and ensure a clear braising broth.

2. **Caramelizing sugar and stir-frying**: Heat the wok over low heat, add a small amount of oil, then add rock sugar. Stir slowly over low heat until the sugar melts, bubbles, and turns a brownish-red color. Immediately pour in one handful of Huadiao wine and one handful of light soy sauce to deglaze and release aroma. Then add enough hot water.  
   **Note**: The entire caramelizing process must be done over low heat. Once the sugar changes color, add liquid immediately to prevent burning. Be sure to use hot water to avoid hot oil splattering.

3. **Braising**: Pour all the braising liquid into the clay pot and add all the spices. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest flame on the gas stove, maintaining a gentle simmer. Cook for 1.5 hours.  
   **Key point**: Do not add salt early during braising, or the beef will become tough and dry.

4. **Seasoning and soaking**: After 1.5 hours, test the beef with chopsticks. If they pierce through easily and the beef falls off naturally when lifted, it is tender. Add salt to taste at this point, and if a darker color is desired, add a little dark soy sauce. Stir well, then turn off the heat. Cover the pot and let the beef cool completely in the braising liquid to fully absorb flavor while keeping the meat tender and moist.

5. **Making the dipping sauce**: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, bird’s eye chilies, and scallions. Add 2 grams of salt and 1 gram of sugar, and mix well. Then add 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons of vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon of Sichuan peppercorn oil. Stir until well combined.

6. **Slicing and plating**: Remove the completely cooled braised beef. Slice thinly on the diagonal using a “sweeping” knife cut. Arrange on a plate and serve with the dipping sauce.

## Health Tips  
Braised beef is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for muscle building and blood replenishment. Be mindful of the salt content in the dipping sauce; those with high blood pressure can use less. The key steps are soaking and allowing the beef to fully absorb flavor after braising. Avoid adding salt too early to prevent the meat from becoming tough.
