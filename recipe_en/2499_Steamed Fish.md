# Steamed Fish
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Fish (such as sea bass) 1 piece
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced for removing fishy smell, shredded for garnish)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (whole segments for removing fishy smell, shredded for garnish)
🌶️ Red chili pepper appropriate amount (shredded for color garnish)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (brush before steaming, plus extra hot oil for final drizzle)
🥄 Steamed fish soy sauce appropriate amount (for seasoning after steaming)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
♨️ Steamer
🍽️ Oval deep plate
🖌️ Oil brush
🥄 Soup spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the fish**: Clean and gut the fish. Split it open along the backbone from the back, so both sides spread symmetrically. Cut the tail connection so the fish can lie flat and stand up.
**Key point**: Push the knife forward along the backbone and then pull back to open both sides symmetrically; cut the tail connection to allow the fish to "stand."
2. **Prepare the three shreds**: Roll the scallions tightly and cut into very fine shreds. Also prepare shredded ginger and red chili pepper.
**Note**: Soak the prepared shreds in clean water to keep them fresh.
3. **Pre-steaming treatment**: Lay the prepared fish flat on the plate. Use an oil brush to apply a thin layer of oil evenly on both sides and the inside of the fish. Place a few slices of ginger and scallion segments on top of the fish.
**Key point**: Brushing with oil prevents the fish from drying out after steaming and keeps it white.
4. **Steam the fish**: Bring the water in the steamer to a rolling boil. Place the fish plate in the steamer, cover the lid, and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.
**Doneness indicator**: The fish turns white and the eyes protrude.
   **Note**: The fish must be added only after the water is boiling.
5. **Post-steaming treatment**: Remove the fish after it is cooked. Use chopsticks to remove the softened ginger slices and scallions from the fish.
**Key point**: Removing the old ginger and scallions eliminates fishy smell and excess moisture.
6. **Prepare the sauce**: Add an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce to the broth at the bottom of the plate, pouring it in from the edge of the plate.
**Key point**: Pouring from the edge preserves the white appearance of the fish; adjust sauce color and saltiness to taste.
7. **Add garnishes**: Take the soaked three shreds (scallion, ginger, chili), drain well, and pile them on top of the fish.
8. **Drizzle with hot oil**: Heat cooking oil to about 70% hot (six parts hot). Use a soup spoon to scoop and quickly drizzle the hot oil over the three shreds on the fish.
**Key point**: The hot oil must be poured directly over the shredded garnishes to fully release their aroma.

## Health Tips
Steamed fish is low in fat and high in protein, rich in Omega-3, making it especially suitable for those on a weight loss diet or focused on cardiovascular health. Be careful not to steam for too long; remove from heat as soon as the fish eyes protrude to avoid tough, dry meat.
