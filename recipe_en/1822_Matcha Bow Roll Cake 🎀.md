# Matcha Bow Roll Cake 🎀
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍵 Matcha powder, as needed  
🔴 Red food coloring, as needed  
🥚 Cake batter, as needed  
🥛 Whipped cream, about 150g  
🧀 Cream cheese, as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🪄 Piping bag  
📦 Square baking pan  
📜 Parchment paper  
🔥 Oven  
🥄 Spatula  
🔪 Offset spatula  
🪚 Serrated knife

## Directions
1. Divide the cake batter into two equal portions. Add red food coloring to one portion to make red batter, and add matcha powder to the other to make green batter. Meanwhile, whip the whipped cream with cream cheese until ready.
2. On a baking pan lined with parchment paper, pipe the red batter into a solid bow pattern, ensuring it is filled fully to prevent deformation during baking.
3. Place the baking pan in a preheated oven and bake for about 2 minutes to set the pattern.
4. Remove the pan from the oven, pour the green matcha batter over it, and gently spread it evenly with a spatula to cover the pattern.
5. Return the pan to the oven and bake for about half an hour, until the cake is fully cooked.
6. Once the cake is out of the oven, immediately cover the surface with parchment paper to trap steam and prevent the skin from drying out, making it easier to peel off later.
7. Allow the cake to cool completely, then turn it over and peel off the bottom parchment paper to reveal the bow pattern. Trim both ends of the cake: cut one end diagonally and the other straight, so the angled end creates a smoother seal when rolled.
8. Spread a thin layer of whipped cream over the pattern side, then pile the remaining cream near one-third of the cake closer to you. Use the parchment paper to roll the cake tightly and wrap it in paper.
9. Place the rolled cake in the freezer to set quickly. Once firm, remove and slice into pieces with a serrated knife.

## Health Advice
This matcha bow roll cake is visually stunning, but the combination of matcha powder and cream cheese is not low in calories. It is best enjoyed as a small afternoon tea treat, with attention to portion control to avoid blood sugar spikes. The cake roll must be chilled until firm before slicing, or the bow pattern may fall apart.
