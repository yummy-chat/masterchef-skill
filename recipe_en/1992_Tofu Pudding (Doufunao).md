# Tofu Pudding (Doufunao)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Dried soybeans 250g (plump and full)  
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking), 2000g (for grinding)  
🧪 Glucono delta-lactone (coagulant) 8g  
🥣 Seasoned water, as needed (complex seasoning liquid)  
🌶️ Chili oil, one spoon  
🥒 Pickled mustard tuber (zha cai), diced, as needed  
🫘 Cooked soybeans, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking, holding soybean milk, and coagulating)  
🌀 Soy milk grinder  
🍲 Thick-bottomed pot  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🍽️ Plate (for covering the basin to keep warm)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Soak the dried soybeans in water overnight to allow them to fully absorb water and expand.  
**Note**: The quality of the soybeans directly affects the yield of soybean milk and the setting result.  

2. Place 8g of glucono delta-lactone at the bottom of the large basin to be used for coagulation, and set aside.  
**Note**: The amount of coagulant must be precise; too much will cause the tofu pudding to taste sour.  

3. Drain the soaked soybeans, then pour them together with 2000g of water into the grinder to grind into raw soybean milk. Add water to the soybean residue and grind again until the residue is loose, ensuring maximum milk extraction.  
4. Use a soup spoon to skim off the foam on the surface of the raw soybean milk to ensure a smooth final product.  
5. Pour the soybean milk into a thick-bottomed pot and heat over low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent scorching on the bottom.  
6. After the soybean milk comes to a boil, continue to cook over low heat for 3–5 minutes to ensure it is fully cooked and to remove the beany flavor.  
**Judgment standard**: Full boiling with no beany flavor indicates it is cooked.  

7. Turn off the heat and let the soybean milk cool slightly. When a thin “skin” forms on the surface, it is at the optimal temperature for coagulation.  
8. Slowly pour the soybean milk along the edge of the basin into the basin containing the coagulant. **Do not pour directly onto the coagulant at the bottom of the basin**. The pouring speed should not be too fast to avoid creating many bubbles.  
9. After pouring, immediately cover the basin with a plate and let it sit undisturbed for 15 minutes. Do not move or stir during this time.  
10. Remove the plate. The surface of the tofu pudding should be smooth like a mirror, and it should not break when the basin is gently shaken, indicating successful coagulation.  
11. Use a flat spoon to gently scoop the tofu pudding into a bowl, taking care to keep its shape intact.  
12. Pour in the seasoned water and chili oil in sequence, then sprinkle with pickled mustard tuber, cooked soybeans, and finally garnish with cilantro.  

## Health Advice  
Tofu pudding is high in calcium and low in fat, making it an excellent source of high-quality plant protein. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or vegetarians. However, the coagulant is high in sodium, so use less soy sauce and pickled mustard tuber. It is recommended to pair with whole grain staples for balanced nutrition.
