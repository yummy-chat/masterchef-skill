# Stir-Fried Black-Boned Chicken with Green Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 black-boned chicken (cut into slightly larger pieces for tender texture)  
🌶️ About 1 kg of Erjingtiao green chili peppers (cut into long sections)  
🫚 Ginger (to taste, smashed)  
🧄 Garlic (to taste, minced)  
🧅 Scallions (to taste, cut into sections)  
🌶️ Large amount of dried chilies (soaked in water in advance)  
🍺 1 can of beer  
🫘 A handful of green Sichuan peppercorns  
🧂 Salt (to taste)  
🍶 A small amount of high-proof liquor  
🌶️ Red chili powder (to taste)  
🫘 Half a bowl of fermented black beans  
🧂 Chicken bouillon (to taste)  

## Utensils & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl (for marinating the chicken)  

## Steps
1. Wash the black-boned chicken and cut it into slightly larger pieces.  
2. Add smashed ginger, minced garlic, scallion sections, green Sichuan peppercorns, salt, and a small amount of high-proof liquor to the chicken pieces. Mix well and marinate for half an hour.  
3. Press the Erjingtiao green chili peppers flat with the back of a knife, then cut into long sections. Soak the dried chilies in water in advance until soft, then drain.  
4. Heat the wok without oil over medium-low heat. Add the green chili sections and dry-fry, pressing them with the wok spatula. Fry until the skin blisters and the peppers soften, then remove and set aside.  
5. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until about 80% hot. Add the soaked dried chilies and the marinated chicken pieces. Stir-fry over high heat quickly until the chicken changes color and the dried chilies release their fragrance and red oil.  
6. Add the red chili powder and fermented black beans, and continue stir-frying until fragrant.  
7. Pour in the entire can of beer, bring to a boil, then reduce to medium heat and simmer until the sauce mostly thickens and the chicken is well-flavored.  
8. Add the dry-fried green chili sections, stir-fry to combine. Season with chicken bouillon and salt before removing from heat, stir well, and transfer to a serving plate.  

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein, and the vitamin C in the chili peppers helps promote iron absorption, making it suitable for those needing to enrich the blood or boost immunity. Be mindful of the amounts of liquor and salt used when marinating the chicken to avoid excessive saltiness. For those with high blood lipids, some of the chicken skin can be removed to reduce fat intake.
