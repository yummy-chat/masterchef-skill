# Tomato and Egg Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 Tomatoes: 3 (score a cross on the top)  
🥚 Eggs: 2  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms: appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger: appropriate amount (minced)  
🧅 Scallions: appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫒 Cooking oil: appropriate amount  
🥫 Tomato paste: a small amount  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon: a small amount  
🥄 Water starch: 2 tablespoons (one portion added to the egg mixture, one for thickening)  
🫗 Sesame oil: a few drops  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Stockpot  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥣 Large bowl (for cooling and mixing egg mixture)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing water starch and tomato paste)  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Score a cross on the top of each tomato, blanch in boiling water until the skin wrinkles slightly, immediately remove and immerse in cold water to cool. Then peel off the skin and slice into pieces.  
   **Key point**: Tomatoes must be peeled, otherwise the skin will float on the soup surface, affecting texture and appearance. The blanching and cooling process uses thermal expansion and contraction to make peeling easier.  
2. Trim the roots off the enoki mushrooms and cut them into small segments. Set aside.  
3. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk until well combined. Add 2 tablespoons of water starch and whisk thoroughly again.  
   **Key point**: Adding water starch to the egg mixture is key to forming cloud-like egg flower flakes.  
4. Heat the wok over high heat, add a base layer of oil, and stir-fry the minced ginger over low heat until fragrant.  
   **Note**: Sautéing the ginger removes any off-smell and purifies the soup flavor.  
5. Add the tomato slices, turn the heat to medium, and stir-fry vigorously until the tomatoes are soft and mushy, releasing red oil and juice. Add a small amount of tomato paste and stir-fry evenly.  
   **Note**: Tomato paste enhances the color and tangy flavor of the soup.  
6. Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the enoki mushrooms. Season with salt and a small amount of chicken bouillon.  
7. Once boiling again, drizzle in the water starch to thicken the soup until it becomes slightly viscous.  
8. Pour the prepared egg mixture evenly into the gently boiling soup in a scattering motion. Immediately use the back of a spoon to gently push the egg flowers apart.  
   **Key point**: After adding the egg mixture, avoid vigorous stirring over high heat; otherwise, the egg flowers will break apart and fail to form. Only use the back of a spoon to gently push.  
9. Turn off the heat, add a few drops of sesame oil, ladle the soup into bowls, and garnish with chopped scallions before serving.  

## Health Tips  
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene, which is more easily absorbed after heating. Combined with the high-quality protein of eggs, this soup is nutritionally balanced and appetite-stimulating, suitable for the whole family. Those with excess stomach acid should reduce the amount of tomato paste. If the soup is too thick, use less water starch to moderate the glycemic response.
