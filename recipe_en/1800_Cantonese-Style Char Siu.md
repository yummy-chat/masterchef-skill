# Cantonese-Style Char Siu
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, about 4 jin (skin-on, with alternating fat and lean, for fatty char siu)
🍖 Pork front leg meat, about 4 jin (predominantly lean, for lean char siu)
🧄 Garlic, 50g (minced)
🍬 White sugar, 300g
🫘 Pork rib sauce, 50g
🫘 Fermented red bean curd sauce, 50g
🫘 Chu hou paste, 50g
🫘 Hoisin sauce, 50g
🫘 Bean paste sauce, 80g
🧂 Light soy sauce, 50g
🧂 Salt, about 30g
🍯 Honey water or maltose water, as needed (for brushing after roasting; maltose must be dissolved into liquid form in advance)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🪣 Large stainless steel basin
🥢 Metal long skewer
🪝 Roasting hook (metal)
🔥 Barrel-style duck roasting oven (commercial)
📄 Oil paper or gauze paper (to wrap the head of meat strips to prevent burning)
🌡️ Oven thermometer
🥄 Large spoon, pot
🪵 Wooden cutting board (for slicing)

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. **Select Meat and Cut**
   - Fatty char siu: Choose skin-on pork belly with alternating fat and lean layers. Cut into thick slices about two fingers wide.
   - Lean char siu: Choose pork front leg meat. Remove tendons and membranes. Cut into strips of similar thickness to the pork belly. For thicker strips, split them from the middle without cutting all the way through, then pound the meat surface with the back of the knife to tenderize and allow flavor to penetrate.
2. **Prepare Marinade**
   - Pour white sugar, pork rib sauce, fermented red bean curd sauce, chu hou paste, hoisin sauce, bean paste sauce, light soy sauce, and salt into a large basin. Add the minced garlic. Mix thoroughly by hand to create a combined marinade.
3. **Marinate**
   - Place the cut fatty and lean char siu strips into the large basin. Add the marinade. Use your hands to mix thoroughly, ensuring each strip is evenly coated with the sauce.
   - Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight to marinate.
   **Note**: The marinating time cannot be shortened; 2-3 hours is insufficient for full flavor penetration.
### Cooking Phase
1. **Skewer and Shape**
   - Remove the marinated meat strips. Handle fatty and lean char siu separately.
   - Arrange the meat strips neatly on a tray. Use a long metal skewer to pierce through one end, securing the entire row of strips horizontally.
   - Attach a roasting hook to the skewer, ensuring the meat skewers hang steadily.
2. **Pre-Roast Treatment**
   - Prepare gauze paper or oil paper. Wet it with water and wrap it around the top end of each char siu skewer near the hook.
   **Key Point**: This prevents the high heat at the top of the roasting oven from burning the head of the char siu black.
3. **Roasting**
   - Light the roasting oven. Preheat to around 200°C.
   - Hang the char siu skewers inside the oven. Leave a small gap in the oven lid for temperature control.
   **Roasting Time and Doneness**:
   - **Fatty char siu**: Roast at 200°C for about 30 minutes, until the surface is glossy and the edges are slightly charred.
   - **Lean char siu**: Roast at 200°C for about 50 minutes. Because lean meat conducts heat slowly, after the fatty char siu is removed, the lean char siu needs to continue roasting for about 20 minutes to be fully cooked.
4. **Glaze with Sugar**
   - Perform immediately after the char siu is done roasting.
   - Heat the honey water or maltose water. While the char siu is still hot, hang it over the pot and use a spoon to evenly drizzle the hot sugar water over the surface of the char siu.
   - After glazing, let it rest for half an hour to allow the syrup to drip naturally and cool.
5. **Slice and Serve**
   - Place the cooled char siu on a cutting board. Slice into pieces of appropriate thickness and plate.

## Health Advice
Cantonese char siu is high in sugar and fat, suitable for occasional enjoyment and not as a daily staple. Pork belly combined with the sugary sauce results in high calories. It is recommended to pair with vegetables to balance nutrition.
