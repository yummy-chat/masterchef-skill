# Dry-Fried Meatballs
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ground Pork 500g (30% fat, 70% lean)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (sliced, for scallion-ginger water)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced, for scallion-ginger water)
🥔 Potato Starch, about 100g (ratio to meat approximately 1:5)
🧂 Ground Sichuan Peppercorn, as needed (made from peppercorns fried until nearly black and ground)
🧂 Salt, as needed (adjust to "six-tenths flavor")
🧴 Soy Sauce 20g (light soy sauce 10g + regular soy sauce 10g)
💧 Scallion-Ginger Water, about 75g (add in batches)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥄 Small Strainer (for filtering scallion-ginger water)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring and testing oil temperature)
✋ Hand (for kneading and slapping to firm)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Large Strainer
🪵 Wooden Chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Make scallion-ginger water: Place sliced scallion and ginger in a bowl, add water, and repeatedly rub with your hands until the water becomes sticky and smooth. Let it sit and set aside.
   **Note**: Fully release the scallion and ginger juice so the water has a rich, sticky-smooth texture.
2.  Add water to the meat: Filter the scallion-ginger water to remove solids, and add it to the ground meat in 2-3 batches. After each addition, stir quickly in the same direction until the water is fully absorbed. Total water added should be about 75g.
3.  Season: Add ground Sichuan peppercorn, salt, and soy sauce in order, and stir evenly.
   **Key**: Control the salt amount to "six-tenths flavor," meaning the saltiness is about 60% of the final product's saltiness; the saltiness will naturally increase after frying.
4.  Add starch and firm: Add dry starch and about 75g of water, then continuously stir and repeatedly slap the meat mixture with your hands or chopsticks until the meat becomes very sticky and elastic.
   **Note**: Do not use too much starch, or the outer crust will become smooth and the texture will become hard.
5.  First fry for shaping: Heat the oil to 50-60% hot. Use the tiger mouth shape to squeeze out the meatballs, scraping them into the oil with a spoon. You may temporarily turn off the heat when adding the meatballs to prevent uneven doneness, then turn the heat to the lowest setting and fry for 1.5-2 minutes, until the meatballs float and turn light yellow. Remove.
6.  Second fry for coloring: Increase the oil temperature to 60-70% hot, add the meatballs, and fry for another 4-5 minutes, until the surface turns a jujube red color. When lifting with a strainer, if it produces a crisp crackling sound, remove them.
   **Note**: Wait until the oil temperature is high before the second fry to achieve a crispy exterior and tender interior while forcing out excess oil.

## Health Advice
This Dry-Fried Meatball dish uses ground pork with 30% fat and 70% lean, providing rich protein and moderate fat. After the second fry, some oil has been expelled, making it suitable for energy replenishment when eaten in moderation. It is suitable for those needing muscle gain or high physical exertion, but those with high blood lipids should eat less. Note the "six-tenths flavor" principle for salt; saltiness will naturally increase after frying, so control the amount used.
