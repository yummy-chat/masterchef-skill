# Street Wok-Charred Fried Rice 🍳
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🧄 Garlic – about 1 head (minced, or used for making sauce)
🥕 Carrot – about 1/2 (diced)
🥬 Cabbage – about 2 leaves (diced)
🥚 Egg – 1-2 (beaten)
🍚 White rice – about 1 large bowl
🫒 Cooking oil – as needed
🧂 Soybean paste – 2 tablespoons
🌶️ Spicy chili sauce – 1 tablespoon
🍬 Sugar – 1 tablespoon
🧂 Flavor powder (MSG/chicken powder) – to taste
🧴 Dark soy sauce – about 1 drizzle (for coloring)
🫕 Beef tallow / bone broth oil – 1 tablespoon
🧅 Scallions – to taste (chopped)

## Tools & Equipment 🔪
🍳 Wok (Chinese iron wok)
🥄 Spatula
🔥 High-power burner
🥢 Chopsticks
🥡 Paper bowl / takeout container

## Instructions 📝
1. Place the wok over medium heat, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Once hot, add minced garlic and stir until fragrant. Then add soybean paste, spicy chili sauce, sugar, and flavor powder. Reduce heat to low and stir until well combined and aromatic. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Sugar neutralizes saltiness and enhances complex savory flavors.
2. Dice the carrot and cabbage into small pieces. Chop scallions. Set aside.
3. Heat the wok over high heat and add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Scramble the eggs quickly, then add the diced carrot and cabbage. Stir-fry until just cooked.
4. Add the white rice. Use the back of the spatula to press and break up the rice. Add 1 tablespoon of the prepared sauce and stir-fry to combine evenly.
   **Note**: Avoid stirring too frequently when the rice is first added. Let it sit on the wok’s surface to fully heat up, which helps develop the wok char (“wok hei”).
5. Drizzle dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok to enhance color. Sprinkle with flavor powder and chopped scallions, then stir-fry quickly.
6. Add 1 tablespoon of beef tallow, turn the heat to maximum, and stir-fry rapidly. Finally, lift the wok off the heat and quickly “steam” the rice in the residual heat a few times, allowing the rice to fully contact the hot wok surface.
   **Key point**: High-heat quick stir-frying and the final “steaming” off the heat are crucial for creating wok hei. The finished dish should be steaming hot and slightly smoky.
7. Serve immediately in a paper bowl for a dry, aromatic, and flavorful wok-charred fried rice.

## Health Tips 🏥
This fried rice is relatively high in oil and sauce, making it calorie-dense. It is recommended to control portion size and pair with light vegetables or soup for a balanced meal. Suitable for manual laborers or those needing a quick energy boost, but those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of sauce used.
