# Water-Braised Egg Cake
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs
💧 Water (about 1.5 times the volume of the egg liquid)
🥔 2 teaspoons potato starch water
🧄 Minced garlic, to taste
🌶️ Chili flakes, to taste
🦐 Antarctic krill meat, to taste
🧅 Scallions, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍶 2 drops white vinegar

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Spoon
🫗 Glass lid

## Cooking Steps
1.  Take a stainless steel bowl, crack in the eggs, add white vinegar and potato starch water, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is even.
2.  Heat the wok over high heat, add oil, and stir-fry the minced garlic and chili flakes over low heat until fragrant.
3.  Pour water into the wok, season with salt, and add the Antarctic krill meat.
4.  Pour the prepared egg mixture into the wok and gently stir with chopsticks to combine.
5.  Cover with a lid, reduce the heat to the lowest setting, and braise until the egg cake is fully set and solidified.
**Key Point**: Use the lowest heat throughout to prevent the bottom from burning.
6.  Turn off the heat and let it rest covered for 3 minutes to allow the residual heat to make the cake more tender and prevent sticking.
7.  Remove the lid, sprinkle with scallions, cut the egg cake directly in the wok into four wedges, then scoop out and plate.

## Health Tips
This dish is enhanced with the umami of krill and the aroma of garlic and chili, offering a tender texture. It is suitable for those needing a high-protein, low-fat diet. Control the amount of starch water to avoid affecting the texture; keep the heat low during braising to prevent burning.
