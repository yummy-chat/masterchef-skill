## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the fish**: Clean the fish thoroughly. First, make straight cuts down to the bone, then slice diagonally into the flesh and flip it up, scoring a crosshatch pattern. The number of cuts depends on the fish size; a large fish typically needs seven cuts per side.
- Key point: The scoring pattern is crucial for the dish's final appearance.
2. **Marinate**: Place the fish in a bowl, add salt, scallion segments, ginger slices, and yellow wine. Rub and massage evenly to ensure the flesh absorbs the marinade.
- Key point: Massaging helps the fish better absorb the marinade.
3. **Prepare starch slurry**: Put mung bean starch in a bowl, add cold water, and stir until smooth. Let it rest and swell before use.
- Key point: Mung bean starch must be used, and it needs sufficient resting time to swell.
4. **Prepare aromatics**: Mince ginger and garlic. Shred scallions and red and yellow bell peppers for garnish.
5. **Coat the fish**: First, evenly dust the marinated fish with dry mung bean starch. Then, dip it into the prepared starch slurry to ensure an even coating.
- Key point: Dusting with dry powder before the wet batter ensures the coating adheres firmly.
6. **First frying**: Heat oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Hold the fish by the tail, lower the head into the oil first, and use a spatula to support the head while slowly sliding the whole fish in. Fry over medium-high heat until set and light yellow, then remove.
- Key point: The head goes into the oil first, and the spatula helps shape the fish into a curved form.
7. **Second frying**: Increase the oil temperature, then return the fish for a second fry until the outside is crispy, the inside tender, and the surface golden brown. Remove.
- Key point: The second frying is essential for achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.
8. **Make the sweet and sour sauce**: Heat a clean pan with a little oil, sauté minced ginger and garlic until fragrant. Add ingredients in the "one-two-three-four" ratio: one part cooking wine, two parts soy sauce, three parts white sugar, four parts rice vinegar, plus half a spoon of salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add water starch to thicken. Finally, stir in one spoon of hot oil.
- Key point: Remember the sauce ratio; adding hot oil at the end makes the sauce more glossy.
9. **Pour sauce and plate**: Place the fried fish on a serving plate, then immediately pour the hot sweet and sour sauce evenly over it.
- Key point: Pour the sauce while both the fish and sauce are hot to release the aroma.
10. **Garnish**: Sprinkle shredded scallions and red and yellow bell peppers on top of the fish.

## Health Advice
This sweet and sour fish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Fish is rich in high-quality protein and omega-3, making it suitable for those needing brain and heart support. However, due to the frying and sweet and sour sauce's high calorie content, portion control is advised. Dusting with starch before battering is key to crispiness, and the second frying step should not be skipped.
