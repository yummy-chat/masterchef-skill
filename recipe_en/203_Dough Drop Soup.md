# Dough Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare side dishes**: Dice the tomato into small cubes, chop the scallion into scallion flowers, chop the cilantro; beat the eggs into egg liquid and set aside.
2. **Make dough drops**: Place the flour in a large bowl. Use a thin-spout kettle to slowly pour water in a thin stream while stirring quickly with chopsticks, until the flour forms flocculent or small lumps of uniform size, avoiding large dough lumps.
   **Key point**: The water stream should be thin and continuous, and the stirring speed should be fast to ensure uniform dough drops.
3. **Sauté and prepare broth**: Pour cooking oil into the pot and heat until 70% hot. Add a portion of scallion flowers and stir-fry until fragrant, then immediately pour in soy sauce to release the aroma, and add enough boiling water right away.
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should be high enough, and the soy sauce must be fried until aromatic before adding water; this is the foundation for a rich broth.
4. **Cook the tomatoes**: After the water boils, add the diced tomato and keep boiling over high heat.
5. **Season the base**: Add salt and white pepper powder to the boiling soup to create a basic savory flavor.
6. **Add dough drops**: Evenly sprinkle the dough drops into the soup in batches, gently pushing them apart with a soup spoon to prevent sticking. Boil over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the dough drops are fully cooked with no raw flour center.
7. **Drizzle in egg flowers**: Once the dough drops are cooked, drizzle the beaten egg liquid into the pot in a circular motion, and immediately gently push the soup with the back of the spoon to let the egg flowers spread evenly, avoiding breaking them up.
8. **Add finishing touches and serve**: Turn off the heat, drizzle in sesame oil, sprinkle the remaining scallion flowers and chopped cilantro, stir well, and serve.

## Health Tips
This dough drop soup, paired with tomatoes and eggs, is nutritionally balanced and warming to the stomach, making it suitable for replenishing energy and vitamin C in winter. Note that the dough drops should be made and cooked immediately to prevent starch aging from affecting the texture. For those with weak digestion, cook for a bit longer to soften the dough pieces.
