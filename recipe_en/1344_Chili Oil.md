# Chili Oil

Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 1 garlic clove  
🌶️ 100 g dried chili flakes  
🟡 15 g toasted white sesame seeds  
🌶️ 1 bird's eye chili  
🌿 3-5 scallions (optional)  

🧂 5 g salt  
🫒 150 ml peanut oil (can substitute with rapeseed oil)  
🌿 10 g five-spice powder (optional)  
🌰 1 black cardamom (optional)  
⭐ 1 star anise  
🌶️ 20-50 Sichuan peppercorns  
🍃 2-3 bay leaves  
🌿 2-3 slices angelica root  
🧅 ginger slices (optional)  
🍬 30 g sugar  
🍶 5 ml white vinegar  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Small ceramic bowl  
🥄 Iron spoon  
🔪 Cutting board  
🍳 Iron wok  

## Steps
1. Peel and mince the garlic, and finely chop the bird's eye chili. Set aside.  
2. Pour the peanut oil into a bowl. Set aside.  

3. Heat the iron wok, add star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, angelica root, ginger slices, and scallions. Fry over low heat until the aromatics turn dark, then remove and discard.  
4. Pour the oil from the bowl into the wok and heat for about 2 minutes.  
5. In an empty bowl, combine the dried chili flakes, toasted white sesame seeds, minced garlic, bird's eye chili, salt, five-spice powder, and black cardamom. Mix well to form the base mixture.  
6. Turn off the heat and let the oil temperature naturally drop to about 210°C (410°F).  
7. Pour the hot oil into the bowl with the base mixture while stirring quickly with a spoon.  
8. After pouring in the hot oil and mixing briefly, immediately add the white vinegar. The oil will re-boil; continue stirring to enhance the aroma.  
9. Allow the chili oil to cool until warm, then add the sugar and monosodium glutamate (MSG), stirring until fully dissolved to mellow the spiciness.  

## Health Advice  
Chili oil is rich in vitamins and capsaicin, which can stimulate appetite and blood circulation. It is suitable for those with cold hands and feet or poor appetite, but individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with light staple foods and avoid eating on an empty stomach.
