# Braised Potatoes and Eggplant
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Long eggplant 2 (remove stems, tear into strips by hand)
🥔 Potatoes 1-2 (peel and cut into irregular chunks)
🥩 Pork belly Approximately 100g (slice thick, pre-cook)
🌿 Cilantro To taste (chopped, for final aroma)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers To taste (sliced into rings, for garnish and flavor)
🧄 Garlic To taste (used twice: once for sautéing the base, then generously added before serving)
🧅 Scallions To taste (minced, for sautéing)
🫚 Ginger To taste (minced, for sautéing)
🧂 Salt To taste (salt water for soaking eggplant + a pinch when pan-frying)
🫘 Soybean paste 1 tablespoon (sauté with scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant)
🍾 Seafood soy sauce A dash (add before adding water for braising, to enhance umami)
🫒 Cooking oil As needed (for pan-frying eggplant and sautéing the base)

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking eggplant in salt water)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula or ladle
🥢 Slotted spoon (for removing pan-fried eggplant)

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the stems from the long eggplants and tear them into strips by hand.
**Key point**: Tearing by hand preserves the eggplant's internal fiber structure, allowing it to better absorb the broth.

2. Add an appropriate amount of salt to clean water and stir to dissolve. Soak the eggplant strips for a short while to prevent oxidation and discoloration.
3. Peel the potatoes and cut them into irregular chunks. Ensure the size is moderate so they cook at the same rate as the eggplant.
4. Remove the soaked eggplant strips and squeeze out the water firmly.
**Key point**: Squeezing out the water reduces oil absorption.

5. Heat oil in a pan. Add the eggplant strips and pan-fry over medium heat, sprinkling with a little salt. Fry until the eggplant is soft and the surface is slightly golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
**Doneness check**: The eggplant changes from white to translucent or light yellow, and the texture becomes soft.
   **Key point**: Pan-frying separately gives the eggplant a caramelized, fragrant flavor.

6. Leave a base oil in the pan. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the edges curl and become slightly crispy.
**Doneness check**: The fatty parts become translucent and release oil.
   **Key point**: Fully rendering the fat from the meat is the foundation of the aroma.

7. Add the minced scallions, ginger, and garlic, along with the soybean paste. Stir-fry quickly over medium heat for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
**Doneness check**: The aromas of the bean paste and aromatics are fully released.

8. Add the potato chunks and stir-fry evenly to coat them with the sauce. Then add the pan-fried eggplant strips, along with a dash of seafood soy sauce, and stir-fry to combine.
9. Add water to the pan, until it is level with or slightly below the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover, and braise for about 15 minutes.
**Key point**: Do not add too much water, to avoid a thin broth.
   **Doneness check**: A chopstick can easily pierce through the potato chunks.

10. Remove the lid and increase the heat to reduce the sauce. When the broth thickens, gently press some of the potato chunks in the pan with the back of a spoon to partially crush them, creating a natural thickener.
**Key point**: Pressing the potatoes is the secret to a thick, rich sauce.

11. Turn off the heat. Generously scatter raw minced garlic, chopped cilantro, chopped scallions, and green and red chili rings over the surface of the dish.
**Key point**: Adding a large amount of raw garlic just before serving greatly enhances the complex aroma. Do not stir, so the toppings maintain their fresh fragrance.

12. Ladle the braised potatoes and eggplant, along with the sauce, into a bowl and serve.

## Health Advice
Braised potatoes and eggplant with pork belly is a high-carb, medium-to-high-fat dish, suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or those needing to gain weight. It is recommended to pair it with a light salad or coarse grain rice to reduce total calories and greasiness. Be mindful of salt and sauce intake; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
