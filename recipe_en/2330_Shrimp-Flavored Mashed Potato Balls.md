# Shrimp-Flavored Mashed Potato Balls
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦐 Fresh shrimp, appropriate amount (heads, shells, and veins removed, tails reserved)  
🥔 Potatoes, appropriate amount (peeled and thickly sliced)  
🧀 Shredded cheese, appropriate amount (as filling center)  
🌾 Starch, appropriate amount (to adjust moisture and consistency)  
🥚 Eggs, 1-2 (beaten, for coating)  
🍞 Bread crumbs, appropriate amount (for outer coating)  
🦐 Shrimp paste/minced shrimp, appropriate amount (added to mashed potatoes for flavor)

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt/seasoning, appropriate amount (for seasoning potato mixture)  
🍫 Decorative sauce, appropriate amount (dark sauce, such as chocolate sauce or seaweed paste, for drawing faces)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
♨️ Oven  
🥄 Handheld blender  
📋 Baking sheet, parchment paper  
🥣 Bowls

## Steps
1. Remove heads, shells, and veins from fresh shrimp. Wash and reserve the tails.  
   **Key Point**: Reserve tails for later shaping and decoration.  
2. Peel potatoes and cut into thick slices.  
3. Arrange potato slices flat on a baking rack and bake in the oven until fully cooked.  
   **Key Point**: Baking instead of boiling reduces moisture, making the mashed potatoes drier.  
4. Place the baked potato slices in a container and blend with a handheld blender into a smooth mash.  
5. Add shrimp paste and starch to the mashed potatoes. Mix until uniform and able to form a ball.  
   **Texture Check**: The mixture should be sticky but not stick to your hands.  
6. Take an appropriate amount of potato mixture, flatten it in your palm, place one prepared shrimp and some shredded cheese in the center. Wrap tightly, shape into a ball, ensuring the shrimp tail is exposed.  
7. Evenly coat each potato ball with beaten egg, then roll in bread crumbs.  
   **Key Point**: Bread crumbs must be fully and evenly coated to ensure crispiness after baking.  
8. Place the raw balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake in the oven until the surface is golden and the bread crumbs change color.  
9. Remove the baked shrimp balls. Use dark sauce to draw eyes and a mouth on the surface to create chick faces.

## Health Advice
These shrimp-flavored mashed potato balls are cute in shape. Baking instead of frying reduces calories, making them suitable as a children's meal or party snack. Be sure to seal the shrimp and shredded cheese tightly to prevent leakage during baking.
