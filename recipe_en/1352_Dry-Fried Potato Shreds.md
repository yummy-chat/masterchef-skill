# Dry-Fried Potato Shreds  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 potato (approx., cut into slightly thicker-than-toothpick strips)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (for making chili oil)  
🌶️ Fresh red chilies, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🧅 Dried shallots (red onion), to taste (finely chopped)  
🟢 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🥔 Potato starch, to taste (for coating the potato strips; must be dry)  
🫗 Homemade chili oil, to taste (pre-fried with dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, about 1.5 tablespoons  
⚫ Freshly ground black pepper, to taste  
🧂 Sea salt, to taste  
🌶️ Chili powder or cumin powder, optional (according to taste)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧺 Bamboo sieve or strainer  
🍳 Oil pot  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle  
🧂 Spice grinder  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the potato strips**: Trim the bottom of the potato flat, then slice thinly. Stack the slices evenly and cut into strips slightly thicker than a toothpick. Soak briefly in cold water, then drain thoroughly in a slotted spoon or bamboo sieve.  
**Key point**: Soaking removes excess surface starch, and thorough drying is essential for crispness.  

2. **Coat with starch**: Sprinkle dry potato starch over the drained strips, then toss gently with your hands to coat each strip evenly with a thin layer.  
**Key point**: The starch must be dry; do not use wet starch from washing, or the strips will turn dark and greasy after frying.  

3. **Make chili oil**: Heat a small amount of oil in a pan over low heat. Add dried chili segments and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry slowly until fragrant and color deepens, then remove and set aside.  
**Doneness check**: Aroma is released, color deepens but not burned.  

4. **First frying of potato strips**: Pour enough oil into a pot and heat to about 60% hot. Fry the strips in batches; do not turn off the heat while adding them to prevent a sharp drop in oil temperature.  
**Safety note**: If the oil level rises rapidly and risks overflowing, immediately turn off the heat.  

5. **Fry until set and cooked**: After about 30 seconds, when the strips are set, gently stir with a ladle. Maintain medium-high heat and fry for 1–2 minutes until the moisture is gone, the surface is golden, and the texture is crisp and firm. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.  
**Key point**: Fry thoroughly until crisp to prevent sogginess later.  

6. **Fry aromatics and combine**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add finely chopped fresh red chilies, minced garlic, and chopped dried shallots. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant, then add the prepared chili oil and mix well.  
**Note**: Do not burn the aromatics.  

7. **Quick seasoning and plating**: Add the fried potato strips to the wok and toss quickly to combine. Season with chicken bouillon powder, freshly ground black pepper, and sea salt. Optionally add chili powder or cumin powder. Finally, add scallion segments, toss rapidly, and serve immediately.  
**Key point**: Use high heat and stir-fry quickly to maintain crispness; seasoning must be fast.  

## Health Tips  
Potatoes are rich in resistant starch and potassium. Deep-frying makes them crispy but significantly increases calories. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable or fruit salad to balance oil intake. Avoid eating large amounts on an empty stomach to reduce digestive strain.
