# Nut Ice Cream
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍌 Banana 2 pieces (for a single flavor; 4 pieces if making two flavors)  
🧈 Cream 105g  
🥛 Almond milk 255g  
🌰 Roasted almonds 100g (reserve some crushed for garnish)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 20g  

> This flavor uses no additional sugar, salt, or other seasonings; sweetness comes primarily from the bananas.

## Kitchenware & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔥 Oven (for roasting raw macadamia nuts; can be omitted if using roasted almonds)  
🥣 Bowl  
🧻 Plastic wrap  
🥤 Blender  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl (or other freezer-safe container)  
❄️ Refrigerator  
🍨 Ice cream scoop  
🔪 Cutting board and knife

## Instructions
### Preparation
1. Place the roasted almonds in the almond milk, stir well, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight to soak. Alternatively, heat to a boil then simmer over low heat until almonds soften to speed up the process.
   **Note**: Soaking softens the nuts and allows their flavor to infuse into the milk, resulting in a smoother ice cream texture.
2. Reserve a small amount of roasted almonds, chop into fine pieces, and set aside for garnish.

### Making & Freezing the Ice Cream Base
1. Pour the soaked almonds (along with the almond milk), banana chunks, cream, and cocoa powder into the blender.
   **Key Point**: All ingredients can be added at once; blend longer until the mixture is smooth and slightly thick for a better texture.
2. Pour the blended ice cream base into a stainless steel bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and freeze for about 4 hours until it reaches a slushy consistency.
3. Transfer the slushy ice cream base back to the blender and blend again until smoother and free of lumps.
   **Key Point**: A second blending prevents separation and improves smoothness.
4. Pour the re-blended base into an ice cream container, smooth the surface, cover, and freeze overnight until fully set.

### Scooping & Garnishing
1. Heat the ice cream scoop with hot water, then scoop out the ice cream.
   **Tip**: A warm scoop makes it easier to scoop hard ice cream.
2. Sprinkle the prepared nut crumbs on the plate as garnish, and serve.

## Health Advice
This homemade nut ice cream uses bananas as a substitute for sugar and is rich in healthy fats and antioxidants from almond milk and cocoa powder, making it suitable for fitness-conscious or sugar-controlled individuals. However, cream and almonds are high in calories, so moderate consumption is recommended.
