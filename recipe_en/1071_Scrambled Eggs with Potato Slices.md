# Scrambled Eggs with Potato Slices  
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 potato (about 200-300 grams)  
🥚 2 eggs  
🫑 Green bell pepper, to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste (cut into rings)  
🫒 Cooking oil, generous amount (for pan-frying, wide oil)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥣 Mixing bowl for eggs  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel the potato and cut into uniform thin slices about 1.5 mm thick. Rinse in clean water to remove surface starch, then drain thoroughly to ensure no excess water remains, preventing oil splatter when added to the pan.  
2. Slice the green bell pepper, mince the garlic, and cut the scallion into rings. Crack the eggs into a bowl, tilt the bowl, and quickly beat with chopsticks to incorporate air, making the scrambled eggs fluffier.  
3. Heat the wok over high heat, add a generous amount of cooking oil, and heat until very hot. Add the drained potato slices, keeping the heat high, and stir-fry quickly until the edges of the slices are charred and the center remains slightly firm, creating a fragrant crispy surface.  
4. Add the scallion rings and salt, continue stir-frying. When the potato slices show obvious charring, drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, quickly tossing to combine, using the wok’s high heat to release the sauce’s aroma.  
5. Add the green bell pepper slices and minced garlic, stir-fry until just cooked.  
6. Evenly pour the beaten eggs over all ingredients in the wok. Do not stir immediately; let the bottom set and the eggs become fluffy, then gently flip with a spatula to turn the eggs and potato slices together.  
7. Keep the heat high and fry the other side until it also reaches a charred, fragrant state. Avoid moving it too much before the other side sets, allowing the eggs to fully absorb the oil in the wok, becoming fluffier and richer.  
8. Once both sides are golden brown and fragrant, remove from the heat and plate.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it a quick and satisfying meal. However, the generous amount of oil used in pan-frying increases calorie content, so portion control is advised. Soaking the potato slices beforehand to remove starch helps reduce sticking, and pairing with green bell pepper adds vitamin C.
