# Rice Cake
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 300 grams of rice  
💧 Water (enough to soak the rice, just covering it) + 180 grams of warm water (for making rice paste)  
🌾 70 grams of all-purpose flour  
🍬 50 grams of white sugar  
🧂 4 grams of high-sugar-tolerant yeast  
🌑 Black sesame seeds, as needed (for decoration)  
🫘 Dried red dates (or dried cranberries), as needed (chopped for decoration)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🏺 Large bowl or rice cooker inner pot (for soaking rice)  
🥤 Cut mineral water bottle (measuring cup)  
🌀 High-speed blender/food processor  
🥣 Glass bowl or disposable paper cup (mold)  
♨️ Steamer and steaming rack  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🍽️ Plate (to cover the bowl during steaming to prevent dripping)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 300 grams of rice in a container and add enough water to cover the rice. If time allows, soak overnight in cold water; if short on time, soak in hot water for 2–3 hours, until the rice grains can be easily broken by hand.
2. Drain the soaked rice, transfer it to a high-speed blender, add 180 grams of warm water, and blend until a fine, smooth rice paste forms with no grains.
3. Pour the rice paste into a large bowl, add 4 grams of high-sugar-tolerant yeast and 50 grams of white sugar, and stir until well combined. Then add 70 grams of all-purpose flour and mix until the batter is smooth. When lifted, the batter should drip slowly, and the lines should not disappear immediately. If the batter is too thick, add a small amount of water; if too thin, add a bit of flour to adjust.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot for the first fermentation. You can heat water in the steamer to 50–60°C, then remove it from heat, place the batter bowl inside, and cover the steamer lid. Ferment for about 1 hour, until the batter expands to twice its original volume.
5. Lightly grease the inner walls of the glass bowl or paper cup with oil to prevent sticking.
6. Stir the fermented batter with chopsticks to release the gas. Then pour it into the greased container, filling it to 70–80% full. Sprinkle black sesame seeds and chopped dried red dates on top for decoration.
7. Place the filled container into the steamer, cover with the steamer lid, and let it rest for a second fermentation of about 15–20 minutes, until the batter rises slightly.
8. Add enough cold water to the steamer, and place the risen rice cake bowl inside. Invert a plate over the bowl to prevent steam from dripping. Cover with the steamer lid, and cook over medium heat. Once the water boils, turn to high heat.
9. Steam over high heat: 55 minutes for a large bowl portion, 25 minutes for small paper cup portions.
10. After the time is up, turn off the heat. Do not immediately open the lid. Let it rest with residual heat for 5 minutes or 3 minutes, then carefully remove the lid.
11. Take out the rice cake, let it cool slightly, then unmold. For the bowl, run a knife around the edge and invert to release; for paper cups, simply tear them open.

## Health Tips
This rice cake is made from fermented rice and flour, making it easier to digest than pure glutinous rice. It is suitable for the elderly and children as breakfast or a snack. However, the sugar content of 50 grams is slightly high; it is recommended to halve the amount or use a sugar substitute.
