# Boneless Chicken Feet in Chili Oil
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken feet (frozen chicken feet need thorough rinsing to remove odor)
🫚 Ginger (some sliced for blanching, some shredded for seasoning)
🧅 Scallion (cut into sections)
🍋 Lemon (3 slices, seeds removed to prevent bitterness from long marination)
🧄 Garlic (minced)
🍶 Cooking wine
🧂 Salt
🍬 White sugar
🍾 Light soy sauce
🥃 Rice vinegar
🌶️ Fresh red chili flakes
🌶️ Chopped chili
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers
🫗 Pickled chili pepper brine
🫗 Chili red oil
🧂 MSG/chicken powder (optional)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Pot
🥄 Strainer
✂️ Scissors
🥣 Glass bowl
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Kitchen knife
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. Rinse chicken feet repeatedly in cold water until the water runs clear, removing grease and impurities.
2. Prepare ingredients: shred ginger, mince garlic; slice lemon thinly and remove all seeds.
3. Place chicken feet in a pot with cold water, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 5-6 minutes, total time about 15 minutes, until chicken feet are fully cooked with no bloody residue.
**Key point**: Starting with cold water removes odor more effectively.

4. Remove chicken feet and immediately soak them in cold water, rinsing repeatedly until no oil foam appears on the water surface.
**Key point**: Alternating hot and cold water gives chicken feet a chewier texture; thorough rinsing removes unwanted odors.

5. Remove bones: First, cut off the nails with scissors. Then cut open the skin from the palm side and sever the central bone tendons. Cut along the toe bone joints. Pry off the large bones by hand, then use scissors to clamp the small toe bones and push them out in reverse to remove small bone segments.
**Key point**: Severing the bone tendons is key to easy deboning; after deboning, carefully check for any remaining bone fragments.

6. Place the deboned chicken feet in a large bowl. Add shredded ginger, minced garlic, lemon slices, salt, white sugar, light soy sauce, rice vinegar, fresh red chili flakes, chopped chili, pickled chili peppers, and pickled chili pepper brine. Mix initially. Then drizzle with chili red oil and toss thoroughly to combine evenly.
**Note**: Adjust spiciness and sweetness to taste; adding a small amount of MSG or chicken powder can enhance umami.

7. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, shaking the bowl occasionally during marination for even flavor distribution.
**Key point**: The longer the marination, the more flavorful; the chicken feet taste best the next day in terms of color and flavor.

## Health Tips
Chicken feet are rich in collagen, low in fat, and high in protein, making them suitable for satisfying cravings while controlling calorie intake. Note that chicken feet have a relatively high sodium content, so people with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation.
