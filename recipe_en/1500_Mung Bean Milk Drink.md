# Mung Bean Milk Drink  
Cuisine: Common Home-style  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Mung beans – about 150g (frozen into mung bean ice cubes)  
🥛 Whole milk – as needed (boxed whole milk is fine)  
🍯 Yellow rock sugar – a handful (optional, can omit to avoid sugar)  
🍋 Lemon juice or white vinegar – a few drops or a bottle cap (optional, for maintaining green color)  
🌼 Dried osmanthus – as needed  
🍯 Osmanthus syrup or honey – as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot  
🫗 Electric immersion blender (or blender)  
🧊 Ice cube tray  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Spoon or spatula  

## Instructions  
1. Place the mung beans into an ice cube tray, fill with water, and freeze into ice cubes.  
   **Note**: Freezing the mung beans before cooking helps them bloom faster.  
2. Put the mung bean ice cubes into a soup pot, and add water until the pot is 80% full.  
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and simmer for 30 minutes until all mung beans have bloomed.  
   **Key point**: If you want to keep the mung bean soup a vibrant green color, add a few drops of lemon juice or one bottle cap of white vinegar while cooking. The effect may vary depending on the pH of your local water.  
4. Use an immersion blender or blender to puree the cooked mung bean soup into a smooth mung bean paste.  
5. Take a cup, and spread an appropriate amount of mung bean paste at the bottom.  
6. Add ice cubes.  
7. Pour in whole milk until the cup is full. This completes the basic version of the mung bean milk drink.  

### Flavor Upgrade  
- After spreading the mung bean paste at the bottom of the cup, first drizzle in some honey, sprinkle with dried osmanthus, then add ice cubes, and finally fill the cup with whole milk. This adds the fragrant aroma of osmanthus and a richer layer of sweetness.  

## Health Tips  
Mung bean paste helps replenish fluids and relieve heat, making it suitable for drinking in summer when sweating heavily. However, milk and sugar increase calorie content, so reduce the amount of osmanthus syrup added during weight loss periods.
