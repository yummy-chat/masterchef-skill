# Fish-Flavored Baked Egg
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 4  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (increases viscosity, prevents collapse)  
🌾 Flour appropriate amount (used with cornstarch, adds structural support)  
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers appropriate amount (minced, used for fish-flavored sauce)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced, used for fish-flavored sauce)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced, used for fish-flavored sauce)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped, used for fish-flavored sauce)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🌶️ Ground white pepper appropriate amount  
💧 Water appropriate amount  
🛢️ Cooking oil appropriate amount (for seasoning pan, sealing, baking, and frying)  
🧂 MSG appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount  
🫗 Soy sauce a little  
🌾 Potato starch slurry appropriate amount (for thickening the fish-flavored sauce)  
🍶 Vinegar appropriate amount (for seasoning the fish-flavored sauce, added last)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Large iron wok (frying pan)  
🥣 Bowls (for mixing batter, holding egg mixture)  
🥄 Ladle (for mixing batter, stirring egg mixture)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing fried baked egg pieces)  
🔥 Gas stove (needs adjustable high and low heat)  

## Procedure
### Preparation Phase
1. Crack 4 eggs into a bowl.
2. In another large bowl, mix cornstarch, flour, salt, and ground white pepper.
**Key Point**: Adding flour prevents the baked egg from collapsing.
3. Add water to the dry ingredients, stir thoroughly by hand until a smooth, lump-free batter forms.
**Key Point**: Must be stirred until smooth with no lumps.
4. Pour the beaten eggs into the prepared batter, stir again until fully combined. Set aside.
**Key Point**: The egg mixture and batter must be completely incorporated.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Season the pan**: Heat the iron wok repeatedly, coating with oil 2-3 times to prevent sticking.
2. **Spread the egg skin**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, pour in about 4/5 of the egg batter.
**Heat**: Medium-low heat.
   **Key Point**: Do not pour all at once, reserve some batter.
3. Quickly rotate the wok to let the batter flow and spread into a large, thin, round egg skin.
**Key Point**: Try to spread it as large and thin as possible.
4. Gently stir the still-liquid egg mixture in the center of the egg skin with a ladle to ensure even heating and gradual setting.
**Heat**: Keep on low heat throughout; requires patience.
   **Key Point**: Movements must be very gentle to prevent tearing the egg skin. High heat causes burning, rough movements cause tearing.
5. **Fold and set shape**: When the egg mixture is almost fully set, turn off the heat. Use your hands to fold the edges of the egg skin towards the center, forming a square parcel.
**Indicator**: Edges are set, center is slightly runny.
   **Key Point**: Use the still-liquid egg to bond the folded layers together; fold one piece over the other.
6. **Seal**: Brush the reserved raw egg batter onto the seams of the folded egg skin to seal it shut.
7. **Bake to set**: Drizzle a little oil along the edge of the wok, turn on low heat, gently rotate the wok to heat the square egg block evenly.
**Heat**: Low heat, slow baking.
   **Key Point**: Rotate the wok so the egg block slides around, ensuring overall even heating.
8. **Flip**: Once one side is set and golden brown, carefully flip the block over. Bake the other side until set and golden brown as well.
9. **Cut**: Remove the baked egg cake from the wok. Let it cool slightly, then cut into evenly sized square or rectangular pieces.
10. **Fry**: Add ample oil to the wok, heat to about 20% oil temperature.
**Indicator**: Low oil temperature; small bubbles appear around the egg pieces when added.
11. **Slow fry**: Gently place the cut egg pieces into the oil.
    **Heat**: Low heat, slow frying.
**Key Point**: Continuously and gently turn the pieces with a ladle for even heating.
12. **Fry through**: Continue frying until the egg pieces puff up in volume and become golden brown and crispy.
**Indicator**: Egg pieces are fully puffed, color is golden brown.
13. **Remove**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried baked egg pieces. Drain excess oil.
14. **Make fish-flavored sauce**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Stir-fry the minced pickled chili, ginger, and garlic until fragrant.
15. **Season the sauce**: Add an appropriate amount of water. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon powder, sugar, and a little soy sauce.
16. **Thicken**: Add the potato starch slurry to thicken the sauce to a glossy consistency.
17. **Finish**: Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in chopped scallions, drizzle in vinegar, and stir to combine. Transfer to a small bowl to serve as a dipping sauce.
18. **Plate**: Arrange the fried baked egg pieces neatly on a serving plate. Serve with the fish-flavored sauce for dipping.

## Health Advice  
This dish uses eggs as the main ingredient, achieving a crispy, puffed texture through deep-frying. It is rich in protein, suitable for those who want a different flavor without meat. However, as a deep-fried item, it is relatively high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Pairing it with light vegetables makes for a more balanced meal.
