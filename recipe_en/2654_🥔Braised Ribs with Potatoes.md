# 🥔Braised Ribs with Potatoes
Cuisine: 🏠Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: 🍁Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
* 🥩Pork ribs 750g
* 🥔Potatoes 300g
* 🫚Ginger 30g
* 🧅Scallions 25g

* 🍶Cooking wine 25ml
* 🍬White sugar 10g
* 🌶️Dried chili peppers 5g
* ⭐Star anise 1g
* 🌸Sichuan peppercorns 1g
* 🍂Cinnamon stick 1g
* 🥄Light soy sauce 10ml
* 🟤Dark soy sauce 5ml
* 🦪Oyster sauce 5ml
* 🫘Broad bean paste 5g
* 🧂Salt 3g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
* 🔪Wok
* 🥣Small bowl and small dish
* 🍽️Paper towels
* 🥢Spatula
* 🫖Electric kettle

## Cooking Steps

**——Pre-processing Stage——**

1.  Peel the potatoes and cut into irregular rolling cut chunks. Immediately submerge in clean water to prevent oxidation and discoloration. Set aside.
2.  Slice all the ginger into thin slices, dividing into two portions of 15g each. Tie 15g of scallions into a knot. Finely chop the remaining 10g of scallions. Set aside.
3.  In a small bowl, sequentially add 10ml light soy sauce, 5ml dark soy sauce, 5ml oyster sauce, and 5g broad bean paste. Stir thoroughly to combine and place beside the stove for later use.
4.  On a small dish, place 5g dried chili peppers, 1g star anise, 1g Sichuan peppercorns, 1g cinnamon stick, and 15g ginger slices. Set beside the stove.
5.  Boil 700ml of water using the electric kettle, ensuring it is boiling when needed for the subsequent stir-frying step.

**——Blanching Stage——**

6.  Place the ribs in a pot with cold water. Add the scallion knot, 15g ginger slices, and 15ml cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling and blanching for 2 minutes to force out internal blood and foam.
7.  Remove the ribs and must rinse off any surface scum thoroughly with hot water. Note: Do not use cold water, as it will cause the meat to shrink sharply and become tough.
8.  Thoroughly pat the cleaned ribs dry with paper towels and set aside on a plate. Note: Any surface moisture will cause severe oil splatter when the ribs are added to the wok for caramelizing.

**——Core Stir-frying Stage——**

9.  Heat the wok, then add cold oil (20ml cooking oil). Add 10g white sugar. Stir constantly over medium-low heat until the sugar has completely melted, bubbling and turning a reddish-brown caramel color.
10. Quickly add the thoroughly dried ribs. Turn to medium-high heat and stir-fry, searing for about 2–3 minutes, until both sides of the ribs are lightly golden and evenly coated with the caramel color.
11. Push the ribs to one side of the wok to create an empty space. Pour in all the contents from the spice dish. Use the hot oil at the bottom of the wok to stir-fry and release the aroma for about 10 seconds.
12. Pour in the remaining 10ml of cooking wine along the edge of the hot wok to release the wok aroma. Immediately pour in all the sauce from the prepared bowl. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until the sauce fully coats the ribs and a rich, savory aroma is released.

**——Braising and Reducing Stage——**

13. At this point, the electric kettle should have boiled water. Directly add 700ml of boiling hot water to the wok, enough to submerge the ribs. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Let it braise for 40 minutes.
14. After 40 minutes, uncover the wok. Drain the potatoes from the water and add them to the wok. Re-cover the lid and continue to braise over low heat for 15–20 minutes, until the potatoes can be easily pierced through with chopsticks.
15. Uncover the wok. Add 3g of salt. Taste the broth at this point and adjust if needed. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry to reduce the sauce for about 3–5 minutes, until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ribs and potatoes. Turn off the heat.
16. Plate the dish. Evenly sprinkle the chopped scallions on top for garnish.

## Health Suggestion
This dish is rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for muscle recovery after workouts and for consumption in cold weather. Potatoes absorb a significant amount of oil, so be mindful of portion sizes to manage calorie intake.
