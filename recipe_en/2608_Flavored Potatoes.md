# Flavored Potatoes
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 2 (peeled, cut into about 0.5cm thick slices)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (shredded)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped)
🧄 Garlic 4-5 cloves (smashed and roughly chopped)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (use only stems, cut into sections)
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for coating)
🍶 White vinegar a few drops (for soaking)
🍬 White sugar 3 tablespoons
🍶 Vinegar 3 tablespoons
🧂 Soy sauce 30mL
🧂 Dark soy sauce 10mL
💧 Water 100mL
🫒 Sichuan peppercorns about 3g
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for frying potatoes and stir-frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl (for soaking potatoes)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🛍️ Food storage bag
💦 Spray bottle
🥣 Small bowl (for sauce)
🥄 Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the peeled potatoes in half, then into slices about 0.5cm thick. Immediately place them in water with a few drops of white vinegar to prevent oxidation and enhance crispness.
   **Note**: Slices should not be too thin, otherwise they will become hard and dry like chips after double frying.
2. Shred the dried chili peppers, chop the scallions, smash and roughly chop the garlic, and cut the cilantro stems into sections.
3. In a small bowl, mix white sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and water. Stir until the sugar dissolves to form the sauce.
   **Key**: Soy sauce already provides saltiness, so no additional salt is needed.
4. Drain the soaked potato slices and place them in a food storage bag. Add enough cornstarch, seal the bag while retaining some air, and shake vigorously to coat each slice evenly.
   **Advantage**: Using a sealed bag is neat and ensures even coating.
5. Spread the coated potato slices on a plate. Lightly spray water mist on the surface using a spray bottle to slightly moisten the starch, preventing it from falling off during frying.
6. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok to medium-high heat (about 160°C/320°F). Add the potato slices one by one. Do not stir immediately; let them set, then gently push them apart. Fry on high heat for about 5 minutes, until the slices float, the surface bubbles, and they turn light yellow. Remove with a slotted spoon.
   **Judgment**: Floating and surface bubbling indicate they are fully cooked.
7. Reheat the oil to medium-high heat again. Add the potato slices for a second fry, cooking for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Quickly remove and drain excess oil.
   **Note**: Do not over-fry during the second fry to maintain crispness.
8. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns and fry over low heat until they change color and release fragrance. Then add shredded dried chili peppers and stir-fry until fragrant. Next, add scallions and garlic, stir-frying until aromatic. Pour in the prepared sauce and bring to a boil over high heat, cooking until the sauce thickens slightly.
9. Quickly add the double-fried potato slices and toss rapidly to coat each slice evenly with the sauce. Finally, sprinkle in the cilantro stem sections, toss a few more times, and serve immediately.
   **Key Point**: Work quickly to coat instantly, maintaining the potato slices’ crispy texture.

## Health Advice
This dish fries potatoes until crispy, then coats them in a sweet and sour sauce. While rich in flavor, it is high in calories. It is recommended as an occasional accompaniment to satisfy cravings, paired with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for those who enjoy a crispy texture, but portion control is advised to avoid excessive oil intake.
