# Soy Sauce Chicken  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Chicken thigh 1 (boneless)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (some sliced for marinating, some cut into sections for sautéing)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced for marinating, some sliced for sautéing)  
🧅 Onion about half (shredded)  
🧂 Yellow wine 30g (for marinating) + 60g (for savory sauce) or as needed (for other sauces)  
🧂 White sugar about 10g (for marinating)  
🧂 Salt 3.5g (for marinating)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 30g (for marinating) + 30g (for savory sauce) or 30g (for sweet and sour sauce) or 13g (for sauce-flavored sauce)  
🧂 Starch appropriate amount (for coating)  
🧂 Rock sugar 4 pieces (optional for savory flavor) or 10 pieces (for sweet and sour flavor)  
🧂 Fragrant vinegar 30g (for sweet and sour sauce)  
🧂 Hoisin sauce 20g (for sauce-flavored sauce, can be substituted with spare rib sauce, chu hou paste, or char siu sauce)  
🧂 Boiling water appropriate amount (for braising)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen knife/scissors (for deboning and cutting tendons)  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels (to dry moisture)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating)  
🍳 Iron pot (for frying and braising)  
🥄 Spatula (for handling)  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the chicken thigh**  
   Debone the chicken thigh, wash it, and thoroughly dry with kitchen paper towels. Use the heel of the knife to cut through the tendons on the chicken to improve texture.  
2. **Marinate**  
   Place the prepared chicken thigh in a bowl, add scallion segments, ginger slices, 30g yellow wine, 30g light soy sauce, 10g sugar, and 3.5g salt. Mix well and marinate for 10–20 minutes to allow the alcohol to fully penetrate and remove any gamey flavor.  
3. **Coat with starch**  
   Remove the marinated chicken thigh and evenly dust both sides with a thin layer of starch to help lock in juices during frying.  
4. **Mix the sauce**  
   - **Savory flavor**: Mix about 30g light soy sauce, 60g yellow wine, and 4 pieces of rock sugar.  
   - **Sweet and sour flavor**: Mix 30g light soy sauce, 30g yellow wine, 10 pieces of rock sugar, and 30g fragrant vinegar.  
   - **Sauce-flavored**: Mix 20g hoisin sauce, 13g light soy sauce, and an appropriate amount of yellow wine.  
   **Note**: Since the chicken already has a base flavor, adjust the saltiness of the sauce accordingly.  
5. **Fry**  
   Heat the pot and add oil, thoroughly coat the surface, then reduce to low heat. Place the chicken thigh skin-side down, then turn to medium heat and fry until one side is golden brown and set. Flip and fry both sides until caramelized and the skin is tightened, then remove.  
   **Note**: A hot pot with hot oil prevents sticking, and frying produces the Maillard reaction while locking in moisture.  
6. **Sauté and braise**  
   Leave some oil in the pot, sauté ginger slices, shredded onion, and scallion segments over medium-low heat until fragrant. Add the fried chicken thigh, pour in the prepared sauce, bring to a boil, and simmer for about 1 minute.  
   Add boiling water until it reaches halfway up the chicken thigh, cover, and simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes until the chicken is tender and flavorful.  
7. **Reduce sauce and serve**  
   Uncover and turn to high heat to reduce the sauce. You can poke the chicken thigh a few times with chopsticks to help it absorb flavor. If you plan to mix with rice, do not reduce the sauce too much; otherwise, reduce until thick and coats the chicken.  
   Remove the chicken thigh, slice into strips, and drizzle with the sauce. Serve with blanched vegetables if desired.  

## Health Tips  
This soy sauce chicken is low in fat and high in protein, suitable for those on a weight loss or muscle-building diet. However, be mindful of the amount of light soy sauce and salt to avoid excessive sodium intake. Do not marinate for too long, or the meat will become overly salty and tough.
