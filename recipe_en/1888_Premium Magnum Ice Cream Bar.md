# Premium Magnum Ice Cream Bar
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
**Ganache Filling**
🍫 70% Dark Chocolate 100g
🥛 Heavy Cream 100g (use after boiling)
🫒 Truffle Oil Approx. 20g
🍬 Crispy Pearls (Chocolate Crisps) Approx. 100g

**Vanilla Ice Cream Base** (Optional)
🥛 Whole Milk 350g
🥛 Heavy Cream 70g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 50g
🍬 Glucose Powder 35g
🥛 Nonfat Dry Milk 10g
🌿 Vanilla Bean 1 piece (scrape out the vanilla seeds)
🧪 Stabilizer 2.5g (Optional)

**Egg Custard Ice Cream Base** (Optional)
🥛 Whole Milk 320g
🥛 Heavy Cream 60g
🥚 Egg Yolks 2 pieces (approx. 36–38g)
🍬 Granulated Sugar 55g
🍬 Glucose Powder 35g
🥛 Nonfat Dry Milk 10g
🧪 Stabilizer 2.5g (Optional)

**Chocolate Crunch Coating**
🍫 Dark Chocolate (homemade or store-bought) As needed
🫒 Odorless Vegetable Oil (e.g., sunflower oil) As needed
🍫 Chocolate Crisps As needed

**Ice Salt Bath**
🧊 Ice Cubes As needed
🧂 Salt 15% of the weight of the ice

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Immersion Blender (Hand Blender)
🥣 Metal Bowl
❄️ Freezer Compartment
🌡️ Thermometer
🥄 Electric/Manual Whisk
🖨️ 3D Printer (Optional, for making molds)
🔥 Heat Gun/Oven (for mold making)
🧁 Mousse Ring or Homemade Ice Cream Mold
🥄 Piping Bag
🥤 Coating Cup (deep cup for dipping the crunch coating)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the Ganache Filling**
   Melt the 70% dark chocolate using a double boiler or microwave. Pour the boiled heavy cream in batches into the melted chocolate, stirring in a circular motion from the center, until fully emulsified and the texture becomes smooth and ribbon-like. Add the truffle oil and mix well, then fold in the crispy pearls. Pipe the ganache into small molds and freeze until firm.
   **Key Point**: The heavy cream must be boiled and added in batches; stirring in a circular motion prevents oil-water separation.

2. **Prepare the Ice Cream Base**
   - **Vanilla Ice Cream Base**: Combine milk, heavy cream, dry milk, granulated sugar, glucose powder, vanilla seeds, and the vanilla bean in a pot. Heat, stirring constantly, to 82°C, then continue stirring for 30–60 seconds. Remove from heat.
   - **Egg Custard Ice Cream Base**: Whisk the egg yolks, dry milk, granulated sugar, and glucose powder until pale. In a separate pot, heat the milk and heavy cream to a boil. Gradually pour the hot liquid into the egg yolk mixture in a thin stream, whisking quickly. Return the combined mixture to the pot, heat over low flame while stirring constantly until it reaches 82°C. The custard is ready when it coats the back of a spatula. Strain and cool for later use.

3. **Hand-Churn the Ice Cream**
   Prepare an ice salt bath: Crush the ice, mix in the salt, until the temperature drops to about -16°C. Place the metal bowl containing the ice cream base into the ice salt bath. Stir every 10 seconds for about 7 minutes, until the liquid thickens and the edges begin to freeze. Switch to a whisk: whisk by hand for about 5 minutes, or with an electric whisk for 30–50 seconds, until the volume expands and the texture becomes light and smooth. When you lift the whisk, the ice cream should cling to the spatula without dripping.

4. **Assemble the Ice Cream Bars**
   Pipe a layer of ice cream into the ice cream molds. Place the frozen ganache filling in the center, then fill the rest of the mold with ice cream. Insert the ice cream stick, smooth the surface, and freeze in the freezer until completely solid.

5. **Make the Chocolate Crunch Coating**
   Melt the dark chocolate, stir in an appropriate amount of odorless vegetable oil to increase flexibility. When the chocolate liquid cools to 40–45°C, mix in the chocolate crisps.

6. **Apply the Crunch Coating**
   Unmold the frozen ice cream bars. Quickly dip them into the chocolate crunch coating liquid, then lift them out promptly, allowing excess chocolate to drip off. The shell will instantly set into a matte, crunchy coating upon contact with the ice cream. Done.

## Health Advice
This ice cream bar contains a ganache filling and a crunchy coating that are rich in sugar and fat, making it high in calories. It is suitable for occasional indulgence as a treat and should not be consumed as a daily refreshment. It is recommended to consume in portions or share with friends to control single-serving intake.
