# Lazy Ice Cream
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫐 Blueberries, to taste
🍓 Berries (strawberries, etc.), to taste
🥭 Mango, to taste (cubed)
🥛 Thick yogurt, to taste (choose a thick, dense type)
🍫 Chocolate, 200 grams (broken into pieces)
🥥 Coconut oil, 50 grams

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
📦 Clear storage container
📃 Parchment paper
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥄 Spoon
🍳 Pan + induction cooker (for double boiling)
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
❄️ Refrigerator (with freezer compartment)

## Steps
1. Wash the fruits that need cleaning and thoroughly dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels to prevent excessive ice crystals after freezing.
2. Place the prepared fruits in a bowl, add thick yogurt, and mix evenly with a silicone spatula. The ratio of fruit to yogurt can be adjusted to personal preference, as long as it coats evenly.
3. Line the bottom of the storage container with a sheet of parchment paper. Use a spoon to scoop the mixed yogurt and fruit, and casually place it in clumps—no molds needed, shapes can be freeform.
4. Place in the freezer and freeze for about 4 hours, until completely solid and easy to handle without falling apart.
5. Put the broken chocolate and coconut oil in a bowl, place over a pan of hot water to melt using the double boiling method. Stir over medium heat until smooth chocolate liquid forms, then remove from heat.
6. Take out the frozen ice cream clumps. Use a spatula to help quickly roll them in the chocolate liquid, ensuring an even coating.
7. Place the chocolate-coated ice cream on parchment paper. Within seconds, the chocolate will solidify into a crispy shell due to the low temperature. Serve immediately once done.

## Health Advice
Making ice cream at home with thick yogurt and fresh fruit allows you to control the calories while also getting calcium and vitamins. It’s suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss, but note that the chocolate shell contains sugar and oil, so it’s best not to overindulge at once. For a healthier option, consider replacing the chocolate with dark chocolate.
