# Teriyaki Salmon
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Salmon 1-3 pieces (thawed in advance in the refrigerator)
🫚 Ginger a small amount (grated or sliced)
🧅 Scallion to taste (chopped)
🫘 White sesame seeds to taste (for garnish)
🍋 Lemon a small amount (juiced or sliced for garnish)
🥄 Light soy sauce 40ml
🥄 Mirin 40ml
🍶 Sake (or rice wine) 40ml
💧 Water 50ml
🍯 Honey 20g
🍋 Lemon juice a small amount (optional)
🥢 Cornstarch slurry a small amount (optional, for thickening)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥢 Tongs
🫙 Grater
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🔥 Stove

## Cooking Steps
1. Pat the thawed salmon dry with kitchen paper towels; no need to marinate.
   **Note**: Drying the surface helps achieve a crispy, browned exterior when frying.
2. Mix the grated ginger, light soy sauce, mirin, sake, water, and honey until well combined to make the teriyaki sauce; set aside.
   **Key point**: Preparing the sauce in advance makes cooking more seamless.
3. Pour a suitable amount of oil into the frying pan, heat over medium-high heat until about 70% hot, then add the salmon. Quickly sear each side for about one minute until the surface develops a browned, caramelized color.
   **Note**: Ensure the pan is hot before adding the fish; only sear the surface, do not fully cook the fish through.
4. Pour all the prepared teriyaki sauce into the pan; the sauce will boil rapidly. Keep the heat on medium-high and continuously spoon the sauce over the salmon, allowing the fish to cook evenly and coat with the glaze.
   **Key point**: Reduce the sauce while basting to ensure even cooking and maintain a tender interior.
5. Squeeze in a small amount of lemon juice if desired.
6. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and white sesame seeds. For a thicker sauce, drizzle in a small amount of cornstarch slurry until the sauce clings to the surface of the fish.
   **Note**: Do not reduce the sauce for too long to avoid overcooking and drying out the fish.
7. Transfer the teriyaki salmon to a plate, garnish with lemon slices, and drizzle the remaining sauce from the pan over the top.

## Health Tips
Teriyaki salmon is rich in high-quality Omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for cardiovascular and cerebrovascular health. It is suitable for those looking to control calorie intake during weight loss. Pair with plenty of vegetables to balance sodium intake, and control the heat during frying to keep the salmon tender and juicy.
