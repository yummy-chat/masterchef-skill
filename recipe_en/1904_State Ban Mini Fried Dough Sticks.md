# State Ban Mini Fried Dough Sticks
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
🧪 Aluminum-free baking powder 6g
🧂 Baking soda 3g
🧂 Salt 5g
🍬 White sugar 5g
🥚 Egg 1
🧈 Butter 30g (can be substituted with equal amount of soybean oil)
💧 Water 250g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥢 Long chopsticks
🍳 Oil pot

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour the flour into a basin, add baking powder, baking soda, salt, and white sugar, and mix evenly by hand.
2. Make a well in the center of the flour, crack in the egg, add melted butter, and mix well.
3. Add water in batches, stirring while adding, and knead into a very soft, sticky dough.
4. Dip your fist in water, and process the dough using a "pressing" technique: press down with your knuckles, then fold. Repeat for about 2–3 minutes.
   **Note**: Do not knead the dough, as it will become extremely sticky.
5. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 10 minutes, then repeat the "press-rest" process twice more, for a total of three presses and three rests. The final dough should be smooth, delicate, and non-sticky.
6. Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap into a log shape, and refrigerate for about 40 minutes.
7. Dust the cutting board with dry flour, take out the dough, gently stretch it long, and roll it into a uniform rectangular sheet about 1 cm thick.
8. Cut the dough sheet into strips about 2–3 cm wide and of equal length.
9. Take one strip, use chopsticks dipped in water to draw a water line down the center, place another strip on top and align them, then use the back of a knife to press firmly down the center.
10. Cut off both ends of the stacked strips; the cut surfaces will naturally draw inward and seal.
11. Hold both ends of the raw piece with your hands and gently stretch it to about 20 cm in length.
12. Heat oil in a pot to about 180°C, and place the raw pieces into the oil.
13. Once they float, continuously flip and press them with long chopsticks to ensure even expansion and heating.
    **Key**: Continuous flipping is the core to achieving round and puffy fried dough sticks.
14. Fry until golden brown all over, remove, and drain excess oil.

## Health Advice
This mini fried dough stick uses aluminum-free baking powder, avoiding the traditional use of alum. The amount of oil used is moderate, making it suitable for occasional indulgence. Note that fried flour-based foods are relatively high in calories. It is recommended that those on a diet or with high blood lipid levels consume no more than two per serving.
