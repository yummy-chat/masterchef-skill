# Matcha Milk Crisp Lye Rolls  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥖 Bread flour 450g  
🥖 Cake flour 50g  
🧂 Sea salt 6g  
🥛 Milk powder 15g (for dough) + 60g (for milk crisp filling)  
🌱 Dry yeast 4g  
🥛 Milk 280g (adjust based on dough dryness)  
🧈 Softened butter 30g  
🍵 Matcha powder 8g  
🥚 Egg liquid 50g  
🍬 White sugar 15g  
🧂 Salt 2g  
⚪ White sesame seeds, as needed (for decoration)  
🧪 Baking soda lye 15g (for lye water, corrosive; wear gloves and a mask when handling)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Bowl/measuring cup  
🥄 Spatula  
🤖 Stand mixer  
📏 Rolling pin  
🍳 Baking sheet  
📜 Alkali-resistant baking paper  
🔪 Scoring knife  
🧤 Heat-resistant gloves  
🔥 Oven  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Make the matcha milk crisp filling: Place 60g milk powder, 30g softened butter, 8g matcha powder, 50g egg liquid, 2g salt, and 15g sugar in a bowl. Stir with a spatula until a smooth paste forms. Set aside.  
2. Make the dough: Add bread flour, cake flour, sea salt, 15g milk powder, dry yeast, and milk to the stand mixer bowl. Knead on low speed until the dough can be stretched into a "rough membrane."  
   **Key Point**: The dough is on the dry side; there is no need to knead to a windowpane membrane.  
3. Rest the dough: Remove the dough, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.  
4. Divide and round: Divide the dough into 10 equal portions. Round each portion, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for another 10 minutes.  
5. Shaping:  
   - Milk crisp logs: Take one dough portion, roll it into an oval sheet, evenly spread the milk crisp filling, leaving the top and edges bare. Roll tightly from top to bottom, pinch the seam and ends to seal, then roll into a log with tapered ends and a thicker middle.  
   - Lye knot: Take one dough portion, roll into a long thin strip. Fold it in half, twist the two ends around each other twice, then bring both ends down and press them into the center to form a knot.  
   - Large heart shape: Take two small dough portions, roll each into a long strip. Place them side by side, pinch one end together, lightly twist the two strands a few times, then bend the other end inward and pinch to form a heart shape.  
   **Key Point**: For filled dough, ensure the seams are completely sealed to prevent leakage during baking. The lye knot dough should be rolled thinner than other shapes.  
6. Freeze: Place the shaped dough pieces on a baking sheet and freeze for 30 minutes until firm.  
   **Note**: Do not cover with plastic wrap during freezing.  
7. Prepare the lye water: Wearing gloves, weigh 500g cold water in a bowl. Add 15g baking soda lye and gently stir until fully dissolved.  
   **Warning**: Baking soda lye is corrosive. Avoid skin and eye contact; clean up any spills immediately.  
8. Soak in lye water: Submerge the frozen dough pieces one by one in the lye water. Soak for about 30 seconds, then remove and let drain slightly.  
9. Score the buns: Transfer the dough pieces to a baking sheet lined with alkali-resistant baking paper. Use a scoring knife to make deep, wide cuts on the surface.  
10. Decorate: Sprinkle white sesame seeds over the lye knots and heart-shaped pieces.  
11. Bake: Preheat the oven to 200°C. Place the baking sheet in the middle rack and bake for about 18 minutes. The buns are done when the surface is an even deep brown color and the cuts have burst open.  
    **Key Point**: The bread will expand during baking. If adjacent buns stick together, gently separate them by hand while they are still warm.  

## Health Advice  
These matcha milk crisp lye rolls are high in calories and carbohydrates, making them suitable for post-workout energy replenishment. However, be sure to wear gloves when handling the lye water due to its corrosive nature. The milk crisp filling contains a significant amount of butter and sugar, so it is recommended to reduce the portion by half to avoid excessive calorie intake.
