# Waffles with Pan-Seared Steak
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 1 egg  
🍬 10g sugar  
🥛 65g milk  
🥛 5g heavy cream  
🧈 12g melted butter (for the batter)  
🌾 65g cake flour (all-purpose or bread flour can be used; cake flour yields softer waffles)  
🧂 2g baking powder  
🥩 1 piece of steak (thinly sliced)  
🍓 Strawberries, as needed (cut into heart shapes)  
🍌 1 banana (sliced)  
🫐 Blueberries, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed (to season the steak)  
🧂 Black pepper, as needed (to season the steak)  
🧈 A small piece of butter (for frying the steak)  
🌿 1 sprig fresh rosemary  
🍦 Vanilla yogurt, as needed (for decoration)  
🍯 Sea salt caramel sauce, as needed (for drizzling)  
🌱 1 mint leaf (for decoration)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🧇 Waffle maker  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔗 Tongs  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🍶 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. In a measuring cup, add the egg, granulated sugar, milk, heavy cream, and melted butter in sequence. Whisk with a hand whisk until well combined. Sift in the cake flour and baking powder, and stir until smooth and free of lumps.
2. Wash and hull the strawberries, then cut them into heart shapes. Peel the banana, trim the ends, and slice it in half lengthwise.
3. Preheat the waffle maker. Pour an appropriate amount of batter onto the cooking plate to cover it well. Close the lid and cook until the batter stops overflowing and the edges are set.
4. Heat a frying pan over high heat. Place the steak in the pan and sear both sides until golden brown and charred. Add the butter and rosemary, using tongs to gently press the steak so the aroma infuses. Remove the steak and let it rest.
   - Note: Since the steak is thin, it cooks quickly; avoid overcooking to prevent toughness.
5. Place the cooked waffle on a plate. Arrange the banana slices, heart-shaped strawberries, and blueberries on top. Place a spoonful of vanilla yogurt in the center, drizzle with sea salt caramel sauce, and garnish with a mint leaf.
6. Plate the rested steak alongside cherry tomatoes and the pan-fried rosemary. Serve.

## Health Advice  
This dish provides a balanced combination of carbohydrates and protein, suitable for muscle building or as a light brunch option. Note that thin steak slices should be cooked briefly to avoid toughness. Using cake flour makes the waffle batter softer, but the sugar and caramel sauce are relatively high in calories; it is recommended to control portions for better health.
