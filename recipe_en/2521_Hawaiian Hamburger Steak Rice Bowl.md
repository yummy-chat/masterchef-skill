# Hawaiian Hamburger Steak Rice Bowl
Other Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef 500g
🧅 Onion half (1/4 finely diced for meat filling, 1/4 sliced for sauce)
🍄 White mushrooms 100g (sliced)
🍚 Rice as needed (pre-cooked)
🥚 Eggs as needed (for sunny-side up fried eggs)
🧈 Butter a little (to enhance flavor when sautéing vegetables)
🧂 Salt as needed
🌶️ Black pepper as needed
🧄 Garlic powder as needed
🥫 Worcestershire sauce as needed
🌾 Flour 2 tbsp
🥣 Beef broth approx. 300 ml
🫒 Cooking oil as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-coated frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍳 Small saucepan
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Soup spoon/rice paddle

## Steps
1.  Finely dice 1/4 of the onion into very small pieces, and cut another 1/4 into thin strips; slice the white mushrooms thinly.
   **Note**: The diced onion must be cut extremely fine, otherwise the patties may fall apart easily.
2.  In a bowl, combine the ground beef with the diced onion, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and a suitable amount of Worcestershire sauce. Mix well with your hands until the mixture becomes sticky and cohesive.
3.  Take about 150-200g of the meat mixture. Toss it back and forth between your palms several times, then gently shape it into a round patty of even thickness.
   **Note**: Tossing helps make the patty firmer; do not press it too thin to keep it juicy.
4.  Heat a non-coated frying pan over high heat, then add cooking oil. Place the patty in the pan and immediately turn the heat to low. Fry until a crust forms on one side, then flip. Fry both sides until fully cooked, then remove and set aside.
   **Key**: After placing the patty in a hot pan over high heat, you must immediately turn to low heat to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
5.  Using the remaining oil in the pan, sauté the onion strips over medium-low heat until softened. Add the mushroom slices and continue sautéing until soft. You can add a small piece of butter for extra flavor.
6.  Sprinkle 2 tablespoons of flour into the pan and stir quickly until no dry flour remains. Pour in about 300 ml of beef broth, stir well, and season with salt, black pepper, and a suitable amount of Worcestershire sauce. Cook until the sauce comes to a boil and thickens.
   **Note**: The flour must be stirred thoroughly to prevent a raw flour taste; if the sauce is not thick enough, you can finally add a cornstarch slurry to adjust.
7.  Place a serving of rice in a bowl, top with the cooked patty, and generously ladle the mushroom and meat sauce over it.
8.  Fry a sunny-side up egg in another pan until the white is set but the yolk is still runny. Place it on top of the patty, and garnish with chopped green onions.
   **Tip**: The sunny-side up egg is the finishing touch for this dish.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for people who are building muscle or need an energy boost. However, the sauce and fried egg are relatively high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with a light salad to balance the nutrition. Control the amount of rice to avoid excessive calorie intake.
