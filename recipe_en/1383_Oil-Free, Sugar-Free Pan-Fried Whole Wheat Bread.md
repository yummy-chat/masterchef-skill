# Oil-Free, Sugar-Free Pan-Fried Whole Wheat Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 180g
🌾 Whole wheat flour 100g
🫙 Low-sugar active dry yeast 3g
🧂 Salt 3g
💧 Cold water 200g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥣 Large bowl
📏 Measuring cup
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍴 Fork
🔗 Spatula
♨️ Steamer lid with holes
🔪 Cutting board

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, add bread flour, whole wheat flour, salt, and low-sugar active dry yeast in sequence. Use chopsticks to mix all dry ingredients evenly.
   **Key point**: Bread flour is essential for a chewy texture; low-sugar yeast is suitable for the oil-free, sugar-free dough environment.

2. Gradually add cold water to the dry ingredients while stirring continuously with chopsticks until no dry flour remains, forming a sticky dough.
   **Key point**: Adjust the amount of water based on the flour's absorption; a sticky dough is normal.

3. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 20 minutes, allowing the dough to naturally develop basic gluten.
   **Judgment**: After resting, the dough will have some elasticity without needing to knead.

4. Wet your hands with a small amount of water, then repeatedly fold the dough from the edges toward the center 4-5 times until it becomes tight and difficult to fold. Finally, shape it into a smooth ball.
   **Key point**: The folding technique replaces kneading and efficiently strengthens the gluten. For a chewier texture, rest for another 20 minutes and repeat the folding once.

5. Cover the dough again with plastic wrap and place it in a warm environment to ferment until it doubles in size.
   **Judgment**: When poked with a finger, the hole does not spring back, indicating fermentation is complete.

6. Divide and shape the dough: Take 2 portions of dough and coat them with whole wheat flour or cornmeal. Gently flatten and press into a pizza base with a thin center and thicker edges. Divide the remaining dough into 4 portions, fold the edges toward the center to form round hamburger buns, and coat the surface with whole wheat flour.
   **Key point**: The thinner the center of the pizza base, the crispier it will be after frying. The hamburger buns should be round and smooth.

7. Cover the pizza base with plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 minutes. Place the hamburger buns in a warm area to ferment until they increase to 1.5 times their size.

8. **Pizza**: Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Place the pizza base in the pan, poke a few holes with a fork, and fry for about 5 minutes until the color changes. Flip and fry for another 5 minutes until the surface is crispy. Add toppings and cheese, pour a small amount of water along the edge of the pan, cover with a steamer lid with holes, and simmer over low heat until the cheese is fully melted.

9. **Hamburger buns**: Heat a non-stick pan over low heat. Place the fermented hamburger buns in the pan and fry for about 6 minutes until the bottom is golden. Flip and fry for another 5 minutes until both sides are golden brown and the buns spring back when pressed.

10. Uncooked dough pieces can be sealed and frozen. Thaw and reheat before use. Cooked hamburger buns can be sealed and frozen for up to 1 week. Before serving, moisten the surface and reheat.

## Health Tips
This whole wheat bread is oil-free and sugar-free, high in fiber and low in calories, making it especially suitable for sugar control and weight loss. Note that a sticky dough is normal; do not add extra flour to knead it. It is recommended to pair it with eggs or avocado for a more balanced nutrition.
