# Homemade Loaf Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 320g
🍬 White sugar 40g
🧂 Salt 3g
🥚 Eggs 2 (about 80g with shells)
🥛 Yogurt 190g (or replace with 150ml water or milk)
🍞 Yeast 4g
💧 Water 10ml (for dissolving yeast)
🧈 Butter 20g (chilled and cubed)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Dough scraper/bench scraper
📏 Rolling pin
🍞 Loaf pan
♨️ Oven
🫙 Plastic wrap
🧤 Heat-resistant gloves
🪵 Cutting board

## Instructions
1. Place the bread flour, white sugar, and salt in a bowl, and mix until well combined.
2. Crack in the eggs, add the yogurt, and stir until no dry flour remains.
3. Mix the yeast with 10ml of water to create a yeast liquid and set aside.
4. Transfer the dough to a cutting board. Use a kneading technique: hold one end of the dough with one hand, push it forward and then roll it back with the other hand. Repeat for about 15 minutes, until a初步 windowpane membrane forms.
   **Note**: Stickiness in the early stages is normal; continue kneading.
5. Pour the yeast liquid over the dough and gradually knead it in until fully incorporated.
   **Key point**: Using the delayed yeast method prevents the yeast from activating prematurely during the extended kneading process.
6. Add the chilled butter and continue kneading using the same technique until the butter is fully absorbed, the dough is smooth, and a strong, translucent windowpane membrane can be pulled.
   **Key point**: Chilled butter helps cool the dough, preventing it from fermenting during kneading.
7. Shape the dough into a ball, return it to the bowl, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and let it rise until doubled in size and the interior is honeycombed.
8. Remove the risen dough. Use a rolling pin to roll it out and release the gas until no further popping sounds are heard, then roll it into a log and divide it into 3 equal portions.
   **Note**: Deflate thoroughly, or the final texture will be coarse.
9. Take one portion and roll it out into a tongue shape. Fold the two ends towards the center, then roll it out into a long strip again. Starting from one end, roll it up into a cylinder. Repeat with the remaining two portions.
10. Place the rolled dough pieces side by side into the loaf pan, cover with plastic wrap, and perform a second proofing until the dough reaches 60% of the pan's height.
11. Without preheating, place the pan directly on the middle rack and bake at 160°C (top and bottom heat) for 40 minutes.
    **Key point**: Not preheating allows the dough to continue proofing briefly after entering the oven.
12. Immediately after baking, put on heat-resistant gloves, remove the pan, invert it to release the bread, and let it cool on a wire rack.

## Health Advice
This homemade loaf uses yogurt to replace part of the liquid, enhancing softness and flavor, making it suitable as a breakfast staple. Bread flour provides ample carbohydrates, ideal for those with high physical exertion. However, its sugar and butter content are not low, so it is advisable to control the portion size during weight loss.
