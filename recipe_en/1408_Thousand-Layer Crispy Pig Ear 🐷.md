# Thousand-Layer Crispy Pig Ear 🐷

**Cuisine:** Northeastern Chinese  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐷 Pig ears, 3 (approx. 950g)  
🧅 Scallion, 50g (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, 30g (sliced)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, 2g  
🌿 Cumin, 2g  
🌿 Bay leaves, 4 pieces  
🌿 Angelica dahurica, 2g (about 3 slices)  
🌿 Nutmeg, 3g (1 piece, cracked open)  
🌿 Star anise, 2g (2 large or 3 small)  
🌿 Cloves, 0.2g (3 buds, strong flavor, strictly control quantity)  
🌿 Galangal, 2g (about half an index finger length)  
🌿 Cinnamon stick, 1g (small piece, strictly control quantity)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, 5g (optional)  
🟡 Gardenia fruit, 3g (optional, for color)  
🧄 Garlic, 30g (pressed into paste)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies, 6g (sliced into rings)  
🌿 Cilantro, 5g (chopped)  
🍶 High-proof baijiu, as needed (for blanching to remove odor)  
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed (for frying spice base)  
🫙 Light soy sauce, 20g (for braising) + 30g (for dipping sauce)  
🫙 Dark soy sauce, 30g  
🍶 Huangjiu or Shaoxing wine, 15g  
🍬 Rock sugar or white sugar, 20g  
🧂 Salt, 30g  
🧂 MSG, 5g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 5g  
💧 Water, approx. 4000ml (for braising stock)  
🍶 Chinkiang vinegar, 20g  
🌰 Sesame oil, 5g  

## Tools & Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
♨️ Pressure cooker (or deep pot)  
📌 Toothpicks  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (2)  
🪓 Cutting board  

## Preparation Steps
1. Use a blowtorch to burn the pig ears inside and out until the surface is charred black, paying special attention to crevices and ear canals. Then soak in warm water for 5 minutes and scrub clean.
2. Place the cleaned pig ears in cold water, add a splash of high-proof baijiu, bring to a boil over high heat, flip, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 3 minutes to fully blanch and remove impurities. Drain and rinse with warm water.
3. Add cold oil to the pan, toss in scallion sections and ginger slices, fry over high heat until lightly golden, then turn off the heat. Once the oil temperature drops slightly, add all spices except the gardenia fruit and stir briefly to release fragrance.  
   **Note:** Turn off the heat before adding spices to prevent burning or oil splatter when liquid is added later.  
   (Pour cold oil into the pan, add scallion and ginger, fry over high heat until lightly golden, then turn off the heat. Let the oil cool slightly, then add all spices except the gardenia fruit and stir a few times to release aroma.  
   **Note:** Turn off the heat before adding spices to avoid burning or oil splatter when adding liquid.)
4. Turn the heat back to medium, add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, huangjiu, rock sugar, salt, MSG, and chicken bouillon. Stir and cook until the sauce begins to bubble, then turn off the heat.
5. Place the blanched pig ears into a pressure cooker. Pour in the fried spice mixture and liquid, add the gardenia fruit, and add about 4000ml of hot water.  
   - Electric pressure cooker: Select the "Beef/Mutton" function.  
   - Stovetop pressure cooker: Cook for 30 minutes after reaching pressure.  
   - Regular pot: Add more water and simmer for about 90 minutes.  
   - **Key:** Cook until the pig ears are very tender. Release pressure immediately once done; do not let them sit.  
   (For electric pressure cooker: Select the "Beef/Mutton" setting. For stovetop pressure cooker: Press for half an hour. For a regular pot: Add more water and simmer for an hour and a half.  
   **Key:** Cook until the pig ears are very soft. Release pressure and open the lid immediately when done; do not leave them to steep.)
6. Remove the pig ears and let them cool slightly. On a sheet of plastic wrap, layer the pig ears alternately (front and back sides facing opposite directions), placing thicker parts in the center. Lift the two ends of the plastic wrap and roll tightly into a compact cylinder, adjusting as you go. Secure the ends.  
   **Key:** Roll as tightly as possible to avoid a long, thin shape.  
   (Take out the pig ears and let them cool a bit. On the plastic wrap, stack the pig ears alternately, thick parts in the middle. Lift the two ends of the wrap and roll firmly, adjusting as you go, to form a tight, thick cylinder. Secure both ends.  
   **Key:** Roll as tightly as possible; do not roll too long or thin.)
7. Use toothpicks to poke evenly spaced holes in the rolled pig ear log to release internal liquid. Place it in a stainless steel bowl, cover with a plate, then weight it down with a water-filled bowl and a cutting board. Press firmly overnight to set.
8. Remove the plastic wrap. Cut the pig ear log in half, then slice into slightly thick pieces and arrange on a plate.
9. In a small bowl, mix garlic paste, bird's eye chili rings, chopped cilantro, light soy sauce, chinkiang vinegar, and sesame oil to make a dipping sauce. Serve alongside the pig ear slices.

## Health Tips
This Thousand-Layer Crispy Pig Ear is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity. However, the braising process adds significant salt; those with high blood pressure should use less light soy sauce in the dipping sauce. While pig ears are a treat, avoid overconsumption in one sitting, as they are relatively high in fat.
