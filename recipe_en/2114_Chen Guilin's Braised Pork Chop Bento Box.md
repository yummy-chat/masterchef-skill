# Chen Guilin's Braised Pork Chop Bento Box
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🥩 Bone-in pork loin chop 1 slice (about 120g, choose a thicker cut)
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves
🥬 Cabbage about 150g
🦐 Dried shrimp 1 tablespoon
🥚 Egg 1 (pre-braised)
🌭 Taiwanese sausage half (sliced)
🥬 Preserved radish (Taiwanese style) to taste
🍚 Rice 1 serving
🌾 Sweet potato starch 20g

## Seasonings
🍶 Rice wine 1 teaspoon
🥡 Light soy sauce 2 teaspoons
🍬 Sugar 1/4 teaspoon
🌫️ White pepper 1 pinch
🍂 Five-spice powder 1 pinch
🧂 Baking soda 1/4 teaspoon
🧂 Salt 1 pinch
🫗 Old braising liquid 1 bowl (or substitute with fresh braising liquid)
🥣 Braising broth to taste

## Tools and Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🔨 Meat mallet (or knife back)
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🔗 Tongs
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🍱 Bento box
🥄 Ladle
🍚 Rice cooker

## Instructions
### Preparation
1. Use scissors to cut the white membrane along the edge of the bone-in pork loin chop. Then, evenly pound both sides with a meat mallet or the back of a knife to tenderize the meat.
**Key point**: The membrane must be completely cut; apply even force when pounding.
2. Add salt and baking soda to water, then soak the pork chop for half an hour.
**Key point**: The ratio of baking soda to salt should be appropriate; soak thoroughly for tenderizing.
3. Remove the pork chop from the water and place it in a bowl. Add crushed garlic cloves, rice wine, light soy sauce, sugar, white pepper, and five-spice powder. Mix well and marinate for at least 2 hours.
**Key point**: The longer the marination, the more flavorful; marinate in the refrigerator.
4. Cut the cabbage into pieces; prepare minced garlic and dried shrimp; slice Taiwanese sausage; set aside preserved radish.
5. Braise the egg in braising liquid and set aside.
### Cooking
1. Lightly coat both sides of the marinated pork chop with a thin layer of sweet potato starch. Fry in a pan with hot oil over medium heat until both sides are golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil.
**Key point**: The sweet potato starch coating should be thin and even; the oil temperature should not be too high; fry until the exterior is golden and set.
2. In the same pan, add a little oil. Stir-fry minced garlic and dried shrimp until fragrant. Add the cabbage and stir-fry until just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
**Heat**: Stir-fry over medium-high heat; keep the cabbage crisp-tender.
3. Submerge the fried pork chop in the old braising liquid for about 10 seconds, then immediately remove. This allows the exterior to absorb the braising liquid while remaining semi-crisp.
**Key point**: The soaking time should be short to prevent the exterior from becoming completely soft.
4. Place hot rice in a bento box and drizzle a ladle of braising broth over it. Arrange the halved braised egg, stir-fried cabbage with dried shrimp, preserved radish, and sliced sausage. Finally, place the whole braised pork chop on top.
**Key point**: Arrange neatly; pouring braising broth over the rice adds flavor.

## Health Tips
This braised pork chop bento box is paired with cabbage and dried shrimp, providing both protein and vitamins. It is suitable for muscle building or busy workdays. However, the use of baking soda for marination and the frying process can result in higher sodium and calorie content. It is recommended to balance it with light vegetables the next day.
