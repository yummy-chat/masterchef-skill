# Bamboo-Pressed Da Dao Golden Silk Noodles  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Egg yolks 130g (choose deep yellow yolks from corn-fed chickens)  
🌾 Flour 200g  
🥬 Cabbage hearts, as needed  
🍲 Boiled cabbage clear broth, as needed (traditional clear broth, requires long simmering)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥣 Metal bowl  
🪵 Large bamboo pole (for pressing the dough)  
🥢 Small rolling pin  
🗡️ Large knife (specially for slicing into thin strips)  
🪑 Chopping board  
🍳 Pot  
🍜 Noodle bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the egg yolks and flour in a metal bowl. Use your hands to thoroughly knead until no white dry flour remains and the mixture forms a uniform yellow paste.  
**Note**: Knead thoroughly; no white dry flour should remain.  

2. Form the mixed ingredients into a dough. Repeatedly knead using a forward-pushing motion until the dough surface is smooth, color is evenly deep yellow, and it is soft and elastic. Do not add any coloring during the kneading process.  
3. Place the dough on the chopping board. Sit on the bamboo pole and press down forcefully repeatedly, performing widening and lengthening operations. Repeat 4-5 times until the dough is pressed into a thin sheet about 0.5 meters wide and 1.4 meters long.  
**Key point**: This traditional handcraft requires even pressure to press the dough firmly and uniformly thin, which is very physically demanding.  

4. Remove the bamboo pole. Use a small rolling pin to roll outward from the center, gradually flattening and enlarging the dough sheet. Repeat the operation to double its size, finally achieving a sheet about 0.5 meters wide, 1.4 meters long, and uniformly thin.  
5. Fold the rolled dough sheet neatly. Stand in a T-step posture, use one hand to hold the dough sheet with a Guanyin palm technique, and hold the large knife in the other hand to slice it into thin strips with full focus. The sliced noodles should be as fine as golden silk.  
**Note**: Concentrate fully to avoid cutting your hand; do not get distracted.  

6. Bring the boiled cabbage clear broth to a boil in a pot. Add the cut golden silk noodles and cook for only 5 seconds, then immediately remove and place into a bowl. Garnish with cabbage hearts, add a small amount of red sauce, and pour in the clear broth to serve.  
**Note**: The noodles are extremely thin; do not overcook, or they will become mushy.  

## Health Advice  
These golden silk noodles are made solely with egg yolks and flour, so they are high in protein and cholesterol. It is recommended to pair them with plenty of vegetables or enjoy them as an occasional indulgent staple. Suitable for replenishing energy in those with high physical exertion, but individuals with high cholesterol should control portion sizes.
