# Vegetable Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥬 Cabbage: appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🎃 Pumpkin: appropriate amount (sliced, with skin)  
🥕 Carrot: appropriate amount (sliced, with skin)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms: 3-4 pieces (score a cross on the surface)  
🦐 Dried shrimp: appropriate amount (to enhance umami)  
🫒 Olive oil: a small amount  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (optional)  
🍶 Miso: appropriate amount (optional)  
🌿 Black pepper: appropriate amount (optional)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥘 Deep pot (with glass lid)  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  
📦 Vacuum sealer (for storing leftover ingredients)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the cabbage into pieces, slice the pumpkin and carrot with the skin on, and score a cross on the surface of the shiitake mushrooms.
   **Note**: Keep the skin on the pumpkin and carrot to maximize the retention of nutrients and dietary fiber.
2. Place the excess cut vegetables into vacuum bags, seal them with a vacuum sealer, and refrigerate; they can be stored for 3-4 days.
3. In a deep pot, layer the cabbage pieces, pumpkin slices, and carrot slices in order.
4. Place the shiitake mushrooms with the cross score facing up on top of the vegetables, and sprinkle in the dried shrimp.
   **Key**: The shiitake mushrooms and dried shrimp form the umami core, effectively enhancing the savory richness of the vegetable soup.
5. Drizzle in a small amount of olive oil.
6. Add enough water to fully submerge all the ingredients.
7. Cover with the lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for about half an hour.
   **Doneness check**: The soup should be clear, the ingredients tender and cooked through, and a complex sweet and savory aroma should be released.
8. Based on personal taste, decide whether to add salt, miso, or black pepper.
   **Note**: Dried shrimp are naturally salty; those who prefer a lighter taste need not add extra salt. The soup base already carries the natural sweetness of the vegetables and the umami of the mushrooms and seafood, so seasoning should be kept simple.

## Health Advice  
This vegetable soup is rich in dietary fiber and various vitamins. It is low in fat and healthy, especially suitable for those who need to manage weight or improve digestion. It is recommended to cook the pumpkin and carrot with the skin on to retain more comprehensive nutrients, but be mindful of the portion size to avoid bloating.
