# Century Egg and Lean Pork Congee  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 **Ingredients**  
* Century eggs (preserved duck eggs)  
* Lean pork  
* Rice  
* Scallions  
* Cilantro  
* Lettuce  
* Ginger  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
* Soy sauce  
* Oyster sauce  
* Salt  
* White pepper  
* Cooking oil  

🥄 **Utensils**  
* Rice cooker  
* Small bowls (several)  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and place it into the rice cooker inner pot. Add 1 liter of drinking water.  
2. Wash the lean pork, pat dry, and rub it evenly with 10ml of cooking oil. Place it into the rice cooker inner pot.  
3. Remove the shells from the century eggs and rinse them. Cut the egg whites into small pieces, crumble the yolks, and add both into the rice cooker inner pot.  
4. Wash and peel the ginger, cut it into thin strips, and place them into the rice cooker inner pot.  
5. Wash the scallions, trim the roots, chop them finely, and set aside in a small bowl.  
6. Wash the cilantro, trim the roots, chop it finely, and set aside in a small bowl.  
7. Wash the lettuce, trim the roots, chop it finely, and set aside in a small bowl.  
8. Mix the soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and white pepper together until well combined, and set aside in a small bowl.  
9. Start the rice cooker’s congee mode and cook the main ingredients until done.  
10. Once the main ingredients are cooked, blanch the lettuce separately in hot water. Open the lid, add the lettuce, scallions, and cilantro to the congee, and stir to combine.  
11. Open the lid, add the prepared sauce to the congee, and stir well.  

## Notes  
* It is recommended to wear disposable gloves when measuring and handling ingredients.  
* Vegetables and herbs can be washed and dried in advance for later use.  
* If serving as breakfast, the main ingredients can be cooked the night before and kept warm until the next morning, then add the toppings and sauce.  

## Health Tips  
Century egg and lean pork congee is nutritionally balanced. Century eggs are rich in minerals but high in sodium, so it is advisable for people with high blood pressure to consume in moderation. Lean pork provides high-quality protein, and the addition of lettuce increases dietary fiber, making it a gentle breakfast option suitable for those with poor digestion.
