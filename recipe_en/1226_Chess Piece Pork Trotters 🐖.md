# Chess Piece Pork Trotters 🐖
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 1 pig trotter (front or hind trotter)
🥩 Ground pork, as needed
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (chopped)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped)
🌶️ Green chili peppers, as needed (minced)
🌶️ Red chili peppers, as needed (minced)
🥚 Eggs, as needed (for the frying batter)
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (for dusting and batter)
⭐ Star anise (Chinese star anise), 2-3 pieces
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small handful
🧄 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections and slices)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 3-4 pieces
🍶 Shaoxing wine, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🍬 Sugar, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌫️ White pepper powder, as needed
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🪚 Hand saw (hacksaw)
🔪 Chef's knife
♨️ Pressure cooker (electric/gas)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥣 Bowls
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Scorch the Pig Trotters**: Hold the raw pig trotter over the gas stove flame and scorch it until the skin turns brown and charred.
   **Key Point**: It must be scorched until the color changes to thoroughly remove the gamey smell and any small hair stubs.

2. **Wash and Scrape**: Place the scorched pig trotter in warm water and scrape the charred surface clean with a chef's knife. Rinse thoroughly.

3. **Saw into Pieces**: Use the hand saw to cut the pig trotter into 3-4 cm thick round sections, making the cut surfaces neat and flat like chess pieces.
   **Key Point**: Using a hand saw prevents bone splintering, ensuring a neat and attractive shape.

4. **Prepare the Meat Filling**: Add salt, sesame oil, white pepper powder, chopped cilantro, chopped scallions, and minced green and red chili peppers to the ground pork. Stir in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky and firm.

5. **Blanch to Set Shape**: Place the pig trotter pieces in a pot of cold water. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and Shaoxing wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Blanch without covering the pot for at least 15 minutes, until the meat tightens, the interior changes color, and no more large amounts of foam appear. Remove and rinse with warm water.
   **Key Point**: Must start with cold water, use plenty of water, and do not cover the pot to help release blood and odor.

6. **Sauté Aromatics**: Heat oil in the wok. Add star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic slices, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the aromatics turn slightly yellow and their fragrance is fully released.

7. **Braise and Season**: Pour Shaoxing wine and light soy sauce over the aromatics. Add the blanched pig trotter pieces and enough boiling water. Then add sugar, salt, white pepper powder, and dark soy sauce for color. Stir to combine, bring to a boil, then transfer everything to a pressure cooker.

8. **Pressure Cook**: Pressure cook in an electric pressure cooker for 40 minutes, or in a traditional gas pressure cooker for 25 minutes once pressure is reached, until the pig trotters are tender and falling off the bone, easily pierced with chopsticks.

9. **Fill with Meat and Dust with Starch**: Let the cooked pig trotter pieces cool slightly until they are no longer too hot to handle. Fill the center holes generously with the prepared meat filling. Then dust the filled side with a layer of cornstarch.

10. **Mix the Frying Batter**: Beat eggs and add an appropriate amount of cornstarch. Stir into a smooth, lump-free batter.

11. **Fry Until Golden Brown on Both Sides**: Heat a non-stick frying pan with a small amount of oil. Place the filled pig trotter pieces meat-side down into the pan. Fry over medium-low heat until the meat filling side is golden brown and set. Flip them over, brush the other side with the egg-starch batter, and continue frying until both sides are golden brown and the outer skin is slightly crispy and fragrant.

12. **Trim and Plate**: Remove the fried pig trotters and use kitchen scissors to trim the edges, making their shape more like round chess pieces. Optionally, drizzle a little of the reserved braising liquid thickened with a cornstarch slurry for extra color and gloss. Arrange on a plate and serve.

## Health Advice
Pig trotters are rich in collagen, making them suitable for those needing to improve skin elasticity. However, they are high in fat, so it is recommended to pair them with vegetables for balanced nutrition. Adding the meat filling increases protein content, but the frying step raises the calorie count. To reduce this, use less oil or fry with minimal oil in the non-stick pan.
