# Private Recipe Wontons
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork tenderloin 500g (lean, without sinew)  
🥚 Preserved duck eggs 3 (optional, for preserved egg and pork filling)  
🥟 Wonton wrappers as needed  
🫚 Ginger 5g  
🧄 Garlic 5g  
🦐 Dried shrimp as needed  
🍘 Dried seaweed as needed  
🌿 Cilantro as needed  
🧅 Chopped green onion as needed  
💧 Water 220g (yields about 200g ginger-garlic water after filtering)  
⚪ Baking soda 3g  
🧂 Salt 5g (for filling) + to taste (for broth)  
🧂 Chicken powder 3g  
🧂 MSG 3g  
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.5g (for filling) + a pinch (for broth)  
🍬 White sugar 8g (for filling) + a pinch (for broth)  
🫒 Sesame oil 5g (for filling) + half a tablespoon (for broth)  
🍯 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon (for broth)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🫗 Strainer/sieve  
📦 Plastic wrap/container  
🌀 Meat grinder  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🫓 Rolling pin  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍜 Bowl  
🥄 Soup ladle/spoon  
🌡️ Thermometer (optional)  

## Instructions  
1. **Pre-process meat and ginger-garlic water**: Slice the pork tenderloin into thin pieces and spread them flat on a plate. Smash the ginger and garlic, place them into 220g water, and squeeze with your hands until the water turns cloudy. Strain to obtain about 200g ginger-garlic water. Store the meat slices and ginger-garlic water separately, then place both in the freezer for about 2 hours.  
   **Note**: The meat must be completely lean and free of sinew; ginger-garlic water is more effective at removing odors than scallion-ginger water and cannot be substituted; freezing at low temperature is key to the filling’s bouncy texture and must be done.  
2. **Initial seasoning**: Place the frozen meat slices into the meat grinder. Add baking soda, salt, chicken powder, MSG, white pepper powder, sugar, and sesame oil.  
   **Note**: All seasoning proportions must be accurately weighed; baking soda tenderizes the meat and must not be omitted or overused.  
3. **Gradually mix until firm**: Remove the ginger-garlic water, now frozen into a slushy state, and break up the ice layer. Add it to the meat filling in three batches: first, add 1/3 and pulse the grinder 20 times; second, add half of the remaining ice water and pulse 20 times; third, add all the remaining ice water and pulse 20 times. Finally, scrape down the filling, let it rest briefly, then continue pulsing in short bursts of about 5 seconds each for 15 times. The finished filling should be smooth, glossy, and paste-like. When lifted with a spatula, it should hold its shape. The overall temperature should feel icy cold to the touch.  
   **Note**: Keep the mixture cold throughout to prevent the filling from becoming watery due to heat from grinding; pulsing prevents the machine from overheating.  
4. **Make preserved egg and pork filling**: Peel and mash 3 preserved duck eggs. Mix half of the prepared meat filling with the mashed eggs until well combined. The other half can be used as plain pork filling.  
5. **Prepare wonton wrappers**: Take a stack of store-bought wonton wrappers. Use a rolling pin to gently roll them thinner from the center outward until the wrappers are semi-transparent.  
   **Note**: Rolling thinner makes the wonton wrappers more tender and less likely to stick together.  
6. **Assemble wontons**: Place a small amount of filling in the center of a rolled-out wonton wrapper. Optionally, add a little mashed preserved egg on top. Fold the wrapper diagonally to form a triangle, then bring the two bottom corners together and pinch firmly to seal, shaping the wonton like a small fish.  
   **Note**: Use less filling rather than more to prevent the wrappers from breaking during cooking or the wontons from expanding excessively and losing their shape.  
7. **Prepare broth base**: In a bowl, add dried shrimp, seaweed, chopped green onion, cilantro, a pinch of white pepper powder, a pinch of sugar, 1 tablespoon light soy sauce, and half a tablespoon sesame oil. Adjust salt and MSG to taste.  
8. **Cook wontons**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Gently add the wontons. Once the water returns to a boil, reduce to medium heat and cook for about 90 seconds until all wontons float to the surface, the wrappers are semi-transparent, and the filling appears puffed.  
   **Note**: Do not overcook; the filling will expand first and then slightly shrink after cooking, which is normal.  
9. **Serve**: First, ladle a spoonful of hot wonton cooking water into the prepared bowl to release the aromas, then scoop the wontons into the bowl.  

## Health Advice  
These wontons are made with lean pork tenderloin and ice water, resulting in a high-protein, low-fat dish suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those managing fat intake. However, note that baking soda and preserved duck eggs are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should monitor their consumption.
