# Tiger Skin Pork Knuckle
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 1 pork knuckle
🧅 Scallions, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🫘 Star anise, as needed
🌿 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🌰 Cardamom, as needed
🍃 Bay leaves, as needed
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, as needed
🌸 Sand ginger (Kaempferia galanga), as needed
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🌸 Cloves, as needed
🌿 Fennel seeds, as needed
🌼 Gardenia fruit (yellow dye), as needed
🍶 High-proof baijiu (liquor), as needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Table salt, about half a teaspoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪝 Meat hook
🪣 Water basin
🪥 Bamboo skewer (for poking holes)

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the surface of the pork knuckle thoroughly.
2. Place the knuckle in a pot with cold water. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and high-proof baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat.
**Note**: Must start with cold water; remove the knuckle when the water boils, the skin changes color, and scum rises to the surface.

3. While still hot, use a bamboo skewer to densely poke holes all over the knuckle skin. Then pour dark soy sauce over the skin and rub it evenly with your hands.
**Key**: Poking holes must be done while hot and densely—this is crucial for forming the tiger skin pattern. Let it air dry briefly after applying the soy sauce.

4. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot (approx. 210°C/410°F). Use a meat hook to lift the knuckle and lower it into the oil, skin side down.
**Heat management**: Start with medium heat, then reduce to low and fry slowly for about 7–8 minutes, until the skin turns a deep reddish-brown and develops a wrinkled, tiger-like pattern. Remove from oil.
**Note**: Be careful of hot oil splattering.

5. Immediately place the fried knuckle into cold water, skin side down, to soak.
**Key**: The thermal shock (hot to cold) makes the tiger skin wrinkles more pronounced.

6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Sauté scallion segments and ginger slices until fragrant, then add the mixed dry spices and stir-fry until aromatic.
7. Add enough water to the wok at once and bring to a boil. Submerge the prepared knuckle into the water, then add the remaining dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and salt.
**Note**: Add water all at once—do not add water midway through cooking.

8. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Slow-simmer until the knuckle is completely tender and falls apart easily.

## Health Advice
This tiger skin pork knuckle is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those needing protein supplementation or seeking skin benefits. However, it is high in fat and salt; individuals with high blood pressure, high blood lipids, or high blood sugar should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the richness and control portion sizes.
