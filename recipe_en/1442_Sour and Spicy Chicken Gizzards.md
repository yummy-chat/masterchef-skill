# Sour and Spicy Chicken Gizzards
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Chicken gizzards (or duck gizzards) 2 pieces
🌶️ Pickled chilies 8~10 (chopped)
🌶️ Wild mountain chilies (small chili pickled) 5~8 (chopped)
🧅 Garlic sprouts (garlic scapes) 3~4 stalks (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (some cut into strips for marinating, some diced for stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic 5~6 cloves (diced)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for cleaning and marinating)
🍶 White vinegar appropriate amount (for cleaning and removing odor)
🫑 White pepper powder appropriate amount (for marinating)
🦪 Oyster sauce about 1 tablespoon (for marinating)
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for marinating and stir-frying)
🥫 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (for coloring)
🥫 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (for enhancing freshness)
🧂 Chicken essence appropriate amount (before removing from heat)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl/basin
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer/wok spatula
🍽️ Bowls and plates
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. **Score the gizzards**
   After cleaning the gizzards, first cut dense, continuous crosswise incisions on the surface, then cut into slices lengthwise. The scoring causes the gizzards to curl quickly during high-heat stir-frying, resulting in a crispy and tender texture that absorbs flavors well.
2. **Clean and remove odor**
   Add salt and white vinegar to the scored gizzards, rub and squeeze thoroughly with your hands, then rinse under running water until the water runs clear. Squeeze out excess water.
**Note**: This dish does not blanch the gizzards; this step is the key alternative method for removing odor and blood.

3. **Marinate for flavor**
   Place the cleaned gizzards in a bowl, add ginger strips, salt, white pepper powder, oyster sauce, and cooking wine. Stir evenly with chopsticks and set aside to marinate. Marinating allows the base flavor to penetrate and further removes odor.
4. **Prepare the aromatics**
   Chop the pickled chilies and wild mountain chilies, cut the garlic sprouts into sections, dice the garlic cloves, and cut the ginger into strips and dice separately. Pickled chilies and wild mountain chilies are the core flavors and must be chopped to fully release their sour and spicy taste.
5. **Quick-fry the gizzards**
   Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok until about 70% hot. Add the marinated gizzards and quickly stir-fry over high heat until they separate, about ten seconds. When the gizzard slices curl and change color, immediately remove and drain the oil.
**Key**: The oil must be hot and the cooking time short to maintain crispness and tenderness.

6. **Prepare the sour and spicy base**
   Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. First, sauté the diced ginger and garlic until fragrant. Then add the chopped pickled chilies and wild mountain chilies, stir-fry over medium-low heat until the oil turns red and the aroma is released. This step forms the foundation of the "sour and spicy" composite flavor.
7. **Combine and season**
   Return the quick-fried gizzards to the wok. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly to combine. Drizzle a small amount of cooking wine along the edge of the wok. Add dark soy sauce for color and light soy sauce for freshness. Continue stir-frying until the gizzards are evenly coated with sauce and aromatics.
8. **Finish and serve**
   Just before removing from heat, add the garlic sprout sections and chicken essence. Quickly stir-fry until the garlic sprouts are just cooked but still crisp. Plate immediately.
**Note**: Add the garlic sprouts at the very end to maintain their bright green color and fresh aroma.

## Health Notes
Gizzards are rich in iron and protein, beneficial for blood enrichment and immune system enhancement. The sour and spicy flavors from pickled chilies and wild mountain chilies can stimulate appetite, but those with excessive stomach acid or gastric ulcers should consume sparingly. Note that salt and oyster sauce contain sodium; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of seasonings used.
