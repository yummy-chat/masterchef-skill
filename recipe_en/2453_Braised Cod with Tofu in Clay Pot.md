# Braised Cod with Tofu in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 1 cod (scaled, gutted, cut into 5 cm pieces)  
🍥 1 block of northern tofu (about 300-400 g, cut into 3 cm thick pieces)  
🥓 50-100 g pork belly (thinly sliced)  
🧅 1 section of scallion (cut into segments)  
🧄 5-6 cloves of garlic (smashed flat)  
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (minced)  
🫘 75 g northeastern soybean paste (yellow bean paste)  
🍶 75 g mixed soy sauce (a blend of light and dark soy sauce)  
🌶️ Chili flakes, to taste  
🌸 1 g Sichuan peppercorns  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🍳 Iron wok (frying pan)  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Water scoop  

## Instructions  
1. Trim the fins off the cod with scissors. Use a knife to scrape off the fine scales on the skin. Cut open the belly and remove the innards. Tear off the black membrane and scrape away the blood attached to the bone. Rinse thoroughly and cut into pieces about 5 cm long.  
**Note**: The black membrane and blood on the bone are the main sources of fishy odor and must be completely removed.

2. Cut the northern tofu into large pieces about 3 cm thick, spread them out, and place in the clay pot. Thinly slice the pork belly, cut the scallion into segments, mince the ginger, and smash the garlic cloves flat.
3. Heat the iron wok over high heat. Add oil and swirl to coat the wok until it smokes. Discard the hot oil and add fresh cold oil.
4. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the slices become crispy and fragrant.
5. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and fry over low heat until the numbing aroma is released, then remove them.
6. Add the scallion segments, minced ginger, and smashed garlic. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Return the pork belly to the wok and stir-fry to combine.
7. Add the chili flakes and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Then add the soybean paste and stir-fry for about 1 minute. Finally, add the mixed soy sauce and stir-fry for 30 seconds, allowing the rich aroma of the sauce to develop.  
**Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning.

8. Pour in an appropriate amount of broth or water, stir well to form a sauce.
9. Pour the sauce into the clay pot. Bring to a boil over high heat and maintain a high heat for 15 minutes.  
**Key**: Cooking over high heat allows the tofu to puff up, the fish to absorb flavor, and the broth to thicken.

10. Serve and enjoy. The tofu is tender yet firm, and the fish is dense with a rich, savory sauce.

## Health Advice  
Cod is low in fat and high in protein. Paired with tofu for calcium and pork belly for added richness, this dish is suitable for weight loss or for those needing extra protein. Note that the soybean paste and soy sauce are high in salt; those with high blood pressure should limit their intake of the broth.
