# Ice Cube Crucian Carp Soup 🐟
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 2 crucian carp
🧅 1 large piece of ginger (sliced into ginger slices)
🥬 1/2 white radish (cut into thin strips)
🌿 3 stalks of celery (finely chopped)
🧊 1 large bowl of ice cubes (core ingredient)
🧂 Salt, to taste (half a spoonful for the soup, a pinch for marinating)
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste (used in two separate additions)
🍶 A splash of white vinegar (for marinating the radish strips)
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for frying the fish and stir-frying)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Fine-mesh strainer
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Soup ladle

## Preparation Steps
1.  Clean the crucian carp, removing the black membrane inside the belly.
   **Key point**: This is a crucial step to remove the earthy fishy smell.
2.  Cut the white radish into thin strips, add salt and white vinegar, toss to combine, and let marinate briefly to remove any raw, bitter taste and maintain crispness.
3.  Slice or shred the ginger, finely chop the celery. Prepare one large bowl of ice cubes.
4.  Heat the wok thoroughly, pour in vegetable oil. Add the crucian carp and ginger slices. Pan-fry over medium heat until both sides of the fish are golden brown and slightly charred.
   **Key point**: The wok must be very hot; this frying step is the foundation for creating a milky white soup.
5.  Pour the entire bowl of ice cubes directly into the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer. Observe the soup changing from clear to cloudy, finally turning milky white.
   **Key point**: Adding ice cubes is the core technique of this recipe, demonstrating that the soup's color is not strongly related to the water temperature.
6.  Use the back of a soup ladle to mash the crucian carp meat. Continue simmering to concentrate the soup, accelerating the release of proteins and fats, making the broth richer.
7.  Add white pepper powder to season and remove any fishiness. Once cooked, pour the entire soup through a fine-mesh strainer into a large bowl, filtering out all fish bones, spines, and residue.
   **Key point**: Filtration is essential to ensure the final product is bone-free, safe, and easy to consume.
8.  Heat another wok, add vegetable oil. Add the ginger shreds and the marinated radish strips, stir-frying quickly. Add a little more white pepper powder, then pour the filtered fish soup into the wok.
   **Key point**: Marinating and stir-frying the radish strips helps them maintain a crisp texture.
9.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for about 2-3 minutes, until the radish strips are cooked through but still retain some crunch.
10. Add half a spoonful of salt and the chopped celery. Stir well and turn off the heat.
    **Key point**: Salt should be added at the very end to avoid adding it too early, which could hinder the release of the soup's fresh flavor.

## Health Tip
This method of adding ice cubes to crucian carp soup rapidly locks in proteins and fats, resulting in a broth that is rich and milky. It is especially suitable for warming the body and supplementing nutrition during winter. As crucian carp have many bones, filtering the soup makes it safer to drink, allowing the elderly and children to enjoy it with ease.
