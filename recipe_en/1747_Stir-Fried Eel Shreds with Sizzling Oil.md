# Stir-Fried Eel Shreds with Sizzling Oil
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

🛒 **Ingredients**
🐟 Eel shreds
🧄 Garlic
🫚 Ginger, minced
🧅 Garlic, minced
🌿 Scallions, chopped

🧂 **Seasonings**
🍶 Cooking wine
🥄 Light soy sauce
🍯 Oyster sauce
🥄 Dark soy sauce
🧂 Salt
🍬 Granulated sugar
🌶️ White pepper powder
🌽 Cornstarch
💧 Water
🐖 Lard
🥜 Vegetable oil (for greasing the pan)

🍳 **Utensils**
🔪 Knife
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl (for mixing cornstarch slurry)
🥄 Spatula
🔥 Stove

📝 **Instructions**
1. Cut the eel into three sections, then slice into thin shreds.
2. Add 0.5g white pepper powder and 3g cooking wine to the eel shreds and mix well. Then add 5g sesame oil and marinate for a moment.
3. Heat the wok, grease it with oil, then add vegetable oil and heat to 60% hot.
4. Add half of the minced garlic and all of the minced ginger, and stir-fry a few times.
5. Add the eel shreds and stir-fry over medium-high heat for 30 seconds.
6. Drizzle 10g cooking wine along the edge of the wok and stir-fry a few times.
7. Add light soy sauce and stir well.
8. Add oyster sauce and dark soy sauce, and stir-fry a few times.
9. Add salt, granulated sugar, and 3g white pepper powder, and stir well.
10. Mix cornstarch with water to make a slurry, pour it into the wok, and cook until the sauce thickens.
11. Plate the dish and sprinkle with the remaining minced garlic and chopped scallions.
12. Heat another pan, add lard and heat to 70% hot, then pour the sizzling oil over the eel shreds.

⚠️ **Key Tips**
- Do not over-rinse the eel blood, or the flesh may turn dark and develop an odor.
- You can ask the vendor to pre-slaughter and prepare the eel.
- Adding extra white pepper powder and sugar helps remove fishy flavor and enhance aroma.

## Health Advice

Suitable for those seeking tender texture, though people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
