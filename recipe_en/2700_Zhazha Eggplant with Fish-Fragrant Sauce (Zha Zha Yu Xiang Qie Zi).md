# Zhazha Eggplant with Fish-Fragrant Sauce (Zha Zha Yu Xiang Qie Zi)
Cuisine: Common Home-Cooked Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍆 1 purple long eggplant (cut into long strips)
🧈 1 piece of firm tofu (crumbled by hand)
🥩 1 small piece of pork belly (about 50-100g, minced)
🧄 3 cloves of garlic (crushed and minced)
🧅 Some scallions (chopped)
🌽 Some cornstarch (for coating the eggplant surface)
🧂 About 1 tsp salt (for salting the eggplant)
🌶️ 1 tbsp chopped chili sauce
🥫 1 tbsp broad bean paste
🧂 2 tbsp light soy sauce
🍶 2 tbsp fragrant vinegar
🍬 2 tbsp sugar
🍾 1 tbsp yellow wine (or cooking wine)
💧 About 2 tbsp water
🌽 1 tbsp cornstarch (for the sauce slurry)
🥃 Some cooking oil

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating and mixing sauce)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Sauce bowl
🔥 Stove

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the eggplant, cut into thick slices, then into evenly sized long strips. Sprinkle with salt, mix well, and let sit briefly to draw out moisture. Drain well, then coat the surface evenly with a thin layer of cornstarch.
2. Crumble the firm tofu into small pieces by hand. Mince the pork belly into a fine mince.
3. Mix the minced garlic, chopped chili sauce, and broad bean paste together, then chop finely to form a combined paste.
4. In a small bowl, combine light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, sugar, yellow wine, water, and 1 tbsp cornstarch. Stir until smooth to make the fish-fragrant sauce slurry.
5. Heat the wok, add oil, then stir-fry the minced pork until it separates and the fat renders. Add the crumbled tofu and stir-fry over medium heat until the tofu dries out and turns golden brown. Remove and set aside.
6. Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the coated eggplant strips and pan-fry over medium-low heat until the surface is slightly charred and the strips are tender.
7. Add the prepared paste to the wok and stir-fry together with the eggplant until red oil releases and the aroma develops.
8. Pour in a small amount of hot water, then add the fish-fragrant sauce slurry. Spread the cooked tofu and pork crumble over the eggplant surface. Cover the wok, reduce the heat to the lowest setting, and braise for 3 minutes.
9. Uncover, toss all ingredients together with the sauce until well combined. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Eggplant and tofu are both low-fat, high-fiber ingredients. Coating the eggplant with starch and pan-frying helps reduce oil absorption, making this dish suitable for those managing their weight but still craving bold flavors. Note that broad bean paste and light soy sauce already contain salt, so limit additional salt during cooking.
