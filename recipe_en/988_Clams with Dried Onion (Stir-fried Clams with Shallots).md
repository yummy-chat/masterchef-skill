# Clams with Dried Onion (Stir-fried Clams with Shallots)
Cuisine Type: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦪 Clams 1000g (live, pre-purged to remove sand)
🧅 Dried onions (shallots/red onion heads) 4 pieces (half shredded, half finely chopped)
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (shredded)
🌶️ Small chili peppers 2 pieces (finely chopped)
🧅 Spring onions (white part) A few stalks (finely chopped)
🧂 Salt Approx. 6g (3g for sand removal + 3g for sauce)
🍶 High-proof white liquor 10g
🫒 Cooking oil Approx. 30g + a little extra (for sand removal)
💧 Water Appropriate amount (for soaking and sand removal)
🌫️ White pepper powder 1 small spoon (approx. 1g)
🍬 White sugar A pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 spoon (approx. 5g)
🧴 Light soy sauce 10g
🍺 Beer Approx. 75g (25g for sauce + approx. 50g for braising)
🌾 Cornstarch Suitable amount
🫗 Sesame oil (or peanut oil) Suitable amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowls (two, for shaking to remove sand)
🥄 Ladle/Wok spatula
🔪 Chopping board and Chef's knife
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing the sauce)

## Preparation Steps
### Step 1: Pre-treatment of Clams
1. Place clams in a bowl. Add 3g salt, 10g high-proof white liquor, and a little cooking oil. Stir well with chopsticks.
2. Add 1 small bowl of water. Cover with another bowl, press down firmly with both hands, and shake vigorously for 3 minutes. This stimulates the clams to expel sand.
3. Add enough fresh water to cover, and let soak for 6 hours. Afterwards, drain the clams in a colander and set aside.
### Step 2: Prepare Aromatics and Sauce
4. Slice half of the dried onions into strips, and finely chop the other half. Shred the ginger. Finely chop the small chili peppers and the white parts of the spring onions.
5. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, combine 3g salt, 1g white pepper powder, a pinch of sugar, 5g oyster sauce, 10g light soy sauce, and 25g beer. Stir until mixed. Add cornstarch and stir until smooth with no lumps. Finally, drizzle in some sesame oil and mix well. Set aside.
### Step 3: Frying Aromatics in Oil
6. Heat the wok over high heat. Pour in about 30g of vegetable oil. When the oil reaches about 50% hot (moderate heat), add the shredded dried onions and shredded ginger. Fry over medium-low heat until golden brown and fragrant. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried onion shreds and ginger shreds, draining the oil. Leave the base oil in the wok.
### Step 4: Stir-frying and Braising
7. Add half of the finely chopped dried onions to the wok and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the drained clams. Stir-fry quickly over high heat for about 40 seconds, until the clams begin to heat up.
8. Pour about 50g of beer along the edge of the wok. Bring to a boil, then immediately cover the wok. Reduce heat to medium-low and braise for about 1 minute. The steam will help the clams open quickly.
### Step 5: Thickening and Seasoning
9. Remove the lid. Add the previously fried dried onion shreds and ginger shreds. Continue to cook for about 1 minute. Then, add the small chili peppers, finely chopped spring onion whites, and the remaining half of the finely chopped dried onions.
10. While stirring the prepared sauce, pour it evenly into the wok. Stir-fry everything quickly to combine. When the sauce thickens and coats the clams evenly, remove from the heat immediately. Overcooking will make the clams tough.
**Key Reminder**: Clams must be shaken and soaked sufficiently during the sand removal process to ensure they are grit-free. Do not braise for too long; remove from heat once the shells open. After adding the sauce, stir-fry quickly. Remove from the wok as soon as the starch thickens to keep the clams tender and smooth.

## Health Advice
This dish of clams with dried onion has a moderate calorie count and is rich in high-quality protein and zinc, making it suitable for those who need nutrition while managing calorie intake. Ensure clams are fully purged of sand for cleanliness. The short cooking time preserves the fresh and tender texture. Those with gout should consume in moderation.
