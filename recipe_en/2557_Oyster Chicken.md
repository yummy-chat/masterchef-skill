# Oyster Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Chicken, appropriate amount (choose a chicken with a small beak, small wings, small feet, and a large bottom; cut into small pieces)
🦪 Oysters, about 10-12 pieces
🫚 Ginger, about one small bowl (sliced)
🧅 Dried shallots, about one small bowl (sliced)
🫒 Scallion segments, about one small handful
🌶️ Red pepper strips, about one small handful
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌿 White pepper powder, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🫗 Light soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, about 1 tablespoon
🌾 Cornstarch, about 1 tablespoon (for marinating the chicken) + an appropriate amount (for cleaning the oysters)
🍶 Rice wine, a small amount
🥜 Peanut oil, an appropriate amount
🫗 Dark soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon
💧 Cool boiled water, an appropriate amount (about half a bowl)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (for pan-frying the oysters)
🥘 Clay pot (for sautéing aromatics, stir-frying, braising, and serving the dish)
🥣 Bowl (for marinating the chicken pieces)
🍽️ Plate (for holding the prepared oysters)
🥄 Spoon (for dispensing seasonings)
🥢 Chopsticks (for picking up and turning ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Rinse the chicken and cut it into even, small pieces. Place them in a bowl.
2.  Add salt, white pepper powder, sugar, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Mix thoroughly until the chicken is evenly coated and flavored.
**Key Point**: The seasonings must be mixed well to ensure the base flavor penetrates evenly.
3.  Add cornstarch and mix well. Then drizzle in a small amount of rice wine and peanut oil, mix again, and let it marinate for 5 minutes.
**Key Point**: Adding the peanut oil last helps lock in moisture and flavor, and prevents oxidation.
4.  Sprinkle the oysters with cornstarch and gently toss them. Rinse with clean water until clean, then drain and set aside.
**Key Point**: Cleaning with cornstarch helps remove impurities and makes the oyster texture smoother.
5.  Heat the wok, add a little peanut oil, and place the oysters in. Pan-fry over low heat for about 1 minute per side, until the surface is golden and set. Remove the oysters along with any juices and set aside.
**Key Point**: Use low heat for pan-frying to seal in the internal juices and keep the oysters tender and juicy.
6.  Heat the clay pot, pour in an appropriate amount of peanut oil, and add the ginger slices. Stir-fry over low heat until the surface turns golden and slightly scorched.
**Key Point**: Fry the ginger until golden and slightly scorched to fully release its aroma.
7.  Add the dried shallot slices and continue to sauté over low heat until fragrant.
8.  Pour in the marinated chicken pieces and stir-fry constantly for about 5 minutes, until the surface of the chicken changes color.
9.  Drizzle a small amount of rice wine along the edge of the pot. Then add the cool boiled water and dark soy sauce, and stir to combine.
**Key Point**: Using cool boiled water for braising helps make the chicken more tender.
10. Cover the pot and cook using a semi-pan-fry, semi-braise method for about 10 minutes.
11. Remove the lid, pour in the pan-fried oysters along with their juices, and gently mix. Cover the pot again and continue to cook for about 5 minutes.
**Key Point**: Add the oysters at the very end to avoid overcooking, which would make them tough.
12. Sprinkle in the scallion segments and red pepper strips. Turn off the heat and let the residual heat of the clay pot cook them until just done. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Oysters paired with chicken provide zinc and protein, making this dish suitable for recovering from staying up late or boosting the immune system. Note that oysters should not be cooked for too long to prevent them from becoming tough.
