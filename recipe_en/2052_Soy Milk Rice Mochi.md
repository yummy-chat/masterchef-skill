# Soy Milk Rice Mochi
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 50g
💧 Cold water 200g
🥛 Milk 125g
🥛 Whipping cream 37g
🍯 Condensed milk 10g
🫘 Cooked black glutinous rice 1 tablespoon
🌰 Cooked soybean flour, as needed (for dusting)
🧂 White sugar 25g

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for mixing flour and water)
🍳 Non-stick pan (for cooking mochi)
🥄 Spatula or silicone scraper (for stirring)
🥛 Measuring cup/measuring spoon
🥄 Spoon (for scooping ingredients)
🥤 Plastic cup (for serving)
🔥 Induction cooktop (medium heat)

## Instructions
1. Place glutinous rice flour and cold water in a bowl, and stir until fully combined with no dry flour lumps.
2. Pour the flour-water mixture into a non-stick pan. Add milk, white sugar, whipping cream, and condensed milk in that order. Heat over medium heat.
   **Note**: The mixture will initially solidify and form small lumps; this is normal. Be sure to use a non-stick pan to prevent sticking and burning.
3. Continuously stir the mixture in the pan with a spatula, maintaining medium heat and stirring constantly, until it solidifies into a cohesive dough.
4. Turn off the heat. Using residual heat, quickly and vigorously stir the mochi dough until it becomes smooth, glossy, and free of lumps.
   **Key point**: This step directly determines the delicate texture of the mochi; stir until completely smooth.
5. Transfer the cooked mochi to a container and let it cool. The mochi achieves its best stretchy texture after cooling.
6. In a cup, layer one scoop of cooled mochi, one scoop of cooked black glutinous rice, then pour in milk tea. Finally, top with a layer of milk foam and sprinkle with cooked soybean flour.

## Health Advice
This soy milk rice mochi is rich in carbohydrates and dairy products, and is relatively high in calories. It is suitable for those building muscle or with high physical energy expenditure. Those sensitive to blood sugar should reduce the sugar content.
