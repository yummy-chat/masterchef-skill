# Lamb Fat Chili Oil
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Lamb fat 750g (1.5 jin, needs washing, blanching, and cutting into pieces)  
🌻 Cooked rapeseed oil 250g (0.5 jin, used to neutralize the greasy texture of lamb fat)  
🧅 Scallion 适量 (cut into sections, used for oil refining to remove gamey smell)  
🫚 Ginger 适量 (sliced, used for oil refining to remove gamey smell)  
🌿 Cilantro 适量 (whole stalks, used for oil refining to remove gamey smell)  
🌶️ Chili powder 适量 (a mix of coarse and fine, coarse for aroma, fine for color)  
🤍 White sesame seeds a handful (sprinkled on chili powder before pouring oil)  
🫘 Broad bean paste about 30g (adds depth and richness)  
🍶 Baijiu a small amount (mixed into chili powder to reduce harshness and enhance aroma)  
🧂 Spice packet (Sichuan peppercorn 3g, angelica dahurica 2g, galangal 2g, bay leaf 1g, cumin 6g, cardamom 1, white cardamom 2g, soaked in water in advance)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and soaking lamb fat)  
🍳 Wok (for blanching and oil refining)  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer (for removing solids)  
🔪 Knife/cutting board (for cutting)  
🥣 Large bowl (for holding chili powder and receiving hot oil)  
🥄 Soup ladle (for scooping hot oil)  
🍽️ Plate (for covering bowl to allow steaming)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the lamb fat into pieces, soak in water and repeatedly rub and wash to remove blood. Then blanch in cold water, remove and rinse off scum, and cut into smaller pieces.  
**Note**: Blanching more thoroughly removes gamey smell and blood water; cutting into small pieces helps fully render the fat later.

2. Soak all dried spices in water in advance and set aside.  
3. Mix coarse and fine chili powders in a bowl, add a small amount of baijiu and stir to combine, then evenly sprinkle a layer of white sesame seeds on the surface.  
4. Add cooked rapeseed oil to the wok, and while the oil is still cold, add the lamb fat pieces, scallion sections, ginger slices, and cilantro. Fry over low heat the entire time, stirring occasionally, until the leftover solids turn golden brown and the oil becomes clear.  
**Key**: Use low heat the entire time; otherwise, the fat will not fully render and the oil will retain a gamey smell.

5. Use a slotted spoon to remove all solids, scallions, ginger, and cilantro from the wok, reserving the oil. Add broad bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, then add the soaked spices and continue frying over low heat until the aromas fully meld.  
**Note**: Do not use too much broad bean paste to avoid turning it into hot pot base flavor; the spices must be soaked to prevent burning.

6. Strain out all spice solids from the wok again to obtain pure flavored oil. Heat the oil to 180°C and then turn off the heat.  
7. Pour the oil in two stages: first, use a ladle to scoop the 180°C hot oil and pour it over the chili powder to release a smoky aroma; after the oil in the wok cools to about 140°C, pour the remaining oil completely into the bowl and stir to combine.  
**Key**: Pouring oil at high temperature releases aroma, while pouring at medium temperature enhances the red and glossy color.

8. Immediately cover the bowl with a plate and let it cool and solidify. Once the lamb fat chili oil solidifies into a paste, it is ready to use, with better melded flavors.  

## Health Advice  
This lamb fat chili oil is rich in quality fats and fat-soluble vitamins, suitable for cold seasons or as a flavor enhancer for noodles. However, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to use no more than one tablespoon per dip. For those needing to control blood lipids or weight, it is advisable to replace part of the lamb fat with vegetable oil or pair it with lettuce or cucumber to cut the grease.
