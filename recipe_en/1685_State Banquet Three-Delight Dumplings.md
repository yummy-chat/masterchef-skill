## Preparation Steps
1. Beat the eggs into a liquid. Heat oil in a wok over low heat until warm. Slowly drizzle the egg liquid into the wok in a thin stream while stirring quickly with chopsticks to create fine, fish roe-like egg crumbles. Remove and set aside to cool.
2. Gradually add the scallion-ginger water to the pork filling in batches, stirring continuously in one direction. Wait until each addition of water is fully absorbed before adding the next, until the filling becomes moist and plump.
3. Add salt, white pepper, and dark soy sauce. Continue stirring vigorously in the same direction to make the filling sticky and firm, until it becomes thick and cohesive.
4. Take a small amount of filling and drop it into a bowl of clear water. If the filling floats to the surface, it indicates that sufficient water has been incorporated.
5. Gently mix the crushed shrimp, chopped wood ear mushrooms, and cooled egg crumbles into the hydrated pork filling using chopsticks. Stir lightly until evenly combined.
6. Sprinkle the large scallion pieces over the surface of the filling, drizzle with sesame oil, and stir gently. Then add the small scallion pieces and mix evenly.
   **Note**: Adding the sesame oil last helps seal the moisture in the scallions and prevents them from releasing water, which could affect the texture.
7. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper. Fold the wrapper in half and pinch the edges together firmly to form a dumpling shape.
8. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the dumplings and cook until they float to the surface and the wrappers become puffy. Remove and place in a bowl.
   **Note**: Dumplings are typically cooked through 1-2 minutes after they float to the surface.

## Health Advice  
This three-delight dumpling recipe uses scallion-ginger water to moisten the pork filling and sesame oil to seal in juices, resulting in tender, flavorful dumplings. Suitable for the whole family, but portion control is recommended to avoid excessive calorie intake.
