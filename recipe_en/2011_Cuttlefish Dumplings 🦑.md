# Cuttlefish Dumplings 🦑
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦑 1 cuttlefish (medium-sized, for ink and meat)  
🌾 300g all-purpose flour  
🌊 150g cuttlefish ink + water (for dough)  
🧂 1/4 tsp salt (for dough)  
🦑 300g cuttlefish meat (after cleaning)  
🐔 300g chicken thigh meat  
🥚 1 egg  
🥬 50g chives (chopped)  
🌽 Corn starch as needed (to prevent sticking when rolling)  
🌶️ 1/2 tsp white pepper powder  
🌿 1/4 tsp Sichuan pepper powder  
🧂 1/2 tsp chicken bouillon  
🫚 1 tsp ginger paste  
🫗 1 tbsp sesame oil  
🧂 1 tsp salt (for filling)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 Bowl  
🥘 Mixing bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🌀 Food processor  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🪢 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board  
🍲 Pot  
🥢 Slotted spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Cut open the cuttlefish belly, remove the ink sac, snip open the sac opening and squeeze the ink into a bowl. Add water to dilute and stir until well combined.
2. Pour 150g of the cuttlefish ink water into a mixing bowl, add 1/4 tsp salt, then add 300g all-purpose flour. Stir with chopsticks until the mixture becomes flaky, then knead into a dough by hand.  
**Note**: Knead only until roughly uniform; do not over-knead.
3. Cover and rest the dough for 15 minutes, then take it out and knead until the surface is smooth. Cover again and rest for 1 hour.
4. Remove the cartilage from the cuttlefish body, rinse clean, peel off the surface membrane, cut off the cuttlefish tentacles, and squeeze out the beak.  
**Key Point**: The cuttlefish must be thoroughly cleaned to avoid any hard bits affecting the texture.
5. Place 300g of cuttlefish meat into a food processor and pulse into a coarse paste with some texture. Then pulse 300g of chicken thigh meat into a similar coarse paste.
6. Add white pepper powder, Sichuan pepper powder, chicken bouillon, salt, and ginger paste to the meat mixture in sequence, and stir to combine evenly.  
**Note**: Cuttlefish meat is naturally moist, so no additional water is needed.
7. Add 1 tsp corn starch and 1 egg, then stir the filling until it becomes firm and cohesive.
8. Chop 50g of chives, mix with 1 tbsp sesame oil, then add to the meat filling and gently combine.
9. Take out the rested dough, poke a hole in the center and stretch it into a ring, then cut it in half. Take one half and roll it into a long log, then cut into evenly sized small pieces.  
**Key Point**: Dust the small dough pieces with corn starch to prevent sticking, then flatten with your palm before rolling.
10. Use a rolling pin to roll each piece into a wrapper with thin edges and a slightly thicker center. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, fold in half, and pinch tightly to seal.
11. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the dumplings, and gently push the bottom with the back of a slotted spoon to prevent sticking.
12. Once the water returns to a boil, add one cup of cold water and keep it at a gentle boil for 4–5 minutes.  
**Key Point**: You may add another splash of cold water during cooking. When all dumplings float to the surface, the skin turns glossy black with no white starch visible, they are ready to be removed.

## Health Advice  
Cuttlefish dumplings use ink in the dough and are rich in protein and minerals, making them suitable for those needing to nourish qi and blood. The combination of chives and chicken with cuttlefish meat creates a tender flavor, but the filling is relatively high in fat. It is recommended to serve with a light vegetable soup.
