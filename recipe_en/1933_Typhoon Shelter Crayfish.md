# Typhoon Shelter Crayfish
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦞 Crayfish 1500g (about 3 jin, choose thin-shelled, small ones)
🍞 Breadcrumbs 500g
🧄 Minced Garlic 200g
🧅 Dried Shallots appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🫘 Fermented Black Beans appropriate amount
🌶️ Shredded Chili Peppers appropriate amount
🧂 Light Soy Sauce about 1 tablespoon
🍶 Cooking Wine about 1 tablespoon
🌾 Cornstarch about 4 tablespoons
🧂 Chicken Bouillon Powder about 1 tablespoon
🌿 White Pepper Powder appropriate amount
🧂 Sea Salt appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (for garnish)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted Spoon/Strainer
🧽 Strainer/Cheesecloth (for rinsing garlic)
🥣 Glass Bowls (for marinating and holding ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the Crayfish**
   - Use scissors to cut off the top part of the head, remove the black sand sac, keep the vein intact, wash clean with water, and drain.
2. **Marinate and Coat with Starch**
   - Add light soy sauce and cooking wine to the crayfish. Gently mix well to marinate and infuse flavor.
   - Then sprinkle in cornstarch and toss until each crayfish is evenly coated with a thin layer of starch.
3. **Rinse the Minced Garlic**
   - Soak and rinse the minced garlic with water to remove sugar and starch. Drain thoroughly using a strainer or cheesecloth.
4. **Fry the Garlic and Breadcrumbs**
   - Heat oil in a wok to about 60% hot. Add the drained minced garlic and fry over medium-low heat until slightly changed in color and floating, releasing fragrance. Quickly remove and drain oil.
   - Keep the same oil at 60% hot. Pour in the breadcrumbs and fry over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until golden brown, crispy, and infused with garlic aroma. Immediately remove and mix with the fried garlic to drain oil together.
5. **Fry the Crayfish**
   - Increase oil temperature to 80% hot. Carefully add the coated crayfish.
   - Maintain high oil temperature and fry for 3–5 minutes until the shells are crispy, the color is bright red, and all have floated to the surface. Remove and drain oil.
6. **Combine and Stir-Fry**
   - Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add garlic slices, chopped dried shallots, fermented black beans, and shredded chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant.
   - Add the fried crayfish and quickly stir-fry to let the flavor penetrate into the bodies.
   - Season with chicken bouillon powder, white pepper powder, and sea salt. Stir-fry evenly.
   - Pour in all the Typhoon Shelter mixture (fried garlic and breadcrumbs). Quickly stir-fry until the mixture evenly coats each crayfish.
7. **Serve**
   - Once well mixed, turn off the heat and plate. Garnish with chopped scallions.
   - **Key Points**: The fried crayfish shells should be crispy enough to eat directly. Pay close attention to the heat for the garlic and breadcrumbs; fry until golden and immediately remove to avoid burning and bitterness.

## Health Advice
Crayfish is high in protein and low in fat, but the Typhoon Shelter preparation makes it higher in calories and sodium. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables and use paper towels to absorb excess oil. People with high blood lipids or gout should consume in small amounts. For healthy individuals, it is advisable not to eat more than 10 pieces per meal.
