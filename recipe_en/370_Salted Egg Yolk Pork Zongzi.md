# Salted Egg Yolk Pork Zongzi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 500g (30% fat, 70% lean, skin removed, cut into large pieces)  
🥚 Raw salted duck egg yolks 10  
🍚 Glutinous rice 600g (long-grain or short-grain glutinous rice both acceptable)  
🧂 Salt 10g (5g for marinating meat + 5g for mixing rice)  
🍬 Sugar 30g (10g for marinating meat + 20g for mixing rice)  
🌶️ White pepper powder 1g  
🥄 Light soy sauce 40g (20g for marinating meat + 20g for mixing rice)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 55g (15g for marinating meat + 40g for mixing rice)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g  
🍶 Rice wine 20g (can substitute with fermented rice liquid)  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) appropriate amount (spray on egg yolks to remove gamey smell)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls  
♨️ Oven  
🥢 Strainer  
🍳 Pot, stove  
🧵 Cotton twine  
♨️ Electric pressure cooker (or regular deep pot)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Marinate the meat**: Mix salt, sugar, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and rice wine evenly to make the marinade. Pour over the pork belly pieces and rub thoroughly, ensuring each piece is evenly coated. Marinate for at least 4 hours. For deeper flavor, refrigerate and marinate overnight.
2. **Prepare the egg yolks**: Wash raw salted egg yolks, spray evenly with baijiu, then place in a preheated 170°C oven and roast for about 5 minutes until the yolks turn translucent. Remove; the purpose is to remove the gamey smell; do not roast until oil seeps out.
3. **Mix the rice**: Rinse the glutinous rice and drain well. Add salt, sugar, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce, mix well, and let sit to marinate for 1 hour, so the rice grains are fully flavored and colored.
4. **Prepare the bamboo leaves**: Cut off the tough stem tips of fresh bamboo leaves. Boil in boiling water for 2 minutes, then remove and soak in clean water to prevent drying and cracking.
5. **Wrap the zongzi**: Take two bamboo leaves, shiny side up, overlapping and slightly staggered. Fold into a cone shape at the 1/3 point. First, fill with a layer of rice, add one piece of pork belly and one salted egg yolk, then cover with enough rice and press firmly. Fold the top leaf down over the filling, tighten the sides inward, fold the excess leaf tip over, and tie tightly with cotton twine. If the leaf breaks and rice leaks, add another leaf to wrap.
6. **Cook the zongzi**: Place the zongzi in the electric pressure cooker, pour in enough boiling water to completely submerge them. Bring to pressure and cook for 1 hour, then let sit with the heat off for 1 hour to steam. If using a regular deep pot, cook over low heat for at least 2 hours.
   **Key**: Must use boiling water to prevent the savory flavor of the meat from leaching into the water; do not boil the bamboo leaves for too long to avoid losing their aroma.

## Health Recommendations  
This salted egg yolk pork zongzi is rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure. However, pork belly and salted egg yolks contain high levels of fat and sodium. It is recommended to consume 1-2 pieces per serving and pair with vegetables to balance nutrition. For those reducing fat, reduce the amount of meat or choose lean meat.
