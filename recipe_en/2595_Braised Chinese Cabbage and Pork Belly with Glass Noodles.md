# Braised Chinese Cabbage and Pork Belly with Glass Noodles
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: ❄️ Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 300g
🥬 Chinese cabbage 500g
🍜 Dried potato glass noodles 50g
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder 10g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 5g
🧂 Salt 15g
🫗 Dark soy sauce 5ml
🫗 Light soy sauce 5ml
🫒 Cooking oil 10-15ml

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl or container
Lid

## Instructions
1. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add the dried glass noodles and cook for 5 minutes. Pour both the noodles and water into a container, cover, and continue soaking until needed.
2. Slice the pork belly into pieces about 3mm thick and set aside.
3. Separate the tender leaves of the Chinese cabbage from the stalks. Slice both into pieces and set aside.
4. Heat the wok over high heat, then pour in 10-15ml of cooking oil. Wait 10 seconds for the oil to heat up.
5. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry continuously until the meat turns white.
6. Drizzle in the dark soy sauce and stir-fry for 1 minute, ensuring the meat is evenly colored.
7. Add the sliced cabbage stalks, salt, and light soy sauce. Stir-fry for 1 minute.
8. Pour in enough water to submerge all ingredients. Add chicken bouillon and thirteen-spice powder. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
9. Drain the soaked glass noodles, cut them into long sections, and place them in a bowl until needed.
10. Add the tender cabbage leaves and stir well. Spread the glass noodles evenly over the top, cover, and continue simmering for another 5 minutes.
11. Taste, then turn off the heat. Reduce the sauce until thickened, then serve.

## Health Tips
This dish balances meat and vegetables, warming the body and providing satiety. Pork belly supplies protein and fat, while Chinese cabbage is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C, making it suitable for winter nourishment. Monitor the salt amount; those with high blood pressure can reduce salt or light soy sauce usage. Glass noodles absorb broth and are higher in calories—those on a weight-loss diet may reduce noodles and increase cabbage.
