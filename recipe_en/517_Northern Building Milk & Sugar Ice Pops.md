# Northern Building Milk & Sugar Ice Pops
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 3
🍬 White sugar 75 g
🍯 Honey 1 tbsp
🌽 Cornstarch 75 g
🥛 Whole milk 800 g
💧 Water approx. 50 g (for adjusting consistency)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Stainless steel pot
🥄 Spoon or spatula
🧊 Ice pop molds
🥢 Chopsticks
📦 Foam box

## Steps
1. Crack the eggs into a stainless steel bowl, add the white sugar and honey, and stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.
2. Add the cornstarch and continue stirring until no dry powder remains and the mixture is uniform.
3. Gradually pour in the whole milk while stirring continuously to prevent lumps, until the mixture is smooth and lump-free.
4. Pour the mixture into a stainless steel pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low-medium heat and continue cooking, stirring constantly to prevent scorching. Cook until the liquid thickens and large bubbles appear, ensuring it is fully cooked through. If it becomes too thick, add about 50 g of hot water to thin it.
5. Remove from heat and let it cool for about 10 minutes to avoid damaging the molds with high temperature.
6. Using a spoon and chopsticks, fill the ice pop molds with the mixture. Do not fill too full; gently tap the molds to release air bubbles.
7. Cover the molds with the lid, insert the ice pop sticks, and place them in a foam box to freeze overnight until completely solid.
8. Remove the molds, rinse the outsides with hot water or briefly soak them, using thermal expansion to release the ice pops easily.

## Health Advice
This ice pop is made with eggs and milk, providing protein and calcium, but is high in sugar and starch. Suitable for summer refreshment in moderation; those with blood sugar sensitivity should consume cautiously.
