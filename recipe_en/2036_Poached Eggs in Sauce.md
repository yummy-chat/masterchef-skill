# Poached Eggs in Sauce
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Recommended Season: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs, about 6
🧅 Chopped scallions, to taste
🫒 Soybean oil, about 1 tablespoon
🧂 Light soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon
🥫 Soybean paste, about 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Deep plate or bowl (steamable)
🥄 Spoon
♨️ Steamer and steamer rack

## Instructions
1. In a deep plate, add soybean oil, light soy sauce, and soybean paste. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until the sauce is well combined.
2. Crack the eggs directly into the plate one by one, keeping the yolks intact. Do not stir.
**Key point**: The eggs are "poached" in the sauce, not beaten.
3. Sprinkle chopped scallions evenly over the eggs.
4. Bring water in the steamer to a boil. Once the water is boiling, place the plate on the steamer rack and cover with the lid.
**Note**: Placing the plate in cold water will affect the texture and quality of the final dish.
5. Steam over high heat for about 15 minutes, until the egg whites are fully set and white. The yolks can be left semi-solid or just set, according to preference.
**Key point**: Adjust steaming time based on the number of eggs and heat level.
6. Remove from the steamer and serve. The sauce at the bottom is savory, and the eggs are tender and smooth.

## Health Advice
This poached egg dish is steamed, keeping oil low and preserving protein, making it suitable for those needing high-quality protein. However, soybean paste and light soy sauce are high in sodium. People with high blood pressure should avoid consuming the sauce at the bottom. It is recommended to serve with mixed grains or vegetables to balance nutrition and avoid excessive sodium intake in a single meal.
