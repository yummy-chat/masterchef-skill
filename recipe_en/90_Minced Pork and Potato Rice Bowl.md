# Minced Pork and Potato Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes (appropriate amount, cut into small cubes)
🥩 Minced pork (appropriate amount)
🫑 Green bell pepper (appropriate amount, cut into rings)
🌶️ Red bell pepper (appropriate amount, cut into rings)
🍚 Cooked rice (appropriate amount)
🧄 Minced garlic (appropriate amount)
🧅 Chopped green onion (appropriate amount)
🫒 Cooking oil (appropriate amount)
🧂 Light soy sauce (3 tbsp)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (1 tbsp)
🦪 Oyster sauce (1 tbsp)
🧂 Salt (1 tsp)
🍬 Sugar (a pinch)
🌾 Cornstarch (1 tbsp)
💧 Water (1 cup)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Bowl/plate (for holding the prepared potatoes)

## Instructions
1. Wash the potatoes and cut into small cubes; cut the green and red bell peppers into rings; mince the garlic and chop the green onion.
2. Mix the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, cornstarch, and water together to form a sauce. Set aside.
3. Heat the cooking oil in the pan over medium-low heat. Add the potato cubes and fry until both sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Maintain medium-low heat when frying the potatoes to avoid a burnt exterior and raw interior.
4. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the minced pork and stir-fry until fully cooked and no longer pink.
5. Add the minced garlic and chopped green onion, stir-frying with the pork until fragrant.
6. Return the fried potato cubes to the pan. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry everything evenly.
7. Cover the pan, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the potatoes to soften and the sauce to thicken.
8. Remove the lid, add the green and red bell pepper rings, and stir-fry quickly until well combined. Turn off the heat.
9. Spoon the cooked mixture over the rice, mix well, and enjoy.

## Health Tips
This dish provides carbohydrates from the potatoes, protein from the minced pork, and vitamin C from the bell peppers, offering a balanced nutritional profile suitable as a main meal. Note that the rice portion should be controlled, as the potatoes themselves are already high in starch.
