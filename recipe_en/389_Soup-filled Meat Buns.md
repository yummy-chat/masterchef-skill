# Soup-filled Meat Buns
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings

🫚 Ginger适量 (for scallion-ginger water)
🍖 Bone Broth 400g
🧂 Salt 14g
🧂 MSG 14g
🌿 Thirteen Spices 4g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 10g
🥫 Dark Soy Sauce 24g
🥄 Premium Soy Sauce 10g
🫒 Seasoning Oil 100g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🪣 Large Basin
🧤 Disposable Gloves
🥟 Steamer Basket
🍲 Steamer Pot
✂️ Scissors

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make scallion-ginger water**: Take 100g of water, add an appropriate amount of scallions and ginger, squeeze with your hands to extract the juice, and set aside.
2. **Prepare seasoned bone broth**: Take 400g of bone broth, add 14g salt, 14g MSG, 4g thirteen spices, and 10g sugar in order, stir well to build a strong base flavor. Then add 24g dark soy sauce and 10g premium soy sauce, and mix thoroughly.
3. **Add scallion-ginger water to meat**: Pour the scallion-ginger water into the large basin with the ground pork. Wearing gloves, mix well so that every grain of meat fully absorbs the scallion-ginger water to remove any gamey smell.
4. **Add seasoned broth**: Pour the prepared seasoned broth directly into the meat filling. Use your hands to mix roughly so the liquid and meat combine, preventing splashing during stirring.
5. **Knead the filling**: Use your hands to knead the meat filling for 3-5 minutes to allow the meat to initially absorb the liquid, forming the first stage of elasticity.
6. **Stir until firm**: Stir the meat filling vigorously in one direction to relax the meat tissue and allow all the liquid to be fully absorbed.
   **Key point**: The filling is ready when pressing it makes it spring back quickly and it does not stick to your hand when tapped. Do not skip this step, or the liquid may separate out.
7. **Add seasoning oil to lock in moisture**: Add 100g of seasoning oil and mix well. This adds aroma and locks in moisture, at which point the filling should be soft, glossy, and moist.
8. **Freeze the filling**: Seal the bowl with the filling using plastic wrap and place it in the freezer until it reaches a slushy consistency, making it easier to wrap.
9. **Wrap the buns**: Take a piece of bun wrapper, place an ample amount of the frozen filling, and seal the bun tightly.
10. **Steam the buns**: Place the wrapped buns into the steamer basket. Once the water is boiling and steam rises, start timing for 15 minutes.
    **Key point**: After the time is up, do not turn off the heat first. Open the steamer lid, then remove the steamer basket. This ensures the filling is fully cooked and the soup is thoroughly extracted.

## Health Advice
These soup-filled buns are rich in high-quality protein and collagen, making them suitable for people in need of energy replenishment. However, they are high in salt and fat, so individuals with hypertension, high cholesterol, or diabetes should consume them sparingly. Pay attention to controlling the steaming time to ensure the soup is abundant and does not leak out.
