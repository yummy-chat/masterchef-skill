# Rice Cooker Chiffon Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 5 eggs (medium-sized, separate egg whites and yolks, refrigerate egg whites until ice crystals form on the edges)  
🥛 50g warm milk (about 40°C, mix with corn oil)  
🫒 50g corn oil (room temperature)  
🌾 80g cake flour (sifted)  
🍬 60g granulated sugar (add to egg whites in three batches)  
🌽 8g cornstarch (added later when whipping egg whites to help stabilize)  
🌿 A few drops vanilla extract (remove odor and enhance aroma, add to egg yolk mixture)  
🍋 A few drops lemon juice (add before whipping egg whites to remove odor and stabilize, can substitute with white vinegar)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker (preheat function needs to be turned on in advance)  
🥣 2 large glass bowls (for egg yolk mixture and egg whites)  
🥄 Hand whisk (for mixing egg yolk mixture)  
⚡ Electric mixer (for whipping egg white meringue)  
🔪 Spatula (for folding and mixing batter)  
🥢 Chopsticks (to eliminate large air bubbles in batter before cooking)  
🧊 Cooling rack (for inverting cake after baking to cool)  
🍴 Cutting board and knife (for slicing the finished cake)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare egg whites**: Place egg whites in a clean, oil-free, water-free container. Freeze until ice crystals appear around the edges of the container. Keep egg yolks at room temperature.
   *Key point*: The container must be oil-free and water-free, otherwise whipping may fail.
2. **Preheat rice cooker**: Turn on the rice cooker's "Cake" or preheat function to warm the inner pot in advance.
3. **Make egg yolk batter**:
   - Add warm milk, corn oil, and vanilla extract to the egg yolks. Whisk with a hand whisk until fully emulsified.
   - Add sifted cake flour. Continue stirring until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.
4. **Whip egg white meringue**:
   - Take the frozen egg whites out and add lemon juice.
   - Beat at high speed with an electric mixer until the volume is maximized and the mixture reaches a soft peak stage.
   - **First sugar addition**: Add 1/3 of the granulated sugar. Continue beating at high speed for about half a minute until the texture is fluffy.
   - **Second sugar addition**: Add another 1/3 of the sugar. Switch to medium speed and beat for about 1 minute until obvious resistance appears in the egg whites.
   - **Third sugar addition**: Add the remaining sugar. Switch to low speed and beat for about half a minute to stabilize the bubbles.
   - **Add cornstarch**: Add cornstarch. Use the mixer on low speed to slowly incorporate along the edges of the container until the meringue is firm, smooth, and glossy.
   *Tip*: If the egg whites are already whipped but the container is too small, switch to a larger container before adding cornstarch.
5. **Combine batters**:
   - Add about 1/3 of the meringue into the egg yolk batter. Use a spatula to fold quickly until just combined.
   - Pour the mixed batter back into the remaining meringue. Fold from the bottom upwards with a spatula, moving quickly and gently, until the color is uniform and no white streaks of meringue remain.
   *Key point*: Folding must be quick and gentle to prevent deflating, which can cause the cake to shrink.
6. **Bake**:
   - Pour the batter into the preheated rice cooker inner pot. Use chopsticks to draw circles in the batter to eliminate large air bubbles and smooth the surface.
   - Close the lid. Select the "Cake" mode and bake for about 1 hour.
   - **Check doneness**: The cake is done when a strong cake aroma is noticeable. If not, add another 10 minutes.
7. **Cool and unmold**:
   - After baking, remove the inner pot and gently tap it on the countertop.
   - Immediately invert it onto a cooling rack and let it cool completely. The cake will detach from the inner pot automatically.
   *Note*: It must be inverted to cool, otherwise the cake may shrink and collapse.

## Health Suggestion  
The key to a successful rice cooker chiffon cake lies in ensuring the container is oil-free and water-free, freezing the egg whites until ice crystals form around the edges, and using a gentle folding technique. Suitable for those who enjoy a soft, fluffy texture. However, 60g of sugar is relatively high; those watching their sugar intake can reduce it to 40g and serve with unsweetened yogurt.
