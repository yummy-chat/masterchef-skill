## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the beef brisket into evenly sized chunks and place into the pressure cooker. Add fermented bean curd, Chu hou paste, southern fermented bean curd, one tablespoon of peanut butter, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, rock sugar, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, and Guangdong rice wine. Stir thoroughly with a cooking spoon to coat the brisket evenly with the sauce.  
2. Cover the pressure cooker with its lid and heat over the fire. Once steam is released, maintain pressure and cook for 10 minutes. Afterward, allow the pressure to release naturally before opening the lid. The brisket should now be tender enough to be easily pierced with chopsticks.  
3. Bring the brisket and its sauce to a boil again over the fire. Pour in a small amount of starch water while stirring continuously, until the sauce thickens, turns glossy and red, and coats the spoon.  
4. In a separate soup pot, bring water to a boil. Add the bamboo pole noodles and blanch for about 1 minute. Remove and plunge into cold water to cool, then return to the boiling water for about 10 seconds. Drain and place into a bowl. Cooling in cold water is key to keeping the noodles chewy; the final reheating removes any raw water taste.  
5. In the soup pot, bring fresh water to a boil. Add the ground dried flounder fish powder and a pinch of salt, stirring until fully dissolved to make the broth. Pour the broth into the noodle bowl, and add the blanched lettuce.  
6. Spoon the braised beef brisket on top, and sprinkle with a little ground white pepper before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This beef brisket noodle dish is rich in savory sauce with tender brisket, suitable for those needing energy and protein. However, it is relatively high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should moderate the amount of sauce used. Cooling the bamboo pole noodles in cold water enhances their chewiness and is key to achieving the ideal texture.
