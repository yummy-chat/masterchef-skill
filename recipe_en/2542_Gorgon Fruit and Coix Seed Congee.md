## Cooking Steps
1. Peel and dice the Chinese yam, slice the red dates, rinse the goji berries and red date slices; rinse the rice and coix seeds separately, then soak them; soak the gorgon fruit in a separate bowl of clean water.
2. Bring water to a boil in a clay pot, then add the soaked rice, coix seeds, red date slices, and goji berries, along with a small amount of chicken fat.
3. Cover and cook for 10 minutes.
4. Remove the lid, add the gorgon fruit and diced Chinese yam, stir well, then reduce to low heat and continue cooking for 30 minutes.
5. When the congee is nearly done, add the lily bulb and cook for another 2–3 minutes.
6. Just before turning off the heat, add another small amount of chicken fat, stir well, and then serve.

## Health Tips
This gorgon fruit and coix seed congee strengthens the spleen, removes dampness, and nourishes qi and complexion. It is especially suitable for spring when dampness is heavy and people tend to feel fatigued. Chinese yam and lily bulb are gentle on the stomach, but coix seeds are slightly cooling in nature, so those with a cold and weak spleen and stomach should use less. Chicken fat, as the finishing touch, enhances the aroma and smoothness of the congee base, but it should be used in moderation.
