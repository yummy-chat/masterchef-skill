# Bo Bo Chicken (Sichuan Cold Skewers)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌶️ Lantern peppers 40g (dried chilies, for chili oil)
🌶️ Bullet peppers 30g (dried chilies, for chili oil)
🌶️ Shizhuhong peppers 30g (dried chilies, for chili oil)
🧅 Scallion 20g (for frying flavored oil)
🧅 Onion 20g (for frying flavored oil)
🧄 Ginger 15g (sliced, for frying flavored oil)
🧄 Garlic 30g (smashed, for frying flavored oil)
🫘 White sesame seeds 100g (raw, for adding to chili oil and broth after frying)
🐔 Chicken carcass 1 (500-700g, for broth, preferably with neck for more fat)
🐔 Chicken feet as needed (side ingredient, blanched and cooked together with broth)
🥦 Broccoli as needed (vegetable skewers, blanched after skewering)
🥔 Potato slices as needed (vegetable skewers, blanched after skewering)
🥒 Lotus root slices as needed (vegetable skewers, blanched after skewering)
🥬 Cauliflower as needed (vegetable skewers, blanched after skewering)
🌭 Sausage as needed (meat skewers, boiled after skewering)
🥚 Quail eggs as needed (meat skewers, boiled after skewering)
🫒 Cooking oil 700g (for frying flavored oil and making chili oil; regular cooking oil is fine for home use)
🥜 Sesame paste 10g (diluted with a little chicken broth)
🫗 Sesame oil 15g
🌿 Sichuan green pepper oil 35g
🧄 Minced garlic 15g
🌶️ Red Sichuan pepper powder 1g
🌶️ Green Sichuan pepper powder 1g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 15g
🧂 MSG 10g
🧂 Salt 20-25g (adjust to taste; broth should be slightly salty)
🍬 White sugar 25g
🧂 White pepper powder 0.5g
🌿 Spice mix for frying flavored oil: White cardamom 15g, red Sichuan pepper 2g, green Sichuan pepper 2g, fennel seeds 2g, sand ginger 1g, one tsaoko peel 1, dried tangerine peel 1g, cinnamon 1g, bay leaves 1g, star anise 1g
🌿 Spice mix for broth: Angelica dahurica 2g, cinnamon 1g, sand ginger 1g, galangal 1g, star anise 2g, amomum 1g, one gardenia 1, cloves 0.2g, cardamom 1g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottom pan (for frying dried chilies)
🔥 Gas stove
🔪 Meat grinder (for grinding fried dried chilies into chili flakes)
🍲 Soup pot (for making broth)
🥅 Strainer (for frying white sesame seeds)
🥄 Ladle (for scooping hot oil and broth)
🥢 Whisk (for stirring)
🥣 Stainless steel or glass bowl (for holding chili flakes, chili oil, and broth)
🪣 Metal strainer (for removing spice residue)
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer (for measuring oil temperature)

## Instructions
### Preparation
1. Cut the lantern peppers, bullet peppers, and Shizhuhong dried chilies into segments. Add a small amount of cooking oil to a cold pan, then add the chili segments. Stir-fry over low heat for 2 minutes, then reduce to the lowest heat and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, until the chilies crackle crisply and the seeds turn golden brown. Remove and let cool completely, then grind into medium-coarse chili flakes using a meat grinder.
**Key point**: Fry until the chilies crackle crisply and the seeds turn golden. Let cool completely after frying, then grind into medium-coarse chili flakes.
2. Cut the scallion into sections, slice the onion, slice the ginger, and smash the garlic. Prepare all dried spices according to the portions.
**Key point**: Try to use all spices for the best flavor.
3. Weigh out 100g raw white sesame seeds and set aside.
4. Blanch the chicken carcass in cold water. When small bubbles appear on the surface, turn off the heat. Remove and rinse. In a separate soup pot, add 3000ml water, the cleaned chicken carcass, 20g scallion sections, 15g ginger slices, and the spice pack for broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and simmer for 75 minutes.
**Key point**: Do not blanch the chicken carcass for too long. The spices for the broth are essential for flavor—do not skip them.

### Cooking
1. Pour 700g cooking oil into a pot and heat over high heat for about 2 minutes until the oil reaches around 110°C. Add all prepared scallion, ginger, garlic, and dried spices. Fry over high heat for 5 minutes until the ingredients are dry and golden. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, then use a strainer to scoop out and discard all the solids.
**Key point**: Make sure to turn the heat to low when removing the solids to prevent the oil from overheating.
2. Heat the oil to about 200°C and keep it on the lowest heat. Place the white sesame seeds in a strainer and submerge it into the oil. Stir with chopsticks and fry until the seeds are light golden and fully floated. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Using a strainer for frying is convenient and easy to control.
3. Let the oil cool to about 190°C and keep it on the lowest heat. Ladle one scoop of hot oil over the chili flakes and stir well. Then gradually pour in the remaining hot oil, one ladle at a time, stirring continuously until all oil is added. Cover and let rest for 24 hours for better flavor.
**Key point**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid burning the chili flakes. Covering and resting for 24 hours enhances the flavor.
4. In a large bowl, combine the sesame paste diluted with a little chicken broth, 15g sesame oil, 35g Sichuan green pepper oil, 15g minced garlic, 1g red Sichuan pepper powder, 1g green Sichuan pepper powder, 15g chicken bouillon, 10g MSG, 20-25g salt, 25g white sugar, and 0.5g white pepper powder. Stir well. Add 1000g of prepared broth, then add 150g of the prepared chili oil and the fried white sesame seeds. Mix thoroughly.
**Key point**: Use enough salt—the broth needs to be slightly salty for the skewers to absorb flavor. However, too much salt can make the vegetables overly salty.
5. Skewer all the ingredients. Using the remaining broth in the pot, bring it to a boil. First, blanch the quick-cooking vegetables for about 1 minute, then cook the other meat ingredients according to their required time. Soak all cooked skewers in the prepared broth for 20 minutes before serving.
**Key point**: The chicken feet have already been cooked in the broth; just skewer them and soak directly in the flavored broth.

## Health Advice
This Bo Bo Chicken is high in oil and sodium, suitable for occasional consumption, not recommended as a daily meal. The chicken carcass broth is nutritious, but vegetable skewers should not be soaked too long to avoid absorbing too much oil and salt.
