# Buddha Jumping Over the Wall
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦪 Abalone (dried, rehydrated)
🦑 Sea cucumber (dried, rehydrated)
🐟 Fish lips (dried, rehydrated)
🐟 Fish maw (dried, rehydrated)
🥩 Tendon (dried, rehydrated)
🦆 Duck gizzards
🥚 Quail eggs
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms
🐚 Conpoy (dried, rehydrated)
🐖 Pork belly
🐔 Old hen (for base broth)
🦆 Old duck (for base broth)
🐖 Pig trotters (for base broth)
🥩 Pork ribs (for base broth and Ingredient A)
🫚 Ginger (for base broth)
🧅 Scallions (for base broth)
🍃 Lotus leaf, 1 piece (for sealing and fragrance)
🧂 Sea salt (for final seasoning)
🍬 Rock sugar, a small amount (for final seasoning)
🍶 Shaoxing wine (yellow wine), 50g (for simmering broth)
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece
🌿 Cinnamon, 3g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Large strainer/skimmer
🏺 Clay pot (crock)
🔥 Charcoal stove
🕸 Fine mesh strainer
🍽 Bowls, plates

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the base broth**: Briefly stir-fry the old hen in a wok until fragrant. Add ginger, scallions, and Shaoxing wine. Blanch the old duck, pork ribs, and pig trotters in boiling water to remove any gamey smell, then drain and stir-fry them in the wok until fragrant. Transfer the stir-fried old hen, old duck, pork ribs, and pig trotters to a clay pot. Pour in 50g of Shaoxing wine, add enough mineral water to cover the ingredients, and add 3g of cinnamon and 1 star anise. Seal the pot opening with a lotus leaf and tie it tightly with string. Place the pot on a charcoal stove and simmer for 12 hours.
**Key point**: Simmering slowly over charcoal fire is the traditional method; the lotus leaf adds a refreshing aroma.

2. **Separate solids from broth**: Remove the simmered base broth, strain it through a fine mesh strainer to discard all solids, and reserve the clear broth.

3. **Simmer Ingredient A in batches**: Divide the remaining ingredients into Ingredient A and Ingredient B. Line the bottom of an empty clay pot with pork ribs, layer in the remaining Ingredient A ingredients, and pour in the strained base broth. Seal the pot with the lotus leaf again and simmer for 24 hours.

4. **Add Ingredient B and final seasoning**: After 24 hours, open the pot. Add the Ingredient B ingredients, along with a small amount of rock sugar and sea salt. Reseal the pot and continue simmering for another 2 hours.
**Key point**: Sea salt enhances umami better than regular iodized salt; adding ingredients in batches is crucial for achieving different textures.

5. **Finish**: After a total simmering time of approximately 38 hours, the broth will be clear, each ingredient will have its distinct texture, and the soup will be rich and sweet. It is ready to serve.

## Health Advice
This Buddha Jumping Over the Wall dish is rich in collagen, making it ideal for nourishing the body in autumn and winter, as well as for those needing to supplement skin nutrients. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive purine intake; it is not suitable for people with gout.
