# Candied Orange Peel Chips
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍊 Fresh orange peels 150g (remove all fruit pulp, use a peeler to scrape off the inner white pith, retaining an appropriate thickness)  
💧 Water 200g (for candy soaking) + an appropriate amount (for first blanching)  
🍬 Yellow rock sugar 150g (can substitute with white granulated sugar)  
🍋 Lemon juice 3-5g (freshly squeezed)  
🧂 Salt 1.5g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Bread knife, peeler  
🔲 Cutting board  
🍳 Sauce pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Strainer  
📜 Parchment paper (or baking paper)  
🛍️ Sealed bag

## Steps
1. **Prepare the orange peels**  
   Cut the fresh oranges open, remove the flesh to get the peels, and use a peeler to scrape off the inner white pith until you feel slight resistance from the blade, retaining an appropriate thickness. First cut the orange peels into thin strips, then crosswise cut them into small chips.  
   **Note**: The white part needs to be thoroughly scraped off to avoid a hard texture in the finished product.

2. **First blanching**  
   Place the orange peel chips into the sauce pan, add 1.5g of salt and enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to medium heat and cook for 2-3 minutes until the chips soften. Drain the salted water using a strainer. Do not rinse the orange peel chips; return them directly to the pan for use.

3. **Candy soaking and boiling**  
   Add 200g of water and 150g of yellow rock sugar to the pan. Heat over medium heat until boiling, occasionally stirring with a spatula to prevent sticking. When the liquid is nearly gone, squeeze in 3-5g of lemon juice, and continue stir-frying over medium heat until the water has completely evaporated.  
   **Judgment criteria**: The chips become translucent and glossy; when lifted with a spatula, they appear thick and do not fall apart.  
   **Key point**：Adding lemon juice prevents the sugar liquid from crystallizing; the moisture must be thoroughly evaporated, but do not use excessively high heat to avoid burning.

4. **Cooling and storage**  
   Pour the finished chips onto parchment paper, spread evenly with a spatula, and let air dry at room temperature for 1-2 days until the surface is dry. Gently separate the chips with your hands, place them in a sealed bag, and store in the refrigerator. If rehydration is needed before use, the chips will naturally separate when soaked.

## Health Advice
Orange peels are rich in pectin and hesperidin. Blanching removes bitterness, and after candy soaking, they become a natural antioxidant snack suitable for baking or tea. When consuming, be mindful of portion size due to the high sugar content, which is unfavorable for blood sugar and weight management.
