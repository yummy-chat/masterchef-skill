# Milk-Scented White Bean Paste Mooncake
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 White kidney beans 200g (soaked overnight)
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (1 for the pastry, 1 for brushing)
🧈 Unsalted butter 125g (cut into small pieces)
🥛 Milk powder 20g
🌾 Cake flour 220g
🌽 Cornstarch 1 tablespoon (for anti-stick)
💧 Water 1 teaspoon (for preparing egg wash)
🍬 Granulated sugar 80g (for stir-frying the bean paste filling)
🍚 Icing sugar 50g (for the pastry)
🌿 Vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon
🍯 Honey 1 tablespoon
🫒 Vegetable oil 80g (for stir-frying the bean paste filling)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥣 Food processor (or blender)
🍳 Non-stick pan
📡 Microwave
🔌 Electric hand mixer
🥄 Mixing bowl
🫗 Sieve
🧁 Mooncake mold (50g)
🔪 Spatula
🖌️ Brush
📋 Baking tray
📄 Baking paper
📦 Airtight container

## Steps
1. Wash the soaked white kidney beans, place them in a pressure cooker, and add water until the beans are submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook under pressure for 20 minutes. After turning off the heat, allow to cool naturally before opening the lid. Drain excess water, then pour the beans into a food processor and blend into a fine paste.
   **Key point**: Pressure cooking thoroughly softens the beans, making it easier to achieve a fine paste.

2. Place 125g of unsalted butter cut into small pieces in the microwave and heat for 40 seconds until the bottom is melted and the top remains soft and solid.
   **Note**: The butter should not be completely melted into liquid; it should remain in a semi-melted, softened state.

3. Mix 1 egg yolk with 1 teaspoon of water until well combined to prepare the egg wash, set aside.

4. Pour the bean paste into a non-stick pan over the lowest heat. Add 80g of vegetable oil and 80g of granulated sugar in two batches. After each addition, stir-fry thoroughly with a spatula until fully absorbed. Continuously stir-fry for about 12 minutes until the bean paste thickens, forms a cohesive mass, and no longer sticks to the pan or spatula.
   **Key point**: Add the oil and sugar in batches, and stir-fry continuously to prevent burning. Once done, remove from the pan, cover the surface with plastic wrap to prevent drying, and let cool to room temperature.

5. Using an electric hand mixer, beat the softened butter and 50g of icing sugar until fluffy and pale. Add 1 egg yolk, 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and 1 tablespoon of honey one at a time, mixing on low speed until combined. Sift in 20g of milk powder and 220g of cake flour. Mix with a spatula or hand mixer on low speed until just combined, then knead into a dough by hand.
   **Key point**: Knead the dough only until it comes together; do not over-knead. The dough should be slightly soft but not sticky. Cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 minutes.

6. Divide the cooled bean paste filling and the rested pastry dough into balls of 25g each. Take one pastry ball, flatten it into a disc, and shape it into a bowl that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Place one bean paste ball in the center, then slowly push the pastry upward using the thumb and index finger to seal the opening tightly. Roll it into a smooth ball.
   **Key point**: The ratio of pastry to filling is 1:1. Ensure the seal is even to avoid trapping air or having the filling leak out.

7. Gently roll the wrapped mooncake ball into a cylindrical shape. Lightly dust the surface of the mooncake ball and the inside of the mooncake mold with a thin layer of cornstarch, then brush off any excess powder. Place the mooncake ball into the mold, press it onto a baking tray lined with baking paper, apply even and vertical pressure to the bottom, then gently lift the mold to release the mooncake.
   **Key point**: The anti-stick dusting should be thin and even. Press firmly, steadily, and vertically to ensure clear and complete patterns.

8. Preheat the oven to 190°C. Place the baking tray in the middle of the oven and bake for 8 minutes. Remove the tray and brush a thin layer of egg wash only on the raised patterns on the mooncake surface. Return to the oven and continue baking for about 8 minutes until the surface turns golden brown.
   **Key point**: Brush the egg wash thinly, only on the raised patterns, to avoid filling in the grooves.

9. Let the mooncakes cool slightly while still warm, then place them in an airtight container and store at room temperature overnight. The next day, the pastry will become glossy, reddish, and softer.
   **Key point**: Sealing while still warm is crucial for promoting oil return, enhancing flavor, and improving texture.

## Health Advice
This milk-scented white bean paste mooncake has a high oil and sugar content, making it suitable as a festive treat to enjoy in small portions. Diabetics or those on a weight-loss journey should consume sparingly. White kidney beans are rich in dietary fiber and protein, but the added oil and sugar during stir-frying offset some of these health benefits. Allow the mooncakes to rest overnight for oil return to ensure the pastry is glossy and soft.
