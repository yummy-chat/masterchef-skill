# Steamed Sea Bass (Qing Zheng Lu Yu)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 sea bass  
🌿 3 scallions  
🧄 1 piece of ginger  
🧂 10-15ml cooking oil  
🧂 10-15ml steamed fish soy sauce  
🧂 10-15ml cooking wine  
🧂 5-10g salt  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Steamer  
🥘 Plate  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the ginger, then cut into thin strips. Cut the white part of the scallions into sections, and the green part into thin strips. Soak the scallion strips in cold water and set aside.  
**Key Point**: Soaking scallion strips in cold water helps them curl up attractively and removes their pungent taste.

2. Clean the sea bass and pat it dry. Make a few diagonal cuts on both sides. Rub the fish with salt to remove the slime, then evenly spread 10g of salt all over the inside and outside of the fish. Marinate for at least 10 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Rubbing the slime off with salt effectively removes fishy odors. Do not shorten the marinating time.

3. Stuff the fish belly with ginger slices and scallion white sections. Sprinkle the remaining ginger slices and scallion white sections on the surface of the fish. Place two chopsticks on the steaming plate and set the fish on top of them, so it is elevated from the plate.  
**Key Point**: Elevating the fish with chopsticks prevents it from soaking in the released juices, reducing fishiness and ensuring even heat distribution.

4. Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Place the plate with the fish inside, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: Adjust the steaming time according to the size of the fish. Usually, steam for no more than 10 minutes over high heat to avoid overcooking.

5. Once steamed, remove the plate, discard the accumulated liquid, and remove the ginger slices and scallion white sections from the fish. Transfer the fish to a clean plate.  
**Key Point**: Discarding the liquid and changing plates removes the fishy juices, enhancing the clean flavor.

6. Evenly drizzle 15ml of steamed fish soy sauce over the fish, then sprinkle fresh ginger strips and scallion strips on top.  
7. Heat 10ml of cooking oil in a pan until it smokes, then pour the hot oil evenly over the fish to release the aroma of the ginger and scallions. Serve immediately.  
**Key Point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the fragrance of the ginger, scallions, and soy sauce.

## Health Tips
Steamed sea bass is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness-conscious individuals, the elderly, and children. Be mindful of the salt amount during marinating; those with high blood pressure should reduce the salt. Using chopsticks to elevate the fish during steaming effectively reduces fishiness, and controlling the steaming time to around 10 minutes ensures the fish remains tender and juicy.
