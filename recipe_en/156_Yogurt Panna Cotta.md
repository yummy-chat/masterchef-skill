# Yogurt Panna Cotta
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🛒 **Ingredients**
- 🥛 Whipping cream 200g
- 🍬 Sugar 40g
- 🥄 Plain yogurt 250g
- 🍮 Gelatin sheets 6g

🧂 **Seasonings**
- No additional seasonings

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 **Kitchen Tools**
- 🍳 Pot
- 🥣 Sieve
- 🧊 Refrigerator
- 🍶 Container

## Instructions
1. Cut the gelatin sheets into small pieces and soak them in cold water to soften.
   **Key point**: Gelatin sheets must be soaked in cold water to avoid deactivation from hot water.

2. Pour the whipping cream and sugar into a pot, heat to 60°C, and stir until well combined.
   **Key point**: Do not heat the whipping cream above 60°C to prevent compromising the texture.

3. Remove from heat, take out the softened gelatin sheets, squeeze out excess water, and add them to the warm cream mixture. Stir until fully dissolved.

4. Allow the cream mixture to cool to 40°C, then add the plain yogurt. Gently stir until evenly combined, avoiding the formation of bubbles.
   **Key point**: Ensure the mixture has cooled to 40°C before adding the yogurt to prevent separation or loss of yogurt activity.

5. Pass the mixture through a sieve twice to ensure a smooth and lump-free texture.
   **Key point**: Straining twice removes clumps, guaranteeing a silky-smooth panna cotta.

6. Divide the mixture into containers and refrigerate for at least 4 hours to allow it to set.
   **Key point**: Insufficient chilling time will affect the setting; overnight refrigeration is recommended for best results.

## Health Advice
Yogurt panna cotta contains relatively high fat and sugar. It is recommended as an occasional dessert, which can be paired with fruit or black coffee to balance the flavor. Best enjoyed in small portions after a meal, and be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive sugar intake.
