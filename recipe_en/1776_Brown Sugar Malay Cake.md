# Brown Sugar Malay Cake
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 200g
🌿 Tapioca starch 200g (for a soft and chewy texture)
🍬 Brown sugar 100g
💧 Hot water 400ml (for dissolving brown sugar)
🍞 Yeast 4g
🧁 Baking powder 3g
🌽 Corn oil 15ml (or other neutral vegetable oil)

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Whisk
📏 Measuring cup/measuring spoons
🫖 Plastic wrap
🖌️ Silicone brush
⬜ Square mold (baking pan or heat-resistant container)
🍲 Steamer

## Instructions
1. Place brown sugar in a bowl, pour in 400ml hot water, stir until completely dissolved, let cool to room temperature and set aside.
2. In another large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, tapioca starch, and yeast evenly.
3. Pour the cooled brown sugar water into the flour mixture, whisk until smooth and free of lumps.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot to ferment until the batter expands to twice its original volume, with dense bubbles appearing on the surface.
5. Add baking powder and corn oil to the fermented batter, whisk thoroughly to completely release large air bubbles, restoring the batter to a fine consistency.
6. Evenly brush the inner walls of the square mold with oil, pour in the batter, and gently tap the mold to remove any remaining air bubbles.
7. Let it rest for 30 minutes for a second fermentation until the batter visibly expands.
8. Bring water in a steamer to a boil, place the mold inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 35 minutes.
9. After steaming, remove and let cool until the cake surface is dry and bouncy to the touch. Run a knife along the inner walls of the mold, invert, and unmold.
10. Allow the Malay cake to cool completely, then cut into square pieces and serve.

## Health Tips
Brown sugar Malay cake has a soft, chewy texture. Brown sugar is rich in iron, which may help warm the body and replenish blood, making it suitable as an afternoon tea dessert. However, be mindful of portion size as the combination of flour and tapioca starch has a high glycemic index. Pair with unsweetened tea to balance the sweetness.
