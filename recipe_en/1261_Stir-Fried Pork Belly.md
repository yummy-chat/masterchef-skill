# Stir-Fried Pork Belly
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients  
🥩 Pork belly approx. 300g (skin removed, thinly sliced)  
🫑 Green bell pepper 1 (cut into pieces)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper 1/2 (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Scallions to taste (use white parts, cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (crushed)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced into diamond shapes)  

## Seasonings  
🫒 Cooking oil to taste  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) approx. 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking wine approx. 1 tablespoon  
🍬 Sugar approx. 1/2 teaspoon  
🧂 Dark soy sauce approx. 1 teaspoon  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chinese chef’s knife  
🪵 Thick wooden cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Soup spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil to the wok, place in the pork belly slices, and stir-fry over medium heat until the slices release oil and the edges curl slightly.  
**Doneness check**: Stir-fry until the meat forms “oil lamp holders” (curled, cup-like shapes).  
   **Key point**: Fully render the pork fat for a more aromatic flavor without greasiness.  
2. Push the pork slices to the side of the wok, add Doubanjiang to the oil and stir-fry until red oil is released, then mix well with the pork slices.  
**Key point**: The Doubanjiang must be fully stir-fried in oil to release its fragrance, enhancing the flavor once the red oil emerges.  
3. Add ginger slices, minced garlic, sugar, cooking wine, and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry evenly until the color is well distributed.  
**Key point**: Sugar is used to balance the saltiness of Doubanjiang and enhance umami; dark soy sauce mainly adds color.  
4. Add green bell pepper pieces, red bell pepper pieces, and scallion white sections. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the vegetables are just cooked, then remove from the wok and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This pork belly dish is rich in protein and fat, paired with bell peppers to supplement vitamin C, making it suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or needing an energy boost. Note that Doubanjiang and dark soy sauce have a high salt content; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount as needed.
