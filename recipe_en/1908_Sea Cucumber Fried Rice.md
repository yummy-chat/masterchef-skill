# Sea Cucumber Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦑 Live sea cucumber, appropriate amount (requires self-preparation)  
🥚 Eggs, 2  
🍚 Rice, about 300-400 grams (day-old cold rice recommended)  
🧅 Scallions, chopped, appropriate amount  
🦀 Crab roe, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🧂 Soy sauce, a pinch  
🐟 Fish sauce, a pinch  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (optional)  
🍗 Chicken powder, a pinch (optional)  
🥜 Peanut oil, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  

## Preparation Steps  
**Sea Cucumber Preparation**  
1. Using scissors, cut from the mouth of the live sea cucumber to half its body length.  
2. Place the sea cucumber in cold water in a pot, bring to a boil over high heat, and continue boiling for 10 minutes until it is semi-dehydrated and dark black. Remove and rinse off surface slime.  
3. Put it into a pressure cooker, add water, and cook for 18 minutes after the steam comes up.  
4. Remove and test with a bamboo skewer; if it pierces easily, the preparation is successful. Rinse under cold water for half an hour, then soak in mineral water. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight; the volume will roughly double.  
5. Cut open the unopened tail end, clean out internal sand and grit, then soak again in mineral water for 2-3 days, changing the water daily, until completely tender.  

**Fried Rice**  
1. Dice the prepared sea cucumber into small pieces; beat the eggs; mix sugar, soy sauce, and fish sauce into a sauce.  
2. Boil water in a pot, quickly blanch the sea cucumber pieces, then drain.  
3. Heat the wok, coat with oil, discard the hot oil, and add cold peanut oil. Pour in the beaten eggs, pressing and breaking them with the back of a spoon until scrambled into small pieces.  
   **Key point**: Hot wok with cold oil prevents sticking; cook eggs until "well-done" for more fragrance.  
4. Turn off the heat, add the rice, and thoroughly mix with the egg pieces. Then turn the heat back to high and stir-fry until each grain is distinct.  
   **Key point**: Mixing with the heat off prevents the rice from becoming sticky.  
5. Add the sea cucumber pieces and the sauce, stir-frying until evenly combined.  
6. Finally, add the chopped scallions and crab roe, quickly toss a few times, then remove from the wok.  
   **Key point**: Scallions and crab roe are added last to preserve fresh aroma and popping texture.  

## Health Advice  
Sea cucumber is high in protein and low in fat; pairing it with eggs and crab roe provides quality amino acids, suitable for those needing muscle building or recovery. However, the preparation process is time-consuming, and sodium content may be high; those with hypertension should control the amounts of soy sauce and fish sauce used.
