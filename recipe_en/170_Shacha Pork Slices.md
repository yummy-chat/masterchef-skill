# Shacha Pork Slices
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin (sliced thinly against the grain, as needed)  
🥬 Lettuce (washed, for lining the plate)  
🧅 Scallions 2-3 (cut into sections and finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic (minced, as needed)  
🫚 Ginger (sliced, as needed)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers 1-2 (diced)  
🫘 Shacha sauce (about 50g)  
🧂 Salt (to taste)  
🧂 Light soy sauce (to taste)  
🍶 Cooking wine (to taste)  
🌾 Potato starch (about 2 tablespoons)  
🌶️ White pepper (to taste)  
🍬 Sugar (a pinch)  
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, for stir-frying the sauce and hot oil drizzling)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Plate  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪚 Cutting board  

## Steps
1. Place the pork slices in a bowl. Add salt, light soy sauce, and potato starch. Mix well and marinate until flavored.  
**Key point**: Coating with starch makes the meat slices more tender and smooth.

2. Heat oil in a wok. Add scallion sections and ginger slices, stir-fry until fragrant. Add shacha sauce and stir-fry over low heat until aromatic. Pour in enough boiling water. Season with salt, a pinch of sugar, and white pepper. Stir to combine.  
**Note**: Shacha sauce must be fully stir-fried to develop a rich broth flavor.

3. Once the broth boils, add lettuce. Quickly blanch over high heat, then remove and drain. Arrange the lettuce on the plate as a base.

4. Keep the broth boiling. Add the marinated pork slices one by one. Gently separate with chopsticks. Cook until the slices change color and float. Remove and place on top of the lettuce. Ladle some broth over.  
**Key point**: Add the meat slices when the broth is boiling so they cook quickly and remain tender.

5. Sprinkle minced garlic, diced red chili, and chopped scallions over the pork. In a separate pan, heat a spoonful of oil until smoking. Pour the hot oil over the toppings to release their aroma. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This shacha pork dish is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for those building muscle or losing weight. However, shacha sauce and the hot oil drizzle are relatively high in calories, so control the portions. Using lettuce as a base adds dietary fiber. Pairing with mixed grain rice helps achieve balanced nutrition.
