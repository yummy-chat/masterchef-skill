# Five-Spice Crispy Soybeans
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Soybeans 1 bowl (pre-soaked)  
🧄 Garlic 3 cloves (peeled, whole)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, numbing peppercorns, dried chili peppers to taste  
🧂 Salt 1 teaspoon  
🍬 White sugar about 3 times the amount of salt  
🌶️ Chili powder to taste  
🌿 Cumin powder to taste  
🌿 Five-spice powder to taste  
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor) to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Steps  
1. Soak the soybeans in advance, blanch them in boiling water for about 2 minutes, then remove and drain the surface water using a slotted spoon.  
2. Add the drained soybeans and whole garlic cloves to cold oil, and cook over the lowest heat throughout.  
**Note**: Starting with cold oil allows the moisture inside the soybeans to slowly evaporate, making them crispy.  
3. Continue frying over the lowest heat for about 10 minutes, until the garlic turns golden brown and the soybean skins become slightly wrinkled, then remove and drain the oil.  
**Key**: Judge the heat by the color of the garlic.  
4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, and stir-fry the Sichuan peppercorns, numbing peppercorns, and dried chili pepper segments over low heat until fragrant.  
5. Add the fried soybeans and stir-fry evenly.  
6. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and about 3 times that amount of sugar, and stir-fry until the seasonings are evenly coated on the soybeans.  
**Note**: The salt-to-sugar ratio is approximately 1:3.  
7. Add chili powder, cumin powder, and five-spice powder, and stir-fry evenly.  
8. Pour baijiu along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly.  
**Note**: Pouring baijiu along the wok edge enhances the complex aroma of the spices, makes the soybeans crispier after cooling, and extends their shelf life.  
9. Remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Advice  
This five-spice crispy soybean dish is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable as a side dish. However, since it is fried and relatively high in calories, it is recommended to consume no more than one small bowl per serving and to control salt and sugar intake. Ensure to fry over the lowest heat throughout to avoid burning, which can produce harmful substances.
