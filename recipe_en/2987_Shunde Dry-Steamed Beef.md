## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef loin into thin slices against the grain, aiming for even thickness.
**Key point**: Cutting against the grain severs muscle fibers, ensuring a tender texture.

2. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, satay sauce, and sugar to the beef slices. Toss first to mix evenly, allowing the base flavors to fully penetrate.
**Key point**: The base flavors must be thoroughly incorporated.

3. Sprinkle in cornstarch and toss again, coating each slice evenly with a thin slurry.
**Key point**: The thin slurry locks in the meat juices.

4. Add julienned ginger and dried tangerine peel, gently mixing to combine.
5. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of vegetable oil, toss to coat the beef, then let it marinate for half an hour to absorb the flavors.
**Key point**: The oil film helps prevent moisture loss during baking, keeping the meat tender and springy.

6. Preheat the oven in advance, select the "steam baking" mode, and set it to 220°C.
7. Spread the marinated beef slices in a single layer on a shallow plate, avoiding overlap to ensure even heating.
8. Place the plate on the middle rack of the oven and bake at 220°C for about 8 minutes.
**Observation**: The beef is done when the edges are slightly charred, and only a small amount of rendered fat remains on the plate, with no excess liquid.

9. Once out of the oven, garnish with spring onion rings or julienned spring onions, and serve immediately.
**Key point**: Enjoy while hot—the beef is tender with a slight char, rich in satay and tangerine peel flavors.

## Health Tips  
This dry-steamed beef is flavored with satay sauce, dried tangerine peel, and ginger. It is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those aiming to reduce fat and build muscle. Be careful not to overbake to preserve the tender and springy texture.
