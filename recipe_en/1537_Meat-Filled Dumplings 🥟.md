# Meat-Filled Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed
💧 Water, as needed (add gradually in small amounts)
🐖 Ground pork, as needed
🧅 Scallion, as needed (use the white part, finely chopped)
🫙 Scallion-ginger water, as needed (add gradually in small amounts)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for kneading dough, seasoning filling, and cooking dumplings)
🌶 Sichuan peppercorn powder, as needed
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🌿 White pepper powder, as needed
🧂 Thirteen-spice or five-spice powder, as needed (optional)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥢 Rolling pin
🥢 Chopsticks
🥘 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍳 Wok or pot
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: Mix the all-purpose flour with a pinch of salt. Gradually add warm water while stirring with chopsticks until the mixture becomes flaky, then knead it into a smooth dough.
   *Key point*: Add water in batches to control the dough’s firmness; salt makes the wrapper more elastic.

2. **Rest the dough**: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20 minutes to fully relax the gluten.

3. **Prepare scallion oil**: Place the finely chopped white parts of the scallion in a bowl, add Sichuan peppercorn powder and sesame oil, then pour in hot oil and stir well.

4. **Season the filling**: Add salt to the ground pork, then gradually add scallion-ginger water while stirring consistently in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed. Next, add chicken bouillon, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper powder, mixing thoroughly. Set aside.
   *Key point*: Stirring continuously in one direction creates a bouncy, tender texture.

5. **Knead the dough again**: Remove the rested dough and knead it until the surface is smooth and no longer sticky.

6. **Roll out the wrappers**: Roll the dough into an even rope, then cut it into evenly sized pieces using a rolling cut method. Flatten each piece, dust with dry flour, and use a rolling pin to push and pull while rotating the dough with your other hand to form a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.
   *Key point*: A thicker center prevents the wrapper from breaking during cooking.

7. **Combine the final filling**: Just before wrapping the dumplings, pour the scallion oil into the meat mixture and stir again to combine.
   *Key point*: Adding the scallion oil last locks in moisture and preserves the scallion flavor.

8. **Wrap the dumplings**: Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center of a wrapper, then pinch the edges tightly shut using your preferred technique.
   *Key point*: Ensure the seal is tight to prevent the filling from leaking out during cooking.

9. **Cook the dumplings**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a spoonful of salt, then gently drop in the dumplings and stir lightly with chopsticks to prevent sticking. After the water returns to a boil, add a bowl of cold water; repeat this process three times. Once all the dumplings float to the surface and appear plump and translucent, remove them with a slotted spoon.
   *Key point*: Adding salt to the water prevents sticking; adding cold water three times ensures the wrapper and filling cook through evenly without the wrapper overcooking.

## Health Tips
Meat-filled dumplings provide a balanced mix of flour, meat, and vegetables, though they are moderately high in calories. It is recommended to serve 10–12 dumplings per person as a main course, paired with vinegar or garlic paste to aid digestion. They are ideal for family gatherings, but be mindful of salt and oyster sauce usage—individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of dark soy sauce.
