# Crispy Lamb Tripe  
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Lamb tripe (lamb omasum), appropriate amount  
🫧 Water, appropriate amount (for blanching)  
🧂 Sesame paste dressing, appropriate amount (for dipping, traditional recipe includes sesame paste, chive flower sauce, fermented bean curd, chili oil, etc.)  
🍃 1 bamboo leaf (to enhance aroma and remove off-flavor from the broth)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Copper pot (for boiling clear broth and maintaining stable water temperature)  
🔗 Long-handled strainer (skimmer, for holding ingredients during quick blanching and removal)  
🍶 Small ceramic plates (preheated to keep the finished dish warm, also used for tapping the strainer edge during removal to help ingredients separate and drain water)  
⏱️ Timer (for precise control of blanching seconds)  
🌡️ Thermometer (to measure and control the water temperature in the pot)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Clean the lamb tripe thoroughly and cut into strips suitable for blanching.  
**Note**: The ingredients must be used fresh on the same day; freezing significantly deteriorates the texture.  

2. Place several small ceramic plates in a pot of water to heat, for keeping the finished dish warm later.  
**Key point**: In the traditional method, hot plates maintain the temperature of the tripe, enhancing the eating experience.  

3. Bring water to a boil in the copper pot, then add one bamboo leaf to infuse the broth with a fresh aroma and remove the organ odor.  
4. Place the sliced lamb tripe into the long-handled strainer.  
5. When the water temperature in the pot stabilizes at 70°C, submerge the strainer in the water to blanch.  
**Key point**: This is the part of tripe that requires the lowest water temperature; too high a temperature or too long a time will make it tough.  

6. Count "1, 2, 3" silently; the entire process takes only 3 seconds. Immediately lift the strainer out of the water upon reaching the time.  
**State check**: Lamb tripe is cooked as soon as it is heated; the extremely short time is essential to maintain its ultimate tender and smooth texture.  

7. Before removing from the strainer, quickly tap the edge of the strainer with the preheated small ceramic plate to help the ingredients separate and fully drain excess water.  
8. Promptly transfer the blanched lamb tripe to the preheated small ceramic plate, serve immediately, and enjoy by dipping in the sesame paste dressing.  

## Health Tips  
The key to crispy lamb tripe lies in precise control of the water temperature at 70°C and blanching for 3 seconds. Longer cooking times result in a tough texture. It is suitable for those who prefer a tender and smooth mouthfeel, but individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation.
