# Summer Refreshing Icy Slush
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍎 Assorted fruits (e.g., mango, lychee, cherry, cucumber, berries, etc.) 200g
🧊 Lightly salted ice cubes 100–300g (adjust based on fruit water content)
🍚 Glutinous rice or cooked zongzi 50–60g
🥛 Milk or coconut milk 20–80ml (adjust based on fruit water content)
💧 Purified water 500ml (for making ice cubes)
🧂 Salt 3g (added to ice-making water)
🍯 Honey, syrup, or condensed milk to taste (optional, for adjusting sweetness)
🥛 Milk powder 20g (optional, makes slush creamier and melt slower)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Steamer or rice cooker (for steaming glutinous rice)
🥤 High-speed blender/juicer/portable blender cup
🛍️ Sealed ziplock bag
🔨 Rolling pin or hard object (for breaking ice)
🧺 Cloth or towel (to wrap ice bag and prevent splashing)
🔪 Knife, cutting board, peeler
🥄 Spoon
🧃 Sturdy straw (for pitting cherries)
🥛 Cup (for peeling mango and serving the finished slush)

## Instructions

### Preparation
1. **Steam glutinous rice**: Soak glutinous rice in advance, then steam until fully cooked using a steamer or rice cooker. If using pre-cooked zongzi, you can use it directly; zongzi gives a chewier texture, while glutinous rice yields distinct grains.
2. **Prepare fruits**:
   - Mango: Cut open, remove pit, scrape flesh off with the rim of a cup.
   - Lychee: Peel and remove pit, be sure to remove the bitter "plug" at the bottom of the flesh.
   - Cherry: Use a sturdy straw to push out the pit from the center.
   - Cucumber: Optional to peel or keep the skin; remove seeds and cut into chunks.
   - Other berries: Rinse and drain well.
3. **Make lightly salted ice cubes**: Mix 500ml purified water with 3g salt, shake well, pour into a ziplock bag, seal half, expel air, then seal completely. Lay flat and freeze into a solid ice sheet.
   - **Note**: Ideal water-to-salt ratio is 500:5; here reduced to 3g to prevent the finished slush from being too salty. Freezing flat creates large ice sheets, easier for subsequent handling.

### Blending the Slush
1. Place prepared fruit chunks, broken salted ice cubes, and milk or coconut milk into a blender. Add honey/syrup/condensed milk now if sweetness is desired.
   - **Key point**: For high-water-content fruits, reduce liquid amount or substitute 20g milk powder for liquid to make slush thicker and slower to melt. For low-water-content fruits, add more liquid as needed to prevent the machine from stalling. Adjust ratios according to fruit characteristics and desired consistency.
2. Blend the base ingredients until smooth, then add the cooked glutinous rice.
   - **Texture control**: For a chewy, grainy texture, pulse briefly to keep rice grains intact. For a smooth, paste-like consistency, add more rice and blend longer until grains dissolve into the mixture.
3. If the blender struggles due to low power, **do not force it**. Use these methods: pulse in short bursts, manually shake the blender to redistribute ingredients, or add ice cubes in small batches.
4. Blend until all ingredients are evenly mixed and reach a semi-fluid, thick consistency.
5. Pour into a glass and enjoy immediately. Do not let it sit too long, or it will melt and lose its icy and textural contrast.

## Health Tips
This slush includes glutinous rice for carbohydrate energy, making it suitable for summer refreshment. However, control the amount of salted ice cubes to avoid excessive sodium intake. For those with diabetes or managing blood sugar, reduce honey and condensed milk, and opt for low-GI fruits instead.
