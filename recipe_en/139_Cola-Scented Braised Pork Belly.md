# Cola-Scented Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, about 2 pounds (cut into evenly sized square pieces)
🍊 Orange peel, from one orange (to add fruity aroma and acidity)
🫚 Ginger, about 5-6 slices (some for blanching, some for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallion, about 2 stalks (tied into knots, some for blanching, some for stir-frying)
🥤 Cola, 1 can (330 ml) (core seasoning)
✨ Star anise, about 2 pieces
🌿 Cinnamon stick, about 1 small piece
🍂 Bay leaves, about 2 pieces
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (for blanching to remove gamey smell)
🧂 Chicken powder, 1 small teaspoon (to enhance flavor)
🧴 Light soy sauce, to taste (for seasoning)
🟤 Dark soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon (for coloring)
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste (two squeezes, to enhance umami)
🍎 Fragrant vinegar, about 1 tablespoon (to provide acidity and balance richness)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (main vessel for stir-frying and braising)
🥄 Soup ladle (for stirring, and holding ingredients and sauce)
🥘 Slotted spoon (to remove pork belly after blanching)
🥢 Chopsticks (to pick out spices and fruit peels after braising)
♨️ Pressure cooker (optional, to shorten braising time)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Scorch the skin side of the pork belly over a flame until charred yellow, then scrape and wash clean.
2.  Cut the pork belly into evenly sized square pieces.
3.  Place the pork belly in a pot with cold water. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and cook until the meat pieces turn white. Remove and rinse with hot water.
4.  Without adding oil to the wok, add the blanched pork belly. Stir-fry slowly over medium-low heat to render the fat, until the surface of the meat pieces turns golden brown.
    **Key Point**: Do not use too high heat when stir-frying. Patiently render the fat out; this is key to achieving a rich but not greasy finished dish.
5.  Pour off most of the rendered fat, leaving a little base oil. Add ginger slices, scallion knots, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and orange peel. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6.  Pour in the entire can of cola, allowing the sweetness and color to be released.
7.  Add enough hot water to completely submerge all the ingredients.
8.  Mix the chicken powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fragrant vinegar together in advance. Pour this mixture into the wok and stir to combine well.
9.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover and braise for about 60 minutes. If using a pressure cooker, cook for about 20 minutes after it reaches pressure.
    **Note**: The braising time must be sufficient to ensure the fatty part becomes soft, tender, and flavorful.
10. After braising is complete, use chopsticks to remove the orange peel, scallion, ginger, and all the spice residues.
11. Turn the heat to high. Continuously turn the meat pieces to reduce the sauce until it thickens, allowing the glaze to evenly coat each piece of meat.
    **Key Point**: Keep stirring during the sauce reduction to prevent the bottom from burning, ensuring each piece of meat is evenly coated with the sauce.
12. Plate the glossy, red-braised pork belly. Serve.

## Health Advice
Although this dish uses fruit peel and fragrant vinegar to cut the richness, it is still relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with vegetables or coarse grains and control the portion size. Suitable for those who enjoy a sweet and savory flavor, to be enjoyed in moderation.
