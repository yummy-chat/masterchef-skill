# Fresh Milk Knife-Cut Mini Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g  
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 6g  
🥛 Milk powder 6g  
🧪 Dry yeast 2.5g  
🥛 Milk 150g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 9g (can substitute with vegetable oil or lard)  
🌾 Cake flour, as needed (for dusting, prevents sticking and creates finer texture)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥛 Glass measuring cup  
🥄 Whisk (or stirring rod)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
📦 Plastic wrap/glass bowl lid  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔪 Bench scraper  
🍲 Steamer and steaming basket (with parchment paper or cloth liner)  

## Procedure
1. Place the milk, fine granulated sugar, and dry yeast together in the measuring cup. Stir with the whisk until the sugar and yeast are mostly dissolved. Then add the milk powder and stir until well combined.
   **Note**: The liquid must be added gradually; do not pour it all in at once. It is normal for the dough to be dry initially; do not add extra water.
2. Place the all-purpose flour on the work surface. Set aside a small amount of cake flour as bench flour for dusting.
3. Gradually pour the mixed liquid into the flour, stirring with the spatula until it forms a shaggy mass. Add the softened unsalted butter and knead by hand into a dough.
   **Note**: Do not pour all the liquid in at once. It is normal for the dough to be stiff and dry at the start; do not add extra water.
4. Use the heel of your hand to push and knead the dough until there are no dry patches. Keep your elbows straight while kneading, using the weight of your upper body through the heel of your palm.
5. Cover the dough with a bowl and let it rest at room temperature for 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Resting relaxes the gluten, making subsequent steps easier.
6. After resting, continue kneading with the heel of your hand. You can roll the dough out into a long log and knead it in sections until the surface is smooth.
   **Note**: If you want a "water-glazed skin" effect, the dough must be kneaded thoroughly. If the dough tightens up again, you may add an additional rest.
7. Shape the smooth dough into a ball, cover it, and let it ferment at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until it is about 1.5 times its original size.
   **Key**: The mini buns require a semi-proofed state. The dough should indent slowly when pressed lightly and show small air bubbles at the bottom. Under-proofing or over-proofing will affect the final result.
8. Remove the dough, sprinkle a moderate amount of cake flour to prevent sticking. Press down to degas by hand. Use the rolling pin to roll the dough into a large sheet about 40cm x 30cm with even thickness. Fold it into thirds like a blanket, then shape it into a rectangle.
   **Key**: Rolling out is a crucial step for degassing. Try to roll it as thin and even as possible, expelling large air pockets.
9. Roll the folded dough out again into a thinner sheet, about 50cm x 16cm. Starting from one end, stretch it slightly while tightly rolling it into a long log.
   **Note**: The rolling must be tight with no air pockets trapped inside, or the final product will have a loose crumb structure. After rolling, you can squeeze the log with your hands to make it more compact.
10. Gently roll the log back and forth on the work surface to achieve a uniform thickness. Trim off the uneven ends. Cut the log into small pieces, each about 5cm wide.
    **Note**: Cut decisively. Ensure the pieces are wide enough so they can stand upright after steaming.
11. Place the shaped buns in the steamer basket lined with parchment paper. Cover with the lid. Use warm water for the final proofing, lasting 20-25 minutes. The buns should feel light, have expanded noticeably, and spring back slowly when gently poked.
12. Bring the water in the steamer to a boil. Place the steamer basket with the buns inside. Cover with the lid and steam over medium heat for 14 minutes.
    **Key**: Place the buns in the steamer after the water has boiled. Use medium heat. Do not open the lid immediately after steaming. Let the buns rest in the steamer for 1-2 minutes to prevent collapsing.

## Health Advice  
This fresh milk knife-cut steamed bun recipe replaces some of the water with milk and milk powder, making it richer in calcium and protein. It is a suitable staple food supplement for children and vegetarians. It is recommended to control the amount of sugar used. Those with high blood sugar can use sugar substitutes or omit the sugar entirely. Precise control of fermentation temperature and time is essential, otherwise, the buns may have collapsed surfaces or uneven internal texture.
