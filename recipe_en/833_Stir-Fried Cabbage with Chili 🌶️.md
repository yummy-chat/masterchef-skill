# Stir-Fried Cabbage with Chili 🌶️

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 1 head of pointed cabbage (loose leaves, crispy and tender)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (soaked in water beforehand)  
🌶️ Fresh red chili peppers, to taste  
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🧈 Lard, to taste (slightly more oil than usual)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🥢 Light soy sauce, to taste (drizzle along the edge of the wok)  
🍶 Aged vinegar, to taste (drizzle along the edge of the wok before removing from heat)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥄 Strainer  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🍽️ Serving plate/bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the cabbage in half, remove the core, separate leaves one by one, wash thoroughly, and make sure to drain completely.  
   **Note**: Draining water is key to success; otherwise, water released during stir-frying will make the cabbage soft and mushy, turning it into "boiled cabbage."
2. Tear the leaves into large pieces by hand. For tougher cabbage stems, gently pound them with the flat side of the knife to make them easier to flavor and improve texture.
3. Prepare the sliced garlic, soaked dried chili peppers, fresh red chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns.
4. Heat the wok over high heat, add enough oil until it reaches 70% hot. Add the drained cabbage and stir-fry quickly over high heat, cooking until it releases moisture, until the cabbage is half-cooked and leaves are slightly wilted.
5. Use a strainer to remove the cabbage and discard the excess liquid.  
   **Doneness check**: The cabbage should be visibly reduced in volume, darker in color, and semi-transparent.
6. In another wok, heat a generous amount of lard, add the sliced garlic, and raise the heat until the oil surface smokes.
7. Add the Sichuan peppercorns, fresh red chili peppers, and soaked dried chili peppers in order, stirring quickly until a fragrant, charred chili aroma develops.  
   **Note**: Oil temperature must be high, but avoid burning to a blackened state, which will create bitterness.
8. Return the half-cooked cabbage to the wok, stir-fry quickly over high heat to allow the cabbage to fully absorb the charred chili flavor, without overcooking.
9. Quickly add salt, white sugar, and MSG, then drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, stirring rapidly to combine.  
   **Key**: Work quickly; drizzling the soy sauce along the hot wok edge activates its caramelized aroma.
10. Before removing from heat, drizzle a circle of aged vinegar along the wok edge, stir-fry a couple of times quickly, then immediately turn off the heat and serve.  
    **Key Point**: High heat causes the vinegar's acidity to evaporate, leaving only its fragrance, preventing overpowering sourness.

## Health Advice
This dish uses lard and high-heat quick stir-frying—preserving the cabbage’s crunch while adding rich fat flavor, making it ideal paired with rice to balance calories. Note the separate stir-frying to prevent water release. Reduce chili for those with sensitive stomachs.
