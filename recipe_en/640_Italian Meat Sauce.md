# Italian Meat Sauce

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Ground beef 500g  
🐖 Ground pork 500g  
🥕 Carrot 1 (finely diced)  
🌿 Celery 1 stalk (peeled and finely diced)  
🧅 Onion 1 (minced)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🍝 Pasta (such as penne), to taste (for serving)  
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil, to taste  
🧈 Butter, 1 small piece  
🍅 Tomato paste 200g  
🍅 Tomatoes (canned or fresh) 500g  
🍷 Dry red wine 150ml  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ Black pepper, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Cast iron pot  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥄 Spoon  
🔥 Oven (optional, for simmering)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Pour olive oil into the pot over high heat. Add the ground beef and ground pork, stirring until the meat changes color and releases liquid. Continue stirring until the liquid has completely evaporated and you hear a distinct sizzling sound of oil again.  
   **Key point**: Drying out the moisture is crucial for removing gamey odors and enhancing flavor. Once done, set the meat aside.  
2. Reduce the heat to low. Using the browned residue left in the pot, add the minced onion, diced carrot, and diced celery, and stir until fragrant. Then add the minced garlic and a piece of butter, stirring until the vegetables soften.  
3. Return the cooked meat to the pot. Add the tomato paste and stir thoroughly to combine. Then add the tomatoes, dry red wine, salt, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Mix well.  
4. Add an appropriate amount of water. Bring to a boil, then cover with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for about 1.5 hours. If using a stovetop, stir occasionally to prevent sticking.  
5. Simmer until the sauce turns a glossy red. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt or black pepper as needed. Add the cooked pasta directly to the pot with the sauce and toss to combine evenly.  

## Health Advice  
This Bolognese sauce is a perfect combination of carbohydrates and protein. However, it is recommended to enjoy the total amount of 500g ground beef and 500g ground pork in separate servings. Pairing it with whole wheat pasta and a generous vegetable salad is healthier. Suitable for those looking to build muscle, but individuals with weak digestion should be cautious with the amount of butter used.
