# Bubble Mini Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 250g
🍬 Fine sugar 15g
🧂 Salt 2g
🥛 Milk powder 12g
🥚 Egg 1 (about 50g, beaten)
🥛 Milk 165g (15g used for dissolving yeast, remaining about 150g mixed with egg)
🍥 Dry yeast 2g
🧈 Unsalted butter 25g (for dough, melted in advance)
🧈 Butter about 5g (for brushing surface after baking, melted in advance)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥄 Manual whisk
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Glass bowl
🥛 Measuring cup/bowl
🪓 Rolling pin
🔪 Dough scraper
♨️ Baking tray
🔥 Oven (requires hot water for proofing assistance)
🖌️ Brush
🧻 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. Activate yeast: Take 15g of milk, add 2g of dry yeast, stir until dissolved, and set aside.
   **Note**: The small amount of yeast is to prevent excessive expansion during cold fermentation.
2. Prepare liquid mixture: Beat the egg and mix well with the remaining approximately 150g of milk.
3. Mix dry ingredients: Place bread flour, fine sugar, salt, and milk powder in a bowl, and whisk thoroughly with a manual whisk.
4. Pour the egg-milk mixture into the dry powder, stir with a spatula until no dry powder remains; then pour in the yeast liquid and continue mixing evenly.
   **Note**: The dough will be quite loose at this stage, which is normal.
5. Add 25g of melted unsalted butter, and stir with a spatula until the butter is roughly incorporated into the dough. Do not overmix.
6. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for an overnight cold fermentation. After fermentation, the dough surface will be smooth and less sticky.
7. Remove the cold-fermented dough and place it on a work surface. Fold it once using a method similar to folding a blanket to make the surface smoother.
8. Return the folded dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap again, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
9. Dust the work surface with flour. Take out the dough, coat both sides evenly with flour, and roll it into a rectangular or square sheet about 1–1.5 cm thick.
10. Use a dough scraper to cut the sheet into small squares. Take one small piece, press the surface with two fingers to make the center bulge, shape it into a round ball, and arrange them in a baking tray, leaving gaps between each ball.
    **Key**: During shaping, try to give the dough an arc to ensure a full and round shape.
11. Place the baking tray in the oven, with a tray of hot water on the lower rack, and proof at about 37°C until the volume doubles.
12. After proofing, remove the baking tray and the hot water. Preheat the oven to 200°C bottom heat and 180°C top heat.
13. Place the baking tray on the lowest rack of the oven and bake for 20–25 minutes, until both the bottom and surface of the bread show an even golden brown color.
    **Note**: Be sure to set the top and bottom temperatures separately to ensure even browning of both the bottom and surface.
14. Immediately after baking, brush the surface of the bread with about 5g of melted butter to add shine and softness.

## Health Advice
This bubble mini bread is soft and stringy, making it suitable for pairing with milk as breakfast or afternoon tea. However, note its relatively high sugar and butter content, so control the portion size during weight loss periods. The flour has high gluten strength; those with weaker digestion can pair it with vegetables or soup.
