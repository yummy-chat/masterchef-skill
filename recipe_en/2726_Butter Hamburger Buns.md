# Butter Hamburger Buns
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥖 Bread flour 230g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 68g (approx. 1.5 large eggs)
🥚 Egg yolk 15g (approx. 1 yolk)
🥛 Milk 67g
🍞 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerance) 2.3g
🍬 Granulated sugar 37g
🧈 Unsalted butter 80g (softened)
🧂 Salt 3.6g
🌾 Dusting flour (bread flour) as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
⚖️ Digital scale
🥣 Measuring cup/bowl
🥚 Whisk
🗡️ Dough scraper
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
📦 Plastic wrap
🍳 Baking sheet
🔥 Oven
🌡️ Thermometer
🖌️ Pastry brush

## Instructions
1. Whisk the whole egg and weigh out 68g of egg liquid; set aside. Add 15g egg yolk, 67g milk, 37g granulated sugar, and 2.3g yeast to the egg liquid, then stir until well combined.
2. Place 230g bread flour on the work surface, make a well in the center, pour in the liquid mixture, and use a dough scraper to mix until no dry flour remains. Use the scraper frequently as the dough will be sticky.
3. Repeatedly slap the dough on the work surface for about 1 minute until the surface becomes smooth.
4. Place the dough in a stainless steel bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes. After resting, the dough should be able to stretch into a thin membrane with smooth, round tears.
   **Key Point**: Refrigerating relaxes the gluten, making it easier to absorb the butter later.
5. Remove the dough, stretch and flatten it into a large thin sheet, evenly spread 80g softened butter on top, then sprinkle with 3.6g salt and spread evenly. Roll up the dough.
6. Using a dough scraper, chop the rolled dough into small pieces. Repeatedly chop, mix, and knead until the butter is fully incorporated and no visible butter chunks remain. The dough will be extremely sticky at this stage—this is normal.
7. Continue slapping the dough for about 3 minutes, checking the temperature during the process; if it exceeds 25°C, cool it down immediately.
8. If the dough temperature is too high, flatten it in the stainless steel bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and place in the freezer for 5 minutes to cool. The dough will become firmer afterward.
9. Slap and shape the dough again until the surface is very smooth, then round it into a ball.
10. Place the dough in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 6–8 hours for cold fermentation.
11. Remove the cold-fermented dough, flatten it, dust with flour, and divide into 6 portions.
12. Flatten each portion into a triangle, fold the three corners toward the center, round the bottom, pinch the bottom tightly, and gently roll into a ball.
13. Place the rounded dough portions on a baking sheet, then gently press them flat with your fingers (dust your fingers with flour).
14. Let them proof in a warm, humid place until doubled in size, then move to room temperature to let the surface dry slightly.
15. Brush the tops of the hamburger buns with the reserved extra egg liquid.
16. Preheat the oven: For a convection oven, bake at 185°C for 8 minutes; for a conventional oven, bake at 220°C top heat and 190°C bottom heat for 8 minutes. Adjust based on your own oven’s performance.

## Health Advice
This hamburger bun recipe uses a relatively high amount of butter, making it high in calories. It is suitable for those needing quick energy replenishment (e.g., post-workout or manual laborers). Pair with low-fat fillings such as vegetables and lean meat to balance nutrition. Cold fermentation helps delay staling, but due to the high sugar and fat content, consume in moderation during weight loss periods.
