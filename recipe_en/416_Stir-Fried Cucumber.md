# Stir-Fried Cucumber
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Cucumber approx. 500g (two pieces)  
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (sliced and minced, kept separate)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add one spoonful each for blanching and stir-frying)  
🍬 White sugar a pinch  
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount (add during blanching, use as base oil for stir-frying)  
💧 Water starch appropriate amount (for a thin glaze before serving)  
✨ Aromatized oil a small amount (drizzle before serving for shine)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the cucumber**: Wash the cucumber, use a knife to scrape off part of the skin to remove bitterness and enhance fragrance. Cut off both ends, split lengthwise into four wedges, slice off the middle core with a flat knife, then cut diagonally into evenly sized thick slices.  
2. **Prepare the aromatics**: Slice a portion of the garlic, mince the other portion, and set them aside separately.  
3. **Blanch**: Add enough water to the wok, bring to a boil over high heat, then add appropriate salt, a pinch of white sugar, and some vegetable oil. Once boiling, add the cucumber slices, blanch over high heat for about 25 seconds until just cooked through and vibrant green, then remove and drain well.  
   **Key point**: Blanching reduces stir-frying time and keeps the cucumber crisp; adding salt, sugar, and oil helps preserve color and enhance flavor.  
4. **Aromatize**: Heat the wok, add a small amount of base oil. When the oil reaches about 50% heat, add the garlic slices and stir-fry over high heat until fragrant.  
5. **Quick stir-fry**: Add the blanched cucumber slices, stir-fry quickly over high heat throughout.  
6. **Season**: Add a spoonful of salt and a pinch of white sugar, then add the minced garlic, stirring evenly.  
   **Key point**: Adding the minced garlic later preserves the pungent aroma of raw garlic.  
7. **Thicken and add aromatized oil**: Drizzle in an appropriate amount of water starch to create a thin glaze, allowing the sauce to evenly coat the ingredients. Finish by drizzling in a little aromatized oil, stir well, and serve.  
   **Key point**: The thin glaze enhances texture, and the aromatized oil gives the dish a bright appearance.  

## Health Tips  
This stir-fried cucumber is low in calories and rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight management and those seeking refreshing meals in summer. Removing the skin and core reduces bitterness, but keeping some skin adds crunch and nutrients.
