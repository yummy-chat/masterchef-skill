# Matcha Ice Cream
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 3 egg yolks (pasteurized eggs optional)
🥛 250ml heavy cream (fat content ≥40%, refrigerated)
🍬 80g fine granulated sugar (baking-grade recommended)
🍵 20g matcha powder
💧 A small amount of warm water (for dissolving matcha powder)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
⚡ Electric hand mixer
🥣 Stainless steel or glass mixing bowl
🔍 Small sieve
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Whisk
🍱 Stainless steel storage container

## Instructions
1. Separate egg whites from yolks, using only the yolks.
   **Note**: If concerned about consuming raw eggs, use pasteurized eggs.
2. Place egg yolks and fine granulated sugar in a mixing bowl. Beat with an electric hand mixer on high speed until the mixture turns pale, thick, and fluffy, with ribbons that hold their shape when drizzled.
   **Key**: Fine granulated sugar dissolves more easily; achieving the ideal whipped state is the foundation for a smooth texture.
3. Pour the refrigerated heavy cream into another large bowl.
4. Sift matcha powder into a small bowl. Add a small amount of warm water and stir with a spatula until a thick, smooth paste forms with no dry powder.
   **Core Technique**: Due to the large amount of matcha powder, adding it directly to the cream would be difficult to mix evenly and could damage the cream's structure. Pre-dissolving with a small amount of liquid is necessary.
5. Gradually add the matcha paste to the heavy cream in parts. Stir with a whisk until combined, then continue whisking until the mixture reaches about 80% whipped.
   **State Check**: When lifting the whisk, the mixture should drip slowly.
6. Pour the whipped egg yolk mixture into the matcha cream mixture. Gently but thoroughly stir with a whisk until fully combined, forming a uniformly colored ice cream base.
7. Pour the ice cream base into a stainless steel storage container. Smooth the surface, cover with a lid, and place in the freezer for about 3 hours.
   **Key**: Using a stainless steel container facilitates rapid freezing, resulting in a finer texture.

## Health Advice
Matcha ice cream has a high sugar content. It is recommended to control portion sizes and enjoy it occasionally as a summer dessert. If consuming raw, use pasteurized eggs. It is rich in dairy fat and high in calories; individuals aiming for weight loss should consume with caution.
