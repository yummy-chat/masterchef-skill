# Pandan Kaya Toast
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Toast bread slices, 2 slices (prepared in advance, can use store-bought or homemade)
🍃 Pandan leaves, a small handful (for juicing)
🥥 Coconut milk, 100g
🥚 Sterile eggs, 1 whole egg + 1.5 egg yolks
🧈 Butter, as needed (for filling)
🍬 White sugar, 55g (can be halved to taste)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🫒 Vegetable oil, a little (for frying toast)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🌀 Blender or immersion blender
🧊 Strainer
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Whisk
🥘 Double boiler (large pot + small bowl)
🍳 Frying pan
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Spatula or spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make pandan juice**
   Cut the pandan leaves into small pieces, add a small amount of water, blend until fine with a blender, then strain through a strainer to extract the juice, yielding about 30g of pandan juice.

2. **Prepare kaya egg mixture**
   In a mixing bowl, combine pandan juice, coconut milk, whole egg, egg yolks, and white sugar. Whisk until evenly blended.

3. **Cook into a jam using a double boiler**
   Place the mixture in a double boiler over medium-low heat, stirring continuously while heating.
   **Key point**: Must stir constantly to prevent the egg mixture from curdling; add a pinch of salt for seasoning during heating.
   **Doneness check**: Stir until the sauce becomes thick and smooth, forming a spreadable paste, then remove from heat.
   The prepared kaya jam can be bottled and stored in the refrigerator.

4. **Fry the toast slices**
   Add a little vegetable oil to a frying pan and fry both sides of the toast slices over low heat until lightly golden and the surface is crispy.
   **Doneness check**: The edges of the toast are fragrant and slightly browned, while the center remains soft.

5. **Assemble kaya toast**
   Cut the fried toast slices in half. Spread a thick layer of pandan kaya jam on the cut side of each half, then place a small slice of butter on top. Cover with the other toast half, press gently, and cut diagonally to serve.
   **Note**: The amount of butter can be adjusted according to preference; best enjoyed hot.

## Health Advice
The combination of pandan leaves and coconut milk offers a unique Southeast Asian flavor, but the sugar content is high. It is recommended to halve the sugar and pair with unsweetened beverages. Suitable as breakfast or afternoon tea, but be mindful of portion control to avoid excess calories.
