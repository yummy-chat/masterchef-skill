# Sauce-Grey (Fermented Tofu in Chili & Sichuan Pepper Oil)

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Old (firm) tofu 1 block (firm texture, suitable for fermentation)  
🍄 Fermentation starter culture (mold spores) appropriate amount (use in winter to promote fermentation)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 2 tablespoons  
🌶️ Coarse chili powder 1 tablespoon  
🫑 Sichuan pepper powder 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt 2 tablespoons (for seasoning and preservation)  
🌿 Five-spice powder 1 tablespoon  
🍶 High-proof grain liquor appropriate amount (for sterilization and fragrance)  
🫒 Fully cooled cooked rapeseed oil sufficient quantity (must cover the tofu completely)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🍃 Bamboo steamer (with leaf lining at bottom)  
🫙 Glass airtight fermentation jar  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Pot  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the old tofu into evenly sized cubes and arrange them in a bamboo steamer lined with leaves, leaving some space between each cube.  
2. Mix fine chili powder, coarse chili powder, Sichuan pepper powder, salt, and five-spice powder together to create a dry spice blend. Prepare the high-proof grain liquor and fully cooled cooked rapeseed oil.  
3. Evenly sprinkle the fermentation starter culture over all surfaces of the tofu, ensuring every side is coated. In winter, the starter helps promote fermentation and increases success rate.  
4. Cover the steamer and place it in a warm environment for 2–5 days, until the tofu surfaces are covered with thick white fluffy mold, indicating fermentation is complete.  
5. Using chopsticks, pick up a piece of fermented tofu, first dip it into the high-proof grain liquor for a moment, then roll it thoroughly in the dry spice blend. The liquor sterilizes and adds fragrance; every side must be evenly coated with dry spices.  
6. Gently place the coated tofu pieces into the glass airtight jar, arranging them neatly without pressing or deforming them.  
7. Pour the fully cooled cooked rapeseed oil into the jar until it completely submerges the tofu pieces. Close the lid and seal the rim with water to block air contact.  
8. Store the jar in a cool place for half a month. Once the flavors have matured and the fermented tofu has developed its character, it is ready to eat.  

## Health Advice  
Fermented tofu is a product of high-quality protein fermentation, rich in probiotics, and beneficial for digestion. However, it has a high salt content, so people with hypertension should consume it in moderation. Eating it in winter can warm the stomach and stimulate appetite, but be careful not to overconsume, as excessive sodium intake may cause water retention.
