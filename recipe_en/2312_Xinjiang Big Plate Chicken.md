# Xinjiang Big Plate Chicken
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken, half (about 1000g, washed and thoroughly dried with paper towels)
🥔 Potatoes, 3 (cut into rolling chunks)
🧅 Onion, 1/3 (cut into rolling chunks)
🌶️ Spiral green chili peppers, 2 (cut into rolling chunks)
🌶️ Red chili pepper, 1 (cut into rolling chunks)
🧄 Garlic, 4 cloves (crushed) + half a whole head (added before serving)
🫚 Ginger, 3 slices
🧅 Scallion, 2 sections

🍬 White sugar, 40g (for caramelizing)
💧 Water, about 20ml (for caramelizing, half the volume of sugar)
🌟 Star anise, 2 pieces
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, about a dozen
🍂 Bay leaves, 4 pieces
🪵 Cinnamon stick, 1 small piece (about thumbnail size, do not overuse)
🪷 Angelica dahurica, 2 small slices
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (cut into large sections, preferably Erjingtiao variety)
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste), one heaping tablespoon
🥫 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons
🥫 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, 1/3 teaspoon
🍬 White sugar, 1 level teaspoon (to enhance freshness)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 teaspoon

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🧻 Paper towels
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the chicken repeatedly until the water runs clear, then thoroughly pat dry the surface with paper towels. Cut the potatoes, onion, and green and red chili peppers into rolling chunks. Slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, crush 4 garlic cloves, and set aside another half head of whole garlic cloves. Prepare the star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, angelica dahurica, and dried chili sections.
2. Place 40g white sugar and two tablespoons of water into the wok. Over medium heat, stir in one direction until the syrup transitions from large bubbles to dense, fine bubbles and the color deepens, then immediately reduce to the lowest heat.
**Note**: Do not let the heat become too low, to prevent the caramel from crystallizing.

3. As soon as the caramel produces large bubbles, add the thoroughly dried chicken and immediately turn to high heat, stir-frying until every piece is coated with caramel and the surface develops a browned edge, the chicken’s own fat is rendered, and the liquid at the bottom of the wok has evaporated.
**Key**: This step requires patience to stir-fry until the chicken is browned and aromatic; it is crucial for the signature flavor of big plate chicken.

4. Add the scallion, ginger, crushed garlic, and 1/3 of the onion chunks. Reduce to medium heat and stir-fry until fragrant. Once the onion softens, add all the dried spices and dried chili sections, and continue stir-frying until aromatic.
5. As soon as you smell the spices, immediately reduce to the lowest heat, add one heaping tablespoon of Pixian doubanjiang, and stir-fry until the red oil releases and the aroma is evident.
**Key**: Must use the lowest heat to prevent the doubanjiang from burning and to remove its raw taste.

6. Drizzle 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce and 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok, slightly increase the heat, and stir-fry to release the fermented aroma of the soy sauce.
7. Add boiling water. Season with 1/3 teaspoon salt, 1 level teaspoon white sugar, and 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon. Add half of the green chili chunks. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover the lid and reduce to a heat slightly above the lowest setting, simmering for 30 minutes.
8. After 30 minutes, add the potato chunks and stir to combine. Cover and reduce to the lowest heat, continuing to simmer for another 20 minutes.
9. Remove any visible spices. Turn to high heat to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, add the remaining green and red chili peppers, onion, and half a head of whole garlic cloves. Stir well and simmer for another minute. Reserve some sauce for mixing with rice or noodles.

## Health Advice
This big plate chicken is rich in protein, but the caramel and doubanjiang make the sodium and sugar content relatively high. It is recommended to pair with whole grain noodles or multi-grain rice, reducing excessive carbohydrate intake from directly mixing with the sauce. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure, but individuals with high blood pressure or those managing sugar intake should consume less of the sauce.
