# Homemade Christmas Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 4 (separate egg yolks and whites)  
🌾 Cake flour 240 grams  
🥛 Whole milk 240 milliliters  
🍬 Granulated sugar 85 grams (40 grams for egg yolks, 30 grams for egg whites, 15 grams for whipped cream)  
🧈 Whipping cream 200 grams  
🍓 Strawberries, as needed  
🫐 Blueberries, as needed  
⚫ Black sesame seeds, a small amount  
❄️ Powdered sugar, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowls (2)  
🥄 Hand whisk  
⚡ Electric mixer  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🍳 Lid (matching the frying pan)  
🥄 Silicone spatula / wooden spatula  
🔘 Round mousse ring / cake mold  
💼 Piping bag  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Cutting board  
🥄 Measuring cup / measuring spoons  
📌 Toothpicks  

## Instructions  
1. Separate the egg whites from the egg yolks. Place the egg whites in a clean, large bowl that is free of water and oil.  
2. Make the egg yolk batter: Add 40 grams of granulated sugar to the egg yolks and mix evenly with a hand whisk. Sift in the cake flour and whole milk, and stir with a zigzag motion until a smooth, thick batter forms.  
3. Whip the meringue: Add the granulated sugar to the egg whites in three portions. After adding the first portion, use an electric mixer on high speed until large bubbles appear. After the second addition, continue whipping on high speed until the large bubbles disappear and fine bubbles form. After the third addition, switch to low speed and whip until the meringue forms stiff peaks.  
4. Add the whipped meringue to the egg yolk batter in three portions, folding gently with a spatula until well combined, resulting in a smooth and thick cake batter.  
5. Without adding oil to the frying pan, preheat it over low heat until warm. Ladle an appropriate amount of batter into the center of the pan, allowing it to spread naturally into a circle. Cover with the lid and cook over low heat until the bottom is set and the surface is firm with many air holes. Flip and cook for about 3 more minutes until the other side is set. Remove and let cool. The batter can make 3-4 crepes.  
6. Add 15 grams of granulated sugar to the whipping cream. Beat with an electric mixer on medium speed until stiff peaks form. Transfer to a piping bag and set aside.  
7. Trim one crepe into a round shape using a round mold. Pipe a spiral layer of cream on top, then cover with a second trimmed crepe and pipe another layer of cream. Repeat until all crepes are used. Smooth the final layer of cream with a spatula.  
8. Attach blueberries around the sides of the cake as decoration. Remove the stems and tips from the strawberries. Pipe a small amount of cream on the tip of each strawberry and place the tip back on to form a Santa Claus shape. Use black sesame seeds for the eyes, and pipe cream for the buttons. Halve the remaining strawberries and arrange them on top of the cake along with the Santa-shaped strawberries and blueberries.  
9. Sift powdered sugar over the entire surface of the cake to resemble snow, then serve.  

## Health Advice  
This cake uses a large amount of cream and sugar, making it suitable for occasional enjoyment during the holidays. However, it is high in sugar and fat, so it is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea and to cut small portions each time. Strawberries and blueberries are rich in vitamins, adding some nutritional value to this dessert.
