# Seafood Mao Cai
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef, appropriate amount (sliced and marinated)  
🫕 Beef tripe, appropriate amount  
🦪 Mussels, appropriate amount (boil for broth, reserve the meat)  
🐚 Scallops, appropriate amount  
🦑 Squid, appropriate amount  
🐣 Goose intestines, appropriate amount  
🩸 Duck blood tofu, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🥦 Cauliflower, appropriate amount  
🥔 Potatoes, appropriate amount  
🪷 Lotus root, appropriate amount  
🥬 Lettuce, appropriate amount  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, appropriate amount  
🫘 Tofu skin, appropriate amount  
🧅 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (for hot oil drizzle)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (for garnish)  

🧂 Broad bean paste, 1 tablespoon  
🌶️ Dried chilies, 1 small handful (part for stir-frying base, part for topping with hot oil)  
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns, 1 small handful  
🍲 Hot pot base, half a packet  
🍬 Rock sugar, 2 pieces  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for marinating beef)  
🫗 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating beef)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (for blanching vegetables, stir-frying base, and boiling)  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Strainer  
🫗 Fine mesh strainer  
🥣 Large bowl or stone pot (for serving)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  

## Steps
1. Place mussels and scallops in a pot, add water, bring to a boil, and cook until the broth turns milky white, then turn off the heat.  
**Key**: Remove the shellfish meat, reserve the broth as stock, and trim the byssus from the mussels.

2. Slice the beef into thin pieces, add cornstarch, light soy sauce, and a pinch of salt, mix well, and marinate.
3. Cut the cauliflower, potatoes, lotus root, lettuce, wood ear mushrooms, and tofu skin into appropriate sizes; cut the duck blood tofu into pieces; wash and prepare the remaining seafood and meats.
4. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add salt, a little oil, and chicken powder, then blanch all the vegetables until cooked.  
**Key**: Remove the vegetables and spread them on the bottom of a large bowl as the base.

5. Heat oil in another wok over medium heat, add broad bean paste, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chilies, and hot pot base. Stir-fry until red oil releases and the aroma is rich.
6. Pour in the reserved seafood broth from step 1, add two pieces of rock sugar, and simmer briefly to blend the flavors.
7. Use a fine mesh strainer to remove the solid residues from the broth, leaving only the bright red soup.
8. Bring a separate pot of water to a boil, quickly blanch the squid, beef tripe, goose intestines, marinated beef slices, and duck blood tofu until just cooked.  
**Key**: Seafood and meats cook quickly; keep the blanching time short to avoid toughness.

9. Place all the blanched seafood and meats into the red broth from step 7, and cook briefly until fully infused with flavor.
10. Pour the cooked ingredients along with the broth into the large bowl containing the vegetable base.
11. Evenly sprinkle dried chili segments and minced garlic over the top of the ingredients. Heat a ladle of oil until smoking and pour it over to release the aroma.
12. Finally, garnish with cilantro segments and scallions, and serve.

## Health Advice  
This seafood mao cai is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, offering balanced nutrition, but sodium intake should be monitored. Pair with rice to balance the spiciness, and reduce the amount of chilies and Sichuan peppercorns for those with sensitive stomachs.
