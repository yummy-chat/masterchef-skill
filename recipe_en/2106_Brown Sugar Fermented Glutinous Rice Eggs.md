# Brown Sugar Fermented Glutinous Rice Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs
🫘 6 red dates
🍒 A small handful of goji berries
🍚 400g fermented glutinous rice (jiuniang)
🍬 About 30g brown sugar (two pieces)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Small bowl
🥄 Spoon
🥄 Strainer
📱 Phone (timer)
🍽️ Plate
🍜 Bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Fill the wok with water, bring to a boil over high heat, then turn off the heat and let it sit for about 15 seconds until the water surface calms down.
   **Note**: Avoid the boiling water current from breaking the eggs.
2. Crack an egg into a small bowl, bring the bowl close to the water surface, and gently slide the egg into the hot water along the edge of the wok.
3. After all eggs are added, cover the wok with a lid and let them steep for about 5 minutes, until the egg whites are set and firm, and the yolks are not fully cooked.
   **Key point**: Use residual heat to set the egg whites, keeping the yolks runny.
4. Turn the heat back on, bring the water to a boil again, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 2 minutes until the poached eggs are fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
5. Add brown sugar to the water used for cooking the eggs, keep it boiling, and stir until fully dissolved.
6. Add the red dates and goji berries, continue cooking for about 3 minutes until fragrant.
7. Pour in the fermented glutinous rice, stir well, and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
8. Return the cooked poached eggs to the wok, cook together with the other ingredients for about 3 minutes, until the broth is rich and the flavors have melded.
9. Turn off the heat, serve, and enjoy while hot.

## Health Advice
This brown sugar fermented glutinous rice egg dish is rich in high-quality protein and iron, especially suitable for women with cold hands and feet in winter or those needing blood nourishment after menstruation. Fermented glutinous rice contains a small amount of alcohol and sugar; those with high blood sugar should control their intake. Eggs must be fully cooked to avoid the risk of Salmonella infection.
