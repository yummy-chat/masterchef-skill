# Oiled Noodles (Youpo Mian)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make and rest the dough**: Mix flour with 3g salt. Add 240g water in three batches, stirring into flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth dough. Cover and rest for 5 minutes, knead briefly until smooth, then cover and rest another 10 minutes.
**Key**: Use the water-absorption method. The dough develops gluten naturally after resting; no need for excessive kneading.
2. **Portion, oil, and rest**: Roll the dough into a long log, divide into 8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a strip. Grease the pan base, arrange the dough strips in the pan. Spray a layer of oil on the surface. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for at least 1 hour.
**Key**: The oil film enhances dough extensibility and prevents drying. Adequate resting is essential for stretching.
3. **Prepare the sauce base**: Add a little oil to a small pot, stir-fry minced ginger over low heat until fragrant. Pour in aged vinegar and light soy sauce. Once it boils, turn off the heat. Strain out the ginger. While hot, add salt, MSG, and chicken bouillon, stirring to dissolve. Set aside to cool.
4. **Prepare vegetables and spices**: Wash soybean sprouts and baby bok choy. Prepare cao guo peel, cassia bark, cumin, Sichuan peppercorns, and star anise.
5. **Stretch the noodles**: Take one dough portion, flatten it. Roll into a long oval sheet. Press a groove down the center with a finger. Hold the two ends of the sheet, gently stretch it longer. Slap the noodle against the cutting board while pulling to make it long and thin. Fold the noodle, then tear it apart along the pressed groove. This yields one wide noodle.
**Doneness check**: If it stretches easily without breaking, the resting is sufficient.
6. **Cook noodles and vegetables**: Bring a generous amount of water to a boil. Add the noodles. When the water first boils, add the soybean sprouts and pour in a little cold water. When it boils a second time, add the baby bok choy and pour in cold water again. When it boils a third time, drain everything, and transfer to a large bowl.
**Key**: Adding cold water three times controls the cooking degree and keeps the vegetables crisp and tender.
7. **Assemble and pour hot oil**: On the noodles, layer in order: scallion flowers, minced garlic, chili pepper flakes, fine chili powder (if using), and a generous amount of white sesame seeds. Place all the spices into cold oil in a pot. Fry over medium-low heat slowly until the spices darken and release fragrance. Strain out the spice solids. Turn the heat to high and heat the oil until it smokes. Immediately pour the hot oil over the seasoning pile on the noodles. Finally, drizzle with the pre-prepared cooled sauce.
**Note**: Pouring hot oil is the most critical step for releasing aroma. The oil must reach smoking temperature to instantly activate the chili and garlic fragrance.
8. **Mix**: Thoroughly toss and mix the noodles, vegetables, sauce, and oiled chili together. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This oiled noodle dish is high in carbohydrates and calories. It is recommended to pair it with a cucumber salad or a clear soup with vegetables to balance nutrition. It is suitable for people with high physical exertion, but those controlling sugar or losing weight should be cautious with a large portion.
