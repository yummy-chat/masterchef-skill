# Home-style Stir-fried Pork
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork, appropriate amount (lean meat, sliced into strips)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🧂 Soy sauce, a small amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Wooden spatula
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🥄 Ladle

## Instructions
1. Slice the pork into strips and set aside. Place the iron wok over high heat until it is scorching hot. Use an infrared thermometer to confirm the wok has reached high temperature, then add cooking oil to coat the wok.
2. Add the pork strips and stir-fry quickly. If the meat is too cold and sticks to the wok, immediately reduce the heat to low and let it sit without moving. Once the wok temperature is even, the meat will naturally release.
3. If the meat continues to stick during later stir-frying, reduce the heat to the lowest setting and add a small amount of water or soy sauce to quickly lower the temperature. The sticking will then disappear.
   - **Add water**: After the sticking disappears, continue heating to evaporate excess moisture, then stir-fry normally.
   - **Add soy sauce**: Turn the heat back to medium and let the soy sauce sizzle until fragrant, coating the pork strips. The meat will become glossy and reddish and will not stick.
4. Stir-fry the pork strips until fully cooked, then remove from the wok and plate. It can be served over rice as a rice bowl.

## Health Tips
This stir-fried pork dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those building muscle or losing weight. However, it should be paired with vegetables for balanced nutrition. If using refrigerated meat, allow it to come to room temperature before cooking. If sticking occurs, a small amount of soy sauce can be added as a remedy; avoid forcibly scraping the meat.
