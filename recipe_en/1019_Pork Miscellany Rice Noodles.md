# Pork Miscellany Rice Noodles
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork liver approximately ¥2 worth (thinly sliced)
🐖 Pork intestines approximately ¥2 worth (thoroughly cleaned, excess fat removed)
🥓 Lean pork approximately ¥3 worth (sliced)
🍃 Goji leaves approximately ¥1 worth (washed)
🍜 Dried rice noodles 1-2 servings (brand: Yao Mei Mei)
🧆 Pork meatballs as needed
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶️ White pepper to taste
🍶 Rice wine a small amount
🫒 Peanut oil as needed
🌾 Cornstarch as needed
🧄 Garlic 1 clove (for cleaning pork intestines)
🧅 Fried garlic granules one tablespoon (to sprinkle on noodles at the end)
🌶️ Chili sauce one small packet (add according to taste)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥘 Small pot (for cooking meat broth)
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for cooking rice noodles)
🥄 Slotted spoon/soup ladle
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🔪 Knife
🖇️ Tongs
🍜 Bowl (for serving the finished dish)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Take one clove of garlic, insert it inside the pork intestines and rinse back and forth to thoroughly remove odor and excess fat.
2.  Slice the pork liver thinly, slice the lean pork, place all meats in a basin, add cornstarch and peanut oil, and mix well to marinate.
3.  Wash the goji leaves, and at the same time bring water to a boil in two pots—one for cooking the meat broth, one for cooking the rice noodles.
4.  After the water in the meat broth pot has boiled, first add the meatballs and cook for a moment, then add the marinated pork liver, pork intestines, and lean pork slices, and cook until done.
- **Note**: Once the meat slices are cooked, proceed to the next step immediately to avoid overcooking.
5.  When the water in the other pot has come to a boil, add the dried rice noodles and cook until they become loose and soft. Remove and place into a bowl for later use.
6.  After the meat slices are cooked, add the goji leaves to the broth and blanch until just wilted. Then season with salt, white pepper, and a small amount of rice wine.
7.  Pour the cooked pork miscellany soup, along with the meat slices, meatballs, and goji leaves, over the rice noodles.
8.  Sprinkle fried garlic granules on top according to preference, and drizzle with chili sauce. Serve.

## Health Advice
This pork miscellany rice noodle dish is rich in iron, protein, and vitamin A, making it suitable for those needing to replenish blood and energy. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
