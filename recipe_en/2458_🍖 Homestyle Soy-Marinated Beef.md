# 🍖 Homestyle Soy-Marinated Beef
Cuisine: 🏮 Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: 🍂 All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank
🍃 Bay leaves
🫚 Ginger
🧅 Scallions
🌿 Cinnamon stick
🌸 Star anise
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns
🍯 Dark soy sauce
🍬 Rock sugar
🍶 Cooking wine
🧂 Light soy sauce
🧂 Salt
🫘 Fermented soybean paste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍴 Toothpicks or fork
📦 Plastic wrap
🍲 Clay pot or stew pot
❄️ Refrigerator
🔪 Meat knife

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the beef shank clean and soak it in water for 1 hour to remove blood.
2. Use toothpicks or a fork to poke holes evenly on the surface of the beef shank. Add cooking wine, Sichuan peppercorns, and ginger slices, mix well, seal the container with plastic wrap, and marinate for 4-6 hours.
3. Cut the marinated beef shank into pieces about 8cm, no more than 10cm.
4. Place the beef pieces in a pot, add cold water until fully submerged, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, cook for 3 minutes, then remove the beef and rinse with warm water.
5. Place the rinsed beef pieces into a clay pot or stew pot, add water to submerge the beef, and bring to a boil over high heat. Add all seasonings except salt.
6. Once the water boils, reduce to low heat and cook for 90 minutes, then add salt.
7. Continue cooking over low heat for another 90 minutes after adding salt. Check the water level every 30 minutes, ensuring it remains at least 80% of the beef's height.
8. After 180 minutes, remove the beef, wrap it tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for several hours.
9. Remove and slice against the grain. Serve immediately; any unsliced beef should be sealed and refrigerated for storage.

## Health Tips
Homestyle soy-marinated beef is rich in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for muscle building and fat loss. However, it is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should limit consumption. Slicing after refrigeration makes it easier to cut and gives it a firmer texture.
