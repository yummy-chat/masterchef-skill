# Dry Pot Chicken

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Difficulty Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Rooster, approximately 1 whole (cleaned, skinned, and cut into small pieces)
🥔 Potatoes, to taste (cut into thick slices)
🪷 Lotus root, to taste (cut into thick slices)
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers, to taste (cut into chunks)
🧅 Onion, to taste (cut into chunks)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (smashed and cut into chunks)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (cut into chunks)
🫑 Scallions, to taste (cut into rings)
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (for garnish)
🧂 Cornstarch, to taste (for coating)

🧂 Salt, to taste (for marinating)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste (for marinating)
🧂 Ground white pepper, to taste (for marinating)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (for marinating)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for marinating)
🧂 Cooking wine, to taste (for marinating)
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste), to taste (for stir-frying base)
🥫 Secret spicy sauce, to taste (for stir-frying base)
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (for stir-frying base)
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (for stir-frying base)
🍂 Thirteen-spice powder, to taste (for mixing)
🪴 Cumin powder, to taste (for mixing)
🌾 White sesame seeds, to taste (sprinkle before finishing)
🫒 Sichuan peppercorn oil, to taste (drizzle before finishing)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔥 Large iron wok (for deep-frying and stir-frying)
🥢 Spotted ladle (for retrieving ingredients)
🔪 Chef's knife (for cutting)
🪓 Cutting board (for cutting)
🥄 Wok spatula (for stir-frying and seasoning)
🪣 Large basin (for marinating chicken)
📦 Metal square tray (for serving)
🧺 Bamboo basket (for holding vegetables before frying)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Rinse the chicken pieces and place them in a basin. Add salt, chicken bouillon, ground white pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and cooking wine. Mix thoroughly and marinate until flavorful.
    **Key point**: Mix in one direction with your hands to help the chicken absorb the seasonings.
2.  After marinating, add cornstarch and mix again to coat evenly, sealing in moisture for a more tender texture.
    **Key point**: The cornstarch should fully coat the chicken pieces to prevent moisture loss during frying.
3.  Slice the ginger, garlic, scallions, onion, and green and red bell peppers. Cut the lotus root and potatoes into slightly thick slices.
4.  Heat enough oil in the wok to 70% hot. Deep-fry the chicken pieces until the surface turns golden and dry-fragrant.
    **Key point**: Extend the frying time for a drier, crispier texture; shorten it for a more tender result.
5.  Remove the chicken pieces and drain the oil. Using the remaining oil in the wok, fry the lotus root and potato slices for about 2 minutes until just cooked through, but not too soft. Remove and set aside.
6.  Heat fresh oil in another wok. Stir-fry the scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add the doubanjiang, secret spicy sauce, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chilies. Stir-fry until the oil turns red and aromatic.
7.  Add the fried chicken pieces and toss to combine. Sprinkle in the thirteen-spice powder, cumin powder, and white sesame seeds. Stir-fry until a complex fragrance develops.
8.  Add the fried lotus root and potato slices, along with the green and red bell peppers and onion chunks. Stir-fry over high heat until evenly mixed.
9.  Just before finishing, drizzle in the Sichuan peppercorn oil and quickly toss to combine. Turn off the heat.
10. Plate the dish, garnish with cilantro, and serve immediately while hot.

## Health Advice
This Dry Pot Chicken is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for consumption after periods of high physical exertion. However, due to its high oil content from deep-frying and stir-frying, its calorie count is relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. For those on a weight loss diet or with high blood lipid levels, consider reducing the deep-frying step or substituting some steps with oven baking.
