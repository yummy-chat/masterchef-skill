# Cold Mixed Fish Scale Jelly
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Preparation Steps  
1. Use a knife to scrape off the grass carp scales and collect them completely.  
   **Note**: The scales are the main ingredient; ensure there are no residues.  
2. Place the scales in a basin, add water, and repeatedly rub them to remove surface mucus and impurities.  
   **Note**: Change the water multiple times while washing until the scales are no longer sticky and the water is clear.  
3. Remove the cleaned scales and put them into a pot. Add 600 ml of water, a few slices of ginger, a few pieces of scallion, and half a spoon of salt.  
   **Key point**: Ginger slices remove the fishy smell, scallion segments enhance the flavor.  
4. Bring the water in the pot to a boil over high heat.  
5. After the water boils, turn to low heat and continue boiling for 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Slow boiling fully releases the collagen from the fish scales.  
6. After boiling, filter the liquid in the pot into a container using a strainer, removing all fish scales and auxiliary residue.  
7. Let the filtered liquid cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or place it in a low-temperature environment until fully solidified.  
   **Key point**: Must be completely cooled before refrigerating to ensure it solidifies into a solid form.  
8. Unmold the solidified fish scale jelly by inverting it onto a cutting board, and cut it into small cubes or other shapes.  
   **State check**: The solidified fish scale jelly should be jelly-like, springy and not crumbling.  
9. In a small bowl, add minced garlic, then sequentially add light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, and chili oil. Stir evenly to make the sauce.  
   **Key point**: Adjust the proportion of seasonings according to personal taste; omit chili oil if not eating spicy.  
10. Pour the prepared sauce over the cut fish scale jelly and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This cold mixed fish scale jelly is rich in collagen, suitable for those concerned with skin elasticity. Grass carp scales may contain trace amounts of heavy metals, so it is recommended to consume occasionally, not excessively. It must be thoroughly cleaned and fully boiled. Those with weak stomachs should eat sparingly to avoid indigestion.
