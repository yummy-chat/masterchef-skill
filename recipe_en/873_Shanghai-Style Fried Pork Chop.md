# Shanghai-Style Fried Pork Chop
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin chops 4 pieces
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount
🌾 Flour appropriate amount
🥚 Eggs appropriate amount
🍞 Breadcrumbs appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🫗 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar a pinch
🌶️ White pepper a small amount
🍶 Cooking wine a small amount
🍾 Shanghai spicy soy sauce (Worcestershire sauce) for dipping

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks
🔥 Gas stove
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Use the blade or back of a knife to pound the pork loin chops until flat and loose, tenderizing the meat for better flavor absorption and even cooking.
2. Finely chop scallions and ginger, add water, and squeeze vigorously with your hands to make scallion-ginger water. Set aside.
3. Season the pork chops with salt, light soy sauce, sugar, white pepper, and cooking wine. Add the scallion-ginger water in small batches, stirring vigorously in one direction until the pork chops fully absorb the liquid and no water remains at the bottom of the bowl. Marinate for 20 minutes to ensure the chops are juicy and not dry.
4. Coat the marinated pork chops in flour, egg wash, and breadcrumbs in that order, shaking off excess after each layer. Press firmly when applying breadcrumbs to ensure they adhere well. Let rest for 5 minutes before frying.
5. Heat oil in a pan. Test the oil temperature by dipping chopsticks in egg wash and dropping it into the oil; small bubbles should form quickly. Add the pork chops and reduce to medium-low heat. Fry each side for about 2–3 minutes until golden brown. To check doneness, poke the thickest part with chopsticks; if it goes through easily, the chop is done. Use enough oil for shallow frying.
6. For a crispier texture, increase the oil temperature and refry the chops over high heat for about 20 seconds until the surface is golden and crunchy. Remove and drain excess oil.
7. Slice and serve with Shanghai spicy soy sauce.

## Health Advice
Pounding the pork chops before marinating helps lock in the scallion-ginger water, keeping the meat juicy and tender—suitable for those needing a high-protein diet. However, since frying is high in calories, pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition.
