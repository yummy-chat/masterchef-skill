# Three Non-Stickies (San Bu Zhan)
Cuisine: Henan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 6 eggs (yolks only, about 240g)  
🍬 70g white sugar  
🧂 1g salt  
🌾 30g starch (potato starch or tapioca starch)  
💧 120g water  
🫒 About 30g cooking oil (used in 3 portions, 10g each)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing egg yolk batter)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing starch water)  
🕸️ Strainer (for straining egg batter)  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🍽️ Plate (for serving)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Separate the egg yolks and place them in a large bowl.  
2. Add the sugar and salt to the egg yolks, and stir with chopsticks until the sugar is completely dissolved.  
**Key Point**: Salt can enhance the layering of sweetness.  

3. In a small bowl, mix the starch and water until smooth and free of lumps.  
4. Pour the starch water into the egg yolk mixture and stir thoroughly.  
5. Strain the mixed egg batter through a strainer to remove any impurities, making the texture finer.  
6. Add 10g of cooking oil to the wok, heat over the lowest heat, and shake the wok or use a spatula to evenly coat the wok walls with oil.  
**Key Point**: The oil should be just slightly warm; the purpose is to prevent sticking.  

7. Pour the strained egg batter into the wok and immediately begin stirring continuously.  
8. Keep stirring and mixing with the silicone spatula; the batter will gradually thicken from a liquid state.  
**Key Point**: Maintain the lowest heat throughout; the action must be fast and continuous to prevent burning.  

9. Continue stirring until the batter forms a cohesive lump with no remaining liquid.  
10. Add the second 10g of cooking oil, and by pressing and stirring, ensure the oil is fully absorbed into the dough.  
11. Add the third 10g of cooking oil, and stir and press again until the oil is completely absorbed.  
12. Continue stirring and pressing until the dough becomes extremely smooth, glossy, and completely non-stick to both the wok and the spatula.  
**Key Point**: The final state should be a smooth and elastic lump.  

13. Remove from the wok and place onto a plate; use the spatula to pat and shape it into a round form.  

## Health Advice  
The mixture of egg yolk and starch has a high glycemic index, so diabetics should control their consumption. The continuous stirring throughout the process helps exercise upper body strength.
