# Imperial Court Beef Shank in Chicken Broth
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank (about 1.5 kg)
🍲 Chicken broth (enough to submerge the beef)
🧅 Scallions, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🌿 Cumin (fennel seeds), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Star anise, 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Chinese angelica (baizhi), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Cloves, 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Cassia cinnamon (guipi), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Kaempferia galanga (shannai), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 White cardamom (baikou), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🌿 Amomum villosum (sharen), 3 qian (approx. 15g)
🧂 Salt, 1 liang (approx. 50g)
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed
🧴 Soy sauce, as needed
🧴 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍶 Yellow wine (Huangjiu), as needed
🌶️ White pepper, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Oil-free iron wok
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥄 Ladle
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🎽 Cloth spice bag
🥢 Pointed tool (such as chopsticks or an awl)

## Preparation Steps

### Prep Stage
1. **Prepare the "flash-cure" spice mix**: Place cumin, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns into an oil-free wok. Dry-fry over low heat until fragrant. Remove from the wok, lightly crush with a rolling pin, then mix thoroughly with the salt.
- Keep on low heat throughout to avoid burning; crushing after dry-frying enhances flavor.

2. **Prepare the beef**: Wash the beef shank. Use a pointed tool to poke several holes into the meat, and make a few deep cuts with a knife.
- Poking holes and making cuts help break the connective tissue, allowing better flavor penetration during marinating and braising.

3. **"Flash-cure" the beef**: Evenly rub and massage the salt-spice mixture over every part of the beef. Place in a basin and marinate for 1 hour.
- The amount of salt should be sufficient; marinate for at least 1 hour – this is key for quick flavor infusion.

4. **Rinse the cured beef**: After marinating, wash the beef thoroughly under running water to remove surface spices and blood.
- Rinsing is essential; otherwise, the braising liquid may become overly salty and cloudy.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Prepare the "oil-water caramel"**: Add a small amount of oil and water to a wok, then add the rock sugar. Cook slowly over low heat, stirring constantly with a ladle, watching the sugar mixture change. When the bubbles shift from large to dense small bubbles, and the color turns to a dark soy sauce shade with blue smoke rising, quickly add a generous amount of scallions and ginger and stir-fry. Immediately pour in warm water to create the caramel water.
- Keep on low heat throughout; the process from adding sugar to completion takes about 2-3 minutes.
   - The sugar turning a deep amber and emitting blue smoke is the critical point.
   - The oil-water method is easier to control; timing for adding aromatics and water is crucial to prevent bitterness or oil splatter.

2. **Blanch the beef to remove gameyness**: Place the rinsed beef in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Skim off any foam. Periodically add a small amount of cold water, repeating several times, keeping the broth at a "barely simmering" state. Continue until no more large amounts of foam appear, then remove the beef and rinse clean.
- Blanch over medium-low heat; time depends on how much blood is released from the beef.
   - The surface foam will change from abundant to sparse and turn white; the broth will clear.
   - Start with cold water; adding cold water during blanching uses temperature differences to force out more impurities; avoid high heat to prevent the meat from becoming tough.

3. **Pressure cook the beef**: Place the blanched beef into the pressure cooker. Pour in enough chicken broth to fully submerge the meat. Add the cloth spice bag containing the six spices, scallion and ginger pieces, light soy sauce, soy sauce, yellow wine, and white pepper. Finally, add the prepared "scallion-ginger caramel" water.
- The chicken broth is the soul for enhancing umami and aroma; the caramel water is mainly for color.

4. **Pressure cooking and resting**: Close the pressure cooker lid. Once steam is released and the weight begins to rock, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 40 minutes. Turn off the heat but do not open the lid. Let the beef rest in the residual heat for another 1 hour.
- Cook for 40 minutes after pressure is reached, then rest for 1 hour with the heat off.
   - The combination of "pressure cooking" + "resting" ensures the beef is tender and fully infused with flavor without becoming overly shredded.

5. **Finishing**: Open the lid. The beef is done when a chopstick can easily pierce through. Remove the beef and the broth together. Let cool, then serve or refrigerate for later use.
- Allowing the beef to cool while soaking in the broth enhances flavor penetration.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. Beef shank is rich in iron, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing to boost iron levels. However, its sodium content is relatively high (from salt, soy sauce, and light soy sauce), so individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables such as cold cucumber salad or lightly stir-fried seasonal greens to balance nutrition.
