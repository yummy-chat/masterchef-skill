# Soft and Juicy Thin-Skinned Soup Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 300g all-purpose or low-gluten flour
🍄 3g dry yeast
🍬 3g white sugar
💧 230g warm water (about 35℃)
🥩 300g pork belly with alternating fat and lean meat
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped)
🫚 2 slices ginger (shredded)
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed
🦪 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
🍯 2 tablespoons sweet bean sauce
🧴 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
🧴 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
🌿 1/2 teaspoon thirteen spices
🧂 1 teaspoon MSG

## Tools and Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Meat grinder
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🪣 Large stainless steel basin
🫙 Plastic wrap
♨️ Steamer with steaming basket
📄 Disposable steaming paper liners

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Phase**
1. **Make the Dough**: Mix 300g flour, 3g yeast, and 3g sugar evenly in a bowl. Pour in 230g warm water and stir with chopsticks into a very sticky dough. Moisten the back of your hand with a little water to smooth the surface of the dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
   **Key Point**: The dough has a high water content; it should appear honeycombed after rising.
2. **Prepare the Meat Filling**: Cut the pork belly into chunks, place in a meat grinder along with scallions and shredded ginger, and grind until the filling retains some texture. Shredded ginger is easier to grind.
3. **Cook the Meat Sauce**: Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a wok and heat until smoking. Add **1/3** of the raw meat filling and stir-fry over low heat until it changes color. Reduce to low heat, then add oyster sauce, sweet bean sauce, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and thirteen spices in order. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Once the sauce forms dense small bubbles, turn off the heat and let it cool until no longer hot to the touch.
   **Key Point**: Frying a portion of the meat filling is crucial for the filling’s aroma; maintain low heat throughout to prevent burning.
4. **Combine the Filling**: Pour the cooled sauce meat filling into the remaining **2/3** raw meat filling, add MSG, and mix well. If desired, pour off excess floating oil, retaining the lard aroma. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate. Let the filling firm up while the dough rises, making it easier to wrap later.

**Making Phase**
1. **Degas the Dough and Prevent Sticking**: The risen dough should appear honeycombed. Stir with chopsticks to degas. Sprinkle a generous amount of flour on your hands and the dough surface, coating the dough evenly with dry flour.
   **Note**: The dough is extremely sticky; use sufficient dry flour for handling.
2. **Shape the Soup Dumplings**: Remove the chilled, firm filling from the refrigerator and mix in fresh scallions. Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion in dry flour, press flat by hand, wrap in filling, and pleat into a dumpling shape.
   **Note**: The filling is easier to wrap when chilled; the dough skin must be fully coated in flour to prevent sticking.
3. **Second Proofing and Steaming**: Place the shaped soup dumplings on steaming paper liners in a steamer basket. Cover and let them proof a second time for about 10–15 minutes. Bring water to a rolling boil in a wok. Once steam rises, place the steamer basket over it and steam over high heat for 12 minutes. Turn off the heat and let sit covered for 3 minutes before opening the lid.
   **Note**: The second proofing makes the dumplings fluffier; always let them rest after steaming to prevent shrinkage from the temperature drop.

## Health Advice
The filling of these soup dumplings combines fatty and lean pork, providing a high protein and fat content. They are suitable for those needing energy replenishment, but individuals with cardiovascular disease or those on a weight-loss diet should consume in moderation. Control the amount of oil used when frying the meat sauce; mixing raw and cooked meat helps reduce the overall greasiness.
