## Instructions
1. Prepare the ingredients: Mince the chicken breast into irregular, shredded-like pieces—do not cut into uniform dices. Finely chop the scallions, mince the ginger, and mince the garlic. Set aside the crushed cooked peanuts and cooked sesame seeds.
2. Prepare the seasoned chili powder: In a bowl, mix the coarse chili powder with salt, matsutake seasoning, and white sugar, stirring until well combined. Be careful with the amount of salt, as the fermented black soybeans are already salty.
3. Heat the oil: Heat the wok and pour in the rapeseed oil. Keep the flame size smaller than the oil surface area in the wok to prevent the oil temperature from getting out of control.
4. Fry the aromatics in cold oil: Add the chopped scallions, minced ginger, minced garlic, and minced chicken breast to the cold oil. Immediately stir with a spatula to break up the chicken and prevent it from clumping. The oil temperature should be around 100–110°C at this stage, which is the moisture evaporation phase. Stir continuously to prevent sticking, and don't worry about burning.
5. Add the fermented black soybeans: When the scallions, ginger, and garlic float to the surface, add the dried fermented black soybeans. Continue frying until all the aromatics turn golden brown. The closer they get to golden brown, the stronger the aroma. However, be careful not to burn them—stop when they are close to brown.
6. Add the chili powder: Pour the seasoned chili powder into the wok. At this point, the oil temperature should be around 110°C. The temperature will drop when the chili is added, so there is no need to worry about burning. Stir continuously and fry the chili powder until it turns amber, a state that looks almost burnt but isn't—this is when the aroma is at its peak.
7. Turn off the heat and season: Once the chili powder reaches an amber color, immediately turn off the heat. Use the residual heat to add the ground Sichuan pepper and stir well to release its fragrance. Then, add the crushed cooked peanuts and cooked sesame seeds, stirring to combine. By now, the oil temperature should have dropped below 100°C, and cooking has completely stopped.
8. Store in a jar: Let the finished product cool completely, then transfer it to a glass jar with an airtight lid. Store in a cool, dark place. The flavor will improve after sitting overnight. With proper storage, it can last for about a year.

## Health Advice
This soybean chili oil uses lean chicken breast instead of traditional high-fat minced meat, reducing saturated fat. However, the rapeseed oil and crushed peanuts are still high in calories, so consumption should be moderated. It is rich in high-quality protein and nutrients from the fermented soybeans, making it a good choice for boosting immunity in spring. However, it is not suitable for those on a weight loss diet or with sensitive digestive systems if consumed in large amounts.
