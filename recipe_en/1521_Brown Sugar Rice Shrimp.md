# Brown Sugar Rice Shrimp  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Rice flour 50g  
🌽 Corn flour 30g  
🫘 Pea starch 150g  
💧 Water (for slurry) 300ml  
💧 Water (for cooking slurry) 1200ml  
🧊 Ice water (for cooling and setting) as needed  
🟤 Brown sugar 120g  
🍬 Rock sugar 60g  
🍋 Lemon half (juice extracted)  
🥜 Crushed peanuts (optional garnish) as needed  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for preparing slurry)  
🍲 Cooking pot (for boiling water, cooking slurry, and making syrup)  
🥢 Rolling pin or stirring stick (for quick stirring to prevent sticking)  
🧇 Slotted spoon with holes (for shaping)  
🥄 Stainless steel soup ladle (for scooping)  
🧊 Glass storage container (for refrigerating brown sugar syrup)  
🍶 Serving bowl (for eating)  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Stage  
1. Mix rice flour, corn flour, and pea starch in a glass bowl.  
2. Add 300ml water and stir until completely smooth with no lumps. Set aside.  
**Note**: Stir again before use to prevent settling.  
3. Prepare a basin of ice water for subsequent cooling and setting.  
4. Chop the block brown sugar into small pieces and set aside.  

### Cooking the Rice Shrimp Slurry  
1. Add 1200ml water to a pot and bring to a gentle boil over high heat.  
2. Stir the prepared slurry again, then slowly pour it into the gently boiling water.  
3. Immediately and continuously stir quickly with a rolling pin or a firm stirring stick to prevent sticking and lumping.  
**Key Point**: The consistency is crucial for the final texture; the slurry must be cooked until translucent for a chewy, bouncy result.  
4. Keep boiling over high heat. The batter will thicken in less than 2 minutes. Continue stirring for about 1 minute until the batter becomes translucent and smooth, and drops in sheets when lifted with the stick.  
5. Place the slotted spoon over the ice water basin. Immediately scoop the cooked batter onto it. The batter will fall through the holes, rapidly cooling and solidifying in the ice water, forming “rice shrimp” shapes—thick at one end, thin at the other.  
**Note**: Work quickly, as the batter will become difficult to shape once cooled.  
6. Let the shaped rice shrimp soak in the ice water for a moment until fully set and cooled. Then, drain with a slotted spoon and set aside.  

### Making the Brown Sugar Syrup  
1. Add the chopped brown sugar, rock sugar, and 200ml water to a clean pot. Bring to a boil over high heat until the sugars are fully dissolved.  
2. Squeeze in the juice of half a lemon.  
3. Reduce to medium heat and continue boiling for 2–3 minutes, until the syrup is covered with dense small bubbles and slightly thickened.  
4. Turn off the heat. Pour the syrup into a glass container and refrigerate.  
**Note**: The syrup will thicken more after chilling, enhancing the flavor.  

### Assembling the Final Dish  
1. Place an appropriate amount of drained rice shrimp into a bowl.  
2. Take the chilled brown sugar syrup out of the refrigerator and drizzle about half a ladle over the rice shrimp.  
3. Optionally, sprinkle crushed peanuts on top for added aroma.  
4. Stir well and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Brown sugar rice shrimp, made from rice flour and starch, has a chewy, refreshing texture. Paired with brown sugar syrup and lemon juice, it is an excellent summer cooling treat. Suitable for those with normal digestive function, but due to its high sugar content, diabetics should consume with caution. It is recommended to eat after meals to avoid irritating the stomach on an empty stomach.
