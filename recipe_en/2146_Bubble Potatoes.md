# Bubble Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 medium-sized potato (recommended to slice vertically into 3-4 mm thick slices)  
🧂 5g salt  
🧪 3g baking soda  
🌽 Corn starch, as needed (for coating the surface)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Steps
1. Peel the potato and slice it vertically along the long axis into pieces about 3–4 mm thick.  
   **Key point**: Must be sliced vertically; horizontal slicing will not achieve good bubbling results.  
2. Place the potato slices in a bowl, add 5g salt and 3g baking soda, mix thoroughly by hand, and marinate for 10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Baking soda is the core ingredient that causes the potato to puff up when fried; marinate until the slices soften, can be bent naturally, and noticeably release water.  
3. Rinse the marinated potato slices several times with cold water, and drain off surface moisture thoroughly.  
   **Key point**: Wash off excess salt and baking soda to avoid being too salty or having residual off-flavors.  
4. Evenly coat the surface of the potato slices with a thin layer of corn starch; ensure it is light and uniform.  
   **Key point**: Coating with starch helps make the final product crispier.  
5. Pour enough cooking oil into the pan and heat to 30% of its maximum temperature (approximately 120°C / 250°F); add the potato slices.  
   **Status check**: After the potato slices are added to the oil, dense small bubbles form around them, and they gradually float up.  
6. Maintain medium-low heat. After the slices float, constantly turn them over with chopsticks to ensure even heating.  
   **Key point**: Repeated turning is key to ensuring uniform temperature and even puffing.  
   **Status check**: The potato slices gradually expand into bubbles and turn golden.  
7. Once all potato slices are fully puffed up and golden on the surface, remove them with a slotted spoon and drain the oil.  
   **Status check**: The final product is golden and crispy, with a hollow bubble structure inside.

## Health Advice  
This dish uses baking soda to puff the potato slices into bubbles, resulting in a crispy texture. However, the sodium content may be slightly high, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Suitable as a snack in small quantities; those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should control their portions.
