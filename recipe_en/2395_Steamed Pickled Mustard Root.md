# Steamed Pickled Mustard Root
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Pickled mustard root (approx. 1300 g)  
🧅 Scallions (approx. 40 g, use both green and white parts, slice diagonally)  
🧄 Garlic (approx. 20 g, about 5 cloves, smashed)  
🫚 Ginger (approx. 15 g, sliced)  
⭐ Star anise (2 pieces)  
🌿 Cassia bark (approx. 1 g, about the length of a thumb joint)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (to taste)  
🍬 White sugar (approx. 25 g, 2.5 level tablespoons)  
🫗 Light soy sauce (approx. 100 g, 10 tablespoons)  
🫗 Dark soy sauce (approx. 60 g, 6 tablespoons)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon (approx. 3 g, 1 teaspoon)  
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, use a generous amount; insufficient oil reduces flavor)  

## Tools & Equipment
🧇 Grater/Shredder  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/large bowl  
🧺 Cloth bag or cheesecloth  
🍳 Wok/Cooking pan  
🥘 Steamer/Steaming basket  
🔥 Stove/Gas range  

## Instructions
1. Use a grater to shred the pickled mustard root into thin strips. Rinse several times with warm water.
2. Place the shredded mustard root in a bowl, add enough warm water to submerge, and soak for 20 minutes to remove saltiness and odor.
3. After soaking, discard the water and repeatedly rinse with warm water, rubbing the shreds, until they no longer taste overly salty.
4. Transfer the rinsed shreds to a cloth bag and squeeze out as much water as possible. **The drier they are, the more fragrant the final dish will be.**
5. Pour a bit more cooking oil into the wok than you would for stir-frying. Once the oil is warm, add the scallion slices, garlic, ginger slices, star anise, cassia bark, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over medium heat.
**Note**: Do not use too much cassia bark, or the flavor will become overpowering.

6. Continue stir-frying until the scallions and ginger begin to develop slightly charred edges. Add the squeezed-dry mustard shreds, quickly break them apart, and stir-fry for about 1 minute to evenly mix the shreds with the aromatics.
7. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add the white sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and chicken bouillon in order.
**Note**: Reducing the heat prevents the seasonings from burning quickly.

8. Immediately after adding the seasonings, turn the heat back to high and stir-fry rapidly to bring out the fermented aroma of the soy sauce. Cook until the color is even and the raw soy sauce smell is gone.
9. Transfer the stir-fried mustard shreds to a plate, cover it with another inverted plate, and place it in the steamer.
10. Bring the water in the steamer to a boil over high heat. Once steam appears, reduce the heat to low and steam for 30 minutes. Turn off the heat and remove.

## Health Advice
This steamed pickled mustard root is rich and savory, ideal with porridge or rice. However, it is high in sodium. Those with high blood pressure or edema should pair it with light vegetables and be mindful of reducing other salt intake.
