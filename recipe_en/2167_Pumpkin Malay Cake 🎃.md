# Pumpkin Malay Cake 🎃
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 Raw pumpkin, about 350g (peeled and cut into chunks; after steaming, yields about 650g pumpkin puree)
🌾 All-purpose flour, 300g
🍠 Tapioca starch, 200g
🫘 Red dates,适量 (chopped, for decoration)
🍬 White sugar, 2-3 tablespoons
🍞 High-sugar-tolerant yeast, 5g
🧁 Baking powder, 3-5g (or baking soda)
🫒 Cooking oil, 15-20ml (to be added to the batter) + additional for greasing the mold and brushing the surface

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer
🥢 Mixing bowl, chopsticks
🔪 Spatula
🥘 Round flat mold
🖌️ Brush (or gloves)
🛡️ Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. **Peel and cut the raw pumpkin into chunks, then steam for 15-20 minutes until fully cooked and soft.**
   **Key point**: Steam until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
2. **Place the steamed pumpkin chunks, white sugar, and an appropriate amount of water into a food processor and blend into a smooth, lump-free pumpkin puree.**
3. **Let the pumpkin puree cool to lukewarm, then add the high-sugar-tolerant yeast and stir until fully dissolved.**
   **Note**: If the temperature is too high, it will kill the yeast; in winter, ensure the yeast is completely dissolved.
4. **Add the all-purpose flour and tapioca starch, and stir with chopsticks into a thick batter with no dry flour or lumps.**
   **Note**: The batter should not be too thin; its thickness determines the final shaping of the cake.
5. **Cover with plastic wrap and perform the first fermentation until the batter volume expands to about double its original size and the interior shows abundant air holes.**
6. **Add the baking powder and 15-20ml of cooking oil to the fermented batter, and stir quickly and thoroughly with chopsticks to release the air.**
   **Note**: Releasing the air must be thorough, as it directly affects the fineness of the final cake's texture.
7. **Evenly grease the inner surface of the mold with cooking oil, pour in the batter, smooth the surface with a spatula, then lift the mold and gently tap it a few times to eliminate large air bubbles.**
8. **Place the chopped red dates on the surface of the batter, and perform a second fermentation until the batter expands to 80-90% full in the mold.**
   **Doneness test**: Gently touch the batter with a finger; it should feel fluffy and slowly spring back.
9. **After the water in the steamer has come to a boil, place the mold inside, cover with a lid, and steam over medium heat for 30-40 minutes.**
   **Note**: Adjust the time based on the thickness of the cake; you can check by inserting a toothpick—it is done when it comes out clean.
10. **After removing from the steamer, brush the surface with a layer of cooking oil while still hot. Let it cool slightly, then run a spatula along the edges of the mold, turn it upside down, and unmold.**

## Health Advice
Pumpkin is rich in β-carotene. Combined with tapioca starch, it gives the Malay cake a soft, chewy, and springy texture, making it suitable for those needing vitamin A supplementation. However, due to its high sugar and starch content, it is advisable for those on a diet or controlling sugar intake to consume it in moderation.
