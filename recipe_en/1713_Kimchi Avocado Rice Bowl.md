# Kimchi Avocado Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice, as needed
🧅 Onion, about 50g, minced
🧄 Garlic, 3 cloves, smashed and minced (remove any sprouts)
🥕 Carrot, half, peeled and thinly sliced
🥬 Korean kimchi, about 100g, chopped
🥩 Ground beef, about 150g (can substitute with ground pork or small chicken pieces)
🥚 Eggs, 3-4
🥑 Avocado, 1, ripe
🍋 Lemon, half (1/3 squeezed into the avocado, rest for garnish and seasoning)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🍾 Cooking wine, a dash
🥢 Light soy sauce, a dash
🧂 Salt, to taste
⚫ Black pepper, to taste
🌶️ Doubanjiang (fermented bean paste) or Gochujang (Korean chili paste), optional
🫘 Black sesame oil, optional

## Cookware and Equipment
🍚 Clay pot or rice cooker
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Frying pan (with lid)
🧂 Mortar and pestle (or bowl and mashing tool)
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions
1. Cook the rice in a clay pot or rice cooker, and keep warm until ready to use.
2. Prepare the vegetables and avocado: Mince the onion and garlic, peel and thinly slice the carrot, chop the kimchi; halve the avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a mortar.
3. Add cooking oil to a frying pan over medium-low heat. Sauté the minced onion and garlic until fragrant.
4. Cover with a lid, turn the heat to medium, and cook for 2-3 minutes until the onion is softened.
   **Note**: This step makes the onion texture softer and sweeter.
5. Add the chopped kimchi and stir-fry over medium heat until there is no excess moisture at the bottom of the pan.
6. Add the ground beef and stir-fry over medium heat until fully cooked and no longer pink. There is no need to brown it excessively.
7. Add the sliced carrot, one cup of water, a dash of cooking wine, and a dash of light soy sauce. You may optionally add doubanjiang, Gochujang, or black sesame oil for extra flavor. Bring to a boil.
8. Reduce the heat to medium, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
9. While the sauce is simmering, squeeze about 1/3 of the lemon juice into the avocado flesh. Add salt and black pepper to taste. Mash with a pestle, leaving some chunky pieces.
   **Note**: The lemon juice prevents the avocado from oxidizing and adds a refreshing tangy flavor.
10. Gently crack 3-4 eggs into the sauce. Use a spoon to carefully move the egg whites around to help them cook evenly, being careful not to break the yolks. Cover and simmer over medium heat for 3 minutes, until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
11. Place a serving of rice in a bowl. Top with the kimchi and beef sauce, including the soft-cooked eggs. Add a large spoonful of avocado mash on top.
12. Garnish with a slice of lemon. For those who prefer more acidity, squeeze fresh lemon juice over the dish before mixing and eating.

## Health Advice
The kimchi avocado rice bowl is rich in healthy fats and protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing an energy boost. However, be mindful of the avocado's high calorie content and consume in moderation. The runny egg yolks and avocado mash create a silky texture; it is recommended to pair this dish with a light side to balance overall nutrition.
