# Onion and Egg Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧅 Purple onion 1 (cut into pieces)  
🥚 Eggs 4  
🫑 Green bell pepper appropriate amount (cut into pieces, for color)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper appropriate amount (cut into pieces, for color)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🧂 Fine salt 3 grams  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance freshness)  
🌿 Ground white pepper appropriate amount  
🍶 White vinegar a few drops (add to the beaten eggs to remove the eggy smell)  
🌽 Cornstarch slurry 2 small spoonfuls (makes the pancake smoother and more tender)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for stir-frying and pan-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel small bowl  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍽️ Large plate (assists in flipping)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  

## Steps  
1.  Cut the purple onion into pieces.  
2.  Heat the wok over the fire until hot, ensuring the bottom of the wok is free of oil and water. Add the onion pieces and stir-fry over the lowest heat until the surface shows a slightly charred, tiger-skin pattern.  
    **Note**: Use the lowest heat throughout to avoid burning; this step reduces the onion's pungency, making it easier to absorb flavors.  
3.  Remove the stir-fried onion with a slotted spoon and set aside.  
4.  Crack the eggs into a small bowl, add a few drops of white vinegar, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg liquid is smooth and well combined.  
    **Note**: White vinegar removes the eggy smell and increases the fluffiness of the egg liquid.  
5.  Place the stir-fried onion in a large bowl, add 3 grams of fine salt, a pinch of sugar, appropriate amount of ground white pepper, green and red bell pepper pieces, the beaten egg liquid, and 2 small spoonfuls of cornstarch slurry. Stir evenly with chopsticks.  
6.  Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat until warm. Add the chopped scallion and minced garlic, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
7.  Pour the mixed onion and egg batter into the wok. Occasionally use a spoon to push the egg liquid that has set at the edges toward the center, allowing the uncooked egg liquid to flow to the bottom of the wok.  
    **Note**: Keep the heat at the lowest setting throughout. Pushing the egg liquid ensures even heating, prevents sticking, and ensures the inside is fully cooked.  
8.  Once the bottom is mostly set, use a spoon to shape the ingredients into a round pancake.  
9.  When the bottom side of the pancake is set, place a large plate upside down over the pancake. Press the plate firmly, quickly flip the wok, and invert the pancake completely onto the plate. Then slide it back into the wok to continue cooking the other side.  
    **Key**: Using a plate to assist the flip ensures the pancake stays whole and does not break.  
10. Once the other side is set and golden brown and fully cooked, use a spoon to cut the pancake into small pieces directly in the wok, then remove and plate.

## Health Tips  
Onion and egg pancake is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for breakfast or a light meal. It is recommended to fry with less oil and serve with multigrain staples. Using the lowest heat throughout prevents sticking, and the addition of green and red bell peppers boosts vitamin C intake.
