# Stir-Fried Chicken Slices with Black Bean, Green Pepper, and Mushroom
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken breast 200g  
🫑 Green pepper appropriate amount (sliced)  
🍄 Button mushrooms appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried red chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫘 Fermented black beans (douchi) appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt 1/8 tsp (for marinating) + 1/8 tsp (for sauce mixture)  
⚪ White pepper powder 1/8 tsp  
🥢 Light soy sauce 2 tsp  
💧 Water 1 tbsp (for marinating) + 1/4 cup (for sauce mixture)  
🌽 Cornstarch 1/2 tsp (for marinating) + 1/2 tsp (for sauce mixture)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for oil-sealing marinade) + 1 tbsp (for velveting) + 1 tbsp (for stir-frying)  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar 1/4 tsp  
🤎 Dark soy sauce 1/8 tsp  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🌀 Microwave (for partial thawing)  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the chicken breast: Trim the fresh chicken breast of any sinew. Slice it thinly along the grain, then cut into strips. If using frozen meat, partially thaw it in the microwave until firm before cutting.
   **Key point**: Always cut along the grain of the chicken to ensure the finished dish is tender and not dry.

2. Marinate the chicken strips: Add salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, and water to the chicken strips. Toss until the liquid is fully absorbed. Add cornstarch and mix well to coat. Finally, drizzle in cooking oil and gently mix to seal. Refrigerate until ready to use.

3. Prepare the vegetables and sauce mixture: Slice the green pepper and button mushrooms, slice the garlic, and cut the dried red chili peppers into sections. In a small bowl, combine salt, dark soy sauce, cornstarch, and water to create the sauce mixture.

4. Velvet the chicken: Heat the wok over high heat, then add cooking oil. Reduce to medium heat and when the oil reaches 40–50% hot, add the chicken strips. Quickly separate them with chopsticks and stir-fry until the surface just changes color. Drizzle fragrant vinegar along the side of the wok, stir-fry evenly, then immediately remove from the wok.
   **Key point**: Do not cook the chicken fully; rely on residual heat from the subsequent stir-fry to finish cooking, preventing the meat from becoming tough.

5. Aromatics and stir-fry the vegetables: Clean and reheat the wok over high heat, then add cooking oil. Over medium heat, briefly stir-fry the garlic slices and dried red chili peppers until fragrant. Add the fermented black beans and stir-fry until aromatic. Then add the sliced green pepper and mushrooms. Stir-fry over high heat for about 1–2 minutes until just cooked but still crisp.

6. Combine and thicken: Return the velvetted chicken strips to the wok. Stir-fry quickly to combine. Re-stir the sauce mixture, then pour it in along the side of the wok. Stir-fry over high heat until the sauce thickens and evenly coats all ingredients. Remove from heat and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Chicken breast is low in fat and high in protein. Paired with fermented black beans, green pepper, and mushrooms, it enhances flavor while providing Vitamin C, making it suitable for those aiming for weight loss or blood sugar control. The marinating and velveting techniques keep the chicken strips tender and not dry. However, fermented black beans are high in salt, so reduce the amount of salt used during seasoning.
