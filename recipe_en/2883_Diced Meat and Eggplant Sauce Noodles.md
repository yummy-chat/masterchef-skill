# Diced Meat and Eggplant Sauce Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April - August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 Round eggplant 300g (cut into fingernail-sized cubes with skin on)  
🥩 Pork belly 100g (diced)  
🍜 Noodles appropriate amount (cooked in advance)  
🌿 Star anise a little  
🧅 Scallion and ginger appropriate amount (minced)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced, added at the end)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 30g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 20g  
🧂 Salt 2g (for seasoning, prepare a little more to soak eggplant cubes)  
🍬 Sugar 5g  
🌶️ White pepper 1g  
💧 Water 200ml  
🌾 Water starch appropriate amount (for a thin slurry)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking eggplant cubes and holding sauce and noodles)  
🥢 Slotted spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Remove the stem of the round eggplant, keep the skin, and cut into fingernail-sized cubes.  
   **Note**: Round eggplant must be used; its flesh is tender, and cutting with the skin on retains a crisp texture.  
2. Add water to a bowl with a little salt, soak the eggplant cubes for 2 minutes, then remove and drain well.  
   **Key**: This step prevents the eggplant from oxidizing and turning black, and reduces oil absorption during cooking.  
3. Add a base oil to the wok, add star anise, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
   **Key**: Low heat fully releases the aroma of star anise, establishing the base flavor for the sauce.  
4. Add the diced pork belly and stir-fry until fully cooked.  
5. Add minced scallion and ginger, stir-fry until fragrant.  
   **Key**: Add scallion and ginger later to avoid burning them over high heat, while fully releasing their aroma.  
6. Pour in the drained eggplant cubes and stir-fry evenly.  
7. Add light soy sauce 30g, dark soy sauce 20g, salt 2g, sugar 5g, and white pepper 1g, stir-fry to evenly coat the ingredients with seasonings.  
8. Add 200ml water, bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to low heat, cover, and simmer for 5 minutes until the eggplant is tender and flavorful.  
9. Turn to high heat, pour in water starch to create a thin slurry, and reduce the sauce until thick enough to coat the ingredients.  
10. Turn off the heat, add minced garlic, and stir well.  
    **Key**: Add garlic at the very end to maximize its aroma, which is the flavor essence of this sauce.  
11. Ladle the sauce over the cooked noodles, mix well, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Eggplant is a vegetable that absorbs oil easily; pairing it with pork belly adds richness. Soaking in salted water reduces oil absorption, making it suitable for those with weaker digestion or who prefer a soft, tender texture. Pay attention to portion size to avoid excessive fat intake.
