# Crystal Pork Skin Aspic
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork skin 1.2 jin (cleaned)
💧 Water approximately 3.6 jin (3 times the weight of pork skin)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (in large pieces)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (in large pieces)
🌿 Bay leaves appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic 2 cloves (crushed)
🍶 Baijiu appropriate amount (for blanching and scrubbing)
🧂 Edible alkali appropriate amount (for scrubbing)
🧂 Salt 5 grams (corresponding to about 3 jin of water)
🥢 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (dipping sauce)
🍇 Vinegar appropriate amount (equal amount to light soy sauce)
🌶️ Chili oil appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch or pan bottom (for treating hair roots on pork skin)
🍲 Cooking pot (for blanching and boiling seasoned water)
🥄 Slotted spoon, tongs
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Large glass bowl (for scrubbing pork skin)
♨️ Steamer
🍶 Heat-resistant container (for setting)
🥏 Small dish (for preparing dipping sauce)

## Preparation Steps
1. If there are residual hair roots on the surface of the pork skin, use a blowtorch or a hot pan bottom to burn them off.
2. Place the pork skin in cold water, add an appropriate amount of baijiu, bring to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling for 2-3 minutes until the pork skin becomes semi-transparent. Remove and let cool slightly.
   **Key point**: Do not start timing from the moment the water boils; cook until the skin turns transparent to remove any off-flavors and excess fat.
3. Place the pork skin with the white fat layer facing up, and use a knife to carefully shave off the fat until only the transparent skin layer remains.
   **Key point**: Thoroughly removing the fat ensures the final product is not greasy and the broth is clear.
4. Cut the pork skin into shreds about the thickness of chopsticks.
5. Place the shredded pork skin in a large bowl, add edible alkali and baijiu, and knead repeatedly with your hands until it is no longer sticky.
6. Rinse thoroughly with clean water 2-3 times until the water runs clear with no oil slick, and the shredded skin is translucent.
7. In a pot, add 3.6 jin of water, along with scallions, ginger, bay leaves, and star anise. Simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes to release the aroma, then remove and discard the spices.
8. Add 5 grams of salt to the seasoned water, then pour in the shredded pork skin and stir well.
   **Key point**: Maintain a ratio of about 1:3 between pork skin and water. Add only a base amount of salt—do not make it too salty.
9. Ladle the mixture into a heat-resistant container, place it in a steamer that has been brought to steaming, cover with a lid or a plate, and steam over medium heat for 2 hours.
   **Key point**: Steaming is key to a clear broth; avoid vigorous boiling to prevent cloudiness.
10. After steaming, the liquid should be noticeably thick and sticky when dipped. While hot, pour the liquid into a clean mold, and gently stir with chopsticks to distribute the shredded pork skin evenly.
11. Allow to cool at room temperature, then transfer to the refrigerator and chill overnight to set.
12. To unmold, run a small knife along the edge of the mold, invert and remove, then cut into large slices.
13. Dipping sauce preparation: Mix minced garlic with light soy sauce, an equal amount of vinegar, and an appropriate amount of chili oil. Serve with the pork skin aspic for dipping.

## Health Advice
This Crystal Pork Skin Aspic is rich in collagen, making it ideal for nourishing the skin. However, people with high blood pressure should control the salt content in the dipping sauce. Steaming over low heat ensures a crystal-clear appearance, and chilling overnight gives the aspic a more Q-彈 (chewy) texture.
