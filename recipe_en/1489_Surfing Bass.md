# Surfing Bass
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice, half a bowl (for making crispy rice)
🐟 Bass, 1 piece (can substitute with mandarin fish, etc.)
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (finely chopped)
🧅 Scallions, to taste
🫚 Ginger, to taste
🧈 Lard, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌶 Pepper, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking rice)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🕸 Fine mesh strainer
🫖 Soup pot/ladle
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak rice in warm water for at least 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
2. Clean the bass, fillet both sides of the fish, and remove the bones. Slice the fish fillets into thin pieces and chop the bones into chunks.
3. Heat oil in a wok to medium temperature. Add the drained rice and fry over medium heat. The grains will initially stick together; continue frying until they separate, turn a light yellow color, and become crispy. Remove and spread on the bottom of a deep plate.
4. Heat the wok, add lard until melted. First, stir-fry ginger slices and scallion segments until fragrant, then add the fish bones. Pan-fry the bones over medium heat until golden brown on both sides, then crush them with a spatula. Pour in boiling water, turn to high heat, and let it roll for 2–3 minutes until the broth turns milky white and thick. Strain the broth through a fine mesh strainer, discard the bone residue, and season only with salt and pepper.
5. Arrange the raw fish slices evenly on the plate on top of the crispy rice, and sprinkle with chopped cilantro. Quickly pour the boiling hot fish broth into the plate—the high heat of the broth instantly "surfs" and cooks the fish slices.
6. Serve by scooping up the fish slices, crispy rice, and broth together for a smooth, tender, and crispy texture with rich layers.

## Health Advice
This surfing bass dish is rich in high-quality protein and DHA, making it suitable for those needing brain nourishment or muscle building. However, the crispy rice adds extra oil and carbohydrates, so those watching their weight should use less. Be sure to slice the fish thinly so it cooks instantly when the hot broth is poured over it, ensuring safety and tenderness.
