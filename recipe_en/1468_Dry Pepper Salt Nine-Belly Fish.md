# Dry Pepper Salt Nine-Belly Fish
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Nine-belly fish 500g  
🧂 White pepper powder 1.5g (for marinating)  
🧂 Salt 1.5g (for marinating)  
🍬 Sugar 1.5g (for marinating)  
🌾 Low-gluten flour 150g (for batter)  
🧂 Baking powder 7.5g (for batter)  
🌾 Glutinous rice flour 50g (for batter)  
🌾 Cornstarch 50g (for batter)  
🥚 Egg white 30g (for batter)  
💧 Water 200g (for batter)  
🧂 Salt 2.5g (for batter)  
🫒 Oil 50g (for batter)  
🌿 Five-spice powder 2.5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🌶️ Sichuan pepper powder 2.5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🌶️ Chili powder 2.5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🧂 White pepper powder 1.5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🧂 Chicken powder 5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🧂 Salt 10g (for dry pepper salt)  
🍬 Sugar 5g (for dry pepper salt)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🌶️ Green and red chili rings, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Small flat pan  
🍲 Large wok  
🥄 Strainer/skimmer  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧻 Kitchen paper  
⚖️ Measuring spoon/gram scale  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the nine-belly fish**: Cut off the sharp teeth at the head. Snip open at the neck, twist, and pull out the innards, discarding them. Cut the fish body into pieces about two fingers wide. For thicker parts, split them lengthwise. Pat dry with kitchen paper to remove surface moisture.  
**Note**: Keep the belly meat; the bones are edible and do not need to be discarded.  

2. **Marinate**: Place the prepared fish pieces in a bowl. Add 1.5g white pepper powder, 1.5g salt, and 1.5g sugar. Gently mix well and marinate for 10 minutes.  
**Key point**: Use dry seasonings for marinating to help achieve a crispier texture after frying.  

3. **Make the batter**: In a bowl, combine low-gluten flour, baking powder, glutinous rice flour, cornstarch, salt, egg white, water, and oil in the specified ratios. Stir until smooth with no lumps. Let it rest briefly until small bubbles form on the surface.  
**Texture check**: When scooped with a spoon, the batter should flow slowly in a continuous stream, with a consistency similar to Greek yogurt.  

4. **Prepare the dry pepper salt**: In a cold small flat pan, place five-spice powder, Sichuan pepper powder, chili powder, white pepper powder, and salt. Dry-roast over low heat for about 1 minute until fragrant. Turn off the heat, then use residual heat to add sugar and chicken powder, mixing well.  
**Note**: Use low heat or residual heat throughout to avoid burning the spices. The prepared pepper salt can be stored in a sealed container for later use.  

5. **First frying**: Heat oil in a wok to around 150°C. First coat each marinated fish piece in a thin layer of dry batter powder, then dip into the prepared batter for even coating. Gently lower them one by one into the oil. Maintain the oil temperature at 150°C and fry for about 2 minutes until the fish pieces float, the coating is set and light golden brown, and the flesh turns white and cooked. Remove from oil.  
**Key point**: Coating with dry powder before battering helps lock in moisture and increases crispiness. Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.  

6. **Second frying**: Raise the oil temperature to 160°C. Return all the fish pieces to the wok for a second fry of about 1 minute, until the coating deepens to a golden color and becomes noticeably crispy. Remove and drain oil.  
**Key point**: Second frying is essential for achieving a crispy exterior.  

7. **Final toss**: Prepare a clean wok with absolutely no oil. First, briefly dry-roast the green and red chili rings, then add all the fried fish pieces. Sprinkle with some dry pepper salt and scallions. Quickly toss the wok or gently mix until evenly coated. Serve immediately.  
**Note**: The wok must be clean and oil-free, or the pepper salt will clump. Toss quickly to avoid residual heat softening the coating. This dish loses some crispiness soon after cooking, so it is best eaten promptly.  

## Health Advice  
This dry pepper salt nine-belly fish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. The crispy fried bones are also edible, making it suitable for those who need calcium supplements and are wary of fish bones. Pay attention to controlling oil temperature and starch usage. For people with high blood lipids, a small taste is recommended.
