# Dry Pot Cauliflower
Cuisine: 🌶️Hunan Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: 🍂All-Season Dish

🧄Ingredients
- 🥬Cauliflower 400g
- 🥩Pork belly 100g
- 🌶️Chili peppers 1-2 pieces
- 🧄Garlic cloves 3-4 pieces

🧂Seasonings
- 🥄Light soy sauce 10ml
- 🍬White sugar 5g
- 🧂Salt 2g
- 🛢️Oil 10ml

🔪Utensils
- 🍳Wok
- 🔪Chef's knife
- 🥣Colander
- 🥢Spatula

📝Steps
1. Soak the cauliflower florets facing downward in lightly salted water for 20 minutes, then wash and break or cut them into small florets.
2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, blanch the cauliflower for 1 minute, remove and immediately rinse with cold water until completely cooled, then drain and set aside.
3. Slice the pork belly into thin pieces; smash the garlic cloves with the flat side of a knife; cut the small red chili peppers into sections; cut the garlic leaves into sections and set aside.
4. Pour oil into the wok, heat until hot, then add the smashed garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Add the sliced pork belly and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface changes color, then continue stir-frying until the fat part renders some oil.
6. Add the red chili sections and cauliflower, stir-fry a few times.
7. Add light soy sauce and white sugar, turn to high heat and stir-fry constantly for 1 minute.
8. Add the garlic leaf sections, stir-fry a few times, then turn off the heat. Cover the wok and let it sit for 1 minute, then serve.

## Health Advice
Rinsing the blanched cauliflower with cold water helps maintain a crisp and tender texture. Rendering the oil from the pork belly makes it more fragrant and less greasy. This dish is rich in dietary fiber and protein, suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss. However, note that pork belly is relatively high in calories, so portion control is advised.
