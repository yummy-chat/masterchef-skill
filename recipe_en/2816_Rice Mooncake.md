# Rice Mooncake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice Flour 80g (for the crust)
🍙 Glutinous Rice Flour 80g (for the crust)
🖤 Black Sesame Seeds 100g (roasted, for the filling)
🥜 Peanuts 100g (with skin, roasted and peeled, for the filling)
🍬 White Sugar 50g (for the filling)
🌽 Corn Starch 10g (added to the filling to increase adhesion)
🥛 Whole Milk 120ml (for kneading the dough)
🍯 Malt Syrup 45g (increases crust adhesion and soft texture)
🧈 Butter or Cooked Cooking Oil 40g (for kneading the dough)
💧 Water 100ml (for blending the filling, actual amount depends on blender capability)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying Pan
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥄 Wooden or Silicone Spatula
🌀 Blender/Food Processor
🍪 Square Mooncake Mold
🧤 Disposable Gloves
🫥 Plastic Wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. **Toast the crust flour**: Pour rice flour and glutinous rice flour into a frying pan. Stir-fry over low heat continuously until the flour turns slightly yellow and releases a rice fragrance. Remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent burning.

2. **Knead the dough**: Transfer the toasted flour to a bowl. Add butter, whole milk, and malt syrup. First, mix with a tool, then knead by hand into a smooth dough.
   **Note**: Malt syrup enhances adhesion and soft texture.
   **Rest the dough**: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest sealed at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

3. **Prepare the filling**: Roast black sesame seeds and peanuts with skin separately over low heat. Let the peanuts cool, then rub off the skins.

4. **Blend the filling**: Place the peeled peanuts, roasted black sesame seeds, white sugar, and corn starch into a blender. Add an appropriate amount of water and blend into a smooth paste.
   **Key point**: The water amount is not fixed; less water makes subsequent frying faster. If the blender struggles, pause and stir before continuing.

5. **Cook the filling**: Pour the filling paste into a frying pan. Stir-fry over low heat continuously until the moisture evaporates, the mixture forms a ball, and no longer sticks to the spatula.
   **Key point**: Stir continuously to prevent burning. Turn off the heat, remove, and let cool completely.

6. **Divide the filling**: Wear disposable gloves and divide the cooled filling into balls of about 15g each. You should get approximately 16 portions.

7. **Wrap and shape**:
   - Take about 20g of the rested dough, roll into a ball, flatten, and place a filling ball in the center.
   - Use the thumb and index finger to slowly push the dough upward to seal, pinching tightly, with the seam facing down.
   - Slightly shape the wrapped ball taller, place it into the square mooncake mold, press down vertically to set the shape, then demold.

## Health Advice
This rice mooncake has moderate calories. Black sesame seeds and peanuts are rich in high-quality fats and protein, making it suitable as a healthy snack. The baking-free process makes the crust softer and chewier. Be mindful of the sugar content to reduce the risk of blood sugar spikes.
