# Braised Beef Bones
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef bones about half a rack (cut along the bone joints into bite-sized pieces)
🧅 Scallions 3-4 stalks (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chilies to taste
🧂 Spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, star anise, cardamom, etc.) to taste
🍶 Cooking wine to taste
🍬 Rock sugar to taste
🫘 Broad bean paste about 1 tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce to taste
🥄 Dark soy sauce to taste
🌿 Ground white pepper a pinch
🧂 Salt a pinch
💧 Water enough to cover

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok (frying pan)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Cutting board, cleaver
🔥 Gas stove
🍽️ Metal tray

## Steps
1. Rinse the beef bones under running water, cut along the bone joints into pieces, then slice into strips, and finally chop into bite-sized pieces, ensuring each piece has some meat attached.
2. Place dried chilies and various spices in a bowl, add clean water and soak briefly to remove dust and prevent burning during later stir-frying.
3. Add enough clean water to the wok, put in the beef bones, start with cold water, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove the beef bones and set aside.
**Note**: Starting with cold water is essential for thorough deodorizing.

4. Heat oil in another wok, add rock sugar, stir-fry over medium-low heat until the sugar melts and turns a light caramel color.
5. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and the soaked spices, stir-fry until fragrant, then add broad bean paste, stir-fry until red oil forms and aroma develops.
**Key**: Always keep the heat at medium-low to prevent the sugar color and broad bean paste from burning.

6. Add enough clean water to the wok, put in the blanched beef bones, making sure the water covers the bones.
7. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, ground white pepper, and a pinch of salt to set the base flavor. Bring to a boil, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover the wok, and braise for 2 hours.
**Key**: The braising time must be sufficient to make the meat tender and flavorful.

8. Once the time is up, use a slotted spoon to remove the braised beef bones, plate them, and optionally drizzle a little of the original broth to enhance the flavor.

## Health Tips
This braised beef bones dish is rich in collagen and calcium, making it suitable for those needing to strengthen bones or replenish energy. However, the spicy and numbing broth is high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
