# Niangpi (Steamed Gluten Noodles)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g (minimum requirement is all-purpose flour; low-gluten flour breaks easily)
🧂 Salt 3g (added when kneading dough to increase firmness)
💧 Cold water about 800g (added in batches to prepare batter)
🥄 Edible alkali a pinch (dissolved in a small amount of warm water)
🧂 Salt to taste (for seasoning)
🧂 MSG to taste (enhances freshness)
🍶 Fragrant vinegar to taste (adds sourness)
🧄 Garlic water 1 tablespoon (adds garlic aroma)
🌿 Mustard paste 1 tablespoon (made by infusing mustard powder with hot water, then steeping and covering to develop pungent aroma)
🌶️ Chili oil to taste (drizzled at the end to add oil fragrance, spiciness, color)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl or mixing basin
☕ Small bowl
♨️ Steamer (with boiling water)
🥏 Round steaming tray (metal pan)
🖌️ Oil brush
🥄 Large ladle
🔪 Large knife
🪓 Cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Place all-purpose flour in a large bowl, add 3g salt, and mix well.
2. Add cold water in batches while stirring, first mixing into a thick paste, vigorously beating until smooth with no lumps, then add remaining water and dilute to an even, smooth consistency with appropriate thickness.
3. Dissolve edible alkali in a small amount of warm water, add to the batter and stir evenly. Let rest for 20 to 30 minutes to allow water and flour to fully combine.
4. Evenly brush the steaming tray with cooked vegetable oil, spoon in an appropriate amount of batter, and swirl the tray to spread the batter evenly across the bottom.
5. Place the steaming tray into the boiling water steamer. First scald briefly, lift and swirl again to ensure even thickness. Cover and steam over high heat for about 5 minutes, until large bubbles form on the surface of the niangpi, indicating it's done.
**Note**: Because niangpi is thicker, the steaming time needs to be slightly longer than for liangpi.

6. Remove the steaming tray and immediately place it in ice water to cool completely. Lift the nimble from the edge to completely unmold the whole sheet.
7. Place the niangpi on a cutting board and cut into wide strips with a large knife.
8. Put the cut niangpi into a bowl, add salt, MSG, fragrant vinegar, garlic water, and mustard paste in order, and finally drizzle with chili oil.
**Key Point**: Seasonings can be adjusted to personal taste; mustard paste and chili oil are crucial for flavor.

## Health Advice
Niangpi is made through steaming, avoiding frying, so it is relatively low in calories. Mustard and vinegar have appetite-stimulating and digestion-aiding effects, making it suitable for consumption when appetite is poor in summer. Adding alkali to the batter can enhance firmness and texture, but those with excessive stomach acid are advised to reduce the amount.
