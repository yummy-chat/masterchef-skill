# Chicken and Silky Egg Rice
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh, about 1 whole drumstick (whole chicken leg)  
🥚 3 pasteurized eggs  
🧅 About half a yellow onion  
🍚 Appropriate amount of rice  
🧂 2 tbsp soy sauce  
🧂 3 tbsp mirin  
🧂 1 tbsp sake  
🧂 6 tbsp water  
🍬 A small amount of white sugar  
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowl, chopsticks  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔗 Lid  
🍚 Rice bowl  

## Steps
1. Prepare the chicken thigh: Cut along the thigh bone to remove it, then cut the chicken thigh into bite-sized pieces.
2. Prepare the egg mixture: Crack the 3 pasteurized eggs into a bowl. Gently whisk with chopsticks, leaving some egg white not fully incorporated to create a silky, layered texture.
3. Prepare the vegetables and sauce: Slice the yellow onion into strips. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, mirin, sake, water, and white sugar until well combined.
4. Sear the chicken: Pour cooking oil into the frying pan and heat over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and sear until golden brown on the surface and fragrant.
   **Key point**: Searing the chicken first enhances the flavor.
5. Sauté the onion and simmer: Add the sliced onion and stir-fry until soft and translucent. Pour in the prepared sauce, cover with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the chicken to absorb the flavors.
6. Add the egg mixture: Evenly pour the egg mixture into the pan. First, cover with a lid and cook over low heat for 1 minute and 30 seconds, then remove the lid and continue cooking over low heat until the eggs are semi-set.
   **Key point**: Do not overcook the eggs; a semi-cooked state is the essence of "silky egg."
7. Serve: Ladle the cooked silky egg and chicken directly over the rice. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
This silky egg chicken rice features tender chicken enveloped in semi-cooked egg, providing high-quality protein with low oil. It is suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those seeking a light diet. It is recommended to use pasteurized eggs for safety, and the amount of rice can be reduced as needed to control calorie intake.
