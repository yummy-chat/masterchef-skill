# Bone Broth 🥣  
Cuisine: Others  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍖 Pork leg bones: 5 jin (fresh with meat)  
🥩 Beef bones: 1 jin (with meat)  
🐔 Chicken carcasses: 2 (cleaned, remove tail, beak shell, and fluff)  
💧 Water: 60-80 jin (for blanching and simmering)  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (for making light salt water to soak bones)  
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor): a small amount (added during blanching to remove odor)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🫕 Large soup pot  
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🕳️ Strainer spoon (skimmer)  
🍽️ Bottom rack (stainless steel spacer to prevent sticking)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🔪 Knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the pork bones, beef bones, and chicken carcasses in separate basins. Add enough light salt water to cover the ingredients and soak for 2 to 3 hours to initially remove blood.  
2. After cleaning the chicken carcasses, remove the tail and thoroughly clean the hard outer shell of the beak and the fluff on the head to avoid any fishy smell.  
3. Put the soaked pork bones, beef bones, and chicken carcasses into a large soup pot. Add enough cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, and blanch for 5 to 6 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Bones must be added to cold water; stir continuously during heating and add a little baijiu to help remove odor; do not turn off the heat immediately after boiling—let the froth fully rise to the surface.  
4. Use a strainer spoon to skim off the froth. Remove the bones and rinse them thoroughly under hot water to clean off any remaining foam, especially paying attention to the details of the chicken carcasses.  
5. In another pot, bring water to a boil. Place a bottom rack at the bottom of the pot, then add the cleaned bones to the boiling water. Simmer over high heat throughout the entire process.  
   **Note**: Bones must be added to boiling water to quickly lock in freshness; the bottom rack prevents the bones from sticking to the pot and producing a burnt taste.  
6. Do not cover the pot for the first 30 minutes of simmering; stir occasionally to allow any residual odor to fully dissipate. After about 30 minutes, when the broth turns milky white, cover the pot and switch to medium-high heat, continuing to simmer with the lid on for another 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Use high heat throughout to fully emulsify fats and collagen, forming a creamy white broth; the order of simmering uncovered first and then covered must not be reversed.  
7. After simmering is complete, switch to low heat to keep warm. The finished broth will be thick and milky white, with a rich and savory aroma, free of any fishy or off-flavors. It can be used for rice noodles, hot pot, clay pot dishes, noodles, and various other dishes.  

## Health Advice  
This broth is rich in collagen and calcium, making it suitable for post-surgery recovery or those needing calcium supplementation. However, due to its high fat content, consumption should be moderated.
