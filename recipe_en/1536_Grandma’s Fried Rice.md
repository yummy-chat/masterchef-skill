# Grandma’s Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Rice 750g (must be completely cooled)  
🥚 Eggs (appropriate amount, yolks and whites separated)  
💧 Water 1 tablespoon (household spoon)  
🥩 Luncheon meat (appropriate amount, diced)  
🥕 Carrot (appropriate amount, diced)  
🥬 Grandma’s preserved vegetables (appropriate amount, including pickled radish, potherb mustard, and leaf mustard)  
🥒 Cucumber (appropriate amount, diced, for color)  
🧅 Scallion (appropriate amount, chopped, added when finishing)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌿 White pepper (essential, do not omit)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥄 Large spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥣 Small bowl  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the cooled rice in a large bowl, add one tablespoon of water, and mix by hand until the rice grains are completely loose and separated.  
2. Add the egg yolks to the loosened rice, continue mixing by hand until each grain is evenly coated with yolk, turning a light yellow color.  
3. Place the egg whites in a separate small bowl, mix by hand to break their strands, and set aside.  
4. Heat oil in a wok, add the prepared egg whites, and stir-fry over medium-high heat until they solidify into small, translucent pieces.  
5. Pour the yolk-coated rice into the wok, keep the heat at medium-high, and stir-fry slowly with a large spoon, breaking up any clumps, until the rice grains are distinct and lightly bouncing in the wok.  
6. Place the diced luncheon meat, diced carrot, and grandma’s preserved vegetables in the center of the rice, stir-fry quickly to combine thoroughly, allowing the toppings to fully merge with the rice.  
7. Season with an appropriate amount of salt and white pepper, then add the diced cucumber, stir-fry evenly to maintain the cucumber’s crisp texture.  
8. Just before finishing, sprinkle in the chopped scallions, stir-fry vigorously over high heat a few times to release the aroma, then serve immediately on a plate.  

## Health Tips  
This grandma’s fried rice uses egg yolks to coat the rice and cooks the egg whites first, doubling the protein content and creating a rich texture. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost or using up leftover rice. Note that the pickled grandma’s preserved vegetables have a high salt content; people with high blood pressure should reduce any additional salt.
