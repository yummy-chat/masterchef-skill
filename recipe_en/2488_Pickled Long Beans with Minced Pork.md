# Pickled Long Beans with Minced Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Tender long beans 2 jin (must select tender beans for a crisp texture)
🥩 Minced pork, appropriate amount
🧂 Table salt (for making brine), appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Table salt (for quick pickling), 60 g (for 2 jin of beans)
🍶 High-proof liquor, 2-3 tbsp
🧄 Minced ginger and garlic, appropriate amount
🌶️ Chopped pickled chili, appropriate amount
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon, appropriate amount
🧅 Chopped scallions, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🌬️ Drying rack
🍲 Stockpot
🏺 Glass pickling jar with water seal
🫙 Airtight glass jar
🍳 Wok

## Instructions
### Preparing the Pickled Beans
1. Pick out any beans with insect damage, wash thoroughly, then spread on a drying rack to dry completely.  
**Note**: Both the beans and any containers they touch must be free of raw water, or they will spoil easily.
2. Make pickling brine: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add salt, stir to dissolve, boil for 5 minutes, then turn off the heat and let cool completely.  
**Note**: The brine must be completely cool before use.
3. Sterilize the container: Wash the glass jar, steam to sterilize, then dry thoroughly and set aside.  
**Traditional Whole Bean Method**
4. Coil the dried long beans along the inner wall of the pickling jar, pressing them in firmly.
5. Pour in the completely cooled brine, ensuring it covers all the beans.
6. Close the lid, fill the rim with clean water to seal, and store in a dark place for 28 days, until the beans turn yellow-green and sour.  
**Quick Chopped Method**
4. Cut the dried beans into small pieces, place in a large bowl, add chili powder, 60 g salt, and 2-3 tbsp high-proof liquor. Mix thoroughly with your hands.
5. Pack into a sterilized airtight jar, press down firmly, seal, and pickle for 7-15 days, until the beans become sour and their color darkens.
### Stir-frying the Pickled Beans with Minced Pork
6. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat, add the minced pork, and stir-fry until the moisture evaporates and the pork turns white, with clear oil at the bottom of the wok.
7. Add the minced ginger and garlic, chopped pickled chili, and doubanjiang. Continue stir-frying until red oil and fragrance release.
8. Add the chopped pickled beans and stir-fry over high heat until well combined.  
**Note**: The pickled beans are already salty, so do not add extra salt during stir-frying.
9. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in chicken bouillon and chopped scallions. Stir quickly to combine, then serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is tangy and spicy to stimulate the appetite, but pickled beans are high in salt. People with high blood pressure should consume in moderation and drink plenty of water with meals. Pickled beans are rich in probiotics, but strict sterilization of containers during the pickling process is essential to ensure hygiene.
