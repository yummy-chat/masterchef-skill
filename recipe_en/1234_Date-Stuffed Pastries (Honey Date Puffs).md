# Date-Stuffed Pastries (Honey Date Puffs)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Year Round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 175g
🌾 Cake flour 150g
🐖 Lard 135g (25g for the dough, 110g for the oil heart)
🍠 Beetroot juice about 80-90g (pure juice, no water added)
🫒 Date filling as needed
🌰 Pine nuts as needed
🌿 Chlorophyll a small amount (mixed with flour to form green dough)
🥚 Egg wash as needed (for brushing the pastry)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Small knife
📏 Ruler
🖌️ Pastry brush
🦯 Toothpick
🍳 Deep fryer
🥢 Slotted spoon
🌯 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. **Make the dough**: Mix all-purpose flour, 25g lard, and beetroot juice to form a smooth dough.
   **Note**: Beetroot juice must be pure juice without added water; after kneading, repeatedly slap the dough until it becomes elastic and smooth.
2. **Rest the dough**: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for about 2 hours to relax.
   **Note**: Sufficient resting time is necessary to eliminate gluten elasticity for easier handling.
3. **Make the oil heart**: Mix cake flour with 110g lard and knead into a uniform dough.
   **Note**: Only need to mix evenly; do not over-knead.
4. **Prepare filling and decoration**: Mix pine nuts into the date filling; combine chlorophyll with a small amount of flour to form green dough, roll into thin strips, and cut into small pieces to serve as date stems.

### Production Phase
1. **Laminate the pastry**: Roll the dough and oil heart into square sheets, place the oil heart in the center of the dough, wrap tightly, and pinch the edges to seal.
   **Note**: Expel all air when wrapping; if air bubbles appear, prick them with a toothpick.
2. **Fold and rest**: Roll the wrapped dough into a large rectangular sheet, trim uneven edges, fold into thirds, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 10-20 minutes. Repeat this step 2-3 times.
   **Note**: Ensure the dough is fully rested before each rolling, or the layers may break.
3. **Slice**: Cut the rested pastry block into thin slices about 1-2 mm thick using a sharp knife.
   **Note**: The knife must be sharp and clean to avoid damaging the layers; seal the slices with plastic wrap to prevent drying.
4. **Fill and shape**: Take a slice of pastry, brush the inner side with egg wash, place a cylindrical piece of date filling at one end, gently roll it up, and pinch the seam to seal; use your thumb and index finger to gather both ends and shape into a date-like form, then insert a piece of green dough as the date stem.
   **Note**: The egg wash acts as an adhesive; sealing must be tight to prevent opening during frying.
5. **Deep fry**: Heat oil in a pot to about 30% of its maximum temperature, add the shaped pastries, and fry over medium-low heat until the layers fully expand, the shell hardens, and the color turns slightly red.
   **Note**: Oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw; remove the finished pastries and drain oil.

## Health Advice
These date-stuffed pastries are rich in lard and sugar, so it is recommended to enjoy them occasionally as a festive dessert rather than in excess, to avoid consuming too much saturated fat and calories. The natural color provided by beetroot juice adds visual appeal but has limited nutritional value; pair with fresh fruit or light tea to balance the menu.
