# Date Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 6 room temperature eggs (about 60g each with shell)
🍬 80~100g brown sugar
🥮 220g date paste (must be brought to room temperature)
🌾 230~260g cake flour (sifted)
🌽 80g corn oil (mild flavor, room temperature)
🥜 About 30g walnut kernels (broken into pieces)
🍇 About 10g raisins
🔴 About 10g goji berries
🧂 About 5g toasted white sesame seeds
🍪 1~2 soda crackers (crushed)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Oven (adjustable to lowest temperature/proofing setting)
🌀 Electric hand mixer
🥣 Thick glass mixing bowl (good heat retention)
🥣 Metal mixing bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥄 Manual whisk
🍞 Square baking pan (about 23×23cm)
🧺 Cooling rack

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring the eggs, date paste, and corn oil to room temperature; sift the cake flour; preheat the oven to the lowest temperature.
2. Place the eggs, brown sugar, and date paste together in the thick glass bowl. Place the bowl, the beaters of the electric hand mixer, and a bowl of corn oil inside the oven and warm at low temperature for about 1 hour. Remove and stir 2~3 times during this period until the egg mixture feels warm to the touch.
   **Key**: Thoroughly warming the egg mixture is essential for successful whole egg whipping.
3. Evenly sprinkle the walnut pieces, raisins, goji berries, toasted white sesame seeds, and crushed soda crackers on the bottom of the baking pan. Set aside.
4. Remove the preheated glass bowl. Use the electric hand mixer at the highest speed to whip in a circular motion. After about 4 minutes, the egg mixture will expand, lighten in color, and show lines on the surface, but the bubbles will still be relatively coarse.
5. Reduce to medium speed and continue whipping for about 2 minutes until the egg batter is smooth and glossy. When lifting the beaters, the dripping batter should leave clear, persistent lines on the surface.
   **Key**: This stage is the ideal endpoint for whole egg whipping. Do not overwhip.
6. In a separate metal bowl, pour in the warm corn oil. Scoop two large spoonfuls of the whipped egg batter into the oil and quickly mix with a manual whisk until the oil and small amount of batter are fully emulsified.
7. Pour the emulsified batter back into the remaining larger portion of the whipped batter. Use a silicone spatula to fold until just combined.
   **Note**: Adding oil before flour significantly reduces the risk of deflating the batter.
8. Add the sifted cake flour all at once. Use the spatula to cut and fold the mixture until the flour is just incorporated and no dry flour is visible. The batter should be thick and glossy.
   **Note**: Do not overmix. Leaving a few spots of dry flour is preferable for a softer cake.
9. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan from a height. Gently tap the pan a few times to release large air bubbles. Use the spatula to help smooth the surface.
10. Place in the preheated oven's middle rack. Bake at 170℃ (top and bottom heat) for about 30 minutes, until the cake's surface stops rising, springs back when lightly pressed, and a rich date aroma is released.
11. Wearing heat-resistant gloves, immediately invert the baking pan onto a cooling rack to unmold and let the cake cool.
    **Key**: Unmold quickly while hot; otherwise, the bottom will absorb moisture and become sticky and wet.

## Health Tips
This date cake is rich in iron and dietary fiber, making it suitable for people with insufficient Qi and blood or prone to fatigue. However, since it contains a fair amount of brown sugar and oil, those watching their sugar intake or trying to lose weight are advised to cut the cake into small pieces and enjoy just one slice at a time.
