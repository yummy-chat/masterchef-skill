# Soy Milk Bun
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥖 240g all-purpose flour
🥛 144g soy milk (for dough, chilled) + 83g (for filling, chilled)
🥛 18g whipping cream (chilled)
🍞 7.5g fresh yeast (or 2.5g instant dry yeast suitable for high sugar)
🧈 24g unsalted butter (room temperature, softened)
🧀 30g cream cheese (softened)
🥚 1 egg yolk (about 17g)
🌾 Appropriate amount of cooked soybean flour (for coating)
🌽 10g cornstarch
🍬 28g granulated sugar (for dough) + 10g (for filling)
🧂 3g salt

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Stainless steel small milk pot
🥣 Hand whisk
🍴 Spatula
💉 Piping bag
🥖 Stand mixer (or hand kneading)
🌡️ Probe thermometer
⚖️ Digital scale
🔥 Oven (convection/standard)
🍞 Heavy baking sheet
🧺 Cooling rack

## Steps
1. **Make soy milk filling**
   Pour half of the soy milk into a small milk pot, add cornstarch and stir until no lumps remain. Then pour in the remaining soy milk, 10g granulated sugar, and the egg yolk, and stir until well combined. Heat over low heat, stirring continuously until thickened and lines appear. Remove from heat and immediately add the softened cream cheese, pressing and mixing with a spatula until smooth and free of lumps. Transfer to a piping bag and refrigerate until needed.
   **Note**: The heat must be kept very low, and stir constantly to prevent scorching; the cream cheese must be fully incorporated with no lumps remaining.

2. **Knead the dough**
   Place the chilled 144g soy milk, whipping cream, 28g granulated sugar, and yeast into the mixing bowl. Add the all-purpose flour and salt. First, knead on low speed until combined, then switch to high speed and knead until the dough reaches about 60% gluten development. Add the softened butter, first knead on low speed until the butter is fully absorbed, then switch to high speed and knead until a thin, stretchable membrane forms.
   **Key**: Make sure the final dough temperature is kept below 26°C; using all-purpose flour gives the bread a texture closer to a "bun," preventing collapse after baking.

3. **First fermentation**
   Shape the dough into a ball and let it ferment at room temperature for 30 minutes.

4. **Divide and pre-shape**
   Press the fermented dough to release gas, then divide it into 6 equal portions. Fold each portion twice, then roll into a ball, pinching the bottom tightly. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for about 25 minutes.

5. **Fill and coat**
   Take a rested piece of dough and roll it out into a round disc with a slightly thicker center and thinner edges. Flip it over. Pipe about 20g of soy milk filling into the center. Wrap the dough completely around the filling like wrapping a baozi, pinch the seam tightly, and gently roll into a ball. Evenly coat the surface of the dough ball with cooked soybean flour, then place it seam-side down onto the baking sheet.
   **Note**: The dough disc should be rolled out large enough to avoid the filling touching the seam, which would affect sealing.

6. **Final fermentation**
   Ferment at 38°C with 80% humidity for about 40 minutes, until the dough has visibly expanded but is not over-fermented.

7. **Bake**
   Place another heavy baking sheet on top of the fermented dough buns. Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack.
   - Convection oven: 160°C for 10 minutes
   - Standard oven: 180°C top and bottom heat for 15 minutes
   **Note**: Because the baking sheet is on top, you cannot directly observe browning. You can briefly pull out the sheet to check; remove from the oven once the desired color is achieved.

8. **Cool**
   Immediately remove from the oven after baking, transfer to a cooling rack, and let cool completely. The buns taste better after cooling.

## Health Advice
Soy milk buns use soy milk to replace part of the milk, reducing the digestive burden for those with lactose intolerance. Soybean flour provides plant protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for people who want to enjoy sweets while controlling sugar intake. For increased satiety, you can replace part of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour, and appropriately control the amount of filling to avoid excess calories.
