# Honey Peach Oolong Cake
Cuisine Category: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍑 Ripe peaches 2-3 (1 for making tea jelly with peel and diced fruit, 1 for approximately 200g fruit puree, 1 for slicing into thin pieces and dicing for decoration)
🍪 Instant noodle biscuits (or other biscuits) 60g (for the crust)
🍵 Oolong tea 100g
🍮 Gelatin sheets 16g (used in portions: 5g for tea jelly, 10g for fruit puree, 5g for surface glaze)
🥛 Whipping cream 240g (recommend lower milk fat content for a lighter texture)
🍬 White sugar 8g (for tea jelly) + 40g (for marinating peach flesh)
🍋 Lemon juice moderate amount (a few drops to brighten the tea jelly, 1 slice for marinating peach flesh)
🧂 Salt moderate amount (adds dimension to the crust)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪣 Bowls (multiple, for soaking gelatin, boiling tea jelly, marinating peaches)
♨️ Microwave
🔨 Crusher or rolling pin
🥄 Whisk or chopsticks
🥐 Electric mixer
🥛 Measuring cup (or bottle cap, for rough measurement)
🧊 Ice water basin (for cooling over water and keeping cream cold)
✂️ Scissors (for unmolding)
🥡 Instant noodle cup (as a mold)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the crust**: Crush 60g of biscuits into the bottom of the instant noodle cup. Add a moderate amount of salt and approximately 20g of melted butter, mix well. Press down firmly and evenly with the back of a spoon. Place in the freezer to set.
   **Key point**: The crust needs to be pressed down flat and evenly to ensure it doesn't crumble after setting.

2. **Make the honey peach oolong tea jelly filling**: Place the peach peel, oolong tea, and 8g of sugar into a bowl. Heat in the microwave on high for 3 minutes until the peach skin color is fully released. If the tea color is dull, add a few drops of lemon juice to brighten it. Cool to 40-50℃, then add 5g of softened and drained gelatin sheets, stirring until fully melted. Remove the peach peel. Pour the tea jelly liquid into the upper half of the instant noodle cup mold. Add a moderate amount of diced peach flesh. Place in the freezer until completely set.

3. **Make the honey peach fruit puree**: Mix 200g of peach flesh, 40g of sugar, and 1 slice of lemon juice. Heat in the microwave on high for 2-3 minutes until bubbling and soft. Pour out approximately 70g of the peach sugar water and reserve for the surface glaze. Puree the remaining peach flesh into a smooth puree. Once the puree has cooled to 40-50℃, add 10g of softened and drained gelatin sheets, stirring until melted.
   **Key point**: Place the bowl with the fruit puree over an ice water bath to cool completely before use.

4. **Whip the cream and mix the mousse**: Pour 240g of whipping cream into a bowl. Set the bowl over an ice water bath. Whip with an electric mixer until it reaches a thick, yogurt-like consistency with visible lines.
   **Key point**: Pour the completely cooled peach fruit puree into the whipped cream. Use a whisk or spatula to gently and evenly mix together. Avoid overmixing to prevent deflating.

5. **Assemble the cake**: Remove the chilled crust from the freezer. Pour in approximately 2/3 of the mousse mixture and spread it evenly. Remove the set tea jelly filling. Tear off the outer instant noodle cup shell. Gently place the tea jelly into the center of the mousse. Press down lightly so it adheres. Pour in the remaining mousse mixture, covering the tea jelly and filling the mold completely. Place in the freezer for 3-4 hours until fully set.

6. **Surface decoration and unmolding**: Make the glaze: Combine the reserved 70g of peach sugar water with 5g of softened and drained gelatin sheets. Heat to melt, then cool until it feels warm to the touch. Remove the set cake from the freezer. Cover the surface with thin peach slices. Pour the cooled glaze liquid over the top. Refrigerate or freeze again until the glaze has set.
   **Key point**: When unmolding, use scissors to cut down the side of the instant noodle cup, then gently tear it away.

## Health Advice
The honey peach oolong cake is rich in Vitamin C and tea polyphenols, making it suitable for cooling down in the summer. However, it contains high amounts of cream and sugar, so it is recommended to control portion sizes and pair it with unsweetened tea for best results. Note that the salt content in the crust can be reduced. Diabetics should consume with caution.
