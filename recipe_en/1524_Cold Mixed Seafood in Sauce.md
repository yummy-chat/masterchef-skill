# Cold Mixed Seafood in Sauce  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐚 Sea snails 2 jin (clean and purge sand)  
🐚 Drill snails 2 jin (clean and purge sand)  
🦪 Oysters 2 jin (clean and purge sand)  
🐚 Razor clams 1 jin (clean and purge sand)  
🦪 Scallop meat 1 jin (clean)  
🐚 Clams 2 jin (clean and purge sand)  
🦪 Abalone 2 jin (clean)  
🐙 Small octopus 1 jin (clean)  
🦞 Lobster tails 1 jin (clean)  
🦀 Crabs 2 jin (clean)  
🧄 Garlic slices 25g  
🫑 Green pepper rings 80g  
🌶️ Small chili peppers 20g  
🍋 Seedless lemon slices 100g  
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds 15g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 300g  
🐟 Steamed fish soy sauce 100g  
🥫 Light soy sauce 120g  
🍬 White sugar 50g  
🐔 Chicken broth 30g  
🌶️ Spicy fresh sauce 200g  
🐚 Scallop and abalone sauce 100g  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar 20g  
🥡 Seafood sauce 80g  
🧂 Chicken powder 25g  
🧂 Flavor powder 25g  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil 100g  
🌶️ Red oil 150g  
💧 Pure water 1250g  
🧅 Scallion segments, as needed  
🫚 Ginger slices, as needed  
🍶 Huadiao wine, as needed

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
📦 Rectangular airtight container  
🍲 Soup pot/wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🥘 Metal tray  
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions  
1. Make concentrated sauce: In a large glass bowl, add oyster sauce, steamed fish soy sauce, light soy sauce, white sugar, chicken broth, spicy fresh sauce, scallop and abalone sauce, apple cider vinegar, seafood sauce, chicken powder, flavor powder, and Sichuan pepper oil in order. Stir until well combined. Add red oil and stir until fully integrated and smooth.  
   **Note**: The concentrated sauce can be refrigerated for 3-4 days and used as needed.

2. Dilute the sauce: Mix 1000g concentrated sauce with 1250g pure water in the specified ratio. Add garlic slices, green pepper rings, small chili peppers, seedless lemon slices, and toasted sesame seeds. Stir well.  
   **Key**: Lemons must be seedless to prevent bitterness, and use pure water.

3. Prepare seafood: Clean all shellfish, snails, and crustaceans thoroughly or purge sand to ensure no grit remains.

4. Blanch: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and Huadiao wine to remove odor. After boiling, blanch each type of seafood separately in batches. Reference times: Sea snails, drill snails, abalone for 2 minutes 30 seconds; oysters for 1 minute 30 seconds; razor clams, scallop meat, clams for 1 minute; small octopus for 1 minute 30 seconds; lobster tails, crabs for 3 minutes.  
   **Key**: Precisely control time based on each seafood’s tolerance to cooking to avoid overcooking or undercooking.

5. Ice bath: Quickly transfer blanched seafood into ice water and soak until fully cooled. Drain thoroughly.  
   **Key**: Rapid cooling ensures a crisp texture.

6. Marinate: Neatly arrange drained seafood in an airtight container. Pour diluted sauce until it completely submerges the ingredients. Seal the lid.

7. Refrigerate: Place the container in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours to allow the seafood to absorb the flavor thoroughly, enhancing the taste.

8. Serve directly after removal. It has a spicy, numbing, and crisp texture with a sweet aftertaste.

## Health Advice  
This saucy seafood dish is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, ideal for replenishing energy in summer. However, the sauce is high in salt and sugar; those with high blood pressure are advised to limit liquid consumption. Abalone, oysters, etc., must be thoroughly cooked to avoid parasite risks.
