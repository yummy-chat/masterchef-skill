# Homemade Potato Chips
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 3 potatoes  
🧂 A small handful of salt (for seasoning after frying)  
🌿 Appropriate amount of cumin powder (or your favorite seasoning powder)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Peeler  
🧀 Slicer (or a knife, for slicing thinly)  
🍲 Pot (for blanching)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🌬️ Drying rack/oven rack (for air-drying)  
🔥 Oven (optional, for low-temperature drying as an alternative to air-drying)  
🍳 Deep pot/wok (for deep-frying)  
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing seasonings)  
🛍️ Storage bag (for storing dried potato slices or finished chips)

## Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and use a slicer to cut them into thin, even slices. Those skilled with a knife can slice them by hand directly.
2. Rinse the potato slices repeatedly in clean water until the water runs clear, to remove surface starch.
3. Fill a pot with water, heat it until warm, then add the potato slices and blanch for about 2 minutes to prevent oxidation and blackening. Be careful not to overcook, as they may break apart.
   **Note**: There’s no need to wait for the water to fully boil; blanch over heat for 2 minutes.
4. Remove the potato slices and spread them flat on a ventilated drying rack or oven rack to air-dry naturally. Alternatively, dry them in an oven at 60°C for about 3 hours, until the slices are completely hard and moisture-free.
5. Pour enough oil into a pot, heat over high heat until it reaches about 80% hot, then reduce to medium-low heat.
6. Grab a handful of dried potato slices and add them to the pot. The slices will quickly puff up and float. Immediately remove them with a slotted spoon to avoid burning.
7. Place the fried chips into a large bowl, sprinkle with salt and cumin powder, and gently mix until evenly coated.
8. Any remaining dried potato slices can be sealed in a storage bag and kept for frying later. Finished chips can be stored in an airtight container and remain crispy for about a week.

## Health Tips
Homemade potato chips have much of the starch removed, making them healthier than store-bought chips. However, they are still high in calories after frying, so it’s recommended to control portions and enjoy them as a snack. Suitable for those who love a crispy texture and want to avoid additives, but people with high blood lipids should consume in small amounts.
