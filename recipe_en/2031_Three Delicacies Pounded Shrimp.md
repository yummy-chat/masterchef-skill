## Preparation Steps
1.  **Prepare the shrimp**
    Remove the heads and shells from the large shrimp, butterfly the backs, and devein. Rinse clean with water.
    **Key point**: Keeping the tails on enhances the dish's visual appeal.

2.  **Marinate and pound into thin slices**
    Add a splash of cooking wine to the shrimp and mix well. Marinate for 5 minutes. Pat dry with a paper towel. Sprinkle cornstarch onto the cutting board and the shrimp's surface. Slowly pound the shrimp with a rolling pin until it becomes a thin slice, embedding the starch into the flesh.
    **Note**: Thoroughly incorporating the starch into the shrimp results in an exceptionally tender and smooth texture.

3.  **Blanch the shrimp slices**
    Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the pounded shrimp slices. Once the shrimp flesh turns white and the tails turn red, immediately remove them and submerge them in cold water to set aside.
    **Note**: The shrimp is cooked once it changes color; avoid prolonged boiling which makes it tough.

4.  **Prepare and blanch the accompaniments**
    Slice the ham and shiitake mushrooms. Cut the wood ear mushrooms into small pieces. Blanch the three ingredients together in boiling water for about half a minute, then remove and drain.

5.  **Stir-fry the accompaniments**
    Add a little oil to the wok. Over low heat, stir-fry the scallion sections and ginger slices until fragrant. Add the blanched ham, shiitake mushrooms, and wood ear mushrooms, and stir-fry briefly. Splash the yellow wine along the edge of the wok to release its aroma.

6.  **Simmer the broth**
    Pour in the chicken broth or meat broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 10 minutes to fully release the flavors of the ham and mushrooms, resulting in a clear broth.

7.  **Season and add the shrimp slices**
    Add ground white pepper, salt, and sugar to taste. Then, add the cooled shrimp slices and gently blanch them for a moment.
    **Note**: The shrimp is already cooked; extended heating will make it tough.

8.  **Thicken and finish**
    Drizzle in a small amount of water starch and stir to thicken the broth into a thin slurry for a silky texture. Finally, add a drop of sesame oil to enhance the aroma.

9.  **Plate**
    Line the bottom of a deep plate with rice crackers. Pour the prepared Three Delicacies Pounded Shrimp and the hot broth directly over the rice crackers.
    **Key point**: The hot broth will soften the rice crackers, which absorb the liquid, resulting in a tender, chewy, and savory texture.

## Health Suggestions
Pounding makes the shrimp exceptionally tender and easy to digest, suitable for the elderly and children. However, the cornstarch and rice crackers increase carbohydrate intake; portion control is advised during weight loss. The broth is flavorful and nutritionally balanced, but the ham and chicken broth are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should consume less of the soup.
