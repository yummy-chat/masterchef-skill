# Seven-Color Hand-Torn Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 young chicken (about 1.5-2 jin, cleaned)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, to taste (finely shredded)
🫑 Yellow bell pepper, to taste (finely shredded)
🥒 Green asparagus lettuce, to taste (finely shredded)
🥕 Carrot, to taste (finely shredded)
🍄 Wood ear mushroom, to taste (finely shredded)
🧅 Onion, to taste (finely shredded)
🫓 Crispy crackers, to taste (fried pastry, for added crunch)
🥜 Crushed peanuts, to taste (toasted or fried and crushed)
🤍 White sesame seeds, to taste (for garnish and aroma)
🧄 Dried shallots, to taste (minced, for the oil-vinegar dressing)

🧂 Half a packet of salt-baked chicken powder (for marinating the chicken)
🫚 Ginger slices, to taste (for the broth base, to remove gaminess and enhance aroma)
🧅 Scallion knots, to taste (for the broth base, to remove gaminess and enhance aroma)
🌼 Gardenia fruit (yellow栀子), to taste (for the broth base, to color the chicken skin yellow)
🫒 Homemade scallion oil, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)
🧂 Freshly ground white pepper, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)
🧂 Freshly ground sea salt, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste (for the oil-vinegar dressing)

## Tools and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating the whole chicken)
🍲 Stockpot (for boiling the aromatic broth)
🥄 Ladle (for pouring the broth over the chicken)
🧹 Skimmer (for assisting with blanching the chicken)
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife (for slicing vegetable shreds and dried shallots)
🥄 Spoon (for mixing the oil-vinegar dressing and drizzling)
🥣 Mixing bowl (for combining the oil-vinegar dressing)
🧊 Bowl of ice water (for soaking the cooked chicken to cool quickly)
🧻 Paper towels (for drying the surface of the chicken after cooling)
🍽️ Large round plate (for final plating)
🥣 Small bowls (for portioning side ingredients)
🧤 Disposable gloves (for hand-tearing the chicken)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Select a young chicken weighing about 1.5 to 2 jin. Have it slaughtered and cleaned.
2.  Place the whole chicken in a glass bowl. Add half a packet of salt-baked chicken powder. Use your hands to rub it repeatedly, ensuring the powder is evenly distributed on both the inside and outside of the chicken. Let it marinate to absorb the flavor.
3.  Finely shred the red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, green asparagus lettuce, carrot, wood ear mushroom, and onion. Soak the shreds in clean water to make them crisp.
4.  Prepare the crispy crackers, crushed peanuts, and white sesame seeds as garnishes for later use.
5.  Prepare the oil-vinegar dressing: In a mixing bowl, place the minced dried shallots. Add homemade scallion oil, light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, freshly ground white pepper, freshly ground sea salt, and chicken bouillon in order. Stir well to combine. Set aside.
**Key Point**: The scallion oil must be homemade for better flavor.
6.  Fill a stockpot with enough water. Add ginger slices, scallion knots, and gardenia fruit. Bring to a boil over high heat to create a colored aromatic broth base.
7.  Lift the marinated chicken. Use a ladle to repeatedly pour the hot broth over the chicken body, allowing the skin to heat evenly, contract, and set.
8.  Fully submerge the whole chicken in the barely simmering aromatic broth. Choose one of the following two cooking methods:
   - **Method One**: Use the "three lifts and three dunks" method. Blanch the chicken in the boiling water for 10-20 seconds, quickly remove, and soak in ice water to cool. Repeat three times for crispy skin.
   - **Method Two**: After the water boils, reduce the heat to low. Fully submerge the chicken, cover the pot, and simmer gently for about 30 minutes until cooked through.
9.  Remove the cooked chicken and immediately soak it in ice water to cool quickly, making the skin firm and crispy and the meat tender.
10. Take the cooled chicken out and dry the surface moisture with paper towels. Wearing disposable gloves, use your hands to tear the chicken meat into small pieces and remove the bones.
11. Begin plating: Pile the shredded chicken in the center of the plate. Drain the soaked colorful vegetable shreds. Shape them into balls and arrange them around the chicken on the plate's edge, following a color sequence.
12. Sprinkle the crispy crackers, crushed peanuts, and white sesame seeds over the chicken.
13. Evenly drizzle the prepared oil-vinegar dressing over the entire dish.
14. Before eating, use chopsticks to thoroughly mix all ingredients with the sauce.
**Note**: Mix and serve immediately to maintain the crispness of the vegetable shreds and the crunchiness of the crackers.

## Health Advice
This seven-color hand-torn chicken is rich in color and nutrition. The chicken is high in protein and low in fat. Pairing it with a variety of vegetable shreds provides dietary fiber. It is suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those watching their calorie intake. However, salt-baked chicken powder is high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount used.
