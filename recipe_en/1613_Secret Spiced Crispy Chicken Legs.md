# Secret Spiced Crispy Chicken Legs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 3 large chicken legs  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (some sliced for marinating, some cut into long sections for the base)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🍶 Yellow wine, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for frying)  

## Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok or deep fryer  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🪥 Toothpicks (for piercing holes)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Use toothpicks to evenly pierce holes all over the surface of the chicken legs to allow the marinade to penetrate.  
2. Add yellow wine, salt, Sichuan peppercorns, white pepper, scallion slices, and ginger slices. Mix well and marinate for 5 hours.  
   **Note**: Remove the Sichuan peppercorns after steaming to avoid affecting the texture.  
3. Line the bottom of a steaming dish with scallion sections, then place the marinated chicken legs on top.  
4. Once the steamer is producing steam, add the chicken legs and steam over high heat for 25 minutes until fully cooked.  
5. Remove the steamed chicken legs and discard the scallions, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns on the surface.  
6. Heat cooking oil in a wok to 180°C (350°F). Fry the chicken legs until the skin is golden and crispy, then remove and drain the oil.  
   **Key**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high, to avoid the outside burning while the inside remains raw.  
7. Cut the fried chicken legs into pieces, arrange on a plate, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
These chicken legs are first steamed and then fried, resulting in a crispy exterior and tender interior. The steaming process removes some of the fat, making them suitable for fitness enthusiasts as an occasional treat. They are high in protein and low in burden, but the frying process will absorb some oil, so it's recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance the calorie intake.
