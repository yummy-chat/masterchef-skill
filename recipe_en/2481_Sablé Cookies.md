# Sablé Cookies

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Butter 100g (no need to soften, cut into small pieces directly)  
🍬 Powdered sugar 45g (can substitute with fine granulated sugar)  
🌾 Cake flour 150g  
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (yolks only)  
🍵 Crushed black tea leaves approx. 2g (for black tea flavor)  
🍋 Lemon zest approx. zest of 1 lemon (for lemon flavor)  
🍫 Cocoa powder approx. 5g (for chocolate flavor)  
🍵 Matcha powder approx. 3g (for matcha flavor)  
🍬 Granulated sugar as needed (for coating the dough surface)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl  
🪥 Sieve  
🔪 Knife  
🧻 Plastic wrap  
📋 Baking sheet + parchment paper  
🔥 Oven (or air fryer)  
🧊 Refrigerator (freezer)  
🧤 Wire cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1. Cut 100g butter into small pieces and place in a large bowl.  
   **Note**: The butter does not need to be softened; this method is especially suitable in winter, helping the dough maintain a more crumbly structure.  
2. Mix 45g powdered sugar with 150g cake flour, then sift into the bowl with the butter.  
   **Key**: Sifting makes the dry ingredients finer and prevents lumps.  
3. Use your hands to combine the butter with the dry ingredients, rubbing between your fingertips until the mixture resembles loose sand with no visible dry flour.  
   **Note**: Use the warmth of your hands to soften and incorporate the butter, ensuring each grain of flour is evenly coated. Avoid over-kneading into a dough.  
4. Add 2 egg yolks, then use your hands to pinch and knead into a smooth dough. Stop as soon as it comes together.  
   **Key**: Using only egg yolks increases crumbliness; do not over-knead to prevent gluten development.  
5. Divide the plain dough into equal portions, making different flavors as desired.  
6. Into each portion of dough, knead in crushed tea leaves, lemon zest, cocoa powder, matcha powder, or other flavor additives until evenly distributed.  
7. Using plastic wrap to assist, shape each flavored dough into a uniform cylinder, triangle, square, or other shape. Wrap and roll to make the shape neater.  
8. Wrap the shaped dough tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for about 15 minutes until firm and set, making it easier to slice later.  
9. Remove the frozen cylindrical dough, brush the surface with a thin layer of water, then roll it in a layer of granulated sugar until evenly coated.  
10. Using a knife, slice the sugar-coated dough or other flavored dough into uniform pieces about 0.5 cm thick.  
    **Key**: The dough should be neither too soft nor too hard; slices should be even in thickness.  
11. Arrange the sliced cookie dough evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving space between them to prevent sticking.  
12. **Baking in oven**: Preheat to the set temperature, top heat 170°C, bottom heat 160°C, bake for about 20 minutes until the edges are lightly colored; adjust time according to your oven's actual temperature.  
13. **Baking in air fryer**: Bake at 150°C for about 20 minutes, no preheating needed. Check the browning midway, bake until the surface is golden and the bottom is colored.  
14. After baking, remove and place the cookies on a wire cooling rack to cool completely; they will become crispy after cooling. If they are not crisp after cooling, return to the oven for a few more minutes.  

## Health Advice  
These cookies use only egg yolks and cold butter, resulting in a crumbly texture, but they are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair them with black coffee or green tea to help slow blood sugar fluctuations. Suitable as an afternoon tea snack, limit to 3-4 pieces per serving. Be careful to distinguish between crushed tea leaves and sesame seeds.
