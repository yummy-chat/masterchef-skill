# Braised Beef Shin in Rice Cooker
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1 piece of beef shank (soak in cold water to remove blood; trim off excess fat)  
🧄 Ginger,适量 (sliced or crushed)  
🧄 Garlic,适量 (whole cloves or crushed)  
🌶️ A few chili peppers (adjust to taste, for aroma and heat)  
🍂 1 spice pouch for braising  
🧂 More light soy sauce (main base flavor, salty and savory)  
🧂 Less dark soy sauce (for color, to achieve a glossy red hue)  
🍬 1 piece of sugar (rock sugar or white sugar, to balance saltiness and enhance flavor)  
💧 A little water (to adjust the concentration of the braising liquid)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker (with steam/braise/cook modes)  
🔪 Knife  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥄 Spice bag (to hold ginger, garlic, and spice pouch, preventing them from disintegrating)  
🥢 Tongs  
🥡 Plastic wrap  
🛍️ Storage bags  
🧊 Refrigerator  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Pre-treat the beef shank**: Soak the beef shank in cold water to thoroughly remove blood; use kitchen scissors to trim off any thick surface fat, then drain and set aside.  
   - **Note**: The more thoroughly the blood is removed, the less gamey the flavor, and the cleaner the braised meat will taste.  
2. **Fill the spice bag**: Place ginger, garlic, and the spice pouch into the spice bag, and tie it tightly.  
3. **Prepare the braising liquid**: Put the prepared beef shank into the rice cooker inner pot, add the spice bag containing ginger, garlic, and the spice pouch, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, chili peppers, and a little water.  
   - **Key point**: Use more light soy sauce and less dark soy sauce; the saltiness comes mainly from the light soy sauce, while the dark soy sauce is only for coloring. The amount of water should not be too large, as the rice cooker seals well and minimizes evaporation, resulting in a richer, more concentrated braising liquid.  
4. **Cook**: Close the rice cooker lid, select the “braise” or “cook” mode, and cook until the beef is tender and a chopstick can easily pierce through it.  
   - **Doneness check**: The beef should turn a deep reddish-brown, and the liquid should become thick. If it's not tender enough after one cycle, repeat the cooking program once more.  
5. **Marinate in the liquid**: Do not remove the beef immediately after cooking; let it naturally soak in the braising liquid for 7–8 hours to allow the savory flavors to fully penetrate the meat.  
6. **Shape and store**:  
   - Remove the beef shank, let it cool slightly, then tightly wrap it in plastic wrap into a cylindrical shape. Refrigerate until completely cooled and set.  
   - Divide the braising liquid into single-use portions in storage bags. It can be refrigerated for 3–4 days, or frozen for longer storage, ready to use as needed.  
7. **Slice and serve**: Take out the chilled, set braised beef shank, slice it thinly, and enjoy it as is or pair it with breakfast, noodles, etc.  

## Health Tips  
Soaking the beef shank in the braising liquid for 7–8 hours allows the savory flavors to penetrate deeply. For lower sodium, use low-sodium light soy sauce to control salt intake, making it suitable for fitness or weight-loss diets when paired with whole-grain staples. The rice cooker remains sealed throughout, preventing moisture loss and yielding a rich, concentrated braising liquid.
