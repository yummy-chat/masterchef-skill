# Chongqing Small Noodles
Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Bullet dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
🌶️ Erjingtiao dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
🌶️ Lantern dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper (flower pepper), appropriate amount
🫘 Dried peas (with skin), appropriate amount
🥩 Pork front shoulder (with balanced fat and lean), appropriate amount
🥬 Yibin pickled vegetables, appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount
🥜 Crushed peanuts, appropriate amount
🍜 Alkaline noodles (or thin hand-rolled noodles), appropriate amount

🫒 Rapeseed oil, appropriate amount
🥫 Pixian broad bean paste, appropriate amount
🥄 Pure sesame paste, about half a small spoon
🫗 Sesame oil, a little (to dilute the sesame paste)
🧈 Lard, one small spoon
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil, a little
🍾 Soy sauce, appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili oil (containing crushed chili and white sesame), appropriate amount
🌰 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine, a little
🧂 Salt, a little

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Blender/food processor
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥣 Seasoning bowl
🍲 Cooking pot

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make Sichuan pepper oil**: Grind raw green Sichuan pepper into powder in a blender; heat rapeseed oil to 50% hot, pour it into the Sichuan pepper powder and stir well.
2. **Make chili flakes**: Mix the three types of dried chili peppers, stir-fry them slowly in a wok over low heat until the color turns slightly red and a fragrance is released, remove from heat and let cool, then grind into coarse chili flakes in a blender.
**Note**: Stir-fry slowly over low heat to avoid burning.
3. **Make chili oil**: Heat rapeseed oil to 50% hot, first scoop a spoonful and pour it into the chili flakes to test the oil temperature. Once the temperature is suitable, pour the hot oil into the chili flakes in batches, stirring while pouring, finally sprinkle in white sesame seeds for added fragrance.
4. **Prepare meat topping**: Finely chop the Pixian broad bean paste; add oil to the wok, stir-fry the bean paste over low heat until fragrant and the red oil is released, add the minced pork front shoulder, stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture in the meat is dried out, add a little cooking wine to remove any unpleasant smell, continue stir-frying until the meat is dry, fragrant, and crispy.
5. **Prepare peas**: Soak the dried peas in advance for at least 10 hours, put them in a pressure cooker, add water and a little salt, and cook under pressure for more than 40 minutes until soft and mushy.
6. **Prepare pickled vegetables**: Rinse the Yibin pickled vegetables with clean water, add a little oil to the wok, stir-fry the pickled vegetables until the moisture is gone and a fragrance is released.
7. **Prepare ginger-garlic water**: Mash the ginger and garlic together into a paste, add a little cold water and mix well.
8. **Prepare the seasoning**: In an empty bowl, add in order the sesame paste diluted with sesame oil, one small spoon of lard, ginger-garlic paste, one spoon of Sichuan pepper oil, an appropriate amount of soy sauce, and an appropriate amount of chili oil, and stir well.
**Key point**: Do not use too much sesame paste to prevent the soup from becoming too thick; use sesame oil to loosen the sesame paste in advance.
9. **Cook noodles and assemble the bowl**: Bring water to a boil in the cooking pot, add the alkaline noodles. Do not cook the noodles for too long. When the noodles are almost done, scoop a small amount of boiling noodle water into the seasoning bowl and stir well. Fish the cooked noodles out and place them in the bowl.
10. **Add toppings**: On top of the noodles, layer in order the stir-fried meat topping, stir-fried pickled vegetables, crushed peanuts, and chopped scallions. Finally, scoop a few spoonfuls of soft peas with a little of their liquid and place on top of the noodles.

## Health Advice
Chongqing small noodles are spicy and satisfying, but the chili oil and lard are not low in calories. It is recommended to control the portion size of the noodles and pair them with a serving of stir-fried vegetables to balance the nutrition. The dried peas and meat topping provide fiber and protein, making them suitable for consumption after physical exertion, but those with a weak stomach should use caution when adding Sichuan pepper oil.
