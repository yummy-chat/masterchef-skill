# Dry-Braised Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork spare ribs (front ribs) 400g, cut into small pieces, choose front ribs with some fat and flat meat
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount, sliced
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount, minced
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount, finely chopped
🧅 Dried shallots, appropriate amount, finely chopped
🦐 Dried shrimp, appropriate amount, chopped
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, 1 small piece, soaked until soft and shredded
🫘 Homemade fermented black bean paste, appropriate amount (includes fermented black beans, minced garlic, dried shrimp, dried shallots, etc.)
🧂 Salt, about 3g
🍬 Sugar, about 1.5g
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce, about 9g
🍶 Rice wine, appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, a little
🫒 Cooking oil, 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Carbon steel wok
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Plate
🔗 Steaming rack
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the pork spare ribs into even small pieces; slice the fresh shiitake mushrooms; soak the dried tangerine peel until soft and shred; mince the garlic; finely chop the ginger and dried shallots; chop the dried shrimp.
2. Mix the homemade fermented black bean paste with the shredded dried tangerine peel evenly and set aside.
3. Take a bowl and add the rib pieces. Add salt, sugar, oyster sauce, minced garlic, finely chopped ginger, finely chopped dried shallots, chopped dried shrimp, the fermented black bean paste mixture, and light soy sauce in order. Stir thoroughly until well combined.
4. Add a little cornstarch and 1 tablespoon of cooking oil, mix well, and marinate.
5. Spread the shiitake mushroom slices evenly on the bottom of a plate. Then place the marinated ribs flat on top of the mushrooms.
6. Place the carbon steel wok on the stove. Heat over high heat for about 5 minutes until the wok turns red.
7. Place the steaming rack inside the heated wok. Carefully set the plate with the ribs on the rack and cover the wok tightly. Do not add any water or liquid throughout the process.
**Key point**: Do not add any water or liquid throughout the process to achieve the "dry-braising" effect.
8. Immediately after covering, reduce the heat to low and continue to cook for about 7 minutes.
9. After a total dry-braising time of about 12 minutes, uncover the wok. Use chopsticks to pick up a piece of rib. If the meat has visibly shrunk and easily separates from the bone, it is cooked through.
10. Turn off the heat. You can garnish with chopped scallions and serve directly from the wok.
**Key point**: The wok must be heated to a high temperature to create the "dry-braising" effect; do not use too much cornstarch to avoid burning; ensure the wok is tightly sealed while cooking over low heat.

## Health Advice
This dry-braised pork rib dish uses intense high heat in a dry wok to bring out the meat's aroma. It is suitable for those needing calcium and protein, but be mindful of its high salt and oil content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for a balanced diet.
