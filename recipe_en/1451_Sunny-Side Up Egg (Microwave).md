# Sunny-Side Up Egg (Microwave)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Egg
🧂 Salt
🫒 Oil

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
📡 Microwave
🥢 Chopsticks
🪡 Toothpick
🥣 Small bowl

## Instructions

### Method for Microwave with Adjustable Power
1. Take a small bowl, pour in an appropriate amount of oil, add salt, and stir well. Tilt the bowl to coat the inner surface evenly with oil.
2. Crack the egg into the bowl.
3. Use a toothpick to poke 5 small holes in the egg yolk, or use chopsticks to poke 1 hole, to prevent it from bursting during heating.
4. Place in the microwave and heat on medium power for 3 minutes.

### Method for Microwave with Fixed Power
1. Take a small bowl, pour in an appropriate amount of oil, add salt, and stir well. Tilt the bowl to coat the inner surface evenly with oil.
2. Crack the egg into the bowl.
3. Use a toothpick to poke 5 small holes in the egg yolk, or use chopsticks to poke 1 hole, to prevent it from bursting during heating.
4. Place in the microwave and heat for 1 minute.
5. If the egg liquid has not largely solidified, add 30 seconds of heating each time until it reaches the desired doneness.
6. If the doneness does not match your preference, heat for an additional 1 minute.

**Note**: A microwave with fixed power can only set the heating time, not the power level. A microwave with adjustable power allows you to control both the power level and time. To identify them, check whether the control panel has options for low, medium, or high power.

## Health Tips
Eggs are rich in high-quality protein and vitamin D. A single sunny-side up egg has approximately 70 calories; adjust your staple food intake accordingly. Suitable for office workers needing a quick protein boost. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt used.
