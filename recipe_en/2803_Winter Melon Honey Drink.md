# Winter Melon Honey Drink
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April - August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥒 Winter melon, appropriate amount (thick slices with skin, cut into bite-sized pieces)  
🍯 Rock sugar, appropriate amount  
🫗 Drinking water, appropriate amount (for boiling sugar water; recommended ratio: 7 taels winter melon to 1 jin water for blending)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (to enhance sweetness)  
🍯 Multifloral honey (or honey), appropriate amount (for final seasoning)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🍲 Cooking pot (transparent glass pot)  
🥤 Blender/high-speed blender  
🥄 Strainer or cheesecloth  
🥄 Spoon  
🧊 Bowl or basin (for ice water)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the winter melon with the skin on into thick slices, then further cut into bite-sized pieces, ensuring each piece retains some of the skin.  
   **Key**: Keeping the skin on gives the final product a vibrant green color.
2. Fill the pot with water, bring to a rolling boil over high heat, add a pinch of salt and the rock sugar, stirring until fully dissolved to create a sugar syrup.  
   **Note**: A little salt enhances the sweetness, making the honey taste more fragrant.
3. Add the winter melon pieces into the boiling rock sugar water, keep the boil going, and blanch for exactly 60 seconds.  
   **Key**: The timing must be precise to ensure the winter melon is just cooked through but not overly soft.
4. Immediately remove the winter melon pieces and quickly submerge them in pre-prepared ice water.  
   **Key**: Rapid cooling locks in the winter melon's vibrant green color and fresh aroma.
5. Place the cooled winter melon pieces and water into a blender, blending until a fine green puree forms.
6. Strain the puree through a strainer or cheesecloth to obtain a clear green juice, discarding all coarse residue.
7. Add an appropriate amount of multifloral honey or honey to the strained winter melon juice and gently stir until well combined.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of honey to personal taste; serve after mixing thoroughly.

## Health Tips
Boiling winter melon with the skin preserves its nutrients. It is recommended for cooling down during summer, but those with high blood sugar should monitor their sugar intake. Honey should be added at the end to avoid heat damage to its active components.
