# Crispy Aromatic Duck
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 1 small Cherry Valley duck (whole, about 2-3 jin)
🧅 1 section of scallion (about 5 cm, some finely minced scallion hearts)
🫚 2-3 slices of ginger
🧂 Salt, to taste (used separately for rubbing the duck, marinade, boiling water, and scallion-pepper salt)
🌿 1 cinnamon stick (about finger length)
⭐ 2 star anise
🌸 About 10 Sichuan peppercorns (for marinating), about 2 tablespoons (for stir-frying pepper salt)
🍶 About 2 tablespoons of yellow wine
🌾 2 parts flour (ratio 2:1 with starch)
🌽 1 part starch
🥚 1 egg
🫒 Sufficient cooking oil (for deep frying, about 1/3 depth of the pot)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chinese chef's knife
🪵 Round wooden cutting board
🥣 Large basin
🍚 Rice cooker
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪄 Rolling pin
🫗 Fine strainer/sieve
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. **Whole Duck Preparation**: Clean the duck thoroughly, remove the tail, esophagus, and other inedible parts. Carefully debone the duck with a knife, keeping the duck skin intact without tearing.
   **Key Point**: Debone gently to avoid piercing the duck skin.

2. **Marinating**: Rub salt evenly over the entire duck, inside and out, paying extra attention to thick areas like the drumsticks. Place the duck in a basin, add cinnamon stick, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, scallion sections, ginger slices, and yellow wine, mixing well to marinate. If short on time, marinate for at least half an hour; for better flavor, marinate for over an hour.

3. **Boiling**: Transfer the marinated duck along with all marinade ingredients into the rice cooker inner pot. Add enough water to submerge the duck, and supplement with additional salt and yellow wine. Select the rice cooking or soup setting and cook for about 40 minutes, until the duck legs visibly shrink and bones are exposed, and the meat is tender.

4. **Making Scallion-Pepper Salt**:
   a. Heat the wok without oil, dry-roast the Sichuan peppercorns over low heat until they darken, become crispy, and release fragrance. Remove and let cool.
   b. Crush with a rolling pin into a powder, then pass through a fine strainer to remove coarse husks.
   c. Mix the Sichuan pepper powder, finely minced scallion hearts, and salt to taste. Chop the mixture evenly with a knife.

5. **Preparing Batter**: In a bowl, mix flour and starch in a 2:1 ratio. Add the egg and a small amount of water, stirring into a pourable batter. Ensure it is not too thin.

6. **Shredding Meat and Coating**: Remove the boiled duck and let it cool slightly. Tear all the meat off the duck by hand, discarding all bones. Pat the surface of the duck meat dry with kitchen paper towels. Mix in the scallion-pepper salt evenly, shape it back into a duck form on a plate, then pour the prepared batter over it, ensuring it is evenly coated.

7. **First Frying for Setting**: Heat oil in the wok to about 70% heat. Slide the batter-coated duck patty into the oil along the edge of the wok. Fry over medium heat until the batter is set and turns light yellow, then remove.

8. **Second Frying for Crispiness**: Increase the oil temperature to 70-80% heat. Return the duck patty to the oil and fry until the outer skin is golden and feels crispy. Remove quickly and drain excess oil.
   **Key Point**: Do not fry too long during the second frying to avoid burning; the oil temperature must be high enough to achieve a crispy exterior and tender interior.

## Health Advice
Crispy Aromatic Duck is rich in protein, but due to deep frying, it is relatively high in calories. It is suitable for fitness muscle-building periods or occasional indulgence. It is recommended to pair it with a cold vegetable salad to balance the greasiness. Duck meat is cool in nature; those with a weak spleen and stomach should consume it in moderation. During the second frying, control the oil temperature to avoid excessive production of harmful substances.
