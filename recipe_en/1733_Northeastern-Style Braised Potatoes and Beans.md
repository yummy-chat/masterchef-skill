# Northeastern-Style Braised Potatoes and Beans
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Recommended Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with skin (moderate amount, sliced)  
🫘 Purple beans 1000g (washed and trimmed)  
🥔 Potatoes 500g (peeled and cut into rolling chunks)  
🧅 Scallion 4 sections  
🫚 Ginger 4 small slices  
🧄 Garlic 3 cloves (smashed) + a little minced garlic (for finishing)  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🫕 Lard (or cooking oil), moderate amount  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder 1 tsp  
🫘 Soybean paste 2 tbsp  
🥡 Light soy sauce 4 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce (optional), moderate amount  
🧂 Salt, to taste

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Bowl/spoon  
🪵 Wooden lid

## Steps
1. Cut pork belly into slightly thick slices, scallion into sections, ginger into slices, smash garlic cloves, peel potatoes and cut into rolling chunks.  
**Key point**: Pork belly must have skin for better texture; potato amount should be about half of the beans—too much will make the dish sticky.
2. Heat lard in the wok, add pork belly slices, and stir-fry over high heat.  
**Visual cue**: Fry until the edges of the pork are slightly browned and fat is rendered.
3. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, smashed garlic, star anise, and thirteen-spice powder. Continue stir-frying until fragrant.
4. Reduce heat to the lowest, add soybean paste and light soy sauce. Stir carefully over low heat.  
**Note**: Must use low heat to prevent the sauce from splattering. Stir until the sauce is aromatic.
5. Add the beans, turn the heat to the highest, and stir-fry until all beans turn bright green.  
**Key point**: Do not rush to add water; make sure the beans are thoroughly stir-fried until the color changes.
6. Add potato chunks and stir-fry again evenly.
7. Add enough water to just barely cover the ingredients. Optionally drizzle in oyster sauce.  
**Key point**: Do not add too much water, or the final sauce will be too thin.
8. Bring the liquid to a boil over high heat, then cover the wok with a lid. Reduce heat to medium-low (slightly above low) and braise for about 30 minutes.  
**Note**: Stir occasionally during braising to prevent sticking and burning.
9. After braising, remove the lid and increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients.  
**Key point**: Do not reduce the sauce completely dry; keep it thick and glossy.
10. Sprinkle in minced garlic and an appropriate amount of salt. Stir quickly and evenly, then serve.  
**Key point**: Adding raw minced garlic just before serving is key to enhancing the flavor.

## Health Tips
Potatoes and beans are rich in dietary fiber, while pork belly provides quality fat and protein, making this dish suitable for replenishing energy in physically active individuals. Beans must be fully cooked to prevent toxicity. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soybean paste and salt.
