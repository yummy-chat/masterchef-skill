# Fish Congee
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Yellow fin fish 1 (flesh sliced, bones reserved)
🍚 Jasmine rice 150 g (rinsed)
🥕 Carrot to taste (diced)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms to taste (sliced)
🥦 Cauliflower to taste (cut into small florets)
🌿 Celery to taste (minced)
🫚 Ginger to taste (some juice reserved for marinating fish, some cut into shreds for congee, some minced)
🥚 Egg white 1
🌽 Cornstarch to taste
🍶 Cooking wine a small amount
🧂 Salt to taste
🫒 Soybean oil a small amount (for sealing the fish after marinating)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for frying fish bones)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🫗 Strainer
🫕 Clay pot
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. Scale and clean the yellow fin fish. Remove the head and bones, fillet the flesh from both sides, remove the skin, and slice diagonally into thin pieces.
2. Mix the fish slices with salt and ginger juice; add egg white, cooking wine, and cornstarch, and continue to mix until coated. Finally, drizzle with a little soybean oil and mix to prevent sticking.
3. Dice the carrot, slice the shiitake mushrooms, cut the cauliflower into small florets, and mince the celery. Cut some ginger into shreds and mince the rest.
4. Heat the wok, coat with oil, and add the fish head and bones. Fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown. Pour in enough boiling water, add the ginger shreds, and boil over high heat until the soup turns milky white. Strain the broth into the clay pot using a strainer, discarding the bone residue.
   **Key Point**: Boiling water must be used for the fish bones to quickly achieve a milky white broth.
5. Bring the fish broth to a boil, add the rinsed rice, and cook over medium heat for 15 minutes. Add the diced carrot and ginger shreds, and cook for another 15 minutes. Then add the sliced shiitake mushrooms and cauliflower florets, and continue cooking for about 15 minutes until the congee thickens.
6. Season with salt to taste. Slide the marinated fish slices one by one into the boiling congee, and cook for about 2 minutes until the fish changes color and is fully cooked.
   **Key Point**: Do not overcook the fish slices after adding them to maintain a tender and smooth texture.
7. Turn off the heat, sprinkle with minced celery, and serve.

## Health Tips
This fish congee, made with a broth from fish bones, allows the rice to fully absorb the savory flavor. It offers a gentle combination of high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it especially suitable for those with weak digestion or who need to warm the stomach. Note that the fish slices should not be cooked for long after being added to the congee—2 minutes is sufficient to cook them through; otherwise, the fish may break apart, affecting both texture and appearance.
