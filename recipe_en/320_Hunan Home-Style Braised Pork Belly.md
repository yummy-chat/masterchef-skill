# Hunan Home-Style Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Skin-on pork belly 500g  
🌶 Dried small chili peppers (adjust to taste)  
🫚 Ginger 3 slices  
🍃 Bay leaves 5 pieces  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece  
⭐ Star anise 3 pieces  
🍬 Rock sugar 6 pieces  
🧂 Light soy sauce 5g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 2g  
🍶 Cooking wine 20g  
🧂 Salt 2g  
🫒 Cooking oil 10g  

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Soup pot  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  

## Instructions
1. Rinse the skin-on pork belly, place it in a pot with cold water, and add 2-3 slices of ginger to remove any gamey smell. Bring to a boil, then remove the pork and rinse off any white foam with cold water.  
**Key point**: Blanching the pork belly in cold water helps remove odors and blood foam more effectively; rinse thoroughly with cold water after removing.

2. Cut the pork belly into 1.5 cm cubes.  
3. Heat oil in a wok over low heat, add rock sugar, and stir continuously until it turns caramel-colored. Add the pork belly cubes and stir-fry over medium heat until evenly coated.  
**Key point**: When caramelizing the sugar, keep the heat low and stir constantly to prevent burning and bitterness.

4. Add ginger slices, star anise, cinnamon stick, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, cooking wine, dried small chili peppers, and salt. Stir-fry over low heat for 1 minute, then add boiling water until the meat is just submerged.  
**Note**: Always use boiling water during simmering to prevent the meat from tightening and becoming tough.

5. Cover and bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent sticking.  
**Key point**: Stirring regularly during simmering is crucial to prevent sticking; do not skip this step.

6. Once the liquid has reduced to about one-third, increase the heat to medium and reduce the sauce until thickened. Serve immediately.  
**Key point**: During the sauce reduction phase, switch to medium heat to quickly concentrate the liquid, giving the braised pork a glossy red color and a softer, stickier texture.

## Health Suggestions
Pork belly is rich in collagen but also high in fat. Pair with vegetables to balance the meal. Those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation. Slow simmering helps render out some of the saturated fat, making the meat more tender, but portion control is still advised.
