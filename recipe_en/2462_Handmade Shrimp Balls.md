# Handmade Shrimp Balls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🍤 Shrimp 500g (fresh shrimp recommended; if using frozen shrimp, pre-treatment to remove slipperiness is required)
🐖 Pork Fat 50g (ratio to shrimp approx. 1:10, to enhance tenderness and moisture retention)
🧅 Spring Onion 20g (about a piece the length of 2/3 palm)
🫚 Ginger 10g (a small piece)
💧 Water 60g (for making scallion and ginger water; use 40g after filtering)
🥚 Egg White 1 (about 30g)

## Seasonings
🍶 White Vinegar 7g (about 2/3 spoon, for treating frozen shrimp)
🍬 White Sugar 7g (about 2/3 leveled spoon, to reduce greasiness and enhance freshness)
🧂 Salt 5g (1.5 leveled teaspoon, key for achieving springy texture)
🌶️ White Pepper Powder 0.3g (a pinch, for removing fishy smell and seasoning)
🌽 Corn Starch 30g (3 leveled spoons, for binding and moisture retention)
🫒 Sesame Oil (to taste, for added aroma)
🧂 MSG (optional, to taste)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass Bowl (for mixing filling)
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board (for cutting shrimp and pork fat, crushing scallion and ginger)
🌀 Meat Grinder (for grinding pork fat into paste)
⚖️ Kitchen Scale (for precise measurements)
🥄 Fine Mesh Strainer (for filtering scallion and ginger water)
🍲 Soup Pot (for cooking shrimp balls)
🥄 Spoon (for seasoning and assisting in shaping balls)
🥄 Slotted Spoon (for skimming foam)
🧻 Kitchen Paper Towels (for drying)

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Stage
1.  Place 500g frozen shrimp in a bowl, add 7g white vinegar, and knead for 20–30 seconds. Rinse thoroughly under running water until no longer slimy to the touch. Finally, pat completely dry with kitchen paper towels.
    **Key Point**: Thoroughly drying the shrimp is essential for achieving a bouncy texture.
2.  Slice 50g pork fat, put it into a meat grinder, and grind into a fine paste. Transfer to a bowl, add 7g white sugar, mix well, and set aside.
    **Key Point**: The finer the pork fat paste, the better; sugar enhances freshness and reduces greasiness.
3.  Crush 20g spring onion and 10g ginger by flattening with a knife. Place in a bowl with 60g water, squeeze to release flavor, then strain through a fine mesh strainer. Reserve 40g of the scallion and ginger water.
    **Key Point**: The scallion and ginger water should be thoroughly squeezed for flavor and strained before use.
4.  Divide the dried shrimp into two portions. Take about 1/3 and cut into small pieces. For the remaining 2/3, flatten each shrimp with the flat side of a knife, then repeatedly chop with the back of the knife for about 2 minutes until a paste without large lumps forms. Mix the shrimp paste with the shrimp pieces.
    **Key Point**: Keeping some chunky pieces enhances texture; flattening then chopping yields better results than using a meat grinder.

### Making Stage
1.  Add 5g salt and a pinch of white pepper to the mixed shrimp. Vigorously knead with your hands until the shrimp becomes stringy and sticky.
    **Texture Check**: The shrimp should become noticeably thick and stretch into strands.
    **Key Point**: Salt is the key to achieving the springy texture (shàng jìn).
2.  Add the prepared pork fat paste to the shrimp and mix well. Then, gradually add the 40g scallion and ginger water in batches, mixing and kneading until the water is fully absorbed and the filling becomes sticky again.
    **Texture Check**: The filling may briefly separate when water is added; continue mixing until it comes together and forms a gel again.
    **Key Point**: The scallion and ginger water must be added in batches and fully absorbed.
3.  Once the water is absorbed, add 30g corn starch and gently mix until no dry powder is visible. Finally, add one egg white and stir in one direction until the filling is very thick and sticky.
    **Key Point**: After adding the egg white, stir in a single direction to increase elasticity.
4.  Repeatedly slap the mixed shrimp filling against the bowl 20–30 times to make it firmer.
    **Texture Check**: The filling should become very springy after slapping.
    **Key Point**: Slapping gives the shrimp balls a more Q (chewy) texture; the prepared filling can be sealed and frozen for later use.
5.  Bring water to a boil in a pot, then reduce the heat to low when dense small bubbles appear at the bottom. Wet your hands, grab some filling, and squeeze out balls from the space between your thumb and index finger. Scoop them off with a wet spoon and gently drop them into the pot. After all balls are in, turn the heat to medium to maintain the water temperature. Cook for about 2–4 minutes, skimming off any foam during cooking.
    **Texture Check**: The shrimp balls are cooked when they float to the surface.
    **Key Point**: The water temperature must not reach a rolling boil, or the shrimp balls may fall apart.
6.  Once the shrimp balls are cooked, add seaweed, salt, chopped spring onion, coriander, and sesame oil according to preference. Bring to a boil and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Shrimp balls are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making them suitable for muscle building and weight loss diets. However, the addition of pork fat and starch increases the calorie content; amounts can be reduced appropriately. Be careful not to overcook them, as the texture will deteriorate.
