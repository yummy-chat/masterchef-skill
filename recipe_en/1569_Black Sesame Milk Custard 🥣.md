# Black Sesame Milk Custard 🥣
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
⚫ Black sesame seeds 80g (5 units)  
🥜 Peanuts 30g (1 unit)  
🍚 Glutinous rice 30g (1 unit, can substitute with regular rice or leftover rice)  
🥚 Egg whites 2  
🥛 Whole milk 200g (6 units)  
🍬 Rock sugar 30–40g  
🍯 Sweetened condensed milk about 15g (two squeezes, can substitute with white sugar)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
⚙️ Blender/food processor  
⚖️ Kitchen scale (or a homemade measuring cup)  
🥣 Small bowls (about 300ml)  
🍲 Steamer  
🥄 Spoon, spatula  
🔖 Aluminum foil/plastic wrap  

## Instructions  
1. Beat the egg whites thoroughly until smooth with no lumps. Add whole milk and sweetened condensed milk, stir to combine, and skim off surface bubbles to make the egg milk mixture. Set aside.  
2. Place black sesame seeds, peanuts, and glutinous rice in a non-stick pan. Stir constantly over low heat for about 5 minutes, until the glutinous rice turns slightly yellow, the peanut skins crack, the sesame seeds release oil, and a nutty aroma emerges.  
**Key point**: Must be stir-fried over low heat until cooked and fragrant—this is crucial to remove bitterness; immediately transfer out of the pan after turning off the heat to avoid burning from residual heat.  

3. Pour the stir-fried dry ingredients into a blender, add 400ml of water, and blend on high speed for about 2 minutes until the mixture is very smooth with no obvious particles.  
4. Return the sesame paste to the non-stick pan, add rock sugar, and cook over low heat, stirring continuously with a spatula along the bottom. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until it bubbles and thickens, then turn off the heat.  
**Note**: Must stir constantly to prevent sticking; adjust cooking time to achieve desired thickness.  

5. Divide the black sesame paste into small bowls and let it sit until the surface forms a skin, which will help with layering later.  
6. Using the back of a spoon as a guide, gently pour the egg milk mixture along the side of the bowl onto the sesame paste, filling to about 70% full, taking care not to disturb the surface layer.  
7. Cover the bowl mouth with aluminum foil or plastic wrap and poke a few small holes. Once the steamer water is boiling and steaming, place the bowls inside. Immediately reduce to low heat and steam for 20 minutes, then turn off the heat and let it rest for another 10–15 minutes, until the center of the custard is fully set and the surface is smooth.  
**Key point**: Be sure to reduce to low heat after the water boils; high heat will cause the custard surface to become honeycomb-like. The resting time allows the center to cook through thoroughly, resulting in a more tender texture.  

## Health Tips  
Black sesame milk custard is rich in high-quality protein, calcium, and the antioxidant components of black sesame seeds. It is suitable for those needing hair nourishment and care, calcium supplementation, and brain health. Note: Those with egg white allergies should use with caution; diabetic patients should reduce the amount of rock sugar and sweetened condensed milk.
