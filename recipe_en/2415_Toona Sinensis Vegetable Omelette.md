# Toona Sinensis Vegetable Omelette
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Toona sinensis, to taste (chopped)
🍄 Mushrooms, to taste (sliced)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes, to taste (halved)
🥚 Eggs, 5 pieces (beaten and set aside)
🥓 Bacon, 3 slices (chopped)
🧅 Onion, to taste (chopped)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)
🧂 Salt, 1 small spoon
🧀 Parmesan cheese, to taste (grated)
⚫ Black pepper, to taste (ground and sprinkled)
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste (drizzled at the end)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (20cm)
🥄 Silicone spatula
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🧀 Grater

## Cooking Steps
1. Pre-cut all vegetables and bacon: chop the toona sinensis, slice the mushrooms, halve the cherry tomatoes, and chop the bacon.
   **Key Point**: All ingredients should be prepared in advance for quick subsequent operations.
2. Crack the 5 eggs into a bowl, add one small spoon of salt, and whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture is uniform.
   **Key Point**: The egg mixture must be fully beaten and pre-seasoned.
3. Heat the frying pan, add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and stir-fry until the onion softens and releases its fragrance.
   **Note**: First stir-fry the onion and garlic to establish the base flavor.
4. Add all the ingredients to the pan, and continue stir-frying until they are fully cooked and the moisture is completely evaporated.
   **Key Point**: The moisture from the ingredients must be evaporated before adding the egg mixture; this is crucial for a dry, firm texture in the final dish; otherwise, the omelette will release water and affect the consistency.
5. Pour the beaten egg mixture evenly into the pan, ensuring it completely covers all the ingredients.
6. Turn the heat to high, and use a silicone spatula to gently push from the edges toward the center, allowing the uncooked egg mixture to flow to the bottom and heat through. Continue pushing and stirring until the edges are set while the center remains runny.
   **Note**: This pushing and stirring method prevents the edges from burning while the center remains undercooked.
7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a lid, and simmer for about 5 minutes; alternatively, turn off the heat and let the residual heat of the pan cook the omelette until the egg mixture is completely set.
   **Key Point**: The method of cooking with residual heat has the highest success rate, keeping the eggs tender and preventing burning.
8. Slide the cooked omelette onto a plate, sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese and ground black pepper, and finally drizzle with an appropriate amount of chili oil before serving.
   **Note**: All seasoning and garnishing should be done after the omelette is removed from the pan to preserve the layers of flavor.

## Health Advice
Toona sinensis paired with eggs and bacon is rich in high-quality protein and spring seasonal antioxidants, suitable for muscle building or spring nourishment. Note to use less salt and oil to avoid excessive sodium intake from the bacon itself.
