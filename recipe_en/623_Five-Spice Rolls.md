# Five-Spice Rolls

## Cooking Steps
1. Finely mince the pork belly and shrimp separately. Crush the water chestnuts, then finely chop. Dice the shiitake mushrooms and celery into small pieces.
**Key point**: After crushing the water chestnuts, chop them finely to avoid large chunks affecting the texture.

2. Place all the chopped ingredients into a large bowl. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, Huadiao wine, sesame oil, white pepper powder, about 2 tbsp of cornstarch, chicken bouillon, and five-spice powder.
**Key point**: Five-spice powder is the core flavor and must be added; white pepper powder is also indispensable.

3. Use your hands to mix and repeatedly slap the filling against the bowl until it becomes sticky and cohesive, forming a ball that holds together without falling apart.
**Key point**: Slapping helps the meat mixture develop elasticity, resulting in a springier texture.

4. In a small bowl, crack the egg and add an appropriate amount of cornstarch. Stir into a smooth paste to use as the sealing glue for the tofu skin.
**Note**: The paste should be thick enough to adhere to the tofu skin but not too thin.

5. Spread out the tofu skin, evenly layer the filling on top, and roll it into a cylindrical log. Apply the egg-cornstarch paste at the end to seal it shut.
**Note**: Spread the filling evenly and ensure the seal is firm to prevent it from unraveling during frying.

6. Place the rolled five-spice rolls in a steamer and steam until the sealed ends are set.
**Purpose**: Pre-steaming stabilizes the rolls and prevents them from coming apart during frying. The steaming time depends on the size of the rolls and heat intensity.

7. Heat oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Gently place the steamed five-spice rolls into the oil and fry over medium-low heat until they turn golden brown and the outer skin is crispy.
**Control**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high, or the outside will burn while the inside remains raw.

8. Remove from the oil and let cool slightly. Cut into small sections and serve with Xiamen sweet chili sauce.

## Health Advice
These five-spice rolls are rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber. The refreshing crunch of water chestnuts helps balance the greasiness of the fried outer layer, making them suitable for those who seek flavor while maintaining a focus on health. Be mindful of controlling the oil temperature to avoid burning the tofu skin.
