# Childhood Spicy Skewers with Chicken Bones
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥡 Chicken frames 3 pieces (for making broth)
💧 Water 15 liters (for making broth, yields about 7.5 liters)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (sections, for broth, chili oil, and chicken bones)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (slices, for broth, chili oil, and chicken bones)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for broth, blanching chicken bones, and stewing)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili flakes 250 grams
🌶️ Xiaomila chili powder 100 grams
🌱 Sesame seeds appropriate amount (5g for chili oil, 4g for tofu skin sauce)
🫒 Rapeseed oil 500 grams
🧅 Onion 15 grams
🧄 Garlic 5 grams
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (for frying flavored oil)
🍬 White sugar 36 grams
🫘 Sweet bean paste 40 grams
🧂 Light soy sauce 30 grams (for tofu skin sauce), 12 grams (for chicken bone sauce)
🧂 Dark soy sauce 6 grams (for tofu skin sauce), 20 grams (for chicken bone sauce), 8 grams (for coloring during stewing)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 20 grams
🦪 Oyster sauce 80 grams (for tofu skin sauce), 15 grams (for chicken bone sauce)
🌿 Coarse cumin seeds 10 grams (for tofu skin sauce), 5 grams (for chicken bone sauce)
🌶️ Extra-fine non-spicy chili powder 10 grams (for tofu skin sauce)
🍲 Chicken essence 20 grams
🥣 Potato starch appropriate amount (mix with water at 1:4 ratio for slurry, for thickening tofu skin sauce and chicken bone sauce)
🫘 Dried tofu skin appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, mushrooms, potato slices, tofu puffs, and other vegetables appropriate amount
🍗 Chicken liver, chicken gizzards, chicken gizzard linings, and other meats appropriate amount

⭐ Star anise 3 grams
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder 1 gram
🫘 Xiangqi sauce (soybean paste) 10 grams

🧈 Chicken fat 5 grams

🌿 Crushed cumin (for final sprinkling on top)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍲 Large stockpot (for making broth)
🥘 Wok (for making chili oil, sauces, and stir-frying chicken bones)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Glass bowls (for mixing seasonings)
✂️ Scissors (for handling dried tofu skin)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam and removing residue)
🔪 Cutting board/workstation
🪣 Large stainless steel basin (for mixing spicy skewers)
🖌️ Brush (to help sauce evenly coat ingredients)
🍶 Thermos flask (for storing the secret sauce)
📐 Funnel (for pouring sauce into flask)
🥄 Silicone spatula or ladle (for stirring and stir-frying)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Making Chicken Bone Broth**
   Fill a large stockpot with 15 liters of water. Add 3 chicken frames, 50g scallion sections, 30g ginger slices, and 25g cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, thoroughly skim off any foam. Then, boil uncovered over the highest heat for 2 hours, until the liquid reduces to about 7.5 liters. Remove all solid ingredients and strain the broth for later use.
   **Key Point**: Keep the pot uncovered, which helps water evaporate and keeps the broth clear.

2. **Making Charred-Fragrant Chili Oil**
   In a bowl, mix 250g Erjingtiao chili flakes, 100g Xiaomila chili powder, and 5g sesame seeds. In a wok, pour 500g rapeseed oil. When the oil reaches 130°C (266°F), add 20g scallion sections, 10g ginger slices, 15g onion, 5g garlic, and an appropriate amount of cilantro. Fry slowly over low heat until the aromatics turn caramel brown, then remove and discard the solids. Heat the oil further to 230°C (446°F) until it smokes. Pour the hot oil directly over the prepared chili mixture and stir well.
   **Key Point**: This chili oil uses only fried vegetables and aromatics, no dry spices, to highlight the charred-fragrant flavor.

3. **Making Tofu Skin Spicy Skewer Sauce**
   In a bowl, mix 36g white sugar, 40g sweet bean paste, 30g light soy sauce, 6g dark soy sauce, 20g chicken bouillon, 80g oyster sauce, 10g coarse cumin seeds, 10g extra-fine non-spicy chili powder, 20g chicken essence, and 4g sesame seeds. Stir to combine. In a wok, add 1000g of the chicken bone broth and the mixed sauce. Bring to a boil and stir well. Prepare a slurry by mixing potato starch with water at a 1:4 ratio. Add the slurry to the boiling sauce in small increments, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens to a smooth, flowing consistency that coats the back of a spoon.
   **Key Point**: The thickening should not be too thick, otherwise it will clump; nor too thin, it needs a certain viscosity.

4. **Preparing Vegetables and Meat Ingredients**
   Cut dried tofu skin into small pieces, rinse with warm water, and squeeze out as much water as possible. Blanch wood ear mushrooms, mushrooms, potato slices, tofu puffs, and other vegetables separately. For chicken liver, chicken gizzards, and chicken gizzard linings, blanch them with scallion, ginger, Sichuan peppercorn, star anise, salt, and cooking wine to add a base flavor.
5. **Braising Chicken Bones**
   Soak 1 kg of chicken bones in cold water for 1 hour to remove blood. Place them in a pot with cold water and add 20g cooking wine to blanch. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then drain and rinse. In a wok, add 2 kg of the prepared chicken bone broth. Add 30g scallion, 15g ginger slices, 0.5g bay leaf, 1g Sichuan peppercorn, 3g star anise, 15g cooking wine, 1g thirteen-spice powder, 6g MSG, 8g salt, and 8g dark soy sauce. Stir to combine. Add the chicken bones, cover with a lid, and braise over low heat for 40 minutes until the chicken bones are tender and falling apart. Remove the chicken bones and reserve the braising liquid.
6. **Making Chicken Bone Sauce and Stir-Frying**
   In a bowl, mix 10g Xiangqi sauce, 50g high-fructose corn syrup, 5g chicken fat, 15g oyster sauce, 20g dark soy sauce, 12g light soy sauce, 8g chicken powder, 5g coarse crushed cumin, and 2g white pepper powder. Add 500g of the reserved braising liquid and stir to combine. Pour this sauce into a wok, add the braised chicken bones, and stir-fry until evenly coated. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of potato starch slurry to thicken, and continue stir-frying until the sauce becomes rich and clings tightly to the chicken bones.
7. **Final Mixing of Spicy Skewers**
   In a large basin, place the prepared tofu skin, vegetables, and meats. Pour in the prepared tofu skin spicy skewer sauce, add a spoonful of the charred-fragrant chili oil, and sprinkle with crushed cumin. Use a brush or gloved hands to repeatedly toss and coat, ensuring all gaps between ingredients are filled with the thick sauce.
   **Key Point**: The mixing technique is crucial to ensure the sauce evenly coats all the ingredients.

## Health Advice
Although the chicken bone broth and double sauces in this dish are rich and savory, they are high in sodium and fat. It is recommended to pair them with light vegetables or rice for balance. People with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
