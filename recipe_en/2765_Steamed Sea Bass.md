# Steamed Sea Bass

Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 sea bass (sea fish have fewer bones, suitable for deboning)
🫘 1 piece of silken tofu (sliced and used as a base)
🧅 Some scallions (whole for steaming to remove fishy smell, green parts shredded for "three shreds")
🫚 Some ginger (sliced for steaming to remove fishy smell, some shredded for "three shreds")
🌶️ A small amount of red chili pepper (shredded for garnish as part of "three shreds")
🧂 Some salt (to season the tofu and fish separately)
🫒 Some cooking oil (brushed on the fish before steaming to lock in moisture, then heated and drizzled after steaming to release aroma)
🫗 Some steamed fish soy sauce (drizzled along the edge of the plate)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
♨️ Steamer
🐟 Steaming plate
🖌️ Silicone oil brush
🥄 Large spoon
🥣 Bowl of water

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the sea bass**: Clean and gut the sea bass. Split it open along the backbone from the back, keeping the belly connected, and remove the main spine.
- **Key point**: Deboning reduces fish bones, making it more suitable for the elderly and children.

2. **Lay the base and season**: Cut the silken tofu into thick slices, lay them flat on the bottom of the steaming plate, and sprinkle with a little salt. Place the prepared fish on top of the tofu, sprinkle salt evenly on both sides, and gently rub it in.
- **Key point**: Salting the fish is the basic seasoning; steamed fish without salt will taste bland.

3. **Brush with oil to lock in moisture**: Use an oil brush to evenly coat the surface of the fish with a layer of cooking oil.
- **Key point**: Brushing with oil locks in moisture, resulting in steamed fish with a white color and non-dry texture.

4. **Prepare for deodorizing**: Place ginger slices and whole scallion sections on top of the fish.
- **Note**: These scallions and ginger are only used for deodorizing during steaming and should be removed afterward.

5. **Prepare the "three shreds"**: Roll the scallion greens tightly and cut into thin strips. Shred the ginger and red chili pepper into thin strips. Soak all strips in a bowl of water.
- **Key point**: Soaking makes the shreds curly and fluffy, with a crunchier texture.

6. **Steam**: Once the steamer water is boiling and producing steam, place the fish plate in and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.
- **Doneness check**: The fish eyes protrude and turn white; chopsticks can easily pierce through the flesh.
   - **Key point**: The fish must be added only after the water is boiling; time for exactly 6 minutes.

7. **Post-steaming treatment**: Remove the steamed fish plate, pour out most of the liquid from the plate, and discard the scallions and ginger used for steaming.
- **Key point**: Discarding the liquid removes fishy smells and prevents the finished dish from being too salty.

8. **Season and sizzle**: Drizzle the steamed fish soy sauce along the edge of the plate. Pile the soaked and drained "three shreds" on top of the fish. Heat one spoonful of cooking oil until smoking, then quickly and evenly pour it over the "three shreds."
- **Key point**: The order is important—first drizzle the soy sauce, then pour the hot oil. The high heat instantly releases the combined aroma of the soy sauce and the "three shreds."

## Health Advice
Steamed sea bass is low in fat and high in protein. Paired with silken tofu for added calcium, it is suitable for the elderly, children, and those on a weight loss diet. Be careful not to steam the fish for too long; 6 minutes locks in tenderness. Discarding the steaming liquid effectively reduces fishy smells and controls saltiness.
