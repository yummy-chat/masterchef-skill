# Golden Soup Beef Rice Noodles
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili 20g  
🥫 Yellow lantern chili sauce 60g  
🎃 Pumpkin (steamed and mashed) 70g  
🍜 Rice noodles, as needed  
🥩 Beef rolls, as needed  
🥬 Pickled mustard greens, as needed  
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed  
🫘 Tofu skin, as needed  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms, as needed  
🥗 Leafy greens, as needed  
🍲 Broth, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🌿 White pepper, as needed  
🧂 Chicken powder, as needed  
🍶 White vinegar, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫙 Blender or food processor  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
♨️ Steamer or steaming basket  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel and cut the pumpkin into chunks, steam until soft, then remove and mash into a puree.  
2. Place the bird’s eye chili, yellow lantern chili sauce, and pumpkin puree into a blender, and blend until smooth with no lumps.  
3. Heat oil in a wok over low heat, add the blended golden sauce paste, and stir continuously until the paste darkens in color and aroma is released. Splash in a little white vinegar, then remove and set aside.  
4. Bring broth to a boil in the wok, add about 2–3 tablespoons of the prepared golden sauce, and stir until the soup turns a clear golden color. Taste and season with salt, white pepper, and chicken powder.  
5. Add the harder-to-cook ingredients such as bean sprouts, tofu skin, enoki mushrooms, and pickled mustard greens, and cook until boiling.  
6. Add the rice noodles, and when they are nearly cooked, add the leafy greens and beef rolls. Once the beef changes color and the greens are just cooked, immediately turn off the heat and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This golden soup beef rice noodle dish is nutritionally rich. The pumpkin puree provides vitamin A, while the bean sprouts and enoki mushrooms add dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing to warm the stomach. Note that the beef rolls and chili sauce are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should limit consumption of the soup.
