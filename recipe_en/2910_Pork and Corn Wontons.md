# Pork and Corn Wontons
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Ground pork 3 jin (1.5 kg, 30% fat, 70% lean)
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms 7 (diced)
🧅 Scallion 1 (minced)
🫚 Ginger about half a palm size (minced or ground ginger powder)
🌽 Frozen sweet corn kernels as needed
🥟 Store-bought square wonton wrappers as needed
🫧 Dried seaweed as needed
🦐 Dried shrimp as needed
🧅 Scallion as needed (minced)
🌿 Cilantro as needed (minced)
🧄 Garlic as needed (minced)

**Seasonings**
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste
🧂 Dark soy sauce to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste
🍶 Cooking wine to taste
🫘 Sweet bean sauce to taste
🫒 Sesame oil to taste
🍬 White sugar to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste
🧂 Salt to taste
🍶 Vinegar to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste
🐖 Lard as needed
🫘 Sesame paste as needed
🥜 Peanut butter as needed
🌶️ Chili oil as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
⚪ Cutting board
🥣 Large mixing bowl
🥄 Measuring spoon
🍳 Stockpot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥄 Mesh strainer
🍜 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Scrub the mushroom caps clean, peel the outer layers of the scallion and trim the root, peel the ginger. Cut the mushrooms into thick slices, then dice. Slice the ginger, then cut into strips and mince. Mince the scallion.
   **Note**: If available, use a food processor to blend the scallion and ginger; ground ginger powder can also be used as a substitute.
2. Place the ground pork, all prepped vegetables, corn kernels, and all filling seasonings into a large bowl. Stir continuously in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and firm.
   **Note**: Taste the filling after mixing, and adjust salt as needed.
3. Lay one wonton wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center and press it flat. Dip a finger in water and moisten three edges of the wrapper, leaving one edge dry. Fold the wrapper in half and pinch the three moistened edges to seal. Apply a little water to one corner of the folded side, then curve the wonton into a crescent shape, bringing the two corners together and pinching them firmly to form a gold ingot shape.
   **Note**: Do not overfill the wontons, otherwise they will be difficult to cook through.
4. Prepare the dry dipping sauce: Place sesame paste, peanut butter, and 2 tablespoons of cooled boiled water in a bowl. Stir in one direction until the paste expands, lightens in color, and becomes smooth. Add the remaining dry dipping sauce seasonings and stir until well combined, achieving a yogurt-like consistency.
5. Prepare the soup base: Place all soup base seasonings, especially the lard, into a soup bowl.
6. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil over high heat. Add the wontons once the water is fully boiling. Immediately after adding the wontons, gently push them along the edge of the pot with the back of a ladle to keep the water moving and prevent sticking and tearing.
   **Note**: For larger wontons, add a small amount of cold water when the water boils and foams to calm it, repeating 3-4 times to ensure the filling is fully cooked. Small wontons are done once they float.
   **Doneness test**: Wontons are fully cooked when they are all floating and the wrappers are translucent.
7. Just before the wontons are done, scoop one ladle of boiling wonton water into the soup base bowl to dissolve the seasonings. Turn off the heat and use a mesh strainer to transfer the wontons into the bowl.
8. Using a mesh strainer, drain the wontons well, transfer them to a plate, pour over the prepared dry dipping sauce, mix well, and serve.

## Health Suggestions
Wontons made with 3 jin (1.5 kg) of pork are ideal for sharing with the whole family. It is recommended not to overindulge at once. Corn kernels add dietary fiber, and pairing with sweet bean sauce enhances the flavor of the filling, making it suitable for an occasional indulgence during weight loss.
