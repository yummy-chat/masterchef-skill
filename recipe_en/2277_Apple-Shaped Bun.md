# Apple-Shaped Bun
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Recommended in autumn, suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g
🌾 Wheat starch 40g
🧂 Baking powder 5g
🦠 Active dry yeast 3g
🟡 Custard powder 2g
🍬 Granulated sugar 10g
🐷 Lard 10g
💧 Water 130g
☕ Cocoa powder appropriate amount (about 1-2g)
🫒 Red beet appropriate amount
🍎 Apple filling appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board
📦 Plastic wrap
🪅 Rolling pin
🦷 Toothpick
🖌️ Brush
🔥 Steamer

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix all-purpose flour, baking powder, dry yeast, custard powder, granulated sugar, lard, and water in order. Custard powder and baking powder can be placed on one side of the flour pile, dissolved in water separately, and then kneaded into the dough.
**Key Point**: Custard powder must be dissolved in water before mixing to ensure uniform color; lard makes the finished product white and shiny; sugar promotes yeast fermentation.
2. Let the initially mixed dough rest and proof for about 10-15 minutes. After it has slightly risen, add wheat starch and knead thoroughly and evenly.
**Key Point**: Adding wheat starch reduces gluten strength, enhances malleability, and makes the finished product firmer and smoother; kneading must be thorough to ensure the dough is fine and free of lumps.
3. Blend Fuji apples into a puree, stir-fry in a wok until the moisture evaporates, finally add an appropriate amount of potato starch and mix well. Let it cool, then roll into small balls for later use.
**Key Point**: The filling must be stir-fried dry to facilitate wrapping; potato starch acts as a binder and thickener.
4. Roll the kneaded dough into a long strip. Tear off small dough pieces of about 20g each. Flatten them, place an apple filling ball in the center, and use a rotating motion with the tiger's mouth to seal the filling.
**Key Point**: Use a "pinch-peach" technique for wrapping, ensuring the dough evenly encloses the filling.
5. Roll the filled dough ball into a round shape, shaping it into an apple form slightly larger at the top and smaller at the bottom.
**Note**: Consider the proofing expansion when shaping; the form should be three-dimensional.
6. Use the head of a date-pit-shaped rolling pin to press a depression into the top center. Use a toothpick to press and rotate at the bottom center to create a calyx indentation.
**Note**: The depth of the indentation should be moderate.
7. Take a small piece of dough, gradually knead in cocoa powder until the color is right. Roll it into a thin strip slightly thinner than a toothpick, then cut into small sections for later use.
**Key Point**: Add cocoa powder gradually for color adjustment; the thin strip should be rolled slightly thinner than the final product, as it will expand during steaming.
8. First, place the apple stem raw piece in the steamer and steam for about 3-4 minutes until cooked. Then place the main apple body raw pieces in the steamer and steam over high heat for 7-8 minutes.
**Key Point**: The apple stem is small, so the steaming time is short; the main apple body should be steamed over high heat, not too long.
9. After steaming, open the lid and, while hot, use a toothpick to reshape the bottom indentation.
**Key Point**: Reshape while hot for easier setting.
10. Use a brush to dip into red beet juice and layer it onto the surface of the apple bodies that have cooled to warm, imitating the color and texture of apple skin. Finally, insert the steamed and cooled apple stems into the top depressions.
**Key Point**: Red beet juice is a natural pigment; coloring requires patience and layering for a three-dimensional effect; the apple stems should be inserted after the main bodies have slightly cooled.

## Health Advice
This apple-shaped pastry uses red beet juice for coloring and natural fillings, suitable for those managing sugar intake or seeking creative shapes. However, the content of wheat starch and lard is not low, so it is best enjoyed with light tea. Also, ensure the filling is stir-fried dry enough; otherwise, it may collapse after steaming.
