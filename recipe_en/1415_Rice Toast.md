# Rice Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice 300g (recipe for a 250g toast mold)
🍯 Honey 12g (provides sweetness)
🍞 Fresh yeast 15g (or substitute with 5g dry yeast)
🧂 Salt 4g (balances flavor, strengthens structure)
🧊 Ice cubes + water 248g (total weight, include water absorbed by soaked rice; use ice cubes to control temperature)
🫒 Vegetable oil 31g
🍫 Cocoa powder 10g (additional for chocolate version, dissolve in warm water first to prevent clumping)
💧 Warm water 20g (for dissolving cocoa powder)
🍫 Dark chocolate 20g (chopped into flakes or small pieces)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Measuring cup/bowl
🔗 Strainer
🔪 Blender
🥄 Silicone spatula
📦 Toast mold (250g, line with oil cloth or parchment paper to prevent sticking)
🫙 Plastic wrap
📜 Parchment paper
🥢 Chopsticks or bamboo skewer
♨️ Oven
🔲 Cooling rack

## Instructions
1. Place the rice in a container and rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear to remove surface starch.
2. Add enough fresh water to the rinsed rice and soak for at least 30 minutes, until the grains expand and turn white.
3. Drain the soaked rice and weigh it.
4. Add the weighed soaked rice, honey, yeast, salt, vegetable oil, ice cubes, and water to the blender.
   **Note**: Adding ice cubes helps control the blending temperature. If making the chocolate version, do not add the cocoa mixture at this step.
5. Blend on high speed for 3–4 minutes until the rice batter is smooth and fine.
   **Note**: Blending generates heat; ensure the final batter temperature is below 40°C to prevent yeast deactivation. If using a low-power blender requiring more time, add the yeast after blending once the batter has cooled to 30°C, then blend for another minute.
6. Cover with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot for the first fermentation, until the batter expands to 2.5 times its volume and shows a honeycomb-like internal structure. When poked with a finger, the hole should not shrink back or collapse.
7. Use a silicone spatula to stir the fermented batter thoroughly until most air bubbles are released and the batter becomes smooth again.
8. Line a 250g toast mold with oil cloth or parchment paper, then pour the degassed batter into the mold.
   **Note**: After pouring, use chopsticks to swirl and smooth the surface, and gently tap the mold to release air bubbles. For the chocolate version, mix a portion of the batter with the cocoa powder dissolved in warm water, then use chopsticks to create a marble pattern.
9. Place the mold in a warm, humid place for the second fermentation until the batter rises to 90% of the mold height.
   **Note**: After fermentation, lightly spray the surface with water to prevent the crust from cracking during baking, then cover with a folded piece of parchment paper.
10. Baking: Reference temperature and time: bake at 165°C in a convection oven for 22 minutes, or at 200°C top heat and 180°C bottom heat in a conventional oven for 30 minutes.
    **Note**: After baking, remove the top parchment paper to check the surface color. Remove from oven once satisfied.
11. Immediately invert the mold to unmold the toast, and quickly remove the oil cloth from all sides to prevent moisture buildup that may cause sogginess.
12. Place the toast on a cooling rack, loosely cover with plastic wrap leaving some ventilation gaps, and let it cool naturally to room temperature.

## Health Tips
Rice toast is gluten-free and suitable for those with gluten intolerance. Its high fiber and low protein content benefit people with sensitive digestion. Always use ice cubes to control the batter temperature during blending to prevent the yeast from deactivating due to overheating.
