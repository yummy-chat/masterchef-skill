# Scalloped Potatoes
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 500g (large yellow-skinned, either waxy or floury)
🥛 Milk as needed (mixed with cream at a 1:3 ratio)
🧈 Cream as needed (mixed with milk at a 1:3 ratio)
🥓 Bacon as needed (smoked flavor recommended)
🧀 Mozzarella cheese generous amount
🧂 Salt as needed
🌶️ Black pepper as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🔥 Oven
🫓 Baking dish (28×19cm, 4cm high)
📜 Parchment paper
🔪 Peeler
🫚 Slicer
🗡️ Knife, cutting board
🥄 Measuring cup, spoon
🧂 Grinder
⚖️ Weight (e.g., a grater, for pressing down)

## Instructions
1. Peel the potatoes, then slice them into even thin slices using a slicer. Both waxy and floury potato varieties are fine; using a slicer improves efficiency.
   **Key point**: Evenly thick slices help ensure uniform heating.

2. Mix the cream and milk at a 1:3 ratio, stirring well to make the sauce. If you prefer a richer dairy flavor, you can increase the proportion of cream.

3. Cut the bacon into strips and set aside.

4. Line the baking dish with parchment paper. Arrange the potato slices neatly in an overlapping pattern (like fish scales), seasoning each layer with salt and black pepper. You can place uneven pieces at the bottom and whole slices on top for a better appearance.

5. Sprinkle the bacon strips over the potatoes, then pour the prepared cream and milk sauce evenly over the top.

6. Cover the surface with another sheet of parchment paper, place a weight on top to press down, and bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for about 2 hours. Using a weight helps set the shape, creates a slightly enclosed environment, and locks in moisture, resulting in a soft and tender texture.
   **Key point**: Pressing down with a weight prevents the potato slices from shifting and keeps the final dish neat.

7. After the first bake, remove from the oven, take off the top parchment paper and weight, and evenly sprinkle a generous amount of mozzarella cheese over the potato surface.

8. Preheat the oven to its highest temperature, place the dish back in, and bake for 10-13 minutes, until the cheese reaches a desirable golden-brown color. Monitor closely during baking to prevent the cheese from burning; oven temperatures vary, so adjust as needed.
   **Key point**: Baking at high heat for a short time allows the cheese to brown quickly without overheating the interior.

## Health Advice
Potatoes are nutritious on their own, but adding cream, bacon, and a generous amount of cheese significantly increases the calorie content. It is recommended to serve with a refreshing salad to balance nutrition. This dish is suitable for those needing high energy or building muscle; those managing their weight should consume it in moderation.
