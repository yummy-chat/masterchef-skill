# It Looks Like Honey (Ta Si Mi)

Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb hind leg meat, about 200g (choose the rump part, remove fascia)  
🥚 Egg whites, 2 pieces  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for marinating and sliding)  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (drizzle before serving)  
🧂 Ginger water, as needed (to remove gaminess and reduce greasiness)  
🧂 Soy sauce, as needed  
🍶 Vinegar, as needed (to cut greasiness, not detectable as sour)  
🍬 White sugar, about 70g  
🌾 Potato starch water, as needed (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Skimmer  
🥄 Ladle/wok spatula  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the lamb**: Remove all visible fascia from the lamb rump meat. Slice diagonally into even, willow-leaf-shaped pieces.
*Key point*: Choose lean meat and thoroughly remove tendons; slicing diagonally into willow-leaf shape aids even cooking and flavor absorption.

2. **Marinate the meat**: Add egg whites to the meat slices. Mix by hand until tacky. Add a pinch of salt for seasoning, then sprinkle in cornstarch and mix until each slice is thinly coated. Finally, drizzle a little cooking oil and mix to prevent sticking.
*Key point*: Egg whites add tenderness and stickiness; the starch layer should be thin; oil sealing helps the meat slide apart without sticking.

3. **Prepare the sauce**: In a bowl, combine ginger water, soy sauce, vinegar, and white sugar. Stir to mix, then add the potato starch water and stir again until well combined.
*Key point*: Sweetness should be prominent; sugar quantity must be generous. Vinegar only reduces greasiness and must not be detectable as sour. Potato starch yields a thicker, more stable slurry.

4. **Slide in oil**: Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok to 60–70% hot. Scatter the marinated meat slices in one by one. Gently push and separate with a ladle. Once the meat changes color, is cooked through, and floats, remove and drain oil.
*Key point*: Oil temperature must be high enough to quickly slide the meat and keep it tender.

5. **Pour sauce and stir-fry**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce. Cook over high heat until it boils and thickens. Immediately add the slid meat slices. Stir-fry quickly to coat the meat evenly with the sauce.
*Key point*: Add the meat only after the sauce has thickened. Stir just enough to coat, preserving the meat’s tenderness.

6. **Finish and serve**: Drizzle sesame oil onto the inner wall of a heated wok spatula, then add the spatula and oil into the wok. Stir-fry quickly two or three times, then remove from heat and plate immediately.
*Key point*: Heating the sesame oil on the spatula releases its aroma more effectively; adding it just before serving enhances the flavor.

## Health Advice  
This dish features tender lamb, but has a high sugar content. It is suitable for occasional consumption. Individuals with high blood sugar or those on a weight-loss diet should control their portion size. Lamb helps nourish the body and warm it, while egg whites provide high-quality protein; however, the 70g of white sugar contributes significant calories.
