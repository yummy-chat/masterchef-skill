# Pork and Green Pepper Strips
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

🐖 Pork shoulder (适量, cut into strips 3-4 cm long and 1.5 cm wide)
🫑 Green pepper (适量, sliced to similar size as pork strips)
🧂 Potato starch (适量, soaked in water in advance, let settle, then pour off the top water and use the wet starch at the bottom)
🧄 Scallion (适量, minced)
🫚 Ginger (适量, minced)
🧄 Garlic (适量, minced)
🍶 Shaoxing wine (a small amount for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for sauce)
🧂 Salt (a small amount for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for sauce)
🧂 White pepper powder (a small amount for marinating)
🫒 Soybean oil (a small amount for coating the batter)
🍬 Sugar (a small amount for sauce)
🧂 MSG (or chicken bouillon) (a small amount for sauce)
🍾 Rice vinegar (a small amount for sauce)
🫗 Soy sauce (a small amount for sauce)
💧 Water (a small amount for sauce, to adjust consistency)
🧊 Water starch (a small amount, made from the prepared wet starch mixed with water, for thickening the sauce)
🫗 Flavored oil (a small amount, drizzled in before removing from heat, such as Sichuan peppercorn oil or a compound sesame oil)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowls (multiple, for soaking, marinating, coating, and mixing sauce)
Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥢 Chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)
🥄 Wok spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Soak and squeeze**: Soak the pork shoulder strips in clean water for about 1 hour, changing the water several times. Remove and squeeze out the blood water thoroughly to eliminate any gamey smell.
2. **Marinate for base flavor**: Add a small amount of Shaoxing wine, white pepper powder, and salt to the pork strips. Mix well and let marinate for 10 minutes.
3. **Coat with wet starch**: Add the settled wet potato starch to the pork strips. Use your hands to mix until each piece is evenly coated. Then drizzle in a small amount of soybean oil and mix again.
4. **Prepare the sauce in advance**: In a bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine, 1 tablespoon of salt, a small amount of sugar, MSG, rice vinegar, soy sauce, a small amount of water, and wet starch. Stir well and set aside.
5. **First fry to set shape**: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until it reaches about 50-60% hot (around 160-180°C). Carefully add the pork strips one by one. Fry over high heat until the surface turns light yellow and the strips become firm. Remove them with a slotted spoon.
6. **Second fry to cook through**: Maintain the oil temperature at about 50% hot (around 160°C), then reduce to medium heat. Return all the pork strips to the wok and fry for about 5 minutes, until tapping them produces a crisp sound and they are fully cooked inside. Remove them.
7. **Third fry to color and render oil**: Increase the heat to high and quickly fry the strips for about 1 minute, until they turn a golden, shiny reddish-brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.
**Note**: The three-time frying process is key to achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior. Do not simplify the sequence.
8. **Quick-fry the green pepper**: Place the green pepper slices in a slotted spoon. Briefly dip them in the hot oil for a few seconds, then remove immediately to keep them bright green and crunchy.
9. **Stir-fry and pour in the sauce**:
   - Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the minced scallion, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the fried pork strips and green pepper, and quickly stir to combine.
   - Stir the prepared sauce again, then pour it along the heated edge of the wok. Immediately stir-fry over high heat, until the sauce evenly coats all the ingredients.
   - Drizzle a small amount of flavored oil along the wok edge to enhance the aroma. Stir-fry for a few more seconds, then remove from heat immediately.
**Key**: Use high heat throughout. Pouring the sauce along the wok edge helps release its aroma. Work quickly to keep the pork strips crispy.

## Health Tips
Pork and green pepper strips are rich in high-quality protein and vitamin C, making them suitable for those looking to build muscle or boost energy. However, due to the three frying steps, the dish is relatively high in calories; it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Be mindful of salt and soy sauce usage; individuals with high blood pressure should consume with caution.
