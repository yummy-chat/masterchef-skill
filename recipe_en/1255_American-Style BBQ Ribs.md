# American-Style BBQ Ribs  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Year Round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork ribs, about 8 ribs (adjust according to number of people)  
🧅 Onion, half  
🧄 Garlic, 2 cloves  
🧂 Salt, 15g  
🌶️ Black pepper, 10g  
🌶️ Chili powder, 3-5g  
🍯 BBQ sauce, 4 tablespoons (use your preferred brand)  
🍬 Granulated sugar, 1 tablespoon  
🥫 Worcestershire sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🌶️ Tabasco sauce, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔥 Oven  
🌀 Food processor/blender  
🥘 Baking tray  
🔩 Aluminum foil  
🖌️ Basting brush  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowl and spoon  

## Steps  

### Preparation Phase  
1. **Make dry rub base**  
   - Finely chop half an onion and 2 cloves of garlic.  
   - Place in the oven and bake at 100-150°C until dry and brittle.  
   - **Note**: If you already have garlic powder and onion powder, you can skip this step and mix the powders directly.  

2. **Prepare the pork ribs**  
   - Remove the white membrane from the underside of the ribs.  
   - **Key point**: Removing the membrane allows the ribs to absorb flavor better and makes them easier to pull apart when eating.  

3. **Make the dry rub mixture**  
   - Place the dried chopped onion, garlic, salt, black pepper, and chili powder in a food processor and grind into a powder.  
   - **Note**: If using ready-made powdered spices, simply mix them together; no need to grind.  

4. **Make the wet sauce**  
   - In a bowl, mix BBQ sauce, granulated sugar, Worcestershire sauce, and Tabasco sauce until well combined.  
   - **Note**: Adjust the amounts of sugar and Tabasco sauce to your taste.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. **Apply dry rub and first bake**  
   - Evenly rub the dry mixture onto both sides of the ribs.  
   - Wrap the ribs completely in aluminum foil and place in the oven.  
   - **Temperature/Time**: Bake at 180°C for 1.5 hours, or slow-bake at 150°C for 2.5 hours.  
   - **Doneness check**: The ribs are done when the meat is tender and easily pulls away from the bone with your fingers.  
   - **Key point**: The foil must be completely sealed to create a steaming environment that cooks the meat thoroughly.  

2. **Remove and prepare for saucing**  
   - Take the baked ribs out of the oven and open the foil.  
   - Turn the ribs over so the underside faces up.  

3. **Apply sauce and second bake**  
   - Use a brush to evenly coat the underside of the ribs with sauce.  
   - Flip the ribs over and coat the top side with sauce.  
   - Place in the oven and bake at 230°C for 15 minutes.  
   - **Key point**: Coating the underside first prevents sauce from sticking to the foil and wasting it when flipping.  

4. **Apply sauce again and third bake**  
   - Remove the ribs and brush the remaining sauce only on the top side.  
   - Bake again at 230°C for 15 minutes.  
   - **Key point**: Only apply sauce to the top side this time to create a caramelized, flavorful crust.  

5. **Finish and serve**  
   - Once baked, remove from oven and pull the ribs apart by hand.  
   - **Key point**: Pulling apart by hand gives a better texture than cutting with a knife and offers a more satisfying eating experience.  

## Health Tips  
These American-style BBQ ribs are rich in high-quality protein and iron, making them suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those with high physical energy expenditure. However, BBQ sauce is high in sugar, so it is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance calories. Removing the membrane makes the meat more tender. For those allergic to chili, paprika can be used as a substitute for chili powder.
