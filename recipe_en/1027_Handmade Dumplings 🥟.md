# Handmade Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟Ingredients  
- Flour 200g  
- Cold water 150ml  
- Lean ground meat 250g  
- Fatty ground meat 20g (optional)  
- Ginger 3g  
- Scallion 15g  
- Salt 3g  
- Egg 1 (use egg white only)  
- Chinese chives (to taste)  

🧂Seasonings  
- Sesame oil 2-3ml  
- Oyster sauce 2ml  
- Light soy sauce 2ml  
- Sesame oil 2ml  

🔧Utensils  
- Rolling pin  
- Kitchen knife (two recommended)  
- Bowl with diameter of 30cm or larger  
- Bowl  
- Cooking pot  

## Steps
1.  Place all the flour in a bowl, add sesame oil, and make a small well in the center of the flour.
2.  Add cold water in 4 to 5 batches, stirring as you go. Stop adding water when the flour forms crumbs and appears slightly dry.
3.  Press the dough together with your hands and knead until it picks up the remaining flour from the sides of the bowl, achieving a clean bowl and smooth dough.
4.  Place the dough on a work surface, cover with the upturned bowl, and let it rest at about 25°C for approximately 45 minutes.
   **Key point**: Recommended resting temperature is 25°C, resting time about 45 minutes.
5.  After resting, roll the dough into a long strip, then knead it back into a ball, roll into a strip again; repeat this process 3 times.
6.  Roll the dough into an even long strip and divide it into 20 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball about 3–3.5cm in diameter.
7.  Flatten each small dough ball. Dust your hands, the work surface, the rolling pin, and the dough surface with a little dry flour to prevent sticking.
8.  Use the rolling pin to roll each dough piece into a round wrapper about 8cm in diameter and 2mm thick, with the center slightly thicker (about 1mm more) than the edges.
   **Key point**: A slightly thicker center prevents the wrapper from breaking when filled.
9.  Place the prepared lean ground meat and fatty ground meat together in a bowl.
10. Finely chop the scallion and ginger, add them to the meat bowl, and mix evenly.
11. Wash the Chinese chives and cut them into pieces shorter than 3mm. Mix with the meat. Then add 2ml each of oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and sesame oil, plus 1 egg white. Stir clockwise with your hand until well combined, and let sit for 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
12. Hold a wrapper in your left hand, with the side facing up as free from flour as possible. Use chopsticks in your right hand to take an amount of filling roughly half the diameter of the wrapper and place it in the center.
13. Fold the wrapper in half along the edge, pinch the seam tightly to seal, ensuring no filling leaks out. There is no need to pleat the edge.
14. Fill a cooking pot with water to about 3/4 of its height. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat, then add the dumplings and reduce the heat to medium.
15. After the water starts bubbling again, add 50ml of cold water along the edge of the pot. Repeat this operation twice. After the third boil, add another 50ml of cold water. Once it boils again, reduce the heat to low and continue cooking for 60 seconds. Then remove the dumplings with a strainer, drain, and plate.
   **Key point**: Do not cover the pot while cooking dumplings. Adding cold water three times prevents the dumplings from boiling continuously, which could cause the wrappers to break.

## Health Advice
Handmade dumplings offer a balanced mix of meat and vegetables, providing protein and dietary fiber. Pay attention to the ratio of chives to lean meat to avoid digestive discomfort from overeating. Suitable for the whole family, but those with high blood pressure should use less soy sauce as a dip.
