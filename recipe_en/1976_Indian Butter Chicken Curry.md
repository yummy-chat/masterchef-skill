# Indian Butter Chicken Curry
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken Thighs (boneless)  
🥛 Yogurt  
🍅 Tomato  
🧅 Onion  
🫚 Ginger  
🧄 Garlic  
🥜 Cashew Nuts  
🥛 Heavy Cream  
💧 Water  
🧈 Butter  
🍋 Lemon Juice  
🌶️ Red Chili Powder  
🟡 Turmeric Powder  
🌿 Coriander Powder  
🧂 Cumin Powder  
🧂 Garam Masala  
🫘 Cardamom Powder  
🌿 Dried Fenugreek Leaves  
🍬 Sugar  
🧂 Salt  
🫒 Cooking Oil  

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🫓 Frying Pan  
🥤 Blender/Food Processor  
🕸️ Strainer  

## Steps  

### Marinating the Chicken  
1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger paste, garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, salt, and lemon juice until well combined.  
2. Add chicken thigh pieces and coat thoroughly with the marinade.  
3. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.  

### Grilling the Chicken  
4. Heat 15ml of cooking oil in a frying pan over high heat.  
5. Place the marinated chicken pieces in the pan and sear until golden brown on both sides, about 3 minutes per side. The chicken does not need to be fully cooked through.  
6. Remove and set aside.  

### Making the Sauce Base  
7. In a pot, add tomato chunks, onion chunks, cashew nuts, ginger, garlic, and 100ml of water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 15 minutes, until all ingredients are completely soft.  
8. Allow to cool slightly, then transfer to a blender and blend into a smooth, fine puree.  
9. Strain through a strainer to remove solids, resulting in a smooth sauce. Set aside.  

### Simmering  
10. In a wok, melt 50g of butter over medium heat.  
11. Pour in the strained tomato sauce, add 5g red chili powder, 5g coriander powder, 3g cumin powder, and 5g salt. Stir-fry for 3–5 minutes, until the sauce darkens in color and oil begins to separate.  
12. Add the grilled chicken pieces and mix well.  
13. Add 10g of sugar and stir.  
14. Pour in 100ml of heavy cream, stir to combine, and simmer over low heat for 10–12 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked and has absorbed the sauce.  
15. Add 3g garam masala and 2g cardamom powder, sprinkle in 3g dried fenugreek leaves, stir to combine, then turn off the heat.  
16. Plate and optionally drizzle a small amount of heavy cream on top as garnish.  

## Health Tips  
This Indian butter chicken curry is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those in need of an energy boost. However, due to its high butter and heavy cream content, it is recommended to serve with whole wheat bread or brown rice and to control portion sizes.
