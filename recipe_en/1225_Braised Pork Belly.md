# Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (with alternating fat and lean layers)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (tied into knots)
🍺 Beer, appropriate amount (enough to just cover the ingredients)
💧 Water, appropriate amount (add until fully submerged)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for color)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for seasoning)
🫘 Fermented soybean paste, appropriate amount (to enhance savory flavor and color)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (to balance the base flavor)
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount (to enhance freshness)
🧂 Braising spice packet, 1 packet (contains over a dozen types of spices)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for blanching)
🥘 Stockpot or stew pot (for braising)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🥄 Soup ladle
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the pork belly and slice into even-thick pieces.
   **Note**: Choose pork belly with alternating fat and lean layers.
2. Place the pork belly slices in cold water, bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then remove and drain.
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps fully release blood and gamey odors, preventing the meat from darkening during braising.
3. In the stockpot, heat a little oil, add ginger slices and scallion knots, and sauté over low heat until fragrant.
   **Note**: Sautéing releases the aroma of ginger and scallion, helping to remove gamey flavors and enhance fragrance.
4. Add the blanched pork belly slices to the pot, then pour in beer until the ingredients are just covered.
5. Add enough water to fully submerge all ingredients.
   **Key point**: Add all liquid at once; do not add water midway.
6. Add dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, fermented soybean paste, salt, and sugar in order.
   **Note**: Dark soy sauce is mainly for color; fermented soybean paste enhances savory flavor and color; salt and sugar balance the base flavor.
7. Add one packet of braising spice.
   **Key point**: A pre-mixed spice packet simplifies the process and enriches the flavor profile.
8. Bring the liquid to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover with a lid, and simmer for 40 minutes, until the pork belly is tender and flavorful.
9. Remove the lid, take out the braised pork belly, and serve.
   **Note**: The remaining braising liquid can be saved for braising other ingredients like tofu skin or eggs next time.

## Health Advice
Braised pork belly is rich in high-quality protein and fat, suitable for those needing an energy boost; it is recommended to pair with vegetables to balance the oil. Limit each serving to 100-150 grams.
