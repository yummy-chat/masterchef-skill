# Pork Ribs with Black Bean Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs 500g (cut into small pieces, soak to remove blood, and pat dry)
🧄 Garlic 25g (finely minced)
🫘 Fermented black beans 10g (choose those without salt crystals on the surface)
🎃 Pumpkin 250g (peeled and cut into large cubes; can substitute with taro, sweet potato, or potato)
🌶️ Green chili pepper 10g (sliced into rings, for garnish)
🌶️ Red chili pepper 10g (sliced into rings, for garnish)
🧅 Scallion 5g (chopped, for garnish)
🫒 Cooking oil 35g
🧂 Salt 4.5g
🍬 White sugar 10g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g
🧂 MSG 3g
💧 Water 75g
🥄 Light soy sauce 5g
🌾 Potato starch 25g
🫒 Sesame oil 5g

## Utensils & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥄 Spatula/measuring spoon
🧻 Paper towels
💧 Measuring cup
♨️ Steamer/steaming pot
🍽️ Plate/steaming dish

## Instructions
1. Cut the pork ribs into small pieces and soak in cold water for at least 30 minutes until the blood has fully seeped out. Remove and rinse, then pat dry thoroughly with paper towels.
**Note**: Do not blanch; rely on soaking to remove the raw smell. The surface must be dry, otherwise it will affect the subsequent absorption of liquid.

2. Flatten the garlic cloves with a knife, then mince finely. Prepare the fermented black beans. Peel and cut the pumpkin into large cubes, then spread them on the bottom of the plate. Slice the green and red chili peppers into rings, and chop the scallion. Set aside.

3. Pour 35g of oil into a wok. When slightly warm, add the minced garlic and fermented black beans. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 1 minute until fragrant. Turn off the heat and remove the mixture along with the oil. Set aside.
**Note**: Stir-fry over medium heat slowly to avoid burning.

4. Add the salt and white sugar to the dried ribs. Mix with your hands until the sugar and salt are completely dissolved and the ribs feel sticky and tacky. Then add the chicken bouillon and MSG, and continue to mix until dissolved.

5. Add 75g of cold water in 5 portions. Each time, add a small amount and mix in one direction until the water is fully absorbed before adding the next portion.
**Key**: The water must be added in portions and fully absorbed each time. The final surface of the ribs should be smooth and appear to have a film, with no standing water at the bottom of the bowl.

6. Add the light soy sauce and mix well. Then add the potato starch and vigorously mix for about 20 seconds, ensuring each piece of rib is evenly coated with a slurry.
**Note**: The starch forms a protective layer to lock in moisture.

7. Pour in all of the fried garlic and black bean oil mixture along with the sesame oil. Mix for about 1 minute to seal in the moisture and enhance the aroma.

8. Arrange the seasoned ribs evenly on top of the pumpkin layer in the plate. Bring water in a steamer to a full boil. Place the plate of ribs into the steamer, cover, and steam over high heat for 30 minutes.
**Note**: Place the ribs into the steamer only after the water has come to a full boil. Steam over high heat with sufficient steam. The time can be slightly adjusted based on the size of the rib pieces.

9. After steaming, use chopsticks to poke the ribs. If the bones protrude visibly, they are fully cooked. A clear broth indicates success, while a cloudy and sticky broth indicates failure. Remove from the steamer and garnish with the green and red chili pepper rings and chopped scallions.

## Health Advice
The combination of pork ribs and pumpkin provides high-quality protein and dietary fiber. However, fermented black beans are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure can moderately reduce the amount of salt. Pan-frying the garlic not only adds flavor but also aids digestion, making it suitable for those with weak spleens and stomachs.
