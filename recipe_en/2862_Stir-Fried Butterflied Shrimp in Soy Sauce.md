# Stir-Fried Butterflied Shrimp in Soy Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 300g prawns (whiskers removed, butterflied, deveined)
🧅 Appropriate amount of onion (cut into pieces, mainly for color)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of red bell pepper (cut into pieces, mainly for color)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (minced)
🫚 Appropriate amount of scallions (cut into sections)
🧈 A small piece of butter
🌽 Appropriate amount of cornstarch (for coating the shrimp before frying)
🍬 Appropriate amount of sugar
🦪 Appropriate amount of oyster sauce
🥫 Approximately 20g soy sauce
🍶 Appropriate amount of cooking wine (slightly more in quantity)
💧 Appropriate amount of cornstarch slurry

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
✂️ Scissors
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🥘 Strainer spoon
🔥 High-heat stove

## Instructions
1. Prepare the prawns: Cut off the whiskers. Use a knife or scissors to cut open the back of the shrimp and remove the vein.
2. Prepare the vegetables: Cut the onion and red bell pepper into pieces. Cut the scallions into sections. Mince the garlic.
3. Prepare the sauce mixture: In a bowl, combine sugar, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and cooking wine. Finally, add a small amount of cornstarch slurry and stir until well mixed. Set aside.
4. Coat the shrimp with starch: Just before cooking, evenly coat the butterflied shrimp with a thin layer of cornstarch.
- The shrimp must be coated with starch right before cooking to prevent the moisture from the shrimp from wetting the starch, which would affect the crispy texture.
5. Flash-fry the shrimp: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until it starts to smoke slightly. Add the coated shrimp, gently stirring to prevent sticking, and quickly flash-fry.
- High oil temperature instantly seals in the shrimp's moisture, achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.
6. Flash-fry the vegetables: Once the shrimp skin is crispy, add the onion and red bell pepper pieces to the oil for a brief flash-fry. Once they are just cooked, remove them along with the shrimp and drain the oil.
7. Sauté the aromatics: Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add a small piece of butter and melt it. Then add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
- The butter is used to add a layer of complex aroma.
8. Return and stir-fry: Return all the flash-fried ingredients to the wok and stir-fry.
- At this point, you can briefly remove the wok from the heat source to prevent the residual heat from burning the garlic.
9. Add sauce and finish: Pour the prepared sauce mixture along the edge of the wok. Immediately turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the sauce evenly coats all the ingredients and its aroma is released. Serve immediately.
- Stir-frying quickly over high heat fully releases the aroma of the sauce.

## Health Tips
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat, but the starch coating, deep-frying, and butter sautéing increase the calorie count. It is recommended to control portion size and pair with vegetables. Suitable for occasional consumption by those looking to build muscle. Individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soy sauce used.
