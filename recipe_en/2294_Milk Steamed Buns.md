# Milk Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥛 Cold milk 190g (taken from the refrigerator)
🍞 Yeast 1 teaspoon (about 3g)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🥛 Measuring cup
🥄 Spoon
🔪 Scraper
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Knife (for dividing dough)
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🧺 Steamer basket (bamboo, with parchment paper)
🍲 Steamer pot
🫓 Rolling pin

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour cold milk into a bowl, add yeast, and stir until dissolved.
   **Key Point**: Using cold milk can inhibit fermentation before shaping, making the bun surface smoother.
2. Add all-purpose flour, stir, and knead into a dough. No need to knead until smooth; just ensure the dry and wet ingredients are evenly mixed.
3. Cover the bowl and let it rest for 5 minutes.
4. Place the rested dough on a cutting board, roll it into a long strip, and evenly divide it into 8 small pieces.
5. Take one small piece, gather the edges towards the center, and pinch to form a round shape.
6. Use a rolling pin to roll the round dough into a long tongue shape, fold both ends towards the center, roll out again, and repeat the folding once more.
7. Flip the folded dough over, pinch the opposite corners together, place the seam side down, and shape into a smooth round bun blank.
   **Key Point**: The repeated folding steps can change the arrangement of gluten strands, enhance structural strength, and prevent collapse.
8. Place all bun blanks into a steamer basket lined with parchment paper, cover with a lid, and let ferment for 1 to 1.5 hours until significantly increased in volume and the surface slowly rebounds when lightly pressed.
   **Key Point**: In the last half hour of fermentation, open the steamer lid to allow the skin to form a thin crust, which helps with setting during steaming.
9. Bring water to a boil in a steamer pot, place the steamer basket on top, and steam over the lowest heat for about 15 minutes.
10. After steaming, turn off the heat, keep the lid on, and let rest for 5 minutes before removing the lid and taking out the buns.

## Health Tips
Using cold milk to mix the dough can slow down fermentation, resulting in a smoother bun surface. These buns are not sweet or oily, suitable as a side dish for meals or as a light snack. Pay attention to controlling fermentation humidity to avoid wrinkling on the surface.
