# Crispy Mung Bean Pastry 🥮
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Peeled mung beans 400g (soak for 2-4 hours or overnight in advance)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 110g (for water-oil dough)  
🍰 Cake flour 90g (for oil dough)  
🐷 Lard 85g (40g for water-oil dough + 45g for oil dough)  
🌽 Corn oil 100g (for stir-frying mung bean filling)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 200g (180g for filling + 20g for water-oil dough)  
🧂 Salt 2g  
💧 Boiling water approx. 45g (for water-oil dough)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🍲 Cooking pot  
♨️ Steamer  
🌀 Food processor (or blender)  
🕸️ Fine-mesh sieve (optional, for sifting)  
🍳 Non-stick pan/flat pan  
🥄 Spatula (silicone scraper)  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔥 Oven  
📜 Baking sheet, parchment paper  
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon  

## Steps  
### Mung Bean Filling  
1. Rinse the peeled mung beans thoroughly, then add enough water and soak until the beans expand.  
2. Place the soaked mung beans in a pot, add water to cover them, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and boil for about 2 minutes. Skim off the foam, remove and rinse repeatedly under running water, then drain well.  
   **Key point**: Boiling is to remove the bean-y smell; rinsing thoroughly is essential.  
3. Spread the drained mung beans evenly on a steaming rack, poke several holes with chopsticks, cover the lid, and steam over high heat after the water boils for about 40 minutes, until the beans can be easily crushed.  
4. Combine the steamed mung beans, 180g sugar, 2g salt, and 100g corn oil in a food processor, then blend into a smooth, lump-free paste.  
5. For an exceptionally fine texture, pass the bean paste through a fine-mesh sieve once.  
6. Transfer the bean paste to a non-stick pan. Cook over low heat, stirring continuously with a spatula for about 30 minutes, until the filling forms a cohesive ball and no longer sticks to the spatula or pan, with a sandy surface texture.  
   **Key point**: If localized burning occurs, remove the burnt bits; adjust the cooking time based on the moisture content.  

### Flaky Dough  
7. Make the water-oil dough: Combine 110g all-purpose flour, 20g sugar, and 40g lard. Pour in 45g boiling water, stir quickly into a flaky mass, then knead into a rough dough. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.  
8. Make the oil dough: Combine 90g cake flour and 45g lard, then knead by hand into a cohesive ball.  
9. Knead the rested water-oil dough until smooth. Divide both doughs into equal small portions, roll each into a ball, and cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying.  
   **Key point**: The two doughs should be similar in firmness to avoid breakage during assembly.  

### Assembly & Shaping  
10. Flatten a water-oil dough piece, roll it into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges, wrap an oil dough ball inside, and seal the opening tightly using your thumb and forefinger.  
11. Place the sealed side up, flatten, roll into a long oval shape, then roll it up from one end to form a cylinder.  
12. Flatten the roll again, roll it out lengthwise, and roll it up once more.  
13. Take a twice-rolled piece, fold the ends toward the center and pinch together. Place the sealed side up, flatten, and roll into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.  
14. Wrap about 34g of mung bean filling in the wrapper, seal with your thumb and forefinger, then place the sealed side down.  
15. Gently press the assembled ball with the sealed side up, or lightly roll it with a rolling pin into a slightly flattened disc.  

### Cooking  
16. **Baking method**: Arrange the pastry discs on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Place in a preheated oven at 180°C (middle rack) and bake for about 25 minutes, until the crust is golden and crispy and the pastries puff up. If you prefer a flatter shape, flip them once halfway through.  
17. **Pan-frying method**: Brush a thin layer of oil on a flat pan, place the pastry discs in, and cook over low heat. Flip frequently for even browning on both sides. Keep the heat low and use minimal oil. If the pastries are thick, make them thinner so the sides can cook through.  

## Health Advice  
The mung bean filling is rich in dietary fiber and plant protein. The flaky crust contains lard and sugar, so the calorie content is relatively high. Consume in moderation. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; those on a diet should enjoy sparingly.
