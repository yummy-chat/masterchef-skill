# Potato Stuffed Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 2 medium-sized potatoes  
🍚 Cooked rice (as needed, pre-cooked)  
🫑 1 green pepper (chopped)  
🥚 1 egg  
🥓 50g ham (diced)  
🫘 1 tablespoon Lao Gan Ma fermented black bean chili sauce  
🍶 1 drizzle of light soy sauce  
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, for roasting potatoes and stir-frying ingredients)  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment  
♨️ Steamer  
🔥 Air fryer (or oven)  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥄 Spoon  
🍚 Rice cooker (for cooking rice)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel the potatoes, cut them in half, and steam in a steamer until fully cooked.  
2. Remove the steamed potatoes, lightly spray with oil, and air fry at 180°C for 15 minutes until the skin is golden and crispy.  
   **Key point**: Steaming before roasting gives the potatoes a crispy exterior and soft interior, preventing them from becoming too dry if roasted directly.  
3. Prepare the filling: Heat oil in a wok, scramble the egg until broken into small pieces, add the chopped green pepper and 1 tablespoon of Lao Gan Ma chili sauce, stir-fry evenly, then set aside.  
4. Once the potatoes are roasted, slightly split them along the cut, use a spoon to scoop out the potato flesh, keeping the skin intact as a container.  
5. Mix the scooped-out potato flesh with pre-cooked rice, scrambled egg and green pepper, and diced ham. Drizzle with a circle of light soy sauce and mix thoroughly.  
6. Stuff the potato rice mixture back into the potato shells, press firmly, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish provides quality carbohydrates, protein, and a small amount of dietary fiber, making it suitable as a quick lunch or bento. Note that the Lao Gan Ma sauce and ham are high in sodium; those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount or use low-sodium alternatives.
