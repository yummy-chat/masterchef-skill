# Deep-Fried Pork Meatballs
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 500g (fat-to-lean ratio 1:1)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🌽 Cornstarch  as needed  
🧅 Scallion  as needed (finely shredded, for scallion-ginger water)  
🫚 Ginger  as needed (finely shredded, for scallion-ginger water)  
🧂 Table salt  about 3g  
🍶 Cooking wine  about 5g  
🌶️ Ground white pepper  about 1.5g  
💧 Water  as needed (for soaking shredded scallion and ginger)  
🫗 Cooking oil  a large amount (for deep frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chinese chef’s knife  
🪵 Round thick cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the pork belly, slice it thinly, then chop into a fine paste.  
2. Prepare scallion-ginger water: Finely shred the ginger and scallion into a bowl, add an appropriate amount of water, squeeze and knead vigorously for 1 minute, then strain to obtain the liquid.  
**Key point**: Do not mix the scallion and ginger solids directly into the meat filling, to prevent them from turning black during frying.  
3. First mixing: Add salt and cooking wine to the meat paste. Firmly slap and stir in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky and bouncy.  
4. Second seasoning: Add ground white pepper, pour in the scallion-ginger water, and continue stirring in the same direction until the liquid is fully absorbed.  
**Note**: Adding ingredients in stages helps achieve thorough flavoring.  
5. Third binding: Add the egg and cornstarch, then continue stirring and slapping until the mixture is firm and cohesive.  
**Key point**: Cornstarch is more suitable for deep frying, as it gives a crispier coating.  
6. First frying: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to about 170°C (when the surface shimmers and bubbles form densely around chopsticks). Use the tiger’s mouth of your left hand to squeeze out meatballs, and use a spoon with your right hand to scrape them into the oil.  
**Note**: The oil temperature must not be below 180°C, otherwise the meatballs may fall apart or stick together.  
7. Fry until all meatballs float to the surface and turn light yellow, then remove and drain oil.  
8. Second frying: Raise the oil temperature to about 190°C, add the meatballs and fry for about 30 seconds until golden brown, then remove and drain.  
**Key point**: Re-frying tightens the outer crust, achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior. The second fry can be repeated if desired.  
9. Drain the oil completely and plate. The outer shell should be golden and crispy.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and calories, suitable for those in muscle-building phases or with high physical exertion. However, people with high blood lipids, blood sugar, or blood pressure should consume sparingly. Control the oil temperature during preparation; the second frying helps expel excess oil, resulting in a crispier texture.
