# Stir-Fried Chinese Yam with Wood Ear Mushroom
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Chinese yam, appropriate amount (peeled and cut into diamond slices)
🍄 Wood ear mushroom, appropriate amount (soaked in advance)
🍒 Goji berries, a small amount (soaked in advance)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (minced)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (minced)
🧂 Salt, 2g
🍯 Chicken extract, 2ml
🍬 Sugar, 2g
🌶️ White pepper powder, 0.5g
🫒 Sesame oil, 10ml
💧 Water, 20ml
🌾 Cornstarch, 3g
🧈 Lard, appropriate amount
🍶 White vinegar, appropriate amount (for blanching)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Stockpot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl (for mixing the sauce)
🍳 Wok
🪄 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the sauce**: Place salt, chicken extract, sugar, white pepper powder, sesame oil, water, and cornstarch into a bowl, and stir until well combined. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Preparing the sauce in advance speeds up the cooking process and prevents last-minute panic during stir-frying.
2. **Blanch**: Bring water to a boil in a stockpot, add an appropriate amount of white vinegar, then add the yam slices and soaked wood ear mushrooms. Blanch until the ingredients are just cooked through, then remove and drain.
   **Note**: White vinegar effectively prevents the yam from oxidizing and turning black, keeping its color bright white.
3. **Aromatics**: Heat the wok, then add lard and melt it. Once the oil is hot, add the minced scallion and ginger, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.
4. **Combine and stir-fry**: Add the blanched yam slices and wood ear mushrooms, and stir-fry quickly to combine. Then add the soaked goji berries, and pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok.
5. **Thicken and serve**: After adding the sauce, stir-fry quickly to evenly coat all ingredients with the thickened liquid. Immediately turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Tips
Chinese yam is rich in dietary fiber and mucilage protein, making it suitable for those with weak digestive systems. Adding white vinegar during blanching prevents discoloration; be careful to control the cooking time to avoid making it too soft. Wood ear mushrooms help cleanse blood vessels and aid in detoxification, while goji berries support eye health. This dish is low in fat and nutritious, especially suitable for those managing blood sugar or seeking weight loss.
