# Cantonese Braised Beef Brisket Noodles
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐂 Beef tendon/brisket (tendon and meaty parts) – appropriate amount  
🍜 Alkaline noodles – 1 pack  
🥬 Lettuce – 1 stalk  
🌱 Chives – appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger – 1-2 slices  
🥫 Chu hou paste – 1 tablespoon  
🥜 Peanut butter – 1 dollop  
🥡 Light soy sauce – a little  
💧 Water – appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Small saucepan  
🥄 Spatula/ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
✂️ Scissors

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the beef brisket in cold water and blanch until fully cooked. Remove and cut into small pieces.  
**Note**: Seasonings can be substituted with satay sauce, fermented tofu, etc., according to personal preference. No additional salt is needed.

2. Put the beef brisket pieces into the pressure cooker. Add ginger slices, chu hou paste, peanut butter, and a little light soy sauce. Pour in water.  
**Note**: Seasonings can be substituted with satay sauce, fermented tofu, etc., according to personal preference. No additional salt is needed.

3. Cover the pressure cooker. Once steam is released, press for about ten minutes until the beef brisket is tender and flavorful.
4. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the alkaline noodles and lettuce. Cook until done, then remove and place into a bowl. A little noodle cooking water can be added.
5. Pour the braised beef brisket along with the thick sauce over the noodles.
6. Finally, sprinkle with chopped chives for aroma and garnish. Mix well and serve.

## Health Tips
Beef brisket is rich in collagen and iron, making it suitable for those needing blood nourishment or joint care. However, it is relatively high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Alkaline noodles have a higher glycemic index; pairing them with lettuce and chives adds dietary fiber and balances nutrition.
