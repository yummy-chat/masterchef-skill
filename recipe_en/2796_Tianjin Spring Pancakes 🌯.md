# Tianjin Spring Pancakes 🌯
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Flour 500g (for dough)
🌾 Flour 50g (for oil paste)
🧂 Salt 5g
🫒 Cooked soybean oil 30g (for dough)
🫒 Cooked soybean oil 60g (for oil paste, cooled)
💧 Hot water 280g (60-70°C)
🥒 Cucumber strips, as needed (for pancake wrapping demo)
🥕 Carrot strips, as needed (for pancake wrapping demo)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Cutting board
🪓 Rolling pin
🍳 Steamer
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Small bowl
🍽️ Round mold

## Steps
1. Place 500g flour in a bowl, add 5g salt and 30g cooked soybean oil, and stir briefly.
**Key Point**: Salt enhances gluten elasticity; oil retains moisture so pancakes stay soft after cooling.
2. Gradually add 280g hot water (60-70°C), stir into fluff with chopsticks, then knead with hands into a smooth dough.
**Key Point**: Hot water balances softness and elasticity, avoiding the hardness of cold-water dough and the stickiness of fully scalded dough.
3. Cover and rest the dough for 30 minutes to relax the gluten.
4. In a small bowl, mix 50g flour with 60g cooled cooked soybean oil until a smooth paste forms.
**Key Point**: Oil paste separates layers better than brushing with plain oil, preventing sticking.
5. Roll the rested dough into a long log, divide into pieces about 30g each. Flatten each piece, then roll into evenly sized small round wrappers.
6. Spread a thin layer of oil paste evenly on one wrapper, place another wrapper on top. Repeat stacking all wrappers, leaving the top one uncoated with oil paste.
7. Gently press the stack with your hand, then pat it with a rolling pin. Flip it over and roll from center outward evenly into a large thin round pancake, slightly smaller than the steamer.
**Key Point**: Patting and flipping prevent wrappers from shifting, ensuring even thickness.
8. Place the rolled pancake into a steamer lined with cloth. Steam over boiling water, covered, for 10 minutes once steam appears.
9. After steaming, remove and let cool until warm to the touch.
10. Transfer the pancake stack to a cutting board. Place a small bowl upside-down in the center, then cut around the bowl's edge with a knife to trim uneven edges for a rounder shape.
11. Gently peel the round pancakes apart one by one, like turning book pages. The finished pancakes are as thin as paper, translucent, soft, and resilient, holding fillings without tearing easily.

## Health Advice
These spring pancakes are made with hot water and a small amount of oil, resulting in a soft and resilient texture suitable for wrapping various vegetables and meats, offering balanced nutrition. Keep the oil paste moderate to avoid high fat; pair with low-oil, low-salt fillings for a healthier meal.
