# Stir-Fried Instant Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Instant noodles 2 blocks
🥚 Eggs 2 pieces
🍖 Ham sausage About 2 sticks (sliced diagonally)
🥬 Bok choy About 4-5 pieces (whole or halved)
🌶️ Green bell pepper About 1 piece (julienned)
🌶️ Red bell pepper About 1 piece (julienned)
🧅 Onion About half a piece (julienned)
🌿 Cilantro About 1 small bunch (cut into sections, added before serving)
🧂 White vinegar A few drops (for beating eggs)
🧂 Salt A pinch (for beating eggs)
🧂 Light soy sauce To taste
🦪 Oyster sauce To taste
🧂 Instant noodle seasoning packet 1 packet
🌶️ Chili flakes To taste
🫒 Cooking oil To taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
♨️ Steamer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Ladle
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the instant noodle blocks into a basin filled with water and soak for 10-20 seconds before removing.
   **Key Point**: Keep soaking time short to prevent the noodles from absorbing too much water and becoming soft.
2. Place the soaked noodle blocks in the steamer basket, cover, and steam for about 8 minutes after the steam rises, until the noodles are loose with no hard core.
   **Key Point**: Steaming gives the noodles a chewy texture, making them less likely to stick together and easier to absorb flavors during stir-frying.
3. Quickly transfer the steamed noodles into cold water to cool, then remove and drain thoroughly using a strainer.
   **Key Point**: Cooling and thorough draining are key to maintaining the noodles’ elasticity and preventing excess water release during stir-frying.
4. In a bowl, crack 2 eggs, add a few drops of white vinegar and a pinch of salt, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks.
   **Key Point**: Adding white vinegar effectively removes the eggy odor and results in fluffier scrambled eggs.
5. Heat a wok with plenty of oil, pour in the egg mixture, and wait until the edges set before gently pushing with a ladle. Stir-fry until set into large pieces, then set aside.
   **State Check**: Eggs are light yellow and formed into large pieces.
   **Key Point**: Avoid stirring immediately after adding the eggs; gently push after they set to achieve large, fluffy scrambled eggs.
6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add chopped green onions and minced garlic, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
   **State Check**: Aroma of scallions and garlic is released, color turns slightly yellow.
7. Turn the heat to high, add the julienned onion and green and red bell peppers, and stir-fry quickly until just tender.
   **State Check**: Vegetables are softened but still retain a crisp texture.
8. Add the sliced ham sausage and bok choy, and continue stir-frying until the bok choy is softened.
   **State Check**: Bok choy color deepens and volume shrinks.
9. Return the prepared noodles and scrambled eggs to the wok, and add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, the instant noodle seasoning packet, and chili flakes.
10. Stir-fry over high heat quickly to evenly combine all ingredients and seasonings.
    **State Check**: Noodles are evenly colored, with no clumps.
11. Before serving, sprinkle in the cilantro sections, stir-fry a few times, then remove from heat and plate.
    **Key Point**: Add cilantro last to preserve its aroma.

## Health Advice
This stir-fried instant noodle dish is rich in carbohydrates and contains some vegetables and protein, making it suitable for a quick energy boost after exercise. However, it is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of seasoning packet used. With the addition of eggs and vegetables, the nutrition is more balanced, making it a suitable meal for students or busy office workers.
