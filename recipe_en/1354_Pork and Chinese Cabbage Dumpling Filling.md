# Pork and Chinese Cabbage Dumpling Filling  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Ground pork (pre-seasoned basic meat filling)  
🥬 Chinese cabbage (chopped)  
🫙 Soybean paste (used with light soy sauce in a 1:2 ratio)  
🧴 Light soy sauce  
🧂 Salt  
🧅🫚💧 Scallion-ginger water  
🫒 Sichuan pepper oil  
🌿 Five-spice powder  
🤍 White pepper powder  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🪢 Cheesecloth (for squeezing out water)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Take a glass bowl, add the ground pork, then add light soy sauce, soybean paste, scallion-ginger water, Sichuan pepper oil, five-spice powder, and white pepper powder. Stir vigorously in one direction until the meat filling becomes sticky and firm. Set aside.  
2. Place the chopped cabbage in a bowl, sprinkle with an appropriate amount of salt, mix well, and let it sit to marinate until the cabbage softens and releases water.  
3. Wrap the marinated chopped cabbage in cheesecloth and squeeze firmly to remove as much excess liquid as possible.  
   **Key point**: Thoroughly squeezing out the water is the crucial step to prevent the dumpling filling from becoming watery.  
4. Add the squeezed-dry chopped cabbage to the prepared ground pork filling and gently mix until just combined. Avoid over-mixing.  
5. Use immediately after mixing to keep the filling dry and non-watery, allowing the sweetness of the cabbage and the aroma of the meat to blend.  

## Health Tips  
The secret to this dumpling filling lies in squeezing out the water from the cabbage to prevent moisture, with soybean paste enhancing the umami. Suitable for those on a low-sodium diet; the amount of salt can be reduced appropriately. The protein and vegetable combination is balanced, but the meat filling is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat no more than 20 dumplings at a time.
