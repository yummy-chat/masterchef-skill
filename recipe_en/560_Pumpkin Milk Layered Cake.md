# Pumpkin Milk Layered Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🎃 1 old pumpkin (use about 2 flat scoops of steamed pumpkin flesh)  
🌾 Tapioca starch: 10 flat scoops for pumpkin batter; 1 bowl (about 10 flat scoops) for milk batter  
🥛 Pure milk: half a carton for pumpkin batter; 1 full carton for milk batter  
🍬 White sugar: 1 flat scoop for pumpkin batter; 2 flat scoops for milk batter (adjust to taste)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥔 Masher  
🍲 Enamel bowl  
🍽️ Flat plate  

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel and deseed the old pumpkin, cut into pieces, and steam in a steamer until fully cooked.  
   **Key Point**: Choose an old pumpkin with orange flesh for higher sweetness and a softer, stickier texture.

2. Place the steamed pumpkin on a flat plate to drain excess water. Take 2 flat scoops of pumpkin flesh, mash into a fine puree with a masher, add 1 flat scoop of white sugar, and stir until completely dissolved. Then add 10 flat scoops of tapioca starch and half a carton of pure milk, stirring until the batter is smooth and lump-free.  
   **Note**: Be sure to stir until no lumps remain, otherwise hard cores will form after steaming.

3. Take 1 bowl of tapioca starch, add 1 full carton of pure milk and 2 flat scoops of white sugar, and stir until the batter is smooth and lump-free.

4. Pour a small amount of cooking oil into the bottom of an enamel bowl, and rotate the bowl to evenly coat the inner walls.  
   **Key Point**: Brushing with oil prevents the cake from sticking to the bowl, making it easier to unmold.

5. Once the water in the steamer is boiling, place the prepared bowl inside. First, scoop in 1 spoonful of the pumpkin batter, gently rotate the bowl to spread it evenly, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the batter is fully set.  
   **State Check**: Each layer must be fully set before adding the next to prevent colors from mixing.

6. On the set pumpkin layer, scoop in 1 spoonful of the milk batter, spread it evenly, and steam until set. Repeat this alternating process until all batter is used up.  
   **Note**: If a layer does not set after 3 minutes of steaming, the batter is too thin; add more tapioca starch to adjust.

7. Submerge the bowl with the finished layered cake in cold water. Once the cake is completely cooled, invert the bowl to unmold it fully.  
   **Key Point**: Cold water is sufficient for quick cooling in winter; once cooled, the cake will not be sticky and will unmold more easily.

8. Place the unmolded layered cake on a cutting board, cut into appropriate pieces, and serve directly.

## Health Advice  
Pumpkin milk layered cake is suitable for the whole family. Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene, and milk provides high-quality protein. However, the tapioca starch content is relatively high, making it calorie-dense. It is recommended to consume 2-3 pieces per serving and not to overindulge as a staple food.
