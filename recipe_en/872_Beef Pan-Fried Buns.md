# Beef Pan-Fried Buns
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 200g all-purpose flour
🌽 30g cornstarch
🧂 2g salt (for dough) + appropriate amount (for filling)
🍬 2g sugar (for dough) + appropriate amount (for filling)
🍞 2g dry yeast
💧 110g water (for dough)
🥩 Approximately half a box of ground beef (needs to be minced finely)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallion (for making scallion-ginger water and garnish)
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger
🌿 Appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorn
🖤 Appropriate amount of black sesame seeds (for garnish)
🌱 Appropriate amount of chopped scallion (for garnish)
🧂 Appropriate amount of light soy sauce
🍶 Appropriate amount of cooking wine
🧂 Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper
🧂 Appropriate amount of thirteen-spice powder
🧂 Appropriate amount of curry powder

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🌯 Rolling pin
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥢 Tongs
🥛 Measuring cup
🍶 Ceramic bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. **Make the dough**: Mix the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, 2g salt, 2g sugar, and dry yeast. Add 110g water and knead into a rough dough. Let it rest to proof.
   - At this stage, there is no need to knead until smooth; forming a rough ball is enough. It will become easier to knead smooth after resting.

2. **Make scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water**: Cut the scallion into sections and slice the ginger. Place them together with the Sichuan peppercorn in a bowl. Pour in hot water and let it steep for later use.

3. **Prepare the beef filling**: Finely mince the ground beef with a knife until it reaches a smooth filling consistency.

4. **Season the beef filling**: Add light soy sauce, cooking wine, dark soy sauce, an appropriate amount of salt, an appropriate amount of sugar, white pepper, thirteen-spice powder, and curry powder to the beef filling. Mix and stir evenly. Then, gradually add the scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water in batches, mixing well after each addition before adding the next. Stir until the filling becomes soft, smooth, and elastic.
   - **Key point**: Add liquid in batches and mix thoroughly. This is key to making the filling juicy.

5. **Divide the dough**: Take out the proofed dough. Knead it until the surface is smooth. Divide it into about 8 equal-sized dough pieces.

6. **Wrap the buns**: Take one dough piece and roll it into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place an appropriate amount of beef filling in the center. Wrap and pleat it into a bun, pinching the seam tightly to seal.

7. **Pan-fry to set**: Pour oil into a pan and heat it. Place the buns in the pan. Cook over medium-low heat until the bottom is golden brown and set, about 2-3 minutes.

8. **Add water and steam**: Pour warm water into the pan until it reaches about 2/3 the height of the buns. Immediately cover with a lid and turn the heat to medium to steam. When the water is mostly evaporated, open the lid and check that the tops of the buns are fluffy and fully cooked. Continue frying until the bottom is crispy and golden.

9. **Plate and garnish**: Remove the pan-fried buns from the pan. Sprinkle with black sesame seeds and chopped scallion. Serve.

## Health Advice
Beef pan-fried buns are rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for fitness muscle-building or those needing energy. However, the filling contains a fair amount of oil and salt, so it is recommended to pair them with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Pay attention to portion control to avoid excessive calorie intake from eating too many at once.
