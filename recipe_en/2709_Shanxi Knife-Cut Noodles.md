# Shanxi Knife-Cut Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (skin removed, ground into mince, retain some graininess)
🧈 Lard 250g
🫒 Soybean oil 250g
🧅 Scallion segments 50g (for flavored oil)
🧅 Minced scallion 40g (for stir-frying the meat mince)
🧄 Ginger slices 30g (for flavored oil)
🧄 Minced ginger 25g (for stir-frying the meat mince)
🧄 Minced garlic 30g
🥕 Carrot slices 30g
🌿 Cilantro 5g (for flavored oil) + a little for garnish
🌿 Bay leaf 1g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 2g
⭐ Star anise 3g
🌱 Cumin seeds 1g
🫘 Chili powder (extra fine, not spicy) 35g
🍲 Broth 3000g
🥬 Baby bok choy, as needed
🍚 High-gluten flour 1000g
🧂 Salt 10g (for kneading dough) + 40g (for seasoning the braising liquid)
💧 Water 420g
🍶 Light soy sauce 25g
🍯 Dark soy sauce for braising 30g
🍯 Dark soy sauce 20g
🍇 Ninghuafu aged vinegar 25g
🍷 Yellow wine 30g
🍬 Sugar 10g
🧂 MSG 20g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 10g
🍲 Oyster sauce 20g
📦 Spice packet 1 (contains Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cumin seeds, and other spices)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥩 Meat grinder (with 6mm hole plate)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🍝 Knife for cutting noodles
🥣 Stockpot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍜 Bowl
🪣 Stainless steel basin
⚖️ Digital scale

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork belly and cut it into large chunks. Mince it using a meat grinder, keeping some graininess; do not grind it too fine.
2. Add soybean oil and lard to the wok. Heat until the fat melts, then reduce to medium-low heat. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, carrot slices, bay leaf, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cumin seeds, and cilantro. Fry slowly until the solids turn caramel-colored. Remove and discard the solids, reserving the oil.
3. Add the ground pork to the flavored oil and quickly stir to break it up. Stir-fry until it just begins to change color. Add minced scallion, minced ginger, and minced garlic, stir-frying until fragrant. Continue stir-frying for about 3 minutes, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce for braising, dark soy sauce, vinegar, yellow wine, and chili powder. Stir-fry, scraping the bottom, for about 5 minutes. Pour in the broth and season with salt, sugar, MSG, chicken bouillon, and oyster sauce. Add the spice packet. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer gently for 30 minutes until the flavors meld.
4. Mix the high-gluten flour and salt together. Make a well in the center and add the water in stages, stirring to combine into a shaggy dough. Knead into a ball. Cover and rest for 15 minutes. Knead again until any dry bits on the surface are incorporated. Rest for another 10 minutes. Repeat this kneading and resting process at least three times, for a total resting time of about 30 minutes, until the dough is smooth. Shape the dough into a long log, press a groove down the middle, lightly brush the surface with water, flip it over, and press it onto the board to secure it firmly for cutting.
5. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Using a knife-cut noodle blade, slice strips of dough directly into the boiling water. When the noodles are nearly done, add the baby bok choy to cook together. The noodles are ready when they float and have no white core. Drain and serve in bowls.
6. Ladle a generous spoonful of the meat sauce over the noodles. Garnish with cilantro. Can be served with aged vinegar and garlic cloves according to preference.

## Health Suggestions
These knife-cut noodles are rich in carbohydrates and fats, making them suitable for times of high physical exertion. However, it is advisable to control the intake of pork belly and oil. People with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with an ample amount of vegetables to balance nutrition and ease digestion.
