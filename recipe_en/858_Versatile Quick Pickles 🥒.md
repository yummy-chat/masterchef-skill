# Versatile Quick Pickles 🥒
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥬
🥬 Chinese cabbage - to taste (washed, cut into sections or strips)
🥕 Radish - to taste (white radish or beauty-heart radish, washed and cut into strips)
🌶️ Red chili peppers (bird's eye chili) - to taste (cut into rings or sections)
🥬 Asparagus lettuce - to taste (washed and cut into strips)
🍖 Ham sausage - to taste (cut into strips)
💧 Cold boiled water - 2500g
🫙 Pickled bird's eye chili brine - 500g (can include a few pickled chilies)
🍶 White vinegar - 300g
🍬 White sugar - 150g
🧂 Salt - 50g
🫚 Ginger slices - to taste
🥃 High-proof liquor - a few drops

## Kitchenware & Equipment 🫙
🫙 Large glass pickle jar with lid
🥣 Several bowls
🥄 Spoon
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥢 Clean, oil-free chopsticks or tongs

## Instructions 🥢
1. Wash the Chinese cabbage, radish, and asparagus lettuce thoroughly, then cut into thick strips or sections; cut the ham sausage into strips of similar thickness. Spread all cut vegetables in a ventilated area to air-dry surface moisture. Ensure no raw water remains to prevent spoilage.
2. Prepare the pickling brine: In a pickle jar or large container, sequentially add cold boiled water, pickled bird's eye chili brine, white vinegar, white sugar, and salt. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until completely dissolved. This brine is concentrated for quick flavor infusion.
3. Layer the dried vegetable strips and ham sausage strips into the clean pickle jar. Top with ginger slices and bird's eye chili rings, pressing down firmly.
4. Slowly pour the prepared pickling brine into the jar, ensuring the liquid completely covers all ingredients. Finally, add a few drops of liquor.
5. Tightly seal the lid and place in a cool, shaded spot. Let it sit for 2 hours.
6. After the time is up, use clean, oil-free chopsticks or a spoon to retrieve and serve. Store remaining pickles sealed, ensuring all utensils used for retrieval are clean, dry, and oil-free.

## Health Advice 🍃
These pickles are low in fat and high in fiber. Through quick fermentation, they become rich in probiotics, making them ideal for stimulating appetite or for those monitoring caloric intake. However, they contain relatively high levels of salt and sugar, so individuals with high blood pressure or those who need to limit salt and sugar consumption should eat them in moderation.
