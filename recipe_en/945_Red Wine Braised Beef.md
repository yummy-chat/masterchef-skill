# Red Wine Braised Beef
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef short ribs 1kg (cut into large pieces)  
🥓 Bacon 3 slices, about 60g (cut into small pieces)  
🧅 White onion 1 (diced)  
🥕 Carrot 2 (cut into rolled chunks)  
🍄 Button mushrooms 200g (quarter each)  
🧄 Pearl onions 150g (boiled in skin, peeled, and halved)  
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves (minced)  
🍅 Tomato paste to taste  
🌾 Flour to taste  
🌿 Bay leaves 3  
🌿 Thyme 2 sprigs  
🌿 Dried parsley to taste (for seasoning and garnish)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶️ Black pepper to taste  
🫒 Olive oil to taste  
🧈 Butter 1 small piece  
🍷 Red wine 450ml (about 2/3 bottle)  
🍖 Beef broth 350ml (or water)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan  
🥘 Lidded stew pot  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🍳 Non-stick pan (for boiling pearl onions)  
🥢 Tongs and spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut bacon into small pieces, and beef short ribs into large chunks. Cut carrots into rolled chunks, dice the white onion, and quarter each button mushroom. Set aside.  
   **Key point**: Do not blanch the beef short ribs—this cut is marbled with fat, and cooking it directly yields better texture.
2. Pour olive oil into a frying pan, add the bacon, and stir-fry until slightly crispy. Use tongs to transfer the bacon to the stew pot.
3. Using the remaining bacon fat in the pan, sear the beef chunks in two batches, browning each side. Avoid overcrowding to prevent the meat from releasing liquid.  
   **Key point**: Keep the pan hot enough to trigger the Maillard reaction, creating a caramelized crust. Transfer all seared beef to the stew pot.
4. Pour about 2/3 of the bottle of red wine into the pan used for searing, bring to a boil, and let it cook for about 2–3 minutes to evaporate most of the alcohol while retaining the wine’s aroma. Then pour the wine along with the browned bits from the pan into the stew pot.
5. Cover the stew pot and reduce the heat to low, starting the braising process.
6. In a separate pot, add olive oil and sauté the diced onion until fragrant. Then add the minced garlic, carrots, and tomato paste, stirring until aromatic.
7. Sprinkle in an appropriate amount of flour and stir-fry until no dry flour remains—this will help naturally thicken the sauce later.
8. Pour in the beef broth, bring to a boil, then add the bay leaves, thyme, dried parsley, salt, and black pepper to season.
9. Pour the cooked vegetable broth entirely into the stew pot, mix well, and continue to braise over low heat for about 90 minutes, until the beef is tender.
10. In a separate pot, place the pearl onions (with skin on) in water and boil for about 8 minutes until cooked. Remove, easily peel off the skin, and cut them in half.
11. In a clean pan, add a small piece of butter, then add the quartered mushrooms and the prepared pearl onions. Sauté them until they develop a caramelized color and a sweet fragrance.
12. Add the sautéed mushrooms and pearl onions to the stew pot, and simmer for another 3–5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
13. Before serving, sprinkle with some dried parsley for garnish. Enjoy with a baguette or mashed potatoes.

## Health Advice
Red wine braised beef is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for winter consumption to supplement nutrition. Bacon and beef short ribs are relatively high in fat, so be mindful of balancing the meal with vegetables to manage caloric intake. It is also recommended to control the amount of red wine used to avoid excessive alcohol intake.
