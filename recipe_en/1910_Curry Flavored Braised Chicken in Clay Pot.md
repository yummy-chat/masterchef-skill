# Curry Flavored Braised Chicken in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients  
🍗 Chicken thighs 3-4 pieces (cut into pieces, squeeze out blood water)  
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (shredded)  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers, as needed (cut into pieces)  
🍺 Beer, 1 can (enough to submerge the ingredients)  

## Seasonings  
🍛 Curry paste, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🧂 Pepper, as needed  
🫒 Sesame oil, 1 tablespoon  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the chicken thighs, chop into small pieces, and squeeze out the blood water by hand.  
2. Place the chicken pieces in a bowl, add curry paste, salt, and pepper. Mix well and marinate for 5 minutes.  
3. Layer the bottom of the clay pot with scallion sections, ginger slices, shredded onion, and potato pieces in order.  
4. Spread the marinated chicken evenly over the potatoes.  
5. Pour in the beer until the liquid submerges the ingredients, then add sesame oil.  
6. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and braise for 18 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Be sure to maintain low heat to prevent the liquid from evaporating and causing the pot to burn.  
7. Remove the lid, sprinkle in the green and red bell pepper pieces. Slightly reduce the liquid, and remove from heat once the peppers are just cooked through.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses beer instead of water for braising, which tenderizes the meat and adds a malty aroma. Potatoes are rich in carbohydrates, making this a good choice for replenishing energy after a workout. Note that curry paste is relatively high in salt, so adjust the amount of salt accordingly when seasoning.
