# Northeast China Stewed Dried Vegetables with Meat

Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Skin-on pork belly, appropriate amount (proportion with hazel mushrooms and dried beans/dried potatoes about 1:1:1, cut into large pieces; the fatty part provides oil and aroma)  
🐖 Pork shoulder/front leg meat, appropriate amount (proportion with pork belly about 1:1, cut into larger pieces; lean meat part)  
🍄 Hazel mushrooms, appropriate amount (proportion with meat about 1:1, remove roots, wash, and squeeze dry; no need to soak in advance)  
🥬 Dried beans, appropriate amount (combined with dried potatoes, proportion with meat about 1:1; need to soak and wash in advance)  
🥔 Dried potatoes, appropriate amount (combined with dried beans, proportion with meat about 1:1; need to soak and wash in advance)  
🍜 Vermicelli, appropriate amount (add to the pot 10 minutes before finishing)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (scallion sections for sautéing; chopped scallions for garnish at the end)  
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (minced or sliced, for sautéing)  
🌿 Star anise, 2-3 pieces (for sautéing)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced, add before finishing to enhance flavor)  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder, appropriate amount (add when the meat is not fully cooked)  
🥤 Light soy sauce, generous amount (for sautéing to release sauce aroma)  
🥤 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (to adjust color if needed)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (add after putting in dried beans and dried potatoes to season in advance)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok (for seasoning the wok, stir-frying ingredients, and stewing with water)  
🥄 Strainer (for washing and squeezing dry hazel mushrooms)  
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking, washing, and draining dried beans and dried potatoes)  
🥄 Soup ladle (for serving the finished dish)  
🥘 Wok lid (for covering during stewing to maintain a boil)  
🔥 Stove (for providing heat)  

## Cooking Steps  
### Preparation Stage  
1. Cut skin-on pork belly into large pieces; cut pork shoulder or front leg meat into even larger pieces to prevent them from breaking down during long stewing.  
2. Remove roots from hazel mushrooms, wash three times with plenty of water, and squeeze dry. No need to soak in advance, but they must be thoroughly cleaned to avoid a sandy texture when eating.  
3. Put dried beans and dried potatoes together in a basin, soak in water for two minutes, then wash and drain. Dried beans are too dry; they must be softened first before washing to avoid breaking them directly.  
4. Rinse the vermicelli briefly and set aside.  
5. Prepare scallion sections, minced ginger, and star anise.  
### Cooking Stage  
1. Season the wok: Heat the wok until the bottom turns white and smokes, turn off the heat, pour in oil to coat the wok, then turn the heat back on. The wok must be well-seasoned to prevent sticking.  
2. Add more oil, and when hot, add the pork belly pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the skin "pops" and renders oil, the edges are golden brown, and the oil becomes clear. Let the pork belly render oil for a longer time.  
3. Add the larger pieces of pork shoulder/front leg meat. Stir-fry over medium heat until the color changes and the surface turns white. Do not overcook the larger pieces.  
4. Add scallion sections, minced ginger, star anise, and thirteen-spice powder. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant. Add thirteen-spice powder when the meat is not fully cooked.  
5. Once the large meat pieces have changed color, pour in a generous amount of light soy sauce to sauté. Stir-fry until the soy sauce releases its aroma and you smell a rich sauce fragrance. Use enough light soy sauce for the sautéing step.  
6. Add the prepared hazel mushrooms, continue stir-frying over medium heat until the mushroom aroma releases and the soy sauce in the wok has dried up.  
7. Adjust the color by adding dark soy sauce as needed, and stir-fry evenly.  
8. Add the soaked dried beans and dried potatoes, add salt, and stir-fry evenly to dissolve the salt. Adding salt at this stage allows the dish to absorb flavor in advance.  
9. Clear a space in the wok bottom to confirm the soy sauce has dried up, then add enough boiling water to submerge most of the ingredients. Must use boiling water.  
10. Cover with the lid and boil over high heat, ensuring the liquid stays at a rolling boil throughout. Add more boiling water if the liquid reduces, keeping it at a full boil is key for flavor fusion and emulsification.  
11. Total stewing time is about 45-50 minutes. About 10 minutes before finishing, spread the rinsed vermicelli on top of all ingredients, cover, and continue to cook over high heat. Do not stir the vermicelli after adding to prevent it from sinking and sticking to the wok bottom.  
12. When time is up, uncover and reduce the liquid over high heat until thickened. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and minced garlic, stir well, and serve.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for warming up in winter. However, pork belly and vermicelli are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Control the amount of salt; those with high blood pressure can reduce the soy sauce.
