# Pumpkin Scallion Pancake 🎃  
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🎃 Pumpkin – appropriate amount (peeled and sliced into thin pieces)  
🌾 Flour – appropriate amount (add based on the consistency of pumpkin puree)  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (for kneading dough, making oil paste, and pan-frying)  
🧅 Chopped scallions – appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder – appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Large bowls (at least 2)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥘 Electric pancake pan  
🖌️ Brush  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel the pumpkin and cut into evenly thin slices. Steam in a steamer until soft and cooked through.  
2. Remove the steamed pumpkin slices and mash them into a fine puree while still hot. Let it cool completely.  
   **Note**: The pumpkin puree must be completely cooled before use; otherwise, adding flour will cook the gluten, affecting the softness and layering of the pancake.  
3. Gradually add flour to the cooled pumpkin puree, stirring with chopsticks until it forms flaky pieces. Drizzle in a little cooking oil and knead into a moist, soft dough by hand.  
4. Make the oil paste: In another bowl, combine chopped scallions, salt, thirteen-spice powder, and a small amount of flour. Pour hot cooking oil into the bowl and quickly stir into a smooth, paste-like consistency.  
5. Roll the pumpkin dough into a long strip and cut into evenly sized portions. Take one portion, flatten it, wrap in an appropriate amount of oil paste, and pinch the opening tightly closed.  
6. Place the wrapped portion with the seam side down. Gently roll it into a round, thin pancake using a rolling pin.  
   **Key Point**: Roll gently to avoid squeezing out the oil paste or tearing the skin; aim for even thickness.  
7. Preheat the electric pancake pan, brush with a layer of cooking oil, place the pancake in, and brush the top with oil as well. Cook for about 2–3 minutes until the bottom is set and golden, then flip.  
8. Continue cooking, flipping once more as needed, until both sides are golden brown and the pancake visibly puffs up. Remove from the pan.  
   **Judgment Standard**: A puffed-up pancake indicates that the inner layers have fully expanded and are cooked through, resulting in a crispier texture.  

## Health Advice  
Pumpkin is rich in β-carotene, which supports eye health and boosts immunity. Pairing it with whole-wheat flour increases dietary fiber, making it suitable for the whole family. However, the oil paste and frying process contribute to higher calorie content, so it is recommended to eat no more than two pancakes per serving. Balance the meal with a side of cold mixed vegetables for a more nutritious combination.
