# Chinese Pastry

Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥄 All-purpose flour 300g (for water-oil dough)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 60g (for water-oil dough)  
🥮 Room temperature lard 216g (96g for water-oil dough, 120g for oil dough)  
💧 Cold water 120g (for water-oil dough)  
🌾 Cake flour 240g (for oil dough)  
🥜 Filling approx. 25g each (e.g., osmanthus chestnut filling, etc., can be prepared in advance)  
🍵 Matcha powder, as needed (for green coloring)  
🎨 Food coloring (red, yellow, purple, etc.), as needed (for coloring)  
🥚 Egg wash, as needed (for decoration and sealing)  
🌱 White sesame seeds, as needed (for decoration)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🤖 Stand mixer (or can be kneaded by hand)  
🫥 Plastic wrap  
🥣 Rolling pin  
🔪 Bench scraper  
🔥 Oven  
📜 Parchment paper  
🖌️ Shaping tools (round end press, knife, brush, etc.)  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the water-oil dough**  
   Place all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, 96g lard, and cold water into a stand mixer. Mix on low speed until combined, then switch to medium speed and knead until the dough is smooth and can be stretched into a thin membrane. Remove, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.

2. **Make the oil dough**  
   Combine cake flour with 120g lard and rub together with your hands until a dough forms, creating a white oil dough. If coloring is desired, add matcha powder or food coloring at this stage or after dividing, and knead until uniform.

3. **Divide and color**  
   Divide the water-oil dough and oil dough into portions as needed and color them differently. Use matcha powder for green portions, and food coloring for other colors. Keep covered with plastic wrap at all times to prevent drying.

4. **Individual wrapping and rolling**  
   Take one portion of water-oil dough, flatten it, and wrap it around one portion of oil dough. Use the heel of your hand to seal tightly. Make a total of 24 individually wrapped dough balls. Let rest for 20 minutes.  
   - First roll: Place seam side up, roll into an oval, then roll up from top to bottom. Let rest for 20 minutes.  
   - Second roll: Rotate the dough 90 degrees, roll out again into a longer strip, and roll up. The pastry base is now ready.

5. **Bulk wrapping and rolling**  
   Roll a large piece of water-oil dough into a rectangle. Shape different colored oil doughs into long strips and arrange them in gradient order on the water-oil dough. Wrap and seal tightly. Perform two rolling steps, each time rolling perpendicular to the previous direction. Finally, roll into a cylinder.

6. **Cut and shape**  
   Cut the bulk-wrapped dough cylinder into round slices. Place cut side down, flatten, and roll into a round disc. For individually wrapped dough, fold in half, flatten, and roll into a round disc. Ensure the pattern remains centered.

7. **Fill**  
   Place about 25g of filling into each pastry disc. Seal tightly and roll into a ball.

8. **Shape designs**  
   - **Gradient plum blossom pastry**: Flatten the filled ball, press 5 lines to create petals, and place a small yellow dough ball in the center as the stamen.  
   - **Green clover**: Flatten a green individually wrapped dough ball after folding, wrap with filling, flatten, and cut into 8 equal sections. Fold two sections inward and pinch into a heart shape, forming a four-leaf clover.  
   - **Autumn chrysanthemum pastry**: Flatten the filled ball, cut into 16 equal sections, pinch the edge of each section to a point. Brush the outer ring and center with yellow food coloring water, sprinkle with sesame seeds, then make shallow cuts on each petal.  
   - **Small purple flower**: Flatten the filled ball, cut into 8 large sections, then make a cut in the middle of each section. Fold the two petal pieces inward to create a hollow effect. Brush the center with egg wash and sprinkle with sesame seeds.  
   - **Longevity peach**: Shape the filled ball into a peach with a pointed tip. Attach small green dough leaves, use a tool to press a crease on the peach body, and finally brush with pink food coloring water to create a gradient blush effect.

9. **Bake**  
   Place the shaped pastries on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Preheat the oven to 145-150°C (293-302°F) and bake for about 20 minutes. Watch the first batch carefully to prevent over-browning. Bake until the layers are distinct and the pastries are fully cooked.

## Health Advice  
This pastry uses lard for flaky layers, resulting in a crispy texture but high calorie content. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened tea to cut the richness, and to limit consumption to 1-2 pieces per serving. Suitable for festive gatherings or afternoon tea. Those with poor digestion or watching their fat intake should enjoy in moderation.
