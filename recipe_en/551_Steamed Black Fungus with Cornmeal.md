# Steamed Black Fungus with Cornmeal  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Black fungus (dried, rehydrated), appropriate amount  
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (julienned)  
🌽 Fine cornmeal, about 1 tablespoon  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (finely diced)  
🌾 All-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon (for cleaning the fungus)  

🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌿 Five-spice powder, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil (raw), to taste  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 1 teaspoon  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, half a tablespoon  
🫘 Sesame seeds, to taste  
🌶️ Chili flakes, 1 tablespoon  
🫒 Hot oil, to taste  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥘 Steamer basket and steamer pot  
🍶 Small metal bowl (for sauce)  
🥄 Metal spoon  
🔗 Metal slotted spoon (for hot oil)  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Add 1 tablespoon of flour to the rehydrated black fungus. Mix well with your hands, allowing the flour to fully absorb impurities from the fungus's surface. Then add water, repeatedly rubbing and washing until the water runs clear. Finally, drain the fungus, squeeze out excess water, and place it in a bowl.  
   **Note**: The adsorbent power of the flour effectively cleans dirt from the fungus's crevices. Be sure to rub and wash until the water is clear.  

2. Add the julienned carrot, an appropriate amount of salt, five-spice powder, and cooking oil to the squeezed-dry fungus. Mix well. Then add about 1 tablespoon of fine cornmeal and mix again, ensuring each piece of fungus and each carrot strip is evenly coated with cornmeal.  
   **Note**: Adding the oil and cornmeal in sequence makes coating easier. The oil prevents sticking and enhances aroma.  

3. Spread the mixed fungus evenly on the steamer basket. Cover with a lid. Steam over high heat. Once steam rises, steam for 8 minutes.  
   **Note**: Spread the mixture evenly and loosely to avoid clumping, which can cause undercooking. Do not over-steam to maintain the fungus's chewy texture.  

4. Remove the steamed fungus and let it cool slightly. Use chopsticks to loosen it to prevent sticking.  

5. In a small bowl, combine sesame seeds, minced garlic, and chili flakes. Pour hot oil over them to bloom the aromatics. Then add light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, cilantro, scallions, and diced red bell pepper. Mix well.  
   **Note**: Pouring hot oil is key to releasing the dry ingredients' aroma. The oil must be high in temperature to quickly bloom the fragrance.  

6. Transfer the cooled, loosened steamed fungus to a plate. Pour the sauce over it, mix well, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish replaces refined carbohydrates with cornmeal, offering a rich source of dietary fiber. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or managing blood sugar levels.
