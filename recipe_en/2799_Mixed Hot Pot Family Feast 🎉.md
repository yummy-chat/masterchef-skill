# Mixed Hot Pot Family Feast 🎉

**Cuisine:** Beijing Cuisine  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Season:** Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 **White Meat (pork butt)** – appropriate amount, pre-cooked, sliced thick  
🐽 **Pork Belly** – appropriate amount, pre-cooked and cut into strips  
🐔 **White Chicken (drumsticks)** – appropriate amount, pre-cooked, deboned, sliced or shredded  
🦐 **Shrimp Meat (with pork fat)** – appropriate amount, for making shrimp dumpling filling  
🥩 **Pork (fat and lean)** – appropriate amount, for making eyebrow meatballs  
🍤 **Dried Shrimp (haimi)** – appropriate amount, for making broth  
🥚 **Eggs** – appropriate amount, for making egg skin and egg shreds  
🥕 **Carrot** – appropriate amount, cut into flower shapes, blanched  
🥬 **Lettuce Stem** – appropriate amount, cut into flower shapes, blanched  
🎍 **Winter Bamboo Shoots** – appropriate amount, cut into comb shapes, blanched  
🍄 **Dried Shiitake Mushrooms** – appropriate amount, rehydrated and steamed with scallion, ginger, cooking wine, and chicken fat  
🥬 **Cabbage Leaves** – appropriate amount, blanched, rinsed in cold water, and squeezed dry  
🍜 **Cellophane Noodles** – appropriate amount, softened in water and laid at the bottom  

🧅 **Scallion, Ginger** – appropriate amount  
🍶 **Yellow Rice Wine (Shaoxing wine)** – appropriate amount  
🧂 **Salt** – appropriate amount  
🌿 **White Pepper** – appropriate amount  
🌾 **Cornstarch** – appropriate amount (about 3g for shrimp filling)  
🥚 **Egg White** – 1 (for shrimp filling)  
🧈 **Chicken Fat** – appropriate amount  
🍬 **Sugar** – a small amount (to enhance flavor)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 **Knife, Cutting Board**  
🍳 **Cooking Pot**  
🥘 **Steamer**  
🫕 **Clay Pot (Sand Pot)**  
🥣 **Porcelain Bowl (inverted to press ingredients)**  
🥄 **Stainless Steel Bowl (for mixing fillings)**  
🍳 **Frying Pan (for making egg skin)**

## Preparation Steps
1. **Pre-cook Meats**: Place white meat, pork belly, and whole chicken drumsticks in cold water with scallion, ginger, and yellow rice wine. Cook until fully done. Pork belly takes about 1 hour until tender, chicken drumsticks about 30 minutes, and white meat about 35–40 minutes. Remove and rinse in cold water, set aside.
   **Note**: Pork belly should be tender but not mushy; chicken drumsticks just cooked through.

2. **Cutting and Preparing Garnishes**: Debone chicken drumsticks, slice or shred into strips; slice white meat and pork belly into thick pieces or strips; slice carrot and lettuce stem into flower shapes, cut winter bamboo shoots into comb shapes. Blanch or steam all vegetables and mushrooms separately.
   **Note**: When arranging, keep the cut side facing up.

3. **Make Eyebrow Meatballs**: Combine ground pork (fat and lean) with chopped scallion, ginger, white pepper, salt, yellow rice wine, cornstarch, and a small amount of water. Stir in one direction until the mixture is firm and elastic. Squeeze the mixture from your fist, scrape off with a knife into spindle shapes (pointed at both ends). Steam for 4–5 minutes to set.
   **Key Point**: Add water to the meat mixture and always stir in the same direction for better elasticity.

4. **Make Shrimp Dumplings**: Combine shrimp filling with chopped scallion, ginger, yellow rice wine, egg white, salt, white pepper, and cornstarch. Mix well. Make thin egg skins using more yolks and less egg white. Place one egg skin, put shrimp filling in the center, fold in half, and pinch the edges to form a dumpling. Steam for 3 minutes.
   **Note**: Make egg skin as thin as possible; when sealing, just pinch together, do not squeeze.

5. **Prepare Cabbage and Noodles**: Blanch cabbage leaves in boiling water until soft, immediately rinse in cold water, squeeze dry; soak cellophane noodles in warm water until soft.
6. **Steam Dried Shrimp**: Add yellow rice wine, scallion, and ginger to dried shrimp. Steam for 20 minutes to obtain a rich shrimp broth.
7. **Assemble the Clay Pot**: Lay cabbage leaves and cellophane noodles at the bottom of the clay pot. Arrange white meat, pork belly, chicken slices, carrot, lettuce stem, winter bamboo shoots, and shiitake mushrooms symmetrically on top, cut side up. Place eyebrow meatballs and shrimp dumplings on the top layer, garnish with mushrooms and egg shreds.
8. **Braise to Blend Flavors**: Pour the steamed shrimp and its broth into the clay pot. Invert a porcelain bowl or plate slightly smaller than the pot's opening on top of the ingredients to press them down. In another pot, bring chicken or meat broth to a boil, season with salt, white pepper, and a small amount of sugar. While boiling hot, slowly pour along the edge of the clay pot until ingredients are submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and braise for about 10 minutes to allow all flavors to meld.
   **Key Point**: Broth must be boiled before seasoning; the inverted bowl prevents ingredients from floating and breaking the arrangement; all ingredients are already cooked, this step is only for flavor integration.

9. **Serve**: Carefully remove the inverted bowl or plate and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This family feast clay pot is nutritionally balanced, with protein from various meats, shrimp, and eggs, paired with a variety of vegetables, making it ideal for gatherings and nourishment. Note that all meats are pre-cooked, so braising time should not be too long to avoid over-softening. People with high blood pressure can reduce salt. While this pot provides comprehensive nutrition, watch your total intake to avoid excess calories.
