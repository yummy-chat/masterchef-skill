# Steamed Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍞 Bread flour 250g  
🍬 White sugar 55g (25g for dough, 30g for filling)  
🥚 Eggs 4 (3 whole eggs + 1 egg yolk for dough, 1 whole egg for filling)  
🧪 High-sugar-tolerant dry yeast 2.5g  
🧂 Salt 1.5g  
🫒 Cooking oil 25g (no strong odor)  
🥥 Shredded coconut 75g  
🥛 Milk powder 30g  
🧈 Butter 40g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔪 Knife  
🔗 Food tongs  
🎞️ Plastic wrap (must be heat-resistant)  
🖌️ Brush  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**: Mix bread flour, 25g white sugar, salt, and high-sugar-tolerant dry yeast until combined. Add 3 whole eggs and 1 egg yolk, stir into a shaggy dough, and knead until slightly sticky. Add the cooking oil, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 1 hour to rest.
   **Note**: Refrigerating allows the gluten to develop naturally with less effort; slightly sticky dough is ideal.
2. **Strengthen the gluten**: Take out the rested dough and use techniques such as slapping, folding, or pushing and rubbing to fully incorporate the oil into the dough until the surface is smooth.
3. **Make the coconut filling**: Place shredded coconut, milk powder, butter, 30g white sugar, and 1 egg in a bowl. Mix with your hands until moist and able to form a ball.
   **Note**: If the filling feels too dry, add a small amount of oil as needed.
4. **Shape the dough**: Roll the dough into a rectangular sheet and make a dividing line in the middle. Spread the coconut filling evenly over one half, brush water on the edge of the other half, and fold it over to cover. Slightly roll the folded sheet longer. Cut the lower half into strips about 1cm wide, twist each strip into a spiral, then roll the entire piece from top to bottom.
5. **Second proofing**: Place the rolled dough into a bowl with the inside brushed with oil. Cover with plastic wrap and let it proof in a warm place until doubled in size.
   **Key indicator**: The dough has visibly risen and springs back slowly when lightly pressed; the bottom of the bowl should not feel hot to the touch to avoid killing the yeast.
6. **Pre-steam decoration**: After proofing, brush the surface of the dough evenly with a layer of egg yolk. Seal the bowl tightly with heat-resistant plastic wrap.
   **Note**: Sealing with plastic wrap prevents condensation from dripping onto the bread and helps achieve an even color on the surface.
7. **Steam**: Place the bowl in the steamer. You can start with cold water or add it after the water has boiled. After the water comes to a boil, reduce to medium heat and steam for 40 minutes.
   **Key**: After steaming, turn off the heat and let it sit for 3 minutes before uncovering to prevent the bread from shrinking due to temperature shock.
8. **Remove from steamer**: Take out the steamed bread, let it cool slightly, then invert and unmold.

## Health Advice  
This steamed bread uses a relatively high amount of oil and sugar, so it is recommended as an occasional dessert rather than a substitute for a main meal. The coconut filling is rich in dietary fiber, but the butter and eggs also add calories. It is best shared with family to reduce individual intake.
