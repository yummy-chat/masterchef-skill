# Braised Crab with Chopped Chili in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 1/2 Onion (shredded)
🫚适量 Ginger (sliced)
🦀 2 Winter Crabs (female swimming crabs, about 1 jin 4 liang 7 qian)
🌱适量 Scallions (chopped)
🌶️ About 2 tbsp Chopped Chili Sauce
🫒适量 Cooking Oil
🍶 About 1 tbsp High-proof White Liquor

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Clay Pot
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🥄 Spatula / Wok Ladle
✂️ Kitchen Scissors

## Cooking Steps
1. Shred the onion, slice the ginger, and chop the scallions. Set aside.
2. Hold the crab by its two rear legs and lift it up. Open the crab shell. Remove the crab heart and the strip-shaped gills on both sides, and cut off the meatless edges of the shell.
3. Cut off the large crab claws and crack them with the flat side of a knife; cut off the crab legs at a diagonal from the base, and cut the crab body in half.
   **Note**: The crab heart, gills, and sand sac on the edge of the shell are inedible and must be thoroughly removed.
4. Heat the clay pot, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. When the oil is hot, add the ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant, then add the shredded onion and stir-fry evenly.
   **Key Point**: Lining the bottom with onion prevents the crab from directly contacting the pot and burning.
5. Arrange the crab pieces neatly on top of the onion (do not add the crab shell yet). Evenly spoon the chopped chili sauce onto each piece of crab meat.
6. Cover the clay pot and braise over medium heat for 8 minutes.
7. After 8 minutes, uncover the pot. Turn the reserved crab shell over and place it back on top of the crab meat.
8. Pour the high-proof white liquor along the edge of the clay pot. Flames will appear instantly, releasing a burst of aroma.
9. Finally, sprinkle with chopped scallions for garnish and serve.
   **Finishing Touch**: Adding high-proof white liquor is key to removing the fishy smell and enhancing the aroma, imparting a rich wok hei.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein, but the chopped chili and oil content are relatively high. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables and multigrain rice for easier digestion. Suitable for winter nourishment, but those with high blood lipids or gout should control their consumption.
