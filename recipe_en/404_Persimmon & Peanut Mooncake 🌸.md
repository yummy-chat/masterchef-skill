# Persimmon & Peanut Mooncake 🌸
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍯 Corn syrup / inverted sugar syrup 150g  
🥜 Peanut oil 50g  
🌾 All-purpose flour 220g  
🍵 Matcha powder 1g  
🥮 Mooncake filling, as needed (e.g., white lotus seed paste, red bean paste)  
🧪 Lye water 4g  
🎨 Orange food coloring, as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🍥 Kneading mat  
🌕 Persimmon-shaped mooncake mold  
🔥 Oven  
📄 Baking paper / aluminum foil  

## Instructions
1. Pour the corn syrup, peanut oil, and lye water into a glass bowl. Whisk quickly until fully emulsified—the liquid should be smooth and silky.  
   **Note**: Emulsification must be thorough; this is the foundation for preventing the crust from becoming oily or cracking.

2. Sift in the all-purpose flour. Wearing disposable gloves, knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough. The initial dough will be sticky—knead patiently until it becomes glossy.  
   **Note**: Do not over-knead, as this could develop gluten and affect the texture.

3. Set aside 20g of the dough and knead in the matcha powder until evenly green—this will form the “persimmon leaf” portion. Add orange food coloring to the remaining dough and knead until evenly orange-red—this will be the “persimmon body.” Cover both portions with plastic wrap and rest for 1 hour.  
   **Key**: Resting is essential; otherwise, the dough will shrink back and be impossible to mold.

4. Roll the rested orange-red dough into a long log and cut into equal portions. Take one portion, flatten it, place a ball of filling in the center, and use your thumb and forefinger to gradually push the dough upward to seal, completely encasing the filling.

5. Press a small amount of green dough into the calyx pattern of the mold first, then place the filled orange-red dough ball inside. Press down firmly and vertically on the mold handle, then release to shape.  
   **Note**: The leaf part must be filled first to achieve a realistic persimmon shape.

6. Preheat the oven. Arrange the shaped mooncakes on a baking sheet lined with baking paper and bake.  
   **Important**: When the baking time is halfway through, quickly cover the baking sheet with a piece of aluminum foil to prevent the tops from browning too deeply or cracking.

7. After baking, remove and let cool. Seal in a container to allow the mooncakes to soften and return oil—they will taste better this way.

## Health Advice
These persimmon-shaped mooncakes are visually appealing, but the syrup and peanut oil are not low in calories. It is recommended to enjoy only one at a time, paired with unsweetened tea to cut the richness. They are perfect for festive occasions, but those with diabetes or watching their sugar intake should only have a very small taste.
