# Stir-Fried Duck with Young Ginger
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Duck meat ½ (about 1000g)  
🌱 Young ginger about 500g (used in two batches)  
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers appropriate amount  
🥒 Pickled ginger appropriate amount  
🌶️ Small green peppers appropriate amount  
🌶️ Millet peppers appropriate amount  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch  
🧈 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount  
🥓 Lard appropriate amount  
🌶️ Broad bean paste 1 tbsp  
🍺 Beer 1 bottle  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🧂 Chicken essence a pinch  
🧂 Ground white pepper a pinch  
🧴 Soy sauce a pinch  

## Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Steps  
1. Cut the duck meat into evenly sized pieces. Slice most of the young ginger into thick slices, and a small portion into thin slices. Cut pickled chili peppers into small rings, and dice the pickled ginger into small pieces. Cut small green peppers and millet peppers into short sections. Prepare the Sichuan peppercorns.  
**Note**: Use an ample amount of young ginger, adding it in two batches—a small portion for stewing to infuse flavor, and the majority for the final stir-fry to keep it crisp and tender.

2. Heat the wok, add rapeseed oil and lard. Once the oil is hot, add Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant, then immediately add the duck pieces. Stir-fry over medium-high heat.  
**Key point**: Continuously stir-fry until the oil becomes clear, the duck pieces are lightly browned on the surface, and most of the moisture has evaporated. This removes gaminess and enhances aroma.

3. Add the pickled ginger and pickled chili peppers, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add broad bean paste, reduce heat to low, and stir-fry slowly until red oil releases and the paste becomes crispy.  
**Note**: Stir-fry the broad bean paste over low heat to prevent burning. Only when the oil turns a bright red will the paste fully release its flavor.

4. Pour in the entire bottle of beer. Add a small amount of young ginger slices, MSG, chicken essence, ground white pepper, and a little soy sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 20 minutes until the duck meat is tender.  
**Key point**: Add a small portion of young ginger early in the stewing to allow the duck to absorb the ginger flavor. The beer helps remove gaminess and adds a complex aroma.

5. In a separate wok, heat a little oil. Add the majority of the young ginger slices and the green and red pepper sections. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat until just cooked but still crisp.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry quickly to preserve the crisp texture of the young ginger and peppers, avoiding overcooking that makes them soft.

6. Pour the stewed duck meat along with its liquid into the wok with the stir-fried young ginger and peppers. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens and evenly coats all ingredients. Then remove from heat and plate.  
**Note**: Use high heat at the end to reduce the sauce, allowing the young ginger to absorb the duck broth while staying crunchy, resulting in a harmonious blend of flavors.

## Health Advice  
Stir-fried duck with young ginger is rich in protein and various vitamins, suitable for nourishing the body in summer. However, duck meat is cool in nature, so those with a cold spleen and stomach should pair it with ginger. Be mindful of the beer amount; this dish is not recommended for people with high uric acid levels.
