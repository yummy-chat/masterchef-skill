# Beef and Radish Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef 500g (chuck, 20% fat, 80% lean)
🥒 White radish 500g (shredded, blanched, squeezed dry, and chopped)
🧅 Scallion 50g (finely chopped)
🧄 Ginger 20g (minced)
💧 Water 250g (for incorporating into the filling)
🧂 Ground Sichuan pepper 3g
🧂 Ground white pepper 2g
🍾 Shaoxing wine 40g
🧴 Soy sauce 40g
🦪 Oyster sauce 30g
🧂 Salt 1-2g (adjust to taste)
🍬 Sugar 2g
🧂 MSG 2g
🫒 Vegetable oil 10g
🫒 Sesame oil 10g

## Equipment
🥒🔪 Box grater
🍲 Pot for boiling
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Ladle
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
❄️ Refrigerator

## Instructions
1. Shred the white radish. Blanch in boiling water for about 30 seconds until softened. Remove, rinse with cold water, squeeze dry, and chop. Finely chop the scallion and mince the ginger.
**Note**: Do not over-blanch; do not wait for the water to return to a boil, or the radish will become mushy and lose its texture.

2. In a bowl, combine the ground beef with scallion, ginger, ground Sichuan pepper, ground white pepper, and Shaoxing wine. Mix well to eliminate any gamey smell and enhance aroma.
3. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, and MSG. Stir vigorously in one direction to evenly color and season the filling.
4. Add the vegetable oil, stir briefly, then add the water in four batches, stirring continuously in the same direction each time. Total mixing time should be about 15 minutes, until the filling has completely absorbed the liquid and becomes thick and cohesive.
**Key point**: Use 250g of water per 500g of meat. Add gradually and start stirring slowly, then speed up for a juicy texture.

5. Add the chopped radish and mix thoroughly with the meat filling.
6. Finally, drizzle in the sesame oil and mix well to seal in moisture and aroma. Refrigerate the filling for about 30 minutes to firm up and set.
7. Wrap the filling in dumpling wrappers, shaping them like gold ingots.
8. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the dumplings and cook until they float. Then add a splash of cold water three times, allowing the dumplings to cook through.
**Note**: Adding cold water prevents the wrappers from bursting due to continuous boiling and ensures the filling is fully cooked.

## Health Tips
Beef and radish dumplings are nutritionally balanced. Beef is rich in iron, while radish aids digestion. They are suitable for those managing fitness and calorie intake. Patience during the water-incorporating step is essential; otherwise, the filling may become dry and tough, affecting the final texture.
