# Fresh Pork Soup Dumplings (Xiaolongbao)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 500g pork front leg meat (30% fat, 70% lean, frozen for 2 hours in advance to remove acidity)
🌾 500g all-purpose flour
💧 280g water (for dough) + 150g water (for scallion-ginger water)
🍬 10g sugar
🧈 15g lard
🧅 Scallions, as needed (some crushed for scallion-ginger water, some chopped for garnish)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (some crushed for scallion-ginger water, some sliced for flavored oil)
🧅 Large scallion/onion, as needed (onion cut into chunks, large scallion cut into sections for flavored oil)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (for flavored oil)
🧂 3g salt
🌶️ 2g white pepper
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🥡 Light soy sauce, to taste
🥡 Dark soy sauce, a small amount
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
⭐ Star anise, as needed (broken into pieces)
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for flavored oil)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer
🎋 Bamboo steamer
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍞 Kneading mat

## Instructions
1. Place the acid-removed ground pork front leg meat in a bowl. Add salt, white pepper, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and a small amount of dark soy sauce in sequence, and stir evenly.
   **Note**: The ground meat must be frozen in advance to remove acidity; do not use room-temperature meat directly.

2. Mix 150g water with crushed scallions and ginger. Squeeze the mixture with your hands to extract the scallion-ginger juice. Pour into the ground meat in two batches. After each addition, stir vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed, the meat filling becomes sticky, and it adheres to the bottom of the bowl.

3. Repeatedly slap the stirred meat filling against the bowl more than ten times to enhance its firmness and elasticity.

4. Pour vegetable oil into a wok. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic, onion chunks, broken star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns to the cold oil. Fry slowly over low heat.
   **Key point**: Maintain low heat throughout. Fry until all solid ingredients turn golden brown, then remove them with a fine mesh strainer. Set the oil aside to cool.

5. Add one spoonful of the cooled flavored oil to the meat filling and mix thoroughly. Seal the meat filling and place it in the freezer for about 2 hours to firm up, making it easier to wrap later.

6. Mix lard with flour evenly. Dissolve yeast and sugar in 280g water, then add the liquid to the flour in batches. Stir with chopsticks to form flocculent dough, then knead into a smooth dough.
   **Note**: The yeast must be dissolved in warm water first; the water temperature should not be too high. Adding lard enhances the shine of the dough skin.

7. Seal the dough and let it ferment naturally at room temperature until it visibly expands in volume, is full of air pockets, and has a fluffy texture. This takes about 1.5 hours.

8. Take out the fermented dough, dust it with dry flour, and repeatedly and firmly knead and press to fully expel the gas, until the dough returns to its pre-fermentation size and has a smooth surface.

9. Roll the dough into a long, thin strip. Use a pinching motion to tear off dough pieces, each weighing about 15g. Dust with dry flour to prevent sticking, and cover with plastic wrap to prevent the surface from drying out.

10. Remove the frozen meat filling and mix in chopped scallions. Take one dough piece, pinch it with your fingers into a wrapper that is thick in the center and thin at the edges. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Use the index finger of your right hand to push and pleat the wrapper while rotating it with your left hand. Finally, pinch the top to seal tightly.
    **Note**: Do not use a rolling pin; shape the wrapper by hand. The filling must be frozen until slightly firm to be easy to wrap.

11. Once the steamer produces steam, place the raw dumplings inside. Steam over medium heat for 8 minutes, then turn off the heat and let them rest for 5 minutes before removing.
    **Key point**: Ensure the water is boiling and producing steam before placing the dumplings in the steamer. Do not open the lid immediately after turning off the heat; let them rest fully to prevent shrinkage.

## Health Advice
Fresh pork soup dumplings use a 30% fat, 70% lean meat filling mixed with scallion-ginger water. They are rich in protein but have a high fat content. It is recommended to eat them with vinegar and ginger shreds to cut the grease. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
