# Stir-Fried Pork with Scallion and Wood Ear Mushroom  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork slices (适量, thinly sliced)  
🧅 Scallion (适量, cut diagonally into sections)  
🍄 Wood ear mushroom (适量, rehydrated before use)  
🫗 Light soy sauce (适量, added in two batches)  
🫗 Dark soy sauce (a little, for color)  
🧂 White pepper powder (适量)  
🌾 Cornstarch (适量)  
🫒 Cooking oil (适量, for marinating and stir-frying)  
🧂 MSG (a pinch)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. Place the pork slices in a stainless steel bowl. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, white pepper powder, and cornstarch. Use your hands to thoroughly mix everything together.  
   **Note**: Then drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil, continue mixing, and let it marinate for 15 minutes to lock in moisture and make the meat tender.  
2. Heat the wok over high heat and add cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork slices. Quickly stir-fry until the meat changes color, then immediately remove and set aside.  
   **Key point**: The meat is done as soon as it changes color; do not overcook.  
3. In the same wok, heat a new batch of oil. Add the scallion sections and stir-fry until fragrant.  
4. Add the rehydrated wood ear mushrooms, a little more light soy sauce, and MSG. Stir-fry until everything is evenly combined.  
5. Return the previously cooked pork slices to the wok. Turn the heat to high and quickly toss all ingredients together until well mixed and heated through. Then remove from heat and plate.  
   **Note**: After returning the meat to the wok, work quickly—only mix and heat through to prevent the meat from becoming tough due to prolonged cooking.  

## Health Tips  
Wood ear mushrooms help cleanse blood vessels, scallions have antibacterial properties, and pork provides high-quality protein. This combination offers balanced nutrition and is suitable for those managing fat intake. When cooking, use less oil and salt.
