# Tomato Crucian Carp and Tofu Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 crucian carp (scored with crescent cuts on the surface)
🧈 About 1 piece of firm tofu (cut into triangle pieces)
🍅 1 tomato (score a cross on top, blanch in boiling water for 30 seconds, then peel and chop finely)
🫚 About 3 slices of ginger
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount for marinating, 1 spoon for seasoning
🍶 Cooking wine: appropriate amount (for marinating)
🥫 1 spoon of tomato paste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Soup ladle

## Steps
1. **Preparation**
   - Slice the ginger, cut the firm tofu into triangle pieces.
   - Score the surface of the crucian carp with crescent cuts, evenly sprinkle a layer of salt, drizzle a little cooking wine, place ginger slices on top, and marinate for 10 minutes to thoroughly remove any fishy smell.
   - Score a cross on the tomato, blanch in boiling water for about 30 seconds, peel, and chop finely; set aside.

2. **Fry the tofu**
   - Heat the pan, add oil. Once the oil temperature rises, add the tofu pieces and fry until both sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
   - **Note**: Do not wash the pan used for frying the tofu; it will be used directly for frying the crucian carp.

3. **Fry the crucian carp**
   - Gently place the marinated crucian carp into the pan. Keep the heat at medium-low and fry for about 2 minutes until the bottom is set.
   - Gently shake the pan to ensure even heating of the fish body. Flip and continue frying for another 2 minutes. Make sure the head and tail are fully fried. Once both sides are well set and the fish skin releases its gelatin, it is ready.

4. **Stir-fry the tomato soup base**
   - Push the fried crucian carp to the side of the pan. Add the chopped tomato to the pan and stir-fry over high heat until the juice is released and the tomato softens.
   - Add 1 spoon of tomato paste and continue stir-frying until the tomato becomes thick and pasty, fully blending with the fish-flavored oil at the bottom of the pan.

5. **Simmer the fish soup**
   - Pour enough **boiling water** into the pan, and add the previously fried tofu pieces.
   - Keep the heat on high and bring to a boil. Continue boiling vigorously for about 10 minutes until the soup turns a milky white with a golden hue and a thick consistency.

6. **Season and serve**
   - Once the soup is thick, add 1 spoon of salt for seasoning.
   - Adjust the saltiness to your taste, then serve. The fish should remain intact, the tofu fully absorbs the broth, and the soup is golden and shiny.

## Health Advice
This tomato crucian carp and tofu soup is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it especially suitable for children, the elderly, or those recovering from surgery who need calcium supplementation. The soup is milky white but moderate in calories. Using boiling water is key to achieving the right texture. It is recommended to control the salt amount to avoid excess sodium.
