# Matcha Cream Cheese Lye Buns
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍰 Cream cheese 130g (for passion fruit cream cheese filling)
🍈 Passion fruit 2 pieces (juice extracted, seeds removed, for filling)
🍬 Powdered sugar 20g (for filling, amount can be adjusted based on passion fruit sweetness)
🌾 Bread flour 278g
🍵 Matcha powder 4.5g
🫗 Active dry yeast 3g
🧂 Salt 3g
🧈 Butter 15g
💧 Ice water 150g (water amount can be adjusted based on dough consistency)
🥛 Milk as needed (for brushing the surface after baking to create a shiny finish)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🧴 Strainer
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Mixing bowl
🔄 Stand mixer
🧁 Piping bag
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍳 Baking tray (lined with parchment paper)
🔥 Oven
🧤 Oven mitts
🔪 Scoring knife
🧤 Double-layer gloves (for lye water handling, corrosion protection)

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Phase**

1. **Make passion fruit cream cheese filling**
   Strain the passion fruit pulp through a strainer to extract juice and remove seeds. Add cream cheese and powdered sugar to the juice, and stir until smooth and free of lumps.
   **Key point**: The amount of powdered sugar can be flexibly adjusted according to the sweetness of the passion fruit; this filling has a relatively thin consistency and is not easy to shape.

2. **Portion and freeze the filling**
   Place the mixed filling into a piping bag, pipe it onto plastic wrap in portions, roll each portion into a thick strip, and arrange them flat on a tray.
   **Key point**: Complete the filling portioning before making the dough and freeze it in advance to solidify, making it easier to wrap later.

3. **Make matcha lye bun dough**
   Combine bread flour, matcha powder, active dry yeast, salt, butter, and ice water in a stand mixer. Knead until the surface is smooth.
   **Texture check**: Knead the dough until smooth; no extensive gluten development is needed.
   **Key point**: The amount of matcha powder is small, resulting in a subtle matcha flavor in the finished product; water amount can be slightly adjusted based on dough softness.

4. **Divide and shape the dough**
   Divide the dough into 6 equal portions. Take one portion, roll it out, wrap a frozen strip of passion fruit cream cheese filling, and seal the seam tightly. Shape the filled dough into an olive shape with a raised center and tapered ends.
   **Key point**: Using pre-frozen filling strips greatly simplifies the process.

5. **Freeze the dough**
   Place the shaped dough pieces on a baking tray and freeze them in the refrigerator for 40 minutes.
   **Key point**: Freezing before dipping in lye water helps maintain shape stability and makes handling easier.

6. **Prepare lye water and dip**
   Prepare the lye water according to the recipe. Wearing double-layer gloves, dip the frozen dough pieces into the lye water, quickly remove them, and place them on a wire rack to drain.
   **Key point**: Double-layer gloves must be worn and checked for damage to prevent lye water from corroding the skin.

7. **Score the dough**
   Using a scoring knife, make deep, long cuts on the surface of the lye-dipped dough pieces. The cuts should extend nearly to the pointed ends.
   **Key point**: The cuts need to be deep and long for a more attractive burst after baking.

**Baking Phase**

8. **Bake the buns**
   Place the scored dough pieces into a preheated oven.
   **Temperature/Time**: 180°C, bake for 20 minutes.
   **Texture check**: During baking, the dough pieces will gradually darken in color, and the cheese filling will expand and overflow through the scored cuts.
   **Key point**: Let them cool slightly after removing from the oven; the overflowed filling will retract a bit. Brush the surface with milk while still hot to create a shiny finish.

## Health Advice
Matcha has antioxidant properties, and passion fruit is rich in vitamin C, but the cream cheese filling and powdered sugar are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume these buns as a snack in small amounts. These lye buns are suitable for replenishing energy before workouts, but those with sensitive stomachs should be cautious about the potential irritation from lye water. Always wear double-layer gloves when handling lye water to prevent chemical burns.
