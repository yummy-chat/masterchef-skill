# Braised Grouper Skin with Abalone Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Dried grouper skin 1 piece (use part)
🥩 Lean pork, as needed
🍖 Pork ribs, as needed
🦶 Pork trotters, as needed
🐔 Old chicken 1 (blanched)
🐚 Dried abalone (8-head), as needed (rehydrated)
🐾 Chicken feet, as needed
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, as needed
🌊 Dried scallops, as needed
🍯 Rock sugar, as needed
🫘 Red dates, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🥬 Asparagus, as needed (for plating)
🍶 Chicken rice dark soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🍲 Stock (optional), as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Oil pot
🫕 Clay pot
🍲 Wok
🔪 Cutting board, knives
🥄 Skimmer

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the dried grouper skin in a steamer and steam over high heat for 30 minutes until soft and curled, then immediately transfer to ice water and soak for 2 hours.
**Key Point**: Ice immediately after steaming to make the skin more springy and bouncy.

2. After soaking, rub off the scales from the surface of the skin, flip it over, and scrape off any blood and impurities on the inner side with a small knife to thoroughly remove the fishy smell.
3. Heat oil in a pot. Add ginger slices and fry until slightly charred, then remove. Add lean pork pieces and rib pieces and fry until golden brown, then remove. Increase the oil temperature, add pork trotter pieces, and fry until colored.
4. Line the bottom of a clay pot with the fried ginger slices. Layer in the fried lean pork, fried ribs, blanched old chicken, dried abalone, fried pork trotters, chicken feet, dried shiitake mushrooms, dried scallops, rock sugar, and red dates.
5. Add water or stock until all ingredients are submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer for 10 hours.
6. Uncover, add chicken rice dark soy sauce for color and seasoning, stir well, then continue to simmer covered over the lowest heat for 2 hours until the abalone sauce is thick.
7. Place the prepared grouper skin in a wok, add ginger slices and an appropriate amount of broth, bring to a boil, then remove the skin and transfer it to the pot with the abalone sauce.
8. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer for another 2 hours until the skin is fully infused with the sauce, soft, and glutinous.
9. Remove the skin and cut into pieces. Arrange on a plate with the abalone and blanched asparagus. Adjust the salt in the abalone sauce to taste, then pour the sauce over the fish skin and abalone.
**Key Tips**: Slow simmering over low heat throughout is the core of the flavor; removing the scales and blood impurities must be thorough, otherwise, it will affect the deodorizing effect; be careful when frying pork trotters as oil may splatter.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in collagen and suitable for those focused on skin health. However, it is high in purines and fat, so those with gout or high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the flavor.
