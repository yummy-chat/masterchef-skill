# Honey BBQ Pork (Char Siu)

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients
🍖 500g skinless pork belly (or skinless pork shoulder/butt)
🧂 2g baking soda
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking)
🧂 2g salt
🍬 40g sugar
🍯 20g honey (plus extra for making the glaze)
🥫 20g light soy sauce
🦪 20g oyster sauce
🍯 20g fermented red bean curd (nam yu)
🍯 10g fermented red bean curd liquid
🥜 20g sesame paste
🥃 5g rose wine (or baijiu/Chinese white liquor)
🍯 50g maltose (or substitute entirely with honey)
🍬 5g white sugar (for the glaze)

## Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧻 Paper towels
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🛍️ Sealable bag
🔥 Oven (with convection fan preferred)
🍗 Wire rack + baking sheet (lined with parchment paper)
🍳 Small saucepan
🍳 Frying pan
🌡️ Thermometer

## Instructions
1. Cut the skinless pork belly into evenly thick strips to ensure even flavor absorption and cooking.
2. Place the pork strips in a bowl, add enough water to cover, add 2g baking soda, gently wash, then soak for 30 minutes.
   - The baking soda solution helps remove gamey odors and tenderizes the meat.
3. Discard the soaking water, rinse thoroughly with clean water, and pat the pork strips completely dry with paper towels.
   - Excess moisture prevents the marinade from adhering properly and affects marination.
4. Mix salt, sugar, honey, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, fermented red bean curd, fermented red bean curd liquid, sesame paste, and rose wine into a smooth, thick marinade.
   - For a simpler version, substitute the homemade marinade with 125g store-bought char siu sauce + 5g baijiu.
5. Place the dried pork strips in a container, pour in the marinade, and massage thoroughly to coat each strip evenly.
6. Cover the container with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 8 hours.
   - If short on time, massage the meat every 15 minutes to speed up marination.
7. Remove the marinated pork from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 1–2 hours to warm up naturally; or place in a sealed bag and submerge in approximately 60°C water for 10–15 minutes until the meat temperature reaches 30–40°C and is no longer cold.
   - This step prevents the outside from burning while the inside remains undercooked, and helps lock in juices.
8. Preheat the oven to 180°C with the convection fan on. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place a wire rack on top.
9. Arrange the warmed pork strips flat on the wire rack and place in the middle of the oven. Bake at 180°C for 15 minutes.
10. Remove the baking sheet, flip the pork, brush with the reserved marinade, then return to the oven and continue baking at 180°C for 10–15 minutes, until the surface is caramelized and the edges are slightly charred.
11. In a small saucepan, combine maltose, white sugar, and 10g water. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until thick and syrupy enough to coat a spoon.
    - Alternatively, combine leftover marinade with honey or maltose and simmer over low heat for a more flavorful glaze.
12. Let the baked char siu cool slightly, slice into thick pieces, arrange on a plate, and drizzle with the prepared honey or glaze.

## Health Advice
This char siu is high in sugar and sodium. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables and mixed grains, and control portion size. Suitable for those needing protein supplementation while watching calorie intake. People with high blood sugar or high blood pressure should consume sparingly.
