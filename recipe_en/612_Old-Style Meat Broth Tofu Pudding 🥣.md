# Old-Style Meat Broth Tofu Pudding 🥣
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🫘 Dried soybeans 150g
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking, enough to cover beans by two knuckles; 1200g for making soy milk; 30g for dissolving glucono delta-lactone)
🧂 Glucono delta-lactone 3g
🍄 Dried black fungus 5g (pre-soaked)
🌼 Dried daylily buds 20g (pre-soaked, tough stems removed)
🥩 Pork loin 50g (cut into thin strips)
🥕 Carrot 20g (cut into thin strips)
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten)
🧅 Scallion 5g (minced)
🧄 Ginger 5g (minced)
🧄 Garlic 5g (minced)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 2g
⭐ Star anise ½ piece
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for making Sichuan peppercorn oil and stir-frying)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder 0.5g
🍾 Light soy sauce 20g
🍾 Dark soy sauce 3g
🍲 Beef bouillon powder 10g (essential seasoning; substitute with MSG and salt if unavailable)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g
🧂 Salt 2g (adjust to taste)
🥔 Potato starch 15g + water 30g (mix into slurry; potato starch is required)
🌿 Cilantro, a small amount (for final garnish)

## Kitchenware & Equipment 🛠️
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking beans, filtering soy milk, and mixing glucono delta-lactone solution)
🔪 Blender (or soy milk maker, juicer)
🏺 100-mesh strainer (or soy milk bag)
🍲 Small stainless steel saucepan (for boiling soy milk)
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for making the gravy)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for frying Sichuan peppercorns)
🥄 Ladle (for skimming foam, stirring, and serving)
🍽️ Large bowl or glass bowl (for holding soy milk to set)

## Preparation Steps 👩‍🍳
1. Place the dried soybeans in a basin, add water until it covers the beans by two knuckles. Refrigerate and soak overnight until the beans can be easily crushed between fingers.
2. Rinse the soaked soybeans repeatedly with cold water, drain, and transfer to a blender. Add 1200g of water and blend twice using the "fruit & vegetable" setting to maximize extraction.
3. Strain the raw soy milk through a 100-mesh strainer or soy milk bag to remove the okara. You should have about 1300ml of raw soy milk.
4. Skim off the foam from the raw soy milk, pour it into a small saucepan, and heat over medium-high heat, stirring constantly. When the soy milk appears to "false boil," immediately reduce to the lowest heat and continue cooking for 2 minutes to remove the beany flavor.
5. After turning off the heat, let the soy milk rest until a "yuba" (tofu skin) film can be lifted from the surface; the temperature should be around 80°C (176°F).
   **Key Point**: If the soy milk is too hot, it will over-coagulate; if too cool, it won’t set. The ideal temperature is when a chopstick can lift the yuba film.
6. In a large bowl, dissolve 3g of glucono delta-lactone in 30g of water, stirring until fully dissolved. From a height, quickly pour the rested soy milk (with the film removed) into the glucono delta-lactone solution. Immediately skim off any surface foam with a spoon, cover, and let it set for 20 minutes. Do not move the bowl during this time.
7. Meanwhile, prepare the gravy ingredients: slice the soaked black fungus and daylily buds, cut the pork loin into thin strips, julienne the carrot, mince the scallion, ginger, and garlic, beat the egg, and mix the potato starch with 30g of water to form a slurry.
8. Make the gravy: Heat cooking oil in a wok over high heat. Place the Sichuan peppercorns in a slotted spoon and fry over low heat until they darken, then discard the peppercorns, leaving the oil. Add half a star anise and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the pork strips and stir-fry quickly until they change color. Add the minced scallion, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry until aromatic. Then add the shredded black fungus, daylily buds, and carrot strips, and stir-fry evenly.
9. Add the thirteen-spice powder and light soy sauce to the wok. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add 500g of water and bring to a boil.
10. Once boiling, add dark soy sauce for color, along with beef bouillon powder, chicken bouillon, and salt. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 2 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
11. Turn the heat back to high. Stir the prepared potato starch slurry well, then drizzle it into the wok in small batches, stirring constantly, until the gravy thickens and becomes glossy. Keep the heat high and drizzle the beaten egg in a circular motion. Once the egg flowers rise, gently push them apart. The gravy is now ready.
12. Once the tofu pudding has set, use a spoon to scoop out slices of the tofu pudding into bowls. Ladle the hot old-style meat broth gravy over the top, and garnish with cilantro before serving.

## Health Tips 🌿
This dish is rich in high-quality plant and animal protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, the gravy contains a high sodium content, so individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the salt. Although the traditional preparation is time-consuming, this dish offers balanced nutrition and is best enjoyed with a side of mixed-grain staples.
