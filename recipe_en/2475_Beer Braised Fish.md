# Beer Braised Fish
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Common carp (or similar freshwater fish) 1-2 pieces (no more than 2 catties, cut into pieces)  
🍅 Tomatoes 2-3 (cut into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (sliced)  
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices  
🌿 Garlic sprouts (green garlic) 2-3 stalks (use only the leaves, cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallions 1 bunch (finely chopped, mainly for rice tofu but optional)  
🍖 Minced meat about 3 yuan worth (for rice tofu, optional)  
🫘 Pickled long beans as needed (for rice tofu, optional)  
🌶️ Pickled chilies as needed (for rice tofu, optional)  
🧂 Light soy sauce as needed (2 tbsp for sauce + 1 tbsp during braising)  
🦪 Oyster sauce as needed (once for sauce + once during braising)  
🍬 White sugar a pinch (add to sauce and during braising)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (add during braising)  
🍺 Beer 1 can (about 330ml, used as braising liquid)  
🥄 Cooking oil as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔥 Wok  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🧺 Colander  
🥣 Mixing bowl/measuring spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks/tongs  
🥄 Spatula  

## Steps  
1. Rinse the fish pieces under running water to remove blood, drain well; cut tomatoes into pieces; cut garlic sprout leaves into sections; slice ginger and garlic.  
2. In a bowl, mix 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, a pinch of sugar, a pinch of salt, and add some water. Stir to combine.  
3. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Place fish pieces skin-side down, cook over medium-low heat for about 2-3 minutes until golden. Flip and cook the other side until golden. Remove and drain excess oil.  
4. Leave a little oil in the wok. Add ginger slices and garlic slices, stir-fry until fragrant. Add tomato pieces, stir-fry over medium heat until soft and mushy.  
5. Layer the fried fish pieces over the tomatoes. Pour in the prepared sauce, then add the whole can of beer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cover and braise for about 8-10 minutes.  
6. Uncover and increase to high heat to reduce the sauce. Gently push the fish pieces, minimizing flipping. When the sauce thickens, sprinkle in garlic sprout leaves, quickly mix evenly, then remove from wok.  
   **Key points**: The beer should roughly cover the fish pieces during braising; prevent sticking when reducing the sauce, gently push only.  

## Health Advice  
This beer braised fish is cooked with tomatoes and beer. The fish is tender and rich in high-quality protein, suitable for those needing nutrition or who prefer rich flavors without excessive grease. The alcohol in the beer evaporates during cooking, but those with sensitive stomachs or gout should eat in moderation.
