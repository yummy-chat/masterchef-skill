# Crispy Bottom Pan-Fried Dumplings · Wontons · Beef Balls
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Frozen dumplings, as needed  
🥟 Frozen wontons, as needed  
🍢 Frozen beef balls, as needed  
🌾 Cornstarch or flour, about two tablespoons  
💧 Water, about one bowl (for making the starch water)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (drizzled along the edge of the pan at the end)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Large non-stick frying pan  
🫙 Transparent lid  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring the starch water)  
🔗 Food tongs (for checking and flipping)  
🔪 Wooden spatula (for serving)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Prepare the starch water: Take one bowl of water, add two tablespoons of cornstarch or flour, and stir until fully dissolved. Set aside.  
   **Key point**: The starch water is essential for forming a crispy bottom.  
2. Arrange the frozen dumplings, wontons, and beef balls directly in the frying pan in a single layer. There is no need to add oil.  
3. Pour the prepared starch water into the pan, with the liquid level reaching about halfway up the ingredients.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of water based on the heat, using the "halfway" point as a reference.  
4. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to low heat and cover with the lid to simmer.  
   **Key point**: Simmer until the water in the pan is mostly evaporated and the ingredients are fully cooked. Be careful not to let the heat get too high, which could cause the water to dry out before the ingredients are cooked.  
5. When the water is almost evaporated, drizzle a circle of cooking oil along the edge of the pan to make the bottom extra crispy.  
6. Continue frying until the water is completely gone and a golden, crispy crust forms on the bottom. Slide the entire batch onto a plate to serve.  
   **Doneness check**: The bottom should be golden, crispy, and intact.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses a starch-water base to enhance the texture of frozen ingredients. It is recommended to pair with vinegar or chili sauce to cut the richness, making it a quick energy booster for busy office workers. Be mindful of the amount of cooking oil used to avoid excess calories.
