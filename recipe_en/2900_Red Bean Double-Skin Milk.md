# Red Bean Double-Skin Milk
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Fresh milk 1 liter (1000g)
🥚 Egg whites 6 (about 120-150g)
🫘 Red beans 100g
🍬 White sugar 90g (used in two portions: 40g for egg whites, 50g for red bean filling)
🍋 Lemon juice a few drops

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Milk pot
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Soup spoon
♨️ Steamer
⚡ Electric pressure cooker
🥣 Bowls (for cooling and skin formation, serving, and steaming)
🫗 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. Pour fresh milk into the milk pot and bring to a boil.
**Key point**: The authentic recipe uses water buffalo milk; here, ordinary fresh milk is used as a substitute.

2. Pour the boiled milk into small bowls and let it cool naturally for 30 minutes until a complete layer of milk skin forms on the surface.
**Key point**: Thorough cooling is crucial for forming the first layer of milk skin.

3. Separate the egg whites and prepare them according to the ratio of 6 egg whites per 1 liter of milk.
4. Add a few drops of lemon juice and 40g of white sugar to the egg whites, and whisk until well combined.
**Key point**: Lemon juice helps remove the eggy smell.

5. Use a toothpick to gently lift a small opening along the edge of the bowl where the milk skin meets the bowl, then slowly pour the milk from the bowl into the egg white mixture. Whisk thoroughly, ensuring the first layer of milk skin remains at the bottom of the bowl.
**Key point**: Be sure to keep the first layer of milk skin at the bottom of the bowl.

6. Strain the combined egg and milk mixture through a strainer 1-2 times to remove bubbles and any unwhisked egg whites.
**Key point**: Straining results in a smoother and more delicate texture.

7. Slowly pour the strained egg-milk mixture back into the bowl containing the reserved milk skin, pouring along the edge of the bowl so that the milk skin naturally floats to the top.
**Key point**: Pour slowly to avoid breaking the milk skin at the bottom.

8. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap. No need to poke holes.
9. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the milk bowls inside, reduce to medium heat, and steam for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest in the residual steam for 5 minutes.
**Key point**: The water must be boiling before adding the bowls; the residual heat allows the double-skin milk to fully set.

10. Prepare the red bean filling: Place 100g of red beans into the electric pressure cooker, add enough water, and cook on the bean setting for 30 minutes until soft and tender.
11. Transfer the cooked red beans to a milk pot, add 50g of white sugar, and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed and the mixture becomes thick and sticky.
**Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent sticking to the bottom.

12. Place a spoonful of red bean filling on top of the set double-skin milk. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Red bean double-skin milk is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for those in need of nutritional supplementation. However, it has a high sugar content, so it should be consumed with caution by people with diabetes.
