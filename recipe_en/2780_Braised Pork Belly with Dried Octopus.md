# Braised Pork Belly with Dried Octopus
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, about 500g (cut into 2.5cm cubes)
🦑 Dried octopus (dried cuttlefish) 1 piece (palm-sized)
🧄 Garlic cloves 10–15 pieces (with skin)
🧅 Onion half (purple-skinned)
🍊 Orange 1 (use the peel)
🧂 Salt to taste
🍶 Rice wine to taste
🍬 Rock sugar a small handful
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 2–3 pieces
⭐ Star anise 1 piece
🍂 Bay leaves 1–2 pieces
🌿 Oregano (or cinnamon) a pinch
🍾 Light soy sauce half a bowl
🍾 Dark soy sauce about half the amount of light soy sauce
🦪 Oyster sauce one tablespoon
🌫️ White pepper powder one tablespoon

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch
🍳 Wok
🕯️ Gas stove
🏺 Clay pot
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Preparation Steps
1. Treat the pork skin: Use a blowtorch to burn the skin of the pork belly until charred yellow, removing any residual hairs and gamey odor. Wipe clean with kitchen paper towels.
   **Note**: This step is especially crucial for removing the gamey smell from pork outside of China.
2. Cut the meat: Slice the pork belly into strips about 2.5cm wide, then cut into uniform cubes.
   **Note**: Do not cut the pieces too small, as they will shrink during braising.
3. Marinate: Add an appropriate amount of salt and rice wine to the meat cubes. Mix well and marinate for 5 minutes. Do not rinse.
   **Key point**: Salt and rice wine effectively remove any gamey smell and enhance aroma.
4. Prepare dried octopus: Use chopsticks to hold the dried octopus and toast it over an open flame until the surface is slightly charred. This releases its savory flavor and removes any fishy smell.
   **Alternative**: If dried octopus is unavailable, dried shrimp can be used and treated in the same way.
5. Sauté garlic: Heat oil in a wok. Add the garlic cloves with their skins on. Sauté over medium-low heat until the skins turn golden and the aroma is released.
   **Note**: The garlic must be kept in its skin for a richer aroma.
6. Brown the meat: Add the marinated meat cubes and rock sugar to the wok. Stir-fry until the meat is coated in caramelized sugar. Splash rice wine along the edge of the wok to give the meat a charred, reddish color and enhance the fragrance.
7. Add aromatics: Add the dried chili peppers, onion chunks, star anise, bay leaves, and oregano. Stir-fry until fragrant.
8. Add water and simmer: Pour in enough hot water to just cover the meat. Add the toasted dried octopus and white pepper powder. Peel strips of orange zest and add them to the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat.
9. Transfer to a clay pot for slow braising: Transfer all the ingredients and liquid from the wok to a clay pot. Add the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Stir to combine.
   **Note**: Only cover the clay pot halfway, leaving a gap while braising. This prevents the pork from becoming too soft and helps maintain a pleasant, tender texture. Braise over medium heat for 30 minutes until the sauce is thickened, the meat is glossy, and the gelatin has been released.

## Health Advice
This braised pork dish is enhanced with dried octopus and orange peel, doubling its umami and refreshing qualities, making it especially suitable for autumn and winter nourishment. Pork belly is high in fat, so it is recommended to serve alongside light vegetables and control the portion size per serving.
