# Bitter Gourd and Sliced Pork Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 1 bitter gourd (seeded and cut into thick slices)  
🥩 Appropriate amount of lean pork (thinly sliced)  
🥔 Large amount of sweet potato starch (for marinating)  
🫚 Several slices of ginger  
🥕 About 100g carrot (cut into flower-shaped slices)  
🫒 A little cooking oil (for marinating)  
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt (used in batches)  
🧂 A little MSG (for final seasoning)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash the bitter gourd, split it lengthwise, scoop out the pith and seeds, and cut into thick slices; cut the carrot into flower-shaped slices, slice the ginger; thinly slice the lean pork.  
   **Note**: The thickness of the bitter gourd slices should be moderate, not too thin, to maintain the texture.  
2. Place the pork slices in a bowl, add a little salt, a generous amount of sweet potato starch, and a small amount of water. Mix well with your hands until each slice is evenly coated with the slurry, then drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix again.  
   **Note**: A sufficient amount of sweet potato starch is key to the tenderness of the meat slices; adding water helps the starch adhere evenly.  
3. Pour enough water into the pot, add the ginger slices, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
4. Once the water is boiling, use chopsticks to add the marinated pork slices one by one into the pot, and cook until the slices change color and float.  
   **Note**: Add the slices one at a time to prevent them from sticking together.  
5. Add the bitter gourd slices and carrot slices, turn the heat to high, and cook for about 1 minute.  
   **Note**: Cook quickly over high heat to keep the bitter gourd crisp.  
6. Add an appropriate amount of salt and a little MSG according to your taste, stir well, turn off the heat, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Bitter gourd can clear heat and relieve summer heat, while the tender pork slices are easy to digest, making this soup suitable for summer consumption. It is recommended to blanch the bitter gourd before cooking to reduce its bitterness; those with high blood lipids should reduce the amount of salt used.
