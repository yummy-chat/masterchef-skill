# Salted Fish and Chicken Fried Rice  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟  Ma You salted fish, about 1 tael (50g)  
🍗  Chicken thigh, 1 piece (skin on, cut into dice)  
🫚  Ginger, about 3 qian (slice some and mince the rest)  
🥬  Lettuce, about 2 taels (only stems, cut into dice)  
🍚  White rice, 1 catty (500g, preferably freshly cooked hot rice)  
🥚  Egg, 1 (beaten into egg wash)  
🦐  Shrimp paste, to taste  
🥜  Peanut oil, a little  
🐖  Lard, a little  
🍬  White sugar, a little  
🧂  Light soy sauce, a few drops  
💧  Steamed salted fish original juice, about 1 spoonful  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳  Wok  
🔥  Stove  
♨️  Steamer (with lid)  
🥣  Bowls and plates  
🥄  Spoons  
🥢  Chopsticks  

## Preparations  
1. **Steam the salted fish**: Place the salted fish pieces on a plate, top with ginger slices, drizzle with a little peanut oil, and steam until tender and the original juice is released.  
**Key**: Ginger slices and oil moisten the dry salted fish, softening it and releasing its essence juice.  
2. **Prepare the sauce**: Pour the steamed fish juice into a small bowl, add an appropriate amount of shrimp paste, and stir well with chopsticks to form a flavorful sauce.  
3. **Shred the salted fish**: Use chopsticks to break the steamed fish into fine shreds, making it easier to coat the rice evenly.  
4. **Prepare the other ingredients**: Dice the chicken thigh into small pieces, mince the ginger into ginger rice, and dice the lettuce stems for later use.  
5. **Stir-fry the chicken**: Heat the wok, add a little lard, then the chicken dice, white sugar, and a few drops of light soy sauce. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 1 minute until the surface is fragrant.  
6. **Pound the salted fish**: Add the minced ginger and shredded salted fish to the chicken, continue stir-frying for about 1 minute until the flavors of salted fish, meat, and ginger are well combined.  
7. **Gold-wrapped-silver fried rice**: Pour in the hot white rice, roughly break it up with a spoon, then evenly drizzle the beaten egg while stirring quickly with chopsticks to coat each grain of rice. Fry until loose and dry.  
8. **Add the sauce**: Pour in the prepared shrimp paste and salted fish juice, stir-fry quickly and evenly. The fried rice will become looser and oilier.  
9. **Add the lettuce dice**: Sprinkle in the lettuce dice, stir-fry a few times over high heat, then immediately turn off the heat. Use residual heat to cook the lettuce dice, keeping them crisp.  
**Key**: Do not stir-fry the lettuce for too long, as it will release water and become soft.  
10. **Brighten and enhance aroma**: Just before serving, add a few more drops of lard and stir-fry evenly.  
11. **Plate**: Transfer to a plate and enjoy while hot.  

## Health Advice  
Salted fish and chicken provide high-quality protein, but the shrimp paste and lard make this fried rice high in sodium and calories. It is recommended to pair it with a light stir-fried vegetable dish to balance nutrition. Suitable for those with high physical exertion. People with hypertension should reduce the amount of salted fish and shrimp paste used.
