# Stir-Fried Pork Intestines with Pickled Cabbage
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥓 Pork intestines 250g  
🥬 Pickled cabbage about 1 head  
🧄 Garlic sprouts 2 stalks  
🫚 Ginger to taste  
🧄 Garlic cloves a few  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers a few  
🍶 Cooking wine to taste (for blanching)  
🥄 Light soy sauce to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok (flat-bottomed)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🧺 Colander  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rub raw pork intestines repeatedly with salt or flour, then rinse clean with water; place in cold water, add ginger slices and cooking wine to blanch. After boiling, skim off the foam, drain, and cut into small sections.  
2. Wash and finely chop the pickled cabbage; wash and cut the garlic sprouts into sections, separating the white parts from the leaves; slice the ginger, crack the garlic cloves, and prepare the dried chili peppers.  
3. Heat the wok without oil or with just a little oil, add the chopped pickled cabbage, stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture evaporates, then set aside.  
4. Heat oil in a clean wok, add ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Add the pork intestine pieces, stir-fry quickly over high heat until the surface turns slightly crispy and golden.  
6. Add the dried pickled cabbage, stir-fry together with the intestines evenly.  
7. Add the garlic sprout leaves, drizzle in light soy sauce, sprinkle with a suitable amount of salt, and quickly stir-fry until the leaves are just cooked.  
8. Turn off the heat, plate, and serve hot.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and fat, suitable for occasional indulgence. Those with weak digestive function should consume it in moderation. Pickled cabbage helps stimulate the appetite; it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition and control intake.
