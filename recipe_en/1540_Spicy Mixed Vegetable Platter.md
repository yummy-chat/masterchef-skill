# Spicy Mixed Vegetable Platter
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Potato, about 250g (shred finely, rinse repeatedly to remove starch)  
🥬 Celtuce, about 250g (shred finely)  
🌿 Asparagus, to taste (cut into sections)  
🥕 Small Radish, to taste (sliced)  
🫑 Red and Yellow Bell Peppers, to taste (cut into chunks)  
🥬 Leafy Greens (e.g., spinach), to taste  
🌽 Baby Corn, to taste (cut into sections)  
🍤 Shrimp, to taste (heads and veins removed)  
🥚 Egg, 1 (hard-boiled and cut into chunks)  

## Seasonings  
🧄 Garlic, 5–8 cloves (minced; half used raw, half for oil splashing)  
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers, to taste  
🧂 Five-Spice Powder, 1 tsp (about 2g)  
🧂 Sichuan Pepper Powder, 1 tsp (about 2g)  
🌶️ Chili Flakes, to taste  
🫒 Cooking Oil: For regular version - twice the amount used for stir-frying; For low-oil version - normal stir-fry amount  
🌿 Sichuan Peppercorns or Numbing Peppercorns, two pinches  
🥄 Light Soy Sauce, 2 tbsp (20–25g)  
🥄 Oyster Sauce, 1 tbsp (10–15g)  
🥄 Vinegar, 1 tbsp (10–15g)  
🍬 White Sugar, 1 tsp (about 3g)  
🧂 MSG, 1 tsp (about 2g, optional)  
🧂 Salt, half tsp (about 2g, adjustable later)  
🥄 Beef Bouillon Powder, 1 tsp (about 3g, not essential)  
🌶️ Chili Oil, Sesame Oil, Garlic Oil, to taste (optional)  
💧 Water, 1 tbsp (10–15g, used in low-oil version)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s Knife, Cutting Board  
🧇 Shredder/Grater  
🥣 Strainer, Colander, Basin  
🍲 Stockpot  
🔥 Stove  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Large Mixing Bowl  
🥄 Bowl, Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prepare Ingredients  
1. Wash and drain all vegetables. Peel the potato and celtuce, then shred them finely using a shredder. Rinse the potato shreds repeatedly under cold water to remove surface starch until the water runs clear, ensuring a crisp texture.  
2. Cut asparagus into sections, slice small radish, cut bell peppers into chunks, section baby corn, mince garlic. Add cilantro and bird’s eye chili according to personal preference.  
3. Remove shrimp heads and veins; the heads can be twisted off in one motion. Hard-boil the egg and cut into chunks.  

### Blanch Ingredients  
1. Fill a stockpot with water and bring to a rolling boil over medium heat.  
2. Blanch in order: First blanch the potato shreds for about 1 minute, then transfer to cold water. Next, blanch the celtuce shreds for 15–20 seconds. Then blanch harder vegetables like asparagus and baby corn. Follow with leafy greens, blanching for about 1 minute. Finally, blanch the shrimp for about 1 minute until pink and curled.  
   **Note**: Blanching water can be reused, but replace it if high-oxalate vegetables have been blanched.  
3. Squeeze excess water from all blanched ingredients by hand to prevent dilution of the dressing later.  

### Prepare the Sauce  
In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, oyster sauce, vinegar, white sugar, MSG, salt, beef bouillon powder, and water. Mix well and set aside.  

### Make the Splashing Oil & Combine  
#### Regular Rich Version  
1. Place the squeezed-dry blanched ingredients, minced garlic, dried chili peppers, five-spice powder, and Sichuan pepper powder into a large mixing bowl.  
2. Pour twice the usual stir-fry amount of oil into a wok and heat over medium heat. Add Sichuan peppercorns or numbing peppercorns, fry until aromatic and darkened, then quickly remove.  
3. Continue heating the oil until it smokes, then swiftly pour it over the seasonings in the bowl. Immediately cover and let sit for 1–2 minutes to fully release the aromas.  
4. Remove the lid, add the prepared sauce, cilantro, and bird’s eye chili. Mix well.  

#### Low-Oil Version  
1. Pour the normal stir-fry amount of oil into a wok and heat over medium heat. Add Sichuan peppercorns or numbing peppercorns, fry until aromatic, then remove.  
2. Turn off the heat. Using residual warmth, stir-fry half the minced garlic and dried chili peppers until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.  
3. Turn the heat back to medium, add five-spice powder and Sichuan pepper powder, stir-fry for about 15–20 seconds.  
4. Pour in the prepared sauce, keep on medium heat and stir until the sauce bubbles and boils, then turn off the heat.  
5. Pour the hot sauce over all the prepared ingredients.  
6. Optionally, add extra chili oil, sesame oil, or garlic oil to taste. Mix well.  

### Plating  
Ensure all ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce. Plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in vitamins and protein. Blanching and squeezing the vegetables dry before mixing reduces the loss of water-soluble vitamins and prevents the dressing from being diluted by excess liquid. The low-oil version replaces a large amount of hot oil with a flavorful sauce, making it healthier and suitable for weight loss.
