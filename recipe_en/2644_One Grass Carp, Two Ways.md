# One Grass Carp, Two Ways
Cuisine Type: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 Grass Carp
🧈 Tofu, as needed
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (some sliced, some minced)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (divided into chopped scallions and scallion segments)
🌶️ Red Bell Pepper, as needed (minced)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying fish, sautéing, and final oil drizzle)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating fish, seasoning sauce, and soup)
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed (for garlic sauce)
🌿 Ground white pepper, as needed (for sauce and soup, to remove fishiness)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s Knife
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🍳 Wok
♨️ Steamer
🍽️ Fish Serving Plate
🥄 Soup Ladle

## Steps
1.  **Prepare the Grass Carp**
    - Remove the two whole fillets from each side along the backbone, and chop the bones into pieces.
    - Cut the fillets into "double-cut slices" (butterfly cuts), place in a bowl, and marinate with a pinch of salt, mixing well.
    **Note**: Wear non-slip gloves while preparing the fish to prevent it from sliding.

2.  **Prepare Ingredients**
    - Mince the scallions, ginger, and garlic; mince the red bell pepper. Cut the tofu into pieces, and set aside the remaining scallions cut into segments.

3.  **Make the Garlic Sauce**
    - Place the minced scallions, ginger, garlic, and red bell pepper in a bowl. Pour in oil heated to 70% hot (about 180°C/350°F) to release the aroma.
    - Add light soy sauce, a pinch of salt, and ground white pepper. Mix well.

4.  **Arrange and Steam the Fish Fillets**
    - Arrange the marinated fish fillets neatly on a plate, following the direction of the fish bone.
    - Spread the prepared garlic sauce evenly over the fish.
    - Once the steamer water is boiling, place the plate in the steamer and steam over high heat for 5 minutes.

5.  **Fry the Fish Bones**
    - Heat oil in a wok over high heat. First, fry the ginger slices until fragrant, then add the fish bone pieces.
    **Key Point**: Do not turn the fish bones immediately after placing them in the wok. Wait until the bottom is set and hardened, then flip to prevent sticking.
    - Fry the fish bones over medium-high heat until both sides are golden brown.

6.  **Make the Fish Soup**
    - Pour enough hot water into the wok.
    **Key Point**: Do not skim off the oil floating on the soup surface during cooking, as this is crucial for achieving a milky white broth. Do not add salt prematurely at any stage.
    - Continue cooking until the broth turns a rich, milky white.

7.  **Add Accompaniments and Season**
    - Once the broth is milky white, add the tofu pieces and scallion segments. Add a suitable amount of salt and ground white pepper to remove fishiness and enhance flavor. Cook for another 2 minutes, then turn off the heat.

8.  **Final Oil Drizzle**
    - Remove the steamed fish fillets and sprinkle fresh chopped scallions on top.
    - Pour a spoonful of oil heated to 70% hot over the scallions to release their fragrance, then serve immediately.

## Health Suggestion
One grass carp, two ways. The steamed fillets are low in fat and high in protein, and the fish bone and tofu soup is calcium-rich and warms the stomach. This dish is suitable for those on a weight loss diet and for the whole family. Note that the garlic sauce should be low in oil and salt, and the steaming time should be controlled within 5 minutes to prevent the fish from becoming tough.
