# Cucumber and Egg Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Cucumber: About 3–4 pieces (shredded and drained of water)  
🥚 Eggs: About 4 pieces (scrambled into fine pieces)  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms: To taste (pre-soaked and finely chopped)  
🥕 Carrot: About one small bowl (shredded, blanched, and finely chopped)  
🧅 Scallions: About 2–3 stalks (finely chopped, divided into two portions)  
🫚 Ginger: About 3 grams (finely chopped)  
🌾 Flour: 500 grams (for making wrappers)  
💧 Water: About 280 grams (mixed with salt for dough)  
🌾 Dry flour: As needed (for dusting while kneading and rolling)  
🧂 Salt: About 1 gram (dissolved in water for dough to strengthen gluten)  
🧂 Salt: About 3 grams (for salting cucumber shreds to extract water)  
🧂 Salt: To taste (for seasoning the filling)  
🌶️ White pepper powder: A pinch (to remove odors)  
🌶️ Sichuan pepper powder: About 1 gram  
🍶 Spiced oil: 1–2 tablespoons (e.g., Sichuan pepper oil)  
🍯 Oyster sauce: About 25 grams (to enhance freshness, not too much)  
🫒 Cooking oil: As needed (for scrambling eggs)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Metal spoon  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥕 Shredder  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Dumpling tray or bamboo mat  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Place flour in a bowl. Dissolve 1 gram of salt in water. Gradually pour the salted water into the flour while stirring with chopsticks until it forms a flaky texture with no dry flour. Knead by hand into a smooth dough, continuing to knead until it reaches the "three glows" (smooth surface, clean bowl, clean hands).  
**Note**: Using salted water for the dough makes the wrappers more chewy.

2.  Cover the kneaded dough and let it rest. After 15 minutes, knead it again for a smoother texture.
3.  Finely chop scallions; finely chop ginger; finely chop rehydrated wood ear mushrooms; shred carrot, blanch in boiling water for 1.5 minutes, then rinse with cold water and finely chop; shred cucumber, briefly chop into lengths of about 2–2.5 cm, not too fine.
4.  Add about 3 grams of salt to the shredded cucumber, mix well, and let sit briefly. Squeeze firmly with your hands to remove excess water.  
**Key**: Thoroughly squeezing out water prevents the filling from leaking and wrappers from breaking during cooking.

5.  Beat the eggs. Heat a wok or pan with oil, pour in the egg mixture, and quickly stir with chopsticks to break into fine pieces. Continue frying until one side of the egg pieces turns slightly golden to remove the eggy smell and enhance aroma. Let the scrambled eggs cool completely before use.  
**Key**: Adding completely cooled eggs to the filling prevents the heat from causing the filling to release water.

6.  Place the squeezed cucumber shreds in a large bowl. Add salt, white pepper powder, Sichuan pepper powder, and spiced oil. Mix lightly. Then add the cooled scrambled eggs, chopped carrot, chopped wood ear mushrooms, ginger, and **most** of the scallions. Finally, add oyster sauce and mix thoroughly.  
**Note**: Add oyster sauce last and do not use too much; reserve a small portion of scallions to be added raw when wrapping dumplings for extra fragrance.

7.  Wrapping dumplings:  
   - Roll the dough into a long log and cut into about 70 evenly sized dough pieces.  
   - Flatten each piece and roll out into a wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.  
   - Place a suitable amount of filling on the wrapper, then add a small spoonful of raw scallions on top.  
   - Fold the wrapper in half and use the "tiger mouth" of both hands to squeeze the edges together, forming a yuanbao (ingot) shape.  
**Key**: The thicker center and thinner edges prevent the dumplings from breaking during cooking; adding raw scallions at the end is the secret to extra fragrance.

8.  Cooking dumplings:  
   - Bring water to a boil. Add the dumplings and gently stir with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking.  
   - Cook over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Once the dumplings float, cook for 1 minute, then add a ladle of cold water.  
   - Bring to a boil again. When all the dumplings float, puff up, and the wrappers become slightly translucent, remove with a slotted spoon.  
**Key**: For vegetable filling dumplings, add cold water once; they are done once they boil again.  

## Health Advice  
These cucumber and egg dumplings are light and low in fat, making them suitable for summer weight loss or light diets. Ensure the cucumber is thoroughly squeezed to prevent the wrappers from breaking. It is recommended to serve with a vinegar and garlic dipping sauce to enhance flavor and aid digestion.
