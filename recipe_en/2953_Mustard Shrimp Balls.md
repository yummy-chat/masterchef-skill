# Mustard Shrimp Balls
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Large fresh shrimp, appropriate amount (needs to be shelled, deveined, and butterflied)
🥚 Egg, half (for marinating the shrimp)
🥔 Original-flavor potato chips, one bag (for plating, crushed to form a base)
🍋 Lemon, about 1/3 (zest for garnish, juice for the sauce)
🫘 Black and white sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for garnish and added aroma)
🌿 Mint leaves, appropriate amount (for final garnish)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder, about 1 teaspoon
🍬 White sugar, about 1 teaspoon
🍶 Cooking wine, about 1 bottle cap
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for marinating, sealing, and as a base oil for the sauce)
🌾 Cornstarch, about 3 tablespoons (amount should be adjusted based on the shrimp's condition; a thin batter coating is ideal)
🥛 Mayonnaise, about 50 grams
🟢 Wasabi, about 5 grams
🟡 Yellow mustard, about 25 grams
🍋 Lemon juice, squeezed from about 1/3 lemon

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Mixing bowls
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🧴 Citrus juicer

## Preparation Steps
1. Butterfly the large shrimp, cutting to about 80% of their depth. Rinse them thoroughly under clean water and remove the vein.
**Note**: The cut must be deep so the shrimp curl naturally into balls during frying.
2. Use kitchen paper towels to thoroughly pat the shrimp dry.
**Note**: Drying the surface is crucial to prevent oil splatter during frying and to allow the shrimp to absorb the marinade better.
3. Add salt, white pepper powder, chicken powder, white sugar, cooking wine, and half of the egg liquid to the shrimp. Mix well with your hands. Add ginger slices and scallion segments, mix well, and marinate for a short while.
**Note**: All seasonings must be thoroughly mixed into the shrimp for flavor.
4. Add cornstarch to the marinated shrimp and mix until each shrimp is coated with a thin batter. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix well to prevent sticking when adding to the pan.
**Note**: Do not use too much cornstarch; the shrimp should only have a light coating. A coating that is too thick will affect how well the sauce adheres later.
5. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine mayonnaise, wasabi, and yellow mustard. Squeeze in the lemon juice and stir until well combined. Set aside.
**Note**: You can adjust the amount of wasabi and lemon juice to your personal taste.
6. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat to 5-6 doneness (approx. 160-180°C). Carefully add the shrimp one by one. Fry over medium heat for about 1 minute to set the shape, then increase to high heat and fry for 10 seconds until the surface is golden and crispy. Immediately remove and drain on paper towels.
**Note**: Control the oil temperature and frying time well. The shrimp should be crispy on the outside and tender on the inside; do not overcook. The shrimp are ready when they curl quickly and turn a golden color.
7. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the prepared mustard sauce and turn the heat to the lowest setting. Once the sauce is warm, add the fried shrimp and stir-fry quickly, ensuring each shrimp is evenly coated with the sauce.
**Note**: The heat must be very low to prevent the sauce from separating.
8. Sprinkle an appropriate amount of black and white sesame seeds over the shrimp and mix well.
9. Crush the potato chips and spread them on a plate as a base. Pile the sauce- and sesame-coated shrimp on top of the crushed chips. Finally, garnish with lemon zest and mint leaves before serving.
**Note**: The crushed potato chips add a crispy texture to the base, creating a contrast with the shrimp balls.

## Health Advice
This dish features shrimp, which are high in protein and low in fat. The combination of mustard and lemon helps stimulate the appetite and aid digestion, making it suitable for those who want to indulge while controlling calorie intake. Pay attention to the oil temperature during frying to avoid excessive oil absorption.
