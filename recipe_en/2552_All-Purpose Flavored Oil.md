# All-Purpose Flavored Oil
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧅 Small scallions 150g (with roots, lightly crushed)
🌿 Small celery 50g (lightly crushed)
🧅 Onion 50g (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 30g (whole cloves or flattened)
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced)
🥕 Carrot 20g (sliced into large pieces)
🌱 Cumin seeds 15g
🫚 Cassia cinnamon 8g
⭐ Star anise 6g
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 5g
🌿 Coriander seeds 6g
🍃 Bay leaves 3g
🫘 Black cardamom 2 pieces (cracked open)
🫒 Soybean oil 1000g (2 pounds)
🧂 Seasonings None (pure spice oil, no salt or soy sauce added)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥅 Strainer (optional)
🥣 Heat-resistant large bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the black cardamom open and soak all dry spices in clean water until moistened.
   **Key Point**: Soaking prevents the spices from burning and helps release their aroma.
2. Lightly crush the small scallions and small celery with the flat side of a knife blade; slice the onion, slice the ginger, and slice the carrot into large pieces.
   **Key Point**: Crushing helps release the internal aroma of the vegetables.
3. Pour the soybean oil into a cold wok. Heat over high heat until 80–90% hot to remove the raw oil smell, then turn off the heat.
   **Note**: Raw soybean oil must be fully heated; otherwise, it will have a raw, unpleasant smell.
4. Allow the oil temperature to naturally drop to about 20% of its original heat.
   **Key Point**: It is essential to wait for the oil to cool; otherwise, adding ingredients immediately will cause them to burn.
5. First, add all the vegetable ingredients, then add the soaked spices. Turn the heat to low and maintain a low flame while frying slowly.
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat low throughout to allow the moisture in the ingredients to evaporate slowly and the aroma to gradually infuse into the oil.
6. Fry until the vegetable ingredients become dry and shriveled, and their color turns golden brown. When stirring with a ladle, you should hear a "rustling" sound. Then turn off the heat.
   **Note**: Do not fry until black, or the oil will become bitter.
7. Pour the oil and fried ingredients together into a heat-resistant large bowl. Gently press down on the solids with a ladle, cover with a lid or a plate, and let it steep for about 10–15 minutes.
   **Purpose**: Use residual heat to further extract aroma. Do not steep for too long to avoid burning.
8. Remove the lid and strain out all the solids using a strainer. The flavored oil is now ready to use.
   **Storage**: Can be stored sealed in the refrigerator.

## Health Advice
This flavored oil is aromatic without being greasy. It is excellent for enhancing flavor in cold dishes or fried rice. However, it is relatively high in calories; use one or two tablespoons per serving. Maintaining low heat throughout the process is crucial.
