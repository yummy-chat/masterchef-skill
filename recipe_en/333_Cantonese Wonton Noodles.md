# Cantonese Wonton Noodles  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Fresh meat wontons, as needed  
🍜 Bamboo pole noodles, as needed  
🥬 Lettuce, as needed  
🐟 Ground dried flounder powder, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
⚫️ White pepper, a pinch  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🍲 Soup pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
✂️ Scissors  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the dried flounder broth**: Add water to the frying pan and bring to a boil. Add the dried flounder powder and an appropriate amount of salt, stirring to combine and form the broth.  
   **Key point**: Dried flounder powder only enhances umami but has very little saltiness on its own, so salt must be added for seasoning, otherwise the broth will be bland.  
2. **Cook the wontons and greens**: Bring water to a boil in the soup pot. Add the wontons and cook until they all float to the surface and the wrappers are fully cooked and change color. When the wontons are almost done, add the lettuce and blanch until soft, then immediately remove and place at the bottom of a bowl.  
3. **Cook the noodles and "pass through cold water"**: In the same pot of boiling water, add the bamboo pole noodles. Cook until the noodles separate, then immediately remove and plunge them into cold water, allowing the noodles to rapidly contract. Remove from the cold water and return to the boiling water for just a few seconds to reheat.  
   **Key point**: Passing through cold water is essential for achieving a springy and chewy texture in the noodles and must not be skipped. The reheating time should be short to avoid overcooking.  
4. **Assemble the bowl**: Lay the blanched lettuce at the bottom of the bowl, place the cold-water-treated bamboo pole noodles on top, scoop in the cooked wontons, pour in the piping hot dried flounder broth, and finally sprinkle with a pinch of white pepper.  

## Health Advice  
In this wonton noodle dish, the bamboo pole noodles become wonderfully chewy after passing through cold water, and the dried flounder broth is savory and delicious, making it suitable for those needing a warming yet not overly greasy meal. Note that wonton wrappers contain alkali, so those with sensitive stomachs should eat in moderation.
