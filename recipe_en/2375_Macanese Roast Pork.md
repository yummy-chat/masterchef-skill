# Macanese Roast Pork

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonality: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 One piece of pork belly with skin (choose evenly fatty and lean portions)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for meat marinade)  
🧂 Chicken powder, to taste  
🧂 Sand ginger powder, to taste  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, a little (for meat marinade)  
🧂 Baking soda, a little (for skin rub)  
🍶 White vinegar, to taste (for brushing the pork skin)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for brushing the skin before the third roasting)  
🧂 Salt, 30 grams (for skin rub)  
🍬 White sugar, to taste (for dipping)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel basin/bowl  
📍 Pricking tool (or bamboo skewer)  
📌 Long metal skewer, hook  
🔥 Large roasting oven (with temperature control)  
🌀 Industrial fan (for aiding drying)  
🌡️ Thermometer (built-in oven)  
🔧 Scraper

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the pork belly: On the meat side, cut parallel slits about two finger-widths apart, cutting deep to the skin but not through it.
2. Prepare the marinade: Mix salt, chicken powder, sand ginger powder, thirteen-spice powder, and a little white sugar into a meat marinade. Evenly rub it onto the meat side and into the slits. Separately, mix 30 grams of salt with a little baking soda into a skin rub, and apply it to the pork skin surface.
**Note**: Do not apply the meat marinade too thickly, to avoid excessive saltiness.

3. Refrigerate and marinate: Place the meat in the refrigerator and marinate overnight to fully infuse the flavors.
4. Skewer, brush with vinegar, and dry: Insert a long metal skewer horizontally through the meat to secure it. Repeatedly brush the pork skin surface with white vinegar, then hang the meat by a hook in a ventilated, dry area. Use a fan to aid drying.
**Key**: The pork skin must be completely dry; it should not feel sticky when pressed with a paper towel. This step directly affects the crispiness.

5. Wrap with aluminum foil: Tightly wrap the bottom and sides of the meat with aluminum foil, leaving only the pork skin exposed.
6. First roasting: Place the meat into the roasting oven at about 180°C (356°F). Roast for 20 minutes until the meat is basically cooked through and the skin is initially set.
7. Prick and tighten the skin: Remove the meat and use a pricking tool to densely prick small holes all over the pork skin. This step helps set the skin and meat, preparing it for blistering.
8. Second roasting: Return the meat to the oven, raise the temperature to 300-400°C (572-752°F), and roast until the pork skin blisters, turns charred black, and makes crackling sounds.
9. Scrape and brush with oil: Remove the meat and use a scraper to clean off the charred black layer from the skin. Then brush a layer of cooking oil onto the pork skin surface. Remove all the aluminum foil.
10. Third roasting: Roast again in the oven, maintaining a temperature of about 200°C (392°F), for about 5 minutes until the pork skin is golden and crispy.
11. Cut into pieces: Let it cool slightly, then chop it into slices. A crisp crunching sound indicates success. It is recommended to dip in white sugar for a salty-sweet flavor combination.

## Health Advice
This Macanese roast pork is rich in protein and fat, suitable for moderate consumption by those with high physical energy expenditure. However, due to its high salt and fat content, individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should only have a small taste. Be mindful of the amount of sugar used for dipping to avoid doubling the calorie intake.
