# Basic Leavened Bread  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour, to taste  
🫙 Dry yeast/instant yeast, to taste (recommended to activate with warm water)  
💧 Water, to taste (equal amount to flour)  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (optional, helps fermentation)  
🧈 Oil/butter, to taste (optional, adds softness)  
🥚 Egg, to taste (optional, improves texture)  
🥛 Milk, to taste (optional, substitute for water)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for flavor, usually essential)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing and observing fermentation)  
🥄 Mixing spatula/spoon  
🫙 Plastic wrap (to cover and prevent drying)  
🪷 Rolling pin (for degassing and shaping)  
🍞 Bread loaf pan (for second proofing and baking)  
🔥 Oven (for baking)  
🧱 Cooling rack (for cooling)  

## Instructions  
1. Dissolve the yeast in warm water no hotter than 40°C, let it sit briefly to activate, then mix with flour and other ingredients to knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Note**: Pre-activating the yeast ensures more even fermentation than mixing dry yeast directly.  
2. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it undergo the first proofing at room temperature of 24–26°C.  
   **How to check**: The dough should double in volume. When you poke it with a floured finger, the indent should not spring back or collapse, indicating it is ready.  
   **Note**: The first proofing mainly affects the bread’s flavor; allow enough time for the yeast to multiply.  
3. Remove the dough, gently press to degas and release large air bubbles, then shape as desired.  
4. Perform the second proofing in a warm, humid place until the dough rises to about 80–90% full in the mold.  
   **Note**: The second proofing determines the bread’s shape and internal texture. Avoid over-proofing, which can weaken the gluten network and cause collapse during baking.  
5. Immediately transfer the proofed dough to a preheated oven for baking.  
   **Note**: Adjust baking temperature and time based on dough size and oven performance. Bake until the surface is golden brown and the interior is evenly soft.  
6. Remove from the oven and cool on a rack. Slice and serve once fully cooled.  

## Health Tips  
For leavened bread, consider incorporating whole wheat flour or grains to increase dietary fiber intake, suitable for those managing blood sugar and weight. Control the amounts of salt and sugar to avoid excessive refined carbohydrates.
