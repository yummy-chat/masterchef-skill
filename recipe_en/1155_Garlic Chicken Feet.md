# Garlic Chicken Feet  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken feet 600g (about 1.2 jin, frozen chicken feet, thawed, trimmed of nails, and cut diagonally)  
🧄 Garlic 1 head (minced, retaining some texture)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (cut into large pieces)  
🧅 Spring onions, as needed (cut into long sections)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed  
🌶️ Red chili peppers / bird’s eye chilies 1 (chopped)  
🥬 Green garlic leaves, as needed (chopped, for finishing)  
🍶 High-proof Chinese liquor, as needed (for blanching to remove odor)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, as needed  
🧈 Lard, as needed  
🧅 Star anise, a little  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, a little  
🍂 Bay leaves, a little  
🫘 Spicy sauce or doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 tbsp (for first stir-fry) + a small amount (for second stir-fry)  
🍶 Yellow wine (or Shaoxing wine), as needed  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (for coloring)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (use sparingly, as other seasonings contain salt)  
🍬 White sugar 1 tbsp  
🌫️ White pepper powder, as needed  
🍶 Aged vinegar (Chinkiang vinegar), as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed  
🧂 MSG, a pinch (optional, added before serving)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪚 Cutting board  
✂️ Scissors  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup spoon  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare chicken feet**: Trim the nails from the thawed chicken feet and cut them diagonally.  
   **Key point**: Cutting them open makes them easier to eat and helps absorb flavors.  
2. **Prepare aromatics**: Cut ginger into large pieces, spring onions into long sections, mince garlic, chop red chili peppers, and chop green garlic leaves.  
   **Key point**: Keep the minced garlic slightly coarse for better texture and flavor.  
3. **Blanch to remove odor**: Place the chicken feet in cold water in a pot. Add an appropriate amount of high-proof liquor, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then remove the chicken feet.  
   **Key point**: Start blanching in cold water; rinse the chicken feet with cold water after removing to prevent sticking.  
4. **First stir-fry**: Heat rapeseed oil in a wok, add ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the blanched chicken feet and stir-fry over medium heat until well combined. Add spring onion sections, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, one tablespoon of spicy sauce, yellow wine, and light soy sauce. Continue stir-frying until the chicken feet are evenly colored.  
5. **Braise for flavor**: Add water, enough to almost cover the chicken feet. Season with a pinch of salt, one tablespoon of white sugar, white pepper powder, dark soy sauce, aged vinegar, and oyster sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Use salt sparingly, as the spicy sauce, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce already contain salt.  
6. **Remove solids**: After braising, remove the lid and use chopsticks to pick out all the ginger, spring onions, and spices.  
7. **Reduce the sauce**: If there is excess liquid, increase heat to high and reduce until slightly thickened, so the sauce coats the back of a spoon.  
   **Key point**: Keep a moderate amount of sauce; do not reduce completely.  
8. **Fry garlic**: In a separate wok or pan, heat lard over low heat until warm. Add the minced garlic and chopped red chili peppers, and stir-fry slowly over low heat until the garlic is fragrant.  
   **Key point**: Fry slowly over low heat to prevent burning.  
9. **Combine and finish**: Transfer the chicken feet and their sauce into the pan with the garlic. Add a pinch of MSG (if using) and stir-fry over high heat, allowing the sauce to thicken further and coat the ingredients.  
10. **Serve and garnish**: Sprinkle in the chopped green garlic leaves, stir-fry quickly until fragrant. Once the sauce is thick and evenly coats the chicken feet, remove from heat and serve.  

## Health Suggestions  
Garlic chicken feet are rich in collagen, making them a good choice for those interested in skin health and joint care. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. After braising, the chicken feet become tender and flavorful, with a rich garlic aroma, making them an excellent side dish.
