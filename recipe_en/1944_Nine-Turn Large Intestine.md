# Nine-Turn Large Intestine

Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 4 pieces of pork large intestine head  
🧅 1 scallion (for threading and shaping) + 1 small section (finely chopped for aromatics) + appropriate amount (for braising)  
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (sliced for braising) + 1 small piece (minced for aromatics)  
🧄 1 small piece of garlic (minced for aromatics)  
🌿 Appropriate amount of cilantro (finely chopped for garnish)  
🍶 Appropriate amount of high-proof white liquor (for cleaning and blanching to remove odor)  
🧂 20 grams of edible salt (for cleaning) + 2 grams (for seasoning during cooking)  
🧪 5 grams of edible alkali (for cleaning) + 1 spoon (for braising)  
🍎 Approximately 50 grams of white vinegar (for cleaning) + 10 grams (for cooking) + a little (for simmering to prevent burning)  
🌾 Half a bowl of flour  
💧 Appropriate amount of water (for cleaning, braising, and cooking)  
🌰 1 nutmeg (cracked open)  
🌼 A little angelica dahurica  
🌱 2 cardamom pods (for braising) + a little (ground into powder for cooking, used in two portions)  
🌾 A little fennel seed  
🪵 2 grams of cinnamon stick (whole piece for braising) + 1 gram (ground into powder for adding flavor during cooking)  
⭐ 1 star anise  
🌸 A few Sichuan peppercorns  
🍬 30 grams of rock sugar  
🍾 Approximately 50 grams of cooking wine  
🍶 10 grams of rice vinegar (for cooking)  
🥢 5 grams of light soy sauce  
🥄 A few drops of dark soy sauce  
🍬 Approximately 10 grams of white sugar (about 3 tablespoons)  
🌶️ Approximately 2 grams of ground white pepper (1 spoon)  
🧂 Approximately 0.5 grams of ground cardamom (added in two portions)  
🧂 1 gram of ground cinnamon  
🧂 A little chicken bouillon powder  
🥜 Approximately 3 grams of peanut oil  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of Sichuan pepper oil  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍽️ Stainless steel tray  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
✂️ Scissors  
🍲 Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon / wok spatula  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  

## Cooking Steps  
1.  **Clean the large intestine:** Add high-proof white liquor to the pork large intestine heads and knead for 2 minutes. Place in a basin, add edible alkali, salt, and white vinegar, and rub for 3 minutes. Turn over during the process and carefully trim hidden lymph nodes. Add half a bowl of flour and an appropriate amount of white vinegar, rubbing both sides. Finally, rinse repeatedly with water until the water runs clear. Turn the smooth side of the intestine wall outward.  
2.  **Prepare the aromatics:** Slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, crack the nutmeg open. Set aside together with angelica dahurica, cardamom, fennel seed, cinnamon stick, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Separately, pound and mince the ginger and garlic, finely chop the scallion, and finely chop the cilantro. Place them separately. After the braised large intestine has cooled, cut it into 3 cm sections.  
3.  **Blanch and shape:** Fill a pot with water and heat to approximately 80°C. Add the large intestine pieces. Insert one scallion into the center of each piece to prevent curling. Add white liquor. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam. Blanch until the large intestine has noticeably shrunk. Remove and immediately plunge into ice water to cool thoroughly.  
4.  **Pressure braise:** Place the blanched large intestine into a pressure cooker. Add the braising aromatics, boiling water, and one spoon of edible alkali. Bring to pressure over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 12 minutes. Turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally. Open the lid, remove the large intestine, and cool it again in ice water.  
5.  **Parboil and stir-fry:** Bring water in a wok to approximately 60°C. Add the large intestine sections. Parboil until the water just begins to bubble, then immediately remove and drain. Heat the wok, add oil to coat, then pour out the excess oil. Add the large intestine sections and stir-fry over low heat until the surface is lightly golden and fat is rendered. Remove and set aside.  
6.  **Caramelize sugar and braise:** Leave a little base oil in the wok. Add rock sugar and a little water. Cook over low heat until a light caramel color forms. Remove the wok from the heat, add the large intestine, and stir-fry to coat evenly with the caramel. Add the minced ginger, garlic, and scallion, stir-frying until fragrant. Splash in cooking wine, rice vinegar, and light soy sauce. Add water. Add a few drops of dark soy sauce for color. Add three tablespoons of white sugar and white pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat.  
7.  **Simmer and reduce sauce:** Simmer over low heat for about 4 minutes. Add salt, a portion of the ground cardamom, and ground cinnamon. Continue to cook for 2 minutes. When the sauce thickens, reduce to the lowest heat and simmer for 1 minute. Add a little rice vinegar to prevent burning. Add chicken bouillon powder and peanut oil, stirring to combine. Ensure the thick sauce evenly coats the large intestine. Finally, sprinkle in the remaining ground cardamom and add Sichuan pepper oil. Gently mix well.  
8.  **Plating:** Arrange the large intestine sections neatly on a plate. Pour the remaining sauce from the wok over the top. Garnish with the finely chopped cilantro.  

## Health Advice  
Although this Nine-Turn Large Intestine dish involves complex procedures, it is high in fat and sodium. It is recommended that people with high blood pressure, high blood lipids, or high blood sugar consume it in very small amounts. Regular diners should pair it with high-fiber vegetables to balance the meal. Thoroughly cleaning the fat during preparation is crucial.
