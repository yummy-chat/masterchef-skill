# Ejiao Cake (Donkey-Hide Gelatin Cake)
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥮 Ejiao (Donkey-Hide Gelatin) 250g (needs to be crushed or ground into powder)
🫘 Dried Red Dates 250g (pitted and cut into small pieces)
🌑 Black Sesame Seeds 250g (pre-roasted until fragrant)
🥜 Walnuts 250g (pre-roasted)
🍒 Goji Berries 120g (washed and dried)
🍶 Yellow Wine 500g (for soaking the ejiao)
🍬 Rock Sugar (yellow) 120g (can substitute with regular rock sugar)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🧻 Cheesecloth (to wrap the ejiao block, preventing splatter when crushing)
🥖 Rolling pin or grinder (to crush or grind the ejiao)
🥣 Large bowl (for soaking the ejiao)
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for roasting black sesame seeds and walnuts)
🥘 Stainless steel deep pot (for boiling the ejiao liquid and mixing ingredients)
🪵 Wooden spatula (for stirring to prevent sticking)
📄 Mold or large bowl (for shaping)
📄 Parchment paper (to line the mold to prevent sticking)
🔪 Knife (for cutting into pieces)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process the Ejiao**: Wrap the ejiao block in cheesecloth and crush it with a rolling pin. The finer the pieces, the shorter the soaking time.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to wrap it in cheesecloth while crushing to prevent debris from splattering.
2. **Soak the Ejiao**: Pour the crushed ejiao into a large bowl, add 500g of yellow wine, stir to combine, cover with plastic wrap, and seal for soaking. Ejiao pieces need to soak for 48 hours; ejiao powder needs 24 hours. Stir multiple times during this period to ensure the ejiao is fully soaked and completely dissolved.
   **Key Point**: Soaking time must be strictly controlled based on the form of the ejiao; stir occasionally to promote dissolution.
3. **Prepare the Supplementary Ingredients**: Pit the red dates and cut into small pieces; place black sesame seeds in a dry pan, stir-fry over low heat until thoroughly cooked and fragrant, then set aside; place walnuts in a dry pan, stir-fry over low heat until the skin easily falls off when rubbed, then set aside; wash the goji berries and drain or air-dry.
4. **Boil the Ejiao Liquid**: Pour the completely dissolved ejiao liquid into a clean stainless steel pot. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Once boiling, reduce to low heat.
5. **Add Rock Sugar**: After reducing to low heat, add 120g of yellow rock sugar. Stir continuously until the sugar is fully dissolved, about 5 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Must stir constantly to prevent sticking to the pot.
6. **Cook to "Hanging Flag" Consistency**: Continue cooking over low heat while stirring. After about 10 minutes, many bubbles will appear; after 15 minutes, it becomes thick; after 20 minutes, it is very thick. Lift some liquid with the wooden spatula. If it can form a stable, inverted-triangle "flag" that does not break easily, the desired consistency is reached.
   **Note**: This is the most critical step. Overcooking makes the texture too hard; undercooking makes it difficult to set.
7. **Add Solid Ingredients**: Keep the heat on the lowest setting. Add in order: chopped red dates, chopped walnuts, and goji berries, stirring well after each addition. Finally, add the black sesame seeds, then turn off the heat. Continue stirring with residual heat until evenly mixed.
8. **Set the Cake**: Line the inside of the mold with parchment paper. Pour in the mixed ejiao cake mixture. Cover the surface with another piece of parchment paper, then press flat with your hand or a tool. Allow it to cool completely before removing from the mold.
   **Key Point**: Must cool until completely hard, otherwise it will be difficult to shape.
9. **Unmold and Cut into Pieces**: Invert the completely cooled and hardened ejiao cake to unmold. Remove the parchment paper and cut into small pieces of the desired shape.

## Health Advice
Ejiao cake nourishes the blood and beautifies the skin. It is suitable for women who have cold hands and feet in winter. However, due to the high content of yellow wine and rock sugar, diabetic patients should avoid it.
