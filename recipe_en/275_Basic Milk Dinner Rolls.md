# Basic Milk Dinner Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 1 egg (for kneading the dough)
🥛 300 ml pure milk
🍞 500 grams high-gluten flour (wheat kernel flour can be used)
🧈 40 grams butter (softened at room temperature, added using the post-oil method)
🫘 White sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)
🥚 Egg yolk liquid, as needed (for brushing before baking)
🧪 5 grams yeast
🍬 80 grams white sugar
🧂 A pinch of salt (small amount)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Dough scraper
🪰 Rolling pin (optional)
🥘 Metal baking pan
🖌️ Silicone brush
♨️ Oven
📜 Aluminum foil
🍽️ Cooling rack

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack 1 egg into a bowl, add 300 ml pure milk, 80 grams white sugar, 5 grams yeast, and a pinch of salt. Stir with chopsticks until the sugar and yeast are mostly dissolved.
   **Key Point**: First mix the liquid with sugar, yeast, and salt to help activate the yeast.
2. Add 500 grams high-gluten flour, stir into a flaky texture until no dry flour remains.
3. Knead the flaky dough into a ball, then transfer to a work surface to continue kneading. If the dough is soft and sticky, use a scraper to help shape it.
4. Use a combination of "washboard-style" rubbing and slapping to knead the dough for 5-6 minutes, until it becomes smooth, elastic, and non-sticky.
   **Note**: Do not add dry flour during the entire process.
5. Stretch open the dough, wrap in 40 grams of softened butter, and continue kneading. The dough will initially break apart, but keep kneading until the butter is fully absorbed.
6. Continue kneading for about 10 minutes. Check if the dough can be stretched into a nearly transparent thin membrane. Stop once this state is achieved.
7. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in the bowl. Seal and perform the first fermentation. This can be done in an oven set to 30-35°C or in warm water using a water bath.
   **Assessment**: The dough should ferment to about twice its original size, with a fine honeycomb interior.
8. Remove the dough, gently knead to release gas, and use a scraper to evenly divide it into 9 portions. Roll each portion into a ball.
9. Place the round dough balls into the baking pan, leaving space between them. Perform a second fermentation for about 30 minutes, until they expand to 1.5-2 times their size, fill the pan, and feel light to the touch.
10. Evenly brush a layer of egg yolk liquid onto the surface of the fermented dough balls, then sprinkle white sesame seeds on top.
11. Preheat the oven to 180°C (upper and lower heat). Place the baking pan on the middle-lower rack and bake for about 25 minutes.
    **Key Point**: Check the color during baking. Once the desired browning is achieved, immediately cover with aluminum foil to prevent burning.
12. Once out of the oven, immediately transfer to a cooling rack to cool. Store sealed for up to 3 days.

## Health Advice
These milk dinner rolls are rich in carbohydrates and protein, making them suitable for post-workout energy replenishment. However, their sugar and butter content is not low, so it is recommended to consume 1-2 rolls per serving during a weight loss phase. Ensure the yeast is fully activated, or the dough will not ferment properly.
