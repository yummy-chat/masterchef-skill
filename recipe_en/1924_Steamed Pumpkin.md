# Steamed Pumpkin
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

🍽️ **Ingredients**  
- Pumpkin 300 g  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- This recipe does not use any seasonings, preserving the natural sweetness  

🔪 **Kitchenware**  
- Steamer  
- Plate  
- Chopsticks  

---

**Cooking Steps**  

1. Wash the outer skin of the pumpkin, cut it open, and remove the pulp and seeds.  
2. Cut the pumpkin into slices about 2 cm thick and set aside.  
3. Pour 1000 ml of drinking water into the steamer.  
4. Arrange the cut pumpkin slices evenly on a plate.  
5. Once the water in the steamer is fully boiling, place the plate with pumpkin into the steamer.  
6. Cover the lid and steam over high heat for 15–20 minutes, until the pumpkin becomes soft and can be easily pierced through with chopsticks, indicating it is fully cooked.  
7. After turning off the heat, carefully remove the plate to avoid burns.  

---

⚠️ **Key Notes**  
- Older pumpkins are usually sweeter and fluffier, recommended as the first choice.  
- Pumpkin skin is rich in nutrients; if steaming with the skin on, be sure to wash the surface thoroughly.  
- Adjust the steaming time according to the size, thickness, and variety of the pumpkin pieces; the standard for doneness is being easily pierced with chopsticks.

## Health Advice
Steamed pumpkin is low in fat and high in fiber, rich in β-carotene, which benefits eye health and immunity, especially suitable for blood sugar-conscious individuals, the elderly, and children. It is recommended to keep the pumpkin skin on, washing it thoroughly before steaming to retain more dietary fiber.
