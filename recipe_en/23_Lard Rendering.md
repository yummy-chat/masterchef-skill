# Lard Rendering
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork leaf lard, 2 jin (approx. 2.4 lbs or 1 kg)  
💧 Water, as needed (enough to cover lard for blanching; for rendering, use 1 jin water per 2 jin lard)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste (cut into large chunks)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced into large pieces)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small pinch (for blanching)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine, to taste (for blanching)  
⭐ Star anise, 2-3 pieces (added during rendering)  
🧂 Salt, a small pinch (for storage)  
🍃 Dried bay leaves, 2-3 pieces (for storage)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board + Chef's knife  
🥄 Ladle or spoon  
🫙 Ceramic jar (or heat-resistant container)  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the leaf lard**: Cut the leaf lard into evenly sized small pieces to ensure consistent heating during rendering, preventing some pieces from burning while others remain under-rendered.
2. **Blanch to remove odor**: Bring water to a boil in a wok, add large chunks of scallion and ginger along with Sichuan peppercorns, and boil for 2-3 minutes until the aromatics release their fragrance. Remove and discard the solids, keeping the infused water.
   - Add the cut leaf lard to the water and blanch for about 1 minute. Do not blanch for too long to prevent excessive fat loss.
   - Remove the leaf lard, rinse first with warm water, then switch to cold water and rinse until the water runs clear. This step helps achieve a whiter final lard.
3. **Water-rendering method**: Place the rinsed leaf lard into a clean wok and add about 1 jin (approx. 1.2 lbs or 500 ml) of water.
   - Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer slowly, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom.
   - Initially, the liquid in the wok will appear milky white. Continue heating until the sound of sizzling water disappears and the liquid becomes clear and transparent, indicating that all the water has evaporated and the rendering is now in the pure fat phase.
   - At this point, add large chunks of scallion and ginger along with 2-3 star anise for fragrance. Maintain medium heat and continue rendering.
   - Observe the color of the cracklings. When the lard cracklings turn light golden brown and the scallion and ginger become golden, immediately turn off the heat. Allow the residual heat to deepen the color slightly to prevent burning, which would create a bitter taste.
4. **Filter and store**: Place a small pinch of salt and 2-3 bay leaves at the bottom of the storage jar.
   - Position a slotted spoon or strainer over the jar opening, then pour the hot oil along with the aromatics from the wok into the jar, filtering out all solids.
   - Cover the jar and allow it to cool naturally in a cool place until solidified. Once set, the lard will be snowy white and smooth in texture, with a long shelf life.

## Health Advice  
Lard is rich in saturated fatty acids and high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables and limit daily intake to less than 15 grams. It is suitable for individuals with high physical energy expenditure or those needing to increase energy intake. Use with caution if you have high blood lipid levels.
