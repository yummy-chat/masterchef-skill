# Pine Flower Tofu and Pork Skin Jelly
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Silken Tofu 2 boxes (about 400g)
🥓 Pork Skin 1 whole piece (about 500g, needs blanching, degreasing, dehairing, and shredding)
🥚 Century Eggs (Preserved Eggs) 3 pieces (diced)
🧂 Baking Soda 5g (for scrubbing the pork skin shreds)
🧅 Scallion Sections 2-3 pieces
🫚 Ginger Slices 4-5 slices
🧂 Salt 10g
🌶️ White Pepper Powder to taste
🌶️ Bird's Eye Chili 1 piece (minced, for dipping sauce)
🧄 Minced Garlic 1 clove (minced, for dipping sauce)
🥄 Light Soy Sauce 1/2 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)
🥄 Fragrant Vinegar 1/2 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)
🫒 Sesame Oil 1/2 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Chili Oil 1 tablespoon (for dipping sauce)

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Meat Grinder/Food Processor
♨️ Steamer
🔪 Knife and Cutting Board
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🍽️ Plate, Airtight Container

## Preparation Steps
1. **Pork Skin Pre-treatment**
   Place the whole pork skin in cold water, bring to a boil, blanch for 5 minutes, then remove and rinse with cold water.
   **Key Point**: After blanching, thoroughly clean off the fat and any remaining hairs.

2. **Cleaning and Shredding**
   Scrape off the fat from the inner side of the pork skin with a knife, remove any hairs from the outer side, then cut the pork skin into thin shreds.
   **Note**: Fat and hairs must be completely removed, otherwise, the texture and clarity of the finished product will be affected.

3. **Washing the Pork Skin Shreds**
   Place the shredded pork skin in a bowl, add 5g of baking soda, and rub it in evenly with your hands. Add water and scrub repeatedly several times, finally rinsing with clean water until clean.
   **Key Point**: Baking soda effectively removes the gamey smell and tenderizes the meat.

4. **Preparing the Century Eggs**
   Peel and dice the century eggs, then spread them on the bottom of a plate and set aside.
5. **Steaming the Pork Skin Broth (Jelly Liquid)**
   Put the cleaned and drained pork skin shreds into a bowl. Add water that is about 5 times the weight of the pork skin, add the scallion sections, ginger slices, 10g salt, and an appropriate amount of white pepper powder. Stir to combine. Place the bowl in a steamer, cover, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and steam for 40 minutes, until the pork skin is soft and the broth is clear.
6. **Making the First Layer (Bottom Layer)**
   Remove the steamed pork skin broth from the steamer. Discard the scallions and ginger. While hot, pour about half of the clear broth into the plate that has the diced century eggs spread at the bottom, making sure the liquid covers the eggs. Set aside to cool naturally until it has just started to set.
7. **Making the Second Layer (Top Layer)**
   Put the remaining two-thirds of the steamed pork skin shreds and the silken tofu into a food processor or meat grinder. Blend into a smooth, white paste. Once the century egg layer in the plate has slightly set from cooling, evenly pour the white paste over the century egg layer.
8. **Chilling to Set**
   Place the prepared jelly in the refrigerator (in the plate) to chill overnight until completely firm.
9. **Unmolding, Slicing, and Dipping Sauce**
   In a small bowl, combine the minced bird's eye chili and minced garlic. Add 1/2 tablespoon of light soy sauce, 1/2 tablespoon of fragrant vinegar, 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon of chili oil. Stir to mix into a dipping sauce. Once the two-layered jelly is completely set, invert the plate to unmold the jelly. Cut it into thick slices and arrange on a serving plate. Drizzle with the prepared dipping sauce and garnish with chopped scallions for serving.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in collagen and high-quality protein, suitable for those looking to improve skin health or supplement calcium intake. However, pork skin jelly is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to consume in moderation. Individuals with weak digestive function or high blood lipids should be mindful of their portion size.
