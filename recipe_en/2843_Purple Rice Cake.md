# Purple Rice Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Purple glutinous rice 400g (can be pre-soaked or cooked directly without soaking)
🍚 White glutinous rice 200g
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🧂 Salt 3g
💧 Hot water 180g (water temperature around 60-70°C)
🌾 Dry flour as needed (for anti-stick)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for greasing and pan-frying)
🍬 Granulated sugar 100g (can be adjusted according to personal taste)
🥛 Condensed milk as needed (to enhance milky flavor)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker / electric pressure cooker
🥣 Large bowl
🧻 Plastic wrap
🥢 Rolling pin
🔩 Flat-bottomed stainless steel basin (for shaping the filling)
🍳 Non-stick pan / cast iron pan
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Knife

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the purple glutinous rice and white glutinous rice thoroughly, place them in the rice cooker, add the same amount of water as for regular rice cooking, start the cooking program, and simmer for about 45 minutes to make purple rice.  
   **Note**: The water amount should be the same as for regular rice cooking; pre-soaking can shorten the cooking time, but it's also fine without soaking.

2. Add salt to the flour and mix well. Gradually pour in hot water at 60-70°C, stirring continuously until it forms flocculent pieces, then knead into a dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 5-10 minutes.

3. After resting, knead the dough until the surface is smooth. Wrap it tightly with plastic wrap and continue resting at room temperature for 20-30 minutes to allow the gluten to fully relax.

4. Once the purple rice is cooked, immediately add the sugar and condensed milk while hot, and stir thoroughly until the sugar is completely dissolved, making the filling.  
   **Key point**: Stirring while hot ensures the sweetness is evenly distributed; sweetness can be adjusted to personal taste.

5. Take a flat-bottomed stainless steel basin, brush with a layer of cooking oil to prevent sticking, pour in the mixed purple rice filling, press it firmly and smooth it out with a spatula. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate to set.

6. Remove the rested dough and divide it into two equal portions. Dust the work surface generously with dry flour, take one portion, and roll it out into a thin, large sheet. Meanwhile, unmold the chilled purple rice filling and cut it into semicircular pieces.

7. Place a semicircular piece of purple rice filling in the center of the dough sheet, wrap it completely with the dough, pinch the edges tightly to seal, and trim off any excess dough around the filling.  
   **Note**: The edges must be sealed tightly to prevent the filling from leaking during pan-frying; excess dough can be reused.

8. Heat a pan over high heat, then brush with enough cooking oil and place the wrapped cake in the pan. Cook over low heat throughout. Once one side is golden and crispy, flip it over, then stand the curved edge of the cake upright against the pan and cook until golden.  
   **Key point**: Use plenty of oil and maintain low heat to ensure the crust is crispy and the filling is fully cooked; total cooking time is a few minutes, until both sides are golden and the crust feels crispy when pressed.

9. Remove the cooked purple rice cake from the pan, let it cool slightly, then cut into small pieces with a knife and serve. The finished cake has a thin, crispy crust and a sweet, soft, and chewy filling, enjoyable both hot and cold.

## Health Advice
Purple rice is rich in anthocyanins and dietary fiber. Combined with glutinous rice, it can strengthen the spleen and stomach and replenish blood, making it suitable for people with anemia or poor digestion. However, glutinous rice and sugar are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat only one piece per serving and not to overconsume.
