# Extra Crispy French Fries 🍟
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 4 black-skinned or yellow-skinned potatoes
🌽 2 tablespoons cornstarch
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍶 Yogurt, to taste
🌶️ Chili powder, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, to taste
🧂 A pinch of cumin powder

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Steamer
🔪 Small knife or chef’s knife
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
📄 Plate

## Instructions
1. Wash floury potatoes thoroughly, steam whole over high heat for about 40 minutes, until a chopstick pierces through easily.
   **Key point**: You can cut them into chunks to shorten steaming time.
2. Let the steamed potatoes cool completely, then peel off the skin with a small knife.
3. Cut the peeled potatoes into thick strips.
   **Key point**: The small bits that fall off while cutting are crucial for a crispy crust—do not discard them.
4. Place about 2 potatoes’ worth of strips in a large bowl, add 2 tablespoons cornstarch, and gently shake the bowl to coat each strip evenly.
   **Note**: It is normal for small lumps of starch or potato bits to mix in; no need to remove them.
5. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat to about 165°C (329°F).
6. Fry the coated potato strips in batches, gently separating them with chopsticks to prevent sticking.
   **Key point**: Do not pour the crumbs left at the bottom of the bowl into the oil.
7. Once golden and slightly browned, quickly remove with a slotted spoon, drain on a paper towel-lined plate, and sprinkle with salt while hot.
8. Optional yogurt dipping sauce: Mix yogurt, salt, chili powder, cilantro, and a pinch of cumin powder until well combined.
   **Note**: Adjust the dipping sauce to your taste.

## Health Tips
It is recommended to serve these fries with a fresh vegetable salad to balance the oil content. Steaming before coating and frying reduces oil absorption. Suitable for occasional indulgence, but high in starch and calories—those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should control portion sizes.
