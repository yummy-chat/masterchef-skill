# Vegetable Radish Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥕 1 carrot (medium-sized, peeled)  
🥒 1 green radish (medium-sized, peeled)  
🌿 2 sprigs of cilantro (about 30g, minced)  
🧅 2 sprigs of scallion (minced)  
🥚 2 eggs  
🌾 About 90-100g flour (add gradually, adjust flexibly)  

🧂 About 9g salt (used in two stages: 8g first to marinate the radish shreds, 1g second for the filling)  
🌿 About 1g (half teaspoon) of thirteen-spice powder (or five-spice powder)  
🧂 About 5g (2 teaspoons) chicken bouillon  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥕 Grater  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl  
👝 Gauze bag or clean cloth bag (for squeezing out water)  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Wooden chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)  
🍽️ Serving plate (lined with paper towels)  

## Instructions  
1. Peel the carrot and green radish, then grate them into fine shreds using a grater. Place them in a large bowl.  
   **Note**: Combining the two types of radishes improves the color and flavor of the finished dish.  
2. Add 8g of salt to the radish shreds, mix well with your hands, and set aside to marinate for 15 minutes until they become soft and release water.  
   **Note**: The amount of salt must be precise, as this forms the base saltiness; do not rinse afterward.  
3. Transfer the marinated radish shreds into a gauze bag and squeeze out the water firmly.  
   **Key**: The drier the shreds, the lighter and fresher the texture of the balls.  
4. Slightly chop the squeezed radish shreds. Mince the scallions and cilantro, and add everything to a large bowl.  
5. Add the minced scallions, cilantro, thirteen-spice powder, chicken bouillon, and the remaining 1g of salt to the bowl. Crack in the 2 eggs. First, break up the egg yolks with your fingers, then mix all the ingredients thoroughly.  
   **Note**: The second addition of salt must be minimal; it is better for the mixture to be slightly bland than too salty.  
6. Gradually add the flour in small batches, mixing well after each addition, until the filling becomes sticky and can be formed into a ball.  
   **Key**: Flour is only a binder; use about 90-100g and adjust flexibly based on the moisture of the filling. Too much flour will affect the texture.  
7. Take an appropriate amount of filling and, using your hands, squeeze it into a round ball using the thumb and index finger. The ball should be slightly larger than a tangyuan (glutinous rice ball).  
   **Note**: Do not make the balls too large to ensure they cook through properly.  
8. Pour a generous amount of oil into a wok. Test the oil temperature with a wooden chopstick: when dense bubbles form around the chopstick when inserted, reduce the heat to low and carefully drop in the balls.  
9. Fry over low heat for about 20 seconds until the balls are set, then gently turn them. Fry for about 2 minutes total, until the balls are firm and hold their shape, and the surface is a light golden color. Remove and drain the oil.  
   **Note**: Do not stir the balls immediately after adding them to the oil, or they may fall apart.  
10. Reheat the oil until it is hot and dense bubbles form. Return all the balls to the wok, increase the heat to medium-high, and fry for a second time for about 1 minute, until the shell is golden brown and slightly crispy. Remove and drain the oil.  
    **Key**: The second frying makes the exterior crispy, but the oil temperature should not be too high, and the frying time should not be too long, or the balls will turn black and taste bitter.  

## Health Tips  
These vegetable radish balls are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, suitable for those who want to control their calorie intake but still enjoy fried food. Squeezing out the water before frying gives a lighter texture; controlling the second frying time prevents bitterness.
