# Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

### 🥣 Ingredients
- 🍞 Flour 400g
- 💧 Hot water 130ml (about 80℃)
- 💧 Cold water 130ml

### 🧂 Seasonings
- 🛢️ Oil (for spreading and frying)
- 🥄 Oil pastry (to be prepared in advance, hot oil pastry recommended)

### 🔧 Utensils
- 🥘 Mixing bowl
- 🍳 Electric pancake pan
- 🧤 Plastic wrap
- 🧹 Rolling pin

### 📝 Steps
1. Place 400g flour in a mixing bowl. Gradually add 130ml hot water (about 80℃) and 130ml cold water in batches, stirring each into flocculent pieces before mixing together to form a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 40 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The half-scalded dough technique results in a crispy exterior and soft interior; adequate resting is needed to enhance elasticity.

2. 10 minutes before the resting time ends, prepare the oil pastry according to the hot oil pastry recipe for later use.

3. Gently press the rested dough to release air, then divide it evenly into four portions.

4. Roll each portion into a ball, then flatten and roll into a round pancake roughly the diameter of the electric pancake pan. Spread 1/4 of the oil pastry evenly over the surface.

5. Cut a slit along the radius of the pancake, then roll it from the cut into a cone shape. Pinch the bottom tightly to seal, preventing the oil pastry from leaking.

6. Press the tip of the cone down into a flat round shape, then roll it into a pancake base about 3mm thick.

7. Preheat the electric pancake pan, brush with cold oil (hot pan, cold oil), place the pancake base in, and brush the surface with oil to lock in moisture. Cover.

8. Cook over high heat for 1 minute, then flip and continue cooking for another minute.

9. Repeat step 8 until both sides are golden brown and fully cooked. Remove from the pan.

**Note**: Ensure sufficient resting time, spread the oil pastry evenly, roll the pancake to about 3mm thickness, and avoid excessively high heat during cooking to prevent burning.

## Health Advice
This pancake uses the half-scalded dough technique, resulting in a crispy exterior and soft interior. It pairs well with light dishes to balance carbohydrate intake. Control the amount of oil pastry used; during a weight loss period, reduce the frequency of brushing oil. It is recommended to eat alongside vegetables and protein to avoid blood sugar fluctuations from consuming it alone.
