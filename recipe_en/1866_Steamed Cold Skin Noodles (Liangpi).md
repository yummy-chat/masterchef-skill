# Steamed Cold Skin Noodles (Liangpi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫓 All-purpose flour 500g (medium gluten flour)
💧 Cold water 230g (for kneading, plus additional for washing dough and adjusting concentration)
🥒 Cucumber to taste (shredded)
🧽 Gluten to taste (steamed from the gluten washed out of the dough)
🧄 Garlic water to taste
🍶 Vinegar water to taste
🌶️ Chili oil to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowls (multiple)
🥄 Soup ladle
🪥 Brush
🥅 Strainer/sieve
🍳 Large iron pot
🥘 Steaming tray for Liangpi (with handles)
📏 Concentration meter (Baumé gauge)
🔪 Large cutting board and large knife

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead and Wash the Dough**
   Mix 500g flour with 230g cold water, knead into a smooth dough. Submerge the dough in clean water and repeatedly knead and wash until elastic gluten is separated and the wash water runs clear.
   **Key**: Wash thoroughly to ensure complete separation of starch and gluten.

2. **Settle the Starch Solution**
   Let the starch solution settle for at least 8 hours, until the starch fully sinks to the bottom and the top layer is clear water.
   **Note**: Insufficient settling time will result in sticky Liangpi, affecting texture.

3. **Process the Starch Solution**
   Carefully pour off the top clear water without disturbing the settled starch at the bottom. Thoroughly stir the thick starch paste at the bottom until smooth.
   **Key**: Pour off water gently to avoid lifting the sediment.

4. **Adjust Starch Solution Concentration**
   Use a concentration meter to check the starch solution; the optimal range is **17–18 degrees**.
   - If too thick: Add a small amount of the previously poured-off clear water to dilute.
   - If too thin: Continue settling until excess water separates.
   **Key**: Concentration directly determines the firmness and elasticity of the Liangpi; too thick results in hard, brittle sheets, too thin yields soft sheets difficult to lift.

5. **Apply and Swirl the Starch Solution**
   Brush a thin layer of oil on the bottom of the steaming tray. Ladle one scoop of starch solution into the tray, and shake horizontally with both hands to spread it evenly across the tray bottom.
   **Note**: Oil prevents sticking; even spreading ensures uniform thickness.

6. **Steam**
   Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil over high heat. Place the tray on the boiling water surface. Quickly rotate the tray by hand once on the water surface to further even out the starch solution. Cover with a heavy lid and steam over high heat for 2 minutes.
   **Doneness Indicator**: The Liangpi is ready when large bubbles form on the surface.
   **Key**: Use high heat and ample steam; a heavy lid creates pressure for quick, even cooking and a glossy surface; rotating upon entry is crucial for uniform thickness.

7. **Cool and Demold**
   Immediately remove the tray and cool it in cold water. Once completely cool, gently lift the entire sheet of Liangpi from the edge with your fingers.
   **Note**: Must be cooled in cold water for clean demolding, while also enhancing the chewy texture.

8. **Slice and Season**
   Fold the Liangpi, cut into wide strips with a large knife, and shake apart. Place in a bowl, top with shredded cucumber and gluten. Add garlic water, vinegar water, and chili oil to taste. Mix well and serve.
   **Tip**: Cut strips about two fingers wide for easy eating and to prevent breaking.

## Health Advice
This Liangpi is primarily carbohydrates. Adding shredded cucumber and gluten provides a small amount of fiber and protein, making it suitable for a summer appetizer or light meal. It is recommended to control the amount of chili oil to avoid excessive calories. Note that salt is lost during the washing process, so adjust sourness with sufficient vinegar water.
