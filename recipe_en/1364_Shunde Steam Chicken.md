# Shunde Steam Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 free-range chicken (about 1kg, choose a tender free-range chicken)  
🫀 Chicken liver, as needed  
🫀 Chicken heart, as needed  
🫀 Chicken gizzard, as needed  
🥒 1 loofah (about 400g, sliced)  
🥕 1 carrot (cut into chunks)  
🌽 1/2 corn cob (cut into sections)  
🫚 15g ginger (sliced, for stir-frying chicken bones, making broth, and steaming seafood)  
🐚 100g sea snails  
🦪 350g clams  
🦐 6–8 large shrimp  
🍄 50g cordyceps flowers  
🌿 10g codonopsis  
🌿 8g polygonatum odoratum  
🌿 15g five-finger fig  
🧄 Sand ginger, as needed (for dipping sauce)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (for dipping sauce)  

🧂 30ml light soy sauce (15ml for marinating, 15ml for dipping sauce)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (3g for marinating, 3g for dipping sauce, final seasoning for broth)  
🍬 8g sugar (3g for marinating, 5g for dipping sauce)  
🧂 2g ground white pepper  
🌾 10g cornstarch  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (25ml for marinating, about 3 tablespoons for stir-frying chicken bones, about 3 tablespoons for hot oil in dipping sauce)  
🌶️ 2 bird's eye chilies (sliced into rings)  
🧅 5g chopped scallions  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Boning knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle with handle  
🥣 Large bowl  
📦 Airtight container  
♨️ Midea Magic Cube Electric Steamer (with three-layer stainless steel steamer baskets)  
🔄 Small food processor  
🥛 Small saucepan  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Debone the whole chicken: Make cuts under the wing joints, in the middle of the breastbone, and along the center of the back. Peel the whole chicken breast and thigh meat away from the carcass. Finally, remove the leg bones to obtain a complete chicken skeleton.  
2. Cutting and Marinating: Cut the deboned chicken meat into strips about 1cm wide. Cut the chicken liver, heart, and gizzard into similar sizes. Mix together, then add light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and ground white pepper, tossing well. Add cornstarch and mix well. Finally, drizzle with cooking oil and marinate.  
3. Prepare Broth Ingredients: Cut the chicken skeleton into small pieces. Cut the carrot into chunks and the corn into sections.  
4. Prepare Steaming Vegetables: Slice the loofah into even pieces.  
5. Make Dipping Sauce: Finely chop sand ginger and garlic using a food processor and place in a bowl. Add salt, sugar, and light soy sauce. Heat cooking oil in a small saucepan, then pour over the mixture to release the aroma. Add chopped scallions and bird's eye chili rings, and stir to combine.  
### Cooking Phase  
1. Stir-fry and Simmer the Broth: Add an appropriate amount of oil to a wok. Over medium heat, stir-fry the chicken skeleton and ginger slices until the chicken skeleton is lightly browned. Add enough water, bring to a boil, and skim off any foam. Add codonopsis, polygonatum odoratum, five-finger fig, carrots, and corn. Reduce to low heat and simmer for about 1 hour.  
2. Assemble and Steam: Once the broth is ready and kept at a rolling boil, place the steamer baskets on top.  
   - Middle layer: Evenly spread the loofah slices first. Then, mix the marinated chicken strips with cordyceps flowers and spread them flat on top of the loofah, avoiding overlapping as much as possible.  
   - Top layer: Arrange the sea snails, large shrimp, and clams on top, and add a few slices of ginger to remove any fishy smell.  
   Cover the steamer lid and steam over high heat for about 5 minutes.  
3. Serving: Once steamed, first remove the top layer of seafood and chicken, and dip in the sand ginger dipping sauce. Then, take out the middle layer of loofah, which has absorbed the flavorful juices. Finally, ladle the chicken broth from the bottom into bowls, season with salt, and enjoy. The entire process is seamless, allowing you to enjoy meat, vegetables, and broth at the same time.

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. Steaming preserves nutrients, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss or nourishing diet. Control the salt in the dipping sauce; those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of sand ginger. Chicken liver and heart are relatively high in cholesterol, so it is recommended to limit consumption to no more than 100 grams per serving.
