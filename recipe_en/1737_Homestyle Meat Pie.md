# Homestyle Meat Pie
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour (medium-gluten flour) about 1000g (do not use high-gluten flour, otherwise the texture will be too tough)
💧 Cold water about 300g (flour to water ratio approximately 5:3)
🐖 Ground pork appropriate amount (fat-to-lean ratio 3:7)
🧅 Scallions generous amount (cut into scallion rings, do not chop too finely)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🧂 Salt about 12g (adjust according to the amount of meat)
🤍 White pepper powder appropriate amount
🫗 Soy sauce appropriate amount (Northern style uses more, for color and flavor)
🫗 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (mainly for color, to make the filling darker)
🍶 Rice wine appropriate amount (to remove gamey flavor; Fujian rice wine is used in the video)
💧 Scallion-ginger water appropriate amount (optional, used to add moisture to the meat filling for tenderness; can be replaced with plain water)
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount (added after the meat filling is seasoned and before adding vegetables, to lock in moisture and enhance aroma)
🥜 Peanut oil/scallion oil appropriate amount (added together with sesame oil to lock in moisture)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl (large bowl)
🫗 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing filling)
🥢 Wooden spatula or filling scraper
🪓 Rolling pin
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🧻 Damp cloth or plastic wrap
🍳 Electric griddle (or frying pan)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the all-purpose flour in a mixing bowl. Gradually add cold water and knead until the dough is very soft. It is normal for the dough to be rough and not smooth initially.
2. Place the dough in the bowl, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rest for about 20 minutes. After resting, knead the dough again until smooth and elastic.
3. To the ground pork, add salt, white pepper powder, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and rice wine. Vigorously stir in one direction until the meat mixture is well-combined, the color darkens, and the texture becomes firm. During this process, you may optionally add a small amount of scallion-ginger water or plain water in batches to make the filling more tender.
**Note**: At this point, you may optionally add a small amount of scallion-ginger water or plain water in batches to make the filling more tender.

4. After the meat filling is seasoned and stirred, add sesame oil and peanut oil and mix well to lock in moisture. Then add the minced ginger and mix. Cut the scallions into scallion rings, keeping them chunky, and do not mix them into the filling yet.
5. Just before assembling, spread the scallion rings over the seasoned meat filling and quickly mix until well combined. Note: Scallions should not come into contact with salt too early, as this will cause off-flavors and water release.
**Key Point**: Scallions should not come into contact with salt too early, as this will cause off-flavors and water release.

6. Take out the rested dough. Without excessive kneading, divide it directly into several large pieces. Take one piece and use a rolling pin to roll it into a large, thin rectangular or tongue-shaped sheet.
7. Evenly spread the mixed meat filling over the dough sheet, leaving the edges clean. Using the folding method, fold from one end towards the center, pinching and sealing the edges as you fold, to form a rectangular pie crust. Note: After folding, do not roll again with the rolling pin. Gently press and shape with your hands to the desired thickness to prevent breaking the crust and leaking the filling.
**Note**: After folding, do not roll with a rolling pin. Gently press and shape with your hands to the desired thickness and shape to prevent breaking the crust and leaking the filling.

8. Preheat the electric griddle and brush with a little oil. Place the pie crust on the griddle, cover, and cook. Because the pie is relatively thick, the cooking time needs to be extended appropriately. Cook until one side is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown as well. The pie is done when both sides are a deep golden brown and the meat filling inside is completely cooked through.
**Key Point**: Cook until one side is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown. The pie is done when both sides are a deep golden brown and the meat filling inside is completely cooked through.

## Health Suggestion
This meat pie is a high-carb, high-fat "carb bomb". It is recommended to serve it with a side of dressed vegetables to balance nutrition. It is suitable for manual laborers or those looking to build muscle, but those on a weight loss journey should control their portion sizes to avoid excessive intake of fats and carbohydrates.
