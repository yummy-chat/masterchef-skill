# Home-Style Braised Pork Belly

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐

## Steps
1. **Boil the pork**: Wash the pork belly and place it in cold water, skin side down. Bring to a boil. You can add yellow rice wine to remove the gamey smell. Keep the water boiling continuously and cook for about 20-30 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through without any blood oozing out. If the meat is quite fatty, maintain a high heat to render out some of the fat.
2. **Handle and cut into pieces**: Remove the meat and rinse it with cold water to cool it down. Use tweezers to remove any remaining hairs on the skin. While still warm, cut the meat into fairly large pieces.
3. **Prepare aromatics and sauce**: Place scallion sections, ginger slices, cinnamon stick, and star anise in a bowl for later use. In another bowl, mix the hoisin sauce, char siu sauce, and light soy sauce together until well combined.
4. **Sauté the aromatics**: Heat the clay pot over high heat, pour in cooking oil, and add the scallion, ginger, and spices. Sauté over medium-low heat until fragrant.
5. **Cook the sauce**: Pour in the mixed sauce and continue heating until it bubbles and releases its aroma.
6. **Add liquid and braise**: Add enough boiling water or the reserved meat broth to cover the meat. Add the cut pork belly pieces. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Add dark soy sauce for coloring. If you are worried about greasiness, you can add a generous amount of yellow rice wine to braise together.
7. **Slow simmer**: Reduce the heat to low, keep the liquid at a gentle simmer, cover the pot, and braise for about 1 hour.
8. **Check for tenderness**: Use chopsticks to gently poke a piece of fat. When there is absolutely no resistance, the meat has reached the ideal state of tenderness.
9. **Final steps**:
   - For deeper flavor and to allow excess fat to render out, turn off the heat, cover the pot tightly, and let it rest for at least 2 hours before serving.
   - If serving immediately, add the pre-soaked wide vermicelli directly to the pot and bring to a boil again.

## Health Advice
Pork belly is rich in protein and B vitamins, but its fat content is quite high. It is recommended to serve it with plenty of vegetables to balance the meal. The wide vermicelli provides carbohydrates, making it a good energy source for those with high physical exertion. Due to the long braising time, people with weak bile secretion should consume it in small amounts to avoid indigestion.
