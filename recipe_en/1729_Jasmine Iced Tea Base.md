# Jasmine Iced Tea Base
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍵 Jasmine green tea 25g  
💧 Hot water 700g (at 80°C)  
🧊 Ice cubes 100g  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🫖 Kettle (with temperature control)  
🍶 Heat-resistant glass measuring cup  
🥄 Strainer  
🍸 Glass pitcher (for cooling the tea)  

## Instructions  
1. Weigh out 25g of jasmine green tea, and heat water to 80°C.  
   **Key point**: Water temperature must be precisely controlled to avoid burning the tea leaves, which would cause bitterness.  
2. Place the tea leaves in a heat-resistant glass measuring cup, then slowly pour in 700g of hot water at 80°C, ensuring the leaves are fully submerged.  
3. Leave uncovered and let steep for 7 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Stick to the exact timing; steeping too long can make the tea astringent, while too short will result in weak flavor.  
4. Strain the tea through a strainer into a glass pitcher, removing all tea leaves to ensure a clear tea.  
5. Immediately add 100g of ice cubes to the hot tea, and stir until the ice has completely melted and the tea is fully chilled.  
   **Key point**: Rapid cooling helps lock in the jasmine aroma, producing a ready-to-use iced tea base. Once cooled, it can be sealed and refrigerated for use in various iced tea drinks.  

## Health Advice  
Jasmine green tea is rich in tea polyphenols and aromatic compounds. It is sugar-free and fat-free, making it a healthy choice for cooling down and cutting through greasiness in summer. Suitable for those who need a pick-me-up but are concerned about caffeine intake, though drinking on an empty stomach may irritate the stomach lining. It is recommended to limit consumption of sweetened versions.
