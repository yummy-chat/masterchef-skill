# Cantonese-Style Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Pork belly 1 large piece (with skin)  
🧂 Coarse sea salt to taste (for curing, rinsed off afterward)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (smashed)  
🧅 Dried shallots to taste  
⭐ Star anise to taste  
🍃 Bay leaves to taste  
🍅 Tomato to taste  
🍬 Brown sugar powder to taste (for caramelizing)  
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste  
🧂 Dark soy sauce to taste  
🍬 Rock sugar to taste  
🍶 Cantonese rice wine to taste (do not use yellow wine)  
🧂 Salt to taste  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer (or steaming pot)  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls/plates  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Keep the skin of the pork belly intact. Score the meat side in a crosshatch pattern, without cutting through the skin. Evenly rub coarse sea salt onto the meat side and let it cure briefly, then rinse off the excess salt.  
**Note**: Coarse sea salt penetrates quickly and rinses off easily, preventing the meat from becoming too salty; scoring prevents the meat from shrinking excessively during heating and helps with flavor absorption.  
2. Place the cured pork belly in a steamer and steam for 20 minutes. Once removed, let it cool slightly, then cut into bite-sized pieces.  
**Key point**: Steaming makes the meat tender and smooth while rendering out some fat, resulting in a cleaner, less greasy final dish.  
3. Heat a small amount of oil in a wok. Add brown sugar powder and stir continuously over low heat until the sugar melts completely and turns a dark caramel color. Remove and set aside.  
**Key point**: The caramel is mainly for coloring and adding complex aroma. Stop at a dark brown hue to avoid bitterness.  
4. Heat oil in the wok. Add the smashed garlic and fry until golden brown, then add the dried shallots and stir-fry until fragrant.  
**Note**: Garlic releases its aroma more slowly, so fry it thoroughly before adding the shallots.  
5. Add the steamed and cut pork belly pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface of the meat begins to release a little oil.  
6. Pour an appropriate amount of Cantonese rice wine along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly to release the wine’s fragrance.  
**Note**: Cantonese rice wine must be used here for its pure rice aroma that does not overpower the meat; do not substitute with yellow wine.  
7. Add star anise and bay leaves, and stir-fry a few times.  
8. Pour in enough water to just cover the meat. Then add salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rock sugar, tomato pieces, and the previously prepared caramel sauce. Stir to combine.  
9. Cover the wok with a lid and braise over medium heat for 30 minutes.  
**Key point**: The braising time must be sufficient to achieve a tender yet chewy texture. Avoid opening the lid frequently during cooking.  
10. After 30 minutes, remove the lid. If the sauce has thickened to a rich consistency, the dish is ready to serve.  
**Finished dish**: Bright red and glossy, with tender and smooth meat that is fatty but not greasy.  

## Health Advice  
This Cantonese-style braised pork belly uses a method of steaming before braising to render out some fat, making it suitable for those who want to reduce greasiness. However, due to the high sugar and sodium content in the seasoning, it is recommended that people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol consume it sparingly.
