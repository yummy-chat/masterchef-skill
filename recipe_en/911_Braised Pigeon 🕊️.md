# Braised Pigeon 🕊️
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐦 3 pigeons (cleaned, claws trimmed, lymph nodes removed, innards discarded)
🧂 70g fine salt (table salt)
🌿 15g thirteen-spice powder
🌿 15g five-spice powder
🍶 100g white vinegar
🍶 30g Zhejiang vinegar (red vinegar)
🍯 50g maltose
🍶适量 Erguotou (Chinese liquor)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
✂️ Scissors
🪝 Metal hooks
🌀 Electric fan
🍳 Deep fryer
🥄 Ladle
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1.  Trim the claws of the prepared pigeons, thoroughly remove any remaining internal organs and blood from the cavity, eliminate the neck lymph nodes, and rinse them clean. Drain well.
2.  In a bowl, combine 70g of fine salt, 15g of thirteen-spice powder, and 15g of five-spice powder. Mix evenly to create the marinade.
3.  Rub the marinade evenly over the inside and outside of the 3 pigeons, massaging to help it adhere.
**Key Point**: The salt's osmotic action will allow the spices to penetrate. Do not worry about it being too salty, as it will be rinsed off later.
4.  After applying the marinade, let the pigeons rest for 2 hours.
5.  Rinse the pigeons under running water to thoroughly wash off the salt and spices. Avoid long soaking to prevent losing the internal spice flavor.
6.  Prepare the crispy skin water: Mix 100g white vinegar, 30g Zhejiang vinegar, 50g maltose, and an适量 amount of Erguotou. Stir until the maltose is completely dissolved.
**Purpose**: To enhance the color and achieve a crispy skin.
7.  Hook each pigeon with a metal hook. First, pour hot water evenly over the pigeon's body to tighten the skin.
8.  Quickly rinse with cold water, then immediately pour the crispy skin water evenly over every part of the pigeon's skin while it is still hot, ensuring full coverage.
9.  Hang the pigeons with the crispy skin water applied and use an electric fan to dry them until the skin is completely dry and no longer sticky to the touch.
**Key Point**: The skin must be fully dried; otherwise, the color will be uneven during frying.
10. Pour oil into the fryer and heat to approximately 150℃ (300°F). Gently place the dried pigeons into the oil, then reduce the heat to medium-low to fry.
11. During frying, continuously turn the pigeons or use a ladle to repeatedly spoon hot oil over their bodies, ensuring even heating and consistent coloring.
12. Fry for about 8–10 minutes, until the skin is a deep red color (jujube red), crispy, and the meat is cooked through. Remove from the oil.

## Health Advice
This braised pigeon dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for muscle building or weight control. However, the salt content and calories from frying are not insignificant. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for a balanced meal. Although the crispy skin is tempting, it is best to consume it in moderation, with one pigeon per serving.
