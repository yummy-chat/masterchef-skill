# Fruit Soufflé
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs (separated into egg whites and yolks)  
🍇 Raisins, to taste  
🫐 Blueberries, to taste  
🍓 Strawberries, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍋 Lemon juice, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, 30g  
🧈 Butter, to taste  
🥛 Condensed milk, to taste  
🍚 Icing sugar (powdered sugar), to taste  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🔧 Silicone spatula  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🍽️ Plate  

## Instructions
1. Separate the egg whites from the yolks. Place the egg whites in a clean, oil-free glass bowl. Set the yolks aside in another bowl.  
   **Note**: Do not let any yolk mix into the whites, as this will prevent them from whipping properly.  
2. Cut the strawberries in half. Rinse the blueberries and drain them well, keeping the surface dry.  
3. Add a pinch of salt to the egg yolks and mix well. Add an appropriate amount of lemon juice to the egg whites, then add 30g of white sugar. Using a whisk, beat quickly until stiff peaks form.  
   **Key point**: Whipping must be fast and vigorous; the meringue state is crucial for the soufflé’s fluffiness.  
4. Pour the mixed egg yolks into the meringue. Fold quickly and evenly, minimizing deflation.  
5. Place the non-stick frying pan over low heat. Add butter and melt it, spreading evenly over the bottom. Pour in the egg mixture. Avoid frequent flipping; let the bottom set.  
   **Heat**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent the butter from burning.  
6. Cover and cook. When the surface is half-set, sprinkle in the raisins.  
7. Carefully flip with the silicone spatula. Continue to cook covered until both sides are golden brown and the inside is fully set. It should spring back when pressed, with no runny egg liquid.  
8. Plate the soufflé. Drizzle with condensed milk, arrange the blueberries and strawberries on top, and finally dust with icing sugar. Serve immediately.  
   **Tip**: The soufflé will deflate as it cools, so enjoy it right away.  

## Health Advice
This fruit soufflé is light and fluffy, making it ideal as an energy-boosting breakfast or a delicate dessert. Be sure to serve it hot to maintain its airy texture. The fruits are rich in vitamins, but the condensed milk and icing sugar are relatively high in calories, so consume in moderation.
