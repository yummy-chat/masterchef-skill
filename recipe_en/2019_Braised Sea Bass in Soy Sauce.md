# Braised Sea Bass in Soy Sauce
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Sea bass 1 piece (cut into chunks)
🧅 Purple onion 1 (sliced)
🫚 Ginger 2-3 slices (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (minced)
🌱 Scallions 2-3 stalks (cut into sections, some reserved for final garnish)
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste
🍶 Cooking wine to适量
🫗 Steamed fish soy sauce to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste
🌾 Cornstarch to taste
🧴 Dark soy sauce to taste
🛢️ Cooking oil to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce)
🚿 Strainer
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🔥 Gas stove
🍽️ Plate, bowl, chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the fish chunks**: Thoroughly rinse the blood and black membrane from the sea bass chunks, then drain well.
   **Note**: All blood inside the fish chunks must be completely removed, otherwise the fishy smell will be strong.
2. **Marinate the fish chunks**: In a bowl, combine salt, white pepper powder, cooking wine, steamed fish soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch. Add the fish chunks and toss thoroughly to coat with a thin batter. Let marinate for 15–20 minutes.
3. **Pan-fry the fish**: Heat an adequate amount of cooking oil in the pan until warm. Add the marinated fish chunks and fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown and set.
   **Key point**: Do not flip the fish chunks frequently after placing them in the pan. Wait until one side is set before turning.
4. **Prepare the sauce**: In a small bowl, mix cooking wine, dark soy sauce, and steamed fish soy sauce. Stir well and set aside.
5. **Braise**: Add ginger slices and sliced onion to the pan with the fried fish. Stir-fry briefly, then pour in the prepared sauce. Cover with a lid and simmer over medium-low heat until the sauce thickens and the fish chunks are fully infused with flavor.
   **Key point**: Covering the pan while simmering allows the fish to better absorb the sauce's flavor.
6. **Finish and garnish**: Remove the lid, sprinkle in scallion sections, and gently toss to combine. Then transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
Sea bass is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for the elderly, children, and those recovering from surgery. When cooking, reduce the amount of salt during marination, as both steamed fish soy sauce and oyster sauce already contain salt. Use medium-low heat when frying the fish to avoid breaking the chunks apart.
