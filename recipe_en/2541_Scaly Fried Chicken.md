# Scaly Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs (upper leg meat) to taste  
🧅 Onion to taste  
🫚 Ginger to taste  
🧄 Garlic to taste  
🌾 Flour generous amount  
🧂 Orleans marinade 35g per 500g of meat  
🧂 Salt to taste  
💧 Water to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Strainer/skimmer  
🧻 Paper towels  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🚿 Spray bottle  
🍳 Deep fryer  
🌡️ Cooking thermometer  
🎬 Plastic wrap  
🥘 Baking sheet  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the skin from the chicken thighs, debone with scissors, and rinse thoroughly with water to remove blood. After deboning, ensure the chicken is drained to about 90% dryness, or pat dry with paper towels. The less residual moisture, the better the marination absorption. Then cut into evenly sized pieces—larger pieces resist overcooking during frying, and uniform size ensures even cooking.
2. Weigh the Orleans marinade at a ratio of 35g per 500g of meat, and mix with a small amount of water to form a paste. Place the chicken pieces in a bowl, add the marinade paste, and optionally add onion strips, ginger slices, and garlic slices. Mix thoroughly by hand.
3. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 3 hours. Overnight marination is preferable for deeper flavor penetration.
4. Pour a generous amount of flour into a large bowl, and optionally add a little salt for base seasoning. Place the marinated chicken pieces into the flour and use a pressing and rubbing technique to coat the chicken, forming small flour clumps on the surface—these are the early scales.
5. Remove the first-coated chicken pieces and lightly spray water evenly over the surface to increase moisture.
6. Return the sprayed chicken pieces to the flour bowl. First, gently cover the moist pieces with dry flour, then press and rub again. Do not press directly on the wet dough, or the coating will fall off. Repeat this coating-spraying-recoating cycle 3–4 times to build up a thick, irregular layered scale pattern.
7. Arrange the coated chicken pieces flat on a baking sheet. They can be fried immediately or frozen for later use.
8. Pour enough oil into a deep fryer and heat to around 160°C. Fry the chicken pieces one by one, maintaining medium heat for about 2 minutes. Once all pieces float and the surface turns golden brown, remove and drain excess oil. Temperature control is key—avoid high heat, which causes burnt exterior and undercooked interior.

## Health Advice
This scaly fried chicken is rich in protein, but due to frying, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance the meal. Suitable for fitness and muscle-building individuals as an occasional treat, but those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should control portions and reduce salt during marination.
