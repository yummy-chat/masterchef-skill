# Sautéed Ground Pork with Hot Pepper Noodles 🍜
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps
1. Prepare ingredients: Set aside the ground pork, diced hot peppers, diced small chili peppers, and chopped scallions in separate bowls.
   **Note**: Ingredients should be diced evenly for uniform cooking.
2. Mix sauce: In a small bowl, combine 3 tablespoons light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 teaspoon Chinese thirteen-spice powder, and 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder. Stir to mix and set aside.
   **Key point**: Pre-mixing the sauce prevents uneven seasoning during frying.
3. Cook noodles: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the wide noodles, and cook until no hard core remains. Remove and immediately immerse in cold water to cool, then drain well.
   **Note**: Rinsing in cold water prevents noodles from sticking and keeps them smooth.
4. Fry ground pork: Add cooking oil to a non-stick pan over medium heat. When the oil is about 60% hot, add ground pork and stir-fry quickly for about 1–2 minutes, until fully cooked and no pink remains.
   **Note**: Ensure oil is hot before adding pork; stir-fry quickly for even cooking.
5. Sauté scallions: Add chopped scallions, stir-fry over medium heat for 30 seconds to 1 minute, until softened and fragrant.
   **Key point**: Fully fry the scallions to release aroma, enhancing the base of the sauce.
6. Add vegetables and sauce: Add diced hot peppers and small chili peppers, then pour in the prepared sauce. Continue stir-frying over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes, until vegetables are just cooked and sauce evenly coats all ingredients.
   **Note**: Stir quickly after adding sauce to avoid local overdosing or excess water release from vegetables.
7. Thicken with slurry: Reduce heat to low, slowly pour in cornstarch slurry while stirring continuously. Cook for about 30 seconds until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, clinging to the spoon.
   **Key point**: Add slurry slowly and stir constantly to prevent lumps; the consistency should evenly coat the ingredients.
8. Assemble: Spoon the hot sautéed ground pork and pepper sauce over the drained noodles. Mix thoroughly with chopsticks so each strand is well coated.
   **Note**: The sauce must be added while hot; the noodles have been cooled in advance, resulting in a warm and refreshing texture after mixing.

## Health Advice
This sautéed ground pork with hot pepper noodles offers a balanced combination of protein and vegetables. However, the oil-fried meat and thickening step can increase calorie content. For a lighter option, reduce the oil and use lean ground pork. Hot peppers and small chili peppers are rich in vitamin C and can help stimulate appetite, but those with sensitive stomachs should reduce the amount of chili.
