# Braised Beef

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket/beef short ribs 1000-1500g (about 2.5 catties, cut into large pieces)
🌶️ Dried chilies 40g (Xinjiang spicy leather chili or Erjingtiao, for making mashed chili)
🧅 Scallions 50g (cut diagonally into sections)
🫚 Ginger 30g (smashed flat)
🧄 Garlic 20g (smashed flat)
🫘 Pixian Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 55g (finely minced)
🌿 Bay leaves about 5 pieces
🌿 Cinnamon stick about 1g
🌿 Black cardamom 1 piece (use only the skin, remove the seeds)
🌿 Star anise 3g (2-3 pieces)
🌿 Sand ginger (Galangal) 3g (4 small pieces)
🌿 Cumin seeds 3g (a pinch)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 3g (a pinch)
🌿 Cilantro to taste (sprinkle before serving)
🫒 Cooking oil a generous amount (for stir-frying the base)
🍶 Light soy sauce 40g (about four tablespoons)
⚪ White pepper powder 2g (about one teaspoon)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g
🧂 MSG 3g
🍬 Rock sugar 13g (about two pieces, thumbnail-sized)
🧂 Salt to taste (add before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure cooker (electric or stovetop)
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🍲 Clay pot/soup pot (for final serving and heating)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the beef into pieces about two fingers wide. Place in a wok with cold water and add enough water to cover. Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring occasionally to allow impurities to rise. After boiling, turn off the heat, remove the beef, and rinse thoroughly with warm water. Drain and set aside.

2. Soak dried chilies in boiling water for 5 minutes until soft. Remove and rinse with cold water, squeeze out excess water, and finely chop.

3. Finely mince the Pixian Doubanjiang and mix evenly with the mashed chili. Cut scallions diagonally into sections, smash ginger and garlic flat. Measure all spices according to the recipe and set aside.

4. Pour a generous amount of cooking oil into a wok. Add scallion sections, ginger pieces, and garlic cloves to cold oil. Heat over high heat until the oil temperature rises, then reduce to medium heat once bubbles appear. Fry until the scallions, ginger, and garlic turn slightly yellow and release their fragrance. Turn to the lowest heat, add the mixed chili and bean paste, and stir-fry over low heat for about 2 minutes until red oil emerges and a rich aroma develops, eliminating the raw bean smell.

5. Add all the spices and continue to stir-fry evenly over low heat.

6. Add the blanched beef pieces. Stir-fry first to coat each piece evenly with the sauce, then turn to the highest heat and stir-fry for about 2 minutes to dry the surface moisture, allowing the beef to lightly stick to the wok.

7. Pour enough boiling water along the edge of the wok to cover. After it comes to a boil, transfer everything, including the liquid, into the pressure cooker inner pot.

8. Add additional boiling water as needed to just cover the beef. Add light soy sauce, white pepper powder, chicken bouillon, MSG, and rock sugar. Close the lid tightly. For an electric pressure cooker, select the "Beef/Mutton" mode. For a stovetop pressure cooker, bring to pressure over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for about 30 minutes.

9. After pressure cooking, first remove the beef. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh strainer to remove chili grounds and spice residues. Return the strained broth and beef to the pot, bring to a boil, and adjust saltiness with salt to taste. Do not reduce the broth too much; keep enough liquid to immerse the beef.
   **Note**: Stir-fry the sauce over low heat to prevent burning and bitterness. The water level in the pressure cooker should just cover the beef; too much water will dilute the flavor. Add salt at the end to adjust according to the broth's saltiness.

10. Transfer to a clay pot or serving bowl, sprinkle with cilantro, and serve.

## Health Tips
Beef is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for replenishing blood and building muscle. However, this braised preparation has a relatively high fat content, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. Pressure cooking can reduce fat oxidation. Those with weak digestion or on a weight-loss diet may eat less meat and enjoy more of the broth.
