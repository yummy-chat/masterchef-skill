# Farmhouse Bowl of Fragrance
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 6  
🥓 Pork belly appropriate amount (thinly sliced)  
🌶️ Green/pointed peppers appropriate amount (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic sprouts 2-3 stalks (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced or sliced)  
🌶️ Yellow tribute pepper appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro a large bunch (cut into sections)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder half tablespoon  
🧂 Salt a pinch  
🍬 Sugar a pinch  
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (for the sauce and for stir-frying pork)  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🥄 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount  
🌿 White pepper powder appropriate amount  
🍶 Huadiao wine/cooking wine appropriate amount (for the sauce and for stir-frying pork)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount (used throughout for frying eggs and stir-frying, key for flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan/iron pan  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Glass bowl (for beating eggs)  
🧴 Small glass bowl (for preparing sauce)  
🥄 Wooden spatula or metal spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Beat the eggs into a bowl and whisk until well mixed; finely chop green and pointed peppers, chop garlic sprouts, thinly slice pork belly, mince or slice garlic and ginger, chop yellow tribute pepper, cut cilantro into sections.
2. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, combine chicken bouillon powder, salt, sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, white pepper powder, and Huadiao wine. Stir until well mixed and set aside.
3. Pour sufficient rapeseed oil into the pan, heat over medium-high heat until hot. Pour in the egg mixture and fry until both sides are golden brown and set, ensuring the eggs are fully cooked.
4. Use a spatula to break the fried egg into large chunks. Set aside.
5. Add a little more rapeseed oil to the pan and heat. Add the pork belly slices, stir-fry over medium-high heat until they change color and edges are slightly crispy, rendering some fat, and bringing out the meaty aroma.
6. Add garlic, yellow tribute pepper, and ginger slices. Stir-fry until fragrant.
7. Splash a small amount of light soy sauce and cooking wine along the edge of the pan. Stir-fry evenly to color and flavor the pork and to release the "wok hei".
8. Add the chopped peppers. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until just cooked but still crisp.
9. Return the egg chunks to the pan. Stir-fry evenly, allowing the eggs to absorb the meaty and spicy flavors.
10. Pour in all the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to evenly coat the ingredients. A little sauce should remain in the pan. Work quickly to prevent burning.
11. Turn off the heat. Add the chopped garlic sprouts and cilantro sections. Stir-fry using residual heat until the garlic sprouts and cilantro are just cooked but still retain a crunchy texture. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish is primarily made with eggs and pork belly, rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle. However, those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. The chili peppers, ginger, and garlic have a warming effect on the stomach and help ward off cold. Those with weak stomachs are advised to eat it with rice to buffer the irritation.
