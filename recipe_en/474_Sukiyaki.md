# Sukiyaki  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Recommended Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Fatty Wagyu beef thin slices – appropriate amount  
🧅 Green onion/scallion – appropriate amount (some cut into sections for pan-frying, some grilled whole and seasoned)  
🧅 Onion – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥬 Napa cabbage – appropriate amount (cut into large pieces)  
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (sliced into rounds)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms – appropriate amount (score the surface with a cross)  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms – appropriate amount (trim roots)  
🍄 Shimeji/Bunashimeji mushrooms – appropriate amount (trim roots)  
🍄 King oyster mushrooms – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧈 Firm tofu – appropriate amount (cut into cubes)  
🫘 Shirataki konjac noodles – appropriate amount  
🥚 Raw pasteurized eggs – several (for dipping)  

💧 Mineral water – 720 ml  
🌿 Dried kombu (kelp) – 1 piece  
🐟 Katsuobushi (bonito flakes) – one handful  
🍶 Dark soy sauce – 320 ml  
🍶 Mirin – 300 ml  
🍬 Granulated sugar – 50 grams  
🧈 Beef fat (or butter) – about 15 grams  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🫕 Snow pot (or small saucepan)  
🍳 Cast iron skillet  
🕸️ Fine-mesh strainer  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. **Make dashi stock**:** Pour 720 ml of mineral water into a snow pot. Add the dried kombu and bring to a boil over heat.  
   **Note**:** Remove the kombu immediately once the water boils to prevent bitterness. Reduce the heat to very low. Place katsuobushi in a strainer, quickly swish it in the pot for a few seconds, then remove. This yields a clear dashi stock.  
2. **Prepare the sukiyaki sauce**:** Add dark soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and grilled scallion sections to the dashi stock. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam, then turn off the heat and set aside.  
3. **Pan-fry aromatics**:** Place beef fat in the cast iron skillet and melt over low heat. Add the sliced onion and raw scallion sections, and slowly fry until lightly browned and fragrant.  
4. **Sear the beef**:** Lay the beef slices flat over the seared scallions. Quickly cook both sides just until the color changes; do not cook through.  
5. **Arrange the side ingredients**:** Add the pan-fried firm tofu, king oyster mushrooms, enoki mushrooms, shimeji mushrooms, napa cabbage, carrot slices, shirataki noodles, and any other side ingredients.  
6. **Pour in sauce and simmer**:** Evenly pour the prepared sukiyaki sauce over the ingredients, filling to about 1/2 to 1/3 the height of the ingredients. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low heat. Simmer for about 3–4 minutes until all ingredients are cooked through and well flavored.  
7. **Dip and eat**:** When eating, lift out the cooked beef and vegetables and briefly dip them into beaten raw pasteurized egg liquid. This gives a smoother, more tender texture.  

## Health Advice  
This sukiyaki is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those controlling sugar and reducing fat. However, the mirin and sugar content is relatively high, so be mindful of the amount of sauce consumed. Dipping the beef in raw egg liquid enhances its smooth texture, but be sure to use pasteurized eggs for safety.
