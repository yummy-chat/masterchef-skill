# Mango Gummy Candy
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥭 Mango: 1 piece (yields about 100 grams of juice)
🍬 Rock sugar: 50 grams
🍮 Gelatin powder: 15 grams (used for setting)
🍯 Maltose syrup: 50 grams (prevents rock sugar from crystallizing)
🌾 Powdered sugar or cooked starch: as needed (for final coating to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🥄 Grater or blender (to process the fruit into a fine puree)
🫗 Strainer/sieve (to filter the puree and obtain smooth juice)
🍳 Small saucepan (with a long handle, used for boiling the syrup)
🥄 Silicone spatula/spatula (to stir the syrup and prevent burning)
💧 Dropper (to inject the syrup into molds)
🍮 Silicone molds (animal shapes)
🧊 Refrigerator (for freezing and setting)
🕸️ Drying rack (with ventilation from above and below, for drying the gummy candies)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing ingredients and coating)

## Steps
1. Peel the mango, and use a grater to grate it into a fine puree or use a blender to blend it into a paste, ensuring the fruit is fully crushed.
2. Pour the fruit puree into a strainer, and press it with a spoon to filter out fibers and impurities, yielding about 100 grams of smooth juice.
   **Key point**: Filtering ensures the final product has a smooth texture without any residue.
3. Pour the juice into a small saucepan, add rock sugar, gelatin powder, and maltose syrup. Stir evenly with a spatula to ensure all ingredients are fully mixed before starting the heat.
4. Turn on medium-low heat and boil, stirring continuously to prevent burning. After about 2 minutes, the syrup will come to a boil. Reduce to the lowest heat and continue simmering slowly. After about 8 minutes, lift the spatula; if the syrup drips slowly in a "reverse triangle" shape, it is ready.
   **Note**: Stirring continuously is essential; the "reverse triangle" shape is the key indicator that the syrup is done.
5. Remove from heat. Immediately use a dropper to inject the syrup into the silicone molds, ensuring the corners are fully filled.
   **Key point**: Filling the molds completely prevents incomplete shapes after unmolding.
6. Place the filled molds in the freezer section of the refrigerator and freeze for about 20 minutes, until fully set.
7. Remove the molds, and press the bottom to release the candies. Place the unmolded gummy candies on a drying rack with ventilation from above and below, and let them dry in a cool, ventilated area for at least 3 hours to remove excess moisture.
   **Key point**: The drying process significantly enhances the chewy texture of the gummy candies.
8. Place the dried gummy candies into a bowl filled with powdered sugar or cooked starch. Roll them to coat evenly with a thin layer, then shake off the excess powder.
   **Key point**: Coating gives the final product a more translucent appearance and prevents sticking.

## Health Tips
Mango is rich in vitamin C, making it a suitable snack to satisfy cravings. Be mindful of sugar intake; people with diabetes or those on a weight loss diet are advised to consume in reduced amounts.
