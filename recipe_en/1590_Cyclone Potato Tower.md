# Cyclone Potato Tower
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Potato Approximately 1 (medium-sized, peeled and washed)  
🌽 Corn Starch 500g  
🌽 Corn Flour 50g  
🌾 Bread Flour 220g  
🍮 Custard Powder 10g  
🍚 Rice Flour 100g  
🍞 Breadcrumbs As needed (1.6mm or 2mm size, avoid 3-5mm fried chicken breadcrumbs)  
💧 Water Powder-to-water ratio 1:1.5 (for batter)  
🍓 Taro/Apple/Strawberry Flavored Powder As needed (optional, for decoration)  
🧂 Baking Soda 5g  
🧂 Chicken Powder 8g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🫒 Cooking Oil Approximately 20 jin (10kg, for deep frying)  
🍅 Ketchup As needed (for decoration)  
🥗 Mayonnaise As needed (for decoration)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Digital Scale  
🥣 Mixing Bowl  
🥄 Hand Whisk or Mixing Spoon  
🕸️ Strainer  
🔩 Puncture Tool  
🍢 Bamboo Skewer (45cm)  
🌀 Potato Tower Machine (Spiral Cutter)  
📦 Rectangular Stainless Steel Box/Container  
🍳 Commercial Extended-Length Electric Deep Fryer  
🥢 Slotted Spoon or Tongs  
🧁 Piping Bag or Squeeze Bottle  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place corn starch, corn flour, bread flour, custard powder, and rice flour into a large bowl. Mix well.
2. Divide the mixed flour evenly into two portions: one portion as coating flour, the other for making the batter.
3. Add baking soda 5g, chicken powder 8g, and salt 5g to the batter-flour portion. Mix well.
4. Add water according to the powder-to-water ratio of 1:1.5, stirring continuously until smooth and lump-free. Then strain through a strainer for a fine and smooth batter.
5. Place the coating flour, batter, and breadcrumbs into separate rectangular stainless steel boxes for easy handling. Have flavored powder ready on the side if using.
6. Peel and wash the potato. Pierce completely through the center using a puncture tool, then insert a 45cm bamboo skewer. You may temporarily soak it in clean water to prevent oxidation (browning).
7. Secure the potato onto the potato tower machine. Turn the handle to cut into a spiral shape. After cutting, gently press down the potato slices while turning the skewer to spread them evenly.
8. Roll the potato tower in the coating flour. Use a spoon to sprinkle flour into all gaps, ensuring full coverage. Shake off excess dry flour.
9. Quickly dip the coated potato tower into the batter, coating it with a thin layer. Lift and let any excess batter drip off.
10. Roll the potato tower in the breadcrumbs. Use a spoon to sprinkle breadcrumbs into all gaps, ensuring full and even coverage.
11. Pour cooking oil into the electric deep fryer and heat to 180°C. Place the prepared potato tower into the oil and fry for 60–90 seconds. After about 40 seconds, the potato tower will float; turn it over to ensure even frying. Fry until golden brown and crispy, then remove.
    **Note**: If not using breadcrumbs, fry the batter-only potato tower directly. The frying time is about 20 seconds shorter than the breadcrumb version.
12. According to preference, squeeze ketchup or mayonnaise onto the fried cyclone potato tower, or sprinkle with flavored powder to make a colorful fruity cyclone potato tower.

## Health Advice
This cyclone potato tower is high in starch and oil. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables and balance calorie intake with exercise. Suitable for sharing at gatherings. Eating one piece to satisfy a craving is fine; overconsumption is not advisable.
