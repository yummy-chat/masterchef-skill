# Sweet and Sour Squirrel Fish
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Grass carp 1 (about 2 jin)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🫘 Green beans, a little (for garnish)  
🥃 Cooking wine, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌾 Starch, to taste (for coating the fish)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, to taste (for deep-frying the fish and making the sauce)  
🍅 Ketchup, to taste  
🍊 Orange juice, to taste  
🍶 White vinegar, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
💧 Water starch, to taste (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Basin  
🥄 Spoon  
🥘 Wok  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Serving plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Clean and prepare the grass carp. Cut off the head. Fillet both sides of the fish along the backbone, removing the bones. Split the tail in half from the center without cutting through, maintaining the whole shape.
2. Place one fillet in a basin. Add scallion, ginger, cooking wine, salt, and water. Soak for 10 minutes, then remove and drain.
3. Make vertical cuts into the thick part of the fish flesh down to but not through the skin. Then, make cross-hatch cuts to create a pinecone-like pattern on the fish flesh.
4. Evenly coat the scored fish fillet with dry starch, ensuring every "pinecone strand" is covered. Shake off any excess starch.
5. **Deep-frying**:
   - Pour ample oil into a wok and heat to around 350°F (180°C).
   - Hold the fish fillet by both ends and first immerse the center, scored part into the oil to set the shape. Then, slowly lower the entire fillet into the oil.
   - Over medium heat, fry for about 1 minute until the surface is golden and crispy, and the fish is cooked through. Remove and drain on paper towels.
6. **Making the sweet and sour sauce**:
   - Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add ketchup, orange juice, white vinegar, and white sugar. Cook over low heat, stirring to combine.
   - Add a small amount of water and bring to a boil. Drizzle in the water starch to thicken the sauce, stirring constantly until the sauce is glossy and thick.
7. Arrange the fried "squirrel fish" on a serving plate. Pour the hot sweet and sour sauce over it. Garnish with green beans and serve immediately.
   **Key points**: When deep-frying the fish, be sure to first set the shape of the scored center section to ensure the "flowers" bloom. Pour the sweet and sour sauce over the fish while it is still hot to maintain the crispy exterior and tender interior.

## Health Advice  
This sweet and sour squirrel fish is high in sugar and oil, suitable for an occasional treat. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume it sparingly and pair it with a serving of stir-fried vegetables for a more balanced meal. When deep-frying the fish, coat it evenly with starch and be careful to avoid oil splatters.
