## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the chicken feet, use scissors to trim all nails, and drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Trimming nails improves hygiene and texture.
2. Place chicken feet in cold water, add scallion knots, ginger slices, and one tablespoon of malt sugar. Bring to a boil, skim off foam, continue boiling for two minutes, then remove and drain completely.
   **Key point**: Malt sugar is used for coloring; use sparingly. Ensure feet are thoroughly drained after removal to prevent oil splatter when frying.
3. Heat the wok, pour in oil to coat, then discard. Add cold oil again, place drained chicken feet in the wok, immediately cover with a lid, and heat over high heat.
   **Key point**: Adding cold oil and quickly covering prevents hot oil splatter injuries.
4. When the popping sound in the wok subsides, reduce to medium heat and continue frying for about 3 minutes, until the chicken feet surface turns golden, blistered, and becomes hard like dried.
5. Place fried chicken feet in a pressure cooker, add one bowl of water, scallions, ginger, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, pour in yellow wine and light soy sauce, add three tablespoons of salt and one tablespoon of white sugar. Cover the lid, bring to pressure, then reduce to low heat and cook for 7 minutes. Stew until the chicken feet puff up and tiger skin wrinkles are evident.
6. In another wok, heat a little lard over high heat, sauté minced ginger and garlic until fragrant, add one tablespoon of spicy bean paste and stir until red oil appears, then add diced green and red chili peppers and stir-fry.
7. Transfer the stewed chicken feet to the wok, add about two tablespoons of the stewing broth, MSG, white pepper powder, adjust color with dark soy sauce, and add a little aged vinegar for flavor. Heat over medium heat, allowing the sauce to reduce while stewing.
   **Key point**: The broth is already salty, so no additional salt is needed.
8. When the sauce thickens, pour in water starch to coat the chicken feet evenly, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve.

## Health Advice
Tiger skin chicken feet are rich in collagen, suitable for those needing to improve skin elasticity, but the frying step increases calories, so it is recommended to eat 6-8 pieces per serving. Due to high salt and purine content, gout patients should be cautious, and it is best to pair with vegetables for a balanced diet.
