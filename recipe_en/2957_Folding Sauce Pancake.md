# Folding Sauce Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Flour, as needed
💧 Warm water, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for kneading, resting, and frying)
🧅 Onion, as needed (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (chopped into green onion pieces)
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🥫 Sauce, as needed (as shown on screen, such as a mix of bean paste, sweet bean sauce, etc.)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍳 Wok
🥖 Rolling pin
🥄 Flat frying spatula
🔪 Wooden cutting board
🧴 Oil spray tool
🥣 Metal serving bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix flour and salt evenly, gradually add warm water, stir until flaky, then add cooking oil, and knead into a smooth dough.
   **Key point**: Add warm water gradually to avoid making the dough too wet.
2. Divide the dough into small pieces, spray oil evenly on the surface, and let rest for 1 hour to relax the gluten.
   **Key point**: Spraying oil prevents the dough surface from drying out during resting.
3. Roll out the dough piece: Spray oil on the cutting board, roll the rested dough extremely thin, then use the rolling pin to roll up the thin dough sheet, forming a pleated dough piece.
   **Condition check**: Roll the dough sheet until nearly transparent.
   **Key point**: Spraying oil on the board and dough effectively prevents sticking; the thinner the dough sheet, the better the layers in the final product.
4. Prepare the sauce: Heat oil in a wok, add diced onion and minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant, then add the mixed sauce and stir-fry evenly to make the special sauce.
   **Key point**: Ensure the base ingredients are fully stir-fried before adding the sauce to avoid burning.
5. Fry the dough piece: Heat oil in a pan, place the rolled dough pieces into the pan one section at a time, fry each side for 1-2 minutes, then flip, and fry until both sides are golden and cooked through.
   **Key point**: Add the dough pieces one at a time to prevent sticking; flip only after one side is set to maintain shape.
6. Brush sauce and garnish: While the fried dough pieces are still hot, brush with the prepared sauce, sprinkle with chopped scallion and white sesame seeds, then cut and plate.
   **Key point**: Brush the sauce while hot to allow the flavor to better penetrate.

## Health Advice
This folding sauce pancake features paper-thin dough and rich sauce flavor, but its calorie content is not to be underestimated—recommended as a breakfast or lunch staple, paired with a light vegetable salad to balance the oil. Be mindful of the sauce amount to avoid excessive sodium intake; diabetics can reduce the sweet bean sauce.
