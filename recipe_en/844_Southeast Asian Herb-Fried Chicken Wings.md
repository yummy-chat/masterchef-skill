# Southeast Asian Herb-Fried Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken wings, as needed (drumettes and mid-joint wings)
🌿 Lemongrass, as needed (cut into sections)
🍃 Kaffir lime leaves, as needed
🍋 Limes, 2-3 (for juicing)
🥗 Mixed salad greens, as needed (for plating)
🍑 Peach, 1 (for plating)
🍊 Apricot, 1 (for plating)
🧂 Fish sauce, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🍬 Sugar (or sugar substitute), as needed
🟡 Curry powder, as needed

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥢 Chopsticks
🫙 Plastic wrap
🧻 Aluminum foil
🍽️ Serving plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the lemongrass into sections, roughly chop the kaffir lime leaves, and cut the limes into wedges. Set aside.
2. Place the chicken wings in a bowl, add fish sauce, salt, sugar, curry powder, lemongrass sections, kaffir lime leaves, and some of the lime juice. Mix thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Marinate for 20-30 minutes to allow the herbs to fully infuse.
3. Do not add oil to the non-stick frying pan. Place the marinated chicken wings directly into the pan along with the lemongrass sections and kaffir lime leaves from the marinade.
   **Key point**: The chicken skin will release its own fat as it heats.
4. Cook over low heat throughout. Fry one side for about 4-5 minutes until golden, then flip. Cover with aluminum foil and steam-fry until the inside is fully cooked.
   **Doneness check**: Both sides are golden brown, and the inside is fully cooked.
5. Just before removing from heat, squeeze in the remaining lime juice. Quickly stir-fry to release the sour aroma using the heat of the pan.
6. Plate the fried chicken wings, and arrange mixed salad greens along with sliced peach and apricot pieces as side garnishes. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Peeling the skin off the chicken wings before frying can reduce saturated fat. Pairing with lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves aids digestion, making this suitable for fitness and weight loss diets. Note that fish sauce is high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt used for seasoning.
