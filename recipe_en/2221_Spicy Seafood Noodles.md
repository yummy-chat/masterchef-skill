## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the seafood thoroughly. Mince the garlic, slice the ginger, cut the red chili pepper into rings, slice the scallions, and separate the white and green parts.
2. Fill the single-handle pot with water, bring to a boil, and set aside.
3. Heat the frying pan over high heat. Add oil, then put in the minced garlic, ginger slices, red chili pepper, and scallion whites. Stir-fry until fragrant.
4. Add the seafood and spread it evenly. Pan-fry over medium heat briefly, then add salt, white pepper powder, chicken bouillon powder, sweet paprika, and tomato paste. Drizzle in the fish sauce. Stir-fry evenly until the seafood is well-coated in the sauce and about 80–90% cooked.  
   **Note**: Maintain medium heat to avoid overcooking the seafood.
5. Turn the heat to the lowest setting. Scoop 2–3 ladlefuls of boiling water from the pot and add to the seafood pan. Cover and simmer for 2 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: Use the lowest heat and residual steam to gently finish cooking the seafood, keeping it tender.
6. While the seafood simmers, add the instant noodle block to the boiling water pot. Cook until softened, then drain and transfer to the deep bowl. Be careful not to overcook the noodles.
7. Check that the seafood is fully cooked. Sprinkle in the scallion greens and turn off the heat. Pour the seafood and its sauce directly over the noodles. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Spicy seafood noodles are rich in high-quality protein and antioxidants (from garlic and chili), making them suitable for those needing energy replenishment or seafood lovers. However, note that instant noodles are high in sodium; it is recommended to reduce the seasoning packet or substitute with a low-sodium broth.
