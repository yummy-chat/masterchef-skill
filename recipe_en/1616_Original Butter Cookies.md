# Original Butter Cookies
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Fermented butter 500g (recommended AOP产区 Isigny, Elle & Vire, or President)  
🍬 Powdered sugar 166g  
🧂 Sea salt 7g  
🥛 Heavy cream 104g  
🍫 White chocolate 21g  
🌾 Bread flour 213g  
🌾 Cake flour 249g  
🥔 Potato starch 104g  
🥛 Milk powder 42g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔥 Oven (with proofing function)  
🤖 Stand mixer or hand mixer  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🤲 Spatula  
🥄 Measuring cup/spoon  
👝 Piping bag  
⭐ 8-pointed star piping tip  
📜 Silicone mat or parchment paper  
🍪 Baking sheet  
⚡ Microwave  
📏 Bench scraper  

## Steps  
**Preparation Stage**

1. **Butter softening**
   Slice the refrigerated fermented butter into thin pieces, place in a mixing bowl, add powdered sugar, and place in the oven with the proofing function on low temperature until the butter can be easily pressed with a finger to leave an indent.
   **Key point**: The butter must be fully softened, not melted into liquid, and not hard and cold. This is the decisive factor for smooth piping later.

2. **Liquid ingredient preparation**
   Pour heavy cream, white chocolate pieces, and sea salt into a measuring cup. Heat in the microwave, stirring until the chocolate is fully melted and the ingredients are well combined into a smooth liquid. Then keep it warm, maintaining a temperature of around 35-40°C.
   **Key point**: The liquid ingredients must be kept warm before adding to the whipped butter to prevent the butter from solidifying upon contact.

3. **Flour mixture**
   Mix bread flour, cake flour, potato starch, and milk powder evenly.
   **Key point**: The dry ingredients need to be mixed in advance to ensure even distribution when combining with the buttercream later.

**Production Stage**

4. **Whip the butter**
   Place the softened butter and powdered sugar in a stand mixer. Mix on low speed first, then switch to high speed and whip until the mixture becomes voluminous, pale in color, smooth in texture, and light and fluffy like feathers.

5. **Add the liquid**
   Slowly pour the warm liquid ingredients into the whipped butter in 2-3 portions. After each addition, mix on low or medium speed until the liquid is fully absorbed and the mixture is smooth, then add the next portion.
   **Key point**: The liquid must be added in portions to ensure thorough emulsification and avoid separation. Continuous mixing helps the liquid and butter blend better.

6. **Combine the batter**
   Sift or add the mixed dry ingredients into the butter mixture in 2-3 portions. Mix on low speed first until no visible dry flour remains, then switch to high speed for about 10 seconds to make the batter uniform.
   **Texture check**: The final batter should be very smooth and delicate. When touched with a finger, it should be slightly sticky, able to form a dough but not easily lifted from the bowl as a whole. If the batter is not sticky at all and too firm, piping will be very difficult.
   **Key point**: Do not add all the dry ingredients at once so that you can observe and control the final batter consistency. Overmixing will make the batter stiff and affect the texture.

7. **Pipe the cookies**
   Fill a piping bag fitted with an 8-pointed star tip with the batter. Pipe even cookie shapes onto a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat or parchment paper.
   **Key point**: When the batter is at the right consistency, piping should be relatively smooth. If the batter is too dry, it will be hard to pipe; if too wet, the patterns will disappear.

8. **Baking**
   Place the baking sheet in the middle of a preheated oven and bake with variable temperature: bake at 150°C for 10 minutes to set the cookies, then reduce to 130°C and bake for another 10 minutes to cook through and make them crispy.
   **Key point**: Baking temperature and time need to be adjusted according to your own oven's performance and the size/thickness of the cookies. The cookies will be soft when taken out of the oven and will harden and become crisp upon cooling.

9. **Cooling and storage**
   Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for a moment, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, store in an airtight container or box.

## Health Advice
These butter cookies have a crisp and tender texture but are high in fat and calories. It is recommended to consume no more than 3-4 pieces per serving, paired with unsweetened tea for balance. Suitable for occasional indulgence; diabetics and those on a weight loss plan should moderate their intake.
