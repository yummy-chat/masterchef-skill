# Seafood Feast
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Large Head Prawns 500g (cut off antennae and rostrum, butterfly-cut to remove vein, then halve lengthwise)  
🦀 Swimming Crabs 4 pieces (remove mouth, gills, and sand sac, then halve crosswise)  
🐚 Abalone 3 pieces (scrub the shell, remove innards)  
🦪 Oysters about 5 pieces (scrub the shells)  
🐚 Clams about 200g (wash clean and drain)  
🐟 Sea Bass 1 piece (cut into chunks and arrange on a plate, marinate with scallion and ginger to remove fishiness)  
🧅 Onion 1 piece (slice into strips to line the bottom)  
🍄 Enoki Mushrooms 1 pack (trim the root and separate into strands)  
🌱 Bean Sprouts appropriate amount (wash clean and line the bottom)  
🧄 Ginger, Scallion, Garlic appropriate amount (slice/mince, used for removing fishiness, stir-frying base, and splashing oil)  
🌶️ Red Chili Pepper appropriate amount (cut into rings for garnish)  
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (garnish)  
🍋 Lemon appropriate amount (slice for garnish)  

🫘 Broad Bean Paste 1 tablespoon (stir-fry for red oil, base flavor)  
🫗 Steamed Fish Soy Sauce appropriate amount (enhance freshness and saltiness)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🍬 White Sugar appropriate amount (enhance freshness and balance saltiness)  
🧂 Chicken Bouillon appropriate amount (boost umami)  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) appropriate amount (remove fishiness and enhance aroma)  
🌶️ Ground White Pepper appropriate amount  
🌿 Cumin Powder appropriate amount (sprinkle over seafood surface)  
🌶️ Chili Powder appropriate amount (add layers of spiciness)  
🤍 White Sesame Seeds appropriate amount (add aroma)  
🫒 Cooking Oil sufficient amount (use in batches: stir-frying base, stir-frying sauce, and splashing oil)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s Knife, Cutting Board  
🪥 Brush (for scrubbing abalone)  
✂️ Scissors (for handling hard crab and prawn shells)  
🍳 Multi-Layer Steamer (for steaming fish, prawns, and crabs)  
🥘 Wok or Soup Pot (for boiling oysters and clams, stir-frying sauce, and heating oil)  
🫕 Large Baking Tray (for holding base vegetables and seafood)  
🥄 Slotted Spoon (for scooping out clams and oysters, and removing fried solids)  
🔥 Induction Cooktop or Ceramic Stove (for heating the baking tray with base vegetables)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash all ingredients clean. Scrub abalone shells repeatedly with a brush, remove innards. Cut off prawn antennae and rostrum, butterfly-cut to remove vein, then halve lengthwise. Pry open crab shells, remove mouth, gills, and sand sac, then halve crosswise. Cut sea bass into chunks and arrange on a plate, add sliced ginger and scallion to marinate and remove fishiness. Scrub oyster shells. Wash clams clean and drain. Slice onion into strips. Trim enoki mushroom roots and separate into strands. Wash bean sprouts. Layer all these items in sequence at the bottom of the baking tray.  
2. Fill the steamer with water and bring to a rolling boil. Once steaming, place the sea bass, large head prawns, and swimming crabs inside, and steam for 6 minutes. Meanwhile, place the baking tray with base vegetables on the induction cooktop and begin heating.  
   **Key Point**: Do not discard the seafood broth produced during steaming; add it later when stir-frying the sauce to greatly enhance umami.  
3. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil, add the oysters, stir with a spoon, cook for 30 seconds, then turn off the heat and let them soak for 3 minutes before removing.  
   **Key Point**: This method makes it easy to open the shells while keeping the meat tender and not overcooked.  
4. Place clams in cold water in a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook until all shells are fully open, then immediately turn off the heat and remove with a slotted spoon.  
   **Key Point**: Avoid overcooking to maintain a tender and juicy texture.  
5. Arrange the steamed fish, prawns, and crabs, along with the boiled oysters and clams, neatly on top of the base vegetables in the baking tray.  
6. Evenly sprinkle minced garlic, red chili pepper rings, cumin powder, chili powder, and white sesame seeds over the surface of the seafood.  
7. Heat oil in a wok. Add 1 tablespoon of broad bean paste and stir-fry over medium-low heat until red oil releases. Add minced ginger, scallion, and garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in an appropriate amount of boiling water and the reserved broth from steaming the seafood. Add steamed fish soy sauce, salt, white sugar, chicken bouillon, baijiu, and ground white pepper. Stir well and bring to a boil. Pour this sauce evenly over the seafood.  
8. In a separate wok, add a generous amount of cooking oil. Add sliced ginger and fry until fragrant, then add scallion segments and fry until both are golden brown. Add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns and fry until deep red. Use a slotted spoon to remove all fried solids completely. Heat the oil again until it starts to smoke.  
9. Use a ladle to evenly pour the hot oil over the surface of the seafood, releasing the aroma of all toppings and the seafood itself.  
10. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and cilantro, and arrange lemon slices for garnish. The dish is ready to be served.  

## Health Advice  
This seafood feast is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for fitness or when replenishing energy. Be mindful of total calorie intake, and pair with vegetables for a more balanced meal. Individuals with gout or high uric acid levels should consume in moderation.
