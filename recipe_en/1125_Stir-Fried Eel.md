# Stir-Fried Eel

Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Yellow eel 1-2 (slaughtered, cut into sections, rinsed and drained)  
🥒 Cucumber 1/2 (peeled and seeded, sliced diagonally)  
🌿 Perilla 1 handful (use only the leaves, shredded)  
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers 5-6 (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🧅 Garlic, to taste (crushed)  
🫑 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections, for finishing)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, generous amount (must be used)  
🧂 Chicken powder, about 1/2 spoon  
🍬 White sugar, a pinch  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 spoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 1 spoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 spoon  
🌶️ Chopped chili sauce, to taste  
🍾 Cooking wine, 1 spoon  
🥃 Baijiu (white liquor), to taste  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🔗 Wok lid  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Seed the cucumber and slice diagonally. Take the perilla leaves, roll them up, and shred finely. Cut the green and red chili peppers into sections. Slaughter the eel, cut into sections, and drain using a strainer.  
**Key point**: The essence of stir-frying eel raw is to not blanch it; stir-fry directly over high heat.  

2. Place the wok over high heat until hot. Pour in a generous amount of rapeseed oil and heat until the oil temperature rises.  
3. Add the drained eel sections and stir-fry over high heat the entire time until the eel flesh tightens and the surface becomes charred and fragrant.  
**Note**: Never blanch the eel for raw stir-frying to maintain its tender texture.  

4. Add ginger slices and crushed garlic cloves, stir-frying quickly until fragrant.  
5. Add wild pepper, pickled pepper, chicken powder, white sugar, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce in sequence. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to evenly coat the eel sections with the sauce.  
6. Add chopped chili sauce, green and red chili pepper sections, cucumber sections, and perilla shreds. Stir-fry thoroughly over high heat.  
7. Drizzle 1 spoon of cooking wine along the edge of the wok, stir briefly, then sprinkle in scallions. Evenly drizzle an appropriate amount of baijiu and immediately cover with the wok lid.  
**Note**: Baijiu may ignite when exposed to high heat; operate with caution. The simmering allows the liquor aroma to infuse.  

8. Reduce to medium heat and simmer for about 3 minutes until the sauce thickens and the fishy smell is eliminated. If the sauce is too runny after uncovering, increase the heat to reduce it. Stir well and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Yellow eel is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with perilla and chili peppers, it helps dispel cold and dampness, making it suitable for summer when appetite is poor. Ensure the oil temperature is sufficiently high during cooking to prevent the eel meat from releasing water and affecting the texture.
