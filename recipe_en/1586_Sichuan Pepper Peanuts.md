# Sichuan Pepper Peanuts
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts in shell 500g
🌶️ Dried chili peppers a handful (shredded into strips)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns to taste
🌶️ Chili powder 2 tbsp
🌿 Sichuan pepper powder 2 tbsp
🧂 Salt 1 tbsp
🍬 Sugar 1 tbsp

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🧻 Kitchen paper
❄️ Refrigerator
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board and scissors
🍽️ Serving container (baking tray, plate, etc.)

## Instructions
1. Soak the peanuts in shell in water for 10 minutes, then peel off the red skin by hand to obtain shelled peanuts.
**Key point**: Peeling each peanut by hand works best; rubbing in bulk will not remove the skin easily.

2. Use kitchen paper to dry the surface of the peanuts, ensuring no raw water remains.

3. Freeze the peanuts in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours.
**Key point**: Freezing makes the fried peanuts crispier and prevents them from sticking together when added to the oil.

4. Pour enough oil into the wok over high heat, but add the frozen peanuts while the oil is still cold.
**Key point**: Adding to cold oil is crucial to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains raw.

5. Once the oil heats up, reduce to medium-low heat and continuously stir the peanuts.
**Key point**: Stir frequently to avoid burning the bottom.

6. When the peanuts turn slightly yellow, immediately remove them with a slotted spoon and spread them flat to cool.
**Key point**: Residual heat will deepen the color; do not wait until they are dark yellow, or they will become bitter.

7. After cooling, break each peanut in half by hand for better flavor absorption.
8. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili strips over high heat, stir-fry until fragrant, then immediately reduce to low heat.
**Key point**: Stir-fry quickly over high heat just until fragrant to avoid burning.

9. Add the halved peanuts, stir-fry evenly, then add chili powder, Sichuan pepper powder, salt, and sugar.
**Key point**: Keep the heat low throughout to ensure the seasonings coat the peanuts evenly.

10. Once evenly mixed, remove from heat immediately and spread on a flat plate to cool completely; the peanuts will become crispy and spicy once cooled.

## Health Advice
This Sichuan pepper peanut dish is high in oil and salt, suitable for occasional indulgence. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should consume in moderation. Freezing before frying enhances crispiness, but be mindful of portion size. Avoid rubbing your eyes after eating to prevent irritation.
