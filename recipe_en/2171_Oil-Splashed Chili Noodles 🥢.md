# Oil-Splashed Chili Noodles 🥢  
**Cuisine:** Shaanxi Cuisine  
**Cooking Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour: 400g  
💧 Room-temperature water: 185g  
🧂 Salt: 4g (for dough)  
🥩 Pork neck meat: 250g (or beef/lamb/chicken breast, thinly sliced)  
🧅 Purple onion: 1 large, cut into chunks  
🫑 Green bell pepper: 1 medium, julienned  
🌶️ Red bell pepper: 1 medium, julienned  
🍅 Tomatoes: 2 medium-small, diced  
🌿 Scallions: 2, chopped  
🧄 Garlic: 4–5 cloves, smashed and minced  
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili: to taste (optional)  
🫒 Cooking oil: as needed (for marinating meat)  
🧂 Light soy sauce: 1 tbsp (for marinade) + 2 tbsp (for stir-fry)  
🍶 Cooking wine: 1 tbsp  
🤍 White pepper powder: ½ tsp  
✨ Sichuan pepper powder: 2 pinches (for marinade) + ½ tsp (for stir-fry)  
🫗 Premium soy sauce (Weijixian): 1 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce: 1 tbsp  
🟤 Cumin powder: ½ tsp  
🍬 Sugar: ½ tsp  
🫗 Dark soy sauce: ½ tsp (for color)  
🐮 Beef bouillon powder: 2 pinches  
🐔 Chicken bouillon powder: 2 pinches  
🧂 Salt: to taste (adjust at the end)  
🌶️ Oil-splashed chili: to taste (for mixing with noodles)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🍲 Pot for boiling  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Measuring spoons  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the dough:** Combine flour, water, and salt in a bowl. Use chopsticks to stir into a shaggy mass, then knead by hand into a firm, non-sticky dough.  
   *Key point:* The dough will be quite firm initially—this is normal. Do not over-knead.  
2. **First rest:** Place the dough back in the bowl, cover with a lid or damp cloth, and rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.  
   *Key point:* Hydration will soften the dough; only a few gentle kneads are needed.  
3. **Knead:** After resting, gently knead in the bowl until the dough softens. Fold it over a few times using the heel of your hand until the surface is smooth.  
   *Key point:* Rely on hydration—gentle handling is enough.  
4. **Second rest:** Return the dough to the bowl, cover, and rest at room temperature for another 15 minutes.  
5. **Cut into pieces:** Transfer the rested dough to a cutting board, fold twice, divide into two portions, and roll each into a long log. Slice diagonally into evenly sized pieces.  
6. **Soak the pieces:** Place all cut dough pieces into cold water and let them sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.  
   *Key point:* The pieces must be fully soaked and rested, otherwise they will tear when pulled.  
7. **Prepare the toppings:**  
   - Marinate the meat with light soy sauce, cooking wine, white pepper powder, and Sichuan pepper powder. Drizzle a little cooking oil and mix well.  
   - Chop vegetables as specified above.  
8. **Cook the noodles:** Bring a pot of water to a boil. Once boiling, turn to high heat. Take a soaked dough piece, press it flat in the center, and gently pull it outward from both ends into a long, evenly thick strip. Drop into the boiling water. After adding all strips, reduce to medium heat and cook for about 1½ minutes.  
   - Noodles are done when they float and have no white, opaque core. If the pot threatens to boil over, add a splash of cold water.  
   - Drain and rinse under cold water to prevent sticking.  
9. **Sear the meat:** Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated meat and stir-fry until it changes color.  
10. **Sauté aromatics:** Add chopped scallions and minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant.  
11. **Cook the onion:** Add onion chunks and stir-fry over medium heat until softened and translucent.  
12. **Season:** Reduce heat to lowest. Add light soy sauce, premium soy sauce, oyster sauce, Sichuan pepper powder, cumin powder, sugar, dark soy sauce, beef bouillon powder, and chicken bouillon powder. Stir quickly.  
13. **Bloom the spices:** Return to medium heat and stir-fry evenly until everything is well combined.  
14. **Cook the tomatoes:** Add diced tomatoes and stir-fry until they break down into a thick sauce.  
15. **Add bell peppers:** Toss in the julienned green and red bell peppers, and stir-fry briefly until just tender-crisp.  
16. **Combine:** Add the cooled noodles to the wok. Increase heat to high and toss quickly, letting the sauce reduce and coat the noodles evenly.  
    *Key point:* Stir-fry quickly and evenly to avoid sticking.  
17. **Serve:** Once the sauce has thickened to your liking, transfer to a plate. Serve with a generous spoonful of oil-splashed chili and mix well.  

## Health Tips  
This noodle dish is balanced with plenty of vegetables and lean meat, providing protein and fiber—ideal for an energizing lunch. Be mindful of the amount of oil-splashed chili and noodles to keep calories in check.
