# Curry Chicken Thigh Rice
Cuisine: Common home-cooked dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥕 Carrot 1 piece (cut into chunks)
🥔 Potato 2 pieces (cut into chunks)
🍠 Sweet potato 2 pieces (cut into chunks)
🧅 Onion 1 piece (cut into large chunks)
🍗 Boneless chicken thigh meat appropriate amount (about 4 servings, cut into small pieces, can substitute with chicken breast but texture will be drier)
🧂 Light soy sauce about 2 tablespoons
🍅 Ketchup about 1 tablespoon
🥛 Milk about 4 tablespoons (can substitute with milk powder and water)
💧 Water about 2 tablespoons
🍛 Curry block appropriate amount (refer to package instructions for quantity)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add to taste before removing from heat)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board
🥄 Metal spoon (for measuring seasonings)
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍳 Glass lid
🥄 Metal ladle
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Work
1. Wash and peel the carrot, potato, sweet potato, and onion. Cut the onion into large chunks. For the carrot, potato, and sweet potato, first slice thickly, then cut into thick strips, and finally into small cubes.
   **Note**: Cutting the onion into large chunks helps avoid excessive spiciness and tears; cutting root vegetables into small cubes allows them to cook through quickly.
2. Wash the boneless chicken thigh meat, remove excess skin, and cut into small pieces.
   **Note**: Chicken thighs are more tender and smooth; if using chicken breast as a substitute, the texture will be drier.

### Cooking Steps
1. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the pan and heat over medium heat. Add the onion chunks and stir-fry until the onion softens and becomes fragrant.
   **Key**: Ensure the onion surface is dry to avoid oil splatter when added to hot oil.
2. Add the chicken thigh pieces to the pan and stir-fry over medium heat until the chicken is fully cooked and changes color.
3. Add about 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce and about 1 tablespoon of ketchup to the pan. Continue cooking over medium heat; no need to stir evenly.
   **Note**: This step highlights the Chinese-style curry approach, distinguishing it from Japanese or Thai styles.
4. Add all the prepared vegetable chunks into the pan and mix well. Then add about 4 tablespoons of milk and about 2 tablespoons of water, keeping the heat on medium.
   **Note**: Liquid can be substituted with milk powder and water; add water to compensate for the sweet potato’s absorbency.
5. Place the curry block into the pan. Use the spatula to press it underneath the ingredients, cover with the lid, and simmer over low heat for 30 minutes.
   **Key**: Use the amount of curry block as directed on the package; pressing it under the ingredients helps it melt fully.
6. Remove the lid. Use the spatula to partially mash the softened potato and sweet potato chunks, naturally thickening the sauce. Taste and adjust saltiness as needed, keeping the heat low.
   **Key**: Mashing some of the root vegetables helps thicken the sauce and enhances flavor integration.
7. Turn off the heat. Serve the cooked curry chicken thighs along with the sauce over rice.
   **Tip**: This dish can be made in larger batches and frozen; simply reheat when ready to eat. It’s great with rice or noodles.

## Health Suggestions
This curry chicken thigh rice uses potato and sweet potato to naturally thicken the dish, reducing the need for extra oil, making it suitable for the whole family to share. Note that curry blocks tend to be high in sodium; those with high blood pressure should reduce salt accordingly. Moderate consumption provides carbohydrates and protein.
