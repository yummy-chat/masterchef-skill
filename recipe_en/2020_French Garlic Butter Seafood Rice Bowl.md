# French Garlic Butter Seafood Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Year-round meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Salmon (skinned and cut into pieces)  
🦐 Black tiger shrimp (peeled, deveined, butterflied, tail left on)  
🦪 Mussels (beards removed, cleaned)  
🍚 Rice (rinsed)  
🧅 Scallion whites (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic (minced)  
🍷 White wine  
🧈 Butter  
🫒 Olive oil  
💧 Water  
🧂 Salt  
🌶️ Black pepper  
🥛 Heavy cream (about 100 ml)  
🌿 Thyme (1 sprig)  
🌿 Parsley (chopped)  
🌶️ Paprika  
🫕 Mussel broth (filtered liquid from cooking mussels)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥘 Stockpot  
🥄 Strainer  
🧪 Measuring cup  
🔥 Oven  
🧤 Oven mitts  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls, basins  
🍽️ Plates  

## Steps
1. Skin and cut the salmon into pieces. Peel, devein, and butterfly the black tiger shrimp, keeping the tail on. Trim the beards from the mussels and rinse clean. Chop the scallion whites and garlic, and chop the parsley. Set aside.
   **Note**: Butterflying the shrimp allows for more even flavor absorption and a better appearance.
2. Heat the stockpot over high heat. Add the mussels and a suitable amount of white wine. Cover and cook until the shells open, then immediately remove the mussels. Strain the liquid in the pot through a strainer into a measuring cup to make the mussel broth.
   **Key**: The broth from cooking the mussels is extremely flavorful and crucial to the final taste. Do not discard.
3. In another pot, melt a suitable amount of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped scallion and cook until fragrant. Add the rinsed rice and stir-fry briefly. Add water and the mussel broth, with the total liquid being approximately 1.6 times the weight of the rice. Bring to a boil, then seal the pot tightly with aluminum foil. Place in a preheated oven at 170°C and bake for 17 minutes.
4. Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan. Add the salmon pieces and sear until golden brown on the surface, then remove. Using the remaining oil in the pan, cook the shrimp until they change color. Season with salt and black pepper, then add the minced garlic and stir until fragrant.
   **Note**: Do not cook the seafood for too long to maintain tenderness.
5. Pour an appropriate amount of the filtered mussel broth into the pan. Add about 100 ml of heavy cream. Return the seared salmon pieces to the pan, add a sprig of thyme, and simmer over low heat to let the sauce meld. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in the chopped parsley and paprika, and stir to combine.
6. Once the rice is baked, remove it from the oven. While hot, add a small piece of butter and toss to combine. Fluff the rice and divide it onto plates. Ladle the garlic butter seafood cream sauce over the rice. Arrange the cooked mussels on top. Finish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley and paprika for garnish.

## Health Advice
This garlic butter seafood rice bowl is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for muscle building or mental work. However, the cream and butter contain considerable calories, so pairing it with a portion of sautéed vegetables is recommended. Ensure seafood is fresh; cook mussels only until the shells open, as prolonged cooking will make them tough.
