# Braised Pork Ribs with Green Beans
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork spare ribs (including neck bones) 3 jin (choose deep red meat with more fat)
🫘 Oil green beans 1 jin (choose tender beans with small pods and tiny beans)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections, used for blanching, stir-frying, and adding aroma)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced, used for blanching and stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (a large amount, minced, added before turning off the heat for aroma)
⭐ Star anise, 1-2 pieces
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small pinch
🍶 Yellow wine, as needed (for blanching to remove gamey flavor; can be omitted if ribs are fresh)
🧂 Salt, about 9 grams (add all at once in advance)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, as needed (optional)
🥄 Light soy sauce, about 20 grams
🥄 Dark soy sauce, about 9-18 grams (for color)
🫒 Cooking oil, a large amount (ample oil needed for stir-frying green beans; also use more oil for stir-frying ribs)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or nonstick pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Spatula (wooden or silicone)
🫙 Lid
🥣 Bowl or basin

## Steps
1. Cut the pork spare ribs into small pieces and wash them. If using the raw stir-fry method, soak in cold water for 5-6 hours until the blood color fades, then drain and squeeze out excess water.
2. Remove the ends and tough strings from the oil green beans. Wash them and make sure to drain thoroughly to prevent oil splatter when adding to the pan.
3. Prepare the aromatics: scallion sections, ginger slices, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns.
4. If not using the raw stir-fry method, blanch the ribs: place the ribs in a pot with cold water, add scallions, ginger, and yellow wine. When the water temperature reaches 60-80°C, stir continuously with a slotted spoon so the ribs bump into each other and release more blood foam. Just before the water boils, reduce the heat to low and let them soak briefly, then remove the ribs.
**Note**: Blanching in warm water removes blood foam more effectively while retaining the meat's aroma; after blanching, do not rinse with cold water—use warm water to wash off the foam, then drain thoroughly.
5. Stir-fry the green beans: add ample oil to the wok. Once the oil is hot, add the green beans over medium-high heat and stir-fry until they turn completely green and the skin wrinkles, showing a tiger-skin pattern. Remove and set aside.
6. Stir-fry the ribs: leave some base oil in the wok or add fresh oil. Add the drained ribs and stir-fry until the surface turns slightly yellow. Then add all the aromatics and stir-fry together until fragrant.
**Key point**: Add the ribs and aromatics to the pan at the same time; the moisture evaporating from the ribs helps prevent the aromatics from burning.
7. Season and color: add thirteen-spice powder and stir-fry evenly. Add enough salt and stir-fry to lock in the flavor. Then add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to bring out the soy sauce's aroma and color the ribs.
8. Braise the ribs: pour enough boiling water along the edge of the wok to submerge the ribs. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and braise for about 40 minutes, keeping the liquid at a gentle simmer.
**Note**: Must add boiling water.
9. Add the green beans: after braising the ribs for 20-30 minutes, lay the stir-fried green beans on top. Gently press them into the liquid with a spatula, and cover with the lid.
**Key point**: Leave a small gap in the lid to prevent the beans from turning yellow. Braise for another 10-15 minutes until the beans are tender.
10. Reduce the sauce over high heat: turn the heat to high and reduce the sauce, stirring constantly to prevent sticking. When the sauce thickens and turns glossy, and only oil remains at the bottom of the wok without liquid, sprinkle in a large amount of minced raw garlic. Stir-fry quickly a few times, then turn off the heat and serve.

## Health Advice
Pork ribs and green beans provide high-quality protein and dietary fiber, but the generous amount of oil used for stir-frying and the fat in the spare ribs make this dish relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with coarse grain staples and reduce other fat intake for the day. Suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing energy replenishment. Those with weaker digestive function should consume in moderation.
