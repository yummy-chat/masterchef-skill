# Old-Style Mixed Rice
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs: 2  
🌭 Chinese sausage: as needed (sliced, pre-steamed or pan-fried)  
🧄 Garlic: as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Millet peppers: as needed (chopped)  
🍚 Cooked rice: about one bowl (pre-cooked)  
🫒 Cooking oil: as needed (for frying eggs, use more than usual for stir-frying)  
🥣 Light soy sauce: 2 tablespoons  
🥣 Dark soy sauce: ½ tablespoon  
🍬 Granulated sugar: 1 teaspoon  
🤍 White sesame seeds: as needed  
🌶️ Chili flakes: as needed  
✨ Five-spice powder: a pinch  
🔥 Hot oil: as needed (for pouring over seasonings to release aroma)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥘 Clay pot or small saucepan (for heating oil)  
🥣 Several empty bowls  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍚 Rice cooker or pot (for cooking rice)  
🍲 Large bowl (for final assembly of mixed rice)  

## Steps  
1.  Take two empty bowls and crack one egg into each, ensuring the bowls are free of water and grease.  
2.  Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped millet peppers, 2 tablespoons light soy sauce, ½ tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 teaspoon sugar, a suitable amount of white sesame seeds, chili flakes, and a pinch of five-spice powder. Mix well and set aside.  
3.  Have a bowl of cooked rice ready. You can optionally mix in or top with the sliced Chinese sausage.  
4.  Heat a suitable amount of cooking oil in a small pot or clay pot until it just starts to smoke. Quickly pour the hot oil over the seasonings in the bowl from step 2, stirring immediately. You should hear a sizzling sound and smell the aroma—this indicates success.  
**Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the fragrance of the garlic, sesame seeds, and chili.  

5.  Pour more oil than usual for stir-frying into the frying pan. Heat over medium-high heat until it just starts to smoke, then gently slide in the eggs from the bowls.  
**Key point**: Keep the heat at medium-high and do not flip the eggs. Fry until the edges of the egg whites are golden and crispy, while the yolks remain runny.  

6.  Place the fried eggs on top of the rice. Pour the sauce prepared in step 4 over everything, trying to cover all ingredients.  
7.  Use a spoon to thoroughly mix the rice, eggs, Chinese sausage, and sauce. Serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This mixed rice is rich in carbohydrates and fats. Pair it with a cucumber salad to balance the calories. It is ideal for a quick energy boost on busy days. For the best texture, mix the rice while the egg yolk is still runny and hot.
