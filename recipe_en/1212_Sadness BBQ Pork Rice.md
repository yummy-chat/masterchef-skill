# Sadness BBQ Pork Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 1 catty (alternating fat and lean)
🥚 Eggs about 4 (for making scrambled eggs)
🌿 Chopped green onions to taste (for garnish)
🫘 Chu hou paste 1 tael
🥜 Sesame paste 3 qian
🧂 Light soy sauce 3 qian
🦪 Hoisin sauce to taste
🧀 Fermented bean curd to taste
🌾 Cornstarch 1 qian
🧂 Salt a little (added to egg mixture)
🌶️ White pepper to taste (added to egg mixture)
🍶 White vinegar a small amount (added to egg mixture)
🍶 Rice wine to taste (for deglazing)
🍬 White sugar 1 small spoon (added when reducing sauce)
🧈 Lard to taste (for frying BBQ pork and eggs)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing marinade)
🍽️ White plate (for serving BBQ pork)
🍚 Ceramic bowl (for assembling the final dish)
🥄 Small white bowl (for holding seasonings)

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean and prepare the pork belly, with alternating fat and lean for best results.
2. In a glass bowl, add Chu hou paste, sesame paste, light soy sauce, hoisin sauce, fermented bean curd, and cornstarch. Stir until well combined to form a marinade.
3. Place the pork belly into the marinade, ensuring the surface is evenly coated. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least one hour.
4. In a bowl, crack the eggs and add a pinch of salt, white pepper, and white vinegar. Then add a small amount of melted lard and stir until the egg mixture is well combined. Set aside.
5. Heat a wok over low heat and add a small amount of lard. Scrape off excess marinade from the pork belly and place it into the wok.
**Key point**: Because the marinade contains sugar and can easily burn, be sure to fry over low heat for about 5-6 minutes until both sides are caramelized and fragrant.
6. Splash rice wine along the edge of the wok to deglaze. Mix the remaining marinade with half a bowl of water, stir, and pour into the wok.
**Key point**: The sauce should partially submerge the pork belly. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and braise for at least 6 minutes.
7. Remove the lid, add one small spoon of white sugar, and keep turning the pork belly over low heat.
**Key point**: Adding sugar creates a gelatinous texture, enhancing the shine and flavor. Continue until the sauce thickens and coats the surface of the pork belly.
8. Pour out the sauce from the wok and set it aside. Add a little more lard to the wok, return the pork belly, and pan-fry over medium heat for 1-2 minutes to make the exterior oilier and more fragrant.
9. Remove the pork belly, let it cool slightly, and then cut into thick slices.
10. Clean the wok, heat a small amount of lard, and pour in the egg mixture.
**Key point**: Use low heat throughout. When the edges of the egg begin to set, gently push the set portions toward the center with a spatula, allowing the uncooked egg to flow to the edges. Repeat until most of the egg is set but the center is still moist and tender, then immediately turn off the heat.
11. Fill a bowl with rice, top with the scrambled eggs, arrange the BBQ pork slices on top, drizzle with the thick sauce, and sprinkle with chopped green onions.

## Health Advice
This BBQ pork rice dish is relatively high in fat and sugar. It is suitable for occasional consumption by those with high physical energy demands. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the greasiness and to control portion sizes to avoid excessive calorie intake.
