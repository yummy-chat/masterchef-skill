# Oolong Milk Tea Ice Cream
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Heavy cream 350g
🍂 Oolong tea leaves 1 tbsp
🧂 Rock salt 1 pinch
🍬 Granulated sugar 60g
💧 Water 100ml
🥚 Egg whites 2
🍡 Tapioca pearls, as needed
🍯 Brown sugar, as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Small saucepan
🥣 Large glass bowl
🌀 Electric hand mixer
🌡️ Thermometer
🫙 Plastic wrap
🧊 Mold
🔪 Spatula
🥃 Small glass cups (optional)
🍲 Pot for boiling
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Instructions
1. Add water and oolong tea leaves to a small saucepan. Bring to a boil over low heat, then continue simmering until the tea concentrate reduces, turns dark brown, and becomes thick.
2. Combine the concentrated tea liquid and granulated sugar in the small saucepan. Heat over low heat to make a syrup. Remove from heat immediately when the temperature reaches 110–115°C.
3. Add rock salt to the egg whites. Use an electric hand mixer to beat until the foam is fluffy and reaches about 40–50% peak stage.
4. While keeping the mixer running, slowly pour the hot syrup in a thin stream into the beaten egg whites.
   **Key point**: The syrup is very hot; be careful to avoid burns. Pour slowly so the egg whites heat evenly, forming a stable half-cooked meringue.
5. Continue beating until the meringue is firm and has a silky, glossy texture.
6. Whip the heavy cream until it reaches about 90% stiffness—clear lines should form and the texture should be smooth. Avoid over-whipping.
7. Add the whipped cream to the meringue in two batches. Gently fold with a spatula or whisk until just combined.
8. Line a mold with plastic wrap, pour in the ice cream mixture, smooth the surface with a spatula, and freeze until fully set. Alternatively, divide the mixture into small glass cups and freeze.
9. Before serving, cook the tapioca pearls and mix with brown sugar to make brown sugar pearls. Drizzle over the fully frozen ice cream.

## Health Advice
This ice cream uses egg whites instead of egg yolks, so its fat content is slightly lower than traditional recipes. However, the brown sugar pearls are high in sugar; it is recommended to consume the same day and not to refrigerate for long periods. Suitable for those who love milk tea but need to control fat intake, though total sugar intake should still be monitored.
