# Tiger Skin Eggs 🥚

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 6-8  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Green chili peppers, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧅 Chopped scallion, appropriate amount (for garnish)  
⭐ Star anise 1-2  
🌿 Cinnamon stick, 1 small piece  
🍶 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🍺 Beer (or cola), 1 can  
🧴 Dark soy sauce, a little  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🌾 Water starch, appropriate amount  
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for garnish)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan  
🫙 Glass pot  
🥢 Spatula / cooking shovel  
🥣 Strainer  
🥣 Small bowl  
🍳 Lid

## Cooking Steps
1. Place eggs in cold water, bring to a boil, then cook for exactly 9 minutes. Turn off the heat, cover, and let them rest for another 3 minutes.  
   **Key point**: 9 minutes boiling + 3 minutes resting ensures the yolks are fully cooked but not overdone, with a perfect texture.
2. Remove the eggs and immerse them in cold water. Peel the shells and use a toothpick to poke small holes evenly over the surface.  
   **Key point**: Poking holes allows the seasoning to penetrate, speeding up flavor absorption.
3. Heat a generous amount of oil in a pan. Add the eggs, cover with a lid, and gently shake the pan to fry until the surface turns golden and wrinkled. Remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: Covering the pan while shaking prevents hot oil from splattering.
4. Leave a small amount of oil in the pan. Add star anise, cinnamon, dried chilies, scallion sections, and ginger slices. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
5. Add the fried eggs, then light soy sauce, sugar, and one can of beer. Add a little dark soy sauce and oyster sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer, allowing the eggs to fully absorb the sauce.
6. Remove the spice residues from the pan. Add water starch to thicken the sauce slightly, so it coats the eggs evenly.
7. Sprinkle with chopped scallion and toasted sesame seeds. Toss well, then plate and serve.

## Health Tips
Tiger skin eggs are rich in protein, making them suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing an energy boost. However, the frying step increases the calorie content, so it’s best to consume in moderation and pair with vegetables for a balanced meal. Poking holes in the boiled eggs helps the marinade penetrate more effectively, while controlling the frying time prevents the yolks from drying out.
