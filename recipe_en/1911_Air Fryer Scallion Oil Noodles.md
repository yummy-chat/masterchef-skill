# Air Fryer Scallion Oil Noodles
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Scallions 25g (cut into sections)  
🍜 Noodles appropriate amount (pre-cooked and drained)  
🍤 Dried shrimp 10g (pre-soaked until soft)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 80g  
🧂 Light soy sauce 15g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 5g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 5g  
🍬 White granulated sugar 10g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Air fryer  
🥣 Tin foil bowl  
🥄 Mixing spoon  
🔗 Tongs  
🍶 Ceramic spoon (to weigh down the tin foil bowl)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the scallion sections into the tin foil bowl, pour in 80g of vegetable oil, then put the tin foil bowl into the air fryer.  
**Note**: The tin foil bowl is lightweight, so place a ceramic spoon on top to press it down, preventing it from being blown up by the hot air.  
2.  Set the temperature to 160°C and bake for 10 minutes.  
3.  Remove the tin foil bowl, add the pre-soaked dried shrimp, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white granulated sugar, then stir well.  
4.  Return to the air fryer, set to 180°C and bake for 3 minutes.  
5.  After removing, pour the pre-cooked and drained noodles into the bowl, mixing thoroughly with the scallion oil sauce.  
6.  Place back into the air fryer, set to 180°C and bake for 2 minutes, until the noodle surface is slightly dry and crisp.  
7.  Remove and enjoy. The finished dish has a rich scallion aroma, with dry, chewy noodles.

## Health Advice
This scallion oil noodle dish uses an air fryer instead of traditional deep-frying, reducing cooking fumes. However, the 80g of oil still contains a high calorie count, so it is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables. The dried shrimp provide umami flavor and calcium, making it suitable for elderly individuals and office workers needing calcium supplements.
