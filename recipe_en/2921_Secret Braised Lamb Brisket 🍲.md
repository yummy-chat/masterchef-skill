# Secret Braised Lamb Brisket 🍲
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐑 Skin-on lamb brisket about 1 plate (cut into large pieces, skin must be flame-singed to remove hair)
🫚 Local yellow ginger to taste (cut into thick slices, skin-on)
🧄 Garlic to taste (cloves)
🧅 Shallots to taste (diced)
🥕 Water chestnuts to taste (peeled and cut into pieces)
🥕 Carrot to taste (cut into rolling knife chunks)
🥕 Daikon radish to taste (cut into rolling knife chunks)
🫘 Fried tofu sticks to taste (deep-fried in advance, then soaked in water to rehydrate)
🫐 Dried longan meat about 1 small handful
🌿 Bay leaves 2 leaves
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 1 small piece
🌿 Five-spice leaves 2 leaves
🍬 Rock sugar slices 2 small pieces
🧅 Garlic chives to taste (cut into sections)
🍶 Rice wine to taste (used in three separate additions)
🥜 Chu hou paste about 1 small porcelain spoon
🧀 Cantonese fermented bean curd 1 small cube + a little fermented bean curd juice
🥜 Peanut butter about 1 small porcelain spoon
🌿 Five-spice powder a small amount
🍶 Light soy sauce a small amount
🍖 Lamb bone broth enough to submerge the lamb

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔥 High-heat stove
🍲 Clay pot
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ White plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare lamb brisket**: Singe the skin of fresh skin-on lamb brisket over an open flame to remove fine hair and enhance aroma. Cut into large pieces, ensuring the skin remains attached to prevent shrinkage during braising.
**Key Point**: Singeing the skin removes fine hair and boosts flavor, a traditional Cantonese method for lamb preparation.

2. **Prepare aromatics**: Cut yellow ginger into thick slices. Prepare garlic cloves and diced shallots. Peel and dice water chestnuts into small pieces, cut carrot and daikon radish into rolling knife chunks, cut garlic chives into sections. Deep-fry the tofu sticks in advance, then soak in water to rehydrate. Prepare dried bay leaves, tangerine peel, five-spice leaves, and dried longan meat.
**Key Point**: Fried tofu sticks must be soaked in advance to avoid becoming overly soft and mushy during braising.

3. **Blanch lamb brisket**: Add water to an iron wok. Just before the water reaches a boil, pour in rice wine. Add lamb brisket and blanch for about 1 minute, then remove and drain.
**Key Point**: Add rice wine before the water boils. Do not blanch for too long to prevent the lamb from becoming tough. Rice wine removes gaminess and adds aroma.

4. **Pan-fry lamb brisket**: Heat the iron wok until it turns white. Add the blanched lamb brisket and pan-fry over medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes until each side is slightly charred. Drizzle rice wine along the edge of the wok for added aroma. Remove and set aside.
**Key Point**: Dry-frying in a hot wok renders excess fat from the lamb and enhances meaty flavor. Adding rice wine after frying further removes gaminess and boosts fragrance.

5. **Infuse aromatics**: Add oil to the iron wok. First, fry the ginger slices until fragrant, then add garlic cloves and diced shallots. Stir-fry over low heat until the aroma is released, then remove and set aside.
**Key Point**: Add ingredients in order to avoid burning. Removing the aromatics prevents charring when cooking the sauce afterward.

6. **Prepare sauce and recombine**: Leave some oil in the wok. Add the revived shallots, ginger, and garlic. Add chu hou paste, Cantonese fermented bean curd, peanut butter, and five-spice powder. Stir-fry over low heat until the sauce is aromatic. Add the pan-fried lamb brisket and toss well to ensure the lamb is fully coated.
**Key Point**: Cook the sauce over low heat to avoid burning the bottom. The lamb must be evenly coated to ensure consistent flavor.

7. **Braise lamb brisket**: Add lamb bone broth to the wok, enough to submerge the lamb. Add bay leaves, tangerine peel, water chestnuts, carrot, daikon radish, five-spice leaves, rock sugar slices, and dried longan meat. Add a little light soy sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover and braise for 40-45 minutes until the lamb is tender and the sauce has thickened.
**Key Point**: Must use lamb bone broth instead of water to preserve the lamb's original flavor. Strictly follow the braising time to achieve tender and soft lamb.

8. **Finish in clay pot**: Take a clay pot. Layer the bottom with fried ginger slices and soaked fried tofu sticks. Pour the braised lamb brisket and its sauce into the clay pot. Add garlic chive sections. Serve while maintaining a gentle boil at the table. Enjoy with fermented bean curd and chili sauce.
**Key Point**: The bottom layer of aromatics in the clay pot adds depth of flavor. Keep the dish bubbling at the table to enhance aroma and dining experience.

## Health Advice
This lamb brisket pot is rich in high-quality protein and collagen. Combined with radish, water chestnuts, and other ingredients, it gently warms the body, dispels cold, and nourishes energy, making it especially suitable for those with cold hands and feet in winter or those with high physical exertion. Note that lamb is warming in nature; those with damp-heat constitution or a sore throat should consume in moderation. Fried tofu sticks and the sauce are relatively high in calories, so it is advisable to pair with leafy greens to balance the meal.
