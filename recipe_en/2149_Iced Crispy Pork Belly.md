# Iced Crispy Pork Belly
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork fatback (pork fat strips) about 300g, cut into coin-thick slices
🥚 Egg 1
🌾 All-purpose flour 1 tbsp
🌽 Cornstarch 1 tbsp
💧 Water as needed (for batter)
🛢️ Cooking oil 10g (added to batter)
🧅 Scallion segments as needed
🫚 Ginger slices as needed
🍶 Shaoxing wine (cooking wine) as needed
🧂 Salt about half a teaspoon
🍬 White sugar 1 tbsp
💧 Water 1 tbsp (for syrup)
🫘 Toasted black and white sesame seeds a small pinch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the fatback**: Cut the fatback into slices about the thickness of a coin.
2. **Blanch to remove odor**: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the fatback slices, along with scallion segments, ginger slices, and Shaoxing wine. After the water returns to a boil, blanch for about 1 minute, then drain and remove scallions and ginger.
*Blanching removes excess fat, keeping the final dish from being greasy.*

3. **Prepare the batter**: In a bowl, combine flour and cornstarch. Add salt, crack in the egg, and add water little by little, stirring until the batter is slightly thicker than yogurt and forms a continuous stream. Finally, add 10g cooking oil and mix well.
4. **Coat the slices**: Pour the batter over the fatback slices and toss to coat evenly, ensuring each slice is well covered.
5. **First fry**: Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok to 350°F (175°C). Over medium heat, add the slices. Do not stir immediately; let them set, then gently separate. Fry for about 2 minutes until light golden.
*Avoid low heat, or the batter will absorb too much oil, making the dish greasy.*

6. **Second fry**: Turn the heat to high and continue frying until the slices turn golden and crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain.
*High heat helps push out any absorbed oil, making the texture more fragrant and less greasy.*

7. **Make the syrup**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add one tablespoon of water and one tablespoon of white sugar. Cook over medium heat for about 2 minutes. When the syrup changes from large bubbles to fine, dense bubbles and turns a light milky white, continue heating until it becomes golden, then immediately turn off the heat.
8. **Coat with syrup**: Off the heat, quickly add the fried fatback slices and toss rapidly to coat each slice evenly with syrup. Sprinkle in the toasted black and white sesame seeds and toss again.
9. **Cool and plate**: Spread the coated slices on a tray, using chopsticks to separate and flatten them. Let cool completely until the sugar shell hardens for the crispiest texture. Plate and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in calories. It is recommended that fitness-conscious individuals and those managing sugar intake consume it in moderation. Those with poor dental health should exercise caution, as it requires significant chewing.
