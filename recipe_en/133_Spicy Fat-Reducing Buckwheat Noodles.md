# Spicy Fat-Reducing Buckwheat Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 **Seasonings**  
🌶️ Hot pot base  
🥜 Peanut butter  
🥛 Whole milk  
🍶 Light soy sauce  
🌶️ Chili oil  
🍎 Vinegar  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn oil  

🥬 **Ingredients**  
🍜 Semi-dried buckwheat noodles  
🥬 Baby cabbage  
🥗 Lettuce  

🛠️ **Utensils**  
🪣 Vegetable washing basin  
🥘 18cm diameter small pot  

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the baby cabbage and lettuce, drain them, and set aside.
2. Pour 500ml of water into the pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then add the buckwheat noodles and baby cabbage. Wait for the water to return to a boil.
3. Reduce the heat to low, add the hot pot base, peanut butter, milk, light soy sauce, and chili oil. Continue cooking for 5 minutes to allow the seasonings to fully meld.
4. Add the lettuce and cook for another 2 minutes until the lettuce is just tender.
5. Drizzle in the vinegar and Sichuan peppercorn oil, turn off the heat, and serve directly from the pot.

⚠️ **Key Points**
- Be sure to use semi-dried buckwheat noodles to ensure a chewy texture.
- The hot pot base, peanut butter, and milk are the core of the flavor and should not be omitted.
- You can add hot pot meatballs, egg dumplings, or other toppings as desired; omit the vinegar if you don't like sourness.

## Health Advice
The combination of hot pot base, peanut butter, and milk is relatively high in calories, so it's recommended to control the portion size. Buckwheat noodles and vegetables provide dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for occasional indulgence.
