# Farewell My Concubine
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦞 Softshell turtle – appropriate amount (cleaned and cut into large pieces)
🐔 Chicken feet – appropriate amount (trimmed nails)
🧅 Ginger – a small bowl, sliced (to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (cut into sections, separate white and green parts)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – appropriate amount (cut into sections to add heat)
🌶️ Dried Sichuan peppercorns – appropriate amount (to add numbing sensation)
🫑 Green and red chili peppers – appropriate amount (sliced into rings, used for final garnish)
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (for greasing the pan and stir-frying)
🍺 Beer – appropriate amount (enough to submerge the ingredients, to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce – appropriate amount (to enhance freshness)
🧴 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount (to enhance flavor)
🧴 Dark soy sauce – appropriate amount (for color; use sparingly)
🌿 Ground white pepper – appropriate amount (to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
✂️ Scissors (for processing the softshell turtle and chicken feet)
🍳 Wok (for blanching, stir-frying spices and main ingredients)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam and removing main ingredients)
🫕 Clay pot (for simmering over low heat)
🥄 Ladle (for adding seasonings)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare Ingredients**: Use scissors to cut the softshell turtle into large pieces and clean thoroughly. Trim the nails from the chicken feet. Slice the ginger. Cut the scallion into sections, separating the white and green parts. Cut the dried chili peppers into sections. Slice the green and red chili peppers into rings and set aside.
2. **Blanch**: Place the softshell turtle pieces and chicken feet in a pot with cold water. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then remove and rinse with cold water.
   **Key Point**: Starting with cold water removes the fishy smell more thoroughly; after removing, rinse with cold water to clean off the foam.
3. **Grease the Pan and Sauté**: Heat the wok, grease it with oil, and add the ginger slices. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
4. **Sauté Aromatics**: Add the white parts of the scallion, dried chili peppers, and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Continue to stir-fry over medium-low heat until the aroma is released.
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat at medium-low to prevent burning and bitterness.
5. **Stir-fry Main Ingredients**: Add the blanched and rinsed chicken feet and softshell turtle pieces to the wok. Stir-fry together with the spices so the main ingredients coat with the aroma.
6. **Season with Beer**: Pour in the beer, ensuring the liquid level covers the ingredients. After it boils, add salt, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and ground white pepper in sequence.
   **Key Point**: The natural sweetness of the beer removes the fishy smell and enhances the aroma. Add the dark soy sauce gradually in small amounts to avoid overly dark color.
7. **Simmer in Clay Pot**: Transfer all contents from the wok to a clay pot. Cover and simmer over low heat for 18 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The clay pot retains heat well, which makes the ingredients more tender and flavorful.
8. **Garnish and Serve**: Once the simmering is done, uncover and garnish with the green scallion sections and green and red chili pepper rings.

## Health Advice
Softshell turtle is high in protein and low in fat, paired with the collagen from chicken feet, making it suitable for nourishment. Braising with beer removes the fishy smell and makes the meat tender. Patients with gout should consume in moderation.
