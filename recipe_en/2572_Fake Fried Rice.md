# Fake Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥦 1 head cauliflower (grated or chopped)
🫑 Appropriate amount of green bell pepper (cut into small dices)
🥕 Appropriate amount of carrot (shredded and then chopped)
🥚 2 eggs
🦐 6 shrimp (heads, shells, and veins removed; heads reserved)
🧂 A pinch of salt
🫗 A dash of light soy sauce

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🧇 Grater
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks or whisk

## Cooking Steps
1. Grate or chop the cauliflower into fine pieces to make cauliflower rice; cut the green bell pepper into small dices; shred the carrot and then chop it into fine pieces.
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and beat until well combined; remove the heads, shells, and veins from the shrimp, and set the shrimp meat aside.
3. Place the non-stick pan over medium heat. Without adding oil, directly add the cauliflower rice and stir-fry for about 1 minute, until the moisture evaporates and it becomes fluffy. Remove and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Dry-fry without oil in the pan, using only heat to dry out the moisture, so the cauliflower rice reaches a fluffy, rice-like texture.
4. Add a small amount of oil to the pan, pour in the beaten eggs, scramble into small pieces, and remove.
5. Add a little more oil to the pan. Place the shrimp heads in and fry over low heat until the shrimp oil turns orange-red. Remove and discard the shrimp heads.
6. Using the shrimp oil in the pan, add the shrimp meat and stir-fry until it changes color.
7. Add the chopped carrot and diced green bell pepper, stir-fry for about 1 minute until just cooked.
8. Add the cauliflower rice, along with a small pinch of salt and a dash of light soy sauce. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds, then finally add the scrambled eggs. Mix well and remove from heat.

## Health Tips
This fake fried rice replaces rice with cauliflower, reducing carbohydrate content while increasing dietary fiber and vitamin C intake. It is suitable for those on a weight loss diet or managing blood sugar levels. The shrimp provide high-quality protein, and the aroma from the pan-fried shrimp oil adds richness to the dish.
