# Potato and Ham Braised Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato (moderate amount, cut into chunks)  
🌭 Ham sausage (moderate amount, sliced into rounds)  
🍜 Instant noodle cakes (2 pieces)  
🧄 Garlic (moderate amount, some sliced for fragrance, and a large amount minced into garlic paste)  
🧅 Scallions (moderate amount, some cut into sections for fragrance, and the rest chopped as scallion greens)  
🫒 Cooking oil (moderate amount)  
🧂 Salt (a pinch)  
🧂 Soy sauce (moderate amount)  
🧂 Instant noodle seasoning packet (1 pack)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or wok shovel  
🥘 Lid  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the potato into chunks, slice the ham sausage into rounds. Slice some garlic and mince the rest into garlic paste. Cut some scallions into sections and chop the rest as scallion greens. Set aside.
2. Heat the wok, add cooking oil, then add the potato chunks. Sprinkle a pinch of salt. Pan-fry over medium heat for about 60 seconds until the bottom is golden and crispy.
   **Note**: Frying over medium heat seals the surface, creating a caramelized layer to enhance aroma.
3. Flip the potatoes, add the ham sausage rounds, and continue stir-frying until the potatoes and ham are distinct and separate.
   **Key**: Frying until the ingredients are distinct helps the sauce coat evenly, improving the texture.
4. Push the ingredients to one side of the wok. Use the remaining oil to briefly stir-fry the scallion sections and sliced garlic until fragrant.
5. Mix them with the potatoes and ham. Drizzle in the soy sauce and stir-fry until everything is evenly colored.
6. Add the instant noodle seasoning packet. Quickly stir-fry to evenly coat all ingredients.
7. Pour in boiling hot water until it is roughly level with the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Note**: Always use hot water; cold water can cause the ingredients to shrink and the noodles to stick together.
8. Place the noodle cakes into the wok. Cover with the lid and reduce to medium-low heat. Braise.
   **Key**: Braise until the potatoes are soft and can be easily pierced with chopsticks, and the noodle cakes have fully absorbed the liquid and softened.
9. Remove the lid. Use chopsticks to loosen the noodles. Add a generous amount of minced garlic and quickly stir-fry to combine.
   **Note**: The minced garlic is the soul of this dish. Use a generous amount and rely on the residual heat of the wok to maximize its aroma.
10. Sprinkle in the chopped scallion greens. Stir-fry a few more times. Once the aroma of garlic and scallions is released, remove from heat and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Potatoes provide dietary fiber, while ham and instant noodles contribute higher carbohydrates and sodium. This dish is suitable for occasional indulgence. Pair it with a serving of green leafy vegetables and reduce the amount of seasoning packet used to control salt intake.
