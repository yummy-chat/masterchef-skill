## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the green pepper and bird's eye chili into rings, chop the green onion into pieces, mince the garlic, and prepare the crushed peanuts and steamed bun.  
   **Note**: All ingredients must be prepared in advance to ensure a smooth stir-frying process.  
2. Heat the iron wok, add oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chopped green onion and minced garlic, and stir-fry over medium heat until the aroma is released.  
   **State Check**: The edges of the green onion and garlic turn slightly burnt, and a strong aroma emanates.  
3. Add the soybean paste and Pixian bean paste, and stir-fry over medium heat until the paste is well combined with the green onion and garlic, and the fragrance of the paste is rich.  
   **State Check**: The oil from the paste shows red oil, and the aroma is intense.  
4. Add the chili powder and thirteen spices powder, then reduce the heat to medium-low and stir-fry slowly until the spices release red oil and the aroma is strong.  
   **Note**: Stir-fry over low heat to prevent the powders from burning; check if the red oil from the chili powder is fully released.  
5. Add the green pepper rings and bird's eye chili rings, season with salt and white sugar, and stir-fry over medium heat until the peppers are just cooked.  
   **Note**: Control the stir-frying time to maintain the crisp and tender texture of the peppers.  
6. Add the crushed peanuts, quickly stir-fry to evenly mix, then turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Quick stir-frying keeps the crushed peanuts crispy.  
7. Stuff the stir-fried pepper mixture into the steamed bun, with generous filling and robust flavor, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish combines chili and pastes, rich in vitamin C and protein, but high in sodium. It is suitable for satisfying strong cravings, but people with high blood pressure should consume it in small amounts.
