# Vegetarian Cabbage Dumplings
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Cabbage: appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)  
🥕 Carrot: appropriate amount (shredded then diced)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms: appropriate amount (cut into 0.5 cm cubes)  
🧅 Scallion: appropriate amount (finely chopped, preferably more)  
🫚 Ginger: appropriate amount (minced)  
🌾 Flour: 500g  
💧 Water: 250~260g  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (used in batches)  
🌿 Thirteen Spices: appropriate amount  
🍶 Yellow wine: appropriate amount  
🥄 Soy sauce: about 25g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon: appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil: appropriate amount (optional)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Strainer (for draining water and scooping dumplings)  
🔪 Wooden cutting board and kitchen knife  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Induction cooktop  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Add a pinch of salt to the flour, gradually add 250~260g of water, knead into a smooth dough, cover and let rest for 10 minutes, then knead again. Repeat kneading three times until the dough is smooth and elastic.  
2. Layer the cabbage leaves, first slice into strips then cut into even small cubes. Add salt, mix well, and let sit for 3~4 minutes to draw out moisture. Rinse twice with clean water, then squeeze dry, retaining some moisture.  
3. Slice the carrot into rounds, then shred and dice into small cubes. Dice the shiitake mushrooms into about 0.5 cm cubes. Finely chop the scallion. Mince the ginger.  
4. Add about one liang of oil to a wok. Sauté the minced scallion and ginger until fragrant, add the Thirteen Spices, then add the carrot cubes and mushroom cubes. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 3 minutes until softened and cooked through, bringing out the sweetness of the carrot.  
5. Splash the yellow wine along the edge of the wok, increase heat to release the aroma, add soy sauce, stir well, then turn off heat and set aside to cool.  
6. Mix the cooled stir-fried ingredients with the squeezed cabbage filling. Add an appropriate amount of salt, chicken bouillon, and a drizzle of sesame oil to taste. Finally, add the remaining chopped scallions and mix thoroughly.  
**Key Point**: Stir-fried ingredients must be completely cooled before mixing in, to avoid scalding the cabbage texture. Adjust seasoning by tasting.  

7. Wrap the dumplings. Boil water, add the dumplings, gently push with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. Once the dumplings float to the surface, the water re-boils and they become puffed, cook for another 1~2 minutes, then scoop out.  
**Note**: Vegetarian filling cooks quickly, so avoid prolonged boiling to prevent the wrappers from breaking.  

## Health Advice  
This vegetarian cabbage dumpling is rich in dietary fiber and various vitamins, suitable for those managing fat intake. The stir-fried mushrooms and carrots enhance the flavor of the filling, but add salt in batches to prevent the cabbage from releasing too much water.
