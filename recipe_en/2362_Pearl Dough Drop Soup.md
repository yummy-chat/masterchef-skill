# Pearl Dough Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍅 Tomato: 2 pieces (about 400g, choose slightly soft ones with the stem still attached for richer flavor)
🌾 All-purpose flour: 100g
🥬 Leaf lettuce: 30g (finely chopped)
🌿 Cilantro: 10g (finely chopped, optional)
🧅 Green onion: 10g (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic: 15g (pressed flat and minced)
🥚 Eggs: 2 (beaten into an egg mixture)
💧 Water: 600g
🧂 Salt: 1g (added to flour)
🧂 Salt: 3g (final seasoning)
🫒 Cooking oil: appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar: 5g
🍶 Light soy sauce: 30g
🦪 Oyster sauce: 10g
🌶️ White pepper powder: 1g
🧂 MSG: 3g (optional)
🫗 Sesame oil: 5g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🍴 Fork (or two chopsticks, used to hold the tomato while roasting the skin)
🥣 Large bowl (metal or ceramic, for mixing dough drops)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring egg mixture and making dough drops)
🥄 Spoon (for adding water, seasoning, and stirring)
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife

## Cooking Steps
1. Insert a fork into the base of a tomato and hold it over an open flame on the gas stove until the skin bursts. Remove the tomato, peel off the skin while it is still hot, and then cut it into small dices.
   **Key Point**: A fork is more secure than a single chopstick for holding the tomato; the skin peels off easily after roasting.
2. Finely chop the leaf lettuce, cilantro, and green onion separately. Press the garlic flat and mince it.
3. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined. Set the egg mixture aside.
4. Place the all-purpose flour in a large bowl and add 1g of salt, mixing well. Hold chopsticks in your right hand and dip your left hand in water. Sprinkle water drop by drop into the flour while quickly stirring with the chopsticks in your right hand. Continue until small, even-sized dough particles form and no dry flour remains at the bottom of the bowl.
   **Key Point**: The drop-by-drop method is easier to control than pouring water from a tap, ensuring evenly sized dough drops. Avoid leaving dry flour at the bottom of the bowl, or the soup will become too thick.
5. Heat oil in a wok over low heat. Add the chopped green onion and minced garlic, stir-frying until fragrant. Add the diced tomatoes and stir-fry over high heat for a few seconds. Add 5g of white sugar, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 2 minutes, until the tomatoes become mushy and release their juice.
   **Key Point**: White sugar neutralizes the acidity and helps the tomatoes break down and release their liquid quickly.
6. Add 30g of light soy sauce, 10g of oyster sauce, and 1g of white pepper powder. Stir-fry evenly, then pour in 600g of water. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil.
   **Key Point**: The amount of water is critical, as it directly affects the final thickness of the soup.
7. Once the broth is boiling vigorously, evenly and slowly sprinkle the dough drops into the wok. Gently push them with a spatula to keep each drop separate.
   **Key Point**: The broth must be at a full boil before adding the dough drops. Sprinkle them in evenly and avoid dumping them all in at once; this is key to preventing the soup from becoming too thick.
8. After the dough drops are cooked, stir the egg mixture again and slowly pour it into the wok in a circular motion. Do not stir immediately; wait for the egg to set and float to the surface, then gently push with a spoon to create beautiful egg flower ribbons.
   **Key Point**: Do not stir immediately after adding the egg mixture, or the egg flowers will break apart and fail to form nicely.
9. Add the chopped leaf lettuce and cilantro. Season with 3g of salt and 3g of MSG, then drizzle with 5g of sesame oil. Stir gently with a spoon until everything is evenly combined, then remove from heat and serve.
   **Key Point**: Add the greens and cilantro last to maintain their crisp texture and fresh aroma.

## Health Advice
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene and vitamin C, leaf lettuce provides dietary fiber, and the dough drops offer carbohydrates. Paired with a light broth, this soup is easy to digest and absorb, making it ideal for stimulating the appetite during summer. Be mindful of the oil and salt amounts; those with high blood pressure should reduce salt intake.
