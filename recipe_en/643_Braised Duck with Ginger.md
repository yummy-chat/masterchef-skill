# Braised Duck with Ginger
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck meat, appropriate amount (cut into long strips)
🫚 Old ginger, a handful (sliced)
🧄 Garlic sprouts, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (sliced)
🫒 Black sesame oil, appropriate amount
🌟 Star anise, appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon, appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🍶 Rice wine or beer, one tablespoon
🧂 Salt, a small amount
💧 Water, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Wok spatula
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the duck meat and cut it into long strips; slice the old ginger; cut the garlic sprouts into sections; slice the red bell pepper.
   **Note**: The ginger must be old ginger as young ginger lacks aroma.
2. Pour black sesame oil into the wok, add the sliced old ginger, and stir-fry over low heat until the aroma is released.
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the ginger slices.
3. Add the duck strips and stir-fry together with the ginger slices until the duck meat changes color and the surface is slightly seared.
   **Key point**: Fresh duck meat does not need to be blanched; direct stir-frying locks in nutrients and umami.
4. Once the duck meat is fragrant, add star anise, cinnamon, and dried chili peppers, and continue stir-frying until aromatic.
5. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce in order, then add one tablespoon of rice wine, and stir-fry evenly to coat the duck meat with color.
6. Add a small amount of salt and stir-fry evenly.
7. Pour in water, enough to submerge the ingredients, cover the wok with a lid, and turn to medium-low heat to braise for 45 minutes.
   **Key point**: Keep the broth at a gentle simmer; check occasionally to prevent it from drying out.
8. Once the braising time is up, remove the lid, sprinkle in the garlic sprout sections and red bell pepper slices, stir-fry quickly and evenly, then remove from the wok and serve.
   **Key point**: Garlic sprouts and red bell pepper mainly add aroma and color; avoid overcooking them—mix evenly and serve.

## Health Suggestion
Duck meat is cool in nature, paired with warming old ginger. Stir-frying in black sesame oil releases its aroma, making this dish suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter. Fresh duck meat stir-fried directly locks in its umami, but since oyster sauce and light soy sauce already provide saltiness, use salt sparingly. Ensure sufficient braising time for the meat to become tender and flavorful; those with weaker digestion should eat less duck skin.
