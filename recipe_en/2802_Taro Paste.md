# Taro Paste
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Taro, approximately 2000 grams
🥛 Whole milk, 200 grams
🍦 Whipping cream, 180 grams
🍬 White sugar, 150 grams
🫒 Cooking oil, 150 grams (added in batches)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Peeler
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
♨️ Steamer
🥣 Large bowl
🥖 Rolling pin
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥄 Strainer, spoon

## Instructions
1.  Peel the taro, cut off both ends, and slice into thick pieces.
   **Note**: Slicing thickly reduces water absorption during steaming, thereby shortening the subsequent stir-frying time.
2.  Place the taro slices in a steamer. Start with cold water over high heat. Once the water boils, reduce to medium heat and steam for 30 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: The taro is fully cooked when it can be easily pierced with a chopstick.
3.  Transfer the steamed taro to a large bowl. Add the whole milk, whipping cream, and white sugar. Mash into a smooth paste using a rolling pin.
   **Key Point**: Ensure the mixture is uniform and the texture is as smooth as possible.
4.  Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a non-stick pan. Add the taro paste and stir-fry continuously over medium-low heat.
   **Key Point**: As you stir-fry, the taro paste will gradually absorb the oil. Add the remaining oil in batches. Continue stir-frying for approximately 20–30 minutes until the paste clings to the spatula and does not fall off easily.
5.  Pass the stir-fried taro paste through a strainer. Press it through with a spoon and scrape it off the bottom to achieve a finer texture.

## Health Advisory
This taro paste recipe includes a high amount of cream and sugar, making it relatively high in calories. It is recommended to control portion sizes or use a sugar substitute and reduce the amount by half. While taro itself is rich in potassium and dietary fiber, the oil used in stir-frying increases its calorie content, making it suitable for occasional enjoyment.
