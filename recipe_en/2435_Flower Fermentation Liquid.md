# Flower Fermentation Liquid
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌸 Camellia flowers, about 50 grams (use only the petals, wash and drain)  
💧 Cold water, 500 grams  
🍬 White sugar, 125 grams  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍲 Stainless steel pot  
🥢 Tongs  
🔗 Drying rack (mesh rack)  
🫙 Glass jar (Mason jar)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🌡️ Warm storage area (cabinet or shelf)  

## Steps  
1. Place the glass jar into a pot of cold water, bring to a boil and continue boiling for 5 minutes to thoroughly sterilize; the jar lid cannot withstand prolonged boiling, so briefly dip it in boiling water. After sterilizing all tools, invert them on a drying rack and allow them to dry completely until no raw water remains.  
2. Pick fresh, pesticide-free camellia flowers, take the petals and gently shake or rinse to remove small insects and dust, remove the stamens, and drain the surface moisture.  
3. Place about 50 grams of petals into the sterilized glass jar; the volume of the petals should account for about half of the jar. Add 125 grams of white sugar, then pour in 500 grams of cold water.  
4. Tighten the lid, shake the jar to fully dissolve the sugar and mix evenly.  
5. Move the sealed fermentation liquid jar to a warm, easily observable location and let it sit to ferment.  
   **Note**: The fermentation time requires observing changes in bubbles and odor; the video does not provide a fixed time; avoid direct sunlight during the process.  

## Health Advice  
Camellia flower fermentation liquid is rich in natural floral fragrance and antioxidants, suitable for spring beauty care or as a beverage flavoring. Pay attention to controlling the sugar amount; diabetics should be cautious; during the fermentation process, observe the bubbles to avoid contamination by unwanted microorganisms.
