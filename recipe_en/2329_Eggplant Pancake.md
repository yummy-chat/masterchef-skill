# Eggplant Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 1 eggplant (peeled and shredded)  
🥚 2 eggs  
🌾 About half a spoon of flour  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for mixing into batter and frying)  
🧂 3 grams of salt (for pickling the eggplant shreds)  
🧂 A pinch of MSG  
🧂 A pinch of thirteen-spice powder  
🍶 2 drops of white vinegar (added to the eggs to remove any fishy smell)

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Peeler, kitchen knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl, small bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon  
🍳 Frying pan  
🍽️ Plate (for flipping)  
🪵 Wooden cutting board

## Steps
1. Peel the eggplant and cut it into shreds about the thickness of chopsticks. Place them in a bowl.  
2. Add 3 grams of salt, mix well, and let sit for 3 minutes to draw out moisture and remove astringency.  
   **Note**: After pickling, the eggplant will become softer and easier to squeeze dry.  
3. Squeeze the eggplant shreds firmly to remove excess moisture, then transfer to another clean bowl.  
4. Add half a spoon of flour and mix well, ensuring the eggplant shreds are evenly coated with a thin layer of flour.  
5. Add scallions, MSG, and thirteen-spice powder, and mix well.  
6. In a small bowl, crack 2 eggs, add 2 drops of white vinegar, and beat until well combined. Then pour into the eggplant-flour mixture.  
   **Note**: The white vinegar effectively removes the eggy smell, and the amount is minimal, so it won’t affect the final taste.  
7. Thoroughly mix the egg mixture with the eggplant batter, then add a small amount of cooking oil and stir until you have a batter that is neither too thin nor too thick.  
8. Pour cooking oil into the pan and heat over high heat. Once the oil is hot, pour in the batter and use a spoon to shape it into a round pancake.  
9. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting and cook slowly until the bottom is set.  
   **Key point**: Always cook over low heat; high heat will burn the outside while leaving the inside raw.  
10. Place a plate upside down over the pancake, flip the pan to transfer the pancake onto the plate, then slide it back into the pan to cook the other side.  
11. Continue cooking over low heat until both sides are golden brown and the inside is fully cooked, then remove from the pan.  
12. Place the cooked pancake on a cutting board and cut into triangular pieces or your preferred shape. Serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber. This pancake is soft and easy to digest, making it suitable for the elderly and children. After using salt to draw out moisture, make sure to squeeze the eggplant dry; otherwise, the batter will be too thin to hold its shape. It is recommended to pair with garlic paste or a vinegar dip to cut through the richness.
