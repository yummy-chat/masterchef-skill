# Hunan-Style Pickles
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 A bundle of long beans (used whole)
🥬 Half a head of cabbage (torn into pieces by hand)
🥕 1 carrot (peeled and cut into strips)
🍚 Half a bowl of rice (leftover rice is fine, used for cooking rice water)
⭐ Star anise (to taste)
🌿 Bay leaves (to taste)
🌰 Cinnamon stick (to taste)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns (to taste)
🧂 60 grams of salt
🍶 Approximately 250 ml of 5-degree white vinegar
🥃 50 grams of high-proof liquor (baijiu)
💧 4 jin (about 2 kg) of clean water (total amount, including water for cooking rice water)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫙 Large glass bowl (fermentation container)
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board
🍳 Pot (for cooking rice water)
🫙 Plastic wrap (for sealing)
🥢 Metal food tongs (for clean serving)

## Instructions
1. Wash all vegetables thoroughly. Keep the long beans in a whole bundle, peel and cut the carrot into strips, and tear the cabbage into pieces by hand. Avoid using a knife.
2. Place the prepared vegetables into a large glass bowl. Add 4 jin of clean water.
3. Add 60 grams of salt, approximately 250 ml of 5-degree white vinegar, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, Sichuan peppercorns, and 50 grams of high-proof liquor to create the pickling brine base.
4. In a pot, add some of the clean water and half a bowl of leftover rice. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook until the rice is soft and the rice water becomes thick and rolling.
5. Pour the boiling hot rice water directly into the bowl containing the vegetables and seasonings. Use the high heat to blanch the vegetables, partially cooking them.
**Key Point**: The rice water must be poured in while boiling hot to ensure the vegetables are sufficiently blanched. Do not stir excessively after pouring to prevent the vegetables from becoming overly soft.
6. Gently stir to fully dissolve the salt, vinegar, and other seasonings. Ensure all vegetables are submerged in the liquid.
7. Completely seal the bowl opening with plastic wrap and let it cool to room temperature.
8. After cooling, refrigerate and let it pickle overnight.
9. When serving, always use clean, oil-free utensils, such as metal food tongs, to avoid contamination that could cause spoilage.

## Health Advice
This Hunan-style pickle is rich in dietary fiber and probiotics, making it ideal for relieving greasiness and stimulating the appetite after meals. However, it has a high salt content, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Ensure all tools are free of oil and water during the pickling process to extend shelf life and prevent spoilage.
