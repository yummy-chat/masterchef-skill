# Braised Pork in Pita Bread (Electric Rice Cooker Version)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1000g pork hind leg (or pork belly can also be used)  
🌾 500g all-purpose flour  
🫙 5g yeast  
💧 250ml water (for kneading dough)  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for blanching and braising to remove gaminess)  
🧂 Cinnamon stick, as needed  
⭐ Star anise, as needed  
🍃 Bay leaves, as needed  
🌶️ Green bell pepper, as needed (to cut richness)  
🌿 A few Sichuan peppercorns (for blanching)  
🧊 25g rock sugar  
🫗 30g light soy sauce  
🫗 30g dark soy sauce  
🧂 Salt, as needed (adjust seasoning after braising)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍚 Electric rice cooker  
🍳 Flat-bottom pan or electric griddle  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🪵 Wooden spatula  

## Steps  
1. Wash the pork and cut into small pieces. Place in cold water, add ginger slices and Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam thoroughly, then continue cooking over medium heat for 3 minutes. Remove, rinse under clean water, and drain well.  
**Note**: Be sure to skim off all the foam during blanching. Cooking for 3 minutes is enough to remove most of the gaminess.  

2. Place the pork pieces into the electric rice cooker. Add scallion, ginger, cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, rock sugar, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Pour in enough water to just cover the meat. Close the lid and press the "Steam Rice" button to braise until the meat can be easily pierced through with chopsticks.  
**Key point**: Do not use too much water. After braising, taste and add salt as needed.  

3. Prepare the dough: Mix the flour and yeast together. Gradually add 250ml water, stirring into flaky pieces, then knead into a smooth and slightly firm dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes, until the dough has slightly puffed up. It does not need to fully double in size.  
**Note**: The dough should be somewhat firm and not over-fermented, otherwise the cooked bread will be too soft and lack chewiness.  

4. Dust a cutting board with dry flour. Take out the dough, knead it briefly to release air, then roll it into a long log and divide into 8 portions. Take one portion, roll it into a long strip, then flatten it into a thin, tongue-shaped oval. Fold it in half, then roll it up from one end, tucking the tail end underneath. Press it flat and roll it out into a round, thin disc.  
**Key point**: Retain some air pockets in the dough to create layers. The disc should be of even thickness.  

5. Preheat a flat-bottom pan or electric griddle. Place the dough disc directly into the pan without oil. Cover with a lid and cook over medium heat for about 3 minutes, until the bottom turns golden. Flip and cook for another 3 minutes. Continue flipping until both sides are golden brown. Press the bread with a spatula; if it springs back quickly and puffs up, it means the inside is fully cooked.  
**Key point**: Covering the pan uses steam to cook the inside through.  

6. Remove the braised meat and chop it finely. Mix with the chopped green bell pepper and chop together briefly. Cut the bread open from the middle, fill with the meat mixture, and spoon a little braising liquid over the filling before serving.  
**Note**: Chopping the filling finely improves the texture. Adding the braising liquid makes the bread more aromatic and flavorful.  

## Health Tips  
This dish uses pork hind leg slowly braised in an electric rice cooker, offering controlled fat content and rich protein, making it suitable for fitness and muscle-building individuals. It is recommended to pair with green bell pepper to cut richness and to control the amount of bread consumed to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake.
