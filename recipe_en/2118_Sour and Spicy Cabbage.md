# Sour and Spicy Cabbage
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Chinese cabbage, appropriate amount (separate leaves and stems)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut white parts into sections, green parts into chopped scallions)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount (cut into sections, remove seeds, soak briefly in water to prevent burning)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for pickling cabbage stems and the sauce)
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon
🧂 Mature vinegar, about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🫙 Chili oil, appropriate amount
🧈 Lard, 1 tablespoon (for stir-frying aromatics, adds fragrance)
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (mixed with lard for cooking)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
💧 Sink (for washing cabbage)
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife (for cutting ingredients)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for pickling cabbage stems and holding ingredients)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup spoon (for mixing sauce and scooping seasonings)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Separate the cabbage leaves one by one and wash them thoroughly. Separate the thicker white stems from the green leaves. Slice the stems diagonally into small pieces, and tear the leaves into large pieces.
2.  Sprinkle a pinch of salt over the cabbage stems, mix well, and let them sit for 5 minutes. Then gently squeeze out excess water; this makes the stems crisper and easier to absorb flavor.
3.  Slice the garlic. Cut the white parts of the scallions into sections and chop the green parts. Cut the dried chili peppers into small sections, remove the seeds, and soak briefly in water, then drain and set aside.
4.  Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine salt, white sugar, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, mature vinegar, sesame oil, cornstarch, and chili oil. Stir well until fully combined.
5.  Heat a wok over high heat and add a little vegetable oil. Add the cabbage stems and stir-fry quickly for about 1 minute until they become slightly translucent and just cooked through. Remove from the wok.
   **Note**: Keep the heat high throughout to preserve a crisp texture.
6.  In the same wok (or a clean one), add 1 tablespoon of lard and some vegetable oil. Reduce heat to low, add the soaked dried chili segments, sliced garlic, and white scallion sections. Stir-fry slowly until fragrant, until the chili peppers darken in color but are not burnt.
   **Key Point**: The combination of oils enhances the dish's aroma; low heat prevents the aromatics from burning.
7.  Immediately turn the heat to high, add the pre-cooked cabbage stems, and stir-fry quickly to raise the temperature.
8.  Add the cabbage leaves, continuing to stir-fry over high heat until the leaves soften and wilt. Stir the prepared sauce again to ensure it is well mixed, then pour it along the edge of the wok.
9.  Quickly stir-fry everything together to coat all ingredients evenly with the sauce. Before removing from the wok, sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve.
   **Key Point**: The leaves must be cooked over high heat to minimize water release, resulting in a drier, more fragrant dish. Always stir the sauce thoroughly before adding to prevent cornstarch from settling.

## Health Advice
Salting the cabbage stems draws out water, making them crispier. Using less oil and quick stir-frying over high heat makes this dish suitable for those watching their calorie intake while enjoying sour and spicy flavors. Although lard and chili oil add fragrance, those with high blood lipids are advised to reduce the amount and can substitute with pure vegetable oil.
