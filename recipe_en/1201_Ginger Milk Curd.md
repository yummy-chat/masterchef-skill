# Ginger Milk Curd

Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Water buffalo milk 150g (must use water buffalo milk, regular milk cannot be substituted)
🫚 Aged ginger 10g (for juicing, only the thick starchy sediment at the bottom after settling is used)
🍬 White sugar as needed (to balance the ginger's spiciness and prevent bitterness in the finished dish)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Pot
🔥 Stove
🫗 Pitcher or milk pot (for controlling the pour stream and height)
🥄 Soup spoon
🥣 Bowl

## Instructions
1. Juice the aged ginger, let it settle, then pour off the clear liquid on top, retaining only about 10g of the thick starchy ginger sediment at the bottom of the bowl.
**Key Point**: Be sure to keep the thick ginger starch at the bottom, as this is crucial for curdling.

2. Pour the water buffalo milk into a pot, add white sugar, and heat over medium-low heat until large amounts of steam rise from the milk's surface and the edges begin to gently simmer. Immediately remove from heat; do not let it come to a full boil.
**Note**: If the temperature is too high, the protein will denature and fail to curdle.

3. Quickly pour the heated water buffalo milk into a pitcher, then from a height of about 15-20 cm above the bowl, pour it into the bowl containing the ginger juice. Use the force of the stream to naturally mix the milk and ginger juice, creating a swirl. **Key: Do not stir with any utensil**.

4. Immediately cover the bowl after pouring, and let it sit undisturbed for about 3-4 minutes. Avoid moving the bowl during this time.

5. Test method: After uncovering, gently place a soup spoon on the milk's surface. If the spoon floats and does not sink, the curd has set successfully.

## Health Advice
This ginger milk curd is high in calcium and easily absorbed, making it suitable for those needing calcium supplementation or seeking warmth in autumn and winter. However, sugar intake should be controlled; diabetic patients should consume with caution.
