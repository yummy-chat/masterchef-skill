# Mango Mille Crêpe Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Whole egg liquid 112g (about 2 eggs)
🌾 Cake flour 54g
🥛 Milk 170g
🧈 Butter 10g (melted)
🥛 Whipping cream 350g
🥭 Mangoes about 4–6 (small Tainong mangoes)
🍬 White sugar 27g
🍬 Powdered sugar 21g

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Whisk (manual or electric)
🔍 Strainer
🍳 Non-stick pan (crêpe pan)
🔥 Stove
🔪 Peeler, knife and cutting board
🔪 Immersion blender
🥘 Small saucepan
⚖️ Digital scale
🔪 Spatula
🎡 Turntable
🍴 Fork

## Instructions
### Prepare the Crêpe Batter
1. Add whole egg liquid and white sugar to a bowl, stir until the sugar dissolves.
2. Sift in the cake flour, stir until mostly smooth with no large lumps.
3. Pour in the milk and mix well, then add the melted butter and stir thoroughly until fully combined.
   **Key point**: Strain the batter twice to ensure a smooth, lump-free texture. If using chilled eggs and milk, gently warm the batter over a water bath before adding the butter.

### Cook the Crêpes
1. Preheat the non-stick pan over medium-low heat until it reaches the proper temperature.
2. Ladle a scoop of batter into the pan, quickly swirl the pan to spread the batter evenly across the bottom, then pour any excess batter back into the bowl.
3. Cook until small bubbles appear densely on the surface of the crêpe, indicating it is done.
4. To remove the crêpe, gently loosen the right side with chopsticks, then lift from the left side for easy release.
   **Note**: Cover the cooked crêpes with plastic wrap to prevent drying. Maintain medium-low or low heat throughout.

### Prepare the Mangoes
1. Peel the mangoes, slice each side of the flesh into 3–4 thin pieces, and cover with plastic wrap to prevent oxidation.
2. Scrape the flesh from the mango pit into a container and blend into a puree using an immersion blender.

### Make the Mango Sauce
1. Pour the mango puree into a small saucepan, heat over low flame, and stir continuously until it thickens into a concentrated sauce.
   **Key point**: No additional sugar is needed. Cool the sauce completely over an ice bath after cooking.

### Whip the Mango Cream
1. Add powdered sugar to the whipping cream and whip over an ice bath at high speed until it reaches about 60–70% stiffness.
2. Add the fully cooled mango sauce and continue whipping until distinct peaks form and the texture is firm.
   **Key point**: The mango sauce must be completely cooled, and the amount should not be excessive, or the cream will become too thin. The final cream should stand firm without flowing.

### Assemble the Cake
1. Place the first crêpe on the center of the turntable.
2. Arrange 3 mango slices in a staggered pattern, spread about 34g of mango cream on top, and smooth it out.
3. Cover with the second crêpe, aligning the thicker edge with the thinner edge of the layer below.
   **Key point**: Stagger the mango slices in each layer, and use no more than 3 slices per layer to ensure balanced structure and harmonious texture.
4. Repeat the process until all ingredients are used.
   **Note**: A 6-inch cake typically requires about 14 crêpes and 13 layers of filling. For an 8-inch cake, double all ingredient quantities.

### Chill and Serve
1. After assembly, refrigerate the cake for 2–4 hours.
2. Slice and enjoy. Once the crêpes and cream have melded together, the cake achieves a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

## Health Advice
Mango mille crêpe cake is a summer "passion bomb," rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber. However, the 350g of whipping cream and powdered sugar contribute significant calories, so it is best enjoyed in small slices as a treat rather than as a dessert staple after meals. Share with friends to avoid overindulging alone.
