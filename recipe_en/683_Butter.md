# Butter
Cuisine Type: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 1 liter heavy cream (35% fat content)
🧈 60g buttermilk (as a starter, can be substituted with plain yogurt)
🚰 Cold water/ice water, as needed (for washing, must be kept cold)
🧊 Ice cubes, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed (optional, add when making salted butter)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍶 Glass jar with lid (for mixing and fermentation)
🔌 Stand mixer or hand mixer (for high-speed whipping)
🥄 Spatula/spoon (for pressing and transferring)
🪣 Stainless steel sieve/cheesecloth (for filtering and separating)
🥣 Large bowl (for washing butter)
🔥 Stainless steel pot (for melting butter)
📄 Parchment paper (for shaping and wrapping)
🫙 Glass bottle/jar (for storing the finished product)

## Instructions
1. Pour 1 liter of heavy cream into a glass jar, add 60g of buttermilk, and stir until well combined.
   **Note**: Do not tighten the jar lid. Let it ferment at room temperature for 1 to 2 days, until the cream is semi-solid and has a strong sour aroma. If buttermilk is unavailable, plain yogurt can be used as a substitute.
2. Place the container with the fermented cream in the refrigerator and chill thoroughly.
   **Key Point**: Thorough chilling is essential for smooth subsequent whipping and separation of oil and water.
3. Pour the chilled fermented cream into the mixing bowl of a stand mixer and whip on the highest speed.
   **Time and Texture**: Whip on high speed for about 2 to 3 minutes. The cream will first thicken, then liquid from the oil and water separation will appear. When the liquid begins to splatter, cover with plastic wrap and continue whipping for about half a minute, until the butter granules are fully separated from the liquid.
4. Pour the mixture through a sieve. The solids retained in the sieve are the butter mass; the filtered liquid is buttermilk, which can be collected and saved separately.
5. Place the butter mass in a large bowl, add cold water and ice cubes, and press and wash repeatedly with a spatula. Discard the cloudy water, replace with clean ice water, and repeat the washing process.
   **Key Point**: The purpose of washing is to remove all residual buttermilk. Repeat washing about twice, until the water in the bowl remains clear.
6. Press the washed butter mass repeatedly against the side of the bowl to squeeze out excess internal moisture.
7. Place the butter mass on parchment paper, fold and wrap it with the paper, shape into desired blocks, and refrigerate to set.
   **Note**: The butter will harden after refrigeration, making it easy to cut into pieces. At this step, an appropriate amount of salt can be added as desired to make salted butter.

## Health Advice
Although homemade butter contains no additives, it is extremely calorie-dense. It is suitable for occasional spreading on bread or for enhancing the flavor of dishes in cooking, but should not be consumed in excess. Butter is rich in saturated fat and vitamin A, making it suitable for individuals needing quick energy replenishment, but those with high blood lipids should moderate their intake.
