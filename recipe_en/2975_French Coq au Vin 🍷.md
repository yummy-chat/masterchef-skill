# French Coq au Vin 🍷
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 2 whole chicken legs  
🧅 1 onion (finely chopped)  
🍄 6-8 white mushrooms (halved)  
🥕 1 carrot (cut into chunks)  
🥓 50-80g bacon (chopped)  
🧄 3-4 cloves garlic (sliced)  
🌿 Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶 Black pepper to taste  
🍷 200-300ml red wine  
🍲 Chicken stock, enough to cover  
🧈 1 small piece of butter  
🌿 2 bay leaves  
🌿 Dried basil to taste  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥘 Dutch oven  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍴 Fork  
🔥 Stove  

## Instructions  
1. Cut each chicken leg into two pieces at the joint. Season both sides evenly with salt and pepper.
   **Key point**: Cutting at the joint makes separation easier.
2. Add a little oil to the frying pan. Place the chicken legs over low heat and sear until golden brown on both sides. Remove from the pan.
   **Note**: Reserve the pan drippings; no need to wash the pan.
3. In the same pan, sauté the chopped bacon until fat renders. Add the butter to melt, then stir in the onion, mushrooms, carrot, and garlic. Cook over medium-low heat until fragrant.
4. Pour in the red wine and bring to a boil briefly to allow the alcohol to evaporate.
5. Return the seared chicken legs to the pan. Add enough chicken stock, salt, black pepper, bay leaves, and dried basil. Stir to combine.
6. Cover tightly with a lid and simmer over low heat for at least 45 minutes, until the chicken is tender and infused with flavor.
7. Before serving, garnish with chopped parsley. Pairs well with rice.
   **Note**: Add the parsley at the end to preserve its fresh aroma.

## Health Advice
This French coq au vin is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, the red wine and bacon add extra calories and sodium, so moderate consumption is recommended. The slow braising process makes the chicken tender and easy to digest, ideal for those with weak digestion. Serve with vegetables to balance the nutrition.
