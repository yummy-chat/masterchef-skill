# Potato Wrapped Shrimp Paste Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato About 2 (sliced into 3mm thick pieces)
🍤 Shrimp paste About 1 box
🥕 Carrot Some (finely diced)
🌽 Sweet corn Some (finely chopped)
🧂 Salt As needed (for marinating potato slices)
🫒 Cooking oil As needed (for spraying)
🌶️ Chili powder As needed (for final seasoning)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating and mixing)
🪥 Toothpicks (for securing rolls)
♨️ Air fryer
🫗 Oil sprayer/oil brush
🍽️ Baking tray/parchment paper (for holding finished product)

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the potato into 3mm thick pieces. Place in a bowl, add salt and mix well. Marinate for 10 minutes until softened. Then rinse with clean water to remove excess salt and drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Potato slices need to be marinated until pliable and bendable without breaking. Ensure they are fully drained after rinsing.
2. Add diced carrot and corn kernels to the shrimp paste and mix evenly.
   **Key point**: Dice vegetables finely to ensure even mixing with the shrimp paste for a consistent texture.
3. Take one softened potato slice, place an appropriate amount of shrimp paste filling in the center, fold it over, and secure by inserting a toothpick through one end to the other.
   **Note**: Avoid overfilling to prevent spillage when folding; the toothpick must be inserted firmly to secure.
4. Place the assembled rolls into the air fryer basket. Spray a uniform layer of cooking oil over the surface.
   **Key point**: Even oil spraying is crucial for a crispy outer layer.
5. Set the air fryer to 180°C and cook for 15 minutes, until the edges of the potato slices are golden brown and the shrimp paste is fully cooked.
6. Remove and sprinkle chili powder while hot. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This dish replaces bread with potato slices for a low-carb, high-satiety option. The shrimp paste paired with corn adds protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss or blood sugar management plan. Be sure the potato slices are thoroughly drained, or they may fall apart during rolling.
