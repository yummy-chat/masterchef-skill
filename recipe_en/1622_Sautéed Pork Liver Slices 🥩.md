# Sautéed Pork Liver Slices 🥩
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons 🌿

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork liver (about ¥11 worth, enough for two servings)
🌶️ Spiral chili peppers, to taste (cut into rolling knife pieces)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (bullet-head variety), to taste
🧄 Garlic, about half a head (sliced, used in two batches)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)
🌿 Green Sichuan peppercorns, a small handful
🧴 Light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little (about half a tablespoon)
🍬 White sugar, a pinch
🍾 Zhenjiang aromatic vinegar, to taste
🧂 Salt, a pinch
💧 Water, about 4 tablespoons
🥔 Potato starch, enough for coating + a small pinch for the sauce
🍯 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🫒 Cooking oil, enough for oil-smoothing method
🍶 Cooking wine (dry yellow wine), to taste
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper, to taste

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥘 Strainers (one large, one small)
🥄 Ladle
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥢 Chopsticks
🍴 Fork
🥄 Small spoon
🥄 Measuring spoon
🌡️ Infrared thermometer (optional, for oil-smoothing method)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1.  Cut the whole piece of pork liver into large strips, split open, and remove the white connective tissue.
- **Key point**: Cut into strips and remove the tissue first to speed up the blood-soaking process.

2.  Rinse the pork liver under cold water twice, then soak in water at about 40°C for 1 hour, changing the water once during this time.
- **Key point**: Must use warm water; cold water is slow to remove blood, while hot water will cook the surface blood, sealing in the smell.

3.  Cut the spiral chili peppers into rolling knife pieces; cut scallions into sections, slice ginger, and cut dried chili peppers into sections. Place some of the chili peppers, scallions, ginger slices, garlic slices, and dried chili pepper sections into one bowl. Add 1 spoon of ground Sichuan pepper. This is the aromatic stir-fry mixture.
- **Key point**: Ground Sichuan pepper is key for removing the smell and must be combined with the aromatic stir-fry mixture.

4.  Prepare the sauce: Place half of the garlic slices into a bowl. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, Zhenjiang aromatic vinegar, salt, water, and finally a small pinch of potato starch. Stir well and set aside.
- **Key point**: Use very little starch in the sauce, as the liver itself is coated; use enough vinegar to taste the fragrance but not the sourness.

5.  Pat the soaked and dried pork liver dry. Cut it into willow-leaf-shaped slices about 3 coins thick. Add 1 spoon of dark soy sauce and mix well to color. Then, add potato starch in batches, mixing until each slice is evenly coated with a thin layer of starch.
- **Key point**: The liver slices should not be too thin; the coating aims to encase the liver with starch, locking in moisture.

### Method 1: Beginner-Friendly
1.  Fill the wok with enough water, add a small handful of green Sichuan peppercorns and an appropriate amount of cooking wine. Bring to a full rolling boil over high heat, then turn off the heat. Gently scatter the coated pork liver slices one by one into the wok without stirring. Let sit for about 15 seconds until most of the liver changes color. Remove with a strainer and drain.
- **Key point**: Adding ingredients with the heat off prevents the coating from falling off; use a strainer instead of chopsticks because the smooth liver is very slippery.

2.  In another wok, heat oil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Add the mixed aromatic stir-fry mixture and stir-fry until fragrant.
- **Key point**: Stir-fry over low heat to prevent the aromatics from burning.

3.  Turn the heat to high. Add the smooth-cooked pork liver. Quickly pour in the prepared sauce. Do not stir immediately; wait for the sauce to come to a boil, then quickly stir-fry until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the liver. Remove from the wok immediately.
- **Key point**: Wait for the sauce to boil before stirring, or the starch will be raw and watery; remove quickly to prevent the liver from overcooking.

### Method 2: Advanced Version
1.  Heat a cast iron wok over high heat to above 350°C. Turn off the heat. Pour in enough cooking oil to coat the bottom of the wok. While the heat is off, add the coated pork liver slices into the center of the oil from close to the wok bottom. Gently push with a wooden spatula. Immediately turn the heat to high and quickly stir-fry until most of the liver changes color. Remove and drain the oil.
- **Key point**: The wok temperature must be high enough; turning off the heat before adding the oil and ingredients is key to preventing oil splatter and loss of coating in a home kitchen; must use a wooden spatula to prevent sticking.

2.  In another wok, stir-fry the mixed aromatic stir-fry mixture over low heat until fragrant. Turn the heat to high. Add the smooth-cooked pork liver. Pour in the sauce. Once the sauce comes to a boil, quickly stir-fry to combine and remove from the wok immediately.

## Health Advice
Pork liver is rich in iron, which helps with blood replenishment, but has a high cholesterol content. It is recommended to pair it with green peppers and garlic, which can promote iron absorption and reduce the smell. Soaking in warm water for 1 hour effectively removes the smell and blood. Suitable for people with anemia, but those with high blood fat should consume in moderation.
