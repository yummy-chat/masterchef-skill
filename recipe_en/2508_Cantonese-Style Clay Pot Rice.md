# Cantonese-Style Clay Pot Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount (washed in advance)
💧 Water, 1:1 ratio to rice by volume
🥓 Chinese sausage, appropriate amount (sliced)
🌽 Corn kernels, appropriate amount
🫘 Green peas, appropriate amount
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount (sliced)
🥦 Broccoli, appropriate amount
🥚 Egg, 1
🐷 Lard, appropriate amount (for greasing the pot and sealing the edges)
🥄 Soy sauce, appropriate amount (for the sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (for the sauce)
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount (for the sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount (for the sauce)
💧 Water, a small amount (for the sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Clay pot
🥄 Wooden spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Metal spoon

## Instructions
1. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and a small amount of water. Stir well and set aside.
2. Grease the inner wall of the clay pot evenly with lard, making sure to coat both the bottom and the sides.
**Key point**: Lard is essential for forming the crispy rice crust.
3. Pour in the washed rice, add water at a 1:1 ratio to the rice by volume. Gently stir to combine, then spread it evenly.
4. Use chopsticks to poke a few ventilation holes in the rice surface. Cover with the lid.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat until the water in the pot is boiling.
6. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, cover, and simmer for 10–15 minutes until the rice is set and the water is mostly absorbed.
**Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning.
7. Quickly open the lid, arrange the sliced Chinese sausage, corn kernels, green peas, shiitake mushroom slices, and broccoli on top. Crack a raw egg into the center.
**Note**: Work quickly to minimize steam loss.
8. Cover the lid again, and drizzle a circle of lard along the edge of the lid to enhance aroma and make the rice at the edges crispier.
9. Continue simmering over the lowest heat for about 5 minutes, until the toppings are cooked and the egg is half-done.
10. Turn off the heat. Do not open the lid. Let it rest for 3–5 minutes using residual heat.
11. Open the lid, drizzle the prepared sauce over, and toss the rice, toppings, and sauce together thoroughly until well combined.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in carbohydrates and fats, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost. However, Chinese sausage and lard are high in salt and fat. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables for a balanced meal.
