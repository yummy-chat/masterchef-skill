# Shredded Potato Wrap
Cuisine: Common home cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato: appropriate amount (shredded)
🥕 Carrot: appropriate amount (shredded)
🥬 Lettuce: appropriate amount (leaves only)
🌿 Cilantro: a little (chopped)
🥚 Egg: 1 (fried into a thin omelet)
🌭 Ham sausage: appropriate amount (sliced)
🍞 Flour: appropriate amount (for making dough and oil paste)
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (for dough and stir-fried filling)
🫗 Cooking oil: appropriate amount (for oil paste, anti-stick, and pan-frying)
🫘 Soybean paste or sweet bean sauce: appropriate amount (for spreading on the wrap)
🌶️ Chili oil: appropriate amount (drizzle to taste)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🤲 Hands (for kneading)
🥖 Rolling pin
🥄 Spoon
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔪 Spatula
🪵 Cutting board
📦 Plastic wrap
🥡 Rectangular stainless steel tray

## Instructions
1. Make the dough: Add a little salt to the flour, then gradually pour in warm water while kneading until a smooth dough forms.
   **Key**: Adding salt enhances the gluten’s elasticity; using warm water makes the dough more pliable.
2. Divide the dough: Cut the dough into 4 equal portions.
3. Prepare the oil paste: Mix flour and hot cooking oil in a 1:1 ratio, stirring until a smooth, lump-free paste forms.
   **Key**: Pouring hot oil into the flour releases a rich aroma and is the foundation for creating a crispy, layered texture.
4. Roll out the dough rounds: Take one portion and roll it into an oval shape. Spread a thin layer of oil paste evenly over it, then fold both sides toward the center. Roll it thin again, spread another layer of oil paste, and fold again.
   **Key**: Repeatedly spreading oil paste and folding is the core technique for achieving multiple layers and a crispy texture in the wrap.
5. Rest the dough: Place all prepared dough rounds in an oiled tray, spray a little oil on the surface, cover with plastic wrap, and let them rest for 30 minutes.
   **Key**: Sufficient resting relaxes the gluten, preventing the dough from shrinking when rolled thin later, resulting in a softer texture.
6. Cook the filling: Heat oil in a non-stick pan. Add the shredded potatoes and carrots, season with salt, and stir-fry until softened and fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
7. Roll out the wrap: Take a rested dough round and roll it out on a cutting board into a very thin circular wrap—the thinner, the better.
8. Pan-fry the wrap: Brush a thin layer of oil on the pan. Place the wrap in the pan and spray a little oil on the surface. Cook over medium-low heat for about 20 seconds until the bottom turns light golden, then flip and cook for another 20 seconds, until both sides are puffed up and show golden brown spots. Remove from the pan.
9. Assemble: Spread a layer of soybean paste or sweet bean sauce evenly over the cooked wrap. Layer the stir-fried potatoes, ham sausage slices, fried egg, chopped green onions, cilantro, and lettuce on top, then drizzle with chili oil.
10. Roll it up: Tightly roll the wrap from the side with the filling. The tighter the roll, the less likely the filling will fall out when sliced. Cut into pieces and serve.

## Health Tips
Shredded potato wraps combine vegetables, protein, and carbohydrates, offering a balanced meal suitable for breakfast or a packed lunch. Be mindful of the amount of soybean paste and chili oil used to avoid excessive sodium and calorie intake.
