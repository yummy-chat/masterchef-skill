# Stir-Fried Pork with Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Spiral chili peppers 3-4 (thin-skinned, thick-fleshed, cut into rolling knife pieces)
🥩 Pork belly, as needed (separate lean and fatty parts, slice thinly)
🫚 Ginger 7-8 slices
🧄 Garlic several cloves (crushed, soaked in clean water)
🫘 Fermented black beans, as needed
🧅 Green garlic, as needed (cut into sections)
🌿 Angelica dahurica 1 slice (enhances aroma and reduces greasiness)
🧴 Dark soy sauce, as needed (for marinating lean meat and seasoning during stir-frying)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for dry-frying chili peppers and seasoning during stir-frying)
🧴 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍬 White sugar, a pinch (for balancing flavors and enhancing freshness)
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, a little (drizzle along the wok edge before serving)
🛢️ Rapeseed oil, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Chinese wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Bowl (for soaking garlic and marinating meat)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the spiral chili peppers and cut into rolling knife pieces. Slice the ginger thinly. Crush the garlic cloves and soak in clean water to prevent bitterness after stir-frying. Separate the fatty and lean parts of the pork belly and slice each thinly. Marinate the lean meat with dark soy sauce and mix well. Cut the green garlic into sections and prepare the fermented black beans.
**Key Point**: Pre-marinating the lean meat enhances its base flavor; soaking the garlic prevents excessive charring and bitterness at high heat.

2. Heat the wok over medium heat without oil, add the chili pepper pieces. Dry-fry, pressing with the back of a spatula until the peppers soften and the skin wrinkles. Add a pinch of salt to aid in moisture removal. Once done, remove and set aside.
**Key Point**: Dry-frying the peppers is a "dehydration and cooking" step that removes raw taste and imparts a smoky flavor.

3. Pour rapeseed oil into the wok, add one slice of angelica dahurica when the oil is slightly warm. Fry over low heat until it turns light yellow and releases its aroma, then remove.
**Key Point**: Angelica dahurica enhances the aroma and reduces greasiness, but control the heat to avoid burning.

4. Add the fatty pork slices. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until they render fat and the edges curl slightly.
**Key Point**: "Fatty meat brings out umami". Full rendering ensures a rich yet non-greasy texture in the final dish.

5. Turn the heat to high. Add the marinated lean meat slices. Quickly stir-fry until they change color.
6. Add the soaked garlic, ginger slices, and fermented black beans. Stir-fry over high heat to release their fragrance.
7. Add the dry-fried chili peppers. Season with salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and white sugar. Quickly stir-fry until well combined.
8. Add the green garlic sections and stir-fry for a few seconds. Drizzle a little fragrant vinegar along the wok edge, stir-fry evenly, and serve immediately.
**Key Point**: Green garlic should remain crisp-tender. The vinegar along the wok edge instantly releases its aroma, elevating the overall flavor.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in vitamin C from the chili peppers and provides quality fat from the pork belly. Be mindful of rice intake when eating. Avoid over-drying the pork belly; keeping it moderately oily results in a better texture.
