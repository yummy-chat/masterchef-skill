# Shredded Pork with Dried Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## 🥩 Ingredients
- Shredded pork tenderloin
- Dried tofu (xiang gan)
- Green pepper (recommended spiral pepper)
- Garlic

## 🧂 Seasonings
- Salt
- Light soy sauce
- Cornstarch
- Chicken bouillon

## 🍳 Cookware
- Wok
- Spatula

## 📝 Steps
1. Add 3ml of light soy sauce and 5g of cornstarch to the shredded pork tenderloin. Mix well and set aside to marinate.
2. Wash the green pepper, cut it into chunks using a rolling cut method, and set aside.
3. Slice the garlic horizontally, and shred the dried tofu.
4. Mix cornstarch with 10ml of water to create a cornstarch slurry.
5. Add 15ml of oil to the wok. Add the marinated shredded pork to the cold oil, gently separate it with a spatula, and once the pork changes color and is cooked through, immediately remove it. Leave the remaining oil in the wok.
6. Add the sliced garlic and shredded dried tofu to the wok, then add 2ml of light soy sauce. Stir-fry evenly.
7. Continue stir-frying for 2–3 minutes, then add the shredded green pepper, stir-frying together.
8. After stir-frying for about 1 minute, return the shredded pork to the wok and mix it evenly with the other ingredients.
9. Pour in the cornstarch slurry to thicken, then add 3g of salt and 2g of chicken bouillon. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens and the ingredients are fully cooked. Remove from heat and serve.

**⚠️ Notes**
- Different woks and stove heat levels can affect stir-frying time. It is recommended to taste-test to check for doneness.
- If you prefer a spicy flavor, you can add chopped bird's eye chili peppers along with the green pepper when stir-frying.

## Health Tips
This shredded pork with dried tofu is a high-quality combination of protein and vegetables. Pork tenderloin is low in fat, and dried tofu is rich in calcium, making it suitable for people looking to build muscle or lose fat. Be mindful to control the amount of cornstarch used for thickening; you can omit the cornstarch slurry if you are trying to lose weight. The green pepper should remain crisp and tender to retain more vitamins.
