# Braised Chicken with Noodles (Dapanji)

Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 free-range chicken (cut into pieces)  
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (cut into rolling chunks)  
🫑 Green bell peppers, as needed (seeded and cut into chunks)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (cut into chunks)  
🌾 500g all-purpose flour (for kneading dough)  
💧 460g water (for kneading dough)  
🧂 6g salt (for kneading dough) + more to taste (for marinating and seasoning)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🫘 Broad bean paste (Doubanjiang), as needed  
⭐ 3 star anise  
🌿 1 piece cinnamon  
🍃 4 bay leaves  
🌸 10g Sichuan peppercorns  
🌰 2 tsao-ko (black cardamom)  
🫚 2g galangal  
🌱 3 pieces sand ginger (Kaempferia galanga)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🍬 Sugar, as needed (for caramelizing)  
💧 Water, as needed (for braising)  
🧂 MSG, as needed  
🌫️ Ground white pepper, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍽️ Plates  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the all-purpose flour with 6g salt. Gradually add 460g water, stirring until it forms a flaky texture, then knead into a smooth dough until the bowl, dough, and hands are all clean.
2. Roll the dough into a thick long log, brush the surface with soybean oil to prevent drying, then divide into evenly sized small pieces—some rolled into 15cm lengths, some into 50-60cm lengths. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 40 minutes.
3. Cut the chicken into pieces, add ginger slices, salt, and cooking wine. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes.
4. Cut the potatoes into rolling chunks, seed the green bell peppers and cut them into chunks, cut the onion into chunks.
5. Heat a small amount of oil in a pan, add sugar, and cook over low heat until melted. Stir continuously until the sugar syrup is covered with foam, then immediately add a suitable amount of water to make caramel color; set aside.
6. In another pan, heat a generous amount of oil over high heat. Add the broad bean paste and stir-fry until the red oil is released. Then add star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, tsao-ko, galangal, sand ginger, dried chili sections, scallions, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant.
7. Add the marinated chicken pieces and stir-fry continuously until the skin shrinks and the surface is slightly dry.
8. Add the prepared caramel and stir-fry evenly. Pour in enough hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Season with salt, MSG, and white pepper. When the chicken is about 70-80% cooked, add the potato chunks and continue braising.
9. When the potatoes are tender and the sauce has thickened, add the onion and green bell peppers. Stir-fry over high heat until just cooked through.
10. Making and boiling the noodles:
   - Take a long dough piece, brush with oil, hold both ends, and stretch and slap it to thin it out into a thin noodle.
   - Take a long dough piece, flatten it, roll it out with a rolling pin, then stretch and slap it into a wide noodle.
   - Take a thick dough piece, flatten and roll it wide, then stretch it into a wide, thin belt shape.
   Cook the prepared noodles in boiling water until done, then drain.
11. Place the noodles on a plate, ladle the braised chicken and sauce over them, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice
The toppings for Dapanji are rich in various spices. Potatoes provide carbohydrates, and chicken offers high-quality protein. Eating it with noodles provides a balanced nutritional profile. However, portion control is important, as the noodles and potatoes are high in carbohydrates. It is suitable for moderate consumption after exercise or when needing an energy boost.
