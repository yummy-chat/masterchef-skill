# Wind and Rain in Harmony 🌿
Cuisine: Anhui Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Dried vermicelli noodles, one handful
🥬 Cilantro, a small amount (for garnish)
🌶️ Chili pepper rings, a small amount (optional)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Use the rich leftover broth from making the New Year's Eve one-pot meal, keeping it in the wok.
**Note**: The broth already contains soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, MSG, and other seasonings; if hot pot base was added earlier, it will have a spicy flavor.

2. Without adding extra water, place the dried vermicelli noodles directly into the broth in the wok, and heat over medium heat.
**Key Point**: Stir the noodles occasionally after adding them to the wok to prevent sticking.

3. Cook until the vermicelli noodles have fully absorbed the broth, becoming soft, tender, and translucent, then remove and plate.
**Key Point**: Do not overcook; retaining a slightly chewy texture is better.

4. Sprinkle with a small amount of cilantro and chili pepper rings for aroma and garnish according to personal preference, and serve hot.

## Health Advice
Dried vermicelli noodles absorb the rich, complex broth, providing carbohydrates and some protein, making them suitable for a quick energy boost. However, the broth is high in oil and sodium, so consumption should be controlled.
