# Spring Tulip Flower Pastry 🍃🌸
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 55g (for water-oil dough)
🌾 All-purpose flour 36g (for oil dough)
🧈 Softened butter 15g (for water-oil dough)
🧈 Softened butter 18g (for oil dough)
🍬 Granulated sugar 10g
💧 Water 25g
🥮 Custard filling, as needed (pre-rolled into balls)
🎨 Food coloring (pink, green), as needed

## Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🤖 Stand mixer
🫓 Rolling pin
🔪 Bench scraper
📦 Plastic wrap
🍳 Baking tray with parchment paper
🔥 Oven
🪥 Toothpick
🔪 Sharp paring knife

## Steps
1. **Make the water-oil dough**: Combine all-purpose flour (for water-oil dough), softened butter, granulated sugar, and water. Knead with a stand mixer until the dough can stretch into a thin film (full gluten development).
2. **Make the oil dough**: Mix all-purpose flour (for oil dough) with softened butter. Use your hands to pinch and blend until no dry flour remains.
3. **Color and divide**:
   - Divide the oil dough into two portions. Keep about 2/3 of it natural (white). Add pink food coloring to the remaining 1/3 and mix evenly.
   - From the water-oil dough, separate a 12–15g piece. Add green food coloring and knead until even to make the green dough.
   - Divide the remaining water-oil dough, natural oil dough, and pink oil dough each into 6 equal pieces.
   **Note**: The pink color can be a bit stronger, as it will lighten during baking.
4. **First round of wrapping and rolling**:
   - Take one piece of water-oil dough and roll it out flat. Divide the pink oil dough into 3 portions and the natural oil dough into 2 portions. Arrange them on the water-oil dough in the order: “pink – white – pink – white – pink”. Wrap and seal the dough.
   - Roll it into a long tongue-shaped (oval) sheet. This arrangement is designed to create a gradient effect.
5. **First roll**: Roll the sheet up lengthwise to form a long cylinder. The cross-section should now show a spiral pattern of pink and white. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest briefly.
6. **Second round of rolling**:
   - Place the cylinder seam-side up, flatten it, and roll it into a narrow rectangular sheet.
   - Fold the ends of the sheet toward the center, then fold in half again.
   - After folding, roll it out into a round disc of suitable size. If it shrinks back significantly, let it rest briefly before rolling again.
7. **Filling and shaping**:
   - Place one portion of custard filling in the center of the disc. Wrap it tightly and shape into a ball.
   - Take a piece of the green dough, roll it into a teardrop shape, and attach it to the sealed seam as the flower base (calyx).
   - Gently shape the entire piece into a tulip bud form—pointed at one end and round at the other.
8. **Freeze and score**: Freeze all shaped pieces for about ten minutes, until the surface is firm. Remove from the freezer. Use a sharp paring knife to cut a “Y” or cross-shaped opening at the top.
   **Note**: The pieces must be frozen solid before scoring, or they will collapse and deform.
9. **Bake**: Place the scored pieces in a preheated oven and bake.
   **Tip**: Since this recipe uses butter for the oil dough, the layers may not be as distinct as with lard, and the result may feel slightly “merged,” but it will still be crisp overall.

## Health Advice
Tulip flower pastries contain butter and sugar, making them relatively high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy 1–2 pieces per serving, paired with unsweetened tea for balance. This is a great recipe for a parent-child baking activity, but be mindful of the food coloring amount. Pregnant women and children should consume in moderation.
