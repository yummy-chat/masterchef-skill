# Candied Sweet Potatoes (拔丝地瓜)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍠 Red-skinned, yellow-fleshed sweet potatoes – 500g
🌽 Cornstarch – 10g
🍬 White sugar – 130g
💧 Water – 110g
🥃 White vinegar – 4g
🫒 Cooking oil – 8g (for making the syrup), plus about 1500g of oil for deep-frying

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Peeler, chef's knife, cutting board
🥘 Soup pot (for blanching)
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for cooling and coating)
🍳 Wok (for deep-frying and making syrup)
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🥄 Spoon (for stirring syrup)
🍽️ Serving plate

## Steps
1. Wash and peel the sweet potatoes, then cut into irregular chunks about 3 cm × 5 cm.
**Note**: Do not cut them too small, or the candied effect will be compromised.
2. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the sweet potato chunks and blanch until the water returns to a boil, then immediately remove them.
**Key**: Blanching reduces frying time and prevents the edges from burning.
3. Immediately transfer the blanched sweet potatoes to cold water to cool, then drain thoroughly.
**Key**: They must be cooled completely; otherwise, residual heat will cook the starch when coating.
4. Place the sweet potato chunks in a bowl. Add the cornstarch in two batches, shaking the bowl to coat each piece evenly with a thin layer.
**Key**: Add in batches for a light, even coating.
5. Heat enough oil in a wok to 150°C (300°F). Add the coated sweet potato chunks and fry over high heat.
**Note**: Do not stir for the first 10 seconds after adding them—allow the chunks to set.
6. Once set, gently loosen them with a slotted spoon. Reduce to medium heat and slowly raise the oil temperature back to 150°C (300°F). Fry until the sweet potatoes are golden, crispy on the outside, and cooked through inside. Remove and drain.
**Key**: No second frying is needed; this ensures a crisp exterior and soft, sticky interior.
7. In another wok, combine the sugar, water, white vinegar, and cooking oil. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly. The syrup will go through four stages: large bubbles → small bubbles → white foam → brown foam. Once the syrup turns a sesame-oil color with fine brown foam, immediately turn off the heat.
8. Quickly add the fried sweet potatoes and toss rapidly to coat each piece evenly with syrup, then immediately transfer to a serving plate.
**Note**: Work fast to prevent the syrup from cooling and hardening before coating is complete.

## Health Advice
Candied sweet potatoes are rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene, making them suitable for those with low energy when consumed in moderation. However, due to frying and the sugar syrup, this dish is high in calories. People with high blood sugar should eat it with caution.
