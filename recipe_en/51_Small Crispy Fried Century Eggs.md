# Small Crispy Fried Century Eggs  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Century eggs 5 pieces  
🌾 Flour as needed (one portion for coating, one portion for thickening the sauce)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 3 tbsp  
🧂 Dark soy sauce as needed (for coloring)  
🫗 Black vinegar 2 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🍬 Sugar 2 tbsp  
💧 Water as needed  
🧄 Minced garlic 1 tbsp  
🌶️ Chopped small chili peppers 1 tbsp  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
♨️ Steamer  
🧺 Fine mesh strainer  
🥢 Wooden chopsticks  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Prepare the base sauce: In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, black vinegar, oyster sauce, sugar, and add an appropriate amount of water. Stir well and set aside.  
2. Steam the century eggs: Place the unpeeled century eggs in a steamer. Once steam rises, steam for 5–6 minutes.  
   **Note**: Must steam before cutting. Runny-centered eggs are prone to falling apart if cut directly; steaming solidifies the yolk, allowing for neat cuts.  
3. Cool and cut: Immediately transfer the steamed eggs to ice water to cool. Peel, then cut in half or into quarters.  
4. Coat with flour: Place the cut egg pieces into flour, ensuring an even, dry coating on all sides.  
   **Key**: The coating must be even; this is the foundation for the crispy exterior.  
5. Thicken the sauce: Pour the prepared sauce into a wok and bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low. Gradually add dry flour in small amounts while stirring quickly, until the sauce becomes very thick and forms threads when lifted with a spatula.  
   **Key**: Strain the thickened sauce through a fine mesh strainer into a bowl for a smooth texture. Then add minced garlic and chopped small chili peppers, stir to combine, and set aside.  
6. Fry: Add oil to the wok and heat over medium heat until dense bubbles form around the tip of a dry wooden chopstick inserted. Add the flour-coated egg pieces.  
   Fry for less than a minute, until the surface is golden and crispy, then remove.  
   **Note**: Frying time is short; remove immediately once the surface is crispy to avoid affecting texture.  
7. Plate and pour sauce: Arrange the fried eggs on a plate and evenly pour the prepared garlic-chili thickened sauce over them.  
   **Tip**: Serve immediately after pouring the sauce to maintain the crispy texture.  

## Health Advice  
Steaming the century eggs before frying can effectively reduce the viscosity of the runny yolk and lower the risk of lead intake, making this a suitable occasional treat. The short frying time helps control oil absorption, but the sauce has a relatively high sugar content. For individuals with high blood sugar, it is recommended to reduce the sugar by half.
