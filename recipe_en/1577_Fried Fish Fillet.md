# Fried Fish Fillet
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Boneless fish fillet, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for both marinating and batter)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (for both marinating and batter)
🍶 Cooking wine, a small amount
🥚 Egg white, a small amount (for marinating)
🌶️ Black pepper, a small amount
🌾 Cake flour, 90 grams
🌽 Cornstarch, 15 grams
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, 8 grams
🌽 Cornstarch, 8 grams
🧁 Baking powder, 4-5 grams
💧 Water, about 120 grams
🫒 Cooking oil, about 10 grams (for batter)
🥚 Whole egg, 1
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for coating fish fillet)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Spoon
🔲 Strainer
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔥 Portable gas stove

## Steps
1.  Prepare the crispy batter: Place cake flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and sugar in a bowl and mix well. Then add glutinous rice flour, cornstarch, and the whole egg in sequence, stirring until no dry flour remains. Gradually add water in batches, stirring continuously to form a smooth, medium-thick liquid batter. Finally, add about 10 grams of cooking oil and stir until fully absorbed.
   **Note**: Add water in batches to avoid making the batter too thin; add the cooking oil last to prevent the batter from "blooming" during frying.
2.  Marinate the fish fillet: Add a pinch of salt, sugar, cooking wine, egg white, and black pepper to the fish fillet. Mix well and marinate for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate.
3.  Prepare for coating: Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 40% hot (around 140°C / 284°F). First, evenly coat the marinated fish fillet with a thin layer of cornstarch, then dip it into the crispy batter, ensuring the surface is fully covered.
   **Key point**: Coating with dry starch first helps the batter adhere better and prevents it from falling off.
4.  First frying for setting: Gently lower the battered fish fillets one by one into the oil. Fry over medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes until the surface hardens and turns light yellow. Remove and place on a strainer to drain excess oil.
   **Key point**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid the outside burning while the inside remains raw.
5.  Second frying for extra crispiness: Raise the oil temperature to 60-70% hot (around 170-180°C / 338-356°F). Return the fish fillets to the oil and fry for about 10 seconds until the coating is golden and crispy. Immediately remove and drain off any excess oil.
   **Note**: The second frying time is very short; the main purpose is to force out internal oil and enhance crispiness.
6.  Serve: Serve hot with dipping sauces such as ketchup or salt and pepper.

## Health Advice
Fried fish fillet is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Due to the double frying, it is relatively high in calories and is best enjoyed occasionally. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to balance the oil intake and control portions. This dish is especially suitable for those needing to supplement protein but preferring smaller, more frequent meals.
