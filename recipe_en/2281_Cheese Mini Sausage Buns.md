# Cheese Mini Sausage Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 350g
🌾 Whole wheat flour 150g
💧 Water 385g
🍞 Low-sugar tolerant dry yeast 5g
🧂 Salt 6g
🌭 Black pepper sausages 10 (pre-cooked)
🥚 Whole egg liquid, as needed (for brushing surface)
🧀 Cheese powder about 18g (mixed 1:1 with corn flour)
🌽 Corn flour about 18g (mixed 1:1 with cheese powder)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
⚖️ Electronic scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl or plastic bag
🔪 Dough scraper
🪚 Rolling pin
🖌️ Brush
🍳 Baking tray
🔥 Convection oven (or home flat oven)
⏱️ Timer
🍴 Bread knife
🛡️ Plastic bag or plastic wrap

## Steps
1. Mix bread flour, whole wheat flour, and salt on the work surface. Make a well in the center, pour in water, and add yeast. Use a dough scraper to gradually incorporate the surrounding flour into the water, mixing until a dough forms.
   - Key point: The dough has no sugar or oil; first allow the water to be fully absorbed by the flour to increase thickness and prevent liquid from spilling.

2. Throw the dough onto the work surface until it becomes relatively smooth on the surface. Spread it out, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
   - Judgment: The dough will be slightly sticky; throwing it reduces stickiness during subsequent kneading. Resting is crucial for gluten development.

3. Stretch the rested dough, flatten it, fold from the edges toward the center, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for another half hour.
   - Key point: Ensure the dough is stretched before folding to guarantee full folding effect.

4. Repeat the stretching, flattening, and folding process once. The dough should now be smoother and less sticky. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest.

5. Shape the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let it undergo the first fermentation at room temperature for about 60 minutes.
   - Judgment: Dip your finger in flour and poke a hole in the dough; if the hole does not shrink back or springs back slightly, the fermentation is complete.

6. Flatten the fermented dough on the work surface with your hand to release gas. Divide it evenly into 10 portions. Round each portion, cover with plastic wrap, and let them rest for 25 minutes.
   - Judgment: Dip your finger in flour and press a small dough piece; if an indentation remains and does not spring back too quickly, the rest is sufficient.

7. Take one small dough piece and roll it into an oval shape. Flip it over, place a sausage on top, and gently roll it up from one end, pinching the seam to seal.
   - Key point: Do not roll the dough too wide or too long; just enough to wrap the sausage and roll it twice.

8. Brush the top of the rolled dough strip with whole egg liquid, then roll it in the mixture of cheese powder and corn flour, leaving the bottom uncoated. Cut into 4 equal pieces with a knife.
   - Key point: Leaving the bottom uncoated saves ingredients and ensures a neat appearance.

9. Arrange the cut bun pieces on a baking tray with space between them. Let them undergo final fermentation at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

10. Place in a preheated oven and bake. For a convection oven, bake at 190°C for about 12 minutes. For a home flat oven, recommend top heat at 210°C and bottom heat at 180°C, baking for about 10 minutes.
    - Key point: The dough has no sugar, so it browns lightly; bake at high temperature quickly. Remove from oven when the surface is lightly browned.

## Health Advice
This cheese mini sausage bun combines whole wheat flour with bread flour, increasing dietary fiber. It is suitable for those who need to control blood sugar or seek a feeling of fullness. However, sausages and cheese powder are high in sodium. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition.
