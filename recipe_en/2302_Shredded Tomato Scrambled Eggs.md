# Shredded Tomato Scrambled Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 Tomatoes 3 (about 650g, choose ones with white spots on the surface for easy mashing; cut 1 into wedges and 2 into large dices, set aside separately)
🥚 Eggs 5
🧄 Garlic 25g (about 6 cloves, minced; half for sautéing, half for finishing)
🌿 Cilantro or Scallions 15g (minced)
🥔 Potato Starch 5g
💧 Water 20g
🍬 Sugar About 5g (half a bottle spoon)
🧂 Light Soy Sauce 10g (one spoon)
🦪 Oyster Sauce 20g (one heaping spoon)
🍅 Ketchup 40g (two spoons)
🧂 MSG 2g
🧂 Salt 2-3g (one small teaspoon)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls (at least 3)
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Wok spatula
🫗 Fine mesh strainer
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. **Process the tomatoes**: Cut 1 tomato into wedges, and the other 2 into large dices. Place them in two separate bowls.
2. **Prepare the Aromatics**: Mince the garlic, and mince the cilantro or scallions. Set aside.
3. **Make the Slurry**: In a bowl, combine potato starch and water. Stir until no lumps remain. Make sure to use potato starch.
4. **Prepare the Egg Mixture**: Crack the eggs into a bowl. Whisk vigorously with chopsticks until smooth and uniform.
5. **Flash-Fry the Eggs**: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok. Heat over high heat until the oil surface shimmers with white smoke. Hold the egg bowl high with one hand and chopsticks with the other. Pour the egg mixture in a thin, steady stream from height into the oil, while quickly stirring in circular motions with the chopsticks. Once the eggs are in the oil, do not move them immediately. Let them puff up and float, then use the chopsticks to break them into tender pieces. Immediately remove them with a strainer, gently press to drain excess oil, and set aside. The oil used for flash-frying the eggs can be filtered through a fine mesh strainer and reserved for other cooking.
6. **Make the Tomato Sauce**:
   - Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. **Turn off the heat**. Add half the minced garlic and stir-fry using the residual heat until fragrant.
   - Once the garlic aroma is released, add the tomato dices. **Turn the heat to high** and stir-fry until soft and broken down, releasing their juices. Add the sugar.
   - When the liquid starts bubbling, reduce the heat to **medium-high**. Continue to simmer until most of the tomato dices have broken down into a thick sauce.
   - Reduce the heat to **the lowest setting**. Add the light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and ketchup. Stir to combine. Then **return the heat to high** and bring the sauce to a boil.
7. **Combine Ingredients and Thicken**: Once the sauce is boiling, add the reserved tomato wedges and the cooked eggs. Stir to combine evenly. Season with salt and MSG. Give the starch slurry another stir, then pour it in along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens and becomes stringy.
8. **Plate and Serve**: Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in the remaining minced garlic and cilantro. Toss briefly using residual heat to combine. Serve immediately.

## Health Notes
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene, which pairs well with egg protein for nutritional complementarity. However, the garlicky oyster sauce-based sauce results in a high sodium content; those with high blood pressure should halve the amount of salt. The flash-frying method for the eggs yields a tender texture but is higher in calories; the acidity of the tomatoes can help cut the richness. Be mindful of portion sizes.
