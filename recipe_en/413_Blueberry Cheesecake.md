# Blueberry Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍌 Bananas (about 3, peeled and mashed)
🌾 Instant oatmeal (to mix with banana mash)
🥚 Eggs (2)
🥛 Milk (to mix with eggs and yogurt)
🍦 Yogurt (as needed)
🫐 Frozen blueberries (as needed)
🍬 Sugar (as needed, for blueberry sauce)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan (as mixing container)
🥮 Round baking pan/cake mold
📄 Parchment paper
🔥 Oven
🔍 Strainer
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Instructions
1.  **Make the crust:** Mash the bananas and mix with instant oatmeal. Use your hands to combine until it forms a moist, moldable paste. Line the mold with parchment paper, pour in the mixture, and press firmly to flatten it against the bottom and sides to form a crust shell.
    **Key point:** The stickiness of the banana mash is essential for shaping. Press firmly to prevent crumbling.

2.  **Pre-bake the crust:** Place in a preheated oven and bake until the crust is set and crisp. Remove and let cool.

3.  **Make the custard filling:** In a clean bowl, whisk the eggs. Gradually add the milk and yogurt, stirring until you have a smooth, light yellow liquid.

4.  **Strain into the mold:** Place a strainer over the cooled crust. Slowly pour the custard mixture through the strainer into the mold, filling it about 80% full.
    **Key point:** Straining removes egg strands and lumps, ensuring a smooth and delicate texture for the filling.

5.  **Bake the cake:** Place back into the oven and bake until the filling is set. Remove and let cool completely, or refrigerate.

6.  **Make the blueberry sauce:** In a pot, combine the frozen blueberries and sugar. Heat over the stove, using a slotted spoon to mash the blueberries as they cook, extracting the juice. Continue cooking until the sauce thickens and turns a deep purplish-red.

7.  **Assemble:** Once the cake is completely cooled, spread the blueberry sauce evenly over the top. Refrigerate for a while to allow the flavors to meld and the structure to set.

## Health Tips
This blueberry cheesecake uses a banana-oatmeal crust, reducing the calories from a traditional butter crust, making it suitable for those who want a low-fat option but don't want to give up dessert. Be mindful to control the amount of sugar in the blueberry sauce and ensure the crust is firmly pressed to prevent breakage.
