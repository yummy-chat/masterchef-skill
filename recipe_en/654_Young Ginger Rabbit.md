# Young Ginger Rabbit  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐇 Rabbit 1 (cleaned, cut into small pieces)  
🫚 Young ginger large amount (julienned, added in two portions)  
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili peppers to taste (sliced into rings)  
🌶️ Xiaomila chili peppers to taste (sliced into rings)  
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (cut into large pieces)  
🧅 Scallion 1 (cut into sections)  
🥚 Egg 1 (whole egg for marinating)  
🧂 Salt to taste (added separately for marinating and seasoning)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste (to enhance freshness)  
🧂 White pepper powder to taste (to remove gaminess and add aroma)  
🥄 Light soy sauce about 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Oyster sauce about 1 tablespoon  
🌾 Cornstarch to taste (for coating and locking in juices)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil generous amount (for sliding and stir-frying)  
🟢 Green Sichuan pepper to taste (to bloom for numbing aroma)  
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 tablespoon (to stir-fry until red oil emerges)  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil (mountain pepper oil) to taste (drizzled before serving)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥘 Stainless steel tray

## Instructions
1. Rinse the whole rabbit clean, carefully remove any surface hairs, repeatedly wash with water to remove blood, first chop into large pieces, then cut into evenly sized small pieces.  
2. Place the rabbit pieces in a bowl, add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and crack in a whole egg. Thoroughly mix by hand until the meat becomes sticky and tacky to the touch.  
3. Add cornstarch and continue mixing evenly, ensuring each piece of rabbit is coated in a thin slurry.  
4. Julienne the young ginger into thin strips. Slice Erjingtiao and Xiaomila chili peppers into rings. Cut garlic into large pieces, and scallions into sections. Set aside.  
5. Pour generous oil into the wok and heat to 60% of max temperature. Slide in the rabbit pieces in batches, quickly separate them with a slotted spoon, and cook over medium-high heat for about 20 seconds until the rabbit turns white and sets. Remove and drain the oil.  
**Note**: Do not slide the rabbit for too long, or the meat will become tough.  
6. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Over medium heat, bloom the green Sichuan pepper, then add the garlic pieces and scallion sections, stirring until fragrant.  
7. Add one tablespoon of doubanjiang and stir-fry until red oil emerges.  
8. Add half of the young ginger strips and stir-fry together with the base ingredients until the ginger aroma is released.  
9. Return the slid rabbit pieces to the wok, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry for about 2 minutes.  
10. Add the remaining young ginger strips, all of the Erjingtiao and Xiaomila chili peppers, and quickly stir-fry until evenly combined, keeping the ginger strips and peppers crisp and vibrant.  
11. Adjust flavor with a little salt and chicken bouillon.  
12. Before serving, drizzle in an appropriate amount of Sichuan pepper oil, quickly toss to combine, and plate. The Sichuan pepper oil must be added last to prevent the aroma from dissipating.

## Health Advice
Rabbit meat is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for weight loss and muscle building. However, the sliding step adds extra calories, so it is recommended to pair it with cold cucumber salad to balance the greasiness. The ginger strips and chili peppers help stimulate appetite and dispel dampness, promoting digestion when consumed in summer. Be mindful of the salt amount to avoid water retention.
