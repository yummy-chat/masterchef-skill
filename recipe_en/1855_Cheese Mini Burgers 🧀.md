# Cheese Mini Burgers 🧀
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef, about 500g
🍞 Small dinner rolls, 3-4 pieces (chopped, to be mixed into the meat)
🥛 Milk, as needed (to soak the chopped rolls)
🍅 Tomatoes, 2 pieces (sliced)
🧀 Cheese slices, 4 slices
🧀 Shredded cheese, as needed
🫘 Black sesame seeds, a little
🍶 Soy sauce (dark soy sauce/oyster sauce), as needed (for coloring the meat)
🍅 Ketchup, as needed
🥗 Mayonnaise, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Spatula (for stirring and flipping)
🔥 Oven
🖌️ Brush (for brushing oil)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the chopped small dinner roll pieces into a bowl, pour in milk to soak until soft and mushy, then mash them by hand.
2.  Add the ground beef and an appropriate amount of soy sauce. Stir vigorously in one direction until the meat mixture becomes sticky and firm.
    **Note**: Achieving this sticky consistency is key to juicy patties; you must stir until the meat is noticeably tacky.
3.  Take the bottom halves of the small dinner rolls. Squeeze on ketchup and mayonnaise, then arrange the tomato slices on top. Set aside.
4.  Divide the meat mixture evenly into several portions. Shape them into round patties similar in size to the dinner rolls.
5.  Add a little cooking oil to a frying pan over medium-low heat. Pan-fry the patties until both sides are golden brown and the inside is fully cooked through.
6.  Place the cooked patties on top of the tomato slices. Add a slice of cheese and some shredded cheese in order, then finally cover with the top half of the dinner roll.
7.  Brush a layer of cooking oil onto the surface of the burgers. Sprinkle evenly with black sesame seeds.
8.  Place the burgers into a preheated oven and bake until the surface is golden brown and the cheese is melted and stretchy. Remove from the oven.

## Health Advice
These mini cheese burgers are rich in protein and calcium, making them suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, the meat filling and cheese are relatively high in calories, so it's best to pair them with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Using small dinner rolls soaked in milk results in a soft texture, making them suitable for children or those with weaker teeth.
