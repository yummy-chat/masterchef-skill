# Bishan Rabbit

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Fresh rabbit meat, about 4 jin (2 kg), chopped into thumb-sized pieces
🍺 Beer, 1 bottle (used for marinating and cooking)
🥚 Egg, 1 (egg white only)
🌾 Sweet potato starch, as needed
🌶️ Pickled chilies, about 150 g (mixed green and red pickled chilies)
🫚 Pickled ginger, about 50 g
🧄 Garlic, about 20 g
🍈 Winter melon, about 500 g (peeled and seeded, cut into 1 cm cubes)
🟢 Green Sichuan pepper, about 5 g (used in cooking and for final topping and oil drizzle)
🔴 Red Sichuan pepper, about 5 g (used in boiling and for final oil drizzle)
🌶️ Dried chilies, about 30 g (cut into segments)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌫️ White pepper powder, to taste
🫒 Rapeseed oil, generous amount (heated for sealing, sliding oil, stir-frying, and final oil drizzle)
🧈 Lard, 1 tablespoon
🥄 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste), about 1 tablespoon
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, to taste
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🥄 Soy sauce, to taste
🍶 Vinegar, a tiny amount (to remove gamey taste and cut grease)
🧅 Scallions, chopped, as needed
🌿 Cilantro, chopped, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating)
🍳 Wok (for sliding oil, stir-frying, braising, and making drizzle oil)
🥄 Spatula (for stirring and seasoning)
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Chop the whole fresh rabbit into thumb-sized pieces. Rinse thoroughly in clean water to remove blood, then squeeze out excess water.
2. Marinate: Add salt and white pepper to the rabbit. Pour in most of the beer and mix well. Add egg white and mix, then sprinkle sweet potato starch and toss until sticky. Finally, drizzle in 1 tablespoon of heated rapeseed oil to seal, mix well, and let marinate.
3. Finely chop the pickled chilies, pickled ginger, and garlic together. Peel and seed the winter melon, then cut into approximately 1 cm cubes. Set aside.
4. Pour a generous amount of rapeseed oil into the wok and heat to about 40% hot (low heat). Gradually slide the marinated rabbit pieces into the oil in small batches along the side of the wok. Gently loosen with a spatula.
**Key Point**: This step is for sliding oil to set the shape and keep the meat tender. The oil temperature should not be too high, and the time should be short. Once the rabbit changes color and sets, immediately remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.

5. Leave a base oil in the wok. Add 1 tablespoon of lard and 1 tablespoon of rapeseed oil, and heat together. Add a small handful of green Sichuan pepper and a small handful of red Sichuan pepper, stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the dried chili segments and stir-fry until they change color and release aroma.
6. Add the chopped pickled chilies, pickled ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant. Add 1 tablespoon of doubanjiang, and stir-fry over low to medium heat until the red oil and aroma are released.
7. Pour in the remaining beer. If the liquid is not enough, add a small amount of water. Bring the broth to a boil and season: add MSG, chicken bouillon powder, white pepper, a pinch of sugar, soy sauce for umami, and finally a "tiny bit" of vinegar to remove gamey taste and cut grease.
8. First, add the winter melon cubes. Simmer on low heat until the winter melon becomes tender but still holds its shape.
**Note**: Adjust the cooking time of the winter melon based on heat and cube size. It is ready when easily pierced with chopsticks but not mushy or falling apart.

9. Add the slid oil rabbit pieces. Continue to braise for about 4–5 minutes, allowing the rabbit to absorb the broth. When the broth reduces to about half and is bubbling, remove from heat.
10. Pour the entire dish (broth and ingredients) into a large bowl. Sprinkle a layer of ground red Sichuan pepper and a layer of ground green Sichuan pepper evenly on top.
11. In a separate wok, heat a mixture of oils (rapeseed and lard). Add dried green and red Sichuan peppers and dried chili segments, fry over low heat until fragrant, then pour the hot oil together with the peppers over the sprinkled pepper layers on the dish to release aroma.
12. Finally, garnish with chopped scallions and cilantro.

## Health Tips
Rabbit meat is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with winter melon, which adds dietary fiber, this dish is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet. Use less salt when marinating, and rely on beer to remove gamey taste and enhance flavor.
