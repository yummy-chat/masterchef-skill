# Vietnamese Spiced Chicken Chop
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Boneless chicken thighs with skin, as needed
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🌶️ Red and green chili peppers, as needed (minced)
🌿 Thai basil (holy basil), one handful (leaves only, minced)
🧅 Shallots (or regular onion), as needed (minced)
🥚 Eggs, as needed (for frying)
🍚 Rice, as needed (as a base)
🧴 Sesame oil, as needed (for marinating and the green sauce)
🐟 Fish sauce, as needed (for marinating and the green sauce)
🥫 Light soy sauce, as needed (for marinating and the green sauce)
🍬 White sugar, as needed (added to the green sauce)
🌿 White pepper powder, as needed (added to the green sauce)
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for heating and pouring to release aroma)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Tongs
🥄 Spoon/Fork

## Preparation Steps
1. **Marinate the chicken thighs**: Add sesame oil, fish sauce, and light soy sauce to the chicken thighs. Mix well and marinate for 20 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
2. **Prepare the green sauce base**: Place the minced garlic and chili peppers into a bowl. Pour hot oil heated until smoking over them to release the aroma.
   **Note**: The oil must be heated until smoking.
3. **Season the green sauce**: Add the minced Thai basil and shallots to the bowl with the oil-scalded garlic and chili. Then add fish sauce, light soy sauce, sesame oil, white sugar, and white pepper powder. Stir well to fully combine the herbal flavors.
4. **Fry the chicken thighs**: Heat a frying pan over high heat. Place the marinated chicken thighs in the pan, skin side down. Fry until golden and crispy, then flip. Fry until both sides are golden and crispy, then turn off the heat. Let the chicken rest in the pan's residual heat for 3-5 minutes to finish cooking through, keeping it tender and juicy.
5. **Fry the egg**: Without washing the pan, use the remaining chicken oil left in the pan. Heat over high heat again. Crack an egg into the pan. Slightly tilt the pan to pool the hot oil. Fry until the egg white is set and the edges are crispy. Cook the yolk according to your preference.
6. **Assemble the dish**: Slice the fried chicken thighs and arrange them on top of the rice. Place the fried egg on top. Drizzle the prepared green sauce over everything. Mixing everything together before eating enhances the flavor.

## Health Advice
This Vietnamese Spiced Chicken Chop is rich in protein and contains various antioxidant herbs, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing an energy boost. However, fish sauce and light soy sauce are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Although the crispy chicken skin is delicious, it is advisable to control fat intake and pair the dish with a generous amount of vegetables for a balanced meal.
