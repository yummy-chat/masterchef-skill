# Braised Chicken with Potatoes
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs 500g (boneless, cut into small pieces)
🥔 Potatoes 360g (peeled, cut into small pieces)
🫚 Black cardamom 2 pieces (cut in half)
🌿 Lemongrass 2 stalks (cut into small sections)
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies 5 pieces
🫚 Ginger 60g
🧄 Garlic 20g
🍅 Tomato 1 piece (peeled and cut into chunks)
🧅 Scallions, chopped, to taste (for garnish before serving)
🦪 Oyster sauce 70g
🥛 Evaporated milk 50g
🧂 Salt, to taste
🫒 Oil, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🫗 Food processor or blender
🥄 Strainer
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥘 Small saucepan

## Steps
1. Debone the chicken thighs and cut into small pieces.
2. Place the black cardamom, lemongrass, and bird's eye chilies in a food processor and pulse into a coarse paste.
3. Place the ginger and garlic in the food processor and pulse into a fine paste.
4. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add half of the ginger-garlic paste and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the black cardamom and lemongrass paste and stir-fry. Subsequently, add oyster sauce, evaporated milk, and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring, until the sauce thickens and darkens in color. Remove and set aside.
5. Place the potato chunks in a saucepan with rice-washing water. Bring to a boil and cook for about 20 minutes until tender. Drain and immediately refresh under cold water. Drain well and set aside.
   **Note**: Boiling potatoes in rice-washing water helps remove astringency, resulting in a soft, creamy texture without falling apart.
6. In a separate wok, heat oil over high heat. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry over high heat until they change color. Then add the prepared sauce and continue stir-frying until the chicken is evenly coated.
   **Note**: At this point, the semi-cooked chicken can be refrigerated for later use in quick cooking.
7. Heat oil in a wok. Add the prepared potato chunks and stir-fry until the surface is slightly rough and textured. Add the remaining sauce and stir-fry until aromatic.
8. Return the cooked chicken to the wok with the potatoes. Add the peeled and chunked tomato pieces. Stir-fry to combine evenly.
9. Cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Braise until all ingredients are fully flavored and cooked through.
10. Sprinkle with chopped scallions before serving.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and resistant starch, making it suitable for muscle building and mental work. However, the evaporated milk and oyster sauce increase sodium and saturated fat content. It is recommended to pair with whole grains or leafy greens to balance the calories. Pay attention to reducing seasoning amounts; people with high blood pressure should consume with caution.
