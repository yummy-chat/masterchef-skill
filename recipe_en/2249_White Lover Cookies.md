# White Lover Cookies
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Butter 55g (softened at room temperature)
🥚 Egg White 55g (room temperature, added in batches)
🌾 Cake Flour 35g (sifted)
🌽 Corn Starch 20g
🥛 Whole Milk Powder 20g
🥛 Heavy Cream 15g
🍬 Powdered Sugar 55g
🧂 Salt 1g
🍫 White Chocolate (as needed for filling)
🧂 Sea Salt (for salted caramel version)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥄 Electric Mixer
♨️ Oven (with convection function)
🍽️ Silicone Mold (2mm thick square)
🔪 Spatula/Scraper
🧂 Sieve
🍳 Baking Tray
📏✂️ Ruler, Craft Knife, Cardboard (optional, DIY mold)

## Instructions
1. Mix the softened butter with powdered sugar, and beat with an electric mixer until light and fluffy in texture and pale in color.
   **Key**: Add the egg white in three batches, ensuring each addition is fully emulsified before adding the next to prevent separation.
2. Add the heavy cream and mix well. Sift in the cake flour, corn starch, whole milk powder, and salt. Use a spatula to fold into a smooth batter. The batter should have some fluidity and not be dry or stiff.
3. Fill the batter into the silicone mold, smooth the surface with a scraper, unmold, and arrange on a baking tray.
4. Preheat the oven to 200°C with the convection function turned on. Bake for 6 minutes, until the edges are visibly golden brown while the center remains white. Remove immediately.
   **Note**: High-temperature quick baking creates a beautiful golden edge and prevents oil separation. Avoid low-temperature slow baking.
5. Melt the white chocolate over a water bath. Pour it onto a flat mold and spread into a sheet about 2mm thick with a scraper. Freeze for about 10 minutes until completely firm. Unmold and cut into square pieces the same size as the cookies.
6. Take two cookie pieces, sandwich one white chocolate piece in the middle, and gently press together.
7. Place the white chocolate in the oven at 130°C. Bake, stirring every 10 minutes, repeating about 5 times, until the white chocolate turns into a smooth caramel color.
   **Principle**: Utilizes the Maillard reaction to produce a toasty and bittersweet flavor.
8. Pour the caramel chocolate liquid into the mold and smooth it out. Sprinkle sea salt flakes on top. Freeze until set, then assemble following step 6 for a richer layer of texture.

## Health Advice
These "White Lover" cookies are high in calories; it is recommended to enjoy 2-3 pieces per serving. Pairing with unsweetened tea can help cut the richness. For those with high blood sugar or on a weight loss plan, the original version is recommended to avoid the extra sugar in the caramel version.
