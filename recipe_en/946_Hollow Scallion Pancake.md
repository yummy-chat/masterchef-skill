# Hollow Scallion Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 300g all-purpose or bread flour
💧 170g water
🧅 A small bunch of scallions (finely chopped)
🫙 1/8 teaspoon yeast
🧂 1/2 teaspoon salt
🫒 2 teaspoons cooking oil (for dough) + additional oil (for brushing and frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl, small bowl
🥄 Measuring spoons
📦 Plastic storage container
🪖 Rolling pin
🍳 Deep frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🌡️ Thermometer (optional)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the chopped scallions in a bowl, mix with salt, and let sit until the scallions soften and release water.
   **Note**: Salting softens the scallions, preventing them from piercing the dough during rolling, which helps the pancake puff up.
2. In another large bowl, combine water, yeast, and 2 teaspoons of cooking oil, stirring well. Add the flour and scallions, mix with a spatula until a shaggy dough forms, then knead into a smooth, medium-firm dough.
3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 15 minutes. Remove and knead until the surface is smooth, then rest for another 15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Two rests allow the gluten to fully relax, making it easier to roll thin later.
4. Roll the dough into a long log and divide into 8 equal portions. Fold the edges of each portion toward the center, gathering them into a round ball.
5. Pour oil into a small bowl, coat each dough ball evenly with oil, and place in a storage container. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours to rest.
   **Key Point**: Oil coating locks in moisture and prevents sticking; thorough resting in the fridge makes it easy to roll very thin.
6. Take out one dough ball, flatten it, and roll it into a very thin round sheet using a rolling pin.
   **Note**: Roll as thin as possible—the thinner it is, the easier it puffs up.
7. Heat oil in a pan to about 200°C (392°F). Carefully add the dough sheet. Once it floats, continuously ladle hot oil over the pancake.
   **Key Point**: High heat and continuous oil ladling are key to the pancake quickly puffing into a hollow ball. Avoid pressing down hard when the pancake is about to puff, as this may pop the bubbles.
8. Flip once puffed, and fry both sides until light golden brown with some toasted spots. Remove and drain on paper towels.

## Health Tips
This scallion pancake is high in calories, making it a suitable occasional treat. Pair with light vegetables and protein-rich foods to balance a meal. It is ideal for those who enjoy a crispy texture and are not strictly controlling oil intake. Note that salting the scallions prevents them from tearing the dough, which is essential for achieving a hollow pancake.
