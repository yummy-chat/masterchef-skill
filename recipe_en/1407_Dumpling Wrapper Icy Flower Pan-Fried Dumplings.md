# Dumpling Wrapper Icy Flower Pan-Fried Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Dumpling wrappers 5 pieces per group (quantity as needed)  
🍉 Watermelon rind about 200g (remove red flesh and hard green skin, use the white part)  
🥚 Egg 1 piece (pre-scrambled into small pieces)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (finely chopped, reserve a small amount for garnish)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper a small amount (diced for garnish)  
🧂 Fine salt 5g (divided into two parts: 3g for marinating to draw out water, 2g for seasoning)  
🧂 MSG a small amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce a small amount  
🫒 Seasoning oil 3g  
🫒 Sesame oil 2g  
🫒 Cooking oil 2g (for the icy flower batter)  
🌾 Flour 7g (for the icy flower batter)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍚 Cutting board  
🥣 Small bowl/basin  
🥢 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
💧 Measuring cup  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Take 5 dumpling wrappers as one group, brush oil between each wrapper and stack them. Press with your palm, then roll out with a rolling pin into a round thin sheet about the thickness of a coin. Place in a steamer, steam over high heat for 8 minutes after the water boils. Remove and let cool naturally, then tear into individual thin sheets and set aside.
2. Remove the red flesh and hard green skin from the watermelon rind and cut into chunks. Blanch in boiling water for 30 seconds, then remove and rinse under cold water. Cut off any remaining light green hard skin, take the white part and dice finely. Add 3g of salt and marinate for 3 minutes, then rinse multiple times with clean water to remove salt. Squeeze out the water thoroughly and set aside.
3. To the squeezed watermelon rind dice, add the scrambled egg pieces and chopped scallion. First add 3g seasoning oil and 2g sesame oil, mix to seal in moisture. Then add 2g salt, MSG, and oyster sauce, stir evenly to form the filling.
4. In a small basin, add 100g clean water, 7g flour, and 2g oil. Stir into a smooth, lump-free thin batter. This is the icy flower batter.
5. Take one steamed thin sheet and lay it flat. Place an appropriate amount of filling on it. Roll one side of the sheet up to wrap the filling, fold both ends inward, then continue rolling into a cylindrical filling roll. Repeat this process to prepare all the filling rolls.
6. Spray a thin layer of oil on the bottom of a non-stick frying pan. Arrange the filling rolls neatly in the pan. Pour in the icy flower batter until it reaches about 1/3 the height of the rolls. Immediately cover with a lid, turn the heat to the lowest setting, and pan-fry for about 2 minutes.
7. When the liquid in the pan is mostly evaporated and a distinct golden, net-like icy flower crust has formed on the bottom, turn off the heat. Invert a plate over the pan, press down firmly, and quickly flip the pan so the dumplings are turned out intact onto the plate. Garnish the surface with shredded scallion and diced red bell pepper before serving.

## Health Tips  
Using watermelon rind as a filling not only turns kitchen scraps into treasure but also adds dietary fiber, making it suitable for those who want a refreshing yet satisfying pan-fried dumpling in summer. Note that the watermelon rind must be thoroughly squeezed dry; otherwise, excessive moisture in the filling will affect the icy flower effect.
