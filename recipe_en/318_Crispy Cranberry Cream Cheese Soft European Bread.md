# Crispy Cranberry Cream Cheese Soft European Bread  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥖 Bread flour 300g  
🍬 Granulated sugar 45g (for dough)  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍞 High-sugar-tolerant dry yeast 4g  
🥛 Ice milk 198g  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 40g (for dough)  
🧀 Cream cheese 55g (for dough)  
🧀 Cream cheese approx. 180g (for filling)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (for Mexican sauce)  
🍚 Icing sugar 30g (for Mexican sauce)  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 30g (for Mexican sauce)  
🌾 Cake flour 30g (for Mexican sauce)  
🍒 Dried cranberries appropriate amount (pre-soaked until soft)  
🍬 Granulated sugar or condensed milk appropriate amount (for filling seasoning, optional)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍞 Stand mixer  
🌡️ Proofing box  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🥚 Whisk  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🌡️ Thermometer  
🍕 Baking tray  
🥅 Cooling rack  
💉 Piping bag  
🔥 Oven (convection oven)  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the cream cheese filling**: Soften approx. 180g cream cheese, then place it into a piping bag. You may add granulated sugar or condensed milk to taste and mix well. Set aside.  
2. **Make the Mexican sauce**: Mix 30g softened butter with 30g icing sugar until well combined. Add 30g room-temperature whole egg liquid in two batches, mixing well after each addition. Sift in 30g cake flour and stir with a spatula until a pipeable paste forms. Transfer to a piping bag and set aside.  
3. **Knead the dough**: Add all bread dough ingredients except the cream cheese for the dough into the stand mixer. Mix on low speed until a dough forms, then switch to high speed and knead until the dough reaches the initial gluten development stage.  
   - **Key point**: Add 55g softened cream cheese and wrap it with the dough. First knead on low speed until the cheese is fully incorporated, then switch to high speed and knead until the dough reaches full gluten development stage.  
   - **Temperature control**: The final dough temperature should ideally be around 26°C.  
4. **First proofing**: Round the dough, place it in a container, and let it proof at 28°C until doubled in size. Test by poking a hole with a floured finger; the hole should not shrink back or collapse. If the dough temperature is too low, extend the proofing time accordingly.  
5. **Divide and pre-shape**: Remove the proofed dough, gently press to release gas, and divide into 6 equal portions. Round each portion, then fold and shape into a short log. Place on a baking tray, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for about 25 minutes.  
6. **Shape and fill**: Flatten the rested dough with your hand and roll it out into a narrow, elongated oval shape using a rolling pin. Flip it over and thin the bottom edge. Pipe a strip of cream cheese filling at the top of the dough, sprinkle with the pre-soaked dried cranberries, then roll it up from top to bottom, pinching the seam to seal.  
   - **Key point**: The dough should be rolled out long and narrow enough to ensure even distribution of the filling.  
7. **Final shaping**: Stretch the filled log to about 50 cm in length. Fold it in half, then tie a single knot near the fold point. Cross the two strands to form a three-strand braid, tuck the ends underneath, and pinch to seal.  
   - **Key point**: The dough strand must be stretched to sufficient length to avoid being too tight during braiding. The shape is a closed three-strand braid.  
8. **Final proofing**: Place the shaped dough pieces on a baking tray and proof at 35°C with 85% humidity for about 40 minutes, until the volume has increased significantly and the dough springs back slowly when lightly pressed.  
9. **Decorate and bake**: Brush the proofed dough pieces with a layer of whole egg liquid, sprinkle a little dried cranberries on top, and pipe the Mexican sauce over them. Place in a preheated convection oven at 180-200°C and bake for 12-15 minutes.  
   - **Key point**: Monitor the browning towards the end of baking. Cover with aluminum foil if necessary to prevent burning.  
10. **Cool after baking**: Immediately transfer the baked bread to a cooling rack. Once slightly cooled, you may dust with icing sugar or milk powder for decoration.  

## Health Tips  
This crispy soft European bread has a rich cream cheese filling, balanced by the tartness of cranberries. It is suitable as a breakfast or afternoon tea treat. However, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea and control the serving size (e.g., half a piece). Note that the dough must be kneaded until a windowpane membrane forms for a soft texture. Do not pipe too much Mexican sauce, or it may melt and collapse during baking.
