# Ciabatta Sandwich
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
### Bread Base Ingredients  
🍞 Bread flour 300g (for the main dough)  
🌾 Flour 100g (for the poolish)  
💧 Water 360g (100g for the poolish + 260g for the main dough)  
🧂 Salt 10g  
🫙 Yeast 1g  

### Sandwich Fillings (shared by three varieties)  
🥖 Ciabatta bread 1 piece (frozen for storage, re-baked before serving)  
🧈 Cream cheese as needed (for spreading on savory sandwiches)  
🫒 Olive oil as needed (for pan-frying and re-baking)  

#### Smoked Salmon Variety  
🐟 Smoked salmon approx. 120g  
🥑 Avocado approx. 35g (half, sliced)  
🧅 Red onion approx. 15g (finely julienned)  
🥬 Arugula approx. 15g  
🌶️ Black pepper to taste (freshly ground)  

#### Yogurt Fruit Variety  
🥛 Greek yogurt approx. 120g  
🍊 Persimmon (Fire Crystal variety) approx. 1 piece (75g, flesh scooped out)  
🫐 Figs approx. 1.5 pieces (50g, cut into pieces)  
🫐 Blueberries approx. 20g  
🌰 Slivered almonds to taste  
🍫 Dark chocolate to taste (shaved into curls)  
🧀 Parmesan cheese to taste (grated)  
🌿 Mint leaves to taste (for garnish)  

#### Kimchi Beef Variety  
🥩 Marbled beef slices (for hot pot) approx. 120g (sliced)  
🥬 Kimchi approx. 15g  
🧅 Onion approx. 50g (half, sliced)  
🧀 Cheese slices 2 slices  
🌶️ Korean chili paste (gochujang) to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔥 Oven (for baking bread)  
♨️ Air fryer (for re-baking frozen bread)  
🍳 Frying pan (for pan-frying bread and cooking fillings)  
🍳 Lid (for covering to melt cheese)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for cutting ingredients)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing dough)  
🛍️ Ziplock bag or plastic wrap (for freezing bread)  
🔧 Vegetable peeler (for shaving dark chocolate curls)  
🧀 Grater (for grating Parmesan cheese)  
🥄 Wooden spatula (for stirring ingredients)  

## Preparation Steps  
### Basic Bread Making  
1. Make the poolish: Mix 100g flour with 100g water evenly. Ferment at room temperature until the surface is full of bubbles and it has a paste-like consistency.  
2. Make the main dough: Mix the fermented poolish with 300g bread flour, 260g water, 10g salt, and 1g yeast. Knead into a smooth dough. Ferment until the volume has increased significantly.  
3. Shape and bake: Divide and shape the fermented dough. Bake in the oven until the surface is golden brown and the volume has expanded.  
4. Freeze for storage: Once the bread is completely cooled, seal it tightly in a ziplock bag or plastic wrap and store it in the freezer.  
   **Key point**: Do not refrigerate; refrigeration accelerates bread staling.  
5. Thaw and re-bake: Before serving, remove the bread from the freezer. Briefly rinse it under running tap water, then place it in a preheated air fryer or oven for a few minutes until the crust becomes crispy.  
   **Key point**: Rinsing with water and re-baking helps restore a texture close to freshly baked.  

### Smoked Salmon Sandwich  
1. Cut the re-baked ciabatta open horizontally. Spread a layer of cream cheese evenly on both cut surfaces.  
2. Layer on the finely julienned red onion, arugula, thin slices of avocado, and finally the smoked salmon slices.  
3. Sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper and serve.  

### Yogurt Fruit Sandwich  
1. Cut the re-baked ciabatta open horizontally. Spread a layer of Greek yogurt evenly on both cut surfaces.  
2. Scoop out the persimmon flesh and spread it like jam over the yogurt layer.  
3. Layer on the fig pieces and blueberries.  
4. Use a vegetable peeler to shave dark chocolate curls over the fruit. Grate Parmesan cheese on top, and garnish with slivered almonds and mint leaves.  

### Kimchi Beef Sandwich  
1. Place the cut ciabatta halves in a frying pan, spray or brush lightly with olive oil on the cut sides. Heat over low heat for about 1-2 minutes until the surface is crispy. Remove and set aside.  
2. Add a little more oil to the pan. Add the marbled beef slices and fry until both sides change color, then remove immediately.  
   **Note**: Do not overcook the beef; keep it tender and juicy.  
3. Add oil to the pan again. Sauté the onion slices until fragrant. Add the Korean chili paste and kimchi, and stir-fry until aromatic.  
4. Return the cooked beef slices to the pan, spreading them over the kimchi and onion. Place the cheese slices on top. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, cover with a lid, and cook until the cheese is completely melted.  
5. Slide the entire melted cheese and beef filling out of the pan onto the heated ciabatta base. Cover with the other half of the bread and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Ciabatta sandwiches, whether paired with smoked salmon, yogurt fruit, or kimchi beef, provide high-quality protein and healthy fats, making them suitable for fitness or weight-loss diets. Note that rinsing the frozen bread with water before re-baking helps restore its texture, but be mindful of the amount of cream cheese or cheese slices used to avoid excessive calorie intake.
