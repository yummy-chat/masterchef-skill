# Scissors-Cut Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn/Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Flour 1000g (about 2 pounds)
🧂 Salt 6g (added to flour for kneading)
🥚 Eggs 3 (added to flour)
💧 Cold water 300g
🥚 Eggs to taste (for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallions to taste
🫚 Ginger to taste
🍅 Tomatoes to taste
🫘 Tofu to taste
🥔 Potatoes to taste
🌱 Bean sprouts to taste
🍄 Mushrooms to taste
🌼 Dried day lily flowers to taste (pre-soaked)
🪵 Wood ear mushrooms to taste (pre-soaked)
🥬 Spinach to taste
🧂 Salt to taste (for seasoning stir-fried vegetables)
🌶️ Ground white pepper to taste
🥄 Light soy sauce to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste
🌶️ Chili oil to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Ladle or spoon
🍜 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 1000g of flour in a bowl. Add 6g of salt and 3 eggs. Gradually add about 300g of cold water while stirring, until the mixture forms flaky pieces.
   **Key Point**: Salt enhances the dough's elasticity; eggs make the noodles softer.

2. Press the flaky dough together into a ball. Transfer it to a cutting board and knead repeatedly until the surface is smooth, the texture is fine, and the gluten is fully developed.

3. Cover the kneaded dough with the bowl and let it rest for 10 minutes to relax the gluten.

4. Hold a piece of rested dough in your hand. Using scissors, cut willow-leaf-shaped noodles close to the dough, one cut at a time. Try to cut them thin and long, ensuring each piece is separate and does not stick.

5. Immediately spread the cut noodles onto a cutting board dusted with dry flour. Gently toss them with your hands so each noodle is evenly coated with dry flour and does not stick together.

6. Prepare your choice of side ingredients: Dice tomatoes, cut tofu and potatoes into strips. Wash bean sprouts and mushrooms. Pre-soak dried day lily flowers and wood ear mushrooms.

7. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. First, scramble the eggs until cooked. Then add scallions and ginger to release their aroma.

8. Add tomatoes and stir-fry until they release juice. Then add tofu, potatoes, bean sprouts, mushrooms, pre-soaked day lily flowers, and wood ear mushrooms. Stir-fry everything together.

9. Season the vegetables with an appropriate amount of salt and ground white pepper. Then add enough water to the wok.

10. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Once the broth is boiling, add light soy sauce and chicken bouillon to further enhance the flavor.

11. Keep the broth at a rolling boil. Evenly add the tossed scissors-cut noodles into the wok. Gently stir with a ladle to prevent sticking to the bottom.

12. Once all the noodles float to the surface, add the spinach. Use the residual heat to blanch it briefly.

13. When the noodles and vegetables are fully cooked, turn off the heat. Drizzle with an appropriate amount of chili oil. Stir evenly, then serve in bowls.

## Health Tips
This scissors-cut noodle dish uses flour noodles paired with a variety of vegetables, offering balanced nutrition. It is especially suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter. It is recommended to control the noodle portion (potatoes and bean sprouts contain carbohydrates) and to use less oil and salt to reduce calories.
