# Candied Pork Fat Strips (Tang Zhan Yang Wei)
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonality: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 A large piece of pure fat pork belly (use only pure fat, no lean meat)  
🥚 2 eggs (egg whites only)  
🌽 Corn starch, as needed (for the egg white batter)  
🍬 White sugar, as needed  
💧 A little water (for making the syrup)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Remove the skin from the pure fat pork belly, place it in a pot of cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 20 minutes until the meat is just cooked through. Remove and let cool slightly.
**Key point**: The cooking time is sufficient when no blood remains in the center of the meat. Letting it cool makes it easier to cut into strips.

2.  Cut the cooked meat into evenly sized strips, about the thickness of a chopstick.
3.  Blanch the meat strips in boiling water again, remove, and spread them flat on a plate lined with kitchen paper towels. Use more paper towels to press down and thoroughly absorb all surface moisture and oil.
   (Throw the meat strips into boiling water for a quick blanching, take them out, spread them on a plate lined with kitchen paper, and press with more paper to absorb any moisture and oil beads.
   **Note**: Absorbing the moisture is key for the batter to stick and for a crispy texture.)

4.  Separate the egg whites from 2 eggs into a bowl. Add an appropriate amount of corn starch and stir into a smooth, flowing, thick egg white batter.
5.  Add the moisture-absorbed meat strips to the egg white batter. Use your hands to toss and mix well, ensuring each strip is coated with batter.
6.  Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok. When the oil temperature reaches about 30% hot (around 90-110°C / 200-230°F), carefully add the battered meat strips one by one. Fry over medium heat until they all float to the surface and are set.
**Note**: Add them one by one to prevent sticking. The initial frying oil temperature should not be too high.

7.  Slightly increase the oil temperature and continue frying for about 5 minutes. The strips should turn a light golden color and become crispy, with a honeycomb-like interior. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Raising the oil temperature helps render out the internal fat, making the finish crispy and not greasy.

8.  Clean the wok. Add a little water and an appropriate amount of white sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly with a spatula. When the syrup changes from large bubbles to small, dense bubbles, becomes thick and white, turn off the heat immediately.
**Note**: Stir constantly throughout this process to prevent the sugar from burning.

9.  Quickly pour the fried meat strips into the syrup. Toss and mix rapidly, ensuring each strip is evenly coated with the syrup.
10. Continue tossing and mixing until the syrup on the surface of the meat strips completely solidifies and turns white, forming an even, powdery sugar coating. Remove from the wok and serve.

## Health Advice  
This dish consists of deep-fried pure pork fat coated in a sugar icing. It is high in calories, with a crispy and sweet texture, suitable for an occasional treat. Diabetics and those on a fitness regimen should consume with caution and watch their intake.
