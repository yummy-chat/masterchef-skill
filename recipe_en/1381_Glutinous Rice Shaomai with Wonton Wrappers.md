# Glutinous Rice Shaomai with Wonton Wrappers
Cuisine Type: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 Wonton wrappers, about 3 yuan worth
🍚 Glutinous rice, 200 grams
🍄 Black fungus, appropriate amount (dried, needs to be soaked)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount
🫛 Sweet peas, about 1 yuan worth
🧅 Scallions, 1 yuan worth (chopped into scallions)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🌿 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Soy sauce, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Cutting board
🫓 Rolling pin
♨️ Steamer/steaming mat
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🍳 Frying pan
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the glutinous rice thoroughly, soak in water for 1 hour, drain, then spread evenly on a steaming tray and steam over high heat for 25 minutes until fully cooked.
2. Spread out the wonton wrappers and soak them in cooking oil for 15 minutes. Stack 4-5 wrappers together and gently roll them out with a rolling pin.
   **Note**: Use gentle motions to prevent sticking or tearing.
3. Stack the rolled-out wonton wrappers on the steaming tray and steam over high heat for 10 minutes. After steaming, carefully peel them apart while hot and spread them out to cool.
4. Finely chop the black fungus, shiitake mushrooms, and carrot. Blanch the sweet peas in boiling water for 2 minutes, then drain. Chop the scallions.
   **Key Point**: Try to keep the chopped vegetables uniform in size.
5. Heat oil in a wok, add the chopped black fungus, shiitake mushrooms, and carrot. Stir-fry over medium heat until soft and fragrant, then add the blanched sweet peas and stir-fry briefly.
6. Add salt, five-spice powder, and soy sauce to taste. Stir-fry until evenly combined.
7. Add the steamed glutinous rice, using a spatula to mix thoroughly until the rice is fully combined with the vegetable mixture and the color is even.
8. Before turning off the heat, sprinkle in the chopped scallions, stir well, then turn off the heat and let the filling cool.
9. Take one wonton wrapper, place an appropriate amount of glutinous rice filling on it, press gently to compact, fold over to enclose the filling, moisten the edges with a little water and pinch to seal, shaping into a rectangle.
   **Key Point**: The wrappers are thin, so handle gently; moistening the edges with water helps seal.
10. Brush a frying pan with a little oil, place the wrapped shaomai in the pan, and pan-fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown and the surface is slightly crispy.
    **Judgment Standard**: The wrapper turns a golden color.

## Health Suggestions
This shaomai uses wonton wrappers as a substitute for traditional shaomai wrappers, greatly lowering the preparation difficulty. The combination of glutinous rice and vegetables provides complex carbohydrates and dietary fiber, making it suitable as a breakfast or snack. However, glutinous rice has a relatively high glycemic index, so it is recommended to pair it with soy milk or vegetable soup for balance.
