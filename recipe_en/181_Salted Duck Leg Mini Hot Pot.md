# Salted Duck Leg Mini Hot Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Salted duck leg 1 piece (about 300-400g, chopped into small pieces)  
🥬 Asparagus lettuce 1 piece (peeled and sliced)  
🫘 Dried tofu skin appropriate amount (cut into strips)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into sections, some for sautéing, some for garnish)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (crushed)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed)  
🌶️ Dried chilies 3-5 pieces  
⭐ Star anise 1-2 pieces  
🫑 Green and red peppers appropriate amount (cut into chunks)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  
🍶 Yellow rice wine appropriate amount  
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🧂 White pepper powder appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon  
🥣 Soup ladle  
🍲 Mini hot pot or clay pot  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Chop the salted duck leg into evenly sized small pieces; peel and slice the asparagus lettuce; cut the dried tofu skin into strips; cut scallions into sections, crush the ginger and garlic.  
2. Place the salted duck leg pieces in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and continue boiling for about 1 minute. Remove and drain.  
3. In a separate pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add scallion sections, crushed ginger, garlic cloves, dried chilies, and star anise. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
4. Add the blanched duck leg pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface turns slightly golden and excess fat is rendered out.  
5. Pour yellow rice wine along the edge of the pot and stir well. Then add light soy sauce and stir-fry until the duck meat is evenly colored.  
6. Add enough water, then add white sugar, white pepper powder, and chicken bouillon (if using). Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes.  
   **Note**: Add water all at once. Avoid adding water during cooking to maintain the soup’s thickness.  
7. Remove the lid, turn the heat to high, and add the asparagus lettuce slices, dried tofu skin strips, and green and red peppers. Cook for about 3 minutes, until the vegetables are just cooked through.  
   **Key point**: Add the side ingredients at the end to keep them crisp.  
8. Transfer all ingredients and soup into a mini hot pot or clay pot. Sprinkle with green garlic sprout sections. Serve while still heating over a burner for the best flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This salted duck leg mini hot pot is ideal for warming up in winter. However, salted duck legs are naturally high in salt. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables, and those with high blood pressure should limit their intake. Asparagus lettuce and dried tofu skin add dietary fiber and protein to balance nutrition.
