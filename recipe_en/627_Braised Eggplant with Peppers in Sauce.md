# Braised Eggplant with Peppers in Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant, appropriate amount (purple-skinned long eggplant, cut into sections)
🌶️ Peppers, appropriate amount (green pointed peppers, whole or cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, minced, appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫒 Oil, appropriate amount (for pan-frying)
💧 Water, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🫘 Fermented soybean paste, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Ladle

## Steps
1.  Prepare the sauce: Mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, fermented soybean paste, sugar, cornstarch, and water together until well combined. Set aside.
2.  Place the wok over high heat to warm up, then add oil. Heat over medium heat, add the eggplant sections, and pan-fry until softened.
3.  Push the eggplant to the side of the wok or remove it temporarily. Add the peppers and pan-fry until the skin develops "tiger-skin" blisters.
4.  Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry evenly, ensuring the eggplant and peppers are well coated with the sauce.
5.  Cover the wok, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise for about 5 minutes, until the sauce thickens and the ingredients are infused with flavor.
6.  Uncover, add the minced garlic and cilantro sections, stir-fry evenly, then remove from heat and serve.

## Health Tips
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber and anthocyanins, while peppers provide vitamin C, making this dish suitable for pairing with staple foods in daily meals. Note to use a moderate amount of oil for slow pan-frying; diabetic patients may reduce the sugar in the seasoning.
