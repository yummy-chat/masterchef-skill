# Colorful Patterned Tangyuan (Glutinous Rice Balls)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 300g  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 177g  
💧 Water 230g  
🥬 Beetroot 1 piece  
💧 Water as needed  
🧃 Beetroot juice 136g  
🌰 Roasted peanuts 200g  
🥜 Pecans 50g  
🍯 Honey dates 50g  
🍬 Brown sugar 60g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 100g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl  
🍳 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔪 Blender  
🫗 Strainer  
🍴 Food processor  
🥄 Spatula  
🧊 Refrigerator  
🍽️ Cutting board  
🔪 Dough cutter  

## Steps
**Make the white dough**
1. Mix 300g glutinous rice flour with 230g water, knead into a loose, crumbly dough.
2. Take about 30g from the dough, press into a small flat disc, and boil in water for about 3 minutes until it floats, creating a "cooked dough binder".
3. Drain the cooked dough binder and return it to the remaining raw dough, kneading vigorously.
   **Key point**: First boil a small portion of dough as a binder, then knead it into the raw dough to significantly improve elasticity and prevent cracking during wrapping.
4. Knead until the dough is smooth, soft, and can be stretched without breaking easily. Wrap in plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.

**Make the purple dough**
5. Peel and chop the beetroot, add some water and blend into juice in a blender. Strain through a sieve to obtain 136g of fine beetroot juice.
6. Mix 177g glutinous rice flour with 136g beetroot juice, knead into a raw dough.
7. Similarly, take about 20g of raw dough, flatten it, boil for 3 minutes as a cooked binder. Drain and knead evenly with the remaining raw dough.
   **Note**: Do not use too much cooked binder, or the dough will become too sticky; the final dough should be a bright purple-red color.
8. Wrap the kneaded purple dough in plastic wrap and rest for 30 minutes.

**Make the filling**
9. Place pitted honey dates, roasted peanuts, pecans, and brown sugar into a food processor, and pulse into a coarse crumb with some visible chunks.
   **Key point**: Only pulse the nuts to a coarse texture to retain crunch for a richer mouthfeel.
10. Pour melted unsalted butter into the food processor and pulse again to combine evenly.
    **Key point**: Do not add water to the filling; use only butter as a binder. This makes the filling firm up when frozen, making it easier to wrap.
11. Remove the filling and divide into uniformly sized small balls. Place on a tray and refrigerate until firm.

**Assemble the tangyuan**
12. Take portions of the rested white and purple doughs, mix, and knead together to create a third light purple dough. Roll all three doughs into long ropes.
13. **Marbled pattern**: Place the three colored ropes side by side, gently roll them to slightly fuse together, then roll into a thinner single long rope. Cut into small pieces with a dough cutter.
14. **Striped pattern**: Take multiple small white and purple ropes, flatten each, stack alternately, roll into a cylinder, cut in half lengthwise, then roll into a thin long rope and cut into small pieces.
15. Take one piece, use your thumb to press and rotate in the center to form a small bowl shape. Place a chilled filling ball inside, slowly close the opening using your thumb and index finger, squeeze tightly, and roll into a round ball.
    **Key point**: Keep the wrapper thickness even when filling. Ensure the opening is tightly sealed. Roll gently to preserve the pattern clarity.

**Cook the tangyuan**
16. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil, then reduce to medium-low heat. Gently drop in the tangyuan, and lightly stir with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking.
    **Note**: Keep the water at a gentle simmer. Avoid boiling vigorously to prevent the tangyuan from breaking.
17. Once all tangyuan float to the surface and have slightly expanded in size, remove them with a slotted spoon.
    **Note**: Tangyuan are cooked as soon as they float. Do not overcook, or the colors will fade and the texture will become too mushy.

## Health Advice
These colorful tangyuan use natural beetroot juice for coloring, paired with a nut and honey date filling. Compared to traditional tangyuan, they offer increased dietary fiber and minerals. Suitable for those managing sugar intake if consumed in small amounts. However, due to the high calories from glutinous rice and butter, it is recommended to eat only 4-5 pieces per serving.
