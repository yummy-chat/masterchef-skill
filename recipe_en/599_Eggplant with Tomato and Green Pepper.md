# Eggplant with Tomato and Green Pepper
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 2 long eggplants (about 250-300g)  
🍅 2 tomatoes (about 100-150g)  
🫑 1 green bell pepper  
🧄 4-5 cloves of garlic  

🧂 2 tablespoons light soy sauce  
🦪 1 tablespoon oyster sauce  
🥫 2 tablespoons sweet bean sauce  
🍅 2 tablespoons ketchup  
💧 5 tablespoons water (or adjust as needed)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (added twice, a pinch each time)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste (added before serving)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Measuring spoons  
🍽️ Bowl/plate  

## Steps
1. Peel, wash, and remove the stem from the eggplant, then cut it into small strips. Wash and remove the stem from the tomatoes, then cut them into small pieces. Remove the seeds from the green bell pepper and slice it into strips. Smash the garlic cloves with the flat side of the knife, then mince them.  
2. Pour enough oil into the pan and heat over high heat. Add the eggplant strips and quickly stir-fry. Add a pinch of salt to help soften them. Once the eggplant becomes soft and wilted, remove and set aside.  
3. Without washing the pan, add a little more oil and heat over medium heat. Add most of the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the tomato pieces, sprinkle with a pinch of salt, and stir-fry to help the tomatoes release their juice quickly.  
4. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, sweet bean sauce, ketchup, and water one by one. Then turn the heat back to medium and stir evenly. Use the spatula to mash the tomato pieces and cook until the mixture becomes a thick, sauce-like consistency.  
5. Add the previously stir-fried eggplant and raw green bell pepper strips. Turn the heat to medium and toss everything together until the eggplant fully absorbs the sauce and the bell pepper is just cooked but still crisp.  
6. Once all ingredients are tender, well combined, and the sauce is thick, sprinkle in chicken bouillon and stir evenly before serving. If the sauce is too dry, add a small amount of water and bring to a boil. Leaving some sauce is ideal for mixing with rice.  

## Health Tips
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber, green bell pepper provides vitamin C, and tomatoes aid digestion, making this dish suitable for people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, and high cholesterol. Note that eggplant absorbs oil easily, so using a non-stick pan can help reduce oil usage. Those with diabetes may reduce the amount of sweet bean sauce.
