# Cold Okra Salad
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Okra, appropriate amount (choose evenly sized)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (finely minced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced into garlic paste)
🌶️ Small red chili peppers, appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for blanching)
🫒 Oil, appropriate amount (for blanching)
🍶 Vinegar, 3 tablespoons
🍲 Steamed fish soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🌶️ Spicy umami sauce, 1 tablespoon
🍬 White sugar, half a tablespoon
🫗 Sesame oil, 2 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Pot (transparent glass pot)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Glass bowls (multiple)
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Plate (black)
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the okra, cut off both ends, and trim to a uniform length. Finely mince the onion, garlic, and small red chili peppers respectively, then place them in a bowl for later use.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add an appropriate amount of salt and oil, and then add the okra. After bringing to a boil over high heat, reduce to medium heat and cook for about 2–3 minutes, until the okra turns bright green, becomes tender, and is fully cooked.
   **Note**: It must be thoroughly cooked, otherwise the texture will be unpleasant with noticeable sliminess. Adding salt and oil helps maintain the bright green color and enhances the base flavor.
3. Quickly remove the blanched okra with a slotted spoon and immediately immerse it in ice water to cool it down rapidly, stopping the cooking process.
   **Note**: The action must be quick; rapid cooling prevents the okra from turning yellow and keeps it crisp and tender.
4. In the bowl containing the minced onion, garlic paste, and chopped chili peppers, sequentially add vinegar, steamed fish soy sauce, spicy umami sauce, white sugar, and sesame oil. Stir well, and let it sit for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
5. Remove the okra from the ice water and thoroughly dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels. Then cut each okra lengthwise in half and make a light cut along the center pulp.
   **Note**: The surface must be completely dried, otherwise the slime secreted by the okra will clog the cut, preventing the dressing from penetrating.
6. Place the prepared okra into a bowl, pour in half of the mixed dressing, gently toss to combine, then neatly arrange on a plate.
7. Evenly drizzle the remaining dressing over the plated okra, and serve.

## Health Advice
This cold okra salad is low in fat and high in fiber, rich in vitamins and mucilage polysaccharides, making it suitable for those with high blood pressure or needing to control blood sugar. Blanching followed by an ice bath locks in the bright green color and crisp tenderness, but be sure to cook it thoroughly, otherwise the texture will be unpleasant.
