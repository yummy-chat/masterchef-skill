# Lamb Skewers
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Lamb (meat with some fat)  
🐑 Lamb tendon (higher fat content)  
🧂 Salt (or a salt-based flavor enhancer) to taste, for a base seasoning on the skewers  
🌶️ BBQ seasoning (cumin, chili flakes) to taste, for sprinkling  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔥 Charcoal grill (deep type, keeping charcoal and skewers at a distance)  
🔩 Metal skewers (for threading)  
🎋 Bamboo skewers (soak in water beforehand to prevent charring)  
🧴 Seasoning shaker  
🖌️ Basting brush  

## Instructions  
**Preparation:**  
1. Burn the charcoal completely until it is fully red, with no half-red, half-black areas. Incomplete charcoal can ignite when exposed to fat and releases carbon monoxide.  
2. If using bamboo skewers, soak them in water in advance to prevent them from turning black during grilling.  
**Grilling:**  
1. Place the threaded skewers on the deep charcoal grill, using fully burned high-temperature charcoal. Follow the principle of "high heat, quick grilling." Once the skewers are on the grill, you may sprinkle a little salt or flavor enhancer for a base seasoning at the start.  
2. Wait until one side is fully set and has developed a charred crust before flipping. Flip only 2-3 times throughout the grilling process—the fewer flips, the better. For lamb tendon with more fat, avoid flipping too often; otherwise, the fat won't render properly, and the lean meat will lose its juices, resulting in a greasy and dry texture. Once both sides have a charred crust, you can spread the meat pieces apart for even heat distribution.  
3. When both sides of the skewers start to release oil, evenly sprinkle BBQ seasoning. Grill for another three to five seconds until you smell the aroma of the spices, then remove from the grill. Timing the seasoning is critical—it must be done after the oil surfaces. Different skewers require different grilling times; those with more fat need to cook a bit longer.  

## Health Advice  
It is recommended to choose lamb with a good balance of fat and lean meat. Use high heat and quick grilling over charcoal to lock in the meat juices, and avoid excessive flipping to prevent a dry texture. Suitable for those building muscle or pursuing a high-protein diet, but grilled food consumption should be moderated.
