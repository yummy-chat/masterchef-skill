# Garlicky Crayfish
Cuisine: Hubei Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦞 Crayfish: about 2 pounds (cleaned, deveined)
🧄 Garlic: 2 whole heads (minced, divided into two portions)
🌱 Garlic scapes: 1 stalk (use the leafy part for aroma before serving)
🫚 Ginger: 1 small piece (sliced, for blanching to remove fishy smell)
🍶 Cooking wine: as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce: as needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce: as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce: as needed
🧂 Salt: as needed
🍬 White sugar: as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🧺 Colander
🧄 Garlic press

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the crayfish thoroughly and drain.  
**Note**: Ensure the crayfish are clean and free of any grit.

2. Slice the ginger; mince the garlic finely and divide into two equal portions; take the leafy part of the garlic scapes and cut into sections.  
**Note**: Use plenty of garlic—it is the core flavor of the dish.

3. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add ginger slices and cooking wine, then blanch the crayfish for 1–2 minutes until the shells turn fully red. Drain and set aside.  
**Key Step**: Blanching effectively removes fishy odors; do not overdo it.

4. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat over medium-high until the oil surface shimmers slightly.
5. Reduce heat to low, add **half** of the minced garlic, and stir-fry slowly until it turns slightly golden and fragrant.  
**Key Step**: Fry over low heat to prevent the garlic from burning and turning bitter.

6. Increase heat to high, add the blanched crayfish, and stir-fry quickly for about 1 minute, ensuring the shells are evenly coated with garlic oil.
7. Splash cooking wine along the edge of the wok, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt. Stir-fry briskly until the shells turn a bright red color.
8. Add the **remaining half** of the minced garlic and white sugar, then stir-fry again until the sauce thickens and coats the crayfish well.  
**Key Step**: Adding raw garlic at the end creates layers of garlic flavor in the final dish.

9. Add the garlic scape leaves, continue stir-frying over high heat for about 30 seconds until they are just cooked but still vibrant green.
10. Turn off the heat, transfer to a serving plate, and serve.

## Health Advice
Garlicky crayfish is rich in garlic aroma and has tender, springy meat, but it is high in oil and salt. Pair it with light vegetables like cucumber or winter melon to balance the meal. Best enjoyed as a shared dish; be mindful of portion size to avoid excessive sodium and cholesterol intake.
