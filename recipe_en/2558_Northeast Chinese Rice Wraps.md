# Northeast Chinese Rice Wraps  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Rice: to taste (depending on appetite, do not steam too sticky)  
🥔 Potatoes: to taste (the soul of large rice wraps, amount based on personal preference)  
🍆 Eggplant: to taste (the soul of large rice wraps, amount based on personal preference)  
🥜 Peanuts: about two handfuls (roasted in an air fryer)  
🥚 Eggs: 4 (for making egg sauce)  
🫑 Green bell pepper: about 80g (finely chopped, divided into two parts: one part finely chopped and stir-fried into the egg sauce, the other part diced for mixing into the rice, seeds removed)  
🌶️ Small red chili peppers: a small amount (finely chopped, for adding color and spice to the rice)  
🧅 Scallions: about 30g (finely chopped, divided into two parts: one part finely chopped and stir-fried into the egg sauce, the other part as scallion pieces for mixing into the rice)  
🌿 Cilantro: to taste (finely chopped, as a garnish mixed into the rice)  
🥬 Lettuce: to taste (as the outer skin for wrapping the rice balls)  

🫘 Fermented soybean paste: 4 level spoons (about 100g, 4 eggs correspond to 4 spoons of paste)  
🦪 Oyster sauce: about 2/3 spoon (15g)  
🧴 Dark soy sauce: about 2/3 spoon (7g, for coloring)  
🍶 Aged vinegar: half a spoon (5g, to neutralize saltiness and enhance aroma)  
🍬 White sugar: 1 teaspoon (3g) + to taste (add 3g to the sauce, can add a small amount more to the rice mixture according to taste)  
🌿 Thirteen-spice or five-spice powder: 1 level teaspoon (1g, adds complex fragrance)  
🧂 MSG: 1 teaspoon (3g, for umami)  
🫒 Cooking oil: as needed (spray a thin layer when frying peanuts; use more oil when stir-frying the egg sauce)  
🧂 Light soy sauce: to taste (adds savory flavor when mixing into the rice)  
🫒 Sesame oil: to taste (adds fragrance when mixing into the rice)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Air fryer  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🍳 Steamer and steaming rack  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥣 Grinding bowl or masher  
🥣 Large bowl  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥢 Spoons, chopsticks, rice paddle  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place peanuts in the air fryer, spray a thin layer of cooking oil and shake to coat evenly, then fry at 180°C for 6 minutes.  
2. Wash the rice thoroughly, adjust the water amount based on the rice's water absorption, aiming for distinct grains that do not stick together after steaming.  
3. Peel the potatoes and cut into slightly thick slices to prevent breaking during steaming; remove the roots from the eggplant, cut lengthwise into four strips, and lay flat on the steaming rack.  
4. Prepare the egg sauce mixture: In a bowl, combine fermented soybean paste, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, aged vinegar, white sugar, thirteen-spice or five-spice powder, and MSG, then stir well and set aside.  
5. Handle the garnishes: Remove seeds from the green bell pepper, finely chop half for the sauce and dice the other half for the rice; finely chop the small red chili peppers; finely chop the scallions, using half for the sauce and half for the rice; finely chop the cilantro and set aside.  
6. Stir-fry the egg sauce:  
   - Heat oil in a wok over high heat until smoking, then reduce to the lowest heat. Slowly pour the beaten eggs in a thin stream while stirring rapidly to form fluffy curds.  
   - Once the eggs are set, add the scallions and chopped green bell pepper reserved for the sauce. If the wok is no longer sizzling, turn the heat to high and stir-fry until fragrant.  
   - Pour in the prepared sauce mixture, stir-fry until aromatic, then use the original sauce bowl to measure an equal amount of water and add it to the wok. Simmer together until the sauce thickens but is not completely dry.  
7. Steam the potatoes and eggplant: Once the steamer water is boiling and producing steam, place the steaming rack inside and steam over high heat for 13–15 minutes, until easily pierced with chopsticks. If using small amounts, they can be steamed with the rice; for larger quantities, steam separately to avoid undercooking.  
8. Transfer the steamed potatoes and eggplant to a large bowl, discard any excess water from the eggplant, and mash into a paste using a grinding bowl or masher.  
9. Mix the rice: In the large bowl with the potato and eggplant mash, add the steamed and fluffed rice, followed by the chopped scallions and cilantro, diced green bell pepper, diced small red chili peppers, fried peanuts, and stir-fried egg sauce. Finally, drizzle with light soy sauce, sesame oil, and white sugar to taste, then mix thoroughly.  
10. Wrap the rice: Take a washed lettuce leaf, place a portion of the mixed rice in the center, and wrap it into a bundle before eating.  

## Health Suggestions  
This Northeastern Chinese rice wrap offers a balanced ratio of carbohydrates and protein, with the mashed potatoes and eggplant providing rich dietary fiber, making it suitable as a main meal for fitness or weight-loss enthusiasts. Note that the egg sauce has relatively high saltiness; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of fermented soybean paste.
