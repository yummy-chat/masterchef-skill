# Old-Style Ice Pops  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Egg yolks from old hens (yolks only)  
🥛 Milk (as a liquid base)  
🥛 Milk powder (listed in the ingredients)  
🌽 Cornstarch (key for thickening; must be fully dissolved)  
🍬 White sugar (provides basic sweetness)  
🍯 Honey (adds layers of flavor)  
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk (listed in the ingredients; enhances creaminess)

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Whisk  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🧊 Ice pop molds  
🍡 Wooden ice pop sticks  
❄️ Refrigerator (freezer compartment)

## Instructions  
1. Crack the egg yolks from the old hens into a clean large bowl.  
2. Add white sugar, honey, and cornstarch to the bowl. Whisk continuously until the cornstarch is completely dissolved, with no dry powder or lumps.  
   **Note**: The cornstarch must be fully dissolved, otherwise it will affect the texture of the final product.  
3. Slowly pour in the milk while stirring, so the batter becomes a smooth liquid mixture.  
   **Note**: The milk should be added gradually with constant stirring to prevent lumps.  
4. Pour the mixed batter into a non-stick pan. Cook over medium heat, stirring continuously.  
   **Doneness indicator**: When the surface of the batter shows large, medium, and small bubbles, and the mixture becomes thick and flows slowly when stirred, it is ready.  
   **Note**: Stir constantly throughout the process to prevent scorching.  
5. Pour the cooked batter evenly into the ice pop molds.  
6. Gently tap the molds to release any air bubbles inside.  
   **Note**: Removing bubbles helps the final product become more compact and smooth.  
7. Insert a wooden ice pop stick into each mold cavity, ensuring it reaches the bottom and stands firmly.  
8. Place the molds in the refrigerator freezer. Freeze until the batter is completely set.  
   **Note**: Freeze thoroughly to ensure the pops hold their shape when unmolded.

## Health Advice  
These old-style ice pops are based on egg yolks and sweetened condensed milk, giving them a rich, creamy flavor. They are a great summer treat but are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat only one at a time. You may substitute some whole milk with skim milk and reduce the amount of sugar to help control blood sugar fluctuations.
