# Jade Stir-Fried Mandarin Fish Rolls
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Mandarin Fish (or Sea Bass) appropriate amount  
🌿 Scallions appropriate amount  
🍄 Dried Shiitake Mushrooms appropriate amount  
🥩 Ham appropriate amount  
🥬 Chinese Broccoli (Gai Lan) appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount  
🍶 White Wine (or Cooking Wine) appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for dusting and thickening)

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Slice the fish**: Slice the fish fillet into double-thin slices about 0.3 cm thick.  
- Key point: Even thickness, ensure the fish skin remains attached.

2. **Marinate**: Gently rub a pinch of salt into the fish slices to give them a base flavor.  
3. **Prepare the filling**: Cut scallions, dried shiitake mushrooms, and ham into small strips or shreds suitable for rolling.  
4. **Roll**: Lay the fish slices flat with the skin side inward, place the filling on the near end, and carefully roll into fish rolls.  
- Key point: Roll with the skin side inward to prevent unrolling during cooking.

5. **Secure**: Gently dust the sealed end of the fish roll with a thin layer of cornstarch to act as a binder.  
6. **Stir-fry the vegetables**: Heat the wok, add oil, sauté ginger slices until fragrant, then add Chinese broccoli and stir-fry over high heat. Season with salt and sugar, cook until just done, then remove and arrange on the plate as a base.  
- Key point: Ensure sufficient wok hei (breath of the wok) to keep it crisp and bright green.

7. **Oil-blanch**: Heat oil in the wok to about 30-40% hot, gently add the fish rolls, and cook over medium-low heat for about 1 minute until they float, turn white, and set. Remove and drain excess oil.  
- Key point: Oil temperature should not be too high; handle gently to prevent them from falling apart.

8. **Braised cooking**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add ginger slices, then the blanched fish rolls. Splash a little white wine along the wok edge to release fragrance. Add water or broth and bring to a boil. Thicken with a thin cornstarch slurry, ensuring the sauce evenly coats the fish rolls.  
9. **Plating**: Pour the braised fish rolls and sauce over the Chinese broccoli. Garnish with a few slices of red bell pepper or ginger slices.

## Health Advice  
This dish features fish, which is high in protein and low in fat, paired with shiitake mushrooms and ham for added flavor. It is suitable for those needing quality protein while controlling calorie intake. However, ham has a high salt content, so people with high blood pressure should moderate their consumption.
