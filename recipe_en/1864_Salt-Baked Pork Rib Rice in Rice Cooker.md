# Salt-Baked Pork Rib Rice in Rice Cooker
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork ribs, appropriate amount (cut into strips)
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (cut into strips)
🍚 Zengcheng Simiao rice, appropriate amount (rinsed)
🫚 Ginger, 3-4 slices (for marinating and steaming)
🥕 Carrot, 1 (shredded)
🥬 Chinese cabbage, 1 (cut into wide strips)
🧄 Fried garlic paste, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt-baked powder, half a packet
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🌿 Black pepper, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker (with micro-pressure function)
🍽️ Steaming rack
🔪 Cutting board + kitchen knife
🍳 Non-stick pan + lid
🥢 Tongs
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🍲 Bowls and plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the pork ribs and pork belly into strips, place in a container, add ginger slices, half a packet of salt-baked powder, and an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Mix well and marinate until flavorful.
**Note**: Ensure the meat surface is evenly coated with the seasonings and fully marinated.
2. Place the rinsed Simiao rice into the rice cooker inner pot and add water.
**Note**: As steam from the meat will drip into the rice during cooking, reduce the water amount slightly to prevent the rice from becoming too soft.
3. Shred the carrot, cut the Chinese cabbage into wide strips, and prepare the fried garlic paste.
4. Place the steaming rack on top of the rice inside the rice cooker inner pot, then arrange the marinated meat along with the ginger slices on the rack.
**Key point**: Use the steaming rack to create layers, allowing the meat juices to drip into the rice for extra flavor while preventing the meat from being directly submerged in water.
5. Close the rice cooker lid, select the rice cooking function, and set the timer for 30 minutes.
**Status check**: When the program finishes, the ribs should be fully cooked and the salt-baked aroma should be rich.
6. Add a small amount of water and the fried garlic paste to a non-stick pan, spread the cabbage and shredded carrot on top, cover with the lid, and simmer over medium-low heat for 3-4 minutes.
**Status check**: The vegetables are done when they become tender and their colors brighten.
7. Add oyster sauce and black pepper to the simmered vegetables, then toss evenly with tongs.
**Note**: Season after cooking to preserve the fresh, crisp texture of the vegetables.
8. Remove the steaming rack, use kitchen scissors to cut the ribs into small pieces, and serve together with the rice and vegetables on a plate.

## Health Advice
This salt-baked pork rib rice combines meat and vegetables in one pot for balanced nutrition. However, salt-baked powder and oyster sauce are high in sodium. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount or pair with light vegetables. A micro-pressure rice cooker preserves the natural flavor of ingredients, making this an ideal one-dish meal for busy workdays.
