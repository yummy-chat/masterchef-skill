# Pineapple Bun (Bo Luo Bao)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Year Round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Unsalted Butter 66g (54g for topping + 12g for dough)  
🍬 Icing Sugar 44g  
🥚 Egg Yolk 1 (approx. 17g, at room temperature)  
🌾 Cake Flour 86g  
🌾 Bread Flour 200g  
🥛 Milk Powder 6g  
💧 Water 102g  
🥛 Milk 18g  
🥚 Whole Egg Liquid 20g (for dough) + as needed (for brushing)  
🍯 Condensed Milk 6g (can substitute with maltose or honey)  
🍄 Fresh Yeast 6g (or 2g instant dry yeast)  
🍬 Caster Sugar 18g  
🧂 Salt 3.5g (0.5g for topping + 3g for dough)  
🧈 Frozen Butter 1 slice (for filling)  
🍎 Hawthorn Roll 1 slice (for filling)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Dough Scraper  
🥢 Rolling Pin  
📄 Parchment Paper  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🥄 Spatula  
🖌️ Pastry Brush  
🔥 Oven  
🕒 Timer  
🍽️ Baking Tray  
🧺 Cooling Rack  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the Pineapple Topping**  
   Place 54g unsalted butter and 0.5g salt on the work surface. Use the palm of your hand to press and soften until smooth. Add 44g icing sugar, continue rubbing evenly with your palm. Add 1 room-temperature egg yolk, mix with a spatula. Finally, add 86g cake flour, cut and mix into a dough using the spatula, avoid over-kneading.  
   **Note**: Operate entirely with hand warmth or a spatula to prevent the topping from releasing oil or developing gluten; the egg yolk must be at room temperature.

2. **Chill the Topping**  
   Roll the topping dough into a log, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate until firm.  
   **Key**: Chilling allows the butter to re-solidify, making it easier to roll out and cover later.

3. **Mix the Bread Dough**  
   Combine 200g bread flour and 6g milk powder, make a well in the center on the work surface. Pour in 102g water, 18g milk, 20g whole egg liquid, 6g condensed milk, 6g fresh yeast, and 18g caster sugar in order. First mix the liquids, then gradually incorporate the surrounding flour, kneading into a dough with no dry bits.

4. **Knead with the Delayed Butter Method**  
   Knead the dough until initially smooth. Add 12g softened unsalted butter and 3g salt. Repeatedly knead using the heel of your hand, combined with slapping motions, until the butter is fully absorbed, the dough is soft, smooth, non-sticky, and elastic.  
   **Doneness Check**: Should be able to pull a thin membrane with slightly jagged edges.  
   **Note**: When the dough is initially sticky, do not add extra flour; persist in kneading.

5. **First Partial Fermentation**  
   Round the dough, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until 1.5 times its original size, achieving a partially fermented state.

6. **Divide and Shape into Rounds**  
   Divide the dough evenly into 6 portions. Fold each portion, then roll into a tight ball, pinching the seam firmly closed and placing it seam-side down.  
   **Note**: Do not make the seam area too thick.

7. **Cover with Topping**  
   Take the chilled topping log and cut it into 6 pieces, each about 25g. Roll each piece into a thin round disc between two sheets of parchment paper. Peel off the top paper and place the disc over a dough ball. Gently cup your hand around it to make the topping adhere to the upper part of the dough.  
   **Key**: The topping must be rolled thin; this step should be done before the second fermentation.

8. **Second Fermentation**  
   Line a baking tray with parchment paper and place the topping-covered dough balls on it. Keep the environment temperature and humidity moderate, and let them dry-ferment for about 60 minutes.  
   **Readiness Indicator**: The topping will naturally crack as the dough expands.  
   **Note**: High temperature and humidity will cause the topping to melt and the cracks to be unclear.

9. **Brush with Egg Wash and Bake**  
   Once fermentation is complete, evenly brush the topping surface with whole egg liquid. Bake in a preheated oven until the surface is golden brown and the cracks are clearly visible.  
   **Tip**: Adjust baking time according to the browning level.

10. **Cool and Assemble**  
    Transfer to a cooling rack immediately after baking. Allow to cool completely. Slice the buns horizontally through the middle and sandwich with one slice of frozen butter and one slice of hawthorn roll.  
    **Key**: The topping is soft when freshly baked and becomes crispy only after cooling; the frozen butter provides a cold, creamy contrast.

## Health Advice
This pineapple bun is high in fat and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the flavor. It is suitable for occasional enjoyment and not recommended as a daily breakfast. The frozen butter filling is high in calories; it can be omitted or replaced with Greek yogurt during weight loss periods.
