# Red Bean Ice Pops  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Red beans 250g (half a jin, pre-soaked for 6 hours)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel to taste (optional, for aroma and appetite stimulation)  
🟤 Brown sugar to taste (provides sweetness and color)  
🥛 Milk to taste  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour to taste (for a smooth, creamy texture)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Soaking container  
🍲 Steamer  
🌀 Food processor (or blender)  
🍳 Cooking pot  
🥄 Stirring tool  
🧊 Ice pop molds (with sticks)  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Instructions  
1. Drain the soaked red beans, add the dried tangerine peel, place in a bowl, and steam in a steamer for 2 hours until the beans are soft and mushy.  
2. Remove the steamed red beans, take half, and blend in a food processor into a fine red bean paste.  
   **Key point**: Mashing half into a paste while keeping the other half as whole grains is crucial for texture variety.  
3. Mix the red bean paste with the remaining whole red beans, and stir in brown sugar until well combined.  
4. In another bowl, combine milk and glutinous rice flour, stirring into a smooth, lump-free slurry.  
5. Pour the milk and glutinous rice flour slurry into the red bean mixture, and stir thoroughly again.  
6. Pour the combined liquid into a pot, heat until boiling, then immediately turn off the heat and let it cool to room temperature.  
   **Key point**: It must be completely cooled before pouring into molds; otherwise, freezing may cause ice crystals, affecting the smooth texture.  
7. Pour the cooled red bean paste mixture into ice pop molds, and insert the sticks.  
8. Place in the freezer compartment of the refrigerator and freeze overnight until set, then unmold and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Ice pops made with red beans and milk are rich in iron and calcium, making them suitable for cooling down in summer with relatively low calories. Pay attention to controlling sugar content; glutinous rice flour can enhance texture but should not be used excessively.
