# Seed Toast  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 French bread flour 1000g  
💧 Water 650g (of which 208g added later is ice water, used in summer)  
🎃 Pumpkin seeds 80g  
🌰 Flaxseeds 150g  
🤍 White sesame seeds 150g  
🌻 Sunflower seeds 120g  
🍞 Pre-ferment (or sourdough starter) 200g (hydration around 65%)  
🫧 Fresh yeast 10g (or dried yeast 3.3g)  
🧂 Salt 20g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔥 Oven (for roasting seeds and baking the toast)  
🌀 Stand mixer (for kneading dough)  
🌡️ Proofer (for final fermentation, controlling temperature and humidity)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
📦 Toast mold  
🍽️ Baking tray  
🪒 Dough scraper  
🧤 Oven mitts  
🥄 Measuring cup  
🫙 Lidded fermentation container (spray with oil to prevent sticking)  
🛏️ Canvas (for resting after pre-shaping)  

## Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Spread pumpkin seeds, flaxseeds, white sesame seeds, and sunflower seeds evenly on a baking tray. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (355°F) for 8 minutes, until lightly colored. Remove and let cool.  
   **Key point**: Use raw seeds; bake until just lightly colored to avoid burning.  
2. Transfer the roasted seeds to a bowl, add 450g of boiling water, mix well with a dough scraper, and let sit until the water is fully absorbed. Then refrigerate until ready to use.  
   **Key point**: Let sit until the water is fully absorbed; refrigerate for freshness.  
3. Pour the French bread flour into the stand mixer. Add part of the 650g water, mix on low speed until a shaggy dough forms with no dry flour. Remove the dough, place it in a container, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Mix until no dry flour remains; refrigeration helps develop gluten and control dough temperature.  

### Production Phase  
1. Place the autolyzed dough into the stand mixer. Add the pre-ferment, fresh yeast, and salt. Mix on low speed until fully combined. Gradually add the remaining 208g of ice water, mixing on low speed until each addition is absorbed before adding the next. Finally, mix on high speed until the dough is smooth and no longer sticks to the bowl, and can be stretched into a thick membrane that doesn't tear easily. Control the dough temperature to around 21°C (70°F).  
   **Texture check**: Dough should not stick to hands, stretch into a thick membrane, and the temperature should not exceed 22°C (72°F).  
   **Key point**: Use ice water in summer to control temperature; ensure each addition of water is fully absorbed before adding the next to avoid damaging the gluten.  
2. Add all the refrigerated, soaked seeds to the main dough. Mix on low speed until evenly distributed and the dough no longer sticks to the bowl.  
   **Key point**: Mix slowly to avoid overworking and damaging the seeds' integrity.  
3. Turn the dough out onto a baking tray dusted with whole wheat flour. Shape it into a rectangle, dust the surface with flour, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 40 minutes. After resting, fold the dough once from bottom to top, place it into a sprayed, lidded fermentation container. Cover and refrigerate for 8 hours or overnight, until the dough has doubled in size.  
   **Texture check**: Dough should double in size, with uniform air bubbles inside.  
   **Key point**: Spray the container with oil to prevent sticking; cold fermentation enhances flavor.  
4. Remove the fermented dough, dust the surface with flour, invert it onto a work surface, gently shape it into a rectangle, and divide into pieces of 360g each. Pre-shape: Place any loose pieces at the bottom, keep the smooth side outward, fold lengthwise into thirds, then lightly roll. Place on a dusted canvas, cover with the canvas, and let rest for 30 minutes.  
   **Texture check**: Dough should not spring back after resting, making final shaping easier.  
   **Key point**: Pre-shaping organizes the dough and creates initial gluten tension.  
5. After resting, take out the dough pieces, gently pat to release gas, fold lengthwise into thirds, and lightly roll again. Place seam-side down into each toast mold.  
   **Key point**: Gently pat to release gas without overworking; preserve bubbles during rolling for a fluffier result.  
6. Place the toast molds into a proofer at 32°C (90°F). Ferment for 50–60 minutes, until the dough rises to about 80–85% full of the mold.  
   **Texture check**: Dough should reach 80% full; a gentle press should spring back slowly.  
   **Key point**: Temperature should not be too high; judge based on the dough height.  
7. Preheat the oven to top heat at 240°C (465°F) and bottom heat at 225°C (435°F). Place the molds into the oven, inject 6 seconds of steam, and bake for about 20 minutes, until the surface is golden brown and tapping the bottom produces a hollow sound. Remove from the oven, unmold, and let cool on a wire rack.  
   **Texture check**: Surface golden brown, hollow sound when tapped.  
   **Key point**: Injecting steam delays crust formation, helping oven spring; unmold immediately to prevent shrinkage.  

## Health Tips  
This seed toast is rich in dietary fiber and high-quality fatty acids, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those managing blood sugar levels. However, it is not low in calories, so it is recommended to pair slices with protein-rich foods for a balanced meal. Note that seeds need to be roasted beforehand for aroma, and cold fermentation can slow aging but avoid over-proofing.
