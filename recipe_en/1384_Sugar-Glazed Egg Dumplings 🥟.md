# Sugar-Glazed Egg Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 3 (beaten until smooth)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped, half for sautéing, half for final garnish)
🧄 Garlic, 1 tablespoon (minced)
🫑 Red and yellow bell peppers, as needed (diced)
🧅 Large scallion, as needed (diced)
🫓 Dumpling wrappers, as needed

🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍬 White sugar, generous amount (for the sugar-glaze main flavor)
🫒 Sesame oil, a small amount
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, as needed (used twice: for the sauce and final drizzle)
🌡️ Hot water, a small amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (for color)
🧂 Oyster sauce, as needed
🌾 Water starch, as needed (for thickening)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Non-stick skillet (two)
🫖 Kettle or thermos for hot water
🥄 Spoon
🪓 Rolling pin, cutting board (if making dumpling wrappers from scratch)
🥘 Stockpot

## Preparation Steps
1.  Crack eggs into a bowl and beat, whisking in one direction until the mixture is smooth and free of strings.
   **Key**: Thorough mixing yields a fine texture, making the eggs fluffier when stir-frying.
2.  Heat oil in a skillet to 70% hot, then add half the scallions and minced garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
   **Key**: The oil must be hot enough; add the egg liquid immediately after the scallions and garlic release their aroma to avoid burning.
3.  Slowly pour the egg liquid into the skillet from a height, allowing it to expand rapidly upon contact with the heat.
   **Key**: Pouring from a height creates a softer, fluffier, shredded texture.
4.  Quickly break the eggs apart with chopsticks, stir-frying over medium-high heat until they become fine, slightly stringy, tender yellow shreds. Remove and set aside.
   **Heat Control**: Maintain medium-high heat throughout to prevent the egg pieces from becoming tough.
5.  In another skillet, combine light soy sauce, white sugar, sesame oil, fragrant vinegar, a small amount of hot water, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat.
6.  Reduce heat to medium and stir continuously, simmering until the sauce is thick, translucent, and noticeably coats a spoon.
   **Consistency Check**: The sugar glaze should be sweet and sour with a thick texture, flowing slowly, and leaving clear marks when a spatula is drawn through.
7.  Add the stir-fried eggs, diced large scallion, and diced red and yellow bell peppers to the sauce. Toss to combine evenly.
8.  Drizzle in the second portion of fragrant vinegar, sprinkle the remaining scallions, then add the water starch to thicken. Stir-fry quickly until the filling is evenly coated in the sauce and fragrant, then turn off the heat.
   **Key**: The filling must reach a "clinging and glossy" state to ensure the dumplings are juicy when bitten.
9.  Take a dumpling wrapper, fill it with an appropriate amount of filling, and pinch the edges tightly to seal.
10. Fill a stockpot with water and bring to a rolling boil. Add the dumplings and cook until they all float to the surface and the wrappers are slightly puffed. Remove and serve.
    **Doneness Check**: After the water boils, add the dumplings. They are cooked once they float; no need to boil for a long time.

## Health Advice
Sugar-glazed egg dumplings provide high-quality protein from eggs, but the generous amount of white sugar results in a high glycemic index. They are suitable for occasional enjoyment rather than as a daily staple. Consider reducing the sugar amount and pairing with vegetables to balance the flavor. Diabetics should consume with caution.
