# Steamed Strawberry Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍓 Strawberries 1 box (use intact ones for decoration, dice the imperfect ones for jam)  
🥚 Eggs 3 (separate egg yolks and whites)  
🌾 Glutinous rice flour approx. 70g (3 measures, can substitute with all-purpose flour or rice flour)  
🥛 Milk approx. 60g (2 measures, can substitute with water)  
🥛 Heavy cream approx. 200g (half bottle, for filling and frosting; can substitute with yogurt)  
🌽 Cornstarch approx. 10g (2 bottle caps, to stabilize the meringue)  
🍬 White sugar approx. 15g (2 bottle caps, for whipping cream)  
🍬 White sugar approx. 50g (6 bottle caps, for whipping meringue)  
🍋 Lemon 1 slice (squeeze juice, for jam and meringue stabilization)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (as cake mold)  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Saucepan (for making jam)  
🍾 Plastic bottle (modified into a whipping tool)  
🛡️ Plastic wrap (to cover batter and prevent dripping)  
🥄 Spatula (for folding batter)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and decorating)  
🥢 Chopsticks (to check batter consistency)  
🧺 Basket (for cooling cake upside down)  

## Steps
1. **Prepare strawberries**: Wash the strawberries thoroughly. Select intact ones for decoration, and cut the rest into small dices.
2. **Make strawberry jam**: Place the diced strawberries, 1 tablespoon of white sugar, and the juice from 1 slice of lemon into the saucepan. Cook over medium-low heat until thick, then set aside to cool.  
   **Note**: Lemon juice helps improve the color and texture of the jam.
3. **Prepare the mold**: Take a round stainless steel bowl. Lightly grease the bottom of the bowl with oil, but do not grease the sides.  
   **Key**: Only greasing the bottom makes it easy to unmold; not greasing the sides helps the cake rise.
4. **Separate the eggs**: Separate the egg whites and yolks into two clean, dry bowls (free of oil and water).
5. **Make the egg yolk batter**: Add the glutinous rice flour and milk to the bowl with egg yolks. Stir with chopsticks until smooth, the batter should form layered ribbons when lifted and fall slowly.  
   **Consistency check**: The batter needs to be thick enough; if it is too thin, the cake may collapse during steaming.
6. **Prepare the steamer**: Before whipping the meringue, fill the steamer with enough water and bring it to a boil.  
   **Note**: Add enough water at once, as the cake needs to steam for about 50-60 minutes without adding more water midway.
7. **Whip the meringue**:  
   - Add lemon juice to the egg whites and whip manually using the modified plastic bottle.  
   - After about 2 minutes, when the egg whites show lines, add the white sugar in batches.  
   - When almost done, add the cornstarch and continue whipping until the meringue can form stiff, upright peaks and does not fall when the bowl is turned upside down.  
   **Key**: The meringue must be whipped to the right consistency, otherwise the cake will not rise properly.
8. **Combine the batters**: Take a small amount of meringue and fold it into the egg yolk batter until combined. Then pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue. Using a spatula, quickly and gently fold by "turning the bowl with your left hand and cutting with your right hand" until no air bubbles are visible and the batter is thick and uniform.  
   **Note**: Be careful with the folding technique to avoid deflating the batter.
9. **Steam the cake**: Pour the batter into the mold, gently tap to release large air bubbles, and cover with plastic wrap to prevent steam from dripping. Once the water is boiling, place the cake in the steamer and steam over medium heat for 55-60 minutes. After turning off the heat, let it sit for 3-5 minutes before opening the lid.  
   **Key**: Covering with plastic wrap is crucial to prevent collapsing; resting after steaming is essential to avoid sudden shrinkage from temperature change.
10. **Cool and unmold**: Remove the cake bowl, invert it onto a basket, and let it cool until warm to the touch. The cake is done if it springs back when pressed. Then unmold it.
11. **Make the cream and filling**:  
    - Pour the heavy cream and white sugar into the bottle. Tighten the lid and shake vigorously until the cream solidifies and no liquid sloshes around.  
    - Slice the cake horizontally into two layers. Place one cake layer in a bowl lined with plastic wrap. Pipe a layer of cream and spread it flat. Spread strawberry jam and diced strawberries on top, then pipe another layer of cream. Place the second cake layer on top and press gently.  
    - Place in the freezer for 15 minutes to set.  
    **Note**: Use the bottle-shaking method to whip the cream; freezing helps stabilize the cake structure.
12. **Decorate**: Unmold the set cake onto a plate. Spread the remaining cream on the surface using a spoon. Transfer the cream from the bottle into a piping bag (or a ziplock bag with a small corner cut). Pipe cream blobs and use the back of a spoon to shape them. Finally, arrange the reserved strawberries on top, and optionally dust with powdered sugar or milk powder.  
    **Style**: Decoration is casual, aiming for a natural layered look.

## Health Advice  
Glutinous rice flour and heavy cream make this cake relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with black tea or black coffee to balance the sweetness. The steaming method is relatively healthier and suitable for spring gatherings or occasional indulgence. However, diabetic patients should control their portion sizes.
