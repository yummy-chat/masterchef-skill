# Black Forest Cake

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍒 Fresh cherries 300g (pitted) + 8–10 pieces (for decoration)  
💧 Water 150g  
🍬 Caster sugar 30g (for maceration) + 15g (for egg yolks) + 90g (for egg whites)  
🥃 Rum 60g  
🥚 Egg yolks 5  
🥚 Egg whites 5  
🍫 Cocoa powder 12g  
🌾 Cake flour 75g  
🧈 Melted unsalted butter 25g  
🍫 Dark chocolate, as needed (for chocolate shavings)  
🥛 Whipping cream, as needed (whipped to stiff peaks)

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Stainless steel pot  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍴 Spatula  
🧂 Sieve  
🔥 Oven (preheat to 150°C)  
🎂 8-inch round cake pan  
🔪 Bench scraper  
🎀 Piping bag with piping tip  
🖌️ Pastry brush  
🌀 Turntable  
🔪 Cake server/knife

## Instructions
1. **Make the boozy cherries**: Wash and pit the cherries. Place them in a pot with water, caster sugar, and rum. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 5 minutes without covering the pot (to prevent alcohol vapor from flaring). Remove from heat and let cool. For best flavor, prepare a day in advance.
2. **Make the chocolate shavings**: Melt the dark chocolate over a water bath, then pour it onto a clean work surface. Use a bench scraper to quickly spread it into a thin, even layer. Let it set at room temperature or in the refrigerator until fully firm.
3. **Make the cocoa sponge cake base**  
   - Separate the egg yolks from the egg whites. Add caster sugar and cocoa powder to the yolks and mix until well combined.  
   - In a separate clean, grease-free mixing bowl, place the egg whites. Gradually add the caster sugar while whipping until stiff peaks form.  
   - Fold one-third of the meringue into the yolk mixture until just combined. Then pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue. Use a spatula to gently fold from the bottom up with quick, light motions; do not stir in circles to avoid deflating.  
   - Sift in the cake flour and fold until no dry flour remains.  
   - Take a small amount of batter and mix it with the melted butter, then pour it back into the main batter and fold quickly and evenly.  
   - Pour the batter into the 8-inch round cake pan. Gently tap the pan to release large air bubbles. Bake in the preheated oven at 150°C on the middle rack for about 55 minutes.  
   - Immediately invert the pan after baking, and let the cake cool completely before unmolding.
4. **Assembly and decoration**  
   - Horizontally slice the cake into 2 or 3 layers.  
   - Place one cake layer on a turntable. Use a pastry brush to evenly soak it with the liquid from the boozy cherries. Pipe a layer of whipped cream and spread the boozy cherries on top.  
   - Place the second cake layer on top, and repeat the brushing, cream piping, and cherry spreading. If slicing into three layers, repeat the process.  
   - Evenly spread a thin layer of whipped cream over the top and sides of the cake for a crumb coat.  
   - Take the set chocolate and scrape it into shavings with a bench scraper. Evenly coat the entire surface and sides of the cake with the shavings, gently pressing them into place.  
   - Use the back of a knife to lightly mark the cake surface into serving portions.  
   - Along these marks, pipe cream rosettes using a piping bag, and place one fresh cherry with stem on each rosette.  
   - Refrigerate before serving. Suggested to cut into 8–10 pieces.

## Health Advice
Black Forest cake contains antioxidant-rich cherries, but the sugar content is high after rum maceration. It is best enjoyed as a festive treat, with one slice per serving. Suitable for sharing with friends, but caution is advised for those with diabetes.
