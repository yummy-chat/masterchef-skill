# Braised Carp with Chinese Cabbage
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

🐟 Ingredients
- Carp (cleaned, about 1.2 jin / 600g)
- Baby Chinese cabbage / Chinese cabbage heart (13 leaves, can add more)

🧂 Seasonings
- Cooking oil (10ml)
- Ginger (3 slices)
- Garlic (3 cloves, cut into pieces)
- Salt (5-8g)
- Dark soy sauce (3ml)
- Light soy sauce (6ml)
- Cinnamon stick (1 piece)
- Star anise (3 pieces)
- Pixian bean paste (20g)
- Dried chilies (4-6, omit if you don’t eat spicy)

🍳 Cookware
- Wok / Braising pot
- Plate

📋 Steps
1. Kill and clean the carp, then make several cuts on both sides of the fish body to allow flavor to penetrate.
2. Trim and wash the baby Chinese cabbage, drain thoroughly, and place it on a plate for later use.
3. Heat the wok, add cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, add a little salt, ginger slices, garlic pieces, Pixian bean paste, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Stir-fry until fragrant.
4. Place the carp into the wok and pan-fry for 3 minutes, flipping every 30 seconds, so both sides of the fish body are evenly colored.
5. Add water, enough to nearly cover (or slightly less than) the fish body. Then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and the baby Chinese cabbage.
6. Braise over high heat for 15–20 minutes. When the sauce has reduced until slightly dry, season with salt. Remove from heat and serve on a plate.

## Health Advice
Carp is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. Pairing it with Chinese cabbage can supplement dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss individuals and daily nourishment. Note that the bean paste and soy sauce are high in salt, so the amount of added salt should be appropriately reduced.
