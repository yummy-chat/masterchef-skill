# Pan-Fried No-Knead Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour, about 250g (3 bottle cap measures)  
🧪 Dry yeast, about 3g (half a bottle cap)  
🧂 Salt, about 2g (inner bottle cap, place separately from yeast)  
🥚 Egg, 1 (about 50g)  
🥛 Thick yogurt, 1 box (about 200g, can substitute with milk but reduce by about 30g)  
🫒 Cooking oil, about 20g (3 bottle caps, can substitute with butter)  
🍵 Matcha powder, about 5g (2 bottle caps, for matcha flavor)  
🍫 Cocoa powder, about 5g (2 bottle caps, for chocolate flavor)  
🫘 Honey red beans, as needed  
🧀 Cheese block, 1 small piece  
🥚 Egg yolks, 2 (about 25g, for custard filling)  
🌽 Cornstarch, about 10g (2 bottle caps, for custard filling)  
🍬 Sugar, about 30g (4 bottle caps, for custard filling)  
🥛 Milk, about 120ml (half a box, for custard filling)  
🍋 Lemon zest, a pinch (for aroma)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🧴 Modified mineral water bottle (used as measuring cup and spoon)  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Small stainless steel saucepan  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (no oil needed for pan-frying)  
🫙 Glass lid  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. In a large bowl, measure the bread flour using the modified mineral water bottle. Add dry yeast, salt, crack in the egg, pour in the thick yogurt, and stir into a flaky mixture with chopsticks. Then add cooking oil and mix evenly to form a dough.  
   **Key point**: Only mix all ingredients until a dough forms; no kneading is needed. The dough being sticky is normal.  
2. Shape the dough in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment at room temperature until doubled in size with a dense honeycomb interior.  
3. Prepare the custard filling: Place egg yolks, cornstarch, sugar, and milk in a small saucepan. Stir thoroughly until smooth with no lumps and no dry powder.  
4. Place the mixture on the stove over the lowest heat. Stir continuously without stopping until the mixture thickens and clear lines remain when a chopstick is drawn through. Remove from heat, add a pinch of lemon zest for aroma if desired, then refrigerate until thickened for filling.  
5. Prepare fillings: Cook honey red beans in advance and refrigerate; cut cheese into small pieces; pipe the custard into a piping bag for later use.  
6. Sprinkle a small amount of flour on the work surface, take out the dough, and gently pat to release air. Do not knead. Use a dough scraper to divide it into about 8 equal portions. Lightly dust hands with flour to prevent sticking.  
7. Turn each small dough piece over, fold in half, then fold in half again. Shape into a ball with the tiger's mouth, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for about 10 minutes.  
8. Shape with filling: Take a rested dough piece, flatten it, turn it over, add a spoonful of honey red beans, and pinch the opening tightly like wrapping a bun. Gently flatten again, place a small piece of cheese, then pinch the opening tightly once more. Shape into a ball with the tiger's mouth, placing the seam side down. Cover the shaped dough with plastic wrap to prevent drying. Repeat for other flavors.  
9. Place the filled dough pieces into the ungreased frying pan. Cover with plastic wrap or a damp cloth, and let them proof again until noticeably larger, about 1.5 times in size, with reduced gaps between the pieces.  
10. Put the frying pan on the stove over the lowest heat. Cover with the lid and pan-fry for about 3 minutes until the bottom is browned. No oil is needed.  
11. Carefully flip the bread. Pour about 2 bottle caps of water into the gaps of the pan, quickly cover with the lid, and continue to pan-fry over the lowest heat for about 3 minutes and 30 seconds.  
    **Doneness check**: The water in the pan has evaporated, both sides of the bread are golden brown, and pressing the side with a spatula feels springy, indicating it is done.  
12. Remove the bread using a plate placed upside down or a spatula. It should be golden brown on both sides and overall fluffy.  

## Health Tips  
This no-knead bread uses yogurt to replace some of the liquid, allowing for better fermentation and is ideal for quick preparation when busy. The custard filling and honey red beans provide additional protein and dietary fiber, but have a higher sugar content, so portion control is recommended. The pan-frying method uses less oil and is healthier, making it suitable for home baking beginners.
