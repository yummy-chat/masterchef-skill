# Passion Fruit Sour Cabbage Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp fillet moderate amount (sliced by the vendor)
🦴 Fish bones moderate amount (remaining parts from the fillet)
🌱 Mung bean sprouts moderate amount (for the base)
🥬 Sour cabbage moderate amount (cut into sections)
🎍 Sour bamboo shoots moderate amount (sliced, personal preference)
🟠 Passion fruit 2 pieces (scoop out the pulp)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili moderate amount (chopped, some for garnish)
🌶️ Pickled wild chili moderate amount (chopped)
🧄 Garlic moderate amount (minced)
🧅 Scallion moderate amount (chopped)
🫚 Ginger a small piece (cut into pieces)
🧂 Salt 2 small spoons (for marinating fish) + appropriate amount (for blanching and soup)
🍬 Sugar 1 small spoon (for marinating fish) + appropriate amount (for blanching and soup)
🌫️ White pepper powder a pinch (for marinating fish)
🥚 Egg white 1 piece (for marinating fish)
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for marinating fish)
🍶 Rice wine or cooking wine a small amount (for marinating fish) + 50g (for deglazing when frying fish bones)
🥜 Peanut oil a little (added last to the marinated fish)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for blanching, stir-frying, and frying fish bones)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for washing, marinating, and holding)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for draining and removing ingredients)
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for cutting and preparing)
🍳 Iron wok (for blanching, stir-frying, frying, boiling, and sliding fish fillets)
🥘 Wok spatula or spatula (for stirring, tossing, and mixing)
🥣 Large soup bowl (for holding the base and presenting the final dish)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Rinse the grass carp fillets once with clean water and squeeze out the excess water; also wash the fish bones to remove surface blood.
2.  In the fish fillets, add 2 small spoons of salt, 1 small spoon of sugar, a pinch of white pepper powder, 1 egg white, an appropriate amount of cornstarch, and a small amount of rice wine or cooking wine. Mix thoroughly, then finally drizzle in a little peanut oil and mix well, ensuring the fillets are tender and smooth.
3.  Chop the bird's eye chili and pickled wild chili finely. Mince the garlic into garlic paste. Chop the scallion into green onion pieces. Cut the sour cabbage into sections. Slice the sour bamboo shoots. Cut the passion fruit in half and scoop out the pulp.
4.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add an appropriate amount of salt, sugar, and cooking oil. Blanch the mung bean sprouts until just cooked. Remove and drain, then spread them at the bottom of a large soup bowl.
**Note**: Blanch vegetables after the water is boiling to preserve nutrients and texture.

5.  Using a clean wok, stir-fry the sour cabbage and sour bamboo shoots over low heat until the moisture evaporates and a sour aroma is released. Remove and set aside. This removes the raw taste and enhances the fragrance.
6.  Add oil to the wok, sauté the ginger pieces over low heat until fragrant. Add the fish bones and pan-fry until both sides are slightly golden. Add the chopped pickled wild chili, deglaze with 50g of cooking wine, pour in approximately 2000 ml of water, and bring to a rapid boil for 2 minutes.
7.  Add the stir-fried sour cabbage and sour bamboo shoots to the broth and cook for 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt and sugar. Then remove all the fish bones and sour cabbage, and layer them over the mung bean sprouts in the bowl.
8.  Add the passion fruit pulp to the broth, keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Gently slide the marinated fish fillets into the wok one by one, avoiding stacking. After about 5 seconds, immediately pour the entire contents (broth and fish) into the large soup bowl. The residual heat will finish cooking the fillets gently.
9.  Sprinkle the minced garlic, chopped scallion, and bird's eye chili over the fish fillets. Pour hot oil (heated to approximately 180°C) over the toppings to sizzle and release their fragrance. Serve immediately.

## Health Suggestions
This dish replaces part of the vinegar acidity with passion fruit; the fruit acid brings a refreshing flavor that can enhance appetite. Grass carp fillets provide high-quality protein, paired with mung bean sprouts and sour cabbage to increase dietary fiber. Be careful not to cook the fish fillets for too long; relying on the residual heat to finish cooking makes them more tender. For a healthier diet, it is advisable to use less oil and salt and control the portion size.
