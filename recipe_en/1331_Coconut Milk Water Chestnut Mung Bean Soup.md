# Coconut Milk Water Chestnut Mung Bean Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Mung beans, half a bowl (pre-washed, add water, and freeze into ice cubes)
🥔 Water chestnuts, about one small bowl (peeled and diced)
🥥 Coconut milk, as needed
🍋 Lemon, 1 slice (squeeze juice)
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker (with inner pot)
🥄 Stirring spoon
🥣 Bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the frozen mung bean ice cubes, one bowl of water, and one slice of lemon into the rice cooker inner pot.
**Note**: Freezing allows the mung beans to "bloom" more easily; lemon juice helps maintain the vibrant green color of the mung beans.

2. Press the "Cook Rice" button and cook until the mung beans bloom.
3. Once cooked, add an appropriate amount of rock sugar and stir until dissolved.
4. Add the diced water chestnuts and an appropriate amount of coconut milk, and continue cooking for 2 minutes.
**Note**: The water chestnuts have a crisp texture, which is the highlight of this dessert soup; you can add coconut jelly for extra texture as desired.

5. Remove from heat and serve. Enjoy the refreshingly sweet and crunchy mung bean soup.

## Health Notes
Freezing mung beans before cooking allows them to break down quickly and saves energy, making them an ideal choice for summer refreshment. The crisp sweetness of water chestnuts and the rich aroma of coconut milk are suitable for those with stable blood sugar levels to consume in moderation. Lemon juice helps preserve color, but use sparingly to avoid excessive sourness that may affect the flavor.
