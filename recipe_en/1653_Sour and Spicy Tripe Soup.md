# Sour and Spicy Tripe Soup
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐷 Cooked Pork Tripe appropriate amount (shredded)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (shredded)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (shredded)  
🥬 Leafy Greens a small amount (shredded)  
🌼 Dried Daylily Buds a small amount  
🍄 Wood Ear Mushrooms a small amount  
🍜 Vermicelli a small amount  
🌿 Cilantro a small amount (sprinkled in at the end)  
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧂 Star Anise, Bay Leaves, and other spices a small amount  
🫒 Cooking Oil appropriate amount  
🍶 Aromatic Vinegar appropriate amount (needs to be sizzled)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken Bouillon Powder a small amount  
🌶️ Ground White Pepper Powder appropriate amount (main source of spiciness)  
🌶️ Pickled Chili Pepper Water appropriate amount  
💧 Water Starch appropriate amount (for thinning slurry)  
🫒 Sesame Oil a small amount (drizzled in before serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (Iron Wok)  
🥄 Soup Ladle  
🥄 Skimmer  
🥣 Small Mixing Bowls  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Shred the cooked pork tripe, ginger, scallion, leafy greens, wood ear mushrooms, and dried daylily buds into thin strips. Cut the dried chili peppers into sections. Prepare the star anise, bay leaves, and other spices.
2.  Heat the wok over high heat, pour in cooking oil. First, add the dried chili pepper sections, then add the star anise, bay leaves, and other spices. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
3.  Once the spices are fragrant, immediately pour in the aromatic vinegar. The vinegar will boil and produce steam upon contact with the hot wok. This is the "sizzling vinegar" step, which removes the raw, sharp sourness, making the sour aroma milder.
**Key Point**: The sizzling vinegar step must be done quickly. Add water immediately after pouring in the vinegar to prevent the vinegar's aroma from evaporating too much.
4.  Immediately after sizzling the vinegar, add water. Bring to a boil over high heat and let it simmer briefly to allow the chili and spice flavors to infuse into the broth. Then, use a skimmer to remove all the spice solids.
5.  Add the tripe shreds, ginger shreds, daylily buds, and wood ear mushrooms to the clear broth. These ingredients are relatively hardy, so add them first and cook for about 2 minutes.
6.  Once the soup returns to a boil, season with salt, chicken bouillon powder, and a generous amount of ground white pepper powder. The pepper powder is the main source of spiciness for this soup.
7.  After seasoning, add the vermicelli and leafy green shreds. Then pour in the pickled chili pepper water to enhance the sour and spicy flavor.
8.  Drizzle in the water starch, gently stir to create a thin slurry, which slightly thickens the soup and allows the ingredients to suspend naturally.
9.  Sprinkle in the scallion shreds and stir to combine. Turn off the heat and ladle the soup into bowls. Drizzle a circle of sesame oil over the top, and finally, garnish with cilantro. Serve immediately while hot for a sour and spicy, appetizing experience.

## Health Advice  
This Sour and Spicy Tripe Soup is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for warming the body and protecting the stomach during autumn and winter. However, it contains a high amount of pepper powder and vinegar. Individuals with excessive stomach acid or sensitive digestion should consume it in moderation. Slicing the pork tripe requires good knife skills; it is recommended to hand-cut it to ensure a good texture.
