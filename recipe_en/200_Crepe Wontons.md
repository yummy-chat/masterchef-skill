# Crepe Wontons
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 High-gluten flour 85g
🥔 Potato starch 15g
💧 Water (for dough) 45g
🧂 Salt (for dough) 1g
🥩 Ground pork 200g
🫚 Ginger 5-6g
💧 Water (for ginger water) about 55g
🍤 Shrimp paste/shrimp 100g
🌾 Wheat starch (as needed)

### Pork Filling Seasoning
🧂 Salt about 1g
🥄 Light soy sauce about 5g
🍬 Sugar about 2g
🌿 White pepper powder about 0.5g
🧂 MSG about 2g
🧂 Chicken bouillon about 1g

### Shrimp Paste Seasoning
🫒 Sesame oil about 4g
🧂 MSG about 1g

### Broth Ingredients
🧅 Chopped green onion and cilantro as needed
🌊 Seaweed as needed
🦐 Dried shrimp as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce as needed
🫒 Sesame oil as needed
🌿 White pepper powder as needed
🧂 MSG as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Rolling pin
🔪 Cutting board
🎁 Plastic wrap/zip bag
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍲 Pot

## Instructions
1. **Make dough**: Mix high-gluten flour, potato starch, and salt. Gradually add 45g of cold water, stir into flocculent, then knead into a smooth dough.
**Note**: The dough is slightly dry; knead until the surface is rough but not crumbly.

2. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 15 minutes. Knead once every 5 minutes (3 times total) until the dough becomes soft and smooth.

3. Seal the dough in a zip bag and let it rest for 1 hour.

4. **Make ginger water**: Crush ginger, add water, squeeze. Strain to obtain about 55g of ginger water.

5. **Season pork filling**: Add salt, light soy sauce, sugar, white pepper powder, MSG, and chicken bouillon to ground pork. Mix well. Add ginger water in 4-5 batches, squeezing each batch until the filling becomes dry and sticky before adding the next. Finally, stir in one direction until the filling is firm.

6. **Prepare shrimp paste**: Add MSG and sesame oil to shrimp paste, mix well. Then combine with the pork filling and mix evenly.

7. **Roll out wrappers**: Sprinkle a generous amount of wheat starch on the cutting board. Press the dough into a rectangle. Use a rolling pin to press up and down, then roll out lengthwise and widthwise. Repeatedly sprinkle starch to prevent sticking. Roll the dough sheet onto the rolling pin, press diagonally, crosswise, then push and roll up and down. Repeat multiple times until the sheet is extremely thin.
**Key point**: Use plenty of wheat starch throughout to prevent sticking. Apply even pressure to avoid tearing.

8. **Cut wrappers**: Fold the dough sheet, trim uneven edges, and cut into square wrappers about 7-8 cm wide.

9. **Wrap**: Place filling in the center of each wrapper. Gently squeeze opposite corners together, then fold and pinch the two sides to seal.
**Note**: The wrapper is very thin; handle gently to avoid tearing.

10. **Prepare broth**: In a bowl, add chopped green onion, cilantro, seaweed, dried shrimp, white pepper powder, MSG, a little light soy sauce, and sesame oil.

11. **Cook**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add wontons. Once they all float, cook for another 20-30 seconds.

12. **Serve**: Pour hot wonton broth into the bowl to dissolve the seasonings, then ladle in the wontons. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
These wontons have paper-thin wrappers and a juicy, tender filling, suitable for those with good digestive function. However, the pork filling is high in sodium, so it is recommended to use less light soy sauce and dried shrimp in the broth.
