# Old-Style Pork Trotters

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐷 2 pork front trotters (ask the market vendor to cut each into 4 pieces)  
🥜 100g white kidney beans (soak for at least 6 hours in advance)  
🫚 30g ginger (4 thick slices, for blanching and stewing)  
🧅 40g scallions (cut into sections, for blanching and stewing)  
🌶️ About 15 Sichuan peppercorns for stewing; 3g for the dipping sauce  
🧄 30g garlic (about 6 cloves, minced)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers, as needed (chopped)  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (chopped)  
🧂 10g salt for cleaning; as needed for seasoning the stew  
🍶 50g white vinegar (for cleaning)  
🍶 20g high-proof liquor  
🌿 2g Angelica dahurica  
🌿 2g Chinese angelica  
🌿 2g Codonopsis pilosula  
🌶️ 40g chili flakes (4 tablespoons)  
🍬 5g sugar (half a flat tablespoon)  
🥄 40g light soy sauce (4 tablespoons)  
🦪 20g oyster sauce (1 heaping tablespoon)  
🧂 3g MSG (1 teaspoon)  
🌶️ 1g ground Sichuan pepper (optional)  
🍶 Aged vinegar, as needed (optional)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🔧 Tweezers  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍲 Glass stew pot  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥘 Small pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
✂️ Scissors  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the Trotters**: Choose pork front trotters. Ask the market vendor to cut each into 4 pieces. Use tweezers to carefully remove any remaining hair and old skin, paying special attention to the nail crevices.  
**Key Point**: Front trotters have more meat and fewer bones; never scorch the trotter skin, as it will affect the soup's color.  
2. **Clean the Trotters**: Place the trotters in a basin. Dry rub with 10g of salt, especially on the skin. Add 50g white vinegar and rub again. Finally, rinse thoroughly with clean water until the water runs clear.  
**Key Point**: You must first dry rub with salt to use friction for decontamination, then add vinegar to rub and remove odor, and only then rinse with clean water.  
3. **Blanch**: Place the trotters in cold water. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, Sichuan peppercorns, and high-proof liquor. After the water boils, continue boiling over high heat for 5 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Boil for another 5 minutes after the water comes to a boil to fully remove the gamey smell. Since you will carefully rinse with warm water after blanching, you don't need to specifically skim off the foam.  
4. **Second Rinse**: Rinse the blanched trotters with warm water. Check and remove any remaining hair that wasn't pulled out.  
**Note**: Be sure to use warm water.  
5. **Stew**: Add about 3000g of clean water to a large pot and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, add the trotters, ginger slices, scallion sections, Sichuan peppercorns, Angelica dahurica, Chinese angelica, and Codonopsis pilosula. Keep the lid off and boil vigorously for 20 minutes. Cover the pot, leaving a small gap, then turn the heat to the lowest setting and stew for 1 hour. Add the soaked white kidney beans, turn the heat to high and bring to a boil, then reduce to the lowest heat and stew for another 2 hours.  
**Key Point**: Add enough water at one time; use a very small amount of aromatics, and absolutely do not add star anise, cinnamon, or cardamom; boiling uncovered over high heat helps remove odor and makes the soup whiter; do not add salt during the stewing process.  
6. **Make the Dipping Sauce**: Place the chili flakes in a bowl. In a separate small pot, heat oil over low heat. Add Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant, then remove the peppercorns. Pour the hot oil in batches over the chili flakes, stirring constantly. While still hot, add minced garlic, sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, MSG, ground Sichuan pepper, chopped cilantro, and chopped bird's eye chilies. Aged vinegar can be added to taste. Finally, add one spoonful of the original trotter stewing broth and stir to combine.  
**Key Point**: Fry the Sichuan peppercorns over low heat to prevent them from turning bitter; the hot oil must thoroughly saturate the chili flakes to release their aroma; using the original broth in the final step makes the dipping sauce more mellow.  
7. **Finishing and Plating**: After stewing is complete, use a spoon to skim off the layer of fat floating on the soup's surface. Season the soup with an appropriate amount of salt. Use a slotted spoon to gently lift the trotters out and place them on a plate. Use scissors to remove the aromatics and ginger slices from the soup. Ladle the milky white broth over the trotters. Serve with the dipping sauce and rice.  
**Key Point**: Skimming off the fat reduces greasiness; the trotters are extremely tender, so handle them gently to prevent them from falling off the bone and coming apart.

## Health Advice
Pork trotters are rich in collagen. When paired with kidney beans, it can promote nutrient absorption, making it suitable for those concerned with skin elasticity. However, trotters are high in purines, so people with gout should avoid eating them. Do not add salt during the stewing process, as the dipping sauce provides sufficient saltiness.
