# 🥕 Gajar Ka Halwa
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

🍽️ **Essential Ingredients**  
🥕 Carrots (red carrots recommended)  
🥛 Whole milk  
🍬 Sugar  
🧈 Ghee — can be substituted with butter  
🌿 Cardamom powder  
🥜 Cashews  
🌰 Almonds  
🍇 Raisins  

🔧 **Kitchen Tools**  
Grater  
Heavy-bottomed pot  
Small pot  

👩‍🍳 **Steps**  
1. Peel the carrots and grate them into fine shreds using a grater.  
2. In a heavy-bottomed pot, add half of the ghee (about 20g) and heat over medium heat until melted.  
3. Add the shredded carrots and stir-fry for 3 to 5 minutes, until the carrots are soft and fragrant.  
4. Pour in 500ml of whole milk and stir well. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and maintain a slow simmer.  
   **Key point**: For a richer texture, replace part of the milk with heavy cream.  
5. Stir continuously for 25 to 30 minutes, until the milk is almost completely absorbed and evaporated by the carrots.  
   **Key point**: This is the most critical step; stir continuously to prevent sticking to the bottom.  
6. When there is almost no liquid left in the pot, add 80g of sugar and stir well.  
7. Adding sugar will release more liquid; continue to stir-fry over medium-low heat for 5 to 8 minutes, until the moisture evaporates again.  
8. Add the remaining 20g of ghee and 3g of cardamom powder, and stir-fry for 2 minutes.  
9. In a separate small pot, fry the cashews, almond slices, and raisins in a small amount of ghee until golden (about 1 minute). Note that raisins will puff up.  
10. Mix half of the fried nuts and raisins into the carrot halwa.  
11. Plate the halwa and sprinkle the remaining nuts and raisins on top as decoration.  

**Note**: This halwa can be served hot or cold, each with a different flavor; using red carrots gives a better color; it can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 4 to 5 days, and reheated in the microwave for 1 minute before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This carrot pudding is rich in vitamin A and healthy fats, making it suitable for winter nourishment or for those needing energy. Note that the sugar and ghee content is relatively high; those controlling sugar intake or losing weight are advised to consume in small amounts.
