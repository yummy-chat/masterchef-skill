# Mutton Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Mutton, appropriate amount (any cut, cut into pieces)  
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced, used for soaking, blanching, and simmering)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (used for soaking and dipping sauce)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (used for soaking, blanching, and simmering)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, appropriate amount (chopped, used for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili, appropriate amount (chopped, used for dipping sauce)  
🌿 Cilantro, chopped, appropriate amount (used for dipping sauce and garnish)  
🧀 Leek fermented tofu, half a piece + a little red fermented tofu juice (used for southern-style dipping sauce)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (added when soaking to accelerate blood extraction)  
🌿 White pepper, a little (one of the "three whites")  
🌿 Angelica dahurica, a little (one of the "three whites")  
🌿 Amomum kravanh, a little (one of the "three whites")  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a little (to enhance flavor in dipping sauce)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a little (for seasoning dipping sauce)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little (to enhance umami and aroma in dipping sauce)  
🫒 Sesame oil, a little (to add fragrance to dipping sauce)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (heated and poured over dipping sauce)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking and preparing dipping sauce)  
🫕 Clay pot (for simmering mutton)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing mutton and assisting in filtering)  
🧻 Cheesecloth (for finely filtering the original broth)  
🥄 Soup ladle (for skimming foam)  
🔥 Gas stove (for heating, simmering on low heat)  
🥣 Small milk pot (for heating oil for dipping sauce)  

## Steps  
1. **Soak the Mutton**  
   Place the mutton in clean water, add ginger slices, garlic cloves, scallions, and a little salt. Soak until the meat turns noticeably pale.  
   **Note**: Adding salt uses osmotic pressure to accelerate blood extraction, enhancing the deodorizing effect.  

2. **Blanch**  
   Put the soaked mutton into a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, and promptly skim off any foam from the surface until no more substantial foam forms.  
   **Note**: Always start with cold water. Skim the foam thoroughly; this is key to removing unwanted odors.  

3. **Reserve the Original Broth**  
   Remove the blanched mutton, and carefully filter the blanching broth using a slotted spoon and cheesecloth to remove impurities and residue. Set the clear broth aside for later use.  

4. **Simmer**  
   Place the blanched mutton into a clay pot, add white pepper, angelica dahurica, amomum kravanh, ginger slices, and scallions. Pour in the filtered original broth. Simmer over low heat for about 90 minutes until the mutton is tender and the broth turns milky white.  
   **Note**: Only use the "three whites" for spices to gently enhance freshness and remove odor without overwhelming the mutton's natural flavor. Maintain a low heat with a gentle simmer throughout.  

5. **Prepare the Dipping Sauce**  
   - **Southern Style**: In a bowl, add mashed leek fermented tofu and red fermented tofu juice. Add chopped bird's eye chili, erjingtiao chili, and cilantro. Stir in chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Heat vegetable oil in a small milk pot until smoking, then pour it over the seasonings to release the aroma. Mix well.  
   - **Northern Style**: In a bowl, add minced garlic and cilantro. Stir in chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Pour sizzling hot vegetable oil over the mixture and mix well.  
   **Note**: The oil must be heated to a sufficiently high temperature to instantly release the aroma of garlic and chilies.  

6. **Serve**  
   Sprinkle a little fresh chopped cilantro over the finished mutton soup. Ladle the soup and meat into bowls and serve with the prepared dipping sauce.  

## Health Tips  
Mutton soup is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it an excellent tonic for winter. It is suitable for those with cold hands and feet, but people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Using the "three whites" to remove odor and enhance freshness during cooking reduces the need for additional seasonings, making it a healthier choice.
