# Roast Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Whole chicken 1.6kg  
🎃 Green-skinned pumpkin half  
🥕 Carrots about 3  
🥔 Potatoes about 2  
🍠 Sweet potato about 1  
🧄 Garlic 2 whole heads + 5 cloves (whole heads for roasting, 5 cloves for marinade)  
🌿 Cumin seeds 1/2 tsp  
🌿 Coriander seeds 1/2 tsp  
🌿 Cumin 1/2 tsp  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 1/2 tsp  
🧂 Salt 2 tsp (for marinade) + to taste (for roasted vegetables)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tsp (for marinade) + 1 tsp (for brushing)  
🫒 Olive oil as needed  
🍯 Honey 1 tsp  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1/2 tsp  
🌽 Cornstarch 1 tbsp (mixed with 1/4 cup water to make slurry)  
🧅 Scallions 2  
🌶️ Red chili peppers 3  
🌶️ Red chili powder to taste (for finishing vegetables)  
🌿 Cumin powder to taste (for finishing vegetables)

## Tools & Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🧂 Mortar and pestle  
🥘 Baking sheet (one large, one small for double baking sheet method)  
🖌️ Brush  
🥄 Strainer  
🥣 Bowl  
🔥 Oven  
🧤 Oven mitts

## Steps
1. **Prepare the whole chicken**: Place the chicken back side up. Using kitchen scissors, cut along both sides of the backbone and remove it. Flip the chicken over, make a cut at the breastbone cartilage, and press firmly to flatten the chicken. Pat the chicken dry inside and out with paper towels, and trim excess fat from the tail.
   *Key point: Flattening ensures even heat distribution and faster cooking.*

2. **Make the dry rub**: In a mortar, combine cumin seeds, coriander seeds, cumin, Sichuan peppercorns, and 2 tsp salt. Grind and mix well. Set aside a portion for later use.

3. **Make the wet marinade and marinate**: In the mortar, add 5 garlic cloves, scallion segments, and red chili peppers. Pound into a coarse paste. Add 1 tsp light soy sauce and mix well. Spread the wet marinade evenly on the inside of the chicken, and tuck some between the skin and breast meat. Place the chicken skin side up, thoroughly pat the skin dry with paper towels, and sprinkle the reserved dry rub evenly on the skin. Tuck the wing tips under the chicken.
   *Key point: The chicken skin must remain dry—this is key for achieving crispy, crackling skin.*

4. **Chill and air-dry**: Place the marinated chicken in the refrigerator for at least 12 hours to allow the skin to dehydrate further, resulting in extra crispiness after roasting.

5. **Prepare vegetables and assemble**: Cut pumpkin, carrots, potatoes, and sweet potato into evenly sized thick slices or wedges. Drizzle olive oil into a large baking sheet, toss the vegetables to coat, and spread a layer in the center. Place the air-dried chicken on top, surround it with the remaining vegetables, and scatter the whole garlic cloves on the surface. Place this baking sheet inside a larger empty baking sheet.

6. **Roast and color**:
   - Preheat the oven to 220°C. Place the baking sheet in the oven and roast for 40 minutes.
   - Remove from the oven. Mix 1 tsp light soy sauce with 1 tsp honey and brush onto the chicken skin.
   - Return to the oven and roast for another 10–15 minutes. Watch closely during the last 5 minutes; if browning too fast, loosely cover with foil; if browning too slowly, use the broiler function to assist. The thickest part of the thigh should reach an internal temperature of 71°C, and the skin should be deep golden or caramelized.
   - Meanwhile, sprinkle the vegetables with salt, cumin powder, and red chili powder to taste. If desired, use the broiler function to roast for about 5 minutes until edges are charred and fragrant, watching carefully.

7. **Rest and make sauce**:
   - Remove the roasted chicken from the baking sheet. Loosely tent with foil and let rest for 15 minutes to allow juices to redistribute.
   - Add a small amount of boiling water to the original baking sheet, scrape up the browned bits from the bottom, and heat briefly. Add the cornstarch slurry and stir until thickened. Add a touch of dark soy sauce for color, taste and adjust salt as needed, then strain through a fine-mesh strainer for a smooth sauce.
   - Spread the vegetables on a serving platter, place the rested chicken on top, fill the gaps with remaining vegetables and roasted garlic, and serve with the sauce on the side.

## Health Tips
This roast chicken is paired with a variety of root vegetables for a balanced meal rich in protein, dietary fiber, and vitamin A—suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those watching their weight. However, the chicken skin and honey glaze add extra calories, so consume in moderation. Chilling and air-drying the skin enhances crispiness; be sure to marinate for at least 12 hours in advance.
