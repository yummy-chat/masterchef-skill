# Bacon Wheat Ear Rolls 🥓🌾
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 250g
🍬 Caster sugar 25g
🫙 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerance) 3g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 30g (about the liquid of one egg)
💧 Water 130g
🧈 Butter 25g (softened at room temperature)
🥓 Bacon 4 slices
🧂 Salt 4g
🌶️ Black pepper, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪵 Wooden bowl (or mixing bowl)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Scraper/dough scraper
⏲️ Kitchen timer
🧻 Plastic wrap/damp cloth
📦 Proofing box (airtight container)
🥖 Rolling pin
🔥 Oven
✂️ Scissors
🖌️ Brush

## Instructions
1. Place water, whole egg liquid, caster sugar, and yeast into a bowl. Stir with a scraper until the sugar and yeast are roughly dissolved, then add the bread flour.
2. Mix until a shaggy dough forms with no dry flour. Knead by hand into a rough dough, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 25 minutes.
   **Key point**: Resting allows gluten to initially develop, saving effort in subsequent kneading.
3. After resting, knead gently a few times until the dough surface becomes smooth. Cut the dough open, add softened butter and salt, and use the scraper to cut and press the butter into the dough. Once the butter is absorbed, knead repeatedly until the dough is no longer sticky and the butter is fully and evenly incorporated.
4. Transfer the dough to a work surface. Knead and slap the dough for about 5 minutes until it is smooth and can be stretched into a thick membrane.
5. Round the dough and place it in a proofing box. Perform the first proofing at a room temperature of around 20°C for about 1 hour.
   **Note**: If the temperature increases by 10°C, proofing time can be reduced by about 5 minutes. If room temperature exceeds 30°C, proof at room temperature for half an hour, then transfer to the refrigerator for half an hour.
6. After proofing, turn the dough out onto a work surface. Gently pat to release air and divide into 4 equal portions using the scraper.
7. Take one portion, flatten it, fold it into a long strip, and let it rest for 15–20 minutes. Then use a rolling pin to roll it into an oval shape, the same length as a bacon slice, and about 2 centimeters wider than the bacon.
8. Place a slice of bacon on top, sprinkle with black pepper. Fold down the top edge of the dough about 1 centimeter over the bacon, then roll it up tightly from top to bottom, pinching the seam to seal.
   **Key point**: The tighter the roll, the fewer large air pockets inside the crumb.
9. Use scissors to make 8–9 evenly spaced cuts into the roll. Cut through the bacon with each cut, but leave the bottom of the dough intact. Then alternately fold the cut pieces to the left and right to form a wheat ear shape.
10. Arrange the shaped dough pieces on a baking sheet. Perform the final proofing at about 35°C with around 75% humidity for about half an hour, until the dough increases to 1.5–2 times its original size and feels light and springy to the touch.
11. Brush the surface of the proofed dough pieces evenly with a layer of whole egg liquid.
12. Bake in a preheated oven: for a home electric oven, bake at 180°C (top and bottom heat) for about 15 minutes; for a convection oven, bake at 165°C for about 11 minutes. The rolls are done when the surface is golden brown and the sides spring back quickly when lightly pressed.

## Health Advice
This bacon and bread combination is a classic pairing of carbohydrates and fats, suitable for a quick energy boost after exercise or as an occasional treat. Be mindful of additional salt intake. Individuals with high blood pressure can reduce salt and replace it with a sprinkle of black pepper for flavor.
