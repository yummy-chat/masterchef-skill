# Tiramisu
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫗 800 ml heavy cream (for homemade mascarpone) + 1 liter (for whipping)  
🥚 6 egg yolks  
🍬 1 tablespoon white sugar (for egg yolks) + 40-50 g (for whipped cream)  
💧 50 ml water (for egg yolks) + 300 ml (for preparing coffee liquid)  
🧀 About 600 g homemade mascarpone cheese (made from heavy cream and lemon juice)  
🍪 Ladyfinger biscuits, as needed (can substitute with fluffy biscuits, toast, or cake slices)  
☕ 2-3 boxes of espresso coffee liquid  
🥛 400 g whole milk (optional, to replace coffee liquid, suitable for children)  
🍋 Lemon juice, as needed (to curdle the cream)  
🌿 3 drops vanilla extract (optional)  
🍷 10 ml coffee liqueur (optional)  
🍫 Cocoa powder, as needed (for final decoration)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🪢 Strainer, cheesecloth  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Whisk  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🔧 Silicone spatula  
🪄 Sieve  
📦 Tin container  
🧵 Cotton string  

## Instructions  
1. **Make Homemade Mascarpone Cheese**: Pour 800 ml heavy cream into a bowl, add an appropriate amount of lemon juice, and quickly whip for about 1 minute until thick and curdled, resembling tofu sediment, with whey separating. Pour the mixture into a strainer lined with cheesecloth, gather the cloth tightly, and tie it with cotton string.  
   **Key Point**: Whip quickly; for draining, suspend it in the refrigerator to "drip-dry" overnight, or press with a weight for 4 hours to fully remove the whey and achieve a firm cheese block.  
2. **Make the Egg Yolk Mixture**: Separate the yolks from 6 eggs. Add 1 tablespoon of sugar and 50 ml water, and stir to combine. Pour into a non-stick pan, heat over the lowest flame, and stir continuously until the mixture thickens.  
   **Key Point**: Maintain the lowest heat and stir constantly to prevent the yolks from curdling; remove from heat once thickened.  
3. **Combine Egg Yolk and Cheese Mixture**: Gradually add about 600 g homemade mascarpone cheese to the warm egg yolk mixture, using an electric hand mixer to whip until fluffy and smooth.  
   **Key Point**: Adding the cheese in stages makes it easier to combine; whip until the volume increases.  
4. **Whip the Cream**: Pour 1 liter of chilled heavy cream into another bowl. Gradually add 40-50 g sugar and whip with an electric hand mixer on high speed for about 3 minutes, until the cream forms clear, stiff peaks.  
   **Key Point**: Cream must be chilled before whipping; whipping to stiff peaks is crucial.  
5. **Combine Final Cheese Mixture**: Pour the whipped cream into the egg yolk and cheese mixture, gently folding with a spatula until the color is uniform.  
   **Key Point**: Use a light folding motion to avoid deflating the cream.  
6. **Prepare the Dipping Liquid**: Mix 300 ml water, 2-3 boxes of espresso coffee liquid, and 10 ml coffee liqueur until well combined. Alternatively, use 400 g whole milk for a child-friendly version.  
7. **Assemble the Tiramisu**: Quickly dip ladyfinger biscuits on both sides in the coffee liquid and arrange neatly on the bottom of the tin container. Spread a layer of the cheese mixture over the biscuits and smooth it with a spatula.  
   **Key Point**: Dip biscuits briefly to prevent them from becoming too soft; repeat the steps to create two layers of biscuits and two layers of cheese mixture.  
8. **Chill and Set**: Cover the tin container and refrigerate for at least 5 hours.  
9. **Decorate and Serve**: Remove the set tiramisu from the refrigerator, dust the surface evenly with cocoa powder using a sieve, slice, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
The egg yolks in tiramisu need to be thoroughly heated to pasteurize; the homemade cheese ensures no risk of raw eggs, making it suitable for adult consumption. Dip biscuits in coffee liquid quickly to avoid them becoming too soft and turning into "tiramisu porridge." This high-energy dessert is ideal for sharing at parties, but those with diabetes or monitoring sugar intake should consume in moderation.
