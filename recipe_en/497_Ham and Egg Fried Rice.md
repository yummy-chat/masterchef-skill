# Ham and Egg Fried Rice  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 One bowl of rice (day-old or drier leftover rice)  
🥚 2-3 eggs  
🌭 2 ham sausages (cut into small cubes)  
🧅 One bunch of scallions  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧴 Light soy sauce, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥄 Spatula  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the ham sausages into small cubes. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them thoroughly with chopsticks until frothy.  
   **Note**: Beat well to incorporate air, which will make the scrambled eggs fluffier.  
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add cooking oil to coat the surface, and once the oil is hot, pour in the beaten eggs.  
   **Key**: High heat allows the egg to expand and set quickly. Stir rapidly with a spatula to keep it fluffy.  
3. Add the rice to the wok and stir-fry quickly, pressing down with the spatula to break up clumps and mix evenly with the eggs.  
   **Note**: Use day-old or drier leftover rice for best results, ensuring each grain stays separate.  
4. Add the ham sausage cubes and continue stir-frying evenly, allowing the salty-savory flavor of the ham to blend with the rice.  
5. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, letting the high heat of the wok wall release the sauce's aroma. Then stir-fry immediately to coat each grain of rice evenly.  
6. Keep stir-frying over high heat for about 40 seconds, allowing the flavors of egg, meat, and rice to fully meld, until the rice turns a uniform golden color.  
7. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in the chopped scallions. Toss gently for about 10 seconds, until the scallions are just cooked but still bright green, then turn off the heat and plate.  
   **Note**: Adding scallions at the end preserves their green color and fragrance. Stir gently to avoid crushing the rice.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is nutritionally balanced but high in sodium. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amounts of ham sausage and soy sauce. The eggs provide high-quality protein, making it a great choice for a quick energy boost on busy workdays.
