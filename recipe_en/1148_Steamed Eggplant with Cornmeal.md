# Steamed Eggplant with Cornmeal  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍆 Eggplant (peeled old eggplant) – to taste  
🌽 Cornmeal – to taste  
🥚 Egg – 1 (yolk only)  
🫑 Green and red peppers – to taste (finely shredded)  
🥜 Peanuts – to taste (chopped)  
🥄 White sesame seeds – to taste  
🧂 Fine salt – 5 g (for soaking eggplant) + to taste (for final seasoning)  
🌶️ Chili flakes – 1 tbsp  
🧄 Garlic – to taste (minced)  
🧂 MSG – to taste  
🍬 Sugar – to taste  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder – to taste  
🛢️ Cooking oil – to taste (for heating and splashing)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl (stainless steel bowl)  
♨️ Steamer  
🥢 Steaming rack (with steaming cloth)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Small bowl  

## Steps  
1. **Shred and soak**  
   Peel the old eggplant and cut into thin shreds. Place in a bowl, add 5 g fine salt and enough water to cover the eggplant. Soak for 5 minutes.  
   **Note**: Salting removes astringency and prevents oxidation (turning black).  
2. **Squeeze out water**  
   Remove the eggplant shreds and gently squeeze out excess water by hand. Place in another clean bowl.  
   **Key**: Retaining some moisture keeps the texture tender; squeezing too dry makes it hard and tough.  
3. **Coat with egg yolk**  
   Add 1 egg yolk to the eggplant shreds and mix evenly by hand, ensuring each shred is coated with egg mixture.  
   **Key**: Even coating of egg yolk is the base for the cornmeal to adhere.  
4. **Coat with cornmeal**  
   Gradually add cornmeal in small amounts while tossing and mixing by hand, until each eggplant shred is evenly coated with cornmeal and none stick together or form clumps.  
5. **Steam**  
   Evenly spread the coated eggplant shreds on a steaming rack lined with a steaming cloth. Cover the lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 8 minutes.  
6. **Remove and loosen**  
   Once steamed, take out and let cool slightly. Gently loosen by hand to prevent sticking into clumps.  
7. **Season and splash with hot oil**  
   Place the loosened steamed eggplant in a bowl. Layer with shredded green and red peppers, minced garlic, chopped peanuts, white sesame seeds, and chili flakes.  
   In another pan, heat cooking oil until smoking, then pour it over the chili flakes and garlic to release aroma.  
   Add salt, MSG, sugar, and thirteen-spice powder to taste. Mix evenly by hand, then plate.  

## Health Tip  
This dish is low in fat and high in fiber. Eggplant is rich in anthocyanins, and cornmeal provides B vitamins. It is suitable for those managing weight and blood sugar. Note that the egg yolk and chopped peanuts are not low in calories; control the amount of oil and salt when seasoning.
