# Pickled Chili Peanuts
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts, appropriate amount (pre-soaked until fully expanded and water-absorbed)
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves, appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, 99 pieces
🌶️ Pickled chilies, appropriate amount (with pickling liquid)
🧂 Salt, one tablespoon
🧂 Sugar, appropriate amount
🍶 Rice vinegar, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Pot (wok)
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour clean water into the pot, add cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and star anise. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer until fragrant.
2. When the water is slightly warm, add the Sichuan peppercorns.
   **Note**: Add the Sichuan peppercorns early so their flavor fully infuses the water.
3. Drain the pre-soaked peanuts and add them to the pot.
4. Add one tablespoon of salt.
5. Once the water returns to a boil, reduce to medium heat and cook for 10 minutes, until the peanuts are cooked through but still have a chewy texture.
6. Use a slotted spoon to remove the peanuts and transfer them to a clean glass bowl. Do not rinse with cold water.
7. Add the pickled chilies.
8. Add sugar and rice vinegar, adjusting the sweet-sour balance to personal taste.
9. Seal the glass bowl with plastic wrap.
10. Refrigerate for 1 hour. The flavor will improve after chilling for a better taste.

## Health Tips
Pickled chili peanuts are rich in protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making them a suitable side dish. The pickled chilies and salt content are relatively high; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. The texture becomes crisper after refrigeration, but be mindful of portion sizes.
