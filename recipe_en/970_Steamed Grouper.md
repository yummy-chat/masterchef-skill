# Steamed Grouper  
**Cuisine:** Cantonese  
**Cooking Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Grouper 1 (split open from the belly and flattened, score the thicker parts)  
🧂 Salt to taste (thinly coat for marinating)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (brush on fish for marinating, and for final hot oil drizzle)  
🫚 Ginger 2 slices (for removing fishy smell)  
🧅 Scallions as needed (whole stalks for steaming, some sliced into shreds for "three shreds")  
🧅 Chinese green onion (large scallion) as needed (sliced into shreds for "three shreds")  
🥕 Carrot as needed (sliced into shreds for "three shreds", can substitute red bell pepper shreds)  
🫙 Steamed fish soy sauce as needed (pour around the edge of the plate)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
♨️ Steamer  
🍽️ Plate  
🥢 Steamer tongs  
🖌️ Silicone brush  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking scallion and carrot shreds)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the Grouper**: Split the grouper open from the belly toward the back, flatten the body, and score the thicker parts.  
**Key point**: Splitting from the belly allows the fish to lie flat, increasing the surface area for even and faster cooking.  

2. **Marinate**: Evenly rub a thin layer of salt all over the fish, then brush a layer of oil.  
**Key point**: Salt gives a base flavor, while oil brightens the skin and keeps the flesh tender.  

3. **Prepare Pre-Steam Toppings**: Lay ginger slices and whole scallion stalks on the marinated fish.  
**Key point**: Ginger and scallion remove fishy odors and enhance aroma.  

4. **Prepare the "Three Shreds"**: Slice carrot, scallion, and Chinese green onion into fine shreds, then soak in water.  
**Key point**: Soaking keeps the scallion shreds fresh and naturally curly, improving the dish’s presentation.  

5. **Steam the Fish**: Bring water to a boil in the steamer, place the fish plate inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.  
**Key point**: The water must be boiling before adding the fish to ensure concentrated heat; start timing when steam emerges from the lid to quickly seal in freshness.  

6. **Remove and Clean**: After steaming, remove the lid, pour off most of the liquid from the plate, and use chopsticks to discard the cooked ginger and scallions.  
**Key point**: Discarding the liquid effectively reduces fishy odor.  

7. **Season and Drizzle with Oil**:  
   - Pour steamed fish soy sauce along the edge of the plate.  
   - Spread the drained "three shreds" over the fish.  
   - Evenly drizzle sizzling hot oil over the "three shreds" to release their aroma.  
**Key point**: Strictly follow the order of "soy sauce first, then hot oil" to use the high heat to unlock the sauce’s savory fragrance, creating the ideal flavor.  

## Health Tips  
Grouper is a low-fat, high-protein ingredient, suitable for weight loss or those needing protein supplementation. Steaming uses minimal oil and salt, preserving the fish’s tenderness, but steamed fish soy sauce is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should use it sparingly.
