# Tomato Meatball Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients  
🥩 Front leg pork, about 1 lb (30% fat, 70% lean, ground into mince)  
🍅 Tomatoes, 2 (large)  
🫘 Fresh soybeans, as needed  
🍄 Oyster mushrooms, as needed (tear into small pieces)  
🪵 Dried black fungus, as needed (pre-soaked)  
🥚 Egg, 1  

## Seasonings  
🧂 Salt, as needed (a little for the meat filling, 2 tbsp for stir-frying tomatoes, adjust at the end)  
🌿 Scallion-ginger water, about 2 oz (crush scallion and ginger, add water, and squeeze out the juice)  
🌶️ White pepper powder, a pinch  
🍶 Light soy sauce, a little  
🌽 Cornstarch, a little  
🧈 Lard, as needed  
🫚 Ginger, a few slices  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥣 Metal bowl  
🪓 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. Take front leg pork with 30% fat and 70% lean, and grind it into mince. If using fresh meat, freeze for 1 hour in advance to make it easier to stir until sticky.  
2. Crush the scallion and ginger, add water, and squeeze out the juice with your hands to make scallion-ginger water. Set aside.  
3. Roast the tomatoes directly over a gas flame until the skin splits. Peel off the skin and cut into small pieces.  
4. Wash the fresh soybeans, tear the oyster mushrooms into small pieces, and prepare the pre-soaked black fungus. Beat the egg in a bowl until smooth.  
5. Add salt, white pepper powder, and light soy sauce to the meat filling. Gradually add the scallion-ginger water in portions, stirring in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed. Add a little cornstarch and mix with your hands until the mixture becomes sticky and gelatinous. Then slap the mixture against the bowl a dozen times until it feels tacky and holds together in a ball.  
6. Melt some lard in the wok. Add the ginger slices and fry until fragrant. Add the tomato pieces and 2 tbsp of salt. Stir-fry over medium heat, pressing the tomatoes with a spatula. If too dry, add a little water. Continue cooking until the tomatoes are soft, broken down, and form a thick paste.  
7. Add enough water to the wok. Add the fresh soybeans, oyster mushrooms, and black fungus. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
8. Moisten your hand with water to prevent sticking. Grab some meat filling and squeeze it through your thumb and index finger to form round meatballs. Use a spoon to help drop them into the soup one by one. After all meatballs are added, reduce the heat from high to medium and cook for 5 minutes, until all meatballs float and enlarge in size.  
9. When the soup comes to a boil again, drizzle in the beaten egg and gently stir to form egg ribbons. Taste and add salt if needed. Turn off the heat, drizzle in sesame oil, stir, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This soup balances meat and vegetables. The meatballs are high in protein and easy to digest. Tomatoes and mushrooms are rich in lycopene and dietary fiber, making this a nourishing choice for the whole family. For a tender texture, use less cornstarch and slap the meat filling more. The salt in the soup is sufficient, so no extra soy sauce is needed.
