# Fresh Meat Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 Low-gluten flour 300g (or all-purpose flour)
🍄 Yeast 3g
🍥 Baking powder 1g (optional, for fluffiness)
🍬 Sugar 10g (5g for dough, 5g for filling)
💧 Water 160g (room temperature, use warm water in winter)
🧈 Lard 10g (can be substituted with regular cooking oil)
🥩 Ground pork 300g (fat-to-lean ratio 3:7 or 2:8)
🫚 Ginger 7–8g (grated into fine paste)
🧅 Scallion 30g (chopped into葱花)
🫙 Light soy sauce 20g (about two tablespoons)
🦪 Oyster sauce 25g (one heaping tablespoon)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g (one teaspoon)
🌿 White pepper powder 0.3g (a pinch, just for flavor, do not overuse)
🫒 Sesame oil 20g (two and a half tablespoons, used to mix scallions first)
🫒 Cooking oil 15g (one and a half tablespoons, added at the end to lock in moisture)
🧂 Salt to taste (add according to preference; original recipe does not include)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl (for mixing dough and filling)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)
🛡️ Plastic wrap/plastic bag (for sealing fermentation and covering raw buns)
🍳 Steamer basket (for proofing and steaming buns)
🍲 Steamer pot (for boiling water to produce steam)
🧱 Pastry board or kneading mat (work surface)
🪓 Rolling pin (for rolling out wrappers)
🔪 Bench scraper (for dividing dough)
🧤 Disposable gloves (for kneading dough and mixing meat filling)
🥣 Small bowl (for preparing dipping sauce, e.g., light soy sauce, black vinegar, chili oil)

## Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. **Make the dough**
   Combine low-gluten flour, yeast, baking powder, and 5g sugar and mix thoroughly. Gradually add water while stirring until the mixture forms flaky pieces. Add lard and knead until the dough is smooth and the bowl is clean. Cover with plastic wrap and let it ferment until doubled in size.
   **Key points**: Ensure the dry ingredients are well mixed; adjust water temperature according to the season; lard makes the dough whiter, softer, and easier to work with.

### Prepare the Filling
2. **Mix the meat filling**
   Add grated ginger, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, the remaining 5g sugar, chicken bouillon, and white pepper powder to the ground pork. Mix well with your hands. First mix the chopped scallions with sesame oil, then add them together with the cooking oil to the meat. Gently mix in the same direction until the meat filling becomes slightly stringy. **Do not overmix**.

### Cooking Phase
3. **Degas and divide**
   Remove the fermented dough and knead repeatedly for 5–6 rounds to degas until the dough is smooth, soft, and elastic. Roll it into a uniform log and use a bench scraper to divide it into 8 pieces.

4. **Roll wrappers and wrap**
   Take one dough piece, dust with a little flour, flatten it with your hand, and roll it into a round wrapper that is slightly thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Add about 1/8 of the meat filling, gently spread it, wrap it using your preferred technique, and pinch the opening tightly to seal.
   **Key points**: Do not roll the wrapper too thin; leave room for the second proofing.

5. **Second fermentation**
   Place the wrapped buns into a steamer basket lined with a damp cloth, leaving enough space between them. Cover the lid and let them proof for 10–15 minutes.
   **Doneness check**: After proofing, the buns should visibly increase in size, and when gently pressed with a finger, the surface slowly springs back.

6. **Steam and rest**
   Add enough water to the steamer pot and bring it to a rolling boil over high heat. Place the steamer basket on top, keep a high flame, and steam for 12 minutes. After turning off the heat, **do not open the lid**; let the buns rest for another 2 minutes before uncovering and removing them.
   **Key points**: Make sure the water is boiling before placing the steamer basket and start timing; resting prevents the buns from shrinking due to sudden temperature change, ensuring a smooth and plump surface.

## Health Tips
Fresh meat buns are a good combination of high-quality protein and carbohydrates, suitable for breakfast or lunch to replenish energy. It is recommended to serve them with a small dish of vinegar or vegetable soup to aid digestion and balance nutrition. Be mindful of portions—2 to 3 buns per serving is enough to avoid excessive calorie intake.
