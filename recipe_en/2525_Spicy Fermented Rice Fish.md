# Spicy Fermented Rice Fish
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Green fish pieces, as needed (can substitute with grass carp or fish tail; belly part preferred)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (shredded)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating)
🍶 Light soy sauce, as needed (optional, choose either this or salt)
🍚 Fermented glutinous rice (jiuniang), as needed
🌶️ Spicy and numbing seasoning, as needed
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed
🛢️ Warm oil, about 1 tablespoon (oil used for frying fish)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🪵 Spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the fish pieces into appropriately sized chunks, place them in a large bowl, add an appropriate amount of salt, and mix evenly with your hands. Marinate for about 20 minutes.
2. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat until it reaches about 50-60% hot. Slide the marinated fish pieces into the oil one by one, and fry over medium heat for about 10 minutes.
**Key point**: Do not stir the fish pieces immediately after adding them to the oil; wait until the surface turns golden and sets. When using an iron wok, avoid excessive stirring to prevent the fish from falling apart. Once set, gently push the bottom with a spatula to prevent sticking, and fry until the surface is golden brown and crispy, then remove and drain the oil.

3. Place the fried fish pieces into a clean large bowl, top with shredded ginger, and sprinkle with salt, fermented glutinous rice, spicy and numbing seasoning, and chili powder.
4. Take about one tablespoon of the warm oil used for frying the fish and drizzle it over the dry seasonings to release the aroma. Wearing disposable gloves, mix all the ingredients and seasonings thoroughly, turning repeatedly to allow the flavor to penetrate and the mixture to cool down, then plate and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and B vitamins produced through fermentation, but it is high in salt and fat. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables, and those with high blood pressure should only taste it in moderation. The fish pieces double in calories after deep frying, so for weight loss, you can use an air fryer instead.
