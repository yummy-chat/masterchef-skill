# Longevity Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫕 200g high-gluten flour  
🧂 1/8 tsp alkaline water  
🧂 1/4 tsp salt  
💧 85g water  
🦐 Dried shrimp, as needed  
🍘 Seaweed, as needed  
🥒 Pickled mustard stems, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (chopped)  
🥚 1 egg  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for hot oil and frying egg)  
🍲 Meat broth or clear broth, as needed (or water with chicken bouillon)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🫗 Sesame oil, as needed  
🌶️ White pepper, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🪓 Rolling pin  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍲 Pot for cooking noodles  
🥣 Bowl 2 (one for mixing and resting dough, one for serving)  

## Steps
1. Place the high-gluten flour in a large bowl, add the dissolved alkaline water and salt. While stirring with chopsticks, slowly pour in water to evenly distribute moisture, then knead into a slightly dry but cohesive dough.

2. Cover the dough with the bowl and let it rest for 20 minutes. Remove and knead until the surface is mostly smooth. Cover and rest again for 20 minutes, then knead until completely smooth.

3. Place the smooth dough in a bowl, cover, and let it rest for 1–2 hours to fully relax the gluten—this is key for rolling it thin later.

4. Flatten the rested dough, and use a rolling pin to roll from the center outward, frequently changing direction. Once the dough sheet is larger, roll it onto the rolling pin, pressing forward with both hands to roll it out. When the dough becomes large and thin, apply pressure only at the ends of the rolling pin to prevent bubbling in the middle. If the dough sticks, dust with a little cornstarch.

5. Spread the very thin dough sheet flat and dust generously with cornstarch to prevent sticking. Use the rolling pin to fold the dough sheet into layers, each upper layer slightly narrower than the one below. Use paper to help trim uneven edges, then use a knife to cut the dough into thin strips. Be sure to dust with enough starch, fold narrowly, and cut finely to prevent sticking.

6. In a serving bowl, add dried shrimp, seaweed, pickled mustard stems, chopped cilantro, and scallion. Heat oil in a wok until smoking, then ladle a spoonful of hot oil over the scallion and cilantro to release aroma. Immediately add light soy sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper.

7. Using the leftover oil in the wok, crack in the egg. When the bottom egg white has set, push the surrounding egg white toward the center to encase the yolk, flip, and fry to desired doneness. Set aside.

8. Bring water to a rolling boil in a pot, add the fresh-cut noodles, and loosen with chopsticks. Cook for about 20 seconds until the noodles float, then immediately remove and place into the serving bowl. At the same time, ladle in hot meat broth, clear broth, or noodle cooking water to mix with the bowl base.

9. Arrange the noodles neatly, top with the fried egg, and enjoy.  
   **Note**: Fresh handmade noodles are very thin—once they float after boiling, they are done. Do not overcook. The broth can be made with simmered stock or water with a little chicken bouillon for flavor.

## Health Tips  
Kneading and resting the dough for two hours provides carbohydrates and protein, suitable for those needing an energy boost. Dried shrimp and seaweed can supplement calcium and enhance umami, but the broth base is high in sodium. Hypertensive patients should reduce the amount of light soy sauce and pickled mustard stems.
