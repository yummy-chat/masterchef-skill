# Dry-Fried Chicken with Potatoes

Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

🧂 **Ingredients**  
- 🍗 Chicken thighs 400g  
- 🥔 Potatoes 200g  
- 🫑 Green bell peppers 60g  
- 🧄 Garlic cloves 10g  

🫙 **Seasonings**  
- 🧂 Salt 3g  
- 🐔 Chicken bouillon 3g (optional)  
- 🌶️ Ground white pepper 2g  
- 🥢 Light soy sauce 5g  
- 🥢 Dark soy sauce 2g  
- 🍶 Cooking wine 5g  
- 🌾 Cornstarch 20g  
- 🫘 Pixian chili bean paste (red oil) 40g  
- 🍬 White sugar 30g  
- 🌶️ Ground Sichuan peppercorns 2g  

🍳 **Tools**  
- Knife  
- Wok  
- Strainer  
- Pot for boiling  

👨‍🍳 **Instructions**  
1. Debone the chicken thighs. Lightly pound the meat with the back of a knife, then cut into 2 cm cubes.  
   (Debone the chicken thighs, pat them with the back of a knife, then cut into 2 cm cubes.)  
2. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and cooking wine to the chicken. Mix until sticky, then add cornstarch and mix well. Finally, drizzle with a little oil to prevent sticking. Marinate for 30 minutes.  
   (Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and cooking wine to the chicken. Mix until sticky, then add cornstarch. Finally, add a little oil to seal in moisture. Marinate for half an hour.)  
3. Peel the potatoes and cut into 2 cm cubes. Blanch in boiling water for 5 minutes, then remove and drain well.  
   (Peel the potatoes, cut into 2 cm cubes, blanch in boiling water for five minutes, then remove and drain.)  
4. Remove the seeds from the green bell peppers and cut into 2 cm square pieces. Place in a strainer and set aside.  
   (Remove the seeds from the green bell peppers, cut into 2 cm pieces, and place them in a strainer for later use.)  
5. Pour an ample amount of oil into the wok and heat to 180°C. Fry the potato cubes for 3 minutes, then remove.  
   (Pour plenty of oil into the wok, heat to 180°C, fry the potato cubes for three minutes, then remove.)  
6. Once the oil temperature returns to 180°C, fry the chicken pieces for 2 minutes, then remove.  
   (When the oil temperature rises back to 180°C, fry the chicken pieces for two minutes, then remove.)  
7. Once the oil temperature returns to 180°C, fry the chicken pieces a second time for 1 minute, then remove.  
   (When the oil temperature rises back to 180°C, fry the chicken pieces again for one minute, then remove.)  
8. Once the oil temperature returns to 180°C, fry the potato cubes a second time for 1 minute. Then pour the potatoes and hot oil directly over the green bell pepper pieces in the strainer. The hot oil will briefly cook the peppers. Drain the potatoes and set aside.  
   (When the oil temperature rises back to 180°C, fry the potato cubes again for one minute. Then pour the potatoes and oil over the green bell peppers in the strainer, letting the hot oil cook the peppers. Drain the potatoes.)  
9. Add 5 ml of oil to the wok. Over low heat, stir-fry the garlic cloves until lightly golden. Add the chili bean paste and stir until fragrant. Add the sugar and stir until melted. Then add the ground Sichuan peppercorns and 40 ml of water. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens.  
   (Add 5 ml of oil to the wok. Over low heat, stir-fry the garlic cloves until lightly golden. Add the chili bean paste and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the sugar and stir until melted. Then add the ground Sichuan peppercorns and pour in 40 ml of water. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens.)  
   **Key point**: Use Pixian red oil chili bean paste, not the regular brown variety.  
10. Add the fried chicken, potato cubes, and green bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry quickly and evenly until all ingredients are coated with the sauce. Serve immediately.  
    (Add the fried chicken, potato cubes, and green bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry quickly to coat evenly with the sauce, then plate and serve.)  

## Health Advice
Dry-fried chicken with potatoes is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy after muscle-building workouts. However, due to the frying and chili bean paste, it is high in calories, so portion control is advised. Those with high blood sugar should reduce the amount of sugar and potatoes and consider pairing with light vegetables.
