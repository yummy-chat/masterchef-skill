# Spicy Shredded Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin (shredded)
🧂 Large scallion white (core removed, shredded)
🌿 Cilantro (cut into sections)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (about a dozen, shredded)
🧂 Light soy sauce (a little for marinating + two drizzles after returning to the pan)
🍶 Cooking wine (a little for marinating + one drizzle along the pan edge)
🧂 Salt (a pinch, for marinating)
🫒 Cooking oil (slightly more than usual for stir-frying, used for both marinating and cooking)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (a little, for color)
🍬 Granulated sugar (a pinch)
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper (to taste)

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls (for marinating meat, soaking chili peppers)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. **Prepare ingredients**: Split the scallion white lengthwise, remove the inner core, and cut into thin strips. Wash the cilantro and cut into sections. Cut the pork tenderloin into thin strips along the grain. Add a little light soy sauce, cooking wine, a pinch of salt, and an appropriate amount of cooking oil to the pork strips. Mix well and marinate for 20 minutes. Cut the dried chili peppers into shreds, soak them in water briefly, then drain thoroughly.
2. **Fry the chili peppers**: Pour slightly more oil than usual into the pan. Heat over medium heat. Add the drained chili pepper shreds and stir-fry until fragrant and the oil turns red.
   **Note**: Ensure the chili shreds are thoroughly drained to prevent oil splatter.
3. **Stir-fry the pork**: Add the marinated pork strips. Stir-fry over medium heat until they change color, then reduce to medium-low heat and continue dry-frying until the surface of the pork strips is completely dehydrated and dry, and the oil in the pan becomes clear.
   **Key point**: Patient dry-frying is crucial for the pork to become dry, aromatic, and chewy.
4. **Aromatics**: Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the shredded scallion and green Sichuan pepper. Stir-fry quickly until fragrant, then stop immediately to avoid overcooking.
5. **Return and season**: Return the dry-fried pork strips to the pan. Turn the heat to high. Drizzle a circle of cooking wine along the edge of the pan. Then add light soy sauce, a little dark soy sauce, and a pinch of granulated sugar. Stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds to evenly coat the pork strips with the sauce.
   **Note**: Drizzling wine along the pan edge uses high heat to release fragrance and remove any gamey smell.
6. **Add cilantro and plate**: Add the cilantro sections. Stir-fry quickly until the cilantro softens and its color deepens. Immediately turn off the heat and transfer to a plate.
   **Key point**: Add the cilantro last to keep it fragrant and crisp-tender, avoiding overcooking.

## Health Tips
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. The dry-frying process reduces the oil content of the pork tenderloin, making it suitable for those on a diet or fitness regimen in moderate amounts. It is recommended to serve with multigrain rice or a cold vegetable dish to balance dietary fiber. Note that chili peppers and Sichuan pepper can irritate the stomach; those with a sensitive stomach should consume in moderation.
