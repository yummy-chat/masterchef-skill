# Braised Ribs with Wide Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs (spare ribs, pre-blanched)
🍜 Wide noodles (sweet potato or potato starch noodles, pre-soaked until soft)
🧅 2-3 scallions (cut into sections)
🫚 3-4 slices of ginger
🌶️ 3-4 dried chili peppers
⭐ 1 star anise
🍃 2 bay leaves
🪵 1 small piece of cinnamon bark
🧂 3 tablespoons light soy sauce
🧂 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
🦪 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
🧂 1 teaspoon salt
🌿 A pinch of thirteen-spice powder

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🍲 Lid
🔪 Knife / cutting board
🔥 Stove
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🍚 White porcelain bowl (for serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. In a small bowl, mix together light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and thirteen-spice powder until well combined. Set the sauce aside.
2. Heat the wok over medium heat, add cooking oil, then sauté scallion sections, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon bark until fragrant.
3. Add the blanched pork ribs and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface turns slightly golden and the edges are a bit crispy.
4. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat the ribs with color.
5. Add enough hot water to fully submerge the ribs.
   **Note**: Be sure to use boiling water to avoid cold water causing the meat to tighten and become tough.
6. Cover the wok with the lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise for 40 minutes.
7. Remove the lid, add the pre-soaked wide noodles, and ensure they are fully submerged in the broth.
8. Cover the wok again and continue braising for another 15 minutes.
9. Remove the lid, turn up the heat to high, and reduce the sauce while stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.
10. Transfer to a serving bowl. The rich broth is excellent for mixing with rice.

## Health Advice
Braised ribs with wide noodles are rich in protein and carbohydrates, making them suitable for replenishing energy after heavy physical activity. However, the fat content in pork ribs is not low, so it is recommended to pair this dish with a lightly stir-fried vegetable to balance the meal.
