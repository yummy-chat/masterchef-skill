# Grass Jelly (Burn Xian Cao)
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Dried grass jelly herb (Xian Cao) 50g (cut into small pieces, washed thoroughly)
💧 Water 1500-2000ml
🍠 Sweet potato starch 50g (can substitute with tapioca starch)
🫘 Honey red beans, as needed
🍡 Taro balls, as needed
🧋 Tapioca pearls, as needed
🧂 Edible alkaline (lye water/sodium carbonate) 3g
🍬 Sugar 50g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🥅 Strainer/Filter
🫕 Large soup pot
🧻 Fine cheesecloth or muslin bag
🥣 Small saucepan
🫙 Glass bowl or measuring cup
🥄 Spoon or stirring stick
🧊 Refrigerator
🔪 Cutting board and knife

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the Grass Jelly Herb**: Cut the dried grass jelly herb into small pieces. Place them in a strainer and rinse thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear and free of sand.
**Key point**: The herb resembles wild grass and contains significant impurities; ensure it is thoroughly washed.

2. **Cook the Grass Jelly Liquid**: Place the washed herb pieces into the large soup pot. Add 3g of edible alkaline and pour in 1500-2000ml of water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
**Indicators of doneness**: The liquid should become deep black and release a strong herbal aroma. The herb leaves should be fully softened by the alkaline.

3. **Strain**: Strain the cooked liquid twice through a fine cheesecloth or muslin bag to obtain a clear, debris-free grass jelly tea.

4. **Season and Thicken**: Return the strained grass jelly tea to the pot. Add 50g of sugar to season and bring to a boil. Separately, mix 50g of sweet potato starch with an equal amount of water to form a slurry. Keep the grass jelly tea at a gentle simmer. Slowly pour in the starch slurry while stirring rapidly and continuously until the liquid thickens to a semi-flowing consistency.
**Key point**: Sweet potato starch or tapioca starch creates the characteristic chewy (Q) texture. Stir continuously during thickening to prevent lumps.

5. **Serve Hot**: Ladle into bowls. While still hot, add honey red beans, taro balls, tapioca pearls, and other toppings as desired. Serve immediately.
**Note**: This version must be eaten while hot, as it will solidify upon cooling.

6. **Make Grass Jelly**: Pour the cooked grass jelly liquid into small bowls. Refrigerate until it naturally solidifies into a jelly. To serve, cut into cubes and combine with coconut milk, honey, or add to milk tea or sago pudding.

## Health Advice
Grass jelly is rich in dietary fiber and plant colloids, low in calories, and helps to cool the body and relieve summer heat. It is especially suitable for summer consumption or for those with a 'hot' constitution. Ensure the herb is thoroughly washed to remove any sand. Diabetic patients should consider reducing the amount of sugar used.
