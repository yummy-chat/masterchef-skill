# Traditional Shanghai Pan-Fried Pork Buns (Shengjian)
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground pork, appropriate amount (beaten until smooth)
🌾 All-purpose flour, 500g
🧂 Yeast, 5g
🧂 Baking powder, 3g
🍬 Granulated sugar, 5g (for the dough)
💧 Warm water, 275g (below 40°C)
🥚 Egg white, 1
🧅🫚 Scallion-ginger water, 85g
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped, appropriate amount (for the filling)
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped, appropriate amount (for garnish)
⚫ Black sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🌾 Flour, 20g (for the flour water)
💧 Water, 500g (for the flour water)
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (for pan-frying)
🧂 Light soy sauce, 8g
🦪 Oyster sauce, 5g
🧂 Salt, 3g
🌶 White pepper, 1g
🌶 Sichuan peppercorn powder, 1g
🍬 Granulated sugar, a pinch (to enhance freshness)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a pinch
🫒 Sesame oil, a pinch

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍳 Non-stick pan or flat-bottomed skillet
🥖 Rolling pin
🥢 Chopsticks (or mixing tool)
🍽️ Tray
🛡️ Plastic wrap
🔘 Lid

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the filling**
   Place the ground pork in a bowl. Add 8g light soy sauce, 5g oyster sauce, 3g salt, 1g white pepper, 1g Sichuan peppercorn powder, a pinch of sugar to enhance freshness, one egg white, and a dash of dark soy sauce for color. Stir vigorously in one direction until the mixture becomes firm and cohesive.
   **Note**: Gradually add the 85g scallion-ginger water in small batches, stirring well after each addition until the meat fully absorbs the liquid, until the mixture is so thick that a pair of chopsticks can stand upright in it. Finally, drizzle in a little sesame oil and sprinkle in the chopped scallions. Gently mix to preserve the scallion aroma. Set aside.

2. **Make the dough**
   In a mixing bowl, combine 500g all-purpose flour, 5g yeast, 3g baking powder, and 5g sugar. Gradually pour in 275g warm water, stirring into a shaggy dough. Knead until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 20 minutes.

3. **Shape the buns**
   Remove the rested dough and knead briefly to release air. Roll into a long log and divide into equal-sized small pieces. Roll each piece into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.
   Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center and wrap into a small bun shape, pinching the seam tightly to prevent the broth from leaking during cooking.
   Arrange all the shaped buns on a tray, cover with plastic wrap, and let them proof for another 15 minutes, until they have visibly expanded.
   (After resting, take the dough out, knead to release air, roll into a log, and cut into equal small pieces, about 12g each. Roll into rounds with thick centers and thin edges.
   Fill with the filling and wrap into small bun shapes, making sure the seams are tightly sealed, otherwise the broth will leak out during frying.
   After wrapping all the buns, arrange them on a tray, cover with plastic wrap, and let them proof for another 15 minutes until they have noticeably risen.)

4. **Pan-fry**
   Preheat a flat-bottomed skillet over medium heat and add a small amount of cold oil. Place the buns seam-side down in the pan. Fry over medium heat until the bottoms turn golden brown.
   Once the bottoms are set, pour the prepared flour water along the edge of the pan, until the water reaches about one-third the height of the buns. Immediately cover with a lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and simmer for 8–10 minutes, until all the liquid has evaporated and a crispy lacy crust (ice flower) has formed on the bottom.
   (Heat a flat-bottomed skillet and add a little cold oil. Place the buns seam-side down in the pan. Fry over medium heat until the bottoms are golden brown.
   After the bottoms are set, pour the prepared flour water along the edge of the pan, with the water level reaching about one-third the height of the buns. Immediately cover the pan, reduce the heat to medium-low, and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes, until the water has completely evaporated and a crispy lace pattern appears on the bottom.)

5. **Finish and serve**
   When you hear the sound of the liquid fully evaporating, gently shake the pan to ensure the buns move freely. Sprinkle with black sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
Traditional Shanghai pan-fried pork buns mainly consist of pork and flour, providing a balanced combination of protein and carbohydrates. However, the filling has a relatively high oil content, so it is recommended to serve with a light congee or vegetables to balance the calories. Suitable for those needing an energy boost. Those with high blood pressure should be mindful of reducing salt intake from dipping sauces.
