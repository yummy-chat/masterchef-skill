# Dry Pot Lamb

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐑 Bone-in lamb (lamb ribs or lamb breast)
🧅 Purple onion
🌱 Garlic sprouts
🥬 Celery
🍶 Cooking wine
🌶️ Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste)
🫚 Ginger
🧄 Garlic
⭐ Star anise
🧂 Cinnamon stick
🌶️ Dried chili peppers
🧂 Soy sauce
🦪 Oyster sauce
🧂 Salt
🍬 Sugar

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
♨️ Pressure cooker
🍲 Clay pot
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the lamb in a pot of cold water and add cooking wine to blanch.
**Key point**: Must use cold water to start, as it helps remove gamey flavors and fully release blood foam.
2. Once the water boils, skim off the foam, remove the lamb, and rinse clean with water. Set aside.
3. Heat oil in a wok, add doubanjiang, and stir-fry over medium heat until the oil turns red and fragrant.
4. Add ginger, garlic, cinnamon stick, star anise, and dried chili peppers, quickly stir-frying until fragrant. Avoid burning.
5. Add soy sauce and a small amount of cooking wine, then immediately add the blanched lamb. Stir-fry until the lamb surface is slightly charred and fragrant.
6. Pour in enough water to cover, bring to a boil, then season with oyster sauce, salt, and sugar.
**Key point**: The flavor should be slightly mild at this stage, as reducing the liquid later will increase saltiness.
7. Transfer all ingredients to a pressure cooker, cover, and cook over medium heat for about 15 minutes after the pressure builds, until the lamb can be easily pierced.
8. Return the cooked lamb to the wok, remove large spices like cinnamon stick and star anise, and increase heat to reduce the sauce.
**Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent sticking, until the sauce thickens and coats the lamb.
9. Line the bottom of a clay pot with shredded onion, then add the lamb with the reduced sauce.
10. Top the lamb with sections of garlic sprouts and celery, drizzle with a spoonful of hot oil to release aroma, and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This Dry Pot Lamb is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for nourishing those with cold hands and feet in winter; however, doubanjiang and salt content are high, so people with high blood pressure should consume the sauce sparingly. Using a pressure cooker to tenderize then reducing the sauce preserves nutrients while shortening cooking time.
