# Hand in Hand 🥢

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pig Trotters – about 2 pieces (cut into chunks, rinsed repeatedly until water runs clear)
🐔 Chicken Feet – about 10 (trim off nails)
🌱 Garlic Sprouts – 3–4 stalks (cut into sections, separate white parts from leaves)
🧄 Garlic – to taste (minced)
🧅 Scallion – to taste (cut into sections)
🍂 Cinnamon Stick – a small piece
⭐ Star Anise – a small piece
🍃 Bay Leaves – a small piece
🥄 Light Soy Sauce – about 2 tbsp
🥄 Dark Soy Sauce – about 1 tbsp (add a little more later if needed for color)
🦪 Oyster Sauce – about 1 tbsp
🍶 Cooking Wine – to taste (for blanching)
🌶️ Homemade Chili Sauce – 2 large tbsp

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Pressure Cooker
🍳 Wok / Frying Pan
✂️ Kitchen Scissors
🔪 Chef’s Knife & Cutting Board
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🥣 Bowls & Plates

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prep Ingredients**: Trim the nails off the chicken feet. Cut the pig trotters into chunks and rinse repeatedly in cold water until the water runs clear. Mince the garlic, cut the garlic sprouts into sections (separating the white parts and leaves), cut the scallion into sections, and prepare the cinnamon stick, star anise, and bay leaves.
2. **Blanch**: Place the pig trotters and chicken feet in a pot with cold water, add cooking wine, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 5–8 minutes. Skim off any foam, then drain well.
   **Key Point**: Starting with cold water removes the gamey smell more thoroughly.
3. **Braise Trotters First**: Heat the pressure cooker over high heat, add a suitable amount of oil, sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, then add the pig trotter chunks, cinnamon stick, star anise, and bay leaves. Stir-fry for even coating. Add enough water to cover the trotters, then season with light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Cover with the lid. Once the pressure valve rises, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 15–20 minutes, until the trotters are soft and tender.
4. **Add Chicken Feet**: After natural pressure release, open the lid. Add the blanched chicken feet and the homemade chili sauce, and stir to combine. If the color is too light, add a little more dark soy sauce. Add a small amount of water, then sprinkle in the garlic sprout leaves.
5. **Reduce the Sauce**: Cook uncovered over medium-low heat for 5–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is shiny, thick, and evenly coats the ingredients. Remove from heat and plate.
   **Key Point**: Adding the chicken feet later keeps them springy and Q‑textured. Adjust the amount of chili sauce to your preferred spice level.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in collagen and is suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity or increase calcium intake. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is best balanced with light vegetables.
