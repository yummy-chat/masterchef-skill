# Pan-Fried Crab with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Tip: Best in Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦀 3 Flower Crabs (shelled, gills removed, cut into pieces, claws smashed)  
🫚 About 20g Ginger (smashed and minced)  
🧅 1 Scallion (diced)  
🌱 A few Spring Onions (cut into sections)  
🌾 Some Dry Starch (for sealing the cut surfaces of the crab meat)  
🧂 About 2g Salt (for marinating)  
🦪 10g Oyster Sauce  
🍶 50g Yellow Wine  
🫒 Some Vegetable Oil (for the pan-frying base and drizzling during cooking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪵 Thick Wooden Cutting Board  
🔪 Chef’s Knife  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
🍵 Small Bowl  
🥄 Soup Spoon  
🍲 Clay Pot  
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Shell the flower crabs, cut into pieces, remove the crab gills and any impurities, and rinse clean. Chop off the sharp claws, remove the crab claws and smash them with the back of a knife.  
**Note**: Crab gills must be thoroughly removed. Smashing the claws helps with flavor absorption.

2. Place the crab pieces in a bowl, add salt, mix well, and marinate for 5 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Use a moderate amount of salt to avoid being too salty. Do not marinate for too long.

3. Smash the ginger and then mince it. Dice the scallion and cut the spring onions into sections. In a small bowl, mix the oyster sauce and yellow wine, stirring well to create the sauce, and set aside.
4. Heat the clay pot, pour in the base oil. When the oil temperature reaches about 100°C, coat the meaty sides of the crab pieces with dry starch, and place them into the pot with the meat side down.  
**Crucial Heat Control**: Keep the heat on very low throughout this step. Slowly pan-fry for about 6 minutes until the cut surfaces of the crab meat are set. Avoid high heat, which can cause the outside to burn while the inside remains raw.

5. Once the bottom of the crab pieces is set, sprinkle in the minced ginger and diced scallion. Cover the pot and continue to pan-fry and steam. Total cooking time for this step is about 6 minutes. During the process, you can drizzle a small amount of vegetable oil along the edge of the pot to enhance the aroma and richness.  
**Note**: Continue to use very low heat throughout. Gently move the pieces with chopsticks to prevent sticking.

6. Remove the lid. Pour the prepared oyster sauce and yellow wine mixture along the edge of the clay pot. Cover the pot again and cook for another 3 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Pour the sauce along the pot's edge to utilize the high temperature there to release a more intense aroma.

7. Finally, sprinkle in the spring onion sections. After they release their aroma, immediately turn off the heat. Plate and serve. The clay pot retains heat, ensuring the dish is steaming hot when served.

## Health Advice
Pan-frying crabs with ginger and scallion over very low heat locks in the natural sweetness of the crab meat. The ginger and scallion eliminate any fishy taste and enhance the aroma, making this dish suitable for autumn nourishment. Be sure to remove the crab gills and seal the cut surfaces with starch to prevent sticking. This dish is high in protein and low in fat, but crab roe is high in cholesterol. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should only eat it in small amounts.
