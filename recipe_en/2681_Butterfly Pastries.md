# Butterfly Pastries
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 100g
🌾 Cake flour 100g
🧈 Unsalted butter (for dough) 40g
💧 Ice water 90g
🧈 Butter for lamination 130g (2 pieces, 65g each)
🍬 Coarse sugar (for coating) as needed
🧂 Salt 4g
🍬 Caster sugar 10g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
⚖️ Digital scale
📦 Plastic wrap
🫓 Rolling pin (standard & rolling type)
🔲 Dough scraper
🔪 Small knife
📜 Baking paper/parchment paper
🔥 Oven
🕳️ Perforated silicone baking mat
🧺 Cooling rack
🛍️ Packaging bags

## Steps
1. Mix bread flour, cake flour, caster sugar, and salt evenly in a bowl.
2. Add diced unsalted butter, rub the butter into the flour with your hands until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs, to break the gluten for easier lamination.
3. Gradually add ice water, mix with a dough scraper, then quickly form into a dough. Knead until no dry flour remains; smoothness or windowpane stage is unnecessary — rough surface is fine.
4. Divide the dough in half, wrap each in plastic wrap, and refrigerate to rest for 30 minutes.
5. Prepare the butter for lamination: On a surface dusted with cake flour, tap and roll each piece of butter into a rectangle about 14cm×7cm. Brush off excess flour, wrap in parchment paper, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
6. Take out one rested dough piece and roll it into a rectangle about 14cm×16cm. Place a chilled butter sheet in the center, fully enclose the butter with the dough, and pinch the seams tightly. Note: the dough should just cover the butter; slight stretching is acceptable for a snug fit.
7. First double turn (four-fold): Dust the work surface and dough generously with dry flour. Press the dough with a rolling pin, then use a rolling pin to roll into a rectangle about 34cm long and 12cm wide. Brush off excess flour, spray with a little water to help adhesion, and perform one four-fold. After folding, make a small cut at each corner with a knife, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate to rest for 30 minutes.
8. Second double turn: Remove the dough, repeat the "press-roll-dust-four-fold-cut corners" step, then refrigerate to rest for another 30 minutes.
9. Third double turn: Remove the dough and complete the third four-fold. The lamination is now complete.
   **Key points**: Dust well with dry flour before each roll to prevent sticking; brush off excess flour and spray with a little water before each fold; apply even pressure when rolling, pressing and extending in sections; if the dough shrinks significantly, rest it in the refrigerator before proceeding.
10. Final shaping: Roll the laminated dough into a large rectangle about 35cm×12cm with even thickness. Use a ruler and scraper to mark the center line on the dough surface, and divide it into 6 equal sections.
11. Spray both sides of the dough with water, then press one side down onto a sheet of paper covered with coarse sugar, coating both sides evenly.
12. Loosely roll both ends toward the center line until they meet at the center. Note: roll loosely to leave room for expansion during baking; the rolls from both ends must be symmetrical.
13. Wrap the rolled dough in plastic wrap and freeze for 1 hour to firm up for easier slicing.
14. Remove the firm roll, trim off uneven ends, and slice into pieces about 0.8-1cm thick.
15. Coat both sides of the cut pastry pieces generously with coarse sugar again, and place them evenly on a baking tray lined with a perforated silicone mat, leaving at least double the space for expansion on all sides.
16. Baking: Bake in a home oven at 180°C for 20 minutes, remove and flip the pastries, then continue baking at 180°C for another 15 minutes until both sides are golden brown. Flipping is essential to ensure even browning and crispy texture.
17. After baking, transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely, then seal in bags to maintain crispness.

## Health Suggestions
Butterfly pastries are high in calories, mainly from the layers of butter and sugar. Consuming a small amount is fine, and they pair well with black coffee to balance the sweetness. When baking, pay attention to the flipping temperature to prevent the pastry from becoming bitter due to over-caramelization.
