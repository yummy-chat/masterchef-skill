# Pan-Fried Rice Crust
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Leftover rice, 1 bowl (leftover rice is easier to handle)  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🌽 Corn kernels, 50g  
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (add a little at a time until each grain is coated with starch)  
🌿 Scallions, chopped, as needed (for final garnish)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, finely diced, as needed (for final garnish)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🍃 Five-spice powder, a pinch  
🍖 BBQ seasoning, as needed (sprinkle after cooking)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing ingredients)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)  
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok (for frying)  
🍽️ Plate (for flipping)  
🔪 Knife and cutting board (for cutting into pieces)  
🍽️ Serving dish  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1.  Place the leftover rice in a bowl, crack in the egg, and mix by hand until each grain of rice is evenly coated with the egg mixture.  
2.  Add the corn kernels, salt, and five-spice powder, and mix again evenly by hand.  
3.  Add the cornstarch in small batches, stirring with chopsticks as you go, until all the rice grains are evenly coated with starch and the mixture appears dry and loose.  
4.  Heat oil in the pan over high heat, then pour in the rice mixture. Spread it into a flat, even pancake shape, making it as thin as possible. Once spread, leave it undisturbed.  
5.  Evenly sprinkle a little water over the surface to help it set.  
6.  Reduce the heat to the lowest setting and fry for about 3–5 minutes. Once the bottom is set, drizzle a little oil over the surface and gently shake the pan to prevent sticking.  
7.  Continue frying over low heat for about 3 minutes, then flip the crust by placing a plate over the pan and inverting it. Return the crust to the pan to fry the other side.  
    **Note**: Keep the heat on the lowest setting throughout to prevent burning.  
8.  Fry the other side over low heat for about 3–5 minutes, occasionally pressing gently with a spoon to ensure even heating, until both sides are golden brown, firm to the touch, and the rice grains are translucent and golden.  
9.  Once cooked, remove the crust from the pan, place it on a cutting board, and cut into bite-sized pieces. Arrange on a plate, and sprinkle with BBQ seasoning, chopped scallions, and diced red bell pepper. Serve.  

## Health Tips  
This rice crust transforms leftover rice into a crispy snack, making it a wonderful little carb treat. However, it requires a fair amount of oil for frying, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables or a salad to balance the calories. The egg and corn kernels add protein and dietary fiber, making it an excellent satisfying choice for those with good teeth.
