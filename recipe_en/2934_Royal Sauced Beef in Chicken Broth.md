# Royal Sauced Beef in Chicken Broth
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef shank about 3 jin (remove membrane, poke holes, make slits)  
🧂 Meat curing mixture: Cumin, star anise, Sichuan pepper (3 qian each) dry-fried and ground, mixed with salt  
🌟 Spice pouch: Baizhi, cloves, cinnamon, sand ginger, white cardamom, amomum villosum (3 qian each) placed in a gauze bag together with the three dry-fried spices  
🧂 Salt to taste (for curing and stewing seasoning)  
🍬 Rock sugar to taste (for caramelizing)  
🧅 Scallion to taste (for sautéing and stewing)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (for sautéing and stewing)  
🍾 Soy sauce to taste  
🥢 Light soy sauce to taste  
🍶 Yellow wine to taste  
🌿 White pepper to taste  
🍲 Chicken broth sufficient amount (stewing base)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥢 Chopsticks (for poking holes)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Slotted spoon  
🧤 Gauze (for wrapping spices)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place cumin, star anise, and Sichuan pepper in a dry wok over low heat, dry-fry until fragrant. Remove and mix with salt, then grind into a powder to make the meat curing mixture.
   **Key point**: Dry-fry over low heat only, do not burn; do not add salt during frying.
2. Soak beef shank in clean water for 1-3 hours to remove blood. Remove and repeatedly poke holes in the meat with chopsticks, then make several shallow slits with a knife. Evenly apply the curing mixture and rub to massage, then cure for 1 hour.
   **Key point**: Poking holes and making slits allows seasoning to penetrate fully; use a generous amount of salt during quick curing.
3. Add a little oil and rock sugar to a wok. Cook over low heat while stirring constantly. When the sugar syrup changes from white to yellow, and then to a soy sauce color and emits blue smoke, quickly add scallion and ginger to sauté until fragrant. Then pour in an appropriate amount of warm water and stir well to make caramel-colored water. Set aside.
   **Key point**: The color change of the sugar happens very quickly; the timing for adding scallion and ginger must be precise.
4. Rinse the cured beef to remove surface spice residue. Place in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, and skim off any foam. Add a small amount of cold water 2-3 times, bring to a boil repeatedly and skim foam until the broth becomes clear.
   **Key point**: Must start with cold water; repeatedly adding cold water uses thermal expansion and contraction to force out internal blood and impurities.
5. Place the blanched beef into a pressure cooker. Pour in enough chicken broth to cover the beef. Add the spice pouch, caramel-colored water, soy sauce, light soy sauce, yellow wine, scallion sections, ginger slices, and white pepper. Adjust salt to taste after tasting.
6. Cover the lid, bring to pressure, and cook for 40 minutes. After turning off the heat, do not open the lid; let it rest using residual heat for another 1 hour.
7. Open the lid. If chopsticks can easily pierce through the beef, it has reached the desired tenderness. Remove, slice, and plate. Drizzle a little of the original broth if desired.
   **Key point**: Using a sufficient amount of original chicken broth is the essence of the royal flavor.

## Health Tips
This royal saucy beef in chicken broth is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for fitness muscle building or those needing to replenish Qi and blood. Beef shank is low in fat and high in protein, but the curing process uses a higher amount of salt. Individuals with high blood pressure are advised to consume in moderation.
