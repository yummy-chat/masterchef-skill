# Shaanxi-Style Tofu Pudding
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Dried soybeans 1 jin (plump, soaked overnight in advance)
💧 Water 1 basin (for soaking soybeans)
💧 Drinking water 8-10 jin (for grinding, adjust based on bean plumpness)
🍖 Broth 2 jin (base for the sauce)
🌟 Star anise 2 pieces
🌟 Cardamom 1 piece
🌳 Cinnamon 2 grams
🌱 Fennel seeds 3 grams
🍂 Bay leaf 1 gram
🫘 Cooked soybeans appropriate amount (for topping)
🥬 Pickled mustard tuber, diced appropriate amount (for topping)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (for garnish)
🧪 Glucono delta-lactone (coagulant) 1.2 grams per jin of cooked soy milk (for coagulation, dissolve in warm water)
🥔 Potato starch 2 grams per jin of cooked soy milk (for coagulation, dissolve in warm water)
🧂 Soy sauce about 1 tablespoon (for sauce seasoning)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for sauce seasoning)
🧂 Chicken powder appropriate amount (for sauce seasoning)
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tablespoon per bowl (for assembly)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Stainless steel stockpot (for boiling soy milk)
🥄 Soup ladle (for stirring, skimming, and serving sauce)
🥣 Large glass bowl (container for pouring and setting)
🍯 Clay pot (for simmering sauce)
🥢 Slotted spoon (for removing spice dregs)
🫗 Strainer/Cheesecloth (for filtering soybean pulp)

## Preparation Steps
1. Select 1 jin of plump dried soybeans and soak them in water overnight until fully expanded.
**Key point**: The plumpness of the beans determines the extraction rate, directly affecting the quality of the final product.

2. Wash and drain the soaked soybeans, then grind them with 8-10 jin of water to make raw soy milk.
**Key point**: Use 10 jin of water if beans are plump, 8 jin if less plump, to ensure proper concentration, preventing it from being too thin to set or too thick and hard.

3. Weigh the coagulant and potato starch according to the proportion and place them at the bottom of a glass bowl. Dissolve with a small amount of warm water and set aside. Each jin of cooked soy milk requires 1.2 grams of coagulant and 2 grams of potato starch.
4. Pour the raw soy milk into the stainless steel stockpot. Heat over low heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking. As it heats, the foam will gradually decrease.
5. Once the soy milk comes to a boil, continue to cook over low heat for 5 minutes to thoroughly cook and remove the beany flavor.
6. Turn off the heat and let the soy milk cool naturally until a skin forms on the surface. This temperature is ideal for adding the coagulant.
7. Holding the pot of cooked soy milk with both hands, pour it slowly and steadily into one spot of the glass bowl containing the coagulant. The force of the pour will mix the coagulant evenly; no stirring is needed.
8. Cover immediately and let it sit undisturbed for about 15 minutes to set.
**Condition check**: The finished product should have a smooth surface without holes or excess water, overall springy, and should wobble with a "hula hula" motion when shaken.

9. Prepare the sauce: Pour 2 jin of broth into a clay pot. Add washed star anise, cardamom, cinnamon, fennel seeds, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil and simmer until the spices release their aroma and the broth turns a light tea color.
10. Remove the spice dregs with a slotted spoon. Add soy sauce for color. Season with salt, chicken powder, and white pepper.
**Key point**: The sauce should be slightly more seasoned than the tofu pudding itself.

11. Scoop out the tofu pudding into bowls. Ladle the prepared sauce over it. Drizzle with a spoonful of chili oil. Sprinkle with cooked soybeans, diced pickled mustard tuber, and cilantro.

## Health Advice
This Shaanxi-style tofu pudding is rich in plant-based protein and calcium, suitable for breakfast or a light meal. However, the sauce and chili oil are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. The smooth texture from the coagulant makes it easy to digest, and pairing it with broth-based sauce offers balanced nutrition.
