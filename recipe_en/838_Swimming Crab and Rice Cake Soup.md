# Swimming Crab and Rice Cake Soup
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦀 Swimming crab 2-3  
🍥 Ningbo rice cake appropriate amount (sliced)  
🍅 Tomato 1 (not too large)  
🍄 Seafood mushrooms appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic 2 cloves (sliced)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
⚪ White pepper powder appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Hot pot  
🥣 Bowl  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Use kitchen scissors to pry open the crab shell, remove gills, intestines, and other inedible parts, then cut the crab body into pieces and set aside.  
2. Peel the tomato and dice into small pieces; slice the garlic and ginger, chop the scallion.  
3. Heat the pan with oil, add crab pieces when the oil is hot, fry until the shell turns red, then remove and set aside.  
4. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the diced tomato and stir-fry until it releases juice and becomes soft, then add the seafood mushrooms and stir-fry.  
5. Add an appropriate amount of water to the pan, bring to a boil, then add the fried crab pieces and rice cake slices.  
6. Cook until the rice cakes are soft and tender, season with salt and white pepper powder to taste, remove from heat, and sprinkle with chopped scallions before serving.  
**Note**: The tomato must be stir-fried until it releases juice for a rich broth; rice cakes should not be cooked too long—they are done when they float to the surface.

## Health Advice  
Swimming crab is a high-protein, low-fat ingredient, while rice cakes provide carbohydrates, making this dish suitable for nourishing in autumn; tomatoes and seafood mushrooms add flavor and vitamin C. However, crab roe is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should limit their intake.
