# Salt and Pepper Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken mid-joint wings, about 1 jin (choose plump, meaty ones, cut in half between the two bones)
🌾 Flour, 4 tablespoons (for crispy batter)
🥔 Cornstarch, 4 tablespoons (for crispy batter)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, 2 tablespoons (for crispy batter)
🧂 Baking powder, 1 small pinch (for crispy batter)
🧅 Dried shallots (or onion), to taste (minced, for flavor base)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced, for flavor base)
🫑 Green bell pepper, to taste (diced, for color and aroma)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, to taste (diced, for color and aroma)
🫚 Scallion, to taste (for marinating chicken wings)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced thick, for marinating chicken wings)
🍶 Hua Diao wine, 1 bottle cap (for marinating)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 10 ml (for marinating)
🧂 Salt, 3 grams (for marinating); 2 grams (for stir-frying)
🍬 Sugar, 3 grams (for marinating); 2 grams (for stir-frying)
🌿 White pepper powder, 3 shakes (for marinating)
🌿 Five-spice powder, 1 gram (for stir-frying, key to salt and pepper flavor)
🧂 MSG, 1 gram (for stir-frying)
💧 Water, about 1 small spoonful (for stir-frying to bloom the aromatics)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board (for preparing chicken wings and ingredients)
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating chicken wings and mixing crispy batter)
🍶 Ceramic bowl (for holding chopped ingredients)
🍳 Wok (for frying and stir-frying)
🥢 Slotted spoon (for removing fried chicken wings and draining oil)
🧻 Kitchen paper (for drying chicken wings)

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Stage
1. **Prepare Chicken Wings**
   Cut the chicken mid-joint wings in half between the two bones, wash thoroughly.
   **Key Point**: Cutting makes them easy to eat without needing to remove bones.

2. **Dry and Marinate**
   Use kitchen paper to completely dry the washed chicken wings. Place them in a bowl, add scallion segments, crushed garlic, thick ginger slices, Hua Diao wine, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and white pepper powder. Rub and mix evenly with your hands, marinate for one hour.
   **Key Point**: Drying thoroughly is key for flavor absorption; rubbing helps seasonings penetrate.

3. **Prepare Crispy Batter**
   In a bowl, mix flour, cornstarch, glutinous rice flour, and baking powder, stir evenly. Add water in batches, gently mix with a spoon until it reaches a pourable yogurt-like consistency. Do not stir vigorously to develop gluten.
   **Key Point**: The batter should be yogurt-like; small lumps will disappear after resting. Avoid stirring in one direction to prevent gluten formation, which affects crispiness.

4. **Prepare Aromatics**
   Mince dried shallots and garlic, dice green and red bell peppers, set aside.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Coat with Batter**
   Remove the scallion and ginger from the marinated chicken wings, place in a clean bowl. Pour the prepared crispy batter over the chicken wings, mix evenly so each piece is uniformly coated.

2. **First Frying**
   Maintain low heat throughout, oil temperature at about 30% hot. Add the battered chicken wings to the oil, fry for about 5-6 minutes until the batter sets and the inside is fully cooked.
   **Key Point**: Do not move them initially after adding; once set, gently separate with chopsticks. The goal is to fully cook the chicken wings.

3. **Second Frying**
   Remove the chicken wings, turn the heat to high and raise oil temperature to 50% hot. Fry again for about 100 seconds until the surface deepens to golden brown and the skin is crispier.
   **Key Point**: Second frying enhances color, makes the skin crispier, and releases excess oil from the wings.

4. **Stir-fry and Toss**
   Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add minced dried shallots and garlic, stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add salt, sugar, five-spice powder, and MSG, immediately add a small spoonful of water, quickly stir to dissolve the seasonings and bloom the aromatics. Once the water evaporates, add the diced green and red bell peppers, then add the fried chicken wings. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until evenly mixed.
   **Key Point**: "Blooming aromatics with water" is a common Cantonese technique that releases fragrance without being greasy.

## Health Suggestions
This salt and pepper chicken wings achieves a crispy exterior using glutinous rice flour and double-frying. While calories are relatively high, the protein content is ample, making it suitable for moderate consumption after exercise. Drying the wings during marination enhances flavor, and controlling oil with the addition of bell peppers creates a more balanced dish. Pair with a vegetable salad or light soup to balance the savory saltiness.
