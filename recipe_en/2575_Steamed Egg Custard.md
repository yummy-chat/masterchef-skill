# Steamed Egg Custard
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs
💧 Hot water (boiling water) 1.2 to 1.5 times the volume of the egg mixture
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked, as needed
🦐 Dried shrimp, as needed
🧅 Chopped green onions, as needed (for seasoning and garnish)
🧂 Salt, about 2 to 2.5 grams
🍾 Soy sauce (or light soy sauce), as needed (drizzled on top after steaming)
🫒 Cooking oil (such as flaxseed oil), as needed (added to the bottom of the bowl before steaming)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍵 Porcelain or glass bowl (container for steaming the eggs, avoid iron bowls)
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🕸️ Strainer
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Lid

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl, add about 2 to 2.5 grams of salt.
   **Note**: Salt helps the egg mixture set and solidify; do not omit.
2. Use chopsticks to stir the egg mixture thoroughly until the color is uniform.
3. Add hot water equal to 1.2 to 1.5 times the volume of the egg mixture and stir well.
   **Key point**: Using boiling water is unique to this method, allowing the egg mixture to reach a semi-cooked state in advance. The amount of water determines the final texture: less water yields a firmer bite, more water yields a softer, smoother texture.
4. Strain the mixed egg mixture through a strainer into the steaming bowl.
   **Required step**: Straining removes foam and unbeaten egg whites, which is key to a smooth surface on the finished product.
5. Drizzle an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the bottom of the steaming bowl, then add the soaked shiitake mushrooms, dried shrimp, and some of the chopped green onions.
6. Place the steaming bowl into the steamer. Insert one chopstick on each side of the steamer's inner pot, and set the bowl on top of the chopsticks, so there is a gap between the bowl's bottom and the pot's bottom.
   **Key point**: Steaming elevated allows for more even temperature distribution, preventing excessive heat at the bottom that could create pores.
7. Cover with the lid, leaving a gap on each side.
   **Key point**: Leaving gaps prevents the temperature inside the steamer from getting too high and the steam from being too intense, avoiding a honeycomb texture from the egg mixture boiling.
8. After steam rises, cook over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes.
9. Turn off the heat, let it sit for a few minutes before removing the lid, drizzle with soy sauce, and sprinkle with the remaining chopped green onions.
   **Doneness check**: The steamed egg custard should have a smooth, mirror-like surface, no honeycomb holes, and a tender, silky texture.

## Health Advice
This steamed egg dish is low in fat and high in protein. Adding shiitake mushrooms and dried shrimp enhances the calcium content and umami flavor, making it suitable for the elderly and children to supplement nutrition. Using boiling water to mix the egg mixture locks in a smooth, tender texture, but those with gout may reduce the amount of dried shrimp.
