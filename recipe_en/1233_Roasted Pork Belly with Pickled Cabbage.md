# Roasted Pork Belly with Pickled Cabbage
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, to taste (thinly sliced)  
🥬 Pickled cabbage, to taste (shredded)  
🧅 Purple onion, to taste (shredded)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (cut into segments)  
🥬 Lettuce, to taste (for wrapping the finished dish)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced, used in two additions)  
🍾 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍾 Dark soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Barbecue seasoning, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat cooking oil in the non-stick frying pan. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry until the surface is fragrant and caramelized, with rendered fat, and crispy. Be careful to avoid localized burning.
**Key point**: Stir-fry until the pork belly surface is fragrant and the fat is rendered out, avoiding burning in spots.

2. Add part of the chopped scallions and minced garlic. Stir-fry together with the pork belly until a distinct aroma is released.
3. Add the shredded pickled cabbage and continue stir-frying for a longer period, allowing the pickled cabbage and pork belly to fully combine and release a tangy aroma.
4. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and barbecue seasoning. Stir-fry until all ingredients are evenly coated with the seasonings and have a uniform color.
5. Add the shredded purple onion, remaining minced garlic, and cilantro segments. Stir-fry quickly until the vegetables are just cooked through, then remove from heat immediately.
**Note**: Avoid over-heating to preserve the crisp and tender texture of the purple onion and cilantro.

6. After removing from the heat, serve wrapped in lettuce leaves. It offers a combination of barbecue char and tangy flavor.

## Health Tips
Pork belly has a high fat content, but pickled cabbage is rich in lactic acid bacteria and dietary fiber, which helps cut the grease and aid digestion. It is best enjoyed wrapped in lettuce leaves to reduce calorie load. It is recommended for occasional consumption, ideally paired with barley tea.
