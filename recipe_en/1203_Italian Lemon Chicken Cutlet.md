# Italian Lemon Chicken Cutlet
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast 1 piece (split in half lengthwise, pound the thicker part)
🧅 Shallot or onion a small amount (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🍋 Lemon 1 (a few slices for garnish, squeeze the rest for juice)
🫒 Capers suitable amount (optional)
🍝 Pasta appropriate amount (as a side)
🍾 White wine 100 ml
🧈 Butter 50 g (cold butter)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🌶️ Black pepper appropriate amount
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount
🌾 Flour a thin layer (for dusting the chicken cutlet)
🌿 Parsley appropriate amount (for garnish)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Heat-resistant frying pan (cast iron skillet)
🥘 Saucepan
🥄 Spatula/tongs
🍋 Lemon squeezer
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Instructions
1. Slice the chicken breast horizontally into two pieces, cover with plastic wrap, and gently pound to an even thickness.  
   **Key point**: Even thickness ensures uniform cooking and tender meat.
2. Finely chop the shallot or onion and garlic; slice a few lemon slices for garnish, squeeze the remaining lemon for juice.
3. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil, add pasta, and cook for about 10 minutes until done. Drain and set aside.
4. Season both sides of the chicken cutlets evenly with salt and black pepper, then dust with a thin layer of flour, shaking off excess.  
   **Note**: Use only a very thin layer of flour to avoid affecting texture.
5. Preheat the frying pan over medium-high heat, add vegetable oil until it shimmers slightly, then reduce to low heat and place the chicken cutlets in the pan.  
   **Key point**: Thorough preheating and reducing heat are crucial to prevent sticking. Fry until golden brown on both sides, then remove and set aside.
6. Using the remaining oil in the pan, sauté the minced onion and garlic over low heat until fragrant.
7. Add white wine and a little lemon juice, bring to a boil. Optionally add capers for extra flavor.
8. Reduce the sauce until only a thin layer remains at the bottom of the pan, then switch to very low heat. Add cold butter cubes and stir continuously until fully melted and the sauce emulsifies into a thick consistency.  
   **Key point**: After adding butter, use the lowest heat or remove from heat to prevent separation and ensure the sauce doesn't break.
9. Return the cooked chicken cutlets to the pan, coat evenly with the sauce over low heat or residual heat, and reheat gently. Add lemon slices and sprinkle with chopped parsley.
10. Plate the cooked pasta, arrange the chicken cutlets on top, and drizzle with the remaining sauce.

## Health Tips
This Italian lemon chicken uses pounding and a light flour coating to achieve a tender texture. The combination of lemon juice and cold butter creates a tangy, rich sauce while keeping the dish low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for fitness and weight-loss diets. Be mindful of the butter quantity to avoid excess calories.
