# Fish with Pork Intestine in Chili Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 Silver carp (deboned, with head and bones cut into sections, and fillet sliced into large pieces)
🥓 Appropriate amount of pork intestine (cleaned in advance, blanched, and cut into pieces)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallion (for blanching, marinating fish fillets, and adding fragrance before serving)
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (for blanching, marinating fish fillets, and stir-frying seasonings)
🥬 Appropriate amount of celery (cut into sections, added before serving)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (sliced, for stir-frying seasonings)
🍶 Appropriate amount of high-proof baijiu (for removing fishy smell when blanching pork intestine)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorn (for releasing aroma when stir-frying the base in hot oil)
🥒 Appropriate amount of pickled ginger (sliced, added together with pickled chili)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of pickled chili (cut into rings, added together with pickled ginger)
🍛 Appropriate amount of doubanjiang (about one tablespoon, stir-fried until red oil releases)
🫕 Appropriate amount of hot pot base (about one piece, to add complex flavor)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dried chili powder (for adding color and aroma when stir-frying the base)
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt (for providing base flavor when marinating fish fillets)
🧂 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon (for enhancing freshness when marinating fish fillets)
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper powder (for removing fishy smell and adding aroma when marinating fish fillets)
🍺 Appropriate amount of beer (a few drops, for removing fishy smell when marinating fish fillets)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dried Sichuan peppercorn (sprinkled on the surface after plating, to add numbing flavor and aroma before pouring hot oil)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dried chili (sprinkled on the surface after plating, to add spiciness and aroma before pouring hot oil)
🤍 Appropriate amount of white sesame seeds (sprinkled on the surface after plating, to add aroma and garnish before pouring hot oil)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large bowl/basin
🥄 Large ladle

## Steps
**Preparation Phase**

1. Place the pork intestine in a pot with cold water, add scallion, ginger, and high-proof baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat, then remove and cut into pieces.
2. Slice the pickled ginger, cut the pickled chili into rings, slice the garlic, and set aside.
3. Clean the fish thoroughly. Separate the head, bones, and fillet: cut the head and bones into sections, and slice the fillet into large pieces.
4. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, and beer to the fish fillets. Rub and mix repeatedly with your hands until the fillets become sticky and firm.
**Cooking Phase**

1. Heat oil in a wok and add lard to melt. Over medium heat, add Sichuan peppercorn, pickled ginger, pickled chili, garlic slices, and doubanjiang in sequence, stir-frying until red oil releases. Then add the hot pot base and dried chili powder, stir-frying until fragrant.
2. Once the base is fragrant, add the pork intestine and stir-fry evenly. Pour in boiling water to submerge the intestine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for about 1 hour until the intestine is tender.
3. Remove the cooked pork intestine and set aside. Add the fish head and bones to the remaining broth, cook for about 2 minutes until just done, then remove and spread them at the bottom of a bowl.
4. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Slide the marinated fish fillets in one by one, gently pushing them with the back of a ladle. Cook until the fillets change color and are just cooked through.
5. Once the fish fillets are cooked, return the reserved pork intestine to the wok. Add celery segments and scallion segments, gently mix, then turn off the heat. Pour everything into the bowl with the fish bones at the bottom.
6. Sprinkle dried Sichuan peppercorn, dried chili, and white sesame seeds over the surface. Heat a spoonful of oil until smoking, then quickly pour it over the surface to release the aroma. Optionally, garnish with cilantro.

## Health Advice
This Sichuan-style fish with pork intestine is rich in high-quality protein and collagen, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, it is high in fat and sodium, so it is recommended to balance the meal with light vegetables. Those with cardiovascular conditions should consume it in moderation.
