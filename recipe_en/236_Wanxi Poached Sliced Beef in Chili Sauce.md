# Wanxi Poached Sliced Beef in Chili Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin, appropriate amount (main ingredient, sliced)
🫘 Dried bean curd sticks, appropriate amount (soaked in advance)
🥬 Lettuce tips, appropriate amount (base vegetable)
🌱 Bean sprouts, appropriate amount (base vegetable)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (part for stir-frying aroma, part minced for final sprinkle)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for scallion-ginger water)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for scallion-ginger water)

🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for marinating beef, stir-frying vegetables, seasoning broth)
🧂 Baking soda, a pinch (for marinating beef, to tenderize)
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating beef)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating beef, for color)
🥚 Egg yolk, appropriate amount (for marinating beef, reserve egg white for processing bean curd sticks)
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for marinating beef, processing bean curd sticks, final thickening)
💧 Scallion-ginger water, appropriate amount (added gradually to marinated beef)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for sealing, pan-frying bean curd sticks, stir-frying vegetables, stir-frying seasonings, final drizzling)
🌶️ Dried chilies, appropriate amount (for making chopped chilies)
🫐 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (for making chopped chilies)
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), about 1.5 tablespoons (for stir-frying the broth base)
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount (for stir-frying the broth base, to increase heat and color)
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount (for seasoning the broth)
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount (for seasoning the broth)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for seasoning the broth)
💧 Water starch, appropriate amount (for a light slurry before finishing the sliced beef)
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (sprinkled before final oil drizzle)
🫘 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (sprinkled before final oil drizzle)
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder, appropriate amount (sprinkled before final oil drizzle)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Ladle
🪣 Strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make chopped chilies**: Add a small amount of oil to the wok, dry-fry dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns over the lowest heat until the chilies become crispy and fragrant. Remove and chop finely on a cutting board with a knife.
**Note**: Must use the lowest heat to prevent burning; the chilies are crispy enough when easily crushed by hand.

2. **Marinate the beef slices**: Slice beef tenderloin. Add a pinch of salt and a little baking soda. Mix and knead until the slices become sticky and developing a tacky texture. Gradually add the scallion-ginger water in small batches, mixing vigorously each time until the liquid is fully absorbed. Add oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and egg yolk, mix well. Then add a small handful of cornstarch and a little chopped chili, mix well. Finally, drizzle a little cooking oil to seal the surface and marinate for 20 minutes.
**Key point**: Mixing is the core step for tenderizing the beef. The slices must fully absorb the liquid and become sticky, which is essential for smoothness and tenderness.

3. **Prepare the bean curd sticks**: Squeeze excess water from the soaked bean curd sticks. Add the reserved egg white from the marinade and a layer of cornstarch. Mix well to coat the surface.
**Note**: This step helps the bean curd sticks develop a crispy skin when pan-fried.

4. **Stir-fry the base vegetables**: Add oil to the wok. Stir-fry the lettuce tips (with tough outer leaves removed) and bean sprouts separately until just cooked. Season with a little salt. Remove and spread them at the bottom of the serving bowl.
**Note**: Keep the vegetables crisp and do not overcook.

5. **Prepare the broth base**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Stir-fry minced scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Reduce heat to low. Add Pixian doubanjiang and chili flakes, stir-fry until the oil turns red and the mixture is fragrant. Add boiling water. Bring to a boil, then simmer over medium heat for about 3 minutes. Use a strainer to remove the solid residue. Season the broth with salt, sugar, chicken powder, and light soy sauce.
**Key point**: Doubanjiang and chili flakes must be stir-fried over low heat to prevent burning. Removing the residue makes the dish taste cleaner and smoother.

6. **Cook the beef slices**: Bring the broth to a boil over high heat, then **immediately turn off the heat**. Gently add the marinated beef slices one by one or quickly scatter them into the broth. Use chopsticks to gently separate them to prevent sticking. **Turn the heat back on**, cook for about 30 seconds until the slices are fully changed color and cooked through. Drizzle in a small amount of water starch to create a light slurry, stir gently, then turn off the heat immediately.
**Key point**: "Adding beef with the heat off" allows the residual heat of the broth to initially set the slices. Turning the heat back on quickly cooks them thoroughly. This method maximizes tenderness and prevents the coating from coming off.

7. **Assemble and drizzle with hot oil**: Quickly pour the cooked beef slices and broth into the bowl over the prepared vegetables and bean curd sticks. On top of the beef, sprinkle chopped chilies, minced garlic, white sesame seeds, and Sichuan peppercorn powder. Heat cooking oil until it is smoking hot and shimmering. Drizzle the hot oil over the seasonings in three batches, listening for the sizzling sound.
**Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the aroma of garlic and chilies.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber. However, doubanjiang and the final hot oil drizzle contribute relatively high calories. It is recommended to serve with light, plain vegetables. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy and numbing flavors, but people with sensitive stomachs should control their intake.
