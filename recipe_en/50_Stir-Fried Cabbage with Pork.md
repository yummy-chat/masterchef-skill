# Stir-Fried Cabbage with Pork
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Cabbage: About 1 whole head (washed, core removed, torn into pieces by hand)  
🥩 Pork belly: About 150g (sliced into thin pieces)  
🌿 Garlic sprouts: Appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper: Appropriate amount (cut into chunks or strips)  
🧅 Green onions: 2-3 stalks (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Dried chilies: 8-10 pieces  
🌸 Dried Sichuan peppercorns: About 1 small handful  
🧄 Garlic: 3-4 cloves (minced)  
🫒 Cooking oil: Appropriate amount  
🍶 Dark soy sauce: Appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt: Appropriate amount  
🍶 Light soy sauce: Appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili oil: Appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🥄 Wok spatula / spatula  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the cabbage in half, remove the hard central core, and tear it by hand into evenly sized pieces.
2. Slice the pork belly into thin pieces; cut the green onions into sections; cut the garlic sprouts into sections; cut the red bell pepper into chunks or slices; mince the garlic. Place the dried chilies, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and green onion sections into a bowl and set aside.
3. Heat the wok over high heat, rotate the wok to evenly heat the inner surface. Pour in a small amount of cooking oil to coat the wok, then discard the excess oil.  
   **Key point**: Properly coating the wok prevents the pork belly from sticking.
4. Add the sliced pork belly and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the edges turn golden brown, the fat renders, and a fragrant aroma is released.  
   **Key point**: Fully rendering the fat ensures the meat slices are crispy and not greasy.
5. Add the green onion sections, dried chilies, and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.
6. Splash a small amount of dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly so the meat slices are evenly colored to a dark reddish-brown.  
   **Note**: Do not use too much dark soy sauce to avoid making the dish black.
7. Add all the cabbage. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the cabbage shrinks in volume, darkens in color, softens in texture, but remains crisp.  
   **Key point**: High heat quick-stirring locks in the crisp texture and prevents excess water from being released.
8. Add an appropriate amount of salt, light soy sauce, and chili oil. Stir-fry over high heat to evenly combine and infuse the flavors.
9. Add the garlic sprout sections and red bell pepper chunks. Stir-fry over high heat until the garlic sprouts are just cooked and the red bell pepper is slightly softened, maintaining their color and texture.
10. Turn off the heat or reduce it to the lowest setting. Sprinkle in the minced garlic. Use the residual heat to quickly stir-fry, releasing a rich garlic aroma.  
    **Key point**: Adding the minced garlic at the end uses the residual heat to release its fragrance, preventing it from burning and turning bitter.
11. Quickly remove from the wok and plate to preserve the crisp texture and the "wok hei" (breath of the wok).

## Health Advice
Cabbage is rich in vitamin U and dietary fiber. Pairing it with pork belly helps promote the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. However, it's necessary to control oil intake, so balancing the meal with multigrain rice is recommended to moderate calorie consumption. Suitable for those needing fiber and energy; note that people with high blood lipids should reduce the amount of pork belly used.
