# Preserved Mustard Greens (Meigan Cai)
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥬 Leafy greens (mustard greens, bok choy) Approximately 50 jin (about 60 lbs), choose varieties with low water content  
🧂 Edible salt As needed (only for salted preserved greens)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Large pot (for blanching greens)  
♨️ Steamer pot with steaming mat  
🫙 Large basin (with lid, for fermenting and kneading)  
👚 Clothes hangers or ropes (for sun-drying)  
📦 Airtight container (for storing the finished product)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Select mustard greens or bok choy with low water content, wash thoroughly, especially the soil at the root of the leaves.  
2. Hang the washed greens in the sun to dry for 2 days, until the leaves become wilted and surface moisture is removed.  
**Note**: This should be done in sunny weather.  

3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the sun-wilted greens and blanch over high heat for about 1 minute. Remove immediately once the color changes.  
**Key point**: Do not cook until soft or mushy, or the finished dried greens will be unusable.  

4. Stack all the blanched greens in a large basin, press down firmly, cover and seal tightly. Ferment at room temperature overnight.  
**How to judge**: The greens will turn yellow the next day.  

5. Remove the fermented yellow greens, hang them in the sun again to dry for 2-3 days, until they are half-soft and half-dry.  
6. Place the half-dried greens on a steamer mat in a steamer pot, press down firmly, and steam over high heat for 15 minutes, until the aroma of preserved mustard greens is released.  
**Tip**: If conditions allow, you can steam and dry three times (三蒸三晒) for better flavor and color. The video only demonstrates it once.  

7. Process according to your needs:  
   - **Plain preserved greens**: Directly sun-dry the steamed greens for 2-3 days until fully dry, then tie them into bundles for storage.  
   - **Salted preserved greens**: Take the softer, cooled steamed greens, add an appropriate amount of edible salt, and knead repeatedly with both hands until every leaf is evenly coated with salt and no excess moisture remains on the surface.  
8. For salted preserved greens, seal and store directly after kneading. For plain preserved greens, store in an airtight container after sun-drying.  
**Explanation**: The more times you steam and dry, the richer the flavor.  

## Health Tips  
Preserved mustard greens are rich in dietary fiber, suitable for people who need to promote digestion or control weight. However, the salted version has high sodium content, so hypertensive patients should consume it in moderation. Full fermentation and multiple rounds of steaming and drying during preparation enhance the flavor, but be careful not to blanch the greens for too long.
