# Cantonese Wonton Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥟 Wontons (appropriate amount, frozen or homemade)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🥬 Chinese chives (appropriate amount)  
🍘 Dried seaweed (appropriate amount)  
🐟 Ground dried flounder fish powder (appropriate amount)  
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)  
🫒 Sesame oil (appropriate amount)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
✂️ Scissors  
🥛 Small milk pot or cup  
🍜 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Cooking Steps
1. Place an appropriate amount of sesame oil, cut Chinese chives segments, and dried seaweed into the bottom of a bowl. Set aside.  
**Key point**: Seasoning the bowl base, using the hot soup to bloom and release the fragrance.  
2. Fill a soup pot with water and bring to a boil. Once boiling, add the wontons and maintain a rolling boil.  
3. In another small milk pot or cup, crack one raw egg and set aside.  
**Key point**: Using a container helps keep the egg shape intact.  
4. Add the ground dried flounder fish powder and salt to the pot with the boiling wontons. Stir well.  
**Key point**: Ground dried flounder fish powder is the essence of Cantonese wonton soup, providing the core umami flavor.  
5. When the wontons float to the surface, gently slide the egg, still in its container, into the pot, then **immediately turn off the heat**.  
**Key point**: Let it rest for 5-6 minutes after turning off the heat to allow the egg and wontons to cook through internally, avoiding continuous boiling which can cause the egg to break apart, foam, or become tough.  
6. After the resting time is complete, first ladle the soup and wontons into the prepared bowl, then carefully scoop out the egg and place it into the bowl.  
**Key point**: Removing the egg last helps maintain its shape better.

## Health Advice  
Cantonese wonton soup is fresh, fragrant, and clear. The ground dried flounder fish powder provides the core umami flavor. It is recommended to use less salt. Seaweed and Chinese chives can supplement dietary fiber. Cooking the egg by simmering off the heat preserves more nutrients. Suitable as a high-protein, low-fat light meal. Note that wonton fillings may be high in fat, especially frozen products, so consumption should be controlled.
