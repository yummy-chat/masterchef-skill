# Succulent Potted Plant Dessert
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍫 Dark chocolate, 2 bars  
🫘 White bean paste, 10 portions  
🧈 White butter, 1 portion  
🥛 Whipping cream, as needed  
🥚 Eggs, 3  
🥛 Milk, 50 g  
🫒 Cooking oil, 50 g  
🌾 Cake flour, 60 g  
🌽 Cornstarch, 10 g  
☕ Freeze-dried coffee powder, as needed  
🥥 Coconut milk, as needed  
🍪 Oreo cookies, as needed  
🎨 White food coloring, as needed  
🎨 Green food coloring, as needed  
🎨 Red food coloring, a small amount  

## Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
♨️ Induction cooker  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🧁 Paper cups  
♨️ Wire rack  
🧁 Piping bag  
👄 Flat piping tip  
📌 Piping nail  
🥚 Electric hand mixer  
🧂 Sifter  
🍳 Square baking pan  
♨️ Oven  
🍪 Round cookie cutter  

## Instructions  
1. Make the chocolate cups: Melt the dark chocolate over a water bath until smooth. Pour it into the paper cups, rotate the cups to coat the insides evenly, then pour out any excess chocolate. Invert the cups onto a wire rack and refrigerate to set.  
   **Note**: Using too little chocolate results in thin, fragile walls; it is recommended to repeat the coating 2-3 times to thicken the walls.

2. Prepare the bean paste buttercream: Mix the white bean paste and white butter in a 10:1 ratio. First, add white food coloring to create a white base, then set aside a portion and color it with green food coloring to match the succulents. Transfer to a piping bag and set aside.  
   **Key**: Adding a contrasting color makes the shades more natural and subdued, avoiding an overly bright and harsh look.

3. Bake the coffee cake base: Combine the eggs, milk, and cooking oil. Sift in the cake flour, cornstarch, and freeze-dried coffee powder, and mix until a smooth batter forms. Pour the batter into a parchment-lined square baking pan and spread evenly. Preheat the oven to 170°C and bake for about 15 minutes, until the surface is golden and cooked through. Remove from the oven, invert onto a wire rack, and let cool.

4. Make the coconut cream: Whip the whipping cream with an electric hand mixer until distinct lines form. Add the coconut milk and continue whipping until it reaches medium peaks and a piping-friendly consistency, creating a coconut-flavored cream.

5. Unmold and cut: Carefully peel away the paper cups to unmold the chocolate cups. Use a round cookie cutter to cut the cake base into small circles.

6. Assemble the potted plant: Place one cake circle at the bottom of a chocolate cup, then pipe a layer of coconut cream on top. Repeat layering with cake circles and cream. Evenly sprinkle crushed Oreo cookies over the top layer of cream to mimic soil.

7. Decorate with succulents: Pipe various succulent shapes using the bean paste buttercream onto a piping nail, then freeze until firm. Use scissors or a small tool to carefully transfer the frozen bean paste flowers onto the crushed Oreo cookies to decorate. Be creative with the succulent shapes; perfection is not required.

## Health Advice  
This dessert layers chocolate, bean paste, and cream for a rich flavor, but it is also high in calories. It is recommended as an occasional dessert for sharing at gatherings, with one small cup per serving. The combination of coffee and coconut milk helps balance the sweetness and richness, making it a good match with unsweetened tea.
