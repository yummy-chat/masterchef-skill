# Braised Beef Rib Strips
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐂 Beef rib strips, appropriate amount (with sinew for better texture)
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves, appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (about 2 tablespoons for braising, half a tablespoon for sauce)
🍶 Huadiao wine, appropriate amount (for blanching, braising, and sauce)
🫙 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring)
🥢 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for savory flavor and color)
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon
🧂 MSG, half a tablespoon
🥄 Water starch, appropriate amount (for thickening)
🫗 Bright oil (seasoned oil), appropriate amount (for aroma and sheen)
🍲 Reserved beef broth, appropriate amount (as sauce base)

## Cookware and Equipment
🫗 Large transparent bowl (for soaking and removing blood)
🍳 Wok (for blanching, sauce making, simmering, and plating)
♨️ Pressure cooker (for quickly tenderizing beef)
🧊 Stainless steel square pan (for holding and pressing beef to set shape)
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board (for slicing beef)
🥄 Chopsticks (for testing tenderness)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming and retrieving beef)
🥄 Wok spatula (for stirring, pushing, and thickening)

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak beef rib strips in clean water for about 2 hours to fully draw out blood.
2. Place the soaked beef in cold water, add Huadiao wine, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off foam, then remove and rinse clean.
3. Transfer beef to a pressure cooker, add enough water, then add cinnamon, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, dried chilies, scallion segments, ginger slices, about 2 tablespoons of salt, and 1 tablespoon of Huadiao wine. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 60 minutes. Turn off heat and let rest for 10 minutes until the pressure valve releases naturally.
   **Key point**: The beef should be easily pierced through with chopsticks, indicating it’s tender.
4. Remove the beef and place it in a stainless steel square pan. Press a plate on top and let it cool naturally to set its shape, allowing for even slicing.
5. Once cooled, slice the beef into pieces about 0.8–1 cm thick.
   **Key point**: Slice slightly thicker for better texture and to prevent crumbling.
6. Pour an appropriate amount of reserved beef broth into the wok. Add Huadiao wine, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, half a tablespoon of salt, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and half a tablespoon of MSG. Bring to a boil and mix well.
7. Gently slide the sliced beef into the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the beef to fully absorb the sauce.
8. Once the sauce has reduced to a suitable consistency, drizzle in water starch to thicken, then add bright oil for aroma and sheen. Gently shake the wok to evenly coat the beef with the thickened sauce.
9. Use the "wok flip" technique to quickly turn the entire batch of beef over, ensuring both sides are evenly coated with sauce, and slide it in one smooth motion onto a serving plate.
   **Key point**: The motion must be fast and steady to keep the beef slices intact.
10. Garnish with a few cilantro leaves for visual appeal, then serve.

## Health Suggestions
Rich in protein and iron, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing blood replenishment. Salt and sugar are added during braising; those with high blood pressure or blood sugar concerns should consume in moderation. Pair with light vegetables to balance the flavor and avoid overly thick starch coating to reduce calorie intake.
