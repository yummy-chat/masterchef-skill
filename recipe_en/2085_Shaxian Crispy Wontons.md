# Shaxian Crispy Wontons
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Difficulty Rating: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 250g pork hind leg meat (fully lean, pre-frozen for 2 hours, cut into small pieces)
💧 100g ice water (with ice chips, added in three portions)
🫓 Approximately 30-40 wonton wrappers (9cm×9cm square)
🌽 Appropriate amount of corn kernels (optional, for the children's version)
🧂 3g salt
🧂 5g MSG
🧂 5g chicken bouillon
🧂 15g baking soda
🍬 5g white sugar
🌿 0.5g white pepper powder
🫒 2g sesame oil
🧄 2g garlic (1 clove, minced)
🫚 2g ginger (optional, minced)
🍲 Appropriate amount of dried seaweed
🌿 Appropriate amount of cilantro/scallions
🍬 A pinch of white sugar (for flavor enhancement at the bottom of the bowl)
🌿 A pinch of pepper powder
🧂 Appropriate amount of MSG
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt
🫒 Appropriate amount of sesame oil
🌶️ Appropriate amount of chili oil (add according to preference before eating)
🫗 Appropriate amount of vinegar (add according to preference before eating)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
⚙️ Home meat grinder
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Measuring bowl/small bowl
🥘 Stainless steel basin/large bowl
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Take the lean pork hind leg meat that has been frozen for 2 hours in advance and cut it into small pieces.
2. Place the meat pieces into the meat grinder, add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, baking soda, white sugar, white pepper powder, sesame oil, minced garlic, and ginger.
**Key Point**: Garlic is the core seasoning and must not be omitted.
3. Add the ice water in three portions and grind:
   - First time: Pour in approximately 33g of ice water, pulse-grind 30 times.
- Second time: Pour in another approximately 33g of ice water, pulse-grind 30 times.
- Third time: Pour in all the remaining ice water, pulse-grind 15 times first, then repeat "hold for 3 seconds and release" 20 times, finally hold the switch continuously until the machine stops automatically.
**Key Point**: Must use ice water, add in portions, strictly follow the pulse count; keep the meat filling cold throughout the process.
4. Pour the ground meat paste into a container, press the surface flat, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least one night.
**Key Point**: Overnight refrigeration is a necessary step for the filling to become crispy.
5. Remove the meat filling. For a children's version, mix a portion with corn kernels.
### Production Phase
1. Take a wonton wrapper, spread meat filling in the center. Optionally, place a small piece of century egg. Fold diagonally into a triangle, then pinch the two acute angles together towards the center, ensuring it is tightly sealed.
2. In a soup bowl, place dried seaweed, scallions, chopped cilantro, a pinch of white sugar, pepper powder, MSG, salt, and sesame oil.
3. Bring water in a pot to a rolling boil, add the wontons. After it returns to a boil, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 1.5 minutes, until the wontons float and the wrappers become translucent.
**Note**: Reduce heat to medium-low after boiling to prevent the wonton wrappers from breaking.
4. First, ladle a spoonful of the hot wonton cooking water into the bowl to release the aroma of the seasonings, stir well. Then, scoop in the wontons. Add chili oil and vinegar according to personal preference before eating.

## Health Advice
The crispiness of these Shaxian crispy wontons comes from the long refrigeration of the meat filling and the clever use of baking soda. Its high-protein, low-fat nature makes it suitable for muscle building or weight control. However, the amounts of baking soda and MSG are relatively high; those with high blood pressure or sensitive kidneys should reduce the seasonings.
