## Preparation Steps  
### Making the Congee Base  
1. Wash the Northeast rice. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the rice and a little cooking oil. Bring to a boil over high heat, then stir continuously to prevent sticking. Cook over high heat until the rice grains are fully bloomed and the congee is thick, smooth, and creamy, forming a base akin to "rice-free congee."  
### Handling and Marinating the Pork Liver  
2. Insert a water pipe into the blood vessels inside the whole pork liver and flush continuously until the water runs completely clear and the liver's color changes from dark red to whitish, fully removing any off-flavors.  
3. Slice the liver into thin or thick slices and place in a slotted spoon or strainer to drain thoroughly.  
**Key Point**: After slicing, the liver must be fully drained to avoid a watery finished dish.  

4. Put the drained liver slices into a bowl. Add, in order, minced ginger, rice wine, salt, sugar, and a generous amount of white pepper. Mix well with your hands. Then add a little cornstarch and mix again. Finally, drizzle with peanut oil and toss to coat, sealing the surface.  
**Note**: Use a little more white pepper than usual; it effectively removes off-flavors and enhances aroma.  

### Quick-Boil Cooking the Congee  
5. In a small pot, add one ladleful of the boiling congee base. Add the marinated lean meat and pork liver, gently stirring to separate. Add ginger shreds and Tianjin preserved vegetable.  
6. Keep the congee at a boil. Once it comes back to a boil, cook for only about 1–2 seconds, until the lean meat and liver are completely changed in color, then turn off the heat.  
**Key Point for Heat Control**: The cooking time must be extremely short to ensure the liver remains tender and crunchy in texture.  

7. Ladle into a bowl, sprinkle with chopped scallions for garnish, and serve hot.  

## Health Tips  
Pork liver and lean meat are rich in iron and high-quality protein, making them suitable for those with anemia or needing to replenish blood. However, people with high cholesterol should consume in moderation. When cooking the congee, pay attention to the heat; turn off the heat as soon as the liver changes color to preserve its tender texture and nutrients.
