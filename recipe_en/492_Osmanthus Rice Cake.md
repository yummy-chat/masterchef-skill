# Osmanthus Rice Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Rice flour 200g (10 units)  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 80g (4 units)  
💧 Water 140g (4.5 units, adjust according to flour condition)  
🌸 Dried osmanthus flowers appropriate amount (for surface decoration)  
🍬 White sugar about 40g (5 bottle caps, sweetness adjustable)  
🍀 Pandan powder or matcha powder appropriate amount (optional, for other flavors)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🧴 Graduated plastic bottle (homemade measuring tool, 1 unit ≈ 20g)  
🥣 Enamel bowl  
📏 20-mesh sieve  
🍳 Steamer (or steamer rack for rice cooker)  
🧻 Cheesecloth  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Place rice flour and glutinous rice flour in a large bowl, and mix initially with chopsticks until even.  
2. Add water, first stir with chopsticks, then repeatedly rub by hand to break up large lumps, allowing water to evenly penetrate the flour.  
   - **Key check**: Take a handful of flour and gently squeeze into a lump; if it holds together when released and remains lumpy when lightly tossed, and breaks cleanly in half, the moisture is appropriate.  
3. Sift the mixed wet flour through a 20-mesh sieve into another bowl, making the flour fine and fluffy.  
4. Add white sugar, and gently mix evenly with your hands.  
5. Lay cheesecloth in the steamer, **loosely** pour in the prepared flour, and gently smooth the surface with a spoon. Do not press down.  
6. Evenly sprinkle dried osmanthus flowers over the surface.  
7. Bring the steamer water to a boil, place the steamer inside, cover, and steam for about 25 minutes.  
8. After steaming, you can directly cut into desired shapes in the steamer; if you want neat slices, pre-cut the raw dough with a knife before placing it in the steamer.  
9. Remove and plate.  

## Health Suggestion  
Osmanthus rice cake, made with rice flour and glutinous rice flour, is soft and easy to digest, making it suitable as an afternoon tea snack in autumn. However, the sugar amount can be halved for a healthier option. Note that sifting and loose spreading are key for a fluffy, non-sticky texture.
