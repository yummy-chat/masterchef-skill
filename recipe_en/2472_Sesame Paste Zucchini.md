# Sesame Paste Zucchini
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥒 Zucchini Approximately 1 (peeled, seeded, and shredded)  
🧄 Garlic To taste (minced)  
🌶️ Small Red Chili Peppers To taste (sliced into rings, for garnish)  
🥜 Sesame Paste About 2 tbsp  
🧂 Salt About 3 grams  
🧂 MSG A pinch  
🍬 Sugar To taste  
🍶 Light Soy Sauce About 1 tbsp  
🍶 Aromatic Vinegar About 1 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster Sauce To taste  
🫒 Sesame Oil To taste  
🌿 Mustard Paste (Horseradish) To taste  
🌶️ Chili Oil To taste (optional)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔥 Wok  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🪵 Cutting Board  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl (for soaking zucchini and mixing sauce)  
🥣 Small Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧴 Colander/Strainer  
🧻 Kitchen Paper  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps
1. Blanch the whole peeled zucchini in boiling water for about 3 seconds, then immediately transfer to cold water.  
   **Note**: Blanching is only to remove surface pesticide residue; the time must be short to avoid cooking through.  
2. Slice the zucchini lengthwise into thin slices about 0.2 cm thick, stopping when you reach the seeded core. Discard the core.  
   **Key Point**: The core has a bitter taste and must be removed, otherwise it will affect the flavor and texture.  
3. Cut the zucchini slices into thin shreds and soak in cold water for about 3 minutes.  
   **Purpose**: This allows the zucchini shreds to absorb water, reduces bitterness, makes them crisper, and prevents discoloration. Do not soak for more than 3 minutes to avoid nutrient loss.  
4. Prepare the sesame paste sauce: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, sesame paste, salt, MSG, sugar, light soy sauce, aromatic vinegar, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and mustard paste. Stir in one consistent direction until the mixture becomes thick and sticky.  
   **Key Point**: Always stir in the same direction; do not stir back and forth, as this may cause the oil to separate from the paste.  
   - If you like spice, add chili oil at this point. Let the sauce rest for 5 minutes after mixing to allow the flavors to meld more deeply.  
5. Drain the zucchini shreds using a colander, removing excess water. Then use kitchen paper to pat dry any remaining surface moisture.  
   **Note**: The zucchini must be thoroughly dried, otherwise the sauce will not cling properly, affecting the overall flavor.  
6. Arrange the zucchini shreds on a plate, piling them into a small mound. Evenly pour the prepared sesame paste sauce over the top, and garnish with small red chili pepper rings. Serve.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in dietary fiber, low in calories, and high in water content, making it suitable for weight loss or a refreshing summer appetizer. The short blanching time preserves the crisp texture, but those with weak spleen or stomach conditions should consume raw or cold dishes sparingly.
