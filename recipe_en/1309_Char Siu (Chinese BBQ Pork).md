# Char Siu (Chinese BBQ Pork)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork neck (梅肉) 600g (1.2 jin, with some fat, lean meat, and tendons)  
🧅 Minced garlic, as needed  
🧅 Minced onion, as needed  
🥜 Peanut butter, as needed  
🥜 Sesame paste, as needed  
🧂 Char siu sauce, as needed  
🧂 Hoisin sauce, as needed  
🧂 MSG (味素), as needed  
🧂 Yellow soybean paste (黄豆酱), as needed  
🧂 Fermented red bean curd sauce (南乳汁), as needed (use more to enhance color and flavor)  
🧂 Garlic powder, as needed  
🧂 Sand ginger powder (沙姜粉), as needed  
🍬 Granulated sugar, a generous amount (to highlight sweetness)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🍯 Maltose, as needed (for marinade and final glaze)  
🍶 Rose wine (玫瑰露酒), about two bottle caps  
🧂 Five-spice powder, as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
♨️ Air fryer  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍴 Fork  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥘 Baking rack (with optional aluminum foil)  
🖌️ Brush  

## Steps  
1.  Slice the whole piece of pork neck into large pieces about 0.5-1 cm thick.  
2.  Soak the meat slices in cold water for 2-3 hours to draw out blood and reduce gamey odor. Drain and pat dry.  
3.  Use a fork to prick small holes evenly on both sides of the meat slices to allow better absorption of flavor.  
4.  In a stainless steel bowl, combine all dry and wet seasonings and mix well. **Note**: Use a generous amount of sugar; the fermented red bean curd sauce is key for color and flavor.  
5.  Add about two bottle caps of rose wine to the marinade and mix again.  
6.  Place the pricked meat slices into the marinade, using your hands to rub thoroughly, ensuring each slice is fully coated with the sauce.  
7.  Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 3 hours, preferably overnight.  
8.  Arrange the marinated meat slices flat on the air fryer baking rack without overlapping.  
9.  Set the air fryer to 180°C and cook for 26 minutes.  
10. After about 13-14 minutes, pull out the basket, flip the meat slices with a fork (the surface should be red and slightly charred at this point).  
11. Once cooked, remove the pork and brush the surface with melted maltose.  
12. Increase the air fryer temperature to 200°C and cook the glazed char siu for another 30-40 seconds to caramelize the sugar on the surface.  
13. Remove the char siu, trim off any burnt edges, and slice diagonally before plating.  

## Health Advice  
Using an air fryer instead of traditional roasting reduces oil intake in this char siu recipe. However, due to the natural fat content of pork neck and the generous amount of sugar in the marinade, it is best consumed in moderation. Pair with a vegetable salad for a balanced meal. Diabetics or those limiting sugar intake should enjoy with caution.
