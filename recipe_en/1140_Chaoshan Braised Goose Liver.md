# Chaoshan Braised Goose Liver
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 1 Lion's Head goose liver (about 600g)
🧂 Coarse salt, as needed (for scrubbing goose wings, feet, and other skin-on parts)
🥚 Goose eggs, as needed (pre-boiled and peeled)
🫘 Bean sprouts, as needed (for lining the plate)
🧅 Braised tofu, as needed (placed underneath to absorb the sauce)

## Seasonings
🌿 Cinnamon stick, 10g
🌟 Star anise, 10g
🍃 Bay leaves, 5g
🌸 Cloves, 2g
🌱 Cumin seeds, 4g
🤍 Cardamom, 4g
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, 3g
🌾 Licorice, 2g
🌰 Black cardamom, 4g
🫚 Galangal, 100g
🐟 Dried sole fish (flatfish), as needed
🧄 Garlic, 100g
🧅 Shallots, 50g
🌿 Cilantro roots, 20g
🥣 Chaoshan aged master braising liquid, 1000ml
🍾 Braised goose soy sauce, 1000ml
🟤 Caramel color, 200ml (or 30g rock sugar)
💧 Purified water, 1000ml
🧂 Salt, as needed
🦪 Fish sauce, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed (for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili or Chaoshan chili sauce, as needed (for dipping sauce)
🍬 White sugar, a pinch (for dipping sauce)
🍶 White vinegar, as needed (for dipping sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥘 Stainless steel soup pot
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🫙 Spice bag
🥄 Slotted spoon
🌡️ Salinity meter
🥄 Ladle
🍽️ Plates

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the dry spices in an oil-free wok and dry-fry over low heat until fragrant. Remove from heat and enclose in a spice bag, tying it securely.
**Key point**: Pre-frying the spices fully releases their aroma.

2. Smash the garlic cloves, smash the shallots, wash and cut the cilantro roots into sections, and slice the galangal. Set aside.

3. In a large soup pot, first pour in the Chaoshan aged master braising liquid, then add the braised goose soy sauce, caramel color, and purified water in order. Add the galangal, garlic, shallots, cilantro roots, and the spice bag.
**Note**: The goose fat on the surface of the aged master braising liquid is key to the flavor and gloss – do not skim it off.

4. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and maintain a gentle simmer. Cook for 30 minutes to fully infuse the spice flavors into the braising liquid.

5. Adjust the saltiness with salt and fish sauce, targeting a salinity of about 4%.
6. Place the cleaned Lion's Head goose liver directly into the braising liquid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to low heat and braise for 40 minutes.
**Key point**: The goose liver does not need to be blanched; remove it immediately once the time is up and let it cool naturally.

7. For skin-on parts like goose wings and feet, scrub them repeatedly with coarse salt, rinse thoroughly, and then add to the pot. For goose eggs, boil and peel them in advance.
- Braising time reference for different parts: Goose gizzard 35 minutes, goose wings 40 minutes, goose feet 30 minutes, goose eggs 60 minutes. Add them to the pot in batches or remove them according to their respective times.

8. Goose intestines preparation: Blanch the bean sprouts and arrange them on a plate. In a separate pot, bring some braising liquid to a boil. Add the cleaned goose intestines and blanch for about 15 seconds. As soon as they curl up, remove them immediately, cut into sections, place them on the bean sprouts, and pour the boiling braising liquid over them.
**Key point**: Goose intestines become tough very easily, so the blanching time must be very short.

9. Dipping sauce preparation: Mix together minced garlic, bird's eye chili or chili sauce, a pinch of white sugar, and white vinegar.
10. When reheating refrigerated braised goose, do not use a steamer. Slice the braised goose and arrange it on a plate. Bring a suitable amount of braising liquid to a boil, and pour it over the goose slices one ladleful at a time. Repeat several times to restore the temperature and texture.

## Health Advice
This Chaoshan braised goose liver is rich in iron and Vitamin A, making it beneficial for blood nourishment and eye health. However, it is high in cholesterol, so individuals with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with bean sprouts and the vinegar dipping sauce to cut the richness, and to limit intake to 2–3 slices per serving.
