# Seafood Longevity Noodles
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Dried noodles, appropriate amount
🥬 Shanghai bok choy, appropriate amount (use the tender center)
🥕 Carrot, 1 piece (sliced thick, then cut into strips)
🌿 Chinese chives, 1 bunch (cut into long sections)
🌶️ Small chili peppers, a few (seeds removed)
🦪 Clams, 8 pieces (soak to remove sand, then rinse clean)
🦐 Shrimp, appropriate amount (remove head and shell, butterfly the back to make phoenix-tail shrimp)
🥚 Egg, 1 piece
🫚 Ginger, 1 piece (sliced)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🧂 MSG, a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🍳 Iron wok/frying pan
🥣 Stockpot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍲 Bowl
⚡ Induction cooker

## Cooking Steps
1.  Peel off the outer leaves of the Shanghai bok choy, keeping only the tender center. Use chopsticks to poke a small hole at the root, then stuff the prepared carrot strips inside. Remove the seeds from the small chili peppers and thread them through the middle of a Chinese chive section to create a "longevity" shape. Set aside.
**Key point**: When poking the hole at the root, do it gently to avoid breaking the core. The carrot strips should be cut slightly thin to make stuffing easier.

2.  Soak the clams to remove sand, then rinse clean. Remove the heads and shells of the shrimp, keeping the tail shell intact. Butterfly the back to devein, creating a phoenix-tail shrimp shape.
**Key point**: The depth of the butterfly cut on the shrimp should be moderate to ensure they curl naturally into a ball shape when heated.

3.  Fill a stockpot with water, add the ginger slices, then put in the clams. Cook over medium heat until the clams open. Skim off any foam to create a clear seafood broth.
**Note**: The clams are done as soon as they open; do not overcook them, or the meat will become tough.

4.  In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add a little cooking oil. Put in the dried noodles and cook over high heat for about 5 minutes until no hard core remains. Remove and divide evenly into bowls. Set aside.
**Key point**: Adding oil to the noodle water prevents the noodles from sticking. Cook the noodles until about 80% done; they will have a better texture after the broth is poured over them.

5.  Bring the seafood broth to a boil again. Season with salt and MSG. First, blanch the Shanghai bok choy shapes and chive sections for about 20 seconds, then remove. Next, blanch the phoenix-tail shrimp until they change color and curl, then remove.
**Key point**: Blanch the vegetables and shrimp separately to avoid mixing flavors and to better control their doneness.

6.  Heat a little oil in a wok or frying pan and fry a sunny-side-up egg until both sides are golden brown. Remove.
**Key point**: When frying the egg, do not use too high heat. Keep the yolk runny or cook it through according to personal preference.

7.  Pour the prepared seafood broth over the noodles. Arrange the blanched shrimp, fried egg, clams, and vegetable shapes on top in order. Serve immediately.
**Key point**: Before pouring the broth, gently stir the bottom to ensure the flavor is evenly distributed. Take care to arrange the ingredients attractively on the plate.

## Health Advice
This seafood longevity noodle dish is rich in high-quality protein, calcium, and dietary fiber. It is especially suitable for elderly people needing nutritional supplementation or for fitness enthusiasts. The broth is umami and the vegetables tender. However, boiling the noodles for too long can raise blood sugar levels. It is recommended to pair them with multigrain noodles or reduce the noodle portion.
