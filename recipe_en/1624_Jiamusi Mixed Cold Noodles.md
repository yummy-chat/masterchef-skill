## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. **Make the cold noodle broth**: In a large basin, add 680g white sugar, 24g Dasida beef powder, 40g pineapple powder, and 32g salt. In a separate small bowl, dissolve 12g baking soda with 220g vinegar essence, then pour into the large basin. Then add 40g original flavor soy sauce, 45g dark soy sauce, 160g apple cider vinegar, 340g Sprite, and 5200g purified water in order. Stir until the sugar and salt are completely dissolved.
   **Key Point**: Baking soda must be dissolved in vinegar essence before adding; the order cannot be wrong.
   Add 280g sliced apple, 320g sliced pear, 280g cucumber strips, 80g green pepper sections, 100g sliced garlic, 100g sliced ginger, and 20g cilantro sections to the basin and mix well. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 24 hours. When using, remove the fruit and vegetable residue with a colander.

2. **Make the compound chili powder**: Stir-fry 75g Erjingtiao chili peppers, 75g lantern chili peppers, and 25g ghost chili peppers in a lightly oiled wok until fragrant. Let cool, then grind together into coarse chili flakes. Cut 130g new generation dried chili peppers into sections and remove the seeds. Prepare 80g extra fine coloring chili powder.
   **Key Point**: None

3. **Make the chili oil**: Pour 2000g cooking oil into a wok. Add 70g scallion, 70g sliced ginger, 20g garlic, 20g onion, 20g cilantro, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaf, 5g fennel seeds, 2g Angelica dahurica, and 4g Qian li xiang to the cold oil. Increase the heat to high to raise the oil temperature. Fry until the ingredients bubble, then reduce to medium-low heat and fry slowly until all the dregs are caramel-colored. Remove the dregs. Raise the oil temperature to 190°C for the first oil pour. Add 80g extra fine coloring chili powder. Lower the oil temperature to 160°C for the second pour. Finally, lower the oil temperature to 130°C for the third pour, and drizzle in 5g Zhenjiang balsamic vinegar to enhance aroma. Let sit and cool for 12 hours before use.
   **Key Point**: The three oil pours are at different temperatures for stimulating aroma, producing color, and fusing flavors. A total of 2000g oil is used for frying the aromatics. 1500g is used to make chili oil; the remaining 500g is for frying chili pepper sections.

4. **Make fried chili pepper sections**: Soak the new generation dried chili pepper sections in 85°C warm water for 20 minutes. Remove and drain well. Pour the remaining 500g aromatic oil into the wok. When the oil reaches 150°C, add the chili pepper sections. Fry over medium-low heat for 5-8 minutes until some are crispy, then remove. Use the same method to fry some Tianjin chili pepper sections to add spiciness if desired.
   **Key Point**: Fry until some chili peppers are crispy, then remove; residual heat will make them crispier.

5. **Make mixed dried shredded beef**: Put the spices with an appropriate amount of water, 1000g beef, 50g yellow wine, scallion, and ginger into a pot and bring to a boil. Skim off the foam, then add 5g salt, 5g dark soy sauce, and 15g light soy sauce. Simmer over low heat for 60 minutes until a chopstick can be inserted. Remove the beef and let it cool completely. Tear it into thin shreds along the grain. Spread evenly on a plate and air-dry overnight. Mix the air-dried shredded beef with the dry seasonings. Then add sesame oil, 50g high fructose corn syrup, and homemade chili oil, and mix well.
   **Key Point**: The beef must be completely cool before shredding. Air-drying overnight is a crucial step.

6. **Make red oil shredded dried tofu skin**: Cut 800g dried tofu skin into shreds. Add 160g white sugar and mix well. Marinate for 60 minutes until water is released. Discard the released liquid. Put the shredded dried tofu skin into a large basin. Add 40g minced garlic, 8g Dasida beef powder, 8g salt, 8g flavor powder, 10g white sesame seeds, 20g extra fine coloring chili powder, 60g 9% rice vinegar, 80g high fructose corn syrup, 12g Sichuan peppercorn oil, and 10g sesame oil. Mix thoroughly by hand.
   **Key Point**: The key is to marinate with sugar first to dehydrate; this makes the shredded dried tofu skin crispy and creates a unique flavor.

7. **Make mixed bean sprouts**: Blanch 1000g bean sprouts in batches for about 30 seconds. Quickly remove and cool in cold water, then drain well. Add 60g minced garlic, 5g salt, 5g chicken powder, 5g flavor powder, 10g Dasida beef powder, 15g extra fine coloring chili powder, 10g white sesame seeds, 50g 9% rice vinegar, 5g sesame oil, homemade chili oil, and 60g high fructose corn syrup. Mix well.
   **Key Point**: None

### Cooking Phase
1. **Cook the cold noodles**: Soak 2.5 kg of dried cold noodles in cold water for 30 minutes. Gently separate any clumps. Bring water to a boil. Add the soaked cold noodles. Quickly loosen them with chopsticks. Cook for **15 seconds**, then immediately remove and cool thoroughly in cold water. Drain well.
   **Key Point**: The cooking time is very short; do not overcook, or the noodles will lose their chewy texture. Must be cooled immediately.

2. **Mix the cold noodles**: Put the cooked and cooled cold noodles into a large basin. Add an appropriate amount of cold noodle broth, two spoonfuls of homemade red chili oil, and the fried chili pepper sections. Wear gloves and mix thoroughly by hand. Ensure the noodles are evenly coated with the red oil and broth.
   **Key Point**: None

3. **Assemble the bowls**: Take a large bowl. First, put in the mixed cold noodles. Then, pour in some broth. On top of the noodles, layer the red oil shredded dried tofu skin, mixed bean sprouts, and mixed dried shredded beef. Finally, sprinkle with cucumber shreds and cilantro sections for garnish.
   **Key Point**: None

## Health Advice
This cold noodle dish is suitable for summer consumption. It is rich in protein and dietary fiber, but it is also high in sugar and calories. It is recommended to consume in small amounts. Those with blood sugar sensitivity should be cautious. The beef and dried tofu skin provide high-quality protein, and the combination with fruits and vegetables contributes to a balanced nutrition.
