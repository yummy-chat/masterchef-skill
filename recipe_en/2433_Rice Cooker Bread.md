# Rice Cooker Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
### Pre-ferment  
🌾 100g bread flour  
💧 100g water  
🍞 1g dry yeast  

### Main Dough  
🌾 300g bread flour  
🥛 150g milk (about 2/3 of a carton)  
🥚 1 egg (about 50g)  
🍯 20g condensed milk (about 2 small packets)  
🍬 60g white sugar (about 2 spoons)  
🧂 4g salt (about 1 small spoon)  
🍞 4g dry yeast  
🧈 30g unsalted butter or vegetable oil (about 1.5 spoons)  

### Decoration  
🌿 Black sesame seeds, as needed  
🥚 1 egg yolk (mixed with a little water to make egg wash)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫙 Plastic wrap or plastic bag  
❄️ Refrigerator (for cold fermentation)  
♨️ Rice cooker (including inner pot)  
🖌️ Silicone brush  
🎳 Rolling pin  
🧫 Silicone kneading mat  
🔪 Dough scraper or knife  
🍶 Small bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  

## Steps  
1. **Make the pre-ferment**: In a bowl, combine 100g bread flour, 100g water, and 1g dry yeast. Stir until a smooth batter forms. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight for fermentation.
   - **Check for doneness**: The mixture should noticeably expand in volume, with large bubbles on the surface and a honeycomb structure inside, emitting a rich aroma.

2. **Mix the main dough**: Place the pre-ferment in a large bowl. Add milk, egg, condensed milk, sugar, salt, 300g bread flour, and 4g dry yeast in that order. Stir in one direction with chopsticks until a soft, wet dough forms with no dry flour.

3. **First cold rest**: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes to relax.

4. **Add fat**: Remove the dough from the refrigerator and add the butter. Use a repeated folding and pressing technique to fully incorporate the fat into the dough, ensuring complete integration and promoting gluten development.

5. **Second cold rest**: Cover again with plastic wrap and refrigerate for another 20 minutes.

6. **Check gluten**: Lightly oil your hands and take a small piece of dough. Gently stretch it. If you can pull a smooth, thin membrane with rounded edges when it tears, the gluten is developed. For stronger gluten, repeat steps 4 and 5.

7. **Divide and shape**: Lightly dust the kneading mat with a little bread flour to prevent sticking. Shape the dough into a long log and use a scraper to divide it into 8-10 small pieces. Take one piece, flatten it to release gas, and shape as desired—either by rolling it out and rounding it with your palm, or rolling it into a log.
   - **Note**: The dough has high hydration and is soft; avoid directly pulling it with your hands. Use a light dusting of flour to prevent sticking.

8. **Place in mold and final fermentation**: Lightly brush the bottom and sides of the rice cooker inner pot with oil. Arrange the shaped dough pieces inside. Cover with the lid and let it ferment at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until the dough doubles in size.

9. **Decorate and bake**: Mix the egg yolk with a little water to make an egg wash. Brush it onto the surface of the fermented dough and sprinkle with black sesame seeds. Press the "Cook" function on the rice cooker and bake for about 45 minutes.
   - **Key point**: You will smell the bread aroma during baking. Open the lid to check once the time is up.

10. **Remove from mold**: Take out the inner pot and invert the bread onto a plate to release it. Let it cool to warm before serving. The finished bread is fluffy and soft, with a rich milky aroma.

## Health Tip  
This bread is nutritionally balanced but relatively high in sugar and fat. It works well as a weekend treat. For weight management, consider reducing the sugar or eating it in portions. Ensure the gluten is kneaded to the windowpane stage, or the bread may collapse easily.
