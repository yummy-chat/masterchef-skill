# Dry-Fried Lotus Root Strips
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🪷 Lotus root approx. 600g (with skin, choose shorter, thicker sections)
🥚 Egg 1 (use only the yolk)
🌽 Corn starch approx. 80-100g (for coating)
🌶️ Dried chili pepper strips approx. 13g
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns approx. 3g
🧄 Garlic approx. 10g (about 2 cloves, minced)
🫚 Ginger approx. 5g (julienned, optional)
🌿 Cilantro stems approx. 15g (cut into small sections)
🤍 White sesame seeds approx. 20g
🧂 Salt 2g (for marinating) + approx. 1.5 tsp (for stir-frying)
🍬 Granulated sugar 3g (for marinating) + approx. 1.5 tsp (for stir-frying)
🍲 White pepper powder approx. 0.3g
🍲 Thirteen-spice or five-spice powder approx. 1 tsp
🍲 MSG approx. 1 tsp
🫒 Sesame oil to taste (drizzle around the edge before removing from heat)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Vegetable peeler, chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl or basin (for washing and marinating lotus root strips)
🕳️ Fine-mesh strainer (for filtering frying oil)
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the lotus root**: Peel the lotus root. First cut it lengthwise into slightly thick slices, then cut along the grain into strips about the thickness of chopsticks.
   - **Key point**: Cutting along the grain prevents grit from the lotus root holes from scattering and keeps the strips intact.
2. **Blanch and wash**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the lotus root strips and blanch over high heat for 20 seconds. Immediately remove and rinse twice under cold water to wash away any dark sediment. Drain well.
   - **Key point**: Blanch briefly to maintain a crisp texture.
3. **Marinate and coat**: Add 1 egg yolk, a pinch of white pepper powder, 2g salt, and 3g sugar to the lotus root strips. Gently toss to combine. Add the corn starch in batches, tossing well after each addition until no dry powder remains. Continue until the surface of the strips is dry and non-sticky, and the strips fall apart individually when picked up.
   - **Key point**: Use only egg yolk; add starch gradually in small batches; final texture should be dry with a thin, firm coating.
4. **Prepare aromatics**: Julienne the ginger, mince the garlic, cut the dried chili peppers into strips, cut the cilantro stems into sections, and set aside the Sichuan peppercorns and white sesame seeds.
5. **First fry**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat over high heat until it reaches 70% hot (approx. 180°C). Scatter the lotus root strips into the oil. Let them sit for about 10 seconds to set the coating, then gently separate them with chopsticks. Keep the heat on maximum and fry for 3-4 minutes, until the strips float and the surface becomes firm.
   - **Key point**: Oil must be hot; let the coating set before stirring; keep the heat on maximum throughout.
6. **Second fry**: Remove the lotus root strips. Maintain maximum heat and allow the oil to return to 70% hot. Add the strips back and fry for 20-30 seconds, until the surface is slightly golden and sounds crispy.
   - **Key point**: Second fry should be brief to make the outer layer extra crunchy.
7. **Fry the aromatics**: Pour out the frying oil and filter it. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Turn off the heat, then add the Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili strips, julienned ginger, and minced garlic. Use the residual heat to release their fragrance. Still with the heat off, add 1.5 tsp sugar, 1 tsp thirteen-spice or five-spice powder, 1 tsp MSG, and 1.5 tsp salt. Turn the heat to medium and cook until the seasonings dissolve. Then reduce to the lowest heat.
   - **Key point**: Spices and seasonings must be added with the heat off; use residual heat to release aroma and prevent burning.
8. **Combine and serve**: With the heat on the lowest setting, add the fried lotus root strips, white sesame seeds, and cilantro stems. Stir-fry quickly to combine. Drizzle a circle of sesame oil around the edge of the wok, toss a few times, then turn off the heat and serve.
   - **Key point**: Stir-fry quickly over low heat to maintain crispness; add sesame oil at the end for aroma.

## Health Suggestion
Lotus root is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C. After deep-frying, the calorie content is relatively high. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables or cold dishes to balance nutrition. Suitable for those who enjoy crispy textures, but those with weak digestive function should consume in moderation.
