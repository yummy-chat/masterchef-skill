# Stir-Fried Cabbage with Vermicelli 🥬🍠
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings 🥬 🍠 🥩 🫚 🧄 🧅 🌶️ 🫘
🥬 Cabbage (known as "大头菜" in Northeast China)
🍠 Vermicelli (sweet potato vermicelli, dried)
🥩 Pork (pork belly or fatty-lean meat, thinly sliced)
🫚 Ginger (sliced)
🧄 Garlic (minced, used in two batches)
🧅 Scallion (chopped)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (optional)
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns (optional)

🫒 Cooking oil
🧂 Salt
🥄 Light soy sauce
🥄 Dark soy sauce
🍾 Chinkiang vinegar

## Cookware and Equipment 🍳 🔪 🪓 ✂️ 🚰 🥄
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🚰 Water basin
🥄 Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the cabbage**: Peel off the outermost old leaves, cut off the yellow hard core at the root, remove any pale yellow tough stems from the leaves, and shred the leaves crosswise into thick strips.
*Key point*: All yellow, woody parts must be removed to avoid bitterness.

2. **Prepare the vermicelli**: Soak in cold water for at least 3 hours until soft. Use scissors to cut into manageable lengths in a water basin, then drain and set aside.
*Key point*: Fully rehydrate, otherwise it will remain undercooked and absorb seasoning unevenly when stir-fried.

3. **Prepare the aromatics**: Thinly slice the pork; slice the ginger, mince half the garlic, chop the scallion; cut dried chili peppers into sections, and prepare the Sichuan peppercorns.
*Key point*: Slice the meat as thin as possible for easy rendering of fat and a tender texture; the minced garlic is used in two batches.

4. **Fry the Sichuan pepper oil**: Pour oil into the wok, add Sichuan peppercorns over low heat, and fry slowly until they turn dark brown and fragrant. Remove and discard the peppercorns.
*Key point*: Use low heat to prevent burning; omit if you dislike the numbing flavor.

5. **Stir-fry the pork**: Leave the base oil in the wok, add the pork slices and ginger slices. Stir-fry over high heat until they change color and release some oil, then turn to low heat and fry slowly until the edges are golden and a significant amount of fat has rendered.
*Key point*: Fully rendering the pork fat removes any gamey smell, enhances flavor, and creates a natural savory coating for the ingredients.

6. **Bloom the aromatics**: Add the chopped scallion, most of the minced garlic, and the dried chili pepper segments. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until fragrant.
*Key point*: None.

7. **Stir-fry the cabbage**: Add all the shredded cabbage. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat until it softens, turns bright green, and the edges become slightly translucent.
*Key point*: Use high heat throughout to prevent the cabbage from releasing too much water. Adjust the amount of cabbage added at one time based on your stove's heat output.

8. **Add vermicelli and season**: Add the vermicelli, immediately turn off the heat. Add salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Finally, drizzle in the Chinkiang vinegar.
*Key point*: Season with the heat off to prevent the cabbage from overcooking and releasing water. Pour the soy sauces over the vermicelli for even coloring.

9. **Toss and finish**: Turn the heat back to high. Quickly toss everything together for about 30-60 seconds until the vermicelli is a translucent, saucy brown. Sprinkle in the remaining minced garlic, toss to combine, and serve immediately.
*Key point*: The vermicelli is already cooked. Do not stir-fry for too long to avoid sticking or becoming mushy and losing its springy texture.

## Health Tips
Cabbage is rich in vitamins and dietary fiber, while vermicelli provides carbohydrates. This combination is balanced and suitable for moderate consumption during a weight loss phase. Pork has a relatively high fat content; the rendered oil should be consumed in moderation. Individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt and soy sauce used.
