# Quick Single-Portion Chicken Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thigh 1 (boneless)
🍚 Cooked rice 1 small bowl (about 150g)
🥬 Cabbage about 50g (shredded)
🥚 Egg 1
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste
🥢 Cornstarch 1 tablespoon (about 5g)
🍬 Sugar to taste
🫒 Cooking oil to taste
🍯 Black pepper sauce to taste
🧂 Black pepper powder to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan (non-stick)
🍚 Rice cooker (for cooking rice)
🥘 Glass lid
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🔗 Tongs
🥣 Bowl
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Steps
1. Debone the chicken thigh, place it in a bowl, add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, cornstarch, sugar, and a small amount of cooking oil. Rub evenly with your hands and marinate for 10 minutes.
2. Heat the pan over medium heat, add oil, place the marinated chicken skin-side down. Cook over medium-low heat until one side is golden, then flip and continue until both sides are golden.
   **Key point**: If black charred bits appear at the edges of the pan during cooking, wipe them clean promptly with kitchen paper towels to avoid a burnt flavor.
3. Use kitchen scissors to directly cut the chicken into small pieces in the pan. Cover with the lid and steam over medium-low heat using the steam inside to cook the chicken through initially.
4. Next to the chicken in the pan, add a bowl of cooked rice, place the shredded cabbage, and crack an egg.
5. Drizzle an appropriate amount of black pepper sauce over the rice and vegetables, sprinkle with black pepper powder, cover the lid, and turn to medium-low heat to continue steaming.
   **Key point**: Steam until the egg reaches the desired doneness, then turn off the heat.
6. Before serving, mix all ingredients in the pan well. Can be enjoyed directly from the pan.

## Health Advice
This chicken rice bowl has a balanced ratio of protein and carbohydrates. The cabbage provides dietary fiber to aid digestion, making it suitable for busy singles or students. When marinating the chicken, reduce the amount of oyster sauce and sugar to avoid excess sodium and sugar. It is recommended to use a runny yolk egg to enhance the texture.
