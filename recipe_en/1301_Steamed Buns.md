# Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour, 500g
🫧 Active dry yeast, 4g
🧂 Aluminum-free baking powder, 4g
🍬 White sugar, 2g
🧈 Lard, 15g
💧 Warm water, about 250g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥛 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Steamer basket
🩹 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. **Prepare yeast water**: Add yeast powder and white sugar to warm water, stir until completely dissolved to activate the yeast.
2. **Mix dry ingredients**: In a bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and lard evenly.
3. **Knead the dough**: Gradually pour in the yeast water, stirring continuously to form flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth dough.
4. **First fermentation**: Place the dough on a work surface and knead vigorously until the surface is smooth. Return to the bowl, seal with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place until it doubles in volume. The interior should show a uniform honeycomb structure when torn open.
   - Fermentation time depends on the amount of yeast, water temperature, and ambient temperature.
5. **Degas and re-knead**: Transfer the fermented dough to a work surface dusted with dry flour. Knead vigorously to expel the gas.
   - **Key point**: Thorough degassing results in buns with a smooth surface and fine pores, and it also prepares the yeast for secondary fermentation by exposing it to oxygen. Skipping this step can lead to buns with a rough surface and a dense texture.
6. **Divide and rest**: Roll the dough into a long log, then divide it into equal-sized pieces. Cover with plastic wrap to prevent the surface from drying out.
7. **Roll wrappers and shape**: Flatten a piece of dough and roll it into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Fill with meat filling, pleat the edges to seal, and pinch off any excess dough at the top.
8. **Second proofing**: Arrange the filled buns in a steamer basket lined with a cloth. Cover and let them proof in a warm place until they visibly increase in size and slowly spring back when gently pressed.
   - The second proofing allows the yeast to produce gas again, ensuring the finished buns are fluffy and soft.
9. **Steam**: Place the steamer basket over a pot of boiling water. Steam over high heat, and once steam escapes, cook for 12 minutes.
   - Use high heat throughout to generate sufficient steam, ensuring the buns become light and fluffy.

## Health Tip
Adding lard to the dough makes the wrappers whiter and softer. After thorough fermentation, the finished buns are as fluffy as clouds, suitable for the whole family. Ensure the second proofing is sufficient; steam only when the buns spring back slowly when gently pressed.
