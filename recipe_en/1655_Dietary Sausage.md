# Dietary Sausage
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 700g pork front leg meat (trimmed of sinew, cut into small pieces)
🍗 400g chicken breast (cut into small pieces)
🌽 Sweet corn, appropriate amount (diced)
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (diced)
🍄 Wood ear mushroom, appropriate amount (diced)
🍄 Shiitake mushroom, appropriate amount (diced, fresh shiitake recommended)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🫧 Pork casings, appropriate amount (for stuffing)
🧂 10g table salt
🧂 6-8g baking soda
🍋 A small amount of lemon juice
🧂 5g black pepper powder
🍬 50g white sugar
🦪 3 tablespoons oyster sauce
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount
🧊 200g ice cubes (added in two batches)
🫒 30-50ml cooking oil

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Meat grinder (for grinding meat paste)
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing filling)
🪓 Cutting board and kitchen knife (for cutting meat and vegetables)
🍳 Non-stick pan (for blanching vegetables, boiling and pan-frying sausages)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing vegetables)
💉 Sausage stuffer (for stuffing sausages)
🧵 Cotton kitchen twine (for tying sausage links)
🪥 Toothpicks or bamboo skewers (for poking holes to release air)
🥢 Chopsticks (for turning sausages while boiling)
🔗 Tongs (for flipping while pan-frying)
♨️ Air fryer (optional, for air-frying the final product)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the meat**: Clean the pork of all sinew, then cut both the pork and chicken breast into small pieces.
   **Key point**: Sinew must be completely removed, otherwise it will affect the texture.
2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Dice the sweet corn, carrot, wood ear mushroom, and shiitake mushroom. Finely chop the scallion. Set aside separately.
3. **Blanch the vegetables**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a little salt. Blanch all vegetable dices except the scallion for 5-6 minutes until cooked through. Remove and immediately rinse under cold water, then thoroughly drain all moisture.
   **Key point**: Ensure the vegetables are thoroughly drained to prevent the filling from becoming too watery.
4. **Grind the meat paste**: Put the pork and chicken pieces in batches into the meat grinder. Add all 10g of salt and 6-8g of baking soda. Grind on high speed for 2-3 minutes, until the meat paste is very sticky and forms strands.
   **Texture check**: The meat paste should show obvious stringiness and strong stickiness.
5. **Season the meat filling**: Transfer the ground meat paste to a large bowl. Add a small amount of lemon juice, 5g black pepper powder, 50g white sugar, 3 tablespoons oyster sauce, an appropriate amount of MSG, and 100g of ice cubes. Stir vigorously in one direction until the ice cubes are completely melted and the filling is firm and springy.
   **Key point**: Stir in one direction only throughout the entire process.
6. **Combine the filling**: Add the remaining 100g of ice cubes and continue stirring until melted. Then add the chopped scallion, 30-50ml cooking oil, and the drained vegetable dices. Mix thoroughly with your hands until all ingredients are well combined.
   **Key point**: Do not substitute ice cubes with water or starch.
7. **Stuff the sausages**: Use the sausage stuffer to fill the pork casings with the prepared filling. Once stuffed, tie the sausage into links using cotton twine. Use a toothpick to poke several small holes on the surface of each sausage link to release trapped air.
   **Key point**: Do not overfill the casings; leave some room at the ends. Be careful with the tying technique to prevent the casing from breaking.
8. **Boil the sausages**: Add enough cold water to a pot and place the sausages in it. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, allowing the water temperature to rise slowly, keeping it below a boil at all times. Continuously turn the sausages with chopsticks to prevent them from sticking to the bottom. Boil for 30-40 minutes.
   **Texture check**: The sausages are done when they all float to the surface and feel very springy to the touch.
   **Key point**: Do not use high heat or allow the water to boil, as this will easily cause the casings to burst.
9. **Pan-fry or air-fry for serving**: Drain the boiled sausages and pat dry. Make a few shallow cuts on the surface. Pan-fry in a little oil over medium heat until golden brown on both sides. Alternatively, air-fry for about 10 minutes until the surface is golden and crispy.

## Health Tips
This dietary sausage uses a combination of chicken and pork with vegetables, providing rich protein with moderate fat content. It is suitable for fitness or weight loss periods as a quality protein source. Note that homemade sausages must be fully cooked and can be refrigerated for no more than 3 days.
