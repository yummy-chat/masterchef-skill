# Colorful Liangpi (Cold Noodles)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 250g dragon fruit juice
🥕 Appropriate amount of carrot juice (equal to the dragon fruit juice)
🥬 Appropriate amount of spinach juice (equal to the dragon fruit juice)
🌾 500g all-purpose flour (divided into four portions, one for each color)
🧂 Appropriate amount of edible alkali
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Stockpot (with lid)
🥄 Strainer
🖌️ Silicone brush
🍕 Flat round container (such as a pizza pan)
🔪 Scraper or knife

## Preparation Steps
1. Divide the all-purpose flour into four equal portions. Add dragon fruit juice, carrot juice, spinach juice, and water to each portion respectively. Knead into dough of medium firmness. Knead until the surface is smooth, then cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 20 minutes.
2. Take each colored dough and repeatedly knead and wash it in clean water until the gluten separates and the water becomes a thick, starchy paste.
3. Combine all the gluten portions together, add an appropriate amount of edible alkali, and mix evenly. Set aside.
4. Let all the starchy water portions settle separately for at least 4 hours to allow the starch to fully sink to the bottom.
5. Carefully pour off the clear water on top of each settled portion, retaining only the starch block at the bottom.
6. Add a small amount of clean water to the starch block and stir into a uniform slurry. Strain the slurry through a strainer into another container.
   **Key point**: Straining removes particles, giving the liangpi a smoother texture.
7. Add an appropriate amount of salt to the strained starch slurry and stir evenly to create four differently colored liangpi slurries.
8. Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the inner wall and bottom of the flat round container.
9. Ladle one scoop of the starch slurry into the container. Gently shake the container so the slurry evenly covers the bottom to a consistent thickness.
   **Key point**: The thickness of the slurry directly affects the texture and doneness of the liangpi. It is recommended to control it between 2–3 mm.
10. Bring water in a pot to a boil. Gently place the container with the starch slurry onto the boiling water so it floats. Gently rotate the container to ensure even heating.
11. Cover the pot and steam over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the surface of the liangpi is fully set, the color becomes translucent, and large bubbles form on the surface – indicating it is done.
    **Note**: Adjust the steaming time according to the actual thickness to ensure it is thoroughly cooked.
12. Remove the container and immediately submerge it in cold water to cool, causing the liangpi to shrink for easy unmolding.
13. Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the surface of the cooled liangpi. Carefully peel the whole sheet of liangpi from the edge and lay it flat.
14. Repeat steps 8 to 13 to steam and make liangpi from all the colored starch slurries.
15. Stack the finished colorful liangpi neatly. Cut into wide strips with a knife. Place in a bowl, add seasonings according to personal preference, and mix well before serving.

## Health Advice
Colorful liangpi is rich in anthocyanins and vitamins from the fruit and vegetable juices. Its main component is carbohydrates, making it suitable as a light main dish or a summer appetizer. Dragon fruit juice can easily cause staining; it is recommended to clean your mouth promptly after eating.
