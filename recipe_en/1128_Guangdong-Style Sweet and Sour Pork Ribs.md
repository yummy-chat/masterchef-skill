# Guangdong-Style Sweet and Sour Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Pork spare ribs or front shoulder (cut into 25-30g pieces, preferably with cartilage)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🌽 Cornstarch approx. 25g (for coating) + extra (for dry coating and thickening)  
🎋 Winter bamboo shoots (cut into comb-shaped slices, blanched to remove bitterness)  
🫑 Green and red bell peppers (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Onion (cut into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic (minced)  
🧅 Scallions (cut into sections)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (used in stages)  
🍬 White sugar a pinch (for marinating) + approx. 60g (for sweet and sour sauce)  
🌶️ White pepper a pinch  
🍶 Rice wine to taste  
🍶 White vinegar approx. 100g  
💧 Water approx. 25g  
🥃 Worcestershire sauce approx. 25g  
🥫 Ketchup (tomato sauce) approx. 25g  
🛢️ Cooking oil as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🔥 Portable stove  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the ribs into 25-30g pieces, blanch in boiling water until cooked through, then drain.
**Key Point**: Choose front ribs or shoulder with cartilage for more tender meat.

2. Add a pinch of salt, sugar, white pepper, and rice wine to the ribs, mix well, and marinate until flavorful.
3. Crack in 1 egg and stir thoroughly until the ribs are evenly coated with egg yolk mixture.
4. Add approx. 25g of cornstarch and stir into a thick egg yolk batter, evenly coating the ribs.
5. Evenly coat the battered ribs with a layer of dry cornstarch, then sprinkle a little water by hand to moisten the surface and remove any dry powder.
**Key Point**: Sprinkling water prevents the coating from falling off during frying, keeping the shell intact.

6. Prepare the sweet and sour sauce: In a bowl, combine white vinegar, approx. 25g of water, approx. 60g of sugar, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and a pinch of salt, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Set aside.
**Note**: This ratio is a reference for "1 catty (500g) of ribs"; adjust proportionally based on the actual weight of the ribs.

7. Blanch the winter bamboo shoot slices to remove bitterness; cut bell peppers and onion into pieces; cut scallions into sections; mince the garlic.
8. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to approx. 100°C. Test with a small piece of scallion; when it slowly floats up, reduce to medium heat.
**Key Point**: When adding the ribs, lower your hand and slide them along the side of the wok to avoid hot oil splashing.

9. Add all the ribs and deep-fry over medium heat for at least 5 minutes until cooked through and the shell is set.
10. Remove the ribs and increase the oil temperature to approx. 160°C.
11. Re-fry the ribs for about 1 minute until the shell is golden and crispy. Remove and drain, then spread them out to cool for 1 minute, allowing residual heat to make the skin crispier.
**Key Point**: Use residual heat to make the skin crispier.

12. In another wok, heat a little oil, stir-fry the winter bamboo shoots, bell peppers, and onion, adding a pinch of salt until just cooked. Remove and set aside.
13. In the same wok, sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, then pour in the prepared sweet and sour sauce. Heat over low heat until the sugar dissolves and the sauce slightly simmers.
14. Drizzle in a little cornstarch slurry to thicken, stirring continuously, until the sauce reaches a pourable, thick consistency.
**Key Point**: The slurry should be on the thinner side rather than too thick.

15. Quickly add the fried ribs, stir-fried vegetables, and scallion sections. Stir-fry over high heat rapidly until all ingredients are evenly coated with the glossy red sauce. Immediately remove from heat and plate when there is no excess sauce left in the wok.

## Health Advice  
Pork ribs are rich in protein and calcium, but the sweet and sour sauce contains a high amount of sugar. It is recommended to control portion sizes and pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Since the ribs are double-fried, they are high in calories, so reduce the portion during weight loss.
