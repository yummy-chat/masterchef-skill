# Tiramisu
Course: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
- 🧀 Mascarpone cheese 450g
- 🍪 Ladyfingers 1 pack
- ☕ Cooled espresso 350ml
- 🥚 Pasteurized eggs 4
- 🍬 White sugar 50g
- 🍫 Cocoa powder 10g
- 🥃 Rum 35ml (optional)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
- 🥣 Container (e.g., glass Lock&Lock)
- 🌀 Electric mixer (or manual whisk)

## Steps
1. Separate the egg whites and yolks of the pasteurized eggs, placing them in two separate oil-free and water-free containers.  
   **Key point**: Using pasteurized eggs ensures food safety for raw consumption.
2. Add 10g white sugar to the egg whites and beat with an electric mixer until soft peaks form.  
   **Key point**: The container for whipping egg whites must be free of oil and water; otherwise, they won’t whip properly.
3. Gradually add 40g white sugar to the egg yolks in three batches, whisking until smooth, evenly combined, and lighter in color.
4. Add the mascarpone cheese to the egg yolk mixture in three batches, folding or whisking until fully incorporated and smooth into a creamy cheese mixture.
5. Add rum and stir until well combined. The amount of rum can be adjusted to taste or omitted.
6. Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the cheese mixture in three batches, using a spatula to cut and fold lightly to avoid deflating.
7. Pour the cooled espresso into a shallow dish. Dip each ladyfinger quickly on both sides into the coffee, then immediately remove. Lay them flat in a single layer at the bottom of the container.
8. Evenly pour a layer of cheese mixture over the ladyfingers, covering them completely. Add another layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers, then another layer of cheese mixture. Repeat alternately until all ingredients are used.  
   **Key point**: The final layer should be cheese mixture, with the surface smoothed flat.
9. Place the container in the refrigerator to set for at least 4 hours, allowing the texture to firm and flavors to meld.  
   **Key point**: The chilling time must not be too short; otherwise, it cannot be cut into clean slices.
10. Remove from the refrigerator, evenly sift and sprinkle cocoa powder over the surface, then serve.

## Health Advice
Tiramisu contains raw egg yolks and a large amount of cheese, making it relatively high in calories. It is suitable as an occasional dessert and should not be consumed in excess. Using pasteurized eggs reduces the risk of salmonella, but pregnant women and individuals with weakened immune systems should exercise caution.
