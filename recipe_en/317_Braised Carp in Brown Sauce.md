# Braised Carp in Brown Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Carp 1 (about 2 jin / 1 kg)
🥩 Pork belly 100g
🧅 Scallions 200g
🫚 Ginger 80g
🧄 Garlic cloves 3-4
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 2
🫒 Oil as needed
🧂 Salt 5g
🍬 White sugar 50g
🍶 Cooking wine 50ml
🧂 Light soy sauce (premium) 50ml
🫘 Dark soy sauce 20ml
🫗 Aged vinegar 50ml
🦪 Oyster sauce 5ml

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Knife
🪵 Cutting board

## Steps
1. Wash the scallions, ginger, garlic cloves, and dried chili peppers separately.
2. Cut the white part of the scallions into sections about 4 cm long, then split each section lengthwise into four strips.
3. Slice the ginger into pieces about 3 mm thick.
4. Crush one garlic clove and mince it; cut the remaining garlic cloves in half.
5. Cut the dried chili peppers into four segments.
6. Slice the pork belly into pieces about 4 cm square.
7. Clean the fish, which should already be scaled and gutted.
8. Make several diagonal cuts on the thick part of the fish's back to allow flavors to penetrate.
9. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 70% hot (210°C). Fry the carp for about 1 minute until the skin is slightly firm. Remove and drain the oil. Pour out the oil from the wok, leaving a small amount as a base.
   **Key point**: Do not turn the fish immediately after placing it in the oil to avoid breaking the skin; wait until the surface is set before flipping.
10. Heat the remaining oil in the wok, add the pork belly slices, and stir-fry until fragrant.
11. Add the dried chili segments, scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic cloves. Stir-fry for about 1 minute.
12. Place the fried carp into the wok.
13. Pour in the cooking wine, aged vinegar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce along the edge of the wok. Add the salt and white sugar, then pour in enough water to submerge the fish.
14. Turn the heat to medium and bring the liquid to a boil.
15. Reduce the heat to low, cover the wok with a lid, and braise slowly to allow the flavors to infuse.
16. After about 15 minutes of braising, remove the lid and discard the scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried chili pepper residues from the liquid.
17. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the liquid has reduced to about one-fourth of its original volume, sprinkle in the minced garlic, turn off the heat, and plate the dish.

## Health Suggestions
This braised carp dish is rich in protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, suitable for individuals needing brain nourishment or recovery. However, the addition of pork belly increases calories, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balance. Although the sweet and sour flavor from the frying and braising process is appetizing, those with diabetes should use less sugar or substitute with a sweetener.
