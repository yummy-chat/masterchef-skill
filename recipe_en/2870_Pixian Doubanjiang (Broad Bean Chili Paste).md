# Pixian Doubanjiang (Broad Bean Chili Paste)
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌱 Broad beans (for making koji)  
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili peppers (fresh, for making chili mash)  
🧂 Sichuan salt (mixed with chili for pickling; use enough to coat and preserve thoroughly)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥄 Wooden pestle (stirring rod, used for turning and sun-drying the paste)  
🏺 Earthenware jar (paste jar, used for fermentation and sunning/dewing)  
🧺 Bamboo basket (used for spreading and airing the koji broad beans)  
🛒 Handcart (for transporting ingredients)  
🥣 Stainless steel basin, 🔪 kitchen knife, 🪵 cutting board (for processing chilies)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Making broad bean koji**: Carbon-mix and dust the broad beans with flour, then air them to develop koji. Strictly control timing, temperature, and humidity. Use arm dexterity to mimic the natural motion of falling rain.  
**Key point**: The koji-making environment must be stable. Mimicking rainfall ensures even flour coating and prevents clumping.  

2. **Making chili mash**: Chop fresh Erjingtiao chilies, mix with Sichuan salt, place in a basin, and expose to sunlight while stirring repeatedly. Continue for about three months until the chilies are fully sun-ripened into a mash.  
**Note**: Stir daily during sun exposure to prevent the surface from hardening and the bottom from molding, ensuring even fermentation.  

3. **Mixing and loading into jars**: Combine the prepared broad bean paste with the chili mash and load into earthenware jars.  
**Key point**: Follow the traditional ratio, typically 1:1 broad bean paste to chili mash. Press down the surface firmly after loading.  

4. **Natural fermentation**: After mixing, undergo a natural fermentation period of 1 to 3 years, relying on sun exposure, night dew, and environmental microorganisms.  
**Key point**: The longer the fermentation, the richer the flavor. Do not interrupt the sunning and dewing process.  

5. **Daily turning and sunning**: Every morning, insert the wooden pestle from the edge of the jar down to the bottom, lift and stir to evenly mix the sun-dried top layer with the untouched bottom. The turning motion requires coordination of waist and arms, using dexterity to develop muscle memory. The goal of turning is uniformity; sunning provides temperature to promote fermentation; dewing allows microorganisms in the humid night air to participate in the transformation, forming a multi-chain reaction.  
**Key point**: Turning and sunning must be done daily, with technique starting light and gradually increasing in intensity. Avoid damaging the paste structure, ensuring even exposure to sun and dew from top to bottom.  

6. **Persist day after day, year after year**, until the paste turns reddish-brown or deep brownish-black, with a rich aroma. The product is then finished.  
**Key point**: Judge doneness by color and aroma: reddish-brown or deep brownish-black with a rich, non-sour aroma indicates a qualified product.  

## Health Advice  
Pixian Doubanjiang is rich in probiotics and amino acids produced during fermentation, which can stimulate appetite and aid digestion, making it suitable for adding a small amount of flavor to cooking. However, due to its high salt content, those with high blood pressure or on a sodium-restricted diet should use it with caution.
