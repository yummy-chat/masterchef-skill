# Stir-Fried Pork Liver with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫁 Pork liver – appropriate amount (choose fresh, elastic, light red)  
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (cut into thick strips)  
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (sliced or minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper – appropriate amount (sliced, for color)  
🧂 Salt – a pinch (used twice: to marinate the liver and to stir-fry scallion)  
🧪 Baking soda – a pinch (to tenderize the liver)  
🍶 Ginger juice and wine – appropriate amount (to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🥄 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount (for marinating and the sauce mixture)  
🌽 Cornstarch – appropriate amount (for coating and thickening)  
🦪 Oyster sauce – appropriate amount (for the sauce mixture)  
🍬 Sugar – a pinch (to enhance flavor)  
🌫️ White pepper – a pinch (to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🍾 Yellow wine – appropriate amount (for the sauce mixture)  
🍶 Rice wine / baijiu – appropriate amount (drizzle before serving to enhance aroma)  
🫒 Vegetable oil – appropriate amount (for greasing the pan, sautéing, and stir-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating liver and mixing sauce)  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  
🧂 Seasoning dish  

## Cooking Steps  
### Preparation Stage  
1. **Select the liver**: Choose fresh pork liver with a glossy surface, good elasticity, and light red color.  
2. **Slice**: Cut against the grain into slices about 0.5 cm thick. Too thin, and it becomes tough; too thick, and it won’t cook through.  
3. **Tenderize**: Add a pinch of salt and baking soda, mix well, and let sit briefly. Baking soda breaks down fibers, making the texture more tender.  
4. **Rinse to remove blood**: Rinse and squeeze repeatedly in plenty of water, soak, and change the water 1–2 times until the water runs clear and the liver turns pale. This removes any fishy odor.  
5. **Marinate and coat**: Drain well. First, add ginger juice and wine and light soy sauce, mix to season. Then add cornstarch and mix until a thin coating forms, sealing in moisture.  
6. **Prepare the sauce mixture**: In a bowl, combine cornstarch, sugar, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, white pepper, and yellow wine. Stir well and set aside. Using yellow wine instead of water for the sauce enhances the aroma.  
### Cooking Stage  
1. **Stir-fry scallion**: Heat oil in the wok, add scallion sections with a pinch of salt. Stir-fry over high heat until lightly charred and fragrant. Remove and set aside. Stir-frying scallion separately ensures a crispy exterior and tender interior.  
2. **Sauté ginger and garlic**: In a clean wok, heat oil over medium heat. Fry ginger strips until they shrink, then add garlic and sauté until lightly golden.  
3. **Blanch the liver**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Add the coated liver slices one by one without stirring. Poach for about 30 seconds until 70–80% cooked and the surface sets. Immediately remove. This sets the shape and removes impurities, keeping the inside tender.  
4. **Stir-fry the liver over high heat**: Heat the wok with oil over high heat. Add the blanched liver and spread out quickly, stir-frying to create seared edges, producing the Maillard reaction and *wok hei* (breath of the wok).  
5. **Splash with wine**: Drizzle rice wine or baijiu along the edge of the wok. The high heat vaporizes the alcohol, removing any fishy taste and leaving a rich aroma.  
6. **Combine and thicken**: Return the scallion to the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce mixture. Stir-fry quickly and evenly until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients. Work fast to avoid overcooking the liver.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in iron and vitamin A, making it suitable for blood nourishment and eye health. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Pork liver must be fully cooked to eliminate the risk of parasites.
