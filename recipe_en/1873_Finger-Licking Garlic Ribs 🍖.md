# Finger-Licking Garlic Ribs 🍖
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork spare ribs 2 jin (whole or cut into pieces)
🧄 Garlic 100g (divided into two parts: minced for frying and pureed for marinating)
🧅 Scallions a small amount
🫚 Ginger a small amount
🥚 Egg 1 piece
🌾 All-purpose flour appropriate amount (mixed half and half with cornstarch)
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount (mixed half and half with all-purpose flour)
🥕 Carrot a small amount (optional, improves frying color)
🧂 Salt a small amount
🍬 Sugar a small amount
🌶️ White pepper a small amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Strainer, paper towels
🌀 Food processor
🪣 Large basin or pot
🍲 Cast iron pot or deep pot (for frying)
🥢 Chopsticks
🌡️ Oil thermometer (optional)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Baking sheet/plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the pork spare ribs in clean water to fully draw out blood, then remove and squeeze dry.
   **Key Point**: Soaking removes blood impurities; squeezing dry helps the marinade adhere.

2. Take part of the garlic and cut into fine mince. Rinse with clean water to remove stickiness, then thoroughly pat dry with paper towels. Set aside for frying.

3. Place the remaining garlic, ginger, and scallion segments into a food processor. Add a small amount of clean water and blend into a puree to create a garlic marinade juice.

4. Place the squeezed-dry ribs into a basin. Add salt, sugar, white pepper, the egg, and the prepared garlic puree. Use your hands to mix thoroughly and massage for at least 1 minute. Marinate for 1 hour.
   **Key Point**: Thorough massage allows the garlic juice to penetrate the meat, enhancing flavor.

5. After marinating, add all-purpose flour and cornstarch to the ribs. Toss and mix until no dry spots remain, forming a sticky batter coating.

6. Pour enough oil into a pot and heat to 160–180°C. Maintain medium-low heat. Add the ribs and deep-fry until fully cooked. Whole ribs take about 12 minutes; small pieces take about 5–6 minutes.
   **Note**: Slow frying over medium-low heat ensures the inside is thoroughly cooked and prevents the outer layer from browning too quickly.

7. In a separate pot or by raising the oil temperature to 190–200°C, remove the cooked ribs and fry again for about 15 seconds until the surface is golden and crispy.
   **Note**: High-temperature short-time frying forces out excess oil and creates a crispy shell.

8. Remove the ribs and drain excess oil. Sprinkle with the pre-fried minced garlic. Serve on a plate.
   **Tip**: Fry the minced garlic slowly over low heat until light yellow to avoid burning and bitterness.

## Health Advice
This garlic rib dish is rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for muscle building and replenishing energy. However, due to deep-frying, it is high in calories. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should control their intake. It is recommended to pair with a light vegetable salad to balance the meal, and limit consumption to no more than 3–4 pieces per serving.
