# Crispy Pan-Fried Zongzi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍙 Savory meat zongzi, as needed
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, about 150g (4 spoons)
🥚 Egg whites, about 150g (equal amount to glutinous rice flour)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for coating the knife, moistening the batter, and frying)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🫖 Hot water container (for heating the knife)
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1.  Immerse the chef's knife in hot water to heat it thoroughly. Remove and wipe dry, then lightly coat the blade with a thin layer of cooking oil.
2.  Use the preheated knife to cut the savory meat zongzi into thick slices. The glutinous rice will not stick easily, ensuring a clean, even cut.
3.  In a bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour and egg whites in a roughly 1:1 volume ratio. Stir until a smooth batter forms, then add a small amount of cooking oil and mix well to create the crispy coating batter.
4.  Pour enough oil into a frying pan. Heat over medium heat until inserting chopsticks immediately produces dense bubbles.
5.  Evenly coat each zongzi slice with the crispy batter, then place them one by one into the pan. The oil should be deep enough to submerge the slices halfway.
6.  Fry until one side is golden brown and firm, then flip. Continue frying until both sides are golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.
   **Note**: Maintain medium heat throughout. Ensure the oil is hot enough before adding the slices to achieve a crispy crust. The used oil can be strained and reused for stir-frying to avoid waste.
7.  Serve hot for a crispy exterior and soft, sticky interior. Pairs well with Pu'er tea to cut the richness and aid digestion.

## Health Tips
This crispy pan-fried zongzi is high in carbohydrates and fat, suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, those with weak digestion or in a weight-loss phase should consume it in moderation. Pairing with Pu'er tea helps cut the richness and aids digestion. It must be eaten hot, as the texture will harden as it cools.
