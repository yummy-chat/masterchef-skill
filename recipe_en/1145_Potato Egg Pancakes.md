# Potato Egg Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Year Round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Yellow-fleshed potatoes 2-3 (sliced into rounds about the thickness of a chopstick tip)
🌾 All-purpose flour about 2 tablespoons (used in two batches)
🌽 Cornstarch about 1 tablespoon
🥚 Eggs 2
🖤 Black sesame seeds to taste
🧂 Salt about 5 grams (3 grams for marinating, 2 grams for batter)
🌱 Cumin seeds about 10 grains
🍂 Chinese five-spice powder a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil about 10 grams (added to batter) + an appropriate amount for frying

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowls 2
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Soup spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and slice into rounds about the thickness of a chopstick tip. Place in a bowl, add about 3 grams of salt, and mix well with your hands. Marinate for 5 minutes.
   **Effect**: The salt draws out moisture from the potato slices, making them softer and more pliable, reducing the risk of breaking.
2. Add clean water to the marinated potato slices, gently rinse to remove excess salt from the surface, then drain well.
3. Transfer the drained potato slices to another dry bowl. Add one tablespoon of flour and gently toss with chopsticks until each slice is evenly coated with a thin layer of flour. Set aside.
   **Key**: This step creates a "base layer" that helps the wet batter adhere better.
4. To the remaining flour left from coating the potato slices, add one tablespoon of cornstarch, 2 eggs, 2 grams of salt, cumin seeds, and Chinese five-spice powder. Gradually add a small amount of water in multiple batches, stirring in one direction each time, until a thick, yogurt-like batter forms. Finally, add about 10 grams of cooking oil and mix well.
   **Consistency**: The batter should flow in a continuous stream when lifted with chopsticks.
   **Note**: Adding oil at the end makes the exterior crispier.
5. Heat the frying pan over medium heat and pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Using chopsticks, pick up a potato slice coated with dry flour, dip it into the batter to coat completely, and place it in the pan. Repeat for all slices, maintaining **low heat** throughout.
   **Warning**: Do not use high heat, or the outside will burn while the inside remains raw.
6. Evenly sprinkle black sesame seeds over all the pancakes in the pan.
7. Once the bottom is golden and set, flip each pancake individually with chopsticks. Continue to cook over low heat on the other side.
8. When both sides are golden brown and the pancake springs back when pressed, remove from the pan.
   **Doneness criteria**: Both sides are golden, and the potato inside is fully cooked and soft.

## Health Advice
The potato base combined with the egg batter gives a crispy exterior and soft interior, but fried carbohydrates are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy as a breakfast or post-workout snack, with a serving of 3-4 pieces. Be sure to rinse off excess salt after marinating the potatoes to avoid high sodium intake.
