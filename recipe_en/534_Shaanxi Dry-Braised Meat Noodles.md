# Shaanxi Dry-Braised Meat Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥡 Flour 3 jin  
🧂 Salt 9 grams (for dough)  
💧 Water 690 grams (add in batches)  
🥩 Fatty pork appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥩 Lean pork appropriate amount (cut into larger cubes)  
🥬 Green vegetables appropriate amount (bok choy or other leafy greens)  
🥙 Mixed vegetable topping appropriate amount (green beans, dried tofu, carrot cubes, etc.)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili segments appropriate amount  
🪵 Cinnamon stick appropriate amount  
🍃 Bay leaves appropriate amount  
🌟 Star anise appropriate amount  
🌰 Black cardamom appropriate amount (seeds removed)  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) appropriate amount  
🧉 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍖 Bone broth appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for braising meat)  
🌶️ White pepper powder appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 1 small spoon  
🧂 Salt a pinch (for tossing noodles)  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🧉 Soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 large spoon  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Strainer spoon  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🍜 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🖌️ Brush  
🔪 Cutting board  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the flour with salt evenly, add water in batches while stirring to form flocculent pieces, then knead into a dough.
2. Knead the dough in the bowl until the bowl is clean and hands are clean, then transfer to the cutting board and continue kneading until the dough is smooth.
3. Roll the smooth dough into a long strip, brush the surface with oil, and divide into evenly sized pieces. Roll each piece into a short strip about 10 centimeters long with even thickness, arrange neatly, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 40 minutes.
4. Prepare the meat topping: Heat oil in a wok, stir-fry the fatty pork slices over medium heat until they release oil and turn dry. Add the lean pork cubes and stir-fry until the color changes. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, dried chili segments, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, star anise, and black cardamom, and stir-fry until fragrant. Splash in baijiu to remove any gamey smell, drizzle dark soy sauce for color. Pour in bone broth, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and braise until the meat is tender. Season with salt, white pepper powder, and chicken bouillon. Finally, add fine chili powder and stir-fry until the oil turns red and glossy.
**Note**: Black cardamom must have seeds removed; stir-fry spices until fragrant before adding liquid seasonings; add chili powder last and stir-fry until the oil is bright red.

5. Pull the noodles: Take a few pieces of rested dough, separate them one by one, brush the surface with oil, and align the ends. Hold the noodles with both hands, alternately stretch and slap them against the work surface until they become long, thin, and evenly thick.
6. Cook the noodles: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the pulled noodles. When the noodles float to the surface, add the green vegetables and cook together until both the noodles and vegetables are done. Drain well.
7. Season and serve: Place the cooked noodles in a bowl, season with salt, MSG, soy sauce, and fragrant vinegar in order. Top with mixed vegetable topping. Ladle on a generous spoonful of meat topping. Finally, drizzle a large spoonful of chili oil. Mix well and enjoy.

## Health Advice  
This Shaanxi Dry-Braised Meat Noodle dish is rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion. However, the fatty meat and chili oil are high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Pairing with the mixed vegetable topping increases dietary fiber and helps balance nutrition.
