# Braised Pork Belly with Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes: 800g (before peeling, cut into irregular large chunks)  
🥩 Pork belly: 300g (sliced to the thickness of a coin)  
🧅 Scallion: about 15g (half a stalk, cut into scallion flowers)  
🧄 Garlic: 10g (2 cloves, sliced)  
🫚 Ginger: 10g (sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro: 10g (chopped, for garnish before serving)  
⭐ Star anise: half a piece  
🫒 Cooking oil: as needed  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder: about 1g (half a teaspoon, can be replaced with five-spice powder)  
🥫 Light soy sauce: about 20g (two tablespoons)  
🥫 Dark soy sauce: about 5g (half a tablespoon, optional if not preferred)  
🥫 Oyster sauce: about 20g (one full tablespoon, optional if not preferred)  
🍬 White sugar: about 3g (one teaspoon)  
🧂 MSG: about 2g (half a teaspoon)  
🧂 Salt: about 1.5–2g (a little less than half a teaspoon, add after tasting)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🪣 Wooden lid  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥄 Ladle or spatula  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl or basin  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel and wash the potatoes, then cut into irregular large chunks; slice the pork belly, place half a star anise on the slices; cut the scallion into scallion flowers, slice the garlic and ginger, and chop the cilantro.  
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add cooking oil, and keep on high heat throughout. Add the pork belly slices and star anise, stir-fry continuously until the fat shrinks and the edges are slightly golden and crispy, and the oil in the wok turns from cloudy to clear.  
   **Key point**: Fully sauté the pork to remove any gamey odor and enhance the aroma.  
3. Reduce heat to low, add the thirteen-spice powder, briefly bloom it using the remaining oil, then add scallion, ginger, and garlic slices. Switch to high heat and stir-fry until fragrant.  
4. Pour in the light soy sauce along the edge of the wok to release the aroma of the soy sauce.  
5. Add the potato chunks and stir-fry over high heat for 1–2 minutes, ensuring each piece is evenly coated with oil and sauce.  
6. Add hot water, then dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white sugar. Stir to combine.  
7. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover with a lid and reduce to low heat. Braise for 20 minutes, maintaining a gentle simmer.  
   **Note**: Do not open the lid during braising.  
8. Remove the lid, increase heat to high, and reduce the sauce, stirring constantly to prevent sticking. Remove the star anise. When the sauce thickens, add MSG, taste, and adjust salt if needed.  
9. Serve on a plate and garnish with chopped cilantro.  

## Health Advice  
Braised pork belly with potatoes is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for replenishing energy in people with high physical exertion. Diabetic patients are advised to control potato intake and pair the dish with leafy greens for a balanced meal.
