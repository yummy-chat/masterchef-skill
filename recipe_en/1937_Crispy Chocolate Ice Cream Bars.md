# Crispy Chocolate Ice Cream Bars
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍫 Dark chocolate 160g (30g for chocolate sauce, 130g for crispy coating)  
🥛 Whole milk 75g (25g for chocolate sauce, 50g for egg custard)  
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (about 100g)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 20g  
🧈 Heavy cream 110g (chill in advance)  
🥥 Coconut oil 40g  
🥜 Roasted melon seeds (or other favorite nuts), to taste  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Small saucepan  
🔥 Induction cooker  
🥢 Hand whisk  
🥣 Small bowl  
🫗 Large glass bowl  
🍡 Ice cream molds  
🥄 Long-handled spoon or silicone spatula  
🥛 Tall glass  

## Instructions
1. **Make the chocolate sauce:** In a small saucepan, combine 30g dark chocolate and 25g whole milk. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the chocolate is fully melted and the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and set aside.  
2. **Make the egg yolk mixture:** In another small saucepan, combine 2 egg yolks and 20g granulated sugar. Whisk continuously until the yolks become pale, increase in volume, and thicken; when you lift the whisk, the ribbons should hold their shape for a moment.  
3. **Make the egg custard:** Gradually add 50g whole milk to the egg yolk mixture, stirring well after each addition. Place over the lowest heat, stirring constantly to prevent scorching. Heat until the mixture noticeably thickens, small bubbles rise from the bottom, and it becomes very viscous. Remove from heat immediately—the yolks are now fully cooked.  
4. **Combine chocolate and egg custard:** Pour the cooked egg custard into the reserved chocolate sauce. Stir until evenly colored, then let cool to room temperature.  
5. **Whip the cream:** Take out the chilled 110g heavy cream and whip to soft peaks—when you lift the whisk, the cream forms a soft, drooping tip.  
6. **Mix the ice cream base:** Pour the cooled chocolate-egg mixture into the whipped cream. Using a silicone spatula, fold gently until combined, being careful not to deflate the cream. Mix until the color is uniform and the batter is smooth and thick.  
7. **Fill molds and freeze:** Pour the ice cream batter into molds, spread evenly, and cover with lids. Freeze for at least 4 hours until fully firm.  
8. **Make the crispy chocolate coating:** Place 130g dark chocolate and 40g coconut oil in a small saucepan. Heat over a water bath or low heat, stirring until completely melted and smooth. Turn off the heat. Pour into a tall glass, add roasted melon seeds to taste, and let stand at room temperature (about 20°C) for roughly 1 minute to cool slightly.  
9. **Unmold and coat:** Remove the frozen ice cream bars from the molds. Quickly dip each bar into the slightly cooled coating, ensuring an even layer.  
   **Note:** The dipping must be fast to avoid a coating that is too thick or that solidifies too quickly due to the cold bar.  
   Let sit briefly until the chocolate coating turns matte, then serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Crispy chocolate ice cream bars use dark chocolate and coconut oil, reducing the dairy fat feel—making them a great summer treat. However, the heavy cream and sugar still contribute significant calories, so it’s best to enjoy just one bar per serving.
