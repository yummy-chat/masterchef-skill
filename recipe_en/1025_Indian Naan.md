# Indian Naan
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Plain yogurt 150g  
💧 Room temperature water 400g  
🫒 Oil or butter 25g  
🍞 Yeast 4g (dry yeast)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 750g  
🍬 Sugar 12g  
🧂 Salt 8g  
🧈 Ghee 20g (for garlic butter)  
🧄 Minced garlic 20g  
🧂 Salt 2g (for garlic butter)  
🌿 Cilantro 5g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spatula  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔪 Cutting board  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🪨 Pizza stone  
🔘 Sieve with handle  
🪣 Damp cloth  
🍳 Small saucepan  
🔥 Oven (capable of 290°C/550°F)  
🖌️ Brush  

## Steps
1. In a glass bowl, add yogurt, room temperature water, sugar, salt, oil, and yeast in order. Stir with a spatula until well combined.
2. Add the flour and stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains, then knead by hand into a dough. The dough will be soft and sticky; do not force it to become smooth at this stage.
3. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap and let rest for 20 minutes to allow the gluten to naturally hydrate.
4. Continue kneading the dough in the bowl for about 1 minute until smooth and elastic, then cover and ferment at room temperature for about 1.5 hours until doubled in size. Optionally, knead the dough for 1 minute halfway through fermentation to create a finer texture.
5. Lightly oil the work surface and hands, then turn out the dough and divide it into 10 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball.
6. Brush the surface of the dough balls with oil, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes to relax the gluten.
7. Place the pizza stone on the middle rack of the oven. Set the oven to its highest temperature and preheat for at least 30 to 60 minutes.
8. Make the garlic butter: In a small saucepan, melt the ghee over low heat. Add the minced garlic and 2g salt, heat until the aromas are well combined, then turn off the heat and let cool. Stir in the chopped cilantro.
9. Stretch a damp cloth tightly over the sieve and secure it. This will be used as a tool to transfer the dough.
10. **Shaping and Baking**: Take one rested dough ball and gently press it flat with your fingers. Alternately toss it between both hands to naturally stretch it into a teardrop shape with thin edges and a slightly thicker center. Place the shaped naan smooth side down onto the damp cloth-covered sieve. Quickly open the oven, invert the sieve onto the hot pizza stone to adhere the naan, and swiftly remove the sieve. Close the oven door and bake at the highest temperature for about 2 to 3 minutes until the naan puffs up with large blisters and develops charred brown spots. If browning is insufficient, briefly use the broil function.
11. Once out of the oven, use tongs to remove the naan, and immediately brush generously with the garlic butter. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice
This Indian naan uses yogurt for fermentation, resulting in a soft texture that pairs well with curry or can be served as a main staple. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to control the portion size to avoid excessive intake.
