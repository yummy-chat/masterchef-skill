# Adazi (Tapioca Pearls)

Origin: Cantonese Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍠 100g tapioca starch (for plain flavor)  
🍠 130g tapioca starch (for passion fruit flavor)  
🍬 20g white sugar (for plain flavor) + 20g (for passion fruit flavor)  
💧 80g water (just boiled, for plain flavor)  
🟡 Approx. 120g passion fruit pulp (for passion fruit flavor)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
♨️ Electric kettle  
🔪 Silicone kneading mat, scraper  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🔥 Induction cooker/stove  
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon  
💨 Spray bottle (for when dough becomes too dry)  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Instructions  
### Plain Adazi  
1. Mix 100g tapioca starch and 20g white sugar evenly in a bowl.  
2. Pour in 80g just-boiled water and quickly stir into flocculent pieces.  
**Key point**: Must use boiling water, otherwise the dough will not form.  
3. After slightly cooling, put on gloves and knead the dough until smooth and non-sticky. Roll into a long strip and cut into small cubes of about 1-1.5 cm using a scraper.  
**Key point**: Do not cut the pieces too large, otherwise it affects the texture.  
4. Bring water in a pot to a boil, add the adazi, and cook for about 1 minute. Remove once all pieces float to the surface and turn semi-translucent.  
5. Immediately place into cold water to cool, achieving a chewy texture.  
### Passion Fruit Adazi  
1. Cut passion fruit in half and scoop out about 120g pulp.  
2. Pour the pulp and 20g white sugar into a pot, bring to a boil, then turn off the heat immediately.  
3. While hot, quickly pour in 130g tapioca starch and stir rapidly into flocculent pieces. Knead into a yellow dough. If the dough becomes too dry, spray a little water and knead again.  
4. Roll the dough into a long strip, cut into small pieces, then roll into small balls.  
5. Once water boils, add the adazi and cook until all float to the surface. Remove and cool in cold water.  

## Health Suggestions  
Adazi are chewy and smooth, rich in tapioca starch, making them suitable as a low-fat dessert. However, they are high in sugar, so people with diabetes or those controlling sugar intake should consume in moderation.
