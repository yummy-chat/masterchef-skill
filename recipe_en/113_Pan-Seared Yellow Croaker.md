# Pan-Seared Yellow Croaker
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Yellow croaker 1 piece (keep whole, do not gut)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🧅 Red shallot appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Red chili pepper appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (chopped)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating the fish)
🌾 Cornstarch (potato starch) appropriate amount (for coating the fish)
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce about 1 tablespoon
🍾 Worcestershire sauce about two-thirds of a bowl (**key seasoning**)
🍬 Sugar about 1 tablespoon

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (Chinese iron wok)
🥄 Soup spoon
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. Scrape off the scales, make a horizontal cut at the fish's anus to sever the intestines, keeping the fish intact without opening the belly.
2. Insert chopsticks through the fish's mouth deep into the belly, twist the chopsticks to separate the internal organs from the body, then pull the chopsticks along with the gills and innards out through the mouth.
**Key point**: Be sure to twist the chopsticks to ensure the organs are completely detached.
3. Rinse the inside of the fish belly with clean water, evenly spread a thin layer of salt on both sides of the fish, and marinate for 5 minutes.
**Key point**: This adds a base flavor and helps the skin set during frying to prevent breaking.
4. Evenly coat both sides of the fish with a thin layer of cornstarch.
**Key point**: The coating should not be thick; it's for creating a crispy skin and will naturally thicken the sauce during the subsequent braising.
5. Heat the wok with oil over medium-low heat, fry the fish for about 3 minutes until golden and set, then flip and fry the other side for another 3 minutes. Remove when the fish is about 70-80% cooked.
**Key point**: Use hot wok with cold oil or let the wok cool slightly before adding the fish to prevent sticking.
6. Leave a base oil in the wok, sauté the minced garlic, ginger, and red shallot until fragrant. Add a little water, then light soy sauce, oyster sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine and bring to a boil to create a tangy, slightly sweet, and mildly spicy glossy sauce.
7. Place the fried yellow croaker into the sauce, continuously spoon the sauce over the fish, and braise gently over medium-low heat.
**Key point**: Do not braise for too long to keep the fish tender; the cornstarch on the fish will naturally thicken the sauce, no need for additional slurry.
8. When the sauce thickens and coats the fish well, sprinkle with red chili pepper rings and chopped scallion. Transfer to a plate for serving.

## Health Advice
Yellow croaker is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for weight loss and muscle building. When frying, do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid damaging nutrients. Pay attention to the amount of salt used in marinating; those with high blood pressure can reduce it as needed.
