# Claypot Rice

Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Thai jasmine rice, 4 bowls
🍚 Glutinous rice, 1 bowl
🥓 Chinese sausage, as needed (sliced)
🥓 Cured pork belly, as needed (sliced)
🥚 Egg, 1 piece
🥬 Choy sum, as needed (blanched in advance)
🧂 Light soy sauce, 4 spoons (using a wooden spoon as measure)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1.5 spoons
🧂 Steamed fish soy sauce, 1 spoon (optional)
🍬 White sugar, 3 spoons
💧 Water, 8 spoons (for the sauce) + as needed (for soaking rice and cooking)
🌶️ White pepper, about three shakes
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil or lard, as needed (for greasing the pan and drizzling along the edge)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for blanching choy sum)

## Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Wooden spoon
🪥 Toothbrush (for evenly applying oil)
🍶 Bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula
🫖 Lid
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1.  Mix Thai jasmine rice and glutinous rice in a 4:1 ratio, place in a stainless steel bowl, add water until the rice is submerged, and soak for 1 hour.
**Key point**: Adding glutinous rice makes it easier to form a crispy crust; the rice must be fully soaked.

2.  Prepare the claypot soy sauce: In a cold pan, add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, steamed fish soy sauce, white sugar, water, white pepper, and sesame oil in order. Stir evenly with a spatula, then bring to a boil over heat. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
**Note**: Use a wooden spoon as a measuring spoon to ensure accurate proportions.

3.  Pour a small amount of cooking oil or lard into the bottom of a non-stick frying pan. Use a toothbrush to evenly coat the bottom and sides of the pan with oil.
**Key point**: Grease thoroughly to prevent sticking and help form a crispy crust.

4.  Drain the soaked rice and pour it into the pan, spreading it flat. Add water until it just covers the surface of the rice. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to the lowest heat, cover with a lid, and simmer for 15 minutes.
**Note**: Do not use too much water; it should only just cover the rice. The simmering time is fixed to ensure the rice is fully cooked.

5.  Remove the lid, arrange the sliced Chinese sausage and cured pork belly on top of the rice. Drizzle a ring of oil along the edge of the pan. Cover the lid again, and continue to simmer on the lowest heat for 5 minutes. During this time, you can move the pan around to ensure even heating at the bottom.
**Key point**: Drizzling oil along the edge is crucial for forming an even crispy crust; rotating the pan prevents local burning.

6.  Remove the lid, crack a raw egg into the center of the rice. Quickly cover the lid and turn off the heat. Use the residual heat to cook the egg until it reaches your preferred doneness (soft or fully cooked).
**Key point**: The heat is off; the egg is cooked by residual heat only, resulting in a more tender texture.

7.  Remove the lid, place the pre-blanched choy sum next to the rice. Drizzle the claypot soy sauce prepared in step 2 over everything. Mix well and serve.
**Note**: Add a pinch of salt to the water when blanching choy sum to keep the vegetables bright green.

## Health Advice
This claypot rice uses glutinous rice to enhance the stickiness of the crispy crust, but it is high in carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables or a light soup to balance the meal. Cured meats are high in salt and fat. People with high blood pressure or those managing fat intake can reduce the amount used and substitute low-sodium soy sauce for part of the sauce. If using a toothbrush for oil application, please mark it as kitchen-only to avoid cross-contamination.
