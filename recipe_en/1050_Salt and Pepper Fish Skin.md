# Salt and Pepper Fish Skin
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (take the fish skin and bones)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (for marinating to remove fishy smell)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for marinating to remove fishy smell)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (for marinating to remove fishy smell)
🥔 Potato starch or cornstarch, appropriate amount (for coating)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for marinating to remove fishy smell)
🧴 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for seasoning and enhancing freshness)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for marinating; for stir-frying with salt and pepper)
🍗 Chicken bouillon powder, appropriate amount (for enhancing umami)
🤍 White pepper powder, appropriate amount (to remove fishy smell and add aroma)
🌶️ White peppercorns, appropriate amount (for stir-frying salt and pepper)
🫑 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (for stir-frying salt and pepper)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating)
🍳 Non-stick pan (for stir-frying salt and pepper)
🥄 Silicone spatula
🌀 Blender (for grinding salt and pepper powder)
🥅 Strainer
🥘 Wok (for deep-frying)
🫗 Stainless steel basin (for mixing finished product)

## Steps
1.  Use a knife to completely remove the fish skin, and chop the fish spine into bite-sized pieces.
2.  Add scallion, ginger, garlic, cooking wine, light soy sauce, salt, chicken bouillon powder, and white pepper powder to the fish skin and bones. Mix well and marinate to absorb the flavors.
3.  Pour white peppercorns and Sichuan peppercorns into a clean, oil-free non-stick pan. Dry-fry over low heat until fragrant and slightly crispy, then add salt and stir-fry together. 🔥 Must be dry-fried throughout without oil, otherwise it will stick and won't form a crispy texture.
4.  Pour the stir-fried salt and pepper mixture into a blender and grind into a fine powder.
5.  Remove the marinated fish skin and bones from the marinade, drain off excess liquid, and pick out the scallion, ginger, and garlic residues. This prevents oil splattering or burning during deep-frying.
6.  Evenly coat the drained fish skin and bones with a thin layer of starch.
7.  Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok. When the oil temperature reaches about 60% hot, first fry the fish bones until the moisture is expelled and they float, then remove. Then fry the fish skin pieces one by one until golden and crispy, then remove. 🔥 If the oil temperature is too low, it won't be crispy; ensure the internal moisture is fully expelled.
8.  Place the fried fish bones and skin into a basin. While still hot, sprinkle in the salt and pepper powder, toss the basin to mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in collagen and calcium, suitable for those needing to supplement skin elasticity and bone health. However, due to the high calorie content after deep-frying, it is recommended to control the portion size and pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition.
