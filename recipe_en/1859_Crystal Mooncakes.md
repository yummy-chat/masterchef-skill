# Crystal Mooncakes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Sago 200g
🧂 White Sugar 35g (for crystal skin)
🌽 Corn Starch 20g
💧 Warm Water 200g
🍠 Taro, as needed (steamed and mashed)
🍠 Purple Sweet Potato, as needed (steamed and mashed)
🍠 Sweet Potato, as needed (steamed and mashed)
🧀 Cheese 10g per piece (optional, for filling)
🥓 Dried Meat Floss 10g per piece (optional, for filling)
🥗 Salad Dressing, a small amount (to mix with meat floss)
🧂 White Sugar, as needed (for filling seasoning)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen Scale
🥣 Large Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks or Mixing Spoon
🍲 Steamer
📜 Steamer Liner or Parchment Paper
🥄 Masher or Spoon
🥮 Mooncake Mold

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Stage
1. Pour sago, white sugar, and corn starch into a large bowl. Add warm water and stir thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Let the mixture rest for 30 minutes after stirring to allow the sago to fully absorb water.

2. Slice the taro, purple sweet potato, and sweet potato separately. Steam until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
   **Key Point**: After removing, mash each into a paste. If the filling is too wet and difficult to shape, stir-fry over low heat in a pan until dry. Adjust sweetness by adding white sugar to taste.

3. Divide the mashed filling into 25g balls. For filled variations, proceed as follows:
   - **Taro and Cheese Filling**: Press 15g taro paste flat, wrap in 10g cheese, seal, and roll into a ball.
   - **Taro and Meat Floss Filling**: Mix meat floss with a small amount of salad dressing, shape into a 10g ball, then wrap with 15g taro paste and seal.
   **Key Point**: The final weight of each filling ball should be uniformly 25g.

### Making Stage
1. Knead the rested sago mixture into a smooth, uniform dough. If too dry, add a small amount of water as needed.
   **Key Point**: The dough should hold together well and be uniform, not crumbly.

2. Divide the dough into 25g portions.

3. Take one piece of dough, flatten it into a slightly larger round disc, place a filling ball in the center, and wrap using the "tiger's mouth" technique to seal, then roll smooth.
   **Key Point**: The ratio of skin to filling is 1:1.

4. Place the wrapped mooncake ball into a mold dusted with a little dry powder, press down vertically to shape, then push out the mooncake blank.
   **Note**: Dusting the mold with a small amount of cake flour or starch prevents sticking and ensures clear patterns.

5. Bring water in a steamer to a boil. Lay a steamer liner or parchment paper on the steaming rack and arrange the mooncake blanks. Steam over high heat for 25 minutes after the water boils.
   **Key Point**: A liner is essential to prevent sticking and skin breakage after steaming. Let the steam dissipate slightly before removing for a more translucent, non-sticky surface. After steaming, the skin will change from white to translucent, with the filling color faintly visible.

## Health Tips
These crystal mooncakes use sago instead of traditional flour, offering lower calories and being gluten-free, suitable for those controlling sugar intake or with gluten sensitivity. The mashed filling is rich in dietary fiber, but attention should be paid to the amount of sugar and cheese added to avoid excess calories.
