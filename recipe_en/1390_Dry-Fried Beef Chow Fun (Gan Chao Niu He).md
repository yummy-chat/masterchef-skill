# Dry-Fried Beef Chow Fun (Gan Chao Niu He)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Fresh rice noodles 250g (separate into individual strands before use)  
🥩 Beef tenderloin 100g (remove sinew, slice thinly against the grain)  
🌱 Bean sprouts 40g (about 50g, trimmed ends removed)  
🧅 Onion 20g (cut into thin strips)  
🧅 Scallions 15g (use green parts, cut into small sections)  
🥬 Chives 30g (cut into sections the same length as scallions)  

🧂 **Beef Marinade**  
🥄 Baking soda 0.5g (about 1 small teaspoon tip)  
💧 Water 5g  
🌿 Black pepper 0.3g  
🍬 White sugar 2g  
🥫 Light soy sauce 5g  
🌽 Cornstarch 3g  
🫒 Cooking oil 10g (added last to seal in moisture)  

🥣 **Sauce Mix**  
🥫 Light soy sauce 10g  
🥫 Dark soy sauce 5g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 5g  
🍬 White sugar 3g  
🧂 MSG 2g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok (lightweight for easy tossing)  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Small teaspoon  
🥢 Chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)  
🥄 Strainer  
🫗 Small pot or container (to catch drained oil)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Gently separate the fresh rice noodles into individual strands to prevent sticking during cooking.  
2. **Marinate the beef**:  
   - Add baking soda and water to the beef slices, mix with hands until sticky and tacky.  
   - Add black pepper, white sugar, light soy sauce, and cornstarch, continue mixing evenly.  
   - Finally, add cooking oil and mix well to coat each slice with an oil film.  
**Note**: Achieving a tacky texture is key to tender beef; the oil film locks in moisture and prevents sticking.  

3. Trim the ends off bean sprouts, cut onion into thin strips, use scallion greens and cut into sections, cut chives into sections the same length as scallions, and set aside.  

4. Mix all ingredients in the sauce bowl until granules are fully dissolved.  
**Note**: Preparing the sauce in advance prevents scrambling during cooking.  

5. **Preheat the wok to prevent sticking**: Heat the iron wok over high heat until smoking, add a thin layer of oil to coat, and heat until a large amount of white smoke forms, creating an oil film.  
**Key**: The wok must be thoroughly heated, otherwise the rice noodles will stick easily.  

6. **Quick-fry the beef**: Add about 3 times the usual amount of oil to the wok, heat until hot, then quickly spread the beef and stir-fry until about 80% cooked. Immediately turn off heat, pour the oil and beef onto a strainer to drain.  
7. **Quick-fry the vegetables**: Leave a base layer of oil in the wok, heat until smoking, add onion strips and bean sprouts, stir-fry rapidly for 7-8 seconds until just cooked, then immediately remove.  
**Note**: Use high heat and fast stir-frying to maintain crispness; do not overcook until soft and watery.  

8. **Pan-fry the rice noodles**:  
   - Pour some of the drained oil back into the wok, heat, then pour out, leaving a small amount as base.  
   - Lay rice noodles flat in the wok, shake the wok to fry for about 7-8 seconds, flip by tossing, repeat 4-5 times until both sides have slightly charred spots.  
**Key**: Prefer shaking and tossing the wok over using a spatula to keep noodles intact and generate "wok hei".  

9. **Combine and serve**:  
   - Place the quick-fried beef, stir-fried onions and bean sprouts, raw scallion sections, and chives on top of the rice noodles.  
   - Pour the sauce mix directly over the ingredients.  
   - Immediately use chopsticks to toss everything together quickly, finish within seconds, turn off heat, and plate immediately.  
**Key**: Maintain high heat throughout; actions must be fast and precise; sauce will burn if it contacts the wok edge; raw scallions and chives will cook through with residual heat, preserving aroma; serve promptly to prevent sticking.  

## Health Tips  
Beef is rich in iron, and paired with rice noodles for carbohydrates, it’s suitable for post-workout energy replenishment. Control oil and sodium intake; the sauce is high in salt, so adjust to taste.
