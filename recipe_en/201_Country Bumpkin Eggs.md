# Country Bumpkin Eggs  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 16 eggs (washed; refrigerated eggs should be brought to room temperature in advance)  
🧅 20g scallion (cut into sections)  
🫚 15g ginger (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (add according to preference)  
🌿 3g Sichuan peppercorns  
🌿 4g cumin seeds  
🌿 0.5g cassia bark (do not overuse, otherwise it becomes bitter)  
🌿 1g Angelica dahurica  
🌿 4g licorice (key spice)  
🌿 3g star anise (about 3–4 pieces; use 3 if large)  
🧂 1 teaspoon salt (for boiling eggs) + 2 teaspoons salt (for marinating)  
🧂 3g chicken bouillon (for marinating) + 5g (for reducing broth)  
🧂 3g MSG (for marinating)  
🍬 20g white sugar (for marinating) + 10g (for reducing broth)  
🍾 20g dark soy sauce (for marinating) + 40g (for reducing broth)  
🍾 70–100g light soy sauce (use 10 spoonfuls if low-sodium light soy sauce, about 7 spoonfuls for regular light soy sauce)  
💧 Water, as needed (enough to cover eggs when boiling; enough to submerge eggs by one knuckle when marinating; about 4 bowls for reducing broth)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for boiling eggs)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing eggs)  
🫕 Clay pot (for marinating; do not use an iron pot)  
🍲 Wok (for reducing and coloring the broth)  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board (for preparing scallion and ginger)  
🥣 Bowl (for measuring seasonings for reducing broth)  
🥄 Soup spoon (for adding ingredients and stirring)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the washed eggs in a flat-bottomed pan, add cold water to cover the eggs, and add 1 teaspoon of salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to medium heat and cook for 8 minutes.  
   **Note**: Bring refrigerated eggs to room temperature half an hour in advance to avoid cracking during cooking; 8 minutes ensures the yolks are just set.  
2. Remove the eggs and immediately immerse them in cold water for 5–10 minutes. Use thermal expansion and contraction to make the shells easy to peel.  
3. Gently tap the larger end of each egg in the water to crack the shell, then peel. Be sure to remove the white membrane between the egg white and shell, otherwise the marinating color will be uneven.  
4. Place the peeled eggs into a clay pot. Add, in order, scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, cassia bark, Angelica dahurica, licorice, star anise, dried chili peppers, 6g salt, 3g chicken bouillon, 3g MSG, 20g white sugar, 20g dark soy sauce, and 70–100g light soy sauce. Pour in water until the surface is about one knuckle above the eggs.  
   **Key point**: Use cassia bark and Angelica dahurica with extreme caution; too much will make it bitter.  
5. Bring the marinade to a boil over high heat, then turn to low heat and simmer for 1 hour. After turning off the heat, let the eggs soak in the marinade for at least 6 hours or overnight to fully absorb flavor and color.  
6. Remove the marinated eggs and place them in a wok. In another bowl, mix 4 bowls of water, 10g white sugar, 5g chicken bouillon, and 40g dark soy sauce, then pour into the wok.  
   **Note**: Do not use the original marinade for the reducing broth, otherwise the flavor will be too strong.  
7. Bring to a boil over high heat and reduce the broth, stirring the eggs constantly with a spoon to coat them evenly. When the broth reduces to about one-third, increase the stirring frequency; when the broth thickens, bubbles change from large to small and burst quickly, and you can no longer scoop up the broth, turn off the heat immediately to avoid scorching.  
   **State judgment**: The color may be uneven just after cooking, but it will become uniformly glossy once cooled.  
8. Serve and plate. The finished eggs are tender, firm, and chewy, with savory and flavorful yolks and a slight sweetness in the aftertaste.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein, suitable for fitness enthusiasts and mental workers. However, 16 eggs have a high sodium content, so people with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. Pair with coarse grains and vegetables for balanced nutrition.
