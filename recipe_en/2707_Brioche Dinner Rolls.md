# Brioche Dinner Rolls
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
🥛 Ice-cold milk 90ml  
🥚 Refrigerated eggs 2 (for the dough)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 85g (room temperature, softened, added in 6 batches)  
🍄 Fresh yeast 9g  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🍬 White sugar 15g  
🥚 Egg wash for surface: 1 egg + 1 tablespoon of water (mix well for brushing)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🍞 Stand mixer (with dough hook)  
🥣 Large bowl (greased to prevent sticking)  
🥄 Spatula  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🧺 Cheesecloth or kitchen towel  
🔥 Baking sheet + parchment paper  
♨️ Oven  
🥢 Whisk  
🖌️ Silicone brush  
❄️ Refrigerator (for chilling liquids and cold fermentation)  

## Instructions  
1. Add bread flour, fresh yeast, salt, and white sugar into the stand mixer bowl. Add ice-cold milk and refrigerated eggs. Mix on low speed until combined, then increase to medium-high speed and knead until the dough can be pulled into a thick but easily tearing membrane.  
   **Note**: Using cold ingredients helps control dough temperature and prevents overheating during kneading.  
2. Add the softened butter in 6 batches, adding one small piece at a time and waiting until fully incorporated before adding the next.  
   **Key**: Butter should be softened until it yields a dent when pressed lightly with a finger, but not melted. The high butter content is characteristic of brioche; adding gradually promotes emulsification and blending.  
3. Continue kneading on high speed until the dough becomes extremely soft, smooth, and shiny on the surface, and can be stretched into a thin, even "windowpane" membrane.  
4. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a large greased bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for cold fermentation until doubled in volume and filled with honeycomb-like air pockets.  
5. Remove the dough, gently press to release gas, and divide into 9 equal portions. Roll each portion into a small ball.  
6. Arrange the dough balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover with a layer of cheesecloth and let proof at room temperature until doubled in size again.  
7. Beat the egg with water to make an egg wash. Use a silicone brush to evenly apply the egg wash onto the surface of the dough balls.  
8. Preheat the oven to 190°C (375°F). Bake for 12‑15 minutes, adjusting slightly according to your oven's characteristics.  
9. Immediately transfer the baked rolls to a wire rack to cool. The buns should have a glossy surface, feel very light when lifted, and spring back quickly when pressed.  

## Health Advice  
This brioche recipe has a high butter content and is relatively high in calories. It is suitable for consumption after exercise or when needing an energy boost, but should be eaten in moderation during weight loss. Cold fermentation of the dough enhances flavor and makes temperature control easier, making it achievable for beginners as well.
