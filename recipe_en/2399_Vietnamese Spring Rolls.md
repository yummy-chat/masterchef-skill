# Vietnamese Spring Rolls
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring and Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Ingredients
🍗 Chicken breast 1 piece  
🦐 Shrimp, as needed  
🥚 Eggs 2 pieces  
🥕 Carrot, as needed (julienned)  
🥒 Cucumber 1 (julienned)  
🥬 Endive, as needed  
🌿 Mint leaves, as needed  
🍋 Lime 1  
🫓 Vietnamese rice paper wrappers, as needed  
🥜 Peanut powder, as needed

### Seasonings
🧅 Scallion, as needed (for boiling chicken to remove odor)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for boiling chicken to remove odor)  
🌶 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (for boiling chicken to remove odor)  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for boiling chicken to remove odor)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (for dipping sauce)  
🍯 Honey, as needed (to enhance flavor in dipping sauce)  
🌶 Chili oil, as needed (to add spice to dipping sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick small milk pot  
🥘 Tamagoyaki pan  
🥣 Measuring cup  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🥢 Chopsticks, slotted spoon  
🍽 Large plate (for soaking rice paper and assembling)  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing dipping sauce)  
🔥 Stove  
💧 Mineral water, as needed (to dilute peanut powder)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Boil chicken**: Place chicken breast in a pot, add enough water, then add scallion segments, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook until fully cooked. Remove and let cool.
   **Note**: Cooking wine and spices effectively remove the chicken's odor. The chicken breast must be completely cooled before shredding into thin strips by hand.

2. **Boil shrimp**: Use the same pot of broth from cooking the chicken. Add the shrimp and cook until they turn red and are fully cooked. Remove and drain.
   **Key point**: Boiling shrimp in the original chicken broth enhances their umami flavor.

3. **Make egg crepe**: Beat the eggs evenly in a measuring cup. Heat the tamagoyaki pan over low heat, pour in the egg mixture, and swirl to form a thin crepe. Cook until set, then remove and let cool. Cut into thin strips.
   **Key point**: Maintain low heat throughout to ensure the crepe is thin without tearing.

4. **Prepare other ingredients**: Julienne the cucumber and carrot into thin strips. Wash the endive and mint leaves, then drain thoroughly.

5. **Prepare dipping sauce**: In a bowl, combine peanut powder with a small amount of mineral water and stir into a paste. Add light soy sauce, lime juice, and honey, then mix well. Finally, add chili oil to taste.
   **Note**: Do not add too much water, or the sauce will become too thin; honey enhances flavor.

6. **Soak rice paper**: Fill a large plate with cold water. Quickly dip both sides of a Vietnamese rice paper wrapper into the water, then immediately remove.
   **Key point**: Do not soak for too long, or the wrapper will become soft, break, and be unworkable.

7. **Assemble spring rolls**: Lay the softened rice paper flat. Place endive, carrot strips, cucumber strips, egg strips, shredded chicken, and mint leaves in the center. Then roll tightly and seal.
   **Key point**: Do not overfill or use too moist fillings. Roll similarly to wrapping cold noodles, ensuring a tight seal.

8. **Plate and serve**: Arrange the finished spring rolls on a plate. Serve with the peanut dipping sauce. Refreshing and not greasy, perfect for hot weather.

## Health Suggestion
This Vietnamese spring roll is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for a weight-loss diet. Ensure the shrimp and chicken are completely cooled before wrapping; otherwise, heat will soften the rice paper and cause it to tear.
