# Traditional Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin 750g (cut into strips the thickness of an index finger)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🥔 Potato starch Equal to the weight of the pork (used in two parts for batter)  
🫘 White sesame seeds To taste (sprinkle before serving)  
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp  
🌶️ White pepper powder 1/2 tbsp  
🧂 Salt A pinch (for marinating) + to taste (for sauce)  
🍬 White sugar 3 tbsp  
🍾 Vinegar 3 tbsp  
🥢 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp  
💧 Water 4 tbsp (for sauce)  
🫒 Cooking oil 2 tbsp (for sealing) + enough for deep frying  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔔 Chef's knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Wooden chopsticks  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Cutting the meat**: Wash the pork tenderloin, remove the edge membrane and fat. First cut into thick slices, then cut perpendicular to the grain into strips the thickness of an index finger. Rinse with clean water and squeeze out excess water.  
**Note**: Cutting perpendicular to the grain breaks the fibers, ensuring the meat strips are tender and not tough.  

2. **Marinating**: Add 2 tbsp cooking wine, 1/2 tbsp white pepper powder, and a pinch of salt to the meat strips. Vigorously mix in one direction for 2-3 minutes, until the strips become sticky and the water is fully absorbed.  
**Key point**: Mix with enough force and in a consistent direction to let the meat absorb the flavors thoroughly and become relaxed.  

3. **Coating**: Push the meat to the side of the bowl. Add about half of the potato starch and crack in 1 egg. Mix evenly, then let rest for 20 minutes. Add the remaining starch and continue mixing until each strip is coated with a thick batter. If too dry, add a tiny amount of water. Finally, add 2 tbsp cooking oil and mix well to lock in moisture and prevent sticking.  
**Note**: Adding starch in batches makes it easier to control the thickness of the batter. The final addition of oil is key for crispiness and preventing sticking.  

4. **First frying for setting**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat over high heat until chopsticks inserted produce dense small bubbles. Reduce to medium heat. Add the meat strips one by one. Once all strips are in, let them sit for about 1 minute without touching. When set, gently separate them with chopsticks. Continue frying for about 2 minutes, until the strips float and turn a light yellow. Remove and drain oil.  
**Judgment criteria**: Set, floating, light yellow color.  

5. **Second frying for crispiness**: Heat the oil over high heat. Add the first-fried strips back and fry for 30-40 seconds, until golden and crispy. Quickly remove and drain oil.  
**Note**: Do not fry too long during the second fry, otherwise the interior will lose too much moisture and become hard.  

6. **Making the sauce**: In a bowl, mix 3 tbsp sugar, 3 tbsp vinegar, 1 tbsp light soy sauce, salt to taste, and 4 tbsp water. Stir well to create the sweet and sour sauce. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over medium heat, pour in the sauce and stir constantly. Cook until the bubbles change from small to large and the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.  
**State check**: Sauce coats the spoon and has dense large bubbles.  

7. **Coating and serving**: Add the second-fried meat strips into the wok. Immediately turn off the heat. Quickly toss the wok to coat each strip evenly with the sauce. Sprinkle with white sesame seeds. Plate and serve.  
**Tip**: Tossing the wok is better than stirring with a spoon for maintaining crispiness.  

## Health Advice  
This sweet and sour pork tenderloin is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. The sweet and sour sauce is appetizing and cuts the grease. However, deep frying and the sweet and sour sauce are high-calorie cooking methods. It is recommended to pair with stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for occasional indulgence. Those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should control their intake.
