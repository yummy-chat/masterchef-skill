# Sweet and Sour Pickled Radish
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 White radish 4 jin (washed, not peeled, cut into slices as thick as a chopstick head)  
🧄 Garlic cloves 20g (sliced)  
🫚 Ginger 15g (sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro 15g (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers 30g (cut into rings or sections)  
🫑 Pickled chili peppers 80g (along with pickling liquid)  
🍬 White sugar 50g (for "sweating")  
🍷 Red vinegar 250g  
🧊 Old rock sugar 360g  
💧 Pickled chili pepper liquid 150g  
🧂 Salt about 15g  
💧 Water 4 jin  
⭐ Star anise 3g  
🌰 Cinnamon stick 3g  
🍃 Bay leaves 2g  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🧺 Bamboo basket (or colander)  
🍳 Wok and slotted spoon  
🔥 Stove  
🫙 Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the white radish**: Wash the white radish, cut off the ends, keep the skin, split it lengthwise in half, then cut into slices about the thickness of a chopstick head. Place in a large glass bowl.
2. **Sweat with sugar**: Add 50g of white sugar to the radish slices. Mix well with your hands so each slice is evenly coated with sugar. Let sit for 15–20 minutes until the radish releases water. This step removes raw, pungent flavors and maintains a crunchy texture.
3. **Rinse and drain**: Pour out the released water. Rinse the radish slices with clean water to remove the surface sugar. Remove and place in a bamboo basket. Let drain completely on the surface to prevent affecting flavor absorption and crispness during pickling.
4. **Make the sweet and sour liquid**: Add 4 jin of water to a wok and bring to a boil. Place star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves in a slotted spoon. Cook over medium-low heat for 1–2 minutes until a light spice aroma is released. Remove the spices. Add 360g of old rock sugar, stir until completely dissolved, then turn off the heat. The sugar water must be allowed to cool naturally to room temperature; hot sugar water will soften the radish.
5. **Initial vinegar mixing**: Return the drained radish slices to a clean glass bowl. Pour in 250g of red vinegar. Mix evenly with your hands so the radish first absorbs the sourness.
6. **Prepare the final pickling mixture**: Pour the cooled sugar water into the bowl with the radish, ensuring it mostly covers the radish. Add the pickled chili peppers and their liquid, sliced garlic, sliced ginger, cilantro sections, and red chili pepper rings. Finally, sprinkle in about 15g of salt. Mix thoroughly. The salt balances the sweetness and accelerates flavor absorption.
7. **Seal and pickle**: Seal the bowl mouth tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate or place in a cool, shaded area. Pickle for 3–4 hours or longer, until the radish becomes semi-translucent and fully absorbs the liquid. The finished product has a sweet, sour, and crunchy texture, with the slight spiciness of pickled peppers and compound spice flavors.

## Health Advice  
Pickling white radish with the skin retains a crunchy texture and more dietary fiber, making it suitable as an appetizer or a side dish to cut grease. However, it is high in sugar and salt. For those on a weight loss or blood pressure control regimen, it is recommended to consume only a few slices per serving.
