# Tomato-Flavored Home-Style Hot Pot
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork Liver 1 small piece (approx. 200g, sliced thinly)  
🧠 Pork Brain 1 piece  
🥩 Beef Rolls 1 box  
🍥 Fish Roe Bags 1 bag  
🍅 Tomatoes 2 pieces (cut into wedges)  
🥬 Greens 1 bunch (approx. 200g)  
🍜 Rice Noodles 1 block  
🧅 Scallions about 3 stalks (cut into sections)  
🍲 Tomato-Flavored Hot Pot Base 1 pack  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔌 Rice Cooker  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🪓 Cutting Board  
🥄 Strainer  
🪣 Water Basin  
🧺 Colander  
🍽️ Bowls and Plates  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Ingredient Pre-processing**
   - Place the pork liver in a colander, rinse repeatedly under running water until no blood remains, drain, slice thinly, and set aside in a bowl.
     **Key Point**: The pork liver must be thoroughly washed to avoid residual blood affecting the flavor; slicing thinly shortens cooking time and facilitates quick flavor absorption.
   - Place the pork brain in a basin of water, gently rinse under a soft stream to remove surface impurities, then remove and drain.
   - Wash the tomatoes, greens, and scallions with clean water; remove the tomato stems and cut into wedges, cut the scallions into long sections, and trim the roots off the greens.
     **Note**: When washing greens, focus on scrubbing the root ends for dirt.
   - Unwrap the fish roe bags and rice noodles, and place them separately into bowls or plates.

2. **Making the Hot Pot Broth**
   - Pour the entire tomato-flavored hot pot base into the rice cooker inner pot, add water up to about 2/3 of the pot's capacity, and stir until the base is fully dissolved.
   - Start the rice cooker on the "Cook" mode and heat until the broth comes to a boil.
   - Once the broth turns a uniform bright red tomato color with no lumps of base and shows continuous bubbling, it is ready for cooking.
     **Key Point**: The amount of water can be adjusted based on the number of diners, ensuring it ultimately covers all ingredients.

3. **Cooking the Ingredients**
   - After the broth boils, first add the tomato wedges and scallion sections, cook for about 5 minutes, until the tomatoes soften and the broth releases a sweet and sour aroma.
   - Add the fish roe bags and pork brain, cook for about 3–4 minutes, until the fish roe bags float and the pork brain is fully set and cooked through.
   - Add the sliced pork liver, cook for about 1 minute, until the liver is completely discolored.
     **Note**: Do not overcook the pork liver, as it will turn tough and hard.
   - Add the greens, cook for about 2 minutes, until the leaves soften while remaining bright green.
   - Finally, add the beef rolls and rice noodles, cook for about 2 minutes, until the beef rolls are fully discolored and the rice noodles can be easily snapped with chopsticks.
     **Key Point**: Quick-cooking ingredients like pork liver and beef rolls should be added last; the cooking time for rice noodles can be adjusted based on personal preference for softness, adding 1 minute for a softer texture.

## Health Suggestions  
Tomato hot pot is rich in vitamin C and iron, while pork liver and pork brain have blood-nourishing and brain-boosting benefits. However, they are high in cholesterol, so individuals with high blood lipids, blood pressure, or blood sugar should consume them in moderation. When cooking pork liver, control the time carefully to avoid overcooking, which leads to a tough texture.
