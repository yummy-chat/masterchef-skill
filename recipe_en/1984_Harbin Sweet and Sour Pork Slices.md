# Harbin Sweet and Sour Pork Slices
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin (pork sirloin), as needed  
🥔 Potato starch, about half the weight of the meat (soak for at least 4 hours in advance, use the thick part)  
🥕 Carrot, julienned, as needed  
🫚 Ginger, julienned, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, sliced, as needed  
🧅 Scallion, julienned, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, cut into sections, as needed  
🍶 Huadiao wine, as needed (for marinating)  
🧂 Salt, large amount (for washing meat) + small amount (for marinating) + 2g (for sauce)  
🥚 Egg white, small amount (optional, for tenderizing meat; debated in video)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (to seal in moisture and prevent sticking)  
🍬 White sugar, 40g  
🍶 White vinegar, 45g  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 5g  
🔖 Sesame oil, 2g  
⚠️ Cooking wine **must not be added** to the sauce (explicitly emphasized by the blogger)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Large deep-fry pan (or iron wok)  
🥄 Large slotted spoon  
🥘 Wok spatula  
🥣 Sauce bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Work
1. Slice the meat: Cut the pork tenderloin into slices about the thickness of a one-yuan coin.
2. Wash the meat: Rub the meat slices with a large amount of salt, then immediately rinse thoroughly under running water. Soak for at least 3 hours until the meat turns white.
3. Marinate the meat: Drain the meat slices, add Huadiao wine and a small amount of salt, and mix well. Optionally add a small amount of egg white.
4. Coat with starch: Add the settled thick wet starch to the meat at a ratio of meat to starch of approximately 2:1. Mix to coat evenly.
5. Seal with oil: Drizzle vegetable oil over the coated meat slices and gently mix.
6. Prepare the sauce: Mix 40g white sugar, 45g white vinegar, 5g light soy sauce, 2g salt, and 2g sesame oil. Add the julienned carrot and ginger. **Important: Do not add cooking wine to the sauce.**
### Cooking
1. First fry: Heat oil in the pan to about 50% hot (approximately 160°C / 320°F). Add the meat slices one by one. Remove them after about 3 seconds to set the coating.
2. Second fry: Raise the oil temperature to about 70% hot (approximately 180°C / 355°F). Add all the first-fried meat slices back in and fry for about 10 seconds until the coating is golden and crispy. Quickly remove and drain excess oil.
3. Combine and sauce: Leave a small amount of oil in the pan and heat it. Immediately add the sliced garlic, julienned scallion, and cilantro sections. Pour in the fried meat slices and the prepared sauce.
4. Stir-fry quickly for a few seconds until the meat slices are evenly coated with the sauce. Remove from the pan immediately. Work quickly to prevent the meat from becoming soggy.

## Health Advice  
Sweet and sour pork slices are high in sugar, oil, and calories. They are suitable for occasional consumption. Diabetics and those on a weight-loss diet should exercise caution. Rubbing the meat slices with salt effectively removes any unpleasant smell. The potato starch needs to be soaked for at least 4 hours in advance.
