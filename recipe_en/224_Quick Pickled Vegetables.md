# Quick Pickled Vegetables
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Mustard greens as needed (can substitute with napa cabbage or baby bok choy)  
💧 Water as needed (for blanching and preparing brine)  
🧂 Table salt as needed  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (pre-washed and sanitized with boiling water)  
🍲 Pot  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍚 Bowl (to invert and press down on vegetables)  

## Instructions
1.  Rinse mustard greens leaf by leaf until clean, ensuring no sand or sediment remains.
2.  Sanitize the stainless steel basin thoroughly by rinsing with boiling water.
3.  Bring water in a pot to a rolling boil, then blanch the mustard greens for a few seconds.
   - **Note**: Remove immediately once the leaves turn bright green; over-blanching will make them mushy.
4.  Add an appropriate amount of table salt to the hot water used for blanching, stirring until fully dissolved to create a brine.
5.  Set the brine and the blanched greens aside separately, **allowing both to cool completely**.
   - **Note**: Hot greens or brine will cause the pickles to spoil and develop a poor texture.
6.  Place the cooled greens into the sanitized basin, pour in the cooled brine, ensuring the liquid completely covers the greens.
7.  Lay a pair of chopsticks horizontally across the greens, then invert a bowl on top to press down, keeping the vegetables fully submerged in the brine.
8.  Place the basin in a cool, dark location. Allow to ferment at room temperature for 2 days before serving.
   - **Note**: Avoid opening the lid frequently during fermentation to maintain a stable anaerobic environment.

## Health Tips
This quick pickle is low in fat and high in fiber, making it a great palate cleanser for rich or greasy foods. However, it has a relatively high salt content, so it should be consumed in moderation by those with high blood pressure. Due to the short fermentation time, nitrite levels are relatively controlled, but it is still advisable not to eat too much at once.
