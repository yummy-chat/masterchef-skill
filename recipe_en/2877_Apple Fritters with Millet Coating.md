# Apple Fritters with Millet Coating
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍎 Apple –适量（peeled, cored, and cut into even small cubes）  
🌾 Millet –适量（rinsed）  
🌽 Cornstarch –适量（used in two batches, for coating millet and apple separately）  
🥃 Cooking oil –适量（for frying and making syrup）  
🧂 Salt – 2 grams（to soak apples and prevent oxidation）  
💧 Water – one tablespoon（for making syrup）  
🍬 White granulated sugar – one large tablespoon  
🌿 White sesame seeds –适量（for final aroma）  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Peeler, kitchen knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon, ladle  
🧻 Paper towels  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Instructions  
1. Peel and core the apple, cut into evenly sized small cubes, place in a bowl, cover with water, and add 2 grams of salt. Soak for 5 minutes to prevent discoloration.  
*Note: Saltwater helps maintain the apple’s color; cube size should be uniform to ensure even cooking.*  

2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the rinsed millet, and cook for 3 minutes until 80% done. Drain, rinse under cold water, and pat dry with paper towels. Transfer the millet to a bowl, and gradually add cornstarch while tossing with your hands, ensuring each grain is evenly coated.  
*Key: Do not overcook the millet, or it will not be crispy after frying; make sure it is thoroughly dry before coating to prevent clumps.*  

3. Heat cooking oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Sprinkle the coated millet into the oil and let it sit for 3–5 seconds to set, then gently stir to break apart any clumps. Fry over medium heat for 3 minutes until it makes a crisp sound when tapped and separates easily. Remove and drain on paper towels.  
*Heat control: 170°C, medium heat for 3 minutes; let it set before stirring to avoid breaking the grains.*  

4. Drain the soaked apple cubes, add cornstarch, and toss to coat each piece thinly. Shake off excess starch using a slotted spoon, and let sit for a moment to allow the starch to absorb moisture. When the oil reaches 170°C (340°F), scatter the apple cubes in and let them set, then gently stir. Fry over medium heat for 1 minute until light golden and crisp. Remove and drain.  
*Tip: Remove excess starch to prevent oil from getting cloudy; do not stir too early to avoid losing the coating; maintain medium heat—low heat may cause the coating to fall off.*  

5. In the wok, add one tablespoon of water and one large tablespoon of sugar. Drizzle a little oil along the edge, and heat over medium heat. The syrup will first turn milky white with small bubbles. Continue heating until it turns the color of sesame oil, then immediately turn off the heat.  
*Temperature check: Sesame oil color is the ideal stage for pulling threads; adding sugar too early or too late will affect the result; avoid stirring too often to prevent crystallization.*  

6. Add the fried apple cubes to the syrup, sprinkle in white sesame seeds, and quickly toss to coat each piece. Immediately pour into the fried millet, and toss rapidly to evenly coat the apple pieces with millet. Transfer to a plate and serve.  
*Key: Work quickly while the syrup is still warm; ensure even coating so each apple piece is covered with golden millet.*
