# Snowflake Hawthorn Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍎 Hawthorn 1 jin (500g) (wash and thoroughly dry the surface moisture)  
🍬 Rock sugar 250g  
💧 Water 250ml  
🍶 White vinegar a few drops (added after the sugar syrup is boiled, before turning off the heat)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🍽️ Stone slab or plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. After washing the hawthorns, use kitchen paper towels to dry the surface moisture of each one individually, ensuring no residual moisture remains.  
   **Key point**: The surface moisture of the hawthorns must be thoroughly dried, otherwise it will affect the sugar coating effect.  

2. Add rock sugar and water to the wok. Heat over medium heat, stirring continuously until the rock sugar is completely dissolved and forms a clear sugar syrup.  

3. Reduce to medium-low heat and continue boiling while stirring constantly. Total time is about 7–8 minutes. Observe the state changes during this period:  
   - At about 1 minute, small bubbles begin to appear;  
   - At about 4 minutes, the bubbles increase and become larger;  
   - At about 7 minutes, the bubbles become dense and fine, the sugar syrup drips in a thick consistency and is transparent overall.  
   **Key point**: Do not boil until the sugar syrup turns white; otherwise, it indicates overcooking and the sugar will not crystallize.  

4. Add a few drops of white vinegar to the boiled transparent sugar syrup, stir quickly to combine, and immediately turn off the heat.  

5. Remove the wok from the heat and let it sit to cool for about 2 minutes without stirring, allowing the sugar syrup to cool to lukewarm, with a shiny and calm surface.  
   **Key point**: If the sugar syrup temperature is too high, it will scald the hawthorns and cause them to release water, affecting the sugar coating.  

6. Pour the dried hawthorns into the sugar syrup. Use a wooden spatula to gently stir continuously, ensuring each hawthorn is evenly coated with the sugar syrup.  

7. Continue stirring. The sugar syrup will gradually change from a sticky and wet state to a white, dry, crystallized state, until the surface of the hawthorns is completely wrapped in a snow-white sugar coating and each one is distinct.  

8. Remove from the wok and plate. Serve and enjoy.  

## Health Advice  
This hawthorn ball is sweet and sour, stimulating the appetite. It is rich in pectin and vitamin C, suitable for digestion and relieving greasiness in autumn. Note that the sugar content is high; people controlling sugar intake should consume in moderation. When making, be sure to thoroughly dry the surface moisture of the hawthorns, otherwise the sugar coating may fail.
