# Crayfish Tofu  
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦞 Crayfish (a specific freshwater crustacean from Northeast China; devein, collect the head roe and body meat)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🥬 Chives, to taste (finely chopped)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🫗 Sesame oil, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl (for washing, filtering, and serving soup)  
🫙 Glass bowl or meat grinder (for grinding crayfish meat)  
🧻 Cheesecloth (for filtering juice)  
🍳 Iron pot or large pot  
🥄 Spatula  
🔍 Toothpick (for deveining)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Wash the crayfish thoroughly. Hold the middle tail fin at the tail, gently twist and pull out the vein. Separate the head from the body, carefully squeeze out the yellow roe from the head and collect it. Discard the head shell and set the body aside.  
2. Put the processed crayfish bodies into a meat grinder or food processor and blend into a very fine paste.  
3. Place a layer of cheesecloth over an empty bowl, pour in the crayfish paste, and squeeze the cheesecloth tightly to filter the pink juice into the bowl. Continue until no more juice can be extracted. Discard the shell residue in the cheesecloth and keep the juice.  
4. Heat oil in a pot, add scallions and stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Pour the filtered crayfish juice into the pot. Gently and slowly push from the bottom of the pot with a spatula to prevent sticking. As it heats, the juice will gradually coagulate into pink, flocculent "tofu."  
6. Once the juice has mostly coagulated, add the collected roe.  
7. After the soup comes to a boil, add the chopped chives, salt, and MSG. Drizzle with sesame oil, stir well, then turn off the heat and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This crayfish tofu dish is high in protein and low in fat. The roe and juice are rich in calcium and umami flavor, making it suitable for spring nourishing or for those needing to boost energy. Note that crayfish is slightly cold in nature, so people with weak spleen and stomach should not eat too much. The shells must be thoroughly filtered to avoid a rough texture.
