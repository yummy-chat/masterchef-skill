# Spanish Mackerel Clay Pot (Zhe Zhe Pot)

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Spanish mackerel, about 200g (boneless fillet, cut into pieces)  
🧅 Small shallots, to taste (finely diced)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (finely diced)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (finely diced)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (finely diced)  
🌿 Celery/cilantro, to taste (cut into sections, added last)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, to taste (sliced into rings, added last)  
🍶 Hoisin sauce, 1 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tbsp  
🧂 Sea salt, to taste  
⚫ Black pepper, to taste  
🍶 Guangdong rice wine/cooking wine, 1 tbsp (for marinating) + to taste (for cooking)  
🥢 Light soy sauce, 1 tbsp  
🫒 Oil, to taste  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥘 Clay pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥄 Spatula  

## Instructions  
1. **Marinate the fish**: In a bowl, mix hoisin sauce, oyster sauce, sea salt, black pepper, rice wine, and light soy sauce. Gently toss the mackerel pieces in the mixture.  
   **Key point**: Handle gently to avoid breaking the fish.  

2. **Sauté the aromatics**: Heat oil in the clay pot over medium heat. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, and shallots. Stir-fry until the edges turn golden brown and fragrant.  
   **Note**: Chop the aromatics slightly larger; frying them thoroughly is the foundation of the clay pot’s flavor.  

3. **Layer the fish and braise**: Spread a layer of celery sections over the aromatics, then neatly arrange the marinated fish pieces on top. Pour the leftover sauce from the bowl over the fish. Cover the pot, drizzle rice wine along the rim, and cook over high heat for 4–5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Adding wine releases aroma and speeds up braising.  

4. **Add garnishes**: After uncovering, gently turn the fish pieces to coat them evenly with the sauce. Add bird's eye chili, celery, or cilantro.  
   **Note**: Garnishes cook quickly, so add them at the final stage.  

5. **Final seasoning**: Toss everything together. Optionally, drizzle a little more Guangdong rice wine along the pot rim, then serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This clay pot dish features tender fish and a rich, savory sauce, making it suitable for those needing high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids. Pair with light vegetables to balance saltiness, and be mindful of the sodium content in hoisin sauce and oyster sauce.
