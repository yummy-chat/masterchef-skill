# Sweet and Sour Shrimp Paste Eggplant Sandwich
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 1 eggplant  
🍤 Shrimp paste, as needed  
🌾 1 bowl of starch (for batter)  
🌾 Half a bowl of flour  
💧 1 bowl of water  
🧂 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce  
🦪 Half a tablespoon of oyster sauce  
🍬 1 tablespoon of sugar  
🍶 2 tablespoons of white vinegar  
🍅 2 tablespoons of ketchup  
🌾 1 tablespoon of starch (for sauce)  
💧 Half a bowl of water (for sauce)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🌿 A little white sesame seeds or chopped scallions  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl/basin  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok/frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔗 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the eggplant into sandwich slices by slicing twice without cutting through and cutting through on the third cut. Fill the slits with shrimp paste, pressing firmly to fill completely.  
2. In a bowl, sequentially add 1 bowl of starch, half a bowl of flour, and 1 bowl of water. Stir with chopsticks until the batter is smooth, even, and free of lumps.  
3. In a bowl, pre-mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, white vinegar, ketchup, 1 tablespoon of starch, and half a bowl of water. Stir until all seasonings are evenly combined to make the sweet and sour sauce. Set aside.  
4. Evenly coat the eggplant sandwiches filled with shrimp paste in the batter. When the oil temperature reaches 60% heat (around 180°C), add the eggplant sandwiches. Fry over medium heat for 5 minutes until set, floating, and lightly golden. Remove. Raise the oil temperature to 70% heat (around 200°C), then re-fry the eggplant sandwiches for about 30 seconds until the surface turns golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Note**: The first frying ensures the interior is fully cooked, while the second frying makes the exterior crispier.  
5. Leave a little base oil in the pan. Sauté scallions until fragrant, then pour in the pre-mixed sweet and sour sauce. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce bubbles and thickens enough to coat a spoon.  
6. Quickly and evenly pour the hot sauce over the fried eggplant sandwiches. Garnish with white sesame seeds or chopped scallions and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Eggplant paired with shrimp paste offers a crispy exterior and tender interior, but the frying and sweet and sour sauce are high in calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for family gatherings or entertaining friends, but be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive oil intake.
