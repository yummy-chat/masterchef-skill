# Xiao An Braised Chicken  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken, appropriate amount (cut into pieces, soak in water for half an hour)  
🥚 Century egg, 1 (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Ginger slices, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount  
🫑 Green Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion whites, appropriate amount  
🫑 Spiral chili peppers, about 2 (cut into segments)  
🥜 Crushed peanuts, appropriate amount  
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (for frying)  
🫘 Sweet bean sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), 1 tablespoon  
🫘 Soybean paste, 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking wine, 2 caps  
🍾 Vinegar, 1 tablespoon  
🧴 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🧴 Sesame oil, 1 tablespoon  
🍺 Beer, about 1 bottle  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍵 Small bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Rinse the chicken pieces twice, soak in clean water for half an hour, drain and set aside; cut the century egg into pieces; cut the spiral chili peppers into segments; slice the ginger; cut the scallion whites into segments; prepare the dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, green Sichuan peppercorns, and crushed peanuts.  
2. Prepare the braising sauce: In a small bowl, combine sweet bean sauce, doubanjiang, soybean paste, cooking wine, vinegar, dark soy sauce, and sesame oil, and stir until well mixed.  
3. Pour an adequate amount of vegetable oil into the pan. Once the oil is hot, add the century egg pieces and fry over medium heat until the surface becomes wrinkly, then remove and drain the oil.  
   **Key Point**: Frying the century eggs until they have a tiger-skin texture helps them better integrate with the sauce.  
4. Leave a base oil in the pan. First, stir-fry the ginger slices until fragrant, then add the drained chicken pieces. Pan-fry over high heat for about 3 minutes, stirring continuously, until the oil in the pan turns from cloudy to clear and the chicken has released its moisture.  
5. Add the dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and green Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry briefly. Pour in the prepared braising sauce and stir-fry until the chicken is evenly coated with the sauce.  
6. Pour in the beer, and add a small amount of hot water if needed to just submerge the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, add a pinch of sugar, then reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover, and braise for 20 minutes.  
7. Uncover, add the fried century eggs, and turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, taste and add a pinch of salt if needed.  
8. Add the crushed peanuts, scallion white segments, and spiral chili pepper segments. Stir-fry over high heat until the chili peppers are just cooked through, then remove from the pan.  
   **Key Point**: Do not stir-fry the spiral chili peppers for too long; keep them crisp and tender.  

## Health Advice  
This braised chicken is rich in high-quality protein. The addition of century eggs and chili peppers enhances the texture and layers of flavor, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost. The sauce has a high salt content, so individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. It is recommended to serve with rice to balance the saltiness.
