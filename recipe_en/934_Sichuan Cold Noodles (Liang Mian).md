# Sichuan Cold Noodles (Liang Mian)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Alkaline noodles (choose round cold noodle noodles)
🥒 Cucumber (shredded)
🌱 Soybean sprouts (blanched)

🧂 Soybean soy sauce (for making compound soy sauce, preferably brewed soy sauce)
🍬 Brown sugar (ratio to soy sauce approximately 1:0.8)
🌿 Spices (star anise, cinnamon, sand ginger, bay leaves, cardamom, fennel seeds, for boiling soy sauce)
🫚 Ginger slices (for boiling soy sauce)
🧅 Scallion segments (for boiling soy sauce)
🧄 Single-clove garlic (mashed into paste, then mixed with cold boiled water to make garlic water)
🧂 Aromatic vinegar
🌶️ Chili oil (better with sediment)
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder
🧂 Salt
🧂 MSG
🥜 Crushed peanuts
🧅 Chopped scallions
🫒 Rapeseed oil (for tossing noodles, to prevent sticking and make noodles smooth)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪣 Large stainless steel tray
🥢 Long chopsticks
🪭 Fan
🍲 Clay pot (or stainless steel pot, for boiling soy sauce)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🧄 Garlic mortar and pestle
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Seasoning bowls

## Steps
1. Add a small amount of water to a clay pot or stainless steel pot. Add ginger slices, scallion segments, star anise, cinnamon, sand ginger, bay leaves, cardamom, fennel seeds, soybean soy sauce, and chopped brown sugar. Simmer over low heat for at least 40 minutes until the spice flavor fully infuses the soy sauce. Strain out the solids and let cool for later use.
**Key point**: Ratio of soy sauce to brown sugar is approximately 1:0.8. Avoid using an iron pot to prevent the soy sauce from turning black.

2. Place single-clove garlic in a mortar and grind into a fine paste. Add an appropriate amount of cold boiled water and stir to make garlic water. Set aside.
3. Blanch soybean sprouts in boiling water until just cooked, then remove and let cool naturally. Wash cucumber and shred.
4. Fill a large pot with plenty of water and bring to a rolling boil. Add the alkaline noodles and immediately stir with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Boil over high heat until the water returns to a boil and the noodles are almost fully cooked but still slightly firm. Add half a ladle of cold water to cool. When the water boils again and the noodles are cooked through, use a slotted spoon to remove and place directly into a large stainless steel tray.
**Key point**: Adding cold water during cooking gives the noodles a chewy texture and prevents them from being undercooked inside.

5. Immediately drizzle with the prepared rapeseed oil. Use long chopsticks to quickly lift and flip the noodles while using a fan to cool them, so the noodles become glossy and smooth.
**Key point**: Lift and fan simultaneously to quickly cool the noodles, achieving a smooth and glossy texture.

6. Place an appropriate amount of fanned cold noodles into a bowl. Add the blanched soybean sprouts, shredded cucumber, salt, MSG, garlic water, Sichuan peppercorn powder, cooled compound soy sauce, aromatic vinegar, chili oil, crushed peanuts, and chopped scallions. Mix thoroughly with chopsticks and serve.
**Key point**: It is recommended to add chili oil last. The seasoning order can be adjusted slightly according to taste.

## Health Tips
This Sichuan cold noodle dish is high in oil and carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with boiled chicken breast or cold-tossed seaweed to balance nutrition. Those with excessive stomach acid should use less aromatic vinegar. Although Sichuan peppercorn powder can stimulate the taste buds, it should not be used excessively.
