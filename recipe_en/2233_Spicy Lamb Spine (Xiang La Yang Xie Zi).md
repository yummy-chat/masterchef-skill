# Spicy Lamb Spine (Xiang La Yang Xie Zi)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐑 Lamb spine 1500g (soak in cold water for 2-3 hours in advance, changing water 1-2 times)
🥕 White radish 300-500g (peeled, cut into rolling knife chunks)
🧅 Scallion 40g (cut into diagonal slices)
🫚 Ginger 20g (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 30g (8 cloves, smashed flat)
🌶️ Dried chilies 30g (mainly lantern chilies, with small hot chilies for heat)
🫑 Green Sichuan pepper 5g (can substitute with red Sichuan pepper)
🧂 Spice packet: Nutmeg 3g, licorice 3g, sand ginger 3g, bay leaf 1g, dried tangerine peel to taste, Sichuan pepper to taste, cinnamon to taste, cumin to taste, fennel to taste, codonopsis to taste (place in a gauze bag and rinse clean)
🌿 Cilantro to taste (sprinkle before serving)

**Red Broth Base**:
🫘 Pixian chili bean paste 50g (choose the red oil variety)
🫘 La Mei Zi sauce 20g (can substitute with equal amount of sweet bean paste)
🫑 Red chopped chili 20g (try not to substitute)
🫘 Fermented black beans 8g (mashed)
🫘 Red fermented bean curd 14g (1 piece, mashed)

**Bowl Sauce**:
🧂 Light soy sauce 15g
🦪 Oyster sauce 15g
🧂 Dark soy sauce 8g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 8g
🧂 MSG 4g
🍬 Rock sugar 20g (can substitute with 20g white sugar)

🍾 High-proof baijiu about 30g (50% ABV or higher, for blanching and stir-frying)
🫒 Cooking oil about 160g
🧂 Salt about 5g (season before serving)
💧 Water 1500ml

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Soup ladle/stir-fry spatula
🥣 Small bowls (2, for mixing red broth base and bowl sauce separately)
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🥄 Slotted spoon
🛍️ Gauze bags (2)
🍳 Wooden lid
🔪 Cutting board, knife
⚖️ Digital scale

## Steps
1. **Prepare lamb spine**: Soak lamb spine in cold water for 2-3 hours, changing water 1-2 times, then drain after blood water is released.
**Key**: Soak thoroughly in cold water to remove blood water.

2. **Prepare vegetables and aromatics**: Peel white radish and cut into rolling knife chunks; cut scallion into diagonal slices; slice ginger; smash garlic flat; prepare dried chilies and green Sichuan pepper.
**Key**: Dried chilies should be mainly lantern chilies for fragrance rather than heat.

3. **Prepare spice packet**: Combine nutmeg, licorice, sand ginger, bay leaf, dried tangerine peel, Sichuan pepper, cinnamon, cumin, fennel, and codonopsis in the specified proportions, place in a gauze bag, and rinse clean with water.
4. **Prepare red broth base**: In a bowl, mix Pixian chili bean paste, La Mei Zi sauce, red chopped chili, mashed fermented black beans, and mashed red fermented bean curd, stirring until well combined.
**Key**: Red chopped chili and La Mei Zi sauce significantly affect the dish’s color and aroma; try not to substitute them.

5. **Prepare bowl sauce**: In another bowl, mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, chicken bouillon, MSG, and rock sugar, stirring until well combined.
**Key**: Do not add salt yet; season to taste before serving.

6. **Blanch to remove gamey smell**: Place the soaked lamb spine in cold water, add about 20g high-proof baijiu, and bring to a boil over high heat. Stir the bottom when the water heats up, and start skimming off foam before the water boils. After boiling, continue skimming for about 3 minutes until the broth is clear with no foam.
**Heat & Timing**: Boil over high heat for about 3-4 minutes.
   **State Check**: The foam changes from pink to gray-white, and the broth becomes relatively clear.
   **Key**: Use enough water; start with cold water and do not add scallion or ginger; skimming before the water boils achieves cleaner results; after blanching, swish the lamb spine in clean broth and remove—do not rinse with water.

7. **Stir-fry the base**:
   a. Clean and dry the wok, pour in about 160g cooking oil.
   b. Add scallion, ginger, and garlic, stir-fry over high heat until aromatic, then reduce to medium heat and fry until edges are golden and fragrant.
   c. Turn to the lowest heat, add the prepared red broth base, stir constantly, and fry for about 1 minute until red oil releases and the aroma develops.
   d. Add dried chilies and green Sichuan pepper, stir-fry over the lowest heat first, then increase to medium heat to release fragrance.
   e. Drizzle a ladle of high-proof baijiu along the edge of the wok to enhance aroma.
**Heat & Timing**: Pay attention to heat adjustments throughout; the sauce must be stir-fried over the lowest heat.
   **State Check**: The base is fragrant, the oil is bright red, and the dried chilies and Sichuan pepper release their aroma.
   **Key**: Use enough oil; fry scallion, ginger, and garlic until golden; stir-fry the sauce slowly over low heat to prevent burning.

8. **Begin braising**:
   a. Add 1500ml water to the stir-fried base and bring to a boil over high heat.
   b. After boiling, pour in the prepared bowl sauce and stir well.
   c. Add the spice packet and the blanched lamb spine, spreading it evenly.
   d. Once it returns to a boil, cover with the lid, reduce to the lowest heat, and braise for 50 minutes.
**Heat & Timing**: After bringing to a boil over high heat, reduce to the lowest heat and braise for 50 minutes.

9. **Add vegetable and continue braising**:
   a. After 50 minutes, remove the lid, add white radish chunks, and toss to combine.
   b. Bring to a boil again over high heat, cover, reduce to the lowest heat, and continue braising for 30 minutes.
**Heat & Timing**: After adding the radish, braise over the lowest heat for another 30 minutes.

10. **Season and serve**:
    a. After the time is up, remove the lid and taste the broth for saltiness.
    b. Add salt to taste as needed.
    c. Sprinkle with cilantro segments and serve.
**Key**: Salt must be added at the end based on actual taste.

## Health Advice
Lamb spine is rich in collagen and calcium, making it suitable for winter nourishment. However, the red oil is high in calories, so pair it with white radish to aid digestion. Those with high blood pressure should control sodium intake and consume less broth.
