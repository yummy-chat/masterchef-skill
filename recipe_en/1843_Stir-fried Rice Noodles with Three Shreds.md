# Stir-fried Rice Noodles with Three Shreds
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥡 Dried rice noodles, as needed  
🥩 Char siu (barbecue pork), as needed (shredded)  
🍖 Sandwich ham, as needed (shredded)  
🥚 1 egg (beaten)  
🌱 Mung bean sprouts, as needed (essential ingredient)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (shredded)  
🌿 Chinese chives, as needed (cut into sections)  
🧄 Scallions, as needed (use green parts, cut into sections)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a little  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (iron)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking noodles)  
🥢 Strainer  
🔥 Gas stove (high heat)  

## Instructions  
1. Soak dried rice noodles in warm water (about 50°C) for 20 minutes until softened. Remove and drain thoroughly.  
2. Shred char siu, ham, and onion. Cut Chinese chives and scallions into sections. Wash and drain mung bean sprouts. Beat the egg and set aside.  
3. Heat a wok over high heat, add a little oil, pour in the beaten egg, then reduce heat to low and tilt the wok to spread into a thin egg crepe. Remove and shred for later use.  
4. Add more cooking oil to the wok and heat to about 160°C. Quickly stir-fry the shredded char siu and ham, then immediately remove with a strainer to drain excess oil.  
5. Using the remaining oil in the wok, stir-fry mung bean sprouts and shredded onion over high heat for about 5 seconds. Remove immediately to keep the sprouts crisp.  
6. Add a little oil to the wok, swirl to coat, then pour out excess oil, leaving a thin layer. Add the drained rice noodles and immediately turn to high heat. Stir-fry while adding light soy sauce for aroma and a little dark soy sauce for color. Continue stir-frying over high heat until the noodles are evenly colored and develop wok hei. Do not add salt at any point.  
7. Add the previously stir-fried bean sprouts, onion, char siu, and ham. Stir-fry over high heat to combine flavors.  
8. Add Chinese chive sections and scallion sections. Quickly stir-fry a few times until just cooked. Plate and top with shredded egg crepe. Optionally sprinkle with a little toasted white sesame seeds for extra aroma.  

## Health Tips
This stir-fried rice noodle dish is rich in protein and contains a small amount of vegetables, but it is also high in oil and sodium. It is suitable as an occasional filling meal. Pair it with a clear soup or a cold vegetable dish to balance nutrition. Those with high blood lipids or who are watching their salt intake should consume in moderation.
