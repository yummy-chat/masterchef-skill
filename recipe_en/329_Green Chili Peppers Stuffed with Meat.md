## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the green peppers**: Hold the pepper by the stem, push it inward, then pull it out completely to remove the core and seeds, leaving a hollow pepper.  
   *Note*: Be gentle to keep the pepper intact.  
2. **Make the filling**: In ground pork, add diced carrots, diced shiitake mushrooms, diced celery, chopped bird's eye chili, scallion-ginger water, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, matsutake seasoning, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, white sugar, and white pepper powder. Stir in one direction with chopsticks until the mixture becomes sticky and firm.  
   *Key*: Stirring until firm ensures a tight and tender texture.  
3. **Stuff and seal**: Use chopsticks to fill the hollow peppers with the meat mixture. Coat the opening with a thin layer of starch.  
   *Key*: Starch prevents the filling from falling out during frying and locks in moisture.  
4. **Pan-fry**: Heat oil in a frying pan over low heat. Place the stuffed peppers in the pan and fry slowly until the pepper skin develops a tiger-skin pattern (wrinkles and spots) and the filling is cooked through. Remove and set aside.  
   *Note*: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning.  
5. **Sauté aromatics**: Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the Jerusalem artichoke, fermented black beans, minced ginger, minced garlic, and chopped green onions. Sauté over low heat until fragrant.  
6. **Braise for flavor**: Return the fried peppers to the pan. Add light soy sauce, vinegar, white sugar, and hot water. Cook over medium-low heat until the sauce reduces and thickens enough to evenly coat the peppers.  
   *Key*: Thickening the sauce ensures it fully coats the peppers, enhancing the flavor.
