# Quick-Fried Pork Large Intestine

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Main Ingredients
🐷 Fresh pork large intestine (about 800g) (**Must** use morning-bought fresh intestines, not frozen)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into short sections)
🌱 Young ginger, to taste (julienned)
🌶️ Small red and green chili peppers, to taste (sliced into rings)
🫙 Pickled ginger, to taste (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (finely diced)
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers, to taste (minced)
🌶️ Wild pepper, to taste
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper, a few grains

### For Marinating
🧂 Baking soda, to taste (**Key**: mix well and let sit for 20 minutes to tenderize the intestines)

### For the Sauce (pre-mixed)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Chicken powder, to taste
🧂 White pepper, to taste (removes odor, adds aroma)
🍶 Vinegar, to taste
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste
🧂 Sichuan pepper oil, to taste (adds numbing sensation)
🧂 Cornstarch slurry, to taste (thickens the sauce)

### For Stir-Frying
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste (base oil)
🧈 Lard, about 20g (adds rich flavor)
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor), to taste (**Key**: pour along the wok edge to deglaze, removes odor and enhances aroma)
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste), to taste (stir-fry until red oil forms)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🕳️ Strainer
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
✂️ Scissors (to split the intestines)
🥣 Large bowls (multiple)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (to measure baking soda)

## Steps
1. **Selection and Cleaning**: Choose fresh pork large intestine. Rub repeatedly with salt, vinegar, and flour to remove surface mucus and impurities. Rinse thoroughly with clean water.
**Note**: Use freshly bought morning intestines that are still warm; frozen product cannot achieve a crispy texture.

2. **Prepare the Intestines**: Use scissors to split the intestines open along the middle, then cut into small pieces about 1 cm wide.
3. **Marinate**: Add baking soda to the intestine pieces, mix well, and let sit for **20 minutes**.
**Key**: Marinating with baking soda ensures the intestines remain tender and crispy after quick frying.

4. **Blanch and Cool**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the marinated intestine pieces and cook for about **2 minutes**. Remove and immediately plunge into ice water to cool rapidly. Drain well.
5. **Prepare Aromatics**: Cut scallions into short sections, julienne young ginger, slice small chili peppers into rings, finely dice pickled ginger and garlic, and mince pickled chili peppers. Place the diced pickled ginger, garlic, minced pickled chili peppers, wild pepper, and green Sichuan pepper together in a bowl and set aside.
6. **Mix the Sauce**: In a large bowl, combine scallion sections, julienned ginger, and chili pepper rings. Add salt, MSG, chicken powder, white pepper, vinegar, light soy sauce, Sichuan pepper oil, and cornstarch slurry. Stir until well combined.
7. **Heat the Oil**: Heat a wok over high heat, add rapeseed oil and lard. Heat until the oil shimmers and begins to smoke.
8. **Quick-Fry the Intestines**: Add the drained intestines and quickly stir-fry until they curl and turn white on the surface.
9. **Deglaze with Liquor**: Pour baijiu along the edge of the wok. The liquid may ignite; quickly stir-fry to let the liquor aroma penetrate the intestines.
**Key**: Pour baijiu along the wok edge to utilize high heat and enhance the aroma; this yields better odor removal.

10. **Fry the Aromatics**: Add Pixian doubanjiang and the prepared aromatics (pickled ginger, garlic, etc.). Stir-fry until red oil releases and a rich fragrance develops.
11. **Add the Sauce**: Pour all the prepared sauce into the wok. Keep the heat on high and quickly toss and stir-fry to evenly coat the ingredients.
12. **Plate and Serve**: When the sauce thickens and clings to the intestines, immediately turn off the heat. Transfer to a serving plate.
**Note**: From adding the sauce to plating, perform all steps in quick succession. Continuous high-heat stir-frying is key to maintaining the crispy texture.

## Health Advice
This quick-fried pork large intestine dish is rich in protein and fat-soluble vitamins but contains high cholesterol and sodium. It is suitable for occasional indulgence. Those with high blood pressure or high blood lipids should consume it sparingly.
