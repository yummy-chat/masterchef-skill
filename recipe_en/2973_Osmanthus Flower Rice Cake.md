# Osmanthus Flower Rice Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Rice (Japonica Rice) 300g (soaked overnight in advance)  
🍚 Glutinous Rice (Round Grain) 100g (soaked overnight in advance)  
🧁 Powdered Sugar (made from ground white sugar) Approx. 49g (16% of total flour weight)  
🌸 Sweet Osmanthus Syrup As needed (for decoration after steaming)  
🌼 Dried Osmanthus Flowers A small amount (for final decoration)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚙️ Dry Grinder  
🪥 Sieve (30-mesh and slightly larger mesh)  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
⚖️ Electronic Scale  
🔲 6-inch Square Mousse Mold (or similar)  
🧻 Cheesecloth/Steaming Cloth  
🍳 Steamer and Steaming Rack  
🔪 Scraper  
🥚 Egg Whisk  
🎀 Piping Bag  
🧤 Heat-Insulated Gloves  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Soak the rice and glutinous rice separately in plenty of water overnight to allow full absorption.  
   **Key Point**: Soaking time must be sufficient; it is recommended to do this the night before.  
2. Drain the soaked rice, then put it into a dry grinder to grind into a fine powder. Run the grinder for half a minute at a time, pause to stir, and repeat about 3 times until the powder reaches a moisture state where it forms a clump in the hand but crumbles at the slightest touch.  
   **Key Point**: Be careful to prevent the dry grinder from overheating; the moisture level of the powder is crucial—no additional water is needed.  
3. Sift the ground rice flour and glutinous rice flour separately through a 30-mesh sieve to remove coarse particles. Cover the sifted rice flour immediately with plastic wrap to prevent drying, then proceed with the glutinous rice flour.  
   **Key Point**: Using your hand to press the flour can improve sieving efficiency.  
4. Weigh out 250g of the sifted rice flour, 62g of the sifted glutinous rice flour, and 49g of powdered sugar. Mix quickly and evenly with an egg whisk.  
   **Key Point**: The mixture must be sifted immediately afterward to prevent the powdered sugar from absorbing moisture and clumping, which would make sieving difficult.  
5. Sift the mixed flour again, being patient to ensure the flour is fine and free of lumps.  
   **Key Point**: This step determines the softness and fineness of the finished product and is quite time-consuming.  
6. Place the mold lined with cheesecloth on the steaming rack. Gently fill it with the sifted flour mixture, using a scraper to lightly level the surface and fill the corners.  
   **Key Point**: Keep the flour naturally loose when filling; do not press it down.  
7. Use a scraper to directly cut the flour block in the mold into desired sizes.  
   **Key Point**: Cutting before steaming results in cleaner edges, and the cuts allow steam to penetrate evenly, ensuring thorough cooking.  
8. Once the water in the steamer is boiling and producing steam, place the mold inside and steam over high heat for about 15 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: Cover the steamer lid with a cloth to prevent condensation from dripping onto the cake surface.  
9. After steaming, gently poke a cake piece with a toothpick. If the cake feels elastic and holds together, it is fully cooked.  
10. Immediately pipe the sweet osmanthus syrup onto each piece of cake using a piping bag. Return the cake to the steamer and steam for another 1–2 minutes to fully infuse the osmanthus aroma.  
11. Remove from the steamer, sprinkle a small amount of dried osmanthus flowers on the surface for fragrance. Let it cool slightly, then lift the cheesecloth to take the cake out of the mold and serve.  
    **Key Point**: Best enjoyed while hot; leftover cake can be cooled, sealed, and frozen. Steam again to restore softness before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This osmanthus flower rice cake combines japonica rice and glutinous rice for a soft, non-sticky texture, making it suitable for moisturizing the lungs and nourishing the stomach in autumn. Those with high blood sugar should consume it in moderation.
