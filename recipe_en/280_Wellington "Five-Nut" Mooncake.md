# Wellington "Five-Nut" Mooncake
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 M9 beef tenderloin
🌾 Low-gluten flour 200g
🌾 High-gluten flour 50g
🍄 Button mushrooms, as needed
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, as needed
🍄 Porcini mushrooms, as needed
🧅 Shallots, as needed
🧄 Garlic, as needed
🍖 Ham slices, as needed
🍯 Mooncake syrup 180g
🧪 Alkaline water 3g
🥜 Peanut oil 20g
🧈 Butter, as needed
🧂 Sea salt, as needed
🌿 Yellow mustard, as needed
🥚 Egg wash, as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Blender/Food processor
🧩 Mooncake mold
♨️ Oven
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🎞️ Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. Wash and cut button mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, and porcini mushrooms into small pieces. Finely chop shallots and garlic. Cut mushrooms into small pieces for easier blending later.
**Key point**: Mushroom pieces should not be too large, otherwise the blending will be uneven.

2. Place the chopped mushrooms into a blender and blend into a fine paste.
**Key point**: No need to add water; rely on the mushrooms’ own moisture.

3. Melt an appropriate amount of butter in a frying pan, add chopped shallots and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
**Note**: Sauté over low heat to prevent burning.

4. Add the mushroom paste, turn to low heat, and stir-fry for about 15 minutes. Season with an appropriate amount of sea salt. Continue until the mushroom moisture is completely dried out, forming a thick, dry paste. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: The moisture must be thoroughly dried out, otherwise it may seep into the crust during wrapping.

5. In a bowl, mix 200g low-gluten flour and 50g high-gluten flour. Add 180g mooncake syrup, 3g alkaline water, and 20g peanut oil. First, mix into a flaky texture with chopsticks, then knead by hand into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 30 minutes. The dough has a high sugar content; after resting, knead it again to soften before use.
**Note**: Do not rest for too long, or the dough may become too soft.

6. Evenly sprinkle sea salt over the M9 beef tenderloin to season.
**Key point**: Grind the sea salt beforehand if needed for better adhesion.

7. Pour enough high-smoke-point oil into a frying pan and heat over high heat. Place the beef in and quickly sear all sides until browned, only a few tens of seconds per side. This step is only for searing and coloring; the interior should remain raw.
**Key point**: The oil temperature must be high enough to quickly lock in the juices.

8. Place the seared beef on paper towels to absorb oil and cool. Once the surface temperature has slightly dropped, evenly brush a thin layer of yellow mustard.
**Note**: Do not use too much mustard; just a thin layer for flavor.

9. Line the mooncake mold with a layer of plastic wrap. Layer ham slices and the dried mushroom paste sequentially.
**Key point**: Ham slices can be slightly overlapped to ensure the beef is completely wrapped.

10. Place the mustard-brushed beef on top of the mushroom paste. Use the plastic wrap to help wrap the beef with the ham and mushroom paste. Shape into a cylinder matching the mold. Place in the refrigerator to freeze for about 30 minutes to quickly set.
**Key point**: Freezing helps the filling hold its shape and prevents it from loosening when wrapped in the crust.

11. Remove the rested dough and knead it smooth. Divide into small portions and roll out into round wrappers of uniform thickness, large enough to fully encase the filling.
**Note**: The wrapper thickness should be even, with the sealing area slightly thicker to prevent breaking.

12. Remove the set filling and place it in the center of the wrapper. Gently push the wrapper upward using the tiger’s mouth technique to fully encase and seal the filling. Handle gently to avoid tearing.
**Key point**: Pinch the seal tightly to prevent leaking during baking.

13. Dust the mooncake blank and the mold with a little dry flour to prevent sticking. Place in the mold and press firmly to imprint the pattern.
**Note**: Do not use too much dry flour, or the pattern may be unclear.

14. Place the pressed mooncakes on a baking tray. Brush the surface with a thin layer of egg wash. Transfer to a preheated oven at 180-200°C and bake.
**Key point**: Brush the egg wash evenly for a beautiful golden color.

15. Bake until the crust surface is golden brown, then remove. Mainly observe the crust condition. The final doneness of the beef is determined by the initial temperature and total baking time.
**Note**: Baking time is about 15-20 minutes, adjust according to your oven’s characteristics.

16. After removing from the oven, let rest for about 5 minutes to “rest the meat,” allowing the juices inside the beef to redistribute, preventing them from leaking out when cut.
**Key point**: Do not skip the resting time, otherwise juices will seep out when slicing.

## Health Advice
This Wellington "Five-Nut" Mooncake creatively combines Western steak with Chinese mooncakes. However, M9 beef tenderloin is high in fat. It is recommended to pair it with sour plum soup or Pu'er tea to aid digestion. The high-sugar crust and ham slices are a high-sodium, high-sugar combination; individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation.
