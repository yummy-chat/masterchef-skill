# Sourdough Orange Peel Bread
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients
🍊 Orange peel (for making starter liquid, amount not specified)
🌾 Bread flour (as needed, for dough)
🍬 Candied orange peel (about half a small bowl, for filling)
💧 Water (as needed, for starter liquid and dough)
🧂 Salt (a pinch, to enhance dough flavor, not explicitly marked)

## Tools & Equipment
🫙 Glass jar (for culturing starter liquid)
🍞 Dough board/work surface (for kneading and shaping)
🔪 Bench scraper/dough cutter (for cutting and moving dough)
🧺 Banneton/proofing basket (for final fermentation and shaping)
🍳 Dutch oven (with lid, for baking)
🔥 Oven
📜 Parchment paper
✂️ Sharp blade (for scoring)

## Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Combine fresh orange peel with an appropriate amount of water and sugar in a sealed jar. Cultivate at a suitable temperature until a large number of active bubbles appear, forming the starter liquid.
2. Mix the activated starter liquid with bread flour and knead until the gluten is fully developed and a thin membrane can be pulled.
3. After the dough has fermented to an appropriate volume, gently pat to release gas. Evenly spread the candied orange peel, roll it up, and tightly seal the seam. Shape into a round or oval loaf.
4. Place the dough smooth side down into a well-floured proofing basket. Seal and refrigerate overnight.

**Baking Stage**
1. Preheat the oven together with the Dutch oven to a high temperature.
2. Invert the proofed dough onto parchment paper. Dust the surface lightly with flour and quickly score patterns with a sharp blade.
3. Place the dough along with the parchment paper into the preheated Dutch oven. Cover tightly with the lid.
4. Bake covered for about 20–25 minutes, then uncover and continue baking for 15–20 minutes, until the surface is deep golden brown and tapping the bottom produces a hollow sound.
   **Note**: Candied orange peel contains sugar and is prone to burning; localized darker spots are normal. Monitor the color throughout.
5. Remove from oven and cool completely on a wire rack before slicing. The finished bread has a crispy crust and a soft, chewy interior, with a subtle citrus fragrance and the sweet, moist taste of candied orange peel.

## Health Tips
It is recommended to serve with Greek yogurt to add protein and balance carbohydrate intake. The orange peel starter liquid contains natural fruit acids; those with excessive stomach acid should consume in moderation. Suitable as a dessert substitute during weight loss, but note that candied orange peel is relatively high in sugar—those monitoring sugar intake should watch portions.
