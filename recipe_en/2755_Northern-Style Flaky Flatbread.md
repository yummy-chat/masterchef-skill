# Northern-Style Flaky Flatbread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥖 High-gluten flour 1000g  
🍚 All-purpose flour 100g  
💧 Water 325g hot water + 325g cold water  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🌿 Chinese five-spice powder 1g  
🫘 Cold-pressed soybean oil 100g  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍲 Mixing bowl or stand mixer  
🪠 Rolling pin  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🔥 Electric griddle or frying pan  
🖌️ Oil brush  
👐 Tongs  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the all-purpose flour with the Chinese five-spice powder and set aside. Heat the cold-pressed soybean oil until it reaches 80% of its smoking point, then pour it into the flour mixture. Stir quickly until smooth, with no dry flour remaining, forming an oil paste.
**Note**: The oil must be hot enough, and stirring must be fast to ensure no lumps.

2. Dissolve the salt in the hot water, then mix with the cold water. First, pour the combined hot water mixture into the high-gluten flour and stir into flaky pieces, then add the cold water and knead into a smooth dough. Apply a small amount of oil to your hands while kneading to prevent sticking.
3. Divide the dough into portions of about 250g each. Roll each portion into a thin rectangular sheet and spread an even layer of oil paste on top. Roll it up from one end into a long strip. Gently stretch the strip, then roll both ends toward the center, stacking them on top of each other. Press into a flat, round shape, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 15 minutes.
4. Take a rested dough piece, press it flat, and use a push-and-pull motion to roll it into a thin round or square flatbread.
5. Preheat the electric griddle to 180°C on both top and bottom. Do not oil the pan. Place the flatbread on the griddle, cover, and cook for 1 minute. Open the lid, brush a layer of oil on the surface, flip it over, and continue cooking for about 30 seconds until both sides are golden brown and the bread puffs up.
6. Remove and serve immediately while hot. This flatbread is as thin as paper, soft yet chewy, and perfect for wrapping various dishes.

## Health Advice  
This flaky flatbread is relatively high in fat and refined carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and protein to balance the nutrition, making it suitable as an occasional staple. Be mindful of portion sizes per meal to avoid excessive calorie intake.
