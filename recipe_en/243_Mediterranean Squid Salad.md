# Mediterranean Squid Salad
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Mixed Salad Leaves (such as lettuce, arugula) - as needed
🍅 Cherry Tomatoes - as needed
🫑 Colorful Bell Peppers - as needed
🥒 Cucumber - as needed
🧅 Onion - as needed
🦑 Squid - as needed
🧀 Parmesan Cheese - as needed
🫒 Spanish Garlic-Stuffed Olives - as needed
🌿 Parsley - as needed
🍋 Lemon Juice - as needed
🫘 Capers - as needed
🧄 Minced Garlic - as needed
🧂 Salt - as needed
⚫ Black Pepper - as needed
🫒 Olive Oil - as needed
💧 Spanish Olive Brine (optional) - as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large Stainless Steel Bowl
🍳 Frying Pan
🥄 Strainer/Slotted Spoon
🥃 Glass Bowl
🔗 Kitchen Tongs
🧀 Grater
🌡️ Salt and Pepper Grinder

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the mixed salad leaves and drain well. Halve the cherry tomatoes, slice or julienne the colorful bell peppers, slice the cucumber, julienne the onion, and chop the parsley.
2. Clean the squid and cut into rings.
3. Add a little olive oil to a frying pan over medium heat. Pan-fry the bell pepper strips until char marks appear on the surface, then remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Pan-frying enhances the aroma of the bell peppers and adds depth of flavor.
4. Add a little more oil to the pan. Add the squid rings and pan-fry over medium heat until they curl, change color, and develop char marks. Remove and let cool.
5. In the large bowl, add the mixed salad leaves, then add the cherry tomatoes, pan-fried bell peppers, cucumber slices, onion strips, squid rings, garlic-stuffed olives, and chopped parsley. Grate the Parmesan cheese over the top.
6. Squeeze in the lemon juice, add the capers, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, and drizzle with olive oil. Gently toss with kitchen tongs until the ingredients are evenly coated with the dressing.
7. Serve immediately after plating.

## Health Advice
This salad is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss periods or light meal enthusiasts. However, squid is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Pan-fried bell peppers and olive oil provide antioxidant vitamins, and the pairing with lemon juice can promote iron absorption.
