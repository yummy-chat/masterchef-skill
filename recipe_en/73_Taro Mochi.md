# Taro Mochi  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍠 Taro (Li Pu variety) 200g  
🟣 Purple sweet potato powder 3g  
🥛 Milk 165g  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour a 50g  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour b 75g  
🌽 Cornstarch 22g  
🧈 Butter 30g  
🥛 Heavy cream 145g  
🍬 White sugar 26g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Food processor  
🔍 Sieve  
🎞 Plastic wrap  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
👜 Piping bag  
🥘 Steamer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the taro into chunks, boil over high heat for about 40 minutes until soft, then place all into a food processor.  
2. Add 30g milk and 25g heavy cream, blend into a paste.  
3. Add 3g purple sweet potato powder and 18g white sugar, continue blending into a smooth taro paste. Set aside.  
4. In a bowl, combine glutinous rice flour b, cornstarch, 135g milk, and 50g white sugar. Mix well and strain once through a sieve.  
5. Cover with plastic wrap, poke small holes in the wrap, and steam over medium heat for 30 minutes.  
6. While steaming, place glutinous rice flour a in a non-stick pan and stir-fry over low heat until slightly yellowed to make hand dust. Set aside.  
7. Remove the steamed glutinous rice mixture, immediately add 30g butter, knead until the butter is fully absorbed into the dough. Refrigerate for 1 hour.  
8. In another bowl, add 120g heavy cream and 8g white sugar, whip until soft peaks form, transfer to a piping bag. Set aside.  
9. Take out the chilled dough, knead for 5 minutes, and divide into 30g portions.  
10. Sprinkle each portion with about 2g hand dust to prevent sticking, then roll into round wrappers.  
11. On each wrapper, first pipe about 5g of whipped cream, then place 30g of taro paste on top.  
12. Wrap the wrapper as you would a bun, sealing the opening. Trim any excess dough if needed.  
13. After wrapping, sprinkle the surface evenly with about 2g hand dust to prevent sticking.  
14. Repeat steps 9 to 13 until all ingredients are used up. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This taro mochi is rich in carbohydrates, suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, it contains higher amounts of cream and sugar, so it is recommended to consume 1–2 pieces at a time to control intake. When making at home, you can reduce the sugar or use a sugar substitute for a lighter calorie option.
