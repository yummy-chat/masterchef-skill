# Braised Suobian Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Year Round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Suobian fish 2 pieces (few bones, tender meat; can substitute with other fish)
🧈 Old tofu (firm tofu) appropriate amount (side dish, absorbs the broth)
🫘 Pickled cowpea appropriate amount (can substitute with pickled vegetables or sour cabbage)
🌶️ Pickled red chili appropriate amount
🫚 Pickled ginger appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (for marinating and garnish)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (for marinating)
🌶️ Dried chili appropriate amount (for aromatic sizzling)
🌿 Sichuan pepper a small pinch (for stir-frying base and sizzling)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating base flavor)
🌿 Ground pepper appropriate amount (for deodorizing during marinating and braising)
🍺 Sichuan pepper beer 1 can (for marinating and broth, adds numbing aroma)
🫘 Aged broad bean paste (doubanjiang) 1 large spoon (for stir-frying red oil)
🧂 MSG appropriate amount (to enhance umami)
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount (to enhance umami)
🍬 White sugar a pinch (to balance spiciness)
🥫 Soy sauce appropriate amount (for color and aroma)
🍶 Vinegar appropriate amount (drizzle along the wok edge before serving)
🫕 Hot oil appropriate amount (for final sizzling of dried chili and Sichuan pepper)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and marinating)
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour boiling hot water over the cleaned suobian fish, scrape off the white foam on the skin, then rinse clean.
2. Cut the fish into bite-sized chunks for even marinating and braising.
3. Place the cut fish pieces under running water, repeatedly rub and wash to remove blood and surface oil film, until the water runs clear.
**Key Point**: Must wash until water is clear, otherwise the final dish will look dull and not clean.
4. Add ginger, scallion, salt, and pepper to the fish pieces, pour in 1 can of Sichuan pepper beer, mix well and marinate.
**Key Point**: Add enough salt to build a base flavor; Sichuan pepper beer is superior to regular beer for deodorizing and enhancing aroma.
5. Cut pickled cowpea into short sections, pickled red chili into segments, slice pickled ginger, cut garlic into pieces, set all ingredients aside in one place.
6. Remove the ginger and scallion used for marinating the fish, add a little potato starch, mix well so each piece of fish is coated with a thin layer of starch.
**Key Point**: The starch forms a protective layer, locking in moisture, making the fish meat more tender and bouncy.
7. Heat oil in a wok. When the oil reaches about 50% heat, scatter the fish pieces along the wok edge, gently slide them, fry until set, then immediately remove and drain oil.
**Key Point**: Do not fry thoroughly; a light yellow surface and set shape is enough, to prevent the fish from breaking apart during subsequent braising. Be careful to add fish along the wok edge to avoid oil splatter.
8. Heat oil in another wok, add a piece of lard. Once hot, first fry Sichuan pepper until fragrant, then add pickled cowpea, pickled red chili, pickled ginger, garlic, stir-fry over low heat until aromatic. Add 1 large spoon of aged broad bean paste and continue stir-frying over low heat until red oil releases and the ingredients are crisp and fragrant.
**Key Point**: Low heat prevents burning; aged broad bean paste is key to the rich red oil aroma.
9. Pour the remaining Sichuan pepper beer into the stir-fried base, bring to a boil over high heat. Add MSG, chicken bouillon, ground pepper, a pinch of white sugar, and an appropriate amount of soy sauce, stir well to adjust the base flavor.
10. Add the pre-fried fish pieces to the wok, turn to low heat and braise slowly for flavor. Then add the old tofu (firm tofu), continue braising over low heat for 5–8 minutes, until the fish is cooked through, the tofu has absorbed the broth, and the sauce is thick and almost dry.
**Key Point**: Slow braising over low heat ensures full flavor absorption; firm tofu holds its shape and does not break easily.
11. When the sauce is almost dry, drizzle a suitable amount of vinegar along the wok edge to enhance aroma. Transfer the fish and tofu to a serving dish. Place dried chili and Sichuan pepper on top, pour over sizzling hot oil, then finally sprinkle with chopped scallion and cilantro.
**Key Point**: The wok-edge vinegar uses high heat to release the vinegar's aroma and deodorize; sizzling with hot oil enhances the numbing and spicy flavor.

## Health Advice
This braised suobian fish has few bones and tender meat, paired with old tofu (firm tofu) that has absorbed the rich broth, making it an excellent source of high-quality protein and calcium. Suitable for the elderly and children. However, pickled vegetables and broad bean paste are high in salt; people with high blood pressure are advised to consume less of the broth.
