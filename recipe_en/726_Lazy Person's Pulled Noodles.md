# Lazy Person's Pulled Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 High-gluten or all-purpose flour 500g  
💧 Cold water 240g  
🧂 Salt 3g (for the dough)  
🧪 Edible alkali 1g (for the dough)  
🥬 Bok choy, as needed  
🧈 Tofu, as needed  
🧈 Fried tofu puffs, as needed  
🍥 Fish balls, as needed  
🍲 Broth about 1 ladle  
🌾 Dry flour (for dusting) as needed (to prevent sticking)  
🫙 Clay pot sauce, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning the clay pot)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed  
🌶️ Ground white pepper, as needed  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tablespoon  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Cutting board  
🪚 Rolling pin  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍳 Wok/large pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🚿 Bowl of cold water  
🍲 Clay pot and stove  
🥢 Chopsticks/spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix 500g flour with 3g salt and 1g alkali. Gradually add 240g cold water, stir into flake-like pieces, then knead into a smooth dough.  
**Note**: Adding alkali makes the noodles smoother and chewier; the water amount is 10g more than usual, the dough should be relatively soft—too soft and it will break easily when cooked.  

2. Knead the dough on the cutting board until the surface is smooth. Return it to the bowl and cover with another bowl. Let it rest for about 30 minutes to fully relax the gluten.  

3. Sprinkle dry flour on the cutting board. Take a portion of the rested dough and roll it out with a rolling pin, making it wide, long, and thin, ensuring even thickness.  

4. Dust the surface of the dough sheet with dry flour and also coat the knife blade. Cut into strips about 1 cm wide, separating each cut to prevent sticking. Finally, cut across the whole row of strips and separate them with your hands.  

5. Pinch both ends of a noodle strip and stretch it outward. Bring the two ends together and pinch them, twist it, and slap it against the cutting board. Repeat the process of bringing ends together, twisting, and slapping 1–2 times. Discard the ends.  
**Key Tip**: Slapping makes the noodle thickness more even and the texture more chewy.  

6. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the stretched noodles and cook until about 70–80% done. Remove and quickly place them into a bowl of cold water to cool, making the noodles smoother and chewier.  

7. Add one ladle of broth to a clay pot. Add bok choy, tofu, tofu puffs, fish balls, and other accompaniments. Bring to a boil over heat and cook until done. Add one ladle of clay pot sauce, and season with appropriate amounts of salt, chicken bouillon, and ground white pepper. Stir and bring to a boil. Add the cooled noodles and toss to combine. Once boiling again, drizzle in one tablespoon of chili oil and serve.  
**Note**: Use medium-low heat throughout. Cook the vegetables first, then add the noodles, allowing the noodles to fully absorb the flavor and broth.  

## Health Suggestions  
These pulled noodles are chewy and springy. The broth paired with vegetables and fish balls provides balanced nutrition, making them suitable for those needing an energy boost or who enjoy noodle dishes. It is recommended to control the amount of chili oil to reduce sodium and fat intake; tofu and fish balls offer high-quality protein, making this dish suitable for weight loss periods.
