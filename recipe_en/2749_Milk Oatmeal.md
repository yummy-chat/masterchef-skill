# Milk Oatmeal
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥛 Milk (pasteurized milk tastes better)
🌾 Oatmeal
🥚 Egg
🧂 Spiced salt (optional)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥛 Breakfast cup
🍳 Pot
🔥 Microwave (used for the quick method)

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour milk into a breakfast cup, and prepare the egg and oatmeal.
   **Note**: Ensure the breakfast cup has sufficient capacity to avoid overflow when mixing later.

2. Cooking oatmeal: Bring 200ml of water to a boil in a pot, add the oatmeal, and keep it boiling for 2 minutes. Remove the oatmeal, drain the cooking water as thoroughly as possible, and immediately place it into the milk cup, stirring well.
   **Key point**: Draining the cooking water prevents the oatmeal from diluting the milk, maintaining a rich and creamy texture.

3. Cooking oatmeal: Place instant oatmeal directly into the breakfast cup, pour in the milk, stir, then put it in the microwave and heat on medium power for 4 minutes. Ensure the total volume of the mixture does not exceed 50% of the container's capacity to prevent overflow during heating. Avoid using a glass cup to reduce the risk of cracking.
   **Key point**: The heating time can be adjusted appropriately based on the microwave's power; the oatmeal should fully absorb the milk and become soft and tender.

4. Frying egg: Heat a small amount of oil in a pan, crack in the egg, and fry each side for about 20 seconds until the egg white is set and the yolk is slightly runny. Before removing from the pan, sprinkle about 3 grams of spiced salt for seasoning.
   **Key point**: Use minimal oil to keep it low-fat; avoid frying the egg for too long to ensure a tender and smooth texture.

5. Plating and serving: Pair the fried egg with the milk oatmeal, and optionally serve with fruits, vegetables, or soda crackers.

## Health Recommendations
Milk oatmeal paired with a fried egg is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for weight loss periods or as an energy-boosting breakfast. It is recommended to choose unsweetened milk and quick-cook oatmeal to avoid rapid blood sugar spikes. Using less oil when frying the egg is healthier, and substituting regular salt with spiced salt can reduce sodium intake.
