# Shredded Pork in Yu Xiang (Fish-Fragrant) Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Best Season: All Year Round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork loin 500g (julienned)
🎋 Winter bamboo shoots, to taste (julienned, blanched)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, to taste (julienned, blanched)
🥚 Egg white, about 1/3 piece
🧅 Spring onions, to taste (chopped, divided into two portions)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced, divided into two portions)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (minced)
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers, to taste (minced)
🍶 Shaoxing wine 5g (for marinating) + 10g (for the sauce)
🍾 Soy sauce 3g (for marinating) + 30g (for the sauce)
🧂 Salt 2g (for marinating) + 3g (for the sauce)
🌾 Cornstarch 5g (for coating the meat)
🫒 Vegetable oil 10g (for sealing the meat)
🍬 Sugar 20g
🧂 White pepper 5g
🍶 Vinegar 30g
💧 Cornstarch slurry 20g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula / wok shovel

## Instructions
1. Cut the pork loin into even, thin strips.
2. Marinate the pork: Add 5g Shaoxing wine, 3g soy sauce, and 2g salt to the pork. Mix well. Add 10g of water in three additions, stirring until each addition is fully absorbed by the meat, making it smooth and translucent. Then add the egg white, 5g cornstarch, and 10g vegetable oil. Stir to coat until the surface is glossy. Set aside to marinate for a moment.
3. Prepare the Yu Xiang sauce: In a bowl, combine 3g salt, 20g sugar, 5g white pepper, 10g Shaoxing wine, 30g vinegar, 30g soy sauce, 20g cornstarch slurry, and 2g chicken bouillon. Stir well and set aside.
4. Blanch the bamboo shoot shreds and wood ear mushroom shreds in advance. Drain and set aside.
5. Heat the wok with cold oil. Add the marinated pork and stir-fry over low heat until the meat changes color and is just cooked through. Do not overcook until dry.
6. Add the minced pickled chilies, minced ginger, and half of the minced garlic. Stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant.
7. Add the blanched bamboo shoot shreds and wood ear mushroom shreds. Stir-fry quickly to combine.
8. Pour the prepared Yu Xiang sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry rapidly.
**Key point**: The cornstarch in the sauce will gelatinize upon heating. Stir quickly to ensure the sauce evenly coats all ingredients.
9. Once the sauce thickens and is absorbed into the pork, add the chopped spring onions and the remaining minced garlic. Stir-fry until evenly mixed, then remove from heat.

## Health Advice
Yu Xiang Shredded Pork is high in protein and low in fat, but the sugar and oil content is considerable. It is recommended to pair it with leafy green vegetables to balance nutrition. Diabetic patients should reduce the sugar. For those on a weight-loss diet, reduce the amount of cornstarch slurry and control the intake of staple foods.
