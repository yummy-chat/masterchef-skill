# Family Roasted Lamb Rack
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lamb rack 1 piece
🌶️ Bird's eye chili 3-4
🧄 Garlic, minced appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion 1 bunch
🧅 Onion, half
🥕 Carrot 1
🥬 Celery 2 stalks
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine a splash
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🤍 Sesame seeds appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili powder appropriate amount
🌿 Cumin powder appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Food processor
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
♨️ Oven
🖌️ Basting brush
♨️ Baking tray/rack, aluminum foil

## Instructions
1. Trim the lamb rack: Cut off excess fat from the edges, and score the meaty side to allow seasoning to penetrate.
2. Finely chop the bird's eye chili, garlic, scallion, onion, carrot, and celery, then blend into a paste using a food processor; if no food processor is available, crush by hand and use directly.
3. Add appropriate amount of salt and a splash of cooking wine to the vegetable paste, and mix well.
4. Evenly coat the lamb rack with the prepared marinade, and marinate for at least 3 hours; for best results, refrigerate overnight if time permits.
5. Place a foil-lined baking tray on the lower oven rack to catch drippings, and put the lamb rack on the middle rack. Roast at 160°C top heat and 200°C bottom heat for 50–70 minutes.
   - After about 30 minutes, the lamb rack will begin to release fat; after about 1 hour, the surface will start to caramelize.
6. Once the surface of the lamb rack is caramelized, remove it from the oven, brush with a layer of cooking oil, and return to the oven to roast for another 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Brushing with oil enhances the final color and appeal.
7. Remove from the oven, sprinkle evenly with sesame seeds, chili powder, and cumin powder, then roast for another 10–20 minutes until the spices are fully aromatic.
   **Note**: This final roast is intended to release the fragrance of the dry spices.

## Health Advice
Lamb rack is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for muscle building or blood replenishment. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables to balance the meal. Using more celery and onion in the marinade helps remove any gamey flavor and cut through the richness, while using a drip tray during roasting reduces fat intake.
