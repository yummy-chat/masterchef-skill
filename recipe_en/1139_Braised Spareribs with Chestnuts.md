# Braised Spareribs with Chestnuts
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork spareribs, to taste (preferably quality ribs, soaked in water to remove blood, then patted dry)
🌰 Chestnuts, to taste (peeled, prepared in advance)
🧅 1 scallion (tied into a knot)
🫚 2 slices ginger
🍬 A handful of rock sugar
🧂 3 tablespoons light soy sauce
🧂 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
🧂 1 teaspoon salt
🌿 A pinch of white pepper
💧 Hot water, to taste (enough to submerge the ribs)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🫕 Clay pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the pork spareribs in clean water and soak for half an hour. Once blood has seeped out, rinse thoroughly, then pat dry completely with kitchen paper.
   **Note**: If the ribs are of good quality, blanching can be omitted, but they must be thoroughly dried to prevent oil splatter when adding to the pan and to ensure proper browning.

2. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the chestnuts and fry over medium-low heat until the surface is golden. Remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Frying the chestnuts prevents them from falling apart during braising and gives them a richer, more fragrant texture.

3. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the rock sugar and reduce the heat to low. Stir continuously until the sugar melts and turns into a dark brown syrup.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout and stir constantly to prevent burning, which would create a bitter taste.

4. Immediately increase the heat to high. Add the dried ribs and stir-fry quickly, ensuring each piece is evenly coated with the caramelized sugar and lightly browned on the surface.

5. Transfer the ribs along with the sugar oil from the wok into a clay pot. Add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce. Pour in hot water until the ribs are submerged. Add the scallion knot, ginger slices, and a pinch of white pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat.

6. Once the broth is boiling, skim off any foam with a soup spoon to keep the broth clear.

7. Add the fried chestnuts. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover the pot, and braise for 1 hour.

8. After 1 hour, uncover the pot. Remove and discard the scallion knot and ginger slices. Season with salt. Cover again and continue braising over the lowest heat for another 20 minutes.

9. Uncover the pot and increase the heat to medium-high to reduce the sauce. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Reduce the sauce to about half its original volume until it becomes thick and coats the ribs.

10. Turn off the heat. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve.

## Health Advice
Chestnuts are rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C. Braising them with spareribs complements the nutrition and is suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter. Be careful to control the heat when caramelizing the sugar to avoid bitterness. Those with gout are advised to consume less of the broth.
