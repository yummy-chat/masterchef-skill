# Homemade Want Want Snow Rice Crackers 🍘
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Ingredients
🌾 115g No. 2 fragrant glutinous rice (soaked for 4 hours, drained, and dry-ground into powder)
💧 100ml boiling water
🌾 115g rice starch
🥔 15g potato starch
🥚 10g meringue powder
🍬 130g powdered sugar (added in two portions)
💧 20g drinking water (for whipping meringue) + 15~50g (to adjust icing consistency later)

### Seasonings
🍋 5g lemon juice
🍯 5g corn syrup (water怡)
🧂 Salt, to taste (dissolved in water for spraying)
🧂 MSG, to taste (dissolved in water for spraying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥄 Electric hand mixer
🌪️ Food processor/blender
⚖️ Kitchen scale
♨️ Pressure cooker
🌬️ Dehydrator or oven with drying function
🔥 Oven (capable of reaching 240°C)
⭕ Round cutter (8cm diameter)
🥖 Rolling pin
💦 Ultra-fine spray bottle
🪥 Long-handled bottle brush (with good elasticity)
🛡️ Transparent protective cover or plastic wrap with stand

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**
   Soak the rice for 4 hours, drain, and dry-grind it into fine powder using a food processor. Take 115g of the rice flour and mix it evenly with 115g rice starch and 15g potato starch.
   Make a well in the center of the mixed powder, pour in 100ml of freshly boiled water, quickly stir into flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth, gently glossy dough.
   **Key point**: Must use boiling water to scald the dough, fully gelatinizing the starch; knead until uniform and translucent.

2. **Steam and pound the dough**
   Place the dough in a pressure cooker, steam for 30 minutes after steam comes up. Remove, and vigorously pound repeatedly with a pestle or hard tool until the dough becomes very smooth, fine, and elastic, with a texture similar to mochi.
   **Key point**: Pound thoroughly to ensure the dough is firm and evenly structured.

3. **Shape and initial drying**
   Divide the pounded dough into portions, and roll out into thin sheets about 3mm thick using a rolling pin.
   Cut out cracker rounds with an 8cm round cutter, and arrange them on a baking sheet.
   Place into a dehydrator or oven, dry at 75°C for 70 minutes, until the cracker blanks are dry and the surface has no excess moisture.
   **Key point**: The cracker blanks must be completely dry after drying, otherwise they may crack during high-temperature puffing.

4. **High-temperature puffing**
   Place the dried cracker blanks into an oven preheated to 240°C, and bake for about 2 minutes.
   **Check for doneness**: The blanks will quickly puff up and swell, with natural cracks forming on the surface.

5. **Spray the seasoning liquid**
   Dissolve an appropriate amount of salt and MSG in a little drinking water, and fill an ultra-fine spray bottle with this mixture.
   Spray the seasoned liquid evenly on both sides of the puffed crackers to simulate a savory base flavor.

6. **Second drying**
   Place the sprayed, moistened crackers back into the dehydrator or oven, and dry at 70°C for 1 hour to completely remove moisture and restore crispness.
   **Key point**: The second drying must be thorough, otherwise the icing will soften easily from moisture.

7. **Make the icing**
   In a bowl, combine 10g meringue powder, about 1/3 of the powdered sugar, and 20g water. Whip with an electric hand mixer at medium-high speed until thick and white.
   Add the remaining powdered sugar in two portions, continuing to whip until the mixture reaches a solid consistency.
   Stir in 5g corn syrup and 5g lemon juice, then gradually add small amounts of water, mixing and whipping, until the icing flows in a smooth, thin thread when lifted, maintaining fluidity without being runny.
   **Key point**: Icing consistency is key—too thin and the pattern will blur; too thick and it won't splatter.

8. **Apply the icing**
   Set up the protective cover. Evenly dip the long-handled bottle brush into the icing.
   Hold the brush in one hand, and use the other hand to quickly and forcefully tap the brush handle. Use the brush head's elasticity to splatter the icing into extremely fine droplets, evenly scattered onto the cracker surface, forming irregular white spots.
   **Key point**: Must use the splattering method to mimic the natural texture of commercial snow crackers; the protective cover prevents messy icing splatter.

9. **Final drying**
   Place the iced crackers into the oven, set to 60~70°C top and bottom heat, and bake for 0.5 to 1 hour, until the white icing is completely hard and dry, forming a crisp sugar coating.

## Health Note
This dish is high in sugar and sodium, suitable for occasional snacking. Avoid overconsumption. When making at home, control the amount of icing and seasoning liquid; reducing sugar and salt is healthier.
