# Summer Cool Fruit Mousse
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍪 Digestive biscuits 30 grams (for the crust)
🧈 Butter 10 grams (melted before use, can be replaced with an equal amount of milk or chocolate)
🍓 Strawberries approximately 80 grams (for puree used in the mousse body) + approximately 30 grams (for the surface jelly)
🫐 Blueberries approximately 80 grams (for puree used in the mousse body) + approximately 30 grams (for the surface jelly)
🥭 Mango approximately 80 grams (for puree used in the mousse body) + approximately 30 grams (for the surface jelly)
🍑 Peach approximately 80 grams (for puree used in the mousse body) + approximately 30 grams (for the surface jelly)
🍮 Gelatin sheets 5 grams (for setting the mousse body, approximately 1 sheet per 80 grams of fruit puree) + approximately 1 gram (for the surface jelly)
🥛 Whipping cream 120 grams (whipped before use, can be replaced with yogurt but the texture will differ)
🍬 White sugar approximately 20 grams (per 80 grams of fruit puree, adjust according to the sweetness of the fruit)
🍋 Lemon juice a small amount (added to the fruit puree to prevent oxidation and discoloration)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
📦 Milk carton or any square container (used as a mousse mold)
🫙 Plastic wrap (to seal the bottom of the mold to prevent leakage)
👜 Resealable bags (for crushing biscuits and fruits)
🪓 Rolling pin or hard bottle cap (for crushing ingredients)
🥣 Bowl or cup (for mixing ingredients and measuring)
🫗 Sieve (for straining fruit puree to make it smooth)
🥚 Whisk or electric mixer (for whipping cream)
♨️ Microwave (for heating to soften harder fruits and jelly liquid)
🪣 Basin or deep dish (for heating to melt gelatin in a water bath)
✂️ Scissors (for cutting open the mold when unmolding)

## Preparation Steps

### Prep Stage
1. **Prepare the Mold**: Cut the milk carton into a square shape, wrap the bottom tightly with plastic wrap, and seal it well to prevent the mousse mixture from leaking. You can also use any suitable container at home.
2. **Make the Crust**: Place 30 grams of digestive biscuits into a resealable bag and crush them. Add 10 grams of melted butter and mix well. Divide the mixture into the bottom of the molds, press it down firmly using a cup, especially ensuring the edges are compact to prevent crumbling when unmolding. After finishing, place in the refrigerator or freezer to set.
3. **Prepare the Gelatin**: Soak the gelatin sheets in ice water until they become soft and bouncy, then set aside. Ice water must be used.
4. **Make the Fruit Puree**:
   - Place the washed fruits into a resealable bag, add a little lemon juice, and crush them into a puree using a rolling pin.
   - For harder fruits, first add sugar and lemon juice, then microwave on high heat for about 50 seconds to soften before crushing.
   - Strain the fruit puree through a sieve to obtain a smooth puree for later use. Straining makes the texture more delicate.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Melt the Gelatin and Mix with Fruit Puree**: Place the softened and drained 5 grams of gelatin sheets into approximately 80 grams of fruit puree. Place the bowl over hot water at about 40–50°C and stir until the gelatin is completely dissolved. Then add approximately 20 grams of white sugar and stir well. Repeat this step to make four different fruit-flavored puree mixtures.
   - **Note**: The water temperature for melting the gelatin should not be too high, as it may damage the gelatin's setting ability.
2. **Whip the Whipping Cream**: Whip 120 grams of whipping cream until it reaches a thick yogurt-like consistency. The purpose of whipping is to incorporate air, giving the mousse a light texture.
3. **Mix the Mousse Batter**: Combine the completely cooled fruit puree-gelatin mixture with the whipped cream, and gently stir until evenly mixed.
   - **Note**: The fruit puree must be completely cooled, otherwise it will melt the cream and affect the texture.
4. **Pour into Molds and Refrigerate**: Pour the different flavored mousse batters into the molds lined with the crust. Gently tap the molds a few times to release air bubbles, then place in the refrigerator to chill until fully set.
   - **Note**: Regular refrigeration takes several hours; if no refrigerator is available, increase the amount of gelatin and let it set at a cool room temperature.
5. **Make and Add the Jelly Layer**:
   - Dilute the remaining approximately 30 grams of fruit puree with a small amount of water, add approximately 1 gram of gelatin sheets, and heat until melted.
   - Let the jelly liquid cool to near hand temperature.
   - Pour the jelly liquid over the already set mousse surface.
   - Place back in the refrigerator to chill until the jelly layer is set.
   - **Note**: The jelly liquid must be cooled before pouring, otherwise it will melt the mousse layer underneath.
6. **Unmold and Decorate**: Once the surface is confirmed to be set, tear off the paper mold. Decorate with cream, fresh fruits, etc. If the mousse is frozen, it is recommended to thaw it in the refrigerator for about 2 hours after unmolding before serving for the best texture.

## Health Tips
This fruit mousse uses four types of fruit puree and is rich in vitamin C, making it suitable for cooling off in summer. However, note that the calories from whipping cream and sugar are not low. For those who are prone to weight gain, it is recommended to reduce the sugar or replace the cream with yogurt.
