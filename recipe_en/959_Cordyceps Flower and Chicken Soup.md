# Cordyceps Flower and Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Dried cordyceps flowers 30g (soak in water for 15 minutes)  
🐔 Old hen 1 whole (remove neck and tail, cut into pieces)  
🫚 Ginger slices a few  
🫘 Dried longan meat 2g  
🍯 Candied date half a piece  
🍬 Rock sugar 1 small piece  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍶 Yellow wine 20g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking and washing)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for handling chicken pieces)  
🍲 Stewing pot (to hold ingredients for double-boiling)  
🍳 Wok (for making the base broth)  
♨️ Steamer (double-boiling equipment)  
🥄 Soup ladle (to skim off excess fat)  

## Steps
1. Soak the dried cordyceps flowers in clean water for 15 minutes, then drain and set aside.
2. Remove the neck, tail, and toenails from the old hen. Split the chicken in half from the back, then rinse thoroughly with clean water at least twice to completely wash away blood, any remaining internal organs, and attached chicken fat. Rinse again and cut into large pieces.
3. Place the chicken pieces into the stewing pot, then add the soaked cordyceps flowers, ginger slices, dried longan meat, candied date, and rock sugar.
4. Pour clean water into a wok, add some salt and 20g of yellow wine, bring to a boil to make the base broth.
   **Key point**: Preparing the base broth in advance can shorten the subsequent stewing time.
5. Pour the boiling base broth into the stewing pot, cover the pot, and place it into the steamer. First, steam on high heat for 20 minutes, then reduce to low heat and continue steaming for 1.5 hours.
   **Note**: Monitor the water level during steaming to prevent it from drying out.
6. Once the steaming time is up, take out the stewing pot, open the lid, and use a soup ladle to skim off the fat from the surface, leaving the broth clear and not greasy.
7. Ladle into bowls and serve.

## Health Advice  
Cordyceps flower and chicken soup is rich in protein and various amino acids, making it suitable for nourishing those who are physically weak, or recovering after childbirth or surgery. Control the amount of salt and rock sugar; patients with high blood pressure are advised to skim off all fat before drinking.
