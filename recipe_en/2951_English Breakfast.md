# English Breakfast  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌭 Sausage: to taste (unspecified type, choose a fatty breakfast sausage)  
🥓 Bacon: to taste  
🩸 Black pudding: 1 link (blood and oat sausage, slice and remove casing)  
🍄 Button mushrooms: to taste (chopped)  
🍅 Cherry tomatoes: to taste  
🥚 Eggs: 2  
🥫 Baked beans in tomato sauce: to taste (canned convenience food)  
🍞 Toast: 2 slices  
🌿 Thyme: to taste (for garnish and aroma)  
🧂 Salt: to taste  
⚫ Black pepper: to taste  
🧈 Butter: 1 small piece  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Egg spatula / Turner  
🥢 Tongs  
🍞 Toaster  
🫖 Teapot  
☕ French press  
🥛 Glass  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Slice the black pudding, remove the outer casing, and set aside.  
2. Add a small amount of cold oil to a frying pan, place the sausages in, and fry over low heat, browning each side.  
   **Key**: Sausages are the hardest to cook through, so start them first and maintain low heat throughout.  
3. Add the bacon, increase to medium-low heat, and cook together until the bacon renders fat and the edges are crispy.  
4. Add the black pudding slices and fry until both sides are crisp.  
5. Add the butter and, once melted, add the chopped mushrooms. Use the meat fat and butter in the pan to enhance the mushrooms' flavor.  
6. Add the cherry tomatoes and fry until the skins split.  
7. Use a tool to push the ingredients aside, leaving a gap, then crack in the eggs, seasoning the tops with salt and black pepper.  
8. Add the baked beans and heat together with the other ingredients.  
9. Once the eggs reach a soft, runny yolk state, turn off the heat and sprinkle with thyme, using residual heat for aroma.  
10. Toast the bread slices in a toaster until golden and crispy.  
11. Prepare a drink of choice and plate everything together.  
    **Note**: Avoid frequent flipping during the frying process to allow the meats to form a crispy crust, and keep the tomatoes and mushrooms juicy.  

## Health Advice  
This English breakfast is high in protein and calories, making it suitable for weekends or days with high physical exertion. It is recommended to pair it with tea to cut through the richness. Consider reducing the amount of bacon and increasing the mushrooms and tomatoes to balance saturated fat intake.
