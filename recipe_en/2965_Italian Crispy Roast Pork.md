# Italian Crispy Roast Pork
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly with skin, about 7-8 jin (one large piece)  
🌿 Fennel powder, 15 grams  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 15 grams  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, 10 grams  
🍋 Lemon zest, from 1 lemon  
🌱 Rosemary, 2-3 sprigs (leaves only)  
🧄 Garlic powder, to taste (for seasoning and coating)  
🧂 Sea salt, to taste (needs to be generous)  
🧂 Black pepper, to taste (needs to be generous)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Grinder  
🔪 Knife  
♨️ Oven  
🧵 Kitchen twine  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Prepare the spice powder: Place fennel powder, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, lemon zest, and rosemary leaves into a grinder. Grind into a fine powder.
   **Note**: All ingredients must be ground to a fine powder; this spice blend is the core of the flavor. The amount used can be adjusted based on the meat quantity and personal taste; you do not need to use it all.
2.  Prepare the pork belly: Scrape the skin clean of any remaining hair and wash it thoroughly. Score the skin with cuts without cutting through to the meat. Slice the meaty part into large pieces about 2–3 cm thick, and layer the meat appropriately to facilitate rolling later.
   **Note**: The pork skin must be cleaned thoroughly; layering the meat is a key step for creating the internal spiral pattern.
3.  Season and coat: Evenly sprinkle the spice powder, a generous amount of garlic powder, sea salt, and black pepper onto the meat side of the opened pork belly. Pat with your hands to help the seasonings adhere evenly.
   **Note**: Be generous with the salt and black pepper, otherwise the final dish will not be salty enough; do not apply the spice powder too thickly to avoid an overpowering flavor.
4.  Roll and tie: Starting from one end, tightly roll the seasoned meat into a thick cylinder. Secure firmly with kitchen twine.
   **Note**: The roll must be very tight. When tying, you can first loop the twine around, then tighten it layer by layer to ensure it holds its shape securely.
5.  Prepare the skin and roast: Use the tip of a knife to poke dense, small holes all over the pork skin of the tied roast. Then, evenly coat the skin with a layer of salt. Place in the oven. First, roast at a low temperature of 150°C for 4 hours until fully cooked through. Then, increase the oven temperature to 230°C and roast for another 40 minutes until the skin is crispy and golden red.
   **Note**: The low-temperature slow roasting makes the meat tender, while the high-temperature fast roasting creates a crispy skin.
6.  Slice and serve: After roasting, remove from the oven and slice while hot. It can be plated and served directly, or used to make sandwiches with bread.
   **Doneness check**: The skin should be audibly crispy, the meat juicy, and the cross-section should show a clear spiral pattern of the spices.

## Health Advice
This Italian crispy roast pork is a high-protein, high-fat dish, suitable for times of high physical exertion or occasional indulgence. It is recommended to serve with a large vegetable salad or a tangy sauce to cut the richness. Control your portion size. Individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of sea salt used on the outer layer.
