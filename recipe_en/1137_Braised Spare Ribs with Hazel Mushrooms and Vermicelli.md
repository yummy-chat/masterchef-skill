# Braised Spare Ribs with Hazel Mushrooms and Vermicelli
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Spare ribs, appropriate amount (front ribs recommended for more tender meat)
🍄 Dried hazel mushrooms, appropriate amount (pre‑rinsed, trimmed, squeezed dry)
🍜 Vermicelli, appropriate amount (potato starch vermicelli recommended, pre‑soaked in cold water until soft)
🧅 1 scallion, cut into sections
🫚 1 piece of ginger, sliced
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
⭐ Star anise, to taste
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste
🌿 1 small piece of cinnamon (optional)
🧂 2 tablespoons thirteen‑spice powder
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste
🥄 Dark soy sauce, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Stockpot / bowl
🔥 Gas stove
🪣 Water basin
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the spare ribs in cold water and blanch over medium‑low heat. When the water is about to reach a boil, reduce to low heat, skim off some of the foam, and blanch thoroughly before removing.  
   **Key point**: Start with cold water, blanch slowly over medium‑low heat, and use plenty of water so the ribs are spread apart.

2. Rinse the blanched ribs in warm water, scrubbing them several times until the water runs clear, then drain well.  
   **Key point**: Use warm water and rub the ribs with your hands for thorough cleaning.

3. Trim off the root ends of the dried hazel mushrooms and pick out any bits of grass. Quickly rinse twice in warm water, squeezing and wringing them out as you wash. After washing, squeeze dry and set aside.  
   **Key point**: The mushrooms must be trimmed to remove soil; do not soak for long – wash quickly and squeeze dry to eliminate earthy flavour. The first two washes are very dirty and must be discarded.

4. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok. Once the oil is hot, add the drained ribs and stir‑fry over high heat until the surface tightens and changes colour.  
   **Key point**: A hot wok with hot oil instantly seals in the ribs’ moisture; the surface is ready when it firms up and turns white or light golden.

5. After the ribs are well stir‑fried, add the scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, dried chilies, and other aromatics, and stir‑fry until fragrant. Reduce to low heat, then add the thirteen‑spice powder, salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce, and stir evenly to colour the ribs.  
   **Key point**: Reduce to low heat after adding the aromatics to prevent burning; add powdered and liquid seasonings only after lowering the heat for better control.

6. Pour the squeezed‑dry hazel mushrooms into the wok and stir‑fry with the ribs over high heat until a rich, intense mushroom aroma is released.  
   **Key point**: The mushrooms must be stir‑fried thoroughly to unlock their fragrance.

7. Pour enough boiling water into the wok to submerge all ingredients. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat, then cover and continue boiling on high for about 40 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Use boiling water to maintain high temperature; boiling on high heat fully releases the flavour of the hazel mushrooms.

8. Once the ribs are tender, add the pre‑soaked vermicelli and continue cooking until the vermicelli is transparent and fully cooked, and the ribs are falling off the bone. Finally, sprinkle with chopped scallions, increase the heat to reduce the liquid until thickened, and serve.  
   **Key point**: Add the vermicelli just before serving; when reducing the liquid, leave enough sauce so that the oil and broth coat the ingredients.

## Healthy Advice
Braised spare ribs with hazel mushrooms and vermicelli is rich in high‑quality protein and dietary fibre, making it ideal for warming the body in autumn and winter. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables.
