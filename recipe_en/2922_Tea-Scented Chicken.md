# Tea-Scented Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the whole chicken: Tuck the chicken leg roots into the chicken cavity to fix the chicken body, preventing deformation during cooking.
2. Prepare the soy sauce mixture, bring to a boil, then reduce to medium-low heat to maintain a gentle simmer.
3. Perform the "three lifts and three dips": Hold the chicken head, submerge the whole chicken into the boiling soy sauce mixture, then immediately lift it out. Repeat three times. This tightens the chicken skin and ensures even heating.
**Key**: Pause briefly after each submersion to allow the liquid to enter the chicken cavity before lifting, ensuring uniform internal and external temperature.
4. Fully submerge the chicken in the soy sauce mixture. After boiling, reduce to low heat or turn off the heat, and steep for about 20 minutes, until the chicken skin turns an attractive sauced red color. Remove and drain.
**Note**: Turn the chicken once during steeping for more even coloring and flavor absorption.
5. Prepare the smoking material: Heat a clean wok over heat without oil. First, add the Tieguanyin tea leaves and stir-fry over medium-low heat until moisture evaporates and the tea aroma releases.
6. Add the brown slab sugar and continue stir-frying until the sugar melts and mixes with the tea, producing a caramel aroma.
**Key**: Do not use excessively high heat to avoid burning the sugar. Stop as soon as you smell the combined sweet and tea fragrance.
7. Place a wire rack over the stir-fried smoking material. Position the braised chicken on the rack and cover the wok tightly with a lid.
8. Heat over medium-high heat to perform the smoking process, about 15–20 minutes. During this time, shake the wok occasionally or adjust the heat to prevent localized burning. The aim is to produce a smoky, aromatic flavor without scorching the base.
**Note**: If the smoke becomes too intense or you smell burning, briefly remove the wok from the heat before continuing. This smoking step allows the tea and caramel aromas to penetrate the chicken—a once nearly lost, intricate technique in this dish.
9. After smoking, remove the chicken and chop it into pieces for serving. The finished chicken skin is date-red and glossy, the meat is tender, the bones fragrant, and the tea aroma rich.

## Health Advice
This dish is smoked with tea and brown sugar and is rich in protein. However, the sugar and soy sauce contribute high sodium content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the flavor. Suitable for those who enjoy rich, smoky flavors; people with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
