# Tender Vegetable Fish Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 3 lbs Basa Fish (thawed, rinsed, and cut into small pieces)
🥔 300 g Chinese Yam (peeled; wear gloves when handling to avoid itching)
🥕 200 g Carrot (peeled)
🥚 3 Eggs
🌿 Chopped Scallions, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌶️ Ground White Pepper, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🦪 Oyster Sauce, to taste

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🌀 Meat Grinder/Food Processor
🥣 Large Bowl
🍳 Non-Stick Pan (or Regular Pan)
🥄 Silicone Spatula/Spoon
🥢 Slotted Spoon
🧊 Sealable Bags

## Instructions
1. Cut the basa fish into small pieces. In batches, place them in a food processor and blend into a fine, sticky fish paste. Transfer to a large bowl and set aside.
   **Note**: Blend thoroughly; the finer the paste, the smoother and more tender the fish balls will be.

2. Peel the yam and carrot, cut into small pieces, and blend in the food processor until you have a fine vegetable paste.
   **Note**: The yam's mucilage can cause itching; it is recommended to wear gloves throughout this step.

3. Add the vegetable paste to the fish paste, then add chopped scallions, salt, ground white pepper, sugar, oyster sauce, and the 3 eggs.

4. Stir continuously in one direction. The mixture will be quite thin at first; continue stirring for about 5–6 minutes until it becomes thick and sticky (develops a bouncy texture).
   **Key Point**: Keep the stirring direction consistent to help the mixture bind properly.

5. Prepare a bowl of water. Form a few test balls and drop them in; if they float to the surface immediately, the mixture is ready. Proceed to the next step.

6. Using your hand or a spoon, shape the mixture into balls. Gently place them into a non-stick pan filled with warm water.

7. Cook over medium-low heat, ensuring the water never reaches a full boil. Use a silicone spatula to gently nudge the balls so they cook evenly and do not stick together.
   **Temperature Control**: Maintain the water at 80–90°C (176–194°F), keeping it barely simmering.

8. Once the balls have noticeably expanded in size, they are cooked through. Remove with a slotted spoon and transfer to a plate to cool.

9. After they have completely cooled, you can store them in sealable bags in the freezer. Use them for soups or serve with noodles as needed.

## Health Tips
Basa fish combined with yam and carrot provides high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for the elderly and children to supplement nutrition. Thoroughly stirring the mixture until it becomes sticky is the key to tender fish balls; starting them in cold water and keeping the heat low to maintain a gentle simmer ensures the balls stay intact and do not fall apart.
