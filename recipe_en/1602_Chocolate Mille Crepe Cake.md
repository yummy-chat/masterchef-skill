# Chocolate Mille Crepe Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Whole Egg Liquid 112g
🥛 Milk 172g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 27g (for crepe)
🧈 Butter 10g (melted)
🌾 Cake Flour 49g
🍫 Cocoa Powder 5g (for crepe) + some (for decoration)
🍫 Valrhona Caribe Dark Chocolate 80g
🧴 Blue Windmill Whipping Cream 400g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 24g (for cream)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥄 Hand Whisk
⚡ Electric Mixer
🔍 Sieve
🍳 Non-stick Flat Pan
🍶 Small Saucepan
🥛 Measuring Cup/Spoon
🔪 Spatula
🔄 Turntable
🔪 Knife
🛡️ Plastic Wrap

## Instructions

### 1. Making the Crepes
1.  Place whole egg liquid, milk, and 27g granulated sugar in a bowl. Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.
2.  Sift in cocoa powder and cake flour. Mix until no dry flour remains.
3.  Strain the batter through a sieve two to three times until smooth and refined.
4.  Add the melted butter and stir until well combined.
5.  Preheat a non-stick flat pan over medium-low heat. Ladle in one scoop of batter, quickly rotate the pan to spread it evenly, and immediately pour any excess batter back into the bowl.
    **Key Point**: The pan temperature should be such that the batter sets quickly upon contact.
6.  Continue heating over low or medium-low heat. When small bubbles form densely on the crepe's surface, it is cooked through.
7.  Run chopsticks along the edge of the crepe to loosen it. Carefully peel it off and place it on a plate to cool.
    **Key Point**: The thinner the crepes, the more delicate the texture.

### 2. Making the Chocolate Cream
1.  Place the Valrhona dark chocolate and 24g granulated sugar in a bowl.
2.  Pour the whipping cream into a small saucepan and heat until just about to boil. Immediately pour it over the chocolate mixture. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
    **Key Point**: Letting it sit allows the heat to fully melt the chocolate.
3.  Using a hand whisk, stir in circles starting from the center until the mixture is completely smooth and combined.
4.  Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the mixture. Refrigerate overnight to fully chill the base for easier whipping later.
5.  Remove the chilled base. Using an electric mixer, whip it until it reaches nearly stiff peaks.
    **Key Point**: Do not whip to completely stiff peaks, or the texture will be coarse and prone to separating.

### 3. Assembly & Finishing
1.  Spread a little chocolate cream on the cake board or plate. Place one crepe on top to secure it.
2.  Evenly spread approximately 31g of the whipped chocolate cream over the crepe.
3.  Cover with another crepe, gently pressing to adhere. Repeat the process of spreading cream and adding crepes until all crepes are used.
4.  Place the most visually perfect crepe on the very top layer.
5.  Use a sieve to dust an even layer of cocoa powder over the cake's surface.
6.  Refrigerate for at least 3 hours to set before serving.
    **Key Point**: The cake tastes better and has a more stable structure after chilling.

## Health Advice
This mille crepe cake is high in calories. It is recommended to slice it thinly and enjoy with black coffee, which helps cut the richness and control portions. Suitable for occasional indulgence by chocolate lovers, but should be consumed in moderation by those managing blood sugar or on a weight loss diet.
