# Clay Pot Ya Fish

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 Ya fish (live fish, from Zhougong River, red tail, remove black membrane, scales can be retained)  
🫘 Tofu, appropriate amount  
🥩 Tripe and offal (miscellaneous parts), appropriate amount  
🍢 Crispy meatballs, appropriate amount  
🧈 Lard, appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger slices, appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic slices, appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion segments, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, a small amount  
🧂 MSG, a small amount  
🧂 Chicken powder, a small amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🫕 Clay pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥅 Strainer  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Slaughter and clean the Ya fish, retain the scales, remove the gills and internal organs, scrape clean the black membrane inside the belly, and score the thick part of the fish’s back with a knife.  
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add lard, then add ginger slices, garlic slices, and scallion segments, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
**Note**: Lard is essential for the traditional flavor base and cannot be omitted.  
3. Lay the Ya fish flat in the wok, pan-fry over medium-low heat until the skin is slightly yellow and set.  
4. Pour pre-prepared milky white stock into the wok; the soup color will quickly turn white.  
5. Add tofu, tripe and offal, crispy meatballs, and other accompaniments.  
6. Season with salt, MSG, and chicken powder; the flavor should be light to highlight the freshness of the fish and soup.  
7. Transfer all ingredients and broth from the wok to a preheated clay pot, simmer over low heat, maintaining a gentle boil, until the ingredients are infused with flavor and the soup is rich and mellow.  

## Health Tips  
Ya fish is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for nourishing the body in all seasons. Paired with tofu and offal, it simultaneously supplements calcium and collagen. Using lard to fry the fish enhances the aroma significantly, but those with high blood lipids can substitute it with rapeseed oil, though the flavor will be slightly diminished.
