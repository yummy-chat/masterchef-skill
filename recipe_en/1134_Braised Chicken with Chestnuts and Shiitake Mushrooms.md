# Braised Chicken with Chestnuts and Shiitake Mushrooms  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October), suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌰 Chestnuts 1 bag (about 1.2 jin / 600g)  
🍗 Small hen 1 (about 2.9 jin / 1.45kg, remove the chicken breast, use bone-in parts)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms appropriate amount (pre-soaked)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (use whole cloves)  
🫚 Ginger 1 large piece (sliced into large pieces)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🍾 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🍾 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍶 Yellow wine/rice wine/cooking wine appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount  
🫒 Rapeseed oil + lard appropriate amount (use mixed)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍲 Clay pot/bowl (for serving)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  

## Steps  
1. Clean the fresh small hen thoroughly, chop into small pieces. Remove the chicken breast, rinse the remaining bone-in parts repeatedly, and drain well.  
   **Key point**: Do not blanch the chicken; stir-fry directly after draining.  

2. Cut a slit at the bottom of each chestnut. Place in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, and cook for 1 minute. Remove and rinse with cold water to cool. Peel off the outer shell and inner skin.  
   **Key point**: The slit helps with peeling; rinsing with cold water after boiling makes the skin easy to remove.  

3. Cut the pre-soaked dried shiitake mushrooms into small pieces. Peel the garlic, keeping whole cloves. Slice the ginger into large pieces. Cut the scallion into sections. Prepare the dried chili peppers.  

4. Add rapeseed oil and a little lard to the wok. Heat until slightly warm, then add the whole garlic cloves and chestnuts. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the surface turns slightly golden and browned. Remove and drain the oil. Set aside.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fried chestnuts will be more aromatic when braised.  

5. Using the remaining oil from frying the chestnuts, add the ginger slices. Then pour in the drained chicken pieces. Stir-fry over high heat until the chicken moisture is evaporated, the meat tightens, and the oil in the wok becomes clear. During stir-frying, add salt to enhance the chicken's flavor.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry over high heat throughout; drying out the moisture is key to bringing out the aroma.  

6. Add the shiitake mushroom pieces, scallion sections, and dried chili peppers. Drizzle in yellow wine and light soy sauce, stir-frying until the wine and soy sauce aromas are released. Then add dark soy sauce and stir-fry evenly to color the chicken.  
   **Key point**: Each seasoning must be fully stir-fried to release its fragrance before adding water.  

7. Add boiling water, enough to roughly level with the chicken. Season with white sugar and chicken bouillon. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Cover the wok and simmer for 8 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Add enough water at once; boiling water is recommended.  

8. Uncover and add the previously stir-fried chestnuts and garlic. Gently stir to combine. Cover the wok again and simmer over low heat for 10 minutes, until the chestnuts are soft and glutinous but remain intact.  

9. Uncover and discard the scallion sections and ginger slices. Increase to high heat and stir-fry continuously until the sauce thickens naturally and evenly coats the ingredients.  
   **Key point**: Thicken the sauce by reducing it; no cornstarch slurry is needed.  

10. Plate the dish and sprinkle chopped scallions on top for garnish.  

## Health Tips  
Chestnuts provide dietary fiber and complex carbohydrates, chicken offers high-quality protein, and shiitake mushrooms contribute fungal polysaccharides. This dish is suitable for autumn nourishment and recovery of physical strength. It is recommended to control the amount of lard used, and those with gout should consume less of the broth.
