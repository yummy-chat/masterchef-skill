# Ciabatta Twists
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 400g  
💧 Water 360ml  
🍞 Fresh yeast 9g (or an appropriate amount of dry yeast)  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🫒 Olive oil 10ml  
🥓 Sausage approx. 15g  
🧀 Mozzarella cheese approx. 35g  
🍅 Oil-cured sun-dried tomatoes approx. 70g  
🫒 Black olives approx. 10g  
🌿 Basil leaves approx. 15g  
🫑 Bell pepper as needed  
🥩 Cantonese-style cured sausage as needed  
🥓 Guizhou crispy pork suet as needed  
🌿 Parsley leaves as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Large mixing bowl  
🔪 Spatula  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
📜 Baking paper  
🍳 Baking tray  
🔥 Oven  
💦 Spray bottle  
🔪 Dough scraper/cutter  
🌀 Air fryer (alternative to oven)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the fresh yeast in a bowl, add water, and stir with a spatula until fully dissolved.  
2. In another large bowl, combine the bread flour and salt. Pour in the yeast water and olive oil, and stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains. Do not overmix.  
3. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for a moment. Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking, lift one corner of the dough and fold it toward the center. Turn the container and fold each of the four sides once. Cover again with plastic wrap and let rest. Repeat the folding process 2-3 times until the dough surface is smooth and elastic.  
4. Cover with plastic wrap and let ferment at room temperature until the dough volume doubles, with visible bubbles on the surface and a springy texture.  
5. Dust the work surface generously with flour. Turn the fermented dough out onto the surface, dust the top with more flour, and gently stretch it into a thick sheet.  
6. Evenly spread the chosen fillings over half of the dough. Fold the other half over the fillings and press gently to seal.  
7. Use a dough scraper to cut the dough into 6 even strips. Lift each strip one by one, twist it gently a few times, and place it on a baking tray lined with baking paper. Separate the dough pieces with baking paper to prevent sticking.  
8. Cover the surface with another sheet of baking paper and let the dough pieces undergo a second proofing until they have visibly expanded and slowly spring back when lightly pressed.  
9. Preheat the oven to 230°C. Place the baking tray on the lower middle rack. As you put the tray in, quickly spray water several times into the oven to create steam. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the surface is golden brown, then remove from the oven.  
10. If using an air fryer: Place the dough pieces in the air fryer basket, spray the surface with water, and bake at 180°C for about 15 minutes.  

## Health Advice  
This ciabatta twist, made from bread flour dough with various fillings, is suitable as a main dish or snack. However, the oil-cured sun-dried tomatoes and sausage are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should use less. Bread flour provides quality carbohydrates, making it a good option for quick energy replenishment after exercise. The cheese and cured sausage are not low in calories, so those managing their weight should limit consumption to 1-2 pieces per serving.
