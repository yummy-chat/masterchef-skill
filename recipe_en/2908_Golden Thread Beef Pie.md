# Golden Thread Beef Pie
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 High-gluten or all-purpose flour 500g
🥩 Beef filling 500g
🫚 Ginger, minced 20g
🧅 Green onion, as needed (chopped into scallion pieces, add just before use)
🖤 Black sesame seeds, as needed (for garnish)
🧂 Salt 3g (for dough) + 8g (for filling)
🧂 Edible alkali 1g
🌶️ Ground white pepper 3g
🧂 Chicken powder 3g
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g
💧 Sichuan pepper water, as needed (add to beef filling in batches)
🫒 Seasoned oil, as needed
🫒 Oil paste, as needed (for spreading on dough)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🫙 Plastic wrap
🔪 Cutting board
🪓 Rolling pin
🔪 Multi-blade dough slitter
🍳 Flat-bottom frying pan (with temperature control, top heat 180°C, bottom heat 200°C)
🥢 Food tongs, spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Place flour in a bowl, add 3g salt and 1g edible alkali, mix well. Gradually add about 240g cold water while stirring, then knead into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: Salt enhances gluten elasticity, alkali improves crispness. Adding water in stages helps kneading evenly.

2. Roll the dough into a long strip, divide into equal portions. Roll each portion into a small strip, arrange them neatly, brush the surface with oil, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 30 minutes until the dough has good extensibility.
3. Add minced ginger, 8g salt, ground white pepper, chicken powder, and light soy sauce to the beef filling. Mix thoroughly.
4. Gradually add Sichuan pepper water in batches, each time stirring in the same direction until the liquid is fully absorbed. Continue until the filling is tender, full, and elastic.
**Key point**: The key to a juicy filling is allowing the beef to fully absorb the Sichuan pepper water.

5. Add an appropriate amount of seasoned oil and mix well to seal in moisture. Finally, add the scallion pieces and mix just before use.
**Note**: Seasoned oil helps prevent the filling from leaking and causing the crust to break during frying. Add scallion pieces last to preserve their freshness.

6. Take one piece of rested dough, flatten it, and roll it out. Hold one end and stretch it outward, then thin out the head portion to create a long strip that is wider at one end and narrower at the other.
7. Spread oil paste evenly over the surface of the dough strip. Form the beef filling into a large ball and place it at the wider end. Lift both sides of the dough strip and cross them over to wrap the filling ball.
8. While stretching the dough strip, roll it up and around the filling ball, wrapping it tightly. Use a dough slitter to cut the remaining dough strip into thin threads, then wrap these threads around the pie crust. Pinch the bottom opening to seal, and shape into a round pie.
9. Press a small amount of black sesame seeds onto the top of the pie crust to adhere firmly.
10. Pour a generous amount of oil into a flat-bottom frying pan and heat until warm. Place the pies in the pan, cover with a lid, and fry at top heat 180°C, bottom heat 200°C for 5-6 minutes until the bottom turns golden brown.
11. Flip the pies, cover again, and continue frying for about 3 minutes until both sides are golden and crispy. Remove from the pan.
**Reminder**: Keep the lid on throughout frying to ensure even heat circulation and thorough cooking.

## Health Advice
This Golden Thread Beef Pie is rich in carbohydrates and protein, suitable for muscle building or those with high physical energy expenditure. Note that the dough contains alkali and salt, so individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. The beef filling is made juicy with Sichuan pepper water, but the frying process results in higher calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition.
