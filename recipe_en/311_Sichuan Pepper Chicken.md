# Sichuan Pepper Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Whole chicken 1 (choose a chicken with good meat quality)
🌿 Scallions (green parts only) appropriate amount (about twice the amount of Sichuan pepper)
🌶️ Red Sichuan pepper (Dahongpao) appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add in small amounts several times)
🍶 Nu'er Hong (or yellow wine) a small amount
🫒 Hot rapeseed oil appropriate amount (enough to cover the Sichuan pepper sauce)
🍬 White sugar a small amount (1 teaspoon)
🥤 Light soy sauce / soy sauce appropriate amount
🍲 Reserved poaching liquid 1-2 tablespoons
🧈 Sesame oil a small amount (for brushing the chicken skin)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board & chef's knife
🥘 Stockpot
🥣 Stainless steel basin / large bowl (for ice water)
🥄 Small bowl & spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
📌 Long needle (or bamboo skewer)
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut off the chicken feet at the joints. Rub the chicken body repeatedly with your hands to fully relax the meat.
2. Make the Sichuan pepper sauce: Soak the red Sichuan pepper in hot water for 5 minutes, then drain. Take the green parts of the scallions and chop them finely. Mix with the soaked Sichuan pepper and chop repeatedly with a knife until it becomes a sticky, emerald-green paste. Place the chopped Sichuan pepper sauce in a bowl, pour in hot rapeseed oil that has been heated until smoking, stir well, and immediately place the bowl in cold water or ice water to cool down quickly, preserving the green color and releasing the aroma.
3. Bring water to a rolling boil. Hold the chicken and immerse it in the boiling water for about 15 seconds. Lift it out to drain the hot water from the cavity. Repeat this three times so the chicken skin contracts and sets quickly.
4. After the third immersion, add ginger slices, scallion knots, star anise, bay leaves, a small amount of salt, and Nu'er Hong to the pot. Before the water comes to a boil again, turn the heat to the lowest setting, cover, and simmer for 25 minutes, maintaining the water at a state where it is almost boiling but only producing tiny bubbles.
5. Pierce the thickest part of the chicken thigh with a long needle. If clear juice seeps out, it is cooked through. Immediately remove the chicken and plunge it into ice water to cool completely, allowing the skin to tighten again.
6. Remove the chicken and pat it dry with kitchen paper towels. Brush a thin layer of sesame oil evenly over the skin to add shine and prevent drying.
7. In a bowl, add a pinch of salt, a small amount of white sugar, an appropriate amount of soy sauce, and 1-2 tablespoons of the reserved poaching liquid. Stir to combine. Then add a generous amount of the prepared Sichuan pepper sauce and stir into a thick, complex sauce.
8. Cut the cooled chicken into even pieces, arrange on a plate, pour the Sichuan pepper sauce over it, and toss well to coat. Serve.
