# Steamed-Free Green Rice Balls  
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌿 Cudweed or mugwort (for juice) about 2-3 jin  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 600 grams  
🌾 Wheat starch 200-250 grams (can substitute with cornstarch)  
🥜 Peanuts 200 grams (roasted and skinned)  
🖤 Black sesame seeds 100 grams (toasted)  
🫘 Red beans appropriate amount (pre-soaked)  
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks appropriate amount (roasted and crushed)  
🥓 Meat floss appropriate amount  
🍠 Taro appropriate amount (steamed)  
🍠 Purple sweet potato appropriate amount (steamed)  
🧈 Maltose 100 grams (can substitute with honey)  
🫒 Cooking oil (or butter) 50-80 grams (preferably mild in flavor)  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount (for seasoning peanut-sesame filling and red bean filling)  
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk appropriate amount (for seasoning red bean filling and taro paste filling)  
🥗 Mayonnaise appropriate amount (for binding egg yolk and meat floss filling)  
🥛 Whipping cream appropriate amount (for taro paste filling)  
🧈 Butter appropriate amount (for enhancing aroma in taro paste filling)  
⚪ Baking soda or edible alkali appropriate amount (for preserving green color when blanching wild greens)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or non-stick pan  
🥤 Blender or food processor (for blending herb puree)  
🔪 Meat grinder (for crushing peanut-sesame filling)  
🕸️ Strainer (for filtering green rice ball juice)  
🥣 Hand whisk (for stirring batter)  
🍚 Rice cooker or pressure cooker (for cooking red beans)  
🧤 Disposable gloves (for anti-stick wrapping)  
🛡️ Plastic wrap (for preserving green rice ball skin)

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Make the Green Juice**: Wash the cudweed. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a little baking soda or edible alkali, blanch the wild greens for about 2 minutes, remove and rinse with cold water, squeeze dry, and form into herb balls.  
**Key Point**: Adding alkali helps maintain the bright green color; rinsing with cold water sets the color.

2. **Extract the Juice**: Place two herb balls in a blender, add an appropriate amount of water, blend on high speed for 2 minutes until a fine puree, strain through a strainer, collect the green juice, and discard the residue.  
**Key Point**: Straining ensures a smooth dough skin.

3. **Prepare Four Types of Fillings**:  
   - **Peanut-Sesame Filling**: Grind the roasted skinned peanuts and half of the toasted black sesame seeds into coarse granules using a meat grinder. Transfer to a bowl and mix with the remaining black sesame seeds and white sugar.  
   - **Chunky Red Bean Filling**: Cook the pre-soaked red beans in a rice cooker or pressure cooker with water (about the amount used for cooking rice). While hot, mix with white sugar and sweetened condensed milk, keeping the bean granules intact.  
   - **Egg Yolk and Meat Floss Filling**: Combine the meat floss and crushed roasted salted duck egg yolks. Add mayonnaise and mix until it can be formed into a ball, with a soft and firm consistency.  
   - **Creamy Taro Paste Filling**: Mash the steamed taro and purple sweet potato into a paste. Add sweetened condensed milk, whipping cream, and butter, and knead until smooth.  
**Key Point**: Each filling should be adjusted to a suitable softness for easy wrapping later.

4. **Prepare the Batter**: Dissolve the maltose in the green juice, stirring until well combined. Gradually add the glutinous rice flour and wheat starch, stirring until no dry powder remains, forming a green batter. Finally, add the cooking oil and mix well.  
**Key Point**: The dry ingredients must be thoroughly stirred to avoid lumps; add the oil last.

5. **Stir-fry the Dough**: Pour the batter into a non-stick pan over medium heat. Stir continuously with a spatula throughout the process. Initially, the bottom will solidify first; continue to stir for about 15-20 minutes. The batter will gradually thicken and clump together, eventually forming a smooth, elastic, dark green glutinous rice dough with uniform color inside and out.  
**Key Point**: Stir constantly to prevent burning; the dough is ready when there is no white raw flour core.

6. **Wrap the Green Rice Balls**: Let the dough cool slightly until it is not too hot to handle. Wear gloves and apply a little oil to prevent sticking. Take an appropriate amount of dough, flatten it, wrap in any filling, use the tiger’s mouth technique to push and seal the opening, and roll into a ball.  
**Key Point**: Wrap while the dough is warm for better elasticity; oil the gloves to prevent sticking.

7. **Storage and Serving**: Place the wrapped green rice balls on parchment paper. If not eating immediately, wrap each tightly in plastic wrap to prevent the skin from drying out. The cooked green rice balls need no further steaming and can be eaten directly; they remain soft and chewy even after refrigeration.

## Health Advice  
Green rice balls are colored with mugwort juice, which is a natural antioxidant. However, glutinous rice flour digests slowly, so those with digestive issues should eat in moderation. The fillings vary; egg yolk with meat floss and taro paste are recommended, but sugar and oil intake should be controlled.
