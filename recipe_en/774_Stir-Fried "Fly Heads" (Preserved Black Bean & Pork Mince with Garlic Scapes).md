# Stir-Fried "Fly Heads" (Preserved Black Bean & Pork Mince with Garlic Scapes)
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Preserved black beans (douchi) – small half bowl
🫚 Ginger – to taste (crushed and minced)
🧄 Garlic – to taste (crushed and minced)
🫑 Green bell pepper – to taste (diced)
🥩 Pork (minced) – 150g (about 3 liang), chopped into mince
🌱 Garlic scapes – 100g (about 2 liang), cut into small dices
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili – 5 pieces (diced)
🫒 Cooking oil – small amount
🍶 High-proof liquor – small amount
🧂 Light soy sauce – one drizzle (to taste)
🍬 Granulated sugar – a pinch (to taste)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Bowls (for prep and serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Crush and mince ginger and garlic; dice green bell pepper, garlic scapes, and bird’s eye chilies into even pieces; prepare the preserved black beans. Mince the pork.
**Key point**: The amount of bird’s eye chili determines the spiciness—keep all diced ingredients roughly the same size.

2. Heat the pan, pour in a small amount of cooking oil, add the pork mince, and stir-fry over medium-low heat.
**Doneness check**: Stir-fry until the pork mince renders a lot of oil and becomes dry and aromatic.
**Key point**: After stir-frying, pour out the excess rendered pork fat, leaving only a thin layer of oil in the pan.

3. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add minced ginger, minced garlic, and preserved black beans. Fry until fragrant, then stir evenly with the pork mince.

4. Drizzle in a small amount of high-proof liquor and stir-fry to remove any off-flavors and enhance fragrance. Then add the diced bird’s eye chilies and green bell pepper.
**Note**: The pan may briefly catch fire when adding the liquor; this is normal—please ensure safety.

5. Drizzle one circle of light soy sauce along the edge of the pan for seasoning, then add the garlic scapes. Turn to high heat and quickly stir-fry for 30 seconds.

6. Sprinkle in a pinch of sugar to enhance freshness, and continue stir-frying for about ten seconds until all ingredients are evenly seasoned.

7. Turn off the heat, plate, and serve. The preserved black beans are savory, the pork mince is dry and aromatic, and the garlic scapes are crisp and tender—an excellent dish to accompany rice.

## Health Tips
Preserved black beans and pork mince contain high salt content. It is recommended to pair this dish with light vegetables to balance sodium intake. This dish is suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors; people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of preserved black beans as needed.
