# Stir-fried Razor Clams with Fermented Black Beans
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Razor clams – appropriate amount (split open in the middle for easy cleaning)  
🫘 Fermented black beans – appropriate amount (chopped a few times to release flavor)  
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Shallots – appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Spring onions – appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers – appropriate amount (sliced for color)  
🍋 Lemon slices – a few (used in the prepared sauce)  

🧂 Soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce – appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar – a pinch  
💧 Starch water – appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil – a pinch (optional)  
🌶️ White pepper – a pinch (optional)  
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount (added during blanching)  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the stir-fry sauce**: In a bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, a pinch of sugar, and an appropriate amount of starch water. Add sesame oil and white pepper to taste if desired. Stir well and set aside.  
2. **Blanch**: Bring water to a boil in a wok, add cooking wine, then quickly blanch the razor clams until the shells separate from the meat. Immediately remove and rinse under cold water to prevent the meat from becoming tough.  
**Key point**: Keep the blanching time short to maintain the clams’ tenderness.  

3. **Remove shells and membranes**: Take the clam meat out of the shells, carefully remove any sand and the black membrane, then drain and set aside.  
**Note**: The black membrane affects texture and must be thoroughly removed.  

4. **Sauté aromatics**: Heat the wok, add oil to coat the surface, and over maximum heat, add the ginger slices, shallots, garlic slices, and spring onion sections. Stir-fry until fragrant, then add the chopped fermented black beans and stir-fry until aromatic.  
5. **Stir-fry**: Splash in cooking wine, add the green and red bell pepper slices and the prepared clam meat. Stir-fry quickly and evenly.  
6. **Coat with sauce**: Pour in the prepared stir-fry sauce, keep the heat on maximum, and stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.  
**Key point**: No additional salt is needed at this stage—the clams are naturally salty, and the soy sauce and oyster sauce provide sufficient saltiness.  

7. **Plate**: Drizzle a little oil for shine, toss briefly, and serve on a plate.  

## Health Tips  
Razor clams are rich in high-quality protein and zinc, helping to boost immunity, but those with high cholesterol should consume them in moderation. Removing the membrane not only improves texture but also reduces sand residue. Avoid overcooking to keep the meat tender.
