# Matcha Streusel Cranberry Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
💧 Ice water 145g (0-5℃)  
🍶 Sweetened condensed milk 12g  
🍬 Caster sugar 22g (for dough)  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🍞 High-sugar tolerance dry yeast 3g (dissolve in a small amount of water)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 25g (chilled, added directly to dough)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (softened at room temperature, for streusel filling)  
🍬 Caster sugar 18g (for streusel filling)  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 6g (room temperature, for streusel filling)  
🥛 Milk powder 28g  
🍵 Matcha powder 2g (or cocoa powder 3g)  
🍒 Dried cranberries approx. 10g  
🥚 Egg liquid as needed (for brushing surface)  
🌰 Almond slices as needed (for surface decoration)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍞 Bread machine (with mixing paddle)  
🍥 Rolling pin  
🔪 Scraper  
🧤 Heat-resistant gloves  
🧊 Cooling rack  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Measuring cup/small bowl  
🥄 Spatula  

## Steps
1. Make matcha streusel filling: Mix softened unsalted butter at room temperature with caster sugar, add room temperature whole egg liquid and mix well, then add milk powder and matcha powder, combine into a dough, and set aside.
   **Key point**: Filling can be prepared in advance. If substituting cocoa powder for matcha powder, you get cocoa streusel filling.
2. Place bread flour, ice water, sweetened condensed milk, caster sugar, and salt into the bread machine bucket. Start the kneading program and knead for about 20 minutes, until the dough can form a thick membrane with jagged edges when torn.
   **Key point**: Use ice water at 0-5℃. Do not add yeast or butter at this stage to avoid friction-generated heat.
3. Remove the dough, cut into small pieces. Dissolve the dry yeast with about 3g of water to form a paste, mix it evenly with the dough pieces, then return them to the bucket. Continue kneading for about 10 minutes to distribute the yeast evenly.
   **Key point**: Dissolving dry yeast in water first makes it easier to incorporate evenly into the dough.
4. Check the dough. If it can form a thin but easily torn membrane, remove it, cut into small pieces, add the chilled unsalted butter, and restart the kneading program for about 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Butter does not need to be softened; add it directly from the refrigerator. Cutting the dough into pieces helps it incorporate quickly.
5. Knead until the dough can form a very thin, translucent, and elastic windowpane membrane that does not tear easily, with smooth edges when torn and noticeable resistance when stretching.
6. Remove the dough, shape it into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for about 20 minutes, until an indentation made with a floured finger does not spring back.
   **Key point**: Insufficient resting will make the dough difficult to roll out and prone to tearing, affecting the bread’s rise.
7. Place the rested dough with the smooth side up. Use a rolling pin to roll it from the center upwards and downwards into an oval shape. Flip it over so the smooth side faces down.
8. Evenly spread about 80g of matcha streusel filling over the dough, sprinkle with dried cranberries, roll it up from top to bottom, and pinch the seam to seal.
9. Use a scraper to cut the roll in half lengthwise. Place the cut sides facing up into the bread machine bucket after removing the mixing paddle.
   **Key point**: Baking with cut sides facing up gives a nicer pattern. Be sure to remove the bottom mixing paddle.
10. Start the fermentation program and let it ferment until the dough is about 80% full.
11. After fermentation, gently brush the dough surface with whole egg liquid. Optionally, sprinkle almond slices for decoration.
    **Note**: As the bread machine lacks an upper heating element, brushing with egg liquid gives the crust a more even and appealing color.
12. Close the lid and start the baking program. Bake for about 40 minutes.
13. Immediately after baking, wear heat-resistant gloves, remove the bucket, invert the bread onto a cooling rack, and let it cool until warm to the touch. Store in an airtight container.

## Health Advice  
Matcha is rich in antioxidants, and cranberries provide vitamin C, but the streusel and sugar make this bread relatively high in calories, suitable for occasional enjoyment. Mastering the windowpane test for the high-gluten dough is key; practice a few times to get the hang of it.
