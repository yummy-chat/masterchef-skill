# Braised White Radish with Cured Meat
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥕 White radish, about 2 pieces (peel and scrape off the spicy layer after peeling)  
🥓 Cured meat, 1 piece (steamed in advance)  
🧅 Scallions, 3 stalks (for steaming the cured meat and braising; some will be removed)  
🫚 Ginger, 3 slices  
🧄 Garlic, 5 cloves (slow-fried in cold oil to make garlic oil for fragrance)  
🍶 Yellow rice wine, as needed (for steaming the cured meat to remove odor)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, a small amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, half a teaspoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Peeler, chef's knife, and cutting board  
♨️ Steamer  
🍲 Braising pot / deep frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Small pot (for frying garlic oil)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the white radish**: First, peel off the tough outer skin, then carefully scrape away the darker, spicy layer. The scraped radish peels can be made into a side dish. Cut the radish into large, irregular chunks.  
2. **Prepare to steam the cured meat**: Lay scallion segments on a plate, place the whole piece of cured meat on top, add two slices of ginger, and drizzle with an appropriate amount of yellow rice wine. Steam the cured meat for 20 minutes in advance to soften it and reduce its saltiness.  
3. **Blanch the radish**: Place the radish in cold water, bring to a boil, and blanch for about 2 minutes to remove any unpleasant taste. Remove and rinse with cold water.  
4. **Slice and prepare ingredients**: Let the steamed cured meat cool slightly, then slice it. Slice another two slices of ginger, tie some scallions into a knot, and peel the garlic cloves. Set aside.  
5. **Start braising**: Place the blanched radish pieces in a pot, add enough boiling water, add the scallion knot and ginger slices, and bring to a boil over high heat. Boil uncovered for 5–6 minutes, then remove the scallion knot to prevent it from developing an unpleasant smell with prolonged cooking.  
6. **Add the cured meat and garlic oil**: Add the sliced cured meat to the pot. Keep the pot uncovered throughout to allow any unpleasant smells from the radish to dissipate fully. Meanwhile, in a separate small pot, add the garlic cloves to cold oil and fry over low heat until golden brown. Pour the fried garlic and a small amount of the garlic oil into the braising pot.  
7. **Season and finish**: When the radish can be easily pierced with chopsticks and its edges appear translucent, add half a tablespoon of oyster sauce, a small amount of salt, and half a teaspoon of chicken bouillon. Stir well and continue simmering for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  
8. **Serve**: Turn off the heat, transfer to a large bowl, and sprinkle with a small amount of chopped scallions.  

## Health Advice  
Cured meat has a high salt content, while radish has a diuretic effect. This dish is suitable for warming the body in winter, but people with high blood pressure should consume the broth sparingly. Steaming the cured meat in advance to reduce its saltiness is key, and pairing it with radish helps reduce greasiness.
