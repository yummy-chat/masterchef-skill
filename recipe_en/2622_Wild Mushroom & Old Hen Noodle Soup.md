# Wild Mushroom & Old Hen Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Old hen, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🦆 Old duck, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🍖 Jinhua ham, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🐖 Pork leg bone, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🐖 Pork trotter, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🐖 Lean pork, appropriate amount  
🐚 Conpoy, appropriate amount  
🍄 Morel mushrooms, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)  
🍄 Matsutake mushrooms, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)  
🍄 Cordyceps flowers, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)  
🍜 Noodles, appropriate amount (straight noodles used in the video)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍲 Large soup pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board and knives  
🍳 Small cooking pot  

## Steps  
1. Cut the old hen and old duck into pieces; chop the pork leg bones and pork trotters; slice the Jinhua ham. Pre-soak the dried wild mushrooms in clean water.  
2. Place all the cut chicken, duck, pork leg bones, pork trotters, lean pork, ham slices, and conpoy into a large soup pot. Add enough cold water.  
   **Key point**: All meat ingredients must be added to cold water, which helps blood foam surface naturally without causing a fishy taste.  
3. Bring the soup to a boil over high heat. When lots of foam rises to the surface, carefully skim off all the foam with a slotted spoon.  
   **Key point**: Thoroughly removing the foam is an important step for ensuring a clear soup and pure flavor.  
4. Reduce to low heat, cover the pot, and simmer for 4 hours, until all the meat becomes tender.  
   **Key point**: Do not add any seasonings during the entire process; rely on the ingredients themselves to release umami and collagen. The soup will gradually turn milky white.  
5. Add the pre-soaked morel mushrooms, matsutake mushrooms, and cordyceps flowers to the pot. Continue simmering on low heat for 2 hours.  
   **Key point**: This is the essential step for naturally blending the unique fragrance of wild mushrooms into the soup. Do not use high heat or shorten the time.  
6. In a separate small pot, bring water to a boil and cook the noodles until done.  
   **Key point**: When cooking the noodles, you can directly use the prepared stock as the broth base, allowing the noodles to fully absorb the umami flavor.  
7. Ladle the cooked noodles and the hot stock into bowls. Season only with an appropriate amount of salt according to personal taste.  
   **Key point**: Only salt is needed for seasoning; minimal seasoning allows the natural complex umami of the ingredients and the wild mushroom flavor to shine.  

## Health Tips  
This noodle soup is rich in collagen and various amino acids. The addition of wild mushrooms provides dietary fiber, offering abundant nutrition. However, individuals with high uric acid should be mindful of the purine content and consume in moderation.
