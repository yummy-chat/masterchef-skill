# Scrambled Eggs with Sliced Mandarin Fish
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Mandarin fish fillet, about 200g (cut into pieces about one finger thick)
🥚 Eggs, 4 pieces
🫚 Ginger slices, 1-2 slices
🧅 Scallions, chopped, as needed
🫑 Red bell pepper, diced, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🍬 Sugar, as needed
🌶️ White pepper, as needed
🫒 Peanut oil, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup spoon
🥣 Skimmer or slotted spoon
🍶 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Add salt, sugar, white pepper, and peanut oil to the mandarin fish fillets. Gently mix and marinate for 5 minutes.
**Note**: Do not add cornstarch during marination. Adding peanut oil makes the fish fillets smoother and more aromatic.

2. Add salt, sugar, white pepper, and peanut oil to the eggs. Whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture flows into a steady, continuous stream when lifted with chopsticks.
**Key**: Adding peanut oil to the egg mixture significantly enhances tenderness and aroma.

3. Heat the wok until smoking, discard the hot oil, then reduce heat to low and add cold oil. Test the oil temperature with chopsticks. When fine bubbles appear around them, pour in the egg mixture. Push the eggs with a soup spoon in one direction. When the eggs are just set with a slightly runny center, immediately remove them with a slotted spoon and set aside.
**Key**: Maintain medium-low heat throughout, and work quickly to ensure the eggs are tender.

4. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the mandarin fish fillets and quickly stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute, until the fillets curl, feel springy to the touch, and form a "lamp bowl" shape. Remove and drain the oil.

5. Leave a little oil in the wok. Return the fish fillets, diced red bell pepper, and chopped scallions. Immediately pour in the remaining uncooked egg mixture. Quickly stir-fry everything together until the eggs are set, then remove from heat immediately.
**Key**: The entire process must be fast to ensure tender eggs and smooth fish.

## Health Tips
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. Mandarin fish and eggs are both high-quality protein sources, making it suitable for those aiming to lose fat and build muscle. However, use less salt when marinating the fish to avoid excessive sodium intake. It is recommended to pair with leafy green vegetables for balanced nutrition. Gout patients should control the portion of fish.
