# Stir-Fried Chinese Cabbage with Yacai 🥬
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: All-year round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Chinese cabbage – appropriate amount (use only the stems and leaves, cut into wide strips)
🧆 Crushed yacai (preserved vegetable) – about half a packet (rinse thoroughly, squeeze dry)
🐖 Ground pork – appropriate amount
🌱 Chinese chives – appropriate amount (cut into sections, added at the end)
🧅 Ginger, scallion, garlic – appropriate amount each (minced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – appropriate amount (cut into segments)
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns – appropriate amount (whole)
🌱 Bean sprouts – appropriate amount (added when stir-frying the pork)
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount (for blanching)
🍬 White sugar – a pinch (for blanching)
🫗 Vegetable oil – appropriate amount (for blanching and stir-frying)
🧂 Chicken powder – appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine – about 2 tablespoons
🥄 Light soy sauce – about 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce – about half a tablespoon
🍯 Dark soy sauce – a little
🥣 Cornstarch slurry – a little (for a light thickening)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🥘 Wok spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the Chinese cabbage and cut into wide strips. Rinse the crushed yacai under water, then soak briefly, drain, and squeeze dry firmly. Cut the Chinese chives into sections, mince ginger, scallion, and garlic, and cut dried chili peppers into segments.
2. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine chicken powder, salt, cooking wine, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir well to create a "pre-mixed sauce" and set aside.
3. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Once boiling, add a little oil, salt, and sugar. Add the Chinese cabbage strips and blanch over high heat, stirring quickly, until just cooked through. Immediately drain well.
**Note**: Blanch quickly over high heat to prevent the cabbage from softening and losing its crisp texture.
4. Heat another wok, add cold oil, then add the ground pork. Stir-fry over medium heat until the pork changes color and breaks apart.
5. Add the minced ginger, scallion, garlic, bean sprouts, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili pepper segments to the pork. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Add the blanched Chinese cabbage and stir-fry quickly to combine.
7. Pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok, stir-fry evenly, then add a little cornstarch slurry to create a light thickening.
**Key**: Stir the sauce again before adding it to the wok.
8. Finally, add the Chinese chive sections and stir-fry over high heat for about 10 seconds until just wilted. Immediately remove from the wok and serve.
**Note**: Chinese chives cook very quickly; do not stir-fry for too long to prevent them from turning yellow and soft.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, making it suitable for those looking to manage weight or improve digestion. Ground pork and yacai provide quality protein, but individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of light soy sauce and oyster sauce.
