# Five-finger Fig, Poria, and Pearl Barley Tea
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥕 Five-finger fig 20g / 40g / 50g (corresponding to 750ml / 1500ml / 2000ml water, respectively)  
🍄 Poria 10g / 20g / 25g (corresponding to 750ml / 1500ml / 2000ml water, respectively)  
🌾 Cooked pearl barley 10g / 20g / 25g (corresponding to 750ml / 1500ml / 2000ml water, respectively)  
🍊 Tangerine peel 3g / 5g (corresponding to 750ml / 1500ml / 2000ml water, respectively)  
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed (optional, add after brewing)  
🍯 Honey, as needed (optional, add after brewing)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🫖 Glass health pot / Kettle  
🔥 Induction cooker  
🥣 Bowl (for rinsing ingredients)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Based on the number of servings, select the corresponding water amount, and weigh the five-finger fig, poria, cooked pearl barley, and tangerine peel in proportion.  
**Note**: If only raw pearl barley is available, dry-fry it in a pan over low heat until it turns slightly yellow and releases its aroma, then use as cooked pearl barley.

2. Rinse all dry ingredients clean with water, break the tangerine peel into pieces, then rinse again.
3. Place the rinsed ingredients into the glass pot, and add the corresponding amount of water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 30 minutes, until the liquid turns golden and gives off a grain and coconut milk aroma.  
**Key Point**: This is a simple version of the recipe, with a naturally light and sweet taste.

4. Turn off the heat, then add rock sugar or honey to taste if desired. Stir well and serve.

## Health Advice  
This dampness-eliminating and spleen-strengthening tea is suitable for people with high dampness and poor digestion in spring, but pregnant women and individuals with weak or cold constitutions should use with caution. It is recommended to drink without added sugar to preserve the natural coconut milk aroma of the five-finger fig.
