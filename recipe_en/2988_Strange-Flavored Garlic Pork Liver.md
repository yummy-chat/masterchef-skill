# Strange-Flavored Garlic Pork Liver
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork liver, appropriate amount (cut into 2cm cubes)
🧄 Garlic, 2-3 heads (peeled whole cloves, pre-cooked)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (some sliced for marinating, some minced for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (divided for marinating, stir-frying, and garnish)
🍶 Cooking wine, a little (for marinating) + 2 tablespoons (for strange-flavor sauce)
🐟 Steamed fish soy sauce, a little (for marinating)
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (to coat the pork liver cubes)
🧂 Salt, a little (for boiling garlic) + a little (for strange-flavor sauce)
🍶 White vinegar, a little (for boiling garlic)
🍬 White sugar, 2.5 tablespoons (for strange-flavor sauce)
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, a little (for strange-flavor sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon (for strange-flavor sauce)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a little (for strange-flavor sauce, for color)
🧂 Light soy sauce, 1.5 tablespoons (for strange-flavor sauce)
🌶️ White pepper powder, a little (for strange-flavor sauce)
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, 1 tablespoon (for strange-flavor sauce)
🍶 High-proof liquor, a little (optional, for drizzling along the pan rim at the end)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🏺 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok or deep pan
🥄 Spatula or wok ladle
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Cut the pork liver into cubes about 2 cm in size.
**Note**: Cubing helps seal in moisture during high-temperature frying, preventing the liver from becoming tough.

2. Rinse the pork liver cubes, drain well, then add ginger slices, scallion segments, garlic slices, cooking wine, and steamed fish soy sauce. Mix well and marinate briefly. Remove the liver and drain any excess liquid again. Add cornstarch and mix until each cube is evenly coated in a thin layer.
**Note**: Coating with starch is key to forming a crispy crust and protecting the tender interior.

3. Peel and rinse the garlic cloves. Add water to a pot, put in the garlic cloves, add a little salt and white vinegar, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and cook until the garlic cloves are tender. Remove and drain.
**Note**: Boiling garlic in salted and vinegared water reduces some of its pungency, making the garlic aroma milder.

4. In a bowl, add in order: 2.5 tablespoons white sugar, a little chicken bouillon powder, a little salt, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, a little dark soy sauce, 1.5 tablespoons light soy sauce, a little white pepper powder, 1 tablespoon fragrant vinegar, and 2 tablespoons cooking wine. Stir well to form the strange-flavor sauce.
**Note**: The strange-flavor sauce should be prepared in advance, featuring a complex taste of salty, sweet, sour, savory, and slightly numbing.

5. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 170-180°C. Add the coated pork liver cubes. Quickly fry until the surface is golden and crispy, and the inside is just cooked through. Remove and drain oil.
**Note**: High-temperature quick frying is the core technique for achieving "crispy outside, tender inside." Avoid prolonged frying to prevent the liver from becoming tough.

6. Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the cooked garlic cloves and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the minced ginger and stir-fry briefly. Pour in the prepared strange-flavor sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat until the sauce thickens and bubbles. Immediately add the fried pork liver cubes and quickly stir-fry to coat evenly with the sauce. Add a generous amount of scallions and toss rapidly.
**Note**: Since the strange-flavor sauce contains sugar and can easily caramelize and thicken when heated, it is essential to stir-fry over high heat the entire time, minimizing the liver's time in the wok to retain its crispiness.

7. Plate the liver over the reserved cooked garlic cloves and garnish with fresh scallions. If conditions allow, drizzle a little high-proof liquor along the hot wok rim to ignite for a burst of aroma—this is a professional technique and can be omitted for home cooking.

## Health Tips
Pork liver is rich in iron and effective for replenishing blood, making it suitable for people with anemia. However, those with high cholesterol should consume it in moderation. Quick frying at high temperature ensures a crispy exterior and tender interior, avoiding overcooking that can make the liver tough.
