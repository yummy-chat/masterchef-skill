# Universal Spicy Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Chopped onion 100g
🧄 Minced garlic 50g
🫚 Minced ginger 30g
🫘 Broad bean paste 120g (finely chopped in advance)
🫘 Soybean paste 100g (finely chopped in advance)
🫘 La Mei Zi chili sauce 30g (adds spicy aroma, can be omitted if not eating spicy)
🦪 Hoisin sauce 20g
🐔 Chicken bouillon 30g
🌰 Sesame seeds about 1 handful
🫗 Soybean oil 250g
🥓 Lard 50g
🌿 Five-spice powder 10g (pre-soaked in liquor)
🍶 Liquor as needed (for soaking five-spice powder)
🫕 Sesame paste as needed (for thickening and richness)
💧 Water as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Large spoon
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon

## Steps
1. Finely chop the onion, ginger, and garlic separately; finely chop the broad bean paste and soybean paste in advance.
2. Add soybean oil and lard to the wok, heat until the lard is completely melted.
3. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and minced ginger, reduce to low heat and stir-fry continuously until the onion aroma is released.
4. When the moisture in the onion, ginger, and garlic has evaporated and they are slightly golden, sprinkle in the sesame seeds and continue stir-frying until the sesame seeds release their fragrance.
5. First add the finely chopped broad bean paste, stir-fry quickly until red oil is released, then add the soybean paste, keep stir-frying over low heat to evenly combine and fully release the fragrance of both pastes.
6. Add the La Mei Zi chili sauce, hoisin sauce, and chicken bouillon, stir-fry evenly.
   **Note**: Keep the wok at a slight simmer to prevent the sauce from burning.
7. Add the five-spice powder pre-soaked in liquor, stir-fry briefly to allow the liquor aroma to evaporate.
8. Pour in an appropriate amount of water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, add the sesame paste, and gently stir until well combined.
9. Keep simmering over low heat, stirring occasionally, allowing the various pastes to fully fuse. Simmer until the sauce thickens and turns glossy red, and can coat the back of a spoon when lifted.
   **Key point**: Be patient during the simmering process; as the water evaporates, the sauce will thicken and the aroma will become richer.
10. Remove from heat and transfer to a bowl. A thin layer of red oil will naturally form on the surface, aiding storage. It is recommended to cover and let rest overnight for better flavor.

## Health Advice
This spicy sauce is relatively high in oil and sodium, making it suitable for flavoring noodles or as a dipping sauce. People with high blood pressure or those on a weight-loss diet should control their intake. When simmering, use low heat and stir patiently to prevent scorching.
