# Steamed Vegetable Dumplings  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Flour, as needed (all-purpose flour)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (added separately to dough and filling)  
💧 Warm water, as needed (for kneading dough)  
🍜 Vermicelli, as needed (soaked in advance and chopped)  
🥬 Chinese chives, as needed (chopped)  
🥕 Carrot, as needed (chopped)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (pre-cooked and crumbled)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (added to vermicelli for color)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed (for seasoning filling)  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (to enhance flavor and seal in moisture of filling)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🫗 Pot lid (for resting dough)  
🔪 Cutting board  
🥢 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer  

## Instructions  
1. Add an appropriate amount of salt to the flour and mix well. Gradually pour in warm water, stirring until it forms flakes, then knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Note**: Salt increases the dough's elasticity; using warm water makes the dough softer.  
2. Cover the dough with a lid and let it rest for 20 minutes.  
3. Add dark soy sauce to the soaked and chopped vermicelli, mixing well to color and season.  
4. In a large bowl, combine chopped chives, prepared vermicelli, chopped carrot, and crumbled cooked eggs. Add salt, chicken bouillon, and sesame oil, and mix thoroughly.  
   **Note**: Chives and carrot should be fresh and finely chopped; eggs must be pre-cooked and crumbled; vermicelli should be soaked until soft.  
5. Take out the rested dough, knead it evenly on the cutting board, roll it into a long strip, and cut into evenly sized dough pieces.  
6. Roll each dough piece into a thin, large circular wrapper.  
   **Key point**: Roll the wrapper as thin as possible for better texture.  
7. Place a generous amount of filling in the center of the wrapper, fold it in half, and pinch the edges tightly to seal.  
   **Note**: Use ample filling and ensure the edges are firmly sealed to prevent splitting during steaming.  
8. Bring water to a boil in the steamer, place the dumplings inside, and steam over high heat for 10 minutes.  
   **Heat and timing**: Steam after the water boils over high heat; the dumplings are done when the wrapper becomes translucent and the filling is fully cooked.  

## Health Tips  
These dumplings feature a soft, chewy wrapper and a fresh filling, rich in dietary fiber and carotene, making them suitable for weight management or calorie-controlled diets. Chives and eggs can produce sulfides, so those with excessive stomach acid should consume in moderation; ensure the edges are tightly sealed during steaming to prevent leakage.
