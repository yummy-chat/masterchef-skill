# Lazy Hamburger
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 Hamburger buns 2 slices (for 1 burger)  
🥚 Eggs about 2 (beaten into egg mixture)  
🧀 Cheese slice 1 piece  
🍖 Ham about 2 slices  
🥬 Lettuce 1-2 leaves  
🍅 Tomato about 2-3 slices  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (spray into the pan to prevent sticking)  
🥗 Mayonnaise as needed (squeeze onto tomato slices before assembling the burger)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick flat pan  
💨 Oil spray bottle  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Bowl (for beating eggs)  

## Steps
1. Wash the lettuce, wash and slice the tomato, set aside.  
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk thoroughly into an even egg mixture.  
3. Preheat the non-stick flat pan over low heat, spray a small amount of cooking oil.  
4. Pour in the egg mixture, gently shake the pan to spread the egg evenly across the bottom. When the bottom of the egg sets but the surface is still slightly damp, place two hamburger bun slices cut-side down side by side into the egg mixture, so the uncooked egg adheres to the bread.  
5. Continue heating over low heat until the bottom egg layer is fully set and the edges can easily lift off the pan. Use a wooden spatula to flip the entire egg layer along with the bun slices, and cook until the egg layer turns golden brown.  
6. While still hot, fold the excess egg layer around the bun inward to wrap the bread.  
7. On one bun slice, layer the cheese slice, ham slices, lettuce, and tomato slices in order, then squeeze on the mayonnaise.  
8. Place the other bun slice wrapped with the egg layer on top, gently press to form a complete burger.  

## Health Tips
This lazy hamburger wraps the bun in an egg layer, increasing protein and satiety, making it suitable for a quick energy boost at breakfast. Pair with unsweetened soy milk or a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Mayonnaise is high in calories, so it is recommended to substitute with a low-fat version or Greek yogurt.
