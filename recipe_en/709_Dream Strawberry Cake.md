# Dream Strawberry Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍪 Oreo cookies 80g (about 20 pieces, cream removed)  
🥛 Milk 230g (20g for the crust, remaining for melting gelatin)  
🍓 Strawberries 250g (half a pound, mashed) + 50g (for the glaze and decoration)  
🍮 Gelatin sheets 15g (about 3 sheets, for the mousse) + 5g (about 1 sheet, for the glaze)  
🥛 Thick yogurt 200g (one container)  
🍬 Sugar 20g (about 3 capfuls)  
💧 Water 50g (about 1.5 units)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🎒 Ziploc bag  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥣 Enamel bowl / large bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
💧 Strainer  
🔪 Knife  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Pot (for double boiler)  
🍰 Cake mold (or bowl)

## Steps
1. Make the crust: Remove the cream from the Oreo cookies. Place them in a bowl lined with a ziploc bag, add 20g of milk, and crush with a rolling pin until well mixed. Press firmly into the bottom of the mold.
   **Key point**: The crust must be pressed firmly to prevent it from falling apart when unmolding.
2. Make the strawberry puree: Wash and hull 250g of strawberries. Mash them in a bowl with a rolling pin until smooth.
3. Soften the gelatin for the mousse: Soak 3 gelatin sheets in cold water until completely softened.
4. Melt the gelatin: Drain the softened gelatin sheets and place in a small bowl. Add the remaining milk and heat over a double boiler, stirring until completely melted and the liquid is smooth with no lumps.
   **Key point**: Must be heated over a double boiler to avoid boiling the gelatin directly, which can render it ineffective.
5. Mix the mousse batter: Pour the gelatin-milk mixture into the strawberry puree and stir well. Add 200g of thick yogurt and 20g of sugar, and stir thoroughly until smooth.
6. Assemble the cake: Stick halved strawberries to the inner walls of the mold with the crust. Pour in the mousse batter.
7. Initial setting: Use a strainer to skim off any bubbles on the surface of the mousse. Refrigerate until completely set.
   **Key point**: Ensure enough refrigeration time for the mousse to fully set with no flow.
8. Make the glaze: Juice 50g of strawberries. Add 50g of water and 1 softened gelatin sheet. Heat and stir until combined, then let cool to room temperature.
   **Key point**: The glaze must be cooled, otherwise it will damage the mousse surface.
9. Glaze and decorate: Remove the set cake from the refrigerator. Pour the cooled strawberry jelly over the top. Gently smooth with a spoon. Refrigerate again until the glaze is set.
10. Unmold and final decoration: Once the cake is fully set, unmold and decorate the top with fresh strawberries.

## Health Advice
This cake uses yogurt and strawberries to replace some of the cream, reducing the saturated fat content compared to traditional mousse. However, the sugar and crust calories should still be noted. Suitable for occasional gatherings; for diabetics or those controlling sugar intake, it is recommended to reduce or omit the sugar.
