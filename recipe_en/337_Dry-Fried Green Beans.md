# Dry-Fried Green Beans
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable all year round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Green beans (适量, main ingredient, ends and strings removed)  
🥩 Pork belly (适量, minced, for added aroma)  
🧄 Ginger (适量, minced)  
🧅 Scallion (适量, minced)  
🧄 Garlic (适量, minced)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns (适量, for numbing flavor)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (适量, cut into sections, for heat and aroma)  
🍶 Cooking wine (适量, to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🧂 Salt (适量, for saltiness)  
🍬 Sugar (适量, to enhance freshness and balance flavor)  
🥄 Soy sauce (used in two batches: first for seasoning, second splash before serving to boost aroma)  
🦪 Oyster sauce (a small amount, to enhance freshness and flavor)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  

## Instructions  
1. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 50% hot. Add the green beans and fry over high heat until the surface wrinkles, turns tiger-skin-like, and the edges are slightly charred. Remove and drain the oil.  
**Key point**: The green beans must be fully cooked. For home cooking, you can use less oil and pan-fry them instead, but ensure they are cooked through and the skin blisters.  

2. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the minced pork belly and stir-fry over medium heat until it changes color, releases oil, and becomes fragrant.  
3. Once the pork is cooked, pour in the cooking wine and stir-fry. Then add the minced ginger, scallion, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Finally, add the dried chili pepper sections and Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry to release a complex aroma.  
**Note**: Add the ingredients in order to ensure the spices are fragrant without burning.  

4. Return the fried green beans to the wok. Stir-fry with the pork and other ingredients over high heat until well combined. Then season with salt, sugar, soy sauce, and oyster sauce.  
5. When a distinct wok aroma (wok hei) develops, drizzle a small amount of soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly a few times and serve immediately.  
**Key point**: The final splash of soy sauce effectively enhances the overall aroma of the dish.  

## Health Advice  
Green beans must be fully cooked, as raw beans may cause food poisoning—be sure to take note. This dish is high in oil and salt, so consumption should be moderated. For those on a diet, a low-oil version can be tried.
