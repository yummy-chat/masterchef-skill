# Beer Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken Thighs - to taste (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallion - to taste (for marinating)
🧄 Ginger - to taste (for marinating)
🧄 Garlic - to taste (for marinating)
🌸 Sichuan Peppercorns - to taste (black seeds removed, chopped with sesame seeds)
🌱 Sesame Seeds - to taste (chopped together with Sichuan peppercorns)
🥬 Celery - a small amount (finely chopped, added to batter)
🌾 Cake Flour - 500g
🌽 Cornstarch - 150g (part for marinating, part for batter)
🍚 Baking Powder - 30g
🍺 Beer - approximately 200g (used in two stages)
🧂 Salt - to taste (for first marination and batter)
🥄 Soy Sauce - to taste (for second marination)
🫒 Sichuan Pepper Oil - to taste (for second marination)
🌶️ Spiced Salt - to taste (for batter)
🫒 Salad Oil - a small amount (for batter)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Deep fryer or pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧺 Strainer or drying rack

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the chicken thighs into pieces and rinse thoroughly with cold water.
   **Note**: Do not use hot water, to avoid making the meat tough.
2. First marination: Place the chicken pieces in a bowl. Add scallion, ginger, garlic, and salt. Pour in beer to submerge the ingredients. Soak for about 15 minutes to remove any gamey smell.
3. Prepare the Sichuan peppercorns: Remove the black seeds from the peppercorns, then chop them together with the sesame seeds using a knife to create a Sichuan pepper-sesame mixture.
   **Key**: Removing the seeds prevents bitterness; chopping releases the full aroma.
4. Discard the liquid from the first marination. Add soy sauce, Sichuan pepper oil, the Sichuan pepper-sesame mixture, salt, and a small amount of cornstarch to the chicken pieces. Wearing gloves, mix thoroughly to ensure the flavor fully penetrates.
5. Prepare the batter: Mix the cake flour, remaining cornstarch, and baking powder. Add salt, chopped celery, Sichuan pepper-sesame mixture, minced scallion and ginger, spiced salt, beer, and a small amount of salad oil. Stir into a thick, smooth batter.
6. Add the marinated chicken pieces to the batter, ensuring each piece is evenly coated.
7. First frying: Heat the oil in a deep fryer to 170°C (340°F). Add the chicken pieces one by one. Fry until set and lightly golden, then remove.
8. Second frying: Increase the oil temperature. Return all the first-fried chicken pieces to the pot. Fry until the coating is caramel-colored and feels crispy, then quickly remove.
9. Use a slotted spoon to scoop out the chicken pieces and place them on a strainer or drying rack to drain excess oil thoroughly.
   **Key**: Ensure the oil is well-drained, or the coating will become soggy.
10. Plate the chicken. Optionally, garnish with fried cilantro pieces.
    **Note**: Do not add additional salt or spiced salt after frying, as this may affect the crispiness of the coating.

## Health Suggestions
This beer fried chicken is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but due to being fried twice, it is high in calories and fat. It is recommended to serve with a fresh vegetable salad to balance dietary fiber. Suitable for occasional indulgence, not for frequent consumption. Those with high blood lipids or those on a weight-loss diet should enjoy in moderation.
