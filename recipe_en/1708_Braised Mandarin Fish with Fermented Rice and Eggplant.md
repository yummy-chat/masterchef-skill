# Braised Mandarin Fish with Fermented Rice and Eggplant
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-seasons meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 mandarin fish (scaled, gutted, and cleaned)
🍆 About 2 eggplants (peeled and cut into strips)
🥕 About 2 carrots (peeled and grated for oil extraction)
🌿 Star anise, to taste
🌿 Cinnamon stick, to taste
🌿 Bay leaves, to taste
🧄 Ginger, to taste (sliced or smashed)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)
🌶️ Red chili pepper, to taste (minced)
🍲 Chicken broth, to taste
🧈 Chicken fat, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 Chicken powder, to taste
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste
🍾 Cooking wine, to taste
🌽 Dry potato starch (or cornstarch), to taste
🍚 Fermented rice (jiuniang), to taste
🌶️ Chili paste, to taste
🥄 Dark soy sauce, a pinch
🥃 Baijiu (white liquor), a pinch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle
🥢 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make carrot oil**: Pour salad oil into a wok, add chicken fat, and heat until melted. When the fat solids float, reduce to low heat, add the grated carrots, and fry slowly until dehydrated, golden brown, and fragrant. Strain out the carrot residue and reserve the oil.
   **Note**: Keep on low heat throughout, stir frequently to prevent scorching; do not let the oil temperature get too high, or the carrots will become bitter.
2. **Prepare spice oil**: Soak star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves in hot water briefly, then drain. Place them in a heatproof container, pour in hot chicken fat at about 150–160°C (300–320°F), and let steep to release fragrance.
   **Note**: The spices need to be moistened beforehand; the oil temperature should not be too high.
3. **Prepare the mandarin fish**: Make deep cuts down to the bone on both sides of the fish in a crisscross pattern. Rinse thoroughly under running water until the flesh turns white, then pat completely dry with paper towels. Rub the fish inside and out, including the cuts, with a marinade made from salt, chicken powder, cooking wine, and light soy sauce. Marinate for about 15 minutes.
   **Key point**: All blood must be washed out, and the fish must be completely dry to prevent oil splatter.
4. **Prepare the eggplant**: Peel the eggplants and cut them into strips about 5 cm x 1 cm (2 inches x 0.4 inches).
5. **Deep-fry the eggplant**: Heat oil to about 170°C (340°F). Deep-fry the eggplant strips over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes, until tender and lightly golden. Remove and drain the oil, then spread them on the bottom of a serving plate.
6. **Deep-fry the mandarin fish**: Evenly coat the marinated fish with a thin layer of dry potato starch. Heat oil to 180–200°C (355–390°F). Gently slide the fish into the oil. Do not move it at first; once it sets, use a ladle to gently nudge it to prevent sticking. Continuously ladle hot oil over the fish, focusing on thicker parts, until the whole fish is golden and crispy, and a chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part. Remove and drain the oil.
   **Key point**: High oil temperature for quick setting; ladling oil ensures even cooking.
7. **Sauté the base and braise**: Leave a little base oil in the wok. Sauté ginger slices, minced garlic, and minced red chili pepper until fragrant. Add the chili paste and stir-fry until the oil turns red. Add the fermented rice and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil.
8. **Season and reduce the sauce**: Add the fried mandarin fish. Season with light soy sauce and a little dark soy sauce for color. Taste and adjust with salt or sugar. Cover, reduce the heat to low, and braise for 3–5 minutes to infuse flavor. Uncover, increase the heat to high, and reduce the sauce until thick. Drizzle a little baijiu along the edge of the wok to release the aroma.
9. **Plate**: Transfer the braised mandarin fish to the plate with the eggplant strips on the bottom. Pour the remaining thick sauce from the wok over the fish.

## Finished Dish Characteristics
The fish is golden, the sauce is a rich red. The fish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. The eggplant soaks up the savory-sweet flavors of the fermented rice and chicken broth, offering a unique, slightly spicy aroma from the fermented rice and complex seasoning.

## Health Tips
This dish features tender fish and sauce-absorbing eggplant, providing a good balance of protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for people looking to build muscle or lose fat. Be mindful of the oil used for deep-frying. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of chili paste and salt.
