# Lettuce and Beef Congee
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice (appropriate amount, frozen into chunks in advance)
🥩 Beef (appropriate amount, thinly sliced)
🥬 Lettuce (appropriate amount, washed and chopped)
🫚 Ginger (appropriate amount, shredded, for marinating beef)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount, for marinating beef and final seasoning)
🌽 Cornstarch (appropriate amount, for marinating beef to lock in moisture)
🫙 Soy sauce (appropriate amount, to enhance flavor and color)
🧂 White pepper (appropriate amount, to enhance aroma and remove gamey taste before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🔥 Gas stove
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥄 Stirring spoon
🥄 Ladle

## Preparation Steps
1.  Freeze the rice into solid chunks in advance.
**Key point**: Frozen rice cooks into a bloomed porridge faster, saving time.
2.  Slice the beef thinly, add cornstarch, a little salt, and shredded ginger, and mix well to marinate.
**Key point**: Cornstarch locks in moisture to keep the meat tender and smooth; ginger removes gamey flavors.
3.  Wash the lettuce and chop it finely; set aside.
4.  Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the frozen rice chunks, cover, and bring to a boil over high heat.
**Key point**: Rice must be added only after the water is boiling.
5.  After boiling, stir until the rice grains are fully bloomed and the porridge base is thick.
**Consistency check**: Rice grains are soft and split open; the porridge base has a noticeable thickness.
6.  Slide the marinated beef slices into the boiling porridge base, and quickly stir to separate with a spoon.
**Key point**: The porridge must remain at a rolling boil; stir the beef immediately after adding to prevent clumping.
7.  Once the beef changes color, add the chopped lettuce.
**Key point**: Add the lettuce last; blanch briefly to maintain a crunchy texture and vibrant green color.
8.  Adjust seasoning by adding an appropriate amount of soy sauce and salt, and stir well.
**Note**: Salt was already added when marinating the beef; adjust final seasoning accordingly.
9.  Turn off the heat, sprinkle in a little white pepper, stir evenly, and serve.

## Health Tips
Beef congee paired with lettuce is low in fat and high in protein, suitable for weight loss or recovery periods. Using cornstarch to lock in moisture during marinating gives the beef a tender texture, but additional salt should be controlled to reduce sodium intake.
