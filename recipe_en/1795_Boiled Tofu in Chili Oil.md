# Boiled Tofu in Chili Oil
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 1 piece firm brine tofu (dense texture, cut into thumb-thick slices)
🥬 Bean sprouts, as needed
🥬 Bok choy, as needed
🍄 Fresh mushrooms, as needed
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)
🧄 Ginger, as needed (minced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (cut into sections)
🧂 Salt, about 2g (for blanching tofu)
🥜 Cooking oil, as needed (for stir-frying base and final oil drizzle)
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed
🍲 Hot pot base, 1 small box (50g, recommend beef tallow fermented Chongqing-style hot pot base)
🍯 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🥔 Potato starch, as needed (mixed with water to make a slurry)

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Soup bowl or large bowl
🥣 Small bowl (for starch slurry and garnishes)

## Instructions
1. Slice the firm brine tofu in half horizontally, then cut into small thumb-thick slices. Wash bean sprouts, bok choy, and fresh mushrooms. Finely chop scallions, ginger, and garlic. Cut dried chilies into sections.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add tofu slices along with about 2g salt, and blanch for about 2 minutes. Remove and transfer tofu along with the hot water into a bowl to keep it warm and prevent sticking.
3. In another pot, bring water to a boil with a little oil and salt. Blanch all the vegetables until just cooked and bright in color, about 10 seconds after the water returns to a boil. Remove and drain thoroughly, squeezing out excess water. Spread the vegetables on the bottom of a soup bowl.
4. Heat oil in a wok over low heat. Add minced scallions, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry until golden and fragrant. Add the entire box of hot pot base and continue stir-frying over low heat until fully melted and aromatic.
5. Once the base is fragrant, add light soy sauce and about 3 ladles of water. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low and simmer for 2-3 minutes to fully release the flavors. Use a slotted spoon to remove any solid residue from the broth.
6. Gently place the warm tofu into the broth. Add about 2g salt and some dark soy sauce. Simmer over medium-low heat for 3-5 minutes to allow the tofu to absorb the flavors.
**Note**: Handle gently to avoid breaking the tofu.
7. Drizzle in the potato starch slurry, stirring gently, to lightly thicken the broth. Pour the tofu and broth over the vegetables in the soup bowl.
8. Sprinkle minced garlic and chopped scallions over the tofu. Place dried chili sections in a ladle, pour in hot oil. Once the chilies start to darken slightly, immediately pour the hot oil and chilies over the garlic and scallions on the tofu to release their aroma.

## Health Tips
This boiled tofu dish is rich in plant-based protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss or vegetarian diets. Blanching the tofu and keeping it in hot water prevents sticking. Squeezing excess water from the vegetables helps avoid a watery broth. When drizzling the final oil, the chilies should only be slightly darkened; adjust spiciness to taste.
