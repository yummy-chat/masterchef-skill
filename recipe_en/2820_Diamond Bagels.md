# Diamond Bagels
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 300g
🍬 Caster sugar 10g (for the dough)
🧂 Salt 5g
💧 Water 170ml (for the dough)
🫙 Yeast 2g (about 1/2 teaspoon)
💧 Water 1L (for boiling bagels)
🧂 Caster sugar 50g or baking soda 8g (for boiling bagels, choose one)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stand mixer or food mixer
🔪 Bench scraper
🪓 Rolling pin
📜 Baking paper
🍲 Large pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫓 Baking sheet
🔥 Oven (with convection/ fan-forced function)
🧊 Cooling rack
⏱️ Timer

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the dough**: Mix the bread flour, 10g caster sugar, salt, and yeast together. Add 170ml water and knead with a stand mixer until the dough surface is smooth.
   - **Status check**: The dough surface is smooth.

2. **Rest and divide**: No initial fermentation is needed. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15 minutes, then evenly divide it into 6 equal portions.

3. **Shape into balls and rest**: Shape each portion into a tight ball. Cover and let rest again until the dough becomes soft and easy to roll out.

4. **Shape the bagels**: Take one rested dough piece, roll it into a rectangle, then roll it up tightly and pinch the seam to seal. Roll it into a long rope. Taper one end to a point, and flatten the other end into a fan shape with a rolling pin. Wrap the pointed end around the flattened end to form a ring, and pinch the join firmly to seal.
   **Key point**: The join must be pinched tightly, otherwise it will open during boiling or baking.

5. **Proof**: Place the shaped bagels on a baking sheet lined with baking paper. Proof at room temperature for about 1 hour, until they have visibly increased in size.
   - **Status check**: The bagel rings become noticeably plumper and lighter.

6. **Boil the bagels**: Bring 1L of water to a boil in a large pot. Add 50g caster sugar or 8g baking soda, keeping it at a gentle simmer. Carefully place the proofed bagels into the water and boil for 20–30 seconds per side. Remove with a slotted spoon, drain excess water, and place them back on the baking sheet.
   **Key point**: Do not boil for too long, otherwise the interior will become too dense; the surface should look slightly wrinkled after boiling.

7. **Bake**: Immediately transfer the boiled bagels to the middle rack of a preheated oven set to 200°C fan-forced (convection). Bake for about 16 minutes. Halfway through, rotate the baking sheet for even browning.
   - **Status check**: The bagel surface turns golden brown and develops cracks.

8. **Cool and finish**: Remove the bagels from the oven and immediately transfer them to a cooling rack. Listen closely – if you hear a faint "crackling" sound from the surface, the "diamond pattern" has formed successfully.
   - **Finished product characteristics**: Crispy crust, chewy interior, with a distinctive diamond-shaped crack pattern.

## Health Advice
These bagels achieve a crispy crust and a chewy, dense interior through boiling in sweetened water followed by high-temperature fan-forced baking. They are moderate in calories and suitable for breakfast or light meals. Pair with avocado or cream cheese to supplement healthy fats and protein, while being mindful of portion size to avoid blood sugar spikes.
