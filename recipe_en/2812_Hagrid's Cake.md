# Hagrid's Cake  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍫 1 chocolate chiffon cake base (6 inches, pre-baked, sliced into 3 layers, each about 2 cm thick)  
🥛 200g + 250g heavy cream (Blue Windmill, for Oreo cream and raspberry cream respectively)  
🍪 20g Oreo cookies (remove filling, ground into powder) + 10g (broken into small pieces) + 10g (whole, for decoration)  
🍫 10g Valrhona chocolate crispy pearls  
🍓 75g raspberry jam  
🍫 20g white chocolate (for writing)  
🫒 Vegetable oil (e.g., coconut oil), as needed (to adjust white chocolate fluidity)  
🍬 12g + 15g powdered sugar  
🧂 0.5g salt  
🎨 Green food coloring, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🔪 Cake leveler/long knife  
🥄 Offset spatula  
🌀 Cake turntable  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🖌️ Piping bag  
📄 Parchment paper  

## Instructions  
1. Divide the pre-baked and cooled 6-inch chocolate chiffon cake base into 3 equal layers, each about 2 cm thick. Set aside.  
2. Remove the filling from the Oreo cookies. Grind 20g into powder, break 10g into small pieces, and reserve the remaining 10g whole for decoration.  
3. Make the Oreo cream: Combine 200g heavy cream, 12g powdered sugar, 0.5g salt, and 20g Oreo powder. Beat with an electric hand mixer until stiff peaks form.  
4. Make the raspberry cream: Combine 250g heavy cream, 15g powdered sugar, and 75g raspberry jam. Beat until stiff peaks form, resulting in an even pink color.  
5. Make the green chocolate sauce: Melt 20g white chocolate over a double boiler. Add enough coconut oil to adjust fluidity, then stir in a few drops of green food coloring until evenly mixed. Transfer to a piping bag with a small cut tip and set aside.  
6. Assemble the base layer: Spread a small amount of Oreo cream on the cake board to secure the first cake layer. Place the first cake layer on top, spread about 50g of Oreo cream, and sprinkle with Oreo pieces.  
   **Note**: The cream does not need to fully cover the edges; maintain a rustic, handmade texture.  
7. Spread about 60g of additional Oreo cream over the Oreo pieces, smooth it out, and evenly sprinkle 10g of chocolate crispy pearls.  
8. Place the second cake layer on top, and repeat steps 6 and 7.  
9. Place the final cake layer on top and gently press down to compact.  
10. Take about 120g of raspberry cream and spread it over the top and sides of the cake. Roughly smooth it with an offset spatula, keeping a rustic, handmade texture.  
11. Use a toothpick to scratch irregular cracks on the surface of the cake.  
12. Referring to the original image, use the green chocolate sauce to write "HAPPEE BIRTHDAE HARRY" on the cake.  
13. Refrigerate the cake for 1 to 2 hours to allow the cream to set and the flavors to meld. Then slice and enjoy.  

## Health Tips  
Hagrid's cake is quite high in calories, so it is recommended to have a small serving. Pair it with black coffee to cut the richness. It is perfect for Harry Potter-themed parties or chocolate lovers. Note that the cream needs to be refrigerated until set before slicing for the best texture.
