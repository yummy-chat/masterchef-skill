# Old-Fashioned Sugar Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Wheat flour 330g (300g for dough scalding, 30g to knead into the rested dough)
🍬 White sugar 20g (for dough scalding) + 1 tablespoon (for filling)
🍯 Brown sugar 1 tablespoon (for filling)
🫘 Sesame seeds ½ tablespoon (mostly white sesame seeds, with a few black sesame seeds, for filling)
🫒 Edible vegetable oil 30g (for dough scalding) + 20g (for kneading dough) + 1 level tablespoon (for filling) + oil for deep frying as needed

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥢 Rolling pin
🫓 Silicone kneading mat
🛡️ Plastic wrap
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Dough scraper
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1.  Bring 600g of water to a boil in a wok. Add 20g of white sugar and 30g of oil, stirring until completely dissolved. Pour in 300g of flour. Stir with a rolling pin over the lowest heat until no dry flour or lumps remain.
   **Note**: The lowest heat prevents the flour from burning; thorough mixing is the key to preventing the sugar cake from cracking later.
2.  Pour the scalded dough onto a silicone mat. Cut it into small pieces to cool completely. After cooling, knead it into a smooth dough ball. Flatten it, then add the remaining 30g of flour and 20g of oil. Knead repeatedly until the oil is fully absorbed. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: Let it cool completely before kneading; kneading in additional flour and oil is the core step for flakiness; seal with plastic wrap to prevent drying.
3.  In a bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of white sugar, 1 tablespoon of flour, ½ tablespoon of sesame seeds, and 1 level tablespoon of oil. Mix until evenly combined.
   **Note**: This filling will not harden even after cooling, allowing for a generous amount of filling that is sweet but not greasy.
4.  Do not knead the rested dough. Directly roll it into a long log and cut it into evenly sized pieces (dough rounds). Shape each piece into a half-sphere, ensuring the cut side faces the same direction. Take one piece, press a deep indentation in the center with your thumb, place a spoonful of the sugar filling inside, and slowly seal the opening using the "tiger mouth" (the web between thumb and index finger). Finally, gently press it into a flat patty of even thickness.
   **Note**: Facing the cut side of all pieces in the same direction reduces the risk of sugar leaking during frying; the opening must be sealed tightly to prevent sugar leakage and cracking during frying; even thickness ensures even cooking.
5.  Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 170°C (340°F) (five heats). Gently slide the raw patties into the oil along the edge of the wok. Do not stir immediately after adding. Once the bottom is set, gently shake the wok to prevent sticking.
   **Note**: A 170°C oil temperature prevents the surface from burning; sliding along the edge avoids oil splatter; only move the patties after they are set to prevent breakage.
6.  Maintain the lowest heat throughout. Use chopsticks to turn the patties frequently and fry for about 2-3 minutes. When the sugar cakes float to the surface, puff up in the center, and turn golden brown and crispy, remove them with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a plate.
   **Note**: The lowest heat avoids a burnt exterior and raw interior; frequent turning ensures even browning; floating and puffing up are signs of doneness.

## Health Advice
Old-Fashioned Sugar Cake is a deep-fried snack high in carbohydrates and fat. It is suitable for occasional indulgence but not for frequent consumption. It is recommended to pair it with green tea or sour plum drink to aid digestion. Diabetics and individuals on a weight loss diet should consume it sparingly.
