# Dry-Braised Pomfret
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Pomfret 2 fish (about 500g, cleaned and dried, cross-hatch scored on both sides)
🥩 Pork leaf fat (or pork belly) to taste (cut into small cubes)
🫚 Ginger to taste (cut into small cubes)
🧄 Garlic to taste (cut into small cubes)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms to taste (cut into small cubes)
🎍 Winter bamboo shoots to taste (cut into small cubes)
🧅 Scallions to taste (chopped)
🌶️ Pixian chili bean paste to taste
🍶 Cooking wine to taste
🍬 White sugar to taste
🍶 Rice vinegar to taste
🌿 White pepper powder to taste
🍶 Edge-of-wok vinegar a small amount
🫒 Sesame oil a small amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Chinese wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥄 Ladle
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the pomfret and pat dry. Score cross-hatch cuts on both sides down to the bone.
2. Cut the pork leaf fat, ginger, garlic, shiitake mushrooms, and winter bamboo shoots into evenly sized small cubes. Chop the scallions.
3. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot. Add the pomfret.
   **Note**: The oil temperature must be high enough. Avoid frequent turning after adding the fish. Gently flip once the fish is set. Fry until golden and firm, then remove and drain oil.
4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the pork fat cubes and stir-fry over medium-low heat until they shrink and render clear fat.
   **Key point**: Fully render the pork fat to enhance the savory aroma.
5. Add the ginger cubes, garlic cubes, shiitake mushroom cubes, and winter bamboo shoot cubes. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Push the ingredients to one side. Add the Pixian chili bean paste to the remaining oil and stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red. Then mix well with the other ingredients.
   **Key point**: The chili bean paste must be stir-fried thoroughly over low heat to remove any raw taste.
7. Splash in the cooking wine and add enough water, enough to submerge half the fish.
8. Add white sugar, rice vinegar, and white pepper powder. Stir to combine. Use a slotted spoon to remove all solid bits from the liquid, leaving only the broth.
9. Bring the broth to a boil. Add the fried pomfret. Reduce to low heat and braise.
10. During braising, continuously spoon the broth over the fish. Carefully flip the fish once. Braise over low heat for about 8-10 minutes, until the broth reduces and thickens and the fish is cooked through.
11. Gently remove the fish and plate it. Increase heat to high and reduce the broth until thick enough to coat a spoon, bright red, and with glistening oil.
12. Turn off heat. Add a small splash of edge-of-wok vinegar and sesame oil to the thick sauce. Sprinkle in the chopped scallions and stir well.
13. Pour the prepared sauce evenly over the fish on the plate.

## Health Advice
Pomfret is low in fat and high in protein. Although the dry-braising method creates a rich aroma, it is also high in oil and salt. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for a balanced meal. Pixian chili bean paste and pork fat enhance the flavor, but those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
