# Noodle Nut Walnut
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Butter 100g (softened at room temperature)
🥚 Egg 1
🍫 Cocoa powder 15g
🌾 Cake flour 210g
🥜 Walnuts appropriate amount (chopped)
🥜 Peanuts appropriate amount (chopped)
🥜 Cashews appropriate amount (chopped)
🥜 Melon seeds appropriate amount (chopped)
🥜 Almonds appropriate amount (chopped)
🌿 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount
🌾 Cooked flour appropriate amount (for binding)
🍬 Soft white sugar 75g (added to dough)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔧 Homemade iron clip
🔪 Ordinary small knife
♨️ Oven
📋 Baking tray
🧻 Cloth (for heat protection)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Mix butter and sugar**: Place the room temperature softened butter and soft white sugar in a bowl. Rub by hand until fully combined into a uniform paste.
   **Note**: Butter must be softened in advance for easier mixing.
2. **Add egg**: Crack the egg into the bowl, continue stirring until the egg is fully incorporated with the butter-sugar mixture, forming a uniform creamy paste.
3. **Color and flavor**: Add cocoa powder, stir until the color is even.
4. **Add flour**: Sift the cake flour and add it to the bowl. Knead by hand into a dough until no dry flour remains, forming a uniform dough.
   **Key**: Do not over-knead, to prevent the dough from developing gluten and affecting the texture.
5. **Prepare filling**: Chop walnuts, peanuts, cashews, melon seeds, almonds, and other nuts. Add cooking oil, white sugar, and cooked flour, mixing until it can be formed into a ball. Divide the filling into small balls of about 18g each.
   **Note**: The nut ratio and amount of oil and sugar can be adjusted to personal taste, as long as the mixture can bind into a ball.
6. **Divide dough and wrap filling**: Divide the dough into small portions of about 12g each. Take one, flatten it, place a filling ball in the center, slowly push the edges upward with the thumb and index finger to enclose the filling, then roll into a ball.
   **Ratio**: Dough to filling ratio is approximately 12g:18g.
7. **Shape the walnut**: Pinch one end of the filled ball into a point to mimic the walnut stem, and pinch a raised "walnut ridge" in the middle to form the basic shape.
8. **Press main lines**: Use the back of a small knife to press a deep line in the center of the "walnut ridge", then press intersecting lines on both sides of the dough to mimic the natural seams of a walnut shell.
9. **Create surface texture**: Use a homemade iron clip to repeatedly pinch the surface of the dough at various angles, creating small indentations and textures to make it uneven, closely resembling a real walnut shell.
   **Key**: This step requires patience; the texture should be naturally irregular to achieve a convincingly realistic effect.
10. **Bake**: Arrange the shaped walnut dough pieces neatly on a baking tray. Place in the preheated oven at the middle level, bake at 180°C (356°F) for about 10 minutes, until the dough is firm and the color has slightly deepened. Remove from the oven.
    **Note**: Baking time should not be too long to avoid excessive moisture loss, which can make the texture dry.

## Health Advice
This noodle nut walnut dish is high in calories but rich in nuts, suitable for those needing energy replenishment. Control the amount of sugar and oil to avoid spikes in blood sugar and weight gain.
