# Boiled Lettuce with Hot Water
Cuisine: Common Home-Cooked Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Lettuce, as needed (washed in advance, stored in a container)
🥚 2 eggs (cracked into a bowl, with a small amount of water added)
🧂 Soy sauce, as needed (drizzled in at the end for seasoning)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Stainless steel milk pot (for boiling water, blanching vegetables, and cooking eggs)
🥣 Small stainless steel milk pot with handle (for serving the finished dish)
🥡 Glass container (for storing raw lettuce before blanching)
🥄 Soup spoon (metal, for scooping boiling water)
🥢 Black soup spoon (for retrieving the cooked eggs)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the washed lettuce into a glass container and set aside.
2. Place a stainless steel milk pot on the stove, add water, and bring to a boil.
3. Use a soup spoon to scoop the boiling water and pour it directly over the raw lettuce in the glass container until the water completely submerges the vegetables.
   - **Key point**: Blanching with boiling water preserves the crisp texture of the lettuce.
4. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a small amount of water, and beat thoroughly with chopsticks to form an egg mixture.
5. Pour the egg mixture along with any remaining water in the bowl into another pot of boiling water, cover with a lid, and keep boiling for 5–8 minutes.
   - **Key point**: Adding water to the bowl before beating helps the eggs turn out more tender and smooth.
6. Once the eggs are cooked to a tender and smooth consistency, use a spoon to scoop them out and place them at the bottom of the small milk pot with a handle.
7. While still hot, drizzle in an appropriate amount of soy sauce, mix well, and serve.

## Health Tips
This dish is low in fat, high in fiber, quick, and refreshing, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or seeking light meals. Blanching the lettuce with boiling water retains its crisp and tender texture, while adding water to the eggs before cooking results in a smoother texture. When adding soy sauce, be careful with the amount to avoid making the dish too salty.
