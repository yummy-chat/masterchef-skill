# Stir-Fried Indian Lettuce with Canned Dace in Black Bean Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Indian lettuce 1 bunch (washed, stems and leaves separated)
🐟 Canned dace in black bean sauce half can (black beans and dace separated)
🫚 Ginger a small piece (sliced)
🧄 Garlic several cloves
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for blanching)
🐖 Lard 1 small piece (for stir-frying and sautéing)
🧂 Salt as needed (for blanching and seasoning)
🍬 Sugar as needed (for blanching and seasoning)
🍶 Cooking wine as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce a little
🥣 Water starch a little

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Large ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the root end of the Indian lettuce into four sections, then make a horizontal cut in the middle to separate stems and leaves, placing them in separate bowls.
2. Open the can of dace in black bean sauce, separate the black beans from the dace pieces; remove the main bone from the dace pieces, and cut the fish into large chunks.
3. Slice the ginger, peel the garlic cloves, and set aside.
4. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add a little oil, salt, and sugar. Blanch the stem pieces first, maintaining medium-high heat.
5. When the stems are about half cooked, add the leaves, blanch for 2–3 seconds until bright green, then immediately remove and drain.
**Key Point**: The blanching time is very short to prevent the vegetables from becoming overdone and releasing water.
6. In another wok, melt the lard, add the black beans, and stir-fry until fragrant.
7. Add the ginger slices and garlic cloves, pour in cooking wine along the edge of the wok to deglaze.
**Note**: Wait until the alcohol smell of the cooking wine dissipates and a complex aroma develops before proceeding.
8. Add the dace pieces and gently stir-fry until evenly mixed.
9. Turn the heat to maximum, add the drained Indian lettuce, and stir-fry quickly.
**Key Point**: High heat and quick stir-frying lock in moisture, maintaining a crisp texture.
10. Add a suitable amount of salt, sugar, and a little oyster sauce, stir-fry quickly to season evenly.
11. Drizzle in a little water starch to thicken, then stir-fry quickly a few more times and immediately remove from heat.

## Health Suggestions
The savory saltiness of dace in black bean sauce combined with the refreshing taste of Indian lettuce creates a perfect calcium-rich and palate-cleansing pairing, especially suitable for office workers needing an appetite boost. Note that while lard adds fragrance, use it sparingly; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of black beans.
