# Braised Pork Belly with Taro

Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 1 large piece (about 500g, ask for a square piece when purchasing)  
🌰 Taro 1 (large, peeled and cut into thick slices)  
🧅 Scallions 5-6 stalks (some tied into knots, some chopped)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced and minced)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (minced)  
🦐 Dried shrimp / dried shrimps 1 small handful  
🌿 Star anise, bay leaves, and other spices appropriate amount  
🍺 Beer about half a can  
🧈 Fermented bean curd 2-3 pieces  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🥫 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🥫 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount  
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt a little  
🍬 Sugar a little  
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for frying the meat and taro, plus a little extra for stir-frying the sides)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Flat-bottomed pan or deep pan  
🧴 Deep fryer (or deep pan + frying basket)  
♨️ Electric steamer or regular steamer  
🥘 Small saucepan or flat-bottomed pan  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large steaming bowl (with lid or can be covered)  
🍽️ Plate  
🪥 Pricking tool (fork or pricking pin)  

## Steps  
1. Place the pork belly in a pot of cold water with star anise, bay leaves, ginger slices, scallion knots, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook until a chopstick can easily pierce through the meat. Remove and drain.  
2. While still hot, use a pricking tool to densely pierce the pork skin, at moderate depth, ensuring the entire surface is evenly pricked.  
3. Heat enough oil in a pan. Place the meat skin-side down into a frying basket and fry over low to medium-low heat until the skin turns golden and blistered. Remove and immediately immerse in cold water until the skin forms a crackling pattern.  
4. Using the hot oil from frying the meat, fry the taro slices until lightly golden on the surface and slightly firm at the edges. Remove and drain.  
5. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine minced ginger, minced garlic, fermented bean curd, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, cornstarch, salt, sugar, and beer. Stir until the fermented bean curd is completely dissolved.  
6. Slice the cooled meat into pieces about 0.5 cm thick. Add the slices to the sauce and mix well with your hands, ensuring each piece is evenly coated.  
7. Take a steaming bowl and alternately layer meat slices and taro slices, starting with meat-skin side down. Pour any remaining sauce from the bowl over the top.  
8. Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the bowl in the steamer, steam over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 1 to 1.5 hours, until the meat is tender and can be easily pierced with chopsticks.  
9. Once steamed, remove the bowl. Discard some of the released liquid. Cover the bowl with a plate, quickly invert it onto the plate, and remove the bowl. Sprinkle with chopped scallions.  
10. In a separate pan, heat a little oil. Sauté minced garlic until fragrant, then quickly stir-fry chives and dried shrimp until just cooked. Serve as a side dish alongside the braised pork.  

## Health Advice  
This braised pork belly with taro is rich in protein and high-quality fats. Taro provides dietary fiber, making it suitable for winter nourishment or for those with high physical energy demands. Note that pork belly is high in fat; those with high blood lipids or those on a weight-loss diet should consume in moderation. When steaming, skim off excess surface oil if possible.
