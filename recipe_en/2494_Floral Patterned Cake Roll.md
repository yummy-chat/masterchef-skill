# Floral Patterned Cake Roll  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 White butter, as needed (softened)  
🍡 White bean paste, as needed (room temperature, ratio 10:1 with white butter)  
🥛 Milk, as needed (to adjust bean paste consistency)  
🎨 White food coloring, a small amount  
🎨 Colorful food coloring, as needed  
🌹 Freeze-dried roses, 2 pieces  
🍬 Sugar, 8g  
🍮 Gelatin sheets, 5g (pre-soaked until soft)  
🥚 Eggs, 5 pieces  
🥛 Milk, 50g (for cake batter)  
🫒 Odorless cooking oil, 50g  
🌾 Cake flour, 60g  
🍬 Granulated sugar, 60g  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed  
🧀 Mascarpone cheese, as needed  
🍚 Powdered sugar, as needed  
🎨 White food coloring, a small amount (for surface frosting)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowls  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🥢 Hand whisk  
🍳 Saucepan  
🥄 Strainer  
🔥 Oven  
🍪 Baking sheet  
📄 Parchment paper  
🍩 Piping bags  
🌸 Piping tips  
📌 Piping nail  
🔪 Offset spatula  
✂️ Piping scissors  
📏 Baking ruler/rolling pin  

## Steps  
### Bean Paste Piped Flowers  
1. Mix softened white butter and room-temperature white bean paste at a ratio of 1:10 until well combined. Gradually add milk in small amounts, mixing until the paste is smooth and easy to pipe.  
2. Divide a portion of the bean paste and add white or other colored food coloring.  
   **Note**: The actual color when mixing may deviate slightly; add coloring in small batches. When piping flowers, it is essential to coordinate the speed of turning the piping nail with your left hand and squeezing the bean paste with your right hand. Uneven speeds can cause the flowers to become distorted or jagged. The angle and direction of the piping tip directly affect the flower shape.  

### Rose Milk Panna Cotta  
3. Add 260g milk, freeze-dried roses, and 8g sugar to a saucepan. Heat until just simmering, then remove from heat.  
4. Add the pre-soaked softened gelatin sheets and stir until completely dissolved. Let it cool to room temperature, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a mold. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until fully set.  

### Cake Roll  
5. **Egg Yolk Batter**: Place egg yolks, 50g milk, and 50g oil in a bowl and whisk to combine. Sift in the cake flour and stir until smooth.  
6. **Meringue**: Gradually add the granulated sugar to the egg whites in several additions, using an electric hand mixer to whip to a soft peak stage.  
   **Key Point**: Do not overwhip the meringue to a stiff peak, as this may cause the cake roll to crack when rolling.  
7. **Mixing**: Add one-third of the meringue to the egg yolk batter and fold in roughly using a hand whisk with a cutting motion. Then pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and fold until just combined. Finally, use a spatula to clean the sides of the bowl, taking care not to deflate the batter.  
8. **Baking**: Pour the batter into a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spread it evenly, and gently tap the sheet on the counter to release large air bubbles. Bake in a preheated oven at 150°C for about 30 minutes, until the surface is golden brown and springs back when lightly pressed.  
   **Key Point**: Immediately after removing from the oven, cover the cake surface with another piece of parchment paper. Allow it to cool completely before peeling off the paper from both sides – this creates a delicate “towel-textured” surface.  

### Assembly & Decoration  
9. Add mascarpone cheese and powdered sugar to heavy cream, and whip until it reaches a spreadable consistency.  
10. Trim the edges of the cake sheet. Spread an even layer of cream over the surface. Pipe a thicker line of cream along the side where you will begin rolling. Place the set rose milk panna cotta bar on top of this cream line.  
11. Using a rolling pin to assist with the parchment paper, roll the cake tightly. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight to set.  
    **Key Point**: Mascarpone cream provides better support than plain heavy cream, which helps the cake roll hold its shape.  
12. Remove the set cake roll from the refrigerator and trim off the uneven ends. Add a small amount of white food coloring to the remaining cream to achieve a pure white color, then spread it over the top of the cake roll.  
13. Using piping scissors, transfer the pre-made bean paste flowers onto the surface of the cake roll. Pay attention to color coordination and varying flower sizes to create a spring-like atmosphere.  

## Health Advice  
This cake roll is high in sugar and fat, making it suitable as a celebratory dessert to be enjoyed in small portions. Those with high blood sugar should carefully control their intake.
