# Secret Chili Oil
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 100g
🌶️ Fine chili powder 100g
⚪ White sesame seeds, to taste
🌿 Five-spice powder 5g
🧂 Salt 8g
🍬 Sugar 5g
🍶 High-proof baijiu (liquor) 20ml
🫒 Cooking oil 50ml + additional as needed
⭐ Star anise 4 pieces
🌳 Cinnamon sticks 3 pieces
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 30+ pieces
🍃 Bay leaves 4-5 pieces
🧅 Onion, to taste
🫚 Ginger slices, to taste
🧅 Scallion, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, to taste
🧄 Garlic slices, to taste

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon
🥄 Ladle

## Instructions
1. In a stainless steel bowl, combine coarse chili flakes, fine chili powder, five-spice powder, salt, and sugar. Mix thoroughly.
   **Note**: A blend of coarse and fine chili is essential; the coarse flakes provide aroma and heat, while the fine powder contributes color.
2. Add 20ml of high-proof baijiu and 50ml of cooking oil. Stir until the chili mixture is moist and loosely combined.
   **Key**: Pre-moistening the chili with alcohol and oil prevents scorching and bitterness when hot oil is added later.
3. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok. Add star anise and cinnamon sticks to cold oil. Heat over low flame and fry slowly for about 2 minutes until fragrant.
   **Note**: Maintain low heat throughout to fully infuse the oil with the spices’ aroma.
4. Add Sichuan peppercorns and bay leaves. Continue frying over low heat for 2-3 minutes. Proceed to the next step as soon as the bay leaves change color.
5. Add onion, ginger slices, scallion, cilantro, and garlic slices. Fry over low heat until all vegetable ingredients turn golden brown and release a rich fragrance.
   **Note**: The fried solids can be reserved for braising meat.
6. Use a strainer to remove all fried solids from the oil, retaining only the clear infused oil. Heat the oil to approximately 70% of its smoking point (about 210°C / 410°F).
   **Key**: 70% hot oil is the standard temperature for the first pour.
7. **First pour**: Ladle about one-third of the hot oil into the chili mixture. Stir quickly and evenly.
   **Purpose**: To release the red oil while preventing localized overheating.
8. Sprinkle in white sesame seeds. Gently mix to combine.
   **Note**: Add sesame seeds after the first pour to avoid direct high heat that could create bitterness.
9. **Second pour**: Add another one-third of the hot oil, pouring while stirring rapidly.
   **Purpose**: To unlock the chili’s aroma.
10. **Third pour**: Pour in all remaining hot oil. Stir until well combined.
    **Explanation**: The three-stage pouring method achieves a layered effect: “first for aroma, second for red color, third for heat.”
11. Let it rest briefly before use. The finished chili oil will be bright red and richly aromatic—perfect for cold dishes, noodle tosses, and more.

## Health Advice
Chili oil is rich in capsaicin and antioxidants, which can stimulate appetite. However, it is high in calories, so a serving of 1-2 tablespoons is recommended. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy food, but caution is advised for individuals with stomach issues or acne-prone skin.
