# Potato Stuffed Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 2 (for making the dough)
🥩 Ground meat appropriate amount (pork)
🧅 Onion 1 (finely diced)
🌾 All-purpose flour appropriate amount (add until the dough can hold together)
🧂 Salt 1 tsp (for dough) + appropriate amount (for filling)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🥤 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🥤 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🌿 Five-spice powder appropriate amount
🖤 Black sesame seeds appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Peeler
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
♨️ Steamer
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Potato masher or spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Electric griddle
🖌️ Oil brush
🫗 Glass lid
🍴 Silicone spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. Peel the potatoes and slice them thinly, then steam them in a steamer until fully cooked.
   **Key Point**: Slice the potatoes as thinly as possible to ensure they cook through faster.
2. Place the steamed potatoes in a bowl and mash them into a smooth puree using a masher. Add one teaspoon of salt and an appropriate amount of flour, stir with chopsticks until it forms flaky bits, then knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
   **Key Point**: Do not use too much flour; just enough to form a dough is sufficient.
3. In the ground meat, add the diced onion, an appropriate amount of salt, cooking wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and five-spice powder. Mix thoroughly and marinate for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to develop.
4. Lightly oil your hands to prevent sticking, then roll the dough into a long log and divide it into evenly sized pieces. Take one piece and press it into a round disc with your palm. Wrap in an appropriate amount of meat filling, pinching the edges tightly shut as you would for a baozi. Place the sealed side down and shape into a round patty.
   **Key Point**: Ensure the edges are tightly sealed to prevent the filling from leaking during cooking.
5. Sprinkle black sesame seeds on the surface of the raw patties and gently press them in to adhere.
6. Preheat the electric griddle and brush with oil over medium heat. Place the raw patties on the griddle and flatten them slightly with your hand. Cook until one side is set and lightly golden, then flip. Cover with a lid and let it cook for 2-3 minutes. Flip again and cook until both sides are golden brown.
   **Key Point**: Cooking covered is crucial to ensure the filling is fully cooked through.
7. Once both sides are golden, gently press the patty with a spatula. If the patty puffs up and springs back quickly when pressed, it indicates the inside is fully cooked. Remove from the griddle.
   **Key Point**: The patty puffing up and springing back quickly is a sign of complete doneness.

## Health Tips
The crust of these potato stuffed pancakes is made from mashed potatoes, making them lower in calories compared to fully flour-based versions. They are suitable for those who want to control their carbohydrate intake but still enjoy stuffed pancakes. The meat filling paired with onions enhances flavor and aids digestion. However, when marinating, reduce the amount of salt and oyster sauce to avoid excessive sodium intake.
