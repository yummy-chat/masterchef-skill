# Sweet Pepper Sausage Boule  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 180g (80%)  
🌾 Whole wheat flour 20g (10%)  
🌶️ Sweet paprika powder 6g (3%)  
🧂 Salt 2.2g (1%)  
🍞 Yeast 48g (24%)  
💧 Water 157g (78.5%)  
🫑 Diced sweet pepper 25g (12.5%, charred with a torch before use)  
🌭 Diced Harbin red sausage 25g (12.5%)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍞 Stand mixer/mixing bowl  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🧺 Proofing basket  
📜 Parchment paper  
🪒 Lame (bread scoring knife)  
🍲 Dutch oven with lid  
♨️ Oven  

## Instructions  
1. Prepare the dough the night before: Mix bread flour, whole wheat flour, sweet paprika powder, salt, yeast, and water until the surface is smooth.  
2. Prepare the filling: Mix the diced sweet pepper with the diced Harbin red sausage, char with a torch until lightly blackened to release flavor, then cool and set aside.  
3. The next day, remove the dough, gently pat to release gas, roll it out into a sheet, and evenly spread the charred filling over it.  
4. Fold the dough to encase the filling, place it seam-side up into a floured proofing basket, and allow it to undergo final proofing.  
5. Preheat the oven to the standard temperature for Dutch oven bread baking, placing the Dutch oven inside to preheat as well.  
6. Once proofing is complete, turn the dough out onto parchment paper. Using a lame, score a diagonal slash, then make another cut close to the edge.  
7. Transfer the dough with the parchment paper into the preheated Dutch oven, cover with the lid, and bake.  
8. After a certain baking time, remove the lid and continue baking until the surface is golden brown and the crust is hard and crisp.  
   **Key point**: The loaf is ready when it has visibly expanded, and the crust is hard, crisp, and golden brown.  

## Health Tips  
This sweet pepper sausage boule combines whole wheat flour and sweet paprika powder for a more balanced nutrition profile, making it suitable as a breakfast or light lunch. The Harbin red sausage and charred sweet pepper offer a unique smoky flavor, but due to the high salt content in the sausage, those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
