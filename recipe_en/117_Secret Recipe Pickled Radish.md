# Secret Recipe Pickled Radish
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥕 White radish, about 2 pounds (washed, skin left on)
🌿 Cilantro roots, 30g (only the roots with a bit of stem attached)
🫚 Ginger, 20g (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, 20g (smashed flat)
🌶️ Small chili peppers, 10g (sliced into rings)
🍬 White sugar, 25g
🧂 Salt, about one small teaspoon
🥄 Light soy sauce, 200ml
🫒 Sesame oil, 10ml
🫙 Sichuan pepper oil, 5ml

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks (two, for assisting with decorative cutting)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the white radish lengthwise into two halves. Place the cut side down on the cutting board and lay one chopstick along each side. Slice straight down until the knife touches the chopsticks, creating deep but intact comb-like cuts. Then split the radish from the middle, and cut into pieces after every 4-5 slices.
   **Key point**: Elevating with chopsticks ensures the bottom remains uncut; this decorative cut makes the radish easier to flavor and more visually appealing.
2. Place the cut radish pieces into a bowl, add 25g white sugar, and mix thoroughly by hand. Let sit for 30 minutes until the radish releases water, shrinks slightly in volume, and becomes more pliable.
   **Key point**: Using sugar instead of salt to draw out water also removes the radish's pungent spiciness.
3. Put the ginger slices, smashed garlic, chili pepper rings, and cilantro roots into another bowl. Add about one small teaspoon of salt and knead by hand for about 1 minute, until the spices release their juices.
   **Key point**: Salt's osmotic pressure quickly extracts the flavor from the spices.
4. Into the spice bowl, sequentially add 200ml light soy sauce, 10ml sesame oil, and 5ml Sichuan pepper oil. Stir well to form the seasoning sauce.
5. Squeeze the pickled radish pieces firmly by hand to remove excess water, then place them in a clean bowl.
   **Key point**: Squeeze out as much water as possible for a crisper texture and better sauce absorption.
6. Pour the prepared sauce along with the spices over the radish, mix thoroughly by hand, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.
   **Note**: The longer it marinates, the more flavorful it becomes. Store in the refrigerator.

## Health Tips
This pickled radish uses sugar instead of salt to draw out water, effectively removing spiciness while maintaining a crisp texture. It is suitable as an appetizer or side dish all year round. High in fiber and low in calories, but note that the soy sauce is high in sodium, so consumption should be moderated. The longer it marinates in the refrigerator, the deeper the flavor.
