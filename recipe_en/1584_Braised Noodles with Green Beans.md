# Braised Noodles with Green Beans
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

**Sauce A (for steaming and braising noodles)**  
💧 Water (1 bowl)  
🧂 Salt (适量)  
🥄 Soybean soy sauce (适量)  
🥄 Light soy sauce (适量)  

**Sauce B (for mixing into finished dish)**  
🥄 Light soy sauce (适量)  
🥄 Soybean soy sauce (适量)  
🧂 Salt (适量)  
🫒 Sesame oil (适量)  
🧄 Minced garlic (适量)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Steamer (with steamer rack and cloth)  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥣 Large bowl (for preparing Sauce A)  
🥄 Small bowl (for preparing Sauce B)  
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the green beans**: Wash the green beans, then break them into segments by hand and set aside.  
2. **Prepare the noodle steaming liquid**: In a large bowl, combine water, salt, soybean soy sauce, and light soy sauce. Stir well to make Sauce A and set aside.  
3. **Prepare the finishing sauce**: In a separate small bowl, combine light soy sauce, soybean soy sauce, salt, sesame oil, and minced garlic. Stir well to make Sauce B and set aside.  
4. **Steam the noodles**: Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Line the steamer rack with a cloth, add the noodles, and sprinkle with a little water. Once steam rises, steam over high heat for about 10 minutes.  
5. **Sauté the pork belly**: Add cooking oil to a cold wok, then add the sliced pork belly. Cook over low heat slowly until the fat renders, the slices become translucent, and the edges are slightly crispy.  
   **Key point**: Start with cold oil, cook over low heat slowly, to fully render the fat.  
6. **Aromatics**: Add the star anise, scallion segments, and ginger slices. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
7. **Stir-fry the green beans**: Add the green bean segments and stir-fry until the color deepens and the skin becomes slightly wrinkled.  
8. **Braise**: Pour in Sauce A. The liquid should be roughly level with or slightly below the green beans. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for about 2 minutes, ensuring the green beans are fully cooked.  
   **Note**: Green beans must be cooked thoroughly to prevent poisoning.  
9. **Steam the noodles**: Loosen the steamed noodles and spread them evenly over the green beans and liquid. Cover with a lid, reduce to low heat, and steam for 5 minutes. This allows the noodles to fully absorb the broth through steam.  
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low and slow to prevent burning; adjust the steaming time based on heat and noodle thickness.  
10. **Finish and mix**: Remove the lid, drizzle in Sauce B, and use chopsticks and a spoon to toss from the bottom up, ensuring the noodles, green beans, pork belly, and sauce are evenly combined. Mix well and serve while hot.  

## Health Tips  
This braised noodle dish with green beans offers balanced nutrition. Pork belly provides protein and fat, while green beans are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost. Note that green beans must be fully cooked to prevent poisoning, and the noodle portion should be moderate to avoid excess carbohydrates.
