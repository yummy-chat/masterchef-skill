# Egg Roll
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 1 egg
💧 Water, as needed (to adjust batter consistency)
🖤 1 tablespoon black sesame seeds
🌾 1 tablespoon flour
🧂 Salt, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🧴 Plastic bottle (with funnel)
🍳 Non-stick pan
📜 Parchment paper
🥅 Strainer (slotted spoon)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Take a clean plastic bottle, attach a funnel, and crack the egg into the bottle.
2. Add an appropriate amount of water into the bottle, along with one tablespoon of black sesame seeds, one tablespoon of flour, and a suitable amount of salt.
3. Tighten the bottle cap and shake the bottle vigorously to mix all ingredients evenly, forming a batter with good fluidity.
   **Key point**: Thorough shaking is essential for a uniform egg roll texture; ensure no dry flour clumps remain.
4. Heat a non-stick pan over low heat and line it with a sheet of parchment paper.
5. Open the bottle cap and squeeze the prepared batter onto the parchment paper in the pan.
6. Immediately use the back of a strainer to spread and flatten the batter outward into a thin, even circular sheet.
   **Key point**: Work quickly after the batter enters the pan to prevent premature setting.
7. Keep heating over low heat until the edges of the egg skin slightly lift and the surface is mostly set. Use chopsticks to swiftly roll the egg skin from one end to the other in one motion.
   **Key point**: The action must be fast and smooth. If the egg skin is overcooked, it becomes brittle and prone to breaking.
8. After rolling, let it set briefly, then remove from the pan.
   **Key point**: Maintain low heat throughout; the entire process from adding batter to rolling takes about 1–2 minutes. The finished product should be golden yellow with black sesame seeds dotted on the surface.

## Health Advice
This egg roll is rich in protein and calcium from black sesame seeds, making it suitable for a quick energy boost at breakfast. However, the use of flour and pan-frying with parchment paper increases calorie content, so portion control is advised. Pair with vegetables or fruit to balance nutrition.
