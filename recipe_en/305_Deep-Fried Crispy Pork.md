# Deep-Fried Crispy Pork

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year round

## Ingredients
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (skin removed, cut into long strips with alternating fat and lean layers)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🌰 Sweet potato starch, 80g
🌾 All-purpose flour, 10g
🧅 Scallion, 1 (tied into a knot)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (toasted and coarsely ground)
🧂 Salt, 5g (for marinating) + appropriate amount (for batter)
🤍 White pepper powder, 3g
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili powder, appropriate amount (for finishing)
🫘 Sesame powder, appropriate amount (for finishing)
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount (for finishing)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🪨 Mortar and pestle
🥣 Glass bowl
🍽️ Plate
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Skimmer

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the pork**: Remove the skin from the pork belly and cut it into long strips with alternating fat and lean layers.
2. **Prepare the Sichuan peppercorns**: Place the Sichuan peppercorns in a dry wok and toast over low heat until fragrant. Grind them into coarse granules using a mortar and pestle.
⚠️ Do not grind the Sichuan peppercorns too fine, as this may cause bitterness during frying.
3. **Marinate the pork strips**: Add salt, white pepper powder, cooking wine, the scallion knot, ginger slices, and some of the coarsely ground Sichuan peppercorns to the pork strips. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
4. **Prepare the egg batter**: Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk until smooth. Add the sweet potato starch, all-purpose flour, salt, and the remaining coarsely ground Sichuan peppercorns. Stir in one direction until a smooth, ribbon-like batter forms.
⚠️ The batter should have a suitable consistency—too thick will result in a hard texture, while too thin may cause the coating to fall off.
5. **First fry (setting)**: Heat oil in a wok to around 40-50% hot. Add the marinated pork strips one by one, gently stirring with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Fry for about 5 minutes, until the strips are set and float to the surface. Remove and drain excess oil.
6. **Second fry (coloring)**: Increase the oil temperature to around 60-70% hot. Add the first-fried pork strips and fry again until the coating is golden brown and makes a crisp sound when tapped. Remove immediately.
⚠️ The second fry is key to achieving a crispy texture; carefully monitor the color change.
7. **Finish and serve**: Drain the fried crispy pork and arrange on a plate. Mix the chili powder, sesame powder, and MSG together, then sprinkle evenly over the crispy pork. Serve.

## Health Advice
Crispy fried pork is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve alongside stir-fried vegetables or cold cucumber salad to balance dietary fiber intake. Suitable for occasional gatherings or indulgence; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
