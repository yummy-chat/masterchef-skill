# Seafood and Pork Zongzi  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐚 Abalone: about 12 (total weight 2.3 jin 9 liang, large size, need to clean, remove shells, and score)  
🦐 Large shrimp: about 24 (large, need to peel and devein)  
🥩 Pork belly: 2 jin 2 liang (black pork, cut into pieces)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms:适量 (small shiitake mushrooms are more fragrant than large ones, soak for 15 minutes in advance)  
🍚 Glutinous rice: 3 jin (rinsed and drained)  
🍃 Zongzi leaves:适量 (dried zongzi leaves, soak in cold water for half an hour or more until soft)  
🧅 Light soy sauce:适量 (for seasoning the rice when stir-frying)  
🧅 Dark soy sauce:适量 (for coloring the meat and rice when stir-frying)  
🧂 Salt: about 2 small spoons  
🧂 MSG: about 1 small spoon  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board (for cutting meat, handling seafood and mushrooms)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying pork belly, seafood, mushrooms, and glutinous rice)  
🔥 Pressure cooker (for quickly cooking the zongzi)  
♨️ Induction cooker (heat source, can set power and time)  
🥄 Strainer spoon (for scooping out ingredients)  
🥣 Large bowl/basin (for rinsing and soaking glutinous rice and zongzi leaves)  
✂️ Kitchen scissors (for trimming zongzi leaves and cutting cotton string)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Soak dried zongzi leaves in cold water for half an hour or more until soft. After soaking, wash them clean.  
2. Place small dried shiitake mushrooms in a bowl, add boiling water, and soak for 15 minutes in advance.  
3. Scrub off the black mucus from the surface of the abalone, remove the meat, score a crosshatch pattern on the thicker side, then cut in half.  
4. Cut the pork belly into pieces of moderate size.  
5. Peel the shrimp and remove the shrimp meat.  
6. Rinse the glutinous rice and drain the water. Set aside.  
7. Pour oil into the wok. Once hot, add the pork belly pieces and stir-fry over medium-high heat until the surface turns golden and lard is rendered. Remove the excess lard and set aside. Add dark soy sauce to the pork belly and stir-fry until evenly colored. Then add the shrimp and abalone, turn to medium-high heat and stir-fry quickly. Finally, add the soaked shiitake mushrooms and stir-fry evenly.  
   **Key point**: Do not stir-fry the seafood for too long to avoid making it tough and hard.  
8. Return the reserved lard to the wok and heat it up. Add the drained glutinous rice and stir-fry over medium-high heat. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, and MSG in sequence. Stir quickly until the rice is evenly colored and seasoned. If the rice becomes too dry while frying, sprinkle in a small amount of boiling water.  
   **Doneness indicator**: The glutinous rice has a uniform color and is stir-fried to about 70% done.  
   **Key point**: The rice for savory zongzi must be stir-fried to release its aroma; if raw rice is used directly, the cooking time will be significantly longer.  
9. Take two softened zongzi leaves, overlap them crosswise, and roll one end into a cone shape. First add a small amount of stir-fried glutinous rice, then place abalone, shrimp, shiitake mushrooms, and pork belly in sequence. Cover the filling with more glutinous rice and smooth the surface. Fold the top part of the leaves down to cover, tuck in the sides neatly, then fold tightly and secure with cotton string by tying a knot.  
   **Key point**: Overlapping the leaves crosswise increases strength. The wrapping must be tight to prevent the zongzi from falling apart during cooking.  
10. Arrange the wrapped zongzi neatly in the pressure cooker inner pot. Add enough water to completely submerge all the zongzi. Close the lid and lock the valve. Start timing once steam is released.  
    **Time and heat**: Induction cooker set at 1300 watts, cook for 25 minutes after steam is released.  
    **Key point**: Let the pressure release naturally before opening the lid. Using a pressure cooker significantly shortens the cooking time.  

## Health Advice  
This seafood and pork zongzi is rich in ingredients. The pork belly and glutinous rice provide ample energy, while the abalone and large shrimp supply high-quality protein, making it suitable for people with high physical exertion. However, glutinous rice and fatty meat are high in oil and fat, so it is recommended to eat only one per serving and pair it with vegetables to cut the richness.
