# Penglai Yellow Croaker 🐟

**Cuisine:** Shandong Cuisine (Lu Cai)  
**Difficulty Level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Autumn (September–October)  

---

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🌿  
- 🐟 1 large yellow croaker (from Ningde, Fujian – deboned with skin intact)  
- 🦀 10+ hairy crabs (steamed, shelled for crab meat and roe)  
- 🥓 Pork skin (as needed, for making aspic)  
- 🐔 Old hen (as needed, for stock)  
- 🍖 Jinhua ham (as needed, for stock and garnish)  
- 🐓 Chicken feet (as needed, for gelatinous stock)  
- 🦐 Shrimp paste (as needed, for sealing)  
- 🫘 Fresh green soybeans (for stuffing and garnish)  
- 🧈 Lard (as needed, for stir-frying stuffing)  
- 🧅 Aged ginger (as needed, for marinating fish and stock)  
- 🧅 Scallions (as needed, for marinating fish and stock)  
- 🌶️ Dried chili peppers (as needed, for marinating fish)  
- 🌿 Sichuan peppercorns (as needed, for marinating fish)  

---

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment 🛠️  
- 🔪 Bamboo knife (homemade, for deboning)  
- ✂️ Scissors (for cutting the backbone)  
- ♨️ Steamer (for steaming crabs)  
- 🍳 Wok (for stir-frying stuffing, making stock, and thickening sauce)  
- 🥄 Strainer (for filtering stock)  
- 🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing stuffing)  
- 🎨 Piping bag (for squeezing shrimp paste to seal the fish)  
- 🛢️ Oil pot (for deep-frying fish)  
- 🥢 Slotted spoon (for retrieving fish)  
- 🥄 Ladle (for seasoning)  

---

## Preparation Steps 📋  

### **Mise en Place**  
1. **Deboning the yellow croaker:**  
   - Insert a homemade bamboo knife into the fish’s mouth, working the blade gently in a "push-pull" motion to separate the flesh from the bones without breaking the skin.  
   - Snip the backbone with scissors and remove the entire skeleton, leaving the fish intact and leak-proof.  
   **Key point:** Work gently during deboning to avoid tearing the skin.  

2. **Marinating the fish:**  
   - Stuff the cavity with aged ginger, scallions, dried chilies, and Sichuan peppercorns.  
   - Pour in *huadiao* wine and water, add salt, and let the fish marinate to remove any fishy odor and enhance aroma.  
   **Key point:** Marinate long enough to ensure effective deodorizing.  

3. **Making the stock and aspic:**  
   - Steam the hairy crabs, then pick out the crab meat and roe.  
   - Sauté the crab shells, fish bones, blanched pork skin, old hen, Jinhua ham, chicken feet, scallions, and ginger together.  
   - Add boiling water and simmer for about 12 hours.  
   - Strain the stock, season, and cool until it sets into aspic. Cut into small cubes and set aside.  
   **Note:** The stock is the core of the flavor—slow simmering is essential. Aspic melts when heated, so keep it cool after dicing.  

4. **Preparing the stuffing:**  
   - In a wok, use lard to sauté the crab meat and roe with minced ginger.  
   - Add green soybeans and pearl fish balls, stir-fry.  
   - Season with salt, white pepper, and pine mushroom seasoning. Let the mixture cool completely.  
   - Once cool, mix the stuffing with the aspic cubes.  
   **Note:** The stuffing must be fully cooled before adding the aspic to prevent premature melting.  

---

## Cooking Steps 🔥  

1. **Stuffing and sealing:**  
   - Fill the marinated fish’s cavity with the prepared stuffing.  
   - Use a piping bag to squeeze shrimp paste into the fish’s mouth, sealing the opening completely so the filling does not leak out.  

2. **Coating and deep-frying:**  
   - Prepare a light, crispy batter and coat the fish evenly.  
   - Heat oil in a wok and deep-fry the fish over medium-low heat, continuously basting the fish with hot oil.  
   - Fry until golden, crispy, and cooked through.  
   - **Handle with care** to keep the skin intact and the stuffing sealed.  
   **Key point:** Control the oil temperature at medium heat to avoid a raw interior with a burnt exterior.  

3. **Making the sauce and plating:**  
   - Remove the fried fish and arrange it on a serving plate.  
   - In another wok, heat clear stock with Jinhua ham and green soybeans.  
   - Season with salt, oyster sauce, and dark soy sauce, then thicken with a cornstarch slurry to create a glossy, glass-like sauce.  
   - Pour the hot sauce evenly over the fish.  
   **Note:** The sauce should be smooth and shiny, covering the fish uniformly.

## Health Advice 🩺  
Yellow croaker and crab roe are rich in high-quality protein and collagen but are also calorie-dense. This dish is ideal for banquets—consume in moderation. People with gout should be cautious, as both crabs and the stock are high in purines.
