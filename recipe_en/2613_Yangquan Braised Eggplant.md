# Yangquan Braised Eggplant
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 1 purple long eggplant (peeled, cut into rolling knife chunks)  
🫑 1 green bell pepper (cut into diamond-shaped pieces)  
🍅 1 tomato (stem removed, cut into chunks, skin left on)  
🧄 Several cloves of garlic (minced)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped)  
🥚 1 egg  
🌾 Flour, equivalent to 1 egg yolk in volume  
🌽 Cornstarch or sweet potato starch, as needed (for dusting, batter, and sauce slurry)  
🧂 A pinch of salt  
🍶 3 tablespoons light soy sauce  
🍶 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce (braising soy sauce)  
🍶 1 tablespoon fragrant vinegar  
🍶 2 tablespoons cooking wine  
🍬 3 tablespoons sugar  
🧂 A pinch of chicken bouillon  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for deep-frying the eggplant; leave a small amount for stir-frying)  
💧 1 teaspoon water  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or skillet  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🥣 Sauce bowl  
🥣 Large bowl  

## Instructions  
1.  Peel the eggplant and cut into rolling knife chunks. Sprinkle with a little salt and toss gently. Let sit until water is released.  
2.  Gradually sprinkle cornstarch or sweet potato starch over the eggplant. Toss gently to coat each piece evenly with a thin layer of starch. Let sit to allow the starch to absorb moisture.  
3.  Cut the green bell pepper into diamond-shaped pieces. Remove the stem from the tomato and cut into chunks. Mince the garlic. Chop the scallions.  
4.  In a small bowl, combine a small amount of starch, 2 tablespoons cooking wine, 3 tablespoons light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon fragrant vinegar, 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce, 3 tablespoons sugar, and a pinch of chicken bouillon. Stir well to make the sauce.  
5.  In another bowl, crack the egg. Add cornstarch and flour in equal volume to the egg yolk. Mix into a smooth batter.  
6.  Pour the prepared egg batter over the starch-coated eggplant pieces. Toss gently to ensure each piece is evenly coated with the batter.  
7.  Pour enough cooking oil into the wok. Heat over high heat until bubbles form rapidly around chopsticks. Add the eggplant pieces one by one. Press lightly with a slotted spoon. Fry until the surface is slightly charred and mottled in color. Remove and drain excess oil.  
8.  Pour out the excess oil, leaving a small amount in the wok. Heat over high heat. Add the chopped scallions and minced garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
9.  Add the tomato chunks. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until they release juice. Add a little water if needed to soften further. Cook until the sauce thickens.  
10.  Turn the heat to high. Add the green bell pepper pieces and stir-fry a few times. Add the fried eggplant pieces. Toss to coat them with the tomato sauce.  
11.  Pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok. Do not stir immediately. Wait until the sauce comes to a boil.  
12.  Quickly stir-fry three times to evenly coat the ingredients with the sauce. Immediately turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.  

## Health Tips  
Yangquan Braised Eggplant is sweet, sour, salty, and fragrant. The deep-fried eggplant, paired with tomato sauce, helps cut the richness, making it a great accompaniment to rice. For those on a diet, portion control is recommended. Vitamin C is prone to loss at high temperatures, so it is best to quickly stir-fry the green bell pepper at the end to preserve nutrients.
