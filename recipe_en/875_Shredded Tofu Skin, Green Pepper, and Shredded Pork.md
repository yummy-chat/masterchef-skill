## Cooking Steps
1. Wash all ingredients. Shred the smoked tofu skin, pork, green pepper, red pepper, and onion; shred the dried chili and ginger, and slice the garlic. Set aside as aromatics.  
   **Note**: All ingredients should be uniformly cut into shreds for even cooking.  
2. Heat the wok, pour in an适量 amount of rapeseed oil, add the shredded pork, and stir-fry over high heat until cooked through.  
   **Key Point**: The wok must be heated before adding oil and meat to prevent the shredded pork from sticking.  
3. Once the shredded pork is fragrant, add the aromatics and shredded onion. Reduce to medium heat and stir-fry until aromatic.  
   **Key Point**: Add the shredded onion first to release fragrance.  
4. Add the shredded smoked tofu skin, stir-fry evenly, and cook for a short while.  
5. In a small bowl, prepare the sauce: add a pinch of salt, an适量 amount of sugar, light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, half a tablespoon dark soy sauce, an适量 amount of cooking wine, and a little white pepper. Mix well to create a savory flavor.  
6. Pour the prepared sauce into the wok, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry until the sauce is reduced, allowing the ingredients to fully absorb the flavors.  
7. When the sauce is nearly absorbed, add the shredded green pepper and red pepper.  
   **Note**: The green and red peppers must be added last, otherwise they will turn yellow and affect the dish's color.  
8. Stir-fry all ingredients over high heat quickly to ensure even heating and flavor absorption. Cook through and bring out the wok hei (breath of the wok).  
   **Key Point**: No need to thicken with starch; rely on high heat and fast stir-frying to fully release the wok hei.  
9. After a few stirs, remove from heat and plate.  

## Health Tips  
This dish combines tofu skin, shredded pork, and green peppers, providing plant protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for calorie control during weight loss. Note that the shredded pork should be stir-fried first to release its aroma before adding the tofu skin to avoid absorbing too much oil. Adding the green and red peppers last helps retain their crunchiness and vitamin C.
