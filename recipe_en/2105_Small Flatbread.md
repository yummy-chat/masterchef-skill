# Small Flatbread
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 500 grams all-purpose or bread flour
💧 170 grams boiling water
💧 170 grams cold water
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥛 Measuring cup
🍞 Silicone kneading mat
🥖 Rolling pin
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan
🔥 Gas stove
🖌️ Wooden brush
🍲 Container with lid or kitchen towel

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour the flour into a bowl. First, add 170 grams of boiling water to scald half of the flour, stirring until it becomes flaky. Then, add 170 grams of cold water to mix the other half. Combine all the flour flakes and knead into a dough.
**Note**: Do not add the boiling water and cold water at the same time, otherwise it will become a warm water dough, losing the softness and chewiness of the half-scalded dough. It is normal for the dough to be sticky at the beginning; you can sprinkle a small amount of dry flour to assist with kneading.

2. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid, and let the dough rest for 15 minutes to relax the gluten and fully soften the dough.

3. Transfer the rested dough to a floured work surface, gently knead for a few minutes until the surface is smooth and no longer sticky.

4. Make a hole in the center of the dough, rotate and stretch it with both hands to form a long strip, then use your palms to squeeze into evenly sized small pieces.

5. Roll each small piece into a round flatbread of even thickness, ensuring the entire piece is uniformly thin; no need to make the center slightly thicker. If sticky, sprinkle dry flour to prevent sticking.

6. Preheat the flat-bottomed pan over high heat and brush with oil. Place the flatbread in the pan and immediately reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Cook one side for about 90 seconds, then flip and cook for another 90 seconds, until large bubbles form on the surface, indicating it is fully cooked.
**Key point**: The pan must be fully preheated before adding the flatbread; you can move the dough slightly while cooking to ensure even heating.

7. Remove the cooked flatbread immediately, stack them in a lidded container or wrap them in a kitchen towel. Use the residual heat to allow them to "soften each other," keeping them soft and not dry.

## Health Advice
This half-scalded dough flatbread uses boiling water and cold water added in separate stages, resulting in a product that is both chewy and soft, making it suitable as a staple food for those with average digestive capacity. However, because flour raises blood sugar quickly, those managing blood sugar levels should pair it with vegetables and protein.
