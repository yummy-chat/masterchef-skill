# Braised Three Delicacies
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🥢 Chopsticks, spatula (for stirring, flipping, and removing fish bones)

## Cooking Steps
1. Split the fish head in half, cut the flesh into pieces, and rinse thoroughly with water. Drain.
**Key Point**: Must clean thoroughly to remove blood and black membrane.

2. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add salt and peanut oil, and whisk until well combined. Set aside.
3. Cut the silken tofu into small pieces. Bring water to a boil in a pot, ladle a spoonful of boiling water over the tofu pieces to soak, removing the beany taste and preheating them.
**Note**: Must use the softest silken tofu for a smoother texture.

4. Heat oil in a clay pot, add the fish head, fish pieces, and ginger slices. Fry until both sides are golden brown. Splash cooking wine along the edge of the pot, immediately pour in boiling water, add scallion knots, and cook over high heat for 5 minutes until the soup turns milky white. Remove the fish head.
5. Let the removed fish head cool, then carefully remove all bones.
**Key Point**: Must ensure no bones remain to avoid choking.

6. Return the removed fish bones to the soup and continue simmering. Once the flavor is released, remove and discard the bones, keeping only the fish soup.
7. Heat oil in a wok, pour out the hot oil, then add cold oil. Pour in the egg mixture. Push the set egg at the bottom to the top, using residual heat to cook to a tender, fluffy state. Remove and set aside.
8. Heat oil in the wok, sauté ginger slices until fragrant. Add the boned fish meat and stir-fry briefly. Splash in rice wine and soy sauce for aroma, then pour in the prepared fish soup. Season with salt, sugar, white pepper, and a little dark soy sauce for color. Add water starch to thicken the soup to a flowing consistency. Add the scrambled eggs and drained tofu, gently mix until well combined, and sprinkle with scallion sections before serving.
**Note**: Handle gently after adding tofu to avoid breaking it.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, suitable for those needing protein supplements and brain health. However, the fish soup and fried eggs contain a moderate amount of oil, so it is advisable to control portion sizes. People with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of salt and soy sauce.
