# Bean Curd with Pork Intestines
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐷 Pork intestines (main ingredient)  
🫘 Bean curd (main ingredient, about one bowl)  
🥒 Pickled radish (sliced)  
🧄 Pickled ginger (sliced)  
🌶️ Pickled chili (cut into sections)  
🧅 Old ginger (crushed and minced)  
🧄 Garlic (crushed)  
🧅 Scallion (cut into sections)  
🌾 Flour (for cleaning)  
🧂 Salt (for cleaning and seasoning)  
🍶 White vinegar (for cleaning and removing odor)  
🍶 Liquor (for blanching and stir-frying to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🛢️ Rapeseed oil (base oil for stir-frying)  
🧈 Lard (for enhancing aroma)  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) (to stir-fry until red oil releases)  
🌶️ Dried chili flakes (to increase spiciness and color)  
🌿 Bay leaves, star anise (to add complex aroma)  
🧂 Ground white pepper (for seasoning)  
🧄 Minced garlic (sprinkled before drizzling hot oil)  
🌶️ Dried chili segments (sprinkled before drizzling hot oil)  
🌿 Chopped scallions (for final garnish)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin (for cleaning pork intestines)  
🍳 Large iron wok (for blanching, stir-frying, and braising)  
🧺 Bamboo basket or slotted spoon (for removing pork intestines)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board (for preparing ingredients)  
🥄 Large soup ladle (for stirring and serving)  
🪵 Wooden lid (for covering while braising)  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the pork intestines in a basin, add enough flour, salt, and white vinegar. Rub vigorously and repeatedly to remove mucus and impurities, then rinse thoroughly with clean water.  
**Note**: Rub firmly; this is key to removing the odor of the pork intestines.  
2.  Add cold water to the wok, put in the cleaned pork intestines, then add scallion knots, ginger slices, and a little liquor. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 4 to 5 minutes. Remove, rinse with clean water, and cut into bite-sized pieces.  
**Key point**: **The pork intestines must be added to cold water**. Do not blanch for too long to avoid a tough texture.  
3.  While blanching, prepare the other ingredients: Crush the old ginger and mince it, crush the garlic, cut the scallion into sections; slice the pickled radish and pickled ginger, and cut the pickled chili into sections. Set aside.  
4.  Place the wok on the stove, add an appropriate amount of rapeseed oil and one spoonful of lard. Heat the oil, then add scallion, ginger, garlic, pickled radish, pickled ginger, and pickled chili. Stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture evaporates, the edges of the ingredients turn slightly yellow, and the aroma is rich.  
5.  Add one spoonful of doubanjiang, reduce the heat to low, and stir-fry until the red oil releases and the sauce is fragrant. Then add the dried chili flakes, bay leaves, and star anise. Continue stirring evenly.  
**Note**: Stir-fry the doubanjiang and chili flakes over low heat to prevent burning.  
6.  Add the cut pork intestines to the wok, increase the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly to coat them evenly with the sauce. Splash a little liquor along the edge of the wok, and continue stir-frying to remove odor and enhance aroma, until the surface of the intestines is shiny and slightly curled.  
7.  Add enough hot water to the wok to completely cover all ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, season with salt and ground white pepper. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and braise for about 40 minutes, until the pork intestines are tender.  
**Key point**: **Add enough hot water at one time**. Do not add water midway to keep the broth rich.  
8.  Once the pork intestines are tender, gently pour the bean curd into the wok. Carefully push it into the broth using the back of a spoon, avoiding breaking it apart. Cover and cook for another 10 minutes, allowing the bean curd to fully absorb the broth.  
9.  Ladle the cooked bean curd and pork intestines with the broth into a large bowl. Sprinkle minced garlic and dried chili segments on top.  
10. Heat a spoonful of oil in a separate wok until it begins to smoke, then quickly drizzle it over the minced garlic and dried chili to release their aroma. Finally, sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This bean curd with pork intestines dish is rich in protein and collagen, but it is also high in fat and sodium. It is suitable for occasional indulgence. Those with high blood pressure or those on a weight loss diet should consume it in moderation. The addition of pickled radish and pickled chili helps stimulate appetite and aid digestion, but it should not be overeaten.
