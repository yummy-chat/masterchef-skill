# Baked Potato Rice Stuffed Boat
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, about 4 (medium-sized, peeled)
🍚 Cooked rice, one large bowl
🥚 Eggs, about 4
🥜 Peanuts, to taste
🍖 Ham, diced, to taste
🫑 Green bell pepper, to taste (finely chopped)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped, divided into two portions)
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (chopped)
🫘 Soybean paste, to taste
🛢️ Cooking oil, to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍲 Cooking pot
♨️ Air fryer
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥣 Large bowl
🔪 Knife
🥄 Spoon or spatula

## Instructions
1. Peel the potatoes and place them in a cooking pot. Add enough water to fully submerge them. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
**Key point**: Adjust steaming time according to potato size; the chopstick should go through with no resistance.

2. Transfer the steamed potatoes to an air fryer. Brush the surface evenly with a layer of cooking oil. Set to 200°C and bake for 30 minutes.
**Key point**: Turn the potatoes halfway through baking to ensure the skin becomes evenly crispy and golden.

3. Heat a wok or frying pan with cooking oil. When the oil reaches about 60% hot, add the beaten eggs and quickly stir-fry into small crumbly pieces.
**Key point**: Stir immediately after adding the eggs to prevent lumps that are too large.

4. Squeeze in an appropriate amount of soybean paste into the scrambled eggs and stir-fry evenly to make an egg sauce.
**Key point**: The soybean paste must be cooked through until fragrant, fully blending with the egg pieces.

5. Add some of the chopped scallions and chopped green bell pepper. Stir-fry quickly a few times until fragrant, then immediately remove from the pan and set aside.
**Key point**: Add the scallions and green pepper last to maintain a crisp and fresh texture.

6. Cut the baked potatoes in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scoop out some of the inner flesh, leaving a complete potato shell about 0.5 cm thick.
**Key point**: The potato shell must remain intact; too thin and it will break, too thick and it will affect the texture.

7. Place the scooped-out potato mash into a large bowl. Add the rice, remaining scallions, chopped cilantro, diced ham, peanuts, and the prepared egg sauce. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
**Key point**: Ensure all ingredients are evenly distributed during mixing so that each bite of filling tastes consistent.

8. Fill the potato shells with the mixed filling, pressing down firmly until full. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Both potatoes and rice are high in carbohydrates. Eating them together provides a strong feeling of fullness, making this dish suitable for those needing a quick energy boost. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to cut the richness, and to control portion size per serving to avoid a rapid spike in blood sugar.
