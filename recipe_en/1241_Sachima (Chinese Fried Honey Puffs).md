# Sachima (Chinese Fried Honey Puffs)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Wheat flour 250g  
🥚 Eggs 3~3.5 (use whole eggs for dough, adjust based on absorption)  
🧂 Baking powder 1.25g  
🍬 White sugar 175g  
💧 Water 60g  
🍯 Honey 75g  
🍈 Red and green candied fruit strips appropriate amount (for decoration, can substitute with dried fruit pieces)  
⚪ Roasted white sesame seeds appropriate amount (to prevent sticking)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Rolling pin  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Two slotted spoons  
🪑 Cutting board  
🧺 Bamboo tray  

## Steps  
1. Mix wheat flour and baking powder in a stainless steel bowl. Add eggs and knead into a smooth dough. Do not add water throughout the process.  
2. Let the dough rest and proof. After it is fully proofed, knead until the surface appears slightly rough and glossy. The firmness should be similar to dumpling dough.  
3. Dust the cutting board with cornstarch to prevent sticking. Roll the dough into a sheet of even thickness. When folding, sprinkle cornstarch between each layer to prevent sticking.  
4. Cut the dough sheet into strips slightly thinner than Chinese chives. Thoroughly separate the strips and place them on a bamboo tray for later use, ensuring each strip is individually separated.  
5. Pour cooking oil into a wok. Heat the oil to 30–40% of its full temperature. Add the strips and use two slotted spoons to continuously turn them. Once the strips quickly float, expand, and turn a light white color, immediately remove them.  
6. In another wok, add water and white sugar. Cook over low heat without stirring. Once the sugar syrup becomes glossy, add honey. Continue cooking until, when lifted with a spoon and dripped, the syrup can be pulled into thin threads.  
7. Quickly pour the fried strips into the syrup. Stir rapidly to evenly coat each strip with syrup.  
8. Sprinkle roasted sesame seeds on the cutting board. Pour in the coated strips and shape into a rectangle. Sprinkle red and green candied fruit strips on the surface. Gently press down with a knife to compact, but leave noticeable air pockets inside.  
9. Once fully set, cut into evenly sized rectangular pieces. You should hear a crispy sound when cutting. The interior should be fluffy with many air holes.  

## Health Advice  
Sachima is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to have 1 to 2 pieces per serving as a snack, paired with unsweetened tea to cut the richness. Since the dough uses whole eggs, the protein content is slightly higher than ordinary pastries, making it suitable as an energy boost.
