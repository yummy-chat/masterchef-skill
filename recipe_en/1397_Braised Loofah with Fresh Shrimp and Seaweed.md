# Braised Loofah with Fresh Shrimp and Seaweed
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Seaweed, appropriate amount (dried seaweed torn into pieces)
🍤 Fresh shrimp, about 10 (peeled, heads reserved)
🥒 Loofah, 1-2 pieces (peeled and cut into thick slices)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥄 Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok, add the shrimp heads and stir-fry, add scallion and ginger, and fry until the shrimp oil turns bright red.
**Key Point**: The red oil must be extracted; this is essential for achieving a milky white broth.

2. Pour in hot water, bring to a boil over high heat for 2 minutes. Once the broth turns white, remove the shrimp heads and solids, and reserve the broth for later use.

3. In another wok, add a base layer of oil, sauté scallion and ginger until fragrant, then add the loofah slices and stir-fry until they turn completely bright green.
**Key Point**: The loofah must be thoroughly stir-fried to remove its raw taste while maintaining a crisp texture.

4. Add the shrimp meat and stir-fry together with the loofah for a short time.
5. Pour in the prepared shrimp broth, bring to a boil, then reduce to medium heat.
6. Season with salt and white pepper powder.
7. Just before removing from heat, add the seaweed, cover the lid, and simmer for 30 seconds until the seaweed softens, then serve.
**Note**: Do not cook the seaweed for too long, as it will become mushy and lose its texture.

## Health Tips
Seaweed is rich in iodine and dietary fiber, shrimp meat provides high-quality protein, and loofah helps clear heat and promote urination. This dish is suitable for hydration and fat control in summer. The red oil extracted from the shrimp heads enhances the broth's color, but be mindful of the oil amount; adding the seaweed just before serving preserves its fresh flavor and crisp texture.
