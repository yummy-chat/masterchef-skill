# Mango Mochi
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Glutinous Rice Flour 160g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 30g
🥛 Whole Milk 210ml
🥭 Mango Approximately 1 piece (peeled, pitted, and cut into large chunks)
🥥 Shredded Coconut Appropriate amount (for final coating, can substitute with powdered sugar or cooked glutinous rice flour)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large Glass Bowl (for mixing batter)
🍲 Stainless Steel Steamer
🥄 Silicone Spatula
🔪 Cutting Board, Chef's Knife
🫗 Small Bowl (for holding cold boiled water and shredded coconut)

## Instructions
1. Mix glutinous rice flour and granulated sugar in a large glass bowl. Slowly pour in whole milk, stirring until the batter is smooth, lump-free, and slightly thick.
   **Note**: If the batter is too thin, the finished product will be difficult to shape; if too thick, the texture will be hard.
2. Peel and pit the mango, then cut into large chunks. Set aside.
3. Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the bowl of batter inside, cover, and steam over medium heat for 25 minutes.
4. Remove the cooked dough. Quickly stir and flip it with a silicone spatula to release heat and prevent excessive sticking.
5. Dip both hands in a small amount of cold boiled water. Take a portion of dough and flatten it into a round disc.
6. Place a piece of mango in the center of the dough disc. Pinch the edges together to seal tightly, then roll into a round ball.
   **Note**: Ensure the seal is firmly closed to prevent the filling from leaking, and shape the ball as round as possible.
7. Roll the glutinous rice ball in shredded coconut, coating the surface evenly.
8. Gently reshape with your hands one last time to make the finished product more round and visually appealing.

## Health Advice
This mochi is rich in carbohydrates, making it suitable for quickly replenishing energy after exercise. However, it is high in sugar and calories, so those with diabetes or monitoring their sugar intake should consume only a small amount. Mango is rich in Vitamin C and pairs excellently with glutinous rice, but attention should be paid to moderate consumption to avoid indigestion.
