# Standard Procedure for Pan-Seared Steak  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 1 steak (about 250g, thickness approximately 2cm, thawed and trimmed of excess fat on edges)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ Freshly ground black pepper, to taste  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment  
📦 Baking tray with a rack or wire rack (for draining blood during thawing)  
♨️ Air fryer or oven (for low-temperature slow roasting)  
🍳 Non-stick or cast iron pan  
🌡️ Food thermometer (optional)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍴 Fork  
🥢 Tongs  
🧂 Salt and pepper grinders  

## Steps  
1. Remove the frozen steak and place it on a baking tray with a rack or wire rack. Thaw slowly in the refrigerator overnight, allowing the blood to drip off without soaking the steak.  
2. Take out the thawed steak. Use a chef’s knife to trim excess fat from the edges. Evenly sprinkle salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.  
3. Preheat the oven or air fryer to 90–100°C. Place the steak inside and roast at low temperature for 6–7 minutes, until the center temperature reaches about 36°C and the surface is dry.  
   **Key Point**: Slow roasting at low heat ensures even internal warming while the heat releases the flavor of the black pepper.  
4. Heat a pan over high heat until it begins to smoke. Add a little oil, then place the steak in the pan. Sear each side for 45 seconds. Then, stand the steak on its edge and sear for 20–30 seconds, until the surface forms a deep brown, crispy crust.  
   **Key Point**: The pan must be heated until smoking to quickly form a crispy crust.  
5. Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest for 2–3 minutes. When you lightly scrape the surface with a fork, you should hear a distinct crispy sound. Resting helps the juices be reabsorbed, making the meat more tender and juicy.  

## Health Advice  
Steak is rich in high-quality protein and has a low glycemic index, making it suitable for muscle building or blood sugar control. Since the edge fat has been trimmed, there is no need to worry about excessive calories. Be careful not to overcook the steak, as it can become tough. Low-temperature slow roasting is the key to maintaining tenderness.
