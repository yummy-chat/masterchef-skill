# Microwave Pudding  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Milk 200ml  
🧈 Whipping cream 135ml (can be substituted with equal amount of milk, but texture will suffer)  
🥚 Eggs 2 (whole eggs)  
🥚 Egg yolks 2  
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon (for caramel)  
🍬 White sugar 4 teaspoons (about 40g, for pudding mixture)  
💧 Water 1 teaspoon (for caramel)  
🌿 Vanilla seeds to taste (optional, for aroma)  
💧 Boiling water as needed (for water bath)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🫙 Heat-resistant glass cups or pudding cups  
📡 Microwave  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥣 Whisk  
🫗 Strainer  
🍽️ White baking dish  
🥄 Spoon  
📦 Plastic wrap (optional)  

## Steps  
1. In a heat-resistant glass cup, add 1 teaspoon of white sugar and half a cup of water. Swirl to mix evenly.  
2. Place in the microwave and heat on medium power for about 2 minutes, until the syrup turns a deep amber color.  
   **Note**: Be sure to use a heat-resistant container.  
3. Remove the caramel cup, add 1 teaspoon of hot water, and microwave again for 20–30 seconds to keep the caramel fluid and prevent it from solidifying completely.  
4. In a measuring cup, combine 200ml milk and 135ml whipping cream. Microwave on high power for about 1 minute until warm.  
   **Key point**: Heat the milk mixture until it is “just warm”—do not overheat, as it may cook the eggs later.  
5. In another bowl, beat 2 whole eggs and 2 egg yolks. Slowly pour the warm milk mixture into the eggs while stirring continuously to combine.  
   **Key point**: It is recommended to use room-temperature eggs for easier blending with the milk mixture.  
6. Add 4 teaspoons of white sugar and vanilla seeds to the mixture. Stir, then let it rest briefly. Stir again to ensure the sugar is fully dissolved and does not settle at the bottom.  
7. Strain the pudding mixture through a strainer to remove bubbles and lumps, ensuring a smooth texture.  
8. Pour an appropriate amount of boiling water into a baking dish. Pour the strained pudding mixture into the pudding cups containing caramel, filling them about 6–7 tenths full. Then place the pudding cups into the hot water in the baking dish.  
   **Key point**: Using a water bath ensures even heating and prevents air holes. It is recommended to use boiling water in the outer dish.  
9. Place the entire baking dish into the microwave. Select medium-low power and heat for 7 minutes first.  
   **Note**: Use medium-low power to heat slowly; high power will cause the interior to boil and create air holes.  
10. After 7 minutes, check the pudding’s condition: if the center wobbles significantly, heat for another 3 minutes. Observe the state after 1.5 minutes; if it is nearly set, add another 1.5 minutes of heating.  
    **Key point**: The total heating time should be flexibly adjusted based on microwave power and pudding quantity. Check the condition multiple times to avoid overheating.  
11. Once heating is complete, remove the pudding cups and let them cool to room temperature. Then refrigerate for at least 4 hours.  
    **Serving suggestion**: Eat directly by scooping; if you want to unmold for plating, run a small knife along the edge of the cup, place a plate over the cup, flip, and gently tap or shake to release.  
    **Alternative**: Can also be made in an air fryer at 180°C for up to 10 minutes until done.  

## Health Advice  
This pudding is nutritionally balanced—milk and eggs provide high-quality protein—but whipping cream and sugar are high in calories, so enjoy it as an occasional treat. During weight loss, reduce sugar or substitute skim milk. When using the microwave water bath, be sure to use medium-low power to prevent excessive air holes in the pudding’s texture.
