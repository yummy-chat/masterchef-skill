# Stir-Fried Clams with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐚 Clams, appropriate amount (main ingredient)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (partially sliced for boiling, partially minced for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (divided into scallion knot, minced scallion white, and chopped scallion greens)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)
🧂 Salt, about 3% concentration (only used for the brine to soak the clams, not for cooking)
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount (for aromatics)
💧 Water starch, appropriate amount (for thickening before serving)
⚠️ Throughout the cooking process, **do not add any additional seasonings**. Rely on the natural umami of the clams and the reserved cooking broth for flavor.

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking and cleaning clams, settling the broth)
🍳 Wok/iron pan (for boiling and stir-frying)
🥄 Wok spatula (for stirring and tossing)
🫖 Lid (for steaming the clams)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing clams)
🔪 Cutting board (for preparing ingredients)
🍶 Glass measuring cup or bowl (for holding the settled broth)
🍽️ Serving bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the clams in about 3% salt water for 1–2 hours. Remove and repeatedly rub the shells under running water, then rinse clean with fresh water to ensure no sand remains.
2. Add a small amount of fresh water to the wok, place a few slices of ginger and a scallion knot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the clams, cover with a lid, and steam. Cook over high heat for about 3 minutes until all clam shells have fully opened, indicating they are cooked. Immediately turn off the heat once the clams open, remove them to a plate, and remove half of the empty shells for easier eating. **Reserve the broth in the wok**.
**Key Point**: As soon as the clams open, immediately turn off the heat, quickly remove them, and remove half of the empty shells. The broth used to cook the clams must be reserved and not discarded.

3. Pour the reserved clam cooking broth into a bowl and let it sit to allow sand particles to settle. Carefully pour the clear top layer of broth into another container for later use, discarding the sediment at the bottom.
4. Clean the wok, heat it, and add a small amount of oil. Add a generous amount of minced ginger, garlic, and minced scallion white. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until fragrant. Once the aroma is released, immediately add the prepared clams, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry to coat the clams evenly with the ginger, scallion, and garlic aroma. Pour the reserved settled clam broth along the edge of the wok, and continue stir-frying over high heat.
5. Once the broth comes to a boil, drizzle in an appropriate amount of water starch. Quickly stir-fry to create a thin glaze, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and coat the clams evenly. Finally, add a generous amount of chopped green scallion greens, toss to combine, and immediately turn off the heat and remove from the wok to maintain the scallions' vibrant green color and aroma.
**Key Point**: Do not add any additional seasonings such as salt or soy sauce throughout the process. The natural savory flavor from the clam broth is sufficient.

## Health Tips
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. Clams are rich in zinc and iron, making them suitable for those needing a zinc supplement. However, individuals with gout should consume less of the broth. With no additional seasonings added, the dish relies on the natural umami of the broth, showcasing the ingredients' original flavors.
