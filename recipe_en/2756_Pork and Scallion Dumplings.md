# Pork and Scallion Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 250g ground pork (30% fat, 70% lean, do not grind too finely)  
🧅 75g scallions (white part only, half used raw, half stir-fried)  
🫚 10g ginger (minced)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more than usual for stir-frying)  
🥄 2 tbsp light soy sauce  
🥄 1 tbsp dark soy sauce  
🦪 1 tbsp oyster sauce  
🍬 1/2 tbsp sugar  
🌿 1/2 tbsp thirteen-spice powder  
🍄 1/2 tbsp beef bouillon powder (optional)  
🧂 1 pinch chicken bouillon  
🧂 1 pinch white pepper powder  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🔥 Induction cooktop  
🥢 Spatula  
🤲 Chopsticks/hands (for mixing and aerating the filling)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the ground pork in a large bowl. Use your hands to break it apart, keeping a coarse, grainy texture.  
   **Key point**: Avoid over-kneading; maintain a chunky texture in the meat filling.  

2. Prepare scallion-ginger water in advance: Cut scallions into segments and slice the ginger. Steep them together with Sichuan peppercorns and star anise in boiling water. Let cool, then strain out the solids. Add the scallion-ginger water to the pork in three additions, each time mixing and slapping the filling in the same direction until the liquid is fully absorbed and the filling becomes sticky and elastic.  
   **Key point**: Adding the water in batches and slapping the filling thoroughly allows it to absorb enough liquid, resulting in a tender, juicy texture.  

3. Prep the ingredients: Finely chop the scallions and mince the ginger. Set aside half of the chopped scallions for raw use. The other half, along with all the minced ginger, will be stir-fried.  

4. Add an appropriate amount of oil to a pan. Once hot, add the scallions and ginger reserved for stir-frying. Cook over low heat for about 20 seconds until fragrant. Turn to medium heat, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, thirteen-spice powder, beef bouillon powder, chicken bouillon, and white pepper powder. Stir-fry evenly to form a sauce, then turn off the heat and let cool completely.  
   **Note**: Sauté the scallions and ginger over low heat to avoid burning; the sauce must be fully cooled before mixing with the pork to prevent cooking the meat.  

5. Pour the cooled sauce into the pork filling that has been hydrated with scallion-ginger water. Stir vigorously in one direction to combine and develop a smooth, elastic binding.  
   **Key point**: Continue stirring in a consistent direction to fully integrate the sauce into the meat and create tackiness.  

6. Just before wrapping the dumplings, add the remaining raw chopped scallions to the filling. Gently mix until just combined, then use immediately.  
   **Note**: Adding the raw scallions at the end preserves their fresh aroma and prevents them from releasing excess water prematurely.  

## Health Tips  
Using scallion-ginger water to hydrate the filling and incorporating the scallions in two stages (half cooked, half raw) locks in the meat juices while retaining the fresh scallion flavor. This dish is suitable for those needing high-quality protein and iron. For individuals with diabetes or high blood lipids, it is recommended to pair the dumplings with vinegar and garlic, and to control portion size (6–8 dumplings is ideal).
