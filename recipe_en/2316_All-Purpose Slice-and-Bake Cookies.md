# All-Purpose Slice-and-Bake Cookies
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Flavorings
🧈 Butter 75g (higher quality recommended for better flavor)
🍬 Powdered sugar 35g (dissolves more easily than granulated sugar, helps cookies hold shape)
🥚 Whole egg liquid 15g (about 1/4 egg; needs to be brought to room temperature in winter)
🌾 Cake flour 115g
🫐 Dried cranberries 40g (optional flavoring; chop finely)
🍪 Oreo crumbs 40g (optional flavoring)
🍫 Cocoa powder 10g (optional flavoring; dough may become dry, add liquid as needed)
🍵 Matcha powder 10g (optional flavoring; dough may become dry, add liquid as needed)
🍂 Earl Grey tea powder 10g (optional flavoring)
🧀 Golden cheese powder 10g (optional flavoring)
🍊 Dried orange peel 20g (optional flavoring)
🧀 Shredded mozzarella cheese 40g (optional flavoring)
🥜 Almond slices 40g (optional flavoring)

**Note**: Flavorings can be freely combined. If adding dry powders makes the dough too dry, add milk or egg liquid as needed; if adding wet ingredients makes the dough too wet, add dry flour as needed.

## Tools & Equipment
🔥 Microwave (to soften butter quickly)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for precise ingredient measurement)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing ingredients)
🥄 Spatula (for stirring, folding, and pressing ingredients)
🪣 Sieve (for sifting powdered sugar and cake flour)
🫓 Baking tray/silicone baking mat (for holding cookie dough during baking)
🔄 Plastic wrap/storage bag (for wrapping shaped dough)
🔪 Knife (for cutting and slicing dough)
♨️ Oven (for baking cookies)
🌪️ Air fryer (as alternative to oven for baking cookies)
🥢 Chopsticks (for beating eggs)

## Procedure
1. Cut butter into small cubes, place in a bowl, and microwave on high for about 15 seconds, or melt over a water bath until it reaches a smooth, paste-like consistency without lumps.
   **Note**: Do not overheat until liquefied, as this will affect the cookies' crispy texture.

2. Beat the egg and measure out 15g of whole egg liquid for later use.
   **Note**: In winter, eggs need to be pre-soaked in warm water to bring them to room temperature; room-temperature egg liquid blends more easily with butter.

3. Sift powdered sugar into the softened butter and stir evenly with a spatula. Pour in the egg liquid and stir again until the ingredients are fully combined into a smooth paste.

4. Sift the cake flour into the butter paste. Use the spatula to mix using a cutting or pressing motion until no dry flour remains.
   **Note**: Avoid vigorous circular stirring to prevent developing gluten, which may cause the cookies to shrink or deform after baking. If the spatula is difficult to use, you can gather the dough by hand, but do not over-knead.

5. Divide the base dough as needed into portions. Add selected flavorings and knead until evenly combined.
   **Note**: The dough should be moist enough not to stick to your hands. Adjust as needed based on the dryness or wetness of the added ingredients.

6. Place the flavored dough on plastic wrap and shape it into a cylinder, triangle, or square. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm.
   **Note**: The dough should be about as firm as refrigerated butter before slicing. Freeze in summer; refrigerate in winter—do not freeze too hard.

7. Remove the firm dough, peel off the plastic wrap, and slice into approximately 0.5 cm thick rounds.
   **Note**: Try to keep the thickness uniform to ensure even browning and prevent thinner slices from burning. If the dough crumbles while slicing, it is too hard—let it soften slightly at room temperature before cutting again.

8. Baking:
   - **Oven**: Preheat to 165°C. Arrange cookie slices flat on a baking tray lined with a silicone mat or parchment paper. Bake in the middle rack for about 20 minutes, until the surface is lightly golden.
   - **Air fryer**: Set to 150°C and bake for about 15 minutes, flipping halfway through, then bake for another 5 minutes. Air fryer temperatures are generally 10–15°C lower than oven temperatures.
   **Note**: Bake until the surface is just lightly colored. The cookies will become crispier after cooling.

## Health Advice
These cookies have a moderate amount of butter and powdered sugar, making them suitable as a casual snack. However, they are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to enjoy 3–5 pieces per serving with unsweetened tea. Control the baking time to avoid over-browning, which can affect the texture. This recipe is especially beginner-friendly for those new to baking.
