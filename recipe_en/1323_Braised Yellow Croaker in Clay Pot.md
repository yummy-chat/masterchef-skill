# Braised Yellow Croaker in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 yellow croaker (scaled, cleaned, and sliced diagonally into thick pieces)
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (cut into chunks)
🧄 6-8 cloves of garlic (peeled)
🧅 6-8 small shallots (peeled)
🧅 2 scallions (cut into sections)
🌿 1 small bunch of cilantro (cut into sections)
🥫 3 teaspoons of Clay Pot Sauce (can substitute with Chu Hou Sauce)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🦪 A little oyster sauce
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (for marinating and braising)
🥡 Soy sauce, to taste
🌶️ A pinch of white pepper
🌾 A little cornstarch
🥜 Peanut oil, to taste (for marinating and sautéing)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥘 Clay pot (sand pot)
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating)
🥄 Spoon
🍶 Squeeze bottle (for cooking wine, soy sauce, etc.)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the yellow croaker, slice it diagonally into thick pieces, keeping the fish intact and not broken.
2. Cut the ginger into chunks, peel the garlic cloves, peel the shallots, and cut the scallions and cilantro into sections. Set aside.
3. Place the fish pieces in a bowl. Add the Clay Pot Sauce, salt, sugar, oyster sauce, cooking wine, soy sauce, and white pepper. Mix well. Then add a little cornstarch and peanut oil, continue to mix, and marinate for 3-5 minutes.
**Key**: Cornstarch and oil help lock in moisture, making the fish tender and smooth.
4. Heat the clay pot, pour in peanut oil, and add the ginger chunks, garlic cloves, and shallots in that order. Sauté over medium heat until fragrant.
**Note**: Ginger withstands heat well, while shallots burn easily; add them in the correct order.
5. Lay the marinated fish pieces flat on top of the aromatics in a single layer, without overlapping, to ensure even cooking.
6. Drizzle some cooking wine along the pot's edge, cover, and cook over high heat for 1 minute, then reduce to low heat and cook for 2 minutes.
**Judgment**: When opened, the aroma should be strong, and a chopstick should easily pierce through the fish, indicating it is cooked through.
7. Turn off the heat, sprinkle scallion sections and cilantro on top, and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Yellow croaker is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for those needing nutritional supplementation. However, the Clay Pot Sauce and oyster sauce contain relatively high salt levels, so use them in moderation. Braising in a clay pot preserves the tenderness of the fish, making it an ideal dish for when appetite is low.
