# Rice and Mung Bean Steamed Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Rice 150g (soaked in advance until soft)
🫘 Mung beans 150g (soaked in advance until soft)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🥛 Whole milk 200g
🫐 Red dates, as needed (for surface decoration)
🍇 Raisins, as needed (for surface decoration)
🌰 Black sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)
🍬 White sugar 40g (can be increased or decreased according to preference)
🍄 High-sugar tolerant yeast 5g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Meat grinder or blender
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🪄 Plastic wrap
🍵 Small bowl
♨️ Steamer

## Preparation Steps
1. Drain the soaked rice and mung beans, place them in a blender, add 200g whole milk, and blend in intermittent pulse mode until smooth.
   **Note**: Use a "press and pause" operating method to prevent the machine from overheating.
2. Add 40g white sugar and 5g high-sugar tolerant yeast, then blend again thoroughly to ensure the rice paste, sugar, and yeast are well mixed.
3. Pour the rice paste into a stainless steel bowl, add 300g all-purpose flour, and stir with chopsticks in one direction until a thick batter forms.
   **Note**: If the batter is too dry, add a small amount of water as needed to adjust; it should be thick enough to cling to the chopsticks without easily dripping off.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and let it ferment in a warm place for 40–50 minutes, until the volume expands to double and a rich honeycomb structure of air bubbles appears inside.
5. Stir the fermented batter thoroughly to release the gas and restore a uniform consistency.
6. Evenly brush a small amount of cooking oil on the inner walls of the small bowl to prevent sticking. Shape the degassed batter into a ball, place it in the bowl, and fill to about 80% full.
7. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap for a second proofing of about 5 minutes.
   **Key**: The second proofing ensures the cake will puff up fully during steaming.
8. Decorate the surface of the batter with red dates and raisins, and sprinkle with a little black sesame seeds.
9. Place in the steamer, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it sit for 3 minutes before removing the lid.
   **Note**: Letting it sit after turning off the heat prevents the steamed cake from shrinking and collapsing due to sudden temperature changes.

## Health Advice
This steamed cake uses rice and mung beans as main ingredients, making it rich in dietary fiber and suitable as a breakfast option for the whole family. It is recommended to reduce the amount of white sugar as needed to lower sugar intake. Those with weaker digestive function should consume in moderation.
