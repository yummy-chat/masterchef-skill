# Tiger Skin Matcha Fig Roll
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn, September-October  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
### Matcha Cake Base  
🥚 Large eggs 5 (yolks and whites separated, egg whites about 50g each)  
🌽 Corn oil 50g (heated to about 70℃)  
🥛 Milk 85g (warm)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 50g (added to egg whites in batches)  
🌾 Cake flour 75g  
🍵 Matcha powder 6g  
🧂 Salt a pinch (added to egg whites)  
🍋 Lemon juice a few drops (added to egg whites)  

### Tiger Skin Cake  
🥚 Egg yolks 8  
🍬 Granulated sugar 40g  
🌽 Corn oil 20g  
🌽 Cornstarch 27g  

### Assembly and Matcha Cream  
🥛 Heavy cream 300g  
🍵 Matcha powder 9g  
🍬 Powdered sugar 24g  
🟤 Figs about 10 (peeled, ends trimmed)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Whisk  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🪞 Flour sifter  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🍳 Baking pan (about 28×28cm)  
📄 Parchment paper  
🧽 Wire rack  
🔪 Offset spatula  
🧁 Piping bag  
🪚 Serrated knife  
📖 Heavy object (like a large book, to help roll tightly)  
🍱 Airtight container  

## Steps  

### Matcha Cake Base  
1. Sift matcha powder into a bowl. Heat corn oil to about 70℃ and quickly pour it over the matcha, stirring until fully combined. Add warm milk and mix well. Sift in cake flour and stir in a zigzag motion until no dry flour remains. Finally, add egg yolks and fold into a smooth, delicate green batter.  
   **Key Point**: Matcha powder is highly absorbent. If the batter is too dry, adjust as needed. The final consistency should be a ribbon-like stream when lifting the whisk.  

2. Add salt and lemon juice to the egg whites. Using an electric hand mixer on high speed, beat the egg whites, adding granulated sugar in three batches. Beat until the peaks form a small hook when lifting the beater.  

3. Add 1/3 of the meringue to the matcha yolk batter. Fold with a spatula until combined. Then pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and continue folding until uniform.  
   **Key Point**: Use a folding motion to avoid deflating the batter by stirring in circles.  

4. Pour the batter into a baking pan lined with parchment paper. Smooth the surface and gently tap the pan a few times to release large air bubbles. Bake in a preheated oven at 150℃ in the middle rack for about 30 minutes.  

5. Remove from the oven and unmold immediately. Let cool on a wire rack. Cover the surface with a sheet of parchment paper to prevent drying.  

### Tiger Skin Cake  
1. Combine egg yolks and granulated sugar. Beat with an electric hand mixer on high speed until the mixture lightens in color, increases in volume, and forms ribbons that hold their shape. Gradually add corn oil, mixing well. Sift in cornstarch and mix on low speed for about 10 seconds until no dry flour remains.  
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly beating the egg yolks is essential for creating the tiger skin pattern.  

2. Pour the batter into a baking pan lined with parchment paper. Spread evenly to the corners. Bake in a preheated oven at 220℃ in the middle rack for 6-7 minutes, until a distinct dark brown tiger skin pattern forms on the surface.  
   **Note**: Use high heat and a short baking time. Watch closely to prevent burning.  

3. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly until warm to the touch. Set aside.  

### Matcha Cream  
1. Combine heavy cream, matcha powder, and powdered sugar. Beat with an electric hand mixer until distinct lines form and the cream is firm.  

### Assembly and Setting  
1. Place the cooled matcha cake base on a fresh sheet of parchment paper. Peel off the bottom parchment paper and trim the edges. Spread an even layer of matcha cream over the cake. Near the end closest to you, pipe a slightly taller "dam" of cream. Arrange the figs neatly in a line. Fill the gaps with cream and cover the surface of the figs.  

2. Using the parchment paper, press a heavy object against the starting end, and roll the cake. Roll tightly, then wrap in parchment paper, twisting the ends. Place in the freezer to set for 15-30 minutes.  

3. Remove the set matcha roll. Spread a thin layer of matcha cream on the unpatterned side of the tiger skin cake. Place the matcha roll at one end and wrap it with the tiger skin cake, rolling it up. Wrap again in parchment paper and refrigerate for at least 2 hours until fully set.  

4. Remove from the refrigerator and slice into thick pieces using a serrated knife. Enjoy.  

## Health Advice  
This Tiger Skin Matcha Fig Roll is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, but it is also high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy as a small afternoon treat. Note that figs have a high sugar content; those with diabetes should consume in moderation. The flavor is best when chilled.
