# Braised Potato and Egg with Meat Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Small potatoes, appropriate amount (with skin, washed)  
🥚 Eggs, 4 pieces  
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (diced)  
🫑 Green pepper, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, appropriate amount (sliced into rings)  
🍶 Huadiao wine, appropriate amount  
🫘 Soybean paste, half a tablespoon  
🍾 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
💧 Water, two tablespoons  
🧴 Dark soy sauce, a little  
🧂 Salt, a little  
🍬 Sugar, a little  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowl (stainless steel bowl/ceramic bowl)  
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the small potatoes with skin and eggs together in a steamer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and steam for 30 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the potatoes.  
   **Note**: Steaming preserves the nutrients of the potatoes to the greatest extent, resulting in a finer texture. Let cool naturally before peeling. Remove the eggs and soak in cold water before peeling.
2.  After peeling the eggs, cut each into four pieces and set aside in a bowl.
3.  Heat a frying pan with oil, add the peeled steamed potatoes, and fry over low heat until golden brown on all sides. When nearly done, add the green pepper pieces and fry until fragrant. Remove and drain oil, then let cool naturally.  
   **Key**: The heat must be low to prevent burning.
4.  Leave a small amount of oil in the pan, add the diced pork belly, and stir-fry over low heat until the fat renders and the aroma is released. Add chopped scallions and stir until fragrant, then drizzle in Huadiao wine to remove any off-flavors and enhance the aroma.
5.  Add half a tablespoon of soybean paste and stir-fry until the sauce is fragrant. Then add light soy sauce and two tablespoons of water, and mix well.
6.  Add a little dark soy sauce for color, then add a pinch of salt and sugar to enhance freshness. Finally, add oyster sauce, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for about 2 minutes, until the sauce is glossy, thick, and the oil separates from the sauce. Turn off the heat and let cool naturally.  
   **Key**: The sauce must be cooked until glossy and thick; this determines the richness of the dish.
7.  Pour the cooled fried potatoes and green peppers into the bowl with the eggs. Drizzle the cooled meat sauce over the top, then sprinkle with cilantro pieces, minced garlic, and small chili pepper rings.
8.  Put on disposable gloves, thoroughly mix all the ingredients and sauce by hand, and plate.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced combination of protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, the pork belly and sauce are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with a stir-fried green leafy vegetable to balance nutrition. Steaming potatoes with the skin on retains more dietary fiber. Individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of the hidden salt in soybean paste and soy sauce.
