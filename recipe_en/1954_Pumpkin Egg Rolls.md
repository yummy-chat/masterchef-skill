# Pumpkin Egg Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🎃 1 pumpkin (recommend high-sweetness varieties like Kabocha squash)
🥚 2 eggs
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (volume ratio of pumpkin puree to flour is about 1:2)
🍚 4 grams yeast
🍬 2 tablespoons sugar (about 30-40 grams)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🍳 Steamer, steamer rack
🥣 Bowls (for mixing, fermenting, and steaming)
🥢 Chopsticks
🫙 Plastic wrap
🖌️ Silicone brush
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. Peel the pumpkin and cut into even slices. Place in a bowl and steam over high heat until soft and tender.
2. While hot, mash the pumpkin into a smooth puree, then add sugar and stir well.
3. Let the pumpkin puree cool until it is no longer hot to the touch, then add the eggs and yeast, and stir to combine.
   **Note**: High temperature will cause the eggs to curdle and deactivate the yeast.
4. Gradually add the flour, stirring with chopsticks into a flaky consistency, then knead by hand into a smooth and soft dough.
5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and place in a warm area to ferment until the dough has doubled in size and the inside is honeycombed.
6. Sprinkle a little dry flour or apply a thin layer of cooking oil on the cutting board. Take out the dough, knead to release air, roll into a long log, divide into evenly sized pieces, and roll each piece into a ball.
7. Take one piece and roll it into a long, tongue-shaped sheet. Roll it up from one end, sealing the seam underneath.
   **Key point**: The thinner you roll it, the more layers the finished product will have.
8. Use a knife to cut the roll in half lengthwise into two pieces.
9. Take a steaming bowl and evenly brush the inner walls with a layer of cooking oil. Place the cut rolls with the cut side facing up, neatly arranged in the bowl. Brush a thin layer of oil on the surface as well.
10. Cover with plastic wrap and let it proof a second time for about 30 minutes.
11. Bring water in the steamer to a boil. Place the bowl with the rolls in the steamer and steam over high heat for 15 minutes. After turning off the heat, let it sit for 3 minutes before removing the lid.
12. After removing from the steamer, invert the bowl onto a plate to unmold.

## Health Suggestions
This pumpkin egg roll is rich in beta-carotene and high-quality protein, making it suitable for people with eye strain or those who need to manage sugar intake. However, the sugar content is relatively high; it is recommended to halve the sugar or use a sugar substitute. Be careful not to let the dough ferment at too high a temperature, as this will deactivate the yeast and prevent the dough from rising.
