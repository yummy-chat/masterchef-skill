# Fruit Grilled Meat
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef 1 large piece (cut into small cubes about 2 cm)
🍍 Pineapple appropriate amount (partly cut into chunks for skewering, partly wrapped with bacon)
🍈 Lychee appropriate amount (peeled)
🥓 Bacon appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion, ginger, and onion appropriate amount (for marinating base flavor)
🍶 Huadiao wine appropriate amount
🥣 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (for both marinating and sauce)
🌶️ Black pepper appropriate amount
🥄 Starch appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🥜 Peanut oil appropriate amount (for sealing during marinating and drizzling on sauce)
🧄 Raw minced garlic appropriate amount
🧅 Diced onion appropriate amount
🍢 BBQ seasoning appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount
🌰 Sesame seeds appropriate amount
🌿 Scallion appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Clear glass bowl
🍢 Metal skewers or bamboo skewers
♨️ Electric grill
🖌️ Silicone brush

## Steps
1. Cut the beef into small cubes about 2 cm. Place in a bowl.Add scallion, ginger, onion, Huadiao wine, light soy sauce, black pepper, starch, and oyster sauce. Mix well. Finally, add peanut oil, mix again, and seal the surface of the beef with oil. Let it marinate for a while.
2. Prepare peeled lychee and cut pineapple chunks. Wrap some pineapple chunks with bacon and set aside.
3. Alternately thread the marinated beef chunks, lychee, and pineapple chunks onto skewers. Separately skewer the bacon-wrapped pineapple chunks.
   **Note**: The sweetness and tartness of the fruit help cut the greasiness.
4. Prepare the BBQ sauce: In a bowl, mix raw minced garlic, diced onion, BBQ seasoning, light soy sauce, sugar, sesame seeds, and scallion. Then, drizzle hot peanut oil over the mixture and stir well.
   **Note**: Drizzling with hot oil is key, as it releases the aroma of the seasonings.
5. Preheat the electric grill. Place the skewers on the grill, keeping them close to the bottom edge. Grill for about 30 seconds, then flip. Move the skewers as needed for even heating. Grill until the surface of the beef turns pale, reaching about 80% doneness.
6. Use a silicone brush to evenly coat the skewers with the sauce. Continue grilling for a short time until the skewers are glossy and have a charred aroma, and the beef is fully cooked inside.

## Health Tips
This fruit grilled meat combines the protein of beef with the vitamin C of fruit. It is suitable for summer appetizers or to cut greasiness at gatherings. However, be mindful of sugar intake; diabetics should consume lychee with caution. The sweetness and tartness of the fruit can somewhat mask the fattiness, so watch the total calorie intake when eating.
