# Fragrant Cheese Bread
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧀 Bread flour 100g (for starter) + 300g (for main dough)  
💧 Water 70g (for starter)  
🍞 Dry yeast 0.5g (for starter) + 3g (for main dough)  
🧂 Salt 1g (for starter) + 3g (for main dough)  
🥛 Milk about 170g (reserve 10g)  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 50g  
🍬 Granulated sugar 9g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 21g (softened at room temperature)  
🧀 Grated Parmesan cheese 12g  
🧀 Cheese shreds appropriate amount (for surface decoration)  
🌾 Cake flour appropriate amount (to prevent sticking)  
🌿 Italian seasoning appropriate amount (optional)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Electronic scale  
🥣 Measuring cup  
🥄 Silicone scraper/spoon  
🪵 Wooden mixing bowl  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🫧 Proofer (or oven proofing function)  
🔥 Oven  
🍞 Silicone mat/worksurface  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🚿 Spray bottle  
🧻 Cotton cloth/kitchen towel  
📄 Parchment paper  
🔪 Scoring knife  
🍽️ Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1.  **Make the starter**: Dissolve yeast in water in a measuring cup, then add bread flour and salt in order, stir until a dough forms, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for overnight fermentation.  
    **Key point**: The starter enhances fermentation flavor and dough elasticity; refrigerated storage should not exceed 3 days; if a sour flavor is desired, the fermentation time can be appropriately extended.  
2.  **Make the liquid mixture**: Using the container that held the starter, add whole egg liquid, milk, granulated sugar, and dry yeast powder, and stir until well combined.  
    **Key point**: If using all-purpose flour, reserve 10g of milk to prevent the dough from becoming too thin.  
3.  **Combine dry ingredients and liquid**: Pour the main dough bread flour and grated Parmesan cheese into a mixing bowl and mix, then pour the liquid mixture in batches, stirring until it forms a shaggy dough with no dry flour.  
    **Key point**: Adjust the amount of liquid flexibly based on the flour's water absorption.  
4.  **Add butter and cut in**: Add butter that has been softened to room temperature, using a cutting method to fully incorporate the butter into the dough.  
    **Key point**: The cutting method prevents the early formation of strong gluten and ensures more even mixing.  
5.  **Knead and rest**: After initial mixing, knead the dough. When the room temperature is high, keep the operation within 10 minutes. Knead until the dough is basically smooth, then round it, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature or in the refrigerator for 20 minutes.  
    **Key point**: After resting, the dough's extensibility increases, and fermentation has already begun; do not knead again.  
6.  **Fold and round**: After resting, fold the dough inward from the four sides twice, turn it over, then slap it against the work surface and round it until the surface is smooth.  
    **Key point**: Simply organize the gluten; avoid over-kneading.  
7.  **First fermentation**: Cover the dough and let it ferment at room temperature for about 40 minutes, until a finger dipped in flour pressed into the dough leaves an indentation that does not spring back or collapse.  
8.  **Divide and pre-shape**: Dust the fermented dough with flour, deflate it, divide it into 3 equal portions, round each portion, cover, and rest for 20 minutes.  
9.  **Prepare the surface decoration**: Sprinkle cake flour on a piece of parchment paper, place the cheese shreds on it, sprinkle with Italian seasoning, and use your hands to mix well so the cheese shreds are evenly coated with flour, then spread them out flat.  
10. **Final shaping**: Dust the rested dough with cake flour to prevent sticking, roll it out into a sheet of even thickness, pat out large air bubbles, turn it over, pinch the top edge thinner and press the bottom edge thinner, roll it up from top to bottom, pinch the seam to seal, and roll it into an even log.  
    **Key point**: When rolling the log, work from the center towards the ends; apply a bit more pressure to areas that are uneven in thickness to adjust.  
11. **Decorate and score**: Dampen a cotton cloth with a spray bottle, roll the dough half a turn on the wet cloth, then roll it half a turn in the decoration mixture, ensuring the surface is fully coated with cheese shreds. Place on a baking sheet, and immediately score 5 shallow diagonal cuts on the surface using a scoring knife.  
    **Key point**: Score before the final fermentation, and make the cuts shallow.  
12. **Final fermentation and baking**: Place the scored dough in a suitable humid environment and let it ferment until about 2.5 times its original size, then move it to a preheated oven and bake quickly at high heat until the surface is golden brown.  
    **Key point**: A significant increase in volume; high-temperature baking achieves a crispy crust and a soft interior.  
13. **Remove from oven and store**: Remove from the oven and transfer to a cooling rack until it reaches hand warmth. Wrap each piece individually with plastic wrap, then place all pieces in a larger bag. Uneaten portions can be stored in the freezer.  

## Health Advice  
This fragrant cheese bread is rich in protein and calcium, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or anyone needing an energy boost. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad for balanced nutrition. Pay attention to controlling the amount of cheese used to avoid excessive sodium intake.
