# Drunken Duck
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦆 One free-range duck (with tougher meat, remove fat, lymph nodes, and feet; wash and pat dry)
🌿 Appropriate amount of Adenophora root
🌿 Appropriate amount of Codonopsis root
🪵 Appropriate amount of cinnamon
⭐ Appropriate amount of star anise
🍂 Appropriate amount of bay leaves
🫘 Appropriate amount of red dates
🫘 A little cardamom
🌿 A little cloves
🍊 Appropriate amount of dried tangerine peel
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (sliced)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallions (cut into sections)
🧴 Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce (for brushing the duck skin for color)
🧂 100g soy sauce
🍶 About 200g rice wine
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil (enough to submerge the whole duck)
🌽 Appropriate amount of starch water (for thickening)
❌ Salt **not added**

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
✂️ Scissors
🍳 Wok
🥅 Fine-mesh strainer / large slotted spoon
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🍲 Lid
🥣 Bowl / container

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the duck**: Remove the lymph nodes on both sides of the duck tail, slit the neck to remove lymph nodes and excess fat, and cut off the feet. Rinse repeatedly to remove all blood, then pat the surface completely dry with kitchen paper towels.
   - Key point: Lymph nodes are a source of off-flavors; they must be cleaned thoroughly. Drying ensures crispy skin and a bright color when frying.
2. **Shape and color**: Evenly brush the duck skin with dark soy sauce. Tuck the wings and feet inward to make the duck compact.
   - Purpose: To achieve a glossy red color and a neat shape after deep-frying.
3. **Deep-fry for color**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 140°C. Use a fine-mesh strainer to support the duck breast side as it goes into the oil. Fry until the skin darkens, becomes slightly crispy, and firm, turning the duck over during the process.
   - Home alternative: If you don't have enough oil for deep-frying, you can pan-fry the duck for the same coloring effect.
   - Effect: Tightens the skin, removes gamey odor, and enhances aroma, making it easier to absorb flavors later.
4. **Stir-fry the sauce base**: Remove the duck and drain the oil. Leave a little oil in the wok, add the ginger slices and scallion sections, and stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant. Pour in 100g soy sauce and 200g rice wine, then immediately turn the heat to high and stir-fry vigorously to fully release the aroma of the wine and soy sauce.
   - Indicator: The wok should be steaming with a strong, rich fragrance.
5. **Braise for flavor**: Return the fried duck to the wok. Add enough water to submerge the duck, then add the Adenophora root, Codonopsis root, cinnamon, star anise, bay leaves, red dates, cardamom, cloves, and dried tangerine peel. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to low heat, cover, and simmer for about 2 hours.
   - Tenderness standard: A chopstick should be able to easily pierce through the thickest part of the duck leg.
6. **Reduce the sauce and thicken**: Carefully remove the duck and place it on a serving plate. Strain the sauce in the wok to remove all solids. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. **There is no need to add more salt.** Once the sauce is thick, drizzle in a suitable amount of starch water and stir until smooth and coats a spoon.
7. **Plate and serve**: Pour the reduced sauce evenly over the duck and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is low in salt and high in protein, making it suitable for banquets and nourishing meals. However, the deep-frying step increases the calorie content, so it is recommended to serve with a light stir-fried vegetable to balance. The duck meat is tender and flavorful, easy to digest, but those with high blood pressure should note the sodium content in the soy sauce.
