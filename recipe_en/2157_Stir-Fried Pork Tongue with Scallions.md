# Stir-Fried Pork Tongue with Scallions  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐷 Pork tongue 2 pieces (use only the tender front part, remove the root)  
🧅 Large scallion 1 (shredded, for scallion oil)  
🌿 Small scallion 1 bunch (cut into sections, separate white and green parts)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper to taste (cut into strips, for color)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (cut into strips)  
🧂 Salt to taste (about 2g for marinating, 1g for sauce)  
🌶️ Ground white pepper to taste (about 1g for marinating, a pinch for sauce)  
🥄 Light soy sauce about 10g (about 5g for marinating, 5g for sauce)  
🥔 Potato starch to taste (for coating)  
🫒 Sesame oil a small amount (added at the end of marinating)  
🍺 Beer 20g (to remove odor and enhance aroma)  
🧂 MSG 1g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking oil as needed (for sliding, and a small amount for stir-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Strainer  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Bowl (for marinating and mixing sauce)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Blanch and Peel the Pork Tongue**  
   Place the pork tongue in cold water, bring to a boil, then cook over medium heat for 1 minute. Remove and immediately submerge in cold water.  
**Key Point**: Cook until the white membrane can be easily removed. Cooling in cold water makes it easier to peel the membrane in the next step.  

2. **Process the Pork Tongue**  
   While still warm, remove or scrape off the white membrane on the surface. Cut off the rough root part. Then slice diagonally into pieces about the thickness of a coin.  
**Key Point**: The white membrane must be completely removed, otherwise it will affect the tender and crispy texture.  

3. **Marinate**  
   Add salt, ground white pepper, and light soy sauce to the pork tongue slices. Mix until sticky. Add potato starch and mix well. Finally, drizzle in sesame oil and mix evenly.  
**Key Point**: Adding oil after coating enhances aroma and prevents sticking during cooking.  

4. **Prepare Aromatics and Sauce**  
   Shred the large scallion. Cut the small scallion into sections. Cut the red bell pepper into strips. Cut the ginger into strips.  
   In a bowl, mix beer, salt, MSG, ground white pepper, oyster sauce, and light soy sauce. Stir until well combined.  
**Key Point**: Prepare the sauce in advance to ensure flavors coat the ingredients quickly during stir-frying.  

5. **Slide the Pork Tongue in Oil**  
   Heat the wok over high heat. Add enough oil and heat to about 70% hot. Add the pork tongue slices, quickly separate them with chopsticks, and cook until the meat changes color and is about 70% done. Immediately remove and drain oil.  
**Key Point**: Use chopsticks to separate the slices to prevent sticking. Do not overcook, or the meat will become tough.  

6. **Fry the Scallion Oil**  
   Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. When it reaches 50% hot, add the shredded large scallion. Fry until the scallion turns golden brown and releases a strong aroma, then remove the scallion.  
**Key Point**: The scallion oil is the main source of scallion flavor for this dish. The fried scallion shreds can be discarded.  

7. **Stir-Fry**  
   Using the scallion oil in the wok, stir-fry the white parts of the small scallion, ginger strips, and red bell pepper strips over medium heat until fragrant. Add the pre-cooked pork tongue slices and quickly stir-fry a few times.  
   Pour in the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok. Turn to high heat and stir-fry until evenly coated.  
**Note**: No special notes, but maintain proper heat control.  

8. **Finish and Plate**  
   Add the green parts of the small scallion. Stir-fry until just wilted and fragrant, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.  
**Key Point**: Add the green parts of the scallion last to keep them bright green and crunchy.  

## Health Tips  
Pork tongue is a low-fat, high-protein ingredient. Stir-frying it with scallions and ginger helps remove odor and enhances aroma. It is suitable for supplementing iron and boosting immunity. However, due to its relatively high cholesterol content, individuals with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
