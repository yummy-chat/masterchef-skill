# Stir-fried Deboned Pork Knuckle with Chili Peppers

Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐

## Steps
1. Wash the pork knuckle, cut it in half from the middle to facilitate deboning and handling.
2. Add the pork knuckle to a pot with cold water and适量 rice wine. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then remove the pork knuckle and rinse thoroughly with clean water.
   **Note**: Starting with cold water helps remove blood and gamey odors more effectively.
3. Heat oil in the wok, sauté the ginger and scallion segments until fragrant. Add enough water, the blanched pork knuckle, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, salt, sugar, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Cover and simmer over low heat.
   **Key point**: Simmer for about 30–40 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce the pork knuckle.
4. Remove the braised pork knuckle, let it cool, then remove all bones. Cut the skin-on meat into cubes about 2 cm in size.
5. Wash the green chili peppers and bird's eye chili, slice them diagonally. To reduce the heat and suit Cantonese tastes, split the peppers and remove the seeds.
6. Slice the dried tofu diagonally into thick pieces. Place the sliced tofu in the braising liquid from the pork knuckle and cook for about 2 minutes to absorb the flavor, then remove and drain.
   **Note**: For home cooking, pre-braised dried tofu can be used to simplify the step.
7. Heat enough oil in the wok to about 150°C. Fry the braised and cubed pork knuckle until the surface turns slightly golden, then remove. Using the same oil, fry the cooked dried tofu until golden brown, then remove.
8. Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the prepared chili pepper segments, a pinch of salt, and a little light soy sauce. Stir-fry until the peppers are cooked through and flavored, then set aside.
   **Key point**: Adding a little water helps the peppers absorb flavor faster while staying crisp.
9. Leave a little oil in the wok, sauté the minced garlic and white parts of the scallion until fragrant. Add the fried pork knuckle, and drizzle a little rice wine along the edge of the wok to enhance the aroma.
10. Next, add the fried dried tofu and stir-fried peppers, along with the fermented black beans. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until well combined.
    **Key point**: Do not add the fermented black beans too early, as they may burn and become bitter.
11. Drizzle a little light soy sauce to season, stir-fry over high heat for a few seconds, then remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Advice
Stir-fried pork knuckle with chili peppers is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those with dry skin or needing joint care. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. When cooking, control the amount of oil and salt used. For a healthier option, the frying steps can be replaced with pan-frying using less oil.
