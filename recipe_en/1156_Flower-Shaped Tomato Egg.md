# Flower-Shaped Tomato Egg
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 1 egg (yolk kept intact, egg white whisked into egg liquid)  
🍅 8–10 cherry tomatoes (uniform size, washed)  
🌿 1 cilantro leaf (or celery leaf, used as decorative stem)  
🧂 None (salt or soy sauce can be added according to personal taste)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥣 Bowl (for holding egg liquid)  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Pour a small amount of oil into the pan and heat over low heat. Arrange the cherry tomatoes in the pan to form a circular flower shape.  
   **Note**: Ensure the cherry tomatoes are as uniform in size as possible and neatly arranged for an attractive petal pattern.  
2. Gently place a whole egg yolk in the center of the cherry tomato circle as the flower center.  
   **Key point**: The yolk must not be broken and should remain perfectly round.  
3. Slowly pour the whisked egg white from the edge around the cherry tomatoes and yolk, letting it spread naturally into a petal shape.  
   **Heat control**: Cook over low heat throughout to prevent the bottom from burning.  
4. Once the egg liquid is fully set and the edges turn light golden, place a cilantro leaf below the egg surface as a decorative stem.  
5. Gently shake the pan to confirm the egg can slide freely, then transfer the dish to a plate.  
   **Doneness check**: The egg liquid should be completely set, the surface dry, and the flower shape stable.  

## Health Tips  
This Flower-Shaped Tomato Egg dish is rich in lycopene and high-quality protein, making it suitable for children and diners who value visual presentation. However, note that underripe cherry tomatoes may be tart; it is recommended to add a small amount of salt or soy sauce for seasoning.
