# Spicy Stir-Fried Seafood Pot
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍤 Shrimp, to taste  
🦀 Hairy crab, to taste  
🐚 Clams, to taste  
🐚 Periwinkles, to taste  
🐟 Abalone, to taste (score in a crisscross pattern)  
🦑 Squid tentacles, to taste (cut into pieces)  
🫘 Long beans, to taste (cut into sections)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Minced garlic, to taste  
🌿 Celery, to taste (cut into sections)  
🌿 Chopped green onions, a little (for garnish)  

🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🫙 Haidilao spicy hot pot base, to taste  
🫒 Oil, to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine, a small amount  
🧂 Soy sauce, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Shallow frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Colander  
🍽️ Large bowl/plate  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Prepare the seafood: Cut the squid tentacles into pieces, and score the abalone in a crisscross pattern. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the clams, periwinkles, and other shellfish, and cook until they open. Remove and drain well.  
   **Note**: Blanching shellfish helps remove sand and reduce fishy smell. Remove as soon as they open to avoid overcooking.

2. Heat oil in the frying pan over low heat. Add dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, minced garlic, and sliced onion. Stir-fry until fragrant and red oil is released.  
   **Key**: Be sure to use low heat to prevent the aromatics from burning.

3. Add the spicy hot pot base. Continue to stir-fry over low heat until the base melts and becomes fragrant, allowing it to fully blend with the oil.

4. Add more difficult-to-cook vegetables like long beans. Increase the heat to medium and stir-fry briefly.

5. Pour in all the blanched and drained seafood. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly, ensuring the seafood is evenly coated with the base and sauce.  
   **Note**: The seafood has already been blanched, so do not stir-fry for too long to prevent it from becoming tough.

6. Sprinkle a small amount of cooking wine along the edge of the pan. Then add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chili oil to season. Continue to stir-fry over high heat.

7. Just before turning off the heat, add the celery sections. Stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds, until the celery is just cooked but still crisp. Turn off the heat.

8. Transfer to a large bowl, sprinkle chopped green onions on top for garnish, and serve.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in seafood and high-quality protein, but it uses a significant amount of spicy hot pot base and oil. It is best suited for sharing at a gathering rather than eating alone. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or a cucumber salad to balance the calories. Those with gout or a sensitive gastrointestinal system should only eat a small amount.
