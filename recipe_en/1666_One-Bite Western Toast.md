# One-Bite Western Toast
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Toast bread: About 6-8 slices  
🟣 Taro: About 1 (peeled and cut into chunks)  
🟣 Purple sweet potato: About 1 (peeled and cut into chunks)  
🥚 Eggs: 3  
🥛 Milk: 60g  
🍬 Sugar: 15g  
🥜 Peanut butter: As needed (savory, used as filling)  
🥛 Condensed milk: As needed (for final decoration)  
🥥 Coconut flakes: As needed (for final decoration)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker (or steamer)  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥣 Large bowl  
🔨 Masher  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍴 Butter knife  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps
1. **Make taro paste filling**: Peel and cut the taro and purple sweet potato into chunks, then steam them in a pressure cooker until cooked. Remove and drain excess water, transfer to a large bowl, add sugar and milk, and mash with a masher into a smooth and creamy paste.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of milk as needed based on the moisture of the taro paste to achieve a smooth consistency.

2. **Assemble the sandwich**: Take toast slices and trim off the crusts. Spread an even layer of taro paste on one slice, top with another slice, and repeat to stack 3-4 layers. Cut the stacked toast in half or into small cubes.  
   **Note**: The filling can be changed according to preference; the trimmed bread edges can be dried in the oven as a snack to avoid waste.

3. **Prepare the egg-milk mixture**: In a bowl, crack 3 eggs, add 15g sugar and 60g milk, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the sugar is fully dissolved.

4. **Coat with egg mixture**: Place the cut toast cubes into the egg-milk mixture, ensuring all six sides are evenly coated and fully soaked.  
   **Key**: For a softer texture, you can extend the soaking time to allow the toast to absorb more liquid.

5. **Pan-fry**: Heat oil in a frying pan. Once hot, add the egg-coated toast cubes and fry over medium-low heat until each side is golden and crispy, then flip. Continue until all sides have a golden-brown crust.  
   **Note**: Use chopsticks for easier flipping to ensure even heat distribution on all sides.

6. **Decorate and serve**: Arrange the fried toast on a plate, drizzle with condensed milk as desired, and sprinkle with coconut flakes.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with black coffee or green tea to cut through the richness. It is suitable for students or office workers needing a quick energy boost. Note that the taro paste already contains sugar, so the amount of condensed milk and coconut flakes can be reduced as needed to avoid large blood sugar fluctuations.
