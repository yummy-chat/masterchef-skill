# Chrysanthemum Pastry
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Flour 500g (1 jin)
💧 Water 250g (half a jin)
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten)
🍒 Hawthorn Cake (Jing糕) an appropriate amount (finely chopped into granules)
🧂 Salt about 3.5g
🫧 Baking soda 1g

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥖 Rolling pin
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix water, salt, and baking soda together, stirring until completely dissolved.
**Key point**: Ensure the salt and baking soda are fully dissolved to avoid lumps in the finished product.
2. Add the beaten egg, mix thoroughly and evenly to create an egg mixture.
3. Pour the egg mixture into the flour, stir and knead into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: The dough should not be too firm; it is best if soft and pliable for easier shaping later.
4. Place the dough in a bowl and let it rest at room temperature for about 10 minutes. If the dough is kneaded thoroughly, the resting time can be appropriately shortened.
5. Roll the rested dough into a long strip, then tear off small pieces slightly larger than a dumpling wrapper piece, ensuring uniform size.
6. Flatten each piece and roll it out with a rolling pin into a thin round sheet slightly larger than a dumpling wrapper.
**Key point**: Do not use too much flour for dusting, otherwise the oil will become cloudy during frying.
7. First fold the round sheet horizontally once, then vertically once, forming a multi-layered fan shape. Use a knife to cut it into thin strips, leaving 1–2 cm uncut at one end.
**Key point**: Cut the strips as finely as possible so that the fried petals are more distinct and layered.
8. Unfold the cut sheet, gently gather the cut strips towards the center by hand, then use two chopsticks to clamp the uncut middle part, while using your fingers to help squeeze the middle, shaping it into a chrysanthemum shape.
**Key point**: There should be a "narrow waist" in the middle; use the chopsticks to clamp tightly to prevent it from falling apart during frying; apply moderate force to avoid breaking the dough.
9. Pour oil into the wok and heat to about 40% hot. Using chopsticks, hold the center of the chrysanthemum pastry raw piece and gently place it into the oil.
**Key point**: After placing it in the oil, gently shake the chopsticks to let the petals spread and set; maintain medium-low heat, the frying process is very fast, so turn it promptly, fry until it turns golden brown all over, then remove.
10. Remove and drain the oil, arrange on a plate lined with clean green leaves, and dot the center of each chrysanthemum pastry with finely chopped hawthorn cake granules as the stamen.

## Health Suggestions
Chrysanthemum Pastry is rich in carbohydrates, suitable for occasional enjoyment as a tea snack. Fried foods should be consumed in moderation. The sourness of the hawthorn cake can help cut the grease, and it pairs better with green tea.
