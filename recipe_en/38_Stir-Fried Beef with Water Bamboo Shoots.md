# Stir-Fried Beef with Water Bamboo Shoots
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef Tenderloin – about 0.5 lb (sliced thinly against the grain)  
🥒 Water Bamboo Shoots – about 0.7 lb (peeled and julienned)  
🫑 Green Bell Pepper – to taste (julienned)  
🌶️ Red Chili Pepper – to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic – to taste (crushed and minced)  
🫚 Ginger – to taste (partially crushed for ginger juice, partially minced)  
🧅 Green Garlic Leaves – to taste (cut into sections)  
🧂 Salt – to taste  
🧂 MSG – to taste  
🧂 White Pepper Powder – to taste  
🧴 Dark Soy Sauce – a little  
🫒 Cooking Oil – to taste  
🫒 Rapeseed Oil – to taste  
🧈 Lard – one spoonful  
🧴 Light Soy Sauce – a little  
🍶 Aged Vinegar – a little

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Iron Wok (Chinese wok)  
🔪 Chef’s Knife  
🪓 Cutting Board  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
🧺 Strainer Basket  
🥄 Spoon  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the beef tenderloin and slice thinly against the grain, striving for even thin slices. Do not wash again after slicing.  
   **Key Point**: Use beef tenderloin for its tender texture, ideal for quick stir-frying—no marinating starch needed.
2. Add salt, MSG, white pepper powder, and a little dark soy sauce to the beef slices. Crush the ginger with a little water to extract the ginger juice, pour the juice into the meat, and mix thoroughly by hand until fully absorbed. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix well to seal in moisture.  
   **Key Point**: No starch is used; ginger juice removes odors and tenderizes. Adding oil at the end prevents sticking when cooking.
3. Peel the outer skin of the water bamboo shoots at the root, slice them diagonally, then cut into julienne strips.
4. Crush the garlic and mince it. Mince the remaining ginger. Finely chop the red chili pepper and set aside together with the minced garlic and ginger. Julienne the green bell pepper. Cut the green garlic leaves into sections.
5. Heat the wok and add rapeseed oil over high heat to coat the wok and prevent sticking. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated beef along with the minced ginger, garlic, and chili peppers. Stir-fry quickly over high heat.  
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat high throughout. Remove the beef as soon as it has all changed color and released some liquid—do not overcook.
6. In a separate wok, add one spoonful of lard. Once hot, add the julienned water bamboo shoots and stir-fry over high heat, seasoning with salt. When the bamboo shoots are just cooked, add the julienned green bell pepper and stir-fry together.
7. Return the previously stir-fried beef along with its liquid to the wok, combining with the bamboo shoots. Drizzle in a little light soy sauce and continue stir-frying over high heat, allowing the bamboo shoots to fully absorb the savory flavor of the beef juices.
8. Add the green garlic leaf sections. Drizzle a little aged vinegar along the edge of the wok to release its aroma with the high heat. Quickly stir-fry everything evenly and immediately remove from heat.  
   **Key Point**: Pour the aged vinegar along the wok edge to fully release its fragrance; stir-fry quickly.

## Health Advice
This dish pairs beef with water bamboo shoots, offering high protein and low fat—ideal for weight loss periods or for cooling and moistening dryness in summer. Note to stir-fry beef quickly over high heat to lock in tenderness, and avoid overcooking the water bamboo shoots to retain their crunch. Use less lard for a healthier option.
