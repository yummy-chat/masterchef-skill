# Braised Noodles with Spare Ribs and Long Beans  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦴 Spare ribs, about 500g (pork rib, pre-blanched)  
🫘 Long beans, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🍝 Dried noodles, appropriate amount (thicker round noodles or hand-pulled noodles)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, 1 head (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, 3-4 (whole)  
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece  
🥄 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, 2 teaspoons  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder, 1 teaspoon  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Ladle  
🥣 Bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Blanch the spare ribs in advance and drain. Cut the long beans into sections. Cut scallions into sections, slice ginger, mince garlic, and prepare dried chilies and star anise.  
2. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the wok. Heat over medium heat until about 50% hot. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, dried chilies, and star anise. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
3. Add the blanched spare ribs. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface is slightly golden and crispy, making the ribs more aromatic.  
4. Pour in the prepared sauce mixture. Stir-fry evenly to coat the spare ribs thoroughly.  
5. Add enough hot water. Cover the wok, reduce heat to medium-low, and braise for 30 minutes.  
6. Remove the lid. Add the long bean sections. Gently stir, then continue braising over medium-low heat for 15 minutes.  
7. Use a ladle to scoop out about half a bowl of the broth and set aside.  
8. Evenly spread the dried noodles over the long beans and spare ribs.  
9. Evenly pour the reserved broth over the noodles to ensure they fully absorb the liquid.  
10. Cover the wok and simmer over low heat for 6 minutes, allowing the steam to cook the noodles until the broth is nearly absorbed.  
11. Remove the lid. Sprinkle a generous amount of minced garlic and scallions on top, letting the residual heat release their aroma.  
12. Use chopsticks to thoroughly mix the noodles, long beans, spare ribs, minced garlic, and scallions until the noodles are dry and fragrant. Transfer to a plate and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Spare ribs provide high-quality protein and calcium, while long beans are rich in dietary fiber. However, spare ribs are high in fat, so it is recommended to pair them with light vegetables to balance calorie intake. Suitable for those needing energy replenishment, but individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation.
