# Potato Buns
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 180g  
🍬 Caster sugar 6g  
🧂 Salt 2g  
💧 Water 110g  
🥔 Cooked potato 50g (mashed and added to dough)  
🍞 Fresh yeast 6g (or dry yeast 2g)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 10g  
🥓 Raw bacon 88g (pan-fried and chopped)  
🍄 Raw button mushrooms 35g (pan-fried and chopped)  
🥔 Raw potato 190g (steamed and mashed into puree)  
🧀 Mozzarella cheese 90g  
🌽 Cornstarch 12g (for surface decoration)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 5g (for surface decoration)  
🫕 Mayonnaise 15g  
🫙 Mustard seeds paste 6g  
⚫ Ground black pepper 1g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Dough scraper/spatula  
🍳 Frying pan  
♨️ Steamer/steam oven  
📄 Baking tray  
🔥 Oven  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the filling**: Finely chop the mushrooms and bacon, then pan-fry until cooked through. Set aside. Steam the potatoes and mash into puree. Add the cooked mushrooms, bacon, mayonnaise, mustard seeds paste, and ground black pepper. Mix well, then add the mozzarella cheese and combine evenly. Divide the filling into portions of about 55g each, shape into balls, and refrigerate until needed.  
   **Key point**: Keeping some texture in the filling enhances flavor; chilling makes it easier to wrap.

2. **Prepare the surface decoration powder**: Mix the cornstarch and cocoa powder evenly. Set aside.

3. **Knead the dough**: Combine the bread flour, caster sugar, and salt evenly. Make a well in the center, pour in the water, mashed cooked potato, and yeast. Stir from the center outward until a shaggy dough forms. Knead until no dry flour remains, then cut the dough open and add the softened butter. Using a dough scraper, employ a "slap-and-fold" technique to knead for about 7–8 minutes, until the dough surface is mostly smooth.  
   **Key point**: The slap-and-fold technique helps develop gluten; the butter must be fully absorbed.

4. **Rest and check the gluten**: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 5 minutes. After resting, stretch the dough. It should form a thick membrane that doesn't easily tear, with jagged edges when it breaks.

5. **First fermentation**: Shape the dough into a round ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let ferment at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until a finger dipped in flour leaves an indentation that does not spring back.

6. **Divide and rest**: Press the dough to release gas, divide it evenly into 6 pieces. Gently press each piece to release gas, then roll into balls. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

7. **Fill and shape**: Take a rested dough piece and flatten it with your hands into a round disc that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Tightly press the chilled filling ball into an oval shape and place it in the center of the dough. Seal the bun using a method similar to wrapping a baozi: first pinch the center together to form a line, then bring the two ends towards the center and pinch tightly. Shape into an irregular oval resembling a potato.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the seam is tightly sealed to prevent filling leakage during baking.

8. **Surface decoration**: Evenly coat the dough surface with the cornstarch and cocoa powder mixture to create a "dirt" texture. Use chopsticks to poke random dimples on the surface to simulate potato eyes, taking care not to puncture the filled center.

9. **Final fermentation and baking**: Place the shaped dough pieces on a baking tray and let ferment until doubled in size. Preheat a convection oven to 170°C and bake for 10 minutes.

10. Enjoy while hot; the bread is soft and the filling is savory with a cheesy pull.

## Health Advice  
Potato buns are primarily carbohydrate-based. It is best to enjoy them while hot for the best cheese pull, but be mindful of your portion size to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake.
