# White-Cut Beef Shank
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Recommended Season: All-year-round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank (whole piece)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili (appropriate amount, minced)  
🌶️ Wild pepper/ pickled chili (appropriate amount, to pair with bird's eye chili)  
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)  
🧅 Spring onion (appropriate amount, cut into sections)  
🧅 Red onion (appropriate amount, sliced thinly, provides a return of sweetness)  
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount, slightly more than for stir-frying)  
🫚 Ginger (a few slices)  
🍶 Cooking wine (appropriate amount)  
🫒 Peanut oil (appropriate amount, use cold oil, do not heat)  
🥡 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount)  

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok/Stockpot  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the beef shank clean. Prepare dipping sauce ingredients such as bird's eye chili, wild pepper/pickled chili, garlic, spring onion, and red onion.
2. Place cold water in a pot, add the beef shank, ginger slices, salt, and an appropriate amount of cooking wine. Note: Must be placed in cold water.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat. Use a soup ladle to skim off any foam, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 1 hour.
   **Key Point**: The foam must be skimmed clean. Slow simmering over low heat is essential to achieve a springy, firm, and non-loose texture.
4. The beef shank is cooked when a chopstick can be inserted easily. Remove and let it cool completely. Note: For a better result, refrigerate overnight to make the meat firmer and easier to slice.
5. Prepare the dipping sauce: Mince the bird's eye chili and wild pepper/pickled chili, mince the garlic, cut the spring onion into sections, and slice the red onion thinly. Place everything into a bowl.
6. Directly add cold peanut oil and light soy sauce to the bowl, stir well, and let it marinate for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
   **Key Point**: Do not heat the peanut oil; keep its raw, rich aroma.
7. Slice the completely cooled beef shank into thin slices, the thinner the better.
8. To eat, pick up a slice of beef shank, fully coat it in the dipping sauce, and optionally roll it with a piece of spring onion for a complex flavor that is savory, mildly spicy, with a return of sweetness.

## Health Advice
White-cut beef shank is rich in high protein and low in fat, making it an ideal choice for fitness and muscle building. However, the dipping sauce is high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance dietary nutrition.
