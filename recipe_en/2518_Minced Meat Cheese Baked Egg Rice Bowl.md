# Minced Meat Cheese Baked Egg Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Boneless beef (snowflake edge, tenderloin) appropriate amount, minced  
🥚 Eggs appropriate amount, beaten into egg liquid  
🧀 Cheese shreds appropriate amount  
🫘 Green beans appropriate amount, diced and blanched in advance  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount, diced  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount, minced  
🍚 Quinoa rice appropriate amount, as the main staple  
🍘 Dried seaweed shreds appropriate amount, for garnish  
🧂 Pearl River Bridge brand umami sauce (or light soy sauce type umami sauce) appropriate amount  
🧂 Black pepper powder appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan  
🔒 Lid  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Bowl or measuring cup (for beating eggs)  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Select boneless beef cuts, mince into ground meat, and set aside.  
2. Dice green beans, blanch in boiling water until cooked through, drain; dice onion, mince garlic.  
3. Heat a flat-bottomed pan over medium heat, add ground beef and stir-fry quickly until it changes color, then add diced onion and minced garlic, stir-frying until fragrant.  
4. Add the blanched diced green beans, stir-fry evenly, season with umami sauce and black pepper powder, stir well, then spread all ingredients evenly over the pan bottom.  
5. Evenly pour the beaten egg liquid over the meat and vegetable mixture, and when the edges of the egg liquid begin to set, evenly sprinkle with cheese shreds.  
6. Cover with the lid, reduce to medium-low heat, and bake until the cheese is completely melted and the egg liquid is fully set, then turn off the heat.  
7. Place the quinoa rice in a bowl, top with the whole baked minced meat cheese egg mixture, sprinkle with dried seaweed shreds, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein, with beef and eggs providing high-quality protein, cheese supplementing calcium, and quinoa rice increasing dietary fiber, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those looking to build muscle. Note that cheese and umami sauce are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of seasonings. Ensure green beans are thoroughly cooked to prevent poisoning.
