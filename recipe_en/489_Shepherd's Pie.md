# Shepherd's Pie  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Ground beef (moderate amount)  
🥔 Potatoes (moderate amount, boiled with skin on)  
🥬 Celery (moderate amount, chopped finely)  
🥕 Carrots (moderate amount, chopped finely)  
🧅 Onion (moderate amount, chopped finely)  
🧀 Parmesan cheese (generous amount, added to mashed potatoes)  
🧈 Butter (1 small piece, added to mashed potatoes)  
🥛 Milk (some, added to mashed potatoes)  
🧂 Salt (moderate amount, 1 spoon for boiling potatoes, a small amount for stir-frying meat filling)  
🌿 Rosemary (moderate amount)  
🍅 Tomato paste (moderate amount)  
🍲 Beef broth (moderate amount, not quantified in the video)  
⚫ Black pepper (moderate amount)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Snow pot (for boiling potatoes)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for stir-frying filling)  
🥄 Wooden spatula/wooden spoon  
🥔 Potato masher  
🍴 Fork (for mixing mashed potatoes and scoring patterns)  
🥘 Ceramic baking dish  
🔥 Oven (with independently controllable top and bottom heat)  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the potatoes with skin on, place them in the pot, add enough water and 1 spoon of salt, cover with lid, and boil over low heat until a knife can easily pierce through and slide off.  
2. Finely chop celery, carrots, and onion.  
3. Heat the pan over medium heat, stir-fry the chopped vegetables until they release moisture, then add ground beef and cook until fully browned.  
4. Once the meat is browned, add a small amount of salt, black pepper, rosemary, and tomato paste for seasoning, pour in beef broth, cover the lid, and simmer until the liquid is fully reduced.  
   **Note**: Beef broth is naturally salty, so do not add too much salt at once.  
5. Once potatoes are cooked, remove them, peel, and mash while hot with a potato masher.  
6. Add a generous amount of grated Parmesan cheese, 1 small piece of butter, and some hot milk to the mashed potatoes, mixing thoroughly with a fork.  
7. Spread the cooked ground beef at the bottom of the baking dish, cover with mashed potatoes, and use a fork to score patterns on the surface.  
8. Bake in the oven at 180°C top heat and 150°C bottom heat for 25 minutes, then increase top heat to 200°C and continue baking until the surface turns golden brown and the potato crust is crispy.  
   **Note**: If the oven cannot independently control temperatures, bake at 190°C throughout, and observe browning during the last 5–10 minutes.  

## Health Advice  
This shepherd's pie is rich in high-quality protein and beta-carotene, suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, Parmesan cheese and butter make it relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to serve with a salad to balance nutrition.
