# Raincoat Cucumber  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Cucumber 1 piece (about 200-300g, exact weight not specified)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍶 Vinegar to taste  
🍋 Lemon juice to taste  
🧅 Minced garlic to taste  
🥣 Light soy sauce to taste  
🍬 Granulated sugar to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil to taste  
🌶️ Fresh chili peppers to taste  
🌶️ Chili oil to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Stirring spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the cucumber using the raincoat slicing technique: first, make straight cuts on one side, cutting to half the thickness of the cucumber without slicing through; flip it over and make diagonal cuts, again without slicing through. When finished, pull it apart to reveal a spiral, connected raincoat shape.  
   **Key point**: Maintain a steady rhythm in your knife work, using your left hand to control the spacing between slices; home cooking does not require pursuing advanced techniques.  

2. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine salt, vinegar, lemon juice, minced garlic, light soy sauce, granulated sugar, sesame oil, fresh chili peppers, and chili oil. Stir until bright red and evenly mixed.  
   **Note**: All seasoning amounts are to taste; adjust the balance of sour, spicy, salty, and sweet according to personal preference.  

3. Pour the prepared sauce directly over the raincoat cucumber.  
   **Note**: You may choose to first lightly salt the cucumber to draw out water, depending on personal habit.  

## Health Advice  
This raincoat cucumber dish features cucumber as the main ingredient—low in calories, high in water content, and rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight control or clearing heat and relieving summer heat. Note that minced garlic and fresh chili peppers can irritate the stomach, so those with a sensitive stomach should use them sparingly.
