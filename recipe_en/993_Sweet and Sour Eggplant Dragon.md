# Sweet and Sour Eggplant Dragon
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍆 2 long purple eggplants  
🌾 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (about 30g)  
🥔 2 tablespoons potato starch (about 30g)  
🥒 Cucumber (appropriate amount, diced)  
🥕 Carrot (appropriate amount, diced)  
🧂 1/2 teaspoon salt (for base flavor)  
🫒 About 75g cooking oil (for batter) + additional for deep-frying  
🍶 A pinch of baking powder (optional)  
🍅 About 75g tomato ketchup  
🍬 75g white sugar  
🍶 About 75g white vinegar  
🧂 A pinch of MSG  
💧 Appropriate amount of water starch  
💧 Appropriate amount of cold water (for batter)  
💧 Appropriate amount of clear broth or water (for sauce)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Wok/deep-frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut off the stem and tip of the eggplant. Use a “winding knife” cut (sashimi-style) to create a continuous, unbroken eggplant dragon.
**Key:** First cut diagonally to a depth of 2/3, then turn over and cut straight down on the other side, spacing cuts about 5mm apart; or use chopsticks as a guide to prevent cutting through.

2. Prepare the batter: Mix flour and potato starch in a 1:1 ratio. Add a pinch of baking powder, salt, and cooking oil. Gradually add cold water and stir until a thick, smooth batter forms.
3. Stretch the eggplant dragon open. Lightly dust with dry flour first, then evenly coat with batter, ensuring all gaps and cuts are fully covered.
4. Heat plenty of oil in a wok to about 180°C. Hold one end of the eggplant dragon and slide it into the oil, coiling it into a dragon shape as it enters. Use a slotted spoon to support the bottom and a ladle to continuously pour hot oil over the top, ensuring even cooking and setting the shape.
**Key:** Deep-fry over medium heat for about 4 minutes. When the crust turns apricot yellow and is firm, remove from oil.

5. Drain the fried eggplant dragon, cut into pieces while hot, and arrange on a plate.
6. Make the sweet and sour sauce: Leave a little oil in the wok. Sauté the tomato ketchup over low heat until red oil appears. Add the white sugar and stir until melted. Then add the diced cucumber and carrot and stir-fry. Pour white vinegar along the edge of the wok, add a little clear broth, salt, and MSG. Thicken with water starch until the sauce is glossy and thick.
7. Pour the hot sweet and sour sauce evenly over the eggplant dragon and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This sweet and sour eggplant dragon is deep-fried and coated with a sweet and sour sauce, making it relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or coarse grains to balance the meal. The winding knife cut allows the eggplant to absorb flavors more easily, but also traps more oil. When draining, use a slotted spoon to press out excess oil.
