# Ginger and Scallion Fish Head Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Fish head, about 2.5 jin (main ingredient)  
🫚 Local yellow ginger, to taste (sliced; some used for marinating, some for stir-frying)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (some sliced for marinating, some whole for stir-frying)  
🧅 Local shallots, to taste (some shredded for marinating, some whole for stir-frying; add shallot segments before serving)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, to taste (shredded, for marinating)  
🌶️ Green and red chili pepper segments, to taste (added before serving)  
🌿 Cilantro/scallion segments, to taste (for garnish)  
🍶 Ginger juice, to taste (for marinating)  
🍶 Rice wine, to taste (for marinating and deglazing)  
🥄 Soy sauce, to taste (for marinating)  
🧂 White pepper, to taste (for marinating)  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (for marinating and braising)  
🥄 Cornstarch, to taste (for coating fish head before frying)  
🧈 Lard, to taste (for stir-frying aromatics)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste (for braising)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for braising)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste (for seasoning)  
🐟 Fish stock, to taste (for braising)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫕 Clay pot  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥣 Plate/bowl  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the fish head**: Clean the fish head, remove the throat bones, teeth, and other strongly fishy parts. Chop into pieces of suitable size.  
**Key point**: It is essential to remove the strongly fishy parts; this is the key to ensuring the final dish is not fishy.  

2. **Marinate the fish head**: In a container, combine ginger slices, garlic slices, shredded shallots, and shredded dried tangerine peel. Add ginger juice, rice wine, soy sauce, white pepper, and sugar. Mix well. Add the fish pieces and toss to coat evenly. Marinate for 5-10 minutes.  
**Key point**: Add the basic seasonings before marinating to infuse the fish head with flavor.  

3. **Pre-frying preparation**: Pat the marinated fish head dry with a paper towel. Then lightly dust it with a layer of cornstarch.  
**Key point**: Patting dry makes it easier to coat with cornstarch and fry; the cornstarch layer should not be too thick; its purpose is to seal in the juices and add crispiness.  

4. **Fry the fish head**: Heat a wok with a small amount of oil. Add the fish head pieces and fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.  
**Heat & Timing**: Medium heat, about 1-2 minutes, until the surface is golden and the pieces are set.  
**Key point**: Frying (shallow-fry), not deep-frying, helps better preserve the fish's fresh flavor.  

5. **Stir-fry the aromatics**: In the same wok, add lard. First, stir-fry the local yellow ginger slices until fragrant. Then add the garlic cloves and whole local shallots. Stir-fry until aromatic.  
**Key point**: Using local yellow ginger and shallots provides better flavor; the aromatics must be stir-fried first.  

6. **Braise the fish head**: Gently return the fried fish head pieces to the wok. Pour rice wine along the edge of the wok and toss to combine. Add fish stock, then season with oyster sauce, sugar, salt, dark soy sauce, and light soy sauce. Braise over medium heat for a short while.  
**Heat**: Medium heat.  
**Key point**: Adding rice wine effectively removes the fishy smell; add the shallot segments and green/red chili pepper segments at the end to preserve their fresh aroma.  

7. **Serve**: Transfer the cooked fish head and sauce into a preheated clay pot. Sprinkle with cilantro segments and scallion segments before serving.  
**Key point**: Transferring to a preheated clay pot helps maintain temperature, producing a "sizzling" sound when served and enhancing the aroma.  

## Health Advice  
Fish head is rich in DHA and collagen, making it suitable for office workers who overuse their brains and elderly people needing calcium supplementation. Ginger, scallions, and dried tangerine peel can remove fishy smells and warm the stomach, but lard and oyster sauce are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the diet.
