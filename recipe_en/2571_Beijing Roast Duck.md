# Beijing Roast Duck
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Duck Approximately 6 lbs (head and feet removed)  
🍎 Apple 1 (stuffed into the duck cavity to prevent drying)  
🍃 Bay leaves to taste  
🪵 Cinnamon stick to taste  
🫚 Ginger slices to taste  
🧅 Scallion segments to taste  
⭐ Star anise to taste  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns a pinch  
🍶 Light soy sauce half a bowl  
🍯 Honey (mixed with water in a 1:1 ratio to make honey water)  
🥫 Sweet bean sauce to taste (for serving)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
♨️ Oven (with rotating rotisserie function)  
🥘 Roasting rack (oven accessory)  
🖌️ Silicone brush  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the duck head and feet, pat the duck dry inside and out with paper towels, and hang to air dry for at least 3 hours until the skin is smooth and dry.  
**Key Point**: The duck skin must reach a smooth and dry state—this is the foundation for crispy skin.

2. In a bowl, combine bay leaves, cinnamon stick, ginger slices, scallion segments, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Add half a bowl of light soy sauce, stir well, and let steep for at least 3 hours to fully release the aromatics' flavors.  
3. Place the air-dried duck into the seasoning mixture, rubbing it evenly inside and out to ensure thorough coloring and flavor absorption. After rubbing, let it dry again to allow the seasoning to penetrate while the skin re-dries.  
4. Thread an apple onto the rotisserie spit, stuff it into the duck cavity, secure the duck onto the spit, and tuck the wings in to prevent them from drooping during roasting.  
5. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F), select the roast chicken mode, place a baking tray on the lower rack to catch drippings, and roast for 30 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Ensure the rotisserie rotates evenly for consistent heating.

6. Remove the duck, brush the honey water evenly over the entire duck, paying special attention to areas like the armpits.  
**Key Point**: The more evenly you brush, the brighter and redder the final color will be.

7. Lower the oven temperature to 150°C (300°F) and continue roasting for 40 minutes until the duck skin turns a glossy, red-brown date color and is fully cooked through.  
8. Remove the duck, detach the rotisserie spit, discard the duck tail and wings, and while hot, slice the meat into thin pieces with skin and flesh attached. Serve with sweet bean sauce and shredded scallions.

## Health Advice
This roast duck is high in fat. It is recommended to pair it with lettuce or cucumber to cut the richness, and to limit consumption to 3–5 slices per serving. It is best enjoyed occasionally rather than frequently.
