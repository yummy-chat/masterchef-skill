# Earl Grey Orange Scone
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥣 All-purpose flour 145g
🧂 Baking powder 5g
🍵 Earl Grey tea powder 3g
🍬 Sugar 15g
🧈 Cold butter 40g (cut into small pieces, refrigerated)
🥛 Heavy cream 125g
🥚 Egg yolk half
🍊 Candied orange peel 15g
🥚 Egg wash as needed (for brushing, can use leftover egg yolk or whole beaten egg)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife
🍽 Glass bowl (large)
🧑‍🍳 Cutting board
🧁 Flour sifter
⚖ Kitchen scale
🥄 Measuring cup/bowl
Dough scraper
Plastic wrap
🥡 Container (for refrigeration)
🔥 Oven
🍳 Baking tray
📄 Baking parchment
🖌 Brush
Tongs
🍯 Airtight glass jar

## Instructions
1.  Cut the cold butter into small pieces, place in a bowl and refrigerate; measure all dry ingredients, sugar, and liquid ingredients.
2.  Sift and mix the all-purpose flour, baking powder, Earl Grey tea powder, and sugar into a glass bowl.
    **Key point**: Sifting gives a finer texture.
3.  Add the refrigerated small butter pieces. Use your fingers to rub the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse, sandy crumbs.
4.  Add the candied orange peel and mix well.
5.  Combine the heavy cream and egg yolk, then pour into the flour-butter mixture.
6.  Gently mix by hand until just combined and no dry flour remains, forming a dough. **Do not knead**. Alternatively, use a dough scraper to cut and mix.
    **Key point**: Avoid kneading to prevent gluten formation; this is key to tender scones.
7.  Place the dough on a surface lined with plastic wrap. Press and shape into a square. Wrap tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate to relax for at least 30 minutes.
    **Key point**: Chilling allows the butter to firm up again, which helps with shaping and creating flaky layers.
8.  Remove the dough, cut directly into squares, and arrange on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
9.  Brush the surface evenly with egg wash.
    **Key point**: Brushing with egg wash gives the finished scones a glossy, shiny surface.
10. Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C for about 20 minutes, or until the surface is golden brown.
11. Let cool completely, then store in an airtight glass jar.

## Health Tip
These scones feature Earl Grey tea and orange peel, whose refreshing floral and fruity aroma can reduce the need for added sugar, making them suitable as an afternoon tea snack. Remember to keep the butter cold and avoid kneading to prevent gluten formation, ensuring a crisp exterior and tender interior.
