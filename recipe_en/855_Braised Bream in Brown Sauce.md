# Braised Bream in Brown Sauce  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 bream (about 1.2 jin, preferably wild-caught from large lakes)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (divided into scallion sections and chopped scallions)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste  
🌶️ Fresh red chilies, to taste  
🥓 Lard, to taste  
🍶 Yellow wine, to taste  
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🌿 White pepper powder, to taste  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, to taste  
🍶 Aged vinegar, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Clean the bream, scrape off the black membrane and blood clots from the belly, and drain well. Score both sides of the fish back with a knife, taking care not to cut through to the belly to prevent the fish from breaking during cooking.  
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add oil and heat until smoking to coat the bottom thoroughly. Discard the hot oil and add fresh cold oil, then place the bream in the wok. Reduce to medium heat and fry.  
   **Note**: Do not move the fish immediately after placing it in the wok. After frying for about 1 minute, gently shake the wok to ensure even heating of the head and tail. Fry one side for about 3 minutes, then flip and fry the other side. Both sides should be golden brown. Remove and set aside.  
3. Clean the wok, add lard, and heat over low heat. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried chilies. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
4. Return the fried fish to the wok, turn the heat to high, and when the oil temperature rises, drizzle yellow wine and light soy sauce along the edge of the wok to release the aroma of the seasonings.  
5. Add an appropriate amount of boiling water, then season with salt, white sugar, white pepper powder, dark soy sauce, and aged vinegar. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce to low heat, and braise for 7 minutes.  
6. Uncover the lid, remove seasoning residue such as scallions and ginger, add fresh red chilies, and turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Continuously spoon the sauce over the fish until the sauce naturally thickens. Transfer to a serving plate.  
   **Note**: Braised fish in brown sauce does not require thickening with starch. Rely on high heat to reduce the sauce, allowing it to evenly coat the fish.  

## Health Suggestions  
Bream is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for those managing weight or maintaining cardiovascular health. It is recommended to pair with leafy green vegetables and use less oil and salt for a healthier option. Those with weak digestion should consume the fish belly fat with caution.
