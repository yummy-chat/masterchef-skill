# Steamed Red Crab with Hua Diao Wine and Chicken Oil
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦀 Red crab (mud crab) - appropriate amount (live)
🫚 Ginger - 2-3 slices (per crab)
🥚 Egg - 1 (separate yolk and white)
🥣 Crab broth (original juice from steaming crab) - 1-2 tablespoons
🐔 Clear chicken broth - 1 tablespoon
🐔 Chicken oil - used twice (about 1 teaspoon drizzled before steaming, 1 tablespoon added to sauce)
🍶 Hua Diao wine - used twice (1-2 tablespoons added to sauce, about 1 teaspoon drizzled after removing from steamer)
🧂 Salt - a pinch
🍬 Sugar - a pinch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪓 Thick cutting board
🔪 Chinese chef's knife
🥄 Long-handled spoon
🥣 Ceramic bowl
♨️ Stainless steel steamer
🍽️ White deep plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Scrub the red crab clean, lift the shell, remove the sand sac, crab lungs, and crab heart. Cut the crab body in half, then into four pieces depending on size. Snip off the tips of the crab claws. Carefully remove the crab roe and place it on top of the crab pieces.
**Note**: Be sure to remove all inedible innards while retaining all the crab roe.

2. Arrange the crab pieces on a plate to reassemble the crab shape, place ginger slices on top, ensuring the crab roe is all positioned on the surface of the crab pieces.
3. Evenly drizzle about 1 teaspoon of chicken oil over the raw crab pieces.
**Key**: Do not add Hua Diao wine at this stage; only drizzle the chicken oil.

4. Place the plate into a preheated steamer. Steam until the crab shell turns from gray-green to bright red and the crab meat is just cooked through.
**Note**: Avoid over-steaming to prevent the crab meat from becoming tough.

5. In a bowl, combine the crab broth, clear chicken broth, the separated egg yolk and egg white, a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar, and 1 tablespoon of chicken oil. Mix thoroughly until well combined. Finally, add 1-2 tablespoons of Hua Diao wine and stir again until the sauce is golden yellow and uniform in texture.
**Note**: Hua Diao wine is the soul of the sauce and must be added last.

6. Evenly pour the prepared sauce over the first-steamed crab. Return to the steamer and steam for about 2 minutes, allowing the aroma of the sauce to infuse into the crab meat.
7. Remove the steamed crab from the steamer. Drizzle about 1 teaspoon of Hua Diao wine over the surface of the finished dish.
**Note**: This step significantly enhances the wine aroma and depth of flavor.

## Health Advice
This crab dish is double-steamed with chicken oil and Hua Diao wine, preserving its freshness while delivering a rich, mellow flavor. It is well-suited for nourishment in autumn and winter. However, the crab roe and chicken oil are high in calories; pairing with light vegetables is recommended to balance the nutrition. Children and those with gout should consume in moderation.
