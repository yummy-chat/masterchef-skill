# Double Pepper Pork Knuckle
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐷 1 pork knuckle (skin scraped clean, thoroughly washed)
🌶️ A few Erjingtiao peppers (sliced into rings)
🌶️ A few Sichuan small red peppers (sliced into rings)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallion (some cut into sections for blanching, some cut into sections for stir-frying)
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (some sliced for blanching, some diced for stir-frying)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (diced)
🌿 Appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorns (for blanching)
🍶 Appropriate amount of cooking wine
🍯 Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce (for rubbing onto the skin for color)
🫘 1 tablespoon of doubanjiang (broad bean chili paste)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dried chilies
🍂 Mixed spices: appropriate amounts of star anise, sand ginger, cassia bark, angelica, grass fruit, cloves, bay leaves, gardenia fruit, and cumin
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper powder
🍬 2 rock sugar pieces
🌿 Appropriate amount of green Sichuan peppercorns (for final oil splashing)
🫒 Sufficient vegetable oil (for deep-frying, stir-frying, and oil splashing)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥄 Wok
🥢 Slotted spoon
🧽 Small knife (for scraping the pork skin)
Multiple bamboo skewers
🥢 Chopsticks
🍲 Pot lid
🥄 Soup ladle and serving spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Instructions
1. Scrape the pork knuckle clean, place it in a pot with cold water. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, blanch thoroughly, skim off the foam, then remove.
2. While hot, densely poke the pork skin with bamboo skewers. Drizzle dark soy sauce over the skin and rub evenly across the entire surface. Set aside in a ventilated area to air-dry for a moment.
3. Cut the Erjingtiao peppers and small red peppers into rings. Dice the ginger and garlic. Cut the scallion into sections. Set aside.
4. Pour sufficient vegetable oil into the wok and heat to 170°C (340°F). Gently place the air-dried pork knuckle into the oil and fry over medium heat until the skin develops a wrinkled, tiger-stripe pattern. For any part not submerged, continuously ladle hot oil and pour over it.
5. Once fried, remove the knuckle. In another pot, leave a small amount of base oil. Add scallion sections, diced ginger, and diced garlic; stir-fry until fragrant. Add the doubanjiang and fry until the oil turns red and aromatic. Then add the dried chilies and all the mixed spices; stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Pour enough clean water into the pot and bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt, white pepper powder, and rock sugar. Add the fried pork knuckle. Cover the pot with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and braise for about 2 hours, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the meat.
7. Remove the braised pork knuckle and place it on a serving plate. Ladle a small amount of the braising liquid over it. Generously top the surface with the Erjingtiao pepper rings, small red pepper rings, and green Sichuan peppercorns.
8. In the wok, heat oil again until it just starts to smoke. Quickly and evenly splash the hot oil over the green Sichuan peppercorns and pepper rings to release their fragrance. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Double Pepper Pork Knuckle is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity. However, due to the deep-frying and braising process, it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balance. Those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation to avoid excessive intake of saturated fats.
