# Old Master's Secret Chicken
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole small chicken (about 1.5 jin, such as bamboo forest chicken)  
🦴 Pork leg or neck bones (as needed, for making broth in advance)  
🍄 5-6 dried shiitake mushrooms (rehydrated)  
🟤 8-10 dried longan (rehydrated)  
🌰 15-20 lotus seeds (rehydrated)  
🟠 8-10 small red dates (rehydrated)  
🔴 About 10g goji berries (rehydrated)  
🧅 2 scallions (cut into sections)  
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (sliced)  
🌿 1 small bunch of cilantro  
🍚 About 100g rice (for smoking base)  
🌲 About 20g small pine branches (for smoking aroma)  
🍵 About 10g jasmine tea (for smoking aroma)  

🧂 About 80g salt (for stir-frying with spices)  
✨ About 1 tablespoon fennel seeds (for stir-frying with salt)  
🌶️ About 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns (for stir-frying with salt)  
⭐ 3-4 star anise (for stir-frying with salt)  
🍂 A plate of dried spices: bay leaves, cinnamon, white cardamom, kaempferia galanga, angelica dahurica, amomum tsaoko, dried tangerine peel, nutmeg, etc. (soaked in water and drained)  
🌶️ 10-15 dried chili peppers  
🫒 About 3 tablespoons vegetable oil  
🐖 About 2 tablespoons lard  
🫗 Soy sauce, to taste  
🍶 Yellow wine, to taste  
🧊 Rock sugar, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for seasoning the braising liquid)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🌫️ White pepper, to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste (for brushing the chicken at the end)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥘 Wok  
🧄 Mortar and pestle or grinding bowl  
🫕 Clay pot  
🍱 Steaming rack  
🔥 Thick-bottomed stainless steel pot or smoking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🖌️ Brush  

## Steps  
1. Place a wok over low heat. Add fennel seeds, Sichuan peppercorns, and star anise. Dry-fry until fragrant. Add about 80g salt and continue stir-frying until the salt grains turn slightly yellow and the aroma is intense. Remove from heat. Pour the seasoned salt mixture into a mortar and grind into a fine powder.  
**Note**: The seasoned salt must be fully stir-fried. When marinating, cover the chicken inside and out thoroughly, and allow enough time for the flavor to penetrate.  

2. Rub the entire chicken, inside and out, with the seasoned salt powder. Let it marinate for 4 hours.  

3. Soak the dried spices in water for about 20 minutes, then drain. Cut scallions into sections, slice ginger, and wash cilantro. Set aside.  
**Note**: Soaking prevents burning during frying and removes bitterness.  

4. Heat vegetable oil and lard in a wok. Add scallion sections and ginger slices, fry until golden. Add the drained spices and cilantro. Reduce heat to low and fry slowly until the spices change color and release aroma. Splash in soy sauce, stir until the sauce is fragrant. Add yellow wine, rock sugar, salt, and oyster sauce. Stir to combine.  

5. Ladle in pre-prepared rich bone broth. Use a slotted spoon to remove the residue. Pour the clear broth into a clay pot and bring to a boil over high heat.  

6. Rinse the marinated chicken thoroughly under running water to remove any surface spice residue. Tuck the chicken legs into the cavity. Stuff the rehydrated shiitake mushrooms, longan, lotus seeds, red dates, and goji berries into the chicken cavity.  
**Note**: Be sure to rinse off the surface marinade completely, otherwise the saltiness will be excessive and the broth will turn cloudy.  

7. Place the chicken into the boiling braising liquid. Use a spoon to ladle the liquid over the chicken. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt or soy sauce if needed. Sprinkle in white pepper. Cover with a lid, reduce to medium-low heat, and cook for 20 minutes. Turn off the heat but do not uncover. Continue to let the chicken steep for 40 minutes.  
**Note**: Steeping is key. It allows the chicken to absorb the liquid at a gentle temperature while staying tender. The chicken is done when a chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part.  

8. Line the bottom of the smoking pot with aluminum foil. Evenly scatter rice, small pine branches, sugar, and jasmine tea over the foil. Cover with a lid and heat over high heat until smoke appears around the edge of the pot.  

9. Quickly place the drained chicken on a steaming rack and set it into the smoking pot. Cover with the lid. Smoke over high heat for 2 minutes, then reduce to low heat and smoke for 1 minute. Turn off the heat and keep the lid closed. Let it rest for 2 minutes.  
**Note**: Do not smoke for too long. The resting time after turning off the heat allows the smoky flavor to settle gently onto the chicken.  

10. Open the lid and remove the chicken. While still hot, brush a layer of sesame oil over the skin.  
**Note**: Sesame oil is a must. It enhances aroma, adds shine, and locks in moisture.  

## Health Advice
This dish is prepared with spiced salt marination, slow braising in bone broth, and smoking with tea leaves and pine branches. It is nutritious but high in sodium. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables, and it is suitable for those needing nourishment but without high blood pressure.
