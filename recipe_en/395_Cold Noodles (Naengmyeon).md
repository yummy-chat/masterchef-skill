# Cold Noodles (Naengmyeon)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty Level: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Cold noodle cakes – about 2 packs (serves a family of three)
🥒 Cucumber – to taste (julienned)
🥚 Egg – about 1 (hard-boiled in advance)
🍖 Ham/grilled sausage – to taste (sliced)
🌿 Cilantro – to taste (cut into segments)
🧴 Concentrated cold noodle soup packet – 1 packet (each packet mixed with 200 ml water; for 2 noodle cakes, prepare 2 batches of soup)
🌶️ Homemade chili oil – to taste
🧂 Cold noodle seasoning packet – 1 packet (included in the package)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Pot
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🚰 Faucet (for rinsing and rubbing the cold noodles)
🥄 Ladle
🍜 Bowls

## Instructions
1. Take the cold noodle cakes, briefly dip them under running water from the faucet, then gently rub to separate the noodles and prevent sticking.
**Note**: Dipping in water is essential to easily loosen the noodle cake.

2. In advance, julienne the cucumber, cut the cilantro into segments, slice the ham or grilled sausage, and hard-boil the egg. Set aside.

3. Place the separated cold noodles into a pot and add cold water (starting with cold water). Bring to a boil over high heat, then cook for 2–3 minutes, timing from when the water boils.
**Key point**: Starting with cold water prevents sticking; start timing once the water boils to ensure a chewy texture.

4. Remove the concentrated soup packet, shake it vigorously before use, and according to the fill line on the packet, mix with 200 ml water in the bowl. Stir well. For a lighter taste, add a bit more water as desired.
5. Immediately remove the cooked cold noodles and rinse them vigorously under the faucet, rubbing to remove the starchy surface layer. Drain well and transfer to a bowl.
**Key point**: Thoroughly rinse off the starchy layer and drain well; this makes the cold noodles refreshing and chewy.

6. Top the cold noodles with the prepared julienned cucumber, cilantro segments, ham slices, and egg. Sprinkle over the included cold noodle seasoning packet, then drizzle with homemade chili oil.
7. Pour the prepared cold noodle soup into the bowl, stir well, and serve. On hot days, replace room temperature water with chilled bottled mineral water for the soup base for a more refreshing taste.

## Health Advice
This cold noodle dish is refreshing and chewy, perfect for cooling down and stimulating the appetite in summer. However, the cold noodle soup packet and seasoning packet are high in salt, so it's recommended to use less of the included seasoning and rely more on cucumber shreds and homemade chili oil for flavor. Rinsing and rubbing the noodles after cooking is key to removing the starchy layer and achieving a springy texture, but be gentle to avoid over-rubbing and compromising the texture.
