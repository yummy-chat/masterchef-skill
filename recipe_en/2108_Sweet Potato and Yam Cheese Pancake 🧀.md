# Sweet Potato and Yam Cheese Pancake 🧀
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Sweet potato: About 150g (total with yam is about 300g)
🥔 Yam: About 150g
🥡 Glutinous rice flour: As needed (ratio to potato mash is about 1:3, pre-roasted)
🍯 Red bean paste filling: About 30g each
🧀 Mozzarella cheese: As needed
🍞 Breadcrumbs: As needed
🛢️ Cooking oil: As needed (for deep frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥘 Steamer
🍳 Non-stick pan
🧺 Small steaming rack (for assisting frying)
🥣 Strainer/skimmer
🍲 Deep fryer/deep pot
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Peeler
🥔 Masher/fork
🥣 Mixing bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the whole sweet potato and the yam; steam the sweet potato whole to reduce excess moisture.
2. Place the prepared sweet potato and yam in a steamer, steam for about 30 minutes after steam rises, until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
3. Heat a non-stick pan over low heat, add glutinous rice flour and stir-fry slowly until slightly yellow and fragrant, then set aside.
4. Divide the red bean paste into small portions. Wrap each portion with a layer of mozzarella cheese shreds to form cheese and red bean paste balls.
5. Mash the steamed sweet potato and yam into a fine paste while hot, and mix evenly.
6. Add the roasted glutinous rice flour to the potato mash and knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
   **Note**: The ratio of sweet potato and yam mash to glutinous rice flour is about 3:1. Too much flour will affect the soft and chewy texture.
7. Take an appropriate amount of dough, flatten it, wrap in one cheese and red bean paste ball, seal the opening, roll into a ball, and gently press into a small pancake shape.
8. Coat the pancake evenly with breadcrumbs on the surface.
9. Pour oil into a pot and heat to 170°C (340°F). Place the pancake in a small steaming rack, then immerse the rack into the oil.
   **Key**: Using a small steaming rack prevents the pancake from sticking to the pot and ensures even heating.
10. Deep-fry over medium heat until the pancake forms a golden, crispy crust. Remove and place on a wire rack to drain excess oil.
    **Doneness Check**: The crust is golden and crispy, while the filling is soft and stretchy.

## Health Tips
Sweet potato and yam provide dietary fiber and slow-release carbohydrates, suitable for those needing stable blood sugar levels. Cheese and red bean paste contribute calcium and iron, but deep frying increases calories, so enjoy in moderation. Best eaten hot, but be careful of burns.
