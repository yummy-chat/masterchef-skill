# Braised Pork for Chinese Meat in Bun (Rou Jia Mo)  
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Recommended Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients  
🐖 Front leg pork, appropriate amount (fat-to-lean ratio 40% fat, 60% lean)  
💧 Water, about 20 jin (for making rich stock)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount  

## Seasonings  
🧂 Salt, 260g  
🍬 White sugar, 300g (for making caramel color)  
🍬 Rock sugar, 15g  
🍶 High-proof liquor, 50g (grain-based)  
🌿 Spice pouch (cumin 8g, cardamom 2 pieces, star anise 5g, cloves 2 pieces, cinnamon 5g, Sichuan pepper 6g, bay leaf 1g, angelica dahurica 2g, black cardamom 5g, nutmeg 6g, amomum villosum 1g, round cardamom 2g, thyme 2g, white pepper 8g)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Large stock pot  
🥢 Bottom rack (to prevent sticking at bottom)  
🪜 Pressing rack (to prevent ingredients from floating)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥄 Oil ladle  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the front leg pork into pieces three fingers wide, soak in clean water for 3 hours to thoroughly remove blood.  
2. Weigh all spices according to the proportion, crack hard spices open, place in a cloth bag, add scallion sections and ginger slices, tie the bag tightly, then soak in clean water to remove dust and reduce pungency.  
3. Grease the wok with a small amount of oil, add white sugar, stir-fry continuously over heat until melted; when the syrup quickly forms dense small bubbles and turns jujube red, pour in boiling water along the edge of the wok to make caramel color.  
**Key**: Adding water too early makes the caramel too light; adding too late makes it too dark. Jujube red is best.  
4. Bring the rich stock to a boil, add the cut fatty pork pieces, and cook until obvious oil slicks form on the surface.  
5. Place a bottom rack in the pot, pour in the caramel color and stir well, add salt, rock sugar, and liquor, stir until completely dissolved; add the soaked spice pouch, then add the soaked lean pork pieces, finally press down with a pressing rack to ensure all ingredients are fully submerged in the liquid.  
6. Bring to a vigorous boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to medium heat, maintaining a "bubbling" boil for about 2 hours, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the meat.  
**Note**: Do not boil over high heat for too long, otherwise the meat will lose moisture and become dry and tough.  
7. Turn off the heat, let the meat continue soaking in the liquid to fully absorb the flavor, then remove.  
**Key**: Soaking is an essential step to achieve the "fatty but not greasy, lean but not dry" texture.  

## Health Advice  
This braised pork is rich in protein and iron, suitable for people with high physical exertion or those needing an energy boost. However, salt and sugar content is high, so those with high blood pressure or on sugar control should consume in moderation. It is recommended to serve with green peppers or lettuce to cut the grease and increase dietary fiber intake.
