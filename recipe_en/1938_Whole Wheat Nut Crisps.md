# Whole Wheat Nut Crisps
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients
🧡 Pistachios 70g (shelled)
🌰 Almonds 60g (chopped)
🥜 Pine nuts 35g
🍈 Dried figs 80g (chopped)
🍑 Dried apricots 40g (chopped)
🍒 Dried cranberries 30g
🌾 Whole wheat flour 90g
🍞 Cake flour 100g
🥛 Milk 255g
🧂 Salt 1.3g
🧂 Baking soda 4.5g
🍋 Lemon juice 15g
🍯 Honey 35g

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Pot (for heating milk)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Spatula or scraper
📦 Baking mold
♨️ Oven (with baking rack)
🧊 Plastic wrap, glass airtight container (for freezing and storage)

## Instructions
1. **Prepare nuts and dried fruit**: Shell the pistachios, chop the almonds, and chop the dried figs and apricots. The size of the pieces will affect the final texture. The ratio of nuts to dried fruit can be adjusted to taste.
2. **Make clabbered milk**: Pour the milk into a pot and heat gently, being careful not to let it boil. Remove from heat, add the lemon juice, and stir to combine. Let it sit for 10 minutes until the milk curdles and forms a yogurt-like consistency.
3. **Mix dry ingredients**: Place the whole wheat flour, cake flour, salt, and baking soda in a large bowl. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
4. **Mix wet ingredients**: Add the honey to the clabbered milk and stir until combined. Pour this mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients.
5. **Form the dough**: Use a spatula to stir the wet and dry ingredients together until no dry flour remains. Add all the nuts and dried fruit, folding gently until the fruit and nuts are evenly coated. Be careful not to overmix.
6. **Transfer to mold and first bake**: Pour the batter into a mold lined with parchment paper. Smooth the surface with a spatula. Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F) and bake the mold for 35 minutes.
7. **Cool and freeze**: Remove the baked dough from the mold immediately and let it cool on a wire rack to room temperature. Wrap the entire dough tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for about 3 hours, until completely firm, which makes slicing into thin pieces easier.
8. **Slice and second bake**: Remove the frozen dough and slice it as thinly as possible. Arrange the slices flat on a baking rack and bake at 150°C (300°F) for a total of 25 minutes, flipping the slices once after about 12-15 minutes to ensure even toasting. Bake until the moisture has evaporated and the color deepens. Let cool completely for a naturally crispy, crunchy texture.
   **Key**: The thickness of the slices directly affects the crispness; flipping prevents burning; the crisps must cool completely to achieve a hard, brittle texture.

## Health Advice
These whole wheat nut crisps are rich in dietary fiber and high-quality nut oils, making them a great energy-boosting snack. However, the honey and dried fruit do contain a fair amount of sugar, so it is recommended to enjoy 3-4 pieces per serving.
