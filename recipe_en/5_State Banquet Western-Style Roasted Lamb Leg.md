# State Banquet Western-Style Roasted Lamb Leg  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1 lamb foreleg (boned and trimmed)  
🥬 Celery, to taste (cut into large chunks)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (cut into large chunks)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste (sliced)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ Ground black pepper, to taste  
🍷 Red wine, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🧊 Refrigerator  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Bone the lamb foreleg and remove the whole trimmed meat.  
2. Cut celery into large segments, onion into large chunks, and carrot into slices. Set aside as marinating vegetables.  
3. Place the deboned lamb leg meat into a large glass bowl, and evenly sprinkle with salt and ground black pepper.  
4. Pour in an appropriate amount of red wine, and add all the prepared vegetables.  
5. Use both hands to thoroughly massage and mix, allowing the seasonings and vegetable flavors to penetrate the lamb.  
6. Seal the bowl tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate at a constant cold temperature for marinating.  
   **Note**: Subsequent roasting steps should be performed according to actual equipment; an oven or alternative appliance can be used.  
7. After marinating, remove and roast in the oven until the exterior is charred red and the interior is tender pink and juicy. Specific temperature and time depend on the equipment and meat thickness.  

## Health Suggestions  
Lamb is rich in iron and high-quality protein, and consuming it in winter can warm the body and boost energy. However, pairing it with plenty of vegetables is recommended to balance the richness. Marinate for at least 4 hours; red wine and vegetables effectively remove gamey odor and enhance aroma.
