# Homestyle Cold Noodles (Liangpi)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: ☀️ Summer (April-August)  

## 🥬 Ingredients  
- Cold noodles (Liangpi)  
- Gluten (Mianjin)  
- 🥒 Cucumber  
- 🧄 Garlic  
- 🌱 Mung bean sprouts  

## 🧂 Seasonings  
- 🧂 Salt  
- 🧅 Chicken bouillon  
- 🦪 Oyster sauce  
- 🟤 Light soy sauce  
- 🟫 Dark soy sauce  
- 🍾 Sesame oil  
- 🍶 Aromatic vinegar  
- 🥜 Sesame paste  

## 🍳 Utensils  
- 🥣 Basin  
- 🍚 Bowl  
- 🍽️ Plate  
- 🧄 Garlic mortar  

## 📝 Steps  

### Preparations  
1. Pour 500ml of water into a pot and bring to a boil over high heat.  
2. Add the mung bean sprouts and boil for 60 seconds over high heat. Remove and rinse with cold water, drain, and set aside on a plate.  
3. Cut the cucumber into thin strips and arrange them on a plate for later use.  
4. Peel 10g of garlic cloves, place them in a garlic mortar, add 1g of salt, and pound into a paste. Then add 10g of water, mix well, and set aside.  
5. Store-bought cold noodles and gluten may have cooking oil on the surface; rinse them clean with water.  
6. Gently squeeze the washed gluten to remove excess water (do not squeeze too hard).  

### Salty Sauce Preparation  
1. In a small bowl, combine 3g of salt, 2g of chicken bouillon, 5g of light soy sauce, 1g of dark soy sauce, 1g of sesame oil, 2g of oyster sauce, and 5g of aromatic vinegar.  
2. Pour in 25-35g of warm water, stir with chopsticks until the salt and chicken bouillon are fully dissolved, and let cool.  

### Sesame Paste Preparation  
1. Place the sesame paste in a small bowl.  
2. Add 4g of salt, 3g of chicken bouillon, 5g of light soy sauce, 1g of dark soy sauce, and 3g of oyster sauce.  
3. Stir the seasonings and sesame paste thoroughly with chopsticks.  
4. In 2-3 batches, add a total of about 10g of water, stirring until smooth after each addition, until reaching the desired consistency.  

### Final Steps  
1. Place the cold noodles in a basin.  
2. Pour in the prepared salty sauce, mix well with chopsticks, and transfer to a small bowl, adding any remaining sauce from the bottom of the basin.  
3. Layer the mung bean sprouts and gluten on top of the cold noodles.  
4. Evenly drizzle the prepared sesame paste over the gluten.  
5. Sprinkle the cucumber strips on top.  
6. Optionally, drizzle with chili oil according to personal preference, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This homestyle cold noodle dish is low in fat and refreshing, making it ideal for summer cooling, but the sesame paste and seasonings are high in salt, so portion control is recommended. The gluten and cold noodles provide a small amount of carbohydrates, while the cucumber and bean sprouts supply vitamins. It can be eaten as a meal during weight loss periods.
