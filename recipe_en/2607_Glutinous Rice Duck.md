# Glutinous Rice Duck
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 1 whole salted duck  
🍚 Glutinous rice, as needed  
🟢 Green peas, as needed  
🥚 1 egg  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper, as needed  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil, as needed  
🌾 Glutinous rice flour, as needed  
🍚 Cornstarch, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥘 Stainless steel tray  
🍳 Large wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
♨️ Steamer  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Rice paddle  

## Steps  
1. Soak the glutinous rice in warm water for 5 to 6 hours.  
2. Prepare the salted duck:  
   - Soak the whole salted duck in warm water for 2 hours to remove excess saltiness.  
   - After cleaning, remove the duck head and bones, keeping the duck skin intact.  
   - Slice off thicker meat sections from the duck body to achieve even thickness; finely chop the trimmed meat and set aside.  
3. Evenly sprinkle a layer of cornstarch on the bottom of a stainless steel tray.  
4. Separate the egg white and mix with an appropriate amount of cornstarch until thick and sticky to make an egg white batter.  
5. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the soaked glutinous rice, and cook for about 2 to 3 minutes until the grains can be easily crushed with fingers and have no white core. Drain well.  
6. Add the chopped duck meat and green peas to the cooked glutinous rice. Season with salt, ground Sichuan pepper, and Sichuan pepper oil. Then add an appropriate amount of glutinous rice flour and mix well.  
   **Note**: Glutinous rice flour helps increase stickiness and chewiness.  
7. Assemble the duck base:  
   - Lay the whole duck skin flat on the cornstarch-sprinkled tray.  
   - Evenly spread the egg white batter over the meat side of the skin.  
   - Lightly sprinkle a thin layer of glutinous rice flour over the egg white batter to enhance crispiness after frying.  
   - Evenly spread the seasoned glutinous rice mixture over the duck meat, smoothing it flat with a rice paddle.  
   **Note**: Do not spread the glutinous rice mixture too thickly.  
8. Place in a steamer and steam for about 40 minutes until the glutinous rice is fully cooked and set. After steaming, pour off excess oil from the tray, press the glutinous rice firmly with a rice paddle, and let cool.  
9. Pour a generous amount of oil into a wok and heat until sufficiently hot. Carefully slide the cooled and set duck base into the oil. Fry over medium heat until the duck skin turns golden brown and crispy, then remove and drain excess oil.  
10. Let the fried duck base cool slightly, cut into neat squares, and plate.  

## Health Advice  
Glutinous rice duck is high in carbohydrates and fat, suitable for occasional consumption rather than as a daily meal. Glutinous rice is difficult to digest, so those with weak digestion should eat in small amounts. The fried duck skin is crispy but high in calories; pairing it with green peas adds dietary fiber.
