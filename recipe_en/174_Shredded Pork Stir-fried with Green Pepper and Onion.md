# Shredded Pork Stir-fried with Green Pepper and Onion

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the pork front leg lean meat clean and shred it; slice the pork fat thinly. Add salt and a small amount of dark soy sauce to the shredded lean meat and mix evenly.
   **Note**: Do not let the meat come into contact with water after cutting.
2. Place the crushed ginger and scallion in a small amount of clean water and squeeze firmly to extract the juice, making scallion-ginger water. Add this water to the shredded meat in batches, stirring quickly in one direction until the meat becomes sticky and firm.
   **Key**: Stir until it feels very sticky; refer to a ratio of "one tael of water per catty of meat."
3. Add a small amount of cornstarch slurry and mix again to seal in the moisture.
4. Seed the green pepper and shred it; cut the onion into thick shreds and then separate them; cut the dried tofu into strips; mince the garlic.
5. Heat the wok, add a small amount of rapeseed oil, then add the sliced pork fat and one slice of ginger. Stir-fry over medium heat until the fat turns transparent, curls, and releases oil.
6. Add the marinated shredded meat, turn to high heat, and quickly stir-fry until all the meat changes color and is just cooked. Immediately remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Do not stir-fry the meat for too long to prevent it from becoming tough.
7. Pour the remaining oil back into the wok and heat it. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry over high heat until fragrant. When the oil temperature is high, add the shredded green pepper, onion shreds, and dried tofu strips. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the vegetables start to wilt slightly.
   **Key**: Ensure the oil temperature is high before stir-frying the vegetables to prevent them from releasing water.
8. Return the cooked meat to the wok, add salt, sugar, MSG, and light soy sauce, and continue stir-frying over high heat until everything is evenly mixed.
9. Before removing from heat, drizzle aged vinegar along the edge of the wok. After the vinegar aroma is released, quickly stir-fry evenly, then immediately turn off the heat and plate the dish.
   **Key**: Use the high heat along the edge of the wok to evaporate the vinegar, leaving only the aroma without any sour taste.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced mix of meat and vegetables. Onions and green peppers are rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, while pork front leg lean meat provides high-quality protein. The pork fat is only used for rendering oil, so control the amount. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet to manage total calorie intake. It is recommended to pair it with mixed grain rice to balance carbohydrates.
