# Shrimp with Scrambled Eggs
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 12 South American white shrimp (butterflied, deveined, and squeezed dry)
🥚 8 eggs (free-range or young hen eggs preferred)
🥬 Some chive shoots (cut into sections)
🧂 A pinch of salt (used in two batches)
🍬 A pinch of sugar (used in two batches)
🍶 A few drops of cooking wine
🌽 A pinch of cornstarch
🫒 Some oil (for marinating, oil-sealing, flash-frying, and stir-frying)
🍶 A few drops of white vinegar

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Large wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🥅 Fine-mesh strainer
🥣 Bowl

## Instructions
1. Butterfly the shrimp, remove the vein, and squeeze dry. Add a pinch of salt, sugar, and a few drops of cooking wine. Stir by hand until the shrimp become sticky and tacky. Add a pinch of cornstarch and mix well. Finally, seal the surface with a little oil. Refrigerate and marinate for 20 minutes.
2. Cut the chive shoots into sections. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them. Add salt, sugar, and a few drops of white vinegar, and stir well. Strain the egg mixture through a fine-mesh strainer and set aside.
3. Heat the wok and add oil. When the oil temperature reaches about 50% hot, add the shrimp and quickly flash-fry until they change color and curl, about 50% cooked. Remove and drain the oil.
4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the chive shoot sections and quickly stir-fry for about 5 seconds until just cooked. Remove from heat, pour into the egg mixture, and add the flash-fried shrimp. Mix everything together evenly.
5. Using residual heat or medium-low heat, pour the mixed egg mixture into the wok. Drizzle a little oil along the edge of the wok. Gently push the egg mixture from the edge with a spatula, allowing the uncooked liquid to flow to the bottom. Stir-fry until the egg mixture is about 80% cooked. Do not cook the shrimp and eggs fully.
6. Transfer to a plate. The residual heat will bring the dish to about 90% doneness, resulting in a tender and smooth texture.

## Health Advice
Shrimp is low in fat and high in protein, paired with eggs and chive shoots, offering a perfect combination of high-quality protein and dietary fiber. It is especially suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those managing calorie intake. Be mindful to use less salt when marinating the shrimp to avoid excessive sodium intake. Cook the eggs over medium-low heat to maintain a tender and smooth texture.
