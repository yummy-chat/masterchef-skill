# Millet Crackers
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 1 bowl of millet (pre-steamed and cooled)
🌾 1 bowl of flour (equal to the millet)
🥚 2 eggs
💧 Water (as needed)
🧄 3 cloves of garlic
🧂 10 grams of salt
🧂 A pinch of MSG
🌿 A small amount of Sichuan peppercorn powder
🌶️ BBQ seasoning (to taste, sprinkled after cooking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Steamer
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Stainless steel slotted spoon
🥖 Rolling pin
🍴 Fork
🔪 Chef's knife
📋 Silicone cutting board/mat
🍳 Deep fryer or wok
🥄 Strainer

## Instructions
1. Evenly spread the millet into a steamer basket lined with a damp cloth. Once the steamer is producing steam, steam for 20 minutes. Remove and let cool.
   **Key Point**: Spreading evenly ensures even heating; steaming preserves a grainy texture and reduces nutrient loss.
2. Pour the cooled millet into a bowl. Add an equal amount of flour, 10 grams of salt, a pinch of MSG, a small amount of Sichuan peppercorn powder, and crack in 2 eggs. Mix until well combined.
   **Key Point**: First mix the dry ingredients and eggs with the millet to ensure even seasoning.
3. Gradually add water in portions, kneading into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
   **Key Point**: Add water slowly; the dough should not stick to your hands.
4. Cover the dough and let it rest for 5 minutes. After resting, do not knead. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a thin sheet about the thickness of a coin.
   **Key Point**: The thinner the sheet, the crispier the final result.
5. Use a fork to poke small holes evenly across the dough sheet. Trim off the irregular edges, then cut into even small squares. Toss them to prevent sticking.
   **Key Point**: Poking holes ensures even heating during frying and prevents puffing.
6. Heat oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Fry dough pieces in batches, adding 3 cloves of garlic. Keep the heat low and fry slowly for 4-5 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Fry in small batches to prevent sticking; remove when the crackers turn golden and translucent and the garlic cloves become golden. The garlic serves as an indicator of doneness.
7. Remove the fried crackers with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. While still hot, sprinkle with BBQ seasoning and toss to coat. Serve.
   **Key Point**: Season while hot for better flavor absorption.

## Health Tips
Millet is rich in B vitamins, which aid digestion. Poking holes before frying helps control oil absorption. Suitable as a snack, but be mindful of portion size. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation. Pair with green tea to reduce greasiness.
