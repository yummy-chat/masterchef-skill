# Waterless Tomato Braised Beef
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef brisket 800g (cut into large chunks)  
🍅 Tomatoes 8 (finely chopped)  
🧅 Onion 1 (finely chopped)  
🥔 Potato 1 (cut into chunks)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶️ Black pepper to taste  
🍝 Tomato pasta sauce 4 tbsp  
🥄 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for searing)  
🌿 Chopped scallions or herbs as needed (for garnish)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Cast iron pot  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪚 Cutting board  
🥢 Food tongs  
🥄 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the beef brisket into large chunks, finely chop the tomatoes and onion, and cut the potato into chunks. Set aside.  
2. Heat oil in the pot over high heat. Add the beef chunks and sear until browned on all sides, then remove.  
3. Using the remaining oil in the pot, add the chopped onion and stir-fry. Use the onion to scrape off any browned bits stuck to the bottom of the pot.  
4. Add the chopped tomatoes. Turn to medium-high heat and season with salt, black pepper, 4 tbsp tomato pasta sauce, and 2 tbsp light soy sauce. Cook until the tomatoes release their juices fully, creating a rich broth.  
5. Return the seared beef to the pot. Toss well with the tomato broth. Cover the pot and reduce to low heat. Braise for 2 hours.  
   **Key point**: Stir the pot occasionally during braising to prevent sticking.  
6. During the last 30 minutes of braising, add the potato chunks. Cover and continue braising until tender.  
7. Before serving, sprinkle with chopped scallions or herbs for garnish.  

## Health Tips  
Beef brisket is rich in high-quality protein and iron. The lycopene and vitamin C in tomatoes become more absorbable after braising, making this dish suitable for autumn/winter nourishment or recovery periods. It is recommended not to add the potato chunks too early to prevent them from breaking down into mush. Stir frequently during braising to avoid sticking.
