# Rose Lychee Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍈 Fresh lychees, about 10-15 (peeled, pitted, and white membrane removed)
🥚 Eggs, 4 (for the cake sponge)
🥛 Milk, 40g
🌽 Corn oil, 35g
🌾 Cake flour, 60g
🍚 Sugar, 40-50g
🌽 Cornstarch, 10g
🍮 Gelatin sheets, 10g (2 sheets, pre-soaked in ice water until soft)
🥛 Heavy cream, about 500g (used in two parts: 300g for filling, 200g for frosting)
🌹 Rose jam, about 60g (30g for lychee juice, 30g for filling cream)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍹 Blender/food processor
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🔍 Strainer/sieve
🥄 Hand whisk
🥣 Mixing bowl
🌀 Turntable
🔪 Spatula
🎨 Piping bag and medium straight nozzle

## Instructions
1. **Make Lychee Rose Juice**: Place the lychee flesh, about 30g rose jam, and a small amount of water in a blender and process until smooth. Strain to obtain about 230g of rose lychee juice. Take a small amount of the juice and mix it with the softened and squeezed-dry gelatin sheets. Heat over a water bath until fully dissolved, then pour back into the remaining juice and stir well. Transfer to a container and refrigerate until fully set.
   **Key Point**: The gelatin sheets must first be softened in ice water; avoid overheating when using a water bath. The mixture should set until it is no longer sticky to the touch.

2. **Whip the Filling Cream**: Place 300g heavy cream, about 30g rose jam, and an appropriate amount of sugar into a pre-chilled mixing bowl. Set the bowl over an ice water bath and whip with a hand whisk until clear, firm ridges form and the whisk holds a stiff peak when lifted.
   **Key Point**: In summer, keep all tools at a low temperature and work in an air-conditioned environment. Do not whip more than 300g at a time. The filling cream must be whipped until stiff.

3. **Assemble the Layers**: Take one layer of cake sponge, evenly pipe the filling cream onto it, place the lychee jelly cut into cubes on top, and fill any gaps with additional piped cream. Cover with the second cake layer and press down gently to secure.

4. **Simple Crumb Coat and Set**: Use the remaining filling cream to lightly coat the sides and top of the cake, sealing in any crumbs. Place the cake in the freezer for about 10 minutes.

5. **Final Frosting**: Rewhip about 200g of frosting cream, pipe it onto the surface of the cake, then lightly press the spatula against the side while spinning the turntable to smooth it out.
   **Key Point**: Work in smooth, continuous motions; avoid repeatedly scraping the same area.

6. **Piping Decoration**: Using a medium straight nozzle, start from the center to pipe the heart of a rose, then pipe petals outward in concentric circles, gradually opening the petals until the entire surface of the cake is covered.
   **Key Point**: The further outward you go, the more the petals should open. The design can be adjusted flexibly.

7. **Finishing Touches**: Sprinkle dried rose petals over the surface of the piped cream roses.

## Health Suggestions
The combination of lychee and rose gives this cake a unique flavor, but lychees are high in sugar. When paired with heavy cream and sugar, be mindful of the total calorie content. It is suitable as a shared dessert for small summer gatherings. Lychees are rich in vitamin C, but should not be consumed in excess at one time. Those with blood sugar sensitivity should enjoy it in moderation.
