# Homemade Pickled Sweet Garlic
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 New garlic 3 jin (must use fresh seasonal garlic)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for cleaning and disinfecting)
🍬 Fine white sugar 500 grams (if using granulated sugar, 600 grams needed)
🍶 White vinegar 1200 milliliters
🥃 High-proof liquor approximately 1 small cup (added before sealing)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🥣 Large glass bowl
🧺 Draining basket
🍲 Glass cooking pot
🫙 Glass airtight jar
🥃 Measuring cup/small cup

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process Garlic**: Use scissors to cut off the garlic stems, leaving about 1-1.5 centimeters on the garlic head. Then peel off the outermost old skin.
2. **Wash and Drain**: Place the garlic heads in a large bowl, add a generous amount of salt and water, stir to wash, and soak for 3-5 minutes. Then place the garlic heads upside down in a draining basket, with the pointed end up, and let them thoroughly air dry in a cool, ventilated place.
   **Key Point**: The water must be completely drained, otherwise the pickled garlic will spoil.
3. **Cook Sugar Syrup**: Add 500 milliliters of water and 500 grams of fine white sugar to a pot. Heat and stir until boiling, and the sugar has completely dissolved and the syrup is clear.
   **Key Point**: Boiling sterilizes and ensures the sugar is fully dissolved.
4. **Prepare Vinegar Mixture**: Turn off the heat. When the sugar water stops boiling vigorously, pour in 1200 milliliters of white vinegar and stir well.
   **Key Point**: The vinegar does not need to be boiled to preserve its sourness and prevent excessive evaporation.
5. **Cool Down**: Allow the sugar-vinegar mixture to cool completely to room temperature.
   **Key Point**: It must be completely cooled, otherwise it will cook the garlic heads and they will lose their crisp texture.
6. **Jar and Seal**: Ensure the garlic heads are thoroughly dried, and the airtight jar has been scalded with boiling water and dried. Place the garlic heads into the jar, pour the cooled sugar-vinegar mixture over them until submerged. Finally, drizzle in a small cup of high-proof liquor, and seal tightly.
   **Key Point**: The cleanliness and dryness of the jar are crucial. The liquor prevents spoilage and accelerates flavor absorption.
7. **Rest and Pickle**: Place the sealed jar in a cool, dark place at room temperature. Do not refrigerate. It will be ready to eat after about one month.

## Health Advice
Pickled sweet garlic is a fermented food rich in prebiotics, which benefits gut health. However, it is high in sugar (each clove contains about 5 grams of sugar), so diabetics should limit their intake. It is recommended to eat it with greasy dishes to cut the fat, and consume 2-3 cloves per serving.
