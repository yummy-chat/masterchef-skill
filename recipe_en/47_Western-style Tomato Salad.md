# Western-style Tomato Salad
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 3 tomatoes (thin-skinned, firm, and mealy)
🥒 1 cucumber (for added color and texture)
🧅 Half an onion (sliced)
🥚 1 egg (use the cooked yolk)
🧂 1 tablespoon salt
🍬 1 tablespoon sugar
🌶️ 2 tablespoons white pepper
🍶 3 tablespoons white vinegar (must be white vinegar)
🌿 1 tablespoon mustard oil (added last)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Pot (for boiling water to blanch tomatoes)
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board (for chopping vegetables)
🥣 Small bowl, spoon (for making dressing)
🍽️ Large flat plate (for presentation)

## Preparation Steps
1. Select tomatoes that are thin-skinned, firm, and mealy. Score a cross on the top, blanch in boiling water for about 5 seconds until the skin curls, then promptly remove and peel. Cut into thick slices.
2. Use a peeler to peel the cucumber in a "striped" pattern, leaving some green skin intact. Slice diagonally into large pieces.
3. Slice the onion into pieces matching the length of the tomato and cucumber slices.
4. Boil the egg for about 10 minutes until fully cooked. Remove the cooked yolk and crush it into a fine powder using the flat side of a knife.
5. Alternately arrange the tomato slices, cucumber slices, and onion slices neatly on a plate, creating a layered pattern of red, green, and white.
6. Prepare the dressing: In a bowl, combine salt, sugar, white pepper, and white vinegar; stir until well mixed. Add the crushed egg yolk and blend. Finally, add 1 tablespoon of mustard oil and stir until combined.
7. Evenly drizzle the dressing over the arranged vegetables.
   **Note**: This dish is tangy and appetizing, with mustard oil highlighting the Western flavor. In traditional preparation, mustard can be substituted.

## Health Tips
This cold dish is rich in lycopene and vitamins, making it ideal for summer appetizers or during a weight-loss diet. Mustard oil and white vinegar are somewhat pungent; those with sensitive stomachs should use them in moderation.
