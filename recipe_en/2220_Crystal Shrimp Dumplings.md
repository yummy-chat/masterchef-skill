# Crystal Shrimp Dumplings
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Large shrimp 2 lbs
🫚 Ginger, to taste
🪷 Water chestnuts, to taste
🌾 Wheat starch 180 g
🥔 Potato starch 60 g
🧂 Salt 1 tsp
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste
🥄 Starch 2 tbsp
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🐷 Lard or vegetable oil, a little
💧 Boiling water 250 ml

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl, white ceramic bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves
🥢 Rolling pin
🍳 Steamer basket, pot

## Preparation Steps
1. Divide the shrimp into two portions. Finely mince one portion into a smooth paste, and cut the other into large chunks. Combine both in a bowl.
2. Add minced ginger, diced water chestnuts, salt, white sugar, and white pepper powder to taste. Then add two tablespoons of starch. Mix well with your hands.
3. Continuously stir the shrimp filling in one direction until it becomes sticky. You can repeatedly slap the mixture a few times to make the texture more bouncy. Add a little cooking oil and mix well. Refrigerate for 15 minutes to make it easier to wrap later.
4. Mix wheat starch, potato starch, and one teaspoon of salt. Slowly pour in 250 ml of freshly boiled water while stirring with chopsticks until the mixture forms flocculent pieces. Add a little lard or vegetable oil and knead into a smooth, pliable dough.
5. Roll the dough into a long strip and cut into evenly sized pieces. Dust with cornstarch to prevent sticking, and cover unused pieces with plastic wrap.
6. Take one dough piece, flatten it, and roll it out into a thin dumpling wrapper using a rolling pin.
7. Remove the chilled shrimp filling. Place an appropriate amount in the center of the wrapper. Gently fold in half, pinch a frilled edge, flatten the sealed end, trim off excess dough, and lightly shape.
8. Place the finished dumplings in a steamer basket lined with a breathable mat. Steam over high heat for 5 minutes after the water comes to a boil.

## Health Tips
These crystal shrimp dumplings are rich in protein and suitable for muscle building or weight loss. However, the wheat starch has a high glycemic index, so diabetic patients are advised to eat them with vegetables. The shrimp filling becomes bouncy after slapping. Be careful not to over-knead the dough to avoid cracking. Beginners can prepare extra wrappers for practice.
