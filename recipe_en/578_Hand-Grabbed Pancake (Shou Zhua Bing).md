# Hand-Grabbed Pancake (Shou Zhua Bing)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Ingredients:
- 🥟 All-purpose flour 200g
- 🥚 Egg 1
- 🥬 Lettuce 30g
- 🍖 Ham 30g
- 🧀 Cheese slice 1 slice

🧂 Seasonings:
- 🧂 Salt 3g
- 💧 Boiling water 100ml
- 💧 Cold water 50ml
- 🫒 Cooking oil 15ml

🍳 Kitchen tools:
- 🥣 Bowl
- 🧈 Rolling pin
- 🍳 Frying pan
- 🧽 Damp cloth

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the flour in a bowl, pour in boiling water and stir into flocculent pieces, then gradually add cold water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Using a mix of boiling and cold water improves the dough's flexibility; the resting time should not be less than 20 minutes, otherwise the dough will be difficult to roll thin.

2. Divide the rested dough into portions of about 100g each, roll each into a ball, then roll out into a thin sheet.

3. Evenly spread cooking oil on the surface of the thin sheet and sprinkle with salt. Roll it up from one end into a snail-like coil and let rest for 10 minutes.

4. Roll the rested dough coil out again into a round pancake of even thickness.

5. Heat the frying pan, add oil, and fry the pancake over low heat until both sides are golden brown and bubbly.

6. On the fried pancake, layer the fried egg, lettuce, ham, and cheese slice in order, then roll it up and serve.
   **Note**: It is recommended to keep the total filling weight under 100g, and the ingredients can be adjusted according to personal taste.

## Health Advice
Hand-grabbed pancakes are rich in carbohydrates and protein, making them a good choice for breakfast to replenish energy. However, ham and cheese are high in sodium, so pairing with lettuce can increase dietary fiber. Be mindful of the amount of oil used during frying to avoid excessive calories.
