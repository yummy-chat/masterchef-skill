# Tai Er Sour Pickled Cabbage Fish  
Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Seabass 1 piece (scaled and head removed, take two fillets, chop the bones into pieces)  
🥬 Sour pickled cabbage appropriate amount (washed and cut into small sections)  
🥕 Pickled radish appropriate amount (cut into thick slices)  
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌼 Chrysanthemum petals appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for oil splashing and garnish)  
🥚 Egg white half  
🧂 Salt a pinch (for marinating fish and seasoning)  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (for marinating fish and seasoning)  
🌶️ White pepper powder a pinch (to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)  
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (to marinate and remove fishy smell)  
🌽 Corn starch appropriate amount (for coating)  
🥜 Peanut oil a little (to seal and lock in moisture)  
🍶 White vinegar appropriate amount (to enhance sourness)  
🍶 Yellow wine appropriate amount (to remove fishy smell when cooking the soup)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount (for oil splashing)  
🫘 Sesame seeds appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🫚 Fresh Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch (for garnish)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Steps  
1. Scale the seabass and remove the head. Take the two fillets, remove the belly bones, and slice the fish diagonally into large pieces; chop the fish bones into large chunks.  
**Key point**: Slicing the fish diagonally makes the pieces larger and thinner.  
2. Place the fish slices in clean water and rinse under a slow stream of water for 10 minutes to remove blood; rinse the fish bones briefly.  
**Key point**: Rinsing makes the fish slices more tender and refreshing.  
3. Wash the sour pickled cabbage and cut into small sections, cut the pickled radish into thick slices, slice the ginger, and cut the dried chili peppers into sections.  
4. Pat the fish slices dry with kitchen paper, add a pinch of salt and mix well, then add half an egg white and vigorously mix until sticky. Then add a pinch of sugar, white pepper powder, cooking wine, and corn starch, mixing well. Finally, drizzle a little peanut oil to seal the surface.  
**Key point**: The fish slices must be thoroughly dried; salt and egg white are key to tenderness and smoothness.  
5. Leave a little oil in the wok, stir-fry the sour pickled cabbage over medium heat until the sour smell is pungent and the fragrance is released. Set aside.  
6. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to about 160°C. First fry the fish head, then fry the fish bones until dry, crispy, and fragrant. Remove and drain the oil.  
7. Leave a little oil in the wok, add the ginger slices, pour in enough boiling water, add the fried fish bones, and add an appropriate amount of yellow wine. Boil over high heat until the soup turns milky white and thick.  
**Key point**: Frying the fish bones is key to the soup’s aromatic and white color.  
8. Once the soup turns white, add the stir-fried sour pickled cabbage and pickled radish slices. Season with white vinegar, salt, sugar, and white pepper powder. Cook for about 1–2 minutes to let the flavors meld.  
9. Use a strainer to remove the fish bones and sour pickled cabbage and spread them at the bottom of a bowl. Remove the wok from heat, slide the marinated fish slices into the soup one by one. Then return the wok to low heat, bring the soup to a gentle boil for a few to a dozen seconds until the fish slices turn white and curl. Turn off the heat.  
**Key point**: Adding the fish slices off the heat prevents coating from falling off and the fish from breaking apart.  
10. Pour the fish slices and soup into the bowl. In another wok, heat rapeseed oil until smoking, turn off the heat, quickly add the dried chili pepper sections and Sichuan peppercorns, and immediately pour the hot oil over the fish slices.  
**Key point**: The oil temperature must be high enough to release the aroma.  
11. Sprinkle with white sesame seeds and fresh Sichuan peppercorns, and finally garnish with chrysanthemum petals before serving.  

## Health Tips  
Seabass is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. Pairing it with fermented sour pickled cabbage helps stimulate appetite and cut through grease, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss periods. However, the soup base and oil splashing contain relatively high fat, so it is advisable to consume less oily soup. Chrysanthemum petals add a fresh aroma, but those with sensitive stomachs should be cautious about the sour and spicy stimulation.
