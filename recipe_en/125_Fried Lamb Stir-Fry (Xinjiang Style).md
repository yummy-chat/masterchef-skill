# Fried Lamb Stir-Fry (Xinjiang Style)
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb hind leg meat, about 500g (cut into large slices)  
🥚 Egg white, 1  
🧅 Onion, as needed (for wrapping)  
🥬 Lettuce, as needed (for wrapping)  
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (for coating)  
🧂 Salt, a small amount  
🍬 Sugar, a small amount  
🌿 Cumin powder, as needed  
🍶 Huadiao wine, as needed  
🥢 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🌿 Cumin seeds, as needed  
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed  
🤍 Sesame seeds, a small amount  
🥃 Baijiu (white liquor), a small amount (added just before serving)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Chinese wok  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowls  

## Steps  
1. Cut the lamb hind leg meat into large slices, place in a bowl, and add a small amount of salt, sugar, cumin powder, Huadiao wine, and light soy sauce. Knead and toss by hand until the meat becomes sticky.  
**Note**: Knead until the sound becomes more viscous, ensuring the seasonings are fully absorbed.  

2. Add 1 egg white, mix well, then add a small amount of cornstarch. Mix thoroughly again to evenly coat the meat slices, locking in moisture.  

3. Heat the wok over high heat and add cold oil. When the oil temperature reaches about 50% hot, add the marinated lamb slices and cook over high heat.  
**Key Point**: Do not stir the meat slices immediately after adding them; let the bottom set first.  

4. Once the lamb has completely changed color, start stir-frying, maintaining high heat throughout. Cook until the meat slices darken in color and become glossy on the surface, and the sound of water in the wok gradually diminishes until it’s nearly inaudible.  
**Note**: Keep the heat high and stir frequently to prevent sticking, ensuring the moisture fully evaporates and the meat surface develops a charred aroma.  

5. Reduce heat to low, add cumin seeds, chili powder, and sesame seeds, and stir-fry quickly to combine.  

6. Drizzle a small amount of Baijiu along the edge of the wok. Use the high heat of the wok to instantly release the aroma, stir-fry quickly to mix well, then remove from heat and serve.  

7. Can be wrapped in lettuce and onion for eating, which enhances the flavor.  

## Health Advice  
Lamb is rich in high-quality protein, iron, and zinc, and pairing it with lettuce and onion helps reduce greasiness and adds fiber, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those needing iron supplementation. However, note the high fat and sodium content; those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should control their intake.
