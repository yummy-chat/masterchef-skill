# Stir-Fried Beef with Onion
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Lean beef 200g (sliced thinly against the grain)  
🧅 Purple onion about half (sliced thinly)  
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers to taste (sliced)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (part crushed for juice, part sliced)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (sliced)  
🧃 Ginger juice to taste (crush ginger with a little water and squeeze out juice)  
🧂 Salt about 1g (used in two separate portions)  
🍚 Baking soda about 3g (optional, add if beef does not become sticky when kneaded)  
🌽 Cornstarch to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil to taste  
🍬 Sugar to taste  
🧴 Light soy sauce to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine a dash  
🌰 Sesame oil/seasoning oil a dash  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. Slice the beef thinly against the grain, place in a large bowl. Add a little salt and ginger juice, knead with your hands until the meat slices become tacky. If it does not become sticky, add baking soda for assistance. Then add cornstarch and mix well to coat. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil to seal in the juices.
   **Key point**: Slicing against the grain is the foundation of tenderness; coating and sealing with oil firmly locks in the meat juices.
2. Cut the onion in half, peel apart the layers and slice thinly. Cut the green and red chili peppers into small slices. Slice the ginger and garlic respectively.
3. Heat the iron wok, pour in enough oil, heat until the oil surface is smoking, then reduce to medium heat. Add the beef slices, quickly separate them with chopsticks, stir-fry until all are变色 (change color), then remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Adding beef to high-temperature oil quickly seals in juices; using chopsticks prevents sticking.
4. Leave some base oil in the wok, over low heat, sauté the ginger slices and garlic slices until fragrant. Then turn to high heat, add the onion slices and green/red chili slices, stir-fry until the onion softens slightly. Reduce to low heat, add salt, sugar, and light soy sauce to season, and stir quickly to combine.
   **Key point**: Reduce heat when seasoning to prevent the seasonings from burning.
5. Return the beef to the wok, turn to high heat, drizzle a little cooking wine along the edge of the wok to create an aromatic burst, stir-fry quickly a few times. Just before plating, drizzle in a little sesame oil or seasoning oil, stir to combine evenly, then plate.
   **Key point**: Do not stir-fry for too long after returning the beef to the wok; stir quickly to maintain tenderness.

## Health Tips  
Beef is high in protein and low in fat, paired with onion and ginger juice to promote iron absorption, suitable for fitness muscle building or people with anemia. Note that beef should be stir-fried quickly after coating to prevent it from becoming tough. Eating onion raw provides better nutrition, but cooked onion is gentler on the stomach.
