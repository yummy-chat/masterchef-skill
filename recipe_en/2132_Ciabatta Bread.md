# Ciabatta Bread
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients  
🌾 High-gluten or all-purpose flour 400g (about 2 bottles, 8 marks)  
💧 Water 320g (about 10 marks, add 30g more if flour has high water absorption)  
🧪 Dry yeast 3-4g  

## Seasonings  
🧂 Salt about 5g (1 bottle cap)  
🫒 Vegetable oil about 15g (half a mark, olive oil, corn oil, or other neutral oils are fine)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale or homemade mineral water bottle measuring cup  
🥣 Large bowl or rectangular container  
🥄 Spatula or rice paddle  
📦 Airtight container or proofing box  
🔥 Oven (or air fryer)  
🍳 Thick-bottomed pan (for stovetop version)  
📜 Parchment paper  

## Steps  
1. Place the flour, yeast, and salt into a container and mix. Be careful to keep the salt and yeast separate to avoid direct contact.  
2. Add the water and stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains and the mixture is sticky. No need to knead.  
3. Pour in the vegetable oil and continue stirring until the oil is fully absorbed and the dough becomes soft.  
4. Apply a little vegetable oil to your hands to prevent sticking. Gently lift the edges of the dough from all sides and fold them toward the center. Stop when you can easily lift the entire piece of dough. This step is meant to shape the dough and begin developing the gluten.  
5. Cover and let the dough rest at room temperature for 20 minutes. If the room temperature is too high, move the container to the refrigerator to prevent rapid fermentation.  
6. Repeat steps 4 and 5, completing a total of three "fold-and-rest" cycles. The dough will become smoother and more extensible after each rest.  
7. Perform the final long fermentation: cover and refrigerate overnight, or place in a warm spot until the dough expands to 2-2.5 times its original volume, with visible bubbles on the surface. Try not to open the lid during fermentation.  
8. Dust a baking sheet or parchment paper generously with flour. Turn the fermented dough out onto it, and dust the top with more flour to prevent sticking. You can lightly shape the dough, but minimize handling to avoid deflating it.  
9. Use a dough scraper to divide the dough into 3-4 equal pieces. Work directly on the parchment-lined baking sheet to avoid moving the dough and preserving the internal air pockets.  
10. Bake.
    - Oven version: Preheat the oven for about 10 minutes. Place the bread on the lower-middle rack and bake at 220°C top heat / 230°C bottom heat for 20-25 minutes, until the surface is golden brown.
    - Stovetop version: Heat a thick-bottomed pan over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Place the dough in the pan, cover, and steam. Flip every 1-1.5 minutes. Keep the lid on for the first 6-7 minutes, then uncover for the remaining 6-7 minutes. Cook until both sides are golden brown and the sides spring back when pressed. Maintain the lowest heat throughout to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.

## Health Tips  
Ciabatta bread is low in sugar and fat, a classic European bread rich in complex carbohydrates. It is suitable as a staple during fitness or weight loss periods, but be sure to pair it with protein and vegetables. The high-hydration dough requires patient folding, and cold fermentation develops flavor better, making it a good recipe for beginners to practice gluten sensing.
