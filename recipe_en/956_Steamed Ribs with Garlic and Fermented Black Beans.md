# Steamed Ribs with Garlic and Fermented Black Beans
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 1250g pork spare ribs (cut into small pieces about 2 cm cubes)
🧄 60g garlic (mashed into garlic paste)
🫘 10g Guangdong Yangjiang fermented black beans (keep whole)
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (shredded)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallions (cut into sections or chopped green onions)
🧂 10g salt
🧂 15g MSG
🍬 30g sugar
🥜 10g peanut butter
🍄 10g black truffle plant-based umami powder (can substitute chicken powder)
🌶️ 10g white pepper powder
🌾 20g starch (dry starch)
🥄 About 1 tablespoon light soy sauce (optional, to improve flavor and color)
🫒 Appropriate amount of garlic oil (obtained from frying garlic paste, used to pour over fermented black beans and seal in flavor)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel basin, small bowl
🧻 Kitchen paper
♨️ Steamer
🍽️ Heat-resistant steaming dish

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the whole slab of ribs along the bone seams, then chop into small pieces about 2 cm cubes. Rinse twice with clean water and drain off the blood water.
2. Add a generous amount of starch and a little salt to the ribs. Mix vigorously to allow the starch to absorb blood and impurities. Let sit for a moment, then rinse repeatedly under running water until the water runs clear. Finally, pat dry the surface with kitchen paper.
3. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to medium-low heat (about 140°C/285°F). Add all the garlic paste and stir constantly over low-medium heat until it turns light golden brown and fragrant. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain. Reserve the garlic oil.
4. Place the dried fermented black beans in a small bowl. While the garlic oil is still hot, spoon one ladleful over the beans. Let sit for about 10 minutes until softened and fragrant.
5. Place the dried ribs in a basin. Add salt, MSG, sugar, peanut butter, black truffle plant-based umami powder, white pepper powder, and 20g dry starch in order. Mix thoroughly by hand until the ribs feel sticky.
6. Add the fried golden garlic paste and the treated fermented black beans. Mix again. Optional: Add about 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce for enhanced soy sauce aroma and color, according to preference.
7. Finally, add an appropriate amount of wet starch and a little garlic oil. Mix to "seal in the oil," locking in moisture and flavor. Marinate for a short while.
8. Bring a steamer full of water to a boil. Place an empty dish in the steamer to preheat for about 1.5 minutes, then remove. Spread the marinated ribs evenly over the hot dish, avoiding thick layers.
9. Place in the steaming steamer over high heat and steam for 10 to 15 minutes until the ribs are fully cooked.
10. After turning off the heat, quickly sprinkle shredded ginger and scallion sections over the ribs. Let rest with residual heat for about 1 minute, then serve.

## Health Suggestions
This dish of steamed ribs with garlic and fermented black beans is rich in protein, but the peanut butter and garlic oil are not low in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for those needing calcium and protein, but those with high blood lipids should control portion sizes.
