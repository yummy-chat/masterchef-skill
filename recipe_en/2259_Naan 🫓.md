# Naan 🫓
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 150g
🍞 Yeast 2g
🧂 Salt 2g
🥚 Egg 1 (reserve a small amount of egg wash for brushing the surface)
🫒 Vegetable oil 18g (for the dough) + a little (for brushing the surface)
🥛 Milk 50g
🌱 White sesame seeds, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔢 Kitchen scale
🥣 Mixing bowl
🫙 Plastic wrap
🔪 Rolling pin
📏 Cutting board
♨️ Air fryer
📄 Parchment paper (cut to fit your air fryer basket size)
🍴 Fork

## Instructions
1.  Combine the flour, yeast, and salt. Lightly beat the egg and reserve a small amount for the egg wash. Mix the remaining beaten egg with the 18g of vegetable oil and milk. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and knead into a smooth dough.
**Note**: First, roughly knead the ingredients into a ball. Let it rest for 10 minutes, then knead again until the surface is smooth. This makes it easier to knead thoroughly.

2.  Cover the dough with plastic wrap and place it in a warm spot to rise until it has increased in volume by 1.5 to 2 times.
3.  Take out the risen dough, knead it to release the air, and divide it into several equal portions. Take one portion and roll it out into a slightly thick round flatbread. Using your hands, push outwards from the center towards the edges to shape the dough into a naan with a thin center and a thicker rim.
4.  Place the shaped naan on a piece of cut parchment paper. First, brush the surface lightly with oil, then brush with the reserved egg wash. Use a fork to poke small holes evenly all over the surface. Sprinkle with white sesame seeds.
**Key**: The parchment paper must be smaller than your air fryer basket to prevent it from being blown up by the hot air and touching the heating element.

5.  Preheat the air fryer to 400°F. Place the naan (with the parchment paper) into the air fryer basket and bake for 8 minutes.
6.  Once the surface is golden brown, remove from the air fryer. Serve immediately while hot. The crust will be crispy, and the inside soft and fluffy.

## Health Tips
This naan is made using an air fryer. Compared to traditional tandoor ovens, it makes it easier to control the amount of oil used, making it suitable for those watching their calorie intake. Pairing it with milk and eggs increases the protein content, making it a great choice for breakfast or a snack. ⚠️ Ensure you let the dough rest before kneading for a softer, fluffier texture.
