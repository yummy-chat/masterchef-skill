# Stir-Fried Beef

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef hind leg 200g (choose a tender part with less sinew, slice thinly against the grain)  
🧅 Onion 50g (shredded)  
🫑 Green pepper 50g (shredded)  
🫚 Ginger 5g (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic 10g (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 8g (cut into segments)  
🧂 Salt 8g (for soaking the meat) + about 1g (added just before serving)  
🧂 Baking soda less than 1g  
🧂 White pepper powder less than 1g  
🫙 Dark soy sauce 5g (for marinating) + 5g (drizzled after turning off the heat)  
🌽 Cornstarch less than 1g  
💧 Water 10-15g  
🫒 Vegetable oil 10g (for marinating) + 20g (for stir-frying) + 5g (for finishing oil)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowls (for marinating and mixing the sauce)  
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon  
🕳️ Strainer or slotted spoon  
🔥 Gas stove/stove top  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the beef**: Slice the beef along the grain, remove tougher sinew sections for other uses, and take the tender part to slice thinly against the grain.  
   **Key point**: You must cut against the grain to break the fibers, ensuring a tender texture.  
2. **Soak to remove odor**: Mix 500g of water with 8g of salt to create a brine, submerge the beef slices for 15 minutes, then remove, rinse with clean water, and squeeze out excess moisture firmly.  
   **Key point**: Brining effectively removes blood and gamey odor while hydrating the beef to make it tender.  
3. **Marinate and coat**: Add baking soda, white pepper powder, and 5g of dark soy sauce to the squeezed beef, and mix well. Combine cornstarch with 10-15g of water to form a slurry, pour it over the beef, and knead and toss until the liquid is fully absorbed. Finally, add 10g of vegetable oil and mix evenly.  
   **Key point**: Baking soda tenderizes, white pepper removes odor, and dark soy sauce provides color; the starch slurry forms a protective layer, and sealing with oil prevents sticking.  
4. **Prepare the vegetables**: Shred the onion and green pepper separately, slice the ginger, mince the garlic, cut the dried chili peppers into segments, and set aside in separate piles.  
5. **Stir-fry the beef**: Heat the wok thoroughly, add 20g of oil, then immediately add the beef slices. Stir-fry over medium heat with chopsticks, quickly separating the slices, for about 1 minute until the beef just changes color, then remove immediately.  
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; stir-fry quickly and remove the beef as soon as it changes color, allowing residual heat to cook it through—this is the key to tenderness.  
6. **Fry the dried chili peppers**: Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the dried chili segments while the oil is still cool or warm, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
   **Key point**: Dried chilies must be added to cold or warm oil to avoid burning.  
7. **Fry the aromatics**: Add the ginger slices and minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant, then add the shredded onion and stir-fry until slightly softened.  
8. **Combine and stir-fry**: Return the stir-fried beef to the wok, quickly toss to combine and let the flavors meld. Finally, add the shredded green pepper and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until just cooked but still crisp, maintaining its color.  
   **Key point**: Add the green pepper last to preserve its color and crunch.  
9. **Turn off the heat and season**: After turning off the heat, drizzle in 5g of dark soy sauce and 5g of finishing oil, and add about 1g of salt to taste. Toss everything evenly and serve.  
   **Key point**: Seasoning after turning off the heat prevents the soy sauce from developing a burnt, bitter taste; finishing oil adds shine and enhances aroma.  

## Health Tips  
This stir-fried beef dish uses three key techniques: brining, tenderizing with baking soda, and stir-frying with hot wok and cool oil. The result is tender, non-dry beef, suitable for those needing a high-protein, low-fat diet. Be mindful of salt intake, especially for those with high blood pressure—use dark soy sauce and additional salt cautiously.
