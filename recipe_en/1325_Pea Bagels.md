# Pea Bagels
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 Bagel dough (plain and matcha flavors)  
🧀 Mascarpone cheese (plain and matcha flavors)  
💧 Water (used as adhesive for dough sheets)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍽️ Cutting board  
🫓 Rolling pin  
🥄 Piping bag  
🖌️ Brush  
🔥 Oven  
🍲 Boiling pot  
📄 Baking paper  
🥢 Silicone spatula  
🥡 Plastic wrap  
♨️ Steamer  

## Steps
1.  Prepare the mascarpone cheese in plain and matcha flavors separately, then place them into piping bags for later use.
   **Key point**: The cheese has a soft texture, so it needs to be piped in using a piping bag, and should not be wrapped directly by hand.
2.  Divide the bagel dough into small portions. One part will be used for wrapping the filling to form the peas, and the other part will be used to make the outer pea pod shell.
3.  Take a small piece of dough, wrap it with an appropriate amount of cheese filling, and roll it into a ball; roll the pea pod dough into a thin, tongue-shaped sheet.
4.  Brush a layer of water on the surface of the dough sheet as an adhesive. Place 3 filled balls on it, fold the sheet over to wrap them, and pinch the seal tightly. Shape it into a pea pod.
5.  Cover the shaped dough pieces with plastic wrap and let them cold-ferment in the refrigerator.
   **Note**: Ensure sufficient yeast when using cold fermentation to guarantee proper fermentation results.
6.  Take the cold-fermented dough pieces out. If there is no significant increase in size, place them in a steamer for supplementary fermentation until the dough expands to 1.5-2 times its original size and appears plump on the surface.
7.  Bring a pot of water to a gentle simmer. Place the dough pieces along with the bottom baking paper into the water. Remove the paper once it's thoroughly wet, and boil each side for about 30 seconds.
   **Key point**: Boiling is a critical step for developing the bagel's signature chewy texture and crust. Both sides must be boiled thoroughly.
8.  Remove the bagels, drain the water, and place them on a baking sheet lined with baking paper. Transfer them to a preheated oven and bake using the top and bottom heating elements. Halfway through, cover with a layer of aluminum foil to prevent excessive browning on the surface. Bake until the surface is evenly browned and fully cooked.

## Health Advice  
This pea bagel is filled with mascarpone cheese, giving it a light and creamy texture, but it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with black coffee or green tea to cut through the richness. The cold fermentation method makes it easy to prepare in advance, saving time during morning preparation.
