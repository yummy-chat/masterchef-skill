# English Muffin

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 330g  
🍞 Yeast 7g  
🥛 Milk 190g  
💧 Water 120g  
🧈 Butter 42g (melted)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🍬 Sugar 25g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🌽 Cornmeal (for dusting)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Oven (for proving environment)  
📡 Microwave (for heating liquids and melting butter)  
🤖 Stand mixer with paddle attachment  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🥣 Large bowl (for first proofing)  
🛡️ Silicone mat  
🔲 Round cutter  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🪄 Lid  
🪄 Spatula  

## Steps  
1. Mix milk, water, and sugar together, then heat in the microwave until warm.  
2. Add yeast to the warm liquid mixture and let it sit for a few minutes until a thick foam forms on the surface, indicating the yeast is activated.  
3. In the stand mixer bowl, first pour in the activated yeast mixture and egg, then add the melted butter.  
   **Note**: Since the butter is hot, adding the egg first prevents the heat from killing the yeast.  
4. Add flour and salt, mix on low speed until no dry flour remains, then increase to medium speed and mix for about 7 minutes. The dough will be very sticky at this stage.  
5. Transfer the dough to an oiled large bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and place in a warm environment for the first proofing until doubled in size and full of air bubbles.  
6. Dust the work surface and hands generously with flour, flatten the proofed dough to about 2–3 cm thick, and cut out small rounds with a round cutter.  
   **Note**: The dough is extremely sticky; all tools in contact need to be well dusted to prevent sticking.  
7. Place the cut pieces on a baking sheet dusted with cornmeal for the second proofing, about 40 minutes.  
8. Place the proofed pieces in a non-stick frying pan, cover with a lid, and cook over low heat throughout.  
   **Key point**: Slow cooking over low heat is essential to ensure the inside is fully cooked.  
9. Cook one side for 5–6 minutes until golden brown, then flip and continue cooking until both sides are golden and cooked through.  

## Health Advice  
This English muffin has a fluffy texture, but the all-purpose flour and butter are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with protein and vegetables to balance nutrition. The activation of yeast is key in the process; be careful that the liquid temperature is not too high to avoid deactivating the yeast.
