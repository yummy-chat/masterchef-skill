# Squid Tentacles Baked in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦑 Fresh squid 2–3 (remove innards, skin, and cartilage; cut both tentacles and body into long strips)
🧅 Scallion 1–2 (cut into sections and shreds)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (sliced and minced)
🧄 Garlic 3–4 cloves (minced)
🌶️ Pickled wild chilies 5–8 (cut into sections)
🌶️ Red bell pepper strips, as needed (for garnish)
🥬 Baby bok choy 10–15 (trim roots, score a cross at the base)

🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste (used separately for marinating and blanching)
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (poured along the edge of the pot)
🍶 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Clay pot
🍳 Wok or stockpot
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Stage
1. Place the squid tentacles into a bowl. Add scallion sections, minced ginger, minced garlic, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt. Put on gloves and mix well. Marinate for 5 minutes.
### Clay Pot Baking
2. Place the clay pot over low heat. Pour in sesame oil and heat until fragrant. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and pickled wild chilies, stirring until aromatic.
3. Pour the marinated squid tentacles along with the marinade into the clay pot. Drizzle a circle of cooking wine along the inner edge of the pot.
**Key point**: Do not add water.

4. Cover with the lid and keep over low heat for 5 minutes.
5. Remove the lid, stir well, and serve.
### Blanched Vegetables
6. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and a little cooking oil. Once boiling, add the baby bok choy and blanch for 1 minute until bright green. Drain well.
7. In the same boiling water, add the sliced squid body pieces and blanch for about 10 seconds, then immediately remove to prevent overcooking.
**Note**: Strictly control the time; squid overcooks easily.

8. Arrange the blanched bok choy on the bottom of a plate. Lay the squid slices on top. Garnish with shredded scallion, shredded ginger, and red bell pepper strips. Drizzle with steamed fish soy sauce.
9. Heat oil until it reaches about 60% hot, then pour it over the scallion shreds and red pepper strips to release their aroma. Serve.

## Health Advice
Squid is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making it suitable for weight loss; however, its cholesterol content is relatively high, so those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Pairing with baby bok choy adds dietary fiber, helping to balance nutrition. It is recommended to use less salt and soy sauce to avoid excessive sodium intake.
