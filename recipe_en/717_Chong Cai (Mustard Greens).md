# Chong Cai (Mustard Greens)

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Tender mustard green tips (just budding tips) appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🍬 White sugar a pinch  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper a pinch  
🥫 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍶 Vinegar appropriate amount  
🫙 Red oil (chili oil) appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chilies appropriate amount  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic cloves appropriate amount  
🫑 Green peppers (cut into pieces) appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok (frying pan)  
🥣 Stainless steel or enamel basin (with lid, for airtight sealing)  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🧺 Colander or plastic basket (for washing and air-drying)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Select tender mustard green tips that have just formed buds during the period from the Beginning of Spring to the Rain Water solar term. Wash them and then dry in the sun until the leaves become wilted. If it is rainy or overcast, spread them in a basket and air-dry for 1-2 days.  
2. Cut the wilted tender mustard green tips into fine particles.  
3. Heat an iron wok, **do not use any oil throughout the entire process**, pour in the vegetable particles, and stir-fry over medium-low heat continuously.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry until you can feel obvious heat when the back of your hand approaches or lightly touches the vegetable particles; do not burn them. The frying time must be appropriate: too long and the greens become soft and not crispy; too short and the pungent flavor is insufficient.  

4. As soon as the frying is done, immediately transfer the vegetable particles into a clean and dry basin, and quickly seal it tightly.  
**Key point**: The action must be fast, the seal must be tight, and do not open it midway.  

5. Leave it sealed at room temperature for 24 hours. When opened, the greens should appear yellowish and emit a strong pungent odor, indicating the fermentation is complete.  
6. Serve it either as a cold dish or stir-fried:  
   - **As a cold dish**: Take the chong cai, add salt, MSG, white sugar, ground Sichuan pepper, light soy sauce, vinegar, and red oil in that order, and mix well.  
   - **Stir-fried**: Heat oil in a wok, first stir-fry dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, and garlic cloves until fragrant, then add green pepper pieces and the chong cai, quickly stir-fry evenly, and remove from the wok.  

## Health Suggestions  
This chong cai is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, making it ideal for a spring seasonal treat. However, its mustard oil is highly stimulating, so those with sensitive stomachs should consume it in small amounts. The cold salad version has a refreshing texture, while the stir-fried version is more aromatic. Sealing it airtight for 24 hours is the key to developing the pungent flavor.
