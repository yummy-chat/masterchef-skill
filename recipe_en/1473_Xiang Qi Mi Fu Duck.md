# Xiang Qi Mi Fu Duck
Cuisine: Xiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck (freshly slaughtered, water duck or other varieties acceptable)  
🥓 Pork belly (optional)  
🧄 Ginger, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🧅 Scallion, to taste  
🌾 Glutinous rice flour, to taste  
🌾 Rice flour, to taste  
🌾 Steamed meat powder, to taste  
🌶️ Fine chili powder, to taste  
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste  
💧 Boiling water, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix glutinous rice flour, rice flour, steamed meat powder, fine chili powder, 5 grams of salt, and white pepper powder together to form a mixed powder mixture.  
**Key Point**: Rice flour is the main ingredient, glutinous rice flour adds a soft and sticky texture, steamed meat powder provides a five-spice base flavor, while chili powder and pepper powder add a complex aroma.

2. Cut the duck into pieces suitable for steaming, slice the ginger, peel the garlic cloves, and slice the pork belly.  
**Note**: The duck must be freshly slaughtered to ensure the authentic flavor of the finished dish.

3. Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of cooking oil, and stir-fry the pork belly slices until the fat renders out; if insufficient oil is released, add a little more cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, add the duck pieces and stir-fry until the skin turns golden brown and changes color.  
**Key Point**: Thorough stir-frying helps render excess fat from the duck meat and tightens the skin, resulting in a chewier texture after steaming.

4. Add the ginger slices, garlic cloves, and the remaining salt, and continue stir-frying until fragrant.  
5. Reduce the heat to low, pour in the mixed powder mixture, and stir-fry so that the duck meat is evenly coated with the rice flour. Add boiling water in small amounts multiple times, stirring continuously, until the duck meat and rice flour achieve a moist and sticky consistency.  
**Key Point**: Add water in small amounts multiple times to avoid clumping or making the mixture too thin, which would affect the texture of the final product.

6. Transfer the stir-fried duck meat into a bowl, place it in the pressure cooker, add water, and steam for 20–25 minutes. If using an older duck, extend the time to over 1 hour.  
7. Before serving, sprinkle with chopped scallions and enjoy.

## Health Advice
This dish features duck meat steamed with rice flour coating; the fat is absorbed by the rice flour, resulting in a soft and sticky texture but high calorie content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. Duck meat is rich in protein and B vitamins, but those with poor digestion should control their portion size.
