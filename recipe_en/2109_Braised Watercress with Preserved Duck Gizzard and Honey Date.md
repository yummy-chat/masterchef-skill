# Braised Watercress with Preserved Duck Gizzard and Honey Date
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (Sep-Oct), Winter (Nov-Dec)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Watercress 1 large bunch (prefer autumn/winter, choose those with white roots)
🦆 Preserved duck gizzard (dried) 2-3 pieces (soak overnight in water to soften, scrape clean, then slice)
🐖 Fresh flat ribs 1-2 pieces
🐖 Pork shank meat appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger slices 2 slices
🍊 Aged tangerine peel 1 small piece
🍈 Honey date 1 piece
🌿 Peppercorns appropriate amount
🌰 Almonds a small amount
🧂 Salt approximately "seven parts" ratio (corresponding to one catty of water)
🍾 Rice wine a suitable amount (sprinkle over the meat)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🫖 Ceramic double-boiler pot
♨️ Large commercial steamer
🍳 Wok / soup pot (for blanching)
🥄 Bowls, soup ladle

## Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Process preserved duck gizzard**: Soak the dried duck gizzards in water overnight until soft. Scrape clean the surface with a knife, then slice.
**Key point**: Must be fully rehydrated and scraped clean, otherwise it will have an off-odor and a hard texture.
2. **Process the meat**: Cut the fresh flat ribs and pork shank into pieces and place them into the double-boiler pot.
3. **Assemble base ingredients**: Into the pot containing the meat, place the ginger slices, aged tangerine peel, processed duck gizzard slices, honey date, peppercorns, and almonds.
4. **Blanch watercress**: In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Blanch the watercress for about 1 minute, then remove and drain.
**Key point**: This step is crucial for the soup's clean, sweet flavor. It removes the watercress's astringency. Do not blanch too long, or the leaves will become mushy.

### Cooking Stage
1. **First braising**: Add water to the pot until it just covers all ingredients. Add salt and a splash of rice wine. Cover the pot and place it in the steamer to braise for 1 hour.
**Key point**: Do not add the watercress at this stage. Adding it too early will cause it to overcook and the soup to become bitter.
2. **Second braising**: After 1 hour, open the steamer. Place the blanched watercress on top of the soup ingredients. Cover the pot again and continue to braise over medium heat for another half hour, until the watercress is tender but still holds its shape, and its clean, sweet flavor has infused the soup.
**Key point**: Braising in two separate stages is key to achieving a clear soup and a clean, sweet taste.

## Health Tips
This soup helps clear heat and moisten the lungs. The combination of watercress, preserved duck gizzard, and honey date can effectively alleviate autumn dryness, making it suitable for those with a dry throat, cough, or who often stay up late. Note that the preserved duck gizzard must be fully softened and scraped clean, otherwise the soup will taste gamey; do not add too many peppercorns, as they can overpower the clean, sweet flavor.
