# Eggplant and Meat Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant, about 1 piece (scraped into long thin slices)
🥩 Ground pork, appropriate amount
🧅 Chopped green onions, appropriate amount (for seasoning and garnish)
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (for sautéing and seasoning)
🌾 Starch, appropriate amount (for coating eggplant rolls)
🌱 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for garnish)

🥣 Meat filling seasoning:
- 🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
- 🥄 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
- 🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
- 🌿 Thirteen spices, appropriate amount

🍯 Sauce preparation:
- 🥄 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
- 🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
- 🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
- 🍬 Sugar, a little
- 🌾 Starch, 1 tablespoon
- 🚰 Water, half a bowl

🛢️ Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (for frying and stir-frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing meat filling)
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🔪 Vegetable peeler
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Metal spoon
🥃 Glass bowl (for holding sauce)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare meat filling**: Mix ground pork with light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen spices, and chopped green onions. Stir in one direction until the mixture becomes firm and sticky. Set aside.
   *Note: Stirring in one direction until firm gives the meat filling a denser texture.*
2. **Prepare eggplant**: Wash the eggplant. Use a vegetable peeler to scrape it lengthwise into thin, long slices.
   *Note: The slices should be as thin and long as possible for easy rolling and even cooking.*
3. **Make eggplant rolls**: Take one eggplant slice, evenly spread a layer of meat filling on it, then roll it tightly from one end to the other. Place the roll with the seam side down.
   *Note: Spread the filling evenly; roll with slight pressure to prevent it from coming apart.*
4. **Coat with starch**: Place the rolled eggplant rolls in starch, turning to coat them evenly with a thin layer of starch on the surface.
   *Note: Coating with starch makes the outer skin crispy after frying and prevents sticking.*
5. **Deep-fry eggplant rolls**: Pour enough vegetable oil into the wok. Heat over medium heat until it reaches about 50% hot (around 150°C/300°F). Add the eggplant rolls and fry for about 6 minutes until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
   *Note: The oil level should be enough to submerge the ingredients. Do not turn them immediately after adding; wait until the outer skin sets before gently flipping. Drain the oil after removing.*
6. **Stir-fry the sauce**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Pour in the pre-mixed sauce. Heat over medium heat while stirring continuously until the sauce thickens.
   *Note: The sauce ingredients must be mixed thoroughly in advance. Stir constantly after adding to the wok to prevent the starch from clumping or burning.*
7. **Coat with sauce**: Add the fried eggplant rolls to the sauce. Quickly and gently stir-fry to coat each roll evenly with the sauce.
   *Note: Stir gently to avoid breaking the eggplant rolls apart.*
8. **Plate and serve**: Sprinkle with chopped green onions and white sesame seeds. Toss evenly and then transfer to a serving plate.
   *Note: Serve hot immediately after plating; the sauce is rich and goes excellently with rice.*

## Health Tips
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber, but it absorbs a lot of oil when fried. The meat filling provides protein. This dish is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balance and to consume it occasionally.
