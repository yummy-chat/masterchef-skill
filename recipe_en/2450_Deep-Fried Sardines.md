# Deep-Fried Sardines
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Sardines 700g (already scaled, gilled, gutted, and cleaned)
🫚 Ginger 10g (smashed and minced)
🧅 Scallion 10g (smashed and cut into sections)
🌾 Starch appropriate amount (for coating the fish)
🧂 Salt about 3g
🍶 Cooking wine about 5g
🧂 Pepper salt appropriate amount (for dipping)
🫒 Soybean oil sufficient amount (for deep-frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🥄 Large soup ladle
🍽️ Plate
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. **Marinate**: Place the minced ginger and scallion sections in a bowl. Add 3g salt and 5g cooking wine, then knead vigorously until juices release. Add the cleaned sardines, mix well, and marinate for 10 minutes.
   - Note: Sardines are seawater fish with natural saltiness, so do not add too much salt; remove the ginger and scallion residue after marinating.

2. **Coat with Starch**: Dredge each marinated sardine in starch, coating evenly with a thin layer.
   - Key point: A thin coating locks in the fish's moisture, achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.

3. **First Fry**: Pour sufficient soybean oil into the wok and heat to 170°C (340°F). Add the starch-coated sardines one by one to avoid stacking. Fry over medium heat for about 2 minutes, until the fish is set and the surface turns slightly yellow and firm. Remove.
   - Note: The oil temperature should not be too low, otherwise the fish may break apart; if using less oil, fry in batches.

4. **Second Fry**: Heat the oil to 190°C (375°F). Add all the first-fried sardines at once and fry over high heat for about 10 seconds, until the color turns golden and the shell is crispy. Remove quickly.
   - Key point: High-temperature second frying forces out excess oil, making the shell crisper.

5. **Plate**: Line a plate with oil-absorbing paper. Use chopsticks to arrange the fried sardines on the plate. Sprinkle pepper salt on the side for dipping.

## Health Suggestions
Sardines are rich in Omega-3 fatty acids and calcium, which are beneficial for cardiovascular and bone health. Pair with fresh vegetables to balance the richness; individuals with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
