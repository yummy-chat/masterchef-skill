# Korean Stone Pot Bibimbap
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Rice, as needed  
🥩 Pork belly, as needed  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, as needed (sliced)  
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (shredded)  
🥕 Carrot, as needed (shredded)  
🥬 Cabbage, 1 leaf  
🥚 Eggs, 2  
🌶️ Korean hot pepper paste, as needed  
🌱 Sesame seeds, a pinch  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker (with inner pot)  
🥒 Vegetable peeler  
✂️ Scissors  
🥄 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash the rice thoroughly and place it in the rice cooker’s inner pot.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of rice according to personal serving size.  
2. Layer the pork belly, shiitake mushroom slices, cucumber shreds, and carrot shreds in sequence on top of the rice.  
   **Note**: Slice the mushrooms with scissors, and shred the cucumber and carrot using a peeler; ingredient amounts can be adjusted to taste.  
3. Cover the ingredients with a cabbage leaf, then crack 2 eggs on top.  
   **Note**: The cabbage leaf prevents the eggs from sinking or sticking, making stirring easier later.  
4. Close the rice cooker lid, select the "Standard Cook" mode, and start the cooking program.  
5. Once cooking is complete, open the lid and add an appropriate amount of Korean hot pepper paste and a pinch of sesame seeds.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of paste to personal taste; start with a small amount, mix, taste, and add more if needed.  
6. Use a spatula to thoroughly mix the rice, ingredients, and hot pepper paste.  
   **Note**: Stir gently to avoid scratching the inner pot’s coating or over-mashing the ingredients; mix until the color is even.  
7. Serve in a bowl. The dish offers a balance of soft and crispy textures, with egg and sauce aromas blending together.  

## Health Advice  
This rice cooker version of stone pot bibimbap offers a balanced nutrition, including carbohydrates, protein, and various vegetables, making it suitable for busy office workers or students. Note that pork belly is high in fat; consume in moderation and control the amount of hot pepper paste to reduce sodium intake.
