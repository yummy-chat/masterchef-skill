# Colorful Cold Noodle Rolls
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Flour 1000g  
💧 Cold water 460g  
🥬 Spinach a small bunch (juiced for green color)  
🥕 Carrot half (juiced for red color)  
🫘 Mung bean sprouts适量 (pre-blanched and drained)  
🫓 Gluten适量 (produced from washing dough, torn into strips)  
🥒 Cucumber 1 (cut into shreds)  
🫒 Cooked vegetable oil适量 (heated to 60-70% hot)  
🫘 Peeled white sesame seeds 60g  
🌶️ Coarse chili powder (Qin pepper) 120g  
🧄 Minced garlic 60g  
🫘 Sesame paste 80g (diluted with cold boiled water)  
🌿 Sichuan pepper powder 8g  
🌿 Cumin powder 8g  
🧂 Salt 20g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 15g  
🌊 Starch water适量 (added to the colored batter)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🔍 Fine strainer/filter

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Mix the flour with cold water and knead into a smooth dough until the bowl, hands, and dough are clean. Place the dough in a bowl, add cold water to submerge it, and repeatedly knead and wash until the water becomes thick and white.  
   **Key point**: During washing, repeatedly knead, turn, and press the dough to ensure the starch is fully released.  

2. Strain the starchy water through a fine strainer to remove impurities and gluten bits. Let it settle for about 10 hours. After settling, carefully pour off the clear water on top, leaving the starchy paste at the bottom. Stir well.  

3. Divide the starchy paste into three portions. Use spinach juice and carrot juice to color one portion green and one red, leaving one portion uncolored. Add an appropriate amount of starch water to each portion and stir well.  
   **Key point**: Vegetable juices should be prepared in advance using a blender. Starch water helps improve texture and consistency.  

4. Blanch the mung bean sprouts in boiling water, then drain. Shred the cucumber using a vegetable shredder. Tear the gluten into small strips and set aside.  

### Making the Chili Oil Sauce
1. Pour the cooked vegetable oil into a wok and heat until 60-70% hot, then turn off the heat.  
2. Add the peeled white sesame seeds, coarse chili powder, and minced garlic in sequence, stirring with a spoon.  
3. Add the diluted sesame paste (with cold boiled water) and stir well.  
4. Finally, add the Sichuan pepper powder, cumin powder, salt, and chicken bouillon. Mix thoroughly again. Let the sauce cool completely before use.  
   **Key point**: All powders and sauces must be thoroughly mixed, and the oil temperature should be well controlled.  

### Steaming the Cold Noodles
1. Brush a thin layer of cooked oil on the steaming tray (lou). Pour in an appropriate amount of colored batter and swirl the tray to spread it evenly across the bottom.  
2. Place the tray into a pot of boiling water, cover with the lid, and steam for 2 minutes once steam rises. Open the lid; if large bubbles form on the surface, the noodle sheet is done.  
   **Key point**: The batter must be swirled to an even thickness. Immediately after removing from the pot, cool the tray in cold water.  

3. Cool the steamed noodle sheet (still on the tray) in cold water until it is no longer hot to the touch. Carefully peel off the entire sheet from the edge and place it on a cutting board.  

### Rolling the Cold Noodle Rolls
1. Lay a noodle sheet flat. Spoon an appropriate amount of cooled chili oil sauce onto the sheet and spread it evenly using the noodle sheet itself.  
2. Layer the blanched mung bean sprouts, gluten strips, and cucumber shreds on top. Toss them together, then roll the sheet tightly from one end to form a roll.  
   **Key point**: The filling should be mixed with the sauce in advance, and the roll must be tight and firm.  

## Health Tips
Colorful cold noodle rolls are rich in vegetables and gluten, providing dietary fiber and plant protein. They are suitable for those seeking a light, low-fat diet. The chili oil sauce is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to consume it in moderation and pay attention to controlling oil and salt intake.
