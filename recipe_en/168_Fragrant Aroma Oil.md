# Fragrant Aroma Oil
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌿 Cumin 8g  
⭐ Star Anise 6g  
🪵 Cinnamon 6g  
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorns 4g  
🌸 Cloves 1g  
🍃 Bay Leaves 1g  
🫘 Amomum Villosum 2g  
🥢 Black Cardamom 1 piece  
🪷 Angelica Dahurica 1g  
🫚 Galangal 2g  
🥥 Nutmeg 2g  
🥜 Cardamom 2g  
🧅 Scallion 60g (cut into sections or thick slices)  
🧅 Small Spring Onion 20g (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro 20g (whole with roots or cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger Slices 30g  
🫒 Soybean Oil (base oil, enough to submerge ingredients)  
🐔 Chicken Fat (to taste, mixed with soybean oil for richness)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer (for rinsing spices and draining)  
🥣 Large Ceramic Bowl (heat-resistant, for resting)  
🥢 Wooden Spatula or Long-Handled Spoon (for gentle stirring)  
🍽️ Round Wooden Lid (or other sealed lid)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Combine the dry spices, quickly rinse under clean water to remove earthy odors, and drain using a strainer. Set aside.
2. Wash and cut the scallion, small spring onion, cilantro, and ginger slices, retaining a bit of moisture.
   **Key Point**: The high moisture content of fresh aromatics helps slow the oil temperature rise during the low-heat phase, protecting the spices from burning.
3. Pour soybean oil and chicken fat into the wok without turning on the heat. Add all fresh aromatics to the cold oil.
4. Then add the drained dry spices to the cold oil, ensuring all ingredients are fully submerged.
5. Turn on low heat, allowing the oil temperature to rise slowly. Use a wooden spatula to gently stir, ensuring even heating.
   **Key Point**: Slow simmering over low heat throughout is the essence of releasing aroma. Never use medium or high heat, or the outer layers will burn while the inner flavors remain unextracted.
6. Continuously observe the wok. When the scallion, cilantro, and other aromatics become shriveled and turn a deep golden brown without charring, immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key Point**: The timing of turning off the heat directly determines the final flavor—too late results in bitterness, too early yields a bland taste.
7. After turning off the heat, pour the hot oil along with all the spent ingredients into a heat-resistant large ceramic bowl.
8. Immediately cover the bowl tightly with a lid, using residual heat to rest the oil until it has completely cooled naturally.
   **Key Point**: Covering and resting is the core step for "extracting aroma" and "blending flavors." It allows deep-seated aromas from the ingredients to fully release and infuse into the oil, resulting in a more robust and richer fragrance when uncovered compared to when freshly made.
9. Once cooled, the Fragrant Aroma Oil is ready. The spent ingredients can be kept in the oil for convenient use later, with the fragrance lasting long.

## Health Tips  
Fragrant Aroma Oil is prepared by slow simmering over low heat, resulting in a rich aroma but high fat content. Use sparingly to enhance dishes. Those with weak spleen and stomach or who avoid greasy foods should pair it with light dishes and avoid excessive intake.
