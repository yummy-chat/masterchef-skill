# Pan-Fried Soufflé Pancake  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 3 eggs (separated into egg whites and yolks)  
🌾 60g cake flour  
🥛 60g milk (can be replaced with water)  
🫐 About half a bowl of blueberries  
🍓 About half a bowl of strawberries  
🍋 1 slice of lemon (squeeze for juice)  
🧂 About 50g white sugar (a small amount for the jam, the rest added to the egg whites in batches)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🧴 Plastic bottle (used as both a measuring cup and a hand whisk)  
🥣 2 oil-free and water-free bowls  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🪵 Wooden spatula  
📄 Parchment paper (optional, cut into strips for shaping)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🫗 Lid  
🍳 Iron plate (optional, placed under the pan for even heat distribution)  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the jam**: In a pot, add blueberries, strawberries, a small amount of white sugar, and lemon juice. Heat over low flame while stirring constantly until the liquid thickens. Remove from heat and let cool. Set aside.  
2. **Prepare the pan**: Use a paper towel to apply a thin layer of cooking oil evenly on the pan's surface. For even browning, place an iron plate under the pan or lift the pan slightly above the heat.  
3. **Make the egg yolk batter**: In a bowl, combine egg yolks, cake flour, and milk. Stir with chopsticks until smooth with no lumps. The batter should be thick, forming a ribbon-like pattern when lifted. If too thin, add more flour; if too dry, add more milk.  
4. **Whip the egg whites**: Place egg whites in an oil-free and water-free bowl. Use a plastic bottle whisk to beat quickly. Once lines appear, add the remaining white sugar in batches. Continue beating at high speed until the meringue forms short, stiff peaks when the whisk is lifted. Ensure the bowl and whisk are completely free of oil and water.  
5. **Combine the batter**: Add one-third of the meringue to the egg yolk batter. Fold gently with a spatula until combined. Then pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue. Fold from the bottom upward until the color is uniform and the batter is thick and glossy. Avoid stirring in circles to prevent deflating.  
6. **Shape in the pan**: Preheat the frying pan over low heat until your hand feels warmth hovering above it. Reduce to the lowest heat. Optionally, use parchment paper rings for shaping. Spoon the batter into the pan.  
7. **Steam-fry the first side**: Add a small amount of water along the edge of the pan, then quickly cover with the lid. Cook over the lowest heat for about 5 minutes. Remove the lid, add a bit more water, cover again, and continue cooking for about 4 minutes.  
8. **Flip**: Once the batter on the surface is set and the color lightens, carefully flip the pancake with a spatula. Cover and cook over the lowest heat for another 5–6 minutes until both sides are golden brown and the inside is fully cooked. Rely on steam throughout to ensure a fluffy texture.  
9. Serve with the cooled jam and fruit.  

## Health Tips  
This soufflé pancake uses a steam-fry method in a frying pan, resulting in a moister texture than oven-baked versions and is beginner-friendly. Properly whipping the egg whites is key to success. Best enjoyed while hot, as it tends to collapse when cooled. The jam can be made with less sugar for a healthier option, suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss.
