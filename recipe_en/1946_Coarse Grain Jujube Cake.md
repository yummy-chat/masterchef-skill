# Coarse Grain Jujube Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red jujubes (Xinjiang Hetian large jujubes, plump and thick, with rich aroma)
🌾 Low-gluten flour 500g
🍄 Dry yeast 1 packet
🥛 Milk as needed (can be substituted with water)
💧 Water as needed
🥣 Steamed glutinous oatmeal, a generous amount
🥜 Mixed nuts (walnuts, peanuts, etc.), as needed
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer
🌀 Blender
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Spatula
🍚 Rice cooker (including inner pot)
🧻 Kitchen paper

## Steps
1. Wash the red jujubes with clean water, place them in a steamer, and steam over medium heat for 15 minutes until soft. Remove, break apart by hand, and discard the pits.
   **Key point**: Softened jujubes can be pitted by hand directly; using Hetian jujubes yields a richer flavor.
2. Put the pitted jujubes into a blender, add an appropriate amount of milk, and blend into a smooth jujube paste without grains.
   **Key point**: Milk enhances the milky aroma, but can be substituted with water.
3. In a glass bowl, pour in the low-gluten flour and dry yeast, mix well, then add the jujube paste. Use a spatula for initial mixing. Gradually add water in small amounts, stirring until the batter reaches a yogurt-like consistency.
   **Key point**: Water must be added in small, multiple batches to avoid thinning the batter; low-gluten flour does not require kneading.
4. Add a generous amount of steamed glutinous oatmeal and mixed nuts, stirring thoroughly until evenly combined.
   **Key point**: Oatmeal can be added generously; adjust the amount of nuts according to taste.
5. Cover the surface of the batter with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place for about 1 hour, until it expands in volume and a rich, fine bubble structure forms on the surface. Remove the plastic wrap, and use a spatula to stir and release some of the large air bubbles.
   **Key point**: After releasing the air, the batter will shrink slightly, but small bubbles should remain to ensure a fluffy texture.
6. Pour a small amount of cooking oil onto the bottom and inner walls of the rice cooker's inner pot, and spread it into a thin layer using kitchen paper. Pour the batter in, controlling the thickness to 6-8 cm. Gently tap the inner pot to release excess air, and evenly sprinkle white sesame seeds over the surface.
   **Key point**: Oil the inner pot for easy unmolding; overly thick batter can cause uneven heating; tapping removes air pockets for a more compact finished product.
7. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, start the cooking program, and after the program finishes, open the lid. Remove and unmold.
   **Doneness check**: The finished product emits a rich jujube aroma, springs back when pressed, and has a fluffy texture.

## Health Advice
This jujube cake replaces some refined sugar with coarse grains and red jujubes, making it rich in dietary fiber and suitable as a breakfast or afternoon snack. Pay attention to the ratio of oatmeal to flour to avoid significant blood sugar fluctuations.
