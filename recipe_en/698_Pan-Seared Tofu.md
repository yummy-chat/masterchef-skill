# Pan-Seared Tofu  
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Firm tofu – 1 block (about 400g, cut into cubes)  
🧅 Scallions – 2-3 stalks (some cut into sections for soaking water, some chopped for garnish)  
🫚 Ginger – 2-3 slices  
🧄 Garlic – 3-4 cloves (minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper – a small amount (minced for garnish)  
🧂 Salt – about 1 teaspoon  
🛢️ Cooking oil – as needed  
🥄 Soy sauce – as needed  
🌶️ Chili oil – as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls (at least 2, for soaking water and dipping sauce)  
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🍽️ Plate  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Slice the firm tofu horizontally into three even slabs, then make two vertical cuts to create evenly sized cubes.  
   **Note**: Handle gently and steadily to avoid breaking the tofu.  
2. In a bowl, add water, scallion sections, ginger slices, and salt. Stir until the salt is fully dissolved to make a scallion-ginger-salt water.  
3. Gently place the tofu cubes into the scallion-ginger-salt water and soak for 5 minutes to allow the flavor to penetrate and remove any beany taste.  
4. Remove the tofu cubes and pat them dry to remove surface moisture, preventing oil splatter or sticking during cooking.  
5. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Once hot, add cooking oil and swirl the pan to coat the bottom evenly.  
   **Key**: Heating the pan before adding oil is essential to prevent sticking.  
6. Place the dried tofu cubes into the pan one by one, keeping them over medium heat and ensuring they do not overlap.  
7. Fry until the bottom is set and golden brown. When the tofu can be easily moved with a spatula, flip each piece.  
   **Note**: Wait until the bottom is fully set before flipping, otherwise the tofu may break.  
8. Continue frying the other side over medium heat until golden brown, giving the tofu a slightly crispy exterior.  
9. Once fried, sprinkle chopped scallions and minced red bell pepper into the pan. Heat briefly to release their aroma, then turn off the heat.  
10. In a separate bowl, combine minced garlic, soy sauce, and chili oil. Stir well to make the dipping sauce.  
11. Arrange the pan-seared tofu on a plate and place the dipping sauce bowl in the center. Serve while hot.  

## Health Advice  
Pan-seared tofu is rich in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss or blood sugar management diet. Control the amount of oil used during frying to avoid excessive calories; use chili oil in moderation in the dipping sauce to maintain its low-fat characteristics.
