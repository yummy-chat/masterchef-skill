# Tomato Broth Vegetable and Shrimp Hot Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 Tomato, appropriate amount (diced, can substitute with tomato paste)
🍄 Enoki mushrooms, half bunch (trimmed roots and washed)
🥬 Napa cabbage, half head (cut into sections)
🍤 Shrimp or shrimp paste, appropriate amount
🧈 Silken tofu, 1 box
🥚 Egg, 1
🥫 Tomato paste, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍳 Stir-fry pan or soup pot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Glass bowl

## Instructions
1. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the pot and heat it. Add the diced tomatoes and stir-fry until soft and juicy.
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry thoroughly to release the tomato's natural sweet and sour flavor.
2. Add the tomato paste and stir-fry quickly until evenly combined.
   **Key Point**: Tomato paste has a rich flavor and can enhance the taste profile.
3. Add an appropriate amount of water and bring to a boil over high heat. Adjust the water amount based on the desired soup volume.
4. Once the soup is boiling, add the napa cabbage sections, enoki mushrooms, and shrimp in order. Finally, carefully add the silken tofu using a soup ladle.
   **Key Point**: Add ingredients in order of cooking resistance. Handle the tofu gently to prevent it from breaking apart.
5. Crack the egg directly into the soup, keeping the yolk intact. Do not whisk it in advance.
6. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for about 3 minutes until the egg reaches the desired doneness.
   **Key Point**: Control the cooking time to achieve a runny yolk. The exact time may need adjustment based on the heat level.
7. Season with an appropriate amount of salt according to taste, then remove from the pot. The seasoning is simple, relying mainly on the tomato flavor. Chili can be added as desired.

## Health Advice
This tomato broth vegetable and shrimp hot pot is rich in lycopene, dietary fiber, and high-quality protein. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or seeking a light and warming meal. Note that shrimp and tofu should not be cooked for too long to prevent the shrimp from becoming tough and the tofu from breaking apart.
