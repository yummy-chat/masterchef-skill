# Dark Gold Mooncake 🖤✨
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍯 Golden syrup 250g
🥜 Peanut oil 90g
💧 Lye water 8g
⚫ Black rice flour 200g (pure black rice flour)
🌾 Plain flour 220g (all-purpose flour)
🌰 Jujube and walnut filling as needed (or red bean paste, pineapple filling — store-bought or homemade)
🥚 Pure egg yolk liquid as needed (for brushing, no water or oil added)

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Hand whisk
🍴 Spatula
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🌸 Mooncake mould
📥 Baking tray
🖌️ Pastry brush
💦 Spray bottle
🔥 Oven

## Instructions
1. Pour golden syrup, peanut oil, and lye water into a glass bowl. Whisk until fully combined; the mixture should flow smoothly when lifted.
2. Add black rice flour and plain flour. First, fold with a spatula into a crumbly texture, then knead by hand into a smooth, fine, and glossy dough.
3. Wrap the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
4. Divide the rested dough according to mould size: 30g of dough for a 75g mould, 20g of dough for a 50g mould.
5. Take one portion of dough, press into a round disc, wrap in filling, and push the dough upward with the tiger mouth to seal tightly.
6. Place the sealed side down into the mooncake mould, press to shape, and arrange on a baking tray.
7. Evenly spray a fine mist of water over the raw mooncakes. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 6–7 minutes, until the surface is set and patterns are clear.
**Note**: Remove and cool for 5–8 minutes, allowing the surface to firm up slightly before egg wash.
8. Dip a brush into pure egg yolk liquid, scraping off excess against the bowl edge repeatedly. Apply only an extremely thin layer of egg wash to the raised patterns and edges of the mooncakes.
**Key**: The egg wash must be thin and applied only locally to avoid blurring the patterns; pure yolk gives the best dark gold sheen.
9. Return the egg-washed mooncakes to the oven, reduce temperature to 140–160°C, and continue baking for about 15 minutes, until fully cooked.
**Note**: Do not use too high a temperature, or the crust will turn bronze rather than dark gold; a rich rice aroma will be released during baking.
10. Allow to cool completely before serving. The crust carries a black rice fragrance, with reduced oil and sugar, suitable for home preparation.

## Health Advice
Dark gold mooncakes replace part of the flour with black rice flour, reducing oil and sugar while increasing dietary fibre. They suit those who seek health without sacrificing festive tradition. Best enjoyed with plain tea; avoid eating on an empty stomach or in excess, especially for those with excess stomach acid.
