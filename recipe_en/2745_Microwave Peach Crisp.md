# Microwave Peach Crisp
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork leaf lard, as needed (for rendering lard)  
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (adjust based on feel)  
🥚 1 egg (yolk only)  
🌱 Black sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
♨️ Microwave  
🥣 Ceramic bowl (with lid)  
🥄 Strainer  
🫗 Small milk pitcher (spouted container)  
🥘 Stainless steel bowl  
📜 Parchment paper  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions
1. Cut the pork leaf lard into strips and place in a lidded ceramic bowl. Heat in the microwave until the lard is completely melted and the cracklings separate.  
   **Key**: Watch closely to prevent splattering or burning.  
2. Allow the lard to cool slightly, then strain through a strainer to remove cracklings and reserve pure lard.  
3. Pour the all-purpose flour into a stainless steel bowl, add the rendered lard, and rub with your hands until the mixture resembles coarse sand.  
4. Make a well in the center of the flour, add one egg yolk, and knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.  
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of lard based on the flour's absorbency.  
5. Divide the dough evenly into small portions. Roll each into a ball, then place in your palm and press the center with your thumb to form a round disc with a sunken middle and slightly thicker edges.  
   **Key**: Avoid obvious cracks on the edges, and maintain even thickness.  
6. Line the microwave turntable with parchment paper, place the dough discs on it, and sprinkle black sesame seeds into the center depression.  
7. Microwave until the surface turns golden brown, the edges show slight cracks, and the texture firms up.  
   **Key**: Watch closely throughout to avoid overcooking.  
8. Let cool slightly before serving. Pairs well with hot milk.  

## Health Advice
Traditional peach crisp is made with lard and is high in fat. It is recommended to eat 1–2 pieces at a time, paired with unsweetened tea to cut the grease. This microwave version eliminates the need for an oven, making it suitable for quick preparation by kitchen beginners. Note that microwave heating should be done in short bursts with frequent checks to avoid burning.
