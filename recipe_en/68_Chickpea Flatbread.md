# Chickpea Flatbread
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧂 **Ingredients**  
- 🫘 Dried chickpeas 200g  
- 🌾 All-purpose flour 250g  
- 🥛 Yogurt 60ml  
- 🧅 Onions 2  
- 🍅 Tomatoes 3  
- 🫚 Ginger 10g  
- 🧄 Garlic 4 cloves  
- 🌶️ Green chili peppers 2  
- 🌿 Cilantro to taste  
- 🍵 Black tea bag 1  
- 🫒 Cooking oil (30ml for stir-frying, 15ml for kneading dough, 500ml for frying flatbread)  
- Warm water about 60ml  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Turmeric powder 3g  
- Red chili powder 5g  
- Cilantro powder 8g  
- Cumin powder 5g  
- Garam masala 5g  
- Pomegranate powder 3g (or substitute with 🍋 lemon juice 15ml)  
- 🧂 Salt 6g  
- Baking soda 2g  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- Wok  
- Deep pot (for frying flatbread)  
- Large bowl  
- Slotted spoon  
- Kitchen paper  

## Cooking Steps

1. Soak the dried chickpeas in water overnight until they expand to about twice their original size.
2. Drain the soaked chickpeas, place them in a pot, add 500ml water and 1 black tea bag, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 25–30 minutes until the chickpeas can be easily mashed with your fingers. Remove the tea bag and reserve the chickpea cooking water.
3. Pour 30ml cooking oil into a wok over medium heat, add chopped onions and stir-fry until golden brown, then add grated ginger and minced garlic and stir-fry for 1 minute.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and stir-fry until fully softened and oil separates, then add turmeric powder 3g, red chili powder 5g, cilantro powder 8g, cumin powder 5g, salt 6g, and stir-fry for 2 minutes to fully release the aroma of the spices.
5. Pour the cooked chickpeas along with the cooking water into the wok, add green chili peppers, and mix well. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes, using the back of a spatula to mash some of the chickpeas to thicken the sauce.
6. Add garam masala 5g and pomegranate powder 3g, stir well, then turn off the heat and set aside.
7. In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour 250g, baking soda 2g, salt 3g. Add yogurt 60ml and cooking oil 15ml, then gradually add warm water and knead into a smooth and soft dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
8. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions. Take one portion, lightly oil your hands, and roll it out into a round flatbread about 15cm in diameter and 3mm thick.
9. Pour 500ml cooking oil into a deep pot and heat over medium-high heat to about 180°C. Gently slide the flatbread into the hot oil. Use a slotted spoon to gently press it down so it puffs up. Fry for 1–2 minutes, then flip and fry until both sides are golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil on kitchen paper.
10. Reheat the chickpea curry and serve the flatbread hot alongside it.

## Health Advice  
Chickpeas are rich in dietary fiber and plant protein. This dish provides long-lasting satiety and is suitable for vegetarians or those who need to control blood sugar. The fried flatbread is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with a refreshing salad for balance.
