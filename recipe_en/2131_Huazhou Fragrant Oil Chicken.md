# Huazhou Fragrant Oil Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken (recommend capon) 1, about 4-5 jin
🫚 Ginger, about 2 pieces
🧅 Red shallots, about 10 pieces
🧅 Dried onions/spring shallots, about 20 pieces
🐚 Small conpoy (dried scallops), about 5 qian
🌿 Cilantro (coriander), about 3 liang (roots attached, washed)
⭐ Star anise, 2 pieces
🍃 Bay leaves, about 2 liang (adjust for household use)
🌱 Chinese chives, about 1 liang
🧄 Sand ginger (Kaempferia galanga), about 2 liang
🥬 Scallions/spring onions, about 2 liang
🥜 Artisanal pressed peanut oil, sufficient quantity
🧂 Salt, 1 jin 2 liang
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic cloves, 5-6 cloves (crushed)
🌰 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Wok
🍲 Large stockpot (deep bucket pot)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🥄 Soup ladle/cooking spatula
🥢 Bamboo skewers/chopsticks
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the whole chicken thoroughly, remove internal organs and any impurities. Select a large chicken, about 4-5 jin.
2. Cut ginger into pieces. Peel red shallots and dried onions. Wash cilantro, Chinese chives, and scallions. Crush the sand ginger.
3. Prepare a sufficient amount of artisanal pressed peanut oil. This oil is irreplaceable and is the core of the flavor.
4. Prepare the fragrant oil: Pour a sufficient amount of peanut oil into a wok. Add ginger pieces, red shallots, dried onions, cilantro, Chinese chives, scallions, sand ginger, star anise, and bay leaves. Fry over low heat for about half an hour.
**Key Point**: Use low heat throughout to avoid burning. Once all the aromatics are dry and have turned a dark color, and the oil has turned emerald green with a hint of gold, it is ready.
5. Strain the fragrant oil using a strainer, discard the aromatics, and set the pure fragrant oil aside.
6. Prepare the poaching broth: Place about 15 jin of water in a large stockpot. Add ginger, scallions, small conpoy, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, maintaining a state where the surface is "barely simmering."
7. Poach the chicken: Hold the chicken by the neck and "dip" it into the broth three times to equalize the temperature inside and outside the cavity. Then place the chicken in the pot and poach for 50-55 minutes, maintaining a bare simmer.
**Notes**: The water must not come to a rolling boil, otherwise the skin will break and the meat will become tough. To check doneness: Insert a bamboo skewer into the thickest part of the thigh; if the juices run clear, it is cooked through.
8. Remove the chicken and let it cool naturally until it is no longer hot to the touch. The skin will tighten, resulting in a crisper and smoother texture. Ensure the skin remains intact.
9. Once cooled, chop the chicken into even pieces, keeping the skin attached and the pieces neatly shaped. Arrange on a serving plate.
10. Generously drizzle the chicken with the prepared fragrant oil and an appropriate amount of light soy sauce. Sprinkle with crushed garlic cloves, fresh cilantro, and white sesame seeds. Ensure the chicken is well coated with the oil and sauce. Serve.

## Health Advice
This Huazhou Fragrant Oil Chicken is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose weight. However, it uses a significant amount of fragrant oil and soy sauce, so individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume it in moderation. Be sure to fry the aromatics over low heat throughout to avoid burning. When poaching the chicken, carefully control the heat and maintain a bare simmer to ensure the skin remains intact and the meat is tender and smooth.
