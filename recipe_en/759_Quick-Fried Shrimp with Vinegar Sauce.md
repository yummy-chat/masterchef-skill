# Quick-Fried Shrimp with Vinegar Sauce
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦐 Fresh sea shrimp approximately 6-7 taels (peeled to obtain shrimp meat)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (use the white part, finely minced into scallion rice)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (partly juiced for marinating the shrimp, partly finely minced into ginger rice)
🌾 Corn starch appropriate amount (partly dry starch for batter, part cornstarch for thickening)
🧂 Salt about 1 teaspoon
🍶 Yellow wine appropriate amount (must use yellow wine, cannot be substituted with cooking wine)
🍬 White sugar about 30 grams
🧴 Soy sauce a small amount (mainly for coloring)
🍶 White vinegar about 20 grams
💧 Cornstarch slurry (starch water) appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (need ample oil for stir-frying)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Chopping board
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon/ladle
🥣 Small bowl

## Cooking Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Take fresh sea shrimp, remove the head and shell. Use a knife to make a shallow cut along the back to devein, squeezing out the black intestinal tract.
2. Rinse the prepared shrimp repeatedly until the water runs clear. Then pat dry the surface and interior thoroughly with a kitchen paper towel.
**Key**: Must thoroughly absorb the moisture; this is crucial for the batter to adhere and for the shrimp to achieve a crisp and tender texture.
3. Finely mince the scallion white part and the ginger into "scallion rice" and "ginger rice". Prepare another piece of ginger, squeeze it by hand to extract the juice.
4. Add salt, ginger juice, and yellow wine to the patted-dry shrimp. Mix well and marinate for a short while.
**Key**: Must use yellow wine, not cooking wine, to remove the fishy smell and impart a base flavor.
5. Take dry corn starch and add water. Mix by hand into a thick but pourable batter.
**Key**: The consistency of the batter should be "able to flow down" – too thin and it won't stick, too thick and it will be heavy.
6. In a bowl, mix water, scallion rice, ginger rice, salt, white sugar, a little soy sauce, and white vinegar to make a sauce mixture. In another small bowl, mix cornstarch with water to form a starch slurry.
**Key**: The taste of the sauce mixture should be "slightly sour and slightly sweet"; soy sauce is only for coloring.

### Cooking Phase
1. Pour the prepared starch batter into the marinated shrimp. Mix well by hand, ensuring each shrimp is evenly coated with a thin layer of batter.
**State Check**: The amount of batter should be moderate; after mixing, the shrimp should be "faintly visible", in a state "between a coating and a batter".
   **Key**: This is one of the origins of the name "quick-fried" (抓炒); the batter should not be too thick. After battering, a little cold oil can be added to prevent drying out.
2. Pour ample oil into the wok and heat to about 40% of its maximum temperature. Add the battered shrimp one by one. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until they are set, float to the surface, show a slight yellow color on the outside, and are just cooked inside.
**State Check**: The shrimp float, and their outer shell is a light golden yellow and slightly firm.
   **Key**: This is not a crispy-fried dish; no need for double frying. The surface is slightly crispy, and the inside is tender and fresh, fundamentally different from crispy-fried dishes like "Sweet and Sour Pork Tenderloin”.
3. Remove the stir-fried shrimp and drain the oil. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, pour in the prepared sauce mixture, and bring to a boil over high heat until the sauce thickens and bubbles. Immediately add the stir-fried shrimp and a little bright oil. Stir-fry quickly and evenly, ensuring the entire process does not exceed 30 seconds, so the sauce evenly coats the shrimp.
**State Check**: The sauce is thick and glossy, tightly clinging to each shrimp.
   **Key**: The action must be quick to ensure the shrimp's outer shell does not soften. The finished dish is golden and bright in color, slightly crispy on the outside, soft and tender on the inside. The taste is slightly sour and slightly sweet, typical of the "small lychee" flavor profile. The shrimp are plump, with no large pieces of scallion or ginger visible.

## Health Advice
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for muscle building and weight loss. However, the batter and stir-frying process increase the calorie count, so oil consumption should be monitored. Ginger juice and yellow wine help remove the fishy smell and enhance freshness, aiding digestion. It is recommended to pair this dish with vegetables for a balanced diet.
