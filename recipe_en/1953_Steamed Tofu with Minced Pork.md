# Steamed Tofu with Minced Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Soft tofu, 1 block
🥚 Eggs, 4
🥩 Ground pork, appropriate amount (70% lean, 30% fat from the front leg)
🧅 Scallions, chopped, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, 1 tablespoon
🌶️ White pepper, appropriate amount
🧈 Lard, appropriate amount
🫙 Spicy sauce, 1 tablespoon (can substitute with doubanjiang)
🍶 Yellow wine, appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount
🌽 Cornstarch, a little more than half a tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Plate (shallow)
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl

## Cooking Steps
1.  Place the soft tofu in a bowl, break it up with your hands and try to crush it into a fine, crumbly texture.
2.  In another bowl, beat the four eggs. Pour the beaten eggs into the bowl with the tofu. Add salt, white pepper, and lard. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
    **Key Point**: The tofu should be crushed finely; adding lard enhances the aroma.
3.  Pour the mixed tofu and egg mixture into a shallow plate. Smooth the surface with the back of a spoon.
    **Key Point**: Using a shallow plate allows the tofu to steam more evenly.
4.  Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Place the plate with the tofu-egg mixture inside. Cover the top of the plate with another plate (or tightly with plastic wrap). Put the lid on the steamer. Once the water returns to a boil and steam is rising, steam over medium heat for 10 minutes.
    **Key Point**: Covering the plate prevents condensation from dripping onto the surface, which would affect setting and appearance.
5.  While the tofu is steaming, heat oil in a separate wok. Add the ground pork and stir-fry over medium heat until the meat changes color and excess fat renders out.
    **Doneness Check**: The pork should be broken up, its color turning white, with clear oil visible in the wok.
6.  Add the minced ginger, garlic, and scallions along with the spicy sauce. Stir-fry until fragrant and the oil turns red.
7.  Splash in the yellow wine and light soy sauce. Stir-fry until aromatic.
8.  Add one bowl of water. Season with a little salt, sugar, MSG, white pepper, and a touch of dark soy sauce. Simmer over medium heat until the sauce reduces and thickens.
9.  Prepare the cornstarch slurry: Dissolve the cornstarch in a small amount of cold water. Before adding, stir the slurry again. Slowly pour it into the sauce in a thin stream, stirring constantly. Add it in batches, observing the thickness of the sauce. Stop when the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Turn off the heat.
    **Key Point**: Do not add the cornstarch slurry all at once. Add it gradually, checking the consistency.
10. Remove the steamed tofu custard from the steamer. Uncover the plate. Evenly pour the minced pork sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve.

## Health Tips
This steamed tofu with minced pork is soft, tender, and savory. The addition of eggs and lard provides calcium and is gentle on the stomach, making it especially suitable for the elderly and children. Be mindful of the amount of spicy sauce and salt used. It can be consumed in moderation even during a weight-loss period.
