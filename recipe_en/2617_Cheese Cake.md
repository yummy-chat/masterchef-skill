# Cheese Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 Pure milk 1680g  
🍚 Fermented rice wine juice about 150g (strained)  
🫘 Red bean paste 130g (rolled into 13 balls of about 10g each)  
🍬 White sugar 80g  
🍶 White vinegar 70g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Milk pot  
🥢 Spatula or spoon  
🧵 Strainer  
🧻 Cheesecloth  
🧁 Mooncake or pastry mold  

## Steps  
1. Pour the fermented rice wine into a strainer and press to extract the juice, collecting about 150g of juice. Roll the red bean paste into a log, divide into 13 small portions, and roll into balls. Set aside.  
2. Place the pure milk and white sugar in a milk pot. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring continuously, until it comes to a boil. Immediately turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Continuous stirring prevents the milk from scorching.  
3. With the heat off, pour in the fermented rice wine juice and stir constantly for about 3 minutes to fully incorporate the flavor into the milk.  
4. Allow the milk to cool to about 60°C, then slowly pour in the white vinegar while stirring.  
   **Key point**: The white vinegar will cause the milk proteins to coagulate, resulting in a curdled, separated mixture.  
5. Pour the mixture into a bowl lined with cheesecloth. Gather the cheesecloth, twist it tightly, and squeeze out the excess whey, leaving behind the solid cheese curds.  
6. Place the cheese curds on a work surface and knead, press, and fold them for about 10 minutes, until the texture changes from crumbly and broken to smooth, sticky, and easily formed into a cohesive ball without falling apart.  
   **Note**: If too dry, add back a small amount of the squeezed-out whey to adjust.  
7. Divide the kneaded cheese mixture into portions of about 20g each. Roll each into a ball and then flatten into a disc.  
8. Place one red bean paste filling in the center of a cheese disc. Use your thumb and index finger to slowly gather the edges, sealing the filling inside. Pinch the seam tightly, then roll it into a smooth ball.  
9. Place in a mold and press to imprint the pattern. Remove from the mold.  
   **Anti-stick tip**: If the mold sticks, dip it in a little whey to prevent sticking.  
10. Finished. Can be eaten directly.

## Health Advice
Homemade cheese cake transforms nearly expired milk into a protein-rich dessert, enhanced with fermented rice wine juice for added flavor. Suitable for dairy lovers or those managing sugar intake. Avoid using too much white vinegar to prevent excessive sourness; the red bean paste filling is relatively high in sugar, so consume in moderation.
