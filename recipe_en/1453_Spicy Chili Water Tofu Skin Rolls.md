# Spicy Chili Water Tofu Skin Rolls
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Dried tofu skin (thin type, cut in half from the middle and set aside)  
🥩 Pork belly 300g (alternating fat and lean, with slightly higher fat ratio)  
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, finely chopped, as needed  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 20g (two heaping spoonfuls, must be fine powder, not coarse chili flakes)  
🧂 Spicy and numbing seasoning (Mala Xian) 5g (half a spoon)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g (two spoons)  
🍗 Chicken bouillon 8g (half a spoon, can substitute with chicken powder or chicken essence)  
🍬 White sugar 6g (two full teaspoons, reduce to 3g if sensitive to sweetness)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 8g (half a spoon)  
🧂 MSG 5g (two level teaspoons)  
🌱 Cumin 3g (one tip of a spoon)  
🤍 White sesame seeds 3g (one tip of a spoon)  
💧 Warm water 350g (cooled to 70–80°C before use)  
🧂 BBQ seasoning, a pinch (optional, sprinkle over the chili water)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (spray on the pan bottom and tofu skin surface during frying)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing the chili water sauce)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for weighing seasonings)  
🍳 Iron wok (for rendering pork lard and cracklings, can season the pan)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing cracklings)  
🍳 Frying pan (for frying the tofu skin rolls)  
🔫 Oil spray bottle (for spraying oil)  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board (for ingredient preparation)  
🥢 Tongs (for handling and rolling the tofu skin)  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prep Work  
1. Prepare chili water: In a large bowl, successively add fine chili powder, spicy and numbing seasoning, light soy sauce, chicken bouillon, sugar, oyster sauce, MSG, cumin, and white sesame seeds in the given proportions. Then pour in 350g of warm water cooled to 70–80°C. Stir thoroughly until completely dissolved with no lumps. Taste and adjust saltiness; it should be close to the saltiness of everyday stir-fried dishes, not overly salty.  
2. Cut the dried tofu skin in half from the middle, then further cut into pieces suitable for the size of your frying pan.  
3. Finely chop the scallions and cilantro separately and set aside in bowls.  
4. Render pork cracklings: Cut the pork belly into thick slices, then dice into roughly 1 cm cubes. Place the pork cubes in a cold iron wok without adding oil. First, stir-fry over maximum heat until the cubes release oil and the surface turns slightly yellow. Then reduce to medium heat and stir-fry to render out excess fat. When you feel there is still fat inside the cubes, reduce to the lowest heat and continue rendering thoroughly to prevent burning and bitterness. Turn off the heat when the cracklings darken in color and no obvious plump fat remains. Remove with a slotted spoon. The rendered lard in the wok can be stored in a sealed container. Once the cracklings are cool enough to handle, chop them finely and set aside.  

### Frying and Rolling  
5. Spray a layer of cooking oil onto the frying pan. Place one piece of dried tofu skin in the pan and spray oil onto its surface as well. Maintain medium-low heat throughout, and heat until the tofu skin begins to bubble.  
6. Evenly drizzle 1.5–2 spoonfuls of chili water over the tofu skin. Optionally, sprinkle a little BBQ seasoning on top. Quickly layer on the chopped cracklings, scallions, and cilantro in order.  
7. When you see the chili water starting to bubble as well, immediately use tongs to roll up the tofu skin tightly into a wrapped bundle.  
8. For each new piece, spray oil again on the pan bottom.  
9. After rolling, do a quick adjustment and remove from the pan. Serve immediately while hot. When bitten into, the chili water will burst in your mouth, with rich fillings and a complex, intense flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and fat, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should control their intake. The combination of pork cracklings and dried tofu skin requires attention to overall calorie consumption.
