# Sauced Lamb Spine (Lamb Backbone)
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍖 Lamb Spine (Lamb Backbone) about 3 pounds (approx. 12 pieces), soaked in water for about 6 hours in advance to remove blood
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (whole cloves)
💧 Mineral Water about 5 liters (for simmering and preparing sauce)

**Spices**
🌿 Chinese Angelica Root appropriate amount
🌿 Galangal appropriate amount
⚪ White Peppercorns appropriate amount (key for removing gamey flavor and reducing greasiness)
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers a few
🌿 Sichuan Peppercorns a pinch

**Seasonings**
🫘 Special Mixed Sauce appropriate amount (mainly Yellow Bean Sauce + Oyster Sauce + a small amount of Chu Hou Sauce)
🍶 Cooking Wine about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Cooking Oil appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stockpot (for blanching)
🥄 Slotted Spoon (for skimming foam)
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying sauce)
♨️ Electric Pressure Cooker or Pressure Cooker (for simmering)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce)

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the lamb spine in clean water for about 6 hours in advance to fully release the blood.
2. Prepare the five spices: Chinese Angelica Root, Galangal, White Peppercorns, Dried Chili Peppers, and Sichuan Peppercorns. Mix Yellow Bean Sauce, Oyster Sauce, and a small amount of Chu Hou Sauce evenly to make the special sauce; set aside.
3. Place the lamb spine in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Key Point**: Continuously skim off the foam with a slotted spoon. After skimming, add one tablespoon of cooking wine, bring to a boil again to let the alcohol evaporate for better deodorization. Blanch for about 30 to 40 minutes.
4. Remove the lamb spine, rinse clean with hot water to remove any remaining blood foam and impurities on the surface, ensuring the broth is clean.
5. Put the cleaned lamb spine and the five spices into the inner pot of the electric pressure cooker. Pour in enough mineral water to submerge the ingredients.
   **Key Point**: Water amount is about 5 liters. Cover the lid and cook for 30 minutes until the lamb spine is fall-off-the-bone tender.
6. In a separate wok, add oil, then add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic cloves. Fry over low heat until golden brown and fragrant.
7. Pour in the pre-mixed special sauce, stir-fry quickly and evenly until the sauce aroma is released.
8. Add a small amount of mineral water to the stir-fried sauce, stir well, and bring to a boil to form a rich sauce.
9. Pour all the prepared sauce into the pot with the lamb spine that has been simmering for 30 minutes, and mix well. Optionally, continue simmering for a short while based on personal taste to enhance the flavor.

## Health Suggestion
Lamb spine is rich in collagen and calcium, making it suitable for winter nourishment and joint care. However, it is high in purines, so people with high uric acid levels should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with white radish or leafy greens to aid digestion and reduce greasiness.
