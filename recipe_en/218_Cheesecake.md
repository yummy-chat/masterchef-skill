# Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 4 eggs (about 180g, usually 3 large eggs suffice)  
🧀 100g homemade cheese  
🧈 30g butter (or a light-flavored cooking oil as substitute)  
🥛 50g water or whole milk  
🌾 30g cake flour  
🍋 A few drops of white vinegar or lemon juice  
🍬 50g white sugar  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 A grease-free, water-free glass bowl (for egg whites)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing egg yolk batter and meringue)  
🥄 Hand whisk  
⚡ Electric hand mixer  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🌀 Round cake pan  
🍲 Deep baking pan  
🪜 Wire rack  
🔥 Oven  
🥛 A cup (for assisting unmolding)  

## Instructions
1. Separate the egg yolks from the egg whites. Place the egg whites in a grease-free, water-free bowl, and place the egg yolks in another bowl.
2. Prepare the egg yolk batter: Add 100g of homemade cheese to the egg yolks and stir to break it up, then gradually add the egg yolks while mixing until well combined.
3. Add 30g of butter and mix until smooth. Then add 50g of water or whole milk and stir to combine.
4. Sift in 30g of cake flour. Use a "Z-shaped" motion to stir until no dry flour remains. Do not stir in circles to prevent the flour from developing gluten and affecting the texture.
5. Prepare the meringue: Add a few drops of white vinegar or lemon juice to the egg whites. Gradually add 50g of white sugar in three batches. Use an electric hand mixer to whip until soft peaks form when the mixer is lifted. Do not overwhip to a stiff peak, as this may cause cracking during baking.
6. Gradually add the whipped meringue to the egg yolk batter in batches. Use a silicone spatula to fold from the bottom upwards until evenly combined. Ensure each batch is fully incorporated before adding the next. Do not stir in circles to prevent deflating. Mix until the batter is uniform in color and smooth in texture.
7. Grease the inner wall of the cake pan with a thin layer of oil, then pour the batter into the pan.
8. Fill a deep baking pan halfway with water, place a wire rack in it, and set the cake pan on the rack. Preheat the oven to 120°C for 15 minutes. Place the baking pan on the lower rack of the oven and bake at 120°C for 90 minutes, then increase the temperature to 160°C and bake for another 10 minutes to brown the top.
9. Once the baking time is up, do not remove the cake immediately. Leave it in the oven for an additional 15 minutes, then take it out.
10. Remove the cake, gently tap the pan to release hot air. After cooling, place the pan on a cup and gently press down on the outer edge of the pan to unmold.
11. For the best texture, refrigerate for at least 3 hours before serving. It will have a mousse-like consistency.

## Health Advice  
This cheesecake uses a water bath method and homemade cheese, resulting in a light texture and low sugar content, making it suitable for those watching their calorie intake. After refrigeration, the texture resembles mousse, but it should be consumed in moderation as butter and cheese still contain relatively high calories.
