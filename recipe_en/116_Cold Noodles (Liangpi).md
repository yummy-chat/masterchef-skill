# Cold Noodles (Liangpi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Pure sweet potato starch, about 200-300 grams (one bowl)  
💧 Cold water, equal to the amount of starch (for making starch slurry)  
🥒 Cucumber, appropriate amount (shredded)  
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (shredded)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🥜 Peanuts, appropriate amount  
🧂 Table salt, 10 grams (dissolved in a small amount of warm water to make salted water)  
💧 Warm water, a small amount (for dissolving salt)  
🌶️ Chili oil, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🍇 Vinegar, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🥄 Soy sauce, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🧄 Garlic water, appropriate amount (added at the end)  
🥜 Sesame paste, appropriate amount (added at the end)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Large bowl (for mixing starch slurry)  
🥄 Small spoon (for measuring salt and starch)  
🧽 50-mesh stainless steel fine strainer (for filtering starch slurry)  
🖌️ Brush (for brushing oil)  
🥣 Small bowl (for holding oil)  
🍳 Large iron wok (for boiling water)  
🫕 Stainless steel flat-bottomed pans, 2-3 pieces (for steaming cold noodles)  
🤲 Scraper (for lifting cold noodles)  
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board (for cutting into strips)  
🥢 Large bowl and chopsticks (for mixing)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Dissolve 10 grams of salt in a small amount of warm water to make salted water and set aside.  
**Key point**: The salt must be dissolved in advance before adding to the starch slurry; dry salt is difficult to dissolve and unevenly distributed.  
2. Take a bowl of pure sweet potato starch, add an equal amount of cold water, let it soak for 5 minutes, then stir to break up any lumps. Use a 50-mesh fine strainer to filter the starch slurry into another bowl.  
**Key point**: Use only pure sweet potato starch; filtering makes the cold noodles smoother.  
3. Slowly pour the prepared salted water into the filtered starch slurry and stir well.  
**Key point**: The salted water increases the elasticity of the cold noodles. Optionally, add a little alum.  
4. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the inner surface of a stainless steel flat-bottomed pan, then float the pan on the boiling water to preheat.  
**Key point**: Fully preheating the container helps the starch slurry set quickly.  
5. Ladle a spoonful of starch slurry into the preheated pan, quickly rotate the pan to evenly spread the slurry over the bottom. Steam over high heat until the slurry changes from white to transparent and large bubbles form on the bottom, indicating it is cooked.  
6. Remove the steamed pan and place it in a basin of cold water for 1-2 minutes. When the cold noodle naturally separates from the pan wall, gently peel off the entire sheet.  
**Key point**: After cooling, the cold noodle will separate on its own; no need to scrape the edges with tools.  
7. Cut the cold noodle into long strips, add shredded cucumber, shredded carrot, cilantro, and peanuts, then season to taste with chili oil, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic water, and sesame paste. Mix well and serve.  
**Key point**: The amount of seasonings can be adjusted according to personal preference.  

## Health Advice
Sweet potato cold noodles are low in fat and gluten-free, making them suitable for people managing blood sugar or those with gluten sensitivity. However, they are high in starch, so it is recommended to pair them with plenty of vegetables and protein (such as chicken breast) for a balanced meal. When steaming, ensure the slurry is spread evenly to avoid uneven thickness affecting the texture.
