# Cranberry Cookies
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Butter 280g (softened to room temperature in advance)
🍚 Powdered sugar 170g
🥚 Eggs about 70g (about 2, beaten into egg liquid)
🌾 Cake flour 400g
🍒 Dried cranberries 140g (chopped into small pieces, can be substituted with raisins)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🔌 Electric hand mixer
🔪 Spatula
🔪 Cutting board and knife
📦 Plastic wrap
🧊 Square baking pan or tray (for shaping and refrigerating)
🍳 Electric baking pan or flat-bottom pan (for use without an oven)
🔥 Oven and baking sheet
🧻 Parchment paper (baking paper)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the softened butter into small pieces, place in a glass bowl, add powdered sugar, and use a spatula to press and fold until smooth butter-sugar paste.
   **Key Point**: Butter should be softened until a finger can easily press an indentation; if too hard it is difficult to mix, if too soft it may separate; using powdered sugar makes mixing easier and gives a finer texture.
2. Add the beaten eggs to the butter-sugar paste in batches, beating thoroughly with an electric hand mixer after each addition until fully emulsified.
   **Key Point**: The egg liquid must be added in batches to avoid separation.
3. Sift in the cake flour, and continue mixing with a spatula using pressing and folding motions until no dry flour remains and a dough forms.
   **Key Point**: Do not stir in circles to prevent developing gluten, which would affect the crumbly texture.
4. Add the chopped dried cranberries to the dough and knead evenly by hand.
5. Lay a piece of plastic wrap on a baking sheet, place the dough on it, and use the plastic wrap to help shape it into a neat rectangular log. Wrap tightly and press firmly.
   **Key Point**: A scraper can help press the shape into a neat form; other dried fruits like raisins can be used as alternatives.
6. Refrigerate the wrapped dough for at least 2 hours, or freeze for 1 hour, until the dough is firm and easy to slice.
7. Remove the set dough, peel off the plastic wrap, trim off uneven edges, and cut into uniform slices about 0.5 cm thick.
8. Bake the cookies:
   - **Flat-bottom pan / electric baking pan method**: Preheat the electric baking pan or flat-bottom pan over medium-low heat, place the cookie slices, cover, and cook for about 10 minutes until the bottom is light golden. Flip and cook for another 6 minutes until both sides are evenly golden brown.
   - **Oven method**: Preheat the oven to 170°C (340°F) for 10 minutes. Arrange the cookie slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, place in the middle rack, and bake at 170°C (340°F) for about 20 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: Both sides of the cookies should be uniformly golden brown.

## Health Advice
This cranberry cookie recipe contains relatively high amounts of butter and powdered sugar, making it suitable for occasional afternoon tea snacks but not for frequent consumption. People with diabetes and those on weight management should control portion sizes and pair with unsweetened tea for a healthier option.
