# Shaanxi Oil-Splashed Chili Oil
Cuisine: Shaanxi cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Qin chili (thread pepper) 120g (remove chili stems, cut into small pieces, keep the chili seeds)
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (cut into chunks, used for frying to remove odor)
🟢 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections, used for frying to remove odor)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced, used for frying to remove odor)
🌿 Small celery appropriate amount (used for frying to remove odor)
🫗 Rapeseed oil about 500g (for greasing the pan and making seasoned oil)
🧂 Salt 12g
✨ Five-spice powder (or thirteen-spice powder) 3g (use sparingly, for enhancing aroma and reducing spiciness)
🍯 A little balsamic vinegar
🤍 White sesame seeds 20g (used in two stages: add some when frying chili peppers, sprinkle the remaining peeled white sesame seeds before splashing the oil)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥄 Wok spatula or iron spoon
🥄 Strainer
🫗 Stone mortar (or mill)
🥣 Bowl (wide bowl, for holding chili powder and the finished product)
🍽️ Plate (for spreading chili to cool and covering the bowl during the resting step)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Choose Shaanxi Qin chilies with strong fragrance, good color, and mild heat. Remove the chili stems, cut into small pieces, and keep the chili seeds.
**Key**: Keeping the chili seeds makes the finished product more mellow.

2.  Cut the onion into chunks, scallions into sections, ginger into slices, and small celery into sections. Set aside.
3.  Heat the wok, add a little rapeseed oil to grease the pan, then add the chili pepper pieces. Stir-fry over low heat the entire time, stirring frequently to prevent burning. When the chili peppers become lighter due to moisture evaporation, add a portion of the white sesame seeds. Continue frying until the sesame seeds change color and are cooked through, then immediately remove from the heat and spread the mixture onto a plate to cool.
**Indicator**: The chili peppers become “lighter as they fry,” the sesame seeds turn color and are cooked, and the chili peppers are crispy.

4.  Put the cooled chili peppers and sesame seeds into a stone mortar. Pound into a moderately fine powder and transfer to a bowl.
5.  Sprinkle 12g of salt, 3g of five-spice powder, and the remaining peeled white sesame seeds onto the chili powder. Mix evenly.
6.  Pour about 500g of rapeseed oil into the wok. Heat over high heat until the oil smokes heavily to eliminate raw flavor, then turn off the heat. When the oil temperature drops slightly, add all the prepared vegetables (be careful of splashing). After the foam on the oil surface reduces, turn on low heat and fry slowly until the vegetables are dry, yellow, and crispy. Use a strainer to remove the vegetables, pressing to squeeze out any remaining oil.
7.  Reheat the seasoned oil to 60-70% hot. Ladle out half of the hot oil and evenly splash it over the chili powder, stirring quickly.
**Key**: This step mainly brings out the fragrant aroma of the chili.

8.  Immediately after, quickly drip a little balsamic vinegar into the bowl. The vinegar will cause the oil to bubble again, unlocking complex aromas.
9.  Immediately pour all the remaining hot oil from the wok into the bowl. Stir thoroughly.
10. Right after splashing the oil, immediately cover the bowl with a plate and let it rest until cool, allowing the flavors to fully meld.
**Tip**: After resting, the oil-splashed chili will have a bright red color and a richer, more concentrated aroma.

## Health Advice
This oil-splashed chili is rich in Vitamin C and capsaicin, which can stimulate appetite and boost metabolism. It is suitable for people with cold and damp constitutions, but those with a sensitive stomach or experiencing excessive internal heat should consume it cautiously. It is recommended to pair with noodles or cold dishes.
