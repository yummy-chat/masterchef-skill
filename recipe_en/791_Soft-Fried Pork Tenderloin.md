# Soft-Fried Pork Tenderloin
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin, about 300g (cut into slices over 3mm thick)  
🥚 Eggs, 5 (use egg whites; must be fresh)  
🧅 Scallion, about 50g (cut into sections and crushed)  
🫚 Ginger, about 20g (sliced and crushed)  
🧂 Cooking wine, about 5g  
🍬 Sugar, about 0.5g  
🧂 MSG, about 1g  
🧂 Salt, about 3g  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (for dusting and batter)  
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn salt, as needed (dipping condiment; made by stir-frying and grinding Sichuan peppercorns and salt)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Whisk (egg beater)  
🍳 Wok  
🌡️ Oil thermometer  
🥢 Skimmer / Slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare scallion-ginger water**: Cut the scallion into sections and slice the ginger. Crush them with the flat side of a knife. Add cooking wine, sugar, MSG, and salt. Squeeze and knead with your hands to extract the juice.  
**Note**: Only use the juice to marinate the meat; discard the scallion and ginger solids to keep the finished texture pure.

2. **Prepare the pork tenderloin**: Cut the pork tenderloin into large slices about 3mm or more thick, roughly 10 slices.  
**Note**: The slices should not be too thin, otherwise they won't be plump enough after frying.

3. **Marinate the meat**: Place the meat slices into the scallion-ginger juice and mix well to marinate.

4. **Whip the egg whites**: Place the 5 egg whites into a clean, grease-free bowl. Whisk rapidly in one direction until stiff peaks form.  
**Note**: The bowl must be free of oil and water, and the eggs must be fresh; otherwise, the whites won't whip properly.

5. **Prepare the egg foam batter**: Gently fold an appropriate amount of cornstarch into the whipped egg whites until evenly combined.  
**Note**: Stir gently to avoid deflating the foam.

6. **Coat the meat**: First, dust each marinated meat slice with a thin layer of dry cornstarch. Then, evenly coat it with the egg foam batter.  
**Note**: Dusting with dry starch before applying the wet batter effectively prevents the coating from falling off.

7. **First frying**: Control the oil temperature at about 100°C. Gently place each coated slice into the oil, using chopsticks to gently separate them and prevent sticking. Fry until set and the color is white. Remove and drain the oil.  
**Key point**: Fry at a low temperature throughout. If the oil is too hot, the egg batter will turn yellow, ruining the soft-fried dish's characteristic white and fluffy texture.

8. **Second frying**: Raise the oil temperature to about 130°C. Add the fried pork slices back to the oil and fry until the color deepens slightly and they puff up and set. Quickly remove them.

9. **Plating**: Plate immediately and serve with Sichuan peppercorn salt for dipping. Best enjoyed hot.

## Health Suggestions  
This soft-fried pork tenderloin uses whipped egg whites for the batter, making it rich in protein. However, as it is deep-fried, the calorie content is high. Pairing it with a vegetable salad is recommended to balance the meal. It is suitable for people who need a high-protein, light-textured dish, but be mindful of the amount of dipping salt to avoid excessive sodium intake.
