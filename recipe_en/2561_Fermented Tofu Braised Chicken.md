# Fermented Tofu Braised Chicken
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Boneless chicken thigh appropriate amount (pat dry surface moisture, cut into small pieces)
🥔 Potato about 1 (peeled or unpeeled, cut into small pieces)
🫑 Green and red bell pepper appropriate amount (for making spice mix)
🌿 Bay leaves several (for making spice mix)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (for making spice mix)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for making spice mix)
🧅 Onion/shallot appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🧅 Scallion/onion sprouts appropriate amount (for final garnish)
🍯 Oyster sauce large amount (ratio to fermented tofu about 2.5:1)
🧀 Fermented tofu one spoon (ratio to oyster sauce about 1:2.5)
🍾 Cooking wine a little (optional, to adjust sauce consistency)
🫒 Cooking oil large amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🌀 Blender/food processor (for making compound spice mix)
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying base and braising)
🥘 Pot (for pre-cooking potato chunks)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce and marinating chicken)
🔪 Knife and cutting board (for cutting ingredients)
🧻 Kitchen paper towel (for drying chicken surface moisture)
🥄 Spatula (for stir-frying ingredients)

## Steps
1. **Make compound spice mix**: Put green and red bell pepper, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and bay leaves into a blender and blend into fine crumbs. Set aside.
2. **Prepare fermented tofu sauce**: Add oyster sauce and fermented tofu to the spice mix, stir until well combined. If the sauce is too thick, add a little cooking wine to adjust the consistency.
3. **Prepare chicken**: Pat the thawed boneless chicken thighs dry with kitchen paper towel, cut into bite-sized pieces.
4. **Prepare aromatics**: Finely chop the onion and garlic.
5. **Prepare side vegetable**: Peel the potato and cut into evenly sized chunks.
6. **Stir-fry base**: Pour enough cooking oil into a wok, heat over medium-low heat. Add minced onion and garlic, stir-fry until fragrant.
7. **Cook chicken**: Add chicken pieces to the wok, add two spoons of the prepared fermented tofu sauce, gently stir-fry until the chicken surface is cooked and changes color.
8. **Marinate chicken**: Transfer all contents from the wok into a bowl, marinate for 30 minutes, occasionally pressing down with a spatula to enhance flavor absorption.
   **Note**: This is a post-marination method, different from the traditional marinate-before-cooking method, resulting in more tender and flavorful chicken.
9. **Pre-cook potatoes**: In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add potato chunks and cook for about 20 minutes until easily pierced with chopsticks.
10. **Stir-fry potatoes**: Drain the cooked potatoes, place them back into the wok used for the chicken. Add one spoon of fermented tofu sauce, stir-fry over medium-low heat until the potatoes are evenly coated.
11. **Combine and braise**: Return the marinated chicken along with any remaining sauce and oil from the bowl to the wok, mix with the potatoes. Ensure all ingredients are coated with sauce. Stir-fry until the chicken is about seventy percent cooked. If it seems too dry, add a little oil or water. Reduce heat to low, cover and braise for 7 minutes.
    **Key point**: The braising time is based on the chicken already being marinated and the potatoes pre-cooked.
12. **Assemble**: Take a large bowl of rice, pour the braised fermented tofu chicken and sauce over the rice. Garnish with chopped scallions. Optionally top with a fried egg cooked using residual heat.

## Health Advice
This fermented tofu braised chicken is rich in savory sauce. The potatoes absorb the broth and are high in carbohydrates, making it suitable for muscle gain or replenishing energy after heavy physical exertion. Those on a low-sodium diet should use less sauce. The post-marination method keeps the chicken tender, and it is recommended to pair with leafy greens to balance dietary fiber.
