# Korean-Style Cheese Fried Chicken Wings

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken wing drumettes 1000g (about 2 lbs, soaked in advance to thaw)  
🍺 Beer 1 can (for marinating, enough to mostly submerge ingredients)  
🥚 Eggs 2  
🌾 Flour as needed (mixed with starch in a 1:1 ratio)  
🌽 Starch as needed (mixed with flour in a 1:1 ratio)  
🧀 Cheese slices 3 slices  
🥛 Milk as needed  
🧈 Butter 1 small piece  
🧅 Onion as needed (cut into sections)  
🧄 Scallion as needed (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger as needed (sliced)  

🌶️ Chili bean paste with red oil 1 tablespoon  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn as needed  
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil a little  
🧂 Salt as needed (for marinating)  
🍬 Sugar as needed  
🧂 Chicken bouillon as needed  
🍶 Light soy sauce as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce as needed  
🌶️ Ground pepper as needed  
🥄 Baking powder a small amount  
🧂 Salt one pinch (for frying powder)  
🌿 Black sesame seeds a little (for garnish)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl / glass bowl  
🥣 Small bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🔥 Portable gas stove  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare chicken wings**: Soak the chicken wing drumettes in cold water until fully thawed, then wash and drain well.
2. **Fry the marinade base oil**: Heat oil in a wok. When the oil temperature rises, turn to low heat, add one tablespoon of chili bean paste with red oil, and stir-fry until the red oil is released, then turn off the heat. Quickly sprinkle in Sichuan peppercorn while the wok is still hot, drizzle in a little Sichuan pepper oil, stir to combine, and set aside to cool.
   **Note**: Use the residual heat to release the aroma of Sichuan peppercorn and Sichuan pepper oil after turning off the heat to avoid burning.
3. **Marinate chicken wings**: Add salt, sugar, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground pepper, the prepared chili bean paste base oil, as well as scallion sections, ginger slices, and onion sections to the chicken wings. Mix thoroughly by hand. Pour in one can of beer, ensuring the ingredients are mostly submerged. Refrigerate and marinate for 2 hours.
4. **Prepare frying powder**: Mix flour and starch in a 1:1 ratio. Add a small amount of baking powder and one pinch of salt. Stir until well combined and set aside.
5. **Prepare batter**: Remove the marinated chicken wings and pour off some of the marinade liquid. Crack in 2 eggs. Add the frying powder gradually, mixing by hand as you add, until the batter flows slowly and evenly coats the chicken wings.
   **Key point**: The batter consistency must be just right—too thin and it won't stick, too dry and the coating will be too thick.
6. **First fry**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat until dense small bubbles form around a chopstick inserted in the oil. Add the chicken wings. Fry until the surface is set, then turn to medium-low heat. Continue frying for about 5 minutes until the chicken wings are cooked through and the surface is light golden. Remove and drain oil.
7. **Optional oil drizzle**: Spoon a ladle of hot oil over the fried chicken wings for extra crispiness.
8. **Make cheese sauce**: In a separate small pot, melt the butter over low heat. Add one tablespoon of flour and stir until well combined. Pour in an appropriate amount of milk and stir over low heat until it comes to a boil. Add 3 slices of cheese. Continue heating on low, stirring constantly, until the sauce is thick, smooth, and flows slowly.
   **Note**: Keep on low heat throughout to prevent the cheese sauce from burning or clumping; the amount of milk can be adjusted based on desired consistency.
9. **Assemble**: Plate the fried chicken wings, drizzle with the prepared cheese sauce, and sprinkle with black sesame seeds for garnish.

## Health Advice  
The cheese sauce paired with fried chicken is high in calories. It is recommended to serve with kimchi to cut the richness. After eating, engage in moderate activity to help with calorie expenditure.
