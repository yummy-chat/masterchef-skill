# All-Purpose Golden and Silver Garlic Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 Garlic, large amount (core ingredient)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, to taste (adds aroma and heat)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (enough to submerge the minced garlic)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste (balances saltiness and enhances umami)
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste (removes odor and adds fragrance)
🧂 Salt, to taste (to adjust saltiness as needed)
💧 Water, to taste (for rinsing minced garlic and adjusting moisture)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Handheld food processor (or garlic mortar)
🥄 Strainer (slotted spoon)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥣 Bowls, mixing bowls
♨️ Air fryer (or oven, microwave)
🖌️ Silicone brush (for brushing oil when grilling oysters)

## Preparation Steps

### Garlic Processing Stage
1. **Prepare the garlic**: Cut off the hard root ends of the garlic cloves, then place the peeled cloves into a food processor and pulse until evenly minced.
   **Note**: Do not smash the garlic with a knife, as this destroys cell structure and releases excessive pungent sulfides, making the garlic too spicy. Chopping directly reduces garlic juice and spiciness.
2. **Rinse and drain**: Place the minced garlic into a strainer, rinse briefly under running water, then firmly squeeze out excess moisture. Choose the next step based on preference:
   - **Retain spiciness**: Use directly after rinsing and squeezing.
   - **Remove spiciness**: Blanch the rinsed minced garlic in boiling water for about 30 seconds, immediately transfer to cold water to cool, then squeeze dry and set aside.
3. **Divide the garlic**: Split the processed minced garlic into two equal portions: one for making golden garlic, the other to keep as silver garlic.
4. **Prepare the "silver garlic"**: Take one portion of minced garlic, add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, white pepper powder, and salt to taste. Mix well and set aside.

### Golden and Silver Garlic Mixing Stage
1. **Fry the "golden garlic"**: Add the other portion of minced garlic to a pan with cold oil. The oil should cover the garlic completely. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally. The garlic will sink initially, then gradually float, with its color changing from white to light yellow to golden. Watch the heat carefully—the flame should not extend beyond the surface of the oil to prevent the edges from burning. Once the garlic is fully floating and has turned golden brown, immediately turn off the heat.
2. **Mix the "golden and silver garlic"**: Right after turning off the heat, pour the seasoned silver garlic into the hot pan with the golden garlic and oil. The residual heat will release the aroma of the silver garlic and seasonings without cooking it further. Stir well to combine. If the minced garlic was thoroughly squeezed dry, no further heating is needed. If there is still excess moisture, return to low heat and stir to evaporate the remaining water.

### Finished Product Application Examples
This garlic sauce is a versatile all-purpose sauce, ideal for dishes such as garlic vermicelli with enoki mushrooms, grilled oysters, and garlic scallops. For different ingredients: when assembling with vermicelli, add a small amount of water to prevent it from drying out and hardening during heating. For ingredients that are not easy to cook, be sure to pre-cook them until half done before adding the garlic sauce, then continue cooking to avoid burning the garlic before the main ingredient is ready. Heating suggestion: air fry at 200°C for about 5 minutes, stirring once halfway for even heating. For a richer, oilier texture, use less water; for a lighter, fresher taste, add a bit more water as needed.

## Health Advice
This garlic sauce uses the golden and silver garlic mixing method, preserving the pungent aroma of raw garlic while adding the caramelized sweetness of cooked garlic. It pairs well with seafood or vegetables. For those with sensitive stomachs, it is recommended to blanch the minced garlic to reduce spiciness, and ensure the oil quantity is sufficient to fully submerge the garlic to prevent burning.
