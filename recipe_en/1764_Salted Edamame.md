# Salted Edamame
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Fresh edamame 2500g (choose plump, fresh pods)
🧂 Table salt 50g (for washing edamame)
🧂 Table salt 100g (for toasting with spices)
⭐ Star anise 10g
🪵 Cinnamon stick 15g
🌱 Green Sichuan pepper 10g
🍃 Bay leaves a few pieces
🌶️ Dried bird's eye chilies 20g
🧪 Edible alkali powder about 2g (1 small spoon)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large stainless steel basin (for washing, marinating, and holding)
🍳 Wok (for toasting spices and boiling)
🥄 Large soup ladle
🥄 Large slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks (for spreading and cooling)
🪵 Cutting board
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Place the edamame in a large basin, add water, and scrub them repeatedly several times to remove surface dirt and sand.
2. In a separate basin of clean water, add the washed edamame and 50g of salt. Scrub with your hands for 2 minutes, then drain well.
   **Note**: Salting and scrubbing helps the edamame cook more easily and absorb flavor better.
3. Prepare the spices: combine 100g salt, star anise, cinnamon stick, green Sichuan pepper, bay leaves, and dried bird's eye chilies in a bowl.
4. Heat the wok over medium-low heat, add the salt and spice mixture, and stir-fry for about 2 minutes until the salt turns into "cooked salt" and the spices become fragrant.
5. Add enough water to the wok, then turn the heat to high and bring to a rolling boil.
6. Once the brine is boiling, add the prepared edamame and immediately add about 2g of edible alkali powder.
   **Key point**: Alkali powder prevents the edamame from turning dark and keeps them bright green.
7. Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 20 minutes, until the edamame are thoroughly cooked and flavorful.
8. Use the slotted spoon to scoop out the edamame and transfer them to a large basin. Immediately spread them out with chopsticks and fan them to cool down.
   **Key point**: Physical cooling helps maintain a bright green color for a longer time.
9. Serve on a plate. They are savory, flavorful, and vibrant green.

## Health Advice
This salted edamame dish is high in protein and low in fat. When braised with spices, it develops a rich flavor, making it ideal as a summer side dish or beer snack. However, note that the salt content is relatively high, so people with high blood pressure or those on a low-sodium diet should consume it in moderation. Edamame are neutral in nature, but those with weak digestion should avoid eating too much at one time.
