# Gate Nail Meat Pie
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫓 All-purpose flour 250g
🐂 Beef 400g (recommend using fatty and lean parts such as ribeye, ribs, or chuck)
🥚 Egg 1
🧅 Scallion 1
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece
🧂 Salt to taste
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (including vegetable oil and sesame oil)
🫘 Dried yellow soybean paste 2 tablespoons
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1/2 teaspoon
🍂 Five-spice powder 1/2 teaspoon
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
🍬 Sugar 2 teaspoons
🍾 Sesame oil 1 tablespoon

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Wooden bowl
🥄 Measuring cup/measuring spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🧤 Ginger grater
🍳 Small milk pan
🥢 Silicone spatula
🍳 Lidded frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks
🫙 Glass bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. **Make the dough**: Place the flour and a pinch of salt in a bowl. First, add 50ml of hot water and stir, then gradually add 100ml of cold water. Knead until the dough is smooth. Brush the surface with a little oil, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest.
**Note**: The semi-scalded dough method makes the crust both soft and chewy.

2. **Prepare the base sauce for the filling**: Chop the scallion finely and grate the ginger into a paste. Dilute the dried yellow soybean paste with 2 tablespoons of water and set aside. Pour 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil into a small milk pan. Add the Sichuan peppercorn powder and five-spice powder to the cold oil, then stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add the ginger paste and a small amount of chopped scallion, stir-fry until aromatic. Then add the diluted soybean paste, stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture evaporates and oil separates on the surface. Remove from heat, add sugar and oyster sauce, mix well, and let cool.
**Key point**: The paste must be stir-fried until dry to fully develop the aroma.

3. **Prepare the meat filling**: Chop the beef into a coarse mince with visible chunks. Add salt, the cooled sauce, egg, and sesame oil. Stir vigorously in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky. Gradually add 50ml of water in 3 batches, stirring well after each addition to ensure the meat absorbs the water. Finally, add most of the chopped scallion and gently mix. Divide the filling into meatballs of about 40g each, and freeze until slightly firm.
**Note**: Add the scallion last and mix gently to avoid a strong onion odor from overmixing. Freezing the meatballs makes them easier to wrap later.

4. **Assemble the pies**: Roll the rested dough into a log, cut into pieces of about 25g each, and roll into round wrappers with thicker centers and thinner edges. Place a frozen meatball in the center, gather the edges to seal like a bun, and pinch tightly. Place the sealed side down and shape into a cylinder with both hands.
**Key point**: Ensure the seal is completely tight to prevent the filling from leaking during frying.

5. **Fry the pies**: Add oil to a frying pan and heat over medium heat. Place the pies in the pan with the sealed side up. Cover and fry until the bottom is golden brown. Flip and continue frying until the other side is colored. Pour a small amount of hot water along the edge of the pan, quickly cover, and steam-fry until the water evaporates completely. Remove the lid, fry both sides again until golden and crispy, and optionally stand the pies on their sides to brown slightly.
**Key point**: Adding water and steaming ensures the thick pies cook through. Wait until no more sizzling sounds and the oil is clear before dry-frying to brown. Be careful of the hot filling when serving.

## Health Suggestions
Beef paired with yellow soybean paste provides ample energy, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion. It is recommended to serve with a side of cold vegetables or clear soup to balance the richness. Ensure the pies are thoroughly steamed during frying to avoid undercooking the filling.
