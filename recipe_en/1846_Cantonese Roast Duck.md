# Cantonese Roast Duck
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 1 whole white-striped duck (intact, no damage)
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (crushed)
🧅 Dried shallots, appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount
🍄 Cordyceps flowers, appropriate amount

🧂 Zhuhou sauce, 10 bottles (marinade paste)
🧂 Miso paste, 5 bottles (marinade paste)
🧂 Fermented red bean curd, 3 bottles (marinade paste)
🧂 Bean sauce, 4 bottles (marinade paste)
🧂 Oyster sauce, 1 bottle (marinade paste)
🧂 Soy sauce, 0.5 jin (marinade paste)
🍬 Sugar, 3 jin (marinade powder)
🌿 Five-spice powder, 1 pack (marinade powder)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, 1 pack (marinade powder)
🌿 Sand ginger powder, 1 jin (marinade powder)
🧂 Salt, 1 pack (marinade powder)
🌿 Licorice, 0.3 jin (marinade powder)
🥃 High-proof liquor, appropriate amount (for mixing the paste)
🍎 Red Zhejiang vinegar, 7 bottles (skin water)
🍾 White vinegar, 3 bottles (skin water)
🍯 Maltose, 3 jin (skin water)
🥃 Erguotou liquor, 1 bottle (skin water)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chinese chef’s knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/basin
🪝 Roasting hook (with ring)
📌 Steel needle
💨 Air pump
🍳 Wok/large soup pot
🚰 Water faucet
🔥 Roasting oven (charcoal oven)
🌬️ Fan/air-conditioned room
🔦 Small flashlight

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the duck’s head, neck, and feet, ensuring the body remains intact and undamaged.
2. Prepare the marinade: Mix the Zhuhou sauce, miso paste, fermented red bean curd, bean sauce, oyster sauce, and soy sauce with an appropriate amount of high-proof liquor to form a paste. Combine the sugar, five-spice powder, thirteen-spice powder, sand ginger powder, salt, and licorice to form a powder. The paste and powder must be prepared separately.
3. Marinate: First, stuff the powder mixture into the duck cavity and spread it evenly on the inner walls. Then, pour the paste mixture in, lift the duck, and shake it to coat the cavity thoroughly. Finally, fill the cavity with ginger, scallion, garlic, dried shallots, cilantro, and cordyceps flowers, then shake again to distribute evenly.
**Key point**: The powder must be spread evenly to ensure consistent flavoring; after filling, shake to let the paste circulate within the cavity.

4. Sewing: Use a long steel needle to tightly sew the cavity opening shut, employing a “pierce, loop, and tighten” technique, and tuck the needle tip into the duck’s abdomen.
**Key point**: Incomplete sewing will cause juice leakage during roasting, affecting the final result.

5. Inflating: Insert the air pump between the skin and meat at the duck’s neck, inflate until the body plumps up, while massaging the skin with your hand to help the air distribute evenly.
**Key point**: Inflating separates the skin from the meat, which is essential for crispy skin.

6. Blanching and cooling: Hook the duck with the roasting hook, evenly pour boiling water over the skin, then quickly rinse the duck body with cold water.
**Key point**: Blanching tightens and sets the skin; cooling prevents residual internal heat from causing subcutaneous fat to seep out, which would affect the subsequent application of skin water.

7. Applying skin water: Heat and mix the red Zhejiang vinegar, white vinegar, maltose, and Erguotou liquor to form the skin water. Use a ladle to pour it evenly over the duck body, ensuring complete coverage.
**Key point**: Skin water is key for coloring and crispy skin; every part must be coated.

8. Air-drying: Hang the duck with the skin water applied in a ventilated area, use a fan to dry the surface until completely dry and not sticky to the touch.
**Key point**: Air-drying allows the skin water to fully penetrate, preventing color loss during roasting.

9. Light and preheat the roasting oven: Fully ignite the charcoal, wait until it burns completely, then close the oven door to create stable embers.
10. Roast in the oven: Hang the air-dried duck inside the oven with the back facing the heat source. Cover the oven lid, leaving a small skylight open. The oven temperature should reach about 200°C after 8-10 minutes, maintain this temperature and roast for approximately 50 minutes.
**Observation and adjustment**: Check the coloring through the skylight; around 30 minutes of roasting, inspect if the color is even, and gently sway the duck if needed. If the color is insufficient, close the skylight to “smother-roast” for about 20 minutes to help develop color.
11. Rest after roasting: Remove the duck after roasting and let it rest for 1-2 minutes. The skin will change from a slightly pale color to a glossy, ruby red due to residual heat.

## Health Advice
Although Cantonese roast duck is delicious, the marinade contains relatively high amounts of sauce and sugar. It is recommended to limit each serving to within 150 grams, paired with stir-fried vegetables or cucumber salad to balance sodium and sugar intake. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood pressure or managing blood sugar should partake sparingly.
