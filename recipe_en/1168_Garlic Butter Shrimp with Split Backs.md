# Garlic Butter Shrimp with Split Backs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧄 Garlic cloves 300g  
🦐 Shrimp as needed (backs split)  
🥬 Lettuce/luffa as needed (finely shredded)  
🌽 Cornstarch 2 tbsp  
🧂 Well salt 1 tbsp  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 1 tbsp  
🌶️ White pepper a pinch  
🦪 Oyster sauce a splash  
🍬 Granulated sugar 2 tbsp  
🧂 Salt 2 tbsp (for pickling lettuce/luffa)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 300g  

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🥣 Blender  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Skimmer/strainer  
🧽 Cheesecloth/filter net  
🥣 Glass bowl  
♨️ Steamer  

## Instructions
1. **Make the garlic sauce**
   - Blend 300g garlic cloves into a fine paste in the blender. Wash with clean water and squeeze dry using cheesecloth to remove sugar, preventing it from burning when frying.
   - Pour 300g vegetable oil into a pot and heat to about 120°C. Add half of the squeezed-dry garlic paste and fry over low heat until golden brown. Turn off the heat and let the garlic steep in the residual oil for a moment. Remove with a skimmer and drain; reserve the garlic oil.
   - Mix the fried golden garlic with the remaining raw garlic paste, 1 tbsp well salt, 1 tbsp chicken bouillon powder, a pinch of white pepper, a splash of oyster sauce, and 2 tbsp cornstarch until well combined. Then pour in the reserved garlic oil and mix thoroughly to make the all-purpose garlic butter sauce.
   **Note**: Washing and squeezing the garlic paste dry and controlling the oil temperature are key to preventing burning. Adding cornstarch helps the sauce adhere better to the ingredients.

2. **Prepare the ingredients**
   - Split the shrimp from head to tail along the back, butterfly the body, wash, and drain thoroughly.
   - Finely shred the lettuce/luffa. Toss with 2 tbsp sugar and 2 tbsp salt, mix well, and marinate for 5 minutes until wilted and release water. Rinse with clean water and drain. Mix in 1.5 tbsp garlic sauce and set aside.

3. **Assemble and steam**
   - Spread the dressed shredded lettuce/luffa on a plate as a bed. Arrange the butterflied shrimp evenly on top. Fill the opening on each shrimp back generously with the garlic sauce.
   - Place in steamer and steam for 8 minutes.
   **Key point**: Steaming for exactly 8 minutes ensures the shrimp remain tender and not overdone.

## Health Tips  
The combination of fried and raw garlic in the sauce enhances flavor while reducing greasiness, making it suitable for those needing to control fat intake. However, note that the sauce contains a moderate amount of oil. Shrimp and lettuce/luffa provide high-quality protein and dietary fiber, and the steaming method preserves nutrients. Consume in moderation.
