# Hand-Pulled Oil-Splashed Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g
🧂 Salt 3g (for dough, to strengthen gluten)
💧 Cold water 140g (room temperature water)
💧 Warm water as needed (30–40°C, for soaking dough pieces)
🌱 Bean sprouts a handful
🥬 Baby bok choy a handful
🧅 Chopped green onions 20g
🧄 Minced garlic 20g
🌶️ Chili flakes as needed (preferably fragrant rather than spicy)
🍂 Chinese thirteen-spice powder 0.5g (or five-spice powder)
🤍 White sesame seeds 10g
🧈 Cooking oil as needed (heated until smoking, for splashing)
🍶 Light soy sauce 20g
🍶 Aged vinegar 10–20g
🍬 Sugar 3g
🧂 MSG 3g
🧂 Salt 2–3g (final seasoning, add to taste)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok/saucepan
🔪 Cutting board
🤲 Strainer (or hand pick)
🥄 Spoon

## Procedure
1. Mix the flour with 3g salt evenly. Gradually add 140g cold water while stirring to form flaky pieces, then knead into a slightly rough, dry-flour-free dough.
**Note**: Salt increases the gluten strength and texture of the noodles.

2. Cover with plastic wrap or a lid and let rest for 10 minutes. Knead the dough once every 5 minutes. After the second kneading, the dough should become smooth and fine.
3. Roll the dough into a long rope and divide into evenly sized small dough pieces. Place them in a bowl and pour in warm water at 30–40°C until the pieces are submerged. Soak for at least 30 minutes.
**Key point**: Prepare boiling water for cooking the noodles in advance.

4. Take one soaked dough piece, flatten it, then stretch it into a thin, long strip by pulling from both ends. Be careful not to stretch it too long. When the water in the pot is boiling, add the stretched noodles and gently stir with chopsticks to prevent sticking.
5. When the water first comes to a boil, add the bean sprouts and pour a circle of cold water along the edge of the pot. When it boils the second time, add the baby bok choy and pour another circle of cold water along the edge. When it boils the third time, immediately turn off the heat.
**Note**: The three additions of cold water precisely control the cooking time and heat, making the noodles chewy and the vegetables crisp.

6. Use a strainer to transfer the cooked noodles and vegetables into a bowl, adding a little of the cooking broth. Sprinkle chopped green onions, minced garlic, chili flakes, thirteen-spice powder, and white sesame seeds on top in layers, ensuring the chili flakes are the top layer.
7. Heat the cooking oil until it smokes, then quickly pour it over the chili flakes and minced garlic to release a rich aroma.
**Key point**: The oil temperature must be high enough to fully bloom the fragrance of the seasonings.

8. Immediately after splashing the oil, add light soy sauce, aged vinegar, sugar, and MSG. Thoroughly toss everything together with chopsticks. Taste and add additional salt as needed for seasoning.
**Note**: Adding salt at the end prevents over- or under-salting and allows precise seasoning.

## Health Suggestion
These oil-splashed noodles are chewy and elastic, with vegetables added to provide dietary fiber, making them suitable as a lunch staple. Pay attention to controlling the amount of chili and oil; for those on a diet, the amount of oil used for splashing can be appropriately reduced.
