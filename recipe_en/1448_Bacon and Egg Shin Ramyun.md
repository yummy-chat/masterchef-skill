# Bacon and Egg Shin Ramyun  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Recommended Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥓 Bacon, appropriate amount (thick-cut recommended)  
🥚 Eggs, 2 whole eggs + 1 egg yolk  
🍜 Shin Ramyun noodle block, 1 piece  
🧀 Cheese, appropriate amount (optional, for added aroma)  
🧂 Shin Ramyun seasoning powder, half a packet  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥣 Pot for boiling  
🥢 Bowl, chopsticks  
🍴 Fork or tongs  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the bacon**: Cut the bacon into small pieces. Thick-cut bacon is recommended for better texture.  
2. **Make the sauce**: In a bowl, beat 2 whole eggs and 1 egg yolk. Add half a packet of Shin Ramyun seasoning powder, and if desired, grate in some cheese to taste. Mix well and set aside.  
3. **Fry the bacon**: Place the bacon pieces flat in a frying pan without oil. Cook over medium heat until the edges turn caramelized dark red and the overall texture is crispy and fragrant. Be sure to spread them out so each piece heats evenly.  
4. **Boil the noodles**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil, add the noodle block, and stir with chopsticks. Cook for about 4 minutes until the noodles are tender but not soft. Avoid overcooking to prevent loss of chewiness.  
5. **Combine and season**: Use chopsticks or tongs to transfer the cooked noodles with a bit of the cooking water directly into the pan with the bacon, allowing the noodles to absorb the rendered bacon fat from the pan. Pour the egg sauce evenly over the noodles. **Turn off the heat immediately after adding the sauce**, and use the residual heat to thicken the sauce until it evenly coats the noodles. If the heat is not turned off in time, the high temperature will cause the egg to solidify into lumps, preventing a smooth sauce.  
6. **Serve**: After turning off the heat, quickly mix the noodles, bacon, and sauce together using tongs or chopsticks. Transfer to a plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein but has a high sodium content, so it is recommended to pair it with unsalted vegetables to balance the meal. It is suitable for young people needing a quick energy boost; those with high blood lipids should use bacon fat with caution.
