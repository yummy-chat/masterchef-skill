# Corn Pancake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
💧 Room temperature water 250g
🍬 Sugar 12g (for activating yeast)
🫙 Yeast 2g
🫒 Oil 12g (to be added to the dough)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🌽 Corn flour (fine cornmeal) 80g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing, kneading, and fermenting)
🥄 Measuring spoon/measuring cup
🍴 Silicone spatula
🪚 Rolling pin
🍳 Flat pan
🫖 Lid (for covering while cooking the pancake and for covering the bowl during fermentation)
🧺 Steamer cloth/cheesecloth (for wrapping after cooking to retain moisture)
🔪 Cutting board/work surface

## Steps
1. Place room temperature water, sugar, and yeast into a bowl and stir until well combined. Add oil, then pour in the all-purpose flour and corn flour. Stir with a spatula until a shaggy dough forms.
   **Note**: Dissolving the sugar and yeast in water first helps activate the yeast.
2. Use your hands to knead the shaggy mixture into a dough. Knead until there is no dry flour on the surface and the dough feels medium-soft. It does not need to be smooth.
3. Cover with a lid and let it ferment at room temperature for 1 hour, until the dough has visibly expanded.
   **Optional**: The fermented dough can be refrigerated for 1-2 days and used as needed.
4. Take out the desired amount of dough, divide it into small portions, and roll each into a ball on the work surface. Lightly brush both sides with oil to prevent sticking. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for another half hour.
5. Sprinkle a little flour on the work surface and roll a small dough ball into a round pancake about 3-4 mm thick. The shape does not need to be perfect.
6. Heat a flat pan over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Place the pancake in the pan and cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Once the bottom is set and dense small bubbles appear on the surface, flip it.
7. After flipping, gently rotate the pancake for even heating. Continue cooking for about 30 seconds. When the pancake puffs up with many bubbles again, or even becomes a large air pocket, it is ready to be removed from the pan.
   **Key point**: Use the change in bubbles to determine when to flip and when to remove the pancake. This is key to the pancake being filled with steam and having a soft texture.
8. Immediately wrap the cooked pancake in a steamer cloth or cheesecloth. The residual heat and steam will make the corn pancake even softer.

## Health Advice
Corn pancake combines corn flour with wheat flour, blending coarse and refined grains. It is suitable as a breakfast or staple food and has a lower glycemic index than plain white flour pancakes. It is recommended to eat it with vegetables and protein to avoid blood sugar spikes from consuming it alone. Pay attention to the heat when cooking the pancake to prevent it from becoming too hard.
