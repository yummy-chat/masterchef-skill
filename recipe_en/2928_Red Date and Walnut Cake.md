# Red Date and Walnut Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Walnut kernels 160g (extracted from shelled walnuts)
🍎 Dried red dates 200g (pitted)
🥔 Sweet potato starch 20g
💧 Water approx. 340ml (300ml for making date paste, 40ml for starch slurry)
🍯 Maltose 150g
🫒 Cooking oil 25g (mild-flavored vegetable oil or butter, for added richness)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large wok
🥛 Blender
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Knife
📄 Parchment paper (two sheets, for anti-stick)
🥢 Rolling pin (for shaping, optional)

## Steps
1. Shell the walnuts to obtain 160g of kernels. Place them in a dry large wok and stir-fry over low heat until the color deepens and a rich nutty aroma is released. Remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Do not add oil; stir-fry thoroughly until crispy and fragrant.
2. Wash the dried red dates and remove the pits one by one.
3. Put the pitted red dates and 300ml of water into a blender, and blend until a fine paste forms.
   **Note**: The water amount should be just enough to allow the blender to run; too much water will extend the subsequent cooking time.
4. Pour the date paste into a large wok, add the maltose, and stir continuously over low heat until fully combined.
   **Key**: Keep the heat low throughout; maltose gives the cake flexibility and setting power.
5. Mix the sweet potato starch with 40ml of water to form a slurry, pour it into the wok, and stir well to combine.
   **Note**: This helps the cake hold its shape and gives it a chewy texture.
6. After the date paste has absorbed the starch slurry, add the cooking oil and continue stirring evenly.
7. Continue stirring over low heat for about 20 minutes. It will be thick at first; after 10 minutes, the moisture will gradually reduce. Finally, the date paste will clump together into a smooth mass that does not stick to the wok or spatula.
   **Key**: Low heat and slow stirring; clumping indicates success.
8. Add the toasted walnut kernels and mix well, ensuring each kernel is coated with date paste.
9. Lay one sheet of parchment paper on a cutting board, pour the cake mixture onto it, and cover with the other sheet. Fold and press repeatedly to eliminate internal gaps, and shape into a flat, square block. Let it cool completely and rest.
   **Note**: Repeated folding creates a dense structure; parchment prevents sticking.
10. Once the cake is completely cooled and firm, remove the parchment paper, trim the edges, first cut into strips, then into small cubes.
    **Note**: Must be completely cool before cutting, otherwise it will deform.

## Health Advice
This red date and walnut cake is rich in dietary fiber and healthy fats, making it a suitable energy snack for people who need to nourish blood and boost brain function. It is recommended to eat 1-2 pieces at a time, and to avoid excessive sugar intake. Diabetic patients should consume with caution.
