# Fried Oil Cake
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g (for making the oil cake skin)  
💧 Drinking water 1500g (for scalding the dough)  
🐷 Lard 50g (for greasing the pan and creating flakiness)  
🍬 Brown sugar 300g (for making the sugar filling)  
🌾 Cooked flour 150g (to adjust the thickness of the sugar filling)  
🫘 Cooked sesame powder 50g (to enhance the aroma of the sugar filling)  
🧊 Sparkling water as needed (key to making the outer skin crispy)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Iron pot  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥢 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
♨️ Deep fryer  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions
1. **Scald the dough**: Add lard to the pot to grease it, then pour out the excess lard, leaving only a thin layer. Add water and bring to a boil. Gradually add the flour to the boiling water, stirring quickly and vigorously with a rolling pin until no dry flour remains, forming a flaky dough.  
   **Key point**: The dough must be scalded with boiling water and stirred thoroughly to ensure the flour is fully cooked, which is essential for a soft texture that does not stick to the teeth.

2. **Steam the dough**: Once stirring is complete, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let the dough steam to fully cook the interior.

3. **Handle the scalded dough**: Remove the steamed dough and quickly spread it out on the cutting board to cool. Once completely cooled, knead it into a ball. Sprinkle with sparkling water and repeatedly knead until evenly absorbed. Then, gradually knead in a small amount of dry flour until the dough is smooth and elastic. Finally, cover the dough with a bowl and let it rest.  
   **Key point**: Adding sparkling water is key to creating flakiness; the dry flour increases elasticity, preventing the cake from cracking during frying. The dough must be completely cooled before handling.

4. **Prepare the brown sugar filling**: Mix the brown sugar, cooked flour, and cooked sesame powder on the cutting board. Use a rolling pin to crush and mix, then rub with your hands until fully combined and free of lumps.  
   **Key point**: Brown sugar tends to clump, so be sure to crush and rub it evenly. The filling should have a moderate consistency—fluid but not too runny.

5. **Shape the oil cakes**: Divide the rested dough into portions of about 50–60g each. Take one portion, knead it smooth, and roll it into a ball. Use your middle finger to make a hole in the center, rotating the dough to form a bowl-shaped wrapper with even thickness. Fill with one spoonful of brown sugar filling, then use the tiger’s mouth (the webbing between thumb and index finger) to close the opening while turning, pinching it tightly. Remove any excess dough, then flatten it with your palm into an oval-shaped raw cake with a slightly thicker center and thinner edges.  
   **Key point**: The filling process should be quick, and the opening must be sealed tightly to prevent sugar leakage. Shape the cake with a thicker center and thinner edges for even heating.

6. **Fry**: Pour enough oil into the pot and heat to 350°F (180°C). Carefully add the raw cakes one by one and fry over medium heat. The cakes will sink to the bottom at first, then gradually float as they heat. Continue frying until the skin puffs up, develops crispy layers, and turns golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Key point**: Control the oil temperature strictly—too high will burn the cakes, too low will make them greasy. Occasionally stir the cakes with chopsticks during frying to ensure even heating and consistent coloring.

## Health Advice
This fried oil cake, with its scalded dough and sparkling water for flakiness, is crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and sweetly fragrant. It’s perfect for an occasional treat. However, due to its high oil and sugar content, it’s best enjoyed with unsweetened tea or a vegetable salad. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should have only a small taste.
