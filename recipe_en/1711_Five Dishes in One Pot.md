# Five Dishes in One Pot
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 1 piece (about 300g, cut into pieces)
🐟 Fish head 1 (split in half, any variety)
🦐 Large prawns 5-6
🧈 Firm tofu 1 large block
🥬 Baby cabbage 1/4 piece (cut lengthwise)
🧅 Purple onion 1/4 piece (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (sliced)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers 2-3 of each (cut into rings)
🥚 Egg 1 (for steamed egg side dish)
🥩 Ground meat 1 small bowl (for steamed egg side dish)

🧂 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🌶️ Chopped chili pepper 2 tablespoons
🍺 Beer half a can
💧 Water 1 large bowl
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🫙 Soy sauce (light/dark) appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar a pinch

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🥣 Bowls (for steaming egg, holding water)
🥢 Chopsticks (to support bowl for steaming, stirring)
🍽️ Plates (for serving)
🥄 Spoons (for serving, seasoning)

## Cooking Steps
1. Pre-cut all vegetables and aromatics neatly: pork belly into pieces, fish head split in half, scallion into sections, ginger into slices, garlic into slices, green and red chili peppers into rings, baby cabbage cut lengthwise.
**Key point**: Cut evenly to ensure synchronized cooking later.

2. Heat the iron wok, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, place the pork belly skin-side down, and fry over medium-low heat until the skin blisters and turns golden brown and fragrant, rendering out the pork fat.
**Key point**: Frying the pork belly first yields lard, which adds more aroma when frying the fish head later.
   **Doneness check**: Skin blistered, color golden.

3. Remove the fried pork belly and set aside. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the fish head skin-side down and fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown.
**Key point**: Frying the fish head removes fishy odor and helps it hold together during braising.

4. Once the fish head is fried, add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic slices, and green and red chili pepper rings to the edge of the wok, using residual heat to release their aroma.
5. Arrange the firm tofu, large prawns, purple onion pieces, and baby cabbage in the empty spaces of the wok. Then return the fried pork belly to the wok, spreading it out as evenly as possible.
**Key point**: Spread ingredients flat to ensure even heating and flavor absorption.

6. Spread the chopped chili pepper over the surface of the fish head to concentrate flavor for the fish.
7. Pour in half a can of beer, then add one large bowl of water. Add salt, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and a pinch of sugar. Gently stir the broth with a spoon. The liquid level should roughly cover the bottom of the wok and partially submerge the ingredients.
8. In a separate small bowl, place the ground meat and a raw egg. Use a pair of chopsticks to suspend the small bowl over the wok so it sits above the ingredients. Then cover the wok with a lid.
**Key point**: Use the steam to simultaneously cook the steamed egg side dish; the lid must be sealed tightly.

9. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to medium-low heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, until all ingredients are cooked through, the broth has thickened slightly, the fish meat has changed color, and the prawns have turned red.
10. Remove the lid. Take out each ingredient and plate separately: drizzle the broth over the fish head, slice the pork belly, and arrange the prawns, tofu, and baby cabbage in separate dishes. Serve the steamed egg side dish on its own. Ready to serve.

## Health Advice
This one-pot dish combines meat and vegetables with diverse ingredients. However, the pork belly and frying method increase the calorie content. It is recommended to pair it with light staple foods and control portion sizes. Suitable for sharing at gatherings; be mindful of individual intake. People with gout should limit consumption of the broth.
