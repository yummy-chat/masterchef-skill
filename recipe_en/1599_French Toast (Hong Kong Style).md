# French Toast (Hong Kong Style)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 2 slices of bread  
🥚 4 eggs  
🥛 Milk, as needed  
🥜 Peanut butter, as needed  
🍯 Blueberry jam, as needed  
🍯 Condensed milk, as needed  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍴 Fork  
🔗 Tongs  
🥄 Wooden spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the egg-milk mixture: Crack the eggs into a stainless steel bowl, add milk as needed, and whisk thoroughly with a fork.  
2. Process the bread: Spread peanut butter on one slice of bread, top with another slice, and press together firmly. Dip the entire sandwich into the egg-milk mixture, ensuring both sides are fully soaked.  
**Note**: If omitting peanut butter, you can directly soak single slices of bread.  
3. Cook the French toast: Add cooking oil to the frying pan over low heat, and place the soaked bread in the pan. Fry until one side is golden brown, then flip and continue frying until both sides are golden.  
**Key**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains undercooked.  
4. Make scrambled eggs: Pour the remaining egg-milk mixture into the pan. Use a wooden spatula to quickly stir and push the eggs. Cook over low heat until the eggs just form soft curds, then immediately turn off the heat.  
**Key**: Stir quickly to maintain a tender texture.  
5. Plate and garnish: Place the French toast on a plate, and put the scrambled eggs beside it. Drizzle with blueberry jam and condensed milk. You can also serve with lemon wedges or fruit to add fragrance and cut the richness.  

## Health Tips
French toast is rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it suitable for breakfast or post-workout energy replenishment. However, peanut butter and condensed milk are high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with fruit to balance the greasiness. Always use low heat during frying to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside undercooked.
