# Braised Lamb Ribs in Clear Broth
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lamb ribs 1000g (tender texture, rinse clean, no need to marinate)
🥕 White radish approx. 400g (half a radish, peel and cut into large chunks, taste a slice beforehand to prevent bitterness)
🍎 Apple approx. 100g (1/3 or half of a large apple, core removed)
🍅 Tomato approx. 100g (half a tomato, stem and seeds removed)
🧅 Onion approx. 60g (1/4 of an onion)
🔴 Red goji berries approx. 15g (optional, rinse clean in advance)
🌿 Cilantro approx. 15g (finely chopped)
🫚 Ginger slices approx. 10g (2 slices)
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments approx. 2g (optional, for aroma)
🧂 Salt 3g (initial addition) + to taste (final seasoning)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g (approx. 1 teaspoon)
🧂 White pepper powder approx. 1g (half a teaspoon)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns approx. 1g (about a dozen, optional)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok (for blanching)
🫕 Clay pot or regular pot (for long, slow simmering)
🔪 Vegetable peeler
🔪 Chef's knife
🥄 Ladle (for skimming foam, skimming oil, and seasoning)
🥄 Slotted spoon (to remove cooked apple and tomato)

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the white radish, cut it in half lengthwise, then slice diagonally into large chunks. Remove the core from the apple and take approx. 100g. Remove the stem and seeds from the tomato and take half. Take 1/4 of the onion. Slice the ginger. Cut the dried chili peppers into segments.
Note: Before cutting the white radish, taste a small slice to ensure it is not bitter; discard if bitter.

2. Add enough cold water to a wok, then add the radish chunks and rinsed lamb ribs. Bring to a boil over high heat to blanch. Once a large amount of foam has surfaced, turn off the heat.
**Key point**: Must start with cold water. The water quantity must be sufficient to fully submerge the ingredients.

3. Rinse the lamb ribs and radish clean with warm water.
**Key point**: Must use warm water to prevent the meat from shrinking and becoming tough when meeting cold water.

4. Place the rinsed lamb ribs and radish into a clay pot. Add in order: apple, tomato, onion, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili segments, chicken bouillon, and 3g of salt.
**Key point**: Adding salt early gives the meat a foundational flavor.

5. Add hot water to the clay pot, enough to fully submerge the lamb. Bring to a boil over high heat, then skim off the first layer of foam. Turn the heat to the lowest setting, cover with a lid, and simmer slowly for 45 minutes, until the lamb is tender.
**Key point**: Simmer slowly over low heat. Leave a small gap with the lid to prevent the broth from boiling over.

6. After the time is up, use a slotted spoon to remove and discard the apple and tomato. Use a ladle to skim off most of the floating oil from the soup surface; the Sichuan peppercorns can be removed together with the oil.
**Key point**: Skimming the oil significantly reduces the greasiness of the broth.

7. Turn the heat back to high. Add white pepper powder. Taste the broth and adjust salt seasoning to your preference. Sprinkle in the diced onion, rinsed red goji berries, and chopped cilantro. Once the broth comes back to a boil, turn off the heat.
**Key point**: Season by tasting gradually to ensure the saltiness is just right.

## Health Advice
Braised lamb ribs in clear broth are warming and nourishing. White radish and apple help neutralize the greasiness of the lamb, making it suitable for winter warming or for those who are physically weak and sensitive to cold. Be sure to skim off the floating oil to reduce calories. People with gout should consume the broth sparingly.
