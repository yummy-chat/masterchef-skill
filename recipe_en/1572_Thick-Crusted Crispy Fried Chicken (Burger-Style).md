# Thick-Crusted Crispy Fried Chicken (Burger-Style)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole chicken leg (deboned and flattened into a large cutlet)  
🌾 250g cake flour  
💧 Approximately 1000ml water (200ml for marinating, 800ml for dipping)  
🌶️ 1 bag Huang Feihong crispy chili peppers (ground into coarse powder)  
🧂 10g salt (for marinating)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for the frying powder)  
🧂 8g chicken bouillon powder  
🧄 1/2g garlic powder  
🫚 1/4g ginger powder  
🧂 A pinch of black pepper  
🍬 15g sugar  
🌶️ 2 tablespoons ground crispy chili powder (for marinating) + 2 tablespoons (for the frying powder)  
🧂 2.5g baking soda  
🧂 A pinch of thirteen-spice powder  
🛢️适量 cooking oil (for frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🌪️ Grinder or rolling pin  
🛍️ Plastic bag (sealable bag)  
🍽️ Plate  
🪣 Deep basin  
🍳 Oil pot  
🥄 Skimming spoon  
🔲 Cutting board  
🔥 Stove  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Debone the whole chicken leg, use the knife tip to cut the tendons, poke small holes in the chicken skin, and flatten it into an even, large chicken cutlet.  
2. Grind the Huang Feihong crispy chili peppers into coarse powder using a grinder or rolling pin.  
3. Place the chicken cutlet in a sealable bag, add 10g salt, 8g chicken bouillon powder, 1/2g garlic powder, 1/4g ginger powder, a pinch of black pepper, 15g sugar, and 2 tablespoons of ground crispy chili powder. Knead for 10 minutes. Pour in 200ml water, remove air from the bag, seal, and marinate in the refrigerator for 6 hours.  
4. On a plate, mix 250g cake flour, 2.5g baking soda, a pinch of thirteen-spice powder, a pinch of salt, and 2 tablespoons of ground crispy chili powder. Stir evenly to make the crispy frying powder.  
5. Pour out the remaining marinade from the bag, add 800ml cold water, and stir to create the dipping liquid.  
6. Remove the chicken cutlet from the marinade, lay it flat on the frying powder, cover it with the surrounding flour, press firmly with both hands (crossing your index fingers) about 7 times, lift it, shake off excess powder, and create an initial scaly texture.  
7. Fully immerse the chicken cutlet in the dipping liquid, quickly remove it, and place it back into the frying powder. Repeat the covering, pressing, and shaking process. Follow the "three dry, two wet" method until the surface shows distinct, irregular scales.  
8. Heat the oil pot to 180°C, add the coated chicken cutlet, fry for 8 minutes initially, remove and drain oil, then let it rest for 3 minutes.  
9. Raise the oil temperature to 190°C, fry for another 2 minutes until the shell is golden and crispy.  
10. Remove, drain oil, cut into pieces, and plate.  

## Health Advice  
This fried chicken undergoes three coatings and twice frying, resulting in higher calories but a crispy texture. It is suitable for occasional consumption and is best paired with a vegetable salad to balance the oil.
