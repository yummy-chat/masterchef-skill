# French-Style Scrambled Egg, Ham, and Avocado Sandwich
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥖 Baguette, half a stick
🥚 Eggs, 2
🥓 Ham slices, 2-3 slices
🥑 Avocado, half
🥬 Lettuce leaves, 1-2 leaves
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for spraying)
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌶️ Black pepper, as needed
🍯 Salad dressing, as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🔫 Oil spray bottle
🧂 Salt grinder
🌶️ Black pepper grinder
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture is smooth and free of lumps.
2. Cut the baguette horizontally, leaving one side attached (do not cut all the way through). Slice the avocado into thin pieces and set aside.
3. Preheat a non-stick frying pan over heat, evenly spray a moderate amount of cooking oil, then reduce to low heat and pour in the egg mixture.
4. Immediately use chopsticks to stir the egg mixture in a fast, circular motion, allowing it to solidify into tender, fluffy curds. Remove from heat promptly while still moist and glossy.
   **Note**: The action must be quick and the heat kept low throughout to prevent the eggs from overcooking; stirring with chopsticks creates a fluffy texture.
5. Sprinkle and grind salt and black pepper over the cooked scrambled eggs to season.
6. Open the bread, first layer the lettuce leaves, then sequentially add the ham slices and avocado slices.
7. Place the scrambled eggs on top of the avocado, squeeze on some salad dressing, cover with the other half of the bread, and gently press together.
   **Key**: Salad dressing enhances flavor while also preventing the bread from drying out.
8. Cut the sandwich diagonally, then plate and serve.

## Health Tips
Avocado and scrambled eggs provide quality fats and protein, making this sandwich suitable as a breakfast option during weight loss. It is recommended to choose a whole-wheat baguette, and the salad dressing can be reduced by half or replaced with Greek yogurt.
