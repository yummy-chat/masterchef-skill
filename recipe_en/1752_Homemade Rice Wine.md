# Homemade Rice Wine
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice 500g (soaked overnight in advance)
🍡 Frozen glutinous rice balls (tangyuan), as needed
🫐 Goji berries, a small handful
🧂 Sweet wine starter (jiuqu) 2g
💧 Cool boiled water 380ml
🍬 White sugar, two tablespoons (optional)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🛢️ Steamer basket and steaming cloth
🍳 Steamer pot
🥄 Spatula
🧤 Disposable gloves
🏺 Ceramic jar with lid (or a basin)
🔌 Rice wine maker (optional)
🍲 Small pot

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the soaked glutinous rice repeatedly until the water runs clear. Drain well and spread evenly in a steamer basket lined with a steaming cloth. Flatten the surface and poke several ventilation holes with chopsticks. Bring water to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 30 minutes, until the grains can be easily crushed between your fingers.
   **Key Point**: Avoid using an electric rice cooker to prevent an overly soft texture; poking holes ensures even steam penetration.

2. Transfer the steamed glutinous rice to a clean large bowl. Use a spatula to turn and spread the rice, allowing it to cool naturally until completely cold.
   **Note**: All containers and utensils used must be sterilized with high heat in advance. Wear disposable gloves during handling to ensure a sterile environment.

3. Add the cool boiled water in batches. Wearing gloves, break apart and knead the rice grains until each grain is loose and separated, with no clumps.
   **Note**: Ensure the glutinous rice has completely cooled before adding water. High temperature will deactivate the wine starter.

4. Evenly sprinkle the sweet wine starter over the rice. Mix thoroughly, ensuring every grain is coated with the starter.

5. Transfer the mixed rice into a dry, oil-free ceramic jar. Flatten and press firmly. Use chopsticks to poke a hole straight down to the bottom in the center.
   **Note**: The central hole is used to observe the subsequent release of rice wine.

6. Seal the jar tightly with its lid. Place in a warm environment at around 30°C to ferment for 2–5 days. Fermentation is complete when a strong aroma of rice wine is detected and clear wine liquid has seeped out of the central hole.

7. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the goji berries and frozen glutinous rice balls, cooking until all the rice balls float to the surface.
   **Indicator**: When the rice balls float, they are cooked.

8. Add two tablespoons of homemade rice wine and an appropriate amount of white sugar. Gently stir until the rice wine disperses and the sugar has completely dissolved, then immediately turn off the heat.
   **Note**: The rice wine must be added last. Simply bring to a boil; prolonged heating will destroy its aroma and texture.

## Health Suggestions
Homemade rice wine is rich in probiotics and B vitamins, which help promote digestion and is suitable for those with a cold spleen and stomach. Control the sugar amount carefully; diabetic patients should consume with caution. Strict sterility must be maintained during fermentation to avoid contamination by undesirable microbes.
