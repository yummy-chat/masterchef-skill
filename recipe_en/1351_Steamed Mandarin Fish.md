# Steamed Mandarin Fish
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧂 Mandarin fish  
🧂 Scallion  
🧂 Spring onion  
🧂 Red chili pepper  
🧂 Ginger slices  
🧂 Light soy sauce  
🧂 Cooking oil  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Steps  
1. Split the mandarin fish from the belly, remove the gills and innards, scrape off the scales, and use the back of a knife to scrape off the slime from the surface.  
2. Score both sides of the fish with a crisscross pattern, lay ginger slices on top, optionally add some lard for extra fragrance; when plating, place a chopstick under the fish to allow steam to circulate evenly.  
3. Split the scallion lengthwise, remove the core, keeping only the two outer layers; split the spring onion lengthwise; remove the seeds and white inner flesh from the red chili pepper. Stack the scallion, spring onion, and red chili pepper, cut into fine shreds, and soak in clean water until needed.  
4. Add enough water to the steamer, bring it to a boil, place the mandarin fish inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 8–10 minutes.  
5. While steaming the fish, heat oil in a separate wok until it begins to smoke.  
6. After steaming, discard the cooking liquid in the plate, remove the ginger slices, place the soaked scallion and chili shreds on top, pour the sizzling hot oil over them, and finally drizzle with light soy sauce before serving.  

## Health Tips  
Steamed mandarin fish is low in fat and high in protein, rich in DHA, making it suitable for those on a diet or in need of brain health support. It is recommended to pair with leafy green vegetables for balanced nutrition. Control the steaming time to 8–10 minutes to avoid overcooking the meat.
