# Homemade Salad Dressing
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs: 2 (about 40g of egg yolk, yolks only)  
🍬 White Sugar: 30g  
🫒 Cooking Oil: 230g (choose a neutral-flavored oil)  
🧴 White Vinegar: 30g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Spoon  
🍶 Small bowl (for holding the cooking oil and white vinegar)  

## Instructions
1. Separate the egg yolks from the whites. Place the yolks in a clean, dry glass bowl.  
   **Note**: Ensure no egg white is mixed in with the yolks, and the bowl must be free of water and oil.  
2. Add the white sugar to the egg yolks. The amount can be adjusted according to personal taste.  
3. Using an electric hand mixer on high speed, beat the egg yolk and sugar mixture for about 3 minutes.  
   **Doneness check**: Beat until the egg yolks visibly lighten in color and increase in volume.  
4. Add one spoonful of cooking oil, and continue beating until the oil is fully incorporated with the egg yolk mixture and it returns to a thick consistency.  
   **Note**: Do not add too much oil at once. The mixture will thin out after adding oil but will become thick again after thorough beating.  
5. Repeat the oil addition and beating process. After adding about 6 spoonfuls of oil in total, the mixture will become very thick.  
6. Add half a spoonful of white vinegar. The mixture will temporarily thin out; continue beating until it thickens again.  
7. Alternate between adding oil (beating until thick) and adding vinegar (beating until it thins out). Repeat this cycle until all the white vinegar and cooking oil have been added and fully emulsified.  
8. Once all ingredients are added, beat thoroughly to ensure no separation of oil and liquid.  
   **Doneness check**: The dressing should be a smooth, thick, glossy paste. When lifted with a spoon, it should not drip easily.  
9. The dressing is ready. Store it in a sealed container in the refrigerator.

## Health Advice
This homemade salad dressing uses raw egg yolks. It is recommended to use pasteurized or high-quality eggs to reduce the risk of salmonella. This product is high in oil and sugar and is suitable for occasional flavoring. Each batch is approximately 2000 calories. Those on a weight loss diet should control their intake.
