# Braised Stuffed Eggplant and Chili with Meat Filling
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Ground pork 250g (hind leg meat)  
🥬 Eggplant 2 (choose straight ones)  
🌶️ Green chili peppers 2 (choose straight ones)  
🧅 Ginger 3g (minced)  
🧅 Scallion 10g (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (about 4 cloves, minced)  
🌿 Cilantro to taste (chopped)  
🍬 White sugar 2/3 teaspoon (for meat filling seasoning) + 1 teaspoon (for braising)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 teaspoon (for meat filling) + 2 teaspoons (for braising)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1/3 teaspoon (for meat filling) + 1/3 teaspoon (for braising)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1/2 teaspoon (for meat filling) + 1/2 teaspoon (for braising)  
🌫️ White pepper powder a pinch (about 0.1g, for meat filling)  
🌽 Cornstarch 1 level tablespoon (for meat filling) + 1/2 teaspoon (for thickening)  
💧 Water 3 teaspoons (about 30g, to mix into meat filling) + about 1.5 bowls (about 400g, for braising) + 4 teaspoons (about 40g, for making starch water)  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing filling)  
🍳 Frying pan (for pan-frying and braising)  
🥄 Spoon (tablespoon, teaspoon, for measuring seasonings)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring, stuffing, and picking)  
🔪 Knife and cutting board (for cutting)  
🔥 Stove  
🫖 Lid (for braising and shielding from oil splatter)  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the meat filling**: Add minced ginger, chopped scallion, 2/3 teaspoon white sugar, 1 teaspoon light soy sauce, 1/3 teaspoon dark soy sauce, 1/2 teaspoon oyster sauce, a pinch of white pepper powder, and 1 level tablespoon cornstarch to the ground pork. Gradually add about 3 teaspoons water. Stir in one direction for about 1 minute until the filling becomes sticky, stretchy, and holds together firmly.

2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Wash the eggplants and remove the stems. Pat dry. Cut each into 3 sections. Make a deep cut in the center of each section, slicing to about 2/3 of the way through, leaving the bottom attached. Wash the chili peppers, remove seeds and stems, and pat dry. Cut each into 3 sections as well.

3. **Stuff the fillings**:
   - Stuffed eggplant: Lightly dust the flesh inside the cut of the eggplant section with dry starch. Fill with an appropriate amount of meat filling, smoothing it flat with chopsticks. Then lightly dust the two cut surfaces with dry starch.
   - Stuffed chili: No need to dust the inside of the chili sections. Directly fill with meat filling, smoothing both ends flat. Lightly dust the exposed meat filling on the cut surfaces with dry starch.
   **Note**: Dusting with dry starch helps the meat filling adhere tightly to the vegetables during frying, preventing it from falling off.

4. **Pan-fry to set**: Pour oil into a frying pan and swirl to coat the bottom evenly. When the oil is hot enough that you feel a strong heat when holding your hand about 5cm above the surface, first place the stuffed eggplants into the pan with the meat filling side down, then add the stuffed chilies. Reduce heat to medium-low after all ingredients are in the pan. Fry until the meat filling side of the eggplants turns golden brown and sets, then flip and continue frying until the chili skins develop blister marks and the eggplants become soft overall.
   **Reminder**: Oil may splatter during frying; you can shield with the lid slightly.

5. **Season and braise**: Add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add 2 teaspoons light soy sauce, 1/3 teaspoon dark soy sauce, 1/2 teaspoon oyster sauce, and 1 teaspoon white sugar in order. Swirl the pan to let the sauce sizzle. Pour in about 1.5 bowls of water. Gently swirl the pan to mix evenly. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover and reduce heat to low. Braise for about 10 minutes to allow the ingredients to fully absorb the flavors.

6. **Reduce sauce and thicken**: After braising, turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the sauce has condensed to an appropriate consistency, pour in the starch water made by mixing 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch with 4 teaspoons water. Let it sit for about 7-8 seconds, then gently swirl the pan to make the sauce thick and glossy.

7. **Serve**: Turn off the heat, sprinkle with chopped cilantro, and plate. Done.

## Health Tips  
This dish features eggplant that absorbs oil and chili that stimulates the appetite. The meat filling provides high-quality protein, making it a good accompaniment to rice. However, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or multigrain rice to balance nutrition. The amount of oil used during pan-frying is already considerable, so minimal additional oil is needed for the subsequent braising.
