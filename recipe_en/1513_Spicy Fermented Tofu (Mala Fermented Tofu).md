# Spicy Fermented Tofu (Mala Fermented Tofu)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Fresh firm tofu, 1 block (cut into cubes approx. 2 cm per side)
🦠 Fermented tofu starter culture, as needed (for even dusting)
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed
🫑 Sichuan peppercorn powder, as needed
🧂 Salt, about half a bowl
🫗 Chili oil (oil from making chili flakes), as needed (must cover the tofu)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Steamer with steaming rack (for steaming and initial fermentation)
🫙 Fine-mesh strainer (for dusting the starter culture)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing dry ingredients)
🫙 Airtight glass jar with clamp lid (for later fermentation and storage)
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1.  Cut the tofu into thick slices, then into small cubes approximately 2 cm per side, ensuring uniform size.
2.  Arrange the tofu cubes evenly on the steaming rack, leaving space between each piece. If using leftover overnight tofu, steam over boiling water for 2 minutes, then let cool. Using the fine-mesh strainer, evenly dust the surface and sides of the tofu cubes with the starter culture, ensuring at least three sides are covered with the culture.
    **Note**: Using a strainer helps distribute the culture more evenly.
3.  Cover the steamer tightly and ferment at room temperature (around 20°C) for 3 days, until the surface of the tofu is covered with dense white mycelium.
    **Key Point**: The container must be sealed to create a suitable environment for the growth of the mold.
4.  In a bowl, mix chili powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, and salt, stirring well to create a spicy dry coating mix.
5.  Wearing disposable gloves, take out the fermented tofu cubes. Gently press and smooth the white mycelium on the surface with your hands. Handle gently to prevent the tofu from crumbling.
6.  Place the smoothed tofu cubes into the spicy dry coating mix. Gently roll them around to ensure all six sides are evenly coated with the dry mix.
7.  Place the coated tofu cubes into a clean, dry, and oil-free airtight glass jar, arranging them neatly in layers.
    **Note**: The jar must be completely dry and oil-free to prevent contamination by unwanted bacteria.
8.  Seal the jar and store at room temperature for 3 days.
9.  After 3 days, open the jar and pour in the chili oil. The amount of oil must completely submerge the tofu.
    **Key Point**: The oil must cover all the tofu to act as an air seal for preservation.
10. Seal the jar again. Continue fermenting for about 15 days until the tofu becomes soft, sticky, and fully infused with flavor, then it is ready to eat.

## Health Advice
This spicy fermented tofu is rich in probiotics and plant protein, suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors but need to control sodium intake; note that the amount of salt can be halved. Maintaining sterile techniques throughout the fermentation process is crucial. Otherwise, contamination by unwanted bacteria is likely, which can affect the fermentation result.
