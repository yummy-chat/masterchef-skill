# Supreme Braised Prawns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Prawns (about 15-20, with whiskers trimmed and backs slit for deveining)
🧅 Scallions (2-3, cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger (3-4 slices)
🧄 Garlic (4-5 cloves)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns (about 10)
🪵 Cinnamon stick (1 small piece)
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed)
🍺 Beer (or yellow wine) (1 can, enough to almost submerge the prawns)
🍬 Rock sugar (4-8 pieces)
🧂 Salt (a pinch)
🧂 Chicken bouillon (to taste)
🥄 Light soy sauce (1 small spoon)
🥥 Coconut juice (about 2/3 cup, using a standard disposable paper cup)
🤍 White sesame seeds (as needed, for garnish)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🫗 Lid for the pan
🥄 Spoon
🧴 Disposable paper cup or glass cup

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Prawns**: Trim the prawns' whiskers. Slit the back from the head-to-body joint to the tail, and remove the black vein.
2. **Infuse the Oil**: Pour enough cold oil into the pan. Once hot, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic cloves. Fry over low heat until lightly golden and edges are slightly charred. Add Sichuan peppercorns and the cinnamon stick, and continue frying until fragrant, the spices are slightly charred, and colors deepen. Use a slotted spoon to remove all spices, leaving the infused oil behind.
   **Note**: Fry the spices until "slightly charred" but not burnt, to fully release the fragrance.
3. **Sear the Prawns**: Lay the prepared prawns flat in the infused oil. Sear over medium heat until one side turns red and translucent. Flip and sear the other side to an orange-red color.
4. **Season and Braise**: Pour in the entire can of beer, enough to almost submerge the prawns. Add rock sugar, salt, chicken bouillon, and light soy sauce. Gently stir to combine. Reduce heat to low, cover, and braise for 15 minutes.
5. **Reduce the Sauce and Enhance Flavor**: Remove the lid and turn the heat to high. Pour in about 2/3 cup of coconut juice. Boil vigorously for about 3 minutes, then remove the prawns and plate them. Continue boiling the sauce in the pan over high heat until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
   **Key Point**: Adding coconut juice is the core flavor enhancer for this dish.
6. **Plate**: Pour the thickened sauce evenly over the prawns. Garnish with white sesame seeds.

## Health Advice
This dish offers high-protein, low-fat prawns. Ginger, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns help warm the stomach and dispel cold. However, note that beer and rock sugar add significant calories, so moderate consumption is advised. Suitable for fitness muscle-building or occasional indulgence.
