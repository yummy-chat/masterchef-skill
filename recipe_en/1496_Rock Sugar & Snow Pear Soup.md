# Rock Sugar & Snow Pear Soup
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍐 3 snow pears (washed in diluted salt water, sliced thin with skin on, or hollowed out to make pear bowls)
🍄 About 1 bowl of snow fungus (soaked in advance, torn into small florets)
🌰 About 10 lotus seeds (soaked in advance)
🟤 3 red dates
🔴 A few goji berries
🍯 Yellow rock sugar to taste (old rock sugar recommended)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🫕 Clay pot
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Soup ladle
♨️ Steamer
🥣 Bowls, plates

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare ingredients**: Wash snow pears in diluted salt water, slice thin with skin on; soak snow fungus in advance, then tear into small florets.
   **Key point**: Keep the pear skin; slicing thin helps release flavor during simmering; snow fungus must be fully soaked until soft.

2. **Simmer snow fungus**: Fill the clay pot with cold water, add snow fungus, heat over low flame until just before boiling, then cover and simmer for 20 minutes, until the liquid starts to thicken.
   **Key point**: Start with cold water and slow simmer over low heat to fully release the gelatin from the snow fungus.

3. **Add pear and lotus seeds**: Pour in pear slices and lotus seeds, cover, and continue to simmer over low heat for 30 minutes, until the liquid is noticeably thick and slightly yellow.
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low during this stage; no need to stir frequently.

4. **Season and finish simmering**: Add yellow rock sugar, red dates, and goji berries. Gently stir until the sugar dissolves, then cover and simmer for another 20 minutes over low heat.
   **Key point**: Add rock sugar at the end; adding too early may cause sticking to the pot, too late won't infuse flavor well; total simmering time is about 1 hour 20 minutes.

5. **Make pear bowls**: Fill hollowed-out pear shells with the simmered soup ingredients, cover with the pear cap, and steam for 20 minutes to create a more refined presentation.
   **Key point**: This step is optional, used to enhance the appearance; do not steam too long or the pear flesh may collapse.

## Health Tips
This rock sugar snow pear soup is rich in gelatin and vitamins. It helps moisten the lungs and relieve coughs, making it especially suitable for dry winter conditions. Diabetics should monitor sugar intake; snow fungus must be thoroughly simmered to aid digestion.
