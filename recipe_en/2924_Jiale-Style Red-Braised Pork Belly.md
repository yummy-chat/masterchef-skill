# Jiale-Style Red-Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with skin, about 2 jin (cut into 3.5cm cubes, must have skin, with alternating fat and lean layers)
🧄 Garlic cloves, 3-4 cloves
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 5 pieces (optional)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small amount (optional)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for garnish)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🍶 Rice wine, appropriate amount (about 1-2 tablespoons)
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Dark soy sauce, a small amount
⭐ Star anise, 4 pieces
🌿 Cinnamon sticks, 2 pieces
🍃 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
🍬 Rock sugar (piece sugar), 1 piece
💧 Water, appropriate amount (must fully submerge the pork)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍲 Clay pot

## Preparation Steps
1. Select pork belly with distinct layers of fat and lean meat, with skin on. Wash and trim away excess lean meat, then cut into cubes of about 3.5 cm.
**Note**: The skin must be retained, and the cube size should be moderate to ensure proper texture.

2. Heat the wok over high heat without adding oil. Directly add the pork belly cubes and stir-fry over medium-low heat.
**Key Point**: Fry until the surface turns slightly yellow and a fragrant, caramelized aroma emerges. Render out excess fat, then remove and set aside. This step helps set the shape, enhance flavor, and reduce greasiness.

3. Using the rendered pork fat in the wok, sauté the garlic cloves until fragrant.
**Tip**: Using the original pork fat from the wok enhances the authentic flavor.

4. Return the fried pork belly to the wok and stir-fry evenly. Drizzle rice wine along the edge of the wok to release the alcohol aroma and remove any gamey smell.
5. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, star anise, cinnamon sticks, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry evenly to color the meat and allow it to absorb the spice flavors.
6. Add enough water to fully submerge the pork.
7. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Add the rock sugar, cover the wok with a lid, and braise for about 40 minutes.
**Key Point**: Do not reduce to low heat; maintain medium heat to allow the sauce to reduce moderately, otherwise the braised pork may become too greasy.

8. After 40 minutes of braising, remove the lid to check. The sauce should be reduced to a thick consistency that clings to the back of a spoon, and the meat cubes should be glossy and translucent red.
9. Transfer the braised pork to a clay pot for insulation. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve.
**Note**: A clay pot retains heat better; avoid continued heating in the original wok to prevent gelatin from separating and burning.

## Health Advice
This braised pork belly is high in fat. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables and limit each serving to 3-4 pieces. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure; those with high blood lipids should exercise restraint.
