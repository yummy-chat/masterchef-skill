# Home-style Bishan Rabbit
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐇 Rabbit 1 (cut into pieces)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic cloves appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into rings/sections)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections, seeds removed)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (for garnish)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating)
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount (for marinating)
🌫️ White pepper powder appropriate amount (for marinating)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for marinating)
🥚 Egg white 1 (for marinating)
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for marinating)
🫒 Salad oil a small amount (for sealing the marinade)
🌶️ Green and red Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base)
🫘 Pixian bean paste 1 large tablespoon (for stir-frying until red oil releases)
🍂 Thirteen-spice powder appropriate amount (for seasoning when returning to the pan)
🍺 Beer about half a can (poured along the edge of the pan)
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍎 Vinegar a small amount
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount
🥃 Sichuan peppercorn oil appropriate amount (before removing from heat)
🫗 Sesame oil appropriate amount (before removing from heat)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪣 Mixing bowl
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
✂️ Scissors
🍳 Wok/iron pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Iron plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the whole rabbit thoroughly and cut into evenly sized pieces. Rinse off all blood water completely.
2. Place the rabbit pieces in a bowl. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, cooking wine, egg white, and cornstarch. Mix and knead until sticky. Finally, drizzle a small amount of salad oil and mix well. Set aside to marinate for a short while.
   **Key Point**: Sealing with oil locks in moisture and prevents the pieces from sticking together when cooking.

3. Slice the ginger, slice the garlic cloves, and cut the scallions into rings. Cut the dried chili peppers into sections and use a bamboo sieve to shake out most of the seeds. Wash the cilantro and set aside.
4. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the dried chili pepper sections and boil for about 5 minutes until the peppers soften. Remove and drain, set aside.
   **Purpose**: To make sticky rice chili, which releases red oil easily during stir-frying and is less prone to burning.

5. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until it reaches 70% hot (about 210°C/410°F). Scatter the marinated rabbit pieces into the oil and quickly stir to separate. Fry for about 30 seconds until the color changes and the meat is just cooked. Immediately remove and drain the oil.
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature must be high, and the action quick, so the outer layer of the rabbit sets while the inside stays tender.

6. Heat oil in another wok. When the oil reaches 50% hot (about 150°C/300°F), add the green and red Sichuan peppercorns, immediately followed by the boiled dried chili peppers, sliced ginger and garlic, and Pixian bean paste. Stir-fry over low heat slowly until fragrant and the red oil releases, until the peppers are glossy and the oil turns red.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent the spices from burning and turning bitter.

7. Once the base is fragrant, add the oil-blanched rabbit pieces and stir-fry evenly. Pour the beer along the edge of the wok. Add thirteen-spice powder, light soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until well combined. Finally, add the scallion sections, drizzle in Sichuan peppercorn oil and sesame oil, stir a few times, and immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key Point**: Add the scallion sections and the last two oils just before removing from the heat to preserve their fresh aroma.

8. Transfer the stir-fried rabbit along with the aromatics to a serving plate. Garnish with cilantro and serve.

## Health Advice
Rabbit meat is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing to control cholesterol. However, using egg white and cornstarch during marination will slightly increase the calorie count. Chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns can boost metabolism, but those with sensitive stomachs are advised to reduce the amount of dried chili pepper seeds used.
