# Whole Egg Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 6  
🍰 Cake flour 300g (already contains sugar, no extra sugar needed)  
💧 Water 75g  
🫒 Cooking oil 75g (vegetable oil)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🔌 Electric mixer  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🖌️ Oil brush  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Crack the eggs into a glass bowl, add water, pour in the cake flour, and stir with a hand whisk for 1 minute until the batter turns golden yellow.  
   **Note**: The cake flour already contains sugar, so no extra sugar is needed.  
2. Switch to an electric mixer and beat the batter for 5 minutes until it turns white, smooth, and fluffy.  
3. Slowly pour in the cooking oil while stirring continuously with a hand whisk until no traces of oil are visible in the batter.  
4. Lightly brush a thin layer of oil on the inner pot of the rice cooker, then pour the batter into the pot.  
   **Note**: Brushing oil helps release the cake and prevents sticking.  
5. Place in the rice cooker, select the "Porridge" or "Soup" function, and heat for 40 minutes.  
6. After heating, remove the inner pot, invert the cake to release it, transfer to a plate, and cut into pieces.  
   **Doneness check**: The cake is fully set with a golden, fluffy surface and a fine, even internal texture.  

## Health Advice  
Whole egg cake is suitable as a simple homemade dessert. Eggs provide high-quality protein, but the cake flour contains sugar and the overall calorie content is relatively high. It is recommended to control portions, eating 1–2 pieces per serving. Suitable for family baking or afternoon tea. Diabetic patients should consume with caution.
