# Glutinous Rice and White Radish Meatballs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 1 white radish (about 1 lb, peeled and shredded)  
🥩 About 1 lb pork front leg meat (minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (some minced for the filling, some chopped for garnish)  
🍚 One bowl of glutinous rice (about 200g, pre-soaked)  
🥚 1 egg  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for drawing out water and seasoning)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🌿 Ground white pepper, to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥒 Box grater  
🥣 2 bowls  
🥢 Chopsticks/spoon  
🍽️ Plate  
♨️ Steamer  

## Steps
1.  Peel the white radish and shred it into fine strips. Mix with an appropriate amount of salt and let it sit for 10 minutes to draw out the water.
   **Key Point**: Use a moderate amount of salt; the goal is to extract water from the radish.
2.  Finely chop the red bell pepper and mince the scallions. Set aside.
3.  Place the minced pork in a bowl. Add the chopped red pepper, some of the minced scallions, salt, light soy sauce, ground white pepper, cooking wine, and oyster sauce. Crack in the egg and mix thoroughly until the meat mixture becomes sticky and firm.
   **Key Point**: Mix until the filling becomes黏性 (sticky) for a better texture.
4.  Squeeze the water out of the salted radish strips thoroughly, then add them to the seasoned meat mixture. Mix again until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to squeeze the radish strips dry to prevent the filling from being too wet and difficult to shape.
5.  Evenly spread a layer of the pre-soaked glutinous rice on the bottom of a plate.
6.  Take an appropriate amount of the filling and roll it between your palms to form smooth, evenly-sized round balls.
7.  Roll each formed ball in another bowl of glutinous rice until the entire surface is evenly coated.
   **Key Point**: Ensure the meatballs are completely covered with glutinous rice with no gaps.
8.  Arrange the coated meatballs neatly on the rice-lined plate. Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Place the plate in the steamer once the water is boiling, cover, and steam over medium-high heat for 15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Make sure to put the plate in only after the water is boiling, and time it accurately to ensure the best texture.
9.  After steaming, remove the lid and sprinkle the remaining chopped scallions on top before serving.

## Health Note
This dish combines white radish with glutinous rice. The meat filling is juicy, and the low-oil steaming method makes it suitable for warming the body in winter with minimal digestive burden. Ensure the radish is well-drained to prevent a soggy texture. It is safe and enjoyable for both the elderly and children.
