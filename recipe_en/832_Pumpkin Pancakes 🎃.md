# Pumpkin Pancakes 🎃
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🎃 250g pumpkin (peeled, cut into pieces, steamed, and mashed into puree)
🍚 250g glutinous rice flour
🌾 80g all-purpose flour (reduces stickiness, increases chewiness)
🍞 3g instant yeast
🍬 150g brown sugar
🥜 60g peanuts (crushed into pieces)
🍯 30g sesame powder
🖤 Black sesame seeds, as needed (for decoration)
🌾 A little cooked flour (optional, to adjust the sweetness of the filling)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥘 Steaming tray
🥣 Large bowl
📋 Cutting board
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Spoon
📦 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1.  Peel and cut the pumpkin into chunks, spread them on a steaming tray, and steam until fully cooked. Remove and immediately mash into a smooth puree.
   **Note**: Using a steaming tray instead of a deep bowl is recommended to avoid excess water absorption, which could affect the dough's firmness.

2.  Mix the 250g of glutinous rice flour and 80g of all-purpose flour in a bowl. In a separate bowl, thoroughly combine the brown sugar, crushed peanuts, and sesame powder. For a less sweet filling, you can add a little cooked flour to adjust.

3.  Add the yeast to the pumpkin puree and stir well. Gradually add the mixed flours to the puree, kneading constantly until a smooth, non-sticky dough forms.
   **Key Point**: Add the flour mixture in batches to better control the dough's firmness.

4.  Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for about 20 minutes, until it becomes slightly puffy.

5.  Divide the rested dough into equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball, then flatten it into a disc. Place a spoonful of the brown sugar, sesame, and peanut filling in the center. Use a "gathering" technique (pleating and pinching) to seal the filling inside. Roll back into a ball, then gently flatten into a patty. Place the pancake seam-side down. You can press black sesame seeds onto the surface for decoration.
   **Key Point**: While sealing, rotate and gather the dough firmly to ensure the seal is tight, preventing the filling from leaking out during frying.

6.  Brush a thin layer of oil in a non-stick frying pan over low heat. Place the pumpkin pancakes in the pan. Gently rotate or move the pancakes with your hand or a spatula to prevent sticking. Fry the bottom for about 3 minutes until golden brown, then flip. After flipping, gently press down on the pancakes with a spatula to ensure even heating. Fry for another 3 minutes, or until both sides are golden brown and the pancakes puff up. Remove from the pan.

## Health Tips
Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene, making it suitable for those who use their eyes a lot or need an immunity boost. However, glutinous rice flour and brown sugar are high in calories, so it's recommended to eat only 1-2 pieces per serving during a diet. The filling of peanuts and sesame provides healthy fats and gives a rich aroma when fried.
