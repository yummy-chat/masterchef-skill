# Poor Man’s Crab Roe Congee 🍲
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🍚 Leftover rice – 1 bowl  
🦐 Fresh shrimp – 4-5 pieces (peeled, tails left intact)  
🧈 Firm or silken tofu – 1 block (diced)  
🥬 Leafy greens – 1 handful (finely chopped)  
🥫 Crab roe paste / salted egg yolk paste – to taste (for mixing with rice)  
🫒 Vegetable oil – as needed  
💧 Water (or broth) – as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment 🍳
🥣 Bowl  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🍲 Clay pot or soup pot  
🥄 Soup ladle

## Instructions 📝
1. Peel the fresh shrimp, leaving the tails intact, rinse and drain. Dice the tofu into small cubes, wash and finely chop the greens. Take the crab roe paste and thoroughly mix it into the leftover rice until the grains are evenly coated and colored.
2. Pour a small amount of vegetable oil into a non-stick pan, add the shrimp along with a splash of water. Pan-fry over medium heat until the shrimp shells turn red and the liquid becomes orange-red. Gently press the shrimp with a spatula to help release their umami and color. Once done, remove the shrimp and set aside, keeping the shrimp oil and liquid in the pan.
3. Pour the seasoned rice into the pan and stir-fry over medium heat until the grains are well coated with the shrimp oil.
4. Add enough water to the pan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cook until the rice is soft and broken down, and the mixture thickens into a congee-like consistency.
5. Once the congee has thickened, first add the diced tofu, then the cooked shrimp and chopped greens. Cook briefly until the greens are just wilted and the tofu is heated through, then turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: The video does not show seasoning with salt or other spices. Adjust by adding salt or white pepper to taste before serving, as desired.

## Health Tips 💚
This congee uses shrimp oil and crab roe paste to mimic the flavor of crab, offering good value for money. However, it is advisable to limit the amount of salted egg yolk paste to avoid excessive sodium intake. The addition of tofu and greens provides dietary fiber and calcium, making this dish suitable for those looking to satisfy a craving while managing calorie intake.
