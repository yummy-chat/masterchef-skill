# Spicy Hand-Torn King Oyster Mushroom
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍄 King oyster mushroom, about 300g
🌶️ Chili powder, to taste (adjust according to preference)
🫘 White sesame seeds, to taste
🫒 Sesame oil, sufficient (for frying and leaving base oil)
🧂 Salt, to taste (adjust according to preference)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥣 Glass bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the king oyster mushrooms. Use your hands to tear them into evenly thick strips along the grain.
**Key point**: Hand-tearing yields a better texture than cutting with a knife, and the strips absorb seasonings more easily.
2. Pour a sufficient amount of sesame oil into the pan. Heat over medium heat until bubbles form on the surface.
3. Add all the king oyster mushroom strips into the hot oil at once. Keep stirring over medium heat continuously.
4. Fry until the mushrooms become soft, shrink in size, and turn light yellow on the surface, indicating that most of the moisture has been fried off.
5. Use a slotted spoon to remove the mushrooms and drain the oil. Transfer them to a clean bowl and set aside. Leave the base oil in the pan; do not discard it.
6. Using the remaining base oil in the pan, turn the heat to the lowest setting or turn it off. Add the chili powder and white sesame seeds, using the residual heat to release their fragrance. Stir until the oil turns a bright red color.
**Note**: Avoid overheating to prevent burning.
7. Pour the prepared spicy oil mixture into the bowl with the mushrooms. Wear gloves and mix thoroughly by hand, ensuring every strip of mushroom is evenly coated. At this point, add an appropriate amount of salt according to taste. If time permits, marinating overnight will enhance the flavor.
**Key point**: Adjust salt according to taste at this step. If time allows, overnight marination yields even better flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish uses sesame oil to fry the king oyster mushrooms, making it relatively high in calories, so it is suitable as a side dish rather than a main dish. Chili powder can boost metabolism, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation. Hand-tearing the mushrooms allows them to absorb flavor more easily than cutting, and it is recommended to pair the dish with light vegetables to balance the taste.
