# Steamed Pork Ribs with Fermented Black Beans

Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps  
1. Place the ribs in a bowl, add 1 tablespoon of cornstarch and water, then rub and rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear.  
**Key point**: Using starch to wash the ribs effectively removes odor and blood.  
2. Drain the cleaned ribs in a colander bowl, then pat dry with kitchen paper to remove surface moisture.  
**Key point**: The ribs must have no raw water to better absorb the seasonings and prevent excess liquid during steaming.  
3. Heat a little oil in a wok over low heat, stir-fry the fermented black beans until fragrant, then add the minced garlic and crushed ginger, and cook until aromatic. Turn off the heat, then add salt, white sugar, white pepper powder, MSG, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce, mixing well with residual heat.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry the black beans over low heat to prevent burning; add liquid seasonings, salt, and sugar after turning off the heat to avoid scorching.  
4. Pour the cooled black bean sauce over the drained ribs, then add 1 tablespoon of cornstarch and a little dark soy sauce. Mix thoroughly and marinate for 15 minutes.  
**Key point**: Ensure each rib is evenly coated with the sauce and starch.  
5. Spread the cut taro chunks on the bottom of a plate, then evenly arrange the marinated ribs on top, avoiding excessive stacking.  
**Key point**: Spread the ribs out to ensure even heating and thorough cooking.  
6. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the plate inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 20–25 minutes, until the ribs are fully cooked and tender.  
**Key point**: Steam when the water is boiling; use high heat to quickly seal in the juices.  
7. Once steamed, remove from the steamer, and garnish with chopped green onions.  

## Health Tips  
Steamed pork ribs with fermented black beans use taro at the bottom to absorb oil, reducing fat intake while allowing the taro to soak up the meat juices. Ribs are rich in protein, suitable for replenishing energy, but portion control is advised to avoid excess salt. This dish is steamed until tender, making it suitable for all ages.
