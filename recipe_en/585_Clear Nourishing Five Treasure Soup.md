# Clear Nourishing Five Treasure Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Mao root (Imperata cylindrica) 1 small bundle (tied up)  
🎋 Bamboo sugarcane 适量 (cut into segments)  
🥕 Carrot 适量 (cut into segments)  
💧 Water chestnuts 适量 (peeled and halved)  
🌽 Corn 适量 (cut into segments, keep the corn silk)  
🧂 Salt a tiny pinch (trace amount, to enhance freshness and bring out sweetness)

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place mao root, bamboo sugarcane, carrot, water chestnuts, and corn segments with silk into the soup pot.
**Key point**: Do not discard the corn silk; add it to the pot as well.
2. Add enough water to fully submerge all ingredients.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 30 minutes to 1 hour.
4. Once cooked, remove and discard the mao root residue.
5. Add a tiny pinch of salt to the soup to season.
**Note**: The soup is naturally sweet; salt is only used to enhance flavor. You should not taste saltiness, and no sugar is needed.

## Health Tips
This clear nourishing five treasure soup is rich in dietary fiber and natural minerals. It is ideal for moistening the lungs and reducing internal heat during seasonal changes, especially recommended for those prone to heatiness or who stay up late. Note that cooking time should not be too long, otherwise the sweetness may turn bitter; use only a tiny pinch of salt to enhance freshness, and do not overdo it.
