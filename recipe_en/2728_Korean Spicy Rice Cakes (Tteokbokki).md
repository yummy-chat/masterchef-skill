# Korean Spicy Rice Cakes (Tteokbokki)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍡 Korean small oblong rice cakes 200g  
🍥 Sliced fish cake 100g  
🧅 Onion 50g (shredded)  
🟢 Scallion 50g (cut into sections)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 15g (optional)  
🫙 Korean chili paste (gochujang) 50g  
🍅 Ketchup 20g  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 20g  
🥄 Soy sauce 20g  
🧂 Salt 2g  
🍬 White sugar 30g  
🧄 Minced garlic 20g  
🧂 MSG 2g (optional)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 2g (optional)  
🍖 Dasida beef stock powder 5g (optional, beef seasoning powder)  
💧 Water 300g (used in stages: 100g for the sauce, 200g for cooking; the 200g portion can be substituted with stock)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Strainer  
🥢 Silicone spatula  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the large sheet of fish cake into bite-sized pieces, such as triangles, rectangles, or squares.
2. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the rice cakes and cut fish cake pieces. Blanch them, then remove and drain.  
   **Key Point**: Blanching removes excess starch from the rice cakes and any unpleasant smell from the fish cake, improving the texture.
3. In a bowl, add in order the Korean chili paste, ketchup, fine chili powder, soy sauce, salt, white sugar, minced garlic, and the optional MSG, chicken bouillon powder, and Dasida beef stock powder. Then pour in 100g of water, and stir until completely combined.  
   **Key Point**: The sauce is the core flavor of the whole dish. Ensure it is mixed thoroughly. The taste can be adjusted slightly according to personal preference.
4. Pour the prepared sauce into a non-stick pan. Add 200g of water and bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Key Point**: The liquid ratio determines the final consistency. Using stock for the 200g portion can enhance the depth of flavor.
5. Add the shredded onion and scallion sections.
6. Add the blanched rice cakes, fish cake pieces, and the butter pieces.
7. Bring to a boil over high heat until the butter has completely melted, then reduce the heat to low. Cover with a lid and simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring several times during cooking to prevent sticking to the bottom of the pan.  
   **State Check**: The sauce will gradually thicken.
8. Once the sauce has thickened and evenly coats the ingredients, turn off the heat. If desired, place a slice of cheese on top to melt. Transfer to a serving plate and sprinkle with white sesame seeds before eating.  
   **Key Point**: Watch closely in the last few minutes to prevent the sauce from drying out.

## Health Advice  
This Korean spicy rice cake dish has a rich sauce and is high in carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise or as an occasional indulgent meal. Be mindful of its high sugar and sodium content. Individuals with high blood pressure or those managing sugar intake are advised to reduce the white sugar and sauce quantities by half.
