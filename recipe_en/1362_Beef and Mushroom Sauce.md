# Beef and Mushroom Sauce  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Year Round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms – to taste (diced)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers – to taste (finely chopped)  
🧅 Small shallots – to taste (finely chopped)  
🥩 Ground beef – to taste  
🧄 Minced garlic – to taste  
🫚 Minced ginger – to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil – to taste (for the base)  
🥫 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste) – 1 part  
🟤 Soybean paste – 2 parts  
🍯 Sweet bean sauce – ½ part  
🍶 Yellow rice wine – to taste  
🧴 Light soy sauce – to taste  
🍬 Rock sugar – to taste  
🧂 White pepper – to taste  
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder – to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil – to taste (added at the end for aroma)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Chinese wok  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Dice the fresh shiitake mushrooms, finely chop the red chili peppers, finely chop the small shallots, mince the garlic, and mince the ginger. Set aside.  
2. Add cooking oil to the wok, place the ground beef in cold oil, and stir-fry over low heat.  
   **Key Point**: Stir continuously until the moisture is completely evaporated, the oil becomes even and fine, and the beef is fully dry-fried. This is key to a rich sauce aroma.  
3. Add the chopped shallots and diced shiitake mushrooms, and stir-fry until the mushroom surface is slightly dry.  
4. Add the minced garlic, minced ginger, and chopped red chili peppers, and stir-fry until the peppers slightly change color.  
5. Add the Pixian doubanjiang, soybean paste, and sweet bean sauce. Stir-fry until fragrant and well combined.  
6. Add the yellow rice wine, light soy sauce, and rock sugar. Reduce to low heat and simmer slowly, stirring constantly to prevent sticking.  
   **Doneness Check**: Continue simmering until the sauce "releases oil," meaning the moisture has evaporated, the oil separates again, and the surface appears glossy.  
7. After the oil releases, sprinkle in the white pepper and thirteen-spice powder, and mix well. Finally, drizzle in the sesame oil, stir quickly to combine, then turn off the heat.  
8. Transfer to a serving dish. Store at room temperature or in the refrigerator for later use.  

## Health Advice  
Beef and mushroom sauce is rich in shiitake mushroom polysaccharides and heme iron, making it suitable for those needing energy and trace elements. However, its sodium content is relatively high, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in reduced amounts. Ensure the moisture is fully dried during preparation to enhance the sauce's aroma and extend its shelf life.
