# Apple Pork Rib Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs: 3 spareribs
🍎 Apple: moderate amount (peeled and cored, cut into pieces)
🥕 Carrot: moderate amount (peeled, cut into pieces)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms: moderate amount (pre-soaked until soft)
🫚 Ginger: moderate amount (sliced, for blanching)
🧅 Scallion: moderate amount (whole or tied in a knot, for blanching)

> Note: Weidamei Yuhaichu Haichang Xian is an optional seasoning mentioned in the video, added before consumption to enhance umami flavor, not used in the core cooking steps.

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Soup pot/wok (for blanching)
♨️ Pressure cooker
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥒 Vegetable peeler
🥣 Baby food processor
👝 Piping bag
🧊 Freezer mold (lidded ice cube tray or baby food tray)
🍚 Rice cooker (for final noodle cooking)

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the pork ribs under clean water; peel and core the carrot and apple, then cut into pieces; pre-soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in cold water until soft; slice the ginger and tie the scallion into a knot, set aside.
2. Place the pork ribs in cold water in a pot, add the ginger slices and scallion knot. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium heat and continue boiling for several minutes until the broth is clear and no more blood foam appears.
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps fully release blood from the ribs; ginger and scallion effectively remove any gamey smell.
3. Remove the blanched ribs and place them together with the carrot pieces, apple pieces, and shiitake mushrooms into a pressure cooker. Add enough water, heat over high heat until steam builds, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook until the ribs are tender and the vegetables are fully cooked.
4. After cooking, take out the ribs, remove the bones and pick out the meat. Place the meat with an appropriate amount of broth into the baby food processor and blend until smooth.
5. Transfer the blended meat puree into a piping bag, evenly squeeze it into each compartment of the freezer mold, cover with the lid, and freeze in the refrigerator until completely solid.
   **Key point**: Using a piping bag makes the filling process cleaner; the frozen "soup stock cubes" can be stored long-term and used as needed.
6. When serving, take an appropriate amount of frozen "soup stock cubes" and place them in a pot with water. Heat until fully melted and the broth comes to a boil. It can also be cooked together with noodles or other staples for a quick meal.

## Health Tips
Apple pork rib soup offers a balanced nutritional mix, with ribs providing high-quality protein and calcium, and apples and carrots supplementing vitamins. It is suitable for baby complementary feeding to boost immunity. Ensure bones are completely removed to avoid choking hazards, and frozen stock cubes should not be stored for more than one month.
