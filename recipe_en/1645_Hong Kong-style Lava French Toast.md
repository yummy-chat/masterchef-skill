# Hong Kong-style Lava French Toast
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 2 thick slices of bread (about 3cm thick, low fermentation, with good rebound when pressed)  
🥜 Peanut butter, to taste  
🥚 2 eggs  
🥛 Evaporated milk, as needed (for egg mixture, 1 tablespoon per egg) + 100g (for filling)  
🧈 10g butter (for filling) + some (for frying)  
🧂 20g condensed milk (for filling) + a little (to bind the bread)  
🍫 Ovaltine powder, to taste (or cocoa powder as a substitute)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowls and a deep dish  
🥄 Spoons  
🥛 Small saucepan  
🧂 Sieve  
🥢 Chopsticks or spatula  

## Instructions  
1. Cut off the crusts from the thick slices of bread. Spread a generous amount of peanut butter on one slice, top with the other slice, and press down lightly to seal.
**Note:** Choose bread that springs back quickly when pressed, so the shape remains upright after frying.
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add evaporated milk at a ratio of 1 tablespoon per egg, and whisk thoroughly until the color is uniform. Pour the mixture into a deep dish and set aside.
3. Prepare the milk filling: In a small saucepan, combine 10g butter, 100g evaporated milk, and 20g condensed milk. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly, until the liquid thickens, then remove from the heat.
**Key:** It is essential to cook until thick; otherwise, the filling will be too runny and leak from the bread's indentation.
4. Preheat a non-stick frying pan over low heat and melt a small piece of butter. Dip the peanut butter sandwich into the egg mixture, ensuring both sides and all four edges are evenly coated.
5. Place the egg-coated sandwich into the pan. Fry all four edges first until golden, then fry both flat sides. Keep the heat low throughout, and cook until each side is an even golden brown. Remove from the pan.
**Note:** Fry each side thoroughly. Slow cooking over low heat prevents burning.
6. Stack the two fried slices of French toast on top of each other, spreading a little condensed milk between them to help them stick. Use the back of a spoon to press a well into the center of the top slice.
7. Pour the pre-cooked thick milk filling into the well. Finally, use a sieve to dust a layer of Ovaltine powder evenly over the top. The filling will flow out when the toast is cut open.

## Health Advice  
This lava French toast is high in calories, with generous amounts of peanut butter, condensed milk, and butter. It is suitable for occasional indulgence rather than as a daily breakfast. Pair it with black coffee or unsweetened tea to cut the richness. Those with high blood sugar should consume it in moderation.
