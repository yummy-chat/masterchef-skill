# Garlic Oyster Sauce Lettuce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Lettuce 2 heads (separate leaves one by one, wash and remove old leaves)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Small chili peppers appropriate amount (sliced into rings, used to enhance color and flavor of the sauce)
🧅 Scallions about 2-3 stalks (chopped into green onion pieces)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
💧 Water a small amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥘 Stockpot (for blanching lettuce)
🍳 Small milk pot (for making hot sauce)
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the washed lettuce and blanch. Once the lettuce turns dark green and softens, quickly remove and drain, then arrange neatly on a plate.
2. In another small pot, heat oil over low heat, add minced garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.
3. Pour light soy sauce and oyster sauce into the pot, add a small amount of water, sprinkle with chopped scallions and small chili pepper rings, and cook until the sauce boils, then turn off the heat.
4. Evenly pour the hot sauce over the arranged lettuce and serve immediately while hot.

## Health Advice
Blanching lettuce results in minimal nutrient loss, and it is rich in vitamins and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing low-calorie, high-satiety meals. While the garlic oyster sauce adds flavor, oyster sauce and light soy sauce are high in sodium, so those sensitive to blood pressure should control their intake.
