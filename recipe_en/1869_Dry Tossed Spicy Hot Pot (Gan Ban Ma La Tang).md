# Dry Tossed Spicy Hot Pot (Gan Ban Ma La Tang)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts 50g (crushed into granular pieces)
🍡 Assorted hot pot balls, to taste (such as fish balls, crab sticks, luncheon meat, etc.)
🥔 Heat-tolerant vegetables, to taste (such as lotus root slices, potato slices, enoki mushrooms, seaweed, etc.)
🥬 Leafy green vegetables, to taste (such as spinach, cilantro, lettuce, etc.)
🍜 Staple food, to taste (wide noodles, yellow noodles, gluten noodles or instant noodles; wide noodles etc. need to be pre-soaked until soft)
🧄 Garlic 50g (30g pressed into paste for base sauce, 20g minced for chili oil)
🥜 Sesame paste 50g
🥜 Peanut butter 50g
🧀 Red fermented bean curd 40g (about 2 pieces)
🥫 Light soy sauce 20g
🥫 Oyster sauce 20g
🍬 White sugar 20g
🍶 Aged vinegar 30g
🌶 Sichuan pepper oil 10g
🧂 Monosodium glutamate (MSG) 3g (optional)
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 3g (optional)
🌶 Chili flakes 20g
🤍 White sesame seeds 10g
🌿 Cumin powder/seeds 10g
🌶 Ground Sichuan pepper 2g
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for oil splashing and frying peanuts)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Air fryer or oven
🥄 Small pot (for boiling ingredients and heating oil)
🥣 Large bowl
🥣 Small bowl
🔪 Blender or food processor
🧄 Garlic press
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks or tongs
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make peanut crumbs**: Lightly spray peanuts with oil and shake to coat. Place in an air fryer and roast at 180°C for 7 minutes. Remove and pour into a blender. Pulse until crumbly with some texture remaining. Set aside.
2. **Prepare base sauce**: In a large bowl, combine sesame paste, peanut butter, red fermented bean curd, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, aged vinegar, Sichuan pepper oil, MSG, and chicken bouillon powder. Mix well. Gradually add 30-50g of water, stirring until the consistency resembles yogurt. Add 30g of pressed garlic paste and stir again to combine. Set aside.
**Note**: The sauce will thicken as you stir; adjust the amount of water based on the actual texture of the sauce.
3. **Prepare chili oil**: In a small bowl, combine chili flakes, white sesame seeds, cumin powder, and ground Sichuan pepper. In a separate pot, heat oil until it begins to smoke, then turn off the heat. Wait until the smoke visibly subsides. Gradually pour the hot oil over the dry ingredients in batches, stirring continuously. Finally, add 20g of minced garlic and mix well.
**Key point**: Allow the hot oil to cool slightly before pouring to prevent burning the spices.
4. **Cook ingredients**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. First, add the hot pot balls and all heat-tolerant vegetables. Cook until done, then add the leafy green vegetables. Blanch until just wilted, then remove and drain thoroughly. Transfer to the bowl with the base sauce.
5. **Cook staple food**: Using fresh water or the same pot, cook the pre-soaked wide noodles, noodles, etc. until they reach the desired doneness. For instant noodles, cooking until they still have a slight bite in the center is ideal. After cooking, drain well and add to the bowl with the base sauce.
6. **Mix and combine**: Pour the prepared chili oil and peanut crumbs into the bowl. Use tongs or chopsticks to thoroughly toss and mix all ingredients with the sauces until evenly coated.

## Health Advice
This dry tossed spicy hot pot has a rich sauce, with peanut crumbs and sesame paste adding quality fats and protein. However, the total calorie content is not low. It is recommended to pair it with more leafy green vegetables to balance nutrition. It is suitable as an occasional indulgent, heavy-flavored meal, and portion control of processed items like hot pot balls should be observed.
