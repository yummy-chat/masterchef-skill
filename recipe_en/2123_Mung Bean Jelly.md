# Mung Bean Jelly

Cuisine Type: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 500g mung beans  
💧 Water, as needed (for blending and starch extraction)  
🧂 1 tablespoon salt  
🧂 1/2 tablespoon edible alkali  
🧄 Garlic, minced, as needed  
🍶 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🍶 Aged vinegar, as needed  
💧 Purified water, as needed (for garlic-vinegar water)  
🌶️ 1 tablespoon chili sauce  
🌶️ Chili oil, as needed  
🥒 Pickled vegetables, finely diced, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, chopped, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🌀 Blender  
🫗 Cloth bag  
🍲 Large pot (stainless steel)  
🥄 Wooden spoon / spoon  
🫙 Container (plastic box)  
🔪 Scraper (jelly scraper) or kitchen knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Small pot  
🍜 Bowl

## Steps
1. Rinse the mung beans thoroughly, then soak in enough water for at least 3 hours until they are plump.
2. Place the soaked mung beans into the blender, add an appropriate amount of water, and blend repeatedly until you get a smooth mung bean paste.
3. Fill a large pot halfway with water, pour the mung bean paste into a cloth bag placed in the pot, tie the bag tightly, and repeatedly knead and wash the bag in the water to extract the mung bean starch, until the bag feels rough and the squeezed-out liquid runs clear.
4. Let the starch water sit for 2-3 hours, then skim off any foam from the surface. Once the starch has fully settled, carefully pour off the clear water on top, leaving only the raw mung bean starch slurry at the bottom.
5. Take about 150g of the settled raw starch slurry and put it in a pot. Add about 750ml of the clear mung bean water poured off earlier, then add 1 tablespoon of salt and 1/2 tablespoon of edible alkali. Stir to combine.
6. Cook over low heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking. Cook until the liquid thickens from a runny consistency, turns darker and translucent, and finally becomes flaky with large bubbles rising. Then turn off the heat.
7. Pour the thickened starch mixture into a clean container and let it cool naturally to set.
8. Mix minced garlic, salt, light soy sauce, aged vinegar, and purified water together to prepare the garlic-vinegar water. Set aside.
9. Once the jelly has completely cooled and set, unmold it. Use a wet scraper to scrape it into thin strips, or cut it into strips with a knife, and transfer to a bowl.
10. Pour the garlic-vinegar water over the jelly, add chili sauce and chili oil, then sprinkle with diced pickled vegetables and chopped scallions. Toss well and serve.

## Health Advice
Mung bean jelly is low in fat and high in fiber, making it suitable as a staple during weight loss. However, those with weaker digestion should consume it in moderation. Ensure adequate settling time during preparation, otherwise the jelly may lack a chewy and bouncy texture.
