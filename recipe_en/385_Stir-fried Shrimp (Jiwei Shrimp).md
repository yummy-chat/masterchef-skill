# Stir-fried Shrimp (Jiwei Shrimp)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Jiwei shrimp (appropriate amount, ask the vendor to cut into two or three sections, remove the whiskers and legs)
🧄 Ginger (appropriate amount, sliced)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections; reserve some scallion greens for garnish before serving)
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (appropriate amount, cut into segments)
🥄 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount)
🦪 Oyster sauce (appropriate amount)
🍬 Sugar (appropriate amount)
🍶 Cooking wine (appropriate amount)
🫒 Cooking oil (appropriate amount)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Wok spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the shrimp**: Cut the jiwei shrimp into sections, remove the whiskers and legs, wash and drain thoroughly, and set aside.
   **Key point**: Cutting into sections makes it easier to season and eat; the shrimp sections cook quickly and evenly over high heat.
2. **Aromatics**: Place the wok over heat and heat until hot. Add cooking oil, then add scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic slices, and dried chili pepper segments. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.
   **Heat level**: Medium-low heat, about 30 seconds, to prevent the aromatics from burning.
3. **Stir-fry the shrimp**: Turn the heat to high, add the prepared shrimp, and quickly stir-fry until the shrimp change color.
   **Heat level**: High heat, quickly stir-fry for 1–2 minutes. The shrimp turn from bluish-gray to red, indicating they are cooked through.
4. **Season**: Drizzle cooking wine along the edge of the wok, then add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar in order. Quickly stir-fry to combine evenly, ensuring the sauce coats the shrimp sections thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Keep stir-frying continuously after adding the seasonings to prevent sticking and to allow the flavors to penetrate the shrimp meat quickly.
5. **Finish and serve**: Sprinkle the reserved scallion greens over the shrimp, stir-fry a few times, and then remove from the wok to a serving plate.
   **Key point**: Add the scallion greens at the very end to preserve their vibrant green color and fresh aroma.

## Health Tips
Jiwei shrimp are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making them suitable for fitness and weight-loss diets. However, when cooking, be mindful of controlling the amount of oil and salt; people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soy sauce and oyster sauce added. Shrimp shells contain calcium, and after cutting into sections, they are easier to digest. Those with weak stomachs can also consume them in moderation.
