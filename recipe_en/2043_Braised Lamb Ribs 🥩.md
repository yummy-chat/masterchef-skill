# Braised Lamb Ribs 🥩
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lean lamb ribs, about 1000g (chopped into small pieces)
🥕 1 carrot (cut into rolling chunks)
🧅 2 scallions (cut into long sections)
🧄 1 large piece of ginger (sliced)
🌱 A few garlic sprouts (cut diagonally into small sections)
🌿 1 bay leaf
🤍 1 small piece of Angelica dahurica
⚪ White peppercorns, to taste
🌶️ 3 Fujian chili king peppers (dried chilies)
🧂 1 tablespoon baking soda (about 1g, for soaking)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🥫 Broad bean paste, about 5g
🟤 Caramel color, 20g
🧴 Dark soy sauce, 2g
🧂 Ground white pepper, 1 tablespoon (about 2g)
🧂 Table salt, about 6g (used in two separate additions)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 5g
💧 Water, as needed (enough to cover the ingredients)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🍲 Lid
🍽️ Bowls, plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the lamb ribs along the gaps between the bones, keeping every two ribs connected. Then chop into slightly larger pieces. Place in a bowl, add water and 1 tablespoon of baking soda, mix well, and soak for 1 hour.
   **Note**: Baking soda helps tenderize the meat, shortening the braising time.
2. Add cold water to a pot, put in the lamb ribs, pour in some cooking wine, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and once the water becomes cloudy, remove the ribs and drain.
   **Key**: Start with cold water to remove the gamey smell; the foam must be thoroughly skimmed.
3. Heat the wok, add a small amount of oil. Once the oil reaches 60% hot (about 180°C), add the lamb ribs and stir-fry over medium heat for about 3 minutes, until the surface tightens and turns slightly golden. Remove.
   **Purpose**: To seal the surface and remove any gamey odor.
4. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, dried chilies, Angelica dahurica, white peppercorns, and bay leaf. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant. Return the lamb ribs to the wok and stir-fry for 1 minute.
5. Add about 5g of broad bean paste, reduce the heat to low, and stir-fry slowly until the lamb ribs are evenly coated with red oil and color.
6. Pour a little cooking wine along the edge of the wok, stir-fry to combine. Then add enough water to submerge the lamb ribs by about 2 cm. Add the caramel color, dark soy sauce, ground white pepper, and about 3g of salt. Stir to combine.
   **Note**: Add enough water in one go.
7. Cover with the lid, simmer over low heat for 40 minutes. Remove the lid, pick out the already softened scallion sections, add the carrot chunks, cover again, and continue simmering over low heat for another 40 minutes.
   **Key**: Maintain low heat throughout to prevent the liquid from drying out.
8. Turn up to medium heat to reduce the sauce. Add the remaining 3g of salt and light soy sauce to adjust the seasoning. Cook until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Finally, add the garlic sprout sections and stir-fry just until they turn a bright green. Remove and serve.
   **Tip**: Keep the garlic sprouts vibrant green; stop cooking as soon as they are just done.

## Health Advice
Braised lamb ribs are rich in protein and iron, making them suitable for winter nourishment. Pay attention to salt intake; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. Baking soda helps tenderize the meat, but do not soak for too long to avoid affecting the texture.
