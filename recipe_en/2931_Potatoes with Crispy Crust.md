# Potatoes with Crispy Crust
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 600g (about 4 pieces, peeled)
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves (minced)
🧅 Scallions, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, to taste
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies, to taste (sliced into rings)
🌶️ Chili flakes 1 level tablespoon and a half (about 15g)
🤍 White sesame seeds 1 level tablespoon (about 10g)
🌿 Cumin powder or cumin seeds 1 level tablespoon (about 10g)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🍂 Bay leaf 1 piece
🌟 Star anise 3 small petals
🌳 Cassia bark a small pinch (about 0.2g)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about 10 pieces
🌱 Fennel seeds a small pinch (about 2g)
🧂 Salt 1 level teaspoon and a half (about 5g) + 2/3 teaspoon (for boiling potatoes)
🧂 MSG 1 teaspoon (about 3g)
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 1 teaspoon (about 3g)
🌿 Sichuan pepper powder 1 teaspoon (about 2g)
🍬 Sugar 2 level tablespoons (about 20g)
🍶 Aged vinegar 1 tablespoon and a half (about 15g)
🌾 Potato starch or cornstarch, as needed (add in small batches, three times total)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife (or crinkle cutter)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Small milk pot (small pot)
🥄 Strainer
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Cut the peeled potatoes into bite-sized rustic chunks. Immediately submerge in water to prevent oxidation and browning.
2. Prepare the sauce:
   - In a small bowl, place chili flakes, white sesame seeds, and cumin powder.
   - In a small pot, add cooking oil. Add bay leaf, star anise, cassia bark, Sichuan peppercorns, and fennel seeds to the cold oil. Heat over medium-low heat until the spices turn golden and the oil begins to smoke, then turn off the heat.
   - Use a strainer to quickly pour the hot oil into the bowl with dry ingredients. Stir rapidly to combine.
   - Add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon powder, Sichuan pepper powder, sugar, and aged vinegar to the chili oil. Stir well and set aside.
3. Blanch: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add 2/3 teaspoon of salt, then add the potato pieces. Cook for exactly 3 minutes. Remove and rinse under cold water, then drain thoroughly.
4. Coat with starch: Gradually add starch to the drained potatoes in small batches, mixing well each time until no dry powder is visible. Repeat three times total. The potato pieces should be separated with a dry surface and no excessive thick coating.
5. First fry: Add enough oil to the pot and heat to 170°C (340°F). Carefully add the potato pieces one by one along the edge of the pot. Fry over high heat for 2 minutes without stirring initially. Once set, use chopsticks to separate them. Remove after frying.
6. First re-fry: Raise the oil temperature to 180°C (356°F). Add the potato pieces and fry over high heat for 2 minutes until golden. Remove.
7. Second re-fry: Bring the oil temperature back to 180°C (356°F). Fry the potatoes for about 1 minute to make them extra crispy. Remove immediately after frying.
8. Mix: Place the fried potatoes in a large bowl. Add minced garlic, pour in the prepared sauce, then add scallions, cilantro, and bird's eye chilies. Mix well with your hands. Serve.

## Health Advice
Potatoes cooked with double frying are high in calories. It is recommended to consume one small bowl per serving and pair with leafy greens to balance the meal. This dish is high in carbohydrates and calories, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume with caution.
