# Chive Corn Pancakes  
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Chives, appropriate amount (finely chopped)  
🌽 Cornmeal, about one tablespoon  
🥚 Eggs, 3 pieces  
🍖 Small sausage pieces, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, about 2 grams  
🌿 Thirteen Spices, appropriate amount  
🍶 White vinegar, about two drops  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl  
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
💧 Oil spray bottle  
🥄 Wooden spoon/large spoon  
🥄 Silicone spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the chopped chives into the large stainless steel bowl, add the cornmeal, and mix thoroughly with your hands, ensuring each piece of chive is evenly coated with cornmeal.  
**Key point**: This step locks in the moisture of the chives and reduces nutrient loss.  

2. In the small stainless steel bowl, crack 3 eggs, add about two drops of white vinegar, and beat vigorously with chopsticks until the egg mixture is uniform.  
**Key point**: White vinegar removes the eggy smell and makes the final product fluffier and softer.  

3. Pour the beaten eggs into the chives coated with cornmeal, add salt, Thirteen Spices, and the small sausage pieces, and stir evenly to form a thick, sticky batter.  
**Key point**: The batter consistency should be thick enough to cling to chopsticks; if it is too dry, add extra eggs rather than water to keep the pancakes soft.  

4. Place the frying pan on the stove and heat it over high heat, then use the oil spray bottle to evenly spray a small amount of cooking oil onto the pan surface.  
5. Use a large spoon to scoop the chive batter into the pan, one spoonful per pancake, and shape into small round cakes.  
6. Cook over medium-low heat throughout. After about 1 minute, once the bottom is golden brown and set, flip using the silicone spatula.  
**Note**: Maintain medium-low heat; high heat will easily burn the pancakes.  

7. Continue cooking the other side until both sides are golden brown and the pancakes are fully cooked through, then remove from the pan and plate.  

## Health Tips  
The combination of chives and cornmeal is rich in dietary fiber, which aids intestinal movement; adding eggs and sausage pieces provides a more balanced protein profile, making it suitable for breakfast or a light meal. Note that sausage pieces are relatively high in salt; individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the amount or choose low-sodium versions.
