# Tomato Fish Fillet Slices  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 sea bass (about 450g, use fillet, bones reserved for other use)  
🍅 2 pink tomatoes (cut into chunks)  
🧅 Some scallion (sliced)  
🧄 Some garlic (minced)  
🫚 Some ginger (minced)  
🌿 Some spring onion (chopped)  
🧂 1 tsp salt (for marinating fish) + 1 tsp salt (for stir-frying tomatoes)  
🌶️ Some ground white pepper (for marinating fish and seasoning soup)  
🥔 2 tbsp potato starch (for coating fish fillets)  
🥄 Some steamed fish soy sauce  
🍬 1 tbsp sugar  
🧂 A pinch of MSG  
💧 1 bowl of boiling water  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Soup bowl  
🥄 Spoon, chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Fish preparation: Debone the sea bass and slice into evenly thick fillets. Add 1 tsp salt, ground white pepper, and 2 tbsp potato starch. Stir in one direction until the surface becomes sticky. Set aside to rest and let the coating set.  
2. Heat the wok with some oil. Sauté scallion, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add tomato chunks and 1 tsp salt. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 2 minutes until the tomatoes soften and release their pulp.  
   **Key point**: Adding salt helps the tomatoes soften and release juice faster.  
3. Pour in 1 bowl of boiling water. Add steamed fish soy sauce, sugar, MSG, and ground white pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling for 2 minutes to blend the flavors.  
4. Reduce to very low heat. Slide the marinated fish fillets one by one into the soup. Gently push them apart with the back of a spoon. Cook until the fillets turn white and curl, indicating they are done.  
   **Key point**: The fish must be added over very low heat to prevent the coating from falling off and the fillets from breaking. Remove from heat as soon as the fillets change color.  
5. Pour the soup and fish together into a soup bowl. Sprinkle with chopped spring onion and minced garlic. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
The combination of tomato and fish is a classic low-fat, high-protein pairing. Sea bass is rich in Omega-3, and the vitamin C in tomatoes helps enhance iron absorption, making it suitable for weight loss diets or mental work. Be sure to add the fish fillets over low heat and stir gently to prevent them from breaking apart.
