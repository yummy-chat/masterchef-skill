# Suzhou Boat Pastry
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 6 types of mixed flour
🐷 Lard, as needed
🍬 Sugar, as needed
🎨 Food coloring, as needed
🍶 Water, as needed

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🪣 Sieve
🥣 Mixing bowl
♨️ Steamer
🔪 Cutting board
✂️ Shaping tools
🖌️ Paintbrush
🏺 Assembly base stand

## Preparation Steps
1. Sift and mix the 6 types of flour together. Add lard, sugar, and water, then knead until the dough is smooth on the surface, the bowl is clean, and the dough does not stick to your hands.
2. Steam the dough in a steamer until fully cooked. Remove and repeatedly knead while hot until the dough becomes elastic and smooth.
**Key point**: Must knead thoroughly while hot to ensure malleability for shaping.

3. Divide the colored dough pieces and roll them into basic shapes as needed, such as small balls or long strips.
4. Using shaping tools, finely carve the basic shapes to create figures like fruits, vegetables, or even human characters. Operation must be meticulous, especially for delicate details like facial features of people. Aim to complete in one pass; if mistakes occur, remake from scratch.
**Note**: Operation requires careful attention, especially for facial details. There is "no turning back once begun," mistakes require remaking.

5. Assemble the shaped components, for example attaching leaves to a peach, to form raw pieces.
6. Steam all raw pieces again in the steamer.
7. After steaming, use a paintbrush for final color accents or detail touch-ups.
8. Place the finished boat pastries on a decorative base stand to form a complete artistic display.

## Health Advice
Suzhou boat pastry is known for its exquisite appearance and delicate texture, suitable for festive gatherings or occasions pursuing visual enjoyment. Note that the amounts of lard and sugar are relatively high, so it should not be consumed in excess. Its nutritional value mainly comes from the composite flours, leaning more towards a food art piece rather than a daily staple.
