# Thin-Skin Chive Boxes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g  
🍬 White sugar 2g  
🍄 Dry yeast 3g  
💧 Warm water 320g (add in batches)  
🍠 Sweet potato vermicelli, as needed  
🌿 Fresh chives, as needed  
🫒 Scallion-ginger oil, as needed (to seal in moisture)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌿 Chinese thirteen-spice powder, to taste  
🥄 Soy sauce, to taste (to season the vermicelli)  
🫒 Cooked oil, as needed (to prevent vermicelli from sticking)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading and resting dough)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for holding vermicelli)  
🍲 Cooking pot (for blanching vermicelli)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Flat-bottom pan/frying pan  
🥢 Spatula, chopsticks  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🥄 Spoon (for filling and mixing)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix the flour, white sugar, and yeast evenly. Add warm water in batches, stirring into a soft and moist dough. Knead repeatedly until the surface is smooth and no longer sticky. Lightly brush the surface with a layer of oil, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest to rise.  
2. Blanch the dry vermicelli in boiling water until soft and tender. Remove and drain. While hot, mix in soy sauce and cooked oil. Let cool, then chop finely.  
3. Wash the fresh chives and drain thoroughly. Finely chop them.  
4. First, drizzle the scallion-ginger oil over the chopped chives and mix well. Then add salt and Chinese thirteen-spice powder to season. Finally, add the chopped vermicelli and mix evenly.  
5. Take a piece of the rested soft dough. Wrap in a generous amount of filling and press it firmly. Using the dough’s elasticity, pleat the edges upward to seal, pinching off any excess dough. Place the sealed side down on an oiled cutting board, and gently flatten into a round patty.  
   **Note**: When wrapping, stretch the dough as thin as possible. Handle gently to avoid tearing.  
6. Pour a sufficient amount of oil into a flat-bottom pan and heat until it reaches about 50-60% hot. Add the patties and fry until the bottom is golden brown and set. Flip and continue frying until both sides are golden brown and fully cooked. Remove from the pan.  
   **Key point**: The dough is very thin and cooks quickly; control the time to avoid drying out and hardening.

## Health Advice  
Chive boxes are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, making them suitable as a staple paired with light dishes. However, since frying uses a fair amount of oil, be mindful of your intake. The vermicelli and chives are easy to digest, ideal for those with weak digestive systems. Because the dough is very thin and prone to tearing, fry over low heat and flip gently.
