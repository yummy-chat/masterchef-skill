# Coconut Chicken Hot Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 whole chicken (deboned and cut into large slices, bones reserved for broth)  
🥥 1 fresh coconut (coconut water reserved)  
🐖 Some cooked pork belly (sliced, leftover from the previous day can be used)  
🌽 1-2 pieces of corn  
🫒 Some red dates  
🥔 Some water chestnuts  
🧂 Some salt  
🍒 A handful of goji berries  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Gas stove  
🍲 Soup pot (with lid)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  

## Cooking Steps
1. Debone the whole chicken, slice the meat into large pieces, and set the bones aside.  
2. Pour fresh coconut water into the pot, add corn pieces, red dates, water chestnuts, chicken bones, and sliced cooked pork belly. Simmer together to concentrate the broth’s flavor.  
3. Once the broth has developed flavor, use a slotted spoon to remove solid ingredients such as chicken bones, pork belly, and coconut meat, leaving the broth clear.  
4. Keep the broth at a rapid boil, add all the chicken slices at once, and cook for several minutes until just done.  
5. Use a ladle to skim off any foam and excess oil produced during cooking. Add goji berries and salt to taste. First, enjoy the soup and meat.  
6. Move the pot to the table and eat while boiling other side dishes. The previously skimmed chicken oil can be returned to the pot according to preference to enhance the aroma.  

## Health Tips
Coconut chicken hot pot is an excellent low-fat, high-protein choice. Coconut water is rich in electrolytes, making it suitable for hydration after exercise or in summer. However, pork belly and chicken skin are high in fat; those watching their weight are advised to limit consumption of the floating oil.
