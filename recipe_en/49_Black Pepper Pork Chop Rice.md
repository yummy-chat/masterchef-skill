# Black Pepper Pork Chop Rice
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 2 pieces pork collar steaks (pat dry with paper towels)  
🧅 1/2 onion (sliced)  
🌶️ 1/2 red bell pepper (sliced)  
🍄 5–6 white mushrooms (sliced)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for marinating)  
⚫ Ground black pepper, to taste (for marinating)  
🫙 Black pepper sauce, to taste (for stir-frying vegetables and drizzling)  
🌽 Cornstarch slurry, as needed (cornstarch mixed with water, for thickening)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥢 Food tongs  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Spatula  
🫗 Measuring cup  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Use kitchen paper towels to pat the pork collar steaks dry. Season both sides evenly with salt and black pepper, then let marinate for a moment.  
   **Key point**: Patting dry is essential for achieving a crispy, browned surface.  
2. Heat the frying pan over medium-low heat without oil. Place the pork steaks directly into the pan; cover if desired. Sear each side for about 2–3 minutes until golden brown and cooked through. Remove and set aside.  
   **Doneness check**: The surface should be golden brown, and the meat should spring back when pressed. The pork’s own fat provides enough oil, so no additional oil is needed.  
3. Leave the rendered fat in the pan. Over medium heat, stir-fry the onion slices and red bell pepper slices until fragrant. Add the mushroom slices and cook until softened.  
4. Pour in the black pepper sauce and stir-fry evenly. Slice the rested pork steaks into strips, return them to the pan, and toss together with the vegetables.  
5. Pour in the prepared cornstarch slurry, stirring quickly over high heat until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients. Then remove from heat.  
   **Note**: The sauce can be kept slightly runny for easier mixing with rice.  
6. Serve the cooked pork strips and vegetables over steamed rice, and drizzle the remaining sauce on top.  

## Health Advice  
Black pepper pork chop rice is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for muscle building or quick energy replenishment. Note that the pork itself contains enough fat, so no extra oil is needed. Pair with vegetables to balance nutrition.
