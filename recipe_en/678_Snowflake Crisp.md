# Snowflake Crisp
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Butter 20g (cut into small pieces)
🍬 Marshmallows 100g (large ones can be snipped into smaller pieces, about 2 cups)
🥛 Milk powder 25g (1 cup, used for adding to the sugar base and dusting to prevent sticking)
🍪 Crackers 100g (special snowflake crisp crackers or any crispy crackers)
🍓 Freeze-dried strawberries 25g (can be substituted with other freeze-dried fruit or nuts)
🍫 Other flavored powders as needed (such as cocoa powder, strawberry powder, kale powder, etc., added together with milk powder)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (or rice cooker, microwave)
🥄 Silicone spatula
🧤 Disposable gloves
📦 Shaping mold (or the non-stick pan itself)
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board
🧻 Plastic wrap
🥛 Measuring cup (can be replaced with a mineral water bottle)
✂️ Scissors (for cutting large marshmallows into smaller pieces)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the non-stick pan over low heat, add the 20g butter pieces, and heat until completely melted into liquid.
2. Add the 100g marshmallows, continuously stirring with a silicone spatula. Heat over low heat until the marshmallows are completely melted and well blended with the butter, achieving a sticky, stretchy consistency.
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat low at all times to prevent scorching; turn off the heat immediately after the marshmallows have melted.
3. After turning off the heat, while still hot, add the 25g milk powder. Stir quickly with the spatula until the powder is fully incorporated into the sugar base.
4. Immediately add the 100g crackers and 25g freeze-dried strawberries (or other mix-ins). Put on disposable gloves and repeatedly fold, pull, and press the mixture by hand to evenly distribute the mix-ins within the sugar base.
   **Note**: Work quickly to prevent the sugar base from cooling and hardening.
5. Transfer the mixed snowflake crisp dough to a cutting board or mold lined with plastic wrap. While still hot, press firmly into a tight rectangular block.
   **Key Point**: The tighter you press, the neater the cut pieces will be and the firmer the texture.
6. After it has completely cooled and set, sprinkle a layer of milk powder or a flavored powder mixture on the surface and the cutting board to prevent sticking.
7. Using a kitchen knife, cut the snowflake crisp into evenly sized pieces. Dusting with powder before cutting helps prevent sticking.

### Alternative Methods
- **Rice Cooker Method**: Place the butter and marshmallows in the rice cooker inner pot. Press the "Cook Rice" button and heat for about 2 minutes, then immediately unplug. Use the residual heat to stir until melted. The subsequent steps are the same as the main method.
  **Note**: Strictly control the heating time to avoid scorching; the non-stick inner pot is convenient and results in less waste.
- **Microwave Method**: Place the butter and marshmallows in a microwave-safe bowl. Heat on high power for about 1 minute and 30 seconds until softened. Remove and quickly add the milk powder and mix-ins. Stir rapidly and shape.
  **Key Point**: Speed is essential; marshmallows will become very sticky as they cool and can easily stick to the bowl, causing waste.

## Health Advice
This snowflake crisp has a relatively high calorie density. It is recommended as an occasional treat, with a serving size of 2–3 pieces. The freeze-dried fruit provides a small amount of vitamins, but the overall sugar and butter content is not low. Children and those monitoring their sugar intake should be mindful of portion sizes.
