# Crispy Fried Chicken Drumettes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken drumettes, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)  
🍋 Lemon, 1 (juiced)  
🍞 Flour, as needed (3 parts for dry coating, 1 part for batter)  
🌾 Starch, as needed (1 part for dry coating, 1 part for batter)  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🥛 Milk, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed (used twice)  
🌶️ Pepper, as needed (used twice)  
🍂 Thirteen Spices, as needed  
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍴 Fork  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Deep pot (wok)  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Use a fork to evenly poke small holes all over the surface of each chicken drumette to allow for flavor absorption.
   **Key Point**: The holes should cover every part to ensure the marinade penetrates evenly.

2. Cut the scallions into sections, slice the ginger, and mince the garlic. Set aside.

3. Place the chicken drumettes in a glass bowl. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, minced garlic, salt, pepper, thirteen spices, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Wearing disposable gloves, mix and rub thoroughly. Squeeze in the lemon juice, mix again, and let marinate for 1 hour.
   **Key Point**: Rub to ensure the seasonings are evenly distributed. Marinate for no less than 1 hour to ensure thorough flavor absorption.

4. In another bowl, combine 3 parts flour with 1 part starch. Add an appropriate amount of salt and pepper, and mix well to create the dry coating.
   **Key Point**: The ratio of flour to starch is 3:1. Ensure the seasonings are mixed evenly.

5. Crack the egg into another bowl. Add 1 part flour and 1 part starch, then pour in an appropriate amount of milk. Stir until smooth, with no lumps, forming a thin, flowable batter.
   **Key Point**: The batter's consistency should allow for even coating. If it is too thick, the coating will be uneven.

6. Dip the marinated chicken drumettes into the batter to coat the surface evenly, then roll them in the dry coating to fully cover the exterior, creating a scaly appearance.
   **Key Point**: Both the batter and dry coating must be applied evenly to form crispy scales.

7. Pour sufficient cooking oil into the pot and heat to medium-low heat (about 40% hot). Add the chicken drumettes, gradually increase the oil temperature, and fry until the crust sets and the inside is cooked through, turning a light golden brown.
   **Key Point**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid a burnt exterior and raw interior. Keep the heat consistent to ensure the inside is fully cooked.

8. Use a slotted spoon to remove the chicken drumettes, drain excess oil, and let them rest for a moment.
   **Key Point**: Resting helps release internal steam, making the skin crispier after the second frying.

9. Raise the oil temperature to medium-high (about 60% hot). Add the chicken drumettes again for a second fry until the crust turns a deep golden brown and the skin is crispy.
   **Key Point**: The second fry forces out excess moisture, greatly enhancing the crispiness. Watch the color carefully to avoid overcooking.

10. Remove, drain the oil, and plate. Serve.

## Health Advice  
This crispy fried chicken drumette dish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but deep-frying is high in calories. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. It is suitable for occasional consumption. Individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their intake.
