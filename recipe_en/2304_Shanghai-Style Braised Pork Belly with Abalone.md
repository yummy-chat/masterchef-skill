# Shanghai-Style Braised Pork Belly with Abalone
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, as needed (cut into large pieces)
🐚 Abalone, several (scored with a crosshatch pattern)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece
🌿 Cinnamon stick, 1 piece
🍃 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
🍬 Rock sugar, a handful (for caramelizing)
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a little (for coloring)
🍶 Yellow wine, as needed (for deglazing and removing odor)
🍬 White sugar, as needed (for seasoning, as the caramelized sugar has no sweetness)
💧 Boiling water, sufficient (for making caramel and braising stock)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🫖 Clay pot
🥄 Spoon (for skimming foam and serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the pork belly and cut into evenly sized cubes; clean the abalone and score the surface with a crosshatch pattern; slice the ginger; prepare the star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves.
2. Caramelize the sugar: Place rock sugar and a small amount of water in the wok. Heat over low heat, stirring continuously. When the sugar turns from oil-like to amber, then to date-red, and small bubbles appear, immediately pour in boiling water. Remove from heat and set aside.
3. Heat the wok with cold oil, add the pork belly pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface is golden and fragrant. Render excess fat, then pour out the oil, leaving the meat pieces in the wok.
4. In the wok with the remaining oil, add the ginger slices, star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves. Stir-fry until fragrant. Splash in the yellow wine to deglaze and remove odor. Add white sugar, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry evenly to coat the meat pieces with color.
5. Pour in the prepared caramelized sugar. Add sufficient boiling water to submerge the meat. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then cover the wok. Reduce the heat to low and braise slowly for about 1.5 hours.
6. 10 minutes before the braising is finished, uncover and add the abalone. Remove the spices from the wok, then cover and continue braising for another 10 minutes.
7. After 10 minutes, uncover and increase the heat to reduce the sauce. Stir constantly until the sauce is thick and evenly coats the meat and abalone. Turn off the heat.

## Health Advice
This braised pork belly with abalone is rich in collagen and high-quality protein, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or in need of nourishment. However, due to its high calorie content, it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. Pre-rendering the excess fat from the pork belly before braising reduces greasiness while preserving the rich flavor; scoring the abalone with a crosshatch pattern helps it absorb flavors and improves texture.
