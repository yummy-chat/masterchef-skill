# Cooling Mung Bean Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Peeled Mung Beans 250g
🍵 Matcha Powder as needed
🫐 Dried Cranberries as needed (chopped)
🥛 Milk as needed
🧈 Butter 40g
🥛 Condensed Milk 50g
🍬 White Sugar 30g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍚 Rice Cooker
🥄 Rice Paddle
🔪 Knife
🥣 Bowl
📏 Measuring Cup
🧊 Plastic Wrap + Rolling Pin
🧁 Molds

## Steps
1. The night before, rinse 250g of peeled mung beans thoroughly and soak them in enough water until fully expanded, to the point where they can be easily broken with a finger.
2. Drain the soaked mung beans and place them into the rice cooker. Add water and press the "Cook" button to heat until fully cooked. When the rice cooker automatically switches to warm, the mung beans should be soft and tender.
3. While hot, use a rice paddle to mash the mung beans into a paste directly in the rice cooker. Add butter, condensed milk, and white sugar, and mix thoroughly until well combined.
4. Press the "Cook" button again and continuously stir the bean paste with a rice paddle. Stop when the moisture has evaporated, the paste clumps together, and it is no longer sticky to the touch or the pot wall. Stir constantly to prevent scorching on the bottom.
5. The finished plain bean paste can be shaped directly. For matcha or cranberry flavors, divide the paste and mix in matcha powder or chopped dried cranberries as needed. If the mixed paste is too dry, add a little milk to adjust the softness.
6. Shaping method:
   - With mold: Fill a mold with an appropriate amount of bean paste, press firmly, and unmold.
   - Without mold: Spread the bean paste onto plastic wrap, shape into an even-thick rectangle, press flat with a rolling pin, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate until set. Remove and cut into small pieces.
7. Serve chilled for a cooling and refreshing treat.

## Health Tips
Mung beans are rich in B vitamins and have heat-clearing and detoxifying effects. However, the addition of butter and condensed milk makes this dessert relatively high in calories, suitable as a small summer treat in moderation. It tastes best when refrigerated. Those with weak digestive systems or cold sensitivity should reduce consumption.
