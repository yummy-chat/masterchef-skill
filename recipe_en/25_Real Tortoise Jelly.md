# Real Tortoise Jelly  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐢 5 grass turtles (use only the shell and plastron)  
🌿 80g smilax glabra (sliced)  
🌱 Mixed herbs (including dandelion, Artemisia capillaris, honeysuckle, chrysanthemum, licorice, Angelica dahurica, rehmannia, lingzhi slices, hemp seeds, and others, in appropriate amounts)  
🌿 Fairy grass (optional, for a deeper black color)  
🌾 Rice flour / water chestnut flour / starch (as needed, for thickening)  
🫚 Ginger slices (as needed)  
🍶 Cooking wine (as needed)  
🍯 Honey / sweetened condensed milk (as needed, for serving)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Large pot (for boiling turtle soup and mixed liquid)  
🔥 Oven  
🧺 Wire rack  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🧽 Strainer or cheesecloth  
🥣 Porcelain bowls or保鲜盒 (for cooling and setting)  
🥄 Stirring spoon or spatula  

## Steps  
1. **Process the turtle shells**: Take only the shells and plastrons of the grass turtles and place them in cold water with ginger slices and cooking wine. Blanch for 20 minutes to remove any gamey smell. Remove and rinse repeatedly with cold water to thoroughly remove any residual fat. Lay the cleaned shells flat on a wire rack and place in the oven at 52°C (125°F) for 12 hours until completely dry.  
   **Key point**: All fat must be removed and the shells must be thoroughly dried to ensure a clean taste and facilitate the extraction of collagen later.  

2. **Prepare the herbs**: Rinse the smilax glabra and mixed herbs with clean water to remove dust and impurities.  
3. **Make the turtle soup**: Place the dried turtle shells and smilax glabra in a large pot with enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 8 hours, until the shells can be crushed easily with fingers and the gelatin is fully extracted. Strain and reserve the liquid.  
4. **Make the herbal soup**: In another pot, add the rinsed mixed herbs and water, then simmer for 2–3 hours. After cooking, strain through cheesecloth to remove the herbal residue, keeping only the liquid.  
5. **Combine and simmer**: Combine the turtle soup and herbal soup in one pot. Simmer over medium-low heat for another hour to allow the two liquids to fully integrate.  
6. **Thicken the mixture**: Take a small amount of the cooled combined liquid and mix it with rice flour to form a smooth, lump-free slurry. Keep the main pot of liquid at a gentle boil, then slowly pour in the slurry while stirring constantly. Continue to simmer over medium-low heat for about 20 minutes, until the liquid thickens noticeably and coats the back of a spoon.  
   **Key point**: Stir continuously during the thickening process to prevent lumps or scorching; the amount of flour can be adjusted flexibly based on the desired consistency.  

7. **Cool and set**: Pour the thick tortoise jelly liquid into clean porcelain bowls or storage containers while still hot. Let it cool to room temperature and naturally set into a jelly.  
8. **When serving**: Pair with honey, sweetened condensed milk, or sugar to balance its extremely bitter flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This tortoise jelly, made with slow simmering and various herbs, has notable heat-clearing and detoxifying effects, especially suitable for those who stay up late or have excess internal heat. However, those with a weak spleen and stomach should not consume it in large quantities. Pay attention to controlling the amount of honey or sweetened condensed milk to avoid excessive sugar intake.
