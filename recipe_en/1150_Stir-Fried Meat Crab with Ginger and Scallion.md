# Stir-Fried Meat Crab with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦀 Meat crab 2 pieces (male blue crabs, sourced from the brackish water boundary of the Pearl River estuary; choosing male crab yields tenderer meat)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced or smashed, generous amount needed)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections, separating white parts and green parts, generous amount needed)  
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for dusting crab pieces and final thickening)  
🐔🧈 Special chicken fat about 1 spoonful  
🍶 Cooking wine a little  
🦪 Oyster sauce a little  
🍬 Sugar a little  
🧂 Salt a little  
🌿 White pepper powder a little  
🦀💧 Crab juice (or broth) a little  
🥣 Cornstarch water appropriate amount (for thickening)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Chopping board  
🍳 Frying pan (or wok)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥄 Measuring spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Clean the crab thoroughly, remove the crab gills, cut off the crab mouth and claw tips, split the body in half, then chop into small pieces. Crack the crab claws with the back of a knife.  
**Note**: Thorough processing makes eating easier.  
2. Evenly sprinkle cornstarch over all crab pieces, gently toss to coat the cut surfaces with a thin layer of starch.  
**Key**: Cornstarch locks in the crab meat's moisture, enhancing tenderness and helping it absorb the sauce more easily.  
3. Slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, and separate the white and green parts.  
**Key**: Use generous amounts of ginger and scallion; cutting them diagonally releases fragrance more effectively.  
4. Heat the pan over medium-low heat, add the chicken fat, and stir-fry the ginger slices until the edges turn golden brown and the ginger oil aroma emerges.  
**Key**: The ginger oil must be extracted to create a base flavor for the crab meat.  
5. Turn the heat to high, add the crab pieces and stir-fry quickly until most of the shell turns red and the meat is about 40-50% cooked.  
**Key**: Maintain high heat throughout to quickly seal in moisture.  
6. Pour cooking wine along the edge of the pan and stir-fry rapidly to combine evenly.  
**Note**: Add wine only after the crab pieces have turned red; adding it too early will damage the crab meat fibers and cause loss of umami.  
7. Add white pepper powder, crab juice, oyster sauce, salt, and sugar in order, stir-fry to combine, cover with the lid, reduce to medium-low heat, and braise for 2 minutes.  
**Key**: Braising allows the crab's umami to merge into the sauce and penetrate the meat.  
8. Remove the lid, add the scallion sections, stir-fry quickly until the scallions are just cooked, then pour in the cornstarch water and stir-fry until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy.  
**Key**: Add fresh scallions at the end to maintain a crisp texture and allow them to soak up the flavorful sauce.  
9. Plate and serve. The ginger and scallion aroma is rich, and the crab meat is tender and flavorful.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat; crab meat is rich in zinc and selenium, making it suitable for autumn and winter nourishment. Pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Gout patients should consume in moderation and ensure removal of crab gills and heart.
