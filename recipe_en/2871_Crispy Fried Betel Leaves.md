# Crispy Fried Betel Leaves
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌿 Betel leaves, as needed (can substitute with perilla leaves)  
🥩 Pork, as needed (minced into filling)  
🥬 Water chestnuts, as needed (finely chopped and mixed into filling)  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, 20g  
🌾 Crispy frying powder, half a packet  
🌽 Cornstarch, a little (for mixing into filling and dusting leaves)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little  
🍶 Cooking wine, a little  
🥢 Soy sauce, a little  
🌿 Thirteen spices, a pinch  
🧂 Pepper-salt powder, a little (for sprinkling after frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Deep pot (milk pot or small frying pot)  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🥣 Several bowls or containers  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the betel leaves and drain the water. Set aside.
2. Prepare the filling: Finely mince the pork and water chestnuts. Combine them, then add salt, sugar, oyster sauce, cooking wine, soy sauce, thirteen spices, and a little cornstarch. Mix thoroughly with your hands until the mixture is well combined and slightly sticky.
3. Prepare the crispy batter: Mix half a packet of crispy frying powder with 20g of glutinous rice flour. Gradually add water while stirring until a thin, continuous-flowing batter forms.
4. Assemble: Lightly dust the surface of a betel leaf with a thin layer of cornstarch. Place an appropriate amount of filling on the leaf, then fold the leaf over or wrap it around the filling.
5. Coat with batter: Dip the filled leaf into the batter, ensuring both sides are evenly coated.
6. Deep-fry: Heat oil to 120°C. Gently place the batter-coated leaves into the oil and fry slowly over low heat.
   **Note**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw. Fry until the batter solidifies, bubbles form, and the leaves turn a light yellow, becoming firm and crispy. Then remove.
7. Season: While still hot, sprinkle a little pepper-salt powder over the fried leaves and serve.

## Health Tips
This fried betel leaf dish uses water chestnuts to add a refreshing texture, keeping the calorie count manageable. It is suitable as an occasional snack. Pairing it with a vegetable salad helps balance the greasiness. For the elderly and children, be sure to remove any tough stems before serving.
