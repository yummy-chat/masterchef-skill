# Fresh Meat Mooncakes
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 280g pork filling (fat-to-lean ratio about 2:8)  
🌾 130g low-gluten flour (80g for oil dough, 50g for water-oil dough)  
🌾 50g high-gluten flour  
🐷 70g lard (40g for oil dough, 30g for water-oil dough)  
🍬 6g fine granulated sugar (for water-oil dough)  
💧 50g warm water (about 40-50 degrees)  
🧂 3g salt  
🍬 5g white sugar (for seasoning)  
🌶️ 1g white pepper powder  
🦪 8g oyster sauce  
🧅 about 24g scallion-ginger water (scallions and ginger soaked in boiling water, cooled, and strained)  
🫒 6g sesame oil  
🌿 15g small scallion segments  

## Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Glass mixing bowl  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🥖 Rolling pin  
📏 Ruler  
🍳 Baking sheet  
⏲️ Timer  
🔥 Oven  
🔴 Red seal stamp and food coloring/dark soy sauce (for decoration)  

## Instructions
1.  Make the oil dough: Place 80g of low-gluten flour on the work surface. Make a well in the center and add 40g of lard. Use the heel of your hand to push and soften the lard, then gradually incorporate it into the flour. Use a scraper to gather any loose flour, and knead until you have a smooth, cohesive dough. Place it in a plastic bag and set aside at room temperature.  
**Note**: Use only the heel of your hand throughout; avoid using your fingers, which can cause stickiness.

2.  Make the water-oil dough: Pour 30g lard, 6g fine granulated sugar, and 50g warm water into a bowl. Stir to emulsify. Mix 50g high-gluten flour and 50g low-gluten flour together. Make a well in the center of the flour mixture, pour in the warm liquid mixture, and use a scraper to push the flour into the liquid. Knead until a rough dough forms.  
**Note**: Using warm water for the dough makes the water-oil dough softer and more flaky.  
    Place the dough on the work surface. Knead it repeatedly with the heel of your hand, then throw it down forcefully several times until the surface is smooth and you can stretch it into a thin, unbreakable membrane. Roll it into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, place the oil dough in the refrigerator to rest for 20 minutes. This helps both doughs reach a similar consistency.

3.  Make the fresh meat filling: To the 280g of pork filling, add in order: 3g salt, 5g white sugar, 1g white pepper powder, 8g oyster sauce, the strained scallion-ginger water, and 6g sesame oil. Stir in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and cohesive. Pick up the filling and throw it back into the bowl repeatedly to make it springy. Finally, mix in the 15g of small scallion segments. Refrigerate the filling until needed.  
**Note**: Chilling firms the filling slightly, making it easier to wrap later.

4.  Enclosing (large-scale method): Press the rested water-oil dough into a round disc that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Place the entire oil dough ball in the center. Use the "tiger mouth" (the web between your thumb and index finger) to gather the edges and seal it tightly. Sprinkle a little dry flour on the surface and bottom of the assembled dough. Use the palms of your hands to press it flat, then use a rolling pin to roll it into a large rectangular sheet, about 30 cm x 36 cm.  
**Key Point**: The dough sheet should be able to slide freely on the work surface; if it sticks, sprinkle a small amount of additional dry flour as needed.  
    Brush off any excess dry flour from the surface. Spray or brush a thin layer of water onto the surface. Starting from one end, roll it up tightly, stretching the dough slightly as you roll, to form a long log. Gently roll and stretch the log to about 40 cm long. Use a scraper to cut it into 10 equal pieces.  
**Note**: Immediately cover the pieces with plastic wrap to prevent them from drying out.

5.  Shaping and filling: Take one piece (with the cut ends facing up and down). Use your palm to press it flat. Then use the "tiger mouth" to gather the edges towards the center, forming a ball. Flatten the ball into a small disc. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a round wrapper, thinner at the edges and slightly thicker in the center. Place about 28g of the meat filling in the center of the wrapper. Cup one hand to form a bowl shape under the wrapper as you gently press the filling down with the other hand. Simultaneously, use the "tiger mouth" of your cupped hand in an "OK" shape to gradually tighten and rotate the wrapper, completely enclosing the filling. Pinch the seam tightly at the top. Pinch off any excess dough from the top.  
**Note**: Removing the excess dough prevents the bottom from being too thick.  
    Place the formed mooncake seam-side down on the prepared baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining pieces.

6.  Decoration and baking: Stamp the top of each mooncake with a red seal that says "Fresh Meat." Place an empty baking sheet on top of the sheet holding the mooncakes.  
**Key Point**: Covering with a second baking sheet prevents direct hot air flow, resulting in flatter tops, lighter coloring, and preventing the crust from falling off.  
    Bake on the middle rack of the preheated oven. After 10 minutes, remove the top baking sheet. Continue baking for about another 10 minutes, or until the mooncakes have puffed up, the flaky layers are distinct, and they are a light golden yellow color. Remove from the oven.

## Health Tips  
Fresh meat mooncakes have a rich, flaky crust and a savory filling, making them perfect for autumn. However, as lard and pork filling are high in calories, it is recommended to eat only 1-2 at a time and pair them with unsweetened tea to help cut the richness.
