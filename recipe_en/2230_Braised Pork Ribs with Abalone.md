# Braised Pork Ribs with Abalone
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐷 Pork ribs (front ribs) 800g (about 2 pieces, snowflake meat is more tender)  
🐚 Fresh small abalones 8 pieces (about 3.5 yuan each)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (for stir-frying to prevent sticking)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into sections, used for braising)  
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves (crushed, added before finishing)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections, added before finishing)  
🌿 Garlic sprouts or chopped scallions appropriate amount (for garnish before finishing)  
⭐ Star anise 1-2 pieces  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece  
🍑 Dried hawthorn 3-4 pieces (or use preserved plums, to tenderize meat and enhance aroma)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch 1 tablespoon (for cleaning the ribs)  
🍶 Yellow wine 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
💧 Boiling water sufficient to submerge the ribs  
🍬 Rock sugar a small amount  
🍾 Dark soy sauce a small amount  
🥫 Pork rib sauce 1 tablespoon (or substitute with hoisin sauce or oyster sauce)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for seasoning when reducing the sauce)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Large iron wok (stir-fry pan)  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥄 Spoon (for scooping abalone meat)  
Slotted spoon, kitchen paper towels  
🥣 Bowls, basin  

## Procedure
1.  Place the ribs in a basin, add one tablespoon of cornstarch and a little water. Use your hands to thoroughly grasp and wash the ribs, then rinse under running water until the water runs clear.
   **Note**: Fresh ribs do not need to be blanched; washing with cornstarch effectively removes impurities and keeps the meat tender.

2.  Drain the washed ribs using a slotted spoon, then pat them completely dry with kitchen paper towels. Ensure they are thoroughly dried.
   **Key**: Thoroughly drying is the foundation for subsequent searing, browning, and releasing fragrance.

3.  Use a spoon to scoop the abalone meat out of the shells. Scrub clean, remove the innards and the beak of the abalone. Score a crosshatch pattern on the surface.
   **Key**: Be sure to remove the beak; the scoring not only improves appearance but also helps absorb flavor.

4.  Heat the wok over high heat, pour in cooking oil to coat the bottom. First, add a few slices of ginger and fry until fragrant. Then, immediately add the dried ribs and sear over high heat until the bottom side is set. Do not move them at this stage.
   **Purpose**: Frying the ginger prevents sticking; searing and setting the meat seals in the flavor.

5.  Keep stir-frying over high heat to evaporate any residual moisture from the ribs until the surface is slightly browned and only clear oil remains at the bottom of the wok. Reduce heat to medium. Add the scallion sections, star anise, cinnamon stick, and dried chilies. Drizzle one tablespoon of yellow wine and one tablespoon of light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly until aromatic.

6.  Add enough boiling water to the wok to submerge the ribs. Add a small amount of rock sugar, the dried hawthorn, some dark soy sauce, and one tablespoon of pork rib sauce. Stir to combine.
   **Note**: Must use boiling water to prevent the meat from shrinking and becoming tough due to the cold liquid. If using a pressure cooker, the amount of water can be slightly reduced.

7.  Transfer all ingredients from the wok into the pressure cooker. Close the lid. Once pressure is reached, reduce the heat to low and cook for 13 minutes.

8.  After pressure cooking, pour the ribs and the liquid back into the wok. Discard the scallions, ginger, star anise, and other aromatics. Increase the heat to reduce the sauce. Taste and add an appropriate amount of salt according to the saltiness.

9.  When the sauce starts to thicken, add the scored abalones. Continue to cook for 3-5 minutes. Once the sauce is thick and glossy, add the crushed garlic cloves and red chili pepper sections. Gently toss a few times.
   **Key**: Abalones cook very quickly; adding them at the end keeps them tender. Adding garlic and chili just before finishing releases their fresh, aromatic spiciness.

10. Turn off the heat. Garnish with garlic sprouts or chopped scallions. Serve immediately on a plate.

## Health Advice
Pork ribs are rich in high-quality protein and calcium, while abalones provide zinc and selenium. They are suitable for people needing nutritional supplementation. Note that abalones must be added at the very end to remain tender. The pressure cooking time should not be too long to prevent the ribs from becoming too dry.
