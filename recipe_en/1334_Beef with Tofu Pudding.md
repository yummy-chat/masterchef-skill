# Beef with Tofu Pudding
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef (appropriate amount, sliced thinly across the grain; can substitute with pork)
🍮 Tofu pudding (silken tofu; appropriate amount; if unavailable, use 2 blocks of soft tofu, hand-torn into pieces)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (appropriate amount)
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns (appropriate amount)
🫘 Dried soybeans (appropriate amount)
🥬 Pickled mustard tuber (or turnip, appropriate amount, finely chopped)
🧅 Ginger (appropriate amount, minced)
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)
🥚 Egg (1, use only the egg white)
🌾 Cornstarch (appropriate amount)
🥜 Crushed peanuts (appropriate amount)
🌿 Cilantro (appropriate amount)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, chopped)
🫘 Doubanjiang (chili bean paste, appropriate amount)
🌶️ Chili powder (appropriate amount)
🧴 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount)
🍶 Cooking wine (appropriate amount)
🌿 White pepper powder (appropriate amount)
🧅🧅 Ginger-scallion water (appropriate amount)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)
🧂 MSG (appropriate amount)
🧂 Chicken bouillon (appropriate amount)
🍬 Sugar (a tiny pinch, to enhance freshness)
🫒 Vegetable oil (appropriate amount)
🧈 Lard (appropriate amount)
🌿 Ground Sichuan peppercorn (appropriate amount)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Chopping board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Slice fresh beef thinly across the grain to break the fibers, ensuring a tender texture.
2. Add white pepper powder, light soy sauce, cooking wine, and ginger-scallion water to the beef slices. Knead with your hands until sticky and firm.
   **Note**: For home cooking, avoid adding baking soda; just knead until the meat feels tacky.
3. Add one egg white and mix well, then add cornstarch and mix again, ensuring each slice is evenly coated.
   **Note**: Add egg white first, then starch, to form a protective layer that locks in moisture.
4. Drizzle a small amount of vegetable oil and gently mix to prevent sticking during cooking and to seal in moisture.
5. If using soft tofu as a substitute for tofu pudding, hand-tear it into irregular chunks for better flavor absorption.
6. In a wok with a little oil, stir-fry dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns over low heat until crispy and color changes. Remove and crush them with a knife to make "knife-cut chili."
7. In a dry wok, toast the dried soybeans until cooked, fragrant, and crispy. Set aside.
8. Finely chop the pickled mustard tuber, ginger, and garlic. Prepare the cornstarch slurry.
9. Heat a mix of oils in the wok over low heat. Add doubanjiang and stir-fry slowly until aromatic and the oil turns red.
   **Note**: Stir-fry over low heat to avoid burning; judge by the deepened color and bright red oil.
10. Add minced ginger and garlic, continue stir-frying over low heat until fragrant.
11. Add chili powder and quickly stir-fry over low heat until fragrant and colored, being careful not to burn.
12. Add enough water and bring to a boil over high heat. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, and a tiny pinch of sugar.
13. Gently place the tofu pudding into the soup, add a little light soy sauce for flavor, and simmer over low heat for a few minutes to allow absorption.
    **Note**: Use the back of a ladle to push gently, avoiding breaking the tofu.
14. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer, then add the beef slices one by one, spreading them out.
    **Note**: Do not stir immediately after adding; wait until they set and change color, then push gently. Avoid a vigorous boil, or the meat will toughen.
15. Once the beef is cooked through, add the cornstarch slurry in batches, gently pushing with a ladle to evenly thicken the broth and coat the ingredients.
    **Note**: Use a "pushing" motion to avoid breaking the tofu; thicken until the broth coats the ladle.
16. Pour the cooked beef and tofu pudding into a large bowl.
17. Sprinkle ground Sichuan peppercorn and knife-cut chili on top. Heat oil until smoking and pour over to release aroma.
    **Note**: The oil must be hot enough to produce a sizzling sound.
18. Garnish with chopped scallions, cilantro, pickled mustard tuber, crispy soybeans, and crushed peanuts for added texture.

## Health Tips
This dish is spicy and tender. Beef provides high-quality protein, and tofu pudding offers calcium, making it suitable for muscle building or calcium supplementation. Do not overcook the beef to prevent toughness. Adjust the amount of chili and Sichuan peppercorn according to your tolerance; those with sensitive stomachs should consume with caution.
