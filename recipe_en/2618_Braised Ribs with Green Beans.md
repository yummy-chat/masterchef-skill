# Braised Ribs with Green Beans
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 3 Pork Spareribs (cut into large pieces)  
🫘 1 small bunch of Green Beans (trim both ends and remove strings, keep whole)  
🥔 1 Potato (peeled and cut into rolling chunks)  
🧄 Several Garlic Cloves (smashed)  
🫚 1 small piece of Ginger (about 20g, sliced)  
🧅 1 small Scallion (about 30g, cut into sections)  
🌸 A few Sichuan Peppercorns  
⭐ 2 Star Anise  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed  
🍶 Scallion oil, as needed  
🥄 20g Oyster Sauce  
🟤 About 3g Dark Soy Sauce  
🍾 Light Soy Sauce, as needed (poured along the edge of the wok)  
🧂 1 teaspoon Salt  
🍬 A pinch of Sugar  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron Wok / Frying Pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🪓 Cutting Board  
🥢 Slotted Spoon  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
🔥 Gas Stove  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the spareribs into large pieces, place them in clean water, and scrub until blood seeps out. Remove and drain well.
   **Key Point**: Fresh ribs do not need blanching for richer flavor; cutting into large pieces holds up better during braising.
2. Trim the ends of the green beans and remove the tough strings, keep them whole; peel the potato and cut into rolling chunks; smash the garlic cloves, slice the ginger, and cut the scallion into sections.
3. Heat the wok and add a base layer of oil. When the oil reaches about 40% heat (warm but not smoking), add the star anise and Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant. Then add the scallion sections and ginger slices, stir-frying until aromatic.
4. Add the ribs, reduce the heat to low, and dry-fry for about 3 minutes, until the surface of the ribs turns slightly yellow and moisture evaporates.
5. Add the green beans and potato chunks, increase the heat to medium-low, and stir-fry for 3 minutes, until the beans are just cooked through and turn bright green, removing any raw taste.
6. Add 20g oyster sauce and about 3g dark soy sauce, then pour light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry evenly to fully coat and color the ingredients.
7. Add enough water to cover all ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 8 minutes, allowing the collagen from the ribs to release.
8. Reduce the heat to low and continue to braise for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent the potato chunks from sticking to the bottom. Monitor the thickness of the sauce.
9. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and a pinch of sugar to enhance flavors. Continue to braise over low heat for 2 minutes, until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients.
10. Add the smashed garlic and an appropriate amount of scallion oil. Quickly stir-fry a few times, then serve on a plate.
    **Key Point**: Adding garlic at the end boosts aroma; scallion oil gives the dish a glossy sheen and richer flavor.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, the ribs are relatively high in fat, so it is recommended to pair this dish with light vegetables for a balanced meal. Ensure green beans are thoroughly cooked to avoid the risk of food poisoning.
