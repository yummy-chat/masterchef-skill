# Homestyle Chicken Popcorn

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast 2 pieces (washed clean, cut into small cubes)  
🍞 Toast bread 4 slices (for making breadcrumbs)  
🥚 Eggs 3 (about 120g, beaten directly into the marinade)  
🧂 Cooking wine 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon  
🍬 Sugar 1 tablespoon  
🌿 Cumin powder 2 tablespoons  
🌶 Black pepper powder 1/2 tablespoon  
🧄 Minced garlic a handful (optional)  
🌾 Cornstarch 2 tablespoons  
🌶 Chili powder to taste (optional, add if you like spice)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for deep frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Electric griddle or frying pan  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥘 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
📦 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. Wash the chicken breast, cut into small cubes, and place in a bowl.
2. Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, cumin powder, black pepper powder, minced garlic, and cornstarch. Crack in the eggs and stir thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined. Seal with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 48 hours. The longer the marination, the more tender and flavorful the meat.  
   **Note**: Sealing and refrigerating is key to ensuring even flavor absorption.
3. Make breadcrumbs: Place the toast slices in a frying pan or electric griddle. Toast over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes until completely dry inside and out. Remove and crush with a rolling pin.

### Cooking Phase
1. Take out the marinated chicken and stir again. Since the eggs were already added during marination, no additional egg wash is needed. Place the chicken pieces directly into the breadcrumbs and toss to coat evenly, pressing lightly to ensure the crumbs adhere well.  
   **Key**: Gently press the breadcrumbs onto the chicken to ensure they stick firmly.
2. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to 350°F (175°C). Add the chicken pieces. Fry over medium-low heat, gently stirring with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. Fry until both sides are golden brown, then remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Key**: Maintain medium-low heat to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw; use paper towels to absorb excess oil after draining.

## Health Tips
Chicken is tender and suitable for fitness enthusiasts looking for a treat, but deep frying significantly increases calorie content. It’s recommended to pair with a vegetable salad for a balanced diet. Marinating for 48 hours enhances the flavor, making it convenient to prepare in advance.
