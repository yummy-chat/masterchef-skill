# Lamb Brisket Claypot (Zhe Zhe Lamb Pot)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Skin-on lamb brisket 600g (keep the skin and fat layer intact)
🦴 Lamb bones approx. 300g (removed from the brisket, for making stock)
🧅 Garlic chives (Chinese leek) to taste
🧄 Pearl onions (dried shallots) to taste
🧄 Garlic cloves to taste
🫚 Ginger to taste (for aromatics)
🫚 Old ginger 50g (for stock)
🌿 Angelica sinensis (dong quai) 50g
🌰 Longan flesh (dried) 50g
🎋 Sugarcane 250g
🥕 Water chestnuts 150g
🥕 White radish approx. 250g
🥕 Carrot approx. 250g
🍊 Dried tangerine peel (chenpi) one piece (approx. 10g, 10-year-aged recommended)
🧂 Fermented red bean curd (nam yu) 10g
🧂 Chu hou paste 100g
🥜 Peanut butter to taste
🧂 Hoisin sauce 15g
🧂 Five-spice powder 15g
🦪 Oyster sauce 100g
🍬 Granulated sugar to taste
🛢️ Peanut oil to taste (for mixing paste and stir-frying)
🍶 Rice wine to taste (for stir-frying)
🍋 Lemon leaf, shredded to taste (sprinkle before serving)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Large stockpot
🫕 Clay pot (zhe zhe pot)
🔥 Portable gas stove
🔪 Cutting board & chef's knife
🥣 Bowls & spoons

## Preparation Steps
1.  Burn off any remaining hair on the skin-on lamb brisket, wash clean. Remove the bones from the inside, keeping the skin and fat layer intact.
**Note**: The skin and fat layer are essential for texture and flavor; do not remove them.

2.  Prepare the raw marinade paste: In a bowl, combine fermented red bean curd, chu hou paste, peanut butter, hoisin sauce, five-spice powder, oyster sauce, and sugar. Mix well. Finally, add peanut oil to adjust to a moderately thick consistency.
**Key point**: This paste coats the cooked lamb and is crucial for the "raw clay pot" (生啫) method.

3.  Prepare stock ingredients: Wash and cut the old ginger, angelica sinensis, longan flesh, sugarcane, water chestnuts, white radish, carrot, and dried tangerine peel into chunks. Set aside.
4.  Cook lamb bones and brisket: Fill a large stockpot with enough water. Add the lamb bones, lamb brisket, and all the prepared stock ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for about 30 minutes. The lamb brisket is ready when a chopstick can easily pierce the flesh but the skin still has some springiness. Remove the lamb brisket. Continue simmering the bone stock until it turns milky white and tastes sweet and clear.
**Note**: Cooking time depends on the brisket's state. It should be tender enough to pierce with a chopstick yet still elastic.

5.  Cut the lamb brisket: Let the cooked lamb brisket cool, then cut into bite-sized chunks.
6.  Coat with paste: Place the cut lamb chunks into the prepared raw marinade paste. Mix thoroughly so each piece is evenly coated.
7.  Clay pot cooking (zhe zhe): Heat a clay pot over high heat. Add oil, then stir-fry ginger, garlic, and pearl onions until fragrant. Once the aromatics release their scent, add the paste-coated lamb chunks. Splash rice wine along the edge of the pot and stir-fry quickly. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat for about 1 minute until the lamb surface is slightly charred and aromatic. Add garlic chives, stir a few times. Turn off the heat and immediately sprinkle with shredded lemon leaves.
**Key points**: Use high heat and fast stir-frying throughout to create "wok hei" via the clay pot's high temperature. The lamb is pre-cooked; this step is only for adding charred aroma. Shredded lemon leaf is the final flavor accent, do not overcook.

## Health Advice
This lamb brisket clay pot is rich in protein and iron, suitable for winter nourishment. However, it is high in calories and sodium. It is recommended to serve with vegetables and control portion sizes. People with poor digestion or high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
