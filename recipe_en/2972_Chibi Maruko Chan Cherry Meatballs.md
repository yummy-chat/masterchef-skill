# Chibi Maruko Chan Cherry Meatballs
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Ground pork 250g (half a catty, small pork)
🍒 Wenchuan cherries 1 bowl (pitted)
🫚 Minced ginger 1 small handful
🧂 Dark soy sauce a little (for color)
🧂 Cooking wine 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon
🧂 Sichuan pepper powder to taste (add as desired)
🥡 Cornstarch about 2 tablespoons

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍳 Oil wok
🪵 Wooden spatula
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧤 Disposable gloves
🧊 Straw (for pitting tool)
🪥 Toothpicks (for poking holes)

## Steps
1. Chop the pork into fine mince.
2. Add a little dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon cooking wine, 1 tablespoon salt, Sichuan pepper powder to taste, minced ginger, and about 2 tablespoons cornstarch to the minced pork. Wearing gloves, stir clockwise until the filling becomes sticky and firm.
3. Use a straw to remove the pits from the cherries and set aside.
4. Moisten your hands with a little water to prevent sticking. Take an appropriate amount of meat filling, flatten it into a patty, place one pitted cherry in the center, wrap and seal the filling, and roll it into a ball. Use a toothpick to poke several small holes on the surface of each ball so that the cherry juice can seep into the meat during frying.
5. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat until dense small bubbles form around the edge of a wooden spatula when inserted. Add the meatballs and fry until the surface turns light golden, then remove.
   **Key point**: Add the meatballs as soon as the oil reaches the bubble stage when the spatula is inserted.
6. When the oil temperature rises until it just starts to smoke, return the fried meatballs to the wok for a second fry for about 30 seconds, until the color deepens and the shell becomes crispy. Quickly remove.
   **Note**: Second frying requires high heat to enhance the crispy texture.

## Health Advice
Chibi Maruko Chan Cherry Meatballs skillfully blend the sweetness of fruit with the savoriness of meat, making them an appetizing choice for summer. It is recommended to control the oil temperature and second frying time to reduce oil intake, and to pair with vegetables for balanced nutrition.
