# Pumpkin Steamed Pork Ribs  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🎃 Pumpkin 250g (peeled and cut into chunks, preferably old pumpkin)  
🥩 Pork ribs 2 pieces (about 600g, rinsed and drained)  
🧄 Minced garlic, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🥃 Cooking wine, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌾 Cornstarch, 1 tablespoon  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Marinating bowl  
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack and lid)  
⏲️ Timer (phone)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Marinate the ribs**: Drain the ribs, then add minced garlic, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, cooking wine, white sugar, salt, and cornstarch. Mix well with your hands, then let sit for about 15 minutes to absorb the flavors.  
   **Key point**: The cornstarch helps lock in moisture, making the steamed ribs more tender and smooth; be sure to mix evenly so every rib is coated with the marinade.  

2. **Arrange the ingredients**: Take a deep plate, spread the pumpkin chunks evenly on the bottom, then place the marinated ribs evenly on top of the pumpkin.  
   **Principle**: Using the pumpkin as a base allows it to absorb the meat juices released by the ribs while steaming, enhancing the overall flavor.  

3. **Steam**: Place the plate on the steaming rack, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for about 30 minutes.  
   **Heat and timing**: Maintain high heat throughout; steam for about half an hour until the ribs are fully cooked and the pumpkin is soft.  
   **Note**: Keep an eye on the water level in the steamer; if it gets low, add water along the edge to prevent dry boiling.  

4. **Serve and enjoy**: Once steaming is complete, take the dish out directly. The ribs will be golden in color and tender in texture, while the pumpkin will be soft and well-infused with flavor.  

## Health Tips  
Pumpkin is rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene. Steaming it with the high-quality protein from pork ribs helps preserve nutrients while reducing cooking oil and smoke. This dish is suitable for those needing energy without greasiness. When marinating the ribs, use salt sparingly to control sodium intake.
