# Simple Vietnamese Spring Rolls
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast 1 piece (palm-sized, cooked and shredded)
🍤 Shrimp about 6 pieces (cooked and set aside)
🥚 Eggs 2 (made into egg crepe)
🌯 Vietnamese rice paper wrappers several sheets
🥒 Cucumber 1 (cut into strips)
🥕 Carrot strips as needed
🥬 Curly endive as needed
🌿 Mint leaves as needed
🧅 Scallion as needed
🫚 Ginger as needed
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns as needed
🍶 Cooking wine as needed
🥜 Peanut powder about 2 tablespoons
🧂 Light soy sauce as needed
🍯 Honey as needed
🍋 Lime 1 (juiced)
🌶️ Chili oil 2 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Soup pot
🍳 Frying pan
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🍽️ Large round plates 2
🥛 Glass measuring cup
🥣 Bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Cooking Steps
1. Place chicken breast in cold water, add scallion segments, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and cooking wine. Cook until done, then remove and let cool. Shred into fine strips along the grain. Cook shrimp until just done, then remove and set aside.
2. Beat the eggs. Heat a frying pan, grease it lightly, pour in the egg mixture, and spread into a thin egg crepe. Remove and cut into strips. Wash the cucumber and cut into strips. Optionally, lightly stir-fry the carrot strips until just done.
3. Fill a large round plate with water. Quickly dip both sides of a Vietnamese rice paper wrapper in the water, then lay it flat on another clean plate.
   **Key point**: The dipping motion must be quick to prevent the rice paper from becoming too soft and breaking. Using two plates improves ease of operation.
4. Take one dampened rice paper wrapper. In the center, layer curly endive, carrot strips, cucumber strips, egg crepe strips, and chicken strips. Finally, place a few mint leaves on top.
5. Fold the left and right sides of the rice paper wrapper toward the center. Then roll it up tightly from the side closest to you, finishing with the seam facing down.
6. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, add the peanut powder and a small amount of water, stirring until smooth and paste-like. Add light soy sauce, freshly squeezed lime juice, and honey, stirring to combine. Finally, add two tablespoons of chili oil to enhance the flavor.
7. Serve the spring rolls together with the dipping sauce. Enjoy immediately for a refreshing and appetizing taste.

## Health Tips
These Vietnamese spring rolls are high in protein and low in fat, paired with plenty of vegetables, making them suitable for weight loss periods. However, the peanut powder and honey in the dipping sauce are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to use them sparingly. They are great for cooling down in summer, but those with digestive issues should be cautious with mint.
