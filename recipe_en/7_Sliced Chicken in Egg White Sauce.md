# Sliced Chicken in Egg White Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast (appropriate amount, remove sinew, pound into paste with the back of a knife)
🥣 Cold chicken broth (appropriate amount, for thinning the chicken paste)
🥚 Egg whites (appropriate amount, added to the chicken paste)
🧄 Ginger and scallion (appropriate amount, mashed to extract ginger-scallion water)
🌶️ White pepper powder (a pinch)
🍶 Cooking wine (a pinch)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)
🌽 Cornstarch (appropriate amount, both dry and wet starch needed)
🫕 Clear chicken broth (or clear broth) (appropriate amount, for making the clear starch-thickened sauce)
🥫 Ham, minced (a little, for garnish)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥄 Flat spatula (or similar tool, for spreading the chicken paste)
🍳 Wok
🥢 Slotted spoon
🫗 Wok spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the sinew from the chicken breast and repeatedly pound it with the back of a knife into a fine paste.
**Key point**: You can place a piece of pork skin underneath or on the cutting board to prevent sticking while pounding.
2. Gradually add cold chicken broth to the chicken paste, stirring in one direction to blend thoroughly and avoid lumps.
3. Add egg whites, an appropriate amount of wet starch, salt, cooking wine, ginger-scallion water, and white pepper powder. Continue stirring until the mixture is firm and smooth, forming a porridge-like chicken batter.
**Note**: To make ginger-scallion water, mash ginger and scallion, add a small amount of water, and let steep to extract the flavor before use.
4. Heat the wok and coat it with oil. Pour in enough low-temperature oil.
5. Using a spoon, gently pour a ladleful of chicken batter over the center of the oil surface, letting it naturally spread out into a sheet and float.
**Key point**: Do not stir. Once the chicken sheet sets and floats, immediately remove it.
6. Gently rinse the cooked chicken sheets in boiling water to remove excess oil, then drain.
**Note**: This step keeps the finished dish light and clean.
7. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add an appropriate amount of clear chicken broth. Season with salt, cooking wine, and white pepper powder. Thicken with wet starch to create a thin sauce, and drizzle with a little clarified oil.
8. Gently push the chicken sheets into the wok and toss with the sauce to coat evenly.
**Key point**: Handle gently to keep the chicken sheets intact.
9. Plate and sprinkle with minced ham for garnish.
**Finished dish standard**: Color pure white, texture tender and smooth like tofu, light and fresh flavor.

## Health Advice
This Sliced Chicken in Egg White Sauce achieves a tofu-like tender texture using chicken paste and egg whites. It is a high-protein, low-fat quality choice, especially suitable for fitness or weight loss periods, or for those needing a light diet. Pay attention to controlling the oil temperature at 80-90℃ to prevent the chicken sheets from becoming tough.
