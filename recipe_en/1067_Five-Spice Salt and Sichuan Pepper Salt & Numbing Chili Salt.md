# Five-Spice Salt and Sichuan Pepper Salt & Numbing Chili Salt
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 50g  
🧂 Salt 100g  
🧂 MSG 10g  
⭐ Star anise 1 piece  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1g  
🌱 Fennel seeds 2g  
🌸 Cloves 2 pieces  
🤍 White peppercorns 2g  
🤍 White sesame seeds 20g  
🦐 Dried shrimp skin appropriate amount (to enhance umami)  
🌶️ Chili powder appropriate amount (for making numbing chili salt)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Dry pan (no oil)  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
⚙️ Grinder  
🥘 Tray or large bowl (for cooling)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Take a clean, oil-free dry pan, place over low heat, and dry-roast the Sichuan peppercorns.
   **Key point**: Test the pan temperature by hand. Roast until the peppercorns are hot to the touch, moisture is removed, and a fragrant aroma is released. Stop immediately; overheating will cause the numbing aroma to dissipate.
2. Add dried shrimp skin, white sesame seeds, star anise, cinnamon stick, fennel seeds, cloves, and white peppercorns. Continue stir-frying over low heat.
   **Key point**: Fry until all ingredients are dry and crispy, the sesame seeds turn slightly yellow, all moisture has evaporated, and a complex aroma is released.
3. Add the salt and stir-fry together until the salt grains turn slightly yellow. Then remove from heat immediately.
4. Pour out the roasted mixture and spread it flat on a tray. Use the residual heat to let the moisture completely dissipate, and allow it to cool thoroughly until completely cold.
5. Pour the completely cooled mixture into a grinder, add MSG, and grind into a powder.
   **Key point**: Do not grind too finely; retain some graininess for a better texture.
6. Divide the ground powder into two bowls: one bowl is directly used as five-spice salt; add an appropriate amount of chili powder to the other bowl and mix well to make numbing chili salt.

## Health Advice  
This homemade seasoned salt is oil-free and sugar-free, rich in antioxidants from Sichuan peppercorns and spices, and suitable for sprinkling on fried chicken or grilled meat to enhance flavor. Due to its high salt content, people with high blood pressure should use it in moderation.
