# Steamed Crab with Garlic Vermicelli  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦀 Two green crabs (or substitute with swimming crabs, bread crabs, etc.)  
🧄 200 grams garlic (minced into garlic paste)  
🍜 50 grams vermicelli (soaked in room-temperature water for 20 minutes until soft)  
🥚 One egg  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped into scallion flowers)  
🌶️ A small amount of red chili pepper (diced for color)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🫒 Peanut oil, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🖌️ Brush  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Use the tip of a knife to pry open the crab shell, keeping it intact. Cut off the crab claws, scrape away the gills and apron, remove the stomach and tips of the legs.  
**Key point**: The crab shell must remain intact for use as a container for the steamed egg; the stomach and gills are inedible.

2. Use a brush to thoroughly scrub the crab shell, body, and claws in water.  
**Key point**: Try not to rinse away the crab roe while cleaning.

3. Cut the crab body in half, then cut into quarters along the legs. Use the back of the knife to crack the crab claws, then cut them in half.  
**Key point**: Cracking the claws helps release flavor.

4. Use a spoon to scoop out the crab roe from inside the shell and set aside. Mince the garlic into small pieces, rinse once with clean water, then squeeze out excess moisture.  
**Key point**: Rinsing the minced garlic removes the mucus that causes bitterness when frying; squeeze dry after rinsing.

5. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok, add half the minced garlic, and fry over medium heat until golden brown, then remove. Mix with the remaining raw minced garlic, add salt, sugar, and peanut oil, and stir well.  
**Key point**: Control the heat when frying the garlic to avoid burning; remove the fried garlic immediately and mix with the raw garlic.

6. Finely chop some scallions and red chili pepper. Beat the egg, add an equal amount of water, salt, and the crab roe, and stir well. Skim off any foam from the surface.  
**Key point**: The ratio of egg liquid to water is 1:1; remove surface foam for a smooth steamed egg texture.

7. Cut the soaked vermicelli into sections, add two spoonfuls of garlic sauce, mix well, and spread evenly on the bottom of a plate. Arrange the crab pieces, claws, and shell on top of the vermicelli.  
**Key point**: The vermicelli serves as the base and must be seasoned first.

8. Spread a layer of garlic sauce over the crab meat. Pour the prepared egg liquid into the crab shell.  
**Key point**: Fill the crab shell completely with the egg liquid.

9. Place the entire plate into a preheated steamer. Steam over high heat for 4 minutes, then reduce to low heat and steam for another 2 minutes, for a total of about 6 minutes.  
**Key point**: Adjust steaming time based on heat intensity to ensure the crab meat and egg liquid are fully cooked.

10. After steaming, remove the plate and sprinkle with scallions. Pour hot peanut oil over the scallions.  
**Key point**: Pouring hot oil releases the aroma of the scallions, enhancing the dish's fragrance and appearance.

## Health Tips  
This dish of steamed crab with garlic vermicelli is high in protein and low in fat, with a garlicky aroma that stimulates the appetite—ideal for autumn nourishment. Note that crab roe is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Adding crab roe to the egg liquid doubles the umami flavor.
