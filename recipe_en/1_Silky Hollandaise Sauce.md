# Silky Hollandaise Sauce  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Egg yolk (1 piece)  
🧈 Butter (60 grams)  
🍋 Lemon juice (1 teaspoon)  
💧 Purified water (half an eggshell's worth)  
🧂 Salt (a pinch)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Stainless steel pot or small saucepan  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl or heat-resistant glass bowl  
🥄 Whisk  
🥄 Spoon  
🔥 Stove  

## Steps  
1. Place the butter in a stainless steel bowl and melt it completely using a double boiler method. Once melted, the butter will naturally separate. Only use the clarified butter from the middle layer: skim off the foam on top and discard the white milk solids at the bottom. Set the clarified butter aside to keep warm.  
   **Key point**: Use only clarified butter to avoid water and impurities affecting emulsification.  

2. In another appropriately sized stainless steel or heat-resistant glass bowl, add the egg yolk, lemon juice, purified water, and salt. Whisk together until well combined.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the egg yolk is from pasteurized, safe-to-eat raw eggs for food safety.  

3. Place the bowl with the yolk mixture over simmering hot water. Use the whisk to stir rapidly in a figure-eight or zigzag pattern. Keep the water at a gentle simmer without high heat.  
   **Key point**: Stir continuously without stopping to prevent the yolk from heating unevenly and clumping.  

4. While stirring, check the bottom of the bowl with the back of your hand. As soon as the bowl feels hot to the touch, immediately remove it from the heat and continue whisking off the heat. If the temperature drops too low, return the bowl to the heat source. Repeat this process until the yolk mixture thickens and its color noticeably lightens.  
   **Key point**: Temperature control is crucial; overheating causes the yolk to clump, while underheating prevents proper emulsification.  

5. When the yolk mixture reaches the desired thickness, slowly pour the clarified butter into the yolk mixture in a very thin, steady stream, while continuously whisking in rapid circular motions. Pour the butter slowly to avoid adding too much at once, ensuring thorough emulsification.  
   **Key point**: The slower and thinner the butter is added, the more stable the emulsification and the silkier the sauce.  

6. Continue stirring until all clarified butter is incorporated and the sauce becomes smooth, thick, and evenly glossy.  

## Health Advice  
Hollandaise sauce is rich in high-quality fats and fat-soluble vitamins. It pairs well with vegetables or poached eggs, but it is high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Individuals with gout or high cholesterol should be mindful of their intake of egg yolks and butter.
