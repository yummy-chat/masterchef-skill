# Little Pig Gets a Blanket
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork ribs, as needed (baby back ribs, soak in water for over 1 hour in advance)
🌾 Self-rising flour, as needed
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (cut into rolling knife chunks)
🥬 Large oil beans, as needed (green beans, broken into pieces)
🌽 Corn, as needed (fresh corn, cut into segments)
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed
🧅 Scallions, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🧄 Garlic, as needed
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed
🍂 Star anise, as needed
🌿 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🍶 Yellow wine, as needed
🥃 Vinegar, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🥄 Oyster sauce, as needed
🥫 Fermented soybean paste, as needed
🍃 Thirteen-spice powder, as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍳 Wok
🥖 Rolling pin
🪵 Cutting board
🔥 Gas stove
🍲 Lid

## Instructions
1. Prepare the dough: In a stainless steel bowl, pour in the self-rising flour. Gradually add warm water while stirring until it becomes flaky. Knead into a dough, cover with a lid, and let it rise.
2. Prepare the ribs: Soak the ribs in water for over 1 hour in advance until they turn pale. Remove and pat dry the surface with a paper towel.
3. Caramelize the sugar: Heat the wok, add oil and rock sugar. Cook over low heat until the sugar melts, turns a date-red color, and forms dense small bubbles.
   **Key point**: Use low heat to avoid burning the sugar, which would make it bitter.
4. Stir-fry the ribs: Pour the ribs into the caramelized sugar and quickly stir-fry to coat. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, star anise, dried chilies, cinnamon stick, and Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry until fragrant. Splash in yellow wine, vinegar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Add the fermented soybean paste and stir-fry until well combined.
5. Braise: Pour hot water into the wok until the ribs are submerged. Add thirteen-spice powder and chicken bouillon powder to season. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then reduce to low heat, cover, and braise for about 40 minutes.
6. Prepare the "blanket": Remove the risen dough, knead to release air, and roll it out into a large, thin round sheet. Sprinkle a little salt on the dough sheet, drizzle cooking oil, and spread evenly. Cut a slit from the radius, roll the sheet up into a coil, tuck the end underneath, press it down gently, and roll it out again into a round flatbread of suitable size.
7. Add vegetables and cover with the blanket: After 40 minutes of braising, add the potato chunks, bean segments, and corn segments. Adjust the salt to taste. Then lay the flatbread flat over all the ingredients. Spoon a little braising liquid over the surface of the flatbread.
8. Simmer: Cover with the lid, reduce to low heat, and continue to simmer for about 15 minutes.
9. Finish: Uncover the lid. First, remove the flatbread and cut it into pieces. For the contents in the wok, you may increase the heat to reduce the sauce until thickened.

## Health Advice
The ribs are tender and the vegetables are flavorful, but the flatbread provides a higher amount of carbohydrates and fat. It is suitable for fitness enthusiasts or manual laborers to replenish energy. During weight loss, it is recommended to reduce intake of the flatbread. Note that the ribs need to be soaked in advance to remove blood, and the flatbread should not be rolled too thick to avoid absorbing too much oil.
