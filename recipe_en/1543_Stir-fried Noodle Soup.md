# Stir-fried Noodle Soup

Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g  
💧 Room temperature water 150g  
🥚 Egg white 1  
🥬 Napa cabbage half (core and leaves processed separately)  
🧅 Scallion half (sliced into scallion pieces)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about 10  
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed  
🫒 Sesame oil about 5g  
🧈 Lard (pork fat) about 10g  
🧂 Light soy sauce as needed  
🧂 Oyster sauce about 5g  
🧂 White pepper powder as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Cutting board & chef's knife  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon & soup ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🧂 Seasoning containers  

## Instructions  
1. Place the flour in a bowl. Gradually add the room temperature water, stirring with chopsticks until flaky. Add the egg white and knead by hand into a smooth dough. The final dough should be slightly moist, not firm.
2. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 to 30 minutes to relax the gluten.
3. Cut the cabbage core into julienne strips about 3mm wide. Cut the cabbage leaves into short, wide pieces. Slice the scallion into generous amounts of scallion pieces.
4. Add vegetable oil, sesame oil, and lard to the wok. Place the Sichuan peppercorns in the cold oil and fry over medium-low heat. When the peppercorns turn dark and fragrant but not burnt, remove them with a slotted spoon and discard.
5. Heat the oil until nearly smoking, then add all the scallion pieces to instantly release their aroma. Immediately add the cabbage core strips and stir-fry quickly. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, letting the heat vaporize the soy sauce to release its fragrance. Keep the heat high throughout this process.
6. After stir-frying, add enough hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then stir in the oyster sauce.
7. Roll out the rested dough into a sheet and cut into noodles about 3mm wide.
8. Shake the noodles loose and add them to the boiling broth. Gently separate them with chopsticks. Cook over medium heat for 5 to 7 minutes, until the noodles have no white core and are soft and smooth. These noodles are best cooked soft, not firm.
9. When the noodles are almost done, add the cabbage leaves. Blanch for about 30 seconds until just tender, keeping a crisp texture.
10. Sprinkle in an appropriate amount of white pepper powder, stir, then remove from the heat.

## Health Tips  
This noodle soup, stir-fried with lard and fragrant Sichuan peppercorns, warms the body and stimulates the appetite, making it suitable for those with cold hands and feet in winter. Note that the noodles should be cooked soft but not mushy, and the cabbage core and leaves should be added separately to maintain the texture.
