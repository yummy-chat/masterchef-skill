# Microwave Red Date Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫒 Pitted red dates 100g
🍬 Brown sugar 60g (adjustable to taste)
🥚 Eggs 3
🌽 Corn oil 20g (or other flavorless vegetable oil)
🌾 All-purpose flour 85g
🧂 Baking powder 5g
🌰 Sesame seeds, walnuts as needed (for garnish)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Microwave oven
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon
📌 Toothpick

## Instructions
1. Place the pitted red dates in a bowl, add about half a bowl of water, and microwave on high heat for 6 minutes until the dates are soft and the water has mostly evaporated.
2. Remove the dates and, while still hot, mash them into a fine paste using the back of a spoon.
3. While the date paste is hot, add brown sugar and eggs, and stir well, using the residual heat to dissolve the brown sugar.
4. Add baking powder, flour, and corn oil, and mix until a thick, even batter forms.
   - Note: The amount of flour and oil can be slightly adjusted based on the batter's consistency; it should be thick and paste-like.
5. Sprinkle sesame seeds and crushed walnuts on top of the batter, then microwave on high heat for about 6 minutes.
6. After heating, insert a toothpick into the center of the cake. If it comes out dry and clean, or if the surface springs back when pressed, the cake is done.
7. Let it cool slightly, then run a knife along the edge of the bowl to loosen and unmold. Slice and serve.

## Health Advice
This red date cake is made in a microwave for convenience. The combination of brown sugar and red dates helps warm the body and dispel cold, making it suitable for those with cold hands and feet in winter or those needing to supplement iron. Be mindful of sugar intake; diabetic patients should consume with caution. Microwave heating times may vary depending on power levels; adjust as needed to avoid overcooking or burning.
