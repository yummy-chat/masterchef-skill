# Spicy Tiger-Skin Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken feet, appropriate amount (pre-blanched and drained)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (for braising)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for braising)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves, appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (for final stir-frying)
🌶️ Fresh chili peppers, appropriate amount (for final stir-frying)
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount (for final stir-frying)
🧅 Chopped scallion, appropriate amount (for garnish)
🍯 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for mixing and coloring, plus 1 tablespoon for braising)
🍯 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 teaspoon
🍬 White sugar, half a tablespoon
💧 Hot water, appropriate amount (to submerge the chicken feet)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for stir-frying the base ingredients)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Basin (for soaking chicken feet)
♨️ Air fryer
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Drain the pre-blanched chicken feet, add dark soy sauce, and mix well to coat evenly.
2. Place the chicken feet in the air fryer, set to 200°C, and cook for 20 minutes, flipping once halfway, until the surface wrinkles and forms a tiger-skin texture.
   **Key Point**: Using an air fryer instead of deep-frying reduces oil usage.
3. Remove the chicken feet and immediately soak them in cold water for 20 minutes, until the skin is visibly wrinkled with a tiger-skin pattern.
4. Put the soaked chicken feet into a pot, add scallion, ginger, dried chili peppers, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, 3 tablespoons light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon, and half a tablespoon white sugar.
5. Pour in hot water until the chicken feet are submerged, cover the pot, and simmer over medium-low heat for 30 minutes.
6. Remove the chicken feet and set aside. Use a slotted spoon to strain out the spice residue, reserving the broth.
7. Heat oil in a separate wok over high heat. Once the oil is hot, add minced garlic, fresh chili peppers, and chili flakes, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
8. Add the chicken feet and reserved broth, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry until the sauce thickens and clings evenly to the chicken feet.
9. Sprinkle with chopped scallion, toss to combine, then remove from heat and plate.

## Health Advice
Chicken feet are rich in collagen but high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to serve with vegetables, and those with high blood pressure should control seasoning amounts. Using an air fryer instead of deep-frying reduces oil intake, while the soaking and braising process still keeps the chicken feet tender and flavorful. Suitable for occasional indulgence.
