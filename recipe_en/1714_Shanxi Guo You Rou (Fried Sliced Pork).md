# Shanxi Guo You Rou (Fried Sliced Pork)

Cuisine: Shanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin 150g  
🌱 Garlic chives (or garlic scapes) 6 pieces  
🍄 Black fungus (wood ear mushrooms) 20g  
🧅 Onion 100g  
🧄 Scallion, ginger, garlic 50g  
🧂 Salt 10g  
🍶 Light soy sauce 20ml  
🍾 Cooking wine as needed  
🍶 Aged vinegar 10ml  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder as needed  
🧂 Chicken bouillon as needed  
🥚 Egg 1  
🌾 Cornstarch 10g  
🛢️ Cooking oil 300ml  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔥 Wok or large skillet  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Soak the black fungus in cold water in advance until fully rehydrated. If short on time, use hot water to speed up the process.  
**Note**: After soaking, wash and tear into small pieces. Set aside.

2. Cut the pork tenderloin into thin slices about 0.3 cm thick. Place in a bowl, add light soy sauce, cooking wine, and Sichuan peppercorn powder. Crack in the egg and mix thoroughly with your hands. Add cornstarch and mix well. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil to seal in moisture. Marinate for 15 minutes.

3. Cut the garlic chives into segments about 3 cm long. Slice the onion into diamond-shaped pieces. Set aside.

4. Heat the wok over high heat and add enough cooking oil. When the oil reaches about 50% hot (150°C / 300°F), add the marinated pork slices. Quickly separate them with chopsticks and fry until the surface is slightly golden and cooked through. Remove and drain excess oil. Set aside.  
**Note**: Control the heat when frying the pork; only fry until the surface is lightly golden to avoid overcooking and making the meat tough.

5. Pour out the excess oil from the wok, leaving about 10ml of oil as a base. Heat until about 70% hot (210°C / 410°F).

6. Add the scallion, ginger, and garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. First, add the garlic chives and stir-fry until just cooked. Then add the black fungus and onion. Season with light soy sauce and Sichuan peppercorn powder. Stir-fry quickly a few times. Then add the fried pork slices and continue stir-frying evenly.

7. Add salt and stir-fry evenly. Just before removing from heat, drizzle in the aged vinegar and chicken bouillon. Quickly toss everything together, then transfer to a plate.  
**Tip**: If you prefer a saucier texture, add a small amount of cornstarch slurry (water + cornstarch) just before removing from heat to create a light glaze.

## Health Tips  
Pork tenderloin provides high-quality protein, while black fungus and garlic chives add dietary fiber. This dish is suitable for those needing iron or managing weight. However, due to the high oil content, it is recommended to pair with light vegetables or multigrain rice and to control daily fat intake.
