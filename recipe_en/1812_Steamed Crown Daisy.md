# Steamed Crown Daisy

Cuisine: Common home cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Crown daisy (适量, washed and cut into large sections)  
🌾 White flour (all-purpose flour) 1 tablespoon  
🌽 Corn flour 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt (a small amount, used separately for the steaming powder and garlic paste)  
🍶 Soy sauce 20 ml  
🍶 Vinegar 30 ml  
🫒 Sesame oil 10 ml  
🌶️ Chili oil 10 ml  
🛢️ Cooking oil 1 tablespoon  
🧄 Garlic (适量, mashed into a paste)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥣 Small glass bowl  
🍲 Steamer (with steaming rack)  
🧄 Mortar and pestle for garlic  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon

## Steps
1. Cut the crown daisy into long sections, place them in a large bowl, add 1 tablespoon of cooking oil, and mix thoroughly by hand.  
   **Key point**: Adding oil first helps lock in the moisture of the vegetables, preventing them from releasing water during steaming and keeping them tender and crisp.
2. In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of white flour, 1 tablespoon of corn flour, and a small amount of salt until well combined to make the steaming powder.  
   **Note**: The ratio of white flour to corn flour is 1:1, and the salt is only for flavor.
3. Peel the garlic, place it in a mortar, add a pinch of salt, and mash into a paste. Then add 20 ml soy sauce, 30 ml vinegar, 10 ml sesame oil, and 10 ml chili oil, stirring well to make the dipping sauce. Set aside.
4. Gradually sprinkle the steaming powder over the oiled crown daisy in small batches, gently mixing by hand until each leaf is evenly coated with a thin layer of powder. Then spread the leaves loosely on the steaming rack, avoiding overlapping or sticking.  
   **Key point**: The powder coating should be thin and even, and the leaves should be spread loosely to allow steam to penetrate, ensuring they are fully cooked and have a dry, loose texture.
5. Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Once steam rises, place the steaming rack inside, cover with the lid, and steam over medium-high heat for 3 minutes.  
   **Note**: Strictly control the time to 3 minutes; longer steaming will cause the vegetables to discolor and lose their crispness.
6. After steaming, remove immediately and use chopsticks to quickly loosen the leaves. Transfer to a serving plate to prevent residual heat from causing clumping.
7. Dip the steamed crown daisy into the prepared garlic sauce from step 3 and enjoy.

## Health Tips
This steamed crown daisy dish is low in fat and high in fiber, making it ideal for cleansing the intestines and reducing fat in spring. Wrapping the vegetables in flour for steaming helps retain nutrients, and the garlic dipping sauce is both appetizing and antibacterial. Be sure to strictly control the steaming time to 3 minutes to maintain a tender, crisp texture.
