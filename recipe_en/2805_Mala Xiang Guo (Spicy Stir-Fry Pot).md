# Mala Xiang Guo (Spicy Stir-Fry Pot)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥦 Broccoli (to taste)
🥬 Bok choy (small bok choy, to taste)
🎍 Parboiled bamboo shoots (to taste)
🍄 King oyster mushrooms (to taste)
🍄‍🟫 White beech mushrooms (to taste)
🦐 Shrimp (to taste)
🐚 Scallop meat (to taste)
🥓 Bacon (to taste)
🌭 Chinese sausage (to taste)
🥩 Sliced beef or lamb rolls (to taste)
🍜 Cellophane noodles (to taste)
🧂 Salt (generous amount, for blanching water)
🫒 Mixed oil (flaxseed oil, sesame oil, peanut oil, to taste, generous amount needed to prevent sticking and enhance aroma)
🌶️ Mala Xiang Guo paste (1 jar, core compound seasoning)
🍾 Light soy sauce (a small amount, for stir-frying and adjusting color and freshness before finishing)
🧴 Dark soy sauce (a small amount, for adjusting color before finishing)
🍶 Cooking wine (a small amount, for stir-frying to release aroma)
🧄 Scallions (to taste, sliced)
🧄 Ginger (to taste, sliced)
🧄 Garlic (to taste, sliced)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/basin
🔪 Cutting board
🥢 Chopsticks/wooden spatula

## Instructions
1. **Prepare ingredients and blanch**
   - Wash and drain all vegetables, slice mushrooms, cut meat into pieces, and prepare cellophane noodles.
   - Bring a large pot of water to a boil, add a generous amount of salt. The water should be ample and kept at a rolling boil.
   - Blanch ingredients in batches in order: first blanch broccoli, bok choy, bamboo shoots, and other vegetables. Remove and drain immediately once the color deepens, to avoid overloading the pot and lowering the water temperature, which can cause the vegetables to turn yellow. Then blanch the mushrooms, remove and drain thoroughly. Next, blanch bacon and Chinese sausage to remove excess salt. Then blanch the seafood; note that water used for seafood should not be reused for vegetables. Finally, blanch the cellophane noodles, then immediately transfer to cold water to prevent sticking once cooked. Sliced beef or lamb rolls can be left unblanched or quickly blanched and set aside.
   - **Key**: All blanched ingredients must be thoroughly drained; excess water will prevent the development of a dry, fragrant texture during stir-frying.
2. **Season the wok**
   - Heat the iron wok until very hot, pour in a generous amount of mixed oil. Swirl the wok to coat the entire surface with oil, then pour out the excess oil or leave a suitable amount as a base. This step effectively prevents sticking, and ample oil is recommended.
3. **Stir-fry the base sauce**
   - Leave a base oil in the wok. First, add the blanched king oyster mushrooms and white beech mushrooms, stir-fry briefly to even out the wok temperature.
   - Add scallion, ginger, and garlic slices, stir-fry until fragrant.
   - Pour in the entire jar of Mala Xiang Guo paste, reduce heat to medium-low, and stir-fry slowly until the red oil is released and the aroma is rich. When stir-frying the sauce, the heat should not be too high; rely on the wok's temperature and stir-fry gently to avoid burning.
4. **Deglaze and stir-fry meat**
   - Once the sauce is red and glossy, drizzle a small amount of light soy sauce or cooking wine along the edge of the wok to quickly release the aroma.
   - Add all the blanched meats and seafood, turn the heat to medium-high, and stir-fry vigorously until the surface moisture of the ingredients is dried, and the wok bottom shows clear oil again with no watery steam.
5. **Add vegetables and cellophane noodles**
   - Add broccoli, bok choy, bamboo shoots, and other vegetables, continue to stir-fry to remove excess moisture.
   - Finally, add the cellophane noodles from the water, and toss quickly to combine. Cellophane noodles should not be stir-fried for too long, as they stick easily to the wok.
6. **Color and finish**
   - According to the desired color, drizzle a small amount of dark soy sauce and light soy sauce to adjust the color, and stir-fry quickly to combine.
   - Check the wok bottom: when only oil remains and no excess liquid, immediately turn off the heat and serve. The finished dish should be oily and dry-fragrant, with no liquid released.

## Health Advice
This Mala Xiang Guo brings together vegetables, mushrooms, seafood, and meat, offering rich nutrition. However, it is relatively high in calories, so portion control is advised. It is recommended to serve with multigrain rice or a clear soup to balance the heavy oil and salt. Those with high blood lipids or sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
