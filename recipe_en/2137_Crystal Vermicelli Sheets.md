# Crystal Vermicelli Sheets
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer, April to August

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing slurry)
🍽️ Flat-bottomed metal or heat-resistant round plate (for shaping)
🍲 Stockpot (for boiling water)
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for cold water cooling)
🔧 Food tongs (for handling the plate)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board (for cutting strips)

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the starch slurry: In a large bowl, mix sweet potato starch with water and add 2 spoons of salt. Stir thoroughly until smooth and free of lumps. Stir again before each use to prevent settling.
2. Bring water to a boil in the pot. Without greasing the flat plate, scoop in about two spoons of slurry and swirl to evenly coat the bottom.
3. Float the flat plate on the surface of the boiling water, using steam and water temperature to fully solidify the slurry surface.
4. Use food tongs to fully submerge the flat plate in the boiling water. Cook for about 1 minute until the vermicelli sheet turns completely transparent.
5. Quickly immerse the plate with the sheet into a basin of cold water to cool. Once cooled, gently peel off the sheet from the edge, removing it whole.
6. Cut the vermicelli sheet into wide strips and place in a bowl. Add garlic water, sliced Thai bird's eye chilies, shredded cucumber, and cilantro sections. Season with a pinch of salt, fragrant vinegar, light soy sauce, and chili oil. Sprinkle crushed peanuts on top and mix well.

## Health Advice
This high-starch cold vermicelli sheet contains almost no fat, making it suitable for appetizing and refreshing the palate in summer. However, those sensitive to blood sugar should pair it with protein or vegetables. Eating sweet potato vermicelli sheets alone is equivalent to consuming a large amount of carbohydrates, so it is recommended as a side dish rather than a main course.
