# Deep-Fried Ribbonfish

Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Ribbonfish – appropriate amount (fresh, with shiny silver membrane, bright eyes, and springy when pressed)
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount
🌶️ Small chili pepper – appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro – appropriate amount
⭐️ Star anise – appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves – appropriate amount
🌳 Cinnamon – appropriate amount
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn – appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili – appropriate amount
🧂 Black pepper – appropriate amount
🧂 Sea salt – appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder – appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount
🧂 Salt and pepper seasoning – appropriate amount (sprinkle after frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Scaling knife or regular knife
✂️ Scissors
🪨 Mortar and pestle
🥘 Small pot
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Glass bowl
🍽️ Plate
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Preparation Steps
1. Use a knife to scrape off the silver membrane from the ribbonfish. Cut off the fins, head, and gills. Cut the fish body into even sections, and make a few shallow cuts on each piece to allow better flavor absorption.
2. Place star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, Sichuan peppercorn, and dried chili into a small pot. Add water, bring to a boil, then remove from heat and let cool to make a spiced water.
3. Crush the scallion, ginger, garlic, small chili, and cilantro in a mortar and pestle. Transfer to a large bowl, add black pepper, sea salt, chicken powder, cooking wine, the cooled spiced water, and an appropriate amount of plain water. Stir well to make a marinade.
   **Note**: It is not recommended to add light soy sauce, as it may cause the fish to darken when fried.
4. Place the ribbonfish pieces into the marinade and let sit for 1 hour, or refrigerate overnight. Remove and pat dry with kitchen paper towels.
5. Heat oil in a wok to approximately 60% hot. Add the ribbonfish pieces and fry over medium heat until set and light golden brown. Remove and drain.
   **Key**: Do not stir immediately after adding to the oil, allowing the fish to set quickly at high heat and prevent it from falling apart.
6. Raise the oil temperature to approximately 70% hot. Return the pre-fried fish to the oil and fry for about 2 minutes, until deep golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain.
7. Sprinkle with salt and pepper seasoning while still hot and serve.

## Health Advice
Ribbonfish is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it beneficial for brain and heart health. However, since it is fried, the calorie content is relatively high. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad for balance. Fresh ribbonfish has a mild fishy smell; using spiced water in the marinade is a key step for removing odor and enhancing flavor.
