# Salted Egg Three-Ingredient Soup 🥣  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp: 6–8 (head-on, peeled body)  
🥚 Salted Egg Yolks: 2 (crushed by hand)  
🍄 Shiitake Mushrooms: about 2 (sliced)  
🍄 White Beech Mushrooms: about 1 small handful (cut into sections)  
🧈 Firm Tofu: about 1 small piece (sliced)  
🌾 All-Purpose Flour: as needed (for making dough sheets)  
🫒 Cooking Oil: as needed  
💧 Water: about 500–600 ml  
🧂 Salt: as needed  
🌿 White Pepper Powder: as needed  
🧅 Scallion: 1 (chopped into葱花)  
🧄 Ginger: as needed (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic: as needed (smashed)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Strainer  
🍽️ Serving Utensils (bowl, spoon)  

## Instructions  
1. Mix all-purpose flour with an appropriate amount of water, knead into a smooth dough, and let it rest for a while.  
   **Key Point**: The dough should be moderately soft and firm to make it easy to stretch thin later.  
2. Peel the shrimp, keep the heads; crush the salted egg yolks; slice the shiitake mushrooms; cut the white beech mushrooms into sections; slice the firm tofu; chop the scallion into葱花; slice the ginger; smash the garlic.  
3. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat, add shrimp heads, ginger slices, garlic, and crushed salted egg yolks. Stir-fry until the shrimp heads turn red and release red oil, and the salted egg yolks become sandy and bubbly.  
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry thoroughly to fully release the aroma of shrimp oil and egg yolks.  
4. Add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat.  
   **Note**: Add the full amount of water at once.  
5. Use a strainer to remove and discard the shrimp heads, ginger slices, garlic, and other residue, keeping the clear broth.  
6. Add the tofu slices, white beech mushroom sections, and shiitake mushroom slices to the broth. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the mushrooms soften and the tofu is heated through.  
7. Stretch the rested dough by hand into very thin sheets, and add them one by one into the broth, ensuring they are separated to prevent sticking.  
   **Key Point**: The thinner the dough sheets are stretched, the quicker they cook, and the smoother the texture.  
8. When the dough sheets float and become translucent, add the peeled shrimp, and cook until the shrimp turn red and curl up, indicating they are done.  
   **Note**: Add the shrimp later to avoid overcooking.  
9. Season with salt and white pepper powder, sprinkle with chopped scallion, then immediately turn off the heat and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. The salted egg yolks provide lecithin, making it suitable for those needing calcium supplementation and immune system enhancement. However, salted egg yolks and salt are high in sodium, so those with hypertension should control the amount used. The dough sheets and tofu are easy to digest, making it suitable for people of all ages.
