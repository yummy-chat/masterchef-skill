# Braised Gluten Balls Stuffed with Meat
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 Gluten balls, one bag (about 20 pieces)
🐖 Pork front leg meat, 500 grams (30% fat, 70% lean)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic, a few cloves
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, a few
🧂 Salt, 5 grams
🌿 White pepper, appropriate amount
💧 Ginger-scallion water, 100 grams (crush ginger and scallions, add water, and squeeze out the juice)
🫙 Soybean paste, 1 tablespoon
🍶 Yellow wine, 1 tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🥄 Dark soy sauce, half a tablespoon
🍬 White sugar, half a tablespoon
🍇 Black vinegar, a few drops
🥜 Sesame oil, a few drops
🌽 Cornstarch, 1 small teaspoon (for thickening)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🥄 Spoon
✂️ Scissors
🎒 Ziplock bag
🍲 Lid

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Make ginger-scallion water:** Crush the ginger and scallions, add 100 grams of water, squeeze firmly with your hands to extract the juice, strain out the solids, and set the liquid aside.
2.  **Prepare the meat filling:** Add 5 grams of salt and an appropriate amount of white pepper to the ground pork. Add the 100 grams of ginger-scallion water in two batches, stirring vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed after each addition. Continue stirring until the meat becomes sticky and forms threads. Then, repeatedly slap the mixture against the bowl a dozen times to make the filling firmer and springier.
3.  **Fill the gluten balls:** Place the meat filling into a ziplock bag and cut a small hole in one corner to create a makeshift piping bag. Use your index finger to poke a small hole in a gluten ball, wiggle it inside to enlarge the cavity, then pipe the filling in until it is full. Use your finger to tuck in any excess filling that spills out.
4.  **Prepare the sauce:** Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and garlic cloves, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the crushed star anise, dried chili peppers, and 1 tablespoon of soybean paste. Reduce the heat to low and stir-fry until the aroma of the bean paste is released.
5.  **Braise:** Add an appropriate amount of water, then pour in 1 tablespoon of yellow wine, 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce, half a tablespoon of dark soy sauce, half a tablespoon of white sugar, white pepper, and a few drops of black vinegar. Stir to combine. Gently place the stuffed gluten balls into the wok. Cover with a lid and simmer over low heat for 15 minutes.
6.  **Reduce and thicken the sauce:** Remove the lid and pick out the spices (star anise, chili, ginger, etc.). Increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Mix the cornstarch with a small amount of water to create a slurry. Drizzle the slurry into the wok in batches, stirring gently, until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the gluten balls.
7.  **Serve:** Just before turning off the heat, drizzle in a few drops of sesame oil. Transfer the gluten balls to a serving plate, pour the remaining sauce over them, and garnish with chopped scallions.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in filling and high in protein. However, gluten balls are highly absorbent of oil. It is recommended to serve with a side of stir-fried seasonal vegetables to balance the total calorie intake. People with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
