## Cooking Steps
1.  Clean and slaughter the fish. Chop the head and bones into pieces, and cut the belly into finger-width strips.
**Note**: Laifeng fish must be cut into strips or pieces, not thinly sliced, to preserve a rustic texture.

2.  Place the fish bones and meat in a bowl. Add crushed ginger, scallions, an ample amount of salt, white pepper powder, and pour in beer. Mix well and marinate for about 10 minutes.
**Note**: Do not marinate for too long to avoid the fish becoming overly salty.

3.  Rinse the marinated fish thoroughly with plenty of water until the blood is gone and the fish turns white. Drain well and remove the ginger and scallions.
**Note**: Thorough cleaning is key to achieving white and delicate fish meat.

4.  Prepare the aromatics and knife-cut chili:
    - Slice pickled chilies into rings and mince. Mince pickled ginger and garlic. Cut scallions into sections/mince.
    - Make knife-cut chili: Add a little oil to the wok, stir-fry dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns over low heat until the color darkens. Remove and chop finely with a knife.
5.  Heat the wok with mixed oil. First, fry the green Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Then, add pickled chilies, minced pickled ginger, and minced garlic in order, stir-frying until fragrant. Next, add the broad bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red and fragrant.
6.  Add the dried chili segments to the wok and stir-fry briefly. Immediately add the knife-cut chili and stir quickly. Pour in water right away to prevent the knife-cut chili from turning bitter with prolonged frying.
7.  Add scallion sections to the broth. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon, sugar, white pepper powder, soy sauce, and vinegar.
8.  Once the broth boils, add the drained fish pieces. Cook over **high heat** until the skin tightens, then immediately reduce to **low heat**. Simmer slowly for about 4 minutes until the fish is just cooked through.
**Note**: First, use high heat to seal in moisture, then low heat to cook through. Avoid prolonged boiling.

9.  Once the fish is cooked through, drizzle in the sweet potato starch slurry in batches. Gently push the fish pieces in the wok until the sauce thickens, turns glossy, and bubbles.
10. Transfer the fish and sauce to a serving plate. Sprinkle the top with minced garlic, ground Sichuan pepper, and chopped scallions.
11. Heat another portion of mixed oil in a clean wok. Fry the dried chili segments until they are about to change color. Quickly splash in half a tablespoon of vinegar. Immediately pour the hot oil over the aromatics on the fish to release a burst of fragrance.

## Health Advice
This dish uses a large amount of pickled chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, and knife-cut chili for a fiery and aromatic flavor. The fish pieces are marinated, cleaned, and gently simmered, resulting in a delicate and flavorful texture. It is suitable for those who enjoy spicy food, but individuals with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs should be cautious due to the high salt and oil content.
