# Whole Wheat Toast Bread
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Whole wheat flour 250g
💧 Room temperature water 240g
🔥 Boiling water 1/3 cup
🍞 Bread flour 250g
🧈 Unsalted butter 40g (softened)
🍚 Yeast 2 teaspoons
🍬 Sugar 40g
🧂 Salt 1/2 teaspoon

## Tools & Equipment
🥛 Small saucepan
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Stainless steel bowl (or glass bowl)
🍞 Stand mixer (with dough hook)
🥖 Rolling pin
🍴 Dough scraper
📦 Plastic wrap
🧁 Loaf pans (2)
🧊 Cooling rack
🔥 Oven

## Instructions
1. Add 1/3 cup boiling water to whole wheat flour and stir evenly to create a scalded dough, which makes the final product exceptionally soft.
2. Add 240g room temperature water and mix well. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight, allowing the bran to fully absorb moisture and soften, helping the bread rise well and have a tender texture.
3. Remove the chilled dough and directly add yeast, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
4. Transfer the mixture to the stand mixer bowl, add bread flour, maintaining a 1:1 ratio of whole wheat flour to bread flour.
5. Mix on speed 2 until evenly combined. Check the hydration level and adjust if necessary.
6. Increase to speed 4 and knead for about 3 minutes, until the dough comes together and the bowl is clean.
7. Add softened unsalted butter. Mix on speed 2 until absorbed, then increase to speed 4 and continue kneading for a total of 12-15 minutes, until the dough is smooth and shiny and can be stretched into a large, strong windowpane. If you cannot stretch it immediately, let it rest for a few minutes and try again.
8. Shape the dough into a smooth ball, return it to the bowl, cover, and let it undergo the first fermentation for about 1.5 hours, until doubled in size, adjusting time based on room temperature.
9. Gently punch down the fermented dough to release air. Divide into 6 equal pieces and round each piece. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 10 minutes to make shaping easier.
10. Take one small dough piece, smooth side up, and roll it into an oval. Flip it over, fold both sides toward the center, then shape into a long strip.
11. Take the first long strip, smooth side up, and roll it out again. Flip it over, roll it tightly from one end into a cylinder. This shaping requires thorough degassing to align the gluten structure.
12. Place the rolled dough pieces into the loaf pans, 3 per pan.
13. Cover with plastic wrap and let the dough undergo the second fermentation in a warm place until it rises to 80-90% of the pan's height.
14. Optionally, brush the surface with a layer of egg wash for a shinier crust.
15. Bake in a preheated oven at 175°C for 30-35 minutes. The bread will first expand and then slightly shrink in the oven, indicating it is done.
16. Immediately after removing from the oven, tap the pans on the counter to prevent the bread from collapsing. Then unmold and let cool completely on a cooling rack. You can tap the bottom of the bread; a hollow sound confirms it is fully baked.
17. Once completely cooled, store in a sealed plastic bag at room temperature.

## Health Advice
This whole wheat toast uses scalded dough and an overnight water roux method, resulting in exceptionally soft texture. It is especially suitable as a breakfast staple for those on a weight-loss diet or controlling sugar intake. It is recommended to pair with eggs or lean meat to balance carbohydrates and protein, avoiding eating it plain in large amounts to prevent blood sugar spikes.
