# Fried Pork Ribs with Fermented Tofu and Garlic

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 12 pieces of pork spare ribs (each 6-8 cm, choose the front rib section)  
🧄 2 liang of garlic cloves (unwashed, to retain original aroma)  
🫧 Sparkling water (soda water) as needed (enough to submerge ribs)  
🧂 5 qian of fermented tofu (Nam Yu)  
🧂 1 qian of chicken powder  
🥜 3 qian of peanut butter  
🍬 4 qian of granulated sugar  
🧂 1.2 qian of salt  
🍚 5 qian of glutinous rice flour  
🍚 4 qian of rice flour  
💧 2 liang of water (for making garlic juice)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🌀 Blender  
🧻 Kitchen paper  
🔥 Induction cooker or gas stove (for frying)  
🌡️ Oil thermometer  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the ribs into 6-8 cm segments, place them in a glass bowl, pour in sparkling water until the ribs are fully submerged, and refrigerate for 1 hour.  
**Note**: Sparkling water tenderizes the meat; it is normal for the ribs to turn pale after soaking.
2. Remove the ribs, use kitchen paper to dry each piece, absorbing surface and internal blood and moisture, and arrange neatly on a plate.  
**Key**: Thoroughly drying ensures the marinade adheres well and the final product is crispy.
3. Place the unwashed garlic cloves and water in a blender, and blend into a concentrated garlic juice.  
**Ratio**: 1:1 garlic to water for purer garlic flavor.
4. In a bowl, pour the garlic juice, then add fermented tofu, chicken powder, granulated sugar, salt, and peanut butter. Stir until fully combined into a marinade.
5. Pour the marinade over the ribs and mix well, then add glutinous rice flour and rice flour, ensuring each rib is evenly coated with the sauce and flours. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 2 hours to marinate.  
**Effect**: The combination of flours yields a crispy exterior and tender interior.
6. Pour oil into a pot and heat to 120°C. Add the ribs one by one. First, fry on low heat for 4 minutes, then turn to medium heat and fry for another 4 minutes, until the bones are slightly exposed and the ribs turn light yellow and set.  
**Key**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
7. Raise the oil temperature to 160°C, and fry the ribs again for 1 minute until the skin is red and crispy. Remove and drain the oil, then plate.  
**Purpose of refrying**: Extract excess oil and enhance the crunchy texture.

## Health Suggestions
This dish is rich in protein and calcium, suitable for muscle gain or recovery periods. However, due to its high calorie content from frying, it is recommended to pair with stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition. Soaking the ribs in sparkling water softens the meat, and controlling the oil temperature during frying helps reduce the formation of acrylamide.
