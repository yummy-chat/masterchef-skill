# Rice Wine (Jiuniang)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 500g round glutinous rice (preferred; high in amylopectin, easy to produce wine)  
💧 250g cool boiled water (for cooling and separating rice grains) + 500g (for secondary fermentation to make rice wine)  
🧂 1g Chuzhai sweet koji (plant-based koji, fragrant, strong wine flavor)  
🧂 Or 2g Angel sweet koji (industrial koji, stable)  
🧂 Or 2g Angel sweet koji (flavor type) (slightly less sweet, slightly stronger wine flavor, recommended)  
🌹 (Optional) 15g dried rose petals (remove stems and pistils, rinse and drain, tear apart)  
🌼 (Optional) 10g dried osmanthus flowers (rinse and drain)  
⚠️ No additional sugar, salt, or other seasonings are used in this recipe. The sweetness comes from the natural fermentation of glutinous rice into glucose.  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking glutinous rice)  
🔲 Colander/strainer (for draining glutinous rice)  
🍳 Steamer and steaming rack (for steaming glutinous rice)  
🥣 Heat-resistant glass bowl/ceramic pot (fermentation container)  
📦 Plastic wrap (for sealing)  
🥖 Rolling pin (for making a well)  
🥄 Silicone spatula/spoon (for mixing rice)  
🔬 Fine mesh strainer (for filtering rice wine)  
🥘 Cooking pot (for boiling to sterilize)  
🫙 Glass sealable bottle (for bottling and storage)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for weighing ingredients)  

## Instructions
1.  Rinse the round glutinous rice until clean, add enough water to soak overnight. After soaking, rinse again and drain thoroughly.
    **Key point**: Sufficient soaking time allows the glutinous rice to fully absorb water, making it easier to gelatinize during steaming.

2.  Line the steamer with a damp cheesecloth. Spread the glutinous rice evenly on top. Poke several ventilation holes in the surface with chopsticks. Steam over boiling water over high heat for about 30 minutes, until the rice grains are translucent, plump, and have no white cores.
    **Note**: Adjust steaming time based on heat; the rice should be fully cooked but not overly soft.

3.  Crush any lumpy koji into powder beforehand. Scald the fermentation container with boiling water to sterilize and keep it warm. If making the flavored version, quickly rinse the dried rose petals or dried osmanthus flowers with cool boiled water, drain them, and tear the petals apart.

4.  Transfer the steamed glutinous rice into the fermentation container. Use a spatula to spread it out and stir to cool. When the temperature drops to 30–35°C, evenly sprinkle the koji powder over the rice. Gently fold and mix so the koji is thoroughly combined with the rice.
    **Key point**: Too high a temperature will kill the yeast, while too low a temperature slows fermentation and can cause sourness. Mix gently to avoid compacting the rice.

5.  Add 250g of cool boiled water. Use a spatula to gently fold and mix, allowing the rice to absorb the water and become separate grains. There should be no standing water at the bottom of the bowl. After mixing, smooth the surface of the rice without pressing it down.
    **Key point**: Standing water at the bottom can cause fermentation to fail; ensure the water is fully absorbed by the rice.

6.  Use a rolling pin to poke a hole straight to the bottom in the center of the rice. Immediately seal the container tightly with plastic wrap.
    **Key point**: The well (hole) makes it easy to observe the liquid produced and prevents the center from getting too hot. The plastic wrap must cling tightly to the container. No need to poke holes, to prevent contamination.

7.  Place the container in a warm spot at 28–32°C for the first fermentation, about 48 hours. Signs of successful fermentation: clear liquid seeps into the well, and the rice mass floats up.
    - If making the flavored version, immediately after adding the koji in step 4, mix in the drained petals/osmanthus flowers, then continue with the remaining steps.
    **Note**: If the temperature is too low, use a proofing box or wrap with a cotton blanket. Avoid frequently opening the lid to check during fermentation.

8.  Secondary fermentation to make rice wine: After the first fermentation is complete, directly add 500g of cool boiled water. Seal again and ferment at the same temperature for 24 hours, until the rice grains are hollowed out, float up significantly, and the liquid has visibly increased.

9.  Strain the fermented fermented rice (jiuniang) or rice wine through a fine mesh strainer. Use a spoon to gently press the rice solids to extract the liquid, then discard the solids. Pour the strained liquid into a cooking pot. Bring to a boil, then maintain a gentle boil for 1–2 minutes to sterilize. Skim off any foam that rises.
    **Purpose**: Boiling to sterilize extends the shelf life when refrigerated. Skimming the foam makes the wine clearer.

10. Allow the boiled wine to cool naturally to room temperature. Carefully pour it into sterilized glass sealable bottles, trying to avoid mixing in the sediment at the bottom. Seal the bottles and store them in the refrigerator. It is recommended to consume promptly. After six months of refrigeration, the wine flavor will still slowly intensify.

## Health Advice
Rice wine is rich in probiotics and contains a small amount of alcohol. It can aid digestion and is suitable for people with a cold spleen and stomach or weak digestive function. Ensure the fermentation temperature is not too high, as this can deactivate the yeast and cause fermentation to fail.
