# Bitter Melon Omelette
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥒 2 bitter melons  
🥚 4 eggs  
🦐 Dried shrimp (Xiaolin shrimp meat), as needed  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, as needed (red, sliced into rings)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧂 3g salt  
🍬 2g sugar  
🍾 A few drops of white vinegar  
🌿 A pinch of white pepper  
💧 2 tablespoons starch water  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Small spoon  
🔪 Knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍽️ Plate  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add whole bitter melons and a spoonful of cooking oil. Blanch for about 30 seconds until the color brightens, then immediately remove and immerse in cold water to cool.  
   **Key Point**: Blanching whole bitter melons keeps their shape intact. Adding oil enhances the color and reduces pesticide residue; cold water maintains a crisp texture.  
2. Cut the cooled bitter melons in half lengthwise. Use a small spoon to carefully scrape out the white pith and seeds, especially the yellow part near the pith, until the flesh is translucent green. Then slice diagonally into thin pieces about the thickness of a coin.  
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly removing the white pith is key to reducing bitterness. Diagonal slicing ensures even thickness and consistent texture.  
3. Crack 4 eggs into a small bowl. Add a few drops of white vinegar and beat thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is smooth.  
   **Key Point**: White vinegar removes the eggy smell and increases fluffiness when frying.  
4. In a bowl with the bitter melon slices, add 3g salt, 2g sugar, a pinch of white pepper, and the chili pepper rings. Mix well. Then pour in the egg mixture and add 2 tablespoons of starch water. Stir until evenly combined.  
   **Key Point**: Seasoning the bitter melon first before mixing with the egg allows the flavors to penetrate better. Starch water makes the final texture smoother and more tender.  
5. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a wok. Add dried shrimp and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Then add the chopped scallions and quickly stir-fry a few times.  
   **Key Point**: Gently frying the dried shrimp over low heat removes any fishy smell and enhances umami. Briefly stir-fry the scallions just until fragrant to avoid burning.  
6. Pour the bitter melon-egg mixture into the wok and spread it into a flat pancake. When the bottom of the egg starts to set, gently push the uncooked liquid egg in the center toward the edges with a spatula so it heats evenly and sets.  
   **Key Point**: Pushing the egg mixture helps it cook evenly and prevents the center from being too thick and undercooked.  
7. Once one side of the egg pancake is golden brown and set, place a plate upside down on top of the pancake. Hold the plate firmly, invert the wok, and let the pancake slide completely onto the plate. Then slide it back into the wok and continue cooking the other side over medium-low heat until golden brown and set.  
   **Key Point**: Using a plate to flip the pancake keeps it intact. Cook over medium-low heat throughout to prevent burning.  
8. After both sides are golden brown, use a spatula to cut the pancake into small pieces directly in the wok. Transfer to a plate and serve.

## Health Advice  
Blanching the bitter melon reduces bitterness, dried shrimp enhance umami, and the eggs are fluffy. This dish is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for cooling down and relieving summer heat. Use less salt; it is safe for people with high blood pressure, but those with a weak spleen or stomach should consume it with caution.
