# Braised Tofu with Pickled Mustard Greens  
Cuisine: Anhui Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Pickled mustard greens (salted vegetable stems) About 1.5 jin (rinsed, squeezed dry, and chopped)  
🫘 Tender tofu Appropriate amount (or firm tofu can also be used)  
🥩 Pork belly Appropriate amount (skin removed, cut into small strips)  
🍄 King oyster mushrooms Appropriate amount (torn into strips)  
🧅 Scallions Appropriate amount  
🧄 Ginger Appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic Appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers Appropriate amount  
🌶️ Red chili pepper A little (for garnish)  
🥬 Garlic sprouts A little (for garnish)  
🫒 Cooking oil A little  
🍶 Yellow wine Appropriate amount  
🍯 Light soy sauce Appropriate amount  
💧 Water Sufficient amount  
🧂 MSG Appropriate amount  
🤍 White pepper powder Appropriate amount  
🍯 Dark soy sauce A little (for color adjustment)  
🧂 Salt Add as needed (not added in the video due to sufficient saltiness from pickled greens)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🍲 Small stainless steel pot  
🔥 Alcohol stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rinse the pickled mustard greens repeatedly under running water, then squeeze them dry thoroughly and chop into fine pieces.  
**Note**: Wash off excess salt and make sure to squeeze out all water.

2. Cut the tender tofu into small cubes; slice the skinless pork belly into thick slices, then cut into small strips.  
**Key**: Cutting into small strips helps render the fat.

3. Wash the king oyster mushrooms and tear them into strips; cut scallions into sections, slice ginger and garlic, and set aside with dried chili peppers; shred red chili pepper and cut garlic sprouts into sections for the finishing touch.  
4. Heat the wok, add a little oil, then add the pork belly strips. Stir-fry over medium heat until the surface is slightly browned and plenty of pork fat is rendered. Remove some of the excess oil.
5. Leave a base of oil and the meat in the wok. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
6. Add the chopped pickled mustard greens. Stir-fry over medium heat for about one minute to fully release the savory, salty aroma.
7. Pour yellow wine and light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly to combine, allowing the wine and soy sauce aromas to bloom.
8. Add enough water, then add the tofu cubes and mushroom strips. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer.
9. After simmering for a while, taste the broth for saltiness and decide whether to add salt. Add MSG and white pepper powder, and drizzle in a little dark soy sauce for color.  
**Note**: Taste before seasoning to avoid over-salting.

10. Continue simmering for another seven to eight minutes until the tofu is fully infused with flavor. Pour the dish into a small stainless steel pot, sprinkle with shredded red chili pepper and garlic sprout sections. Place the pot on a lit alcohol stove and eat while it stays warm.

## Health Advice  
Braised tofu with pickled mustard greens is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber. Tofu is low in fat and easy to digest, making it suitable for those aiming to control weight or maintain a light diet. The pork belly provides a small amount of animal fat, but pickled mustard greens are high in salt, so people with high blood pressure should avoid drinking too much of the broth.
