# Two-Way Mandarin Fish 🐟

**Cuisine:** Cantonese  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Season:** All Year Round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🐟 1 Mandarin fish (remove head, take two fillets, chop bones into strips, slice fillets into thin pieces)  
🫘 Some old tofu (cut into strips)  
🥬 Some baby bok choy (washed and halved)  
🧅 Some scallions (for ginger-scallion juice, stir-frying, and garnish in shreds)  
🧄 Some ginger (for ginger-scallion juice, stir-frying, and shreds)  
🧄 Some garlic (for stir-frying)  
🌶️ Some dried chilies (for stir-frying)  
🌶️ Some red chilies (shredded for garnish)  
🥚 Some egg white (for marinating fish slices)  
🌽 Some cornstarch (for marinating fish slices and bones, and for thickening)  
🫒 Some soybean oil (for marinating, frying, stir-frying, and drizzling)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for ginger-scallion juice, marinating fish bones, blanching, and seasoning sauce)  
🧂 Some light soy sauce (for cooking fish bones and tofu)  
🧂 Some dark soy sauce (for coloring)  
🍶 Some cooking wine (for removing fishy smell)  
🧂 Some steamed fish soy sauce (or light soy sauce) (for drizzling over fish slices)  

## Cookware & Equipment 🍳
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl/Basin  
🥄 Ladle  

## Instructions 📝
1. Remove the head of the mandarin fish. Use a flat knife to slice along the backbone to remove both fillets. Chop the bones into strips. Slice the fillets diagonally into thin pieces.  
   **Key Point:** When slicing the fish, keep the blade flat against the bone, press down, and push forward to cleanly separate the flesh from the bone.

2. Cut the tofu into strips and halve the bok choy. Finely shred the ginger, scallions, and red chilies; set aside.
3. Crush the scallions and ginger, then add water and a pinch of salt. Squeeze to extract the ginger-scallion juice.
4. Mix the fish slices with the ginger-scallion juice until sticky and firm. Add egg white and mix well, then add cornstarch and mix again. Finally, drizzle in a little soybean oil and toss to coat.  
   **Key Point:** Coat the fish in stages, finishing with oil to seal in moisture, ensuring the slices remain tender and smooth.

5. Season the fish bone strips with salt and let marinate for 5 minutes, then coat with cornstarch.
6. In a wok, stir-fry ginger, scallions, garlic, and dried chilies until fragrant. Add water, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and salt. Bring to a boil to create the cooking sauce; set aside.
7. Heat oil in a wok to 170°C (340°F). Fry the tofu strips until lightly golden on the surface, then remove and drain.
8. At the same oil temperature, fry the fish bones until golden brown on the surface, then remove.
9. Place the fried fish head, bones, and tofu into the prepared cooking sauce. Add cooking wine.
10. Cover and braise over medium heat for 5 minutes.
11. Thicken the sauce with cornstarch slurry until glossy. Sprinkle with scallion leaves and red pepper slices for garnish, then transfer to a plate.
12. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil with salt and a little oil. Blanch the bok choy until cooked, then remove and arrange at the bottom of a serving plate.
13. Add a little more salt to the water. Scatter the fish slices in and cook until they change color, then remove immediately.  
    **Key Point:** Use plenty of water and a short cooking time to keep the fish tender; this method is called “dry dragging.”

14. Arrange the fish slices over the bok choy. Top with shredded ginger, scallions, and red chilies. Drizzle with steamed fish soy sauce.
15. Heat oil to 170°C (340°F) and pour it over the shredded aromatics to release their fragrance.  
    **Key Point:** The hot oil must be sizzling enough to fully awaken the aroma of the ginger and scallions.

## Health Tips 💪
Mandarin fish is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with tofu and bok choy, this dish is nutritionally balanced and easy to digest, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those monitoring calorie intake. Be careful to cook the fish slices only until 70% done to avoid overcooking; control the oil temperature when frying the bones to minimize oil absorption.
