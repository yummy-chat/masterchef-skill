# Dry-Fried Beef Rolls
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef rolls: appropriate amount (pre-blanched)
🧅 Onion: appropriate amount (shredded)
🧄 Garlic: appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers: appropriate amount (shredded)
🌿 Cilantro: appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧂 Light soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
🧂 Dark soy sauce: 1 tablespoon
🧂 BBQ seasoning: 2 tablespoons
🫒 Cooking oil: appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Pre-blanch the beef rolls, then drain. Mince the garlic, shred the onion, shred the dried chili peppers, and cut the cilantro into sections.
**Key point**: Blanching the beef rolls helps remove blood foam and gamey smell, preventing excess water from being released during stir-frying.
2. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high heat, add oil, and stir-fry the blanched beef rolls until the surface is slightly charred. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Ensure the beef is fried until slightly charred to achieve the distinctive dry-fried aroma and texture.
3. In the same pan, add a fresh base of oil. Add minced garlic, shredded onion, and shredded dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
4. Return the fried beef rolls to the pan. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and BBQ seasoning. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat until the beef is evenly coated with the seasoning.
5. Just before turning off the heat, add the cilantro sections. Stir-fry quickly, allowing residual heat to warm the cilantro, keeping it green and aromatic.
6. Plate and serve immediately while hot.

## Health Tips
Beef rolls are rich in high-quality protein and iron, but are also high in fat and calories. It is recommended to pair them with plenty of vegetables and control portion sizes per meal. Suitable for those looking to build muscle or replenish iron. Those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
