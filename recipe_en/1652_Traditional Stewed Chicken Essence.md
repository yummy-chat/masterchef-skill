# Traditional Stewed Chicken Essence  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 old free-range chicken (about 3 jin, freshly slaughtered)  
🍚 A small amount of rice (for making sealing rice paste)  
💧 Water, as needed (for mixing the rice paste)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for final seasoning)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍯 Stewing pot with lid  
📄 Oil paper (or high-temperature resistant plastic wrap)  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 2 bowls (1 inverted in the stewing pot to collect the chicken essence, 1 for holding rice and the finished product)  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Steamer  
🕳️ Fine mesh strainer  
🍽️ Stainless steel plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Slaughter and clean the old chicken, then chop into pieces.  
2. Cut the oil paper into a circle slightly larger than the mouth of the stewing pot. Take a small amount of rice, add water, and stir into a thick rice paste.  
3. Place an inverted small bowl in the center of the stewing pot, then arrange the chicken pieces around the bowl.  
**Key point**: The inverted bowl uses the principle of negative pressure to allow the chicken essence to flow into the bowl, preventing the chicken from reabsorbing the broth.  
4. Wipe the rim of the stewing pot dry, evenly apply the rice paste, cover with the oil paper, press to seal tightly, then place the ceramic lid on top.  
**Key point**: Ensure a complete seal to prevent external steam droplets from entering and affecting the purity of the chicken essence. If oil paper is unavailable, high-temperature resistant plastic wrap can be used as a substitute.  
5. Once the steamer is producing steam, place the stewing pot inside and steam over medium-low heat for 4 hours.  
6. Remove the stewing pot, take out the chicken pieces, and uncover the inverted small bowl. The bowl will contain golden chicken essence.  
7. Strain the chicken essence through a fine mesh strainer into a clean bowl, add a pinch of salt to taste, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This traditional stewed chicken essence concentrates the essence of an entire chicken. It is extremely low in calories yet rich in protein and amino acids, making it especially suitable for elderly people needing nourishment, postoperative recovery, and those suffering from physical exhaustion. Ensure thorough straining to prevent any bone fragments from remaining.
