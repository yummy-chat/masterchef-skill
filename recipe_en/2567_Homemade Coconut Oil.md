# Homemade Coconut Oil
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥥 6 coconuts (for coconut meat and coconut water)  
💧 Water, as needed (enough to allow the blender to run smoothly)  
_No seasonings added_

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for collecting coconut water and mixing coconut milk)  
🔪 Cleaver, kitchen knife (for opening coconuts, peeling, and dicing)  
🌀 Blender (for blending coconut milk)  
🧵 Cheesecloth (for straining coconut milk)  
🍳 Wok (for boiling coconut milk)  
🥄 Wooden spatula (for stirring)  
🥢 Fine-mesh strainer (for removing coconut residue and final filtering)  
🫙 Glass airtight jar (for storing coconut oil)  
🔥 Oven/baking sheet (for drying coconut flakes; if no oven, a wok can be used)

## Instructions
1.  Find the softest of the three holes on top of the coconut, pierce it with a chopstick, and pour the coconut water into a bowl.
2.  After draining, strike the coconut with the back of a knife or hit it firmly to break it open, then pry off the coconut meat.
3.  Use a knife to shave off the brown skin on the back of the coconut meat, obtaining white coconut flesh. Clean it, first cut into strips, then into small cubes.
   **Key point**: Remove all the brown skin to prevent affecting the final oil's color and purity.
4.  Place the coconut cubes into a blender, add a small bowl of water, and blend into a smooth, fine white coconut milk with no grains.
5.  Line a bowl with cheesecloth, pour in the coconut milk, gather the cloth, and squeeze firmly to extract the milk, leaving behind moist coconut flakes.
   **Key point**: Squeeze thoroughly to maximize the amount of milk collected.
6.  Pour all the coconut milk into a clean, oil-free wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 80 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
   **Determining doneness**: After about 60 minutes, oil droplets will appear on the surface and the liquid will separate. After about 80 minutes, the solids will turn golden-brown and settle at the bottom.
7.  When the solids are golden-brown and tumbling in the oil, reduce to the lowest heat. Use a fine-mesh strainer to scoop out all the coconut residue, pressing it with a spatula in the strainer to extract any remaining oil.
8.  The oil in the wok will sizzle as it contains water. Continue heating over medium heat until the sizzling sound stops completely and the oil surface is calm, indicating the water has evaporated.
   **Key point**: Ensure all water is evaporated, or the oil will spoil easily.
9.  Turn off the heat. Allow the oil to cool to room temperature or until it is no longer hot to the touch. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer into a dry glass jar, and seal airtight for storage.
10. Spread the moist coconut flakes from step 5 evenly on a baking sheet. Bake at 150°C (300°F) for about 30 minutes, turning them several times. Remove and let cool to obtain dry coconut flakes.
    **Key point**: If no oven is available, dry the flakes in a wok over low heat.

## Health Advice  
Consume in moderation. Homemade coconut oil is rich in medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), making it suitable for ketogenic diets or fitness enthusiasts, but it is high in calories, so use sparingly. Be sure to evaporate all water before storage to prevent spoilage.
