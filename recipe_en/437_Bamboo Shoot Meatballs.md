# Bamboo Shoot Meatballs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🟤 1 Horseshoe bamboo shoot (peeled, sliced, then cut into fine shreds)
🥩 Lean pork, appropriate amount (trimmed from pork leg bones)
🫚 A small piece of ginger (minced)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (use the white part, minced)
🌶️ A small amount of red chili pepper (optional, for garnish)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥄 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste
🌿 Ground white pepper, to taste
🌾 3 teaspoons sweet potato starch

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍲 Steamer (with steaming rack)
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. Peel the horseshoe bamboo shoot. First slice it thinly, then cut into fine shreds. Rinse in clean water and drain well.
   **Key Point**: The finer the bamboo shoot shreds are cut, the better they will blend with the meat filling.

2. Slice the lean pork, then chop with two knives into a fine mince.

3. Add the bamboo shoot shreds, minced scallion whites, minced ginger, salt, steamed fish soy sauce, ground white pepper, and 3 teaspoons of sweet potato starch to the minced meat.
   **Key Point**: Sweet potato starch increases the stickiness of the meat filling and helps it hold its shape.

4. Use your hands to mix everything together well. Repeatedly throw the mixture against the bowl until it becomes firm, bouncy, and forms a cohesive paste.

5. Shape the mixture into evenly sized meatball rounds and place them on a plate.

6. Add enough water to the steamer and bring to a full boil over high heat. Place the plate of meatballs in the steamer. Steam over high heat for about 10 minutes until the meatballs are fully cooked.
   **Note**: Adjust the steaming time according to the size of the meatballs. They are done when the inside is completely cooked through.

## Health Notes
Bamboo shoot and lean pork meatballs are low in fat and high in protein. The bamboo shoot shreds provide dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for spring weight loss and for those with weak spleen and stomach. Be careful not to use too much sweet potato starch, or the final texture will be overly dense and sticky.
