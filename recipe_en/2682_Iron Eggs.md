# Iron Eggs  
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 13 (approx. 450g)  
🍃 Bay leaves 3g  
🪵 Cinnamon stick 10g  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 20g  
🍬 Rock sugar 100g  
🍃 Tea leaves 20g  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 150g  
🥄 Light soy sauce 150g  
💧 Water 1500ml (enough to cover the eggs)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Saucepan  
🍲 Wok or deep pot  
🔥 Induction cooker  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Large bowl (for cooling eggs)  
🍽️ Plate (for air-drying eggs)  
🥄 Wooden spoon or spatula  
💨 Hair dryer or fan (to speed up air-drying)  

## Instructions  
1. Place eggs in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and maintain a gentle simmer for 7 minutes. Control the heat to prevent eggs from cracking due to vigorous boiling.  
2. Remove eggs and immediately immerse them in cold water to cool. When cool enough to handle, gently crack and peel off the shells.  
3. Prepare the marinade: In a pot, add bay leaves, cinnamon stick, star anise, dried tangerine peel, rock sugar, tea leaves, then pour in dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and water. Stir well and bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Note**: The seasonings already contain salt, so no additional salt is needed.  
4. Once the marinade boils, reduce to low heat. Add the peeled eggs, maintaining a gentle simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure even coloring.  
5. Remove the eggs and place them on a plate. Use a hair dryer or fan to dry the surface for about 15 minutes, or let them air-dry in a ventilated area.  
   **Key point**: The eggs must be thoroughly dried after marinating to gradually develop the firm texture of "iron eggs."  
6. Bring the marinade to a boil again, return the dried eggs to the pot, and simmer over low heat for 20 minutes. Remove and dry again for 15 minutes.  
7. Repeat the "20-minute simmer + 15-minute drying" cycle a total of 6 times. The eggs will darken in color and become firmer with each cycle, ultimately achieving a shiny black color and a firm yet elastic texture.  

## Health Advice  
Iron eggs are high in sodium and sugar; it is recommended to consume only 1-2 eggs per serving. Those with high blood pressure or blood sugar concerns should consume with caution. The repeated simmering and drying process tightens the protein structure, but overconsumption may increase digestive burden.
