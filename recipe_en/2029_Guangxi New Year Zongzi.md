# Guangxi New Year Zongzi
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Glutinous rice  
🥜 Lotus seeds  
🥜 Peanuts  
🍤 Dried shrimp  
🥩 Pork belly  
🥚 Salted egg yolk  
🌿 Zongzi leaves  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Large pot (with lid)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🔥 Stove  

## Steps  
1. Stack two or more zongzi leaves, fold them into a funnel shape, and fill in order with a layer of glutinous rice, lotus seeds, peanuts, dried shrimp, and marinated pork belly. If using salted egg yolks, add them at this step as well. Finally, cover with glutinous rice.  
   **Note**: All dry ingredients should be soaked in advance; the filling should be placed in the center to avoid leakage during wrapping.  
2. Wrap the zongzi leaves tightly around the filling to seal completely, and tie firmly with cotton string to ensure the zongzi does not come loose.  
3. Add enough clean water to a large pot, place the wrapped zongzi in, and ensure the water completely submerges them.  
4. Cover and simmer over medium heat for about 4.5 hours.  
   **Key point**: Check the water level periodically, adding hot water as needed to keep the zongzi fully submerged; after cooking, you may turn off the heat and let it rest for a while to make the rice softer and more glutinous.  
5. Use a slotted spoon to remove the zongzi, drain excess water, and transfer to a basin to serve.  

## Health Advice  
Guangxi New Year Zongzi is primarily made with glutinous rice and pork belly, making it high in calories. It is suitable for winter nourishment or when expending significant physical energy. Those with poor digestion should consume it in small amounts. Lotus seeds and peanuts provide dietary fiber, helping to balance the greasiness.
