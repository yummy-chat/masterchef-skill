# Roasted Gluten with Chili Oil and Nut Topping
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

🌾 Gluten Flour (Gluten Flour), as needed (add according to water ratio)
🥜 Crushed Peanuts 150g
🫘 Soybean Flour 50g
🌿 Cumin Seeds (crushed) 50g
🌻 Sunflower Seeds (shelled) 80g
🫗 Toasted Sesame Seeds 100g
🫗 Raw Sesame Seeds 50g
🌶️ Extra-Fine Non-Spicy Coloring Chili Powder 150g
🧂 Salt, as needed (15g for nut topping; 12g per 500g water for gluten dough)
🍬 Granulated Sugar 20g
🧄 Garlic Powder 8g
🍛 Curry Powder 15g
🧂 Flavor Powder (Wei Fen) 10g

🌿 Gromwell Root (Zi Cao) 5g (for coloring chili oil)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the Nut Topping**
    Combine crushed peanuts, soybean flour, cumin seeds, sunflower seeds, 15g salt, granulated sugar, garlic powder, curry powder, flavor powder, chicken flavor essence, toasted sesame seeds, and onion powder. Grind in a grinder until half-crushed, retaining a slight granular texture.

2. **Make the Chili Oil**
   - **Spice Powder**: Mix all spices together and grind into a fine powder. Set aside.
   - **Mixed Sauce**: Mix oyster sauce, chicken juice, spare rib paste, hoisin sauce, chicken bouillon powder, MSG, meat flavor powder, and the thinned sesame peanut butter until well combined.
   - **Fry the Oil**: Add soybean oil, chicken fat, and lard to a pot. Heat until melted. When the oil temperature reaches 130°C, add the aromatics (ginger, scallion, onion, cilantro, garlic). Fry until caramelized. Immediately add the gromwell root, fry for no more than 5 seconds, then remove both the aromatics and gromwell root with a slotted spoon.
   - **Heat oil to 200°C, turn off heat, add ingredients in four batches**:
     - Add raw sesame seeds, stir well;
     - When oil cools to 180°C, add the spice powder, stir well;
     - When oil cools to 160°C, add the extra-fine chili powder, stir well;
     - When oil cools to 100°C, add the mixed sauce, stir well.
   - Pour the chili oil into a container. Store in a cool, dark place for at least 24 hours. Stir thoroughly again before use.

3. **Make the Gluten Dough**
   - Dissolve salt and 1g meat flavor essence in water. Keep the water temperature at 45°C. While stirring the water, gradually add the gluten flour in batches until flocculent. Knead into a dough, squeezing out excess water, leaving only a thin film of water on the surface.
   - Cover and let rest at room temperature for 1 hour. The dough is ready when a cut section appears smooth and fine.
   - Press the rested dough into a flat disc about 1 cm thick. Cut into 2 cm wide strips, then cut into small pieces of about 40g each.
   - Hold one end of a gluten piece with a pair of chopsticks. Stretch the gluten with your right hand while your left hand helps wrap it around the chopsticks. When you reach the center, spread the end piece over the wrapped gluten to seal. Rotate to set the shape, then push from both ends to compact the gluten ball. Remove the chopsticks.

4. **Boil the Gluten Balls**
    Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the wrapped gluten balls. Gently push with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. Boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 15–20 minutes. Longer cooking time results in a firmer texture. Remove immediately and plunge into cold water to cool.

5. **Cut and Skewer**
    Use a gluten slicing machine to cut the cooked and cooled gluten balls into a spiral pattern. Thread a skewer through the cut gluten, twisting the skewer and pushing it down to position the gluten securely.

6. **Coat with Oil and Roast**
   - Dip the skewered gluten strings into the rested chili oil, ensuring the gaps are fully coated.
   - On a gas grill set to medium-low heat, place the gluten skewers. Grill slowly until the surface sizzles and forms a slightly crispy crust. Evenly sprinkle the nut topping over the gluten and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein but also high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad or clear tea to balance the greasiness. Suitable as an occasional indulgent snack, not as a daily meal. Individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should consume in moderation.
