# Litti Chokha
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

🍆 **Ingredients**  
- Whole wheat flour  
- Roasted chickpea flour (can substitute with ground roasted chickpeas)  
- Eggplant  
- Tomato  
- Potato  
- Onion  
- Green chili  
- Ginger  
- Garlic  
- Cilantro  
- Lemon  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Mustard oil (can substitute with rapeseed oil as second choice)  
- Cumin seeds  
- Indian black salt (can substitute with regular salt)  
- Red chili powder  
- Sesame seeds  
- Ghee  
- Salt  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- Oven or open flame  
- Baking tray  
- Large bowl  
- Fork or mashing tool  

---

### Preparation Steps

**Making the Litti Filling**  
- In a large bowl, add 120 grams of roasted chickpea flour, chopped onion, green chili, and ginger.  
- Add 3 grams of cumin seeds, 5 grams of sesame seeds, 3 grams of red chili powder, 3 grams of Indian black salt, and 3 grams of salt.  
- Drizzle in 15 milliliters of mustard oil and 10 milliliters of lemon juice, and sprinkle in chopped cilantro leaves.  
- Stir thoroughly to mix evenly. The filling should be loose but able to hold together when pressed. Set aside.  

**Making the Litti Dough**  
- Place 200 grams of whole wheat flour in a large bowl.  
- Gradually add about 100 milliliters of warm water, kneading as you go, until a smooth and soft dough forms.  
- Let the dough rest for 15 minutes.  
- Divide the dough into 8 equal portions.  
- Press each portion into a round disc about 8 centimeters in diameter, slightly thicker in the center.  
- Place about 15 grams of filling in the center of each disc.  
- Gather the edges to encase the filling, pinch the seal tightly, and roll into a ball.  

**Baking the Litti**  
- Preheat the oven to 200°C.  
- Arrange the littis on a baking tray and bake for 25–30 minutes, flipping once, until the surface is golden brown and cracked.  
- If using an open flame, place the littis directly over charcoal or a low gas flame, turning continuously, until evenly charred.  
- Once baked, immediately brush each litti with ghee while hot to allow it to soak in.  

**Making the Chokha**  
- Roast the eggplant, tomato, and potato directly over an open flame or in a 200°C oven for 20–25 minutes, until the skin is charred and the inside is fully soft.  
- After roasting, peel off the skin and mash the eggplant, tomato, and potato separately with a fork or by hand.  
- Combine the mashed vegetables together.  
- Add chopped garlic, green chili, 4 grams of salt, and 15 milliliters of mustard oil.  
- Stir thoroughly to mix evenly.  
- Garnish with cilantro leaves.  

**Serving**  
- Arrange the littis on a plate alongside the chokha vegetable mash.  
- Serve hot. Break off pieces of litti with your hands and dip into the chokha.  

---

💡 **Key Notes**  
- If roasted chickpea flour is hard to find, make it at home: dry-roast chickpeas until deep brown, then grind into a powder.  
- Mustard oil is the soul seasoning; it is not recommended to replace it. The second choice is rapeseed oil.  
- The dough must rest for 15 minutes to ensure softness.  
- Roast the vegetables until the skin is charred and the inside is fully soft to enhance the smoky flavor.  
- For best flavor, serve immediately while hot.  

## Health Advice
This Litti Chokha is rich in whole wheat, chickpeas, and vegetables, providing ample dietary fiber and protein, making it suitable for fitness or blood sugar management. Although the smoky flavor from roasted vegetables is aromatic, avoid excessive charring to prevent the formation of harmful substances. Best enjoyed hot for optimal taste.
