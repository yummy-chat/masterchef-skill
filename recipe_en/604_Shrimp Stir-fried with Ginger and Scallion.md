# Shrimp Stir-fried with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp 400g  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections, separate white part and green part)  
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt a pinch  
🦪 Oyster sauce a little  
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount  
🧈 Butter a small piece  
🍯 Soy sauce a few drops  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Plate/bowl  

## Steps  
1. Cut off the shrimp whiskers, slit the back, and remove the vein.  
**Key point**: Opening the back allows the shrimp to absorb flavor more deeply.  

2. Mince the garlic, slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, and separate the white part from the green part for later use.  
**Key point**: The white and green parts of the scallion have different heat tolerance and need to be added at different stages.  

3. Toss the prepared shrimp in cornstarch to coat evenly with a thin layer.  
**Key point**: Cornstarch creates a crispy shell when fried at high heat, sealing in the shrimp's moisture and keeping it tender.  

4. Pour enough cooking oil into the pan and heat until it lightly smokes. Add the coated shrimp and fry quickly for 10–20 seconds until both sides are golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.  
**Note**: Must use high oil temperature for quick frying; low oil temperature will cause the shrimp to lose moisture and become dry and tough.  

5. Leave a little oil in the pan, add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the ginger slices and stir-fry until the garlic turns slightly yellow and the edges of the ginger slices curl.  
6. Add a small piece of butter and let it melt. Pour the cooking wine along the edge of the pan. After the alcohol evaporates, add an appropriate amount of water, season with salt and oyster sauce, and bring to a boil.  
**Key point**: Pour the cooking wine along the edge of the pan to fully release its aroma.  

7. Add the fried shrimp and the white parts of the scallion to the pan, and stir-fry evenly.  
8. Drizzle in a few drops of soy sauce, sprinkle in the green parts of the scallion, and stir-fry quickly over high heat for a few moments until the green parts are just cooked. Remove from the pan and plate.  
**Key point**: Add soy sauce last to enhance color; add the green parts of the scallion last to keep them bright green and crisp, avoiding overcooking.  

## Health Advice  
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with ginger and scallion, it helps dispel cold and warm the stomach, making it suitable for autumn and winter nourishment or fitness meals. Note that the frying time should be short to prevent the meat from becoming tough; for a lighter option, reduce the butter by half.
