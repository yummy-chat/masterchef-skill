# Fried Peanuts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Dried raw peanuts, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (enough to submerge the peanuts)
🧂 Salt, as needed (optional, for making salt and pepper flavor)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Strainer
🥢 Wooden chopsticks
🍽️ Plate
🥣 Bowl
🔥 Portable gas stove (or other heat source)

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour enough cooking oil into the wok, and add the dried raw peanuts to cold or warm oil.
   **Key Point**: Control the oil temperature to around 100°C (212°F) to ensure even heating of the peanuts. The peanuts must be dry; no need to wash them.
2. Maintain low to medium heat for slow frying, stirring occasionally, for about 3–4 minutes.
   **Determining Doneness**: The peanut skin changes from light red to deep red, and the bubbles in the oil become less dense.
   **Note**: This step aims to evaporate the internal moisture of the peanuts. Do not rush or use high heat.
3. When the peanuts turn a darker color and the oil bubbles decrease, immediately remove them with a slotted spoon and spread them out on the strainer or a plate to cool.
   **Key Point**: Spread them out immediately to prevent the peanuts from continuing to cook with residual heat and becoming burnt.
4. Continue heating the oil in the wok to about 150°C (302°F) using high heat.
5. Return the previously fried peanuts to the hot oil for a second frying, for a very short time, about 10 seconds.
   **Key Point**: This is the core step for making the peanuts crispy. Frying too long will burn them.
6. Immediately remove the peanuts and spread them out again to cool.
   **Determining Doneness**: At this point, the peanuts should be a beautiful jujube red color.
7. **[Optional]** If making a salt and pepper flavor, sprinkle an appropriate amount of salt over the peanuts while they are still slightly warm, and toss to combine evenly.
   **Key Point**: Use the residual heat to help the salt adhere to the peanuts. The peanuts will become crispy only after they have completely cooled; they are not crispy while hot.

## Health Advice
Fried peanuts are rich in unsaturated fatty acids and protein, but due to high-temperature frying, they are high in calories. It is recommended to eat a small handful (about 20 grams) at a time, suitable as an accompaniment to cold dishes or as a snack with drinks. Pay attention to controlling the oil temperature and heat to avoid producing excessive acrylamide.
