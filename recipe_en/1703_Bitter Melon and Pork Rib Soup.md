# Bitter Melon and Pork Rib Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦴 Pork spare ribs (cut into small fist-sized pieces)
🥒 Bitter melon 2
🫘 Soybeans 200g (soak in advance for 2 hours)
🫚 Ginger 2 pieces (smashed)
🧅 Scallions (tied into knots)
🥬 Chaoshan pickled mustard greens (Dongcai) about 30g
🍶 Cooking wine (Huangjiu)
🍚 White vinegar about 8g
🧂 Salt (for treating the bitter melon, not added directly to the soup)
💧 Water (for blanching and stewing)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Clay pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board

## Instructions
1. Soak the soybeans in water for 2 hours. Cut the spare ribs into small fist-sized pieces. Cut the bitter melon in half, remove the seeds and pith, and slice crosswise into large chunks. Smash the ginger and tie the scallions into knots.
2. Add water to a pot, then add the ribs, smashed ginger, and scallion knots. Place the pot over high heat with cold water and add some cooking wine. Bring to a boil.
* Starting with cold water helps draw out blood and impurities, reducing any gamey smell.

3. Once the water boils, skim off any foam. Add the white vinegar and continue boiling for 3 minutes.
* The white vinegar reduces greasiness and helps tenderize the meat.

4. Remove the ribs, rinse them clean with water, and transfer them to a clay pot.
5. Add the soaked soybeans, smashed ginger, and scallion knots. Pour in boiling hot water and cook on high heat for 5 minutes.
* Add a little more white vinegar midway. Once it returns to a boil, immediately skim off any foam, then remove the scallion knots to prevent them from overcooking and clouding the soup.

6. Meanwhile, prepare the bitter melon: Place the bitter melon pieces in a bowl, add a small handful of salt, rub them, and soak in water for 40 minutes to reduce bitterness.
7. Add the soaked bitter melon pieces one by one to the boiling soup pot.
8. Add about 30g of Chaoshan pickled mustard greens and bring back to a boil over high heat. Skim off any new foam, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 20 minutes until the ingredients are tender.
* The pickled mustard greens are naturally salty and savory, so no additional salt is needed to avoid over-salting.

## Health Advice
Bitter melon and spare rib soup helps clear heat and relieve summer heat. The soybeans provide calcium. It suits those prone to internal heat or needing extra protein during summer. The bitterness of the melon is greatly reduced after salting, but those with weak spleen and stomach should not drink too much.
