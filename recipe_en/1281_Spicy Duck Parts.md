# Spicy Duck Parts  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients  
🥩 Duck necks, duck frames, duck feet (about 4 jin total)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (some for stir-frying, some added during braising for extra heat)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, to taste  
🫘 Cardamom, to taste  
⭐ Star anise, to taste  
🌿 Baizhi (Angelica dahurica), to taste  
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste  
🌾 Lemongrass, to taste  
🍒 Hawthorn slices, to taste  

## Seasonings  
🥘 Broad bean paste (doubanjiang) 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Light soy sauce 1 bowl  
🥄 Dark soy sauce half a bowl  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little  
🫙 Sweet bean paste (or yellow bean paste) 3 tablespoons  
🍬 Rock sugar, about a dozen pieces  
🍺 Beer half a can  
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Stainless steel stockpot (for blanching, making braising liquid, and braising)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying the base)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking spices)  
🥄 Strainer (for filtering spice water)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam and scooping)  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board (for cutting)  

## Instructions  
1. Place duck necks, duck frames, and duck feet in cold water, add ginger slices, and soak for 1 hour to thoroughly remove blood.  
   **Key point**: A long soak effectively reduces gamey odor.  

2. Place Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, cardamom, star anise, baizhi, bay leaves, lemongrass, dried chili peppers, and hawthorn slices in a bowl. Soak in hot water for 5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Pre-soaking releases aroma and prevents burning during later oil frying.  

3. Discard the soaking water. Put the duck parts back into the pot, add scallion sections, ginger slices, cooking wine, and fresh water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then boil for 3 more minutes. Remove and rinse clean with water.  
   **Key point**: Blanch thoroughly to remove impurities and strong odors.  

4. Heat oil in a wok. Add the drained, soaked spices and stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Add 1 tablespoon of broad bean paste and stir-fry until red oil is released.  
   **Key point**: Do not overheat, to avoid burning the spices and bean paste, which would cause bitterness.  

5. Pour the stir-fried base into a large pot of hot water. Bring to a boil, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sweet bean paste, rock sugar, ginger slices, beer, and salt. Stir well.  
   **Key point**: All seasonings should be added before the duck parts. Taste and adjust saltiness after boiling.  

6. Continue to boil the braising liquid for about 10 minutes to meld the flavors. Add extra Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili peppers according to personal taste to increase spiciness.  

7. Add the blanched duck parts. The braising liquid should just cover the ingredients. Cover with a lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 30 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Cook the duck parts until done; do not overcook. The subsequent soaking is key for flavor infusion.  

8. Turn off the heat. Use a slotted spoon to gently press the duck parts into the braising liquid, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover with the lid and let soak for 8 hours or longer.  
   **Key point**: A long soak makes the meat firm and allows the flavor to penetrate deeply, far more effective than prolonged boiling.  

9. After soaking, reheat to melt any solidified braising liquid. Set aside a portion of the old liquid for preservation if desired. Leave some liquid and the duck parts in the pot. Increase heat to reduce the sauce, stirring evenly so the duck parts are coated with a thick, glossy sauce.  
   **Key point**: Letting it cool or refrigerating after reducing the sauce enhances flavor—spicy, aromatic, with firm and chewy meat.  

## Health Advice  
This spicy duck dish is rich in protein but high in sodium, making it suitable for gatherings. Those with high blood pressure should moderate consumption and drink plain water. Soaking for 8 hours allows the duck parts to be fully flavored, with firm and chewy texture.
