# Chive, Tofu, and Vermicelli Vegetarian Filling  
Cuisine: Henan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Chives, as needed (washed and chopped)  
🧈 Tofu, as needed (cut into small cubes)  
🍜 Vermicelli, as needed (soaked until soft and cut into segments)  
🧅 Scallion, 50g  
🫚 Ginger slices, 20g  
🌿 Cumin seeds, 8g  
🍃 Bay leaves, 3g  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, 5g  
🌰 Black cardamom, 2 pieces  
⭐ Star anise, 6g  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, 5g  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for making seasoned oil)  
🫗 Seasoned oil, 80g per 500g of filling (homemade, see steps)  
🧂 Salt, 8g (for approximately 500g–1kg of filling)  
🧂 Chicken powder, 3g  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 15g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🫙 Transparent lid  
🥣 Large bowl or basin  
🥄 Ladle (for stirring)  

## Steps  
1. **Make the seasoned oil**: Place a wok over heat and pour in cold vegetable oil. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, cumin seeds, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, black cardamom, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Heat slowly over low heat, stirring the bottom constantly to prevent sticking.  
**Key point**: Turn off the heat immediately when the scallion and ginger turn golden and the aroma is released. Cover with a lid and let it cool naturally. The residual heat will fully release the flavors of the spices while preventing the oil from becoming too hot and turning bitter. Once cooled, strain out the solids to obtain a clear seasoned oil.  

2. **Prepare the vegetable filling**: Chop the chives, cut the tofu into small cubes, soak the vermicelli until soft and cut into short segments. Place everything together in a large bowl.  
**Key point**: None  

3. **Lock in moisture and mix**: Following the ratio of 80g seasoned oil per 500g of filling, first pour the seasoned oil over the vegetable mixture and stir thoroughly to combine.  
**Key point**: The oil must be added first to coat the cut surfaces of the vegetables, forming a protective layer that locks in moisture and prevents water release and discoloration.  

4. **Season**: Add salt, chicken powder, and oyster sauce. Gently mix until just combined.  
**Note**: Do not add dark soy sauce or other dark-colored seasonings to this vegetarian filling, as they will affect the bright color and clean taste of the filling.  

## Health Tips  
Chives, tofu, and vermicelli form a classic vegetarian filling combination that is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for weight loss or vegetarian diets. Note that chives tend to release water easily; adding oil first before seasoning effectively locks in moisture.
