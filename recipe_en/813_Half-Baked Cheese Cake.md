# Half-Baked Cheese Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Egg white 72g
🥚 Egg yolk 30g
🍬 Sugar 10g (for egg yolk mixture)
🌽 Cornstarch 5g
🧀 Cream cheese 150g
🥛 Milk 100g
🥛 Heavy cream 25g
🧈 Butter 22g
🍬 Sugar 30g (for meringue, added in three batches)

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing ingredients and whipping egg whites)
🥄 Hand whisk (for stirring egg yolk mixture and mixing batter)
⚡ Electric hand mixer (for whipping meringue)
🔪 Spatula (for folding and cutting)
🍲 Small thick-bottomed pot (for cooking milk mixture)
🍰 Oval metal mold (for assembling half-baked cheese cake)
📜 Parchment paper (for lining the mold to prevent sticking)
🫖 Piping bag (for piping cheese batter)
🔥 Oven
🍳 Baking pan (for water bath baking)
🕸️ Sieve (for straining cheese batter)

## Instructions
1.  Separate the eggs, placing yolks and whites into separate containers. Place the bowl of egg whites in the freezer until a thin layer of ice forms around the edges.
2.  Add 10g sugar and 5g cornstarch to the egg yolks. Whisk with a hand whisk until smooth, fine, and no lumps remain.
3.  Soften the cream cheese over hot water, stirring with a spatula until smooth and lump-free.
4.  In a small thick-bottomed pot, add the milk, heavy cream, and butter. Heat over low heat until the butter melts and the mixture is well combined. Increase heat to medium and heat until small bubbles appear around the edges, then remove from heat. Slowly pour the hot milk mixture in small amounts into the egg yolk mixture, whisking quickly as you pour to prevent the yolks from curdling. Pour the mixture back into the pot. Cook over low heat, stirring continuously. As soon as the liquid begins to thicken, immediately remove from heat and use residual heat to stir until smooth.
5.  Pour the cooked custard (pastry cream) into the softened cream cheese and mix until well combined and smooth.
6.  Strain the cheese batter once through a sieve. Cover it directly with plastic wrap (touching the surface) to prevent a skin from forming.
7.  Take out the frozen egg whites. Whip with an electric hand mixer on high speed. When large bubbles (fish-eye bubbles) appear, add 1/3 of the sugar. Continue whipping on high speed until the foam becomes fine, then add another 1/3 of the sugar. Reduce speed to medium. When soft peaks start to form, add the remaining sugar. Reduce speed to low and whip the meringue until it reaches a soft peak stage, displaying a state between a large drooping hook and a small drooping hook.
8.  Add 1/3 of the meringue to the cheese batter. Use a spatula to roughly fold about 20 times. Briefly whisk the remaining meringue with the hand whisk to restore its smoothness. Pour all the pre-mixed cheese batter back into the meringue. Using a spatula, gently cut and fold from the center outwards for about 20-30 times until evenly combined with no visible clumps of meringue.
9.  Line the inner walls of the mold with parchment paper. Place an approximately 0.5cm thick slice of chiffon cake or old-fashioned sponge cake at the bottom.
10. Transfer the cheese batter into a piping bag. Pipe it into the mold until about 80-90% full.
11. Place the mold(s) into a baking pan. Pour room temperature water into the baking pan to create a water bath. After preheating the oven, bake at 200°C top heat / 100°C bottom heat for 10-15 minutes, then let it rest in the turned-off oven (with residual heat) for 10 minutes. If your oven cannot control top and bottom heat independently, a reference plan is: bake at 150°C for 20 minutes, then raise the temperature to 180°C and bake for another 5 minutes to brown the top. Check after 10 minutes of baking. Once the surface turns golden brown, remove from the oven. The cake body should jiggle slightly when shaken.
12. Allow to cool slightly after baking before unmolding. It is highly recommended to refrigerate overnight before eating for a more cohesive flavor and a more moist, tender texture.

## Health Advice
This half-baked cheese cake is rich in protein and calcium, suitable for people needing an energy boost. However, cream cheese and butter are high in calories, so it's best to enjoy it in moderation, especially after exercise. The taste improves after refrigeration, reducing any greasiness.
