# Five-Nut and Jinhua Ham Mooncake 🥮
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Ingredients
🥩 Jinhua ham shreds 100g (remove tough tendons, beat until fluffy like velvet)
🫘 Low-sugar white lotus seed paste 200g (acts as a binder)
🥓 Pork fat 200g (use only the fatty part, cut into very small dice)
🌰 Watermelon seeds 20g (preferably pre-roasted)
🌰 Olive kernels 20g (preferably pre-roasted)
🌰 Walnuts 20g (preferably pre-roasted)
🌰 Apricot kernels (bitter almonds) 20g (can partially substitute with pine nuts)
🌰 White sesame seeds 20g (preferably pre-roasted)
🍬 Candied winter melon 10g (cut into very fine dice)
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 5g (soak in water, scrape off the inner pith, then chop finely)

### Seasonings
🧂 Salt 2g (for curing the lard)
🍬 Fine sugar / caster sugar 100g (for curing the lard)
🍶 High-proof baijiu 4g (for curing the lard, can substitute with rose wine)
🧂 Soy sauce 5g (for mixing the filling, can use light soy sauce + dark soy sauce)
🌿 Five-spice powder 2g (for mixing the filling)
🍶 Rose wine 10g (for mixing the filling, can substitute with Shanxi Fenjiu)
🍯 Golden syrup 130g (for making the dough)
🧪 Alkaline water 6g (for making the dough)
🛢️ Peanut oil 66g (for making the dough)
🌾 Cake flour 200g (for making the dough)
🥚 Egg yolk wash, as needed (egg yolk to water ratio 1:0.5, for brushing the surface)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowls (for mixing filling and dough)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for precise measurements)
🥄 Whisk (for emulsifying dough liquids)
🔪 Knife and cutting board (for preparing ingredients)
♨️ Steamer (for steaming the cured lard)
📦 Airtight bag or container (for oil recovery and storage)
🔥 Oven (for baking mooncakes)
📜 Parchment paper (non-stick)
🖐️ Hand whisk (for emulsifying dough liquids)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1.  **Prepare the cured lard (bingrou)**
    Cut the pork fat into very small dice. Add salt, fine sugar, and high-proof baijiu. Mix well and let marinate at room temperature for 1 day. After marinating, place in a steamer. Once the water is boiling, steam for 10 minutes. Strain off the liquid and set aside.
**Key point**: The smaller the lard dice are cut, the finer the texture; it should become translucent after steaming.

2.  **Prepare other filling ingredients**
    - Remove tough tendons from the Jinhua ham shreds. Use a whisk or mixer paddle attachment to beat them until they are fluffy like velvet.
- Soak the dried tangerine peel in water for 5 minutes, scrape off the inner pith, and chop finely.
- Cut the candied winter melon into very fine dice.
- Have all nuts ready, preferably using pre-roasted ones or roasting them in advance.
### Production Stage
3.  **Make the five-nut and Jinhua ham filling**
    In a large bowl, mix the soy sauce, rose wine, and five-spice powder until well combined. Add the watermelon seeds, olive kernels, walnuts, apricot kernels, white sesame seeds, chopped candied winter melon, and chopped tangerine peel. Mix well. Then add the steamed and drained cured lard and lotus seed paste. Mix thoroughly with your hands. Finally, add the fluffy Jinhua ham shreds. Mix and squeeze until the filling has a soft but firm consistency and can form clumps.
**Key point**: No additional sugar or oil is added to the filling; sweetness and oiliness come from the lotus seed paste, cured lard, and candied winter melon; the final mixture should be able to hold together in a ball.

4.  **Make the dough**
    In a bowl, combine the golden syrup, alkaline water, and peanut oil. Use a hand whisk to stir until fully combined and emulsified. Sift in the cake flour. Fold with a silicone spatula until no dry flour remains. Do not knead to avoid developing gluten. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 2 hours.
**Key point**: Using cake flour improves the melt-in-the-mouth texture; avoid overworking the dough to prevent gluten formation.

5.  **Assemble and shape the mooncakes**
    - **Large mooncake**: Roll each of the two 125g dough portions into a round sheet slightly larger than the mold. Place 400g of filling on one sheet and shape it slightly. Cover with the second sheet of dough, and pinch the edges tightly to seal. Place into the mold, press firmly, and unmold.
**Key point**: Adjust the size of the raw mooncake based on your mold's actual capacity; in the video, a mold capacity of 650g was found to be optimal for the large mooncake. If the shape is unsatisfactory after unmolding, you can flip it and press again.

6.  **Bake**
    Preheat the oven. Lightly spray the surface of the raw mooncakes with water. For the large mooncake, bake at 200°C for 5 minutes to set the shape. Remove, brush with two layers of egg yolk wash, then reduce the temperature to 170°C and bake for 15-20 minutes, until the desired color is achieved.
**Key point**: Brush the egg yolk wash thinly and evenly; baking time and temperature may need adjustment based on your oven.

7.  **Oil recovery and storage**
    After baking, place the mooncakes on a wire rack to cool completely. Immediately transfer them to an airtight bag or container and let them rest at room temperature for 1-3 days for the oil recovery process.
**Key point**: Oil recovery is a crucial process where the crust absorbs moisture and oil from the filling, becoming soft and allowing the flavors to meld; the crust will be hard and dry when first out of the oven, becoming oily and soft only after this period.

## Health Advice
This mooncake is extremely high in calories. It is suitable for festive, small-portioned tasting and should not be consumed as a staple food. It is recommended to pair it with strong tea to cut the richness, and to be mindful of your overall fat and sugar intake for the day.
