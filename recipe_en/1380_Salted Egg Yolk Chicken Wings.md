# Salted Egg Yolk Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken wings (drumettes and flats) 500g  
🥚 Salted egg yolks about 5 pieces (recommend using whole salted duck eggs, taking only the yolks)  
🌾 Cake flour about 2/3 of total flour mixture weight  
🌽 Cornstarch about 1/3 of total flour mixture weight  
🧂 Baking soda or baking powder 2 pinches (for crispier coating, optional)  
🧄 Garlic 3 cloves (sliced)  
🍬 Sugar 2 tablespoons  
🍶 Vinegar half tablespoon  
🌶️ White pepper powder half tablespoon  
🧂 Salt 2 pinches  
🌶️ Chili powder 2 tablespoons (optional, adjust to taste)  
🧄 Garlic, scallion, and ginger powder half tablespoon  
🥛 Milk or water enough to submerge ingredients  
⚫ Black pepper to taste (optional)  
🧈 Butter as needed (for stir-frying salted egg yolk sauce)  
🧀 Cheese powder to taste (optional, for final seasoning)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥘 Deep sauté pan or deep fryer  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoons  
🧲 Strainer/skimmer  
🥢 Wooden chopsticks/spatula  
♨️ Air fryer (alternative method)  
📤 Stainless steel tray  
🥛 Glass cup/small bowl  

## Preparation Steps

### Prep Phase
1. **Prepare chicken wings**: Rinse and soak chicken wings in clean water repeatedly. Focus on squeezing the bone gaps to remove blood, until no significant blood can be pressed out.
**Key point**: Thoroughly squeezing out blood is crucial to remove any fishy smell. Those sensitive to odor must handle this step carefully.

2. **Marinate chicken wings**: Drain the prepared chicken wings and place in a bowl. Add sugar, vinegar, white pepper powder, sliced garlic, salt, chili powder, and garlic/scallion/ginger powder. Mix well, then pour in milk or water until the liquid reaches the same level as the ingredients. Mix again. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
**Key point**: Do not score the chicken wings to keep them juicy. The liquid must fully submerge the chicken wings.

3. **Prepare salted egg yolks**: Place whole salted duck eggs in cold water and boil until cooked. After cooking, remove the shells and take out the yolks. Set aside.
**Key point**: Use whole salted duck eggs for better flavor and to avoid poor-quality substitutes.

4. **Prepare coating flour**: In a tray, mix cake flour and cornstarch. Add baking soda or baking powder and black pepper to taste. Mix well by hand.
**Key point**: The ratio is more important than exact gram measurements. Once mixed evenly, it forms a versatile frying flour.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Coat chicken wings**: Remove chicken wings from the marinade, drain excess liquid, and place in the flour mixture. Press firmly with your hands to ensure the flour adheres tightly to the chicken wings, then shake off excess flour. Let the coated wings rest until the surface becomes slightly moist from internal moisture, then repeat the coating process once.
**Visual cue**: After coating, the surface should be dry and flaky (scaly texture).
   **Key point**: Press firmly for a secure coating. Letting the wings rest and re-coating produces a crispier crust.

2. **Cook chicken wings**:
   - **Deep frying method**: Pour enough oil into a pot and heat over high heat. Test the oil temperature with a piece of leftover coating batter—if it floats immediately, the oil is ready. Reduce to medium heat or maintain a suitable flame. Add the chicken wings and fry for about 5–7 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove and drain oil.
**Key point**: Ensure the chicken wings are dry before adding to the oil to prevent splattering. Let them set in the oil before stirring.
   - **Pan-frying method**: Add slightly more oil than usual for stir-frying to a non-stick pan. Heat over high heat, then reduce to low. Add the chicken wings and fry for about 1.5 minutes until the bottom is golden. Flip and continue frying for about 14 minutes total until fully cooked and golden.
**Key point**: Use low heat to prevent burning the outside while the inside remains raw. Flip frequently to prevent burnt crumbs from sticking.
   - **Air fryer method**: Place in the air fryer and cook at 180°C for 15 minutes, spraying with oil and flipping halfway through.
3. **Prepare salted egg yolk sauce and combine**: In a non-stick pan, melt a small amount of butter over low heat. Add the cooked salted egg yolks and stir-fry slowly over low heat, stirring continuously, until the yolks are fully melted and become a fluffy, sandy foam. Quickly add the fried/pan-fried chicken wings and toss rapidly to coat evenly with the sauce.
**Visual cue**: The yolks should be fully melted and form a foamy texture.
   **Key point**: Use low heat throughout to prevent the yolks from turning black. Ensure the chicken wings are fully coated with the sauce.

## Health Tips
It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad to balance the cholesterol load from high protein and fat. Salted egg yolks are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
