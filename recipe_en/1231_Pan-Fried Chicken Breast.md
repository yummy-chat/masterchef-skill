# Pan-Fried Chicken Breast

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 8 chicken breasts (fresh, sliced in half horizontally)  
🫚 Ginger, julienned, to taste  
🧅 Scallion segments, to taste  
🍋 1 lemon (juiced)  
🌽 30g cornstarch  
🌱 80g tapioca starch  
🍞 Breadcrumbs, to taste (optional, for extra crispiness)  
🧂 1/2 tablespoon salt  
🌶️ Spicy and numbing seasoning, to taste (optional spice blend)  
🧂 Ground pepper, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🧂 Baking soda, to taste  
🥄 6 tablespoons light soy sauce  
💧 300ml water  
🌽 Corn oil, to taste (to seal in moisture)  
🍳 Cooking oil, to taste (for pan-frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl, stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🧤 Food-grade gloves  
🪵 Large wooden basin (for coating with breadcrumbs)  
🍳 Electric griddle or non-stick pan  
🥢 Food tongs  
🍴 Fork  

## Steps  
1. Slice each chicken breast horizontally in half. Gently pound both sides with the back of a knife to loosen the meat.  
   **Note**: Use fresh chicken breasts; do not use frozen ones.  
2. Place the prepared chicken in a container. Add julienned ginger and scallion segments. Squeeze in the lemon juice and mix thoroughly. Marinate for 10 minutes to remove any odor.  
3. Prepare the wet marinade: In a bowl, combine 1/2 tablespoon salt, spicy and numbing seasoning, ground pepper, sugar, baking soda, 6 tablespoons light soy sauce, and 300ml water. Stir until well mixed.  
4. Pour the wet marinade over the chicken. Mix well, then refrigerate for 3 hours to allow the chicken to fully absorb the liquid.  
5. Remove the marinated chicken and discard the ginger and scallions. First, add 30g cornstarch and mix well. Then add 80g tapioca starch and mix thoroughly.  
6. Pour in an appropriate amount of corn oil and mix well to seal in moisture. Marinate for another 3 hours. At this point, the chicken is ready and can be frozen for later use.  
7. For a crispy texture, coat the marinated chicken breast in breadcrumbs. Preheat an electric griddle or non-stick pan and add cooking oil.  
8. Place the chicken in the pan and cook over medium heat. Cook for about 2 minutes until the bottom is set, then flip. Cook for 5-6 minutes total until both sides are golden brown and the center is cooked through.  
9. Remove from the pan and serve. The chicken is crispy on the outside, tender and juicy on the inside.  

## Health Advice  
This pan-fried chicken breast uses lemon and light soy sauce to eliminate any gamey flavor. Marinating with baking soda makes the chicken breast exceptionally tender, making it suitable for protein supplementation during weight loss. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad to balance the calories. Control the amount of oil during frying to prevent the outer coating from absorbing too much oil. Be careful not to use too much baking soda, as it can affect the texture.
