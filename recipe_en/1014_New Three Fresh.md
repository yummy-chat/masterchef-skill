# New Three Fresh
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Cabbage 450g, finely shredded
🌶️ Thin-skinned small peppers 80g, sliced diagonally
🍅 Tomatoes 350g, sliced
🧄 Garlic 20g, sliced
🧂 Sugar 3g
🧂 Salt 2g
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g
🧂 MSG 3g
🐷 Lard or vegetable oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the wok over high heat and add lard, heating until the oil surface begins to smoke slightly.
**Note**: The oil temperature must be high enough to facilitate the subsequent aromatics.

2. Add the sliced garlic and stir-fry quickly until the aroma is released.
**Note**: Work quickly to prevent the garlic from burning.

3. Add the tomato slices, then add the sugar and salt. Stir-fry until the tomatoes soften, release their juice, and form a paste.
**Note**: If the heat is too high, adjust to medium heat to prevent the liquid from drying up; adding salt helps the tomatoes release juice faster.

4. Once the tomatoes have turned into a sauce, drizzle the light soy sauce and oyster sauce along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry evenly.

5. Add the shredded cabbage and pepper slices, turn the heat to the highest setting, and stir-fry quickly until evenly combined.

6. Control the cooking time according to personal preference, until the cabbage leaves become soft but still retain some crunch. Add the MSG, stir-fry evenly, and then remove from the wok.
**Note**: No additional salt is needed for this dish; the cabbage will release water during stir-frying, so there is no need to worry about scorching.

## Health Advice
This dish features a low-fat combination of cabbage, tomatoes, and peppers. It is rich in vitamin C and lycopene, making it suitable for those managing their weight or needing to boost immunity. Lard is high in calories, so vegetable oil can be used as a substitute to reduce fat intake.
