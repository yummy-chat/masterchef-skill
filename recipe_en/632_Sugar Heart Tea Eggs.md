# Sugar Heart Tea Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Raw-edible eggs, as needed  
🍃 Tea leaves, as needed (for spice pouch)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (for spice pouch)  
🌿 Fennel, as needed (for spice pouch)  
🍂 Bay leaf, one piece (for spice pouch)  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, as needed (for spice pouch)  
⭐ Star anise, as needed (for spice pouch)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, one (for spice pouch)  
🍵 Matcha powder, as needed (for final garnish)  
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed (for caramelizing)  

🧂 Light soy sauce, 50g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 20g  
🧂 MSG, one spoon  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, one spoon  
🍶 Huadiao wine, as needed  
🥣 Chicken broth, as needed  
💧 Water, as needed (for caramelizing)  
🧂 Salt, not explicitly shown (possibly for boiling eggs or seasoning)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥘 Glass pot or soup pot  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🍵 Tea whisk or fine sieve (for sifting matcha powder)  
📦 Airtight container with lid  
❄️ Refrigerator (for cold marinating)  

## Instructions  
1. Prepare the spice pouch: Mix Sichuan peppercorns, fennel, bay leaf, cinnamon, star anise, bird's eye chili, and tea leaves. Place them into a spice bag and set aside.  
2. Caramelize the sugar: Place rock sugar in a wok. Cook over medium-low heat until it turns a jujube red color. Slowly pour water along the edge of the wok, stir to combine, then transfer to a glass bowl to create the caramelized sugar water.  
   **Key point**: Control the heat while caramelizing to avoid burning and bitterness; be careful to prevent oil splatter when adding water.  
3. Prepare the marinade: Mix the caramelized sugar water with chicken broth, Huadiao wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, MSG, and chicken bouillon. Stir well to combine.  
4. Simmer the marinade: Add the spice pouch to the marinade. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer until the spice flavors are fully released.  
   **Note**: Allow sufficient simmering time to ensure the spice essence infuses into the marinade.  
5. Cook the soft-boiled eggs: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Gently add the raw-edible eggs and cook for exactly 5 minutes and 15 seconds.  
   **Key point**: Adjust the cooking time slightly based on the stove's heat and egg size; use only raw-edible grade eggs.  
6. Cool and peel: Immediately remove the cooked eggs and submerge them in ice water to cool. Once completely cooled, gently peel the shells, taking care to keep the egg whites intact.  
7. Marinate for flavor: Place the peeled soft-boiled eggs into the cooled marinade, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate overnight to marinate.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the marinade has cooled completely before adding the eggs to prevent further cooking that could affect the runny yolk; allow sufficient marinating time for thorough flavor absorption.  
8. Plate and garnish: Remove the marinated tea eggs, cut them in half, and use a tea whisk or fine sieve to evenly sift matcha powder over the cut surfaces. Serve.  

## Health Advice  
The marinade for tea eggs has a high salt content. People with high blood pressure or edema are advised to consume only the egg whites and avoid drinking the marinade. Soft-boiled eggs must be made with raw-edible grade eggs to mitigate the risk of salmonella.
