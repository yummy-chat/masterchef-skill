# Osmanthus Glutinous Rice Stuffed Lotus Root
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice 300g (soak overnight in advance, at least half an hour)
🥒 Lotus root 3 sections (about 800g, choose tightly sealed ends and round shape)
🫘 Red dates 100g (optional)
🥜 Peanuts 50g (optional, with skin for easier color absorption)
🌸 Dried osmanthus flowers 10g (add 10 minutes before removing from heat)
🍬 Rock sugar 50g
🍯 Brown sugar 50g
🌼 Osmanthus syrup (candied osmanthus) 适量 (for final drizzling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥢 Chopsticks (for filling rice)
🪥 Toothpicks (for securing lotus root lid)
🍲 Cast iron pot or regular stew pot (simmer on low heat)
♨️ Pressure cooker/electric pressure cooker (to speed up cooking)
🔪 Vegetable peeler/paring knife (for peeling)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for draining glutinous rice)

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the lotus root, cut off about 1-2 cm from one end as a lid.
   **Note**: Choose lotus roots with tightly sealed ends to prevent rice from leaking out.
2. Take the main body of the lotus root, fill the holes with the soaked and drained glutinous rice, use chopsticks to pack each hole tightly.
   **Note**: If the glutinous rice has been soaked overnight, fill to 90% fullness; if soaked for a shorter time, fill to 70-80% to prevent expansion from cracking.
3. Put the lid back in place, insert toothpicks at a 45-degree angle to secure, then gently tap the toothpicks with the back of a knife to ensure a tight seal.
4. Place the sealed lotus root into the pot, add red dates, peanuts, rock sugar, and brown sugar, pour in enough water to fully submerge the lotus root.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 4 hours.
   **Key**: Turn the lotus root every half hour to ensure even coloring; if the water level drops, add hot water.
   **Pressure cooker method**: First dissolve rock sugar and brown sugar in a small amount of water, add the lotus root and other ingredients, add water to cover, pressure cook for about 1 hour; after opening the lid, reduce the sauce over high heat while continuously basting the lotus root for better coloring.
6. 10 minutes before removing from heat, sprinkle in the dried osmanthus flowers.
7. After turning off the heat, if using a pressure cooker, it is recommended to soak the lotus root in the broth overnight for better flavor absorption; lotus root simmered slowly in a regular pot can be eaten directly, but soaking enhances the flavor.
8. For easier slicing, refrigerate the lotus root before cutting. Slice into slightly thick pieces, arrange on a plate, drizzle with the sauce, and garnish with osmanthus syrup.

## Health Advice
This osmanthus glutinous rice stuffed lotus root has relatively high sugar and carbohydrate content, suitable as an autumn dessert in small portions. Glutinous rice is difficult to digest, so the elderly and children should control portion sizes. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened green tea to cut the richness, or consume as part of a meal rather than on an empty stomach.
