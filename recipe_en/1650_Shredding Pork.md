# Shredding Pork

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin with skin (appropriate amount)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper or cloth

## Steps
1. Prepare the meat: Wash and dry the pork loin with skin. Use a knife to remove the surface sinew and fat, leaving only lean meat.  
   **Key point**: The sinew must be removed; otherwise, it will be hard to cut through and the texture will become poor after cooking. Wash the meat before cutting.

2. Prepare the cutting board and knife: Ensure the cutting board is stable and dry. Wash the chef's knife, and let a thin layer of water remain on the blade.  
   **Key point**: A dry cutting board prevents slipping. A moist blade prevents fresh meat from sticking. The knife must be sharp.

3. Trimming and positioning: Cut the prepared meat into pieces that are easy to handle, and place them at the edge of the cutting board for convenient cutting.

4. Slice the meat: Observe the muscle fibers. Cut against the grain, keeping the blade at a slight angle. First, press the blade into the meat, then use the left hand's "bridge of the palm" to hold the meat steady, and slice evenly into thin pieces about 1 mm thick. After cutting the first slice, lay the knife flat on it as a guide and weight, then slice the second piece. Repeat to ensure uniform thickness.  
   **Key point**: Cutting against the grain is crucial for tender meat. The left hand's pressing method naturally controls thickness using the palm's structure. Keep the blade angle as consistent as possible.

5. Shred the meat: Stack the sliced meat neatly. Cut perpendicular to the meat fibers, slicing into thin strips. After each cut, use the knife's side to gently push the strips aside to prevent sticking.  
   **Key point**: If the knife feels dull, shred one slice at a time. Cut steadily and precisely to maintain even thickness.

## Health Advice
This dish focuses on knife skills practice, containing almost zero fat and calories. It is suitable for those looking to improve their cooking skills while monitoring calorie intake. Be sure to remove the meat's sinew and keep the cutting board dry and slip-free to avoid hand injuries.
