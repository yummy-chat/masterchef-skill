# Old Beijing Zhajiang Noodles (Noodles with Fried Sauce)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 1500g pork belly with skin (cut into 1.2-1.5 cm cubes)
🧅 500g scallions (cut into coarse scallion segments)
🫘 750g dried yellow soybean paste
🫘 750g yellow soybean paste
🍶 Appropriate amount of huangjiu (yellow wine)
💧 2000g water
🫒 1000g cooking oil
🌿 About a dozen Sichuan peppercorns
⭐ 3 star anise segments

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Round wooden cutting board
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🥄 Wooden-handled iron spatula/spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the dried yellow soybean paste and yellow soybean paste in a basin at a 1:1 ratio. Add huangjiu and 4 jin of water, stir until well combined and dissolved, forming a uniform sauce.
**Note**: The total weight ratio of sauce to meat should be approximately 1:1. The sauce must be diluted in advance to prevent clumping when added to the pan.

2. Pour the cooking oil into a wok and heat to 50-60% hot. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and star anise segments, fry over medium-low heat until fragrant and the star anise turns dark brown, then remove and discard.
**Note**: This step is "smoking the oil," adding a base aroma to the oil.

3. Add the pork belly cubes to the wok and stir-fry over medium heat until the cubes shrink and release a large amount of oil.
**Key**: Ensure the fat in the meat is fully rendered; this is essential for the sauce to be fragrant without being greasy.

4. Slowly pour in the prepared sauce, reduce to medium-low heat, and stir well to immerse the meat cubes in the sauce.
5. Simmer over low heat for about 1 hour, continuously using the spatula to push from the bottom upward to prevent sticking.
**State Check**: The sauce will gradually evaporate, darken in color, and finally, oil will "return" to the surface, floating on top.
   **Note**: Maintain low heat and stir constantly; sufficient simmering time allows the sauce and meat aromas to meld.

6. Once oil "returns" to the sauce surface, add all of the scallion segments, stir-fry quickly over medium heat for about 7 minutes, allowing the scallion aroma to fully infuse into the sauce.
**Key**: This is "reverse smoking the oil"; adding a large amount of scallions at the end provides fresh aroma without becoming bitter from prolonged cooking.

7. Stir evenly until the sauce is thick and glossy. Turn off the heat, remove from the wok, and pour over cooked noodles.

## Health Tips
Zhajiang noodles offer balanced nutrition, with pork belly providing high-quality protein and fat, but the oil content is high. It is recommended to pair with vegetables such as cucumber shreds and bean sprouts to increase dietary fiber. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure; individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their intake.
