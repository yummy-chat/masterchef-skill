# Matcha Sourdough Boule  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour (quantity not specified)  
🫙 Sourdough starter (natural yeast, revived after frozen storage)  
🍵 Matcha paste (spread inside the dough)  
💧 Water (quantity not specified)  
🧂 Salt (quantity not specified)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🧺 Banneton proofing basket (for the dough's second fermentation)  
🍳 Dutch oven (with lid, used to provide a steam environment during baking)  
🔥 Oven  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥖 Rolling pin (for shaping)  
🔪 Bread scoring lame  
📄 Parchment paper  

## Steps  
1. Mix the bread flour, water, salt, and revived sourdough starter together, and knead into a smooth dough. Proceed with the bulk fermentation.  
   **Note**: The sourdough starter, revived after frozen storage, may be unstable in condition, making fermentation hard to control.  
2. Roll the dough out into a sheet, spread matcha paste evenly in the middle, fold it over, seal the seam, and round it into a ball.  
3. Place the dough into the banneton proofing basket for the second fermentation.  
   **Key point**: Ferment until the dough nearly overflows the basket.  
4. Preheat the oven, and preheat the Dutch oven together with it.  
5. Remove the Dutch oven, line it with parchment paper, and invert the proofed dough into the pot.  
6. Dust the surface of the dough and score it with the bread lame.  
7. Cover the Dutch oven with its lid and bake in the oven.  
   **Note**: The video does not mention specific baking temperature or time.  
8. During the middle to late stage of baking, uncover the lid and continue baking until the surface is brown and the loaf springs back when pressed.  
   **Note**: The final shape may be quite flat, but the internal crumb and texture are acceptable.  
9. Remove and let cool before slicing; the inside will reveal green streaks from the matcha paste.  

## Health Tips  
This matcha sourdough boule uses a sourdough starter for fermentation. Although it is more challenging, it is suitable for beginners wanting to try their hand at artisan bread. Pay attention to the condition of the revived starter, and ensure the room temperature is not too low during fermentation. The final product has a moderate calorie count, and the matcha paste enhances the flavor, making it ideal for health- and aesthetic-conscious baking enthusiasts.
