# Very Soft Whole Wheat Toast
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Whole wheat flour 100g (for Polish starter)
💧 Water 100g (for Polish starter)
🍞 Fresh yeast 2.7g (for Polish starter)
🌾 Bread flour 205g (for main dough)
🧂 White sugar 15g
🍯 Honey 15g (dissolved in part of the water)
🧂 Salt 3g
🍞 Fresh yeast 6g (for main dough, first soak and dissolve in part of the water)
🥚 Egg 1
💧 Water 70g (for main dough, divided into two parts)
🧈 Butter 20g (needs to be softened)
🌾 Oat flakes as needed (for surface decoration)
⚪ White sesame seeds as needed (for surface decoration)
⚫ Black sesame seeds as needed (for surface decoration)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫙 Glass sealing jar (for fermenting Polish starter)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stand mixer with dough hook
🌡️ Food thermometer
📦 Toast loaf pan
🔥 Oven
🔗 Cooling rack
🥛 Measuring cup
🥄 Spatula/scoop
💨 Spray bottle
🔪 Bench scraper
🛡️ Plastic wrap
🔩 Aluminum foil

## Cooking Steps
1. Dissolve the fresh yeast in a small amount of water, then add the whole wheat flour and remaining water, and mix until well combined. Cover the container and let it ferment at room temperature until the volume expands to 2-3 times. Then, transfer it to the refrigerator for an overnight cold fermentation until ready to use.
   **Key Point**: Cold fermentation enhances the flavor of the dough, and the extended fermentation time gives the bread a more delicate texture.

2. Divide the 70g of water for the main dough into two parts: mix 30g with 6g of fresh yeast to dissolve, and mix the remaining 40g with the honey until well combined.

3. Add the bread flour, white sugar, salt, egg, honey water, yeast water, and the fermented Polish starter into the stand mixer bowl. Mix on low speed until the ingredients are well combined and have formed a rough dough.

4. Switch to medium speed and knead until the dough can be pulled into a thick film with serrated edges when torn. Add the softened butter.

5. Continue kneading on medium speed until the butter is fully absorbed and the dough can be pulled into a thin, resilient, and smooth windowpane membrane, then stop kneading.
   **Key Point**: The ideal dough temperature after kneading is about 25°C. At this point, the dough has good extensibility.

6. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a bowl, and cover with plastic wrap. Let it ferment at room temperature for about 1 hour until the volume doubles.

7. Lightly dust the work surface with some flour. Turn out the fermented dough, gently press it down to release the gas, and divide it evenly into 3 portions. Shape each portion into a ball.

8. After resting the dough balls for a short while, flatten them by hand or roll them out with a rolling pin. Roll each portion up tightly and pinch the bottom seam to seal.

9. Spray the surface of the rolled dough pieces with water, then roll them in the mixed oat flakes, white sesame seeds, and black sesame seeds to coat the surface evenly with the grains.

10. Place the coated dough pieces into the toast loaf pan in order. Let them proof in a warm and humid environment for about 40 minutes, until the dough rises to 80-90% full of the pan.

11. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Place the loaf pan in the oven and bake for about 30 minutes.
    **Note**: Check the surface color after about 20 minutes of baking, and cover with aluminum foil promptly to prevent burning.

12. Immediately remove the bread from the oven after baking. Tap the pan vigorously to release the steam, and turn the toast out onto a cooling rack to cool.

## Health Tips
This whole wheat toast uses a Polish starter to enhance softness, and honey and eggs increase its nutritional value, making it suitable for breakfast or a fitness snack. Pay attention to the amount of sugar and butter used. Whole wheat flour is rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for those pursuing a low-GI diet.
