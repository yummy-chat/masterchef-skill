# Braised Fish without Water in Rice Cooker with Large Flower Rolls  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 Large Carp  
🌾 250g Flour  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced, divided for marinating and stir-frying)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (cloves for stir-frying, minced garlic for final garnish)  
🌱 Cilantro, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (scallion segments soaked in water, chopped scallions for final garnish)  
🌶️ A few dried chili peppers  
🫑 Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (diced)  
🥓 A small piece of pork skin (white strips, for added aroma)  
🍶 1 bottle cap of Huadiao wine (for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for braising)  
🌿 1 teaspoon ground white pepper  
🌶️ 1 tablespoon chili sauce (e.g., Renren Hui chili sauce)  
🦪 A little oyster sauce  
🍯 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍯 A little dark soy sauce  
🫘 Fermented soybean paste, appropriate amount  
🍬 A little granulated sugar (for marinating fish) + 3g (for kneading dough)  
🧂 A little salt  
🌿 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount  
🍞 3g active dry yeast  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🔪 Chef's knife  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🥣 Small bowl  
🫗 Cooking oil bottle  
🥄 Wok spatula or cooking spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Clean the carp thoroughly: cut off all fins, slit open the belly, remove the internal organs, and especially scrape off the blood along the backbone, black membrane, and fish teeth.  
**Key Point**: Removing fins, black membrane, and blood along the backbone are crucial steps for eliminating fishy odor.  

2. Cut the fish: Make a straight cut two finger-widths above the tail, slice the meat off both sides along the backbone up to the head, then cut off the head, splitting it in half. Cut the rib section with bones into strips about one finger-width wide.  

3. Initial marinating: Place all fish pieces in a bowl. Add one bottle cap of Huadiao wine and the scallion-ginger water (prepared by soaking scallion segments and ginger slices in water for 5 minutes and squeezing them). Add 1 teaspoon of ground white pepper. Gently mix well.  
**Key Point**: First, mix with scallion-ginger water and white pepper to effectively remove fishy odor and make the fish meat more tender.  

4. Prepare yeast water: Place 3g active dry yeast in a bowl. Add 125g of warm water at about 35°C (95°F). Stir with your hand until the yeast is completely dissolved.  
**Note**: Water temperature must not exceed 40°C (104°F), or the yeast will be killed, preventing the dough from rising.  

5. Knead the dough: Add 250g flour and 3g granulated sugar to the yeast water. Knead into a smooth dough. Cover tightly and let it rise until doubled in volume. Sugar promotes fermentation, resulting in a fluffier final product.  

6. Secondary marinating: To the initially mixed fish pieces, sequentially add 1 tablespoon chili sauce, a little oyster sauce, light soy sauce, a little dark soy sauce, an appropriate amount of fermented soybean paste, a little granulated sugar, a little salt, and an appropriate amount of five-spice powder. Mix thoroughly. Marinate for 20 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Use less salt, as the various sauces already contain saltiness. Ensure every piece of fish is evenly coated with the marinade.  

7. Sauté aromatics: Preheat the rice cooker for 2 minutes. Pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Add onion pieces, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.  

8. Arrange and braise: Place the marinated fish strips on top of the sautéed aromatics. Put the fish head in the center. Pour all remaining marinade from the bowl over the fish. Add a few pieces of pork skin and 1 tablespoon of Huadiao wine. Cover the lid tightly. Start the rice cooking program and braise for about 15 minutes.  
**Key Point**: No extra water is needed. The cooking relies on the moisture from vegetables, fish, and sauce for a waterless braising process.  

9. Shape the flower roll: Take out the risen dough. Punch it down and knead until smooth. Roll it into a long log. Coil one end inward to form a spiral disc shape. Then flatten it with your palm into a thick disc.  

10. Steam the flower roll: Open the rice cooker lid. Place the raw dough disc on top of the braised fish. Cover the lid. Let it proof for 15 minutes using residual heat or by starting the program again. Then continue steaming until the flower roll is cooked through.  

11. Final garnish: Remove the flower roll. Sprinkle chopped scallions, chopped cilantro, minced garlic, and diced red bell pepper over the fish. Cover the lid and let it steam for another 3 minutes to infuse the flavors into the fish.  

## Health Tips  
This dish uses pork skin for added aroma and is braised without water in a rice cooker. It has a rich sauce and flavorful fish, making it suitable for those pursuing a healthy diet and disliking fishy tastes. Paired with flower rolls as a staple, carbohydrates and protein are cooked together in one pot. However, the sauces are relatively high in salt; people with high blood pressure should consume less of the cooking liquid.
