# Sichuan Peppercorn Sour and Spicy Boneless Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Boneless chicken feet 4 jin (net weight after deboning; you can buy raw chicken feet and debone them yourself)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn 3g (for blanching)  
⭐ Star anise 2-3 pieces (for blanching)  
🍃 Bay leaves 2 pieces (for blanching)  
🌿 Chinese angelica 1g (for blanching, about half the size of a thumbnail)  
🧅 Scallion 20g (for blanching, cut one section in half lengthwise)  
🫚 Ginger 10g (for blanching, cut into 3 thin slices)  
🧄 Garlic 120g (sliced)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili 160g (sliced into rings)  
🫑 Fresh Sichuan green peppercorns 160g (rinsed clean and drained)  
🍬 White sugar 35g (increase to 80g if you prefer a sweeter taste)  
🧂 MSG 40g  
🧂 Donggu Yipinxian light soy sauce 80g (can substitute with regular light soy sauce, but reduce amount as needed, as regular soy sauce is saltier)  
🥫 Pantai Thai sweet chili sauce 400g (choose a brand with moderate saltiness)  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar 280g  
🍶 Zhejiang red vinegar 160g  
🫙 Pickled chilies 160g (cut into sections)  
💧 Pickled chili brine 240g  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn and chili extract 160g (can substitute with chili extract if unavailable)  
💧 Cool boiled water 160g  
🫒 Sesame oil 80g  
🌶️ Red chili oil 120g (can substitute with homemade chili oil)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥢 Whisk  
🫙 Stainless steel basin or food container (for marinating)  
🧺 Colander  
🍽️ Wooden lid  
🔥 Gas stove  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Steps  
1. **Blanch to remove odor**: Place boneless chicken feet in a pot with cold water. Add Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, bay leaves, Chinese angelica, scallion sections, and ginger slices. Bring to a boil over high heat. Just before the water comes to a full boil, immediately reduce to the lowest heat. Cover and cook for 7 minutes. Turn off the heat. Without uncovering the lid, let it rest for another 7 minutes using residual heat. This ensures the chicken feet skin remains intact and the texture is crunchy and bouncy.
2. **Rinse and drain**: Remove the chicken feet. Rinse thoroughly under cold water or ice water. Remove any spice residue. Drain completely.
3. **Chill to set**: Place the drained chicken feet in the refrigerator for 30 minutes. This makes the texture more Q-弹 (chewy and bouncy).
4. **Prepare the composite seasoning**: In a large bowl, combine white sugar, MSG, and light soy sauce. Whisk until the mixture becomes sticky. Then, add in sequence: Thai sweet chili sauce, apple cider vinegar, Zhejiang red vinegar, pickled chili sections, pickled chili brine, Sichuan peppercorn and chili extract, cool boiled water, sesame oil, and red chili oil. Stir well to combine. Finally, add the sliced garlic, bird's eye chili rings, and fresh Sichuan green peppercorns. Mix thoroughly. Key point: Ensure the sugar and MSG are completely dissolved.
5. **Combine and marinate**: Pour the chicken feet into the prepared seasoning mixture. Toss to coat evenly, ensuring each chicken foot is fully covered with the sauce. Seal the container and refrigerate for at least 12 hours. Flip the chicken feet once or twice during marination for better flavor absorption.
6. **Finished product**: After 12 hours of marination, remove the chicken feet. They will have a bright red color, a Q-弹 (chewy and bouncy) texture, and a balanced flavor of sour, spicy, umami, aromatic, and numbing.

## Health Advice  
This Sichuan Peppercorn Sour and Spicy Boneless Chicken Feet dish is high in protein and low in fat, but it is high in sodium. It is recommended to pair it with light staple foods and vegetables to balance the saltiness. Suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors. People with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs should consume with caution.
