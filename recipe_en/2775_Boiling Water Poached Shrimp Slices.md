# Boiling Water Poached Shrimp Slices
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Fresh shrimp 400g (white prawns, butterflied and shelled, tail segment optional)
🥒 Cucumber 1 (shaved into thin slices)
🥬 Chinese lettuce 1 (shaved into thin slices)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers适量 (used in two batches)
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns适量
🧄 Ginger 1 small piece (minced)
🧄 Garlic several cloves (minced, used in two batches)

🧂 Salt适量
🌫️ White pepper powder适量
🍶 Cooking wine适量
🫘 Pixian bean paste 2 tablespoons
🍲 Hot pot base 1 small piece (about 15-20g, optional)
🥄 Light soy sauce a little
🍬 Sugar a little
🌶️ Chili powder适量 (for final sprinkle)
🫒 Hot oil 1 tablespoon (for final drizzle)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥒 Vegetable peeler
🥣 Glass bowl/container
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍲 Clay pot/deep bowl
🔨 Meat mallet/rolling pin

## Instructions
1. **Prepare fresh shrimp**: Shell the fresh shrimp, optionally leaving the tail segment intact. Make a deep slit along the back without cutting through.
**Key point**: Butterflying helps with flavor absorption and flattening.

2. **Marinate shrimp**: Add a pinch of salt, white pepper powder, and cooking wine to the shrimp. Mix well and let marinate briefly.
3. **Prepare vegetables**: Shave cucumber and Chinese lettuce into thin slices using a peeler. Mince ginger and garlic.
4. **Pound shrimp slices**: Evenly coat the marinated shrimp with dry cornstarch. Use a meat mallet or rolling pin to pound them into thin slices.
**Key point**: Pound as thin as possible to prevent curling during blanching and ensure a tender, smooth texture.

5. **Quick-fry vegetables**: Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Quickly stir-fry cucumber and Chinese lettuce slices with a pinch of salt and dried chili peppers until just cooked. Transfer to a bowl and spread as a base layer.
**Doneness check**: Vegetables should darken in color and soften slightly while remaining crisp.

6. **Prepare broth**: Add more oil to the wok. Cook shrimp heads over medium-low heat, stirring to release red shrimp oil. Add minced ginger, garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers, stir until fragrant. Add Pixian bean paste and hot pot base, cook over low heat until red oil releases. Add enough water and bring to a boil. Season with salt, a little light soy sauce, and sugar. The broth should be slightly saltier than usual.
7. **Poach shrimp slices**: Once the broth is boiling, add shrimp slices one by one over high heat. Let them sit undisturbed for about 10 seconds to set, then gently separate with chopsticks. Cook for about 1 minute until they change color, curl, and float.
**Note**: Do not overcook to keep the shrimp tender.

8. **Plate and drizzle oil**: Remove shrimp slices and place on top of the vegetables. Ladle in some of the broth. Sprinkle with chili powder, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and minced garlic. Heat one tablespoon of oil until smoking and drizzle directly over the top to release aroma.

## Health Tips
This poached shrimp dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it a satisfying option during weight loss. However, the broth is high in oil and salt, so it is best to limit consumption of the soup. The thinly pounded shrimp slices are easier to digest and can be enjoyed in moderation by the elderly and children.
