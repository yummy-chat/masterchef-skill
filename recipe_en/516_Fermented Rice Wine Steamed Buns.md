# Fermented Rice Wine Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 500g all-purpose flour
🫙 5g yeast
🧂 3g double-acting baking powder
🍬 2g sugar
🐖 25g lard
🍚 100g fermented rice wine juice
💧 200g water (adjustable up to 250g depending on flour absorption)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🪵 Dough board
🥟 Steamer basket
🍳 Wok (as the steamer base)
🧣 Damp cloth or bowl (to cover the dough to prevent drying)

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour flour, yeast, baking powder, and sugar into a mixing bowl. Mix well.
2. Combine fermented rice wine juice and water. The total liquid amount should be about 250g.
3. Gradually add the mixed liquid to the dry ingredients in batches, stirring with your hand until it forms a flaky texture with no dry flour. After initially kneading into a dough, add the lard.
4. Knead the dough flakes into a rough dough. Cover with a damp cloth or bowl and let it rest for 10 minutes to soften and become easier to knead.
5. Transfer the rested dough to a dough board. Use the heel of your palm to knead repeatedly until the surface is smooth and the texture is fine.
   **Note**: Hand-kneading fully develops the gluten, resulting in softer, fluffier steamed buns and more even fermentation.
6. Return the dough to the bowl, cover it, and let it ferment in a warm place for about 40 minutes, until it doubles in volume and a finger poke leaves an indent that does not spring back or collapse.
7. Remove the dough and knead it to release gas. Roll it into a long log and tear off pieces of about 80g each. When tearing, pull from the side to preserve the smooth surface.
8. Knead each piece again. Use the heel of your palm to smooth the surface, pinch the seam tightly, turn it seam-side down, and shape into a round bun with no wrinkles on the surface.
9. Place the dough pieces into a steamer basket lined with a damp cloth, leaving space between them. Cover and let them rest for 15–20 minutes, until they visibly expand and feel light and slowly spring back when gently touched.
10. Add enough cold water to the wok, place the steamer basket on top, and cover with the lid. Turn the heat to high. After the water boils and steam rises, reduce to medium-high heat and continue steaming until the buns are fully puffed up with a smooth, shiny surface.
    **Key point**: Starting with cold water allows the buns to undergo a final rise during heating, resulting in a softer texture.
11. Do not open the lid immediately after turning off the heat. Let the buns sit in residual heat for 2–3 minutes, then quickly remove the lid and take out the buns to prevent sudden shrinking due to temperature drop.

## Health Suggestions
The fermented rice wine juice gives the buns a subtle sweetness and aroma, promotes fermentation, and makes them easier to digest. This is suitable for people with weaker digestive function or those looking to add flavor to their staple food. Although lard enhances tenderness, those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood fat should replace it with an equal amount of vegetable oil to control fat intake.
