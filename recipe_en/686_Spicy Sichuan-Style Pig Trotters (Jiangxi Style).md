# Spicy Sichuan-Style Pig Trotters (Jiangxi Style)
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐷 Pig trotters – appropriate amount (pre-blanched)  
🌿 Cilantro – appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallions, ginger – appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – appropriate amount  
⭐ Star anise – appropriate amount  
🍃 Bay leaves – appropriate amount  
🌰 Cinnamon stick – appropriate amount  
🥄 Light soy sauce – 4 tablespoons total (3 for braising + 1 for stir-frying)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce – 2 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce – 2 tablespoons total (1 for braising + 1 for stir-frying)  
🧂 Salt – 1 teaspoon  
🫗 Hot water – appropriate amount  
🧄 Minced garlic – appropriate amount  
🌶️ Fresh red chili peppers – appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili flakes – appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar – 1 tablespoon  
🍲 Reserved braising liquid – appropriate amount  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Deep frying pan  
🥢 Food tongs  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Mixing bowls  
🥄 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the blanched pig trotters in the pot. Add scallions, ginger, dried chili peppers, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick. Drizzle with 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Pour in hot water until the ingredients are submerged, then cover with a lid.  
   **Key Point**: Simmer over medium-low heat for 1 hour. The trotters must be cooked until tender and flavorful; insufficient time will affect the texture.  

2. Use food tongs to remove the pig trotters. Use a slotted spoon to discard all spices. Reserve the liquid in the pot separately.  

3. Heat oil in a clean pot. Add minced garlic, fresh red chili peppers, and chili flakes. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.  
   **Note**: Do not use high heat to avoid burning the base ingredients.  

4. Add the braised pig trotters. Add 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the trotters are evenly coated with the shiny red base.  

5. Pour in the reserved braising liquid. Continue to cook over high heat until the sauce thickens. Once the sauce has reduced and thickened, sprinkle in the cilantro and stir-fry evenly before serving.  
   **Key Point**: The sauce must be reduced to a thick consistency that clings to the trotters. Add the cilantro at the very end to keep it vibrant green and aromatic.  

## Health Advice  
Pig trotters are rich in collagen, making them suitable for those who need skin and joint care. However, they are high in fat, so excessive consumption should be avoided. The chili peppers can stimulate the appetite and aid digestion, but those with a sensitive stomach should control the spiciness level.
