# Old Dough Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Flour, as needed (medium or high gluten, half a catty for making the starter, one catty for the main dough)  
💧 Warm water, 130g (for making the starter), about 240g per catty of flour (for the main dough)  
🍚 Rice wine (fermented rice juice), 30g  
🦠 Wine starter (sweet wine starter/rice wine starter), 5g  
🥣 Old dough starter (levain), about 120g per catty of flour  
🧂 Edible alkali (alkaline powder), 5g (dissolved in 50g warm water)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl (for kneading and fermenting dough)  
🥄 Bowl/small dish (for holding ingredients)  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🔪 Cutting board/work surface  
🍵 Steamer basket  
♨️ Steamer pot  

## Instructions
### Making the Old Dough Starter
1. In a bowl, combine warm water, rice wine, and wine starter, stirring until the wine starter is completely dissolved.
   **Note**: The water amount for kneading should be slightly more than usual to make the dough wetter, which helps microbial activity thrive.
2. Gradually add the liquid to half a catty of flour, stirring as you go, and knead into a smooth dough.
3. Seal the dough with plastic wrap and place it in a warm spot to ferment naturally until it increases in volume, shows a distinct honeycomb-like porous interior, and gives off a sour aroma. Depending on the ambient temperature, this may take several hours to overnight.

### Kneading the Main Dough
1. Tear the fermented old dough starter into small pieces, and thoroughly mix it with dry flour at a ratio of about 120g of starter per catty of flour.
   **Key**: The starter must be fully torn and mixed with the dry flour to distribute the culture evenly; otherwise, subsequent fermentation and the final product will be inconsistent.
2. Gradually add about 240g of warm water, stirring until it forms a flaky texture, then knead into a rough dough.

### Fermenting the Main Dough
1. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm spot.
   **Note**: Old dough ferments slower than yeast, so it requires more time. Traditionally, it can be left on a heated brick bed to ferment overnight.
2. When the dough has noticeably increased in volume, shows a fine honeycomb-like interior, and gives off a sour smell, fermentation is complete.

### Adding Alkali to Neutralize and Kneading
1. Dissolve 5g of edible alkali in 50g of warm water to make an alkaline solution.
2. Remove the dough and place it on a work surface. Tear it open, and using your hand, dip it into the alkaline solution and apply it to the dough in small amounts, repeatedly kneading until the sour smell is no longer obvious.
   **Key**: Judge the alkali amount by smell; stop adding alkali once the sourness disappears.
3. After adding the alkali, knead the dough thoroughly until it is smooth and even, avoiding localized strong alkali taste or yellow spots in the finished product.

### Shaping and Second Proofing
1. Roll the kneaded dough into a long strip and divide it into evenly sized small pieces.
2. Knead each piece thoroughly, then roll them round with both hands to form smooth steamed bun blanks.
3. Place the bun blanks in a steamer basket lined with a cloth or brushed with oil, leaving enough space between each.
4. Cover the steamer lid and allow a second proofing until the blanks slightly increase in size, feel light to the touch, and small air pockets reform inside.

### Steaming
1. Fill the steamer pot with enough water and bring it to a vigorous boil over high heat.
2. Place the steamer basket on top, cover tightly, and steam over high heat for 15 minutes.
3. **Key**: After turning off the heat, let it sit covered for 3–5 minutes before opening the lid to prevent the buns from suddenly shrinking and collapsing due to the temperature change.

## Health Advice
Old dough steamed buns use natural fermentation, making the carbohydrates easier to digest, which is suitable for those with weak digestive function. Diabetic patients should control their consumption. It is recommended to eat them with vegetables and protein to help stabilize blood sugar.
