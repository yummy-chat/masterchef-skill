# Fried Dual-Flavor Meatballs
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 500g ground pork belly (with alternating fat and lean)
🥚 3 eggs
🍞 150g bread crumbs (crumbled steamed bun)
🌽 50g corn starch
🧂 3g baking soda
💧 80g water (mixed with baking soda to make soda water)
🌶️ Fine chili powder, to taste (for spicy flavor)
🫚 30g minced ginger
🧂 10g salt
🌿 5g ground pepper
🌿 1g thirteen-spice powder
🥄 20g premium soy sauce
🫒 A little five-spice flavored oil

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍳 Deep fryer or pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Spoon (for scooping meatballs)
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Steps
1.  Prepare the base meat mixture: Place the ground pork belly, minced ginger, salt, ground pepper, thirteen-spice powder, and premium soy sauce in a bowl. Stir in one direction until well combined.
   **Note**: Add all seasonings at once.
2.  Make the soda water: Mix the baking soda with water to create soda water.
   **Key point**: Soda water is the key to making the meatballs fluffy inside.
3.  Incorporate the soda water: Gradually add the soda water, stirring vigorously in the same direction each time, until the meat mixture becomes sticky and firm and has fully absorbed the liquid.
4.  Add auxiliary ingredients: Beat in the eggs and mix well. Then add the bread crumbs and corn starch, continuing to stir in the same direction until evenly combined.
   **Note**: Bread crumbs add crispness, and starch prevents the meatballs from falling apart. Do not use too much of either, or the meatballs will become hard.
5.  Divide and season: Divide the meat mixture into two portions. Keep one portion as the original five-spice flavor. Add fine chili powder to the other portion and mix well to create a spicy flavor. Finally, drizzle a small amount of five-spice flavored oil into each portion and mix well.
   **Purpose**: The flavored oil adds aroma and prevents the meatballs from sticking together.
6.  First fry for setting: Heat oil in a pot to 160°C. Form meatballs by squeezing the mixture through the opening between your thumb and forefinger. Use an oiled spoon to scoop the meatballs into the pot. Maintain a stable oil temperature and continue adding meatballs without overcrowding. Fry until the meatballs float to the surface, turn a light golden brown, and are set, then remove.
7.  Second fry for crispness: Increase the oil temperature. Add the meatballs back for a second fry until they turn an even, deep golden brown and the surface is crispy. Remove and drain on a wire rack or paper towel.
   **Key point**: The second fry ensures an even color and a crispier texture.
8.  Repeat the same frying method for the spicy meatballs: first fry to set, then fry again for crispness.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in fat. It is recommended to serve with a refreshing vegetable salad or a vinegar dipping sauce to cut the richness. Although pork belly is delicious, it is advisable to eat only 5-6 meatballs per serving. Suitable for family gatherings. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt and pepper in the dipping sauce.
