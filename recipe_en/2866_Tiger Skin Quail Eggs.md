# Tiger Skin Quail Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Quail eggs 500g
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)
🧅 Scallion 1/2 stalk (sliced)
🪵 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece (broken apart)
⭐ Star anise 1 piece (broken apart)
🍺 Beer as needed
🌿 Chopped scallion as needed (for garnish)
🫗 White vinegar as needed (for soaking)
🦪 Oyster sauce about 5g
🧂 Table salt about 1g
🧂 Chicken bouillon about 2g
🍬 White sugar a pinch
🍯 Caramel color a small amount
🍾 Light soy sauce about 4g
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed
🌊 Water starch as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Large iron pot
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer ladle
🥣 Small soup bowls (a pair, for shaking to crack shells)
🪓 Chopping board
🧹 Brush
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the quail eggs in a bowl, add cold water and white vinegar, soak for 1 minute, then use a brush to scrub the surface of each egg clean of impurities.
2. In a pot, add cold water and quail eggs, cook over low heat for 8 minutes. Keep the water surface slightly simmering throughout to prevent the eggshells from cracking.
3. After cooking, remove the eggs and rinse with cold water. Place them in one small bowl, cover with another bowl, and shake vigorously until the eggshells are evenly cracked. Then put the eggs in water and easily peel off the shells.
4. Slice the ginger, slice the scallion, and break apart the cinnamon stick and star anise. Set aside.
5. Heat the wok, pour in sufficient oil. When the oil reaches 50% hot, add the peeled quail eggs. Reduce to medium-low heat and fry for 2 minutes. Gently push the eggs apart with a spoon to prevent sticking. Fry until the skin turns golden and develops a tiger-like pattern, then remove.
6. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the ginger and scallion slices, stir-fry over low heat until golden, then remove and discard the ginger and scallion.
7. Using the base oil in the wok, stir-fry the star anise and cinnamon stick over low heat until fragrant. Add oyster sauce, water, and beer, then bring to a boil.
8. Once the broth is boiling, season with salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, caramel color, and light soy sauce.
9. Add the fried quail eggs, reduce to medium-low heat, and simmer for 6 minutes to fully infuse the flavor.
10. When the broth is slightly reduced, drizzle in an appropriate amount of water starch to thicken the sauce. Remove from the wok and plate. Garnish with chopped scallion.

## Health Suggestions
Quail eggs are rich in high-quality protein and phospholipids, making them suitable for replenishing energy. However, after frying and braising, the calorie content is higher. It is recommended to pair them with light vegetables to balance the meal. Pay attention to portion size to avoid consuming too many due to their small size.
