# Men's Saozhi Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork (leg meat, with alternating fat and lean, fat and lean separated)
🧈 Dried tofu or firm tofu (cut into small cubes)
🧅 Onion (cut into small cubes)
🧄 Garlic (minced)
🫒 Scallion (cut into green onion slices)
🍜 Noodles (machine-made fresh noodles or handmade noodles)
🫒 Cooking oil (a small amount for the base)
🧂 MSG (a pinch)
🧂 Chicken bouillon (a pinch)
🌶️ Chili flakes (to taste, adjust according to preference)
🍾 Cooking wine (to taste)
🍎🍾 Apple cider vinegar (a small amount, can substitute with aromatic vinegar)
🍬 Rock sugar (7-8 pieces)
🧄💧 Garlic water (minced garlic + MSG + chicken bouillon + water, mixed)
🫘 Soybean paste (3 tablespoons)
🫙 Red braised soy sauce (one drizzle, can substitute with dark soy sauce)
🫙 Light soy sauce (one drizzle)
🧂 White pepper (a few shakes)
🧂 Salt (to taste, adjust saltiness)
🌶️🫙 Chili oil (optional, drizzle before serving)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok (non-stick pan)
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🥢 Chopsticks
🍲 Cooking pot

## Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork. Cut the fat part into small strips and mince the lean part into ground meat. Set aside separately.
2. Cut the dried tofu into small cubes and the onion into small cubes.
3. Mince the garlic, add a pinch of MSG and chicken bouillon, and soak in water to make garlic water. Set aside.
4. Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of base oil. Add the fat strips and stir-fry over high heat until the fat changes color and the lard renders out.
5. Add the dried tofu cubes, reduce to medium heat, and stir-fry with the rendered fat until the tofu surface turns golden brown and the fat becomes crispy and fragrant.
6. Add the chili flakes and stir-fry quickly until fragrant, then immediately add the onion cubes and stir-fry evenly.
7. Add the ground lean meat and stir-fry until the meat is completely cooked through.
8. Drizzle cooking wine and a small amount of apple cider vinegar along the side of the wok, stir-fry quickly to remove any gamey smell.
9. Keep the heat at medium and stir-fry until the moisture in the lean meat evaporates, concentrating the flavor of the saozhi.
10. Add the rock sugar and stir until melted, then pour in the garlic water.
11. Add 3 tablespoons soybean paste, one drizzle of red braised soy sauce, one drizzle of light soy sauce, a pinch of chicken bouillon, and white pepper. Stir-fry thoroughly until the sauce aroma is released.
12. Taste and adjust. The saozhi should be saltier than if eaten directly. Add salt as needed. Before removing from the pan, drizzle in chili oil if desired.
13. In a separate pot, cook the noodles. Once the water boils, add the noodles. If the pot threatens to boil over, add a small amount of cold water.
14. Once the noodles are cooked, remove and immediately plunge into ice water. Drain well and place into a bowl.
15. Top the noodles with the stir-fried saozhi and sprinkle with green onion slices before serving.

## Health Advice
This saozhi noodle dish uses lard for aroma and rock sugar to balance the spiciness, but the combination of fatty meat and sauce results in high calories. It is recommended to pair it with a serving of stir-fried vegetables for a balanced meal. The dried tofu and lean meat provide protein, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or craving a hearty meal. Be mindful of the portions of chili oil and noodles.
