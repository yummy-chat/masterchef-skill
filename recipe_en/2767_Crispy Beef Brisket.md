# Crispy Beef Brisket
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season dish

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket 3 jin (beef rib section, deboned)
💧 Water 8 jin (for braising)
🧄 Crushed garlic 2 liang
🫚 Old ginger 2 liang
🧅 Dried shallots 1 liang
⭐ Star anise 3 pieces
🍃 Bay leaves 5 pieces
🌾 Cake flour 3 liang (for batter)
🫧 Baking powder 3 qian (for batter)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 1 liang (for batter)
🌽 Cornstarch 1 liang (for batter, plus about 1 liang for coating surface)
💧 Water 1 liang (for batter)
🥚 Egg white 1 (for batter)
🫒 Vegetable oil 3 qian (for batter)
🧂 Salt 5 fen (for batter)
🫘 Chu hou paste 2 liang
🍶 Rice wine 2 liang
🥡 Light soy sauce 3 liang
🍬 Rock sugar 2 liang
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
🧂 Huai salt to taste (dipping)
🌶️ Chili powder to taste (dipping)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottomed frying pan
🍲 Deep soup pot (large pot)
🥘 Oil pot (deep pot)
🔪 Cutting board and chef’s knife
🥄 Strainer
🥢 Chef’s tongs/chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the beef brisket into long strips. Heat the pan dry over medium heat without oil, and pan-fry for about 3 minutes until the surface is golden brown, rendering the beef fat.
**Key Point**: Use the rendered beef fat as the base oil to lock in the meat juices and enhance aroma.
2. Use the beef fat in the pan to sauté crushed garlic, old ginger, and dried shallots until fragrant. Add chu hou paste and stir-fry until aromatic.
**Note**: Chu hou paste is the core flavor of this dish; ensure it is thoroughly stir-fried.
3. Return the beef brisket to the pan, pour rice wine along the edge of the pan, and ignite it for a quick flambé to enhance the aroma.
**Note**: Exercise caution when using an open flame; this step effectively removes any gamey smell and boosts fragrance.
4. Add sufficient hot water, then add rock sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, star anise, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat, cover with a lid, and braise for 40 minutes.
**Key Point**: Use hot water to prevent the meat from shrinking and becoming tough; rock sugar helps tenderize the brisket faster.
5. The brisket is tender when a chopstick can easily pierce through it. If it is not soft enough, braise for an additional 15 minutes.
6. Remove the brisket and let it cool slightly, then cut into finger-thick strips. First, evenly coat with a thin layer of cornstarch, then dip into the prepared batter, ensuring an even coating.
**Key Point**: Coating with cornstarch improves batter adhesion, ensuring a crispy crust that does not fall off after frying.
7. Pour a generous amount of oil into a pot and heat to medium temperature. Add the battered brisket and fry for about 2 minutes until set and floating, then remove.
8. Raise the oil temperature to approximately 160°C, then return the brisket for a second fry for about 1 minute until golden and crispy. Remove.
**Note**: The second fry helps expel excess oil, making the crust crispier; use a strainer to remove any oil sediments during frying to prevent burning and bitterness.
9. Drain the oil, cut into bite-sized pieces, and plate. Serve with huai salt and chili powder for dipping.

## Health Advice
The brisket is braised first and then fried, resulting in a crispy exterior and tender interior. However, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables for balance. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive fat intake.
