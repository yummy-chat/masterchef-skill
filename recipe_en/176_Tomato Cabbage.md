# Tomato Cabbage
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Cabbage, about half a head (separate stems and leaves, slice stems diagonally, cut leaves into large pieces)
🍅 Tomato, about 1-2 (cut into chunks)
🌿 Celery, a few stalks (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, 3-5 cloves (minced)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🥫 Ketchup, to taste
🍶 Black vinegar, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Small bowl, spoon, chopsticks
🍳 Wok
🗑️ Strainer (for draining)
🍽️ Long plate, chopsticks (for serving and presentation)

## Instructions
1. Add light soy sauce, salt, white sugar, ketchup, and black vinegar to a small bowl in order, stir to combine, and set aside as the sauce.
   **Note**: Preparing the sauce in advance shortens cooking time and allows for quick flavor infusion.
2. Place the wok over heat, add cooking oil, and once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
   **Key Point**: Fully release the garlic aroma.
3. Turn the heat to high, add the cabbage stems and tomato chunks. Stir-fry quickly until the cabbage stems begin to soften and the tomatoes release their juices.
   **Note**: High heat and quick stir-frying keep the vegetables crisp and tender.
4. Add the cabbage leaves, continuing to stir-fry over high heat until the leaves soften slightly and shrink in volume.
   **Key Point**: Add the leaves later to prevent them from becoming overly mushy.
5. Add the celery sections, then immediately pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly to combine evenly.
6. Keep the heat on high. Once the sauce comes to a boil and evenly coats the ingredients, and the ingredients are fully colored and aromatic, turn off the heat.
   **Note**: Remove from heat quickly to maintain a crisp texture.
7. Immediately transfer to a plate and serve.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in lycopene and dietary fiber, with a low-fat, refreshing flavor. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or needing to manage blood pressure. When cooking, add the cabbage stems first and the leaves later to preserve a crisp texture and retain more vitamin C.
