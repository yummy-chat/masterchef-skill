# Chicken Feet in Fermented Bean Curd Sauce (Nan Ru Feng Zhao)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All year round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken feet, about 1 lb (washed, split in half lengthwise)
🧄 Garlic, a few cloves
🧅 Scallions, a few segments
🌶️ Dried chilies, a few pieces
🍂 Bay leaves, a few pieces
⭐ Star anise, a few pieces
🍬 Rock sugar, 6 pieces
🧂 Red fermented bean curd (Nan Ru), 2 pieces
🥜 Peanut butter, 1 tablespoon
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for blanching)
🍾 Light soy sauce, added in two batches (about 45 ml)
🎨 Dark soy sauce, a little (for color)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok or stew pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce)
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the fermented bean curd sauce**
   Place 2 pieces of red fermented bean curd and 1 tablespoon of peanut butter in a bowl. Add warm water and stir until smooth. Set aside.
**Key point**: This is the core flavor sauce.

2. **Clean and blanch the chicken feet**
   Wash the chicken feet and split them in half lengthwise. Place them in cold water in a pot, add cooking wine, and bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off the foam, cook for about 3 minutes, then drain.
**Note**: Starting with cold water removes odor more thoroughly; splitting makes the feet more flavorful.

3. **Sauté the aromatics**
   Heat a thin layer of oil in another pot over medium-low heat. Add garlic cloves, scallion segments, dried chilies, bay leaves, and star anise. Stir-fry until fragrant.
4. **Stir-fry the chicken feet**
   Add the blanched chicken feet and stir-fry carefully. Drizzle in a splash of light soy sauce and stir-fry for about 1 minute. Add the prepared fermented bean curd sauce, followed by two more splashes of light soy sauce and a little dark soy sauce. Stir until evenly coated.
**Key point**: Add light soy sauce in two batches—first for seasoning, then for blending; dark soy sauce is only for color.

5. **Braise**
   Add enough boiling water to cover the chicken feet. Add 6 pieces of rock sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and braise for 40 minutes until the chicken feet are tender.
**Note**: Must use boiling water to maintain texture.

6. **Reduce sauce and serve**
   Uncover and turn the heat to high. Stir and reduce the sauce. The sauce will thicken naturally from the collagen in the chicken feet, coating them without need for a slurry. Turn off the heat, plate, and garnish with chopped scallions if desired.

## Health Advice
Chicken feet in fermented bean curd sauce are rich in collagen, suitable for those needing a collagen boost. However, fermented bean curd is high in salt; those with high blood pressure are advised to limit consumption of the sauce.
