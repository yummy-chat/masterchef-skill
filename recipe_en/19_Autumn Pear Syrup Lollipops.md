# Autumn Pear Syrup Lollipops  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍐 Red pears 3~5 (or other juicy pears)  
🍬 Yellow rock sugar 300 grams (can be replaced with regular rock sugar)  
🍯 Maltose 100 grams  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Peeler  
🧀 Grater (or blender)  
🧻 Cheesecloth  
🍲 Small pot  
🥄 Wooden spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍭 Lollipop mold (including paper sticks)  
🍳 Baking tray  
📄 Parchment paper  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel the red pears and grate them into fine shreds using a grater, or blend them into pear puree using a blender.  
2. Place the pear puree in a bowl lined with cheesecloth, lift the four corners of the cheesecloth, and squeeze firmly to extract the pear juice. Discard the pear pulp.  
3. Pour 500 grams of pear juice into a small pot. Add 300 grams of yellow rock sugar and 100 grams of maltose.  
4. Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring until the rock sugar is completely dissolved. Reduce to medium-low heat and continue to simmer for about 30 minutes.  
   - **Note**: Stir continuously throughout to prevent sticking. Observe the bubbles to gauge moisture evaporation: around 10 minutes, large bubbles appear; after 20 minutes, bubbles become smaller; at 30 minutes, bubbles are dense and tiny, indicating most moisture has evaporated.  
5. Once the syrup is ready, turn the heat to the lowest setting. Dip a chopstick into the syrup and immediately drip it into a bowl of cold water.  
   - **Key test**: If the syrup solidifies and hardens in the water, and makes a crisp sound when bitten, it is done. Otherwise, continue simmering and repeat the test.  
6. When the desired consistency is reached, immediately remove from heat. Quickly spoon the syrup into the mold with paper sticks pre-inserted, or pour it onto a baking tray lined with parchment paper.  
   - **Key**: Work quickly to prevent the syrup from solidifying in the pot.  
7. Allow to cool completely until hardened. Remove the lollipops from the silicone mold, or gently peel them off the baking tray or parchment paper.  

## Health Advice  
These autumn pear syrup lollipops replace some of the syrup with pear juice, offering moistening and cough-relieving benefits. They are suitable as a children's snack during seasonal transitions. However, they are still primarily sugar-based, so consumption should be limited to no more than two per serving. It is recommended to enjoy them slowly with warm water to alleviate sweetness.
