# Bowl-Style Shark Fin Soup (Wan Zai Chi)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Vermicelli 50g (soaked in warm water for 10 minutes, cut into short sections)
🍗 Chicken thighs 2 (cooked and shredded into chicken strips, the cooking broth reserved as base soup)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms appropriate amount (pre-soaked, shredded)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms appropriate amount (pre-soaked, shredded)
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten and set aside)
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount (mixed with water to form a slurry)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for removing odor when cooking chicken thighs)
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (for coloring)
🌶️ White pepper appropriate amount (add before serving)
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount (add before serving)
🍾 Zhejiang vinegar or aromatic vinegar appropriate amount (add before serving)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Bowls (for soaking, beating eggs, and serving)
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak vermicelli in warm water for 10 minutes, drain, and cut into short sections. Set aside.
2. Rinse chicken thighs, place in a pot with an appropriate amount of cooking wine, and add water to cover. Cook until done. Remove and let cool, then shred the chicken into thin strips. Reserve the cooking broth as the base soup.
3. Rinse the pre-soaked shiitake mushrooms and wood ear mushrooms, shred them, and set aside.
4. Bring the reserved chicken broth to a boil. Add the shredded wood ear mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, and chicken strips.
5. Season with an appropriate amount of oyster sauce and salt. Add dark soy sauce to adjust the color. Stir to combine.
6. Add the cut vermicelli sections and cook briefly.
7. **Turn off the heat**, drizzle in the prepared cornstarch slurry to thicken, and stir quickly until the soup becomes glossy and thick.
> Key: The heat must be turned off when adding the cornstarch slurry; otherwise, the starch will clump instantly in boiling soup and fail to create a smooth, silky consistency.
8. Drizzle in the beaten egg, gently stir to form even egg ribbons, then immediately remove from heat and ladle into bowls.
> Key: Do not overcook after adding the egg; stir gently and remove promptly to keep the egg ribbons tender and smooth.
9. Before serving, sprinkle with white pepper, and drizzle with a little sesame oil and Zhejiang vinegar or aromatic vinegar according to personal taste.

## Health Notes
This bowl-style shark fin soup uses vermicelli as a substitute for real shark fin, making it both eco-friendly and affordable. It is rich in protein and dietary fiber, suitable for those needing an energy boost. The chicken broth base with shiitake mushrooms and wood ear mushrooms adds umami flavor, but the thickening agent slightly increases the calorie count, so those on a diet should consume in moderation.
