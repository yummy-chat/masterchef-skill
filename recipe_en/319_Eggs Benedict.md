# Eggs Benedict
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 High-gluten flour 280g
🥛 Milk 250ml
🧂 Dry yeast 4g
🍬 Granulated sugar 15g
🧂 Salt 4g (2g for muffin dough, 2g for Hollandaise sauce)
🧈 Melted butter, as needed (for muffin dough)
🌽 Cornmeal, as needed (for coating dough surface)
🥚 Pasteurized eggs 2 (1 for poached egg, 1 for Hollandaise sauce)
🥓 Pork belly bacon, as needed
🍋 Lemon juice 5ml
🍶 White vinegar 15ml (for poaching eggs)
🌿 Chives, as needed (for garnish)
🥬 Arugula, as needed (for garnish)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl (glass bowl)
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍳 Frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🍲 Saucepan
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥚 Small bowl
🫕 Heatproof bowl and hot water pot
🥄 Whisk
🕳️ Strainer
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Preparation Steps

### English Muffins
1. Sprinkle dry yeast into milk and let it sit to activate.
2. In another bowl, mix high-gluten flour, granulated sugar, and 2g salt. Pour in the milk-yeast mixture and a little melted butter, stirring until no dry flour remains.
3. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 30 minutes. Fold the dough from the edges to the center, turning the smooth side up; fold once every 30 minutes, three times in total, until the surface is smooth.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and ferment until doubled in volume, with the interior full of air pockets.
5. Remove the dough, punch out the air, divide evenly into 6 portions, and roll each into a ball.
6. Cover with plastic wrap and proof a second time until 1.5 times the original size.
7. After proofing, coat both sides of the dough balls with cornmeal.
8. Place a clean, oil-free frying pan over low heat. Add the dough balls, lightly press with a spatula, cover, and cook each side for 4–5 minutes until lightly golden.
9. Let cool, then insert a fork into the center from the side and twist apart.

### Poached Eggs
1. Bring water to a boil in a saucepan, add white vinegar, and stir the water with a spoon to create a vortex.
2. Crack an egg into a small bowl and gently slide it into the center of the vortex near the water surface.
3. Turn off the heat, cover, and let sit for 3 minutes until the egg white is set and the yolk is runny.
4. Remove with a slotted spoon and cool in cold water; set aside.

### Fried Bacon
1. Pat the bacon dry with kitchen paper towels.
2. Fry in a frying pan until crispy and golden, then remove.
3. Using the remaining oil in the pan, toast the cut side of the English muffin halves until lightly browned.

### Hollandaise Sauce
1. Place the yolk of one pasteurized egg in a heatproof bowl set over a hot water bath. Add 2g salt and whisk rapidly with a whisk until the mixture is fluffy and light in color.
2. Gradually add the melted butter in small amounts, whisking vigorously after each addition until fully emulsified.
3. Finally, squeeze in the lemon juice and continue whisking until smooth, thick, and slowly flowing.

### Assembly
1. Take half an English muffin and layer with fried bacon and a poached egg.
2. Drizzle with Hollandaise sauce, sprinkle with chopped chives and arugula for garnish, and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in calories and cholesterol, with bacon and Hollandaise sauce rich in fat. The runny yolk of the poached egg requires pasteurized eggs to ensure safety. It is recommended for occasional consumption; pairing with black coffee can help balance the greasiness.
