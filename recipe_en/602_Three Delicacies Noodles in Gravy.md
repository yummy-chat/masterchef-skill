# Three Delicacies Noodles in Gravy
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Dried black fungus 10g (about 100g after soaking)  
🌸 Dried daylily buds 30g (about 80g after soaking, rinse thoroughly with multiple water changes, remove tough stems)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms 15g (or 100g fresh shiitake mushrooms)  
🥩 Pork belly 150g (with alternating fat and lean, sliced thinly)  
🥚 Eggs 2 (beaten well)  
🧅 Scallions 30g (chopped, half for sautéing, half for finishing)  
🫚 Ginger 15g (cut into thick shreds)  
⭐ Star anise 1  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more for scrambling eggs)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 3 tablespoons (about 30g)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1.5 tablespoons (about 15g)  
🥄 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon (about 20g)  
🫘 Soybean paste 2/3 tablespoon (about 35g, not Pixian bean paste)  
🍬 Sugar 1 teaspoon (about 5g)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon (or MSG) 1 teaspoon (about 3g)  
🥔 Potato starch 1 level tablespoon (about 10g, mixed with 5 tablespoons water)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a pinch (about 2g, for making peppercorn oil)  
🫒 Cooking oil 3 tablespoons (about 30g, for drizzling hot oil)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥘 Small saucepan (or small pot)  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowls, basins  

## Steps  
1. Soak dried black fungus and dried daylily buds in water in advance. Rinse daylily buds multiple times with fresh water, remove tough stems, and cut into 1-inch segments; shred the black fungus; slice fresh shiitake mushrooms; chop scallions, dividing into two portions; cut ginger into thick shreds; thinly slice pork belly; beat eggs well. Set aside pork belly slices, ginger shreds, and star anise together.  
**Note**: Daylily buds contain colchicine, so they must be thoroughly rinsed with multiple water changes.  

2. Heat a wok over high heat until very hot, then add a generous amount of cooking oil and heat until the oil surface begins to smoke. Slowly pour in the beaten eggs while quickly stirring with chopsticks, cooking until the eggs are fluffy and slightly golden on the surface. Remove and set aside.  

3. Without cleaning the wok, use the residual oil in the pan over high heat to add pork belly slices, ginger shreds, and star anise. Stir-fry until the edges of the pork belly are slightly charred and fat has rendered.  
**Note**: Must stir-fry thoroughly to remove any gamey smell and enhance the aroma.  

4. Add 1/2 teaspoon of thirteen-spice powder and let it bloom briefly, then add half of the chopped scallions and stir-fry until fragrant. Reduce the heat to medium, pour light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce along the edge of the wok, stir-fry quickly to develop a savory aroma, then add soybean paste and stir well.  
**Note**: When adding paste-type seasonings, reduce heat to medium to prevent burning.  

5. Increase heat to high, add shiitake mushroom slices and stir-fry for about 1 minute, then add two bowls of water and bring to a boil.  

6. Once the soup is boiling, add sugar and chicken bouillon for seasoning. Taste at this point; since the seasonings already contain salt, additional salt is generally unnecessary.  

7. Add the daylily bud segments, shredded black fungus, and the scrambled eggs, stir to combine, bring to a boil again, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  

8. Turn the heat to high, stir the prepared starch slurry again, then drizzle it into the pot in three batches, gently pushing with the back of a spoon after each addition. Only add the next batch when the gravy has thickened slightly. The finished gravy should be glossy and slightly thinner than yogurt.  
**Note**: Keep the heat on high and add the slurry in batches to prevent the gravy from turning into a paste.  

9. Transfer the gravy to a basin and sprinkle the remaining half of the chopped scallions on top. In a separate small pot, add 3 tablespoons of cooking oil and Sichuan peppercorns, fry over medium-low heat until the peppercorns turn a deep purple color, then remove them. Continue heating the oil until it starts to smoke, then quickly pour the hot oil over the scallions to release their fragrance, and stir to combine.  
**Note**: Do not burn the Sichuan peppercorns, or they will taste bitter.  

10. Place cooked noodles in a bowl, ladle the Three Delicacies Gravy over the top, mix well, and serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This Three Delicacies Noodles in Gravy is rich in ingredients, with mushrooms and pork belly providing quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for the whole family. Note that daylily buds must be thoroughly rinsed to remove colchicine, and when thickening the gravy, stir quickly over high heat to prevent clumping.
