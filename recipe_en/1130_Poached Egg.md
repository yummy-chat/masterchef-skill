# Poached Egg
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Edible raw eggs, as needed  
🍶 White vinegar, a small amount (helps coagulate egg whites)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Pot (for boiling water)  
🥄 Spoon (for creating a whirlpool)  
🔲 Slotted spoon (for straining egg whites and removing the finished egg)  
⏲️ Timer  

## Steps
1. Crack the egg into a slotted spoon, draining off the thin egg white, retaining only the thick egg liquid.  
   **Key Point**: Removing the thin egg white helps achieve a rounder and more attractive finished shape.  
2. Fill a pot with water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, keeping the water at a gentle simmer, and add a small amount of white vinegar.  
   **Note**: There should be small bubbles on the water's surface, but avoid vigorous boiling.  
3. Use a spoon to quickly stir the water in the pot, creating a whirlpool.  
4. Hold the strained egg close to the water's surface and slowly pour it into the center of the whirlpool.  
5. Cook for about 3 minutes, until the egg white is fully set and envelops the yolk, with the yolk remaining runny.  
6. If not serving immediately, use a slotted spoon to lift the egg out and submerge it in cold water to stop the residual heat from cooking and prevent the yolk from firming up.  
7. Remove and drain off the water. Serve with bread, salad, or as desired.  

## Health Advice  
Poached eggs have a delicate white and a runny yolk, rich in high-quality protein and lecithin, making them suitable for fitness or blood sugar control diets. Use eggs that are safe to eat raw and strictly control the 3-minute cooking time to prevent the yolk from becoming overcooked.
