# Purple Perilla Pan-Fried Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork spare ribs about 300g (chopped into small pieces)  
🌿 Purple perilla 1 small bunch (remove tough stems, cut into sections)  
🧅 Onion half (cut into chunks)  
🫑 Green bell pepper 1 (cut into rings)  
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves  
🧂 Oyster sauce to taste  
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍬 White sugar to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil to taste  
🍾 White liquor (Guangdong “8+1”) to taste  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wooden spatula or chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Wash ribs and drain. Add ginger slices, garlic, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and a little cooking oil. Mix well and marinate for about 15 minutes.  
**Key point**: While mixing, try to let the seasoning penetrate, resulting in more flavorful and tender meat after marinating.  

2. Heat the wok, add oil. Once hot, put in the marinated ribs, cover the wok lid, and pan-fry over low heat.  
   **Time**: About 5-8 minutes until both sides are evenly golden brown and the surface is slightly charred.  
**Key point**: Low heat with the lid on locks in the ribs' juices, preventing the surface from burning while the inside remains undercooked.  

3. Add onion and green bell pepper, turn to medium heat, and stir-fry evenly. Splash white liquor along the edge of the wok, and continue stir-frying for about 2 minutes until the onion softens and the alcohol smell evaporates.  
**Note**: White liquor removes gamey smell and enhances aroma. Stir-fry until the alcohol smell is completely gone.  

4. Add the purple perilla sections and quickly stir-fry for about 1 minute until the perilla darkens in color and releases its fragrance. Immediately turn off the heat and serve.  
**Key point**: Add purple perilla at the end to avoid losing its aroma from prolonged cooking, maintaining its fresh and tender texture.  

## Health Advice  
Purple perilla not only removes gamey smell and enhances aroma, but also aids digestion. It is suitable for people with weak stomachs to consume in moderation. Note that ribs should only be pan-fried until slightly charred; avoid overheating to prevent the formation of harmful substances.
