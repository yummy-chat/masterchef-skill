# Pan-Fried Oysters with Eggs  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Oysters – about 500g (shucked oyster meat, gently rinsed under running water and checked for shell fragments)  
🥚 Eggs – 4 (beaten and set aside)  
🧅 Garlic Sprouts – to taste (finely chopped, for mixing into the batter)  
🧅 Scallion Whites – to taste (finely chopped)  
🌶️ Red Chili Pepper – a small amount (sliced for garnish, optional)  
🥄 Sweet Potato Starch – 3 teaspoons  
💧 Water – a small amount  
🧂 Oyster Sauce – to taste  
🧂 White Pepper – to taste  
🧂 Salt – to taste  
🫒 Cooking Oil – to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or Frying Pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Mixing Bowl (for preparing the oyster batter)  
🥢 Mixing Utensils  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Prepare the oyster batter: Drain the oysters, then add the chopped garlic sprouts, chopped scallion whites, oyster sauce, white pepper, and salt. Mix well. Add 3 teaspoons of sweet potato starch and a small amount of water, stirring until a flowable batter forms.  
**Key Point**: Sweet potato starch helps create a more translucent and crispy outer layer after frying.  
2. Place the wok over high heat and add enough cooking oil. Heat until the oil temperature rises.  
3. Pour in the oyster batter and fry over medium heat. Gently push the batter with a spatula to spread it into a round pancake shape. Fry until the bottom is set and appears translucent.  
4. Pour the beaten eggs evenly over the surface of the oyster pancake. Tilt the wok to help the egg spread. Wait until the egg is mostly set.  
5. Perform a large flip to turn the entire pancake over. Continue frying until both sides are golden brown and the edges are crispy.  
6. Transfer to a plate and garnish with red chili pepper slices if desired.  

## Health Tips  
Oysters are rich in zinc and high-quality protein, making them suitable for people needing zinc supplementation or those recovering from staying up late. However, they are relatively high in cholesterol, so individuals with high blood lipids should control their intake. Using garlic sprouts and oyster sauce for seasoning can reduce the amount of salt needed, making the dish healthier.
