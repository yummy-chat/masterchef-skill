# Steamed Turbot  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (Jan–Apr), Summer (Apr–Aug)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 1 turbot  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for marinating scallion segments and garnish scallion shreds)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for marinating ginger slices and garnish ginger shreds)  
🌶️ Red chili pepper, as needed (for garnish chili shreds)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (a thin layer to rub over the fish body)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (to brush the fish and for the final hot oil drizzle)  
🫙 Steamed fish soy sauce, as needed (for final seasoning)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍽️ Plate  
🥢 Chopsticks (to place under the plate)  
🖌️ Brush  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking the three shreds)  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Instructions  
1. Clean the turbot: remove the internal organs from the side slit on the back of the fish.  
2. Score both sides of the fish with diagonal or straight cuts.  
3. Place two chopsticks crossed on the plate, then lay the fish flat on top.  
4. Rub the fish surface with a thin layer of salt, then brush with cooking oil. Add scallion segments and ginger slices for marinating.  
5. Prepare the "three shreds": stack scallion leaves, ginger slices, and red chili slices together, then quickly slice into fine shreds. Soak them in clean water until use.  
6. Place the plate with the fish into a steamer with boiling water.  
   **Note**: The water must be boiling before placing the fish in.  
7. Steam over high heat for about 8 minutes.  
   **Doneness check**: The fish flesh should be fully white, and the eyes should protrude.  
8. Remove the steamed fish and discard the fishy liquid that has collected on the plate.  
9. Drizzle steamed fish soy sauce over the fish.  
10. Take an appropriate amount of the three shreds from the water, drain, and arrange them on top of the fish.  
11. Heat cooking oil in a spoon until smoking, then quickly and evenly drizzle the hot oil over the three shreds on the fish.  
    **Key point**: The hot oil releases the aroma of the three shreds and enhances the flavor of the steamed fish soy sauce.  
12. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips  
Turbot is low in fat and high in protein, rich in Omega-3, making it suitable for weight loss diets and cardiovascular health. The short steaming time preserves nutrients; be sure to discard the fishy liquid before seasoning.
