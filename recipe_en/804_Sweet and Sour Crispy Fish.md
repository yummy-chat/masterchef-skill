# Sweet and Sour Crispy Fish
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (deboned, meat sliced diagonally into pieces)
🥕 Carrot, as needed (finely chopped)
🍅 Tomato, as needed (finely chopped)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (sliced)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (chopped or crushed)
🧂 Salt, as needed (only for washing fish pieces, not for marinating)
🍶 Yellow wine, a little
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed
⭐️ Star anise, 1 piece
🌳 Cinnamon stick, 1 piece
🍃 Bay leaf, 1 piece
🟢 Cumin seeds, a small pinch
🍅 Ketchup, as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a spoon
🦐 Hoisin sauce, 1 spoon
🟫 Five-spice powder, as needed
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed
🧂 Soy sauce, 3 spoons
🍾 White vinegar, as needed
🍯 Honey, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying fish and making sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🧻 Paper towels
🍳 Wok
🫕 Deep frying pot
🥄 Ladle/wok spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. **Prepare the fish**: Debone the grass carp and slice the meat diagonally into evenly sized pieces.
2. **Wash the fish pieces**: Place fish pieces in a bowl, add salt, and rub well to remove surface slime. Rinse thoroughly with water twice until the fish looks translucent. Drain well.
   **Key point**: Rubbing with salt effectively removes fishy smell; rinsing leaves the fish cleaner.
3. **Dry the fish pieces**: Use paper towels to completely dry the surface of the fish pieces.
4. **Marinate the fish pieces**: Add a little yellow wine, white pepper powder, and light soy sauce to the dried fish pieces. Gently mix and marinate for about 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Do not add salt in this step, or the fish skin may stick during frying.
5. **Prepare the aromatics**: Finely chop the carrot and tomato; slice the scallion and ginger; chop or crush the garlic.
6. **Prepare the sauce**:
   - Heat a wok over high heat, add a base layer of oil, then stir-fry the scallion, ginger, garlic, chopped carrot, and chopped tomato until fragrant.
   - Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and cumin seeds. Continue stir-frying until fragrant.
   - Add the ketchup and stir-fry over high heat until red oil releases.
   - Add enough water, then add the hoisin sauce, oyster sauce, five-spice powder, rock sugar, and soy sauce. Stir well, then reduce heat to low and simmer slowly.
   - When the sauce thickens to a rice-gruel consistency, add the white vinegar and honey, and stir to combine.
   - Strain the sauce through a fine strainer to remove solids, reserving the clean sauce for later use.
   **Key point**: Ensure the ketchup is stir-fried until it turns red; straining makes the sauce smooth and glossy.
7. **Fry the fish pieces**:
   - Heat oil in a deep frying pot to 160°C (six-tenths hot). Add the marinated fish pieces one by one. Fry until set and lightly golden, then remove.
   - Raise the oil temperature to 180°C (seven-tenths hot). Return all the fish pieces to the oil for a second fry. Fry until the skin is golden and crispy, then quickly remove and drain excess oil.
   **Key point**: The second fry is essential for a crispy skin; control the oil temperature to avoid burning.
8. **Coat and plate**: While the fried fish pieces are still hot, pour the strained sauce over them. Use chopsticks to quickly toss the fish pieces so each piece is evenly coated with sauce. Let the fish soak in the sauce for 5-6 minutes to fully absorb the liquid, then remove and pile onto a serving plate.
   **Key point**: The fish must be hot when mixed with the sauce so the heat helps the skin absorb the sauce, achieving a crispy outside, tender inside, and juicy result.

## Health Advice
Grass carp is rich in protein, and the ketchup provides lycopene, making this dish suitable for those needing an energy boost or appetite stimulation. However, as the fish is deep-fried, the calorie content is higher. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. Control the oil temperature during frying to avoid excessive fat absorption.
