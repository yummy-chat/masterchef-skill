# Black Pepper Chicken Nuggets

Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken breast 1000g  
🥚 Egg 1 (whole egg, for the filling)  
🥚 Egg whites 2 (for the filling)  
🫒 Cooking oil 60g (added to the meat mixture)  
🌾 Starch 60g (for the filling)  
🥚 Eggs 2 (for coating, beaten)  
🌾 Flour appropriate amount (about 1 bowl, low-gluten or all-purpose, for coating)  
🧂 Salt 10g  
🍬 Sugar 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons  
⚫ Black pepper powder 1 tablespoon  
⚪ White pepper powder 1 tablespoon  
🫚 Ginger powder (or ginger juice) 1 tablespoon

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍽️ Plate (lined with parchment paper or plastic wrap)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Oil pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the chicken**: Cut 1000g of chicken breast into small pieces, then manually chop into a fine paste.  
   **Key point**: Chopping by hand retains more meat texture than using a meat grinder, resulting in a better mouthfeel.

2. **Mix the filling**: Place the chicken paste in a large bowl. Add 1 whole egg, 2 egg whites, 60g cooking oil, 10g salt, 1 tablespoon sugar, 2 tablespoons light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon black pepper powder, 1 tablespoon white pepper powder, 1 tablespoon ginger powder, and 60g starch in sequence.  
   **Key point**: Egg whites and cooking oil make the filling smoother and more tender.

3. **Stir until sticky**: Use chopsticks to quickly stir the filling in one direction until it becomes sticky and evenly mixed.  
   **Key point**: Stirring in one direction creates stickiness in the filling, preventing it from falling apart during frying and resulting in a firm, springy texture.

4. **Shape and freeze**: Lightly oil your hands to prevent sticking. Take an appropriate amount of filling, flatten it, and shape it as desired. Place the shaped nuggets on a plate lined with parchment paper or plastic wrap. Once all are done, freeze for at least 1 hour until completely firm and solid.  
   **Key point**: Freezing solidifies the shape, making it easier to coat without deformation.

5. **Prepare the coating ingredients**: Beat 2 eggs into a bowl for the egg wash; prepare another bowl of flour.

6. **Coat the nuggets**: Remove the frozen chicken nuggets. First, evenly coat each nugget with egg wash, then coat with a thin layer of flour, gently shaking off any excess.  
   **Key point**: The coating sequence is “egg wash → flour”; if not frying immediately, return to the freezer to prevent softening.

7. **First frying**: Heat oil in a pot to 170°C (medium-low heat). Gently add the nuggets. Do not stir immediately after adding. Fry for 10-20 seconds until the outer layer sets, then gently push with chopsticks to prevent sticking.  
   **Heat/Time**: Medium-low heat, fry for about 2-3 minutes.  
   **Doneness check**: Nuggets are set and light yellow in color.  
   **Key point**: Oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw; turn frequently to ensure even cooking.

8. **Second frying for color**: After cooking, increase to high heat and re-fry for a few tens of seconds until the surface turns an even golden yellow. Quickly remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Key point**: The second frying helps expel excess oil, making the crust crispier and the color more appealing.

## Health Tips  
These black pepper chicken nuggets are high in protein, making them suitable for muscle-building individuals and those who want to eat meat without gaining weight. It is recommended to control the frying oil temperature and pay attention to the time during the second frying to avoid excessive oil intake.
