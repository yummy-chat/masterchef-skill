# Spicy Sichuan Sausage
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Recommended Season: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork Belly 1.5 kg (partially fatty)
🥩 Pork Hind Leg Meat 3.5 kg (mostly lean)
🌭 Pork Casings, as needed (pre-washed and cleaned)
🌶️ Ground Sichuan Pepper 50 g
🌶️ Chili Flakes 200 g (omit if you don’t eat spicy)
🧂 Black Pepper Powder 20 g
🧂 Ginger Powder 15 g
🧂 Salt 100 g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 50 g
🥫 Light Soy Sauce 50 g
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) 100 g (for aroma and preservation)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting Board & Chef’s Knife
🪣 Stainless Steel Pot (for mixing and marinating the meat filling)
🔄 Sausage Stuffer (or a homemade funnel made from a water bottle)
🥛 Glass Bowl
♨️ Steamer & Steaming Rack
🧷 Toothpicks (to pierce holes for venting)
🎗️ Twine (for tying sections)
🧤 Disposable Gloves

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork belly and hind leg meat, then cut into small cubes.
   **Key point**: Removing the skin ensures a better final texture.

2. Place the meat cubes in a large bowl. Add the ground Sichuan pepper, chili flakes, black pepper powder, ginger powder, salt, and granulated sugar in order. Pour in the light soy sauce and baijiu. Put on gloves and mix thoroughly by hand. Cover and marinate for 2 hours.
   **Note**: Gloves are necessary to avoid skin irritation from the chili. Marinating allows the filling to fully absorb the flavors.

3. Slip the pre-washed pork casing onto the sausage stuffer and tie a knot at the end. Fill the stuffer with the marinated meat filling and begin stuffing. Use one hand to push the stuffer while the other hand guides the casing to control the speed and tightness, filling to a moderate firmness.
   **Tip**: Avoid making the sausage too loose or too tight.

4. Once filled, tie a knot at the other end. Use twine to tie the sausage into sections. Pierce a few small holes in each section with a toothpick to release air. This helps with drying and prevents the casing from bursting during steaming.

5. Hang the stuffed sausages in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight to air-dry until they feel firm and dry to the touch.

6. After drying, rinse the sausages with clean water to remove surface dust. Place them in a cold steamer, then steam for 30 minutes once the water comes to a boil.

7. Remove the steamed sausages, slice into thick pieces, and serve. They can also be stir-fried with vegetables like green peppers.

## Health Advice
This spicy Sichuan sausage is high in salt and fat. It is recommended to eat it with plenty of vegetables. Those with cardiovascular disease should consume it sparingly. After air-drying for 8 days, steam for 30 minutes to ensure it is fully cooked and sterilized.
