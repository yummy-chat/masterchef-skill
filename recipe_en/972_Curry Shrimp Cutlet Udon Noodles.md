# Curry Shrimp Cutlet Udon Noodles
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp cutlet 2 pieces (pre-made shrimp cutlet)  
🥚 Soft-boiled egg 1 piece  
🍜 Udon noodles appropriate amount  
🥔 Potato appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🍅 Tomato appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (diced)  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (diced)  
🥦 Broccoli appropriate amount (broken into small florets)  
🍛 Curry block appropriate amount  
🌾 Flour appropriate amount (for coating shrimp cutlet)  
🥚 Egg wash appropriate amount (for coating shrimp cutlet)  
🍞 Breadcrumbs appropriate amount (for coating shrimp cutlet)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for spraying)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Electric cooking pot  
♨️ Air fryer  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Plates and bowls  
🥢 Chopsticks / spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Dice tomato, carrot, and onion; cut potato into small pieces; break broccoli into small florets; prepare pre-made shrimp cutlet, soft-boiled egg, and udon noodles.  
   **Key point**: Cut vegetables into uniform sizes for even cooking.  
2. Coat shrimp cutlet sequentially with flour, egg wash, and breadcrumbs, ensuring each layer is evenly covered. Gently press the breadcrumbs firmly with your hands after coating.  
   **Key point**: Pressing the breadcrumbs prevents the coating from falling off during baking, ensuring a complete and crispy exterior.  
3. Add a little oil to the electric cooking pot, sauté onion, tomato, and carrot dices until fragrant, then add potato pieces and stir-fry until vegetables soften.  
   **Note**: Keep the heat on medium-low throughout the stir-frying process.  
4. Add an appropriate amount of water and curry block, stir over medium-low heat until the curry block is completely dissolved, and cook until the sauce thickens.  
   **Key point**: Stir continuously during cooking to prevent the curry from sticking to the bottom; judge doneness when vegetables are soft and the sauce is thick.  
5. Place the coated shrimp cutlet in the air fryer basket, spray the surface evenly with oil, and bake at 180–200°C for 15–20 minutes until the exterior is golden and crispy.  
   **Key point**: Spraying oil enhances crispiness; flipping halfway through is recommended for even heating.  
6. Add udon noodles, broccoli, and soft-boiled egg to the cooked curry base, and heat briefly until the ingredients absorb the flavor.  
   **Key point**: Do not overcook the udon noodles; heat just until tender to avoid a mushy texture.  
7. Slice the baked shrimp cutlet and arrange it on top of the curry udon noodles.  

## Health Advice  
This curry shrimp cutlet udon noodle dish is rich in vegetables, but the shrimp cutlet, whether fried or air-fried, is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a light vegetable soup to balance the meal. The soft-boiled egg and udon noodles provide quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for a quick energy boost during busy times. Note that pre-made shrimp cutlet may contain additives, so it is best consumed occasionally.
