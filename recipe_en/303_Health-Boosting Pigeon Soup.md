# Health-Boosting Pigeon Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lean pork, appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)
🕊️ 1 pigeon (cleaned)
🌿 3-4 pieces of Codonopsis
🍂 1 slice of Angelica
🍒 A small handful of Goji berries
🧄 1 small piece of ginger (sliced)
🧂 Salt, to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫕 Clay pot (with lid)
🥣 Soup ladle

## Instructions
1. Cut the lean pork into small pieces. Place them together with the whole pigeon into a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat and let it boil for a while. Skim off any foam. Remove and rinse thoroughly with clean water. Drain and set aside.
2. Put the blanched and cleaned lean pork and pigeon into a clay pot. Add enough clean water, then add codonopsis, angelica, and ginger slices.
3. Cover the pot with a lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer slowly for about 2 hours.
   **Note**: If using an electric rice cooker, the time can be reduced to about 1 hour.
4. After the simmering time is up, open the lid and add salt to taste. Then sprinkle in the goji berries.
5. Cover the lid again and continue to simmer over low heat for about 2 minutes. Once the goji berries become soft, turn off the heat and serve.

## Health Tips
This pigeon soup is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it especially suitable for people with qi and blood deficiency, physical weakness, or those recovering from surgery. Codonopsis and angelica have warming and nourishing properties, but those with a hot constitution or who are prone to getting overheated should not consume it excessively.
