# Perfect Steamed Egg Custard
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 2 large eggs
🍖 250g meat broth (or 195g water)
🧂 1g salt
🫗 Appropriate amount of sesame oil
🧴 Appropriate amount of light soy sauce
🌿 Optional: chopped green onions

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🌀 Whisk
🥄 Measuring cup/spoon
🍳 Steamer
🍽️ Plate (larger than the bowl rim, for covering upside down)
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add the meat broth and salt, and whisk thoroughly with a whisk until the egg mixture is evenly colored and fully combined.
   **Note**: If using salted meat broth, reduce or omit the salt as needed.
2. Strain the mixed egg mixture through a sieve, or use a spoon to skim off any surface foam, then pour into a suitable steaming bowl.
3. Bring water to a boil in the steamer, place the bowl of egg mixture inside, cover the bowl rim with a plate turned upside down, reduce the heat to low, and steam for about 6 minutes.
4. After turning off the heat, do not immediately remove the lid; let it rest with residual heat for 5 minutes.
5. Remove the steamed egg custard, drizzle with an appropriate amount of light soy sauce and sesame oil, and sprinkle with chopped green onions as desired.
   **Doneness check**: The surface should be smooth, the interior tender and set; a gentle shake of the bowl should cause the whole custard to quiver.

## Health Tips
Eggs are rich in high-quality protein. Pairing them with meat broth adds calcium and enhances flavor. Steaming is a low-fat, healthy cooking method, making this dish especially suitable for children and the elderly to supplement nutrition. Note that straining and covering the bowl with a plate help prevent a honeycomb texture, resulting in a more delicate mouthfeel.
