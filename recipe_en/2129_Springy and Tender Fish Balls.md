# Springy and Tender Fish Balls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Basa fillet 400g (thaw, rinse with ice water, pat dry, cut into pieces, and refrigerate)
🦑 Baby squid 100g (use only the white body part, pat dry, and dice)
🧅 Scallion 20g (cut into pieces)
🫚 Ginger 20g (cut into pieces)
💧 Water 20g (for blending with scallion and ginger)
🥚 Egg white 1
🧂 Salt 8g (reduce at home as needed)
🍬 Sugar 2g
🧂 MSG 1g
🌶️ White pepper powder 1g
🐷 Lard 20g (can substitute with vegetable oil)
🌾 Sweet potato starch 20g (sweet potato starch recommended; can also use tapioca starch or potato starch)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🧻 Paper towels
🌀 Food processor
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Slotted spoon
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🕸️ Strainer
🫙 Plastic wrap
🧤 Gloves (optional, for mixing)

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. **Prepare the fish**: Thaw frozen basa fillet in ice water below 10°C. Use paper towels to thoroughly pat dry all surface moisture. Cut into small pieces and refrigerate.
   **Key point**: Keep everything cold throughout. Do not over-rinse during cleaning. Must pat dry completely.
2. **Prepare the squid**: Remove the tentacles for other use. Take the white body part, rinse in ice water and pat dry. Dice finely, then process in a food processor into a smooth squid paste.
   **Key point**: Baby squid has strong gelling properties, effectively improving the elasticity of the basa.
3. **Make scallion-ginger juice**: Cut scallion and ginger into pieces. Blend with 20g water in a food processor into a paste. Strain through a fine mesh to extract concentrated juice.
   **Key point**: Use the extracted juice rather than adding water directly. This reduces extra moisture, making the fish balls springier.

### Cooking and Poaching
1. **Chop the fish paste**: Remove the chilled basa pieces. Finely chop by hand with a knife until smooth, with no large chunks visible.
2. **Mix and stir until stiff**: Combine the basa paste and squid paste in a mixing bowl. Add the scallion-ginger juice. Stir continuously in one direction until noticeable resistance develops.
   **Key point**: Always stir in the same direction to avoid breaking down the protein structure.
3. **Add base seasonings**: Add salt, sugar, MSG, and white pepper powder. Continue stirring in the same direction until resistance increases significantly and the paste becomes sticky.
4. **Add auxiliaries and knead by throwing**: Add egg white, lard, and sweet potato starch. Mix well. Then, pick up the paste with your hands and repeatedly throw it back into the bowl about 100 times, until the paste is visibly elastic, very sticky, and feels heavy to handle.
   **Key point**: Throwing is the core step for achieving a springy texture. Do not skip.
5. **Chill and rest**: Cover the paste with plastic wrap and refrigerate for about 30 minutes, until the temperature drops back below 10°C. This helps with shaping later.
6. **Shape and drop into water**: Fill the pot with enough cold water. Scoop up a handful of paste and squeeze it through your thumb and forefinger to form a ball. Use a spoon to scrape it off into the cold water.
   **Key point**: Must drop into cold water. Do not stir before the balls are set.
7. **Gently poach at low temperature**: Heat over medium-low heat slowly. When small bubbles appear around the edge of the pot, pour in a bowl of cold water. Repeat this "adding cold water" step a total of three times, keeping the water temperature between 80–90°C to cook the fish balls through.
   **Key point**: Adding cold water prevents the temperature from rising too high, ensuring the fish balls cook evenly inside and out.
8. **Cool and set**: After the third addition of cold water, when bubbles reappear, remove the fish balls with a slotted spoon. Immediately plunge them into ice water or cold water to cool quickly, which preserves their elasticity.

## Health Tips
These fish balls use basa combined with squid to enhance elasticity. The calorie content is manageable and they are rich in high-quality protein, making them suitable for weight loss or for people with poor teeth. Operating at low temperatures throughout and the "intermittent cold water addition" cooking method prevent a loose texture.
