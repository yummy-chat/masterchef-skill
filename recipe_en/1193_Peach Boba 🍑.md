# Peach Boba 🍑

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍑 Peaches 2–3 (use the peel and some of the flesh)  
🌾 Tapioca starch 100 g  
💧 Water 80 g (for kneading dough; also prepare extra for cooking boba and boiling the peel liquid)  
🥛 Milk as needed (for the base)  
🍬 White sugar as needed (for marinating the fruit, making jam, and boiling the peel liquid)  
🍋 Lemon juice as needed (added to the peel liquid for color fixing and flavor)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board (for cutting and scoring)  
🥣 Hand blender (to puree the peach flesh)  
♨️ Microwave (for heating jam and boiling the peel liquid)  
🍽️ Perforated steamer tray and scraper (for shaping the boba)  
🍲 Cooking pot (for boiling the boba)  
🥄 Strainer (for scooping out the boba)  
🍶 Bowls, dishes, plastic wrap (for serving and preventing sticking)

## Instructions
1. Wash the peaches, score the skin with a knife in a cross pattern, peel off the skin, and cut the peeled flesh into small pieces. Take some of the peach flesh and puree it into a smooth paste using a hand blender.  
2. Place the peach peels in a bowl, add white sugar, water, and a little lemon juice. Heat in the microwave on high until the liquid turns pink.  
3. Add white sugar to the peach puree and heat in the microwave until it becomes soft, thick, and jam-like to make peach jam.  
4. While hot, pour the peach peel pigment water into the tapioca starch. Quickly stir into flakes, then knead into a smooth pink dough.  
   **Key point**: The water must be hot to fully gelatinize the starch, allowing it to form a dough; the more thoroughly the dough is scalded, the chewier the boba will be.  
5. Flatten the dough onto a perforated steamer tray. Press with a scraper to force the dough through the holes, forming small granules. Collect the granules and boil them in boiling water until they all float to the surface.  
6. Remove the boba and immediately soak them in cold water to cool, then drain.  
   **Note**: Cooling in cold water makes the boba more chewy and smooth.  
7. In a bowl, add the cooled boba, pour in enough milk, and spoon in the peach jam. Gently mix and serve.

## Health Tips
Peaches are rich in vitamins and dietary fiber. Homemade boba is healthier than commercially available milk tea and is a great summer treat. However, tapioca starch has a higher glycemic index, so people with diabetes or those on a weight loss plan should control their portion sizes.
