# Green Pepper, Ham, and Egg Stir-Fry
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Spiral green peppers 200g (about 3, choose thin-skinned ones)
🌭 Ham sausage 100g (1 piece, regular supermarket ham sausage)
🥚 Eggs 150g (4 small or 3 large)
🌶️ Small chili peppers to taste (about 6, omit if you don’t like spice)
🧄 Garlic 30g (4 cloves, sliced)

🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for sautéing green peppers and frying eggs)
🥓 Lard as needed (for stir-frying ham; use regular oil if unavailable)
🫗 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons (about 20g)
🫗 Dark soy sauce a few drops (about 2g)
🥫 Oyster sauce 1 heaping tablespoon (about 20g)
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon (about 2g)
🧂 MSG 1 teaspoon (about 3g)
🌾 Cornstarch about 5g (cornstarch or potato starch)
💧 Water about 50g (for the starch slurry)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Small bowls (for beating eggs and mixing starch slurry)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the green peppers**: Pat the washed spiral green peppers flat with the knife blade, then cut into small rolling knife-cut pieces.
   **Key point**: Patting them flat helps the green peppers absorb flavor more easily.

2. **Prepare the other ingredients**: Slice the ham sausage diagonally; slice the garlic; slice the small chili peppers diagonally.

3. **Prepare the eggs**: Crack the eggs into a bowl, keeping the yolks intact. Do not whisk them.
   **Note**: Beginners are advised to crack the eggs into a bowl first to avoid burning your hands when adding them directly to the pan.

4. **Prepare the starch slurry**: In a small bowl, combine half a level tablespoon of starch with 5 tablespoons of water. Stir well until the ratio is about 1:10. Stir again before use to prevent settling.

5. **Dry-fry the green peppers**: Heat the empty wok over high heat until your hand feels hot when held 5cm above the surface. Spray in a thin layer of oil. Add the green peppers. Keep the heat high, and while patting them with the spatula, stir-fry until the peppers soften and develop a tiger-skin pattern. Then remove them from the wok.
   **Key point**: Do not over-soften or burn the peppers, as they will become bitter.

6. **Fry the eggs**: Clean the wok and heat it over high heat. Add a small amount of oil to coat the surface. When the oil begins to smoke, pour in the egg mixture. Immediately break the yolks with the spatula for faster cooking. Once the bottom is set, flip and fry both sides until golden brown. Cut into large chunks with the spatula and remove from the wok.
   **Note**: Fry over high heat throughout. Overcooking the eggs makes them more fragrant. Do not use too much oil for frying the eggs.

7. **Stir-fry the ham and aromatics**: Turn off the heat to let the wok cool slightly, then add lard. Turn the heat back to high. Once the lard melts and begins to smoke slightly, add the ham sausage. Stir-fry over high heat until the edges are golden brown. Then add the sliced garlic and small chili peppers, and stir-fry until fragrant.

8. **Combine with the green peppers**: Add the dry-fried green peppers. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok to release the aroma with the high heat. Stir-fry until evenly mixed.

9. **Add the eggs and season**: Add the fried egg chunks. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add a few drops of dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, and MSG. Stir-fry over low heat until well combined.

10. **Thicken and serve**: Turn the heat back to high. Stir the starch slurry again to recombine, then pour it into the wok. Wait 5–6 seconds for the starch to gelatinize, then stir-fry quickly until the sauce becomes glossy and thick, evenly coating the ingredients. Serve immediately.

## Health Suggestions
This dish is rich in protein and calories, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or who need a quick energy boost. The green peppers provide vitamin C, but the ham sausage is high in salt, so individuals sensitive to blood pressure should control their intake.
