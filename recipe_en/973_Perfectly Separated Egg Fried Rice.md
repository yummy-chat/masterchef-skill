# Perfectly Separated Egg Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Leftover rice, as needed
🥚 Eggs, as needed (egg whites and yolks separated)
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (diced)
🥕 Carrot, as needed (diced)
🥫 Canned luncheon meat, as needed (diced)
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌶️ Black pepper, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Spoon
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the leftover rice in a bowl, add a small amount of water, and mix by hand until the grains are loose and not sticking together.
2. Separate the egg whites and yolks. Pour the yolks into the prepared rice and quickly mix by hand until each grain of rice is evenly coated with the egg mixture.
3. Add a small amount of oil to the pan and heat over medium-low heat. Pour in the egg whites and quickly scramble until cooked. Remove and set aside.
4. Without adding more oil, directly pour the yolk-coated rice into the same pan. Keep the heat on medium and stir frequently until the rice grains bounce on their own in the pan and are perfectly separated.
5. Add the cooked egg whites, diced cucumber, diced carrot, and diced luncheon meat. Stir-fry until evenly combined.
6. Add salt and black pepper. Stir-fry quickly and remove from heat immediately.

## Health Advice
The key is to coat leftover rice with egg yolks, which ensures each grain is separate and reduces the risk of sticking. This method is suitable for those who need to control oil intake. Adding cucumber and carrot increases dietary fiber. However, luncheon meat is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should use it sparingly.
