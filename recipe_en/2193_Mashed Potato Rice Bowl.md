# Mashed Potato Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, appropriate amount (cut into rolling chunks)
🍆 Eggplants, appropriate amount (cut into rolling chunks)
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount
🥩 Ground pork, appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (add before serving)
🌶️ Thai bird's eye chili, appropriate amount (add before serving)
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (add before serving)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🥣 Sweet bean sauce (or Pixian doubanjiang), appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion and ginger, appropriate amount
🍶 Huangjiu (or cooking wine), appropriate amount
🥢 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥢 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount
🍄 Matsutake seasoning, appropriate amount
🌫️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
💧 Water, appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Steamer / Steaming pot
🫗 Lid
🥄 Potato masher or spoon
🍜 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash potatoes and eggplants, cut into rolling chunks; rinse rice, add appropriate water, and cook.
   **Note**: Cut chunks into uniform sizes for even steaming.
2. Place potato and eggplant chunks in a steamer, sprinkle with a little salt on the surface. Steam over medium heat after it comes to a boil for about 25 minutes until soft.
   **Key Point**: Adding salt enhances the base flavor.
3. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a wok over high heat. Stir-fry the ground pork over medium-high heat until fully changed color, loose, and dry.
   **Key Point**: The meat should be broken apart and fried dry.
4. Add minced scallion, ginger, and sweet bean sauce. Stir-fry until the sauce melts and red oil appears.
   **Key Point**: Releasing red oil is key to a rich, aromatic meat sauce.
5. Splash huangjiu along the edge of the wok, then add light and dark soy sauce, stirring evenly.
   **Key Point**: Utilizing the high heat at the wok edge activates the aroma of the sauce and wine.
6. Pour in the steamed potatoes and eggplants, mash them with a masher or spoon, and mix well with the meat sauce.
   **Key Point**: Retaining some chunky texture improves the mouthfeel.
7. Add an appropriate amount of water and stir. Reduce heat to low and simmer for about 5 minutes until the sauce thickens and flavors meld.
   **Key Point**: Adjust water quantity to preference—add less for a thicker sauce, more for a soupier result.
8. Invert or spread the cooked rice over the meat sauce, cover with a lid, and simmer over the lowest heat for about 5 minutes so the rice fully absorbs the sauce aroma.
9. Before serving, sprinkle chopped cilantro, chili rings, and minced garlic on top. Thoroughly mix the rice, toppings, and the bottom layer of mashed potato meat sauce together.
   **Key Point**: Cilantro, chili, and garlic are essential. Stir until every grain of rice is coated with the sauce.

## Health Advice
This mashed potato rice bowl is rich in carbohydrates and dietary fiber, suitable for fast energy replenishment by manual laborers or those seeking muscle gain. However, those with high blood sugar should control their portion size. It is recommended to pair it with a serving of leafy greens to balance nutrition and aid digestion.
