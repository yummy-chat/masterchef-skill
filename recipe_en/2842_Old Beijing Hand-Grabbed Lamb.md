# Old Beijing Hand-Grabbed Lamb  
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Lamb rib rack, including the brisket section, about 13 ribs  
🥕 Ivory white radish, to taste (cut into rolling chunks)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste (cut into rolling chunks)  
🌿 Celery, to taste (lightly crushed and cut into sections)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (cut into chunks)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (whole stalks, some finely chopped)  
🧅 Scallion whites, to taste (finely chopped)  

🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🌶️ Dried chili segments, to taste  
🥢 Cooked soy sauce (dark soy sauce), to taste (boiled and cooled)  
🍶 Rice vinegar, to taste  
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste  
🧄 Minced garlic, to taste  
⚪ White pepper powder, to taste  
🌿 Bay leaves, a few (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, wooden cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Iron wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🥄 Slotted spoon, soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the whole lamb rib rack into segments of two ribs each along the bone joints, and rinse thoroughly with water.  
**Note**: Find the bone joints when cutting to make it easier to separate the meat from the bone after cooking.  

2. Prepare the soaking aromatics: Lightly crush the celery and cut into sections, cut the onion into chunks, and wash the whole cilantro stalks.  
3. Place the lamb ribs in a bowl, add enough water to submerge them, then add the celery, onion, cilantro, Sichuan peppercorns, and a little salt. Gently stir and soak for at least 3 hours.  
**Key**: Fully draw out the blood water, allowing the vegetable aromas to infuse into the meat.  

4. Blanching: Place the soaked lamb ribs in a pot with cold water. Add white radish chunks, a little celery leaves, and Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat, continuously skimming off any foam until the broth is clear.  
**Key**: The foam must be completely skimmed off to ensure a clear and sweet broth.  

5. Braising: Transfer the blanched lamb ribs to a pressure cooker. Add water or the strained blanching broth, then add carrot chunks, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, celery, and a little salt. Once the pressure cooker reaches pressure, reduce the heat to low and cook for 30 minutes. If using a regular pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours.  
6. Prepare two dipping sauces:  
   - **Three-Oil Sauce**: In a bowl, combine finely chopped cilantro, finely chopped scallion whites, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Add the cooled cooked soy sauce and mix well.  
   - **Chili Oil**: In a bowl, combine finely chopped cilantro, white pepper powder, and minced garlic. In a separate pan, heat oil and fry the dried chili segments until fragrant. Pour the hot oil and chilies into the bowl, then add the cooled cooked soy sauce.  
7. Remove the lamb ribs and arrange on a serving plate. Drizzle a little of the cooking broth over them to keep moist. Serve with the two dipping sauces.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein, but the lamb and dipping sauces are high in fat. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables. People who are overweight or have high blood pressure should watch their salt intake.
