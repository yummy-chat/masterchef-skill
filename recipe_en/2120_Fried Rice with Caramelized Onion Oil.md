# Fried Rice with Caramelized Onion Oil
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Onion, about 1 piece (diced)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers, as needed (sliced into rings)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed
🍚 Rice, about 1 bowl (leftover cold rice recommended)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 2 parts
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 part
🥄 Oyster sauce, 1 part
🍬 White sugar, 1 part
💧 Water, 1 part
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (neutral-flavored vegetable oil recommended)
🫒 Sesame oil, a small amount (added just before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Dice the onion into evenly sized pieces, slice the green and red chili peppers into rings, and mince the garlic.
   **Key point**: Uniform onion dice ensures even cooking.
2. In a small bowl, combine light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, and water in a ratio of 2:1:1:1:1. Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved to make the sauce.
   **Key point**: Preparing the sauce in advance prevents scrambling during cooking and ensures balanced flavor.

### Cooking Phase
1. Place the wok over heat and add enough cooking oil. When the oil reaches about 50% hot, add the diced onion and stir-fry over medium-low heat.
   **Key point**: Fry until the edges of the onion turn golden brown and the pieces become soft and fragrant. Remove the onion and set aside, leaving the oil in the wok. Achieving a caramelized aroma is essential for the "onion oil" flavor.
2. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the chili pepper rings, minced garlic, and white sesame seeds. Stir-fry quickly over medium-low heat until fragrant.
3. Pour the prepared sauce into the wok, increase to medium heat, bring to a boil, and stir rapidly to combine.
   **Key point**: Stir promptly after adding the sauce to prevent uneven saltiness or scorching.
4. Add the rice to the wok. Over medium heat, quickly break it apart and stir-fry so that each grain is evenly coated with the sauce, appearing glossy and distinct.
5. Return the reserved caramelized onion to the wok, increase to high heat, and stir-fry quickly to combine. Drizzle in a small amount of sesame oil, stir, and immediately remove from heat.
   **Key point**: Adding the onion at the end preserves its caramelized texture and layered flavor.

## Health Tips
Fried rice with caramelized onion oil is rich in onion sulfides and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing heart protection or blood sugar control. Using leftover cold rice helps keep grains separate, and frying the onion over medium-low heat prevents burning.
