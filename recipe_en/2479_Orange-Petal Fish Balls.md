# Orange-Petal Fish Balls
Cuisine: Hubei Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Grass carp (Waan fish) 1 piece (take about 6 taels of net meat)  
🧅 Scallions 3-4 stalks (for scallion-ginger water)  
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (about 15 grams)  
🥚 Egg whites 2  
🌾 Cornstarch (starch) 2 tablespoons (about 20 grams, needs to be dissolved in water)  
🧊 Ice cubes as needed  
🍶 Yellow wine as needed  
🧂 MSG 3 tablespoons  
🧂 Salt about 5 tablespoons (for 6 taels of fish meat)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🌀 Blender  
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🧽 Gauze bag  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Wok  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the grass carp. Use a knife to scrape the fish paste along the direction of the fish bones. Immediately put the scraped fish paste into clean water to soak, in order to remove blood and fishy smell.  
**Key**: Must scrape along the grain to ensure efficiency and purity of the fish paste.  

2. Place ginger slices and scallion segments in a bowl, add water and squeeze firmly to release the juice. Then pour in yellow wine and a large amount of ice cubes to make iced scallion-ginger yellow wine water.  
**Key**: Squeeze firmly to fully release the flavor; the ice cubes provide a low-temperature environment for subsequent mixing, preventing the fish from becoming fishy.  

3. Remove the soaked fish paste, wrap it in a gauze bag, and squeeze out excess water, so that it can better absorb the seasoning liquid later.  
4. Put the squeezed fish paste, egg whites, and the dissolved cornstarch water into the blender. Start blending at low speed, then gradually pour in the iced scallion-ginger water in batches while gradually increasing the speed. Blend for several minutes until the fish paste is initially mixed.  
**Key**: The cornstarch must be dissolved in water in advance; the scallion-ginger water must be iced and added in batches.  

5. After the fish paste absorbs most of the liquid, add MSG and continue blending.  

6. When the fish paste has fully absorbed the water, becoming fine and sticky, add salt and quickly blend again until well mixed.  
**Key**: Salt must be added only after all the liquid is absorbed; adding salt too early will hinder the fish's water absorption. Salt promotes the coagulation of fish protein, forming elasticity. If the fish paste is too dry during mixing, you may add a small amount of iced scallion-ginger water before adding salt as needed.  

7. Pour the blended fish paste into a basin. Repeatedly pick it up with your hand and throw it back down, until the fish paste has strong elasticity and stickiness, sticking to the hand and able to pull out thin strands.  
**Key**: Hand throwing is the key to the fish balls' Q (chewy) texture; do not skip this step. If the fish paste can still absorb water, you may continue to add a small amount of iced scallion-ginger water at this point to adjust to the ideal consistency.  

8. Heat a sufficient amount of warm water in a wok. Shape the fish paste into orange-petal shapes and gently drop them one by one into the water. Cook over low heat, maintaining a gentle simmer, until the fish balls are set and float.  
**Key**: Use low heat throughout, keeping the water surface barely simmering. Too high a water temperature may break apart the fish balls or make the texture tough.  

## Health Advice  
Fish balls are low in fat and high in protein, suitable for muscle gain and weight loss. However, the amount of salt used in this recipe is relatively high; those with high blood pressure should reduce the salt. Hand throwing is crucial for the Q (chewy) texture.
