# Steamed Squid with Corn
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌽 Corn, appropriate amount (cut into about 1cm thick slices for layering)  
🦑 Small squid, 3 pieces (remove innards and cartilage, retain the ink sac; cut tentacles in half lengthwise)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (finely diced)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🫗 Steamed fish soy sauce, appropriate amount (for drizzling after cooking)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for the final hot oil drizzle)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🍲 Steamer (water must be brought to a boil in advance)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
✂️ Clean kitchen scissors  
🥄 Wok (for heating oil for the final drizzle)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Arrange the corn slices neatly on the bottom of a plate. Place the cleaned squid on top of the corn.
2. Fill the steamer with water and bring to a boil. Place the plate in the steamer, cover, and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The squid is cooked through when it turns white and curls up.
3. Remove the plate. Use clean kitchen scissors to cut the steamed squid into rings for easier eating.
4. Evenly drizzle the steamed fish soy sauce over the dish. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and diced red bell pepper.
5. In a separate wok, heat the cooking oil until smoking hot. Pour the hot oil over the scallions and red bell pepper to release their aroma.

## Health Advice  
Steaming the squid on top of the corn allows the seafood's flavor to infuse into the corn while avoiding the high calories of frying, making it suitable for weight loss diets. Squid has a relatively high cholesterol content, so those with high blood cholesterol levels should consume it in moderation.
