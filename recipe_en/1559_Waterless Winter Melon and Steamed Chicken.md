# Waterless Winter Melon and Steamed Chicken  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Winter melon, about 500g (peeled and cut into thick slices)  
🍄 Cordyceps flowers, about 100g (washed)  
🍗 Chicken thigh, 400g (deboned and sliced)  
🫚 Red dates, 10g (pitted)  
🫚 Ginger slices, 5g  
🧅 Shallots, 15g (sliced)  
🍒 Goji berries, 5g  
🌿 Chopped green onions, as needed (sprinkle on top before serving)  

🧂 Salt, about 3g (2g for marinating chicken, 1g for sprinkling on winter melon to help release liquid)  
🫗 Light soy sauce, 10g  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 15g  
🧂 Chicken powder, 2g  
🍬 Sugar, 3g  
🧂 White pepper, 2g  
🌾 Cornstarch, 5g  
🫒 Peanut oil, 10g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan with a transparent lid  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix the sliced chicken thigh with ginger slices, shallots, red dates, goji berries, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, chicken powder, white pepper, and cornstarch. Toss everything together evenly. Finally, add peanut oil to seal in moisture and let it marinate for 15 minutes to fully absorb the flavors.  
**Note**: Ensure the seasonings and cornstarch thoroughly coat the chicken. Adding peanut oil at the end locks in moisture, resulting in a smoother, more tender texture.  

2. Evenly arrange the thick winter melon slices at the bottom of the pan and sprinkle a little salt on top. Then, lay a layer of cordyceps flowers over the winter melon. Finally, spread the marinated chicken along with its marinade evenly over the cordyceps flowers.  
**Key order**: The winter melon, which contains the most moisture, goes at the bottom; the cordyceps flowers in the middle absorb the juices; the chicken on top is directly heated by steam.  
   **Note**: Spread the chicken as evenly as possible and avoid stacking to ensure uniform cooking.  

3. Cover the pan with the lid and cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes. Observe through the transparent lid—once a large amount of steam is produced and condensed water droplets start to fall, the dish is ready.  
**Note**: Keep the lid tightly sealed to create a steam cycle using the natural moisture from the ingredients. Maintain medium heat throughout to prevent burning the bottom.  

4. After the time is up, open the lid. If the chicken has turned completely white and is cooked through, and the winter melon appears translucent, it’s done. Sprinkle chopped green onions on top and serve.  
**Note**: If the chicken still looks pink after opening the lid, cover and steam for another 1-2 minutes. Avoid opening the lid too often to prevent steam loss.  

## Health Tips  
Winter melon hydrates, chicken is low in fat, and paired with cordyceps flowers and goji berries, this dish is high in protein and low in calories—ideal for those managing calorie intake but still craving meat. Ensure the chicken is well-marinated and avoid opening the lid too frequently during steaming to prevent steam loss, which could affect the texture.
