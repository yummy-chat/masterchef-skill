# Lazy Braised Pork Belly with Quail Eggs
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🥚 Quail eggs, about 10-15 (cooked and shelled)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧂 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🍬 Rock sugar, a few pieces  
💧 Water, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Milk pot  
🫗 Stainless steel steaming rack  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the sauce**: In a bowl, mix oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and salt evenly to make a combined sauce and set aside.
*This step is key to the "lazy version" as it eliminates the need for separate caramelization, using the mixed sauce to complete seasoning and coloring in one go.*

2. **Start braising**: Place the pork belly pieces and ginger slices into a milk pot. Pour in the prepared sauce, add rock sugar, and enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and braise for about 30-40 minutes until the meat is tender.
3. **Steam eggs in the same pot**: Place a stainless steel steaming rack on top of the milk pot, add raw quail eggs, cover the pot, and use the steam from braising the meat to cook the quail eggs. Remove, rinse with cold water, and peel them. Set aside.
4. **Combine and infuse flavor**: After the braised pork is tender, remove the lid. Gently add the peeled quail eggs to the pot, stir evenly, and continue to simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes.
*Adding the quail eggs later allows them to fully absorb the sauce while preventing them from becoming too soft from prolonged cooking.*

5. **Reduce the sauce and serve**: Braise until the sauce thickens noticeably and the meat is coated in a glossy red sauce, then turn off the heat and plate.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost, but the pork belly is high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables. Control salt intake; quail eggs are high in cholesterol, so those with hyperlipidemia should consume them in moderation.
