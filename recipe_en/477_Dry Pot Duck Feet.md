# Dry Pot Duck Feet
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck feet, about 12 pieces (trim off toenails, rinse clean)
🥔 Potato, half (peeled and cut into strips)
🪷 Lotus root, half (peeled and cut into strips)
🥬 Celery, 3-4 stalks (leaves removed, cut into segments)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, 1 (sliced)
🧅 Onion, half (sliced, for lining the pot bottom)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (ginger to garlic ratio 1:4, finely diced)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (garlic to ginger ratio 4:1, finely diced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste
🍃 Green Sichuan pepper, to taste
🧅 Scallions, a few stalks (cut into segments)
🍲 Spicy hot pot base, 40 grams (broken apart)
🍺 Beer, about 1 can (used in two parts)
🧴 Light soy sauce, 6 grams
🧴 Dark soy sauce, a little
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 spoon
🧂 Table salt, a little
🍬 White sugar, a little
🧂 Cumin powder, 1 spoon
🫒 Scallion oil (clarified oil), to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Strainer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Dry pot

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare duck feet and accompaniments**: Trim the toenails off the duck feet and rinse clean. Peel the potato and lotus root and cut into thick strips. Remove leaves from celery and cut into segments. Remove seeds from red bell pepper and slice. Slice the onion and place in the dry pot as a base. Finely dice the ginger and garlic. Cut scallions into segments. Break the hot pot base apart and set aside.
2. **Blanch the duck feet**: Add water to a pot. Place the duck feet in cold water, add part of the beer, and bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off the foam, then reduce to low heat and cook for 20 minutes until the duck feet are fully cooked. Drain well.
3. **Flash-fry the accompaniments**: Heat ample oil in a wok. When the oil reaches medium temperature (50% hot), add the potato and lotus root strips. Fry over low heat until cooked through, then remove and drain the oil.
4. **Stir-fry the base**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Heat to low temperature (40% hot), add the diced ginger, garlic, dried chili peppers, and green Sichuan pepper. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add the broken hot pot base and stir-fry until melted and aromatic.
5. **Braise the duck feet**: Add the cooked duck feet and stir-fry to combine. Drizzle light soy sauce and dark soy sauce along the side of the wok. Stir-fry again, then pour in the remaining beer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and braise for 10 minutes to infuse the duck feet with flavor.
6. **Second seasoning and finishing**: Remove the lid. Add chicken bouillon, salt, and white sugar. Stir-fry to combine. Add the red bell pepper slices and stir-fry until just cooked. Add the celery segments and scallion segments, stir-fry briefly. Add the fried potato and lotus root strips and quickly stir-fry to combine.
7. **Enhance aroma and serve**: Sprinkle in cumin powder and drizzle with scallion oil. Stir-fry quickly over high heat. Transfer to the dry pot lined with sliced onion and serve.
**Note**: The sliced onion at the bottom will release its fragrance when heated. The dish can be kept warm while eating.

## Health Advice
Duck feet are rich in collagen. Paired with lotus root and potato for added dietary fiber, this dish is suitable for those who want a treat while focusing on health. It is recommended to reduce the amount of salt and hot pot base to avoid excessive sodium intake.
