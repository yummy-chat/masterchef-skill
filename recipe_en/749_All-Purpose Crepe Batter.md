# All-Purpose Crepe Batter
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 1 room temperature egg  
🌾 30g cake flour  
🥛 120g milk  
🍬 13g white sugar  
🧈 10g butter (or corn oil or other neutral-flavored vegetable oil)

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Whisk or spoon  
🥣 Measuring cup/measuring spoon  
🔍 Strainer  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🔥 Gas stove/induction cooker  
🔪 Spatula  
📡 Microwave  
📄 Parchment paper  
🍽️ Plate  
📦 Plastic wrap  
❄️ Refrigerator

## Instructions
1. Prepare the batter: In a bowl, sequentially add the egg, flour, milk, sugar, and butter; mix until smooth.  
   - Note: The butter must be melted into a liquid or fully softened in advance to ensure a smooth, lump-free mixture.
2. Strain and rest: Strain the mixed batter through a strainer to remove lumps and bubbles, then let it rest for 10 minutes to relax the gluten.

### Traditional Pan Method
3. Preheat the pan: Over the lowest heat, heat until it feels warm when you hold your hand a few inches above the pan.
4. Spread the batter: Pour a ladleful of batter into the pan and quickly tilt the pan in a circular motion to spread the batter into a thin, even layer covering the bottom. Pour any excess batter back into the bowl.  
   - Key point: If the batter doesn't stick to the pan, return the pan to the heat and warm it slightly. If the batter sets and clumps immediately, remove the pan from the heat to cool it down.
5. Cook: Keep the heat low and cook until the entire surface of the crepe is covered with small, dense bubbles, indicating it is fully cooked. If the heat is uneven, gently shake the pan.
6. Remove: Using a spatula, gently lift the edge of the crepe, then invert the pan or directly peel it off. Place a piece of plastic wrap between each crepe to prevent sticking.
7. Repeat: Before cooking the next crepe, return the pan to the heat for about 5 seconds to restore the temperature.

### Quick Microwave Method
8. Line a plate with a sheet of parchment paper. Pour a small amount of batter onto it and spread it into a thin, round crepe.
9. Place in the microwave and heat on high power for about 1 minute and 30 seconds, until the batter is completely set.
10. Carefully peel off the crepe after removing it from the microwave. This method yields slightly thicker crepes but requires no cooking skills.

### Storage & Usage
11. Cooked crepes can be covered with plastic wrap and stored in the refrigerator. Before use, take them out; they will soften and become more supple.
12. They can be used directly as the outer layer for mille crepe cakes, Swiss rolls, or pancakes. To make pancakes, take one crepe, place ice cream and fruit pieces in the center, fold it up, and wrap it tightly in plastic wrap. Freeze until firm for an ice-cream-like texture.

## Health Tips
Crepe batter is primarily made from eggs, milk, and flour, offering moderate calories and serving as a good base for desserts. Be mindful of the sugar and butter amounts; during weight loss, you can reduce the sugar or substitute with vegetable oil. Resting the batter is key to making the crepes more tender; refrigeration afterward softens them for a better texture.
