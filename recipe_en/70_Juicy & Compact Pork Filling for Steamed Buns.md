# Juicy & Compact Pork Filling for Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 500g ground pork (30% fat, 70% lean)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (some cut into strips for soaking, some chopped for final mixing)
🫚 20g ginger (cut into strips)
🌸 5g Sichuan peppercorns
💧 250g water (for soaking scallions, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns)
🧂 5g salt
🧂 5g chicken bouillon powder
🌶️ 1g white pepper
🌿 2g thirteen-spice powder
🍶 20g light soy sauce
🍬 3g sugar
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
♨️ Steamer/steaming pot
🧤 Gloves
🥄 Spoon/spatula

## Instructions
1. **Prepare scallion-ginger-Sichuan pepper water**: Place scallion strips, ginger strips, and Sichuan peppercorns in a bowl. Add 250g of water and let soak to create a liquid that removes odor and enhances flavor for “water beating”.
2. **Basic seasoning**: To the ground pork, add salt, chicken bouillon powder, white pepper, thirteen-spice powder, light soy sauce, and sugar.
- Note: Sugar helps meld the flavors together for a smoother taste.
3. **Initial mixing**: Wearing gloves, thoroughly mix the pork and seasonings by hand.
4. **Gradual “water beating”**: Pour the scallion-ginger-Sichuan pepper water into the pork in 2 ~ 3 batches. After each addition, vigorously stir in the same direction until the liquid is fully absorbed by the meat.
- Key point: Adding water in stages allows the meat to fully take in the moisture—this is the core of a juicy filling.
5. **Stir until elastic and “throw” the filling**: Continue stirring in the same direction consistently, and repeatedly pick up and throw the meat mixture back into the bowl. Continue until all water is added and the filling becomes sticky and springy.
- Why this is done: Stirring in one direction and throwing the filling encourages the meat to “tighten up,” resulting in a compact, bouncy texture.
6. **Seal with oil**: Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil and gently mix. This creates a protective film over the surface of the meat to prevent it from releasing liquid later.
7. **Add scallions**: Finally, sprinkle in the chopped scallions and gently mix just until combined. Avoid overmixing, which may cause the scallions to release water or lose flavor.
8. **Assemble and steam**:
   - Wrap the prepared filling in bun wrappers, pleating to seal.
   - Place the buns in a steamer lined with a cloth, and let them rise a second time.
   - Bring water in the steamer to a boil, then place the steamer on top, cover, and steam for 10 minutes after steam begins to escape.
   - Note: After steaming, do not turn off the heat first; remove the steamer lid directly. If the heat is turned off first, condensation from the lid may fall onto the buns, causing yellow spots and a less smooth surface.
9. **Remove**: Immediately take the steamer off the heat. You now have fluffy, white, soft buns with a juicy and compact filling.

## Health Note
The 30% fat to 70% lean pork ratio, combined with the “water beating” and throwing technique, creates a “physical therapy” method that yields buns with a juicy, abundant filling and a bouncy texture. Those on a weight loss diet should watch their portion sizes. The Sichuan pepper water eliminates odor and enhances aroma more gently than cooking wine, making it suitable for those with sensitive stomachs.
