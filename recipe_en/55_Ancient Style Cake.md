# Ancient Style Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 12 eggs (each about 50-60 grams)
🥛 130 ml whole milk
🌽 160 ml corn oil
🌾 190 grams cake flour
🍬 140 grams white sugar (added in 3 portions)
🍋 A little lemon juice (a few drops, to remove odor and enhance flavor)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for egg liquid and mixing batter)
🥄 Egg whisk (for stirring batter and egg yolk mixture)
🔌 Electric hand mixer (for whipping meringue)
🍳 Non-stick pan (for heating corn oil)
🔪 Silicone spatula (for smoothing batter)
📦 Square baking pan (lined with parchment paper, for holding cake batter)
🛁 Large baking pan (for water bath)
🧤 Baking gloves (for handling hot molds)

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the eggs one by one into a large, oil-free and water-free bowl. Separate the yolks and whites by hand, ensuring the container and hands are oil-free and water-free, and the yolks remain intact.
   **Note**: The container and hands must be oil-free and water-free, and the yolks must not be broken.
2. Mix the egg yolks with 130 ml of whole milk, and whisk evenly with an egg whisk. Set aside.
3. Pour 160 ml of corn oil into a non-stick pan and heat to 60–80°C.
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; small oil bubbles appearing is sufficient.
4. Gradually pour the hot oil into 190 grams of cake flour in portions, stirring evenly with an egg whisk each time, to form a scalded flour paste.
   **Key point**: Scalding the flour is a crucial step for the cake's delicate texture.
5. Add the egg yolk and milk mixture to the scalded flour paste in two additions, stirring each time until smooth and lump-free, forming a yellow egg yolk batter.
   **Note**: The batter is quite thick; adding in portions makes it easier to mix evenly.
6. Add a little lemon juice to the egg whites to remove any odor. Use an electric hand mixer on low speed to beat until large bubbles appear.
7. Add 140 grams of white sugar to the egg whites in 3 portions, beating on medium speed until soft peaks form (wet, slightly soft meringue).
8. Place a portion of the meringue into the egg yolk batter and roughly mix with an egg whisk.
9. Pour the batter back into the remaining meringue and quickly fold together using an egg whisk or silicone spatula until the batter's texture can stack and does not quickly spread out.
   **Key point**: Using an egg whisk mixes quickly and reduces the risk of deflating the batter.
10. Pour the cake batter from a height into the parchment-lined square baking pan, and smooth the surface with a spatula.
11. Place the baking pan into a larger baking pan and pour in one cup of cold or warm water.
12. Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack, and bake at 150°C (top and bottom heat) for 60–70 minutes.
13. After baking, remove from the oven, unmold while hot, and cut into pieces.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein, suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing a snack. However, it has high sugar and oil content, so it is recommended to control the portion size per serving to avoid excessive calorie intake. The water bath method provides a moist texture, but avoid opening the oven door frequently during baking to prevent the cake from collapsing.
