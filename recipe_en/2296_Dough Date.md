# Dough Date

**Cuisine:** Beijing Cuisine  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 125g  
🍚 Dry yeast 2g  
💧 Room temperature water 65g (use a portion to dissolve yeast, mix the remainder with red yeast rice powder)  
🍬 White sugar 5g  
🧂 Baking powder 2g  
🔴 Red yeast rice powder 4g  
🫘 Dried jujubes, as needed (steam, remove pits and skins to make jujube paste; reserve a small amount finely diced)  
🍫 Cocoa powder, a small amount (for making dark-colored dough)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, a small amount (for frying jujube paste filling)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🫗 Small bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🤲 Rolling pin (for assistance)  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  
🍽️ Cutting board  
🪥 Toothpick  
♨️ Steamer and steamer basket  
🧻 Aluminum foil  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the colored dough**  
   - Combine flour, sugar, and baking powder in a mixing bowl.  
   - Dissolve dry yeast in a small amount of water until no granules remain, then mix the remaining water with red yeast rice powder to form a uniform jujube-red liquid.  
   - Pour both the yeast water and red yeast rice water into the dry ingredients, and knead into a dough.  
**Key point:** Red yeast rice powder must be fully dissolved in advance to avoid uneven color in the final product.  

2. **Knead thoroughly and rest**  
   - Transfer the dough to a cutting board, and knead vigorously until the color is even and the texture is smooth and moist.  
**Key point:** Kneading must be thorough; otherwise, the texture after steaming will not be fine, and the surface will not be smooth.  
   - Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for a while.  

3. **Prepare the jujube paste filling**  
   - Steam the jujubes until soft, remove pits and skins, and mash into a paste.  
   - Heat a small amount of vegetable oil in a pan, and stir-fry the jujube paste over low heat until it thickens and can form a ball. Optionally, mix in a small amount of diced jujube pieces to add chewiness.  
4. **Divide and fill**  
   - Roll the rested red dough into a long strip, divide into portions of about 10g each for the dough and about 8g each for the filling, and roll into balls.  
   - Cover unused dough portions with plastic wrap to prevent drying.  
   - Flatten a dough portion, place jujube paste filling in the center, gather the edges upwards using the tiger's mouth technique, and pinch the seam tightly to seal.  
5. **Shape**  
   - Roll the filled dough ball in your palm into an oval shape to mimic a jujube.  
   - Use your little finger to gently press a round indentation at one end of the oval to simulate the jujube stem attachment point.  
   - Use a toothpick to poke a small hole at the other end to prepare for inserting the stem.  
6. **Make and attach the stem**  
   - Take a small piece of the red dough, add a small amount of cocoa powder, and knead until it becomes a dark brown color.  
   - Roll it into an extremely thin long strip, then cut into small segments to serve as stems.  
   - Steam the stem segments in the steamer for 2 minutes to set, then remove and insert them into the holes at the top of the dough jujubes.  
7. **Steam**  
   - Arrange the raw dough on a steamer basket lined with a damp cloth, and allow to proof briefly until slightly puffed.  
   - Place over boiling water and steam over high heat for 8 minutes.  
8. **Press wrinkles**  
   - Remove from the steamer immediately and work while hot.  
   - Prepare a sheet of aluminum foil and crumple it randomly.  
   - Place the hot steamed dough jujubes into the crumpled foil, gently wrap and squeeze, so the irregular impressions from the foil remain on the dough surface, creating a realistic jujube texture.  
**Key point:** Must work while hot; the foil should have fine, wrinkled creases, and handle quickly and gently to achieve natural-looking impressions.  

9. **Finish**  
   - Remove the dough jujubes with pressed wrinkles. They will closely resemble real dried jujubes in appearance, with a fluffy texture and a delicate filling.  

## Health Advice  
This dough jujube uses red yeast rice for coloring and jujube paste for the filling. The outer skin is soft, and the filling is sweet, making it suitable as a low-oil, low-sugar Chinese pastry. Jujubes are rich in iron and dietary fiber, which can help nourish blood and Qi. However, each piece contains about 10 grams of carbohydrates, so those monitoring sugar intake are advised to consume 2-3 pieces per serving.
