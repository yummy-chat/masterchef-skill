# Stir-Fried Ribs with Smashed Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ribs 500g (pork spare ribs, cut into small pieces)
🌶️ Luo-si chili peppers about 400g (about 7, thin-skinned peppers)
🫚 Ginger about 10g (sliced)
🧄 Garlic about 25g (4 cloves, sliced)
🌶️ Small chili peppers about 40g (sliced diagonally into rings, omit if you don't like heat)
🫘 Fermented black beans about 20g (one and a half tablespoons, roughly chopped)
⭐ Star anise 1
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (used twice)
🍶 Yellow wine about 10g (one tablespoon)
💧 Water about 600ml (two bowls, use hot water)
🧂 Light soy sauce about 20g (two tablespoons)
🍬 Sugar about 3g (one teaspoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce about 20g (one heaped tablespoon)
🧂 Dark soy sauce about 6g (half a tablespoon)
🧂 Chicken bouillon about 3g (one teaspoon)
⚠️ Salt omit (fermented black beans, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce already provide saltiness)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Bowl
♨️ Electric stew pot

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the ribs into small pieces. Soak in cold water for about 30 minutes to remove blood, then drain and pat dry with paper towels to prevent oil splatter during frying.
**Key Point**: Do not blanch the ribs; only soak to remove blood to preserve the meaty flavor.

2. Flatten the Luo-si chili peppers with the side of a knife, focusing on crushing the stems for better flavor absorption. Remove stems and cut into long segments. Slice ginger and garlic. Slice small chili peppers diagonally into rings. Roughly chop the fermented black beans. Prepare the star anise.
**Key Point**: When flattening the peppers, the stems must be crushed to allow flavors to penetrate.

3. Heat the wok over high heat, add cold oil, then heat. Add ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant.
4. When the ginger slices are browned at the edges, add the ribs. Spread them flat and sear for about ten seconds before stirring. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until the moisture evaporates, the ribs turn slightly golden, and the oil at the bottom of the wok becomes clear.
**Key Point**: Spread the ribs flat without moving them initially to prevent sticking; stir-fry over medium heat until the oil is clear, indicating moisture is gone.

5. Splash yellow wine along the edge of the wok, add star anise, and pour in hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 40 minutes.
**Key Point**: Adding hot water and boiling before turning to low heat results in a creamier broth.

6. After stewing, remove ginger slices and star anise. If there is too much liquid, increase heat to reduce until about one small bowl remains.
7. Clean the wok, add a thin layer of oil, and heat over high heat. Add the pepper segments, pressing with a spatula while stir-frying until softened with a tiger-skin pattern on the surface.
**Key Point**: Pressing and stir-frying ensures even heating and quick development of the tiger-skin pattern.

8. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil to the wok and continue stir-frying for a moment.
9. Add fermented black beans, small chili peppers, and garlic slices, stir-frying until fragrant. Splash light soy sauce along the edge of the wok to release aroma, then add the reserved ribs and broth, stirring well.
**Key Point**: Splashing light soy sauce along the wok edge enhances the sauce's aroma through high heat.

10. Reduce to medium-low heat, add sugar, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and chicken bouillon, stirring to combine. Increase heat to reduce until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients evenly. Turn off heat and serve.
**Key Point**: Fermented black beans, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce already contain salt, so no additional salt is needed.

## Health Advice
Ribs are rich in protein, while chili peppers are high in vitamin C, creating a complementary nutritional pairing. However, fermented black beans and soy sauce contain high sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume less broth. Capsaicin in peppers can aid digestion and is suitable for those with poor appetite, but individuals with stomach issues should consume with caution.
