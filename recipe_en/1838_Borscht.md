# Borscht
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef brisket (main ingredient, for simmering soup)  
🌿 Celery (cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot (peeled, cut into chunks with a rolling cut)  
🥔 Potato (cut into small pieces)  
🧅 Onion (sliced)  
🍅 Tomato (score a cross on the top, blanch and peel, then mash into a paste)  
🥬 Cabbage (shredded, added last)  
🌭 Red sausage (cut into small pieces)  
🌱 Green onion (as needed, for simmering beef brisket)  
🫚 Ginger (as needed, for simmering beef brisket)  
🍶 Cooking wine (as needed, to remove gamey smell when simmering beef brisket)  
🥫 Tomato paste (as needed, Xinjiang tomato paste recommended)  
🧂 Salt (as needed, for final seasoning)  
⚫ Black pepper (as needed, freshly ground, for final seasoning)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Deep pot/soup pot  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🍳 Wok  
🫖 Clay pot (for holding the cooked beef and soup)  
🥄 Strainer (to remove green onion and ginger)  
🥣 Masher  
🔪 Peeler  
🥢 Tongs  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Steps  
1. Wash the beef brisket. Place it in a deep pot with green onion, ginger, and cooking wine. Add enough water and simmer over low heat for 40 minutes to make the beef broth. Remove the green onion and ginger. Set aside the beef and broth.  
2. Prepare the vegetables: Cut the celery into small pieces. Peel the carrot and cut into chunks with a rolling cut. Cut the potato into small pieces. Slice the onion. Shred the cabbage.  
3. Prepare the tomatoes: Score a cross on the top of the tomatoes. Blanch in boiling water for about 30 seconds, then remove and rinse with cold water. Peel and mash into a paste using a masher.  
4. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry until slightly charred brown. Add the celery and carrot in sequence and stir-fry. Then add the tomato paste and tomato paste, and stir-fry until well combined.  
5. Transfer the stir-fried vegetables to a clean soup pot. Add the beef broth (with green onion and ginger removed) and the beef. Add enough water and simmer over low heat for 1 hour.  
6. Add the red sausage pieces. Continue to simmer over low heat for 30 minutes.  
7. 10 minutes before finishing, add the shredded cabbage and gently stir to combine. Once the soup is done, season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.  
   **Key point**: Add the cabbage last to maintain a crisp texture.  

## Health Advice  
This borscht is rich in protein and various vegetable fibers, making it suitable for warming up in winter. Beef brisket is easy to digest after simmering, but red sausage is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair with whole wheat bread to balance carbohydrates and control salt intake.
