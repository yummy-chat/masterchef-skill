# Braised Lamb Trotters
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Fresh lamb trotters (singed to remove hair), appropriate amount  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount  
🧅 Sliced ginger, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a small amount  
🧂 Soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 Maltose, appropriate amount (mixed with water at a 1:20 ratio to form a sugar water solution)  
🍶 Heavy rice wine, appropriate amount  
🌿 Braising spices (cloves, fennel, cinnamon, star anise), appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🔪 Knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Ladle  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍲 Lid  

## Cooking Steps
1. Thoroughly rinse the lamb trotters with clean water to remove impurities and any residual hair.  
2. Prepare the maltose water: Dissolve maltose in hot water at a ratio of 1:20 (maltose to water), stirring until fully dissolved.  
3. Place the cleaned lamb trotters into the maltose water, then toss and mix by hand to coat each trotter evenly with the syrup.  
4. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the wok and heat to approximately 120°C. Fry the lamb trotters.  
   **Key point**: Fry over medium heat until the skin is crispy and the color turns bright red. Control the oil temperature to avoid burning.  
5. In another wok, heat a small amount of oil over high heat. Add the sliced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant.  
6. Add the fried lamb trotters, then pour in an appropriate amount of heavy rice wine and soy sauce. Stir-fry evenly.  
7. Add the braising spices, salt, a small amount of oyster sauce, and the shiitake mushrooms. Pour in enough water to completely submerge the lamb trotters.  
8. Bring to a boil over high heat, then stir the bottom of the wok to prevent sticking. Cover with the lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for about 1 hour until the lamb trotters are tender and soft.  
9. Remove the lamb trotters from the wok. Strain the cooking liquid to discard any spice and ingredient residue. Return the clear liquid to the wok, then increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly until thickened.  
10. Once the sauce has thickened, return the lamb trotters and shiitake mushrooms to the wok. Stir-fry over medium heat until the rich sauce evenly coats the surface of the trotters. Remove from heat and plate.

## Health Advice
This braised lamb trotter dish is rich in collagen and protein, making it suitable for those needing joint nutrition or seeking improved skin elasticity. However, it is high in calories and fat, so it is recommended to pair it with refreshing vegetables. The frying and slow-braising process may result in some loss of B vitamins, so avoid excessive consumption.
