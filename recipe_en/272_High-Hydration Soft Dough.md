# High-Hydration Soft Dough
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 2 jin (about 2.4 lbs / 1 kg)  
💧 Room temperature water 1 jin 8 liang (about 1.8 lbs / 800 g) (9 liang water per jin flour)  
🫒 Cooking oil (for preventing sticking)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍶 Small bowl (for oil)  

## Instructions  
1. Pour the flour into a large glass bowl. Use a spoon to make a well in the center. Pour all the water in at once.  
**Note**: Strictly follow the ratio of 1 jin flour to 9 liang water. The water must be added all at once, not in batches.  

2. Use your fingertips to gradually push the dry flour around the well into the water, mixing until no visible liquid water remains. The flour should become flocculent, and the sides of the bowl should stay dry.  
**Key point**: Do not use the palm of your hand to contact the batter. Only use your fingertips. This is the first step to avoid sticky hands.  

3. Using four fingers, fold the dough from the bottom up, then press it down firmly. Repeat the "fold, press, and knead" motion for several minutes, allowing the flour and water to fully combine and eliminating excess surface moisture.  
**Key point**: Do not knead the dough as you would with conventional dough.  

4. Dip your hands in a little oil to prevent sticking. Lift the dough with both hands, shake and stretch it, then forcefully throw it down and fold it over. Repeat this many times until the dough becomes elastic and the surface begins to smooth out, resembling "jelly."  
**Purpose**: Strengthening the gluten by throwing and folding makes the dough shinier and chewier.  

5. Let the dough rest in the bowl for about 10-15 minutes to allow the gluten to relax, making the dough even softer.  

6. After resting, repeat the lifting and throwing action from step 4. Throw and fold until the dough is very smooth and shiny, forming a soft, elastic whole that does not stick to your hands or the bowl.  

7. The dough is now ready for making fried dough sticks (youtiao), fried dough cakes (youbing), vegetable pies (caihe), and various other flour-based dishes.  

## Health Advice  
This high-hydration dough is suitable for making youtiao or youbing, with a soft and chewy texture. Pay attention to controlling the oil temperature to prevent excessive oil absorption. It is suitable for those who enjoy soft flour-based foods, but as it is high in carbohydrates and calories, consume in moderation during weight loss periods.
