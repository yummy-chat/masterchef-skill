# Steamed Pork with Rice Flour
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 500g Pork Belly with Skin (sliced into 0.5cm thick pieces)
🍚 Appropriate amount of regular japonica rice (soak for half an hour in advance, then drain)
🌿 Appropriate amount of star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, and cumin (for toasting the rice flour)
🍶 20ml Huadiao rice wine
🧂 30ml light soy sauce
🌶️ 0.5g ground white pepper
🧅 20g scallion (chopped)
🫚 20g ginger slices
🍬 20g brown sugar
🍯 50g sweet bean sauce
🧆 50g fermented tofu

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔪 Cutting board
🥖 Rolling pin
🌀 Blender (optional)
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍲 Large blue-and-white porcelain bowl (or other steaming bowl)
🍽️ Plate
♨️ Steamer
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1. **Toast the Five-Spice Rice Flour**
   - Place the drained rice, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, and cumin into a dry wok. Toast over low heat for about 20 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice turns a golden-brown color.
   - Remove from heat, take out the whole spices, break them into smaller pieces, and crush them with a rolling pin into coarse rice flour, or use a blender to grind into fine powder.
   **Key Point**: Toast over low heat to avoid burning; break the spices apart before grinding to release their flavors.

2. **Prepare the Pork Belly**
   - Cut the pork belly with skin into slices about 0.5cm thick.
   **Key Point**: Make sure to keep the skin on; it improves the texture after steaming and prevents the meat from falling apart.

3. **Marinate the Pork Belly**
   - Add Huadiao rice wine, light soy sauce, ground white pepper, chopped scallion, ginger slices, brown sugar, sweet bean sauce, and fermented tofu to the pork slices. Put on gloves and mix thoroughly, ensuring every slice is evenly coated. Let marinate for 1 hour.
   **Key Point**: Mixing must be thorough; ensure each slice of meat is fully coated with the marinade.

4. **Combine Rice Flour and Pork Slices**
   - Pour the toasted five-spice rice flour into the marinated pork and mix well until each slice is evenly coated with a layer of rice flour. No additional seasonings are needed.

5. **Arrange in the Steaming Bowl**
   - Lightly grease the inner surface of the steaming bowl with oil. Arrange the pork slices neatly in the bowl, placing the best-looking slices at the bottom. Fill any gaps and corners with any remaining rice flour and spices.
   - Cover the bowl with a plate to prevent condensation from dripping in.
   **Key Point**: Greasing the bowl makes unmolding easier; arranging the meat neatly ensures a more attractive final presentation.

6. **Steam**
   - Once the steamer is producing steam, place the covered bowl inside. Reduce the heat to low and steam for 1 hour. If time allows, extending the steaming time to 3 hours will result in a softer, more tender texture.
   **Key Point**: After steam appears, reduce to low heat to maintain a steady steam flow; longer steaming makes the meat more tender and flavorful.

## Health Advice
Steamed pork with rice flour is relatively high in fat. It is recommended to serve with plenty of leafy green vegetables to help balance the nutrition. This dish is suitable for occasional indulgence; people with high blood pressure or high cholesterol should consume it in moderation.
