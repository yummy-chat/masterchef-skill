# Pan-Seared Mackerel
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 frozen mackerel
🧂 Coarse salt (sea salt) to taste
🫒 Cooking oil to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Heavy cutting board + chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧺 Bamboo basket
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Food tongs
🫙 Plastic wrap + storage container

## Steps
1. Thaw the frozen mackerel, place it on a heavy cutting board, and cut into evenly thick slices.
   **Note**: Do not remove the scales; only remove the gills and internal organs.
2. Place the fish pieces in a stainless steel bowl, evenly sprinkle with a generous amount of coarse salt, and marinate for about 30 minutes.
3. After marinating, thoroughly rinse the fish pieces under clean water to remove excess salt on the surface, then drain well.
   **Note**: Rinsing prevents the fish from becoming too salty due to residual salt.
4. Arrange the fish pieces flat on a bamboo basket and sun-dry for about 3 hours.
   **Note**: The goal is to remove surface moisture and produce slight fermentation for a drier, more aromatic texture, but not to fully dry the fish.
5. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a frying pan and heat over medium heat until the oil surface begins to smoke slightly.
   **Key point**: Ensure the pan and oil are hot enough before adding the fish to prevent sticking.
6. Brush a thin layer of oil on the surface of the dried fish pieces, then gently place them into the hot oil pan.
   **Heat**: Fry over medium heat. Wait until the bottom side turns a charred brown color.
   **Tip**: You can also pour any remaining oil from the plate into the pan for use.
7. Once one side is golden brown and fragrant, use food tongs to flip the fish and continue frying the other side until similarly done.
   **Judgment**: If unsure, lift slightly to check, and fry until the desired level of char and aroma is reached.
8. Once both sides are fried, remove the fish with tongs and plate. The skin should be dry and fragrant, and the fish golden brown. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice
Mackerel is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids and high-quality protein, making it suitable for heart and brain health. However, the marinating process uses a high amount of salt, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Pairing with light vegetables is recommended to balance the flavor.
