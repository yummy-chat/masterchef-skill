# Three Flavored Skinless Chicken Thigh Fillets
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Skinless and boneless chicken thighs 2.0 kg (divided into 3 portions)
🧂 Baking soda a pinch (for tenderizing the meat)
🍗 New Orleans seasoning powder to taste (for one portion of chicken)
◼️🌶️ Black pepper sauce to taste (for one portion of chicken)
🧂 Salt-baked seasoning powder to taste (for one portion of chicken)
🧂 Salt a pinch (to supplement flavor if the black pepper flavor is too spicy)
🍾 Light soy sauce a splash
🦪 Oyster sauce a splash
🌾 Cornstarch to taste (to lock in moisture)
🫒 Cooking oil a splash (for sealing in flavor)
🥬 Lettuce to taste (side dish)
🍍 Pineapple to taste (side dish)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Stainless steel bowl or square tray (for marinating chicken)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥢 Food tongs
🫙 Sealed bag with sealing clip
❄️ Refrigerator

## Preparation Steps
1. Divide the skinless and boneless chicken thighs evenly into three portions and place them in separate containers.
2. Add a pinch of baking soda to each portion of chicken, then add New Orleans seasoning powder, black pepper sauce, and salt-baked seasoning powder respectively to create three flavors; if the black pepper flavor is too spicy, you may add a pinch of extra salt to supplement the taste.
   **Note**: The amount of baking soda is minimal, used only to tenderize the meat.
3. Add a splash of light soy sauce, a splash of oyster sauce, and an appropriate amount of cornstarch to each portion of chicken.
   **Key Point**: Cornstarch effectively locks in the moisture of the chicken, resulting in a more tender texture after frying.
4. Wearing gloves, thoroughly mix each portion of chicken thighs with the seasonings by hand, ensuring the surface is evenly coated.
5. Drizzle a splash of cooking oil over the surface of each portion of chicken, gently toss to seal in the moisture and freshness with the oil.
6. Place the three marinated portions of chicken thighs in the refrigerator to chill for at least 3 hours, allowing them to fully absorb the flavors.
7. Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium heat, add the marinated chicken thighs, and cook over medium-low heat until both sides are browned and the inside is fully cooked through.
   **Doneness Check**: Insert a chopstick into the chicken; if no blood flows out, it is cooked.
8. Place any uneaten marinated chicken thighs into a sealed bag, remove the air, seal, and store in the freezer; thaw in advance before eating.
   **Note**: Avoid soiling the sealing clip when bagging, ensuring a proper seal.

## Health Tips
Skinless chicken thigh fillets are low in fat and high in protein, and pairing them with lettuce and pineapple makes for a more balanced meal, suitable for fitness or weight loss individuals. Adding baking soda during marination can tenderize the meat, but be sure to control the amount to avoid bitterness.
