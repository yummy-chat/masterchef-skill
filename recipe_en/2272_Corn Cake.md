# Corn Cake
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌽 Cooked corn kernels, about 250g (boiled or canned, thoroughly drained)  
🥔 Potato starch, as needed (enough to coat the corn kernels)  
🍬 White sugar, as needed (to sprinkle on the finished product)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫗 Soup ladle (for pouring hot oil)  
🥣 Strainer (to drain corn kernels)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Rinse the cooked corn kernels with clean water, place them in a strainer and drain thoroughly. Add potato starch in batches, stirring until each kernel is evenly coated with a thin layer of dry powder.  
   **Key point**: The water must be completely drained, otherwise it will easily clump and be difficult to shape; potato starch is preferred.  
2. Pour cooking oil into the wok, heat to 150–160°C, then turn off the heat. Remove most of the hot oil and set aside. Spread the coated corn kernels evenly over the bottom of the wok, shaping them into a circle or rectangle. Gently press flat and compact with your palm.  
   **Key point**: Operate with the heat off, using the residual heat of the wok to initially gelatinize and bind the starch.  
3. Use a soup ladle to scoop two ladles of the reserved hot oil, and drizzle evenly over the corn kernels to quickly gelatinize the dry starch and form a cake shape.  
4. Turn the heat back to medium, pour the remaining hot oil back into the wok along the edge, ensuring the oil submerges the corn cake. Once all the oil is back in the wok, turn the heat to high and continue frying until the corn kernels turn golden, the surface bubbles become smaller, and the cake is crispy throughout.  
   **Key point**: Maintaining a continuous rise in oil temperature is crucial for a crispy, non-soggy final result.  
5. Remove the corn cake, drain excess oil, plate, and sprinkle evenly with white sugar.  

## Health Tips  
Corn cake primarily consists of corn kernels, which are coated in starch, deep-fried, and topped with sugar, resulting in a high calorie content. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables or protein. Diabetics and individuals on a weight loss regimen should be mindful of their intake.
