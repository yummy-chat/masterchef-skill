# Fried Cumin Mushrooms
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Oyster mushrooms 500g  
🥚 Egg 1 piece  
🌾 Flour approx. 75g  
🫒 Salad oil (for batter) approx. 75g  
💧 Water approx. 75g  
🧅 Scallion white 1 small section  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (used in batches)  
🧂 MSG appropriate amount  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper appropriate amount  
🌿 Cumin appropriate amount  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowls (2 pieces)  
🥄 Metal slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut off the roots of the oyster mushrooms and tear them into bite-sized pieces by hand.  
   **Key point**: Keep pieces uniform in size for easy eating.  
2. Fill a wok with water and heat until small bubbles appear at the edges. Once the water is boiling, add the mushrooms and cook for about 1 minute until it returns to a boil.  
   **Note**: Only add the mushrooms after the water is fully boiling.  
3. Remove immediately and plunge into cold water to cool. Drain in a slotted spoon and gently press with your palm to remove excess water—do not squeeze forcefully.  
   **Key point**: Cooling makes the texture crisper; remove water gently to retain some moisture.  
4. Cut the scallion and ginger into irregular thin shreds, place in a bowl, add a little salt, toss a few times, and let sit briefly until juices emerge.  
   **Key point**: Salt quickly draws out the scallion and ginger juices.  
5. In a bowl, crack the egg, add flour, salad oil, and water, and stir until smooth and creamy. When lifted, the batter should form a thread, and the trail left in the bowl should not disappear immediately.  
   **Key point**: Adding salad oil to the batter is key for a fluffy, bubbly texture after frying.  
6. Place the drained mushrooms in a bowl, drizzle with a small amount of scallion-ginger juice, then add salt, MSG, ground Sichuan pepper, and cumin. Toss evenly by hand to marinate.  
   **Note**: Do not use too much scallion-ginger juice to prevent burning during later frying.  
7. Gently squeeze the marinated mushrooms to remove excess liquid, then add to the batter and mix well, ensuring a thin coating on each piece. Heat oil to 60% hot, and add mushrooms one by one. Fry until they float, surface shows small bubbles, color turns light yellow, and shape is plump, then remove.  
   **Key point**: Add mushrooms one at a time to prevent sticking; 60% oil temperature ensures quick setting; oil in the batter makes mushrooms fluffy and bubbly.  
8. Remove the first-fried mushrooms and use a fine mesh strainer to scoop out any batter residue from the oil. Raise the oil temperature to 70% hot, add all the mushrooms back for a second fry for about 1 minute, until the color turns deep golden and the exposed parts appear reddish, then remove and drain oil.  
   **Key point**: Second frying drives out excess oil, making the coating crispy and not greasy.  
9. Plate and serve. The outside is crispy, the inside tender, with a rich cumin and Sichuan pepper flavor.

## Health Advice  
This fried cumin mushroom dish is crispy on the outside and tender inside, with its texture derived from oil in the batter and double frying, but it is also high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with refreshing cucumber salad or lettuce salad to balance the greasiness. Suitable for those who enjoy a crispy texture, but those with high blood lipids or on a weight-loss diet should consume in moderation.
