# Shredded Three-Side Dish with Meat Topping
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork front leg meat (about 150g, with some fat)  
🫚 Ginger (a small piece, minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (about 10g, cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallion (1 stalk, chopped)  
🥒 Cucumber (about 1.5 small cucumbers)  
🥬 Baby cabbage (4 leaves)  
🥕 Carrot (a small amount, for color)  
🥬 Purple cabbage (3-4 half leaves, stems removed)  
🫘 Dried tofu skin (about half a sheet)  
🍜 Vermicelli (1-2 rolls)  
🌿 Cilantro (a small bunch, optional)  
🧄 Garlic (4-5 cloves, minced)  
🫒 Cooked rapeseed oil (a generous amount)  
🧂 Light soy sauce (about 1 tablespoon)  
🫒 Sesame oil (to taste)  
🌶️ Chili oil (to taste)  
🧂 Salt (about 6g)  
🍬 White sugar (1 tablespoon)  
🍶 Vinegar (1 tablespoon, 5-6% acidity)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the pork front leg meat into shreds and mix with a small amount of minced ginger. Cut dried chili peppers into sections, chop the scallion, and mince the garlic. Set aside.
2. Press the cucumber flat, then slice it diagonally into thin pieces, and cut into shreds about the thickness of chopstick tips. For the baby cabbage, slice the thick stems thinly along the fiber direction, then cut into fine shreds. Cut the carrot into very fine shreds. Remove stems from purple cabbage and cut into fine shreds. Cut the dried tofu skin into shreds. Chop the cilantro. Place all vegetable shreds, minced garlic, and cilantro into a large bowl.
3. Add cold water and vermicelli to a pot. Bring to a boil, then immediately turn off the heat. Cover and let steep for about 1 minute until soft. Remove and rinse briefly with cold water. Drain well and spread over the vegetable shreds.
**Key point**: Do not rinse the vermicelli too thoroughly with cold water, or it will lose its chewy texture.
4. Pour a generous amount of cooked rapeseed oil into a wok and heat it. Add the shredded meat and minced ginger, stir-fry until the meat changes color. Add the dried chili pepper sections, and continue stir-frying over medium heat until the meat shreds are slightly charred and give off a fragrant, smoky aroma.
**Note**: The charring of the meat shreds is crucial for the overall aroma of the dish.
5. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Drizzle in the light soy sauce. Use the residual heat of the wok to let the soy sauce sizzle and release its fragrance until it bubbles. Immediately sprinkle in the chopped scallion, stir-fry quickly a few times, then turn off the heat.
6. Pour the hot meat topping along with the hot oil directly over the vermicelli.
7. Drizzle with sesame oil and chili oil. Add about 6g of salt, 1 tablespoon of white sugar, and 1 tablespoon of vinegar.
**Key point**: Use enough salt to “hold the flavor”, as vegetables will release water when salted. The sugar and vinegar should be balanced. Do not use vinegar with too high acidity.
8. Put on disposable gloves and thoroughly mix all ingredients and seasonings by hand. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This cold salad is rich in vegetables and provides a good amount of protein, making it suitable for weight loss periods or when you need a light, refreshing dish to cut through greasiness. Searing the meat shreds until slightly charred and pouring hot oil over them is key to enhancing the flavor, but be mindful that the total oil content is relatively high. Those watching their oil intake can reduce the amount appropriately. Do not steep the vermicelli for too long, or it will become sticky after mixing, affecting the texture.
