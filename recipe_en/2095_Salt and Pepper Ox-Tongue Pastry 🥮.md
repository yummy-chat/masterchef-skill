# Salt and Pepper Ox-Tongue Pastry 🥮
Cuisine: Other ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
**Water-Oil Dough**
🌾 All-purpose flour 110g
🍬 White sugar 20g
🐖 Lard 40g (corn oil can be substituted)
💧 Boiling water 45g

**Oil Dough**
🌾 Cake flour 90g
🐖 Lard 45g

**Filling**
🌾 Cooked flour 100g (cake flour substitute available)
🖤 Black sesame powder 80g (made from ground cooked black sesame seeds)
🥜 Peanuts 15g (roasted, peeled, and ground)
🌰 Walnuts 15g (roasted and ground)
🍬 Fine white sugar 80g (granulated sugar or powdered sugar can be substituted)
🐖 Lard 100g
🧂 Sichuan pepper salt 5g (2g Sichuan peppercorns + 8g salt, dry-fried together until fragrant, ground, then use 5g of the mixture)

## Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥖 Rolling pin
🔥 Oven
🔪 Food processor/grinder
🫙 Plastic wrap
📄 Parchment paper
🥄 Spatula

## Instructions
### Preparation
1. **Make the water-oil dough**: Combine all-purpose flour, white sugar, and lard. Add boiling water and stir quickly. Knead into a smooth dough. Wrap in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: Boiling water partially gelatinizes the flour, enhancing gluten extensibility for easier rolling.

2. **Make the oil dough**: Mix cake flour and lard. Knead into a dough. Divide into 13g pieces and set aside.

3. **Prepare cooked flour**: Cover raw flour with plastic wrap. Steam over boiling water for 20 minutes. If lumpy, sift. If heavily clumped, first break it up with a food processor, then sift.

4. **Prepare black sesame powder**: Grind cooked black sesame seeds in a food processor. If using raw seeds, roast them first.

5. **Prepare peanut and walnut crumbs**: Roast peanuts and walnuts in the oven at 160°C for about 6 minutes. Peel the peanuts (walnut skin is optional). Grind together using a food processor.

6. **Make Sichuan pepper salt**: Mix Sichuan peppercorns and salt in a 1:4 ratio. Dry-fry in a clean, dry pan over low heat for about 1 minute until fragrant. Remove and grind in a food processor.

7. **Mix the Sichuan pepper filling**: Combine cooked flour, fine white sugar, lard, black sesame powder, peanut and walnut crumbs, and Sichuan pepper salt. Knead into a dough. Divide into 34g balls, making 10 total.

### Assembly
1. **First roll**: Divide the rested water-oil dough into 21g pieces. Flatten each into a disc (thicker in the center). Wrap one oil dough piece inside, sealing with a pinching motion. With the seam side up, flatten it. Roll it with a rolling pin into an ox-tongue shape, then roll it up. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for about 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: Roll evenly to avoid tearing the dough and exposing the oil layer. Resting allows the gluten to relax, preventing shrinkage.

2. **Second roll**: With the seam side up, flatten the first roll. Roll it again into a long ox-tongue shape and roll it up. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for about 10 minutes.

3. **Fill**: Take a rested spiral. Find the seam. Press your thumb into the center, folding the ends together to seal. With the seam side down, shape it into a ball and flatten it. Roll into a disc (thicker in the center, thinner at the edges). Place a filling ball in the center. Use your thumb and forefinger to slowly push the dough upwards, sealing the top tightly.
   **Key point**: Seal the opening firmly to prevent the filling from leaking during baking.

4. **Final shape**: Roll the filled ball into a slightly elongated log, then press it flat into an ox-tongue shape. A mold can be used for shaping.

5. **Bake**: Preheat the oven to 180°C. Bake in the middle rack for 20 minutes. Typically, no egg wash is applied. Bake until golden brown.

## Health Tips
This Salt and Pepper Ox-Tongue Pastry uses lard for flakiness, making it relatively high in calories. However, black sesame and nuts provide quality fatty acids and minerals, making it suitable as an occasional treat. Consume in moderation to avoid excessive calorie intake. Diabetics may reduce the sugar or substitute with a sugar alternative.
