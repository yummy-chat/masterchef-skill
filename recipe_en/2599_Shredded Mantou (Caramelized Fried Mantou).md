# Shredded Mantou (Caramelized Fried Mantou)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Mantou (Steamed Bread) 2 pieces (cut into slices about one finger wide, then re-cut into diamond-shaped pieces)
💧 Warm water as needed (for soaking mantou pieces)
🧂 White sugar about 1.5 tablespoons (for making syrup)
🫒 Cooking oil a small amount (added to syrup to prevent sticking)
🌰 Black sesame seeds as needed (sprinkled before serving)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board (for cutting mantou)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking mantou pieces)
🥄 Slotted spoon or spider strainer (for draining and removing fried mantou)
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for deep-frying mantou and making syrup)

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the mantou into slices about one finger wide, then cut into strips, and finally cut diagonally into diamond-shaped pieces.
**Key Point**: The shape of the cut affects the final texture and how well the syrup coats the pieces.
2. Prepare half a bowl of warm water, and soak the cut mantou pieces in the water for about 30 seconds.
**Key Point**: Use warm water, and control the soaking time to around 30 seconds.
3. Remove the soaked mantou pieces from the water and squeeze out the moisture from each piece by hand.
**Key Point**: Squeeze out the water thoroughly; this is key to making the mantou crispy after frying and preventing it from absorbing too much oil.
4. Pour oil into the wok and heat to 50% hot (about 150°C). Add the squeezed mantou pieces.
**Key Point**: Do not stir the mantou pieces immediately after adding them to the oil. Wait until they are set before gently moving them.
5. Maintain medium heat and fry the mantou pieces for about 5-6 minutes, until the surface is an even golden yellow and the texture is crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key Point**: Avoid high heat (easily burns) and low heat (absorbs too much oil). The mantou pieces should be golden and crispy; you can hear a crisp sound when tapping them with a slotted spoon.
6. In another clean wok, add one tablespoon of water, one and a half tablespoons of white sugar, and a small amount of oil. Turn to medium heat and start making the syrup.
**Key Point**: Adding a small amount of oil prevents the syrup from sticking to the pan. Do not use low heat, as it will be difficult to make good syrup.
7. Continue to boil and stir, observing the changes in the syrup: first large bubbles appear, then fine and dense bubbles, and finally the color turns slightly yellow.
**Key Point**: The syrup-making process requires patience to observe the state changes.
8. When the syrup turns a sesame oil color (amber) and you can lift a spoonful that forms a continuous, unbroken thread, reduce the heat to the lowest setting.
**Key Point**: This is the best moment for making shredded/toffee coating.
9. Quickly add the fried mantou pieces into the wok and stir-fry rapidly to coat each piece evenly with the syrup.
**Key Point**: Work quickly to ensure even syrup coating.
10. Sprinkle in an appropriate amount of black sesame seeds, stir-fry a couple more times, then remove from the wok and plate.
**Key Point**: Serve and eat as soon as possible for the best pulling/shredding effect.

## Health Advice
The main calories in this shredded mantou dish come from deep-frying and the syrup, making it suitable as an occasional sweet treat. Soaking the mantou in water and squeezing it dry before frying helps reduce oil absorption. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the sweetness.
