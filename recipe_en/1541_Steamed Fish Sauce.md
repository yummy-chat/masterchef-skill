# Steamed Fish Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Mandarin Fish (Siniperca chuatsi) 1 piece  
🥬 Celery appropriate amount  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount  
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount  
🫑 Green bell pepper appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (use part for broth and part for shredded garnish)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (use part for broth and part for shredded garnish)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount  
🥓 Ham strips appropriate amount  
🍄 Shiitake mushroom strips appropriate amount  
🌶️ Red bell pepper strips appropriate amount (for garnish)  
💧 Water 5 jin (approx. 2.5 kg)  
🧴 Maggi Seasoning 3 liang (approx. 150 g)  
🐟 Fish sauce 3 liang (approx. 150 g)  
🍾 Light soy sauce about 1.5 bottles  
🥃 Seasoning sauce (such as Weishida brand) 3 liang (approx. 150 g)  
🧂 MSG 6 liang (approx. 300 g)  
🌑 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (for color adjustment)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon, soup ladle  
♨️ Steamer  
🍽️ Fish plate  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring 5 jin of water to a boil, add vegetable ingredients such as celery, onion, carrot, green bell pepper, scallion segments, ginger slices, and garlic cloves. Simmer over low heat until the vegetables are completely softened and their fresh aroma is fully released.
**Key point**: To achieve a rich base flavor, simmer for at least 6 hours to allow the vegetable freshness to infuse into the broth.
2. Use a slotted spoon to remove all the boiled vegetable residue. Add Maggi Seasoning, fish sauce, light soy sauce, seasoning sauce, and MSG to the vegetable broth, stirring to combine.
**Note**: The focus at this stage is flavor integration. If the sauce color is too light, add a small amount of dark soy sauce to adjust the color, resulting in a bright, appealing reddish hue.
3. Bring the seasoned vegetable broth to a boil again, ensuring the seasonings are thoroughly combined. Then turn off the heat. The steamed fish sauce is now ready.
4. Clean and prepare the mandarin fish, then steam it until cooked through.
5. Remove the steamed fish, discard the watery liquid released during steaming (which can be fishy), and evenly arrange scallion shreds, ginger shreds, ham strips, and shiitake mushroom strips on top of the fish.
6. In a separate pan, heat a spoonful of scallion oil until hot. Pour it over the toppings on the fish, listening for a sizzle that releases the aroma; then pour the hot fish sauce along the edge of the plate, **avoiding pouring it directly onto the fish meat** to prevent the fish from darkening.
**Key point**: The sequence is to first pour the oil, then add the sauce.

## Health Advice
This steamed fish sauce incorporates various vegetables and seasonings, offering a delicious flavor, but it has a relatively high sodium content. People with high blood pressure are advised to use it in moderation. Mandarin fish is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for post-surgery recovery and brain workers.
