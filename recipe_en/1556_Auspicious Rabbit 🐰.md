# Auspicious Rabbit 🐰

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Chinese yam, as needed (peeled, steamed, and used as the main filling)
🍠 Purple sweet potato puff pastry dough, as needed (used for the rabbit body and ears)
⚪ White puff pastry dough, as needed (used for the inner ear, eyes, whiskers, teeth, and other decorations)
🔴 Red dough, a small amount (used for the base of the eyes and the nose)
⚫ Black dough, a small amount (used for the pupils of the eyes)
🧂 Sugar, as needed (added when stir-frying the yam filling)
🫒 Salad oil, as needed (added when stir-frying the yam filling)
🥚 Egg wash, as needed (used as adhesive for sticking dough, filling, decorative parts, and sealing)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥖 Rolling pin (for rolling out puff pastry sheets)
🖌️ Pastry brush (for applying egg wash)
✂️ Scissors (for trimming dough and cutting rabbit fur shapes)
🔧 Shaping tool (round-tipped, used to press eye sockets for shaping)
🌡️ Thermometer (for measuring oil temperature)
🍳 Oil pot (for deep-frying the shaped pieces)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing finished fried items)
🪥 Small stick (toothpick/skewer, inserted into the bottom of the finished piece for support)

## Production Steps
1. **Prepare the yam filling**: Peel the yam and steam it until cooked. Mash it while still hot. In a pan, add a little salad oil and sugar, then add the yam mash and stir-fry until it forms a cohesive ball and is no longer sticky.
   **Key Point**: The filling must be stir-fried until dry to make it easier to wrap and shape later.

2. **Prepare the puff pastry dough**: Take the purple sweet potato puff pastry dough and cut off two pieces. Use a rolling pin to roll each piece into a thin sheet about 1mm thick.
   **Key Point**: The dough sheets should be thin. Dust the surface with dry flour to prevent sticking.

3. **Wrap the rabbit body**: Brush a layer of egg wash onto one purple dough sheet. Take an appropriate amount of yam filling and roll it into an oval shape. Place it in the center of the dough sheet. Fold both sides of the dough sheet toward the middle, completely encasing the filling. Brush egg wash on the seal and pinch it tightly. Use scissors to trim off any excess dough at the bottom, making the bottom flat.
   **Key Point**: Make sure the seal is pinched tightly and brushed with egg wash to prevent it from splitting open during frying.

4. **Shape the head details**: On the top of the wrapped dough, use scissors to make approximately three small diagonal cuts to create a raised "rabbit fur" shape.

5. **Make the ears**: Take two small purple dough sheets and stack them together. Trim them into a shape that is wider at the top and narrower at the bottom, resembling an ear. Cut thin strips from the white dough and make a few cuts at one end to create a fluffy texture. Attach the textured white dough strip to the purple ear piece. Brush egg wash on the back of the ear and set aside.
   **Key Point**: The ears are made by stacking two dough sheets; no filling is needed.

6. **Make the facial features**: Use red dough as the base and black dough for the pupils. Roll them up and slice to make the eyes. Use small red dots for the nose. Use white dough to make two large front teeth. Use the prepared white thin strips for the whiskers.

7. **Assemble**: Use the shaping tool to press eye sockets into the appropriate positions on the rabbit's head. Press the eyes into the sockets. Attach the nose and whiskers. Use a tool to lift the two teeth and stick them below the nose. Finally, insert the two long ears into the reserved positions on top of the head.
   **Key Point**: All parts must be securely attached using egg wash as adhesive.

8. **Support and set shape**: Insert two small sticks from the center of the bottom of the finished piece to provide support, allowing it to stand upright.
   **Key Point**: This step is crucial for ensuring the finished piece maintains its upright shape after frying.

9. **Deep-fry**: Pour oil into a pot and heat it to around 150°C. Place the shaped piece in a slotted spoon and submerge both the spoon and the piece in the oil. Maintain the oil temperature and deep-fry until the layers of pastry open up, the shape is set, and it is cooked through.
   **Heat or Time**: Oil temperature around 150°C, medium-low heat for deep-frying. Time depends on size; ensure it is fully cooked through.
   **Doneness Check**: The piece floats to the surface, the pastry layers are clearly opened, and the color has not turned golden brown.
   **Key Point**: Use enough oil to nearly submerge the main body; the bottom is thicker, so it needs a bit more time to fry.

## Health Advice
This yam and purple sweet potato rabbit pastry has a light filling and a charming appearance, making it perfect for festive gatherings or to delight children. When deep-frying, be careful to control the oil temperature to avoid burning the pastry or leaving the inside undercooked. It is best enjoyed while hot.
