# Beef Hot Pot Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients
🥩 Beef slices 30g (choose slices with clear texture and no water-retaining agent)
🥚 Egg 1
🧅 Onion 1 (cut into thick shreds)
🥬 Greens appropriate amount (such as bok choy or choy sum)

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (used for seasoning and helping egg white set when cooking poached egg)
🌶️ Black pepper or white pepper appropriate amount
🥤 Light soy sauce approximately 30g (preferably with amino nitrogen ≥ 1.2g/100ml)
🦪 Oyster sauce approximately 18g (preferably with high oyster juice content)
🍶 Yellow wine or rice wine approximately 25g (must not contain spices)
🍬 White sugar a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil a small amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl

## Steps
1. Fill a pot with enough water, bring to a full boil over high heat. Add the beef slices, remove immediately once the beef changes color. Rinse off the blood foam with warm water and drain.
   **Note**: Do not stir forcefully with chopsticks to prevent the beef from breaking apart.
2. In another pot, add enough water and an appropriate amount of salt. Heat until small dense bubbles appear at the bottom. Gently slide the egg into the water near the surface. Keep at a gentle simmer until the egg white is fully set. Remove to get a poached egg.
   **Key**: Do not let the water boil vigorously throughout the process, otherwise the egg will break apart.
3. Use the water from boiling the egg to blanch the greens, then remove and set aside.
4. Add a small amount of cooking oil to a non-stick pan. Add the onion shreds and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant and softened. Then turn off the heat.
5. After turning off the heat, add black pepper, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, yellow wine, and white sugar. Stir evenly with chopsticks.
6. Turn the heat back on to bring the sauce to a boil. Add a small amount of water. Once boiling again, add the blanched beef slices and cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute until the meat slices are evenly coated with sauce. Do not overcook to prevent the meat from becoming tough.
7. Serve the rice in a bowl. Top with the cooked beef and onions. Drizzle the sauce over it. Arrange the poached egg and greens on top.

## Health Advice
Beef hot pot rice has good protein and iron content, but the beef slices are relatively high in fat. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables to balance the calories. Suitable for a quick lunch but not advisable for frequent consumption.
