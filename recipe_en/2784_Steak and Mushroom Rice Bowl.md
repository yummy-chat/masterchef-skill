# Steak and Mushroom Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ribeye steak: appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)
🍄 Mushrooms: appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🧅 Onion: appropriate amount (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic: appropriate amount (minced)
🟢 Scallions: appropriate amount (chopped)
🍚 Rice: appropriate amount (cooked rice)
🧂 Black pepper powder: appropriate amount (used once for marinating and once for stir-frying)
🍶 Cooking wine: appropriate amount (for marinating)
🦪 Oyster sauce: appropriate amount (used once for marinating and once for stir-frying; use generously when stir-frying)
🧂 Light soy sauce: about 2 tablespoons
🧂 Dark soy sauce: about 1/3 tablespoon
🍬 White sugar: appropriate amount
🫒 Olive oil: appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating the beef)
🧄 Garlic press
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🔥 Induction cooker

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the ribeye steak into small pieces, ensuring uniform size to avoid uneven doneness.
   **Note**: The steak pieces should not be too large, or it will affect the final texture.
2. Place the cut steak pieces in a bowl, add black pepper powder, cooking wine, and oyster sauce, and mix well to marinate, allowing the base flavor to penetrate.
   **Note**: Use just enough seasoning to flavor the meat; do not overdo it.
3. Dice the onion into fine pieces and cut the mushrooms into pieces. Use a garlic press to mince the garlic, then mix with the diced onion and set aside.
   **Note**: Mixing the minced garlic with the diced onion enhances the aroma when stir-fried together later.
4. Heat the frying pan over the stove, pour in olive oil, and stir-fry the diced onion and minced garlic over medium heat.
   **Visual cue**: Stir-fry until the diced onion and minced garlic release their fragrance with no strong pungent smell.
5. Add the marinated steak pieces, turn the heat to medium-high, and stir-fry quickly.
   **Visual cue**: The surface of the steak pieces should all change color, reaching about 3–5 minutes of doneness.
6. Add the mushroom pieces, stir-fry evenly, then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, black pepper powder, and white sugar.
   **Key**: Use a generous amount of oyster sauce; the dish’s savory-sweet flavor mainly relies on it.
7. Continue stir-frying until the mushrooms release a lot of liquid, then keep stir-frying until the ingredients are basically cooked through.
8. Add the cooked rice, and use a wooden spatula to mix thoroughly, ensuring each grain of rice is coated with the sauce, resulting in a glossy sheen.
9. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in a generous amount of chopped scallions, mix well, and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and B vitamins, making it suitable for fitness and muscle-building individuals. The oyster sauce and sugar content are relatively high, so it is recommended to control the rice portion to avoid excessive calorie intake. Mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber, which helps balance the greasiness of the steak.
