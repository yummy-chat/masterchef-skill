# Floating Oil Tofu
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 1 block firm tofu (mashed into a fine paste)
🥕 1/2 carrot (sliced into decorative or regular slices)
🍄 About 10 wood ear mushrooms (soaked, then sliced into small pieces)
🥚 Appropriate amount of egg whites (added in three batches)
🌾 Appropriate amount of starch (in water for batter and thickening)
🧂 A pinch of salt
🍶 A pinch of Huadiao wine
🍬 1/2 tablespoon sugar
🧈 1 tablespoon chicken fat
🍲 Appropriate amount of scallion and ginger water (obtained by soaking scallions and ginger)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. **Prepare the Tofu**: Place the firm tofu on a cutting board and repeatedly press it with the flat side of a knife until it becomes a fine tofu paste.
**Note**: The finer the paste, the smoother and more tender the final product.

2. **Prepare the Vegetables**: Cut the carrot into decorative flower slices or regular thin slices. Cut the soaked wood ear mushrooms into small pieces and set aside.

3. **Make the Tofu Batter**:
   - Add a small amount of salt and Huadiao wine to the tofu paste, stirring in one direction until well combined.
   - Add the egg whites in three separate batches, mixing thoroughly after each addition until fully incorporated, making the batter light and fluffy.
   - Finally, add an appropriate amount of water-starch mixture and continue stirring until the batter is uniformly smooth.
**Note**: The egg whites must be added in batches and mixed thoroughly; this is key to achieving the tender texture of the "floating oil" slices.

4. **Fry the Tofu Slices**:
   - Pour enough oil into a wok and heat it to 30% of its maximum temperature (about 90°C/195°F).
   - Using a soup spoon, scoop an appropriate amount of tofu batter and gently slide it into the oil close to the surface, allowing it to naturally spread into a slice shape.
   - Once the tofu slice is set and floats, gently flip it with chopsticks and fry until both sides are white and the slice is cooked through. Remove and drain excess oil.
**Note**: The oil temperature must be controlled at 30% heat; higher temperatures will make the tofu slices tough and hard. Handle gently to avoid breakage.

5. **Blanch to Remove Oil**: In another pot, bring water to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Gently blanch the fried tofu slices for a moment, then remove and immediately plunge into cold water to cool.
**Note**: Keep the water at a gentle simmer to avoid breaking the tofu slices; cooling them results in a more refreshingly tender and springy texture.

6. **Final Cooking**:
   - Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Briefly stir-fry the carrot slices and wood ear mushroom slices, then add the scallion and ginger water and an appropriate amount of boiling water.
   - Season with 1/2 tablespoon salt and 1/2 tablespoon sugar. Gently push the drained tofu slices into the liquid.
   - Reduce to medium-low heat. While gently shaking the wok, drizzle in the water-starch mixture to create a thin glaze. Use the back of a soup spoon to gently push the ingredients, ensuring the sauce coats them evenly.
   - Just before serving, drizzle in 1 tablespoon of chicken fat and mix gently. Transfer to a plate.
**Note**: Do not stir-fry during the entire final cooking process; rely on shaking the wok and gently pushing with the back of the spoon to keep the tofu slices intact. The sauce should be a clear, glossy "glass glaze," not thick or cloudy.

## Health Advice
By mashing firm tofu and mixing it with egg whites before frying, "floating oil" tofu is made, providing low fat and high protein—suitable as a main dish during weight loss. Care should be taken to keep the oil temperature at 30% heat to avoid burning, and avoid stirring during the final cooking to prevent the tofu from breaking.
