# Salt-Baked Peanuts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Dish

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts (with red skin), as needed
🧅 Scallion, as needed (sliced)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (sliced)
🧂 Salt, one whole bag (for baking, reusable)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🌿 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🍃 Bay leaves, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Kettle (for boiling water)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon/Colander
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Metal spoon
🪵 Wooden cutting board
📦 Metal square tray (for cooling)
🍽️ Serving plate

## Steps
1. Place the peanuts in a stainless steel bowl, pour in boiling water, and soak for 5 minutes to soften the skins, allowing flavor to penetrate and preventing burning during baking.
2. Add Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers to the water with the peanuts to infuse flavors.
3. Slice the scallion, ginger, and garlic; set aside.
4. Clean the wok, ensuring it is free of oil and water. Pour in the entire bag of salt, and stir-fry over high heat until the salt grains are hot.
5. Add the soaked and drained peanuts along with some of the spices into the hot salt, and stir-fry quickly.
   - **Key**: Immediately reduce to the lowest heat after adding the peanuts, and stir-fry slowly over low heat throughout.
6. Continue stir-frying over the lowest heat for about 7–8 minutes. Add the sliced scallion, ginger, and garlic, and continue stir-frying.
7. Stir-fry until the peanuts are completely dry, the salt grains naturally fall off, the surface is slightly yellow with tiger-stripe marks, and a popping sound is heard. Immediately turn off the heat.
   - **Indicator**: The popping sound and slightly yellow skin indicate a crispy texture.
8. After turning off the heat, continue stir-frying for about 2 minutes using residual heat to enhance crispiness.
9. Pour the contents of the wok into a slotted spoon, sifting out the hot salt. Keep only the peanuts and spices.
   - **Note**: Let the sifted salt cool, then store it in a sealed container. It can be reused for salt-baked pork liver, soybeans, and other dishes.
10. Pour the peanuts into a metal square tray, spread them flat, and let them cool naturally. They will become crispier once fully cooled, then serve on a plate.

## Health Advice
Eating peanuts with their skin is rich in antioxidant polyphenols and B vitamins, but they are high in calories, so it is recommended to consume in small amounts each time. The salt-baking method results in high sodium content, so those with high blood pressure should monitor their intake.
