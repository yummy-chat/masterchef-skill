# Cold Tossed Yuba (Liang Ban Fu Zhu)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Peanuts 200g  
🥢 Yuba (Dried Bean Curd Sticks) 300g (pre-soaked)  
🥬 Celery 50g (shredded)  
🥕 Carrot 50g (shredded)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (shredded)  
⭐ Star Anise 2 pieces  
🪵 Cinnamon Stick 1 piece  
🧂 Salt 5g (for boiling peanuts) + 2g (for seasoning)  
🐔 Chicken Bouillon (or Chicken Essence) 10g  
🍶 Vinegar 20g  
🫒 Sesame Oil 10g  
🧄 Minced Garlic, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili Oil, appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Cooking Pot  
🥣 Bowl for soaking  
🥣 Large bowl/ basin (for mixing)  

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Boil Peanuts**: Add water to a pot, then add the peanuts, 5g of salt, star anise, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for 10 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the peanuts soak in the liquid until completely cooled. Drain well.
    **Key Point**: Soaking after turning off the heat allows the peanuts to fully absorb the flavor.
2.  **Soak Yuba**: Add a little salt to cold water and stir to dissolve. Add the dried yuba and soak for about 2 hours, until the yuba is completely soft with no hard core.
    **Key Point**: Adding salt can shorten the soaking time and prevent the outside from becoming mushy while the inside remains hard.
3.  **Prepare Vegetables**: Shred the softened yuba. Shred the celery, carrot, and onion separately.
4.  **Blanch**: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the shredded yuba, celery, and carrot. Blanch over high heat for about 10 seconds, just until they are cooked but still crisp. Immediately remove and quickly rinse under cold water. Drain well.
    **Note**: The blanching time must be short. Rinsing with cold water helps maintain a crunchy texture.
5.  **Mix**: Place the drained yuba, celery, and carrot into a large bowl. Add the shredded onion and the cooked peanuts. Then add 2g of salt, chicken bouillon, vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and chili oil in that order. Mix thoroughly until well combined.

## Health Advice
Cold tossed yuba is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for summer consumption, offering a heat-clearing and detoxifying effect. The combination of peanuts and yuba is good for those who need to supplement protein. However, chili oil and peanuts are relatively high in calories, so consume in moderation during a weight loss period.
