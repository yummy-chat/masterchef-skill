# Black Pepper Beef Tenderloin
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin, appropriate amount (cut into thick slices, then into strips as thick as an index finger)
🫑 Green bell pepper, appropriate amount (cut into strips)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (cut into strips)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into strips)
🧂 Black pepper, generous amount (added in two stages: for marinating the beef and for making the black pepper sauce)
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating) + 2 tablespoons (for the sauce)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for marinating)
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for marinating) + 1 tablespoon (for the sauce)
🟤 Dark soy sauce, half a tablespoon (for the sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon (for the sauce)
🍬 White sugar, 2 tablespoons (for the sauce)
💧 Water, 10 tablespoons (for the sauce)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for oil-blanching, a generous amount) + a small amount of base oil (for stir-frying the vegetables)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowls (for marinating the beef and mixing the sauce)
🥄 Spoons (for checking oil temperature and scooping)
🥢 Chopsticks (for separating the beef strips and stirring to thicken the sauce)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing the oil-blanched beef)

## Preparation Steps
1.  First cut the beef tenderloin into thick slices, then cut into strips as thick as an index finger.
2.  In a bowl, add the beef strips, black pepper, light soy sauce, cooking wine, and cornstarch. Mix well with your hands and marinate for 15 minutes.
3.  Cut the green bell pepper, red bell pepper, and onion into strips.
4.  In a bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce, half a tablespoon of dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, 2 tablespoons of white sugar, 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, 10 tablespoons of water, and a generous amount of black pepper. Stir until well mixed.
5.  Pour a generous amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 150°C. Add the marinated beef strips and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Stir-fry until about 90% cooked, then immediately remove with a slotted spoon and drain the oil.
**Key Point**: The oil temperature should not be too high. Quickly separate the strips after adding to ensure even cooking.

6.  Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the green and red bell pepper strips and onion strips. Stir-fry until fragrant and softened.
7.  Pour in the prepared black pepper sauce, then return the oil-blanched beef strips to the wok. Keep the heat on medium and stir quickly with chopsticks to evenly coat the ingredients with the sauce. Once the sauce has thickened and fully coats the beef strips, turn off the heat and serve.

## Health Advice
Beef tenderloin is rich in high-quality protein, suitable for muscle building, but the oil-blanching step increases the fat content. It is recommended to reduce the amount of oil or pre-cook using an air fryer. Black pepper helps promote blood circulation, and pairing it with bell peppers can supplement Vitamin C. Note: Control the salt amount during marination to avoid excessive sodium intake.
