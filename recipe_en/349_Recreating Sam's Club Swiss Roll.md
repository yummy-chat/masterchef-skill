# Recreating Sam's Club Swiss Roll
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Fresh Eggs 4 (chilled, separate yolks and whites)  
🌾 Cake Flour 40g (sifted)  
🥛 Whole Milk 40g  
🫒 Vegetable Oil 40g (mild flavor)  
🍬 Granulated Sugar 40g (for meringue)  
🌽 Corn Starch 5g  
🍃 Twinings Earl Grey Tea Leaves 5g (crushed)  
🧈 Heavy Cream 150g  
🍬 Granulated Sugar 10g (for cream)  
🍠 Taro Paste 2 tbsp  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large Bowls 2 (one for egg whites must be grease-free and water-free)  
🔌 Electric Hand Mixer  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🧂 Sifter  
🥄 Silicone Spatula  
🍳 28×28cm Square Baking Pan  
📜 High-Temperature Resistant Baking Mat/Parchment Paper  
🔥 Home Oven  
🥖 Rolling Pin  
🔪 Knife  

## Instructions  
1. **Preparation**: Thoroughly clean the bowl for egg whites until grease-free and water-free; separate the yolks and whites from 4 chilled eggs, ensuring no yolk gets into the whites; preheat the oven to 150°C (top and bottom heat) for 10 minutes.  
2. **Make the Egg Yolk Batter**: Add 40g milk and 40g vegetable oil to the egg yolks, stir until emulsified; add the sifted 40g cake flour and mix well; add 5g crushed Earl Grey tea leaves and combine evenly.  
   **Note**: When lifting the whisk, the batter should leave a trail that quickly disappears.  
3. **Whip the Meringue**: Beat the egg whites with an electric mixer on high speed until fine bubbles form; add 40g granulated sugar all at once; once soft peaks appear, add 5g corn starch; reduce to medium-low speed and beat until the whisk attachment forms a firm, large hook; finally, spin the bowl while beating on low speed to refine air bubbles.  
   **Key**: The whisk attachment must be grease-free; the meringue should have clear, persistent lines when stirred.  
4. **Combine the Batters**: Add about 2 scoops of meringue to the egg yolk batter and quickly mix using a folding motion with the whisk; pour back into the remaining meringue, then use a silicone spatula to fold and cut until no white meringue remains and the batter is smooth and thick.  
   **Note**: Work gently and quickly, avoiding circular stirring to prevent deflating.  
5. **Bake**: Line the baking pan with a high-temperature resistant mat or parchment paper; pour in the batter and spread evenly, then gently tap the pan to release large air bubbles; place in the middle-lower rack of the oven and bake at 150°C for about 38 minutes until the surface is golden brown and feels dry and springy to the touch.  
6. **Demold and Cool**: Immediately after baking, lift the cake out of the pan.  
   - For **right-side-out roll**: Keep the top side facing up, cover with parchment paper, and let cool completely.  
   - For **inside-out roll**: While still hot, invert the cake, peel off the baking mat, cover with parchment paper, and let cool.  
   **Note**: For right-side-out rolls, the cake must be fully cooled before rolling to prevent cracking.  
7. **Make the Taro Cream**: Add 10g granulated sugar to 150g heavy cream, beat until lines appear, then add 2 tbsp taro paste; continue beating until stiff with clear lines.  
   **Note**: Add the taro paste just before the cream is fully whipped to avoid interfering with whipping.  
8. **Rolling**: Trim one edge of the cake into a diagonal; spread the cream evenly, leaving the edges slightly thinner; use a rolling pin to lift the starting edge of the parchment paper at the bottom, press firmly, then pull the paper forward and push the roll; slide the cake roll to the center of the paper and tighten to set.  
9. **Chill and Slice**: Refrigerate the cake roll for at least 4 hours; remove, place seam-side down, and slice into pieces about 2.5cm thick, wiping the knife clean after each cut.  

## Health Advice  
This Swiss roll features a dual filling of Earl Grey tea and taro paste, offering rich flavor with moderate sugar and oil, making it suitable as an afternoon tea dessert. Be mindful of portion size (1-2 pieces per serving) and pair it with unsweetened tea for a better choice.
