# Youting Crucian Carp

Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 4-5 medium-sized crucian carp (with roe preserved)  
🥒 Pickled radish (shredded)  
🌶️ Pickled chilies (mixed green and red, finely chopped)  
🫚 Pickled ginger (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic (crushed)  
🧅 Scallions/spring onions (tied knots, sections, and chopped)  
🫚 Ginger (crushed for marinating, minced)  
🥬 Celery (cut into short sections)  
🥜 Peanuts (fried and crushed into granules)  
🌿 Cilantro (chopped)  
🥫 Pickled mustard tuber granules  
🧂 Salt  
🌶️ White pepper powder  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor)  
🥄 Cooking wine  
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste, about 1 tablespoon)  
🌶️ Knife-cut chili (optional)  
🧂 MSG  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder  
🍬 White sugar  
🥄 Soy sauce  
🥄 Vinegar  
🌿 Spice powder (or Chinese thirteen-spice blend)  
🌶️ Dried chilies  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns  
🍚 Fermented glutinous rice juice (filtered from fermented glutinous rice)  
🛢️ Mixed oil (vegetable oil + lard)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥣 Stainless steel basin/large bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  

## Steps  
1. Scale, gut, and clean the crucian carp, keeping the roe. Make three diagonal cuts on each side of the fish.  
2. Place the fish in a basin and add crushed ginger, scallion knots, salt, white pepper powder, and baijiu. Mix well and marinate for 10 minutes.  
3. Shred the pickled radish. Finely chop the pickled chilies and pickled ginger and mix together. Crush the garlic. Prepare the scallion sections and celery sections. Set aside.  
4. Prepare the dipping sauce bowl: Add crushed peanuts, pickled mustard tuber granules, minced garlic, chopped scallions, and chopped cilantro into the bowl.  
5. Heat the wok over high heat, pour in the mixed oil, and fry a small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Then add the "three pickled" mixture and crushed garlic, stir-frying until a sour aroma emerges.  
**Key point**: Ensure the lactic acid aroma from the pickled vegetables is fully released.  
6. Add the doubanjiang and stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant.  
7. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon powder, white pepper, sugar, soy sauce, and vinegar. Adjust the taste.  
8. Gently place the marinated fish into the soup one by one, cover the wok, and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 5-6 minutes, until the fish is about 80% cooked.  
**Note**: Maintain a low heat to prevent the fish from breaking apart and ensure a tender texture.  
9. Remove the lid, add the scallion sections and celery sections, sprinkle in the spice powder, and gently push to mix evenly. Turn off the heat.  
10. Transfer the fish and soup into a large basin.  
11. In a separate wok, heat oil over low heat and add minced ginger, minced garlic, dried chili sections, and Sichuan peppercorns. Fry until fragrant. When the oil color slightly changes, quickly pour in one tablespoon of fermented glutinous rice juice. Immediately pour the oil and aromatics over the fish in the basin.  
**Key technique**: Add the fermented glutinous rice juice quickly to use the oil's heat to release its fragrance.  
12. Garnish with chopped scallions and chopped cilantro. To serve, dip the fish pieces in the dipping sauce, adding a little broth from the cooking liquid if desired.  

## Health Advice  
The pickled chilies and doubanjiang give the dish a strong flavor, and the crucian carp is tender and well-infused. However, the sodium content is high; those with high blood pressure should control their use of the dipping sauce. The crushed peanuts in the dipping sauce provide high-quality fats, and pairing with celery helps achieve a balanced nutritional profile.
