# Ginger and Scallion Chicken Salad
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Chicken thighs 4 pieces (approx. 400g)
🧂 Salt-baked chicken powder 5g
🫚 Ginger 50g
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk
🧂 Salt 5g
🍬 Sugar 5g
🫗 Oil 35ml

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🍲 Steamer
🔪 Knife
🥢 Cutting board
🥄 Dipping sauce bowl
🫖 Small pot

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the chicken thighs clean and place them in a bowl.
2. Add the salt-baked chicken powder and 5ml of oil to the bowl. Mix well and let marinate for 15 minutes.
**Note**: Marinate for at least 15 minutes to ensure the flavor is absorbed.

3. Bring water to a boil in the steamer. Place the marinated chicken thighs in and steam over high heat for 20 minutes.
**Key point**: The steamer must be preheated with boiling water, and maintain high heat throughout steaming.

4. Finely chop the ginger into ginger paste, shreds, or granules. Cut the scallion into 0.5cm sections.
5. Place the chopped ginger and scallion into a dipping sauce bowl. Add salt and sugar.
6. Pour the remaining oil into a small pot and heat until it reaches 60-70% hot.
**Note**: Control the oil temperature to 60-70% hot to avoid burning the ginger and scallion, which would cause bitterness.

7. Pour the hot oil over the ginger and scallion in the bowl to release their aroma.
8. Once the chicken thighs are steamed, remove them and let cool slightly. Tear into shreds about 1cm thick by hand.
**Note**: Tear the chicken into shreds about 1cm thick; thinner shreds may affect the texture.

9. Drizzle the ginger-scallion oil over the chicken shreds. Mix well and serve.

## Health Advice
This ginger and scallion chicken salad uses steaming instead of frying, preserving the high-quality protein and low fat content of chicken thighs, making it ideal for those on a weight-loss diet or controlling oil intake. The aroma released by hot oil on ginger and scallion can stimulate appetite. However, since the salt-baked chicken powder is already salty, add extra salt sparingly.
