# Belt Noodles with Dipping Sauce
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 500g all-purpose flour  
💧 230-240g room temperature water (added in batches)  
🍅 Tomatoes, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (minced)  
🌼 Daylily buds, appropriate amount (soaked ahead of time)  
🍄 Black fungus, appropriate amount (soaked ahead of time)  
🥚 Eggs, appropriate amount (beaten)  
🌿 Small celery, appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  
🧂 3g salt (for dough) + appropriate amount (for seasoning)  
🌿 Star anise, 2g  
🌿 Cinnamon, 2g  
🍃 Bay leaves, 1g  
🌿 Fennel seeds, 3g  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, 3g  
🌿 Angelica root, 2g  
🌿 Cloves, 2 pieces  
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount  
🧂 White pepper, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili powder, appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic paste, appropriate amount  
⚪ White sesame seeds, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍲 Stainless steel stock pot  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🍽️ Flat plate  
📦 Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare Spice Water**: Clean the star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, fennel seeds, Sichuan peppercorns, angelica root, and cloves. Place them in a bowl and pour in boiling water. Let steep until the flavor is released.
**Key points**: Use boiling water for steeping rather than boiling to create a milder, smoother flavor.

2. **Make Garlic Chili Oil**: In a bowl, combine garlic paste, chili powder, and white sesame seeds. Pour one ladle of hot cooking oil over the mixture to release the aroma.
**Key points**: None.

3. **Knead the Dough**: Add 3g of salt to the all-purpose flour and mix well. Gradually add room temperature water, stirring into a flaky texture, then knead into a dough. The dough should initially be relatively firm to prevent it from becoming too soft after resting.
**Key points**: The dough should be moderately firm, leaning toward the firmer side.

4. **Knead the Dough Further**: Place the dough on a work surface. Repeatedly knead and fold in the same direction until the surface is smooth and the dough forms a long strip, creating a smooth gluten network.
**Key points**: Knead thoroughly to ensure the gluten network is evenly developed.

5. **Shape the Dough Pieces and Rest**: Divide the long strip of dough into pieces weighing 50-60g each. Pinch the cut ends toward one side, then roll into cylindrical strips. Arrange the pieces neatly on a flat plate brushed with oil, brush the surface of the pieces with another layer of oil, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 40 minutes.
**Key points**: Allow sufficient resting time for the dough to achieve good elasticity.

6. **Prepare the Tomato Dipping Sauce**: Heat oil in a wok over high heat. First, stir-fry the minced ginger until fragrant, then add the tomato pieces and scallion sections, stir-frying until the tomatoes release their juice. Add a small amount of water and pour in the strained spice water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer until the sauce thickens slightly. Add the soaked daylily buds and black fungus, and season with salt, chicken powder, and white pepper. The seasoning should be slightly bold. Pour in the beaten egg liquid, and turn off the heat once the egg flowers float to the surface.
**Key points**: Season the dipping sauce a bit more heavily to complement the plain noodles.

7. **Stretch and Cook the Noodles**: Take one rested dough piece, flatten it, and gently roll it out with a rolling pin. Hold the ends of the flattened dough piece with both hands and gently stretch it outward to the desired length and thickness. You can tap it on the work surface to adjust the thickness.
**Key points**: Use a "holding" technique, relying on the dough's natural elasticity to avoid forceful pulling.

8. **Cook the Noodles and Blanch the Greens**: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the stretched noodles and cook until they float to the surface and have no white core. Just before the noodles are done, add the small celery and blanch briefly.
**Key points**: Do not overcook the noodles; maintain a chewy texture.

9. **Assemble the Bowl**: Place a small amount of noodle cooking water at the bottom of a bowl, then ladle in the noodles and greens. In a separate small bowl, serve the tomato dipping sauce and pour a spoonful of garlic chili oil over it. When eating, pick up the noodles and dip them into the sauce bowl before eating.
**Key points**: The noodle water prevents the noodles from sticking together, and the dipping sauce should be enjoyed hot.

## Health Advice  
This Belt Noodles with Dipping Sauce is rich in carbohydrates, making it suitable for those with high energy needs or after significant physical exertion. It is recommended to consume the garlic chili oil in moderation to avoid irritating the stomach and intestines.
