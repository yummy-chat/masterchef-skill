# Salmon and Chicken Thigh Rice Casserole  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Salmon  Approx. 300g (two pieces, skin can be left on)  
🍗 Chicken Thighs  Two pieces (boneless)  
🍚 Rice  Approx. 1 cup (rice cooker measuring cup)  
🥕 Carrot  Approx. half (diced)  
🧅 Onion  Approx. half (diced)  
🍄 Shimeji Mushrooms  A small handful (trimmed and separated)  
🫘 Green Peas  A small handful  
🥦 Broccoli  1 small head (cut into small florets)  
💧 Water  As needed (normal amount for cooking rice)  
🧂 Salt  To taste  
🥄 Light Soy Sauce  To taste  
🟡 Yellow Mustard  To taste (for drizzling after serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice Cooker  
🍳 Frying Pan (non-stick)  
✂️ Kitchen Scissors  
🔪 Knife, Cutting Board  
🥣 Strainer  
🥄 Rice Paddle  
🍲 Bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and set it aside in the rice cooker inner pot.  
2. Prepare the vegetables: Cut the carrot and onion into small dices; trim and separate the shimeji mushrooms; rinse the green peas; cut the broccoli into small florets.  
   **Note**: Cut all vegetables to a size similar to the green peas to ensure even cooking and easy eating.  
3. Without adding oil to the frying pan, place the salmon pieces skin-side down. Fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown, then remove and set aside.  
   **Key Point**: Salmon is rich in natural oils, so no additional oil is needed.  
4. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the diced carrot, diced onion, shimeji mushrooms, and green peas. Stir-fry over medium heat for about 1–2 minutes, until the vegetables are just cooked and fragrant.  
5. Pour all the stir-fried vegetables onto the uncooked rice in the rice cooker inner pot. Add an appropriate amount of water, salt, and light soy sauce. The water amount should follow your usual rice-cooking standard.  
6. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, select the rice cooking mode, and let it run until the program finishes.  
7. While the rice is cooking, clean and dry the frying pan. Add the chicken thighs and fry over medium-low heat until the skin is golden and crispy. Flip and continue frying until fully cooked, then remove and set aside.  
8. In another pot, bring water to a boil and add a pinch of salt. Blanch the broccoli florets until just cooked, then remove and drain.  
9. Once the rice cooker switches to keep-warm mode, open the lid. Use a rice paddle to break the salmon into flakes and gently mix it with the rice and vegetables.  
10. Serve the mixed salmon and vegetable rice in bowls. Top with the pan-fried chicken thighs and blanched broccoli. Drizzle with yellow mustard according to personal preference.  

## Health Advice  
Salmon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids, chicken thighs provide high-quality protein, and the variety of vegetables ensures balanced nutrition. This dish is suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those pursuing a balanced diet. Note that pan-frying salmon with its skin on reduces oil usage, but the overall calorie count is still relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to control total intake.
