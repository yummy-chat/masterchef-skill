# French Cream Cheese Bread 🥖
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 300g
🍬 Caster sugar 18g
🧂 Salt 3g (used when adding butter)
🌱 Dry yeast 3g (low-sugar tolerant type)
🥛 Milk 216g
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (softened at room temperature)
🍒 Dried cranberries 40g (pre-soaked and drained)
🍊 Dried orange peel 30g (pre-soaked and drained)
🧀 Cream cheese 135g (plain, unsweetened)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🍴 Dough scraper
⏱️ Timer
🫙 Plastic wrap
🔥 Baking tray
🧊 Cooling rack
🌡️ Digital thermometer
🪷 Rolling pin
🍴 Fork
📄 A4 paper (cut into strips for surface decoration)

## Steps
1. Wash the dried cranberries and dried orange peel, soak thoroughly, then drain well.
   **Key point: Ensure fruit is well drained to prevent a sticky dough.**
2. Combine milk, caster sugar, and dry yeast in a bowl. Add bread flour and mix with a dough scraper until no dry flour remains.
3. Fold and press the dough towards the center by hand until the surface begins to smooth. Flatten the dough, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate to rest for 20 minutes.
   **Assessment: After chilling, the dough should stretch easily without being sticky and have elasticity. Flattening increases cooling surface area, helping strengthen gluten and inhibit fermentation.**
4. Remove the dough and cut into small pieces. Add softened butter and salt. Continue folding and pressing to knead the butter into the dough until no butter lumps remain and the dough becomes smooth again.
5. Transfer the dough to a work surface. Fold, press, or knead until smooth, then start slapping the dough for about 2 minutes.
   **Note: When it is hot, hand heat can easily warm the dough; slapping helps develop the gluten quickly.**
6. If the stretched dough still tears easily, flatten the dough, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and freeze for 5 minutes to bring the dough temperature down to about 24°C.
7. Remove and continue slapping for 1–2 minutes, until the dough can be stretched into a strong, unbreakable membrane without an oily sheen.
8. Flatten the dough and evenly spread the drained dried fruit over it. Roll up, cut into small pieces, stack them, then gently knead to distribute evenly and form a ball. Perform a first proofing at room temperature for half an hour.
   **Note: After mixing in the fruit, gently knead into a ball; do not over-knead.**
9. Divide the proofed dough into two portions. Roll each into a ball, gently press to release gas, fold the edges toward the center and press firmly to form a solid ball. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for half an hour.
   **Assessment: When the dough does not spring back when pressed, it is ready for shaping.**
10. Dust both sides of the dough with flour. Roll each into a circle of about 20cm in diameter and place on a baking tray. Evenly spread plain cream cheese pieces over one sheet, cover with the other sheet, and press the edges together to seal.
    **Key: The dough must not stick to the work surface to roll a perfect circle; no need to sweeten the cream cheese.**
11. Use a fork to poke holes in the surface. Lay strips of A4 paper crosswise over the top, dust with flour, then carefully remove the paper strips to create a diamond lattice pattern.
    **Purpose: Poking holes prevents the bread from puffing up and deforming during baking.**
12. Place in a preheated convection oven at 175°C and bake for about 20 minutes. Remove to a cooling rack to cool.
    **Adjust baking time according to your oven.**

## Health Advice
This French Cream Cheese Bread is rich in calcium and dietary fiber. The cranberries and dried orange peel balance the richness of the cream cheese, making it suitable as a breakfast or afternoon tea item. Pair it with black coffee to enjoy, but be mindful of portion sizes.
