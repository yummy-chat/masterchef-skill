# Vegetarian Mixed Cold Dish  
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧅 Star anise 3g  
🌿 Cinnamon bark 3g  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 4g  
🌰 Long pepper 2g  
🟤 Cardamom 2g  
🍃 Angelica dahurica 3g  
🍂 Bay leaves 2g  
🌱 Cumin seeds 5g  
🧅 Scallion 20g (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger 12g (sliced) + 50g (peeled ginger piece)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 3g  
🧄 Garlic 100g (peeled)  
💧 Water 2 jin (for simmering spices)  
💧 Cool boiled water 2 jin (for ginger-garlic juice)  
🥬 Assorted vegetables as needed (e.g., broccoli, wood ear mushrooms, dried tofu sticks, tofu skin, kelp, bracken fern, lotus root slices, cucumber, long beans, lettuce, etc.)  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar 60g  
🍾 Soy sauce 40g  
🥢 Seasoned soy sauce 30g  
🧂 Salt 30g  
🧂 MSG 18g  
🍬 White sugar 5g  
🌰 Cooked soybean oil 20g  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 large spoonful (with both oil and sediment)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok/cooking pot  
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon  
🥣 Mortar and pestle  
🥣 Bowl/basin  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing basin  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Bring two jin of water to a boil in a pot, then reduce to low heat and add the mixed spices.  
**Note**: Simmer over low heat for about 3 minutes until a light aroma of the spices is released. Overcooking may cause bitterness. After simmering, strain out the spice residue using a strainer and set the spiced water aside.  

2. Place the garlic and peeled ginger in a mortar. Add a small amount of cooked soybean oil while pounding, and grind vigorously into a fine ginger-garlic paste.  
**Key point**: The cooked soybean oil prevents the ginger and garlic from oxidizing and discoloring.  

3. Pour the pounded ginger-garlic paste into two jin of cool boiled water, stir evenly, and make ginger-garlic juice.  
4. In another pot, heat the pot and add cold oil. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, and dried chili pepper segments. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.  
5. Pour the fragrant vinegar along the edge of the pot to release the sour aroma, then bring to a boil.  
6. Once the vinegar has boiled, immediately pour in the spiced water. Add soy sauce, seasoned soy sauce, salt, MSG, and white sugar in order. Stir well, bring to a boil again, then turn off the heat.  
**Note**: Only bring to a boil. Strain out all the residue using a strainer to obtain a clear compound seasoning liquid. Allow it to cool before use.  

7. Place the prepared assorted vegetables in the mixing basin. Drizzle with the cooled compound seasoning liquid and ginger-garlic juice, and add one large spoonful of chili oil.  
8. Use a spoon to thoroughly toss the mixture from the bottom upward, ensuring the sauce evenly coats the vegetables. Then plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This cold dish is flavored with spices and enhanced by ginger and garlic, making it suitable for stimulating the appetite and aiding digestion in summer. However, it has a relatively high salt and oil content, so consumption should be moderated. Those with high blood pressure should control their intake.
