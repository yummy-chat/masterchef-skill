# Steamed Dumplings with Zucchini, Egg, and Shrimp
Cuisine Type: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Zucchini appropriate amount (shredded into thin strips)
🥚 Eggs appropriate amount (pre-scrambled and crumbled)
🍤 Shrimp appropriate amount (use prepared cleaned shrimp)
🥟 Wonton wrappers appropriate amount (store-bought)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating zucchini strips to draw out water)
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (provides saltiness and umami)
🥄 Oyster sauce appropriate amount (provides umami and color)
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount (adds aroma)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for brushing the steamer basket to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for holding, marinating, and mixing the filling)
♨️ Steamer (for steaming the dumplings)
🖌️ Oil brush (for brushing oil on the steamer basket surface)
🍽️ Plate (for serving the finished product)
🥣 Bowl (for holding accompaniments)

## Cooking Steps
1. Shred the zucchini into thin strips and place them in a bowl. Add an appropriate amount of salt, mix well, and let marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: After marinating, you must thoroughly squeeze the water out of the zucchini strips by hand. This is crucial to ensure the filling does not release water and the dumpling wrappers do not become soggy.
2. Crumble the pre-scrambled eggs and place them in the bowl together with the squeezed-dry zucchini strips and the shrimp.
3. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Stir well with a spoon to fully combine all ingredients and seasonings.
4. Take one wonton wrapper and place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold the wonton wrapper in half and pinch the edges firmly to seal.
   **Key point**: The edges of the wonton wrapper must be pinched tightly to prevent the filling from leaking out during steaming.
5. Bend the two ends of the dumpling towards the center and pinch them together to shape it into a rounded dumpling form.
6. Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil on the steamer basket. Arrange the wrapped dumplings neatly in the steamer basket, leaving space between each dumpling.
   **Key point**: Brushing with oil effectively prevents the dumplings from sticking to the steamer basket.
7. Bring the water in the steamer to a boil. Place the steamer basket with the dumplings into the steamer, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for 12 minutes.
   **Doneness check**: The dumpling wrappers will change from white to a translucent appearance, and the filling will be fully cooked.
8. Remove from the steamer and plate. Serve with chili oil or a vinegar dipping sauce for enhanced flavor.

## Health Tips
These steamed dumplings are high in protein and low in fat. Zucchini is rich in water and fiber, making them suitable for weight loss periods or when appetite is low during summer.
