# Mei Cai Kou Rou (Preserved Vegetable Pork Belly)
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork belly 200 g  
🍖 Preserved mustard greens (mei cai) 30 g  
🧂 Five-spice powder 2 g  
🧂 Cooking oil 300 ml  
🧂 White sugar 5 g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 30 ml  
🧂 Light soy sauce 20 ml  
🌶️ Small chili pepper 1 piece  
🧄 Minced garlic 10 g  
🧂 Salt 2 g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2 g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Pot  
🥘 Large bowl  
🥘 Plate  
🥘 Steamer  

## Steps
1. Soak the preserved mustard greens in clean water for 1 hour.
2. Pour 50 ml of cooking oil into the pot, place the pork belly skin-side down, fry for 1 minute, then remove and scrape the skin clean.
3. Add boiling water to the pot, place the pork belly in, and boil over high heat for 20 minutes until a chopstick can pierce through. Remove the pork belly.
4. Evenly spread dark soy sauce, five-spice powder, and white sugar over the surface of the pork belly, and let it marinate for 15 minutes.
5. In another pot, heat oil and fry the pork belly over medium heat until both sides are golden brown, about 3-5 minutes.
6. In another pot, heat oil, add the preserved mustard greens, along with the small chili pepper, minced garlic, chicken bouillon, and salt. Stir-fry until the moisture from the greens has evaporated.
7. Slice the pork belly into pieces 0.5-1 cm thick, place them in a large bowl, then layer the stir-fried preserved mustard greens on top.
8. Place in a steamer and steam over medium heat for 45 minutes.
9. Take a plate, invert it over the large bowl, and flip the pork belly onto the plate.

## Health Tips
Pork belly is high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the calories. The preserved mustard greens absorb the fat, enhancing the flavor, but people with high blood pressure should be mindful of the amounts of dark soy sauce and salt used.
