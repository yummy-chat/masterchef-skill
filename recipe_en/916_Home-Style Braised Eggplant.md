# Home-Style Braised Eggplant
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 3 long eggplants (cut into rolling knife pieces)
🍅 Some tomatoes (diced)
🫑 Some green and red bell peppers (cut into angled pieces or slices)
🧄 10g garlic (minced)
🧅 Some scallions (minced)
🫚 Some ginger (minced)
🧂 Some salt (for salting eggplant)
🍬 20g white sugar
🍶 20g vinegar
🍷 5ml huadiao wine
🥢 20ml light soy sauce
🥄 5ml dark soy sauce
🌽 3g cornstarch (for sauce) + 1 teaspoon (for coating eggplant)
🫒 10ml sesame oil
💧 30ml water
🫗 Some cooking oil (for frying eggplant)
⭐ 1 star anise

## Cooking Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chinese chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl (for salting and washing eggplant)
🥣 Small glass bowl (for mixing sauce)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Silicone spatula
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Metal strainer (for removing star anise)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the long eggplants, remove the stems, and cut into rolling knife pieces. Place in a large bowl, add an appropriate amount of salt, and mix well. Let sit to marinate until water seeps out on the surface. Then let sit for an additional 2–3 minutes to fully release water.
   **Key point**: Rolling knife cuts increase the surface area for heat exposure, speeding up cooking. Salting removes raw bitterness and forces out water, reducing oil absorption.
2. In a small bowl, combine minced garlic, white sugar, vinegar, huadiao wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, and water. Stir well to form the sauce and set aside.
3. Add clean water to the salted eggplant, wash off the surface salt, then remove and firmly squeeze out as much water as possible—the drier, the better.
   **Key point**: Squeezing out water is essential to prevent the eggplant from absorbing oil.
4. Return the squeezed eggplant to a clean large bowl, add one teaspoon of cornstarch, and toss the bowl so that each piece of eggplant is evenly coated with a thin layer of starch.
   **Key point**: The thin starch layer gelatinizes when exposed to high heat, forming a protective barrier that further prevents oil absorption.
5. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat to 40–50% hot. Add the starch-coated eggplant, spread it out flat, and fry over medium heat until one side is golden and set. Flip and shake the pan for even heating. Fry until the eggplant becomes soft and golden on all sides.
6. Add one star anise to the fried eggplant. Pour in the minced ginger and scallions and diced tomatoes. Stir-fry evenly. Cover the wok, reduce to medium heat, and braise for about 2 minutes, until the eggplant is completely soft with no firm core.
   **Key point**: Braising allows the eggplant to become thoroughly tender and flavorful.
7. Uncover the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil. Immediately add the green and red bell pepper pieces. Quickly stir-fry until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the eggplant.
   **Key point**: Add the bell peppers at the end to preserve their color and crisp-tender texture.
8. Use a strainer to remove the star anise. Turn off the heat. Stir-fry evenly and serve.

## Health Advice
Although the braised eggplant has been treated with salting and starch to reduce oil absorption, it will still absorb some oil during frying. It is recommended to serve with a plate of stir-fried leafy greens to balance the meal. Suitable for those craving a rich flavor without too much greasiness, but be mindful of rice intake as the sweet and savory sauce can stimulate the appetite.
