# Passion Fruit and Orange Special Blend
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

🍊 **Ingredients**
- Passion fruit
- Orange
- Jasmine green tea leaves
- White granulated sugar
- Ice cubes
- Honey (optional)
- Mint leaves or other green leaves (optional, for garnish)

🧂 **Seasonings**
- White granulated sugar
- Honey

🔪 **Kitchenware**
- Manual juicer
- Container
- Plastic wrap
- Clear glass (recommended 350~400 ml)

📝 **Steps**
1. Take 3 passion fruits, scoop out the pulp and juice, and place into a container.
2. Add 30 grams of white granulated sugar and 10 grams of honey (if no honey, substitute with 5 grams of white granulated sugar), and stir until well combined.
3. Seal with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 12 to 24 hours.
4. Place 3 to 6 grams of jasmine green tea leaves into another container, pour in 150 ml of boiling water, and steep for 6 minutes.
5. Add 90 grams of ice cubes to the brewed tea, stir to accelerate cooling, and once the ice cubes have melted, strain out the tea leaves to obtain approximately 240 ml of tea liquid.
6. Cut the orange lengthwise in half (knife direction perpendicular to the line between the stem and blossom ends), and from one half, slice a complete orange round for later use.
7. Cut the remaining orange halves into quarters, use a manual juicer to extract the orange juice, and set aside in a container.
8. Place 70 grams of ice cubes at the bottom of a clear glass, and pour in all of the orange juice.
9. Press the reserved orange slice against the inside wall of the glass.
10. Take one serving of the marinated passion fruit and pour it over the ice cubes.
11. Slowly pour in the jasmine green tea until the glass is full, creating a layered effect.
12. Optionally, place mint leaves or green leaves on the surface for garnish.
13. Stir well before drinking.

⚠️ **Key Points**
- The passion fruit must be marinated for 12 to 24 hours; if the quantity is too small to balance the ratio, prepare a double batch.
- Recommended ratio for jasmine green tea is 1~2:50:30 for tea leaves, water, and ice cubes, ensuring it is strained after cooling.
- When cutting the orange, the knife direction must be perpendicular to the line between the stem and blossom ends to facilitate slicing and juicing.
- During assembly, place ice cubes at the bottom of the glass first, and pour in the tea slowly to achieve layering.
- If jasmine green tea is unavailable, substitute with sparkling soda water and omit the tea brewing step.
- Honey can be replaced with white granulated sugar; the garnish of green leaves is optional.

## Health Advice
This passion fruit and orange special blend is rich in vitamin C and suitable for cooling down and cutting through greasiness in summer. However, the sugar and honey used in the marination have a high total sugar content; it is recommended to reduce the amount or omit the honey. Those with sensitive stomachs should avoid drinking it on an empty stomach, and the amount of ice can also be appropriately reduced.
