# Red Bean Marble Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
🌾 Cake flour 50g  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🍬 Fine sugar 30g  
🥛 Milk powder 6g  
🥚 Egg 45g  
🧊 Ice-cold milk 150g  
🍞 Dry yeast 3g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g  
🫘 Oil-based red bean paste 100g  
🫘 Water-based red bean paste 100g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Stand mixer  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🔗 Rolling pin  
🔪 Dough scraper  
📏 Ruler  
✂️ Scissors  
🖌️ Pastry brush  
📦 Toast mold (450g)  
🔥 Oven  
🧤 Oven mitts  

## Instructions  
1. Add bread flour, cake flour, salt, fine sugar, milk powder, egg, ice-cold milk, and dry yeast into the stand mixer bowl. Mix on low speed until a dough forms, then increase to high speed and knead until the dough reaches the initial expansion stage.  
   **Note**: Be sure to use ice-cold milk; the dough does not need strong elasticity to retain shaping ability for later steps.  
2. Add the room-temperature softened unsalted butter. Continue mixing until the butter is fully incorporated and the gluten has developed to a level suitable for shaping—no need to reach full expansion.  
3. Remove the dough, flatten it, wrap in plastic wrap, and freeze for 30 minutes to relax.  
4. Place the oil-based and water-based red bean paste in a bowl. Knead thoroughly by hand until evenly combined.  
   **Note**: Mixing the two types of bean paste balances the texture while ensuring the filling has good malleability.  
5. Place the mixed bean paste on parchment paper and roll it into a square sheet approximately 20cm x 20cm. Freeze it alongside the dough for later use.  
6. Take out the frozen dough and roll it into a sheet 20cm wide and 40cm long. Place the frozen bean paste sheet in the center. Fold the top and bottom ends of the dough over the bean paste, then pinch all seams tightly to seal.  
   **Key point**: A tight seal is essential for clear marbling. Lightly dust both sides with a small amount of bread flour to prevent sticking.  
7. Gently press and roll the dough from the center outward with a rolling pin, again into a sheet 40cm long and 20cm wide. If air bubbles appear, prick them with scissors to release. Perform a three-fold.  
8. Wrap the folded dough in plastic wrap and freeze for 10 minutes to relax. Remove, brush the surface with bread flour, and roll into a sheet 20cm wide and 50cm long.  
9. Perform a four-fold on the rolled-out sheet.  
   **Note**: If the dough shrinks significantly or the filling tends to burst, adjust the four-fold to a three-fold; for more distinct layers, stick with the four-fold.  
10. Wrap the four-folded dough in plastic wrap again and freeze for a third time for 10 minutes. Remove, brush with bread flour, and roll into a sheet 20cm wide and 30cm long. Fold it in half lengthwise.  
11. Gently press the folded dough flat with your hands. Using a sharp knife, cut lengthwise into four equal strips with the cut sides facing up to show the layers. Slightly stretch each strip, then braid them into a four-strand plait, always keeping the cut sides facing upward.  
12. Tuck the ends of the plait underneath and pinch them tightly. Place the entire braid into a 450g toast mold. Ferment at 37°C with 75% humidity for about 1.5 hours, until the dough expands to 90% full of the mold.  
13. Cover the toast mold with its lid and place in a preheated oven. Bake at 200°C top heat / 180°C bottom heat for approximately 40 minutes, until the toast is evenly browned. Remove from the oven and unmold immediately. Cool on a wire rack.

## Health Advice  
This red bean marble toast is rich in carbohydrates and contains a moderate amount of protein, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, the red bean paste has a high sugar content, so it is recommended to consume 2–3 slices per serving.
