# Soul-Burning Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, one bag (peeled and cut into large chunks)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, half a tablespoon
🥄 Light soy sauce, two tablespoons
🥄 Red-cooking soy sauce, two tablespoons (or substitute with one tablespoon of dark soy sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce, one tablespoon
🍬 Sugar, one tablespoon
🧂 Salt, half a tablespoon (for the sauce) + a pinch (for blanching)
🍶 Cooking wine, one tablespoon
🍇 Vinegar, a small amount
🌶️ Chili powder, to taste (optional, sprinkle after frying)
🫒 Cooking oil, enough to coat the bottom of the pan
🌊 Water starch, as needed (for thickening)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for mixing the sauce)
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Take a bowl, add half a bowl of water, then sequentially add thirteen-spice powder, light soy sauce, red-cooking soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, half a tablespoon of salt, cooking wine, and a small amount of vinegar. Stir until fully combined to prepare the red-cooking sauce. Set aside.
2. Peel the potatoes and cut into large chunks. Place them in a pot of cold water, add a pinch of salt, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 7-8 minutes until a chopstick can be inserted but the potatoes are not yet soft and mushy. Drain thoroughly.
   **Note**: The blanching time should be adjusted flexibly based on the size of the potato chunks to avoid overcooking and breaking apart.
3. Heat a pan over high heat, add enough oil to coat the bottom. Place the drained potato chunks in the pan, let them sit without flipping until a crispy crust forms on the bottom, then gently turn them over. Continue frying until both sides are golden and crispy.
   **Key Point**: Shake the pan to ensure even heating. Optionally, sprinkle chili powder for added fragrance according to personal preference.
4. Pour in the prepared sauce, bring to a boil over high heat and cook for 2 minutes. Reduce to medium-low heat, drizzle in water starch to thicken, gently shake the pan to coat the potatoes evenly with the sauce. Once the sauce thickens, turn off the heat and plate.
   **Key Point**: After thickening, avoid excessive stirring to prevent damaging the crispy outer layer of the potatoes.

## Health Suggestion
Potatoes are rich in dietary fiber and potassium, making them suitable for moderate consumption by those needing to control blood sugar or blood pressure. Reducing the amount of oil used during frying can lower calorie intake and help avoid post-meal drowsiness.
