# Steamed Fish Deodorizing Pretreatment
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 grass carp (fresh sea fish can also be used as a substitute)
🧂 None (this video only demonstrates the deodorizing process, no seasonings involved)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🍲 Pot
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the three sources of fishy smell inside the fish belly: scrape off the black membrane on the fish belly; cut off or remove the fishy bone at the throat; carefully scrape off the blood attached to the spine near the central ridge of the fish belly, which has the strongest fishy smell.
   **Key Point**: The attached blood must be scraped until no residue remains.

2. Prepare hot water at about 80℃: mix boiling water and cold water in a ratio of about 5:1. The water temperature should feel slightly hot to the touch but not boiling.

3. Quickly blanch the processed fish in 80℃ hot water. The mucus on the fish's surface will turn white and decompose due to the heat.
   **Key Point**: The blanching time should be short to avoid cooking the fish meat.

4. Remove the fish, then use a knife to scrape off the floating mucus and impurities along the fish skin until the fish body is smooth and clean.
   **Key Point**: Scrape gently to avoid breaking the fish skin.

5. The deodorizing process is now complete. Proceed with normal steaming or cooking steps as usual.

## Health Suggestions
Deodorizing steamed fish is key to healthy cooking. It is low in fat and high in protein, suitable for weight loss periods, the elderly, and children. Note that blanching in 80℃ hot water is sufficient; avoid cooking the fish meat.
