# Custard Cream
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Milk 175g
🍦 Vanilla pod ½ piece (scrape out the vanilla seeds, the pod can be boiled together for extra aroma)
🥚 Egg yolks 2 pieces
🍬 Caster sugar 30g
🌾 Cornstarch 10g
🧈 Butter 17.5g (add after removing from heat to enhance flavor and gloss)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🫕 Stainless steel heavy-bottomed pot
🥄 Whisk
🌡️ Thermometer
🔍 Flour sieve
🥣 Shallow dish or baking tray
🔪 Spatula
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🌀 Electric mixer
👝 Piping bag and nozzle

## Steps
1.  Slice the vanilla pod lengthwise, scrape out the vanilla seeds, and put both the seeds and the pod into the milk.
   **Note**: The pod adds aroma but should be removed before mixing with the egg yolk mixture.
2.  Heat the milk over low heat until small bubbles appear around the edges, then immediately remove from heat to avoid boiling.
3.  Add caster sugar to the egg yolks and whisk until the mixture turns pale, thick, and leaves a ribbon trail that doesn't disappear immediately when the whisk is lifted. Sift in the cornstarch and stir until no dry flour remains.
4.  Once the milk cools to about 40℃, gradually pour it into the egg yolk mixture in small batches while stirring quickly, until the liquid is smooth, even, and free of lumps.
   **Key point**: This step prevents the hot milk from cooking the egg yolks and causing lumps.
5.  Pour the mixture back into the pot and cook over low heat, stirring continuously and quickly, especially at the bottom and center. The liquid will first thicken and may develop lumps; keep stirring until the lumps disappear and the cream becomes smooth, glossy, and flows slowly. The entire thickening process should take less than a minute.
   **Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent burning; the final consistency should be a smooth, flowing cream when the whisk is lifted.
6.  Remove from heat and immediately add the butter, stirring until fully melted. Quickly spread the cream into a shallow dish, or cool it in an ice water bath. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the cream and refrigerate for 1-2 hours until fully set.
   **Key point**: Rapid cooling and direct contact with plastic wrap prevent bacterial growth and skin formation on the surface.
7.  Remove the set custard cream and beat it with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy, similar to the texture of thick yogurt. It is then ready to be piped with a piping bag.

## Health Advice
Custard cream is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it ideal for enhancing the texture and nutritional value of baked desserts. However, egg yolks, sugar, and butter are high in calories, so portion control is recommended during weight management.
