# Tomato Hot Pot Broth
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍅 4 tomatoes (peeled after blanching, cut two into large chunks and two into thick slices)  
🧅 Appropriate amount of onion (finely minced, about half a bowl)  
🌿 Appropriate amount of celery (finely minced, about half a bowl)  
🧄 A handful of garlic (minced into garlic paste)  
🧈 A piece of butter  
🌿 2 bay leaves  
🧂 A pinch of salt  
🥄 Appropriate amount of light soy sauce  
🍬 Appropriate amount of sugar  
💧 Appropriate amount of boiling/hot water (added in two batches)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥣 Blender or food processor  
🥄 Strainer (slotted spoon)  
🍲 Soup pot or hot pot basin  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥄 Wooden spatula  

## Instructions  
1. Score a cross on the surface of the tomatoes, blanch in boiling water, then peel. Cut two tomatoes into large chunks, place in a blender, add minced garlic, and blend into a tomato puree. Cut the other two tomatoes into thick slices and set aside.  
2. Finely mince the onion and celery separately.  
3. Place the wok over heat, add a piece of butter and heat until melted. Add bay leaves, minced onion, and minced celery. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant, being careful not to burn.  
4. Pour in the prepared tomato puree, simmer briefly, then add the thick tomato slices and a pinch of salt.  
5. Continue simmering until the broth thickens and the tomatoes are cooked through. Season with light soy sauce and sugar, adjusting the flavor to a balanced sweet-and-sour taste.  
6. Add an appropriate amount of boiling water and boil vigorously for 2 minutes.  
7. Strain the cooked broth through a strainer into a hot pot basin. Use a spoon to press the tomato and vegetable solids in the strainer into a puree, allowing the flavors to fully infuse into the broth.  
8. Add more hot water to the strained broth to adjust the consistency, and place a few fresh thick tomato slices as a garnish.  
9. Once the broth comes to a boil, it is ready for cooking various ingredients.  

## Health Advice  
Tomato hot pot broth is rich in vitamin C and lycopene, making it ideal for cutting through grease and for those who do not like spicy food. Be mindful of the amount of butter used, and use less salt to avoid excessive sodium intake. Add sugar in moderation to prevent blood sugar fluctuations.
