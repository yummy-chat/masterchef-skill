# Coke Braised Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken wings, about 8-10 pieces
🧅 Green onion, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🍺 Beer, 4 small spoons
🥤 Coca-Cola, 1 can (about 330ml, classic flavor recommended)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🧴 Light soy sauce, 1 spoon
🧴 Dark soy sauce, half a spoon
🧂 Salt, add as desired (not added in the video, relying on the saltiness from the soy sauce and cola)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍲 Stockpot
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Ladle
🥣 Skimmer or slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Lid for the wok

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the green onion into sections, and slice or shred the ginger. Set aside.
2. Place the chicken wings in a pot of cold water. Add cooking wine, green onion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and star anise. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and cook until the wings change color. Remove and drain thoroughly.
   **Note**: Starting with cold water helps draw out blood. Drain the wings well to prevent oil splatter during pan-frying.
3. Heat the wok over high heat and add a thin layer of oil. Once the oil is hot, reduce to medium-low heat and add the chicken wings. Pan-fry until both sides are golden and slightly crispy, minimizing the number of times you flip them.
4. Add the shredded ginger and stir-fry for about 20 seconds until fragrant.
   **Note**: Add the ginger only after the wings are fried, to prevent it from burning.
5. Drizzle in 4 small spoons of beer, then turn the heat to high and stir-fry. Let the alcohol evaporate to remove any fishy smell. When the beer is nearly reduced, add 1 spoon of light soy sauce and half a spoon of dark soy sauce. Stir well to combine.
6. Pour in the entire can of cola. The liquid should just about cover the chicken wings—do not add extra water. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and braise until the liquid is reduced by half.
7. Remove the lid, turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly with chopsticks or a spatula. Continue until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the chicken wings.
   **Note**: Stir continuously during the reduction to prevent burning. The final color should be a glossy amber—turn off the heat once achieved.
8. Transfer to a serving plate. Optionally, sprinkle with toasted white sesame seeds for garnish.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in sugar and protein. It is recommended to serve with light vegetable sides. Individuals sensitive to blood sugar should control their portion size. Be sure to stir frequently during the reduction to avoid burning.
