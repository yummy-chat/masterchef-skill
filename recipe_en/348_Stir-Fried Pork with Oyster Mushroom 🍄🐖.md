# Stir-Fried Pork with Oyster Mushroom 🍄🐖
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish 🏠
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons 🌿❄️🌸🍂

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🍄 Oyster mushroom – to taste
🐖 Pork front leg meat – to taste (separate lean and fatty parts, slice thinly)
🧄 Garlic – to taste (crushed)
🌶️ Red bell pepper – to taste (julienned)
🧅 Green garlic sprouts – to taste (cut into sections)
🧂 Salt – to taste
🥫 Dark soy sauce – to taste
🧅🫚💧 Green onion and ginger water – to taste
🌽 Cornstarch – to taste
🍬 White sugar – a pinch
🐔 Chicken bouillon – to taste
🥫 Light soy sauce – to taste
🍶 Vinegar – to taste (splashed in before removing from heat)
🫒 Vegetable oil – a small amount (for the base oil)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment 🔧
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥄 Spatula
🍳 Wok
🔥 Stove

## Cooking Steps 👨‍🍳
1. Wash the oyster mushroom, tear it into small strips along its grain. Separate the fatty and lean parts of the pork front leg meat, slice both thinly. Julienne the red bell pepper. Cut the green garlic sprouts into sections. Crush the garlic.
2. Add salt, dark soy sauce, and green onion and ginger water to the lean meat slices. Mix well until the liquid is fully absorbed. Then add cornstarch and mix thoroughly to coat.
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the oyster mushroom until softened, then remove and drain well.
4. Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of vegetable oil. Add the fatty pork slices. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders, the edges curl slightly, and the fat becomes translucent.
   **Note**: Do not remove the rendered lard; leave it in the wok to enhance flavor.
5. Add the coated lean meat slices. Turn to medium-high heat and quickly break them apart, stir-frying until the color changes.
   **Note**: Stir quickly to prevent the meat slices from clumping.
6. Add the crushed garlic cloves. Stir-fry until fragrant and the edges of the garlic turn slightly golden.
7. Add the julienned red bell pepper. Stir-fry over high heat until slightly softened and just cooked through.
8. Add the blanched oyster mushroom. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to combine and create a "wok hei" (breath of the wok).
9. Add salt, white sugar, chicken bouillon, and light soy sauce to season. Stir-fry until the seasoning evenly coats the ingredients.
10. Sprinkle in the green garlic sprout sections. At the same time, splash a small amount of vinegar along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly a few times, then remove from heat and plate.
    **Note**: Add the green garlic at the end to keep it crisp. The vinegar splashed along the wok edge releases a rich, aromatic tang.

## Health Tips 💪
Oyster mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber and fungal polysaccharides. Paired with lean pork for high-quality protein, this dish is suitable for weight-loss periods and for those looking to boost immunity. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of dark soy sauce used. Blanching removes the earthy taste of oyster mushrooms but should not be prolonged to avoid nutrient loss.
