# Boiled Choy Sum
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Choy sum, as needed (only the tender front part)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (2-3 sections of white part, sliced diagonally into thin strips)
🌶️ Dried red chili peppers, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🧂 Salt, three teaspoons (for blanching, to make the water slightly salty)
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more than for stir-frying, added to the blanching water to form an oil film)
🥜 Peanut oil, as needed (for the hot oil drizzle)
🍶 Steamed fish soy sauce, as needed (drizzled at the end)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍲 Stockpot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking in ice water)
🍳 Wok
🍽️ Serving plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the choy sum: Cut off the old roots, keep the tender front part, and trim the heads neatly; cut the thicker root sections of the stems into thick slices and set aside.
2. Slice the white part of the scallion diagonally into thin strips, and cut the dried red chili peppers into thin strips.
3. Fill a pot with plenty of water and bring to a boil. Add salt and cooking oil, creating a lightly salted water with an oil film on the surface.
4. First, add the thick stem slices. Blanch in boiling water for about 20 seconds until they turn bright green. Quickly remove and immerse in ice water. Drain well once fully cooled.
5. Next, add the tender choy sum leaves and stems. Blanch in boiling water for about 30 seconds until they turn a deep green. Immediately remove and immerse in ice water to cool, then drain well.
6. Plating: Spread the thick stem slices on the bottom of the plate, and neatly arrange the choy sum on top.
7. Lay the scallion strips and red chili pepper strips over the choy sum.
8. Heat peanut oil in a wok until it emits heavy smoke. Quickly and evenly drizzle the hot oil over the scallion and chili pepper strips.
9. Drizzle steamed fish soy sauce along the edge of the plate and serve.
**Key points**: Use enough water for blanching to prevent a sudden temperature drop; the oil must be heated until smoking to fully release the aroma of scallion and chili; the ice water bath is essential to keep the choy sum green and crisp.

## Health Advice
This boiled choy sum dish is low in fat and high in fiber, rich in vitamin C, and is especially suitable for those managing their weight or looking to cleanse the palate and cut through greasiness. Blanching followed by an ice water bath locks in the vibrant green color and crisp texture, but steamed fish soy sauce is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
