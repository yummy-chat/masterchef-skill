# Black Pepper Pan-Seared Shrimp
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Large Shrimp 1 lb (any variety works, such as white shrimp, tiger prawns, or greentail prawns)  
🧈 Butter 1 small piece  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  
🧂 Soy sauce as needed  
🍶 Cooking wine as needed  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance freshness)  
🌿 Black pepper powder as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl (for mixing sauce)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or wok ladle  
🥢 Long chopsticks (for flipping)  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the shrimp thoroughly, snip off the antennae, and make a shallow cut along the belly lengthwise to help the flavor penetrate.  
2. Prepare the pan-searing sauce: In a bowl, mix soy sauce, cooking wine, and sugar, stirring until combined. Set aside.  
3. Heat the frying pan over high heat, add cooking oil, then lay the shrimp in a single layer in the pan. Pan-sear until the bottom changes color, then sprinkle with black pepper powder.  
   **Note**: Arrange the shrimp evenly; cooking the black pepper together with the shrimp fully releases its aroma.  
4. Flip each shrimp using long chopsticks, and continue pan-searing until both sides are crispy and the shells turn red.  
5. Place the butter in the center of the pan, and wait until it fully melts and blends with the shrimp oil.  
6. Pour in the prepared pan-searing sauce, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry for 5–10 seconds until the sauce evenly coats the shrimp. Remove from heat immediately.  
   **Key Point**: Quick stir-frying over high heat promotes a caramelization reaction between the sugar and cooking wine, enhancing the overall depth of aroma.  

## Health Tips  
Shrimp are a high-quality, low-fat protein. Paired with the rich aroma of black pepper and butter, this dish is suitable for muscle-building and weight-loss diets. Note that butter and soy sauce are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should control their intake.
