# Finger-Licking Original Fried Chicken  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken drumsticks (yellow-feathered chicken, preferably older chickens)  
💧 Water 1 liter (for the brine)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 250 g  
🥛 Nonfat dry milk powder 10 g  
🥚 Egg white powder 1 g (optional, for better adhesion)  
🌽 Cornstarch 2 g  
🧂 Salt 30 g for brining + 9 g for coating  
🧂 MSG 3 g for brining + 10 g for coating  
🧄 Garlic A pinch of minced garlic  
🌿 White pepper powder 5 g  
🌿 Black pepper powder 4 g (freshly ground at the end)  
🧄 Garlic powder 3 g  
🧅 Onion powder 2 g  
🌶️ Chili powder 0.5 g  
🧂 Celery salt 0.5 g  
🌶️ Paprika 0.15 g  
🌿 Cumin powder 0.0625 g  
🌰 Nutmeg powder 0.03125 g  
🌿 Thyme 0.03125 g  
🌿 Oregano 0.0625 g  
🌿 Allspice 0.4 g  
🫒 Sunflower oil As needed (about 1/3 of the pot’s capacity)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥛 Glass measuring cup  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🫗 Mortar and pestle  
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon  
🍲 Enameled cast iron pot (with lid)  
🌡️ Oil thermometer  
🥢 Metal tongs/slotted spoon  
🔥 Oven  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Prepare the brine: Mix 1 liter of water, 30 g salt, 3 g MSG, and a pinch of minced garlic. Stir until fully dissolved.  
2. Prepare the dry coating base: Combine all-purpose flour, nonfat dry milk powder, egg white powder, cornstarch, 9 g salt, and 10 g MSG. Mix well.  
3. Grind the spices: Except for black pepper, place all spices along with 10 g MSG from the coating in a mortar. Grind thoroughly into a uniform powder, then mix with the dry coating base. Add freshly ground black pepper at the end.  
4. Wash the chicken drumsticks, then submerge them in the brine. Refrigerate and marinate for 12 hours.  
5. Remove the marinated drumsticks, gently rinse off excess salt with water, and shake lightly 7 times to drain.  
6. Coating: Place the drumsticks in the coating mixture. Use your hands to press and rub the flour onto the chicken. Repeat rolling 10 times, then press in a clockwise direction 7 times to ensure the coating adheres tightly. Finally, gently tap the two drumsticks together to shake off excess flour.  
   **Key point**: The pressing and rubbing technique is essential for creating a flaky, textured crust.  
7. Heat sunflower oil in the enameled cast iron pot to 175°C. The oil should fill about 1/3 of the pot.  
8. Place the coated drumsticks flat into the pot. Cover with the lid and fry over medium heat for 5 minutes. Rotate the pot 180 degrees and fry covered for another 5 minutes. Remove the lid, reduce heat to low, flip the drumsticks, and fry for 5–8 minutes more until the crust is golden brown and sounds crisp.  
9. Remove the drumsticks and let them drain on a wire rack for about 15 seconds. Immediately transfer to an oven preheated to 80°C to rest for 8 minutes. This helps the crust become crispier while keeping the meat juicy.  
10. Serve and enjoy. The crust is crispy, the chicken is tender, and the flavor is rich.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but the brining and coating process results in high sodium content. Pair it with a fresh vegetable salad to balance it out. Suitable for occasional consumption. People with high blood pressure should only eat in small amounts.
