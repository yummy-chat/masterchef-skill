# Sandpot Garlic Fish 🐟
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 Mandarin fish (cleaned, cut into thick slices)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (peeled into cloves)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (cut into small pieces)
🧅 Spring onion bulbs, to taste (cut into small pieces; can substitute with onion)
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers, to taste (finely diced, for color)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped, for aroma)
🧄 About 50g homemade garlic sauce (used in two batches)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili, to taste (optional, for extra heat)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🍾 Erguotou (high-proof white liquor), to taste (drizzle and ignite before serving)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Fine mesh strainer
🧻 Paper towels
🍲 Sand pot (clay pot)
🔥 Stove
🥢 Chopsticks/spoon
🧻 Damp towel (to place on the lid for sealing and heat conduction)

## Instructions
1. Clean the mandarin fish after slaughter, cut into evenly thick slices. Rinse in clean water 1-2 times until the water runs clear, removing any blood. Drain the fish pieces using a fine mesh strainer, then pat dry with paper towels.
2. Cut garlic, ginger, and spring onion bulbs into evenly sized pieces. Dice the green and red bell peppers, and chop the scallions.
3. To the dried fish pieces, add about 50g garlic sauce, cooking wine, salt, and sugar. Mix well, then add cornstarch and toss until the fish is evenly coated. Add bird’s eye chili if desired.
4. Heat the sand pot over high heat, pour in cooking oil. First, stir-fry the ginger pieces until fragrant, then add garlic cloves and spring onion bulbs, stir-frying over medium heat until all aromatics release their fragrance.
5. Evenly spread the marinated fish pieces over the aromatics in a single layer. Drizzle another layer of garlic sauce on top, then pour cooking wine along the edge of the pot. Cover with the lid and cook over medium heat for 3-4 minutes.
**Doneness check**: A skewer should easily pierce the thickest part of the fish when fully cooked.

6. Remove the lid, sprinkle with diced bell peppers and chopped scallions. Re-cover the lid, drizzle Erguotou along the edge, and immediately ignite. Once the flame naturally extinguishes, serve.
**Note**: Use high-proof liquor only; exercise caution when igniting.

## Health Tips
This dish is cooked using the sand pot method, preserving the fish’s tenderness and garlic aroma. It is suitable for fitness and weight-loss diets. Control the amount of salt and cooking oil to reduce calorie intake.
