# Green Bean and Egg Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Long green beans, about 300g
🥚 Eggs, 4 pieces
🍤 Dried shrimp, a small handful
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for spraying, coating the pan, and frying)
🍶 White vinegar, 2 drops
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🌶️ Ground pepper, to taste
🌽 Cornstarch slurry, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🕳️ Slotted spoon
💨 Oil spray bottle
🍽️ Plate (for flipping)
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Arrange the washed whole green beans on the steaming rack and spray with a little cooking oil. Once the water in the steamer is boiling, place the rack in and steam for about 8 minutes until fully cooked.
   **Key point**: Steaming shortens subsequent cooking time, makes the beans easier to absorb flavors, and preserves nutrients.

2. Crack 4 eggs into a bowl, add 2 drops of white vinegar, and whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture is even.
   **Key point**: White vinegar removes the eggy smell and makes the egg fluffier.

3. Remove the steamed beans, rinse them under cold water, drain well, and cut into small, even pieces.

4. Place the bean pieces into a bowl, add salt, sugar, ground pepper, the egg mixture, and an appropriate amount of cornstarch slurry, and stir well to combine.
   **Key point**: Adding cornstarch slurry gives the finished dish a smoother and more tender texture.

5. Heat the wok until smoking, pour in a spoonful of cold oil to coat the entire surface, then pour out the hot oil, leaving a thin layer of oil on the bottom.
   **Key point**: Using the "hot wok, cold oil" method effectively prevents sticking.

6. Over low heat, stir-fry the dried shrimp until fragrant to remove any fishy smell.

7. Pour the green bean and egg mixture into the wok. Using a spatula, gently push from one side, allowing the uncooked egg mixture to flow to the edges. Cook over medium-low heat until the bottom is mostly set.

8. Place a plate on top of the pancake, invert the wok to transfer the pancake onto the plate, then slide it back into the wok to cook the other side until both sides are golden and set.
   **Key point**: Using a plate to flip the pancake prevents it from breaking apart.

9. Cut the pancake into irregular pieces directly in the wok using the spatula, then serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Green beans are rich in dietary fiber, and eggs provide high-quality protein. This dish is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet. Ensure the beans are thoroughly steamed to prevent saponin poisoning. Dried shrimp are naturally salty, so reduce the amount of salt accordingly. Serve with multigrain rice for increased satiety.
