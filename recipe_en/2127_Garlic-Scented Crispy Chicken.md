# Garlic-Scented Crispy Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thighs, appropriate amount (cut into large pieces)  
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (for coating)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (used once for marinating and once for stir-frying minced garlic)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (used once for marinating and once for stir-frying minced garlic)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount  
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating chicken)  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the chicken thighs into large pieces, place them in a bowl, add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, white sugar, and cooking wine. Mix well and marinate for half an hour.
   **Note**: Ensure sufficient marinating time so the chicken is fully flavored.

2. Evenly coat both sides of the marinated chicken thighs with cornstarch.

3. Pour oil into a wok and heat until warm. Add the cornstarch-coated chicken, and pan-fry over medium-low heat, about 4 minutes per side, until the surface is golden and crispy.
   **Note**: Control the heat to avoid burning.

4. Remove the pan-fried chicken and set aside.

5. In another wok, heat oil, add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add light soy sauce and oyster sauce, stirring evenly.

6. Add the pan-fried chicken back in, stir-fry quickly to coat the chicken evenly with the garlic-scented sauce.
   **Note**: Stir-fry evenly to ensure the garlic aroma and sauce fully adhere to the chicken.

7. Serve on a plate and enjoy.

## Health Advice  
Garlic-Scented Crispy Chicken is rich in protein, but coating with cornstarch and frying increases calories; it's suitable for consumption with vegetables during fitness muscle-building phases. Pay attention to oil temperature to avoid excessive acrylamide formation.
