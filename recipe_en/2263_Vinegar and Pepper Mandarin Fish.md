# Vinegar and Pepper Mandarin Fish
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 mandarin fish (cleaned and gutted)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallions (chopped scallions, fine scallion shreds for garnish)
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (minced)
🌿 Appropriate amount of cilantro (cut stalk and leaves into sections)
🍲 One large bowl of rich chicken broth (enough to submerge the fish)
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper powder
🐖 One stir-fry spoon of lard
🍶 Appropriate amount of Shaoxing wine
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt
🧂 Appropriate amount of MSG
🍶 Approximately 50g vinegar
🫗 Two drops of sesame oil

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup spoon
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl/basin
🍽️ Glass fish plate

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Prepare aromatics**: Cut scallions in two ways – chopped scallions for the soup, and fine scallion shreds for garnish. Cut cilantro into inch-long sections. Mince the ginger.
2.  **Clean the fish**: Remove any remaining innards and gills from the fish belly. Use scissors to cut off all fins. Gently scrape the black membrane off the fish skin with the back of a knife, being careful not to break the skin or flesh.
3.  **First blanching**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the mandarin fish for about 20 seconds, ladling hot water over the fish to ensure even heating. Immediately remove and submerge in cold water. Once cooled, scrape and wash the fish skin again until it is clean and white, thoroughly removing any fishy smell.
4.  **Score the fish**: The belly side is the front. Score the front side with a crosshatch pattern, cutting down to the bone, with cuts no more than 2 cm apart. Score the back side only with diagonal slits to prevent the fish from falling apart during cooking.
5.  **Second blanching**: Soak the scored fish in boiling water for about 1 minute, until the cuts open, the fish eyes turn white, and the mouth slightly opens. Remove the fish.
6.  **Prepare the serving plate**: Pour in approximately 50g of vinegar and two drops of sesame oil into the fish plate in advance.
7.  **Fry the soup base**: Heat the wok and melt the lard. First, fry the white pepper powder over low heat until fragrant. Immediately add the chopped scallions and minced ginger, stir-frying until aromatic.
8.  **Season the broth**: Pour in the rich chicken broth. Add Shaoxing wine, salt, and MSG. Once the soup comes to a boil, use a slotted spoon to remove the scallion and ginger pieces, leaving a clear soup.
9.  **Cook the fish**: Gently place the mandarin fish into the broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to the lowest heat. Maintain a very gentle simmer and cook the fish slowly for about 15 minutes until it is just cooked through. This step uses no frying, keeping the soup a milky white color.
10. **Serve**: Carefully lift the fish out and place it on the prepared plate with vinegar and sesame oil. Slowly pour the hot soup over the fish. Garnish with fine scallion shreds and cilantro sections before serving.

## Health Advice
This Vinegar and Pepper Mandarin Fish is primarily a clear-braised dish. White pepper powder and vinegar can gently stimulate the appetite, making it suitable for those with poor digestion or pursuing a low-fat diet. It is recommended to eat it hot; the white pepper powder in the soup helps ward off cold and warm the body, but those with excessive stomach acid should consume less of the vinegar soup.
