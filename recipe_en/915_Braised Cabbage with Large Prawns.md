# Braised Cabbage with Large Prawns
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Chinese Cabbage (Napa Cabbage) About half a head (leaves only, tear into large pieces; yellow-hearted cabbage is recommended for a better appearance)  
🍤 Large Prawns (Jumbo Shrimp/Tiger Prawns) 10-15 (preferably fatty jumbo shrimp, shelled with tails left on, heads and whiskers reserved)  
🧅 Scallions To taste (cut into sections, used for oil extraction and broth)  
🫚 Ginger To taste (sliced, used for oil extraction, broth, and frying the prawns)  
🌿 Cilantro About 1 small bunch (only the stems, added before serving for aroma)  
🧂 Salt To taste  
🌶️ White Pepper To taste  
🍶 Rice Wine A small amount  
🦪 Oyster Sauce A small amount  
🌾 Cornstarch Slurry To taste (cornstarch mixed with water, used for a light thickening)  
🫒 Homemade Shrimp Oil Used twice (derived from stir-frying the prawn heads and whiskers; used as base oil when frying the prawns and drizzled at the end for enhanced flavor)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or Frying Pan  
🔪 Knife and Scissors  
🌀 Food Processor (Blender, used to grind prawn heads and whiskers for broth)  
🥣 Strainer  
🍚 Bowl  
🔥 Stove  

## Steps
### Preparation
1. Peel off the outer tough layers of the Chinese cabbage, using only the leaves. Tear into large pieces by hand.  
**Note**: Hand-tearing keeps the fibers intact, making it easier for the cabbage to absorb flavor.

2. Cut off the prawn rostrum, whiskers, and legs. Devein by cutting along the back and removing the vein. Shell the prawns, keeping the last tail segment and tail fin intact, forming a "phoenix tail" shape. Set aside the prawn heads and whiskers separately.  
**Key Point**: Do not discard the prawn heads and whiskers; they are essential for extracting umami flavor.

### Making Shrimp Oil
3. Add oil to the wok over low heat. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, prawn heads, and whiskers. Continuously stir-fry and press the heads until the oil turns red and releases a rich aroma.  
**Key Point**: Include the whiskers in the stir-frying. Strain the finished shrimp oil through a strainer and set aside.

### Making Shrimp Broth
4. Place the oil-extracted prawn heads and whiskers into a food processor. Add an appropriate amount of water and process into a paste.
5. Heat oil in another wok. Sauté ginger slices and scallion sections. Pour in the prawn paste mixture. Cook for about half an hour to fully extract the flavor. Strain through a fine strainer to obtain a clear, golden, and rich pure shrimp broth.  
**Doneness Check**: The broth should be thick in consistency and golden in color.  
   **Key Point**: This is an advanced "turning waste into treasure" technique that greatly enhances the dish's freshness.

### Cooking
6. Add the first batch of homemade shrimp oil to the wok, then add ginger slices. Add the prepared prawns and pan-fry over medium heat until both sides turn red and the surface has a slight charred aroma.  
**Doneness Check**: The prawn bodies should curl and turn red. Using shrimp oil enhances the color and flavor.

7. Add scallion sections to the fried prawns. Then add all the torn cabbage leaves. Turn the heat to maximum and stir-fry quickly until the cabbage wilts and shrinks.
8. Add salt, white pepper, a small amount of rice wine, and oyster sauce. Stir-fry to combine evenly.  
**Key Point**: Absolutely do not add soy sauce, as it will ruin the color and mask the fresh flavor.

9. Pour in the strained shrimp broth. Braise together with the cabbage and prawns until the flavors meld. The cabbage can be cooked a little longer. When the broth has reduced to an appropriate consistency, pour in the cornstarch slurry to create a light glaze.  
**Note**: Using cornstarch creates a glossier finish.

10. Before removing from the wok, sprinkle in the cilantro stem sections. Drizzle in a small amount of shrimp oil. Quickly toss to combine, then plate. The final drizzle of oil further brightens the color and enhances the aroma.

## Health Advice  
This dish has a rich flavor from the shrimp oil, and the cabbage thoroughly absorbs the freshness of the shrimp broth, making it appealing even for those on a diet. It is suitable for seafood lovers, but as prawn heads are high in cholesterol, individuals with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
