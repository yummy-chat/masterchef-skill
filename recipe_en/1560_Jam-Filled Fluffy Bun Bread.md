# Jam-Filled Fluffy Bun Bread  
Cuisine: Others  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 415g  
🍬 Sugar 40g  
🧂 Salt 6g  
🥚 Whole egg 1  
🥛 Milk 258g (adjust based on room temperature; use cold milk if room temperature is 25℃)  
🍞 Dry yeast 4.5g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 22g (softened in advance)  
🍓 Jam as needed (e.g., raspberry jam)  
🍚 Cake flour as needed (for surface decoration)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Stand mixer with dough hook  
🔪 Dough scraper  
Rolling pin  
🍰 6-inch round cake pans ×2  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔥 Oven  
🍴 Spatula/small knife  
🕸️ Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1. Accurately weigh all ingredients. Evenly grease the interior and bottom of the pans with oil, then line with parchment paper, ensuring the sides are fully covered as well.  
   **Key**: The parchment paper must completely cover the pans to prevent the bread from sticking.  
2. Add the bread flour, sugar, and salt to the stand mixer bowl. Mix on low speed until combined.  
3. Add the whole egg and milk. Mix on low speed until no dry flour remains, then add the dry yeast.  
4. Continue mixing until the dough comes together, then add the softened butter.  
   **Key**: The butter should be softened in advance. There is no need to worry about the dough's consistency; add the butter as soon as it comes together.  
5. First, mix on low speed until the butter is fully absorbed into the dough. Once no butter is visible, switch to high speed and knead until the dough reaches about 80% gluten development. This recipe does not require kneading to a windowpane stage.  
6. Remove the dough, slap it against the work surface a few times, then shape it into a ball. Place it in a container, cover with a damp cloth, and let it proof at room temperature for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.  
   **How to check**: Dip a finger in flour and poke a hole in the dough. The hole should not shrink back or collapse.  
7. Remove the dough, gently press to release gas, and divide it into 4 equal portions. Shape each into a ball.  
   **Key**: Handle the dough gently when shaping to avoid damaging the surface.  
8. Cover with a damp cloth and rest at room temperature for 20 minutes. Refrigerated resting is also possible but will extend the subsequent proofing time.  
9. Take one rested dough piece, shape it into a long strip, flatten it, and use a rolling pin to roll it into a rectangular sheet that is narrower at the top and wider at the bottom.  
   **Key**: Do not let the rolling pin go over the top and bottom edges of the dough; roll from the middle outward. If the thickness is uneven, roll the thicker parts toward the thinner areas.  
10. Flip the dough sheet over. Sprinkle a thin layer of flour on the lower half, then use a bench scraper to cut it into thin strips, leaving the top portion uncut.  
    **Key**: The flour prevents the strips from sticking; thinner strips yield a more attractive finished product.  
11. Evenly spread jam on the uncut upper portion, leaving a border to prevent overflow.  
12. Roll downwards from the jam-spread side. Make one turn and press firmly, then continue rolling downward from both ends of the roll simultaneously to create a tighter seal and prevent the jam from leaking.  
13. Place the rolled dough pieces into the pans one by one, pressing the seams flat with your hands to prevent deformation after baking.  
14. Proof in an environment at 37°C with 75% humidity until the dough rises to about 80–90% full of the pan.  
15. Evenly sift a layer of cake flour over the surface of the proofed dough. This helps prevent over-browning during baking.  
16. Bake in a preheated oven on the middle-lower rack at 150°C (top heat) and 200°C (bottom heat) for about 25 minutes.  
17. Remove from the pan immediately after baking and place on a cooling rack to cool, preventing shrinkage and excess moisture.  

## Health Advice  
This jam-filled fluffy bun bread uses milk and a small amount of sugar, making it suitable as an energy supplement for breakfast or afternoon tea. Note that jam has a high sugar content; pairing with unsweetened tea can help balance it. Bread flour provides satiety, but the calorie count is not low, so it is best enjoyed in portions and not consumed excessively at one time.
