# Cumin Potato Slices  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 1 potato (medium-sized)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped)  
🧂 1 tablespoon salt (for blanching)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🌿 Cumin seeds, as needed  
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed  
✨ Ground cumin, as needed  
🌾 Crispy sesame seeds, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍲 Pot for boiling  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫗 Oil spray bottle  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Peel the potato and slice it to the thickness of a one-yuan coin.  
**Key Point**: Slices that are too thick won’t puff up easily, while slices that are too thin may burn.  

2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add one tablespoon of salt, and blanch the potato slices for about two minutes.  
**Doneness Check**: The slices are ready when they become slippery to the touch; remove them and drain thoroughly.  
**Note**: The water must be completely drained; otherwise, oil may splatter during frying, and the slices won’t puff up easily.  

3. Heat a flat-bottomed pan, spray a little oil, and lay the potato slices flat in the pan. Fry over medium-low heat.  
**Doneness Check**: Fry until the potato slices gradually puff up like little wallets and turn golden brown.  

4. Using an empty space in the pan, toast the cumin seeds until fragrant.  
**Key Point**: Raw cumin seeds must be toasted first to release their aroma.  

5. Add the fried potato slices to the toasted cumin seeds, drizzle with light soy sauce, and sprinkle with chili flakes, ground cumin, and crispy sesame seeds. Toss to combine evenly while sprinkling.  
6. Sprinkle with chopped scallions, quickly toss to mix, and serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is high in carbohydrates and low in fat. Pair it with a portion of protein, such as chicken breast or tofu, to avoid rapid blood sugar spikes. Suitable for satisfying cravings during a weight loss phase, but be sure to drain the potato slices thoroughly after blanching to reduce oil absorption.
