# Sliced Pork in Chili Oil

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Recommended Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 300g pork loin (trimmed of sinew, sliced against the grain to the thickness of about one and a half coins)
🥬 Chinese cabbage, cabbage, soybean sprouts, and lettuce or other greens, total weight ≤500g (adjustable types)
🌶️ 50g dried chilies (keep seeds)
🌿 12g Sichuan peppercorns
🧄 1 head of garlic (3 cloves sliced, the rest minced)
🧅 10g scallions (white part, sliced into scallion flowers)
🫚 Ginger, about 1/6 the size of a palm (minced)
🥚 1 egg
🌽 30g cornstarch (mixed with 17g water to form a slurry) + about 5g (for later thickening)

**For Chili Oil Paste**
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount

**For Marinating**
🧂 Salt, about 2g

**For Broth Base**
🫘 40g Pixian doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste, finely minced)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (used in batches)
💧 600~650g water
🧂 1g white pepper powder
🍬 5g sugar
🧂 5g dark soy sauce
🦪 5g oyster sauce
🧂 5g chicken bouillon powder

**For Aromatics Splash**
🧄 Garlic, minced, as needed
⚪ White sesame seeds, optional, as needed
🫒 Hot oil, as needed (heated until smoking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Small saucepan or measuring cup
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Soup ladle
🫙 Digital scale
🧻 Paper towels
🥘 Small pot (for heating oil)
🥣 Large bowl (for serving)

## Instructions

### Preparation
1. **Make Chili Oil Paste**
   Cut the stems off the dried chilies, keeping the seeds. Add a little oil to the wok over low heat. Add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry slowly until the chilies deepen in color and the Sichuan peppercorns turn purplish-red. Remove immediately and let cool. Once completely cooled, chop finely or crush into coarse pieces. Set aside.
   **Key Point**: Keep heat low throughout to prevent burning and bitterness.

2. **Prepare Vegetables**
   Wash all vegetables, cut as needed. Keep total weight under about 500g.

3. **Prepare Aromatics**
   Mince ginger. Slice the white part of scallions into scallion flowers. Slice 3 cloves of garlic; crush and finely mince the remaining cloves. Keep garlic slices and minced garlic separate.

4. **Marinate the Pork**
   Trim sinew from the pork loin, pat dry with paper towels. Slice against the grain into pieces about one and a half coins thick. Add salt and mix well. Add the whole egg, crush the yolk and stir continuously in one direction for about 3 minutes, until the meat feels dry and sticky and a glue-like texture appears at the bottom of the bowl. Gradually add the prepared thick cornstarch slurry, continuing to mix in one direction until well incorporated and the meat feels firm. Set aside.
   **Key Point**: Slicing against the grain ensures tenderness; stirring thoroughly until gluey is essential; using a wet cornstarch slurry yields a more silky texture.

### Cooking
1. **Stir-fry Vegetables for the Base**
   Leave a little oil in the wok. Add all vegetables and stir-fry over high heat until they soften and wilt. Add about 1g of salt for basic seasoning. Remove and spread evenly on the bottom of the serving bowl.

2. **Prepare the Broth Base**
   Add an amount of oil typical for stir-frying to the wok. When the oil is just warm, reduce heat to the lowest setting. Add the finely minced doubanjiang. Stir-fry slowly over low heat until the oil turns a bright red and a rich aroma releases. Add scallion flowers, minced ginger, and sliced garlic; stir-fry until fragrant. Add two large spoonfuls of the prepared chili oil paste. Stir-fry briefly. Pour in about two and a half bowls of water. Turn heat to high and bring to a boil.
   **Key Point**: Doubanjiang must be stir-fried thoroughly over low heat to eliminate the raw bean smell.

3. **Cook the Pork Slices**
   Once the broth is boiling, season with white pepper, sugar, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken bouillon powder. Reduce heat to low. Gently add the marinated pork slices one by one or in small batches. After all slices are added, let them sit undisturbed for 15 seconds. Then, using the back of a spoon, gently push them apart. Increase heat to high and cook for about 50 seconds. As soon as the pork changes color, turn off the heat immediately. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the pork slices onto the vegetable base.
   **Key Point**: Adding pork over low heat and letting it sit prevents the coating from coming off; short cooking time keeps the meat tender.

4. **Pour the Broth**
   Check the consistency of the broth. If not ideal, mix about 5g cornstarch with two tablespoons of water to make a slurry. Add the slurry to the broth in small amounts while stirring, until it reaches a slightly thickened, light gravy consistency. Turn off the heat. Pour the broth evenly over the pork slices.
   **Note**: The gravy should not be too thick; it should just lightly coat the meat.

5. **Splash with Hot Oil for Aroma**
   Pile the minced garlic on top of the pork slices. In a separate small pot, heat oil until it smokes. Immediately pour the hot oil over the garlic and chili flakes to release the aroma. Garnish with scallion flowers or cilantro, if desired.

## Health Advice
Sliced Pork in Chili Oil is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, but it is a high-fat, high-sodium dish. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or mixed-grain rice to balance sodium intake. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure. Those with sensitive digestive systems should consume with caution.
