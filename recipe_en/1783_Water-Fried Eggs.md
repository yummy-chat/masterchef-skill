# Water-Fried Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 2 eggs
💧 About 2 tablespoons of water
🧂 A pinch of salt (sprinkle after cooking)
🌿 A pinch of mixed dried herbs or pepper (sprinkle after cooking)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Wooden spatula
🔍 Glass lid

## Steps
1. Place the non-stick frying pan on the stove and heat it thoroughly. Once the pan is fully preheated, the egg whites will set quickly when the eggs are added.
2. Crack 2 eggs into the pan. Observe the egg whites turning white and setting immediately at the edges.
3. Slowly pour about 2 tablespoons of water along the edge of the pan.
4. Immediately cover with a glass lid.
   - **Heat control**: If using a thick-bottomed pan, you can turn off the heat immediately and let the residual heat steam the eggs for 2-3 minutes. If using a thin-bottomed pan, maintain low heat for 1-2 minutes until enough steam is generated inside, then turn off the heat and let it steam.
   - **Key point**: Use the steam and residual heat of the pan to fully set the egg whites while keeping the yolks runny.
5. After steaming, remove the lid. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and mixed dried herbs or pepper according to personal taste.

## Health Tips
Water-fried eggs use a small amount of water and steam for cooking, reducing fat intake by about 90% compared to oil-fried eggs. This method is suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing to control blood lipids. Use a non-stick pan and control the heat precisely; the final doneness depends entirely on the steaming time.
