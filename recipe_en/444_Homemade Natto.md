# Homemade Natto
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Soybeans: Appropriate amount (must use fresh soybeans from the current year)  
🧫 Natto starter powder: 1 small sachet (dissolve in 10 ml of water)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🫙 Stainless steel inner pot (sterilized and dried)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking and steaming soybeans)  
🍲 Steamer  
🌡️ Natto maker  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧻 Paper towels  
🧪 Small bowl (for dissolving natto starter powder)  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Rinse the stainless steel inner pot with boiling water, then dry thoroughly, ensuring no residual moisture remains.  
2.  Select fresh soybeans from the current year, wash them clean, and soak in water for 24 hours.  
3.  Discard the soaking water, transfer the soybeans to a glass bowl, and steam over high heat for 2 to 3 hours.  
4.  Pour the steamed soybeans along with their liquid into the sterilized inner pot, and let cool to room temperature.  
5.  Take one small sachet of natto starter powder, add 10 ml of water, and stir to dissolve, creating the starter solution.  
6.  Evenly pour the starter solution over the cooled soybeans and mix thoroughly.  
7.  Add a small amount of water to the natto maker, place the inner pot inside, cover with the lid, and ferment for 20 hours with the power on.  
8.  After fermentation, if a white film appears on the surface of the beans, stir with chopsticks; obvious sticky, stringy threads indicate successful fermentation.  

## Health Advice  
Homemade natto is rich in nattokinase and probiotics, which help promote blood circulation and intestinal health, making it suitable for daily health maintenance. However, individuals with gout or poor kidney function should monitor their legume intake carefully.
