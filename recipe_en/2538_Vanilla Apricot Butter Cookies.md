# Vanilla Apricot Butter Cookies
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍑 Apricots, to taste (pitted and halved)
🍬 Rock sugar, 30% of the weight of the apricots
🧈 Softened butter, 130g (for cookies) + 110g (for filling)
🍚 Powdered sugar, 100g
🥚 Room temperature egg yolk, 1 (for cookies) + 24g (for filling)
🌰 Almond flour, 30g
🌾 Cake flour, 225g
🍂 Crushed black tea leaves, 3g
🧂 Salt, 2g
🌿 Vanilla bean paste/extract/pods, to taste (for jam and custard)
🍬 Granulated sugar, 23g
🥛 Milk, 42g
🍋 Lemon juice, to taste
🍫 White chocolate, 30g (melted)
🧀 Cream cheese, 95g (softened)

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥣 Stand mixer
🔄 Rolling pin
📜 Baking parchment paper
⭕ Round cutter and patterned stamp
🔥 Oven
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🥢 Handheld electric mixer
🎨 Piping bag
📦 Sealable bag and sealer
🧊 Glass storage container

## Instructions
### Preparation Phase
1. **Make the vanilla apricot jam**: Place the pitted and halved apricots together with the rock sugar and some vanilla bean paste into a non-stick frying pan. Heat over the lowest heat, stirring and mashing the fruit. Cook until extremely thick, then squeeze in some lemon juice. Turn off the heat and let cool.
   **Note**: No need to marinate beforehand to release pectin; simply simmer directly until thick.

2. **Make the black tea cookie dough**: Place the softened butter, powdered sugar, and salt into the stand mixer. Use the paddle attachment to mix until no dry powder remains. Add the room temperature egg yolk and continue beating until light and fluffy. Switch to the dough hook attachment. Add the sifted cake flour, almond flour, and crushed black tea leaves. Mix until no dry powder remains. Do not overmix to avoid developing gluten. Place the dough between parchment paper and roll it out flat. Freeze for about half an hour until firm.

3. **Make the cream cheese filling**:
   - Whisk together the egg yolk and granulated sugar. Heat the milk to 60°C (140°F), pour it into the egg yolk mixture and combine. Return the mixture to the pan and heat over low heat until it reaches about 80°C (176°F). Add the vanilla bean paste and stir until thickened. Remove from heat and let cool.
   - Combine the cooled custard, softened cream cheese, melted white chocolate, and softened butter. Use an electric mixer to beat until light and fluffy.
   **Note**: Strictly control the temperature of the milk and egg mixture; if the butter is over-softened, the cream cheese filling may be too soft to hold its shape.

### Production Phase
4. **Bake the cookies**: Remove the frozen dough. Use the cutters to stamp out shapes. Bake in the oven until the edges are lightly golden. Remove and let cool completely on a wire rack.
   **Note**: Adjust the baking temperature and time according to your own oven's characteristics.

5. **Assemble**: Take one cooled cookie. Use a piping bag to pipe a ring of cream cheese filling around the edge. Fill the center with the vanilla apricot jam. Top with another cookie and press gently to seal.

6. **Chill to set and package**: Place the assembled cookies into a glass storage container. Refrigerate for 12 hours until the filling is firm. Remove from the container, place into a sealable bag, seal, and store in the freezer.
   **Tip**: The texture is better after freezing, similar to an ice cream sandwich.

## Health Advice
Vanilla apricot butter cookies are rich in Vitamin A and dietary fiber, but they also contain high amounts of butter and sugar, making them suitable as an occasional treat. It is recommended to pair them with unsweetened tea to balance the taste. Be mindful of daily intake, and enjoy in moderation as part of a balanced diet.
