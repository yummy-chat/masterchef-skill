# Braised Tofu Pudding with Beef and Roasted Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili peppers, to taste (choose tender ones, about a large handful)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies, to taste (about 5-6)  
🥩 Beef, about 300g (slice thinly against the grain)  
🫘 Tofu pudding, about 500g (used as a base)  
🌿 Green Sichuan peppercorns, a small handful  
🧄 Ginger, a small piece (finely diced)  
🧄 Garlic, a few cloves (finely diced)  
🧅 Scallions, a small bunch (some for ginger-scallion water, some chopped)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🥫 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste  
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste  
🧈 Lard, about 1 tablespoon (used in combination with vegetable oil)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, to taste  
🍶 Ginger-scallion water, about 2-3 tablespoons  
🥄 Homemade roasted chili sauce, about 2-3 tablespoons  
🥢 Sichuan pepper oil, a few drops  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🧺 Bamboo sieve  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🍚 Large serving bowl  

## Instructions
1.  Pick fresh Erjingtiao chili peppers, rinse them clean, and drain well.  
**Note**: Choose tender peppers; after washing, ensure the surface moisture is drained.

2.  Place the rinsed Erjingtiao chilies and bird's eye chilies into a hot, dry iron wok. Constantly press and stir-fry with a spatula until the peppers soften and develop charred black "tiger skin" spots.  
**Note**: Do not add oil during this process; press to ensure even heating and fully release the charred aroma.

3.  Transfer the roasted peppers to a cutting board to cool completely. Then add a handful of fresh green Sichuan peppercorns and chop together into a fine mixture to make the "roasted chili."  
**Note**: Must cool completely before chopping to avoid burning your hands; some texture can be retained.

4.  Slice the beef thinly against the grain.  
**Note**: Slicing against the grain is key to ensuring tender beef.

5.  Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce to the beef slices. Gradually add the ginger-scallion water in batches, stirring in one direction until absorbed. Finally, add cornstarch and mix well. Marinate for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: Add the ginger-scallion water in batches and mix thoroughly, allowing the beef to fully absorb the liquid. Add cornstarch last to seal in the moisture.

6.  Finely dice the ginger and garlic. Chop some scallions for garnish and set aside.
7.  Make the roasted chili sauce: Heat the wok, add an appropriate amount of vegetable oil and one tablespoon of lard. Once hot, add the diced ginger and garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add the chopped roasted chili mixture and stir-fry evenly. Season with salt and chicken bouillon, then add a tablespoon of oyster sauce to enhance the flavor. Stir-fry a few times and remove from the wok.  
**Note**: Stir-fry over medium heat quickly, just until fragrant, to avoid burning.

8.  Heat another clean wok, add an appropriate amount of vegetable oil and one tablespoon of lard. Sauté the diced ginger, garlic, and chopped scallions until fragrant. Add about one tablespoon of the prepared roasted chili sauce and stir-fry to release its aroma.
9.  Add enough hot water or stock to the wok, bring to a boil over high heat, and season with salt, chicken bouillon, and white pepper.  
**Note**: The amount of liquid should be enough to submerge the tofu pudding and beef.

10. Once the broth is boiling, gently slide the tofu pudding into the wok in large pieces using a spoon. Cook over medium heat for 3-5 minutes until flavorful.  
**Note**: Handle gently to avoid breaking the tofu pudding.

11. Remove the cooked tofu pudding and place it at the bottom of the large serving bowl.
12. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Gently slide the marinated beef slices into the wok one by one, lightly separating them. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fully变色 and cooked through.  
**Note**: Do not overcook the beef slices to prevent them from becoming tough.

13. Once the beef is cooked, add a few drops of Sichuan pepper oil, stir to combine, and pour the entire contents (broth and beef) over the tofu pudding in the bowl.
14. Finally, drizzle a spoonful of the prepared roasted chili sauce over the top of the dish.  
**Note**: Adding the raw roasted chili sauce at the end significantly enhances the layers of charred flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish features tender beef and tofu pudding that absorbs the flavorful broth, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle or increase protein intake. However, with the combination of Erjingtiao and bird's eye chilies, individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume it with caution.
