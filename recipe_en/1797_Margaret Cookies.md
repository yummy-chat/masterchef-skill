# Margaret Cookies
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Butter 100g (needs to be softened at room temperature until it can be indented with a light press)  
🍬 Powdered sugar 50g  
🥚 Cooked egg yolks 3 (if eggs are small) or 2 regular eggs (yolks only)  
🌽 Cornstarch 100g  
🌾 Cake flour 100g  
🧂 Salt 1g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔌 Electric mixer  
📏 Strainer/flour sifter  
🍽️ Baking sheet (lined with parchment paper)  
🍳 Electric griddle or frying pan (as oven alternative for baking)

## Steps
1. Soften the butter at room temperature in advance until it can be indented with a light finger press, avoiding melting it into a liquid.
2. Add powdered sugar and salt to the softened butter, and beat with an electric mixer until the color lightens, the volume increases, and it becomes fluffy and feathery.  
   **Note**: Beating sufficiently incorporates more air, making the final product crisper.
3. Press the cooked egg yolks through a strainer into the beaten butter, and mix until well combined.  
   **Note**: If you don’t have a strainer, you can crumble the yolks by hand and add them directly, kneading several times to ensure they are finely dispersed.
4. Sift in the cornstarch and cake flour sequentially, gently fold by hand, then knead into a dough until the color is uniform.  
   **Note**: Sifting the dry ingredients prevents lumps; the final dough should be relatively dry, able to form a ball without sticking to the hands.
5. Take about 20g of dough per piece, roll into a ball, place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and gently press the center with your thumb, allowing the edges to crack naturally.
6. **Oven method**: Place the baking sheet in the middle of an oven preheated to 170°C, and bake at 170°C with top and bottom heat for about 15 minutes.  
   **Note**: Preheat the oven for 10 minutes in advance; cookies will be soft when first out of the oven and become crispy after cooling.
7. **Electric griddle/frying pan method**: Place the cookie dough pieces into an electric griddle or frying pan, cover with a lid, and cook over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes, flipping halfway through. Let cool before eating.  
   **Note**: Adjust the heat and time flexibly based on the actual situation to avoid burning.

## Health Tips
These Margaret cookies have a high content of butter and powdered sugar, with a high energy density, suitable as an occasional snack and not for frequent consumption. The addition of cooked egg yolks increases protein and lecithin, but overall they remain a high-fat dessert. The elderly and children should control their serving size.
