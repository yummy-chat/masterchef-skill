# Hand-Pulled Beef Jerky
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef 1000g (choose parts with rough texture such as large cucumber strip, beef bottom, or beef brisket, remove all sinew)
🌿 Celery 30g (cut into sections)
🧅 Onion 50g (about 1/6, cut into thick shreds)
🥕 Carrot 30g (half a thin one, 1/3 of a thick one, sliced)
🌱 Cilantro 30g (keep roots, loosen by patting, cut into inch-long sections)
🧄 Garlic 30g (about 6 cloves, flattened)

**First Marinade (Dry Spices):**
🌸 Sichuan Peppercorns 3g (a large pinch)
🌾 Cumin Seeds 2g (a small pinch)
⭐ Star Anise 2 pieces (broken into small petals)
🍂 Bay Leaves 6 pieces (crushed)
🪶 Angelica Dahurica 2g (broken into small pieces)
🧂 Salt 3g (1 level teaspoon, do not overdo)

**Second Marinade (Liquid and Powder Seasonings):**
🍶 Light Soy Sauce 10-20g (2 spoons if using reduced-salt soy sauce, suggest 1 spoon for regular soy sauce)
🦪 Oyster Sauce 20g (1 heaping spoon)
🍬 White Sugar 10g (1 level spoon, for enhancing freshness)
🌿 Cumin Powder 10g (1 level spoon, can substitute with curry powder or chili powder)
🍂 Five-Spice Powder 1g (1 tip of a spoon)
🧂 Salt 5g (1.5 level teaspoons, total control at 5g, do not overdo)

**Before Baking:**
🫒 Cooking Oil 50g (5 spoons, coat each beef strip evenly)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen Knife
🪓 Chopping Board
🥣 Large Bowl (for mixing marinade)
🧤 Disposable Gloves (for mixing)
🔥 Oven (requires top and bottom heat function; convection fan function is optional)
📋 Baking Tray
📄 Parchment Paper (to line tray and prevent sticking; replace midway to absorb moisture)
🧤 Oven Mitts

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase

1. **Prepare the Beef**
   - Remove all sinew from the 1000g of beef. Cut along the grain into thick slices about one finger wide, then cut along the grain into strips about the thickness of a thumb. If the strips are too long, cut them in half.
   **Key Point**: Must cut along the grain, this is crucial for the jerky to be “hand-pulled.” Choosing parts with rough texture makes cutting easier and yields better texture.

2. **Prepare the Aromatic Vegetables**
   - Cut celery into sections, onion into thick shreds, carrot into slices, loosen cilantro roots by patting then cut into inch-long sections, flatten the garlic cloves.
   **Key Point**: Do not grind the vegetables and spices into powder or juice; use them directly, otherwise the flavor will be too strong.

3. **First Marinade**
   - Place the cut beef strips into a large bowl. Add all the prepared aromatic vegetables, along with Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, broken star anise, crushed bay leaves, small pieces of angelica dahurica, and 3g of salt. Mix thoroughly by hand, rubbing the vegetables into the beef, for 1-2 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Mix thoroughly to infuse the beef with flavor. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for **6-8 hours**. Do not marinate overnight, or the vegetable flavor will be too strong.

4. **Second Marinade**
   - Remove the marinated beef from the refrigerator. Pick off all the vegetable and spice residue stuck to the surface. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, cumin powder, five-spice powder, and salt to the clean beef strips. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
   **Key Point**: After mixing, let it sit for another 30 minutes to marinate. No need to cover with plastic wrap or a lid at this step.

### Cooking Phase

1. **Pre-Baking Preparation and Preheating**
   - 10 minutes before the beef marination ends, preheat the oven to 120°C (248°F) with top and bottom heat. If a convection fan function is available, it is recommended to turn it on.
   **Key Point**: Preheating in advance ensures temperature stability.

2. **Apply Oil and Arrange on Tray**
   - Add 50g of cooking oil to the marinated beef strips and mix well, ensuring each strip is coated with oil. Line a baking tray with parchment paper and arrange the beef strips one by one, leaving a small gap between them.
   **Key Point**: The beef will shrink during baking, so no need to leave large gaps when arranging.

3. **First Bake**
   - Place the baking tray into the preheated oven and bake at 120°C (248°F) for 1 hour.

4. **Replace Parchment Paper and Flip**
   - After one hour, remove the tray. There will be a lot of liquid exuded at the bottom. Replace with a new sheet of parchment paper and flip the beef strips. As they have shrunk in size, they can be combined onto one tray. Place the tray only on the upper rack of the oven.
   **Key Point**: Must replace the parchment paper, otherwise the exuded moisture will “steam” the jerky soft. After replacing the paper, continue baking only on the upper rack.

5. **Second Bake and Assessing Doneness**
   - Bake again at the same 120°C (248°F) with top and bottom heat for another hour. Remove and check the doneness: the surface should feel hard when tapped with chopsticks; the inside should still feel slightly soft when pinched. This is the final product.
   **Doneness Assessment and Key Point**: At this stage, the beef jerky is hard on the outside and chewy on the inside, with excellent texture. If a drier, crispier result is desired, continue baking in 10-minute increments. Note that the texture will become harder after cooling, so adjust according to preference.

6. **Storage**
   - Allow to cool completely before sealing for storage.
   **Key Point**: Can be refrigerated for 7 days or frozen for 1 month.

## Health Advice
Hand-pulled beef jerky is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for fitness muscle building or as a snack to curb cravings. However, it has a relatively high sodium content, so daily intake should be controlled. It is recommended to pair it with cucumber or tomato to balance the saltiness. Those with weak digestive function should not consume large amounts on an empty stomach.
