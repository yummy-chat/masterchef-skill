# Tomato and Meatball Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients  
🥩 Front pork meat, appropriate amount (30% fat, 70% lean ratio)  
🥚 Eggs, 2-3 (use egg whites, reserve yolks)  
🍅 Tomatoes, about 5  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount  

## Seasonings  
🧅 Ginger and scallion water, appropriate amount (water infused with ginger and scallions)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount  
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, appropriate amount  
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Large bowl/container  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍲 Large soup bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the front pork meat. Finely chop the fat and lean meat separately. Place the lean meat filling into a bowl on its own.  
**Key point**: Frequently turn the meat while chopping to ensure evenness.  

2. Gradually add ginger and scallion water to the lean meat filling in small amounts, mixing until fully absorbed. Add the fat meat filling and mix well. Add salt and stir vigorously until the mixture becomes sticky and forms a paste. Add the egg whites. **Stir vigorously in one direction** until it becomes a thick paste. Finally, add cornstarch and continue stirring in the same direction until well combined.  
**Key point**: The order of adding ingredients is "first water, second oil, third salt, fourth egg white, fifth cornstarch." Stir in one direction throughout the entire process.  

3. Check the meat filling's consistency: ① Invert the bowl; the filling should not fall out. ② Drop a small amount of filling into clean water; it should float. The larger the floating portion, the more successful the preparation.  

4. Score the top of each tomato with a cross, blanch in boiling water, and peel off the skin. Cut into irregular chunks. Divide into two portions and set aside.  

5. Heat lard in a wok over medium heat. Add half of the tomato chunks and stir-fry until they release juice. Add salt and sugar, and cook until the tomatoes partially break down.  

6. Add water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for 7-8 minutes to extract the tomato flavor. Season with salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, and white pepper powder.  

7. Reduce the heat to low. Shape the meat filling into balls using your hands, and use a spoon to gently slide them into the pot. Once all meatballs are added and set, add the remaining half of the tomato chunks.  
**Key point**: Keep the heat low when shaping the meatballs to prevent them from breaking apart. The meatballs are considered set once they float to the surface.  

8. Once the meatballs are fully cooked, turn off the heat. Pour the reserved egg yolks into the pot, allowing the residual heat of the soup to form egg ribbons.  

9. Transfer the soup to a large soup bowl and garnish with cilantro.  

## Health Tips  
Tomato and meatball soup is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it ideal for stimulating appetite and supplementing nutrition in summer. Properly stirring the meat filling until sticky is key to achieving a tender texture. It is recommended to use a 30% fat, 70% lean ratio to avoid greasiness.
