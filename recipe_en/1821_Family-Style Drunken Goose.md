# Family-Style Drunken Goose
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦢 1 large goose (cut into larger pieces)  
🍶 1.2 jin (600g) of 38-proof rice wine  
🥬 Appropriate amount of garlic sprouts (cut into sections)  
🌿 Appropriate amount of celery (cut into sections)  
🫚 Appropriate amount of ginger (sliced)  
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (whole cloves or slightly crushed)  
🍳 Appropriate amount of cooking oil  
🍾 Appropriate amount of soy sauce  
🍯 Appropriate amount of drunken goose sauce (recipe not shown)  
⭐ 1 star anise  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (or iron pot)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔥 Stove (gas stove)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the large goose thoroughly and chop it into larger pieces.  
**Key Point**: The goose pieces should be slightly larger to remain intact after prolonged braising.

2. Heat the wok over high heat, then add an appropriate amount of cooking oil and heat over medium-high heat.  
**Key Point**: None.

3. Add the goose pieces and stir-fry over high heat until the surface turns slightly golden and the moisture evaporates, releasing a fragrant aroma.  
**Key Point**: None.

4. Add the sliced ginger and garlic cloves, and continue stir-frying until the aroma of ginger and garlic is released.  
**Key Point**: None.

5. Pour in the soy sauce and stir-fry to color the goose meat, then add the drunken goose sauce and stir-fry thoroughly to combine.  
**Key Point**: None.

6. Add one star anise and stir-fry briefly.  
**Key Point**: None.

7. Pour in 1.2 jin (600g) of 38-proof rice wine.  
**Key Point**: Use enough rice wine to provide a sufficient alcoholic base for braising.

8. Ignite the alcohol in the wok and let the flame burn for a moment to burn off the pungent alcohol smell, leaving only a mellow wine fragrance.  
**Note**: Be extremely careful during this step, keep away from flammable materials, and avoid excessive flames.

9. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to the lowest setting, and braise for 20 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Simmer over low heat to ensure the goose meat becomes tender and fully infused with flavor.

10. After 20 minutes, remove the lid, turn up the heat to high, and stir-fry to reduce the sauce until it thickens and evenly coats the goose meat.  
**Key Point**: None.

11. When the sauce is reduced to the desired consistency, sprinkle in the garlic sprout sections and celery sections, stir-fry quickly to combine evenly, then remove from the heat and serve.  
**Key Point**: Add the garlic sprouts and celery at the end to maintain their vibrant green color and crisp texture.

## Health Tips
Goose meat is rich in high protein and low cholesterol, making it suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter. However, as some alcohol remains after braising with rice wine, it is recommended to avoid driving after consumption.
