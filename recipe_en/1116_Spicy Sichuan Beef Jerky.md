# Spicy Sichuan Beef Jerky
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐂 Beef lean meat 500g (cut into chopstick-thick strips)
🧅 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced/crushed)
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (cut into sections)
🧅 Small scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🍶 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Dark soy sauce a little (for color)
🌶️ Chili flakes appropriate amount (a mix of coarse and fine is better)
🫙 Sichuan peppercorn powder appropriate amount
🫙 Five-spice powder or thirteen-spice powder appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount (for caramelizing)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon a little
🍲 Broth or water appropriate amount
🫒 Sesame oil a little
🫘 Toasted white sesame seeds appropriate amount

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🕳️ Skimmer or strainer
🍽️ Serving plates or bowls

## Instructions
1. Cut the beef into strips. Place in cold water in a pot, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then remove and drain.
2. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat until hot. Add the beef strips and fry until the surface is browned and the meat is firm. Remove and drain. For a drier, crispier texture, fry a second time.
3. Leave a little oil in the wok. Add white sugar and heat over low heat, stirring constantly with a spatula. When it bubbles densely and turns an amber color, immediately add the beef strips. Stir-fry to coat each strip evenly.
**Note**: The caramelizing step is crucial. Keep the heat low, not high. When dense bubbles appear and it turns amber, add the meat immediately to prevent the syrup from turning bitter.

4. Pour broth or water into the wok. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, Sichuan peppercorn powder, chili flakes, and five-spice powder or thirteen-spice powder. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes to fully infuse the beef.
**Note**: Do not use too much liquid, otherwise it will be difficult to dry later.

5. When the liquid is almost absorbed, reduce the heat to low and stir-fry continuously. Add chicken bouillon and small scallion sections. Continue stir-frying until all moisture is completely evaporated and the beef strips are dry on the surface with a fragrant, slightly charred aroma.
**Key**: The drying process must be done over low heat with slow stir-frying. The drier it becomes, the chewier the texture and the longer it can be stored.

6. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in toasted white sesame seeds and drizzle a little sesame oil. Quickly toss and mix evenly using residual heat.
7. Remove to a serving plate. It tastes even better after cooling.

## Health Advice
This spicy Sichuan beef jerky is high in protein and low in fat, but the frying and caramelizing steps make it relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume in small amounts. Suitable for those needing to supplement iron or for fitness enthusiasts aiming to build muscle.
