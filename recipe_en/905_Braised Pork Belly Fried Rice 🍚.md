# Braised Pork Belly Fried Rice 🍚
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount (dry rice suitable for fried rice)
🥚 Eggs, appropriate amount (beaten)
🫑 Small green peppers, appropriate amount (sliced)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Green onions, appropriate amount (chopped)
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount
🧴 Vinegar, a small amount
🍺 Beer, appropriate amount (enough to submerge meat; can substitute with yellow wine or water)
🧂 Light soy sauce, one drizzle
🧂 Dark soy sauce, half a drizzle
🍬 Rock sugar, seven or eight pieces
🦪 Oyster sauce, one tablespoon
🧂 Salt, a small amount
🫒 Cooking oil (including rendered pork fat), appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle
🍽️ Lid
🥣 Bowls (for storing rendered pork fat and braised pork)

## Instructions
1.  Cut the pork belly into evenly sized small pieces; beat the eggs; slice the small green peppers; slice the ginger and garlic; chop the green onions.
   **Key Point**: The meat pieces should be uniform in size to ensure even cooking.
2.  Place the pork belly pieces in a cold wok, stir-fry over medium heat until the edges turn golden and a generous amount of pork fat has rendered out. Drain the rendered fat and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Dry-frying forces out the fat, reducing greasiness.
3.  Drizzle cooking wine and a small amount of vinegar along the edge of the wok, stir-fry over high heat until the vinegar aroma dissipates. Add the ginger slices, garlic slices, and star anise, stir-frying until fragrant.
4.  Pour in the beer, ensuring the liquid level submerges the meat. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rock sugar, and oyster sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and braise for 25 minutes.
5.  Remove the lid, add a small amount of salt to taste. Turn the heat to high and briefly reduce the sauce, leaving some liquid. Remove the braised pork and set aside.
6.  Heat a separate wok, add an appropriate amount of oil. When the oil reaches 50% heat (about 150°C / 300°F), pour in the beaten eggs. Gently stir-fry over low heat to create loose, fluffy egg threads, then remove from the wok.
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; slowly push the egg liquid to form delicate, fluffy egg strands.
7.  Leave some base oil in the wok, sauté the chopped green onions until fragrant. Add the rice, stir-fry continuously over medium-high heat for 3-5 minutes, until the grains are distinct, dry, and fluffy.
8.  Pour in the braised pork and its sauce, and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat each grain of rice with the sauce and allow it to absorb the meat juices.
9.  Add the fluffy egg threads and the sliced green peppers, stir-frying until the peppers are just cooked through, then turn off the heat.
10. Plate and serve hot.

## Health Advice
Braised Pork Belly Fried Rice is high in fat and carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the calorie intake. The Vitamin C in green peppers can help promote iron absorption. Be mindful of portion sizes; people with diabetes or high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
