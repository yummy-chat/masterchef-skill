# Dry-Fried Red-Braised Pork Belly
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Premium pork belly, appropriate amount (blanched whole, then cut into large pieces)
🌿 Celery, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Dried chili segments, appropriate amount
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (used in stages for blanching and stir-frying)
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount
🥜 Peanuts with chili, appropriate amount (key ingredient)
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🔴 Red yeast rice, appropriate amount (wrapped in cheesecloth for coloring)
🧅 Scallions, a little (for blanching)
🧄 Ginger, a little (for blanching)

🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (used three times: blanching celery, braising, and final stir-fry)
🍶 Yellow wine, one tablespoon
🥄 Soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount
🌫️ White pepper powder, two pinches
🍬 Sugar, a small pinch (used twice: during braising and final stir-fry)
🐔 Chicken bouillon powder, a little
🥔 Sweet potato starch, a thin coating (for dusting)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Soup bowl (for holding the reserved broth)
🥘 Bowl or basin (for mixing starch)
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1.  Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a little salt, blanch the celery sections for about 10 seconds, then remove and drain.
2.  In another pot, add water and place the whole pork belly in cold water. Add scallion sections and ginger slices. Bring to a boil over high heat, then skim off any foam.
3.  After skimming the foam, add Sichuan peppercorns. Continue cooking until the pork belly is just cooked through, then remove and let cool.
**Note**: Sichuan peppercorns must be added after skimming the foam to prevent them from being removed with it.

4.  Strain the pork cooking broth through a slotted spoon to remove any solids, and reserve the broth for later use.

5.  Cut the pork belly into slightly larger pieces. Prepare all ingredients and seasonings.

6.  Heat the wok over low heat, add a little oil, then add the pork belly pieces. Slowly render the fat until the surface is golden brown and oil has been released. Pour off any excess rendered lard.
7.  Drizzle soy sauce and one tablespoon of yellow wine along the edge of the wok. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly to cook off the alcohol.
8.  Pour in the reserved broth. Add the cinnamon stick, star anise, and the wrapped red yeast rice. Season with MSG, white pepper powder, a small pinch of sugar, and a small pinch of salt.
9.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and braise for 40 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the pork pieces cool completely in the broth.
10. Remove the cooled pork pieces and drain. Place them in a bowl and coat with a thin layer of sweet potato starch. Let them rest until the starch is absorbed.
11. Heat oil in the wok to 180°C (350°F). Deep-fry the pork pieces until the surface is slightly crispy and golden-red. Remove and drain on paper towels.
12. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili segments to the cold oil. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
13. Add the chopped scallions, the fried pork pieces, and the blanched celery sections. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly. Add a small pinch of salt, a small pinch of sugar, and chicken bouillon powder. Sprinkle in the toasted sesame seeds and ground Sichuan pepper. Stir-fry until everything is well combined.
14. Finally, add the peanuts with chili. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat for a few seconds, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Tips
This dish uses red yeast rice for coloring and employs a method of first braising, then deep-frying, and finally dry-frying, resulting in rich, layered textures. However, as the pork belly is fried twice and then dry-fried, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve it with a side of stir-fried vegetables and to control your portion size. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; people with high cholesterol should consume in small amounts.
