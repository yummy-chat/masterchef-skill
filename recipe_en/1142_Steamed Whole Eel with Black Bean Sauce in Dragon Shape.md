# Steamed Whole Eel with Black Bean Sauce in Dragon Shape
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

🧅 Scallions,适量 (chopped)

🫘 20g Yangjiang fermented black beans (rinsed and minced)

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍲 Stockpot (for boiling water)  
♨️ Steamer  
🥘 Concave plate/steaming plate  
🥣 Basin (for mixing sauce and marinating)  
🥄 Spoons, chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Kill the live eel by cutting off the head to bleed it. Blanch in 80°C hot water for 10 seconds, remove, and scrape off the white mucus layer on the surface with a knife. Then gut and remove the gills. Rinse thoroughly under clean water to remove all blood residue.  
**Note**: The mucus on the eel's skin is a protective secretion. It must be scraped off after blanching, otherwise it will affect the texture and cleanliness of the finished dish.  
2.  Place the cleaned eel on a cutting board. Cut it into slices about the thickness of two coins, keeping the bottom connected so the slices remain attached. This allows the eel to be coiled into a dragon shape.  
**Key point**: Keeping the slices connected ensures the fish body stays intact and can be successfully coiled into a round shape.  
3.  Mince the Yangjiang black beans, mince the ginger, chop the scallions, and dice the red and green chili peppers. Set aside.  
4.  Prepare the black bean sauce: In a basin, combine the minced black beans and ginger. Add a pinch of salt, sugar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, white pepper powder, sesame oil, Guangdong rice wine, and 1 tsp cornstarch. Mix well.  
**Note**: Use salt very sparingly, as the black beans, soy sauce, and oyster sauce are all salty. Avoid making it too salty.  
5.  Place the cut eel into the basin with the black bean sauce. Use your hands to thoroughly mix and coat each piece of fish with the sauce. Finally, drizzle in an appropriate amount of peanut oil and mix again. Marinate for a short while.  
6.  Coil the marinated eel into a round shape and place it in a concave plate to form a "dragon" shape. Spread any remaining black bean sauce from the basin over the top of the fish.  
7.  Fill a steamer with enough water and bring to a boil. Once steam is rising vigorously, place the fish plate inside. Steam over high heat for 10-15 minutes.  
**Key point**: Steaming seafood requires plenty of water, high heat, and strong steam. This ensures the fish cooks quickly and stays tender and smooth.  
8.  After steaming, remove the plate. Insert a bamboo skewer or chopstick into the thickest part of the fish. If it goes in easily and no bloody juice seeps out, the fish is cooked through.  
9.  Sprinkle the diced red and green chili peppers and chopped scallions on top. Heat a spoonful of peanut oil until smoking and pour it over the surface to release the aroma. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
Freshwater eel is rich in high-quality protein and DHA, making it suitable for people who do brain work and those needing collagen supplementation. However, its cholesterol content is relatively high; people with high blood lipids, blood pressure, or blood sugar should consume it in moderation. Black beans and soy sauce contain significant salt; control the amount used during seasoning to avoid excessive sodium intake.
