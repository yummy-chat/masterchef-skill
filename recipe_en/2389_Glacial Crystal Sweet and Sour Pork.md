# Glacial Crystal Sweet and Sour Pork
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Pork belly 1 lb (sliced thinly)  
🍍 Pineapple 1 (part diced for wrapping and stir-frying, part trimmings for making sauce)  
🧅 Scallions to taste (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (sliced)  
🍇 Fruit garnish (raspberries, blueberries, cherry tomatoes, grapes, etc.) to taste  
💧 Scallion-ginger water to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste  
🍶 Rose wine to taste  
🌾 Cornstarch about 60g (for batter and dry coating)  
🌾 Wheat starch about 40g  
🥄 Baking powder about 3g  
🥚 Egg whites about 100g  
🫒 Vegetable oil about 50g (added in three batches to the batter)  
💧 Water about 100g (for sweet and sour sauce)  
🍅 Ketchup about 60g  
🍶 White vinegar about 30g  
🍬 Sugar about 50g  
🍶 Chinkiang vinegar about 5g  
🌾 Cornstarch slurry to taste (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🌡️ Oil thermometer (or chopsticks)  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel the pineapple, cut part into small dices, and set aside the remaining trimmings for making the sauce; slice the pork belly into thin pieces.
2. Prepare the marinade: mix scallion-ginger water, salt, white pepper powder, and rose wine, then add the pork slices and marinate briefly.
3. Take one marinated pork slice, place a pineapple dice in the center, wrap the meat around it into a ball, and pinch the seams tightly closed.
4. Roll the stuffed meat balls in cornstarch to coat them evenly with a thin layer, then shake off excess.
5. Prepare the batter: mix cornstarch, wheat starch, and baking powder. Add egg whites and stir until smooth, then add vegetable oil in three batches, mixing until the batter is smooth and lump-free.
6. Heat oil in a wok to about 320–350°F. Coat each meat ball with batter, then deep-fry over medium-low heat for about 2 minutes until set. Remove.
7. Increase the oil temperature, add all meat balls back and fry for about 30 seconds until the coating is golden, crispy, and puffy. Remove and drain.
8. In a separate pan, add water, ketchup, white vinegar, sugar, Chinkiang vinegar, and pineapple trimmings. Simmer over low heat to extract flavor. Remove the pineapple trimmings and strain the sauce. Set aside.
9. Leave a little oil in the pan, sauté scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic slices. Pour in the strained sweet and sour sauce and pineapple dices. Bring to a boil over high heat.
10. Add cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce until it coats a spoon.
11. Quickly add the fried meat balls, toss rapidly to coat each piece evenly with sauce. Remove from heat immediately.
12. Spread crushed ice on a plate, place the sweet and sour pork on top, and garnish with fruits.

## Health Advice  
Pork belly paired with pineapple is tangy and appetizing but high in calories. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood pressure, high blood fat, or high blood sugar should eat sparingly. The double-frying process is key to a crispy coating; blot with paper towels after frying to reduce oil.
