# Lean Meatballs  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 1000g pork hind leg (remove tendons and fat)  
🌱 10g scallions (for scallion-ginger water)  
🫚 10g ginger (for scallion-ginger water)  
🍠 300g sweet potato starch  
🌿 Dried seaweed, as needed  
🦐 Dried shrimp, as needed  
🧂 20g salt (for meat paste; additional for broth base)  
🍬 10g white sugar (for meat paste; additional for broth base)  
🌶️ 3g white pepper (for meat paste; additional for broth base)  
🧪 10g baking soda  
🍶 Vinegar, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🌀 Blender  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🥢 4 chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥄 Strainer  
📦 Airtight container or bag  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Select and Prepare the Meat:** Trim the pork hind leg by removing all tendons and fat, keeping only the lean meat. Hind leg meat is more exercised, resulting in a firmer texture.  
2. **Blend the Meat Paste:** Cut the prepared meat into small pieces and place them in the blender in batches. Alternate between high and low speeds, blending for about 10–15 seconds per batch. Avoid adding too much at once. **Note:** Prevent the machine from running too long consecutively to avoid overheating, which can toughen the meat. If the paste isn’t fine enough, blend once more.  
3. **Prepare Scallion-Ginger Water:** Soak 10g scallions and 10g ginger in 300ml water. Squeeze vigorously to release juices, then let sit for 10 minutes. Strain out the solids before use, reserving only the liquid.  
4. **Season:** Transfer the blended meat paste to a large bowl. Add 20g salt, 10g white sugar, 3g white pepper, and 10g baking soda in order.  
5. **Knead Until Sticky:** Using 4 chopsticks, stir the meat paste continuously in one direction for about 25 minutes, until resistance increases noticeably, the paste turns slightly white, and becomes extremely sticky. This step is key to achieving a bouncy texture; do not change the stirring direction.  
6. **Add Scallion-Ginger Water in Batches:** Gradually pour the scallion-ginger water into the meat paste in small amounts, stirring in the same direction after each addition until fully absorbed before adding more.  
7. **Add Sweet Potato Starch in Batches:** Similarly, add 300g sweet potato starch in several portions. Stir in the same direction after each addition until no dry powder remains, continuing until all starch and scallion-ginger water are incorporated.  
8. **Cook the Meatballs:** Bring water to a boil in a pot. Dip a spoon in hot water to prevent sticking, then scoop the meat paste into the boiling water one spoonful at a time. Gently stir to prevent clumping. The meatballs are cooked when they all float to the surface. Test one by pinching it; if it feels springy, it’s done.  
9. **Prepare the Broth and Serve:** Place dried seaweed, dried shrimp, a pinch of white pepper, and salt, sugar, and vinegar to taste in a serving bowl. Ladle in a few spoonfuls of hot meatball cooking water to dissolve the seasonings. Add the cooked lean meatballs, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This lean meatball dish is high in protein and low in fat. However, the sweet potato starch and baking soda add hidden carbohydrates and sodium, so portion control is recommended. Suitable for those in muscle-building phases or who enjoy a chewy texture. Caution is advised for those with weak digestion.
