# Provençal Ratatouille
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients
🫑 Red bell pepper 1
🫑 Yellow bell pepper 1
🥒 Green zucchini appropriate amount
🥒 Yellow zucchini appropriate amount
🍆 Eggplant appropriate amount
🍅 Tomato appropriate amount
🧅 Onion 1/4
🧄 Garlic 1 clove
🌿 Thyme appropriate amount
🌱 Basil leaves appropriate amount

## Seasonings
🫒 Olive oil appropriate amount
🧂 Salt (sea salt) appropriate amount
🧂 Black pepper appropriate amount

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Wok
🌀 Blender
🍲 Oven-safe shallow pot or baking dish
♨️ Oven
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥒 Vegetable slicer (or knife)
📦 Aluminum foil
📄 Parchment paper

## Instructions
1. **Prepare bell peppers**: Hold red and yellow bell peppers over an open flame on a gas stove using chopsticks, turning to char the skin until completely blackened. Quickly wrap in aluminum foil and let steam until cool, then peel off the charred skin. Cut into pieces and set aside.
   **Key point**: Wrapping in foil for steaming makes peeling easier.
2. **Prepare tomatoes**: Score a cross on the top of each tomato, blanch in boiling water briefly, then quickly peel off the skin. Cut into pieces and set aside.
3. **Slice vegetables for arrangement**: Slice green zucchini, yellow zucchini, eggplant, and some tomatoes into round slices of even thickness, keeping them separate.
   **Key point**: Consistent slice thickness ensures even doneness during baking.
4. **Sauté the base**: Drizzle olive oil into a wok, add diced onion, sliced garlic, and thyme, and sauté over low heat until fragrant.
5. **Simmer the sauce**: Add tomato pieces and bell pepper pieces, stir well, add a small amount of water, cover, and simmer over medium-low heat until vegetables are soft.
6. **Blend and season**: Remove the lid, season with salt and black pepper, turn off the heat, and add a handful of fresh basil leaves. Pour all contents into a blender and blend into a smooth, fine orange sauce.
7. **Assemble the baking dish**: Spread an even layer of sauce on the bottom of the baking dish. Stack the sliced vegetables in small piles, alternating colors, then arrange them tightly in a spiral pattern on top of the sauce until fully covered.
8. **Pre-bake preparation**: Sprinkle a little sea salt over the vegetables and spray with a thin layer of olive oil. Cut a piece of parchment paper into a circle matching the size of the dish opening and place it directly over the vegetables.
9. **First bake**: Preheat the oven to 150°C (300°F), place the dish on the middle rack, and bake for 30 minutes.
   **Note**: Covering with parchment paper prevents the vegetable surface from drying out too quickly and keeps them moist.
10. **Second bake for browning**: Remove the dish, carefully peel off the parchment paper. Increase the oven temperature to 200°C (400°F), return the dish, and bake for about 18 minutes until the vegetables are lightly browned on the surface and edges slightly curled.
11. **Finish and garnish**: Remove from the oven, sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper, and garnish with a few fresh basil leaves before serving.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and lycopene, with almost zero cholesterol, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet in moderate amounts. Be careful to control the amount of olive oil to prevent the eggplant from absorbing too much oil.
