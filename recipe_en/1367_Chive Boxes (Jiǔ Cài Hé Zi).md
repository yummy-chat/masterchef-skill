# Chive Boxes (Jiǔ Cài Hé Zi)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧅 500g chives (chopped)
🦐 100g shrimp
🥚 3 eggs (beaten)
🌾 250g flour
🫙 Appropriate amount of sesame oil
🧂 5g salt
🍳 Appropriate amount of cooking oil (for pan-frying)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🔘 Rolling pin
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan

## Instructions
1. Place 250g of flour into a large bowl. Gradually add an appropriate amount of water, knead into a smooth dough, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 30 minutes.
2. Wash and drain the chives thoroughly, then chop them finely; beat the eggs and set aside. Mix the chopped chives, beaten eggs, and 5g of salt together evenly to form the filling.
3. Remove the rested dough, roll it into a long log, and divide it into evenly sized pieces. Roll each piece into a thin, round wrapper about 15 cm in diameter.
4. Take one wrapper, place an appropriate amount of chive filling in the center, add a few shrimp on top, fold it in half, and pinch the edges tightly to seal with no gaps.
5. Heat a flat-bottomed pan over medium heat and pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil. When the oil reaches about 50% hot (moderate temperature), place the assembled chive boxes in the pan. Pan-fry over medium-low heat until one side is golden brown, then flip. Fry each side for about 3–4 minutes, until both sides are golden and crispy.
6. Remove from the pan and transfer to a plate. Let cool slightly before serving.

## Health Advice
Chives are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, while eggs and shrimp provide high-quality protein, making this dish suitable for those needing eye care and immune system support. Note that chives are slightly warming in nature, so individuals with yin deficiency and internal heat should consume them in moderation. Control the amount of oil used during pan-frying to reduce fat intake.
