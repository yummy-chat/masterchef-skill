# Pan-Fried Dumplings
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 Raw dumplings 8-10 (any filling, such as meat or vegetable, no specific type required)
💧 Starch water 1 cup (mix starch with water to form a milky liquid; cornstarch or potato starch can be used; the amount should be enough to submerge about one-third of the dumpling bottoms)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (about 1-2 tablespoons)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick flat-bottomed pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Long chopsticks
🍲 Glass lid
🥣 Measuring cup/bowl (for mixing and storing starch water)

## Steps
1. Arrange the raw dumplings neatly in a cold or slightly warm non-stick flat-bottomed pan, forming a circle, leaving a little space between each dumpling.
2. Pour the prepared starch water evenly into the pan, with enough water to submerge the dumpling bottoms.
   **Note**: Starch water is key to forming the crispy "lace" bottom.
3. Drizzle an appropriate amount of cooking oil along the edge of the pan; the oil helps raise the temperature inside the pan, making the bottom crispier and preventing sticking.
4. Immediately cover with the lid, and cook over medium or medium-low heat.
   **Judgment**: Observe through the glass lid; when the starch water has mostly evaporated and the top skin of the dumplings becomes translucent, it indicates the top is steamed through.
5. Remove the lid, continue frying over medium-low heat until all moisture on the bottom has evaporated and you hear a sizzling sound from the oil, with the bottom forming a golden, crispy crust.
   **Key**: Gently shake the pan; if the dumplings can move as a whole, it means the bottom is set and not stuck to the pan.
6. Turn off the heat, use chopsticks to help slide the entire connected batch of pan-fried dumplings out or flip them onto a plate. The finished product should have a complete net-like crispy bottom, with soft and tender dumpling tops.

## Health Advice
The crispy lace bottom of pan-fried dumplings is formed by starch water, which increases carbohydrate intake. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition, making it suitable for those with higher energy expenditure. Be mindful of the amount of oil used to avoid excess calorie accumulation.
