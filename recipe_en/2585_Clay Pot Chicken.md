# Clay Pot Chicken  
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 1 Qingyuan chicken (about 900g, with internal organs, neck lymph nodes removed, claws and tail trimmed)  
🌱 Scallions, to taste  
🧅 Ginger, to taste  
🧂 1 small packet of "Qian Ji" salt-baked chicken seasoning powder  
🧂 1 small packet of "Qian Ji" gardenia yellow seasoning powder (for coloring)  
🫒 About 30g vegetable oil (preferably rapeseed oil)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🍴 Stainless steel scissors  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔥 Oven  
🍳 Aluminum foil (recommended to have enough for multiple layers)  
📜 Baking parchment paper  
🧤 Oven mitts  
🍽 Large plate  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥄 Small bowl/spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the prepared whole chicken in a container and evenly pour boiling water over the skin to quickly tighten it.  
**Note**: Scalding the skin helps the marinade penetrate better.  

2. Mix the salt-baked chicken powder, gardenia yellow powder, and vegetable oil into a smooth paste.  
3. Evenly coat the chicken inside and out with the marinade, ensuring the cavity and skin are fully covered. Refrigerate for 8–12 hours to marinate overnight.  
**Note**: Insufficient marinating time will result in weaker flavor.  

4. Remove the marinated chicken, stuff the cavity with scallions and ginger slices, pour in any remaining marinade, and tuck the claws into the cavity to shape the chicken compactly.  
5. Lay two sheets of aluminum foil in a cross shape, place the chicken in the center, and wrap tightly in multiple layers, sealing the seams at the bottom to prevent juice leakage. Wrap with an additional layer of foil for reinforcement.  
**Note**: An extra layer of foil helps prevent liquid from leaking during baking.  

6. Line a baking tray with parchment paper, place the wrapped chicken on it, and set it on the middle-lower rack of the oven. Bake at 200°C (actual temperature) for 2 hours without turning.  
7. After baking, carefully cut open the foil with scissors. The skin should be golden and glossy, and the meat should easily separate from the bones. Serve by hand-shredding or chopping into pieces, and pair with dipping sauce for extra flavor.  
**Key**: The entire process involves low-temperature steaming, resulting in tender, juicy chicken with all juices sealed in the foil.  

## Health Advice  
Clay pot chicken is cooked slowly at a low temperature, preserving the chicken’s moisture and protein, making it suitable for muscle gain or weight control. However, the salt-baked powder is high in sodium, so pair it with light vegetables to balance intake.
