# Pumpkin Red Bean Paste Buns
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 Old pumpkin, as needed (peeled, seeded, and sliced thinly)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, as needed (add gradually depending on moisture of pumpkin puree)
🫘 Red bean paste filling, as needed
🔴 Goji berries, as needed (1 berry to top each bun)
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Steamer
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪥 Toothpick

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel and seed the pumpkin, cut into uniform thin slices, place in the steamer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes, until easily pierced with chopsticks.
2. Remove the pumpkin slices, drain excess water with a slotted spoon, transfer to a stainless steel bowl, and mash into a fine puree.
3. Add 1 tablespoon of white sugar, then gradually add glutinous rice flour, kneading into a smooth, non-sticky dough. Cover and let rest for 5 minutes.
4. Roll the dough into a long log, then cut into evenly sized pieces.
5. Roll each piece into a ball, shape into a small bowl, wrap in an appropriate amount of red bean paste filling, fold and pinch the opening tightly, then roll back into a round ball.
   **Key point**: Ensure the seal is tight to prevent filling from leaking during steaming.
6. Use a toothpick to gently press pumpkin-like patterns on the surface, with moderate pressure. Press 1 goji berry on top as decoration.
7. Line the steamer with a cloth, place the raw buns inside. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes, until visibly expanded and golden on the surface.
   **Note**: The pumpkin must be thoroughly drained after steaming, otherwise too much glutinous rice flour will be needed, affecting texture. Add the glutinous rice flour gradually to avoid making the dough too firm.

## Health Advice
Pumpkin paired with glutinous rice flour provides dietary fiber and complex carbohydrates, suitable as a slow-carb option for afternoon tea or breakfast. However, glutinous rice flour has a higher glycemic index; diabetic patients should consume in moderation. Store-bought red bean paste filling tends to be high in sugar; a homemade reduced-sugar version can be used to balance overall sweetness.
