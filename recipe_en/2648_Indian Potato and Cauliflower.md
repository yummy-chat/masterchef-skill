# Indian Potato and Cauliflower
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🧂 **Seasonings**
- 🧂 Salt 5g
- 🌿 Cumin seeds 3g
- 🟡 Turmeric powder 3g
- 🌶️ Red chili powder 3g
- 🌱 Coriander powder 5g
- 🍛 Garam Masala 3g

🥬 **Ingredients**
- 🥔 Potatoes 2 (about 250g), peeled and cut into 2cm pieces
- 🥦 Cauliflower 1 (about 300g), broken into florets
- 🧅 Onion 1 (about 100g), chopped
- 🍅 Tomato 1 (about 100g), chopped
- 🫚 Ginger 8g, grated
- 🧄 Garlic 3 cloves (about 9g), minced
- 🌶️ Green chili 1, chopped
- 🌿 Coriander leaves 15g (for garnish)
- 🛢️ Cooking oil 30ml

🍳 **Cookware**
- Wok (with lid)

📝 **Instructions**
1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into 2cm cubes.
2. Break the cauliflower into even florets, cutting larger ones smaller if needed.
3. Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat until it reaches about 50% hot.
4. Add cumin seeds and fry until they darken and release aroma (about 10 seconds, be careful not to burn).
5. Add the chopped onion and stir-fry until translucent and slightly golden (about 3–4 minutes).
6. Add the grated ginger, minced garlic, and chopped green chili, and continue stir-frying for 1 minute.
7. Add the chopped tomato and stir-fry until soft and pulpy (about 2–3 minutes).
8. Sprinkle in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt, and stir-fry for 1 minute to blend the spices with the vegetables.
9. Add the potato pieces and stir-fry for 2 minutes until they are evenly coated with the spice mixture.
10. Add the cauliflower florets and gently toss to combine.
11. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and let it cook for 12–15 minutes, uncovering and stirring every 3–4 minutes to prevent sticking.
12. When the potatoes can be easily pierced with a chopstick and the cauliflower edges are slightly charred, sprinkle in the Garam Masala and stir-fry for 1 minute.
13. Turn off the heat, garnish with coriander leaves, and serve.

## Health Advice
Potatoes and cauliflower form a low-carb combination, while turmeric and cumin have anti-inflammatory properties, making this dish especially suitable for blood sugar control and boosting immunity. Using less oil and slow simmering helps retain nutrients; those with high stomach acid should reduce the amount of green chili.
