# Lamb Pilaf
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount (soak for 30 minutes in advance)
🥩 Lamb ribs, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🥕 Carrots, appropriate amount (cut into wide strips, better with two colors)
🧅 White onion, appropriate amount (sliced, also known as onion)
🍇 Raisins, appropriate amount (added later)
🌿 Cumin powder, appropriate amount
🌶 Sichuan peppercorn powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount

## Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan or wok
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Containers (for soaking rice and prepping ingredients)
♨️ Rice cooker (optional, as an alternative for simmering)

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour sufficient oil into the pan and heat until very hot. Add the lamb ribs and stir-fry over high heat until the surface is browned and lamb fat renders.
2. Add the sliced onions and carrot strips, and continue stir-frying to allow the carrot’s nutrients to fully combine with the oil.
3. Sprinkle in cumin powder and Sichuan peppercorn powder, season with salt, and stir-fry until the onion slices become slightly translucent.
4. Pour in boiling water, just enough to cover the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover the pan, and simmer for 10–20 minutes.
5. Taste the broth for saltiness; it should be slightly saltier than soup you would normally drink. If insufficient, add salt.
6. Drain the soaked rice and spread it evenly over the ingredients in the pan, ensuring the liquid level is roughly even with the rice.
7. Cover the pan and simmer over medium heat until there is no visible moisture on the surface of the rice.
8. Use the blunt end of chopsticks to poke a few small holes into the rice, then sprinkle the raisins evenly on top.
9. Cover the pan again, reduce the heat to the lowest setting, and continue simmering for another 10 minutes.
10. Remove the lid, thoroughly mix the rice, vegetables, and lamb, and serve.

## Health Tips
Lamb pilaf is rich in high-quality protein and beta-carotene, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing energy replenishment. However, it is high in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with a light vegetable salad to balance nutrition. People with sensitive stomachs should control their portion size to avoid discomfort from the richness of the lamb.
