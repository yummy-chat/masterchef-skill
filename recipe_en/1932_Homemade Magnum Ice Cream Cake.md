# Homemade Magnum Ice Cream Cake  
Cuisine: Others  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍰 Store-bought cake trimmings – as needed (for layers, slice thinly)  
🍦 Yogurt-flavored ice cream – 1 large box  
🍦 Strawberry-flavored ice cream – 1 small box  
🍦 Green plum green tea-flavored ice cream – 1 box  
🍦 Chocolate-flavored ice cream – 1 small cup (for top layer)  
🥭 Mango – as needed (cut into small pieces)  
🍌 Banana – about half (sliced)  
🫐 Blueberries – a few (for decoration)  
🍫 Dark chocolate – about 200g (for glaze)  
🧈 Cocoa butter (or coconut oil) – about 70g  
🥜 Chopped hazelnuts – as needed  
🥜 Chopped pistachios – small amount  
🍬 Chocolate balls (e.g., Ferrero Rocher) – 2 pieces  
🧂 No additional seasonings  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl (as mold and ice bath container)  
🥢 Chopsticks – 3 pieces (to build a glaze drip rack)  
🔪 Knife  
🥄 Spatula / spoon  
🍶 Heat-resistant glass jar  
📡 Microwave  
🪣 Basin (to catch dripping glaze)  
🥄 Strainer  
✂️ Scissors  
🧻 Plastic wrap  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare a cold working environment**  
   - Fill a large bowl with crushed ice so the bowl is surrounded by ice.  
   - Take another clean bowl, line the inside with plastic wrap, and use three chopsticks to form a stable triangular stand over the bowl opening.  
2. **Prepare ingredients**  
   - Slice the store-bought cake trimmings horizontally into thin pieces that fit into the bowl.  
   - Cut mango into small pieces and slice banana.  
3. **Assemble the ice cream cake**  
   - Set the plastic-wrap-lined bowl into the crushed ice.  
   - Layer in order: first layer of yogurt-flavored ice cream, spread evenly and coat the sides of the bowl; press a cake slice gently on top; second layer of strawberry-flavored ice cream, top with mango pieces; third layer of green plum green tea-flavored ice cream, top with banana slices; finally, spread the chocolate-flavored ice cream over the top and smooth it out.  
   - Keep the temperature low during the process to prevent the ice cream from melting too much.  
4. **Freeze to set**  
   - Place the assembled cake (still in the bowl) in the freezer and freeze until completely firm.  
5. **Make the chocolate glaze**  
   - Put dark chocolate and cocoa butter into a heat-resistant glass jar. Heat in the microwave on high for 1–2 minutes, remove and stir, repeat until completely melted and smooth.  
   - Strain the chocolate liquid through a strainer, then let it cool to about 39°C.  
   - Add chopped hazelnuts and stir well.  
   - Note: Temperature is key – too hot will melt the ice cream, too cold will reduce fluidity.  
6. **Unmold**  
   - Take out the frozen cake, peel off the plastic wrap, and carefully remove the outer bowl.  
   - Place the cake on the chopstick stand with a basin underneath to catch dripping glaze.  
7. **Glaze and decorate**  
   - Pour the warm chocolate glaze steadily from the top center of the cake, allowing it to naturally cover all sides and drip down.  
   - Once the glaze has slightly set, use a homemade serrated piping bag to pipe chocolate cream, arrange chocolate balls and blueberries on top, and sprinkle with chopped pistachios.  
   - The glazing motion should be decisive and continuous for a natural drip effect. Serve immediately after glazing or freeze briefly to set the shell.  
8. **Slice and serve**  
   - Cut with a knife – the inside will show distinct layers, while the outer chocolate shell is crisp and the inner ice cream is creamy and cold, with cake and fruit in between.  

## Health Advice  
This homemade ice cream cake is high in calories. It is recommended as an occasional summer treat, enjoyed in portions to avoid excessive intake of sugar and fat. Best shared with friends; those with weak digestion should consume with caution.
