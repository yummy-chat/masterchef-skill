# Coconut Pandan Layered Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌿 Pandan leaves 100g (washed and chopped)  
🥥 Water chestnut flour 250g (150g for green batter, 100g for white batter)  
💧 Water as needed (300g for pandan juice, 350g for sugar water A, 200g for sugar water B)  
🥛 Coconut milk 400g  
🍬 Fine sugar 180g (100g for sugar water A, 80g for sugar water B)  
🧂 Baking soda a pinch (for color retention)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Blender/Immersion blender  
🥄 Strainer  
⚖️ Electronic scale  
🥣 Whisk  
🍮 Heat-resistant glass container  
♨️ Steamer/Steaming oven  

## Steps  
1.  Make pandan juice: Place the washed and chopped pandan leaves and 300g water into a blender and blend until fine, then strain to extract the juice. Set aside.  
   **Note**: Pandan leaves must be thoroughly washed, and the straining should be adequate to obtain a fine juice.  
2.  Prepare two types of raw batter:  
   - Green raw batter: Mix 150g water chestnut flour with 350g pandan juice, stir well, and strain. Set aside.  
   - White raw batter: Mix 100g water chestnut flour with 400g coconut milk, stir well, and strain. Set aside.  
   **Note**: After mixing the flour with liquid, it must be strained to ensure no lumps.  
3.  Cook two types of sugar water:  
   - Sugar water A: Boil 350g water with 100g fine sugar until fully dissolved.  
   - Sugar water B: Boil 200g water with 80g fine sugar until fully dissolved.  
4.  Make green cooked batter: Remove boiling sugar water A from heat, immediately pour in 90g green raw batter, stir quickly until it becomes a translucent cooked batter, then pour it back into the remaining green raw batter, stir well, and strain.  
   **Note**: The sugar water must be boiling before turning off the heat and pouring into the raw batter; after mixing, it must be strained again to ensure a smooth texture.  
5.  Anti-discoloration treatment: Add a pinch of baking soda to the green batter and stir evenly.  
   **Purpose**: To prevent the pandan from turning yellow during high-temperature steaming.  
6.  Layered steaming:  
   - Pour a layer of green batter into the bottom of a heat-resistant glass container, place it into a pre-steamed steamer/steaming oven, and steam over high heat until fully set.  
   - Remove the container, pour a layer of white batter to cover evenly, and steam again over high heat until set.  
   - Repeat alternating green and white batters until all batter is used up.  
   **Key point**: The next layer can only be poured after the previous layer is completely set; adjust steaming time based on layer thickness until set.  
7.  Cool and unmold: Allow the layered cake to cool completely, run a knife along the inner wall of the container, and invert to unmold.  
8.  Slice: Cut the cake into diamond-shaped pieces or thin slices; thin slices yield a better texture.  
   **Note**: Only after cooling completely will the cake firm up, making it easy to cut clean layers.  

## Health Advice  
This coconut pandan layered cake is rich in dietary fiber and medium-chain fatty acids from coconut milk, making it suitable as a low-sugar dessert alternative. However, its sugar content is relatively high; those controlling sugar intake may reduce sugar as needed. Pandan leaves are rich in natural chlorophyll; combined with baking soda for color protection, the green layers can maintain their color longer.
