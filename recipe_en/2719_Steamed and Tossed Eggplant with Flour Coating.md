# Steamed and Tossed Eggplant with Flour Coating
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Old eggplant, as needed (peeled and cut into thin strips)
🌶️ Green and red peppers, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🧅 Chopped scallions, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed
🥜 Roasted crushed peanuts, as needed
🫘 Roasted sesame seeds, as needed
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (added in small batches)
🧂 Salt, 3 grams (for marinating; additional to taste)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🍛 Thirteen-spice powder, a pinch
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed
🫗 Hot oil, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Steamer
🧻 Steamer basket (lined with parchment paper)
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1.  Peel the old eggplant and cut into thin strips. Place in a bowl, add 3 grams of salt, and mix well. Marinate for 5 minutes, then squeeze out some moisture.
   **Note**: Retain some moisture when squeezing to prevent the eggplant from becoming dry; marinating removes astringency and prevents oxidation and darkening.
2.  Add flour gradually in small batches, gently mixing until each strip is evenly coated with a thin layer of flour, with no dry lumps.
   **Note**: Do not knead vigorously, to avoid the flour clumping.
3.  Line the steamer basket with parchment paper. Spread the flour-coated eggplant strips evenly in a single layer. Steam over high heat until steam rises, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 8 minutes.
   **Key**: Spread evenly to ensure even cooking.
4.  Remove from steamer and let cool slightly. Loosen the strips and place in a bowl. Add green and red pepper strips, chopped scallions, minced garlic, and chili flakes. Pour hot oil over the ingredients.
   **Note**: The hot oil must be poured directly onto the toppings to fully release their aroma.
5.  Add roasted crushed peanuts, roasted sesame seeds, and adjust with additional salt, sugar, and thirteen-spice powder. Mix well and transfer to a serving plate.
   **Tip**: Adjust seasoning quantities to personal taste, ensuring the eggplant is evenly flavored.

## Health Advice
This steamed and tossed eggplant dish uses a flour coating and steaming instead of frying, significantly reducing calories while providing rich dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss or oil-controlled diets. Marinating and squeezing the eggplant before steaming removes astringency and maintains a soft, sticky texture. When adding green and red peppers and using hot oil to release aroma, control the oil amount to avoid adding extra fats.
