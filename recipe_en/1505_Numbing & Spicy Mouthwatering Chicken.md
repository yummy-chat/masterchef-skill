# Numbing & Spicy Mouthwatering Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 whole chicken (head and wing tips removed, cut in half)
🧅 A few scallions
🫚 1 piece of ginger
⭐ Star anise, to taste
🌿 Sand ginger (kaempferia galanga), to taste
🌸 Chinese nutmeg (Amomum), to taste
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste
🌶️ Dried Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (for boiling the chicken)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (for dry-roasting and grinding)
🥜 Oil-roasted peanuts, to taste
🥬 Pea shoots, to taste
🧄 1 clove garlic (minced)
🤍 White sesame seeds, to taste
🍲 Reserved chicken poaching broth, to taste (cooled)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, to taste
🧂 Ground white pepper, to taste
🍶 Aged black vinegar, to taste
🥃 Soy sauce, to taste
🌶️ Red chili oil, about 1 large bowl
🥜 Sesame paste, 1 scoop
🌿 Sichuan green pepper oil (tengjiao you), to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍲 Stainless steel stockpot
🍳 Wok or iron pan
🥄 Stainless steel ladle
🥣 White porcelain bowls
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowls
🍲 Large serving bowl
🥢 Spatula
🥢 Rolling pin

## Cooking Steps
1.  Rinse the whole chicken. Remove the head and wing tips, then chop the body in half.
2.  Place the chicken pieces in a pot with cold water. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and a spice bundle. Cover and boil for about 30 minutes.
**Key point**: The chicken must be added to cold water to better expel blood and keep the meat tender.
3.  In another dry pan, dry-roast the Sichuan peppercorns over low heat until they darken slightly and release their fragrance. Transfer to a cutting board and crush them into coarse pieces using a rolling pin or the side of a knife.
**Note**: Do not add oil when dry-roasting the Sichuan peppercorns. Use low heat to avoid burning. Crushing them releases a stronger numbing aroma.
4.  Remove the cooked chicken and let it cool completely to room temperature. Cut into even strips and place in a large bowl.
5.  Prepare the sauce: In a stainless steel bowl, combine the cooled chicken poaching broth with salt, white sugar, MSG, chicken bouillon powder, ground white pepper, crushed Sichuan peppercorns, minced garlic, aged black vinegar, soy sauce, red chili oil, sesame paste, Sichuan green pepper oil, and white sesame seeds. Stir well with a spoon to combine.
6.  Sprinkle the oil-roasted peanuts over the chicken. Pour the prepared sauce evenly over the chicken. Garnish with fresh pea shoots.
**Key point**: Serve immediately after garnishing to keep the pea shoots fresh, green, and crisp.

## Health Advice
This mouthwatering chicken dish is high in protein and low in fat. Eating the chicken cold allows for better nutrient absorption, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during a weight loss phase. However, the red chili oil and sesame paste are high in calories, so it is recommended to add more vegetables, consume less of the sauce, and control portion sizes.
