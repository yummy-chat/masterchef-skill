# Original Water Chestnut Cake
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Fresh water chestnuts, about 150g after peeling (provides a crispy texture)  
🌾 Water chestnut flour, 250g  
💧 Water, 600ml (for mixing batter) + 700ml (for cooking syrup)  
🍯 Brown slab sugar, 250g (sweetness base)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Peeler, chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowls (for mixing batter and holding chopped water chestnuts)  
🍲 Pot (for cooking syrup)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and scooping)  
🗑️ Strainer (for filtering batter)  
♨️ Steamer  
🧊 Glass container (steaming mold)  
🔗 Tongs (for removing high-temperature containers)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the fresh water chestnuts to obtain about 150g of flesh, then cut them into fine pieces and set aside.
2. Place 250g of water chestnut flour and 600ml of water in a bowl. Stir with chopsticks until no dry flour particles remain to form a batter.  
   **Key Point**: Water chestnut flour granules are coarse; after stirring, strain the batter through a strainer to ensure a smooth and fine texture.
3. Add 700ml of water and 250g of brown slab sugar to a pot. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat.
4. Stir the strained batter again, then use a spoon to scoop out 2-3 spoonfuls in 2-3 batches, adding them to the boiling sugar water.  
   **Note**: Stir quickly after each addition to prevent clumping. Cook until the liquid in the pot becomes a thick paste, then turn off the heat.
5. Slowly pour the cooked paste from the pot into the remaining raw batter bowl, while stirring quickly to mix into a uniform and thick raw-cooked batter.
6. Add the chopped water chestnuts to the raw-cooked batter and stir evenly.
7. Pour the mixed batter into a glass container, place it in a preheated steamer, cover with a lid, and steam over medium-high heat for 25 minutes.  
   **Doneness Check**: Remove it when the entire cake becomes transparent.
8. Remove the steamed cake, pour off any condensed water droplets on the surface, and let it cool completely at room temperature.  
   **Key Point**: Only when fully cooled should you cut along the container's edges, invert it, and unmold.
9. Slice or dice the unmolded cake into pieces.  
   **Tip**: To make white water chestnut cake, substitute the brown slab sugar with 180g of white sugar.

## Health Advice  
This water chestnut cake has a refreshing texture, is low in fat and oil-free, and water chestnuts are rich in dietary fiber. The steaming method helps preserve nutrients well. However, diabetic patients should be mindful of the sugar content and consume it in moderation.
