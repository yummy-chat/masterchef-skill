# Watermelon Jelly
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍉 Watermelon, about half (ripe, thin-skinned, with dense flesh, for juicing)
🧊 Agar (agar powder or strips) 9g (setting agent, needs to be soaked in water until soft)
🧊 Rock sugar 50g (for cooking the agar sugar water)
💧 Water 350g (to be boiled with rock sugar for dissolving agar)
🍯 Rock sugar water, as needed (for seasoning after refrigeration and setting)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Spoon
🥣 Strainer
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍳 Wok or saucepan
🥢 Stirring spoon
🧊 Rectangular baking pan or large bowl (for holding the liquid during refrigeration and setting)
❄️ Refrigerator
🍨 Small porcelain bowls (for final serving)

## Preparation Steps
1. Select a ripe watermelon: Gently tap the opposite side of the melon and feel for vibration. The melon skin should slightly tremble, and the stem end should appear slightly indented.
2. Cut the watermelon in half. Use a spoon to scoop out the red flesh into a strainer placed over a bowl. Gently squeeze the flesh with your hands to extract the juice and fine pulp.
   **Key Points**: Extract only the juice and extremely fine pulp. Strain out seeds and coarse fibers. **Do not use a blender**, as it will break down cell walls and affect the texture.
3. Add 350g of water and 50g of rock sugar to a pot. Heat over medium heat until the rock sugar is completely dissolved and the sugar water comes to a boil.
4. Add the 9g of agar that has been pre-softened in water to the sugar water. Stir continuously over medium heat until the agar is completely dissolved and the liquid is clear with no particles.
   **State Check**: When scooping up the liquid with a spoon, there should be no visible pieces or granules of undissolved agar.
5. Once the agar sugar water comes to a boil, pour in all the watermelon juice. Cook over medium heat until it boils again, then immediately turn off the heat after a brief boil.
   **Note**: Do not boil over high heat for too long. Some separation of the pigment after boiling is normal. This will naturally create a layered effect, lighter at the bottom and darker at the top, after cooling and setting.
6. Skim off any foam from the surface. Pour the mixture into a baking pan or large bowl. Allow it to cool slightly, then place it in the refrigerator to chill until the liquid is completely set and jelly-like.
7. Use a knife to cut the set watermelon jelly into small pieces. It is recommended to cut diagonally, creating pieces of varying thickness and irregular, natural shapes. Place into small bowls. Drizzle with a little rock sugar water to taste and garnish with mint leaves.

## Health Tips
This watermelon jelly is rich in vitamins and water, making it ideal for cooling down in summer. However, it is high in sugar, so individuals with diabetes or those controlling their sugar intake should consume in small amounts. It tastes best when chilled, but those with weak spleen or stomach are advised to let it come to room temperature before eating.
