# Lean Meat Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin, appropriate amount (fresh, minced into ground meat)  
🍈 1 dried jujube  
🫚 A few slices of ginger  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for final seasoning)  
💧 Water, appropriate amount (for soaking and simmering)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Electric stew pot (double-boiled)  
🥣 Stewing bowl  
🥄 Cutting board and knife  
🔗 Bowl (for soaking)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mince the fresh pork tenderloin into a paste, place it in a bowl, and add enough water to soak for 2–3 hours.  
**Note**: Thorough soaking helps achieve a clearer broth and a richer meat flavor.

2. After soaking, pour the minced meat along with the soaking water into the stewing bowl. Add the ginger slices and 1 dried jujube, then cover the bowl with its lid.
3. Add an appropriate amount of water to the electric stew pot, place the stewing bowl inside, cover the outer lid, and double-boil for at least 1.5 hours.  
**Criteria**: The finished soup should be golden and clear, with the minced meat naturally forming a cake-like shape floating on the surface.

4. Remove the stewing bowl, add a pinch of salt to season, and serve. No excessive seasoning is needed to highlight the natural sweet and pure flavor.

## Health Suggestions  
Lean meat soup is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for post-surgery recovery or individuals with weak spleen and stomach. This soup is low in fat and salt, but patients with chronic kidney disease should control their intake.
