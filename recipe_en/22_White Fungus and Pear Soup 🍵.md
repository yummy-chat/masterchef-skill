# White Fungus and Pear Soup 🍵
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍄 White fungus (snow fungus) – 1 large piece (soaked in cold water, squeezed dry, and shredded)  
🍐 Pears – 2 (Qiuyue pears recommended; keep the peeled skins, core removed, flesh intact)  
🫘 Red dates (jujubes) – 10 (pitted)  
🍒 Goji berries – 10 g (rinsed)  
🧊 Rock sugar – 50 g (adjust sweetness to taste)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking white fungus)  
🍲 Glass pot (for simmering)  
✂️ Scissors (for shredding white fungus)  
🔪 Pear corer (to remove core while keeping pear shape intact)  
🥄 Strainer (for removing pear skins)  
🥄 Soup ladle (for stirring and serving)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the white fungus in enough cold water for about 1 hour, with the root end facing down to fully absorb water.
2. Squeeze the soaked white fungus dry and use scissors to cut it into fine pieces.  
   **Note**: The finer the white fungus is cut, the easier it releases gelatin during simmering, resulting in a richer, smoother texture.
3. Wash the pears, spiral-peel the skins with a knife and set aside; use a corer to remove the core from the center, leaving the whole pear flesh intact.
4. Pit the red dates and rinse the goji berries; set aside.
5. Add enough water to the pot, then add the pear skins, the prepared white fungus pieces, and the cored pear centers.  
   **Note**: Pear skins and cores are rich in pectin and flavor compounds; boiling them together enhances aroma and color.
6. Bring the liquid to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes, until the white fungus has visibly softened and released its gelatin.
7. Remove the lid, and add the pitted red dates, goji berries, and the whole pear flesh.
8. Bring to a boil over high heat again, then reduce to low heat, cover, and continue simmering for 30 minutes.
9. Turn off the heat, add rock sugar, and gently stir with a soup ladle to dissolve fully using residual heat.
10. Use a strainer to remove the pear skins from the pot and discard them.
11. Place the pot in a basin of ice water to cool quickly to about 50°C until the soup becomes thick and gelatinous. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice  
White fungus and pear soup is rich in gelatin and vitamins, ideal for moistening dryness in autumn, especially for those with scratchy throat or dry skin. Adjust rock sugar amount to taste; diabetics should consume with caution.
