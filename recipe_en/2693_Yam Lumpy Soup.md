# Yam Lumpy Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🟤 1 Chinese yam (pat and chop into irregular small pieces)
🍅 1 tomato (peeled and cut into pieces)
🥬 Appropriate amount of bok choy (finely chopped)
🧂 Appropriate amount of soy sauce, salt, sugar, sesame oil

🧅 2 scallions (chopped)
🥚 3 eggs (beaten into egg liquid)
🧂 Salt to taste
🧂 MSG to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste
🫒 A few drops of sesame oil

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Wok
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪓 Cutting board
🚰 Sink
🫖 Electric kettle
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. 🔥 Hold the yam with skin over an open flame to burn off the surface fuzz, then rinse clean and scrape off the skin to prevent the sap from causing skin irritation.
2. 🥬 Finely chop the bok choy; roast the tomato over an open flame until the skin wrinkles, then peel and cut into pieces; chop the scallions.
3. 🥚 Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks into egg liquid.
4. 🍠 Pat the peeled yam with the side of a knife to crack it, then roughly chop into irregular small pieces and set aside in a bowl.
5. 🔥 Heat the wok, add oil, stir-fry the tomato pieces until fully soft and mushy, with a thick, glossy red sauce.
6. 🫖 Add enough boiling water to the wok, bring to a boil, then reduce to medium heat. Season with salt, MSG, and white pepper powder.
7. 🍠 Add the yam pieces, cook on medium heat for about 3 minutes until the yam is fully cooked.
8. 🥚 Keep the soup boiling, slowly pour the egg liquid in a thin stream into the wok to form egg flowers, then immediately add the chopped bok choy.
   **Note**: Use Wenxian iron stick yam; regular culinary yam may make the soup cloudy and affect the taste.
9. 🥄 Stir well, ladle into a large bowl, drizzle with a few drops of sesame oil, and sprinkle chopped scallions on top.

## Health Tips
This yam lumpy soup is rich in dietary fiber and high-quality protein, benefiting the spleen and stomach. It is suitable for those with poor digestion or on a weight-loss diet. Note that the sap from the yam skin can cause itching; rinse thoroughly after removing the fuzz before handling.
