# Whole Sheep Butchery
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐑 1 whole sheep (male, about 20+ jin, low fat content)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔗 Hooks and rack  
🔪 Boning knife (professional boning knives should be thinner, longer, and sharper)  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🧻 Terry cloth towel  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Hang the whole sheep**: Hang the cleaned whole sheep on a hook so that the body hangs vertically and stretches, making it easier to work with the knife.
2. **Remove special organs**: Remove the sheep’s penis and testicles. Take out the lamb kidneys from both sides of the spine within the abdominal cavity, and trim away excess fat.
3. **Split the whole sheep in half**: Locate the bone seam along the sheep’s spine. Start cutting from the neck, following the spinal bone seam downward, and split the whole sheep into two halves (left and right sides).
4. **Separate the hind leg**: At the joint between the hind leg and the torso, cut along the muscle grain and bone seam to detach the whole hind leg. Pay attention to the “socket tendon” at the hip joint—adjust the angle to remove it.
5. **Debone the hind leg**: Place the hind leg flat on the cutting board. Keep the knife blade tight against the bone, cut along the fascia, rotate as you cut, and remove the leg bone completely, aiming to leave no meat on the bone.
6. **Separate the front leg**: Find the shoulder blade (fan bone) of the front leg, cut along the connective tissue around it, and separate the front leg meat from the shoulder blade.
7. **Trim the lamb rack and ribs**: Identify the rib area. Remove the “cloud skin” (a tough membrane) from the inner side of the ribs. Once this membrane is removed, the clean ribs and meat will be exposed.

## Health Tips
This dish is suitable for cooks with professional butchery skills. It is high in protein but extremely challenging to prepare. Beginners are advised to start practicing with lamb legs. The lamb kidneys and fat removed during butchery can be saved for making kebabs to avoid waste.
