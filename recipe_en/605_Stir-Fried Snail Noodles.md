# Stir-Fried Snail Noodles
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 2 eggs  
🍢 100g hot pot balls (sliced)  
🥬 1 baby cabbage (cut into pieces)  
🍄 3 mushrooms (sliced)  
🥬 4 stalks of bok choy (whole leaves)  
🧅 1 small section of scallion (chopped into green onion)  
🍜 1 portion of dry snail noodles (cooked according to instructions and rinsed in cold water)  
🥫 Snail noodle seasoning packets (wood ear shreds, dried radish, sour bamboo shoots, dried tofu skin)  
🧴 1 tbsp light soy sauce (optional)  
🍬 2 pinches of sugar  
🧂 1 pinch of chicken bouillon  
🥣 Half to 1 packet of snail noodle soup base (added in batches)  
🍾 Vinegar, to taste  
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Cutting board + knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Small spoon  
🥣 Bowls (for beating eggs, holding seasonings)  

## Instructions  
1. Fill a pot with cold water and cook the dry snail noodles according to package instructions. Once cooked, remove and rinse under cold water, then drain and set aside.  
   **Key Point**: Rinsing with cold water makes the noodles more resilient and prevents sticking.  

2. Slice the hot pot balls into thin pieces, chop the scallion into green onion, cut the mushrooms and baby cabbage, and wash the bok choy.  
   **Note**: Set the quick-cooking bok choy aside separately and add it just before removing from the heat to avoid overcooking.  

3. Beat the eggs into a liquid.  

4. Add a generous amount of cooking oil to the wok. Heat over high heat until the oil is hot, then pour in the beaten eggs. Let them set slightly, then quickly break them apart with chopsticks.  
   Reduce to medium heat and stir-fry until the eggs are fluffy and fully cooked; there is no need to remove them from the wok.  

5. Add the sliced hot pot balls to the wok, stir-fry with the eggs over medium heat for about 1 minute until the balls soften. Use a spatula to break the eggs into smaller pieces.  

6. Add the scallion, baby cabbage, and mushrooms. Stir-fry over medium heat for about half a minute until the vegetables release water and soften.  

7. Reduce the heat to low. Add 1 tbsp light soy sauce, 2 pinches of sugar, 1 pinch of chicken bouillon, and half a packet of snail noodle soup base. Stir well, then add 60–80 ml of water to create a small amount of sauce.  
   **Key Point**: Start with half the soup base packet; adjust by tasting later to avoid making it too salty.  

8. Add the cooked and rinsed noodles, along with the wood ear shreds, dried radish, and sour bamboo shoots from the snail noodle packets. Drizzle in vinegar and chili oil to taste. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting and stir-fry until everything is evenly combined.  
   **Key Point**: Use the lowest heat to prevent the noodles from drying out or burning.  

9. Add the bok choy, increase the heat to medium, and stir-fry until the leaves soften.  
   If the wok seems dry, add a small amount of water along the edge to prevent burning.  

10. Finally, add the dried tofu skin pieces and quickly toss to coat them in the sauce.  

11. Taste before turning off the heat. If the saltiness or richness is insufficient, add the remaining snail noodle soup base in small batches, stirring well, then remove from the heat.  

## Health Advice  
This stir-fried snail noodle dish includes a good mix of vegetables and protein. However, the snail noodle soup base and hot pot balls are high in sodium. It is recommended to reduce the amount of soup base used and dilute with extra water to lower sodium intake. This dish is suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors as an occasional treat. For weight management, be mindful of the noodle portion when consuming it as a main meal.
