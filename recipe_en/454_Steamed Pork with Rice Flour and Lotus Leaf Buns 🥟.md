# Steamed Pork with Rice Flour and Lotus Leaf Buns 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 500g (cut into slices about 1cm thick)  
🍚 Round-grained rice (japonica) 100g  
🍚 Long-grained rice (indica) 100g  
🍠 Sweet potato about 200g (cut into rolling chunks)  
🧅 Scallion 10g (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger 5g (minced)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 400g (for making lotus leaf buns)  
🍄 Dry yeast 4g  
🍬 Sugar 4g (to aid fermentation)  

🌶️ Pixian broad bean paste 20g  
🌶️ Chili flakes 10g  
🥜 Sweet bean paste 10g  
🥡 Light soy sauce 10g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g  
🧀 Fermented red bean curd (Hongfang) 1 piece (about 15g, mashed)  
🍶 Fermented glutinous rice juice (laozao juice) 40g  
🧂 Star anise 0.5 piece  
🍂 Sichuan peppercorns 1g  
🪵 Cinnamon stick 1g  
🌸 Clove 1 piece (do not overuse)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 20g  
💧 Water 200g (for mixing with rice flour)  
💧 Water 200g (for making dough)  
🛢️ Cooking oil as needed (for brushing lotus leaf buns to prevent sticking)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Strainer  
🌀 Blender or grinder  
🥄 Stainless steel bowl  
🔥 Multi-tiered steamer  
🫖 Heat-resistant bowl  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍴 Knife back or comb (to press lotus leaf patterns)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the pork belly into slices about 1cm thick and 6cm long.  
2. Heat a wok, add vegetable oil. Stir-fry scallions and ginger over low heat until fragrant. Add Pixian broad bean paste and stir-fry for about 1 minute until red oil is released. Turn off heat, add chili flakes, and mix well to create the base sauce.  
3. Transfer the base sauce to a bowl. Add sweet bean paste, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, mashed fermented red bean curd, and fermented glutinous rice juice. Mix well. Add the pork belly slices, gently toss to coat. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.  
   **Note**: Toss gently to avoid breaking the meat slices.  
4. Combine round-grained rice and long-grained rice, wash and drain. Add star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon, and 1 clove. Pour into a dry wok and stir-fry over low heat for about 10 minutes until the grains turn slightly yellow and crisp, with a slight popping sound. Let cool, then grind into coarse rice flour using a blender.  
5. Mix the rice flour with an equal amount of water to form a wet paste.  
6. Remove the marinated pork, add the wet rice flour paste, and toss thoroughly until each slice is evenly coated with a thick layer of rice flour. Lightly oil the inside of a bowl. Arrange the pork slices skin-side down in the bowl, spread the remaining rice flour paste on top and into gaps, then layer sweet potato chunks on top.  
7. Soften dried lotus leaves in hot water, drain, and completely wrap and seal the bowl with the leaves. Bring a steamer to a boil, place the bowl inside. Steam over high heat, then reduce to low heat and steam for 2 hours.  
8. Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water, let stand for 5 minutes. Gradually mix into the all-purpose flour to form a dough. Let rest for 10 minutes, then knead until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap and let ferment until doubled in size.  
   **Note**: Adjust fermentation time based on room temperature.  
9. Divide the fermented dough, roll into strips, and cut into pieces about 50g each. Shape into balls and let rest for 10 minutes. Roll into oval discs. Brush half of each disc with oil, fold in half. Use the back of a knife to press lotus leaf patterns. Place the buns in a steamer basket, let rise over warm water for about 20 minutes until they slowly bounce back when pressed.  
10. Steam the lotus leaf buns over cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then steam over medium heat for 15 minutes. Turn off heat, let rest for 3 minutes before opening the lid.  
11. Remove the lotus leaf from the steamed pork, invert the bowl onto a plate, and unmold. Split the lotus leaf buns open, fill with steamed pork and sweet potato, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Pork belly combined with rice flour and sweet potato, when steamed, allows the fat to partially melt, resulting in a soft, sticky texture. However, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; those with high cholesterol or on a weight-loss diet should consume in moderation.
