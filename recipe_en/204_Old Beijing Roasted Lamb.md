# Old Beijing Roasted Lamb
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lamb (belly meat) one large piece (with skin, alternating fat and lean)
🧂 Sichuan peppercorns 20 grams (for Sichuan pepper salt)
🧂 Salt 2.5 grams (for Sichuan pepper salt)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for blanching and braising broth)
🌰 Cardamom appropriate amount (cracked open)
🌿 Angelica dahurica appropriate amount
🌰 Amomum villosum appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves appropriate amount
🌰 White cardamom appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for stir-frying base and deep-frying lamb)
🍶 Yellow bean paste appropriate amount
🧴 Soy sauce approximately 225 grams (three tablespoons)
🍬 Sugar 15 grams
🍶 Yellow rice wine appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Chopping board

## Steps
1.  Place lamb in cold water, add a small amount of Sichuan peppercorns, scallion sections, and ginger slices. Blanch for about 10 minutes, skim off foam, until the meat is just cooked. Remove and drain.
2.  Prepare Sichuan pepper salt: Put 20 grams of Sichuan peppercorns into a clean wok. Dry-roast over low heat for about 5 minutes until color darkens and aroma is strong. Pour out and let cool, then mix with 2.5 grams of salt. Crush into powder using the bottom of a bowl. Set aside.
3.  Add oil to the wok. First, fry cinnamon and star anise over low heat until fragrant. Then add cracked cardamom, angelica dahurica, amomum villosum, bay leaves, and white cardamom, stir-frying until aromatic. Add yellow bean paste and stir-fry until the bean paste aroma is released.
4.  Pour sufficient water into the wok. Add scallion sections, soy sauce, sugar, and yellow rice wine. Bring to a boil over high heat to create the braising broth.
5.  Place the blanched lamb into the braising broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 1 hour, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the lamb and the meat is tender. Remove and drain.
6.  Heat plenty of oil in the wok to 70% of its smoking point. Add the lamb piece and deep-fry for about 40 seconds, until the skin is crispy and browned. Immediately remove.
7.  While hot, slice the lamb into thick pieces. Serve with Sichuan pepper salt.
**Note**: Do not over-fry; the goal is to form a crispy exterior and seal in the juices. Slice the meat while hot to maintain the crispiness of the skin.

## Health Suggestion
Lamb is rich in protein and suitable for winter warming, but braising and then deep-frying results in high calories. It is recommended to pair with refreshing vegetables. People with high blood pressure should reduce dipping in Sichuan pepper salt.
