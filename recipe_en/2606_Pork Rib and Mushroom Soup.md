# Pork Rib and Mushroom Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok

🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Chop the pork ribs into small pieces along the bone joints for easier flavor absorption and eating.  
2. Quickly rinse the mushroom soup packet under clean water and set aside with the ginger slices.  
3. Add enough water to the wok, put in the ribs, and heat over high heat until the water is about to boil.  
   **Note**: Ensure ample water and stir occasionally during heating to remove blood foam through friction.  
4. When the water is slightly bubbling but not fully boiling, immediately remove the ribs with a slotted spoon.  
   **Key point**: Removing before boiling reduces fat loss and keeps the meat tender.  
5. Rinse the removed ribs with warm water to get rid of any residual blood foam.  
6. Bring water to a boil in the clay pot, then add the rinsed ribs, mushroom soup packet, and ginger slices.  
   **Key point**: Must add to hot water to keep the soup clear and prevent the ribs from releasing more blood foam.  
7. Keep the pot boiling with the lid off, and simmer until the ribs are tender and fully flavored.  
8. Before turning off the heat or before serving, add an appropriate amount of salt and scallions for seasoning.  
   **Note**: Do not add salt during simmering; adding salt too early will cause protein to coagulate, making the ribs difficult to become tender.  

## Health Tips  
Pork rib and mushroom soup is rich in high-quality protein and mushroom polysaccharides, suitable for gentle nourishment during post-surgery recovery or for individuals with weakened immune systems. Always add salt at the end to avoid affecting the tenderness of the ribs.
