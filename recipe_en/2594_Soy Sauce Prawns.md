# Soy Sauce Prawns
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Large prawns, about 500g  
🧄 Minced garlic, a little  
🧅 Minced shallot, a little  
🌶️ Minced green and red chili peppers, a little (for color)  
🌿 Chopped cilantro, a little  
🥄 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (divided into two uses: one for coating the prawns, one for making a slurry for thickening)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (enough for deep frying the prawns)  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount  
🥢 Soy sauce, appropriate amount  
💧 Water, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, a little (to enhance freshness)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon (use with a fine-mesh strainer to prevent oil splatter)  
🥢 Spatula  
🔥 Gas stove

## Cooking Steps
1.  Prepare the prawns: Use scissors to cut off the heads and legs. Slit open the belly of each prawn and remove the vein along the back, so they curl into a “gold ingot” shape when fried.
2.  Prepare the aromatics: Finely mince the garlic, shallot, green and red chili peppers, and chop the cilantro. Set aside.
3.  Coat with starch: Place the prepared prawns in a large bowl, add an appropriate amount of cornstarch, and toss until each prawn is evenly coated with a thin layer.
4.  Fry the prawns: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 180°C. Add the starch-coated prawns and fry over medium heat for about 40 seconds to 1 minute, until the shells are crispy and completely turned red. Remove and drain the oil.
   **Note**: Using a fine-mesh strainer as a shield can prevent hot oil from splattering; if you are concerned about splattering, you can pan-fry instead.
5.  Bloom the aromatics: Using the residual oil in the wok, add the minced garlic, shallot, and chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6.  Prepare the sauce: Pour cooking wine along the edge of the wok to deglaze, then add soy sauce, an appropriate amount of water, and a little sugar. Cook over medium heat for about 1 minute to allow the flavors to infuse into the liquid.
7.  Thicken the sauce: Drizzle a small amount of cornstarch slurry into the sauce, stirring continuously, until the sauce slightly thickens. **Key point**: The slurry should not be too thick, or it will have trouble coating the prawns.
8.  Combine and serve: Add the fried prawns to the wok and stir-fry quickly to coat evenly. Remove from heat and plate immediately. **Key point**: Stir-fry quickly to keep the prawn shells crispy.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those managing their blood sugar. However, the deep-frying and soy sauce contribute to higher sodium content, so it’s best to pair it with vegetables to balance the meal. For a healthier option, choose low-sodium soy sauce and control the amount of sauce used. For the elderly and children, you may cut open the prawn shells beforehand for safer eating.
