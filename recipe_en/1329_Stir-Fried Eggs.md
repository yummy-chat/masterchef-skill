# Stir-Fried Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Scallion (cut into shreds)
🫚 Ginger (cut into shreds)
🧄 Garlic (crushed or sliced)
🥚 2 eggs
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥄 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
🥄 1/2 tablespoon dark soy sauce
🥄 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
🌽 1 teaspoon cornstarch
💧 1/2 cup water
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed

## Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for beating eggs)
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. Cut the scallion into inch-long fine shreds, and shred the ginger. Set aside.
2. Crush the garlic with a knife, then roughly chop into pieces; keep them in chunks, do not mince.
3. Crack the two eggs into a large bowl. Whisk continuously in one direction until the egg liquid is evenly mixed and frothy.
4. In a small bowl, combine salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, cornstarch, and 1/2 cup of water. Stir until the cornstarch is fully dissolved to make the seasoning sauce. Set aside.
5. Heat the wok until it starts to smoke, then add oil. Pour in the egg liquid. Once the bottom sets and turns golden, flip it over. Fry until both sides are golden brown with a crispy, tiger-stripe surface.
   **Note**: When flipping, use a spatula to assist. Move steadily to avoid breaking the egg pancake.
6. Directly in the wok, use the spatula to cut the fried egg pancake into irregular chunks. Transfer to the large bowl used earlier for beating the eggs. Set aside.
7. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the scallion shreds, ginger shreds, and garlic pieces. Stir-fry quickly until the edges of the scallion and ginger are slightly charred, releasing a rich, fragrant aroma.
8. Return the egg chunks to the wok. Stir-fry a few times. Stir the seasoning sauce again, then pour it in along the edge of the wok.
9. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly, ensuring each piece of egg is evenly coated in the sauce. When the sauce thickens, turns glossy and red, and is almost fully absorbed, turn off the heat. Leave a little sauce remaining. Plate and serve.
   **Key**: Do not let the sauce dry out completely; leaving a small amount enhances the flavor when mixed with rice.

## Health Tips
Eggs are rich in high-quality protein. Stir-fried with ginger, scallion, and garlic, this dish is aromatic and appetizing, suitable for the whole family. Use a hot wok and cold oil to prevent sticking. Do not overcook the eggs to keep them tender.
