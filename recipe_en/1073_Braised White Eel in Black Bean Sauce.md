# Braised White Eel in Black Bean Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 White eel (250g, gutted and cut into segments)  
🥬 Fermented black beans (1 tablespoon)  
🧄 Garlic (2 cloves, minced)  
🧄 Ginger (3 slices, julienned)  
🌿 Scallion (1 stalk, cut into sections or julienned)  
🌶️ Red chili pepper (10g, julienned, for garnish, optional)  
🧂 Light soy sauce (1.5 tablespoons)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce (0.5 teaspoon)  
🍬 Sugar (0.5 teaspoon)  
🫒 Sesame oil (0.5 teaspoon)  
🧂 Cornstarch (1 teaspoon, optional, for marinating)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Steamer  
🔌 Electric steamer  
🥘 Heat-resistant dish  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Small bowl  

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the white eel segments under running water to remove any residual slime. Optional step: Gently rub the white eel segments with 3g salt and 1 teaspoon cornstarch, marinate for 5 minutes, then rinse clean.
2. In a small bowl, combine 1 tablespoon fermented black beans, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1.5 tablespoons light soy sauce, 0.5 teaspoon dark soy sauce, 0.5 teaspoon sugar, 0.5 teaspoon sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon water, stirring well to make the sauce. If a richer black bean flavor is desired, add an extra 0.5 tablespoon of fermented black beans.
3. Arrange the white eel segments flat in a heat-resistant dish, avoiding overlapping as much as possible. Evenly scatter the julienned ginger on top, then pour the prepared black bean sauce over them.
4. Bring 500ml of water to a boil in a steamer or electric steamer. Place the dish inside, cover with a lid, and steam over medium-high heat for 8–10 minutes, until the white eel flesh turns white and no blood remains.
5. Once steamed, remove the dish. Garnish with julienned scallion and optional red chili pepper strips. For extra aroma, drizzle hot sesame oil on top. Serve immediately with steamed rice.

## Health Advice
Steamed white eel in black bean sauce is rich in high-quality protein and omega-3 fatty acids, particularly beneficial for those needing brain nourishment and cardiovascular care. White eel has a high cholesterol content; it is recommended that individuals with hyperlipidemia consume it in moderation and pair it with whole grain rice for a more balanced meal.
