## Preparation Steps  

### Preparation Phase  
1. Place fresh sweet potato leaves in a basin, sprinkle with salt, and rub evenly with hands. Marinate for 10 minutes.  
**Note:** Salting helps sterilize, enhances crispness, and removes bitterness.  
2. After marinating, add water to the basin and rinse thoroughly twice to wash off excess salt.  
**Note:** Rinse multiple times to prevent the final dish from being too salty.  
3. Place the washed leaves in a strainer, squeeze out water completely with hands, then transfer to another clean basin and loosen them.  
**Key point:** Water must be squeezed out thoroughly.  
4. Add egg white and rub evenly to fully coat the sweet potato leaves.  
**Principle:** The egg white forms a protective film when heated, reducing moisture and nutrient loss.  
5. Add julienned carrot and one spoon of cornmeal, rub again until each leaf is evenly coated with the powder, loose and not sticking together.  
**Key point:** Even coating is crucial for good texture.  
6. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add small chili rings, minced garlic, chopped scallions, salt, MSG, black and white sesame seeds, roasted crushed peanuts, and one spoon of chili powder.  

### Steaming & Assembly  
1. Heat oil in a pan until smoking, then quickly pour it over the sauce bowl to release aroma.  
**Note:** The oil must be hot enough to “fry” the aromatic ingredients.  
2. Add light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, and oyster sauce to the oiled sauce bowl, mix well to form the sauce, and set aside.  
3. Loosen the coated sweet potato leaves and spread them evenly on the steaming rack lined with steaming cloth.  
**Note:** Must be loosened and spread flat to avoid clumping.  
4. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the steaming rack in, cover, and heat over high heat until steam rises.  
5. After steam appears, reduce to medium heat and continue steaming for 5 minutes.  
6. Turn off the heat when time is up, remove the lid, and let the steamed leaves cool naturally.  
**Doneness check:** Leaves should turn a darker color and be fully cooked.  
7. Plate the cooled steamed sweet potato leaves and drizzle with the prepared sauce before serving.  

## Health Advice  
Sweet potato leaves are rich in vitamins and dietary fiber. Steaming with cornmeal preserves nutrients maximally, making this dish suitable for blood sugar control or weight loss. Be sure to wash off excess salt thoroughly after marinating to avoid saltiness; using egg white to coat helps keep the leaves a vibrant green.
