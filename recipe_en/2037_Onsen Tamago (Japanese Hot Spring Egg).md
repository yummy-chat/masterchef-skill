# Onsen Tamago (Japanese Hot Spring Egg)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Sterile eggs, about 5 pieces (must be edible raw grade)  
🧂 Seasonings: None  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔥 Gas stove  
🍳 Cooking pot  
⏱️ Timer  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel basin / bowl (for ice water)  
🍽️ Plate / bowl (for serving eggs)  

## Instructions  
1. Fill the pot with enough water and heat over high heat until it comes to a full boil.  
2. Once the water is boiling, immediately turn off the heat. Gently lower the sterile eggs into the hot water using a slotted spoon.  
   **Note**: Make sure to turn off the heat before adding the eggs to avoid continuous cooking.  
3. Start the timer for exactly **7 minutes and 11 seconds** from the moment the eggs enter the water. Do not cover the pot during this time.  
   **Key**: Accurate timing is essential for the success of onsen tamago.  
4. As soon as the timer goes off, use the slotted spoon to remove all eggs and immediately transfer them into prepared ice water.  
   **Purpose**: Rapid cooling stops residual heat from further cooking the eggs.  
5. Let the eggs soak in the ice water for at least 5 minutes until fully cooled.  
6. Take one cooled egg, gently tap it against the edge of a bowl, and carefully crack it close to the bottom of the bowl.  
   **Note**: Tap and open the egg gently, keeping it close to the bowl's bottom to prevent the yolk from breaking from the impact.  

## Health Tips  
This onsen tamago requires almost no seasoning, is high in protein, and is suitable for fitness or calorie-conscious individuals as a breakfast item or salad addition. The key step lies in precise temperature control; if the timing is off, the egg white may be too raw or too cooked. It is recommended to use edible raw grade eggs to ensure food safety.
