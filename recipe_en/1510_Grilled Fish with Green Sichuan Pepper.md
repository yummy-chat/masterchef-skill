# Grilled Fish with Green Sichuan Pepper
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Sea bass / Yellow croaker / Mandarin fish, about 1-2 fish (firm flesh, few main bones)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (some sliced, some kept whole)
🌿 Fresh green Sichuan pepper, appropriate amount
🌿 Dried green Sichuan pepper, appropriate amount
🫑 Hangzhou pepper, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount
🌱 Cumin, a small pinch
🧂 Salt, 4-5 grams per 500g of fish
🍶 High-proof baijiu, appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce / Soy sauce, a small amount
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount
🍇 Vinegar, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🫒 Green Sichuan pepper oil, appropriate amount

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife, scissors
🔥 Blowtorch (optional)
🔥 Oven (optional)
🍳 Iron wok
🥘 Stainless steel baking pan
🖌️ Brush

## Instructions
1. Scale and clean the fish. Butterfly it by cutting from the back along the bone to the belly. Chop through the head, cut open the belly bones, remove the internal organs and blood along the bones, and pat the surface dry with paper towels.
2. Evenly sprinkle salt over the fish. Rub the high-proof baijiu, scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic slices, and cumin together to release their juices, then pour over the fish. Refrigerate and marinate for 30 minutes to 12 hours. If marinating for more than 1 hour, rinse and pat dry the surface before pan-frying or grilling.
3. Sear or grill the fish skin to create a charred aroma and remove any fishy smell. **Iron wok method**: Heat the wok until smoking, coat with oil, then pour out the hot oil and add a small amount of cold oil. Pat the fish skin dry. Cook over medium-high heat, searing one side until golden brown and set before flipping. Only the skin needs to be charred; the inside can remain undercooked. Alternatively, use a blowtorch or a high-temperature oven to quickly char the skin until golden.
4. In a separate wok, add an appropriate amount of oil and sauté the scallion, ginger, and cumin until fragrant. Turn off the heat, add light soy sauce, white sugar, vinegar, and high-proof baijiu, then turn the heat back on, add water, and bring to a boil. Add the seared or grilled fish. Simmer over medium-low heat for 10-20 minutes, until the fish is cooked through and flavorful.
**Key point**: If using a serving vessel that retains heat, simmer for 10 minutes. If plating directly, simmer for the full time until fully cooked.

5. Prepare the green Sichuan pepper topping entirely over low heat. Add green Sichuan pepper oil to a wok. Over low heat, briefly fry the fresh green Sichuan pepper until fragrant, then remove it. Using the same oil, stir-fry the garlic cloves until the surface is golden. Add the dried green Sichuan pepper and fry until the numbing aroma is released. Finally, add the Hangzhou pepper segments and stir-fry quickly until just cooked.
6. Transfer the braised fish to a serving dish. Pour the stir-fried green Sichuan pepper topping along with the hot oil over the fish. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Grilled fish with green Sichuan pepper is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. The fish is easily digestible and absorbable, making it suitable for weight loss or fitness diets. Note that the marinating time should not exceed 12 hours, or the fish will be too salty. Fresh green Sichuan pepper should not be cooked for too long, or the numbing aroma will dissipate.
