# Korean Bibimbap
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients  
🍚 Rice, as needed  
🥩 Beef shank 500g (cut into pieces about 2 cm)  
🥒 Zucchini 1 (sliced into pieces about 3 mm)  
🥕 Carrot 1 (shredded)  
🌱 Mung bean sprouts, as needed  
🍄 White mushrooms 3 (sliced)  
🌽 Corn kernels, as needed  
🥬 Spinach, a handful  
🍳 Egg 1 (fried sunny side up)  
🌿 Crushed seaweed and sesame seeds, as needed (for garnish)  

## Seasonings  
🌶️ Korean chili paste 2 tbsp  
🫗 Soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp  
🥤 Sprite 3 tbsp  
🧂 Mayonnaise 1 tbsp (optional)  
🍖 Dasida beef stock powder 1 tbsp (optional)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (final seasoning)  
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed  
🌿 Black pepper, a pinch (for marinating beef)  
🧂 Salt (for marinating beef), a pinch  
🧂 Salt (for blanching), as needed  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🧽 Grater  
🍲 Pot  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Strainer/colander  
🥄 Spoon  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prep Phase  
1. Cook rice: Rinse rice twice, add water until it just reaches the first knuckle of your finger when touching the rice surface, and cook in the rice cooker.  
   **Note**: Do not wash rice too vigorously; measure water using the finger knuckle method.  
2. Prepare vegetables: Slice zucchini, shred carrot, wash bean sprouts and corn kernels, slice mushrooms, remove old leaves and roots from spinach and wash it.  
   **Note**: Use a fork to assist with the last part of shredding to avoid injury.  
3. Prepare beef: Cut beef shank against the grain into pieces about 2 cm, add a pinch of black pepper, a pinch of salt, and one tablespoon of cooking oil, mix well and marinate.  
   **Key Point**: Must cut against the grain to prevent the meat from being tough.  
4. Blanch vegetables: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add a suitable amount of salt. Blanch each vegetable separately for 15–20 seconds until the color brightens, then remove immediately. Spinach must be blanched to remove oxalic acid; after removing, squeeze out excess water and cut into sections.  
   **Note**: Adding salt while blanching makes vegetables brighter.  
5. Fry egg: Prepare one fried sunny side up egg and set aside.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. Fry beef pieces: Heat a frying pan and brush with a layer of oil. Add beef pieces and cook over medium heat for about 30 seconds. Remove immediately once all sides are browned to prevent the meat from becoming tough due to residual heat.  
   **Key Point**: Strictly control the cooking time to keep the beef tender.  
2. Prepare bibimbap sauce: In a bowl, add Korean chili paste, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and Sprite in order. Then add mayonnaise and beef stock powder according to preference. Stir until well combined to a thick yogurt-like consistency. Adjust salt to taste if needed.  
3. Assemble: Place rice in a large bowl. Arrange all vegetables, beef pieces, and the fried egg on top. Drizzle with bibimbap sauce, sprinkle with crushed seaweed and sesame seeds. Mix thoroughly before eating.  

## Health Advice  
This bibimbap is rich in ingredients, and blanching vegetables helps retain nutrients well, making it suitable for those needing a balanced intake of vitamins and protein. Choosing beef shank and cutting against the grain reduces greasiness, but be mindful of the sugar and oil content from the Sprite and mayonnaise in the sauce to avoid excess calories.
