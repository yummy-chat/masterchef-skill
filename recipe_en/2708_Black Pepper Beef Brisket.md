# Black Pepper Beef Brisket

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Recommended Season: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef brisket, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🫑 Green bell pepper, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (some sliced for stewing, some sliced for stir-frying)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌿 Scallion, appropriate amount (tied into a knot, used for stewing)  
🍾 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (used during stewing to remove odor)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (used once for the stewing broth base flavor, once for vegetable base flavor, and once for sauce seasoning)  
🧂 Black pepper, appropriate amount (core seasoning)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🧴 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar, about one small teaspoon  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Soup pot/bowl  
🥄 Spoon/ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the onion, green bell pepper, and red bell pepper into pieces similar in size to the beef brisket pieces. Set aside.
2. Prepare the black pepper sauce: In a small bowl, add one ladle of the reserved broth from stewing the beef brisket. Add black pepper, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, sugar, and chicken bouillon. Stir until evenly dissolved. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Using the beef broth as the base results in a richer flavor.
3. Place the beef brisket in cold water in a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once a large amount of foam rises to the surface, skim it off, then remove the beef brisket.
   **Key point**: Starting in cold water and skimming the foam after boiling effectively removes odor and impurities. Condition check: Remove the meat when a grayish-brown foam appears on the surface and the meat changes color.
4. Place the blanched beef brisket into clean water. Add the scallion knot, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer gently for about one and a half hours, until the beef brisket is tender.
   **Key point**: Simmering over low heat is essential for ensuring the beef brisket becomes soft and tender.
5. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add the onion, green bell pepper, and red bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat until the peppers soften slightly. Season with a pinch of salt. Stir-fry until just cooked through, then remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Vegetables should retain a crisp texture and bright color.
6. Add a little more oil to the wok. Add ginger slices and garlic slices. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Pour in the prepared black pepper sauce. Continue to simmer over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens.
   **Key point**: The sauce must be stir-fried until thick to evenly coat the ingredients. Condition check: The ginger and garlic slices turn slightly yellow and release fragrance; the black pepper sauce becomes viscous after boiling.
7. Add the stewed beef brisket to the wok. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly for about 1 minute, ensuring the brisket is evenly coated with the sauce. Return the previously stir-fried vegetables to the wok. Stir-fry together with the beef brisket over high heat for about 1 minute until all ingredients are well combined and the sauce coats everything completely.
   **Key point**: The stir-frying time after returning the vegetables to the wok should be short to maintain their crisp texture.
8. Plate and serve hot.

## Health Tips  
Beef brisket is rich in protein and iron. Combining it with onions and bell peppers provides vitamin C, making it suitable for those needing to nourish blood and qi or build muscle. Note that the beef should be stewed until tender. Using the original broth for the black pepper sauce enhances the flavor, but oyster sauce and chicken bouillon contain significant sodium, so those with high blood pressure should control the amount used.
