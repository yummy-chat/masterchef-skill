# Chive and Shrimp Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 All-purpose flour 1 jin (500g)
💧 Water about 550-560g (5.5 to 5.6 liang)
🦐 Shrimp 1 jin (500g, about 8 liang after thawing)
🥩 Pork 3 liang (150g)
🌿 Chives about 8 liang (400g)
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk
🫚 Ginger 1 piece
🌶 Sichuan peppercorns about 60 pieces
🧂 Salt 1g (for kneading dough) + to taste (for filling)
🫒 Vegetable oil about 1 liang (50g, for mixing meat filling) + as needed (for mixing chives)
🥢 Light soy sauce and dark soy sauce mixed in a 1:1 ratio, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫗 Large glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🪓 Wooden cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Large soup spoon
🥢 Rolling pin
🪵 Wooden tray/cutting board
🍳 Glass lid

## Steps
1. **Knead and Rest the Dough**
   Add 1g salt to the flour. Gradually add water while stirring with chopsticks to form flaky dough. Knead into a ball. Cover and rest for 10 minutes. Knead once, then rest for another 10 minutes. Repeat until the surface is smooth and glossy.
   **Note**: Salt strengthens gluten; control water precisely. Alternating kneading and resting makes dough more elastic.

2. **Prepare Ingredients**
   Slice pork thinly, then cut into strips, and mince finely. Flatten each shrimp with the back of a knife into a sheet; do not chop. Mince scallion and ginger. Wash chives and chop finely.
   **Key point**: Shrimp must be flattened into sheets to ensure a springy texture in the final dumpling.

3. **Make Sichuan Pepper Water**
   Place about 60 Sichuan peppercorns and 3 liang (150g) water into a wok. Bring to a boil, then turn off heat. Let cool, then strain to obtain the Sichuan pepper water.

4. **Season the Pork Filling**
   Gradually add the cooled Sichuan pepper water to the minced pork, stirring continuously in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky. Add salt to taste, the mixed soy sauce, about 1 liang (50g) vegetable oil, and chicken bouillon. Continue stirring until thick and cohesive.

5. **Combine Shrimp with Pork Filling**
   Add the flattened shrimp to the pork mixture. Gently mix until shrimp are evenly coated with the meat mixture.

6. **Prepare and Combine Chives**
   First mix the chopped chives with enough vegetable oil to coat evenly, which seals in moisture. Then add to the shrimp and pork mixture. Mix by hand until well combined.
   **Key point**: Chives must be tossed with oil first and added last to prevent water from releasing.

7. **Form the Dumplings**
   Place a dumpling wrapper in your palm. Add filling. Fold in half. Either press together with both thumbs and forefingers into a round, pleated shape, or pinch edges tightly to form a crescent shape. Ensure the seal is tight.

8. **Cook the Dumplings**
   Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Gently drop in dumplings. Stir gently with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. When water returns to a boil, add one ladle of cold water. Repeat this process twice. Dumplings are done when they float to the surface and are plump and puffy, and spring back quickly when pressed.
   **Doneness test**: Dumplings are fully cooked when they are plump and springy to the touch.

## Health Tips
Chive and shrimp dumplings are rich in protein and dietary fiber, making them suitable for those needing an energy boost and improved digestion. When preparing the filling, chives should be mixed with oil first to seal in moisture. Diabetic individuals should control the amount of dipping sauce used.
