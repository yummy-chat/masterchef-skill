# Hand-Kneaded Old-Fashioned Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 High-gluten flour 500g  
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 30g  
🥚 Egg 50g (about 1)  
🥛 Whipping cream 60g  
🍶 Yakult 1 bottle (about 100g, can be replaced with water)  
💧 Water 160g  
🫙 Dry yeast 4g (or fresh yeast 12g)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 40g (softened)  
🧂 Salt 7.5g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🪵 Wooden bowl  
🔪 Dough scraper  
⏲️ Timer  
🍞 Kneading mat  
🔥 Oven (conventional or convection)  
🖌️ Brush  
🥘 Cooling rack  
🧻 Damp cloth (for covering dough)  

## Steps
1. Pour the egg, whipping cream, Yakult, and water into a measuring cup, stir to combine evenly, then add the dry yeast and stir well.
   **Key point**: Thoroughly mixing the liquids in advance can save effort during subsequent hand-kneading.
2. Pour the high-gluten flour and fine granulated sugar into a wooden bowl, mix evenly with a whisk, then pour in the yeast-liquid mixture, and stir with a dough scraper until no dry flour remains and a dough forms.
   **Key point**: Do not add salt at this stage. Use the "autolyse method" to let the flour and liquid fully combine, allowing gluten to develop as it rests.
3. Cover the rough dough with a damp cotton cloth and let it rest at room temperature for 20-30 minutes, until you can stretch it into a rough but unbreakable thick membrane.
4. Mix the softened butter and salt together evenly. Take out the rested dough, flatten it, and spread the butter-salt mixture evenly over the surface, then fold it into the dough.
5. Begin kneading: The dough will be sticky initially. Use a grabbing, pinching, and cutting motion to incorporate the butter, then switch to a slapping and folding technique for about 5 minutes, until the dough is no longer sticky and the surface is smooth. After rounding it into a ball, if there are cracks on the surface, continue to slap and fold several times until the surface is smooth, elastic, and crack-free, with a uniform internal structure and no butter pockets.
   **Key point**: The slapping technique is a labor-saving and efficient kneading method. Be sure to knead until the dough is smooth and non-sticky.
6. Round the kneaded dough into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest at room temperature for 20 minutes, until it springs back when tapped and does not immediately bounce back when pressed.
7. Press down to release gas, then divide into 9 equal portions. Round each portion into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest again for 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Keep the surface smooth when rounding to avoid tearing.
8. Shaping: Take one small dough piece and roll it out into an oval shape. Flip it over so the smooth side is down, and roll it up from the long side into a cylinder. Fold the cylinder in half, leaving a small edge at the bottom unfolded. Press down to seal, then roll it up into itself. Next, roll it into a strip about 35cm long, tie it into a single knot, and tuck the two ends underneath, pinching to seal.
   **Key point**: If the dough shrinks back significantly, let it rest a bit longer. Leaving a small edge makes the seal tighter.
9. Place the shaped dough pieces into a 28×28cm square pan. Let them proof at 37°C with 75% humidity until doubled in size, with a noticeable volume increase and the pieces pressing against each other.
10. Baking: After the oven is fully preheated, bake in a conventional oven at 220°C bottom heat / 160°C top heat for 20-25 minutes, or in a convection oven at 170°C for 20 minutes, adjusting the time based on browning and doneness.
11. Immediately after removing from the oven, brush the surface of the bread with melted butter or vegetable oil.
    **Key point**: Brushing with oil while hot helps lock in moisture, keeping the crust soft for a longer time.

## Health Advice  
This old-fashioned bread uses Yakult to replace part of the water, which can enhance the fermentation flavor and softness, making it suitable for a home hand-kneading experience. High-gluten flour and whipping cream provide ample energy. It is recommended to pair with vegetables or protein for balanced nutrition. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive calorie intake.
