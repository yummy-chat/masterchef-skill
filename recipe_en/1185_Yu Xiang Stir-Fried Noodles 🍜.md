# Yu Xiang Stir-Fried Noodles 🍜
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Noodles (instant noodles or dried noodles) – appropriate amount  
🥚 Eggs – 1-2  
🥩 Sausage – appropriate amount (cut into strips; can substitute with other cured meats or seafood)  
🥬 Greens (e.g., bok choy) – appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (minced)  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (use more for scrambling eggs and to coat the pan)  
🍶 “Everyone’s Sauce” – appropriate amount (can substitute with Pixian bean paste)  
🧂 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🧂 Fragrant vinegar – appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar – appropriate amount  

## Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for beating eggs, stirring, and tossing)  
🥣 Bowl (for holding beaten eggs and drained noodles)  
🥄 Strainer (for removing cooked noodles)  

## Instructions
1. Beat the eggs; cut the sausage into strips; chop the scallion and mince the garlic; wash the greens and set aside.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the noodles, and cook until about 80% done. Immediately remove and plunge into cold water to cool quickly, then drain thoroughly.
   **Note**: For non-fried noodles such as dried noodles, toss with a little cooking oil after cooling to prevent sticking. Instant noodles contain their own oil, so this step can be skipped.
3. Heat the wok until very hot, then turn off the heat. Add cooking oil to coat the wok, then turn the heat to medium. Pour in the beaten eggs and quickly stir with chopsticks until set and the edges are fluffy and crisp. Remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Use enough oil to ensure the eggs turn out fluffy and crispy.
4. Using the remaining oil in the wok, turn the heat to **low**, add the chopped scallion, minced garlic, and “Everyone’s Sauce”, and stir-fry until fragrant.
   **Note**: Be sure to use low heat to prevent the sauce, scallion, and garlic from burning.
5. Turn the heat to high, add the sausage strips and greens, and stir-fry until just cooked.
6. Add the scrambled eggs and drained noodles, then **immediately turn off the heat**. While the heat is off, add the light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, and white sugar.
   **Key**: If you are slow or a beginner, you must turn off the heat to season to avoid burning.
7. Use chopsticks to quickly and evenly mix all ingredients and seasonings.
8. Turn the heat back to high and stir-fry quickly until the liquid seasonings have completely evaporated, the noodles are dry, and the oil has re-coated the noodles. Serve immediately.
   **Note**: It is essential to stir-fry until dry, otherwise the noodles will clump and lack a dry, fragrant texture.

## Health Suggestions
Yu Xiang stir-fried noodles are relatively high in fat and carbohydrates, making them suitable for quickly replenishing energy after exercise. For those on a weight loss plan, it is recommended to reduce the amount of sauce and oil. Vegetables and eggs provide protein and dietary fiber. Choosing non-fried instant noodles is better for health.
