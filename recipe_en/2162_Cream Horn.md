# Cream Horn
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥐 Low-gluten flour 126g
🧈 Unsalted butter 117g (chilled and firm)
🧂 Salt 2.5g
🍬 Icing sugar 4g
🫧 Dry yeast 0.5g (for flakiness, not for leavening)
💧 Ice water 53g
🥛 Milk 150g
🥚 Egg yolks 2
🍚 Caster sugar 40g (for pastry cream)
🌽 Cornstarch 18g
🧈 Unsalted butter 6g (added last to pastry cream)
🧴 Vanilla extract 3g
🥛 Whipping cream 150g
🍚 Caster sugar 10g (for whipping cream)
🥚 Egg wash as needed (1 whole egg)
🍚 Coarse sugar as needed (for decoration)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔥 Oven (convection)
♨️ Induction cooker/gas stove
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Measuring cup/measuring spoons
🫗 Large bowl/mixing bowl
🥘 Small saucepan
🔪 Bench scraper
🪓 Rolling pin
📥 Baking tray
🫥 Plastic wrap
📏 Ruler
🔪 Knife
🖌️ Brush
🫧 Electric mixer
💧 Piping bag and nozzle
📦 Homemade cone molds (foil + A4 paper)
🧤 Oven mitts

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, mix together the low-gluten flour, icing sugar, and dry yeast. Cut the chilled firm unsalted butter into small cubes, add to the bowl, and rub into the flour mixture with your fingers until it resembles small crumbs. Dissolve the salt in the ice water, pour into the flour-butter mixture, and gather into a rough mass using your hands. Do not knead smooth.
   **Key Point**: The butter must remain cold and firm; the rougher the dough, the better; work quickly to prevent the butter from softening.

2. Roll the A4 paper into a cone shape. Cut the foil and fold the edges inward, wrap it around the paper cone, and seal both ends to create a cone mold.
   **Key Point**: The cut side of the foil should face inward to avoid contact with the food.

3. Whisk the egg yolks and caster sugar until combined, then add the cornstarch and mix well. Heat the milk with the vanilla extract over low heat until it just starts to steam. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture while whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the small saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring continuously, until thickened and the whisk leaves a trail that doesn't disappear. Remove from heat, add the unsalted butter, and stir until smooth.
   **Key Point**: When adding hot milk, pour slowly and whisk constantly to prevent the egg yolks from curdling; stir continuously while heating to avoid burning; achieve a moderate thickness.

4. Spread the cooked pastry cream onto a baking tray, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface, and let it cool completely.
   **Key Point**: The plastic wrap must touch the surface of the cream to prevent a skin from forming.

5. Place the rough dough into a plastic bag and roll it out to an 18cm × 16cm rectangle. Freeze for 30 minutes. Remove and roll into a rectangle about 30cm × 18cm, perform a single three-fold, cover, and refrigerate for 15 minutes to relax.
   **Key Point**: Press tightly during folding, leaving no gaps; dust the work surface and dough with low-gluten flour to prevent sticking.

6. Take out the dough and roll it into a rectangle about 45cm × 16cm, perform a single four-fold. Pull the corners to release tension, wrap, and freeze for 15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Brush off excess dry flour before folding; spray a little water to help adhesion if needed.

7. Repeat the rolling and four-fold steps twice. If the dough becomes too firm, press it gently to soften before proceeding. After the final fold, wrap and freeze for 15 minutes to relax.
   **Key Point**: Roll evenly, applying pressure uniformly; move the dough and add flour as needed to prevent sticking.

8. Remove the fully relaxed dough and roll it into a rectangle about 40cm × 20cm and 2-3mm thick. Trim the uneven edges, then cut into strips about 2.5-3cm wide.
   **Key Point**: Roll evenly and to the correct size; cut neatly.

9. Take one strip of dough and spiral it around the homemade mold, overlapping each turn by about half the width. Brush the surface evenly with egg wash, then roll it in coarse sugar to coat completely.
   **Key Point**: Wrap tightly and evenly; cover thoroughly with egg wash and sugar.

10. Place the wrapped dough pieces on a baking tray. Bake in a preheated convection oven at 170°C for about 20 minutes, or until golden brown.
    **Key Point**: Bake directly without proofing; watch for puffing, distinct layers, and golden color.

11. While still hot, remove the pastry from the molds and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
    **Key Point**: Remove from molds while hot; the pastry becomes brittle once cooled.

12. Whisk the fully cooled pastry cream until smooth. Whip the whipping cream with caster sugar to medium peaks (8 parts beaten). Gently fold the smooth pastry cream into the whipped cream until combined. Transfer the cream filling to a piping bag and pipe into the completely cooled pastry shells.
    **Key Point**: The pastry must be completely cool, otherwise the cream will melt; the filling should be smooth and airy.

## Health Advice
This cream horn has distinct flaky layers but is high in calories due to the large amount of butter used in the folds. It is recommended to serve with black coffee to cut the richness and enjoy as an occasional treat. Be sure to remove the molds while hot to prevent the pastry from breaking.
