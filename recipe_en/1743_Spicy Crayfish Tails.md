# Spicy Crayfish Tails
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦞 Crayfish tails, two jin (weighed after thawing; red pre-cooked frozen tails require no deveining)  
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments, one bowl (spiciness can be adjusted per preference, e.g., Erjingtiao or Ghost Pepper)  
🌿 Red Sichuan peppercorns, three tablespoons (choose high-quality ones to ensure sufficient numbing flavor)  
🫚 Ginger, three slices (julienned)  
🌱 Cilantro, a large bunch (cut into sections, must be added at the end when removing from heat)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (use generously, key for removing fishy smell)  
🥫 Oyster sauce, half a bottle (main source of flavor)  
🍬 White sugar, two tablespoons (household soup spoon)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch  
💧 Water, one and a half bowls (household rice bowl measure, just enough to barely cover the crayfish)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Prepare the crayfish: If using live ones, pinch the middle segment of the tail, rotate it 90 degrees, and pull out the vein. Red pre-cooked frozen tails can be used directly without deveining.
2.  Prep the ingredients: Place dried chili segments, red Sichuan peppercorns, julienned ginger, and cilantro sections into separate bowls and set aside.
3.  Pour a generous amount of cooking oil into the wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili segments, and julienned ginger to the cold oil. Stir-fry over low heat slowly until a sizzling sound is heard.
    **Note**: The amount of oil must be sufficient to effectively remove the fishy smell.
4.  Pour in half a bottle of oyster sauce, turn the heat to high, and stir continuously to allow the oyster sauce to release its aroma. Be careful to avoid burning.
5.  Add the crayfish tails and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat them. High-quality pre-cooked frozen tails do not require blanching.
6.  Add two tablespoons of white sugar, a pinch of chicken bouillon, and pour in one and a half bowls of water. The water level should just barely cover the crayfish tails.
7.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Simmer gently for 5 minutes, maintaining a slight bubbling state.
8.  Turn off the heat. Let the crayfish tails soak in the broth for 2 minutes.
    **Key**: Use the process of temperature first dropping then rising to help the tails absorb flavor better.
9.  Turn the heat back to high to reduce the sauce until it thickens, but leave a moderate amount of liquid. Add a large bunch of cilantro sections and stir-fry quickly to combine evenly.
    **Note**: Cilantro must be added at the end to enhance the flavor. Leaving a little sauce can be used to mix with noodles or for dipping.
10. Plate the dish. It tastes even better after cooling down.

## Health Suggestions  
This dish uses generous amounts of oyster sauce and Sichuan peppercorn to remove fishiness and enhance aroma, making it suitable for stimulating the appetite in summer. However, it is high in oil and sodium. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or mixed grain rice. Crayfish tails are rich in protein, but portion size per serving should be controlled to avoid excessive calorie intake.
