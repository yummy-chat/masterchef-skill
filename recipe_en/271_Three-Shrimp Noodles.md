# Three-Shrimp Noodles
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 5 lbs river shrimp with roe (yields approx. 45g shrimp roe, approx. 820g shrimp meat)  
🧅 Onion, as needed  
🌿 Celery, as needed  
🥕 Carrot, as needed  
🥚 2 duck eggs  
🍞 400g high-gluten flour  
🧂 4g edible alkali  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (approx. 2g for coating shrimp, trace amount for stir-frying roe)  
🌾 2g potato starch  
🍶 Premium light soy sauce, approx. 1 tbsp  
🫚 2 slices ginger  
🫒 Sunflower oil, sufficient amount  
💧 Water, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🕳️ Perforated plastic rice-washing basket  
🧻 Cotton gauze or fine-mesh sieve  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Soup pot  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🍝 Pasta machine  
🫙 Glass jar  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the live female shrimp in the perforated rice-washing basket and rub them vigorously to naturally dislodge the roe. Filter repeatedly through cotton gauze to obtain clean raw shrimp roe.  
**Note**: Must use female shrimp with roe; remove impurities such as fish scales during filtering.  

2. Peel the shrimp meat by hand, keeping it intact. Briefly boil the shrimp heads in boiling water, then remove and peel out the red shrimp brains. Reserve the shrimp shells.  
3. Finely chop the onion, celery, and carrot and set aside. Mix the high-gluten flour with the duck egg liquid, edible alkali, salt, and an appropriate amount of water; knead into a crumbly dough. Use a pasta machine to roll and cut into thin noodles.  
4. Add a small amount of water and the ginger slices to the raw shrimp roe. Cook over medium-high heat until the roe turns orange and is fully cooked, then reduce to medium heat and stir-fry continuously for about 1 hour until it becomes granular. Pass through a sieve to remove any impurities.  
**Note**: Be patient when drying the roe; avoid burning.  

5. Slowly deep-fry the shrimp heads in a sufficient amount of sunflower oil over medium heat until the oil becomes clear and the shrimp heads are crispy. Filter to obtain a bright red shrimp oil and store in a bottle. Do not discard the fried shrimp heads.  
6. Stir-fry the fried shrimp heads together with the chopped onion, celery, and carrot. Add water to cover and simmer for about 30 minutes. Filter to obtain a clear and sweet shrimp broth.  
7. Add salt, egg white, and potato starch to the shrimp meat; stir to coat evenly. Refrigerate for 20 minutes. When the oil temperature reaches approx. 120°C (248°F), slide in the shrimp meat and stir-fry until it loosens and slightly changes color; remove immediately.  
**Key point**: Use low temperature and short frying time to keep the shrimp tender and smooth.  

8. Leave a little oil in the wok. First, stir-fry the shrimp brains until red oil is released. Add the shrimp meat and stir-fry over high heat for a few seconds, then add the dried shrimp roe and continue stir-frying over high heat for about 10 seconds. Remove from heat.  
**Key point**: The combined stir-frying time is extremely short to prevent the shrimp meat from becoming tough.  

9. Place the premium light soy sauce and two tablespoons of hot shrimp broth in a bowl. Add the cooked noodles tossed with a little oil. Top generously with the three-shrimp mixture. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and drizzle with a spoonful of homemade shrimp oil. Mix quickly and serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This three-shrimp noodle dish is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making it suitable for summer nutrition. When stir-frying the shrimp roe, control the heat patiently to avoid burning. When briefly frying the shrimp meat in oil, use low heat and a short time to maintain a tender texture. As the shrimp roe and premium light soy sauce contain a relatively high amount of salt, pay attention to the overall salt usage.
