# Stir-Fried Potato Slices with Meat
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes (yellow-skinned potatoes, for better texture)  
🥩 Pork belly (skinned and sliced)  
🌱 Garlic sprouts (bruised and cut diagonally into sections)  
🌶️ Red chili peppers (finely chopped)  
🧅 Scallions (cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic (bruised)  
🫘 Fermented black beans  
🫒 Cooking oil, a little  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, a little  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🍾 Cooking wine, a little (drizzle along the side of the pan)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a little  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, a little (optional, for coloring)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Vegetable peeler  
🥄 Ladle  
🥄 Strainer  

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into diamond-shaped slices about the thickness of a 50-cent coin (approx. 1/8 inch or 3 mm). Place them in clean water to rinse off surface starch, then soak until ready to use.
2. Remove the skin from the pork belly and slice it thinly.
3. Finely chop the red chili peppers. Bruise the garlic sprouts and cut diagonally into sections. Cut the scallions into sections. Bruise the garlic.
4. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the potato slices and blanch. As soon as the water returns to a boil, immediately remove them and drain well.
**Note**: Remove them as soon as the water boils again to maintain crispness.

5. In a separate pan, add a little cooking oil. Stir-fry the pork belly slices until they release their fat.
6. Add the fermented black beans and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the scallion sections and bruised garlic, stirring until aromatic.
7. Drizzle the cooking wine along the side of the pan to release its aroma. Then drizzle in the light soy sauce, stir-frying to bring out the savory soy fragrance.
8. Add the blanched potato slices. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry for about half a minute. Add a little water, then season with salt, white sugar, and chicken bouillon. If the color is too light, you can add dark soy sauce for color. Continue stir-frying until the liquid is mostly absorbed and the flavors have melded.
9. When the liquid is nearly dry, add the chopped red chili peppers and stir-fry briefly.
10. Just before serving, add the garlic sprout leaves and stir-fry until just wilted.
**Key point**: Add the garlic sprout leaves at the very end to keep them bright green and aromatic.

## Health Tips
Blanching the potato slices before quick stir-frying preserves their crispness and reduces oil absorption, making this dish suitable for those who want to control carbohydrate intake while still satisfying cravings. The pork belly provides quality fat, so use minimal additional oil; pairing it with garlic sprouts and red chili peppers may help boost metabolism.
