# Homemade Garlic Confit in Oil (Microwave Method)
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine (Southern Fujian/Chaoshan Style)
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced, divided into two portions: one for garlic confit in oil, the other for crispy fried garlic)
🧅 Shallots, as needed (long variety recommended, minced)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (enough to completely submerge the ingredients in each bowl)

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Microwave oven
🥣 3 heat-resistant bowls
🥄 Spoon (for stirring)
🫙 Airtight storage container (for storing the finished product)

## Instructions
1.  Divide the minced garlic into two portions: keep one portion as is for making garlic confit in oil; rinse the other portion in clean water to remove surface mucilage, then drain. This portion is for making crispy fried garlic.
**Key Point**: Washing off the mucilage from the minced garlic removes bitterness, ensuring the crispy fried garlic tastes fragrant and crisp, not bitter.

2.  Place the minced garlic for confit, the minced garlic for frying, and the minced shallots into three separate heat-resistant bowls. Pour cooking oil into each bowl until the ingredients are completely submerged.
**Key Point**: The oil must completely cover the ingredients to prevent the exposed parts from burning during heating.

3.  Place all three bowls into the microwave. First, heat on medium-high power for 5 minutes. Then, continue heating in short intervals of about 1-2 minutes each, avoiding prolonged single heating sessions which can cause burning.
**Key Point**: Short, intermittent heating allows you to monitor the status and prevents a sudden temperature spike that could char the ingredients.

4.  During the heating process, open the microwave every 1-2 minutes. Use the spoon to stir the ingredients in each bowl evenly, ensuring uniform heat distribution.
**Key Point**: Stirring allows the ingredients to have full contact with the hot oil, preventing localized overheating.

5.  Continue heating until the minced garlic or shallots turn a light yellow and begin to float in the oil. Immediately remove the bowls from the microwave. The residual heat in the oil will continue to cook the ingredients until they are golden brown and crispy.
**Key Point**: Using residual heat completes the final cooking stage, preventing overcooking which leads to a burnt, bitter taste.

6.  After removing from the microwave, stir again evenly. Let the mixture cool down completely.
**Key Point**: The residual heat continues to work during the cooling process. Stirring prevents clumping.

7.  Once completely cool, transfer the three prepared seasonings into separate, clean, airtight storage containers.
**Storage Suggestion**: It is recommended to consume the garlic confit in oil within one week. The crispy fried garlic and crispy fried shallots can be stored longer if refrigerated.

## Health Tips
Garlic and shallots are rich in sulfides, which can help boost immunity. When using a microwave, carefully control the oil temperature and heating time to prevent burning, which can create harmful substances. Suitable for daily flavoring; can be consumed in moderation by people with cardiovascular conditions.
