## Cooking Steps  

### Preparation Phase  
1. **Make the pepper-salt dipping powder**: Place a dry, oil-free wok over low heat. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and stir-fry slowly until they release their aroma and become brittle. Transfer the toasted peppercorns to a cutting board and crush them with a rolling pin. Pass the crushed peppercorns through a fine mesh sieve to remove the coarse particles and impurities, keeping only the fine powder. Mix the fine peppercorn powder with an appropriate amount of salt.  
**Key point**: The Sichuan peppercorns must be stir-fried until they can be crushed easily with a pinch. Sifting ensures a smooth dipping powder, removing the coarse husks and impurities.  

2. **Prepare the shrimp**: Devein the green shrimp, rinse them thoroughly under running water, then squeeze out the excess water by hand or pat them dry with a kitchen towel.  
**Key point**: Remove the vein completely. Squeeze out as much water as possible; otherwise, it will affect the batter’s adhesion and the final texture.  

3. **Marinate the shrimp**: Add an appropriate amount of salt, white pepper powder, and Hua Diao wine to the drained shrimp. Mix everything well with your hands and let it marinate for a while so the flavors penetrate.  
**Key point**: This step gives the shrimp a basic flavor and removes any fishy smell. Do not skip or reduce the amount.  

4. **Prepare the batter**: Separate the egg whites from 2 eggs and beat them lightly. Add the dry mixture of flour and starch (mixed in a 1:1 ratio). Gradually add a small amount of water, mixing until the batter is smooth and free of lumps. Finally, add a few drops of vegetable oil and mix well again.  
**Key point**: The proportion of flour to starch must be exactly 1:1 – this ensures the finished product has both structure and tenderness. The batter should be smooth with no lumps. Adding oil makes the crust glossier after frying.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. **Dust and coat the shrimp**: Lightly roll the marinated shrimp in dry flour so they are evenly coated with a thin layer. Shake off any excess flour. Then dip the floured shrimp into the prepared batter, making sure they are evenly coated.  
**Key point**: Dusting with dry flour helps the batter stick better to the shrimp. However, do not use too much flour, or it will negatively affect the texture of the crust.  

2. **First low-temperature fry**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat it to 30-40% hot (around 110–130°C / 230–266°F). Gently place the battered shrimp one by one into the oil. Fry over low heat for about 3 minutes.  
**Key point**: The oil temperature is the most critical factor for this dish – it must be kept at 30-40% hot and fried slowly over low heat. If the oil is too hot, the shrimp will turn dark and become tough, which does not match the “soft-fried” requirement.  

3. **Drain and serve**: Once the shrimp are set and have turned an even, light golden color, remove them with a slotted spoon. Drain off excess oil thoroughly. Plate and serve with the homemade pepper-salt dipping powder.  
**Key point**: The crust of the finished shrimp should be soft and not hard or crispy – this is the core characteristic of “soft frying,” which is distinctly different from crispy fried foods.  

## Health Tips  
Shrimp are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. Using egg white batter for soft frying absorbs less oil than traditional crispy frying, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet. It’s recommended to pair with the pepper-salt powder, but be mindful of the salt intake.
