## Instructions
1. Cut the beef short ribs into pieces, place them in a pot with water, and soak for 2–3 hours. Change the water once during this time to fully remove blood.
2. Core the apple and pear, then cut into pieces. Cut the scallions into sections and slice the ginger. Peel the potatoes and cut them into pieces. Set aside.
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the baby bok choy, then immediately transfer to cold water. Drain and squeeze out excess water.
4. Place the blanched bok choy in a container. Add minced garlic, gochujang, gochugaru, 2 tbsp perilla seed powder, fish sauce, and black pepper. Mix well to season.
   **Note**: Store-bought kimchi can be used instead to save time.
5. Drain the soaked beef short ribs and add fresh water. Bring to a boil, skim off any scum, then remove the ribs and drain.
6. Bring a pot of water to a boil in the red cast iron pot. Add the blanched beef short ribs, apple pieces, pear pieces, scallion sections, ginger slices, star anise, and doenjang. Stir to combine.
7. Cover with a lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and braise for about 2 hours. Check occasionally and add water if needed to prevent burning.
   **Note**: If using a pressure cooker, about 30 minutes is enough to achieve tenderness.
8. After braising, remove the lid. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to discard the apple, pear, scallion, ginger, star anise, and other solid seasonings.
9. Add the potato pieces and seasoned bok choy. Stir to combine, then cover and continue braising for about 15 minutes until the potatoes are tender and the sauce has thickened.
   **Key point**: Do not add the potatoes too early, or they will become mushy.
10. Turn off the heat. Arrange the beef short ribs on a plate, garnish with scallion slices and a sprinkle of perilla seed powder.
    **Note**: If the fruit is blended into a puree before adding to the pot, the sauce will be thicker and more flavorful.

## Health Tips  
Adding apple and pear while braising beef short ribs naturally tenderizes the meat and reduces greasiness. This is suitable for those needing iron supplementation and controlling fat intake. Fish sauce and chili paste are high in sodium; those with high blood pressure should limit consumption of the broth.
