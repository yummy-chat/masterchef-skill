# Green Sichuan Pepper Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (filleted into fish fillets, bones separated)
🌶️ Green Thai chilies 500g (half cut into rings, half minced)
🫑 Pickled green chilies 150g (minced)
🫚 Pickled ginger 50g (minced)
🥒 Pickled radish 100g (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic 100g (minced)
🧅 Old ginger 50g (minced)
🫘 Tofu 1 block (cut into pieces)
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper 50g (used in two batches)
🍠 Sweet potato starch 30g (for coating fish fillets)
🧅 Scallions 2 (chopped)
🍶 Cooking wine as needed
🧂 Salt generous amount (for curing) + to taste (for seasoning)
🌶️ White pepper as needed
🧂 MSG as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon as needed
🌶️ Red Sichuan pepper as needed (for marinating)
🫒 Rapeseed oil about 200ml
🥓 Lard about 50g

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🥡 Slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🪣 Large basin
🧺 Basket or colander (for rinsing fish)

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean and prepare the grass carp, fillet into slices, and chop the bones into sections. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, cooking wine, white pepper, and a generous amount of salt to both the fillets and bones. Mix well and marinate for a few minutes. Then rinse repeatedly under running water until the water runs clear. Drain thoroughly.
**Note**: The key to tender and smooth fish is the heavy salting followed by thorough rinsing.
2. Separate the fish fillets from the bones. Add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and red Sichuan pepper to the fillets. Mix well to marinate. Then add sweet potato starch and mix until evenly coated.
**Note**: The base flavor and starch coating directly affect the tenderness of the finished dish.
3. Cut half the green Thai chilies into rings and set aside. Mince the remaining half together with regular green chilies. Mince together the pickled green chilies, pickled ginger, and garlic. Finely dice the pickled radish.
4. Heat the wok, add the rapeseed oil and lard. When the oil reaches about 60% heat, first add a small handful of green Sichuan pepper and fry until fragrant. Then add the minced pickled green chilies, ginger, and garlic, followed by the minced green chilies and diced pickled radish. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until aromatic and the lactic acid flavor of the pickles is released.
**Note**: A blend of oils is more aromatic. Add ingredients in order, fully stir-frying to release the pickled flavor.
5. Add the fish bones and stir-fry briefly. Add enough water or stock to cover. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook until the broth turns white and fragrant. Skim off any foam.
6. Season the broth with salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, and white pepper. Add the tofu or other side dishes and cook until done. Remove and arrange them at the bottom of a serving basin.
**Note**: Do not add too many side dishes; remove them once cooked and use as a base.
7. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Scatter the coated fish fillets one by one into the pot. Do not stir immediately. Wait until the fillets are set and turn white, then gently push them apart. Cook until about 80-90% done.
**Note**: Do not stir immediately after adding the fillets, or the coating will come off.
8. Pour the cooked fish fillets and broth into the basin over the arranged side dishes.
9. Sprinkle scallions, the reserved green Thai chili rings, and green Sichuan pepper over the fish. In a separate pot, heat the mixed oil to about 60% temperature. Add green Sichuan pepper and green Thai chili rings, fry until fragrant, then quickly pour the hot oil along with the fried aromatics over the fish.
**Note**: The oil must be very hot to fully release the complex aromas of scallions, Sichuan pepper, and chilies.

## Health Suggestions
Green Sichuan pepper fish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those needing calcium supplementation. However, it contains pickled vegetables and a relatively high amount of oil, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. The coated fish fillets are tender and smooth, but the aromatic oil layer is intensely numbing and spicy; please control your intake accordingly.
