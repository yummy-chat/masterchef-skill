# Spicy Spiral Shells in Sauce
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐚 Spiral shells (whelks) appropriate amount
🧅 Onion appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili pepper appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount
🫑 Fresh Sichuan pepper appropriate amount
🍋 Lemon 1
🫚 Scallion appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount
🍾 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
💧 Boiling water appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar 1 tbsp
🌶️ Chili seasoning appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🫗 Soy sauce 4 tbsp
🥒 Wasabi appropriate amount
🍶 Huadiao wine appropriate amount
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Stockpot
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧊 Ice water basin
🍽️ Plate
📦 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. **Prepare Aromatics**: Slice part of the ginger, mince the rest; cut onion into shreds; slice chili pepper into rings; cut cilantro into sections; slice part of the lemon and set the rest aside; crush garlic and mince into a paste.
2. **Blanch Spiral Shells**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add whole scallions, ginger slices, cooking wine, Sichuan peppercorns, and salt. Once fragrant, add the cleaned spiral shells.
   **Key Point**: Add the shells after the water boils. Boil over high heat until the meat is just cooked, then remove immediately. Do not overcook, or the meat will become tough.
3. **Cool in Ice Water**: Quickly plunge the boiled spiral shells into ice water to cool them down rapidly, ensuring a crisp and tender texture. Drain and set aside.
4. **Prepare the Sauce**: In a large bowl, add oyster sauce, boiling water, and white sugar, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Then add chili seasoning, light soy sauce, soy sauce, garlic paste, minced ginger, onion shreds, chili pepper rings, cilantro sections, fresh Sichuan pepper, and some lemon slices. Finally, squeeze in wasabi and Huadiao wine, and mix well.
5. **Marinate**: Pour the drained spiral shells into the sauce and gently mix. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for half an hour to allow the shells to fully absorb the sauce.
6. **Plate**: Remove and arrange on a plate. Garnish with fresh lemon slices.

## Health Tips
This dish is spicy with a fresh and sour kick, perfect for stimulating the appetite in summer. Spiral shells are rich in protein, but the dipping sauce has a high sodium content, so those with high blood pressure should control their intake. It is recommended to pair with iced cucumber or plain congee to balance the spiciness and increase satiety.
