# Beggar's Chicken
Cuisine: Jiangsu (Su) Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 free-range chicken (giblets, feet, tail, esophagus, and neck cysts removed)  
🌿 3 star anise  
🌿 3g cinnamon  
🌿 5g angelica root (baizhi)  
🌶️ 3g dried Sichuan peppercorns  
🍃 3 bay leaves  
🧅 1 small bunch of scallions  
🧅 1 onion (sliced)  
🫚 1 piece of ginger (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic slices, as needed  
🍊 1 slice of dried tangerine peel  
🍎 Half an apple (sliced)  
🍃 1-2 lotus leaves (pre-soaked until soft)  
🪨 Clay (suitable amount, can be purchased online)  
🧂 30g salt  
🌶️ 30g ground white pepper  
✨ 30g thirteen-spice powder  
🧂 80g chicken bouillon powder  
🦪 120g oyster sauce  
🥄 250g light soy sauce  
🍶 150g huadiao rice wine  
🍛 30g curry powder  
🧂 35g sand ginger powder

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🫗 Grinder or mortar and pestle  
🔥 Oven (with adjustable top and bottom heat)  
🪣 Large mixing bowl  
🪵 Rolling pin or wooden stick

## Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the Chicken**: Remove the giblets, feet, tail, esophagus, and neck cysts from the chicken. Rinse thoroughly and drain. Use a toothpick to prick the chicken skin evenly in several places to allow marination to penetrate.  
**Key Point**: Ensure neck cysts are completely removed; pricking helps the marinade soak in.

2. **Make the Spice Powder**: Heat a wok without oil, add salt, star anise, cinnamon, and angelica root, and dry-fry for 2-3 minutes. Then add dried Sichuan peppercorns and bay leaves, continue frying for about 1 minute until fragrant. Remove and let cool. Grind into a fine powder using a grinder.  
**Key Point**: Add spices in batches—the later-added ones require shorter frying time; a finer powder yields better results.

3. **First Seasoning and Massage**: Sprinkle the chicken with ground white pepper, thirteen-spice powder, and chicken bouillon powder. Drizzle with oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and huadiao rice wine. Use your hands to rub and massage the chicken for about 10 minutes, ensuring the cavity is also coated.  
**Key Point**: Massage thoroughly to allow the seasonings to fully penetrate.

4. **Second Seasoning**: Add curry powder and sand ginger powder, and continue mixing evenly.  
**Key Point**: Separating the two stages of seasoning adds depth and complexity of flavor.

5. **Aromatic Vegetables and Fruits, Then Marinate**: Add scallions, onion slices, ginger slices, garlic slices, tangerine peel, and apple slices. Use your hands to crush these ingredients to release their aroma, then rub onto the chicken for 5 more minutes. Stuff all the crushed aromatics into the chicken cavity. Refrigerate and marinate for 2 hours.  
**Key Point**: Crushing the vegetables and fruits better releases their fragrance; refrigerating allows deeper marination.

6. **Prepare the Clay**: Take clean clay, add water gradually, and knead into a thick, sticky paste that can adhere to your hands.  
**Key Point**: The clay should be thick enough—too thin won't coat well, too dry will crack.

7. **Wrap the Chicken**: Remove the marinated chicken. First, wrap it completely with the pre-soaked lotus leaves, securing with kitchen twine. Then wrap another layer of aluminum foil over the lotus leaves to lock in moisture and maintain hygiene.  
**Key Point**: The lotus leaves must be sealed tightly; the aluminum foil should be well-sealed to prevent juice leakage.

8. **Apply the Clay**: Evenly spread the prepared clay over the aluminum foil layer to form a shell about 3 cm thick, ensuring no gaps or uncovered spots.  
**Key Point**: The clay shell should be of uniform thickness and free of cracks, otherwise it may burst during baking.

9. **Bake**: Place the clay-wrapped chicken on a baking tray in the preheated oven middle rack. Bake at 250°C (top and bottom heat) for 3 hours.  
**Key Point**: Preheat the oven thoroughly; sufficient baking time ensures the meat becomes fork-tender.

10. **Crack Open and Serve**: After baking, let it cool slightly. Use a rolling pin or wooden stick to crack open the hardened clay shell, then peel away the aluminum foil and lotus leaves. The chicken will be tender to the point of falling off the bone, juicy, and full of aroma.

## Health Tips
Beggar's Chicken uses a method of lotus leaf wrapping and clay shell slow-roasting, resulting in a dish that is low in fat and high in protein, suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those needing nourishment. However, the marinade contains oyster sauce and light soy sauce, which are high in sodium—those with high blood pressure should be mindful of consuming the sauce in small amounts.
