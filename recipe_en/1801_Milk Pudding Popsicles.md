# Milk Pudding Popsicles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Milk 500ml (about 2 cartons, main liquid base)
🍼 Milk powder 80g (about 4 scoops, enhances milk flavor and thickness)
🍬 White sugar 60g (about 2 scoops, adjustable to taste)
🌽 Cornstarch 20g (about 1 scoop, thickener for a firmer texture)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🔥 Portable stove or stovetop
🥄 Silicone spatula (prevents burning)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Knife (for cutting milk cartons)
🥢 Chopsticks or popsicle sticks (optional)
🧃 Empty milk cartons (washed and dried, used as molds)
🍽️ Plate (to support the molds)

## Instructions
1. Wash and dry the empty milk cartons, then cut each in half with a knife to make two popsicle molds. If there are small holes at the cut edge, seal them with tape to prevent leakage.
   **Note**: Ensure the molds are clean and leak-proof.
2. Add milk, milk powder, white sugar, and cornstarch to the pan. Before turning on the heat, stir thoroughly with a spatula until fully combined and no lumps remain.
   **Key**: Mix completely before heating to avoid lumps.
3. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring continuously with the spatula, until the mixture visibly thickens and coats the spatula without easily dripping off, forming a thick paste, then turn off the heat.
   **Note**: Keep the heat on medium-low throughout to prevent burning. Residual heat in the pan after turning off will continue cooking, so stir for a moment longer to let the paste cool to the right consistency.
4. Pour the cooked and slightly cooled paste into the prepared milk carton molds, filling them to 80-90% full.
5. If using popsicle sticks or chopsticks, insert them into the center of the paste in each mold to secure them.
6. Place the molds carefully in the freezer and freeze until completely solid.
7. To serve, remove from the freezer, tear or cut open the milk carton to unmold, and enjoy.

## Health Advice
These pudding popsicles use milk and milk powder for a rich milky flavor, replacing heavy cream with cornstarch for a lower-calorie treat perfect for summer indulgence. Homemade popsicles allow for controlled sugar content, but consume in moderation. For those with sensitive stomachs, eat slowly and chew thoroughly.
