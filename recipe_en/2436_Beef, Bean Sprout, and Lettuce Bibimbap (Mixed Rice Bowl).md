# Beef, Bean Sprout, and Lettuce Bibimbap (Mixed Rice Bowl)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin 100g (sliced)
🌱 Bean sprouts 130g
🥬 Lettuce 70g (shredded)
🍚 Brown rice 150g (cooked weight)
🫚 Ginger 5g (julienned)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic 1 tsp
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tsp (for marinating) + 2 tbsp (for the sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tsp (for marinating) + 1 tbsp (for the sauce)
🍬 Sugar 1 pinch (for marinating)
🍬 Zero-calorie sweetener 1/2 tsp (for the sauce)
🌰 White sesame seeds 1/2 tsp
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tsp
🫒 Sesame oil a few drops
🌽 Cornstarch 1 tsp
🧂 Black pepper 1 pinch
💧 Purified water 2 tbsp
🌿 Dried seaweed flakes, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Bowl
🍲 Small saucepan (with a strainer)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Tableware

## Preparation Steps
1. Marinate the beef: Slice the beef tenderloin. Add 1 tsp light soy sauce, 1 tsp oyster sauce, 1 pinch of sugar, 5g julienned ginger, 1 pinch of black pepper, a few drops of sesame oil, and 1 tsp cornstarch. Mix well and let it marinate.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Blanch the bean sprouts, then remove and drain well. Set aside.
3. Using the same pot of water, maintain the water temperature around 80°C. Gently poach the marinated beef slices over low heat until they change color and are fully cooked. Remove immediately to avoid overcooking.
   **Key Point**: Using the poaching method eliminates the need for oil while keeping the beef tender and juicy.
4. Prepare the sauce: Combine green and red chili peppers, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1/2 tsp zero-calorie sweetener, 1 tsp minced garlic, 1 tsp chili oil, 2 tbsp purified water, and 1/2 tsp white sesame seeds. Stir until well mixed.
   **Key Point**: Since the beef is not stir-fried with oil, adding chili oil to the sauce provides the necessary fat content.
5. Assemble the bibimbap: Place the brown rice at the bottom of a bowl. Layer on the shredded lettuce, blanched bean sprouts, and poached beef slices. Drizzle the prepared sauce over the top, and finally sprinkle with dried seaweed flakes. Serve.

## Health Tips
This dish replaces white rice with brown rice and combines beef with bean sprouts. It is low in fat, high in protein, and rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or seeking a feeling of fullness. The poaching time for the beef must be strictly controlled to prevent the meat from becoming tough.
