# Oil-Splashed Scallion Eggs
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 4 Eggs
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)
🫒 Salad oil, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, 1 spoon
🧂 White pepper powder, 1 spoon (used when beating eggs), and sprinkle a suitable amount after cooking
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, 1 spoon (can be substituted with chicken stock concentrate or omitted)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan
🔥 Stove
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing the egg mixture)
🥣 Small bowl (for holding the reserved hot oil)
🥄 Silicone spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Chop the scallions and set aside.
2. In a large bowl, add the chopped scallions, crack in 4 eggs, and add 1 spoon of salt, 1 spoon of white pepper powder, and 1 spoon of chicken bouillon powder.
- **Key point**: The seasonings can be adjusted to personal taste; chicken bouillon powder can be substituted with chicken stock concentrate or omitted.
3. Use chopsticks to thoroughly whisk and mix all the ingredients in the bowl until smooth.
4. Heat the pan over fire and pour in an appropriate amount of salad oil. Once the oil is hot, pour most of the hot oil into a small bowl for later use, leaving a small amount of oil in the pan as a base.
- **Key point**: The oil needs to be heated to a sufficient temperature.
5. Pour the whisked scallion egg mixture into the pan.
6. Once the bottom of the egg mixture has slightly set, gently stir with a silicone spatula, then pour the previously reserved hot oil over the egg mixture.
- **Key point**: This "oil-splashing" step allows the egg to heat evenly and thoroughly, releasing an aroma similar to "flash-frying" or "stir-frying".
7. Continue gently folding the egg mixture from the outside in with the silicone spatula, ensuring even heat distribution.
- **Doneness check**: Fold until the egg mixture reaches a semi-set, tender state.
8. When the egg mixture is about 90% cooked, turn off the heat and let it rest in the pan for about 30 seconds.
- **Key point**: Use the residual heat of the pan to gently set the eggs, preventing them from becoming tough or hard.
9. Gently slide the egg onto a plate and fold it in half.
10. Sprinkle a layer of white pepper powder over the folded scallion egg.
- **Key point**: The white pepper powder sprinkled after cooking is the crucial step that gives it its "nostalgic flavor".

## Health Tips
Oil-splashed scallion eggs are rich in high-quality protein and scallion flavor, making them suitable for those needing a quick energy boost. However, the salad oil usage is relatively high; for those on a weight-loss diet, reduce the amount of oil used for splashing. Individuals with cardiovascular conditions can substitute olive oil and reduce the amount of salt and chicken bouillon powder.
