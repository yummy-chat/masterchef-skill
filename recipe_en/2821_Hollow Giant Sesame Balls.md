# Hollow Giant Sesame Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous Rice Flour 200g (for cooking dough balls) + 200g (for mixing dough)
💧 Boiling Water 120g
🍬 White Sugar 120g
🧂 Baking Powder 8g
🧂 Baking Soda 3g
🖤 Black Sesame Seeds to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥣 Small Bowl
🍲 Cooking Pot
🔗 Slotted Spoon
🍳 Deep Fryer
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Small Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the dough balls**: Mix 200g glutinous rice flour with 120g boiling water, knead into a smooth dough, then roll into small pieces. Boil in water until they all float to the surface, remove and drain.
   **Note**: Must use boiling water to scald the flour to ensure the dough's stickiness and elasticity.
2. **Mix dry ingredients**: In another bowl, combine 200g glutinous rice flour, 120g white sugar, 8g baking powder, and 3g baking soda, stirring thoroughly.
3. **Knead the final dough**: Add the cooked dough balls from step 1 to the dry ingredients, knead repeatedly until all ingredients are combined into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
4. **Shape and coat**: Divide the dough into evenly sized small pieces. Dip each piece in water to wet the surface, then evenly coat with a layer of black sesame seeds.
5. **Fry at low temperature**: Pour cooking oil into the pot and heat to about 120°C. Add the sesame-coated raw balls. Gently push and continuously press with a slotted spoon to ensure even heating and gradual expansion of the balls in the oil.
   **Key point**: The initial oil temperature must not be too high, otherwise the skin will set quickly, preventing the hollow expansion inside.
6. **Remove from oil**: Keep turning and pressing. When the surface of the balls turns an even light golden color, remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil thoroughly.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in carbohydrates and sugar. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened tea or vegetables to balance blood sugar levels. Suitable for occasional indulgence, but not for those managing sugar intake or on a weight loss diet.
