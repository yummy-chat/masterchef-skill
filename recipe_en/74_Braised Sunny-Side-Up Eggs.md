# Braised Sunny-Side-Up Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 3-4  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped)  
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (to prevent sticking)  
⭐ Star anise, 1  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 2-3  
🧅 Scallions (large), to taste (cut into sections or slices)  
🧄 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)  
🍾 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍚 White sugar, to taste  
🍾 Dark soy sauce, a little  
💧 Hot water, about half a small bowl (enough to roughly submerge the eggs)  
🫗 Sesame oil, a few drops  
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste (mixed with water to make a slurry)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🪵 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing cornstarch slurry, etc.)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix cornstarch with a little water to form a slurry and set aside; prepare the scallions, ginger, and garlic, slicing or chopping as needed. Place the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, and heat over medium heat. Sprinkle in a pinch of salt in a circular motion to prevent the eggs from sticking.  
2. Gently crack the eggs into the pan, keeping them intact. Fry until the bottom whites are set and the edges are crispy and golden brown. Flip the eggs and fry the other side until golden. Remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: Maintain medium heat throughout. Flip only after the first side is fully set to ensure the eggs remain intact.  
3. Leave a small amount of oil in the pan. Add the star anise and dried chili pieces first, then the scallions, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, being careful not to burn.  
4. Return the fried eggs to the pan. Drizzle in light soy sauce and white sugar, pour in about half a small bowl of hot water, and add a little dark soy sauce for color.  
   **Key point**: Be sure to use hot water.  
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the eggs to fully absorb the sauce.  
6. Add a few drops of sesame oil, then pour in the cornstarch slurry to thicken. Gently shake the pan or use the spatula to push the eggs lightly, being careful not to break them. Once the sauce is thickened and evenly coats the eggs, it is done.  
7. Plate the eggs and sprinkle with chopped scallions and toasted sesame seeds.  

## Health Advice  
Braised sunny-side-up eggs are rich in protein, but the frying and braising process increases fat and sodium intake. It is recommended to pair them with light vegetables and multigrain rice for balanced nutrition. This dish is suitable for a quick energy boost, but those with high blood pressure, high blood lipids, or high blood sugar should consume the sauce sparingly.
