# Kung Pao Shrimp
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍤 Shrimp (frozen or fresh, butterfly-cut and deveined)  
🧅 Scallions (white part, cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic (sliced)  
🥜 Fried peanuts  
🌶️ Dried lantern chili peppers  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a small amount  
🍬 Sugar, 1 part  
🍶 Vinegar, 2 parts  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🥫 Soy sauce, to taste  
🥄 Cornstarch slurry (cornstarch + water), a small amount  
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste (for coating)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Small bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer, spoon  

## Steps  
1. Thaw the shrimp if frozen, butterfly-cut and devein, then rinse and drain. Cut scallions into sections, slice ginger and garlic. Prepare fried peanuts, dried lantern chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns.  
2. Prepare the Kung Pao sauce: In a bowl, combine sugar and vinegar in a 1:2 ratio. Add salt, soy sauce, and a small amount of cornstarch slurry. Stir until well mixed. Stir again before use to prevent the salt and sugar from settling.  
3. Season the shrimp by mixing with salt to give them a base flavor. No other marinade is needed. Just before cooking, evenly coat each shrimp with cornstarch.  
4. Heat oil in a wok to about 140°C (284°F). Test with one shrimp: if bubbles form rapidly around it, add all the shrimp. Fry over high heat until the surface is golden, crispy, and starting to harden. Shortly before removing the shrimp, add the scallion sections and fry alongside for about 10 seconds. Use a strainer to scoop out everything together and drain the oil.  
5. Leave some oil in the wok. Add ginger slices and garlic slices, stir-fry until fragrant. Then add Sichuan peppercorns and dried lantern chili peppers, and sauté until the aroma of Sichuan peppercorns is released.  
6. Turn the heat to high, pour in the well-mixed Kung Pao sauce, and stir-fry quickly until the sauce becomes fragrant and thickens. Add the fried shrimp, scallion sections, and peanuts. Drizzle in a little oil, stir-fry quickly to combine evenly, and then remove from the wok. **Key point**: Do not stir-fry for too long after adding the shrimp and peanuts, or the peanuts will soften and the shrimp will become overcooked and tough, compromising the crispy texture.  

## Health Tips  
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with peanuts, which provide quality unsaturated fatty acids, this dish is suitable for muscle gain and fat loss. Be mindful of oil and sugar intake. People with diabetes can reduce the sugar-to-vinegar ratio as needed. Note that coating the shrimp with starch and deep-frying significantly increases the calorie content; it is recommended to pair with light vegetables for a balanced diet.
