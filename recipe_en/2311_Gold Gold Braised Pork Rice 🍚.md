# Gold Gold Braised Pork Rice 🍚
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork belly, about 5 jin (approx. 2.5 kg)
🥓 Pork skin, as needed (blanched with pork belly, then diced)
🧅 Red shallots, as needed (fried into crispy shallots, can substitute with purple onion)
🥚 Eggs, 3 (fried into egg floss)
🧅 Scallions, about 2 sections (for blanching meat)
🫚 Ginger, about 3-4 slices (for blanching meat)
🍬 Rock sugar, one small handful (for caramelizing)
⭐ Star anise, 2 pieces

🍶 Yellow rice wine, half a bowl + additional (for removing gamey flavor and making sauce)
🧂 Taiwanese soy paste (or dark soy sauce), 10 tablespoons (for 5 jin of meat)
🧂 Light soy sauce, 10 tablespoons
🍶 Shanxi aged vinegar, 1 tablespoon
🥣 Red fermented bean curd juice, 2 tablespoons
🥣 Red fermented bean curd, 2 pieces (do not mash)
🌿 Ground white pepper, 6 shakes
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (to adjust color)
🍶 Baijiu or rose wine, a splash (added before finishing)
🧂 Salt, to taste (add at the end as needed)

## Cookware & Equipment
🥘 Stockpot (for blanching pork belly and skin)
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying meat, making egg floss, and crispy shallots)
🫕 Clay pot (optional, for braising)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for drizzling egg liquid when making egg floss)
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board

## Instructions

### Preparation
1. Place the pork belly and pork skin in a pot of cold water. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and yellow rice wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skimming off any foam. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes. Remove the pork belly. Add the whole piece of pork skin back into the pot, add more yellow rice wine, and cook for another 10 minutes.
**Note**: Do not overcook the pork skin, or it will lose too much collagen.

2. Remove the pork belly and pork skin and let them cool slightly. Cut the pork belly into strips about 1 cm wide. Scrape off the inner fat layer of the pork skin, then cut it into strips and then into dice, same size as the pork belly strips.
3. Beat 3 eggs. Heat oil to a low temperature. Using a slotted spoon, drizzle the egg liquid into the oil, stirring constantly. Fry until the egg liquid forms small bubbles, then remove to make egg floss.
4. Slice the red shallots. Fry them in oil. When they turn yellow but not fully colored, turn off the heat. Let the residual heat finish cooking them. Quickly remove and cool to prevent bitterness. Reserve the frying oil for later use.
5. Combine 10 tablespoons of soy paste, 10 tablespoons of light soy sauce, half a bowl of yellow rice wine, and 1 tablespoon of Shanxi aged vinegar. Mix well to form the sauce. Set aside.

### Cooking
1. Place the pork belly strips and pork skin dice into a dry wok (no oil or water). Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fat renders and the meat volume visibly reduces. Pour off some of the rendered lard, then add a little of the reserved oil from frying the shallots.
**Note**: The pork skin contains water, so be careful of oil splattering during stir-frying.

2. Add a small handful of rock sugar and stir until melted. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry evenly. Check the color; if it is too light, add some dark soy sauce. Then add the fried crispy shallots and egg floss, and stir-fry to combine.
3. Pour in boiling water to cover the ingredients. Add 2 tablespoons of red fermented bean curd juice, 2 pieces of red fermented bean curd, 6 shakes of ground white pepper, and 2 star anise pieces. Cover with a lid and simmer over the lowest heat for at least 2 hours (a clay pot can simmer for 3-4 hours).
**Note**: If using a regular pot, seal the lid edge with a damp towel and stir the bottom every 30 minutes to prevent sticking. Simmer until the sauce thickens and the meat is tender.

4. Once the braising time is up, remove the lid and take out the star anise pieces. Increase the heat to high, drizzle a splash of baijiu or rose wine along the edge of the pot, and quickly reduce the sauce until it is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
**Note**: Finally, adjust saltiness to taste. If the flavor is already salty enough, no additional salt is needed.

## Health Advice
This braised pork rice dish, made with pork belly and skin, is rich in collagen. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost or improved skin elasticity, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables for balanced nutrition. Those with high blood lipids or who are trying to lose weight should consume it in moderation.
