# Homemade Marshmallows
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥄 Cornstarch 250g (to be cooked and used as anti-stick powder)
🍬 Gelatin sheets 10g (needs to be softened in cold water in advance)
🥚 Eggs 2 (about 90g, only the egg whites are used)
🍚 White sugar 20g (added to the egg whites for whipping)
🍚 White sugar 120g (used for boiling the syrup)
💧 Water 40g (used for boiling the syrup)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan (for cooking cornstarch)
🥛 Small milk pot or small pot (for boiling syrup and gelatin solution)
🥣 Glass bowls 2 (for separating egg whites, whipping egg whites, and mixing)
🔌 Electric hand mixer (for whipping egg whites and mixing marshmallow liquid)
🥄 Wooden spatula (for stirring starch and mixing sugar liquid)
🪔 Sieve (for sifting cooked starch over the marshmallows)
🍽️ Baking tray (for spreading starch, piping marshmallow shapes, and drying)
🫘 Piping bag or ziplock bag (for filling with marshmallow liquid for piping)
🫗 Measuring cup (for assisting in filling the piping bag)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 250g of cornstarch in a dry non-stick pan. Stir continuously over low heat until the starch turns slightly yellow and is thoroughly cooked. Remove from heat and pour it into a baking tray or large flat plate, spreading it evenly. Set aside.
   **Key point**: This is the anti-stick powder, must be fully cooked.
2. Break the 10g of gelatin sheets into small pieces, soak in cold water until softened, then remove and drain. Set aside.
3. Separate the egg whites and yolks from the 2 eggs. Place about 90g of egg whites into a clean, grease-free, and water-free glass bowl.
4. Add 20g of white sugar to the egg whites. Use an electric hand mixer on high speed to whip for about 3 minutes, until the meringue forms stiff peaks. Set aside.
5. In a small pot, add 120g of white sugar and 40g of water. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spatula, until the sugar is completely dissolved and the syrup comes to a boil. Remove from heat immediately.
   **Key point**: Must use low heat and stir continuously to prevent the syrup from burning and turning into caramel.
6. While hot, add the softened gelatin sheets to the hot syrup. Stir quickly until completely melted.
7. Immediately pour the hot syrup in a thin, steady stream into the whipped meringue. The hot syrup helps pasteurize the egg whites.
8. Immediately after pouring, use the electric hand mixer on high speed to beat for about 10 minutes. The mixture should expand to about double its volume and become very thick. The batter is ready when you lift the mixer and the drips hold their shape without dissolving back in.
   **Key point**: The mixture will naturally thicken as it cools down during whipping. Be patient and whip thoroughly.
9. Transfer the whipped marshmallow batter into a piping bag or a ziplock bag. Snip a small opening at the tip.
10. On the baking tray covered with cooked starch, pipe the marshmallow batter into your desired shapes.
11. After piping, use a sieve to dust another layer of cooked starch evenly over the surface of the marshmallows.
12. Place the baking tray in a cool, ventilated area. Let the marshmallows rest and dry for at least 5 hours to set.
13. Once set, gently lift the marshmallows. Use your hands or a brush to shake or brush off the excess starch from the surface. They are ready to eat.
    **Key point**: Removing excess starch improves the texture; for a chewier consistency, let them dry overnight.

## Health Advice
Homemade marshmallows are primarily made with egg whites and gelatin. Their sugar content is still relatively high. It is recommended to consume them in small amounts, suitable for an occasional treat or for use in baking decorations. Children and those with high blood sugar should control their intake.
