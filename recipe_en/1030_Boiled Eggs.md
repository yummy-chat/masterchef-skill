# Boiled Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs: As needed (adjust quantity as desired)  
- Seasonings: None

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Electric Cooker  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥤 Paper Cup (for cold water to cool eggs)  
⏱️ Timer

## Steps
1. Fill the pot with enough water and heat over high heat until it reaches a full boil.
2. Once the water is boiling, gently place the eggs into the pot, cover with a lid, and start timing immediately.  
   **Note**: Keep the heat on high throughout the process; do not reduce to low heat.
3. Use a slotted spoon to remove the eggs at the following timings:  
   - Runny yolk: **5 minutes 30 seconds**  
   - Jammy yolk: **6 minutes 30 seconds**  
   - Slightly runny yolk: **7 minutes 30 seconds**  
   - Half-scrambled, half-creamy yolk: **8 minutes 30 seconds**  
   - Fully cooked yolk: **9 minutes 30 seconds**  
   **Key**: At each timing point, remove only the corresponding egg and proceed immediately to the next step.
4. Immediately place the removed egg into a paper cup filled with cold water to rapidly cool it down, stopping the residual heat from cooking further.
5. After cooling, peel the egg to observe or taste the different levels of doneness.

## Health Tips
Boiled eggs are rich in high-quality protein and are suitable as a snack for those on a weight loss plan or fitness regimen. Note that runny-yolk eggs should be made with pasteurized eggs; for the elderly and children, it is recommended to cook them until the yolk is fully set.
