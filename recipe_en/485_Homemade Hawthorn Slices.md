# Homemade Hawthorn Slices
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍎 Hawthorn 1500g  
🧂 Salt (for cleaning hawthorn)  
🍬 White Sugar 200g  
💧 Water 80ml  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🥤 Straw (for removing pits)  
🔪 Kitchen Knife, Cutting Board  
🕸️ Wire Rack  
🔥 Oven (optional, set to 65°C for drying)  
🌀 Blender/Food Processor  
🧵 Strainer  
🍳 Non-Stick Pan  
🥄 Wooden Spatula/Silicone Spatula  
🫓 Rolling Pin  
🫙 Plastic Wrap (optional, for anti-stick)  
⭕ Round Mold (or a cup)  
📦 Airtight Container  

## Instructions  
1. **Wash the Hawthorn**: Place the hawthorn in a bowl, add some salt and water, rub thoroughly, then discard the washing water.  
2. **Remove Pits and Slice**: Insert a straw from the bottom of the hawthorn to remove the pit and stem, then cut the pitted hawthorn into thin slices.  
   - **Key Point**: Using a straw to core is efficient and keeps the hawthorn relatively intact.  
3. **Dry the Hawthorn Slices**: Spread the slices evenly on a wire rack and air-dry in a ventilated area, or dry in an oven at 65°C until completely dry and crisp, crumbling easily when pinched.  
4. **Make Hawthorn Powder**: Pour the fully dried hawthorn slices into a blender and grind into a fine powder.  
5. **Sift**: Sift the hawthorn powder through a strainer. Re-grind any coarse powder left in the strainer to ensure a fine and uniform texture.  
6. **Cook the Sugar Syrup**: In a non-stick pan, add 80ml water and 200g white sugar. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning, until the syrup thickens and can be drawn into thin threads when lifted with a spatula.  
   - **Key Point**: The degree of syrup cooking determines solidification. If too thin, it won't set; if too thick, the taste may be bitter or overly hard.  
7. **Mix into a Dough**: While the syrup is still hot, pour it into the hawthorn powder in batches, stirring with a silicone spatula. Let cool slightly, then knead into a dough by hand.  
   - **Note**: Must mix while hot, otherwise the syrup will solidify. If the dough is too dry, add a small amount of hot water to adjust.  
8. **Roll and Shape**: Transfer the dough to a cutting board, roll it into a thin sheet with a rolling pin, then use a round mold or cup rim to cut out round slices.  
   - **Key Point**: The thinner you roll, the better the final texture. A pasta machine can achieve even thinner sheets.  
9. **Air-Dry to Set**: Place the shaped hawthorn slices flat on a cutting board and air-dry at room temperature for at least 2 hours, until the color lightens and the texture hardens.  
   - **Key Point**: Thorough drying helps the sugar “sugar-frost,” creating a sandy, crisp texture.  
10. Once dried, store in an airtight container and seal. Eat as desired. It is sour and sweet, stimulating the appetite.

## Health Advice  
Homemade hawthorn slices are rich in pectin and vitamin C, making them suitable for stimulating appetite and cutting grease when lacking appetite. However, they have high sugar content, so consumption should be controlled. Ensure thorough drying to prevent moisture return and mold growth.
