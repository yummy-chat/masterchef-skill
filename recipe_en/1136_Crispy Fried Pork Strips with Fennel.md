# Crispy Fried Pork Strips with Fennel
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork loin 500g
🧅 Scallion 20g
🫚 Ginger 10g
🧄 Garlic 30g
💧 Water 80g
🌿 Fennel seeds 3g (toasted and crushed)
🥔 Sweet potato starch 60g
🌾 Cake flour 60g
🍶 Soy sauce 20g
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g
🧂 Salt 3g
🍬 Sugar 20g
🌿 Cumin powder 7g
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder 2g
🧂 MSG 2g
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 2g
🧂 Baking soda 2g
🫒 Cooking oil 10g (for the batter)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥤 Food processor
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥖 Rolling pin
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Deep fryer or pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
📦 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1.  Trim any sinew from the pork loin. Cut into thick slices along the grain, then cut into strips slightly thicker than a chopstick.
   **Key**: Always cut along the grain. Do not make the strips too thick to prevent a dry, tough texture.
2.  Place the scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and 80g of water into a food processor. Blend until a very fine juice forms. No need to strain.
3.  Place the fennel seeds in a dry pan. Toast over low heat until fragrant and lightly golden. Pour out and let cool. Place in a plastic bag and crush into a powder using a rolling pin.
4.  Put the pork strips in a bowl. Add the crushed fennel, soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, sugar, cumin powder, thirteen-spice powder, MSG, and chicken bouillon powder. Mix well. Then pour in the scallion-ginger-garlic juice and gently mix. Cover with plastic wrap and marinate in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
5.  Remove the marinated pork. Add the sweet potato starch, cake flour, and baking soda. Gently mix until combined. If the mixture is too dry, add a small amount of water until each strip is evenly coated with a batter that doesn't drip. Finally, add 10g of cooking oil and mix well.
6.  Pour enough oil into the pot and heat to about 170°C. Reduce the heat to low. Gently add the pork strips one by one, keeping them separated. Once the outer shell is set, gently flip them with chopsticks. Fry until the surface is firm and a light golden brown.
   **Note**: Do not stir immediately after adding the strips to prevent the batter from falling off. The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
7.  Remove all the fried strips. Increase the heat to high and bring the oil temperature to about 200°C. Fry the strips in two batches for about 1 minute each, until the shell is hard and a deep golden brown. Remove quickly and drain on paper towels.
8.  Serve on a plate and enjoy. The texture is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, with a rich fennel aroma and a slight sweetness in the aftertaste.

## Health Advice
Fennel can aid digestion and help cut through the grease, but as these crispy fried pork strips are deep-fried and high in calories, it is recommended to pair them with refreshing vegetables or sour plum soup to balance the flavor. Suitable for an occasional indulgence. Be mindful of portion sizes. Individuals with high blood lipids should only have a small taste.
