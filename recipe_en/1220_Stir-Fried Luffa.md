# Stir-Fried Luffa

Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥒 Luffa 1 (peeled, core removed, cut into evenly sized strips)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced into garlic paste)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch
🍬 White sugar, a tiny bit
💧 Water, two tablespoons
🥄 Water starch, one tablespoon
🫒 Sesame oil, five to eight drops
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (about 2-3 times the volume of luffa for blanching; reserve a small amount for stir-frying)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl, spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Steps
1. **Prepare the luffa**: Peel off the tough skin, cut in half lengthwise, then into sections. Remove the white, spongy core in the center, and cut the flesh into evenly sized strips.
   **Note**: The core must be removed. This is key to preventing excess water release and maintaining a crisp texture.
2. **Prepare the sauce**: In a bowl, combine salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, water, and water starch. Finally, add the sesame oil and stir well to combine. Set aside.
   **Note**: Preparing the sauce in advance shortens cooking time and ensures even flavor distribution.
3. **Blanch the luffa in oil**: Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat to 180°C (350°F). Quickly add the luffa strips and stir to separate them. Blanch for about 15–30 seconds, until they turn a bright, vibrant green. Immediately remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.
   **Note**: Blanching in oil is the key step to keeping the luffa vivid green and crisp-tender. The time should be short.
4. **Fry the garlic**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant and lightly golden, but not burnt.
   **Note**: Do not use high heat, as this could burn the garlic and make it bitter.
5. **Return and stir-fry**: Return the drained luffa strips to the wok. Stir-fry over high heat for about 10 seconds to coat the luffa with the garlic aroma.
6. **Thicken and serve**: Pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok. Immediately stir-fry quickly to evenly coat the luffa with the sauce. Once the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, turn off the heat promptly and transfer to a serving plate.
   **Note**: Work quickly after adding the sauce to prevent the starch from clumping. Turn off the heat in time; residual heat is sufficient.

## Health Tips
Luffa has a high water content. This light stir-fry, made with a small amount of oil and garlic, is ideal for clearing heat and replenishing fluids in the summer. It is recommended to use less oil and salt. People with high blood pressure or those trying to lose weight can enjoy it safely, but those with a weak or cold spleen and stomach should not eat too much.
