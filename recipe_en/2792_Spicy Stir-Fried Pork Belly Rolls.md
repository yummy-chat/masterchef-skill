# Spicy Stir-Fried Pork Belly Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly rolls, as needed
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed
🌶️ Small chili peppers, as needed
🫑 Green peppers, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a tablespoon
✨ Thirteen-spice powder, 1 teaspoon
🍬 White sugar, half a tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Chopsticks (for picking up finished dish)

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the garlic into mince, and slice the small chili peppers and green peppers into small sections or diagonal pieces. In a bowl, mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen-spice powder, and white sugar together, stirring evenly to make the sauce.
   **Note**: Ingredients should be cut into even sizes for consistent doneness. Preparing the sauce in advance allows for more efficient cooking.
2. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat it. Add the pork belly rolls and stir-fry over medium-high heat until the meat slices are fully browned and the edges are slightly charred, rendering out the fat.
   **Key Point**: Fully rendering the fat from the pork belly gives the meat a crispy, aromatic texture without greasiness.
3. Add the minced garlic and small chili peppers, keeping the heat high, and stir-fry until the garlic aroma and spiciness are released.
4. Add the green pepper sections, then immediately pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to coat all ingredients evenly with the sauce. Once the sauce thickens and the green peppers remain crisp, remove from the heat immediately.
   **Key Point**: After adding the green peppers, quickly pour in the sauce and stir-fry over high heat to prevent the peppers from becoming soft and losing their crisp texture.

## Health Advice
Pork belly is rich in fat, so it is recommended to pair it with mixed grain rice and lightly sautéed vegetables to reduce greasiness and increase dietary fiber. This dish is suitable for adults who enjoy strong flavors and have no cardiovascular concerns, but those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soy sauce used.
