# Home-Style Stir-Fried Shredded Potatoes
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes: Appropriate amount (cut into slightly thicker shreds, uniform in thickness)  
🥩 Pork (tenderloin or front leg): Appropriate amount (shredded)  
🥬 Celery: Appropriate amount (cut into thin shreds)  
🫚 Ginger: Small amount (shredded)  
🧅 Scallions: Appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic: Small amount (minced)  
🫒 Cooking oil: Appropriate amount (suggest slightly more than usual for stir-frying)  
🍶 Light soy sauce: Appropriate amount (drizzled along the edge of the wok)  
🍶 Dark soy sauce: A little (for coloring)  
🧂 Salt: Appropriate amount (added after the shredded potatoes go into the wok)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (non-stick or carbon steel wok recommended)  
🥄 Spatula  
🍲 Lid  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the shredded potatoes**: Cut the potatoes into slightly thicker, uniformly sized shreds. Rinse thoroughly with clean water several times to completely remove surface starch, then drain and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Rinsing prevents stickiness during cooking and ensures a clean, crisp texture.

2. **Prepare the accompaniments**: Shred the pork, shred the ginger, cut the celery into thin shreds, chop the scallions, and mince the garlic.

3. **Heat the wok and add oil**: Heat the wok until it starts to smoke, then add sufficient cooking oil to create a hot-wok-cold-oil state. This compensates for insufficient home stove heat and prevents sticking.

4. **Stir-fry the shredded pork**: Over medium-high heat, add the shredded pork. Let it sit until the edges change color and set before starting to stir. Stir-fry until the moisture evaporates, the pork aroma is rich, and the oil takes on the meat's flavor.

5. **Aromatize and season**: Add the chopped scallions and stir-fry until fragrant. Drizzle the light soy sauce and dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok, using the high temperature of the wok wall to release the soy sauce aroma. Light soy sauce enhances freshness, dark soy sauce adds color.

6. **Add shredded potatoes and salt**: Spread the shredded potatoes evenly over the pork. Sprinkle with salt. Do not stir at this point. Wait until white smoke rises from the edge of the wok, indicating the bottom is heated.

7. **Stir-fry over high heat and steam**: Once the wok aroma rises, stir-fry quickly to ensure even heating and uniform color. If the shredded potatoes are still undercooked and the heat is insufficient, cover the wok and steam for about 30 seconds, then immediately uncover to reduce moisture. Stir frequently to prevent burning.

8. **Add celery and finish**: When the shredded potatoes are nearly done, add the celery shreds and stir-fry quickly until evenly mixed. Just before removing from the wok, sprinkle in the minced garlic and toss lightly a few times before plating. The finished dish should be tender yet intact, with distinct shreds and a layered aroma.

## Health Tips  
Potatoes and celery provide dietary fiber, while the shredded pork adds protein, making this dish suitable for a balanced meal. It is recommended to rinse the shredded potatoes thoroughly after cutting to remove starch, which helps reduce the glycemic response. Stir-frying the pork first to render its oil can reduce the amount of additional oil needed, avoiding greasiness.
