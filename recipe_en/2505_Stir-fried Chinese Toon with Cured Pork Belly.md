# Stir-fried Chinese Toon with Cured Pork Belly
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Chinese toon (spring buds) about 300-400 grams (remove old leaves, blanch, then chop)
🥩 Cured pork belly about 150-200 grams (alternating fat and lean, sliced thinly)
🧄 Garlic sprouts 2-3 pieces (cut into diagonal sections)
🌶️ Dried chilies 10-15 pieces
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 10-15 pieces
🧂 Salt to taste
🫕 Lard as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Gently wash the fresh Chinese toon buds in clean water to remove dust, then drain.
**Note**: A simple wash is sufficient; avoid excessive rubbing to preserve texture.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the Chinese toon to blanch. Remove immediately once the color changes.
**Key**: Do not blanch too long; remove as soon as the color changes to retain the freshness and nutrients of the toon.
3. Chop the blanched Chinese toon into small pieces. Slice the cured pork belly thinly. Cut the garlic sprouts into diagonal sections. Set aside.
4. Heat the iron wok, add an appropriate amount of lard and melt it. Add the sliced cured pork belly and stir-fry over medium heat.
**Key**: Stir-fry until the cured pork belly becomes translucent and releases its oil, at which point the aroma is richest.
5. Add the dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-frying together with the cured pork belly until fragrant and spicy.
6. Add the chopped Chinese toon and garlic sprout sections. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the ingredients are evenly mixed and thoroughly cooked.
7. Finally, add salt to taste, stir-fry evenly, then remove from heat and plate.

## Health Advice
Chinese toon is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. Blanching reduces nitrite levels, making it suitable for boosting immunity in spring. Cured pork belly is high in salt; people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation and pair it with coarse grain rice to balance sodium intake.
