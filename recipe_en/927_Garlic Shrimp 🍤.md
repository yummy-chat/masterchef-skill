# Garlic Shrimp 🍤
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍤 Fresh shrimp 500g
🧄 Garlic 100g (smash and finely mince)
🥬 Garlic sprouts 1 stalk (use leaves, finely chopped)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili 2 pieces (finely chopped)
🌶️ Xiaomila chili 2 pieces (finely chopped)
🌽 Cornstarch a small amount (for marinating shrimp)
🍶 Cooking wine about 3g
🥄 Light soy sauce about 3g
🧂 Table salt half spoon (about 2g)
🌿 White pepper a small amount (about 1g)
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount (about 1g)
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Wok spatula
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Place 500g fresh shrimp in a bowl, add an appropriate amount of ice cubes and freeze for 1 hour.
   **Key point**: Must freeze before peeling to prevent the shell from sticking to the meat.
2. After freezing, remove and start peeling from the head. Remove the head and the sand sac, peel off the front section of the shell, squeeze out the tail, and extract the whole shrimp meat. Use a toothpick to remove the dark vein on the back.
3. Rinse the prepared shrimp meat with clean water, then cut horizontally into two slices.
4. In a bowl, add cooking wine, light soy sauce, half spoon of salt, a small amount of white pepper, and an appropriate amount of sugar. Mix well. Add the shrimp and toss to coat. Midway, add a small amount of cornstarch and mix well. Marinate for 5 minutes.
5. Smash 100g garlic and mince finely. Soak in clean water and set aside. Finely chop the garlic sprout leaves, Erjingtiao chili, and Xiaomila chili. Set aside.
6. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add the soaked minced garlic and blanch. Once boiling, remove and drain.
   **Key point**: The goal is to cook and remove the starch from the garlic to prevent it from burning during subsequent frying.
7. Heat an appropriate amount of vegetable oil in the wok. Add the cooked minced garlic and fry over low heat until fragrant and golden. When the garlic turns light yellow, remove and drain the oil. Set aside.
8. Leave a small amount of the garlic oil in the wok and heat. Add the marinated shrimp one by one. Pan-fry until one side is set, then flip. Cook until the shrimp change color and are fully cooked.
9. Once the shrimp are cooked, add the fried minced garlic, then add the chopped garlic sprouts and chilies. Stir-fry quickly and evenly.
10. After stir-frying evenly, remove from heat and plate.

## Health Tips
This garlic shrimp dish is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for fitness or calorie-conscious individuals. After frying, the garlic becomes less pungent and its aroma is milder, but note that the fried garlic oil is relatively high in calories and should not be consumed in excess. The cornstarch makes the shrimp tender and smooth; using less oil when pan-frying makes it healthier.
