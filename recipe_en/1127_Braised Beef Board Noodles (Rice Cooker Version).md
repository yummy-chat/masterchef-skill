# Braised Beef Board Noodles (Rice Cooker Version)
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef Shank 3 pieces (cut into approx. 2-3 cm cubes)  
🥩 Beef Brisket 1 piece (cut into approx. 2-3 cm cubes)  
🌾 High-Gluten Flour 500g  
🧂 Edible Alkali 3g (for kneading dough)  
💧 Water 260ml (for kneading dough)  
🫒 Cooking Oil 1 tbsp (for kneading dough)  
🌽 Corn Starch as needed (for anti-stick)  
🧅 Onion as needed (cut into chunks)  
🧄 Garlic as needed (whole cloves)  
🫚 Ginger as needed (in pieces)  
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers as needed (cut into segments)  
🌶️ Chili Powder as needed  
🍶 Rice Wine as needed  
🫘 Doubanjiang (Broad Bean Paste) 2 tbsp  
🫘 Huangdoujiang (Soybean Paste) 2 tbsp  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce as needed  
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce as needed  
🧂 Salt as needed (for sautéing beef base, can be slightly salty)  
🍬 Rock Sugar a few pieces  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🧅 Scallions as needed (for garnish)  
🧂 Salt 5g (for kneading dough)  
🧂 Spice Packet 1 (contains star anise, cinnamon, fennel seeds, etc.; adjust as needed)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔪 Knife and Cutting Board  
🍲 Pot (for blanching, cooking noodles)  
🥄 Strainer/Slotted Spoon  
🍳 Wok or Frying Pan  
♨️ Rice Cooker or Pressure Cooker  
🥛 Measuring Cup  
🥣 Large Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🛍️ Ziplock Bags or Food Storage Bags  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the beef shank and beef brisket into approx. 2-3 cm cubes. Place in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, remove beef and rinse, then drain. Strain the blanching liquid and set aside.  
2. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the beef cubes in batches and stir-fry until all moisture has evaporated and the surface is slightly charred.  
3. Add ginger (chunks), garlic cloves, and onion chunks, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add doubanjiang and huangdoujiang, stirring continuously until red oil appears and the aroma is rich.  
4. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, dried chili segments, and chili powder. Stir-fry until evenly combined. Finally, add rock sugar and MSG, stir-fry briefly, then turn off the heat.  
5. Transfer the sautéed beef into the inner pot of the rice cooker. Pour in the reserved beef blanching liquid until the beef is just covered. Add the spice packet, and close the lid.  
6. Select the "Cook Rice" or "Stew" function and braise for about 40 minutes. For a chewier texture, press the cook button once. For a more tender texture, press it twice.  
7. Prepare the dough: Mix high-gluten flour, 5g salt, and edible alkali. Gradually add 260ml water and 1 tbsp cooking oil, kneading into a medium-firm dough. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes. Take out and knead again until smooth.  
8. Dust the work surface with corn starch. Roll the dough into a thick sheet, then cut into strips about one finger width wide. Generously sprinkle with corn starch to prevent sticking. Place in a container, cover, and let rest for another 30 minutes.  
9. Take 4 rested dough strips. Gently flatten and slightly roll them out. Stack them together, then evenly stretch the dough from both ends. Slap the dough against the work surface 5-6 times to lengthen and thin it out. Fold and slap a few more times to form the board noodles.  
10. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the stretched board noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes until done. Transfer to a bowl with some noodle cooking water.  
11. Ladle the braised beef and its sauce over the noodles. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve.  
    **Note**: Extra braised beef can be portioned into ziplock bags and frozen. Simply reheat when ready to use.  

## Health Suggestions  
Beef shank and brisket provide high-quality protein and iron, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing blood enrichment. Pay attention to controlling the amount of salt and doubanjiang; those with high blood pressure should choose carefully. Homemade board noodles are healthier, but high-gluten flour and alkali may increase digestive burden. It is recommended to pair with vegetables.
