# Soft Fried Mushrooms
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Oyster mushrooms, as needed (main ingredient, washed)  
🌾 Flour, 300g  
🌾 Cornstarch, 150g (2:1 ratio with flour)  
🥚 Egg, 1 piece  
🍺 Beer, 300ml (for batter, to increase crispiness and malt aroma)  
🫒 Salad oil, as needed (added to batter for extra crispiness)  
🧂 Salt, about 1g (to marinate mushrooms and draw out moisture)  
🧂 Five-spice powder, as needed (to enhance aroma)  
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder, 2g  
🧂 Salt, 1g (added to batter for base flavor)  
🧂 Optional dry seasoning, as needed (e.g., salt and pepper mix, chili powder, to sprinkle after cooking)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl (for marinating mushrooms and mixing batter)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring marinated mushrooms, mixing batter, and coating)  
🥄 Soup spoon (for adding salad oil and showing batter consistency)  
🍳 Wok (for deep-frying)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing mushrooms and draining oil)  
🍽️ Plate (for serving)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the washed oyster mushrooms in a large bowl, add about 1g of salt, mix well, and marinate to draw out excess moisture.  
**Note**: The purpose of marinating is to remove excess water from the mushrooms, resulting in a better texture after frying.  

2. In another large bowl, mix 300g flour and 150g cornstarch in a 2:1 ratio. Add 1 egg, five-spice powder, 2g Sichuan peppercorn powder, and 1g salt. Stir thoroughly to combine.  
3. Pour 300ml of beer into the dry mixture and stir until a smooth batter with no dry lumps forms.  
**Key**: Beer is one of the keys to a crispy batter.  

4. After the batter is ready, add an appropriate amount of salad oil and stir again until evenly mixed.  
**Key**: Salad oil also contributes to the crispiness of the batter.  

5. Squeeze out excess moisture from the marinated mushrooms, add them to the batter, and mix with chopsticks to ensure each piece of mushroom is evenly coated.  
6. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to around 50% hot (about 150°C/300°F).  
**Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.  

7. Carefully add the battered mushrooms one by one into the oil. Fry over medium heat until set, floating, and turning white.  
**Key**: Add mushrooms one at a time to prevent sticking.  

8. Remove the initially fried mushrooms with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.  
9. Heat the oil to around 60–70% hot, then add the mushrooms back for a second fry.  
**Key**: The second fry is crucial for achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.  

10. Fry until golden brown and the shell is crispy, then remove immediately and drain oil.  
11. Plate the mushrooms and sprinkle with your choice of dry seasoning according to personal preference.  
**Tip**: Sprinkling seasoning while hot enhances the flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses beer-based batter to fry mushrooms, resulting in a crispy exterior and tender interior, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition and is suitable for occasional consumption. People with high blood pressure should use less salt and pepper seasoning.
