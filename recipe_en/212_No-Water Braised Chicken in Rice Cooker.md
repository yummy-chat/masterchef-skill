# No-Water Braised Chicken in Rice Cooker  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Whole chicken 1 (remove the tail, cut into large pieces)  
🧅 Scallion 1 (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧅 Chopped scallions appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount  
🧂 Table salt appropriate amount (used in two separate portions)  
🌶️ Chili powder appropriate amount  
🫘 White sesame seeds appropriate amount  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder appropriate amount  
🌿 Ground Sichuan pepper appropriate amount  
🫒 Hot cooking oil appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🍇 Fragrant vinegar appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife + Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍚 Rice cooker (including inner pot)  
🥣 Small porcelain bowl  
🥄 Seasoning bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut off the chicken tail and chop the chicken into large pieces. Make two cuts on the surface of the drumsticks.  
   **Note**: Scoring the drumsticks helps the seasoning penetrate during marination, enhancing flavor absorption.  
2. Slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections; prepare minced garlic, chopped cilantro, and chopped scallions separately and set aside.  
3. Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl, add ginger slices, scallion sections, cooking wine, and table salt. Massage and knead the chicken thoroughly with your hands for 15 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Massaging allows the chicken to fully absorb the seasonings, maximizing the removal of any off-flavors and enhancing flavor.  
4. Place a small porcelain bowl upside down in the center of the rice cooker's inner pot. Arrange the marinated chicken pieces around the small bowl, and place a few more ginger slices on top.  
   **Note**: Do not add any water; cook entirely relying on the moisture and fat released by the chicken itself. The inverted small bowl will collect the original chicken broth released during cooking.  
5. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, select the "Gourmet" cooking mode, and set the time to 45 minutes for braising.  
   **Doneness check**: After braising, the chicken skin should be yellow and the meat white, with firm and tender texture.  
6. While braising, prepare the spicy cold sauce: Take a seasoning bowl, add minced garlic, chili powder, white sesame seeds, Sichuan peppercorn powder, and ground Sichuan pepper. Pour in heated cooking oil to release the fragrance. Then add table salt, white sugar, light soy sauce, and fragrant vinegar. Finally, add chopped scallions and cilantro, and stir well.  
   **Key point**: Pouring hot oil over the dry spices fully activates their aroma; once stirred, you get a versatile spicy cold sauce.  
7. After braising is complete, remove the chicken and plate it. Also, take out the original chicken broth from the small bowl in the center.  
8. Pour the prepared spicy cold sauce over the chicken to enjoy both the original chicken flavor and the spicy mouth-watering chicken style simultaneously.  

## Health Advice  
No-water braised chicken in a rice cooker requires no extra water, relying solely on the chicken's own moisture to cook. The result is tender and smooth, and you can also collect original chicken broth. Chicken is rich in high protein and low fat, suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose fat. The spicy sauce is higher in calories, so it is recommended to control the amount used.
