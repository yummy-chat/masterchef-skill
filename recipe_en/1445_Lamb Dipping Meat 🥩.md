# Lamb Dipping Meat 🥩
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (Sep-Oct), Winter (Nov-Dec)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lamb leg meat, 1 whole piece (boned, skinned, cut into large chunks)
🌿 Chinese celery, 2-3 stalks (cut into sections, for boiling lamb to remove odor)
🧅 Onion, 1 (sliced, for boiling lamb to remove odor) + 1 (minced, for dipping sauce)
🥕 Carrot, 1 (sliced, for boiling lamb to remove odor)
🧅 Green onion, 2-3 sections (for boiling lamb to remove odor) + 2-3 sections (minced, for dipping sauce)
🧄 Ginger, 1 piece (for boiling lamb to remove odor) + 1 small piece (minced, for dipping sauce)
🍎 Apple, 1 (minced, for dipping sauce)
🍅 Tomato, 1 (minced, for dipping sauce)
🧄 Garlic, about 1 head (minced, for dipping sauce)
🌿 Cilantro, 1 small bunch (minced, for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies, 2-3 (minced, for dipping sauce)
🌿 White cardamom, about 5-8 pods
🌿 Angelica dahurica, about 2-3 slices
🌿 White pepper, about 1 teaspoon
🍶 Light soy sauce, about 4 tablespoons
🍾 Rice vinegar, about 2 tablespoons
🧂 Salt, about 2 tablespoons
🧂 MSG, about 1 tablespoon
🍬 Granulated sugar, about 3 tablespoons
🫒 Sesame oil, about 2 tablespoons
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder, to taste (salt-roasted)
🤍 Toasted sesame seeds, to taste
🌿 Cumin powder, to taste (optional)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍲 Large stockpot
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Small bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Bone the lamb leg, remove excess membrane, and cut the meat into large chunks.
2. Cut Chinese celery into sections, slice onion, slice carrot, cut green onion into sections, slice or smash ginger. Set aside.
3. Finely mince apple, tomato, onion, garlic, cilantro, bird's eye chilies, ginger, and green onion separately. Set aside.
4. Place lamb chunks in cold water in the pot and bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Key point**: Must use cold water to fully draw out blood and impurities.
5. After boiling, use a slotted spoon to carefully skim off any foam floating on the surface.
6. After skimming the foam, add white cardamom, angelica dahurica, and white pepper.
7. Keep the surface barely simmering over medium-high heat, continuing to skim foam until the broth changes from cloudy to relatively clear.
   **Key point**: The foam must be thoroughly skimmed.
8. Perform the "broth clearing" technique: repeatedly add a small amount of cold water to bring the broth to a boil again, allowing deeper impurities to rise, then skim again. Repeat several times until the broth is very clear.
   **Key point**: This step is crucial for obtaining a clear broth, which can be reserved for later use.
9. Add the Chinese celery sections, onion slices, carrot slices, green onion sections, and ginger slices. Cover with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer gently for about 20-30 minutes, until the lamb is cooked through and still tender.
   **Doneness test**: The lamb should be easily pierced with chopsticks but still offer a slight resistance.
   **Key point**: Do not overcook, or the meat will become tough and dry.
10. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a large bowl, combine all the minced ingredients. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, salt, MSG, sugar, sesame oil, Sichuan peppercorn powder, toasted sesame seeds, and optional cumin powder. Stir well to combine.
    **Key point**: The dipping sauce contains a variety of ingredients; mix thoroughly to blend the flavors.
11. Remove the cooked lamb from the pot. Let it cool slightly, then slice thinly against the grain and arrange on a serving plate.
    **Key point**: Slicing against the grain ensures a more tender texture.
12. Serve the dipping sauce in a small bowl. Dip the lamb slices and enjoy.

## Health Advice
Lamb is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with the fruit and vegetable dipping sauce (apple, tomato, etc.), it is refreshing, helps cut the richness, and provides vitamins. Suitable for warming nourishment in autumn and winter. However, the dipping sauce contains sugar and soy sauce; those needing to control blood pressure may reduce these amounts.
