# Steamed Silken Tofu with Minced Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🛍️ Clean plastic bag (for crushing tofu)  
🥣 Bowls (multiple, for beating eggs, mixing tofu and egg mixture, seasoning egg mixture)  
🍽️ Plate (to cover the bowl during steaming to prevent dripping, and for serving the finished dish)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for beating eggs)  
♨️ Steamer (for steaming the tofu and egg custard)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying the meat sauce)  
🥄 Spoon (for scooping egg mixture, stir-frying, and pouring sauce)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour the silken tofu into a clean plastic bag, crush it thoroughly with your palm until smooth, then transfer to a plate.
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly crushing the tofu ensures better integration with the egg mixture, aiding in setting and resulting in a smoother texture.
2. Crack 4 eggs into a bowl, add a few drops of white vinegar, and beat well with chopsticks.
   **Key Point**: White vinegar removes any eggy odor and enhances the tenderness of the egg mixture.
3. Pour half of the beaten egg mixture into the crushed tofu, stir until evenly combined, and spread flat.
4. To the remaining egg mixture, add about 2g salt and about 1.5 times the volume of warm water, and stir until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Adding warm water and salt gives the egg custard a base flavor and a more tender texture.
5. Pour the seasoned egg mixture into the plate with the tofu, and mix again until evenly combined.
6. Place the mixed tofu and egg mixture into the steamer, cover the bowl with a plate, put the lid on, bring to a boil over high heat, then steam over medium heat for 12 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 3 minutes.
7. Heat oil in a separate wok over high heat, add the minced pork, and stir-fry over low heat until cooked and separated.
8. Add the chopped scallions and minced garlic, stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, then add the Pixian bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until red oil is released and aroma develops.
9. Pour in the Shaoxing wine, then drizzle in the light soy sauce to stimulate the sauce aroma.
10. Add 3 tablespoons of water, bring to a boil over high heat, then adjust with dark soy sauce, about 2g salt, a pinch of sugar, and stir well.
11. Add the wet starch to thicken the sauce, then turn off the heat and set aside.
12. Remove the steamed tofu and egg custard, uncover the plate, and while still hot, evenly pour the prepared meat sauce over the surface. Garnish with chopped scallions and diced red chili pepper for decoration.

## Health Advice  
The combination of tofu and egg mixture, steamed to a double-smooth texture, paired with the flavorful meat sauce, provides high-quality protein and calcium, making it especially suitable for the elderly, children, and fitness enthusiasts. Due to the relatively high salt content in the bean paste and light soy sauce, those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt used.
