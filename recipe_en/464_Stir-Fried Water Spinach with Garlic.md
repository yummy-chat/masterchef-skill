# Stir-Fried Water Spinach with Garlic
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Fresh water spinach 250 g  
🧄 Half a garlic bulb (minced into garlic paste)  
🧂 Cooking oil 45 ml  
🧂 Salt 2 g  
🍬 White sugar 3 g  
🧂 Light soy sauce 8 ml  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash and trim the water spinach, discarding any wilted leaves and tough stems. Evenly cut into segments about 8 cm long for easy stir-frying.  
**Key point**: Do not cut the segments too long, or they will be difficult to stir-fry evenly.

2. Pour a small amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat until it just starts to smoke. Swirl the wok to coat the walls with hot oil, evenly covering the bottom, then add the remaining oil.  
**Note**: Heating the wok before adding oil prevents ingredients from sticking.

3. Add the minced garlic, reduce the heat to low, and stir-fry for 10–15 seconds until fragrant.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry the garlic over low heat to avoid burning.

4. Quickly add the water spinach to the wok, turn the heat to high, and using a spatula in one hand and chopsticks in the other, continuously toss and stir until the spinach softens and turns bright green.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry over high heat throughout to maintain a crisp texture.

5. Remove the chopsticks and switch to using only the spatula. Quickly stir-fry the softened water spinach for 15–20 seconds to ensure even heating. Sprinkle in 2 g salt, 3 g sugar, and 8 ml light soy sauce.  
**Note**: Mix the seasonings quickly after adding.

6. Continue stir-frying over high heat for about 10 seconds until the seasonings are well incorporated, then remove from heat and serve.

## Health Tips
Stir-fried water spinach with garlic is high in fiber and low in fat. It is ideal for cooling and relieving heat in summer, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or those needing to control oil intake. However, note that the original recipe uses a relatively large amount of oil; it is recommended to reduce it to 30 ml.
