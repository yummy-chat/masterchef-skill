# Steamed Razor Clams with Garlic Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐚 Razor clams, appropriate amount (main ingredient, need to blanch and remove the membrane)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (divided into scallion segments for blanching, minced white part for sauce, and chopped scallions for garnish)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced, for blanching to remove fishy smell)  
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount (used in two batches: half fried, half raw for the sauce)  
🍜 Vermicelli, appropriate amount (soaked in advance, used as a base)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, appropriate amount (chopped, mixed with raw garlic)  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for blanching to remove fishy smell)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for frying garlic and final drizzling)  
🍾 Steamed fish soy sauce, appropriate amount (for the garlic sauce)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (for the garlic sauce)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Pot (for blanching, frying garlic, and heating oil)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl/Container (for mixing seasonings and tossing vermicelli with sauce)  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Steamer or pot with lid (for steaming)  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Add the cleaned razor clams and blanch. Remove immediately as soon as the shells just begin to open.
   **Note**: Do not blanch for too long; remove as soon as the shells open to prevent the meat from becoming tough.
2. Allow the razor clams to cool slightly. Tear off the black membrane from the edge of each clam's meat. Ensure it is completely removed, otherwise, it will affect the texture.
3. Soak the vermicelli in cold or warm water until soft, then drain. Toss with a small amount of the prepared garlic sauce and spread evenly on the bottom of a plate to prevent drying.
4. Make the garlic sauce: Add cooking oil to a cold pan. Add half of the minced garlic and fry over medium-low heat until the garlic turns slightly yellow and fragrant.
   **Key point**: Always add the garlic to cold oil to prevent it from burning.
5. Turn off the heat. Immediately add the remaining raw minced garlic, minced white scallion parts, and chopped small chili peppers. Stir well using the residual heat. Once slightly cooled, add steamed fish soy sauce and oyster sauce, and mix thoroughly to form the garlic sauce.
6. Spread the sauce-tossed vermicelli on the bottom of the plate. Arrange the prepared razor clams on top. Use a spoon to evenly coat each piece of clam meat with the remaining garlic sauce.
7. Once the steamer is producing steam, place the plate inside and steam over high heat for 3 minutes.
   **Key point**: Do not steam for more than 3 minutes, otherwise, the razor clam meat will become tough.
8. Remove from the steamer and sprinkle with fresh chopped scallions. Heat a spoonful of cooking oil in another pot until it starts to smoke, then quickly drizzle it over the scallions and garlic sauce to release the aroma.

## Health Advice  
Steamed razor clams with garlic sauce are high in protein and low in fat, suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing to supplement zinc and selenium. The vermicelli absorbs the flavorful garlic broth, but be mindful of the intake of this staple. Removing the membrane after blanching is a crucial step, otherwise, the texture will be affected.
