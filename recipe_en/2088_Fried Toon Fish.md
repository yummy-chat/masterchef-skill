# Fried Toon Fish

Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Toon leaves, as needed (fresh seasonal toon, washed, drained, and arranged into bundles)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (egg whites only)  
🌾 Flour, 1 part  
🌽 Cornstarch, 1 part  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning the batter and making Sichuan pepper salt)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (a small amount added to the batter for crispiness, a larger amount for deep frying)  
🌸 Sichuan pepper, as needed (dry-roasted and mixed with salt to make Sichuan pepper salt)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for dry-roasting Sichuan pepper and deep frying toon)  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🥢 Rolling pin (for crushing the cooled Sichuan pepper)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing the batter)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and measuring seasonings)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the Sichuan Pepper Salt**  
   Place the Sichuan pepper in a dry wok and dry-roast over low heat until fragrant. Remove and let cool completely. Crush the cooled Sichuan pepper with a rolling pin, then mix thoroughly with an appropriate amount of salt to make the dipping salt.

2. **Prepare the Batter**  
   Separate the egg whites. Mix flour and cornstarch in a 1:1 ratio, then add to the egg whites. Season with a pinch of salt and a small amount of cooking oil, stirring evenly to form a batter. Adding oil to the batter helps make the fried coating crispier.

3. **Prepare the Toon**  
   Wash the toon leaves thoroughly, drain well, and arrange into small bundles. Set aside.

4. **Coat and Deep Fry**  
   Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 160–180°C (320–356°F).  
   Evenly coat each toon bundle with the batter, then place into the hot oil and fry until the coating turns golden and crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.

5. **Plate the Dish**  
   Arrange the fried toon bundles on a plate. Serve with the homemade Sichuan pepper salt. The exterior is crispy, the interior is tender, and the dish is filled with the aroma of spring.

## Health Advice  
Toon leaves are rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, but deep frying increases the calorie content. It is recommended to pair this dish with light vegetables to balance the meal. Suitable for a springtime taste, but those with a weak spleen or cold stomach should not consume too much.
