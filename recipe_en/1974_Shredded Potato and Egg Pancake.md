# Shredded Potato and Egg Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato – appropriate amount (cut into thin shreds)
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (cut into thin shreds)
🥚 Eggs – 4 (2 mixed into the batter, 2 added at the end)
🌾 Flour – about 1 bowl
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (chopped)
⚫️ Black sesame seeds – appropriate amount
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon – appropriate amount
🌿 Thirteen Spice – appropriate amount
🍅 Ketchup – appropriate amount (for final decoration)
🥗 Mayonnaise – appropriate amount (for final decoration)

## Cookware and Equipment
🫗 Large bowl (for mixing)
🍳 Non-stick pan
🫖 Lid
🥄 Spatula

## Steps
1. Place the shredded potato and shredded carrot into a large bowl.
   **Note**: The shreds should be cut evenly in thickness to ensure even cooking.
2. Beat in 2 eggs, add salt, chicken bouillon, and Thirteen Spice. Mix well with your hands to fully incorporate the seasonings.
3. Add about 1 bowl of flour and continue mixing with your hands until all the vegetable shreds are evenly coated with the batter and can stick together into clumps.
4. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Take an appropriate amount of the vegetable batter and shape it into a ring with a hollow center directly in the pan.
   **Key Point**: The ring must be pressed firmly to prevent it from falling apart during frying; the oil temperature should not be too high, use medium-low heat.
5. Crack a whole egg into the hollow center of each ring, keeping the yolk intact.
6. Sprinkle chopped scallion and black sesame seeds over the egg.
7. Cover the pan with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and cook slowly for about 3 minutes, using the steam to help the top layer of the egg set faster.
8. Once the bottom is set and golden brown, carefully flip the pancake with a spatula. Continue cooking covered on low heat for about 3 minutes, until both sides are golden brown and the egg inside is fully set.
   **Note**: Flip gently to prevent the egg from spilling out or the pancake from falling apart.
9. Once cooked, remove from the pan. Squeeze ketchup and mayonnaise over the top according to your preference and serve.

## Health Advice
This shredded potato and egg pancake is rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it suitable for breakfast or post-workout energy replenishment. Shred the potato thinly and fry over low heat with minimal oil to reduce calories.
