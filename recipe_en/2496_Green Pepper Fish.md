# Green Pepper Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 grass carp (about 2-3 jin, cleaned and gutted, bones cut into pieces, fillet sliced into evenly thick pieces)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of green bird's eye chili (sliced into rings)
🫑 Appropriate amount of regular green pepper (sliced into rings or finely chopped)
🫑 Appropriate amount of pickled green pepper (finely chopped after slicing)
🌿 A small pinch of dried green Sichuan peppercorns (about 5g)
🌿 A small handful of fresh green Sichuan peppercorns (about 10g)
🧄 Appropriate amount of ginger (some minced, some used for ginger and scallion water)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (minced)
🥔 A bit of sweet potato starch (about 10g, for coating)
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt (used in batches)
🍶 Appropriate amount of cooking wine (for marinating to remove fishy smell)
🧂 Appropriate amount of MSG
🧂 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper
🫒 One spoonful of rapeseed oil (needs to be cooked in advance)
🥓 A lump of lard (about 20g)
💧 Appropriate amount of water or stock (enough to submerge ingredients)
🧅 Appropriate amount of ginger and scallion water (added in small batches for fragrance and removing fishy smell)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🥄 Ladle
🍳 Wok
🥢 Strainer
🥣 Large bowl/container
🫙 Small bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Slice the bird's eye chili and regular green pepper into rings, finely chop the pickled green pepper, and mince the ginger and garlic. Clean the grass carp by removing scales and fins. Split the fish from the back to separate meat from bones. Angle the knife to slice the fish fillet into evenly thin pieces. Chop the fish bones into pieces.
2. Add sufficient salt and cooking wine to both the fish bones and fillets, mix well, and marinate for a few minutes. Rinse repeatedly with plenty of clean water until the water runs clear and the fillets turn white. Then squeeze out excess water.
3. Add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, and white pepper to the fish fillets. Add ginger and scallion water in small batches, stirring constantly in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky and binds. Add sweet potato starch and mix evenly. Finally, drizzle a little cooking oil to lock in moisture.
**Note**: Use starch sparingly; always stir in the same direction.
4. Heat the wok, add rapeseed oil and lard. First, fry the dried green Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Then add the minced ginger and garlic, chopped pickled green pepper, green pepper rings, and fresh green Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry over low heat until aromatic.
5. Add the fish bones and stir-fry until the surface changes color. Add water or stock. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Season with salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, and white pepper. Cook until the fish bones are done. Remove and arrange them at the bottom of a bowl.
6. Keep the fish stock in the wok boiling, then reduce to low heat so the surface just simmers. Gently slide the fish fillets into the stock one by one along the edge of the wok. Lightly push them with the back of a ladle. Cook until about 80% done, then remove and arrange them on top of the fish bones in the bowl.
7. Bring the remaining stock in the wok to a boil again. Slowly pour it into the bowl.
8. In a separate wok, heat oil. Add dried green Sichuan peppercorns, fresh green Sichuan peppercorns, and green bird's eye chili rings. Quickly fry until fragrant. Pour the oil and seasonings over the fish fillets in the bowl. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish features fish, which is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with green peppers and Sichuan peppercorns, it can help boost metabolism. It is suitable for those on a diet or needing to warm the body. Note that when poaching the fish fillets, the heat should be gentle to avoid overcooking and affecting the texture.
