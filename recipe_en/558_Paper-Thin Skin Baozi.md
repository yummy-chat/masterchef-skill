# Paper-Thin Skin Baozi
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 All-purpose flour 250g
🌾 Wheat starch 25g (increases skin transparency; substitute with potato starch if unavailable)
🍜 Dried vermicelli noodles 70g
🥬 Cabbage 300g
🥕 Carrot 250g
🌿 Chinese chives 100g
🥚 Eggs 3
🧅 Scallion approx. 15g (for making seasoned oil)
🫚 Ginger approx. 10g (for making seasoned oil)
🍃 Bay leaves 2
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns about 10 pieces
🌿 Cumin seeds 1 small pinch
⭐ Star anise 2 (small ones)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (includes oil for seasoned oil, dough, and scrambling eggs)
💧 Cold water 80g
💧 Boiling water 80g
🌾 Dry flour as needed (to prevent sticking)
🧂 Salt 8g for pickling vegetables, approx. 2/3 tsp for filling
🌶️ White pepper 0.5g
🍬 Sugar 3g
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g
🥄 Dark soy sauce 10g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g
🧂 MSG 3g
🫒 Sesame oil 5g
🛢️ Seasoned oil slightly more than usual cooking oil (made by frying scallion, ginger, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, star anise; strain and reserve oil)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl/metal basin
🍳 Wok
♨️ Steamer/steamer basket
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🥬 Grater
🥖 Rolling pin
🍵 Small bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🛍️ Plastic bag
⚖️ Kitchen scale (optional)

## Steps
1.  Soak dried vermicelli noodles in boiling water until softened. Drain and chop into small pieces.
**Key point**: Soften in boiling water only, do not cook further to maintain texture.

2.  Finely chop Chinese chives and set aside in a small bowl. Do not mix with other ingredients yet to prevent flavor transfer.

3.  Shred cabbage and grate carrot into a large bowl. Add 8g of salt, mix well, and let sit for 10 minutes until vegetables soften and reduce in volume.

4.  Add cooking oil to a wok. Place sliced ginger, scallion segments, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, and star anise in cold oil. Heat over medium heat until oil is hot, then reduce to medium-low and fry slowly until spices turn golden and crackle. Turn off heat, strain out solids, and reserve the seasoned oil.
**Key point**: Do not burn the spices.

5.  Using the same wok, add a little more oil and scramble 3 eggs over medium-low heat, stirring constantly with chopsticks until broken into fine crumbs. Set aside to cool.

6.  Rinse pickled vegetables under cold water to remove excess salt, squeeze out water thoroughly, and chop finely.

7.  Combine chopped vegetables, vermicelli, and cooled scrambled eggs in a large bowl. Add white pepper, sugar, salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, MSG, and the reserved seasoned oil. Mix thoroughly.

8.  Make the dough: Divide 250g flour into two portions. Mix the smaller portion with 25g wheat starch. Pour 80g boiling water directly over the starch and stir quickly to cook. Add 80g cold water and mix into a shaggy dough. Add 10g cooking oil and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and rest for 5 minutes, then knead again until smooth.
**Key point**: Wheat starch must be cooked with boiling water; oil keeps the dough moist and prevents cracking.

9.  While the dough rests, add the reserved Chinese chives to the filling. Drizzle with 5g sesame oil and mix again.
**Key point**: Add chives last; sesame oil locks in moisture and preserves freshness.

10. Dust a work surface with dry flour to prevent sticking. Roll the dough into a log and cut into about 15 equal pieces. Flatten each piece and roll into a round wrapper slightly thicker in the center and thinner at the edges, until translucent enough to see the surface pattern underneath.
**Key point**: Wrappers should be thin yet elastic; leave a wider edge for pleating.

11. Place a wrapper in your palm and add about 70g of filling. Pleat and pinch the edges to seal into a bun shape. If filling is loose, press gently with your thumb.

12. Bring water in a steamer to a boil. Line the steamer basket and arrange the buns. Cover and steam over high heat. Once steam appears, continue steaming for 9 minutes.

13. Turn off the heat immediately. Do not remove the lid or take out the buns right away. Let them cool slightly in the steamer; the skin will become more translucent and chewy. Then serve.

## Health Advice
Paper-thin skin baozi are filled with a variety of vegetables, have thin skins and generous fillings, and offer balanced nutrition. They are suitable for people on a diet or those needing more dietary fiber. Adding chives at the end locks in freshness and prevents discoloration during steaming.
