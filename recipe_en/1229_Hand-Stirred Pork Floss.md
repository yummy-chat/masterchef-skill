# Hand-Stirred Pork Floss
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin, about 1532g (remove sinew and fat, cut into large pieces)  
🫚 Ginger,适量 (sliced)  
🧂 Table salt, 1 spoon  
🍬 White sugar, 2 spoons  
🥄 Light soy sauce, about 5 circles (divided into two uses)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Long chopsticks (two pairs)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove all sinew and fat from the pork tenderloin, then cut into large pieces along the grain.
2. Place the meat pieces in a pressure cooker, add ginger slices, 1 spoon of salt, 2 spoons of white sugar, and about 3 circles of light soy sauce. Pour in enough water to reach halfway up the meat pieces. Cover and cook for about 30 minutes.
3. After cooking, remove and discard the ginger slices. Pour the meat pieces and broth into a wok. Use a spatula to break the meat into shreds.
4. Boil over high heat for about 5–8 minutes, until the broth is mostly absorbed and the meat shreds have fully absorbed the liquid.
5. Reduce to medium heat. Switch to using two pairs of long chopsticks to quickly stir and toss the mixture. During this step, add about 2 circles of light soy sauce for color and flavor adjustment.
6. Once the meat shreds are noticeably drier, reduce to the lowest heat. Continue stirring for about 40 minutes, until the meat shreds are extremely dry, fluffy, and resembling cotton. Remove from heat.
   **Key point**: Stir continuously and patiently throughout the entire process to prevent sticking and burning; you can use your fingers to pinch and test for dryness.

## Health Advice  
This homemade pork floss is rich in protein, suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing an energy boost, but be mindful of controlling sugar and sodium intake. It is recommended to pair with whole wheat bread or porridge, and avoid excessive consumption as a snack.
