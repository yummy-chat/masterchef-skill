# Potsticker Skin Vegetable Rolls
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥟 Potsticker skins, as needed (processed in groups of 5 per set)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🌿 Chinese chives, as needed (chopped)
🥕 Carrot, as needed (diced)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, as needed (diced)
🦐 Small dried shrimp, as needed (dried seafood, for umami)
🫗 Cooking oil, as needed (for coating skins, mixing filling, and frying)
🧂 Salt, as needed (only for scrambling eggs)
🍶 Vinegar, as needed (added to egg mixture)
🫙 Seasoning oil, as needed (mixed into filling before other seasonings)
🍬 Sugar, as needed
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Small metal bowl
🥢 Rolling pin
🥘 Steamer (with steamer rack and liner)
🍳 Wok
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare potsticker skins**: Take potsticker skins, evenly brush each one with oil, stack 5 skins per set, lightly press together with your palm, then roll out with a rolling pin into a thin disc about the thickness of a coin.
**Note**: Each skin must be sufficiently coated with oil to prevent sticking later; maintain even thickness while rolling.

2. **Steam the skins**: Line the steamer rack with a liner, place the rolled-out skins on it, cover with the lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 7 minutes. Remove and let cool naturally.
**Note**: The steamed skins must cool completely, otherwise they may break or stick when wrapping.

3. **Scramble the eggs**: Beat eggs in a bowl, add salt and a little vinegar, and whisk with chopsticks. Heat a wok over low heat, pour in the egg mixture, and quickly stir with chopsticks to break into small, fine pieces. Remove and let cool.
**Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high; stir immediately and quickly after adding eggs to form even, small pieces. Vinegar helps remove the eggy smell and makes the eggs fluffier.

4. **Prepare the filling**: In a large bowl, add the chopped chives, diced carrot, and diced wood ear mushrooms. First, drizzle in seasoning oil and mix thoroughly, then add the cooled scrambled eggs, along with sugar, thirteen-spice powder, and small dried shrimp. Mix evenly.
**Note**: Use oil first to seal in the moisture from the vegetables and prevent them from releasing water. The eggs already contain salt, so no additional salt is needed in the filling to avoid being too salty or causing the chives to release water.

5. **Assemble the rolls**: Carefully peel the steamed skins apart one by one. Lay one skin flat, place an appropriate amount of filling on it, fold the left and right sides inward, then roll tightly from one end. Place with the seam side down.
**Note**: Press the seams firmly to prevent the filling from leaking out during frying.

6. **Fry the rolls**: Spray or brush a little oil in a frying pan, place the rolls in the pan, and fry over low heat. Turn frequently until both sides are evenly golden brown. Remove from the pan.
**Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to allow the outer skin to become crispy slowly and the inside to heat through thoroughly. Aim for a golden color and avoid burning.

## Health Tips
This dish cleverly uses potsticker skins as wrappers, making it low in fat and carbs while rich in vegetable fiber, perfect for satisfying cravings during weight loss. Small dried shrimp and eggs provide high-quality protein, while chives and wood ear mushrooms aid digestion. Note that no extra salt is needed in the filling to avoid excessive sodium. It is best enjoyed hot, as the skin will become tough when cooled.
