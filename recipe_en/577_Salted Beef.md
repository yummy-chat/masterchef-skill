# Salted Beef

Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef shank 2 whole pieces (fresh)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 40g (for curing)  
🧂 Salt 300g (for curing) + 30g (for the brine)  
🍬 Rock sugar 5g  
🌿 Spice packet (Sichuan peppercorns 4g, star anise 7g, cinnamon 7g, bay leaves 2g, 1 cardamom, cloves 3 pieces, angelica root 2g, dried ginger 5g, hawthorn 3g, galangal 4g, sand ginger 4g, fennel seeds 10g)  
🍷 Red yeast rice appropriate amount (pack separately)  
🧅 Fried shallot and ginger oil 200g  
🍶 Baijiu (high-proof liquor) appropriate amount  
🫗 Braising oil (oil from the surface of the broth) appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for toasting salt and peppercorns)  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🛍️ Spice bag or muslin bag  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧤 Gloves  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Soak the beef shank in lightly salted water for 3 hours to initially remove blood.
2.  Toast the seasoned salt: Place a dry, oil-free flat-bottomed pan over low heat. Add 300g salt and 40g Sichuan peppercorns. Toast, stirring frequently, until the salt grains turn slightly yellow and a fragrant aroma is released. Remove from heat, stir quickly to cool, and spread out to cool completely.
3.  Evenly rub the cooled seasoned salt all over the surface of the beef. Apply firm pressure to ensure the salt mixture adheres completely.
4.  Curing: Sprinkle a layer of seasoned salt onto plastic wrap. Place the beef on top, then sprinkle another layer of salted salt over it. Wrap the beef tightly with the plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours to allow the savory saltiness to penetrate the meat.
5.  Place the spices into a gauze bag and tie it tightly. Soak in water, then rinse. Pack the red yeast rice in a separate bag.
6.  Prepare the brine: Pour beef bone broth into a pot and bring to a boil. Add the spice bag, red yeast rice bag, and the fried shallot and ginger oil. Reduce heat to a low simmer and cook until the broth turns a vibrant red color and the spice aroma is released. Remove the red yeast rice bag. Add 30g salt and the rock sugar, stirring until dissolved.
7.  Remove the cured beef. Shake off excess salt grains and Sichuan peppercorns from the surface.
8.  Place the beef into the prepared brine. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of baijiu.
9.  Cover the pressure cooker. Once pressure is reached, cook over medium heat for 25 minutes. Then, turn off the heat. Without opening the lid, let the beef sit in the residual heat for 45 minutes.
10. Open the lid. Use a slotted spoon to remove the beef. Briefly dip it in and out of the brine a few times.
11. While still hot, drizzle the beef with braising oil and mix well. Use plastic wrap to tightly roll the beef into a log shape to set. Refrigerate until completely cold.
12. Remove from refrigerator, discard the plastic wrap, slice the beef, and arrange on a plate.

## Health Advice  
This salted beef is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness and weight loss goals. However, due to the high salt content from the curing process, it is recommended to serve with light, simple vegetables.
