## Cooking Steps
1. Finely shred the cabbage, then rinse together with the bean sprouts under clean water. Drain thoroughly, squeezing out as much water as possible.  
   **Note**: Vegetables must be thoroughly drained, otherwise excess water will be released during cooking, affecting the dry and chewy texture of the shredded flatbread.  
2. Add salt and Shaoxing wine to the eggs, then beat until well combined. The Shaoxing wine helps make the scrambled eggs more tender and smooth.  
3. Heat cooking oil in a frying pan, pour in the egg mixture, and stir quickly over medium-high heat until it forms fluffy, light yellow curds. Remove and set aside.  
4. If needed, add a little more oil to the pan. Add the drained cabbage shreds and bean sprouts, stirring rapidly over high heat until just wilted. Then add the minced scallions, ginger, and half of the minced garlic, stirring until fragrant.  
5. Spread the cooked vegetables evenly over the bottom of the pan. Place the shredded flatbread on top in an even layer. Drizzle with light soy sauce and dark soy sauce. **Do not stir at this stage.**  
6. Cover the pan with a lid and cook over medium-low heat for about 1 minute.  
   **Key Point**: Steaming the flatbread prevents it from becoming sticky and gluey when stirred directly, keeping it pleasantly dry and chewy.  
7. Remove the lid, add the reserved scrambled eggs, then drizzle in the Sichuan pepper oil, the remaining minced garlic, and the vinegar. Stir quickly and thoroughly until the shredded flatbread is evenly colored and all ingredients are well combined, then serve immediately.  
   **Note**: Adding the garlic and vinegar at the very end preserves their maximum aroma and bright, tangy depth.

## Health Notes
This vegetarian stir-fried flatbread features cabbage and bean sprouts as its base, making it light and low in fat—ideal for those seeking to control calories while still getting carbohydrates. The flatbread remains dry and non-sticky thanks to the brief steaming step. To maintain the desired texture, ensure the vegetables are thoroughly drained before cooking. The final addition of minced garlic and vinegar enhances flavor while keeping the dish balanced and not greasy.
