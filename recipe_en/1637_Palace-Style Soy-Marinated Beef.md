# Palace-Style Soy-Marinated Beef
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef shank 2 large pieces
🍲 Chicken broth enough to cover the beef
⭐ Star anise appropriate amount
🌿 Dahurian angelica root appropriate amount
🌸 Cloves appropriate amount
🌿 Cumin appropriate amount
🪵 Cinnamon appropriate amount
🫚 Kaempferia galanga appropriate amount
🫘 Cardamom appropriate amount
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
🌿 Amomum villosum appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🍬 Rock sugar appropriate amount

🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧴 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🧴 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Yellow wine approximately 1 bottle
🌶️ White pepper powder appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure cooker
🔪 Cutting board
🪡 Bamboo skewer/needle
🪄 Rolling pin
🧺 Spice bag/cheesecloth
🥄 Skimmer
🍽️ Assorted bowls and plates

## Preparation Steps
1. **Dry-fry spices to make the cure**: Cumin, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns into a dry, oil-free wok. Dry-fry over low heat until the color darkens and aromas release. Pour out and let cool, then crush with a rolling pin. Mix with salt to make "Sichuan pepper salt."
- Key point: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning and bitterness.

2. **Prepare the beef**: Soak the beef shank in cold water for 1-3 hours to remove blood. Remove and use a bamboo skewer to poke dense holes all over the meat.
- Key point: Poking holes helps with later marinating, and the connective tissue contracts to expel impurities during blanching.

3. **Marinate**: Evenly rub the Sichuan pepper salt all over the beef. Massage and marinate for about 1 hour.
4. **Fry scallion, ginger, and caramel color**: Add oil to the wok, add rock sugar, and slowly cook over low heat until the sugar melts, turns dark brown, and emits blue smoke. Quickly add the scallion sections and ginger slices, stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in an appropriate amount of warm water, stir to combine, and set aside.
- Key point: The moment the caramel emits blue smoke is the perfect time to add the scallion and ginger. Be careful of oil splattering when adding water.

5. **Rinse off the marinade**: Rinse the marinated beef with clean water to remove surface spice residue. Drain well.
6. **Blanch to remove gamey odor**: Place the beef in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat. Use a skimmer to continuously skim off the foam until the broth becomes clear, then remove the beef.
- Key point: Starting with cold water and constantly skimming foam is crucial for a clear and pure broth.

7. **Combine in the pot and season**: Place the blanched beef into the pressure cooker. Pour in enough chicken broth to cover. Put the remaining spices into a spice bag and add to the pot. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, yellow wine, white pepper powder, scallion sections, ginger slices, the prepared scallion, ginger, and caramel color mixture, and an appropriate amount of salt.
8. **Pressure cook**: Cover the lid. Once steam is released, cook under pressure for 40 minutes, then turn off the heat.
9. **Steep for flavor**: Do not open the lid. Let the beef continue to steep in the hot broth for 1 hour.
- Key point: The residual heat steeping makes the meat more tender and allows the flavors to fully penetrate.

10. **Finish**: Remove the beef once a chopstick can easily pierce through it. Slice and serve on a plate.

## Health Advice
Beef is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for those building muscle or needing to replenish blood and energy. However, as pressure cooking can result in higher sodium content, it is recommended that those with high blood pressure consume it in moderation. Remember to pair it with vegetables to balance nutrition and avoid eating it on an empty stomach.
