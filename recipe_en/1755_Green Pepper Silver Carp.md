# Green Pepper Silver Carp
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 silver carp (about 5-7 jin, scaled and gutted, separate the head, bones, and meat)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of Erjingtiao green peppers (some cut into rings and minced, some cut into sections)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of pickled wild peppers (minced)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (minced or finely chopped)
🫚 Appropriate amount of pickled ginger (minced or finely chopped)
🫚 Appropriate amount of old ginger (minced or finely chopped)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallions (minced or finely chopped)
🥚 1 egg (use the egg white)
🧂 Appropriate amount of sweet potato starch (for coating)
🫘 Appropriate amount of dried vermicelli (pre-soaked until soft, optional)
🧈 Appropriate amount of lard (one of the mixed oils for stir-frying the base)
🫒 Appropriate amount of vegetable oil (for stir-frying the base and final oil drizzling)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of green Sichuan peppercorns (used twice)
🧂 A large amount of table salt (for deodorizing the fish) + a small amount (for marinating)
🧂 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon
🧂 Appropriate amount of MSG
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper powder (can partially substitute with whole peppercorns)
🍬 A pinch of sugar (to enhance freshness)
🫗 Appropriate amount of stock (can substitute with water)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok (frying pan)
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Ladle (for stirring and scooping)
💧 Sink (for washing)
🔥 Stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Whole fish preparation**: Clean the silver carp, scrape off the black membrane inside the belly, separate the head, bones, and the meat. Split the head in half, chop the bones into pieces, and set aside the meat for slicing.
**Note**: The black membrane must be completely removed.

2. **Tenderizing the fish**: Place the fish bones and pieces in a basin, add enough salt, mix well, and marinate for 2 minutes. Then rinse thoroughly under running water until no saltiness remains.
**Note**: Use a generous amount of salt and rinse thoroughly. This method removes the fishy smell and makes the meat tender.

3. **Slicing the fish**: Lay the boneless fish meat flat. Hold the knife at a 45-degree angle and slice into slightly thick pieces using a push-pull motion.
**Note**: Slightly thicker slices prevent the fish from falling apart during cooking. For larger slices, tilt the knife more.

4. **Marinating the fish slices**: Rinse the fish slices, squeeze out excess water. Add a small amount of salt, chicken bouillon, MSG, and white pepper powder. Mix until sticky. Then add the egg white and mix until fully absorbed. Finally, add sweet potato starch and mix evenly.
**Note**: Must squeeze out water thoroughly. The order of seasonings, egg white, and starch is crucial; mix until sticky.

5. **Preparing the ingredients**: Mince or finely chop the pickled ginger, old ginger, garlic, and scallions; mix and set aside. Take a portion of the green peppers, cut into rings and mince, then mix with the ginger-garlic paste and the minced pickled wild peppers. Cut the remaining green peppers into sections and set aside separately for the final aroma infusion. If using vermicelli, pre-soak until soft.
**Note**: The two forms of green peppers must be prepared and kept separate.

6. **Stir-frying the base**: Add mixed oil to the wok. When the oil is hot, first fry the green Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Then add the mixed ginger, garlic, and green pepper paste. Keep stir-frying over medium-low heat until the aroma is fully released.
**Note**: Fry the Sichuan peppercorns first, then add the paste.

7. **Frying the fish bones**: Once the base is fragrant, add the drained fish bones and stir-fry until the surface changes color.
*If desired, splash a small amount of cooking wine along the wok edge to reduce any fishy smell.*

8. **Making the soup**: Add enough stock and bring to a boil over high heat. Add the pre-soaked vermicelli. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, and a pinch of sugar. Keep boiling for a few minutes until the flavors from the bones and seasonings infuse into the soup and the soup turns slightly milky white.
9. **Plating the base**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the fish bones, vermicelli, and most of the solid seasonings. Place them at the bottom of the serving bowl. Keep only the soup in the wok.
10. **Poaching the fish slices**: Bring the soup in the wok to a gentle simmer. Add the fish slices one by one, spreading them out. Gently push them with the back of a ladle to prevent sticking. Cook until the fish slices just turn white, then immediately turn off the heat.
**Note**: Must add slices one by one. Poach briefly at a gentle simmer; do not overcook.

11. **Plating and drizzling with oil**: Pour the fish slices and soup into the bowl over the base. Sprinkle the reserved green pepper sections and another handful of green Sichuan peppercorns on top. In a separate pan, heat vegetable oil until smoking, then quickly pour it over the green peppers and Sichuan peppercorns to release their aroma.
**Note**: The hot oil must be smoking hot to fully release the fresh and numbing flavors.

## Health Advice
This dish features tender fish and the refreshing aroma of green peppers, but the amount of oil and salt is not low. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or multigrain rice to balance the meal. Silver carp is rich in high-quality protein and DHA, making it suitable for mental workers and those needing nutritional supplements. However, people with high blood pressure should limit their intake of the soup base.
