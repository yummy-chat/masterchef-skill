# Pan-Seared Argentine Red Shrimp
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍤 Argentine red shrimp 2-3 pieces  
🌿 Cilantro 3 sprigs  
🍋 Lemon 1 slice  
🧅 Onion 10g  
🧄 Ginger 10g  
🧄 Garlic 10g  
🧂 Sea salt (ground) 5g  
🌶️ Black pepper (ground) to taste  
🥂 White wine 20ml  
🧂 Soy sauce 1ml  
🫒 Olive oil 2 tablespoons  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🧻 Paper towels  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove Argentine red shrimp from the freezer 1 day in advance, transfer to the refrigerator for natural thawing; if the back has been cut and the vein removed, it can be used directly.
2. Rinse the thawed red shrimp with clean water, thoroughly pat dry the surface moisture with paper towels.
3. Slice the ginger, cut the onion into small cubes, separate the cilantro leaves and stems then chop the leaves, crush the garlic and chop into fine pieces.
4. Heat a non-stick pan over high heat, pour in 2 tablespoons of olive oil, once the oil is hot, add the ginger slices, onion cubes, and cilantro stems, stir-fry for about 1 minute.
5. Remove and discard the ginger, onion, and cilantro stems from the pan.
6. Turn to medium-high heat, place the red shrimp in the pan, ensuring each shrimp's one side is fully in contact with the pan, sear for about 2 minutes, while brushing a layer of oil on the surface of each shrimp.
7. When the bottom shell turns slightly golden brown, flip the shrimp, evenly sprinkle the chopped garlic, gently shake the pan to ensure even heating.
8. After searing for about 1 minute, pour 20ml of white wine along the edge of the pan.
9. Continue searing for another 1 minute, then turn to medium-low heat, evenly sprinkle sea salt and black pepper to season.
10. Add one drop of soy sauce to each shrimp to enhance the flavor.
11. Garnish with chopped cilantro leaves, remove from pan and plate.
12. Slice the lemon and arrange it on the side of the plate.

## Notes
- Thawing must be done naturally in the refrigerator to avoid affecting the texture with running water thawing.
- The shrimp must be thoroughly dried before going into the pan, otherwise, oil will splatter and affect the searing of a crispy shell.
- Add the garlic promptly after flipping to prevent burning.
- Adjust the lemon quantity according to personal taste; use less or none if you prefer less acidity.
- Serve hot for the best texture.

## Health Tips
Argentine red shrimp is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, suitable for fitness or weight management individuals, but should be paired with vegetables for balanced nutrition. The frying oil temperature is relatively high, so control the oil amount to avoid excessive calorie intake.
