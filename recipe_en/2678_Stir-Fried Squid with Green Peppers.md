# Stir-Fried Squid with Green Peppers
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦑 Small squid 3-4 pieces (scored with a crisscross pattern)  
🦑 Large squid about 2 pieces (cut into pieces)  
🌶️ Green bell pepper 2 (seeded and cut into pieces)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper 3 (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Young ginger a small piece (about 30g, sliced)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (sliced)  
🧅 Scallions适量 (cut into sections)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers a few (for fragrance)  
🧂 Salt适量 (for blanching)  
🫒 Cooking oil适量  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) one tablespoon  
🍬 White sugar适量  
🍾 Cooking wine a little  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chinese chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer/skimmer  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the squid**: Clean the small squid by removing the innards, eyes, and cartilage, and peel off the black membrane. Make diagonal cuts at a 2/3 depth, then turn the knife and make crosswise cuts to create a crisscross pattern, and finally cut into pieces. Clean the large squid in the same way and cut into pieces. Set the tentacles aside separately.  
   **Key point**: Control the depth of the cuts to 2/3 to avoid cutting through. When making the cuts, move your hand back steadily and work quickly.
2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Seed and cut the green and red bell peppers into pieces. Slice the young ginger and garlic, and cut the scallions into sections.
3. **Blanch**: Fill the wok with enough water, add a little salt and a few drops of cooking oil. After the water boils, first blanch the green and red bell pepper pieces until just cooked, then remove and drain. Next, add the squid pieces and tentacles and blanch for about 5 seconds. Remove immediately once the squid curls up.  
   **Key point**: The blanching time for the squid must be very short; remove as soon as it curls, otherwise, the texture will become tough.
4. **Stir-fry aromatics**: Heat oil in another wok, add the scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic slices, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the doubanjiang and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the red oil is released.  
   **Note**: When stir-frying the sauce, keep the heat at medium-low to avoid burning and making the sauce bitter.
5. **Combine over high heat**: Add the blanched green and red bell pepper pieces and squid to the wok. Add a little white sugar and cooking wine. Quickly turn the heat to high and stir-fry vigorously so that all ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce. Remove from the wok immediately.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry over high heat the entire time to prevent the squid from overcooking and maintain a crisp and tender texture.

## Health Tips  
Squid is rich in high-quality protein, and green bell peppers provide vitamin C, making this dish suitable for supplementing nutrition during weight loss. However, squid has a high cholesterol content, so people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
