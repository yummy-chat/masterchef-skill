# Minced Pork Scallion Rolls  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Flour, as needed (high-gluten or all-purpose)  
🍄 Dry yeast, as needed  
🥩 Ground pork, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)  
💧 Warm water, as needed (for kneading dough)  

🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (for seasoning meat filling)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (for seasoning meat filling, for color)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed (for seasoning meat filling, to enhance flavor)  
🌿 Chinese thirteen-spice powder, as needed (for seasoning meat filling and making chili oil)  
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed (for making chili oil)  
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed (for making chili oil)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for making chili oil)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for making chili oil)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading dough and mixing filling)  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥢 Chopsticks (one pair, to assist in cutting dough rolls)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and spreading filling)  
🍳 Wok or saucepan (for heating oil)  
♨️ Steamer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Knead dough: Mix flour with yeast, add warm water and stir into flocculent pieces, knead into a smooth dough. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap, and let it rise until doubled in volume.  
   **Note**: Knead the risen dough repeatedly to expel air.

2. Prepare meat filling: Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and Chinese thirteen-spice powder to the ground pork, and mix well.

3. Prepare chili oil: In a bowl, combine chili flakes, white sesame seeds, Chinese thirteen-spice powder, and salt. Pour heated cooking oil (until smoking) into the bowl, and stir well to form chili oil mixture.

4. Roll out dough and assemble: Roll the risen and degassed dough into a large, evenly thin sheet. First, spread a layer of the prepared chili oil mixture evenly over the surface, then spread a layer of meat filling evenly, and finally sprinkle scallions on top.

5. Roll and shape: Starting from one side, roll the filled dough sheet into a long log. Place one chopstick on each side of the log, then cut into connected strips with a knife. Divide into smaller segments. Take one segment, hold both ends with hands, fold inward to bring them together, and pinch them firmly to form a scallion roll blank with cut sides facing up.

6. Second proofing: Place the blanks in the steamer with cold water, cover with a lid, and let them proof for 5 minutes.

7. Steam: Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue steaming for 15 minutes.  
   **Note**: After steaming, the rolls will have visibly increased in volume with clear layers.

8. Remove from steamer and serve. The rolls can be torn apart layer by layer; the dough is soft, with a spicy and flavorful taste.

## Health Advice  
Minced pork scallion rolls use fermented dough paired with meat filling and chili oil, offering a relatively balanced nutrition profile, suitable as a staple or snack. However, note that they are relatively high in calories, so it is advisable to pair them with light vegetables. Control the proofing and steaming times appropriately to ensure a soft and fluffy texture.
