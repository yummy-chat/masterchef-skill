# Ganba菌 Fried Rice
Cuisine: Yunnan cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍄 Ganba fungus (a Yunnan specialty, to be washed and finely chopped)
🍚 Rice (preferably leftover rice or rice with less moisture)
🥚 Eggs, 2-3 (beaten, partly used to coat the rice)
🍖 Yunnan ham, cubed
🥜 Cooking oil, as needed
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥄 Light soy sauce (just a little for color adjustment, not too much)

## Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula

## Steps
1. Mix the rice with some of the beaten egg until each grain is coated.
2. Heat the wok with cold oil, then add the Ganba fungus and diced ham, stir-frying until fragrant.
3. Add the egg-coated rice and stir-fry quickly until the grains are separate, dry, and fluffy.
   **Key point**: High heat and fast action are essential to bring out the aroma of the ingredients.
4. Season with salt.
   **Key point**: To highlight the natural umami of the Ganba fungus and Yunnan ham, use little or no soy sauce to keep the fried rice light in color.
5. Just before removing from heat, add "guo bian cong" (scallions stir-fried at the edge of the wok). Stir well and serve.

## Health Tips
Ganba fungus is rich in dietary fiber and mushroom polysaccharides. Yunnan ham provides quality protein and flavor but is high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should use less ham. Using leftover rice reduces starch retrogradation, and pairing with eggs balances nutrition.
