# Homestyle Stir-Fried Lamb with Homemade Nut Dipping Powder
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb  about 300g (belly or front leg meat, cut into pieces)  
🥜 Cashews  appropriate amount  
🥜 Pistachios  appropriate amount  
🥜 Pine nuts  appropriate amount  
🥜 Macadamia nuts  appropriate amount  
🥜 Almonds  appropriate amount  
🥜 Pecans  appropriate amount  
🧅 Onion  appropriate amount (sliced)  
🫑 Bell peppers (red, yellow, green)  appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🧂 Salt  appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili powder  appropriate amount  
🧂 Cumin powder  appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce  a small amount  
🧈 Sesame paste  appropriate amount (can be mixed with peanut butter, ratio about 4:1)  
🥜 Peanut butter  a small amount  
🫒 Cooking oil  appropriate amount (for marinating, frying, and stir-frying)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Glass square bowl  
⚙️ Food processor (high-speed blender)  
🍳 Wok (iron pan)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🪵 Wooden spatula  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Instructions
1. **Marinate the lamb**: Place the cut lamb pieces into a bowl. Add salt, chili powder, cumin powder, a small amount of light soy sauce, and sesame paste. Use your hands to mix and massage thoroughly. Finally, drizzle in cooking oil and mix again. Let it sit for 10 minutes.  
**Key point**: The combination of sesame paste and peanut butter enhances the aroma best. Adding oil locks in moisture, making the meat tenderer and preventing sticking in the pan.

2. **Make the nut dipping powder**: Place cooked cashews, pistachios, pine nuts, macadamia nuts, almonds, and pecans into the food processor. Add chili powder, cumin powder, and a small amount of salt. Pulse until ground into a coarse powder, retaining some graininess.  
**Note**: Do not grind the nuts too fine; retaining some texture creates a richer mouthfeel.

3. **First fry the lamb**: Heat oil in the wok to 50% hot. Add the marinated lamb and fry over high heat until the pieces are fully set and the surface changes color. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain the oil.  
**Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high; avoid the outside being burnt while the inside remains raw.

4. **Second fry the lamb**: Raise the oil temperature to 60% hot. Add the first-fried lamb back in. Fry quickly over high heat until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.  
**Key point**: The second fry is crucial for achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior.

5. **Stir-fry the vegetables**: Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the sliced onion and bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry until just cooked through but still crisp.  
**Note**: Do not overcook the vegetables to maintain their sweet and crunchy texture.

6. **Combine and plate**: Return the second-fried lamb to the wok. Pour in the nut dipping powder. Stir-fry and toss quickly over high heat to evenly coat all ingredients with the powder. Remove from the wok immediately.  
**Note**: Keep the stir-frying time as short as possible to prevent the vegetables from releasing water and the nut powder from burning.

## Health Advice  
This stir-fried lamb paired with homemade nut dipping powder is rich in protein and healthy fats, making it suitable for post-workout meals or for those looking to build muscle. Note that both lamb and nuts are high in calories, so it’s best to pair this dish with refreshing vegetables or serve it as part of a main meal, avoiding excessive consumption as a snack.
