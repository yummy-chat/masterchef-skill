# Braised Eggplant and Ground Pork Noodles 🍆
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 1 round eggplant (peeled, cut into small cubes about 1 cm)
🥩 250 g ground pork
🌶️ 1 green hot pepper (remove seeds, cut into rings or strips)
🧄 2-3 stalks of garlic sprouts (separate white parts from green leaves)
🧄 4-5 cloves of garlic (minced)
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (minced)
🧅 Half a scallion (chopped)
🧂 Salt, as needed (to draw water from eggplant, for boiling noodles, and for seasoning)
🥄 About 3 tablespoons light soy sauce (to marinate ground pork) + more as needed (for stir-frying the sauce)
🍶 About 1 tablespoon yellow wine
🫗 Dark soy sauce, as needed (key seasoning)
🌿 A pinch of white pepper
🧂 A pinch of MSG or chicken bouillon (optional)
🫒 A few drops of sesame oil
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl (to salt and squeeze eggplant)
🍽️ White porcelain bowl/plate (to hold prepared ingredients)
🥄 Stainless steel slotted spoon (to scoop out ground pork and drain oil)
🍳 Wok
🍲 Soup pot (to cook noodles)
🥄 Wok spatula/soup ladle
⚡ Electric kettle (to boil water)

## Cooking Steps
1. Add salt to eggplant cubes, mix well, and let sit for 10-15 minutes. Squeeze out as much liquid as possible with your hands and set aside.
   **Note**: Squeezing out the liquid is key to preventing the eggplant from absorbing too much oil. Be thorough.
2. Cut the green hot pepper into rings. Finely chop the white parts and green leaves of the garlic sprouts separately. Mince the ginger and garlic. Chop the scallion.
3. Marinate the ground pork with 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce, mixing well to color and season.
4. Heat oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Add the ground pork and stir-fry until it changes color and becomes crumbly, with the moisture evaporated and edges slightly crispy. Splash in the yellow wine to remove any gamey smell. Remove from the wok and drain excess oil.
   **Note**: Do not let the oil get too hot, to avoid the pork being burnt on the outside but raw inside.
5. Using the oil left in the wok, stir-fry the squeezed eggplant cubes over medium-low heat. Spread them out and fry until the surface is slightly yellow, the moisture has reduced, and the eggplant releases its aroma.
6. Add the minced ginger, garlic, white parts of the garlic sprouts, and scallions. Stir-fry until fragrant. Return the ground pork to the wok and mix well. Add dark soy sauce and light soy sauce, stirring to color everything evenly.
7. Pour in enough boiling water to create a sauce. Add a pinch of white pepper and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 1 to 2 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly.
8. Just before removing from heat, add the green hot pepper rings and the green leaves of the garlic sprouts. Quickly stir-fry until just cooked. Drizzle in a few drops of sesame oil to enhance fragrance and shine.
9. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add a spoonful of salt. Cook the hand-pulled noodles until tender. When the water returns to a boil, add a splash of cold water. Cook until the noodles have no white core, then drain.
10. Ladle the braised eggplant and pork sauce over the noodles, mix well, and serve.

## Health Suggestions
This is a classic Northern Chinese home-style noodle dish. The eggplant becomes soft and tender after stir-frying, pairing beautifully with the ground pork. It is recommended to serve with a side of cucumber salad or lightly stir-fried seasonal vegetables to balance the nutrition and cut the richness. Because the eggplant is salted and squeezed, the sodium content is relatively high; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of soy sauce used.
