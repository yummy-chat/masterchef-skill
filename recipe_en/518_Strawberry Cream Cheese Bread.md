# Strawberry Cream Cheese Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (April-June)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌸 Fresh red dragon fruit juice 285g (for dough, frozen into slush in advance) + 10g (for crumble)
🍋 Lemon juice 20g (added to dragon fruit juice to prevent oxidation) + 10g (for filling)
🌾 Bread flour 400g
🧂 Salt 8g
🥛 Milk powder 12g
🍬 Fine sugar 48g (for dough) + 30g (for crumble) + 40g (for filling)
🫧 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerance) 5g
🧈 Unsalted butter 40g (for dough) + 40g (for crumble) + 150g (for filling), all softened in advance
🌾 Cake flour 40g (for crumble)
🧀 Cream cheese 80g (for filling)
🥛 Heavy cream 150g (for filling)
🍋 Lemon zest from half a lemon
🥚 Egg white as needed (for brushing surface)
🍓 Strawberries as needed (halved for decoration)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Stand mixer
🥢 Whisk
🔥 Oven
🌡️ Proofing box / thermometer
🍴 Bench scraper
📏 Rolling pin
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥛 Measuring cup
🥄 Spatula
📦 Piping bag + star tip
🖌️ Brush
🔪 Serrated knife

## Instructions
1. **Prepare dragon fruit juice**: Blend fresh red dragon fruit into a puree, mix in 20g lemon juice, and freeze into a slush consistency.
   **Note**: Adding lemon juice prevents the juice from oxidizing and discoloring, helping to maintain the color after baking; freezing into a slushy consistency helps control the dough temperature during kneading.

2. **Make pink crumble**: Mix 40g softened butter, 30g fine sugar, and 10g dragon fruit juice together. Freeze for about 30 minutes until firm. Remove and chop into small pieces with a bench scraper. Sift in 40g cake flour, rub into even sand-like crumbs with your hands, and freeze again until needed.

3. **Make lemon cream cheese filling**: Mix cream cheese and heavy cream together, soften over a water bath at about 60°C (140°F), add 40g fine sugar, and whisk until slightly thickened. Add 150g thoroughly softened butter, continue beating until smooth, then finally add lemon zest and 10g lemon juice, mixing with a spatula.
   **Key**: All ingredients must be at the same temperature and fully softened, otherwise the mixture may separate. If separation occurs, warm the mixture in a 40°C (104°F) oven before re-whisking.

4. **Knead the dough**: Combine bread flour, milk powder, and salt in the mixing bowl. Add the frozen slushy dragon fruit juice, 48g fine sugar, and yeast. Mix on low speed for 2 minutes, then switch to high speed and knead for about 10 minutes until a thick membrane forms. Add 40g softened butter, knead on low speed for 3 minutes until absorbed, then switch to medium-high speed and knead for about 2 minutes until a thin, stretchy membrane forms.
   **Note**: The final dough temperature should be around 23.5°C (74°F). The dough should feel non-sticky and have a slight droop; it is better to under-knead slightly than to over-knead.

5. **First proofing**: Round the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and proof at room temperature (not exceeding 28°C/82°F) for about 40 minutes, until doubled in size and a finger poke leaves an indentation that doesn't spring back.

6. **Divide and rest**: Gently punch down the dough to release gas. Divide into 10 equal portions, round each piece, cover with a damp cloth, and rest for about 30 minutes.

7. **Shape**: Take one piece of dough, dust the surface lightly with flour, and flatten. Roll out into a tongue shape. Flip over, fold the top third down twice, fold the bottom third up twice, pinch the seams in the center to seal, then roll into a strip about 20cm (8 inches) long, maintaining even thickness.
   **Note**: Folding from top and bottom towards the center prevents the bread from tilting sideways during baking. When rolling into a strip, start from the thickest part and work towards the ends.

8. **Surface decoration and final proofing**: Brush the surface of the bread strips with egg white, then roll them in the pink crumble to coat evenly. Place on a baking tray and proof at 37°C (99°F) and 75% humidity for about 30-40 minutes, until doubled in size.
   **Note**: Use only egg white, not whole egg, to prevent the surface from turning yellow after baking.

9. **Bake**: Preheat the oven. Bake at 150°C (302°F) top heat and 200°C (392°F) bottom heat, placed in the middle rack, for about 15 minutes.
   **Note**: Lower top heat helps maintain the pink color; higher bottom heat ensures the bread is fully baked and the bottom is browned, preventing it from tasting underdone the next day.

10. **Assemble**: Allow the bread to cool completely. Cut in half lengthwise with a serrated knife. Pipe in the lemon cream cheese filling and decorate with halved strawberries.

## Health Note
The dough made with dragon fruit juice is rich in anthocyanins, which have antioxidant properties. However, the sugar and butter content is high, making this suitable as an occasional dessert. The cream cheese filling and heavy cream provide calcium and protein, but are calorie-dense. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened tea.
