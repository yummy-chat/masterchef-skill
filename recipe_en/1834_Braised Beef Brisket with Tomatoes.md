# Braised Beef Brisket with Tomatoes
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket appropriate amount (fresh, with tendons and fat, cut into large dice)
🍅 Tomatoes appropriate amount (peeled, cut into large wedges)
🧅 Onions about 2 (cut into wedges or strips)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (smashed)
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 piece (rinsed to remove dust)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🥄 Light soy sauce 20 g
🍶 Cooking wine (Huangjiu) appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🍬 Rock sugar 10 g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare ingredients**: Cut the beef brisket into 3.5–4 cm dice, cut the onion into wedges or strips, peel the tomatoes and cut into large wedges, smash the ginger, and rinse the cinnamon stick to remove dust.
   **Note**: Do not cut the beef brisket too small, or the texture will not be satisfying.
2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add the smashed ginger and cinnamon stick, and stir-fry until fragrant.
3. Add the onion and stir-fry until softened.
4. Add the beef brisket pieces and stir-fry over high heat for about 3 minutes, until the surface of the beef turns pale and the onion aroma has infused.
5. Pour 20 g of light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, stir-fry quickly to reduce the liquid, making the beef more fragrant.
6. Add an appropriate amount of cooking wine to remove any gamey smell, and stir-fry evenly.
7. Add salt and 10 g of rock sugar, and stir-fry until the rock sugar has dissolved.
8. Pour in enough hot water to submerge the ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover with a lid, and braise until the beef is tender.
9. Add the tomato wedges and stir-fry until the tomatoes soften and release their juice.
10. Turn up the heat to reduce the sauce. Once the sauce is thick and glossy red, remove from heat. Optionally garnish with cilantro for extra aroma.

## Health Tips
Beef brisket is rich in iron and protein, while the vitamin C in tomatoes enhances iron absorption, making this dish especially suitable for those with anemia or needing Qi-boosting nourishment. When braising, control the amount of salt; those with high blood pressure should limit consumption of the broth.
