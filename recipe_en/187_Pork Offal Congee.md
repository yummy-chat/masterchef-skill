# Pork Offal Congee
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Pork offal (10 yuan portion)  
🩸 Pork blood cake (2 pieces)  
🍚 Frozen leftover rice (1 block)  
🧄 Scallions (to taste)  
🧄 Garlic (2–3 cloves)  
🫚 Ginger (a few slices)  
🧂 Cornstarch (to taste)  
🧂 Salt (to taste)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine (to taste)  
🌶️ White pepper (to taste)  
🫒 Cooking oil (a little)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🫕 Clay pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Kitchen knife & cutting board  

## Steps  
1. Place the frozen leftover rice in a clay pot, add enough water, drizzle in a little cooking oil, then put a soup ladle and a pair of chopsticks across the rim of the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
**Key point**: The soup ladle placed at the bottom prevents sticking, and the combination of chopsticks and ladle helps prevent the congee from boiling over.  

2. Clean the pork offal and slice it thinly. Set aside the pork blood cake. Finely chop the scallions. Slice the ginger into large pieces.  
**Key point**: Organs such as liver should be sliced thinly to ensure quick cooking.  

3. Add salt, cornstarch, and the large ginger slices to the sliced offal. Mix well to marinate.  
**Key point**: Cutting the ginger into large pieces makes it easy to remove later, and cornstarch tenderizes the meat.  

4. After the congee base comes to a boil, reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer until the rice grains are fully bloomed and the congee becomes thick.  
**Key point**: Stir occasionally during this process to prevent sticking to the bottom.  

5. Once the congee base is ready, uncover and remove the ginger slices. Add the marinated offal and crumbled pork blood cake to the pot. Stir well, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for another 1–2 minutes, until the offal turns white and is fully cooked.  
**Key point**: The congee will bubble up again after adding the offal; be careful to prevent overflow.  

6. Turn off the heat. Add salt, Shaoxing wine, and white pepper to taste. Stir to combine.  
**Key point**: Add the Shaoxing wine and white pepper at the end to better release their aromas.  

7. Serve in bowls, and sprinkle with chopped scallions according to personal preference.  

## Health Tips  
Pork offal congee is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for those who need to nourish the blood or warm the stomach. However, pork liver is high in cholesterol, so it is recommended to limit consumption to no more than one bowl per serving. Using frozen leftover rice helps the grains bloom faster, and remember to remove the ginger slices before eating for a safer experience.
