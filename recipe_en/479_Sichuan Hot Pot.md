# Sichuan Hot Pot
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Tofu skin, as needed
🥩 Beef, as needed
🥩 Filling (meat filling or shrimp paste), as needed
🥬 Lettuce, as needed
🍄 Enoki mushrooms, as needed
🌱 Hair moss, as needed
🌶️ Hot pot base (Haorenjia brand), 1 pack (contains beef tallow pack, base seasoning pack, and dried seasoning pack)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔩 Metal rod
🍳 Deep fryer
🔪 Slicer
🔥 Induction cooker
🍲 Dual-flavor hot pot
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Roll the tofu skin tightly and evenly around the metal rod, then use scissors to cut the rolled tofu skin into pieces to make crispy tofu skin rolls.
2. Place the raw rolls in medium-hot oil and fry until golden and crispy, then remove and drain the oil.
3. Place an appropriate amount of filling on the tofu skin, wrap it into a wonton shape, ensuring the seal is tight and does not come apart.
4. Use a slicer to cut the frozen beef into thin, even slices.
5. Wash the lettuce, enoki mushrooms, and hair moss, then drain the water and set aside.
6. Add an appropriate amount of water to the pot, then add the beef tallow pack, base seasoning pack, and dried seasoning pack in order. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and keep it boiling. Once the base seasoning has fully dissolved and the soup has turned a bright red color, it is ready.
7. First, add the longer-cooking crispy tofu skin rolls and tofu skin wontons to the pot. Then, cook the meat slices for 10 to 20 seconds until they change color and are done. Cook the vegetables for 1 to 2 minutes until they soften, then serve to keep the ingredients fresh and tender.

## Health Advice
The crispy tofu skin rolls and wontons are rich in high-quality protein, suitable for muscle building. Enoki mushrooms and lettuce provide dietary fiber, aiding digestion. Be mindful of the sodium content in the hot pot base; those with high blood lipids should consume less of the oily soup.
