# Pickled Vegetables (Bath-Style Pickles)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥒 Vegetable sticks 1400g (white radish, carrot, lettuce, Chinese radish, beetroot radish, and other root vegetables, cut into strips)
🍉 Fruit pieces appropriate amount (watermelon rind, peach, cantaloupe, etc., can be added for pickling)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for pre-treatment dehydration)
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount (for pre-treatment dehydration)
🌶️ Pickled wild pepper brine 500g (including a few wild peppers)
🍶 White vinegar 150g
💧 Purified water 300g (can be replaced with Sprite)
🍬 White sugar 150g
🧂 Salt 15g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 15g
🌶️ Small chili peppers approximately 15g (cut into sections, totaling 30g with garlic)
🧄 Garlic approximately 15g (crushed, totaling 30g with small chili peppers)
🍋 Lemon 30g (sliced)
🌿 Spices 15g (dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, etc.)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl
🧺 Strainer or colander
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥢 Chopsticks or spoon
🏺 Pickling jar
🧤 Disposable gloves
❄️ Refrigerator (use when ambient temperature exceeds 25°C)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Pre-treat vegetables and fruits**: Wash all vegetables and fruits thoroughly, cut into strips or chunks, totaling 1400g. Add appropriate salt and sugar, mix well, and marinate briefly to wilt, dehydrate, and shrink. After the water is released, rinse thoroughly with clean water to remove excess salt and sugar, then drain well.
**Note**: Root vegetables are preferred; vegetable scraps can also be used to avoid waste; rinsing must be thorough.
2. **Prepare pickling brine**: In a clean large bowl, sequentially add pickled wild pepper brine 500g, white vinegar 150g, purified water 300g, white sugar 150g, salt 15g, chicken bouillon 15g. Then add small chili pepper sections, garlic cloves, lemon slices, and spices such as dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves. Stir until all seasonings are completely dissolved.
**Note**: All ingredients must be precisely weighed; purified water can be replaced with an equal amount of Sprite.
3. **Load the jar and pickle**: Place the drained vegetable sticks and fruit pieces into a clean pickling jar. Pour in the prepared pickling brine, ensuring the liquid completely covers the ingredients. Cover with the inner lid and outer lid, then fill the rim channel with water to create a seal.
**Note**: The rim water must not dry out, as this is key to ensuring a sealed fermentation; if the ambient temperature exceeds 25°C, move the pickling jar to the refrigerator.
4. **Wait for the finished product**: It is ready to eat after 24 hours of pickling at room temperature. The best flavor is achieved after 72 hours. It is recommended to finish within 7-10 days.

## Health Advice
This pickle, after dehydration with salt and sugar, has a refreshingly crisp and tender texture. Paired with pickled wild peppers and fruit pieces, it helps stimulate the appetite and cut through grease, making it suitable for those looking to control calorie intake. Note that the pickling time should not exceed 10 days; refrigeration can extend the shelf life.
