# Braised Pork Trotters with Peanuts
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐷 Pork trotters (front hooves) 1.5 jin (cut into large pieces)
🥜 Red-skinned peanuts, appropriate amount (pre-cooked until soft)
🧄 Ginger, about 2 liang (smashed)
🧄 Garlic, about 1 liang (whole or smashed)
🧅 Dried shallots, about 1 liang (cut into pieces)
🌿 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
⭐ Star anise, 3 pieces
🧅 Green onions, appropriate amount (chopped)
🧂 Fermented red bean curd, half a piece (about one piece)
🥫 Chu Hou paste, appropriate amount
🍶 White vinegar, appropriate amount (for blanching)
🥢 Light soy sauce, about 3 qian
🍬 Rock sugar, about 2 liang
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for cooking peanuts)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (splashed along the pot edge)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Large wok
🔪 Chopping board, large chef’s knife
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥘 Clay pot
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Pre-cook the peanuts**: Add the red-skinned peanuts to boiling water, then add bay leaves, star anise, and salt. Simmer over low heat for 30 minutes until soft. Drain and set aside.
2. **Prepare the pork trotters**: Wash the raw trotters and cut into large pieces. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add white vinegar, then blanch the trotters for about 1 minute. Remove, wash, and drain.
**Key point**: Blanching in boiling water with white vinegar helps lock in the gelatin, reducing its loss during braising.
3. **Sauté the aromatics**: Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add ginger, garlic, and dried shallots, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add the fermented red bean curd and stir-fry until aromatic.
4. **Stir-fry the trotters**: Add the trotter pieces and stir-fry until they are evenly coated with the fermented bean curd and aromatics. Add the Chu Hou paste and continue stir-frying to enhance the aroma.
5. **Deglaze and add water**: Splash cooking wine along the edge of the wok to add fragrance. Then add enough hot water to cover all the ingredients. Add all the water at once.
6. **Season and braise**: Add light soy sauce and rock sugar, stirring to dissolve. Cover and simmer over low heat for 40 minutes, until the trotters are tender.
**Key point**: Using rock sugar instead of white sugar allows it to penetrate the meat, enhancing the gelatinous texture and sheen.
7. **Add the peanuts**: After 30 minutes of simmering, uncover, add the cooked peanuts, cover again, and continue simmering for another 10 minutes. At this point, you can turn the heat to medium, using the starch released from the peanuts to naturally thicken the sauce.
8. **Thicken the sauce and serve**: When the sauce is thick enough to coat a spoon, heat the clay pot. Transfer the trotters and peanuts to the clay pot, sprinkle with chopped green onions, and serve.

## Health Advice
Pork trotters are rich in collagen, and when paired with peanuts, they can help improve skin elasticity. However, they are high in calories, so consumption should be moderated. This dish is suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter. People with poor digestion or those trying to lose weight should eat less.
