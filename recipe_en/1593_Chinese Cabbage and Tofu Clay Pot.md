# Chinese Cabbage and Tofu Clay Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Egg Tofu 8 pieces (cut into chunks)
🥬 Chinese Cabbage approximately 150g (rinsed and separated)
🥩 Pork Belly approximately 50g (cut into 3-4 slices)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into segments)
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed)
🌶️ Red Chili Peppers 2 (cut into rings, for garnish)
🦐 Dried Shrimp a pinch
🌽 Potato Starch approximately 100g (some for coating tofu, some for thickening)
🧂 Salt to taste
🦪 Oyster Sauce approximately 1 tablespoon
🍂 Dark Soy Sauce a dash
🌿 Ground White Pepper a dash
💧 Boiling Water as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Wok
🍲 Clay Pot
🔪 Chef's Knife
🪓 Cutting Board
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Evenly coat the surface of the tofu chunks with a thin layer of potato starch.
   **Note**: The coating must be even so that after frying, a crispy crust forms and the tofu does not easily break apart during simmering.
2. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat until the oil temperature is high. Add the starch-coated tofu chunks and fry for about 3 minutes until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
   **Note**: The oil temperature must be high enough; otherwise, the tofu will absorb too much oil and the crust will not be crispy.
3. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add the sliced pork belly, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the slices are lightly browned and fragrant.
4. Add the scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried shrimp. Continue to stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Pour in enough boiling water, season with salt, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and ground white pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
6. Add the fried tofu and cook briefly for 1-2 minutes, allowing the tofu to initially absorb the broth.
7. Line the bottom of a clay pot with the rinsed Chinese cabbage leaves. Transfer the pork belly slices, tofu, and broth from the wok into the clay pot.
   **Note**: Placing the cabbage at the bottom prevents scorching and adds a subtle sweetness.
8. Cover with a lid and cook over medium heat for about 8 minutes, until the cabbage is tender and the tofu is fluffy and has absorbed the broth.
9. Mix a small amount of potato starch with water to create a slurry. Pour the slurry along the edge of the pot, gently push the tofu to evenly coat with the thickened sauce, and garnish with chopped scallions and red chili pepper rings.

## Health Tips
This Chinese cabbage and tofu clay pot is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing nutritional supplementation while preferring a light diet. The tofu becomes soft after absorbing the broth, but the pork belly and frying step add considerable fat. It is recommended to control portion sizes or reduce the amount of meat slices.
