# Shredded Cabbage in Shrimp Broth (Shanxi Style)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥬 Baby cabbage/cabbage heart 1-2 heads (cut into thin shreds)  
🥚 Eggs 2 (beaten, with a pinch of salt)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 3 tbsp (for making the batter-thickened broth)  
🦐 Shrimp, as needed (for making broth and shrimp oil)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (sliced)  
🦯 Ginger 2-3 slices (for blanching shrimp to remove fishiness)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for blanching shrimp to remove fishiness)  
🧂 Chicken powder, a pinch (optional; the author considers it unnecessary)  
🌶 White pepper, a pinch (added in cold weather to warm the body)  
🫗 Sesame oil, a drizzle (added after turning off the heat for fragrance)  
🫒 Cooking oil, a large bowl (for frying shrimp and making egg floss)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a few (appeared in preparation stage but not used later)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Portable gas stove or gas range  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🥣 Bowls  

## Steps  
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add ginger slices and cooking wine. Blanch the shrimp until just cooked, then remove and set aside. Reserve the cooking liquid. Drain the shrimp and lightly crush them with the back of a knife to release flavor later.  
2. Wash the baby cabbage or cabbage heart and cut into thin shreds. Slice the garlic. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and beat until well combined. Set aside.  
3. Place the crushed shrimp into a dry wok (no oil or water). Dry-fry over low heat to evaporate moisture.  
4. Pour in a large bowl of cooking oil. Fry the shrimp over low heat until they are completely dried, crispy, and free of moisture.  
   **Key Point**: Shrimp oil is the flavor essence of this dish.  
5. Using a soup ladle, skim off a portion of the clear shrimp oil and set aside. Leave the remaining oil and shrimp in the wok.  
6. Directly add water to the wok containing the fried shrimp and remaining oil. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 10 minutes.  
7. Strain the shrimp broth through a slotted spoon to remove the shells and meat, yielding a clear shrimp broth. Set aside.  
8. Heat the wok and add a small amount of the reserved shrimp oil. Stir-fry the shredded cabbage until soft and wilted. Turn off the heat and set aside.  
9. Clean the wok. Pour in about a large bowl of oil and heat to 30% hot (low-medium heat).  
10. Slowly pour the beaten egg mixture into the oil in a thin stream while constantly stirring with a spatula to form loose, fluffy egg floss.  
11. Once the egg floss is set, increase the heat to high and fry again until deep brown, crispy, and aromatic.  
12. Turn off the heat. Use a slotted spoon to remove the egg floss and drain excess oil. Set aside.  
13. Without cleaning the wok, use the remaining oil in it to sauté the sliced garlic until fragrant.  
14. Reduce heat to low-medium. Add 3 tablespoons of flour and stir-fry continuously until the flour turns slightly yellow and releases a nutty aroma.  
    **Note**: Toasting the flour is key to creating a thick broth—avoid burning it.  
15. Immediately pour in the strained shrimp broth. Adjust the thickness by adding hot water as needed.  
16. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add the stir-fried shredded cabbage and fried egg floss.  
17. Once it returns to a boil, cover the wok and simmer over low heat for about 2 minutes.  
18. Turn off the heat. Season with salt to taste. Optionally add a pinch of chicken powder or white pepper.  
19. Drizzle with a little sesame oil for fragrance, stir well, and serve.  
    **Finished Dish Characteristics**: The broth is golden and thick; the cabbage is soft and melts in the mouth; the egg floss absorbs the broth, crispy on the outside and tender inside; overall savory and rich.

## Health Suggestions
This dish is based on shrimp oil for depth, with egg floss adding aroma, and the cabbage is soft and easy to digest, making it suitable for warming up in winter. Pay attention to the heat when toasting the flour to avoid burning and affecting the texture. The egg floss is fried twice, making it relatively high in calories; those on a diet may reduce the amount consumed as needed.
