# Rock Sugar and Tremella Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍐 1 Fengshui Pear (peeled and cored, flesh cut into large chunks, skin and core reserved)  
🍄 1 dried tremella mushroom (soaked in cold water for at least 1 hour in advance, torn into small florets)  
🧂 2 pieces of yellow rock sugar  
🟤 Chinese red dates, to taste (washed)  
🔴 Goji berries, to taste  
🌸 2 cloves (key spice, used to enhance aroma and remove astringency when boiling the pear water)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Transparent glass pot (convenient for observing the cooking state)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing residues)  
🥣 Soup spoon (for stirring and serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour enough cold water into the pot, add the pear skin, pear core, and cloves, and bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Note**: Boil until the water releases color and aroma, do not boil for too long.
2. Use a slotted spoon to remove and discard the pear skin, pear core, and cloves, retaining the clear pear broth.
3. Add the soaked and torn tremella into the pear broth, and bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Key point**: Immediately stir quickly after boiling, and add a small amount of cold water. This helps the tremella release its gum more quickly.
4. Reduce to low heat and simmer slowly until the broth becomes noticeably thick and the tremella has fully released its gum.
5. Add the yellow rock sugar and stir until completely dissolved.
6. Add the cut pear chunks, cover the pot, reduce to low heat, and simmer for 15 minutes until the pear flesh is soft and tender.
7. Remove the lid, add the Chinese red dates and goji berries, continue cooking for 5 minutes, then turn off the heat.

## Health Advice
Rock sugar and tremella soup nourishes the lungs and beautifies the skin, making it especially suitable for consumption during the dry autumn and winter seasons. The key to the tremella releasing its gum lies in thorough soaking and quickly stirring after adding cold water. Diabetic patients should reduce the amount of rock sugar.
