# Hong Kong-Style Beef Offal
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef Ribs 5 jin (fresh, chopped into pieces)
🫀 Beef Heart appropriate amount
🫁 Beef Lung appropriate amount (pre-washed)
🥓 Beef Intestine appropriate amount (remove most of the fat, leave a little, wash by turning inside out)
🐂 Beef Tripe appropriate amount
🐂 Beef Spleen (Beef Pancreas) appropriate amount
🥕 White Radish 1 piece (put in whole)
🫚 Ginger about 10g (ginger chunks, added in batches)
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers about 10g
🍊 Dried Tangerine Peel about 4g
⭐ Star Anise 3 pieces
🌸 Cloves about 3g
🌿 Lemongrass about 2g
🍂 Bay Leaves about 2g
🪵 Cinnamon Stick 5g
🌱 Licorice Root 5g
🌰 Black Cardamom 2 pieces
🌾 Fennel Seeds about 3g
🫙 Chu Hou Paste 2 spoons (about 10g)
🫘 Cant-Style Bean Paste about 6g
🫘 Soy Bean Paste about 5g
🥜 Peanut Butter 5g
🫘 Sesame Paste 3g
🫙 Fermented Soy Paste 5g
🧀 Fermented Tofu 5g
🧀 Fermented Red Bean Curd 12g
🫒 Peanut Oil appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking Wine about 10g (added twice)
🍬 Rock Sugar 10g
🦪 Oyster Sauce about 20g
🧂 Salt appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Pressure Cooker
🍳 Wok
🥘 Large Stock Pot/Soup Pot
🥄 Skimmer/Strainer
🔪 Cutting Board and Chef's Knife
🧤 Spice Pouch/Tea Infuser Bag

## Cooking Steps
1.  Chop the beef ribs into sections. Place the dried chili peppers, dried tangerine peel, star anise, cloves, lemongrass, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, licorice root, black cardamom, and fennel seeds into a spice pouch and tie it tightly. Set aside. Prepare the offal: Tear off excess fat from the beef intestines, turn them inside out and wash clean; wash the beef heart, tripe, spleen, and lung separately.
2.  Place the beef ribs and the spice pouch into the pressure cooker. Add enough clean water. Bring to high heat until pressure is reached, then cook under pressure for 20 minutes. Reduce to low heat and cook under pressure for another 20 minutes.
3.  In a separate pot, bring clean water to a boil. Add cooking wine. Place all whole pieces of beef offal and ginger slices into the boiling water. Bring to a vigorous boil, skim off the foam. Blanch for about 20 minutes. Remove the offal and drain.
4.  Pour the beef ribs and their broth from the pressure cooker into the large stock pot. Add enough clean water. Skim off any foam from the surface. Add the blanched beef offal. Cook over medium-low heat for about 2 hours.
5.  In a bowl, combine Chu Hou paste, Cant-style bean paste, soy bean paste, peanut butter, sesame paste, fermented soy paste, fermented tofu, and fermented red bean curd. Mix until smooth. Heat a wok over low heat, add peanut oil. Stir-fry the mixed paste until fragrant.
6.  Pour the stir-fried paste into the stock pot containing the simmering offal. Add the whole white radish, ginger chunks, cooking wine, and rock sugar. Cook uncovered, continuing to simmer for about 2 hours.
7.  Just before serving, season with oyster sauce and salt. Remove the offal and white radish from the pot. Cut into pieces and arrange on a serving platter. Ladle an appropriate amount of the braising liquid over the top. Serve.

## Health Advice
Beef offal is rich in protein and iron, but has high cholesterol and purine content. It is recommended to consume with plenty of vegetables. Those with gout or high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Long, slow cooking makes nutrients more absorbable, but portion control is still advised.
