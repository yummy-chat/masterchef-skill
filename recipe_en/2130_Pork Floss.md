# Pork Floss
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 3 pounds of pork hind leg lean meat (must remove all sinew and membrane)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for boiling meat to remove odor)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (basic seasoning)  
🍬 White sugar, as needed (to enhance freshness)  
🥢 Steamed fish soy sauce, as needed (to enhance color and sweetness)  
🫒 Olive oil or tea oil, as needed (drizzle along the edge of the pan when stir-frying)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Iron wok (with lid)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🌀 Blender  
🍽️ Plate  
🫙 Sealed jar  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Carefully remove all surface sinew and membrane from the pork hind leg lean meat, then cut into large chunks.  
   **Key Point**: Must use hind leg meat; front leg meat has too much sinew, which affects the texture of the final product.  
2. Add water to the wok, then place the meat chunks, ginger slices, salt, and white sugar.  
   **Key Point**: Simmer over low heat for more than two hours, until the meat chunks can be easily broken apart by lightly pressing with the wok spatula.  
3. Remove the ginger slices, add the steamed fish soy sauce, then turn up the heat to high and stir-fry, allowing the meat shreds to fully absorb the liquid.  
   **Note**: Do not remove the meat chunks and discard the liquid during the process; the liquid is key to infusing flavor.  
4. When the liquid in the wok is mostly evaporated, drizzle olive oil or tea oil along the edge of the pan.  
   **Purpose**: Prevents sticking and adds aroma.  
5. Switch to low heat throughout, and continue stir-frying for about 40 minutes.  
   **Status Check**: Fry until the moisture in the meat shreds has completely evaporated, the color turns golden brown, and the volume is significantly reduced.  
6. Turn off the heat, allow the meat shreds to cool completely, then pour them into the blender.  
   **Key Point**: Must cool completely before blending.  
7. Use the blender to process the meat shreds into a cottony, fine pork floss.  
   **Key Point**: Blend until it reaches a melt-in-your-mouth, fluffy consistency, suitable for children.  

## Health Advice  
This homemade pork floss has all sinew thoroughly removed and contains no additives, making it suitable for iron supplementation in babies. However, control the amounts of salt and sugar to avoid being too salty. It is recommended to serve with congee or bread for a more balanced nutritional intake.
