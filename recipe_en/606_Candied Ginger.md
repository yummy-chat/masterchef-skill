# Candied Ginger
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫚 Tender ginger, approximately 500g (peeled, sliced into 2mm thick pieces)  
🍬 White sugar, 400g (for marinating and stir-frying)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🔥 Stove  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prep Phase  
1. Wash and peel the tender ginger, then slice into pieces about 2mm thick.  
   **Note**: Choose tender ginger, which has less fiber; ensure slices are evenly thick for better flavor absorption.  
2. Rinse the ginger slices in clean water 3-5 times to reduce spiciness, then remove and thoroughly drain.  
3. Add 400g white sugar to the ginger slices, mix well, and marinate for at least 5 hours until the sugar is completely dissolved, the slices soften, and they have fully absorbed the sugar.  

### Stir-Frying and Sugar Coating  
4. Pour the marinated ginger slices along with the sugar liquid into the wok, heat over medium heat, and stir continuously with a wooden spatula until it comes to a boil.  
   **Note**: Stir constantly throughout to prevent sticking or burning.  
5. After boiling, continue stir-frying for about 6 minutes until the syrup thickens, then reduce to low heat and keep stirring.  
6. Stir-fry for approximately 12 more minutes until the syrup is extremely thick, covered with fine bubbles, and white granular sugar crystals begin to form on the edges of the pan. Immediately turn off the heat.  
   **Key Judgment**: The appearance of white sugar crystals along the pan's edge signals that sugar coating is imminent.  
7. After turning off the heat, continue stir-frying with residual heat for about 2 minutes until all moisture has evaporated and the syrup crystallizes into a white sugar coating evenly covering each ginger slice. Then remove from the pan.  

## Health Advice  
This candied ginger is high in sugar; it is recommended to consume just a few slices at a time as a small snack, especially suitable for cold seasons or after meals to warm the stomach and dispel chills. Diabetics and those managing sugar intake should consume with caution.
