# Taro Basque Cheesecake 🍰
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥔
🥔 Taro (peeled) about 360g
🍠 Purple sweet potato about 40g
🧀 Cream cheese about 240g (softened at room temperature)
🥚 Eggs 2 (at room temperature)
🥛 Heavy cream about 130g (for cheesecake batter) + about 130g (for surface decoration)
🧈 Butter about 45g (can substitute with corn oil)
🍬 Granulated sugar about 30g (for taro paste) + about 50g (for cheesecake batter) + about 10g (for surface cream)
🥛 Condensed milk about 30g (optional)
🥛 Milk about 100g
🌾 Cornstarch about 10g

## Cookware & Equipment 🍲
🍲 Steamer
🔥 Oven (or air fryer/steamer as substitute)
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Wooden spatula or mixing spoon
🥄 Strainer
🧲 Sieve
🍰 6-inch cake pan
📄 Parchment paper
🌀 Electric hand mixer
🎂 Piping bag and nozzle
🔪 Fruit knife or slicing knife
🎠 Turntable (for assisting frosting)

## Instructions 📝
1. **Pre-treatment & Steaming**: Peel and slice the taro. Peel and cube the purple sweet potato. Place in a steamer and steam over high heat until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
   - **Key Point**: Use Lipu taro for a softer, stickier texture and better marbling.

2. **Make Taro Paste**: Place 360g steamed taro and 40g steamed purple sweet potato in a large bowl. While still hot, add 30g granulated sugar, 30g condensed milk, 45g butter pieces, and 100g milk. Use a strainer or masher to press and mix into a smooth, evenly colored taro paste.
   - **Key Point**: Working while hot makes mixing easier; butter can be substituted with corn oil.

3. **Line the Base**: Line the cake pan with parchment paper. Spread the taro paste into the pan to a thickness of about 1–1.5 cm, pressing it flat.

4. **Make Cheesecake Batter**:
   - Beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and lump-free. Add 50g granulated sugar and mix well.
   - Gradually add the beaten eggs, mixing thoroughly after each addition.
   - Sift in 10g cornstarch and mix well.
   - Add 130g heavy cream and stir until combined.
   - **Key Point**: All ingredients should be at room temperature; if lumps form, heat over a water bath and stir until smooth.

5. **Assemble & Bake**: Strain the cheesecake batter through a sieve into the cake pan. Preheat the oven to top heat 220°C and bottom heat 210°C. Bake on the middle rack for about 24 minutes, until the surface is deep caramelized.
   - **Alternative**: If no oven, use an air fryer or steam over high heat in a steamer (after it's steaming) for about 6 minutes.

6. **Cool & Set**: Remove from the pan after baking. Let cool, then refrigerate when no longer hot to touch for at least 4 hours or overnight.

7. **Make Taro Cream**: Whip 130g heavy cream with 10g granulated sugar until thick and flowing. Add about 130g of the sifted taro paste. Continue whipping until the cream is evenly lavender-colored and has clear patterns.

8. **Decorate**: Remove the chilled cake. Use a fruit knife or offset spatula to evenly spread the taro cream over the top and sides. For the top, use a piping bag with a nozzle to pipe decorative cream swirls.
   - **Key Point**: Using a fruit knife or slicing knife helps achieve a smoother frosting finish.

9. **Final Chill**: After decorating, refrigerate briefly to set the cream before serving.

## Health Advice 🍃
This taro Basque cheesecake is rich in taro flavor and has a dense, silky texture, making it suitable as a festive dessert. However, due to its high sugar and cream content, it is recommended to control portion size and pair with unsweetened tea. Due to its high caloric density, moderation is advised during weight loss periods.
