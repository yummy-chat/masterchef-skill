# Lazy Braised Pork Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork neck (pork collar)  About 250g  
🧅 Red shallots  To taste  
🧄 Garlic  To taste (minced)  
🌿 Scallions  To taste  
🫙 Chu Hou paste  To taste  
🫙 Spare rib sauce  To taste  
🧂 Salt  A pinch  
🍬 Sugar  To taste (can add more)  
🍶 Rice wine  To taste (can add more)  
🥄 Soy sauce  To taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🗑️ Strainer (or slotted spoon)  
🔗 Tongs  

## Instructions  
1.  Use the back of a knife to pound the pork neck until the fibers are loosened, then cut into bite-sized chunks.  
   **Key point**: Pounding makes the meat more tender and prevents it from becoming dry during long cooking.
2.  Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, combine Chu Hou paste, spare rib sauce, a pinch of salt, sugar, rice wine, and soy sauce. Add the minced garlic and chopped red shallots, and mix well.  
   **Key point**: The sugar and rice wine will caramelize during the long braise, producing a toasty aroma. It's recommended to use slightly more.
3.  Add the meat pieces to the marinade. Use your hands to thoroughly mix everything, ensuring each piece is coated with the sauce. Marinate for 5–10 minutes.
4.  Remove the marinated meat pieces using tongs. Separate the solid ingredients (shallots, garlic) from the liquid sauce, and set both aside separately.
5.  Line the bottom of the rice cooker inner pot with a layer of cleaned scallion sections.
6.  Spread the separated solid ingredients (shallots, garlic) evenly over the scallions. This prevents burning and adds flavor.
7.  Arrange the marinated meat pieces evenly on top of the solid ingredients.
8.  Pour all of the separated liquid marinade over the meat. This will serve as the braising liquid.
9.  Close the lid. Select the "Regular Cook" or "White Rice" setting, with a total cooking time of about 30 minutes.  
   **Note**: About 15 minutes into the cooking process, open the lid and use tongs to flip the meat pieces. This ensures even heating and flavor absorption. Then continue cooking.  
   **Tip**: Red shallots or small onions will become sweet and soft after long braising.
10. Once the cooking cycle is complete, the sauce should be thick and the meat a rich brownish-red color and fully cooked. Remove the meat, slice it, and serve over rice. Drizzle the thick sauce from the pot over the top.  
    **Key point**: The final pour of sauce is crucial for flavor and presentation. The thick sauce is excellent for mixing into the rice.

## Health Tips
This braised pork rice is rich in protein, but contains a significant amount of sugar and sauce. It is recommended to serve with a side of stir-fried green vegetables to balance the nutrition. It is suitable for busy office workers, but those with high blood lipids should be mindful of their portion size.
