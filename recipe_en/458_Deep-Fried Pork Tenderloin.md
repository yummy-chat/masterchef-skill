# Deep-Fried Pork Tenderloin
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin, about 200g
🌽 Cornstarch, 2 tablespoons
🌾 All-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon
💧 Water, as needed
🧅 Scallion, about 10g (one section)
🫚 Ginger, about 10g (a few slices)
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🌶️ Ground white pepper,适量
🍶 Shaoxing wine,适量
🫒 Vegetable oil, 10ml (for crisping the batter)
🫒 Vegetable oil, sufficient amount (for frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Bowls/plates
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spider strainer

## Instructions
1.  Cut the pork tenderloin into thick slices, then cut into evenly sized strips.
**Note**: The strips should be uniform in size to ensure even cooking.

2.  Add a pinch of salt, ground white pepper, and Shaoxing wine to the pork strips. Add the scallion sections and ginger slices. Mix thoroughly with your hands, then marinate for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate.
**Note**: Mixing thoroughly is important to remove any gamey smell. Remove the scallion and ginger before coating with batter.

3.  Prepare the batter: In a bowl, combine 2 tablespoons of cornstarch and 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour. Add enough water and stir until smooth and flowable, with no lumps. Finally, add 10ml of vegetable oil and mix well.
**Consistency**: When lifted with a spoon, the batter should flow in a continuous thin stream.
   **Key**: The batter should not be too thick or too thin. Adding oil is crucial for achieving crispiness.

4.  Place the marinated pork strips (with scallion and ginger removed) into the batter. Coat each strip one by one with a thin layer of batter, ensuring each piece is fully covered.
5.  Heat sufficient oil in a wok to 160-170°C (medium-high heat). Carefully add the battered pork strips one by one into the oil. Fry over medium heat until set and light golden in color.
**Note**: Do not stir immediately after adding to the oil. Wait until the outer shell sets, then gently separate the strips with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Remove and drain once set.

6.  Increase the oil temperature. Return all the fried pork strips to the wok for a second fry. Fry over medium heat until the outer shell turns golden brown and makes a crispy, sizzling sound when shaken.
**Key**: The second fry forces out excess oil from the interior, achieving a crispy exterior and tender interior. This is the crucial step for deep-fried pork tenderloin.

7.  Quickly remove and drain the oil. Plate and serve immediately while hot. Can be enjoyed with salt and pepper dip.

## Health Advice
This deep-fried pork tenderloin is rich in protein, but due to high-temperature frying, it has a high calorie and fat content. It is recommended to pair it with a fresh vegetable salad or cold cucumber salad to balance nutrition. Suitable for occasional consumption. Those with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, high blood sugar, or those on a weight loss plan should have it sparingly.
