# Braised Prawns in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Large prawns, about 1 jin or more (enough to fill a clay pot in 3 layers)  
🍄 Mushrooms, to taste (cut into small pieces)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Green and red peppers, to taste (cut into rings)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (use leaf sections)  
🍾 Cooking wine, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste  
🫘 Puning soybean paste, 1 tablespoon  
🥡 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons  
🌽 Cornstarch, to taste  
💧 Water, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🦷 Toothpick  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Wooden spoon  

## Steps  
1. Using a toothpick, gently lift out the vein from the back of each large prawn at the third segment. Repeat this for all prawns.  
**Note**: Selecting the third segment to remove the vein helps keep the prawn body intact and avoids damage.  

2. Place the prepared prawns in a bowl. Add cooking wine, salt, and white pepper powder. Mix thoroughly by hand and marinate for 15 minutes.  
**Note**: Mixing ensures the seasonings adhere evenly. Adequate marination is necessary to remove fishy odor and infuse flavor.  

3. Slice the ginger, slice the garlic, cut the mushrooms into small pieces, cut the green and red peppers into rings, cut the scallions into sections, and prepare the cilantro leaf sections. Set aside.  
**Note**: Cutting mushrooms into small pieces makes them easier to absorb flavor. Prepping all ingredients in advance improves efficiency.  

4. In a bowl, add 1 tablespoon of Puning soybean paste and 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce. Then add an appropriate amount of cornstarch and water. Stir with a spoon until smooth and lump-free.  
**Note**: Cornstarch must be fully stirred to avoid lumps during cooking. The sauce should be of moderate thickness.  

5. Preheat the clay pot, then add a small amount of cooking oil. Add the ginger slices, garlic slices, and mushroom pieces. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant and the mushrooms become soft.  
**Note**: The clay pot must be preheated before adding oil to prevent sticking. Use low heat to avoid burning.  

6. Place the marinated prawns one by one on top of the stir-fried mushrooms, filling the clay pot in 3 layers to ensure the ingredients are fully covered.  
**Note**: Arranging the prawns to cover the pot ensures even heat distribution during braising.  

7. Sprinkle the green and red pepper rings over the prawns. Then evenly pour the prepared sauce over the prawns, covering all ingredients.  
**Note**: The sauce must be poured evenly to ensure every prawn comes into contact with it.  

8. Cover the clay pot with its lid. Braise over medium heat for 3-5 minutes, until the prawns are completely red and fully cooked.  
**Note**: Do not braise for too long to avoid overcooking the prawn meat. The indicator of doneness is the shell turning completely red and the meat becoming firm.  

9. Turn off the heat and remove the lid. Sprinkle scallion sections and cilantro leaves over the braised prawns. Serve immediately.  
**Note**: Sprinkle the garnishes and serve right away to preserve the fresh and tender texture of the ingredients.  

## Health Tips  
This dish of braised prawns in a clay pot is high in protein and low in fat. Prawn meat is easily digestible, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss plan or fitness regimen. Note that Puning soybean paste and light soy sauce contain relatively high salt levels; individuals with high blood pressure may consider reducing the amount of sauce used.
