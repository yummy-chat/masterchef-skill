# Cantonese Black Pepper Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧄 Garlic 20g (some sliced, some minced)  
🥬 Celery 50g  
🧅 Onion 80g (some cut into chunks, some minced)  
🫑🌶 Green and red bell peppers 50g  
🍅 Tomatoes 2 pieces  
🥕 Carrot 150g  
🥔 Potato 150g  
🦴 Roasted beef bones, as needed  
💧 Water 8 jin  
🌾 Cake flour 80g  
🧈 Butter 60g (divided into 50g and 10g)  
🧂 Salt 15g  
🧂 Chicken powder 15g  
🌶️ Black peppercorns 8g  
🍾 Dark soy sauce, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🔥 Oven  
🔍 Fine mesh strainer/filter  
🥄 Soup ladle/spatula  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Bowl/container  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Heat a wok, melt 50g of butter. First, add the sliced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Then, add the onion chunks, celery, green and red bell peppers, tomatoes, carrots, and potatoes. Stir-fry until all vegetables release their aroma.
2. Transfer the stir-fried vegetables to a pressure cooker. Add 8 jin of water and the roasted beef bones. Cover the lid and cook over high heat for 15 minutes, then reduce to low heat and cook for 40 more minutes.
3. Spread 80g of cake flour on a baking tray. Place it in an oven preheated to 160°C and bake for 10–15 minutes, until the flour turns slightly yellow and releases a fragrant aroma.
4. Strain the cooked beef bone broth. Remove about 1/3 of the broth and let it cool. Sift the roasted flour into the cooled broth and stir until smooth. Then, pour this mixture back into the remaining hot broth, stirring well to combine, creating the base soup.
5. In a clean wok, melt 10g of butter. Add the minced onion and minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the black peppercorns and stir-fry until they release their aroma.
6. Pour the base soup into the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt and chicken powder. Use dark soy sauce to adjust the color. Stir continuously and cook until the sauce thickens and becomes smooth.

## Health Advice  
This black pepper sauce is based on a vegetable broth and enhanced with beef bones for flavor, providing comprehensive nutrition but with a relatively high sodium content. It is suitable for pairing with steak or stir-fries to enhance taste. Individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt used.
