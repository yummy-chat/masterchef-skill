# Creamy Low-Sugar Mung Bean Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 500g Peeled Mung Beans (soak for over 6 hours in advance)  
🥛 250ml Milk  
🧂 60g White Sugar  
🥛 70g Milk Powder  
🧈 80g Butter (can substitute with corn oil, but flavor will differ)  
🫘 Approx. 300g Red Bean Paste (roll into 20g portions; detailed recipe not shown in video)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large Bowl (for soaking and rinsing)  
🍚 Rice Cooker (for cooking mung beans)  
🥤 Blender or Food Processor (for making mung bean puree)  
🍳 Non-Stick Pan (for stir-frying mung bean paste)  
🥄 Silicone Spatula (for stirring and mixing)  
🍽️ Chopping Board (for kneading and portioning dough)  
🧤 Disposable Gloves (to prevent sticking)  
🧁 Mung Bean Cake Mold (for shaping; can shape manually without mold)  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare Mung Beans**
   - Rinse peeled mung beans twice with clean water. Add enough water and soak for over 6 hours until beans are fully hydrated and can be easily crushed between fingers.
   - **Key Point**: Ensure sufficient soaking time so the beans absorb enough water for easy cooking.

2. **Cook Mung Beans**
   - Pour the soaked beans and soaking water into the rice cooker inner pot. Select the "Cook Rice" setting and cook for about 40 minutes until the beans are soft and mushy.
   - **Note**: Must cook with water to ensure sufficient moisture and prevent burning.

3. **Blend Mung Bean Puree**
   - Transfer the cooked mung beans to a blender. Add 250ml milk and 60g sugar, then blend into an extremely smooth puree. Blend multiple times until no graininess remains.
   - **Key Point**: The finer the puree, the smoother the final texture.

4. **Stir-Fry Mung Bean Paste**
   - Pour the mung bean puree into a non-stick pan. Do not heat yet. Stir in 70g milk powder, then add 80g butter. Turn on low heat and continuously stir with a silicone spatula for about 25–30 minutes.
   - Texture changes: First 10 minutes – moisture reduces significantly, mixture thickens; about 15 minutes – dough starts to clump together, not sticking to pan or spatula; finally – when pressed by hand, feels smooth and non-sticky.
   - **Key Point**: Keep heat low and stir constantly to prevent burning.

5. **Cool and Knead Dough**
   - Remove the stir-fried mung bean dough onto a chopping board. Wearing gloves, repeatedly knead and fold the dough to help it cool and refine its texture. Let cool to room temperature before use.

6. **Divide and Fill**
   - For filled version: Divide dough into 30g portions. For plain version: Divide into 50g portions.
   - Flatten each portion, place a pre-rolled red bean paste ball in the center, and close the dough around it using a pushing motion with your thumb and forefinger. Ensure the seal is tight without cracks, then roll into a ball.
   - **Key Point**: Accurate portion weights are important; seal the filling tightly.

7. **Shape with Mold**
   - Place the filled dough ball into the mold, press gently to fit the shape, then press the mold handle against a flat surface to release the shaped cake. If no mold is available, shape into small squares by hand.
   - **Key Point**: Pressing on a flat surface ensures even shaping.

8. **Chill and Serve**
   - Refrigerate or freeze all finished mung bean cakes. They taste best after chilling—cold, refreshing, and melt-in-your-mouth, with enhanced flavor.

## Health Tips
This low-sugar mung bean cake uses milk and milk powder to partially replace sugar, making it suitable for those managing blood sugar or on a weight-loss diet. However, the butter and red bean paste filling are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat 1–2 pieces per serving. Best texture when refrigerated; not recommended for freezing.
