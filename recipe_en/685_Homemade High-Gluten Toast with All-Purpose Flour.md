# Homemade High-Gluten Toast with All-Purpose Flour
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g (main dough) + 70g (for washing gluten)
🥛 Milk powder 8g
🥚 Egg 1
🥛 Milk 115ml
🍞 Dry yeast 3g
🍬 Sugar 21g
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk 15g
🧈 Butter 30g (softened)
🧂 Salt 2g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for kneading and washing gluten)
⚙️ Stand mixer
🥢 Rolling pin
🍞 Lidded toast pan
♨️ Oven
🧺 Cooling rack
🎁 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. **Obtain pure gluten**: Take 70g all-purpose flour, add an appropriate amount of water to knead into a basic dough, and let it rest until the surface becomes smooth. Place the rested dough into clean water and repeatedly knead and wash, changing the water multiple times, until the water runs clear, resulting in yellow, elastic pure gluten.
**Note**: If the dough becomes smooth quickly after resting, it indicates gluten has formed; during washing, thoroughly remove the starch to obtain pure gluten.

2. **Knead the toast dough**: Place 250g all-purpose flour, milk powder, egg, milk, sugar, sweetened condensed milk, dry yeast, and pure gluten into the stand mixer. Knead until the dough comes together and can form a rough membrane. Add the softened butter and salt, and continue kneading until the dough can be stretched into a thin, translucent windowpane membrane.
**Key**: The windowpane membrane is the foundation for a soft and shreddable toast; it must be kneaded to full development.

3. **Initial dividing and resting**: Divide the kneaded dough into 3 equal portions, round each portion, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest to fully relax the dough.
4. **First rolling**: Take one dough portion, press to release air, roll it into a long strip with a rolling pin, roll it up from one end, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 15 minutes.
5. **Second rolling**: Roll the rested dough log out again to release air, shape it into a long strip, roll it up from one end, and pinch the seam tightly.
**Note**: Make sure to pinch the seam securely to prevent it from opening during proofing and baking.

6. **Final proofing**: Place the rolled dough pieces into the lidded toast pan, cover with the lid, and proof until the dough rises to 80% full of the pan.
**Judgment**: Once it reaches 80% capacity, it is ready for the oven; over-proofing will cause the toast to collapse.

7. **Baking**: Preheat the oven, place the pan inside, and bake at 150°C for 35 minutes.
8. **Remove and cool**: Immediately unmold the toast after baking, and place it on a cooling rack to cool completely.
**Note**: Unmolding promptly prevents the toast from shrinking; the texture is better after it has fully cooled.

## Health Tips
Homemade gluten increases the chewiness of the toast, making it suitable for those who enjoy a Q-elastic texture. However, the process of washing gluten requires patience. Gluten provides high protein and low fat, helping to increase satiety, though butter and sweetened condensed milk are still relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with vegetables and fruits for balanced nutrition.
