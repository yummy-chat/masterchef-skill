# Farmhouse Stir-fried Pork

Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (适量, skin removed, sliced thinly against the grain)
🌶️ Green peppers (line peppers/Erjingtiao peppers) (适量, cut into rolling chunks)
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies 2-3 (cut into diagonal sections)
🧄 Garlic 5-6 cloves (crushed, single-clove garlic recommended)
🧈 Lard 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt (a little, added in two batches)
🥣 Dark soy sauce (a little)
🧂 MSG (a little)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Chinese wok (stir-fry pan)
🥄 Soup spoon
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/container
🔥 Gas stove

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the skin from the pork belly and slice thinly against the grain. Cut the green peppers into rolling chunks, and slice the bird's eye chilies diagonally. Crush the garlic and set aside.
2. Heat the wok over high heat without adding oil. Add the green pepper chunks and dry-fry over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until the skin wrinkles and charred spots appear. Season with a little salt, then remove and set aside.
**Note**: Dry-frying removes the raw, astringent taste of the peppers and enhances their aroma, which is key to achieving *wok hei*.
3. Add lard to the wok. Once hot, add the pork belly slices and stir-fry slowly over medium-low heat for 5-8 minutes until the slices are completely curled, the edges are lightly golden, and the fat has fully rendered.
**Note**: Slow stir-frying over low heat is essential. If too much oil renders out, you can pour off some.
4. Add a little salt and an appropriate amount of dark soy sauce to the fried pork. Stir-fry until evenly colored, then add the crushed garlic and fry until fragrant.
5. Add the dry-fried green peppers and bird's eye chilies. Stir-fry quickly until well combined. Add a little MSG to enhance the flavor, toss to mix, and remove from the wok immediately.
**Note**: The final stir-frying should be quick to keep the green peppers crisp. MSG should be added at the end to avoid prolonged high heat.

## Health Advice
This farmhouse stir-fried pork is rich in fat from the pork belly. It is recommended to serve with brown rice or leafy green vegetables to balance the nutrition. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost, but people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
