# Beef Meatballs
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Preparation Steps
1. Crush scallion and ginger with the back of a knife, place in a bowl, add cold water, and soak for at least 10 minutes to make scallion-ginger water.
2. Place ground beef in a basin. Gradually add scallion-ginger water and Huadiao wine, stirring continuously in one direction until the meat filling absorbs the liquid and becomes moist.
3. Add salt, white pepper powder, Sichuan pepper powder, chicken powder, and oyster sauce. Continue stirring until evenly combined.
4. Add water to adjust the consistency of the filling. Pour in the whole egg liquid, soaked cornstarch water, and flour. Continue stirring in the same direction until uniform.
**Key Point**: The combination of starch and flour is crucial for the meatballs' long-lasting crispy exterior.
5. Add salad oil and sesame oil. Mix by hand, repeatedly slapping the meat filling against the bowl to develop elasticity and cohesion until the filling becomes sticky and springy.
6. Pour sufficient cooking oil into the wok and heat until bubbles form rapidly when a chopstick is inserted. Lightly oil your palm, squeeze the filling through your thumb and index finger to form round balls, and scoop them into the oil with a spoon.
**Key Point**: Do not stir the meatballs immediately after adding them. Wait until the exterior sets and they float, then gently push them with a slotted spoon.
7. Once the meatballs are set, remove them. Reheat the oil to a higher temperature, return the meatballs for a second fry. Fry over high heat for about 1 minute, then reduce to medium heat until the meatballs turn a deep golden-red.
**Judgment Standard**: Shake the meatballs with a slotted spoon. If you hear a crisp rustling sound, the exterior is crunchy and the interior is fully cooked.
8. Remove, drain excess oil, and plate. The meatballs have an extremely crispy exterior and a tender, juicy interior. They can be eaten directly or frozen for later use in steaming, braising, or stewing.

## Health Advice
This beef meatball dish is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for muscle building or blood replenishment. However, as a deep-fried food, it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad for balanced nutrition and to control portion sizes per meal to avoid excessive calorie intake.
