# Stir-Fried Mushrooms and Eggplant

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Long eggplant, appropriate amount (peeled, core removed, sliced diagonally into thick pieces)
🍄 Mushrooms, about 10 pieces (washed, sliced into thick pieces)
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (sliced into thin pieces)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for soaking eggplant, plus 1 small pinch for seasoning)
🫒 Camellia oil, about 1 tablespoon (for stir-frying mushrooms)
🫒 Cooking oil, about 2-3 tablespoons (for stir-frying pork belly and eggplant)
🫘 Soybean paste, 1 spoon
🫘 Sweet bean paste, half a spoon
🧴 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (pour along the edge of the pan)
🍶 Cooking wine, a small amount (pour along the edge of the pan)
💧 Hot water, appropriate amount (just enough to cover the ingredients)
🍬 Sugar, 1 and a half spoons
🌶️ White pepper, 2 shakes
🥔 Potato starch, appropriate amount (mixed with water to make a slurry)
🧄 Raw garlic, minced, appropriate amount (add before removing from heat)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large wok
🔪 Chef's knife, wooden cutting board
🥄 Ladle
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Small bowl

## Cooking Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Peel the long eggplant, cut it in half lengthwise, use a knife to remove the soft core inside, then slice diagonally into thick pieces.
2. Place the sliced eggplant in a bowl, sprinkle with a pinch of salt and toss evenly, add water to soak for about ten minutes, then drain well.
   **Note**: Soaking in salted water prevents the eggplant from oxidizing and turning black, and removes astringency.
3. Wash the mushrooms and slice into thick pieces.
4. Slice the pork belly into thin pieces, mince the raw garlic into garlic mince, mix potato starch with water to make a slurry, and set aside.

**Cooking Stage**
1. Heat the wok, pour in camellia oil, add the mushroom slices, and stir-fry over medium heat until the mushrooms soften and shrink. Remove and set aside.
2. Without washing the wok, pour in cooking oil, add the pork belly slices, and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface of the meat is lightly charred and a lot of pork fat is rendered.
3. Add the drained eggplant slices, quickly toss the wok to coat the eggplant evenly with oil. If there is too much oil in the wok, use a spoon to remove some.
4. Reduce the heat to low, add soybean paste and sweet bean paste. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant at the bottom of the wok, then stir-fry evenly with the eggplant and meat slices.
   **Key point**: The sauce must be stir-fried slowly over low heat to avoid burning and to fully release its fragrance.
5. Keep the heat on low, pour dark soy sauce and cooking wine along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry evenly.
   **Key point**: Pouring along the edge of the wok can release a richer aroma.
6. Return the previously stir-fried mushrooms to the wok.
7. Add hot water, just enough to cover all the ingredients, then add sugar, salt, and white pepper, and stir evenly.
   **Note**: Hot water must be used to keep the meat tender.
8. Cover the wok with a lid, turn to medium heat, and braise for 7 minutes, allowing the eggplant and mushrooms to fully absorb the broth.
9. Remove the lid, turn to high heat to reduce the sauce, while constantly stirring with a ladle to prevent sticking. As the sauce thickens, taste and make final adjustments.
10. Pour in the potato starch slurry to thicken, while swirling the wok to coat the ingredients evenly, achieving the desired consistency.
11. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in the raw garlic mince, quickly stir to combine, and immediately plate and serve.
    **Key point**: Add raw garlic mince only after turning off the heat to maximize the garlic aroma and enhance the flavor of the dish.

## Health Tips
Mushrooms and eggplant are rich in dietary fiber, combined with the high-quality protein from pork belly. This dish is nutritionally balanced but relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with brown rice and control the amount of oil used. Suitable for those needing an energy boost, but should be consumed in moderation by those with high blood lipids.
