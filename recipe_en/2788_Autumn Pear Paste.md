# Autumn Pear Paste
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍐 Snow pear 20 jin (in-season pears, high water and sugar content)
🫒 Red dates 120 grams (pitted)
🌿 Luo Han Guo 2 pieces (broken into pieces)
🫚 Ginger 40 grams (sliced; omit if coughing due to lung heat)
⚪ Chuanbei 7 grams (added at the end, ground into powder)
Seasonings: No additional seasonings needed; do not add water or sugar.

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Peeler, kitchen knife, and cutting board
🍲 Stainless steel soup pot
🧽 Cheesecloth (or filter cloth)
🥣 Glass bowl (to collect pear juice)
🍳 Flat-bottom pan/wok
🫗 Grinding bowl and pestle
🫙 Glass jar (for storing the finished product)
🥄 Wooden spatula or long spoon (for stirring and preventing sticking)

## Steps
1. Wash and peel the snow pears, cut them open to remove the cores, and chop the pear flesh into small pieces.
2. Pit the red dates, slice the ginger, and break the Luo Han Guo into pieces. Separately grind the Chuanbei into a fine powder in the grinding bowl and set aside.
3. Place all the chopped snow pears, red dates, ginger, and Luo Han Guo into a stainless steel pot. **Do not add water**. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 30–40 minutes until the ingredients are soft and mushy. Turn off the heat and let cool.
4. Pour the cooled pear mixture into a strainer lined with cheesecloth, wrap tightly, and squeeze firmly to extract the pear juice. Discard the pear pulp.
5. Pour the extracted pear juice into a flat-bottom pan. Boil over high heat for 15–20 minutes. After it comes to a boil, skim off any foam from the surface to evaporate excess water.
6. Once the liquid has reduced noticeably, dissolve the Chuanbei powder in a small amount of cold water, then pour it into the pan and stir well.
   ⚠️ Do not add Chuanbei too early, as its medicinal properties may be overly diminished.
7. Reduce the heat to low, stirring constantly to prevent sticking to the bottom. Cook until the bubbles in the pot change from large, sparse bubbles to dense, small ones. When a spatula is lifted, the paste should form a continuous thread. A drop of the paste placed in cold water should solidify quickly into a lump.
8. Immediately pour the finished paste into a clean, dry glass jar while it is hot, and seal tightly for storage. After cooling, the paste will turn darker in color and become thicker in texture. To serve, dissolve it in warm water and drink.

## Health Tips
This Autumn Pear Paste is made by slow-simmering snow pears, red dates, Luo Han Guo, and Chuanbei. It is highly effective for moistening the lungs and relieving coughs, especially suitable for autumn dry coughs or throat discomfort. Note that those coughing due to lung heat can omit the ginger. No sugar is added throughout the process; the natural sweetness of the fruit is sufficient.
