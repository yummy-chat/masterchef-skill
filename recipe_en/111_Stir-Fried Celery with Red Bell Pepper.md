# Stir-Fried Celery with Red Bell Pepper
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Celery (main ingredient, remove tough outer strings and cut into shreds)
🌶️ Red bell pepper (as needed, for color, cut into shreds similar in thickness to celery)
🧂 Salt (a little, used for blanching and in the sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce (a fingertip’s worth, about 5-10 grams)
💧 Water (two tablespoons, about 30-40 ml)
🍬 Sugar (a fingertip’s worth, about 5 grams)
🌽 Cornstarch (a small pinch, for a light slurry)
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, a few drops for blanching, about 15-20 ml for stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic (1-2 cloves, minced into garlic paste)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔪 Vegetable peeler
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🥄 Spoon
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Wok spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Use a vegetable peeler to remove the tough outer strings from the celery, then cut it crosswise into thin shreds. Cut the red bell pepper into shreds similar in thickness to the celery. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Removing the tough strings is crucial to ensure a tender, non-fibrous texture.

2. In a small bowl, add salt, oyster sauce, water, sugar, and cornstarch. Stir until fully combined to make the sauce. Set aside.
   **Note**: Prepare the sauce in advance to ensure even seasoning and to prevent the cornstarch from settling when added to the wok.

3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add a little salt and a few drops of cooking oil. Quickly blanch the celery and red bell pepper shreds until their colors brighten, then immediately remove and plunge them into cold water to cool. Drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Adding oil and salt to the boiling water helps keep the vegetables vibrant green; blanch briefly, then cool quickly for a crisp texture.

4. Heat a wok over medium heat and add a moderate amount of oil. Sauté the minced garlic over low heat until fragrant. As soon as the aroma releases, immediately turn the heat to high and add the drained celery and red bell pepper shreds.
   **Note**: Once the garlic is fragrant, add the main ingredients right away to prevent the garlic from burning and turning bitter.

5. Pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat, tossing quickly. Once the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients, turn off the heat.

6. Transfer the stir-fried celery to a serving plate and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Celery is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, low in calories, and high in water content, making it suitable for weight management and blood sugar control. Quick blanching and stir-frying preserve its crisp texture and nutrients. Oyster sauce contains salt, so reduce the amount of added salt when using it; those with high blood pressure may omit the oyster sauce.
