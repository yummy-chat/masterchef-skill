# Kimchi (Pickled Vegetables)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August), Autumn (September–October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫘 Long beans (sword beans) – appropriate amount  
🥕 Carrots – appropriate amount  
🥬 Cabbage (head cabbage) – appropriate amount  
🥕 White radish – appropriate amount  
🌶️ Red chili peppers – appropriate amount  
🌿 Bay leaves – 2 pieces  
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns – a handful  
🧂 Edible salt – 120–130 grams  
🍬 Rock sugar – 15 grams  
🍶 White liquor – a little  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🏺 Glass pickling jar  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Wash all vegetables including long beans, carrots, white radish, cabbage, and red chili peppers thoroughly. Halve the cabbage and rinse again.  
   **Key point**: All ingredients must be thoroughly washed.  
2.  Place the washed ingredients in a ventilated area to fully air-dry surface moisture.  
   **Key point**: Must be completely dried; raw water can cause the pickles to spoil.  
3.  Clean the glass pickling jar and thoroughly dry the inside.  
   **Key point**: The container must be free of water and oil.  
4.  Add about 2 liters of clean water to a pot, then add 2 bay leaves, a handful of Sichuan peppercorns, and 120–130 grams of edible salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer. Turn off the heat once obvious white crystals appear at the pot’s edge, and let it cool completely before use.  
   **Key point**: The brine must be completely cooled before use.  
5.  Cut the dried long beans into segments about 2 cm long. Cut the carrots and white radish into strips or slices. Tear the cabbage into large pieces.  
   **Key point**: Cutting the beans into small pieces helps them absorb flavor more easily.  
6.  Pour a little white liquor into the dry glass jar, shake the jar so the liquor coats the inner wall evenly, then pour out the remaining liquor.  
   **Key point**: White liquor effectively sterilizes and prevents mold on the surface.  
7.  Layer all the cut vegetables into the jar in order, and place 15 grams of rock sugar on top.  
   **Key point**: Rock sugar gives the pickles a crisper texture.  
8.  Slowly pour the completely cooled pickling brine into the jar until all vegetables are fully submerged.  
9.  Tightly seal the jar’s inner and outer lids to ensure a good seal. Place the jar in a cool, dark place to pickle.  
   **Key point**: Avoid direct sunlight to prevent excessive temperature or light from affecting fermentation; in hot conditions like summer, it is ready to eat after about 7 days.  
10. After pickling is complete, use clean, oil-free chopsticks to remove the pickles for consumption.

## Health Advice  
Pickled vegetables are rich in probiotics and dietary fiber, helping to stimulate appetite and aid digestion in summer. Due to their high salt content, individuals with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation. The entire process must be kept free of oil and water to prevent spoilage.
