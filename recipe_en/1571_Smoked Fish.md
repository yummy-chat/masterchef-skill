# Smoked Fish
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Carp, approximately 1500g (one 3+ jin fish)
🫚 Ginger, 10g (5g for marinating fish, 5g for making sauce)
🧅 Scallion, 25g (10g for marinating fish, 15g for making sauce)
🌿 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
⭐ Star anise, 1 piece (use half if large)
🌳 Cinnamon stick, 0.5g
🤍 Cardamom, 3 grains (optional)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, 2g
🌱 Fennel seeds, 1g
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 5g (optional)
🧂 White pepper powder, 0.5g
🧂 Salt, 1g (about 1/3 teaspoon)
🍶 Cooking wine / Shaoxing wine, 10g
🍬 Rock sugar, 150g (can reduce to 50g if strongly averse to sweetness)
🧴 Light soy sauce (low sodium), 150g (if using regular soy sauce, reduce to 80-100g)
🧴 Dark soy sauce, 10g
🥫 Seafood sauce, 40g (can substitute with oyster sauce or maltose)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 3g
💧 Water, 500g
🧂 Five-spice powder, 1.5g (optional)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok / Iron pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🔪 Chef’s knife
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🪵 Cutting board
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥄 Spoon
🧻 Paper towels

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. **Process the fish**: Scrape off scales and surface slime, remove innards and blood inside the belly, cut off all fins and tail, rinse thoroughly, and drain.
2. **Cut fish into pieces**: Lay paper towels on the cutting board to prevent slipping. Cut off the head for other uses; dry the fish body, and cut into large pieces about two fingers wide. For thicker parts, cut along the backbone to split. Use the heel of the knife to press down for easier cutting.
3. **Marinate the fish pieces**: Smash ginger and scallion with a knife blade, place in a large bowl, add white pepper powder, salt, and cooking wine. Squeeze with your hands to release juice. Add fish pieces, mix thoroughly, and marinate to infuse flavor.
4. **Prepare sauce ingredients**: Slice scallion diagonally into sections, slice ginger; prepare all dry spices. Combine rock sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, seafood sauce, chicken bouillon, and 500g water in a basin. Stir until rock sugar mostly dissolves to make the sauce mixture.
### Making the Sauce
1. Add a small amount of oil to the wok. Place scallion sections and ginger slices into cold oil. Stir-fry over high heat until slightly yellow and fragrant.
2. Add all dry spices, stir-fry quickly for a few times, then pour in the prepared sauce mixture. Dried chili peppers can be added according to taste.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Keep the lid off and simmer for 15 minutes.
4. Use a slotted spoon to remove all solid residues. Turn to high heat and reduce the sauce for about 4 minutes, stirring constantly.
**State check**: When you scoop up the sauce with a spoon, it should coat the back of the spoon and have a thick, syrupy consistency.
5. Turn off the heat. After the surface foam dissipates, pour the sauce into a bowl. Stir in five-spice powder while hot, then let it cool completely before use.
### Frying the Fish
1. **First fry**:
   - Pour enough oil into the wok. Heat over high heat to about 210°C. Test with a dry scallion leaf – it should quickly float to the surface when the oil is ready.
   - Pat each marinated fish piece dry with paper towels.
   - Over medium-high heat, place fish pieces one by one along the edge of the wok, without overlapping.
**Key point**: Do not stir the fish pieces for the first 2 minutes after adding. Once set, gently separate them.
   - Fry for about 6–7 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and set. Remove and drain oil.
2. **Second fry**:
   - After removing all fish pieces, increase the heat to high and raise oil temperature to about 240°C.
   - Add all fish pieces at once. Keep at maximum heat and re-fry for about 2 minutes, until deep golden and crispy. Remove quickly.
**Note**: Do not extend the re-frying time to avoid burning, and be careful of oil splatters.

### Soaking for Flavor
Immediately place the hot fried fish pieces into the completely cooled sauce. Soak for about 3 minutes, turning occasionally so each side is evenly coated. Remove and plate. Tastes better when served cold.

## Health Advice
Smoked fish is high in sugar and oil. It is recommended to consume 2–3 pieces per serving with light vegetables. Diabetic patients should be cautious. Carp is rich in high-quality protein, but double frying increases calories. An air fryer can be used instead to reduce oil intake.
