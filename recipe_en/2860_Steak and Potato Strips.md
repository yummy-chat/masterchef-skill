# Steak and Potato Strips
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef (lean meat with a little fat) – 1 piece  
🥔 Potatoes – 2 pieces  
🌿 Herbs (cilantro or parsley) – to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine – 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Light soy sauce – 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Ground pepper – to taste (for marinating and finishing)  
🧂 Cornstarch – 1 tablespoon  
🫒 Cooking oil – 1 tablespoon (for marinating) + as needed (for frying)  
🦪 Oyster sauce – 1 teaspoon  
🌶️ Chili powder – to taste (optional)  
🧂 Freshly ground pepper / cheese powder – to taste (for garnish, optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥣 Bowl  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥢 Tongs or clip  
🥄 Spoon  
🧻 Kitchen paper  

## Instructions
1. Thaw the beef in advance, and cut it into strips along the grain.
2. Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, ground pepper, cornstarch, and cooking oil to the beef strips. Mix until the cornstarch is fully dissolved, then let it marinate for 20 minutes.
3. Peel the potatoes and cut them into strips.
4. Heat the frying pan over medium heat, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, and fry the potato strips. Sprinkle with a little ground pepper for aroma. Fry until the surface is lightly browned, then remove and set aside.
5. Without cleaning the pan, turn the heat to medium-high and add the marinated beef strips. Fry until the surface is browned and a charred aroma appears. If liquid releases from the beef, turn the heat to high to reduce it, and add a little more oil if needed.
6. Return the fried potato strips to the pan. Pour in any remaining marinade from the bowl, add oyster sauce, and stir-fry until everything is evenly coated with the sauce.
7. Plate the dish, sprinkle with chopped cilantro or parsley, and top with freshly ground pepper or cheese powder as desired.

## Health Tips  
Steak and Potato Strips offer balanced nutrition: beef provides high-quality protein and iron, while potatoes supply carbohydrates and potassium. It’s ideal for muscle building or as a lunch energy boost. When frying, use less oil, and those with high blood pressure should reduce salt intake.
