# Fried Milk  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 Whole milk 500ml  
🍬 White sugar 25g  
🌽 Cornstarch 50g (for milk paste)  
🧀 Cheese slices 2 slices (optional)  
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten into egg wash)  
🍞 Breadcrumbs as needed  
🌽 Cornstarch as needed (for coating)  
🫗 Cooking oil as needed (for deep frying)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🔥 Portable gas stove or stovetop  
🥣 Round metal bowl or flat container  
🛡️ Plastic wrap or plastic bag  
🔪 Knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🌡️ Oil thermometer (optional)  
♨️ Oven or air fryer (for non-fried version)  

## Steps  
1. Pour milk, 25g sugar, and 50g cornstarch into a dry, oil-free non-stick pan. You can add cheese slices. **Do not turn on the heat yet**. Stir with a spatula until smooth and no lumps remain.  
2. Turn on medium heat and continuously stir the milk paste. Be sure to scrape the bottom and sides of the pan to prevent localized overcooking.  
   - **Correct consistency check**: When the spatula passes through, clear lines appear and slowly disappear. When scooped up, the paste falls in clumps slowly.  
   - **Beginner tip**: Take a small amount of the paste and let it sit in a bowl for 1 minute. If it solidifies into a bouncy texture similar to "rice tofu," it’s ready. If it is still thin and soft, return it to the pan to heat and stir further.

3. Pour the cooked milk paste into a container lined with plastic wrap or a plastic bag. Smooth the surface. Let it cool, then seal and refrigerate for at least 3 hours until fully set and springy to the touch.  
4. Remove the milk jelly from the mold and cut into evenly sized pieces. Prepare three plates with cornstarch, egg wash, and breadcrumbs. Coat each milk piece first lightly with cornstarch, then dip in egg wash, and finally coat thoroughly with breadcrumbs.  
5. Choose a cooking method based on your equipment:  
   - **Oven version**: Preheat to 190°C. Bake for about 15 minutes until the surface is golden and the pieces puff up slightly.  
   - **Air fryer version**: Cook at 190°C for 10 minutes until the surface is crispy and golden brown.  
   - **Deep-fried version**: Heat oil to about 180°C. Fry over medium heat until the coating is golden and crispy, then remove and drain excess oil.

## Health Advice  
This fried milk is high in calcium and sugar. It is suitable for occasional indulgence, but diabetics and those on a weight loss diet should be cautious. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the greasiness, and to limit serving size to 3–4 pieces at a time.
