# Passion Fruit Sour Soup Beef
Cuisine: Yunnan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef tenderloin/round steak 300g (thinly sliced)  
🍈 Golden passion fruit 3 (flesh scooped out)  
🍅 Tomatoes 3 (2 peeled and diced, 1 cut into wedges)  
🍋 Lime 1 (sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro to taste (added at the end)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (some minced for marinating meat, some minced for sautéing)  
🌶️ Pickled chili/chopped chili to taste  
🥄 Soy sauce 2 tbsp for marinating, a little for the broth  
🥄 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🌫️ White pepper powder to taste  
🥡 Cornstarch 1.5 tbsp  
🥚 Egg white half or 1  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍬 Sugar to taste (to balance the acidity)  
🐟 Fish sauce to taste (for a finishing touch of umami)  
🫒 Vegetable oil to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🔥 Stove  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Slice the beef thinly, add 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, white pepper powder, 1 clove minced garlic, half an egg white, and 1.5 tbsp cornstarch. Mix well with your hands and marinate.  
**Key point**: Marinating makes the beef tender and flavorful.  
2. Score a cross on the bottom of 2 tomatoes, blanch in boiling water, peel, and dice finely. Cut the remaining tomato into wedges. Halve the passion fruit and scoop out the flesh.  
3. Heat a clay pot with a little oil, add sliced ginger and minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add pickled chili/chopped chili to taste and stir-fry until aromatic.  
4. Add the diced tomatoes, stir-fry over high heat until they soften and break down into a thick, sandy paste.  
**Key point**: The tomatoes breaking down into a paste is key to a rich broth.  
5. Add an appropriate amount of water, pour in the passion fruit flesh, season with salt, a little soy sauce, sugar, and fish sauce. Stir, bring to a boil, taste, and adjust seasoning.  
6. Drop the marinated beef slices one by one into the boiling soup, cook for about 1 minute, then turn off the heat.  
**Note**: Do not overcook the beef. Let it finish cooking in the residual heat of the clay pot to keep it tender and smooth.  
7. Immediately after turning off the heat, add the tomato wedges, lime slices, and cilantro. Let the residual heat release their aroma.  
**Key point**: Cilantro pairs particularly well with the flavor of this dish.  

## Health Advice  
This passion fruit sour soup beef is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene. It is tangy and appetizing, suitable for those with poor summer appetite or needing protein supplementation. Be careful not to overcook the beef; letting it finish in residual heat keeps it tender. Those with excessive stomach acid are advised to limit consumption of the broth.
