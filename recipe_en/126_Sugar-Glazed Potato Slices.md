# Sugar-Glazed Potato Slices
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes – to taste (choose medium-sized ones, slice into rounds about 3 mm thick)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – to taste (shredded)  
🧅 Scallions – to taste (use the white part, shredded)  
🫚 Ginger – to taste (shredded)  
🧄 Garlic – to taste (sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro – to taste (cut into sections)  
🫘 Roasted sesame seeds – to taste  
🧂 Salt – to taste (for blanching)  
🫒 Cooking oil – sufficient amount (generous oil, enough to submerge the potato slices)  
🧴 Light soy sauce – to taste  
🍶 Rice vinegar – to taste (reserve a little for drizzling before removing from heat)  
🍬 White sugar – to taste  
🌰 Sesame oil – a few drops  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍲 Boiling pot  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Seasoning spoon  
🔗 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash and peel the potatoes, then cut into uniform rounds about 3 mm thick.  
**Note**: Ensure even thickness for consistent doneness during cooking.  

2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add an appropriate amount of salt, then blanch the potato slices. Cook until they turn semi-transparent, then immediately remove and drain.  
**Note**: Do not soak the slices in advance; blanch them directly.  

3. In a separate pot, heat a generous amount of cooking oil over high heat. Add the blanched potato slices and deep-fry until most of the moisture has evaporated, the surface turns an even golden-red, and the texture becomes crispy. Remove and drain excess oil. Set aside.  
**Note**: Continuously stir the slices to ensure even heating.  

4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the shredded dried chili peppers and fry over low heat until they change color and release their aroma.  
**Note**: Do not use high heat to avoid burning.  

5. Add the shredded scallions, shredded ginger, and sliced garlic. Stir-fry quickly until fragrant.  
6. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, then add rice vinegar, white sugar, and a few drops of sesame oil.  
**Note**: Use the heat of the wok to release the aroma when adding the soy sauce.  

7. Simmer the sauce over medium-low heat, stirring continuously until it becomes thick, glossy, and can coat a spoon.  
8. Quickly add the fried potato slices to the sauce and stir-fry rapidly to evenly coat each slice with the sauce.  
**Note**: Work quickly to maintain the crispiness of the potato slices.  

9. Turn off the heat, drizzle a little rice vinegar along the edge of the wok, sprinkle with cilantro sections and roasted sesame seeds, and toss to combine evenly. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and an appropriate amount of protein to avoid excessive intake of sugar and oil mixtures. Suitable for occasional indulgence; not recommended for diabetics.
