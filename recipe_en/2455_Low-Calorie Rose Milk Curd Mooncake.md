# Low-Calorie Rose Milk Curd Mooncake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Year Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 12 scoops of Yili Xinhuo Fiber-Added Milk Powder (mixed at a ratio of 1 scoop powder to 125ml water)  
💧 1500ml warm water (approximately 50°C)  
🌹 0-sugar rose jam, as needed (can be substituted with bean paste or lotus seed paste)  
🍋 40g white vinegar or lemon juice  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥛 Glass measuring cup/pitcher  
🍳 Saucepan  
🧺 Cheesecloth/strainer  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥮 Mooncake mold  
🪵 Wooden tray/plate  

## Instructions  
1. Mix the milk powder with warm water at 50°C according to the ratio, stirring thoroughly until completely dissolved to form milk liquid.  
2. Pour the milk liquid into a saucepan and heat over the lowest heat until warm, keeping the surface gently moving but not boiling. Maintain low heat throughout; excessive temperature will cause subsequent curdling to fail or result in a rough texture.  
3. Add the white vinegar to the warm milk in small batches, observing as you go, until the milk visibly separates into white curds and pale yellow whey, then stop.  
4. Pour the mixture into a strainer lined with cheesecloth, filtering out the whey, and wrap the curds in the cheesecloth.  
5. Gently squeeze the cheesecloth bundle by hand to remove excess moisture until no large amount of liquid drips out, yielding solid milk curd. The degree of squeezing directly affects mooncake shaping; if too wet, it will be difficult to mold.  
6. Divide the milk curd dough into evenly sized portions, roll them into balls, and set aside.  
7. Take one portion and press it into a wrapper in your palm, place an appropriate amount of rose jam in the center. Use the tiger’s mouth (the webbing between thumb and index finger) to slowly push the wrapper upward to seal, until the filling is completely enclosed, then roll into a ball. The wrapper thickness should be moderate, the filling not excessive, and work gently to prevent leakage.  
8. Place the wrapped ball into the mooncake mold, gently press the handle to shape, then release from the mold and arrange on a plate.  
9. Sprinkle edible dried rose petals on the surface or around the mooncake for decoration.  

## Health Advice  
This low-calorie rose milk curd mooncake uses homemade milk curd to replace traditional high-fat crust, suitable for people controlling sugar and reducing fat. Note that the lemon juice must be added in batches to ensure successful curdling. The texture is more refreshing after refrigeration. Do not use high heat when heating the milk, as it will cause protein curds to form.
