# Creamy Crisps
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Whipping cream 50g
🍬 Sugar 35g
🥚 Egg white 35g
🌾 Cake flour 35g
🍵 Matcha powder 4g (substitute part of the cake flour when making matcha flavor)
🍫 Cocoa powder 4g (substitute part of the cake flour when making chocolate flavor)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Spoon
🔍 Flour sifter
💼 Piping bag
🥠 Macaron mold (35-holes, optional)
🍳 Regular baking sheet
📄 Parchment paper
♨️ Oven

## Instructions
1.  Pour the whipping cream and sugar into a mixing bowl, stir until the sugar is completely dissolved, and the mixture becomes slightly thick, resembling yogurt.
2.  Add the egg white, stir thoroughly until evenly combined.
3.  Sift the cake flour into the bowl. Sifting is necessary to prevent lumps.
4.  Mix all ingredients evenly until the batter is thick but pourable, with lines that disappear slowly when drizzled. If the batter is too thick, add a little more whipping cream as needed; if too thin, adjust by adding more cake flour.
5.  To make other flavors, replace 4g of cake flour with an equal amount of matcha powder or cocoa powder, and follow the same method.
6.  Transfer the prepared batter into a piping bag, snip a small opening at the tip, and pipe it onto the macaron mold or a regular baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Each round should be about the size of a one-yuan coin.
7.  Place the baking sheet with piped batter into the oven. When using the mold, bake at 160°C for about 16 minutes; when using parchment paper, bake at 160°C for about 6 minutes, until the edges of the cookies are lightly golden.
   **Note**: The freshly baked cookies are soft. They will only become crispy after completely cooling.
8.  Remove from the oven. Wait until fully cooled, hard, and crisp, then remove from the mold or parchment paper. Be sure to wait until completely cooled before removing, otherwise they will break easily.

## Health Advice
These creamy crisps are high in sugar and whipping cream, making them a high-calorie snack suitable for occasional indulgence rather than daily consumption. The protein comes from a small amount of egg white, so the nutritional value is limited. It is recommended to pair them with fruit or tea to balance the sweetness; ensure they are completely cooled before removing to avoid breakage.
