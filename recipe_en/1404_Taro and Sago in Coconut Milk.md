# Taro and Sago in Coconut Milk
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients  
🍠 Taro: about 1/3 of a large taro (peeled and cut into chunks)  
🍠 Purple sweet potato: about 1 (peeled and cut into chunks, for color, optional)  
🍚 Sago: about 1/3 bowl  
🥥 Coconut milk: to taste (can be substituted with milk, but will lose the coconut aroma)  
🍬 Sugar: to taste (adjust according to personal preference)  

## Kitchenware  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥄 Ladle  

## Instructions  
1. Peel the taro and purple sweet potato, then cut into evenly sized chunks.  
2. Place the taro chunks and purple sweet potato chunks into the rice cooker inner pot, and add about 1/3 bowl of sago.  
3. Pour in enough boiling water.  
4. Cover the lid and start the "Steam/Cook" function, cooking for about 45 minutes, or until the rice cooker automatically switches to the keep-warm setting.  
5. Open the lid and check: the sago should be completely transparent with no white center. If there are still white centers, cover the lid and let it rest using residual heat until fully transparent.  
6. Use a ladle to remove excess liquid to make the dessert thicker.  
7. Add sugar and coconut milk, stir well, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Taro is rich in dietary fiber and potassium, suitable for those with poor digestion or needing potassium supplementation; sago is pure carbohydrates with a high glycemic index, so diabetic patients should consume it in moderation. Coconut milk is high in calories; substituting with skimmed milk can reduce the calorie burden.
