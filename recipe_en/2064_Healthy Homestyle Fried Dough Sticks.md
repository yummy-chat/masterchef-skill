# Healthy Homestyle Fried Dough Sticks
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 2
🌾 Cake flour 500g (about 1 lb)
🥛 Whole milk 125g (half a carton)
💧 Water 150ml
🥄 Active dry yeast 5g
🧪 Baking powder 5g
🧂 Baking soda 5g
🧂 Salt 5g
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed (5g for dough, large amount for frying)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥢 Chopsticks (multiple pairs)
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥢 Long chopsticks (or frying chopsticks)
🍳 Deep frying pan
🛡️ Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1.  Sift the cake flour into a bowl. Add the eggs, active dry yeast, baking powder, and baking soda. Stir slightly with chopsticks.
2.  Gradually pour in the milk and water, stirring with chopsticks until it forms a flaky mixture. Then add the salt and knead into a dough by hand.
   **Key Point**: The dough should be as soft as pancake dough. Adjust the amount of water slightly according to the flour's absorbency.
3.  Add about 5 grams of vegetable oil to the dough. Repeatedly press and fold the dough with the back of your hand until the oil is fully incorporated and the dough is moist.
   **Note**: This step is key for creating flaky layers.
4.  Lightly brush the surface of the dough with oil, place it back in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for about 2 hours, until the dough is very soft and pliable.
5.  Sprinkle dry flour on a cutting board. Take out the rested dough and gently roll it into a thick sheet.
6.  Cut the dough sheet into strips about 3 cm wide. Stack two strips together and press firmly in the center lengthwise with a chopstick to make them stick together.
   **Key Point**: This is the traditional technique for shaping the dough sticks to ensure they expand when fried.
7.  Pour enough oil into a pan and heat over medium heat until it reaches about 70% of its smoke point.
   **Note**: Oil temperature is critical for puffing; it should not be too low or too high.
8.  Take one dough piece, hold both ends, gently stretch it to a suitable length, and carefully place it into the hot oil.
   **Tip**: Lower the dough gently to prevent splashing hot oil.
9.  Continually turn the dough sticks with long chopsticks for even cooking. Fry until they are uniformly golden brown and the exterior is firm.
   **Indicator**: The dough sticks will quickly puff up and float. Turn them frequently for a plump shape.
10. Remove with chopsticks, drain excess oil, and arrange on a plate.

## Health Tips
This version of fried dough sticks replaces traditional alum with milk and eggs, increasing protein and calcium content, making it suitable for occasional consumption. However, fried foods are high in calories, so it is recommended to pair them with soy milk or a coarse grain porridge to balance the meal.
