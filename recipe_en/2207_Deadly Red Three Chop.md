# Deadly Red Three Chop

Cuisine: Yunnan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Green peppers (screw peppers/line peppers, choose according to spiciness preference)
🍅 Tomatoes, about 2
🥩 Ground pork, to taste
🧅 Scallions (chopped scallions)
🫚 Ginger (minced ginger)
🧄 Garlic (minced garlic)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (used in two separate additions)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (used in two separate additions)
🍶 Cooking wine, a splash
🌽 Cornstarch, a pinch
🍾 Light soy sauce, a small amount
💧 Water, a splash

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating ground meat and blanching tomatoes)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden spatula/wok spatula

## Instructions
1. **Prepare green peppers**: Wash the green peppers, cut into small cubes, then chop a bit more finely to allow them to blend with the ground meat.
**Key point**: Choose the green pepper variety based on desired spiciness, such as screw peppers.
2. **Prepare tomatoes**: Score a cross on the top of each tomato, place in a bowl, and pour boiling water over them for 30 seconds. Remove, peel, and cut into small cubes.
**Key point**: Be careful not to burn yourself; peeling gives a finer texture.
3. **Marinate ground meat**: Add a splash of water, a small pinch of salt, a small pinch of sugar, a splash of cooking wine, and a pinch of cornstarch to the ground pork. Mix thoroughly with your hands and let it rest.
**Key point**: Mix well to allow the meat to absorb the water and seasonings, resulting in a tender texture.
4. **Prepare aromatics**: Have the chopped scallions, minced ginger, and minced garlic ready.
5. **Fry aromatics**: Heat the wok, add cold oil, then add the chopped scallions, minced ginger, and minced garlic. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
**Key point**: Using cold oil in a hot wok prevents sticking.
6. **Cook the ground meat**: Add the marinated ground meat, quickly break it apart, and stir-fry until completely changed in color.
**Key point**: Break the meat apart quickly to avoid clumping.
7. **Cook the tomatoes**: Drizzle in a small amount of light soy sauce, add the tomato cubes, and continue stir-frying until the tomatoes release their juice and combine with the meat. Season with a small pinch of salt and sugar.
8. **Add green peppers**: Add the chopped green peppers, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds until the peppers are just cooked but still crisp.
**Key point**: Do not overcook the green peppers; keep them crisp and bright green.
9. **Serve and plate**: Remove from the wok and serve. Pairs excellently with rice.

## Health Tips
Tomatoes are rich in vitamin C, and green peppers contain capsaicin; together they provide antioxidant synergy. However, adding starch and sugar when marinating the meat may slightly raise the glycemic index. Diabetics are advised to consume in small amounts with mixed grains rice. For those watching their weight, although chopped green peppers are low in calories, they should not be eaten in excess.
