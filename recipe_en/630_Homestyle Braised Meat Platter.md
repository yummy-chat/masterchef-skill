# Homestyle Braised Meat Platter
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦆 Duck Wings 6-8 pieces  
🦆 Duck Feet 6-8 pieces  
🦆 Duck Head 1 piece  
🦆 Duck Neck 1 piece  
🥚 Quail Eggs 10 pieces  
🥒 Lotus Root 1 section (sliced)  
🫘 Dried Tofu 2 pieces  
🫘 Fried Tofu Puffs 3 pieces  

🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers to taste  
🌿 Cumin to taste  
⭐ Star Anise 2-3 pieces  
🌳 Cinnamon Stick 1 small piece  
🍃 Bay Leaves 3-4 pieces  
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorns to taste  
🌸 Angelica Dahurica 1 slice  
🌿 Sichuan Pepper to taste  
🫘 Cardamom 1 piece  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce 3 tbsp  
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce 1 tbsp  
🍬 Rock Sugar approx. 30g (for caramelizing)  
🫘 Soybean Paste 1 tbsp  
🫒 Cooking Oil a little  
🍶 Cooking Wine approx. 20ml  
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍺 Beer approx. 200ml (can substitute with water)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥘 Saucepan (for blanching)  
♨️ Pressure Cooker (for braising)  
🍳 Wok (for caramelizing sugar)  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  

## Steps  
1. Wash the duck wings, feet, head, and neck. Trim the nails from the feet and head, and thoroughly clean the interior of the duck head to remove impurities.  
2. Soak all dried spices in warm water for 10 minutes to remove bitterness.  
3. Prepare the braising sauce: Mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rock sugar, soybean paste, and a little cooking oil until well combined. Set aside.  
4. Place the duck parts in a pot of cold water, add ginger slices and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium heat and blanch for about 5 minutes. Remove and rinse under cold water until clean.  
5. Place the duck parts into the pressure cooker. Add the soaked spices, braising sauce, ginger slices, salt, and beer. In a separate wok, add a small amount of water and rock sugar. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar turns a deep red color and forms dense small bubbles. Remove from heat and immediately pour it into the pressure cooker.  
   **Note**: When caramelizing the sugar, keep stirring over medium-low heat to prevent burning and bitterness.  
6. Add enough hot water to the pressure cooker to cover the ingredients. Close the lid. Once steam pressure builds, cook for 15–20 minutes until the duck parts are tender and flavorful.  
7. After natural pressure release, open the lid. Add the quail eggs, dried tofu, fried tofu puffs, and lotus root slices. Cook uncovered over medium-low heat for another 10–15 minutes, allowing the side ingredients to fully absorb the braising liquid.  
8. Remove all braised items, arrange on a platter, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This braised platter is rich in protein and calcium, but also high in sodium. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables. People with high blood pressure should control their portion size. Although duck parts are delicious, they should not be consumed in excess.
