# Stir-fried Clams with Loofah and Mushrooms
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦪 Fresh live clams approx. 250g (video mentions buying for 13 yuan)
🍄 White mushrooms (Agaricus bisporus) approx. 150g (stemmed and sliced)
🥒 Loofah (sponge gourd) 2 pieces (peeled and cut into rolling knife chunks)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (mashed, for removing fishy smell)
🧄 Garlic 5–6 cloves (sliced)
🧂 Salt to taste
🫒 Cooking oil a little (for blanching loofah)
🥓 Lard a little more (for stir-frying base and mushrooms)
🍬 White sugar a pinch
🌫️ White pepper a pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce a little
🫗 Sesame oil a little (drizzle before removing from heat)
🌊 Water starch appropriate amount (cornstarch mixed with water)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer, soup spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔥 Stove
🥣 Bowl, chopsticks

## Steps
1. **Prepare clams**: Wash live clams clean. Add a small amount of water to the wok, add the mashed ginger, pour in the clams, and cover with a lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for about 2 minutes until all clam shells open. Remove the clams, reserving the broth at the bottom of the wok. Remove the clam meat from the shells, trim off the black fringe, and set aside.
2. **Blanch loofah**: In another wok, bring water to a boil, add a pinch of salt and a little cooking oil, then add the loofah chunks. Blanch over high heat for about 1 minute, then remove. Adding salt and oil helps maintain the vibrant green color of the loofah.
3. **Stir-fry base and mushrooms**: Place the wok over heat, add a generous amount of lard. When the oil is slightly warm, fry the ginger slices and garlic slices over low heat until fragrant. Add the mushroom slices, turn to medium heat, and stir-fry until the mushrooms release their moisture and shrink noticeably in size, becoming soft and wilted.
4. **Combine and season**: Add the blanched loofah and prepared clam meat to the wok. Stir-fry evenly. Season with salt, white sugar, white pepper, and oyster sauce.
5. **Braise and thicken**: Pour in the reserved clam broth, cover the wok, and braise over medium heat for about 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Remove the lid; if there is too much liquid, reduce it quickly over high heat. Then stir in the water starch to thicken the sauce until it coats the ingredients.
6. **Finish**: Just before turning off the heat, drizzle in a little sesame oil, toss well, and plate.

## Health Advice
The combination of loofah, mushrooms, and clams is low in fat, high in protein, and rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for clearing heat and promoting dampness in summer. However, clams are relatively high in purines, so those with gout should consume them in moderation. This dish uses lard to enhance flavor; people with high blood pressure, high blood lipids, or high blood sugar may reduce the amount or substitute with vegetable oil.
