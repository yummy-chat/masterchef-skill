# Sweet Osmanthus
Cuisine Category: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌼 Fresh edible osmanthus 50g (impurities removed, washed and air-dried)
🍬 White sugar 300g
💧 Drinking water 150g
🍋 Lemon juice 30g (freshly squeezed or bottled)
🧂 Salt 1g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥛 Saucepan
🌡 Food thermometer
🥄 Metal spoon
🫙 Glass jar with a tight seal

## Preparation Steps
1. Add white sugar and water to the saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 6 minutes. During the simmering process, use a food thermometer to monitor the temperature. When the syrup stabilizes at around 110℃ and reaches a slightly thick, flowing consistency, it is ready.
   **Key point**: This step is similar to making inverted sugar syrup; heat control is crucial. A temperature of 110℃ ensures the syrup has the right concentration.
2. Add the prepared osmanthus, lemon juice, and salt to the syrup. Bring to a boil again over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 4 minutes, maintaining the temperature at around 110℃. Stir gently during this time to ensure the osmanthus is evenly incorporated into the syrup.
   **Key point**: Lemon juice prevents the sugar from crystallizing. Insufficient heat or time may cause the final product to clump. After simmering, the syrup will turn a golden yellow color.
3. Allow the cooked sweet osmanthus to cool to room temperature. Then, transfer it to a dry, clean glass jar with a tight seal and store it sealed.
   **Note**: Refrigerating after sealing can extend the shelf life. Once opened, it is recommended to consume it as soon as possible.

## Health Advice
Although osmanthus contains sugar, the addition of lemon juice and salt helps balance the blood sugar response. Consuming it in moderation can help moisten dryness. It is recommended to pair it with clear tea or yam cake to avoid extra calories.
