# Garlic Pesto Pasta
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Sweet basil leaves 100g (use only the leaves, remove any blackened or oxidized parts)  
🧀 Parmesan cheese 60g (grated into fine shreds)  
🧄 Garlic (single-clove garlic) a few cloves (sliced)  
🌰 Pine nuts 20g  
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil approx. 150ml (for blending the pesto)  
🍝 Wide pasta appropriate amount  
🦐 Shrimp appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for blanching basil, cooking pasta, seasoning pesto, and pan-frying shrimp)  
⚫ Black pepper appropriate amount (for seasoning pesto and pan-frying shrimp)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥘 Saucepan  
🍳 Frying pan  
🌀 Blender/food processor  
🥄 Strainer  
🥣 Bowl (for ice water)  
🖐️ Kitchen paper  
🧀 Grater  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🥢 Tongs

## Cooking Steps
1. **Process the basil leaves**: Pick whole leaves and remove any blackened or oxidized parts. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add salt, then blanch the basil leaves for 2 minutes. Immediately remove and plunge into ice water to cool completely. Squeeze out excess water, then pat dry with kitchen paper.  
   **Key point**: Must ice bath after blanching; this is essential to prevent basil from oxidizing and turning black, keeping the color vibrant green.

2. **Prepare the ingredients**: Slice the garlic and grate the Parmesan cheese into fine shreds. Set aside.

3. **Blend the pesto**: Place the prepared basil leaves, pine nuts, grated cheese, and sliced garlic into the blender. Add appropriate salt and black pepper. Start the machine, and while blending, slowly pour in the extra virgin olive oil through the top opening. Continue blending until all ingredients form a smooth and creamy green sauce.  
   **Key point**: Must use extra virgin olive oil and add it while blending to achieve an emulsified smooth texture.

4. **Cook the pasta**: In another pot, bring water to a boil, add salt, then add the wide pasta. Cook according to the package instructions until desired doneness. Drain, reserving a small amount of pasta cooking water.

5. **Pan-fry the shrimp**: Heat a little olive oil in a frying pan. Add the shrimp, season with salt and black pepper, and cook until golden brown on both sides and fully cooked. Remove from the pan.

6. **Combine the pesto with pasta**: In the same pan used for the shrimp, add the cooked pasta along with a small amount of reserved pasta water. Toss to combine. Turn off the heat, then add 1-2 spoonfuls of pesto. Use tongs to quickly mix, ensuring the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce.  
   **Key point**: Add the pesto after turning off the heat to prevent high temperature from causing the sauce to oxidize and lose its color and flavor.

7. **Assembly and plating**: Plate the tossed pasta, arrange the pan-fried shrimp on top, sprinkle with a handful of pine nuts, and finish with a few shavings of Parmesan cheese. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Pesto is rich in antioxidants from basil and healthy fats, making it suitable for mental workers and those aiming to control weight. However, pesto is relatively high in calories, so pairing it with shrimp helps increase high-quality protein intake. When preparing, be sure to mix the pesto after turning off the heat to avoid high temperatures damaging its color and flavor.
