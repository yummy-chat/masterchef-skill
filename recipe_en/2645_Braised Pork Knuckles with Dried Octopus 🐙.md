# Braised Pork Knuckles with Dried Octopus 🐙
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork knuckles 2 (split in half, cleaned and hair-free)
🐙 Dried octopus 1 (key ingredient for umami)
🫚 Ginger about 3-4 slices (for blanching) + about 5-6 slices (for stir-frying)
🧅 Spring onions 2-3 stalks (for blanching to remove odor)
🧄 Garlic 4-5 cloves (flattened)
🧅 Dried shallots (red shallots) 3-4 (cut into chunks)
🌿 Chinese celery to taste (cut into sections, for final garnish)
🧄 Garlic chives to taste (cut into sections, for final garnish)
🌶️ Red bell pepper 1/4 (cut into chunks, for final garnish and color)
🍶 Rice wine to taste (for blanching + drizzling along the wok edge during stir-frying)
🫒 Cooking oil to适量 (for stir-frying aromatics)
🧀 Fermented red bean curd 2-3 pieces (mashed into a paste)
🥫 Chu hou paste about 1 tablespoon
💧 Hot water to taste (enough to submerge the pork knuckles)
🫗 Dark soy sauce about 1 tablespoon (for coloring)
🍬 Rock sugar about 3-4 small pieces
🧂 Salt to taste
🌽 Cornstarch a little (for thickening, optional)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for blanching, stir-frying, and braising)
🫕 Clay pot (for serving and keeping warm at the table)
🔥 Blowtorch (for roasting the dried octopus to remove odor and enhance aroma)
🥄 Spatula
✂️ Scissors (for cutting the softened octopus into small pieces)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Thoroughly burn off any remaining hair on the pork knuckles, then scrape and wash until no hair residue remains.
2.  Use a blowtorch to roast the dried octopus all over until the surface shows char marks and releases a fragrant smell, then wash it clean.
3.  Bring a pot of water to a boil, add ginger slices, spring onion sections, and a splash of rice wine. Once boiling, add the pork knuckles and blanch for about 1 minute. Remove and rinse off any surface scum.
4.  Heat another wok over high heat and add oil. One by one, add the ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried shallot chunks. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the aromatics are slightly browned at the edges and fragrant.
**Key Point**: This step is crucial for building the flavor base of the dish.
5.  Once the aromatics are fragrant, add the mashed fermented red bean curd and chu hou paste, and stir-fry evenly. Add the blanched pork knuckles and stir-fry until evenly coated with the sauce.
**Note**: Control the heat when stir-frying the sauce to prevent it from burning and sticking.
6.  Drizzle rice wine along the edge of the wok, then pour in hot water. Add dark soy sauce, rock sugar, and salt, and stir to combine. Add the cleaned dried octopus.
**Key Point**: Using hot water prevents the pork knuckles from shrinking; maintain a steady simmer over medium heat.
   **Heat and Timing**: Cover with a lid, reduce heat to medium, and braise for about 35 minutes, until the pork knuckles are tender and the sauce has thickened.
7.  Remove the lid and increase the heat to reduce the sauce until it is thick and glossy. If desired, drizzle in a little cornstarch slurry to thicken. Use scissors to cut the octopus into small pieces. Transfer the cooked pork knuckles, octopus, and sauce to a preheated clay pot. Garnish with celery sections, garlic chive sections, and red bell pepper chunks.
**Key Point**: The clay pot should be preheated so the dish continues to simmer at the table, enhancing the wok hei (breath of the wok) and heat retention.

## Health Tips
Pork knuckles are rich in collagen, making them suitable for those concerned with beauty and joint health. The dried octopus adds umami but is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Use less salt and sugar during braising, and pair with celery and garlic chives to balance the richness.
