# Zucchini Pan-Fried Dumpling Boxes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Zucchini – appropriate amount (shredded, then chopped)  
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (finely diced)  
🦐 Shrimp – appropriate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🥚 Eggs – appropriate amount (scrambled and broken into small pieces)  
🥟 Pre-made dumpling wrappers – appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount (used twice: for salting the zucchini and seasoning the filling)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon – appropriate amount  
🫗 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce – appropriate amount  
🌶 Ground pepper – appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil – appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (for brushing the pan and frying the boxes)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl (for salting the zucchini and mixing the filling)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for chopping ingredients)  
🍳 Non-stick pan (for frying the boxes)  
🥢 Chopsticks or spoon (for stirring the filling)  
🔗 Tongs (for flipping and picking up the cooked boxes)  

## Instructions  
1. Shred the zucchini into thin strips, place in a bowl, add about 1 spoonful of salt, mix well, and let sit for 10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Salting draws out moisture from the zucchini, removing any raw taste and preventing the filling from becoming watery later.  
2. After salting, firmly squeeze the zucchini shreds with your hands to remove as much liquid as possible, then chop the squeezed zucchini into small pieces.  
   **Key point**: Make sure to squeeze out all the excess moisture – this is crucial for the texture and appearance of the final dish.  
3. Finely dice the carrot, cut the shrimp into small pieces, and scramble the eggs, then break them into small crumbs.  
4. In a bowl, combine the squeezed and chopped zucchini, diced carrot, shrimp pieces, and scrambled egg crumbs. Add salt, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground pepper, and sesame oil. Mix thoroughly to form a filling.  
5. Take a pre-made dumpling wrapper and gently stretch it with your hands to make it larger. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold one side of the wrapper over the filling toward the center, then fold the other side over to meet it, creating a rectangular shape. Finally, flip the entire box over so the folded side is facing down.  
   **Key point**: The wrapper should be stretched larger; after folding, flipping it so the creased side is facing down helps the box hold its shape when frying.  
6. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into a non-stick pan. Once the oil is hot, place the assembled boxes into the pan with the creased side facing down. Fry over low heat throughout the entire process.  
7. When the bottom turns golden and set, flip the boxes and continue frying until the other side is also golden brown. Remove from the pan.  
   **Key point**: Fry over low heat to ensure the outer skin is crispy and the filling is fully cooked.  

## Health Tips  
This zucchini and shrimp-filled pan-fried box is moderate in calories, making it suitable for weight loss periods or for those who prefer a light, refreshing taste. Be sure to thoroughly squeeze the moisture out of the zucchini – otherwise, the boxes may break open and leak filling during frying.
