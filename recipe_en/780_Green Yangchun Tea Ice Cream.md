# Green Yangchun Tea Ice Cream
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍃 Green Yangchun Tea Leaves  Approx. 20g  
🥛 Milk  Appropriate amount (to be boiled with tea)  
🧁 Whipping Cream  Appropriate amount  
🥛 Whole Milk Powder  Appropriate amount  
🍬 White Granulated Sugar  Appropriate amount  
🍬 Glucose Powder  Appropriate amount  
🍪 Cookie Biscuits  Appropriate amount (for the base)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Small Saucepan  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🔪 Blender (Food Processor)  
🍦 Ice Cream Maker (requires pre-frozen inner bowl)  
🥄 Silicone Spatula  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl  
🧊 Airtight Container  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the Milk Tea Base**: Place the Green Yangchun tea leaves and milk in a small saucepan and heat until just simmering, then remove from heat.  
   **Key Point**: Control the heat while heating to prevent the milk from boiling over. Allow to cool after cooking, then let it steep for 1-2 hours or overnight for the tea and milk flavors to fully infuse.  
2. **Strain the Tea Liquid**: Strain the steeped milk tea liquid and discard the tea leaves, retaining only the liquid portion.  
3. **Mix the Ingredients**: Combine the strained milk tea liquid, whipping cream, whole milk powder, white granulated sugar, and glucose powder in a blender and blend until smooth and uniform.  
4. **Churn the Ice Cream**: Pour the mixture into the pre-frozen inner bowl of the ice cream maker and churn for about 30 minutes until a soft-serve ice cream consistency is reached.  
5. **Freeze to Set**: Transfer the churned soft ice cream into an airtight container or similar vessel and freeze in the freezer until fully hardened.  
6. **Plate and Serve**: Use an ice cream scoop to scoop the set ice cream into balls and place on top of a crushed cookie biscuit base.  

## Health Advice  
This Green Yangchun tea ice cream uses high-quality green tea, which is rich in tea polyphenols and antioxidants, making it suitable for refreshing the palate during spring. However, it contains high levels of sugar and cream, so it is recommended to enjoy in moderation and control your daily dessert intake.
