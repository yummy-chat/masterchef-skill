# Potato and Long Bean Braised Pork
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps
1. Wash small potatoes with skin on, wash long beans, slice pork belly, cut scallion into sections and slice ginger, wash celery, hot pepper, and cilantro whole, and drain well.
2. Place the small potatoes and long beans in the steamer, cover tightly, and steam over high heat until steam rises, then turn to medium heat and steam for 15 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce the potatoes. Remove.
3. Heat the wok, add适量 oil, place the steamed small potatoes in, and fry over low heat until the surface dries and turns slightly golden. Remove.
4. Leave the base oil in the wok, add the sliced pork belly, and stir-fry over low heat until the surface is golden and the fat is fully rendered.
5. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and bay leaves, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
6. Add the steamed long beans and fried small potatoes, and stir-fry quickly to combine.
7. Pour in one flat bowl of light soy sauce and a little dark soy sauce, stir-fry quickly twice to bring out the fermented aroma of the soy sauce.
8. Cover the wok, turn to medium-low heat, and braise for 20 minutes. Do not add water throughout; rely on the moisture from the ingredients and the soy sauce to form the braising liquid.
9. After uncovering, place the whole celery, hot pepper, and cilantro on top of the ingredients, cover the wok again, and braise over the lowest heat for another 20 minutes. Midway, flip the vegetables to ensure even flavor absorption.
10. After braising, uncover and transfer all ingredients to a plate, and pour the remaining braising liquid from the wok over the top.

## Health Suggestions
This braised dish balances meat and vegetables. Potatoes and long beans provide dietary fiber, while pork belly supplies energy, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or a craving for rich food. Pork belly has high fat content; you can reduce the amount of meat and use the rendered fat instead of extra oil. Braising whole vegetables retains their fresh fragrance, but people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of light and dark soy sauce.
