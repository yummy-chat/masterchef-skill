# Braised Pork Belly with Taro
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn/Winter meals

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with skin 1 piece (whole piece)
🥔 Taro 2 (medium-sized)
🥬 Lettuce appropriate amount (for plating decoration)
🧄 Minced garlic appropriate amount
🧀 Fermented red bean curd appropriate amount
🌿 Five-spice powder appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount
🟤 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount
🟡 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Rice wine appropriate amount
💧 Water appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Oil wok (large wok)
🥄 Oil strainer (long-handled slotted spoon)
🥘 Stock pot
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce and assembling)
♨️ Steamer
🍲 Clay pot

## Steps
1. **Prepare taro**: Peel the taro and cut into thick slices.
2. **Boil the pork**: Place the whole piece of pork belly with skin into a stock pot and cook until done, then remove.
3. **Prick the pork skin**: Use a special fork to densely prick small holes into the cooked pork skin.
**Note**: Pricking holes prevents the oil from splattering during frying and helps the skin become wrinkled and absorb flavors.
4. **Deep-fry the taro**: Heat oil to 120°C, add the taro slices and deep-fry for about 5 minutes until the edges are golden brown. Remove and drain oil.
5. **Deep-fry the pork**: Heat oil to 150°C, place the pricked pork skin-side down into the oil strainer and deep-fry for about 2 minutes until the surface is evenly golden brown.
**Doneness check**: Surface should be evenly golden brown.
6. **Soak the pork**: Quickly remove the fried pork and soak it in cold water until the skin becomes wrinkled and crispy.
7. **Slice**: Cut the soaked pork and fried taro into slices of even thickness.
**Key point**: The meat slices should not be too thick.
8. **Prepare the sauce**: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, fermented red bean curd, and five-spice powder. Add sugar, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, rice wine, and water. Stir to form a marinade sauce.
9. **Marinate and arrange**: Mix the pork slices and taro slices in the sauce evenly. Then, arrange them in a bowl in alternating layers with the pork skin facing downward.
**Key point**: The pork skin must face downward so that it appears on the top layer after being inverted.
10. **Steam**: Place the bowl into a steamer and steam over medium heat for 35–40 minutes.
11. **Serve with sauce**: After steaming, invert the bowl onto a clay pot lined with lettuce. Return the original juices to the pot, heat and pour over the braised pork before serving.
**Doneness check**: The pork is tender, and the taro is soft and glutinous.

## Health Advice
This braised pork belly with taro is rich in protein and carbohydrates but high in fat content, suitable for small portions during autumn and winter nourishing meals. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables and control the serving size. Those with cardiovascular conditions should consume with caution.
