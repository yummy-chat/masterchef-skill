# Supreme Duck Neck
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck Neck about 4 jin
🐔 Chicken Carcass 2 pieces (remove the tail)
🦴 Pork Leg Bone 1 piece (crushed)
💧 Water 20 jin (for brining)
🍶 Cooking Wine 100 grams (for brine) + 50 grams (for broth)
🫚 Ginger Slices 100 grams (for brine) + 60 grams (for broth)
🧂 Salt 600 grams (for brine) + 200 grams (for braising liquid)
🧅 Scallion Sections 60 grams
🫘 Soybean Oil 1000 grams
🐖 Lard 500 grams
🌿 Spice Bag 1 portion (soak in warm water for half an hour in advance)
🌶️ Dried Chili Pepper (Small Red Pepper) 250 grams
🌿 Sichuan Peppercorn 65 grams
🍬 Rock Sugar 100 grams
🧂 Chicken Bouillon 50 grams
🧂 MSG 100 grams
🧪 Meat Flavor Enhancer 10 grams
🧪 Ethyl Maltol 5 grams
🌶️ Red Yeast Rice Powder 35 grams

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Large Stainless Steel Bucket (for braising)
🥄 Slotted Spoon / Ladle
🍳 Wok
🔗 Tongs
🧴 Fine Mesh Strainer / Fine Gauze
🥣 Glass Bowl
⚖️ Kitchen Scale

## Cooking Steps
1. **Wet Brine the Duck Necks**
   - Add 20 jin of water to the braising bucket. Add 100 grams of cooking wine, 100 grams of ginger slices, and 600 grams of salt. Stir until completely dissolved.
- Place the cleaned duck necks into the saltwater. Brine for 4 hours.
**Key Points**: The wet brining method better removes gamey flavors and infuses taste; the duck necks must be completely submerged in the brine.

2. **Prepare the Stock**
   - In a separate pot, add the chicken carcasses and the crushed pork leg bone. Add enough water, then add 60 grams of scallion sections, 60 grams of ginger slices, and 50 grams of cooking wine.
- Bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam. Reduce to medium-high heat and simmer for 2 hours.
- After simmering, turn off the heat. Strain the liquid through a fine mesh strainer to remove the solids, reserving the stock.
**Key Points**: Add extra water at the beginning of simmering to account for evaporation; the leg bone can be reused once, discard the chicken carcasses.

3. **Blanch the Duck Necks**
   - Remove the brined duck necks. Place them in a pot with cold water. Heat until boiling and scum rises, then remove the duck necks.
- Rinse the duck necks thoroughly under cold water. Drain and set aside.
**Key Points**: Using cold water to start the blanching allows blood and impurities to be more fully extracted; rinsing thoroughly avoids any residual gamey smell.

4. **Prepare the Braising Oil**
   - Soak the spice bag in warm water for half an hour in advance. Remove and drain.
- Pour the soybean oil and lard into a wok. Heat to about 130°C.
- Add the drained spices. Fry over medium heat for about 2 minutes until fragrant, then turn off the heat.
**Key Points**: The spices must be soaked beforehand to prevent burning when added to the oil; fry just until fragrant, reserve both the oil and the spices for later use.

5. **Prepare the Braising Liquid**
   - Pour 20 jin of the prepared stock into the braising bucket.
- Add, in order, 200 grams of salt, 50 grams of chicken bouillon, 100 grams of MSG, 10 grams of meat flavor enhancer, 5 grams of ethyl maltol, and 35 grams of red yeast rice powder. Stir to combine.
- Pour in all of the braising oil prepared in the previous step.
- Then add 250 grams of dried chili peppers, 65 grams of Sichuan peppercorns, and 100 grams of rock sugar. Stir again to combine. Turn on the heat and bring the braising liquid to a boil.
**Key Points**: The red yeast rice powder is for coloring, giving the final product a reddish-brown hue; the amounts of dried chili and Sichuan peppercorn can be adjusted to taste; rock sugar adds a subtle sweetness and gloss; bring the braising liquid to a boil before using it to braise the duck necks.

6. **Braise the Duck Necks**
   - Once the braising liquid is boiling, add the blanched duck necks to the liquid.

## Health Advice
This spicy duck neck is rich in protein but high in salt and fat. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and whole grains to balance sodium intake. It is suitable for gathering snacks, but those with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs should eat in moderation.
