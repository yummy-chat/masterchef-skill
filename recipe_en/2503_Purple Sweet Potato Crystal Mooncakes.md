# Purple Sweet Potato Crystal Mooncakes
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌴 Sago 150g  
💧 Water 200g  
🥔 Tapioca Starch 200g  
🌾 Wheat Starch 100g  
🍠 Purple Sweet Potato (as needed for filling)  
🥛 Condensed Milk (as needed for sweetness and flavor)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl or Container  
🌀 Blender  
♨️ Steamer  
🥮 Mooncake Mold  
🍽️ Plate  
🔪 Knife  

## Instructions
1. **Make the Sago Paste**: Soak the sago in water for 10 minutes, then pour it along with the water into a blender. Blend until a smooth, lump-free sago paste forms.  
*Note*: The soaking time must be a full 10 minutes to ensure the sago absorbs enough water for even blending.  

2. **Prepare the Purple Sweet Potato Filling**: Steam the purple sweet potato until soft and tender. While still hot, add an appropriate amount of condensed milk and stir until well combined. Roll the mixture into evenly sized purple sweet potato balls.  
*Key Point*: Adjust the amount of condensed milk to taste. The filling should not be too thin; it should be firm enough to hold its shape.  

3. **Knead the Dough**: Mix the tapioca starch and wheat starch together, then pour in the sago paste. Knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.  
*Note*: Strictly follow the starch proportions. Knead until the dough is soft, free of cracks, and suitable for wrapping the filling.  

4. **Wrap the Filling**: Take a suitable amount of dough and flatten it into a round disc. Place one purple sweet potato ball in the center, then use the thumb-and-index-finger pinching method to wrap it closed. Roll it into a smooth ball, ensuring the surface is seamless and crack-free.  
*Motion Tip*: Handle gently when wrapping to avoid the filling leaking out.  

5. **Shape the Mooncakes**: Place the wrapped ball into the mooncake mold and press gently. Release the mold to obtain mooncake blanks with decorative patterns.  
*Key Point*: Apply even pressure to ensure clear patterns and uniform mooncake thickness.  

6. **Steam the Mooncakes**: Arrange the mooncake blanks in the steamer and steam over high heat for 15 minutes.  
*Doneness Check*: The outer skin should change from white to translucent, with the filling faintly visible. The texture should be soft but not collapsed.  
   *Note*: Do not steam too long to prevent the mooncakes from becoming overly soft and deforming. Let them cool slightly before serving.

## Health Advice
Purple sweet potatoes are rich in anthocyanins, while sago and tapioca starch provide carbohydrates for energy, making these mooncakes a suitable festive treat. Note that condensed milk contains sugar, so those with diabetes or monitoring sugar intake should enjoy in moderation. After steaming, the semi-translucent skin reveals the filling, making them both visually appealing and delicious.
