# Hand-Held Chicken Legs  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan  
🫙 Glass bowl (or a heavy object)  
🧻 Aluminum foil/kitchen paper  

## Instructions  
### Preparation Stage  
1. Soak the chicken legs in cold water to thoroughly remove blood and reduce any gamey smell.  
2. Using kitchen scissors, cut from the ankle of the chicken leg, debone along the meat and tendons, fully flatten the leg, and squeeze out any remaining blood.  
3. Lightly poke holes in the flattened chicken with the tip of a knife to break the tendons, preventing shrinkage during frying.  
4. Place the chicken legs in a large bowl, add scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, cooking wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, chicken bouillon, and salt. Mix well, then refrigerate and marinate overnight.  

### Frying Stage  
1. Place the pan on the stove without any oil. Heat over medium heat until it just starts to smoke. Place the chicken legs skin-side down in the pan.  
   - Place kitchen paper at the edge of the pan to absorb rendered fat; cover immediately with aluminum foil and press with a heavy object.  
2. Keep frying over medium-low heat with the weight for 3 minutes. Once fragrant, remove the weight and aluminum foil.  
3. Flip the chicken legs; the skin side should now be caramelized in color.  
   - After flipping, cover again with aluminum foil and press with the heavy object.  
4. Continue frying over medium-low heat with the weight for another 3 minutes.  
5. Remove the aluminum foil and weight. Pour the prepared water starch into the pan.  
   - Reduce to medium-low heat and fry both sides of the chicken legs until the exterior is golden and crispy.  
6. Remove from the pan and plate. Sprinkle with cumin and chili flakes. Serve.  

## Health Tips  
Removing the skin from the chicken legs reduces fat by about 40%, making it suitable for high-quality protein intake during weight loss. The longer the marination, the more tender the meat, but be cautious with the amount of dark soy sauce and salt; those with high blood pressure should reduce the dosage.
