# Braised Beef Ribs
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef ribs (to taste, cut into bite-sized pieces)
🥔 Potatoes (to taste, peeled and cut into rolling knife chunks)
🫑 Green and red peppers (a few, cut into chunks for color)
🧅 Scallions (to taste, cut into sections, used separately for blanching and stir-frying)
🫚 Ginger (to taste, sliced, used separately for blanching and stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic (to taste, minced, used for stir-frying)
🧂 Baking soda (to taste, added when soaking the ribs to tenderize the meat)
🍶 Cooking wine (to taste, added during blanching to remove odor)
🫒 Cooking oil (to taste)
🫘 Broad bean paste (1 tablespoon, stir-fried to release red oil)
🌶️ Dried chilies and dried Sichuan peppercorns (to taste, to add spicy and aromatic flavor)
🫚 Spices (star anise, cinnamon, etc.) (to taste, to provide a complex aroma)
🧂 Salt (to taste)
🧂 Chicken bouillon (to taste)
🧂 White pepper (to taste)
🧂 Light soy sauce (1 tablespoon)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (a little, mainly for coloring)
🦪 Oyster sauce (to taste)
🍬 Rock sugar (a few pieces, to enhance flavor, add sheen, and mellow the spiciness)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking and washing the ribs)
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the beef ribs into bite-sized pieces; do not wash them yet.
2. Add baking soda to the ribs and soak in clean water for about 20 minutes to tenderize the meat.
3. Peel the potatoes and cut into rolling knife chunks; cut the green and red peppers into chunks; cut the scallions into sections, slice the ginger, and mince the garlic.
4. Add enough cold water to a pot, add the ribs, and include some scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil for about 5 minutes, skim off the foam, then remove the ribs and rinse them clean under running water.
5. Heat oil in a wok. Once the oil is hot, add the remaining scallion sections, ginger slices, minced garlic, spices, dried chilies, and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant, then add one tablespoon of broad bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until red oil is released.
6. Add the blanched ribs and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface of the ribs is dry and fragrant.
7. Add enough clean water to submerge the ribs. Season with salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and rock sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, cover, and simmer for about 1 hour.
8. Once the ribs are tender, add the potato chunks and continue to simmer until cooked through. Just before serving, add the green and red pepper chunks, stir-fry until just cooked, and serve.

## Health Advice
Beef ribs are rich in protein and calcium, but also high in fat. It is recommended to pair them with vegetables to increase dietary fiber. Suitable for occasional consumption by those looking to build muscle; individuals with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should manage their intake.
