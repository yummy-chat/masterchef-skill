# Homestyle Braised Delicacies
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken Fat 400g  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🧅 Small Onions (Shallots), to taste (optional)  
🦆 Duck Gizzards, to taste  
🦆 Duck Feet, to taste  
🦆 Duck Wings, to taste  
🦆 Duck Tongues, to taste  
🌶️ Dried Spicy Chilies, to taste  
🍂 Bay Leaves, to taste  
⭐ Star Anise, to taste  
🌿 Fennel, to taste  
🌸 Sichuan Peppercorns, to taste  
🤎 Cardamom, to taste  
🌰 Cloves, to taste  
🍥 Cinnamon Bark, to taste  
🍬 Rock Sugar 100g (for caramelizing) + additional (for seasoning and enhancing flavor)  
🍶 Rice Wine, to taste  
🥃 Brandy, approx. 200ml  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍾 Soy Sauce 250g  
🐟 Fish Sauce, to taste (MSG can be used as a substitute)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Stockpot (large)  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥄 Fine Mesh Skimmer  
🔥 Gas Stove  
🪣 Stainless Steel Tray  

## Steps
1. **Render Chicken Fat and Fry Aromatics**: Place 400g of chicken fat in a wok over medium heat and stir-fry until the fat is fully rendered. Add ginger, garlic, and scallions, and fry until golden brown. Remove the fried aromatics and sediment, leaving the oil in the wok.  
**Key Point**: Set aside the fried aromatics for later use in the broth.

2. **Caramelize Sugar**: Using the chicken oil in the wok, add 100g of rock sugar and stir-fry over low heat until the sugar melts completely, turns a deep amber color, forms fine bubbles, and flows in a steady stream when lifted. Immediately pour in boiling water to make the caramel water.  
**Key Point**: Maintain distance when adding boiling water to avoid burns.

3. **Prepare Braising Broth Base**: Pour the caramel water and fried aromatics into a stockpot with enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add **all dried spices**. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes to fully release the spice flavors.  
**Key Point**: Wrap the dried spices in a cotton bag beforehand for easier removal later.

4. **Pre-treat Main Ingredients**: Place duck gizzards, feet, wings, and tongues in cold water with some rice wine. Bring to a boil and blanch. Remove, rinse thoroughly under cold water, and drain.  
**Key Point**: Blanching removes blood and odors, ensuring a clear broth.

5. **Season the Broth**: Into the simmered spice broth, add approx. 200ml brandy, salt to taste, 250g soy sauce, fish sauce to taste, and rock sugar. Stir to combine.  
**Note**: Brandy can be substituted with high-proof white spirits; the flavor will differ slightly.

6. **Braise**: Add the pre-treated ingredients to the seasoned broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to low heat. Keep at a gentle simmer and soak the ingredients for about 30 minutes.  
**Note**: Use the "immersion braising" method to avoid overcooking the meat.

7. **Remove**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the braised items, drain off excess broth, and place on a tray. Discard the spice packet.  
**Note**: Cool the items with a fan for a crispier skin.

8. **Save the Master Stock**: Use a fine mesh skimmer to thoroughly remove any spice residues and impurities from the broth. Add a **new, equal-sized spice packet** of dried spices. Bring the broth to a boil again and keep boiling for about 10 minutes. Allow it to cool naturally. Once cooled, cover and store in the refrigerator for future use.  
**Key Point**: Periodically replenish spices and seasonings; the master stock improves with repeated use.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein and collagen, making it a great accompaniment to rice or as a snack with drinks. However, duck feet and wings are high in purines, so gout sufferers should consume in moderation. The ginger and garlic in the spices have warming properties, suitable for all seasons. Be mindful of the high salt and sugar content and control portions accordingly.
