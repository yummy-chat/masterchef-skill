# Sautéed Pork Liver Tips
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Fresh pork liver, appropriate amount (remove tendons, slice diagonally into thin triangular pieces)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, appropriate amount (pre-soaked, torn into pieces)
🥬 Asparagus lettuce, appropriate amount (sliced)
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (sliced)
🌿 Cilantro stems, appropriate amount (cut into sections)

💧 Soy sauce, appropriate amount (for marinating and the sauce bowl separately)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (adjust based on soy sauce saltiness)
🧂 MSG, appropriate amount
🧂 White pepper, appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (to color the sauce bowl)
🥄 Water starch, appropriate amount (for marinating and thickening the sauce bowl)
🫗 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (added when stir-frying the vegetables)
🫒 Sesame oil, a small amount (drizzled in before serving)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🍽️ Plate
🔥 Stove

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the pork liver**: Place the fresh pork liver on a cutting board. Use a knife to carefully remove all white tendons to ensure a tender texture.
2. **Cut into liver tips**: Using a diagonal cutting technique, slice the liver into triangular pieces of uniform thickness with three pointed ends, known as "liver tips."
3. **Marinate and coat**: Place the liver slices in a bowl. Add an appropriate amount of soy sauce and toss to color evenly. Then add an appropriate amount of water starch and mix well, ensuring each slice is thinly coated to lock in moisture.
4. **Prepare the sauce bowl**: In another bowl, sequentially add soy sauce, MSG, white pepper, water starch, and a small amount of dark soy sauce. Stir until well combined to create the sauce mixture; set aside.
5. **Prepare the vegetables**: Slice the garlic, cut the cilantro stems into sections, and slice the wood ear mushrooms, asparagus lettuce, and carrot separately.
6. **Blanch in oil**: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to 60–70% hot. Scatter the marinated liver slices into the oil and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Blanch until 50–60% cooked, then immediately remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Ensure the oil is hot enough to prevent the coating from falling off; keep the time very short to maintain tenderness.

7. **Sauté the aromatics and vegetables**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over medium heat, sauté the garlic slices until fragrant. Then add the scallion, wood ear mushrooms, asparagus lettuce slices, and carrot slices. Stir-fry quickly for a few turns, then drizzle cooking wine along the edge of the wok.
8. **Combine and thicken over high heat**: Immediately add the blanched liver slices to the wok, along with the prepared sauce bowl. Turn the heat to high and quickly stir-fry with a spatula to rapidly thicken the sauce and coat all ingredients evenly, achieving a "wrapped sauce" effect.
**Key point**: Work as quickly as possible to avoid overcooking the liver tips. The finished dish should have glossy sauce and bright oil.

9. **Drizzle sesame oil and serve**: Drizzle a small amount of sesame oil along the edge of the wok, toss quickly to mix, then immediately plate.

## Health Advice
Pork liver is effective for replenishing iron and improving blood health, but it is high in cholesterol. It is recommended to consume it 1–2 times per month. Pairing it with wood ear mushrooms and asparagus lettuce can increase dietary fiber. Keep the blanching time short to prevent the liver tips from becoming tough and difficult to chew, while also reducing oil absorption.
