# Braised Chicken in Oil
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐓 Whole chicken 1.4 kg (remove backbone, collarbone, breast cartilage, flatten)
🥔 Potatoes appropriate amount (peeled and cut into chunks)
🥜 Roasted peanuts 1 tablespoon
🍪 Caramel cookies 2 pieces
🌱 White sesame seeds 1 tablespoon

🧅 Scallions 2 stalks (mashed to extract juice)
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (mashed)
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves (mashed)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 5 (mashed)
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 1 teaspoon (for marinating, mashed)
⭐ Star anise 2 (mashed)
🧂 Salt 1 teaspoon (for marinating)
🥢 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon
🥢 Maggi seasoning 1 tablespoon
🌿 Ground coriander seeds 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Chili powder 2 tablespoons
🍂 Ground Sichuan pepper 1 teaspoon (for barbecue seasoning)
🧂 Salt ½ tablespoon (for barbecue seasoning)
🧂 MSG 1 teaspoon (for barbecue seasoning)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (sprinkled on potatoes later)
🫒 Vegetable oil 1 full bottle (oil level should be even with the chicken)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🔪 Small knife
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🥖 Rolling pin
🥘 Deep roasting pan or deep baking dish
📄 Aluminum foil
🔥 Oven (with broil function)
🥄 Lid and wooden spoon (optional, for draining oil)
🧺 Fine-mesh strainer (optional, for filtering roasting oil)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the whole chicken**: Place the chicken back side up. Use kitchen scissors to cut along both sides of the backbone and remove the entire backbone. Be careful to avoid the main meat portions on the back while cutting to reduce waste.
   - Follow the backbone's direction to butterfly the chicken.
2. **Remove the collarbone**: Locate the collarbone at the front of the chicken breast. Use a small knife to cut the meat attached to the bone, then use your fingers to find the cartilage connection of the collarbone, gently break it off, and remove the entire collarbone.
   - Take advantage of the cartilage's fragility; no excessive force needed.
3. **Remove the breast cartilage**: Lay the chicken flat. A white piece of cartilage is visible along the centerline of the breast. Use a small knife to cut open the surface meat to expose the cartilage. Press down on both sides until you hear a "crack" sound, loosening the cartilage, then remove it. This step makes the whole chicken flatter, aiding subsequent marinating and roasting.
4. **Prepare the marinade**: Place scallion segments, ginger slices, and garlic cloves in a bowl. Use a rolling pin to mash them until they release juice. Add dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and salt, and mash together. Finally, pour in light soy sauce and Maggi seasoning, and mix evenly.
   - Thorough mashing fully releases the aromatics and ginger-garlic juice, enhancing penetration.
5. **Marinate the chicken**: Place the prepared whole chicken in a large bowl, pour in the marinade, and rub repeatedly with both hands to ensure the marinade penetrates every part of the chicken. Cover and refrigerate overnight, turning once midway for even flavor absorption.
6. **Prepare for roasting**: The next day, remove the marinated chicken and gently scrape off any adhering spice residue. Place the chicken skin side up in a deep roasting pan. Fill the gaps around the chicken with potato chunks, then pour in the entire bottle of vegetable oil. The oil level should basically cover all ingredients, and the potatoes must be fully submerged in the oil, otherwise they won't cook through easily.
   - Potatoes exposed above the oil result in a dry, hard texture after roasting.
7. **First low-temperature roasting**: Tightly seal the roasting pan opening with aluminum foil. Place it in the middle rack of an oven preheated to 150°C (302°F). Roast for about 1 hour and 25 minutes.
   - The aluminum foil seal creates an "oil-braising" environment, locking in steam and heat, allowing the chicken to slowly cook in low-temperature oil, keeping the meat tender.
8. **Prepare the barbecue seasoning**: During roasting, crush the roasted peanuts and mix them with crushed caramel cookies, ground coriander seeds, chili powder, ground Sichuan pepper, white sesame seeds, salt, and MSG until evenly combined.
   - Caramel cookies provide a subtle sweet aftertaste that blends with the spiciness and nutty aroma, serving as the core flavor for the final surface seasoning.
9. **Remove and drain oil**: When the first roasting time is up, put on heat-resistant gloves to remove the roasting pan. Peel off the aluminum foil. Cover the pan opening with a lid, leaving a gap, and use a wooden spoon to help drain most of the hot oil. The oil can be filtered and stored for later use. Leave a small amount of concentrated chicken jus at the bottom of the pan without pouring it out.
10. **Sprinkle seasoning and broil**: Evenly sprinkle the barbecue seasoning over the chicken and potatoes. Optionally, add a little extra salt to the potatoes. Place the roasting pan back in the oven, turn on the top broil function, and roast for about 3 minutes, until a caramelized layer forms on the surface and the spice powder is slightly toasted. Remove immediately.
    - This step is very short; stay by the oven to watch and avoid burning.
11. **Plate and serve**: Transfer the braised chicken to a large serving plate, arrange the potatoes around it, and serve. The chicken skin is crispy and reddish-brown, while the inner meat is juicy; the potatoes are fully infused with chicken oil and spices, soft and flavorful.

## Health Suggestions
The chicken is slowly cooked at low temperature, resulting in crispy skin and tender meat, but the use of a full bottle of vegetable oil results in higher calorie content. It is recommended to pair with refreshing vegetables to balance intake. Suitable for fitness enthusiasts looking to build muscle, but portion control of potatoes and oil is advised.
