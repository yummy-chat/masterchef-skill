# Daqian Dry-Braised Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Crucian carp – 2 fish (about 1.5 kg total, cleaned and scored)
🥩 Pork belly – about 200 g (cut into small pieces for meat topping)
🧅 Ginger – as needed (for marinating and stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic – as needed (cut into pieces)
🫚 Scallion/Onion – as needed (whole scallions for marinating, chopped scallions for garnish)
🫚 Pickled ginger – as needed (finely minced)
🌶️ Pickled chili (pickled peppers) – as needed (finely minced)
🧂 Salt – as needed (for marinating and seasoning the broth)
🍶 Cooking wine – as needed (for removing fishy smell)
🫘 Pixian broad bean paste – about 1 tablespoon
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns – a handful
🌶️ Dried chilies (bullet-head and lantern peppers) – a large bowl (cut into sections)
🧂 Chicken bouillon – as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
✂️ Scissors
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Wok spatula/spatula
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🧺 Bamboo basket

## Steps
1.  Scale and gut the crucian carp, thoroughly clean the black membrane and blood near the bone, and make a few cuts on the thickest parts of the fish body.
2.  Place the fish in a basin, add scallion sections, ginger slices, cooking wine, and salt. Rub evenly over the fish body and inside the cavity. Marinate for a while.
3.  Smash and chop ginger and garlic into pieces. Cut scallions into sections or chop for garnish. Finely mince pickled ginger and pickled chilies. Cut dried chilies into sections. Cut pork belly into small pieces. Set aside all prepared ingredients separately.
4.  Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to 70% hot. Carefully place the marinated fish in and fry over medium heat until one side is set and golden brown. Flip and fry the other side to the same state. Remove and set aside.
    *Note: Do not move the fish immediately after placing it in the oil. Wait until it is set before turning.*
5.  Leave a little base oil in the wok. Add the pork belly pieces and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the moisture evaporates and the edges of the meat pieces become browned and curled.
    *Key: The meat topping must be stir-fried until it is “golden-brown and curled”. This is the foundation of the dry-braised flavor.*
6.  Add the ginger and garlic pieces, scallion sections, minced pickled ginger, and minced pickled chilies. Stir-fry until fragrant.
7.  Add one tablespoon of Pixian broad bean paste. Continue stir-frying until the red oil separates.
    *Note: The broad bean paste must be fried thoroughly to release its color and aroma, avoiding a raw paste taste.*
8.  Add the dried Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili sections. Stir-fry quickly and evenly to avoid burning.
9.  Pour in about half a ladle of water. Bring to a boil, then season with salt and chicken bouillon to make the broth.
10. Gently place the fried fish into the broth. Reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly, maintaining a gentle boil.
    *Key: This is the core stage of “dry-braising”. Patiently let the broth gradually reduce, allowing the flavor to fully penetrate the fish.*
11. Once the broth is mostly reduced, becomes thick, and tightly coats the fish, turn off the heat. Carefully transfer the fish to a plate first. Then spoon the meat topping and concentrated broth from the wok over the fish.
12. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve.

## Health Tips
This dry-braised fish uses pork belly topping to enhance its umami and richness, providing a good source of protein. However, the frying of the fish and the browning of the meat involve a relatively high oil content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the calorie intake. Pickled chilies and broad bean paste are high in salt, so individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of their overall consumption.
