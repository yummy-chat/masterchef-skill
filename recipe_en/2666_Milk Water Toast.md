# Milk Water Toast
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 500g bread flour (100g for tangzhong, 400g for main dough)  
🍬 34g granulated sugar (10g for tangzhong, 24g for main dough)  
🧂 9g salt (1g for tangzhong, 8g for main dough)  
💧 150g boiling water (must be boiling, for tangzhong)  
💧 184g water (for main dough, ice water recommended)  
🥛 160g milk (for main dough)  
🍞 12g fresh yeast (or 4g dry yeast)  
🧈 24g unsalted butter (for main dough)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Stand mixer  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🫙 Proofing box  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍞 Loaf pan  
🔥 Oven  

## Instructions  
### Prepare Tangzhong  
1. Mix 100g bread flour, 10g granulated sugar, and 1g salt in a bowl until well combined.  
2. Pour in 150g boiling water and stir quickly until a dough forms.  
   **Note**: The water must be boiling to ensure the flour is fully gelatinized.  
3. Knead the dough until smooth, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and cool completely to room temperature before use.  

### Knead the Main Dough  
4. Place all main dough ingredients except the unsalted butter and cooled tangzhong into the stand mixer bowl.  
5. Mix on low speed until no dry flour remains, then switch to medium speed until the dough comes together and becomes slightly smooth, with a thick膜 (membrane) that can be stretched.  
6. Add the completely cooled tangzhong dough, mix on low until fully incorporated, then switch to high speed until the dough is smooth and can be stretched into a thin膜 (membrane).  
7. Add 24g unsalted butter, mix on low until fully absorbed, then switch to high speed until the dough can be stretched into a smooth, elastic "glove膜 (membrane)."  
   **Key Control**: Total kneading time is approximately 15–25 minutes; monitor the dough temperature closely. In summer, use ice water and attach ice packs to the mixer bowl to prevent the dough from overheating and fermenting prematurely.  

### Bulk Fermentation  
8. Shape the kneaded dough into a round ball, place in a proofing box, cover, and let it ferment at room temperature for about 40 minutes.  
   **State Check**: The dough should double in volume. Dust your finger with flour and poke a hole; the hole should slowly spring back and leave a small indent. If the dough is too soft and collapses, fold it before continuing.  

### Divide & Shape into Balls  
9. Gently deflate the fermented dough by pressing lightly. Divide into 4 equal portions using a dough scraper.  
   **Note**: The dough is sticky due to high hydration; dust your hands and work surface with a little flour to prevent sticking, and handle gently.  
10. Fold each piece lightly twice, then round it into a ball, pinching the bottom seam tightly.  

### Bench Rest  
11. Place the rounded dough pieces in the proofing box, cover, and let rest at room temperature for 25 minutes to relax the gluten for easier rolling.  

### Shaping  
12. Take one rested dough piece, flatten it with your hand, and roll it out into a long tongue shape about 25 cm long. Flip it over.  
13. Roll it up gently from top to bottom, with the seam facing down. Place it horizontally into the loaf pan, still with the seam facing down.  
    **Note**: Roll with moderate pressure—neither too tight nor too loose.  

### Final Fermentation  
14. Place the loaf pan in a warm, humid area for the final fermentation until the dough rises to about 80% of the pan's height. Gently press the side with a finger; it should spring back slowly.  
    **Note**: Under-fermentation will prevent the toast from rising properly; over-fermentation may cause collapse or a sour taste.  

### Baking  
15. Preheat the oven in advance.  
    **Recommended Baking Temperature**:  
    - Mountain-shaped toast: Upper heat 180°C, lower heat 220°C;  
    - Square-shaped toast: Upper heat can be slightly higher than lower heat.  
16. Bake for about 25 minutes until the top and bottom are evenly browned.  
17. Once out of the oven, immediately tap the pan firmly, invert the toast onto a wire rack, and let it cool completely.  

## Health Advice  
This milk water toast is rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those needing an energy boost. Pair it with vegetables or protein-rich foods for balanced nutrition, and be mindful of portion sizes to avoid blood sugar fluctuations.
