# Yogurt Mochi Sticks
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous Rice Flour 150g (about 1 bowl)
🌽 Corn Starch 40g (about 2 tablespoons)
🍬 White Sugar 45g (about 1 tablespoon)
🥛 Milk 250ml (1 carton)
🍵 Matcha Powder 3g (about 1 tablespoon, for matcha flavor)
🍦 Plain Yogurt about 30g (filling, can be substituted with cream, ice cream, etc.)
🫒 Cooking Oil 30g (about 2 tablespoons, for the dough, can be substituted with butter)
🍚 Cooked Glutinous Rice Flour as needed (hand flour, made by dry-frying raw glutinous rice flour until slightly yellow)

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Large Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks or Spoon
🫗 Strainer
♨️ Steamer (or Microwave)
🍳 Frying Pan (for making hand flour)
🫙 Plastic Wrap
🥄 Rolling Pin
🔪 Cutting Board

## Instructions
1.  Place glutinous rice flour, corn starch and white sugar together in a large bowl and mix well. Add milk and stir until smooth and lump-free batter.
    **Key Points**: Stir until there are no dry flour lumps.
2.  Strain the batter through a sieve at least once to ensure a fine texture with no clumps.
3.  Take a portion of the strained plain batter and add matcha powder. First mix a small amount of batter with the matcha powder until smooth, then combine with the remaining batter and mix evenly to make matcha-flavored batter.
4.  Place the plain and matcha batters into separate bowls. Cover with plastic wrap and poke several small holes in the plastic wrap with a toothpick for ventilation.
    **Note**: Poking holes prevents condensed steam from dripping down, keeping the batter surface smooth.
5.  Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the bowls of batter inside and steam over medium heat for 15 minutes, until a chopstick inserted into the center comes out clean with no liquid batter.
6.  While steaming, prepare the hand flour: Place a frying pan over low heat. Add an appropriate amount of raw glutinous rice flour and dry-fry until slightly yellow and fragrant. Remove and let cool.
7.  Remove the cooked dough while hot. Add cooking oil. Wearing gloves, repeatedly knead and press the dough until the oil is fully absorbed and the dough becomes smooth and stretchy.
    **Key Points**: If the dough is too dry, add a little milk at a time and knead it in.
8.  Lay a piece of plastic wrap on the cutting board and sprinkle with an appropriate amount of hand flour. Place the dough on top and press it flat. Roll it out into a rectangular or oval sheet about 0.5cm thick.
9.  Spread plain yogurt in the center of the dough sheet. Fold the two side edges of the dough sheet over the filling to meet in the middle. Then fold the top and bottom ends over, pinch the seams to seal, turn the packet seam-side down, and shape it into a long strip.
    **Note**: The seams must be pinched tightly to prevent the filling from leaking out during subsequent steps.
10. Lightly sprinkle the surface with some hand flour to prevent sticking. Cut into pieces or serve as a whole stick. The finished product is soft, chewy, and springy, with a cool and smooth filling inside.

## Health Advice
These mochi sticks have a soft, chewy texture and a sweet taste, suitable for an occasional treat. The content of glutinous rice flour and white sugar is relatively high, so it is recommended to control consumption, especially for those with blood sugar sensitivity or during weight loss. Using yogurt as a filling is lighter than cream and is a healthier option, but total calorie intake should still be noted.
