# Christmas Wreath and Bow Bagels
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 High-gluten flour 200g  
🧫 Dry yeast 2g  
🍬 Sugar 6g  
🧂 Salt 3g  
💧 Water 120g (for the dough)  
🎨 Food coloring or matcha powder as needed (for coloring)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🌀 Stand mixer  
🔥 Oven  
🍲 Deep pot (for boiling bagels)  
🔪 Bench scraper/dough cutter  
📄 Parchment paper  
✨ Aluminum foil  
🥘 Baking sheet  
💦 Spray bottle  

## Preparation Steps  
**Prepare the Dough**  
1. Place high-gluten flour, dry yeast, sugar, salt, water, and food coloring or matcha powder into the stand mixer. Mix at medium speed for about 7 minutes until a smooth dough forms.  
   **Note**: To ensure effective kneading with the stand mixer, it is recommended to double the recipe amount to create three equal portions of dough in different colors.  
2. Divide the three-colored dough into small pieces and directly roll them into long strips.  
   **Key Point**: Do not shape the pieces into rounds after dividing, as this may tighten the gluten and make rolling more difficult.  
3. Take three strips of different colors and braid them into a plait. Connect the ends to form a circular wreath-shaped base. Combine colors as desired.  
4. Roll a small piece of red dough into a thin round sheet. Cut, fold, and wrap the center according to the illustrated method to create a bow decoration.  
5. Place the bow decoration at the joining point of the wreath base.  
   **Suggestion**: When the main wreath is green or white, a red bow is more striking; if the wreath already contains significant red, the decorative effect will be diminished.  

**Boil and Bake**  
6. Bring water to a boil in a deep pot. Place the bagel base along with the parchment paper into the boiling water. Boil each side for 30 seconds, then remove and place onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.  
   **Key Point**: Boiling is essential for creating the chewy crust of the bagel; since shaping takes time, the dough has already been fermenting at room temperature, so additional proofing is usually unnecessary.  
7. Place the baking sheet into an oven preheated to 210°C and bake for 20 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: During baking, open the oven as needed and cover the bagel surface with aluminum foil to prevent excessive browning and preserve the vibrant colors.  
8. Once baking is complete, remove and serve.  

**Note**: If the finished surface has slight uneven textures, it may be due to gluten breakage from pulling during rolling, which does not affect eating quality or overall appearance.  

## Health Advice  
The bagel wreath is visually appealing, but as a wheat-based dish, it is high in carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair it with protein and vegetables for balanced nutrition.
