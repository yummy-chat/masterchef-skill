# All-Purpose Chili Oil Dressing for Cold Dishes
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer, all-season meals

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Coarse chili powder 150g (for aroma)
🌶️ Fine chili powder 100g (for bright red color)
🫒 Rapeseed oil about 500g (for chili oil base)
🧅 Scallion scallion segments 50g
🫚 Ginger ginger slices 30g
🌿 Bay leaves 2g
🪵 Cinnamon bark 3g
⭐ Star anise 4g
🌰 Black cardamom 2 pieces
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 3g
🫘 White cardamom 2g
🌾 Fennel seeds 6g
🌸 Cloves 3 pieces
🧂 Light soy sauce 60g
🧂 Premium soy sauce 40g
🧂 Salt 6g
🍬 White sugar 4g
🍶 Fragrant vinegar (or aged vinegar) 300g (needs to be heated in advance)
🫚 Minced ginger appropriate amount (for heating vinegar)
🧈 Toasted sesame seeds appropriate amount
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) a little
🌿 Scallion a little (for aroma and color)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer/slotted spoon
🥣 Heat-resistant large bowl
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Ingredient and condiment plates
🥣 Small bowl

## Instructions
1. Prepare the two types of chili powder and the mixed spices. The spices need to be pre-washed and soaked in water until moistened to prevent burning during frying.
**Key Point**: Coarse chili powder adds aroma, fine chili powder adds color.

2. Place 300g of fragrant vinegar and the minced ginger into a pot. Heat over low heat until the vinegar barely simmers and the aroma is released, then turn off the heat and let it cool to make the base vinegar mixture.
**Key Point**: Raw vinegar is harsh; heating it makes the taste mellow.

3. Pour the rapeseed oil into the wok. Heat over high heat until it smokes heavily, then turn off the heat. Add the sliced ginger and scallion segments. Use the residual heat to fry until the ginger and scallions are dry and yellow, then remove and discard them.
**Key Point**: This step removes the raw, pungent smell of rapeseed oil.

4. Once the oil temperature drops to about 30-40% hot (around 90-130°C), add the soaked and moistened dried spices. Turn on low heat and fry slowly. When the spices become fragrant and slightly yellow, immediately turn off the heat and remove the spices.
**Key Point**: Spices must be protected by soaking; remove them when slightly yellow to prevent the oil from becoming bitter.

5. Reheat the spiced oil over heat until it reaches 60% hot (around 180°C), then turn off the heat. First, evenly sprinkle the coarse chili powder into the hot oil, then immediately add the fine chili powder. Finally, quickly splash in a little baijiu.
**Key Point**: High oil temperature for the coarse powder to enhance aroma; slightly lower temperature for the fine powder to enhance color; baijiu instantly releases a complex aroma.

6. Pour the chili oil into a heat-resistant large bowl. While hot, sprinkle in the toasted sesame seeds. Cover and let it cool completely.
**Key Point**: Covering during cooling allows the flavors to fully integrate.

7. In another bowl, combine the 300g of heated fragrant vinegar, 60g of light soy sauce, 40g of premium soy sauce, 6g of salt, and 4g of white sugar. Stir well to make the base dressing.
**Key Point**: White sugar harmonizes the flavors, making the dressing smooth.

8. Application for cold dishes: Take a portion of cold ingredients. Add one spoon of the base dressing, one spoon of chili oil, and a little chopped scallion. Mix well and serve.
**Key Point**: This is the basic formula for an all-purpose dressing; ratios can be adjusted according to the ingredients.

## Health Suggestions
The chili oil cold dish dressing is versatile and can be paired with ingredients like cucumber, tofu skin, and chicken. The chili oil and spices provide a double layer of flavor. It is recommended to pair the dressing with plenty of seasonal vegetables when making a cold dish to increase dietary fiber and cut the richness. Individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of the amount of light soy sauce and salt used, and can substitute with low-sodium soy sauce.
