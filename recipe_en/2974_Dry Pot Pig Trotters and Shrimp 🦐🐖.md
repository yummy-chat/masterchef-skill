# Dry Pot Pig Trotters and Shrimp 🦐🐖
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pig trotters, appropriate amount (cleaned, cut into small pieces)
🦐 Shrimp, about 2 jin (cleaned, whiskers and legs removed, optional)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers, appropriate amount (cut into sections or strips)
🌿 Celery, a handful (cut into sections)
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (for garnish)
🧂 Scallions, ginger, appropriate amount (for blanching and sautéing)
🧂 Cooking wine, appropriate amount
🍂 Braising spices (star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, dried chilies, etc.), appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (crushed)
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste), one tablespoon
🍲 Homemade hot pot base, one bowl
🌶️ Dried chilies, appropriate amount
🫙 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar powder (or white sugar), appropriate amount
🌿 Cumin, appropriate amount
🤍 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Wok spatula/spoon
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Plate

## Steps
1. Clean the pig trotters thoroughly, split them in half, and cut into evenly sized pieces. Clean the shrimp and remove the whiskers and legs.
2. Add cold water to a pot, place the pig trotter pieces in, add scallions, ginger, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove and rinse clean.
3. Put the pig trotters back into the pot, add enough water, then add the braising spices, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Cover the lid and braise for 1 hour, until the trotters are tender and flavorful.
4. In another pot, pour in enough oil and heat to 70% hot (about 180°C). Add the shrimp and fry until the shells are crispy and the bodies turn red and curl. Remove and drain the oil.
5. Raise the oil temperature, add the braised pig trotter pieces, and fry until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add scallions, ginger, and garlic, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the doubanjiang and stir-fry until the red oil releases. Then add the homemade hot pot base and stir-fry until melted.
7. Add the dried chilies and dried Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the fried pig trotters and shrimp, and stir-fry quickly to combine.
8. Add the onion pieces and green/red chili pepper sections, stir-fry until just cooked. Just before turning off the heat, add the celery sections and stir-fry until fragrant.
9. Season with salt, chicken bouillon, sugar powder, and cumin. Sprinkle in the white sesame seeds, stir-fry well to combine, then turn off the heat.
10. Transfer the stir-fried ingredients to a plate, garnish with cilantro, and serve. The finished dish is crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, with a numbing, spicy, and savory flavor.

## Health Advice
Pig trotters are rich in collagen, and shrimp provide high-quality protein. However, this dish is high in oil and calories, so it is recommended for occasional consumption. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, high cholesterol, or those on a diet should eat only a small amount. Pairing with celery and onion can increase dietary fiber and balance the greasiness. Be sure to drink plenty of water.
