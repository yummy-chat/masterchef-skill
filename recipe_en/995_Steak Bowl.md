# Steak Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Thick-cut steak, half piece  
🥬 Lettuce, as needed  
🥚 Egg, 1 piece  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
⚫ Black pepper, as needed  
🍇 Black vinegar, as needed  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🔥 Oven  
🥘 Baking sheet (with rack)  
🥣 Saucepan  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🍽️ Deep plate  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare soft-boiled egg**: Place the egg in a saucepan with cold water, heat until boiling, then reduce to low heat. Cook until the yolk is semi-set. Remove and immerse in ice water to cool. Peel and set aside.  
   **Note**: Precise control of cooking time is required to achieve a yolk that is "at the boundary of just being set" in texture.  
2. **Sear the steak**: Heat the non-stick pan over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil. Place the half piece of thick-cut steak in the pan, sprinkle a little salt on the surface, and sear until both sides are browned and caramelized.  
3. **Oven finish**: Transfer the seared steak to a baking sheet and place in an oven preheated to 180°C (350°F). Bake for about 5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Thick-cut steak cannot be fully cooked through by searing alone; the oven ensures even doneness from surface to center.  
4. **Prepare side ingredients**: Wash and drain the lettuce, then spread it on the bottom of a deep plate. Cut the soft-boiled egg in half.  
5. **Slice and plate**: Remove the baked steak from the oven. Slice against the grain into thick pieces and arrange on top of the lettuce.  
6. **Final seasoning**: Evenly sprinkle an appropriate amount of black pepper over the steak. Place the soft-boiled egg halves beside it. Finally, drizzle with an appropriate amount of black vinegar and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Steak is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for muscle building or blood replenishment. Pairing it with lettuce and soft-boiled egg helps balance nutrition. Be careful not to overdo the black vinegar, so it does not mask the natural flavor of the steak.
