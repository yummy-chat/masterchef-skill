# Celery, Smoked Tofu, and Chicken Thigh Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🍄 Yeast 3-4g
🍬 Sugar a pinch
💧 Warm water 150ml (not hot to the touch)
🍗 Chicken thighs 2 (about 150-200g after deboning)
🥬 Celery appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🧈 Smoked tofu appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped)
🧄 Ginger and garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🧂 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🌽 Cornstarch appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon a pinch
🌿 Five-spice powder appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🌶️ Lao Gan Ma chili sauce about 2 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
♨️ Steamer and steaming mat

## Instructions
1. Dissolve the yeast and sugar in 150ml warm water by stirring until fully dissolved. The water temperature should feel warm but not hot to the back of your hand to avoid deactivating the yeast.
2. Gradually pour the yeast water into the flour and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest in a warm place until it has doubled in size with a honeycomb-like interior.
3. Debone the chicken thighs and cut the meat into small cubes. Add minced ginger and garlic, cooking wine, salt, and cornstarch, then mix well. Marinate for 15 minutes.
4. Finely chop the celery, cut the smoked tofu into small cubes similar in size to the chicken cubes, and chop the scallions. Set aside.
5. Add the chopped celery, smoked tofu cubes, and scallions to the marinated chicken. Season with light soy sauce, chicken bouillon, five-spice powder, oyster sauce, and about 2 tablespoons of Lao Gan Ma. Stir thoroughly to combine.
6. Heat oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Add the filling mixture and stir-fry for 5-6 minutes until red oil renders and the smoked tofu turns slightly crispy on the surface. Remove from heat and let it cool completely.
7. Take the risen dough, knead it briefly to release air, then roll it into a long log. Cut into evenly sized pieces. Flatten each piece and roll it into a wrapper that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges.
8. Place an appropriate amount of the completely cooled filling in the center of each wrapper. Pleat the edges to seal and form the buns.
9. Arrange the buns on a steaming tray lined with a steaming mat, cover, and let them proof a second time until visibly puffed up and the surface springs back slowly when lightly pressed.
10. Once the steamer water is boiling and steam is rising, place the buns inside and steam over high heat for 10 minutes. Turn off the heat, let them rest for 3 minutes before opening the lid to prevent the buns from collapsing.

## Health Suggestions
These buns use chicken thigh meat instead of traditional pork, providing higher quality protein. Combined with celery and smoked tofu, they offer increased dietary fiber, making them suitable for those on a weight-loss diet. Pan-frying the filling before wrapping enhances the flavor, but it is essential to cool it completely to prevent the hot filling from affecting the second proofing of the dough, which could cause the buns to collapse.
