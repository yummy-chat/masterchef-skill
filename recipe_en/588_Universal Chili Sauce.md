# Universal Chili Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌶️ Green chili peppers, as needed  
🌶️ Red hot peppers, as needed  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (whole or cut into sections)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (cut into rings)  
🍃 Bay leaves, a few pieces  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, 1 small piece  
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, 2 tablespoons  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 bowls  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, half a bowl  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, 1 bowl

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫙 Glass jar (sealable)

## Steps
1. Wash the green chili peppers, red hot peppers, and bird's eye chili peppers thoroughly, dry them completely, then chop them finely and place in a bowl.  
   **Key point**: Moisture must be completely dried off, otherwise it will affect the crispness of the chili peppers and their shelf life.  
2. Add 2 tablespoons of white sugar and a generous amount of minced garlic, mix evenly with your hands, and let marinate for 15 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Using sugar to marinate makes the chili peppers more flavorful while maintaining a crunchy texture.  
3. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the wok, add sliced ginger, scallion sections, cilantro, onion rings, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick. Fry slowly over low to medium heat until all aromatics turn golden brown. Use a slotted spoon to remove and discard the aromatics, reserving the infused oil.  
4. Add 2 bowls of light soy sauce, half a bowl of dark soy sauce, and 1 bowl of fragrant vinegar to the infused oil in the wok. Stir well, bring to a boil, then turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Light soy sauce and dark soy sauce are already salty enough, so no additional salt is needed.  
5. Place the marinated chili peppers into a clean glass jar, sprinkle with white sesame seeds, and pour the sauce from step 4 into the jar only after it has completely cooled down.  
   **Key point**: The sauce must be fully cooled, otherwise it will soften the chili peppers and ruin their crunchy texture.  
6. Close the lid, refrigerate overnight, and it is ready to eat.  
   **Key point**: Refrigeration allows the flavors to fully meld, resulting in a better taste.

## Health Tips
This chili sauce is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants, but its high sodium and oil content should be noted. It is suitable as a condiment for meals and should not be consumed in excess. Ensure all moisture is removed during storage to maintain freshness; refrigeration can extend shelf life. It is recommended to pair with light staple foods to balance daily nutritional intake.
