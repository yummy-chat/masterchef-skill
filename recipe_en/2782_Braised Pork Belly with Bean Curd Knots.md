# Braised Pork Belly with Bean Curd Knots
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Bean curd knots 250g
🥩 Pork belly Approximately 600g (with skin, cut into 1.5cm pieces)
🧅 Scallion 1-2 stalks (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 4-5 cloves (whole)
⭐ Star anise 1 piece (broken apart)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 3-4 pieces
🫒 Cooking oil A small amount
🍶 Yellow wine 1 tablespoon
🍯 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon
💧 Boiling water Approximately 1.5 liters
🍬 Rock sugar 1 small piece (can be substituted with white sugar)
🧴 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon
🍇 Aged vinegar 1 teaspoon
🫙 Pork rib sauce/seafood sauce 1 tablespoon (can be substituted with oyster sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon (optional)
🧂 Salt To taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Cooking spoon (for stir-frying, removing oil, adding seasonings)
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Chopping board
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, blanch the bean curd knots for 2 minutes, then remove and drain.
   **Key point**: Blanching removes the beany flavor and makes the bean curd knots more tender and chewy.
2. Cut the pork belly into 1.5 cm cubes. If available, use a blowtorch to singe the pork skin, then scrape and wash it clean.
3. Add a small amount of oil to the wok over medium heat, stir-fry the pork belly until the surface is slightly browned and fat is rendered.
   **Key point**: Spoon out the excess rendered fat, leaving only a thin layer of oil.
4. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic cloves, broken star anise, and dried chili peppers, stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
5. Turn the heat to high, pour in the yellow wine, and stir-fry quickly to release the aroma.
6. Add the light soy sauce, stir-fry over high heat for about half a minute to develop the soy sauce fragrance and evenly coat the meat.
7. Add enough boiling water, then add the rock sugar, dark soy sauce, aged vinegar, and pork rib sauce, stir to combine.
   **Note**: Do not add salt at this point.
8. Cover the wok, reduce the heat to low, and braise for 30 minutes.
9. Uncover, use a spoon to remove the scallion, ginger, star anise, and other solids. Taste the broth for saltiness, add salt to taste, then add the blanched bean curd knots.
10. Cover the wok and continue to braise over low heat for 15 minutes, allowing the bean curd knots to fully absorb the broth.
11. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce until it thickens and evenly coats the ingredients, then turn off the heat.
12. Transfer to a plate and garnish with chopped scallions if desired. The flavor is even better after being reheated the next day.

## Health Advice
Bean curd knots provide high-quality plant protein, and braising them with pork belly can reduce greasiness, making this dish suitable for those who need to supplement protein but control fat intake. However, pork belly itself is high in calories, so it should be consumed in moderation. Patients with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of dark soy sauce and pork rib sauce.
