# Paopao Orange Tea
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

---

🍊 **Ingredients**  
- Paopao oranges  
- Jasmine green tea  
- Ice cubes  
- Cane sugar syrup (optional)

---

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Cane sugar syrup (optional)

---

🔪 **Utensils**  
- Blender  
- Clear glass (approximately 300 ml recommended)  
- Tea brewing container  
- Strainer  

---

📝 **Steps**  
**Jasmine Green Tea Preparation**  
1. Place 2–4 grams of jasmine green tea leaves into a brewing container, pour in 100 ml of boiling water, and steep for 6 minutes.  
2. Add 60 grams of ice cubes to the brewed tea, stir to accelerate cooling, and once the ice has completely melted, strain out the tea leaves using a strainer to obtain approximately 160 ml of jasmine green tea liquid.  

**Final Preparation**  
3. Take 1–2 paopao oranges (total weight over 200 grams), peel off the skin, remove the fruit segments and discard the white pith, then weigh out 130 grams of flesh for later use.  
4. Add the 130 grams of flesh and 130 ml of jasmine green tea liquid into the blender.  
5. If additional sweetness is desired, add 10 grams of 1:1 cane sugar syrup (optional), secure the blender lid, and blend for 20 seconds.  
6. Pour the blended mixture into a clear glass, then take a small amount of remaining flesh, tear it into small pieces, and sprinkle them on the surface of the drink before serving.  

---

⚠️ **Key Points and Notes**  
- Maintain a 1:1 ratio of flesh to tea liquid; increase amounts proportionally for larger servings.  
- Other citrus varieties with easily separable flesh (such as ugly oranges) can be used as a substitute for paopao oranges to ensure a smooth texture.  
- Rapidly chilling the green tea with ice cubes helps avoid excessive astringency; control blending time to 20 seconds to achieve a uniform consistency.  
- Cane sugar syrup is an optional seasoning; add according to personal preference.

## Health Suggestion
This beverage is rich in vitamin C and tea polyphenols, making it suitable for replenishing hydration and providing antioxidant support during winter. Be mindful of the syrup quantity to avoid excessive calorie intake. It is recommended to pair with light snacks and avoid drinking on an empty stomach to prevent irritation of gastric acid.
