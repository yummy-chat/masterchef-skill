# Red Bean Paste Rice Wine Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Dried red beans 125g (for filling)
🌾 Bread flour 212g (30g for tangzhong + 182g for dough)
🍚 Fermented glutinous rice (rice wine) 60g (30g rice grains + 30g liquid)
🧈 Unsalted butter 15g
🍬 Granulated sugar 70g (40g for filling + 30g for dough)
🧂 Salt 3g
🍄 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerant) 2g
💧 Water 407g (312g for cooking red beans + 95g for dough)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Electric pressure cooker
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Saucepan
🤖 Stand mixer (with dough hook)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Dough scraper
🌡️ Probe thermometer
🔥 Oven
📦 Baking trays (2)
📜 Parchment paper
🪟 Cooling rack
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. **Make the red bean filling**
   - Wash 125g dried red beans, add 312g water, place in electric pressure cooker, and select the "bean cooking mode" to cook until soft and tender.
   - Pour the cooked beans along with the liquid into a frying pan, stir-fry over medium heat, adding 40g granulated sugar during the process.
   - Stir-fry until the mixture noticeably thickens and has reduced fluidity when scraped with a spatula, then turn off the heat and spread to cool for later use.
   **Key**: Hot filling is wet; it firms upon cooling. Do not dry it out too much, or the texture will be affected; if too wet, it can cause hollow interiors and wrinkled, shrunken crusts after baking.

2. **Make the rice wine tangzhong**
   - In a saucepan, weigh 30g rice wine grains and 30g rice wine liquid, bring to a boil over medium heat, then remove from heat immediately.
   - Pour the boiled rice wine mixture into a bowl containing 30g bread flour, and quickly stir into a smooth paste.
   - Cover the paste surface tightly with plastic wrap lightly coated in oil, and let it cool before use.
   **Key**: The high heat cooks the flour and evaporates some alcohol, resulting in a more mellow aroma in the finished product.

3. **Knead the dough**
   - Place 182g bread flour, 30g granulated sugar, 3g salt, 2g instant dry yeast, 95g water, and all of the cooled rice wine tangzhong into the stand mixer bowl.
   - Knead on speed 3 for about 3 minutes until a dough forms, then increase to speed 6 and knead for about 5 minutes until a thick membrane can be stretched.
   - Add 15g softened butter, knead on speed 3 for about 3 minutes until the butter is absorbed, then increase to speed 6 and knead for about 4 minutes until a thin, transparent windowpane membrane can be stretched with a smooth, clean break edge.
   **Note**: If room temperature exceeds 20°C, use ice water. The finished dough temperature should not exceed 26°C; if too high, refrigerate for the first proof. Do not add the butter too early.

4. **First proof and portioning**
   - Round the dough, cover, and proof at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
   - Remove the dough, pat to deflate, divide evenly into 6 portions, and round each portion.
   **Key**: A slightly shorter first proof results in a fuller, rounder finished bun.

5. **Rest and fill**
   - Cover the rounded dough pieces with a damp cloth and rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
   - Take one dough piece, place seam side up, and flatten with the side of your hand into a round disc, thicker in the center and thinner at the edges.
   - Place about 50g of cooled red bean filling in the center of the disc, then gather the edges and pinch to seal tightly. Gently lift and pull to center the filling, and place seam side down.
   **Finished state**: The unbaked bun has a slightly domed center with a neat, sealed bottom.

6. **Second proof and baking**
   - Place the unbaked buns on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Proof at 30°C for about 40 minutes.
   - After proofing, cover the buns with a piece of parchment paper, and gently press a second baking sheet on top.
   - Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack, and bake at 200°C top heat and 190°C bottom heat for about 20 minutes, until both top and bottom surfaces are evenly browned.
   **Note**: If not sufficiently browned, extend the baking time as needed.

7. **Remove from oven and cool**
   - Remove the top baking sheet and parchment paper immediately after taking the buns out of the oven. Transfer the buns to a cooling rack to cool completely.
   **Characteristic**: The buns will not collapse or wrinkle after cooling; the skin is smooth and plump.

## Health Suggestion
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron. Combined with a fermented dough, they are easier to digest and suitable for moderate consumption by people managing blood sugar. Note that rice wine contains significant sugar; diabetics may consider reducing the sugar or substituting as needed.
