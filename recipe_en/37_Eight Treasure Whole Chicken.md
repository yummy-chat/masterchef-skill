# Eight Treasure Whole Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Whole chicken, about 2.6 lbs
🫘 Mung beans, about 1.2 lbs (skinned, soaked overnight)
🥩 Diced pork, as needed
🍄 Diced shiitake mushrooms, as needed
🌰 Chestnuts, as needed (shelled)
🫘 Lotus seeds, as needed (soaked, steamed, core removed)
🌱 Fresh lily bulbs, as needed
🦪 Conpoy, as needed
🥚 Salted egg yolks, as needed
🫚 Ginger, several slices
🧅 Scallions, several stalks
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste
🍾 Light soy sauce, a small amount (for brushing chicken skin)
🍶 Rice wine, as needed
🥫 Dark soy sauce, half a small bowl
🦪 Oyster sauce, 2 tablespoons
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Large wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Glass bowl
♨️ Steamer
🥄 Soup spoon
📌 Long needle (for releasing air)
🍳 Wok (for making sauce)
🫕 Large basin or bowl
🍽️ Large serving platter

## Steps
1. **Prepare the eight treasure filling**
   Mix together the skinned, soaked mung beans, diced pork, diced shiitake mushrooms, chestnuts, steamed and cored lotus seeds, fresh lily bulbs, conpoy, and salted egg yolks. Season with salt, sugar, and white pepper powder, and mix well.
2. **Debone the whole chicken**
   Start cutting at the chicken neck, being careful not to cut too far down to avoid piercing the skin. Separate the skin from the meat, break the joints, and use the back of the knife to help remove the bones completely while keeping the chicken skin intact, forming a "bag-like" shape.
   **Key point**: The cut should not extend past the chicken's shoulder area.
3. **Check the chicken skin for holes**
   Pour water into the chicken cavity from the neck opening. Lift the chicken to check for leaks, ensuring there are no breaks.
4. **Stuff the filling and seal**
   Fill the prepared eight treasure stuffing into the chicken cavity. For a 2.6 lb chicken, use about 1.2 lbs of filling; do not overstuff. Bring the chicken head over the two wings, thread it through the middle, and tie a knot to secure the chicken's shape.
5. **Blanch to set the shape**
   Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the stuffed chicken until the skin tightens and changes color.
   **Note**: Use a long needle to prick holes in the chicken skin to release air and prevent the skin from bursting. Blanch for about 1–2 minutes.
6. **Brush for color**
   Remove the chicken and, while still hot, evenly brush the surface with a small amount of light soy sauce.
7. **Deep-fry to set the shape**
   Heat oil to about 150–160°C (300–320°F). Fry the chicken for about 2 minutes until the skin is golden brown and tightened.
   **Note**: Again, use the needle to release air to prevent the skin from bursting.
8. **Prepare the steaming sauce**
   Heat a wok with a little oil. Sauté ginger slices and scallion segments until fragrant. Splash in rice wine. Add enough water, then season with 2 tablespoons of salt, 1 tablespoon of sugar, half a small bowl of dark soy sauce, 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce, and a few peppercorns. Bring to a boil.
9. **Steam to infuse flavor**
   Place the fried chicken in a large bowl. Pour the steaming sauce over the chicken. Place in a steamer and steam over medium heat for 1.5 hours, until the chicken is tender and the filling has absorbed the sauce and expanded.
10. **Plate and drizzle with sauce**
    Remove the steamed chicken and arrange it on a serving platter. The platter can be garnished with blanched broccoli. Drizzle the reserved original sauce from the bowl over the chicken and serve.

## Health Advice
This Eight Treasure Whole Chicken is rich in nutrients. The mung beans and lotus seeds in the filling aid digestion, while the conpoy and salted egg yolks provide high-quality protein. It is suitable for those who are weak or need an energy boost. Note that it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to serve with light vegetables. For those with high blood pressure, reduce the amount of dark soy sauce and oyster sauce.
