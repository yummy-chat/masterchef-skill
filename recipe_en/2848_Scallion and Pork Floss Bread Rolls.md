# Scallion and Pork Floss Bread Rolls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 460g (for dough)
🌾 Bread flour 50g (for tangzhong)
🍬 Caster sugar 56g (for dough)
🍬 Caster sugar 5g (for tangzhong)
🧂 Salt 7g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 82g
💧 Water 180g (use ice water in summer)
🍄 Fresh yeast 15g (or 5g instant dry yeast)
🥛 Milk powder 28g
🧈 Unsalted butter 46g
🧅 Chopped scallions, as needed
🍘 Seaweed pork floss, as needed
🌾 White sesame seeds, as needed
🥄 Mayonnaise, as needed (for surface grid, filling, and dipping ends)
🫗 Boiling water 55g (for tangzhong)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍞 Stand mixer (with dough hook)
⚖️ Digital scale
🥛 Glass measuring cup
🌡️ Thermometer
⏲️ Timer
🥣 Rolling pin
🔲 Non-stick baking pan (28x28cm)
📄 Baking paper
🗡️ Plastic scraper (for poking holes)
🔥 Convection oven
🌬️ Cooling rack
🔪 Serrated knife
💼 Piping bag
🧤 Gloves
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
### Prepare Tangzhong
1. Mix 50g bread flour, 5g caster sugar, and 55g boiling water until no dry flour remains.
2. Cover tightly with plastic wrap, refrigerate until cool, then use.
   **Key point**: Tangzhong can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator to make the bread softer and more moist.

### Knead the Dough
1. Place ice water, whole egg liquid, caster sugar, salt, yeast, milk powder, bread flour, and chilled tangzhong into the stand mixer.
2. Knead on low speed for 3 minutes until no dry flour remains, forming a sticky dough.
3. Switch to high speed and knead for about 7 minutes until the dough can be stretched into a rough membrane. Dough temperature should be 24–25°C.
   **Note**: In summer, always use ice water. If the dough temperature gets too high, freeze the dough for 5 minutes to cool it down before continuing.

### Add Butter
4. Add softened unsalted butter, wrap it with the dough, place back in the bowl, and knead on low speed for 3–4 minutes until the butter is fully absorbed and the dough comes together again.
5. Switch to high speed and knead for 2–3 minutes until the dough reaches full gluten development. The final dough temperature should not exceed 28°C.
   **Key point**: Wrapping the butter helps it incorporate faster and prevents sticking; in summer, keep the total kneading time short.

### First Fermentation
6. Round the dough and place it in a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and ferment at around 25°C for about 60 minutes until doubled in size.
   **Check**: Dip a finger in flour and poke a hole; the hole should slowly bounce back without collapsing.

### Divide and Rest
7. Remove the dough and divide it into 2 equal portions. Pre-shape each into a square by folding and sealing the edges, then cover with plastic wrap and rest for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Pre-shaping helps with even rolling later; resting relaxes the gluten to prevent shrinkage.

### Roll and Shape
8. Lightly dust the work surface and dough with flour to prevent sticking. Roll each rested dough piece into a square about 28×28cm.
9. Transfer the dough sheet to a baking pan lined with baking paper, gently press it into the corners, and thin the edges.
10. Use a plastic scraper to poke holes evenly across the dough surface to prevent large bubbles during baking.
    **Key point**: When rolling, ensure the bottom does not stick to the work surface. Try to roll to the desired size in one go to avoid repeated stretching.

### Second Fermentation and Decoration
11. Place the baking pan in a warm, humid place and ferment for about 30–40 minutes until the dough sheet expands to about 1.5 times its original size.
12. Brush the surface with whole egg liquid, then evenly sprinkle chopped scallions, seaweed pork floss, and white sesame seeds.
13. Pipe mayonnaise in a grid pattern on the surface.
    **Key point**: Mayonnaise helps the filling stick and moisturizes the crust, preventing cracking after baking.

### Baking
14. Preheat the convection oven to 180°C. Place the baking pan in the oven and bake for 11 minutes until the surface is golden brown.
    **Key point**: Bake at high heat for a short time; overbaking makes the crust thick and hard, causing cracks when rolling.

### Remove from Oven and Roll
15. Immediately after baking, invert the bread sheet onto a cooling rack and peel off the bottom baking paper.
16. Cover the top with a new piece of baking paper and let cool for about 10 minutes until warm.
17. Flip the bread sheet so the bottom faces up, spread a layer of mayonnaise, and sprinkle with an appropriate amount of seaweed pork floss.
18. Using the bottom baking paper and the edge of the baking pan, tightly roll up the bread. Wrap tightly in baking paper, twist the ends closed, and let set for about 1 hour.
    **Key point**: Roll while the bread is still warm; it will crack if cooled completely. Make sure to wrap tightly and let it rest, or it will unroll.

### Cut and Decorate
19. Unwrap the baking paper, trim off the uneven ends, and cut each roll into 3 equal pieces.
20. Spread mayonnaise on the cut ends of each piece and dip them in seaweed pork floss.

## Health Advice
These scallion and pork floss bread rolls are relatively high in calories and carbohydrates. They are suitable as an occasional breakfast or energy snack. For weight loss, it is recommended to control the portion size. Bread flour and eggs provide quality protein and B vitamins, while mayonnaise and sugar make this a high-energy food. Pairing with unsweetened black coffee is suggested to balance the flavor.
