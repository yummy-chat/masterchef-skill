# Quick Puff Pastry
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 100g all-purpose flour (or 50g high-gluten flour + 50g low-gluten flour)
🧈 100g chilled butter (cut into approximately 1cm cubes, divided into 20g and 80g portions)
💧 45g cold water
🧂 1g salt

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Food processor
🫓 Silicone kneading mat
🪒 Dough scraper
🥖 Rolling pin
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🧺 Sifter/flour container (for dusting)

## Instructions
1. Place the all-purpose flour, salt, and 20g of butter cubes into the food processor. Pulse 8 to 10 times until the mixture resembles fine sand.
   **Key point**: First, fully incorporate a small amount of butter with the flour to prevent gluten development later.
2. Add the remaining 80g of butter cubes. Gently shake to distribute evenly, then pulse 2 times.
3. Add half of the cold water and pulse 2 times. Then add the remaining water and pulse 4 times.
   **Consistency check**: The mixture should be flaky, with visible large butter chunks remaining. This is key for flakiness.
4. Turn the mixture out onto a lightly floured kneading mat. Quickly gather and press it into a dough ball. Do not knead.
5. Dust the dough surface with flour and roll it into a rectangular sheet approximately 0.5 cm thick.
6. Perform a single three-fold on the dough sheet, using a dough scraper to prevent sticking.
7. Starting from the folded end, tightly roll the dough into a cylindrical log.
8. Dust the surface with flour, flatten the log from top to bottom, and shape it into a square. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
9. Remove the rested dough, dust with flour, and roll it out to 0.5 cm thickness. Use a 9 cm round cutter to cut out circles.
10. Press the dough circles into egg tart molds to shape them. Fill with egg tart custard to nine-tenths full.
11. Preheat the oven to 200°C (actual temperature). Bake for about 25 minutes, until the surface is golden brown and crispy.

## Health Advice
This puff pastry uses butter chunks instead of lamination to create layers. It is high in calories but produces distinct, flaky layers. It is suitable for occasional consumption or as a quick dessert for guests. Ensure the dough rests adequately in the refrigerator to prevent shrinkage during baking.
