# Peanut Lazy Tofu
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Soybeans (soaked overnight in advance)
🥜 Peanuts (most ground into a paste, a small portion crushed for garnish)
🥬 Two bunches of bok choy (suitable for summer; can be substituted with napa cabbage in winter)
🧅 Scallions (scallion segments for boiling beans, chopped scallions for stir-frying)
🧄 Ginger (ginger slices for boiling beans)
🧄 Garlic (minced garlic for stir-frying)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns (for boiling soybeans)
⭐ Star anise (for boiling soybeans)
🧂 Salt (used twice: 10 grams for boiling beans, 2 grams for stir-frying)
🧂 MSG, a pinch
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste (optional)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Plate
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🔄 Hand-cranked meat grinder (or food processor)
🥣 Small bowl

## Steps
1. Pour water into a wok, add the soaked soybeans, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, scallion segments, ginger slices, and 10 grams of salt.
2. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce heat to low. Cook for 20 minutes until the soybeans can be easily crushed between fingers.
3. In another clean wok without oil, dry-roast the peanuts over low heat until cooked through. Remove, let cool, and rub off the skins.
   **Key Point**: Use low heat throughout to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
4. Place about 2/3 of the peeled peanuts and all the cooked soybeans into the meat grinder. Add some of the soy bean cooking liquid and grind into a fine, thick paste. Set aside.
5. Crush the remaining 1/3 of the peanuts with the flat side of a knife. Place in a small bowl and set aside.
6. Heat oil in a wok over low heat. Sauté the chopped scallions and minced garlic until fragrant.
7. Add the sliced bok choy and stir-fry quickly over high heat.
8. Add two ladles of water and bring to a boil. Season with 2 grams of salt and a pinch of MSG.
9. Pour in the ground soybean-peanut paste and immediately reduce heat to the **lowest setting**.
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat on the lowest setting and stir continuously to prevent scorching. Cook until it comes to a boil; for a thicker consistency, extend the cooking time as desired.
10. Before removing from heat, drizzle in a little sesame oil. Transfer to a bowl and top with crushed peanuts and chopped scallions. Add chili oil to taste if desired.

## Health Tips
Peanut Lazy Tofu is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for vegetarians and those aiming to control calorie intake. Adding Sichuan peppercorns and star anise when boiling the beans aids digestion. However, when cooking the bean paste, keep the heat low and stir continuously to prevent scorching, which can affect the texture.
