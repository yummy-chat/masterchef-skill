# Homemade Crunchy Noodle Snack  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍝 Dried noodles (thin type) appropriate amount  
🍄🍗 Mushroom and chicken frozen puree cubes 2 pieces  
🌾 White sesame seeds appropriate amount  
🧂 Seasoning powder appropriate amount (specific ingredients not specified)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (sesame oil or salad oil)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Knife  
🔥 Oven  
📜 Baking tray and parchment paper  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add dried noodles and mushroom chicken frozen cubes, and cook together until the noodles soften and the frozen cubes are completely melted.  
   **Note**: Cooking noodles and frozen ingredients together saves time and enhances the base flavor.  
2. Remove the cooked noodles, drain well, and place them in a bowl. While still hot, add seasoning powder, cooking oil, and white sesame seeds, then mix thoroughly with chopsticks.  
3. Lay plastic wrap on a cutting board, place the mixed noodles in the center, roll them into a long cylindrical shape of even thickness using the plastic wrap, and twist both ends tightly.  
   **Key**: Roll as tightly as possible, expelling air, to prevent loosening when slicing later.  
4. Place the noodle roll in the freezer until completely frozen and firm to the touch.  
   **Note**: Must be frozen solid, otherwise it cannot be sliced smoothly.  
5. Remove the frozen noodle roll and slice it into even rounds about 0.5–1 cm thick.  
   **Key**: Ensure uniform thickness for even baking.  
6. Line a baking tray with parchment paper, arrange the slices flat on it, and bake in a preheated oven at 160–180°C until golden brown, moisture is removed, and the texture is hard and crispy.  
   **Note**: Baking temperature and time should be adjusted flexibly according to your oven; monitor the condition closely.  

## Health Advice  
Homemade crunchy noodle snacks are baked instead of fried, reducing oil intake. However, they are high in carbohydrates, so consume in moderation. Suitable for an occasional treat, not recommended as a daily snack.
