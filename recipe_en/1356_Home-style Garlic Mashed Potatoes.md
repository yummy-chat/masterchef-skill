# Home-style Garlic Mashed Potatoes
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, about 400g (peeled)
🧈 Butter, 25g
🥛 Milk, about 100g
🧄 Garlic, 3 cloves (sliced or chopped)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌿 Black pepper, generous amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Peeler
🔪 Chef's knife
🍲 Stockpot
🥛 Small saucepan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍴 Fork
🥣 Glass bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into chunks. Slice the garlic and chop the scallions.
2. Place the milk and sliced garlic in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat until bubbles form at the edges and it is about to boil, then turn off the heat. Let it sit and steep to extract the garlic flavor.
3. Bring water to a boil in a stockpot. Add the potato chunks and a little salt. Cook over medium heat for about half an hour, until a knife can be easily inserted. Drain the potatoes thoroughly.
4. While still hot, place the cooked potatoes into a bowl. Add the butter, salt, and a generous amount of black pepper. Mash with a fork, leaving a few small lumps for texture.
5. Gradually add the warm garlic milk to the mashed potatoes in small amounts, stirring thoroughly after each addition, until the texture is thick and smooth.
   **Note**: The amount of milk can be adjusted based on the consistency; it does not need to be exact. The garlic slices can be strained out or left in, as desired.
6. Serve on a plate. Sprinkle with the remaining black pepper and chopped scallions to taste.

## Health Advice
This garlic mashed potato dish contains butter and milk. It is high in carbohydrates but provides strong satiety, making it suitable as an energy boost after exercise. Be mindful of the amount of salt and black pepper used to avoid excess. Leaving some lumps when mashing helps prevent over-gelatinization, which can cause blood sugar spikes.
