# Tomato and Shrimp Ball Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 2 tomatoes (added whole to the pot, peeled and crushed after softening)  
🦐 Shrimp paste, as needed (shaped into balls)  
🧅 Chopped green onions, a little (for garnish)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for basic savory flavor)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or stew pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. In a pot where meat such as chicken wings or ribs is already simmering, keep the heat on medium-low. Add the whole tomatoes and cook together with side ingredients like potatoes until the tomato skin cracks and softens.  
2. Use a slotted spoon to remove the softened tomatoes. Let them cool slightly, then peel off the skin. Crush the tomatoes into a paste by hand or with a spoon, and return the paste to the original broth, stirring well to turn the soup red.  
3. Bring the soup to a gentle boil again, then reduce to medium-low heat, maintaining a slight movement on the surface. Squeeze the shrimp paste into ball shapes, and drop them into the soup one by one.  
4. Do not stir immediately after adding the shrimp balls. Once they naturally float to the surface and turn red, gently push the bottom of the pot to prevent sticking. Cook for about 1 more minute, then turn off the heat.  
5. Taste the soup, add salt as needed for seasoning, then pour into a serving bowl. Sprinkle with chopped green onions.  

## Health Tips  
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene and vitamin C, while shrimp paste provides high-quality protein. This dish is low in fat and easy to digest, suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or needing nutritional supplementation. Note that shrimp balls should not be stirred immediately after entering the pot; they are done when they float, as prolonged cooking will make them tough.
