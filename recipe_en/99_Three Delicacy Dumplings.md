# Three Delicacy Dumplings
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork front leg meat 1000g (fat-to-lean ratio 1:9)
🦐 Dried krill 50g (no need to soak, chopped)
🍤 Fresh shrimp 50g (whole or cut into small pieces)
🥬 Chinese chives 150g (chopped)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced together with the meat)
🧂 White pepper powder 2g
🧴 Light soy sauce 50g
🧴 Dark soy sauce 16g
🫒 Sesame oil 16g
🧂 Salt 9g
💧 Water approximately 300g (added in three batches)
🥜 Peanut oil approximately 100g (preheated and cooled)

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Meat grinder
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍲 Cooking pot

## Instructions
1.  Place the pork and ginger into a meat grinder and grind into an extremely fine filling, achieving a consistency where chopsticks inserted stand upright without falling.
    **Note**: The filling must be fine enough to fully absorb the water.

2.  Add white pepper powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt to the filling. Stir evenly in one direction.
    **Key**: Do not add thirteen-spice powder, Sichuan pepper powder, or cooking wine, as they may overpower the fresh flavors.

3.  Add approximately 300g of water to the filling in three batches. After each addition, stir vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed and resistance increases significantly before adding the next batch, until the filling is well hydrated.
    **Key**: Adding water in batches and stirring continuously is the core method for achieving a tender and juicy filling.

4.  Pour the preheated and cooled peanut oil over the filling and stir evenly to make the filling glossy and oiled.
    **Note**: The oil must be added after the water; the order cannot be reversed.

5.  Add the chopped krill and shrimp in sequence, gently mixing each time. Finally, add the chopped chives and gently mix until just combined.
    **Note**: Add the chives last and do not use too large an amount; avoid over-mixing to prevent the filling from becoming loose.

6.  Wrap the filling into dumplings. Boil them in a pot of water until they float and the wrappers puff up, then remove.
    **Key**: Use plenty of water for boiling, and add two splashes of cold water to ensure the wrappers are cooked through and the filling is done.

## Health Advice
This three delicacy dumpling offers balanced nutrition. Pork provides high-quality protein and B vitamins, while shrimp and krill supplement calcium and zinc. Chives aid digestion. It is suitable for those needing extra energy or with anemia. However, as chives are warm in nature, those with stomach heat should consume in moderation.
