# Wang Xilou's Mixed Endive Salad
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Endive (curly endive) one small bowl  
🧄 Minced garlic to taste  
🫚 Minced ginger to taste  
🧅 Minced scallion to taste  
⚪ White sesame seeds to taste  
🌶️ Dried chili pepper rings to taste  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  
🍬 Sugar to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🫗 Sesame oil to taste  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors  
🥣 Large bowl  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥄 Small bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Use scissors to trim away the tough outer roots of the endive. Rinse thoroughly under clean water, then soak in clean water for 20 minutes. Remove and drain completely. Cut into bite-sized pieces with a knife.
2. In a small bowl, place the minced garlic, minced ginger, minced scallion, white sesame seeds, and dried chili pepper rings as the base.
3. Pour cooking oil into a wok and add the Sichuan peppercorns. Heat over medium-low heat until the oil reaches about 70% hot (when the peppercorns release a strong aroma). Quickly pour the hot oil and peppercorns over the base mixture in the small bowl to release the fragrance.
4. Add sugar, salt, sesame oil, and fragrant vinegar to the aromatic mixture in the small bowl. Stir thoroughly with a spoon to create the dressing.
5. Pour the prepared dressing over the endive pieces. Use chopsticks to toss from the bottom upward until evenly mixed. Plate and serve.
   **Note**: The amounts of seasonings in the dressing can be adjusted according to personal taste. The ideal balance is savory with a hint of sour and sweetness, moderate numbing and spiciness that does not overpower the vegetable's flavor.

## Health Tips
Endive is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber. Consuming it in spring helps clear the liver, improve vision, and promote digestion. It is especially suitable for those experiencing internal heat or needing to manage blood sugar levels. When making a cold salad, use less salt and oil, and avoid high-temperature cooking to preserve nutrients. It is best to prepare and consume immediately.
