# Homemade Cola
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Cinnamon, to taste
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, to taste (aged dried tangerine peel recommended)
🌱 Coriander seeds, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, about 1 small bunch (roots must be included)
🍋 Lemon, to taste (some sliced, some juiced)
🍂 Black tea, to taste (rinsed before use)
💧 Water, to taste (including hot water added when caramelizing sugar)
🍬 White sugar, to taste (for caramelizing)
🧊 Aged rock sugar, to taste (adds sweetness depth)
🧂 Baking soda, to taste (reacts with lemon juice to create bubbles)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Shallow white pot
🥄 Spoon
🧊 Glass bowl
🕸️ Strainer
🔥 Gas stove

## Steps
1. Place cinnamon, dried tangerine peel, coriander seeds, and other dry spices into the pot.
   **Key Point**: Cinnamon is essential; aged dried tangerine peel provides better flavor.
2. Turn on the heat, add white sugar to the dry spices, and stir-fry over medium-low heat.
   **Visual Cue**: Cook until the sugar is completely melted and the mixture turns a light reddish-brown.
   **Key Point**: Caramelizing the sugar is the crucial step for achieving cola's color.
3. Quickly pour hot water into the caramelized sugar.
   **Visual Cue**: The liquid in the pot boils vigorously.
   **Note**: Adding hot water to hot caramel will cause a violent reaction; operate with caution.
4. Add cilantro with roots, lemon slices, aged rock sugar, and rinsed black tea to the pot. Keep on medium heat and simmer for about 10 minutes.
   **Key Point**: All ingredients must be added together to allow their flavors to fully meld.
5. Turn off the heat. Strain the mixture in the pot using a strainer to extract the dark brown liquid, discarding the solid residue.
6. Squeeze fresh lemon juice and mix it with baking soda in a glass bowl.
   **Visual Cue**: The mixture should bubble vigorously.
7. Immediately pour the mixed lemon and soda water into the filtered cola base, stirring gently.
   **Visual Cue**: A large number of bubbles form in the liquid, mimicking a carbonated beverage.
   **Key Point**: This step is essential for creating bubbles and should be done right before drinking.
8. Pour the prepared cola into a glass filled with ice and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Homemade cola uses herbs and caramelized sugar to mimic the classic flavor, containing fewer additives and less syrup than commercial versions. It is suitable for occasional cravings or those controlling sugar intake. Note that the bubbles produced by the reaction of baking soda and lemon juice require immediate consumption, as the carbonation will dissipate over time.
