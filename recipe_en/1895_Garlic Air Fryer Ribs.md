# Garlic Air Fryer Ribs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork spare ribs, about 2 pounds (soak in water for two hours in advance, cut into small sections)
🧄 Garlic, half a head (about 50 grams, minced)
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (mix with minced garlic to make garlic seasoning)
🍶 Yellow wine, as needed
🧂 Soy sauce, as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed
🌶️ Ground black pepper, as needed
🧂 Salt, a little
🍬 White sugar, as needed
🧂 MSG, as needed
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (or use a starchy garlic seasoning version)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Air fryer
💨 Spray oil bottle
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the pork spare ribs in clean water for two hours in advance, wash and drain, then cut into small sections.

2. Use kitchen paper towels to thoroughly dry the surface moisture of each rib section.
   **Key point**: Drying the moisture is a prerequisite for flavor absorption and forming a crispy texture.

3. Place the ribs in a bowl. Add yellow wine, soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground black pepper, salt, white sugar, and MSG in sequence. Use your hands to mix and knead thoroughly for about seven to eight minutes, until all the liquid is absorbed.

4. In advance, mix the minced garlic with sesame oil to make garlic seasoning. Pour it into the ribs and mix well. Then add an appropriate amount of cornstarch and toss until each rib section is evenly coated with a thin layer of batter.
   **Note**: Mixing the minced garlic with sesame oil in advance prevents the garlic from drying out during cooking and releases its aroma.

5. Let the marinated ribs sit for two hours to allow full flavor absorption.

6. Line the air fryer basket with parchment paper. Place the ribs one by one with the bone facing downward, spreading them flat without overlapping. Use a spray oil bottle to evenly spray a layer of oil on the surface of the ribs.

7. Cook at 180°C for 13 minutes.

8. Pull out the basket. Use chopsticks to turn each rib piece over.

9. Cook again at 180°C for about 8 to 13 minutes, until both sides are deep reddish-brown, the surface is slightly charred, and you hear a sizzling sound of oil bubbling. The garlic particles should be golden.
   **Key point**: The second cooking time should be adjusted flexibly based on the performance of your air fryer and the condition of the ribs. Be sure to observe during the last few minutes to avoid burning.

10. Remove and plate.

## Health Advice
This garlic rib recipe uses an air fryer to reduce oil intake, but the marinade has a high sodium content. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the meal. Ribs are rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making them suitable for people in recovery from fitness or needing a boost in energy. Those with high blood lipids should control their portion size.
