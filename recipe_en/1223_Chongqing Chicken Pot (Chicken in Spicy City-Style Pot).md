# Chongqing Chicken Pot (Chicken in Spicy City-Style Pot)

Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh (drumstick) 1000g (cut into 3-4cm pieces, can be substituted with chicken wings or whole chicken)  
🧅 Onion approx. 150g (cut into chunks)  
🥬 Celery approx. 150g (cut into segments)  
🍢 Assorted meatballs, tofu puffs, rice cakes, etc. to taste (as side dishes, add according to preference)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili to taste (sprinkle in before serving to enhance flavor)  
🌿 Cilantro to taste (sprinkle in before serving for garnish)  
🫘 Sesame seeds to taste (sprinkle in before serving for garnish)  
🧄 Ginger slices 20g  
🧄 Garlic slices 20g  
🫒 Cooking oil 50g  
🍶 Yellow wine 50g  
🫕 Hot pot base paste about 15g (use per 500g of chicken)  
🥣 Broth 500g (can reduce to 200g if no side dishes are added)  

**Chicken Pot Marinade (approx. 400g total, use 100g marinade per 500g chicken)** :  
🦪 Oyster sauce 100g  
🫘 Chu Hou paste 120g  
🍲 Satay sauce 80g  
🦐 Hoisin sauce 70g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 65g  
🧂 Salt 8g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 10g  
🧂 MSG 5g  
🧂 Thirteen Spices powder 2g  
🧂 Four-Treasure Concentrated Freshness Powder 1g  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🫗 Measuring cup (with lid)  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🫕 Clay pot (deep pot)  
🥄 Stir-fry spatula  
🔥 Gas stove/induction cooktop  
🫙 Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps  

1. **Make the Chicken Pot Marinade**  
   In a large bowl, sequentially add oyster sauce, Chu Hou paste, satay sauce, hoisin sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, chicken bouillon powder, MSG, Thirteen Spices powder, and Four-Treasure Concentrated Freshness Powder. Weigh accurately with a digital scale and stir thoroughly until well combined.  
   **Note**: Once the paste is mixed evenly, it can be transferred to a non-metal container, sealed, and refrigerated for storage. It can be kept for 2-3 weeks.  

2. **Marinate the Chicken**  
   Wash the chicken thighs and cut into pieces. Place in a large bowl and pour in the marinade at a ratio of 100g marinade per 500g chicken. Wearing gloves, repeatedly knead and mix the chicken with the marinade until each piece is evenly coated in the paste. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 4 hours.  

## Cooking Steps  

1. **Stir-fry Aromatics and Vegetable Base**  
   Heat the clay pot and add 50g cooking oil. When the oil reaches 50% heat (approx. 150°C/300°F), add 20g ginger slices and 20g garlic slices. Stir-fry until fragrant. Then add 150g onion chunks and 150g celery segments. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the vegetables soften and release their aroma.  

2. **Stir-fry Chicken and First Braise**  
   Add the marinated chicken along with some of the marinade liquid into the clay pot. Stir-fry together with the vegetable base until well combined. Splash in 50g yellow wine, add about 15g of crushed hot pot base paste, and continue stir-frying until the sauce and ingredients are integrated. There should be no water added at this point. Cover the pot and braise over low heat for 2 minutes.  
   **State Check**: When you open the lid, there should be sufficient liquid released from the chicken and vegetables.  

3. **Add Broth for Stewing**  
   Gently stir to prevent sticking to the bottom, then pour in 500g broth.  

4. **Second Braise**  
   Cover the pot again and braise over medium heat for 5 minutes, allowing the chicken to cook through fully and absorb the liquid.  

5. **Add Side Dishes and Reduce Sauce**  
   Open the lid and add the side dishes: first add the longer-cooking ingredients like meatballs and root vegetables, then add the quick-cooking leafy greens. Do not cover the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat until the side dishes are cooked through while reducing the sauce to a thick consistency. This takes about 3 minutes.  

6. **Plate and Serve**  
   Turn off the heat. Garnish with chopped bird's eye chili, sesame seeds, and cilantro according to preference. The sauce should be rich and thick, evenly coating all ingredients.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein, but the marinade and hot pot base paste contain significant amounts of sodium and sugar. Those on a weight-loss plan should monitor total calorie intake. The collagen in the broth is beneficial for joints. For the side dishes, it is recommended to choose low-fat varieties of meatballs if available.
