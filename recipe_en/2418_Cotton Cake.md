# Cotton Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Milk 50g  
🍊 Orange Juice 40g (strained)  
🌽 Corn Oil 80g  
🌾 Cake Flour 110g (sifted)  
🥚 Eggs 8 (separated into whites and yolks)  
🌽 Cornstarch 10g  
🍬 Granulated Sugar 70g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl (grease-free and water-free)  
🪮 Sieve  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🥄 Whisk  
🔌 Electric Hand Mixer  
🍴 Spatula  
🍳 Square Baking Pan (28×28cm, lined with baking cloth)  
🔥 Oven  
🧺 Cooling Rack  
🫗 Plastic Wrap  
🧻 Baking Cloth & Parchment Paper  

## Instructions  
1. **Emulsify Liquids**: Combine milk, orange juice, and corn oil. Whisk thoroughly with a hand whisk until emulsified.  
2. **Scald Flour**: Sift in the cake flour. Mix quickly using a "draw a line" or cutting motion until no dry flour remains. Avoid overmixing.  
3. **Separate Eggs**: Separate the egg whites from the yolks. Place the whites in a grease-free and water-free bowl; freeze for 5 minutes to increase stability. Set the yolks aside.  
4. **Make Egg Yolk Batter**: Add the egg yolks to the flour mixture. Mix with a whisk until completely smooth and lump-free. When lifting the whisk, the dripping batter should form lines that slowly disappear. If too dry, add a little milk; if too thin, add a little cake flour. Cover and set aside.  
5. **Preheat Oven**: Preheat the oven to 150°C (upper and lower heat). Place an empty baking pan on the lowest rack and pour in hot water.  
6. **Whip Egg Whites**: Add all the granulated sugar to the egg whites. Beat at the highest speed with an electric hand mixer until clear lines appear.  
7. **Stabilize Meringue**: Add the cornstarch. Briefly mix with the beaters, then continue beating at the lowest speed until the meringue is smooth and forms firm, small peaks when lifted. Lines in the bowl should be clear.  
8. **Combine Batter**: Take 1/3 of the meringue and quickly fold it into the egg yolk batter. Then pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue. First, roughly mix with the hand mixer, then switch to a spatula. Use cutting and folding motions to mix thoroughly until the batter is thick and glossy.  
9. **Pour into Pan & Remove Air Bubbles**: Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan from a height of 20cm. Gently tap the pan twice to release large air bubbles.  
10. **Bake**: Place the pan in the lower-middle rack of the oven. Bake at 150°C for 55-60 minutes, until the surface is golden brown and springs back when lightly touched.  
11. **Cool Upside Down**: Immediately after baking, invert the cake onto another piece of baking cloth. Peel off the bottom cloth. Cover with a clean piece of parchment paper to prevent drying. Transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely.  
12. **Slice**: After cooling, flip the cake over and slice into pieces. Serve with whipped cream or jam if desired.  

## Health Tips  
This cotton cake uses a water bath method, resulting in a moist and tender texture suitable for the elderly and children. The orange juice adds a refreshing fruity aroma, but the sugar content is not low, so control the serving size. Be careful not to overmix when scalding the flour. The meringue must be beaten to firm peaks; otherwise, the cake may collapse.
