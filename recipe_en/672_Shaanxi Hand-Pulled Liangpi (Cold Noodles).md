# Shaanxi Hand-Pulled Liangpi (Cold Noodles)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍜 Hand-pulled Liangpi (pre-steamed semi-finished product, as needed)  
🧈 Gluten (as needed, torn into small pieces)  
🌶️ Coarse chili powder (Qin椒) 80g  
🌶️ Fine chili powder (Qin椒) 80g  
🧂 Spice powder 50g (ground from Sichuan pepper, fennel seeds, cinnamon, galangal, cardamom, star anise, bay leaves, and black cardamom in proportion)  
🫘 Peeled white sesame seeds 50g  
🫘 Sesame powder 30g  
🧅 Scallions, ginger, onion, cilantro (as needed, chopped for oil preparation)  
🍬 Rock sugar powder (a small amount, to reduce spiciness)  
🍶 Baijiu (a small amount, for cooling and enhancing aroma)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil 1000g (about 2 jin)  
🧂 Salt 30g (for seasoning water)  
🧂 MSG 6g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 3g  
🌿 Fennel seeds, bay leaves, Sichuan pepper (a small amount each, for seasoning water)  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥄 Long-handled ladle (horse ladle)  
🥣 Bowl (for holding dry ingredients, chili oil, etc.)  
🍽️ Plate  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the Spice Powder and Aromatics**: Weigh and set aside the spice powder, coarse chili powder, fine chili powder, peeled white sesame seeds, and sesame powder. Chop the scallions, ginger, onion, and cilantro for later use.  
2. **Make the Seasoning Water**: Add about 2 jin of water to a pot and bring to a boil. Add a small amount of fennel seeds, bay leaves, and Sichuan pepper. Simmer until fragrant, then remove and discard the solids. Turn off the heat, add 30g salt, 6g MSG, and 3g chicken bouillon powder, stirring until dissolved. Pour out and let cool for later use.  
   **Note**: Do not boil the solids for too long, or the flavor will become overpowering and affect the clean taste. Seasonings should only be added after turning off the heat.  
3. **Prepare the Rapeseed Oil**: Pour 2 jin of rapeseed oil into a wok and heat over high heat until thick smoke rises continuously to remove the raw oil flavor. Turn off the heat and let the oil cool naturally.  
   **Key Point**: The rapeseed oil must be fully heated.  
4. **Fry the Aromatics**: Once the oil temperature drops and no longer smokes, add the scallion segments, ginger slices, onion chunks, and cilantro. Fry over low heat slowly until the aromatics become dry and golden. Remove and discard the solids, retaining the flavored oil.  
   **Purpose**: To remove any oily taste and infuse the oil with vegetable fragrance.  
5. **Make the Chili Oil**:  
   - **First Pour**: When the oil starts to produce small bubbles, add 50g of peeled white sesame seeds and fry until they float.  
   - **Second Pour**: Immediately sprinkle in 50g of spice powder and quickly mix evenly.  
   - **Third Pour**: Once the oil temperature drops to about 180°C (356°F), add 80g of coarse chili powder, stirring constantly to ensure even heating and to release the aroma.  
   - **Fourth Pour**: After the oil temperature lowers further, add 30g of sesame powder and 80g of fine chili powder simultaneously, stirring quickly to prevent burning.  
   **Note**: Strictly follow the oil temperature stages when adding ingredients. Different powders have varying heat tolerance, so adding them in stages ensures layered flavors.  
6. **Season and Rest**: Add a small amount of rock sugar powder to the final chili oil and mix well. Immediately drizzle in a small amount of baijiu and stir thoroughly. Pour the prepared chili oil into a bowl, cover, and let it rest overnight to allow the flavors to fully meld, resulting in a thicker, richer oil.  
7. **Prepare the Liangpi**: Fold the hand-pulled Liangpi and cut into thin strips. Gently separate them with your hands to prevent sticking. Tear the gluten into small pieces.  
   **Note**: Fermented Liangpi naturally has a slight sourness, so additional vinegar is usually not needed.  
8. **Mix and Serve**: Place an appropriate amount of Liangpi and gluten in a mixing bowl. First, add a spoonful of the seasoning water. Then, dip the Liangpi strips into the chili oil bowl to coat each piece evenly in the red oil. Mix thoroughly until the noodles are glossy and red.  

## Health Tips  
This Liangpi dish is rich in spiciness, but the technique of adding oil in stages and using rock sugar to reduce the heat balances the sensation, making it suitable for those who love spicy food but are concerned about getting overheated. The seasoning water is light, and the fermented Liangpi naturally has a slight sourness, making it appetizing and relatively low in fat. Resting the chili oil overnight enhances its flavor significantly.
