# Creamy Salmon with Vegetables
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Salmon fillet 1 piece (thick-cut)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes 6-8 (halved)
🧅 Red onion to taste (diced)
🧄 Garlic to taste (minced)
🥬 Fresh spinach 1 large handful
🧀 Parmesan cheese approx. 20g (grated or powder)
🧂 Olive oil to taste
🧂 Salt 1 tsp
🧂 Ground black pepper to taste
🧈 Butter 1 small piece
🥛 Heavy cream half can (or use whole milk, slightly less)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🧀 Grater
🍽️ Serving plate
🔪 Knife & cutting board

## Cooking Steps
1. Halve the cherry tomatoes, dice the red onion, and mince the garlic.
   **Key Point**: Halving cherry tomatoes helps maintain their shape for a more attractive presentation.

2. Add olive oil to a frying pan, place the salmon fillet in, and sear one side until golden and crispy on the surface, then remove and set aside.
   **Key Point**: For a deeper color, extend the searing time slightly; a bit more oil helps with browning.

3. Without washing the pan, add a little more oil, stir-fry the diced onion and minced garlic over low heat until fragrant.
   **Note**: Be sure to use low heat to avoid burning.

4. Add the cherry tomatoes and stir-fry until slightly softened and releasing some liquid.
   **Key Point**: This step draws out some moisture, making the sauce richer.

5. Pour in the heavy cream, add butter, ground black pepper, and Parmesan cheese. Turn to medium-high heat and cook until bubbling, stirring until the sauce is smooth and thickened.

6. Return the seared salmon to the pan and cook for about 3 minutes, allowing the fish to absorb the sauce fully.
   **Key Point**: For a more well-done texture, extend the cooking time by another 3 minutes.

7. Add the fresh spinach and toss gently using the residual heat of the sauce until the spinach wilts.
   **Key Point**: The residual heat in the pan is enough to wilt the spinach; no additional heating is needed.

8. Slightly reduce the sauce until it evenly coats the ingredients. Spoon some sauce and vegetables onto the plate first, then place the salmon on top for serving.

## Health Tips
Salmon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids. Paired with spinach and tomatoes, this dish is nutritionally balanced, suitable for weight loss or those needing brain health support. Note that cream and cheese are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to control portions. Pairing with whole-wheat bread or a salad can help achieve a more balanced nutritional profile.
