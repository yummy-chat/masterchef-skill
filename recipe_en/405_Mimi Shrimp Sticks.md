# Mimi Shrimp Sticks  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Leftover rice, about half a small bowl  
🦐 Fresh shrimp, about 10 pieces (peeled and deveined, steamed, and made into shrimp paste)  
🥚 Egg white, 1 piece (use only the egg white)  
⚠️ No seasonings added  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🍲 Steamer  
🍽️ Plate (to cover the shrimp while steaming to prevent water ingress)  
🌀 Food processor  
🌀 Electric egg beater  
🥄 Spatula  
💼 Piping bag  
🍳 Baking tray  
📄 Baking paper  
🔥 Oven  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare shrimp paste**  
   Peel and devein the fresh shrimp, turning them into shrimp meat. Place in a bowl, cover with a plate, and steam until cooked. Remove and use a food processor to blend into a smooth shrimp paste.  
   **Key point**: Steaming removes the fishy smell and firms up the shrimp meat, making it easier to blend later.

2. **Whip the egg white**  
   Place the egg white in a grease-free and water-free container. Use an electric egg beater to whip until stiff peaks form.

3. **Mix the batter**  
   Gradually add the whipped egg white into the shrimp paste. Use a spatula to fold or cut and mix until evenly combined, with no lumps of egg white left. Be careful to avoid deflating the mixture.

4. **Pipe into strips**  
   Transfer the batter into a piping bag. Pipe evenly shaped strips onto a baking tray lined with baking paper, leaving gaps between each strip.

5. **Bake**  
   Preheat the oven. Place the baking tray in the middle rack and bake until the shrimp sticks turn a light orange-yellow color and become firm and crisp.  
   **Note**: The video does not provide specific temperature and time. Watch the color to avoid burning.

6. **Cool and enjoy**  
   Remove the baking tray. Let the shrimp sticks cool until they become crispy. They can be served as a snack or an on-the-go treat.

## Health Tips  
This homemade Mimi shrimp stick uses fresh shrimp instead of additives. It is a calcium-rich, low-fat healthy snack, especially suitable for picky children and fitness snacks. Be careful not to mix egg yolk or oil into the egg white when whipping, or the shrimp sticks will collapse into shrimp patties.
