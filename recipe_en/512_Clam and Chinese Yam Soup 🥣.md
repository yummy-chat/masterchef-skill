# Clam and Chinese Yam Soup 🥣
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐚 Clams 150g (soak in salted water in advance to expel sand)
🥩 Lean meat appropriate amount (finely diced)
🥔 Chinese yam (mountain yam) appropriate amount (peeled and diced)
🌿 Dried seaweed appropriate amount (soaked in water in advance)
🌱 Celery appropriate amount (minced)
🥚 Eggs appropriate amount (beaten into egg liquid)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (crushed, used for boiling clam broth)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder appropriate amount (can be omitted for children)
🌾 Water starch appropriate amount (for thickening)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Stockpot or glass pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the Chinese yam and cut into small dices.
   **Note**: Wear gloves when peeling to prevent the mucilage from irritating the skin.
2. Cut the lean meat into small dices; soak the clams in salted water to expel sand.
3. Add water to a pot, put in the yam dices, and bring to a boil. Continue simmering for 3 minutes until the yam is fully cooked, then remove and set aside.
4. In another pot, add water, ginger pieces, and clams. Cook until the clams open, then immediately remove the meat. Strain the broth through a slotted spoon to obtain clear clam broth.
5. Pour the clam broth back into the pot and bring to a boil. Add the soaked seaweed, cooked yam dices, and diced lean meat in sequence.
   **Key point**: Before adding the diced lean meat, first mix it with a little cold water to prevent clumping when added to the pot.
6. After boiling, add the clam meat, salt, and white pepper powder to taste, and stir well.
7. Drizzle in the water starch to thicken the soup, stirring continuously, and cook until the broth is smooth and thick.
8. Sprinkle in the minced celery, then evenly drizzle in the beaten egg liquid. Let it sit for a few seconds, then gently push it to form egg flower flakes. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Chinese yam strengthens the spleen and nourishes the stomach, while clams provide high-quality protein. This soup is suitable for those with poor digestion or who need zinc supplementation. Be sure to let the clams expel sand thoroughly to avoid affecting the texture.
