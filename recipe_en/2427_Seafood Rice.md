# Seafood Rice  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp (with shell, head and body separated)  
🌭 Sausage (sliced)  
🧅 Onion (half, chopped)  
🧄 Garlic (chopped)  
🍚 Rice (uncooked)  
🐚 Mussels  
🍊 Kumquat (for juicing)  
🌿 Parsley/Cilantro (chopped)  
🫒 Olive oil (as needed)  
🌶️ Paprika (for aroma, not spicy)  
🍅 Tomato paste (can substitute with ketchup or canned tomatoes)  
🧈 Butter (a small piece)  
🧂 Salt (for final seasoning)  
🥃 Erguotou (optional, for aroma)  
🍲 Chicken broth (mixed with water in a 1:1 ratio, or use water only)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥢 Metal tongs  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Separate shrimp heads from bodies; chop onion and garlic, slice sausage, halve kumquats, and chop parsley.  
2. Pour olive oil into the pan, add shrimp heads, and fry over low heat until they turn completely red. Use metal tongs to press the shrimp heads firmly, releasing the full flavor from the roe. Remove the shrimp heads, keeping the shrimp oil in the pan.  
3. Keep the heat low and use the shrimp oil to sauté the chopped onion and garlic until fragrant.  
4. Once the onion is soft and translucent, add the sliced sausage and stir-fry.  
5. Add the shrimp bodies and stir-fry until they change color.  
   **Key point**: Adding shrimp bodies later prevents their moisture from affecting the browning of earlier ingredients.  
6. Add the uncooked rice and stir-fry until the grains fully absorb the oil, becoming translucent at the edges. Add black pepper and butter, and stir to combine.  
7. Pour in the chicken broth and water mixture, enough to cover the rice by about one and a half finger joints.  
8. Once the liquid is boiling, add the mussels.  
9. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat, maintaining a gentle simmer.  
   **Key point**: Heat control is key—keep a light boil. For firmer rice, cook for about 18 minutes; for softer, cook for about 23 minutes. If the liquid evaporates, add half a cup of water.  
10. During simmering, add paprika, tomato paste, and Erguotou, and stir to combine.  
11. Before removing from heat, squeeze in kumquat juice, sprinkle with chopped parsley or cilantro, and stir to blend flavors.  
12. Once the rice reaches the desired doneness, turn off the heat, plate, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This seafood rice, with shrimp oil sauté and rice absorbing seafood broth, is nutritionally balanced but calorie-dense—best paired with a light salad and portion control. The red oil from shrimp heads is rich in astaxanthin, beneficial for antioxidant needs, but those with high uric acid should limit shellfish intake.
