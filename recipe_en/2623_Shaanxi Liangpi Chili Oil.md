# Shaanxi Liangpi Chili Oil
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes – to taste
🌶️ Fine chili powder – to taste
⚪ White sesame seeds – to taste
🐖 Lard – to taste
🫒 Rapeseed oil – to taste
🧅 Onion – to taste (cut into chunks)
🧄 Scallion – to taste (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger – to taste (sliced)
🫘 Spice powder (cloves 3g, cardamom 2g, cinnamon 15g, Sichuan pepper 15g, black cardamom 15g, bay leaves 20g, cumin 60g, star anise 40g, ground into powder)
🍶 Fragrant vinegar – to taste
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) – to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Stainless steel wok/frying pot
🌡️ Digital oil thermometer
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪵 Wooden spoon
🔥 Portable gas stove/gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour rapeseed oil into the pot, heat over high heat to 260°C (500°F), and cook the oil thoroughly.
2. Turn off the heat or remove from heat, wait for the oil temperature to naturally drop to below 200°C (392°F), add lard and the deodorizing ingredients (onion, scallion, ginger). Gently stir with a slotted spoon, using the moisture from the vegetables and the aroma of lard to remove the raw oil smell.
3. When the oil temperature drops to 200°C (392°F), add white sesame seeds and wait for them to float.
4. As soon as the sesame seeds float, immediately and evenly sprinkle in the spice powder. Work quickly to allow the spices to fully infuse and release their fragrance at the optimal temperature.
5. When the oil temperature drops to 180°C (356°F), first add the coarse chili flakes, then immediately add the fine chili powder. Stir well to prevent burning.
6. Immediately pour in the fragrant vinegar to release the aroma, then add the baijiu to further lower the temperature and stimulate a complex fragrance.
7. Remove the chili oil from the pot, let it sit uncovered overnight to allow the flavors to fully meld and settle. Seal and store the next day.

## Health Advice
This chili oil is rich in capsaicin and sesame aroma, which can stimulate appetite and blood circulation. It is suitable as a seasoning for Liangpi or a daily dipping sauce. Note that lard and rapeseed oil are high in calories; it is recommended to limit each serving to 1–2 tablespoons to avoid excessive intake. The spiciness is relatively strong, so those with sensitive stomachs or mouth ulcers should consume in moderation.
