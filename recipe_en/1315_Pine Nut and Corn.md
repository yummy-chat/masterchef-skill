# Pine Nut and Corn

Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 🌽 Corn kernels 200g (sweet corn recommended)
🥜 Pine nuts (roasted) 30g
🥕 Carrot 50g (finely diced, optional)
🛢️ Cooking oil 15 ml
🍬 White sugar 10 g
🧂 Salt 1 g
🌾 Cornstarch 5 g
💧 Water 20 ml (for making cornstarch slurry)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥘 Strainer (for blanching)
🥣 Bowl (for mixing cornstarch slurry)

## Cooking Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the corn kernels and diced carrot and blanch for 1 minute. Drain well.
   **Key Point**: Blanching cooks the corn kernels through and removes any raw taste. The carrot dice softens slightly for faster stir-frying later.

2. Heat the wok over a flame and pour in the cooking oil. Briefly stir-fry the diced carrot, then add the corn kernels and stir-fry together until well combined.

3. Add the white sugar and salt. Stir-fry until the seasonings dissolve and coat the ingredients.

4. Mix the cornstarch with water to form a slurry. Pour it into the wok and stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens slightly and evenly coats the ingredients.

5. Add the roasted pine nuts and stir-fry quickly until they are evenly mixed with the corn and carrot.

6. Transfer to a plate and serve hot.
   **Note**: For extra flavor, dry-fry the pine nuts over low heat until lightly golden and fragrant. If using canned corn, drain it well in advance to prevent too much liquid from being released during stir-frying. Do not use high heat throughout to avoid scorching or burning the pine nuts.

## Health Tips
Pine nut and corn is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin E, making it suitable for office workers who frequently use their eyes and those needing heart health support. Be mindful of sugar intake; those with diabetes can reduce the sugar or use a substitute.
