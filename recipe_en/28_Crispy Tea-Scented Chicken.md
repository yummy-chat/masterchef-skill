# Crispy Tea-Scented Chicken
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 three-yellow chicken (under 1000g, split open from the back and flattened)  
🍵 2 tbsp green tea (ground into powder for batter; reserve some to brew a bowl for tea jelly)  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (mixed with tea powder and braising liquid to form a batter)  
🧂 10g salt (for marinating)  
🌿 5g five-spice powder (for marinating)  
🍯 2 tbsp brown sugar (for braising liquid)  
🥄 2 tbsp soy sauce (for braising liquid)  
💧 1 tbsp water (for braising liquid)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
✂️🔪 Kitchen scissors, cutting board (for preparing the chicken)  
🍳 Small saucepan (for heating spices and simmering braising liquid)  
🥣 Mortar and pestle (for grinding tea powder)  
♨️ Steam oven or steamer (for steaming the chicken)  
🥘 Roasting rack, baking sheet (for drying and roasting)  
🖌️ Basting brush (for applying batter)  

## Instructions  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Use kitchen scissors to cut the chicken open along the back, remove the wishbone, and flatten the chicken by pressing it down for even heat distribution.  
2. Heat salt and five-spice powder in a small saucepan to release their aroma, then rub the mixture evenly over both sides and into crevices of the chicken. Marinate for 1 hour.  
3. Rinse the marinated chicken thoroughly, then steam in a steam oven for 6 minutes to cook the skin.  
   **Key Point**: This step locks in moisture and flavor, crucial for achieving "crispy skin and tender meat."  
4. Place the steamed chicken on a roasting rack and air-dry naturally for at least 30 minutes until the skin surface is dry.  
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly drying the skin is essential for crispiness after roasting.  
5. Bring soy sauce, brown sugar, and 1 tbsp water to a boil in a small saucepan, then let cool to make the braising liquid. Set aside.  
6. Grind green tea leaves into a fine powder, then mix with cornstarch and the cooled braising liquid to form a smooth batter.  
7. Brush the batter evenly over both sides of the dried chicken, ensuring full coverage.  

### Roasting Phase  
1. Place the chicken meat-side up on a roasting rack and roast in convection mode for 10 minutes until the surface begins to color.  
2. Flip the chicken skin-side up and continue roasting for 10 minutes until the skin turns a deep reddish-brown and becomes crispy.  
   **Doneness Check**: The skin should be glossy and date-red in color, with a crispy texture.  
3. Remove the roasted chicken, let rest briefly, then chop into pieces and plate. The chicken is tender and juicy, with crispy skin and a delicate tea aroma paired with complex spice flavors.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses a steam-roast combination to lock in moisture while achieving crispy skin, making it suitable for those seeking texture with controlled oil intake. Avoid over-marinating to prevent saltiness, and pair with vegetables for balanced nutrition.
