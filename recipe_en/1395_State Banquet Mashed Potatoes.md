# State Banquet Mashed Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potato, about 2 (main ingredient)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (for black pepper sauce)
🧈 Butter, appropriate amount
🥛 Milk, appropriate amount
🥫 Condensed milk, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, a small amount
⚫️ Black pepper, appropriate amount
🍶 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount
💧 Hot water, appropriate amount
🌽 Starch water, appropriate amount (starch mixed with water for thickening)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
♨️ Steamer
🥣 Large bowl (for holding and mashing potatoes)
🥄 Spoon
🍴 Potato masher
🍲 Milk pot or small pot (for making black pepper sauce)
🥢 Spatula or chopsticks
🍝 Plate
🎞️ Plastic wrap (to line the bowl for easy unmolding)

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and slice them into evenly thick pieces.
   **Key Point**: Slices must be uniform to ensure even cooking during steaming.
2. Place the potato slices in the steamer, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat until steam rises, then continue steaming for about 15 minutes until the potatoes are soft and tender.
   **Judgment Standard**: Easily pierced through with chopsticks.
3. Transfer the steamed potato slices to a large bowl and use a potato masher to mash them into a smooth, lump-free puree.
   **Texture Assessment**: Should be powdery and sandy.
4. Add butter, milk, condensed milk, and a small amount of salt to the mashed potatoes, and stir until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Stir until all ingredients are fully incorporated and the mashed potatoes are smooth in texture.
5. Line a bowl with plastic wrap, scoop the seasoned mashed potatoes into it, press firmly, and shape into a hemisphere.
   **Key Point**: Plastic wrap helps in clean unmolding and creating an attractive shape.
6. Heat oil in a pan, add the shredded onion, and stir-fry over low heat.
   **Texture Assessment**: Cook until the onion darkens in color and the edges become slightly caramelized.
   **Key Point**: Slow frying over low heat brings out caramelized flavors, forming the base of the sauce.
7. Turn off the heat, immediately add black pepper, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white sugar, and stir well.
   **Key Point**: Adding seasonings after turning off the heat prevents the soy sauce from burning and turning bitter due to high temperature.
8. Turn the heat back on, add hot water to the pan, and bring to a boil.
9. Remove and discard the cooked onion.
10. Once the sauce comes to a boil again, slowly drizzle in the starch water while continuously stirring with chopsticks or a spatula.
    **Texture Assessment**: Stir until the sauce becomes thick and smooth, and can coat the back of a spoon.
11. Invert the shaped mashed potatoes onto a plate and remove the plastic wrap.
12. Evenly pour the prepared black pepper sauce over the mashed potatoes. Garnish with chopped green onions or diced bell peppers if desired.
    **Key Point**: The sauce should be poured while hot.

## Health Advice
This mashed potato dish uses butter and condensed milk to enhance the texture, combining high-quality carbohydrates with dairy. It is suitable for those looking to build muscle or increase energy intake. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive calorie consumption.
