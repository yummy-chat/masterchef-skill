# Steamed Mushrooms with Quail Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍄 Mushrooms About 8 (stems removed to form bowl-shaped indentations)  
🥚 Quail Eggs About 8  
🧅 Scallions Appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧂 Black Pepper Appropriate amount  
🍾 Steamed Fish Soy Sauce Appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🧽 Washing basin  
🍽️ Plate  
♨️ Electric steamer  

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the mushrooms, use your fingers to completely remove the stems from the base, forming bowl-shaped indentations. Arrange them in a plate with the concave side facing up.
2. Evenly sprinkle a thin layer of black pepper into the indentation of each mushroom.
3. Carefully crack a quail egg into each indentation, keeping the yolk as intact as possible.
4. Place the plate into the electric steamer, cover with the lid, and steam for about 8–10 minutes after the water boils, until the egg liquid is completely set.
5. Remove the steamed mushrooms and quail eggs, drizzle with an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce, and sprinkle with chopped scallions.
   **Key Points**: The steaming time can be slightly adjusted based on heat; aim for the egg liquid to set and the mushroom caps to be slightly tender; do not use too much steamed fish soy sauce to avoid masking the mushroom and egg aromas.

## Health Advice  
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. Mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber, while quail eggs provide high-quality protein and B vitamins. It is suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing to control calorie intake. Note that quail eggs are high in cholesterol; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
