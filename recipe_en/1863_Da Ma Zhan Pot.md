# Da Ma Zhan Pot

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Overnight roasted pork belly, about 300g (sliced thick, trim off the overly salty bottom part)  
🧈 Firm tofu, about 800g (sliced thick)  
🥬 Chinese chives, about 150g (cut into sections)  
🫘 Dried bean curd sticks, about 150g (pre-soaked until soft, or deep-fried then soaked)  
🧅 Garlic, 50g (smashed)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Red chili pepper, about 50g (sliced)  
🧅 Green garlic, 50g (cut into sections)  
🦐 Shrimp paste, 10g  
🍬 Granulated sugar, 5g  
🧂 Light soy sauce, a dash  
🌾 Cornstarch, 15g (mixed with water to form a cornstarch slurry)  
🧈 Lard (or cooking oil), as needed  
🧂 Salt, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥘 Clay pot  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Slice the roasted pork belly into thick pieces, trimming off any overly salty bottom parts. Slice the firm tofu into thick pieces, and sprinkle both sides evenly with a little salt to marinate briefly. Cut the Chinese chives into sections, smash the garlic, slice the ginger, slice the red chili pepper, cut the green garlic into sections. Pre-soak the dried bean curd sticks until soft and set aside.
2.  In a bowl, combine the shrimp paste, granulated sugar, light soy sauce, and an appropriate amount of water. Stir well to make the seasoning sauce and set aside.
3.  Heat lard in a wok. Place the roasted pork belly slices skin-side down. Pan-fry over medium-low heat for about 1 minute, until the skin is crispy and the meat is fragrant. Remove and set aside, retaining the lard in the wok.
**Key Point**: Frying skin-side down helps restore crispiness to softened skin. A pinch of salt can be sprinkled for extra flavor.

4.  Using the reserved lard in the wok, add the drained tofu pieces. Pan-fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
**Key Point**: Salting the tofu beforehand helps it absorb flavor more easily, creates a better crust, and prevents it from breaking apart during frying.

5.  Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the smashed garlic and sliced ginger, stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in the prepared shrimp paste seasoning sauce and bring to a boil.
6.  Place the pan-fried pork belly and tofu into the wok. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and braise for 5 minutes.
7.  Add the pre-soaked bean curd sticks. Continue to braise until the sauce slightly thickens and reduces.
8.  Pour in the prepared cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce. Then, add the green garlic sections and red chili pepper slices. Finally, add the Chinese chives sections. Quickly stir-fry everything together until well combined.
**Key Point**: The Chinese chives must be added last, as they cook instantly with residual heat; prolonged cooking will make them tough and old.

9.  Transfer all the ingredients into a pre-heated clay pot. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips  
This Da Ma Zhan Pot is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. The roasted pork belly and tofu provide a feeling of fullness. However, shrimp paste is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Chinese chives and green garlic are rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion.
