# Garlic Butter Shrimp
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦐 Large shrimp (recommend black tiger shrimp or whiteleg shrimp)
🧄 Garlic
🧈 Unsalted butter (recommend Anchor)
🍷 White wine (optional)
🍋 Lemon
🫒 Olive oil

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥢 Kitchen tongs
🧻 Kitchen paper
🪡 Toothpick

## Instructions
1. Remove the heads, shells, and tails of the large shrimp, using a toothpick to devein. Rinse clean and pat dry with kitchen paper.
2. Mince the garlic into small pieces and set aside.
3. Heat the frying pan over medium heat and add 10 ml of olive oil.
4. Once the oil is hot, add the shrimp and cook for 1 to 1.5 minutes per side until they change color, then remove and set aside.
   **Note**: Do not overcook the shrimp, or the texture will become tough.
5. In the same pan, add the butter. Once melted, add the minced garlic and sauté over low heat for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. If using white wine, add it now and cook until the alcohol evaporates, about 1 minute.
7. Return the shrimp to the pan and stir-fry together with the garlic butter sauce for about 1 minute.
8. Squeeze in the lemon juice, stir well, and immediately turn off the heat.
9. Plate the shrimp and drizzle the remaining sauce from the pan on top.

## Health Advice
This garlic butter shrimp is rich in high-quality protein and healthy fats, making it suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose fat. However, butter is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to serve with vegetables. Pay attention to the cooking time for the shrimp to avoid overcooking, which can make the texture tough.
