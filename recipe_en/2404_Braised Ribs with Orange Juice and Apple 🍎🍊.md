# Braised Ribs with Orange Juice and Apple 🍎🍊  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Pork ribs – about 500g  
🍎 Apple – 1 (cut into thick slices)  
🧃 Orange juice – about half a cup  
🫚 Ginger – 3–4 slices  
🧅 Spring onion – about 2 (cut into sections)  
🧂 Light soy sauce – about 2 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce – about 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt – 1 teaspoon (or to taste)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  
🍶 Measuring cup  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rinse the pork ribs clean. Add a little oil to a non-stick pan and pan-fry the ribs over medium heat until both sides are golden brown, rendering out excess fat.  
**Key point**: Pan-frying enhances the aroma and firms up the meat.  
2. Leave a little oil in the pan, add ginger slices and spring onion sections, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
3. Add the pan-fried ribs and apple slices. Pour in the orange juice, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt. Stir well, then add enough hot water to reach halfway up the ingredients.  
**Key point**: Using hot water helps the meat become tender more easily.  
4. Cover the pan and braise over medium-low heat for about 30 minutes. Finally, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce until it thickens.  
**Doneness check**: The sauce becomes thick and coats the ribs, and a chopstick can easily pierce through the meat.  
5. Serve on a plate and enjoy.  

## Health Tips  
Pork ribs are rich in high-quality protein and calcium. Paired with orange juice and apple, this dish is sweet and sour, appetizing, and suitable for the whole family to replenish nutrition. It is recommended to choose leaner ribs to reduce fat intake. Diabetic patients should monitor the amount of orange juice to control blood sugar fluctuations.
