# Street Beef Burger
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

🥚 Room temperature whole egg liquid 110g  
🍬 Powdered sugar 50g

🍶 9-degree white vinegar 110g

### Caramelized Onions  
🧅 Onion strips 600g  
🍷 Red wine 50g  
🍶 Black vinegar 20g  
🍬 Brown sugar 8g  

### Mexican Smoky Burger Sauce  
🫙 Salad dressing 200g  
🥛 Three-flower egg milk 30g  
🌱 Mustard seed paste 16g  
🍅 Tomato ketchup 80g  
🧅 Chopped onion 8g  
🌶️ Chipotle chili sauce 20g  
🌶️ Montesita smoked chili powder 10g  
🧄 Garlic powder 4g

### Honey Mustard Mayonnaise  
🥚 Mayonnaise 200g  
🍯 Honey mustard sauce 80g  
🥒 Pickled cucumber slices 10g (use homemade pickled cucumbers)  
🍅 Tomato ketchup 20g

### Cheese Waterfall Sauce

🧈 Butter 10g

## Preparation Steps
### Hamburger Buns
1. Pour the high-gluten wheat flour, powdered sugar, whole egg liquid, and whole milk into the stand mixer bowl. Mix on low speed for 7 minutes. In the last two minutes, add the high-sugar-tolerant yeast powder, and continue mixing on low speed until evenly combined.
2. Add the softened butter and salt. Mix on low speed for 10 minutes, then beat on high speed for 15 minutes until the dough surface is smooth and a thin membrane can be pulled out.
3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and proof in a 40℃ proofing oven for 30 minutes, until it doubles in volume and has a honeycomb texture inside.
4. Remove the dough, punch down to release gas, and divide it into portions of 90g each. Tuck the edges and roll into balls.
5. Place the dough balls on a baking sheet, proof again in a 40℃ proofing oven for 30 minutes, until they increase to 1.5 times their original size.
6. Brush the surface with whole egg liquid, sprinkle with black sesame seeds, and place in a preheated oven at 200℃ on the lower-middle rack. Bake for about 14 minutes until the surface is golden brown.
   **Note**: For small home ovens worried about uneven browning, quickly rotate the baking sheet halfway through baking. The buns must cool completely before being stored in an airtight container.

### Pickled Cucumbers
1. Wash the small cucumbers, soak them with the container in boiling water for 10 minutes, then remove and let cool. Discard the soaking water.
2. In a separate pot, bring water, 9-degree white vinegar, pickling salt, white sugar, black peppercorns, and white mustard seeds to a boil until the salt and sugar completely dissolve.
3. Return the cucumbers and onion strips to the container, and immediately pour in the hot pickling liquid.
4. Allow it to cool naturally to below 30℃, then cover with a lid that has been sterilized with high-proof alcohol. Refrigerate and marinate for 7 days.
   **Note**: The cucumbers must be blanched; the pickling liquid must be boiled; during the sealed marination, keep it refrigerated.

### Sauce Preparation
1. **Mexican Smoky Burger Sauce**: Place all ingredients in a food processor and pulse until combined and well mixed.
2. **Honey Mustard Mayonnaise**: Mix all ingredients together, then use a food processor to blend them smoothly.
3. **Cheese Waterfall Sauce**: In a small saucepan, combine cheese, butter, milk, and whipping cream. Heat until thick and pourable.

### Caramelized Onions
1. Heat butter on a griddle until melted. Add the onion strips, a pinch of salt, and black pepper. Sauté until softened.
2. Spread the onions flat on the griddle and cook over low heat slowly until the moisture evaporates and sugars caramelize, turning a light brown color.
3. Mix the red wine, black vinegar, and brown sugar together. Pour over the onions, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry until the sauce is absorbed and the onions are a deep brown color. Remove from heat.
   **Key Point**: Patiently cook the onions low and slow until soft and caramelized, then pour in the seasoned liquid and stir-fry.

### Beef Patties
1. Cut the beef chuck and brisket into pieces according to a 6:4 ratio. Freeze until it reaches -8℃, then remove, mix, and grind using a meat grinder. Do not over-grind.
2. Add the onion puree, beaten egg, garlic powder, white pepper powder, black pepper powder, oyster sauce, and salt to the ground meat. Mix and knead by hand until well combined and the mixture has a firm texture.
3. Divide the meat mixture into balls. Place on the griddle and press firmly with a patty press to form thin patties.
4. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper. Cook until one side is crispy and browned, then flip and cook the other side to the same level.
5. Place a slice of cheese on the cooked patty and use a blowtorch to melt the cheese.

### Assembly
**Mexican Smoky Chili Double Beef Burger**
1. Use a butter roller to spread melted butter on the cut sides of the hamburger buns. Place them on the griddle and cook until the cut sides are golden and crispy.
2. Simultaneously, cook the pineapple slices on both sides until caramelized.
3. On the bottom bun, squeeze Mexican Smoky Burger Sauce. Layer with romaine lettuce, caramelized onions, and tomato slices.
4. Place one cheese-topped beef patty. Add the cooked pineapple slices, then stack another cheese-topped beef patty on top. Finish with three slices of pickled cucumber.
5. Squeeze another layer of Mexican Smoky Burger Sauce on the top patty. Cover with the top bun. Insert a decorative skewer to secure.

**Cheese Waterfall Double Beef Burger**
1. On the bottom bun, squeeze Honey Mustard Mayonnaise.
2. Layer with romaine lettuce, caramelized onions, tomato slices, and one cheese-topped beef patty.
3. Add cooked pineapple slices, then place one slice of bacon, followed by another bacon slice. Then add the second cheese-topped beef patty and pickled cucumber slices.
4. Cover with the top bun. Generously pour the warm Cheese Waterfall Sauce over the top until it completely coats the entire burger.

## Health Advice
This street beef burger is rich in protein but also high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition, and it is suitable for occasional consumption. Individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of the sodium content in the sauces and pickled cucumbers.
