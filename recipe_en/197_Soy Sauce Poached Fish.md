# Soy Sauce Poached Fish
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Yunmeng fish (golden thread fish), about 6-8 pieces
🥕 Dried radish, to taste (diced)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, to taste (sliced into rings)
🫘 Fermented black beans, 1 teaspoon
🧄 Garlic sprouts, to taste (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices
🧄 Garlic, a few cloves (sliced)
🧂 Chicken powder, 1 tablespoon
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧴 Light soy sauce, half a bowl
🧴 Dark soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🌫️ White pepper powder, to taste
💧 Water, 1 bowl
🫒 Fried shallot oil, to taste (added in two batches)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (with lid)
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Spoon
✂️ Scissors
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife

## Instructions
1. Clean the fish thoroughly. Trim off the fins and any excess parts, then drain well.
2. Prepare the soy sauce mixture in advance: In a bowl, combine chicken powder, white sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, white pepper powder, and water. Stir until well mixed and set aside.
**Key Point**: Pre-mix the sauce to determine the base flavor.

3. Slice the ginger, slice the garlic, cut the bird's eye chili into rings, dice the dried radish, and cut the garlic sprouts into sections.
4. Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add the ginger slices and garlic slices, and stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Add the dried radish, bird's eye chili rings, and fermented black beans. Continue stir-frying until a complex aroma is released.
6. Lay the prepared fish flat over the aromatics to ensure even heating.
7. Pour the prepared soy sauce mixture along the edge of the wok, ensuring the liquid covers the fish. Then drizzle in a teaspoon of fried shallot oil and cover with the lid.
8. Bring the liquid to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and cook for 3-5 minutes, until the fish turns white and the sauce thickens to a caramel color.
9. Remove the lid, scatter the garlic sprout sections over the fish, and drizzle in another teaspoon of fried shallot oil. Turn off the heat immediately and serve.
**Key Point**: Add the garlic sprouts right before serving to keep them bright green; the final drizzle of oil enhances the aroma layers.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing nutritional supplementation. However, soy sauce and oyster sauce are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the salty umami flavor. The fish is easy to digest and can be enjoyed by both the elderly and children.
