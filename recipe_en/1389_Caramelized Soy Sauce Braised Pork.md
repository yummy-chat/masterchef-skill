# Caramelized Soy Sauce Braised Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍬 White sugar 1 tablespoon
💧 Water 1/2 tablespoon (for dissolving sugar) + enough boiling water (for rinsing the caramel)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🥚 Eggs as needed (hard-boiled and peeled)
🥩 Pork belly as needed (cut into chunks, blanched)
🧅 Scallions as needed (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger as needed (sliced)
🪷 Lotus root 1 piece (about 200g, sliced)
🦪 Oyster sauce as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce as needed
🧂 Salt as needed (**more than usual for stir-frying**)
🍾 Cooking wine as needed
🌿 Braising spice packet 1 pack (containing star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, etc.)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Large soup ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. Hard-boil the eggs in advance, then peel; cut the pork belly into chunks, blanch in boiling water, then drain well.
   - Blanching removes blood and odors.
2. Cut scallions into sections, slice ginger, slice lotus root, and prepare the braising spice packet.
3. In the wok, add 1 tablespoon of white sugar and 1/2 tablespoon of water. Heat over low heat until the sugar is completely melted.
4. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, continue heating over low heat, and stir constantly.
   - Keep the heat on **low** throughout to prevent the caramel from burning and turning bitter.
   - **State check**: The sugar liquid will go through the stages of “large bubbles → lemon yellow → date red → deep date red with large bubbles.”
   - **Key point**: You must wait until the sugar liquid turns **deep date red with large bubbles** before adding liquid—this is the optimal caramel stage for braised pork.
5. Immediately pour enough boiling water into the wok, stirring quickly to combine, to create the caramelized water.
   - Pour the boiling water quickly; the wok will bubble vigorously—be careful.
6. Add the peeled hard-boiled eggs, blanched pork belly, scallion sections, ginger slices, and lotus root slices into the caramelized water.
7. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, and cooking wine in order.
8. Place in the braising spice packet, making sure the liquid roughly covers all ingredients.
9. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce to low heat, and braise for about 30 minutes.
10. After turning off the heat, do not remove the ingredients immediately; let them soak in the braising liquid for another 20 minutes to fully absorb the flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein and dietary fiber from the lotus root, making it suitable to serve with staple foods. However, pork belly has a high fat content, so it is recommended to control portion sizes. People with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt and pair it with vegetables to balance nutrition.
