# Home-style Meat Pie
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Flour 500g  
🐖 Ground pork 500g  
💧 Water approx. 300g (for dough) + approx. 200g (for filling)  
🧅 Spring onion 50g (finely chopped)  
🫚 Ginger approx. 5g (minced)  
🦐 Dried shrimp 10g (chopped)  
🫙 Yeast 5g  
🧂 Baking powder 3g  
🍬 Granulated sugar approx. 6g  
🫒 Cooking oil (for dough) approx. 10g  
🧂 Edible alkali 1.4g  
🌶️ White pepper 1.4g  
🧂 MSG 5g  
🍶 Dark soy sauce 30g  
🍶 Light soy sauce 20g  
🫘 Fermented bean paste 40g  
🫒 Sesame oil 10g +适量 (for final mixing with spring onion)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔌 Electric griddle  
🫙 Large stainless steel bowl  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔲 Chopping board  
🖌️ Brush  
⚖️ Electronic scale (or measuring tools)  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. **Make the dough**: Mix 500g flour, 5g yeast, 3g baking powder, and approx. 6g sugar. Gradually add approx. 300g water, stirring with chopsticks until flaky, then knead into a dough. Once formed, add approx. 10g cooking oil and knead until smooth. If the dough is too firm, dip your fist in a little water and punch it into the dough gradually until it becomes soft and supple. Cover and let rest for about ten minutes.
**Key point**: The dough should be smooth, soft but not overly sticky; punching water into the dough is a key technique for softening it; resting time does not need to be long.

2. **Prepare the alkaline seasoning water**: Dissolve 1.4g edible alkali in 200g water, then add 1.4g white pepper and 5g MSG, stirring until well combined.
**Note**: The seasoning water should be prepared in advance and used all at once.

3. **Prepare the meat filling**:
   - Add 30g dark soy sauce and 20g light soy sauce to 500g ground pork and mix well.
   - **Pour the alkaline seasoning water in all at once** and immediately stir vigorously and continuously in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed and the filling becomes sticky, elastic, and stringy.
   - Add 40g fermented bean paste and mix evenly.
   - Add minced ginger and chopped dried shrimp in sequence, drizzle 10g sesame oil directly over the ginger and dried shrimp, then mix well.
   - Finally, add the chopped spring onion, drizzle a little more sesame oil over the onion, and gently mix.
**Key point**: Adding the seasoning water all at once and stirring quickly is key to a juicy filling; sesame oil is added in two stages—the first to release the aroma of ginger and dried shrimp, the second to prevent the spring onion from releasing water; adding the spring onion last reduces water loss and keeps it fresh.

4. **Shape and cook the pie**:
   - Dust the rested dough with a little flour on the chopping board and roll it into a rectangular sheet about 3mm thick.
   - Spread the prepared meat filling evenly over the dough sheet, leaving a 1–2cm border around the edges.
   - Fold one end of the dough sheet about one-third over the filling, then fold the other end over to completely enclose the filling, forming a rectangular pie with three layers.
   - Gently press and roll the pie from the center outward with a rolling pin to an even thickness, sizing it to fit the electric griddle.
   - Preheat the electric griddle, brush a layer of oil on the bottom plate, place the pie in, and brush the top with oil as well.
   - Cover and cook until one side is golden, then flip and cook until both sides are golden and the pie puffs up.
**Key point**: Roll the dough evenly, not too thin or thick; align the folded edges; roll the pie gently to prevent the filling from squeezing out; brushing oil during cooking gives an even color and a crispier crust.

5. **Finish**: Remove the cooked meat pie, place it on a chopping board, cut into pieces for easy serving, and arrange on a plate.

## Health Advice  
This meat pie is high in carbohydrates and fat, so it is recommended to serve with light vegetables or soup to avoid overloading digestion from a single excessive portion. The addition of alkaline water to the meat filling makes it more tender and juicy, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation.
