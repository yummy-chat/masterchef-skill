# Glutinous Rice Sticks  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 150g glutinous rice flour (for making the paste)  
🍚 500g glutinous rice flour (to be added to the paste for kneading; keep a little extra for dusting the board)  
💧 380ml boiling water  
🌿 30ml cooking oil  
🧂 5g baking powder  
🍬 Powdered sugar, as needed (for the final coating)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Cutting board  
Rolling pin  
🔪 Knife  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥘 Baking sheet  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Make the glutinous rice paste: Place 150g glutinous rice flour in a bowl, pour in 380ml boiling water, and stir with chopsticks in one direction until it becomes a thick, sticky, stretchy paste.  
   **Key point**: Must stir until the batter shows a clear stretchy texture.  
2. Add 30ml cooking oil and continue stirring evenly; the paste will become smoother and more refined.  
3. Add 5g baking powder, then gradually add the 500g dry glutinous rice flour, stirring into a flaky texture. Then knead by hand into a glutinous rice dough.  
   **Texture check**: Knead until the dough is no longer sticky and is relatively soft.  
4. Dust the cutting board with dry glutinous rice flour and roll the dough into a thin sheet. Thickness can be adjusted according to preference, determining the final thickness of the sticks.  
5. Dust the sheet with dry flour, cut into wide strips, stack them, then cut into thin long strips. Lightly shake them apart after cutting. For a more refined look, roll the long strips into round shapes.  
6. Shake off excess dry flour from the strips. Heat oil in a wok to around 30–40% hotness, add the raw strips, and fry over medium-low heat.  
   **Texture check**: The strips will slowly float to the surface.  
7. Once all the sticks have floated, quickly turn them over and increase to high heat. Fry until both sides are golden brown, about three to five minutes.  
   **Texture check**: When they are golden brown overall and hollow and crispy inside, remove them.  
8. Use a slotted spoon to remove and drain excess oil, then place them on a baking sheet.  
9. While still hot, sprinkle generously with powdered sugar and shake the baking sheet to coat the sticks evenly.  
   **Key point**: Skipping the syrup-making step simplifies the process.  

## Health Advice  
Glutinous rice sticks are crispy on the outside and hollow inside, but the combination of glutinous rice flour and deep-frying makes them high in calories. Pair with unsweetened tea to cut the grease, and eat only a small handful each time. They make a good festive snack, but for diabetics or those controlling sugar intake, replace the powdered sugar with a sugar substitute.
