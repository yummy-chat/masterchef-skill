# Home-style Stir-fried Eggplant
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant 1-2 pieces
⭐ Star anise 1 piece (optional)
🦐 Dried shrimp half a handful (optional)
🧅 Scallions 2 stalks (optional)
🥩 Ground pork 50g (optional)
🧂 Soy sauce to taste
🍬 Sugar to taste (optional)
🍶 Vinegar to taste (optional)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Cutting board
🔪 Chef’s knife
🥣 Bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the eggplant, cut in half lengthwise, then slice each half diagonally into diamond-shaped pieces. Place in a bowl and set aside.
2. Wash and chop the scallions into small pieces. Break the star anise into pieces. Place both on the cutting board and set aside.
3. If using ground pork, add 10ml of oil to the pan, stir-fry over medium heat until the meat changes color, then remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Do not overcook the ground pork; keep it tender.
4. Heat the wok over high heat until all moisture inside has evaporated.
5. Add cooking oil, with enough to about twice the depth of the pan’s bottom.
6. When the oil reaches 60% hot (about 180°C), add optional aromatics like star anise, dried shrimp, and scallions. If not using, heat the oil directly to 90% hot (about 210°C).
   **Note**: If using a home stove with limited heat, the oil temperature may not rise noticeably after reaching 70% hot (about 200°C). You can proceed to the next step without forcing it to 90%.
7. When the oil reaches 90% hot (about 210°C), add the eggplant pieces and stir-fry quickly for about 40 seconds.
8. Hold the spatula parallel to the wok surface, pour soy sauce onto the spatula so it flows down the blade. Use about 2.5 spatula-fulls per serving.
9. When the eggplant becomes half-soft, add the pre-cooked ground pork and stir-fry quickly to combine.
10. If using sugar and vinegar, add them now and continue stir-frying for 20 seconds, then immediately remove from the heat.
    **Key point**: Sugar and vinegar must be added at the very end; stir-fry for only 20 seconds before removing from heat.
11. Continue stir-frying until all eggplant pieces change color and become soft, then remove from heat and plate.
    **Note**: Keep the heat high throughout. Do not add water, or the eggplant will become soggy and lose its texture. Soy sauce already contains salt, so no additional salt is needed for this dish. Taste before serving; if too bland, add a tiny pinch of salt, and next time increase the soy sauce amount. If using dried shrimp, reduce the soy sauce accordingly.

## Health Tips
Eggplant absorbs oil easily, so ensure enough oil during cooking to avoid burning. It pairs well with rice, but be mindful of total calorie intake.
