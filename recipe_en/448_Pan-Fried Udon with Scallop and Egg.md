# Pan-Fried Udon with Scallop and Egg
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Udon noodles, about one serving (pre-softened in microwave)
🥬 Cabbage,适量, julienned
🥕 Carrot,适量, julienned

🥚 Egg, 1 piece

## Instructions
1. Julienne the cabbage and carrot into thin strips; pre-thaw the scallops; place the udon noodles in a bowl and heat in the microwave until softened; julienne the seaweed and set aside.
2. Heat the non-stick frying pan, add cooking oil, then add the julienned cabbage and carrot. Stir-fry until softened.
3. Spread the softened udon noodles over the vegetables, drizzle with Japanese BBQ sauce, and add one tablespoon of XO sauce. Quickly stir-fry over medium heat using chopsticks to evenly coat the noodles and vegetables with the sauce.
   **Note**: Stir-fry quickly to prevent the vegetables from overcooking and losing their crispness.
   (Place the softened udon noodles over the vegetables, drizzle with Japanese BBQ sauce, add one tablespoon of XO sauce, and quickly toss with chopsticks over medium heat until the sauce evenly coats the noodles and vegetables.
   **Note**: Toss quickly to avoid the vegetables from overcooking and losing their crisp texture.)

4. In a separate pan or using the same non-stick pan, fry a sunny-side-up egg. Meanwhile, add the thawed scallops next to the egg, sprinkle with ground black pepper, and fry together until the egg edges turn golden brown and the scallops are thoroughly cooked.
   **Note**: The scallops can be cooked using the residual oil from frying the egg; no additional oil is needed.
   (Fry a sunny-side-up egg, and at the same time, place the thawed scallops next to the egg. Sprinkle with ground black pepper and fry together until the egg edges turn golden brown and the scallops are thoroughly cooked.
   **Note**: The scallops can be cooked using the residual oil from frying the egg; no additional oil is needed.)

5. Plate the stir-fried udon noodles, top with the fried egg and scallops, and finally sprinkle with julienned seaweed. Adding the seaweed last helps retain its aroma and crispy texture.

## Health Advice
This dish offers a balanced combination of vegetables, protein, and carbohydrates. The scallops and eggs provide high-quality protein, making it suitable for a quick energy boost on workdays. Note that XO sauce and Japanese BBQ sauce contain relatively high salt levels; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount as needed.
