# Mushroom and Chinese Sausage Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms (flower mushrooms are better) appropriate amount
🥓 Chinese sausage (lap cheong) appropriate amount (sliced thinly, choose good quality)
🧅 Spring onion or onion appropriate amount (julienned)
🌱 Scallions appropriate amount (prepare plenty, finely chopped)
🍚 Rice 2 cups (long-grain aromatic rice or new rice recommended)
🫒 Light soy sauce 25g
🟤 Dark soy sauce 7g
🦪 Oyster sauce 12g
🍬 White granulated sugar 1.5g (reduce amount if using caster sugar)
🧂 Salt 1.5g
💧 Mushroom soaking water 27g (about 3 tablespoons, for sauce) + about 2 cups (for steaming rice)
🫒 Cooking oil (cold-pressed rapeseed oil or peanut oil) appropriate amount (for frying scallion oil, reserve about 25g as base oil for steaming)
💧 Cold water appropriate amount (to supplement if mushroom soaking water is insufficient)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🧻 Cheesecloth or filter cloth
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Measuring cup or small bowl
🥢 Strainer or slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🍚 Rice cooker and inner pot
🥄 Rice paddle

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare mushrooms**: Rinse the dried shiitake mushrooms to remove surface dust, then soak in boiling water for about 1 hour until fully softened with no hard core. Use scissors to remove the stems and slice. Wrap in cheesecloth and squeeze out excess water. Reserve the mushroom soaking water.
   **Note**: Must rinse before soaking, otherwise the mushroom water cannot be used. Squeezing out water reduces stir-frying time and prevents oil splatter.
2. **Prepare Chinese sausage and spring onion**: Slice Chinese sausage thinly; julienne the spring onion.
3. **Prepare sauce**: In a bowl, mix light soy sauce 25g, dark soy sauce 7g, oyster sauce 12g, white sugar 1.5g, and salt 1.5g. Add mushroom soaking water 27g and stir well.
   **Key point**: Adding mushroom water dilutes the sauce to prevent it from becoming too thick and scorching during cooking.
4. **Fry scallion oil and crispy scallions**: Pour sufficient cooking oil into the wok. Add the julienned spring onion to cold oil. Fry over the lowest heat, stirring constantly, until the onions are shriveled and golden brown. Immediately separate the crispy scallions from the oil using a strainer. Reserve both for later use.
5. **Stir-fry mushroom mixture**: Leave about 25g of scallion oil in the wok as base oil. Add the squeezed-dry mushroom slices and stir-fry over the lowest heat for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant and some edges turn slightly golden. Pour in the prepared sauce, stir to combine, bring to a boil, then immediately turn off the heat. Transfer the cooked mushrooms and sauce to a bowl. Keep the Chinese sausage slices raw; do not stir-fry them.
6. **Assemble in the pot**: Place the rinsed rice into the rice cooker inner pot. First, pour in an equal amount of mushroom soaking water. Then add the cooked mushroom mixture and raw Chinese sausage slices. Gently stir a few times so that the mushrooms and sausage are mainly on the surface.
   **Key point**: Ensure the rice is at the bottom and the toppings are on top; otherwise, the rice may remain undercooked.
7. **Cook and steam**: Start the rice cooker on the "delicate cook" or "wood-fired rice" setting. If no such setting is available, let the rice soak for 15 minutes before starting the cooking cycle. After the cooking cycle ends, do not open the lid immediately. Let it rest on warm for 10-15 minutes.
8. **Add scallions**: Open the lid. Use the rice paddle to mix the rice and toppings well. Then sprinkle in a generous amount of fresh, finely chopped scallions and mix again. Add the crispy scallions according to preference.
   **Key point**: Scallions must be added at the very end to preserve their fresh aroma and vibrant green color.

## Health Tips
Shiitake mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber and fungal polysaccharides. Chinese sausage provides protein and fat but is high in sodium. It is recommended to serve with a simple stir-fried vegetable to balance nutrition. Suitable for those needing a quick energy boost. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of Chinese sausage.
