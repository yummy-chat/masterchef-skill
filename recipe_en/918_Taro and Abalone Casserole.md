# Taro and Abalone Casserole
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Lipu taro, about 3-4 jin (choose large ones with white flesh and purple streaks)
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (on the fattier side)
🐚 Abalone, appropriate amount (about 5-6)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (peeled)
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into chunks)
🫒 Lard, a little

🧂 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Steamed fish soy sauce, a little (must be very little)
🍯 Spare ribs sauce, appropriate amount
🌿 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
⭐ Star anise, 2 pieces
🌿 Cinnamon, 1 small piece
🫚 Galangal, 1 small piece
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, a little
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 2-3
🍶 Yellow rice wine, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, about 4 tablespoons
🧂 Salt, a little
🧂 MSG, a little
🌶️ White pepper powder, a little
🐔 Chicken bouillon, a little
💧 Boiling water, appropriate amount (for diluting the sauce)
🥃 Chicken rice dark soy sauce, a little

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥘 Clay pot
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1.  Peel the taro and cut into rectangular slices about 1 cm thick. Cut the pork belly into slices of similar thickness to the taro slices.
2.  Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, mix oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, steamed fish soy sauce, spare ribs sauce, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon, galangal, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, yellow rice wine, sugar, salt, MSG, white pepper powder, and chicken bouillon. Add boiling water, chicken rice dark soy sauce, and lard, and stir well.
3.  Line the bottom of the clay pot with a bamboo mat. Alternately arrange the raw taro slices and pork belly slices, stacking them neatly on the bamboo mat. Arrange the abalones around them.
4.  Spread the sliced scallion, sliced ginger, garlic, and onion on the bottom of the clay pot.
5.  Evenly pour the prepared sauce over the ingredients.
6.  Cover with a plate instead of the pot lid, and cook over heat for about 15 minutes.
7.  Remove the cover, turn to high heat to reduce the sauce. During this time, use a ladle to repeatedly baste the sauce over the ingredients until the sauce thickens.
8.  Turn off the heat, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve.

## Health Tips
Taro is rich in dietary fiber, while abalone and pork belly provide high-quality protein. However, the sauce and pork belly are high in fat. It is recommended to pair this dish with leafy greens for a balanced diet. This dish is suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter, but those with high blood lipids should eat it sparingly.
