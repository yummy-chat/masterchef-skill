# Mala Tang with Sugar
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Sesame paste 8 tablespoons
🥜 Peanut butter 2 tablespoons
🌶️ Sichuan pepper powder to taste
🌶️ Chili powder to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste
🍶 Light soy sauce about 2 circles (added in batches)
💧 Warm water small amounts, added gradually (for thinning the paste)
🍲 Beef tallow hotpot base 1 piece
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns to taste
🌶️ Small green chili peppers to taste (optional, extremely spicy)
🍥 Fish balls to taste
🦀 Crab sticks to taste
🥔 Potatoes to taste (sliced)
🍜 Wide glass noodles to taste
🫘 Tofu skin to taste
🥒 Cucumber to taste
🍜 Instant noodles 1 block
🥩 Sliced fatty beef to taste
🌭 Sausages to taste
🌱 Bean sprouts to taste
🥬 Lettuce to taste
🍬 White sugar about 1 whole box (large amount)
🫒 Sesame oil to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Wooden spoon
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🍽️ Large plate
🥣 Small bowl

## Instructions
1.  Prepare the "80-20 paste": In a bowl, add 8 tablespoons of sesame paste and 2 tablespoons of peanut butter. Add an appropriate amount of Sichuan pepper powder, chili powder, and chicken bouillon. Drizzle in about 2 circles of light soy sauce. Gradually add warm water in small amounts, stirring continuously in one direction with a wooden spoon until the paste is smooth, well-combined, and has no oil separation. Set aside.
2.  Prepare the side ingredients: Slice the potatoes. Cut the tofu skin into strips. Section the cucumber. Wash the lettuce and bean sprouts. Prepare the wide glass noodles and instant noodles. Thaw or cut the fatty beef, sausages, fish balls, and crab sticks as needed. Slice the small green chili peppers into rings for those who prefer extreme spiciness.
3.  Prepare the broth base: Heat a wok using the cold oil method. Add the dried chili peppers and dried Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Immediately add the beef tallow hotpot base. Stir-fry over low heat until the base is completely melted and the aroma is rich.
4.  Cook the base soup: Add enough boiling water to the wok. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring to a boil. Then drizzle in about 2 circles of light soy sauce. Add the sliced small green chili peppers. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for about 3 minutes until the soup turns a vibrant red and the flavors meld. If desired, use a slotted spoon to remove the dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, and other solid residue, resulting in a clearer soup.
5.  Cook the ingredients in batches: Keep the soup base in the wok at a rolling boil. Add the ingredients in batches according to their cooking times. First, add the longer-cooking ingredients like potato slices, wide glass noodles, and fish balls. Cook until the potatoes are soft and the glass noodles are transparent. Then, add the tofu skin, bean sprouts, sausages, instant noodles, fatty beef, and lettuce. Once the beef changes color and the lettuce wilts, quickly remove all ingredients with a slotted spoon, draining the soup, and transfer them to a large plate.
6.  Assemble and season: On the large plate containing the cooked ingredients, first ladle in 2 tablespoons of the hot cooking broth. Then evenly pour the prepared "80-20 paste" over everything. Sprinkle generously with white sugar. Finally, drizzle with an appropriate amount of sesame oil.
7.  Mix and serve: Thoroughly mix all the ingredients with the paste, sugar, and sesame oil, ensuring every piece is well coated with the sauce. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This Mala Tang is high in oil, salt, and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to cut the richness and to control the portion size to avoid excessive calorie intake. It is suitable for those who love bold flavors, but individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume it with caution.
