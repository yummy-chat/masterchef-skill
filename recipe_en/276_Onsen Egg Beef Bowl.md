# Onsen Egg Beef Bowl
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef rolls 400g
🧅 White onion 250g (thinly sliced)
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (cut into flower shapes or slices)
🥦 Broccoli appropriate amount (cut into small florets)
🥚 Eggs 2
🍚 Rice appropriate amount
🧂 Black pepper powder 2g
🧂 Premium soy sauce (or light soy sauce) 55g
🍶 Sake 35g
🍶 Mirin 35g
🍬 White sugar 10g (omit if you don't like sweetness)
💧 Water 500g
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥣 Saucepan
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon, spatula
🥣 Bowl
🌡️ Thermometer

## Steps
1. **Prepare ingredients and sauce**: Thinly slice the white onion. Prepare the carrot and broccoli as needed. In a bowl, combine black pepper powder, premium soy sauce, sake, mirin, white sugar, and 250g of water. Stir well to make the sauce. Set aside another 250g of water separately.
2. **Pre-cook the onsen egg**: Fill the saucepan with enough water and bring to a rolling boil. Turn off the heat and immediately add about 250g of cold water. The water temperature should now be around 70°C. Gently place the eggs in, cover, and let sit for 10 minutes. After the time is up, remove the eggs and rinse them under cold running water to cool down. Then soak them in cold water for 2 minutes. This makes them easier to peel while the yolk remains softly set.
3. **Blanch the beef**: Heat half a pot of water until small bubbles appear at the bottom. Add the beef rolls and quickly separate them with chopsticks over high heat. As soon as most of the beef changes color and turns a pinkish-white, remove it immediately and drain off excess liquid.
   **Key point**: Keep the blanching time short to prevent the beef from becoming tough.
4. **Sauté the onion base**: Heat oil in the wok or pan. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry over high heat until it becomes soft, translucent, and the edges turn slightly golden. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry until fragrant.
5. **Simmer the vegetables and beef**: Add the reserved 250g of water to the wok and bring to a boil over high heat. Place the broccoli and carrot in a single layer, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2 minutes. Next, add the blanched beef slices, turn the heat back to high, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until the sauce evenly coats all the ingredients. Turn off the heat.
6. **Assemble and serve**: Fill a bowl with rice. Top with the beef and vegetables, spoon over the sauce, and finally place the onsen egg on top.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein and iron, but be mindful of the rice and sugar amounts. It is suitable for consumption when physical energy expenditure is high. The onsen egg must be made with fresh eggs, and be cautious of the risk of salmonella.
