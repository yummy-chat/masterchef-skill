# Homemade Tea Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 20
🍵 Black Tea (Lapsang Souchong) 10g
🌿 Fennel Seeds 5g
🌰 Black Cardamom 2
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers 3
⭐ Star Anise 4
🍃 Bay Leaves 4
⚫ Sichuan Peppercorns 3g
🪵 Cinnamon Stick 10g
🍊 Dried Tangerine Peel 10g
🌿 Angelica Dahurica 15g
🧂 Salt First 2 tablespoons (for cleaning), then 15g
🍾 Light Soy Sauce 100ml
🍾 Dark Soy Sauce to taste
🍬 Rock Sugar 30g
🫚 Ginger Slices 30g
🌿 Cilantro Stems 20g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Transparent Glass Bowl
🫕 Transparent Glass Pot
⏲️ Timer
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🥄 Small Porcelain Spoon/Spatula
🪵 Cutting Board

## Steps
1. Place the eggs in a basin, add 2 tablespoons of salt and enough water, then scrub until the eggshells are clean.
2. Put the cleaned eggs into a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for exactly 6 minutes.
   - Starting with cold water ensures the eggs heat more evenly.
3. Immediately remove the cooked eggs and immerse them in cold water to cool for 5 minutes, until completely chilled.
   - The "hot and cold" contrast separates the egg white from the shell membrane, making peeling easier and creating a marbled pattern.
4. Place the cooled eggs on a cutting board, gently press and roll with your palm to crack the shells evenly into a web-like pattern.
   - Roll horizontally once, then vertically once, ensuring the cracks are fine and uniform.
5. In a separate pot over low heat, add all the spices and black tea, dry-fry until fragrant and the edges of the spices are slightly charred.
   - Spices can be broken into smaller pieces beforehand to better release their flavor.
6. Add rock sugar to the pot, stirring over low heat until the sugar completely melts and turns a deep amber caramel color.
7. Pour 100ml of light soy sauce along the edge of the pot to release the savory aroma, then add enough water to cover.
8. Add dark soy sauce to the broth for color, then add 15g of salt, ginger slices, and cilantro stems.
   - Cilantro stems are key for enhancing the aroma and should not be omitted.
9. Bring the broth to a boil over high heat, then add the cracked eggs, ensuring the broth completely submerges them.
10. Reduce to low heat, cover the pot, and simmer for 15 minutes, then turn off the heat.
11. Do not remove the eggs after turning off the heat; let them naturally cool in the broth, preferably soaking overnight for the flavors to fully penetrate.
    - For storage, place the eggs and broth together in the refrigerator, where they can be kept for about a week.

## Health Suggestions
These tea eggs are rich in high-quality protein, but the brine contains a significant amount of salt and soy sauce. It is recommended to eat 1-2 eggs per serving, and those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. The flavor is best after soaking overnight, but do not soak for more than 3 days to avoid excessive saltiness.
