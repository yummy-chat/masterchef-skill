# Dried Abalone
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦪 South African dried abalone, size 12 and 16 per catty
🐔 Old hen, 1 (roasted or fried, to enhance umami and aroma)
🦴 Pork ribs, 3 catties (roasted, to enhance meat flavor and prevent scorching at the bottom)
🐾 Chicken feet, 2 catties (roasted, to increase gelatinous texture)
🐷 Pork trotters, 3 catties (roasted, for richer meat flavor and collagen)
🥓 Jinhua ham, 2 catties (long-lasting aroma enhancement)
🐚 Dried scallops (conpoy), 200 grams (to enhance freshness, easy to break apart, place on top)
🫚 Ginger peel, 50 grams (to remove fishy odors and enhance aroma)
🍲 Stock, approximately 10 catties
🌾 Red fermented rice, 50 grams (soak to extract water for coloring)
🍬 Rock sugar, approximately 30 grams
🦪 Lee Kum Kee Premium Oyster Sauce, approximately 30 grams (enhances freshness, contains salt)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Clay pot / stewing pot (main cooking pot)
🍲 Another clay pot (for preparing stock)
🍳 Wok (for final sauce reduction)
🥄 Soup ladle
🎞️ Plastic wrap
🥣 Glass bowl / stainless steel basin
🍴 Strainer
🪥 Toothbrush (for cleaning abalone)
🧂 Fine mesh strainer (for filtering abalone sauce)
🔥 Induction stove / gas stove

## Steps
1. Initial soaking of dried abalone: Place dried abalone in purified water, seal with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight.
2. Cleaning dried abalone: Remove the esophagus at the head, use a toothbrush to thoroughly scrub the antennae and black residue along the edges.
3. Cooking dried abalone: Place cleaned abalone in water at 90°C, simmer over low heat for about 1 hour, until softened when pressed with a finger.
4. Second soaking: After cooking, seal immediately with plastic wrap while hot. Once cooled, refrigerate again overnight to fully soften and expand the abalone.
5. Layering in the pot: Line the bottom of the main cooking pot with half the pork ribs, then layer with old hen, pork trotters, Jinhua ham, soaked dried abalone, top with dried scallops and chicken feet, and finally add ginger peel. Note: Pork ribs at the bottom prevent scorching; chicken feet and scallops should be placed on top to avoid sticking. Use another clay pot to prepare stock.
6. Initial simmering: Pour in stock, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer continuously for 24 to 36 hours. Key: Do not interrupt the heat at any point, or the abalone's molten heart will tighten; replenish stock from the stock pot as needed, or add boiling water if stock pot runs out.
7. Seasoning: Soak red fermented rice in some stock for 20 minutes to obtain red fermented rice water. Add rock sugar, oyster sauce, and red fermented rice water to the pot for coloring. Note: Do not add seasonings in advance, or the abalone will shrink; this recipe requires no salt, as Jinhua ham and oyster sauce provide saltiness.
8. Sauce reduction: Continue simmering for about 2 hours. Remove all solids (pomace and auxiliary ingredients), keeping only the abalone. Strain the abalone sauce, return it to the pot with the abalone, and boil over high heat to reduce the sauce until thick, ensuring each abalone is evenly coated.
9. Plating: Remove abalone for plating, pour the thickened sauce from the pot over them, and serve.

## Health Tips
This dried abalone dish is rich in collagen and protein, suitable for those needing to supplement collagen or for convalescents. However, it is high in calories and sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume with caution. Note that the cooking heat must never be interrupted, or the molten heart will contract.
