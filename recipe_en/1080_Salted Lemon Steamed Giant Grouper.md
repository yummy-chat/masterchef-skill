## Preparation Steps  
1. Make a cut along both sides of the bone under the fish belly so that the fish body can lie flat, ensuring an attractive shape and even heat distribution when steamed.  
2. Evenly rub salt on both sides of the fish. Marinate for 5 minutes to season, then rinse thoroughly with clean water to remove excess salt and fishy smell.  
3. Place the fish on a plate, top with shredded ginger and chopped salted lemon, and sprinkle a little shredded lime peel on top.  
4. Drizzle with chicken fat to form a protective layer over the fish under high-temperature steam, locking in moisture and freshness.  
5. Place in a high-heat steamer and steam for 12 minutes. Insert a bamboo skewer into the thickest part of the fish’s back; if it goes in easily, the fish is done.  
6. After steaming, reserve the liquid from the plate. Heat chicken fat in another wok and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.  
7. Arrange shredded scallion and red bell pepper on the fish, pour the hot garlic-infused chicken fat over the top, and finally drizzle with an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce.  

## Health Advice  
Giant grouper is rich in high-quality protein, and salted lemon helps stimulate appetite and aid digestion, making it suitable for those with weak spleen and stomach or poor appetite. Note that salted lemon is high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount; precise steaming time is crucial, as overcooking will make the meat tough.
