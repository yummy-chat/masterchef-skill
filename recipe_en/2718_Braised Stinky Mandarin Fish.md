# Braised Stinky Mandarin Fish
Cuisine: Anhui Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Stinky mandarin fish 1 (about 430g)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, to taste  
🍚 Rice wine, to taste  
🥄 Lard, 1 small spoon  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔥 Large iron wok/frying pan  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Spatula  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Rinse the stinky mandarin fish clean, score both sides of the fish with a diagonal knife pattern, and pat dry the surface moisture with kitchen paper towels.  
2. Pour oil into the wok and heat it. Gently slide the dried fish into the wok. Fry one side over medium heat until set and golden brown, then carefully flip and fry the other side to the same state.  
**Key point**: Patting dry prevents hot oil from splattering; minimize turning during frying to keep the fish skin intact and unbroken.  

3. Push the fried fish to the side of the wok. Use the remaining oil in the wok to sauté scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic cloves, and dried chili peppers until fragrant. Add a small spoon of lard and melt it to enhance the aroma.  
4. Add one spoonful of water to the wok, then season with salt, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and rice wine in order.  
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Cover the wok with a lid and braise for about 10 minutes until the fish is infused with flavor and the sauce has thickened.  
6. Remove the lid, gently flip the fish, and continue braising until the sauce is thick with a bit remaining. Sprinkle with scallion segments and red chili pepper segments, cook briefly, then remove from the wok and plate.  
**Key point**: Flip the fish gently and slowly to keep the fish body intact and unbroken.  

## Health Advice  
Stinky mandarin fish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, but the brining process results in high salt content; people with high blood pressure should control their intake. Adding lard for aroma during braising is recommended; pair with light vegetables to balance the greasiness.
