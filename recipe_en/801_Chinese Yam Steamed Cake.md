# Chinese Yam Steamed Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🟤 1 Iron Stick Chinese Yam (peeled and sliced, yielding about 350g of cooked yam paste)
🌾 200g Rice Flour
🌾 100g Cake Flour
🌾 50g Tapioca Starch
💧 Water, as needed (enough to submerge the yam, about 100–120ml)
🖤 Black Sesame Seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)
🍚 5g Active Dry Yeast
🍬 30g Granulated Sugar

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Spatula
🔪 Chef’s Knife and Cutting Board
🥣 Bowls (at least 2)
♨️ Steamer
🥤 Blender or High-Speed Blender
🥢 Whisk or Chopsticks
🎂 6-inch Round Springform Cake Pan
🫙 Plastic Wrap

## Instructions
1. Peel the Chinese yam and cut into thin slices. Place in a bowl. If your skin is prone to irritation, it is recommended to wear gloves.
   **Note**: The yam’s mucilage may irritate the skin; wearing gloves can prevent this.

2. Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Add the yam slices and steam over high heat for 15 minutes, until easily pierced with a chopstick.
   **Key Point**: Adjust steaming time according to the thickness of the slices to ensure they are completely soft.

3. Transfer the steamed yam slices to a blender. Add enough water to just submerge the yam, and blend into a smooth paste. Pour into a large bowl.
   **Note**: Control the amount of water so the yam paste has a moderate consistency; too much water will make the batter too thin.

4. Let the yam paste cool until warm, then add the dry yeast and granulated sugar. Stir to combine.
   **Key Point**: If the temperature is too high, it will kill the yeast. Ensure it has cooled to warm before adding.

5. Add the rice flour, cake flour, and tapioca starch one by one. Use a whisk or chopsticks to stir in one direction until the batter is smooth, free of lumps, and thick with a stretchy consistency.
   **Key Point**: Stir vigorously to develop gluten in the batter, which ensures a fluffy steamed cake.

6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it ferment in a warm place until the batter has doubled in volume and is filled with honeycomb air pockets.

7. Remove the plastic wrap and stir the batter with chopsticks to release large air bubbles. Lightly grease the inside of the cake pan with oil or line it with parchment paper. Pour in the batter and gently tap the pan to level the surface.

8. Place the pan in a warm spot for a second fermentation, about 25 minutes, until the batter rises to 80% full in the pan and the surface looks plump.

9. Evenly sprinkle black sesame seeds on the surface. Once the steamer water is boiling, place the pan inside, cover, and steam over high heat for 35 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 3 minutes before removing the lid.
   **Note**: Maintain high heat and full steam throughout the process. Do not open the lid during steaming, and be sure to rest it after turning off the heat.

10. Remove and let cool slightly. Gently press around the edges of the pan to release the cake, then invert to remove it before serving.
    **Tip**: Unmolding while still slightly warm makes it easier, and the cake will not stick to your hands.

## Health Advice
Chinese yam steamed cake uses yam paste to replace part of the staple grains. It is rich in dietary fiber and mucilaginous protein, making it suitable for those with weak digestion or needing to control blood sugar. It is recommended to pair it with soy milk or milk as a breakfast option, and avoid eating it alone to prevent a rapid spike in blood sugar.
