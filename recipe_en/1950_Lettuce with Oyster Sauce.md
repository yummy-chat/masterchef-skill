# Lettuce with Oyster Sauce
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 Ingredients
- 1 head of lettuce (200 g ± 50)
- 4-5 cloves of garlic (made into garlic paste or minced)

🧂 Seasonings
- 6-8 ml oyster sauce
- 10 ml light soy sauce (for the sauce)
- 0.5 g salt (for blanching) + 0.5 g salt (for the sauce)
- 1 g sugar
- 5-8 ml cooking oil (for sautéing garlic) + 2-3 ml cooking oil (for blanching)

🍳 Kitchenware
- Pot
- Bowl
- Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the lettuce leaf by leaf, removing any wilted or yellow leaves.
2. Pour 1 liter of water into a pot, add 2-3 ml of cooking oil and 0.5 g of salt, and bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Key Point**: Adding oil and salt helps keep the lettuce bright green and adds a base flavor.
3. Once the water is boiling, add the lettuce, ensuring the leaves are fully submerged, and blanch for 10 seconds, then immediately remove.
   **Key Point**: The blanching time must be kept within 10 seconds to maintain a crisp and tender texture.
4. Remove the blanched lettuce, drain excess water, and arrange neatly on a plate.
   **Key Point**: Make sure to drain the water thoroughly to prevent diluting the sauce later.
5. In a bowl, mix 10 ml of light soy sauce, 6-8 ml of oyster sauce, 0.5 g of salt, 1 g of sugar, and add 10-15 ml of water. Stir until fully combined.
6. Heat another pot over medium heat, pour in 5-8 ml of cooking oil, then add the minced garlic, stir-frying until the garlic fragrance is released.
7. Once the garlic is fragrant, pour in the prepared sauce, bring to a boil, then immediately turn off the heat.
8. Evenly pour the cooked sauce over the lettuce.
   **Key Point**: Pour the sauce evenly, ensuring each piece of lettuce is coated with the sauce.

## Health Tips
Blanching lettuce for 10 seconds retains its crisp texture and most of the vitamins. Sautéing the garlic paste and pairing it with oyster sauce enhances the flavor, making this dish suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or looking to increase vegetable intake. Be sure to drain the water thoroughly to avoid diluting the sauce, and people with cardiovascular conditions should reduce the salt amount.
