# Fresh Jasmine Mousse Cake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍪 13 cookies (90g)
🧈 40g butter
🥛 250g whipping cream
🥄 100g thick yogurt
🍵 2 bags jasmine green tea powder (2g total, 1 bag for mousse, 1 bag for tea jelly)
🧊 11g gelatin sheets (about 4.5 sheets, 2.5 sheets for mousse, 2 sheets for tea jelly)
💧 100g water
🌸 Jasmine buds, as needed (for decoration)
🧂 45g white sugar (40g for mousse, 5g for tea jelly)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍜 Instant noodle bucket (with lid)
🧴 Plastic bottle (with lid)
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Pot
🥢 Chopsticks/spoon
✂️ Scissors
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. **Make the cookie crust**: Crush the cookies into fine crumbs and place them in the instant noodle bucket. Add melted butter and mix well. Press firmly with the bottom of a bottle or similar tool to compact. Transfer to the refrigerator to chill and set.
   **Key points**: Butter must be fully melted; cookie crumbs must be pressed firmly to ensure a stable base.
2. **Soak the gelatin**: Soak the gelatin sheets in cold water until softened, then drain and set aside.
3. **Whip the cream**: Pour the whipping cream, white sugar, and one bag of jasmine green tea powder into a clean plastic bottle. Seal the lid tightly and shake vigorously until the cream expands to about 1.5 times its original volume and reaches a thick, yogurt-like consistency.
   **Key points**: The container must be completely free of oil and water; shake thoroughly to fully aerate the cream.
4. **Make the yogurt-gelatin mixture**: Place the softened gelatin sheets and yogurt together in a bowl. Heat over a water bath, stirring until the gelatin is completely dissolved. Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature.
5. **Combine the mousse mixture**: Pour the cooled yogurt-gelatin mixture into the plastic bottle containing the whipped cream. Seal the lid tightly and shake well to combine.
   **Note**: The yogurt-gelatin mixture must be fully cooled, otherwise it will cause the whipped cream to melt and deflate.
6. **Pour and freeze the mousse**: Pour the mousse mixture into the instant noodle bucket with the cookie crust. Gently tap the bucket a few times to release air bubbles. Freeze for at least 4 hours, or until fully set.
7. **Make the jasmine tea jelly**: Mix water, white sugar, and one bag of jasmine green tea powder together. Add the softened gelatin sheets and heat over a water bath, stirring until the gelatin is completely dissolved. Let cool until close to body temperature.
8. **Pour the tea jelly layer**: Slowly pour the cooled tea jelly liquid over the set mousse surface. Gently tap the bucket to level the surface.
   **Note**: If the tea jelly liquid is too hot, it will melt the mousse layer; pour gently to maintain clear layers.
9. **Freeze again**: Place the cake back in the freezer for 10 minutes to allow the tea jelly layer to initially set.
10. **Unmold and decorate**: Remove the cake from the freezer. Use scissors to cut and peel off the instant noodle bucket shell. Transfer the cake to a plate and decorate the tea jelly surface with jasmine buds. Unmold gently to keep the cake intact.

## Health Advice
This jasmine mousse cake features a butter cookie crust paired with jasmine tea jelly, offering a fresh and pleasant flavor ideal for spring and summer afternoons. Yogurt and gelatin provide a small amount of calcium and protein, but the whipping cream and sugar content are relatively high, so moderate consumption is recommended.
