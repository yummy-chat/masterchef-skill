# Winning Chocolate Cake
Cuisine Type: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Egg yolk 40g  
🥚 Egg white 97g  
🥛 Milk 40g  
🌽 Corn oil 51g  
🍫 Dark chocolate 10g (pre-chopped)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 10g  
🌾 Cake flour 44g  
🍬 Caster sugar 51g (20g for yolk mixture, 31g for meringue)  
🍋 Lemon juice or white vinegar 3g  
🧂 Salt 1g  
🍷 Dark rum 10g (optional)  
🍫 Valrhona Caribbean dark chocolate 50g (for cream)  
🥛 Blue Windmill whipping cream approx. 280g (for cream)  
🍫 Dark chocolate 50g (for glaze)  
🥛 Whipping cream 100g (for glaze)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (multiple)  
🔌 Handheld electric mixer  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥄 Measuring cup/measuring spoons  
🍲 Small saucepan  
♨️ Double boiler setup  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🍰 6-inch round cake pan  
🌬️ Cooling rack  
🎂 Turntable and offset spatula  
🌡️ Infrared thermometer  
🔪 Serrated knife  
☕ Measuring cup with spout  

## Instructions  
### 1. Preparation Stage  
1. Accurately weigh all ingredients. Chop the dark chocolate for the cake, and sift the cocoa powder for later use.  
2. Separate the egg whites from the yolks. Place the egg whites in a clean, grease-free bowl and freeze until the edges develop a thin layer of ice.  

### 2. Making the Chocolate Chiffon Cake  
1. **Prepare the yolk mixture**: Add 20g caster sugar to the egg yolks and whisk until the color lightens and the sugar is mostly dissolved. In another bowl, combine milk, corn oil, chopped chocolate, and sifted cocoa powder.  
2. Heat the chocolate mixture over a double boiler until above 40°C/104°F, stirring until the chocolate is fully melted and emulsified. Pour it into the yolk mixture and mix well. Add the rum if using. Sift in the cake flour and mix until smooth, shiny, and free of dry lumps.  
3. **Prepare the meringue**: Remove the egg whites with thin ice, add the salt and lemon juice, and beat on high speed until large bubbles form. Add the 31g caster sugar in three batches, continuing to beat until stiff peaks form. Finally, quickly stir with a spatula or hand whisk to restore smoothness.  
4. **Combine the batter**: Add one-third of the meringue to the yolk mixture and roughly fold together. Then pour this mixture back into the remaining meringue. Fold using the "J-shape folding method" for about 40-50 strokes until the batter is even, fine, and has no marble streaks.  
5. **Bake**: Pour the cake batter into the pan from a height. Lightly tap the pan to release air bubbles. Bake in a preheated oven. For a small oven, bake at 140°C/284°F for about 80 minutes. For a large oven, reference 150°C/302°F for 50-60 minutes. The cake is done when it springs back upon pressing and a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Immediately after baking, drop the pan from a height of 10cm, then invert it onto a cooling rack to cool completely.  

### 3. Making the Chocolate Cream  
1. Place 50g dark chocolate and 15g caster sugar in a bowl. Heat the whipping cream until just before boiling, then immediately pour it over the chocolate. Let it stand for 5 minutes.  
   **Note**: Add an extra 30-40g of whipping cream to compensate for loss during the process.  
2. Stir in a circular motion from the center outward until smooth and shiny, like a ganache. Cover with plastic wrap touching the surface and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.  
3. Remove the chilled ganache. Beat with an electric mixer starting at low speed until the lines are clear and the texture is like soft ice cream. Avoid over-beating.  

### 4. Making the Glaze Ganache  
1. In a small bowl, combine 50g dark chocolate and 100g whipping cream. Heat over a double boiler, stirring until fully melted and smooth. Remove from heat.  
2. Let it cool. Use a thermometer to check the temperature. Use it when it cools to around 30°C/86°F. The temperature must not be too high or too low.  

### 5. Assembly and Decoration  
1. Unmold the completely cooled cake. Use a serrated knife to slice it horizontally into 3-4 even layers. Trim off the uneven top.  
2. Place one cake layer on the turntable and spread about 100g of the chocolate cream over it. Place the second layer on top and spread another layer of cream. Add the third layer. Roughly spread the remaining cream over the top and sides to cover evenly.  
3. Transfer the cake onto a wire rack with a tray underneath. Slowly pour the glaze ganache (at the correct temperature) from the center of the cake, letting it naturally cover the top and drip down the sides. First pour over the center, then add more along the edges to ensure even coverage.  
4. Scrape off any remaining ganache left in the bowl with a spatula and gently smooth it onto the sides for a more even finish. Wipe the edge of the base clean. Refrigerate for 1-2 hours to set before slicing.  

## Health Advice  
This chocolate cake is rich in antioxidants, but it has a high caloric density. It is suitable as an occasional dessert and is not recommended for daily consumption. Be mindful of portion sizes. Pairing it with unsweetened tea or black coffee can balance the sweetness.
