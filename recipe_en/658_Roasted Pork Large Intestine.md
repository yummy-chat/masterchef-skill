# Roasted Pork Large Intestine
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 2 pieces pork large intestine (use fresh large intestine head)
🧅 Ginger, appropriate amount (to remove odor and enhance aroma)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (to remove odor during blanching)
🧅 Scallion segments, appropriate amount (to enhance aroma)
🌿 Bay leaves, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount each (collectively known as aromatics, to enhance aroma)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (to provide base flavor)
🧂 Light soy sauce/soy sauce, appropriate amount (to enhance flavor)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring)
🍶 Rice wine, appropriate amount (to enhance aroma)
🍬 Rock sugar, a small amount (to enhance aroma and brightness)
🌶️ Cumin powder/whole cumin, appropriate amount (dry dipping seasoning)
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount (dry dipping seasoning)
🌰 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (dry dipping seasoning)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (to brush the surface during roasting)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for cleaning the intestine)
🥄 Slotted spoon (to remove the intestine)
🍳 Wok (for blanching)
♨️ Pressure cooker (for braising the intestine)
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife (to cut the intestine)
🔥 Oven (for roasting the intestine)
🪝 Roasting rack and baking tray (to hold the intestine skewers)
🥢 Bamboo skewers (to thread the intestine)
🥣 Small bowl and spoon (to mix the dry dipping seasoning)
✂️ Scissors (to trim the intestine)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation
1. Turn the fresh large intestine head inside out, remove internal impurities, excess fat, and lymph nodes, and repeatedly rub and rinse with clean water until the water becomes clear.
   **Key point**: Must be thoroughly cleaned to remove any unpleasant odor. Cleaning the lymph nodes and scum is especially important.
2. Mix the cumin, chili flakes, and white sesame seeds evenly to prepare the dry dipping seasoning. Set aside.

### Cooking
1. Add cold water to a pot, place the intestine, ginger slices, and cooking wine in it. Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling for 1–2 minutes.
   **Note**: Must start with cold water to fully draw out the odor.
2. Remove the intestine, rinse it under cold water to clean off surface scum.
3. Place the intestine into a pressure cooker. Add ginger, scallion, aromatics, salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice wine, and rock sugar. Pour in enough clean water to submerge the ingredients.
4. Cover the lid, bring to high pressure, and cook for about 15 minutes. Allow the pressure to release naturally before opening.
   **Key point**: Adjust the pressure cooking time based on the thickness of the intestine; it should be tender and fully flavored.
5. Remove the intestine and let it cool. Cut it into rings about 2–3 cm thick, and thread them onto bamboo skewers.
   **Alternative**: If bamboo skewers are unavailable, place the intestine rings directly on the roasting rack for roasting.
6. Preheat the oven to 250°C. Place the skewered intestine in the middle rack and roast for about 25–30 minutes.
   **Doneness check**: After about 10 minutes of roasting, the intestine will start to release oil and the surface will shrink; continue roasting until the surface is golden and crispy.
7. During roasting, brush a thin layer of cooking oil evenly over the surface to help create a crispy skin.
8. Once the intestine skin is crispy, remove it from the oven. Sprinkle the mixed dry dipping seasoning evenly over it, then return to the oven to roast for an additional 1–2 minutes to release the aroma of the spices.
   **Note**: The final roasting time with seasoning should not be too long to avoid burning the spices and making them bitter.
9. Remove from the oven and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Roasted large intestine is rich in protein and contains a small amount of fat, but its cholesterol content is relatively high. It is recommended to consume in moderation, especially for those needing an energy boost. Individuals with high blood pressure or high blood lipids should control their intake.
