# Large Fried Dough Sticks

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g  
🧂 Baking powder 4g  
🧂 Baking soda 2g  
💧 Water 100–110g (adjust based on flour absorbency)  
🥚 Egg 1 (about 50g)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 20g (for the dough, can be substituted with melted butter or lard)  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🍬 Sugar 4g (helps with browning)  
🫒 Frying oil as needed (generous amount, vegetable oil is fine)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (one large, one small)  
🥢 Whisk or chopsticks  
🔪 Spatula/dough scraper  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔪 Dough cutter  
🛍️ Plastic bag or wrap  
🍳 Deep fryer or wok  
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer  
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon

## Instructions
1. **Mix dry ingredients**: Place all-purpose flour, baking powder, and baking soda in a large bowl. Whisk thoroughly to ensure the leavening agents are evenly distributed.
2. **Mix wet ingredients**: In a small bowl, crack the egg, add water, salt, sugar, and 20g vegetable oil. Whisk until the salt and sugar are completely dissolved and the liquid is emulsified.
3. **Form dough clumps**: Gradually pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients while stirring with chopsticks until a uniform, crumbly dough forms with no dry flour remaining.
4. **First pressing**: Use your fists to repeatedly press and fold the dough with a scraper until it just comes together. Cover with plastic wrap and **rest for 20 minutes**.
   - **Do not knead**: Only press or fold the dough throughout the process to avoid developing gluten. The initial rough and sticky dough is normal.
5. **Second pressing**: After resting, continue pressing with your fists until the dough becomes smooth and glossy. If needed, rest for another 10–20 minutes and repeat this step.
6. **Shaping and proofing**: Dust the work surface with flour to prevent sticking. Shape the smooth dough into a rectangle. Seal it in a plastic bag and **proof at room temperature for 4 hours**, or **proof in the refrigerator overnight**.
7. **Roll and cut**: Remove the proofed dough. Dust the work surface and dough surface with plenty of flour. Roll into a rectangle about 0.5cm thick and 45–50cm long. Use a dough cutter to slice into even strips about 2–3cm wide.
8. **Assemble pairs**:
   - Dip a chopstick in water and draw a line of water down the center of every other strip.
   - Place a strip without a water line on top of a strip with a water line.
   - Use the back of a knife or a chopstick to press firmly down the center of the stacked pairs, ensuring they stick together well.
   - **Key point**: The water line and pressing are essential for creating the "two halves" and puffing up during frying.
9. **Frying**: Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok or deep fryer to **190°C–200°C**, then reduce to medium-low heat to maintain the temperature. Take one dough pair, gently stretch it by holding both ends, and first lower the middle into the oil before releasing the ends to prevent oil splatter. The dough should float to the surface within about 3 seconds. Immediately use long chopsticks to continuously stir and flip the dough sticks for even heating and expansion. Fry until golden brown and puffed, about 2–3 minutes total.
   - **Key note**: Maintaining a stable oil temperature and constant flipping are crucial for large, round, and fluffy dough sticks.

## Health Advice
While fried dough sticks are delicious, high-temperature frying makes them high in calories and fat. It is recommended to pair them with soy milk or vegetables to cut the grease, and to enjoy them occasionally. Due to their high sodium and fat content, those with high blood pressure or on a weight loss diet should consume them in very limited amounts.
