# Golden Pumpkin Mandarin Fish Soup 🐟

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable all year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 1 mandarin fish (chop bones into pieces, slice fillets into thin pieces)  
🍠 About 1/4 pumpkin (sliced, steamed, and mashed into puree)  
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (sliced and placed at the bottom of the bowl)  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for final garnish)  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (for making scallion-ginger juice, removing fishy smell when frying bones, and sprinkling white scallion pieces at the end)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for making scallion-ginger juice, removing fishy smell when frying bones)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (sprinkle minced garlic at the end)  
🧂 Cooking wine, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for scallion-ginger juice, seasoning the soup, and final seasoning)  
🥚 A small amount of egg white (for marinating the fish fillets, do not use too much)  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (for marinating the fish fillets)  
🫘 Soybean oil, as needed (for sealing in moisture during marination, frying bones, and final aroma splash)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed (sprinkle on top of fish fillets at the end)  
🫙 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (sprinkle on top of fish fillets at the end)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥘 Strainer or slotted spoon  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Serving dish (e.g., glass baking dish)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the fish**: Remove bones from the mandarin fish, slice the fillets into thin pieces, and chop the bones into pieces.  
   *Note*: Knife skills are important; try to slice the fish evenly in thickness.

2. **Make scallion-ginger juice**: Flatten scallions and ginger, add cooking wine and salt, then squeeze and rub vigorously to extract juice. This will be used to marinate the fish fillets.  
3. **Marinate the fish fillets**:  
   - Place the fish fillets in a bowl. First, pour in the scallion-ginger juice and mix until the fillets become sticky and tacky to the touch.  
   - Add a small amount of egg white and continue mixing.  
   - Add cornstarch and mix well.  
   - Finally, drizzle in a little soybean oil and gently mix to seal in moisture.  
   *Key point*: The marinating order must not be reversed. Do not use too much egg white. The oil coats the starch, and the starch wraps the fish, preventing the coating from falling off during cooking.

4. **Make the golden soup base**: Slice the pumpkin and steam over high heat for 15 minutes until soft. Remove and use a spoon to mash into a fine puree.  
   *Key point*: The pumpkin puree is the key to achieving the golden color of the soup.

5. **Fry the fish bones**: Heat the wok over high heat, then add cold oil. Once the oil is hot, add the fish bone pieces and fry over medium-high heat.  
   - Fry for about 1 minute until the bottom is set and firm, then gently shake the wok. Flip and fry until both sides are golden brown.  
   *Key point*: A hot wok with cold oil prevents sticking; do not flip too early.

6. **Simmer the fish broth**: After the bones are fried, add scallions and ginger to remove the fishy smell. Immediately add enough boiling water, then turn to high heat and cook until the broth turns milky white.  
   *Key point*: Adding boiling water yields the best results; do not add salt during simmering, as it may affect the color of the broth.

7. **Season the soup**: Once the broth is milky white, stir in the pumpkin puree and season with salt.  
8. **Handle the fish bones**: Continue cooking for about 2 minutes after seasoning, then use a slotted spoon to remove all the bones. Arrange them at the bottom of the serving dish, which has been lined with cucumber slices.  
9. **Cook the fish fillets**: Keep the golden soup boiling in the wok. Gently drop in the marinated fish fillets, spreading them out. Cook until the fillets turn white and the soup comes to a boil again, then remove.  
   *Key point*: Fish fillets cook very quickly and should not be boiled for too long.

10. **Plate**: Arrange the cooked fish fillets evenly over the fish bones, then pour the golden soup from the wok over the top.  
11. **Aroma splash**: Evenly sprinkle white scallion pieces, minced garlic, dried chili peppers, and dried Sichuan peppercorns over the surface of the fish fillets. Heat soybean oil to about 60% hot (around 180°C), then quickly pour it over the seasonings to release their fragrance.  
    *Key point*: The oil should be at 60% hot; too hot will burn the chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns, making them bitter.

12. **Garnish**: Place cilantro on top and serve.

## Health Tips  
This golden pumpkin mandarin fish soup uses pumpkin puree to create a tempting golden broth, adding both color and beta-carotene. It is suitable for those needing eye care or looking to control calorie intake. The fish fillets are thinly sliced and quickly cooked, with a small amount of egg white and cornstarch for marination, making this a high-quality, low-fat, high-protein choice. Overall, it uses less oil and salt, and the cucumber slices at the bottom add a refreshing touch.
