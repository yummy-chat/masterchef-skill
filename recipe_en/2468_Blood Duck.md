# Blood Duck  
Cuisine: 🌶️ Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: 🌍 Suitable for all seasons  

---

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
- 🥩 Fresh young duck meat 2000g  
- 🩸 Fresh duck blood 250ml (add vinegar when collecting blood during slaughter, stir clockwise with chopsticks to prevent coagulation)  
- 🫚 Ginger 6 slices (can add 1-3 extra slices)  
- 🧄 Garlic cloves 6 cloves  
- 🧅 Scallions 2, chopped and set aside  
- 🌶️ Chili peppers 1000g (green peppers/line peppers/beauty peppers optional; for extra heat, add 5-8 small chili peppers or bird's eye chilies, chopped and set aside)  
- 🍾 Liquor (choose one): 50ml high-proof baijiu + 150ml water, or 200ml beer, or 200ml rice wine  
- 🥄 Light soy sauce 10ml  
- 🍶 Cooking wine 30ml  
- 🧂 Salt 8g  
- 🧂 Chicken bouillon 5g  
- 🌿 Sichuan pepper oil (optional) 3-6 drops  

## Cookware and Equipment  
- 🔪 Chef's knife  
- 🥘 Wok  
- 🥢 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the fresh young duck meat into pieces about 3 cm, add cooking wine and ginger slices, mix well, and let sit briefly to remove blood.  
**Key Point**: Marinating helps remove gamey smell and initially infuse flavor.  

2. Heat the wok over high heat, pour in about 100ml cooking oil, heat until slight smoke rises. Add the marinated duck meat pieces, stir-fry quickly over high heat until the surface changes color and moisture evaporates.  
**Key Point**: High-heat stir-frying locks in duck meat juices, preventing it from becoming tough.  

3. Once the duck meat has fully changed color, pour in the chosen liquor, then add about 200ml boiling water so the liquid just covers the duck meat. Cover with a lid, reduce to medium heat, and braise for 15 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Add liquor before water; alcohol evaporation removes gamey smell, and boiling water shortens cooking time.  

4. When the water boils, remove the lid, add ginger slices and garlic cloves, stir well. Cover again and continue braising for 10 minutes.  
**Key Point**: Add ginger and garlic at this stage to avoid adding too early, which would cause flavor loss.  

5. Remove the lid, add the chopped chili peppers, stir-fry continuously until the peppers soften. Then pour in the fresh duck blood, stir quickly to ensure every piece of duck meat and chili pepper is evenly coated with blood.  
**Key Point**: After adding duck blood, stir-fry quickly to prevent the blood from coagulating into lumps.  

6. Continue stir-frying until the duck blood has completely turned black. Add salt, chicken bouillon, and scallion segments. If using Sichuan pepper oil, add 3-6 drops at the same time. Stir well again, then turn off the heat.  
**Key Point**: Duck blood turning black indicates it is fully cooked; do not stir-fry for too long after seasoning.  

7. Plate and serve immediately.  
**Key Point**: Blood duck is best enjoyed hot for optimal texture.  

## Health Advice  
Blood duck is rich in protein and iron, suitable for those with high physical exertion or those needing to replenish blood. However, it is high in chili peppers and oil, so those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation. When cooking, ensure the duck blood is stirred quickly to avoid lumps affecting texture.
