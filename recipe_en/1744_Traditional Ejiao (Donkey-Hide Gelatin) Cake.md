# Traditional Ejiao (Donkey-Hide Gelatin) Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐴 Fresh donkey hide 5 jin (core ingredient, must be thoroughly degreased and boiled)  
🌰 Walnuts 280g (pre-toasted until fragrant, broken into small pieces)  
⚫ Black sesame seeds 200g (pre-roasted until fragrant)  
🫐 Pitted red dates 300g  
🍒 Goji berries 150g  

🍾 Cooking wine as needed (for removing odor when blanching the donkey hide)  
🧂 Table salt as needed (for scrubbing the donkey hide)  
🌾 Flour as needed (used with salt to scrub off oil)  
🍯 Yellow rock sugar 300g  
🍷 Yellow wine (Huangjiu) 280ml  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Large pot (for prolonged boiling of donkey hide)  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer (for separating the gelatin liquid)  
🍳 Wok/non-stick pan (for stir-frying ingredients and cooking the ejiao paste)  
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and mixing ingredients)  
🥢 Wooden spatula/scraper (for stirring during cooking to prevent sticking)  
📄 Oil paper (to line the mold to prevent sticking)  
📦 Baking pan/mold (for shaping the ejiao cake)  
🔪 Knife (for slicing)  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Donkey hide pretreatment**: Soak fresh donkey hide in clean water for 5 hours. Place in cold water, add cooking wine and scallion segments, boil for 20 minutes. Remove and rinse under cold water. Split in half, scrape off the inner fat, and cut into thick strips.  
2. **Deep cleaning**: Add salt and flour to the donkey hide strips. Wearing gloves, rub for 5–8 minutes. Rinse repeatedly with clean water until the water is clear and odorless.  
3. **Toast the ingredients**: Toast walnuts over low heat until fragrant, then break into small pieces. Roast black sesame seeds over low heat until cooked. Pit the red dates. Set aside the goji berries.  
4. **Boil to extract gelatin**: Place the cleaned donkey hide strips in a large pot, add enough clean water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for more than 10 hours, until the hide is completely soft and the broth is thick and milky white. Use a slotted spoon to remove the hide residue, and reserve the gelatin liquid.  
5. **Cook the ejiao paste**: Pour the gelatin liquid into a wok. Add yellow rock sugar and yellow wine. Bring to a boil over high heat until the sugar dissolves, then reduce to medium-low heat and stir continuously for 15–25 minutes, until the liquid thickens and forms a continuous ribbon when lifted with the spatula.  
   **Key point**: Heat control and constant stirring are both essential to prevent scorching on the bottom.  
6. **Divide and add ingredients**: Ladle out half of the cooked gelatin liquid into a mold lined with oil paper and let it cool to form an ejiao block. Continue cooking the remaining half over low heat for about 10 minutes, until the liquid drips slowly from the spatula in a triangular shape.  
   **Order of adding ingredients**: First add the red dates and walnuts, stirring quickly to combine. Then add the black sesame seeds and goji berries, stirring vigorously until fully mixed.  
7. **Shape and cool**: Quickly transfer the mixed paste to a baking pan lined with oil paper. Cover with another layer of oil paper and press firmly with your hands or a tool to compact. Refrigerate for 2–3 hours until completely set.  
8. **Slice and store**: Unmold, first cut into thick strips, then slice into pieces about 1 cm thick. Serve at room temperature or store sealed in the freezer.

## Health Advice
This traditional ejiao cake is rich in high-quality collagen and various nuts. Suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter, especially for those with insufficient qi and blood or cold hands and feet. However, due to its high calorie and sugar content, it is recommended to consume only 1–2 slices per day. Diabetic patients should exercise caution.
