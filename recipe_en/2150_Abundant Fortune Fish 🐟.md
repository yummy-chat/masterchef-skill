# Abundant Fortune Fish 🐟
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 Mandarin fish (separated into head, tail, bones, and fillets)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste  
🟢 Asparagus lettuce, to taste  
🟤 Chinese yam, to taste  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, to taste (pre-soaked)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped into green onion pieces)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for washing and marinating fish fillets)  
🧪 Baking soda, a pinch (for washing fish fillets, removing fishy smell and whitening)  
🍶 White vinegar, a pinch (used together with baking soda for washing)  
🌽 Cornstarch/potato starch, to taste (for coating)  
🍶 Cooking wine, a little  
🌫️ White pepper powder, a little  
🫒 Scallion oil, to taste (for marinating and final drizzle)  
🦪 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste (heated for pouring)  
🧂 MSG, a pinch (optional, used during marinating, not explicitly shown in video)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
✂️ Kitchen shears  
🪚 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧻 Kitchen paper/absorbent cloth  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Process the Mandarin Fish**: Remove the head of the mandarin fish. Cut along the backbone to remove the fillets on both sides, keeping the head, bones, and tail intact. Separate the skin from the flesh, and slice the flesh diagonally into thin pieces. Trim the tail into a neat round shape with scissors.  
**Note**: When removing the bones and skin, keep the knife close to the bone and skin to ensure complete meat removal without waste.

2. **Wash the Fish Fillets**: Add a generous amount of salt to the fillets and rub vigorously until sticky. Add baking soda and white vinegar and continue rubbing. Add a little starch and rub again, then rinse thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Pat the fillets completely dry with kitchen paper.  
**Key Point**: Washing must be thorough to remove fishy smell and slime; drying completely is essential for successful coating, otherwise the batter won’t stick.

3. **Make Fortune Cubes**: Peel the carrot, asparagus lettuce, and Chinese yam, and cut them into evenly sized cubes. Use a small knife to carve the character “吉” (fortune) on the surface of each cube: cut into one side to half depth, then turn 90 degrees and cut the other side, repeating to complete.

4. **Initial Blanching**: Blanch the pre-soaked wood ear mushrooms, fortune cubes, fish head, tail, and bones in boiling water until cooked. Remove and drain, set aside.

5. **Marinate and Coat**: Add cooking wine, white pepper powder, salt, and cornstarch to the dried fish fillets and mix well. Finally, drizzle scallion oil and gently mix.  
**Consistency Check**: The fillets should be evenly coated with a thin batter, feeling smooth and tender. The oil seals in moisture and prevents sticking.

6. **Poach the Fish Fillets**: Bring water to a boil in a wok, then reduce to low heat. Add the fillets one by one, and once they change color and float, quickly remove them.  
**Key Temperature Point**: Maintain low heat throughout to avoid vigorous boiling, which can break or toughen the fillets.

7. **Plating**: Layer the blanched wood ear mushrooms and fish bones on the bottom of the plate. Place the fish head and tail at the front and back. Arrange the poached fillets neatly in the center, and decorate the edges with the fortune cubes.

8. **Pour Sauce and Serve**: Sprinkle a generous amount of chopped scallions over the fish fillets. Heat scallion oil in another pan until hot but not smoking, and drizzle evenly over the fillets and scallions. Then heat the steamed fish soy sauce and pour it around the fish along the edge of the plate.  
**Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the scallions, but hot enough to release their aroma; heating the soy sauce enhances its flavor.

## Health Tips  
Mandarin fish is low in fat and high in protein, and the accompanying vegetables are rich in fiber and vitamins, making it suitable for weight control and immune system enhancement. Using baking soda and vinegar to wash the fillets helps remove fishy smell and brighten the color, but rinse thoroughly to avoid any residue affecting the taste.
