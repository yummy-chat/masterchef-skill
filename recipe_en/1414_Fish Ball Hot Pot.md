# Fish Ball Hot Pot
Cuisine Type: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Silver carp 1 (about 4 jin, use the head for soup, the body for fish balls)
🥕 Arrowhead适量 (to be cooked together with the fish head in soup)
🍄 Button mushrooms适量 (sliced and set aside)
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (mashed to make scallion-ginger water)
🧄 Ginger适量 (mashed to make scallion-ginger water; set aside extra ginger slices for frying the fish)
🥚 Egg 1 (use only the egg white)
🌾 Cornstarch a little (mixed with the egg white and added to the fish)
🧂 Salt 5 grams (for seasoning the fish balls) + a few spoonfuls (for seasoning the soup)
🍶 Huangjiu (yellow wine)适量 (pour along the edge of the pan after frying the fish head to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)
🌿 White pepper适量 (dilute with a little fish soup before adding after the soup is cooked)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for frying the fish head and bones, and boiling the soup)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for scooping out fish balls and draining)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing the fish filling)
🍲 Clay pot / Hot pot (for serving the finished dish)
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife (for preparing ingredients)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for weighing ingredients)
🧰 Fine mesh strainer (for straining scallion-ginger water)
✂️ Scissors (for cutting off the fish teeth)

## Steps
1. **Process the fish head**: Use scissors to cut off the teeth area of the fish head, scrub the internal black membrane and blood clots, rinse clean, and drain.
   **Note**: Fish teeth have a strong fishy smell and must be completely removed.
2. **Remove the fish meat**: Rinse the fish meat with clean water several times to remove blood. Using a knife at an angle, scrape the meat off layer by layer until you see the red meat on the skin, then stop.
   **Note**: The scraped fish meat should be fine in texture and free of any residual bones.
3. **Prepare scallion-ginger water**: Mash the scallion and ginger and place them in a bowl. Add 200 grams of clean water, then squeeze the scallion and ginger with your hands to extract the juice. Set aside.
   **Ratio**: 350 grams of water for every 500 grams of fish meat.
4. **Prepare egg white-starch mixture**: Crack 1 egg white into a bowl, add a little cornstarch, and stir thoroughly until well combined.
5. **Mix the fish filling**: Take 350 grams of fish meat, add 5 grams of salt, gradually add 200 grams of the scallion-ginger water in small portions, then add the egg white-starch mixture. Stir by hand continuously in one direction.
   **Note**: If you encounter any fish bones while stirring, remove them promptly.
6. **Knead until sticky**: After the fish meat has absorbed the water, add more scallion-ginger water. Repeat the process of adding water and stirring 3 to 4 times. After adding all 200 grams of water, continue stirring until the fish meat becomes as sticky and thick as lard, so that it only falls off your hand when you shake it forcefully.
   **Note**: The fish meat can absorb up to 1.5 times its weight in water.
7. **Shape the fish balls**: Grab a handful of fish filling and squeeze it out from between your thumb and index finger to form a ball. Use a spoon to scoop it off and drop it into cold water.
   **Note**: Let the fish balls rest in cold water to set their shape first.
8. **Cook the fish balls**: Once all the fish balls are shaped, turn on the heat to low and gently simmer them until they are cooked through. When the fish balls float to the surface, they are done.
   **Note**: Simmering over low heat yields a better texture. After cooking, remove the fish balls and soak them in cold water for later use.
9. **Fry the fish head**: Heat oil in a wok. First, fry the ginger slices briefly until fragrant, then add the fish head. Fry one side until browned, then flip it over.
   **Note**: The fish head must be thoroughly fried to achieve a rich, milky white soup color.
10. **Fry the fish bones**: In a separate pan, heat oil, add ginger slices and the fish bones, and fry until the surface is golden and slightly charred.
    **Note**: If your pan is large enough, you can fry the fish bones together with the fish head.
11. **Boil the fish soup**: After the fish bones are fried, return the fish head to the pan. Pour huangjiu along the edge of the pan to release the aroma, then add clean water. Bring to a boil over high heat and continue boiling for a few minutes until the soup turns a rich, milky white color.
12. **Add the side ingredients**: Add the arrowhead and button mushrooms. Adjust the salt to taste. Dilute a small amount of white pepper with a little fish soup before adding it. Cook the arrowhead for about 5 minutes.
13. **Combine and finish**: Finally, add the fish balls and cook briefly. Sprinkle with chopped scallions, then remove from the heat and serve.
    **Note**: The fish balls are already cooked, so do not boil them for long.

## Health Advice
This fish ball hot pot is rich in high-quality protein and various vitamins. It is suitable for people who need nutritional supplementation or to nourish the spleen and stomach. Note that the fish balls have a relatively high salt content, so those with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation.
