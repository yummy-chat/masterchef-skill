# Thai Brunch 🥘
Cuisine Source: Other 🍴  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
**Main Ingredients**  
🦑 Squid ½ (blanched and sliced)  
🦐 Large shrimp (blanched and halved)  
🥭 Green mango (julienned)  
🍚 Glutinous rice (stir-fried with lemongrass, then ground into powder)  
🌿 Lemongrass 2 stalks (stir-fried with glutinous rice for aroma)  
🍜 Rice noodles  
🥩 Beef short ribs 2 pieces  
🧈 Firm tofu (diced)  
🧅 Asian shallot (partly sliced, partly for base)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🌱 Bean sprouts  
🌿 Cilantro  
🥜 Crushed peanuts  
🍈 Lime ½ (juiced, partly cut into wedges for plate garnish)  
🍵 Tea powder (for Thai milk tea)  
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk/evaporated milk  

**Seasonings**  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili 2–3 pieces  
🌶️ Chili flakes  
🧂 Fish sauce  
🍬 Sugar  
🧂 Salt  
⚫ Black pepper  
🧃 Tamarind paste (made by soaking tamarind until soft, straining, then mixing with sugar and fish sauce)  

## Equipment & Tools  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥣 Mortar and pestle  
🔄 Blender/food processor  
🥘 Stockpot  
🍳 Cast iron skillet  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫗 Fine mesh strainer  
🍴 Knife, cutting board  
🫙 Bowls, cups, plates  

## Instructions  
1. Place glutinous rice and lemongrass in a dry frying pan over medium-low heat. Stir constantly until the rice turns golden and fragrant. Let cool, then grind into a coarse powder using a blender.  
   **Note**: If galangal and kaffir lime leaves are unavailable at home, lemongrass alone is sufficient.

2. Soak tamarind in boiling water until soft. Mash with your hands, then strain through a sieve to extract the pulp. Mix in sugar and fish sauce to taste.  
   **Note**: Even if using seedless tamarind, straining is recommended for a smooth sauce.

3. Blanch large shrimp and squid in boiling water until just cooked. Immediately transfer to an ice bath to cool, giving them a firmer, springier texture.

4. Slice Asian shallot, dice firm tofu, julienne green mango, and cut lime into wedges. Set aside.

5. Place some ground glutinous rice powder in a mortar. Add bird's eye chili, cilantro, sliced Asian shallot, sugar, and lime juice. Pound with a pestle until the mixture is broken down and aromatic, with all flavors well combined.

6. Add julienned green mango, shrimp, and squid. Drizzle in fish sauce and gently toss to combine. Adjust flavor by adding more rice powder or chili flakes as desired.

7. Heat oil in a pan. Pan-fry the tofu cubes over medium heat until golden on all sides. Add sliced Asian shallot and stir-fry briefly, then crack in one egg and scramble.

8. Add rice noodles and bean sprouts. Drizzle in tamarind paste and fish sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the noodles are evenly coated and nicely colored.

9. Heat a cast iron skillet until very hot. Place the beef short ribs on the skillet. Season both sides with salt and black pepper. Cook to your preferred doneness.

10. Plate the stir-fried noodles. Top with the steak, lime wedges, crushed peanuts, and chili flakes. Raw bean sprouts can be served on the side. If concerned about safety, quickly blanch or toss them in the residual heat of the pan.

11. Brew strong tea by steeping tea powder or leaves in hot water. Stir in sweetened condensed milk or evaporated milk until well combined. Let cool completely.

12. Fill a glass with ice cubes, then pour in the Thai iced tea.

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in protein. The green mango and lemongrass aid digestion, making it ideal for summer. Be mindful of portion sizes for rice noodles and glutinous rice to avoid excessive carbohydrates.
