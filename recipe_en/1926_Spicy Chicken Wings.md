# Spicy Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken wings, about 15 pieces  
🌶️ Dried chilies, 1 bowl (large amount, pre-washed with water)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (white parts and green parts separated)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (julienned)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (sliced)  
🌿 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, a small handful  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating)  
🍶 Light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🍾 Cooking wine, 1 tablespoon  
🌿 White pepper, as needed  
🌽 Cornstarch, 1 heaping tablespoon (or other starch)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, 1 tablespoon (for coating)  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, as needed  
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large mixing bowl  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks (to test oil temperature)  

## Steps  
1. Rinse chicken wings and place them in a large bowl. Add salt, light soy sauce, cooking wine, and white pepper. Mix well with your hands until sticky, then let marinate.  
**Note**: Add liquid and powder seasonings first, mix well, then coat with starch.  

2. Add cornstarch and stir in one direction to evenly coat the wings with the starch paste. Finally, drizzle with vegetable oil and mix well. This prevents the coating from falling off during frying and gives a shinier color.  
3. Wash dried chilies with water and drain. Julienne ginger, slice garlic, cut scallion whites into sections, and scallion greens into sections. Set aside.  
4. Pour oil into the wok and heat over medium heat until it reaches 50-60% hot. Test with chopsticks: rapid, dense bubbles should form around them. Fry chicken wings for about 2 minutes, letting them set before turning. Fry until golden brown and oil bubbles become small, then remove.  
**Key point**: This oil temperature quickly solidifies the starch surface, preventing sticking.  

5. Over high heat, raise the oil temperature to 70% hot. Quickly re-fry the chicken wings until the skin deepens in color and becomes crispy. Remove and drain oil.  
**Key point**: High-temperature re-frying forces out excess oil, making the skin crispier and less greasy.  

6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over medium heat, add ginger, scallion whites, garlic, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and a large amount of dried chilies. Stir-fry until the spicy and numbing aroma is released.  
**Note**: Pre-washing the dried chilies prevents them from turning black and becoming bitter when stir-fried.  

7. Add the fried chicken wings, then thirteen-spice powder and chili powder. Stir-fry quickly over medium heat until well combined.  
**Note**: The chicken wings were already seasoned during marinating, so no additional salt is needed.  

8. Just before serving, sprinkle in scallion greens. Stir-fry a few times quickly, then immediately plate.  

## Health Advice  
Spicy chicken wings are high in oil and spice, suitable for occasional indulgence. Those with cardiovascular sensitivities or during periods of internal heat should reduce intake. Pair with cold cucumber salad or plain congee to balance the heat.
