# Crispy Fish Skin Noodle Soup with Yellow Croaker  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 yellow croaker (deboned, fish meat reserved, bones reserved)  
🍜 Appropriate amount of Fujian thin noodles  
🌿 Appropriate amount of celery (finely chopped)  
🥬 Appropriate amount of pea shoots  
🧅 Appropriate amount of ginger (some sliced for frying fish bones, some shredded for marinating fish)  
🌾 250g low-gluten flour  
🥔 75g cornstarch  
🍥 15g baking powder  
💧 425g water (for batter)  
🫒 250g oil (for batter)  

🍶 Appropriate amount of Fujian old wine (or cooking wine)  
🥢 Appropriate amount of light soy sauce  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of white pepper powder  
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt  
🧂 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon  
🌿 Appropriate amount of white pepper powder  
🫒 Appropriate amount of oil (for frying fish bones and fish fillet)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍲 Pot  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Finely chop the celery; debone the yellow croaker and reserve the bones; slice the fish meat, add Fujian old wine, light soy sauce, white pepper powder, a pinch of salt, chicken bouillon, and shredded ginger, mix well, and marinate for a while.  
2. Make the batter: Mix 250g low-gluten flour, 75g cornstarch, 15g baking powder, 425g water, and 250g oil, stirring until smooth and lump-free. Set aside.  
3. Make the fish soup: Heat oil in a wok, add the fish bones and ginger slices, fry until both sides are golden brown, pour in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, and continue boiling over high heat until the soup turns milky white.  
   **Key point**: Using cold water and boiling over high heat are essential for a milky white broth. After cooking, crush the fish bones in the soup with a slotted spoon to enhance the sweetness, then strain out the fish soup.  
4. Fry the fish fillet: Coat the marinated fish meat evenly in the batter, heat oil to 60-70% hot, fry over low heat until golden and crispy, then remove and drain.  
5. In another pot, bring water to a boil, add the thin noodles, stir with chopsticks, cook until soft, then immediately remove. Quickly blanch the pea shoots in boiling water and remove.  
6. Bring the strained fish soup to a boil, add an appropriate amount of salt and white pepper powder to season.  
7. Place the cooked noodles in a bowl, arrange the pea shoots on top, sprinkle with chopped celery, pour the boiling fish soup over to loosen the noodles, then top with the fried fish fillet.  

## Health Tips  
Yellow croaker soup with thin noodles is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for busy individuals to replenish energy. However, the batter-fried fish fillet is high in calories, so it is recommended to balance it with light vegetables. Note that the thin noodles are easily overcooked, so quick handling is required during cooking.
