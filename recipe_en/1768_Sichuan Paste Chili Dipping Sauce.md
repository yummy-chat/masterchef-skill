# Sichuan Paste Chili Dipping Sauce  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (cut into sections)  
🌱 Scallions, to taste (chopped)  
🥬 Houttuynia cordata (fish mint), to taste (diced)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, to taste (sliced into rings)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🥄 Soy sauce/light soy sauce, to taste  
🍶 Vinegar, to taste (a tiny bit)  
💧 Boiled water or vegetable cooking broth, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥣 Mortar (duiwo) and pestle  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥢 Bowl, chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the iron wok over heat without oil, warm on low flame. Add dried chili segments and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry continuously until the color darkens and a smoky chili aroma is released.  
**Note**: Must dry-fry without oil, keep the heat low to avoid burning and bitterness.  
2. Pour the stir-fried dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns into the mortar, pound with the pestle into coarse chili flakes.  
3. In a bowl, add the pounded chili flakes, then successively add salt, MSG, minced garlic, cilantro sections, chopped scallions, diced houttuynia cordata, and bird's eye chili rings.  
4. Pour in soy sauce, a small amount of vinegar, and an appropriate amount of boiled water or vegetable cooking broth. Stir well and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This paste chili dipping sauce is high in sodium and spicy, suitable for stimulating appetite but should not be consumed excessively. Those with sensitive stomachs should proceed with caution. It is rich in vitamin C and antioxidant components, making it a classic dipping sauce in Sichuan cuisine.
