# Cheese Baked Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍅 Tomato, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)
🧅 Purple onion, about 1/3 (cut into small cubes)
🌽 Assorted frozen vegetables (corn, peas, carrots, etc.), appropriate amount
🍚 Leftover rice, appropriate amount
🥚 Egg, 1
🍖 Ham, appropriate amount (cut into cubes)
🧀 Mozzarella cheese, sufficient amount (to cover the surface)
🧂 Salt, 1 spoon
🍅 Ketchup, appropriate amount (squeezed in a grid pattern)
🍯 Honey, a few spoons
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for greasing the pan)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Brush

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the tomato, purple onion, and ham into small cubes respectively, and set aside.
2. In a large glass bowl, add the diced onion, diced tomato, assorted frozen vegetables, and leftover rice. Crack in the egg, and add one spoon of salt.
3. Thoroughly mix by hand until the rice is loose and well combined with the other ingredients.
4. Place the frying pan on the stove, pour in a small amount of cooking oil, and spread it thinly with a brush.
5. Evenly spread the mixed rice into the frying pan and press it flat.
6. Squeeze ketchup in a grid pattern over the rice surface, then drizzle with a few spoons of honey.
7. Sprinkle the diced ham evenly on top, then cover fully with a sufficient amount of mozzarella cheese shreds over the entire pan surface.
8. Cover with a lid and cook over low heat.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning the bottom.
9. Observe through the lid. Once the cheese is fully melted and bubbling, turn off the heat and remove from the pan.

## Health Advice
Cheese baked rice incorporates vegetables and eggs to enhance nutrition, but cheese and honey are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to control portion sizes and enjoy it as an occasional main dish. Note that ham and ketchup contain sodium; those with high blood pressure should consume sparingly.
