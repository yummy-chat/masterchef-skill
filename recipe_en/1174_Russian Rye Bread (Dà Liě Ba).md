# Russian Rye Bread (Dà Liě Ba)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 High-gluten flour 560g
🌾 Low-gluten flour 140g
🥛 Milk powder 14g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 90g
🟤 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerant) 7g
🥛 Milk 286g (room temperature)
🥚 Eggs 2
🧈 Unsalted butter 90g (softened)
🧂 Salt 8g
🥜 Walnuts approx. 80g (chopped)
🍇 Raisins approx. 40g
🍇 Dried cranberries approx. 40g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Measuring cup (with markings)
🍴 Fork
🔪 Dough scraper
🥖 Rolling pin
📏 Ruler
🪤 Baking tray (non-stick)
🧻 Baking paper (parchment paper)
♨️ Proving drawer (or oven with proving function)
🖌️ Baking brush
🔪 Lame (sharp)
🔥 Oven
🧤 Oven mitts
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔗 Wire rack/cooling rack

## Steps
1. **Prepare the liquid mixture**: In a measuring cup, whisk 2 eggs until broken up. Add 286g of room temperature milk and mix well. Then add 90g of fine sugar and 7g of instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerant), stirring until completely dissolved.
   **Key point**: Mixing the liquid ingredients evenly first reduces difficulty during subsequent hand kneading.

2. **Prepare dry ingredients**: On the work surface, mix 560g of high-gluten flour, 140g of low-gluten flour, and 14g of milk powder evenly.
   **Key point**: Adding low-gluten flour improves the mouthfeel of the bread and makes hand kneading less strenuous.

3. **Mix into a dough**: Create a well in the center of the dry flour pile and pour in the mixed liquid. Use a dough scraper to gradually scrape the surrounding flour into the liquid, mixing until it becomes flaky with no visible dry flour. Use your hands to press it into a dough.
   **Key point**: Working on a surface without boundaries is more suitable for handling this recipe's relatively dry dough.

4. **Add butter and salt and knead**: Add 8g of salt and 90g of cubed softened unsalted butter to the initially formed dough. Use your hands to repeatedly pinch, squeeze, and knead until the butter and salt are fully incorporated into the dough.
   **Key point**: The large amount of butter provides a rich creamy aroma and moisture retention. The purpose of kneading is just to incorporate evenly; it doesn't need to be perfectly smooth.

5. **First rest**: Gather the kneaded dough into a simple ball, cover with a damp cloth, and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: It must be covered with a damp cloth or plastic wrap to retain moisture and prevent the surface from drying out.

6. **Knead until smooth**: After resting, the dough is easier to knead. You can repeat the "cut-gather-knead" method several times to make the dough more uniform.
   **Key point**: If using a stand mixer for this step, knead until the dough reaches about 70-80% gluten development.

7. **Divide and pre-shape**: Divide the dough into two equal portions. Knead and smooth each portion of dough, shaping it into a smooth elongated log.
   **Key point**: Larger dough pieces may result in a softer final product.

8. **Second rest**: Cover the pre-shaped dough logs with a damp cloth and rest again for 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: The resting time must be sufficient, otherwise the dough will shrink back when rolled out later.

9. **Roll out the dough**: Take one rested dough log, flatten it slightly, and use a rolling pin to roll it into a rectangular sheet, approximately 40cm long and 25cm wide.
   **Key point**: If the dough sheet shrinks back significantly, cover with a damp cloth and rest for another 5-10 minutes.

10. **Add filling and roll up**: Evenly spread the chopped walnuts, raisins, and dried cranberries over the dough sheet. Gently press them down. Then, starting from the top, tightly roll the dough down towards you. Give the roll a squeeze after each complete turn. Once rolled, place it seam-side down and squeeze the whole log again firmly.
    **Key point**: Just wash and dry the dried fruits; do not soak them to avoid creating gaps. The roll must be tight to minimize air pockets.

11. **Final proofing**: Place the shaped bread logs seam-side down on the baking tray, leaving space for expansion. Put them in a proving drawer or oven set to 30°C (86°F) and 75% humidity. Proof for about 40 minutes, until the dough has noticeably increased in size, about 1.5 times.
    **Key point**: Do not over-proof, as it will affect the texture.

12. **Decoration and baking**: Remove the proofed bread logs. Brush the surface evenly with three layers of whole egg wash. Using a sharp lame, score the surface with evenly spaced diagonal slashes. Bake in a preheated oven. For a home convection oven: top and bottom heat at 170°C (338°F) for about 45 minutes. For a fan-forced oven: 155°C (311°F) for about 40 minutes. Bake until the crust is a deep golden brown and tapping the bottom produces a hollow sound.
    **Key point**: Brushing multiple layers of egg wash increases the shine. You can rotate the baking tray halfway through for even browning. This bread needs a low and slow bake to ensure the inside is cooked through.

## Health Advice
Dà Liě Ba is rich in oil and sugar, combined with nuts and dried fruits, making it quite energy-dense. It is suitable as a breakfast or post-workout snack, but portion control is important. It provides dietary fiber and vitamin E, which are beneficial for gut health and act as antioxidants. However, people with diabetes or those on a weight loss diet should consume it in moderation.
