# Marseille Fish Soup (Bouillabaisse)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Sea bass 450g (boned and cut into pieces)
🐟 Mixed fish assortment 250g (heads, tails, and small fish for broth; middle sections can be reserved)
🦐 Mantis shrimp 6 pieces (for broth)
🦪 Mussels 6 pieces (washed, live)
🥬 Fennel bulb 200g (cut into sections, core flavor, do not omit)
🧅 Onion 100g (cut into sections)
🥬 Celery 150g (cut into sections)
🍅 Tomato 200g (about 2 pieces, peeled and cut into chunks)
🥔 Potato 100g (peeled and cut into large chunks)
🧄 Garlic cloves 8 cloves (5 cloves for broth, 3 cloves for garlic sauce, minced)
🍞 White toast 1 slice (cut into cubes, for making garlic aioli)
🌿 Parsley flakes to taste (for garnish)
🫒 Olive oil to taste (for sautéing broth and emulsifying sauce, add sauce in batches)
🍷 White wine 50ml
🥫 Tomato paste 80g
💧 Mineral water 1500ml
🧂 Sea salt to taste
🧂 Black pepper to taste
🌿 Thyme 2 sprigs
🍃 Bay leaves 2 pieces
🌸 Saffron 1 small pinch (for broth) + 1 small pinch (for sauce, essential)
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 1 piece (replaces traditional dried orange peel)
🍶 White wine vinegar 10ml (for soaking saffron)
🌶️ Paprika 1/2 teaspoon
🥚 Raw egg yolk 1 piece
💧 Water 10ml (for making sauce)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife, kitchen scissors (for handling fish and cutting vegetables)
🍳 Deep frying pan with lid (for sautéing and initial simmering)
🍲 Stockpot (for simmering broth for over 1 hour)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for retrieving potato chunks)
🌀 Blender/Food processor (for pureeing solid soup ingredients)
🥄 Fine-mesh strainer (for straining soup solids for a silky broth)
🥣 Glass bowl (for making garlic aioli)
🥄 Hand whisk (for emulsifying sauce)
🥣 Small bowl or measuring cup (for soaking saffron, etc.)

## Preparation Steps
1. Heat olive oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Add onion, fennel, celery, and some minced garlic. Sauté over medium-low heat until vegetables are soft. Then add the fish bones, heads, tails, and mantis shrimp intended for the broth. Stir-fry until slightly golden and a caramelized aroma develops.
   **Key point**: Thoroughly caramelizing the ingredients creates a rich, layered flavor for the broth.

2. Add tomato paste, tomato chunks, thyme, bay leaves, dried tangerine peel, and a small pinch of saffron. Stir to combine well.

3. Pour in white wine and stir briefly. Immediately add 1500ml mineral water and sea salt. Transfer all ingredients to the stockpot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer covered for at least 1 hour. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavor.

4. In a small bowl, soak a small pinch of saffron in white wine vinegar. In a glass bowl, combine bread cubes, paprika, minced garlic, and 10ml water. Stir to let the bread cubes fully absorb the liquid and soften. Add raw egg yolk and whisk to combine. Slowly drizzle in olive oil in batches while continuously whisking until the sauce thickens and emulsifies into a mayonnaise-like consistency. Finally, season with salt.
   **Note**: Olive oil must be added slowly in batches, or the sauce may separate.

5. Scoop the solid ingredients from the simmered broth into a blender. Add a small amount of the broth and blend into a smooth paste. Strain the paste through a fine-mesh strainer back into the pot, retaining only the clear, orange-red broth.
   **Key point**: Straining is essential for achieving a silky texture; do not skip this step.

6. Bring the strained broth to a boil. Add potato chunks and sprinkle in a little more saffron. Cook until the potatoes are easily mashed with a spoon. Then add the reserved fish pieces and mussels. Boil for a few minutes until the fish is cooked through and white, and the mussels have opened.
   **Note**: Do not overcook the fish to keep it tender.

7. Place the fish, mussels, and potato chunks into deep bowls. Ladle the hot broth over them. Sprinkle with parsley flakes. Traditionally, serve with toasted baguette slices: rub the bread surface with a garlic clove, spread with garlic aioli, then dip the bread into the soup before eating.

## Health Advice
Marseille fish soup is rich in high-quality protein and omega-3 fatty acids, making it suitable for those needing cardiovascular support or anti-inflammatory benefits. Ensure mussels and fish are thoroughly cooked. Individuals with gout should consume in moderation.
