# Mango Pancake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 230ml whole milk
🍬 45g sugar (25g for batter, 20g for whipped cream)
🌾 50g cake flour
🌽 30g cornstarch
🥚 4 eggs (about 150g)
🧈 10g butter (or cooking oil, melted before use)
🥛 300g heavy cream (chill for at least 3 hours in advance)
🥭 About 2 mangoes (take the flesh and cut into chunks)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Hand whisk
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🌀 Electric mixer
🥄 Spatula
🍽️ Plate
🫗 Strainer (sieve)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the batter**: Pour 230ml whole milk into a bowl, add 25g sugar, 50g cake flour, and 30g cornstarch. Use a hand whisk to stir in a "Z" motion until no dry flour remains. Then add 4 eggs, continue mixing until the eggs and batter are fully combined. Finally, pour in 10g melted butter and stir until smooth and lump-free.
2. **Strain**: Pass the batter through a strainer into another bowl to remove any lumps, ensuring a fine texture for the pancake skin.
3. **Whip the cream**: Take 300g well-chilled heavy cream, add 20g sugar, and use an electric mixer on high speed to whip for about 1 minute until distinct peaks form—when lifting the beater, the cream should stand in firm, stiff peaks.
4. **Cook the pancake skins**: Heat a non-stick pan over the lowest flame. When the pan feels warm at a hand’s distance, ladle about 60-70g of batter into the pan. Quickly swirl the pan to spread it evenly into a thin round layer. Keep the heat low and cook until the batter sets and large bubbles rise in the center. Use a spatula to loosen the edges and remove the skin, placing it on a plate to cool.
   - **Note**: The pan should not be too hot, or the batter will set before spreading evenly. Let each skin cool completely before stacking to prevent sticking.
5. **Assemble the pancakes**: Take a cooled pancake skin and place it smooth side down. Spoon a generous amount of whipped cream in the center, then add mango chunks. Fold the left and right sides of the skin over the filling, then fold in the top and bottom to form a square. Place it seam side down.
6. The assembled mango pancakes can be refrigerated for 2 hours to set. After chilling, the skin is tender and the filling is cool, enhancing the texture.

## Health Advice
Mango pancakes are rich in vitamin C and high-quality protein, making them suitable as an afternoon tea dessert. However, they are high in calories, so it’s recommended to eat 1-2 pieces at a time. Those allergic to mangoes should be cautious, and people with high blood sugar can reduce the sugar amount as needed.
