# Stir-Fried Pork Kidney with Cucumber
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Cucumber about 1-2 pieces (choose prickly regular cucumbers, peel in strips, remove core, slice diagonally into thick pieces)
🐷 Pork kidney about 2-3 pieces (choose fresh kidneys with surface membrane, remove membrane, eliminate the ureter and gland, score in a crisscross pattern)
🫚 Ginger 2-3 slices (for aromatics)
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (minced)
🌶️ Wild pepper (pickled chili) 5-6 pieces (chopped, provides sour and spicy flavor)
🫑 Red bell pepper a small amount (sliced, for color)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (used twice: once for scrubbing pork kidney to remove odor, once for marinating cucumber to draw out water and season)
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount (pour along the edge of the wok when stir-frying pork kidney to remove odor and enhance flavor)
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount (add when stir-frying pork kidney to enhance freshness and color)
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (add when stir-frying pork kidney to provide salty and umami flavor)
🍬 Sugar a small amount (added separately when stir-frying pork kidney and cucumber to balance taste and enhance freshness)
🌶️ White pepper powder a small amount (sprinkle in during final mixing to remove odor and enhance flavor)
🥣 Water starch appropriate amount (pour in before removing from wok to thicken into a light sauce)
🫒 Clarified oil (cooked oil) a small amount (pour in before removing from wok to add sheen)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪚 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥢 Strainer
🥢 Chopsticks
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🥣 Bowl
🥣 Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the surface membrane of the pork kidney, cut in half lengthwise, thoroughly remove the white ureter and gland in the center, then score the surface in a wheat-ear pattern.
**Key point**: The ureter and gland must be completely removed; this is the main source of gamey odor. Scoring helps the kidney cook quickly over high heat and maintain an attractive shape.

2. Add salt to the processed pork kidney, rub and mix with hands to marinate for 1-2 minutes, then rinse repeatedly with clean water until the water runs clear. Thoroughly pat dry the surface with kitchen paper towels.
**Key point**: Scrubbing with salt is essential for removing odor. The water must be completely dried to achieve a fragrant stir-fry without oil splattering.

3. Use a peeler to peel the cucumber in strips, creating a "patterned" look. Cut in half, remove the seeds and core, then slice diagonally into thick pieces.
**Key point**: Removing the core and peeling in strips keeps the cucumber crunchy and prevents it from releasing too much water during stir-frying.

4. Place the cucumber slices in a bowl, add salt, rub to mix, and marinate for about 5 minutes. Drain the released liquid.
5. Heat the wok with cold oil, add ginger slices and minced garlic to release fragrance. Add the dried pork kidney and stir-fry quickly over high heat. Pour cooking wine along the edge of the wok, add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and a little sugar. Continue stir-frying for about 1.5 minutes, until the kidney curls, changes color, and is about 80% cooked. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Maintain high heat and fast stir-frying throughout. Total cooking time should be around 1.5 minutes to ensure the kidney stays tender; avoid overcooking.

6. Heat oil in another wok, add chopped wild pepper to release fragrance. Add the marinated cucumber slices and red bell pepper slices. Stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute until the cucumber is just cooked but still crunchy.
**Key point**: The cucumber is already marinated and cooks quickly, so stir-fry time should be short to preserve its crispness.

7. Return the pork kidney to the wok, stir-fry quickly to combine evenly, and sprinkle in a little white pepper powder.
8. Pour in water starch to thicken into a light sauce, stir-fry quickly to coat the ingredients. Before removing from the wok, add a little clarified oil, stir briefly, and plate.

## Health Notes
Pork kidney is rich in iron and protein, low in fat and high in protein, helping to replenish blood and enhance physical strength. Cucumber is high in water and low in calories, making it ideal for cooling and relieving heat in summer. Those with poor kidney function should consume pork kidney in moderation. Be sure to thoroughly remove the ureter and gland, and control cooking time to maintain tenderness.
