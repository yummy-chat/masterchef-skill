# Water-Stretched Noodles  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 High-gluten or all-purpose flour 300g  
🥩 Lean beef 200g (sliced)  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount  
🫑 Green bell pepper appropriate amount  
🍅 Tomato appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)  
🧂 Salt 3g (for dough)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp (for marinating) + 2 tbsp (for stir-frying)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce a pinch (for marinating) + 1 tbsp (for stir-frying)  
🥄 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp (for marinating) + 1 tbsp (for stir-frying)  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for marinating)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for marinating and stir-frying)  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder 1 tsp  
🍲 Chicken bouillon 1 tsp  
💧 Cold water 170g (for dough) + appropriate amount (for soaking dough pieces and cooling noodles)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Pot for boiling  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Large basin (for cooling noodles in cold water)  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. In a mixing bowl, combine 300g flour and 3g salt. Gradually add 170g cold water, stir with chopsticks into flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth dough.  
   - Adding salt enhances gluten toughness. Add water gradually. The dough must be kneaded until smooth, which is key to preventing the noodles from breaking when stretched.  
2. Roll the dough into a long log, cut into evenly sized small pieces, flatten each piece, and soak in cold water for 20 minutes.  
   - Soak thoroughly in cold water to make the dough soft and highly elastic.  
3. Slice the beef, add 1 tbsp light soy sauce, a pinch of dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, and an appropriate amount of cornstarch. Mix well, then drizzle a little cooking oil and mix again. Marinate for 10 minutes.  
   - The oil locks in moisture and prevents sticking when cooking.  
4. Cut the onion, green bell pepper, and tomato into chunks or slices. Prepare chopped scallion and minced garlic.  

### Boiling Noodles & Stir-Frying  
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Remove the soaked dough pieces, hold each by both ends, and evenly stretch into wide noodles. Boil over high heat for about 3 minutes until the noodles float and change color, indicating they are cooked through.  
   - Immediately remove and cool in cold water, then drain. This gives the noodles a chewier texture.  
2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat until 70% hot. Add the beef slices and quickly stir-fry until they change color. Immediately remove from the wok.  
   - Beef must be stir-fried quickly over high heat and removed as soon as it changes color to prevent toughness.  
3. Leave a little oil in the wok. Sauté scallion and garlic until fragrant. Add onion, green bell pepper, and tomato, and stir-fry until just cooked.  
4. Add 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, 1 tsp thirteen-spice powder, and 1 tsp chicken bouillon. Stir well to combine.  
5. Return the cooked beef and drained noodles to the wok. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the sauce evenly coats every noodle and ingredient. Serve immediately.  
   - Final stir-fry must be done quickly over high heat to fully meld the flavors.  

## Health Tips  
This dish of water-stretched noodles with lean beef and various vegetables provides balanced protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those managing weight or building muscle. Note that the beef should be stir-fried quickly over high heat to stay tender, and cooling the noodles in cold water gives them a chewier texture.
