# Sichuan Pepper-Scallion Hand-Torn Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 Sanhuang chicken (whole, about 1.2-1.5kg)
🧅 Onion, to taste (julienned)
🫑 Yellow bell pepper, to taste (julienned)
🌶️ Red bell pepper, to taste (julienned)
🫑 Green bell pepper, to taste (julienned)

🧅 Scallion, to taste (for cooking chicken and making oil)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste (used separately for cooking and seasoning chicken)
🌿 Angelica dahurica, a small amount
🌿 Fennel seeds, a small amount (shared for cooking chicken and making oil)
🌸 Gardenia fruit, a small amount
🌶️ Green and red Sichuan peppercorns (mixed), a small amount
🌶️ Bullethead chili peppers, a small amount
🌶️ Xin'erdai chili peppers, a small amount
🌿 Cinnamon stick, to taste
🧂 Dried Sichuan peppercorns (green), to taste
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste
🍲 Chicken broth, 2 tablespoons (reserved from cooking)
🧂 Ground white pepper, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste
🌶️ Sichuan pepper oil, a small amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok (for cooking chicken and making oil)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for cooling, tearing, and mixing chicken)
🥄 Fine-mesh strainer (for filtering Sichuan pepper oil)
🥄 Regular spoon (for adding seasonings)
🥢 Chopsticks/hands (for mixing well)

## Instructions
1. Wash the whole chicken and place it in a pot with cold water. Add scallion, ginger, cooking wine, salt, as well as angelica dahurica, fennel seeds, gardenia fruit, green and red Sichuan peppercorns, bullethead chili peppers, and Xin'erdai chili peppers. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and cook for about 40 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part of the drumstick with no blood emerging.
*Note: Must start in cold water; the spices help color the chicken and add complex aroma.*
2. Immediately remove the cooked chicken and immerse it in a prepared ice water bath to cool, allowing the skin to tighten and become more Q-springy in texture.
3. Prepare Sichuan pepper oil: Pour more oil into the wok and heat. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, cinnamon stick, fennel seeds, and bay leaves. Sauté over low heat until fragrant, then remove the solids. Next, add dried Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili peppers, continuing to cook over low heat until fragrant. Add 2 tablespoons of chicken broth and 1 tablespoon of ground white pepper. Simmer over the lowest heat for 7-8 minutes. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer to obtain the pure oil mixture, i.e., Sichuan pepper oil. Set aside a few dried chili peppers for garnish if desired.
*Key: Cook over low heat throughout to avoid burning the Sichuan peppercorns and chili peppers, which would cause bitterness.*
4. Once the chicken is completely cooled, use your hands to tear the meat and skin into thin strips or small pieces. Do not use a knife.
5. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white sugar, and light soy sauce to the torn chicken and mix evenly first. Then add julienned onion, yellow bell pepper, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper. Drizzle generously with the homemade Sichuan pepper oil and a small amount of Sichuan pepper oil, and toss thoroughly until well combined. Plate and serve.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in high-quality protein, low in fat, and high in vitamins, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those wanting a satisfying yet calorie-controlled meal. The Sichuan pepper oil is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to control the amount used to avoid extra burden.
