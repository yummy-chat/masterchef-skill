# Brandy Baked Giant Freshwater Prawns
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Giant freshwater prawns about half a catty (cut off legs and antennae, slit the belly)  
🧅 Shallots to taste (some finely chopped, some halved)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (some finely chopped, some whole and blanched)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (finely chopped)  
🧅 Spring onion to taste (finely chopped)  

🍾 Brandy 2 tablespoons  
🍷 Dry white wine 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🧂 Chicken powder to taste  
🥫 Light soy sauce to taste  
🥫 Oyster sauce to taste  
⚪ White pepper powder to taste  
💧 Water half a bowl  
🫒 Cooking oil to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
✂️ Scissors  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Glass bowl/seasoning bowl  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. Prepare the prawns: Use scissors to cut off the legs and antennae. Be sure to cut a slit along the belly for flavor absorption and even cooking; do not cut through the shell, otherwise the prawns will burst and deform during cooking.
2. Blanch the garlic: Place the whole garlic cloves in a slotted spoon, blanch briefly in boiling water, then drain to remove raw bitterness and release sweetness.
3. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, mix brandy, salt, chicken powder, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper powder, and half a bowl of water. Stir well, then add the chopped ginger, shallot, and garlic.
4. Marinate the prawns: Add the prepared prawns to the marinade, toss thoroughly to coat, and let marinate for 5 minutes.
5. Sauté in the clay pot: Heat the clay pot over medium-low heat, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, then add the halved shallots and blanched whole garlic cloves. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Arrange and bake: Place the prawns evenly over the garlic and shallots, pour the remaining marinade from the bowl over them, cover with the lid, and cook over medium heat for about 3 minutes, until the sauce is mostly reduced and the prawns are fully red.
7. Garnish and serve: Turn off the heat, immediately sprinkle with chopped spring onions and shallot shreds, and let the residual heat of the clay pot release their aroma before serving.

## Health Advice  
This brandy-baked prawn dish is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for fitness or blood sugar-conscious individuals. However, the oyster sauce and chicken powder in the marinade are high in sodium; those sensitive to blood pressure should limit consumption of the sauce.
