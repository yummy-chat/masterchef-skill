# Osmanthus Yam Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Chinese yam (iron stick yam) 3 pieces
🍚 Glutinous rice flour appropriate amount (pre-roasted over low heat until cooked)
🫘 Red bean paste appropriate amount
🍊 Orange appropriate amount (sliced as base)
🍯 Osmanthus honey appropriate amount (for final drizzle)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife
🍽️ Plate
🥄 Spoon
🥣 Glass bowl
🧁 Round pastry mold (rose shape)
🍽️ Dark stone slab or plate (for serving)

## Instructions
1. Wash and peel the yam, cut into long sections, and steam in a steamer until fully cooked.
   **Key Point**: Steaming time is about 20 minutes, adjust based on the thickness of the yam.
2. Place the steamed yam on a plate and mash it into a smooth, fine paste using a spoon.
3. Heat a dry, oil-free pan, add glutinous rice flour, and stir continuously over low heat until it turns slightly yellow and releases a rice fragrance. Remove from heat and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Stir constantly during the process to prevent local burning.
4. Gradually add the roasted glutinous rice flour to the yam paste, knead by hand into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
   **Key Point**: Adjust the amount of glutinous rice flour based on the moisture of the yam paste, just enough to form a dough that does not stick.
5. Dust the inside of the pastry mold with a little roasted glutinous rice flour to prevent sticking. Fill the mold with a small piece of dough, press firmly, and release to form a rose-shaped cake.
6. Slice the orange into thin rounds and arrange them on a plate. Spread a thin layer of red bean paste evenly over each orange slice, then gently press the cake onto the bean paste to secure it.
7. Drizzle osmanthus honey evenly over the cake surface just before serving.
   **Key Point**: Add osmanthus honey right before serving to avoid prolonged soaking, which can affect the aroma and texture.

## Health Suggestions
Osmanthus Yam Cake uses yam as the main ingredient, which nourishes the spleen and stomach, making it suitable for those with weak digestion or those seeking a low-fat dessert. Note that although the glutinous rice flour is pre-roasted, if you need to control sugar intake, reduce the amount of osmanthus honey.
