# Dry-Braised Giant Prawns

Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Giant prawns, about 500g, trimmed of whiskers, pincers, and legs, with the sand sac and vein removed, and the back split open

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥄 Slotted spoon or ladle  
🍽️ Plate  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Use scissors to trim the prawns’ whiskers, pincers, and legs. Remove the sand sac from the head area. Cut open the back at the joint between the head and body, remove the vein, rinse, and drain.  
2. Pour cold oil into the wok, lay the prepared prawns flat in the wok, and heat over medium heat.  
**Key point**: Use cold oil to prevent the exterior from burning while the interior remains raw.  
3. Pan-fry until both sides of the prawns are fully red.  
4. Add hot water to the wok, then immediately drizzle in the Huadiao wine.  
**Key point**: Add water before wine to avoid a sudden temperature drop in the wok, allowing the wine aroma to blend better.  
5. Add salt, white sugar, and ground white pepper. Stir well with a ladle. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover the wok and reduce to medium-low heat. Braise for about two and a half minutes until the prawns are fully red and curled.  
6. Remove the braised prawns with chopsticks and arrange them neatly on a plate. Leave the residual sauce and the ginger and scallion shreds in the wok. Increase the heat to reduce the sauce until it is slightly thickened, then drizzle in the sesame oil.  
7. Pour the thickened sauce, along with the ginger and scallion shreds, evenly over the prawns. Place a sprig of cilantro in the center as garnish.  

## Health Tips  
This dish of dry-braised giant prawns is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, helping to replenish energy. However, the sugar content is relatively high; those controlling blood sugar or body weight are advised to consume the sauce sparingly. The prawn meat is tender, the ginger and scallion enhance the aroma, and the Huadiao wine adds a unique flavor. Best enjoyed while hot.
