# Berry Yam Mash
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Yam 2 pieces (about 500g, preferably iron stick yam or Huai yam, the starchy type)
🍓 Strawberries 250g (can be substituted with other fresh or frozen berries)
🫐 Mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, blackberries, etc.) 250g
🧈 Butter 20g (optional, to enhance flavor of yam mash)
🥛 Milk 100ml (to adjust consistency, choose one option with heavy cream)
🥛 Heavy cream About 130ml (to adjust consistency, choose one option with milk and butter)
🍬 White granulated sugar 70g (adjust to taste)
🍋 Lemon juice About 5g (about half a lemon, can be omitted)

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Peeler (recommend wearing gloves when handling)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Steamer
🔥 Gas stove or induction cooktop
🍲 Non-stick pan or stainless steel pan (to prevent sticking when making jam)
🥄 Spatula
🍠 Masher or rolling pin
🥤 Blender (optional, for pureeing fruit into a fine paste)
🥣 Soup pot (also can be used to make jam)

## Instructions
1. **Yam Preparation**: Wearing gloves, peel the yam, rinse it clean, break into small sections, and place in the steamer.
   *Note: The yam's mucilage can easily cause skin irritation. Be sure to wear gloves when handling. If itching occurs, soak in hot water for relief.*
2. **Fruit Preparation**: Cut large fruits like strawberries into small pieces and set aside.
3. **Steaming the Yam**: Place the yam in a steamer with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 20 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
4. **Making the Jam**: Combine the cut fruit with white sugar, mash with a masher or puree in a blender. Add lemon juice and stir to mix. Pour into a non-stick pan or soup pot, bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and simmer slowly.
   *Key Point: Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook until thickened. When lifted with a spatula, the jam should cling to it, and a finger drawn across the back leaves a clear, persistent mark. Cooking time depends on the fruit's water content. Not adding water makes concentration easier.*
5. **Making the Yam Mash**: While still hot, place the steamed yam in a bowl and mash it into a paste with a masher. Gradually add the butter and milk, or directly add the heavy cream, stirring continuously until the yam mash is soft and smooth.
   *Key Point: Adjust the amount of liquid flexibly based on the yam's actual moisture content, adding little by little.*
6. **Assembly and Decoration**: Shape the yam mash into a small mound in a bowl, drizzle with jam, and garnish with fresh fruit and mint leaves. Tastes better when chilled before serving.

## Health Advice
This berry yam mash replaces high-carb staples with yam, which is rich in dietary fiber and pectin, making it suitable for those controlling sugar or losing weight as a satisfying treat. Note that yam mucilage can cause allergies, so be sure to wear gloves when handling.
