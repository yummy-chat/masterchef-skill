# Ginger and Scallion Stir-Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 1/2 Qingyuan chicken (chopped into small pieces)  
🫚 About 1 small plate of yellow ginger (sliced)  
🧅 About 1 small plate of scallions (white parts and green parts separated)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for marinating)  
🌾 A little cornstarch (for marinating)  
🫗 1 tablespoon chicken fat (for marinating) + appropriate amount (as base oil for frying)  
🍶 Appropriate amount of wine (poured along the edge of the wok to enhance aroma)  
🦪 1 tablespoon oyster sauce  
🥢 1 teaspoon light soy sauce  
🍬 A little sugar  
🌶️ A little white pepper  
💧 About 50ml water (a small amount to prevent dry frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rinse the chicken pieces thoroughly and pat them completely dry with kitchen paper towels.  
**Key point**: The moisture must be completely removed to achieve a crispy sear.  

2. Add a pinch of salt and a little cornstarch to the chicken pieces and mix well. Then add 1 tablespoon of chicken fat and mix again. Set aside to marinate for a short while.  
**Key point**: Salt provides a base flavor, cornstarch locks in the juices, and chicken fat seals in the aroma.  

3. Heat the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of chicken fat, and spread the chicken pieces in a single layer in the wok. Sear over medium-low heat for about 1 minute on one side, then flip and sear for another minute until both sides are golden brown.  
**Doneness check**: The surface should be golden and exude a crispy aroma.  

4. Remove the chicken pieces and set aside, leaving the remaining oil in the wok.  

5. Add the sliced ginger and the white parts of the scallions to the wok. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.  
**Key point**: The ginger and white scallion parts must be stir-fried first to release their aroma.  

6. Return the chicken pieces to the wok and stir-fry. Pour an appropriate amount of wine along the edge of the wok; it may ignite and create wok hei (breath of the wok).  
**Key point**: The wine must be poured along the edge of the wok.  

7. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, a little sugar, and white pepper. Stir-fry quickly until the seasonings evenly coat the chicken pieces.  

8. Add about 50ml of water and stir-fry to mix evenly.  
**Note**: Do not add too much water to avoid turning this into a braised chicken dish, which would lose the dry, aromatic texture.  

9. Continue stir-frying over medium heat for about 3 minutes, allowing the chicken to absorb the sauce. Do not cover the wok during this process.  
**Key point**: Not covering the wok preserves the wok hei and the dry, aromatic texture.  

10. When the sauce is mostly reduced, add the green parts of the scallions. Stir-fry quickly until they are just wilted, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.  
**Key point**: Add the green scallion parts at the very end to keep them bright green and fragrant.  

## Health Advice  
This ginger and scallion stir-fried chicken dish is rich in protein and moderate in fat, making it suitable for people needing an energy boost or those looking to build muscle and lose fat. Be mindful to control the amount of oil and salt to avoid excessive intake of sodium and saturated fat.
