# Pork Skin Jelly
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork skin 1 jin (500g)
💧 Water Ratio to pork skin weight (pressure cooker 1:4, regular pot 1:5)
🧂 Salt To taste (for cleaning, used together with white vinegar for scrubbing)
🍶 White vinegar To taste (for cleaning, deeply removes grease and odor)
🥫 Soy sauce A small amount (added during boiling for color and base flavor)
🧄 Minced garlic To taste (for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Chili powder To taste (for dipping sauce)
🍶 Vinegar To taste (for dipping sauce, added before pouring hot oil)
🔥 Hot oil To taste (poured over to release aroma of dipping sauce)
🧂 Salt To taste (for seasoning the dipping sauce)
🥫 Soy sauce To taste (for seasoning the dipping sauce)
🧂 MSG To taste (for seasoning the dipping sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, 🪵 Cutting board (for shredding and slicing)
🥣 Glass bowl (for washing pork skin shreds and setting the jelly)
🍲 Pressure cooker / Regular soup pot (for boiling)
🥄 Ladle (for stirring)
🧺 Strainer (for showing the state of pork skin shreds)
🍽️ Plate (for serving)
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing dipping sauce)

## Cooking Steps
1. Blanch the pork skin, then scrape off the inner fat thoroughly, and rinse repeatedly with warm water.
   **Note**: All fat must be removed completely, otherwise it will affect the setting and texture.
2. Cut the pork skin into fine shreds, the finer the better, to help release the gelatin fully.
3. Add salt and white vinegar to the pork skin shreds, rub vigorously, then add warm water and wash repeatedly until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Rubbing with salt and vinegar deeply removes the fishy smell and residual grease.
4. Place the pork skin shreds in the pot, add the correct proportion of water and a little soy sauce.
   - Pressure cooker: Cook under pressure for 20–30 minutes after the steam comes up, then let the pressure release naturally.
   - Regular pot: Simmer over medium-low heat for 1–1.5 hours, stirring occasionally.
   **Judgment Standard**: The pork skin shreds should be so soft that they crumble easily when pinched, and the gelatin is fully released.
5. Pour the cooked jelly soup into a glass bowl, let it cool naturally until completely set and firm.
   **Note**: The water-to-pork-skin ratio is the key to setting the jelly; do not adjust arbitrarily.
6. Once set, unmold the jelly by inverting the bowl, and cut into thick slices for plating. A well-made jelly is springy, bouncy, and not easily broken when dropped.
7. Prepare the dipping sauce: Put minced garlic, chili powder, and vinegar in a bowl, pour in hot oil to release the aroma, then season with salt, soy sauce, and MSG, and mix well.
8. Dip the jelly slices in the sauce and enjoy. The jelly is tender and slippery, paired with a spicy, sour, and aromatic flavor.

## Health Advice
This pork skin jelly is low in fat and rich in collagen, suitable for those looking to improve skin elasticity. However, individuals with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation, as a small amount of grease may remain in the gelatin. Pay attention to the amount of salt in the dipping sauce to avoid excessive sodium intake.
