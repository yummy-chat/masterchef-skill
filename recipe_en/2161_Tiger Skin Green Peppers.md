# Tiger Skin Green Peppers
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Thin-skinned large green peppers, about 10 (stemmed, seeded, washed, and drained)
🧄 Garlic, 4-5 cloves (smashed and minced)
🐖 Lard, a little (to grease the pan and enhance flavor)
🫘 Dried fermented black beans (or Lao Gan Ma fermented black bean sauce), to taste
🧂 Soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon
🍬 White sugar, a pinch
🧂 Salt, a pinch
💧 Water, about half a small bowl
🍶 Black vinegar (aged vinegar), about 1 tablespoon (drizzled along the pan edge before removing from heat)
🥣 Cornstarch slurry (to thicken lightly)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Spatula (or slotted spatula)
🥣 Small bowl
🍽 Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Dry-fry green peppers to create tiger skin**
   Heat the wok until smoking, without adding oil. Put in the drained green peppers. Heat over medium heat, constantly turning and lightly pressing the peppers with a spatula to ensure they make even contact with the pan. Once one side develops charred tiger skin spots, flip over and cook the other side to the same degree. When the peppers are softened, remove them from the pan.
   **Note**: The green peppers must be thoroughly drained to prevent oil splatter. Pressing helps form an even tiger skin quickly, but do not overcook them; retain some crunch.

2. **Fry fermented black beans and garlic until fragrant**
   Add a little lard to the pan and heat until melted. Reduce heat to low, first add the fermented black beans and stir-fry until aromatic, then add the minced garlic and continue frying until the fragrance is fully released.
   **Note**: Keep the heat on low throughout to avoid burning. Have the black beans and garlic ready beforehand to shorten the cooking time.

3. **Prepare sauce and braise for flavor**
   Drizzle soy sauce along the pan edge. Once the soy sauce aroma emerges, add white sugar, salt, and a small amount of water. Stir well to create a seasoning liquid. Return the dry-fried green peppers to the pan, turn the heat to medium, and braise for about 2 minutes, allowing the peppers to fully absorb the sauce.
   **Note**: Do not add too much water; keep just enough liquid. Maintain a gentle simmer during braising to ensure the peppers are well flavored.

4. **Thicken sauce, add vinegar, and serve**
   Before removing from heat, drizzle black vinegar along the pan edge and quickly stir to incorporate. Then pour in the cornstarch slurry to create a thin coating, stir rapidly until the sauce lightly thickens and evenly coats the peppers. Transfer to a plate.
   **Note**: Add black vinegar at the end to preserve its sour aroma. The cornstarch slurry should be thin; avoid making it too thick, which would ruin the dish's refreshing quality.

## Health Advice
Tiger skin green peppers are rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, making them suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss. However, fermented black beans and lard are high in salt and fat, so use them in moderation. Diabetics can consume this dish in appropriate amounts, paying attention to reduce the use of sugar and cornstarch slurry.
