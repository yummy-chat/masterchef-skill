# Lotus Seed Crystal Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Dried lotus seeds 400g (seedless lotus seeds recommended)
🥔 Potato starch 50g (adds chewy texture)
🌾 Wheat starch 150g (makes the finished product translucent)
🍵 Matcha powder 3g (for matcha-flavored crust)
🍫 Cocoa powder 3g (for cocoa-flavored crust)
🍬 White sugar 200g (for stir-frying the lotus seed filling, add when the filling is relatively dry)
🍬 White sugar 70g (for the sugar water used to scald the crystal crust dough)
🌿 Cooking oil 200g (for stir-frying the lotus seed filling, add in batches and stir-fry until fully absorbed)
🌿 Cooking oil 30g (for kneading the crystal crust dough, knead in batches)

## Cookware and Equipment
🌀 Blender or sieve (to puree or strain the steamed lotus seeds into a fine paste)
🍚 Rice cooker (for steaming the lotus seeds)
🍳 Non-stick wok (for stir-frying the lotus seed filling)
🥘 Stainless steel pot (for boiling the sugar water to scald the dough)
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for kneading dough and dividing portions)
🧁 Mooncake mold (to press patterns onto the crystal cakes)
🥟 Steamer basket and steamer (for steaming the raw crystal cakes)

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. Soak dried lotus seeds in clean water for 4 hours until soft.
   **Note**: Using seedless lotus seeds simplifies the process.
2. Discard the soaking water and rinse the lotus seeds clean.
3. Place the lotus seeds in a rice cooker, add water about 1 cm above the seeds, and steam for 1 hour until they are mushy to the touch.
4. Blend the steamed lotus seeds into a fine paste using a blender, or press through a sieve to make a paste.
   **Note**: If the blender struggles, add a small amount of water, but not too much, otherwise the subsequent stir-frying time will be significantly extended.
5. Mix potato starch and wheat starch well and set aside.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Make the lotus seed filling**: Pour the lotus seed paste into a non-stick wok and stir-fry over medium-low heat. Add 200g of cooking oil in batches, ensuring each batch is fully absorbed before adding the next. When the filling is relatively dry, add 200g of white sugar and continue stir-frying until the sugar is completely melted and the filling clumps together without sticking to the spatula. Remove from heat, let cool, and refrigerate for later use.
   **Note**: Only add the sugar when the filling is relatively dry; adding it too early will cause the sugar to melt and thin the filling, extending the stir-frying time.
2. **Make the crystal crust**: Add 250ml of water and 70g of white sugar to a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat until the sugar melts, then immediately turn off the heat. Quickly pour in the mixed starches, stirring simultaneously until no dry flour remains. Remove and let cool until warm enough to handle.
   **Note**: Scald the dough with boiling water; add the flour mixture immediately after turning off the heat, using the residual heat to thoroughly cook the dough. This is key to ensuring a translucent crust.
3. **Knead the crystal crust**: Form the scalded dough into a ball. Add 30g of cooking oil in 3 batches, kneading each batch thoroughly before adding the next. Finally, knead into a smooth, soft, and pliable dough.
   **Note**: Kneading for a bit longer ensures the oil is fully incorporated, giving the finished product a chewier texture.
4. **Divide the crystal crust portions**: Roll the dough into a log, then divide into portions of about 35g each. Cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying.
5. **Prepare the lotus seed filling**: Take the refrigerated lotus seed filling. If it has hardened, knead it until soft, then roll into a log and divide into portions of about 10g each. Roll each into a ball and set aside.
6. **Color the dough**: Take 1/3 of the crystal crust dough, add matcha powder, and knead until evenly colored. Roll into a log and divide. Take another 1/3, add cocoa powder, and knead evenly. The remaining 1/3 is plain.
7. **Assemble and shape**: Flatten a piece of crystal crust dough, place a ball of lotus seed filling in the center. Use your thumb and forefinger to slowly push the edges of the crust upward to enclose the filling. Pinch the seam tightly together and shape it into a smooth ball. Place it into a mooncake mold and press down vertically to imprint a clear pattern.
   **Note**: Ensure the seam is tightly sealed to prevent the filling from leaking during steaming.
8. **Steam**: Place the assembled raw crystal cakes into a steamer basket lined with a steaming cloth. Once the water is boiling and steam is rising, place the basket in the steamer and steam over high heat for 6 minutes. After steaming, turn off the heat and let the cakes rest in the steamer for 5 minutes before removing the lid.
   **Note**: Letting the cakes rest in the steamer prevents the crust from shrinking or collapsing due to a sudden temperature drop.
9. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

## Health Advice
This dessert is high in carbohydrates and oil. It is recommended to eat only 1-2 pieces at a time to satisfy a craving, and it pairs well with green tea to aid digestion. It is suitable for sharing during festivals or as an elegant tea snack, but individuals with diabetes or those on a weight loss plan should carefully control their intake.
