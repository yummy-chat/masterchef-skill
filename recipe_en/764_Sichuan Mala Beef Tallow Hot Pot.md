# Sichuan Mala Beef Tallow Hot Pot
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐂 Beef tallow 20 jin (base for hot pot)
🧅 Ginger about 3 jin (sliced)
🧅 Scallions about 3 jin (cut into sections)
🌶️ Dried chili (bullet head) a large amount (to make Ciba chili, provides spiciness and red color)
🌶️ Lantern chili add a bit more (not extremely spicy, provides aroma and shape)
🌿 Mixed spices (cardamom seeds removed, star anise, san nai, cassia bark, dried tangerine peel, bay leaves, cloves, white cardamom, monk fruit) an appropriate amount each
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns an appropriate amount
🫘 Red oil bean paste about 3-4 jin
🫘 Douchi (fermented black beans) an appropriate amount
🍚 Fermented rice (jiuniang) a little bit
🍬 Rock sugar a little bit
🍶 Baijiu 3 bottle caps (for soaking spices and final aroma enhancement)
🧂 Salt an appropriate amount (for seasoning when cooking the hot pot)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok (for wide oil)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🪣 Large slotted spoon
🥄 Long-handled wok spatula/spatula
🥣 Stainless steel basin/bowl
🧺 Bamboo woven basket
🔥 Firewood stove (or stable high-heat stove)
🍲 Hot pot basin and side dish bowls and plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the seeds from the cardamom. Mix with star anise, san nai, cassia bark, dried tangerine peel, bay leaves, cloves, white cardamom, and monk fruit in a basin. Pour in the baijiu to soak and set aside.
**Key point**: Do not grind the spices. Soaking them in alcohol helps release their aroma and removes any off-flavors.

2. Place the bullet head dried chilies into a pot of boiling water and cook until completely soft. They are ready when you can squeeze out red juice by hand. Remove, drain, place on a cutting board, and chop into coarse pieces. This is the Ciba chili.
**Key point**: The chilies must be fully cooked through to make chopping easier and to release the red oil during frying.

3. Slice the ginger and cut the scallions into sections. Set aside separately.
4. Place the large iron wok over heat. Add 20 jin of beef tallow. Heat over medium-high heat until completely melted into liquid oil.
5. When the oil temperature reaches about 40-50% hot, first add the ginger slices and fry for a moment, then add the scallion sections. Reduce to medium-low heat and fry slowly. Use a long-handled spatula to stir constantly. Fry until the ginger and scallions are golden and crispy.
**Key point**: This step adds a foundational aroma to the beef tallow. The fried ginger and scallions must be thoroughly removed with a slotted spoon afterward to prevent them from turning black and bitter during subsequent cooking.

6. When the oil temperature drops to about 50% hot, add the Ciba chili. Stir over medium heat to disperse, then reduce to medium-low heat. Stir continuously to prevent sticking and burning at the bottom.
7. Continue stirring and simmering until the oil color changes from dark yellow to a bright, vivid red, and the Ciba chili releases its red oil.
**Key point**: This is the key to the base's color. Be patient and control the heat carefully.

8. Reduce the heat to low. Drain the excess alcohol from the spice mixture that was soaked in baijiu, then add it to the wok. Stir well and simmer slowly for a few minutes until the aroma is released.
9. Add a large amount of lantern chilies. Continue to stir.
10. Add about 3-4 jin of red oil bean paste. Stir slowly over medium-low heat until the bean paste's moisture has evaporated, the grains separate, and a rich, concentrated bean aroma is released.
11. Add the douchi and stir-fry until fragrant.
12. Add a small amount of fermented rice and rock sugar in sequence. Stir to combine well.
**Key point**: Do not add too much fermented rice and rock sugar. The flavor can be adjusted further when cooking the hot pot later.

13. Drizzle three bottle caps of baijiu along the edge of the wok. Stir quickly to combine, then turn off the heat.
14. Scoop the finished hot pot base into a stainless steel tray. Allow it to cool and solidify naturally into a block. To serve, take out one piece, place it in a hot pot, add water or stock, bring to a boil, season with salt as needed, and then you can start cooking ingredients.

## Health Advice
This spicy beef tallow hot pot is rich in oil and spice. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables and tofu to balance the meat. People with high blood lipids, gout, or sensitive stomachs should limit their consumption frequency.
