# Jasmine Iced Tofu Pudding
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Soy milk powder 30g (1 small pack, can substitute with milk powder to make jasmine milk pudding)  
❄️ White jelly powder 14g (about 1.5 bottle caps; instant-dissolve type recommended)  
🍵 Jasmine tea drink 250ml (half bottle, room temperature or chilled)  
🍈 Lychee 7 pieces (peeled and pitted, for decoration)  
🥛 Fresh milk as needed (chilled, pour into the bowl at the end)  
🌸 Dried jasmine petals a pinch (for decoration, optional)  
🍬 Sugar/rock sugar to taste (add according to preference; not used in the video)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (with lid, heat-resistant)  
🥤 Plastic cup (for activating the heating pack or holding cold/hot water)  
🥄 Spoon  
🪣 Cold water basin (for accelerating cooling and setting)  

## Steps  
1. Pour the jasmine tea into a stainless steel bowl, add the white jelly powder and soy milk powder, and stir with a spoon until fully dissolved with no powdery lumps.  
   **Key point**: The powders can be added directly to room-temperature tea liquid; no preheating is needed.  
2. Choose one of two heating methods:  
   — If using a heating pack: Place 30g of heating pack at the bottom of a plastic cup, add a small amount of cold water to activate it. Immediately place the stainless steel bowl with the mixture on top of the heating pack, cover with the lid, and let it sit for 5–8 minutes until the liquid in the bowl boils.  
   — If no heating pack: Directly use boiling water to dissolve the soy milk powder and white jelly powder, replacing the jasmine tea as the mixture.  
3. After heating, remove the lid and skim off any foam from the surface with a spoon.  
4. Place the stainless steel bowl in a cold water basin to cool. Let it sit until the bowl wall is no longer warm and the liquid has fully set into a quivering jelly.  
5. Arrange the peeled and pitted lychee meat on top of the set tofu pudding, pour in chilled fresh milk, and sprinkle with dried jasmine petals. Add sugar or rock sugar to taste if desired.  

## Health Tips  
This jasmine iced tofu pudding is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber, offering a cooling and refreshing treat ideal as a low-calorie dessert for summer afternoons. Lychee and jasmine enhance the flavor, but they are high in sugar; those monitoring sugar intake can reduce the amount of lychee or omit sugar.
