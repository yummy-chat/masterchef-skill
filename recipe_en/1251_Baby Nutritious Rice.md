# Baby Nutritious Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice appropriate amount (washed)
🥔 Chinese yam appropriate amount (peeled, cut into pieces)
🥬 Spinach appropriate amount (chopped)
🌭 Ham sausage appropriate amount (diced)
🍘 Dried seaweed flakes appropriate amount (chopped)
🦐 Large shrimp appropriate amount (shelled and deveined recommended)
🍖 Lamb chops appropriate amount (rinsed only, no marinating needed)
🥬 Baby cabbage appropriate amount (chopped)
🧂 None (no seasonings used in this dish)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍚 Rice cooker (with inner pot)

## Steps
1. Wash the rice thoroughly, then pour it into the rice cooker inner pot and spread it evenly.
2. Peel the Chinese yam and cut into pieces, chop the spinach, dice the ham sausage, chop the dried seaweed, and chop the baby cabbage.
3. Place the chopped Chinese yam, spinach, ham sausage, and dried seaweed into the inner pot, and gently mix them with the rice.
4. On top of the mixed ingredients, arrange the prepared large shrimp and lamb chops in sequence, and finally sprinkle the chopped baby cabbage evenly over them.
   **Note**: It is recommended to shell, butterfly, and devein the large shrimp; rinse the lamb chops clean without marinating.
5. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, select the normal cooking program, and start it.
   **Key point**: Do not add extra water; the moisture from the vegetables and ingredients is sufficient to steam the rice. Do not open the lid or stir during cooking.
6. After the rice cooker switches to keep warm mode, let it rest for another 5 minutes, then open the lid and serve.

## Health Advice
This baby nutritious rice is rich in ingredients, suitable for providing picky-eating children with a variety of protein and vitamins, especially recommended for babies who do not like vegetables. Ensure the lamb chops and shrimp are fully cooked. Ham sausage has a high sodium content, so it can be reduced or omitted for infants and toddlers.
