# Apple Milk Pancake 🥞
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍎 Apple: 1 medium-sized, grated into fine shreds
🌾 All-purpose flour: about 2 tablespoons
🥚 Eggs: 2
🥛 Whole milk: half a can (about 120-150 ml, adjust based on batter consistency)
🫒 Cooking oil: a little (spray oil preferred, for pan-frying)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🧀 Grater
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍽️ Plates: 2 (one for flipping, one for serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the apple and grate it into fine shreds using a grater. Place into a stainless steel bowl.
   - Note: Apple shreds should be of moderate and even thickness, neither too coarse nor too fine.
2. Add flour, eggs, and milk to the bowl. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks until a smooth, thick batter forms.
   - Key: The batter should be thick enough to be lifted by chopsticks without flowing too quickly. If too dry, add a little milk; if too thin, add a little flour to adjust.
3. Preheat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Spray a very small amount of oil and spread it evenly across the pan with a silicone spatula.
   - Note: Use minimal oil to avoid greasy results.
4. Pour the prepared apple batter into the pan. Use the silicone spatula to scrape all batter into the pan.
5. Gently press and spread the batter into a round, thin pancake using the silicone spatula, aiming for even thickness.
   - Key: Spreading it thin helps it cook through easily.
6. Cook over medium-low heat for about 2-3 minutes, until the bottom is set, the surface begins to solidify, and the edges look translucent.
   - Note: Do not use high heat, or the outside will burn while the inside remains undercooked.
7. Flip the pancake: Place a plate slightly smaller than the pan's opening upside down over the pancake. Hold the plate with one hand, quickly flip the pan so the pancake falls onto the plate, then slide it back into the pan to complete the flip.
   - Note: This method is beginner-friendly; perform the action decisively.
8. Continue cooking the other side over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown and the pancake is fully cooked through.
9. Remove the cooked apple pancake, place it on a cutting board, cut into 8 triangular pieces with a knife, and serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Apple milk pancakes are rich in carbohydrates and protein. The dietary fiber from apples aids digestion, making them suitable as breakfast or an afternoon tea snack. The sweetness mainly comes from the apples, so be mindful of overall sugar intake; diabetics should consume in moderation. Using minimal oil for pan-frying is key to avoiding high-calorie greasiness and maintaining health.
