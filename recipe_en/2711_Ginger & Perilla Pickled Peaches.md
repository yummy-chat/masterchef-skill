# Ginger & Perilla Pickled Peaches
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍑 Firm peach, about 2 (or slightly underripe fuzzy peaches for better texture)
🌿 Perilla leaves, as needed (for boiling water)
🫚 Young ginger (ginger slices), as needed
🧂 Salt, about 1.5 tbsp (for brining)
🍚 Sugar, about 1.5 tbsp (for brining)
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed (to season the perilla water)
🍶 White vinegar, 200g (plain white vinegar—key for color change)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Glass pot (for boiling perilla water)
🥣 Glass bowl or container (for mixing, brining, and refrigerating)
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🌯 Plastic wrap (for sealing during refrigeration)

## Instructions
1. Place the perilla leaves in a pot with water, bring to a boil. Keep at a gentle boil for about 5 minutes until the aroma is released, then turn off the heat. Remove and discard the perilla leaf solids.  
**Note**: Let the perilla water cool to 30–40°C (warm) before using.

2. Pit the firm peaches and cut them into chunks. Slice the young ginger thinly.  
3. Place the peach chunks and ginger slices in a bowl. Add about 1.5 tbsp salt and 1.5 tbsp sugar. Mix well with your hands and let sit for about 20 minutes.  
**Key point**: This brining step removes some moisture and astringency from the peaches.

4. After brining, rinse thoroughly under running water to completely remove surface salt and sugar. Drain well.  
5. Add the peach chunks and ginger slices to the cooled perilla water. Pour in 200g of white vinegar and gently stir to combine.  
**Key point**: When vinegar is added, the perilla water will quickly turn a beautiful purplish-red.

6. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 24 hours. The longer it sits, the more flavorful it becomes.  
7. Serve cold. The peach chunks are crisp, with a sweet-and-sour, appetizing taste, carrying the fresh flavor of young ginger and the aroma of perilla.

## Health Tips
Ginger & Perilla Pickled Peaches uses firm peaches pickled in vinegar—a refreshing and digestion-stimulating treat, ideal for cooling off in summer. Young ginger warms the stomach, and perilla aids digestion. However, it has relatively high salt and sugar content, so moderate consumption is recommended.
