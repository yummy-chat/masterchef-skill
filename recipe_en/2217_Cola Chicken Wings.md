## Cooking Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Clean the chicken wings. Use kitchen scissors to cut a slit through the middle of each wing, piercing both sides.
   **Key point**: Do not cut crosswise, nor blanch the wings, as this will make the chicken tough and cause blood to produce an unpleasant odor.
2. Rinse the cut wings again, then pat them completely dry with kitchen paper towels.
   **Key point**: If not thoroughly dried, it will affect the marinating and cause sticking during frying.
3. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, a small amount of black pepper, light soy sauce, and yellow wine to the dried wings. Use your hands to mix and rub thoroughly so the wings absorb the marinade. Marinate for about 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: The amounts of black pepper, yellow wine, and light soy sauce must be controlled to avoid overpowering flavors or excess moisture; mix thoroughly to ensure even seasoning.

### Cooking Phase
4. Heat the iron wok thoroughly over high heat. Pour in oil, swirl to coat the wok, then pour the oil out. Reheat the wok over high heat, turn off the heat, and pour the reserved oil back in to coat the wok a second time.
   **Key point**: This repeated "heat-coat" process effectively prevents sticking, even with a regular iron wok.
5. After coating, pour out the hot oil. Turn the heat to medium. Place the marinated wings in the wok. Keep the heat on medium and do not flip them too often. Fry until both sides are golden brown, then remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Use only a small amount of oil in the wok; medium heat prevents burning and maintains wok temperature.
6. Using the oil left from frying the wings, add fresh scallion sections and ginger slices. Sauté until fragrant. Pour soy sauce along the edge of the wok to create a burst of aroma. Add the fried wings back in and stir-fry briefly to fully release the soy sauce's fragrance.
   **Key point**: The soy sauce must be thoroughly sizzled; maintain medium heat.
7. Pour about half a bottle of cola into the wok. If the liquid is insufficient, add water until the wings are just submerged. Add salt. Cover the wok and simmer over medium-low heat for 3-5 minutes, until the wings are cooked through and flavored. Remove the scallion and ginger pieces.
   **Key point**: Do not use too much cola; ensure the salt level is adequate, otherwise the final dish will be overly sweet and cloying.
8. Increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Add dark soy sauce for coloring as needed. Continuously stir the wings with the spatula until the sauce thickens, becomes glossy, and evenly coats the wings. Serve immediately.
   **Key point**: Chicken wings are high in collagen; stir constantly during sauce reduction to prevent the wings from sticking and burning.

## Health Advice
This cola chicken wing dish is rich in protein, easily absorbed, and suitable for muscle building or satisfying cravings. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure should use less salt. As cola contains sugar, no additional sugar is needed. Watch your overall calorie intake.
