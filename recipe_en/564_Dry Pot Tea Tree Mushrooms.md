# Dry Pot Tea Tree Mushrooms
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍄 Fresh tea tree mushrooms 1000g (trim roots, wash, and tear into small strips)
🍄 Dried tea tree mushrooms appropriate amount (soak, press, then cut into sections)
🥓 Pork belly 1 small piece (skin removed, slightly fatty, sliced)
🥓 Smoked skin-on cured pork belly 1 small piece (sliced)
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (finely chopped)
🌶️ Erjingtiao peppers appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🌶️ Xiaomila peppers appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🧅 Garlic sprouts 3 stalks (cut into small sections)
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (finely shredded, for lining the dry pot bottom)
🌶️ Dried chilies appropriate amount
🫘 Green Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
🧈 Beef tallow hot pot base 1 small piece (chopped)
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount (for deep frying and stir-frying)
🦪 Oyster sauce about 4g
🧂 Chicken bouillon about 2g
🍬 White sugar about 1g
🌿 Ground white pepper a pinch
🥄 Light soy sauce about 5g (pour along the edge of the wok)
🍺 Beer appropriate amount (or cooking wine)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
✂️ Scissors
🫗 Pressure cooker
🍳 Wok (large iron wok)
🥄 Slotted spoon
♨️ Dry pot (with burner base)

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the root ends with soil from fresh tea tree mushrooms, wash thoroughly under water, drain, then tear from the root into small strips. Set aside.
2. Trim the root ends from dried tea tree mushrooms, soak in water for 20 minutes, then transfer to a pressure cooker with fresh water. Pressure cook over medium-low heat for about 20 minutes until softened. Remove and cut into small sections. Set aside.
3. Remove skin from pork belly and slice. Slice the cured pork belly. Finely chop garlic, finely chop Erjingtiao and Xiaomila peppers, cut garlic sprouts into small sections. Finely shred the onion and spread it on the bottom of the dry pot.
4. Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok to 50% hot. Add the torn fresh tea tree mushrooms and deep-fry over medium-low heat for about 2 minutes until the moisture evaporates and they soften. Remove and drain oil.
**Note**: Ensure the mushrooms are thoroughly dried of moisture to achieve a dry and fragrant texture.
5. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the sliced pork belly and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders. Then add the sliced cured pork belly and stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Add the minced garlic, dried chilies, and green Sichuan peppercorns. Continue stir-frying over medium-low heat until the aroma is intense.
7. Add the chopped beef tallow hot pot base and stir-fry until melted and fragrant. Then add the pressed dried tea tree mushroom sections and stir-fry until the moisture evaporates and the fragrance is rich.
**Note**: The dried tea tree mushrooms must be stir-fried until dry and aromatic, avoiding a soft and wet texture.
8. Add oyster sauce, chicken bouillon, white sugar, and ground white pepper in order. Stir-fry evenly. Then pour light soy sauce along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly to release the sauce's aroma.
9. Splash in an appropriate amount of beer and stir-fry evenly to help remove any off-flavors and enhance fragrance.
10. Add the deep-fried fresh tea tree mushrooms and stir-fry evenly. Finally, add the garlic sprout sections and fresh chili pieces. Increase the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the garlic sprouts are just cooked.
**Key**: Add the fresh tea tree mushrooms later to retain their crispness. Stir-fry garlic sprouts over high heat to keep them bright green.
11. Remove from the wok and place into the dry pot lined with shredded onions. Transfer the pot to the burner base to heat and keep warm. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Mushrooms are high in fiber and low in fat. The small amount of pork belly and cured pork belly is used for flavor, making this dish suitable for those aiming to control carbohydrates and increase dietary fiber intake. Note that cured pork belly is high in salt. Individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the amount or blanch it beforehand to remove some salt.
