# Hand-Pulled Flatbread 🥞
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
💧 Boiling water 150g
💧 Cold water 110g
🧂 Salt 5g
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for brushing dough, work surface, dough sheets, and frying)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (glass bowl)
🥢 Rolling pin
🔗 Wooden or silicone spatula
🔥 Electric griddle
🫙 Plastic wrap
🖌️ Oil brush

## Steps
1. Mix 500g all-purpose flour with 5g salt evenly, then divide into two equal portions. Use 150g boiling water to scald half of the flour, and 110g cold water to mix the other half. Then combine both types of dough crumbs into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: The semi-scalding method gives the flatbread a texture that is both soft and chewy.

2. Brush a layer of oil evenly over the dough surface, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for 1 hour.

3. Oil the work surface to prevent sticking. Remove the rested dough, knead it into a long strip, and divide into evenly sized dough pieces.

4. Take one dough piece and roll it into a thin rectangular sheet with a rolling pin, then brush a layer of oil evenly over the surface.

5. Fold the dough sheet from one end like a fan into a long strip, then gently stretch it slightly and roll it up from one end, tucking the end underneath to form a spiral-shaped dough.
**Key point**: The folding technique creates multiple layers.

6. Cover all the spiral dough pieces with plastic wrap and let them rest again for 20 minutes.

7. Take a rested dough piece, flatten it, and roll it into a thin round flatbread base.

8. Preheat the electric griddle, brush a little oil, place the flatbread base on it, brush a layer of cold oil on the surface, cover with the lid, and cook for about 1 minute.
**Key point**: Brushing with cold oil and cooking with the lid on makes the inside soft.

9. When the flatbread puffs up and the bottom turns golden brown, flip it over. Continue cooking until the other side is golden and fully cooked, then remove from the griddle.

## Health Advice
Hand-pulled flatbread uses the semi-scalding technique, resulting in a moderate texture suitable for breakfast or snacks. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and protein in a single serving to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake. Pay attention to controlling the amount of oil used. Healthy individuals can consume it in moderation, while those with weak digestive function should eat less.
