# Sugar-Free Ciabatta  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 1600g  
💧 Water 1280g  
🍞 Dry yeast 3g  
🧂 Salt 28g  
🌿 Malt extract 4g  
🌽 Corn flour, as needed  
🫒 Olive oil, as needed  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
📦 Proofing box  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🥛 Measuring cup  
⚖️ Digital scale  
⏱️ Timer  
🧻 Baking parchment  
🔥 Oven  
🪨 Baking stone  
🥖 Rolling pin  

## Steps  
1. In a bowl, add water and malt extract, stir to combine, then add dry yeast and stir until dissolved. Mix bread flour with salt, pour into the liquid, and use a dough scraper to gather the dry flour from the sides of the bowl. Mix until a rough, shaggy dough forms with no dry flour.  
   **Note**: The dough has a very high hydration and will be extremely sticky at this stage. Only mix until combined; do not overmix.  

2. **First rest and fold**: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 1 hour. Dip your hands in water, stretch the dough from the edges to the center, folding it over itself. Flip the dough, gather it into a ball, cover again, and let it rest for another 60 minutes.  

3. **Second fold**: Dip your hands in water, use a dough scraper to lift the dough from the bottom, stretch it, and fold it over itself. Flip it over, shape it into a ball, cover, and let rest for 60 minutes.  

4. **Third fold**: Stretch the dough to check the gluten development. Fold the dough like a blanket into a square block, cover, and let rest for 30 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Dip your hands in water before each fold to prevent sticking. Folding strengthens the dough and promotes large air pockets, with each rest period lasting about 1 hour.  

5. Divide the dough into two pieces. Fold each piece once, then place in a proofing box. Brush the surface with olive oil. Let one box proof in a warm place for about 2 hours. The other box can be refrigerated and used within 2 days.  
   **How to check**: The dough should at least double in size, with large bubbles visible on the surface.  

6. Sprinkle a generous amount of corn flour on the work surface and the dough surface. Invert the proofed dough onto the work surface. Gently shape it into a rectangle of even thickness with your hands, being careful not to burst the bubbles.  
   **Note**: Corn flour makes the crust crisper and prevents the bottom from collapsing; shape gently to preserve the gas in the dough.  

7. Use a dough scraper to divide the dough into 8 equal pieces. Lightly tidy the cut edges and corners into rectangles. Place on baking parchment and let it final-proof at room temperature for 1 hour.  
   **Note**: Keep shaping simple to maintain the rustic, slipper-like shape of the ciabatta.  

8. After final proofing, drizzle a little olive oil on the surface of the dough pieces. Use your fingertips to gently press dimples into the dough, releasing a few large air bubbles.  
   **How to check**: The dough should retain clear imprints from your fingertips when pressed.  

9. Preheat the oven with the baking stone at 230°C for at least 1 hour. Use a suitable board to transfer the dough pieces onto the hot baking stone in the oven. Bake at 230°C for about 20 minutes. If your oven has a steam function, spray steam for 5-10 seconds immediately after loading the dough.  
   **Key point**: The baking stone must be fully preheated to provide intense initial heat, causing the bread to expand and form a crispy crust.  

## Health Tips  
Sugar-free ciabatta has high hydration, no sugar, and low oil, making it suitable for those managing blood sugar and reducing fat. However, bread flour still has a moderate glycemic index, so it is best paired with protein and vegetables.
