# Jin Tou Ba Nao (Beef Tendon and Trim Cuts Stew)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef tendon and trim cuts 1000g, rinse frozen product several times, cut into 2-3cm pieces  
🧅 Scallion 40g (20g sections for blanching, 20g sliced for stir-frying aromatics)  
🫚 Ginger 25g (10g slices for blanching, 15g slices for stir-frying aromatics)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (smash flat)  
🍃 Bay leaves 3 pieces  
🌰 Cardamom pod shell 1 piece (remove the seeds, use only the shell)  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🌳 Cinnamon stick 1g (do not use too much)  
🌸 Angelica root 2g  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
🫒 Vegetable oil, slightly more than usual for stir-frying  
🍶 High-proof baijiu 20g (for blanching to remove odor)  
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (chili bean paste) 20g  
🫘 Yellow soybean paste 20g  
🍅 Tomato paste 60g (about 2 tablespoons, can substitute with equal amount of ketchup)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine (or cooking wine) 20g  
🥢 Light soy sauce 30g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g  
🧂 White pepper powder 0.5g  
🍬 Rock sugar 15g (5-6 pieces the size of a pinky finger joint)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 4g (about 2 teaspoons)  
🌿 Cilantro (optional, for garnish)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or skillet  
🔪 Cutting board + knife  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
♨️ Pressure cooker (electric or stovetop)  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Strainer or slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl/container  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the beef tendon and trim**: Place the frozen mix in a bowl and rinse several times to remove blood. Cut large pieces into 2-3 cm cubes.  
   **Note**: Frozen product must be thoroughly washed; account for shrinkage when cutting.  

2. **Prepare aromatics**: Slice ginger, cut scallion into sections and slices, and smash garlic. Prepare bay leaves, cardamom shell, star anise, cinnamon, angelica root, and dried chilies.  
   **Key**: Remove the seeds from the cardamom and use only the shell; use only 1g of cinnamon to avoid bitterness.  

3. **Mix the sauce**: In a bowl, combine Pixian doubanjiang, yellow soybean paste, and tomato paste; stir to mix well. Set aside.  
   **Note**: Tomato paste provides a richer flavor; substitute with equal ketchup if unavailable.  

4. **Blanch to remove odor**: Place the beef in a pot with cold water. Add the blanching scallion sections, ginger slices, a small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns, and the baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and turn off the heat as soon as it reaches a full boil. Immediately remove the beef.  
   **Note**: Start with cold water and use enough water; turn off the heat promptly once boiling.  

5. **Rinse the meat**: Rinse the blanched meat thoroughly with warm water to remove any remaining foam from the surface.  
   **Note**: Do not use cold water, as it will cause the meat to tighten and become tough.  

6. **Fry the aromatics**: Pour oil into a wok over low heat. Add the stir-fry scallion slices, ginger slices, and garlic. Increase to high heat and fry until the scallion slices bubble and the ginger and garlic turn slightly yellow. Add all the dried spices and stir-fry until fragrant, about 2 minutes.  

7. **Fry the sauce**: Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add the mixed sauce and stir to combine. Once the pot temperature has dropped slightly, increase to high heat and stir-fry until the sauce releases its full aroma, about 3 minutes.  
   **Key**: Use low heat to prevent burning, then high heat to amplify the sauce’s fragrance.  

8. **Stir-fry the meat**: Add the drained beef and stir-fry quickly over high heat for 1 minute, ensuring each piece is coated with the sauce.  
   **Note**: Stir immediately after adding the meat to prevent the spices from sticking and burning.  

9. **Deglaze with Shaoxing wine**: Pour the Shaoxing wine along the edge of the wok and continue stir-frying until the alcohol has fully evaporated.  
   **Key**: Pour along the hot wok edge to use the instant evaporation to remove any odor.  

10. **Season and add water**: Reduce to the lowest heat. Add light soy sauce and oyster sauce along the wok edge, and stir to combine. Then pour in boiling water until it just barely covers the beef. Increase to high heat, add white pepper, rock sugar, and chicken bouillon. Stir well.  
    **Note**: Do not add too much water; it should just cover the meat. Use small rock sugar pieces for easier melting.  

11. **Pressure cook**: Transfer the meat and liquid into a pressure cooker. For an electric pressure cooker, select the “Beef/Lamb” mode and cook for 35 minutes. For a stovetop pressure cooker, bring to pressure, then reduce to low heat and cook for 25-30 minutes.  
    **Key**: If using a stovetop pressure cooker, be sure to reduce to low heat to prevent excessive pressure.  

12. **Reduce sauce and serve**: Once cooked, remove the meat pieces and strain the liquid to remove the spice residue. Return the meat and strained liquid to the wok. Increase to high heat and reduce the liquid until it becomes slightly thick and glossy. Plate and garnish with cilantro.  
    **Note**: Reducing the liquid until thick makes the flavor richer; straining removes impurities for a cleaner texture.  

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in collagen and protein, ideal for those needing an energy boost or joint support. However, the sauce and rock sugar content are relatively high; consume in moderation if you have high blood pressure or are on a weight loss plan.
