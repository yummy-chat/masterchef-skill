# French Stuffed Tomatoes
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef mince 200g
🥩 Pork mince 200g
🍅 Tomatoes 6 (choose large tomatoes with stems attached)
🧅 Onion to taste (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic to taste (finely chopped)
🥓 Ham to taste (or bacon, diced)
🍳 White bread 1 slice (crust removed, diced)
🥛 Milk a little (to soften the bread)
🥚 Egg 1
🌿 Thyme a little
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶️ Black pepper to taste
🫒 Olive oil to taste

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, chopping board
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Bowl
🔥 Oven
🥘 Baking tray
🍴 Fork
🥄 Spoon

## Steps
1. Wash the tomatoes, then cut off the top with the stem horizontally to form a lid. Use a spoon to scoop out the inner tomato pulp. Place the hollowed-out tomato shells upside down on a baking tray to drain excess water.
2. Finely chop the onion and garlic; dice the ham. Remove the crust from the bread and dice it, then soften it with a little milk. Mash it into a paste with a fork.
3. Pour a little olive oil into a frying pan, add the diced ham and sauté over low heat until fragrant. Add the chopped onion and garlic and stir-fry until aromatic. Finally, add the scooped-out tomato pulp, stir-fry and simmer until the liquid evaporates, the mixture thickens, and it is as dry as possible. Remove and allow to cool.
   **Key point**: Patiently simmering and reducing is a crucial step for enhancing flavor.
4. In a large bowl, combine the beef and pork mince. Add the cooled ham-tomato mixture, black pepper, thyme, the softened bread paste, and the egg. Mix all ingredients thoroughly with your hands, then add salt to taste.
5. Use a spoon to fill the drained tomato shells with the meat stuffing. Fill them generously, smooth the top so they are plump. Drizzle a little olive oil over the surface of the stuffing, then bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 30 minutes.
6. Remove the baking tray, cover each tomato with its reserved lid, then return to the oven and bake at 180°C for a further 15 minutes. Ready to serve.

## Health Advice
This dish balances the richness of the meat with the sweet and sour flavor of the tomatoes. It is a good low-fat, high-protein choice for summer, suitable for those who are working out or managing their blood sugar. However, pay attention to controlling the amount of oil in the baking tray.
