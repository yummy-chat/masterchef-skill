# Braised Soybean Skin "Vegetarian Pork Belly"  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥠 Dried soybean skin (yuba)  About 4-5 sheets (cut into strips about 10 cm wide)  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms  About 1-2 small bunches (torn into small florets about the thickness of one finger)  
🧴 Light soy sauce  1 tablespoon  
🧴 Oyster sauce  1 tablespoon  
🍶 Dark soy sauce  Half a tablespoon  
🧂 Salt  1 teaspoon  
🍬 Sugar  1 teaspoon  
💧 Water  Half a small bowl  
🌾 Water starch  As needed (for thickening before removing from heat)  
🌿 Chopped green onions  As needed (for garnish)  
🌰 Toasted sesame seeds  As needed (for garnish)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board & chef's knife  
🧹 Toothpicks (for securing the soybean skin rolls)  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing the sauce)  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥢 Chopsticks or spatula  

## Steps  
1. Cut the dried soybean skin into strips about 10 cm wide. Soak in warm water for 30 seconds, then remove and drain thoroughly.  
   **Note**: Do not soak for too long; ensure the soybean skin is well-drained.  
2. Tear the enoki mushrooms into small florets about the thickness of one finger.  
3. Take one piece of the prepared soybean skin, place a small bundle of enoki mushrooms on it, roll it up tightly from one end, and secure the roll in the middle with a toothpick. Repeat this step to make several rolls.  
4. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, sugar, and half a small bowl of water. Stir until the sugar and salt are fully dissolved.  
   **Key Point**: Mixing the sauce in advance ensures even flavor distribution and saves cooking time.  
5. Heat the frying pan over high heat, then add a thin layer of oil to coat the bottom. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat to low and gently pan-fry the soybean skin rolls.  
   **Note**: Maintain low heat and patiently fry both sides of the rolls until they are golden brown and slightly crispy.  
6. After both sides are golden brown and slightly crispy, pour in the prepared sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to medium-low heat and braise. Gently shake the pan occasionally so the rolls are evenly coated with the sauce and absorb the flavors thoroughly.  
   **Key Point**: Braising allows the soybean skin and enoki mushrooms to fully absorb the savory flavor of the sauce.  
7. When the sauce has slightly thickened, add the water starch to thicken further. Continuously rotate the pan for about 30 seconds so the sauce coats the ingredients evenly.  
8. Turn off the heat. Remove all toothpicks, then plate the rolls. Garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds for added fragrance.  
   **Note**: Be sure to remove all toothpicks before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This is a high-protein, low-fat vegetarian dish. Dried soybean skin is rich in plant protein, and enoki mushrooms provide dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss or a vegetarian diet. Use low heat when pan-frying to avoid excess calories.
