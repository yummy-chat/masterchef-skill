# Mini Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g (protein content 9-12%)  
🧪 Instant dry yeast for high-sugar dough 2.5g  
🥛 Ice-cold milk 145g (use cold liquid in summer to control temperature)  
🍼 Milk powder 25g (optional, for extra milky flavor)  
🍬 White sugar 16g (optional, slightly sweet; use regular yeast if sugar is omitted)  
🧈 Fat 8g (optional, lard/coconut oil/butter; gives shiny surface)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥤 Cut plastic beverage bottle (homemade measuring cup with markings)  
🧢 Bottle cap (as a measuring tool for yeast, sugar, and milk powder)  
🔪 Electric meat grinder/mixer (pulse mode for kneading)  
🧽 Silicone kneading mat  
🔨 Rolling pin  
🍱 Glass food container (to hold the dough)  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🥟 Bamboo steamer  
🧻 Steamer cloth (dampened to prevent sticking)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🛍️ Sealed storage bag/container (to store finished buns)  

## Instructions  
1. Place the all-purpose flour and milk powder into a mixing cup. Add the ice-cold milk, white sugar, and fat. Sprinkle the instant dry yeast for high-sugar dough on the surface.
   **Note**: Sprinkle yeast directly on the liquid surface; no need to dissolve in advance. Keep all liquids at refrigerated temperature to prevent premature fermentation.
2. Using an electric mixer on pulse mode, mix for 1–2 minutes until the dough comes together and the surface is initially smooth.
   **Note**: If the dough is too sticky, add a small amount of dry flour. If no machine is available, knead by hand until uniform.
3. Remove the dough and knead repeatedly with the heel of your hand until the surface is smooth and non-sticky. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate (freeze) for 10 minutes to relax.
   **Key point**: No initial proofing is needed. Freezing quickly lowers the dough temperature, relaxes the gluten, and results in a smooth finished surface.
4. Remove the dough, dust both sides with dry flour, and roll out to a thin sheet of about 0.5 cm thickness. Use a bottle cap or mold to cut out small round buns. Re-knead and re-roll the scraps for reuse.
5. Line the bamboo steamer with a dampened steamer cloth. Place the buns with space between them, cover, and proof at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until they visibly expand to about 1.5 times their original size and slowly spring back when lightly pressed.
   **Note**: Proof at room temperature in summer; no need for hot water. Use a bottle cap or similar item as a reference to compare height changes.
6. Place the steamer over cold water. Heat over high heat until the water boils, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 7–8 minutes.
   **Note**: Be sure to reduce to medium heat once steam appears, to prevent the bun surfaces from becoming uneven due to high heat.
7. After turning off the heat, let the buns rest for 1 minute before opening the lid to remove them, preventing collapse from temperature shock.
8. The finished mini steamed buns can be sealed and frozen for storage. Simply reheat when serving.

## Health Tips  
These mini buns are made with natural ingredients and are healthier than store-bought shredded bread. They are suitable as a children's snack or a guilt-free treat for those managing sugar intake. Note that freezing the dough to relax it is a crucial step for a smooth surface. In summer, 30 minutes of proofing is sufficient; avoid over-proofing.
