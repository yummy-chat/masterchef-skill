# Mirror Case Tofu  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Brine tofu (firm tofu) 1 large piece (about 400-500g)  
🥩 Pork belly about 250g (30% fat, 70% lean, minced)  
🥬 Winter bamboo shoots about 50g (finely chopped; fresh shoots need blanching in advance)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms about 30g (soaked and finely chopped)  
🦐 Dried shrimp about 15g (finely chopped)  
🫚 Scallions as needed (minced)  
🫚 Ginger as needed (minced)  
🥚 Egg ½ (for the meat filling)  
🍤 Shrimp as needed (blanched, for garnish)  
🥬 Baby bok choy (or leafy greens) as needed (blanched, for border decoration)  

## Seasonings  
🍶 Rice wine as needed  
🧂 Salt as needed  
🍬 White sugar 3g (for filling) + 5g (for sauce)  
🌶️ White pepper as needed  
🌾 Potato starch as needed (for filling and dusting tofu interior)  
🥄 Light soy sauce as needed  
🥄 Dark soy sauce as needed  
🧅 Scallion segments, ginger slices, cinnamon stick, star anise as needed (for sautéing)  
🫗 Cooking oil as needed (for frying and stir-frying)  
🫗 Scallion oil (or sesame oil) 1 tablespoon (to finish the sauce)  
💧 Water starch slurry as needed (for thickening)  

## Cooking Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Ladle  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Melon baller  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔥 Gas stove  
🧽 Fine mesh strainer (for filtering the sauce)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the tofu**: Cut the brine tofu into evenly sized rectangular pieces.  
   **Key point**: Firm tofu must be used; its dense texture ensures it won’t break easily when fried.  

2. **Prepare the aromatics**: Finely chop the winter bamboo shoots, shiitake mushrooms, and dried shrimp separately. If using fresh bamboo shoots, blanch them in boiling water for 10 minutes to remove oxalic acid.  

3. **Make the meat filling**: Mince the pork belly and mix with the chopped bamboo shoots, mushrooms, dried shrimp, scallions, ginger, rice wine, salt, sugar (3g), white pepper, starch, and half an egg. Stir vigorously in one direction and slap the mixture until it becomes sticky and cohesive.  
   **Key point**: The filling must be well stirred until sticky; otherwise, it may fall apart during cooking.  

4. **Fry the tofu**: Pour enough cooking oil into the wok and heat to 180°C (350°F). Fry the tofu pieces over medium-low heat until golden brown and the surface is firm. Remove and drain excess oil.  
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; fry until fully set and a hard crust forms on the surface.  

5. **Open the case and scoop out the core**: Let the tofu cool slightly. Use a small knife to cut an opening on one side to form a “lid.” Then use a melon baller to carefully scoop out the inside of the tofu, keeping the outer shell intact.  

6. **Fill the tofu cases**: Dust the inside of each hollowed tofu case evenly with potato starch. Fill with the meat mixture, smooth the top, and cover with the reserved tofu “lid.”  
   **Key point**: Dusting with starch is essential to prevent the filling from separating from the tofu.  

7. **Sauté and make the sauce**: Heat a little oil in the wok, then sauté scallion segments, ginger slices, cinnamon, and star anise over low heat until fragrant. Add light and dark soy sauces, stir well, then pour in enough water. Add rice wine, salt, and sugar (5g). Bring to a boil over high heat.  

8. **Braise the tofu**: Place the filled tofu cases opening-side up into the sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Cover and braise for about 15 minutes, until the sauce reduces, the tofu darkens in color, and the filling is fully cooked.  

9. **Prepare the garnishes**: In a separate pot of boiling water, blanch the baby bok choy and shrimp until just cooked. Drain.  

10. **Prepare the thickened sauce**: Strain the braising liquid through a fine mesh strainer into a clean pot. Bring to a boil, adjust seasoning with salt if needed, then stir in water starch slurry until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Finally, drizzle with scallion oil for luster.  

11. **Plate and pour the sauce**: Arrange the tofu cases on a serving plate, surround with blanched baby bok choy, place one shrimp on top of each tofu piece, and evenly pour the thickened sauce over everything.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses fried tofu as a shell to encase a meat filling, offering complementary nutrition and making it suitable for those needing extra protein. However, since deep-frying and pork belly are high in calories, it’s best to pair with light vegetables for a balanced meal. Control the amount of oil used, and those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation.
