# Sorghum Candy
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌽 Corn Starch 60g (toasted for dusting)
🍠 Sweet Potato Starch 100g
💧 Water 250ml
🍋 Lemon Juice 50g
🍬 White Sugar Approximately 500g (added in four portions: 200g, 100g, 100g, 100g)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-Stick Frying Pan (for toasting starch)
🥣 Glass Bowl
🫕 Large Non-Stick Pot (for boiling syrup; avoid iron pots)
🪷 Sieve
🥄 Wooden Spatula
🟫 Square Mold
🔪 Cutting Board and Knife

## Instructions
1. Place the frying pan over low heat, add the corn starch, and stir constantly until it turns slightly yellow and fragrant. Remove and set aside.
2. In a bowl, combine the sweet potato starch with water and stir until smooth with no lumps. Then pour in the lemon juice and mix well.
3. Strain the starch mixture through a sieve into a large non-stick pot, and add 200g of white sugar.
   - **Note**: A non-stick pot must be used; avoid iron pots throughout the process, or the final product will have an iron taste.
4. Turn the heat to medium, and stir continuously for about 1 minute, until the mixture thickens.
5. Reduce the heat to low, add 100g of white sugar, and stir constantly until completely dissolved. Then add another 100g of white sugar, and continue stirring and cooking for about 12 minutes until dissolved. Finally, add the last 100g of white sugar, and keep stirring until fully dissolved.
   - **Key**: After each addition of sugar, stir until fully dissolved before adding the next. Maintain low heat and stir continuously throughout to prevent scorching.
6. Once all the sugar is dissolved, take a small amount of the syrup and let it cool to test the texture; it should be chewy. When the stirring resistance increases noticeably and the texture becomes springy, it is ready to be removed from the pot. The entire boiling process takes about 13 minutes.
7. Line the bottom of the mold with a layer of toasted corn starch, then pour in the prepared syrup. Dust your hands with toasted starch and press the syrup flat while it is still hot. Let it cool until fully set. Invert the mold to release the candy, then on a cutting board dusted with toasted starch, first cut into strips, then into pieces. Coat each piece evenly with toasted starch to prevent sticking.

## Health Advice
This sorghum candy achieves its springy, chewy texture through a large amount of white sugar, making it suitable for an occasional treat. People with high blood sugar or those needing to control sugar intake should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to rinse your mouth after eating to prevent cavities.
