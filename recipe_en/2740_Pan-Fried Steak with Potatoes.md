# Pan-Fried Steak with Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Steak, about 2 pieces
🥔 Potato, 1 piece (peeled)
🍶 Cooking wine, 1 tablespoon
🥢 Light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🌿 Black pepper, to taste (used multiple times)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (more needed for frying potatoes)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍽️ Plate
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔥 Stove

## Steps
1. Cut the steak into finger-thick strips. Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, and an appropriate amount of black pepper. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes.
2. Peel the potato. First slice into thick pieces, then cut into thick strips similar in size to the steak strips.
3. Pour cooking oil into the pan. When the oil temperature reaches about 40% hot, add the potato strips. Pan-fry slowly over medium-low heat.
   **Key point**: Use more oil and low heat to ensure the potatoes are cooked through on the inside first.
4. When the surface of the potatoes becomes sticky, sprinkle with salt and black pepper to season.
   **Key point**: The sticky surface is the best time for seasoning to penetrate.
5. Increase to medium heat and fry the potato surface until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.
6. Add a little more oil to the pan. When the oil is hot, add the marinated steak strips. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat.
   **Key point**: Quick frying over medium-high heat ensures the surface of the steak is seared quickly.
7. When the steak is cooked and the surface shows a browned color, add the potato strips back in and mix together.
8. Drizzle in oyster sauce and sprinkle with additional black pepper. Stir-fry quickly and evenly, then serve.

## Health Tips
The combination of steak and potatoes provides high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for muscle building or energy replenishment. It is recommended to control the amount of oyster sauce used to reduce sodium intake. Blanching the potatoes before frying can reduce oil absorption.
