# Kung Pao Potato Cubes
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 2 medium-sized potatoes (cut into cubes about 1.5 cm)
🥒 Half a cucumber (seeded and diced)
🥕 Half a carrot (diced)
🥜 A handful of peanuts (pre-fried and set aside)
🌶️ 5-8 dried chilies (cut into sections)
🧅 1 stalk of scallion (diced)
🧄 3-4 cloves of garlic (sliced)
🌿 A small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns

**Coating and Sauce**
🌽 1 tbsp cornstarch (for coating potato cubes)
🧂 1 tbsp sugar
🧂 1 tbsp fragrant vinegar
🧂 Half tbsp light soy sauce
🧂 A small amount of dark soy sauce (for color)
🧂 A pinch of salt
🥔 1 tbsp potato starch (for sauce thickening)

**Other Seasonings**
🫘 About 1 tbsp doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
🛢️ Appropriate amount of cooking oil
🛢️ A small amount of seasoning oil (drizzled before serving to enhance brightness and aroma)

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🍲 Steamer
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Bowls
💨 Oil spray bottle
🥢 Spatula

## Steps
1. Peel and dice the potatoes, spread them flat on a steaming tray, and spray an even layer of cooking oil on the surface to lock in moisture. Once the steamer water boils and steam rises, steam over high heat for 5 minutes, until a bamboo skewer can easily pierce through. Remove and immediately plunge into cold water to cool, then drain.
**Note**: Do not over-steam; cooling in cold water makes the texture more delicate.

2. Halve the cucumber lengthwise, remove the inner seeds, and cut into small cubes. Also dice the carrot and set aside.
**Note**: Removing the cucumber seeds ensures a crisp texture.

3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tbsp fragrant vinegar, half tbsp light soy sauce, a pinch of salt, a small amount of dark soy sauce, and 1 tbsp potato starch. Stir thoroughly until no dry starch lumps remain.
**Key**: The sauce must be mixed in advance to ensure the starch is fully dissolved.

4. Place the drained potato cubes in a bowl, add 1 tbsp cornstarch, and toss with your hands to evenly coat each cube with a thin layer of starch.
5. Heat enough cooking oil in a wok to 50% heat, add the coated potato cubes, and fry over the lowest heat, turning occasionally, until all sides are golden and the surface is crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.
**Key**: Use low heat to prevent burning on the outside while remaining raw inside.

6. Add the cucumber and carrot cubes to the wok, stir-fry over medium heat until just cooked, then remove together with the fried potato cubes and set aside.
7. Leave a base oil in the wok, add dried chili sections, diced scallion, sliced garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
8. Add 1 tbsp doubanjiang and continue stir-frying over low heat until the red oil is fully released.
**Key**: The doubanjiang must be stir-fried until the red oil appears for the best aroma.

9. Stir the prepared sauce again, pour it into the wok, and simmer over low heat until the sauce thickens and bubbles.
10. Add the fried potato cubes, vegetables, and pre-fried peanuts, then turn to high heat and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat all ingredients with the sauce.
11. Drizzle a small amount of seasoning oil along the edge of the wok, toss quickly, then serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish replaces deep-frying with steaming and shallow frying, reducing its oil content. However, the sweet and sour sauce and peanuts still contain some calories. It is suitable for those with poor appetite who wish to avoid greasy food, but attention should be paid to controlling overall carbohydrate intake.
