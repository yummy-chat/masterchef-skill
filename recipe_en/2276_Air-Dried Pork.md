# Air-Dried Pork
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork front leg or hind leg meat (lean only), as needed (do not use tenderloin)  
🫚 Ginger slices, as needed  
🍶 Rice wine, as needed  
🧂 Table salt, 1 tablespoon  
🌿 Five-spice powder, 1 tablespoon  
🌶️ Chili powder, a pinch (optional; omit for five-spice flavor)  
🫑 Green Sichuan peppercorns, a pinch (optional; omit for five-spice flavor)  
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon (add last after mixing to enhance freshness)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Airtight container or bowl  
🧤 Gloves  
🔗 Drying rack (oven rack)  
♨️ Oven or dehydrator  
🌀 Fan (alternative tool if no machine is available)  
🍲 Steamer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Wash the pork, slice against the grain into thick pieces or long strips, and place in an airtight container.  
2. Add ginger slices, rice wine, salt, five-spice powder, and optionally chili powder and green Sichuan peppercorns.  
   **Note**: Wearing gloves, repeatedly knead and mix for 5-10 minutes to thoroughly infuse the seasonings into the meat. Finally, add white sugar and continue mixing evenly to enhance freshness.  
3. Marinate for 5-6 hours until the meat is fully flavored.  
4. Spread the marinated meat strips flat on a drying rack, avoiding overlap.  
   - **Machine Drying**: Use an oven or dehydrator set at 35-45°C (95-113°F) and dry at low temperature for over 12 hours.  
   - **Air Drying**: Use an electric fan to blow directly for 2-3 days; this must be done in cold weather to prevent spoilage.  
   - **Doneness Check**: The meat is ready when the surface is dry and firm to the touch.  
5. Bring water to a boil in a steamer, place the air-dried meat inside, and steam on high heat for 35 minutes to ensure it is fully cooked.  
6. Remove and serve immediately. The meat is reddish in color, chewy and stringy in texture, and becomes more flavorful with each bite.  

## Health Advice  
This air-dried pork is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or for replenishing energy in winter. However, it is high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to balance the taste and avoid overeating.
