# Egg Zhajiang Noodles (Fried Sauce Noodles)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Cucumber, to taste (julienned, optional)
🌶️ Spiral chili peppers, 100 grams (about 5 thin or 3 thick, cut into rolling knife pieces or rings)
🌶️ Red chili peppers, 20 grams (for garnish, optional; for spiciness, substitute with bird's eye chili)
🧅 Scallions, 60 grams (about a palm-length section, chopped)
🥚 Eggs, 4 pieces
🫘 Soybean paste, 100 grams (about 4 heaping tablespoons, choose a slightly sweet variety)
🦪 Oyster sauce, 20 grams (about 1 heaping tablespoon)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 10 grams (about 1 tablespoon)
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 7 grams (about 2/3 tablespoon)
🍶 Aged vinegar, 5 grams (about 1/2 tablespoon)
🍬 Sugar, 3 grams (about 1 teaspoon)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, 1 gram (about 1/2 teaspoon, optional)
🧂 MSG or chicken bouillon, 3 grams (about 1 teaspoon)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying eggs)
💧 Water, roughly equal in volume to the sauce

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (multiple)
🥘 Small stainless steel pot
🥄 Soup spoon or spatula
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥢 Wooden chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the spiral chili peppers into rolling knife pieces or rings, slice the red chili peppers into rings, split the scallions lengthwise into four sections and then chop, and julienne the cucumber.
2. In a bowl, combine the soybean paste, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, aged vinegar, sugar, thirteen-spice powder, and MSG, and stir thoroughly to mix into a sauce.
3. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat until the mixture is even and frothy. Prepare just before use to avoid drying out.
4. Place the wok over high heat, pour in enough cooking oil, and heat until the oil surface is noticeably smoking.
5. Reduce the heat to low or turn off the heat, and slowly pour the egg mixture into the wok in a thin stream while quickly stirring in one direction with chopsticks to form silky shreds of egg.
6. Once the egg is mostly set, add the chili peppers and scallions, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant and the peppers are slightly softened.
7. Pour the prepared sauce into the wok. Do not stir immediately. Then, use the bowl that held the sauce to measure an equal amount of water and set aside.
8. Stir-fry the sauce until aromatic and the color is even, then pour in the reserved water and continue to stir until combined.
9. Turn the heat to medium-high and reduce the sauce, cooking until it thickens slightly, then turn off the heat. Avoid over-reducing.
   **Key point**: Adding water is the essential step for creating a savory, meaty depth in the sauce.
10. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and cook noodles of your choice. Once cooked, drain and immediately rinse under cold water, then drain well and place in serving bowls.
    **Note**: Rinsing with cold water removes surface starch, prevents sticking, and results in a more refreshing texture.
11. Top the noodles with cucumber shreds and ladle the prepared egg sauce over them. Mix well before eating.

## Health Advice
Egg zhajiang noodles are rich in protein, and the cucumber shreds help balance the richness, making them suitable for a quick weekday lunch. Note that soybean paste is high in salt; those with hypertension should reduce the amount. Draining the noodles well after rinsing with cold water can help moderate the glycemic response.
