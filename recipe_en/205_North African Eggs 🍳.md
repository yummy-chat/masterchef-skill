# North African Eggs 🍳
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef, about half a bowl
🍅 Oil-cured tomatoes, 1 tablespoon (small tomatoes, garlic, and herbs pre-soaked in oil and roasted)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🥖 Baguette, 1 small loaf (cut into large chunks)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Frying pan with lid
🔪 Knife
🍽️ Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Spoon
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Prepare the oil-cured tomatoes the night before.
2. Add a small amount of oil to the frying pan. Add the ground beef and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the meat changes color.
3. Add 1 tablespoon of oil-cured tomatoes along with their juices. Mix well with the ground beef and cook until the sauce is just simmering.
4. Gently crack two eggs on top of the tomato and meat mixture, keeping the yolks intact and unbroken.
5. Cover the pan with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer until the egg whites are fully set, with the yolks still runny.
6. Turn off the heat. Insert the cut baguette chunks into the pan around the eggs in a circle. Dip directly into the sauce and egg yolk when eating.

## Health Tips
This North African egg dish is rich in protein and lycopene, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those managing blood sugar as a brunch option. However, note that the ground beef and oil-cured tomatoes are not low in fat, so it is recommended to control the amount of bread dipped in the sauce.
