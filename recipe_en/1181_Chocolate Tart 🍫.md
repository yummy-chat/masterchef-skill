# Chocolate Tart 🍫
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍪 Caramel cookies 1 pack (or use Oreo, digestive biscuits, etc.)
🍫 Dark chocolate approx. 145g (130g for tart base sauce, about 15g for filling)
🥛 Milk approx. 60g
🥜 Crushed nuts as needed
🌾 Oatmeal approx. 20g (optional)
🈚 No additional seasonings (sweetness comes from cookies and chocolate itself)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🎒 Plastic bag + 🥢 Rolling pin (for crushing cookies)
🥣 Bowl 2 or more
🧴 Cut plastic bottle (as a measuring cup for milk)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)
♨️ Large bowl with hot water (for double-boiling chocolate)
📄 Plastic wrap/cling film (non-stick mat)
🥄 Spoon (for shaping assistance)
🛍️ Piping bag or ziplock bag (for squeezing filling)

## Instructions
1. 🍪 Put the cookies into a plastic bag, crush them into fine crumbs using a rolling pin, then pour into a bowl. Add crushed nuts and oatmeal, and mix well.
2. 🍫 Break 130g dark chocolate into small pieces, place in a bowl with milk, then set the bowl over a basin of hot water to double-boil. Stir continuously until fully melted into a smooth chocolate sauce.
   **Note**: Must use indirect heat to avoid burning the chocolate; stir until no lumps remain to ensure a silky texture.
3. 🥣 Pour the warm chocolate sauce into the cookie crumbs, stir thoroughly with chopsticks until all dry ingredients are evenly coated. The mixture should be easy to form into a ball without sticking to your hands.
   **Note**: If the mixture is too wet, add a small amount of extra cookie crumbs; different cookies have varying oil absorption, so adjust as needed.
4. ✋ Take an appropriate amount of the mixture, roll into a ball, then press and rotate with your thumb to form a deep indentation in the center. Shape into small bowl-like tart bases, and place them one by one on a surface lined with plastic wrap.
5. 🛍️ Melt the remaining 15g dark chocolate, transfer to a piping bag, snip a small opening at the tip, and quickly fill the indentation of each tart base.
   **Note**: When room temperature is low, chocolate may harden easily; work quickly for the filling step. If you don't have a piping bag, use the corner of a ziplock bag instead.
6. ⏳ Let the finished chocolate tarts rest at room temperature until the filling sets from liquid to semi-solid, then they are ready to eat.

## Health Tips 💡
This no-bake chocolate tart's sugar primarily comes from cookies and chocolate, with almost no fiber or protein to buffer it, potentially causing a rapid spike in blood sugar. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened yogurt or fruit, and use the size of a palm as a portion control guideline.
