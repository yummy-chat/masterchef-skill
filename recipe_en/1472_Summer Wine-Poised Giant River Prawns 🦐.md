# Summer Wine-Poised Giant River Prawns 🦐
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦐 750g giant river prawns (live, with rich roe)
🫚 A small piece of ginger (about 3-4 slices)
🧅 A small bunch of scallions (about 2-3 stalks, cut into sections)
🍋 1 lemon (sliced and deseeded)
🌿 2 star anise
🌿 1 cinnamon stick
🌿 2 bay leaves
🌿 About 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
🌿 About 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste
🧂 320ml light soy sauce
🧂 30ml dark soy sauce
🍬 200g rock sugar
🍶 500ml Huadiao wine
🥃 50ml brandy (optional)
🍊 2 pieces dried tangerine peel
🍑 5 preserved plums (sour plums)
🫙 Wasabi, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for blanching)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Large stockpot
🥘 Snow pear pot (or small saucepan)
🥣 Glass bowl/container
📦 Airtight storage container

## Steps
1.  **Prepare ingredients:** Rinse the giant river prawns in clean water. Slice the ginger, cut the scallions into sections, and slice and deseed the lemon. Prepare the dried spices: star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, and dried chili peppers.
2.  **Cook the prawns:** Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add ginger slices and cooking wine. When the water returns to a boil, add the giant river prawns and cook for about 2–3 minutes, until the shells turn red and they curl up. Immediately remove and place in a glass bowl to cool naturally.
    **Key point:** Once cooled, you can trim the prawns' antennae and legs to save marinating space.
3.  **Make the cooking liquor:** Place a snow pear pot over heat. Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, and dried chili peppers. Dry-toast over low heat, stirring gently, until fragrant. Then add rock sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, scallion sections, ginger slices, dried tangerine peel, preserved plums, and wasabi. Simmer over low heat for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
    **Key point:** Immediately after turning off the heat, **remove the pot from the burner**. Then pour in the Huadiao wine and brandy to prevent excessive alcohol evaporation. Finally, add the lemon slices and let the mixture cool completely.
4.  **Combine and marinate:** Once both the marinade liquid and the prawns have cooled completely to room temperature, place the prawns in an airtight container. Pour the cooled marinade over them, ensuring they are fully submerged. Seal the container and refrigerate for 24 hours.
    **Key point:** Do not marinate for too long, or it will become too salty. It is perfectly flavorful after one day. The remaining marinade, if kept hygienic, can be reused for other seafood.

## Health Advice
Roe-rich drunken prawns are a good source of high-quality protein and zinc, making them an excellent summer appetizer. However, they are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should limit their intake. It is recommended to pair them with a mixed grain rice and a side of lightly stir-fried seasonal vegetables. The optimal marinating time is 24 hours.
