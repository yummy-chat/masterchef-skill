# Black Diamond Toast  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 469g  
🌾 Bread flour 650g  
🌾 Cake flour 194g  
🍫 Cocoa powder 37g  
🥛 Milk powder 21g  
🍬 Caster sugar 78g (for dough)  
🍬 Caster sugar 175g (for cake)  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 65g (for dough, softened at room temperature)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 38g (for cake batter)  
🍫 Dark chocolate 100g (65% cocoa solids, for melting)  
🍫 Dark chocolate chips approx. 75g (for surface decoration)  
🫧 Fresh yeast 19g (or dry yeast 6.4g)  
🥛 Milk 450g (for dough, ice-cold milk recommended)  
🥛 Milk 144g (for cake batter)  
🥛 Whipping cream 50g  
🧈 Butter a little (for piping lines on surface)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍞 Toast mold (250g)  
🥣 Stand mixer (with dough hook and whisk attachment)  
🔪 Spatula  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer  
🔥 Oven (with proofing function)  
📜 Baking parchment  
🔲 Dough scraper  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🧁 Sieve  
🫕 Double boiler or heatproof bowl set over simmering water  
🥄 Piping bag or spoon  

## Instructions  
### Preparation  
- Accurately weigh all ingredients and set aside.  
- Soften the unsalted butter for the dough at room temperature; set aside the unsalted butter and milk for the cake batter. Set aside 100g dark chocolate for melting, and crush about 75g into crumbs.  
- Sift the cake flour and cocoa powder together twice and set aside.  
- Spray the inside of the toast mold with release oil or grease with butter, then line with baking parchment for easy removal.  

### Dough Part  
1. Place ice-cold milk, whipping cream, caster sugar, fresh yeast, bread flour, milk powder, and salt into the mixing bowl.  
   **Note**: In summer, use ice-cold milk to control dough temperature.  
2. Mix on low speed for about 3 minutes until combined, then switch to high speed for about 10 minutes, until a thick membrane forms with jagged edges when torn.  
3. Add the softened unsalted butter, mix on low speed for 3 minutes until incorporated, then switch to high speed for 2–4 minutes, until a thin, elastic windowpane membrane forms. Dough temperature should be around 24°C, not exceeding 26°C.  
4. Shape the dough into a ball and proof at room temperature for 40 minutes.  
5. Divide the dough into approximately 130g pieces; first work with half, and refrigerate the other half to prevent over-proofing. Shape each piece into a ball.  
6. After resting for 20 minutes, roll each piece into a long shape, thin the bottom edge, roll it up tightly from top to bottom into a cylinder, and place seam side down.  

### Cake Batter Part  
7. While the dough is resting, place the whole egg liquid and caster sugar into the mixing bowl, and warm over a double boiler to 46–47°C, making sure not to exceed 50°C.  
8. Melt the milk and the unsalted butter for the cake over a double boiler, add 100g dark chocolate, and stir using residual heat until smooth to make a chocolate mixture.  
9. Whip the warmed egg mixture with the whisk attachment on high speed for 6–7 minutes, until it expands, turns pale, and forms distinct ribbons; when you lift the whisk, the batter should hold a figure-eight shape for a few seconds. Then switch to low speed for 3 minutes to refine the bubbles.  
10. Pour the chocolate mixture (cooled to 40–50°C) into the egg batter, and mix on low speed or fold by hand with a spatula for about 1 minute until roughly combined.  
11. Sift the sifted cake flour and cocoa powder mixture all at once into the batter, and fold gently with a spatula about 50 times until no dry flour remains. Be careful not to overmix.  

### Assembly & Baking  
12. Place the rested and shaped dough in the center of the parchment-lined mold.  
13. Pipe or spoon the cake batter into the mold to cover the dough, about 210g per mold, filling to 80% full.  
14. Sprinkle chocolate chips on top. You can pipe a thin line of butter along the center of the dough to encourage a nice crack during baking.  
15. Bake at top heat 160°C, bottom heat 200°C, for about 39 minutes, until the cake is fully baked, the surface cracks open, and color is even.  
    **Note**: Baking time should be adjusted according to your oven’s actual performance.  
16. After baking, lift the parchment to remove the toast from the mold, and cool on a wire rack.  

## Health Advice  
This Black Diamond Toast is relatively high in calorie density. It is suitable for consumption before or after workouts, or when energy expenditure is high, but should not be eaten in excess on a daily basis. The use of ice-cold milk in the dough and the precise whipping technique help control dough temperature. Beginners should strictly follow each step.
