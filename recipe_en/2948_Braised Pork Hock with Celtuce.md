# Braised Pork Hock with Celtuce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork hock approximately 1400g (lean meat removed from bone, bone chopped into small pieces)
🥬 Celtuce approximately 500g (peeled, cut into rolling chunks)
🌿 Scallion appropriate amount (white parts only)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount
🫘 Soybean paste approximately 50g (1 tablespoon)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce approximately 15g
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon a pinch

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥄 Ladle
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. Heat the wok, add cold oil to coat the surface, then add the pork hock pieces. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until the meat turns white, fat renders, and a meaty aroma is released.
2. Add scallion white sections, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, and soybean paste. Continue stir-frying until the sauce is fragrant.
3. Add enough water to cover, then season with dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, salt, cooking wine, and chicken bouillon. Stir to combine.
4. Add the cut celtuce chunks and mix well.
5. Transfer all ingredients into a pressure cooker and seal the lid tightly. Once steam is released, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 12 minutes.
   **Key point**: After turning off the heat, allow the pressure to release naturally. Do not manually release the pressure, as this may affect the texture and pose a safety hazard.
6. Once the pressure has naturally released, open the lid and serve directly.

## Health Tips
Pork hock is rich in collagen, while celtuce provides dietary fiber. Cooking together in a pressure cooker makes nutrients easier to absorb, but portion control is recommended. Suitable for those needing protein supplementation or concerned with joint health.
