# Supreme Meatballs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 500g (with alternating lean and fat)
🍞 White steamed bun 1 piece (peeled before use)
🥚 Egg 1 piece
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (finely minced)
🥄 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt half tablespoon
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (add gradually in small batches)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Deep bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Wooden chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)

## Cooking Steps
1. Finely mince the ginger and chop the pork belly into ground meat. Soak the steamed bun in clean water for 1 minute, then squeeze out the excess water and crumble it into small pieces. Mix thoroughly with the ground meat.
2. Crack the egg into the meat mixture and add light soy sauce, thirteen-spice powder, salt, and minced ginger. Mix well by hand. Then gradually add cornstarch in small batches while stirring continuously in one direction until the meat mixture becomes sticky, firm, and easy to shape.
   **Key Point**: Stirring until the mixture is firm gives the meatballs a dense and elastic texture.
3. Pour an adequate amount of cooking oil into the wok. Insert wooden chopsticks into the oil; when fine bubbles rapidly appear around them, reduce the heat to medium-low to maintain the oil temperature.
4. Take an appropriate amount of meat mixture in your left hand and squeeze it out between your thumb and index finger to form a meatball. Use your right hand to scoop it off and gently drop it into the oil. Fry over medium-low heat until all meatballs are evenly browned and turn a dark brown color, then remove and let cool.
   **Key Point**: Keep the meatballs uniform in size when forming them; smooth movements ensure round shapes.
5. Increase the heat to high to raise the oil temperature. Add the cooled meatballs for a second fry of about 30 seconds, then quickly remove and drain the oil.
   **Key Point**: The second fry is crucial for a crispy exterior; do not fry too long to avoid burning.
6. Arrange on a plate and serve. The finished product is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

## Health Advice
Pork belly has a high fat content, so it is recommended to pair the meatballs with a vegetable salad or a light soup to balance the oil. Control the oil temperature during frying and use the double-frying technique to reduce oil absorption. Suitable for an occasional indulgence, not as a regular staple.
