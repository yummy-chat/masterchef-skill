# Tomato Red Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meals  

🍅Ingredients  
- Ground beef 500g  
- Garlic cloves 2  
- Carrot half a piece  
- Celery one stalk  
- Onion half a piece  
- Tomato sauce 300g  
- Milk 300ml  

🧂Seasonings  
- Olive oil  
- Sugar 2g  
- Salt 10g  
- Black pepper 5g  
- Dried basil or thyme (optional)  

🍳Cookware  
- Frying pan or stew pot  
- Spatula  

📝Preparation Steps  
1. Finely chop the carrot, celery, and onion into small dices; slice the garlic cloves.  
2. Add 10 ml of olive oil to the pan, heat it up, then add the vegetable dices. Stir-fry over high heat until they slightly change color, then remove and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Do not over-stir the vegetables; maintain a crisp texture.  
3. Add another 10 ml of olive oil to the pan, add the sliced garlic and stir-fry for about 10 seconds. Then add the ground beef, sugar, salt, black pepper, and dried herbs. Stir-fry over high heat until the beef is crispy and granular.  
   **Note**: The ground beef must be broken up to avoid clumping; use less salt at this stage as the saltiness of the tomato sauce varies.  
4. Return the cooked vegetable dices to the pan, add the tomato sauce, and continue stir-frying until evenly mixed.  
5. Slowly pour in the milk in several batches, stirring well after each addition. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. When the sauce thickens to a rich consistency, turn off the heat and serve.  
   **Key point**: Add the milk gradually to avoid thinning the sauce; stir frequently during the later simmering stage to prevent burning. The salt content of different tomato sauce brands varies; taste and adjust salt before serving. This sauce can be frozen and reheated before use.  

## Health Advice
This tomato red sauce is rich in protein and lycopene, making it suitable for people needing iron supplementation or immune support, but salt intake should be controlled. It is recommended to pair with whole wheat pasta for a low-fat, satisfying, and healthier meal.
