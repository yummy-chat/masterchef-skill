# Fresh Pepper Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Silver carp, 2 fish (chop bones and head into pieces, slice fillets into thin slices)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili peppers, to taste (cut into rings)
🌶️ Red chili peppers, to taste (cut into rings)
🫚 Young ginger, to taste (cut into shreds)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers, to taste (cut into rings)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (some cut into sections, some minced)
🧄 Ginger, to taste (minced)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🌾 Potato starch or cornstarch, to taste
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste), about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Sichuan pepper oil, to taste (drizzle before serving)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🪣 Large basin
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Large serving bowl
🥢 Chopsticks, bowls

## Cooking Steps
1. Stun the live fish, scrape off the scales, remove the internal organs, and rinse thoroughly with clean water.
2. Fillet both sides of the fish along the backbone, then slice diagonally into evenly thick pieces. Chop the fish bones and head into chunks and set aside.
3. Add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, and cooking wine to the fish fillets. Stir vigorously in one direction until sticky, then add starch and continue to mix until evenly coated.
**Key point**: Stir thoroughly so the fillets absorb the seasoning and are evenly coated with starch.
4. Cut the Erjingtiao chili peppers and red chili peppers into rings. Cut the young ginger into shreds, the bird's eye chili peppers into rings, the scallions into sections and mince some, and mince the ginger and garlic. Place each in separate dishes and set aside.
5. Heat an appropriate amount of cooking oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and stir until fragrant. Then add the minced scallions, ginger, garlic, and doubanjiang. Reduce to medium-low heat and stir-fry until the red oil is released and the aroma is rich.
**Key point**: The doubanjiang must be stir-fried until the red oil separates, which makes the base flavorful.
6. Add the young ginger shreds, bird's eye chili peppers, Erjingtiao chili rings, and red chili rings. Stir-fry quickly until just cooked, being careful not to overcook to maintain their crisp texture.
7. Pour in enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, and season with salt and chicken bouillon.
8. Once the broth is boiling, first add the fish bones and head. Cook for about 2-3 minutes until just done, then remove with a slotted spoon and place at the bottom of a large serving bowl.
9. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Gently slide the marinated fish fillets into the pot one by one, using the back of a spoon to gently push them apart to prevent sticking. After about 1 minute, when the fillets turn white and curl, add the remaining fresh chili peppers and young ginger shreds.
**Key point**: The broth should not be boiling vigorously when adding the fillets, to avoid washing off the starch. The fillets are done as soon as they turn white; do not cook too long to prevent them from becoming tough.
10. Before serving, drizzle in the Sichuan pepper oil, then pour everything, including the broth, into the bowl over the fish bones. Serve immediately while hot.
**Key point**: Add the Sichuan pepper oil at the very end to preserve its numbing aroma.

## Health Advice
This fresh pepper fish is rich in high-quality protein and a small amount of healthy fats, making it suitable for muscle building and weight loss. Be mindful of the oil and salt content in the broth; those with high blood pressure should consume the broth sparingly. The tender texture of the fish fillets relies on starch coating, but diabetics should manage their carbohydrate intake accordingly.
