# Lucky Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Young hen (never laid eggs) approx. 2.8-3 jin (choose one with plump tail fat)  
🧅 Red shallots a small amount (minced)  
🫚 Ginger a small amount (minced)  
🫘 Fermented red bean curd half a piece (mashed)  
🥣 Hoisin sauce two tablespoons  
🌿 Five-spice powder a small amount  
🍬 White sugar a small amount (for marinade + crispy skin water)  
🫘 Chu hou paste appropriate amount (diluted with water, key to enhancing flavor layers)  
💧 Water appropriate amount (to dilute chu hou paste)  
🍶 White vinegar 250 grams (about half a bottle)  
🍇 Red Zhejiang vinegar 100 grams  
🍯 Maltose appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍽️ White deep dish  
🥣 Various small bowls  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🪡 Long needle (with thread)  
🪝 Stainless steel hook  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🍲 Large soup pot (for deep frying oil)  
🥄 Large soup ladle  
🌀 Electric fan  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Rinse the chicken inside and out until clean, then thoroughly dry all moisture with kitchen paper towels.  
2. Mix the minced red shallots, minced ginger, mashed fermented red bean curd, hoisin sauce, five-spice powder, white sugar, and diluted chu hou paste into a marinade.  
3. Evenly spread the marinade all over the chicken's body and cavity, gently massaging to let the flavors penetrate.  
4. Use a long needle to sew up the tail and neck openings to prevent juice from leaking out.  
5. Pass the stainless steel hook through the skin and meat just below the chicken's neck, securing it firmly.  
6. Boil a pot of water, then use a spoon to ladle the boiling water evenly over the chicken skin, causing it to tighten and shrink quickly.  
7. Again use kitchen paper towels to absorb all moisture from the chicken.  
8. In a basin, mix white vinegar, red Zhejiang vinegar, and maltose, stirring until combined into a crispy skin water.  
9. Repeatedly and evenly pour the crispy skin water over the chicken, especially under the wings and on the inner thighs.  
10. Hang the chicken in a ventilated area and blow dry with an electric fan for at least 2 hours, until the skin feels completely dry, tight, and non-sticky to the touch.  
**Key**: Drying must be thorough, otherwise the skin won't be crispy.  

11. Pour ample oil into the large soup pot, heat to 100-110°C, and maintain a low heat.  
12. Place the dried chicken in the oil, continuously using a spoon to ladle hot oil over the chicken, and deep-fry for 25-28 minutes.  
**Note**: Keep the temperature low throughout, allowing the hot oil to slowly penetrate and cook the chicken through, avoiding a burnt exterior and raw interior.  

13. Once the chicken turns an even jujube red or amber color, and the skin is crispy with small bubbles, it is ready to be removed.  
14. Lift it out to drain the oil, then cut into pieces or tear by hand to serve.  

## Health Advice  
This dish uses fermented red bean curd, chu hou paste, and maltose for multi-layered coloring, offering a rich flavor, but it is high in oil and sugar, suitable for occasional gatherings; those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation.
