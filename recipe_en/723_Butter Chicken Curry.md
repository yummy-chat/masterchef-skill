# Butter Chicken Curry  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thighs 400g (boneless, skinless, cut into small pieces)  
🧅 White onions 3 (diced)  
🥜 Cashew nuts 100g  
🍅 Canned diced tomatoes 1 can  
🥛 Sour cream / unsweetened Greek yogurt as needed  
🌿 Fresh cilantro leaves as needed  
🍚 Basmati rice as needed (for serving)  
🫓 Indian naan bread as needed (for serving)  

🧂 Garam masala powder as needed  
🧂 Turmeric powder as needed  
🧂 Cumin powder as needed  
🌶️ Indian chili powder as needed (mild heat, add to taste)  
🧂 Salt as needed  
🍬 Sugar as needed  
🍋 Lemon juice as needed  
🧈 Butter as needed (added in two batches)  
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed  
💧 Water as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥤 Blender  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Remove bones and skin from chicken thighs and cut into small pieces.  
   **Key point**: Removing the skin prevents the sauce from becoming too greasy.  
2. Place chicken pieces in a bowl. Add ginger paste, garlic paste, garam masala powder, Indian chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, lemon juice, and salt. Finally add sour cream and mix well with your hands. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight.  
   **Key point**: Sour cream locks in moisture and makes the chicken more tender.  
3. Place a non-stick frying pan on a cold stove. Add vegetable oil and a small piece of butter. Turn on the heat and once melted, add diced onions and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Then add minced garlic and stir until fragrant.  
4. Add garam masala powder, turmeric powder, and cumin powder. Stir until aromatic.  
5. Pour in the canned diced tomatoes, some water, and cashew nuts. Reduce heat to low and cook until the cashews become soft.  
   **Key point**: Cashews are key to making the sauce thick and creamy.  
6. Pour the cooked sauce into a blender and blend until smooth and silky.  
7. Heat oil in another pan over high heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and fry until both sides have a distinct golden-brown sear.  
   **Key point**: High-heat searing mimics the smoky flavor of a traditional Indian tandoor.  
8. Pour the blended sauce back into the pan with the chicken. Mix well to combine. Cover with a lid and simmer over low heat until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken evenly.  
9. Adjust seasoning with salt and sugar to taste.  
10. Turn off the heat. Add a small piece of cold butter and stir until melted and incorporated using residual heat.  
    **Key point**: Adding cold butter after turning off the heat preserves its fresh, buttery aroma.  
11. Serve on a plate. Drizzle a few drops of heavy cream on top and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.  

## Health Tips  
This butter chicken curry uses yogurt-marinated chicken and cashews for thickening, replacing cream for a healthier option. It is high in protein and contains good-quality fats. Serve with brown rice or whole wheat naan, and moderate the amounts of cashews and butter. Suitable for muscle-building diets; those on a weight-loss plan may reduce the portion of rice.
