# Coix Seed and Adzuki Bean Water  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Coix seeds (Job's tears), about 3 tablespoons  
🫘 Adzuki beans (red small beans), about 3 tablespoons  
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking, rinsing, and boiling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🫙 Glass jar (for storing raw ingredients)  
🥄 Wooden spoon (for scooping ingredients)  
🍳 Flat-bottom pan (for dry-roasting coix seeds)  
🍲 Cooking pot (for boiling the water)  
🥛 Glass cup (for serving the finished drink)

## Cooking Steps  
1. Take about 3 tablespoons of coix seeds and 3 tablespoons of adzuki beans, placing each in separate bowls.  
   **Key point**: Use equal proportions.

2. Add water to the bowls and soak for 20 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.  
   **Key point**: Ensure sufficient soaking time and rinse well.

3. Place the rinsed and drained coix seeds into a dry flat-bottom pan. Dry-roast over low heat for 5–6 minutes, until the seeds turn slightly yellow and release a fragrant aroma.  
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning.

4. Bring water to a boil in a cooking pot. Once boiling, add the dry-roasted coix seeds and the soaked, rinsed adzuki beans. Boil over high heat for 15 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Add the ingredients only after the water has reached a rolling boil.

5. After boiling for 15 minutes, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let it steep for 15 minutes to soften the ingredients and enhance the flavor.  
   **Key point**: Allow enough steeping time after turning off the heat to fully soften the ingredients.

## Health Advice  
Coix seed and adzuki bean water is a classic summer remedy for dispelling dampness and reducing swelling, suitable for those with damp-heat constitution or a tendency to edema. Note that adzuki beans are different from red beans; this drink should not be cooked into a sweet porridge and eaten as a staple. For those with a cold constitution, add two slices of ginger to balance the cool nature.
