# Traditional Old-Style Guobaorou (Double-Cooked Pork Slices)
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork tenderloin 300g  
🌿 Scallion 50g  
🧅 Ginger 30g  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves  
🥕 Carrot 10g (optional)  
🌿 Cilantro 10g  
🧂 Cooking wine 5ml  
🧪 White vinegar 55g (recommended 9° acidity)  
🍬 White sugar 65g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🧂 Light soy sauce 4g  
🧂 MSG (a pinch)  
🧪 Baking soda 5g (optional)  
🌰 White sesame seeds 5g (optional)  
🧂 Potato starch 170g (Method 1) or 210g (Method 2)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 70g (Method 2)  
🫒 Cooking oil (approx. 3-10ml for batter)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍲 Boiling pot or deep frying pot  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Strainer  
🥣 Bowls, basin  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process the Pork**
   Cut the pork tenderloin into 8mm thick slices. Remove white connective tissue. Rinse with water to remove blood. For a more tender texture, add 5g baking soda, mix well, and let sit for 5 minutes, then rinse 1-2 times with water to remove excess baking soda.
2. **Marinate the Slices**
   Add 3g salt and 5ml cooking wine to the pork slices. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes.
3. **Prepare the Batter**
   **Method 1**:
   Add the 170g of potato starch to an excess amount of water and stir well. Let sit for 60 minutes until it settles into layers. Pour off all the clear water on top, retaining the starch paste at the bottom. Add the marinated pork slices, 5ml water, and 3ml cooking oil. Mix well until each slice is evenly coated.
**Method 2**:
   Mix 210g potato starch with 70g all-purpose flour. Gradually add water, stirring until the mixture reaches a yogurt-like consistency. Add 10ml cooking oil and 1g baking soda, and stir well. Then add the marinated pork slices and mix until each slice is evenly coated.
4. **Prepare the Sweet and Sour Sauce**
   Mix 65g white sugar, 55g white vinegar, 4g light soy sauce, and 2g salt. Stir well and set aside. For a thicker sauce, you can additionally mix 4g water with 1.5g cornstarch and stir it in.
5. **Prepare the Garnishes**
   Shred the scallion, ginger, and carrot. Cut the cilantro into sections. Slice the garlic.
6. **Fry the Pork Slices**
   **First Fry**: Heat oil to 170°C. Add the slices one by one. Fry until the surface is set, the batter no longer sticks to chopsticks, the meat floats, and the color is light yellow. Remove.
   **Second Fry**: Raise oil temperature to 200°C. Fry all slices again until the outer shell is golden and crispy. Remove.
   **Third Fry**: Raise oil temperature to 220°C. Quickly fry again until the slices are a reddish-golden color. Remove and drain excess oil.
7. **Stir-Fry**
   Leave about 20ml of oil in the wok. Pour in the prepared sweet and sour sauce. Heat over high heat until it boils and thickens to a syrupy consistency. You may remove 1/2 of the sauce for later use. Add the fried pork slices, shredded scallion, sliced garlic, shredded carrot, and cilantro sections. Pour the reserved sauce over them. Quickly stir-fry 3 times, ensuring the sauce evenly coats all ingredients.
8. **Plate and Serve**
   Transfer to a plate. Optionally sprinkle with white sesame seeds. Serve immediately.
**Key Tips**: The pork slices must be exactly 8mm thick; too thick or too thin will affect the texture. Strictly control the oil temperature during frying to avoid a non-crispy crust (too low) or burning (too high). The sauce should be boiled until thick but not crystallized into a sugar syrup; watch the heat carefully.

## Health Advice
This Guobaorou is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. It is rich in protein and carbohydrates, but high in fat and sugar. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance intake and control portion sizes to avoid excess calories.
