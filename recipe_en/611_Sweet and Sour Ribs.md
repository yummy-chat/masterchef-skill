# Sweet and Sour Ribs
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork ribs
🍬 White sugar
🫒 Cooking oil
🧂 Light soy sauce
🦪 Oyster sauce
🧂 Dark soy sauce
🧂 Chicken bouillon
🧅 Ginger slices
🌰 Sesame seeds
🥫 Ketchup
🧂 Rice vinegar
🌿 Five-spice powder

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the ribs and ginger slices together in cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat until a large amount of foam appears, then reduce to medium heat and maintain a steady boil. Finally, lower to a simmer and blanch for 2–3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: After blanching, rinse the ribs with boiling water to prevent the meat from becoming tough due to cold water.

2. Rinse the ribs repeatedly with boiling water for 2–3 passes, thoroughly removing any blood foam. Drain well.

3. Pour enough cooking oil into a pot and heat to about 170°C (340°F). Add the ribs and fry for 3–5 minutes until the surface turns slightly golden brown. Remove and drain excess oil.
   **Key point**: Before frying, lightly dust with a small amount of dry starch to enhance crispiness.

4. In a separate clean pot over low heat, add 50ml hot water and 30g white sugar. Gently stir until the sugar is completely dissolved and turns a pale yellow.

5. Pour the fried ribs into the sugar water pot and quickly stir-fry for 30 seconds. Then add in order: 5ml rice vinegar, 5ml light soy sauce, 5ml oyster sauce, 2g chicken bouillon, 10g ketchup, and 0.5g five-spice powder. Continue stir-frying for 30 seconds to evenly coat the ribs with the seasoning. Then add boiling water until the ribs are just submerged.

6. Bring to a boil over high heat, add 5ml dark soy sauce for color, and quickly reduce the sauce. If the rib pieces are large, reduce to low heat and simmer for 5–10 minutes to let the flavor penetrate.

7. Transfer to a plate and sprinkle with 2g sesame seeds. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Sweet and sour ribs are rich in high-quality protein and calcium, but the frying process and sweet-and-sour sauce are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair them with stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition. This dish is best enjoyed as an occasional treat and not as a daily staple. Those with high blood sugar or on a weight loss diet should consume it in moderation.
