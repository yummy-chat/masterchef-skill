# Cold-Eating Rabbit 🐇  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Rabbit meat  
🧅 Small scallion  
🧅 Large scallion  
🧅 Onion  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers  
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper  
⭐ Star anise  
🪵 Cinnamon stick  
🍃 Bay leaves  
🌰 Sand ginger (Kaempferia galanga)  
🍚 White cardamom  
🌿 Fennel seeds  
🌱 White sesame seeds  
🧄 Garlic  
🧄 Ginger  

🧂 Salt  
🧂 MSG  
🫗 Oyster sauce  
🍶 Cooking wine  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
✂️ Scissors  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel and mince garlic and ginger into a paste; set aside. Clean star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, sand ginger, white cardamom, and fennel seeds; set aside.  
2. Cut dried chili peppers into segments about 2 cm long, rinse, and drain; set aside.  
3. Wash small scallion, large scallion, and onion; cut the onion into chunks; set aside.  
4. Chop the rabbit meat into cubes about 2 cm in size. Mix with salt, cooking wine, and MSG, then marinate for 15 minutes.  
5. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 40% hot (around 120°C). Add small scallion, large scallion, and onion. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Once the scallions and onion are slightly charred, remove them.  
6. Turn the heat to high to raise the oil temperature to about 80% hot (around 180°C). Add the rabbit meat, then reduce to medium-low heat and fry until the surface is slightly golden brown. Remove the meat.  
   ⚠️ Note: The oil must fully submerge the rabbit meat; if not, add more oil immediately.  
   ⚠️ Note: The oil must cover the rabbit meat; if it doesn’t, add more promptly.  

7. Raise the oil temperature, then add dried chili peppers, green Sichuan pepper, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, sand ginger, white cardamom, and fennel seeds. Reduce to low heat and fry until the chili segments become crispy.  
   ⚠️ Note: Chili peppers burn easily; maintain low heat throughout. Once most moisture has evaporated, proceed to the next step.  
   ⚠️ Note: Chili peppers scorch easily; use low heat and wait until the moisture is nearly gone before moving on.  

8. Return the fried rabbit meat to the wok. Add oyster sauce and stir-fry for a few minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.  
9. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in minced garlic, minced ginger, and white sesame seeds. Stir well to combine.  
10. Let the dish rest overnight to fully absorb the flavors for the best taste.  

## Health Tips  
This cold-eating rabbit dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it a satisfying treat for fitness and weight-loss enthusiasts. However, it’s spicy and oily, so consume in moderation; those with sensitive stomachs should pair it with plain congee. For the best flavor, refrigerate overnight before eating.
