# Sichuan Pepper Oil
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌶️ Red Sichuan Peppers: 4 parts  
🌿 Green Sichuan Peppers: 1 part  
🫚 Ginger Slices: to taste  
🫒 Cooking Oil: 4:1 ratio to total Sichuan peppers  
🫗 Sesame Oil: half a spoon  
🍶 High-proof Baijiu: half a spoon  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer  
🫙 Airtight Container  
🍲 Stockpot (for warming water)  

## Steps  
1. Mix the red and green Sichuan peppers in a 4:1 ratio. Rinse thoroughly with clean water 2-3 times until the water runs completely clear.  
   **Key point**: Wash away surface impurities fully to prevent a bitter taste in the finished product.  
2. Soak the cleaned peppers in 60°C (140°F) warm water for 2 minutes, allowing them to rehydrate and absorb enough moisture. Remove and drain.  
   **Key point**: This step prevents the peppers from burning too quickly during frying.  
3. Pour cooking oil into a wok, add ginger slices to the cold oil, then add the soaked peppers.  
   **Key point**: Starting with cold oil and heating slowly helps release the aroma fully and avoids burning.  
4. Maintain medium heat while frying, continuously cooking off the moisture from the peppers. When the oil surface stabilizes and steam noticeably reduces, add half a spoon of sesame oil.  
   **State check**: Add sesame oil when evenly sized fish-eye bubbles appear on the oil surface.  
5. Continue cooking over medium heat. Once the peppers and ginger slices are completely dried out and sound crisp when stirred, drizzle half a spoon of high-proof baijiu along the edge of the wok.  
   **State check**: Peppers and ginger slices begin to float and feel brittle to the touch.  
6. Observe the oil surface. When the peppers and ginger slices float entirely and the heat is just right, immediately turn off the heat. Pour the finished Sichuan pepper oil along with the solids into a clean container and seal quickly.  
   **Key point**: Turn off the heat decisively and seal fast to prevent aroma loss.  
7. Place the sealed container of Sichuan pepper oil into a steamer. Once steam rises, steam over medium heat for 30 minutes.  
   **Key point**: This secondary steaming allows the aromatic compounds in the peppers to fully integrate with the oil, resulting in a richer flavor.  
8. Remove and let cool naturally until no steam remains. Use a slotted spoon to strain out the peppers and ginger slices. Store the clear Sichuan pepper oil in a sealed jar. Use as needed.  

## Health Advice  
This Sichuan pepper oil is rich in pepper alkaloids and antioxidants, making it ideal for dressing cold dishes or enhancing noodles. However, it is high in calories, so it is recommended to use only 1-2 spoons per serving. Individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake to avoid excess sodium consumption.
