# Kitchen Candle Candy  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍬 White sugar, 3 small spoons  
💧 Water, as needed (just enough to cover the sugar)  
🧂 Baking soda, a pinch (do not overuse, or it will affect the taste)

## Utensils & Equipment  
🥄 Large iron spoon (used as container for boiling sugar and shaping)  
🥢 Wooden stick (for stirring and assisting shaping)  
🔥 Gas stove (provides heat source, use the lowest flame)  
🪵 Cutting board (for cooling the finished product)

## Steps  
1. Set the gas stove to the lowest flame. Place the large iron spoon over the heat, add 3 small spoons of white sugar and enough water to just cover the sugar.  
**Note**: Tools must remain clean and dry.

2. Heat undisturbed for about 1 minute until the sugar syrup boils. After boiling, you may slightly lift the spoon to prevent burning, and stir gently if necessary.  
**Note**: Keep the heat low to prevent the syrup from caramelizing.

3. Continue boiling over low heat for about 6 minutes, until the syrup darkens to a light yellow color, bubbles become smaller, and the texture thickens, with most of the water in the spoon evaporated.  
**Key point**: When the syrup is thick with no obvious moisture, remove it from heat immediately.

4. After removing from heat, quickly add a pinch of baking soda. Act fast.  
**Note**: It is better to use too little baking soda than too much; excess will cause the candy to taste bitter.

5. Immediately stir rapidly and continuously in a circular motion with the wooden stick to thoroughly mix the baking soda with the hot syrup, causing the candy mass to expand.

6. When you feel the edges of the candy mass slightly puffing up, stop stirring and allow it to naturally expand and set.

7. After setting, slowly pull the wooden stick upward extremely gently.  
**Note**: The stick must be pulled out very slowly, otherwise the candy mass will collapse instantly and fail to puff up.

8. Place the spoon back over the gas stove and rotate it over low heat to warm the bottom slightly, so the candy loosens for easy unmolding.

9. Place the unmolded candy on a cutting board to cool naturally. Once completely cooled, it will harden and set, yielding a crispy, porous texture.

## Health Advice  
Kitchen Candle Candy is high in sugar and calories, suitable for a small treat in winter to satisfy cravings. Avoid overconsumption to prevent blood sugar spikes or tooth decay. When making it, pay attention to heat control and the amount of baking soda to avoid bitterness or collapse. This recipe is best for patient sweet lovers.
