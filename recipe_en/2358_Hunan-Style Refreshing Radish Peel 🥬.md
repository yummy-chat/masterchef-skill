# Hunan-Style Refreshing Radish Peel 🥬  
Cuisine: Hunan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥬  
- White radish 10 jin (peel only, with a little flesh attached)  
- Ginger appropriate amount (shredded)  
- Garlic appropriate amount (sliced)  
- Small chili peppers (小米辣) appropriate amount (cut into rings)  
- Salt appropriate amount  
- White sugar 2 tbsp (used in two batches)  
- Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
- Aromatic vinegar or rice vinegar appropriate amount  
- Chinese liquor (白酒) one small bottle cap  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment 🔪  
- Knife  
- Chopping board  
- Stainless steel basin  
- Large ceramic bowl  
- Plastic wrap  

## Steps 📝  

1. Scrub the white radish's surface clean, cut off both ends, then section into pieces. Use a knife to shave off the radish peel, ensuring each piece has a little flesh attached—this is key for a crisp texture.  
2. Place the radish peel in a stainless steel basin, add an appropriate amount of salt and one tablespoon of white sugar. Toss well and marinate for 30 minutes to remove the pungent taste.  
3. After marinating until liquid releases, rinse the radish peel with cold boiled water or purified water, then squeeze out excess moisture by hand to allow better absorption of the sauce.  
4. Cut small chili peppers into rings, shred the ginger, and slice the garlic. Set aside.  
5. Place the squeezed-dry radish peel in a large ceramic bowl, add shredded ginger, garlic slices, and chili pepper rings.  
6. Add light soy sauce, aromatic vinegar, one tablespoon of white sugar, and one small bottle cap of Chinese liquor.  
   **Note**: Liquor enhances aroma and extends shelf life.  
7. Use a spoon to thoroughly mix all ingredients and seasonings until the sauce roughly covers the radish peel.  
8. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.  
   **Note**: The longer it marinates, the richer the flavor.  

## Health Tips 🥗  
Radish peel is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C, low in calories and high in water content, making it ideal as an appetizer during weight loss. Squeezing out moisture during marination enhances crispness, but due to high salt and vinegar, consume in moderation; those with sensitive stomachs should be cautious.
