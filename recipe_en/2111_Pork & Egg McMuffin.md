# Pork & Egg McMuffin

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐

## Steps
1. Cut the pork shoulder and pork tenderloin into small pieces, place them in a blender, add one egg white, curry powder, salt, black pepper, and cornstarch, then blend until smooth.
   **Key point**: The meat mixture does not need to be blended until very fine. Leave a little graininess for a better texture.
2. Remove the meat mixture, repeatedly throw it back and forth until it becomes firm. Divide into portions of about 80 grams each, roll into balls, and refrigerate so the patties become more elastic after cooking.
3. Make the omelet: Beat the eggs. Add a small amount of oil to a small saucepan, pour in the egg liquid in batches, stirring it inward constantly with a spatula to create folds. When the egg liquid is half-set, remove it. Pour in the remaining egg liquid, and when it is half-set, slide the previously cooked omelet back into the pan on top. Flip and cook until done, forming a round thick omelet.
4. Remove the refrigerated meatballs and press them into patties of even thickness using a round mold. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-low heat, and cook the patties until both sides are golden brown and fully cooked.
5. Split the English muffins in half, and spread butter evenly on the cut sides. In order, layer the omelet, a cheese slice, and the cooked meat patty, then top with the other muffin half.
6. Freeze storage and reheating: Wrap the sandwich tightly in baking paper and freeze it sealed. When ready to eat, first microwave for about 4 minutes using the defrost setting. Remove the wrapping, then place in an air fryer at 180°C for 5 minutes until the exterior is crispy.

## Health Advice
This Pork & Egg McMuffin is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those who work out or have a tight schedule. Pair it with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition and reduce sodium and saturated fat intake.
