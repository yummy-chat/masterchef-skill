# Pork and Preserved Vegetable Guokui (Chinese Pan-Baked Pastry)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥣 All-purpose flour 600g (for dough)  
🥣 All-purpose flour 30g (for oil paste)  
💧 Hot water 380ml (approximately 70°C)  
🥩 Pork hind leg 400g (minced)  
🥬 Preserved mustard greens 300g (soaked in advance, chopped)  
🧅 Scallions a handful  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for brushing surface)  
🤍 White sesame seeds appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt 6g (for dough)  
🧂 Salt 10g (for filling)  
🧂 Salt 2g (for oil paste)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder 3g (for filling)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder 2g (for oil paste)  
🌿 Five-spice powder 3g (for filling)  
🌿 Five-spice powder 2g (for oil paste)  
🥢 Light soy sauce 20g  
🥢 Dark soy sauce 10g  
🫒 Hot oil 30g (for aromatics)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Rolling pin  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🖌️ Brush  
🍳 Baking tray  
♨️ Oven (or flat pan)  
📦 Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix 600g all-purpose flour with 6g salt. Gradually add 380ml hot water (about 70°C), stirring into flocculent pieces before kneading into a smooth dough. If the dough is difficult to knead smooth, let it rest for a few minutes before kneading again. After kneading, brush the surface with a layer of cooking oil, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 1.5 hours.
2. Mince the pork hind leg into a paste. Soak and drain the preserved mustard greens in advance, then chop finely. Combine the minced pork, preserved mustard greens, and scallions. Add 10g salt, 3g Sichuan peppercorn powder, 3g five-spice powder, 20g light soy sauce, and 10g dark soy sauce. Stir vigorously in one direction until the mixture is well combined and firm. Let it sit to absorb the flavors.
3. Take 30g all-purpose flour and mix with 2g salt, 2g five-spice powder, and 2g Sichuan peppercorn powder. Heat 30g cooking oil until smoking, then pour it over the flour mixture. Quickly stir into a smooth oil paste to release the aroma.
4. Shape the rested dough into a long log and divide it into pieces of about 60g each.
5. Take one piece and roll it into a long strip. Evenly brush with the oil paste, roll it up from one end, pinch the ends tightly to prevent leakage, and press it flat vertically. Repeat for all pieces. Take the first flattened piece and roll it into a round wrapper. Add an appropriate amount of filling, then seal the edges tightly using a baozi-wrapping technique.
6. After filling all pieces, start with the first one and roll it out into a large, thin sheet, thin enough to vaguely see the filling through the dough. A few small tears are acceptable; the thinner the sheet, the crispier the result after baking.
7. Use the rolling pin to lift and transfer the dough sheet onto a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Evenly sprinkle white sesame seeds on the surface and press them firmly into the dough. Preheat the oven to 220°C, then bake for about 12 minutes until the surface is golden. If no oven is available, use a flat pan without oil, and cook over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown and fully cooked.

## Health Advice  
This Guokui is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables or soup, making it suitable for manual laborers who need to replenish iron and carbohydrates. Preserved mustard greens are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of filling accordingly.
