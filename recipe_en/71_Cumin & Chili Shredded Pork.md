# Cumin & Chili Shredded Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin, appropriate amount (shredded)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (shredded)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (shredded)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount (shredded)
🧂 Cooking wine, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a little
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little
🌶️ Coarse chili powder, 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Fine chili powder, 1 tablespoon
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn powder, appropriate amount
🌿 Cumin powder, appropriate amount
🌱 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, a little
🧂 MSG, a little

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥢 Spatula

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a wok, using slightly more oil than for everyday stir-frying.
2. When the oil reaches about 80% hot, add the shredded pork. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the moisture evaporates.
3. Once the pork is dry, lightly browned on the surface, and oil has rendered, add shredded ginger, scallions, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant.
4. Splash in cooking wine along the edge of the wok. Add light soy sauce, thirteen-spice powder, a little dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Stir-fry evenly to color and season the pork.
5. Add coarse chili powder, fine chili powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, cumin powder, and white sesame seeds.
**Key point**: Lower the heat at this stage to prevent the seasonings from burning and turning bitter.
6. Stir-fry over low heat until the chili oil is released and the cumin aroma is strong.
7. Finally, add a little sugar and MSG. Stir-fry quickly and evenly, then remove from heat and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein but high in oil content; it is recommended as an occasional treat. Pair with light vegetables and staple foods to control overall calorie intake. Chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns can stimulate the appetite, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
