# Cement Fried Chicken  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 5 chicken drumsticks  
🫚 50g ginger (sliced)  
🧄 100g garlic cloves  
🧅 100g onion (cut into chunks)  
🍺 1 can of beer  
🦑 Squid ink powder, to taste  
🧂 10g salt  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
⚪ Ground white pepper, to taste  
🌿 15g thirteen-spice powder  
🌿 Cumin powder, to taste  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder, to taste  
🦪 40g oyster sauce  
🫗 Soy sauce, to taste  
🍅 Ketchup, to taste (for dipping)  
🥗 Mayonnaise, to taste (for dipping)  
🍖 BBQ seasoning, to taste (for dipping)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🧑‍🍳 Blender (food processor)  
🫗 Large basin (for soaking and marinating)  
🍳 Wok (for deep frying)  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🍴 Fork or sharp object (for poking holes)  
🥄 Chopsticks or slotted spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Score both sides of the chicken drumsticks with a knife, then poke several small holes all over the surface with a fork.  
   **Note**: Scoring and poking helps blood seep out and allows flavors to penetrate.  
2. Place the prepared drumsticks in a basin, add enough clean water to cover, and soak for at least 2 hours. Remove and drain thoroughly.  
   **Note**: Ensure blood is fully soaked out and the meat is completely drained.  
3. Put the sliced ginger, garlic cloves, onion chunks, and beer into a blender. Blend until smooth to form the marinade liquid.  
   **Note**: Blend until fine to release the aromas.  
4. Pour the marinade liquid over the drained drumsticks. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, thirteen-spice powder, cumin powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, oyster sauce, and soy sauce. Mix well by hand.  
   **Note**: Ensure the seasonings are thoroughly combined with the chicken.  
5. Place the drumsticks in the refrigerator and marinate for at least 3 hours to allow full flavor absorption.  
6. Remove the marinated drumsticks. Rinse briefly under clean water to remove loose marinade residue on the surface. Drain well.  
   **Note**: Rinse only briefly; do not soak for an extended time.  
7. Place the drained drumsticks into the squid ink powder. Press firmly with your hands to coat the drumsticks evenly and tightly with the powder.  
   **Note**: Press firmly to ensure the coating adheres securely and does not fall off easily.  
8. Let the coated drumsticks rest for a moment until the dry powder on the surface becomes moist and darkens in color.  
   **Indicator**: The powder layer on the surface is moist and the color has deepened.  
9. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to 180°C (350°F). Gently lower the drumsticks into the oil. Reduce to medium heat and fry for about 10 minutes.  
   **Note**: Add the chicken when the oil is at 180°C (350°F). During frying, occasionally turn the drumsticks with chopsticks to ensure even heating and prevent burning.  
10. Fry until the outer coating is firm and the color is uniformly black. Remove and drain excess oil. Serve on a plate. Can be enjoyed with ketchup, mayonnaise, or BBQ seasoning as dipping sauces.  
    **Indicator**: The outer crust is crispy, and the color is an even, deep black.  

## Health Tips  
Squid ink powder is rich in minerals and antioxidants, but deep frying results in high calorie content. Pairing with a vegetable salad is recommended for balance. This dish is suitable for those seeking a novel texture and high protein intake. Be mindful of oil consumption and avoid overindulgence to prevent上火 (internal heat).
