# Pig Trotters Popcorn 🍿
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings 🥘  
🐷 Pig trotters 2000g (cut into pieces)  
🧅 Scallions 2 (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger (sliced) to taste  
🍶 Cooking wine to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil (for frying, generous amount)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥘 Stockpot (for blanching and simmering trotters)  
🍳 Wok or deep pot (for frying)  
🔗 Wire rack or grill rack (for air-drying trotters)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Steps
1. Place the pig trotters in a pot with cold water, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then skim off the foam.
2. Reduce heat to medium and continue cooking for about 40 minutes, until the trotters are cooked through but not yet tender.
3. Remove the trotters from the pot and lay them flat on a wire rack. Air-dry or low-temperature dry until the skin becomes dry and firm.
   **Indicator**: When touched with fingers, the skin feels noticeably firm.
4. Pour a generous amount of oil into the pot and heat to around 80-90% of its capacity. Add the air-dried trotter pieces.
5. Continuously stir them with a slotted spoon and fry until the trotters puff up like popcorn, turning golden brown.
6. Quickly remove the fried trotters and immediately immerse them in cold water to soak.
   **Indicator**: The hot trotters rapidly absorb water upon contact with cold water, becoming fluffy and soft.
7. Drain the soaked trotters and divide them into sealed bags. Store in the freezer.

## Health Advice  
Pig trotters are rich in collagen but also contain high levels of fat and calories. It is recommended to pair them with vegetables for balanced nutrition. Suitable for those needing protein supplementation or focusing on skin health, but individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their intake.
