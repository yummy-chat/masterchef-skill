# Salt-Baked Sesame Chicken  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 1 whole chicken  
🌿 Celery, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧅 Shallots, appropriate amount (cut into chunks)  
🌿 Fresh sand ginger, appropriate amount (cut into chunks)  
🌰 Raw white sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for final sprinkle on surface)  
🧂 Soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount  
🌿 Sand ginger powder, appropriate amount  
🌿 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount  
🌿 White pepper powder, appropriate amount  
🫙 Chu hou paste, appropriate amount  
🫙 Hoisin sauce, appropriate amount  
🫙 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, a little  
🍯 Honey, appropriate amount (mixed with dark soy sauce in a 1:1 ratio for glaze)  
🍾 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (mixed with honey in a 1:1 ratio for glaze)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel stockpot (for marinating)  
♨️ Steamer  
🔥 Oven  
🖌️ Basting brush  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare for Marinating**: Place the whole chicken in a stainless steel stockpot. Add celery sections, scallion sections, chopped cilantro, shallot chunks, and fresh sand ginger chunks. Then add sand ginger powder, five-spice powder, white pepper powder, Chu hou paste, hoisin sauce, oyster sauce, a little sugar, soy sauce, and garlic.  
**Note**: Soy sauce and garlic can easily be overused, so control the quantity.  

2. **Massage for Flavor**: Use your hands to thoroughly mix and rub all the seasonings evenly into the chicken, both inside and out, to ensure even coating.  
**Key**: Massaging must be even to allow the flavors to penetrate.  

3. **Refrigerate to Marinate**: Seal the massaged chicken and refrigerate for 10-12 hours.  
**Key**: The marinating time must be sufficient for thorough flavor infusion.  

4. **Steam**: Place the marinated chicken into a steamer lined with a steaming cloth. Once steam rises, steam for 1.5-2 hours until the chicken is tender and the meat easily separates from the bone.  
5. **Prepare the Glaze**: In a small bowl, mix honey and dark soy sauce in a 1:1 ratio until well combined.  
6. **Apply the Glaze**: Remove the steamed chicken while hot, and use a basting brush to evenly coat the chicken skin with the honey and dark soy sauce mixture.  
7. **First Baking**: Place the glazed chicken in the oven and bake for about 15 minutes until the skin turns an appealing golden brown.  
**Check**: The chicken skin should be golden and glossy.  

8. **Sprinkle Sesame and Second Baking**: Remove the chicken from the oven, generously sprinkle raw white sesame seeds evenly over the surface. Return to the oven and bake for about 2 more minutes until the sesame seeds are toasted and fragrant.  
**Key**: This step gives the sesame seeds a cooked aroma and allows them to fuse with the chicken skin.  

9. **Cut and Plate**: Remove the salt-baked sesame chicken, let it cool slightly, then chop into large pieces and arrange on a plate for serving.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein, making it suitable for muscle building or for those needing to replenish energy. However, the sauces and honey are high in sugar, so it is recommended to control the portion size and pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition.
