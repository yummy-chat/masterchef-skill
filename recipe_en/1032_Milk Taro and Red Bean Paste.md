# Milk Taro and Red Bean Paste
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December), All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red beans, about 1 rice bowl (needs to be frozen with water one day in advance)  
🍠 Lipu taro, 1 rice bowl (peeled and cut into pieces)  
🍡 Taro balls, half a rice bowl  
🥛 Milk, 1 rice bowl  
🍬 Rock sugar, half a rice bowl

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥣 Blender  
🍶 Rice bowl

## Instructions
1.  Transfer the red beans, which have been frozen with water one day in advance, into the rice cooker inner pot. Add water up to the maximum water level line. Start the "Cook Rice" mode and cook until the red beans are completely soft and mushy, with the skins burst open.
2.  Scoop out about half of the cooked red beans and put them into a blender. Blend into a smooth bean paste. Then, pour the paste back into the rice cooker.
   **Key Point**: This step is essential for thickening the soup.
3.  Add the taro pieces, taro balls, and rock sugar into the rice cooker.
4.  Start the "Cook Rice" mode again and cook until the taro becomes soft and the taro balls float to the surface and turn translucent.
5.  Once cooked, open the lid, pour in the milk, and stir well before serving.
   **Note**: The milk must be added last to avoid losing flavor and nutrients from prolonged high-temperature boiling.

## Health Tips
Red beans and taro provide high-quality carbohydrates and dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for warming the body in winter or replenishing energy after exercise. Adding the milk last helps retain calcium, but the half-rice-bowl amount of rock sugar is quite high; those who need to control sugar intake may reduce it by half.
