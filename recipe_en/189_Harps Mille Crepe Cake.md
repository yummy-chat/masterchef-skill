# Harps Mille Crepe Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### 🍰 Sponge Cake
🥚 Egg yolks 3  
🍬 Granulated sugar 10g (mixed with egg yolks)  
🌾 Cake flour 30g (sifted)  
🧈 Butter 20g (melted)  
🥚 Egg whites 2  
🍬 Granulated sugar 20g (added in batches while whipping egg whites)

### 🥞 Crepe Batter
🥚 Whole eggs about 150g (about 3)  
🌾 Cake flour 120g (sifted)  
🧈 Butter 20g (melted)  
🥛 Cream 20g  
🍬 Granulated sugar 40g  
🥛 Milk 280g  
🧂 Salt 1g

### 🍓 Filling Fruits
🍈 Melon/honeydew melon as needed  
🍓 Strawberries as needed  
🥝 Kiwis as needed  
🍌 Bananas as needed

### 🧀 Fresh Cheese Filling (Top Decoration)
🧀 Cream cheese 150g  
🥛 Sour cream 10g  
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk 15g  
🍬 Sugar 20g  
🥛 Cream 150g (added gradually)

### 🍦 Whipped Cream
🥛 Heavy cream 600g  
🍬 Sugar 48g (ratio about 8%)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Whisk  
🔥 Oven (180°C for baking sponge cake)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (about 24cm)  
🧁 Piping bag  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🫗 Flour sifter  
🍴 Spatula  
🥄 Scraper  
🪣 Baking tray/silicone mat  
🎂 Cake turntable/base  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Plate (for pressing and shaping)  
🫙 Plastic wrap/sealable bag

## Instructions
1. **Make the sponge cake base**  
   Stir the egg yolks with 10g granulated sugar until combined. Add 20g granulated sugar to the egg whites in batches and whip to stiff peaks. Take 1/3 of the meringue and mix it evenly with the egg yolk mixture, then pour back into the remaining meringue and gently fold with a whisk. Sift the cake flour in two batches and fold with a scraper until no dry flour remains. Take a small amount of the batter and mix with the melted butter, then return it to the main batter and fold evenly.  
   **Note**: The egg whites must be whipped thoroughly; fold gently and quickly to prevent deflating.

2. **Prepare the crepe batter**  
   Mix the whole eggs, cake flour, melted butter, cream, granulated sugar, milk, and salt together until smooth. Sift 3-4 times until fine and lump-free.

3. **Prepare the filling and cream**  
   Peel and cut the fruits into pieces or slices. Add 48g granulated sugar to the heavy cream and whip to a spreadable consistency. Make the fresh cheese filling: Mix the cream cheese, sour cream, sweetened condensed milk, and granulated sugar until combined. Gradually add 150g cream and stir until smooth.

4. **Cook the crepes**  
   Preheat the frying pan to about 160°C. Pour in an appropriate amount of batter and quickly swirl to cover the bottom, then pour out the excess batter. Cook over medium heat until small bubbles appear on the surface and edges lift. Loosen the edges with a knife and peel off the crepe. You can make about 20 crepes; uneven first two are normal.

5. **Assemble the cake**  
   Place a sponge cake layer on the base, spread a layer of cream, and cover with a crepe. Spread a thicker layer of cream, arrange one type of fruit in a single layer, fill gaps with cream, and cover with another crepe. Alternately layer bananas, kiwis, strawberries, and other fruits. Every few layers, press down gently with a plate to make the cake firm and even.  
   **Note**: Be sure to fill fruit gaps with cream to ensure stable slices.

6. **Top decoration and refrigeration**  
   Spread the fresh cheese filling evenly on the top layer and sprinkle with sponge cake crumbs for decoration. Refrigerate the cake for 1-2 hours until fully set, resulting in clear layers when sliced.  
   **Note**: The longer it chills, the more stable the structure and the better the flavor melding.

## Health Advice
This mille crepe cake combines sponge cake, thin crepes, abundant fruits, and cheese filling, offering relatively comprehensive nutrition. However, it is high in calories and best suited for sharing at gatherings or as an occasional dessert. It is recommended to control portion sizes and pair with a light diet.
