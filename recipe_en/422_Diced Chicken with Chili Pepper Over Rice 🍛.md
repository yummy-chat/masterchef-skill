# Diced Chicken with Chili Pepper Over Rice 🍛  
**Cuisine**: Sichuan  
**Cooking Difficulty**: ⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Tip**: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thighs, about 500g (diced)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, to taste (sliced into rings or small pieces)  
🫑 Green peppers, to taste (sliced into rings or small pieces)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon  
🧂 MSG, 1 teaspoon  
🌿 Ground white pepper, 1 teaspoon  
🌶️ Chili flakes, to taste  
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed  
🍚 Rice, as needed (cooked in advance)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl (stainless steel basin)  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Spatula  
🥄 Measuring spoons  
🍚 Rice cooker or steamer  
🍽️ Serving bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the chicken thighs into evenly sized cubes and place them in a large bowl.  
**Note**: Uniform cubes ensure even cooking and better absorption of flavors.  
2. Add 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of MSG, and 1 teaspoon of ground white pepper to the bowl. Mix thoroughly and marinate for 15 minutes.  
**Note**: Ensure each piece of chicken is coated with the seasonings, and allow enough time for marinating to enhance flavor.  
3. Slice the small chili peppers and green peppers into rings or small pieces, and mince the garlic. Set aside.  
4. Heat the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil, and stir-fry the marinated chicken pieces.  
**Key Point**: Cook until the chicken is fully变色 (色 refers to color change) and the surface is slightly browned and crispy. Remove and set aside. The slightly crispy texture adds aroma.  
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, reduce the heat to medium-low, and quickly stir-fry the minced garlic and chili flakes until fragrant and the oil turns red.  
**Note**: Avoid high heat to prevent the chili flakes from burning.  
6. Add the small chili pepper rings and green pepper rings, and stir-fry until they are just cooked but still crisp.  
7. Return the cooked chicken to the wok, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds to allow the chicken to absorb the chili aroma.  
8. Serve the stir-fried chicken with chili peppers directly over the pre-cooked rice.  
**Key Point**: Have the rice ready in advance; serving hot enhances the flavor.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein, but it contains a high amount of chili and oil. It is recommended to pair it with a simple stir-fried vegetable to balance the nutrition. Suitable for those with high physical exertion, but individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume it with caution.
