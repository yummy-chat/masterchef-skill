# Cantonese Salted Bone Congee  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Rice: appropriate amount (frozen into ice cubes with water in advance)  
🦴 Pork bones (pork spine/ribs): appropriate amount (salted overnight in advance)  
🥕 Carrot: appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)  
🫚 Ginger: appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥜 Peanuts: about a small handful (mixed with a little salt and oil, microwaved, then cooled)  
🥬 Lettuce: appropriate amount (finely shredded)  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount (for marinating pork bones and seasoning peanuts)  
🫒 Cooking oil: a little (for mixing with peanuts)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot  
🧺 Strainer  
🥄 Ceramic or wooden spoon  
♨️ Microwave  
🔪 Cutting board + knife  
🥣 Bowls, plates  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Pork Bone Preparation**: Marinate the pork bones with salt the night before. Before cooking, rinse under water with a strainer repeatedly to remove excess salt on the surface, preventing the final dish from being too salty.  
2. **Rice Preparation**: Freeze the rice with water into ice cubes. Use these directly for cooking the congee to achieve a thick and creamy texture more easily.  
3. **Prepare Toppings**: Mix peanuts with a little salt and oil, microwave for about 1 minute, then let cool completely until crispy. Finely shred the lettuce, cut carrot into small cubes, and slice the ginger.  
4. **Cook the Congee Base**: Bring water to a boil in a clay pot, add the frozen rice ice cubes. After boiling over high heat, the congee base will quickly become thick.  
5. **Add Main Ingredients**: Once the congee base is boiling, add the rinsed pork bones, ginger slices, and carrot cubes. Bring to a boil over high heat. Note: the pork bones must be added when the congee base is at a rolling boil to avoid locking in any unpleasant smell.  
6. **Simmer**: Reduce to medium heat and simmer slowly. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom. Cook until the rice grains bloom and the congee is thick.  
7. **Add Lettuce**: Pour the shredded lettuce into the congee, stir well, then immediately turn off the heat. Use the residual heat to wilt the lettuce, keeping it crisp and tender.  
8. **Bowl and Garnish**: Ladle into bowls and sprinkle with the pre-prepared crispy peanuts. Ensure the peanuts are completely cooled to maintain their crunchy texture.  

## Health Suggestions  
Pork bones provide calcium and protein, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing calcium supplementation. Since the marinated pork bones are high in salt, patients with high blood pressure are advised to consume less of the broth.
