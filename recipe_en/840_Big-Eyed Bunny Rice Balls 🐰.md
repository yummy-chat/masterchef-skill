# Big-Eyed Bunny Rice Balls 🐰
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Wild greens (mugwort/cleavers, etc.) as needed (washed and blanched)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 430g
🧈 Butter 42g
🍬 Sugar 42g
💧 Green juice 342g (wild greens blended with water, adjust amount based on dough consistency)
🥡 Wheat starch (tang flour) 130g
♨️ Boiling water 235g (to cook the wheat starch)
🎨 Food coloring (red, black, etc.) as needed
🥮 Taro paste filling as needed (prepared in advance, slightly soft)
🍘 Purple rice, salted egg yolk, and pork floss filling as needed (prepared in advance)
🫒 Cooking oil (e.g., corn oil) as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Cooking pot
🥤 Blender/food processor
🥣 Mixing bowl
🤖 Stand mixer (with paddle attachment)
🔪 Cutting board, knives, rolling pin
🛡️ Plastic bags, heat-resistant plastic wrap
🧤 Disposable gloves
💨 Piping tips (3 sizes)
🖌️ Fine brush
♨️ Steamer/steaming pot

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make Green Juice**: Wash the wild greens. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add half a spoon of baking soda, blanch the greens, then remove. Place the blanched greens in a blender, add a small amount of water, and blend into a smooth green juice. Use directly without straining for a deeper color.
**Key Point**: Baking soda is crucial for maintaining the bright green color.

2. **Knead Glutinous Rice Dough**: In a mixing bowl, combine glutinous rice flour, butter, and sugar. Add the green juice and knead into a soft, pliable green dough. Adjust the amount of green juice based on the flour's absorbency and the juice's thickness.
3. **Knead Wheat Starch Dough**: In a separate bowl, place the wheat starch. Pour in freshly boiled water all at once and quickly stir into a translucent white dough.
**Note**: Boiling water is required to cook the starch, forming a moldable semi-transparent dough.

4. **Combine Doughs**: Place the glutinous rice dough and wheat starch dough into a stand mixer. Use the paddle attachment to mix until fully combined and uniform in color. If no stand mixer is available, knead vigorously by hand. The combined dough will be softer and more elastic.
5. **Prepare Decorative Dough**: Take a small amount of the white wheat starch dough and divide it into portions. Use food coloring to create pink, black, and other colors as needed.
6. **Divide and Preserve Moisture**: Roll the combined dough into a log and cut into equal portions. Place the portions in a sealed plastic bag to prevent drying.
7. **Fill the Balls**: Wear disposable gloves. Press a portion into a round disc, thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Place filling in the center and use the tiger mouth technique to slowly seal and roll it into a ball. Gloves prevent sticking and protect the dough from hand heat.
8. **Make Decorations**: Lay plastic wrap on the work surface. Roll out the colored decorative doughs and use three different sizes of piping tips to cut out round pieces to assemble the big-eyed bunny eyes. Use pink dough to cut thin strips for blushes or ears. Plastic wrap prevents cut pieces from sticking.
9. **Assemble and Decorate**: Place the filled rice balls on greaseproof paper cups. Use a fine brush to lightly apply water where decorations will be attached, then place the eyes, blush, and other pieces, ensuring they stick firmly.
10. **Steam**: Place in a steamer or steam oven and steam for about 12-15 minutes, until the dough is fully cooked and the color deepens to a glossy green. Adjust time based on the size of the rice balls.
11. **Final Touches and Packaging**: Once steamed, immediately brush with a layer of cooking oil. While slightly cooled, wrap each rice ball individually with heat-resistant plastic wrap. Brushing oil and wrapping with plastic wrap are essential for maintaining softness, preventing drying, and preserving the shape.

## Health Tips
These rice balls are made with wild green juice and glutinous rice flour, giving them a soft and chewy texture. However, glutinous rice is difficult to digest, so it is recommended to eat only 1-2 per serving, paired with green tea to cut the richness. They are perfect for spring outings, but diabetics should monitor sugar intake.
