# Pumpkin Sourdough Boule 🎃
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour, approx. 250 g (for the base dough)
💧 Water, approx. 200 g (for the base dough)
🧂 Salt, to taste (dough seasoning)
🍞 Sourdough starter, as needed (cultured from blueberry ferment liquid)
🫙 Active dry yeast, as needed (for auxiliary fermentation)
🎃 Pumpkin, approx. 100 g (steamed, peeled, and seeds removed, then mashed; choose a dry, floury variety)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for kneading and mixing ingredients)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for weighing ingredients)
🍳 Baking rack/tray (for placing the dough during bulk fermentation)
🧺 Banneton proofing basket (for shaping and final proofing)
🧵 Kitchen twine, approx. 1 m (for tying the dough to create pumpkin ridges)
🔪 Craft knife/lame (for scoring decorative patterns on the surface)
🍲 Dutch oven (for baking the dough, maintaining humidity)

## Instructions
1. Steam the pumpkin until tender, then peel, remove seeds, and mash with a fork. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Choose a “floury” pumpkin variety with low moisture content to prevent the mash from making the dough too wet and weakening the gluten structure.
2. Combine bread flour, water, salt, sourdough starter, and dry yeast. Knead into a smooth base dough.
   **Note**: If incorporating pumpkin mash directly into the dough, adjust the water amount based on its moisture content; in this method, the pumpkin is used as a filling.
3. Roll out the base dough, spread the pumpkin mash evenly over it, then fold and roll it up so the mash is completely enclosed inside the dough.
4. Shape the dough into a round and place it in a floured banneton for final proofing.
5. Once proofed, invert the dough and carefully turn it out. Take 4 pieces of twine, tie them together at the center, and wrap them around the dough like tying a parcel, pressing the twine evenly into the dough to create pumpkin ridges.
   **Key point**: The depth to which the twine is pressed determines how pronounced the final pumpkin pattern will be.
6. Dust the dough surface with a layer of bread flour, then use a craft knife to score decorative patterns.
   **Key point**: Scoring after dusting with flour makes the lines more distinct and the appearance more attractive.
7. Place the shaped dough into a preheated Dutch oven, cover, and bake. Baking parameters: First stage at 250°C (482°F) covered for 20 minutes; second stage uncover, lower temperature to 230°C (446°F), and bake for another 10 minutes, until the surface is golden brown.
8. After baking, insert small twigs as decoration. Slice and serve with yogurt, fruit, or salad to make a brunch.

## Health Suggestions
Pumpkin is rich in β-carotene, and sourdough bread provides high fiber—together they are ideal for nourishing the eyes and moistening dryness in autumn. For those with weaker digestive function, it is recommended to cut into small pieces and chew slowly. Be careful to control the moisture in the pumpkin mash, or the dough will become too sticky and difficult to handle.
