# Garlic Ramen with Ground Meat
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Instant noodle cake, 1 serving
🥩 Ground meat (pork or beef, fat-to-lean ratio 2:8), as needed
🧄 Whole garlic, 1 head (cut off the top horizontally to expose the cloves)
🥚 Fresh egg, 1 (optional)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)
🧂 Instant noodle seasoning packet, 1 pack (including powder and sauce packets, use all)
🫒 Olive oil, a small amount (for drizzling over garlic)
🫗 Cooking oil, a small amount (for frying ground meat)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
♨️ Air fryer or oven
🍳 Cast iron pot / soup pot
🍴 Kitchen tongs
🥄 Wooden spoon or chopsticks
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Small bowl, spoon
🔥 Gas stove
🥘 Oven rack / baking sheet

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut off the top of the garlic head horizontally to expose the cloves, drizzle with a little olive oil, and place in an air fryer or oven until golden and soft.
   **Key Point**: The oil prevents the garlic from burning and enhances the flavor.
2. Prepare the ground meat with a 2:8 fat-to-lean ratio; finely chop the scallions and set aside.
3. Heat a small amount of oil in a pot over low heat until warm, add the ground meat, and stir-fry until fully cooked and no longer pink.
4. Add an appropriate amount of water to the pot, bring to a gentle boil, add all the seasoning packets from the instant noodles, stir well, reduce to low heat, and simmer for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
5. Place the roasted whole garlic into the soup, use kitchen tongs to hold the garlic stem, squeeze firmly to release the softened garlic paste into the soup, discard the garlic skin, and continue cooking for about 5 minutes until the soup color changes from deep red to a milky white, appearing cloudy.
   **Key Point**: Roasted garlic loses its spiciness and retains only a rich aroma, forming the soul of the noodles.
6. After the soup base is ready, add the instant noodle cake and cook over medium heat for about 3 minutes.
7. Crack a raw egg onto the noodles, use a spoon to ladle the hot soup repeatedly over the egg white until the white is set while the yolk remains runny; alternatively, stir the egg directly into the soup to form egg ribbons.
8. Once the egg reaches the desired doneness, turn off the heat, sprinkle with scallions, and serve.
   **Key Point**: Add the scallions last to preserve their aroma.

## Health Advice
Roasting the garlic and squeezing the paste into the soup can boost immunity and provide antioxidant benefits, making it suitable for warming up in autumn and winter. Ground meat with a 2:8 fat-to-lean ratio offers quality protein, but the seasoning packet is high in salt, so it is advisable to limit soup consumption.
