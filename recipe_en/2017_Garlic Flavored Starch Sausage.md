# Garlic Flavored Starch Sausage
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 500g pork shoulder (chopped into soybean-sized pieces)  
🧄 100g garlic (half used raw, half stir-fried)  
🌾 500g sweet potato starch (can substitute with potato starch, corn starch, or mung bean starch)  
💧 Water, as needed (500g for garlic water, about 350g for starch slurry)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌿 About 3g thirteen-spice powder (or five-spice powder, Sichuan pepper powder)  
🥄 20g sweet potato starch (for making cooked starch paste)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Large bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🌀 Funnel  
📌 Needle  
🧵 Cotton string  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the pork shoulder into large slices, then chop into soybean-sized pieces. Crush the garlic and mince into two portions. Clean the small pig intestine casing and set aside.
2. Heat a little oil in a wok, stir-fry half of the minced garlic until fragrant, add 500g water, and bring to a boil over high heat.
3. Dissolve 20g sweet potato starch in a small amount of water to make a starch slurry. After the garlic water boils, add salt to taste, then pour in the starch slurry while stirring. Cook until slightly thickened and translucent, then remove from heat and let cool until warm to the touch.
4. Place the remaining sweet potato starch in a large bowl, add about 350g water and stir to form a raw starch slurry. Then add the pork pieces, the remaining raw minced garlic, and thirteen-spice powder, and mix well.
5. Pour the cooled cooked starch paste into the slurry and mix thoroughly, ensuring the mixture evenly coats the meat pieces without settling easily.
6. Tie one end of the casing, fit the other end over the funnel mouth, and pour in the slurry. Do not fill too full; leave about 10 cm of casing for tying. Tie off sections every 30 cm with cotton string, and poke several small holes in each section with a needle to release air.
7. Heat water in a pot until it reaches a "simmering edge" state (just before boiling). Add the sausages and keep at a gentle simmer over low heat. For the first 10 minutes, avoid high heat; gently turn with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking, and use a needle to release air again. Cook until the sausages float, turn lighter in color, and spring back when pressed, then remove.
8. Let cool naturally after removing, then slice and serve.  
**Key Tips**:  
- Do not chop the meat too finely; maintain a granular texture.  
- The cooked starch paste must be completely cooled before mixing with the raw slurry to prevent starch settling and ensure uniform texture.  
- Do not overstuff the casing; leave space for expansion and poke holes with a needle to prevent bursting during cooking.  
- Keep the water at a gentle simmer throughout; vigorous boiling will cause the sausages to burst or become tough.

## Health Advice  
This garlic flavored starch sausage is rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for manual laborers and those needing energy replenishment. However, due to the casing and fat content, it is recommended that individuals with high blood pressure or high blood lipids consume in moderation. Pay attention to controlling salt and seasoning amounts to avoid excessive sodium.
