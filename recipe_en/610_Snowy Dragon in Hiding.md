# Snowy Dragon in Hiding
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦞 1 Southern reef lobster (about 1.5 jin)
🥦 Broccoli, as needed (blanched for plating)
🥓 Ham bits, as needed (for aroma and garnish)
🦀 Crab roe, as needed (for garnish and color)
🥜 Olive kernels (or nuts/dried fruit), as needed (for garnish)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating lobster balls and seasoning the egg white milk mixture)
🍬 Sugar, as needed (for marinating lobster balls and seasoning the egg white milk mixture)
🌾 Cornstarch, a pinch (for marinating lobster balls), plus additional, dissolved in water to make cornstarch slurry (to be added to the egg white milk mixture)
🧂 Chicken powder, as needed (for seasoning the egg white milk mixture)
🥚 Egg whites, as needed (for marinating lobster balls and making the egg white milk mixture)
🥛 Milk, as needed (for making the egg white milk mixture)
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed (for stir-frying lobster balls and cooking the egg white mixture)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Chinese wok
🔪 Cutting board, knives
✂️ Kitchen shears
🥄 Wok spatula, slotted spoon
🍲 Steamer
🥣 Mixing bowls
🥢 Chopsticks
🧹 Pastry brush

## Steps
1. **Butcher the Lobster**
   Use kitchen shears to cut around the edge of the lobster's head shell. Twist the body to separate it from the head. Immediately submerge the body in ice water to make the meat contract, becoming firmer and whiter.
   **Note:** Operate carefully to keep the shell and meat as intact as possible.

2. **Steam the Lobster Head**
   Tie the antennae of the separated lobster head with kitchen twine so they stand upright. Place in the steamer and steam over high heat for about 3 minutes until cooked. Remove and set aside for plating.

3. **Remove Lobster Meat and Cut into Balls**
   While the head is steaming, use shears to completely remove the meat from the shell. Take the body meat out of the ice water, pat dry with paper towels, and cut crosswise into evenly sized lobster balls.
   **Crucial:** It must be cut crosswise to sever the muscle fibers, preventing the meat from falling apart during stir-frying.

4. **Marinate the Lobster Balls**
   Add salt, sugar, a pinch of cornstarch, and egg white to the lobster balls. Stir thoroughly in one direction and marinate for a short while.
   **Function:** Seasons the meat and makes the texture smoother.

5. **Prepare the Egg White Milk Mixture**
   Beat the egg whites, then add milk, salt, sugar, and chicken powder. Stir to combine. Dissolve a small amount of cornstarch in water to make a slurry, then pour it into the mixture and stir well.
   **Note:** The cornstarch must be dissolved first before adding to prevent lumps.

6. **Stir-fry the Lobster Meat**
   Heat the wok, then add enough cooking oil. Heat the oil to 100–110°C. Add the marinated lobster balls. Gently push them apart with the spatula and stir-fry for about 20–30 seconds, until the meat is just set and turns semi-translucent white. Immediately remove using a slotted spoon and drain the oil.
   **Crucial:** The oil temperature and time must be accurate. Err on the side of shorter cooking time to keep the meat tender.

7. **Cook the "Snowy" Egg White**
   Pour out most of the oil from the wok, leaving only a thin layer. Turn the heat to low. Pour in the egg white milk mixture. Using the spatula, continuously and gently push the mixture in one direction. Stop when the egg white has coagulated into a fluffy, snow-like or tender tofu-pudding-like texture, still pure white.
   **Note:** The entire process must be on low heat with gentle stirring to prevent the egg white from turning yellow and tough.

8. **Combine and Serve**
   When the egg white has just formed a tender, smooth "snow," quickly add the stir-fried lobster balls. Gently mix until just combined. Drizzle in a little hot oil (mingyou) for sheen, then remove from the wok immediately.
   **Crucial:** Work quickly. Remove from heat immediately after combining, or the egg white will become tough from prolonged heat.

9. **Plate the Dish**
   Place the cooked egg white and lobster mixture on the plate. Arrange the steamed lobster head on one side and the blanched broccoli on the other. Finally, garnish with olive kernels, ham bits, and crab roe.

## Health Advice
This dish, "Snowy Dragon in Hiding," features low-fat, high-protein lobster as its star, complemented by milk and egg whites. It is nutritionally balanced and easy to digest, making it suitable for those needing high-quality protein. However, note that olive kernels and crab roe are relatively high in calories; use sparingly for garnish during weight loss phases.
