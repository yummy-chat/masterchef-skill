# How to Clean Live Abalone
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## 🥬 Ingredients  
🐚 Live abalone, as needed  

## 🧂 Seasonings  
🧂 Salt, to taste  

## 🍳 Utensils & Equipment  
🪥 Toothbrush  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Pot  
🫖 Kettle  
🍽️ Plate  

## 📝 Steps  
1. Place the live abalone in a glass bowl, sprinkle with an appropriate amount of salt, and gently toss with a spoon to evenly coat the surface of the abalone with salt.  
2. Pour approximately 50°C warm water into the bowl, enough to submerge the abalone, and soak for 5 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Maintain the water temperature strictly at 50°C and soak for exactly 5 minutes to soften any attached impurities for easy brushing.  
3. Remove the abalone and carefully scrub the surface of each one with a toothbrush to remove all impurities.  
4. Place the scrubbed abalone back into the bowl, pour approximately 75°C warm water into the bowl, and soak for 10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Soak at 75°C for 10 minutes to help the abalone meat naturally separate from the shell.  
5. Take out one abalone and gently peel the meat away from the shell by hand. It should separate very easily without force.  
6. Tear off the internal organs attached to the back of the abalone meat, and squeeze or cut off the abalone mouth at the front (inedible part).  
7. Place the cleaned abalone meat on a plate for later use. The surface should be clean and intact, with no internal organ residue.  

## Health Advice  
Treating abalone by scrubbing with salt and soaking in warm water effectively removes internal organs and impurities. Suitable for those who enjoy fresh flavors, but those with high cholesterol should be cautious about consumption amounts.
