# Pan-Fried Tofu in Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Firm tofu 1 block (cut into mahjong-sized pieces)
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (minced)
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten into a uniform egg wash)
🌽 Cornstarch as needed (for coating)
🌽 Cornstarch 1 small spoon (for thickening the sauce)
🌿 Scallions as needed (chopped)
🥡 Light soy sauce 2 spoons
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 spoon
🍬 Sugar half spoon
💧 Water as needed (for preparing the sauce and simmering)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (about half the height of the tofu pieces, for frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Small bowl (for preparing the sauce)
🥣 Bowl (for holding the egg wash)
🍽️ Square baking dish (for holding the cornstarch)
🍳 Small frying pan
🥢 Long wooden chopsticks
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the tofu**: Cut the firm tofu into mahjong-sized pieces. Firm tofu is resilient and less likely to break during cooking.
2. **Prepare the aromatics**: Mince the garlic, chop the scallions, and set aside. Adjust the amount of minced garlic to taste.
3. **Prepare the sauce**: In a small bowl, combine 2 spoons of light soy sauce, 1 spoon of oyster sauce, 1 small spoon of cornstarch, half a spoon of sugar, and the minced garlic. Stir until well mixed. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water to thin it out. Preparing the sauce in advance makes the subsequent steps smoother.
4. **Prepare the coating ingredients**: Have the beaten egg wash and an appropriate amount of cornstarch ready.
5. **Heat the oil**: Pour cooking oil into a small frying pan, enough to reach about half the height of the tofu pieces. Heat over medium-high heat to about 150°C. To check the oil temperature: insert a wooden chopstick into the oil; if dense small bubbles form around it, it's ready. Using a small pan and the shallow-frying method saves oil at home.
6. **Coat the tofu**: Choose one of the following sequences based on preference:
   - **Crispy texture**: First evenly coat the tofu pieces with cornstarch, then fully dip them in the egg wash.
   - **Thick batter texture**: First fully dip the tofu pieces in the egg wash, then coat them with cornstarch.
   After coating, shake off any excess powder before placing them in the pan. Keep your hands clean during the process.
7. **Fry**: Place the coated tofu pieces one by one into the hot oil. Fry over medium-high heat until both sides are golden brown and set, ideally with one side achieving a slightly charred color. Medium-high heat helps create a firmer, thicker coating, making it easier to flip the pieces.
8. **Add the sauce and simmer**: Pour all the prepared sauce into the pan, then add enough water to reach half the height of the tofu pieces. Maintain a medium heat so the sauce stays at a gentle simmer. Let it simmer slowly, allowing the sauce to gradually penetrate the fried coating.
9. **Serve**: Once both sides of the tofu have absorbed the sauce, sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve. If plating individually, pour the remaining sauce from the pan over the tofu for an appetizing color, which also goes well with rice.

## Health Advice
The crispy coating of pan-fried tofu in sauce locks in the flavor, but the frying process adds extra calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Firm tofu is rich in plant protein and calcium, making it suitable for vegetarians and those needing calcium supplementation.
