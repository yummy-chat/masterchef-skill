# Braised Platter
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork ribs, as needed
🥩 Pork belly, as needed
🍗 Chicken wings, as needed
🍗 Chicken legs, as needed
🐷 Pig ears, as needed
🥜 Peanuts, as needed (for platter garnish)
🥬 Lettuce, as needed (for platter garnish, decoration and to cut grease)
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (for platter garnish, decoration and to cut grease)
🍬 Rock sugar, 120 grams (for caramelizing)
🫒 Soybean oil, 750 grams (as braising oil base)
🫚 Ginger slices, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 3 pieces (not for spiciness, only to remove gamey smell and enhance aroma)
🌿 Mixed spices, 1 portion (star anise, bay leaves, cumin, cinnamon, and 16 other spices)
🧴 Braised soy sauce, as needed (to supplement color)
🧂 Salt, 180 grams
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 30 grams
🍬 Rock sugar (a few pieces), a small amount
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for caramelizing sugar, frying braising oil)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing residue)
🫕 Stockpot (for simmering braising broth and braising ingredients)
🕸️ Stainless steel mesh strainer (used as bottom grate to prevent ingredients from sticking)
🧤 Cheesecloth or spice bag (for wrapping spice residue)
🥣 Bowl (used as top weight to press down ingredients and prevent floating)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Ingredient Pre-treatment**: Wash the pork ribs, pork belly, chicken wings, chicken legs, and pig ears. Soak them in salted water overnight.
   **Note**: In summer, refrigerate the soaked ingredients and change the water. This fully draws out the blood, removes the gamey smell, and loosens the meat texture for better flavor absorption.
2. **Caramelizing Sugar**: Add a little oil to the wok, put in 120 grams of rock sugar, and stir over low heat until melted. Keep stirring. When the sugar turns into large bubbles and becomes a jujube-red color, quickly pour in hot water along the edge of the wok. Simmer to make caramelized sugar water and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Do not overcook, or the caramel will become bitter.
3. **Making Braising Oil**: Pour 750 grams of soybean oil into the wok. Add ginger slices and dried chili peppers to the cold oil, then add the washed mixed spices. Fry slowly over low heat to infuse the spice aroma into the oil. When the ginger edges turn slightly yellow and the spices become fragrant and crisp, turn off the heat. Remove the residue and set it aside. This oil is the braising oil, which adds richness and sheen to the braising broth.
4. **Preparing the Braising Broth**: Bring 6 kg (12 jin) of stock to a boil. First, add the prepared caramelized sugar and an appropriate amount of braised soy sauce to adjust the broth to a bright red color. Then, pour in the prepared braising oil. Wrap the fried spice residue in cheesecloth and place it in the broth. Add 180 grams of salt, 30 grams of chicken bouillon, a few pieces of rock sugar, and an appropriate amount of baijiu for seasoning.
   **Key Point**: The braising broth should be saltier than soup meant for direct drinking, so the ingredients become fully flavorful.
5. **Braising in Batches**: Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. First, add the pork ribs and pork belly, and braise for 40 minutes. After 40 minutes, add the chicken legs, chicken wings, and pig ears. Press down with a stainless steel mesh strainer to prevent sticking, then add a bowl on top to prevent ingredients from floating. Continue braising for 20 minutes.
6. **Soaking for Flavor**: Once the braising time is up, turn off the heat. Do not remove the ingredients immediately; let them soak in the braising broth for a period. This is a crucial step for flavor absorption.
7. **Plating and Serving**: After soaking, remove the ingredients and cut them into appropriate shapes. Arrange on a platter with refreshing vegetables like peanuts, cucumber sticks, and lettuce to form a braised platter.

## Health Advice
This braised platter is rich in protein and various spices, suitable for muscle building and satisfying cravings. However, it is high in salt, so individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. Soaking for flavor is the core step for infusing the ingredients.
