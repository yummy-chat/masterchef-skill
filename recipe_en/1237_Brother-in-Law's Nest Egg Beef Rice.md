## Cooking Steps  
1. Wash the two servings of rice thoroughly, add water until it reaches about one knuckle above the rice surface, add a pinch of salt and a little cooking oil, and cook as usual.  
   **Key Point**: Adding salt and oil makes the rice grains separate and gives a sweeter, more fragrant taste.  
2. Cut the ginger into strips and set aside. Slice the beef tenderloin thinly, place it in a bowl, add ginger strips, cooking wine, dark soy sauce, one tablespoon of light soy sauce, one tablespoon of sugar, ground white pepper, and cornstarch. While mixing evenly, gradually add small amounts of water, and finally drizzle in a little sesame oil. Marinate for 20 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: Adding water while mixing makes the beef more tender. Marinating for 20 minutes ensures full flavor absorption.  
3. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, combine two tablespoons of light soy sauce, one tablespoon of sesame oil, one tablespoon of sugar, and a little water. Stir well and set aside.  
4. Once the rice is cooked, open the lid, spread the marinated beef slices evenly over the rice, close the lid again, and continue to cook for about 10 minutes.  
5. Open the lid again, crack an egg into the center of the rice, and immediately close the lid. Use the rice cooker's keep-warm function to cook until the egg white is set and the yolk is about 80% done or to desired doneness.  
   **Key Point**: Use residual heat from the keep-warm function to avoid overcooking and making the egg tough.  
6. Open the lid, sprinkle scallions on top, pour the prepared sauce over everything, and mix the rice, beef, and egg well before serving.  

## Health Tips  
The combination of beef and eggs with rice provides high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for muscle building or those with high physical exertion. However, the sugar and cornstarch used in marinating increase the calorie content. It is recommended to pair with a light vegetable dish to balance nutrition.
