# Spicy Beef and Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Purple onion, appropriate amount (minced)
🫘 Silken tofu, one box (cut into cubes)
🥩 Beef slices, appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili powder, generous amount
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), 1 tablespoon
💧 Water, about 1 cup
🥣 Cornstarch slurry, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 MSG, a pinch
🍬 White sugar, a pinch (to enhance freshness)
🧂 Salt, optional
🌿 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)
🌫️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🍳 Frying pan
🥣 Bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. Slice the garlic and mince the onion. Cut a small slit in the bottom of the silken tofu box, invert it to release, and cut into evenly sized cubes.
2. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the sliced garlic and minced onion, and stir-fry until fragrant.
   **Key point**: Ensure the aroma of garlic and onion is clearly released.
3. Add a generous amount of chili powder and 1 tablespoon of doubanjiang. Stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant.
4. Lay in the beef slices, using chopsticks to separate them. Pan-fry until the beef is fully cooked and changes color.
5. Pour in about 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil, then gently slide in the tofu cubes. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the flavors and the sauce to slightly thicken.
6. Drizzle in the cornstarch slurry in batches, gently stirring the ingredients in the pan, until the sauce thickens to a glossy consistency.
7. Season with light soy sauce, MSG, and a pinch of white sugar. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and white pepper powder. Toss evenly, then remove from heat and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein; beef provides iron, and tofu offers calcium. However, the chili powder and doubanjiang are high in sodium, so it's recommended to pair it with a light stir-fried vegetable dish to balance the flavors. Suitable for warming up in winter; those with high blood pressure should reduce the salt.
