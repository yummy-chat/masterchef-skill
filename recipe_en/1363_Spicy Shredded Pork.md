# Spicy Shredded Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin (tenderloin) 250g
🌿 Cilantro 100g (whole stalk cut into inch-long segments)
🌶️ Dried chili shreds 30-70g (store-bought chili shreds should be rinsed and drained; can reduce amount)
🧅 Scallion 10g (sliced into葱花)
🫚 Ginger 5g (cut into fine shreds)
🧄 Garlic 10g (sliced)
🧂 Salt 3g
🧂 Baking soda 1g
🧂 White pepper powder 0.2g
🧂 White sugar 3g
🫗 Light soy sauce 10g
🫗 Dark soy sauce 5g
🫗 Oyster sauce 10g
🧂 Cornstarch 8g
🧂 Cooking oil as needed (10g for marinating + for frying chili shreds and stir-frying)
🧂 MSG half teaspoon
💧 Water 60g (added in batches to moisten the pork)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Metal bowl (for marinating pork)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Phase**
1. Cut the pork loin into shreds along the grain, rinse twice with water, then thoroughly squeeze out excess moisture.
   **Key**: Cutting along the grain yields better texture; moisture must be fully squeezed out, otherwise it affects the subsequent moistening.
2. Add salt, baking soda, white pepper powder, white sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce to the pork shreds. Mix well by hand, stirring in one direction until the shreds become tacky, sticky, and clump together.
   **Note**: The amount of white pepper powder must be precise; too much will create an off-flavor. Baking soda is key for tenderizing; use accurate amount.
3. Add the 60g of water in 3 batches to the pork. After each addition, stir vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed and there is noticeable resistance before adding the next batch.
   **Key**: Adding water in batches allows the pork shreds to fully absorb moisture, resulting in a smoother, more tender texture.
4. Add cornstarch and mix well to coat, then drizzle in 10g of cooking oil and stir evenly to prevent sticking during cooking.
5. Slice the scallion into葱花, cut ginger into fine shreds, slice the garlic, and cut the whole cilantro stalks into small inch-long segments.
6. Rinse the dried chili shreds several times with water to remove salt, then drain thoroughly and set aside.

**Cooking Phase**
7. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok. Add the drained chili shreds to cold oil, then turn to high heat and stir-fry to evaporate moisture. When dense small bubbles appear in the oil, reduce to medium heat.
   **Heat judgment**: Fry over medium heat for about one minute until the chili shreds become firm and stand upright, and some sections turn slightly yellow. Remove from heat and let residual heat finish frying. Remove the chili shreds, leaving the chili oil in the wok.
   **Key**: Residual heat will make the chili shreds crispier and prevent burning.
8. Heat the chili oil in the wok to about 50% hot. Add the marinated pork shreds along with the scallion, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry over high heat, quickly loosening the shreds with chopsticks, for about one minute until the pork shreds are fully cooked and separated.
9. Add the cilantro segments and stir-fry quickly until the cilantro wilts. Add half a teaspoon of MSG, stir evenly, then turn off the heat.
10. First, transfer the cilantro and pork to a plate, then top with the fried chili shreds.
    **Key**: Placing the chili shreds on top afterward maintains their crisp texture and prevents them from softening.
