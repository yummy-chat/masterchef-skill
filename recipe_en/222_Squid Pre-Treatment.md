# Squid Pre-Treatment  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦑 1 squid (fresh, intact and glossy skin)  
⚠️ This video only demonstrates pre-treatment; no seasonings are used.  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board  
✂️ Kitchen shears  
🚰 Water basin/sink (for cleaning and working in water)  

## Steps  
1. Check whether the squid skin is intact and glossy to assess its freshness.  
2. Using kitchen shears, cut along the connection below the squid’s head. Be careful to avoid muscle tissue, and separate out the internal organs. Reserve the edible reproductive glands, and discard the liver and ink sac.  
3. Work in water to remove the internal organs, then rinse clean. Pull out the transparent cartilage on the squid’s back plate and the gills on both sides.  
4. Peel the squid skin: The skin has three layers. You must tear off the innermost layer to peel it completely. Start tearing from the tail, and treat parts separately for greater convenience. Removing the skin prevents the squid from becoming tough after stir-frying.  
5. Handle the squid head: Cut open the head and clean the area where it connects to the internal organs. Insert kitchen shears into the connection, twist to open it up, then squeeze out and remove the teeth from the squid’s beak.  
6. Remove the squid eyes: **You must work in water** to prevent eye juice from splattering. Use your fingers to push from the outside inward, squeeze out the eyes, and pull them off.  
7. Handle the squid tentacles: Remove the cartilage on the suction cups. You can tear off the entire strip of suction-cup cartilage with the skin, or keep the suction cups according to personal preference.  
8. Rinse all processed parts again until clean, and place them in a basin for later use.  

## Health Advice  
Squid is highly fresh and low in fat, making it a quality source of protein suitable for people trying to lose weight. When handling, be sure to remove the ink sac and eye juice to avoid soiling clothing.
