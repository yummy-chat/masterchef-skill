# Braised Seafood with Chili
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧅 Red Onion – 1 (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic – about 5 cloves (minced)
🌿 Fennel – 1 small handful (chopped, added in two batches)
🍋 Small Lime – about 4 (halved)
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers – 5-6 (cut into small pieces)
🍅 Canned Tomatoes – 1 can (about 240g, with juice)
🦪 Mussels – about 10 pieces
🦐 Shrimp – about 10 pieces
🐟 Yellow Croaker Fillets – about 4 slices
🐚 Scallops – about 6 pieces
🫒 Olive Oil – as needed (enough to coat the bottom of the pot)
🧂 Paprika – about 1 teaspoon
🧂 Chili Powder – about 1/2 teaspoon
🌿 Herbes de Provence – about 1 teaspoon
🍺 White Beer – about half a bottle
🥫 Tomato Ketchup – about 2 tablespoons
🧂 Salt – to taste

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🍳 Small soup pot
🥄 Spatula/Wooden spatula
🌀 Blender/Food processor
🥛 Measuring cup

## Preparation Steps
1. Dice the onion, mince the garlic, chop the fennel, and halve the small limes. Cut the dried chili peppers into small pieces with scissors. Wash all seafood and drain well.
2. Heat the soup pot over low heat, add enough olive oil, then add the diced onion and minced garlic. Slowly sauté until the onion is soft, translucent, and fragrant.
3. Add the dried chili pepper pieces and stir-fry evenly.
4. Add the whole can of tomatoes (with juice), paprika, chili powder, and Herbes de Provence. Stir-fry evenly.
5. Pour in the white beer and stir to combine.
   **Key point**: White beer is recommended for added flavor complexity.
6. Pour all the contents of the pot into a blender and blend into a smooth, lump-free thick soup.
   **Note**: If you don’t have a blender, you can skip this step and use a chunky broth instead.
7. Pour the blended thick soup back into the pot, add the tomato ketchup, and stir to combine.
8. Add the mussels and shrimp to the soup, cover the pot, and simmer over low heat for about 3-5 minutes, until the shrimp turn pink and the mussels open.
   **Key point**: Add quick-cooking ingredients later to avoid overcooking.
9. Remove the lid, add the yellow croaker fillets and scallops, and continue to simmer over low heat for about 2 minutes, until the fish turns white and the scallops are cooked through.
10. Adjust the seasoning with salt to taste, scatter in the halved small limes and most of the chopped fennel, stir briefly, then turn off the heat.
    **Key point**: Add the small limes last to preserve their fresh, tangy flavor and aroma.
11. When serving, first arrange the solid seafood in the bowl, then ladle the soup over it, and finally garnish with the remaining chopped fennel.

## Health Advice
This braised seafood with chili is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or mental workers. People with gout should limit consumption of the soup, as mussels are slightly high in purines.
