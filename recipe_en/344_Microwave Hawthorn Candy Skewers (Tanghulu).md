# Microwave Hawthorn Candy Skewers (Tanghulu)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍓 Strawberries – appropriate amount (choose varieties with low juice content and a sweet-sour taste)  
🧂 White granulated sugar – syrup ratio: fill to the first groove line of a mineral water bottle  
💧 Water – syrup ratio: fill to the third groove line of a mineral water bottle  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🧴 Empty mineral water bottle – cut open and used as a measuring tool  
🍵 Heat-resistant ceramic bowl  
🕒 Microwave  
🥢 Chopsticks – for stirring, testing the syrup, skewering fruit, and dipping in syrup  
🍽️ Plate – for cooling and setting  
🫙 Glass bowl – for washing strawberries  
🌯 Plastic wrap – to cover strawberries before use  

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the strawberries and dry them completely or wipe off all surface moisture. Skewer them with toothpicks or bamboo skewers and cover with plastic wrap for later use.  
   - Note: Any moisture on the fruit's surface will prevent the syrup from adhering.

2. Prepare the syrup: Use the cut mineral water bottle as a measuring tool. Pour white granulated sugar up to the first groove line, then add water up to the third groove line.  
3. Pour the sugar and water into a heat-resistant ceramic bowl. Place it in the microwave and heat on maximum power for 30 seconds each time.  
4. Repeat heating and observe; when the syrup changes from white to a light yellow color, proceed to the next test step.  
5. Prepare a bowl of cold water. Dip a chopstick into the syrup and then into the cold water:  
   - If the syrup is soft and sticky, heat for another 30 seconds and test again.  
   - If the syrup becomes hard and brittle quickly, making a crisp sound when tapped, the syrup is ready.  
   - Key point: This is the only reliable method to judge the syrup's consistency.

6. Quickly rotate the skewered strawberries in the syrup one full turn, then immediately remove them. Hold them in the air for about 10 seconds before placing them on a plate.  
   - Note: Do not overcoat—a thin layer is sufficient. Holding them in the air prevents syrup drips from sticking to the plate.

7. Let the skewers cool until the sugar coating is completely hard and brittle, then enjoy.

## Health Tips
This tanghulu recipe uses a microwave to make the syrup—a convenient and efficient method. Be sure the fruit surface is completely dry, or the sugar coating will not adhere. This treat is high in sugar; people with diabetes or those controlling sugar intake should only taste it sparingly. It is best enjoyed as a small winter snack.
