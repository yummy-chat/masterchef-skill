# Pickled Chinese Cabbage (Sour Cabbage)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Chinese cabbage (recommended Shandong cabbage for soft texture; Beijing No. 3 variety also acceptable)
🧂 Edible salt (use at a ratio of 10g salt per 1kg cabbage)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍲 Large iron pot
🥄 Slotted spoon, large ladle
🫙 Large clay fermentation jar
🪨 Rectangular stone weight for pressing (or clean river stones, weighing at least 50–60 jin / 25–30 kg)
🪣 Stainless steel basin

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the Chinese cabbage, cut off the root end, and split it lengthwise into two halves.
2. Bring water to a boil in a large iron pot. Place the cabbage halves cut-side down into the boiling water and blanch for 30–40 seconds, until all leaves turn bright green. Remove, drain, and spread out to cool.
3. Line the bottom of a clean large clay jar with a few layers of outer damaged leaves. Then arrange the cooled cabbage halves cut-side down tightly and neatly in the jar. Evenly sprinkle a layer of salt over each layer of cabbage.
4. After all cabbage is layered, shape the top so it is slightly higher in the center and lower around the edges. Sprinkle any remaining salt over the top.
5. Place a clean heavy stone weight on top, ensuring the cabbage is firmly pressed down and will not float up.
6. Set the jar in an environment at 8–15°C for natural fermentation, which takes about 20 days to 1 month.
- **Note**: During fermentation, the cabbage will gradually release liquid. If too much liquid accumulates near the rim, use a clean tool to skim off some, always keeping the cabbage submerged in the brine.
- **Key observation**: The brine will become cloudy and develop sourness and bubbles—this is normal fermentation. If the room temperature is too high, open windows promptly to lower it and prevent the pickled cabbage from rotting.
7. Once the cabbage turns uniformly yellow-brown or golden-yellow and develops a rich fermented aroma, it is ready to use.

## Health Advice
Pickled Chinese cabbage is a traditional winter food in northeastern China. It is rich in probiotics, aids digestion, and pairs well with greasy stews to help balance the stomach. However, due to its high salt content, those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
