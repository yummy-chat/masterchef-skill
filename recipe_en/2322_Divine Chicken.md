# Divine Chicken
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients  
🐔 Whole chicken 1 (deboned, yielding a complete chicken skin pouch)  
🥓 Jinhua ham appropriate amount (for making broth)  
🐾 Chicken feet appropriate amount (for making broth)  
🥩 Pork skin appropriate amount (for making broth)  
🦀 Hairy crab appropriate amount (steamed, then shelled for crab meat, crab roe, and crab powder)  
🌰 Lotus seeds appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
💠 Water caltrop appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🥔 Water chestnut appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🧅 Arrowhead appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🌿 Wild rice stem appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🥒 Lotus root appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🌾 Gorgon fruit appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🌱 Water shield appropriate amount (one of the Eight Water Immortals)  
🦐 River shrimp appropriate amount (blanched before adding)  
🐖 Pork leaf lard appropriate amount (for wrapping the whole chicken)  
🍃 Fresh lotus leaf appropriate amount (for wrapping the whole chicken)  
🧵 Cheesecloth appropriate amount (for breathable wrapping)  

## Seasonings  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (stuffed into the chicken cavity during marination)  
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (stuffed into the chicken cavity during marination, added when making broth)  
🌿 Spices appropriate amount (stuffed into the chicken cavity during marination)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marination, seasoning the filling)  
🍶 Huadiao wine appropriate amount (for marination)  
🥃 Soy sauce appropriate amount (for marination)  
🥃 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (for coloring during marination)  
🌶️ White pepper appropriate amount (for marination, making broth)  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount (for marination)  
🧄 Old ginger appropriate amount (for removing odor when making broth)  
🍷 Yellow wine (Nü'er Hong) appropriate amount (mixed with yellow mud for sealing)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
✂️ Scissors (for deboning the whole chicken)  
🔪 Knife (for preparing ingredients)  
🪓 Cutting board (work surface)  
🥣 Bowl (for marination, holding)  
🍲 Clay pot (for making broth)  
🪵 Bamboo grate (lined at the bottom of the clay pot to prevent sticking)  
🔥 Oven (for baking the mud-sealed chicken)  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Debone the whole chicken**
   Cut at the joints from the upper part downward along the bone structure, completely separating the skeleton while keeping the chicken skin pouch intact. Be careful not to puncture the skin during the process.
   **Key Point**: After deboning, inject clean water into the chicken skin pouch to test for leaks; it must be watertight, otherwise, the broth will seep out during subsequent cooking.

2. **Prepare the broth**
   Line the bottom of a clay pot with a bamboo grate, add Jinhua ham, chicken feet, and pork skin. Pour in boiling mineral water, add old ginger and white pepper, and simmer over low heat for 5 hours until the broth is rich and creamy white.
   **Key Point**: The broth must be completely cooled and solidified into a jelly before it can be used to mix the filling.

3. **Prepare the crab powder**
   Steam the hairy crabs until cooked, then cool. Remove the crab meat, crab roe, and crab powder and set aside.

4. **Stir-fry the Eight Water Immortals filling**
   Dice the lotus seeds, water caltrop, water chestnut, arrowhead, wild rice stem, lotus root, and gorgon fruit into small pieces. Heat oil in a wok, add scallion and ginger, stir-fry until golden brown, then remove. Add the crab powder and stir-fry until fragrant, then add the diced Eight Water Immortals and stir-fry. Season only with salt to highlight the natural flavors of the ingredients. Turn off the heat, then mix in the solidified broth jelly, blanched river shrimp, and water shield. Stir well and allow to cool naturally into a filling.

5. **Marinate**
   Evenly rub salt, Huadiao wine, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, white pepper, and white sugar all over the inside and outside of the deboned chicken skin pouch. Stuff scallion, ginger, and spices into the chicken cavity. Marinate for 3 hours.

6. **Stuff and seal**
   Fill the chicken cavity completely with the cooled and solidified filling. Tie the neck area tightly with kitchen twine to prevent the filling from spilling out during baking.

7. **Layer the wrapping**
   Wrap the whole chicken tightly, first with pork leaf lard, then with fresh lotus leaves, and finally with cheesecloth. Secure each layer tightly with kitchen twine.
   **Note**: The cheesecloth must remain breathable; do not use plastic wrap or other airtight materials.

8. **Apply mud and bake**
   Mix thirty-year Nü'er Hong yellow wine with yellow mud to form a paste. Evenly coat the outermost cheesecloth layer with this mud as an insulating layer. Place in the oven and bake at high heat for 1 hour and 30 minutes.

9. **Crack open and enjoy**
   Once baked, remove from the oven. Crack open the hardened mud shell, peel away the lotus leaves and cheesecloth. The chicken should be tender, the skin crispy and aromatic, and the filling juicy. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice
This Divine Chicken dish incorporates the Eight Water Immortals and crab powder, offering a well-balanced and nutritious meal ideal for autumn nourishment. The innovative design of using solidified broth jelly in the filling is creative, but attention must be paid to controlling the salt content.
