# Bing Dwen Dwen Meat Buns  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍞 All-purpose flour, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, a little (for activating yeast)  
🥄 Yeast, 2 grams  
🥩 Pork and scallion filling, as needed (prepared in advance)  
🌈 Food coloring (black, red, yellow, green, blue, etc.), a little  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board/work surface  
🥢 Chopsticks  
♨️ Steamer  
🌡️ Thermometer  
🧻 Steamer cloth  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Note**: Water temperature should be around 40°C for optimal yeast activation. The dough needs to be kneaded until it reaches the "clean bowl, clean hands, clean dough" state.  
2. Divide the kneaded dough into several small portions, add different colors of food coloring to each, and knead thoroughly until evenly colored, creating black, red, yellow, green, blue, and other colored doughs. Keep the white dough as the main body.  
3. Roll out the white dough into a round wrapper, fill with the pre-prepared pork and scallion filling, seal the opening tightly, and roll into a ball to form the panda's body.  
4. Referring to the Bing Dwen Dwen design, shape the black dough into ears, eye sockets, nose, and limbs. Attach them one by one to the white dough and decorate to create the expression. Then, roll the colored dough into thin strips and wrap them around the face as the "ice ribbon."  
5. Add warm water at about 40°C to the steamer, place a damp steamer cloth on the rack, put the shaped raw buns inside, and cover with the lid for proofing.  
   **Key**: Proof until the volume expands to 1.5–2 times the original size, and a gentle finger press slowly springs back. In winter, this takes about 40 minutes.  
6. After proofing, turn the heat to high and bring the water to a boil, then continue steaming over high heat for 10 minutes.  
7. **Note**: The water temperature should be around 40°C for optimal yeast activation. The dough needs to be kneaded until it reaches the "clean bowl, clean hands, clean dough" state.  

## Health Advice  
Bing Dwen Dwen shaped buns provide high-quality protein from the pork and scallion filling, and the dough supplies carbohydrates. They are suitable for the whole family to make together. Note that the amount of food coloring should be minimal, and only edible grade coloring should be used for children.
