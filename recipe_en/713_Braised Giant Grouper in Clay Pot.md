# Braised Giant Grouper in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 giant grouper (rinsed clean, fish head cut in half, body cut into thick slices)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (green parts cut into sections, white parts julienned)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (some sliced, some julienned)  
🧄 Garlic, several cloves (minced, for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ 1 red chili pepper (minced, for dipping sauce)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (for marinating fish and dipping sauce)  
🫘 1 tablespoon yellow bean paste  
🫘 Several fermented black beans  
🌿 A pinch of white pepper  
🥔 2 tablespoons sweet potato starch  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (to coat the bottom of the clay pot and for drizzling)  
🧂 Aged vinegar, as needed (for dipping sauce)  
🍬 A pinch of sugar (for dipping sauce)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫕 Clay pot  
🥄 Chopsticks  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the giant grouper clean. Cut the fish head in half and the body into thick slices. Drain well.  
2. Add light soy sauce, yellow bean paste, fermented black beans, white pepper, and sweet potato starch to the fish pieces. Mix well and marinate for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: Sweet potato starch can be used as a substitute for potato starch, making the fish more tender and improving the coating.  

3. Line the bottom of the clay pot with scallion green sections and ginger slices. Brush with a layer of sesame oil to prevent sticking and enhance aroma.  
4. Arrange the marinated fish pieces over the scallions and ginger. Cover with the lid and cook over medium heat for 10 minutes.  
**Key point**: Do not add any water throughout the process. Use the fish's own moisture and the marinade to create steam, preserving the original flavor.  

5. Once the fish has changed color and an aroma is released, turn off the heat. Sprinkle with the prepared julienned scallions, ginger, and red chili pepper. Drizzle with a little hot sesame oil to release the fragrance, then serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
Giant grouper is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, making it suitable for people needing to boost immunity, especially for spring nourishment. Using sweet potato starch during marination helps lock in moisture, making the flesh more tender. However, yellow bean paste and light soy sauce are high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should control their intake.
