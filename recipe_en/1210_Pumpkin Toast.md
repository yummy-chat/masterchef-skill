# Pumpkin Toast
Cuisine: Others
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 500g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 60g
🥚 Egg yolk 17g
💧 Water 135g
🎃 Pumpkin puree 150g (made from roasted pumpkin with skin)
🍞 High-sugar-tolerant fresh yeast 15g (or high-sugar-tolerant dry yeast 5g)
🧈 Unsalted butter 60g (softened in advance)
🍬 Granulated sugar 50g
🧂 Salt 5g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔥 Electric oven (with fermentation, baking, and crisp reheating modes)
📦 450g lidded toast loaf pan
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥛 Measuring cup
🔪 Dough scraper
🫓 Rolling pin
🛡️ Plastic wrap/mixing bowl
👐 Kneading mat or work surface
🔪 Knife

## Steps
1. **Roast the pumpkin**: Slice the pumpkin with skin into thin pieces, roast in the oven until soft, cooked, and slightly browned on the surface. Let it cool, then mash into a puree.
   - **Key point**: Roasting with the skin adds a caramelized aroma.
2. **Autolyse the dough**: Make a well in the bread flour, add water, whole egg liquid, egg yolk, pumpkin puree, granulated sugar, and salt. Mix the liquids first, then incorporate all the flour. Knead until no dry flour remains.
   - **Note**: Do not add yeast or butter at this stage.
3. **Rest and relax**: Cover the dough with an inverted bowl and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour, allowing full hydration and initial gluten development.
4. **Add yeast and butter**: Add fresh yeast and softened butter to the rested dough. Begin kneading, incorporating slapping and folding motions.
   - **Key point**: With a high amount of butter, the dough will be very sticky at first. Continue kneading until the butter is fully absorbed, slapping for about 10 minutes.
5. **Chill and rest**: Wrap the kneaded dough and refrigerate for 10 minutes.
6. **Check gluten and shape**: Remove the dough; it should stretch into a thin, translucent windowpane. Roll the dough into a large thin sheet, roll it up from top to bottom, then divide into 6 equal portions.
   - **Purpose**: Realign the gluten for a finer texture.
7. **Divide and initial rest**: Round each small piece of dough, cover with plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 10–15 minutes.
8. **First roll**: Take one dough piece, roll it out into a long tongue shape, thinning it well. Flip it over, press the bottom edge thin, and roll it up from top to bottom.
   - **Key point**: Roll as long and thin as possible without tearing the gluten—this is essential for a stringy texture.
9. **Intermediate rest**: Cover the rolled dough pieces and let them rest again for 10–15 minutes.
10. **Second roll**: Perform a second rolling, thinning, flipping, pressing the bottom edge thin, and rolling up again.
    - **Key point**: This is the final shaping; again, roll as long and thin as possible.
11. **Pan and final fermentation**: Place 3 dough rolls into each toast pan, with the seams facing the same direction. Cover the lid, place in the oven using the fermentation mode at 38°C, and ferment for about 1 hour, until the highest point of the dough is about 3.5 cm from the top of the pan.
    - **Note**: In summer, check frequently to avoid over-fermentation leading to collapse.
12. **Bake**: After fermentation, without preheating, set the oven to bake mode. Bake at 180°C (top and bottom heat) for 35 minutes.
13. **Remove and store**: Immediately remove from the oven, tap the pan to release the loaf, and place it on a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and seal in a bag for frozen storage.
    - **Reheating recommendation**: Before serving, reheat in the oven using the “crisp” or “air fry” mode at 180°C for about 18 minutes for the best texture.

## Health Advice
The combination of pumpkin puree and bread flour makes this toast rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene, suitable for those needing eye care and blood sugar control. Note the relatively high sugar and butter content; it is recommended to consume in small portions during weight loss periods.
