# Shandong Salad
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine (Shandong Cuisine)
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Cabbage head - appropriate amount (shredded, not too thin)
🫘 Dried tofu skin - appropriate amount (shredded)
🧄 Garlic - appropriate amount (minced)
🍜 Vermicelli - appropriate amount (soaked in warm water for 10 minutes, then cut once)
🌿 Cilantro - appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili oil - appropriate amount
🫒 Sesame oil - appropriate amount
🧂 MSG - 1 spoon
🍬 White sugar - 2 spoons
🧂 Salt - 1.5 spoons
🍶 White vinegar - appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Shred the cabbage head, with the thickness adjusted to maintain a crisp texture.
2. Shred the dried tofu skin into thin strips.
3. Mince the garlic into fine pieces.
4. Soak the vermicelli in warm water for 10 minutes until softened, then drain and cut once to prevent excessive length.
5. Place the shredded cabbage, shredded dried tofu skin, minced garlic, and soaked and cut vermicelli into a large bowl, then add cilantro.
6. First drizzle chili oil over the top of the ingredients, then drizzle with sesame oil.
**Key Point**: Add oil first to allow the oil to coat the ingredients, especially the cabbage shreds, sealing them to prevent water from seeping out.
7. Add 1 spoon of MSG, 2 spoons of white sugar, and 1.5 spoons of salt, then stir thoroughly with chopsticks to blend and infuse the seasonings.
8. Finally, drizzle an appropriate amount of white vinegar.
**Key Point**: Add vinegar last; the sourness of white vinegar and the sweetness of white sugar combine to create a balanced sweet and sour-spicy flavor.
9. Mix well again and plate, ensuring there is no liquid pooling at the bottom of the dish.

## Health Tips
This cold dish is rich in dietary fiber and plant protein, making it suitable for weight control and vegetarians. Soaking the vermicelli until soft and cutting it once makes it easier to mix, but avoid soaking for too long as it may affect the texture. Adding vinegar at the end preserves its refreshing sourness; using less oil and salt makes it healthier.
