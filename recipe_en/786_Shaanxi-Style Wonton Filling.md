# Shaanxi-Style Wonton Filling
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Main Ingredients
🥩 450g lean ground pork
🥓 50g fatty ground pork (ratio approximately 1:9 with lean meat, keeping it light and not greasy)
🥟 Wonton wrappers, as needed
🥚 1 egg (for added tenderness and binding)
🌿 Scallions, as needed (for making scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for making scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (for making scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water)

### Seasonings
🧂 7g salt
🧂 3g MSG
🧂 2g ground white pepper
🧂 A small amount of baking soda (to make the filling fluffy, not dense)
🫗 Approximately 80g scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water (prepared in advance by steeping in boiling water, then strained)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for sealing the filling and locking in moisture)
🌿 Dried seaweed, as needed (for the soup base)
🧅 Chopped scallions, as needed (for the soup base)
🧂 A pinch of salt, MSG, and ground white pepper (for seasoning the soup base)
🦴 Bone broth or boiling water, as needed
🫒 A few drops of sesame oil (drizzled after serving in a bowl)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowls (for holding the filling, preparing the scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water, and steeping the soup base)
🥄 Spoons (for measuring seasonings)
🍲 Pot (for cooking the wontons)
🥢 Slotted spoon (for scooping the wontons)
🍜 Soup bowls (for serving the finished dish)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place scallion segments, ginger slices, and Sichuan peppercorns in a bowl. Pour boiling water over them and let steep until fragrant. Once cooled, strain out the solids and reserve approximately 80g of the scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water for later use.
**Key point**: Prepare this in advance. Use only the liquid, not the solids.

2. Place 450g of lean ground pork and 50g of fatty ground pork in a large bowl.
**Key point**: The proportion of fatty meat in wonton filling should be much lower than in dumpling or bun fillings, to keep it light and not greasy.

3. Add 7g salt, 3g MSG, and 2g ground white pepper to the meat mixture. Mix well with your hands.
**Key point**: Use only these three basic seasonings. Do not add colored seasonings like soy sauce or oyster sauce, to highlight the natural savory flavor of the meat.

4. Add a small amount of baking soda powder to the seasoned meat mixture. Mix well.
**Key point**: Baking soda causes the meat filling to release gas when heated, preventing the cooked filling from becoming dense, hard, or sinking to the bottom.

5. Gradually add the scallion-ginger-Sichuan peppercorn water to the meat mixture in small portions. After each addition, vigorously stir the mixture in one direction with your hand until the liquid is fully absorbed.
**Key point**: Add the liquid in portions and always stir in the same direction to develop the texture. Purpose: First, to allow the meat filling to absorb moisture and become tender. Second, to infuse the filling with the aromatic flavors of the scallion, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns to remove any gaminess.

6. Crack a raw egg into the meat filling that has been hydrated. Again, mix thoroughly with your hand until evenly combined.
**Key point**: The egg adds tenderness to the filling and acts as a binder, helping the filling hold together without falling apart during cooking.

7. Smooth the mixed filling flat in the bowl. Drizzle a layer of cooking oil over the surface and spread it evenly.
**Key point**: This step is "sealing with oil" to lock in the moisture inside the filling and prevent it from drying out.

8. Take a wonton wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling on it. Loosely gather the wrapper with your hand and use a chopstick to push the center to seal.
**Key point**: The wrapping method is simple. Because the filling has binding properties, it will not easily fall apart in the pot.

9. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the wontons and cook until they all float to the surface and the wrappers appear puffed and translucent.
**Doneness check**: Wontons floating and wrappers puffed indicates they are cooked through. At this point, the wontons will not be mushy or falling apart.

10. Place dried seaweed, chopped scallions, a pinch of salt, MSG, and ground white pepper in the bottom of a serving bowl. Pour in boiling bone broth or hot water to create the soup base.
**Key point**: None.

11. Scoop the cooked wontons into the soup bowl. Finally, drizzle a few drops of sesame oil on top before serving.
**Key point**: None.

## Health Advice
This wonton filling uses baking soda for a fluffy texture, and the fat ratio is controlled at 1:9, resulting in a light, non-greasy mouthfeel. It is suitable for those who wish to control their calorie intake while enjoying a hot soup dish. Note that the amount of baking soda should not be excessive, otherwise the wonton wrappers may puff up too much.
