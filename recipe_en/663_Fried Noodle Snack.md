# Fried Noodle Snack
Category: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍝 Raw noodles (regular dried noodles or fresh noodles, cut into approximately 3-4 cm lengths)
🍇 Raisins (one handful)
🤍 White sesame seeds (to taste)
🖤 Black sesame seeds (to taste)
💧 Water (one tablespoon)
🍬 White sugar (one tablespoon, ratio to water approximately 1:1)
🫒 Cooking oil (for deep frying) (generous amount, at medium-low temperature of 170°C/340°F)
🫒 Cooking oil (for caramelizing sugar) (a small amount, about a few drops, added along edge of pan)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon
🥄 Spoon
📦 Square tray or baking sheet
🔧 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the raw noodles on a cutting board and cut them into approximately 3-4 cm lengths. Set aside in a plate.
2. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to medium-low temperature (170°C/340°F). Gently scatter the cut noodles and slide them into the oil along the edge of the wok. Fry over medium heat for 2-3 minutes; avoid low heat which can cause the noodles to absorb too much oil. Once the noodles are golden brown and crispy, remove them with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.
3. Clean the wok, then add one tablespoon of water and one tablespoon of white sugar. To prevent sticking, drizzle a small amount of oil along the edge of the wok.
4. Cook over medium heat for about 2 minutes, until the syrup forms fish-eye sized bubbles and turns slightly yellow. Then, reduce to the lowest heat.
5. Continue cooking until the bubbles change from large to dense and small, and the syrup turns a light yellow color. Immediately add the fried noodles, raisins, white sesame seeds, and black sesame seeds. Stir-fry quickly to coat each noodle strand evenly with syrup.
6. Pour the mixture into a square tray while still hot. Use a spatula to press it down firmly and evenly. The tighter it is pressed, the less likely it will crumble when cut.
7. Let it cool until no longer hot to the touch. Invert the pressed noodle block onto a cutting board and cut it into evenly sized pieces. Serve immediately.

## Health Suggestion
This dish is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended as an occasional indulgent snack. Enjoying it with green tea can help reduce greasiness. Those with high blood sugar should control their sugar intake; the amount of white sugar can be halved or replaced with a sugar substitute.
