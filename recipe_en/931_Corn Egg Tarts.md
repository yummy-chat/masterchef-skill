# Corn Egg Tarts
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌽 Raw corn kernels, about 100g (from half a cob of corn)
🥛 2 boxes of Want Want milk (125ml each; 1 for making corn milk, 1 for the egg tart liquid)
🥚 2 whole eggs
🥚 2 egg yolks
🌽 Cornstarch, about 15g (3 bottle caps' worth)
🍬 White sugar, about 3–4 bottle caps' worth (2–3 for corn jam, 1 for egg tart liquid)
🍯 Honey, a little (for brushing before toasting)
🌽 Cooked corn kernels, about half a cob's worth
🥧 6 plain egg tart shells
🥧 Plain egg tart liquid, as needed (or use the simple homemade recipe below)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Food processor or blender
🥄 Strainer
🍳 Saucepan
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Stirring rod or chopsticks
♨️ Air fryer or oven
🔫 Kitchen torch (optional)
🥣 Bowls or basins
🧁 Tartlet foil cups

## Steps
1. Place the raw corn kernels and 1 box of Want Want milk into a food processor. Blend until a smooth paste forms. Strain through a strainer to remove the corn husks and reserve the smooth liquid.
   **Key point**: The ratio of raw corn to milk is about 1:1. Straining yields a fine corn milk liquid.

2. Pour the strained corn milk liquid into a saucepan. Add 2 egg yolks, about 15g of cornstarch, and 2–3 bottle caps' worth of white sugar. Stir well. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens noticeably and becomes a semi-solid paste. Turn off the heat. Strain the jam again, then mix in the pre-cooked corn kernels until evenly combined.
   **Key point**: Keep the heat low and stir continuously throughout to prevent lumps. A second straining makes the corn milk jam even smoother.

3. Make the simple egg tart liquid: In a bowl, crack 2 whole eggs, pour in 1 box of Want Want milk, add 1 bottle cap's worth of white sugar, and mix thoroughly. Strain once. Alternatively, you may use pre-made egg tart liquid directly.
   **Key point**: Want Want milk has a higher sweetness level and can replace heavy cream. This recipe's sweetness is about 30% sweet.

4. Pour the egg tart liquid into the tart shells. Place into a preheated air fryer or oven and bake:
   - Air fryer: 200°C for about 13 minutes
   - Oven: 200°C for about 25 minutes
   Bake until the tart shells are golden brown and the egg tart liquid has set with some charred spots.

5. On the surface of each baked plain egg tart, spread a thick layer of corn milk jam. Place a slice of cooked corn kernel on top. Brush with a little honey, sprinkle with white sugar, and torch with a kitchen torch until the surface turns a caramel color.
   **Note**: The torch step is just for color. If you do not have a torch, you can omit it. When torching, control the flame to avoid burning.

## Health Advice
This dessert uses Want Want milk instead of heavy cream, resulting in moderate sweetness. Corn and eggs provide dietary fiber and protein, making it suitable as a summer dessert. However, the calorie content is not low, so it is recommended to eat 1–2 per serving.
