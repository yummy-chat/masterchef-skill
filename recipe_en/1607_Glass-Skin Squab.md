# Glass-Skin Squab

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🕊️ Squab, as needed (choose squab around 25 days old)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🧅 Shallots, as needed  
🫚 Ginger, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed  
🌾 Cake flour, 30g  
🌾 Bread flour, 15g  
🍞 Baking powder, 5g  
🌾 Wheat starch, 5g  
🧂 Baking soda, 10g (5g for the skin water, 5g for the glass batter)  
🥚 Eggs, 2 (use the egg whites)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🍬 Sugar, as needed  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, as needed  
🌿 Five-spice powder, as needed  
🥃 Erguotou liquor, about two capfuls  
🍶 White vinegar, as needed  
🍶 Zhejiang vinegar, as needed  
🍯 Maltose, as needed  

## Equipment & Tools  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
✂️ Scissors  
🌀 Blender  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Pot  
🌬️ Fan  
📦 Plastic wrap  

## Steps  
1. Slaughter and clean the squab, removing the internal organs, lymph nodes, wing tips, and feet.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the lymph nodes and areas prone to burning are thoroughly cleaned to guarantee a beautiful finished product.  

2. Prepare the marinade: Mix the salt, sugar, thirteen-spice powder, and five-spice powder evenly, then rub it all over the inside and outside of the squab.  
3. Make the vegetable aromatics: Place the garlic, shallots, ginger, scallions, and Erguotou together in a blender and blend until crushed. Rub this mixture into the cavity of the squab to add layers of fragrance.  
4. Marinating: After rubbing the marinade evenly, marinate for at least 2 hours. It is recommended to marinate overnight in the refrigerator to ensure the meat and bones are fully flavored.  
5. Prepare the skin water: Mix water, white vinegar, Zhejiang vinegar, Erguotou, and maltose together. Finally, add 5g of baking soda and stir until well combined. Set aside.  
6. Prepare the glass batter: Mix 30g cake flour, 15g bread flour, 5g baking powder, 5g wheat starch, and 5g baking soda. Add enough water to form a thin paste and let it rest for 2 hours to ferment. Beat the 2 egg whites until frothy, then pour them into the fermented batter and mix until smooth.  
7. Rinse the marinated squab quickly under running water to remove the surface aromatics. Do not soak for too long, or the flavor will be lost.  
8. Blanch to set: Pour boiling water evenly over the squab's body to tighten the skin, making it easier to apply the batter later.  
9. Immediately immerse the squab in cold water to cool the skin and prevent oil from rendering. Then hang it up and use a fan to dry the surface.  
10. First coat of skin water: Pour the skin water evenly over the squab's surface. Use the fan again to dry it completely.  
11. Apply the glass batter: Once the skin water is fully dry, evenly pour the glass batter (mixed with egg whites) over the squab's skin. You can apply it twice in succession to increase thickness. Dry again with the fan until the skin turns white.  
12. First frying: Heat the oil to around 140°C (284°F). Place the squab in the oil, reduce the heat to low, and deep-fry for 8 minutes. After about 3 minutes, the skin will turn a caramel color. After about 5 minutes, the squab will float. Fry gently over low heat to ensure the inside is fully cooked.  
13. Remove the squab from the oil. Turn the heat to maximum and continuously pour the hot oil over the squab's body for 2–3 minutes, until the squab is fully cooked and the skin is golden red and crispy. Pouring hot oil over high heat helps render excess fat and creates a glass-like, crispy crust.  
   **Key point**: Pouring hot oil over high heat is crucial for forming the crispy skin. Continue pouring until the color is even.  

14. Once done, remove from the pot, chop into pieces, and plate for serving.  

## Health Suggestions  
This Glass-Skin Squab undergoes three drying steps and two frying steps. It is rich in protein but high in calories. It is recommended to serve it with light vegetables to balance the oiliness. Enjoy it as an occasional treat rather than a daily nourishing meal. The crispy effect relies on maltose and baking soda, so be mindful of your intake.
