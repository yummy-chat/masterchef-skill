# Double Meat Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 250g pork belly (skin removed)  
🥩 250g beef tenderloin  
🥚 2 eggs  
🍚 appropriate amount of rice  
💧 appropriate amount of water (for cooking rice, about 1cm above the rice)  

🫚 a little ginger (minced)  
🍶 appropriate amount of cooking wine  
🍶 1-2 drops of vinegar  
🧊 2 small pieces of rock sugar  
🫒 appropriate amount of cooking oil  
🐷 a little lard (optional, for scrambling eggs)  
🫘 1 tablespoon of soybean paste  
🧂 1 circle of light soy sauce (about 15ml)  
🧂 half a circle of braising soy sauce or dark soy sauce (about 8ml)  
🧂 a little chicken bouillon  
🌾 appropriate amount of water starch  
🔥 1 tablespoon of hot oil (about 15ml, for final drizzling)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
♨️ rice cooker  
🍳 wok  
🌀 electric meat grinder  
🥄 spatula  
🔪 cutting board and chef's knife  
🥣 bowl or basin  
🔥 portable gas stove  
🫖 electric kettle  
🔌 power source (e.g., Bull track socket)  

## Procedure
1. Rinse the rice, drain, and place it in the rice cooker. Crack in 1 egg, mix thoroughly with your hands so each grain of rice is evenly coated in the egg mixture.
   **Note**: The egg mixture must be fully combined with the raw rice. This step is key to making the rice soft and fragrant.
2. Add water, start the cooking program, and steam the rice.
3. Cut the skinless pork belly and beef tenderloin into small pieces. Use an electric meat grinder to mince them separately.
   **Note**: If time permits, hand-chopped mince will yield a better texture.
4. Heat the wok over high heat, pour in a small amount of cold oil. Once the oil is hot, add the minced pork first and stir-fry quickly until it changes color completely.
5. Add the minced ginger and stir-fry to remove any gamey smell.
6. Add the minced beef and stir-fry together with the minced pork until the beef's moisture evaporates and the mince makes a sizzling sound.
7. Pour in the cooking wine and 1-2 drops of vinegar along the edge of the wok, stir-frying quickly to allow the alcohol and sourness to evaporate.
8. Reduce to medium-low heat. Add the rock sugar and continue stir-frying until the sugar has completely melted, the mince turns a dark red color, oil renders out, and the texture is dry and aromatic.
9. Push the mince aside in the wok to create a small space. Pour in the other beaten egg, stir-fry quickly until set, then mix it with the mince, creating an "egg flower wrapping the meat" effect. A little lard can be added for extra fragrance.
10. Add enough hot water to completely submerge the ingredients.
11. Add 1 tablespoon of soybean paste, 1 circle of light soy sauce, and half a circle of braising soy sauce. Stir to combine.
12. Cover the wok, reduce to low heat, and braise for about 15 minutes.
13. Increase to high heat to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, add a little chicken bouillon for seasoning.
14. Drizzle in the water starch while stirring continuously, until the sauce thickens and clings tightly to the minced meat.
15. Turn off the heat. Splash 1 tablespoon of hot oil over the meat sauce, stirring quickly to combine.
16. Serve the steamed rice on a plate, ladle the double meat sauce and its gravy over the top, and sprinkle with chopped green onions.

## Health Advice  
This double meat rice bowl is packed with protein, but the fat content of pork belly and beef tenderloin differs significantly. Pair it with a vegetable salad to balance the richness. Suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those with high physical exertion, but reduce the rice portion to control carbohydrates.
