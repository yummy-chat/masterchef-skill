# Thousand Chili Cold Shrimp 🦐

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All-year-round

## Instructions
1. Take fresh shrimp and peel them. Make a cut along the back to increase the surface area for better flavor absorption and a nicer presentation.
2. Place the deveined shrimp in a bowl. Add salt and cooking wine. Use your hands to mix and rub until the shrimp become sticky and develop a gel-like texture.
3. Prepare the vegetables and garlic water: Crush the cucumber and cut into segments. Shred the onion. Cut the lemon in half and set aside. In a separate bowl, soak the minced garlic in the mixed chicken bouillon water to create garlic water.
4. Heat a non-stick pan over high heat and add oil. Heat the cold oil until it is just about to smoke. Add the marinated shrimp and stir-fry over medium heat until they are fully changed in color and the edges are slightly browned. Splash in a little aromatic vinegar, then quickly remove the shrimp with a slotted spoon.
**Note**: Adding vinegar enhances aroma and removes any fishy smell.
5. Using the remaining oil in the pan, pour in the prepared garlic water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately turn off the heat. While the sauce is still warm, add white sugar, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, ground white pepper, dark soy sauce, and optional red oil or Lao Gan Ma. Stir continuously to combine.
**Key**: Always turn off the heat before adding the seasonings to prevent high heat from damaging the flavors.
6. Take a large bowl. First, lay down the cucumber segments and shredded onion. Then, top with the cooked shrimp. Pour the sauce over everything and mix well.
7. Place the mixed dish in the refrigerator to chill for about 30 minutes. This allows the sauce to adhere better and infuse deeper, while also making the shrimp and cucumber crunchier.
8. Just before serving, remove from the fridge. Squeeze the juice of half a lemon over the top and mix well. Add the lemon juice at the very end to keep its refreshing sourness.

## Health Tips
🦐 Shrimp are high in protein and low in fat. Paired with cucumber and onion, this dish is refreshing and helps cut through grease, making it suitable for weight loss or as an appetite stimulant in summer. When marinating the shrimp, you can slightly reduce the amount of salt to avoid being too salty. Adding the lemon juice at the end helps preserve its vitamin C content.
