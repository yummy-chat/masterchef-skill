# Steamed Pork with Rice Flour
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 500g Pork Belly (with alternating layers of fat and lean)
🍚 100g Steamed Pork Rice Flour (recommend Lee Kum Kee brand or homemade)
🥔 300g Potatoes (or 300g pumpkin, for the bottom layer)
🧅 10g Minced Ginger
🧄 10g Minced Garlic
🧂 15ml Light Soy Sauce
🧂 10ml Dark Soy Sauce
🍷 15ml Cooking Wine
🌶️ 10g Pixian Broad Bean Paste (optional)
🍬 5g Granulated Sugar

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Steamer, deep bowl, plastic wrap, chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the pork belly and cut into slices 5cm long, 3cm wide, and 0.5cm thick.
2. Finely mince the ginger and garlic, with particle diameter not exceeding 1mm.
3. In a large bowl, add the sliced pork belly, along with 15ml light soy sauce, 10ml dark soy sauce, 15ml cooking wine, 10g Pixian broad bean paste, 10g minced ginger, 10g minced garlic, and 5g granulated sugar.
4. Mix evenly with chopsticks, cover with plastic wrap, and marinate at room temperature (20-25°C) for 30 minutes.
5. After marinating, add 100g steamed pork rice flour and continue mixing for 2 minutes, ensuring each slice of meat is evenly coated.
6. Peel the potatoes and cut them into slices 0.8cm thick and 5cm x 5cm, about 300g.
7. Line the bottom of a deep bowl with the potato slices, avoiding overlapping as much as possible.
8. Evenly layer the coated pork belly slices on top of the potatoes, pressing them down firmly.
9. Add 2000ml of water to the steamer and bring to a boil over high heat.
10. Place the bowl with the ingredients into the steamer and cover with the lid.
11. Steam over medium heat for 60 minutes.
12. After the time is up, insert a chopstick into the center of a meat slice. If it penetrates easily with no significant resistance, the meat is fully cooked.
13. If not done, continue steaming for another 10-15 minutes until the meat is tender and the fat has rendered.
14. Remove and plate, ready to serve.

## Notes
- The marination time must not be less than 30 minutes to ensure thorough flavor absorption.
- During steaming, if the water level drops below 1cm from the bottom of the steamer, immediately add 500ml of hot water (above 90°C) to maintain sufficient steam.
- The chopstick penetration test is key to determining doneness; the meat should be tender before removing from the steamer.
- Do not cut the bottom vegetables too thin, as they may become overly soft and lose texture after steaming.

## Health Advice
Steamed pork with rice flour is rich in protein and fat, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing to gain weight. However, individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of Pixian broad bean paste. The potatoes or pumpkin used as the base provide dietary fiber and vitamins, helping to reduce the greasiness of the pork belly. It is recommended to control the serving size, not exceeding 150g per portion.
