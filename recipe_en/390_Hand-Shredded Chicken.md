# Hand-Shredded Chicken

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Year Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Whole chicken 1 (about 1.5kg, free-range Guangxi chicken)  
🥜 Peanuts appropriate amount (pre-fried, crushed and skin removed)  
🌿 Cilantro a little (cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallion whites appropriate amount (shredded, small scallions recommended)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers a little (optional, minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper a little (shredded, for color)  
🧄 Garlic a little (minced into paste)  
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds 2 tbsp  
🧂 Salt 30g + appropriate amount (for ice water)  
🧂 Salt-baked chicken powder half a packet + 1/3 packet  
🍶 Gardenia water appropriate amount  
🧂 Sand ginger powder 5g  
🧴 Soy sauce a little  
🫗 Chili oil a little  
🫒 Peanut oil appropriate amount  
🫒 Scallion oil appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🪓 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🍽️ Large round plate  
🥄 Small stainless steel bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix the salt with half a packet of salt-baked chicken powder evenly, and rub it all over the inside and outside of the whole chicken. Marinate for 20 to 30 minutes.  
**Key point**: Make sure to rub the mixture into the chicken cavity.  

2. Evenly drizzle the pre-soaked gardenia water over the chicken to color it.  
3. Once the steamer is steaming, place the whole chicken inside, pour in the remaining gardenia water, and cover.  
**Time and heat**: Total cooking time is about 25 minutes; the chicken needs to be turned halfway through.  

4. After steaming for 10 minutes, open the lid, turn the chicken over, and continue steaming covered for another 15 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked.  
5. Once steamed, immediately remove the whole chicken and immerse it in ice water with an appropriate amount of salt for about 20 minutes, until the chicken is completely cooled.  
**Key point**: Adding salt to the ice water locks in the base flavor; the ice bath makes the chicken skin springy and the meat firm.  

6. Wearing gloves, tear off the chicken head, wings, and drumsticks. Split the chicken from the back, remove the skin whole and set aside, then tear all the meat from the carcass into thin strips.  
**Key point**: The chicken must be completely cooled before tearing; the skin must be kept intact.  

7. Place the shredded chicken in a bowl. Add 1/3 packet of salt-baked chicken powder, 5g sand ginger powder, a little soy sauce, a little chili oil, minced garlic, crushed peanuts, sesame seeds, cilantro sections, shredded scallion whites, and shredded red bell pepper. Mix well by hand.  
8. Drizzle in an appropriate amount of peanut oil and scallion oil, and mix again until the chicken is glossy and fragrant.  
9. Pile the mixed chicken onto a plate. Arrange the head, wing tips, drumstick bones, and other carcass pieces on top for shaping. Then mince the chicken skin and cover it neatly over the chicken surface to restore the appearance of a whole chicken.  
**Key point**: The final presentation should look like a whole chicken.  

## Health Suggestions  
Hand-shredded chicken, after being ice-bathed, has springy skin and firm meat, making it suitable as a high-protein, low-fat dish for fitness or blood sugar-conscious individuals. However, the salt-baked powder and seasonings are high in sodium; it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance. The aroma of sand ginger and scallion oil can stimulate the appetite, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume the spiciness cautiously.
