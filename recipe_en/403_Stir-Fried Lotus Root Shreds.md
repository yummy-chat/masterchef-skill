# Stir-Fried Lotus Root Shreds
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🪷 Tender lotus root, appropriate amount (peeled, first sliced then cut into even shreds)  
🫑 Green bell pepper, appropriate amount (julienned)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (julienned)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (crushed and minced)  
🧅 Garlic sprouts (garlic stems), appropriate amount (cut into small rings to form garlic sprout flowers)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for blanching + seasoning)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (added during blanching)  
🥓 Lard, appropriate amount (added during stir-frying for aroma)  
🧂 MSG, a pinch (optional)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🍶 White vinegar, a small amount (drizzled along the edge of the pan just before finishing)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup ladle (wok spatula)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Strainer  
🥣 Basin (or large bowl, for washing and soaking the lotus root shreds)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Select tender lotus root, peel, then slice and cut into even shreds.  
2. Place the lotus root shreds in clean water and rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear, washing away excess starch.  
3. Julienne the green and red bell peppers, crush and mince the garlic, and cut the garlic sprouts into small rings.  
4. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a pinch of salt and a little cooking oil, then blanch the lotus root shreds for about 15 seconds. Remove immediately once they are just tender.  
   **Key Point**: Keep the blanching time short; adding salt and oil helps maintain the shreds' white color and crisp texture.  
5. Quickly remove the blanched lotus root shreds, rinse with cold water to cool, and drain well.  
   **Key Point**: Rapid cooling further enhances crispness.  
6. Heat another wok, add lard to melt, then stir-fry the minced garlic and julienned green and red bell peppers until fragrant. Add the prepared lotus root shreds and stir-fry over high heat throughout.  
7. After mixing well, add salt, light soy sauce, and MSG in order. Just before finishing, drizzle a small amount of white vinegar along the edge of the pan and quickly toss to combine.  
8. Add the garlic sprout rings, stir-fry quickly a few times, then serve and plate.  

## Health Suggestions  
Blanching the lotus root shreds followed by cooling in cold water helps maintain a crisp texture, making it suitable for appetite stimulation in summer or for controlling calorie intake. Washing away excess starch can reduce the glycemic load, but it is recommended that individuals with high blood lipids use lard sparingly.
