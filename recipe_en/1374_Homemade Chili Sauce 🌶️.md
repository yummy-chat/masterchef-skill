# Homemade Chili Sauce 🌶️
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ 200g bird's eye chili (minced)  
🌶️ 500g erjingtiao chili (stems removed, minced)  
🍄 350g king oyster mushroom (finely diced)  
🫚 100g ginger (minced)  
🧄 100g garlic (minced)  
🧅 A few scallions (tied into knots)  
🌿 A few stalks of cilantro (with roots)  
🧂 15g table salt  
🌾 20g toasted white sesame seeds  
🌶️ 30g coarsely ground chili flakes  
🫘 50g fermented soybean paste (yellow bean paste)  
🛢️ 700g rapeseed oil  
⭐ 2 star anise  
🌿 2 small pieces of cinnamon bark  
🍃 2 bay leaves  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Stainless steel basin/bowl  
🍳 Large iron wok  
🥄 Wok spatula/soup ladle  
🕳️ Strainer/slotted spoon  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Step-by-Step Instructions  
1. Mince the bird's eye chilis. Remove the stems from the erjingtiao chilis and mince them. Mix both types of chili together, add 15g of salt, stir well, and let sit to marinate.  
   *Key Point*: The finer the chilis are minced, the easier the spiciness and flavor will release. If you are sensitive to heat, substitute with large green or red bell peppers.  

2. Dice the king oyster mushrooms into small pieces. Mince the ginger and garlic separately. Set aside.  

3. In a bowl, combine the toasted white sesame seeds and coarsely ground chili flakes to make a dry seasoning mix. Set aside.  

4. Pour the rapeseed oil into the wok and heat to 60% (about 180°C / 350°F). Add the star anise, cinnamon bark, bay leaves, scallion knots, and cilantro with roots. Reduce to medium-low heat and fry slowly for 3–5 minutes until the aromatics darken and become dry. Remove and discard them.  

5. Increase the oil temperature to 70% (about 210°C / 410°F). Add the diced king oyster mushrooms and fry for about 3 minutes until golden brown. During this time, add the minced ginger and garlic and fry until fragrant.  

6. Add the marinated chili mixture. Bring to a boil over high heat, stir well, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for 4 minutes until the chilis are cooked through (no longer raw).  
   *Key Point*: The chilis must be cooked through to prevent the finished sauce from spoiling easily.  

7. Add the dry seasoning mix of white sesame seeds and coarsely ground chili flakes. Continue stirring and frying for 2 minutes to release color and aroma.  

8. Add the fermented soybean paste (yellow bean paste), stir-fry for 1 minute, then remove from heat.  
   *Key Point*: Add the soybean paste last to avoid bitterness from prolonged frying.  

9. Transfer to a container to cool completely. Seal in a glass jar for storage. Use as needed.  

## Health Tips  
This chili sauce is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber. The king oyster mushrooms provide plant-based protein. It is suitable for use as a condiment in small amounts. Note that it is high in oil and salt. People with high blood pressure, high blood lipids, high blood sugar, or sensitive stomachs should control their intake. For storage, ensure it is sealed and refrigerated. It is recommended to finish within two weeks for safety.
