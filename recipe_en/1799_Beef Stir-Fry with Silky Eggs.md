# Beef Stir-Fry with Silky Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐂 Beef Tenderloin, about 250g (sliced thinly against the grain)  
🥚 Eggs, 5 pieces  
🌽 Cornstarch, about 1 tablespoon (mixed into a thick cornstarch slurry)  
🧅 Scallions,适量 (finely chopped)  
🍯 Oyster Sauce,适量 (for marinating the beef)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for seasoning the egg mixture)  
🌶️ White Pepper Powder,适量 (for seasoning the egg mixture)  
🫒 Cooking Oil,适量 (used in multiple stages: to seal the beef, mix into the egg mixture, grease the pan, and during cooking)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Iron Wok  
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl (for mixing the egg mixture and marinating the beef)  
🪓 Wooden Cutting Board  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Slice the Beef**: Identify the direction of the beef's grain. Slice thinly against the grain to break up the fibers.  
   **Note**: Slicing against the grain is the key to ensuring tender beef.  

2. **Marinate the Beef**: Mix the cornstarch with a small amount of water to form a thick slurry. Add some of this cornstarch slurry and the oyster sauce to the beef slices. Use your hands to knead and mix until the beef becomes sticky. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix well to seal in the moisture.  
   **Note**: It is essential to knead until the beef releases a sticky texture (膠質), making the slices fully tacky.  

3. **Prepare the Egg Mixture**: Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add the remaining cornstarch slurry, a pinch of salt, white pepper powder, and an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture is smooth and uniform.  
   **Note**: The cornstarch slurry and cooking oil help make the eggs silkier and more tender.  

4. **Grease the Pan and Pan-Fry the Beef**: Heat the iron wok until it smokes. Pour in oil to fully grease the pan, then discard the oil. Add a fresh amount of cold oil to the wok. Lay the beef slices flat in the pan. Turn the heat to medium. Once one side changes color, flip the slices. Fry until both sides are lightly charred and no red is visible. Remove the beef from the wok and let it cool completely.  
   **Note**: The wok must be thoroughly heated for greasing. Do not stir the beef immediately after adding it to the pan; you can shake the wok to ensure even heating. The cooked beef must be completely cool before mixing with the egg mixture, otherwise it will cook the raw egg.  

5. **Combine the Ingredients**: Add the cooled beef to the egg mixture. Add the chopped scallions. Gently mix everything together.  

6. **Stir-Fry to Finish**: Re-grease the iron wok, then turn off the heat. Pour the combined beef and egg mixture into the wok. Turn the heat back to medium-low. Using a spatula, gently push the edges of the egg mixture that are setting toward the center, allowing the uncooked egg to flow to the bottom of the pan. Continue pushing until most of the egg is set, with the center still wet and slightly runny (半流心狀態). Remove from the wok immediately.  
   **Note**: The heat must be turned off before adding the egg mixture to ensure a low oil temperature. Turn the heat on again after the mixture is in the wok. The constant pushing ensures even cooking, keeping the texture silky. Do not cook the eggs until fully set.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for people aiming to build muscle or lose weight. However, since the oyster sauce used to marinate the beef already contains salt, reduce the amount of salt added to the egg mixture accordingly. The silkiest texture is achieved when the scrambled eggs are slightly undercooked. Be sure to use fresh eggs to minimize the risk of salmonella.
