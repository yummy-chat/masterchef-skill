# Sichuan Red Oil
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Erjingtiao dried chili peppers, appropriate amount (for making糊辣椒, to enhance aroma)  
🌶️ Medium-coarse chili powder, appropriate amount (to enhance aroma and spiciness)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder, appropriate amount (for coloring)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, appropriate amount (oil to chili total ratio approximately 4:1 to 5:1, pure rapeseed oil recommended)  
🤍 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (use sesame seeds with skin, a slightly larger amount)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (halved)  
🌱 Scallion, appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount  
🍂 Cao Guo (black cardamom), appropriate amount (cracked open, seeds removed, only the shell used)  
🍂 Star anise, appropriate amount  
🍂 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount  
🍂 Bay leaves, appropriate amount  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), a small amount (for soaking dry spices)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for final seasoning)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
♨️ Wok  
🥄 Iron ladle  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ White plate  

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. **Make糊辣椒**
   Place Erjingtiao dried chili peppers in a wok, stir-fry over low heat until the color darkens and the surface is slightly charred, easily crumbled by hand. Once done, pour out, remove charred black seeds, let cool, then crumble into coarse flakes by hand.
**Key point**: Use low heat throughout to avoid burning and bitterness.

2. **Prepare chili powders**
   Place medium-coarse chili powder and fine chili powder separately; do not mix in advance.

3. **Prepare aromatic vegetables**
   Slice ginger, halve onion, cut scallion into sections, and wash cilantro. Combine all in a bowl and set aside.

4. **Process dry spices**
   Crack open Cao Guo, remove seeds, and use only the shell. Place with star anise, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves in a bowl. Add a small amount of Baijiu to soak briefly to release aroma.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Refine the rapeseed oil**
   Pour rapeseed oil into the wok and heat until a large amount of green smoke appears. Scoop up a ladle of oil and observe: the color changes from dark yellow to clear and transparent; or drop a drop of oil onto a white plate; if it appears clear, the oil is refined. Once refined, quickly turn off the heat.
2. **Make sesame oil**
   While hot, scoop a ladle of hot oil and pour it into a bowl containing white sesame seeds. Stir with chopsticks to release the sesame aroma, then set aside to cool.
3. **Fry aromatic oil**
   After the oil temperature has dropped slightly but is still high, add the mixed aromatic vegetables and the Baijiu-soaked dry spices into the wok. Fry over medium-low heat until the vegetables are completely dehydrated, turning yellow and dry, and the aroma is fully infused into the oil. Then, use a slotted spoon to thoroughly remove all solid residues from the wok.
4. **Create red oil by adding oil in batches**
   **First addition**: When the oil temperature drops to about 50% hot, scoop out about one-third of the oil, and pour it ladle by ladle into a bowl containing the medium-coarse chili powder, stirring continuously. If the chili powder does not burn and produces many bubbles, the temperature is correct.
**Second addition**: After the oil temperature drops further, add the prepared糊辣椒 flakes into the bowl and stir evenly. Use this oil temperature to extract the smoky and burnt aroma without causing bitterness.
**Third addition**: When the temperature in the bowl drops again, add the fine chili powder and stir evenly. Use the residual heat to develop the color, avoiding high heat that would burn it.
**Fourth addition**: Pour all the remaining aromatic oil from the wok, which has now cooled to about 20% hot, into the bowl. This neutralizes the overall temperature and adjusts the consistency of the red oil.
5. **Rest for infusion**
   After all ingredients are evenly mixed, let rest to cool. Allowing it to sit for about two hours for color and aroma to fully infuse yields better results.

## Health Tips
This red oil uses a low-temperature, batch-pouring process, allowing the chili aroma to release in layers while avoiding the production of harmful substances from high heat. It is suitable for adding flavor to cold dishes or noodles, but those with high blood lipids should control their intake due to the high oil content.
