# Silvery Dragon Slices Soup
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Instructions
1.  Debone and fillet the seabass. Remove the belly and slice the fillets diagonally.
    **Key point**: Rinse the fish slices thoroughly with clean water and drain well to remove any fishy smell and ensure the coating adheres properly.
2.  Add a pinch of salt, white pepper, and 1 tbsp potato starch to the fish slices. Mix well to coat evenly and set aside to marinate.
3.  Heat the wok over high heat, add oil. When hot, add the fish bones and meaty trimmings. Fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown.
4.  Add scallions and ginger slices, stir-fry until fragrant to eliminate any fishy smell. Immediately pour in enough boiling water. Keep the heat on high and boil for about 3 minutes until the broth turns milky white.
    **Note**: You must add boiling water and maintain high heat — this is the key to getting a quickly whitened broth.
5.  Use a skimmer to remove the cooked fish bones and arrange them on a serving plate. Sprinkle with minced scallions and minced bell peppers. Drizzle with steamed fish soy sauce and black vinegar to make poached fish bones.
6.  While the fish broth in the wok is still boiling, carefully slide the marinated fish slices into the broth one by one.
    **Key point**: Add the slices one at a time to prevent sticking. Do not stir immediately after adding, or the coating may come off.
7.  Season the soup with salt and white pepper. As soon as the soup returns to a boil and the fish slices turn white and are cooked through, turn off the heat.
8.  Ladle the fish slice soup into a soup bowl. Garnish with a few chopped scallions or minced bell peppers if desired. Serve hot.

## Health Note
This osmanthus (seabass) fish slice soup is low in fat and high in protein. The fish is tender and easy to digest, making it suitable for moistening autumn dryness and for those needing high-quality protein. Do not overcook the fish slices in the soup; remove from heat as soon as they are just cooked through to keep them silky and tender.
