# Spicy Braised Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole chicken (about 1500g, separated into about 800g chicken pieces and about 700g chicken bones)  
🥔 2 potatoes (about 350g, peeled and cut into large chunks)  
🥕 1 carrot (about 110g, cut into rolling chunks)  
🧅 About 120g onion (cut into small pieces)  
🫑 3 thin-skinned green peppers (about 60g, cut diagonally into segments)  
🍄 About 100g enoki mushrooms (roots removed, torn into small bundles)  
🍄🟫 4 dried shiitake mushrooms (about 10g, rinse only)  
🫒 3 red dates (about 15g)  
🧄 8 cloves garlic (4 cloves about 20g for soup, 4 cloves about 20g minced for paste)  
🫚 2 small slices ginger (about 5g)  
🧅 1 scallion (about 100g, sliced diagonally)  
🌶️ About 30g coarse chili powder  
🌶️ About 30g fine chili powder  
🧂 About 15g beef bouillon powder (Daesang)  
🍬 About 30g sugar  
🧂 About 5g MSG  
🥫 About 40g light soy sauce  
🍶 About 20g cooking wine  
🦪 About 25g oyster sauce  

## Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Clay pot  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Vegetable peeler  
🍳 Wok or regular pot (for blanching)  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Small golden bowl, kitchen scale, spoon  

## Steps  
1. **Break down the chicken**: Using kitchen scissors, remove the head, wing tips, and tail. Tear off excess fat. Peel and cut off the neck, then remove the legs. Cut the chicken meat into pieces about the size of a quarter-palm. Cut the chicken carcass into pieces, and set aside with the neck meat for making broth. Remove all lymph nodes, innards, and excess fat.  
2. **Wash and blanch**: Wash the chicken bones and meat separately. Rinse the chicken pieces a few more times and drain well. Place the chicken bones in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, immediately turn off the heat, and remove them. Rinse with warm water. Bring another pot of water to a boil, quickly blanch the chicken pieces until the water boils again, immediately turn off the heat, and remove them. Rinse with warm water, remove any remaining fine feathers from the skin, and squeeze out excess water.  
3. **Prepare the vegetables**: Peel the potatoes and cut into large chunks. Cut the carrot into rolling chunks. Cut the onion into small pieces. Slice the scallion diagonally. Cut the thin-skinned green peppers diagonally into segments. Remove the roots of the enoki mushrooms and tear them into small bundles. Rinse the dried shiitake mushrooms without pre-soaking.  
4. **Make the sauce**: In a bowl, combine coarse chili powder, fine chili powder, beef bouillon powder, sugar, MSG, light soy sauce, cooking wine, oyster sauce, and minced garlic. Stir until smooth to form a thick paste.  
5. **Prepare the broth**: Add about 2000ml of water to the clay pot. Add the blanched chicken bones, scallion trimmings, red dates, dried shiitake mushrooms, ginger slices, and 4 peeled garlic cloves. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook at full heat for 18 minutes without covering. The broth should reduce to about 1400ml and be richly aromatic.  
6. **Strain the broth**: Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Use a slotted spoon to remove all solids, leaving only the clear broth.  
7. **Add the main ingredients**: To the clear broth, add the potato chunks, carrot chunks, onion pieces, and blanched chicken pieces. Pour in all the sauce and stir to combine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and braise for 10 minutes without covering.  
8. **Add quick-cooking vegetables**: Add the green pepper segments and scallion slices. Continue braising uncovered for 8 minutes.  
9. **Add enoki mushrooms**: Add the enoki mushrooms. Keep cooking over medium heat for another 5 minutes, until the potatoes are tender, the enoki mushrooms are cooked through, and the soup has thickened.  
10. **Serve**: Sprinkle with a few fresh scallion segments and red and green chili rings for garnish, and serve immediately.  

## Health Tips  
This spicy braised chicken soup is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for warming up in autumn and winter or recovering from fatigue. Be mindful to control the amounts of chili and sugar used. Those with sensitive stomachs should avoid drinking too much of the broth.
