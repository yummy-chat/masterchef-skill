# Stone Plate Eggs 🥚
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 6-8 (half can be substituted with silken tofu)
🧅 Onion 1/2 (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic 6-8 cloves (minced)
🌶️ Green chili peppers appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers appropriate amount (sliced into rings)
🍚 Rice 1 bowl
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (rice bran oil recommended)
🧉 Korean chili paste 2 tbsp
🥄 Soy sauce 2 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
🍬 Sugar 1 tbsp
🌶️ Chili flakes 1 tbsp (optional)
🧂 Beef bouillon powder 1/2 tbsp (can substitute with chicken bouillon or MSG, use half the amount)
💧 Water 6 tbsp (can substitute with Sprite)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥘 Frying pan
🥣 Bowl and spoon
🫖 Lid

## Steps
1. **Prep Ingredients**: Finely dice the onion, mince the garlic, and slice the green chili peppers and bird's eye chili peppers into rings.
   **Note**: When cutting the onion, hold a mouthful of water, or place the cut pieces in cold water, to reduce eye irritation.
2. **Make the Sauce**: In a bowl, combine Korean chili paste, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, chili flakes (if using), beef bouillon powder, and water. Stir until well blended.
   **Note**: This amount is for a serving of 6-8 eggs for two people. Adjust the quantities proportionally.
3. **Cook the Base**: Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until the onion softens.
   **Note**: Ensure the surface moisture of the ingredients is low enough to prevent oil splatter.
4. **Simmer the Sauce**: Pour the prepared sauce into the wok with the onion and garlic. Stir and cook over medium heat for about 30 seconds until the sauce thickens slightly. Add the green and red chili pepper rings, mix well, then turn off the heat.
5. **Fry the Eggs**: Heat a generous amount of oil in a frying pan, ensuring it's well coated. Turn the heat to medium and gently crack the eggs into the pan.
   **Note**: The pan must be thoroughly heated and oiled to prevent sticking. If using a pan prone to sticking, allow more time for heating.
6. **Combine and Simmer**: Once the egg whites are set, immediately reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Evenly spread the cooked sauce over the eggs. If using eggs that are not safe for raw consumption, cover the pan with a lid and simmer for 2-3 minutes until the egg whites are fully cooked and the yolks are runny or just set.
7. **Add Rice and Mix**: Remove the lid, place a bowl of rice on top of the eggs and sauce. Keep the heat on the lowest setting. Use a spoon to thoroughly mix the rice, eggs, and sauce until evenly combined.
   **Note**: Use residual heat or continue on low heat to fully cook the eggs. Mix until the rice reaches your desired consistency and serve.

## Health Advice
Eggs are a high-quality source of protein. Paired with onions, chili peppers, and Korean chili paste, this dish is nutritious, appetizing, and suitable for those needing an energy boost. Be mindful of the sodium content in the eggs and sauce. Individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of soy sauce and oyster sauce used.
