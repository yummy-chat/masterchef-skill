# Soul Barbecue Sauce  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥕 Carrot (moderate amount, sliced)  
🧅 Onion (moderate amount, shredded)  
🌿 Cilantro (moderate amount, cut into sections)  
🥬 Celery (moderate amount, cut into sections)  
🌿 Tsao-ko (1 piece, cracked and seeds removed)  
⭐ Star anise (2 pieces)  
🌿 Cassia bark (moderate amount)  
🍂 Bay leaves (moderate amount)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns (moderate amount)  
🌿 Clove (1 piece)  
🧄 Minced ginger (moderate amount)  
🧄 Minced garlic (moderate amount)  
🧅 Scallion (moderate amount)  
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes (moderate amount)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder (moderate amount)  
🌿 Cumin powder (moderate amount)  
🌸 Sichuan pepper powder (moderate amount)  
⬜ White sesame seeds (moderate amount)  
🦪 Oyster sauce (a little over half a spoon)  
🐔 Chicken essence (half a spoon)  
🐟 Steamed fish soy sauce (one spoon)  
🥫 Light soy sauce (one spoon)  
🍬 White sugar (one small spoon, adjustable)  
🧂 MSG (one spoon)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Strainer  
🥄 Spoon/soup spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Prepare vegetable broth: Fill the pot with water, add the sliced carrot, shredded onion, cilantro sections, and celery sections. Crack the tsao-ko and remove the seeds, soak briefly in warm water along with star anise, cassia bark, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, and clove.  
   **Note**: Tsao-ko must have seeds removed, otherwise it will taste bitter.  
2. Once the vegetable broth releases its aroma, strain out the solid ingredients with a strainer, and reserve the vegetable broth for later use.  
3. Prepare seasoned oil: Add base oil to the pot. While the oil is still cool, add the warm-water-soaked spices. Fry over low heat until the aroma is released, then add minced ginger, minced garlic, and scallion in sequence.  
4. Continue frying over low heat until the ginger, garlic, and scallion turn slightly golden and the aroma is fully released. Then, remove all solid ingredients with a strainer, reserving the seasoned oil.  
5. Make chili oil: Once the temperature of the seasoned oil has dropped slightly, add the coarse chili flakes and fine chili powder, stirring quickly to release the red oil using the residual heat.  
   **Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high, otherwise the chili will burn and become bitter.  
6. Add cumin powder and Sichuan pepper powder, stir quickly to release the fragrance.  
7. Pour in the strained vegetable broth, then add oyster sauce, chicken essence, steamed fish soy sauce, and light soy sauce in sequence. Stir well and bring to a boil.  
8. Add white sugar and MSG, sprinkle in white sesame seeds, reduce to medium-low heat and simmer, stirring constantly to prevent sticking to the bottom.  
9. Simmer until the sauce thickens, with uniformly sized fish-eye bubbles appearing on the surface, and it naturally shines and glistens, then turn off the heat.  

## Health Advice  
This barbecue sauce is simmered with vegetable broth and spices, rich in dietary fiber and natural umami flavor, suitable for those needing low-fat seasoning. However, the sugar and MSG content is relatively high, so it is recommended to control the dipping amount. Those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of light soy sauce and oyster sauce.
