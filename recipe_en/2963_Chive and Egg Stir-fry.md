# Chive and Egg Stir-fry
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Chives appropriate amount (white stems and green leaves cut into sections, placed separately)
🥚 Eggs 3
🌶️ Red bell pepper strips appropriate amount (for garnish and flavor)
🧂 Salt a pinch (for seasoning eggs) + appropriate amount (for stir-frying)
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowl, chopsticks
🍳 Wok, spatula
🍽️ Serving plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash and trim the chives, cut into sections, and place the chive stems and leaves separately; slice the red bell pepper into strips and set aside.
   **Note**: The stems and leaves of chives have different levels of doneness, so placing them separately ensures even cooking later.
2. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is uniform.
   **Key point**: Adding salt to the egg liquid in advance gives it a base flavor, making the scrambled eggs more aromatic.
3. Place the wok over heat, pour in cooking oil, and when it reaches about 40% heat, add the egg mixture. Once the bottom is set and the center is slightly thick, use a spatula to push the egg liquid towards the edge of the wok. Fry until both sides are golden and fluffy, with a slightly crispy surface. Cut into pieces with the spatula, then remove and set aside.
   **Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid instantly scorching the egg liquid; spreading the egg liquid ensures even heat and consistent texture.
4. Add oil to the wok again over high heat. First, stir-fry the chive stems until they are just cooked, then add the chive leaves and red bell pepper strips.
   **Key point**: Stir-fry the stems before adding the leaves to prevent the leaves from overcooking, maintaining an overall crisp and tender texture.
5. Add the appropriate amount of salt and chicken bouillon powder, then pour in the previously fried egg pieces. Keep the heat high and stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds to evenly distribute the seasoning and ensure the chives are just cooked. Remove from the wok.
   **Key point**: Use high heat and stir-fry quickly throughout; do not cook for too long to prevent the chives from releasing water and becoming tough, which affects their crisp texture.

## Health Tips
Chives are rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, and eggs provide high-quality protein, making this dish suitable for protecting the liver and boosting immunity in spring. Note that separating the chive stems and leaves during stir-frying helps maintain a crisp texture. Those with a cold stomach or prone to bloating should not consume too much.
