# Cumin Lamb
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lamb rolls 700g (choose pieces with alternating fat and lean, naturally irregular texture, avoid processed meat)
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (bullet-head chilies, cut into shreds)
🧅 Purple onion, half (cut into thick strips)
🌿 Cilantro, 1 bunch (cilantro stems cut into segments, stir-fry with onion; cilantro leaves can be used as a bed on the plate)
🧑‍🍳 Ginger, 1 small piece (cut into shreds)
🌿 Cumin seeds, to taste (use cumin seeds, not ground cumin, to prevent burning and enhance flavor)
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), a small amount (for blanching to remove gamey odor)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (slightly more to prevent sticking)
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste (generous amount)
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste
🧂 Salt, about 5g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok (cast iron wok recommended)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel basin/large bowl
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Small bowl
🥢 Wok spatula/spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Fill the wok with enough water. Add lamb rolls to cold water, add a small amount of baijiu, and heat over high heat until small bubbles form at the bottom. The water must not come to a boil. Stir with chopsticks to ensure even heating of the lamb. Once the lamb changes color, immediately turn off the heat.
**Key point**: The blanching temperature must be controlled just before boiling to avoid toughening the meat.

2. Remove the lamb with a slotted spoon, immediately rinse repeatedly with warm water to wash away blood foam and impurities. Do not use cold water, as the lamb fat will solidify and be difficult to rinse off.
**Key point**: Warm water rinsing maintains oil fluidity, ensuring thorough cleaning.

3. Firmly squeeze the rinsed lamb with your hands to remove all internal moisture. This must be completely dry—moisture is the root cause of water release and stewing during stir-frying.
**Key point**: Moisture must be completely squeezed out; otherwise, the lamb will release water during stir-frying, affecting the dry, aromatic texture.

4. Cut dried chilies into shreds, purple onion into thick strips, cilantro stems into segments and place together with the onion, set aside cilantro leaves for the plate base, and cut ginger into shreds. Place the onion strips, cilantro stems, and cumin seeds in the same bowl in advance for use.
**Key point**: Onion, cilantro stems, and cumin seeds in the same bowl facilitate quick addition later.

5. Heat the wok over high heat, pour in slightly more cooking oil to coat the wok surface. When the oil is hot, add the squeezed-dry lamb and ginger shreds. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the lamb releases oil, the edges become slightly curled and golden brown, and the moisture is cooked off. If the gamey smell is strong, discard the rendered oil; if mild, keep the oil as a base.
**Key point**: Cook over high heat throughout; quickly drying the moisture is key to the dry, aromatic texture.

6. Use a slotted spoon to remove the stir-fried lamb and drain the oil.
7. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add the dried chili shreds to warm oil, and fry over low heat until they turn brown and fragrant. Then immediately turn off the heat, and using the residual heat, add the cumin seeds and quickly stir-fry until fragrant to prevent burning.
**Key point**: Add cumin seeds after turning off the heat; residual heat is enough to release aroma without burning.

8. Add the prepared onion strips and cilantro stems, quickly stir-fry for about 5 seconds until just cooked.
9. Return the stir-fried lamb to the wok, stir-fry evenly, then turn off the heat. Add white pepper powder, light soy sauce, and salt.
**Key point**: Season after turning off the heat to prevent the soy sauce from burning at high temperatures.

10. After seasoning, turn the heat back to high and quickly stir-fry for about 30 seconds to 1 minute to release the soy sauce aroma and remove excess moisture. When the dish is dry and fragrant with no liquid, it is ready to plate. The plate can be lined with cilantro leaves.
**Key point**: High-heat stir-frying to reduce liquid ensures the dish is dry and aromatic, avoiding a wet, soft texture.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and iron, suitable for those with high physical exertion or who are sensitive to cold. However, lamb rolls have high fat content; it is recommended to discard the oil rendered during stir-frying to control calorie intake. Consume with vegetables to balance the diet.
