# Fried Pork Belt Noodles (Guo You Rou Ban Mian)  
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef Tenderloin 200g (no tendons or fat, slice thinly against the grain)  
🍅 Tomatoes 2 (diced)  
🌶️ Green Bell Peppers (Screw Peppers) 2 (about 100g, cut into rolling knife chunks; substitute with sweet bell peppers if you can’t handle spice)  
🧅 Onion 1/4 (about 50g, cut into chunks)  
🍄 Dried Wood Ear Mushrooms about 50g (pre-soaked)  
🧅 Scallion about 30g (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger about 10g (shredded)  
🧄 Garlic about 15g (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried Red Chili Peppers 4 (soaked in boiling water until soft, then chopped)  
🥚 Egg 1 (use only the egg white)  
🌾 All-Purpose High-Gluten Flour 400g  

🧂 Salt 4g for dough + 1/3 teaspoon for marinating beef + 1 teaspoon (about 5g) for stir-frying sauce  
🌿 Ground Sichuan Pepper 0.3g for marinating beef + 0.3g for stir-frying sauce (substitute with five-spice powder or Chinese thirteen-spice powder)  
🥢 Light Soy Sauce 5g for marinating beef + 30g (3 tablespoons) for stir-frying sauce  
🥢 Dark Soy Sauce 5g for marinating beef + 7g (about 2/3 tablespoon) for stir-frying sauce  
🫒 Cooking Oil 12g for dough + 12g for marinating beef + enough for resting dough pieces + about one pan base for stir-frying  
🌽 Corn Starch 12g (1.5 level tablespoons)  
🍬 White Sugar 7g (2 teaspoons)  
🌫️ White Pepper Powder 0.3g  
🧂 MSG 4g (1 teaspoon)  
🍶 Aged Vinegar 8g (half tablespoon)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing Bowl  
🪓 Rolling Pin  
🍽️ Plate  
🫙 Plastic Wrap  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥘 Cooking Pot  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫗 Bowl  

## Preparation Steps  

### Prep Stage  
1. Make handmade pulled noodle dough pieces.  
   - Mix 400g flour with 4g salt. Gradually add 215g cold water, stirring into floss. Add 12g cooking oil and knead into a dough. Rest for 15 minutes, kneading every 5 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. Rest for another 10 minutes.  
   - Divide the dough into two portions. Roll each into a thick sheet, cut into strips, then roll each strip into a round strand.  
   - Brush oil on a plate, arrange the rolled strands, brushing oil between each layer and on top. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for at least 1 hour. Can be prepared the night before and refrigerated.  
2. Marinate the beef.  
   - Slice beef tenderloin thinly against the grain. Add salt, ground Sichuan pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, a little water, and shredded ginger. Mix until the liquid is fully absorbed and the meat feels sticky.  
   - Add corn starch and egg white. Stir vigorously in one direction for about 2 minutes until sticky and resistant. Finally, drizzle in two tablespoons of cooking oil and mix well to seal in moisture.  
3. Prepare side ingredients and seasonings.  
   - Soak dried red chili peppers in boiling water for 5 minutes, rinse with cold water, squeeze dry, and chop finely.  
   - Core and dice tomatoes; cut green bell peppers into rolling knife chunks; cut onion into chunks; soak wood ear mushrooms; chop scallion, shred ginger, slice garlic. Place green bell peppers, onion, and wood ear mushrooms on one plate. Place chopped chili peppers, scallion, ginger, and garlic in a bowl.  

### Cooking Stage  
1. Stir-fry beef and vegetables quickly.  
   - Heat cooking oil in a wok to 60% hot. Add marinated beef and quickly separate with a spatula. After about 20 seconds, immediately add green bell peppers, onion, and wood ear mushrooms. Stir briefly, then remove all with a slotted spoon and drain oil. Do not overcook the beef.  
**Key Point**: Keep the beef cooking time short to maintain tenderness.  

2. Cook the stir-fried meat sauce (Guo You Rou topping).  
   - Leave a little oil in the wok. Over high heat, stir-fry scallion, ginger, garlic, and chopped dried chili peppers until fragrant.  
   - Add diced tomatoes. Stir-fry over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook until tomatoes release juice. Add white sugar, white pepper powder, and ground Sichuan pepper. Stir well.  
   - Turn heat to maximum. Add the pre-fried beef and vegetables. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat. Drizzle light soy sauce and dark soy sauce along the side of the wok, stir-frying until fragrant.  
   - Add half a bowl of water and bring to a boil. Add MSG and salt to taste. Once boiling again, drizzle aged vinegar along the side of the wok. Stir and remove from heat.  
3. Cook the noodles.  
   - Stretch each rested dough piece into a long, thin noodle. Drop into boiling water and separate with chopsticks. After the water boils, add a splash of cold water. Bring to a boil again, add another splash. After the third boil, noodles are fully cooked.  
4. Assemble the dish.  
   - Remove noodles with a slotted spoon and place in a bowl. Ladle the stir-fried meat sauce over the noodles. Mix well and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish provides high-quality protein and dietary fiber from beef and wood ear mushrooms. The handmade pulled noodles are filling and suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure. Pair with a cold cucumber salad or lightly stir-fried greens to balance the oil and acidity of the tomatoes.
