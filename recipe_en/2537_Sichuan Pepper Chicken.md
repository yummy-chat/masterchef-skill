# Sichuan Pepper Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All year round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Whole chicken 1 (chopped into pieces)
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper a large amount (used twice, part for oil, part for final sprinkle)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Green and red small chili peppers appropriate amount (halved)
🧂 Salt a pinch
🧂 Chicken bouillon a pinch
🌫️ White pepper powder a pinch
🥢 Light soy sauce a pinch
💧 Ginger and scallion water appropriate amount (for removing odor)
🥡 Dry starch appropriate amount (added last and mixed well)
🫒 Cooking oil a large amount (for oil-bathing, double frying, and stir-frying aromatics)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🥄 Large slotted spoon
🥣 Large bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Rinse the whole chicken, chop off the feet and wing tips, then cut into evenly sized pieces.
2. Place the chicken pieces in a basin. Add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, and ginger and scallion water. Gently mix evenly. Finally, add dry starch and mix again. Marinate for 10 minutes.
**Note**: Mix gently to avoid breaking the chicken.

3. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to 70% hot. Add the chicken pieces. Use a slotted spoon to gently separate them. Fry over medium heat until the surface is set and lightly golden.
**Note**: If the chicken pieces are large, extend the frying time to ensure they are fully cooked.

4. Remove the chicken pieces. After the oil temperature rises, fry them again until the surface is golden and a crispy crust forms. Remove and drain oil.
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add a large amount of green Sichuan pepper. Stir-fry over low heat until the numbing aroma is released. Remove most of the Sichuan pepper and set aside.
**Key**: Maintain low heat to avoid burning.

6. Using the remaining oil in the wok, stir-fry minced ginger, garlic, and scallions over high heat until fragrant. Add the halved green and red small chili peppers and stir-fry until the spiciness releases.
7. Add the fried chicken pieces back in. Return the previously stir-fried green Sichuan pepper. Quickly stir-fry everything together so the flavors fully combine.
8. Turn off the heat. Transfer to a bowl and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Sichuan Pepper Chicken is rich in protein, making it suitable for those building muscle or with high physical energy expenditure. The frying process and high oil content make it relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the diet. Green Sichuan pepper contains volatile oils that can stimulate appetite, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation to avoid gastric irritation.
