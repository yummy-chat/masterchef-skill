# Turtle Jelly
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August), All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Turtle jelly powder 25 g
💧 Cold water 120 ml
💧 Boiling water 500 ml
🍬 White sugar 100 g
🍯 Honey optional
🥛 Condensed milk optional

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Small pot
🥄 Stirring tool
🍚 Mold
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 25 g of turtle jelly powder and 120 ml of cold water into a pot. Stir until fully dissolved with no lumps.
**Key point**: Ensure no lumps remain, as this affects texture.

2. In a separate container, add 100 g of white sugar and pour in 500 ml of boiling water. Stir until the sugar is completely dissolved.
3. Slowly pour the sugar water into the turtle jelly powder mixture while stirring quickly to prevent clumping.
**Key point**: The sugar water must be poured slowly and stirred rapidly to avoid clumps.

4. Place the mixed liquid on the stove. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking to the pot.
**Key point**: Maintain medium-low heat and stir continuously throughout to avoid burning the bottom.

5. Heat until the liquid becomes thick and small bubbles begin to form on the surface. Immediately turn off the heat.
6. Quickly pour the liquid into molds and allow it to cool and set naturally. For best texture, refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours before serving.
**Key point**: Do not move the container during cooling and setting to prevent failure in setting.

## Health Advice
Turtle jelly is low in calories and fat-free, making it suitable for cooling off in summer or as a dessert during weight management. However, commercially available turtle jelly may have a high sugar content, so diabetics should consume with caution.
