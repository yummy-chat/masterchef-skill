# Sweet and Sour Shrimp
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp,适量 (peeled and deveined by hand)  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🌽 Cornstarch,适量  
🧂 Salt, about 5g (2g for marinating shrimp, 3g for sweet and sour sauce)  
🫒 Salad oil, a small amount (added to egg batter)  
🧄 Minced ginger,适量  
🧄 Minced garlic,适量  
🍶 Rice vinegar, equal amount to sugar (1:1)  
🍬 White sugar, equal amount to rice vinegar (1:1)  
💧 Water, equal amount to sugar and vinegar (1 part)  
🥣 Water starch,适量 (for thickening)  
✨ Bright oil, a little (added before removing from heat)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Strainer  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Stove  

## Steps  
1. Wrap the shrimp in cheesecloth and thoroughly squeeze out surface moisture. Add about 2g of salt, mix well, and marinate to initial flavor.
2. Prepare the egg batter: Beat 1 whole egg. Add cornstarch in 2–3 batches, stirring each time until no lumps remain, until the batter is thick and flows slowly in a ribbon. Then add a small amount of salad oil and mix well.
3. Coat the shrimp: Lightly coat the marinated shrimp with a thin layer of dry cornstarch, then place them into the egg batter and mix well, ensuring each shrimp is evenly coated.
4. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 140°C (fourth heat). Add the shrimp one by one. Once set, gently break them apart with a strainer. Raise the oil temperature to about 180°C or higher, then fry again until the shrimp are golden and crispy. Remove and drain oil.
5. Prepare the sweet and sour sauce: Leave a little base oil in the wok. Sauté minced ginger and garlic until fragrant. Add rice vinegar, white sugar, and an equal amount of water in order. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add about 3g of salt.
6. When the sauce bubbles evenly in dense "fish-eye bubbles," add an appropriate amount of water starch to thicken. Stir until the sauce becomes thick and can coat the back of a spoon.
7. Pour the fried shrimp into the sweet and sour sauce. Drizzle in a little bright oil and stir-fry quickly so the shrimp are evenly coated with the sauce. Immediately remove from heat and plate.
   **Key**: The stir-frying must be done quickly to maintain the shrimp’s crispy texture.

## Health Advice  
Shrimp is high in protein and low in fat. Paired with a sweet and sour sauce, it is appetizing and complements rice. It is suitable for those who need high-quality protein and wish to avoid greasy foods. Note that the sweetness of the sauce should be controlled; those with diabetes should consume in moderation.
