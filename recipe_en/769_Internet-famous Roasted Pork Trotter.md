# Internet-famous Roasted Pork Trotter
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Year-round Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork trotters 1000g (choose front trotters, remove hair, trim the "round part", split in half from the middle)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (whole or tied in knots)
⭐ Star anise appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon stick appropriate amount
🍃 Bay leaves appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount
🔴 Red yeast rice appropriate amount (for coloring)
🥄 Light soy sauce a little
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount
🥄 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount (to balance the color from red yeast rice)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🤍 Sesame seeds 1 portion
💚 Cumin powder 1 portion
❤️ Chili powder 1 portion (mix sesame seeds, cumin powder, and chili powder in a 1:1:1 ratio)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
♨️ Pressure cooker
🔥 Oven
🥘 Baking tray, aluminum foil
🥣 Bowl, spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the pork trotters in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and blanch for 5 to 10 minutes. Skim off any foam, remove, and rinse clean under cold water.
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps remove the gamey smell and blood foam more thoroughly.
2. In a clean wok, add oil and sauté sliced ginger and scallion knots until fragrant. Add star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry over low heat until aromatic.
3. Add the pork trotters and stir-fry briefly. Pour cooking wine along the edge of the wok to remove any unpleasant smell. After the alcohol evaporates, add enough water to cover.
4. Add red yeast rice, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat.
5. Transfer the pork trotters and broth into a pressure cooker. Close the lid, heat over high heat until steam releases, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 15 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce the skin.
   **Key point**: Cook until tender to ensure the best roasting results.
6. Remove the trotters and drain the liquid. Place them on a baking tray lined with aluminum foil. Evenly sprinkle the mixed sesame seeds, cumin powder, and chili powder over them.
7. Preheat the oven to 220°C. Place the baking tray in the middle rack and roast for about 20 minutes. Once the edges of the skin start sizzling and bubbling, continue roasting for an additional 5 minutes until the skin becomes crispy.
   **Doneness check**: Bubbling skin with sizzling sounds indicates the ideal crispy texture.

## Health Advice
Pork trotters are rich in collagen but high in calories, so they are suitable for occasional enjoyment rather than weight-loss meals. Pair with light vegetables to balance the richness, and control the heat during roasting to avoid charring.
