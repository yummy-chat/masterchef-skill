# Cookie Biscuit
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Butter 120g (needs to be softened, recommended brands: President, Anchor)
🥛 Whipping Cream 100g (room temperature)
🍚 Icing Sugar 70g (can be reduced to 55g, can be substituted with granulated sugar but the pattern will slightly collapse)
🧂 Salt 1.5g
🌾 Cake Flour 220g
🍵 Matcha Powder 6g (optional, replace an equal amount of flour)
🍫 Cocoa Powder 10g (optional, replace an equal amount of flour)

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Small Knife
🥣 Mixing Bowl
🔌 Electric Hand Mixer
🍴 Silicone Spatula
🧁 Sifter
👝 Piping Bag + Medium 8-tooth Piping Tip (sn7092)
📜 Baking Sheet and Parchment Paper
♨️ Oven or Air Fryer

## Instructions
1. Cut the butter into small pieces and place it together with the whipping cream until fully softened, ensuring both reach room temperature.
   **Note**: Soften at room temperature in summer; in winter, soften over warm water or with a slight microwave heat, but do not let the butter melt into liquid.
2. Place the softened butter in a mixing bowl, add the icing sugar and salt.
3. Beat with an electric hand mixer on high speed for about 1 minute 30 seconds, until the butter increases in volume, lightens in color, and becomes fluffy and feathery.
   **Key**: Avoid over-beating, otherwise the pattern will easily disappear after baking; icing sugar should ideally be without lumps.
4. Add the room temperature whipping cream in 3 additions, each time beating on low speed (speed 1) until fully emulsified, then add the next.
   **Key**: Scrape down the butter that is not mixed on the sides of the bowl with a spatula; only mix until combined, do not over-beat.
5. Sift in the cake flour.
   **Note**: Sifting prevents lumps and makes the batter smoother.
6. Use the spatula to cut and fold in a "one" shape, then press and mix until no dry flour remains and the batter is smooth.
   **Key**: Do not stir in circles to avoid developing gluten in the flour, which affects the crispy and tender texture.
7. Fill the piping bag fitted with the piping tip with the batter, and pipe shapes onto the baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
   **Note**: If the room temperature is high, the piped cookies can be refrigerated for 10 minutes to set, making the pattern more defined.
8. Bake:
   - **Oven**: Preheat in advance, bake at 180°C (356°F) middle rack for about 20 minutes, until the edges and surface are evenly golden brown.
   - **Air Fryer**: Bake at 170°C (338°F) for about 10 minutes, then flip and bake for another 5–10 minutes, adjusting based on the browning level.
   **Key**: The cookies will be soft when first out of the oven; they must cool completely on the baking sheet to become crispy. Do not move them while hot.
9. After completely cooling, seal and store to keep them crispy and prevent crumbling.

## Health Suggestions
Although butter is flavorful, it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair these cookies with black coffee or plain tea to both cut the richness and reduce intake. The icing sugar can be reduced to 55 grams for a moderate sweetness, suitable for those controlling sugar intake. These cookies are crispy and delicious, but moderation is advised.
