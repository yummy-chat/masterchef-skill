# Seaweed and Egg Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍘 Dried seaweed, whole sheet (use a whole sheet of dried seaweed)
🥚 Eggs, as needed (beaten and set aside)
🦐 Dried shrimp, as needed (tiny shrimp or small dried shrimp)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)
🧂 Salt, 2g
🌶️ White pepper, as needed (adjust to taste)
🍎 Vinegar, 10ml
🫒 Sesame oil, 10ml

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large soup bowl (for holding the soup base, seasonings, and finished soup)
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for dry-roasting the seaweed and dried shrimp)
🔥 Gas stove (provides heat source)
🫗 Measuring cup with spout (convenient for pouring the egg liquid in a circular motion)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the chopped scallions, salt, white pepper, vinegar, and sesame oil in a large soup bowl as the soup base seasonings.
    **Key point**: All seasonings are placed directly into the final soup-serving bowl; this is a characteristic of this method.
2.  Heat the flat-bottomed pan over low heat. Place the whole sheet of dried seaweed in the pan, gently pressing it with your hand to ensure even heating. Roast each side for 1-2 minutes until the color changes from black to dark green and the texture becomes crisp. Remove from pan, tear into small pieces, and place them into the seasoning bowl.
    **Note**: Do not use high heat, as this may burn the seaweed; dry-roasting fully releases the seaweed's umami flavor.
3.  In the same pan, keep the heat on low. Add the dried shrimp and dry-roast, stirring until they turn slightly yellow, which removes the seafood smell and enhances their savoriness. Once done, pour them directly over the seaweed in the bowl.
4.  Bring water to a boil in the pot. As soon as the water is fully boiling, immediately turn off the heat.
5.  With the heat off, pour the beaten egg liquid into the pot in a circular motion, allowing it to naturally form thin, billowy, cloud-like egg blossoms.
    **Mnemonic**: "First boil, then off, then circle pour" - ensures the egg blossoms are light and tender, not overcooked.
6.  Pour the hot soup containing the egg blossoms directly into the bowl holding all the ingredients and seasonings. The high heat will instantly release the aromas. Serve immediately.
    **Key point**: No need to re-boil; pouring the hot soup instantly completes the dish.

## Health Advice
Seaweed and egg drop soup is low in fat and high in protein, rich in iodine and calcium, making it suitable for those needing to supplement these trace elements. Dried shrimp are high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount. Pouring the egg liquid after turning off the heat is the key to achieving tender and silky egg blossoms.
