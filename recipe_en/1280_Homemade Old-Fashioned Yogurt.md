# Homemade Old-Fashioned Yogurt
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥛 Whole milk – one pot (amount depends on jar size)
🍶 Store-bought plain old-fashioned yogurt – about 100-150g (whisk until smooth)
🐉 Dragon fruit cubes – to taste (for decoration, add before serving)
🍊 Orange cubes – to taste (for decoration, add before serving)
*No additional seasonings; sweetness comes from milk flavor and fruit*

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Glass cooking pot
🥄 Stainless steel soup spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🫖 Small lidded porcelain jars – several
♨️ Stainless steel steamer (with steaming rack, for water bath insulation)
🔘 Glass steamer lid
🥣 Bowl (for heating jars with hot water)

## Steps
1. Pour the whole milk into the pot, heat until boiling, then remove from heat and let it cool.
   **Key point**: Cool until it reaches a warm, not hot, temperature.
2. Use chopsticks to thoroughly whisk the store-bought yogurt until smooth and free of lumps.
3. Rinse each small porcelain jar inside and out with hot water to warm it up.
4. Pour the whisked smooth yogurt into the milk that has cooled to a warm temperature, and stir well to combine.
5. Divide the mixed milk liquid into the pre-warmed porcelain jars.
6. Add an appropriate amount of water to the steamer, heat until the water feels noticeably hot to the touch when inserting a hand but tolerable for a brief moment, then immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key point**: Stop heating after turning off the heat; fermentation relies solely on residual warmth.
7. Place the filled jars onto the steamer rack, cover the steamer with its lid, and let them ferment with residual heat.
   **Note**: Rely entirely on the residual heat of the hot water for about 90 minutes, until the milk surface solidifies and looks like "tofu pudding."
8. Remove the jars, let them cool completely, then cover with lids and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
9. Before serving, optionally decorate with fruit such as dragon fruit cubes and orange cubes to enhance flavor.

## Health Tips
Homemade old-fashioned yogurt is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, suitable for growing children or the elderly who need calcium supplementation. Note that the fermentation temperature should not be too high to avoid damaging the activity of probiotics.
