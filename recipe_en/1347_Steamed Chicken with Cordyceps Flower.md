# Steamed Chicken with Cordyceps Flower
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 1 small hen (remove the tail, chop into small pieces)  
🧅 Some onion (sliced)  
🍄 Some cordyceps flowers (washed in advance)  
🧅 Some scallion segments  
🧂 Some soy sauce  
🧂 Some salt  
🌾 A little cornstarch  
🫒 A little peanut oil  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🪵 Round cutting board (one side for chopping bones, one side for slicing meat)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍽️ Iron plate or copper plate  
♨️ Steamer  

## Steps
1. Remove the tail from the small hen and chop into uniform small pieces.  
**Note**: Chicken is sensitive to alcohol and sugar. Cooking wine will ruin the steamed chicken's flavor, and sugar will conflict with the chicken's natural umami. Therefore, do not add cooking wine or sugar during marination.
2. Slice the onion, wash the cordyceps flowers in advance, and prepare the scallion segments.
3. Place the chicken pieces, onion slices, cordyceps flowers, and scallion segments in a bowl. Add an appropriate amount of soy sauce, salt, and a little cornstarch. Mix thoroughly by hand.  
**Key point**: Seasoning does not need to be complex; soy sauce and salt are sufficient. The cornstarch helps coat the seasonings, making the finished dish more tender and smooth.
4. After mixing well, drizzle in a little peanut oil and mix again to lock in the chicken's moisture.
5. Evenly spread the mixed ingredients in a single layer on an iron plate or copper plate.
6. Bring water in the steamer to a boil over high heat. Once steam rises, place the plate inside, cover with the lid, and steam.  
**Timing**: On a professional high-power stove, steam for about 4 and a half minutes. On a home stove with lower heat, steam for about 7 minutes. During steaming, open the lid to turn the ingredients and check for doneness.
7. When the time is up, open the lid and use chopsticks to turn the chicken pieces to check. If no blood seeps out, it is ready to serve.  
**Doneness indicator**: When the chicken shows no blood, it is fully cooked, with a tender and smooth texture and clear broth.  
   **Flavor tip**: The broth is very flavorful and pairs well with rice.

## Health Tips
This steamed chicken with cordyceps flower is low in fat and high in protein. Cordyceps flowers are rich in polysaccharides, making it suitable for fitness and health-conscious individuals. The steaming time should not be too long; 7 minutes can keep the chicken tender, juicy, and the broth can also be used to mix with rice.
