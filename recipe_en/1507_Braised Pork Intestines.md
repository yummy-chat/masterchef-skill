# Braised Pork Intestines
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork intestines (fresh) to taste
🦴 Skeleton (pork bones or chicken frame) to taste
🫚 Ginger about 30g
🧅 Scallion about 30g
🌿 Fennel seeds 8g
⭐ Star anise 5g
🌿 Cardamom 1
🍂 Cinnamon stick 3g
🌿 White cardamom 2g
🍃 Bay leaf 1g
🌼 Gardenia fruit 2
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 3g
🌸 Cloves 2
🫙 Sichuan peppercorns 20g
🧂 Salt 100g
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) to taste
🧊 Rock sugar to taste
🫒 Vegetable oil (soybean oil) 500g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🍲 Soup pot
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧤 Spice bag
🍽️ Stainless steel strainer
🖌️ Brush
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Prepare the pork intestines**
    - Turn the intestines inside out, remove most of the fat from the inner side, leaving a small amount.
    - First wash: Add baking soda and flour, rub vigorously, then rinse clean with water.
    - Second wash: Add white vinegar and flour, rub again, then rinse clean.
    - Third wash: Rub with salt, then rinse thoroughly with water.
    - Fill the intestines with water, turn them back to the right side using gravity, drain, drizzle with baijiu, rub in, and marinate for 15 minutes.
    - Place the intestines in cold water, bring to a boil, skim off the foam, boil until impurities are released, then remove and rinse clean.
    **Key point**: Repeated rubbing and washing is the core step for removing the gamey smell and cannot be omitted.

2.  **Prepare the spice mixture**
    - Place all dried spices into a spice bag, soak in water briefly to remove dust and excess medicinal taste.
    - Prepare seasoned oil: Add 500g soybean oil to a wok, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and Sichuan peppercorns in cold oil. Simmer over low heat until the ginger and scallion turn dry and yellow. Remove from heat, let cool, strain out the solids, and reserve the oil.
    - Prepare caramelized sugar: In another pot, add a little oil and rock sugar, stir constantly until melted, forms dense bubbles, and turns date-red. Quickly pour in an appropriate amount of boiling water, stir to combine, creating a sugar-color liquid.

3.  **Make the basic braising liquid**
    - Place the skeleton (bones) in a soup pot, add enough water to make about six jin of broth. Remove the bones.
    - Place a stainless steel strainer at the bottom of the pot, then add the soaked spice bag and the prepared sugar-color liquid.
    - Keep on a low heat and simmer for 20 minutes to fully release the spice flavors, until the liquid turns amber.

4.  **Braise the pork intestines**
    - Place the blanched pork intestines into the braising liquid, then pour in the pre-made seasoned oil.
    - Add 100g salt. The liquid should be slightly saltier than normal for the intestines to absorb the flavor well.
    - Cover with a stainless steel strainer, place a bowl filled with water on top as a weight to ensure the intestines remain submerged in the liquid.
    - Braise over medium-low heat for 1 hour, then turn off the heat and let the intestines soak in the liquid for another 1 hour.
    **Note**: This braising liquid is only to be used for braising offal-type ingredients; do not mix it with other uses to avoid flavor crossover.

5.  **Finishing and serving**
    - Remove the weight bowl and strainer, use a slotted spoon to take out the pork intestines.
    - While hot, brush the surface of the intestines with the layer of fat oil from the top of the braising liquid to give them a glossy look and prevent them from drying out.
    - Chop into pieces or slice and arrange on a plate for serving.

## Health Advice
This braised pork intestine dish is rich in protein and contains a moderate amount of fat, but is also high in cholesterol and sodium. It is suitable for occasional indulgence. It is recommended to pair with high-fiber vegetables and avoid excessive consumption; people with high blood lipids or high blood pressure should eat with caution.
