# Simple Artisan Bread  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 Bread flour 250g (**must use bread flour**)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 10-20g (divided into two portions, most mixed with flour, a small amount used to activate yeast)  
🧂 Salt 1g  
💧 Water 160g (reserve about 15g to activate yeast)  
🍄 Yeast 3g  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (optional, adds smoothness to dough; softened butter can be substituted)  
🍑 Dried apricots as needed  
🥜 Pecans as needed  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Wooden spoon or rolling pin  
🍴 Scraper  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🖊️ Marker  
📦 Square fermentation box (with lid, for cold fermentation)  
🔥 Oven  
🍳 Baking tray  
📄 Parchment paper  
🔪 Sharp knife or blade  
🧂 Sifter  
🔪 Bread knife and cutting board  

## Instructions  
1. In a large bowl, mix the bread flour, most of the granulated sugar, and salt together, stirring with a wooden spoon until combined.  
2. Measure 160g of water in a measuring cup. Pour about 145g of water into the flour bowl, reserving about 15g in the cup.  
3. Add the yeast and a small spoonful of granulated sugar to the reserved 15g of water. Let it sit for 1-2 minutes until a thick foam appears on the surface, indicating the yeast is activated.  
4. Pour all of the activated yeast water into the flour bowl. Stir with a wooden spoon until roughly no dry flour remains, then knead by hand into a rough dough.  
5. If using oil, add the desired amount at this stage and continue kneading until the oil is fully absorbed into the dough.  
6. Add the dried apricots and chopped pecans, kneading them into the dough until roughly evenly distributed.  
7. Shape the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.  
8. After resting, take out the dough, stretch and fold it. You can fold it once in each direction to strengthen the gluten.  
9. Shape the dough again, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for another 30 minutes.  
10. Repeat step 8 for a second fold.  
11. Return the folded dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and proof at a warm place for about 1 hour, until the dough has roughly doubled in size.  
12. Remove the proofed dough, gently press to release gas, and shape as desired. The shaping method should follow the rolling technique used during folding.  
13. Place the shaped dough into a container lined with parchment paper, cover with plastic wrap, and perform the final proof. You can choose:  
   - **Cold proof overnight**: Refrigerate overnight, then bake the next day.  
   - **Same-day proof at room temperature**: Proof at room temperature for 40 minutes to 1 hour, until the dough has visibly expanded.  
14. Before baking, transfer the dough to a baking tray. Dust the surface with flour using a sifter, then make a long slit lengthwise on top using a sharp knife or blade.  
15. Preheat the oven to 200°C (392°F). Place the bread in the middle rack and bake for about 25 minutes, until the surface is deep golden brown.  
16. After baking, let the bread cool on a wire rack, then slice with a bread knife to serve.  
   **Note**: There is no need to develop a windowpane membrane; sufficient gluten forms through multiple rests and folds. When scoring the bread, make a decisive cut to help it expand evenly during baking.  

## Health Suggestions  
This rustic bread is enriched with dried apricots and pecans, offering dietary fiber and healthy fats, making it suitable as a breakfast or light staple. Pair it with unsweetened yogurt to boost protein and increase satiety. Be mindful that dried fruit contains sugar, so those with diabetes should control portion sizes.
