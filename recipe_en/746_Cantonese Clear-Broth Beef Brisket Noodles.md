# Cantonese Clear-Broth Beef Brisket Noodles
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket (tender brisket/fatty brisket) 3 jin  
🦴 Beef ribs 5 jin  
🥕 White radish 1 piece (choose firm, heavy ones)  
🌰 Soybeans 100g  
🫚 Old ginger (with skin) 10g  
🌿 Astragalus 8g  
🌿 Solomonseal 15g  
🌿 Dried Chinese yam 30g  
🌿 Codonopsis 3 stalks  
🌿 Adenophora 5 stalks  
🌿 Licorice 3 slices  
🍊 Tangerine peel (dried) 5g  
🌿 Angelica 2g  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍬 Rock sugar about 20g  
🍶 Rice wine (or cooking wine) about 15g  
🍜 Alkaline noodles to taste  
🧅 Chopped scallions a sprinkle (for finishing)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🪣 Large soup pot (deep enough for making broth)  
🥘 Small soup pot or wok (for cooking noodles)  
🥄 Large soup ladle  
🥢 Strainer/sieve  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Glass bowl (for washing and soaking herbs, and cooling noodles)

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash all herbs clean with water to remove dust and impurities; rinse beef brisket and beef ribs; peel the white radish and set aside.
2. Add about 30 jin of clean water to the large soup pot. Place the beef ribs and whole beef brisket into cold water, then add all herbs, old ginger with skin, whole white radish, and soybeans. Pour in the rice wine.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once the water is boiling, carefully skim off all foam. Then reduce to low heat, maintain a gentle simmer, and cook slowly for about 1.5 hours.
4. After 1.5 hours, add salt and rock sugar to taste, gently stir, and continue simmering over low heat for another half hour to allow the salt to penetrate the beef.
5. When a chopstick can easily pierce the beef brisket, it is tender. Remove the whole brisket and let it cool; discard the white radish from the pot. Continue simmering the remaining beef bones, herbs, and broth over low heat for about 6 hours, reducing the broth to about 10 jin, resulting in a golden, clear broth.
6. Bring a separate pot of water to a boil, add a little salt, and cook the alkaline noodles. When the water boils again, add a bowl of cold water to prevent the noodles from being overcooked on the outside while remaining raw inside. Once the noodles are cooked, immediately remove them and plunge into cold water to cool. Then lift them out of the cold water, return to boiling water briefly to reheat, and quickly drain.
7. Slice the beef brisket into thick pieces of suitable size. Place the noodles in a bowl, top with brisket slices, ladle the hot clear broth from the large soup pot over the noodles, and sprinkle with chopped scallions.

## Health Tips
This clear-broth beef brisket noodle soup, slow-cooked with herbs and beef bones, has a rich and nourishing broth. It is suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter or for restorative care. However, salt intake should be controlled, and those with high blood pressure should avoid drinking the concentrated broth.
