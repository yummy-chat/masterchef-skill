# Braised Noodles with Spare Ribs and Long Beans
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Spare ribs 500g (cut into pieces)  
🫘 Long beans (cowpea) 250g (cut into sections)  
🍜 Fresh noodles for 2-3 servings  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (some sliced for blanching, some shredded for stir-frying)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (minced, preferably white part of large scallions, or use small spring onions if unavailable)  
🧄 Garlic 5-6 cloves (flattened and minced, divided into two portions: one for stir-frying, one for finishing)  
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching to remove odor)  
🍯 Sweet bean paste 1 tbsp  
🥘 Spare rib paste 1 tbsp (can substitute with yellow soybean paste or other sauces)  
🥢 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance flavor)  
🧂 Chicken essence a pinch (to enhance flavor)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add sparingly, adjust to taste)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking and washing)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying and braising)  
🥘 Soup pot (for blanching)  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Strainer, spoon, chopsticks  
🧻 Kitchen paper  

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the spare ribs into pieces, soak in cold water and rub to remove blood, then drain.
   **Note**: Soaking helps reduce the fishy smell.

2. Remove the ends of the long beans and cut into long sections; slice some ginger, shred some; mince the scallions; flatten and mince the garlic, dividing into two portions: one for stir-frying and one for finishing.

3. Fill a soup pot with cold water, add the spare ribs, cooking wine, ginger slices, and scallion whites. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Skim off the foam, remove the ribs, and pat dry with kitchen paper.
   **Key**: Add ribs to cold water; pat dry thoroughly after removing to prevent oil splattering during stir-frying.

4. Heat the wok, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Over medium heat, stir-fry the spare ribs until they change color. Add the stir-fry garlic, minced scallions, and shredded ginger, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add sweet bean paste, spare rib paste, and light soy sauce, stirring evenly.
   **Key**: Add the sauces only after the ribs change color to avoid burning.

5. Pour in hot water (at least 60°C) until the ribs are submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes.
   **Key**: Use hot water to prevent the meat from tightening.

6. Remove the lid, evenly spread the long bean sections into the wok. Add oyster sauce, sugar, and chicken essence. Taste and add a small amount of salt. Cover and simmer over low heat for 8 minutes, until the beans are soft and the liquid is level with the ingredients. Ladle out half a bowl of the broth and set aside.
   **Key**: Use salt sparingly, as the noodles will absorb the liquid later.

7. Evenly spread the fresh noodles over the ingredients. Optionally, spray a little cooking oil to prevent sticking. Cover and simmer over medium heat for 5 minutes, until the noodles are half-cooked and soft.

8. Remove the lid, use chopsticks to turn and separate the noodles. Pour in the reserved broth and continue heating over medium heat, stirring and turning for about 2 minutes, until the noodles are evenly coated and the liquid is absorbed.

9. Just before turning off the heat, sprinkle in the finishing garlic, stir evenly, and serve.

## Health Advice  
Braised noodles with spare ribs and long beans is a perfect combination of carbohydrates and protein. Pair it with a simple stir-fried vegetable dish for balanced nutrition. Use less salt and oil during cooking for a healthier version. Suitable for those needing energy replenishment, such as manual laborers, while individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should control their portion size.
