# Hot and Spicy Soup (Henan Style)
Cuisine: Henan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Lean beef, 1 small bowl (thinly sliced)
🫘 Raw peanuts, as needed (soaked in advance)
🌼 Daylily buds, as needed (soaked in advance)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, as needed (soaked in advance)
🌿 Shredded kelp, as needed (soaked in advance or rinsed clean)
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms, as needed (shredded)
🍄 Oyster mushrooms, as needed (torn into small pieces or shredded)
🍜 Glass noodles, as needed (soaked until soft in advance)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (finely chopped)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (chopped, for final garnish)
🌾 High-gluten wheat flour, about 500g (yields about 200g of gluten; must use high-gluten or extra-strong flour)

🧂 Soy sauce, as needed (for sautéing)
🍶 Huangjiu (Chinese rice wine), as needed (to remove gamey smell)
🌶️ White pepper powder, generous amount (the main spicy component in Hot and Spicy Soup)
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed (dry-roasted in advance to enhance aroma, then ground)
🌿 Five-spice powder, a pinch (do not overuse)
🧂 Salt, about 2 tablespoons
🧴 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (for color adjustment)
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (add before serving to enhance aroma)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok/iron pot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Small milk pot (for dry-roasting chili flakes)
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for kneading dough, washing gluten, and storing starchy water)
🔥 Stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Knead and rest the dough**: Add enough water to the high-gluten flour and knead into a slightly soft dough. Let it rest for as long as possible—the longer the rest, the more chewy the gluten.
2. **Wash the gluten**: Add clean water to the rested dough and repeatedly squeeze and wash it to extract the gluten and milky white starchy water. **The starchy water must be reserved**. Wash at least three times until the water runs clear and the gluten turns yellowish and translucent.
3. **Prepare the ingredients**: Thinly slice the beef. Soak all dried ingredients in advance. Finely chop scallions and ginger. Chop the cilantro.
4. **Dry-roast chili flakes**: Place the chili flakes in a dry, oil-free pan. Roast over low heat until dry and fragrant. Remove and grind them into a powder. This step adds a smoky aroma.
5. **Sauté the base**: Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the beef slices and stir-fry until they change color and release fragrance.
6. **Season the base**: Add the chopped scallions and ginger, stir-fry until fragrant. Add soy sauce and stir briefly, then add huangjiu to remove any gamey smell.
7. **Make the broth**: Add enough clean water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat.
8. **Add the vegetables**: Once the water is boiling, add the peanuts, daylily buds, wood ear mushrooms, shredded kelp, glass noodles, oyster mushrooms, and shiitake mushroom shreds in order. **Kelp, gluten, and peanuts are the three essential ingredients**. Kelp enhances the umami flavor.
9. **Season the soup**: Add a generous amount of white pepper powder, the roasted chili flakes, a pinch of five-spice powder, and about 2 tablespoons of salt. If the soup looks pale, add a little dark soy sauce to achieve a reddish-bright color.
   - **Key points**: The "heat" in Hot and Spicy Soup comes primarily from white pepper, so use it generously. Use five-spice powder sparingly to avoid overpowering the other flavors.
   (Add generous white pepper, roasted chili flakes, a tiny pinch of five-spice powder, and 2 tablespoons of salt. If the soup is pale, add dark soy sauce to color it reddish-bright.
   **Key points**: The heat mainly comes from white pepper, so use a lot. Use five-spice powder sparingly, don't let it overwhelm the taste.)
10. **Add the gluten**: Tear the washed gluten into flat pieces and drop them one by one into the boiling soup. Do not pull them into long strips; the torn pieces will be large and smooth.
11. **Thicken the soup**: Stir the reserved starchy water to combine it well, then slowly pour it into the pot while continuously stirring. The soup will gradually thicken.
    - **Secret**: Using the starchy water from washing the gluten results in a smoother texture than using cornstarch.
    (Stir the reserved starchy water well, slowly pour it into the pot while stirring continuously. The soup will get thicker and thicker.
    **Key point**: Using starchy water from washing gluten gives a much smoother texture than using cornstarch.)
12. **Finish cooking**: Reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly until the gluten pieces float to the surface, become translucent and springy, and the soup is thick. Just before turning off the heat, drizzle in sesame oil. Ladle into bowls and sprinkle with chopped cilantro.
    - **Note**: Use a wooden or stainless steel ladle to serve the soup to prevent it from thinning out.

## Health Advice
This bowl of Hot and Spicy Soup is an advanced stomach-warmer. The combination of beef, gluten, and a generous amount of pepper helps dispel cold and induce sweating, making it ideal for damp, cold weather or when you have a poor appetite. Peanuts and mushrooms add dietary fiber, but be mindful of salt intake—people with high blood pressure should limit their consumption of the broth. For a more balanced meal, it is recommended to serve the soup with deep-fried dough sticks or sesame flatbread. Also, the thick starchy water at the bottom of the bowl is relatively high in calories, so it should not be consumed in excess.
