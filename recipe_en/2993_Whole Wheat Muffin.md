# Whole Wheat Muffin
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients
🌾 Whole wheat flour 90g
💧 Water 90g
🍞 Bread flour 184g
🥛 Milk 83g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 26g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 6g
🧈 Unsalted butter 6g
🧂 Salt 3g
🌽 Corn flour, as needed

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt 3g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🔧 Spatula
🫙 Plastic wrap
⚖️ Kitchen scale
📋 Baking sheet, 2 pieces
🔥 Oven
🌬️ Cooling rack

## Preparation Steps
### Prepare the Pre-ferment (Tangzhong Starter)
1. Mix water, 1g low-sugar-tolerant dry yeast, and whole wheat flour in a bowl. Stir with a spatula until a uniform dough forms with no dry flour.
   **Note**: Stir until just combined; no need to knead.
2. Cover with plastic wrap and let ferment at room temperature until about doubled in size.

### Make the Main Dough
1. Tear the fermented pre-ferment into small pieces and place in a bowl. Add milk, whole egg liquid, 1g yeast, and sugar. Stir to combine evenly.
2. Add bread flour and salt. Use a spatula to fold and mix until a flaky dough forms with little dry flour.
3. Add unsalted butter. Knead or slap the dough by hand until the butter is fully incorporated, and the surface is smooth with even color.
   **Key Point**: Mix into flakes before adding butter to avoid sticky hands from premature kneading. Gluten windowpane is not required; just aim for a smooth, even surface.
4. Flatten the dough in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for about 30 minutes to relax.
   **Key Point**: Refrigeration relaxes the dough, promoting gluten formation and making it easier to handle. After removal, wet your hands and easily stretch a thin, elastic membrane.

### Fermentation and Shaping
1. Fold the dough and shape it into a tight, smooth round. Cover with plastic wrap and ferment at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
   **Judgment**: The dough visibly expands; when pressed with a floured finger, the indentation remains without springing back.
2. Gently press the dough to release gas and divide evenly into 6 portions (about 80g each).
3. For each portion, turn the smooth side outward, envelop the rough side, fold, and roll into a tight ball with a taut surface.

### Final Fermentation and Baking
1. Place the shaped dough balls on a baking sheet, cover with plastic wrap, and perform the final fermentation until a floured finger pressed into the dough leaves a slow-rebounding, half-depressed indentation.
2. Sift a layer of corn flour over the surface of the fermented dough.
3. Gently place another baking sheet on top of the dough and set in the preheated middle rack of the oven.
   **Key Point**: Covering with a baking sheet flattens the muffin tops and ensures even heat distribution, creating the classic muffin shape.
4. Use convection mode and bake at 165°C for 10 minutes.

### Cooling After Baking
1. Remove the baking sheets immediately after baking. Transfer the muffins to a cooling rack and cool to hand-warm temperature.
   **Key Point**: Remove from the pan promptly to prevent the bottoms from becoming soggy due to condensation.

## Health Advice
This whole wheat muffin uses the pre-ferment method to enhance texture, combining whole wheat and bread flour for a balanced mix of dietary fiber and protein. Ideal for breakfast during weight loss or as a post-workout snack. Note the very low sugar and butter content; the texture is denser. Pair with eggs or avocado to enhance flavor.
