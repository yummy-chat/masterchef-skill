# Easy No-Bake Snow Skin Mooncakes
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Snow skin premix 200g
💧 Boiling water 200g
🍠 Purple sweet potato powder to taste
🎃 Pumpkin powder to taste
🍓 Strawberry powder to taste (for coloring, additionally 15g for filling)
🍚 Cooked glutinous rice flour to taste
🥛 Whipping cream 120g (for two types of cream cheese fillings)
🧀 Cream cheese 200g
🍪 Oreo crumbs 30g
🫐 Dried cranberries 15g
🧂 White sugar 20g (added to whipping cream in portions)
🍈 Durian flesh to taste
🫘 Unsweetened red bean paste filling to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫖 Electric kettle
🥣 Bowl
🧤 Disposable gloves
🥖 Rolling pin
🥮 50g mooncake mold
🍽️ Tray
🎞️ Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. **Make the cream cheese filling**
   - Whip 60g whipping cream with 10g white sugar until it reaches a thick yogurt-like consistency, then add 100g softened cream cheese and mix well.
   - Strawberry cream cheese filling: Add 15g strawberry powder and 15g dried cranberries, mix well, then refrigerate until frozen solid.
   - Oreo cream cheese filling: Add 30g Oreo crumbs, mix well, then refrigerate until frozen solid.
**Note**: The fillings need to be frozen solid in advance to prevent deformation when wrapping.

2. **Boil water**
   - Bring 200g water to a full boil, ensuring the amount is precise.
3. **Knead the dough**
   - Pour the boiling water into the snow skin premix. First, stir with chopsticks until it forms flocculent pieces, then knead by hand into a smooth, non-sticky, and elastic dough.
**Key Point**: The premix is unsweetened, giving the finished product a light rice aroma.

4. **Color the dough**
   - Divide the dough into several portions and knead in purple sweet potato powder, pumpkin powder, and strawberry powder separately until the color is even; for a swirled effect, knead gently to preserve a marble pattern.
5. **Portion the dough and fillings**
   - Roll the snow skin dough and each filling into balls of about 25g each to improve wrapping efficiency.
6. **Wrap the filling**
   - Take one portion of snow skin dough, flatten it, place the filling in the center, lift the edges of the dough and pinch them together, then pinch the remaining two sides together. Remove any excess dough at the bottom, and roll into a thin cylinder.
   - Roll the surface in cooked glutinous rice flour to prevent sticking.
7. **Press into mold**
   - Place the floured dough ball into a 50g mooncake mold, press firmly, then push out evenly. The pattern should be clear.
8. **Storage**
   - The finished mooncakes must be sealed and refrigerated. They taste best within 2-3 days. They can be frozen for up to half a month.

## Health Tips
These snow skin mooncakes are low in sugar and oil, making them suitable for those managing their sugar intake. However, the cream cheese filling and cream are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat only 1-2 pieces at a time. Using snow skin premix eliminates the need for steaming, making the process simple and easy for beginners to master.
