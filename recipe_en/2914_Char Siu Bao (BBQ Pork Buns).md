# Char Siu Bao (BBQ Pork Buns)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly 250g (half a catty, cut into small pieces)  
🧅 Onion half (finely chopped)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g  
🍬 White sugar 1 tablespoon (for kneading dough) + 1 tablespoon (for filling)  
💧 Warm water about 250–260ml (adjust according to flour absorption)  
🍚 Yeast 5g  

🧉 Char siu sauce 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Light soy sauce about 3 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon  
🫘 Soybean paste 1 tablespoon  
💧 Water about 2–3 tablespoons (to dilute the sauce)  
🫚 Ginger powder 1 tablespoon  
🥃 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) 1 tablespoon  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🥣 Small bowl, spoon  
🪠 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer, steaming basket  
📄 Parchment paper  

## Instructions  
1. **Knead and proof the dough**: Pour flour into a large bowl, make a well in the center, add yeast and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Gradually pour in warm water, first dissolving the yeast, then stir into flaky pieces. Knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot until doubled in volume—when pulled apart, it should show a dense honeycomb structure.  
   *Key point: The dough should be slightly soft; add water gradually for better control of firmness.*  

2. **Prepare the char siu sauce**: In a bowl, mix char siu sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, soybean paste, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 2–3 tablespoons of water. Stir until well combined and set aside.  

3. **Cook the filling**: Heat a frying pan without oil, directly add the pork belly pieces and stir-fry until fat renders. Add ginger powder and baijiu to remove any gamey smell, stir evenly, then add the chopped onion and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in the prepared char siu sauce, increase heat, and stir-fry until the sauce thickens and coats the meat pieces well. Remove from heat.  
   *Key point: The sauce must be reduced until sticky; let the cooked filling cool slightly, then freeze for 1–2 hours to solidify, making it easier to wrap later.*  

4. **Deflate and portion the dough**: Sprinkle a little dry flour over the proofed dough. Knead vigorously to release gas for about 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and free of air pockets. Roll into a long log and cut into evenly sized dough pieces.  

5. **Roll out wrappers and fill**: Roll each dough piece into a round wrapper with a thick center and thin edges. Place an appropriate amount of the frozen char siu filling in the center.  
   *Key point: A thick center prevents the bottom from breaking; keep the filling cold and solid for easier wrapping.*  

6. **Pleat and shape**: Hold the wrapper in your left hand, pressing the filling down with your thumb. With your right thumb and index finger, lift and pleat the edges while turning, then pinch the top tightly to seal.  

7. **Second proofing**: Place each bun on a piece of parchment paper, arrange in the steamer basket, cover with the lid, and let proof for about 30–40 minutes until the buns have visibly expanded and feel light and puffy when lifted.  
   *Key point: The second proofing must be thorough—this is key to fluffy buns.*  

8. **Steam**: Bring water in the steamer to a boil, then place the buns inside. Steam over high heat for 15 minutes. After turning off the heat, do not open the lid immediately—let the buns rest in the steamer for 3–5 minutes before uncovering.  
   *Key point: Steam over boiling water; the resting period prevents the buns from shrinking or collapsing due to sudden temperature change.*  

## Health Advice  
Pork belly is high in fat, so it is recommended to serve with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for occasional indulgence; those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids may consider reducing the amount of sauce used.
