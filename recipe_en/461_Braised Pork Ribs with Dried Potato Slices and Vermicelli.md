# Braised Pork Ribs with Dried Potato Slices and Vermicelli

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour oil into the wok and heat over medium heat. Add the blanched ribs and stir-fry continuously until the surface turns slightly golden brown.  
**Note**: Browning the ribs creates a caramelized layer that locks in juices, resulting in a more tender texture.

2. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until the spices release a strong aroma.  
**Note**: The spices must be stir-fried until fragrant before seasoning, otherwise their aroma won't fully develop.

3. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt. Stir-fry quickly to combine, ensuring each piece of rib is evenly coated with the sauce and turns a glossy red.  
**Note**: Stir-fry to ensure the sauce coats the ribs evenly, preventing uneven coloring.

4. Pour in hot water and add the dried potato slices. Cover with the lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and braise for 40 minutes.  
**Note**: Hot water must be used; cold water will cause the meat to tighten and become tough. Dried potato slices do not need to be pre-soaked; add them directly to absorb the broth.

5. Remove the lid, add the softened vermicelli, cover again, and continue braising for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: The vermicelli has been pre-soaked; do not braise for more than 10 minutes to avoid it becoming too soft and losing texture.

6. Increase heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients, sprinkle in the chopped scallions, gently toss a few times, and serve.  
**Note**: Stir frequently during high-heat reduction to prevent sticking. Adjust the sauce thickness to personal preference.

## Health Advice  
This dish of braised pork ribs with dried potato slices and vermicelli is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy after physical exertion. However, it is high in fat and salt, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Be mindful of salt control during braising; soaking the vermicelli before adding it helps prevent it from absorbing too much broth and becoming overly salty.
