# No-Knead Oil-Free Sugar-Free Bagels
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 200g  
💧 Water 120g  
🍞 Active dry yeast 2.5g  
🧂 Salt 2g  
⚫ Black sesame seeds 10g  
🍬 White granulated sugar 40g (for boiling bagel water)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥛 Measuring cup with刻度  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Cutting board  
🌿 Rolling pin  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  
🍲 Stainless steel pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔥 Oven  
🧻 Baking tray  
📄 Parchment paper  

## Instructions  
1. Dissolve the yeast in water and stir until completely dissolved to create a yeast mixture.  
2. In a large bowl, combine bread flour, salt, and black sesame seeds. Pour in the yeast mixture and stir with chopsticks until a wet, shaggy dough forms. Then, roughly knead it into a ball with your hands.  
   **Note**: Do not over-knead; it is fine if the dough is not smooth at this stage.  
3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Chilling allows the gluten to develop naturally, making subsequent shaping easier.  
4. Remove the dough and fold it inward and press down 4-5 times until the surface becomes smooth.  
   **Check for doneness**: The dough should be smooth and non-sticky, with no cracks.  
5. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions, roll each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 10 minutes.  
   **Purpose**: Relaxes the gluten, preventing shrinkage when rolling out.  
6. Take one dough ball and roll it into a rectangular sheet. Tightly roll it from the long side into a log. Flatten one end into a fan shape, wrap it around the other end, and pinch the seam tightly to form a ring.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the seam is sealed well, or it will crack during boiling; slightly stretch the center hole.  
7. Place the shaped bagels on a baking tray lined with parchment paper, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 30-40 minutes.  
   **Check for doneness**: The bagels should be noticeably larger and slowly spring back when lightly pressed.  
8. In a stainless steel pot, add about 1.5 bottles of water and 40g of white sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Use a slotted spoon to lower bagels into the sugar water, boil for 30 seconds per side, then remove and drain.  
   **Note**: Keep the water at a gentle simmer to avoid violent bubbling that can deform the bagels.  
9. Preheat the oven to 230°C (top heat) and 180°C (bottom heat). Return the boiled bagels to the parchment-lined baking tray, place on the middle rack, and bake for about 12 minutes until golden brown.  
   **Check for doneness**: The crust should be crisp, the bagel springy when pressed, and a hollow sound when tapped on the bottom after removal.  
10. Basic bagels can be enjoyed as is, or sliced horizontally and spread with cream cheese or filled to make sandwiches.

## Health Tips  
These low-oil, low-sugar bagels are rich in protein and dietary fiber, making them ideal as a staple food during weight loss. Note that boiling time should not be too long, or the crust will become thick.
