# Hot Pot Sesame Dipping Sauce
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons (especially winter)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Pure sesame paste, as needed  
🥜 Peanut butter, as needed  
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, a small bunch  
🌿 Chopped scallions, as needed  
🫒 Dried chili flakes, as needed  
🧅 Minced scallion whites, as needed  
🧂 Toasted white sesame seeds, as needed  
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, as needed  
🦐 Fermented shrimp paste, a small amount  
🥫 Soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 White sugar, a little  
🫘 Fermented bean curd, as needed  
🌱 Chinese chive flower sauce, as needed  
🌶️ Freshly fried chili oil, as needed  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass or ceramic bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🫗 Strainer  
♨️ Stove  

## Instructions
1. Place Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon, and bay leaves in a bowl. Pour in boiling water and let steep to create a five-spice water. Set aside to cool.  
2. In another bowl, combine pure sesame paste and peanut butter. Stir with a spoon until evenly mixed.  
3. Gradually add the five-spice water in batches, stirring continuously in one direction with the spoon until the paste thickens and reaches a creamy consistency.  
**Key point**: Add only a small amount of water each time, stirring until fully absorbed before adding more, to prevent the paste from becoming watery.  
4. Add soy sauce, a small amount of fermented shrimp paste, and white sugar. Continue stirring until well combined.  
**Note**: Fermented shrimp paste is key to the flavor of Beijing hot pot sesame sauce; use sparingly, not in excess.  
5. Add fermented bean curd and Chinese chive flower sauce. Stir thoroughly until the sauce is smooth, with a consistency similar to runny yogurt.  
6. Prepare freshly fried chili oil: Place dried chili flakes, baijiu, salt, minced scallion whites, and toasted white sesame seeds in a bowl. Heat vegetable oil in a pan until it begins to smoke, then pour it over the dry ingredients in three batches, pausing briefly between each pour.  
**Note**: The oil must be hot enough to smoke, and pouring in batches fully releases the aroma of the chili and spices.  
7. Ladle the prepared sesame sauce into small bowls. Top with the chili oil, including chili flakes and sesame seeds, and sprinkle with cilantro before serving.  

## Health Tips
Thinning sesame paste with five-spice water and pairing it with fermented bean curd and Chinese chive flower sauce not only provides excellent calcium supplementation but also aids digestion, making it ideal for winter consumption. Be mindful not to use too much fermented shrimp paste, as excessive saltiness can overpower the meat's flavor.
