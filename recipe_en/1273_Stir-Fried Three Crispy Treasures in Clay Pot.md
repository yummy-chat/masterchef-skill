# Stir-Fried Three Crispy Treasures in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Shrimp, appropriate amount (pat dry the surface beforehand)
🐟 Fish fillet, appropriate amount (crispy grass carp can be used for extra crunch)
🦑 Squid, appropriate amount (pat dry and score in a crosshatch pattern)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (use only freshly smashed garlic; do not use pre-soaked garlic)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (finely diced)
🧅 Shallots (red onion heads), appropriate amount
🫑 Green and red bell peppers, appropriate amount (optional, to balance texture)
🧅 Scallions, cut into sections, appropriate amount (add just before serving)
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (add just before serving)
🍶 Cantonese clay pot sauce, appropriate amount (recommend using a thick, ready-made sauce)
🍶 Cooking wine or baijiu, appropriate amount (for flambéing to enhance aroma)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (use cold oil in a hot wok)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥘 Clay pot (clay pot for stir-frying)
🥄 Wok spatula
📄 Paper towels
🔥 High-heat stove burner

## Instructions
1. Spread the shrimp and squid flat on paper towels. Gently press with your hand to thoroughly pat dry the surface moisture.
**Note**: Drying the ingredients is a critical step to ensure a dry, fragrant final dish and to avoid a watery texture.

2. Smash the garlic with the flat side of a knife. Finely dice the ginger. Slice the shallots. Score the squid in a crosshatch pattern. Cut the bell peppers into chunks. Cut the scallions into sections. Wash and cut the cilantro into sections.
**Note**: Smashing garlic releases its aroma faster than chopping it. Scoring the squid helps the sauce adhere better and ensures even cooking.

3. Heat the clay pot over high heat until it is very hot. Pour in cold oil. Add the diced ginger and smashed garlic. Stir-fry over medium heat for 2–3 minutes, until the garlic pieces are lightly browned on the surface and fragrant.
**Note**: Adding cold oil to a hot pot helps prevent sticking.

4. First, add the patted-dry shrimp. Stir-fry over medium heat for 2–3 minutes, until the shrimp shells turn bright red and the flesh is initially set. Add the fish fillets and continue stir-frying for 1–2 minutes, allowing the fish to absorb the base aromas, until the surface tightens and the color lightens. Then add the shallots and stir-fry over medium heat for about 30 seconds to fully release their rich aroma.
**Note**: Shrimp are thicker and take longer to cook, so they should be added first.

5. Drizzle the thick Cantonese clay pot sauce evenly over the ingredients. Stir-fry quickly to coat all ingredients thoroughly with the sauce.
**Note**: The sauce must be thick to cling to the ingredients without overpowering their natural flavors.

6. Add the green and red bell pepper chunks and stir-fry evenly. This adds moisture and balances the texture.

7. Add the patted-dry squid. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly for about 1 minute, until the squid curls up into a crosshatch pattern and the surface tightens.
**Note**: Squid is thin and cooks quickly, becoming tough and shrinking severely if overcooked. Do not add it too early.

8. Splash cooking wine or baijiu along the edge of the pot. Ignite the alcohol to create a flame inside the pot and maintain the flame for about 10 seconds.
**Note**: Flambéing is a key step to create the signature "wok hei" (breath of the wok) aroma of clay pot dishes, further enhancing the fragrance of the ingredients.

9. Add the scallion sections and cilantro. Stir-fry quickly to combine, then reduce the heat to medium, cover, and simmer for 4–5 minutes. Remove from heat and serve immediately.
**Note**: Adding the aromatic herbs at the very end preserves their fresh fragrance and prevents them from becoming bitter or losing their aroma during prolonged cooking.

## Health Tips
Shrimp, fish, and squid—three crispy treasures in one dish—are high in protein and low in fat, making this suitable for muscle building and weight loss. However, the clay pot sauce is relatively high in sodium, so be mindful of your total salt intake. Ensure ingredients are thoroughly dried to lock in a dry, crispy, and tender texture.
