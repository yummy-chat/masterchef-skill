# Braised Vegan Chicken (Sù Jī)

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 2 sticks of vegan chicken (cut into approximately 1 cm thick slices)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (use the white sections)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (sliced)
🌟 Star anise, as needed
🪵 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🍃 Bay leaves, as needed
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed
🧈 Lard, as needed
🍶 Yellow wine (Shaoxing wine), as needed (pour along the edge of the wok)
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed (pour along the edge of the wok)
🥄 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🍬 Rock sugar, approximately 15g (can substitute with white sugar)
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌫️ White pepper powder, as needed
🧂 MSG (monosodium glutamate), as needed
💧 Water, as needed (enough to level with the ingredients)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥄 Spatula
🥘 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl or basin (for cold water soaking)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Cut the vegan chicken into even slices about 1 cm thick. Cut the scallion into sections, slice the ginger and garlic. Set aside together with the star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and dried chilies.
   **Note**: The thickness must be even; slices that are too thin will break easily, and slices that are too thick will not absorb flavor well.
2.  Heat oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Add the vegan chicken slices and fry slowly. Fry until one side is golden brown and forms a crispy crust, then flip and continue frying until both sides are golden brown.
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; rotate the wok occasionally for even heating. Creating a crispy crust helps with absorbing the sauce later.
3.  Remove the fried vegan chicken slices and immediately immerse them in cold water for about 10 minutes. Once the slices have softened from water absorption and developed a honeycomb interior, drain them thoroughly.
   **Principle**: Thermal expansion and contraction create internal pores, which facilitates sauce absorption.
4.  In a separate wok, heat lard. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic slices, stir-frying over medium-low heat until fragrant. Then add the star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and dried chilies, stir-frying until they release their aroma.
   **Note**: Add the ingredients in batches to prevent the dry spices from burning.
5.  Add the soaked and drained vegan chicken slices. Turn the heat to high. Pour the yellow wine and light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry quickly until the sauce's aroma is released.
   **Key Point**: Pouring along the wok edge uses high heat to volatilize and enhance the fragrance.
6.  Add water. Season with salt, rock sugar, dark soy sauce, white pepper powder, and MSG. Mix well.
   **Note**: Do not add excessive water. The amount of rock sugar can be slightly more to achieve the desired sweetness and gloss characteristic of braised dishes.
7.  Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes to allow the vegan chicken to fully absorb the sauce and flavor.
8.  Remove the lid. Once the sauce has thickened, use chopsticks to pick out and discard the scallion sections, ginger slices, and all spice residues. Serve immediately.
   **Characteristics**: The finished dish has a glossy red color. The vegan chicken is tender and juicy, with a honeycomb interior. Each bite is filled with the rich, savory-sweet sauce.

## Health Suggestions
The frying and cold water soaking process creates a honeycomb structure in the vegan chicken, enabling it to absorb a significant amount of sauce. Consequently, the calorie content increases, so portion control is advised. The lard and rock sugar enhance the flavor, but it is recommended to serve this dish with light vegetables for a balanced meal.
