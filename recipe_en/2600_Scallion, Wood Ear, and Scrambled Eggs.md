# Scallion, Wood Ear, and Scrambled Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Instructions  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the soaked wood ear mushrooms, blanch over high heat for about 1 minute, then remove and drain.  
2. Heat the wok, add a little cooking oil to coat the surface, then pour it out. Add fresh cooking oil, heat to about 40% hot, pour in the beaten eggs, and gently stir with a wok spatula over medium heat until the eggs set into pale yellow curds. Remove and set aside.  
3. In another wok, add lard and heat over low heat. Add the Sichuan peppercorns, fry slowly until fragrant and the color deepens, then remove the peppercorns and reserve the peppercorn oil.  
4. In the peppercorn oil, add the ginger slices and broken star anise, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Add the diagonally cut scallion sections, stir-fry over medium-low heat until slightly wilted and fragrant, being careful not to overcook to preserve texture.  
6. Splash light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, stir quickly to release the aroma, then add yellow rice wine along the edge. Simmer over low heat for about half a minute to let the wine fragrance meld.  
7. Add a small bowl of water, season with salt, sugar, MSG, and white pepper powder, and stir to combine.  
8. Return the blanched wood ear mushrooms and scrambled eggs to the wok, add the red chili pepper slices for color, adjust the color with a little dark soy sauce if needed, and stir to mix evenly.  
9. Splash water starch along the edge of the wok, stir-fry until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients evenly.  
10. Before removing from heat, drizzle sesame oil, toss to combine, then plate.  

## Health Tips  
This dish uses lard and peppercorn oil to enhance flavor, making it suitable for those needing high-quality protein and dietary fiber. Individuals with high blood pressure should watch the amount of salt and dark soy sauce. Blanching the wood ear mushrooms makes them crisper, and scrambling the eggs until pale yellow preserves more nutrients.
