# Spicy Fried Chicken Drumsticks
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 8 chicken drumsticks (washed and drained)
🌾 200g cake flour
🌽 100g cornstarch
🥚 5 eggs (about 230g)
🫚 5 slices ginger
🧅 Some onion (shredded)
🧄 Some garlic (sliced or crushed)
🍋 A little lemon (for juice)
🧂 2 spoons salt
🍬 1 spoon sugar
🌶️ 3 spoons chili flakes (omit if not spicy)
🍅 1 spoon ketchup
🫒 15g cooking oil (for marinating) + enough (for deep frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥄 Measuring cup and measuring spoons
🍳 Wok (for deep frying)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. Rinse the chicken drumsticks repeatedly until the water runs clear, then drain.
   **Note**: Washing away blood water and draining is the basis for removing gamey smell and ensuring seasoning.
2. Poke holes in the surface of each drumstick with a toothpick, avoiding cutting the skin.
   **Note**: Poking holes helps the flavor penetrate; not cutting the meat locks in moisture, keeping the meat tender.
3. Place the drumsticks in a large bowl. Add salt, sugar, chili flakes, ginger slices, shredded onion, garlic, ketchup, squeeze in lemon juice, and finally drizzle in the marinating oil.
4. Use your hands to thoroughly mix and coat the drumsticks with all the seasonings, ensuring each drumstick is evenly covered.
5. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 3 hours, or marinate overnight.
   **Note**: Long marination is necessary for full flavor infusion.
6. Prepare the coating: Mix the cake flour and cornstarch evenly.
7. Prepare the egg wash: Beat the eggs into a liquid in a separate bowl.
8. Remove the marinated drumsticks. First roll them in the egg wash, then place them in the coating to be evenly covered, gently shaking off any excess powder.
   **Note**: Shaking off excess powder prevents too many crumbs during frying, making the crust thinner and crispier. One layer is usually enough.
9. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat to about 40-50% hot over medium-low heat.
   **Note**: The oil temperature should not be too high; if it's too high, the outside will burn while the inside remains raw; if too low, the drumsticks will absorb too much oil.
10. Place the coated drumsticks into the oil one by one. Do not move them right after they go in; wait until the crust is set before turning, to prevent the coating from falling off.
11. Fry over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes, occasionally turning to ensure even heating, until a chopstick can be easily inserted into the thickest part without any bloody liquid seeping out. Remove and drain the oil.
12. Raise the oil temperature to about 70% hot. Return all the drumsticks to the oil for about 15 seconds to re-fry, until the skin quickly becomes golden and crispy. Immediately remove and drain the oil.
    **Note**: Re-frying is the key to a crispy texture; do not overdo it, or the drumsticks will burn.

## Health Advice
Spicy fried chicken drumsticks are rich in protein, but portion control is necessary to avoid excessive fat and calorie intake. They are suitable for occasional indulgence and not recommended as a daily staple, especially for those on a weight loss diet.
