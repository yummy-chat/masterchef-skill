# Seaweed Rice Ball Sandwich
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 White rice about 150g (leftover rice heated until warm)
🍖 Pork floss about 20g
🌿 Seaweed (nori) 1 sheet
🍅 Tomato half (sliced evenly)
🥬 Lettuce 2-3 leaves (washed and drained)
🧀 Cheese slice 1 slice
🫒 Mayonnaise as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife
🌯 Plastic wrap
♨️ Microwave

## Steps
1. Wrap leftover rice in plastic wrap and microwave until warm.
   **Key point**: The rice should have a moderate texture; too dry or too wet will affect the texture of the rice ball.
2. Slice the tomato into even thin slices, wash the lettuce and drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Draining the lettuce prevents the sandwich layers from becoming too wet, keeping the seaweed crispy.
3. Take one sheet of seaweed and use a knife to make a cross cut in the center, cutting about half the diameter of the seaweed.
4. On the four sections of the seaweed, place rice, pork floss, tomato slices, lettuce with cheese slice, and mayonnaise.
   **Key point**: Spread the ingredients evenly; the mayonnaise adds moisture and a savory flavor.
5. Fold the seaweed in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction to wrap it into a square rice ball.
   **Key point**: Fold tightly to prevent loosening or cracking.
6. Wrap the rice ball tightly with plastic wrap and let it set for a moment.
7. Remove the plastic wrap and cut in half with a knife before serving.
   **Key point**: Keep the knife clean and dry for a neater cut; wrapping in plastic wrap before cutting helps shape the ball and prevents it from falling apart.

## Health Tips
The seaweed rice ball sandwich combines carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables, making it suitable for a quick lunch or picnic. Choosing low-fat mayonnaise can help control calories, while the savory combination of pork floss and cheese, paired with the freshness of lettuce and tomato, provides a more balanced nutrition.
