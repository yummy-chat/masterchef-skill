# Pleatless Thumb-Sized Pan-Fried Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients
🥩 Ground pork 100g
🍤 Shrimp (适量, diced)
🧅 White scallion (适量, finely shredded)
🥟 Store-bought dumpling/wonton wrappers (适量)
🌿 Chopped scallion (适量)
🦐 Dried shrimp (a small handful)

## Seasonings
🧂 Chicken bouillon (a pinch)
🍬 Sugar (a pinch)
🧂 Salt (a pinch)
🥄 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1 tbsp
🫒 Sesame oil (适量)
🦪 Oyster sauce ½ tbsp
🍶 Cooking wine (a pinch)
💧 Scallion-ginger water (适量)
🌶 Ground Sichuan pepper (适量)
🌿 White pepper (a pinch)

**Crisp Lace Water**
🌾 All-purpose flour ½ tbsp
🥔 Cornstarch ½ tbsp
💧 Water 5 tbsp
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp
🥚 Beaten egg 1 tbsp

**Dipping Sauce**
🧂 Salt (a pinch)
🌿 White pepper (a pinch)
🫒 Sesame oil (a pinch)
💧 Boiling water (适量)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥣 Glass bowl
🔪 Cutting board & knife
🥄 Small bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍴 Spatula

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the meat filling**
   Add chicken bouillon, sugar, salt, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, oyster sauce, and cooking wine to the ground pork. Gradually add scallion-ginger water in small batches, stirring in one direction until the mixture becomes firm and pale in color.
   **Note**: Always stir in the same direction, or the filling will not bind properly.

2. **Prepare the scallion oil and shrimp filling**
   Finely shred the white scallion, sprinkle with ground Sichuan pepper, and drizzle with sesame oil; mix well. Dice the shrimp and toss with a pinch of white pepper. Combine the seasoned pork, scallion mixture, and diced shrimp, mixing evenly. Set aside.

3. **Prepare the crisp lace water**
   Combine flour, cornstarch, water, sesame oil, and beaten egg in a bowl. Stir thoroughly until smooth.
   **Key point**: The beaten egg is the secret to forming a golden, web-like crispy crust.

4. **Prepare the dipping sauce**
   Add dried shrimp, a pinch of salt, white pepper, and sesame oil to the remaining beaten egg. Pour in boiling water, stir well, and let cool. Set aside.

5. **Assemble the buns**
   Take a store-bought dumpling wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, and pinch the edges tightly to seal into a small, pleatless bun shape.

6. **Pan-fry the buns**
   Heat a non-stick pan over high heat, then add cold oil. Arrange the thumb-sized buns neatly in the pan. Once the oil is hot and you hear a sizzling sound, reduce the heat to low. Fry for about 2 minutes until the bottoms are set and lightly golden.

7. **Add the crisp lace water**
   Pour the prepared crisp lace water evenly into the pan, just enough to cover the bottoms of the buns. Keep the heat on the lowest setting, cover with a lid, and steam for 5 minutes.

8. **Evaporate the liquid**
   Remove the lid. If there is still excess liquid in the pan, increase the heat to high and cook until the water has completely evaporated and a crispy, golden web has formed on the bottom. Optionally, gently flip the buns and briefly fry the other sides.
   **Optional**: Add a small amount of chili oil at this step for extra color and flavor.

9. **Serve**
   Sprinkle with chopped scallion, carefully transfer to a plate, and serve hot with the dried shrimp egg drop dipping sauce.

## Health Advice
These thumb-sized pan-fried buns use store-bought dumpling wrappers for quick assembly, saving you the trouble of making dough—perfect for satisfying a craving on busy days. The combination of pork and shrimp, beaten with scallion-ginger water, creates a tender and juicy filling. However, be mindful of the salt in the dipping sauce; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. The golden crispy web formed by the egg in the crisp lace water is visually appealing, but it is relatively high in calories.
