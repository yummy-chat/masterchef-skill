# Stir-fried Dried Mustard Green with Pork

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps  
1. Slice the pork belly, add half a spoon of dark soy sauce, and mix well. Marinate to color the meat.  
2. Keep the dried mustard green dry; no need to blanch. Heat the wok, add a little oil, and dry-fry the dried mustard green until no obvious moisture remains. Remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: Dry-frying is a key step to ensure the dried mustard green stays green and crispy; do not skip it.  
3. Add oil to the wok again to coat the surface, then add the marinated pork belly slices. Stir-fry until the surface turns golden and the fat renders out.  
4. Add the sliced garlic and ginger, and continue stir-frying until fragrant.  
5. Return the dry-fried dried mustard green to the wok, and mix well with the pork slices.  
6. Add about 3 grams of salt, oyster sauce, and soy sauce. Quickly stir to combine and season.  
7. Add the green garlic sections, and stir-fry until just cooked.  
8. Before turning off the heat, sprinkle in the small chili pepper rings and MSG. Use residual heat to mix well, then serve.  
   **Note**: Add the small chili peppers at the end only to enhance color and aroma, avoiding overcooking that would soften them.  

## Health Tips  
Dried mustard green is low in fat and rich in dietary fiber. Paired with pork belly, it provides protein and is suitable for calorie control during weight loss. Dry-frying is a key step to retain a crispy texture and reduce oil absorption. The rendered fat from the pork belly adds flavor, but those with high blood lipids should consume sparingly.
