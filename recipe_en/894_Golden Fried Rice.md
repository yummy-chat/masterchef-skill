# Golden Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice, appropriate amount (day-old rice recommended)
🥚 Eggs, 3 (yolks only, egg whites reserved for other use)
🥚 Salted egg yolks, appropriate amount
🥩 Luncheon meat, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)
🍤 Shrimp, appropriate amount
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into chopped scallions, added at the end)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount
🧂 White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for blanching, sautéing, and frying)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Chinese wok
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spatula

## Instructions
1. Place the rice in a bowl, add salt, chicken powder, and white pepper powder, and mix well to season.
   **Key**: Season the rice before frying.
2. Separate the egg yolks from the egg whites; reserve the yolks. Cut the luncheon meat into small cubes. Blanch the shrimp and luncheon meat cubes in boiling water, then drain.
3. Pour the egg yolks into the seasoned rice and mix thoroughly with your hands, ensuring every grain of rice is evenly coated with the egg mixture.
   **Key**: Even mixing is essential for the formation of golden fried rice.
4. Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok. Quickly blanch the shrimp and luncheon meat cubes in the hot oil until they change color and become fragrant, then remove and drain.
   **Note**: Use a hot wok and hot oil, and stir-fry briefly over medium heat.
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the onion cubes and stir-fry over medium heat until soft and translucent.
   **Note**: The onion must be sautéed until fragrant first.
6. Add the crushed salted egg yolks and stir-fry over medium heat until they bubble and release a sandy, aromatic fragrance.
   **Key**: Fully bring out the aroma and sandy texture of the salted egg yolks.
7. Pour the remaining egg yolk liquid into the wok, quickly break it apart with the spatula, and stir-fry into evenly set egg crumbles.
8. Add the rice coated with egg mixture. Continuously stir-fry over medium-high heat until the grains are distinct and begin to jump in the wok.
   **Key**: Thoroughly stir-fry the rice to release its fragrance—this is an important step to develop wok hei.
9. Return the blanched shrimp and luncheon meat cubes to the wok, and stir-fry evenly with the rice.
   **Note**: Since the rice has already been seasoned, no additional seasonings are needed at this stage.
10. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in the chopped scallions, and toss well using residual heat.
    **Key**: Scallions must be added at the end to preserve their aroma and vibrant green color.

## Health Advice
This golden fried rice offers balanced nutrition and is rich in protein. However, salted egg yolks and luncheon meat are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. Day-old rice contains more resistant starch, resulting in a lower glycemic response, making it suitable for those managing blood sugar levels.
