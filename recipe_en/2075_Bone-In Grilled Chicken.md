# Bone-In Grilled Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken approx. 6 jin 8 liang (cut into bone-in pieces)  
🍺 Beer 2 bottles (for marinating)  
🫒 Peanut oil to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍬 Sugar to taste  
🌿 Cumin powder to taste (sprinkle after grilling)  
🤍 White sesame seeds to taste (sprinkle after grilling)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍢 Metal skewers  
🔥 Rectangular charcoal grill  
🕸️ Metal grill grate  
🍽️ Large metal round plate  

## Steps
1.  Slaughter and clean the chicken, chop into small bone-in pieces, and thread onto metal skewers.  
**Note**: Meat pieces must be bone-in to highlight the "bone-in grilled" feature.

2.  Marinate the skewered chicken pieces with peanut oil, beer, salt, and sugar until flavorful.  
**Key point**: Beer is essential; it tenderizes the meat and imparts a subtle malty aroma and light sweetness.

3.  Arrange the marinated chicken skewers closely together on the grill grate of the charcoal grill and grill directly over open flame.  
**Note**: Grilling time is very short, about three to four minutes. Monitor the color change of the chicken and the melting of fat.

4.  Flip appropriately during grilling to ensure even heat. Grill until the skin is slightly charred and the inside is fully cooked, being careful not to burn.

5.  After grilling, sprinkle hot chicken with cumin powder and white sesame seeds, then transfer to a plate to serve.

## Health Suggestion
This bone-in grilled chicken uses beer to tenderize the meat, making it suitable for those needing high protein, such as fitness enthusiasts. However, charcoal-grilled food has a significant calorie count; it is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad for balance.
