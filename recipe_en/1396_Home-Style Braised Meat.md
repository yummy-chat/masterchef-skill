# Home-Style Braised Meat

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦴 Pork leg bone 800–1000g (for making simple broth)
🥩 Meats (choose as desired, appropriate amount): Beef shank/beef leg meat, pork trotters, pork hock, pork belly, pork intestines, duck heads, duck necks, duck intestines, etc.
🥚 Eggs (appropriate amount, boiled and peeled for later use, optional)
🥬 Vegetables/Tofu products (appropriate amount, optional): Potatoes, lotus root slices, kelp knots, fish tofu, tempura, green hot peppers, etc.
🫘 Dried red dates about 10 (for fragrance and color)
🫚 Ginger about 50g (sliced, half for blanching, half for stir-frying)
🧅 Scallion about 30g (white part, cut into pieces, for blanching and stir-frying)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers about 30g (for spiciness, adjust to taste)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about 15g (for numbing sensation)

🧂 **Spice packet**: 2 star anise, 1 piece of cassia bark (about half an index finger long), 1 cardamom, 3-4 pieces of angelica dahurica, 5 pieces of kaempferia galanga, 3 bay leaves, 2 amomum villosum, a small pinch of cumin, a small pinch of dried tangerine peel, 10-15 cloves, 5-6 white cardamom, 2-4 hawthorn berries (place all except cloves in a non-woven fabric spice bag)
🫙 Broad bean paste/soybean paste 6 tbsp (about 60g)
🥄 Light soy sauce 7-8 tbsp (about 80g)
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1-3 tbsp (10-30g, for coloring)
🦪 Oyster sauce 3 tbsp (about 30g)
🍶 Yellow wine/Huadiao wine 7 tbsp (about 70g, do not use cooking wine)
🍬 Rock sugar a handful (about 100g, reduce by half if you don't like sweetness)
🧂 MSG/Chicken bouillon 1 tbsp (about 10g, optional)
🧂 Salt 1 tbsp (about 10g, the sauces are already salty, use sparingly)
🍾 High-proof liquor appropriate amount (for blanching intestines/duck parts to remove odor)
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching regular meats to remove odor)
🥄 Thirteen-spice powder about 3 tbsp (20-30g, a simplified home version to replace the spice packet, optional)
🧂 Edible lye/baking soda, salt appropriate amount (for cleaning pork intestines)
🫒 Cooking oil a generous amount (for stir-frying the base)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (non-stick pan)
♨️ Pressure cooker (electric pressure cooker) or rice cooker
🍲 Clay pot (optional, for making broth)
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon/soup ladle
⚖️ Kitchen scale (not essential)
🧀 Plastic wrap (for shaping beef)
🧃 Non-woven fabric spice bag/gauze bag

## Preparation Steps
### I. Preparation Phase
**1. Ingredient Preparation and Cleaning**
- For pork trotters, pork hock, and other meats with skin, if the skin has hair, burn it black and scrub clean with a steel wool ball.
- Soak all meats in cold water to fully draw out the blood.
- **Special treatment for pork intestines**: Even if using pre-cleaned intestines, they still need to be rubbed vigorously several times with edible lye or baking soda and salt to thoroughly remove any dirt and odor.

**2. Blanching**
- Intestines, offal, and duck parts: Place in cold water, add a splash of high-proof liquor, bring to a boil over high heat until water just starts to bubble, then turn off the heat. Remove and rinse with hot water.
- Regular meats: Place in cold water, add ginger slices, scallion pieces, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to low heat and skim off any foam. Remove and rinse with hot water.
  **Key Point**: Must use hot water for rinsing to prevent the meat from contracting due to cold water, which would affect its tenderness.

**3. Vegetable Pre-treatment**
- For potatoes, lotus root slices, kelp, and other vegetables, boil in a separate pot until cooked, then remove and set aside.

**4. Making a Simple Broth**
- In a clay pot, add about 3 liters of water, 800-1000g pork leg bone, ginger slices, and Huadiao wine. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 1.5 hours. If using a pressure cooker, cook for 40 minutes after reaching pressure.

**5. Preparing the Spice Packet**
- Assemble the spices according to the spice packet recipe, place them in a spice bag, and tie it tightly.

### II. Cooking Phase
**1. Stir-frying the Base**
- Pour more cooking oil than usual for stir-frying into the wok and heat over medium heat.
- Add ginger slices and scallion pieces, and stir-fry until fragrant.
- Add dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry for 15-20 seconds until the aroma is released.

**2. Stir-frying the Sauce for Flavor**
- Reduce to low heat, add broad bean paste or soybean paste, and stir-fry over medium heat for about 15 seconds until the fragrance of the paste is released.
- Then add the rock sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, MSG, and salt, and stir briefly to combine.

**3. Combining the Braising Liquid**
- Pour 2.5-3 liters of the prepared broth into the wok.
- Add the spice packet and dried red dates.
- Pour in the yellow wine or Huadiao wine, stir to combine, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 10 minutes to allow the spices to infuse.

**4. Braising the Meats**
- Add the blanched meats to the braising liquid.
- **For direct slow braising**: Simmer over low heat until tender.
- **For quick braising in a pressure cooker or rice cooker**: Transfer the braising liquid and meats to the inner pot of the pressure cooker or rice cooker and set the appropriate program.
  - Reference times for a pressure cooker: Pork trotters 18 minutes, pork belly 13 minutes, pork hock 25 minutes, beef shank 35 minutes, beef leg meat 30 minutes, duck heads 15 minutes, duck intestines 8 minutes, pork or beef intestines 18-25 minutes.
  **Note**: Times must be adjusted flexibly based on the size of the meat pieces and the power of your appliance.

**5. Marinating for Flavor**
- After braising is complete, do not remove the meat immediately. Let the meats soak in the braising liquid for at least several hours. Soaking overnight gives the best flavor.

**6. Final Processing**
- Wrap the braised beef tightly in plastic wrap while it's still hot, and refrigerate until firm for easier slicing.
- Drain the used spice packet thoroughly and it can be frozen and reused.
- The braising liquid can also be filtered and frozen for use as an "old marinade" for future braising.

## Health Advice
This braised meat is deliciously rich, but be mindful of its high salt and fat content. It is best enjoyed occasionally as a treat. People with high blood pressure or those on a weight loss diet should minimize their intake of the liquid. Serving it with blanched vegetables can help balance the nutrition to some extent.
