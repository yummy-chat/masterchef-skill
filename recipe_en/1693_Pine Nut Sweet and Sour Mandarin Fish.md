# Pine Nut Sweet and Sour Mandarin Fish  
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 mandarin fish (about 500g, pre-cleaned)  
🥜 A small amount of pine nuts (for garnish)  
🟢 A small amount of green peas (for garnish)  
🥚 1/2 whole egg  
🌾 Cornstarch (starch), as needed  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (for marinating)  
🍶 A little cooking wine  
🍅 Ketchup, as needed  
🍬 30g granulated sugar (for sweet and sour sauce)  
🍶 30g rice vinegar (approx.)  
🌾 Starch slurry, as needed (for thickening)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle  
🍽️ Serving plate

## Preparation Steps  
1. Scrape off any remaining scales from the fish surface and rinse thoroughly with clean water.  
2. Cut off the fish head just behind the gills at the neck, separating the head from the body.  
3. Using a flat knife, slice along the backbone to remove one fillet, keeping the knife close to the bone and cutting toward the tail without severing it; flip the fish and repeat on the other side. Remove the backbone, then trim away the rib bones from the belly.  
**Note**: When slicing, keep the blade tight against the bone to ensure the fillet remains intact and unbroken.  
4. Place the fillet skin-side down. First, make straight cuts into strips about the width of a chopstick, without cutting through the skin. Then rotate the fillet and make 45-degree diagonal cuts at the same width, again without cutting through the skin, to create a "lychee flower" scoring pattern.  
**Key**: This is the foundation of the pine nut shape. Cuts must be evenly spaced and must not cut through the skin.  
5. Rinse the scored fillet and fish head in clean water, then squeeze out excess moisture. Add salt, sugar, cooking wine, and half a beaten whole egg. Mix well and marinate for 5 minutes.  
6. Evenly coat the marinated fillet and fish head with dry cornstarch, ensuring every crevice is covered. Gently shake off excess starch.  
7. Heat oil in a wok to about 350°F (170°C). Holding the tail, first lower the fillet into the oil and fry for about 5 seconds to set the shape. Then continuously ladle hot oil over the fillet to ensure even cooking. At the same time, fry the fish head.  
**Note**: Home stoves have lower heat, so extend frying time as needed. Fry for 3–5 minutes total, until the fish is golden and crispy.  
8. In a separate pan, bring water to a boil and blanch the green peas until just cooked. Drain and set aside.  
9. For the sweet and sour sauce: In a wok, add some water, ketchup, 30g sugar, and a pinch of salt. Stir over heat until the sugar is fully dissolved. Once the sauce comes to a boil, add starch slurry and stir until thickened. Finally, add 30g rice vinegar, bring to a boil, and immediately turn off the heat.  
**Key**: Adding the vinegar at the end better preserves the sour flavor.  
10. Arrange the fried fish on a plate, pour the hot sweet and sour sauce over it, and garnish with pine nuts and green peas.

## Health Tips  
This mandarin fish dish is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it ideal for autumn nourishment, especially for office workers who use their brains frequently. Control the oil temperature when frying to avoid excessive oil absorption. Diabetics or those monitoring sugar intake should reduce the amount of sweet and sour sauce.
