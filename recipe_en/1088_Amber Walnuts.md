# Amber Walnuts
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Walnuts: about 300g (preferably whole)  
🧈 White sesame seeds: about a handful  
🧊 Rock sugar: 100g  
💧 Water: 70g  
🍯 Maltose: 40g  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔥 Oven (for pre-roasting walnuts)  
🍳 Wok (for making syrup and coating)  
📄 Baking sheet (lined with parchment paper)  
🥄 Wooden spatula  

## Cooking Steps
1. Spread the walnuts evenly on a baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven at 150°C for 15 minutes (top and bottom heat). Remove and let cool naturally.  
   **Key point**: Baking makes the walnuts more fragrant and crispy, and the skin can be easily rubbed off. If you dislike the bitterness, you can remove the skin.
2. Pour 70g of water into a wok, add 100g of rock sugar and 40g of maltose, and heat over medium-low heat.  
   **Note**: You can stir gently at the beginning to help dissolve, but do not stir continuously to prevent crystallization.
3. Observe the syrup: When large bubbles appear and the syrup gradually turns into a thick, dark brown color with dense bubbles, the syrup is ready.
4. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting, quickly add the pre-roasted walnuts, and stir rapidly to coat each walnut evenly with the syrup.
5. Sprinkle in a handful of white sesame seeds, stir briefly, then immediately remove from heat and transfer out.
6. Pour the coated walnuts onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. While still hot, use a tool to separate any walnuts that are stuck together, then let them cool naturally.  
   **Key point**: Separate them quickly before the syrup hardens. Once cooled, the sugar shell will be crispy and prevent clumping.

## Health Tips  
Amber walnuts are rich in unsaturated fatty acids and vitamin E, making them a suitable energy snack for mental workers, vegetarians, and children. However, the syrup is high in calories, so it is recommended to consume only a small handful at a time and not excessively.
