# Braised Chicken Legs with Potatoes and Vermicelli
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken legs, as needed (cut into pieces, blanched and set aside)
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (peeled, cut into rolling chunks)
🍝 Vermicelli, as needed (soaked in warm water until soft)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a tablespoon
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon
💧 Hot water, as needed (enough to cover ingredients)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Strainer

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour cooking oil into the wok, add rock sugar, and heat over low heat until the sugar melts and turns a reddish-brown color.
   **Key Point**: Stir slowly over low heat to prevent burning; stop once the sugar melts and turns reddish-brown.
2. Add the blanched chicken leg pieces, and stir-fry quickly over medium-low heat until the chicken surface is evenly coated with caramel color.
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry quickly over medium-low heat to ensure even coloring.
3. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and dried chili peppers, and continue stir-frying until the aromatics release a rich fragrance.
   **Key Point**: Control the heat to avoid scorching.
4. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and salt, and stir-fry evenly to fully incorporate the seasonings with the chicken.
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry until the seasonings evenly coat the chicken.
5. Pour in hot water until it is level with the ingredients, add the potato chunks, cover the wok, and simmer over medium-low heat for 10 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Using hot water helps maintain a stable temperature inside the wok.
6. Uncover the wok, add the softened vermicelli, cover again, and continue to simmer over medium-low heat for another 10 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Add the vermicelli later to prevent it from becoming overly soft.
7. Uncover the wok, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, and once the sauce thickens, it is ready to serve.
   **Key Point**: Reduce the sauce until it coats the ingredients; stir to control sticking.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced mix of protein, carbs, and vegetables, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and daily energy replenishment. Be mindful of the vermicelli and rock sugar intake; those with high blood sugar should consume in moderation.
