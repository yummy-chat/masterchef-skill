# Cola Fried Egg Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs About 4 pieces  
🥟 Dumpling wrappers As needed  
🫑 Red bell pepper As needed (diced)  
🟡 Yellow bell pepper As needed (diced)  
🧅 Purple onion As needed (diced)  
🌱 Scallions As needed (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic As needed (minced)  
🧅 Leek As needed (cut into sections)  
🧂 Salt A pinch (for frying eggs)  
🥤 Cola 2 cans  
🧂 Light soy sauce As needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce As needed  
🌾 Cornstarch slurry As needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Chinese wok  
🥄 Spatula  
✂️ Scissors  
🥢 Fork/chopsticks  
🍲 Pot for boiling  
🥣 Bowls  

## Cooking Steps
1. Dice the red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and purple onion into small even cubes, chop the scallions, mince the garlic, cut the leek into sections, and place each in separate bowls. Prepare enough dumpling wrappers.
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of oil, sprinkle in a pinch of salt, and crack in the eggs one at a time. Fry each side for about 30 seconds until golden brown, then remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Adding a small amount of oil and salt to the pan prevents sticking and adds a base flavor to the eggs.
3. Keep the remaining oil in the wok, add the minced garlic and leek sections, and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in 2 cans of cola, add light soy sauce and oyster sauce, then add the fried eggs. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer until the eggs are flavorful and coated in a glossy reddish sauce.
   **Note**: Using the leftover oil from frying the eggs enhances the aroma of the sauce.
4. Use scissors to cut the fried eggs in the wok into bite-sized pieces. Pour in the cornstarch slurry to thicken, then add the diced red and yellow bell peppers, diced purple onion, and chopped scallions. Mix well to form the filling.
   **Key point**: The cornstarch slurry thickens the filling to prevent it from falling apart during wrapping; add the vegetables last to retain their crisp texture.
5. Take a dumpling wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, seal the edges tightly to form a dumpling, and arrange them on a bamboo tray. Be careful not to overfill the dumplings to avoid bursting during cooking.
6. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the dumplings and gently stir with a spatula to prevent sticking. Cook until all dumplings float to the surface, the wrappers become translucent, and they feel springy when pressed, then remove promptly.
   **Key point**: Once the dumplings float, remove them quickly to avoid overcooking, which can cause the wrappers to break.

## Health Advice  
This dish uses cola as a substitute for sugar, which can somewhat reduce the intake of refined sugar, but it is still high in carbohydrates and calories. It is suitable for occasional indulgence rather than as a daily staple. The fried eggs provide high-quality protein, and pairing them with bell peppers and onions adds dietary fiber. It is recommended to serve with a portion of leafy greens to balance the nutrition.
