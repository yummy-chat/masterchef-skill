# Cold Pig Ear Salad
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐷 2 pig ears
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for deodorizing the braising liquid)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for deodorizing the braising liquid)
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (for lining the plate)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, as needed (sliced into rings)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (cut into sections)
🌿 Dried chili peppers, as needed (for aromatic oil)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (for aromatic oil)
⚪ White sesame seeds, as needed (for aromatic oil)
🌿 Star anise, as needed
🌿 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, about 2 tablespoons (1 tablespoon for braising, 1 tablespoon for dressing)
🍶 Shaoxing wine, as needed
🧂 Salt, about 15 grams
🫙 Dark soy sauce, as needed
🥫 Fermented soybean paste, about 1 tablespoon
🍬 Rock sugar, 40 grams
🍬 White sugar, as needed
🧂 MSG, as needed
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🌿 Ground Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🌶️ Homemade chili oil, about 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🍲 Stockpot
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Large basin (for mixing)
🍜 Bowl (for mixing sauce)
🧹 Small knife/brush (for cleaning pig ears)

## Steps
1. Clean the scorched parts on the surface of the pig ears. Use a small knife or brush to scrape and rinse thoroughly, focusing on removing any remaining hair inside the ear canal.
2. Add cold water to a pot, place the pig ears in it, bring to a boil over high heat, and continue boiling for 3 minutes to remove the blood. Remove the pig ears.
   **Note**: Always blanch in cold water.
3. Caramelize sugar: Add a little oil to the wok, add 40 grams of rock sugar, stir-fry over medium heat until melted, then reduce to the lowest heat and continue stirring until the syrup turns a date-red color with dense small bubbles. Immediately pour in boiling water.
   **Key**: When the syrup reaches a date-red color with dense small bubbles, add water immediately; a few seconds of delay will cause bitterness.
4. Prepare the braising liquid: Add ginger slices, scallion sections, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and Sichuan peppercorns to the caramelized sugar water. Then add 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, about 15 grams of salt, dark soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of fermented soybean paste. Stir to combine.
   **Key**: The saltiness of the braising liquid should be higher than that of everyday stir-fries for the ingredients to absorb the flavor; the soybean paste adds depth to the braised taste.
5. Place the blanched pig ears into the braising liquid. Add more water if needed to submerge the ingredients. Cover, simmer over low heat for 1 hour, then turn off the heat and let the pig ears soak in the liquid for another hour.
   **Key**: The soaking step is essential and critical for flavor absorption.
6. Remove the braised pig ears, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate until firm. Refrigeration hardens the texture, making it easier to slice thinly.
7. Take out the chilled pig ears. Trim off the overly thick, fatty base, then slice diagonally against the grain into thin slices. The thinner, the better.
8. Make the aromatic oil: Place Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, and white sesame seeds in a bowl. Pour one spoonful of boiling hot oil over them to release the aroma.
   **Note**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the fragrance.
9. Put the sliced pig ears into a large basin. Add minced garlic, bird's eye chili rings, cilantro sections, then add salt, white sugar, MSG, 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of fragrant vinegar, sesame oil, ground Sichuan peppercorns, and 1 tablespoon of homemade chili oil. Mix thoroughly.
10. Line the bottom of a plate with a layer of cucumber slices. Top with the dressed pig ears, and serve.

## Health Tips
Pig ears are rich in collagen but high in calories. Pairing with cucumber balances the texture. It is recommended to reduce salt and sugar; people with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
