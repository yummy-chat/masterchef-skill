# Coconut Taro Sago Dessert
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Taro 400g (peeled and washed, cut into 2cm cubes)
🌾 Sago appropriate amount
🥥 Coconut milk 100g
💧 Water 500g (for cooking taro) + 800g (for cooking syrup)
🧊 Ice cubes appropriate amount (for cooling sago)
🧂 Rock sugar 50g (added when cooking taro) + 150g (added when cooking syrup)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🍳 Cooking pot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Strainer
🫗 Glass bowl
📦 Plastic wrap

## Cooking Steps
### Cooking the Taro
1. Pour 500g of water into the pot, add 50g of rock sugar, and add the taro cubes.
**Key point**: Taro must be added to cold water.

2. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam from the surface to keep the liquid clear.
3. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes, until the taro is fully cooked and sandy in texture.
**Doneness check**: Taro becomes sticky with a sandy edge.

4. Pour the cooked taro along with the liquid into a bowl and set aside to cool.
### Cooking the Sago
5. Bring another pot of water to a boil, add the sago, and stir with a spoon to prevent sticking.
**Key point**: Sago must be added only after the water has boiled, otherwise it will dissolve.

6. Once the water boils again, reduce to the lowest heat and cook for 10 minutes.
7. Turn off the heat, pour the sago along with the hot water into a bowl containing cold boiled water or ice water, and let it sit for a moment.
**Key point**: Use residual heat to completely eliminate the white core in the center of the sago.

8. Remove the sago with a strainer, drain well, and set aside.
### Making the Coconut Sago Dessert
9. Add 800g of water to the pot, put in 150g of rock sugar, and add the drained sago.
10. Bring to a boil over high heat, stirring until the rock sugar is completely dissolved.
11. After the water boils and the sugar dissolves, pour in 100g of coconut milk.
12. Stir continuously with a spoon until the mixture comes to a boil again.
**Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent burning.

13. Pour out the cooked coconut sago dessert.
14. Mix the previously cooked and cooled taro with the coconut sago dessert until evenly combined.
15. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 4 hours.
**Key point**: Chilling enhances the flavor; this is the recommended way to enjoy this summer dessert.

16. Remove from the refrigerator and enjoy. The coconut syrup is sweet and smooth, the sago is chewy, and the taro is soft, powdery, and sandy.

## Health Advice
This dessert is rich in dietary fiber and quality carbohydrates. Taro provides potassium and vitamins, making it suitable for replenishing energy in summer. Diabetic patients should reduce the amount of rock sugar used. Those with sensitive stomachs should avoid consuming it excessively cold.
