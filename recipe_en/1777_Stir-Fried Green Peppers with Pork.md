# Stir-Fried Green Peppers with Pork
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Green peppers, appropriate amount (choose thick-fleshed varieties)
🥩 Pork, appropriate amount (preferably pork shoulder, or use pork belly or lean meat)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (smashed and sliced)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)
🧄 Garlic, a large amount (smashed and cut into large chunks; the chunks should be large to withstand stir-frying)
🧂 Salt, about 3.5 grams
🥢 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (can be generous)
💧 Water starch, a small amount (for a thin slurry, optional to add before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔥 Wok
♨️ Gas stove
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon (for draining green peppers)
🥣 Bowls (for holding prepared ingredients)
🥄 Wooden spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the green peppers**: Under running water, use your fingers to push the stem of each whole green pepper inward, remove the inner seeds and core, wash, then tear into pieces. Remove the white membrane inside, drain, and set aside.
   - Key point: Removing seeds and membrane improves texture and cleanliness.
2. **Prepare the meat and aromatics**: Slice the pork shoulder. Smash the ginger with the flat side of a knife, then slice it, and place it in a bowl with the pork slices. Smash the garlic and cut into large chunks. Chop the scallions.
   - Key point: Smashing the ginger helps release its flavor more easily; placing it with the meat ensures they go into the wok together.
3. **Thoroughly heat the wok**: Heat the wok over high heat until it is fully hot. Turn to low heat, pour in a small amount of oil to coat the wok, then pour out the hot oil. Wait a moment for the wok to fully heat through and reach an even temperature.
   - Key point: Home stoves have low heat, so the wok must be thoroughly heated to store enough heat, preventing a sudden temperature drop when ingredients are added.
4. **Stir-fry the pork slices**: Reheat over medium heat, leaving a thin layer of oil in the wok. Add the pork slices and ginger slices. Spread and stir-fry them in a small area directly over the flame.
   - State check: Stir-fry until the pork changes color and the surface is lightly browned.
      - Key point: Keep the stirring range small to maintain the ingredients in the high-temperature zone, preventing the wok from cooling down.
5. **Add scallions**: Once the pork changes color, add the chopped scallions. Briefly stir-fry until fragrant.
6. **Add green peppers**: Add the green pepper pieces and stir-fry over medium heat. Do not stir too frequently; allow the peppers to heat up and release moisture in the wok.
   - Key point: Stirring too quickly or using a wok that isn't hot enough will cause the vegetables to release too much water, resulting in a "boiled" dish.
7. **Listen for the sound**: Continue stir-frying until the sound in the wok changes from a watery "sizzle" to a crisp, dry "rustling" sound of quick stir-frying.
   - State check: A crisp sound indicates that the surface moisture of the vegetables has evaporated.
8. **Season**: Immediately add salt and light soy sauce. Stir-fry quickly and evenly to let the soy sauce develop a fragrant wok hei (breath of the wok).
9. **Add garlic**: Add the large smashed garlic chunks. Stir-fry quickly a few times to preserve the garlic's aroma.
10. **Thickening (optional)**: If you are worried about the dish releasing liquid, drizzle a thin stream of water starch along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry rapidly and evenly to coat the ingredients with a thin slurry.
    - Key point: The slurry must be very thin. The goal is to "stealth-thicken" to lock in flavor and moisture, not to create a thick, heavy sauce.
11. **Serve**: Once evenly mixed, remove from the wok and plate immediately.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein and vitamin C, making it suitable for those needing to supplement iron, build muscle, or boost immunity. Pay attention to controlling the amount of soy sauce and salt; individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the seasonings.
