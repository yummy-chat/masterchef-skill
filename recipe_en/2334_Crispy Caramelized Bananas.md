# Crispy Caramelized Bananas
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍌 Bananas 2-3 (cut into approximately 2-3 cm pieces)
🌽 Cornstarch about 1 tbsp (for coating banana pieces)
🥣 Batter appropriate amount (white paste, for coating)
🫘 White sesame seeds appropriate amount (for garnish)
🍬 White sugar 2 parts
💧 Water 1 part (sugar to water ratio 2:1)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for frying) + about 1 tsp (for bottom oil in syrup) + a small amount (for greasing the plate to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Flat plate (pre-greased with oil)
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. **Prepare Bananas**: Peel the bananas and cut into approximately 2-3 cm long pieces. Place in a bowl.
2. **Coat with Dry Starch**: Add one tablespoon of cornstarch to the banana pieces. Gently toss and mix until each piece is thinly coated with starch.
3. **Prepare Batter**: Prepare a bowl of white batter and set aside.
4. **Prepare Plate**: Take a flat plate and evenly spread a layer of cooking oil on the bottom to prevent sticking.
5. **First Fry with Batter**: Pour ample oil into the wok and heat to about 160°C. Dip each starch-coated banana piece into the batter to coat evenly, then gently place into the oil. Quickly turn them after adding to prevent "tails" from forming. Fry until the bananas float and the surface is set. Remove and drain oil.
6. **Second Fry**: Increase the oil temperature to about 180°C. Add the first-fried bananas back into the oil and fry for about 1-2 minutes until the shell hardens and turns a light golden yellow. Remove and drain oil.
7. **Third Fry**: While preparing the syrup, increase the oil temperature again. Add the bananas for a third fry, about 30 seconds to 1 minute, to make them hot and crispy. This is one of the key steps for successful caramel pulling.
8. **Make the Syrup**: Use a clean wok. Add one part water, two parts white sugar, and a small spoonful of base oil. Heat over medium-low heat.
   - **Temperature Judgment**: The sugar water will boil with large bubbles → as the water evaporates, the bubbles become smaller and denser → the color will begin to turn slightly yellow.
   - When the syrup turns the color of sesame oil and can be pulled into thin threads with a spoon, the heat is just right.
9. **Coat and Plate**: Once the syrup is ready, immediately add the third-fried hot bananas into the wok. Quickly toss or stir-fry to evenly coat each banana piece with syrup.
   - **Note**: Work quickly; operating off the heat is safer. Then transfer the coated bananas to the pre-greased plate and immediately sprinkle with white sesame seeds.
10. **Serve Immediately**: Crispy caramelized bananas must be eaten hot. Once cooled, the sugar will harden and cannot be pulled into threads.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in sugar and calories, suitable for occasional consumption, not for frequent eating. It should be approached with caution, especially for those with high blood sugar or those on a weight loss plan. It must be eaten hot to achieve the caramel pull; be careful to avoid burns. The texture is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
