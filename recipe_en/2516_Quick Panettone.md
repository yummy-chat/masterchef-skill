# Quick Panettone
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Whole eggs 2 (about 107g)
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (about 36g)
💧 Water 180g
🌾 Bread flour 400g
🧈 Unsalted butter 160g (softened)
🍇 Macerated dried fruit 170g (mix of raisins, candied orange peel, dried apricots, etc., soaked overnight in rum, drained and patted dry before use)
🍬 Granulated sugar 48g
🧂 Salt 4g
🍄 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerance) 4g
🥃 Rum as needed (for soaking dried fruit)
🌰 Almond flour 32g
🍬 Powdered sugar 24g (for almond crust, plus extra for dusting)
🌾 Cake flour 8g
🥚 Egg whites 40g (for almond crust)
🌰 Sliced almonds as needed

## Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large mixing bowl
🥄 Whisk
🔪 Bench scraper
🧊 Work surface (oil-free and water-free)
🫙 Plastic wrap
📦 Square metal baking pan
🌡️ Thermometer
🔥 Oven (convection or conventional)
🧁 4-inch panettone paper molds
🎂 Piping bag
❄️ Refrigerator (fridge/freezer)
🥢 Skewer

## Instructions
1. Soak the mixed dried fruit in rum overnight. Before use, drain and pat dry with paper towels to remove excess liquid. Set aside.
2. In a bowl, combine whole eggs, egg yolks, water, granulated sugar, and instant dry yeast. Whisk thoroughly until well mixed and the yeast is fully dissolved without lumps.
3. On the work surface, mound the bread flour into a well. Pour in the liquid mixture. Using a bench scraper, gradually incorporate the flour from the outside inward until a rough, shaggy dough forms, then knead into a sticky mass.
4. Using the bench scraper, repeatedly pick up the dough, slap it down, and fold it over until it holds together and can form a thick membrane. Cover with a bowl and rest for 10 minutes.
5. Wet your hands slightly with water to prevent sticking, and continue slapping and kneading the dough for about 5–8 minutes until the surface is smooth. **Key point**: Monitor the dough temperature throughout. If it exceeds 24°C, immediately transfer the dough to a greased square pan, cover with plastic wrap, and freeze at -20°C for 10 minutes to cool.
6. After cooling, spread the dough flat. Evenly distribute the softened butter and salt. Use folding, pressing, and cutting motions to fully incorporate the butter. The dough will become very wet and sticky initially; persist with the bench scraper to assist cutting and mixing until the butter is completely absorbed.
7. Continue slapping and kneading the dough for about 5 minutes until a thin, strong windowpane membrane forms with smooth edges, and the dough temperature remains between 20–24°C.
8. Round the dough and place it in a container. Choose a resting method based on dough temperature: if below 20°C, rest at room temperature for 30 minutes; if above 20°C, refrigerate for 30 minutes. After resting, if the dough is not firm enough, perform one fold to strengthen it, then round again.
9. Cover the rounded dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 3–8 hours until it has visibly risen and the surface is smooth and not sticky.
10. Remove the fermented dough. Gently pat it out into a rectangular sheet of even thickness. Evenly scatter the macerated dried fruit over the surface. Fold left and right sides inward, then fold top to bottom to enclose the fruit. Finally, round the dough.
11. Divide the dough into portions of about 270g each. Gently round each portion without over-degassing, and place into 4-inch paper molds.
12. Perform the final proofing in a warm, humid place until the dough rises to near the edge of the paper mold. It should spring back slowly when gently pressed, without collapsing.
13. While proofing, preheat the oven and prepare the almond crust: mix almond flour, powdered sugar, and cake flour together. Add the egg whites and stir until smooth and lump-free. Transfer to a piping bag.
14. Pipe the almond crust in a spiral on top of each proofed dough. Sprinkle with sliced almonds and powdered sugar. Bake: in a convection oven at 170°C for about 20 minutes; in a conventional oven at 180°C top heat / 200°C bottom heat for about 25 minutes.
15. Once baked, if the bread has risen high above the mold, immediately insert a skewer from the bottom and hang it upside down on a wire rack to cool completely, preventing shrinkage. **Key point**: Hanging upside down helps maintain the vertical structure of the air pockets, preventing collapse under its own weight.

## Health Advice
Panettone is high in butter and sugar, making it suitable for holiday sharing. It is not recommended for daily overconsumption. The dried fruit, enhanced by rum soaking, offers upgraded flavor, but those monitoring sugar intake should enjoy it in moderation.
