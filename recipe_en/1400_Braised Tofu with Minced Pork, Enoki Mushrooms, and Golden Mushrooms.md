# Braised Tofu with Minced Pork, Enoki Mushrooms, and Golden Mushrooms
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Firm Tofu 1 block (North tofu, needs to be fried in advance)
🥩 Minced Pork appropriate amount (about 1 small bowl)
🧅 Purple Onion appropriate amount (shredded for the base)
🍄 Enoki Mushrooms appropriate amount (for the base)
🧅 Scallion a small amount (chopped for garnish)
🌶️ Red Bell Pepper a small amount (diced for garnish)
🌟 Star Anise 1 piece
🌶️ Pixian Broad Bean Paste about 1 tablespoon
🍶 Huadiao Wine (or cooking wine) appropriate amount
🧂 Light Soy Sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce a small amount
🍬 White Sugar a pinch
🧂 MSG (or chicken powder) a pinch
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (slightly more than usual)
🧄 Minced Ginger, Garlic, and Scallion appropriate amount

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔥 Gas Stove
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🥄 Wok Spatula or Ladle
🍲 Clay Pot (or casserole)
🔪 Chef's Knife
🪓 Cutting Board
🫖 Kettle

## Steps
1. Place the whole block of tofu into a slotted spoon and carefully slide it into a wok with oil heated over medium heat for frying.
**Key Point**: Must use medium heat; high heat will burn the tofu. During frying, gently push the tofu with a spatula to prevent sticking to the bottom.
2. Fry for about 3 minutes, until the tofu surface forms small bubbles and turns golden brown. Remove with a slotted spoon, drain the oil, and let it cool.
**Key Point**: The tofu must cool before cutting, making it less likely to break and better able to absorb the flavors.
3. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add the star anise and fry until fragrant, the color darkens, and it is slightly singed.
**Key Point**: Use low heat to coax out the aroma of the star anise, avoiding burning it which creates bitterness.
4. Add the minced pork. Stir-fry over low heat until the fat renders out from the meat.
**Key Point**: Must use low heat for this step; the goal is to render out the fat from the minced pork itself.
5. Add the minced ginger, garlic, and scallion and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the Pixian broad bean paste and stir-fry until the red oil releases.
**Key Point**: The bean paste must be fried thoroughly, otherwise, it will have a raw taste and affect the final color of the dish.
6. Splash the Huadiao wine along the edge of the wok to remove any gamey smell and enhance the aroma. Then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, a pinch of sugar, MSG, and a little more salt than you would typically use for stir-frying. Stir-fry evenly to combine.
**Key Point**: Use more salt because the tofu and water added later will need to absorb the seasoning.
7. Remove the cooked minced pork sauce and set aside in a bowl.
8. Once the fried tofu has cooled, cut it into slices about the thickness of three stacked coins.
9. Layer the bottom of a clay pot (casserole) first with shredded onion, then with enoki mushrooms. Arrange the sliced tofu flat on top of the vegetables.
10. Evenly pour the prepared minced pork sauce over the tofu slices.
11. Add boiling water to the clay pot until the liquid level just reaches the top of the tofu (barely submerging it).
12. Cover the pot with its lid and simmer over low heat for 15 minutes.
13. After braising, remove the lid and garnish with chopped scallions and diced red bell pepper. Serve directly from the pot.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein, with tofu providing plant-based protein and minced pork supplementing animal protein, making it suitable for those needing nutritional supplementation. Note that the amounts of broad bean paste and salt are relatively high; patients with high blood pressure should consider reducing the seasoning additions.
