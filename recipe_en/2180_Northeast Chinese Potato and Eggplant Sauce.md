# Northeast Chinese Potato and Eggplant Sauce
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 Long eggplant about 4 pieces (tear into irregular small chunks by hand)  
🥔 Potato 1 large (peeled, cut or tear into small chunks)  
🌶️ Green bell pepper about 4 pieces (2 cut into rolling chunks, the rest finely diced and set aside)  
🧄 Garlic 1 head (most minced for stir-frying, a small portion reserved for adding at the end)  
🧅 Scallion half (chopped into scallion pieces)  
🌿 Cilantro to taste (chopped, added when finishing)  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder to taste (optional)  
🫘 Soybean paste 4 tablespoons  
🍶 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce one swirl (about 1 tablespoon)  
🧂 MSG to taste (optional)  
🫒 Cooking oil slightly more than usual for stir-frying  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥣 Large bowl (for holding prepared vegetables)  
🥄 Wooden spatula or wok spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. Tear the long eggplant into irregular small chunks by hand; peel the potato and cut or tear it into small chunks; cut 2 green bell peppers into rolling chunks. Place all prepared vegetables together in a large bowl and set aside.
**Note**: Kitchen beginners can use a knife to cut the potato to avoid hand injury.

2. Mince most of the garlic into garlic pieces, chop the scallion into scallion pieces; dice the remaining green bell peppers finely, mince the remaining garlic into garlic pieces, chop the cilantro. Mix all the minced and diced ingredients together in a small plate, to be used as the "soul" finishing garnish when serving.
3. Pour slightly more cooking oil than usual for stir-frying into the wok. Heat the oil until hot, then add the scallion pieces and some of the garlic pieces, stir-frying until fragrant.
4. If using thirteen-spice powder, add it at this point and stir-fry until aromatic.
5. Pour the eggplant chunks, potato chunks, and rolling-chunk green bell peppers together into the wok. Quickly stir-fry a few times to evenly heat the ingredients.
6. Add the soybean paste, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Continue stir-frying evenly until a rich sauce aroma develops.
7. Add water, just enough to barely submerge the vegetables. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer slowly for about 20 minutes.
8. When there is still plenty of liquid left in the wok, use the wok spatula to mash and crush the potato chunks and eggplant chunks, turning the mixture into a thick paste.
**Key**: Be sure to mash the ingredients before the liquid dries up; otherwise, they may burn easily.

9. Depending on the thickness of the sauce, reduce it as needed to reach the desired consistency. If you want to enhance the umami, you can add a little MSG.
10. Before turning off the heat, pour the reserved garlic pieces, cilantro pieces, and diced green bell peppers into the wok. Quickly stir evenly, then remove from heat and plate.
11. The finished dish is a dark brown paste with green garnishes on the surface. It has a sticky and soft texture and tastes especially good when served with rice.

## Health Advice  
This Northeast Chinese Potato and Eggplant Sauce is rich in dietary fiber and potassium, which help control blood pressure and promote digestion. However, soybean paste and oyster sauce are high in sodium; therefore, patients with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of sauce they mix with their rice.
