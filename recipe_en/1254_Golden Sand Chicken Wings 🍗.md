# Golden Sand Chicken Wings 🍗
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken mid-joint wings 10 pieces (approx. 360-380g, soaked to remove blood)
🧄 Garlic 35g (about 7 cloves, made into garlic water)
💧 Water 280g (for making garlic water)
🥚 Raw salted egg yolks 4 pieces
🥚 Egg 1 piece (yolk only, steamed together with salted egg yolks)
🧀 Custard powder 10g (optional, for fragrance and color)
🌽 Cornstarch 20g (about 2 tablespoons; use 3 tablespoons if not using custard powder)
🧈 Butter 30g
🧂 Salt 3g (about 1 level teaspoon)
🥡 Light soy sauce 30g (about 3 tablespoons)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g (about 1 level teaspoon)
🍶 Cooking wine 5g (about half a tablespoon)
🍬 Sugar 5g (about 1 teaspoon)

## Cookware & Equipment
🪣 Water basin
🌀 Meat grinder or food processor
🕳️ Strainer
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
♨️ Steamer
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Frying pan (optional, for pan-frying)
🥣 Small bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the chicken wings in clean water for about ten minutes, then use your hands to squeeze the thinner bone of each wing from bottom to top to force out internal blood clots. Rinse thoroughly under running water. After processing, squeeze dry with your hands.
**Key point**: Thoroughly removing blood is the key to eliminating fishy odor; otherwise, the finished dish will have an off-flavor and develop dark spots.

2. Use a toothpick to poke many small holes on both sides of each chicken wing. Pour the garlic water over the chicken wings, add salt, light soy sauce, chicken bouillon, and cooking wine. Use your hands to mix well until the seasonings dissolve, then marinate for at least half an hour.
**Key point**: Use only the garlic water, not the garlic solids; cooking wine quantity should be moderate; marinating time must be sufficient.

3. Place 4 raw salted egg yolks and 1 whole raw egg in the steamer. Steam over cold water, starting the timer when steam begins to rise, and cook for 10 minutes. Remove the egg, cool in cold water, and peel. Only take the yolk, and together with the salted egg yolks, use the side of a knife to crush them finely. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
**Key point**: Raw salted egg yolks are more fragrant than cooked ones; mixing chicken egg yolk with duck egg yolk enhances color and richness; be sure to remove the thin membrane from the egg yolks.

4. Mix 10g custard powder and 20g cornstarch evenly.

5. Squeeze out excess marinade from the marinated chicken wings. Place them in the mixed powder, pressing the powder onto both sides, including the bone ends. After the first coat, let them rest briefly for the powder to absorb moisture, then apply a second coat, pressing firmly. Finally, shake off any excess dry powder.
**Key point**: Leaving a little moisture on the wing surface helps the powder adhere; apply two coats and press firmly; do not omit the bone ends.

6. Pour sufficient oil into the wok and heat over high heat to about 160°C (320°F). Add the coated chicken wings one by one. Do not stir them immediately after adding; wait until the coating sets, then gently separate them with chopsticks. Immediately turn to medium-low heat and fry for 5 minutes until all wings float. Once floating, turn heat to maximum for 1 minute for a final crisp, then remove and drain oil.
**Temperature guide**: 160°C to start → medium-low heat for 5 minutes → maximum heat for 1 minute.
   **Key point**: Do not move the wings initially; fry at medium-low heat until cooked through, then fry at high heat to expel oil and enhance crispiness.

7. Using residual heat, place 30g butter in a separate pan and melt over the lowest heat. Add the crushed salted egg yolk mixture and stir-fry patiently over the lowest heat. When the yolks bubble up into fine, dense foam, add 5g sugar and stir well. Immediately add the fried chicken wings and toss quickly until each wing is evenly coated with the golden sand sauce. Serve immediately.
**Key point**: Maintain the lowest heat throughout to prevent the egg yolks from burning and turning bitter; butter enhances milky aroma; toss quickly.

## Health Advice
This Golden Sand Chicken Wings dish is rich in protein and the fat-soluble nutrients from salted egg yolks. It is suitable for muscle-building individuals and flavor enthusiasts, but it is relatively high in calories. Pair with vegetables for a balanced diet. Due to the use of butter and deep-frying, those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should consume in moderation.
