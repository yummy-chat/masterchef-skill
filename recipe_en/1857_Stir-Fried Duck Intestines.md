# Stir-Fried Duck Intestines
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck intestines 1000g (fresh, thoroughly cleaned)  
🧄 Garlic approx. 20g (crushed and minced)  
🌶️ Red pickled chilies 20g (finely chopped)  
🫚 Old ginger 20g (crushed and minced)  
🌶️ Thai bird’s eye chilies to taste (chopped, omit if not spicy)  
🌶️ Erjingtiao chilies to taste (chopped)  
🫚 Young ginger a small piece (julienned)  
🥬 Celery a few stalks (cut into small sections)  
🧅 Spring onion bulbs a few (cut into small sections)  
🧂 Edible salt approx. 50g (for washing) + 1g (pre-seasoning)  
🧂 Baking soda approx. 2g (for washing)  
🌾 Dry starch approx. 60g (for washing)  
🍶 Cooking wine to taste (for washing and blanching) + 5g (for stir-frying)  
🌿 Ground white pepper approx. 0.5g + to taste (seasoning)  
🍬 White sugar a pinch + to taste (approx. 1g, for enhancing freshness)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 5g  
🍇 Aged vinegar approx. 1g  
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 4g  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1g  
🥄 Water starch to taste (for thin slurry)  
🫒 Clarified oil (or sesame oil) a little  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍲 Large pot (for blanching)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Stir-fry spatula  
🍳 Chinese wok (stir-fry pan)  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Duck Intestines**  
   - Scrape off the yellow fat and impurities from the surface of the intestines with a knife. Place them in a bowl, add approx. 50g salt, 2g baking soda, 60g dry starch, and an appropriate amount of cooking wine. Rub and mix with your hands for 5 minutes.  
**Key**: Use friction to remove slime and fishy smell; baking soda helps keep the intestines tender and crispy.  
   - Rinse the duck intestines repeatedly with clean water until the water runs clear. Remove and squeeze out excess moisture.  
- Straighten each intestine, arrange neatly, and cut into sections approx. 15cm long.  
2. **Prepare Aromatics and Pre-Seasoning**  
   - Finely chop the garlic, red pickled chilies, old ginger, Thai bird’s eye chilies, and Erjingtiao chilies separately; julienne the young ginger, cut celery into sections, and cut spring onion bulbs into sections.  
- In a bowl, place the julienned young ginger, celery sections, and spring onion bulb sections. Add 1g salt, approx. 0.5g ground white pepper, a pinch of white sugar, 5g light soy sauce, and approx. 1g aged vinegar. Mix well and set aside.  
3. **Blanch the Duck Intestines**  
   - Bring water to a boil in a pot, add an appropriate amount of cooking wine, then add the duck intestine sections. Blanch on high heat for about 10 seconds until the intestines curl and turn white, about 60% cooked. Immediately remove and drain.  
**Key**: Blanching time must be extremely short, otherwise the intestines will become tough and lose their crispness.  
4. **Sear and Stir-Fry**  
   - Heat the wok, add oil to coat the surface, then pour out the hot oil. Add fresh base oil and heat until it reaches about 60% hot.  
- Add minced ginger and garlic, and chopped red pickled chilies. Stir-fry until fragrant. Add 4g doubanjiang, cook on low heat until the oil turns red.  
- Add the blanched duck intestines and the pre-seasoned aromatics bowl. Turn to high heat and stir-fry quickly to combine evenly.  
- Add additional white sugar and ground white pepper to taste. Drizzle 5g cooking wine along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry.  
- Add 1g dark soy sauce for color. Pour in water starch to create a thin slurry, stirring evenly.  
- Before serving, drizzle a little clarified oil or sesame oil, toss well, and plate.  
**Key**: Use high heat throughout for quick stir-frying to ensure the duck intestines remain tender and crispy.  

## Health Advice
Duck intestines are rich in protein but relatively high in cholesterol, making them suitable for occasional consumption by fitness enthusiasts. Adding baking soda during blanching helps maintain a crispy texture, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables to balance dietary fiber.
