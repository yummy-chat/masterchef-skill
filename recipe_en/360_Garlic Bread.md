# Garlic Bread
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 Active dry yeast 2g  
💧 Room temperature water 200g  
🌾 Bread flour 200g  
🌾 Durum flour 100g (can substitute with whole wheat flour or bread flour)  
🍬 Sugar 12g  
🧂 Salt 2g (for dough)  
🧂 Salt 4g (for garlic filling)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 24g  
🧄 Garlic 1 whole head  
🧅 Scallion white part 1 stalk  
🧈 Unsalted butter 50g  
🥚 Egg wash as needed (for brushing surface)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍳 Skillet  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥘 Cast iron pot (or baking sheet)  
🔥 Oven  
📄 Parchment paper  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🖌️ Brush  

## Instructions  
1. Dissolve active dry yeast in 200g room temperature water, stirring until fully dissolved.  
2. In a mixing bowl, combine bread flour, durum flour, sugar, 2g salt, and vegetable oil. Pour in the yeast water and stir until no dry flour remains. Do not over-knead at this stage.  
3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 20 minutes. Then knead for about 1 minute until the dough surface is noticeably smooth.  
4. Lightly oil the dough surface, cover with a lid or plastic wrap, and place in a warm area for the first fermentation.  
   **Note**: Oiling prevents the dough surface from drying out.  
   Ferment for about 1.5–2 hours until the dough doubles in volume.  
5. Prepare the garlic filling: Finely chop the whole head of garlic and mince the white part of the scallion. Place unsalted butter in a skillet and melt over the lowest heat. Add the minced garlic, scallion, and 4g salt. Sauté for about 5 minutes until fragrant, then remove and let cool completely.  
   **Note**: Use the lowest heat throughout to prevent burning the garlic. Ensure the filling is completely cooled before use.  
6. Deflate the fermented dough, divide evenly into 4 portions, and roll each into a ball. Lightly dust with flour to prevent sticking, then use a rolling pin to roll each into a round disc of similar diameter.  
7. Assemble on parchment paper: Place one dough disc, spread an even layer of the cooled garlic filling. Top with a second dough disc, spread filling again. Repeat for all 4 layers, leaving the top layer unfilled.  
8. Shaping: Use a round tool to gently press an imprint in the center of the dough as a safety line. Cut the dough into 8 equal wedges using a knife, first making a cross, then dividing further. On each "petal," make one lengthwise cut down the middle. Working with both sides of the cut, grasp and twist outward twice, pinch the ends together, and tuck them underneath. Arrange into a flower shape.  
   **Note**: The center imprint is a safety line; do not cut through it, or the petals will separate.  
9. Cover the shaped bread with plastic wrap for the final fermentation. Let it rise for about 45 minutes to 1 hour until visibly larger and light to the touch.  
10. Brush the fermented bread surface evenly with egg wash.  
11. Preheat the oven to 205°C. Transfer the bread with parchment paper to a baking sheet or cast iron pot. Bake in the middle rack for 25–28 minutes until golden brown on the surface and the exposed garlic filling turns golden.  
    **Note**: Adjust baking time based on your oven; aim for a golden surface.  

## Health Suggestions  
This garlic bread is rich in carbohydrates and butter, making it suitable for post-exercise energy replenishment. However, the spiciness of garlic may irritate the stomach, so pair it with a vegetable salad to balance the greasiness.
