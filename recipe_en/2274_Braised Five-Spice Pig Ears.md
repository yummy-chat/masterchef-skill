# Braised Five-Spice Pig Ears
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐷 Pig ears 2-3 pieces (singe off any remaining hairs, clean thoroughly)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections, used for frying the braising oil)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced, used for frying the braising oil)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed (pre-soaked in water)  
🦴 Broth or bone stock 6 kg (used to prepare the braising liquid)  

### Spice Packet  
🌿 Star anise 10g  
🍂 Bay leaves 5g  
🌱 Kaempferia galanga 5g  
🌸 Cloves 1g  
⭐ Cardamom 4g (crack open)  
🌾 Cumin seeds 3g  
🌿 Licorice 3g  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 5g  
🥜 Nutmeg 2g  
🌰 Amomum villosum 8g  
🌳 Cinnamon bark 5g  

### Braising Liquid Seasoning  
🫘 Soybean oil 300g  
🥓 Lard 100g  
🟤 Caramel color, as needed (prepared and set aside)  
🟤 Dark soy sauce, as needed (to adjust color)  
🧂 Salt 150g  
🧂 Chicken powder 20g  

### Cold Toss Seasoning  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🍶 Vinegar, a pinch  
🧂 Light soy sauce, a pinch  
🧄 Garlic water, as needed  
🌶️ Chili oil, two spoonfuls  
🧅 Shredded scallions, as needed  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for garnish)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Large pot (for blanching and braising)  
🍳 Wok (for preparing the braising oil)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing ingredients)  
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and soaking)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (to hold braising oil and seasonings)  
🪢 Cloth bag (for the spice packet)  
🥘 Food weight or sieve (to keep pig ears submerged)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for slicing and prepping)  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Pig Ears**: Hold the pig ears over an open flame to singe off any remaining hairs, then scrub thoroughly with clean water to remove all impurities and odor.
   **Note**: Ensure they are completely clean with no burnt marks left on the surface.

2. **Blanching**: Place the cleaned pig ears into a pot of cold water and set it over heat.
   **Key Point**: Start with cold water so that the blood is gradually released.
   - After the water boils, continue cooking for 2–3 minutes; a large amount of foam will rise.
   - Stir constantly during blanching to ensure even heating.
   - Immediately transfer the ears to cold water for a second wash, then drain and set aside.

3. **Make the Spice Packet**: Weigh out all spices according to the recipe, and crack the cardamom. Place the spices in a cloth bag and soak in clean water for 5 minutes to remove dust and strong scents. Drain.

4. **Prepare the Braising Oil**: Heat 300g soybean oil and 100g lard in a wok over low heat until the lard melts completely. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, and pre-soaked dried chili peppers, and fry slowly over low heat.
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat low and stir constantly to prevent burning. When the scallions and ginger turn yellow and the dried chilies release their fragrance, immediately remove all the fried solids.

5. **Handle the Fried Solids**: Let the fried scallions, ginger, and dried chilies cool, then stuff them into the spice packet. Tighten the bag. The oil left in the wok is the braising oil; pour it into a bowl and set aside.

6. **Prepare the Braising Liquid**: Bring 6 kg of broth to a boil. Add the prepared caramel color and stir well until the liquid turns a reddish-brown. Adjust the color with a bit of dark soy sauce if needed. Add 150g salt and 20g chicken powder, and stir to combine.
   **Key Point**: The braising liquid should be slightly salty so that the pig ears absorb the flavor fully.

7. **Begin Braising**: Place the blanched pig ears into the braising liquid. Add the assembled spice packet, and finally pour in the prepared braising oil.
   **Key Point**: Use a weight or sieve to keep the pig ears and spice packet submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and braise for 15 minutes.

8. **Soak for Flavor**: After turning off the heat, do not remove the pig ears. Let them soak in the braising liquid for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate deeply.

9. **Remove and Prepare**: After soaking, take out the pig ears. Brush a layer of the oil from the surface of the braising liquid over the ears to prevent them from drying out. Allow them to cool naturally at room temperature.

10. **Slice**: Place the cooled pig ears on a cutting board and slice them as thinly as possible.

11. **Toss with Seasonings**: Put the sliced ears into a bowl. Add shredded scallions, a pinch of salt, vinegar, light soy sauce, garlic water, and two spoonfuls of chili oil. Toss until well combined. Plate and garnish with small scallion pieces and cilantro.

## Health Tips  
Braised five-spice pig ears are rich in collagen and make an excellent appetizer for drinks. The braising process uses a significant amount of salt, so those with high blood pressure should consume the braising liquid sparingly. For the cold toss, consider adding shredded vegetables for a more balanced meal, and be mindful of the garlic water quantity to avoid irritating the stomach and intestines.
