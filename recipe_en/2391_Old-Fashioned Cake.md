# Old-Fashioned Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 6 eggs (about 270g with shells, or 5 regular eggs)
🍬 100g white sugar
🌾 120g cake flour
🌱 White sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration and aroma)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass mixing bowl (grease-free and water-free)
🔌 Electric hand mixer
🧁 12-cup muffin/cake pan
🥄 Baking spoon (for scooping batter and unmolding)
🧤 Oven mitts
🔥 Oven

## Instructions
1. Crack the eggs into a grease-free and water-free mixing bowl, and add 100g white sugar.
2. Beat with an electric hand mixer on high speed for about 5 minutes, until the egg mixture turns pale white and triples in volume. When lifting the mixer, the drips should leave a ribbon-like trail that doesn't disappear quickly.
3. Sift 120g cake flour into the beaten egg mixture. Mix on low speed with the electric hand mixer for about 10 seconds, until no dry flour remains and the batter is smooth and thick. Do not overmix to avoid deflating.
4. Pour the cake batter into a 12-cup muffin pan, filling each cup about 90% full. If the pan is prone to sticking, brush a thin layer of oil beforehand.
5. Evenly sprinkle white sesame seeds over the surface of the batter.
6. Preheat the oven to 170°C. Place the pan on the middle rack and bake at 170°C with top and bottom heat for 25 minutes.
   **Note**: Watch closely during the last few minutes of baking to prevent the surface from browning too much or burning.
7. After baking, wear oven mitts to remove the pan. While still warm, use a spoon to gently pry the edges of the cakes to unmold.

## Health Advice
This old-fashioned cake is high in sugar and carbohydrates, making it suitable for a quick energy boost after exercise, or as an afternoon snack paired with unsweetened tea. Those sensitive to blood sugar should have only a small amount and avoid eating on an empty stomach.
