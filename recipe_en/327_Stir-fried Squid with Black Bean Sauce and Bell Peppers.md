## Cooking Steps
1. Peel off the outer skin of the squid, remove the innards and eyes. Score the inside with diagonal cuts, then cut into pieces. Rinse and drain well.
2. In a small bowl, mix oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, chicken powder, sugar, and sesame oil. Stir to combine and set aside.
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the squid pieces until they curl and change color, then remove immediately.
- Key: Keep the blanching time short to maintain tenderness.
4. Heat oil in a wok. Quickly flash-fry the blanched squid in the hot oil, then remove and drain excess oil.
- Key: Flash-frying locks in moisture, resulting in a tender texture.
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over medium-high heat, stir-fry the minced garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add the bird's eye chili, fermented black beans, and chopped chili sauce, and stir-fry until aromatic.
6. Add the bell pepper pieces and stir-fry briefly. Then add the squid and the prepared sauce, tossing everything together over high heat.
7. Splash cooking wine along the edge of the wok and stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in the cornstarch slurry to form a thin glaze. Continue stir-frying until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients evenly. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
Squid is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. Paired with bell peppers and fermented black beans, it helps stimulate the appetite and aid digestion, making it ideal for those watching their calorie intake while craving a flavorful dish. Be careful to keep the blanching and flash-frying times short to avoid toughening the texture; individuals with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of chopped chili sauce and light soy sauce.
