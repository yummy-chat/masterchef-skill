# Street-Side Chow Mein
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Noodles, as needed (fresh noodles or alkaline noodles suitable for stir-frying)
🥚 Eggs, 2-3
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed
🥕 Carrot, half (julienned)
🥬 Leafy greens, a few stalks (such as Shanghai bok choy)
🍖 Ham, 1-2 slices (sliced)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)
🧅 Onion, half (julienned)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (for color)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for tossing noodles and stir-frying)
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed
🫗 Steamed fish soy sauce, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Pot for boiling
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Strainer or slotted spoon
🥄 Wok spatula or spatula
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Large bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the noodles and cook until 80% done. Remove quickly and rinse under cold water, then drain thoroughly.
   **Key Point**: Cooking the noodles to 80% doneness and rinsing them keeps a smooth texture and prevents sticking or becoming too soft during subsequent stir-frying.

2. Place the drained noodles in a large bowl, add an appropriate amount of dark soy sauce and cooking oil, and mix evenly with your hands so the noodles are uniformly colored and not sticky.
   **Key Point**: Mixing oil first before coloring helps the noodles separate more easily during stir-frying and ensures an even color.

3. Heat another pan or wok over high heat, add oil, pour in the beaten eggs, quickly scramble until cooked, then remove and set aside.

4. Leave some base oil in the pan, add the bean sprouts and julienned carrot. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until softened, then add the leafy greens and ham slices, and stir-fry evenly.
   **Note**: Vegetables must be cooked until wilted, meaning they soften and release moisture, ensuring a harmonious texture when mixed with the noodles later.

5. Add the prepared noodles, along with salt, white pepper powder, steamed fish soy sauce, and light soy sauce. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly, ensuring all ingredients are thoroughly mixed and the noodles are heated through.
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry over high heat throughout to maintain wok hei (breath of the wok). Add the seasonings immediately after the noodles enter the pan to use high heat to release their aroma.

6. Just before serving, add the scallion sections, julienned onion, and the previously cooked scrambled eggs. Stir-fry over high heat for a few seconds, and once the scallion fragrance is released, quickly remove from the pan.
   **Key Point**: Add scallions and onion at the end to retain their crispness and fresh aroma; return the eggs only to mix quickly, avoiding overcooking that makes them tough.

## Health Advice
This chow mein is rich in vegetables and balanced with meat and vegetables. However, the noodles and oil content are relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or correspondingly reduce the intake of other main dishes. It is suitable for a quick energy boost, but those with high blood sugar or on a weight loss diet should control their portion size.
