# Stir-Fried Pork Intestines with Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork intestines, about 500g (cleaned thoroughly, choose thinner parts)  
🌶️ Green pointed peppers, to taste (cut diagonally, select the greener front part)  
🌶️ Spiral peppers, to taste (cut diagonally)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut diagonally into thick slices)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced or julienned)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), to taste (used twice, for fragrance and deodorizing)  
🍶 Vinegar, to taste (sprinkle along the edge of the wok before removing from heat)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok (honeycomb bottom)  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife  
🥄 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat cooking oil in the wok until hot. Add the pork intestines and stir-fry over medium heat until the fat renders and the surface is slightly charred.  
   **Key point**: Be sure to fully release the aroma and excess fat from the intestines for a richer flavor later.
2. Add scallion slices, ginger slices, and dried chili peppers. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until fragrant.
3. Add dark soy sauce and stir-fry for color. Then season with salt, oyster sauce, and white sugar.
4. Pour a circle of baijiu along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly to combine.
5. Add boiling water to just cover the ingredients. Turn to medium-high heat, cover the wok, and braise for about 10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: The braising time must be sufficient for the intestines to become tender and absorb the flavors.
6. Remove the lid, add the green pointed peppers and spiral pepper pieces. Turn to high heat and stir-fry quickly until the peppers are just cooked.
7. Sprinkle a little vinegar along the edge of the wok, then add a splash of baijiu. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat until well combined, then remove from the wok.  
   **Key point**: Vinegar and baijiu help remove odors and enhance fragrance; high heat quick stir-frying brings out the flavors.

## Health Tips
Pork intestines are rich in protein and iron but high in cholesterol and fat. It is recommended to pair them with green peppers to promote vitamin absorption. People with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
