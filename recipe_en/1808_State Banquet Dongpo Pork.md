# State Banquet Dongpo Pork
Cuisine: Zhejiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 One piece of pork belly with skin (about a large square piece)  
🧅 A bunch of scallions (for lining the bottom)  
🫚 4-5 slices of ginger  
🍶 Aged Huadiao wine (a little for blanching, a generous amount for braising)  
⭐ 1-2 star anise  
🌿 A small piece of cinnamon  
🍬 An appropriate amount of rock sugar  
🥫 An appropriate amount of dark soy sauce  
🔴 A pinch of red yeast rice powder  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔥 A wok (oil-free and water-free, for searing the skin)  
🥣 A soup pot (for blanching and shaping)  
🔪 A scraper or chef's knife  
🪓 A cutting board  
🍽️ A baking tray + a weight (such as a small pot)  
🍲 A clay pot or wok (for braising and reducing the sauce)  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Sear the skin**: Place the pork belly skin-side down in a hot, oil-free and water-free wok. Rub it back and forth until the skin is evenly darkened.
- Note: The wok must be hot to achieve even charring of the skin and remove any off-flavors.
2. **Scrape clean**: Immediately immerse the seared meat in cold water. Use a knife to scrape off the charred black bits from the skin, revealing a golden color.
3. **Blanch and shape**: Place the whole piece of meat in cold water in a pot. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, and a little Huadiao wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 40 minutes. Skim off any foam and floating fat during this time.
- Key: Start with cold water and simmer over low heat to cook the meat through and achieve initial shaping.
4. **Press the meat to remove fat**: Remove the meat and place it on a baking tray. Press a heavy pot filled with water on top. Let it sit for a while to squeeze out excess fat and make the meat more compact.
5. **Cut into pieces**: Cut the pressed meat into evenly sized square pieces.
6. **Fry to render fat**: In another pot, arrange the meat pieces skin-side down in a neat row. Fry over medium heat until a large amount of fat renders out and the skin is slightly charred. Then, remove.
- Purpose: To further render out fat and add a charred aroma.
7. **Caramelize scallions for the base**: Spray a tiny amount of oil in the braising pot. Cover the bottom of the pot entirely with a whole layer of scallions.
8. **Arrange meat and add seasonings**: Place the fried meat pieces skin-side down on top of the scallions. Pour in a sufficient amount of aged Huadiao wine. Add star anise, cinnamon, ginger slices, extra scallion sections, rock sugar, dark soy sauce, and red yeast rice powder. Then add water until the liquid level is even with the meat.
9. **Simmer over low heat**: Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn to a very low heat. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes.
- Key: Add enough wine and water at once. Use a "slow, deliberate" heat to allow the aroma of the wine and scallions to infuse into the meat.
10. **Reduce the sauce over high heat**: After braising, turn the heat to high. Continuously spoon the sauce over the meat. Reduce until the sauce is thick and the meat has a glossy, rich red color.
- Note: Use high heat to reduce the sauce for a deeper color and more concentrated flavor.
11. Remove from the pot and plate. The flavors of wine and scallions blend together. The meat pieces are square and intact, fatty but not greasy.

## Health Advice
Dongpo Pork's main ingredient is pork belly, which is rich in protein and fat and provides a significant amount of energy, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion to consume in moderation. The multiple steps of removing fat during the cooking process and the long simmering help reduce the greasy feeling, but individuals with high cholesterol should exercise restraint.
