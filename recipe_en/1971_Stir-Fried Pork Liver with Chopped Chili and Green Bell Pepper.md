# Stir-Fried Pork Liver with Chopped Chili and Green Bell Pepper
Cuisine: Common Home-Cooked Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork liver, 1 piece
🫑 Green bell pepper (green pepper), 1 piece
🧅 Scallions, to taste
🧄 Garlic, to taste
🫚 Ginger, to taste
🫒 Vegetable oil, to taste
🌶️ Chopped chili sauce, to taste
🧂 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste
🌫️ White pepper powder, a pinch
🥔 Sweet potato starch, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, to taste (for cleaning)
🍬 White sugar, a pinch
🧂 MSG, a pinch
🍶 High-proof white liquor, a splash

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the pork liver into thin pieces, add salt and knead for 2 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with clean water and drain.
   **Key Point**: Repeated rinsing removes any off-flavor and ensures a clean taste.
2. To the drained pork liver, add steamed fish soy sauce, white pepper powder, and sweet potato starch. Mix well until the surface is evenly coated with a thin batter.
   **Key Point**: Coating the liver helps keep it tender and smooth after stir-frying.
3. Cut the green bell pepper into chunks, slice the scallions into sections, slice the garlic, and mince the ginger. Set aside.
4. Pour vegetable oil into the wok and heat until it reaches 50% hot. Add the coated pork liver and stir-fry over medium heat until 90% cooked. Remove and drain the oil. Set aside.
   **Key Point**: The oil temperature should not be too high to prevent the liver from becoming tough.
5. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the chopped chili sauce, scallions, garlic, and ginger, and stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Add the bell pepper chunks and stir-fry. Add a pinch of white sugar and MSG, then splash high-proof white liquor along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly over high heat.
   **Key Point**: The high-proof liquor evaporates upon contact with heat, effectively removing any off-flavor and enhancing aroma.
7. Add the pre-cooked pork liver back to the wok. Turn to medium-high heat and stir-fry quickly until evenly coated with the sauce and well-seasoned. Remove from the heat and plate immediately.

## Health Suggestion
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, making it suitable for blood nourishment and eye care. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should control their intake. Pairing it with bell peppers and chopped chili not only removes any off-flavor but also enhances the aroma. It is recommended to stir-fry quickly with minimal oil to maintain a tender texture.
