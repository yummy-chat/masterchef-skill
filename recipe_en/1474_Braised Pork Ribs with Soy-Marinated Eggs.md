# Braised Pork Ribs with Soy-Marinated Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork ribs, as needed  
🥓 Pork belly, as needed (one large piece)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (in shell)  
🧅 Onion, half (purple onion, for sautéing)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🫚 Ginger, as needed  
🧅 Green onion, as needed  
🌿 Star anise, as needed  
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed  
🫘 Broad bean paste, one large tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning meat before frying or final seasoning, adjust to taste)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Large wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowls, plates  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Pour cooking oil into the wok, heat over medium heat until warm. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic cloves, and sauté until fragrant.  
   **Key Point**: The oil must be hot enough before adding the ingredients to fully release their aroma.  
2. Add the chopped onion pieces, rib pieces, and pork belly pieces. Turn to medium-high heat and fry until the surface is slightly browned and crispy.  
   **Key Point**: Frying helps render excess fat, enhancing the overall aroma.  
3. Add enough hot water to completely submerge all ingredients. Add the washed eggs (in their shells). Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer.  
   **Note**: The eggs are cooked with the meat at this stage to prepare them for becoming marinated eggs.  
4. Add star anise, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers to the broth. Then add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and one large tablespoon of broad bean paste. Stir well to give the broth a reddish-brown color.  
   **Key Point**: This step is the foundation of the braised flavor and color; mix thoroughly.  
5. Simmer over medium-low heat for about 15–20 minutes, until the broth naturally thickens and the meat becomes tender and flavorful.  
6. Once the broth is thick, remove the eggs. Briefly soak them in cold water, then peel off the shells. Return the peeled eggs to the wok and cook with the meat for a short while, allowing the eggs to be coated in the broth, thus becoming soy-marinated eggs.  
7. Remove the braised ribs, pork belly, and marinated eggs, and arrange them on a plate. Drizzle the thick broth from the wok over the top.  

## Health Advice  
Ribs and eggs are rich in high-quality protein and fats, making them suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing energy supplementation. Individuals with high blood pressure or high blood lipids should limit their intake. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition, while also controlling the amount of braising liquid consumed.
