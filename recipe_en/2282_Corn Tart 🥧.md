# Corn Tart 🥧
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥧 Tart shells – 6 pieces (store-bought, frozen)  
🥛 Whole milk – 135g (for egg tart liquid)  
🥛 Heavy cream – 40g (for egg tart liquid)  
🥚 Egg yolks – 3 pieces (approx. 60g)  
🥛 Condensed milk – 10g  
🍬 White sugar – 30g (for egg tart liquid)  
🌽 Canned sweet corn kernels – 50g  
🥛 Whole milk – 50ml (for corn milk paste)  
🧈 Butter – 8g  
🍬 White sugar – to taste (for stir-frying corn milk paste, approx. 8g)  
🥛 Heavy cream – 150ml (for whipping)  
🍬 White sugar – 8g (for whipping cream)  
🌽 Fresh corn on the cob – 1 ear  
🥣 Crunchy oats – to taste  
🍯 Honey – to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Air fryer  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🌀 Blender/food processor  
🥛 Whisk  
🕸️ Strainer  
👝 Piping bag  
🔥 Kitchen torch (for baking)  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🔪 Knife  
🔪 Cutting board  
🖌️ Pastry brush  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the egg tart liquid**: Mix 135g milk, 30g white sugar, and condensed milk together until the sugar is fully dissolved. Add the egg yolks and 40g heavy cream, stir until well combined. Strain the mixture through a sieve for a smooth texture.  
   **Key point**: Straining makes the egg tart liquid silkier, resulting in a texture similar to custard sauce.  
2. **Prepare the tart shells**: Use the tip of a knife to poke several small holes in the bottom of the frozen tart shells' foil cups to prevent the bottom from becoming soggy during baking.  
3. **Make the corn milk paste base**: Place the canned sweet corn kernels and 50ml milk into a blender and blend into a smooth puree. Strain through a sieve and pour into a non-stick pan for later use.  
4. **Prepare the fresh corn**: Cook the ear of fresh corn, let it cool, then cut it horizontally into thin slices next to the cob and set aside.  
5. **Bake the egg tarts**: Pour the egg tart liquid into the tart shells until 90% full. Place in a preheated air fryer. Bake at 200°C for 10 minutes, then reduce to 180°C and bake for another 8 minutes. For a crispier bottom, remove the foil cups and flip the tarts to bake for an additional 2 minutes.  
6. **Stir-fry the corn milk paste**: Add butter and an appropriate amount of white sugar to the strained corn milk puree. Stir-fry over low heat until thick. Strain again through a sieve. Cover the paste tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for later use.  
   **Key point**: A second straining makes the paste even silkier; refrigeration thickens it for easier mixing with cream later.  
7. **Whip and mix the cream**: Whip 150ml heavy cream with 8g white sugar until soft peaks form. Gently fold in the chilled corn milk paste until evenly combined to make the corn cream filling.  
8. **Assemble**: Sprinkle an appropriate amount of crunchy oats into the baked tarts. Use a piping bag to pipe in the corn cream filling. Top each tart with a slice of fresh corn.  
9. **Torch and brush with honey**: Use a kitchen torch to lightly scorch the surface of the corn until brown spots appear. Finally, brush a layer of honey on top for shine and enhanced flavor.  
   **Key point**: Torching triggers the Maillard reaction, boosting aroma and flavor depth.  

## Health Tips  
This corn tart combines the crispiness of egg tarts, the creaminess of whipped cream, and the sweetness of corn—perfect as a summer treat. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it’s best enjoyed as an occasional indulgence, preferably shared. Note that canned corn has a high sugar content; for those with diabetes, replace it with fresh corn kernels and reduce sugar accordingly.
