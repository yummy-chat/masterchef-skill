# Southern Milky Fragrant Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Pure milk 500 ml
🍬 White sugar 50 g
🧪 Active dry yeast 8 g
🌾 All-purpose flour 800 g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🥣 Glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔗 Rolling pin
🔪 Knife
♨️ Steamer basket
🧻 Steamer cloth/liner
📦 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1. Pour 500 ml of pure milk into a measuring cup, add 50 g of white sugar and 8 g of yeast. Stir with chopsticks until the yeast and sugar are mostly dissolved.
2. Pour the mixed liquid into 800 g of all-purpose flour. First stir with chopsticks into flaky pieces, then use your hands to knead into a dough.
   **Note**: The dough is quite soft. Sticking to hands during initial kneading is normal. It is acceptable as long as it forms a ball; a non-smooth surface is fine.
3. Cover the dough surface with plastic wrap and let it rest for about 10 minutes to allow the gluten to fully develop.
4. Remove the rested dough and continue kneading until the surface is smooth.
5. Place the smooth dough back in the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it undergo the first fermentation at room temperature for about 1–2 hours.
   **Indicator**: Poke a hole in the dough with a flour-dusted finger; if the hole does not shrink back and the inside reveals a honeycomb structure when pulled apart, fermentation is complete.
   **Note**: Fermentation time depends on room temperature; higher temperatures shorten the time.
6. Transfer the fermented dough to a work surface, dust with a little dry flour to prevent sticking, and repeatedly knead and press to release gas until the dough returns to its pre-fermentation size. Divide the dough into two equal portions.
   **Note**: This step aims to expel the gas produced during fermentation.
7. Take one portion of dough and use a rolling pin to roll it into a large thin sheet. Then tightly roll it from one end to the other, forming a cylindrical log.
   **Note**: Rolling further eliminates fine air pockets; rolling must be tight to produce distinct layers in the steamed buns.
8. Slightly flatten the rolled log with your hands, then cut it with a knife into evenly sized steamed bun pieces.
   **Note**: The cut surface should show clear spiral layers.
9. Place the steamed bun pieces spaced apart on a steamer basket lined with a steamer cloth, leaving gaps between them. Cover the lid and let them undergo a second fermentation for about 30–40 minutes.
   **Indicator**: The bun pieces should visibly increase in volume, feel fluffy, and spring back when lightly pressed.
   **Note**: Adjust the time flexibly based on appearance.
10. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Place the steamer basket on top and steam over high heat for 15 minutes.
11. After turning off the heat, let the buns rest in the steamer for an additional 5 minutes before lifting the lid.
    **Note**: Resting prevents the buns from shrinking due to a sudden temperature drop.

## Health Suggestion
Milk paired with steamed buns provides quality protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for breakfast and can be eaten with eggs. Those with high blood sugar should control their intake. Rolling and layering the dough before steaming gives the buns a chewier texture.
