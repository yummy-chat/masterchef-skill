# Oil-Splashed Tofu
Cuisine: Home-style
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Firm tofu (torn into irregular chunks by hand)
🌿 Cilantro (cut into small sections)
🧅 Scallion (use the white part, remove the core, and cut diagonally into thin shreds)
🌶️ Red bell pepper (seeded and shredded)
🧄 Garlic (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (cut into sections or shreds)
🧂 Salt
🫙 Donggu Yipinxian (or seafood soy sauce/light soy sauce)
🦪 Oyster sauce
💧 Water
🍬 Granulated sugar
🧂 Chicken bouillon
🫒 Hot oil (heated until smoking)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Saucepan
🍳 Wok or small milk pot
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon
🍽️ Deep plate or large bowl

## Preparation Steps
1.  Tear the firm tofu into irregular pieces by hand; the smaller the pieces, the easier they absorb flavor.
2.  Cut the cilantro into small sections. For the scallion, use the white part, split it in half, remove the core, and slice diagonally into thin shreds. For the red bell pepper, remove the seeds, slice thinly, and cut diagonally into shreds. Slice the garlic. Mix the scallion shreds, red bell pepper shreds, and garlic slices together. Cut the dried chili peppers into sections or shreds and set aside.
    **Note**: Cutting the scallion diagonally breaks its fibers, preventing it from getting stuck in teeth; use a generous amount of scallion to highlight its aroma.
3.  In a small pot, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, water, sugar, and chicken bouillon to prepare the sauce. Set aside.
4.  Bring a pot of water to a boil, add 1 teaspoon of salt, then add the tofu pieces. Boil over medium-high heat for 5 minutes, then remove and drain thoroughly.
    **Key Point**: Blanching removes the beany taste and makes the tofu more tender; ensure it is drained very well to avoid diluting the sauce.
5.  Arrange the tofu on a plate, scatter the cilantro sections around the edges, and pile the mixed scallion shreds, red bell pepper shreds, and garlic slices in the center of the tofu.
6.  Bring the sauce from step 3 to a boil over high heat until large bubbles form and it thickens slightly, then turn off the heat. Pour the sauce evenly over the tofu and toppings.
    **Note**: The sauce must be boiled and well combined until it reaches a slightly thick consistency to fully release its flavor.
7.  Top the tofu and toppings with the dried chili pepper shreds. In another pot, heat oil until smoking, then quickly pour the hot oil over the dried chili peppers.
    **Key Point**: The oil must be heated until smoking to release the aromatic charred flavors from the dried chili peppers and scallion.

## Health Tips
This dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness or fat-control diets. Blanching the tofu removes its beany taste and helps it better absorb the sauce's flavor. Firm tofu naturally contains salt, and the sauce and oyster sauce already provide flavor, so those with high blood pressure may omit additional salt.
