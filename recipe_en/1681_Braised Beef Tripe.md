# Braised Beef Tripe
Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐄 Beef tripe about 1 whole (rinsed clean)
🌿 Celery appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Erjingtiao (green chili peppers) appropriate amount (cut into rings)
🌶️ Xiaomila (red chili peppers) appropriate amount (cut into rings)
🧄 Pickled ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers appropriate amount (minced)
🌿 Cilantro a little (for garnishing when serving)
🧅 Old ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🍺 Beer about 1 can
🧂 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🧂 MSG appropriate amount
🧂 White pepper appropriate amount
🌶️ Doubanjiang (broad bean chili paste) about 1 tablespoon
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a little
🌿 Spices (cinnamon, star anise, etc.) a little

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🥣 Water basin
🥄 Soup ladle
🥄 Spatula

## Steps
1. Place the beef tripe in a water basin, add clean water, and rinse it thoroughly.
**Note**: No need to over-cleanse; just rinse until clean.

2. Pour a large amount of water into the wok. Add the beef tripe while the water is still cold, along with scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat to blanch and remove any gamey smell.
**Key**: Must start with cold water and add scallions, ginger, and cooking wine to fully eliminate off-flavors.

3. Remove the blanched beef tripe and place it on a cutting board to cut.
**Key**: Slice thinly against the grain at an angle, similar to slicing beef, or the texture will be compromised.

4. Cut the celery into sections. Cut the Erjingtiao and Xiaomila into rings respectively. Set aside.
**Note**: Add the side ingredients only after the tripe has finished braising, to keep them crisp and tender.

5. Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok. When the oil is hot, add the Sichuan peppercorns, minced pickled ginger, and minced pickled chili peppers. Then add the doubanjiang. Stir-fry over low heat until red oil is released and the aroma is fragrant.
**Key**: Stir-fry slowly over low heat to avoid burning.

6. Next, add the sliced old ginger, scallion sections, and spices such as cinnamon and star anise. Stir-fry until evenly combined.

7. Add the sliced beef tripe and stir-fry together with the base mixture, ensuring the tripe is evenly coated with red oil and sauce.

8. Pour enough clean water into the wok, then add one can of beer. Bring to a boil over high heat.

9. Add salt, chicken bouillon, MSG, and white pepper to season. Stir to combine.

10. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise for about 50 minutes until the tripe is tender and fully infused with flavor.
**Key**: Adjust the time according to the texture of the tripe; it is done when it can be easily pierced with chopsticks.

11. After the time is up, remove the lid. Add the prepared celery sections, Erjingtiao rings, and Xiaomila rings. Turn up the heat to high and stir-fry for 1–2 minutes until just cooked through.
**Key**: Do not stir-fry the side ingredients for too long; keep them crisp and tender.

12. Plate the dish and garnish with a little cilantro.

## Health Advice
Beef tripe is rich in collagen and iron, making it suitable for those with anemia or in need of nourishing the spleen and stomach. However, it is high in cholesterol, so consume it in moderation. Pairing with pickled ginger and chili peppers can aid digestion, but those with sensitive stomachs should control the spiciness level.
