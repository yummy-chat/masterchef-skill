# Pickled Pepper Beef
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin 300g
🌶️ Pickled peppers (wild peppers) about 35g (sliced into rings, squeezed dry)
🌶️ Chopped pickled peppers about 20g
🧄 Garlic 4 cloves (about 20g, diced)
🫚 Ginger about 13g (3g crushed for scallion-ginger water, 10g diced)
🧅 Scallion about 7g (crushed for scallion-ginger water)
🌶️ Small chili peppers 3 (about 15g, sliced into rings)
🥬 Celery 1 stalk (about 80g, leaves and roots removed, thick stems split and cut into sections)
🧂 Baking soda 1/2 teaspoon (about 1g)
🧂 Salt about 0.5g (for marinating beef)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2 teaspoons (about 4g, divided for marinating and sauce)
🧂 White pepper a pinch
💧 Scallion-ginger water 60g (made by crushing scallion and ginger in water, squeezing, and filtering)
🌽 Corn starch or potato starch about 7g (5g for marinating, 2g for sauce slurry)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (about 20g for sealing beef, 3 times usual amount for stir-frying, normal amount for aromatics)
🥄 Light soy sauce 1/2 tablespoon (about 5g)
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1/3 tablespoon (about 3g)
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 teaspoon tip (about 5g)
🍬 White sugar 1 full teaspoon (about 4g)
🫒 Sichuan pepper oil 2/3 tablespoon (about 10g)
💧 Water about 20g (for sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Small bowl, measuring spoon, kitchen scale
🫗 Strainer (fine mesh)
🍳 Wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🪄 Wok spatula (or spatula)

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef tenderloin into three large pieces along the grain. Select the side with chaotic grain, and slice thinly against the grain.
**Note**: Must cut across the grain; this is key to tender, non-chewy beef.

2. Crush scallion and ginger in a bowl, add 80g water, squeeze firmly by hand until the water turns cloudy, then filter to get about 60g scallion-ginger water.
3. Marinate the beef:
   a. Add baking soda, salt, chicken bouillon, and white pepper to the beef slices. Gently mix well.
   b. Add scallion-ginger water in three batches, each time stirring vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed and the beef becomes sticky.
   c. Add corn starch and mix well. Finally, add cooking oil and mix to seal.
**Note**: Adding water in batches and stirring in one direction is key for the beef to absorb moisture and stay tender; sealing with oil prevents sticking during cooking.

4. Slice pickled peppers into rings and squeeze dry. Dice ginger and garlic. Slice small chili peppers into rings. Cut celery into sections.
**Note**: Pickled peppers and chopped pickled peppers must be squeezed dry, otherwise the dish will be too salty.

5. Mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, chicken bouillon, Sichuan pepper oil, dry starch, and water together until no dry starch lumps remain.
6. Heat the wok until smoking. Spray in a small amount of oil to coat the wok, continue heating until the oil smokes, then pour out the hot oil.
7. Add oil 3 times the usual amount for stir-frying to the wok. When the oil temperature reaches a point where chopsticks inserted produce dense small bubbles, add the beef and quickly spread it apart. Remove and drain when about 80% changes color.
**Note**: Use plenty of oil and work quickly to ensure tender beef. Then wash and dry the wok.

8. Add a normal amount of cooking oil to the wok and heat until slightly smoking. First, add pickled peppers, chopped pickled peppers, diced ginger, and diced garlic to bloom the aromatics. Then add small chili peppers and celery sections. Stir-fry over high heat for about 30 seconds until the celery is just cooked.
9. Add the beef without stirring first. Stir the sauce well and pour it in along the wok edge. Then stir-fry quickly for 10-15 seconds until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.
**Note**: Do not stir before adding the sauce to allow the high heat of the wok bottom to release the soy aromas; keep stir-frying short to prevent the beef from overcooking.

10. Turn off the heat and immediately plate.

## Health Tips
This pickled pepper beef is high in protein and low in fat. Beef tenderloin paired with pickled peppers is appetizing and helps cut through greasiness. However, it is high in sodium. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of pickled peppers and chopped pickled peppers. Celery is rich in fiber and can balance the spicy kick, making it suitable to serve with brown rice.
