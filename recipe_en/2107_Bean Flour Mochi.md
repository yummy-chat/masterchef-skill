# Bean Flour Mochi  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 Milk 1 box (250ml, also used as a measuring tool)  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 1 box (about 130g)  
🌽 Cornstarch 5 bottle caps (about 25g)  
🍬 White sugar A little more than 2 bottle caps (about 16g, adjustable to taste)  
🫘 Cooked soybean flour Appropriate amount (for coating)  
🛢️ Cooking oil 3 bottle caps (about 15g, use a neutral-flavored oil)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🫗 Single-use heating pack (for self-heating food)  
🪣 Blue plastic basin (for placing the heating pack and bowl)  
🧤 Disposable gloves (or plastic bags)  
🧻 Plastic wrap  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Pour the milk, glutinous rice flour, cornstarch, white sugar, and cooking oil into a stainless steel bowl.  
2. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until no dry flour particles remain, forming a smooth batter.  
3. Place the single-use heating pack in the plastic basin, add cold water, and once steam is produced, set the bowl with the batter into the basin to heat indirectly.  
   **Note**: Be careful of steam burns throughout the process; if no self-heating pack is available, you can cook over low heat in a non-stick pan, stirring continuously.  
4. Stir continuously during heating until the batter is completely set, becoming a translucent, non-flowing solid paste, indicating it is fully cooked.  
5. Spread plastic wrap on the cutting board to prevent sticking, then pour out the cooked dough. Wearing disposable gloves, repeatedly knead and stretch the dough to make it more pliable.  
6. Roll the kneaded dough into a long strip and cut into small pieces with a knife.  
7. Roll the cut small dough pieces in cooked soybean flour until evenly coated.  
8. Arrange on a plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This bean flour mochi uses a self-heating pack instead of traditional steaming, with calories mainly from glutinous rice and sugar. It is recommended to consume 6 to 8 pieces per serving, suitable as an afternoon tea snack. Those with high blood sugar should control their intake.
