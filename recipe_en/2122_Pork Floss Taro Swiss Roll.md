# Pork Floss Taro Swiss Roll
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Taro (Guangxi variety) 100g (cut into small pieces)
🍠 Purple sweet potato powder 3g (for coloring)
🧈 Unsalted butter 5g
🥛 Milk 30g (for taro paste)
🍦 Heavy cream 300g
🍬 Granulated sugar 24g (for taro cream)
🍬 Granulated sugar 60g (for whipping egg whites)
🥚 Eggs 5 (separate yolks and whites, refrigerate egg whites)
🌽 Corn oil 50g
🌾 Cake flour 60g (sifted)
🥓 Pork floss (pork sung) as needed (for filling and surface decoration)
🍘 Seaweed (nori) as needed (crushed, mixed with pork floss)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Steamer (for steaming taro)
🥣 Blender (for making taro paste and cream)
⚡️ Electric hand mixer (for whipping egg whites)
♨️ Oven (top and bottom heat mode, preheated in advance)
🍪 Baking sheet
📜 Parchment paper (line the baking sheet to prevent sticking)
🎀 Piping bag (for taro cream)
🔪 Offset spatula
🪨 Marble rolling pin (to assist in rolling the cake roll)
🔪 Knife (for cutting the finished product)

## Steps
1. Cut the taro into small pieces and steam in a steamer until easily pierced with a chopstick. Remove and set aside.
   **Key point**: Steam until completely soft to ensure a smooth taro paste without hard cores.
2. Place the steamed taro, purple sweet potato powder, unsalted butter, and milk into a blender. Blend into a smooth and fine puree.
3. Add the heavy cream and 24g of granulated sugar to the taro puree. Continue blending until the color is a uniform light purple, creating a taro cream. Transfer to a piping bag and set aside.
   **Note**: Blend until no lumps remain and the texture is smooth.
4. Add corn oil to the egg yolks and whisk with a hand whisk until fully combined. Sift in the cake flour and mix until a smooth, lump-free batter forms.
   **Key point**: Ensure the corn oil and egg yolks are fully emulsified. Do not add milk or egg whites at this stage.
5. Add 60g of granulated sugar to the egg whites. Use an electric hand mixer to whip to stiff peaks. Smooth out the bubbles to make the meringue finer.
6. Add the meringue to the egg yolk batter in three additions. First, fold using the hand whisk, then use a spatula to mix thoroughly, avoiding deflation.
7. Pour the batter into the parchment-lined baking sheet. Gently shake the sheet to let the batter level naturally.
   **Key point**: No need to deliberately spread; rely on the batter's fluidity to fill the sheet.
8. Place in the preheated oven. Bake at 150°C (top and bottom heat) for 30 minutes until the surface is golden brown and the cake springs back when lightly tapped.
9. Once the cake roll has cooled, evenly spread the taro cream over the surface. Sprinkle with pork floss and crushed seaweed.
   **Note**: Use a slightly generous amount of cream to facilitate rolling.
10. Use the marble rolling pin to press against the front end of the cake roll. Gently lift the parchment paper to roll the cake tightly. Wrap both ends with plastic wrap and place in the freezer to set for 40 minutes.
    **Key point**: Apply even pressure while rolling to avoid cracking. Freeze for sufficient time to allow clean cuts.
11. Remove the set cake roll. Pipe the remaining taro cream onto the surface, spread smooth with an offset spatula, generously cover with pork floss and seaweed, then cut into thick slices.

## Health Advice
This pork floss taro Swiss roll combines sweet and savory flavors, with a creamy taro filling and fragrant pork floss with seaweed, making it an ideal afternoon tea treat. However, it is relatively high in sugar and fat. It is recommended to enjoy 1–2 pieces per serving and to be mindful of overall intake.
