# Taiwanese Braised Pork Rice
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 **Ingredients**
- 500g pork belly with skin (cut into 0.7cm × 0.7cm × 2.5cm strips)
- 25g shallots (minced)
- Appropriate amount of garlic (minced)
- 4 eggs (hard-boiled, peeled, with slits cut into the whites)
- Appropriate amount of rice

🧂 **Seasonings**
- 15ml cooking oil
- 75ml light soy sauce
- 35ml rice wine
- 20g rock sugar
- 6g white pepper powder
- 6g five-spice powder
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 star anise

🔪 **Kitchenware**
- Wok
- Knife

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the pork belly into 0.7cm × 0.7cm × 2.5cm strips. Freezing for 30 minutes beforehand makes this easier.
2. Mince the shallots and garlic, and set aside.
3. Hard-boil the eggs, peel them, and make a few slits on the egg whites to help them absorb flavor.
4. Heat a wok over high heat, add 15ml of cooking oil to coat the bottom, and wait 5 seconds for the oil to heat up.
5. Add the pork belly strips and stir-fry until the meat turns slightly white. Drizzle 10ml of rice wine along the edge of the wok, and continue stir-frying until no more oil is rendered.
6. Add the minced shallots and stir-fry for 1 minute until fragrant with oil and onion aroma. Then add the minced garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
7. Push the pork to the side of the wok, add the rock sugar, and heat until it melts and bubbles into caramel. Then mix it evenly with the pork.
8. Add the light soy sauce and stir-fry until aromatic.
9. Splash in 25ml of rice wine, add enough water to submerge the pork, and then add the white pepper powder, five-spice powder, star anise, bay leaves, and boiled eggs. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and braise for 1 hour.
10. During the simmering, be sure to taste and adjust the saltiness. Stir every 15 minutes to check for sticking. If it becomes too dry, add 100ml of water.
11. After 1 hour, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce until it is thick and amber in color.
12. Serve a bowl of rice, top with the braised pork, and place a braised egg on the side.

## Health Advice
This braised pork rice is rich in collagen and savory sauce, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, due to its high fat and sugar content, it is recommended to pair it with vegetables for balanced nutrition. Those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
