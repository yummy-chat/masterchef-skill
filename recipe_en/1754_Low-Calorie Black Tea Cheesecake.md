# Low-Calorie Black Tea Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧀 Cream cheese 100g (half a block)
🥛 Plain yogurt 420g (3 containers)
🥚 Eggs 2 (room temperature)
🍵 Black tea leaves 4g (2 bags, 2g each)
🌽 Cornstarch 25g (about 5 bottle caps)
🌰 Chestnuts as needed (about 8 pieces, for decoration)
🍬 Sugar substitute 20g (about 4 bottle caps, can be replaced with regular white sugar)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🔪 Spatula
🍳 Small pot (for water bath heating)
🕸️ Wire rack
📦 Foil mold or 6-inch cake pan (lined with parchment paper)
🔥 Oven
❄️ Refrigerator
🔍 Sieve
🥄 Small spoon, bottle cap (for measuring dry ingredients)

## Steps
1. Place the cream cheese in a mixing bowl and stir with a spatula until smooth and lump-free.
2. Add the sugar substitute and mix well.
3. Beat the eggs into a liquid, then add to the cheese mixture in batches, stirring until fully absorbed before adding the next batch.
   **Note**: If the mixture separates, place the mixing bowl over hot water in a water bath and stir continuously until smooth again.
4. Add the plain yogurt and stir until well combined; the batter will become thinner.
5. Add the black tea leaves and stir well; then sift in the cornstarch and mix until no dry powder remains.
   **Key point**: Since the cheese amount is small, increase the cornstarch slightly to help the cake set.
6. Strain the batter through a sieve twice; press the leftover tea leaves through the sieve back into the batter to retain flavor.
7. Preheat the oven. Pour the strained batter into the lined mold, place it on the middle rack, and bake at 200°C for about 20 minutes, until the surface is golden.
8. Remove from the oven and let cool on a wire rack, then refrigerate overnight to allow the cake to firm up. A small amount of oil may seep to the surface, giving a glossy appearance.
9. After chilling, spread a layer of yogurt or cream on top, arrange the chestnuts, and dust with powdered sugar for decoration. When cut, the inside will be soft and creamy, with a smooth texture. Enjoy.

## Health Tips
This low-calorie black tea cheesecake replaces large amounts of cream with plain yogurt and uses a sugar substitute and cornstarch for setting. It's relatively low in calories, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss. The texture becomes silky after being refrigerated overnight. Be mindful of the chestnut decoration amount to maintain its low-calorie nature.
