# Chiffon Cake
Cuisine Type: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs  
🍬 Sugar  
🥛 Milk (or water)  
🧈 Cooking oil (or butter)  
🌾 Cake flour  
🍋 Lemon juice or white vinegar (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔥 Oven  
🌀 Electric mixer  
🥧 Aluminum alloy anodized cake pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Mixing bowls  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Take two clean, dry containers and separate the egg whites from the yolks. The egg white container must not contain any grease, and the yolk container must not contain any water.  
2. In another bowl, add cooking oil and cake flour, stir, then add egg yolks, milk, and 1/4 of the sugar in sequence. Mix evenly with a spatula using a folding motion.  
3. Sift in the cake flour in batches, stirring in a "Z" shape with the spatula until no dry flour remains. Set aside.  
4. Add lemon juice or white vinegar to the egg whites. Add the remaining sugar in three batches, beating with an electric mixer from medium to high speed until stiff peaks form.  
5. Take 1/3 of the meringue and fold it into the egg yolk mixture until combined. Then pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and continue folding until fully incorporated.  
6. Pour the batter into the cake pan and gently tap the pan a few times to release large air bubbles.  
7. Preheat the oven to 150°C top heat and 160°C bottom heat. Place the pan on the lower rack. Bake at the same temperature for 3/5 of the total baking time, then adjust to 160°C top heat and 170°C bottom heat for the remaining 2/5 of the total time.  
8. After baking, tap the pan to release steam, immediately invert it to cool for 10 minutes, then unmold.  

## Health Tips  
Chiffon cake has a soft and fluffy texture, making it suitable for the whole family. It is recommended to keep the sugar amount between 50–70 grams, making it a good option for satisfying cravings during a low-sugar diet or as an afternoon snack. Be careful not to overmix the egg yolk batter, as this can cause the flour to develop gluten and affect the texture.
