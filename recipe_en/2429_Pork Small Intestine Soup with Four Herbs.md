# Pork Small Intestine Soup with Four Herbs  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork small intestine 500g  
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices  
🍄 Poria 10g  
🥔 Dry-fried Chinese yam 10g  
🌰 Gordon euryale seed 10g  
🫘 Lotus seed 10g  
🧂 Edible salt to taste  
🌾 Flour to taste (for cleaning the small intestine, not for seasoning)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫕 Clay pot  
🥄 Ceramic spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the pork small intestine in a basin, add flour and knead repeatedly, then rinse thoroughly with water to remove surface mucus and bitterness.  
2. Tie the cleaned small intestine into small knots to prevent excessive shrinking during simmering.  
3. Bring water to a boil in a pot, blanch the small intestine knots, then remove and rinse again with water.  
4. Place the blanched small intestine knots into the clay pot, add ginger slices, Poria, dry-fried Chinese yam, Gordon euryale seed, and lotus seeds.  
**Key point**: It is recommended to use a clay pot for simmering. The salt should be added in advance at this step, then fill with water to 90% full.  
5. Cover the lid and simmer over low heat for 1 hour until the soup is reduced to about half a pot.  
**Note**: Maintain low heat throughout. The soup should appear milky white, the small intestine should be tender and chewy, and the herbal aroma should be infused into the broth.  

## Health Suggestions  
This Four Herbs soup strengthens the spleen and dispels dampness, suitable for people with weak spleen and stomach or heavy dampness. The small intestine is rich in protein but high in cholesterol, so avoid excessive consumption at one time. It is recommended to pair with a small amount of vegetables for balanced nutrition. Caution is advised for gout patients.
