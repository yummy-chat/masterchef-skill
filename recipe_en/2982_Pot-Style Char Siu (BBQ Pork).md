# Pot-Style Char Siu (BBQ Pork)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork collar butt 500g (with alternating fat and lean, beautiful marbling)
🥤 Soda water approximately 1000ml (for soaking, to make the meat more tender)
🧄 Smashed garlic 50g
🫘 Fermented red bean curd 25g
🥫 Char siu sauce 75g
🦪 Hoisin sauce 75g
🌿 Five-spice powder 10g
🍬 White sugar 50g
🥜 Peanut butter to taste
🥡 Light soy sauce (Knorr Premium Soy Sauce) 50g
🍶 Rice wine 25g (added in 2-3 batches)
🍯 Honey 25g

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spoon
🔗 Tongs
🥣 Airtight container/bowl

## Steps
1.  Select pork collar butt with alternating fat and lean, evenly distributed marbling, and cut into long strips.
**Note**: The choice of meat cut is crucial.

2.  Repeatedly pound the meat strips with the back of a knife to loosen the texture.
**Note**: Pounding makes the meat more tender and easier to absorb flavors.

3.  Place the pounded meat strips in a bowl, pour in soda water, and soak for 10 minutes.
**Note**: Soda water is alkaline and neutralizes the acidity of the meat, making it more tender.

4.  Make the marinade: Combine smashed garlic, fermented red bean curd, char siu sauce, hoisin sauce, five-spice powder, white sugar, peanut butter, and light soy sauce in a bowl. Mix until uniform.
**Note**: The various sauces form the foundation of char siu's complex flavor.

5.  Drain the soaked meat strips thoroughly and add them to the prepared marinade. Mix well to ensure every piece of meat is coated with the sauce.
**Note**: The meat needs to release absorbed liquid to better absorb the seasoning flavors.

6.  Place the marinated meat in the refrigerator to marinate for two hours.
**Note**: Chilled marination allows the flavors to penetrate deeper into the meat.

7.  Heat the wok until hot, then add cold oil. Place the marinated meat strips in the wok and pan-fry over medium-low heat.
**Note**: Hot wok, cold oil prevents sticking. Slowly fry to render the meat's fat and release aroma.

8.  Fry until the surface of the meat pieces is slightly caramelized and juices start to seep out.
**Doneness check**: The meat surface shows a Maillard reaction, and aroma is abundant.
   **Note**: Meat soaked in soda water retains moisture; it will release water first before drying out during frying.

9.  Add rice wine along the edge of the wok in 2-3 batches.
**Note**: Adding in batches helps with alcohol evaporation and flavor integration.

10. Pour the small amount of remaining marinade from the meat bowl into the wok. Cook over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes, simmering together with the meat to reduce the sauce.
**Doneness check**: The sauce gradually thickens and coats the meat.
    **Note**: This process allows the meat to fully absorb the sauce, achieving a flavorful state.

11. Remove the pan-fried cooked meat pieces with tongs and set aside on a plate.
**Note**: The meat is mostly cooked through.

12. Simmer the remaining sauce in the wok over medium-low heat until it becomes thick and gelatinous.
**Doneness check**: The sauce is visibly reduced and thickened.

13. Add honey to the thickened sauce, stir briefly until just combined, then turn off the heat.
**Note**: Honey turns sour if heated too high; just bring to a slight simmer, do not boil for long.

14. Heat a little oil in another wok. Briefly pan-fry the already cooked char siu again over gentle heat, being careful not to burn it.
**Doneness check**: The surface develops further caramelized aroma.
    **Note**: This step aims to enhance the "charred" flavor of the char siu.

15. Slice the char siu and arrange on a plate. Drizzle the prepared honey sauce over the top and serve.
**Note**: Drizzling sauce adds sheen and flavor.

## Health Advice
This char siu uses soda water to tenderize the meat and honey for finishing. It is relatively high in protein and sugar, suitable for fitness enthusiasts for energy replenishment, but those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
