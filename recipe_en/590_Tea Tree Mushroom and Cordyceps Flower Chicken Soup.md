# Tea Tree Mushroom and Cordyceps Flower Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Steps
1.  Chop the chicken thighs into small pieces. Place them in a pot with cold water, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine.
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps fully remove blood; scallion, ginger, and wine remove gaminess and enhance aroma.
2.  Bring to a boil over high heat, blanch for about 10 minutes, skim off the foam, then remove the chicken pieces and rinse them clean under running water.
3.  Place the blanched chicken pieces into a small stewing pot. Add tea tree mushrooms, cordyceps flowers, red dates, lotus seeds, lily bulbs, and a little shredded ginger.
4.  Pour in drinking water until it just covers all the ingredients.
5.  Bring water in the steamer to a boil. Place the stewing pot inside, cover with its own lid and the steamer lid. Reduce to medium-low heat and steam slowly (double-boiling method) for 2 hours.
6.  Once done, remove the pot. Season with edible salt and chicken bouillon to taste, and gently stir to combine.

## Health Tips
This soup is rich in protein and various mushroom polysaccharides, making it suitable for autumn to moisten dryness and boost immunity. The chicken has already been blanched to reduce fat, making it relatively low in calories. Those with poor digestion or who dislike greasiness can remove the chicken skin before stewing. It is recommended to season at the end, as tea tree mushrooms and cordyceps flowers have a natural umami flavor; adding salt too early will mask their inherent taste.
