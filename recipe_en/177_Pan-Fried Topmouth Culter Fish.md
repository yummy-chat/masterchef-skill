# Pan-Fried Topmouth Culter Fish
Cuisine: 🎨 Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: 🌿 All-Season Meal  

---

🧄 **Ingredients**  
- 🐟 Topmouth culter (2 jin best)  
- 🫚 Minced ginger 20g  
- 🧅 Scallion half (about 50g)  
- 🧄 Garlic 4 cloves  
- 🌶️ Green pepper  
- 🌿 Cilantro  
- 🌶️ Dried chili peppers 4–6 (optional)  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- 🥄 Salt about 50g (for curing)  
- 🍶 Cooking wine 100ml  
- 🥢 Light soy sauce 4ml  
- 🟤 Dark soy sauce 2ml (optional)  
- 🍬 White sugar 10g  
- 🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)  
- 🌾 Chicken bouillon  
- 🌿 Thirteen spices  
- 🍎 Aged vinegar  

🍳 **Cookware**  
- 🔪 Knife and cutting board  
- 🥘 Wok  
- 🧊 Refrigerator  
- 🪢 Hanging rope (for air-drying)  

---

📝 **Steps**  
1. Butcher the fish by splitting it from the back (do not cut the belly open) and clean thoroughly.  
2. Evenly rub salt over the fish’s surface, pour in 80ml cooking wine and 20g minced ginger, then refrigerate in the crisper drawer for 1–2 days to cure.  
3. Remove the cured fish, hang it with a rope and air-dry until half-dry, about 1–2 days (adjust based on temperature and sunlight).  
4. Before cooking, rinse the fish under clean water and drain well to prevent oil splatter while frying.  
5. Heat the wok over high heat, then reduce to low heat, add cooking oil to coat the entire surface, and slide the fish into the wok skin-side down along the edge.  
6. Once the fish is in the wok, let it sit without moving for about 30 seconds before gently shaking the wok to prevent the skin from tearing.  
7. Fry the skin side for about 1 minute, then flip and fry the other side for 1–2 minutes until both sides are golden brown.  
8. Push the fried fish to the side of the wok, leave some space, add doubanjiang and stir-fry until fragrant, then add minced ginger and garlic.  
9. After the aromatics are fragrant, pour in the remaining cooking wine, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce, then add hot water until it reaches the same level as the fish or slightly below.  
10. Turn to medium-high heat and cook for 5–10 minutes, then add green pepper segments, white sugar, chicken bouillon, thirteen spices, and aged vinegar.  
11. Reduce to low heat and cook for 2–5 minutes, then add scallion and cilantro. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice
This topmouth culter is cured, air-dried, then pan-fried and simmered, resulting in a firmer texture, but its salt content is relatively high. People with high blood pressure should reduce the salt during curing. The fish is rich in high-quality protein and DHA, making it suitable for mental workers and adolescents.
