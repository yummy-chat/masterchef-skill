# Fish Skin Peanuts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Red-skinned peanuts 500g
🥚 Egg 1
🌾 Flour as needed (add in small batches, just enough to coat peanuts)
🍬 White sugar 150g
🧂 Sichuan pepper-salt 5g
🌶️ Chili powder 3g
🪴 Cumin powder 2g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn powder 3g
🫗 Cooking oil as needed (for deep frying)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🫙 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🥣 Soup ladle
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Soak the peanuts in warm water for 1 hour.
   **Key point**: Soaking prevents burning during frying and results in a crispier texture.
2. Drain the peanuts and place them in a dry, oil-free wok. Add warm water to just cover the peanuts. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the white sugar. Reduce to medium heat and cook for about 15 minutes, skimming off any foam during cooking. Continue until the sugar water is fully absorbed and the peanuts are well-coated with sugar.
   **Key point**: Skimming the foam ensures the finished product has a good color and does not turn dark; the sugar water must be fully absorbed.
3. Spread the cooked peanuts out on a flat surface and let them cool completely.
   **Key point**: The peanuts must be completely cool, otherwise the egg will cook when added.
4. Mix the Sichuan pepper-salt, chili powder, cumin powder, and Sichuan peppercorn powder together to form a seasoning powder. Set aside.
5. Crack the egg into the cooled peanuts. Gently shake the bowl so each peanut is evenly coated with egg. Then, add the flour in small batches, continuing to shake the bowl so the peanuts are coated with a thin, even layer of flour.
   **Note**: Avoid using your hands to mix, as this can cause the flour to clump unevenly.
6. Let the coated peanuts rest for 5 minutes so the flour fully absorbs surface moisture. This prevents the coating from falling off or sticking during frying.
7. Pour enough oil into the wok for deep frying and heat to about 140°C (284°F, or 4/10 heat). Add the peanuts and fry over medium-low heat. Do not stir immediately; wait for the coating to set before gently moving the peanuts to prevent them from sticking to the bottom.
   **Doneness check**: Fry over medium-low heat for about 15 minutes total. The peanuts are done when they all float to the surface, turn a reddish-brown color, sound crisp, and the kernel inside is slightly yellow.
   **Key point**: Because of the sugar content, it is essential to fry over medium-low heat, otherwise the peanuts will burn easily.
8. Remove the peanuts with a slotted spoon and drain off the oil. While still hot, sprinkle with the prepared seasoning powder and toss quickly to coat evenly.
   **Key point**: The seasoning must be added while the peanuts are hot, otherwise it will not stick well and will lack aroma.
9. Plate and serve.

## Health Advice
These fish skin peanuts are high in sugar and oil, making them a high-calorie food. It is recommended to consume in small portions and not as a staple food. People with high blood sugar or those on a weight-loss diet should consume with caution.
