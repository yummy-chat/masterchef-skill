# Classic Sour Cream Swiss Roll
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Egg whites 170g (frozen until thin ice forms on the edges)  
🥚 Egg yolks 85g  
🥛 Milk 20g  
🌽 Corn oil 50g  
🍶 Sour cream 70g (can be substituted with equal amount of whipping cream)  
🌾 Cake flour 70g  
🧂 Sea salt 1.5g (can be substituted with regular salt)  
🍬 Caster sugar 75g (added to egg whites in three portions)  
🍋 Lemon juice about 7-8 drops (can be substituted with white vinegar)  
🥛 Whipping cream 300g (for filling)  
🍬 Sugar 18g (for whipping the cream)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (for egg yolks, egg whites, and mixing batter)  
🥄 Whisk  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing liquids)  
🕸️ Flour sifter  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🌡️ Food thermometer  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🗜️ 28×28cm square baking tray  
📄 Parchment paper  
🪢 Rolling pin  
📏 Small ruler or dough scraper  
🧣 Frozen kitchen towel (to wrap around piping bag)  
🧴 Plastic wrap  
♨️ Oven  
💧 Bowl of ice water (for whipping cream over ice)  
🧽 Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
### Preparing the Egg Yolk Batter  
1. Separate the egg yolks and whites, placing the egg whites in a bowl and freezing until a thin layer of ice forms around the edges.  
   - Key: Beating egg whites while cold creates a more stable and fine meringue.  
2. In a small bowl, mix milk, corn oil, and sour cream with a whisk until fully combined and smooth.  
3. Heat the liquid mixture over a water bath to about 40°C.  
4. Slowly pour the warm liquid into the egg yolks while whisking quickly and continuously.  
5. Sift in the cake flour, mix until smooth with no dry spots, scrape the sides of the bowl, then strain the egg yolk batter once.  
6. Cover the bowl of egg yolk batter with plastic wrap and keep it warm in about 40°C water, maintaining it at around 36°C.  

### Whipping the Meringue  
1. Take the frozen egg whites out, add sea salt and lemon juice.  
2. Beat with an electric mixer on high speed until large bubbles appear, then add 1/3 of the caster sugar.  
3. Continue beating on high speed until the foam becomes fine, then add another 1/3 of the caster sugar.  
4. Continue beating on high speed until distinct lines appear, then add the remaining caster sugar.  
5. Switch to medium-low speed to refine the bubbles, beat until the meringue forms short, stiff, or slightly curved peaks when the beater is lifted.  
   - Judgment: Short, straight, or slightly curved peaks; do not over-whip, or it will crack during rolling.  
   - Key: Avoid beating on high speed throughout; low speed refinement makes the meringue more stable.  

### Mixing the Batter and Baking  
1. Add 1/3 of the meringue to the warm egg yolk batter and quickly mix in a circular motion with a whisk until combined.  
2. Pour the batter back into the bowl with the remaining meringue, fold a few times with a spatula, then use a cutting and folding motion, mixing gently and quickly about 40 times until the batter is uniform, glossy, and free of meringue lumps.  
   - Key: Mix quickly to minimize deflating; ensure the spatula reaches the bottom of the bowl.  
3. Pour the batter from a height into the parchment-lined baking tray, gently shake or smooth the surface, and tap the tray twice to release large air bubbles.  
4. Bake in the preheated oven on the middle rack at 190°C for 10 minutes, then reduce to 150°C for 15 minutes.  
   - Principle: High heat first to set, then lower heat to bake through, preventing excessive shrinking.  
5. Immediately tap the tray after removing from the oven to release steam, then lift the cake sheet using the parchment paper onto a cooling rack, loosen the parchment around the edges but keep the bottom, and let cool until warm to the touch.  

### Whipping the Filling Cream and Assembly  
1. Add sugar to the whipping cream, place the bowl over ice water, beat on high speed with an electric mixer until lines appear, then switch to low speed until stiff peaks form (straight peaks when the beater is lifted).  
   - Must whip over ice water; otherwise, it may fail if too warm.  
2. Flip the cooled cake sheet onto new parchment paper, and diagonally cut each end at a 45° angle.  
3. Fill a piping bag with the whipped cream, and wrap the bag with a frozen kitchen towel to prevent hand warmth from melting the cream.  
4. Pipe several lines of cream about 1cm from the edge nearest you, creating a "mound" in the center for the filling, and spread the remaining cream evenly.  
5. Using a rolling pin to help roll the parchment, roll the cake from the end with the mound in one smooth motion.  
6. Use a small ruler or scraper to press against the bottom while pulling the parchment inward to tighten the roll, then wrap tightly with parchment paper.  

### Chilling to Set  
Refrigerate the wrapped cake roll for at least 1 hour, then remove the parchment paper, trim off the uneven ends, and slice into pieces to serve.  

## Health Advice  
This sour cream Swiss roll uses a cold meringue method and variable temperature baking to ensure a tender and flexible cake body, making it ideal as an afternoon tea treat. Be careful not to over-whip the meringue, or the cake may crack when rolling. Additionally, always whip the filling cream over ice water to avoid failure in hot weather.
