# Autumn Hued Frosted Cake  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Butter 60g (for bean paste frosting) + 150g (for crumb coat buttercream)  
🫘 Low-sugar white bean paste Approx. 125g (half of a 250g package)  
🥛 Heavy cream Approx. 150g (for filling) + 80g (to be added to buttercream)  
🥭 Mango Appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)  
🍬 Sugar 20g  
🎨 Fruit/vegetable powder or food coloring Appropriate amount (for coloring)  
🍰 Baked cake layer 1 (with a sunken center)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spatula  
🔌 Electric mixer  
🍴 Serrated knife  
🔧 Cake turntable  
🗡️ Offset spatula  
🎽 Piping bag  
🌸 Piping tips (straight tip, curved tip, leaf tip)  
🔩 Piping nail  
📄 Parchment paper  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  

## Instructions  
1. **Make the bean paste frosting**: Soften 60g of butter at room temperature until it can be easily pressed with a finger. Stir it smooth with a spatula. Add about 125g of low-sugar white bean paste and mix until completely smooth and even. Add fruit/vegetable powder or food coloring as needed for color. Cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying.  
2. **Make the crumb coat buttercream**: Soften 150g of butter at room temperature. Add 20g of sugar and beat with an electric mixer until pale and fluffy. Gradually add 80g of heavy cream in batches, beating well after each addition, until a stable buttercream forms.  
3. **Make decorative bean paste flowers**: Fill a piping bag fitted with the appropriate tip with the colored bean paste frosting. Attach a small piece of parchment paper to a piping nail. Holding the piping nail, pipe rose shapes or five-petal flowers. Place the finished flowers (still on the piping nail) into the freezer until fully hardened, then carefully remove them from the parchment paper.  
4. **Prepare the cake layer and filling**: Use a serrated knife to horizontally slice the sunken cake layer into two even layers. Place one layer on a turntable. Spread a generous amount of heavy cream whipped to stiff peaks into the sunken center, level it, arrange mango cubes on top, and spread another layer of stiff cream. Place the second cake layer on top. The filling cream must be whipped stiff enough to support the fruit and the top cake layer.  
5. **Apply the crumb coat**: Using an offset spatula, apply the buttercream evenly to the sides of the cake and smooth. Then apply it to the top and smooth. The buttercream is firm and provides good support for the final decoration. Place the crumb-coated cake in the freezer to set.  
6. **Final decoration**: Remove the set cake from the freezer. Optionally, apply a thin layer of light orange or other colored buttercream to the surface for an autumn feel. Using the remaining bean paste frosting, pipe a ring on top. Take the frozen hardened bean paste flowers and arrange them along the ring to form a wreath. Finally, use green bean paste frosting to pipe leaves in the gaps between the flowers as a finishing touch.  

## Health Advice  
This cake uses bean paste frosting to replace some butter, reducing the total calories. Mango provides vitamin C, but the buttercream and butter still contain a high amount of fat, so it is best enjoyed occasionally rather than as a daily snack. The texture is better after being chilled and set, but those with diabetes need to monitor sugar intake.
