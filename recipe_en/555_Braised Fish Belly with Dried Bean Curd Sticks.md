# Braised Fish Belly with Dried Bean Curd Sticks

Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐

## Steps
1. Scale and clean the grass carp belly, then cut into finger-sized strips.
**Key point**: Using the belly part yields a better texture; the type of fish can be substituted according to preference.

2. Add适量 salt, a pinch of sugar, a dash of cooking wine, a dash of oyster sauce, and适量 soy sauce to the fish pieces. Mix well. Then add适量 cornstarch and mix evenly. Set aside to marinate.
**Key point**: Cornstarch helps lock in moisture and seasonings.

3. Place the fried dried bean curd sticks in a bowl and pour in boiling water to soak for about 10 minutes until softened. Drain well.
**Key point**: Use boiling water for rehydration.

4. Peel the garlic, cut the ginger into small pieces, cut the scallion whites into sections, cut the shiitake mushroom into small pieces, and cut the green and red bell peppers into pieces.
5. Heat the wok with plenty of oil. First, fry the ginger pieces over medium heat until fragrant. Then add the garlic cloves and fry until golden and fragrant. Remove and set aside.
**Key point**: The oil used for frying ginger and garlic can be reserved as base oil.

6. When the oil temperature reaches around 140°C (284°F), carefully add the marinated fish pieces one by one. Fry until set and golden brown on the surface. Remove and drain the oil.
**Temperature check**: The fish pieces should be golden and crispy on the outside.
   **Home alternative**: Can be pan-fried with less oil.

7. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Return the fried ginger pieces, garlic cloves, and 10g of braising sauce. Stir-fry over high heat until fragrant. Add the shiitake mushroom pieces and stir-fry. Splash in a dash of cooking wine and soy sauce to deglaze.
**Key point**: Ensure the aromatics are fully released.

8. Add the soaked dried bean curd sticks. Pour in enough water to just cover the bean curd sticks. Reduce to medium heat and cook until the bean curd sticks soften and absorb the flavor.
**Key point**: The bean curd sticks and fish have different cooking times; braising the bean curd sticks first prevents the fish from becoming too soft.

9. Once the bean curd sticks are tender, gently add the fried fish pieces. Add a dash of oyster sauce, salt, sugar, and dark soy sauce for color and seasoning. Braise over medium heat to allow the sauce to meld.
**Key point**: Add the fish pieces now to prevent them from breaking apart from prolonged cooking; a few dried chili peppers can be added for heat.

10. When the sauce thickens, add the scallion sections and green and red bell pepper pieces. Drizzle in a small amount of cornstarch slurry to create a light glaze. Gently shake the wok to coat the ingredients evenly. Finally, drizzle a dash of finishing oil and toss. Transfer to a preheated clay pot for serving.
**Key point**: Avoid vigorous stirring after adding the fish; the cornstarch slurry gives the sauce a glossy finish; the finishing oil enhances the dish's sheen.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, suitable for those needing nutritional supplementation. However, the fried fish pieces and dried bean curd sticks are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables. For individuals with high blood lipids, pan-frying the fish with less oil and reducing the amount of dried bean curd sticks is advisable.
