# Sauce-glazed Tofu
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Tofu, appropriate amount (preferably old tofu or firm tofu, cut into uniform pieces)
🧄 Minced garlic, 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Small chili peppers, 1 tablespoon (sliced into rings)
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (cut into segments)
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (for frying tofu and stir-frying)
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount
🧂 Cumin powder, appropriate amount
🧂 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 teaspoon
🍬 Granulated sugar, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Water starch, appropriate amount (for thickening)
💧 Water, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
♨️ Air fryer (optional alternative)
🥄 Soup spoon
🍢 Skewers (for presentation)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the tofu into uniformly sized pieces, mince the garlic, slice the small chili peppers into rings, and cut the cilantro into segments. Set aside.
2. Pour sufficient oil into the wok and heat to about 60% hot. Add the tofu pieces and fry over medium heat for approximately 6 minutes, until the surface turns golden brown.
   **Key Point**: Ensure the oil temperature is sufficiently high before adding the tofu to prevent sticking. Alternatively, an air fryer can be used at 200°C for 20 minutes.
3. Remove the fried tofu, drain excess oil, and place in a bowl. Set aside.
4. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic, chili flakes, cumin powder, and white sesame seeds. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
5. Add water to the wok, along with light soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, and granulated sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat.
6. Gradually add the water starch in multiple batches while stirring continuously. Cook until the sauce thickens to a desired consistency.
   **Note**: Add the water starch in small amounts and stir constantly to prevent lumps. Stop when the sauce coats the ingredients well.
7. Add the small chili pepper rings and cilantro segments to the thickened sauce. Stir until well combined.
8. Pour the prepared sauce over the fried tofu while it is still hot, ensuring the tofu is thoroughly coated. Serve immediately.
   **Key Point**: The sauce must be poured while hot so that the tofu absorbs the flavors fully, resulting in a crispy exterior, tender interior, and rich aroma.

## Health Suggestions
Tofu is an excellent source of plant-based protein. When fried, it becomes crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making it suitable for vegetarians or those on a weight-loss diet in moderation. Ensure the oil temperature is sufficiently high to minimize oil absorption. Reducing the amount of salt and oyster sauce in the sauce can make the dish healthier.
