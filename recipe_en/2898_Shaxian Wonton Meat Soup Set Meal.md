# Shaxian Wonton Meat Soup Set Meal
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Wonton meat 1 pack (Shaxian style)  
🦪 Century eggs 2 (cut into even pieces)  
🥓 Chinese sausage 1 (sliced diagonally)  
🍞 Steamed buns 5 (sliced)  
🥬 Lettuce as needed (torn and washed)  
🧂 Cooking oil as needed (for frying buns/sausage, sautéing scallions, and drizzling garlic)  
🧅 Scallion whites as needed (cut into sections for stir-frying, minced for sauce)  
🧄 Minced garlic as needed (for blanching sauce)  
🫙 Steamed fish soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🍶 Aged vinegar 1 tbsp  
🍬 White sugar a pinch  
🥬 Winter preserved vegetables as needed (core ingredient for Shaxian wonton soup)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (for blanching lettuce)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
♨️ Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🔪 Chef's knife + Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls/Plates  
🥢 Chopsticks + Spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Fry bun slices and sausage slices**: Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat to 50% oil temperature. Add the bun slices, frying and turning them frequently. Fry for about 1 minute until golden and crispy, then remove. Next, add the sausage slices and fry until the edges curl and a sweet wine aroma is released, then remove. Arrange together with the cut century eggs on a plate.  
**Note**: If the oil amount is relatively small, turn frequently to avoid burning.

2. **Cook wonton meat soup**: Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, add the scallion white sections and stir-fry until fragrant. Add 1 bowl of water and some winter preserved vegetables. Once the water boils, add the wonton meat one by one. Cook over medium-high heat for about 3 minutes until the outer skin becomes translucent.  
**Key point**: Do not add the wonton meat before the water boils, to prevent sticking and falling apart; winter preserved vegetables are the soul ingredient for the broth.

3. **Prepare blanching sauce**: In a small bowl, add minced garlic and minced scallion whites. Pour in hot oil to release the aroma. While still hot, add steamed fish soy sauce, aged vinegar, and a pinch of white sugar. Stir well and set aside.

4. **Blanch lettuce**: In a separate pot, bring water to a boil, add a pinch of salt and a few drops of oil. Once boiling, add the lettuce and blanch until the color turns bright and vibrant green. Immediately remove and drain, then place in the center of the plate with century eggs and fried sausage. Evenly drizzle the blanching sauce on top.  
**Key point**: Do not add the lettuce before the water boils, to prevent it from turning yellow; be sure to drain thoroughly before drizzling the sauce.

5. **Plate assembly**: Serve the fried bun slices on a separate plate. Ladle the wonton meat soup into a bowl. The set meal is complete.

## Health Advice  
This set meal has a relatively balanced ratio of protein and vegetables, but the fried bun slices and fried sausage significantly increase the calorie content. It is recommended to control portions and enjoy it as an occasional treat. The light wonton soup and lettuce can help balance the greasiness; be careful not to drizzle too much blanching sauce to avoid excessive sodium intake.
