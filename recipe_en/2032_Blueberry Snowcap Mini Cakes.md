# Blueberry Snowcap Mini Cakes
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Plain yogurt 1.2L (after filtering, yields about 500g thick yogurt cheese)
🫐 Fresh blueberries 120g
🍪 Oreo cookies 13 pieces (90g, remove filling, use only the black cookie part)
🧈 Butter 40g
🍮 Gelatin sheets 20g (4 sheets; 10g for mousse liquid, 10g for milk cap)
🧂 Granulated sugar 45g
🍋 Lemon juice Juice of half a lemon

## Tools & Equipment
✂️ Plastic beverage bottle (cut open to use as mold)
🎀 Plastic wrap, rubber bands (seal bottom to prevent leakage)
🥣 Large bowl, steamer rack or strainer
🧻 Face towel or cheesecloth (for filtering yogurt)
🍽️ Microwave
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🍾 Rolling pin or bottle (to crush cookies)
🍳 Pot, stove (to melt butter)
🥄 Spoon, large basin
🧊 Refrigerator
🍽️ Plate, small spoon

## Steps
1.  **Prepare yogurt cheese:** Place plain yogurt in a steamer rack lined with a face towel or cheesecloth, set over a bowl, wrap well, and refrigerate overnight to drain the whey, yielding thick yogurt cheese. Reserve about 200g for the milk cap and use the rest for the mousse liquid.
2.  **Make blueberry sauce:** Wash the blueberries, add granulated sugar and lemon juice, cover with plastic wrap, and microwave on high for 2 minutes. Remove, take off the plastic wrap, and microwave for another 3 minutes, stirring until it becomes a slightly thick jam-like consistency. Let cool completely.
    **Key point:** The blueberry sauce must be completely cooled; hot sauce will destroy the gelatin's setting ability.
3.  **Make the cookie crust:** Remove the filling from the Oreo cookies and crush them into fine crumbs. Pour in the melted butter and mix well. Divide the mixture into 8 homemade molds, press down firmly with a flat tool, and refrigerate to set.
4.  **Mix the mousse liquid:** Soak 10g of gelatin sheets in ice water until soft, then drain. Add to the completely cooled blueberry sauce and stir until melted. Then, pour the blueberry-gelatin mixture into the reserved yogurt cheese and stir until smooth to create a purple mousse liquid.
    **Note:** If the blueberry sauce is still slightly warm, let it cool a bit more before mixing with the yogurt cheese.
5.  **Pour into molds and refrigerate:** Pour the mousse liquid into the molds over the set cookie crusts. Refrigerate until fully set. Alternatively, freeze for an ice cream-like texture without ice crystals. If frozen too hard, let it sit in the refrigerator for a while before serving.
6.  **Make the yogurt milk cap:** Soak another 10g of gelatin sheets until soft, then melt completely using the microwave or a water bath. First, mix a small portion of the reserved thick yogurt with the melted gelatin, then pour in the remaining yogurt and mix until fully combined.
7.  **Unmold and decorate:** Once the mousse is set, carefully cut open the sides of the molds with scissors to unmold. Drizzle the yogurt milk cap over the top and garnish with two fresh blueberries.

## Health Tips
This Blueberry Snowcap Mini Cake uses yogurt cheese as a substitute for cream, making it lower in calories. However, the cookie crust and gelatin still contain some calories. It is suitable for those who want to enjoy dessert without worrying too much about weight gain. Blueberries are rich in antioxidants. Be sure to let the blueberry sauce cool completely, otherwise it will affect the gelatin's setting ability.
