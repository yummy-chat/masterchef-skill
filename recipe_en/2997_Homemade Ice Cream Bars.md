# Homemade Ice Cream Bars
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Whole Milk 2 cartons (500ml total)
🍬 White Granulated Sugar approx. 50g
🌾 Corn Starch approx. 20g
🍼 Milk Powder approx. 80g
🍫 Dark Chocolate approx. 43g
🍪 Oreo Cookies 1 small pack (approx. 3-4 pieces, crushed)
🍫 Chocolate (for the shell) approx. 120g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-Stick Pan
🔥 Gas Stove
🥄 Silicone Spatula
🥛 Milk Cartons (used as ice cream bar molds)
✂️ Scissors
📡 Microwave
☕ Ceramic Cup
👝 Piping Bag (homemade or store-bought)
🎒 Resealable Bag (for homemade piping bag)

## Instructions
1. Prepare the ice cream bar molds: Cut open the tops of two milk cartons and rinse the insides clean. Reinforce the cut edges with tape to prevent leakage when pouring the mixture.
2. Measure dry ingredients: Measure out 50g white granulated sugar, 20g corn starch, and 80g milk powder separately.
   **Key Point**: Corn starch gives the ice cream a smooth, creamy texture with no ice crystals. It can be substituted with rice flour or glutinous rice flour.
3. Make the base milk mixture: Pour both cartons of whole milk, the white granulated sugar, corn starch, and milk powder into the non-stick pan. Do not turn on the heat yet. Stir with a silicone spatula until no dry powder remains, to prevent the starch from clumping.
4. Cook the milk mixture until thick: Turn the heat to low. Stir continuously with the silicone spatula while heating, about 5–10 minutes, until the mixture thickens and can cling to the spatula or pan walls, resembling sweetened condensed milk.
   **Key Point**: Stir constantly throughout the process to prevent scorching on the bottom.
5. Make the chocolate ice cream bars: Add the crushed dark chocolate (approx. 43g) to the hot milk mixture. Stir until the chocolate is completely melted and incorporated using the residual heat.
6. Set and freeze: Pour the chocolate milk mixture into the milk cartons, insert ice cream sticks, and place in the freezer until completely frozen solid.
7. Make the chocolate shell: Place the chocolate (approx. 120g) in a ceramic cup. Heat in the microwave on medium power for about 1 minute, until melted. Stir until smooth. Allow the melted chocolate to cool to near hand temperature before use; if it is too hot, it will melt the ice cream.
8. Dip the shell: Remove the fully frozen ice cream bars from the milk cartons. Holding the stick, quickly dip the bar into the melted chocolate, then lift it out immediately. Let it sit for about 10 seconds for the shell to set.
   **Key Point**: The dipping action must be quick.
9. Decorate: Transfer any remaining melted chocolate to a piping bag. Pipe decorative lines onto the surface of the shell.

## Health Tips
This homemade ice cream uses corn starch to replace ice crystals, resulting in a smooth texture, perfect for a summer treat. Be mindful of the sugar content; it is suitable for those who enjoy sweets but wish to reduce additives. Wash your hands after eating to avoid any residue.
