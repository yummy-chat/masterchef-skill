# Lamb Skewers
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Lamb hind leg (knuckle area) Appropriate amount, cut into cubes about 1.5 cm  
🧈 Lamb fat Appropriate amount, cut into pieces  
🧅 Onion 1, sliced  
🫚 Ginger Appropriate amount, sliced  
🥚 Egg 1  
🧂 Salt Appropriate amount (lightly seasoned)  
🌿 Ground white pepper Appropriate amount  
🍛 Curry block 1 whole piece  
🌱 Coarsely ground black pepper Appropriate amount  
🌾 Raw cumin seeds Appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili flakes Appropriate amount  

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok, spatula  
♨️ Oven  
🥘 Baking tray  
🗞️ Aluminum foil  
🖌️ Basting brush  
📌 Red willow or bamboo skewers  

## Steps
1.  Cut the lamb hind leg and lamb fat into evenly sized cubes of about 1.5 cm, cutting along or through internal sinews; slice the onion, and thinly slice the ginger.
**Key**: Keep meat cubes uniform in size to ensure even cooking during roasting.

2.  Place the meat and fat cubes in a bowl. Add the sliced onion, ginger slices, a small amount of salt, and ground white pepper. Crack in the egg. Use your hands to mix thoroughly and evenly. Marinate for 10 minutes.
**Key**: Use less salt initially to leave room for later basting; the egg tenderizes the meat, making additional tenderizer unnecessary.

3.  Pour a little oil into a wok. When the oil reaches about 40% heat, add the coarsely ground black pepper and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the curry block. Cook over low heat until completely melted and well combined. Set aside as the basting sauce.
**Key**: Be sure to cook slowly over low heat to prevent the curry or pepper from burning.

4.  Thread the marinated meat onto skewers, alternating lean meat and fat, leaving small gaps between pieces. If using bamboo skewers, wrap the ends in aluminum foil to prevent burning.
**Key**: Do not thread the skewers too tightly, as the meat will shrink and compress during roasting.

5.  Preheat the oven to 230°C. Arrange the lamb skewers flat on a baking tray. Place the tray on the middle rack of the oven and roast for about 20 minutes, until the surface is slightly browned and charred, fat has rendered, and edges are crisp.
**Key**: The middle rack ensures even heat distribution and prevents the surface from blackening too quickly.

6.  Remove the baking tray. While hot, use a basting brush to evenly coat each skewer with the prepared curry sauce. Then, sprinkle with raw cumin seeds and chili flakes to taste.
**Note**: Basting while hot allows the sauce to adhere better; adjust the amount of seasoning to your preference.

7.  Return the basted lamb skewers to the oven and roast again at 230°C for about 5 minutes, until the cumin aroma is strong and the sauce has dried and adhered to the meat. Serve immediately.
**Note**: Do not over-roast during this second round, as the meat may become dry. This step is only to release the spice flavors and set the sauce.

## Health Tips  
This lamb skewer recipe uses egg to tenderize the meat and an oven for controlled temperature, making it healthier than traditional grilling methods. However, it should still be consumed in moderation. Suitable for meat lovers who need to manage oil intake. Pairs well with cucumber or yogurt to balance the richness.
