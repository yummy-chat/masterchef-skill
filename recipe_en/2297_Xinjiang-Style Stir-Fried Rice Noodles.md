# Xinjiang-Style Stir-Fried Rice Noodles
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef tenderloin 100g (slice thinly against the grain, can substitute with boneless chicken thigh)  
🌶️ Spicy dried chili peppers (Xinjiang meat chili/pig intestine chili) 50g (dried red chilies)  
🥬 Celery 100g (cut into sections, split thick stalks in half)  
🥒 Pickled vegetables 50g (can use Northeast Chinese suancai or Sichuan pickles)  
🧅 Chopped onion 50g  
🧅 Chopped scallion 20g  
🧅 Minced ginger 10g  
🧄 Minced garlic 20g (divided into two portions, add one portion at the end for flavor)  
🍜 Xinjiang thick rice noodles 1 serving (soak for at least 8 hours in advance and cook separately for about 15 minutes)  

**Marinade for meat:**  
🧂 Baking soda 0.5g (can substitute with half an egg white)  
🍬 Sugar 1g  
🍶 Cooking wine 5g  
🍶 Light soy sauce 5g  
🌾 Cornstarch 2g (add after mixing and absorbing)  
🫒 Vegetable oil 10g (add last and mix well to seal in moisture)  

**Stir-fry seasonings:**  
🌶️ Chili paste 20g (pure chili paste; if unavailable, use soaked and blended chilies)  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) 20g  
🫘 Sweet bean paste 20g (can substitute with soybean paste; add sugar to enhance flavor)  
🍅 Tomato paste 20g (pure tomato paste without sugar; if unavailable, substitute with half a fresh tomato)  
🍶 Light soy sauce 10g  
🧂 White pepper powder 0.5g (can substitute with five-spice powder, Sichuan pepper powder, or cumin powder)  
🍶 Dark soy sauce 3g  
🧂 Salt to taste  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking peppers, marinating meat, and holding ingredients)  
✂️ Scissors (for trimming pepper stems)  
🌀 Blender (for blending peppers)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for prepping ingredients)  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥄 Spatula/wok shovel  
🥢 Slotted spoon/strainer (for removing noodles)  
🥄 Spoon (for measuring seasonings)  
🥣 Small bowl/measuring cup (for holding liquid seasonings)  

## Instructions  
### Preparation Stage  
1. Soak 50g of spicy dried peppers in hot water for 30 minutes until soft. Squeeze out excess water and trim off the stems.  
**Key point**: Hot water speeds up rehydration; adjust spiciness with other pepper varieties as desired; be sure to squeeze dry after soaking.  

2. Blend or chop the soaked peppers in a blender until coarsely ground—no need for an overly fine texture.  
**Key point**: Coarse grains retain texture.  

3. Slice 100g of beef tenderloin thinly against the grain.  
**Key point**: Choose beef tenderloin or boneless chicken thigh for tenderness.  

4. Add baking soda, sugar, cooking wine, and light soy sauce to the meat. Mix thoroughly by hand until the liquid is fully absorbed. Then add cornstarch and mix. Finally, add vegetable oil and mix well. Marinate for about 30 minutes.  
**Key point**: Mix ingredients step by step; sealing with oil at the end locks in moisture.  

5. Prepare about 100g of celery cut into sections, 50g pickled vegetables, 50g chopped onion, 20g chopped scallion, 10g minced ginger, and 20g minced garlic.  
**Key point**: For thick celery, split in half before cutting into sections.  

### Cooking Stage  
6. Heat the wok over high heat and add 2-3 times the usual amount of vegetable oil. Add the marinated beef slices. Stir-fry quickly over high heat with cold oil until the edges of the beef are slightly charred. Remove immediately.  
**Key point**: Quickly separate the slices; remove as soon as they change color and are slightly charred to prevent overcooking.  

7. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the blended peppers and stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute until the moisture reduces and the aroma is released.  
**Key point**: Fry off excess moisture to awaken the fragrance.  

8. Add chopped onion, minced ginger, and chopped scallion to the wok. Continue stir-frying over high heat for about 1 minute until fragrant.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry the aromatics until their fragrance is strong.  

9. Add chili paste, doubanjiang, sweet bean paste, and tomato paste. Stir-fry over medium-high heat for about 1 minute until red oil is released and the sauces are well blended and fragrant.  
**Key point**: The sauces must be thoroughly cooked with sufficient red oil.  

10. Add light soy sauce, white pepper powder, and some water. Bring to a boil, then add the pre-cooked thick rice noodles. Return to a boil, then reduce to low heat and cook for about 10 minutes until the noodles have fully absorbed the sauce, are well-flavored, and the sauce thickens.  
**Key point**: The noodles should be pre-soaked and cooked to about 80% doneness.  

11. Add dark soy sauce to adjust the color, making the dish a bright, appealing red.  
12. When the noodles are well-flavored, add the celery sections and pickled vegetables. Keep the wok boiling and cook just until the celery is tender-crisp.  
**Key point**: Vegetables should not be overcooked to preserve texture and color.  

13. Add the previously stir-fried beef slices and cook for about 1 minute to heat the beef through and allow it to absorb some sauce.  
**Key point**: Add the beef last to prevent it from becoming tough.  

14. Turn off the heat, add most of the minced garlic, and quickly toss with chopsticks until well combined.  
**Key point**: Add garlic after turning off the heat; use residual heat to release its aroma. Toss and serve immediately.  

## Health Advice
The combination of beef and vegetables is nutritious, but the sauces and oil content are relatively high. Pair with light vegetables or tea to balance the flavors. Suitable for those who enjoy bold tastes; people with high blood lipids or sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
