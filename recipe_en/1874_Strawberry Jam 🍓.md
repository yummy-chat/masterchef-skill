# Strawberry Jam 🍓
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍓 Strawberries 500g
🍬 Granulated sugar 50g
🍋 Lemon juice 25g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Copper pot
🍯 Glass sealable jar
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥣 White porcelain bowl
🔪 Chef’s knife

## Instructions
1. Wash the strawberries and cut them into small pieces. Place them in a stainless steel bowl, add 50g of granulated sugar, and mix well. Let sit for 2 hours to macerate and release their juices.
2. Pour the macerated strawberries along with their released juices into a copper pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately add 25g of lemon juice.
3. Continue boiling for about 10 minutes, adjusting the heat as needed depending on how much liquid is released. If less liquid is released, reduce to medium or low heat.
4. Fill a white porcelain bowl with cold water and drop a small amount of the boiling jam into it. If the jam firms up into a clump in the cold water without dispersing, the ideal thickness has been reached.
   **Note**: There is no need to skim off any foam on the surface; it will eventually dissolve back into the juice.
5. While still hot, pour the finished strawberry jam into a glass sealable jar and seal it for storage.
   **Note**: This homemade jam has a lower sugar content, so its shelf life is relatively short. It is recommended to consume it as soon as possible.

## Health Advice
This jam has a lower sugar content, and the vitamin C in the strawberries is well preserved. It is suitable for pairing with whole wheat bread for breakfast or mixing into sugar-free yogurt. It can also be enjoyed in moderation during weight loss periods. Store sealed in the refrigerator and consume within one week, as the short shelf life is due to the absence of added preservatives.
