# Old-Style Cooked Cabbage
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Dried vermicelli 70g (use thicker vermicelli for stewing, not Longkou vermicelli)
🥬 Large Chinese cabbage leaves 320g (about 6 leaves with some stem, tear into large pieces by hand)
🥩 Pork belly 100g (slice into thin pieces)
🌿 Cilantro 20g (remove leaves, cut into small sections)
🧅 Scallion 10g (cut into chopped scallions)
🫚 Ginger 5g (slice or julienne)
🧄 Garlic 15g (three cloves, slice)
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments to taste (add according to preference)
⭐ Star anise 1 piece
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (about 10g for soaking vermicelli, additional for cooking)
🧂 Dark soy sauce about 5g (use in two stages: half a spoon for soaking vermicelli, about 1/3 spoon for seasoning)
🍶 Aged vinegar 30g (about three spoons)
🧂 Light soy sauce 30g (about three spoons)
🍬 White sugar 2g (about 2/3 teaspoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g (half a spoon)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g (1 teaspoon)
🧂 Salt about 1-3g (about 1g for stir-frying cabbage to build a base flavor, add 1-2g before finishing according to taste)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking vermicelli, holding ingredients)
🥄 Colander (to drain vermicelli)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
✂️ Scissors (to cut vermicelli)
🥄 Wok spatula/wooden spoon
🥄 Soup spoon/rice spoon (to measure seasonings)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place dried vermicelli in a basin, pour in boiling water until completely submerged. Soak until the vermicelli softens, becomes translucent, and can be easily broken with fingers. Drain well, use scissors to cut into manageable lengths, and set aside in a basin.
   **Key Point**: For a chewier texture, soak in cold water for 6-7 hours in advance; after draining, mix with 1 spoon of cooking oil and half a spoon of dark soy sauce to prevent sticking and add color.
2. Hand-tear the cabbage leaves into large pieces and place them in a basin.
3. Cut scallion into chopped scallions, slice or julienne ginger, slice garlic, cut dried chili peppers into segments, and cut cilantro stems into sections. Place scallions, ginger, garlic, and dried chili peppers together for later use.
4. Slice pork belly into thin pieces and set aside with the star anise.

## Cooking Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Stir-fry Cabbage**: Heat the wok and add a layer of cooking oil. When the oil begins to lightly smoke, switch to medium-high heat. Add the torn cabbage and stir-fry quickly. Add about 1/3 teaspoon of salt to help the cabbage release water. Stir-fry until the cabbage softens, shrinks in volume, and the edges become slightly charred. Remove and set aside. No need to clean the wok.
   **Note**: Do not add water throughout the process; rely on the cabbage's own moisture for stir-frying.
2. **Stir-fry Pork and Aromatics**: Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil to the same wok, heat over high heat. Add the pork belly slices and star anise, quickly breaking them apart. Stir-fry until the pork belly is slightly charred and oil is rendered, then immediately add scallions, ginger, garlic, and dried chili pepper segments. Continue stir-frying over high heat until fragrant.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Mix and Season**: Once the aroma is released, return the previously stir-fried cabbage to the wok. Drizzle aged vinegar along the side of the wok, stir-fry quickly to release the vinegar aroma. Then drizzle light soy sauce along the side of the wok, continuing to stir-fry over high heat to bring out the soy sauce fragrance. Reduce heat to low, add dark soy sauce, sugar, oyster sauce, and chicken bouillon. Mix and stir the seasonings evenly.
2. **Add Vermicelli and Reduce Sauce**: Pour the prepared vermicelli into the wok, immediately turn the heat back to high. Stir-fry quickly, allowing the vermicelli to fully absorb the sauce and flavors from the wok until there is virtually no sauce left at the bottom.
   **Key Point**: Taste and adjust saltiness; if needed, add an additional 1-2 grams of salt. For a lighter flavor, no additional salt is required.
3. Once the sauce is absorbed, add the cilantro segments, stir-fry briefly, then turn off the heat immediately. Plate and serve.

## Health Recommendations
This dish provides dietary fiber mainly from cabbage, carbohydrates from vermicelli, and protein and fat from pork belly, offering strong satiety, making it suitable for individuals with high physical exertion. However, attention should be paid to controlling total calorie intake, and it is recommended to pair with a light, low-oil, and low-salt cold dish to balance nutrition.
