# Stuffed Long Peppers
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌶️ Green long peppers, as needed (washed, cut in half lengthwise from the middle)  
🥩 Pork filling, as needed (minced until fine and sticky)  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (some minced for the filling, some cut into sections for sautéing)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (minced for the filling)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (slightly more, minced for sautéing)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (for seasoning the filling and enhancing the sauce)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, as needed (for color in the filling and sauce)  
🧂 Salt, about 3.5g (for the filling, optional) + about 4g (for base flavor in the sauce)  
🌿 Black pepper, as needed (a bit more to remove off-flavors and enhance aroma)  
🍬 White sugar, as needed (to enhance freshness in the filling) + 17g (in the sauce, ratio 1:2 with vinegar)  
🍶 5° aromatic vinegar, 30g (in the sauce, sweet-sour ratio 1:2)  
🧂 Premium light soy sauce, 18g (to boost freshness in the sauce)  
💧 Water, about 1/10 of the pork filling amount (for moisture in the filling) + 50g (for simmering in the sauce)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for greasing the pan, frying, and sautéing aromatics; use less oil for frying)  
🌾 Dried starch, as needed (to seal the filling after stuffing to prevent it from falling out)  
🌊 Water starch, as needed (for final thickening, optional)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok  
🪵 Spatula  
⚖️ Scale (for weighing sauce seasonings)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the Peppers**: Wash the peppers and remove the stems. Carefully cut them in half lengthwise from the middle, avoiding damage to the pepper bodies.  
2. **Season the Pork Filling**: Minced pork filling until fine and sticky. Add minced scallion and ginger, light soy sauce, black pepper, white sugar, and dark soy sauce. Stir in one direction until well combined and firm. If the filling seems dry, add about one-tenth of the filling amount of water, then beat in one egg and mix well.  
   - **Key Point**: Do not add any oil to the filling throughout the process. Maintain a consistent stirring direction to allow the meat fibers to fully lock in moisture.  
3. **Prepare the Aromatics**: Cut scallion into sections. Mince the garlic.  
4. **Make the Sweet-Sour Sauce**: In a bowl, combine 17g white sugar and 30g aromatic vinegar. Add 18g premium light soy sauce, a little dark soy sauce, about 4g salt, and 50g water. Stir well and set aside.  
   - **Note**: Do not add water starch to the sauce in advance.  
5. **Stuff the Peppers**: Fill the split peppers with the pork filling, using an amount about half the thickness of the pepper. Press tightly at the opening, then coat with a layer of dried starch to seal.  
   - **Key Point**: Use a moderate amount of filling, press it firmly, and the dried starch will effectively prevent the filling from falling out during frying.  
6. **Fry the Peppers**: Heat the wok thoroughly, add a small amount of oil to coat the pan, and ensure the oil temperature is not too high. Wipe the pepper surfaces dry, place them in the wok over low heat, and maintain medium-low heat for slow frying.  
   - **Doneness Check**: Fry until the pepper skins develop a tiger-skin pattern.  
   - **Key Point**: Keep the wok hot and oil at a moderate temperature to prevent splattering; cook over medium-low heat throughout for even heating and to avoid oil absorption; fry in batches to prevent sticking.  
7. **Sauté the Aromatics**: Remove the fried peppers from the wok. Leave a very small amount of oil in the wok, reduce to low heat, and add scallion sections and minced garlic. Sauté slowly until fragrant.  
   - **Doneness Check**: The garlic should turn slightly yellow and release a rich aroma.  
   - **Key Point**: Use minimal oil and low heat to prevent the garlic from burning.  
8. **Simmer and Reduce the Sauce**: Return the fried peppers to the wok, turn the heat to high, and pour the prepared sweet-sour sauce along the edge of the wok. Keep the heat high to quickly bring the sauce to a boil and coat the ingredients.  
   - **Doneness Check**: The sauce should nearly submerge the filling and gradually thicken.  
   - **Key Point**: Cook quickly over high heat for a short time to keep the filling tender and smooth. When the vinegar aroma has nearly dissipated and the sauce is thick, if a glossy finish is desired, drizzle in a little water starch, toss quickly to combine evenly, and remove from the wok.  

## Health Advice  
Stuffed long peppers are rich in protein. The combination of pork filling and eggs provides balanced nutrition, making it suitable for muscle building or as a flavor variation. Use less oil when frying. Peppers are high in vitamin C, so avoid prolonged cooking to preserve texture. Be mindful of portion sizes.
