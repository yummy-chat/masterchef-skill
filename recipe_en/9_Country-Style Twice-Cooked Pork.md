# Country-Style Twice-Cooked Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork hind leg 1 piece (about one large piece)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper 1 (seeded and sliced)  
🥬 Garlic sprouts 1 small bunch (lightly crushed and cut diagonally into sections)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (lightly crushed for boiling meat, some minced)  
🌶️ Pickled chilies a few (chopped)  
🧅 Scallion 1 (cut into sections)  
🫘 Fermented black beans a few  
🥃 Yellow wine to taste  
🌿 Green Sichuan peppercorns a few  
🪵 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece  
🫒 Rapeseed oil a little  
🫘 Red oil chili bean paste to taste  
🍬 White sugar a little  
🥄 Light soy sauce to taste  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔥 Blowtorch (or iron pan for scorching the skin)  
🧽 Steel wool  
🔪 Chef knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Large iron wok  
🫖 Wooden lid  
🥣 Seasoning bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Hold the whole piece of pork hind leg skin-side up and use a blowtorch to burn the surface until charred black.  
2. Submerge the charred pork in clean water and scrub with steel wool to remove the burnt layer. Rinse and drain.  
3. Cut the cleaned pork into large strips. Add enough cold water to a large iron wok, place the pork strips in, and add the lightly crushed ginger, scallion sections, yellow wine, green Sichuan peppercorns, and cinnamon stick. Cover with the lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook until a chopstick can easily pierce through the pork. Remove.  
4. Immediately submerge the cooked pork in ice water and cool to room temperature to reduce oil splatter during subsequent stir-frying.  
5. Slice the cooled pork into thin slices about 3 mm thick. Ensure even thickness; too thick will be greasy.  
6. Mince the ginger, chop the pickled chilies, and place them with the fermented black beans in a small bowl for later use.  
7. Heat the large iron wok, add a little rapeseed oil to coat, then add the pork slices. Stir-fry over medium-high heat until the slices are slightly curled, the fat portion is translucent, and excess oil has rendered out.  
8. Add the minced ginger, chopped pickled chilies, and fermented black beans, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the red oil chili bean paste and stir-fry until the red oil releases, coating the meat slices evenly.  
9. Add the sliced red bell pepper and stir-fry until just cooked. Add a little white sugar to enhance freshness.  
10. Add the garlic sprout sections and stir-fry evenly. Drizzle light soy sauce and fragrant vinegar along the edge of the wok, stir-fry quickly to combine, then remove from heat.  
**Key Tip**: Maintain medium-high heat throughout; the meat slices are best when stir-fried until slightly curled. Since the chili bean paste contains salt, the amounts of light soy sauce and fragrant vinegar should be adjusted accordingly.  

## Health Advice  
This twice-cooked pork has a high fat content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or multigrain rice. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure. People with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
