# Boiled Lettuce with Soy Sauce (Bai Zhuo You Mai Cai)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Lettuce (you mai cai) - appropriate amount (washed and cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic - appropriate amount (minced)  
🧅 Scallion (white part) - appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper - appropriate amount (cut into thin strips)  
🫒 Cooking oil - appropriate amount (used twice)  
🥣 Steamed fish soy sauce - appropriate amount  
🫗 Aged vinegar - appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil - appropriate amount  
🌶️ Chili oil - appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt - appropriate amount (about one spoonful)  
🥛 White vinegar - appropriate amount (about one spoonful)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪚 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🕳️ Strainer  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Wash the lettuce and cut into sections about the length of a finger.  
2. Place minced garlic, minced scallion (white part), and red bell pepper strips in a bowl.  
3. Heat cooking oil until it reaches 60% of its smoking point, pour it over the garlic, scallion, and red bell pepper to release their aroma, then add steamed fish soy sauce, aged vinegar, sesame oil, and chili oil. Stir well to make the blanching sauce and set aside.  
**Key point**: The oil should be at 60% of its smoking point to fully release the aroma of the aromatics.  

4. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add one spoonful of salt and one spoonful of white vinegar.  
**Key point**: Adding salt and white vinegar is the core technique to keep the lettuce bright green and crunchy.  

5. Add the lettuce and blanch over high heat for about 40 seconds. Once the vegetable changes color and is just cooked, remove immediately.  
**Note**: Keep the heat high throughout to avoid overcooking and softening.  

6. Use a strainer to remove the lettuce, drain it thoroughly, and arrange it neatly on a plate.  
7. Evenly pour the prepared blanching sauce over the lettuce and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Boiled lettuce with soy sauce is a low-calorie, high-fiber option suitable for those on a weight-loss or blood pressure control diet. Adding salt and white vinegar during blanching helps lock in the green color and crunch, but use the sauce sparingly to avoid excessive sodium intake.
