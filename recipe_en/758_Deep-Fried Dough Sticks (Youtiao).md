# Deep-Fried Dough Sticks (Youtiao)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g  
🥚 Egg 1  
🥛 Milk 50g (if unavailable, substitute with 45g water)  
💧 Water 70g  
🌾 Dry flour as needed (to prevent sticking during rolling and shaping)  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍬 White sugar 5g  
🥄 Baking soda 1g  
🥄 Aluminum-free baking powder 5g  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🍽️ Cutting board (silicone mat)  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
Rolling pin  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Large wok  
Slotted spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the flour, salt, white sugar, baking soda, and aluminum-free baking powder in a large bowl. Mix thoroughly until evenly combined.  
   **Key point**: Baking soda and baking powder are essential and their proportions cannot be altered.

2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients. Crack in the egg, then add the milk and water. Use chopsticks to stir the liquid into the flour mixture until it forms flaky pieces, then use your hands to help gather all the dry ingredients into a rough dough.  
   **Note**: Avoid stirring in circular motions throughout this process to prevent overworking the dough, which would affect the final crispiness.

3. Grease the cutting board with a layer of oil, and also oil the back of your hands. Place the dough on the board, form a fist, and use the flat of your fist to press and flatten the dough. Then fold it as follows: fold the top third down, the bottom third up, the left third to the right, and the right third to the left. After each fold, press it flat again with your fist.  
   **Key point**: Use pressing (instead of kneading) to organize the gluten without overworking it.

4. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 10 minutes.

5. Remove the plastic wrap and repeat the pressing, folding, and flattening process once. Cover again with plastic wrap and let rest for another 10 minutes. The dough should gradually become smoother.

6. Repeat the pressing and folding process one more time. Shape the dough into a smooth rectangle, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for a final 20 minutes.

7. Roll the dough up from one end into a long log. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap, twisting the ends to secure it. Allow for a long resting period: rest at room temperature for 4-8 hours, or rest in the refrigerator for 8-16 hours.  
   **Note**: Resting for too long will cause the baking powder to lose effectiveness, preventing the dough sticks from puffing up; during hot weather or high temperatures, refrigeration is recommended.

8. Sprinkle a generous amount of dry flour on the cutting board. Remove the rested dough and gently roll it out with a rolling pin into a rectangular sheet about 0.5 cm thick, 10-11 cm wide, and 60 cm long. Trim off the irregular edges, then cut the dough into strips about two fingers wide.

9. Dip a chopstick in water and draw a vertical line of water down the center of every other strip of dough. Pick up the strips without the water line and place them on top of the strips with the water line, pressing the two pieces together firmly.

10. Use the back of a knife to press down firmly along the center of the stacked strips lengthwise. You may also trim off any uneven ends with the knife.

11. Pour a sufficient amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat it to about 180°C.

12. Take one dough stick piece. Gently stretch it with both hands to a suitable length. First, dip the middle part into the oil, then simultaneously release both ends, allowing the dough stick to slide naturally into the oil.  
    **Key point**: The dough stick should float to the surface within 3-4 seconds of entering the oil. Immediately use chopsticks to turn and rotate it constantly, ensuring even heating and rapid expansion.

13. Once the dough stick no longer expands significantly, turn it occasionally and fry until both sides are an even golden brown. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a rack.  
    **Note**: You can fry 3-4 pieces at a time, but wait for one piece to float before adding the next to prevent them from sticking together.

## Health Advice  
While youtiao are delicious, high-temperature frying destroys B vitamins and increases oil intake. It is recommended to pair them with soy milk or a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Those with high blood lipids or high blood pressure should only have a small taste.
