# Snakehead Fish Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Snakehead fish (live fish) Approximately 1500g (2 fish)  
🧅 Ginger As needed (partially smashed for ginger-scallion water, partially sliced)  
🟢 Scallion leaves As needed (smashed for ginger-scallion water)  
🥚 Egg white 1  
🌾 Potato starch About 20g  
🫒 Vegetable oil About 10g (for marinating)  
🍎 Red dates 7 (cut into small pieces, pitted)  
🍒 Goji berries As needed  
🟢 Scallion As needed (rub soft for soup, remove at the end)  
🧂 Salt As needed (slightly more for cleaning, 1 spoon for marinating, adjust to taste at the end)  
🌶️ White pepper A small half spoon (for marinating), adjust to taste when seasoning  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar As needed (optional, for blanching fish bones)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪵 Round thick wooden cutting board  
✂️ Scissors  
🔪 Chef's knife (Chinese slicing knife)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (2, one for fish fillets, one for fish bones)  
🥄 Stainless steel slotted spoon (for draining)  
🍳 Wok (Chinese iron wok)  
🥄 Wok spatula (for stirring, crushing, and skimming)  
🕳️ Fine-mesh strainer (fine strain, for filtering fish soup)  
🕳️ Strainer basket (to assist filtering)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut open the snakehead fish from the belly, trim off the dorsal and ventral fins, and remove the small bones. Horizontally slice off the "hard side" with the backbone, then chop the backbone, head, and tail into small pieces. For the "soft side" with ribs, slice off the ribs at an angle and chop them into small pieces as well. For the remaining pure meat, thinly slice at a 30-degree angle.
**Key**: Distinguish between the "hard side" and "soft side"; the thinner the fillets, the better the texture.

2. Place the fish bones and fillets in separate bowls, add an appropriate amount of salt, mix, then add clean water and repeatedly wash by hand, changing the water several times until it runs clear. Use a slotted spoon to remove and squeeze out as much water as possible.
**Key**: Repeatedly wash until the blood is completely gone, which helps remove the fishy smell.

3. Marinate the fish fillets: Combine the smashed ginger and scallion leaves with water, squeeze to extract flavor, making ginger-scallion water. Add ginger-scallion water, 1 spoon of salt, and a small half spoon of white pepper to the fish fillets. Mix in one direction until the liquid is absorbed. Add the egg white and mix well, then add the potato starch and mix evenly. Finally, drizzle in the vegetable oil and gently mix to seal in moisture.
4. Prepare the accompaniments: Cut the red dates into small pieces, pitted; set aside the goji berries; slice the ginger; rub the scallions soft and set aside. Rubbed scallions release flavor more easily when cooking the soup.
5. Blanch the fish bones: Add clean water to the wok, place the washed fish bones in, optionally add some fragrant vinegar, cook over medium heat until done, then remove and drain. This step further removes the fishy smell.
6. Fry and process the fish bones: Heat the wok, oil the surface, then add a base layer of oil and cold oil. Add the blanched fish bones, reduce heat to low, and slowly fry for about 6 minutes. During this time, move the wok to ensure even heating, and turn the bones once after 3 minutes. When the bones are fragrant and golden, use the spatula to thoroughly crush the fish bones in the wok, and you can switch to high heat to burst the aroma.
**Key**: Fully fry and crush the fish bones; this is the foundation for a rich, milky white soup.

7. Cook the fish soup: Add enough **boiling water** to the crushed fish bones, add the ginger slices and rubbed scallions, bring to a boil over high heat, and skim off the foam. Reduce to medium heat and cook for 5 minutes, until the soup turns thick and milky white. Use a fine-mesh strainer to discard all fish bones, fish spines, scallions, and ginger, keeping only the pure white soup.
**Key**: Boiling water is essential for a milky white soup; filtering must be thorough.

8. Add the red date pieces and goji berries to the filtered white soup, and cook for 2 minutes. During this time, continue to skim off any foam and excess oil.
9. Season and add the fish fillets: Season the soup with an appropriate amount of salt and white pepper to taste. Briefly remove the wok from the heat, then slide the marinated fish fillets in one by one or scattered. Return the wok to the heat and cook over medium heat until the soup comes to a boil.
**Key**: The wok must be removed from the heat when adding the fish fillets to prevent the batter from falling off, ensuring a tender and smooth texture.

10. Once the soup boils, immediately remove from heat and serve in a bowl. Enjoy.

## Health Suggestions
This snakehead fish soup is rich in high-quality protein and collagen, making it suitable for post-surgery recovery or individuals with weak constitutions. Crushing the fish bones and boiling them thoroughly is key to achieving a milky white soup; filtering must be thorough to avoid small bones. Note that the fish fillets should be added while the wok is off the heat and gently poached to maintain tenderness, preventing the fillets from breaking apart and affecting the texture.
