# Oat Pocket Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Rolled oats 50g
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking oats)
🍞 Dry yeast 2g
🌾 Flour 200g
🧂 Salt 2g (added to dough)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (added to dough)
💧 Water, as needed (for kneading, add gradually until dough is soft and slightly sticky)
🧂 Sea salt, a pinch (for salad dressing)
⚫ Black pepper, a pinch (for salad dressing)
🍇 Balsamic vinegar, as needed (for salad dressing)
🫒 Olive oil, as needed (for salad dressing)
🌿 Pesto, as needed (for pesto beef filling)
🥚 Scrambled eggs, as needed (for scrambled egg and ham filling)

## Utensils & Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for soaking oats, mixing, and fermenting dough)
🪥 Spatula (for mixing)
🧫 Plastic wrap (for covering during fermentation)
🧻 Damp cloth (for covering during proofing)
🪓 Rolling pin (for rolling out bread)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for dry frying without oil)
🪚 Spatula (for flipping and removing)
🔲 Cooling rack (for cooling)
🔪 Cutting board (for work surface)

## Steps
1. Place rolled oats in a bowl, add enough cold water to mix, and let soak for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Use cold water for soaking to allow the oats to fully absorb and soften.
2. To the soaked oats, add dry yeast, flour, salt, and sugar in that order. While stirring, add water in batches and knead into a soft, slightly sticky dough.
   **Note**: The dough does not need to be kneaded until smooth; it will be somewhat coarse due to the oat flakes.
3. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment at room temperature for about 1 hour, until it has noticeably expanded in volume and has a honeycomb-like internal structure with air pockets.
4. Remove the dough, gently knead to release the air, roll it into a long log, and divide it evenly into 8 pieces. Roll each piece into a small ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest for 15 minutes.
   **Note**: A damp cloth prevents the dough surface from drying out.
5. Dust the work surface with a little dry flour. Take one rested dough ball and use a rolling pin to roll it into a round disc about 3–5 mm thick and slightly larger than the palm of your hand.
6. Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat for about 1 minute without oil. Place the rolled-out dough disc into the pan.
7. Cook the first side for about 30 seconds. When small bubbles appear on the surface, flip it over. Cook the second side for about 30 seconds, then flip again.
   **Key indicator**: The bread should puff up like a balloon. Remove from the pan when it is fully inflated. If it hasn't puffed completely, you may flip it one more time.
8. Transfer the cooked bread to a cooling rack to cool slightly. It can then be easily opened to form a pocket. Fill with your desired filling and serve.
   **Note**: Fillings can be customized to personal preference, such as Greek salad, pesto beef, salami and cheese, chicken, or scrambled egg and ham.

## Health Advice
The combination of oats and whole wheat flour makes this pocket bread rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable as a staple food during weight loss. However, be mindful of controlling the oil and salt used in the fillings. The dry-frying process after fermentation reduces oil intake, making it a good option for those who need to manage their blood lipid levels.
