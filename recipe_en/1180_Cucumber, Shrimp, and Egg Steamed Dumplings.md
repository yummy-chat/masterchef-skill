# Cucumber, Shrimp, and Egg Steamed Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Preparation Steps
1. Shred the cucumber into thin strips, place in a bowl, add some salt, and mix well. Let it sit for 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture.
   **Key Point**: Salt helps the cucumber strips release water, preventing the filling from becoming soggy.
2. Squeeze the pickled cucumber strips firmly with your hands to remove as much water as possible.
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly squeezing out moisture ensures the steamed dumplings have a refreshing texture and a sturdy bottom.
3. Place the squeezed cucumber strips, prepared shrimp, and scrambled crumbled eggs together in a bowl.
4. Add light soy sauce, sesame oil, and oyster sauce. Mix thoroughly with chopsticks or your hands until the seasoning is evenly distributed on the ingredients.
5. Take one dumpling wrapper, add an appropriate amount of filling, and fold into a willow-leaf shape by pinching the edges together, pressing inward, and repeating until the bottom is tightly sealed.
   **Key Point**: If the seal is not tight, the dumplings may crack and leak juice during steaming.
6. Arrange the wrapped dumplings evenly on the steamer rack, leaving space between them to prevent sticking during steaming.
7. Bring water to a boil in the steamer, place the steamer rack on top, cover tightly with the lid, and steam over high heat for 15 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: The dumpling skin is cooked when it becomes translucent.
8. Once steamed, remove the dumplings and serve with dipping sauces such as sesame chili oil.

## Health Advice
Squeezing the cucumber strips to remove moisture is key, as it prevents the filling from becoming soggy, making this dish suitable for a light, oil-controlled summer diet. Shrimp and eggs provide high-quality protein, suitable for all ages, but be mindful to control the amount of light soy sauce and oyster sauce to reduce sodium intake.
