# Mint Fried Pork Ribs

Cuisine: Yunnan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs, as needed (cut into pieces)  
🧄 Garlic, plenty (sliced)  
🌿 Fresh mint leaves, a large handful  
🌾 All-purpose flour, about one small spoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (a bit more)  
🥢 Oyster sauce, as needed  
🍯 Honey, as needed  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed  
⚫ Black pepper, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍲 Deep pot/stainless steel pot  
🥄 Metal mesh strainer  
🔄 Silicone spatula/tongs  
🌡️ Food thermometer  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Instructions
1. Wash the pork ribs and drain them thoroughly.
2. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, honey, cooking wine, and one small spoon of all-purpose flour to the ribs. Mix well with your hands and marinate for 30 minutes.  
**Note**: Use slightly more light soy sauce than the other seasonings; cover the bowl with baking paper during marinating to prevent the surface from drying out.
3. Wash the mint leaves and make sure to thoroughly dry the surface moisture to prevent oil splatter.
4. Pour enough oil into the pot and heat to 120–150°C. Place the garlic slices in the strainer, fry in the oil until about 70% golden, then remove and drain.  
**Note**: Use the strainer for easy removal, stir constantly; residual heat will continue to deepen the color.
5. Keep the oil temperature at 120–150°C. Add the marinated ribs and fry for about 3 minutes until fully cooked and lightly golden on the surface.  
**Note**: If the pot is small, fry in batches.
6. Raise the oil temperature to 180°C. Add the dried mint leaves and fry quickly until crispy and dark green, then remove immediately.
7. Keep the oil temperature at 180°C. Re-fry the previously fried ribs for about 30 seconds until the surface is crispy and the color deepens.
8. Place the fried ribs, garlic slices, and mint leaves into a large basin. Drain excess oil, then sprinkle with chili flakes, black pepper, and salt. Quickly toss to mix evenly.
9. Serve immediately while hot for the best flavor.

## Health Advice
This mint fried pork ribs dish uses plenty of garlic slices and mint leaves, which are refreshing and help cut the grease. However, the ribs are deep-fried twice at high temperatures, resulting in relatively high calories. It is recommended to pair with a light cold dish or vegetable soup to balance. Suitable for those who enjoy crispy textures, but those with high blood lipids or during fat-loss periods should control their intake.
