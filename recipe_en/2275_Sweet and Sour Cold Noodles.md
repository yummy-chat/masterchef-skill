## Cooking Steps  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the dried noodles, and add the yellow powder to turn the broth and noodles a purplish-red color. Cook until the noodles are fully done, then drain.  
2. Clean and dry the pot, then add the chopped scallion, minced garlic, white sesame seeds, and dried chili flakes, piling them together at the bottom of the pot.  
3. Pour cooking oil into the pot, heat until the oil temperature rises, and drizzle the hot oil evenly over the dry seasonings to release their aroma.  
4. Add vinegar and soy sauce to the oil-drizzled seasonings, and stir well to create a basic sour and salty sauce.  
5. Add the cooked pink noodles to the pot with the sauce, add one spoonful of green sauce, and toss thoroughly with chopsticks until evenly mixed.  
6. Shred cucumber over the mixed noodles, sprinkle with cilantro segments, toss again, and then plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This cold noodle dish uses vinegar and oil-drizzled chili to stimulate the appetite and cut through greasiness, making it suitable for hot days when you have no appetite. However, the oil-drizzled dried chili flakes are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance it out. For children, reduce the amount of chili, and ensure the yellow powder is food-grade edible coloring.
