# Shanghai-Style Braised Pork Chops with Scallions
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork chops, several pieces (with some thickness)  
🧅 A large bunch of scallions (cut into sections about 5-8 cm long)  
🥚 1 egg (for battering)  
🌽 Cornstarch or potato starch, as needed (for battering)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for making ginger-scallion water)  
🧂 A pinch of salt  
⚪ A small amount of white pepper  
🧴 About 25g light soy sauce (used in two batches)  
💧 Ginger-scallion water, as needed (add gradually in small amounts)  
🍬 Sugar, as needed (adjust to taste)  
🟤 About 10g dark soy sauce  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Non-stick pan  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥢 Spatula or tongs  

## Steps
1. **Prepare the pork chops**: Use the back and flat side of a knife to repeatedly pound the pork chops, breaking up coarse fibers. Then use the knife tip to cut through the white sinew around the edges to prevent curling during cooking, making the meat more tender.
**Key point**: Pound until the meat is noticeably thinner and softer, but be careful not to tear it.

2. **Marinate**: Add a pinch of salt, a small amount of white pepper, and a small amount of light soy sauce to the pork chops. Mix well. Gradually add ginger-scallion water in batches, kneading thoroughly until the liquid is fully absorbed, to remove odor and enhance tenderness.
**Key point**: Add ginger-scallion water in small, multiple batches, waiting for each batch to be fully absorbed before adding the next.

3. **Batter**: Add 1 egg and a few spoonfuls of starch. Mix thoroughly until each pork chop is evenly coated with a thin layer of batter, achieving a batter-coating consistency.
**Note**: Use enough starch so that a thin batter clings to the surface of the pork chops; do not make it too thick.

4. **Pan-fry the pork chops**: Use slightly more oil than usual in the pan. Heat over medium-high heat. Place the pork chops in the pan and fry one side until golden and set before flipping. Fry each side for about 1-2 minutes until both sides are colored. Remove and set aside.
- Do not move the pork chops immediately after placing them in the pan to prevent the batter from falling off. Fry until set and colored, as they will be braised later.

5. **Fry the scallions**: Using the remaining oil in the pan, reduce the heat to low. Spread the scallion pieces evenly across the bottom of the pan. Slowly fry until the surface is scorched yellow and the scallion aroma is rich, about 2-3 minutes.
**Key point**: Fry slowly over low heat to prevent the scallions from burning and becoming bitter.

6. **Braise**: Place the pan-fried pork chops on top of the scallions. Add an appropriate amount of sugar. Drizzle in about 25g light soy sauce and 10g dark soy sauce. Add water until it reaches the level of the ingredients. Cover with a lid and braise over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes.
- The saltiness from the light soy sauce is sufficient; no additional salt is needed.

7. **Reduce the sauce**: Remove the lid and adjust to medium-low heat. Slowly reduce the sauce until it is slightly thickened and the color turns a glossy red. Do not reduce it too much; leaving a little sauce for mixing with rice is better.
**Key point**: While reducing the sauce, constantly shake the pan or gently push the ingredients to prevent sticking.

## Health Tips
Pounding the pork chops makes them tender and smooth. This dish is rich in protein and suitable for muscle building. However, the combination of frying and sugar-oil is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balanced nutrition.
