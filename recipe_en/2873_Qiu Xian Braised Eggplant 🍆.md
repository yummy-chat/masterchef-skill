# Qiu Xian Braised Eggplant 🍆  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍆 3 long purple eggplants  
🧄 A generous amount of garlic (for making ample garlic paste)  
🧅 Some scallions (chopped)  
🌿 Some cilantro (chopped)  
🫘 Some toasted white sesame seeds  
🧂 Some salt (about 1 spoon when mashing garlic, adjust to taste at the end)  
🍾 About 1 spoon of soy sauce  
🫒 Some sesame oil (or vegetable oil) (for heating and splashing)

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
♨️ Steamer (with lid)  
🥣 Mortar and pestle  
🍳 Wok (or small pot)  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the stems from the eggplants, cut them in half lengthwise, then cut into evenly sized long strips.  
   **Key point**: Even shapes ensure simultaneous steaming.

2. Peel the garlic and place it in the mortar, add about one spoon of salt, and use the pestle to mash repeatedly into a fine garlic paste, then transfer to a bowl.  
   **Key point**: Adding salt while mashing garlic helps extract juice and enhances aroma, making it easier to mash.

3. Add about one spoon of soy sauce to the garlic paste and mix well. Set aside.  
   **Note**: Since salt was added during mashing, no extra salt is needed here; adjust saltiness at the end.

4. Once the steamer is producing steam, spread the eggplant strips evenly on the steaming rack, cover, and steam over medium-high heat for 15 minutes until the eggplants are completely soft.  
   **Key point**: Make sure to place the eggplants in the steamer only after steam has risen to avoid prolonged cooking time that affects texture.

5. In a separate pot, heat an appropriate amount of sesame oil until it begins to smoke, then pour it evenly over the garlic paste and soy sauce mixture in the bowl, releasing a rich garlic aroma.  
   **Note**: If the oil is not hot enough, the aroma will be weak; heat until visibly smoking.

6. Remove the steamed eggplants, let them cool slightly, then tear them into thin strips by hand and neatly arrange them on a plate.  
   **Key point**: Tearing by hand preserves the eggplant's fibrous structure better than cutting, allowing it to absorb the sauce more easily.

7. Pour the prepared garlic paste sauce evenly over the eggplant strips.

8. Sprinkle scallions, cilantro, and toasted white sesame seeds on top as desired to enhance color and flavor layers.

## Health Tips  
Eggplants are rich in dietary fiber. Steaming keeps them low in oil and fat, making them suitable for weight control or those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids. Adding salt while mashing garlic enhances aroma, but be mindful of the soy sauce amount to avoid excessive saltiness.
