# Catalan Pudding
Cuisine: Others  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥛 Milk 250g  
🥚 Egg yolks 3  
🧈 Heavy cream 60g  
🍬 White sugar 40-45g  
🍋 Lemon zest about 1g (only yellow part, white pith removed)  
🍊 Orange zest about 1g (only orange part, white pith removed)  
🌿 Vanilla bean 1/2 (split and seeds scraped out)  
🌽 Cornstarch 10g  
🍬 Golden sugar as needed (for sprinkling and torching)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🍲 Saucepan  
🧂 Strainer  
🔪 Peeler, small knife  
🥣 Glass or stainless steel bowl  
🧊 Refrigerator  
🔥 Kitchen torch  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Baking tray  
🥣 Shallow container  

## Steps  
1. Zest the lemon and orange, taking only the colored part and removing the white pith. Split the vanilla bean and scrape out the seeds. Set aside.  
2. Place egg yolks, white sugar, and cornstarch in a bowl. Use a hand whisk to stir until smooth, even, and free of lumps.  
3. Pour milk and heavy cream into a saucepan. Add the vanilla seeds and pod, along with the lemon and orange zest.  
   **Note**: The original recipe includes cinnamon and star anise; these are omitted here according to personal preference.  
4. Heat the milk mixture until it boils, then remove from heat. Slowly pour it into the egg yolk mixture in three batches, whisking quickly with the hand whisk to prevent the yolks from curdling.  
5. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until it reaches about 80°C. Once the custard thickens and leaves a clear trail when you scrape the bottom of the pan, immediately remove from heat.  
6. Strain the custard through a sieve into a shallow container. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight until fully set.  
7. Remove the set pudding. Evenly sprinkle a layer of coarse golden sugar on the surface. Tilt the container to let excess sugar fall off, ensuring a uniform sugar layer.  
8. Use a kitchen torch to evenly caramelize the sugar layer until it melts, bubbles, and turns a deep amber color, forming a thin, crispy caramel crust. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This pudding is rich in calcium and protein, making it a suitable afternoon treat. However, it is high in sugar, so it is recommended that those with diabetes or managing sugar intake consume it in moderation. The texture is smoother when chilled. When torching the caramel crust, control the heat carefully to avoid burning.
