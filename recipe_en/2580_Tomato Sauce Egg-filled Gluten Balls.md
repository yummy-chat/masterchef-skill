# Tomato Sauce Egg-filled Gluten Balls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Tofu 1/2 block (either firm or soft, mashed)
🥚 Eggs 4
🥠 Oil gluten balls (fried gluten puffs) about 10 pieces
🍅 Tomatoes 3
🌽 Cornstarch a little (for making water starch slurry)
💧 Water as needed
🫚 Ginger a little
🧅 Scallion a little
🧂 Salt to taste (used separately for egg mixture and when stir-frying tomatoes)
🥓 Lard as needed
🍾 Light soy sauce as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce as needed
🍬 White sugar as needed
🧂 Monosodium glutamate (MSG) as needed
🌶️ White pepper powder as needed
🫒 Sesame oil a little

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Iron wok
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack)
🥄 Spoon
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍲 Clay pot or deep dish

## Cooking Steps
1.  Mash the tofu in a bowl, add eggs, a pinch of salt, and cornstarch that has been dissolved in water beforehand. Mix thoroughly until a uniform filling forms.
   **Key Point**: Dissolve cornstarch in water first before mixing it in; this helps the egg mixture better coat the tofu, improving the filling's cohesiveness.
2.  Poke a small hole in each oil gluten ball. Use a finger to gently rotate and expand the space inside. Use a spoon to fill the egg mixture into each ball, filling up to about 70% full to prevent overflow during steaming.
   **Note**: Do not overfill; the volume will expand as it steams.
3.  Score a cross on the top of each tomato. Blanch them briefly in boiling water, peel off the skin, and then dice into small pieces.
4.  Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the filled oil gluten balls in the steamer and steam over high heat for 10 minutes until the egg mixture is fully set. Remove, use a spoon to gently separate any stuck-together balls, and let them cool for later use.
   **Key Point**: Separate them promptly after steaming to avoid them sticking together when cooled.
5.  Heat lard in an iron wok until hot. Add ginger slices and scallion segments, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the diced tomatoes, pressing them with a spatula while stir-frying. Add a pinch of salt early, and cook over medium-low heat until they become soft and release their juices into a sauce.
   **Note**: Adding salt early when stir-frying tomatoes helps them release juice and break down into a sauce more quickly.
6.  Add a small amount of water to the wok. Season with light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, MSG, and white pepper powder. Add the steamed oil gluten balls, cover with a lid, and braise over medium heat for 5 minutes to allow them to fully absorb the sauce.
7.  Slowly drizzle in the water starch slurry, gently stirring with a spatula to thicken the sauce until it coats the ingredients evenly. Finally, drizzle in a little sesame oil. Transfer to a serving plate and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish uses egg-filled oil gluten balls paired with tofu, providing high-quality protein, suitable for people of all ages who need nutritional supplementation. Note that oil gluten balls themselves absorb oil, so the amount of lard and sugar used in cooking should be moderate to control calorie intake.
