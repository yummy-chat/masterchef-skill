# Echire Butter Cookies
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Softened butter 75g  
🧈 Browned butter 15g (made from additional butter)  
🌰 Almond flour 20g  
🌾 Cake flour 75g  
🍬 Powdered sugar 35g  
🥚 Egg yolk 15g  
🧂 Sea salt 1g  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Spatula  
🔍 Flour sifter  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🌀 Piping bag  
🍳 Stainless steel pot  
🧽 Strainer  
🧁 Muffin pan  
🔥 Oven  
📜 Silicone mat/parchment paper  

## Instructions
1. Make browned butter: Cut butter into small pieces and place in a stainless steel pot. Heat over medium-low heat until completely melted, producing abundant foam and turning amber in color. When brown sediment appears at the bottom, immediately remove from heat. Strain through a strainer to remove sediment, obtaining clear browned butter. Let cool and set aside.
2. Mix the dough: Place softened butter in a glass bowl and mix with a spatula. Add powdered sugar, sea salt, and almond flour, mixing until well combined. Then add egg yolk and cooled browned butter, mixing thoroughly. Sift in cake flour and fold using a cutting motion until no dry flour remains and the dough is uniform.
   **Note**: Use a cutting motion to prevent gluten development; sift all dry ingredients in advance.
3. Pipe and chill: Transfer the dough into a piping bag. Pipe evenly into the bottom of muffin pan cups, filling them about 70% full. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour until the dough is firm and set.
4. Bake: Remove the pan from the refrigerator. Gently press the surface of the dough with your finger. Bake in a preheated oven at 160°C on the middle rack for about 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: Baking time and temperature should be adjusted according to your oven's actual conditions. Remove when the surface turns golden brown and lace-like edges appear.
5. Remove from the pan after baking and let cool before serving.

## Health Tips
These cookies combine the nutty aroma of browned butter. Although high in calories, they are rich in quality fats and a small amount of protein, making them suitable as an occasional energy snack. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid overconsumption.
