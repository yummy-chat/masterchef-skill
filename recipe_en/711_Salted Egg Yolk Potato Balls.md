# Salted Egg Yolk Potato Balls
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Red potatoes, about 4
🌾 Wheat starch, as needed
💧 Boiling water, about 100g
🧈 Butter, 40g
🥚 Salted egg yolk sauce, as needed
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Steamer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Spoon/fork
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the skin-on red potatoes thoroughly and steam over high heat in a steamer for about 1.5 hours, until the skins crack and the insides are completely soft and fluffy.  
   **Key point**: The potatoes must be steamed until the skins crack naturally to ensure the insides are tender and fluffy.
2. Remove the steamed potatoes, let them cool slightly, then peel off the skins. Be careful of heat when handling.
3. Place the peeled potatoes in a bowl and mash them into a puree using a spoon or fork. For a finer texture, further press with the flat side of a knife.  
   **Key point**: Mash thoroughly so the potato puree has no lumps.
4. Prepare the scalded wheat starch: Pour about 100g of boiling water into a bowl containing wheat starch and stir quickly and evenly until the wheat starch is fully cooked and gelatinized.  
   **Key point**: Use freshly boiled water to achieve gelatinization.
5. Add the potato puree, butter, salt, and white pepper powder to the scalded wheat starch, and knead into a smooth and uniform dough. The dough should be golden yellow, evenly textured, and non-sticky. If too dry or too wet, adjust by adding a little dry wheat starch.
6. Roll the dough into a long log and divide it into evenly sized small pieces.
7. Take one small piece, flatten it, wrap it around a pre-frozen salted egg yolk filling, and seal the edges by rolling it into a ball.  
   **Key point**: The filling must be frozen for at least 1 hour beforehand to make it easier to wrap; try to encase as much filling as possible.
8. Pour oil into a wok and heat to a medium temperature (about 60% hot). Add the raw potato balls and deep-fry over medium heat.
9. Once the potato balls are in the oil, do not stir them first. After the surface has set, gently push them with a slotted spoon for even heating. Fry until the outer skin is golden and crispy, the balls have expanded in size, and they float to the surface, then remove.  
   **Key point**: Stirring before the surface sets may cause the balls to fall apart.
10. Remove the fried potato balls, drain the oil, and plate them. They can be eaten directly or served with ketchup/sauce.

## Health Advice
Salted egg yolk potato balls are rich in carbohydrates and high-quality fats, making them suitable for replenishing energy in people with high physical exertion. However, note their high oil and salt content. It is recommended to enjoy them occasionally as a snack rather than consuming them in large quantities as a main meal.
