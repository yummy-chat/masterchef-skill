# Northeast-Style Old-Style Guobaorou (Double-Cooked Pork Slices)
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork tenderloin, about 1 piece (sliced horizontally into thin pieces, slightly thinner than chopsticks)  
🥔 Potato starch 200g (soak in water in advance to let sediment settle, discard the clear water on top, use the wet starch)  
🌽 Cornstarch 1 tsp (for the sweet and sour sauce)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (julienned)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste (julienned)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (cut into segments)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste (julienned)  
🍬 White sugar 10 tsp  
🍶 White vinegar (9-degree rice vinegar), equal amount to the sugar  
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tsp (about 5ml)  
🧂 Salt, half a tsp (for the sweet and sour sauce, about 2g)  
🫒 Sesame oil, half a tbsp  
🌶️ White pepper powder, a pinch  
🫚🧅 Ginger-scallion water, a little (made by soaking ginger slices and scallion segments in water, for marinating)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for washing and rubbing the meat slices)  
🫒 Soybean oil, as needed (for frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Glass bowl/basin  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Strainer/slotted spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Slice the pork tenderloin horizontally into pieces about the thickness of chopsticks. Gently pound them with the back of a knife to tenderize. Rub with a pinch of salt to draw out blood, then rinse thoroughly with water and drain.
**Key**: Cutting horizontally against the grain cuts the fibers, making the meat more tender.

2. Mix potato starch with water in advance and let it settle. Pour off the clear water on top, and use the wet starch paste at the bottom. The wet starch will have a non-Newtonian fluid consistency. Knead and mix it until it becomes firm and sticky.
**Key**: The wet starch must be thoroughly kneaded until it develops a firm, springy texture.

3. Prepare the sweet and sour sauce: Mix 10 tsp white sugar, an equal amount of white vinegar, 1 tsp light soy sauce, half a tsp salt, half a tbsp sesame oil, and 1 tsp cornstarch. Stir until well combined.
**Key**: The sugar and vinegar must be in strict equal amounts; 9-degree rice vinegar is preferable. The cornstarch helps the sauce cling to the meat.

4. Add salt, white pepper powder, and ginger-scallion water to the meat slices. Mix and rub until the liquid is fully absorbed. Then pour in the wet starch paste and mix thoroughly until each slice is evenly coated. Finally, drizzle in a little soybean oil and mix to prevent sticking.
**Key**: Ensure the batter is evenly distributed; the oil helps prevent sticking.

5. Pour enough soybean oil into the wok and heat to 60% hot. Add the meat slices one by one. Fry over medium heat until they float and turn a light yellow color. Remove and drain.
**Key**: The oil temperature should not be too low to prevent the batter from falling off. Fry in batches to avoid sticking.

6. Raise the oil temperature to 70-80% hot. Return all the fried meat slices to the wok for a second round of frying. Fry over high heat for about 10-15 seconds until the outer shell is golden and crispy. Quickly remove and drain the oil.
**Key**: The second frying ensures a crispy exterior and tender interior; keep the time short.

7. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Pour in the sweet and sour sauce and cook over high heat until it bubbles thickly and becomes viscous. Add the fried meat slices, along with sliced garlic, julienned scallions, julienned carrots, and cilantro segments. Stir-fry quickly to evenly coat all ingredients with the sauce. If desired, reserve a little sauce to drizzle on at the end.
**Key**: Stir-fry quickly to keep the meat slices crispy. Add the ingredients as soon as the sweet and sour sauce comes to a vigorous boil.

8. Plate and serve immediately while hot.

## Health Advice  
Guobaorou is high in fat and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables. It is suitable for occasional indulgence but should not be eaten frequently. Although the crispy exterior and tender interior are tempting, those with high blood sugar or on a weight loss diet should consume it in moderation.
