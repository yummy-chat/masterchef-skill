# Chocolate Nut Tangyuan Skewers  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 Frozen filled tangyuan 1 box  
🍫 Chocolate (block or bar) as needed  
🥜 Nuts (walnuts, almonds, etc.) as needed  
🧂 Seasonings: no additional  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl or basin (for cold water rinse)  
🛍️ Plastic bag  
❄️ Refrigerator  
🔨 Garlic press or nut cracker  
🍢 Bamboo skewers  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the tangyuan, and cook over high heat until they all float to the surface, then immediately remove.  
2. Quickly transfer the cooked tangyuan into cold water to cool, preventing sticking and firming the surface.  
3. Drain the water, place the tangyuan in a plastic bag, and freeze for about 30 minutes until the surface hardens.  
4. Using the residual heat from the water used to cook the tangyuan, immerse a plastic bag containing the chocolate into the hot water to melt it in a water bath until smooth and sauce-like.  
   - Control the water temperature and soaking time to avoid burning the chocolate or causing oil-water separation.  
5. Remove the frozen tangyuan and thread them onto bamboo skewers.  
6. Evenly drizzle the melted chocolate over the tangyuan skewers, working quickly, as the cold tangyuan will cause the chocolate to set rapidly.  
7. While the chocolate is still not fully set, immediately sprinkle with pre-crushed nuts.  
8. Add a small amount of decorative powder, such as matcha powder, on top.  
9. Place the decorated tangyuan skewers back in the freezer for about 30 minutes to fully harden and crisp the chocolate shell.  
10. Remove and serve immediately—crispy on the outside, chewy on the inside, cool and refreshing.  

## Health Advice  
These tangyuan skewers are relatively high in calories; it is recommended to eat 2-3 skewers per serving. They are suitable as a party snack or a summer cold treat. Nuts are rich in unsaturated fatty acids, and chocolate provides antioxidants, but be mindful of sugar intake.
