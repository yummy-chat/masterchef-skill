# Braised Beef Brisket and Tomato Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Common Homestyle Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket 2 jin (fresh, with some fat, soak in salted water for 3 hours in advance to remove blood)
🍅 Tomatoes 3 jin (blanch in boiling water, peel, and cut into large irregular chunks)
🍜 Alkaline fine noodles appropriate amount
🥬 Baby bok choy appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion, diced 40 grams
🫚 Ginger, sliced 30 grams
🌶️ Red chili pepper, sliced into rings 80 grams
🫒 Soybean oil 240 grams
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) appropriate amount (for removing gamey smell)
🥫 Tomato paste 80 grams
🧂 Premium light soy sauce 40 grams
🧂 Table salt 15 grams (added to the pot after 2 hours of simmering) + 2 grams (for the bowl base)
🧂 White pepper powder 8 grams (spice sachet)
🪵 Cinnamon stick 3 grams (spice sachet)
⭐ Star anise 3 grams (spice sachet)
🌱 Cumin seeds 5 grams (spice sachet)
🧂 MSG appropriate amount (bowl base)
🧂 Ground pepper appropriate amount (bowl base)
🧂 Soy sauce two drops (bowl base, light soy sauce for enhancing freshness)
🍖 Beef bone broth appropriate amount (for making the bowl base soup)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🍳 Wok
🥘 Stockpot (for boiling noodles and blanching vegetables)
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🥄 Wooden spoon/soup ladle
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking and cleaning the beef)
🍜 Ceramic bowl (for preparing the bowl base and serving)

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the beef brisket in salted water for 3 hours in advance until the blood seeps out and the meat turns pale. Rinse under clean water 2-3 times and drain thoroughly.
2. Blanch the tomatoes in boiling water, peel them, and cut into irregular chunks similar in size to the beef brisket. Place the white pepper, cinnamon, star anise, and cumin seeds into a spice sachet and rinse clean.
3. Heat the wok over high heat, add the soybean oil. Once the oil is hot, add the diced scallions, sliced ginger, and red chili pepper rings, stir-frying until fragrant.
4. Add the drained beef brisket and stir-fry vigorously over high heat until the fat begins to render slightly and the meat surface tightens. Splash in the baijiu along the edge of the wok and stir-fry to remove any gamey smell.
5. Add half of the tomato chunks, the tomato paste, and the premium light soy sauce. Continue stir-frying until the tomatoes break down into a sauce and the mixture turns a bright red, glossy color.
6. Add enough clean water all at once, ensuring the water level is about two to three finger-widths above the meat. Add the spice sachet. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to the lowest heat, maintaining a gentle simmer with small bubbles (like fish eyes). Slow-cook for about 2.5 hours, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
7. After approximately 2 hours of simmering, remove the spice sachet. Add 15 grams of salt, stir to combine, and continue to simmer over low heat for another 30 minutes to let the beef absorb the flavor.
8. Once the broth has thickened and the beef is tender and soft, add the remaining tomato chunks. Stir to combine, heat through, and immediately turn off the heat. The base is ready.
9. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Cook the alkaline fine noodles until done. At the same time, blanch the baby bok choy.
10. In the serving bowl, place the salt, MSG, ground pepper, two drops of soy sauce, and a handful of finely chopped scallions. Pour the boiling hot beef bone broth over the seasonings to dissolve them.
11. Add the cooked noodles and blanched bok choy to the bowl. Ladle the braised beef brisket and tomato sauce on top, add a spoonful of the meaty gravy, and garnish with cilantro. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This braised beef brisket and tomato noodle soup is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost or recovery. The slowly simmered beef brisket becomes tender and easy to digest, but be mindful of the noodle portion size to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake.
