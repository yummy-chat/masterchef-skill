# Instant Noodle Takoyaki 🐙
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Instant noodles: 1 block (about 100–120 grams)
🥚 Egg: 1
💧 Water: small amount (to adjust batter consistency)
🥬 Lettuce: to taste (chopped)
🍤 Shrimp: to taste
🐙 Octopus pieces: to taste
🧈 Butter: to taste (for greasing the pan before cooking)
🍯 Sweet chili sauce (or teriyaki sauce): to taste (for drizzling on the finished product)
🥗 Salad dressing (or mayonnaise): to taste (for drizzling on the finished product)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Strainer or colander
🧊 Blender (or food processor)
🍳 Takoyaki pan
🥢 Bamboo skewer (long skewer)
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the instant noodles and cook until softened and separated, then drain using a strainer and transfer to a blender.
   **Note**: The noodles must be fully separated, but do not overcook until too soft.
2. Add the egg and a small amount of water to the blender, blend into a smooth, pourable batter-like paste, then pour into a bowl and set aside.
   **Note**: Add water in batches to adjust the consistency until it forms a smooth paste.
3. Heat the takoyaki pan over medium-low heat, dip a chopstick in butter and evenly coat the inside of each round hole.
   **Evaluation**: The pan is properly greased when it is slightly warm and the butter has melted.
4. Use a spoon to pour the batter into the holes, filling them to about 80% full.
   **Note**: Do not overfill; leave room for flipping later.
5. When the bottom of the batter is slightly set and the edges begin to change color, add a small amount of chopped lettuce, shrimp, and octopus pieces into each hole.
   **Key**: Do not add too much filling, as it may affect forming into balls.
6. Continue heating until the batter is fully set and the bottom is golden brown, then use a bamboo skewer to flip the balls about 90° or 180°, allowing the uncooked batter to flow down and contact the pan bottom.
   **Note**: Flip gently, using chopsticks to assist if needed.
7. Flip and cook repeatedly, ensuring all sides of the balls are evenly heated, until they form complete spheres with a uniformly golden and crispy outer shell.
   **Evaluation**: The shells are crispy and evenly browned, indicating doneness.
8. Remove the balls with a bamboo skewer and plate them. Squeeze sweet chili sauce and salad dressing on top as desired.

## Health Advice
This instant noodle version of takoyaki replaces traditional batter with noodles, resulting in a crispy texture, perfect for an occasional indulgence. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. The shrimp and octopus pieces provide high-quality protein, but be mindful of the amount of sweet chili sauce used to avoid excessive sodium.
