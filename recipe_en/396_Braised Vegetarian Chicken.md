# Braised Vegetarian Chicken

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 350g vegetarian chicken (cut into 7-8mm thick slices)
🧅 Scallions (white part cut into sections, green part minced)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices
🧄 Garlic, a few cloves (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (optional)
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (for frying)
🧂 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
🧂 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce (for color)
🦪 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
🧂 A pinch of salt
🍬 1 tablespoon sugar
💧 Boiling water, enough to cover the vegetarian chicken

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🍽️ Plate
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥢 Chopsticks or spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the vegetarian chicken into slices 7–8 mm thick. Slices that are too thick won't absorb flavor well, while slices that are too thin are prone to breaking.
   **Note**: Keep the slices uniform in thickness for even cooking and flavor absorption.

2. Heat a non-stick pan over high heat and add a generous amount of vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the vegetarian chicken slices. Fry over medium-high heat until both sides are golden brown, the edges curl up, and a crispy shell forms on the surface. Then remove from the pan.
**Key point**: While frying, let one side set before flipping to ensure a crispy exterior and tender interior.

3. Leave a small amount of oil in the pan, reduce the heat to low. Add ginger slices, scallion white sections, garlic slices, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant.

4. Return the fried vegetarian chicken to the pan. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, salt, and sugar. Gently stir to combine evenly.

5. Pour in enough boiling water to submerge the vegetarian chicken. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cover and simmer for a while to allow the vegetarian chicken to absorb the sauce, becoming tender while still retaining a slightly fibrous texture.

6. Remove the lid and turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Turn off the heat once the sauce has thickened. Sprinkle with minced scallion greens and gently toss to combine before serving.
   **Note**: Do not reduce the sauce until it is too dry; the goal is a thick sauce that coats the vegetarian chicken.

## Health Advice
After frying and braising, the vegetarian chicken has slightly more calories than when simply stir-fried, but it is rich in plant-based protein. It is suitable for vegetarians or those looking to reduce red meat consumption. Be mindful of the amount of oil used and avoid excessive frying to maintain a healthy dish.
