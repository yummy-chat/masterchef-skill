# Sand Tea Beef and Tofu Clay Pot
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef (tenderloin or lean cut) to taste (choose leaner parts, slice thinly against the grain)  
🫘 Soft tofu to taste (must use soft tofu, cut into pieces)  
🫑 Bell peppers (green, yellow, red) to taste (diced, mainly for color)  
🧅 Shallots (red onion) to taste (minced)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (minced)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (minced)  
🧅 Scallions a little (chopped)  
🌾 Sweet potato starch three tablespoons (for marinating the beef, can substitute with cornstarch)  
🥜 Sand tea sauce to taste (slightly high in calories, can reduce as needed)  
🥄 Light soy sauce one tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking wine two tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce one tablespoon  
🧂 Chicken powder one tablespoon  
⚪ White pepper a pinch  
🧂 Salt a pinch  
🫒 Tea oil a little (helps retain moisture)  
💧 Water two tablespoons (for marinating meat) + appropriate amount (for making the broth)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥢 Wooden spoon/chopsticks  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔥 Induction cooker/gas stove  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare ingredients**: Dice the bell peppers, cut the soft tofu into pieces, slice the beef thinly against the grain, mince the shallots, garlic, and ginger, and chop the scallions.  
   - **Key point**: Use soft tofu; slice the beef thinly for quick cooking and tenderness.  
2. **Marinate the beef**: Place the beef slices in a bowl, add minced scallion, ginger, and garlic, white pepper, salt, light soy sauce, cooking wine, oyster sauce, chicken powder, water, and tea oil. Mix until the beef becomes sticky and tender.  
   - **Key point**: Mixing until sticky is the foundation for tenderness.  
3. **Coat the beef**: Add sweet potato starch to the marinated beef and mix evenly.  
   - **Key point**: This step is crucial for a chewy texture; use cornstarch as a substitute if sweet potato starch is unavailable.  
4. **Sauté aromatics**: Heat the clay pot, add oil, then add minced shallots, garlic, and ginger. Sauté over medium heat until fragrant.  
   - **Key point**: Fully release the aroma of the spices.  
5. **Prepare the broth**: Add sand tea sauce and stir evenly, then add an appropriate amount of water. Bring to a boil over medium heat to create a red and glossy sand tea broth. Adjust seasoning with a pinch of white pepper and chicken powder.  
   - **Key point**: Reduce sand tea sauce as needed to control calories.  
6. **Cook the tofu**: Gently place the soft tofu pieces into the sand tea broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes to let the tofu absorb the flavors.  
   - **Key point**: Avoid overcooking the tofu to maintain its shape.  
7. **Add the beef**: Place the marinated beef slices into the pot one by one. Cook over low heat for about 2 minutes until the beef changes color and is cooked through.  
   - **Key point**: Do not overcook the beef; cook gently over low heat to keep it tender.  
8. **Garnish and serve**: Sprinkle with diced bell peppers and chopped scallions, then turn off the heat and serve.  
   - **Key point**: Add the bell peppers at the end to keep them crisp and add vibrant color.  

## Health Advice  
This sand tea beef and tofu clay pot is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for muscle building or calcium supplementation. However, sand tea sauce is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to use it sparingly to control sodium intake. Beef and tofu are easy to digest, making this dish suitable for the whole family.
