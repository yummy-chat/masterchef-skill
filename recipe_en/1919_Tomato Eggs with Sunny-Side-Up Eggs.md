# Tomato Eggs with Sunny-Side-Up Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 Tomatoes 2-3 pieces (peeled, cut into uneven chunks)  
🥚 Eggs 5-6 pieces  
🧅 Onion Appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic Appropriate amount (minced)  
🌿 Scallions Appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🧂 Salt A small amount  
🫒 Cooking oil Appropriate amount  
🧴 Light soy sauce A small amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  

## Steps
1. Wash the tomatoes, score a cross on the top, blanch in boiling water for about 15 seconds, then immediately immerse in cold water. Use the principle of thermal expansion and contraction to separate the skin from the flesh, and peel off the skin.
2. Cut the peeled tomatoes into uneven chunks; small pieces for cooking the sauce, and larger pieces for retaining fruit texture. Mince the garlic and onion, cut the scallions into sections, and set aside.
3. Heat the wok, then pour in cold oil. Evenly sprinkle a small amount of salt, and crack the eggs one by one into the wok, keeping the yolks intact. Fry over medium heat on one side for about 45 seconds, until the egg whites are set and the edges are golden brown. Remove the sunny-side-up eggs.
4. Leave a little oil in the wok, add minced garlic and chopped onion, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the small tomato chunks, stir-fry over medium-low heat until soft and mushy, with bubbling juices. Drizzle in a small amount of light soy sauce to enhance flavor.
5. Add an appropriate amount of hot water to the wok, place the fried sunny-side-up eggs back in, then add the large tomato chunks. Ensure the eggs are fully immersed in the broth. Simmer over medium-low heat for a short time, allowing the eggs to fully absorb the broth.
6. Sprinkle with scallion sections, then turn off the heat. The tomato eggs with sunny-side-up eggs can be served over rice with the broth.

## Health Suggestions  
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene, and eggs provide high-quality protein. This dish is suitable for all ages, especially those needing to supplement vitamin C and energy. Pay attention to controlling the amount of oil and salt; those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of light soy sauce.
