# Crispy Egg Waffle Pockets
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 2 tablespoons (can use plain, low-gluten, or high-gluten flour)
💧 Water 2 tablespoons
🥚 Eggs 2
🍞 Yeast about 1 gram
🧂 Baking soda a pinch (dissolve in a small amount of water)
🫒 Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
🍬 Brown sugar 1 teaspoon
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon
🥩 Pork floss to taste (filling)
🫘 Red bean paste to taste (filling)
🫐 Blueberry jam to taste (filling)
🌿 Seaweed to taste (filling)
🥗 Mayonnaise to taste (filling)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Large spoon, teaspoon
🪄 Spatula
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the batter**: In a large bowl, combine flour, water, eggs, brown sugar, white sugar, yeast, and vegetable oil. Stir in one direction with chopsticks until smooth and lump-free.
   **Key point**: Measure flour, water, eggs, and oil using "2 tablespoons," and sugar and yeast using teaspoons. Thorough mixing is essential for a smooth outer skin.
2. **Refrigerated fermentation**: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
   **Key point**: Refrigerated fermentation allows for advance preparation, ready to use the next day; the yeast will not fully activate at this temperature.
3. **Activate the batter**: Remove the batter from the refrigerator the next day. In a small bowl, dissolve baking soda in a little water, then pour it into the batter and stir well. The batter should thin out and become pourable.
4. **Preheat the pan**: Preheat a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat without oil.
   **Key point**: Cooking without oil is crucial for a crispy crust.
5. **Spread the batter**: Pour a large spoonful of batter into the center of the pan. If the batter naturally and slowly flows to spread out, the pan temperature is correct. Gently use the back of the spoon to spread the batter into a round shape.
   **State check**: If the batter solidifies immediately upon contact with the pan, the heat is too high. Turn off the heat or reduce to the lowest setting.
6. **Cook over low heat**: Maintain low heat throughout. Once the batter surface has set and no liquid remains, place the filling on one half of the batter circle.
   **Key point**: No need to flip; judge doneness of the bottom by observing the surface setting.
7. **Fold and set**: Use a spatula to fold the other half of the batter circle over the filling. Continue cooking over low heat until the edges naturally lift and turn golden brown, indicating a crispy crust.
8. Remove and enjoy immediately while hot. The outer skin is thin and crispy, and the filling can be savory or sweet.

## Health Advice
This crispy egg waffle pocket has a relatively high oil content in the batter. Pairing it with pork floss or red bean paste adds calories, making it suitable as a breakfast or afternoon snack, but portion control is advised to avoid excessive calorie intake. Eggs and flour provide quality protein and carbohydrates, while fillings like mayonnaise or seaweed can add dietary fiber.
