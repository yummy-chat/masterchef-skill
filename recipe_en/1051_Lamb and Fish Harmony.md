# Lamb and Fish Harmony
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐑 Lamb leg with skin, appropriate amount (cut into mahjong-sized pieces)  
🐟 Mandarin fish, 1 piece (fish meat cut into pieces, fish bones chopped into chunks; can also be replaced with crucian carp)  
🥕 Carrot, 1 piece (cut into rolling chunks)  
🥬 White radish, 1 piece (cut into rolling chunks)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (for marinating and stir-frying)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for marinating and stir-frying)  
🌿 Celery, appropriate amount (use only the celery stalks)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (use only the cilantro stalks, sprinkle cilantro leaves at the end)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (cut into garlic leaf flowers)  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for removing fishy smell)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for marinating and final seasoning)  
🌶 White pepper powder, appropriate amount (for marinating and enhancing freshness while removing gamey smell)  
🥓 Lard, appropriate amount (for frying fish bones)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Clay pot or soup pot  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🕸️ Strainer  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Pressure cooker (optional, for quickly stewing lamb until tender)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Put the lamb leg with skin into cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then remove and drain, cut into pieces about 3 cm square.
**Key point**: Blanching removes blood impurities and gamey smell.

2. Remove the meat and bones from the mandarin fish, cut the fish meat into pieces, and chop the fish bones into sections. Add cooking wine, salt, white pepper powder, scallion, and ginger to the fish pieces, mix well, and marinate for 30 minutes.
**Key point**: Marinating removes fishy smell and seasons the fish meat.

3. Cut the carrot and white radish into small rolling chunks, blanch in boiling water for 2 minutes, remove and rinse with cold water, then set aside.
4. Add cold oil to the wok, sauté ginger slices and scallion until fragrant, add the lamb pieces and stir-fry until the surface is slightly browned, then splash in cooking wine.
**Key point**: Stir-frying the lamb further removes gamey smell and enhances aroma.

5. Transfer the stir-fried lamb along with the juices into a soup pot, add enough boiling water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 1.5 hours.
6. In another pot, add lard, sauté ginger slices and scallion until fragrant, add the fish bones and fry until golden brown, then pour in boiling water and boil over high heat for 7-8 minutes until the soup turns milky white. Use a strainer to remove the fish bones and residue, and reserve the fish soup.
**Key point**: Fish soup must be boiled over high heat with boiling water to become milky white.

7. Pour the strained fish soup into the pot with the stewed lamb, add the blanched radish chunks, and continue to simmer together.
8. In another pot, bring water to a boil, quickly blanch the marinated fish pieces, turn off the heat as soon as the water boils, remove the fish pieces, and place them in a clay pot.
9. Add the cooked lamb, celery stalks, and cilantro stalks to the clay pot, finally season with salt and white pepper powder, and sprinkle with garlic leaf flowers before serving.
**Key point**: White pepper powder is key to enhancing freshness and removing gamey smell; fish soup must be boiled over high heat until milky white.

## Health Tips  
Lamb and fish are rich in high-quality protein, paired with radish to aid digestion, suitable for autumn and winter nourishment and for individuals with weak constitution. Note that the fish soup must be boiled over high heat until milky white, and white pepper powder should not be omitted, as it removes gamey smell and enhances freshness.
