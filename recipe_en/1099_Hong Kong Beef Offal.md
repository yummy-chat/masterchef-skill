Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Instructions
1. Crush the cardamom. Place it and all other dry spices from Group B into a wok. Dry-fry over low heat until fragrant, then transfer into a spice bag and set aside.  
2. Add water to a pressure cooker, then add beef bones, the spice bag, galangal, and shallots. Once pressurized, reduce heat and cook for approx. 30 minutes. Strain to obtain clear broth; keep the spice bag.  
3. Place all beef offal in cold water and blanch for approx. 10 minutes. Remove, rinse thoroughly, and cut into appropriately sized pieces.  
**Note**: Beef lung, intestine, tendon, and bile are more heat-resistant and require longer cooking; water-rehydrated honeycomb tripe and some tripe are softer, so they should be added later.  

4. Heat oil in a large iron pot, sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add all Group A pastes except curry cubes. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until aromatic and red oil releases.  
5. Pour the clear beef bone broth into the stir-fried paste mixture. Stir well to form the sauce broth base.  
6. First, add the tougher offal pieces (beef lung, intestine, tendon, bile) and white radish chunks. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for approx. 1 hour.  
7. Add the tender tripe and honeycomb tripe, along with rock sugar and curry cubes. Continue simmering over low heat for approx. 30 minutes.  
8. Test the offal with the blunt end of a bamboo skewer; if it pierces easily, the offal is tender and ready.  
9. Adjust the color and saltiness: use dark soy sauce for color, and fish sauce and salt for final seasoning.  
10. Serve with Guilin chili sauce for dipping.  

## Health Advice
This beef offal dish is rich in collagen, iron, and zinc, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, the sauce has a high sodium content, so those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. White radish helps absorb oil and reduce greasiness; it’s recommended to eat plenty of it alongside the dish.
