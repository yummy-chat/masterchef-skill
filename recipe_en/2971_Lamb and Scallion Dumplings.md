# Lamb and Scallion Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 400 grams
🧂 Salt 2 grams (for the dough)
💧 Room temperature water 192 grams
🧅 Scallions 2 pieces (white and green parts handled separately)
🐑 Lamb leg (with fat) 500 grams
🍲 Chicken broth 150 grams (can substitute with fish broth)
🌿 Five-spice powder 1/8 teaspoon
🌶 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1/8 teaspoon
⚪ White pepper powder 1/4 teaspoon
🫚 Grated ginger 1 tablespoon
🍯 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons
🧂 Salt 1/4 teaspoon (for final seasoning of the filling)
🫒 Cooking oil 3 tablespoons (for making scallion oil)
🌿 Whole Sichuan peppercorns 2 teaspoons (for frying scallion oil)
🌾 Dry flour as needed (to prevent stickiness when rolling wrappers)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪵 Wooden bowl (or mixing bowl)
🥛 Measuring cup
🥢 Rolling pin
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Ladle, bowls

## Preparation Steps
**Kneading and Resting the Dough**
1.  Mix all-purpose flour with salt, slowly add room temperature water while stirring until it forms flaky pieces, then knead into a firm dough.
    **Key Point**: The water-to-flour ratio is about 48%. Initially, it just needs to form a ball; a smooth surface is not required.
2.  First rest for 20 minutes, take out and knead for about 30 seconds until the gluten tightens.
3.  Second rest for 20 minutes, knead again for 30 seconds until the dough becomes smooth.
4.  Final rest for 1 hour to allow the gluten to fully relax.

**Making the Filling**
1.  Finely chop the white parts of the scallions; shred the green parts for making scallion oil.
2.  Dice the lamb leg, then chop by hand, retaining a slightly coarse texture.
3.  Add five-spice powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, white pepper powder, grated ginger, and light soy sauce to the meat. Mix initially.
4.  Gradually add the chicken broth to the filling, stirring quickly in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed and the filling becomes sticky and cohesive.
5.  Spread the chopped white scallion parts over the surface of the meat filling.
6.  Heat 3 tablespoons of cooking oil in a pan, fry the shredded green scallion parts until they turn golden brown, then add the whole Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant. Remove the aromatics.
7.  Pour the hot scallion oil over the scallions on the filling and mix thoroughly while hot.
    **Optional**: Chop the fried scallions and mix them back into the filling for extra fragrance.
8.  Finally, add 1/4 teaspoon of salt to taste, and mix well.

**Rolling Wrappers and Shaping**
1.  Roll the rested dough into a long log. Divide into equal small pieces by cutting or pinching.
2.  Dust the small pieces with a little dry flour, flatten them, and roll them into round wrappers with a thicker center and thinner edges using a rolling pin.
    **Advanced Tip**: Rotate the wrapper while rolling using both hands for better efficiency.
3.  Place a suitable amount of filling in the center of the wrapper. Fold it in half and pinch the center shut. Then, use your thumb and forefinger to squeeze both sides toward the middle, forming a ingot shape. Ensure the seal is tight.

**Cooking**
1.  Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the dumplings and gently push them with the back of a slotted spoon to prevent sticking to the bottom.
2.  When the water boils again, add a small bowl of cold water. Repeat this process 2–3 times.
3.  Once all the dumplings float to the surface and appear plump and swollen, they are ready to be removed.
    **Key Point**: Adding cold water prevents the boiling water from bursting the dumpling wrappers, ensuring the wrappers are cooked through and the filling is fully cooked.

**Recommended Dipping Sauce**
Mix vinegar, light soy sauce, chili oil, and cilantro to create a dipping sauce. Enjoy the enhanced flavor when paired with the dumplings.

## Health Advice
Lamb and scallion dumplings are rich in protein and iron, making them suitable for those with high physical exertion or who tend to feel cold. However, control the salt content in the dipping sauce to avoid excessive sodium intake.
