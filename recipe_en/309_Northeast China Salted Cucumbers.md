# Northeast China Salted Cucumbers
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Cucumbers 5 jin (wash and remove stems, cut into small pieces with the pulp)
🌶️ Thin-skinned peppers 250g (half jin, wash and remove stems, flatten and cut into diagonal strips)
🌿 Celery 250g (half jin, flatten thick parts and cut into small sections)
🧄 Garlic 2 heads (slice)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (about half a palm size, cut into large slices)
🧂 Salt 250g
🍾 Light soy sauce 250g
🍬 White sugar 125g
🍶 White vinegar 75g (can be added when serving)
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) 75g
🧂 MSG 30g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large basin
🧽 Gauze or mesh bag
🍳 Wok + steamer rack (used together for pressing out water)
🪣 Small pot
🥄 Mixing tool
🧊 Refrigerator

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut washed and stemmed cucumbers lengthwise into quarters, then into sections. For larger pieces, make 1–2 extra cuts to ensure each piece has pulp. Flatten thin-skinned peppers and cut into diagonal strips. Flatten thick parts of celery and cut into small sections.
2. Place cucumbers, peppers, and celery into a large basin. Add 250g salt. Mix by hand until the salt grains are completely dissolved. Marinate for about 4 hours, until cucumbers are slightly translucent, flexible, and do not break easily when bent.
3. Transfer the marinated ingredients into a gauze or mesh bag. Spread evenly on a wok set with a steamer rack. Place a heavy basin filled with water on top to press. Press for over 2 hours, ensuring even pressure.
4. In a small pot, add 250g light soy sauce, 125g white sugar, 75g white vinegar, and 75g baijiu. Stir over high heat until the sugar is completely dissolved. Bring the sauce to a boil, then turn off the heat and let it cool naturally.
5. Slice garlic. Cut ginger into large slices.
6. Place the pressed cucumbers and other ingredients into a clean, oil-free and water-free container. Add sliced ginger and garlic, 30g MSG, and the completely cooled sauce. Mix thoroughly by hand.
7. Cover with a lid or plastic wrap. Refrigerate overnight. Serve the next day.
8. When serving, add more white vinegar to soak according to preference. Best enjoyed with plain rice and warm water.

## Health Advice
This salted cucumber dish is high in salt. It is best paired with light staple foods. People with high blood pressure should taste sparingly. Celery and peppers are rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion, but the sodium content doubles after pickling, so they should not be consumed in large quantities as vegetables.
