# Thai Hand-Labeled Red Tea  
Origin: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All year round  

🧂 **Ingredients**  
- Tea powder  
- Condensed milk  
- White sugar  
- Milk  

🛠️ **Utensils**  
- Kitchen scale  
- Graduated container  
- Hong Kong-style milk tea filter bag  

📋 **Steps**  
1. Bring 600 ml of water to a rolling boil over high heat.  
2. Place 20 g of tea powder into the filter bag, pour the boiling water through the filter bag, and repeat the filtering process 20 times.  
3. Using a kitchen scale, measure 24 g of condensed milk, 24 g of white sugar, and 18 ml of milk, and place them in a kettle with a capacity of at least 1000 ml.  
4. Pour the filtered tea into the kettle and stir until the white sugar is completely dissolved.  
5. Refrigerate the kettle for at least 4 hours to allow the tea to cool and the flavors to meld.  
6. Before serving, add 6–8 ice cubes.  

⚠️ **Notes**  
- The quantities of water, tea powder, white sugar, milk, and condensed milk must be strictly measured according to the proportions.  
- The tea must be filtered enough times (20 passes) to ensure a rich tea flavor.  
- The refrigeration time should be no less than 4 hours to guarantee a smooth texture.  
- Ice cubes are optional and should be added just before serving.  

## Health Advice  
The combination of condensed milk, milk, and white sugar makes this Thai Hand-Labeled Red Tea high in sugar; it is recommended to enjoy it as an occasional dessert. To control sugar intake, reduce the amount of white sugar or use low-fat condensed milk.
