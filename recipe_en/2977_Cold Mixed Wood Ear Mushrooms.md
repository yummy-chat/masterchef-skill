# Cold Mixed Wood Ear Mushrooms
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

---
🥬 **Ingredients**
*   Dried wood ear mushrooms 20g (or fresh wet wood ear mushrooms 120g)
*   Garlic cloves 2-3
*   Thai bird's eye chilies 2

🧂 **Seasonings**
*   Salt 2g
*   White sugar 5-10g
*   Light soy sauce 15ml
*   Vinegar 15ml
*   Sesame oil 5ml
*   Wasabi approximately 2cm (optional)

🍳 **Cookware**
*   Cooking pot
*   Medium or large bowl
*   Knife
*   Cutting board

---
**📝 Steps**

1.  **Prepare dried wood ear mushrooms**: Place the dried wood ear mushrooms in about 400ml of water and soak for 45 minutes. If using fresh wet wood ear mushrooms, skip this step.
2.  **Clean the wood ear mushrooms**: Trim off the tough hard stems from the soaked wood ear mushrooms and wash them thoroughly.
3.  **Blanch the wood ear mushrooms**: Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the cleaned wood ear mushrooms, and cook over high heat for 1.5-2 minutes.
4.  **Prepare the sauce**: Mince the garlic cloves and chop the Thai bird's eye chilies, put them in a bowl. Add salt, white sugar, light soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, and wasabi in order, then stir to combine.
5.  **Mix together**: Remove the cooked wood ear mushrooms from the pot, drain them thoroughly, and place them into the bowl with the prepared sauce.
6.  **Plate and finish**: Mix the wood ear mushrooms well with the sauce, then transfer to a serving plate.

---
⚠️ **Key Points**
*   The stems of the wood ear mushrooms must be removed and washed thoroughly to avoid affecting the texture.
*   The blanching time for the wood ear mushrooms should not be too long; 1.5-2 minutes is enough to keep them crispy.
*   Let it sit for 15 minutes after mixing to allow the wood ear mushrooms to cool and absorb the flavors for a better taste.

## Health Tips
This cold mixed wood ear mushroom dish is rich in dietary fiber and wood ear mushroom polysaccharides, with very low calories. It is suitable for people during fat-loss periods or those needing to cleanse the intestines. Note that wood ear mushrooms should not be soaked for more than 2 hours; overnight soaking can easily breed bacteria.
