# Snow Pear, Apple, and Lean Pork Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Lean pork 250-300g (tenderloin or shin meat, cut into pieces and blanched)  
🍐 Snow pear 1 (cored and cut into pieces, with skin)  
🍎 Apple 1 (cut into pieces, with skin)  
🍄 Snow fungus (white fungus) half a piece (soaked in advance, yellow stem removed)  
🥜 Dried figs to taste  
🌰 Apricot kernels (sweet and bitter) to taste  
💧 Water sufficient quantity (enough to cover ingredients)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Electric soup pot (stainless steel inner pot)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Bowls  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
⚗️ Strainer (for blanching)  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the lean pork into pieces. Place in cold water and bring to a boil to blanch. Skim off the foam, then remove and drain.  
2. Wash the snow pear and apple, keeping the skin. Core the snow pear and cut into pieces; cut the apple directly into pieces.  
3. Soak the snow fungus until soft, then cut off the hard yellow stem at the center. Set aside along with the dried figs and apricot kernels.  
4. Place all ingredients into the electric soup pot. Add enough water to submerge the ingredients.  
5. Activate the soup cooking function on the electric pot and cook for approximately 1 hour or more.  
- **Key point**: No additional salt or sugar is needed; the natural sweetness of the fruit is used for flavor. Dried dates are omitted to avoid excessive sweetness.  
6. Once the soup is clear, the fruit is soft, and the lean pork is fully cooked, it is ready to be served.  
- **Suggested benefits**: This clear and sweet soup may aid digestion and help relieve coughs and phlegm.  

## Health Advice  
This soup is rich in vitamins and lung-nourishing ingredients, making it suitable for warm consumption during the dry autumn and winter seasons or during recovery from a cough. However, as pork contains a moderate amount of fat, it is recommended to drink more of the broth and eat less of the meat. Bitter apricot kernels contain trace amounts of cyanogenic glycosides, so consumption should be controlled for pregnant women and children.
