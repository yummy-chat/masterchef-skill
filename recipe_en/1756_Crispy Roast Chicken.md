# Crispy Roast Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 three-yellow chicken (about 2.5 jin, tender chicken around 100 days old)
🧅 1 onion (finely chopped)
🧄适量 garlic (crushed and minced)
🫚适量 ginger (crushed and minced)
🌿 A little cassia bark
🌟 A little star anise
🍃 A little bay leaf
🧂 10g thirteen-spice powder
🧂 5g five-spice powder
🧂 10g sand ginger powder
🧂 50g salt
🍶 100g vinegar
🍶 20g Zhejiang vinegar
🍯 30g maltose
🍶 A little Erguotou (Chinese liquor)
🧂 A little baking soda (optional)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chopping board, kitchen knife
🥣 Glass bowl
🪝 Metal hook (for hanging the whole chicken)
🍳 Wok (for boiling water and deep-frying the chicken)
🥄 Soup ladle, slotted spoon
🌀 Electric fan
🪣 Large basin (for rinsing the marinated chicken)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the aromatics**: Finely chop the onion. Crush and mince the ginger and garlic. Mix together and set aside.
2. **Make the marinade**: In a bowl, combine the chopped onion, minced ginger and garlic. Add thirteen-spice powder, five-spice powder, sand ginger powder, salt, cassia bark, star anise, and bay leaf. Mix well with your hands until the vegetables release liquid. The amount of salt cannot be reduced; the mixture must release enough juice to properly infuse the chicken.
3. **Marinate the chicken**: Place the chicken (with feet removed) into the marinade bowl. Rub the marinade evenly all over the chicken, inside and out. Marinate for two hours, ensuring every part is thoroughly coated.
4. **Make the crispy skin water**: In another bowl, combine vinegar, Zhejiang vinegar, maltose, a little Erguotou, and a little baking soda. Stir until the maltose is fully dissolved and the vinegar and sugar are well blended.
5. **Rinse off the spices**: Remove the marinated chicken. Quickly rinse the skin under running water to remove the spice bits. Do not soak for long, to avoid diluting the chicken's flavor.
6. **Blanch the skin**: Hang the chicken on a hook. Pour boiling water over the chicken skin from top to bottom, causing the skin to shrink, tighten, and set.
7. **Apply the crispy skin water**: While the chicken is still hanging, evenly pour the crispy skin water over the entire chicken, ensuring every part is coated.
8. **Dry the skin**: Use an electric fan to blow air on the chicken until the skin is completely dry. Judgment criteria: The skin should feel dry and not sticky to the touch. The skin must be completely dry, otherwise it will not become crispy after frying.
9. **First fry (cooking)**: Pour enough oil into a wok and heat. Gently place the whole chicken in the oil and fry over low heat. Continuously use a ladle to scoop hot oil and pour it over the chicken. Fry for 10-15 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked. Judgment method: Pierce the thickest part of the chicken thigh with a toothpick; if no blood runs out, it's done. This step is for cooking the chicken through, so use low heat to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.
10. **Second fry (crisping)**: Remove the chicken. Increase the heat to high and bring the oil to about 70% hot. Return the chicken to the oil for a quick second fry, allowing the skin to puff up and become crispy under the high heat. Fry until the skin is golden red and glossy. The second fry should be brief, just until the skin is crispy.

## Health Advice
This crispy roast chicken is rich in protein. Although the skin is delicious, it is also high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Suitable for healthy adults as an occasional treat. For the elderly and children, be sure to remove the bones before serving.
