# Chinese Cabbage Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Spinach 130g (blended with juice for dough)
🧊 Ice cubes and water as needed (for cooling spinach juice)
🌾 All-purpose flour 420g (divided, for green and white dough)
🍗 Bone-in chicken thigh 1kg (about 5 thighs, deboned and minced)
🥬 Chinese cabbage 350g (about 1/4 head, chopped)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms 100g (stems removed, chopped)
🧅 Scallions 4 (white and green parts chopped separately)
🫚 Ginger 1 tbsp (grated into paste)
🧂 Salt as needed (used in stages)
🌿 Five-spice powder 1/8 tsp
🌶 Sichuan peppercorn powder 1/4 tsp
🌶 White pepper powder 1/2 tsp
🫗 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp
🫗 Dark soy sauce 1 tbsp
🫒 Sesame oil 2 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
🍲 Chicken broth 1/2 cup
🫒 Cooking oil 3 tbsp

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Stockpot
🥤 Blender
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large bowls
🔪 Cutting board and knives
🍳 Wok
🥖 Rolling pin
🫙 Plastic wrap
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔧 Dough scraper

## Steps
1.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the washed spinach and blanch for about 10 seconds until softened. Quickly remove. Transfer to a blender, add a few ice cubes and a small amount of water, and blend into a smooth spinach juice, yielding about 210g.
   **Key Point**: Keep the blanching time short; adding ice cubes helps maintain a vibrant green color. The finer the juice, the more even the dough color.

2.  Take 420g all-purpose flour, add 1/4 tsp salt, and pour in all the spinach juice. Stir into a shaggy mass, then knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 10-15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The ratio of flour to liquid is approximately 2:1.

3.  Take another 420g all-purpose flour, add 1/4 tsp salt, and gradually add 190g water. Stir into a shaggy mass, then knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 10-15 minutes.

4.  Remove both the green and white doughs, knead each for 1 minute, return to bowls, cover, and rest for 10-15 minutes. Repeat this process once. Finally, knead both doughs until smooth on the surface, cover, and let rest for a final 1 hour.

5.  Cut the Chinese cabbage into thin strips, then chop finely. Place in a bowl, add 1 tsp salt, mix well, and let sit for 15 minutes to draw out water. Afterwards, squeeze out the moisture thoroughly and set aside.

6.  Finely chop the scallion whites and greens separately. Remove the stems from the shiitake mushrooms and chop finely. Debone the chicken thighs, cut into small pieces, then mince into filling.

7.  Heat 3 tbsp of oil in a wok over high heat. Add the chopped scallion whites and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the chopped shiitake mushrooms and 1/2 tsp salt, stir-fry until the mushrooms are softened, slightly browned, and intensely aromatic. Remove from heat and set aside.

8.  Place the minced chicken filling in a large bowl. Add grated ginger, five-spice powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, and oyster sauce in order. Gradually add the 1/2 cup of chicken broth, using chopsticks to vigorously stir in one direction until the filling becomes very sticky, elastic, and reaches a semi-solid, stringy state.
   **Key Point**: Add the broth in stages and thoroughly whip it in; this is key for a juicy filling. Stirring until elastic gives the filling a springy, chewy texture.

9.  Add the squeezed-dry Chinese cabbage, the cooled cooked mushroom and scallion oil mixture, and the chopped scallion greens to the seasoned meat filling. Mix briefly and simply using a dough scraper.
   **Key Point**: Vegetables should be added last to avoid over-mixing, which can cause them to release water.

10. Roll the rested white dough into a long log. Flatten the green dough and roll it into a rectangle thin enough to encase the white log, with an even thickness. Sprinkle a little water on the green sheet and spread it. Place the white log at one end, then use a dough scraper to help tightly roll the green sheet around the white log, forming a two-color long log. Roll the log gently to make it even in thickness and length.

11. Cut the two-color log into pieces about 1cm thick. Dust with dry flour to prevent sticking. Flatten each piece with your palm, then roll into a round dumpling wrapper with a slightly thicker center and thinner edges.
    **Key Point**: Cut the pieces evenly. When rolling, ensure the green edge remains on the outer perimeter of the wrapper.

12. Take one dumpling wrapper and place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold the wrapper in half. First, pinch the center tightly. Then, use the tiger-mouth area formed by your thumb and index finger to pinch the edges tightly, simultaneously pushing towards the center to create pleats, forming a shape with a plump belly in the middle and pleated edges.

13. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the dumplings, gently pushing with the back of a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. When the water returns to a boil, add one bowl of cold water. Repeat the cold water addition twice. Once all dumplings float to the surface, the wrappers appear translucent and plump, remove them with a slotted spoon.

## Health Suggestions
These Chinese cabbage dumplings feature a chicken filling within a spinach dough, offering a combination of low fat, high protein, and rich dietary fiber. They are suitable for weight control or muscle building. Note: Remove fat from the chicken thighs, and squeeze the Chinese cabbage dry to reduce sodium intake. Those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt added.
