# Home-style Claypot Rice with Pork Ribs  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Glutinous rice approx. 10% (for thicker crispy rice crust)  
🍚 Pearl rice approx. 30% (for chewy texture)  
🍚 Thai jasmine rice approx. 60% (for main aroma)  
🥩 Lean pork ribs moderate amount (chopped into small pieces, less fat)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms moderate amount (pre-soaked and cleaned)  
🥬 Lettuce moderate amount  
🫑 Green bell pepper moderate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper moderate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🧅 Onion moderate amount (cut into small pieces)  
🫚 Ginger moderate amount (sliced into thin pieces)  
🧄 Garlic moderate amount (sliced into thin pieces)  
🧅 Scallion moderate amount (cut into small sections)  
🫘 Fermented black beans moderate amount (minced)  
🧂 Table salt 1 teaspoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce 3 grams  
🥄 Light soy sauce 5 grams  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 3 grams  
🧂 White pepper a pinch  
🫒 Sesame oil moderate amount (for marinating and drizzling along the pot edge)  
🧂 Baking soda moderate amount (for cleaning the ribs)  
🫒 Cooking oil moderate amount (for brushing the pot and marinating)  
💧 Water starch moderate amount (optional, to keep ribs juicy)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥘 Claypot  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🖌️ Oil brush  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔥 Commercial gas stove (or home stove)  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the mixed rice**: Mix glutinous rice, pearl rice, and Thai jasmine rice in a ratio of about 1:3:6. Rinse and soak in fresh water for 3 hours.  
2. **Soak the shiitake mushrooms**: Soak dried shiitake mushrooms in water for 1 hour, clean, and cut in half.  
3. **Clean the ribs**: Chop the ribs into small pieces. Add baking soda and water, scrub several times to remove blood, then squeeze out excess water.  
4. **Marinate**: Add ginger slices, garlic slices, scallion sections, minced fermented black beans, shiitake mushrooms, salt, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper to the ribs. Mix well. Optionally add water starch for juiciness. Finally, drizzle with a little sesame oil and mix. Add green and red bell pepper pieces and onion pieces, mix, and set aside to marinate.  
5. **Preheat and oil the pot**: Heat the claypot over medium-low heat to about 90°C. Use an oil brush to evenly coat the interior wall with cooking oil.  
6. **Add rice and water**: Drain the soaked rice and spread it evenly in the claypot. Add fresh water until it just covers the rice.  
7. **First stage rice cooking**: Cover the pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to very low heat and simmer for about 18 minutes, until the water is mostly absorbed and the rice has set.  
8. **Poke holes for ventilation**: Uncover and use chopsticks to poke several holes in the rice.  
9. **Arrange the ribs**: Evenly spread the marinated ribs and all the accompanying ingredients on top of the rice.  
10. **Drizzle oil and simmer**: Cover the pot, drizzle a circle of sesame oil along the edge, and continue simmering over very low heat for about 26–28 minutes. During the last 8 minutes, wear gloves and tilt the claypot in different directions to ensure even heating around the edges, promoting the formation of crispy rice crust.  
11. **Add greens and serve**: After simmering, add lettuce, cover again, and let the residual heat wilt it. Serve immediately.  

## Health Suggestions  
Pork ribs are rich in high-quality protein. Pairing them with mixed grain rice and shiitake mushrooms increases dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for those needing energy replenishment. Be mindful of salt and oyster sauce usage; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amounts as needed.
