# Baby Mixed Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Cooked rice, appropriate amount
🥔 Chinese yam, 1 small section
🍅 Tomato, half
🥬 Purple-leaf lettuce, 1 leaf
🐟 Cod fillet, 1 piece
🍪 Teething biscuit, 1 piece
🥚 Egg, 1
🥄 Lard, 1 spoonful

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍱 Electric lunch box
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Finely dice the Chinese yam and tomato on the cutting board into small pieces.
   **Note**: The size of the pieces should be suitable for the baby's chewing ability.
2. Place the cooked rice, diced yam, diced tomato, purple-leaf lettuce, cod fillet, and teething biscuit together in a stainless steel bowl.
3. Crack a raw egg into the bowl and use a spoon to stir all ingredients until evenly coated with the egg mixture.
4. Add one spoonful of lard and continue stirring until fully combined.
5. Place the stainless steel bowl into the electric lunch box, cover with the lid, and heat until all ingredients are fully cooked and remain warm.
   **Note**: Adjust the heating time and mode according to the electric lunch box instructions, ensuring the fish fillet and egg mixture are completely set.
6. When feeding, let the baby gnaw on the teething biscuit first. If the baby does not like vegetables such as purple-leaf lettuce, use a spoon to crush and mix the leaves into the rice, ensuring they are blended together before feeding.

## Health Tips
Finely dicing the Chinese yam and tomato helps the baby swallow easily. Cod and eggs provide high-quality protein, and adding lard increases energy density, making this suitable for babies over ten months old. Choose boneless cod fillets and ensure the fish fillet and egg mixture are fully cooked.
