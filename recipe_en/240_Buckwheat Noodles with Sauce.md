# Buckwheat Noodles with Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

🛒 **Ingredients**  
- 🥢 Buckwheat noodles 100g  
- 🥒 Cucumber 0.5 piece  
- 🥕 Carrot 0.5 piece  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- 🌶️ Lao Gan Ma chili sauce 20ml  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- 🍲 Boiling pot  
- 🍽️ Plate  
- 🥢 Mixing tool  
- 🔪 Kitchen knife  

📝 **Steps**  
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add buckwheat noodles, and cook over medium heat for 8–10 minutes until tender with no hard core. Drain well, then rinse with cold water or ice water for later use.  
   **Key point**: Chilling the noodles in the refrigerator after draining makes them more refreshing and chewy.  

2. Wash the cucumber and carrot, and cut them into even strips (about 5 cm long and 0.5 cm thick).  

3. Place the cooked buckwheat noodles, cucumber strips, and carrot strips into a plate. Add Lao Gan Ma chili sauce and mix thoroughly with chopsticks or a mixing tool until the sauce evenly coats all ingredients, then serve.  
   **Note**: The toppings can be replaced with shrimp, minced meat, etc.; the sauce can also be substituted with peanut butter, sesame paste, etc. according to preference. Buckwheat noodles have similar calories to other noodles and should not be solely relied upon as a low-calorie staple.  

## Health Advice
Buckwheat noodles are rich in dietary fiber and B vitamins. Paired with cucumber and carrot, they increase vitamin intake, making them suitable for those seeking a balanced diet. Note that Lao Gan Ma chili sauce is high in salt and fat, so use it in moderation to avoid excessive sodium intake.
