# Bean and Meat Sauce Buns
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Flour, as needed  
🫧 Yeast, 2 grams  
🫘 Green beans, as needed (finely chopped)  
🥩 Ground pork, as needed  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (finely chopped)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed (finely chopped)  
🫘 Soybean paste, 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, 1 teaspoon  
🧂 MSG, 1 teaspoon  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🫖 Steamer or steaming basket  
🔪 Cutting board  
🪔 Rolling pin  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Mix the flour and yeast evenly, then gradually add warm water, stirring until flaky. Knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Key point**: Using warm water helps activate the yeast.  
2. Place the kneaded dough in a warm area to rise until it doubles in volume.  
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil, blanch the green beans for 3 minutes, then remove and rinse with cold water. Chop them finely and set aside.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat, add the ground pork and stir-fry until it changes color. Add the scallion and ginger and stir-fry until fragrant.  
   **Note**: Stir-fry over medium heat; cook until the meat is completely browned.  
2. Add the soybean paste, dark soy sauce, salt, and MSG to the meat. Stir-fry evenly until the meat is well colored.  
3. Add the chopped green beans to the wok and stir-fry together with the meat filling until well combined. Remove from heat and let it cool.  
4. Take out the risen dough, knead it to release air, then roll it into a long log. Cut into evenly sized pieces.  
5. Roll each piece into a wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place a generous amount of the bean and meat filling in the center, pleat the edges to seal, and ensure the seal is tight.  
   **Key point**: The seal must be tightly pinched to prevent leakage; the shape is flexible.  
6. Place the assembled buns in a steamer lined with a damp cloth, cover, and let them rise for a second time for about 10 minutes.  
7. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, then place the steamer on top. Steam over high heat for 15 minutes.  
8. Turn off the heat and let the buns sit covered for 5 minutes before opening the lid.  
   **Key point**: Resting prevents the buns from collapsing and shrinking due to a sudden temperature drop.  

## Health Tips  
Bean and meat sauce buns are rich in protein and dietary fiber, making them a convenient portable lunch for busy office workers. However, note that soybean paste is high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. Always let the buns rest for 5 minutes after steaming before uncovering to prevent them from shrinking and collapsing.
