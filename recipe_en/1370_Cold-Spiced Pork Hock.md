# Cold-Spiced Pork Hock

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍖 1 braised pork hock (pre-braised, no sugar, no food coloring, no artificial flavors)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into strips, pre-soaked in water)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ 3-5 small chili peppers (chopped, optional for spicy preference)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (needs to be diluted with boiling water)  
🍶 1 tablespoon vinegar  
🥡 1.5 tablespoons light soy sauce  
🍬 Half a tablespoon sugar  
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount  
🌸 Sichuan pepper oil, appropriate amount  
🍯 A little honey

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪚 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🥘 Small pot/wok (for boiling water)

## Instructions
1. Remove the bone from the braised pork hock and slice into thick pieces of suitable size. Cut the onion into strips, soak in water for a while, then drain and spread on the bottom of a plate. Mince the garlic and chop the small chili peppers; set aside.  
   **Note**: Soaking the onion strips removes the pungent taste and adds a crunchy, tender texture.
2. Prepare the dressing: Take a small amount of boiling water and dilute the oyster sauce until smooth. Then add vinegar, light soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and Sichuan pepper oil in order, and mix thoroughly.  
   **Note**: Oyster sauce must be diluted with boiling water first, otherwise it is difficult to mix evenly, affecting the coating effect.
3. Add the minced garlic and chopped chili peppers to the sauce mixture, and finally squeeze in a little honey. Stir again to combine.  
   **Key point**: Minced garlic helps cut the grease and enhance the aroma, while honey softens the overall flavor.
4. Arrange the sliced pork hock neatly on top of the onion strips, evenly pour the prepared dressing over them, and serve.

## Health Advice
Although pork hock is flavorful, it is high in fat. It is recommended to eat it with onion strips to help reduce greasiness and increase dietary fiber. This dish is suitable for occasional indulgence; people with high blood lipids should consume only in moderation.
