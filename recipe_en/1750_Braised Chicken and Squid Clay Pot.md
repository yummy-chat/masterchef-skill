# Braised Chicken and Squid Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Free-range chicken, half (chopped into large pieces)
🦑 Dried squid (local dried squid), 1
🫚 Ginger, to taste (cut into chunks)
🧄 Garlic, to taste
🧅 Small onions, to taste
🌿 Scallions, to taste
🫑 Green and red bell peppers, to taste (cut into chunks, for color)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (used twice: a little for marinating, poured along the wok edge during stir-frying)
🥡 Light soy sauce, to taste (added once during marinating and once during stir-frying; slightly more for braised dishes)
🍯 Oyster sauce, to taste (to enhance freshness during marinating, and added again during braising)
🌶️ White pepper powder, a pinch (used once during marinating and once for thickening sauce before serving)
🌾 Cornstarch (potato starch), a pinch (mixed in during marinating, and used to make a slurry before serving)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🍂 Dark soy sauce, a little (for coloring during braising)
🫗 Sesame oil, a little (added to the thickening sauce before serving)
🥄 Braising sauce, one small spoonful (homemade by the blogger, ingredients unspecified)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Wok (Chinese carbon steel wok)
🥄 Wok spatula
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the dried squid**: Soak the dried squid in warm water for about 30 minutes until soft. After soaking, remove the hard transparent quill in the middle and the beak from the head, keeping the outer skin. Wash clean, then score the inner side in a crosshatch pattern, and cut into triangular pieces.
**Note**: Ensure sufficient soaking time; the quill and beak must be thoroughly removed; scoring helps the squid curl nicely and absorb flavors better.

2. **Marinate the chicken**: Cut the free-range chicken into large pieces and place in a bowl. Add salt, sugar, a little cooking wine, a generous amount of light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper powder, and a little cornstarch. Mix well with your hands and marinate for about ten minutes.
**Note**: Marinate for enough time to allow the chicken to fully absorb the flavors.

3. **Fry the aromatics**: Heat oil in the wok. First, add ginger chunks and fry until fragrant. Then add garlic and small onions in sequence, frying until aromatic. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
**Note**: Add aromatics in batches according to their doneness to fully release their fragrance.

4. **Pan-fry the chicken**: Leave a base amount of oil in the wok. Add the marinated chicken pieces, spreading them out. Fry until golden brown on one side, then flip and continue frying until both sides are golden brown and fragrant.
**Note**: The chicken must be fried until sufficiently fragrant and cooked through—this is key to the dish’s flavor; add a little more oil if needed during frying.

5. **Combine and braise**: Once the chicken is fragrant, add the prepared squid and one small spoonful of braising sauce. Return the fried aromatics to the wok. Pour cooking wine along the wok edge and stir-fry over high heat until fragrant. Then add light soy sauce for a quick stir-fry, and mix well. Add enough water to submerge the ingredients, then add oyster sauce for freshness and a little dark soy sauce for color.
**Note**: Ensure all ingredients are well stir-fried until fragrant before adding water; after adding water, reduce to medium-low heat and braise for 2-3 minutes to let the squid’s umami fully blend with the chicken and aromatics.

6. **Thicken the sauce and serve**: After braising, pour in a slurry made from cornstarch water, a pinch of white pepper powder, and sesame oil. Stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients. Finally, add the cut green and red bell peppers, stir-fry a few times, and serve.
**Note**: Add the thickening slurry just before serving; add the bell peppers last to preserve their color and crisp texture.

## Health Advice
The braised chicken and squid clay pot is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for those needing nourishment. However, light soy sauce and oyster sauce are high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition, use less salt during braising, and rely on the squid’s seafood flavor to enhance taste for a healthier meal.
