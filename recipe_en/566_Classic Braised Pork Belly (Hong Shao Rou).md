# Classic Braised Pork Belly (Hong Shao Rou)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with alternating layers of lean and fat (appropriate amount, 60% lean and 40% fat, cut into large pieces)
🧅 Small scallion (one section, tied into a knot)
🫚 Ginger (appropriate amount, sliced)
🧂 Rock sugar (appropriate amount)
🧂 Star anise (appropriate amount)
🧂 Cinnamon stick (appropriate amount)
🧅 Scallion segments (appropriate amount)
🧂 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount)
🍶 Hua Diao wine (appropriate amount)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🫕 Clay pot
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat the iron wok, place the whole piece of pork belly skin-side down and sear until golden and blistered to remove any hairs and impurities. Then put it into cold water, bring to a boil, and cook for about one minute before removing.
2. Cut the blanched pork belly into large pieces.
3. Stir-fry the pork pieces over high heat until a significant amount of lard is rendered. Be sure to skim off the excess lard—this is the key to achieving a rich but not greasy texture.
4. Reduce to medium-low heat, add star anise, cinnamon stick, scallion segments, ginger slices, and rock sugar. Stir-fry together until the pork pieces develop a glossy, reddish color.
5. Add light soy sauce and Hua Diao wine, then immediately pour in hot water until the pork is just covered.
6. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the tied scallion knot. Continue boiling over high or medium heat for 5 minutes.
7. Transfer all ingredients from the wok to a clay pot. Add salt, cover, and simmer over low heat for 45 minutes.
8. Remove the lid, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens and evenly coats the pork pieces, remove from heat and plate.

## Health Tips
This braised pork belly is rich in high-quality protein and fat, making it suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or those looking to gain weight. However, people with high blood lipids or those on a weight-loss diet should consume it in moderation. Skimming off the excess lard helps retain flavor while reducing the fat burden.
