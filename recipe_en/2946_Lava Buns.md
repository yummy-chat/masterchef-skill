# Lava Buns
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 All-purpose flour 300g
🧈 Lard 12g
🫧 Dry yeast 3g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 25g
🫧 Baking powder 3g
💧 Warm water 180g (approximately 35°C)
🥚 Salted egg yolks 60g
🧈 Butter 60g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 30g
🥛 Light cream 120g
🥛 Milk powder 15g
🟡 Custard powder 3g
🍮 Gelatin 3g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Whisk
🔪 Spatula
⚙️ Food processor
🪷 Sieve
🔥 Oven
🍲 Steamer
🫓 Kneading mat
🪚 Rolling pin
🔪 Pastry cutter
🫙 Plastic wrap
⚖️ Digital scale
🌡️ Infrared thermometer

## Preparation Steps
1.  Spray raw salted egg yolks with white liquor, then bake in a preheated oven at 170°C for 8–10 minutes until oil surfaces. Remove and grind in a food processor, then sift into a fine powder.
**Key point**: Bake until the yolks glisten with oil and deepen slightly in color; sifting ensures a smooth, lump-free filling.

2.  Add dry yeast and fine granulated sugar to 35°C warm water, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved. Mix all-purpose flour with baking powder, then gradually add the yeast water, stirring with chopsticks into a shaggy mixture. Add lard and knead into a rough dough.
**Key point**: Dissolve the sugar as much as possible; lard makes the dough whiter, moister, and more aromatic; vegetable oil can substitute for lard if unavailable.

3.  Cover the rough dough with a bowl or plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 10 minutes. Remove and knead on the work surface until roughly smooth.
**Key point**: Resting the dough makes it easier to smooth; it’s recommended to work on the countertop.

4.  Return the kneaded dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment until doubled in size.
**Key point**: If the room temperature is low, place in the oven to ferment for 1 to 1.5 hours; test by poking with a floured finger—if the indentation doesn’t spring back, it’s ready.

5.  Soak gelatin sheets in cold water for about 3 minutes until softened, then remove and set aside.
6.  Melt the butter, then add fine granulated sugar, light cream, milk powder, and custard powder sequentially, whisking until smooth.
7.  Add the salted egg yolk powder and softened gelatin to the liquid mixture, stirring over hot water until the gelatin is fully dissolved.
**Key point**: The temperature should not be too high, as it may affect the gelatin’s setting ability.

8.  After mixing the liquid thoroughly, strain it through a sieve into a wide dish, cover with plastic wrap, and freeze for about 30 minutes until it reaches a non-sticky, moldable consistency.
**Key point**: Using a wide dish shortens freezing time; straining ensures smoothness; the texture should be scoopable without sticking to your hands.

9.  Divide the frozen lava filling into small balls of about 25g each and set aside.
10. Remove the fermented dough, knead to release gas, and divide into small pieces of about 45g each. Roll them into balls, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest for 10 minutes.
11. Take one dough piece, flatten it with the smooth side down, and roll into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place a filling ball in the center, pinch the edges to seal tightly using the "tiger mouth" closing technique, then roll it into a round shape.
**Key point**: The seal must be tight to prevent the lava filling from bursting during steaming; the wrapper should be thicker in the center and thinner at the edges to avoid tearing at the bottom.

12. Place the assembled buns on a steamer tray lined with parchment paper, cover with a lid or damp cloth, and let them proof for about 20 minutes.
**Key point**: Proof until they visibly increase in size and feel light to the touch.

13. Bring water to a boil in the pot, place the steamer tray inside, and steam over high heat for 8 minutes. After turning off the heat, do not open the lid immediately; let them rest for 2 minutes.
**Key point**: Steaming must start with boiling water and be done over high heat; resting after turning off the heat prevents the buns from shrinking.

## Health Advice
Lava buns are high in sugar and fat, suitable for occasional indulgences. Pair with green tea to balance the taste. Salted egg yolks are rich in lecithin but high in calories; those with high blood lipids should enjoy in moderation.
