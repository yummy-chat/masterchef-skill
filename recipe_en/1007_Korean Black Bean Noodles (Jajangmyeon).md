# Korean Black Bean Noodles (Jajangmyeon)
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 1000g high-gluten flour
🧂 2g edible alkali (dissolved in water)
🧂 5g salt (dissolved in water)
💧 420g water (for dough)
🍞 14g dough improver (mixed with flour)
🧅 30g ginger slices (for oil frying)
🧅 60g scallion segments (for oil frying)
🫘 840g chunjang (600g for frying oil, 240g for stir-frying)
🧅 2200g onion (1700g cut into large chunks, 500g cut into small pieces)
🥬 300g cabbage (cut into small pieces)
🥩 300g pork (with alternating fat and lean, diced)
🧄 100g minced garlic
🍬 120g white sugar
🧂 100g MSG
🥔 40g potato starch (for thickening)
💧 60g water (for making water starch)
🫒 500g vegetable oil (for frying aromatics and chunjang)
🐖 100g lard
🧂 120g soy sauce

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍝 Pasta machine
🍳 Wok
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Mixing bowl
🔪 Knife and cutting board
📦 Airtight container

## Steps
1. **Make the dough**: Dissolve 2g edible alkali and 5g salt in 420g water. Mix 1000g high-gluten flour with 14g dough improver, then pour in the alkali-salt water and mix into dough flocculent.
2. **Press and cut the noodles**: Use a pasta machine to repeatedly press the dough flocculent into a smooth sheet 2.5mm thick, then cut into noodles using a 2.5mm round pasta cutter, shake loose, coil into portions, and refrigerate.
   *Note: Repeated pressing improves the noodle's chewy texture.*
3. **Prepare the vegetables**: Cut 1700g onion into large chunks and 500g into small pieces; cut 300g cabbage into small pieces and set aside.
4. **Pre-process the chunjang**: Add 500g vegetable oil to a wok, then add 30g ginger slices and 60g scallion segments into the cold oil. Fry over low heat until caramel brown, then remove. When the oil cools to 130°C, add 600g chunjang. Stir continuously over medium-low heat for 5 minutes until the sauce is fragrant, then scoop out and set aside.
   *Key point: Control temperature and stir constantly to prevent burning; the fried chunjang is more aromatic and easier to store.*
5. **Stir-fry the diced pork**: Pour 200g of the prepared oil and 100g lard into the wok. Add 300g diced pork and stir-fry until it changes color, then add 100g minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
6. **Enhance the pork's aroma**: Pour in 120g soy sauce and stir-fry until a sauce aroma emerges.
7. **Stir-fry and season the vegetables**: Add the large onion chunks and cabbage. Stir-fry over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes until the vegetables soften and release water. When they are half-softened, add 120g white sugar and 100g MSG, and stir well.
   *Note: Do not add salt at this stage.*
8. **Add the chunjang**: Add the pre-processed 240g chunjang and stir-fry until the ingredients are evenly colored and the onion becomes translucent.
9. **Add raw onion and thicken**: Pour in the remaining small onion pieces and quickly stir-fry a few times. Mix 40g potato starch with 60g water to make water starch, then drizzle it into the wok while stirring rapidly. Cook until the sauce is thick and smooth.
   *Key point: Adding raw onion at the end preserves its crisp texture; pour the water starch in batches and stir quickly to prevent lumps.*
10. **Cook the noodles**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil, cook the noodles until done, then remove them. First rinse them briefly in cold water, then return them to the noodle broth to heat through, and drain well.
    *Key point: Rinsing with cold water and then reheating is an essential step to keep the noodles smooth and chewy.*
11. **Assemble**: Place the noodles in a bowl, top with the black bean sauce, and serve with shredded cucumber.

## Health Advice
This jajangmyeon has a well-balanced ratio, with chewy noodles and a rich, flavorful sauce. However, it is relatively high in calories and sodium. It is recommended to control the portion size and pair it with shredded cucumber or kimchi to increase dietary fiber. The combination of pork and chunjang provides energy, making it suitable for times of high physical exertion. However, those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or cholesterol should be mindful of the salt content from the soy sauce and chunjang.
