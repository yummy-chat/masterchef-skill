# White-Cut Pork Knuckle 🐖
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Best for: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork knuckle, about 1 piece
🧵 Cotton string, as needed (for tying and shaping)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (added twice: blanching and simmering)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (added twice: blanching and simmering)
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for blanching to remove gamey flavor)
🌿 Spices (star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, etc.), as needed (for enhanced aroma during simmering)
🥄 Light soy sauce, a small amount (for seasoning; too much will darken the color)
🌶️ Red oil (chili oil), as needed (the soul of the final seasoning)
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed (for the dipping sauce)
🍶 Vinegar, as needed (for the dipping sauce)
🍬 Sugar, as needed (for the dipping sauce)
🌿 Chopped scallions, as needed (for garnish and added aroma)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🍲 Pot
♨️ Pressure cooker
📦 Plastic wrap
🍽️ Plate
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1.  **Cleaning and cutting**: Scrape the skin of the pork knuckle clean with a knife, then rinse thoroughly under running water. Cut into the thick part of the meat along the grain of the fibers without cutting through the skin, so the meat side can be spread flat.
    **Note**: Scraping the skin helps remove gamey flavors; when cutting, keep the meat attached to the skin for easier rolling and shaping later.

2.  **Tying and shaping**: Roll the cut pork knuckle tightly in one direction into a cylinder, and tie it securely with cotton string.
    **Key point**: The tighter it is rolled, the firmer and more presentable the slices will be; when tying, pull the string tight to ensure it does not come loose during simmering.

3.  **Blanching**: Place the tied pork knuckle in a pot of cold water, add scallion segments, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove the pork knuckle and rinse it clean under running water.
    **Note**: It must be placed in cold water to fully draw out blood from the interior.

4.  **Simmering**: Put the pork knuckle into a pressure cooker, add enough water to cover it, fresh scallion and ginger, the spices, and a small amount of light soy sauce. Cover the lid, heat on high until steam is released, then reduce to medium heat and pressure cook for 20 minutes.
    **To check doneness**: After natural pressure release, the pork knuckle is done when a chopstick can easily pierce through it.
    **Note**: Use only a small amount of light soy sauce to enhance flavor; too much will darken the color. Wait for the pressure to release naturally before opening the lid.

5.  **Cooling and setting**: Remove the cooked pork knuckle, wrap it tightly with plastic wrap while still hot, and refrigerate for about 3 hours until completely cooled and firm.
    **Key point**: When wrapping with plastic wrap, be sure to roll it tightly—this is crucial for a firm and springy texture; if air is not expelled or the wrap is loose, the slices may fall apart.

6.  **Slicing and seasoning**: Remove the plastic wrap and cotton string, slice the set pork knuckle into thin pieces, and arrange neatly on a plate. Mix minced garlic, vinegar, and sugar to make a dipping sauce, then combine with red oil and stir well. Evenly drizzle the sauce over the pork knuckle slices, and finally sprinkle with chopped scallions.
    **Note**: The thinner the slices, the better the texture; red oil is the soul of this dish's seasoning—its aroma and color depend on it. Use it generously, adjusting to taste, preferably more rather than less.

## Health Tips
This white-cut pork knuckle is rich in collagen, making it beneficial for skin nourishment and joint nutrition, but it is also high in fat and calories. People with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should consume it in moderation. The thinner the slices, the more springy the texture; refrigeration for setting is essential and cannot be skipped, otherwise the slices will fall apart easily.
