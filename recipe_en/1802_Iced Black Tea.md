# Iced Black Tea
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍂 Black tea 20g  
🍬 Rock sugar 250g  
🍋 Yellow lemon 2  
🍋 Green lemon 1  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 1 piece  
🫖 Hot water 1.5L  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for scrubbing lemons), 1 tsp (for pickling lemons)  
🍬 White sugar 2 tbsp (for pickling lemons)  
🧊 Ice cubes appropriate amount (for serving)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Knife  
🥣 Strainer  
🍳 Pot (for roasting tea, caramelizing sugar, and boiling tea)  
🥣 Glass bowl/container (for pickling lemons and holding tea)  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Prepare dried tangerine peel and lemons**: Soak the dried tangerine peel until soft, then use a knife to scrape off the white pith inside to avoid bitterness when boiling. Scrub the lemon surface with salt, rinse clean, and slice thinly. Use chopsticks to remove seeds to prevent the drink from becoming bitter.
2.  **Pickle the lemons**: Place the lemon slices in a container, add 2 tbsp of white sugar and 1 tsp of salt, mix well, and let sit to marinate and release the lemon flavor.
3.  **Roast the tea**: Heat a dry pot over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Add the black tea and roast until fragrant, then immediately remove and set aside.
4.  **Caramelize the sugar**: Place rock sugar in a dry pot over low heat and stir constantly. Once the sugar has completely melted and turned a light amber color, turn off the heat.  
    **Key point**: Turn off the heat when it is nearly amber. Use residual heat to prevent burning, which will give the tea a brighter color and richer flavor.
5.  **Boil the tea**: After turning off the heat, add the roasted tea leaves to the pot and stir a few times. Immediately add 1500ml of hot water and the prepared dried tangerine peel. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes, until the tea turns a deep red color.
6.  **Strain**: Use a strainer to filter out the tea leaves and dried tangerine peel, ensuring a clear tea liquid.
7.  **Cool and combine**: Let the tea cool to room temperature, then add the pickled lemon slices and ice cubes. Stir well.  
    **Key point**: The tea must be cooled before adding the lemons. High heat will destroy vitamin C and make the drink bitter.
8.  **Serve**: Pour into a glass and enjoy. This iced tea is refreshing and cuts through grease, making it especially suitable for pairing with oily foods like hot pot.

## Health Advice
This iced black tea has a high sugar content (250g of rock sugar). It is recommended to reduce the amount of sugar or use a sugar substitute. Diabetics and those monitoring their sugar intake should consume with caution. Lemons are rich in vitamin C, but high temperatures can destroy the nutrients, so be sure to add them only after the tea has cooled.
