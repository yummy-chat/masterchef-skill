# Konjac Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

---

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 3 eggs  
🌰 10g konjac powder  
🍫 10g cocoa powder  
🧂 50g erythritol  
🍋 1g cream of tartar  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔥 Oven  
⚡ Electric hand mixer  
🥧 Metal cake pan (any shape)  
📄 Baking paper  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🧤 Oven mitts  
🥣 Egg separator (optional)  

---

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 150°C (300°F).
2. Prepare two dry bowls: one for egg whites and one for egg yolks. The bowl for egg whites must be free of oil, but may contain a trace amount of water droplets; the bowl for egg yolks must be completely free of water droplets. Separately the egg whites and yolks completely, ensuring no yolk gets into the whites. To prevent overflow during whipping, the volume of egg whites should not exceed 1/8 of the whipping bowl's capacity.
3. Add 1g cream of tartar to the egg whites. Using an electric hand mixer, whip on high speed. When large bubbles appear, add all 50g erythritol. Reduce speed to medium-low and continue whipping until stiff peaks form. Test by lifting the mixer head: the meringue should form short, upright peaks. Invert the bowl; the meringue should not fall out. Keep the mixer head close to the bottom of the bowl during whipping to ensure evenness.
4. Add the egg yolks to the whipped meringue and mix on medium-low speed until uniform. Then add 10g cocoa powder and 10g konjac powder. First, incorporate them using a cutting-and-folding method: Hold a kitchen knife in your right hand, cut into the center of the batter from the bottom, scrape towards the 8 o'clock position up the side of the bowl, lift the batter, and fold it back into the center. Simultaneously, rotate the bowl counterclockwise from 9 o'clock to 7 o'clock with your left hand. Repeat at a steady pace of about 2 cycles per second. Once mostly combined, use the electric hand mixer on low speed to mix until completely uniform.
5. Line the cake pan with baking paper, pressing it to fit the bottom and sides. Pour the batter into the pan and smooth the surface with a kitchen knife. Gently tap the pan on the counter to release large air bubbles. Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack and bake for 25 minutes. When done, remove using oven mitts.
6. Drop the pan vertically from a height of about 15 cm (6 inches) onto the counter to release the hot air from inside. Wearing oven mitts, invert the pan and let it cool for about 10 minutes. Once completely cooled, unmold the cake. Use a kitchen knife to slice and serve.

## Health Tips
The combination of konjac powder and erythritol gives this cake a low calorie count, making it suitable for those controlling sugar intake or on a diet craving a treat. However, konjac powder has a strong satiating effect; overconsumption may cause bloating. Successfully whipping the egg whites is crucial—ensure the bowl is free of oil and water, and whip to stiff peaks.
