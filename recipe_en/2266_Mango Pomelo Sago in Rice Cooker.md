# Mango Pomelo Sago in Rice Cooker
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥭 Mango - about 1 (part mashed, part diced)
🍊 Pomelo - about half (red flesh only)
🍚 Sago - 1 measuring cup
🥥 Coconut milk/cream - to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker
🥤 Blender/food processor
🥄 Colander/sieve
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Instructions
1. Cut mango flesh into chunks. Place a portion in a blender, add a little water, and blend into a puree. Dice the remaining mango into small cubes and set aside. Peel the pomelo and separate the red flesh, then set aside.
2. Pour the sago into the rice cooker inner pot. Add boiling water up to the "porridge" water level mark.
   - **Key point**: Never use cold water.
3. Select the "quick cook" mode on the rice cooker and cook for about 20 minutes.
4. Open the lid and check. At this point, the sago should have a white core the size of a pinhead in the center.
5. Close the lid, select the "keep warm" mode, and let it rest for 3–5 minutes, until the white core disappears completely.
6. Open the lid again to confirm that the sago is fully transparent.
7. Scoop out the cooked sago and immediately rinse it under cold water for a chewier texture.
8. Return the sago to the rice cooker inner pot. Add the mango puree, pomelo flesh, diced mango, and coconut milk in sequence. Stir well and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Boiling the sago quickly in hot water and then letting it steam locks in a chewy texture. Paired with mango and coconut milk, this dessert helps cool you down in summer while providing vitamin C. Note that sago is relatively high in calories, so avoid overconsumption.
