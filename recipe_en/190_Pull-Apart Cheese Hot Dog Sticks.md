# Pull-Apart Cheese Hot Dog Sticks
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All-year meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (steamed in advance, mashed into puree)  
🌭 Ham sausages, as needed (for the core support)  
🧀 Mozzarella cheese shreds, as needed (choose varieties that stretch easily)  
🥣 Starch, as needed (first coating layer)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (beaten into egg wash)  
🍞 Breadcrumbs, as needed (outermost coating)  
🧂 BBQ seasoning, as needed (mixed into potato puree for flavor)  
🍅 Ketchup, as needed (for decorating the finished surface)  
🥗 Mayonnaise, as needed (for decorating the finished surface)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥔 Potato masher (or spoon)  
🌯 Plastic wrap  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Deep pot / deep fryer  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Steam the potatoes, then mash them into a smooth puree while still hot. Add BBQ seasoning and thoroughly mix and knead until well incorporated.  
2. Cut evenly spaced slits on the surface of the ham sausage, without cutting through the bottom, creating a lantern-like shape. Fill the cut gaps with mozzarella cheese shreds, allowing the sausage to naturally spread open.  
3. Lay a piece of plastic wrap flat, spread an appropriate amount of potato puree into a flat sheet, place the cheese-filled sausage on top, and use the plastic wrap to tightly wrap it, shaping it into a thick, long stick.  
   **Note**: Plastic wrap prevents sticking and helps shape it firmly.  
4. Follow the "three-step coating" process: First, roll the wrapped stick evenly in starch; then, dip it into the egg wash to fully coat the surface; finally, roll it in breadcrumbs for an even coating.  
5. Pour cooking oil into a pot and heat to 40% hot. Place the hot dog sticks in and fry over medium heat until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain oil.  
   **Key point**: Oil temperature should not be too high to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains raw.  
6. Squeeze ketchup and mayonnaise successively over the surface of the fried hot dog sticks. Serve immediately; the pull-apart cheese effect is best when hot.  

## Health Tips  
The potato puree wraps the cheese hot dog, resulting in a crispy exterior and tender interior with an excellent cheese pull effect. Ideal as party snacks or indulgent treats. Control the frying temperature to prevent the outside from burning while the inside is raw; this combination is relatively high in calories, so pair it with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition.
