# Curly Fries
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Yellow potatoes (approximately 200g each)
🌾 22189 coating powder 200g
🧂 Salt 20g (for batter)
🌶 Coloring paprika powder 8g (for batter)
💧 Water 280g (for batter)

**Creamy Garlic Flavor Sprinkle**:
🧄 Garlic powder 60g
🥛 Nonfat dry milk powder 80g
🧪 Yeast extract 10g
🍬 Granulated sugar 100g
🧂 Flavor powder 20g
🌾 Maltodextrin 25g
🥛 Non-dairy creamer 5g
🧂 Salt 20g
🌿 Dried parsley flakes 2g

## Cookware & Equipment
🔧 Curly fries spiral cutter
🔧 Grinder/pulverizer
🥄 Electric hand mixer
🍳 Deep fryer
🥣 Large mixing bowl
🥢 Metal strainer/fry basket
🥢 Chopsticks/long-handled tongs

## Cooking Steps
1.  Mix garlic powder, nonfat dry milk powder, yeast extract, granulated sugar, flavor powder, maltodextrin, non-dairy creamer, salt, and dried parsley flakes. Grind into a fine, even powder using a grinder. Set aside.

2.  Secure the peeled yellow potato onto the curly fries spiral cutter and rotate the handle to cut into a spiral shape. Each potato typically yields two portions: the larger spiral used as the base layer, and the smaller spiral placed on top.

3.  Soak the cut spiral fries in clean water for about 20 minutes to thoroughly remove surface starch. Remove and drain off excess surface water.

4.  Mix the 22189 coating powder, 20g salt, and coloring paprika powder. Add 280g water and stir with an electric hand mixer until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.

5.  Place the drained fries into the batter. Gently toss by hand to ensure each fry is evenly coated with a thin layer of batter.

6.  Heat oil to 180℃. Carefully lower the battered fries into the oil. Gently stir with chopsticks or long-handled tongs to prevent sticking. Fry until the outer skin is golden and the texture is crispy, then remove and drain on a metal wire rack.
   **Note**: Frying time depends on the fries' condition; they must be golden and crispy.

7.  Place the freshly fried hot fries into a large bowl. Sprinkle with the prepared creamy garlic flavor sprinkle. Toss the bowl or gently mix to ensure the sprinkle evenly coats the surface of each fry.

## Health Tips
Potatoes are rich in carbohydrates, and the creamy garlic flavor sprinkle contains sugar and fat, making this dish relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad or a low-sugar beverage to balance, and to control the serving size per meal.
