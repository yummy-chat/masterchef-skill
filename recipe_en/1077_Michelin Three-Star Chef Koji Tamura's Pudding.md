# Michelin Three-Star Chef Koji Tamura's Pudding
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients
🥛 Milk 200g
🧀 Cream Cheese 100g (softened to room temperature)
🥚 Eggs 4
🥛 Heavy Cream 100g
🥛 Condensed Milk 60g

## Seasonings
🍬 White Sugar 20g (for custard batter)
🍬 White Sugar 40g (for caramel)
💧 Water 15g (for caramel)
💧 Hot Water 15ml (added after caramel is cooked)
🌿 Vanilla Extract a pinch

## Cookware & Equipment
🍶 Saucepan
🔥 Induction Cooktop/Stove
🥣 Large Glass Bowl
🥄 Whisk
🪣 Strainer
🍮 Pudding Cups/Baking Dishes
🔥 Oven
Baking Pan
🧻 Kitchen Paper Towel
🔪 Small Knife
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. Mix white sugar and water, heat over medium heat. **Do not stir** during heating. Once the sugar syrup turns reddish-brown, immediately remove from heat. Carefully add 15ml of hot water after removing from heat. While hot, pour the caramel into the bottom of the pudding cups. Gently shake to evenly coat the bottom of the cups. Let sit to cool and solidify.

2. Pour milk, heavy cream, and vanilla extract into a saucepan. Heat to about 60°C, do not boil.

3. In a large bowl, add the room temperature softened cream cheese and 20g of white sugar. Press and stir repeatedly with a spatula until completely smooth with no lumps. Then add condensed milk and stir until evenly combined.

4. Add the eggs to the cheese mixture in four portions. After each addition, whisk thoroughly until well combined.

5. Slowly pour the heated milk mixture from step 2 into the egg batter while continuously stirring to prevent the egg from cooking and solidifying.

6. Strain the mixed custard batter twice: first through a coarse strainer, then through a fine strainer. Pour into the pudding cups with caramel at the bottom, filling to 80% full. Gently use a kitchen paper towel to remove surface bubbles.

7. Place the pudding molds into a deep baking pan. Pour hot water into the baking pan, reaching at least two-thirds of the height of the molds. Bake in a preheated oven at 160°C for 30 to 40 minutes. Gently shake the mold; it is done when the center jiggles slightly but has set.

8. After baking, remove from the oven. Let cool naturally, then refrigerate overnight. To unmold, run a small knife along the edge of the cup. Place a plate upside down over the mold, flip over, and gently shake the mold left and right. When you hear a "thud," lift the mold off.

## Health Advice
This pudding uses cream cheese to replace some of the eggs, resulting in a richer texture but higher calorie content. It is recommended to consume one-quarter per serving, suitable as a weekend delicate dessert. Note that condensed milk and caramel have high sugar content; those on a sugar-restricted diet should consume with caution.
