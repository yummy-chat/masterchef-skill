# XO Sauce
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Year Round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦪 Dried scallops (conpoy) 5 jin
🍤 Dried small shrimp (golden hook shrimp) 3 jin
🍖 Jinhua ham approximately 1500 g (torn into shreds)
🐟 Dried halibut (dried flatfish) approximately 100 g (burned and ground into powder)
🦐 Shrimp roe approximately 500 g
🌶️ Erjingtiao dried chili peppers approximately 100 g (soaked in water for 2 hours in advance)
🧅 Dried shallots (small onions) approximately 3000 g (minced)
🧄 Garlic approximately 350 g (minced into paste)
🫒 Chili oil 500 g
🍶 Huadiao wine 250 g (for removing fishy smell and enhancing aroma)
🧊 Rock sugar 250 g (ground into powder in advance)
🧂 Salt (not added; the saltiness of the ham is sufficient)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥢 Steamer
🔥 Blowtorch
🪨 Mortar and pestle
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥄 Iron spatula
🪣 Large basin

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Pre-treatment of dried shrimp and dried scallops**: Soak the dried shrimp and dried scallops separately in water, add Huadiao wine to remove the fishy smell, place in a steamer and steam over high heat for 30 minutes, then turn to low heat and steam for another 30 minutes. After steaming, remove and break them apart with a spatula. Reserve the steaming liquid.
**Note**: Adding Huadiao wine during steaming effectively removes the fishy odor from dried seafood.
2. **Pre-treatment of dried halibut**: Use a blowtorch to burn the surface of the dried halibut until slightly charred, shake off the black burnt bits, and grind into powder using a mortar and pestle.
3. **Pre-treatment of Jinhua ham**: Use a blowtorch to sear the surface of the Jinhua ham block to release the aroma, then wash it clean in water. Place in a steamer and steam over high heat for 30 minutes, then turn to low heat and steam for another 30 minutes. Remove and tear into fine shreds along the grain.
4. **Pre-treatment of other ingredients**: Grind the rock sugar into powder using a mortar and pestle; mince the garlic, dried shallots, and the soaked Erjingtiao dried chili peppers separately; portion and set aside all ingredients according to the stage.
### Cooking Stage
1. **Fry the aromatic oil**: Heat a sufficient amount of oil in a wok. First, add the minced dried shallots and fry until the moisture evaporates and they turn slightly yellow. Then add the minced garlic and fry together until they turn slightly yellow. Remove and drain the oil, set aside.
**Indicators**: Remove the ingredients immediately when the moisture starts to dry and the color turns slightly yellow to avoid burning.
2. **Simmer the main ingredients**: In the oil used for frying the aromatics, add the minced Erjingtiao chili peppers, then add the drained steamed dried shrimp and dried scallops. Simmer over medium-low heat for about 1 hour, stirring the bottom of the wok continuously with a spatula to prevent sticking.
**Indicators**: Simmer until a significant amount of moisture has evaporated from the wok and the aromas of the various ingredients have melded together.
3. **Combine and season**: After 1 hour, add the Jinhua ham shreds and shrimp roe, mix well. Before removing from heat, add the ground halibut powder, rock sugar powder, and chili oil. Then add the previously fried and reserved dried shallots and minced garlic, stir-fry evenly. Finally, continue to stir-fry for about 10 minutes to fully blend all ingredients and seasonings.
**Key Point**: Most ingredients added at this stage are relatively dry; ensure they are mixed thoroughly to avoid localized excessive sweetness or spiciness.
4. **Finish and rest**: Turn off the heat, remove the cooked XO sauce, and let it rest for 1-2 days to allow the flavors to meld and ferment naturally. The color of the sauce will gradually deepen to a dark brownish-red, with a more prominent savory and sweet aftertaste.
**Note**: The flavor of the freshly made sauce is not yet fully integrated; the resting process is a necessary step.

## Health Advice
This XO sauce is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, suitable for individuals needing zinc supplementation to strengthen the body. However, it has a high sodium content, so those with high blood pressure should control their consumption.
