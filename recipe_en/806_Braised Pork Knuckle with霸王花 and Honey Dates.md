# Braised Pork Knuckle with霸王花 and Honey Dates  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January – April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌼 Dried霸王花 (Pitaya Flower) – appropriate amount (soak in advance for 2 hours)  
🍖 Pork knuckle (front leg) – 1 piece (tender meat, thick skin, rich in gelatin)  
🦴 Pork spare ribs (fan bone) – appropriate amount (for extra umami)  
🍬 Honey dates – 2 pieces  
🥜 Southern apricot kernels & Northern apricot kernels – appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel (Chenpi) – a little (optional)  
💧 Water – about 6 catties (12 regular rice bowls)  
🧂 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount (for dipping)  
🫒 Peanut oil – 2 drops (for finishing the dipping sauce)  
🍬 White sugar – a small amount (added when soaking the霸王花)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Glass soup pot  
🥣 Regular rice bowl (for measuring water)  
🥄 Spoon (for skimming foam, stirring, and serving)  
🥢 Chopsticks (to test tenderness and pick up ingredients)  
🍽️ Small dish (for side ingredients and dipping sauce)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for cutting pork knuckle and preparing ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Soak the霸王花**: Place the dried霸王花 in clean water, add a small amount of white sugar, and soak for about 2 hours.  
**Note**: Sugar reduces bitterness and astringency, making the texture smoother.

2. **Prepare the pork knuckle**: Wash the whole pork knuckle thoroughly. Choose the front leg part, as the meat is more tender and the skin is thick with rich gelatin.

3. **Prepare the aromatics**: Slice the ginger, and set aside the dried tangerine peel, honey dates, southern and northern apricot kernels.

4. **Boil water and add ingredients**: Pour about 6 catties of water into the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add ginger slices, dried tangerine peel, honey dates, southern and northern apricot kernels.

5. **Skim the foam**: After the soup comes to a boil, cook for about 5 minutes, then skim off any foam from the surface. Turn to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes.  
**Key point**: Skimming the foam at this stage ensures a clear broth.

6. **Add the meat**: Add the spare ribs and the whole pork knuckle. Continue simmering over low heat.

7. **Add the霸王花**: After the meat and bones have simmered for half an hour, add the soaked霸王花 and continue to simmer for another 30 minutes.  
**Key point**: Do not add the霸王花 too early, or the soup may turn sour.

8. **Remove the pork knuckle**: After a total simmering time of about 1 hour, when a chopstick can easily pierce through the skin and meat, remove the whole pork knuckle.  
**One soup, two flavors**: The pork knuckle can be sliced and eaten with dipping sauce.

9. **Continue simmering the broth**: After removing the pork knuckle, continue to simmer the broth over low heat for 20 minutes.

10. **Boil on high heat**: Turn to high heat and boil for 5–10 minutes until the soup becomes rich and milky white.  
**Key point**: This step is crucial for achieving a creamy white soup color.

11. **Prepare the dipping sauce**: Add two drops of peanut oil to the light soy sauce and mix well.  
**Finishing touch**: Peanut oil paired with animal meat is the secret to elevating the aroma of the dipping sauce.

12. **Finish**: Ladle the soup into bowls. Slice the pork knuckle and serve with the dipping sauce.

## Health Tips
This soup is nourishing and moistening for the lungs, ideal for dry spring weather. The gelatin-rich pork knuckle, combined with honey dates and霸王花, helps to moisten dryness and promote bowel movement. Note that those with a weak spleen and loose stools should not drink too much. When dipping the pork knuckle in soy sauce, be mindful of salt intake.
