# Stir-Fried Crispy Grass Carp with Ginger and Scallion
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🐟 Crispy grass carp belly (about 1 pound, deboned, black membrane scraped clean, cut into mahjong-sized thick chunks)
🫚 Local yellow ginger (appropriate amount, sliced)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections)
🌶️ Bell peppers (red and yellow, appropriate amount, sliced)

## Seasonings
🍬 White sugar (about 10 grams, for marinating)
🧂 Light soy sauce (about 15 ml, for marinating)
🦪 Oyster sauce (about 30 ml, used in two portions: 15 ml for marinating, 15 ml for stir-frying)
🍶 Rice wine (about 50 ml, used in three portions: some for marinating, most for deglazing, a small amount for a second deglazing)
🌶️ White pepper (about 5 grams, for marinating)
🫒 Peanut oil (appropriate amount, for frying)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Thick-bottomed non-stick wok
🔥 Gas stove
🔪 Knife
🍽️ Cutting board
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1.  Take the fish belly and debone it. Use a knife to carefully scrape off the black membrane inside the fish belly.
2.  Slice the fish belly into thick pieces along the direction of the bones, then cut them in half into chunks about the size of mahjong tiles.
3.  Place the fish chunks in clean water, gently wash them by hand, and drain. Add white sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, some rice wine, and white pepper. Gently mix with chopsticks and marinate for 10 minutes.
4.  Heat the wok until white smoke rises, then reduce to low heat and pour in peanut oil. Add ginger slices and stir-fry over medium heat until the edges of the ginger slices turn slightly yellow and the ginger oil aroma is released.
5.  Spread the marinated fish chunks flat into the wok. Pan-fry over medium-high heat for about 2 minutes until the surface of the fish is golden brown and the aroma is released.
6.  Pour most of the rice wine along the edge of the wok, stir-fry quickly to let the liquid catch fire, using the flames to burn off the fishy smell.
7.  Pour in the remaining oyster sauce, stir-fry evenly, then add the bell pepper slices and scallion sections. Continue stir-frying over high heat.
8.  Pour a small amount of rice wine along the edge of the wok again, and stir-fry quickly and evenly. The total stir-frying time is about 2 and a half minutes. Ensure the fish chunks are cooked through and the vegetables are just tender, then serve.
**Key Point**: Crispy grass carp cooks quickly, so do not stir-fry for too long to maintain its crunchy texture.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. The crunchy texture of crispy grass carp is easy to digest, making it suitable for fitness or blood sugar control individuals. Ginger, scallion, and rice wine help remove the fishy smell and enhance the aroma. However, as the fish chunks are marinated with sugar and oyster sauce, it is recommended that individuals with high blood pressure reduce the amount of light soy sauce.
