# Pork Meat Pie 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings 🥩
🥩 Pork shoulder 500g (with a balance of fat and lean, ground into filling)
🧅 Scallions about 100g (add before wrapping)
🫚 Ginger, minced 14g
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g
💧 Cold water 160g
🧂 Salt 3g (add to flour)
🌶️ White pepper 1g
🍂 Thirteen-spice powder 0.5g
🥄 Light soy sauce 25g
🥄 Dark soy sauce 7g
🧂 Salt 5g (add to filling)
🫒 Refined rapeseed oil 30g
🍬 Sugar 5g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment ⚖️
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🫙 Plastic wrap
🔪 Dough scraper
🫓 Electric griddle

## Preparation Steps 📝
1. Knead the dough: Mix 250g all-purpose flour with 3g salt evenly. Gradually add 160g cold water, first stirring into flocculent pieces with chopsticks, then knead into a dough by hand. Use a fist-pressing method to knead until the surface is smooth and slightly sticky to the touch.
   **Note**: Use more pressing technique to reduce gluten formation, resulting in a softer texture.
2. First resting: Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes to fully relax the gluten.
3. Prepare the filling: In 500g ground pork, add 14g minced ginger, 1g white pepper to remove odor, then add 0.5g thirteen-spice powder, 25g light soy sauce, 7g dark soy sauce, 5g salt, 30g refined rapeseed oil, and 5g sugar. Stir in one direction until the mixture is well combined and slightly sticky. Add about 100g chopped scallions just before wrapping and mix well.
   **Key points**:
   - Do not add water to the filling, otherwise it will shrink and the crust may break when heated.
   - Use less salt; the filling should taste slightly milder than the finished product.
   - Scallions must be added just before wrapping to preserve their fresh aroma.
4. Portion the dough and second resting: Lightly oil the work surface and your hands to prevent sticking. Remove the rested dough, shape into a long log, and divide into evenly sized portions. Roll each portion into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest again for 10 minutes, until the dough does not spring back when pressed and feels relaxed.
5. Shape and fill: Take one rested dough piece and press it directly by hand into a round disc with a thicker center and thinner edges. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Wrap using a bun-making technique, pinching the edges to seal tightly. Turn it over with the sealed side down, and gently press it into a flat round patty.
   **Note**: Ensure the seam is tightly sealed to prevent leakage; press gently to achieve even thickness.
6. Cook: Preheat the electric griddle over medium heat. Place the raw meat pies on the griddle, cover with the lid, and cook for 3-5 minutes on one side until set and golden brown. Flip and cook for another 3-5 minutes, until both sides are golden brown and the pie puffs up, indicating it's cooked through.
   **Note**:
   - No additional oil is needed, as the fat from the filling is sufficient.
   - Do not use excessively high heat or cook too long, to prevent the filling from releasing too much liquid.

## Health Advice 🥗
This pork meat pie uses pork shoulder, which has a balanced fat-to-lean ratio. Note that no water is added to the filling to prevent the crust from breaking. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost, but consumption should be moderated due to the relatively high calories from the dough and fat.
