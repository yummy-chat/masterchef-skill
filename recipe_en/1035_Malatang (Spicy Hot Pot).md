# Malatang (Spicy Hot Pot)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Rapeseed oil 700g
🧈 Beef tallow 300g
🌶️ Dried chilies 100g (for making ciba chili)
🫘 Green Sichuan peppercorns 30g (pre-soaked in baijiu)
🫘 Red Sichuan peppercorns 30g (pre-soaked in baijiu)
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste) 150g
🫘 Douchi (fermented black beans) 30g (finely chopped)
🌿 Spice powder 20g (pre-soaked in warm water)
🍬 Rock sugar 10g (powdered)
🧂 Sesame paste appropriate amount (mixed with peanut paste in an 8:2 ratio)
🥜 Peanut paste appropriate amount (mixed with sesame paste in an 8:2 ratio)
🧅 Scallions, ginger, onion, celery appropriate amount (for frying spice oil)
🫕 Broth one pot (pre-made thick white broth)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder appropriate amount
🧂 White pepper powder appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar a little (to enhance flavor)
🦪 Oyster sauce one tablespoon
💧 Cool boiled water appropriate amount (to dilute sesame paste gradually)
🌿 Scallions a little (for final garnish)
🥬 Various vegetables, tofu products, meatballs, etc. appropriate amount (for boiling)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🪣 Stockpot/soup pot
🥣 Large bowl/container (for storing braised base)
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing sesame paste)
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🥄 Spatula/soup ladle

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. Cook the dried chilies until soft, then finely chop into a paste to make ciba chili. This is key to the color and spiciness of the base.
2. Soak the spice powder in warm water, and soak both green and red Sichuan peppercorns (30g each) in baijiu to prevent burning and promote aroma release.
3. Prepare celery segments, scallion segments, shredded ginger, and shredded onion.
4. Mix sesame paste and peanut paste in an 8:2 ratio, add one tablespoon of oyster sauce and stir well. Gradually add cool boiled water to adjust to the desired consistency, stirring thoroughly after each addition before adding the next.
5. Prepare a pot of thick white broth in advance and set aside.

### Frying the Base
1. Add rapeseed oil and beef tallow to the wok. Heat until the tallow melts, then add scallions, ginger, onion, and celery. Fry until the vegetables turn dry and yellow, then turn off the heat and remove the solids to obtain spice oil.
2. When the oil temperature drops to about 30–40% heat, add the ciba chili. Stir constantly over low heat, keeping the chili flakes afloat, until about 80% of the moisture has evaporated.
3. Add doubanjiang, continuing to stir over low heat without stopping, until the fragrance is released.
4. Add the soaked green and red Sichuan peppercorns and finely chopped douchi. Stir over low heat until the alcohol smell dissipates.
5. Add the soaked spice powder and powdered rock sugar. Continue stirring over low heat until the moisture from the ingredients has mostly evaporated.
6. Turn off the heat, transfer to a container, seal tightly, and let it braise for 24 hours. Only then will the color and flavor reach their best.

### Preparing the Soup Base
1. Bring the broth to a boil, season with salt, chicken powder, white pepper powder, and a little white sugar. The soup should be seasoned slightly more strongly.
2. Add the braised base, bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to fully infuse into the soup.

### Boiling & Assembly
1. Depending on how quickly each ingredient cooks, use a strainer to blanch them in batches in the rolling soup: root vegetables and meatballs take longer to cook, while leafy greens cook quickly.
2. Place the cooked ingredients into a bowl, pour the malatang soup base over them, drizzle with the prepared sesame paste, and sprinkle with a little scallion.

## Health Advice
Malatang is high in calories due to beef tallow, spices, and sesame paste. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables and tofu, and to consume less of the soup base to reduce oil intake. Suitable for those who enjoy bold flavors but need to manage their weight. Be careful to avoid burning your mouth.
