# Crispy Fried Stinky Tofu with Sauce
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Old tofu 1000g (high water content, needs to be drained in advance)
🧄 Garlic 5-6 cloves (about 25g, to be made into garlic water)
🥣 Sesame paste 20g (a generous half spoon)
🫙 Bottled stinky tofu 50g (3 pieces, mashed)
🌶️ Chili flakes 20g (two level spoons)
🌿 White sesame seeds 10g (one level spoon)
🌿 Cumin powder/cumin seeds 15g (one and a half level spoons)
🌿 Thirteen-spice/five-spice powder 0.5g (a tiny pinch, do not overdo)
🫗 Light soy sauce 10g (one spoon)
🫗 Oyster sauce 10g (half a spoon)
🍬 White sugar 5g (one teaspoon)
🧂 MSG 3g (one teaspoon)
🫒 Cooking oil sufficient amount (for pouring over dry ingredients, heat until smoking; also sufficient amount for deep frying)
💧 Water about 120g (50g for garlic water, 20g to dilute sesame paste, 30g to dilute stinky tofu, and enough to adjust the mixed sauce)
🌿 Scallions, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Food processor or mortar and pestle
🥣 Small bowl
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok (also used for deep frying)
🥄 Spider strainer
🧻 Paper towels
🥢 Chopsticks
♨️ Air fryer (optional, for a lower-oil version)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board

## Instructions
1. Drain the old tofu in advance, pour off any accumulated water in the bag, pat the surface dry with paper towels, and cut into thick squares, sized for easy eating.
   **Key point**: The tofu has high water content; thorough drying is essential for safe deep frying.
2. Place the garlic and about 50g of water into a food processor and blend into garlic water. Set aside.
   **Key point**: Garlic water enhances flavor and prevents the final sauce from becoming too thick.
3. In a small bowl, add 20g of sesame paste. Gradually add about 20g of water, stirring to dilute until it has a consistency slightly thicker than yogurt. Add the mashed bottled stinky tofu (3 pieces), then add about 30g more water and stir until smooth and well combined. This is the sesame stinky tofu base sauce.
   **Key point**: Do not use too much sesame paste, or it will mask the stinky tofu flavor; add water in stages to thin it out.
4. In a separate large bowl, combine the chili flakes, white sesame seeds, cumin powder, and thirteen-spice powder. Mix well. This is the chili oil dry mixture.
5. Heat oil in a wok until smoking. Gradually pour the hot oil over the dry mixture in the bowl, stirring constantly. While still hot, add the light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, and MSG. Stir to combine.
   **Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the aroma of the dry ingredients.
6. Pour the garlic water and the sesame stinky tofu base sauce into the bowl with the chili oil. Stir until well combined to create the final sauce.
   **Consistency check**: The final sauce should be slightly runny, not too thick.
7. Deep fry the tofu:
   - Pour sufficient oil into the wok and heat over high heat. When small ripples appear on the surface and you feel intense heat holding your hand 20cm above the oil, place one piece of dried tofu along the edge of the wok. If it floats to the surface after about 6 seconds, the oil temperature is correct.
   - Pat each piece of tofu dry with paper towels, then carefully add them to the oil one by one. After about 20 seconds, the tofu will set. Gently separate any pieces that are sticking together using chopsticks. Keep the heat high. Starting from when half the tofu is in the wok, fry for about 5 minutes, until the tofu turns a golden-yellow color and the exterior is hard. Remove and drain. Pieces added later may need slightly longer frying time.
   **Note**: Fry in two batches. After the first batch is done, wait about 30 seconds for the oil temperature to return to 200°C (about 400°F) before frying the second batch.
8. Place all the fried tofu into a large bowl. Pour the prepared sauce over it, sprinkle with scallions and cilantro, and toss everything together until well coated. Serve immediately.

## Health Notes
This fried stinky tofu dish is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a refreshing vegetable salad to balance the meal. Old tofu is a good source of plant-based protein, but deep frying doubles its calorie count. Those watching their weight can use an air fryer to reduce oil usage.
