# 🍖 Drunken Ribs  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

🍖 Ingredients  
- Ribs 200 g  
- Garlic 2 cloves  
- Scallion 1 stalk  
- Egg yolk 1 piece  
- Sweet potato starch 30 g  

🧂 Seasonings  
- White sugar 10 g  
- Cooking oil 500 ml  
- Fish sauce 5 ml  
- Sesame seeds 5 g  
- Ketchup 5 g  
- Aromatic vinegar 5 ml  

🔪 Utensils  
- Large bowl  
- Small bowl  
- Spoon  
- Pot (for deep frying)  
- Plate  

📝 Steps  
1. Add 5 g of sweet potato starch and some water to the ribs, scrub them, repeat 2–3 times, drain well after washing, and place in a large bowl for later use.  
2. Add fish sauce, remaining sweet potato starch, and egg yolk to the ribs, and mix thoroughly.  
3. Add the ribs to the oil one by one (do not pour in the entire bowl at once), ensuring each piece does not stick together.  
4. Once all ribs are in the pot, flip them every 30 seconds using a spoon, and fry until the surface turns golden.  
5. Remove the ribs, let them rest for 1 minute, then return them to the oil for a second fry until the surface turns crispy golden. Remove and place in a large bowl for later use.  
6. In a small bowl, add minced garlic, aromatic vinegar, white sugar, fish sauce, ketchup, chopped scallion, and sesame seeds. Stir to combine, then pour in 5 ml of hot oil to release the aroma.  
7. Pour the prepared sauce over the ribs, mix well, and plate.  

## Health Advice  
This Drunken Ribs dish is crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and sweet and sour in flavor, but it is high in calories due to deep frying. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Suitable for occasional indulgence, the ribs are rich in protein, and moderate consumption can support growth and development in children and adolescents.
