# Bracken Root Braised Fish with Broad Bean Paste
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Recommended Season: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Carp 1 (about 1.7–1.8 jin, cleaned and scored)
🌿 Bracken root (houttuynia cordata) to taste (finely diced, added in two batches)
🌶️ Pickled red chili to taste (finely minced)
🫚 Pickled ginger to taste (finely minced)
🧄 Garlic to taste (finely minced)
🧅 Scallion to taste (for marinating, crushed; final garnish, chopped)
🫚 Ginger to taste (for marinating, crushed; for sautéing, finely minced)
🥬 Pickled vegetables to taste (diced, optional)
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste
🍶 Cooking wine to taste
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a few
🫘 Broad bean paste 1 tbsp
🧂 MSG to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste
🍬 Sugar a small amount
🍾 Soy sauce a little
🍶 Vinegar a small amount (added in two batches)
🌾 Water starch to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Metal bowl
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1.  Clean the carp thoroughly, score a series of diagonal cuts on both sides of the back, and make cross cuts near the tail.
2.  Place the fish in a bowl, add crushed ginger and scallion, salt, white pepper powder, and cooking wine. Rub the ginger and scallion to release their aroma, then evenly rub the fish all over. Marinate to infuse flavor and remove any fishy taste.
3.  Finely mince together the pickled red chili, pickled ginger, and garlic; dice the bracken root into small pieces; finely chop some scallions for garnish.
**Note**: The finer the pickled chili, ginger, and garlic are minced, the more fully their aroma will be released when stir-frying.
4.  Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 60% hot. Add the marinated fish and deep-fry until set and the surface is golden and crispy, then remove.
**Note**: For home cooking, pan-frying can be used instead of deep-frying to reduce oil usage.
5.  Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, add a spoonful of lard. When the oil is hot, add a few Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant.
6.  Add the minced pickled chili, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry over low heat until the aroma and lactic acid smell of the pickled vegetables are released.
7.  Add one tablespoon of broad bean paste. Continue to stir-fry over low heat until it becomes fragrant and the red oil is released.
**Key**: Keep the heat low throughout the sauce-making process to fully release the aroma and red oil.
8.  Add about one and a half ladles of water to the wok. After it boils, season with MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, sugar, soy sauce, and a small amount of vinegar.
9.  Gently place the fried fish into the sauce. Reduce the heat to low and braise slowly, continuously spooning the sauce over the fish. Halfway through, sprinkle in a small amount of diced bracken root to enhance the flavor.
10. After one side is well flavored, carefully flip the fish and continue to braise the other side until the fish is fully infused with flavor.
**Key**: "Rapid fire for tofu, slow fire for fish." Be patient with slow braising and continuously baste the fish.
11. Carefully transfer the braised fish to a serving plate.
12. Bring the remaining sauce in the wok to a boil. Add the water starch to thicken into a rich gravy. Add most of the remaining diced bracken root and scallions, then drizzle in a small amount of vinegar. Stir until the gravy bubbles up in "fish-eye" sized bubbles.
13. Pour the thick gravy evenly over the fish and serve.

## Health Advice
This Bracken Root Braised Fish with Broad Bean Paste is rich in high-quality protein and the probiotics from fermented pickled vegetables, making it excellent for stimulating appetite. However, the deep-frying step increases the calorie content, so it is recommended to control the oil level or pan-fry instead. Bracken root (houttuynia cordata) is cold in nature, so those with a weak or cold spleen and stomach should consume it in moderation.
