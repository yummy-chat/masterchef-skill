# Pan-Seared Sea Bass
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 sea bass (can be substituted with freshwater bass)
🧅 1/2 onion (chopped)
🧄 2 cloves of garlic (chopped)
🍅 Tomato puree, to taste
🌿 1 sprig of thyme
🍋 1/2 lemon (juiced)
🌶️ A few bird's eye chilies (optional, chopped)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes, to taste (halved, for garnish)
🥬 Asparagus, to taste (pre-cooked by pan-searing or blanching)
🍾 Dry white wine, about 100 ml
🧂 Salt, to taste
⚪ White pepper, to taste
✨ Five-spice powder, a pinch (optional)
🌾 Cornstarch, a pinch (optional, to coat the fish to prevent sticking)
🫒 Olive oil, to taste
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🌶️ Smoked paprika (sweet paprika), a pinch

## Cookware and Equipment
✂️ Kitchen shears
🔪 Chef's knife
🍲 Saucepan
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🔗 Metal spatula
🪵 Wooden spatula
🔧 Tongs

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Use kitchen shears to cut off the sharp fins of the sea bass to prevent injury during handling.
2. Starting from the back of the fish, lay the knife flat and slide it slowly along the backbone to remove the fillets from both sides, yielding two fillets.
3. Cut the remaining fish bones into small pieces, rinse clean with water, and set aside.
4. Remove the small bones from the belly side of the fillets and lightly score the skin side twice to prevent curling during cooking.
5. Finely chop half an onion and two cloves of garlic, dividing them equally into two portions: one for making fish stock, the other for sautéing the sauce base.
6. Prepare the tomato puree, chopped bird's eye chilies, and the juice from half a lemon.

### Cooking Phase
1. **Making the Fish Stock**
   - Heat a little oil in a saucepan, add the cleaned fish bones, and pan-fry until fragrant.
   - Add one portion of the chopped onion and garlic, stirring well with the bones.
   - Pour in about 30 ml of white wine and cook until the alcohol has fully evaporated.
   - Add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, and skim off any foam from the surface.
   - Add one sprig of thyme, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 20 minutes.
   - Strain the stock through a slotted spoon or strainer to obtain a clear fish stock. Set aside.

2. **Pan-Searing the Fish Fillets**
   - Season both sides of the fillets evenly with salt and white pepper. Add a pinch of five-spice powder if desired.
   - To prevent sticking, lightly dust the fillets with a thin layer of cornstarch.
   - Heat a non-stick frying pan over high heat, then add enough olive oil.
   - Place the fillets **skin-side down** in the pan. Use a metal spatula to gently press them down to set the shape, then reduce the heat to **low** and cook slowly.
   - Once the skin is golden and crispy, flip the fillets and continue cooking over low heat until the other side is cooked through, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.

3. **Sautéing the Sauce Base**
   - Using the same pan used for the fish, add a little more olive oil. Add the remaining portion of chopped onion, garlic, and bird's eye chilies.
   - Sauté until fragrant, then pour in about 70 ml of white wine and cook until the alcohol has evaporated.
   - Add the tomato puree and a pinch of smoked paprika, stirring continuously until the oil turns red and fragrant.

4. **Making the Final Sauce**
   - Pour the reserved fish stock into the pan and mix well with the tomato base.
   - Squeeze in some lemon juice, and season with salt and a pinch of sugar.
   - Turn the heat to high and reduce the sauce until it thickens to a desired coating consistency.

5. **Plating**
   - Start by spreading a layer of the finished tangy and spicy sauce on the plate.
   - Arrange the pre-cooked asparagus on top as a base layer.
   - Place the pan-seared fish fillet on top of the asparagus, skin-side up.
   - Garnish with halved and pan-seared cherry tomatoes, lemon slices, etc.

## Health Tips
Sea bass is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, making it suitable for those looking to reduce fat and build muscle. For a healthier preparation, use less oil and salt. Be mindful of the amounts of white wine and tomato puree to avoid excessive sodium intake.
