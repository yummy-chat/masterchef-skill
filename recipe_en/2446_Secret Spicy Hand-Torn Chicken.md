# Secret Spicy Hand-Torn Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 Qingyuan chicken (about 2 jin, cleaned and with nails trimmed)
🧅 1 small bunch of scallions (including scallion knots and white scallion shreds)
🫚 A few slices of ginger (sliced)
🧄 Appropriate amount of garlic (1:1 volume ratio with onion)
🧅 Appropriate amount of onion (1:1 volume ratio with garlic)
🍋 Half a lemon (sliced and seeded)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of bird's eye chili (omit if not eating spicy)
🌿 Appropriate amount of cilantro (cut into segments)

🧂 1 tablespoon salt
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil
🌶️ Appropriate amount of Sichuan pepper powder
🌶️ Appropriate amount of five-spice powder
🌶️ Appropriate amount of fine chili powder
🦪 4 tablespoons oyster sauce
🫗 2 tablespoons homemade secret spicy oil
🫒 A small amount of sesame oil
🤍 Appropriate amount of sesame seeds

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Large pot
🔪 Food processor
🔪 Meat grinder
🍳 Wok
🥣 Large bowl
✂️ Scissors

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the chicken**: Trim the nails of the Qingyuan chicken, clean it, drain, and set aside.
- **Note**: The Qingyuan chicken weighs about 2 jin. Different chicken breeds may result in slightly different textures.

2. **Make the secret spicy oil**: Cut the onion and garlic into chunks in a 1:1 ratio, then blend them into a fine paste in a food processor. Heat the wok over high heat, add a generous amount of cooking oil, and add the garlic and onion paste. Cook on high heat until the paste floats up. Reduce to low heat, add the Sichuan pepper powder, five-spice powder, and fine chili powder, and stir well. Turn off the heat, add the oyster sauce, and stir again. The secret spicy oil is ready. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
- **Key points**: The garlic and onion paste must be ground sufficiently fine; it must be fried until the paste floats before adding the powdered spices, otherwise the oil temperature will be insufficient to release their aroma; oyster sauce must be added only after turning off the heat to prevent high heat from damaging its flavor.

3. **Cook and steam the chicken**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the scallion knots, ginger slices, and one tablespoon of salt. Place the whole chicken in the pot, cover it with a clean plate to fully submerge the chicken in the water, and then cover the pot with a lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately turn off the heat. Let it sit with residual heat to steam for 30 minutes.
- **Doneness check**: The chicken should be fully cooked through by residual heat, with no blood at the bones.
   - **Key points**: Using a plate to press the chicken ensures even heating; the steaming time must be a full 30 minutes, and the lid must not be opened during that time.

4. **Ice water bath**: Prepare a basin of ice water in advance. Remove the steamed chicken and quickly submerge it in the ice water until completely cooled.
- **Key points**: This step is essential. The ice water bath tightens the chicken skin, making it springy, and keeps the meat firm but not dry—key to the texture.

5. **Shred and mix the chicken**: Remove the cooled chicken, drain well. Wearing gloves, debone the chicken and shred the meat into thin strips. Place in a large bowl. Add the white scallion shreds, ginger shreds, lemon slices, bird's eye chili rings, and cilantro segments. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons of the secret spicy oil, a small amount of sesame oil, and the sesame seeds. Mix thoroughly by hand. Finally, squeeze in fresh lemon juice and mix again evenly.
- **Key points**: The finer the chicken is shredded, the more flavorful it becomes; the lemon juice must be added last to preserve its fresh, tangy aroma, as adding it earlier would cause the heat to dissipate its fragrance.

## Health Advice
This dish uses Qingyuan chicken and employs an ice water skin-tightening technique, making it high in protein and low in fat, suitable for consumption during weight loss. However, the secret spicy oil uses a significant amount of oil, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the fat. Those with high blood pressure or stomach discomfort should reduce the amount of oyster sauce and chili.
