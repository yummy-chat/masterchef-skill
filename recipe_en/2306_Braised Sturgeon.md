# Braised Sturgeon

Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Sturgeon center-cut thick fillets, moderate amount (with skin, approx. 2cm thick)  
🧅 Onion, moderate amount (julienned)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, moderate amount (seeded and julienned)  
🫑 Spiral chili pepper, moderate amount (julienned)  
🧅 Scallion, moderate amount (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger, moderate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, moderate amount (crushed or whole cloves)  
🧂 Salt, moderate amount (to prevent sticking when frying the fish and for seasoning)  
🍺 Beer, moderate amount (substitute for cooking wine to remove fishiness and enhance aroma)  
🥄 Steamed fish soy sauce, moderate amount (to enhance freshness and flavor)  
🍾 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (for coloring)  
🫒 Scallion oil, a small amount (to add fragrance and shine before serving)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Heat the wok, add oil, and sprinkle a small spoonful of salt along the bottom of the wok. Place the sturgeon pieces skin-side down into the wok.  
**Note**: The salt helps prevent sticking and makes the fish skin firmer.  

2. Fry the fish skin over medium-high heat until golden brown and set, then flip and continue frying the other side until slightly golden brown.  
3. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic cloves, stir-frying until fragrant.  
4. Pour beer along the edge of the wok, then add enough water to cover half of the fish pieces.  
5. Season with salt, steamed fish soy sauce, and a small amount of dark soy sauce. Stir evenly, cover the wok, and cook over medium-high heat for 6 minutes.  
**Key Point**: The sauce will gradually reduce during cooking; do not uncover the lid frequently.  

6. Remove the lid, use a slotted spoon to discard the scallion, ginger, and garlic remnants. Add julienned onion, red bell pepper, and spiral chili pepper, then drizzle in a small amount of scallion oil.  
7. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Once the sauce comes to a boil again and the vegetables are just cooked, transfer the fish pieces along with the sauce to a plate and serve.  

## Health Advice  
Sturgeon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids and collagen. The braising method preserves the gelatin from the fish skin, making it suitable for those needing brain nourishment and joint support. Beer replaces cooking wine to remove fishiness, but gout patients should consume in moderation.
