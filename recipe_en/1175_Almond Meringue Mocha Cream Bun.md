# Almond Meringue Mocha Cream Bun
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
🍬 Caster sugar 37g (for dough)  
☕️ Coffee powder 3g (freeze-dried, ground, or instant)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 5g  
🥛 Milk 170g  
🥚 Egg yolk 1 piece (about 16g)  
🧫 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerance) 2.5g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 37g (softened at room temperature)  
🥚 Egg white 35g (for meringue)  
🍬 Caster sugar 35g (for meringue)  
🌰 Almond flour 31g  
🥜 Walnuts, chopped, as needed (for topping)  
🍬 Icing sugar, as needed (for topping)  
🥚 Egg wash, as needed (for brushing)  
🥛 Heavy cream 300g  
🍫 Dark chocolate (pure cocoa butter) 60g  
🧂 Salt 5g  

## Tools & Equipment
🥄 Dough scraper  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍳 Baking tray  
📜 Parchment paper / silicone mat  
📜 Baking paper  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🥄 Whisk  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🎂 Piping bag  
🧊 Wire cooling rack  
📦 Airtight container / ziplock bag  

## Instructions
1. Weigh and mix together the bread flour, caster sugar, coffee powder, and cocoa powder until well combined.  
2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients. Pour in the milk and egg yolk, add the yeast. Initially mix the liquids together, then gradually incorporate the dry flour into the liquid, mixing until a shaggy dough forms.  
   **Key Point**: Slightly moisten your hands with water to prevent sticking. Use a rubbing motion to initially combine.  
3. Continue kneading the dough by rubbing until the dough surface, work surface, and your palms are clean and dry, and the dough reaches the expansion stage—capable of forming a thick, easily broken membrane.  
4. Spread the dough flat, then add the softened butter and salt.  
   **Key Point**: Add salt later. Knead vigorously until the butter is fully absorbed.  
5. Use a combination of kneading and slapping techniques to fully incorporate the butter into the dough. Knead for about 5 minutes and slap for about 5 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth again and can form a thinner but still easily broken membrane.  
6. Shape the dough into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest for 5 minutes.  
7. After resting, continue slapping the dough for about 5 minutes, until it reaches full gluten development—capable of forming a thin, resilient membrane.  
8. Shape the dough into a ball, place in a container, cover with a damp cloth, and let ferment at room temperature for 1 hour, until doubled in size.  
   **Check**: Dust your finger with flour and poke a hole in the dough—it should not spring back or collapse.  
9. During fermentation, prepare the meringue: Stir the egg white and caster sugar until the sugar dissolves, then add the almond flour and mix well. Transfer to a piping bag and set aside.  
   **Key Point**: The mixture is quite runny; do not cut the tip of the piping bag yet to prevent leakage.  
10. After the first rise, gently press the dough to release gas, divide evenly into 6 portions, round each piece, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest for 30 minutes.  
    **Key Point**: Resting thoroughly is essential; otherwise, the dough will be difficult to roll out.  
11. Take one rested dough piece and roll it into a tongue shape. Flip it over, then roll it up from top to bottom. Pinch the seam tightly and shape into an olive-like form.  
12. Brush the surface of the shaped buns with egg wash, then coat generously with chopped walnuts. Place them on a baking tray lined with a silicone mat or parchment paper.  
13. Let the walnut-coated buns proof for about 1 hour, until doubled in size.  
14. Evenly pipe a layer of the meringue mixture onto the proofed buns, then dust with icing sugar.  
    **Key Point**: Pipe the meringue in a dense pattern without making it too wide.  
15. Bake in a preheated oven. For home-use conventional oven: Top heat 210°C / Bottom heat 180°C, bake for about 16 minutes. For convection oven: 175°C, bake for about 15 minutes.  
    **Check**: The meringue on the bun surface turns golden brown, and the buns are fully puffed and set.  
16. Prepare the chocolate cream filling: Combine heavy cream, caster sugar, and dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Heat over a water bath, stirring until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth.  
    **Key Point**: Stir until fully emulsified and silky.  
17. Refrigerate the chocolate cream mixture for at least 4-5 hours, until it is thick and cold.  
18. Take the chilled chocolate cream mixture and whip it with an electric hand mixer until distinct peaks form and the texture is smooth.  
19. Once the buns have completely cooled, slice them open in the middle and pipe in the whipped chocolate cream.  
    **Key Point**: Ensure the buns are fully cooled before piping in the cream.  

## Health Advice
This almond meringue mocha cream bun is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to share with friends or enjoy as a slow-carb weekend treat. The chocolate cream needs to be chilled in advance before whipping. Pay attention to portion control to avoid excessive intake.
