# Perfect Puffs  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Butter: as needed (used for both the crust and the choux pastry batter)  
🌾 Cake flour: as needed (crust portion: butter:sugar:cake flour = 1:1:1; choux pastry batter: exact amount not specified)  
🍬 Sugar: as needed (used for the crust, in equal proportion with butter and cake flour)  
🥛 Milk: as needed (liquid for the choux pastry batter)  
💧 Water: as needed (liquid for the choux pastry batter)  
🥚 Eggs: about 1+ (egg mixture must be added in batches, based on the final batter consistency)  
🍫 Chocolate cream: as needed (for filling the puffs)  
🧂 Salt: a pinch (to balance sweetness)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing the crust and holding the batter)  
🔪 Knife  
🪚 Cutting board  
🍳 Non-coated flat pan/stainless steel milk pan (for boiling, scalding, and stirring the batter)  
🕸️ Strainer (to sift the cake flour)  
🥄 Spoon  
🎞️ Plastic wrap (for wrapping and shaping the crust)  
🥖 Rolling pin (to help roll out the crust)  
🍽️ Baking sheet  
♨️ Oven  
🧁 Piping bag (for piping the choux pastry batter and filling)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for poking holes at the bottom of the puffs)  

## Steps  
1. **Make the crust**: Place butter, sugar, cake flour, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Rub with your fingertips into even, sand-like crumbs. Knead into a dough, wrap in plastic wrap, shape into a cylinder, and freeze until firm.  
   *Key point*: After freezing, the slices must match the size of the piped choux pastry batter.  
2. **Scald the batter**: Pour butter, milk, water, and a pinch of salt into a non-coated pan. Heat over low flame until completely boiling, then immediately remove from heat. Add sifted cake flour and quickly stir until no dry flour remains and the dough holds together.  
   *Key point*: The liquid must be boiling to cook the flour thoroughly—this is the crucial first step for the puffs to expand.  
3. **Stir the batter**: Return the pan to low heat and continuously stir the dough for about 1 minute, until a thin layer of batter evenly coats the bottom.  
   *Key point*: Stirring evaporates excess moisture; the drier the batter, the stronger the puff’s expansion and the better the hollow center.  
4. **Cool and add egg mixture**: Transfer the batter to a large bowl and spread it out. Let cool until no longer hot to the touch. Add the beaten egg mixture in batches, using a spatula to press and mix thoroughly until fully absorbed.  
   *Consistency check*: When lifted with a spatula, the final batter should form a smooth-edged inverted triangle that slowly drips off.  
   *Note*: The amount of egg mixture is not fixed—adjust based on batter consistency; if the batter becomes too thin, it cannot be fixed.  
5. **Pipe the puffs**: Fill a piping bag with the batter and pipe evenly sized rounds onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a non-stick mat, leaving enough space between each for expansion.  
6. **Apply the crust**: Remove the frozen crust cylinder from the fridge, slice into pieces about 0.2 cm thick, and gently place on top of each piped batter blob.  
7. **Bake**: Preheat the oven to 180°C. Place the baking sheet in the middle rack and close the door quickly. Bake for about 30 minutes, until the puffs are fully expanded and golden brown on the surface.  
   *Key point*: Do not open the oven door during baking, or the puffs will collapse; after the time is up, leave the door closed and let the puffs rest inside for 3-5 minutes before removing to prevent shrinkage from temperature changes.  
8. **Fill the puffs**: Once the puffs are completely cooled, poke a small hole at the bottom with chopsticks and pipe in the chocolate cream.  
   *Note*: The puff shells must be fully cooled, or the filling will melt and leak out.  

## Health Advice  
These puffs require strict temperature and moisture control, ideal for those pursuing perfect texture. The butter and sugar content is relatively high, so enjoy in moderation.
