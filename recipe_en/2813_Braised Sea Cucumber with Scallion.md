# Braised Sea Cucumber with Scallion  
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 4 rehydrated sea cucumbers  
🧅 The white part of 1 large scallion  
🫗 20-25ml cooking oil  
🫕 20g oyster sauce  
🧂 5g light soy sauce  
🍬 2g sugar  
🧴 2g starch  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the scallion white part into segments about 1 cm long, and cut the sea cucumbers into similar 1 cm pieces. Set them aside separately.  
2. In a small bowl, mix together the oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and sugar, stirring well to form a sauce.  
3. In another small bowl, combine the starch with an appropriate amount of water to make a slurry. Set aside.  
4. Heat the wok over high heat, then add the cooking oil and bring it up to temperature.  
**Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning the scallions during the subsequent frying step.  

5. Add the scallion white segments, reduce the heat to low, and slow-fry for about 3-5 minutes until the scallion pieces turn slightly golden and release their aroma. Be careful not to let them burn.  
**Key point**: Slow-frying over low heat allows the scallion fragrance to fully develop while keeping the scallion pieces intact.  

6. Using chopsticks, remove the fried scallion segments and set them aside on a plate.  
7. With the remaining oil in the wok, pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry over medium heat for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.  
8. Add the cut sea cucumber pieces and stir-fry for about 1 minute, ensuring they are evenly coated with the sauce.  
9. Add about 100ml of water, reduce the heat to medium-low, and simmer for about 5 minutes to let the sea cucumbers fully absorb the flavors.  
10. When the liquid in the wok has nearly evaporated, add the starch slurry along with the previously fried scallion pieces. Quickly stir-fry everything together to thicken the sauce and coat the ingredients.  
11. Once the sauce becomes thickened and glossy, turn off the heat and transfer the dish to a serving plate.  
**Key point**: After adding the starch slurry, stir quickly to avoid lumps. The dish is ready when the sauce fully coats the ingredients.  

## Health Tips  
Braised sea cucumber with scallion is rich in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for health-conscious individuals. Oyster sauce has a high sodium content, so those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of seasoning used.
