# Taro Cake
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Taro, approximately 650g (cut into medium-thick shreds)
🌭 Chinese sausage, 2 pieces (diced)
🦐 Dried shrimp, a handful
🍚 Rice flour, 280g
🌽 Cornstarch, 35g
💧 Warm water, 300ml
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🥜 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🌿 Five-spice powder or ground pepper, appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (non-stick)
🥄 Silicone spatula
♨️ Steamer
🥘 Round flat steaming tray (or deep dish)
📝 Parchment paper

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the rice flour and cornstarch together. Gradually add warm water while stirring to form a smooth batter. Set aside to rest.
2. Peel the taro, first slice it, then cut into shreds. Dice the Chinese sausage. Prepare the dried shrimp and chopped scallions separately.
3. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into a flat-bottomed pan. Sauté the diced Chinese sausage and dried shrimp over low heat until fragrant.
4. Add the taro shreds. Turn the heat to medium and stir-fry until half-cooked. Then add salt and five-spice powder or pepper, mixing well.
   **Key point**: Add the seasonings at this stage to give the taro shreds a base flavor.
5. Turn off the heat. Stir the rice batter again to recombine, then pour it into the pan. Quickly stir to ensure every taro shred is evenly coated with the batter.
   **Note**: This step must be done quickly after turning off the heat to prevent the batter from clumping prematurely.
6. Line the steaming tray with parchment paper. Pour in the mixed taro cake batter. Use a silicone spatula to press and flatten it firmly.
7. Bring water in the steamer to a boil. Place the steaming tray inside, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for 25 minutes.
8. Once steamed, remove from the steamer. Sprinkle the top with white sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Let cool slightly, then run a knife around the edge of the tray to help release the cake.
9. After unmolding, cut into small pieces. Serve directly, or pan-fry in oil until golden brown on both sides.

## Health Advice
Taro cake is rich in dietary fiber and carbohydrates, providing a feeling of fullness. It is suitable for those needing an energy boost or with weak spleen and stomach. However, Chinese sausage and dried shrimp are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
