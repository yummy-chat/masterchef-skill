# Home-Style Twice-Cooked Pork
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly, about 1/4 strip (pre-steamed, sliced thinly)
🧅 Garlic sprouts, 1 bunch (cut diagonally into sections)
🌶️ Green hot pepper, 2 (cut diagonally into chunks)
🌶️ Red hot pepper, 1 (cut diagonally into chunks)
🧄 Garlic, half a head (sliced or smashed)
🫚 Ginger, 1 small piece (sliced)
🫘 Fermented black beans, 1 teaspoon
🫘 Broad bean paste, 1 tablespoon
🥄 Steamed fish soy sauce, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok (carbon steel or non-stick)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥢 Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the pork belly in cold water or steam it until cooked through. Remove and let cool. Using a sawing motion, slice into thin pieces about 3 mm thick.
2. Add a small amount of oil to the wok. Add ginger slices, garlic, and fermented black beans. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
3. Add 1 tablespoon of broad bean paste. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until red oil is released and the sauce is aromatic.
4. Add the sliced pork belly. Keep stir-frying over medium heat until the slices curl slightly, the fatty parts release oil, and the edges become slightly crispy, giving off a charred aroma.
5. Drizzle an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce along the side of the wok. Stir quickly to evenly coat the meat with the sauce and soy aroma.
6. Add the green and red pepper chunks and garlic sprout sections. Turn to high heat and stir-fry quickly until just cooked, allowing the garlic sprout aroma and chili spiciness to blend.
7. Before removing from heat, drizzle a small amount of cooking wine along the side of the wok. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat to mix well. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Pork belly is rich in protein and B vitamins, but it has a high fat content. It is recommended to eat it with garlic sprouts and peppers to aid digestion and reduce greasiness. Suitable for those needing energy replenishment or winter nourishment. People with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
