# Fried Shallots with Garlic
Cuisine: Taiwanese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧅 Shallots (large amount, peeled and drained)  
🧄 Garlic (a small amount, for frying with shallots)  
🫒 Cooking oil (enough to fully submerge the ingredients)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Immersion blender (for chopping)  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Strainer  
🫙 Glass storage container (for storing the finished product)  
🥣 Bowl or basin  

## Instructions  
1. Place the shallots in a bowl and soak in water until the outer skin softens. Remove, peel off the dry skin, discard any damaged parts, and drain thoroughly.  
- After draining, the shallots can be refrigerated and stored for 1–2 weeks.  
2. Using an immersion blender, finely chop the drained shallots together with a small amount of garlic.  
- Prepare the garlic to be fried together with the shallots by rinsing once and draining well; this prevents it from becoming bitter after frying.  
3. Transfer the chopped shallots and drained garlic into a frying pan. Pour in cooking oil, making sure the oil completely covers the ingredients.  
- Start with a cold pan and cold oil, and fry over low heat throughout the entire process.  
4. Turn on low heat and continuously stir with a wooden spatula to prevent sticking or uneven heating.  
- Pay close attention when the ingredients start to float and turn a light yellow color.  
5. Once the fried shallots turn golden brown, immediately turn off the heat and let them continue cooking with the residual heat for a moment.  
- Avoid frying until dark brown, as the color will continue to deepen after removing from heat. Overcooking will produce bitterness.  
6. Immediately after turning off the heat, pour the contents of the pan through a strainer to separate the fried shallots from the hot oil, draining as thoroughly as possible.  
- The faster you strain, the better you prevent the residual heat from continuing to cook them.  
7. Allow the drained oil to cool completely to room temperature, then pour it back into an airtight container with the fried shallots to store them submerged.  
- The oil must be fully cooled before mixing, to ensure product quality and shelf life.  
8. Seal the container and store in the refrigerator. Use as needed.  

## Health Tips  
The key to making fried shallots is to use low heat throughout. Ensure the shallots are thoroughly drained before chopping to prevent oil splattering. This condiment is ideal for tossing with noodles or vegetables, but due to its high oil content, consumption should be moderate. Those with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should only consume in small amounts.
