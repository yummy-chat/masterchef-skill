# Ham, Egg, and Cheese Sandwich  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧀 Shredded cheese, as needed  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🥩 Ham slice, 1  
🥬 Lettuce, about 1 leaf  
🍅 Tomato, 1 slice  
🍞 Toast bread, 2 slices  
🧴 White sauce (salad dressing or mayonnaise), as needed  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 White silicone spatula  
🥄 Yellow silicone spatula  
🔪 Knife  
🧴 Squeeze bottle  

## Steps  
1. Heat a frying pan over low heat. No oil is needed, or brush with a thin layer of oil. Evenly sprinkle the shredded cheese into the pan, forming a hollow ring.  
   **Key point**: Leave enough space in the center of the ring for the egg to be added later.  
2. Crack the egg into the center of the cheese ring. Keep the heat low and fry.  
   **State check**: The egg white gradually sets, while the yolk remains runny or soft-cooked.  
3. Once the bottom of the egg is mostly set, place the ham slice and one slice of toast bread on top of the egg in sequence. Gently press to adhere.  
   **Key point**: Be gentle to avoid breaking the cheese ring structure.  
4. Carefully flip the entire cheese egg pancake using a silicone spatula so the bread side is facing down. Continue frying until the cheese layer is golden brown and slightly crispy, fully encasing the egg and ham.  
   **State check**: The edges of the cheese layer are crispy, and the overall structure is intact.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the cheese layer does not break apart when flipping.  
5. Remove the sandwich from the pan with the bread side facing up. Squeeze white sauce over the bread, then layer on the lettuce leaf and tomato slice in sequence.  
6. Cover with the other slice of toast bread. Fold the entire sandwich in half, gently press down with a spatula to set the shape, then serve on a plate.  
   **Key point**: Pressing after folding helps the ingredients meld together, making it easier to eat.  

## Health Tips  
This sandwich provides protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for breakfast or post-exercise energy replenishment. Cheese and sauce are high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with fruit or a vegetable salad to balance nutrition. Lettuce and tomato provide a small amount of vitamins, but be mindful of total calorie intake and control the portion size.
