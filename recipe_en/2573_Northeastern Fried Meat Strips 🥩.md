# Northeastern Fried Meat Strips 🥩  
**Cuisine**: Northeastern Chinese  
**Cooking Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Suggestion**: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork shoulder (梅花肉) 400g (preferred for its balanced fat and lean meat; front or hind leg meat can also be used)  
🥬 Sweet bell pepper (柿子椒) ~70g (torn into irregular pieces)  
🧅 Scallion 10g (chopped into scallion flowers)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (finely minced, used in two batches)  
🧂 Salt 2g (for marinating the meat strips)  
🍶 Potato starch 160g (must use potato starch for a crispy texture)  
💧 Water 130g (for batter)  
🫒 Cooking oil 20g (added to the batter for a crispier fried coating)  
🌟 Light soy sauce 20g  
🍋 Aged vinegar (陈醋) 10g (adds vinegar aroma and slight acidity; optional)  
🧂 MSG 3g  
🍬 Sugar 13g (mainly to balance saltiness and soften the flavor)  
🧴 Sesame oil 3g (for fragrance; do not overuse)  
🌽 Cornstarch 7g (for thickening)  
💧 Water 70g (for the sauce)  
🫒 Cooking oil (for frying) as needed (use generously)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Large bowl, soup bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon, regular spoon  
🌡️ Oil thermometer  

## Preparation Steps  
**Preparation Phase**  
1. Cut the pork shoulder into thick slices about one finger-width, then into small strips of the same width.  
2. Add 2g of salt to the meat strips and mix until the salt dissolves and the meat becomes sticky. If concerned about gamey flavor, add a pinch of white pepper (do not use cooking wine).  
3. Add 160g potato starch and 130g water, mixing thoroughly until the batter is very dry but still flows slowly. Then add 20g cooking oil and mix again until the batter has a consistency like lard.  
4. Tear the bell pepper into pieces, chop the scallion into flowers, and mince the garlic. Combine 1/3 of the minced garlic with some scallion flowers and set aside; keep the remaining garlic separate.  
5. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine 20g light soy sauce, 10g aged vinegar, 3g MSG, 13g sugar, 3g sesame oil, 7g cornstarch, and 70g water. Stir well and set aside. No additional salt is needed.  

**Frying Phase**  
1. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat over high heat. Check the oil temperature: use a thermometer, or dip chopsticks in the batter and drop it into the oil. If bubbles appear quickly within 3 seconds and the batter floats, the oil is around 150°C (302°F) and ready. **Key point**: Fry in batches to prevent the oil temperature from dropping too quickly.  
2. First fry: Gently place the meat strips into the oil, keeping your hand close to the surface (do not drop from height). Do not stir immediately; after about 20 seconds, gently stir with chopsticks once the strips are set. Fry over high heat for 2 minutes, then remove.  
3. Second fry: Heat the oil to about 190°C (374°F). Add all the meat strips and fry over high heat for 2 minutes, then remove.  
4. Third fry: Continue heating over high heat until the oil reaches 210°C (410°F) or higher. Fry the meat strips for 90 seconds until the exposed meat turns dark red, then remove.  
5. Prepare the bell pepper: Place the pepper pieces in a slotted spoon and dip them into the hot oil for about 10 seconds. Drain excess oil and combine with the fried meat strips.  

**Final Stir-Fry**  
1. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Without turning on the heat, add the reserved garlic-scallion mixture and stir-fry using the residual heat of the wok.  
2. Turn to medium heat and pour in the prepared sauce. Stir continuously until it thickens to a paste-like consistency.  
3. **Key point**: Add a spoonful of hot frying oil to the sauce and stir quickly to "activate" it.  
4. Add the meat strips and bell pepper to the wok, pour the sauce over them, and stir-fry quickly to coat evenly.  
5. Sprinkle with the remaining raw minced garlic, stir-fry a couple more times, then plate and serve.  

**Important Notes**  
- When coating the meat strips, the batter must be thick and dry, not thin, or it won't become crispy.  
- Three rounds of frying are key for crispiness; increase the oil temperature gradually and control the timing precisely.  
- Do not add salt to the sauce; the salt from the marinade and soy sauce provides enough flavor to avoid making it too salty.  
- Adding hot oil to the sauce at the end enriches the glaze and helps it cling better to the meat.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for muscle building or those with high physical energy needs. However, since it is fried three times, it is calorie-dense, so those on a weight-loss plan should control portions. Pair it with a light cucumber salad or steamed vegetables to balance the richness.
