# Nine-Day Garlic Fried Sweet and Sour Ribs
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April - August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs, about 1.5 jin (cut into 5 cm pieces)
🧄 Garlic cloves, 100 g (crushed and minced into garlic paste)
🥚 Egg, 1 (whole egg)
🌾 Sweet potato starch, 2 tbsp
🧂 Salt, 3 g
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, 5 g
🍬 White sugar, 12 g
🍶 White vinegar, 2 tbsp
🍶 Black vinegar, 5 g
🍯 Light soy sauce, 3 g
🫒 Cooking oil, a little (to prevent sticking)
🍯 Ice flower plum sauce, to taste
🍅 Ketchup, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste (for sauce)
🍶 Red vinegar, to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Slotted spoon and soup spoon
🔥 Induction cooker
🥢 Long chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Wash and drain the ribs, cut into 5 cm pieces, and rinse repeatedly in water until the water runs clear and the ribs turn white.
- Remove all blood to prevent the ribs from darkening after frying.
2. Crush the garlic cloves and mince into garlic paste.
3. Place the ribs in a bowl, add garlic paste, salt, chicken bouillon powder, sugar, white vinegar, black vinegar, and light soy sauce, and mix well.
4. Drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix again, then cover with plastic wrap and marinate for 30 minutes.
**Cooking Stage**
1. Remove most of the garlic paste from the marinated ribs, leaving a little.
2. Crack in one whole egg and mix well, then add two tablespoons of sweet potato starch and mix until each rib is evenly coated with a thick, sticky batter.
3. Pour enough oil into a pot and heat to 150°C.
4. Add the ribs one by one, fry over medium-low heat for 4-5 minutes until set, golden, and cooked through, then remove.
5. Raise the oil temperature to 180°C, add the ribs back for a second fry for 15-20 seconds until the color deepens and the outer shell is crispy, then remove immediately.
6. In another pan over low heat, add ice flower plum sauce, ketchup, white vinegar, sugar, and a little red vinegar, and stir with a soup spoon until smooth and slightly warm.
7. Plate the ribs and serve with the dipping sauce on the side.

## Health Advice
This garlic fried sweet and sour rib dish is crispy on the outside and tender inside, making it a great summer appetizer. However, due to its high calorie content from deep-frying, it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balanced nutrition. Using a double-frying method and controlling the oil temperature can help reduce oil absorption. Those with high blood sugar should be mindful of the sugar content in the dipping sauce.
