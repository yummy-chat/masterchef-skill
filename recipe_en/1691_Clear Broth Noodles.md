# Clear Broth Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 A handful of noodles (dried noodles, such as hanging noodles)  
🥚 1 egg  
🥬 1-2 heads of lettuce  
🧂 A pinch of salt (sprinkle into the oil before frying the egg, and for seasoning the broth)  
🧂 Two tablespoons of light soy sauce  
🍶 One tablespoon of aged vinegar  
🫒 One tablespoon of sesame oil or lard  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Iron pan (wok)  
🥣 Bowl (for holding the egg, preparing the broth, and serving the noodles)  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the scallions and chop them finely. Set aside.
2. **Fry a sunny-side-up egg**: Heat the pan and add oil. Once the oil is hot, first sprinkle in a few grains of salt, then crack in the egg.
   **Key point**: Fry over low heat throughout to prevent sticking and keep the egg white intact. Fry until the egg white is set and the edges are golden but not burnt. The yolk can be left runny or cooked through according to preference.
3. **Cook the noodles and blanch the greens**: Without cleaning the pan, add water directly and bring to a boil. Once the water is boiling, add the noodles and the washed lettuce.
   **Key point**: Use the residual oil and flavor in the pan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Cook the noodles until they have no hard center, and the lettuce is blanched. Remove both.
4. **Prepare the broth**: In a serving bowl, add the chopped scallions, two tablespoons of light soy sauce, one tablespoon of aged vinegar, a pinch of salt, and one tablespoon of sesame oil or lard. Pour in the boiling water from the pan and stir to combine into a clear broth.
5. **Assemble**: Place the cooked noodles and lettuce into the broth bowl. Top with the fried sunny-side-up egg.

## Health Advice
Clear broth noodles are low in fat and light, making them suitable for an upset stomach or when controlling calorie intake. The fried egg provides high-quality protein. For increased satiety, consider pairing with a small amount of lean meat or tofu. Avoid boiling the noodle water for too long to reduce purine content.
