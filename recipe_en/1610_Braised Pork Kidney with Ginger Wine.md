# Braised Pork Kidney with Ginger Wine
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Steps
1. **Prepare the pork kidney**: Halve the kidney lengthwise, completely remove the white sinew and deep red membranes, then slice thinly into butterfly cuts.
2. **Clean and marinate**: Rub the kidney slices with salt to remove any gamey odor, gently rinse 1-2 times with cold water, and drain. In a separate bowl, squeeze ginger with rice wine to extract ginger juice; pour this over the kidney slices, add a pinch of salt, and mix well to marinate.
3. **Prepare the side ingredients**: Fry two sunny-side-up eggs; slice the ginger; pit the red dates; slice the lean pork and coat lightly with cornstarch; cut the celery into sections.
4. **Simmer the broth**: Bring about 1 liter of water to a boil in the pot. Add the fried eggs, sliced ginger, angelica sinensis, pitted red dates, and wolfberries. Sprinkle in ground white pepper and a splash of rice wine. Keep it at a rolling boil for about 15 minutes, until the broth turns slightly milky white.
5. **Add the pork**: Gently drop in the cornstarch-coated lean pork slices. Do not stir immediately; let it cook until the broth becomes a rich milky white.
6. **Add the kidney**: Pour in the marinated kidney slices along with the marinade. Cook for about 2 minutes.
7. **Season and serve**: Skim off any foam. Season with salt and brown sugar powder. Finally, stir in the celery sections, turn off the heat, and serve immediately.

## Health Note  
Pork kidney is rich in iron and zinc; paired with ginger and rice wine, it helps warm and nourish the body, making it ideal for those with cold hands and feet in autumn and winter. However, its cholesterol content is relatively high, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
