# Butter and Wasabi Crayfish
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Crayfish, appropriate amount
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into segments)
🧈 Butter, two pieces
💚 Wasabi, appropriate amount
🧴 Light soy sauce, two tablespoons
⚪ White pepper, a pinch
🍶 Cooking wine, one tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, half a tablespoon
🧂 Chicken powder, one tablespoon
🍬 White sugar, half a tablespoon
💧 Water, half a bowl
🤎 Dark soy sauce, half a tablespoon
🌾 Cornstarch, two tablespoons
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🪓 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Frying pan
🥢 Chopsticks
🍴 Spatula
🍽️ Serving plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Use kitchen scissors to trim the antennae and pincers of the crayfish, taking care to retain the roe in the head. Make a cut along the belly side of the shrimp to "butterfly" it, then rinse thoroughly with clean water.
   **Key Point**: Retain the roe; "buttering" the shrimp makes the meat firm, bouncy, and easier to absorb flavors.

2. Add two tablespoons of cornstarch to the prepared crayfish and gently toss with your hands to evenly coat each shrimp with a thin layer of starch.
   **Key Point**: The thin starch layer helps the shell better absorb the sauce during frying.

3. In a bowl, combine appropriate amount of wasabi, two tablespoons of light soy sauce, a pinch of white pepper, one tablespoon of cooking wine, half a tablespoon of oyster sauce, one tablespoon of chicken powder, half a tablespoon of white sugar, half a bowl of water, and half a tablespoon of dark soy sauce. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks until the wasabi is completely dissolved and the seasonings are well combined.
   **Key Point**: The wasabi must be fully mixed with the other seasonings to avoid clumps.

4. Heat a frying pan over high heat and add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, place the starch-coated crayfish into the pan one by one. Fry over medium-high heat for about one minute per side until the shells turn golden brown and emit a fragrant aroma.
   **Key Point**: The pan and oil must be hot so the shrimp sear quickly, setting the shape and making the shells crispy.

5. Add two pieces of butter on top of the fried shrimp. Once the butter melts and sizzles, immediately add a generous amount of minced garlic.
   **Key Point**: The quickly melting butter imparts a rich, creamy aroma.

6. Pour the prepared sauce mixture into the pan and stir-fry together with the shrimp. Once the sauce comes to a boil and evenly coats each shrimp, continue cooking until the sauce thickens and is fully absorbed by the shrimp.
   **Key Point**: The starch layer on the shrimp helps absorb the sauce, enhancing the flavor.

7. Add the scallion segments and quickly stir-fry a few times to release the scallion aroma. Immediately turn off the heat, slide the shrimp out of the pan, and plate them.
   **Key Point**: Do not overcook the scallion segments; retain their fresh, aromatic flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein, but the butter, sugar, and oil content are also significant. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables to balance the calories. Although the roe is delicious, it is high in cholesterol; individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Wasabi has antibacterial and appetite-stimulating properties, but use it in appropriate amounts to avoid irritation.
