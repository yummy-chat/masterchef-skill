## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the lamb shank into sections and soak in cold water for 2 hours to fully release blood.
   **Key Point**: Sufficient soaking time is needed to completely draw out the blood, ensuring clean meat.
2. Cut the white radish and carrot into diamond-shaped pieces. Prepare scallion sections, ginger slices, angelica dahurica, and dried chili peppers. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil, blanch the white radish and carrot pieces to remove their pungent and raw taste, then drain and set aside.
3. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, Xiaomi chili pepper rings, and chopped scallion. Drizzle with hot oil to release the fragrance. Then add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, Chinese black vinegar, chili oil, a tiny amount of sesame oil, and regular sesame oil. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro and stir to combine. Set aside.
4. Place the lamb in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove the lamb and drain.
   **Key Point**: The meat must be added to cold water to allow the blood foam to fully rise, resulting in a clear, non-gamey broth.
5. Pour oil into the pot, add scallion sections, ginger slices, angelica dahurica, and dried chili peppers, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the lamb and stir-fry evenly, then immediately add enough boiling water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for 20 minutes, until the broth gradually turns white.
6. Add the blanched white radish and carrot pieces, stir to combine, cover and continue simmering for about 15 minutes, until the radish is tender.
   **Key Point**: Adding the radish later helps it maintain its shape and fully absorb the flavor of the broth.
7. Uncover, turn up the heat to reduce the sauce until slightly thickened and the radish is well flavored. Transfer to a serving plate, garnish with cilantro if desired, and serve with the dipping sauce.

## Health Advice
This braised lamb shank with radish is warming and nourishing. The radish, having absorbed the rich meat broth, is sweet, tender, and delicious. It is especially suitable for people who tend to feel cold in winter or need to replenish energy. Soaking and blanching the lamb effectively reduces its gamey smell. Although the dipping sauce is tasty, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to dip lightly rather than soak the meat in it.
