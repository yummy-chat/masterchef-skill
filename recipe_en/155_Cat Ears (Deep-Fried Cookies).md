# Cat Ears (Deep-Fried Cookies)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🟤 Brown Sugar, 60g
⚪ White Sugar, 60g
💧 Hot Water, 150g (to dissolve brown sugar) + 150g (to dissolve white sugar)
🫒 Cooking Oil, 20g (brown sugar dough) + 20g (white sugar dough)
🌾 Cake Flour, approximately 350–380g (brown sugar dough) + approximately 350–380g (white sugar dough)
🫙 Soy Sauce, as needed (to add color to the brown sugar water)

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥢 Rolling Pin
📦 Plastic Wrap
🍽️ Tray
❄️ Refrigerator
🔪 Chef’s Knife
🍳 Non-Stick Pan
🥄 Slotted Spoon

## Steps
1. Prepare the sugar water: Dissolve the brown sugar and white sugar in their respective amounts of hot water, stirring until fully dissolved, to make brown sugar water and white sugar water.
   **Note**: Add a little soy sauce to the brown sugar water and stir well to deepen the color.
2. Knead the dough: When the sugar water has cooled to warm (not hot to the touch), add 20g of cooking oil to each. Then pour in the cake flour, first stirring into a flaky texture, then kneading into a smooth, moderately firm dough to obtain one brown and one white dough.
3. Rest the dough: Wrap each dough in plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 15–20 minutes.
4. Roll and layer: Roll out the rested doughs into large thin sheets of similar size and thickness. Spray or brush a layer of water onto the white dough sheet, then place the brown sugar dough sheet on top, gently smoothing it down.
   **Key point**: Applying water helps the two layers adhere tightly.
5. Roll and freeze: Apply another layer of water to the surface of the stacked two-color dough, then tightly roll it lengthwise into a long log. Wrap the log in plastic wrap and freeze for about 2 hours, until completely firm.
6. Slice: Remove the frozen dough log and cut into thin slices about 2–3 mm thick.
   **Note**: After cutting each slice, slightly shift the dough before cutting the next to prevent the slices from sticking together.
7. Deep-fry: Pour enough oil into the pan and heat to 50–60% hot. Add the slices one by one into the oil—they will quickly float to the surface. Keep the heat at medium-low and gently turn them with chopsticks to ensure even cooking, frying for just over 1 minute.
8. Check for doneness: When the cookies change from soft to firm, the color darkens slightly, and the surrounding oil bubbles become significantly smaller, it indicates that the moisture has been fried out, and they are ready to be removed.
9. Drain oil: Place the fried cat ears on a slotted spoon or paper towels to drain excess oil before serving.

## Health Advice
These two-color cat ear cookies are high in calories and are best enjoyed occasionally as a snack. Those on a weight loss or sugar-restricted diet should only have a small taste. When deep-frying, be careful to control the oil temperature to avoid burnt exteriors and raw interiors. Once cooled, store in an airtight container to maintain a crispy texture.
