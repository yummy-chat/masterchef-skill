# Bamboo Rice

Cuisine: Yunnan cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Glutinous rice 750g (soaked in advance for about two days until grains crumble easily when pinched)  
🍌 Bananas 10 (peeled and mashed into a paste)  
🎋 Fresh bamboo tubes 10 (cleaned, keeping the inner membrane intact)  
🌽 Corn husks (or straw) as needed (for sealing the openings)  
🥥 Thick coconut milk 200ml  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Masher  
🥄 Small spoon  
🔥 Oven  
🥄 (Optional) Funnel  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Drain the soaked glutinous rice and place it in a bowl. Add the mashed bananas and coconut milk, then mix thoroughly by hand until the rice grains are coated and form a moist, sticky paste.  
2. Use a small spoon to fill the bamboo tubes with the rice paste, filling them to about 80–90% full. Gently tap the bamboo tubes during filling to compact the paste and avoid air gaps.  
3. Take rolled corn husks and tightly plug the openings of the bamboo tubes.  
4. Preheat the oven to 160°C for the top heating element and 150°C for the bottom heating element. Place the bamboo tubes in the oven and roast for about 90 minutes, until the surface of the bamboo turns slightly golden and the aroma is released.  
- **Note**: Traditionally, the best flavor is achieved by roasting directly over charcoal or wood fire; the oven is used here as an alternative.  
5. Remove the bamboo tubes and let them cool slightly. Split or crack open the bamboo tubes along the side. The inner membrane will stick to the rice and come out together, ready to enjoy.  

## Health Tips  
This bamboo rice features soft and sticky glutinous rice, blended with the sweetness of coconut milk and bananas, making it a good choice for an energy-boosting meal. However, glutinous rice has a high glycemic index, so it is recommended that people with diabetes or those controlling blood sugar consume it in small amounts. Ensure the glutinous rice is thoroughly soaked and the roasting time is sufficient for the bamboo aroma to infuse the rice.
