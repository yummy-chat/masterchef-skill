# Baked Rice with Minced Meat and Cheese
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Rice – appropriate amount  
🥩 Minced meat – appropriate amount (pork or beef)  
🧅 Onion – appropriate amount (finely minced)  
🍅 Tomatoes – about 3 (peeled and stemmed)  
🧀 Shredded cheese – appropriate amount  
🌿 Dried parsley – a pinch (for garnish after cooking)  
🧂 Cooking oil – appropriate amount  
🧄 Minced garlic – appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🥫 Tomato paste – appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥄 Strainer  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥢 Spatula  
🔥 Oven  
🫕 Lidded saucepan  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly, blanch in boiling water until just broken but still firm, then drain and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Do not blanch for too long; the rice grains should remain half-cooked for a better texture after baking.  

2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat, add the minced meat, and stir-fry until it changes color, breaking it apart and allowing moisture to evaporate.  
   **Note**: The minced meat should be stir-fried until dry and fragrant to prevent excess moisture from affecting the final texture.  

3. Add minced garlic and minced onion, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
   **Key point**: The garlic and onion should be cooked until translucent, fully releasing their aroma.  

4. Place the whole peeled and stemmed tomatoes into the wok, cover with a lid, and simmer. Once the tomatoes soften from the heat, use a spatula to crush them into a sauce.  
   **Note**: Keep the heat low during simmering to avoid scorching the bottom of the wok.  

5. Add salt, light soy sauce, and tomato paste, stirring evenly with the meat and vegetable sauce to form the base.  

6. Spread the drained rice evenly over the sauce surface, gently pressing it flat with a spatula to ensure good contact between the rice and sauce.  
   **Key point**: Do not stir after spreading the rice, to avoid uneven absorption of the sauce.  

7. Sprinkle a thick layer of shredded cheese over the rice, covering the surface completely.  
   **Note**: Use enough cheese, otherwise it will not form a full, stretchy layer after baking.  

8. Place the wok into a preheated oven at 200°C and bake for about 10–15 minutes, until the cheese is fully melted, bubbly, and golden brown. If using a lidded saucepan, you can bake over low heat on the stovetop, covering with the lid, then removing the lid to let the surface moisture evaporate after the cheese melts.  
   **Key point**: When baking in the oven, monitor the cheese coloring to avoid burning; when using a saucepan, bake slowly over low heat, sealing with the lid to help the cheese melt.  

9. Remove from heat, sprinkle dried parsley on top while hot, and serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This baked rice dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, but the cheese and minced meat are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with a side of lightly stir-fried vegetables. Suitable for those looking to build muscle or with high energy needs; for those on a weight-loss diet, use cheese in moderation.
