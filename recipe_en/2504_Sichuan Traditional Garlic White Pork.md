# Sichuan Traditional Garlic White Pork

Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Second-cut pork belly (the second cut near the tail, with alternating fat and lean meat, more lean meat)
🧄 Purple-skinned single-clove garlic (purple skin, single clove, stronger flavor)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for cooking the meat and preparing the soy sauce)
🧅 Scallion segments, appropriate amount (for cooking the meat and preparing the soy sauce)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (to remove the meaty smell)
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount (for preparing the soy sauce)
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount (for preparing the soy sauce)
🍃 Bay leaves, appropriate amount (for preparing the soy sauce)
🌾 Fennel seeds, appropriate amount (for preparing the soy sauce)
🥜 Cardamom, appropriate amount (for preparing the soy sauce)
🧂 Soy sauce (mixed with brown sugar in a 3:1 ratio to prepare the soy sauce)
🍯 Brown sugar (mixed with soy sauce in a 3:1 ratio)
🧂 Salt, a pinch (added when mashing the garlic)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (to remove the meaty smell)
🌶️ Chili oil/Red oil, appropriate amount (drizzled at the end)
🌿 Chopped scallions, appropriate amount (scattered at the end)
🍲 Meat broth, a small amount (for mixing with the garlic paste)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Strainer
🧽 Fine-mesh strainer
🥣 Mortar and pestle
🥄 Soup spoon, regular spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the soy sauce**: Pour a bowl of water into a wok. Add ginger slices, scallion segments, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, fennel seeds, and cardamom. Pour in the soy sauce and brown sugar. Simmer over low heat until the liquid thickens. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer to remove the solids, yielding thick soy sauce. Set aside.
2. **Cook the pork**: Place the whole piece of second-cut pork belly in boiling water with ginger, scallions, Sichuan peppercorns, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 15 minutes, until the meat feels firm when pressed with a finger. Remove the meat and submerge it in the cooking broth until completely cool.
3. **Prepare the garlic paste**: Peel the purple-skinned single-clove garlic, crush it with the flat side of a knife, then place it in a mortar. Add a pinch of salt and grind into a fine paste. Transfer to a bowl and mix in a small amount of the meat cooking broth until smooth.
**Note**: Adding salt while grinding makes the garlic paste thicker; if no mortar is available, use the back of a knife to crush it.

4. **Slice and blanch the meat**: Slice the cooled pork into large, thin slices. Place the slices into a strainer and dip into boiling water for a few seconds, then remove immediately.
**Key point**: The slices should be as thin as possible; blanching warms the meat and helps it absorb flavors better.

5. **Assemble the dish**: Arrange the blanched meat slices neatly on a plate. First drizzle with the prepared soy sauce, then spread the garlic paste, and finally drizzle generously with chili oil. Scatter chopped scallions on top.
**Key point**: The order of the sauces must not be reversed. Traditionally, no MSG, chicken powder, or sugar is added.

## Health Advice
Garlic white pork is balanced with fat and lean meat, paired with homemade soy sauce and garlic paste. It can be enjoyed occasionally during a weight-loss diet. Pair it with a light stir-fried vegetable to balance the richness. For best flavor, prepare the garlic paste fresh. Soaking the cooked pork in broth until completely cool makes it easier to slice thinly.
