# Fujian-Style Fried Rice Cakes
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Rice cakes / white rice cakes (any shape)
🌿 Scallions
🧂 Cooking oil 50ml
🧂 Soy sauce 15ml
🧂 Salt 1-2g × number of servings

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok or pan
🥣 Bowl
🔪 Cutting tool

## Cooking Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the rice cakes and cook until fully done. Stir occasionally during cooking to prevent sticking.
2. Fill a bowl with water, then remove the cooked rice cakes and soak them in the bowl to prevent sticking.
3. Cut the scallions into small pieces, separating the white parts from the green parts; cut the greens into small sections and set aside.
4. If adding eggs, scramble them in advance and set aside.
5. Heat the pan, add 30ml of cooking oil, and stir-fry the white parts of the scallions until most turn golden brown and fragrant. Pour out the scallion oil and set aside.
6. Heat another pan, add 20ml of cooking oil, and stir-fry the eggs, greens, and other side ingredients evenly.
7. Drain the water from the bowl of rice cakes, then pour the rice cakes into the pan.
8. Add soy sauce and salt, and stir-fry evenly.
9. Turn off the heat, drizzle in the previously prepared scallion oil, stir evenly, and serve.

## Health Tips
Rice cakes are rich in carbohydrates, making them suitable for people with high activity levels to replenish energy. Using scallion oil for seasoning can reduce the amount of salt needed, lowering sodium intake. It is recommended to pair with vegetables and protein to help slow down blood sugar spikes. Be mindful of portion sizes.
