# Stir-Fried Winter Melon  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 Winter melon, about one plate (peeled, sliced to about two coins' thickness)  
🥩 Pork belly, a small amount (about 5–6 slices, sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, 1 clove (crushed)  
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers, a small amount (finely chopped or sliced into rings)  
🧅 Scallions, a small amount (cut into sections, white and green parts separated)  
🧅 Garlic chive flowers, a small amount (sprinkle before serving)  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount  
🧂 Salt, a small amount (added in two batches)  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (poured along the edge of the wok)  
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, a small amount (to enhance freshness)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a small amount (optional)  
🍶 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (for color)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Spatula/wok spatula  
🥣 Bowl  

## Steps  
1. Peel the winter melon and slice it to about two coins' thickness.  
2. Crush the garlic, finely chop the green and red chili peppers, cut the scallions into sections, and separate the white and green parts.  
3. Heat the wok over medium heat, add a small amount of cooking oil, add the winter melon slices, and stir-fry over medium heat.  
   **Key point**: Add a small amount of salt during stir-frying to give the winter melon a base flavor.  
4. Stir-fry until the winter melon becomes slightly soft and translucent, about 80–90% cooked, then remove and set aside.  
5. In another wok, heat over medium heat, add a small amount of cooking oil, and add the pork belly slices. Stir-fry over medium heat.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry until the pork belly releases its fat and the edges are slightly crispy, releasing the aroma.  
6. Add the crushed garlic, green and red chili peppers, and the white parts of the scallions. Stir-fry until the aroma is released.  
7. Pour the cooking wine along the edge of the wok to create a sizzling aroma.  
8. Add the light soy sauce and stir-fry evenly to release the savory flavor.  
9. Return the pre-cooked winter melon slices to the wok, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry to combine.  
   **Note**: The winter melon is already pre-cooked; this step mainly mixes the flavors.  
10. Final seasoning: Add a small amount of salt, sugar to enhance freshness, and a small amount of dark soy sauce for color.  
    **Note**: Be cautious with salt, as it was already added earlier; adjust the dark soy sauce based on the desired color of the dish.  
11. Stir-fry quickly to ensure the seasonings are evenly coated on the ingredients.  
12. Before serving, sprinkle the garlic chive flowers, stir to combine, and then plate.  

## Health Tips  
Winter melon has high water content and low calories. Pairing it with pork belly provides fat and protein, making it suitable for clearing heat and promoting urination in summer. It is recommended that people with high blood pressure reduce the amount of salt used, as the pork belly itself has a savory and fresh flavor.
