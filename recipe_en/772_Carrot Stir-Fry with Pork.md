# Carrot Stir-Fry with Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Instructions
1. Wash the pork front leg meat, remove the skin, separate the fat and lean meat, and slice both thinly. Add a pinch of salt, dark soy sauce, and a little water to the lean meat slices. Mix with your hands until sticky and tacky, then add a little cornstarch and mix well to lock in the meat juices.
   **Key**: The front leg meat is already tender, and a simple coating ensures a smoother, silkier texture.

2. Peel the carrots, cut them diagonally into segments, then slice into diamond shapes. Slice the ginger, crush the garlic, cut the red chili peppers into rings, and cut the green garlic leaves diagonally into segments.
   **Key**: Add the green garlic leaves just before removing from the wok to preserve their fragrance and crisp green color.

3. Heat the wok over high heat, add a little oil to coat the bottom, then add the fat meat slices. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders. If too much lard is released, you can scoop out some for other uses.

4. Add the ginger slices to the wok and stir-fry with the fat over medium heat until fragrant. Add the marinated lean meat slices, turn the heat to high, and quickly stir-fry until the lean meat is completely cooked with no pink color. Remove and set aside.

5. Using the remaining fragrant oil in the wok, add the carrot slices and crushed garlic. Stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute.

6. Add salt, white sugar, MSG, and light soy sauce to the carrots. Stir-fry over high heat until evenly combined.

7. Return the cooked lean meat to the wok, add the red chili pepper rings, and quickly stir-fry to blend the flavors. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in the green garlic leaves, turn the heat to high, drizzle the aged vinegar along the edge of the wok. Use the high heat to release the vinegar's aroma, quickly stir-fry a few times, and immediately remove from the wok.
   **Key**: The aged vinegar must be drizzled along the edge of the wok. The high temperature quickly evaporates the sourness, leaving only the vinegar's fragrance without a noticeable sour taste.

## Health Advice
Carrots are rich in β-carotene, and stir-frying them with pork fat can enhance the absorption efficiency of vitamin A, making this dish suitable for those needing eye care or immune system support. Since the lean meat has been marinated and coated, adjust the salt and MSG amounts when seasoning to avoid excessive saltiness.
