# Stir-Fried Green Pepper and Century Egg Noodles with Smashed Chilies
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Long eggplant 400g (2 pieces, choose tender ones with thin skin)
🥚 Century eggs (preserved eggs) 2 pieces
🌶️ Screw chili peppers 200g (about 4 pieces, use linear peppers if you don't want it too spicy)
🍝 Fresh noodles 400g (serves two)
🧄 Garlic 30g (about 6 cloves, minced)
🌶️ Small red chili peppers/red chili peppers 40g total (sliced into rings)
🧂 Salt 2g (added when frying the peppers)
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 15g
🌶️ Fine chili powder 5g (for color)
🫒 Cooking oil 60g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 2g (a pinch)
🍾 Light soy sauce 30g
🍶 Aged vinegar 10g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g
🍬 White sugar 5g
🧂 MSG 3g
🫒 Sesame oil 4g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Steamer
🍳 Wok (for dry-frying)
🥘 Small saucepan (for frying Sichuan pepper oil)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls/basin
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1. Remove the stem from the eggplant and cut it lengthwise into quarters. Bring the steamer water to a boil, spread the eggplant pieces flat on the steamer rack, and steam over high heat for 10 minutes.
2. After 5 minutes of steaming, open the lid, add the whole century eggs (in their shells), and continue steaming for another 5 minutes. Remove and let cool.
   **Note**: The century eggs should be steamed in their shells inside a bowl to prevent the egg liquid from overflowing due to direct steam contact.
3. Remove the stem from the screw chili peppers and lightly crush them with a knife. Heat the wok without oil, add the peppers and 2g of salt. Press and stir-fry over high heat until charred spots (tiger skin) appear, then reduce to medium heat and continue stir-frying until the peppers are soft and wilted.
   **Key**: Avoid burning the peppers, or they will become bitter.
4. Slice the small red chili peppers/red chili peppers into thin rings. Crush the garlic and mince it. Combine and set aside.
5. Once cooled, tear the steamed eggplant into strips by hand, gently squeezing out excess moisture. Also tear the stir-fried peppers into strips. Peel the century eggs and cut them into pieces using scissors.
6. Cook the fresh noodles in boiling water. Rinse them in cold water, drain well, and place them in a large bowl.
7. Layer the eggplant strips, chili pepper strips, and century egg pieces on top of the noodles. Sprinkle over the red chili pepper rings, minced garlic, coarse chili flakes, and fine chili powder.
8. In a small saucepan, add the oil and Sichuan peppercorns. Heat over medium-high heat. Once the peppercorns float to the surface and turn a dark red color, remove them with a slotted spoon. Turn the heat to high and heat the oil until it smokes.
9. Pour the hot Sichuan pepper oil over the chili flakes and toppings to release their fragrance. Then add the light soy sauce, aged vinegar, oyster sauce, white sugar, MSG, and sesame oil.
10. Wearing disposable gloves, thoroughly mix all the ingredients and seasonings together. Taste for seasoning first, then add more salt if needed. Mix well and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and protein. Eggplant aids digestion, and century eggs supplement minerals. However, it is high in fat and sodium, making it suitable for occasional consumption. It is recommended to pair it with a light vegetable soup to balance nutrition. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt and oyster sauce used.
