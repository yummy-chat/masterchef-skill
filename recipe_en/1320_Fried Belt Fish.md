# Fried Belt Fish
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Belt fish (head and tail removed,内脏 removed, silver scales retained)  
🧅 Scallion 30g (cut into sections, for marinating)  
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced, for marinating)  
🍋 Lemon 3 slices (for marinating, to remove fishy smell, enhance aroma, and tenderize meat)  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍶 Huadiao wine 20ml  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns 32 pieces  
🌾 Starch appropriate amount (sprinkle a thin layer for coating)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (ample oil for frying)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
✂️ Scissors (for trimming fins)  
🔪 Knife, cutting board (for cutting into sections and scoring)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating)  
🍳 Pot (for frying)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing fish)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for testing oil temperature and adding fish to pot)  

## Steps
1. **Prepare the belt fish**: Use scissors to cut off the fins. Cut open the belly from the underside and remove the内脏. Thoroughly wipe away the black membrane and blood attached to the bone inside the belly. Retain the silver scales on the surface. Score one side of each belt fish section with a knife, then cut into pieces.
   **Key point**: The silver scales are rich in nutrients and do not need to be scraped off. The black membrane and attached blood must be completely removed to eliminate fishy smell.

2. **Marinate**: Place the fish pieces in a bowl. Add salt, Huadiao wine, scallion sections, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and lemon slices. Mix well by hand and marinate for 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: Lemon slices can tenderize the meat and remove fishy smell. The marinating time should not be too short to ensure flavor absorption.

3. **Coat with starch**: Remove the marinated fish pieces. Evenly sprinkle a thin layer of starch over the surface, gently shake off excess powder, so that a dry coating of starch covers the fish pieces.
   **Note**: The starch helps the fish hold its shape, making the skin crispier after frying.

4. **Fry**: Pour enough oil into the pot and heat to medium temperature (around 50% of maximum heat). Add the coated fish pieces one by one. Do not stir immediately after adding; gently shake the pot to ensure even heating. Once the starch sets and the fish turns slightly yellow, use a slotted spoon to gently separate them. Continue frying until both sides are golden brown, then remove.
   **Note**: Maintain medium oil temperature throughout. Do not add too many fish pieces at once to avoid sticking and oil temperature drop.

5. Remove and drain oil, then plate and serve.

## Health Advice  
Retaining the silver scales when frying belt fish is a good habit, as they are rich in lecithin and unsaturated fatty acids, suitable for those needing to boost brain function or protect cardiovascular health. However, frying is high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with vegetables to balance nutrition.
