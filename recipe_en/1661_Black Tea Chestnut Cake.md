# Black Tea Chestnut Cake
Cuisine Type: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 4 Eggs (separated into yolks and whites)
🌾 About 55g Cake Flour (measure 3 units with a bottle, do not press tight)
🥛 About 40g Milk (can be substituted with water)
🫒 About 35g Vegetable Oil
🍵 3 Tea Bags (1 for cake base, 1 for milk jelly, 1 for chestnut paste)
🍫 About 5g Cocoa Powder
🌽 About 5g Cornstarch
🥛 125ml Want Want Milk (1 box, can be substituted with regular milk + sugar/condensed milk)
🫧 About 3.5g Gelatin Sheets (half sheet, soaked in cold water until soft)
🌰 About 200g Chestnut Kernels (weight after cooking, about 250g raw chestnut kernels)
💧 Water, as needed (for boiling chestnuts)
🧈 About 20g Butter
🥛 About 320g Whipping Cream (20g for chestnut paste, 300g for chestnut cream)
🍬 White Sugar, as needed (50g for egg whites, 20g for chestnut paste, 15g for chestnut cream)
🍾 About 5g Rum (optional)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl (multiple)
⚡ Electric Hand Mixer
🍶 Mineral Water Bottle (alternative for hand-whisking egg whites)
🥄 Silicone Spatula
🍚 Rice Cooker
🍳 Small Saucepan
🍽️ Small Dish (for setting milk jelly)
🔪 Knife (for slicing cake, chestnut cake, etc.)
🌀 Cake Turntable
🎂 Offset Spatula
💼 Piping Bag + Piping Tip
🕸️ Sieve
📡 Microwave

## Instructions
1. **Make Black Tea Milk Jelly**
   - Soak gelatin sheets in cold water until softened.
   - Pour the Want Want milk and 1 tea bag into a saucepan. Heat until boiling, then remove from heat, cover, and steep for 5 minutes.
   - Remove the tea bag. Drain the softened gelatin sheets and add them to the warm milk, stirring until fully dissolved.
   - Pour the milk jelly mixture into a suitably sized small dish. Let it cool, then refrigerate until set.
   **Note**: If Want Want milk is unavailable, use regular milk with added sugar or condensed milk, or use half milk and half cream for a richer texture.

2. **Make Chestnut Paste and Filling**
   - Add chestnut kernels, water, and 1 tea bag to a pot. Boil until soft.
   - Remove a portion of the soft chestnuts, cut them into small pieces, and set aside for the filling.
   - Leave the remaining chestnuts in the pot. Add sugar, butter, and whipping cream. Mash and heat the mixture until it becomes a thick paste.
   **Key Point**: For a finer texture, use a blender to puree the mixture. If doing it manually, patiently mash and stir-dry any excess moisture. The chestnut paste can also be shaped and cut into pieces for a chestnut cake filling.

3. **Make Rice Cooker Cake Base**
   - **Egg Yolk Batter**: In a bowl with egg yolks, add cake flour, milk, vegetable oil, the contents of 1 tea bag, and cocoa powder. Mix until smooth and the batter flows slowly in a thick ribbon.
   - **Meringue**: Add white sugar to the egg whites. Using an electric hand mixer on high speed, beat until soft peaks form. Add cornstarch and continue beating on medium-low speed until stiff peaks form.
   - **Combine**: Gently fold one-third of the meringue into the egg yolk batter until just combined. Then pour the egg yolk mixture back into the remaining meringue. Fold and cut through the mixture quickly until just incorporated, being careful not to deflate the batter.
   - **Bake**: Pour the batter into the rice cooker inner pot. Gently tap the pot on the counter a few times to release large air bubbles. Press the "Cook" button and bake for about 50 minutes.
   **Doneness Test**: Lightly press the surface of the cake with a finger. If it springs back quickly, it is done. If not, continue baking for another 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Rice cooker cakes are soft and fluffy. If the batter becomes runny after combining, it indicates the egg whites have deflated significantly.

4. **Make Chestnut Cream**
   - Add white sugar to the whipping cream. Use an electric hand mixer to whip until distinct lines appear.
   - Add an appropriate amount of the prepared chestnut paste and mix well. This is the chestnut cream for frosting and filling.

5. **Cool and Slice**
   - After baking, invert the cake to cool completely. Once cool, unmold and horizontally slice into about 4 layers.

6. **Assemble the Cake**
   - On the cake turntable, layer in order: Place one cake layer, spread a layer of chestnut cream, and sprinkle with chopped chestnut pieces. Top with a second cake layer, spread chestnut cream, and arrange slices of the set milk jelly. Add a thin layer of chestnut cream, then a layer of chestnut meat. Finally, top with the third cake layer.
   - Use the remaining chestnut cream to frost the cake. Using a scraper or offset spatula, create lines on the sides.

7. **Decorate**
   - Pass some chestnut paste through a sieve, then place it in a piping bag. Pipe strips and edge decorations on top of the cake. Sprinkle with anti-humidity powdered sugar or milk powder.
   - Make caramel: Put 2 tablespoons of white sugar and 1 tablespoon of water in a microwave-safe container. Microwave on high for about 3 minutes. Drizzle the caramel over prepared chestnuts and sugar wafers, then arrange them on top of the cake. Optionally, decorate with gold leaf.
   **Key Point**: When making caramel, carefully monitor the heat to avoid burning. The overall decoration should evoke an autumn atmosphere.

## Health Notes
This cake is rich in dietary fiber from chestnuts and polyphenols from black tea, making it suitable for autumn nourishment. However, sugar and oil content should be controlled. It is recommended that diabetics and those on a weight loss plan enjoy only small slices.
