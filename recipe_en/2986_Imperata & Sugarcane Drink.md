# Imperata & Sugarcane Drink
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🪴 Sugarcane 500g (peeled, split open)  
🥔 Water chestnuts 500g (peeled and washed)  
🌿 Imperata root 100g (washed)  
🥕 Carrot 250g (optional, peeled and cut into rolling chunks)  
💧 Water 2000g  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Soup pot or clay pot  
🫖 Health pot (optional alternative)  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the sugarcane, wash it clean, and split it open from the middle; peel and wash the water chestnuts; peel and cut the carrot into rolling chunks; wash the imperata root.  
**Note**: Sugarcane used here is thinner and harder than regular sugarcane—it must be peeled and split to release its sweetness.

2. Place all ingredients into the pot and add 2000g of water.  
**Note**: The water-to-ingredient ratio should be well controlled; either a pot or a health pot can be used.

3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 1 hour.  
4. Once done, ladle out and serve. The soup will be clear in color, sweet and fragrant.

## Health Tips
This imperata and sugarcane drink is an excellent summer beverage for clearing heat and moisturizing dryness. It is low in calories with no additives, suitable for those experiencing上火 (excessive internal heat) or dry mouth and throat. Those with a cold spleen and stomach should drink it warm and avoid excessive consumption.
