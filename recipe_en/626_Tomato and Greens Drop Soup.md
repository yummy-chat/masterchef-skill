# Tomato and Greens Drop Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 All-purpose flour, as needed
🥬 Shanghai bok choy, 2–3 stalks
🍅 Tomatoes, 2
🧅 Purple onion, half
🥚 Egg, 1
💧 Water, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🧂 Compound seasoning powder (or salt), as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Food processor
🍲 Rice cooker (with cooking/soup function)
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🥄 Spoon

## Instructions
1. Place some of the all-purpose flour and the washed and drained Shanghai bok choy into a food processor. Blend until a dough forms, then add the remaining flour and continue blending until loose, evenly-sized dough flakes form.
   **Key point**: The greens must be thoroughly drained; add flour in batches to prevent the dough flakes from becoming too large or clumping.
2. Dice the tomatoes into small pieces, chop the purple onion into small dice, and beat the egg into a liquid. Set aside.
   **Key point**: The smaller the tomato dice, the easier it is to release red oil and juice when stir-fried.
3. Add an appropriate amount of cooking oil to the rice cooker. Sauté the diced purple onion until fragrant, then add the diced tomatoes and stir-fry until soft and juicy, with the broth base turning a bright red color.
4. Add enough water and bring to a boil.
5. Gradually sprinkle the dough flakes into the boiling broth in small batches, gently stirring to prevent sticking. Cook until the dough flakes float and darken in color, indicating they are fully cooked.
6. Slowly pour the beaten egg into the broth. Once the egg is fully set, use kitchen scissors to snip the Shanghai bok choy into small pieces and add them to the pot.
   **Key point**: Pour the egg slowly to form thin sheets; add the greens at the end to maintain a crisp texture.
7. Add an appropriate amount of compound seasoning powder to taste, stir well, and cook for another 1–2 minutes. Adjust seasoning after tasting, then serve.

## Health Tips
This soup incorporates greens into the dough, paired with tomatoes and onion for savory flavor, offering balanced nutrition and easy digestion. It is suitable for the whole family year-round, especially for those needing a warm meal or children who are picky about vegetables, helping to supplement various vitamins discreetly. It is recommended to reduce the amount of seasoning powder and make full use of the natural umami of tomatoes and onion to control sodium intake. Adding the greens at the end preserves their crisp texture and more nutrients.
