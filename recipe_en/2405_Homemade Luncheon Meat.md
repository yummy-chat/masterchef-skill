# Homemade Luncheon Meat
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork fat 100g (sliced thinly)  
🥩 Lean pork 500g (remove sinew, 150g sliced for meat chunks, 350g cut into pieces for meat paste)  
💧 Water 20g (for marinating meat slices) + 60g (for making ice water)  
🧂 Salt 2g (for marinating) + 4-6g (for powder mix)  
🍬 White sugar 3g (for marinating) + 5g (for powder mix)  
🥔 Potato starch 5g (for marinating) + 20g (for powder mix)  
🔴 Red yeast rice powder 0.8g (for marinating) + 0.2g (for powder mix, for coloring, optional)  
💎 MSG 3g (for powder mix)  
🐔 Chicken bouillon powder 3g (for powder mix)  
⚪ White pepper powder 0.5g (for powder mix)  
⚫ Black pepper powder 1-2g (for powder mix)  
🥬 Ginger powder 0.5g (for powder mix, optional)  
🧄 Garlic powder 1g (for powder mix, optional)  
🍛 Curry powder 1g (for powder mix, optional)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Bowls, plates  
🌀 Meat grinder  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🧴 Oil spray bottle  
📦 Metal mold (small loaf pan)  
🪩 Aluminum foil  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Spatula, spoon  
🌡️ Thermometer

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the pork fat into thin pieces and spread them on a plate. Remove the sinew from the lean pork. Cut 150g into thin slices for large meat chunks, and cut the remaining 350g into pieces for making meat paste. **Note**: Remove sinew thoroughly to prevent it from tangling the meat grinder blade.

2. Use the back of a knife to pound the 150g lean meat slices crosswise and lengthwise until loosened. Add 2g salt, 3g sugar, 5g potato starch, 0.8g red yeast rice powder, and 20g water. Mix evenly until sticky and tacky, and the water is fully absorbed.  
   **Key Point**: Pounding makes the meat chunks tender and ensures even coloring.

3. Mix 4-6g salt, 5g sugar, 20g potato starch, 0.2g red yeast rice powder, 3g MSG, 3g chicken bouillon powder, 0.5g white pepper powder, 1-2g black pepper powder, and optional ginger powder, garlic powder, and curry powder to taste. Combine evenly to form the powder mix.

4. Place the pork fat slices, marinated meat slices, and unmarinated lean meat chunks into a bowl. Prepare 60g water separately. Place the bowl and the water in the freezer for about 1 hour, until the water freezes into ice and the meat surface hardens.  
   **Key Point**: Freezing and temperature control are crucial for successful emulsification and achieving a bouncy texture.

5. Remove the frozen ingredients. First, grind the pork fat slices in the meat grinder at high speed into fine granules. Add the 350g lean meat chunks, using an intermittent method of "grind for 3 seconds, pause" for 5 cycles. Add the mixed powder mix and the crushed ice water. Continue grinding in the same manner for about 45 cycles, keeping the meat temperature below 13°C throughout. If the temperature rises too high and causes the grinder to stop, wait a moment before continuing. Using crushed ice water helps cool down the mixture and protect the blade.

6. The meat paste should have a glossy, emulsified appearance and be extremely sticky—difficult to drop off the spatula. Add the marinated meat slices into the meat paste and mix briefly to combine, keeping the large meat chunks intact.

7. Spray oil on the inner walls of the mold to prevent sticking. Use the spatula to vigorously stir the meat filling in one direction to expel air, until you hear a "puffing" sound. Layer the meat filling into the mold, pressing flat and compacting each layer with a spoon dipped in water. Shape the surface so that the center is lower and the edges are higher. Finally, cover with aluminum foil.  
   **Key Point**: Thoroughly degassing prevents holes in the finished product. Dipping the spoon in water prevents sticking. Shaping the surface compensates for expansion during steaming.

8. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the mold inside, and steam over the lowest heat for 40 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 15 minutes before removing.  
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat very low throughout steaming to avoid boiling that may damage the internal structure.

## Health Tips
This is a homemade luncheon meat with a balanced ratio of fat to lean meat. Making it at home reduces additive intake and is suitable for those on a low-salt diet. Keeping the meat cold during grinding is the key to achieving a tender and bouncy texture.
