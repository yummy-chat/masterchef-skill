# Steamed Egg Custard
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 3 eggs
💧 Water, as needed (about 1.5 to 2 times the volume of the egg mixture)
🧂 Soy sauce, a little (to drizzle before serving)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for beating and holding the egg mixture)
🍴 Fork (for whisking the eggs)
♨️ Steamer and steaming rack
🍽️ Plate or plastic wrap (to cover the container holding the egg mixture and prevent condensation from dripping in)
⏲️ Timer

## Instructions
1. Crack the eggs into a stainless steel bowl and whisk thoroughly with a fork until the egg mixture is uniform.
2. Add water that is about 1.5 to 2 times the volume of the egg mixture, and stir gently to combine.
3. Cover the container holding the egg mixture tightly with a plate or plastic wrap.
4. Once the steamer is steaming, place the container inside and steam over medium heat for 6 to 7 minutes.
**Note**: High heat will cause the egg mixture to boil internally, resulting in a honeycomb texture. Medium heat must be used for slow steaming.
5. When the timer goes off, immediately turn off the heat. Do not open the lid; let it rest for another 3 to 5 minutes using the residual heat inside the steamer.
6. Remove the steamed egg custard, cut it with a knife, and drizzle with a little soy sauce before serving.
**Key Point**: Covering the container and resting are essential for a smooth surface and tender texture; otherwise, steam droplets will ruin the smoothness.

## Health Tips
This steamed egg custard is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet and individuals with chewing difficulties.
