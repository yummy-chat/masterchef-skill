# Boya Juexian (Boya’s Zither) Cake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty rating: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs (separate egg whites and yolks)
🥛 100 g milk (40 g for cake base, 60 g for matcha panna cotta)
🌽 35 g corn oil
🌾 50 g cake flour
🍵 1 packet Earl Grey tea powder
🍬 50 g granulated sugar (for meringue)
🐮 2.5 g gelatin sheets (pre-soaked until soft)
🍵 5.5 g matcha powder (2.5 g for panna cotta, 3 g for matcha cream)
🥛 380 g heavy cream (30 g for panna cotta, 200 g for Boya Juexian cream, 150 g for matcha cream)
🌸 6 g Boya Juexian tea powder (jasmine green tea flavored beverage powder)
🥜 To taste, salted pecans
🍘 To taste, crispy flakes
🍅 Approx. 3 green tomatoes (for top decoration)
🧂 24 g granulated sugar (14 g for Boya Juexian cream, 10 g for matcha cream)
🌸 A few dried jasmine flowers (for decoration)

## Tools & Equipment
⚡ Rice cooker (for baking cake base)
🥄 Electric hand mixer, manual whisk
🍽️ Spatula
🪝 Flour sifter
🥣 Round mold / bowl (for slicing cake base and shaping panna cotta)
🧻 Plastic wrap
❄️ Refrigerator (for chilling and setting)
🔄 DIY piping turntable (steamer rack + basket)
🗡️ Offset spatula
🥄 Spoon (for side texture decoration)
🧁 Piping bag + piping tip
🥢 Bamboo skewer (backup)

## Instructions
1. Make the egg yolk batter: Mix 4 egg yolks, 40 g milk, and 35 g corn oil until well combined. Sift in 50 g cake flour and 1 packet Earl Grey tea powder, and stir until smooth and lump-free.
2. Whip the meringue: Gradually add 50 g granulated sugar to 4 egg whites, whipping until stiff peaks form—when the whisk is lifted, the peaks stand straight.
3. Combine the batter: Fold 1/3 of the meringue into the egg yolk batter until roughly combined. Then pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and fold with a cutting motion using a spatula until thick and smooth. Pour the cake batter into the rice cooker inner pot, gently tap to release air bubbles, and press the cook button to heat for about 50 minutes. Remove and invert onto a cooling rack. Once completely cooled, slice evenly into three layers, wrap in plastic wrap, and set aside.
   **Note**: The chiffon cake must be fully cooled before slicing, otherwise it will break easily.
4. Make the matcha panna cotta: Heat 60 g milk, 30 g heavy cream, 8 g granulated sugar, and 2.5 g matcha powder in a double boiler, stirring until combined. Add 2.5 g pre-soaked gelatin sheets and stir until melted. Strain the mixture into a round mold lined with plastic wrap and refrigerate until set.
5. Make the Boya Juexian cream: Combine 200 g heavy cream, 14 g granulated sugar, and 6 g Boya Juexian tea powder. Whip with an electric mixer until distinct lines form and the cream is firm.
6. Assemble the cake: Place one cake layer on the turntable. Spread a layer of Boya Juexian cream, then sprinkle salted pecans and crispy flakes. Spread another layer of cream, place the unmolded matcha panna cotta on top, and cover with more cream. Place the second cake layer on top, repeat the filling steps, then finish with the third cake layer. Use the remaining Boya Juexian cream to coat the sides and top. Freeze for 10 minutes to set.
   **Note**: Use enough nuts and crispy flakes to cover the filling layer. The panna cotta can be trimmed to match the size of the cake layers after unmolding.
7. Make the matcha cream: Whip 150 g heavy cream, 10 g granulated sugar, and 3 g matcha powder until lines appear. Remove the chilled cake from the freezer and use an offset spatula to evenly spread the matcha cream over the sides and top. Beginners can use the back of a spoon to apply the cream to the sides, creating a natural texture.
   **Note**: Do not take too long with the frosting, as the cream may melt.
8. Decorate: Pipe decorative swirls of matcha cream on top of the cake. Place the green tomatoes on top and sprinkle with dried jasmine flowers for garnish.

## Health Advice
This cake combines matcha, jasmine, panna cotta, and crispy flakes for a rich, layered texture, but it is also high in calories. It is best enjoyed as a shared afternoon tea treat, avoiding excessive consumption by yourself. Suitable for those who enjoy creative desserts; be mindful of sugar and cream intake, and pair with unsweetened tea for a better balance.
