# Honey Yam
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Yam (peeled and cut into chunks)
🫘 Red dates (soaked in water and washed in advance)
🌾 Potato starch (适量, for coating)
🍬 White sugar (about 4 spoons)
🍇 Fragrant vinegar (about 3 spoons)
🍅 Ketchup (about 3 spoons)
🌱 White sesame seeds (适量)
🫒 Cooking oil (generous amount, for frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl (for washing red dates and coating with starch)
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board
🍳 Oil pan/wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥣 Small bowl and spoon

## Steps
1. Soak the red dates in water in advance and wash off surface dust.
2. Peel the yam and cut into moderately sized chunks.
3. Place the cut yam chunks into a bowl with potato starch, toss and pat to evenly coat the surface with a thin layer of starch, ensuring they do not stick together.
4. Pour a sufficient amount of cooking oil into the pan and heat over high heat until the oil reaches 60% hot.
5. Add the starch-coated yam chunks one by one into the oil pan, turn to medium-low heat and fry slowly until the surface turns golden brown, develops a mottled hard crust, and floats. Fry until fully cooked in one go; no need for double frying.
6. In a small bowl, mix white sugar, fragrant vinegar, and ketchup, stir until the sauce is well combined.
7. Take a clean non-stick frying pan, pour in a little oil, add the prepared sauce, add white sesame seeds, heat over medium-low heat until the sauce boils and dense small bubbles appear, and the vinegar aroma wafts out.
   **Note**: Start with a cold pan and cold oil to prevent the sauce from burning instantly.
8. First add the soaked red dates into the bubbling sauce, then immediately add the fried yam chunks.
9. Quickly stir-fry with a spoon, ensuring the sauce evenly coats every piece of yam and red date, and prevent sticking to the bottom, until fully coated.

## Health Advice
Yam is rich in dietary fiber and mucilage protein, which can strengthen the spleen and benefit the stomach, making it suitable for moistening dryness in autumn. However, this dish uses generous oil and a large amount of sugar, so it is recommended to consume no more than 5 pieces per serving. Those with high blood sugar should be cautious.
