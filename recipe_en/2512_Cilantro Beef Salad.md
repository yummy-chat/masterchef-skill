# Cilantro Beef Salad
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin 250g (horizontally sliced into thin pieces)
🌿 Cilantro to taste (cut into sections)
🧅 Purple onion to taste (julienned)
🥜 Peanuts to taste
💧 Sichuan peppercorn water 50ml (add in batches for marinating)
🧂 Light soy sauce 15ml (for marinating meat) + 30ml (for dressing)
🧂 Salt 2g
🍬 Sugar 1g (for marinating meat) + 2g (for dressing, add while hot)
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.2g
🌽 Cornstarch 10g
🫒 Cooking oil to taste (for sealing the marinade)
🧄 Minced garlic 1 portion
🌶️ Fresh chili peppers 1 portion
🤍 Sesame seeds 1 portion
🫒 Vegetable oil to taste (mix with equal parts sesame oil for pouring over base)
🫒 Sesame oil to taste (mix with equal parts vegetable oil for pouring over base)
🍶 Vinegar 30ml
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn oil 10ml

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large glass bowl
🍲 Cooking pot
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1. Slice the beef tenderloin into thin pieces following the "slice beef horizontally" principle to cut across the long fibers.
2. Rinse the sliced beef repeatedly in clean water to remove any gaminess, then drain thoroughly and set aside.
3. Marinate the beef: Gradually add 50ml of Sichuan peppercorn water to the beef, mixing by hand to allow the beef to fully absorb the liquid. Then add 15ml of light soy sauce and mix well to enhance the umami flavor. Add 2g salt, 1g sugar, and 0.2g white pepper powder, mixing to season evenly. Then add 10g cornstarch, coating the beef surface with a thin layer. Finally, add an appropriate amount of cooking oil, mixing to seal in moisture and prevent the slices from sticking during cooking. Let it rest and marinate.
4. Cut the cilantro into sections and julienne the purple onion. Set aside.
5. Blanch the beef: Bring water to a boil in a cooking pot, then reduce to low heat, maintaining a temperature of around 90°C. Scatter the beef slices into the water. Do not stir immediately after adding; let them cook for about half a minute. Once the slices start to float, gently stir to ensure even cooking. Continue to cook for about 1 minute until all the slices are fully cooked and floating. Use a slotted spoon to remove and drain.
**Key point**: Keep the heat low to prevent the coating from coming off the meat; let the water move the meat rather than stirring directly.
6. Prepare the dressing: Place minced garlic, fresh chili peppers, and sesame seeds in a bowl. In a wok, mix equal parts vegetable oil and sesame oil, heat until smoking, and pour the hot oil over the ingredients in the bowl. Quickly add 2g sugar while hot and stir until dissolved. Then add 30ml light soy sauce, 30ml vinegar, and 10ml Sichuan peppercorn oil. Mix well.
7. Combine and serve: Place the blanched beef, onion strips, cilantro sections, and peanuts into a large bowl. Pour the prepared dressing over the top. Wearing disposable gloves, toss everything together evenly. Transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Tips
Beef is rich in high-quality protein, iron, and zinc. Paired with cilantro and onion for dietary fiber, this dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness muscle building and individuals with iron deficiency. Note that the dressing and peanuts have a higher calorie content; during weight loss, reduce the amount of peanuts. It is recommended to serve with brown rice.
