# Spaghetti Bolognese
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍝 Spaghetti, about 150-200g
🧅 Onion, half (minced)
🍅 Tomato, 1 (diced)
🥩 Ground pork (or ground beef), to taste
🧀 Shredded mozzarella cheese, to taste (for sprinkling after plating)
🥫 Ketchup, about 2 tablespoons
🍅 Tomato paste, about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for pasta water)
🌿 Ground black pepper, to taste
💧 Water or broth, to taste (for sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥘 Pot (for boiling pasta)
🥄 Wooden spatula or spoon
🥢 Long-handled tongs
🥄 Metal spoon
🫗 Oil bottle
🔪 Cutting board and knife

## Steps
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a pinch of salt, then add the spaghetti. Cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
2. Meanwhile, prepare the sauce: mix ketchup and tomato paste together until well combined.
3. Heat oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Sauté the minced onion until translucent and fragrant.
4. Add the ground pork, breaking it apart and cooking until browned and cooked through.
5. Pour in the prepared tomato sauce mixture, stir well to combine. Add some water or broth, then reduce heat to low and simmer until the sauce thickens and turns a bright red color.
   **Note**: Stir occasionally during simmering to prevent sticking.
6. Place the cooked spaghetti directly into the sauce pan. Use tongs or a wooden spatula to quickly toss and mix until the noodles are evenly coated with the meat sauce.
7. Plate the pasta and sprinkle with ground black pepper and shredded mozzarella cheese. For melted cheese, briefly heat in an oven or microwave.

## Health Tips
Spaghetti Bolognese is rich in protein and lycopene, making it a good post-workout energy boost. However, the oil and cheese in the sauce are high in calories, so portion control is advised. Those with sensitive stomachs can cook the pasta a bit softer and avoid undercooked onion to reduce irritation.
