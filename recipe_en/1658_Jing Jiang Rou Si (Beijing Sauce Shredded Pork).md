# Jing Jiang Rou Si (Beijing Sauce Shredded Pork)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1 piece pork loin (slice first, then shred)  
🧅 2 stalks large green onion whites (use only the white parts, finely shred)  
🍯 1 portion dry yellow soybean paste  
🍯 1 portion sweet bean sauce  
🍯 0.5 portion acacia honey  
🍶适量 Shaoxing Hua Diao wine  
🫚适量 ginger juice  
🧂适量 salt  
🌶适量 ground white pepper  
💧适量 scallion-ginger water  
🥄适量 light soy sauce  
🥚适量 egg white  
🌾适量 starch water  
🍬 3 teaspoons sugar  
🫒适量 sesame oil  

## Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Bowl  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Slice the pork loin first, then finely cut into even shreds.  
**Key point**: Push gently and pull slowly, slice first then shred, ensuring the shreds do not break.

2. In a bowl, combine dry yellow soybean paste, sweet bean sauce, acacia honey, Shaoxing Hua Diao wine, and ginger juice, and mix well.  
**Key point**: This is the technique for achieving a complex and rich sauce flavor.

3. Once the steamer is steaming, place the sauce bowl inside and steam over high heat for 40 minutes. Remove and set aside.  
**Key point**: Steaming removes the raw taste of the paste, making the sauce flavor milder.

4. Add salt, ground white pepper, scallion-ginger water, light soy sauce, egg white, and starch water to the shredded pork. Knead and mix repeatedly until sticky.  
**Key point**: Knead until the shreds become sticky and the moisture is fully absorbed, achieving a tender and moisture-locked state.

5. Halve the large green onion whites lengthwise, remove the core, slice into thin pieces, and then finely shred.  
**Key point**: The shredded onion will not get stuck in your teeth and will have a crisp texture.

6. Heat oil in a wok to 50% hot, add the shredded pork, and gently separate with chopsticks by moving left and right.  
**Key point**: Remove the shreds as soon as they change color to avoid overcooking, which makes them tough.

7. Place 3 teaspoons of sugar in the wok and stir-fry over low heat until it turns an amber caramel color. Pour in the steamed sauce and stir-fry over medium heat until the aromas meld.  
**Key point**: Fry the caramel color together with the sauce to fully combine the sauce fragrance with the caramel aroma.

8. When oil bubbles rise in the wok, add the pre-blanched shredded pork. Stir-fry quickly over high heat for about 10 seconds, ensuring every shred is evenly coated with sauce.  
**Key point**: Remove from the wok as soon as the bottom is dry with no excess sauce; stir-fry quickly to keep the shreds tender.

9. Line a plate with the shredded green onion whites, top with the cooked shredded pork, and serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
Jing Jiang Rou Si is a high-protein, low-saturated-fat quality choice. Pork loin is rich in B vitamins, and pairing it with shredded green onions can aid digestion. Suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or needing to control blood lipids. However, the sauce is high in sodium, so those with blood pressure sensitivity should use less sauce.
