# Classic Cantonese Clay Pot Rice (Lap Mei Fan)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Long-grain fragrant rice (Siyang rice), to taste (soak for 30 minutes in advance, drain, then mix with a pinch of salt and oil)  
🥩 Cantonese lap cheong (Chinese sausage), to taste (sliced thinly)  
🥓 Cured pork belly (lap yuk), to taste (sliced thinly)  
🥚 Egg, 1 piece  
🥬 Bok choy (green vegetables), to taste (blanched and set aside)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (julienned, for making sauce)  
🧄 Scallion, to taste (part for making sauce, part chopped as garnish)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, to taste (rehydrated, part for making sauce, reserved mushroom soaking water)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for mixing rice, greasing clay pot, drizzling along the pot edge, and making sauce)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for mixing rice)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (for making sauce)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste (for making sauce)  
🍬 White sugar, to taste (for making sauce, to enhance umami)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫕 Clay pot  
🔥 Stove  
🍳 Small pot or wok (for making sauce)  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking rice and holding prepared ingredients)  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🧤 Gloves (for mixing rice and greasing)  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps
### Making the Sauce
1. In a small pot, heat cooking oil over low heat. Add scallion sections, julienned onion, and rehydrated shiitake mushrooms. Fry until fragrant.  
**Key point**: Fry the aromatics over low heat to fully release their fragrance; cook until a rich aroma is noticeable.

2. Add light soy sauce, reserved mushroom soaking water, oyster sauce, and white sugar. Simmer over medium-low heat until the aroma is rich.
3. Remove all solid residues. Set the sauce aside in a bowl.
### Preparing the Ingredients
1. Slice the lap cheong and cured pork belly thinly. Set aside.
2. Wash the long-grain rice, then soak for 30 minutes. Drain thoroughly, then mix with a pinch of salt and peanut oil.
3. Evenly grease the inner surface of the clay pot with a layer of cooking oil. Add the mixed rice and pour in water.
**Key point**: The rice must be soaked in advance to absorb enough moisture; greasing the clay pot and mixing oil into the rice are crucial for forming a crust and preventing sticking.

### Cooking the Rice and Adding Toppings
1. Cover the clay pot with its lid. Bring to a boil over high heat.
2. When the water on the rice surface is mostly absorbed and "fish-eye bubbles" appear, immediately turn the heat to the lowest setting.
3. Evenly arrange the sliced lap cheong and cured pork belly on top of the rice. Crack a raw egg in the center, and place the blanched greens beside it.
**State check**: The optimal time to add toppings is when the rice is almost cooked and the water is just absorbed; the residual heat and steam will cook the vegetables and toppings.

### Steaming and Creating the Crispy Rice Crust
1. Put the lid back on the clay pot. Drizzle a circle of cooking oil along the edge of the lid.
2. Wearing gloves, gently rotate and tilt the clay pot so the oil flows to every corner of the bottom.
3. Keep the heat on the lowest setting. Continue to steam for 8-10 minutes, until you hear a slight "sizzling" sound from inside the pot.
**Core technique**: Drizzling oil and rotating the clay pot are key to creating an evenly golden crispy crust; maintaining low heat throughout prevents the bottom from burning.

### Serving
1. Turn off the heat. Open the lid and evenly drizzle the prepared sauce over the rice. Sprinkle with chopped scallions. Serve immediately.
**Tip**: Adding the sauce at the very end instantly releases its aroma, while allowing you to adjust the saltiness to your taste.

## Health Advice  
Lap Mei Fan is rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates. However, lap cheong and cured pork belly are high in sodium. It is recommended to pair the dish with plenty of vegetables. Individuals with high blood pressure should control their portion size.
