# Homemade Budget Unagi Donburi  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh meat appropriate amount (deboned and cut into pieces, serves about 2-3 people)  
🍄 White mushrooms appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic cloves a few pieces  
🐟 Grilled eel 1 piece (makes 3 servings)  
🥚 Eggs appropriate amount  
🥛 Milk a small amount (for scrambled eggs)  
🍚 Rice appropriate amount (3 servings)  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (used twice)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (used twice)  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil a small amount (used in batches)  
🍶 Sushi vinegar appropriate amount  
🌿 Dried seaweed flakes appropriate amount  
🧴 Mayonnaise appropriate amount (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
📋 Baking tray  
♨️ Oven  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥢 Rice paddle  

## Steps  
1. **Marinate chicken thigh**: Debone and cut chicken thigh into pieces, add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, and sugar, mix well to season, finally drizzle a small amount of cooking oil and mix.  
   **Note**: Sealing with oil at the end locks in the moisture of the chicken, resulting in more tender meat after stir-frying.  
2. **Prepare sushi rice**: After cooking, add sushi vinegar to the hot rice and mix well with a rice paddle.  
   **Key point**: Sushi vinegar gives the rice a Japanese flavor and enhances the overall texture.  
3. **Prepare scrambled egg mixture**: Beat eggs, add a pinch of salt and a small amount of milk, and stir evenly.  
   **Key point**: Milk neutralizes the dryness of the eggs, making the scrambled eggs more tender and smooth.  
4. **Bake eel**: Place the grilled eel on a baking tray and heat in the oven.  
   **Purpose**: Heating brings out more aroma and enhances the flavor of the eel.  
5. **Stir-fry chicken thigh**: Heat a frying pan, add the marinated chicken thigh, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until fully cooked and color changes.  
   **Doneness check**: Chicken turns from pink to white, with a slight sear on the surface.  
6. **Add white mushrooms**: Push the cooked chicken to one side of the pan, use the chicken fat in the pan, add sliced white mushrooms and garlic cloves, and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the mushrooms soften and the garlic aroma is released.  
   **Key point**: Using the chicken's own fat to cook the mushrooms makes the flavors more cohesive.  
7. **Season**: Drizzle an appropriate amount of light soy sauce into the pan, stir-fry evenly to coat and season the ingredients.  
8. **Make scrambled eggs**: In a separate clean frying pan, pour in the egg mixture, heat over medium-low heat, and gently push the eggs with a wooden spatula.  
   **Doneness check**: Eggs are set but remain moist and tender; avoid overcooking.  
9. **Assemble**: Place sushi rice in a bowl, top with scrambled eggs, then add the cut baked eel pieces, sprinkle dried seaweed flakes, squeeze mayonnaise, and serve with the stir-fried white mushrooms and chicken.  

## Health Advice  
This unagi donburi is rich in protein, with chicken thigh and mushrooms adding nutritional layers, making it suitable for busy office workers to replenish energy. Note that eel and sauces are high in sodium; patients with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of sauce used.
