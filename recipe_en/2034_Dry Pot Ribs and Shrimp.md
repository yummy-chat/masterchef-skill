# Dry Pot Ribs and Shrimp

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🥩 Pork spare ribs, about 2 lbs (chopped into small pieces)

## Cooking Instructions
1. Snip off the whiskers of the live shrimp, wash thoroughly, and drain.
2. Chop the pork spare ribs into small pieces, soak in clean water for about 10 minutes, adding crushed ginger and scallion to remove the gamey smell. Drain, then season with salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, Sichuan pepper powder, light soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Mix well and let marinate.
3. Peel and cut the potato and lotus root into thick strips. Cut the celery into sections and set aside.
4. Heat plenty of oil to 350°F (180°C). Add the marinated ribs and fry until about 80% cooked. Remove and drain.
**Note**: All main ingredients and accompaniments should be fried to achieve a dry, crispy texture.
5. **Caution**: Increase oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Return the pre-fried ribs for a second fry of about 30 seconds to make the exterior crispy. Remove and drain.
6. Using the same oil, fry the shrimp, potato strips, and lotus root strips in order. Fry until the shrimp turn red and the vegetables become golden and crisp. Remove and drain.
7. In a separate wok, heat oil. In order, stir-fry the minced ginger, minced garlic, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chilies until fragrant. Add the chili bean paste and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the oil turns red. Then add the secret spicy sauce and chili powder, stirring well to avoid burning.
8. Add all the fried ingredients (ribs, shrimp, potato strips, lotus root strips) into the wok. Stir-fry quickly to coat everything evenly with the seasoning.
9. Season with salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, cumin powder, thirteen-spice powder, sesame oil, and Sichuan pepper oil. Finally, add the celery sections and stir-fry until just cooked.
10. Transfer to a serving plate. Garnish with white sesame seeds and celery leaves or cilantro leaves.

## Health Tips
Both shrimp and ribs are deep-fried, resulting in higher calorie content. It is recommended to pair them with light vegetables and control portion sizes. Suitable for those who enjoy bold flavors and need an appetite stimulant. However, people with high blood pressure or high cholesterol should reduce the amount of chili bean paste and salt used.
