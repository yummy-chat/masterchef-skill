# Chive Boxes
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Meal for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 225g all-purpose flour  
💧 60g boiling water  
💧 75g room temperature water  
🍄 1/8 tsp yeast (very small amount, only to increase dough softness)  
🧈 1 tbsp cooking oil (for kneading dough)  
🌱 240g chives (washed, drained, and chopped)  
🥚 3 eggs  
🍜 2 small bundles vermicelli noodles (cooked until soft, rinsed under cold water, and chopped)  
🧂 1/8 tsp salt (added to flour)  
🧂 1/4 tsp salt (added to egg mixture)  
🧂 1/4 to 1/2 tsp salt (finally added to filling, adjust to taste)  
🫒 2 tbsp sesame oil  
🦪 1 tbsp oyster sauce  
🫚 1 tsp ginger paste  
🌶️ 1 tsp white pepper powder  
✨ 1/8 tsp five-spice powder  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for pan-frying)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Knife  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Flat-bottomed frying pan  
🫗 Lid  
🍽️ Plate  
🧻 Kitchen paper  

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the flour with 1/8 tsp salt. First, pour in the boiling water and quickly stir to combine, then add the yeast dissolved in room temperature water. Add 1 tbsp cooking oil and knead into a dough with no dry flour. Cover and let rest for 20 minutes. Remove and knead until smooth. Cover and let rest again for 1 hour to allow the gluten to fully relax.
2. Wash the chives and thoroughly drain the water, then chop. Beat the eggs with 1/4 tsp salt. Cook the vermicelli in boiling water for about 1 minute until just soft. Quickly remove and rinse under cold water. Drain and chop.
3. Heat oil in a wok over low heat. While pouring in the egg mixture, stir quickly with chopsticks to scramble into fine, small pieces. Remove from heat as soon as the eggs are fully set to avoid overcooking.
4. Place the chives, scrambled eggs, and vermicelli in a large bowl. First, add 2 tbsp sesame oil and mix well to coat the chives and lock in moisture. Then add oyster sauce, ginger paste, white pepper powder, and five-spice powder and mix. Finally, add 1/4 to 1/2 tsp salt according to taste and mix again.
5. Knead the rested dough into a log and divide into 6 equal portions. Take one portion, dust with dry flour to prevent sticking, and roll out into an oval wrapper that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges.
6. Spread the filling over half of the wrapper and use a spoon to flatten it. Fold the wrapper in half. Lightly press with your hand to release air, then press the edges firmly with your fingertips to seal. Starting from one end, fold the edge, using your thumb to push the front edge backward and press it against the back edge to form a continuous wave-like pleat facing one side, until the entire edge is sealed.
7. Add a small amount of oil to the flat-bottomed frying pan and coat the surface. Heat over low heat. Once the oil is hot, place the chive boxes in the pan. Cook slowly over low heat. Once one side is set and golden brown, flip. After both sides are colored, cover the pan with a lid and cook for a moment to ensure the edges and interior are fully cooked. Cook until both sides are golden brown and the wrappers puff up, then remove from the pan. Place on a plate lined with kitchen paper to absorb excess oil. Stacking them helps the steam keep the wrappers soft.

## Health Advice
Chives are rich in dietary fiber, and eggs provide high-quality protein. This combination offers balanced nutrition, making it suitable for those needing energy while managing weight. When preparing the filling, first use sesame oil to coat the chives and lock in moisture, preventing the filling from becoming too wet. When frying, use low heat to ensure the interior is fully cooked.
