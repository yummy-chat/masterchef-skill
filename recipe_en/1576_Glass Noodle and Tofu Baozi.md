# Glass Noodle and Tofu Baozi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed
🍞 Dry yeast, as needed
💧 Warm water, as needed
🧈 Firm tofu (northern tofu), as needed, cut into small pieces
🍠 Sweet potato glass noodles or mung bean glass noodles, as needed, softened in hot water in advance and chopped
🧅 Scallions, as needed, chopped
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste), as needed
🌶️ Chili flakes, as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🍂 Thirteen-spice powder, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt, 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍳 Iron wok or skillet
🥖 Rolling pin
🥢 Chopsticks or spoon
🔪 Cutting board
♨️ Steamer (with steamer basket and lid)
🔍 Glass lid for viewing

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the dough**: Mix the flour and dry yeast evenly. Gradually add warm water and stir into flocculent pieces. Knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest to ferment until doubled in volume.
   **Note**: Warm water helps activate the yeast.

2. **Prepare the sauce**: In a small bowl, combine and mix well the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen-spice powder, and salt. Set aside.

3. **Cook the filling**: Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the tofu pieces and stir-fry until the surface turns slightly golden. Add the doubanjiang and chili flakes, and stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant. Add the softened and chopped glass noodles, stir-fry evenly, then pour in the prepared sauce and continue stir-frying. Finally, sprinkle in a generous amount of chopped scallions and mix well. Turn off the heat and let the filling cool completely before using.
   **Note**: The filling must be completely cooled before wrapping to avoid burning the dough. Adding scallions at the end helps preserve their fragrance.

4. **Make the wrappers**: Place the fermented dough on a floured cutting board. Knead it repeatedly to release air. Roll into a long log and divide into equal-sized pieces. Roll each piece into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.
   **Note**: The wrapper should not be rolled too thin.

5. **Assemble**: Place an appropriate amount of the cooled filling in the center of a wrapper. Pleat and pinch the edges to seal tightly.

6. **Second proofing and steaming**: Arrange the wrapped baozi one by one in the steamer basket, leaving enough space between them. Cover with the lid and let them proof for about 10 minutes. Bring a pot of water to a full boil, then place the steamer on top and steam for 15 minutes over high heat. Turn off the heat and let them rest for 5 minutes before opening the lid to remove.
   **Note**: This second proofing makes the baozi texture softer. Resting after steaming prevents them from collapsing.

## Health Advice
As a vegetarian filling staple, glass noodle and tofu baozi provide calcium from tofu and carbohydrates from the noodles, making them suitable for those seeking a light diet. Be sure to let the filling cool completely before wrapping to avoid damaging the dough. These baozi are moderate in calories, but portion control is still recommended.
