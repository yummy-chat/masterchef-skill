# Water-Boiled Sliced Pork (Shuizhu Roupian)
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork loin or tenderloin 450g (remove sinew, slice thinly)  
🥬 Chinese cabbage leaves, as needed (tear by hand)  
🥒 Lettuce, as needed (slice thinly)  
🧄 Garlic, 1 whole head (mince, divide into three portions)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (partially crush for scallion-ginger water, partially mince)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (partially flatten for scallion-ginger water, partially slice)  
🌶️ Dried chilies (lantern chili, Erjingtiao, Qin pepper for fragrance, Qiubei chili for heat), as needed  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns (recommend Dahongpao or Gongjiao), as needed  
🥚 Egg white, 1  
🌾 Potato starch or sweet potato starch, 30g (soak in cold water, use the settled wet starch)  
🧂 Edible alkaline water or baking soda, about 2g per 500g meat  
🧂 White pepper powder, a small amount  
🍬 White sugar, a small amount  
🧂 Salt (about 2g for marinating, 2g for stir-frying vegetables, adjust in the broth as needed)  
🫒 Ripe pressed rapeseed oil, sufficient amount  
🫘 Pixian broad bean paste, sufficient amount  
🥫 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🫗 Toasted white sesame seeds, as needed  
💧 Scallion-ginger water (scallion sections and ginger slices, flattened, add water and 2-3g salt, squeeze, then strain), about 40-50g per 500g meat  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
♨️ Air fryer  
🔪 Grinder or food processor  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🌡️ Thermometer (optional)  

## Cooking Steps
1. **Soak the starch**: Add 30g starch to cold water, let it settle without stirring. Once the starch sinks to the bottom, pour off the clear water on top and reserve the wet starch.
*Key point: Let the starch settle naturally; use only the settled portion.*

2. **Make scallion-ginger water**: Flatten scallion sections and ginger slices, add water and 2-3g salt, squeeze by hand until the water becomes cloudy, then strain and set aside.
*Key point: The salt uses osmotic pressure to quickly extract the flavor from scallion and ginger.*

3. **Slice the meat**: Remove sinew from the pork and slice against the grain into thin pieces about 1-2mm thick, ensuring uniform thickness.
*Note: Loin meat must have sinew removed; slices should be about the thickness of a coin.*

4. **Marinate and coat (velveting)**:
   - Add edible alkaline water or baking soda, white pepper, sugar, and salt to the meat slices, mix well;
   - Add the scallion-ginger water in 2-3 additions, mixing until the water is fully absorbed by the meat each time;
   - Add one egg white and mix well;
   - Add the wet starch and mix until each slice is evenly coated, and the mixture drips slowly when lifted.
*Key point: The order of velveting is seasoning, adding water, egg white, then starch; coat thoroughly to prevent the coating from coming off.*

5. **Make knife-cut chili**:
   - Sichuan peppercorns: Place in air fryer, spray with a little oil, roast at 150°C for 10 minutes, then grind into a coarse powder;
   - Dried chilies: Place in air fryer, spray with a little oil, roast at 140°C for 6 minutes, let cool, then grind into a coarse powder along with the seeds.
*Note: Roast Sichuan peppercorns and dried chilies separately to prevent burning; chilies need to be roasted until crispy.*

6. **Prepare vegetables and aromatics**:
   - Slice lettuce thinly, tear Chinese cabbage leaves into pieces by hand;
   - Mince garlic and divide into three portions: one to mix with minced ginger, one to mix with sliced scallions, and one kept separate.

7. **Stir-fry the vegetables**: Add a little rapeseed oil to the wok, sauté one portion of minced garlic until fragrant, add the cabbage and lettuce, add 2g salt, stir-fry over medium-high heat until the vegetables soften and release water, then transfer to the bottom of a serving bowl.
*Key point: Quick stir-fry to remove raw taste; do not overcook.*

8. **Make the sauce base**:
   - In a clean wok, add a generous amount of rapeseed oil, add a sufficient amount of Pixian broad bean paste, stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red and the aroma is strong;
   - Add minced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant, then add another portion of minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant;
   - Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, immediately turn to the lowest heat, and stir-fry until the moisture from the soy sauce evaporates, deepening the sauce aroma;
   - Add 1 spoonful of ground Sichuan peppercorns and 2 spoonfuls of ground chilies, stir-fry to release the compound spicy and numbing aroma.
*Note: Use enough oil; stir-fry the bean paste patiently until the oil separates; after adding soy sauce, stir-fry over the lowest heat to dry out the moisture.*

9. **Cook the meat slices**:
   - Add enough water to the sauce base, bring to a boil over high heat, taste and adjust salt;
   - Turn to the lowest heat, keep the soup surface just before a full boil, slide the meat slices one by one, scattered, into the wok;
   - Let sit for about 20 seconds after adding, until the outer layer sets, then gently push with the back of a spatula to prevent sticking;
   - Turn to medium-high heat, once the soup comes back to a boil, cook for 5-10 seconds, then immediately turn off the heat.
*Key point: Add meat slices over the lowest heat to prevent the coating from coming off; cooking time is very short to ensure tenderness.*

10. **Assemble and pour hot oil**:
    - Pour the cooked meat slices together with the soup into the bowl with the vegetables;
    - Sprinkle the surface in order with ground Sichuan peppercorns, chili flakes, one portion of minced garlic, one portion of sliced scallions, and optionally toasted sesame seeds;
    - In a separate pot, heat oil to about 220°C, then quickly and evenly pour it over the aromatics on the surface, creating a loud sizzle and releasing a complex aroma.
*Key point: The oil temperature must be high, and the amount of oil should be generous; pouring hot oil is the finishing touch.*

## Health Advice
This water-boiled sliced pork, after velveting and a short cooking time, results in tender meat with controllable fat content. The addition of cabbage and lettuce increases dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing a high-protein, low-fat diet. However, the broad bean paste and oil content are relatively high; for those with high blood pressure or during a weight-loss phase, it is recommended to drink less of the soup base and pair it with a light, cold dish.
