# Easy Skewers
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lean beef, about 15 yuan worth (sliced and marinated)
🥒 Cucumber, 🥦 Cauliflower, 🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, and other vegetables, to taste (choose according to preference)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (used for marinating and seasoning)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🌶️ Doubanjiang (broad bean paste), 1 large spoonful
🍲 Spicy hotpot base, half a packet
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste
⚪ White sesame seeds, to taste
🫗 Rapeseed oil, enough to cover the bottom of the pan

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Marinating bowl
🍳 Wok
♨️ Rice cooker inner pot
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Ladle
🍢 Bamboo skewers

## Steps
1. Slice the beef into thin pieces. Add light soy sauce and cooking wine, mix well, and marinate for 30 minutes.  
**Notes**: Mix thoroughly so the beef absorbs the seasonings. Marinating effectively removes gamey odors and enhances flavor.

2. Cut the selected vegetables into bite-sized pieces and skewer them one by one with bamboo skewers; set aside.  
**Notes**: Keep the vegetable pieces uniform in size to ensure even cooking.

3. In a cold wok, add rapeseed oil until the bottom is covered. Heat until hot, then reduce to low heat. Add one large spoonful of doubanjiang and stir-fry slowly over low heat until red oil emerges.  
**Key point**: Stir-fry slowly over low heat to prevent the paste from burning.

4. Add half a packet of the spicy hotpot base. Continue stir-frying over low heat until fully melted and fine oil bubbles appear on the surface.  
**Key point**: Maintain low heat to avoid scorching the base.

5. Add a pinch of sugar to enhance flavor. Stir to combine, then add enough boiling water. Bring to a boil over high heat for 3 minutes to allow the soup base flavors to fully meld.  
**Key point**: Boil for sufficient time to allow the soup base flavors to blend.

6. Pour the cooked soup base into the rice cooker inner pot. Add the appropriate amount of sesame oil and white sesame seeds, and set aside.  
**Notes**: Adjust the amount of sesame oil and white sesame seeds according to personal preference.

7. In a separate cooking pot, add plenty of water and bring to a boil over high heat. Blanch the skewered ingredients until fully cooked.  
**Notes**: Using ample water ensures even heating; ingredients must be fully cooked.

8. Immerse the blanched ingredients into the prepared red soup base. Add an appropriate amount of light soy sauce to season. Let it rest for 30 minutes to allow full absorption of flavor.  
**Notes**: Rest for 30 minutes to let the ingredients fully absorb the soup base flavor.

## Health Tips
Beef paired with vegetables provides both protein and dietary fiber. However, the spicy hotpot base and doubanjiang are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume less of the soup base.
