# Beef Dumplings  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

🔪 Food processor

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the cooked beef chunks into a food processor, add a small amount of water, and blend into a fine meat paste. Transfer the meat paste to a bowl, add the chopped scallions, and use chopsticks or a spoon to stir in one direction until evenly combined, forming the beef filling.  
   **Note**: Adding water helps the food processor run smoothly; stirring in one direction makes the filling more cohesive.  
2. Take one dumpling wrapper and place an适量 amount of beef filling in the center. Dip a finger in a small amount of water and moisten the edge of the wrapper all around. Fold it in half and pinch the edges firmly to seal, ensuring the filling does not leak out.  
   **Key Point**: Moistening the edge with water enhances adhesion and prevents the dumplings from opening during cooking.  
3. Add enough water to a stockpot and bring it to a boil over high heat. Once the water is boiling, gently drop in the dumplings. Use the back of a slotted spoon to gently push along the edge of the pot to prevent sticking to the bottom or to each other.  
4. Keep the heat at medium-high and cook until all the dumplings float to the surface, the skins are puffed up, and the sides spring back quickly when pressed. Then turn off the heat. Use a slotted spoon to remove the dumplings and plate them.  
   **Judgment Standard**: The dumplings floating is the basic signal; puffiness and springing back indicate they are fully cooked inside.  

## Health Advice  
Beef dumplings are rich in high-quality protein and iron, making them suitable for people who need to replenish blood or build muscle. However, store-bought dumpling wrappers are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should use less soy sauce in their dipping sauce. It is recommended to pair with vinegar and minced garlic, which enhances the flavor and helps reduce oil intake.
