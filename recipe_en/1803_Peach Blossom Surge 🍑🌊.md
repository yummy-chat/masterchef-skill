# Peach Blossom Surge 🍑🌊
**Cuisine:** Beijing Cuisine  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Rice – appropriate amount (freshly steamed, used while hot)  
🍤 Shrimp – appropriate amount (cleaned and patted dry)  
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (sliced into shapes similar to the shrimp)  
🫛 Green peas – appropriate amount  
🥚 Egg white – appropriate amount (for coating)  
🌾 Cornstarch – appropriate amount (for making egg white batter and thickening)  
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (finely minced)  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (chopped)  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount (for sliding shrimp, frying rice crust, and stir-frying sauce)  
🥫 Ketchup – appropriate amount (needs to be fried in oil to release red oil and aroma)  
🍶 Shaoxing wine – a small amount (to remove fishy smell; too much will affect freshness)  
🍬 White sugar – appropriate amount (key to sweet and sour flavor, use generously)  
🌶️ White pepper – a small amount  
🍾 White vinegar – appropriate amount (added before removing from heat to maintain acidity)  
💧 Water starch – appropriate amount (for thickening)

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker (for steaming rice)  
🍳 Non-stick pan or baking sheet (for pressing rice to make rice crust base)  
🫓 Electric griddle (Method 1: for baking rice crust)  
🔥 Oven (Method 2: for baking rice crust)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying sauce, sliding shrimp, and frying rice crust)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing shrimp and rice crust)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for making batter and marinating shrimp)

## Instructions

### Preparing the Rice Crust
1. After the rice is steamed, take it out while hot and place it on a non-stick pan or baking sheet. Dip your fingers in water and press the rice evenly and firmly into a thin cake.
**Note:** The rice must not be too cold, otherwise it won't stick together; press firmly to ensure the rice crust has a tight structure.

2. Dehydrate the pressed rice cake until dry and crispy:
   - **Electric Griddle Method:** Place it in the electric griddle, cover and bake. Open the lid repeatedly during the process to release steam, until the moisture evaporates and the crust becomes crispy.
   - **Oven Method:** Place it in a preheated oven at 230°C and bake for about 30 minutes. Turn off the heat, open the oven door to release steam, then close the oven door and let it steam for about ten minutes. Repeat this process twice until the crust is crispy.
**State Check:** The rice crust is ready when it is golden brown on the surface, hard and dry, and breaks apart easily when snapped.

### Preparing the Ingredients
3. Clean the shrimp and pat them completely dry with a paper towel.
**Note:** The shrimp must be thoroughly dried, otherwise it will affect the coating and texture.

4. In a bowl, prepare the egg white and cornstarch batter and mix well. Add the dried shrimp and toss until they are evenly coated with a thin layer of batter.
**Note:** Prepare the batter first, then mix in the shrimp to avoid breaking the shrimp during repeated stirring. After mixing evenly, you can seal it with a little oil to prevent drying out.

5. Slice the carrot into shapes similar to the shrimp. Mince the ginger, slice the garlic, and chop the scallion.

### Cooking Process
6. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the green peas and carrot slices and blanch for about 1 minute until tender. Drain and set aside.
**Note:** Blanching removes the raw taste of the carrots and makes the green peas more vibrant.

7. Heat oil in a wok. When the oil reaches about 50% heat (150°C/300°F), add the coated shrimp. Gently stir them apart with a spatula and stir-fry until they change color and are just set. Immediately remove and drain the oil.
**Note:** Home stoves have lower heat, so the oil temperature should be slightly higher to allow the shrimp to set quickly, locking in moisture and keeping them tender.

8. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the minced ginger, garlic, and scallion and sauté until fragrant. Add the ketchup and stir-fry over low heat until the red oil is released and the aroma develops. Pour in a bowl of water along the edge of the wok and bring to a boil.
**Note:** The ketchup must be stir-fried sufficiently to release its color and flavor.

9. Add the blanched green peas and carrot slices. Season with a small amount of Shaoxing wine, a generous amount of white sugar, an appropriate amount of salt, and a small amount of white pepper. Mix well.

10. Once the sauce is seasoned, add the water starch to thicken it. Finally, add the white vinegar, stir to combine, and turn off the heat.
**Note:** Use enough sugar to create a prominent sweet and sour flavor. Add the white vinegar last to preserve its tanginess. The sauce should be ample in quantity, as this is a braised dish.

### Plating the Dish
11. In a separate wok, heat a generous amount of oil. When the oil temperature reaches above 60% heat (180°C/350°F), break the dried rice crust into pieces and fry them in the oil until they puff up and turn golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain the oil, then arrange them on a plate.
**Note:** The oil must be hot enough for the rice crust to fully puff up and become crispy.

12. Quickly pour the pan-fried shrimp and the hot, thick sauce over the freshly fried rice crust. The sizzling sound (刺啦) will indicate success. Serve immediately.
**Note:** The sauce must be poured promptly over the hot crust to achieve the sizzling effect and the desired contrast of crispy exterior and tender interior.

## Health Advice
Shrimp are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. The rice crust, being deep-fried, is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair this dish with light vegetables to balance the meal. It is suitable for individuals needing to build muscle or replenish energy quickly. Be mindful of oil intake, and individuals with diabetes should reduce the amount of sugar used.
