# Spicy Beef Pancake
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef 250g (half a jin)
🫓 Flour 500g (one jin)
💧 Water 260g
🧅 Scallion, as needed (finely chopped, added as you go)
🧄 Minced ginger 10g
🧂 Salt (filling) 4g
🧂 Salt (dough) 3g
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed (to taste)
🧂 Ground pepper 2g
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder 2g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorn water, as needed (add gradually to the beef filling)
🫒 Sichuan peppercorn oil, as needed (to enhance richness and numbing aroma)
🥮 Oil pastry, as needed (for brushing the dough for flakiness)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for brushing the dough strips and frying the pancakes)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🫗 Strainer
🔪 Cutting board
🥢 Rolling pin
🍳 Electric griddle
🥄 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1.  Prepare the spicy beef filling: Add minced ginger, 4g salt, 2g ground pepper, 2g thirteen-spice powder, and chili powder to the ground beef. Gradually add Sichuan peppercorn water in batches, stirring until the meat absorbs the liquid and becomes tender. Then stir in an appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorn oil to enhance richness and numbing aroma.
**Key**: Use Sichuan peppercorn water and oil instead of ground Sichuan peppercorn to avoid bitterness and ensure the meat stays tender and moist.

2.  Prepare the scallions: Finely chop the scallions and set aside separately.
**Note**: Do not mix the scallions into the filling in advance, as they will release water and develop unwanted odors if left to sit.

3.  Knead and rest the dough: Mix 3g salt and 1g baking soda into the flour. Gradually add 260g water while stirring, then knead into a smooth dough. Divide the dough into pieces of about 100g each. Roll each piece into a strip about 20cm long. Arrange the strips neatly on a cutting board, brush the surface with oil, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and let rest for about 40 minutes to allow the gluten to relax fully.
**Key**: Adding a small amount of baking soda makes the dough crispier and smoother.

4.  Shape and fill: Take a rested dough strip and roll it to taper from one end to the other, then flatten it and roll it into a large, thin rectangular sheet. Evenly spread a layer of oil pastry over the dough sheet. Place a generous amount of beef filling and scallions on the wider end of the sheet. Fold the two sides inward to enclose the filling.
**Key**: Roll the dough sheet thin and even, and press the filling firmly when wrapping to prevent leakage.

5.  Roll and shape: Hold both ends of the wrapped portion, stretch the dough outward while rolling it forward. Stretch and roll simultaneously, keeping the roll tight. When you reach the end, tuck the edge under the rolled piece and press it firmly onto the bun. Stand the cylindrical bun upright, then press inward with your palm to shape it into a round patty.
**Key**: Stretching while rolling creates more layers and results in a crispier bun.

6.  Fry: Pour oil into the electric griddle, enough to reach about one-third the thickness of the bun. Place the bun in the griddle, rotate it to ensure it sticks flat to the bottom, then close the lid. Set the lower temperature to 210°C and the upper temperature to 200°C. After about 5 minutes, when the bun puffs up slightly, flip it and continue frying. Fry until both sides are golden brown, then remove.
**Key**: Use sufficient oil and flip the bun carefully to ensure even heating.

## Health Advice
Using Sichuan peppercorn water instead of ground Sichuan peppercorn in the beef filling helps lock in moisture and avoids bitterness. It is recommended to serve with a side of cold mixed vegetables to balance the spiciness. This dish is suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors but have a sensitive stomach, in moderation.
