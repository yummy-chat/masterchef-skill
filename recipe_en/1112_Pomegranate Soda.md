# Pomegranate Soda
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫗 Red pomegranate – to taste (use seeded flesh, keep the seeds intact)
🧊 Ice cubes – to taste
🥤 Canned sparkling water – to taste
🧪 Seasonings – none

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥃 Clear glass
👝 Ziplock bag
🥄 Spoon
🥤 Straw

## Steps
1. Remove an appropriate amount of pomegranate seeds and place them into a clean ziplock bag.
   **Note**: Keep the pomegranate seeds intact to avoid premature breakage and juice loss.
2. Evenly press the ziplock bag with the palm of your hand to squeeze the pomegranate seeds, extracting some juice while preserving the seed shape.
   **Note**: Press with moderate force to prevent the bag from bursting; do not fully mash, retain the granular texture of the seeds.
3. Add an appropriate amount of ice cubes to a clear glass, filling it to about 1/3 to 1/2 of its capacity to maintain a refreshing coldness.
4. Open the canned sparkling water and slowly pour it along the inner wall of the glass over the ice cubes, filling to about 80% of the remaining cup volume.
   **Note**: Pour slowly to reduce carbonation loss and preserve the drink’s fizz.
5. Pour the pressed pomegranate seeds and their juice from the ziplock bag into the sparkling water.
   **Note**: Add both the juice and seeds to enhance the fruit aroma and create a visual accent.
6. Insert a straw and gently stir the drink in the glass to thoroughly mix the juice and sparkling water.
   **Note**: Stir gently to avoid excessive agitation that may deflate the bubbles and affect texture.

## Health Tips
Pomegranates are rich in antioxidants, making them ideal for autumn to combat dryness; no added sugar, relying on the fruit’s natural sweetness for a low-calorie, healthy option. The pomegranate seeds can be chewed, but those with weak digestive systems are advised to strain the drink before consuming.
