# Chiffon Cake  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 3 egg yolks (room temperature)  
🥚 3 egg whites (can be refrigerated in advance)  
🌽 30g corn oil  
🥛 24g milk  
🌾 35g cake flour (sifted in advance)  
🍬 40g granulated sugar  
🍋 A few drops of lemon juice  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚡ Electric hand mixer  
🥢 Hand whisk  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🪣 Sieve  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🍰 6-inch round chiffon cake pan  
🔥 Oven  
🍽️ Cooling rack or colander (for upside-down cooling)  
🥣 2 stainless steel bowls  
🔪 Small knife  

## Instructions  
1. Separate the egg yolks from the egg whites. Place the yolks in a bowl and the whites in another clean, oil-free, and water-free bowl.  

2. **Make the egg yolk batter**: Add corn oil and milk to the room-temperature egg yolks. Use a hand whisk to stir thoroughly until emulsified. Sift in the cake flour and mix in a "Z" shape or irregular motion until the batter forms lines that do not disappear quickly.  
   **Key point**: Sift the cake flour in advance; avoid over-mixing to prevent gluten development; adjust the amount of flour based on the batter's consistency.  

3. **Whip the meringue**: Add a few drops of lemon juice to the egg whites. Use an electric hand mixer on high speed to whip until large bubbles form. Add the granulated sugar and continue whipping until stiff peaks form—when the whisk is lifted, the peaks stand upright and firm.  
   **Key point**: Monitor the whipping process closely; at stiff peaks, the meringue should be tight and resistant; lemon juice helps deodorize and stabilize the egg whites.  

4. **Combine the batters**: Take about 1/3 of the meringue and add it to the egg yolk batter. Use a spatula to quickly fold until just combined. Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue. Use the spatula to fold from the bottom, turning the bowl, until the batter is completely uniform and glossy. Stop immediately.  
   **Key point**: Starting with a small amount of meringue balances the density and prevents deflating; folding must reach the bottom, not just stir the surface.  

5. **Pour and bake**: Pour the cake batter into the 6-inch chiffon pan from a height, smooth the surface, and gently tap the pan to release large air bubbles. Place in the preheated oven on the lower-middle rack. Bake at 160°C top heat and 140°C bottom heat for about 50 minutes.  
   **Optional step**: About 10 minutes into baking, before the surface cracks, quickly use a knife to score a cross shape to guide the crack pattern, then immediately close the oven door. Perform this step quickly to avoid temperature drop that could cause shrinking.  

6. **Remove and cool**: Once baked, immediately remove the cake from the oven, tap the pan once, and quickly invert it onto a cooling rack or colander. Let it cool completely in this upside-down position.  
   **Key point**: Cool upside down to prevent the cake from shrinking or collapsing; only remove from the pan after it is completely cool.  

## Health Tips  
Chiffon cake is a low-oil, low-fat baking option, suitable for those who want to control calories while enjoying a dessert. Ensure the egg whites are whipped to the right consistency and avoid deflating during folding to maintain a fluffy texture.
