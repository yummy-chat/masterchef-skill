# Braised Potatoes, Green Beans, and Eggplant
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant, as needed (cut into rolling knife strips)
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (cut into finger-thick long strips)
🫘 Green beans (yardlong beans or snap beans), as needed (cut into 4-5 cm sections)
🧄 Garlic, 1 head (minced, divided into two portions)
🌿 Cornstarch, as needed (for coating eggplant + 1 tbsp for sauce)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for frying and as base oil)
🥄 Light soy sauce, 3 tbsp
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 tsp
🍬 Sugar, 1 tbsp
💧 Water, half a bowl

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl (for mixing sauce)
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the eggplant into rolling knife strips, place in a bowl, add enough cornstarch, and toss evenly with your hands so each piece is thinly coated with cornstarch.
**Key point**: Coating with starch helps reduce oil absorption during frying.
2. Peel the potatoes and cut them into finger-thick long strips; remove the strings from the green beans and cut into about 4-5 cm sections; peel the garlic and mince, then divide into two portions for later use.
**Key point**: None.
3. In a bowl, combine 3 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1 tsp chicken bouillon, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tbsp cornstarch, and half a bowl of water. Stir well to form the sauce and set aside.
**Key point**: None.
4. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat over high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the starch-coated eggplant. Fry over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the eggplant is set and the color deepens. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Frying quickly over high heat reduces oil absorption and maintains texture.
5. Reduce to medium heat, add the potato strips, and fry over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until the surface is golden and the inside is cooked through. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Potatoes must be fried thoroughly; otherwise, the texture will be hard and raw.
6. Continue over medium heat, add the green bean sections, and fry for about 5 minutes, until the skin wrinkles and the color deepens. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key point**: Green beans must be fully fried to remove rawness and harmful substances.
7. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, reduce to medium heat, add most of the minced garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant and slightly yellow.
**Key point**: None.
8. Return the fried potatoes, green beans, and eggplant to the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry quickly to combine, allowing the sauce to thicken and evenly coat the ingredients.
**Key point**: Stir-fry quickly after adding the sauce to prevent burning.
9. Add the remaining minced garlic, stir-fry a few more times, and then remove from the wok.
**Key point**: Adding garlic before serving enhances the layered garlic aroma of the dish.

## Health Tips
Although this dish is mainly composed of vegetables, it is high in calories due to frying. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or cold dish and to control the intake of staple foods. Ensure green beans are thoroughly fried for safety. Coating eggplant with starch and frying quickly effectively reduces oil absorption.
