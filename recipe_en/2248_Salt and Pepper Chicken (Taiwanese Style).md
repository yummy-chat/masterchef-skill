# Salt and Pepper Chicken (Taiwanese Style)
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧄 360g skin-on chicken thigh (or substitute with chicken breast, requiring thorough marination)
🌿 A suitable amount of Thai basil (wash and thoroughly dry the surface moisture)
🥔 A suitable amount of coarse sweet potato starch
🫘 2 pieces of fermented tofu
🥢 10g light soy sauce
🌿 <1g five-spice powder
🥚 1 egg white
🧂 1g salt (for pepper salt)
🌶️ 1g white pepper powder (for pepper salt)

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. **Prepare the chicken**: Cut the skin-on chicken thigh into strips, then into small pieces.
2. **Prepare the marinade**: Place fermented tofu and light soy sauce in a bowl, mash the tofu and mix well.
3. **Marinate**: Pour the marinade over the chicken pieces, add five-spice powder, mix thoroughly, cover, and marinate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
4. **Coat with egg white**: Add egg white to the marinated chicken, stir until the chicken surface becomes sticky.
5. **Prepare the coating**: Pour coarse sweet potato starch into a large basin.
6. **Coat the chicken**: Add chicken pieces in batches into the starch, quickly toss with chopsticks to coat each piece evenly. Lift and shake off excess dry starch; lightly press on any uncoated spots to add more starch.
7. **Prepare Thai basil**: Pluck the leaves; if washing, thoroughly dry all surface moisture.
8. **First fry**: Pour oil into the wok. Add Thai basil to the cold oil, heat over medium-low heat. When the oil starts to crackle, remove the basil with a slotted spoon. Add chicken pieces one by one, fry over medium-low heat for about 1–2 minutes until the surface sets but does not color, then remove.
**Key point**: Use the Thai basil to test the oil temperature; add chicken pieces one by one to prevent sticking.
9. **Second fry**: Heat the oil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Return all the chicken pieces to the wok at once, fry briefly until the surface quickly turns golden brown and the skin is crispy.
**Key point**: High-temperature second frying is the key to a crispy exterior.
10. **Mix and season**: When the chicken is almost done, add the previously fried Thai basil to the wok, fry together for a few seconds, then remove everything and drain the oil. Mix salt and white pepper powder to make pepper salt, sprinkle over the salt and pepper chicken, and toss gently to combine.

## Health Advice
This salt and pepper chicken is rich in protein, but due to double frying, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a green salad or light side dishes to balance the greasiness. Suitable for young people gathering for a treat; those with high blood sugar, blood pressure, or cholesterol should consume in moderation.
