# Stir-Fried Green Beans with Shredded Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Green beans, to taste (sliced diagonally into thin strips)  
🥩 Pork, to taste (cut into thin strips)  
🧄 Garlic, 2 cloves (minced, added before serving)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers, to taste (cut into segments or rings)  
🌿 Star anise, 1 piece (added when stir-frying the pork)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the green beans and slice them diagonally into thin strips; cut the pork into thin strips; mince the garlic; cut the small chili peppers into segments. Set aside.
2. Heat the wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil. Once the oil is hot, add the shredded pork and star anise. Stir-fry over high heat until the surface of the pork turns slightly golden.
3. Add the small chili pepper segments and stir-fry quickly until fragrant.
4. Add the green bean strips and stir-fry them directly with the pork, mixing well.
   **Note**: Do not blanch the green beans to maintain a crisp texture.
5. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, MSG, and oyster sauce in sequence, stirring well to combine.
6. Continue stir-frying until the moisture from the green beans evaporates, and the beans become soft, deepen in color, and are fully cooked.
7. Before serving, sprinkle in the minced garlic and stir-fry quickly to combine, then plate.
   **Key**: The garlic must be added at the very end to preserve the raw garlic aroma.

## Health Advice
Green beans must be thoroughly cooked to avoid food poisoning. This dish is suitable for those who need a high-protein, low-fat diet; control the amount of oil and salt used during cooking.
