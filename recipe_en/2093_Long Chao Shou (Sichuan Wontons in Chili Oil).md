# Long Chao Shou (Sichuan Wontons in Chili Oil)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Shrimp, as needed (main filling ingredient)
🐖 Ground pork, as needed (main filling ingredient)
🫚 Ginger, 1 piece (to be ground with shrimp)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for filling and garnish)
🥟 Wonton wrappers, as needed
🫒 Rapeseed oil, as needed (for making chili oil, must use rapeseed oil)
🫘 Chili bean paste (Doubanjiang), 1 tablespoon
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed
🍶 Cooking wine, a little
🧂 Salt, as needed (for filling and bowl base separately)
🧂 Chicken powder (or chicken bouillon), as needed (for filling and bowl base separately)
🧂 White pepper powder, a little (for filling and bowl base separately)
🍾 Light soy sauce, as needed (for filling and bowl base separately)
🥚 Egg white, a little
🌾 Cornstarch, a little
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced, for bowl base)
🫙 Prepared chili oil, 2 tablespoons (core seasoning for bowl base)
🍶 Vinegar, 1 tablespoon (for bowl base)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🥘 Small milk pot (for making chili oil)
🌀 Food processor (meat grinder, for making shrimp filling)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for scooping wontons)
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing filling, preparing bowl base, and serving)
🥄 Soup spoon (for scooping seasonings, stirring, and ladling soup)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare Chili Oil**
   Pour rapeseed oil into a small milk pot and heat to 60% hot. Add 1 tablespoon of chili bean paste, reduce heat to low, and slowly simmer until the oil turns red. Then add dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, bay leaves, and other spices. Continue to simmer on low heat until the oil is bright red and fragrant, then turn off the heat. Pour the chili oil along with the spices into a bowl and set aside.
   **Note**: Use low heat throughout to prevent the chili bean paste and spices from burning. Rapeseed oil is essential.

2. **Make Shrimp Filling**
   Place shrimp, ginger, and scallion segments into a food processor and blend into a paste with a slightly coarse texture. Transfer the paste to a bowl. Add cooking wine, salt, chicken powder, white pepper, and light soy sauce. Gradually add small amounts of ginger-scallion water, stirring vigorously in one direction until the mixture becomes cohesive. Then add a little egg white and cornstarch, mix well, and knead and slap the mixture a few times to increase elasticity.
   **Note**: Retaining some texture improves mouthfeel; ensure the filling absorbs moisture and becomes springy during stirring.

3. **Wrap the Wontons**
   Take one wonton wrapper and place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Dip your finger in water and moisten the edges of the wrapper. Fold it in half to form a triangle, pressing the edges firmly to seal. Then, bring the two bottom corners of the triangle together, bend them inward, and pinch them tightly to create the classic wonton shape.
   **Note**: The edges must be sealed tightly to prevent breakage during cooking.

4. **Prepare the Bowl Base**
   In a serving bowl, add minced garlic, 2 tablespoons of prepared chili oil, 1 teaspoon of chicken powder, a pinch of salt, 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of vinegar, and a little white pepper. Stir well to combine and set aside.
   **Note**: Adjust saltiness and sourness to taste.

5. **Cook the Wontons**
   Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil, then reduce to medium-low heat. Gently drop the wontons in one by one, using a spoon to gently stir and prevent sticking. Use the "add three cups of cold water" method: after the water returns to a boil, add a small amount of cold water, repeating three times. Cook until all wontons float to the surface and the wrappers become translucent and tender.
   **Note**: Avoid boiling vigorously at high heat the entire time, as it may cause the wrappers to break.

6. **Assemble and Serve**
   Use a slotted spoon to scoop out the cooked wontons, drain excess water, and place them into the prepared bowl with the seasoning base. Ladle a spoonful of hot wonton cooking water into the bowl to allow the seasonings and chili oil to meld. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve immediately.
   **Note**: Pouring hot soup helps release the aroma of the chili oil and seasonings.

## Health Suggestions
The wonton filling is primarily shrimp and pork, providing good protein. However, the chili oil and chili bean paste are relatively high in calories, so it is advisable to control portion sizes. This dish is suitable for office workers looking for a warming and satisfying meal. Those with sensitive stomachs should reduce the amount of chili oil.
