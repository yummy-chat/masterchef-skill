# Loofah and Fried Egg Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 1 loofah (peeled, cut into rolling chunks)  
🥚 4 eggs  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (to coat the loofah thinly)  
🧂 A pinch of salt (for marinating the loofah)  
🧈 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧂 A pinch of chicken bouillon  
🌶️ A pinch of ground white pepper (about three shakes)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning)  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Vegetable peeler  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Soup ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel the loofah and cut into rolling chunks. Place in a bowl, add a little salt, and mix well to marinate until water releases. Squeeze out excess water, then evenly coat with a thin layer of cornstarch.  
   **Note**: Marinating and squeezing out water prevents the loofah from releasing water during stir-frying; coating with cornstarch helps maintain its shape and texture.  
2. Heat oil in the wok over high heat. Crack in 4 whole eggs and fry until set. Use a spatula to break the yolks, fry both sides until golden brown, then remove to a cutting board and cut into small pieces. Set aside.  
   **Key**: Do **not wash the wok** after frying the eggs; retain the residual oil and wok essence (wok hei).  
3. Using the residual oil in the wok, add the cornstarch-coated loofah pieces. Stir-fry over medium heat until the edges are lightly browned.  
   **Note**: Do not overcook; stop once the edges are browned.  
4. Add enough **boiling water** to the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat and continue boiling for 1 minute to quickly turn the soup milky white.  
   **Key**: Must use boiling water — this is essential for achieving a milky white broth.  
5. Return the fried egg pieces to the wok. Add a pinch of chicken bouillon, a little white pepper, salt to taste, and a pinch of sugar to enhance freshness. Stir to combine.  
6. Keep the heat high. Adjust seasoning as needed, then turn off the heat and serve.  
   **Finished dish characteristics**: Soup is milky white, loofah is vibrant green and tender, eggs are soaked with flavorful broth.  

## Health Tips  
Loofah is rich in water and dietary fiber. This soup is low in fat and refreshing, making it ideal for summer cooling or during a weight-loss diet. The fried eggs, coated in the milky broth, provide easily absorbable protein, but be mindful of oil usage to avoid a greasy texture.
