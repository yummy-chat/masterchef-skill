# Sichuan Cold Noodles (Chuan Bei Liang Fen)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Pea starch 0.5 kg
💧 Water 4.75 kg (0.75 kg cold water for mixing, 3.75 kg boiling water for cooking)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, to taste
🧅 Chopped green onions, to taste
🧄 Garlic (minced)
🍶 Vinegar, generous amount (to cover minced garlic)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, to taste
🍬 White sugar, generous amount
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste
🥢 Light soy sauce, to taste
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil, to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste
🌶️ Chili oil (including sediment), to taste
🫘🥫 Lao Gan Ma black bean sauce, 1 tablespoon

## Cookware and Equipment
🫕 Stainless steel bowl / large pot
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Wooden chopsticks
🔪 Knife, cutting board
📦 Rectangular plastic storage container
🍽️ Plate
🥣 Spoon, bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix pea starch with cold water, stir until smooth with no lumps.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, reduce to low heat, slowly pour in the starch slurry while stirring quickly and continuously in one direction with wooden chopsticks.
3. Keep on low heat, stir continuously for about 8‑10 minutes, until the mixture becomes thick and translucent, with a clear pea fragrance; when lifted with chopsticks, it should form long strings.
**Note**: High heat can easily scorch the bottom; stirring must be continuous.
4. Pour the cooked starch paste into a rectangular plastic container, let it sit to cool naturally until fully set.
5. Once set, invert to remove, cut into strips or pieces, and arrange on a plate.
6. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add minced garlic, pour in enough vinegar to cover it; then add salt, MSG, chicken bouillon powder, white sugar, sesame oil, light soy sauce, Sichuan pepper oil, oyster sauce, and bird's eye chili rings. Finally, drizzle with chili oil (including sediment), add one tablespoon of Lao Gan Ma black bean sauce, and stir well.
**Key**: Use enough white sugar so that a distinct sweet-and-sour taste is noticeable; chili oil must include the sediment from the bottom.
7. Pour the prepared sauce over the cold noodles, sprinkle with chopped green onions, and serve.

## Health Tips
This cold noodle dish is made from pea starch, which is low in fat and rich in dietary fiber. It is refreshing and appetizing in summer. However, the sauce has high content of sugar, oil, and chili. It is recommended that people with diabetes or those controlling weight reduce the amount of chili oil.
