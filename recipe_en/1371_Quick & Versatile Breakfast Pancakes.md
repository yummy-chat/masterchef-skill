# Quick & Versatile Breakfast Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🌽 Cornstarch 100g
🥚 Eggs 4
💧 Water 400ml
🫒 Vegetable oil 30ml
🥬 Cabbage 200g (shredded)
🥕 Carrot 80g (shredded)
🧅 Scallions 10g (chopped)
🍤 Shrimp 200g
🥓 Bacon 60g
🫓 Kimchi 80g
🥬 Chives 50g
🦑 Squid rings 80g
🍖 Ham 100g
🌽 Corn kernels 100g
🧀 Shredded cheese 80g
🥒 Daikon radish 400g (shredded)
🍤 Dried shrimp 20g

🧂 Salt 5g (for batter) + to taste (for filling seasoning)
🧂 Baking powder 5g
🧂 Black pepper to taste
🧂 White pepper to taste
🧂 Sesame oil to taste

## Cookware & Equipment
🥄 Whisk
🍦 Ice cream scoop
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔪 Spatula
♨️ Air fryer
📦 Airtight container
🫓 Baking sheet
📄 Parchment paper

## Instructions
1. **Make the versatile batter**: Combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, 5g salt, and baking powder in a large bowl. Add eggs, vegetable oil, and water. Whisk until completely smooth and lump-free, with a thick consistency.
   **Key point**: Mix the batter thoroughly to ensure no dry flour lumps remain.

2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Shred the cabbage and carrot. Toss with salt, let sit for a few minutes, then squeeze out excess water thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Squeeze out all moisture to prevent the pancakes from becoming watery during frying and to preserve texture after freezing.

3. **Mix the filling**: According to your desired flavor, add the prepared vegetables along with corresponding meats, seafood, cheese, etc., into the versatile batter. Then add scallions, salt, black pepper, white pepper, sesame oil, and other seasonings. Stir to combine evenly.

4. **Fry the pancakes**: Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat and lightly spray with oil. Use an ice cream scoop to drop batter into the pan, gently flattening with a spatula.
   **Heat & Doneness**: Fry over medium-low heat until one side is set and the surface is no longer runny, then flip. Cook until both sides are golden brown.
   **Key point**: For okonomiyaki-style pancakes, place a slice of bacon on top before flipping. For kimchi pancakes, press thin to achieve a crispy texture. For daikon radish pancakes, you can stack them thicker to recreate a crispy exterior and tender interior.

5. **Freeze for storage**: Arrange the cooked pancakes on a baking sheet or in an airtight container lined with parchment paper, separating each pancake with a layer of parchment paper. Freeze until firm, then you can separate and store individual pancakes for easy access.

6. **Reheat and serve**:
   - **Air fryer**: Place frozen pancakes directly into the air fryer and heat at 180°C (350°F) for 8 minutes.
   - **Pan**: Heat a pan over low heat, add frozen pancakes, and fry until both sides are golden brown. For extra flavor, fry an egg on the side first, then place the frozen pancake on top of the egg once the white sets, and cook together. Cheese pancakes will become stretchy when reheated.

## Health Tips
This breakfast pancake offers a balanced combination of vegetables and protein, making it suitable for a quick and nutritious breakfast. The addition of baking powder creates a fluffier texture, but pan-frying can be higher in calories. Using an air fryer for reheating is recommended to reduce oil intake.
