# Dongjiang Salt-Baked Hand-Torn Chicken  
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Whole chicken 1 (about 1.5–2 kg prepared chicken)  
🌿 Spice packet 1 (contains star anise, cinnamon, and other common braising spices; exact types not specified in video)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍲 Large stockpot  
🥄 Long-handled ladle  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥢 Long chopsticks  
🪣 Large basin (for ice water cooling)  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Bring a pot of enough water to a boil, add the spice packet and a generous amount of salt to make a seasoned broth.  
**Key point**: The amount of salt must be sufficient, as it will be the main source of saltiness for the chicken.  

2. Prepare a large basin of ice water, enough to fully submerge the whole chicken. Ensure the ice water is very cold.  

3. Place the whole chicken into the gently simmering seasoned broth, using the "dipping method": fully submerge the chicken, cook briefly, then lift to let the liquid inside the cavity drain out, then submerge again. Repeat this several times.  
**Key point**: Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Repeatedly lifting and submerging ensures even doneness inside and out. Cook until just done; test by inserting chopsticks into the thickest part of the thigh — no blood should ooze out, and the skin should be tight and golden brown.  

4. Quickly transfer the cooked chicken into the ice water, fully immersing it until the chicken is completely cooled.  
**Key point**: Rapid cooling makes the skin crisp and the meat firm.  

5. Remove the chicken, drain well, and place on a cutting board. Cut off the head, neck, feet, wings, and legs. Separate the chicken from the bones along the skeleton. Use a knife to cut large pieces of meat, then tear by hand along the grain into strips or chunks.  
**Key point**: Hand-tearing best preserves the fibrous texture and juices. Skin-on and skinless meat can be torn separately for easier plating later.  

6. Take a suitable amount of reserved chicken cooking liquid, add salt and sand ginger powder, and stir well to make a seasoning sauce.  
**Key point**: Sand ginger powder is the key flavor of this dish. Adjust saltiness to taste.  

7. Plating: First, place the bones and smaller meat pieces at the bottom of the plate. Then layer the hand-torn meat on top. Finally, arrange the more intact chicken skin on the top layer. Drizzle the seasoning sauce evenly, and sprinkle with white sesame seeds.  
**Key point**: Drizzle the sauce evenly, and arrange the plating neatly and attractively.  

## Health Tips  
This salt-baked hand-torn chicken is rich in protein and moderate in fat, suitable for people building muscle or controlling weight. However, it is relatively high in salt, so those with high blood pressure should consume only a small amount. The ice water bath makes the skin crisp and the meat firm. Be careful to control the cooking time to avoid overcooking.
