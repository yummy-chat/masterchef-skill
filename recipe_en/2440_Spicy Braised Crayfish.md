# Spicy Braised Crayfish
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦞 Crayfish – as needed (scrubbed clean, heads and veins intact, drained well)
🧅 Scallions – 3–4 stalks (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger – 1 large piece (sliced)
🌿 Dried spices – as needed (including star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, bay leaves, dried tangerine peel, sand ginger, Sichuan pepper, cloves, fennel seeds, gardenia)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – 1 large handful
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns – 1 handful
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) – as needed
🌶️ Chili powder – as needed
🍺 Beer – 4 cans
💧 Water – about half a large basin
🧂 Salt – as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon – as needed
🧂 Ground white pepper – as needed
🧴 Light soy sauce – as needed
🧴 Dark soy sauce – as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Large basin
🪵 Wooden lid
🔥 Earth stove and firewood

## Instructions
1. Cut scallions into sections and slice the ginger; combine all dried spices, dried chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns and set aside.
2. Heat cooking oil in the wok to 70% hot, add scallion sections and ginger slices, stir-fry until fragrant, then add doubanjiang and cook until red oil releases.
3. Add the mixed dried spices, dried chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry quickly until well combined, then add chili powder and continue stir-frying until aromatic.
4. Pour in 4 cans of beer and about half a basin of water, then bring to a boil over high heat.
5. Once the broth is boiling, season with salt, chicken bouillon, ground white pepper, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Keep boiling over high heat for 5 minutes to fully release the flavors of the spices.
6. Add the drained crayfish into the rolling spiced broth, cover with the wooden lid, and boil over high heat for 20 minutes.
7. After 20 minutes, turn off the heat and let the crayfish steep, covered, for another 30 minutes to fully absorb the braising liquid.
8. Once steeped, transfer the crayfish and broth together into a large basin and serve.

## Health Advice
Spicy braised crayfish is high in protein and low in fat, but also high in sodium and purines. It is suitable for occasional indulgence; people with gout or high blood pressure should consume with caution. It is recommended to pair with vegetables such as cucumber or edamame to balance nutrition, and avoid using beer as the only accompanying soup.
