# Braised Chinese Cabbage with Tofu
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Chinese cabbage, about 1/4 head (separate stems and leaves)
🧈 Brine tofu, 1 large piece (cut into 4 thick slices)
🍜 Vermicelli, 1 small handful (soak in cold water in advance to soften)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🥩 Pork belly, about 100g (sliced)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, about 8 pieces (soak in boiling water, reserve the soaking liquid)
🦐 Dried shrimp, 1 small handful
🍇 Wolfberries, about 10 pieces
🌰 Red dates (jujubes), 2-3 pieces
🧅 Scallion, 1 section (cut into segments)
🧄 Ginger, about 5g (sliced)
🧂 Salt, to taste (2 spoons for blanching + final seasoning)
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed
🥡 Light soy sauce (or premium soy sauce), about 1 spoon

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok (with honeycomb texture)
🍲 Soup pot
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Wok/soup ladle
🥣 Bowl (for soaking mushrooms, holding ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the cabbage**: Cut the cabbage stems lengthwise into wide strips, and cut the leaves even wider. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add salt, then blanch the cabbage. When the water returns to a boil, immediately remove the cabbage and plunge it into cold water. Drain and squeeze out excess water. Set aside.  
   **Key point**: Cutting lengthwise preserves texture; blanching removes raw bitterness; cooling maintains crispness and color.

2. **Cut the tofu**: Slice the brine tofu into large thick pieces for easier frying and to prevent breakage.

3. **Pan-fry the tofu**: Heat the wok, add oil to coat, then add more oil and heat until very hot. Place the tofu slices in the wok, reduce to medium-low heat, and fry until one side is golden and set. Flip and fry the other side until golden. Remove and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Hot wok and hot oil prevent sticking; if it sticks, gently lift the tofu to let oil seep underneath. Golden browning on both sides is essential for a milky broth.

4. **Pan-fry the eggs**: Using the remaining oil in the wok, fry the eggs into sunny-side-up eggs. Remove and set aside.

5. **Sauté the pork belly**: Reheat the wok with oil. Add ginger slices and pork belly slices. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the pork belly renders fat and the edges turn slightly crispy.  
   **Key point**: Fully render the pork fat—this is key to a rich, flavorful broth.

6. **Fry the aromatics**: Add scallion segments, squeezed-dry shiitake mushrooms, and dried shrimp. Stir until fragrant. Drizzle soy sauce along the edge of the wok and stir until a savory aroma develops.  
   **Key point**: Adding soy sauce along the wok edge releases its fragrance.

7. **Simmer the broth**: Pour in the reserved shiitake mushroom soaking liquid, then add enough boiling water. Add the fried tofu and eggs. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Key point**: Do not add salt at this stage. Use enough boiling water and cook over high heat to achieve a milky broth.

8. **Boil until the broth turns white**: Cover the wok and continue boiling over high heat for about 5 minutes, until the broth becomes milky white.

9. **Season and add vermicelli**: Once the broth is white, season with salt. Add the softened vermicelli and cook until translucent and tender.  
   **Key point**: Salt must be added later, otherwise the proteins won't emulsify and the broth won't turn white.

10. **Add the cabbage**: When the vermicelli is cooked, add the squeezed-dry cabbage. Bring to a boil, then turn off the heat.  
    **Key point**: Do not overcook the cabbage—keep it crisp.

11. **Garnish and serve**: Sprinkle with wolfberries, red dates, and a little chopped scallion to taste. Serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight control and nutritional supplementation. However, the pork belly and fried eggs keep the calorie content moderate. It is recommended to reduce the amount of pork belly or substitute with lean meat, and not to consume too much vermicelli.
