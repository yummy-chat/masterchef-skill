# Home-Style Braised Fish with Tofu 🐟🧈  
Cuisine: Sichuan 🏔️  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients  
🐟 Silver carp – 1 whole (scaled and gutted, fish bones cut into sections, fish flesh sliced into thick pieces, head reserved)  
🧈 Firm tofu – 1 block (cut into thick slices)  
🥬 Sichuan pickled mustard greens (suan cai) – 1 small bowl (finely chopped)  

## Seasonings  
🧂 Salt – to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon – to taste  
🌿 White pepper – a pinch  
🧄 Ginger-scallion water – as needed (homemade, for deodorizing)  
🌾 Dried starch – a handful (for marinating fish slices)  
🫒 Cooking oil – as needed (for stir-frying base and hot oil drizzle)  
🥓 Lard – 1 tbsp  
🌶️ Pickled chili (pao jiao) – to taste (finely chopped)  
🌶️ Pickled ginger (pao jiang) – to taste (finely chopped)  
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) – 1 tbsp (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic – to taste (smashed)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – a handful (for oil drizzle)  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns – to taste  
🧅 Scallions – 1 stalk (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro – to taste (optional, for garnish)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating fish)  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Ladle  
🍲 Large soup bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the fish**: Slit the silver carp from the back and split into two halves. Cut the fish bones into sections, slice the flesh into moderately thick pieces, and keep the head intact.  
   **Key point**: Do not slice the fish too thin to prevent them from breaking apart during cooking.  

2. **Marinate**: In a bowl, add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and ginger-scallion water to the fish slices. Mix until sticky. Then add a handful of dried starch and toss until the fish is evenly coated with a thin layer. Marinate the fish head and bones similarly, but without starch.  
   **Key point**: After mixing the fish slices to a sticky consistency, let them rest briefly for a more tender texture.  

3. **Prepare the aromatics**: Cut the tofu into thick slices. Finely chop the pickled mustard greens, pickled ginger, and pickled chili. Smash the garlic and set aside.  

4. **Stir-fry the base**: Heat oil in a wok, add lard. First, stir-fry the pickled mustard greens until fragrant. Then add the pickled ginger and pickled chili and stir-fry. Next, add the doubanjiang and stir until the oil turns red. Finally, add the smashed garlic and stir-fry until aromatic.  
   **Key point**: The pickled mustard greens must be dry-fried to release their full sour aroma.  

5. **Simmer the broth and tofu**: Add enough water to the wok. Bring to a boil, then season with salt and chicken bouillon. Add the tofu slices, reduce to medium-low heat, and simmer for 5–10 minutes until the tofu fully absorbs the broth.  

6. **Add the fish bones**: Add the marinated fish head and bones to the wok. Cook for about 15 minutes.  
   **Note**: Keep the heat at medium after adding the bones, allowing the broth to turn milky white.  

7. **Add the fish slices**: Reduce to low heat or turn off the heat. Gently add the fish slices one by one into the broth. Let the residual heat cook them until they turn white, then turn off the heat immediately.  
   **Key point**: Do not stir vigorously after adding the fish slices. Immersing them off-heat preserves their tenderness and integrity.  

8. **Serve and drizzle with hot oil**: Sprinkle scallion sections on top, then transfer the entire dish to a large soup bowl. Place dried chili pepper segments and Sichuan peppercorns on the surface. In a separate pan, heat oil until smoking, then pour it over the aromatics to sizzle and release their fragrance. Garnish with cilantro and serve.  
   **Note**: The oil must be hot enough to fully activate the aroma of the chili and Sichuan peppercorns.  

## Health Tips  
The combination of tofu and fish provides calcium and high-quality protein, offering complementary nutrition. Pickled vegetables and doubanjiang are high in sodium, so it is recommended to limit consumption of the broth and control overall salt intake. This dish is ideal for warming the stomach during cold weather.
