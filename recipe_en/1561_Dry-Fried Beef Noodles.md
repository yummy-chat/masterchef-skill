# Dry-Fried Beef Noodles
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef (lean, trimmed of sinew) – to taste
🍜 Fresh noodles – to taste
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers – to taste
🧅 Onion – to taste
🥕 Carrot – to taste
🧅 Scallion – to taste (for aromatics)
🧄 Ginger – to taste (for aromatics)
🧄 Garlic – to taste (for aromatics, can use more)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers – to taste
🌱 Chinese chives or garlic sprouts – to taste (for finishing)

🧂 White pepper powder – to taste
🧂 Black pepper powder – to taste
🍶 Light soy sauce – to taste (for marinating beef and stir-frying base separately)
🌾 Cornstarch – to taste
🫒 Cooking oil – to taste (for sealing the beef, greasing the pan, and stir-frying the base)
🧂 Salt – a small amount (for noodle boiling water)
🥄 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste, or Sichuan-style cooking paste) – 1 spoon
🧂 Half-crushed cumin – to taste
🫘 Toasted sesame seeds – to taste

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/container
🥄 Wok spatula or wooden spatula
🥢 Long chopsticks
🫗 Oil bottle

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the beef**: Trim off any sinew, then slice against the grain into pieces about 1 cm thick, aiming for even thickness.
**Note**: Cutting against the grain severs the muscle fibers, preventing a tough, dry texture.

2. **Marinate the beef**: Add white pepper powder and black pepper powder to the beef slices and mix well. Add a small amount of light soy sauce and mix well. Add cornstarch and rub until the beef is coated in a thin, even layer. Finally, drizzle in enough cooking oil and toss until each slice is separated and not sticking together.
**Key point**: Try not to wash the beef with water before marinating. If you do wash it, thoroughly pat it dry; otherwise, it will release water during cooking. Sealing with oil is crucial for preventing sticking and locking in moisture.

3. **Prepare the aromatics and vegetables**: Mince the scallion, ginger, and garlic. Cut the dried chili peppers into segments. Shred the green and red chili peppers, onion, and carrot. Cut the Chinese chives or garlic sprouts into sections.

4. **Cook the noodles**: Bring a generous amount of water to a rolling boil, add a pinch of salt, then add the fresh noodles. Cook for the shortest time possible. Immediately drain and rinse under cold water 2-3 times, then drain well.
**Key point**: Enough water, a rolling boil, and salt help make the noodles springy. Rinsing with cold water removes excess surface starch, preventing stickiness and increasing chewiness.

5. **Quick-fry the beef**: Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of oil to coat the pan, then pour it out. Reheat the wok to create an oil film. Add the marinated beef slices, quickly spread them apart over high heat, and stir-fry just until they change color. Immediately remove from the wok.
**Key point**: The double oil-coating creates a temporary non-stick film. The beef is cooked as soon as it changes color; slightly charred spots on the surface are a source of flavor.

6. **Stir-fry the base**: Do not wash the wok. Heat it over high heat, then add enough cooking oil. Add the minced scallion, ginger, and garlic, and the dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add the Pixian doubanjiang and continue to stir-fry over low heat until the red oil is released. Splash in light soy sauce and let it sizzle to release aroma.
**Key point**: Achieving a bright, red oil is the core of this dish’s flavor and color.

7. **Combine and stir-fry**: Add the quick-fried beef, drained noodles, shredded green and red chili peppers, shredded onion, and shredded carrot all at once into the wok. Switch to a pair of long chopsticks. Quickly toss and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, allowing the surface moisture of the noodles to evaporate and the noodles to be evenly coated in the red oil.
**Key point**: Using chopsticks helps to separate the noodles more effectively. When you start to hear a crackling sound from the wok, it indicates the moisture has evaporated.

8. **Season and finish**: Sprinkle in the half-crushed cumin and toasted sesame seeds. Toss everything to combine. Finally, add the Chinese chives or garlic sprout sections and quickly stir-fry just until wilted. Serve immediately.
**Note**: Chinese chives or garlic sprouts should not be overcooked; just until wilted to retain their color and fresh aroma.

## Health Suggestions
This dry-fried beef noodle dish is rich in protein and vegetable fiber, but it has a higher oil and salt content. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or vegetable salad to balance the meal. Cutting the beef against the grain effectively prevents a tough, dry texture, making it a suitable quick energy boost for busy office workers.
