# Crucian Carp Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Crucian carp (scales left on, cleaned thoroughly)
🥄 Pan-fried egg (referred to as "rooster egg" in the text)
🧈 Silken tofu (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallion (including scallion segments for seasoning and chopped scallion for garnish)
🫚 Ginger (for seasoning)
🍒 Goji berries (for garnish and color)
🧂 Salt (for final seasoning)
🌶️ Ground white pepper (for final seasoning)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Fine-mesh strainer
🍳 Lid

## Cooking Steps
1.  Place the crucian carp with scales intact directly into the pan and fry until golden brown on both sides. A small amount of oil may be added, or none at all.
   **Key point**: Keeping the scales prevents sticking during frying and increases gelatin content.
2.  Use a spatula to mash the fried fish into pieces, and continue stir-frying until fragrant.
3.  Add scallion segments and ginger slices, then pour in a sufficient amount of boiling hot water.
   **Key point**: Freshly boiled water is essential—this is the key to achieving a rich, milky-white broth.
4.  Boil over high heat for 10 minutes, until the broth turns thick and milky white.
5.  Use a fine-mesh strainer to filter out all fish residue from the broth, retaining only the pure liquid.
6.  Add the silken tofu pieces and the pan-fried egg into the strained broth.
7.  Cover with a lid and continue cooking for 5 minutes to allow the ingredients to infuse.
8.  Remove the lid, add salt and ground white pepper to taste, and stir well.
   **Note**: Season at the end to avoid adding salt too early, which may affect the broth's freshness.
9.  Sprinkle goji berries and chopped scallion on top for garnish. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Leaving the scales on the crucian carp while making soup helps retain more calcium and collagen, making it suitable for elderly people needing calcium supplementation, pregnant women, and those recovering from illness. However, individuals with high blood pressure should limit salt intake, and those with gout should consume rich broths sparingly.
