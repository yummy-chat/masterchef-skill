# Salt and Pepper Pork Strips
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

🧂 Seasonings  
- Salt  
- Salt and pepper powder  

🥩 Ingredients  
- Pork loin  
- Egg  
- Green onion and ginger water  
- Flour  
- Starch  
- Custard powder (for color and aroma, can be omitted if unavailable)  
- Water  
- Oil  

🍳 Kitchenware  
- Knife  
- Bowl  
- Chopsticks  
- Wok  
- Refrigerator  

📝 Steps:  
1. Wash the pork loin thoroughly, debone, flatten with the knife blade, cut into thick slices, then cut into thick strips.  
   (Wash the pork loin clean, remove the bones, flatten with the back of the knife, cut into thick slices, then cut into thick strips.)  
2. Add salt and pepper powder, mix well. Once it becomes sticky, **gradually** add the green onion and ginger water, and marinate in the refrigerator for 20 minutes.  
   (Add salt and pepper powder, mix well. Once it becomes sticky, gradually add the green onion and ginger water, and marinate in the refrigerator for 20 minutes.)  
3. Prepare the batter: Add 80 g flour, 20 g starch (note: 20 g starch, reserve the remaining 60 g for later use), 2-3 g custard powder, and 1 g salt.  
   (Prepare the batter: Add 80 g flour, 20 g starch, 2-3 g custard powder, and 1 g salt.)  
4. Crack in one egg, stir, then gradually add 100 g water, then add 10 g oil, stir repeatedly until the batter is fully mixed and slightly thick.  
   (Crack in one egg, stir, then gradually add 100 g water, then add 10 g oil, stir repeatedly until the batter is fully mixed and slightly thick.)  
5. Take out the remaining 60 g starch. Take out the pork strips, coat them with a layer of starch, then coat them with the batter.  
   (Take out the remaining 60 g starch. Take out the pork strips, first coat with a layer of starch, then coat with the batter.)  
6. Add oil to the wok, enough to submerge the ingredients, and heat to about 150 ℃ - 160 ℃. Add the pork strips and fry until light golden brown, then remove. When first added, the strips may stick together; do not move them. Once set, use chopsticks to turn them, and they will separate.  
   (Add oil to the wok, enough to submerge the ingredients, heat to about 150-160 degrees. Add the pork strips and fry until light golden brown, then remove. They may stick together initially; do not move them. Once set, use chopsticks to turn them, and they will separate.)  
7. When the oil temperature rises again to 150 ℃ - 160 ℃, add the pork strips for a second fry until golden brown, then remove.  
   (When the oil temperature rises again to 150-160 degrees, add the pork strips for a second fry until golden brown, then remove.)  
8. Sprinkle with salt and pepper powder, mix well, and serve.  
   (Sprinkle with salt and pepper powder, mix well, and serve.)  

## Health Suggestion
This salt and pepper pork strip dish is rich in protein, but its calorie content is high due to deep frying. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables (such as cold cucumber salad or stir-fried seasonal vegetables) to balance the meal. Suitable for muscle-building individuals or as an occasional treat. Those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in small amounts.
