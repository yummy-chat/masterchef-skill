# Millet Porridge
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Millet 100g
💧 Water 2000g

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Regular Pot
🔥 Pressure Cooker
⚡ Rice Cooker

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the millet in a bowl, add clean water and gently rinse it once. Stir with your hand, then pour off the water to remove only surface dust. Do not rub or scrub.
   **Key Point**: Millet only needs to be rinsed to remove surface dust. Do not wash excessively, otherwise the millet oil will be lost.

2. Fill the pot with water and bring to a full boil over high heat.

3. When the water is fully boiling, pour the rinsed millet into the pot.

4. Stir with a spoon to prevent the millet from sticking to the bottom. Keep boiling over high heat for 6–10 minutes, stirring several times during this period.

5. Reduce to medium or low heat and continue simmering for 15–20 minutes. Leave the lid slightly ajar to prevent the millet oil from boiling over. Continue stirring several times during this period to prevent sticking to the bottom.
   **Key Point**: Millet must be added after the water has come to a boil; do not add alkali to preserve the authentic millet flavor. If using a pressure cooker or rice cooker, adjust the water amount to 1800g per 100g of millet, though the texture will not be as good as when using a regular pot.

## Health Tips
Millet is rich in B vitamins and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those with weak stomachs or those needing gentle stomach nourishment. Be careful not to over-rinse to avoid losing the millet oil; it is recommended to pair with protein-rich foods (such as eggs or soy products) for more balanced nutrition.
