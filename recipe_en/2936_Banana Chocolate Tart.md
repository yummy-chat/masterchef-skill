# Banana Chocolate Tart
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍌 Banana 1 (sliced for filling) + 1 slice (for top decoration)  
🌾 Instant oatmeal 3 packets  
🥛 Thick yogurt as needed (for crust) + about 1/3 box (for ganache)  
🍫 Dark chocolate 2 bars (about 86g)  
🥜 Chopped nuts as needed (for edge decoration)  
🍞 Toast or cake slice 1 (optional, for added layer)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Bowl  
🫙 Round container (e.g., plastic box, used as mold)  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Knife  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🪣 Metal basin (for hot water bath)  
🧊 Sieve  

## Steps
1. Line the inner wall of the container with plastic wrap, ensuring complete coverage and enough excess for later wrapping.  
2. Pour the instant oatmeal into a bowl, add thick yogurt in batches, and knead with hands until it forms a pliable wet dough.  
3. Press the oatmeal dough evenly into the bottom and sides of the container to form a tart shell of uniform thickness, with the edge flush with the container rim.  
4. If using toast or cake slice, trim the edges, flatten it, and place it on the tart base, then spread a layer of yogurt to add texture.  
5. Slice one banana into uniform round pieces and arrange them tightly over the tart base, making the height level with the tart shell edge.  
6. Break the dark chocolate into pieces and add it to the thick yogurt.  
7. Prepare a basin of hot water, place the container with chocolate and yogurt in the hot water, and heat in a water bath while stirring continuously until the chocolate is fully melted, forming a smooth ganache. Ensure the water temperature is not too high to avoid steam entering the container.  
8. Pour the ganache evenly over the banana slices, smoothing it with the back of a spoon to completely cover the banana layer.  
9. Sprinkle a layer of chopped nuts evenly around the edge of the chocolate layer.  
10. Chill the tart with the container in the refrigerator for 30 minutes, or let it sit in a cool place below 20°C for about 1 hour, until the chocolate layer is fully set.  
11. Once fully set, lift the plastic wrap to unmold, peel off the plastic wrap, use a sieve to evenly dust the surface with powdered sugar or soy milk powder, and place a slice of banana on top for decoration.  

## Health Advice
This dessert replaces the traditional crust with oatmeal, increasing dietary fiber and reducing refined carbohydrate intake, making it suitable as an energy boost. It is recommended to use dark chocolate with at least 70% cocoa content to reduce sugar, but the overall calorie content is still high, so it is advisable to consume in portions, with 1/4 piece per serving.
