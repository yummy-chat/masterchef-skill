# Large Head Prawn, Taro, and Radish Rice
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Lipu taro, 1 small piece (cut into fingernail-sized cubes)  
🥕 White radish, 1 small piece (cut into fingernail-sized cubes)  
🍚 Wuchang rice from Northeast China, 200 grams  
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (diced)  
🥓 Knife-board fragrant cured meat (salted pork), appropriate amount (diced)  
🥓 Cured pork belly, appropriate amount (skinned and diced)  
🌭 Chinese sausage, 1 piece (diced)  
🦐 Large head prawns (Mozambique prawns), 4-5 pieces (butterflied and deveined)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🌱 Garlic sprouts, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  
🍶 Shaoxing wine (or Guangdong Rose Dew wine), 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 teaspoon  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🌶️ White pepper, appropriate amount  
🧂 Soy sauce (or light soy sauce), appropriate amount  
💧 Water, a small amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Steamer (or steaming pot)  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🥣 Strainer/Sieve  
🍲 Stockpot  
🔪 Cutting board and knives  
🥘 Stainless steel container (for steaming)  
🍲 Clay pot (or deep dish)  

## Instructions  
1. **Parboil the rice**: Bring water to a boil, add the rice, and stir to prevent sticking. Cook for about 5-6 minutes until the grains are half-cooked. Remove and drain in a sieve, then spread out to cool.
2. **Stir-fry the base**: Heat a little oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Add the diced pork belly and stir-fry until it renders fat and turns golden and crispy. Immediately add the diced knife-board fragrant cured meat, cured pork belly, and Chinese sausage, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the chopped scallions and garlic sprouts, and continue stir-frying for about 20 seconds. Add the diced taro and white radish, and stir-fry until well combined. Pour the Shaoxing wine along the edge of the wok, stir, then add the light and dark soy sauces. Continue stirring until the ingredients are evenly colored.
3. **Mix in part of the rice**: Add about one-third of the parboiled rice to the wok and stir-fry thoroughly with the base ingredients, allowing the rice to coat evenly with the sauce and absorb the aromas.
4. **Layer and steam**: Spread the stir-fried seasoned rice evenly at the bottom of a stainless steel container and press down firmly. Then, layer the remaining two-thirds of the plain white rice on top and press down firmly as well. Place in a steamer and steam at 100°C (212°F) for 20 minutes, until the taro is fully cooked.
5. **Prepare the prawns and combine**: While the rice is steaming, heat a non-stick pan over high heat. Without adding oil, dry-fry the large head prawns until both sides change color. Season with salt and white pepper. Add a small amount of water and soy sauce, cover, and simmer until the prawns are cooked through.
6. **Final mixing and plating**: Remove the steamed rice. Thoroughly mix the top and bottom layers of rice (the white and colored rice) until evenly combined. Transfer to a clay pot or deep dish. Arrange the braised prawns on top of the rice, drizzle with the remaining sauce from the pan, and garnish with chopped scallions.
   **Key point**: Steaming with the white rice on top keeps it fluffy; the final mixing creates a "marbled" effect. The soy sauce gravy from braising the prawns is a flavor highlight and should not be omitted.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber. The combination of taro and radish aids digestion and makes it suitable for winter nourishment. However, as it contains various cured meats, individuals with hypertension should be mindful of the salt intake.
