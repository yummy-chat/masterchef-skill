# Pear and Rock Sugar Cup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Winter (November to December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍐 Pear, as needed (whole, with skin on, as the cup body)  
🫐 Red dates, as needed  
🍒 Goji berries, as needed  
🌿 Fritillaria, as needed (optional, for cough relief)  
🍬 Old rock sugar, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for scrubbing the pear skin)  
💧 Water, as needed (for steaming)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Knife  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Small bowl (to hold the pear cup for steaming)  
♨️ Steamer

## Preparation Steps
1. Take the pear and scrub the skin with salt, keeping the skin on, then wash and set aside.  
   **Key point**: The pear skin has a cooling and heat-clearing effect, so do not peel it off.  
2. Slice off the top of the pear horizontally, about 1/4 of the way down, to use as a "lid". Use a spoon to scoop out the core, creating a hollow cavity.  
   **Key point**: Do not hollow out or puncture the pear body; keep it intact as a container.  
3. Fill the hollowed pear cup with red dates, goji berries, and old rock sugar in layers. For cough relief, add an appropriate amount of fritillaria powder if needed.  
4. Pour a small amount of water into the pear cup, then place the cut top back onto the pear.  
5. Once the steamer produces steam, place the pear cup in a small bowl, set it in the steamer, and steam over water for about 30 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Must be steamed over water, not directly exposed to boiling water.

## Health Suggestions
This pear and rock sugar cup is a natural "dessert remedy" for moistening the lungs and stopping coughs in winter, especially suitable for dry or itchy throats or mild coughs. Note that fritillaria is cold in nature, so it should be used with caution in people with a cold constitution or during pregnancy. Add rock sugar in moderation to avoid excessive sweetness that may affect the health benefits.
