# Beef Sauce (Beef Ragù)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 2.4 jin (1.2 kg) lean beef (choose cuts with minimal sinew; boil whole, then cut into large cubes)
🍄 80 g dried shiitake mushrooms (rehydrate in boiling water, remove stems, dice slightly smaller than the beef cubes, squeeze until half-dry)
🧅 8 liang (400 g) spring onion (or purple onion) (slice into slightly thicker shreds along the grain)
🧄 2–3 heads garlic (mince, rinse twice with water, drain well to prevent bitterness when frying)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (mince into small pieces)
💧 1 bowl of reserved shiitake soaking water
🌶️ 120 g Pixian chili bean paste (doubanjiang) (finely chop before use)
🫘 100 g yellow soybean paste (about 3 small spoons)
🥣 50 g sweet bean paste (preferably made from wheat flour)
🍾 60 g Wei Ji Xian soy sauce (about 5 spoons)
🧂 2 g black pepper powder
🧂 Black peppercorns, to taste (for boiling the beef)
🧂 White pepper powder, to taste (for boiling the beef)
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🫒 About 1 jin (500 g) peanut oil

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫙 Dry glass jars
🥢 Wooden spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef into large chunks. Place in cold water in a pot. Add ginger slices, black peppercorns, white pepper powder, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, cover, and reduce to the lowest heat. Simmer for 40 to 60 minutes until a chopstick pierces easily with no blood oozing out. Remove from the pot, let cool, then cut into large cubes.  
**Note**: Do not add salt while boiling the beef, as salt will cause the protein to tighten and the meat will not become tender.

2. Quickly rinse the dried shiitake mushrooms with boiling water to remove dust. Change the water, press a plate on top to submerge them, and soak for about 10 minutes. Squeeze out excess moisture, cut off the tough stems, dice into pieces slightly smaller than the beef cubes, then squeeze again until half-dry but not completely dry.
3. Slice the spring onion into slightly thicker shreds along the grain. Mince the garlic, rinse twice with water to remove surface garlic juice, and drain thoroughly. Mince the ginger.
4. In a bowl, combine the finely chopped Pixian chili bean paste, yellow soybean paste, sweet bean paste, Wei Ji Xian soy sauce, and black pepper powder. Stir well and set aside.
5. Pour the oil into a cold pan. Without preheating, add the spring onion shreds, minced ginger, and drained garlic. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until the aromatics turn slightly yellow.
6. Add the squeezed shiitake mushroom cubes. Continue stir-frying until the sound of water in the pan noticeably diminishes, removing the mushrooms’ moisture.
7. Add the beef cubes and continue stir-frying until the surface of the beef becomes lightly caramelized, fully releasing the savory flavors of the ingredients.
8. Pour all the prepared sauce mixture into the pan. Stir-fry evenly until the **oil and sauce separate**, and the oil in the pan becomes clear and bright.  
**Key**: The sauce must only be added after the beef is well stir-fried.

9. Pour in the reserved bowl of shiitake soaking water. Continue to cook until all the water has evaporated and the second **oil-sauce separation** occurs in the pan, then turn off the heat.
10. Transfer the beef sauce into dry glass jars. Drizzle a thin layer of oil on top to seal. Store in the refrigerator, covered, and avoid introducing any raw water when serving.

## Health Advice
This beef sauce is high in protein and rich in shiitake polysaccharides. It is ideal for pairing with noodles or rice for an energy boost. However, it contains a relatively high amount of oil. It is recommended to limit each serving to no more than two spoons. Individuals with cardiovascular conditions should control their consumption.
