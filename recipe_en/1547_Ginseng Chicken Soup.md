# Ginseng Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 whole chicken (about 2-3 jin, remove feet, neck, and tail)
🌿 1 fresh ginseng root (cultivated)
🍚 About 2 tablespoons glutinous rice (soaked for half a day in advance)
🌰 3 ginkgo nuts (peeled)
🌰 4 chestnuts (raw)
🧄 4 cloves garlic (to be stuffed into the chicken cavity) + an appropriate amount (for cooking the soup)
🫘 2 red dates
🧅 Half an onion (purple-skinned, to enhance the soup's flavor)
🌿 4 slices astragalus (2 slices for stuffing, 2 slices for cooking the soup)
🌿 2 slices angelica sinensis (1 slice for stuffing, 1 slice for cooking the soup)
🧂 An appropriate amount of red sea salt (for cooking the soup)
🌶️ An appropriate amount of black peppercorns (whole, for cooking the soup)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🍲 Large soup pot
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Earthenware casserole or soup bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the whole chicken, cut off the chicken feet, neck, and tail. Rinse any residual blood clots and impurities inside the cavity thoroughly under running water.
   **Note**: Be sure to remove internal blood clots. The removed parts often have a strong odor and are unhygienic.
2. Stuff the chicken cavity in order with ginkgo nuts, 2 slices of astragalus, 1 slice of angelica sinensis, raw chestnuts, 4 cloves of garlic, and red dates. Then, fill it to about 70-80% full with the pre-soaked glutinous rice.
   **Note**: Angelica sinensis has a strong aroma, so use it sparingly. The glutinous rice will expand when heated, so do not overfill.
3. Cross the chicken legs inward and tuck them to secure the cavity's opening, preventing the stuffing from leaking out during cooking.
4. Bring plenty of water to a boil in a soup pot. Add black peppercorns, red sea salt, 1 slice of angelica sinensis, 2 slices of astragalus, half a purple-skinned onion, and fresh ginseng. Keep the water boiling.
5. Gently place the whole chicken into the boiling soup. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Skim off any foam. The opening will tighten further as the chicken meat contracts from the heat.
6. Transfer the entire pot of ingredients to a pressure cooker. Close the lid tightly. After the pressure builds, cook for 20 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it sit for another 10 minutes.
7. Open the lid. Strain the soup through a strainer into an earthenware casserole or soup bowl. Then, retrieve the whole chicken and ginseng, etc., and place them into the bowl. It is now ready to serve.
   **Note**: Straining will result in a clear broth, improving the texture and presentation.

## Health Advice
This ginseng chicken soup is mildly nourishing and suitable for people who are physically weak, have poor digestion, or want to build up their health in autumn and winter. The stuffing of glutinous rice and medicinal herbs creates a rich broth after simmering. Be mindful of the salt amount to avoid excessive sodium intake. It is recommended to pair it with a light vegetable salad to balance the nutrition.
