# Sugar-Free Chocolate Lava Boule
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 T65 Flour 330g (30g for poolish)
🦠 Low-Sugar Tolerant Dry Yeast 1.5g (0.5g for poolish)
💧 Water 264g (30g for poolish, reserve 10g for main dough)
🧂 Salt 6g
🍫 Cocoa Powder 10g
🍫 Oven-Safe Chocolate Chips 60g
🍊 Dried Orange Peel 80g
🥃 Rum 50g (for soaking dried orange peel, can substitute with water)
🧀 Cream Cheese 150g (for lava cheese filling)
🍬 Fine Sugar 18g (for cheese filling)
🥛 Heavy Cream 22g (for cheese filling)
🧀 Cheddar Cheese Slice About 1/4 slice (for filling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥛 Measuring Cup
🔪 Spatula/Dough Scraper
🥣 Glass Bowl
📦 Plastic Wrap
⚖️ Kitchen Scale
🧵 Bread Proofing Cloth
🧹 Rolling Pin
👝 Piping Bag
🍳 Baking Sheet
📜 Parchment Paper
🔥 Oven
⏱️ Timer

## Preparation Steps
**Preparation Phase**
1. **Prepare Rum-Soaked Orange Peel**: Mix dried orange peel with rum, seal and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Strain excess liquid before use.
   **Key Point**: If avoiding alcohol, you can soak in water.
2. **Prepare Poolish**: In a measuring cup, dissolve yeast in water, add flour, and stir until no dry flour remains. Cover with plastic wrap and ferment at room temperature until the volume peaks and then slightly deflates.
   **Key Point**: Use low-sugar tolerant yeast; watch for signs of deflation after fermentation.

**Main Dough Preparation**
3. **Mix Main Dough**: Combine water and yeast, add poolish and break it down. Then add flour, salt, and cocoa powder, stirring with a spatula until no dry flour remains and the dough is evenly colored.
   **Key Point**: This is a high-hydration dough; no kneading needed. It will be sticky and rough initially.
4. **First Rest and Fold**: Cover with plastic wrap and rest at 25°C for 30 minutes. Wet hands to prevent sticking, stretch one side of the dough and fold it toward the center. Repeat for all four sides, then flip so the smooth side faces up.
   **Key Point**: If room temperature exceeds 30°C, rest half the time at room temperature and half in the refrigerator.
5. **Second Rest and Fold**: Cover with plastic wrap and rest again for 30 minutes. Repeat the folding process. The dough should be able to stretch into a thin film. Perform a total of two folds.
   **Key Point**: If the dough sticks while folding, wet your hands promptly. The dough will be firmer after two folds; let it rest for an additional 5 minutes.
6. **Add Dried Fruit and Chocolate Chips**: After 5 minutes of rest, place the drained orange peel and oven-safe chocolate chips onto the dough. Enclose them with the dough and use a dough scraper to chop and fold until evenly distributed.
   **Key Point**: Use a chop-and-fold method to avoid overworking the dough, which can break the gluten.
7. **Bulk Fermentation**: Shape the dough into a round, place it seam-side down, cover with plastic wrap, and ferment at 25°C for 60 minutes until doubled in size. Dust a finger with flour and poke a hole; it should not spring back.
   **Key Point**: If room temperature exceeds 30°C, ferment half the time at room temperature and half in the refrigerator.

**Dividing and Filling**
8. **Prepare Lava Cheese Filling**: Soften cream cheese, add fine sugar and heavy cream, and mix until smooth. Stir in orange peel, transfer to a piping bag, and refrigerate.
   **Key Point**: Refrigeration firms the filling, making it easier to wrap.
9. **Divide and Pre-shape**: Generously dust the proofing cloth with flour, turn out the dough, and coat both sides with flour. Gently pat into a square and use a dough scraper to divide evenly into 6 portions. For each portion, place the smooth side down, fold the edges toward the center, and pinch the bottom seam tightly.
   **Key Point**: Use plenty of flour to prevent sticking; pinch the bottom seam firmly.
10. **Rest**: Place the dough seam-side down on the floured proofing cloth. Use the cloth's folds to separate the pieces, cover, and rest for 30 minutes.
    **Key Point**: The folds in the proofing cloth provide support, aiding later fermentation and shape retention.
11. **Fill and Shape**: Take a rested dough piece, place it seam-side up, and flatten into a round with a thicker center and thinner edges. Pipe about 36g of cheese filling into the center and add a Cheddar cheese slice. Gather the edges toward the center, leaving a small opening. Turn seam-side down and gently rotate on a floured surface to seal into a round.

**Final Proof and Baking**
12. **Final Proof**: Place the filled dough seam-side down on the floured proofing cloth, using the cloth's folds for support. Proof at room temperature for about 40 minutes until it springs back slowly when pressed.
13. **Bake**: Transfer the dough seam-side up onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and place in a preheated oven. Convection oven: Bake at 230°C for 10 minutes, then reduce to 200°C and bake another 10 minutes until the cheese slice is colored. Conventional oven: Bake at 230°C for 20 minutes until the cheese slice is colored.

## Health Advice
This sugar-free boule uses cocoa powder as a substitute for sugar, combined with high-fiber orange peel and quality cheese filling, making it suitable for blood sugar control or as a diet-friendly treat. Pay attention to the activity of low-sugar tolerant yeast, and be patient with the high-hydration dough folding to develop gluten properly.
