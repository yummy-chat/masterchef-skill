# Braised Fish Head with Chopped Chili Peppers  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 fish head (frozen, thawed in advance, scrubbed with salt to remove slime and fishy smell)  
🥬 Pickled vegetables, to taste (chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (minced)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers, to taste (chopped)  
🌶️ Chopped chili peppers, to taste (drained, unwashed)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (rubbed and used as a base, some for garnish)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for scrubbing the fish head)  
🥓 Lard, to taste (used once for stir-frying the aromatics and once for seasoning)  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🌫️ A pinch of white pepper  
🍺 A splash of beer  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks (for checking doneness)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the fish head**: Wash the fish head and rub it repeatedly with salt to remove surface slime and fishy smell. Rinse clean, then split it open and make nine evenly spaced cuts for better flavor absorption.  
2. **Prepare the aromatics**: Chop the pickled vegetables, garlic, ginger, and bird's eye chili peppers. Use a slotted spoon to drain the excess liquid from the chopped chili peppers. Rub the scallions to release their aroma and set aside.  
3. **Line the pot**: Line the bottom of the clay pot with the rubbed scallions to prevent the fish head from sticking and to add scallion fragrance.  
4. **Add the main ingredient**: Place the scored fish head flat on top of the scallions. Evenly scatter the chopped pickled vegetables, minced garlic, minced ginger, bird's eye chili peppers, and drained chopped chili peppers over the surface of the fish head.  
5. **Make the sauce**: Heat the wok and melt a portion of the lard. Add the minced ginger, minced garlic, bird's eye chili peppers, and pickled vegetables. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Immediately pour the hot oil and aromatics over the fish head.  
6. **Season**: Add a little more lard, sugar, white pepper, and a splash of beer on top of the fish head. No additional water is needed.  
7. **Cook**: Cover the clay pot with its lid and simmer over low heat for about 15 minutes, allowing the seasonings to slowly meld with the fish head.  
8. **Check for doneness**: Insert chopsticks into the thickest part of the fish. If they go through easily without any blood oozing out, it's fully cooked. Turn off the heat and serve directly in the pot.  
**Note**: Slow simmering on low heat ensures the fish head remains tender and not dry; the chopped chili peppers and pickled vegetables are already salty, so no additional salt is needed later.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in collagen and capsaicin, which can stimulate appetite, but watch your salt intake. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy food and don't easily get breakouts; gout patients should consume sparingly. The chopped chili peppers and pickled vegetables are already sufficiently salty, so no extra salt is required.
