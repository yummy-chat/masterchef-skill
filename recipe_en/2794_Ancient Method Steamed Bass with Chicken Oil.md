# Ancient Method Steamed Bass with Chicken Oil

## Steps
1.  Clean the bass thoroughly, make sure to remove the black membrane and blood from the belly. Cut a few slits on both sides of the fish for better flavor absorption and even cooking. Rub a little salt all over the fish and set aside to marinate.
2.  Cut the scallions into sections or shreds and set aside. Put the shredded pork, shredded shiitake mushrooms, and shredded ginger in a bowl. Add salt, cornstarch, and a little oil. Mix well.
3.  Take the fish plate, line the bottom with a few scallion sections. Place the fish flat on top. Drizzle one tablespoon of white vinegar over the fish to remove fishy smell and enhance freshness. Then drizzle about one tablespoon of chicken oil. Finally, evenly spread the mixed shredded meat and ingredients on top of the fish.
4.  Move the plated fish into a preheated steamer that is boiling and steaming.**Must use high heat**. Steam for about 12 minutes.
**Key**: High heat steaming quickly seals in the fish's fresh flavor; adjust the time according to the size of the fish.

5.  Once steamed, remove from the steamer. Gently insert chopsticks into the thickest part of the fish's back. If they go in smoothly, the fish is cooked.
6.  Place a generous amount of shredded scallions on top of the steamed fish. Heat an appropriate amount of chicken oil until it is very hot. Pour the hot oil over the scallions from a height to release their aroma. Finally, pour light soy sauce along the edge of the plate.
**Key**: Pouring hot oil over scallions is a classic Cantonese steaming technique that greatly enhances the aroma; adding the soy sauce at the end preserves the original flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish uses chicken oil to steam the fish, making it rich and flavorful, but it is relatively high in fat. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the calories. Bass is low in fat and high in protein, suitable for weight loss periods and post-surgery recovery. High-heat steaming to lock in freshness is key; pay attention to the steaming time to avoid overcooking.
