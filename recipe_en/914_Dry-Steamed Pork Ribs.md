# Dry-Steamed Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork spare ribs 500g (lean ribs, cut into small pieces, preferably single bone pieces)  
🫚 Ginger 8g (cut into fine shreds)  
🧄 Garlic 25g (flattened and minced)  
🧅 Spring onion 15g (white part placed with garlic and ginger, green part cut into scallions for use before serving)  
🌾 Potato starch 30g (for coating)  
🫘 Fermented black beans 15g (chopped, choose ones without salt crystals on the surface)  
🫒 Cooking oil about 5 tablespoons (heat until smoking, pour over ginger, garlic, spring onion, and fermented black beans to release aroma)  
🍬 White sugar 10g  
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.2g (a small amount, do not overdo)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g  
🧂 MSG 3g  
🥄 Light soy sauce 15g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 15g  
🥫 Red fermented bean curd juice 10g (if omitted, add some salt as compensation)  
🫒 Sesame oil 10g (mix in at the end)  
💧 Water 80g (for the steamer base)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl/ basin  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Small pot (for heating oil)  
🍲 Large iron pot or cast iron pot (essential for steaming)  
🥘 Steaming rack  
🍽️ Stainless steel plate (or high-temperature resistant ceramic plate)  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Cut the pork ribs into small pieces, soak them in clean water for at least half an hour, discard the bloody water, rinse twice, then pat dry with kitchen paper towels.
2.  Cut the ginger into fine shreds, flatten and mince the garlic, separate the white and green parts of the spring onion, chop the white part, and chop the green part for later use. Chop the fermented black beans. Place the white spring onion, minced garlic, ginger shreds, and chopped fermented black beans in the same bowl.
3.  Pour about 5 tablespoons of cooking oil into a small pot, heat over high heat until the oil smokes, immediately pour it over the mixture in the bowl, and stir well.
4.  In the bowl, sequentially add white sugar, white pepper powder, chicken bouillon, MSG, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and red fermented bean curd juice. Stir to combine into a sauce.
5.  Pour the sauce over the ribs. First, mix evenly with your hands, then stir in one direction for about 1 minute until there is no excess oil liquid at the bottom of the bowl and the surface of the ribs becomes sticky.
6.  Add the potato starch and mix well. Finally, add the sesame oil and mix well again. At this point, the ribs' surface should be moist and sticky.
7.  Add 80g of water to the iron pot and place the steaming rack inside. Evenly spread the ribs in a single layer on the stainless steel plate, place it on the steaming rack, and cover with the lid.
8.  Bring to a boil over high heat until steam appears, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 17 to 25 minutes. In the last 2 minutes, turn the heat back to high to create a slightly charred aroma in the pot.
9.  Turn off the heat, remove the ribs, and sprinkle with the reserved green scallions. The bottom of the plate should have virtually no liquid, only a small amount of oil.

## Health Advice
This dry-steamed pork rib dish uses oil to release the aroma of fermented black beans and garlic, resulting in a smooth and tender texture without being greasy. It is rich in protein, making it suitable for muscle building or recovery periods. However, note that pork ribs themselves have a relatively high fat content. It is recommended to pair with a plate of lightly stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition and control the rice portion size.
