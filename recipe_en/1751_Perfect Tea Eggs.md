# Perfect Tea Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs Enough to fill one pot (use eggs that have been stored for over a week, as they are easier to peel)  
⭐ Star anise 15 grams (choose pieces with dark color and large, open segments)  
🌳 Cinnamon stick 10 grams (removing the outermost green skin enhances the aroma)  
🍃 Bay leaves 5 grams (choose fresh, fragrant leaves and avoid yellow, stale ones)  
🍵 Jasmine tea 10 grams (about 5 small tea bags, or substitute with other tea from home)  
🫗 Light soy sauce About two hot pot ladle spoons (for seasoning and coloring)  
🍬 Brown sugar About one hot pot ladle spoon (can substitute with rock sugar or white sugar, but brown sugar offers better flavor)  
🧂 Salt Approximately 17 grams (adjust to taste; the liquid must taste very salty to ensure thorough flavoring)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥄 Two soup spoons (for cracking the eggshells)  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl (for holding the spices)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Fill the rice cooker inner pot with enough cold water to completely submerge the eggs, and always use cold water for the initial cooking stage.  
2. Add the washed eggs. Be sure to avoid using fresh eggs from the same day; use eggs that have been stored for over a week for easier peeling later.  
3. Heat the rice cooker until the water boils, then cook for 10 minutes to fully hard-boil the eggs.  
   **Note**: While the water is coming to a boil, watch for air bubbles emerging from the eggshells. If no bubbles appear, the eggs may be too old and could have blocked pores, posing a risk of explosion; handle with extra care.  
4. Remove the cooked eggs and gently crack the shells with two soup spoons, creating an even, fine network of cracks to allow flavor absorption and form patterns.  
5. Return the cracked eggs to the rice cooker. Add star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, jasmine tea, light soy sauce, brown sugar, and salt. Adjust the amount of salt so the liquid tastes distinctly salty to ensure thorough infusion.  
6. Bring to a boil again and cook for another 10 minutes, then switch from the cooking mode to the keep-warm mode.  
   **Key**: Use the rice cooker's keep-warm function to allow the eggs to soak for a long time. The longer the soaking—several hours or overnight—the richer the flavor.  
7. After soaking, the eggs are ready to serve. To reuse the marinade, remove the first batch of eggs, add new eggs, follow step 1 to cook them, step 4 to crack them, then supplement with appropriate amounts of salt, brown sugar, and light soy sauce to taste, and repeat steps 5-6. The old marinade will produce an even better flavor.  
   **Note**: The marinade can be used repeatedly; it becomes more fragrant the more it is used.  

## Health Advice  
These tea eggs are high in salt, so people with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation. The combination of egg whites and tea polyphenols is beneficial. Using eggs stored for over a week makes peeling easier, and creating fine, dense cracks aids in flavor absorption.
