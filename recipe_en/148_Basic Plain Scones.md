# Basic Plain Scones
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Cake flour 300g
🌾 All-purpose flour 200g
🍬 Caster sugar 100g
🧂 Salt 4g
🧂 Aluminum-free baking powder 10g (do not reduce)
🧈 Chilled butter, as needed (not softened; adjust amount based on total weight)
🥛 Chilled heavy cream, as needed
🥚 Chilled whole eggs, as needed
🥚 Egg yolk 1 (for brushing)
🫒 Corn oil, a few drops (to add to egg wash)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Digital scale
🧊 Baking sheet
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Whisk
🧽 Dough scraper
🪥 Rolling pin
🔪 Sharp knife
🖌️ Pastry brush
🔥 Oven (convection/conventional)
🧊 Silicone mat
🧀 Box grater
🌬️ Cooling rack

## Instructions
1. Place cake flour, all-purpose flour, caster sugar, salt, and aluminum-free baking powder on a work surface. Mix thoroughly by hand.  
   **Note**: Do not omit or reduce the baking powder.

2. Using the largest grating side of a box grater, grate the chilled, unsoftened butter into coarse shreds directly onto the dry ingredients. Gently toss with your hands to coat each butter shred evenly with the flour mixture.

3. Spread the butter-flour mixture onto a baking sheet and freeze for at least 30 minutes.

4. Beat the chilled whole eggs into a liquid, then mix with the chilled heavy cream until well combined to form the liquid mixture. Return to the refrigerator to chill until needed.

5. Remove the frozen butter-flour mixture from the freezer. Add the chilled liquid mixture and immediately use a dough scraper to quickly cut and fold the mixture until mostly no dry flour remains. Transfer to a silicone mat.  
   **Key point**: The baking powder begins reacting as soon as the liquid is added, so work quickly.

6. Using your hands and the dough scraper, press and fold the mixture into a cohesive dough until no obvious dry flour remains on the surface. **Do not knead**.  
   **Key point**: Avoiding kneading is crucial for flakiness; it prevents gluten development.

7. Flatten the dough, sprinkle lightly with a small amount of cake flour, and cut into four equal parts using the dough scraper. Stack the pieces on top of each other. Repeat this process of "flattening—sprinkling flour—cutting—stacking" a total of 3 times to create layers.

8. Gently press and gather the stacked dough together. Dust the edges with a little flour and roll the dough on the work surface to shape it into a thick cylinder. Then use a rolling pin to roll it into a round disc about 18cm in diameter and 2cm thick.

9. Using a sharp knife, cut the disc in one clean motion into 8 triangular pieces. Avoid sawing back and forth during cutting to preserve the layered edges.

10. Arrange the pieces spaced apart on a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat.

11. Add a small pinch of salt and a few drops of corn oil to the egg yolk and mix well to make an egg wash. **Brush only the tops of the scones, avoiding the cut sides**.  
    **Key point**: Protecting the cut sides ensures the flaky layers are clearly visible after baking.

12. Preheat the oven in advance. Recommended baking temperatures: convection oven at 165°C for about 18 minutes; conventional oven at 180°C for about 20 minutes. Adjust according to your own oven. Aim to have the scones baked and colored within 20 minutes.  
    **Key point**: Bake until the tops are golden brown and the sides feel crisp when pressed. Baking too long will dry them out; too short may leave the insides underdone.

13. Transfer the baked scones to a cooling rack to cool. Serve and enjoy the crisp exterior and soft interior.

## Health Advice
The soul of these scones lies in the "cold" treatment—chilled butter, cold liquid, and frozen butter-flour mixture. Avoiding kneading is what creates the crisp exterior and soft interior. However, given the combination of 100g sugar and 200g flour, it is recommended to enjoy these with unsweetened tea or coffee to avoid blood sugar spikes.
