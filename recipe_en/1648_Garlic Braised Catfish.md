# Garlic Braised Catfish
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 catfish (Suanbian fish)
🧄 1 whole head of solo garlic (peeled)
🌶️ Pickled peppers, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫚 Pickled ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🌶️ Wild mountain peppers, appropriate amount
🫑 Green pepper rings, about 2 tablespoons
🫚 3-4 slices of ginger
🧅 1-2 scallions (cut into sections)
🫒 Mixed oil (rapeseed oil + lard), half and half
🫘 1 tablespoon of broad bean paste (Doubanjiang)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🌫️ White pepper powder, to taste
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste
🍾 Soy sauce, to taste
🧂 MSG, a pinch
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch
🍬 Sugar, a pinch
🌊 Water starch, as needed
🍶 Vinegar, a small amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Iron spatula
🪓 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🚰 Sink
🔥 Gas stove
🥢 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
1.  **Prepare the fish**: Scale, gut, and wash the catfish. Use a flat knife to fillet both sides of the fish along the backbone. Chop the backbone into pieces and cut the fillets into bite-sized chunks. Keep the bones and fillet meat separate.
2.  **Wash the fish**: Place the fish pieces in a bowl. Rinse thoroughly under running water 2-3 times to remove blood, then drain.
    **Note**: Be sure to wash thoroughly to remove any muddy flavor.
3.  **Marinate the fish**: Add ginger slices, scallion sections, a generous amount of salt, white pepper powder, and cooking wine to the drained fish. Mix well with your hands and marinate for 10-15 minutes.
    **Key point**: Use enough salt to ensure the fish meat has a base flavor.
4.  **Stir-fry the aromatics**: Heat the wok and add the mixed oil. When hot, add the peeled whole garlic cloves and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the garlic skin is slightly golden and fragrant. Then, add the pickled pepper sections, pickled ginger slices, and wild mountain peppers, stir-frying until fragrant.
5.  **Develop the red oil**: Add one tablespoon of broad bean paste and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the oil turns red and fragrant.
6.  **Simmer the broth**: Add enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Season with soy sauce, MSG, chicken bouillon, a pinch of sugar, and white pepper powder. Stir to combine into a broth.
7.  **Simmer the fish**: Remove the ginger and scallion from the marinated fish. Gently slide the fish pieces into the broth one by one. Immediately reduce the heat to **low** after adding the fish. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer and cook for about 5-8 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
    **Key point**: Use low heat throughout. Do not boil vigorously over high heat, as it will make the fish tough or cause it to fall apart.
8.  **Thicken the sauce**: Once the fish is cooked and the liquid has reduced to an appropriate amount, sprinkle in the green pepper rings. Drizzle in the water starch and gently push to mix, allowing the sauce to thicken and become glossy, coating the ingredients. Aim for a "bright and clear" sauce.
    **Key point**: The starch slurry should be thin and evenly applied.
9.  **Plate and serve**: Just before removing from the wok, drizzle a small amount of vinegar along the edge of the wok. Quickly stir to combine, then serve.
    **Key point**: The vinegar helps to elevate the complex flavors but should not be cooked for too long.

## Health Advice
This garlic-braised catfish is rich in protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for those needing to boost brain function or strengthen bones and muscles. However, the lard and broad bean paste make it relatively high in calories, so individuals with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Slow cooking over low heat is the key to achieving tender, silky fish that doesn't fall apart, so avoid overcooking.
