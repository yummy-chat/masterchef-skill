# Electric Rice Cooker Sponge Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 3 Eggs
🌾 90 grams Cake Flour
🌽 30 grams Corn Oil (or other neutral vegetable oil)
🥛 30 grams Whole Milk
🍇 Dried Cranberries or Raisins, as needed (optional)
🍬 60 grams White Sugar
🍶 3 drops White Vinegar (can substitute with lemon juice)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍚 Electric Rice Cooker
🌀 Electric Hand Mixer
🥣 Large Glass Bowl
🥄 Silicone Spatula or Rice Paddle
🧁 Strainer
🥣 Small Bowl or Saucepan
🖌️ Brush
🧻 Damp Towel

## Preparation Steps
1. Take out the inner pot of the electric rice cooker, evenly brush a thin layer of oil on the inner wall, and press the "Keep Warm" button to preheat.
   **Key Point**: Preheating in advance helps the batter set quickly after being added to the pot, preventing deflation.
2. Take a large, grease-free and water-free bowl, crack in the eggs, add the white vinegar and most of the white sugar, and use an electric hand mixer on low speed to initially combine.
   **Note**: The bowl must be kept dry and clean, otherwise it will affect the whipping.
3. Prepare a basin of hot water, place the egg mixture bowl over the hot water, and beat the whole eggs on high speed over the water bath until the volume visibly increases and the color lightens. When the mixer is lifted, the egg batter should drip slowly and the trail should not disappear easily.
   **Key Point**: Beating over a water bath is more stable and easier to succeed; the degree of whipping determines the success of the cake.
4. Sift the cake flour into the batter in two batches, using a spatula to fold from the bottom up until no dry flour particles remain.
   **Key Point**: Avoid stirring in circles to prevent the egg batter from deflating.
5. In a small bowl, mix the corn oil and milk. After stirring well, scoop in two spoonfuls of the egg batter and quickly mix into a smooth combined liquid.
   **Key Point**: This step prevents the direct addition of the oil-water mixture from causing deflation.
6. Pour the pre-mixed milk-oil mixture back into the large bowl of egg batter. Continue to gently fold with a spatula until the batter is smooth, shiny, and has no obvious separation.
7. Pour the batter from a height into the preheated inner pot of the electric rice cooker. Gently tap the pot a few times to release large air bubbles. Sprinkle with dried cranberries or raisins if desired.
8. Cover the pot, block the steam vent with a damp towel, select the "Cake" function, and bake for about 40 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Blocking the steam vent helps create a closed environment similar to an oven inside the pot.
9. Once the time is up, open the lid, invert the cake onto a cooling rack to cool slightly. When it is no longer hot to the touch, unmold and cut into pieces. The cake should be fully risen, golden on the surface, and spring back when pressed.

## Health Advice
This electric rice cooker sponge cake uses whole egg whipping instead of baking powder, making it healthier, but the sugar content is not low. It is recommended to reduce the sugar to 40 grams or use a sugar substitute. It is suitable as breakfast or an afternoon snack, and pairing it with unsweetened soy milk can help balance blood sugar. Although the cake has a soft and tender texture, attention should still be paid to calorie intake.
