# Steamed Zucchini Dumplings
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 1 old zucchini (about 40-50 cm long, peeled, seeded, and shredded)  
🌾 500 grams (1 jin) of flour  
🥚 3 eggs  
🌶️ 1 spiral green pepper (diced into rice grain-sized pieces)  
🦐 1/2 small bowl of dried shrimp (as needed)  
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped (as needed)  
🫚 Fresh ginger, minced (as needed)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for drawing out water and for the egg mixture)  
🫒 About 20 grams of cooking oil (for kneading dough) + additional oil for scrambling eggs and infusing flavor  
🍶 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🌿 A pinch of Sichuan pepper powder (or substitute with five-spice powder or thirteen-spice powder)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
♨️ Steamer  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🧀 Grater or shredder  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the dough**: Pour the flour into a large bowl. Slowly pour about 80°C hot water into half of the flour, stirring to form flaky pieces. Add about 20 grams of cooking oil and mix well. Gradually add cold water, knead into a smooth dough, cover with a damp cloth, and let rest.  
2. **Prepare the zucchini**: Peel the old zucchini, cut it in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scoop out the seeded core. Shred the zucchini into thin strips using a grater.  
3. **Draw out water and rinse**: Add salt to the shredded zucchini and mix well. Let sit for 3-4 minutes to draw out water. Rinse once with clean water, then squeeze out excess water thoroughly by hand. Set aside.  
4. **Prepare aromatics**: Beat the eggs in a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and whisk well. Dice the spiral green pepper into rice grain-sized pieces. Finely chop the scallions and mince the ginger.  
5. **Scramble the eggs**: Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Pour in the beaten eggs and immediately stir quickly with chopsticks to break into small, fine curds. Fry until fragrant and slightly browned for best flavor. Transfer to a plate and set aside.  
6. **Infuse the seasoning oil**: Heat a small amount of oil in the same wok. Add dried shrimp, chopped scallions, and minced ginger. Stir-fry until fragrant. Remove from heat, immediately pour in light soy sauce, and use the residual heat to release the aroma. This creates the seasoned sauce.  
7. **Mix the filling**: Place the squeezed-dry zucchini shreds in a large bowl. Top with the scrambled eggs. Pour the hot seasoned sauce over. Sprinkle with a little Sichuan pepper powder. Mix everything thoroughly until evenly combined and glossy.  
8. **Assemble the dumplings**: Roll the rested dough into a log, cut into portions, and roll out into wrappers. Place a portion of filling in the center of a wrapper. Fold the wrapper in half, pinching the center to seal, then firmly pinch the edges closed. Shape into plump dumplings. If the filling is generous, gently press it inward while sealing.  
9. **Steam the dumplings**: Prepare a steamer by lining the steamer basket with a damp cloth or brushing with oil to prevent sticking. Arrange the raw dumplings in the steamer, leaving space between them. Bring water to a boil over high heat. Once steaming, reduce to medium heat and steam for 12-15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the dumplings rest in the covered steamer for 2-3 minutes before opening the lid.  
**Key point**: Adjust steaming time according to dumpling size. Resting with the lid on keeps the wrappers moist and prevents shrinkage from sudden temperature change.  

10. **Serve**: The steamed dumpling wrappers will be slightly translucent and plump. Best enjoyed hot.  

## Health Advice  
Controlling the moisture content of the zucchini filling is crucial. Rinsing after drawing out water prevents the filling from becoming too salty, making it suitable for those watching their salt intake. The filling is rich in dietary fiber and calcium from the dried shrimp, making it suitable for all ages. However, note that the wrappers are relatively high in calories, so avoid overconsumption.
