# Spicy Prawns
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 South American prawns, as needed (de-whiskered, butterflied, deveined)  
🥔 Potato, about 200g (cut into thick strips)  
🥒 Cucumber, 3 pieces (cut into strips)  
🧅 Onion, half (sliced)  
🧅 Scallions, a bunch (cut into sections)  
🌿 Cilantro, a bunch (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed (minced)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, half a tael (cut into sections)  
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns, one small bowl  
🧂 Hot pot base paste, a small piece  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🥄 Soy sauce, as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little  
🍬 Sugar, a little  
🍗 Chicken broth, a little (or replace with chicken bouillon)  
🌶️ Sichuan pepper oil, as needed  
⚪ White sesame seeds, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍳 Large wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat to about 120°C. Add the potato strips, lower the heat, and fry for 3-5 minutes until cooked through, then remove. Raise the oil temperature to 150-180°C, add the South American prawns, and fry quickly at high heat until the shells are crispy, locking in the moisture. Then add the sliced onion and flash-fry for 1-2 seconds. Remove together with the prawns and drain the oil.
2. Leave a little oil in the wok. Over low heat, sauté the minced ginger and garlic until fragrant. Add the Sichuan peppercorns and hot pot base paste, pour in the cooking wine to "deglaze the wok," and stir over low heat until the base paste melts and releases its aroma. Then add the dried chili pepper sections and continue to stir over low heat until fragrant.  
**Note**: Adding cooking wine helps blend the aromas; maintain low heat throughout to prevent the spices from burning.

3. Add the fried prawns and onion, and stir-fry evenly. Drizzle soy sauce along the edge of the wok to "sear," then add oyster sauce, sugar, and chicken broth, and stir-fry evenly. Add the cucumber strips, fried potato strips, scallion sections, and cilantro sections, and stir-fry evenly. Finally, drizzle in the Sichuan pepper oil and stir-fry evenly.
4. Before serving, sprinkle in white sesame seeds, toss to combine, and remove from the wok.

## Health Tips  
This spicy prawn dish is rich in high-quality protein and relatively low in fat, but since it is deep-fried and includes hot pot base paste, the calorie content increases significantly. It is best enjoyed occasionally rather than as a daily meal. Pair with light vegetables or brown rice for a balanced diet. Those with sensitive stomachs should reduce the spiciness and oil content.
