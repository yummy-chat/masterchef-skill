# Crispy Shredded Potato Pancake
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 2 potatoes (shredded into thin strips, do not rinse)  
🥚 1 egg  
🌾 2 tablespoons starch (acts as a binder)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste  
🌿 Thirteen spices to taste  
🌶️ Chili powder to taste (sprinkle on surface at the end)  
🌿 Cumin powder to taste (sprinkle on surface at the end)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🫙 Transparent lid  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🪵 Wooden tray  
🍴 Spatula/silicone spatula  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Shred the potatoes into thin strips. Do not rinse with water; retain the surface starch to enhance stickiness.  
2. Place the shredded potatoes in a bowl. Add salt, chicken bouillon, and thirteen spices. Crack in the egg, then add the starch. Use your hands to mix everything evenly, ensuring each shred of potato is coated with the batter.  
3. Heat the pan, then add oil. Once the oil is hot, add the mixed shredded potatoes. Quickly wear gloves and press the potatoes into a round pancake of even thickness.  
   **Note**: The pan and oil must be fully heated to prevent sticking.  
4. Cover with a lid, reduce to medium-low heat, and cook for 3 minutes until the bottom is set and golden brown.  
5. Carefully flip the pancake. Continue cooking over medium-low heat for another 3 minutes until the other side is golden and crispy.  
6. Before removing from the pan, evenly sprinkle chili powder and cumin powder on the pancake surface. Adjust the amount to your taste.  
7. Remove from the pan. Use kitchen scissors to cut and plate. Enjoy while hot for an outer crispy, inner tender texture.  

## Health Advice  
Crispy shredded potato pancake is rich in carbohydrates and protein, providing quick energy. However, since it is pan-fried, it is relatively high in calories; those on a weight-loss diet should control their portion. Retaining the potato starch enhances stickiness, but be careful with oil temperature during cooking to avoid excessive oil absorption.
