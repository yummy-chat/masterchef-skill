# Mom's Seafood Pot
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦑 Squid 1 whole (cleaned, cross-scored, and cut into pieces)  
🐚 Abalone 4 (scrubbed clean and sliced)  
🧈 Egg Tofu 2 boxes (cut into thick rounds or segments)  
🥬 Small Bok Choy to taste (added at the end)  
🍒 Goji Berries to taste (for garnish)  
🧅 Ginger 1 piece (bruised until loose)  
⚪ White Peppercorns a small handful (about 20)  
🍶 Hua Diao Wine to taste (for removing fishy smell and enhancing aroma)  
💧 Mineral Water enough (to submerge all ingredients)  
🧂 Salt to taste (season before serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting Board and Chef's Knife  
♨️ Pressure Cooker (with inner pot)  
🔥 Portable Gas Stove  
🫕 Clay Pot  

## Steps  
1. Clean the squid, score a cross pattern, then cut into pieces; scrub the abalone clean and slice thinly; cut the egg tofu into thick rounds or segments.  
   **Key point**: The depth of the scoring on the squid should be moderate to ensure it curls nicely when heated; abalone slices should be uniform in thickness.  
2. Bruise the ginger until loose to fully release its flavor.  
3. Place the prepared squid pieces, abalone slices, and egg tofu into the inner pot of the pressure cooker.  
4. Add the white peppercorns, bruised ginger, and an appropriate amount of Hua Diao wine.  
5. Pour in enough mineral water to fully submerge all the ingredients.  
   **Key point**: Using mineral water makes the soup taste clearer and sweeter; using only ginger and no scallions ensures the soup remains clear.  
6. Cover the lid, cook under pressure for about 1 hour after it starts steaming, until the soup is clear, the seafood is tender, and the tofu is infused with flavor.  
7. After cooking, transfer the soup and ingredients to a clay pot and heat. Add the small bok choy and salt to taste, then sprinkle with goji berries before serving.  
   **Key point**: Add the small bok choy at the very end to keep it fresh, green, and crisp; add salt just before serving to avoid affecting the soup's freshness too early.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, making it suitable for winter nourishment. Egg tofu has a higher calorie content; those with high blood pressure should control the amount of salt used. Pressure cooking fully locks in the seafood's freshness; it is recommended to serve with mixed grain rice for a balanced nutritional intake.
