# Salt and Pepper Lobster
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦞 Lobster tail about 2 tails (shelled and cut into pieces)
🫑 Green bell pepper 1 piece (diced)
🧅 Shallots to taste (diced)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili to taste (diced)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns to taste
🧂 Coarse salt to taste
⚫ Black peppercorns (coarsely ground) to taste (mistakenly added in the video, but actually used)
🌽 Cornstarch to taste (for coating)
🫒 Cooking oil to taste (for frying and stir-frying)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan (for making salt and pepper mixture)
🫙 Mortar and pestle (for grinding salt and pepper mixture)
🫗 Sieve (for sifting salt and pepper mixture)
🍳 Wok (deep pot, for deep-frying and stir-frying)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing lobster)
🍴 Spatula
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board

## Cooking Steps
1. Place a frying pan over heat without oil. Add Sichuan peppercorns and coarse salt. Dry-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Remove and transfer to a mortar. Add coarsely ground black peppercorns and grind into a fine powder. Sift through a sieve to obtain homemade salt and pepper powder.
2. Shell the lobster tails and cut into pieces. Pat dry with a kitchen towel. Evenly coat each piece with a layer of cornstarch, ensuring the surface is fully covered.
3. Dice the green bell pepper, shallots, and bird's eye chili separately; set aside.
4. Pour enough oil into a wok. Heat until the oil temperature rises, then add the cornstarch-coated lobster pieces. Deep-fry over medium heat until golden brown and the meat is cooked through. Remove and drain the oil.
**Key Point:** Fry thoroughly in one go, ensuring the lobster pieces have a hard shell, turn red and golden. Subsequent stir-frying is only for coating with flavor.

5. Heat a separate wok with a small amount of base oil. Add the diced green bell pepper, shallots, and bird's eye chili. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant and the vegetables have softened.
6. Add the fried lobster pieces to the wok. Stir-fry quickly, then immediately sprinkle with the homemade salt and pepper powder. Stir-fry again quickly to evenly coat all lobster pieces with the seasoning. Remove from heat and serve.
**Key Point:** Stir-fry quickly to prevent the lobster meat from becoming tough.

## Health Tips
Lobster is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, but deep-frying increases its calorie content. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables. Suitable for those needing protein supplementation; those with high blood lipids should control portion sizes.
