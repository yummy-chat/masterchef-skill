# Gin Fizz
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍸 Gin  
🫧 Sparkling soda water  
🍋 Lemon  
🧊 Ice cubes  
🌿 Fresh greens (for garnish)  
🍬 Cane sugar syrup  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🔧 Manual citrus juicer  
🧴 Cocktail shaker (optional)  
🥄 Spoon  
🍹 Glass (capacity 350–400 ml)  
🔪 Cutting board  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Lemon preparation**: Cut the lemon in half crosswise, with the knife blade perpendicular to the line between the lemon’s tip and stem. From one half, slice one lemon wheel for garnish. Then cut the remaining half into quarters, juice the quarters using a citrus juicer, and set the juice aside in a container.
   **Note**: The lemon wheel is used for garnish. The lemon juice should yield about 20 ml; the amount can be adjusted to taste.

2. **Base mixing**: Choose a clear glass with a capacity of 350–400 ml. Add 100 g of ice cubes. Pour in 30–40 ml of gin, 20 ml of lemon juice, and 30–40 g of cane sugar syrup. Stir well with a spoon.

3. Place the prepared lemon wheel into the glass. Then slowly pour sparkling soda water along the inner wall of the glass until full. Gently lift and lower the spoon to stir evenly.

4. Place the garnishing greens on the surface of the liquid. The drink is ready to serve.
   **Alternative method**: Pour the ice cubes, gin, lemon juice, and cane sugar syrup from Step 2 into a cocktail shaker. Shake thoroughly, then pour into the glass. If the ice is too crushed, filter it out and add fresh ice cubes, then proceed with Step 3.

## Health Advice
This Gin Fizz uses lemon juice to balance the sweetness of the syrup, and the sparkling soda water provides a refreshing texture, making it suitable for gatherings or summer refreshment. Although gin contains botanical ingredients, it is still an alcoholic beverage. It is recommended to drink in moderation and avoid consuming on an empty stomach or in excess. Lemon juice is rich in vitamin C, but the syrup is relatively high in calories; those watching their sugar intake may reduce the amount used.
