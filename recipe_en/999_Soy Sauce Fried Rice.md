# Soy Sauce Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 A bowl of Heilongjiang long-grain fragrant rice
💧 Water, as needed (for steaming, rice-to-water ratio approx. 1:1)
🥚 2 eggs
🧅 Scallions, as needed (chopped into green onion pieces, used in two batches)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (cut into sections, used for making compound soy sauce)
🫒 1 tablespoon peanut oil
🧂 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
⭐ A pinch of star anise
🪵 A small piece of cinnamon stick
🫑 A pinch of Sichuan peppercorns
🍬 A few rock sugar crystals
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed (can be used directly if not making compound soy sauce)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel strainer
🍚 Rice cooker (pressure type, non-venting)
🍳 Wok (iron or non-stick wok)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Non-stick spatula or wooden spatula
🌡️ Food thermometer
🥢 Chopsticks, spoon

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. Rinse the rice thoroughly using a strainer, then drain completely.
2. Place the drained rice in the rice cooker, add an equal amount of water, and let it soak for 20–30 minutes before cooking as usual.
3. Once cooked, immediately use chopsticks to fluff the rice, releasing steam, then let it cool at room temperature or refrigerate to expel excess moisture.
4. Transfer the cooled rice to a bowl, add the beaten eggs, and mix thoroughly so each grain is coated with egg, avoiding clumps.
5. Chop the scallions into pieces; use half for mixing with the rice and half for the final step. Cut the cilantro into sections and set aside.

### Making Compound Soy Sauce
6. Place the wok over low heat without heating, add 1 tablespoon of peanut oil, and put in the cilantro sections, scallion sections, star anise, cinnamon stick, Sichuan peppercorns, and rock sugar.
7. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the aromas are fully released, then add 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce, bring to a boil, and immediately turn off the heat. Let it steep, and strain out the solids before using.
   **Note**: If you prefer not to make this, regular light soy sauce can be used as a substitute.

### Frying Phase
8. Heat the iron wok thoroughly, using a thermometer to ensure the bottom reaches 400°C (752°F) or above. Add a small amount of oil to coat the wok, then pour it out, leaving just a thin layer.
9. Keep the heat on high throughout. Once the oil is hot, quickly add the rice mixed with egg.
   **Key**: Do not stir frequently when the rice first goes in; allow the bottom to set and heat evenly, promoting moisture evaporation.
10. Once the bottom is set and the rice is evenly heated and begins to dry out, use the spatula to stir-fry continuously, driving off excess moisture.
    **Note**: If moisture remains high, briefly reduce to medium heat to dry the rice slowly, but ensure it is evenly heated.
11. Turn off the heat when the rice is loose and each grain is distinct.
12. With the heat off, drizzle in the prepared compound soy sauce, and mix quickly using the residual heat of the wok to evenly color each grain.
13. Turn the heat back on, add the reserved scallions, and stir-fry rapidly until the aroma of scallions is released, then immediately remove from the wok.

## Health Tips
This soy sauce fried rice works best with leftover or cold rice, making it easier to achieve distinct grains. Coating the rice with egg not only enhances flavor but also reduces the amount of oil needed. It is recommended to serve with vegetables to balance the carbohydrate ratio; those sensitive to blood sugar should control portion sizes.
