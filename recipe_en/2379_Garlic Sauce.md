# Garlic Sauce
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧄 Garlic: Appropriate amount (minced)  
💧 Water: In a 1:1 ratio with the minced garlic (for simmering the sauce)  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce: 1/3 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster Sauce: 1 teaspoon  
🧂 Salt: 3 grams  
🍬 White Sugar: 5 grams  
🌶️ White Pepper: A pinch  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥄 Strainer  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spoon (soup spoon)  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Steps
1.  Finely mince the garlic cloves, place them in a bowl, and rinse with cold water.  
    **Key Point**: Must rinse with cold water to remove excess garlic mucus, ensuring a golden, crispy texture and no bitterness when frying.  
2.  Drain the rinsed minced garlic using a strainer, and squeeze out excess moisture with your hands.  
    **Key Point**: Remove as much moisture and mucus as possible.  
3.  Pour cold oil into the wok, add half of the minced garlic, maintain medium heat, and stir continuously with a spoon.  
    **Key Point**: Add the garlic to cold oil to avoid uneven heating and coloring.  
4.  Fry the minced garlic until golden brown, then turn off the heat.  
    **Key Point**: After turning off the heat, use the residual oil heat to further deepen the color of the garlic, resulting in "golden garlic."  
5.  Use the strainer to separate the fried "golden garlic" from the "garlic oil," leaving a small amount of garlic oil in the wok.  
6.  Add the remaining raw minced garlic, and stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.  
    **Key Point**: Fully release the aroma of the raw garlic.  
7.  To the fragrant raw garlic, add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, water (in a 1:1 ratio with the original minced garlic), salt, white sugar, and white pepper in order. Increase the heat to high and bring the sauce to a boil.  
8.  Maintain high heat and boil, stirring continuously, until most of the water has evaporated and the sauce becomes thick and sticky.  
    **Key Point**: Allow the sauce to thicken naturally through water evaporation; no need to add a starch slurry.  
9.  Return all of the previously fried "golden garlic" to the wok, mix well with the thickened sauce, and turn off the heat.  
    **Key Point**: Remove from heat immediately after mixing; do not cook for a prolonged period.  

## Health Tips
This garlic sauce is rich in allicin, which has antibacterial and immunity-boosting properties, making it suitable for steamed dishes or cold salads. However, be mindful of the salt and oyster sauce content; those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
