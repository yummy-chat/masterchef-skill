# Basic Dough Kneading Techniques
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 Flour 1000g (about 2 jin, typically all-purpose flour)  
💧 Room temperature water 460g  
🧂 No seasonings (this tutorial is for making plain dough)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Kitchen knife (for cutting the dough to check its condition)  
🥢 Plastic wrap or another bowl (for sealing the dough to rest)  

## Instructions
1. Pour the flour into a stainless steel bowl. Add the water gradually in small amounts while stirring the mixture with your hand inside the bowl.
   **Key Point**: After each addition of water, stir thoroughly to fully incorporate the water and flour; pour water toward the dry flour areas.
2. While adding water and stirring, use your hand to scrape off any flour sticking to the sides and bottom of the bowl, mixing it into the flaky dough.
   **Condition Check**: No residual flour or batter should remain on the bowl walls.
   **Key Point**: Add water, stir, and scrape the bowl simultaneously—this is the core action to achieve a "clean bowl."
3. Use both hands to press the flaky dough in the bowl firmly into a mass, continuously folding the dough over itself.
   **Key Point**: Use a "pressing" action rather than "kneading," as it makes it easier for the flour to form a ball, and the flour sticking to your hands will gradually incorporate into the dough, initially achieving "clean hands."
4. Leave the initially formed dough ball in the bowl. Cover it with another bowl or plastic wrap, and let it rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Resting allows the gluten in the dough to relax, which is a crucial prerequisite for thorough and smooth kneading later.
5. Remove the rested dough and place it on a cutting board. Use a "left hand presses a dent, right hand folds back" technique to knead: Knead the dough from left to right into a long log, fold it over, press it flat again, and repeat.
   **Condition Check**: Knead until the dough surface is smooth and fine.
   **Key Point**: Do not knead the dough into a compact ball; instead, knead it into a long log to concentrate the force from your palm, making it easier to knead thoroughly.
6. Cut the kneaded dough with a knife to inspect its internal condition.
   **Condition Check**: The cut surface should be flat and smooth, with no raw flour particles or lumps inside. This is the final standard for a thoroughly and smoothly kneaded dough.
7. After completion, the dough is ready for subsequent noodle or pastry preparation. A thoroughly kneaded dough yields a chewier, more resilient texture.

## Health Tips  
The key to making dough lies in achieving "clean bowl, clean hands, clean dough." The core is to add water gradually and press and knead thoroughly. Resting the dough for 20 minutes relaxes the gluten, resulting in a smoother, chewier dough. Suitable for noodle dishes requiring a firm texture, such as dumplings or noodles.
