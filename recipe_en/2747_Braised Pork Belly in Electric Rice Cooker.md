# Braised Pork Belly in Electric Rice Cooker
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly, about 2-3 jin (cut into pieces)  
🥚 Eggs, about 8 (boiled, peeled, and fried until tiger-skin texture)  
🫑 Green peppers, 2 (fried until tiger-skin texture)  
🫓 Lotus leaf buns or multi-grain buns, 10 pieces (as a staple)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🧄 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (used whole)  
🧂 Fermented bean curd, 1 piece  
🫘 Soybean paste, a little  
🥄 Light soy sauce, half a spoon (soup spoon)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)  
🍬 Rock sugar, to taste  
🌿 Five-spice powder, to taste (can replace compound spices like Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, etc.)  
🍶 Huadiao wine, about 1-2 spoons (soup spoon), enough to submerge the meat (used as the main cooking liquid, no water needed)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍚 Electric rice cooker (main cooking equipment)  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for frying side ingredients)  
🥣 Colander (for soaking and rinsing meat pieces)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the pork belly into pieces, soak in clean water for 1 hour, then remove and drain. Mix well with Huadiao wine and marinate for 30 minutes.  
**Note**: Fresh pork belly does not need blanching; this method removes the gamey smell while preserving the meat's freshness and nutrients.  

2. Boil the eggs, peel them, and deep-fry the whole eggs until they develop a tiger-skin texture. Wash the green peppers and fry them until they also have a tiger-skin texture. Set aside.  
3. In the inner pot of the electric rice cooker, place 1 piece of fermented bean curd, a little soybean paste, and half a soup spoon of light soy sauce.  
4. Add the cut pork belly pieces and drizzle with an appropriate amount of dark soy sauce.  
5. Add the dried chili pepper sections, scallion sections, ginger slices, rock sugar, and five-spice powder to taste.  
6. Use your hands to mix all ingredients and seasonings thoroughly and evenly.  
7. Pour in enough Huadiao wine so that the liquid submerges the meat pieces.  
**Key point**: Do not add water; simmering with Huadiao wine softens the meat fibers.  

8. Close the lid, select the “Steam Rice” or similar cooking program, and start braising.  
9. After the program ends, open the lid, stir, and add the fried tiger-skin eggs.  
10. Close the lid again and run the same cooking program for another cycle.  
11. After the program ends, open the lid, add the tiger-skin green peppers, then place a steaming rack over the meat and arrange the lotus leaf buns or multi-grain buns on top.  
12. Close the lid and start the rice cooker program again to steam the buns until done.  
13. After the program ends, remove the buns, and serve the braised pork belly, tiger-skin eggs, and tiger-skin green peppers along with the sauce in a dish.  

## Health Advice  
Pork belly, after slow braising in Huadiao wine, becomes tender and easily digestible, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with multi-grain buns and green peppers to balance the meal. Tiger-skin eggs and green peppers add dietary fiber and vitamins, making this dish suitable for those watching their salt intake.
