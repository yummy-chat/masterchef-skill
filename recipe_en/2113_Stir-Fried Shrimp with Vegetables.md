# Stir-Fried Shrimp with Vegetables
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp, appropriate amount (butterflied and deveined, thoroughly squeezed dry)  
🥒 Cucumber, appropriate amount (seeded and cut into strips)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount (seeded and sliced)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (part crushed for juice for marinating, part sliced for stir-frying)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (part squeezed for juice for marinating, part chopped for stir-frying)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥚 Egg white, appropriate amount (for coating)  
🧂 Salt, a little (used multiple times)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (used multiple times)  
🌽 Cornstarch, a little (for coating and slurry)  
🍬 Sugar, a little (to enhance freshness)  
🧂 White pepper powder, a little (to remove fishy smell and enhance aroma)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a little (optional, can be omitted if not preferred)  
💧 Water, a little (for slurry)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. Butterfly the shrimp to remove the vein, wash thoroughly, and squeeze dry completely.
   **Key Point**: If the shrimp are not dry enough, the coating will easily slip off during marinating.
2. Add crushed scallion and ginger with a little salt to the shrimp. Squeeze out the juice from the scallion and ginger by grasping, and rub the shrimp until the surface becomes sticky. Let marinate for 5 minutes. After marinating, remove the scallion and ginger, add egg white and mix well, then add a little cornstarch and mix to coat evenly. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix.
   **Key Point**: Achieving stickiness is key to preventing the coating from slipping off; refrigerating the coated shrimp for about 20 minutes improves texture.
3. Prepare the slurry: In a bowl, combine salt, sugar, white pepper powder, cornstarch, and a little chicken bouillon. Pour in water and stir until well mixed.
   **Key Point**: Preparing the slurry in advance ensures a smooth cooking process and even flavor distribution.
4. Seed the cucumber and slice diagonally into strips. Seed the red bell pepper and slice. Slice the ginger and garlic. Chop the scallion.
   **Key Point**: Removing the cucumber seeds maintains its crunchiness; the shape of the vegetables should be coordinated with the size of the shrimp.
5. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add a little salt and cooking oil. Add the cucumber strips and red bell pepper slices, blanch for about half a minute until the color turns vibrant green. Quickly remove and drain.
   **Key Point**: Adding oil and salt to the water helps maintain the vegetables' color and adds a base flavor; blanching time should be short to keep them crisp and tender.
6. Heat the wok until smoking, pour in cold oil to coat the wok, then discard. Add an appropriate amount of fresh cold oil, creating a "hot wok, cold oil" condition. Add the shrimp and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Stir-fry over medium heat until the shrimp are fully变色 and curled.
   **Key Point**: The hot wok, cold oil technique is key to preventing sticking; for home cooking, you can use less oil and pan-fry instead of deep-frying.
7. Remove the cooked shrimp. Leave a little base oil in the wok. Add the chopped scallion, sliced ginger, and sliced garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the blanched vegetables and stir-fry evenly. Return the shrimp to the wok, immediately pour in the slurry, and quickly stir-fry until the sauce evenly coats all ingredients.
   **Key Point**: Work quickly after returning the shrimp to the wok to prevent them from overcooking.
8. Drizzle a little finishing oil before removing from heat, stir-fry a few more times rapidly, and plate.

## Health Tips  
Shrimp are high in protein and low in fat, making them an excellent choice for weight loss; cucumber, once seeded, is crunchy and low in calories, pairing with red bell pepper to provide Vitamin C. Suitable for fitness enthusiasts, the elderly, and children, but be mindful of controlling the amount of oil used in stir-frying.
