# Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 500g all-purpose flour  
🫙 5g Angel yeast  
🍬 3g sugar (to aid fermentation)  
💧 250g warm water (to dissolve the yeast)  
🥬 Bean sprouts, as needed (blanched and chopped)  
🍜 Vermicelli, as needed (softened and chopped)  
🌱 Chives, as needed (finely chopped)  
🥚 Eggs, as needed (scrambled into small pieces)  
🧂 A pinch of baking soda (to prevent chives from discoloring and releasing water)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (to mix with chives and for the filling)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🍛 Thirteen spices, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste  
🍤 Dried shrimp, as needed  
🔥 One tablespoon of hot oil (to be poured over the filling at the end)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥛 Measuring cup/glass (for preparing yeast water)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring yeast water, mixing dough, scrambling eggs, and mixing filling)  
🥣 Mixing bowl (for kneading and fermenting the dough)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for chopping ingredients and dividing the dough)  
🍳 Non-stick pan (for scrambling eggs)  
🥖 Rolling pin (for rolling out dumpling wrappers)  
🍲 Steamer basket (for steaming the buns)  

## Steps
1. **Activate the yeast**: Add 5g yeast and 3g sugar to 250g warm water. Stir with chopsticks until completely dissolved.
   *Key point: Pre-dissolving the yeast in warm water significantly boosts its activity, shortening the fermentation time.*
2. **Knead and ferment the dough**: Gradually pour the yeast water into 500g all-purpose flour, stirring with chopsticks to form flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place until doubled in size, with a honeycomb-like interior.
   *Key point: The dough must be kneaded until smooth and fully proofed to ensure the bun wrappers are soft and fluffy.*
3. **Prepare the filling**:
   - Blanch the bean sprouts, squeeze out excess water, and chop them. Soak the vermicelli in warm water until soft, then chop.
   - Finely chop the chives, mix with a little cooking oil and a tiny bit of baking soda.
   - Beat the eggs, scramble them in a pan with quick stirring using chopsticks, then let cool.
4. **Mix the filling**: Combine the scrambled eggs, chopped bean sprouts, and chopped vermicelli. Season with salt, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen spices, white pepper, and dried shrimp. Mix well. Finally, pour one tablespoon of hot oil over the filling to release the aroma, and stir again. Allow the filling to cool completely before adding the prepared chives, gently mixing them in.
   *Key point: Be sure to let the filling cool before adding the chives; otherwise, the heat will soften the chives and cause them to release water, affecting the texture and making it difficult to wrap.*
5. **De-gas and portion the dough**: Sprinkle a cutting board with dry flour. Take out the risen dough and knead it vigorously to release the gas until smooth and firm. Roll the dough into a long log and cut it into evenly-sized pieces.
   *Key point: De-gas the dough thoroughly; when you cut it, the cross-section should have no visible air pockets. This ensures the bun wrappers are fine and smooth.*
6. **Roll out the wrappers**: Press each dough piece flat and roll it into a round wrapper with a slightly thicker center and thinner edges.
   *Key point: The wrapper should not be too thin, otherwise, the high heat during steaming will kill the yeast, causing the buns to not rise properly and become hard.*
7. **Shape and second proofing**: Fill the wrappers with filling, pleat and seal the edges. Place the shaped buns on a steamer lined with a damp cloth or brushed with oil. Cover and let them rise a second time until they noticeably increase in size and feel light to the touch.
8. **Steam**: Place the steamer in a cold water pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and steam. After steaming, turn off the heat. Do not open the lid immediately; let the buns rest for 2-3 minutes before uncovering.
   *Key point: Starting with cold water allows the buns to heat evenly as the temperature rises. Resting prevents the buns from shrinking due to a sudden temperature drop.*

## Health Tips  
The soft bun wrappers and varied filling, rich in dietary fiber and protein, make this suitable as a balanced breakfast or light meal. However, chives can irritate the stomach; those with poor digestion should consume in moderation and be mindful of salt and oil intake.
