# Flavorful Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Baby back ribs 600g (choose tender ribs; larger ribs tend to be tougher)  
🧄 Garlic 15g (minced)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (minced, with a few slices reserved for marinating)  
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers appropriate amount (diced)  
🧅 Scallion whites 1 stalk (chopped)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 5g (cut into small sections)  
🌿 Cilantro a few sprigs (cut into small sections)  

🧂 Baking soda 1 spoon (about 1.5g, for cleaning the ribs)  
🧂 Salt 1g (for marinating)  
🧂 White pepper 1 spoon (about 1g)  
🍾 Light soy sauce 4g  
🍾 Cooking wine 3g  
🍾 Oyster sauce a pinch  
🧂 Five-spice powder 1 spoon (about 1.5g, plus a little more for later stir-frying)  
🫒 Garlic oil 10g (added at the end of marinating)  
🌶️ Lao Gan Ma (or fermented black beans) 2 spoons (about 5g)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 1g  
🌰 Toasted sesame seeds appropriate amount  
🫒 Scallion oil a drizzle (added just before serving)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Thick wooden cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Chinese wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Wok spatula  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the baby back ribs into evenly sized small pieces, using tender ribs for best results.  
2. Place the ribs in a bowl, add 1 spoon of baking soda and a little water, and scrub with your hands for 3 minutes. Then rinse thoroughly with plenty of water, drain, and squeeze out excess moisture.  
   **Note**: Scrubbing with baking soda helps remove blood and tenderize the meat.  
3. To the squeezed-dry ribs, add salt, white pepper, light soy sauce, cooking wine, ginger slices, oyster sauce, and five-spice powder. Mix well, then finally add garlic oil and mix again. Marinate for 10 minutes.  
   **Note**: Ensure all seasonings are thoroughly mixed in.  
4. Prepare the garlic, ginger, green and red bell peppers, scallion whites, dried chili peppers, and cilantro as specified above and set aside.  
5. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok over high heat until it reaches 60% hot, then reduce to low heat. Add the ribs one by one. Deep-fry over low heat for about 3 minutes until the ribs are golden brown, dry, and fragrant. Remove and drain on a slotted spoon.  
   **Note**: Must add ribs over low heat to prevent burning on the outside while staying raw inside; frying time should ensure the ribs are fully cooked and the surface is crispy.  
6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, keep the heat low, and stir-fry the dried chili pepper sections until they darken in color and release a spicy aroma.  
   **Note**: Keep on low heat throughout to avoid burning and bitterness.  
7. Add the minced garlic, ginger, chopped scallion, and diced green and red bell peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant, then add Lao Gan Ma and mix well.  
8. Add the fried ribs, along with chicken bouillon, a little more five-spice powder, and toasted sesame seeds. Increase the heat to high and quickly stir-fry until everything is well combined. Drizzle in a little scallion oil, add the cilantro sections, and stir-fry a few more times quickly before serving.  
   **Note**: Do not stir-fry the cilantro for too long after adding it, or the flavor will be greatly diminished.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and fat, suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, since it is deep-fried, the calorie content is high. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. People with cardiovascular diseases should control their intake. Although baking soda can tenderize the meat, it is not advisable for the elderly and children to consume too much.
