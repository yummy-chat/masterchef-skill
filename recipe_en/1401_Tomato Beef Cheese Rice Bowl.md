# Tomato Beef Cheese Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ground beef 200g
🍅 Tomato 2 (peeled and cut into chunks)
🧅 Onion 1/2 (finely chopped)
🍚 Rice 1 bowl
🧀 Cheese slices as needed
🧀 Mozzarella cheese large amount
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶 Black pepper to taste
🟡 Yellow curry 1 piece
🍶 Teriyaki sauce 1 tablespoon
🍅 Tomato paste large amount
🫒 Cooking oil as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Bowl (for blanching tomatoes and inverting rice)
🫖 Lid
🍴 Utensils (spoon/fork, for mixing and serving)

## Instructions
1.  Make a shallow cross cut on the top of the tomatoes, soak in boiling water until the skin curls, then peel off the skin. Remove the stem and cut into small chunks. Finely chop the onion.
   **Note**: The cross cut should be of moderate depth; soaking in boiling water makes peeling easy.
2.  Heat cooking oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Add the ground beef and stir-fry until the surface turns slightly browned and the meat is crumbled.
3.  Add the chopped onion and tomato chunks. Continue stir-frying until the vegetables soften and release their juices.
4.  Add salt, black pepper, the yellow curry piece, teriyaki sauce, and tomato paste one by one. Stir well to combine, allowing the seasonings and vegetable juices to form a rich sauce.
5.  Invert a bowl of rice into the center of the sauce. Place one cheese slice on top of the rice and generously sprinkle mozzarella cheese around it.
6.  Cover the pan tightly with the lid. Cook over medium-low heat for 2–3 minutes, until the cheese is completely melted.
   **Note**: Adjust the time based on the heat to avoid burning the bottom of the pan.
7.  Remove the lid. Fully mix the rice, sauce, and cheese together. Serve immediately.
   **Key Point**: Ensure thorough mixing so that every grain of rice is evenly coated with the sauce and cheese.

## Health Advice
This dish provides lycopene and iron from the tomatoes and beef, and calcium from the cheese. It is suitable for those looking to build muscle or boost energy. However, calorie density is relatively high, so it is recommended to control the amount of rice or pair it with a light vegetable salad for balanced nutrition.
