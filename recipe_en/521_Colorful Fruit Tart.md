# Colorful Fruit Tart
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Tart Crust
🎂 Cake flour 120g
🍞 Bread flour 120g
🍬 Caster sugar 20g
🧂 Salt 5g
🧈 Unsalted butter 140g (cold butter cut into small pieces)
🥚 Egg 1
🥛 Milk 25g

### Almond Butter Filling
🧈 Unsalted butter 200g (softened in advance)
🍬 Icing sugar 150g
🥛 Skim milk 8g
🥛 Sour cream 30g
🥚 Eggs 3 (brought to room temperature in advance)
🌿 Vanilla pod 1/4 (scrape out vanilla seeds)
🌰 Almond flour 200g
🍊 Orange zest, to taste

### Pastry Cream
🥚 Egg yolks 2
🍬 Caster sugar 30g
🎂 Cake flour 15g
🥛 Milk 200g
🌿 Vanilla pod 1/4
🧈 Unsalted butter 15g

### Decoration and Flavoring
🍉 Assorted fruits (blueberries, raspberries, orange, white pomelo, grapefruit, kiwi, banana, grapes, etc.), to taste
🍶 Cointreau orange liqueur, about 5~8g
🧈 Gelatin sheets, about 3.5g (combined with about 50g water to make a glaze)
🥛 Whipping cream 60~100g (for making whipped cream)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🔪 Spatula/cutting and mixing tool
🥄 Whisk (handheld)
⚖️ Digital kitchen scale
🫙 Plastic wrap
🪟 Rolling pin
🥧 Tart molds (various sizes)
🍴 Fork
🍳 Small saucepan
🔍 Strainer
🍊 Zester/grater
🔥 Oven
📦 Baking tray/rack
📰 Parchment paper
🪨 Baking beans/pie weights
🍶 Glass jar
🥣 Small square dish/bowl
🖌️ Brush
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Patterned plate

## Instructions
### Tart Crust
1. Mix bread flour, cake flour, caster sugar, and salt together. Add cold butter cut into small pieces, and use a spatula to cut and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
   **Key point**: Butter must remain cold to avoid melting; do not overmix.
2. Combine the egg and milk, then add gradually to the butter-flour mixture, gently kneading into a dough.
   **Judge the consistency**: The crumbly butter-flour mixture should come together into a dough once the liquid is absorbed.
3. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
   **Note**: Refrigerate, do not freeze, to avoid the dough becoming too hard.

### First Layer Filling
4. Combine softened butter with icing sugar, skim milk, and sour cream; mix well.
5. Bring the eggs to room temperature in advance, add the scraped vanilla seeds, and beat. Add the egg mixture to the butter mixture in several additions, mixing well each time to prevent separation.
6. Sift in the almond flour and mix with a cutting and folding motion. Finally, fold in the orange zest for aroma.

### Second Layer Filling
7. Whisk the egg yolks, caster sugar, and cake flour together into a smooth paste.
8. Add the vanilla pod and seeds to the milk, heat over low heat until just simmering, then remove the vanilla pod.
9. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture while whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the saucepan and heat over low heat, stirring continuously, until thickened and coats the back of a spatula.
10. Remove from heat, add the butter, and stir until melted and smooth. Cover with plastic wrap directly touching the surface and set aside to cool.

### Blind Baking the Crust and Assembly
11. Remove the chilled tart dough, roll it out to a size slightly larger than the mold. Line the tart mold, pressing the dough in to fit, and prick the bottom with a fork.
12. Line with parchment paper, fill with baking beans, and bake in a preheated oven at 170°C for about 30 minutes until set and golden.
13. Remove from the oven, take out the baking beans and paper, unmold, and let cool.
14. Spread an even layer of almond butter filling over the bottom of the tart crust. Bake again at 170°C for about 40 minutes.
15. Once out of the oven, brush the surface with Cointreau orange liqueur while still hot.

### Final Assembly
16. After the tart has completely cooled, layer on the pastry cream, then the whipped cream.
17. Carefully arrange the prepared fruits on top.
18. Soak the gelatin sheets in ice water until softened. Drain the water, then add about 50g of hot water and stir until the gelatin dissolves. Strain to make a glaze, and brush over the fruit to give it a shiny finish.
19. Refrigerate to set before serving.

## Health Advice
This colorful fruit tart is rich in vitamins and dietary fiber, but the butter, icing sugar, and cream are not low in calories. It is recommended to share as a party dessert rather than consuming an entire tart alone. Suitable for baking enthusiasts looking for a challenge; remember to control overall sugar intake.
