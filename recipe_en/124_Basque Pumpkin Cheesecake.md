# Basque Pumpkin Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🎃 Pumpkin puree 250g  
🧀 Cream cheese 210g (needs to be softened at room temperature)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 35g  
🌽 Cornstarch 15g  
🥚 Eggs 4 (about 200g, at room temperature)  
🥛 Heavy cream 170g (at room temperature)  
🥜 Walnuts a small handful (crushed and set aside)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Blender  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Measuring cup  
🍰 6-inch round cake pan (with removable bottom)  
🧻 Parchment paper  
🔥 Oven  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🔨 Sealed bag + rolling pin or knife handle (for crushing walnuts)

## Steps
1. Place pumpkin puree, cream cheese, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and eggs into the blender.  
   **Key Point**: All ingredients need to be taken out of the fridge beforehand and brought to room temperature to ensure even mixing.

2. Start the blender and blend until smooth and free of lumps.

3. Transfer the blended batter to another container, add the heavy cream, and mix evenly with a hand whisk.  
   **Key Point**: Heavy cream should be added last and mixed manually until well combined.

4. Line the cake pan with parchment paper, strain the mixed batter through a sieve, and pour it into the pan.  
   **Key Point**: Straining helps achieve a finer texture in the final product.

5. Place the pan in the preheated oven on the middle rack, bake at 225°C for 24 minutes, then increase to 230°C and bake for another 5 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: High-temperature baking creates the signature charred surface.

6. After baking, remove the cake and let it cool at room temperature.  
   **Key Point**: After cooling, refrigerate overnight for better flavor.

7. Before serving, sprinkle crushed walnuts on top for added crunch.

## Health Tips
The combination of pumpkin puree and cream cheese is rich in vitamin A and calcium, making it ideal for replenishing energy in autumn. After refrigeration, the texture resembles pumpkin cheesecake ice cream. Be mindful of portion sizes; the sugar amount has already been halved, so avoid overconsumption.
