# Green Pepper and Eggplant Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
💧 Warm water 260g (close to body temperature)
🫙 Yeast 5g
🍬 Sugar 5g (for dough)
🧂 Baking powder 3g (optional)
🫒 Cooking oil 15g (lard is better)
🍆 Eggplant 750g (choose tender-skinned ones)
🫑 Green peppers (spiral peppers) 250g (avoid thick-skinned ones)
🧅 Scallion 50g (chopped for filling)
🧂 Salt 2g (for stir-frying filling)
🫘 Soybean paste 50g
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g
🥄 Dark soy sauce 3g (for color)
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 20g
🌶️ Fine chili powder 10g
🍬 Sugar 6g (to enhance flavor)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g

## Cookware and Equipment
🫕 Stainless steel bowl (for kneading and fermenting dough)
🥣 Glass bowl (for holding dough and filling)
🍳 Wok
🥢 Rolling pin
♨️ Steamer (with steamer cloth)
⚖️ Kitchen scale

## Steps
1. Mix all-purpose flour and baking powder evenly. In a separate bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water, stirring until fully dissolved.
   **Key point**: Different flours have varying water absorption. Reserve a small amount of water and add as needed based on dough firmness.
2. Gradually pour the yeast water into the flour, stirring to form flocculent pieces. Add cooking oil and knead into a dough. The dough will be rough at this stage; this is normal.
   **Key point**: It is recommended to wear gloves when kneading.
3. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for 5 minutes. Take it out and knead briefly until smooth. Return to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and place in a warm spot to ferment until doubled in size.
   **Key point**: Fermentation time is not fixed. Check the state—the dough should double in volume, and a finger poke should not cause collapse.
4. Dice the eggplant into small cubes, dice the green peppers, and chop the scallion into small pieces.
   **Key point**: Eggplant cubes should not be too small, as they will shrink during stir-frying. Do not salt them in advance; add directly to the wok.
5. Heat oil in a wok, add the diced eggplant and green peppers along with salt, and stir-fry over high heat for 4-5 minutes until the moisture evaporates and the eggplant softens and becomes translucent.
6. Reduce to low heat. You may add a little more oil to make the filling shinier. Add soybean paste, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, coarse chili flakes, fine chili powder, sugar, and chicken bouillon. Stir-fry evenly until the aroma of the sauce emerges, then turn off the heat.
   **Key point**: Stir-fry the filling patiently to dry out the eggplant moisture. After turning off the heat, transfer the filling to the refrigerator to cool quickly, preventing oil leakage during wrapping.
7. Take out the fermented dough, place it on a work surface, and knead to release air. Roll the dough into a long thin strip, gather it back, and repeat 2-3 times until the dough is smooth and fine.
   **Key point**: Releasing air is key to a smooth and delicate bun skin.
8. Roll the dough into a thick long strip and divide into 14-16 pieces. Flatten each piece and cover with plastic wrap to prevent drying.
9. Take out the cooled eggplant filling and mix it with the prepared scallions.
   **Key point**: The filling should not be too hot, as it may kill the yeast in the dough skin.
10. Take one dough piece, dust with flour, and roll it with a rolling pin into a round wrapper that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges.
    **Key point**: The wrapper should not be rolled too thin; keep some thickness so the buns become fluffy after steaming.
11. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center of the wrapper. Use your thumb, index finger, and middle finger to pleat the edges, then pinch to seal tightly.
12. Place the wrapped buns in a steamer lined with steamer cloth, leaving space between them. Fill the steamer pot with water, heat until warm, then turn off the heat. Place the steamer basket inside, cover, and let the buns proof for a second time for 15 minutes.
    **Key point**: Use the warm water and residual heat in the pot for proofing; the heat should be off during this process. The proofed buns should visibly increase in size and spring back when lightly pressed.
13. After proofing, cover the steamer tightly, turn the heat to high, and start timing once the water fully boils and large amounts of steam emerge from the edges. Steam over high heat for 12 minutes.
14. Immediately turn off the heat when time is up. Leave the lid on and let the buns rest for another 2 minutes before opening and removing.
    **Key point**: Resting them helps prevent the buns from shrinking or collapsing due to sudden exposure to cold air.

## Health Advice
Eggplant is rich in dietary fiber, and green peppers provide vitamin C. However, soybean paste and oil content are relatively high, making this bun suitable as a meal replacement during a weight-loss period, but not to be eaten in excess. Those with blood sugar or blood pressure sensitivities should control their intake and reduce the amount of salt and sauce in the filling during cooking.
