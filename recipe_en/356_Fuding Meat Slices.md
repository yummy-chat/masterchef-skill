# Fuding Meat Slices
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 650g lean pork hind leg meat (all fat must be removed)
🌾 200g sweet potato or cassava starch (tender meat version); additional 180~250g (optional for chewy meat version)
💧 350ml ice water
🧅 Scallions, to taste
🌿 Cilantro, to taste
🌶️ Yellow chili pepper (with juice), to taste
🦐 Dried shrimp, to taste
🧂 1/2 tablespoon salt (for meat filling) + to taste (for bowl base)
🧂 1/2 tablespoon chicken bouillon
🌫️ White pepper, to taste (used in two portions)
🧄 Ginger, minced, to taste
🥄 3~5g baking soda
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥘 Metal tray
🫙 Plastic wrap
🌀 Large meat grinder
🍲 Cooking pot
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon/chopsticks
🍽️ Scraper or spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Pre-treat the meat**: Thoroughly remove all fat from the lean pork hind leg meat. Cut into small pieces and place in a metal tray. Cover with plastic wrap and freeze for 1–2 hours until the meat is icy.
2. **Grind the meat**: Place the frozen meat pieces into a large meat grinder. Add 1/2 tablespoon salt, 1/2 tablespoon chicken bouillon, white pepper to taste, and minced ginger to taste. Cover and grind for about 10 seconds until finely minced.
3. **Vigorously mix in water for gelatinization**: Add 3~5g baking soda to the minced meat. Gradually add 350ml ice water in three portions, mixing thoroughly after each addition until fully absorbed. Mix repeatedly until the meat paste becomes sticky, stringy, and shows obvious gelatinous texture.
**Note**: Do not run the meat grinder continuously; use a “mix a bit, pause a bit” method to prevent overheating.
4. **Incorporate starch**: Add 200g sweet potato or cassava starch. Continue mixing intermittently until the meat paste is sticky, stringy, and evenly blended. This is the tender meat version.
**Texture check**: The meat paste should be thick and form strings when lifted—this indicates success.
5. **Make the chewy meat version**: Based on the tender meat paste, add an additional 180~250g sweet potato starch. Stir continuously in one direction until the paste turns white and becomes firmer in texture. This version yields chewier meat slices.
6. **Cook the meat slices**: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Spread the meat paste evenly on a scraper. Use a spoon or chopsticks to scrape thin slices into the boiling water. Cook on high heat until all meat slices float and the soup returns to a boil, ensuring the meat is fully cooked.
**Key tips**: Scrape as thinly as possible for a smoother texture; the meat slices are fully cooked once they all float to the surface.
7. **Prepare the bowl base**: In a bowl, add chopped scallions, chopped cilantro, salt to taste, white pepper, yellow chili pepper, dried shrimp, and a little sesame oil. Pour in the hot boiling broth from cooking the meat slices and stir well.
8. **Serve**: Scoop the cooked meat slices into the prepared bowl and serve immediately.
9. **Store leftover meat paste**: Any uncooked meat paste can be stored in an airtight container and frozen for later use. Simply cook directly from frozen the next time.

## Health Tips
Lean pork hind leg meat, combined with ice water and vigorous mixing to create a gelatinous texture, provides high protein and low fat, making it suitable for muscle building and weight loss. Be careful not to use too much baking soda, as it can affect the texture. Use either sweet potato or cassava starch—the tender meat version offers a smooth, silky texture, while the chewy meat version is firmer. Choose according to personal preference.
