# Stuffed Fish Balls
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Ingredients
🐟 Basa fish 600g (scrape to obtain fish paste, can be substituted with other fish)
🐖 Pork 100g (minced for filling)

### Seasonings
#### For the Fish Paste
🧂 Salt 3g
💧 Water 90g (add in batches)
🌾 Sweet potato starch 150g (for a chewy texture)

#### For the Meat Filling
🧂 Salt 2g
🍬 Sugar 3g (to enhance freshness)
🌿 Ground pepper 3g
🌿 Ginger powder or minced ginger appropriate amount (to remove fishy smell)
🫒 Cooking oil 20g
🧅 Chopped scallions a handful

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Stockpot
🥣 Large bowl/glass bowl
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🥢 Chopsticks/mixer
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the basa fish, place it on a cutting board, and use the back of a knife to scrape the fish meat into a fine paste. Be careful to use the back of the knife, as the blade will bring in tendons and affect the texture. After scraping, remove any tendons.
   **Key points**: You must use the back of the knife to scrape; the blade will bring in tendons and affect the texture. Remove tendons after scraping.

2. Place the fish paste in a large bowl, add 3g salt and 90g water, then add 150g sweet potato starch, stirring while adding until there is no dry powder and the mixture is thick and sticky.
   **Key points**: The water must be added in batches and stirred until the mixture firms up. The starch should also be added gradually while stirring until the fish paste is thick and sticky.

3. Mince the pork and place it in a bowl. Add 2g salt, 3g sugar, 3g ground pepper, appropriate amount of ginger powder or minced ginger, 20g cooking oil, and a handful of chopped scallions. Stir in one direction until well combined.
   **Key points**: All seasonings should be stirred in the same direction to fully incorporate and firm up the meat filling.

4. Wet your hands slightly with water to prevent sticking. Take an appropriate amount of fish paste and flatten it. Place the prepared pork filling in the center. Use the Tiger's mouth (space between thumb and index finger) to gather the fish paste and wrap it around the meat filling. Pinch the opening tightly and shape it into a smooth, round ball.
   **Key points**: The opening must be pinched tightly to prevent the filling from leaking out during cooking.

5. Place the prepared fish balls one by one into a pot filled with cold water. After adding all of them, bring to a boil over high heat. Gently stir with a slotted spoon during cooking to prevent sticking. Once the water boils, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 3 minutes. Press the floating balls with a slotted spoon.
   **Key points**: They must be placed in cold water to ensure even heating and to prevent deformation. Press the balls during cooking to ensure the meat filling inside is fully cooked.
   **State check**: The balls are basically done when they float to the surface.

6. Remove the cooked fish balls with a slotted spoon, place them in a basin of cold water to rinse and cool down. Remove and drain.
   **Key points**: Cooling them down will make the fish balls more chewy.

## Health Advice
These stuffed fish balls, made with basa fish and pork filling, are rich in protein and moderate in fat, making them suitable for those who need nutritional supplementation but need to control calorie intake. Note that the amount of sweet potato starch is relatively high, so diabetic patients should consume them in moderation.
