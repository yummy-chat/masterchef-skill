# Chive-Flavored Snail Meat
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐚 Snail meat  
🥬 1 bunch of chives  
🌶️ 2-3 green long hot peppers  
🌶️ 2-3 red long hot peppers  
🧄 A few cloves of garlic  
🫚 1 small piece of ginger  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🫙 About 1 tablespoon of pickled wild pepper sauce  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed  
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🍬 About 1 teaspoon of sugar  
🌫️ A pinch of white pepper powder  
💧 Half a small bowl of water  
🫒 A little rapeseed oil  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥄 Strainer  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥣 Bowl  

## Steps
1. Place the snail meat in a basin, add salt, and rub repeatedly to remove impurities. Rinse with water until the water runs clear, then drain and set aside.  
   **Note**: Rub thoroughly and rinse until the water is clear to ensure impurities are completely removed.

2. Cut the chives into segments about 3 cm long. Halve the green and red long hot peppers, remove the seeds, and chop finely. Crush the garlic cloves and mince into garlic paste. Crush the ginger and mince into ginger paste. Combine the chopped peppers, garlic paste, and ginger paste and set aside.  
   **Note**: Crush and finely mince the ginger and garlic, and chop the peppers finely for better aroma release.

3. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the snail meat, and blanch for 30 seconds. Remove and drain thoroughly.  
   **Note**: Add to boiling water; do not blanch for too long—just enough to remove impurities and off-flavors.

4. In a separate wok, add a little rapeseed oil and heat to about 50% hot. Add the ginger paste and pickled wild pepper sauce, and stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.  
   **Note**: Rapeseed oil enhances aroma; the base ingredients must be stir-fried until fragrant.

5. Add the blanched snail meat and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface moisture evaporates and the snail meat appears slightly dry.  
   **Visual Cue**: The snail meat noticeably loses moisture and appears slightly dry on the surface for improved texture.

6. Splash in cooking wine and light soy sauce, stirring well. Add half a small bowl of water, salt, sugar, and white pepper powder. Simmer over medium heat for 2 minutes to infuse the snail meat with flavor, then remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: After seasoning, simmer thoroughly to allow the snail meat to absorb the flavors.

7. Add a little more rapeseed oil to the wok, then add the chopped green and red peppers and garlic paste. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.  
   **Note**: Sauté the aromatics until fragrant to create a base for the next steps.

8. Return the simmered snail meat and its juices to the wok. Increase the heat to high and stir-fry quickly for about ten turns to fully combine the snail meat with the aromatics.  
   **Note**: Stir-fry quickly over high heat to release aromas and ensure even blending.

9. Add the chive segments and stir-fry over high heat until the chives are just wilted and turn bright green. Remove from heat and plate immediately.  
   **Visual Cue**: The chives turn bright green with a crisp, tender texture.  
   **Note**: Do not overcook the chives to avoid sogginess and loss of texture.

## Health Advice
Chives are rich in vitamins and dietary fiber, while snail meat is high in protein and low in fat, making this dish suitable for nourishing the liver in spring. Ensure the snail meat is thoroughly cooked, and add the chives at the very end, stir-frying quickly to preserve their crisp texture.
