# Braised Beef in Brown Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef shoulder, about 750g (cut into mahjong-sized cubes, choose pieces with even fat and sinew)
🥔 Yellow-skinned potatoes, 2 (peeled and cut into chunks)
🥕 Carrot, 1 (peeled and cut into chunks)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices (for braising, remove at the end)
🧅 Onion, half or 1 (for braising, remove at the end)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 1 small handful (adjust to taste)
🧂 Baking soda, 1 teaspoon
🧂 Salt, 1 tablespoon (for marinating) + about 1/4 teaspoon (adjust when stir-frying)
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon (for caramelizing)
🥜 Vegetable oil, about 3 tablespoons
🥫 Sa cha sauce, 1 teaspoon (optional substitute: Pixian broad bean paste or Tom Yum paste)
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder, 1/8 teaspoon
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder, to taste (about a spoonful)
🧂 Soy sauce, to taste
💧 Boiling water, enough to just cover the beef

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🍲 Stockpot (for blanching)
🍳 Wok
♨️ Pressure cooker
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🥄 Cooking spatula/spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Add 1 teaspoon baking soda and 1 tablespoon salt to the beef cubes. Mix thoroughly with your hands and let marinate for a while.
   **Key point**: Baking soda and salt tenderize the meat, making the beef softer.
2. Place the marinated beef in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove the beef with a slotted spoon and drain.
   **Key point**: Blanching in cold water effectively removes blood and gamey odors.
3. Pour about 3 tablespoons of oil into a wok. Add 1 tablespoon of sugar. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar melts into a jujube-red color and small bubbles form.
   **Key point**: If the caramel is too light, the color won't develop; if overcooked, it will taste bitter.
4. Immediately add the blanched beef and stir-fry quickly, coating each piece evenly with the caramel.
5. Add the sa cha sauce, thirteen-spice powder, Sichuan peppercorn powder, dried chili peppers, and about 1/4 teaspoon salt. Stir-fry until fragrant.
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of salt based on the saltiness of the sauce you choose.
6. Pour in enough boiling water to just cover the beef. Add the ginger slices, onion, and soy sauce to taste. Stir well, then transfer all ingredients into a pressure cooker.
7. Cover the pressure cooker. Bring to high pressure over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for 20–25 minutes. Turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally before opening the lid.
   **Key point**: This step only braises the beef, ensuring the meat reaches the desired tenderness.
8. Remove the cooked ginger slices and onion. Add the potato and carrot chunks, gently stirring to submerge them in the liquid.
9. Cover the pressure cooker again. Bring to high pressure over high heat, then cook for 3 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally, then open the lid and serve.
   **Key point**: Potatoes and carrots cook quickly; a short pressure cook yields soft, intact pieces.

## Health Advice
Braised beef in brown sauce is rich in high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for blood enrichment and muscle building. However, as the potatoes and sauce contain a fair amount of fat, control your portion size. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad for balanced nutrition.
