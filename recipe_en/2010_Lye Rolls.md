# Lye Rolls
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 510 grams (120 grams for the starter, 390 grams for the main dough; can be partially substituted with French bread flour)
🧂 Salt 7 grams (1 gram for the starter, 6 grams for the main dough), plus an additional 20 grams for the lye water
💧 Water 240 grams (70 grams for the starter, 170 grams for the main dough)
🍄 Active dry yeast approx. 5.2 grams (0.2 grams for the starter, 5 grams for the main dough)
🧈 Unsalted butter 40 grams (softened before adding to the main dough)
🧀 Cream cheese approx. 25 grams per roll (for cranberry cream cheese filling)
🍒 Dried cranberries about a dozen per roll (for cranberry cream cheese filling)
🧀 Cheddar cheese, mozzarella cheese, corn sausage as needed (for ham and cheese filling; recommend using generously)
🫘 Honey red beans as needed (for red bean lye sticks)
🧪 Food-grade lye (sodium hydroxide) 22 grams

## Tools & Equipment
🪵 Mixing bowl (wooden bowl)
🔪 Dough scraper
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🫓 Rolling pin
🫙 Plastic wrap or damp cloth
📦 Baking sheet
📜 Parchment paper (high-temperature resistant; do not use regular baking paper)
🪒 Blade (razor blade)
✂️ Scissors
🧤 Baking gloves
❄️ Refrigerator
🔥 Oven

## Instructions
1. Prepare the starter: Mix 120 grams all-purpose flour, 1 gram salt, 70 grams water, and 0.2 grams yeast until no dry flour remains. Seal and refrigerate for at least 12 hours. For quicker use, increase yeast to 1 gram and ferment at 30°C (86°F) for 1-2 hours until the interior shows a rich honeycomb structure.

2. Knead the main dough: Tear the fermented starter into pieces and place in a bowl. Add 170 grams water, 5 grams yeast, 390 grams all-purpose flour, and 6 grams salt. Stir until shaggy, then add 40 grams softened butter and knead into a dough. When the dough becomes firm, use a dough scraper to repeatedly cut and reassemble it about 9 times until the surface is relatively smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and refrigerate for 15 minutes to relax.

3. Divide and pre-shape: Remove the dough and flatten it. Divide evenly into 10 portions. Round each portion, tucking the seams underneath. Place on a baking sheet, seal, and refrigerate for 15 minutes to relax.

4. Shape:
   - Cranberry Cream Cheese Rolls: Flatten and gently roll out a portion. Wrap in cream cheese and dried cranberries. Pinch the seams tightly to seal.
   - Ham and Cheese Rolls: Same as above, but fill with a mixture of cheese cubes and corn sausage.
   - Red Bean Lye Sticks: Roll out the dough into a long oval, flip it over. Spread honey red beans on the front two-thirds, then roll up and taper both ends.
   - Lye Pretzels: Roll the dough into a rope about 50 cm (20 inches) long, with a thicker middle and thinner ends. Lift both ends, cross them twice, then press the ends onto the top sides and pinch to secure.
   Work quickly during shaping to prevent the dough from softening at room temperature.

5. Freeze for setting: Place the shaped dough pieces on a baking sheet, cover with plastic wrap, and freeze for at least 30 minutes until firm.

6. Prepare lye solution and dip: In a container, first pour 600 grams of warm water at 40°C (104°F), then slowly add 22 grams food-grade lye and 20 grams salt. Stir until completely dissolved. Wear gloves throughout this process and keep the lye solution away from children. Dip the frozen dough pieces into the lye solution for 15-20 seconds, then remove and drain.

7. Score and bake: Line a baking sheet with high-temperature parchment paper and place the dough pieces on it. For pretzels, make a horizontal slash across the thick part. For red bean sticks, make three vertical slashes. For round rolls, use scissors to cut a cross on top until the filling is exposed.
   - Conventional oven: Top heat 220°C (428°F), bottom heat 200°C (392°F), bake for about 15 minutes.
   - Convection oven: 180°C (356°F), bake for 13-15 minutes.
   Bake until the surface turns deep reddish-brown and the slashed areas show white cracks.

8. Finish: The finished rolls have a crispy crust and a dense, chewy interior, with a rich wheat aroma and a savory, slightly bitter finish.

## Health Advice
These lye rolls are rich in carbohydrates and protein, making them suitable for energy replenishment before or after exercise. However, their sodium content is high, so individuals sensitive to blood pressure should limit consumption. Always wear gloves when handling the lye solution to prevent skin burns.
