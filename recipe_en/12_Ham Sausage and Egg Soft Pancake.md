# Ham Sausage and Egg Soft Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌭 Ham sausage, appropriate amount (diced into small pieces)
💯 Cold water, 3 tablespoons
🌾 Flour, 1 tablespoon
🥚 Eggs, 3 pieces
🌿 Scallions or cilantro, appropriate amount (chopped)
🧂 Salt, 3 grams
🍖 Small sausage bits, a pinch (to enhance flavor)
🍶 White vinegar, a dash (to remove eggy smell and add fluffiness)
🛢️ Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for batter and for greasing the pan)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing batter)
🥛 Measuring cup (for measuring cold water)
🥄 Soup spoon (for measuring water, flour, and batter)
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring)
🫗 Small bowl (for beating eggs)
🍳 Iron pan (for cooking pancakes)
🔪 Spatula (for flipping)
🧻 Kitchen paper (for greasing and absorbing oil)

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the diced ham sausage into a large bowl. Add 3 tablespoons of cold water and stir until evenly combined. Then add 1 tablespoon of flour and stir with chopsticks until a smooth batter forms with no lumps.
   **Key Point**: Adding cold water to the ham sausage first is the secret to a soft pancake texture.
2. Add 3 grams of salt and a pinch of small sausage bits to the batter, and stir well to season.
3. Crack 3 eggs into a small bowl, add a dash of white vinegar, and whisk until the eggs are beaten and smooth.
   **Note**: White vinegar helps remove the eggy smell and makes the eggs fluffier.
4. Pour the beaten eggs into the batter. Add chopped scallions or cilantro and a drizzle of cooking oil. Stir to form a batter of moderate consistency.
   **Note**: Adding oil makes the pancake more fragrant.
5. Heat the iron pan until it starts to smoke. Pour in a tablespoon of cold oil and swirl the pan to coat the sides evenly. Once obvious oil streaks appear on the pan's edge, pour out the hot oil.
   **Key Point**: This "hot pan, cold oil" technique creates a non-stick layer and provides excellent anti-stick results. Then use kitchen paper to wipe away any remaining oil from the pan.
6. Place the pan over the lowest heat. Scoop a spoonful of batter into the pan and quickly rotate the pan to spread it into a round, thin pancake.
7. When bubbles appear on the pancake's surface and the edges begin to lift naturally, flip it over and cook for about 3 to 5 seconds, until both sides are golden brown and cooked through.
8. Fold the cooked pancake, remove it from the pan, and serve on a plate. Enjoy while hot.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced combination of carbohydrates and protein, making it an ideal quick breakfast for busy office workers or students. Note that ham sausage and small sausage bits are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure may reduce the amount or opt for low-sodium versions.
