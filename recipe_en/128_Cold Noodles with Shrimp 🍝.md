# Cold Noodles with Shrimp 🍝
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍝 1 bundle of spaghetti (single serving)
🦐 20-30g fresh or frozen shrimp (peeled and deveined)
🧄 3-4 cloves of garlic (coarsely minced)
🥒 1 cucumber (julienned)
🧅 2 scallions (chopped into green onions)

## Seasonings
🧂 1/8 teaspoon salt (for marinating shrimp)
🧂 1/4 teaspoon salt (for dry seasoning mix)
🧂 A pinch of salt (for frying garlic)
🌶 1/8 teaspoon white pepper powder
🧂 1 teaspoon cornstarch
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (to toss noodles after boiling, about 3 tablespoons for frying garlic, and for the final hot oil pour)
🌶 1 tablespoon chili powder
🧂 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
🫒 1 teaspoon sesame oil
🥣 1 tablespoon Chinkiang vinegar
🥣 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
🧂 1 tablespoon fish sauce
🍬 1 teaspoon sugar
🧂 2 tablespoons water

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Soup pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Large bowl
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Instructions
1. Bring water to a boil in a soup pot. Add the spaghetti, leave the lid slightly ajar, reduce to low heat, and cook for 7 minutes. Drain well, then toss with a little cooking oil to prevent sticking.

2. Rinse the shrimp. Add salt, white pepper powder, and cornstarch, then mix well and let marinate briefly.

3. Coarsely mince the garlic, chop the scallions into green onions, and julienne the cucumber.

4. In a large bowl, combine the chopped scallions, chili powder, sesame seeds, and salt to make the dry seasoning mix. Set aside.

5. Pour about 3 tablespoons of cooking oil into a wok. Add the minced garlic to the cold oil with a pinch of salt. Stir-fry slowly over low heat until the garlic turns golden brown.
   **Key point**: Remove the garlic immediately when it is nearly golden but still slightly white in the center; residual heat will continue to cook it into golden, crispy garlic bits.

6. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the marinated shrimp and stir-fry over medium heat. Remove as soon as the shrimp are completely pink and curled to avoid overcooking.

7. Continue heating the oil left in the wok until any moisture evaporates and the oil surface begins to smoke slightly. Immediately pour the hot oil over the dry seasoning mix in the bowl from step 4 to release the aromas.

8. Into the same bowl with the oiled seasoning, add the sesame oil, Chinkiang vinegar, light soy sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and water. Stir well to make the seasoning sauce.

9. Add the cooked noodles to a serving bowl, top with the shrimp and cucumber shreds, pour the seasoning sauce over everything, and sprinkle with the crispy fried garlic bits. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This cold noodle dish with shrimp is high in protein and low in fat, making it a refreshing and appetite-stimulating meal for summer, suitable for those on a weight loss diet. Marinating the shrimp ensures a tender and smooth texture, while the crispy fried garlic bits add extra flavor. However, be mindful of the amount of oil used.
