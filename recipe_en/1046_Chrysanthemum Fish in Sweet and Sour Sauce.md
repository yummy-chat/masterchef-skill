# Chrysanthemum Fish in Sweet and Sour Sauce
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Grass carp 1 (take the skin-on fillet, about 250g)
🧄 Garlic 2 cloves (minced)
🧂 Salt to taste
🍶 Ground white pepper to taste
🥔 Potato starch as needed (for coating)
🍅 Ketchup 1 tablespoon
🍬 White sugar 2 tablespoons
🍶 White vinegar a little
💧 Water ½ tablespoon
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for deep-frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife/slicing knife
🪓 Round wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🔥 Induction cooktop

## Preparation Steps
1. **Cut the fish**: Take one skin-on grass carp fillet. Slice diagonally into large connected pieces, then cut along the grain into strips to form a chrysanthemum shape. Soak in water to wash off blood, then drain.
   **Key point**: Ensure the bottom of each slice remains attached to the skin to prevent falling apart.
2. **Marinate and coat**: Sprinkle a little salt and ground white pepper over the chrysanthemum-shaped fish, let marinate briefly, then evenly coat with a thick layer of potato starch, making sure every crevice is covered.
   **Key point**: The coating should be thick and even to achieve the chrysanthemum shape after frying.
3. **Deep-fry**: Heat plenty of oil in a wok to 160°C (320°F). Hold the fish tail and let the flesh hang naturally. First, drizzle hot oil over the fish to set the shape, then place the whole piece into the oil. Fry over medium-low heat for about 3 minutes until set and cooked through. Remove. Raise the oil temperature, then fry again until the surface turns slightly yellow and crispy. Remove, drain oil, and arrange on a plate.
   **Key point**: Initial frying sets the shape; the second fry removes excess oil and increases crispiness.
4. **Prepare the sweet and sour sauce**: Leave a little oil in the wok, add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add ketchup, white sugar, water, and white vinegar. Heat over 1300W on an induction cooktop, stirring continuously, until the sugar is fully dissolved and the sauce thickens.
5. **Plate**: Pour the prepared sweet and sour sauce evenly over the fried chrysanthemum fish and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein and contains a small amount of minerals, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost or who enjoy a crispy texture. However, deep-frying and the sweet and sour sauce are high in calories. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables to control overall fat intake.
