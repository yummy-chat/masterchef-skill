# Orleans Chicken Pizza
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍞 High-gluten flour 3000g (can substitute with all-purpose flour)  
🍞 Low-gluten flour 1500g (can substitute with all-purpose flour)  
🧂 Salt 30g (for dough)  
🍬 Sugar 250g (for dough)  
🍄 Yeast 50g  
💧 Water 2400g  
🧈 Butter 300g (or cooking oil)  
🧄 Garlic 8 heads (minced)  
🧅 Onions 5 (minced)  
🍅 Tomatoes 15 (peeled and minced)  
🍗 Chicken thighs 12 (deboned)  
🌶️ Green bell peppers to taste (sliced into rings)  
🌶️ Red bell peppers to taste (sliced into rings)  
🧀 Shredded cheese to taste  
🍗 Orleans seasoning powder 140g  
🧂 Salt 25g (for sauce)  
🍬 Sugar 25g (for sauce)  
⚫ Black pepper 15g (for sauce)  
🫗 Soy sauce 40g (for sauce)  
🌿 Bay leaves 4 (for sauce)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🪣 Large stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍴 Fork  
🥟 Steamer basket  
🥡 Food processor  
🍳 Wok and frying pan  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🛡️ Baking rack or cooling rack  

## Steps
1. **Make the crust**
   - In a mixing bowl, combine high-gluten flour, low-gluten flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough.
   - Once the dough comes together, add butter and continue kneading until the surface is smooth and not sticky.
   - Place the dough in a warm area, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise until doubled in volume.

2. **Make the pizza base sauce**
   - Use a food processor to mince the garlic, onions, and tomatoes separately.
   - Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add minced garlic and stir until fragrant. Then add minced onions and tomatoes, followed by salt, sugar, black pepper, soy sauce, and bay leaves.
   - Reduce heat to low, stir continuously, and cook until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat and set aside.

3. **Prepare the Orleans chicken**
   - Debone the chicken thighs and place them in a bowl. Add the Orleans seasoning powder, mix well, and marinate for 2 hours.
   - Heat a frying pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken thighs and cook until both sides are golden brown. Remove, let cool, and cut into small pieces.

4. **Precook the crust**
   - Deflate the risen dough and divide it into portions of about 250g each.
   - Roll each portion into a ball, let it rest briefly, then roll it out into a round disc. Use your fingers to push up the edges to form a rim. Prick the bottom evenly with a fork.
   - Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Line the steamer basket with parchment paper, place the dough disc inside, cover, and steam for 12 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 3 minutes before removing.

5. **Assemble the pizza**
   - Place the steamed crust on a work surface and spread a layer of pizza sauce evenly over it.
   - Sprinkle a layer of shredded cheese on top of the sauce.
   - Arrange the cooked Orleans chicken pieces on top.
   - Add rings of green and red bell peppers.
   - Finally, sprinkle a generous layer of shredded cheese to cover all the ingredients.

6. **Package and store**
   - Place the assembled pizza into a vacuum-seal bag, remove the air, and seal it.
   - Freeze immediately. Before eating, thaw the pizza, then bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 18 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the surface is golden brown.

## Health Advice
This pizza is rich in protein and the calcium from the cheese, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise. However, the crust and sauce contain relatively high sugar and fat, so it's recommended to eat only half a serving during weight loss. Note that the Orleans seasoning powder already contains salt, so reduce the amount of salt in the sauce to prevent excessive sodium intake.
