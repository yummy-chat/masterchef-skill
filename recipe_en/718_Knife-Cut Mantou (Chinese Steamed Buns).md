# Knife-Cut Mantou (Chinese Steamed Buns)

Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 500g all-purpose flour
💧 250ml water
🍬 10g sugar (to aid fermentation)
🧪 3-5g dry yeast (adjust by season; increase to 5g in winter)
🧂 2g baking powder (optional, for fluffier texture)
🐖 Approximately 15g lard (optional, for whiteness, softness, and shine)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🥣 Mixing bowl
🫓 Rolling pin
🔪 Knife
🍽️ Silicone mat
🫖 Bamboo steamer
🧻 Silicone steamer liner
🛡️ Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix water, sugar, and dry yeast in a measuring cup, stirring until the yeast is completely dissolved to create a yeast mixture.
   **Note**: Adjust the yeast amount according to the season; increase to 5g in winter.
2. Pour the flour into a mixing bowl. If using, add the baking powder and lard.
   **Note**: Both baking powder and lard are optional additions to enhance the fluffiness and shine of the mantou.
3. Slowly pour the yeast mixture into the flour, stir with chopsticks until it forms flaky pieces, then knead by hand into a smooth dough.
   **Condition Check**: The dough surface should be smooth, slightly white, and soft in texture. Southern-style mantou dough is typically softer.
4. Return the kneaded dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and place in a warm spot for the first fermentation.
   **Condition Check**: Ferment until the volume doubles and the inside is filled with honeycomb-like air pockets.
   **Key Point**: Judge fermentation completion by the dough's condition, not solely by time. Inadequate first fermentation cannot be compensated for in later steps.
5. Remove the fermented dough, press to release gas, then roll it out into a sheet with a rolling pin. Roll it up from one end into a long log.
   **Note**: This shaping process expels gas and prepares the dough for the second fermentation.
6. Use a knife to cut the long dough log into evenly sized mantou pieces. Place them into the steamer lined with a steamer liner.
7. Cover the steamer lid and let the mantou undergo the second fermentation.
   **Time**: Rest at room temperature for 20-30 minutes; extend to 30-40 minutes in cold weather.
   **Condition Check**: The pieces should visibly increase in size and quickly spring back when lightly pressed with a finger.
   **Key Point**: This is the second crucial step for fluffy mantou; ensure fermentation is adequate.
8. Add water to the pot and place the steamer on top. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and steam for 20 minutes.
   **Note**: If the second fermentation is thorough, the initial water temperature is not critical.
9. Turn off the heat and let the mantou sit covered for 2 minutes before opening the lid.
   **Key Point**: Prevents the mantou from shrinking or wrinkling due to the temperature difference between inside and outside.

## Health Tips
Mantou are refined carbohydrates. It is recommended to pair them with eggs or soy milk to increase protein intake and prevent blood sugar spikes. Substitute 1/3 of the flour with whole wheat flour for more dietary fiber.
