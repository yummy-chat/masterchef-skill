# Braised Pork in Aged Vinegar
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly approx. 1500g (leaner cut, cut into large chunks)
🧅 Scallions as needed (scallion segments for braising, chopped scallions for garnish)
🫚 Ginger as needed (sliced)
🍊 Dried tangerine peel as needed (the aged, the more aromatic)
🌶️ Hot peppers as needed (for meat-stuffed flatbread)
🫓 Flatbread 2 pieces (for meat-stuffed flatbread)
🥄 Light soy sauce as needed
🧂 Salt approx. 10g
🍬 Rock sugar as needed (cannot be too little)
🍶 Yellow wine 3 tablespoons (naturally brewed, do not use cooking wine containing alcohol or caramel color)
🍇 Black vinegar (aged vinegar) 1 tablespoon (more is not better)
🧴 Dark soy sauce as needed (for color)
💧 Water as needed (must completely submerge the meat chunks)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🫕 Clay pot
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥢 Chopsticks (to test doneness)
✂️ Tweezers (to remove hair)
🥄 Ladle or soup spoon (for adding seasonings, skimming fat)
🥣 Bowl (to hold skimmed fat)
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1.  Rinse the pork belly. Place in a pot with cold water, add enough water to cover generously. Bring to a boil over high heat, then maintain a vigorous boil for 20-30 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the skin. This removes any gamey smell from the skin.
    **Note**: Adjust boiling time based on the thickness of the meat chunks; ensure they are cooked through.

2.  Remove the pork and rinse thoroughly under cold water. If there are any remaining hairs on the skin, pluck them out with tweezers. Cut the pork into fairly large square chunks.
    **Key point**: Do not cut the chunks too small, or they will break apart easily during the long braising process with vinegar.

3.  Place the pork chunks into the clay pot. Add the scallion segments, sliced ginger, dried tangerine peel, light soy sauce, salt, rock sugar, and dark soy sauce.
    **Key point**: Use sufficient amounts of salt and rock sugar. It is better to be slightly saltier than bland.

4.  Pour in 3 tablespoons of yellow wine and 1 tablespoon of black vinegar.
    **Key point**: Vinegar is crucial. 1 tablespoon is enough; too much will leave a lingering sour taste.

5.  Add water until all the meat chunks are completely submerged, preventing the skin from being exposed and becoming tough.

6.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and braise for 30 minutes.

7.  Uncover and remove the softened scallion segments. Re-cover and continue braising over low heat for another 40 minutes, until the meat is extremely tender and most of the fat has rendered out.

8.  Using a ladle or spoon, skim off most of the floating oil from the pot to prevent scorching when reducing the sauce.

9.  Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. The liquid will gradually thicken. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom. The high heat helps the vinegar smell to dissipate.

10. Near the end of the reduction, sprinkle in a generous amount of chopped scallions, stir-fry to combine evenly, then turn off the heat.

11. Chop the hot peppers. Chop the braised pork. Mix the chopped pork with the chopped hot peppers and stuff them into the flatbread. Drizzle with the meat sauce.

## Health Advice
This Braised Pork in Aged Vinegar uses the acidity of the vinegar to cut the richness, aiding digestion and cardiovascular health. It is suitable for people needing an energy boost, but those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Skimming off the floating fat during braising reduces the calorie content, and pairing it with flatbread provides a more balanced meal.
