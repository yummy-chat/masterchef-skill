# Rice Cooker Ciabatta
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Flour 500g  
🍬 White Sugar 5g  
🧂 Yeast Powder 3g  
💧 Warm Water 270g  
🥩 Pork appropriate amount (minced into filling)  
🍆 Eggplant appropriate amount (diced)  
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (diced)  
🌶️ Hot Pepper appropriate amount (diced)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (minced)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (used twice: to draw out water, to season filling)  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🌿 Thirteen Spices a pinch  
🫒 Sesame Oil appropriate amount  
🧴 Light Soy Sauce appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍚 Rice Cooker (with "Wood Fire Rice" or "Cook Rice" function)  
🪵🔪 Cutting Board, Chef's Knife  
🥣 Large Basin or Bowl (for mixing filling)  
🥢🥄 Chopsticks, Spatula (for stirring, flipping)  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Knead the dough: In the rice cooker inner pot, pour in flour, white sugar, yeast powder, add warm water, stir into a dough with chopsticks, and continue beating until the dough becomes elastic and the surface is smooth.  
   **Note**: Warm water helps activate the yeast; thorough beating forms the gluten network.  
2.  Proofing: Cover with the lid and let it rest and proof for 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: White sugar promotes fermentation, making the finished product fluffier.  
3.  Prepare the filling: Mince the pork into a paste in a basin; mix the diced eggplant with a pinch of salt, let it sit for a moment, then squeeze out the water thoroughly, and combine with the pork mince; then add minced scallion and ginger, diced carrot, and diced hot pepper.  
   **Key point**: Eggplant must be salted to draw out water and squeezed completely dry to remove the astringent taste and prevent the filling from releasing water and turning black.  
4.  Season: Add a pinch of salt, MSG, thirteen spices, sesame oil, and light soy sauce to the filling, and stir evenly in one direction with chopsticks.  
5.  Assemble: Take out the dough that has proofed to double its size, dig a well in the center, spread the seasoned meat filling inside and press it firm; gather the dough edges towards the center, pinch tightly to seal, fully enclosing the filling, then lightly press into a round cake shape.  
   **Key point**: The filling should be ample, and the dough must have good elasticity; press the dough cake slightly thin for even cooking.  
6.  First searing: Place the dough cake into the rice cooker, cover the lid, and press the "Wood Fire Rice" button. After the program ends, open the lid to check; the cake surface should be slightly set.  
7.  Flip and sear: Use a spatula to flip the cake, cover the lid again, press the "Wood Fire Rice" button again, and cook until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.  
8.  Simmer: After the second program ends, utilize the residual heat inside the pot to continue simmering briefly, then remove from the pot.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish cleverly combines carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables, offering balanced nutrition, suitable as a satiating meal for a fast-paced lifestyle. Note that the eggplant must be thoroughly drained of water, otherwise, the bottom of the cake may become soggy and affect the texture when cooked in the rice cooker. It is recommended to serve with a clear soup or salad to reduce greasiness.
