# Mango Lava Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 250g  
🍬 Granulated sugar 20g (for dough)  
🧂 Salt 4g  
🥛 Milk powder 8g  
🥭 Mango puree 170g (about 1 ripe large mango, flesh blended into a slightly chunky puree, divided equally into two portions: one for dough, one for filling)  
🥛 Cold milk about 100g  
🥚 Egg yolk 1  
🍞 Dry yeast 3g (or fresh yeast 8g)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (softened)  
🥭 Dried mango about 20g (cut into small pieces)  
🌰 Almond flour 23g  
🌾 Cake flour 5g  
🥚 Egg white 1 (about 35g)  
🧁 Heavy cream 200g  
🍶 Mayonnaise or softened butter as needed (for shaping dough)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 35g (for crispy topping)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 15g (for filling)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥤 Blender  
🍞 Stand mixer  
🔥 Oven (steam oven preferred)  
🍞 250g loaf pan  
🥄 Hand whisk  
🥄 Spatula  
👝 Piping bag  
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
### Prepare Mango Puree
Peel and pit the ripe mango, take about 160–170g of flesh, place in a blender, and pulse briefly until it becomes a slightly chunky puree. Divide equally into two portions and set aside.

### Knead the Dough
Combine bread flour, 20g sugar, salt, and milk powder in the stand mixer bowl. Add one portion of mango puree, cold milk, and egg yolk. Mix on low speed until a dough forms. Add the dry yeast, then knead on low then medium-high speed until the dough reaches the early development stage. Add softened unsalted butter, knead on low until incorporated, then switch to high speed and knead until full development is achieved.
**Key point**: Control dough temperature throughout; you can place an ice pack around the bowl.

### First Fermentation
Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a container, and ferment at 30°C in a humid environment for about 1 hour, until doubled in size. Test by dipping a finger in flour and poking the dough; it should not spring back or collapse.

### Degas, Divide, and Rest
Remove the fermented dough, gently press to release gas, divide into 2 equal pieces, shape each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 20 minutes.
**Key point**: Proper resting makes the dough easier to shape and results in a softer texture.

### Final Shaping
Flatten a rested piece of dough, flip it over, spread a little mayonnaise or softened butter in the center, and sprinkle half the dried mango pieces over it. Fold the edges of the dough toward the center, pinch the seams tightly, flip it over, and roll into a ball.
**Key point**: Use a non-rolling shaping method by simply wrapping the filling to maintain a soft texture; ensure the bottom is tightly sealed to prevent leakage during baking.

### Final Fermentation
Place the shaped dough seam-side down into a 250g loaf pan, gently press to fit. Ferment at 38°C with over 75% humidity for about 1 hour, until the dough fills 80–90% of the pan.

### Prepare Almond Crispy Topping and Apply
During fermentation, make the crispy batter: Mix egg white with 35g sugar using a hand whisk until the sugar dissolves, then add almond flour and cake flour, stirring into a pourable batter. Once fermentation is complete, evenly drizzle the crispy batter over the dough surface.

### Baking
Preheat the oven in advance, place the loaf pan inside, and bake for about 25 minutes, until the top and bottom are evenly browned. Remove from the oven and immediately tap the pan to release steam, unmold the bread, and cool on a wire rack.

### Prepare Mango Cream Filling
Add the other portion of mango puree, heavy cream, and 15g sugar to the blender, blend until thick but still pourable and smooth.

### Assembly
Once the bread is completely cool, insert a chopstick from the top center and gently move it around inside to create a hollow space. Transfer the mango cream filling to a piping bag, pipe it into the bread until it feels full. Refrigerate briefly to set. Before serving, dust the surface with powdered sugar or milk powder.

## Health Advice
This mango lava toast is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, but high in sugar and cream. It is recommended to enjoy as a small afternoon serving. Diabetics should control intake. The natural sweetness of mango puree allows for a possible reduction in sugar. Each 100g contains about 400 kcal, making it suitable for post-exercise energy replenishment.
