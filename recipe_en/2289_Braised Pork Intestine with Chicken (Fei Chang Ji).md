# Braised Pork Intestine with Chicken (Fei Chang Ji)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🐖 Pork Intestine about 1 bowl (thoroughly cleaned)
🐔 Free-range Chicken 1 whole (cut into uniform pieces)
🥔 Potatoes 2-3 (cut into rolling chunks)
🥬 Konjac 1 piece (sliced)
🧄 Scallions 2 (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (sliced)
🧄 Garlic 1 head (sliced; garnish with garlic sprouts at the end)
🫙 Pickled Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)
🌶️ Pickled Chili Peppers 1 small handful (cut into sections)
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers about 1 small handful (for stir-frying and oil splashing)
🫑 Dried Sichuan Peppercorns about 1 small handful (for stir-frying and oil splashing)
🌿 Garlic Sprouts or Scallions 2 stalks (chopped, for garnish at the end)
🍂 Spice Sachet 1 (containing star anise, sand ginger, cinnamon, and bay leaves)

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt to taste (for cleaning intestine and seasoning)
🌾 Flour 1 handful (for cleaning intestine)
🍶 White Vinegar to taste (for cleaning intestine)
🍶 Cooking Wine to taste (for blanching to remove odor)
🫘 Broad Bean Paste about 1 tablespoon (to stir-fry until red oil releases)
🥄 Light Soy Sauce to taste (to enhance freshness)
🥄 Dark Soy Sauce a small amount (for coloring)
🌿 White Pepper Powder to taste (to remove odor and enhance aroma)
🛢️ Cooking Oil to taste (for stir-frying and oil splashing)

## Tools and Equipment
🍳 Large Iron Wok
🪓 Cutting Board
🔪 Chef's Knife
🥄 Ladle/Spatula
🪣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥢 Slotted Spoon/Strainer
🥣 Large Bowl/Roasting Pan

## Instructions
1.  Place the pork intestine into a bowl. Add salt, flour, and white vinegar. Rub vigorously on both sides 3-4 times to thoroughly remove slime and odor. Rinse clean with water and set aside.
2.  Rinse the whole chicken. Split it in half, then chop into evenly sized pieces. Cut potatoes into rolling chunks and slice the konjac. Cut scallions into sections; slice ginger and garlic heads. Slice pickled ginger and cut pickled chili peppers into sections or slices.
3.  Place the pork intestine into a pot of cold water. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, cooking wine, and the spice sachet. Bring to a boil over high heat and skim off any foam. Reduce to medium heat and cook for about 30 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce the intestine.
4.  Remove the cooked intestine to cool. Cut into sections and set aside.
5.  Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry quickly until the chicken changes color, the juices evaporate, and the oil in the wok becomes clear.
6.  Create a space in the center of the chicken pieces. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, pickled ginger, pickled chili peppers, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry until fragrant, then combine with the chicken and stir-fry together. Add one tablespoon of broad bean paste and continue stir-frying until red oil and aroma develop.
7.  Add the pork intestine sections and stir-fry evenly. Pour in enough water. Season with salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes.
8.  Remove the lid. Add the konjac and potatoes. Gently stir to combine. Cover and continue simmering for 15-20 minutes, until the potatoes are tender and the broth has thickened. Turn off the heat.
9.  Transfer the braised pork intestine and chicken to a large bowl. Sprinkle a handful of dried chili peppers and dried Sichuan peppercorns on top. Heat oil in a separate wok until smoking. Quickly pour the hot oil over the dried seasonings to release their fragrance. Finally, sprinkle chopped garlic sprouts or scallions for garnish.

## Health Advice
Pork intestine and chicken are rich in collagen but also high in cholesterol and fat, so they are suitable for occasional consumption. Potatoes and konjac can absorb some of the oil. It is recommended to eat more of the vegetables and less of the broth.
