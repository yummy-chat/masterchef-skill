# Corn and Pork Rib Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 Pork ribs, appropriate amount
🌽 Corn, appropriate amount (cut into small sections)
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (cut into rolling cuts)
🫒 Red dates, appropriate amount
🍄 Cordyceps militaris, appropriate amount

🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🍶 White vinegar, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
♨️ Electric pressure cooker
🥣 Soup pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Large spoon

## Steps
1. **Pork Ribs Pre-treatment**: Soak the pork ribs in clean water for about 1 hour, then wash and thoroughly squeeze out excess water.
   **Note**: Do not blanch; soaking is enough to remove blood, preserving the meat's original flavor.
2. **Vegetable Preparation**: Cut the carrot into rolling cuts; cut the corn into small sections; wash the red dates; prepare the cordyceps militaris.
3. **Blanch Vegetables**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the corn and carrot, blanch for about 1 minute, then remove and drain.
   **Key Point**: Blanching in advance removes impurities and helps keep the soup clear.
4. **Pan-fry Ribs**: Heat the wok, add oil, then add the pork ribs. Pan-fry over medium heat until the surface is slightly browned and caramelized.
   **Indicator**: The ribs should appear golden and slightly crispy on the surface.
5. **Initial Seasoning**: Pour light soy sauce and white vinegar along the edge of the wok, quickly stir-fry until the vinegar aroma dissipates, then add the blanched corn and stir-fry to coat the corn with oil.
   **Purpose**: The white vinegar and light soy sauce work together to remove gamey smell and enhance flavor.
6. **Add Water and Simmer**: Pour in enough boiling water, bring to a boil over high heat, and quickly skim off any foam. Add the red dates and cordyceps militaris.
   **Note**: Must use boiling water, and thoroughly skim off foam for a golden and clear soup.
7. **Pressure/Simmer Cooking**: Transfer the soup ingredients to an electric pressure cooker and select the soup cooking mode; alternatively, use a regular pot and simmer over low heat for 30 minutes.
   **Key Point**: At this stage, **do not** add carrots, or the soup will become cloudy and the carrots will become too mushy.
8. **Return to Pot and Finish**: After cooking, pour the soup back into the wok, add the blanched carrot pieces, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for another 10 minutes until the carrots are tender.
9. **Final Seasoning**: Finally, add salt to taste, then serve.

## Health Tips
This soup is rich in protein, dietary fiber, and vitamin A, making it suitable for those needing nutritional supplementation and immune system enhancement. Note that when pan-frying the ribs, use minimal oil; those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt.
