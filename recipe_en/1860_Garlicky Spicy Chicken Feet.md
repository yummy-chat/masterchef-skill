# Garlicky Spicy Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 13 chicken feet (about 800g, nails trimmed, palm slit, cut into pieces)
🧅 20g scallion (cut into sections, for braising)
🫚 20g ginger (sliced, for braising)
🧄 180g garlic (blitzed into coarse granules in a food processor)
🌶️ 80g fresh Xiaomi chili peppers (sliced diagonally into rings)
🌶️ 20g dried chilies (cut into shreds or small pieces)
🌿 20g cilantro (cut into sections, for finishing)
🍂 2 bay leaves
⭐ 2 star anise
💠 4g Angelica dahurica
🌿 3g Sichuan peppercorns
🍶 20g high-proof baijiu (can substitute with Huadiao wine or yellow wine)
🧂 5g salt (3g for braising, 2g for sauce)
🧂 70g light soy sauce (50g for braising, 20g for sauce)
🧂 25g dark soy sauce (20g for braising, 5g for sauce)
🦪 20g oyster sauce
🍬 10g sugar
🧂 3g chicken bouillon
🌶️ 10g dried chili flakes
🌶️ 10g fine chili powder
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (use a little more for stir-frying to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Strainer or skimmer
🍲 Lid
🥣 Measuring cup or bowl
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🧄 Food processor (for mincing garlic)

## Instructions
1.  Use scissors to trim the nails off the chicken feet. Cut a slit in the palm pad and squeeze out the mucus. Cut each foot lengthwise along the long bone, then cut the palm section in half again, so each foot is divided into three pieces. Place the chicken feet in cold water, rub and rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear, then drain.
2.  Prepare aromatics for braising: Cut scallion into sections, slice the ginger, and set aside bay leaves, star anise, and Angelica dahurica.
3.  Prepare stir-fry ingredients: Blitz garlic into coarse granules in a food processor. Cut dried chilies into shreds or pieces. Slice fresh chilies into rings. Cut cilantro into sections.
4.  Prepare sauce: In a bowl, mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, chicken bouillon, and salt. Stir until combined and set aside.
5.  Blanch to remove gamey smell: Place chicken feet in a pot with cold water. Add Sichuan peppercorns and high-proof baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat. Once a lot of foam rises, turn off the heat. Remove the chicken feet and rinse them thoroughly with warm water.
6.  Braise chicken feet: Add 1300ml of water to the pot. Add the blanched chicken feet to the cold water. Heat over high heat. Just before the water boils, skim off any foam with a strainer. Once fully boiling, add the scallion sections, ginger slices, bay leaves, star anise, Angelica dahurica, salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir to combine. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
7.  Stir-fry spicy aromatics: Heat a separate pan or wok with a generous amount of oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, fresh chili rings, and dried chili shreds. Stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute until very fragrant. Add the dried chili flakes and fine chili powder, and continue stir-frying until the oil turns red.
**Note**: Use a little more oil to prevent the garlic from sticking to the pan.
8.  Combine and simmer: Remove the braised chicken feet from the braising liquid and add them to the pan with the spicy aromatics. Stir-fry to coat evenly. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant. Then, add 2 bowls of the original braising liquid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Cover and simmer for 3 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the chicken feet.
9.  Reduce sauce and serve: Increase the heat to medium-high to reduce the sauce, leaving a thick and glossy sauce – do not reduce it completely dry. Add the cilantro sections and quickly stir-fry until just combined. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Chicken feet are rich in collagen but are also calorie-dense. It is recommended to serve them with vegetables and to control your total intake. They are suitable for adults who enjoy gnawing on bones and a chewy texture. People with high blood lipids should consume them in moderation.
