# Strawberry Bomb
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍓 Strawberries 200g (100g diced, 100g halved for decoration)
🍰 8-inch chiffon cake base, 1 piece
🥛 Heavy cream 600g (300g for filling, 300g for frosting)
🍬 Powdered sugar 36g (18g for filling, 18g for frosting)
🍯 Strawberry jam 120g
❄️ Non-melting powdered sugar, as needed (for decoration)

## Cookware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (18cm diameter)
🍽️ Plastic wrap
👝 Piping bag
🔺 Triangle piping tip
🔪 Spatula/offset spatula
🌀 Electric hand mixer
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🎠 Turntable
🪚 Serrated knife
🥣 Bowls of various sizes (for cutting cake rounds)

## Instructions
1.  Bake the chiffon cake, let it cool, then slice into 3 even layers.
2.  Wash the strawberries, pat dry with a paper towel. Dice 100g and halve the remaining 100g for decoration.
3.  Make the strawberry cream filling: Combine 300g heavy cream, 18g powdered sugar, and 120g strawberry jam. Whip with an electric hand mixer until stiff peaks form.
4.  Take one cake layer and use a bowl to cut out a 13cm diameter circle. Keep both the inner circle and the outer ring. Repeat with another cake layer, using a bowl to cut out a 14cm diameter circle. Keep the inner circle and outer ring.
5.  Line the stainless steel bowl with plastic wrap. Place one whole cake layer at the bottom. Place the 13cm diameter outer ring vertically against the bowl wall, securing it to the base with a little strawberry cream.
6.  Fill with 150g of strawberry cream and smooth it out. Spread 100g of diced strawberries on top. Add another 50g of strawberry cream and smooth it. Cover with the 13cm inner cake round, pressing down gently.
7.  Repeat with the 14cm diameter outer ring. Spread another 100g of diced strawberries over it. Fill with the remaining strawberry cream and smooth it. Cover with the 14cm inner cake round. Wrap the whole cake in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 20 minutes to set.
8.  Remove from the refrigerator, invert the bowl to unmold the cake. Remove the bowl and plastic wrap. Make the frosting: Whip 300g heavy cream with 18g powdered sugar until soft peaks form. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a triangle tip. Evenly pipe the cream all over the cake surface. Use a spatula to smooth it into a spherical shape, following the cake's curve.
9.  Use the remaining cream to pipe small rounds on the surface. Use a spatula to randomly smear them, creating irregular patterns. Place the halved strawberries "haphazardly" on the surface. Finally, sift a layer of non-melting powdered sugar over the top.
10. Refrigerate the decorated cake for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld and the texture to improve before serving.

## Health Advice
Strawberries are rich in Vitamin C and antioxidants. The cream provides fat and energy, and together with the cake base, they create a rich flavor profile. However, this dessert is high in sugar and calorie-dense, so consumption should be controlled. It is ideal for sharing at parties and not for consuming large quantities alone to avoid excessive sugar intake.
