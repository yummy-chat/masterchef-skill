# Braised Beef in Casserole
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket (or beef shoulder, beef flank) about 1 kg
🥬 Celery 1-2 stalks
🧅 Onion 1
🥕 Carrot 2
🥔 Potato 2
🧄 Garlic 3-5 cloves
🍅 Tomato 2
🥐 Puff pastry 2 sheets
🧂 Salt to taste
🍷 Red wine 150-200 ml
🥃 Brandy 30-50 ml
🌿 Bay leaves 3-4 pieces
⚫ Coarsely ground black pepper to taste
🌱 Thyme to taste
🧈 Butter about 30 g
🥫 Tomato paste about 3 tablespoons
🧂 Beef stock powder to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Wooden cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl
🥄 Stir-fry spatula
🍳 Large iron wok
♨️ Pressure cooker
🫗 Fine mesh strainer
🔥 Oven
🍲 Single stew pot

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process the beef**: Cut the beef brisket into 3 cm cubes.
2. **Prepare vegetable aromatics**: Cut celery into sections, onion and carrot into chunks; separately prepare potato and carrot in irregular chunks, mince garlic, and cut tomatoes into chunks.
3. **Marinate the beef**: Place the beef with celery sections, onion chunks, and carrot chunks in a large bowl. Add salt, red wine, brandy, bay leaves, coarsely ground black pepper, and thyme. Mix well and marinate for about 20 minutes.
   - **Key point**: The alcohol in the marinade removes gamey flavors and imparts a complex aroma.
4. **Sear the beef**: Heat the iron wok until smoking, add a small amount of oil, then add the marinated beef in batches. Sear over high heat until the surface is golden brown, occasionally adding butter to enhance dairy aroma.
   - **Key point**: Beef must be added in batches to avoid a temperature drop that hinders browning.
5. **Cook the vegetables**: Using the residual oil in the wok, add the vegetable aromatics used for marinating and stir-fry briefly with the seared beef.
6. **Make the sauce**:
   - Use a slotted spoon to remove all meat and vegetables. Strain the liquid in the wok through a fine mesh strainer into another container to obtain a pure broth.
   - In a separate pan, melt butter over low heat, add minced garlic and stir until fragrant.
   - Add onion chunks and tomato chunks, stir-fry until soft, then add tomato paste and stir-fry until the oil turns red and the raw sour taste is gone.
   - Pour the strained beef broth back into the pan, stir well, and bring to a boil.
   - **Key point**: Tomato paste must be fully cooked to remove its raw sour taste and release a deep aroma.
7. **Pressure cook**: Bring the seared beef and some vegetables to a boil in the sauce, add beef stock powder to taste, then transfer everything to a pressure cooker. Cook for about 30 minutes after reaching pressure, until the beef is tender.
   - **Key point**: The exact cooking time depends on the size of the meat cubes and the meat quality.
8. **Prepare side vegetables**: While the pressure cooker is working, deep-fry the potato and carrot chunks until about 70-80% cooked and lightly golden on the surface, then set aside.
9. **Combine and reduce**:
   - Return the cooked beef and some of the braised vegetables to the wok with the sauce.
   - Add the fried potato and carrot chunks, along with fresh celery sections.
   - Simmer over medium heat until the sauce thickens, then add a small piece of butter, stir until melted, and turn off the heat.
   - **Key point**: Adding celery sections at the end keeps them crisp; finishing with butter adds gloss and aroma.
10. **Bake**:
    - Divide the finished beef and sauce into individual stew pots, placing vegetables at the bottom and beef on top.
    - Cover each pot with a round sheet of puff pastry, pressing the edges firmly to seal.
    - Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 10-15 minutes, until the pastry is golden and puffy.
    - **Key point**: Oven temperature and time should be adjusted according to your equipment; the pastry is done when it has expanded and changed color.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene. The beef is cooked until tender for easy digestion, but the puff pastry and butter are high in calories. It is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables to balance nutrition. Be mindful of salt and beef stock powder usage; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce them by half.
