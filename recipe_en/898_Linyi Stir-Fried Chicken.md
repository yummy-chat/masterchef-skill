# Linyi Stir-Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐓 1 young rooster (about 1–1.5 jin, must choose a young rooster)  
🫚 1 large piece of ginger (some cut into large slices to render oil, some cut into small slices for stir-frying)  
🧅 1–2 scallions (cut into sections)  
🧄 About 1 head of garlic (smash, use in two batches)  
🌶️ 1–2 green bell peppers (cut into rolling knife chunks)  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of red bell pepper (cut into rolling knife chunks, for color)  
🌶️ A small handful of dried chilies  
🧂 1 small spoon of stir-fried chicken spice powder (cinnamon, angelica, star anise, fennel, cardamom, Sichuan pepper, dried ginger, cardamom, bay leaf, cloves, etc., ground into powder)  
🍯 1 large spoon of mixed sauce (sweet bean sauce, soybean paste, and Pixian broad bean paste mixed in proportion)  
🍶 Appropriate amount of Huadiao wine  
🧂 Appropriate amount of soy sauce  
💧 Hot water, about enough to submerge the chicken  
🫒 1 small spoon of sesame oil  
🧂 A little Sichuan pepper/numbing pepper (optional)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔥 Gas stove (needs to provide high heat)  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔫 Blowtorch (to singe off chicken fluff)  
🌀 Blender/grinder (to grind spice powder)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥢 Slotted spoon (to remove ginger slices)  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  

## Steps  
1. Clean the young rooster, use a blowtorch to singe off any residual fluff, wash, and chop into small pieces.  
**Key point**: Must use a young rooster for tender meat.  

2. Wash dried spices like cinnamon, angelica, and star anise, then grind into fine powder with a blender; mix sweet bean sauce, soybean paste, and Pixian broad bean paste in proportion and set aside.  
**Note**: Spices must be ground into powder for full flavor release.  

3. Cut the large ginger into large slices and small thin slices separately; cut scallions into sections; smash the garlic, divide into two portions; cut green and red bell peppers into rolling knife chunks.  

4. Heat oil in the wok, add the large ginger slices, fry over medium heat until the edges turn golden and slightly curled, then remove and discard the ginger slices, leaving the ginger oil in the wok.  
**Key point**: Ginger oil is the base aroma of the entire dish; ginger slices must be fried to release a rich fragrance.  

5. Turn the heat to high and heat the ginger oil until smoking, pour in the chicken pieces and quickly spread them flat. Let them sear undisturbed for about 2 minutes, rotating the wok continuously so the chicken heats evenly. Once you hear a “crackling” sound and the chicken surface turns golden and crispy with clear oil, it’s ready.  
**Key point**: Use high heat and a hot wok; don’t move the chicken initially to force out moisture and oil, achieving a crispy sear.  

6. Keep the heat high, add the spice powder, half of the scallion sections, half of the smashed garlic, the small ginger slices, dried chilies, and optional Sichuan pepper/numbing pepper, stir-fry until fragrant.  

7. Add the mixed sauce, quickly stir-fry to evenly coat the chicken, until the color turns red and glossy and red oil releases.  
**Key point**: The sauce must be fully stir-fried to develop aroma and adhere to the chicken pieces.  

8. Pour Huadiao wine along the edge of the wok, add soy sauce, stir-fry evenly, then add hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer until the chicken is tender.  
**Key point**: Must use hot water to ensure the chicken texture stays tender, not dry; ensure enough water volume.  

9. When the sauce has reduced to a thick consistency, add the remaining garlic, scallion sections, and green and red bell pepper chunks. Stir-fry until the bell peppers are just cooked but still crisp.  
**Key point**: Adding garlic later preserves its strong aroma; bell peppers stay crunchy.  

10. Drizzle in sesame oil, mix evenly, then plate.  
**Key point**: Sesame oil is essential for brightness and fragrance; it’s the finishing touch.  

## Health Advice  
Linyi stir-fried chicken is a classic Shandong dish. The young rooster is tender without being dry, and the ginger oil base creates a rich aroma. Suitable for those who enjoy meat and spiciness. Note that the sauce has a high salt content, so control the saltiness.
