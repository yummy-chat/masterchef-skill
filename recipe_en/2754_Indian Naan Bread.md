# Indian Naan Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
- 🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
- 🥛 Yogurt 60ml
- 🍄 Active dry yeast 3g
- 🍬 Sugar 10g
- 🧂 Salt 5g
- 🛢️ Cooking oil 15ml (for kneading)
- 💧 Warm water 100ml (about 40°C)
- 🧈 Ghee or butter 30g (for spreading)
- 🌑 Black sesame seeds 5g (optional)
- 🌿 Cilantro leaves 10g (optional)
- 🧄 Garlic 3 cloves, about 9g (for garlic version, optional)

## Cookware & Equipment
- 🥣 Large bowl
- 🍳 Skillet (cast iron recommended) or oven
- 🥖 Rolling pin
- 🧽 Damp cloth or plastic wrap
- 🥢 Food tongs

## Preparation Steps
1. Stir the dry yeast and sugar into warm water at about 40°C until dissolved. Let it sit for 5 minutes until fine foam appears on the surface, indicating the yeast is activated.
2. Place all-purpose flour and salt in a large bowl, mixing evenly.
3. Pour the activated yeast water, yogurt, and cooking oil into the flour.
4. Knead by hand into a smooth and soft dough. The dough should be slightly softer and a bit stickier than regular dough. Continue kneading for about 8-10 minutes.
5. Lightly coat the dough surface with a little oil to prevent drying. Cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and leave to ferment in a warm place for at least 1 hour, until the dough doubles in volume.
6. Remove the dough, gently knead to release air, and divide into 6 equal portions, each about 80 grams.
7. Lightly dust your hands with a little flour or oil. Take one portion and roll it out with a rolling pin into a long oval or teardrop shape, about 4-5 mm thick. For the garlic version, sprinkle crushed garlic and cilantro leaves on top, gently roll to embed into the dough surface. Black sesame seeds can also be sprinkled on.
8. Heat a skillet over high heat until very hot—a drop of water should evaporate instantly. Brush a thin layer of water on one side of the dough. Place the brushed side down into the pan. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until golden brown spots appear on the bottom and the dough begins to bubble up.
9. Flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes. If you have an open flame gas stove, use food tongs to hold the naan directly over the flame for a few seconds; the bread will puff up rapidly.
10. Remove the cooked naan and immediately spread ghee or butter on the surface. Serve hot.

## Health Tips
This Indian naan bread made with yogurt in the dough is soft and increases calcium and protein intake. It pairs well with curries or stews. Each piece spread with butter contains about 180-200 calories. For weight control, it is recommended to consume 1-2 pieces per serving.
