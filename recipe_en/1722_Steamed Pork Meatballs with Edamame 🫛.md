# Steamed Pork Meatballs with Edamame 🫛
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork front leg (30% fat, 70% lean)   about 500g
🫛 Edamame beans   about 135g
🥬 Young lotus root   1 small section (finely chopped)
🍠 Sweet potato starch   as needed (can substitute with cornstarch)
🧂 Salt   as needed
🍾 Light soy sauce   as needed
🌿 White pepper powder   as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce   as needed
🧅🫚 Scallion-ginger water   500g meat to 100g water (crush scallion and ginger, soak in water, then extract juice)
🍬 Sugar   as needed
🧂 MSG   as needed
🧈 Lard   1 tablespoon
💧 Water   about 1 bowl

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
⚙️ Meat grinder
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing meat filling)
🥣 Bowl (for scallion-ginger water and starch water)
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Spoon
🍽️ Ceramic deep dish
♨️ Steamer
🔥 Electric wok or gas stove (for heating)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Grind the pork, then place it in the refrigerator to chill for about 2 hours to make it easier to mix until firm.
2.  Wash the edamame beans and pour them into a deep dish. Add salt, sugar, MSG, one tablespoon of lard, and one bowl of water. Mix well to create the base layer.
3.  Finely chop the lotus root. Crush the scallion and ginger, then soak in water to extract the juice. Dilute the sweet potato starch with a small amount of cold water and set aside.
4.  Remove the chilled ground meat from the refrigerator. Add salt, light soy sauce, white pepper powder, and oyster sauce for initial seasoning.
5.  Add the scallion-ginger water in two batches. After each addition, forcefully stir and slap the meat in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed, the mixture becomes sticky, and resistance increases.
6.  Pour in the diluted sweet potato starch water and mix well. Then, add the chopped lotus root and stir to combine.
7.  Vigorously slap the entire mass of meat filling against the bowl a dozen times to increase its elasticity.
8.  Grab a handful of the meat filling and squeeze out a meatball from between your thumb and index finger. Use a spoon to scoop it off and place it into the prepared edamame dish.
9.  Preheat the steamer until steam is rising. Place the dish into the steamer, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for 15 minutes, until the meatballs are fully cooked and the edamame is tender.
    - Note: The dish must be placed into the steamer only after steam has fully risen to ensure the meat remains tender.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a good balance of protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for children and the elderly to supplement their nutrition. It is recommended to use less lard and oyster sauce to control sodium intake.
