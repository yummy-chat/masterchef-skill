# Stir-Fried Beans (Ji Douzi)
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Oval soybeans – appropriate amount (choose oval soybeans for a better aroma)  
🧅 Ginger – appropriate amount (smash and finely mince into ginger rice)  
🧄 Garlic – appropriate amount (minced garlic, about twice the amount of ginger)  
🌿 Spring onion – appropriate amount (finely chopped scallions, about twice the amount of garlic)  
🌶️ Pickled red chili peppers – appropriate amount (finely minced into fish pepper paste, key for the fish-fragrant flavor)  
🧂 Salt – a pinch (for washing the beans and seasoning)  
🫒 Cooking oil – appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon – a pinch  
🧂 MSG – a pinch  
🌿 White pepper powder – a pinch  
🍬 White sugar – about 4 tablespoons  
🥫 Light soy sauce – a small amount  
🍶 Vinegar – amount equal to the white sugar  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok spatula or soup ladle  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl or large basin (for fish-fragrant sauce and “stirring” the beans)  
🫙 Glass lid (for simmering the beans)  

## Steps  
1. Prepare ingredients: Smash the ginger and garlic, then finely mince separately. Mix garlic and ginger in a ratio of about 2:1 (garlic to ginger). Finely chop the spring onions, using about twice the amount of garlic, and set aside in a separate bowl. Finely mince the pickled red chili peppers and set aside in a separate bowl.  
2. Wash the beans: Rub the soybeans with water and a pinch of salt to remove impurities and any insects. Rinse and drain well. Set aside.  
3. Cook the fish-fragrant sauce: Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the minced pickled chili peppers and stir-fry until the oil turns orange-red. Add the minced ginger and garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add a small amount of water, followed by salt, chicken bouillon, MSG, white pepper powder, a generous amount of white sugar, and a small amount of light soy sauce. Stir to combine, then add vinegar in an amount equal to the white sugar. Adjust to create the fish-fragrant sauce, then transfer to a large basin and set aside.  
4. Dry-fry the soybeans: Place the wok over low heat without adding oil. Add the soybeans and stir-fry slowly, stirring frequently, until the bean skins develop leopard-patterned brown spots, the beans crack open, and they taste crisp and crunchy (“crunchy snap”).  
5. Stir the beans: Quickly pour the hot, dry-fried soybeans into the basin containing the fish-fragrant sauce. Sprinkle a generous amount of chopped spring onions on top, then immediately cover with a lid and let it sit for a while, allowing the dry beans to fully absorb the fish-fragrant flavor and the heat to release the aroma of the scallions.  
6. Remove and stir well before serving.  

## Health Tips  
This dish combines dry-fried soybeans with a fish-fragrant sauce, creating an interplay of crisp texture and sweet-sour-spicy flavors. Soybeans are moderately high in calories, and the pickled peppers, sugar, and vinegar contribute to higher sodium and sugar content. It is recommended to consume in moderation as an appetizer and enjoy occasionally. Suitable for those who love Sichuan flavors and crispy textures; those with weak digestion should chew slowly and thoroughly.
