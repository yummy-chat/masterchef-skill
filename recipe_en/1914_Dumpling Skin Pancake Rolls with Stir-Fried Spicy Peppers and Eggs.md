# Dumpling Skin Pancake Rolls with Stir-Fried Spicy Peppers and Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫓 Dumpling skins, about 15-20 pieces  
🌶️ Green peppers (spiral peppers), about 2-3 pieces  
🥚 Eggs, 4 pieces  
🧅 Scallions, finely chopped, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Small red chili peppers, a few pieces  
🧂 Salt, about 1 teaspoon  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl  
🪓 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula or slotted spoon  

## Preparation Steps
1. Take the dumpling skins, dip the edges in oil one by one, stack five skins per pile, evenly spread oil between each skin, flatten with the palm of your hand, then use a rolling pin to roll them into thin pancakes about the thickness of a coin.  
   **Key point**: Each skin must be evenly coated with oil, otherwise they cannot be separated smoothly after steaming.

2. Wash the spiral peppers, remove the seeds, tap them with the knife handle to loosen the fibers, then cut into rolling chunks.  
   **Key point**: Tapping loosens the fibers, making them easier to cook and absorb flavor.

3. Place the chopped scallions and small red chili peppers in a small stainless steel bowl, then crack the 4 eggs directly into the bowl without stirring for now.

4. Place the rolled-out thin pancakes into the steamer basket. Once the steamer is steaming, steam for 8 minutes. Remove and let cool slightly, then peel apart layer by layer and set aside.  
   **Note**: Let the pancakes cool until they are no longer hot to the touch before peeling apart to prevent sticking and burns.

5. Without adding oil to the wok, pour in the pepper chunks. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the skin is slightly charred and develops a leopard-spot pattern. Remove and set aside.

6. Add a small amount of oil to the wok. Heat until hot, then add the pre-fried peppers.

7. Pour the egg mixture (with scallions and small red chili peppers) directly into the wok, covering the peppers. Maintain medium-low heat and do not stir immediately.  
   **Key point**: Allow the egg mixture to set on top of the peppers first to form a caramelized layer on the surface, releasing the egg and caramelized aromas.

8. Once the bottom of the egg mixture is set, break it apart with a spatula and stir-fry evenly with the peppers.

9. Add one teaspoon of salt to season, stir-fry evenly, then remove from the wok.  
   **Note**: Season only with salt to highlight the natural flavor of the peppers and the freshness of the eggs.

10. Take one thin pancake, place an appropriate amount of the stir-fried spicy peppers and eggs on it, fold the two sides of the pancake inward, then roll it up like a spring roll and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish uses dumpling skins as a substitute for traditional pancakes, reducing carbohydrate intake. The combination of spicy peppers and eggs provides protein and vitamin C, making it suitable for consumption during weight loss. Be careful not to use too much oil when stir-frying the peppers to avoid increasing the calorie count.
