# Corn Oil White Sandwich Bread  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable year-round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 250g bread flour  
🥛 150g ice-cold milk (approx.)  
🥚 1 egg  
🌽 20g corn oil  
🍬 20g sugar  
🧂 4g salt  
🍄 3g active dry yeast (or 9g fresh yeast, choose one)  

## Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥛 Measuring cup/spoon  
🍞 Stand mixer  
🌡️ Proofer or warm, humid place  
🔪 Bench scraper  
🥖 Rolling pin  
📦 Pullman loaf pan (with lid)  
🔥 Oven  
🧊 Cooling rack  
🧤 Oven mitts  

## Instructions  
1. Add bread flour, ice-cold milk, egg, corn oil, sugar, salt, and yeast to the stand mixer bowl.  
   **Note**: Use ice-cold milk to control dough temperature; add liquid oil all at once, no need for delayed oil method.  
2. Mix on low speed until dough comes together and forms a shaggy mass.  
3. Increase to high speed and knead for about 6-7 minutes until dough is smooth and can be stretched into a thin, elastic membrane.  
   **Important**: Final dough temperature should be kept below 26°C.  
4. Remove dough onto the counter, slap it a few times, and round it into a smooth ball.  
5. Place dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp cloth, and let rise at room temperature for about 1 hour until doubled in size.  
   **How to check**: Dust a finger with flour and poke a hole; the hole should not spring back or collapse.  
6. Turn out the risen dough, gently pat to release gas, avoiding tearing.  
7. Divide dough into three equal portions, cutting while preserving as much smooth surface as possible.  
8. Shape each portion into a round with the smooth side facing out. If sticky, dust hands with a very small amount of flour.  
9. Cover rounded dough portions with a damp cloth and let rest at room temperature for 20 minutes to relax the gluten.  
10. First roll: Take one dough portion, flatten it, and use a rolling pin to roll from the center outward and inward, forming a trapezoid that is narrower at the top and wider at the bottom, with even thickness. Pop any large air bubbles. Flip it over, then roll from top to bottom, not too tightly.  
11. Cover all rolled dough portions with a damp cloth and let rest again at room temperature for 20 minutes.  
12. Second roll: Flatten one dough portion and roll it into a strip about 25 cm long, with moderate pressure, avoiding rolling too thin or tearing the skin. Flip it over and shape into a rectangle that is narrower at the top and wider at the bottom.  
13. Roll from top to bottom, achieving about 2.5 turns. Avoid too many rolls, which can cause tearing during proofing or baking.  
14. Place all three rolls in the same direction into the Pullman loaf pan for final proofing. Proof until the dough reaches 80-85% of the pan’s height.  
15. Put on the lid, place in a preheated oven at 200°C top heat and 180°C bottom heat, on the lowest rack, and bake for 40 minutes.  
16. Immediately after baking, tap the pan against the counter to release steam, then turn out the loaf onto a cooling rack to cool completely.  
    **Key**: Tap and unmold immediately to prevent the loaf from collapsing or pinching.  

## Health Tips  
This corn oil white sandwich bread replaces butter with vegetable oil, reducing saturated fat, making it suitable for a light diet or those with lactose intolerance. Bread flour provides carbohydrates, and with the egg and milk, it works well as breakfast or post-workout fuel. Pair with protein and vegetables to make a balanced sandwich. Slice and freeze to lock in moisture and slow staling.
