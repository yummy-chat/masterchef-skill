# Boiled Three Fresh Ingredients
Common Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 sea bass (about 600g, lean and lively, scaled, deboned, and sliced into fillets, head and bones reserved)
🦐 About 500g fresh shrimp (butterflied, deveined, sharp tips of heads trimmed)
🥩 Some pork tenderloin (sliced thinly)
🥬 Some napa cabbage (torn into pieces by hand)
🥒 1 cucumber (peeled and cut into strips)
🧅 1 large green onion (sliced diagonally)
🧄 Some garlic (minced)
🌶️ Some green and red small chili peppers (cut into rings)
🧂 About 2 tablespoons salt (for the soup) + a pinch (for marinating meat/fish)
🌫️ Some ground white pepper (for marinating meat/fish to remove fishy smell)
🥔 2 teaspoons potato starch per portion (for coating meat slices and fish fillets)
🍶 Some cooking wine (to remove fishy smell from fish broth)
🍬 Some sugar (to enhance freshness)
🫚 A few slices of ginger (for frying fish bones)
🫒 Some cooking oil (for frying fish bones + for the final aromatic oil splash)
🌶️ Some dried chili peppers (soaked in water beforehand)
🌿 Some dried Sichuan peppercorns (soaked in water beforehand)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chinese chef's knife
🪵 Round thick wooden cutting board
🥒 Vegetable peeler
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/glass bowl (for marinating ingredients, soaking chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns)
🧤 Food gloves
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Wok
♨️ Steamer
🥄 Strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the sharp tips of the shrimp heads, butterfly the shrimp by slicing along the back, remove the vein, and rinse clean.
2. Scale the sea bass, use a flat knife to remove the fillets from both sides along the bone, slice the fillets diagonally. Chop the fish head and bones into large pieces and set aside.
3. Slice the pork tenderloin thinly; tear the napa cabbage into pieces by hand; peel the cucumber, trim the ends, and cut into strips; slice the large green onion diagonally.
4. Add a pinch of salt, some ground white pepper, and 2 teaspoons of potato starch to the meat slices and fish fillets separately. Wear gloves and mix well to coat. If it's too dry, add a little water.
5. Soak the dried chili peppers and dried Sichuan peppercorns in water until soft; mince the garlic; cut the green and red small chili peppers into rings; set aside.
6. Arrange the prepared shrimp in a plate with the shell side down and the meat side up. Steam in a preheated steamer over boiling water for 3 minutes until the shrimp turn red. Remove and set aside.
7. Pour some cooking oil into the wok. Add the fish bones and ginger slices. Fry over medium-high heat until both sides are golden brown. Pour in enough boiling water. Keep boiling over high heat for 3 minutes until the broth turns milky white.
   - Key: Fry the fish bones until fragrant and add boiling water. Boil vigorously over high heat to emulsify the oil, resulting in a milky white broth. Leave the surface oil to enhance aroma.
8. Add the large green onion segments, 2 tablespoons of salt, a little MSG, sugar, and cooking wine to the fish broth. Add the napa cabbage pieces and cucumber strips. Cook over high heat for about 1-2 minutes until the cabbage stems are slightly translucent and just cooked. Remove the vegetables and fish bones and place them in the bottom of a large bowl.
9. Keep the soup boiling. First add the meat slices, quickly separate them with chopsticks. Cook over high heat for about 1 minute until fully cooked, then remove. Next, add the fish fillets. Cook until they just curl and turn white, then remove immediately.
   - Key: Meat slices take longer to cook than fish fillets, so they must be added first. Keep the soup boiling throughout to quickly gelatinize the surface starch, resulting in tender, smooth textures and clear soup.
10. Arrange the meat slices and fish fillets on top of the vegetables. Place the steamed shrimp on the other side. Pour the fish broth over everything. Sprinkle with minced garlic and green and red chili pepper rings. In another pan, heat cooking oil until it smokes. Add the soaked dried chili peppers and dried Sichuan peppercorns. Fry until fragrant, then strain out the solids using a strainer. Pour the hot oil over the garlic and chili pepper rings. Serve immediately.
    - Key: The oil temperature must be high to release the aroma. Straining out the solids ensures the finished dish is clean and not cloudy.

## Health Advice
This Boiled Three Fresh Ingredients dish uses steaming for the shrimp, blanching for the meat slices, and poaching for the fish fillets, replacing traditional deep-frying. It is low in fat and high in protein, especially suitable for those on a weight loss diet or fitness regimen. Note that the vegetables are already fully seasoned when cooked in the fish broth, so no additional dipping sauce is needed when eating.
