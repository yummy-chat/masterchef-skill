# Steamed Hairy Crab
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦀 Hairy crabs, about 10 (live crabs, tied with string)  
🫚 Ginger, 2 small pieces (1 piece sliced for steaming, 1 piece minced for dipping sauce)  
🧅 Scallions, 1 small bunch (tied into knots for steaming)  
🍶 Aromatic vinegar, to taste (base for dipping sauce)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a little (to enhance umami in the dipping sauce)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Deep pot and steaming rack  
🪢 Fine string (for tying live crabs)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
✂️ Scissors  
🔨 Small hammer (one of the crab eating tools, for cracking the claws)  
🪓 Long-handled axe/knife (one of the crab eating tools, for prying open the crab belly and shell)  
🩺 Tweezers (one of the crab eating tools, for removing inedible parts and picking out crab meat)  
🍴 Small fork (one of the crab eating tools, for extracting leg meat)  
🥄 Small spoon (one of the crab eating tools, for scooping out crab roe)  
🥣 Dipping sauce dish, soup spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Secure the live crabs firmly with fine string: first gather the crab claws and legs, wrap horizontally twice, then cross and tie tightly vertically to prevent struggling during steaming, which could cause leg breakage or flipping.  
**Key point**: The binding must be tight to avoid movement from steam entry, ensuring even heat distribution.  

2. Slice 1 piece of ginger, and tie the scallions into knots. Add enough water to the deep pot, put in the ginger slices and scallion knots, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
3. Once the water is boiling, place the hairy crabs belly-side up evenly on the steaming rack, cover, and reduce to medium heat, steaming for 16 minutes.  
4. Disassemble the crabs:  
   - Cut off all crab legs and claws. Snip off the joints at both ends of the legs, use the small fork to push out the leg meat; gently tap the claws with the small hammer to extract the claw meat.  
- Use the small hammer to loosen the shell around the edges, pry open the crab belly, and lift off the top shell. Using the tweezers, remove and discard the inedible parts: the stomach at the front of the shell, the heart under the black membrane in the center of the body, the gills on both sides, and the mouth at the front.  
- Use the small spoon to scrape the crab roe/paste into a small dish. Use scissors to cut the crab body in half from the center, then cut lengthwise into four pieces. Use the tweezers to pick out the body meat and combine it with the roe/paste.  
5. Prepare the dipping sauce: Crush and finely mince the remaining piece of ginger, place it in a small dish, add enough aromatic vinegar and a little light soy sauce, and stir well.  
6. Drizzle the ginger-vinegar sauce over the prepared crab meat before eating. This counteracts the cooling nature of the crab and enhances the flavor.  

## Health Advice  
Hairy crabs are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. The roe and paste contain abundant phospholipids and vitamins but also have high cholesterol levels; therefore, those with high blood lipids should consume them in moderation. Pairing with ginger-vinegar sauce helps dispel coldness, cut through richness, and enhance flavor while effectively neutralizing the crab's cooling properties.
