# Salt-Roasted Corn
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌽 Fresh corn cobs 3 pieces  
🧂 Salt 6 grams  
💧 Warm water 20 grams  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥘 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🔧 Awl (pointed tool)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🥄 Spoon  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  

## Instructions  
1. Place the corn cobs into a pot of boiling water and blanch. Once the water returns to a boil, time 10–15 seconds, then immediately remove and transfer to cold water to cool.  
**Note**: Brief blanching softens the kernels, making it easier to remove them from the cob and preventing breakage.  

2. Use an awl to pierce lengthwise through the center of a corn cob, then use your hands to split the cob open from the middle, allowing the kernels to fall off in sheets.  

3. Place the removed kernels into clean water and stir to remove any floating corn silk. Drain well, then thoroughly pat dry with kitchen paper towels.  
**Note**: The kernels must be completely dry to prevent oil splattering or affecting the texture when cooking.  

4. In a small bowl, combine 20 grams of warm water and 6 grams of salt, stirring until the salt is fully dissolved to make a saltwater mixture; set aside.  

5. Clean and heat the wok, ensuring it is completely dry and free of oil or water. Add the corn kernels and stir-fry over high heat for 1–2 minutes until the surface of the kernels is slightly puffed and mostly cooked through.  

6. Pour the prepared saltwater along the edge of the wok, then reduce the heat to low and continue stir-frying.  
**Key**: Immediately reduce to low heat after adding the saltwater to prevent the water from evaporating too quickly and burning the corn.  

7. Stir-fry until a white salt frost appears around the edge of the wok and salt crystals are evenly coated on the kernels. Turn off the heat and serve.  

## Health Suggestions  
This salt-roasted corn is low in calories and rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable as a snack or side dish during weight loss. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount consumed.
