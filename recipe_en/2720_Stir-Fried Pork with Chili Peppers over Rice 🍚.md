# Stir-Fried Pork with Chili Peppers over Rice 🍚
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥩
🫘 Pork – to taste (sliced, any cut)
🫑 Green chili peppers – to taste (cut into chunks, seeds removed)
🧄 Garlic – to taste (sliced)
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili – to taste (cut into sections or rings)
🍚 Rice – to taste (cooked)
🧂 Dark soy sauce – to taste (for marinating) + ½ tablespoon (for sauce)
🍶 Cooking wine – to taste
🌾 Cornstarch – to taste
🧂 Salt – to taste
🫗 Light soy sauce – 2 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce – 1 tablespoon
🍬 Sugar – a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil – to taste

## Cookware & Equipment 🍳
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍚 Bowl
🥄 Rice paddle

## Instructions 🧑‍🍳
1. Place sliced pork in a bowl. Add dark soy sauce, cooking wine, and cornstarch. Mix well with your hands and marinate for 10 minutes.
2. Remove seeds from green chili peppers and cut into chunks. Slice garlic. Cut bird’s eye chili into sections or rings.
3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, add 2 tablespoons light soy sauce, ½ tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, and a pinch of sugar. Stir to combine and set aside.
4. Heat the wok, do not add oil. Add the green chili chunks and salt. Stir-fry over medium heat until the chilies are just cooked through, then remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Adding salt while dry-frying the chilies helps them cook through and infuses a base flavor.
5. Pour oil into the wok and heat. Add the marinated pork slices. Stir-fry over high heat until the meat changes color and the edges are slightly charred.
**Note**: Avoid over-stirring, as this can make the meat tough.
6. Add the sliced garlic and bird’s eye chili. Quickly stir-fry until fragrant.
7. Add the cooked green chili peppers back to the wok. Pour in the prepared sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until everything is evenly coated with the sauce.
8. Turn off the heat. Place the cooked white rice directly on top of the stir-fried pork and chilies in the wok. Use a spatula to thoroughly mix the rice with the dish right in the wok. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice 💚
Stir-fried pork with chili peppers and rice is a high-carb, high-oil, and high-salt combination. It is recommended to pair it with a portion of lightly stir-fried vegetables to balance the meal, and to control the rice portion to avoid excess calories. This dish is good for stimulating appetite when feeling low on hunger, but people with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt and dark soy sauce used.
