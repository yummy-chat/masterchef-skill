# Salt and Pepper Potato Cubes
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes, appropriate amount (cut into evenly sized cubes)  
🥚 Egg yolks, 2  
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (add in small batches)  
🧄 Minced garlic, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Diced green and red peppers, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)  
🧅 Diced onion, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)  
🧂 Salt and pepper powder, appropriate amount (add after turning off the heat)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Steamer basket  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Gas stove (use the lowest heat throughout)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prep Phase  
1. Cut the potatoes into evenly sized cubes and spread them flat in the steamer basket.  
   **Note**: Steaming shortens the subsequent pan-frying time and reduces nutrient loss.  
2. Once the steamer produces steam, place the steamer basket inside and steam for 12–20 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through, indicating doneness.  
3. Quickly transfer the steamed potato cubes into cold water to cool them down, then scoop them out with a slotted spoon and drain any surface water.  
   **Note**: Running them under cold water stops the cooking process; draining the water prevents oil splatter during frying.  
4. Place the drained potato cubes in a stainless steel bowl, add 2 egg yolks, and gently mix until each piece is coated with the egg yolk liquid.  
   **Note**: Mix gently to prevent breaking; egg yolks aid the Maillard reaction, giving a more appealing color.  
5. Add cornstarch in small batches, gently mixing as you go, until each potato cube is evenly coated with starch.  
   **Note**: Adding in batches prevents clumping; mix gently to avoid breakage.  
6. Place the starch-coated potato cubes into a slotted spoon and lightly shake off any excess starch.  
   **Note**: Removing excess starch prevents the cubes from absorbing too much oil during frying, resulting in a lighter texture.  

### Frying and Seasoning  
1. Heat oil in a wok, add the potato cubes, and spread them flat across the bottom. Fry over the lowest heat until the bottom is set.  
   **Doneness Check**: A hard crust forms on the bottom, and the cubes slide freely when the wok is shaken.  
   **Note**: Spreading them flat ensures even heating.  
2. Flip the potato cubes and continue frying over the lowest heat until all six sides are golden and crispy.  
   **Doneness Check**: The surface turns golden brown, and pressing with a spatula reveals a crispy, hard crust.  
   **Note**: Be patient when flipping; use low heat throughout to avoid burning.  
3. Add minced garlic, diced green and red peppers, and diced onion. Stir-fry until the aromatics release their fragrance.  
   **Note**: Do not stir-fry for too long to preserve the crunchiness of the aromatics.  
4. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in the salt and pepper powder, and use the residual heat to toss evenly, ensuring the seasoning coats the cubes.  
   **Note**: Season after turning off the heat to prevent the high temperature from destroying the salt and pepper aroma.  
5. Transfer to a plate, pile into a small mound, and serve hot for the best taste.  

## Health Tips  
By first steaming and then frying, the starch and egg yolk coating create a crispy shell that reduces oil absorption while retaining vitamin C in the potatoes. This dish works well as a home-style side, but the salt and pepper powder is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
