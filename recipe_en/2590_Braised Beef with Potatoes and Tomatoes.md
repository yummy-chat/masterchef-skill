# Braised Beef with Potatoes and Tomatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Year Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef
🥔 Potatoes
🍅 Tomatoes
🧅 Onion
🧄 Scallions
🫚 Ginger
🍶 Cooking wine
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns
⭐ Star anise
🍃 Bay leaves
🛢️ Oil
🍬 White sugar
🍬 Rock sugar
🥢 Light soy sauce
🟤 Dark soy sauce
🌶️ Black pepper
🌶️ White pepper
🧂 Salt

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🔪 Cutting board
🍲 Pressure cooker
🥘 Wok
🫕 Clay pot
🫕 Stewing pot

## Steps
1. Beef preparation. Soak the beef in cold water for half an hour to remove blood, or place in cold water and bring to a boil until the surface changes color, skim off any foam, and remove.
   **Key point**: Either soaking or blanching can remove blood; if blanching, start with cold water.

2. Vegetable preparation. Peel the potatoes and cut into large pieces about 5cm per side. Score a cross on the top of each tomato, blanch in boiling water, then peel, core, and cut into small pieces about 3cm. Dice the onion into 0.5–1cm pieces. Chop 4g of scallion into small pieces, cut the rest into 5–8cm sections. Slice the ginger.

3. Pressure cook the beef. Place the prepared beef in a pressure cooker, add cold water to cover, add the scallion sections, ginger slices, and 20g cooking wine. Cover and cook under pressure for 20 minutes after the steam builds. Remove the beef, cut into large pieces about 5cm, discard the ginger slices, and reserve the cooking liquid.
   **Key point**: The cooking time should be enough for the beef to become tender; adjust according to the meat's texture.

4. Heat the wok with oil. When the oil reaches about 40–50% heat, add the Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and bay leaves. Stir-fry until fragrant, then remove the spices just before they darken. Add the beef pieces and stir-fry until aromatic. If it seems likely to burn, add a little of the reserved beef broth in small batches.

5. Add 15ml light soy sauce, 15ml cooking wine, and pepper. Then add 5–10g tomato paste, ketchup, or one canned tomato. Also add the diced onion and stir-fry until the onion is translucent. A small amount of salt can be added to help the onion release water and prevent burning.
   **Note**: Do not add too much salt, as further seasoning will be needed.

6. Add the tomato pieces and stir-fry until the tomatoes are completely softened and dissolved.

7. Pour in all the reserved beef broth. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer uncovered. Stir occasionally during simmering to prevent sticking.
   **Key point**: The added broth should be kept warm; simmering uncovered prevents the potatoes from breaking down too early.

8. 30–40 minutes before finishing, add the potato pieces and season with white sugar or rock sugar and salt, adjusting to a balanced sweet and savory taste by tasting. Continue to simmer over low heat until the beef is tender and ready to serve.
   **Key point**: The total amount of soy sauce should not be too high, as the sauce will concentrate and become saltier. A thinner sauce should taste slightly mild; additional light soy sauce can be added later. Do not use chicken bouillon or MSG, as they may overpower the beef's natural flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. Potatoes contain high carbohydrates, so people with diabetes should control their portion size.
