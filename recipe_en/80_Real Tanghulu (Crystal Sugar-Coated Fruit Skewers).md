# Real Tanghulu (Crystal Sugar-Coated Fruit Skewers)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍓 Strawberries, as needed  
🫐 Blueberries, as needed  
🍇 Grapes, as needed  
🥤 Clear carbonated beverage (such as Sprite), as needed  
💧 A small amount of water  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🧊 Silicone ice ball mold  
🪥 Toothpicks  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Pour the Sprite into the silicone ice ball mold and freeze in the freezer until completely solid into ice balls.  
2. Wash the strawberries, blueberries, grapes, and other fruits. Pat them dry with a kitchen paper towel, then skewer them with toothpicks.  
3. Place a large number of frozen Sprite ice balls into a stainless steel bowl. Add a small amount of water or Sprite to create an ice-water mixture.  
   **Key Point**: Ensure there are enough ice balls to keep the temperature sufficiently low.  
4. Quickly dip the fruit skewers into the Sprite ice ball mixture, soaking and rolling for a few seconds.  
   **Key Point**: Work quickly to ensure the fruit surface is evenly coated with the cold liquid.  
   **Indicator**: A transparent, hard, and crispy sugar shell quickly forms on the surface of the fruit.  
5. Remove and serve. The shell is glossy and crispy, while the fruit inside remains juicy.  

## Health Tips  
This tanghulu replaces traditional sugar boiling with frozen carbonated beverages, resulting in lower calories, making it suitable for those seeking a low-calorie dessert. Choosing fruits rich in vitamin C, such as strawberries and blueberries, helps boost immunity. Note that the soaking time should not be too long to prevent the fruit from becoming soft.
