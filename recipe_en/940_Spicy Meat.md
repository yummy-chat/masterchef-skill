# Spicy Meat
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork collar butt (shoulder cut, sliced slightly thin)
🥓 Pork belly (sliced slightly thick, for rendering oil)
🫑 Green pepper (cut into rolling knife chunks)
🧅 Scallion (cut into rings or horse ear slices)
🫚 Ginger (julienned)
🧄 Garlic (sliced or crushed, with some slices reserved for the end)
🌶️ Dried red chili peppers (to taste)
🫘 Sichuan peppercorns (to taste)
🫘 Dried fermented black beans (douchi) (to taste)
🧂 Cooking wine (for marinating)
🧂 Light soy sauce (for marinating and seasoning)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (for seasoning and coloring)
🧂 Salt (for final seasoning, adjust to taste)
🦪 Oyster sauce (added before serving)
🫒 Cooking oil (for coating the pan and stir-frying)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥢 Wok spatula
🥣 Bowls, plates

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the pork collar butt into slightly thin pieces, and the pork belly into slightly thick pieces.
   **Note**: Cut along or away from the sinew to prevent the meat from breaking apart during stir-frying.
2. Cut the green pepper into rolling knife chunks, the scallion into rings, the ginger into julienne strips, and the garlic into slices. Set aside the dried red chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and fermented black beans.
3. Heat the wok, add oil to coat the surface, then pour out the excess, leaving a thin layer of base oil or adding fresh cold oil. Add the pork belly slices. Stir-fry over medium heat until the belly slices curl and release their fat.
   **Heat level**: Medium heat.
   **Doneness check**: The belly slices curl and fat is rendered.
4. Once the pork belly is done, reduce to low heat and add the pork collar butt slices. Stir-fry until they just change color, turning pinkish-white but not fully cooked.
   **Key point**: Use low heat or residual heat to gently cook, preventing the lean meat from becoming tough.
5. As soon as the pork collar butt changes color, immediately add the fermented black beans, scallion rings, ginger strips, garlic slices, dried chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry over low heat to release the aromatics while preserving the meat's moisture.
6. Once the aromatics are fragrant, add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce, then turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly to develop the wok hei, releasing the savory and fresh flavors of the soy sauce.
7. Immediately after the soy sauce is fragrant, add the green pepper chunks and continue stir-frying over high heat until the peppers soften slightly but remain crisp.
8. Add salt and oyster sauce, stirring well to combine. Add oyster sauce at the end to preserve its freshness; since soy sauce already provides saltiness, add salt judiciously.
9. Finally, add the reserved garlic slices, stir-fry quickly a few times, then serve. Adding garlic slices at the end retains the raw garlic aroma.

## Health Advice
Spicy meat is rich in protein and quality fats, making it suitable for manual laborers with high energy expenditure, especially in winter. The rendered fat from the pork belly aids in the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins, but individuals with high blood lipids should reduce consumption. It is recommended to pair with green peppers to supplement vitamin C.
