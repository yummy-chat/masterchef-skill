# Bubble Milk Tea (Pearl Milk Tea)
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potato starch 60g (for making tapioca pearls)
🍬 Brown sugar 20g (for coloring and flavoring pearls)
🍂 Black tea 10g (for making milk tea base)
🥛 Whole milk 500g (for making milk tea)
🍚 White sugar 40g (for adjusting milk tea sweetness)
💧 Water 60g (for boiling brown sugar) + 100g (for brewing tea base)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Pot
🔥 Induction cooker
🥢 Chopsticks
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Knife
🥄 Strainer/sieve
🥣 Glass bowl
🥛 Measuring cup

## Instructions
1.  Place 60g water and 20g brown sugar in a pot. Bring to a full boil over high heat until large bubbles form, then turn off the heat.
2.  Immediately add 60g potato starch and stir quickly with chopsticks until evenly combined. The hot liquid will cook the starch, forming a dough.
**Note**: The water must be fully boiling, otherwise the starch cannot form a dough.
3.  Transfer the dough to a cutting board and knead until smooth and pliable. Roll into a long rope and cut into evenly sized pieces. Roll each piece into a round pearl shape between your palms.
4.  Sprinkle a layer of dry potato starch on the cutting board. Place the shaped pearls on top and gently roll them to coat, preventing sticking.
5.  Bring another pot of water to a boil. Add the pearls, reduce heat to medium, and cook for about 10 minutes, until all pearls float and become translucent and glossy.
**Note**: If the center of the pearls remains white and opaque, return them to the pot and cook for a few more minutes.
6.  Remove the cooked pearls and immediately rinse them in pre-cooled boiled water 3 times until completely cooled. This gives the pearls a chewy, Q-shaped texture without stickiness.
7.  Bring 100g water to a boil in a pot, then turn off the heat. Immediately add 10g black tea, stir, cover the pot, and steep for 3 minutes.
8.  Add 500g whole milk and 40g white sugar to the tea infusion. Stir over high heat until the sugar dissolves. Once it comes to a boil, immediately turn off the heat.
**Note**: Only bring to a boil; do not boil for a long time.
9.  Place an appropriate amount of the cooked and cooled pearls at the bottom of a cup. Strain the milk tea through a sieve into the cup and serve.

## Health Advice
For homemade bubble milk tea, it is recommended to reduce the sugar to 20g, as the original recipe's combination of 40g white sugar and brown sugar can easily raise blood sugar levels. This beverage is suitable for occasional indulgence and should not be consumed as a daily drink.
