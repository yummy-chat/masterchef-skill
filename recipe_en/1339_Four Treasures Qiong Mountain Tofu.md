# Four Treasures Qiong Mountain Tofu  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Egg whites 1 bowl  
💧 Water 1.5 bowls (if eggs are refrigerated) or 2 bowls (if eggs are at room temperature)  
🐔 Chicken gizzards (diced) appropriate amount  
🍗 Chicken meat (diced) appropriate amount  
🍤 Shrimp (peeled) appropriate amount  
🥒 Luffa (diced) appropriate amount  
🦀 Crab roe appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🧂 Salt a pinch (used in two places)  
🍬 Sugar a pinch (used in two places)  
🛢️ Oil a splash (for blanching and for making the broth)  
🍗 Chicken powder appropriate amount  
🧂 Ground white pepper appropriate amount  
🫗 Ginger-scallion chicken oil 1 tablespoon  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫗 Fine-mesh strainer / colander  
🌡️ Steamer or steaming pot  
🍽️ Flat-bottomed dish  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Large soup spoon  

## Instructions  
1. Crack the egg whites into a bowl and beat well with chopsticks until smooth. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer to remove the foam, ensuring a smooth surface on the egg mixture.  
2. Add water to the strained egg mixture, then season with a pinch of salt and sugar. Stir well and pour into the flat-bottomed dish.  
   **Key point**: Be sure to remove the foam with a strainer and steam over low heat to ensure the steamed egg whites are silky and tender like tofu, with a smooth, mirror-like surface.  

3. Place the flat dish into a steamer or steaming pot and steam over low heat for about 12 minutes, until the egg mixture is fully set.  
4. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a splash of oil, then blanch the diced chicken, chicken gizzards, and shrimp. Drain well.  
5. In another wok, add a splash of oil and water, then season with salt, sugar, chicken powder, and ground white pepper to make a broth. Bring to a boil.  
6. Return the blanched meats to the wok, then add the diced luffa. Cook for 1–2 minutes until tender.  
7. Just before turning off the heat, add 1 tablespoon of ginger-scallion chicken oil and stir well. Pour the cooked toppings and broth over the steamed egg whites.  
8. Finally, garnish with crab roe as desired.  

## Health Advice  
The steamed egg whites in this dish are silky and tender like tofu. Paired with chicken gizzards and shrimp, it offers a high-quality, high-protein combination, making it suitable for those aiming to build muscle or follow a low-fat diet. Note that crab roe is relatively high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids, blood pressure, or blood sugar should enjoy it in moderation.
