# Low-Fat Corn and Pork Rib Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork spare ribs, about 1 lb (rinsed clean)  
🥒 Chinese yam, 200-300 g (peeled and cut into large chunks)  
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms, 5-6 (stems removed and sliced)  
🍅 Tomatoes, 2 (cut into pieces)  
🌽 Sweet corn, 1 ear (cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallions or onion, to taste (chopped, for sautéing)  
🫚 Ginger, 3 slices (for blanching and simmering)  
🌿 Scallions or cilantro, to taste (sprinkled in at the end)  
🧂 Cooking wine, a dash (for removing odor during blanching)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (add gradually after tasting)  
🍬 Rock sugar, a small amount (about 2-3 small pieces, optional for enhancing flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for washing ingredients)  
🍳 Frying pan (for sautéing the base)  
🥘 Clay pot or rice cooker (for simmering the final dish)  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Peeler (for preparing the yam)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the ribs in a pot with cold water, add scallion segments, ginger slices, and a dash of cooking wine. Bring to a boil over medium heat, skim off the foam thoroughly, then remove and drain.
   **Note**: Starting with cold water fully removes the odor; ensure the foam is completely skimmed off.
2. Prepare the vegetables: Wear gloves to peel the yam, then cut into large chunks. Remove the stems from the mushrooms and slice them. Cut the tomatoes into pieces. Chop the scallions or onion.
3. Heat a frying pan, spray in a little cooking oil. Over medium heat, sauté the chopped scallions until fragrant. Add the tomato pieces and stir-fry, crushing them, until they soften and release juice.
4. Add the blanched ribs to the pan and stir-fry together with the tomato mixture.
5. Transfer all ingredients from the pan to a clay pot. Add the mushrooms, corn sections, and 3 slices of ginger. Pour in enough hot water to submerge the ingredients.
   **Key point**: Use hot water; otherwise, the rib meat will become tough.
6. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes.
7. Remove the lid. Add salt to taste in small batches along with a little rock sugar, then add the yam chunks. Continue simmering over low heat for about 15 minutes, until the yam is soft and tender.
   **Note**: Add salt gradually in small amounts and taste as you go; the rock sugar is mainly for enhancing flavor and can be omitted during a weight-loss period.
8. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in scallions and cilantro, letting the residual heat release their aroma. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
This low-fat corn and pork rib soup uses spare ribs paired with a variety of vegetables, offering a balance of high-quality protein and dietary fiber. It is suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or who prefer light meals. Remember to wear gloves when peeling the yam to avoid irritation, blanch the ribs to remove odor, and use enough hot water for simmering to keep the meat tender and soft.
