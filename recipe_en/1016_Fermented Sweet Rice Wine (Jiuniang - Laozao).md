# Fermented Sweet Rice Wine (Jiuniang / Laozao)  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🟤 Glutinous rice 800g (round glutinous rice recommended)  
🟤 Angel brand sweet wine yeast 8g  
🔴 Osmanthus flowers (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔧 Rice cooker or steamer  
🔧 Thermometer (optional but recommended)  
🔧 One clean, airtight glass or ceramic container  
🔧 Rolling pin  
🔧 Plastic wrap (if needed)  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the glutinous rice clean, place it in the rice cooker, add water, and cook using the steaming setting until done.  
   **Key point**: Using round glutinous rice gives a better texture; ensure the rice is fully cooked but not too mushy.

2. Turn the cooked rice out and let it cool, using clean utensils to cool it down to 30°C.  
   **Key point**: If the temperature is too high, it will kill the yeast; if too low, fermentation will be slow. It is crucial to control the temperature within this range.

3. Dissolve the Angel sweet wine yeast in 20ml of warm water, sprinkle it evenly over the glutinous rice, and mix thoroughly so that the yeast comes into full contact with the rice.  
   **Key point**: The water used to dissolve the yeast should be at a similar temperature to the rice to avoid a large temperature difference.

4. Add water to the glutinous rice and continue mixing evenly so the rice absorbs enough moisture. Let it sit for 2–3 minutes to allow the grains to fully absorb the water.  
   **Key point**: Adding water increases the amount of liquid produced, but do not add too much; mix gently to avoid breaking the rice grains.

5. Use a rolling pin to make a small well in the center of the glutinous rice to allow liquid to seep out during fermentation.  
   **Key point**: The well makes it easy to observe the liquid output and also helps with ventilation.

6. Transfer the mixed rice into a clean container, gently press the surface flat, and cover with a lid or seal tightly with plastic wrap.  
   **Key point**: The container must be free of oil and water, or fermentation may fail; the seal should be tight to prevent contamination by unwanted microorganisms.

7. Place in an environment at 28–32°C for 24–48 hours of fermentation. Do not shake or move the container violently during fermentation.  
   **Key point**: A constant temperature is crucial; this can be achieved using a fermentation box, electric blanket, or near a radiator. Too high a temperature will kill the yeast, while too low a temperature will slow or even halt fermentation.

8. Signs of successful fermentation: Clear liquid seeps into the central well, a mild, elegant rice wine aroma is present, and there is no sour or off smell. If mold spots or a sour odor appear, the fermentation has failed and the product should not be consumed.  
   **Key point**: Fermentation time varies with temperature; it may be as short as 24 hours in summer and as long as 48 hours in winter.

9. Immediately after fermentation is complete, transfer the container to the refrigerator for storage. Osmanthus flowers can be added at this stage for extra flavor. Use clean utensils each time when taking out the product; refrigerated, it can be stored for 7–10 days.  
   **Key point**: To stop the fermentation, steam the rice for 10 minutes to kill the yeast before refrigerating; a second fermentation is also possible.

## Health Tips  
Fermented sweet rice wine is rich in probiotics and B vitamins. It can aid digestion and gently tonify qi, making it suitable for people with a cold constitution or those needing to regulate their digestive system. Be mindful of the amount consumed; about half a small bowl per serving is appropriate. Diabetic patients should add less sugar when seasoning.
