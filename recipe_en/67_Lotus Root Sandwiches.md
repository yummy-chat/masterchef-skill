# Lotus Root Sandwiches  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥒 Lotus root – appropriate amount (peeled and cut into sandwich slices)  
🥩 Ground pork – appropriate amount (80% lean, 20% fat)  
💧 Seasoned water – appropriate amount  
🫒 Sichuan pepper oil – appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce – appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar – appropriate amount  
🧂 White pepper powder – appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil – appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch – appropriate amount  
🌾 All-purpose flour – appropriate amount  
🧂 Baking powder – appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt – a small amount  
🥜 Peanut oil – appropriate amount  
💧 Water – appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Bowl  
🍳 Deep pot  
🥄 Strainer  

## Instructions  
1. **Prepare the lotus root**: Peel the lotus root. Place two chopsticks parallel on either side of a lotus root section. Cut vertically with the knife, stopping the first cut when it touches the chopsticks. Remove the chopsticks, then cut completely through on the second cut to create a sandwich slice with the bottom still attached.  
   **Key point**: Using chopsticks ensures the slice is not cut all the way through, creating an even pocket.

2. **Make the filling**: Place the ground pork (80% lean, 20% fat) into a bowl. Add seasoned water, Sichuan pepper oil, light soy sauce, white sugar, white pepper powder, sesame oil, and cornstarch. Mix vigorously until well combined and the mixture becomes sticky.  
   **Key point**: Stir thoroughly until the filling forms a cohesive, springy texture.

3. **Fill the lotus root sandwiches**: Take a sandwich slice of lotus root, open the pocket, and fill it with an appropriate amount of meat filling. Gently press together so the lotus root and filling adhere tightly.  
   **Key point**: Use a moderate amount of filling to avoid tearing the lotus root or causing overflow.

4. **Prepare the crispy batter**: In a bowl, mix cornstarch, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and a small amount of salt. Add peanut oil, then gradually add water while stirring until you achieve a smooth, pourable batter.  
   **Key point**: The batter should be thick enough to evenly coat the lotus root sandwiches—too thin and it won't stick, too thick and the coating will be overly heavy.

5. **Coat the sandwiches**: Dip each filled lotus root sandwich into the batter, ensuring an even coating on all sides.  
   **Key point**: Make sure no area is missed, otherwise the meat filling may burn during frying.

6. **First fry**: Heat a generous amount of oil in a deep pot over medium heat. Gently add the battered lotus root sandwiches one by one. Fry until they float, the coating is set, and they turn a light golden color, then remove.  
   **Key point**: Use medium heat first to cook through and set the coating, avoiding high heat which can burn the outside while leaving the inside raw.

7. **Second fry**: Increase the oil temperature. Add all the pre-fried lotus root sandwiches and fry again over high heat until the surface is golden and crispy. Remove and drain the oil.  
   **Key point**: The second fry is crucial for a crispy exterior—fry quickly to achieve crunchiness and remove immediately once the color deepens.

## Health Tips  
Lotus root is rich in dietary fiber. Pairing it with lean meat filling reduces greasiness, making it suitable for those who want to control calorie intake but still enjoy fried food. The second fry increases calorie content, so it's recommended to serve with a vegetable salad for balanced nutrition, and limit consumption to 2–3 pieces per serving.
