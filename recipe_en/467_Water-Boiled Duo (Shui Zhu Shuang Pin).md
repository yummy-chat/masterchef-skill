# Water-Boiled Duo (Shui Zhu Shuang Pin)
Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 One mandarin fish (about 600g)  
🦀 About 600g winter crab  
🥒 2 cucumbers  
🥬 1/4 head Napa cabbage  
🧅 Scallions, to taste  
🫚 Ginger, to taste  
🧄 1 head garlic  
🌶️ 1 small bowl dried chilies  
🌸 1 small handful Sichuan peppercorns  
🤍 White sesame seeds, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ Ground white pepper, to taste  
🥔 2 tbsp potato starch  
🧂 1 tsp chicken bouillon  
🍶 Cooking oil, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🥒 Vegetable peeler  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowls/basin  
🍲 Stainless steel pot  
🥄 Strainer/skimmer  
♨️ Steamer  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Scale the mandarin fish, separate the head, tail, and bones. Slice the cleaned fillet diagonally into large pieces. Rinse under running water until the water runs clear, drain, and pat dry with kitchen paper towels.  
2. Remove the crab shell, discard the gills, mouth, and heart. Snip off all the tips of the crab legs, crack the claws with the flat side of a knife, and chop the body into four large pieces. Keep the roe on the shell, place ginger slices on top of the shell and pieces, and set aside.  
3. Peel and slice the cucumbers. Tear the Napa cabbage leaves into small pieces, then wash both.  
4. Cut some scallions into sections and some into葱花 (scallion blossoms). Slice the ginger and mince the garlic.  
5. Add salt, ground white pepper, and potato starch to the fish slices. Gently toss in one direction to coat evenly, taking care not to break the slices.  
6. After the steamer water comes to a boil, place the crab shell and pieces in (shell side up) and steam over high heat for 6 minutes.  
7. Heat oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Add the fish head, tail, and bones, and fry until golden on both sides. Add scallion sections and ginger slices, stir-frying until fragrant.  
**Note**: Avoid flipping the fish too often during frying to prevent the bones from breaking.  

8. Pour in enough boiling water, add 2 tsp salt and 1 tsp chicken bouillon. Boil vigorously for about 5 minutes until the broth turns milky white.  
**Key**: You must use boiling water to achieve the rich, white broth.  

9. Keep the fish broth at a rolling boil. Add the cucumber slices and Napa cabbage pieces, cooking until just tender. Remove and spread them in the bottom of a large soup bowl.  
**Note**: Do not overcook the vegetables; keep them crisp for the best texture.  

10. Evenly arrange the steamed crab pieces and shell on top of the vegetables.  
11. With the broth boiling, add the marinated fish slices one by one. Once the broth boils again and the fish slices turn white and curl, immediately remove them with a skimmer and place on top of the crab.  
**Note**: Do not stir the broth after adding the fish slices to prevent breakage.  

12. Gently pour the broth from the pot along the edge of the bowl to cover the ingredients.  
13. Quickly rinse the dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns, then drain. In a separate pot, heat a generous amount of oil until it starts to smoke, then add the chilies and peppercorns and fry until fragrant.  
14. Spread a generous amount of minced garlic over the fish slices, then immediately pour the hot oil (with the chilies and peppercorns) over the garlic to release the aroma.  
**Key**: The oil must be hot enough to fully bloom the flavors.  

15. Sprinkle with scallion blossoms and white sesame seeds before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and relatively low in fat, making it suitable for fitness and muscle-building goals. Since the broth contains a fair amount of oil, be mindful of your calorie intake. Steaming the crab preserves its natural freshness, while the tender fish slices and vegetables contribute to a balanced nutrient profile.
