# Salted Pork and Rice with Greens 🥬  
Cuisine: 🏷️ Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: 🌱 Winter (November-December)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Rice  
🥬 Greens (recommended short-stem Shanghai bok choy)  
🥬 Salted pork (lightly salted)  
🥬 Winter bamboo shoots (optional)  
🧂 Lard  
🧂 Cooking wine (optional)  
🧂 White sugar (optional)  
🧂 White pepper powder (optional)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🍳 Rice cooker  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  

## Preparation Steps  
1. If using winter bamboo shoots, slice them thinly, place in cold water and bring to a boil, continue boiling for 10 minutes, then drain to remove bitterness.  

2. Cut the salted pork into 1 cm cubes.  

3. In a wok, add 10g of lard, then add the salted pork cubes and bamboo shoot slices. Drizzle with cooking wine and sprinkle with sugar. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the pork turns translucent and bubbles, then remove and set aside.  
   **Note**: If the salted pork is too salty, soak the lean meat in a 5% sugar water solution for 20 minutes, or mix with fresh pork belly cubes to reduce saltiness.  

4. Wash the greens, separating stems and leaves. Cut stems into 0.5 cm cubes and leaves into 2-3 cm long, 1-1.5 cm wide rectangular pieces.  

5. In a separate wok, stir-fry the stem pieces until they turn emerald green, then remove and set aside.  

6. Rinse the rice and place it in the rice cooker, adding the appropriate amount of water.  

7. Spread the fried salted pork, bamboo shoot slices, and stem pieces over the rice without stirring.  
   **Key Point**: Do not stir during cooking to maintain layered texture.  

8. Start the normal cooking cycle. During the last 10 minutes, open the lid quickly and spread the leaf pieces over the rice surface, then continue cooking until the cycle ends.  

9. After cooking, let it rest for 5 minutes, then drizzle with 5g of lard, sprinkle with white pepper powder, and quickly mix evenly.  

## Health Advice  
The lard and salted pork in this dish provide rich flavor, suitable for supplementing energy in winter, but those with high blood pressure should control the amount of salted pork. Pair with light vegetables to balance sodium intake. It is recommended to use lightly salted pork or mix with fresh pork belly to avoid excessive saltiness.
