# Assorted Vegetable Balls
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥕 Carrot, about 1 root (shredded)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, about 5-6 pieces (soaked until soft, then shredded)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, about a handful (soaked until soft, then shredded)
🥬 Spinach, about a handful (blanched, squeezed dry, then chopped)
🥚 Eggs, 2 pieces (scrambled and chopped)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)
🦐 Dried shrimp, appropriate amount
🥜 Crushed peanuts, appropriate amount
🫘 Cooked sesame seeds, appropriate amount
🌽 Cornstarch, about 60 grams (for mixing into the filling to add stickiness)
🌽 Fine corn flour, appropriate amount (for coating the outside)

🧂 Cooked oil, 1 tablespoon (used in blanching and mixing the filling)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Sugar, appropriate amount
🧂 Thirteen Spices, a pinch
🧄 Garlic paste, appropriate amount (for dipping sauce)
🌶️ Chili powder, appropriate amount (for dipping sauce)
🥜 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for dipping sauce)
🍎 Aromatic vinegar, appropriate amount (for dipping sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon/Strainer
🔥 Stove
🔪 Cutting board, Chef's knife
🥣 Large bowl
♨️ Steamer, Steaming pot
🥣 Bowl, Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare Ingredients**: Scramble the eggs in a pan, break them into small pieces with a spatula, let cool, then chop finely. Shred the carrot, soaked shiitake mushrooms, and wood ear mushrooms into thin strips. Blanch the whole spinach in boiling water with a little cooked oil added, remove promptly once wilted, drain and cool in cold water, squeeze out excess water, then chop finely.
   **Note**: Adding cooked oil when blanching spinach helps retain its vibrant green color; the blanching time should be short. All vegetables must be thoroughly squeezed dry to prevent the filling from releasing water.

2. **Mix the Filling**: Place the shredded carrot, shredded mushrooms, shredded wood ear mushrooms, chopped spinach, and chopped scrambled egg into a large bowl. Add the chopped scallions, dried shrimp, crushed peanuts, and cooked sesame seeds. Mix well.
   **State Check**: All ingredients should be chopped fine and roughly the same size.

3. **Season and Seal Moisture**: First, drizzle in one tablespoon of cooked oil. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks so the oil evenly coats the vegetables. Then add salt, chicken powder, sugar, and a pinch of Thirteen Spices. Mix well again.
   **Key Point**: Always add the oil before the seasonings. The oil seals the cut surfaces of the vegetables, preventing them from releasing water after adding salt. Season lightly to highlight the natural flavors of the ingredients.

4. **Add Binder**: Add the cornstarch. Wearing disposable gloves, thoroughly mix everything by hand, squeezing and massaging until the filling becomes sticky and holds together easily.

5. **Form Balls and Coat**: Take an appropriate amount of filling in your palm. Compress and roll it repeatedly in your hands to form a tight, firm ball. Roll the ball in the fine corn flour to coat it with a thin layer. Take it out, gently squeeze it in your hands again, and roll it in the corn flour one more time.
   **Key Point**: The "squeeze-roll-squeeze-roll" process ensures the outer coating binds firmly to the inner filling, preventing them from separating after steaming.

6. **Steam**: Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Line the steamer basket with a cloth. Place the vegetable balls in the basket. Steam over high heat for 8 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The vegetables are already mostly cooked by blanching, so the steaming time should not be too long. 8 minutes is enough to cook the outer coating while keeping the vegetables tender.

7. **Make Dipping Sauce**: In a bowl, combine garlic paste, chili powder, and white sesame seeds. Pour hot oil (heated to about 180°C / 350°F) over them to release the aroma. Let it cool slightly, then add aromatic vinegar and stir well. The vegetable balls are light in flavor, so pairing them with this spicy-sour dipping sauce enhances the taste.

## Health Advice
These assorted vegetable balls are rich in dietary fiber and various vitamins. The combination of carrot, spinach, and wood ear mushrooms helps promote gut health and boost immunity. Suitable for people on a weight loss diet or those needing to control blood sugar. Note the moderate amount of cornstarch and avoid making the dipping sauce too salty.
