# Canned Yellow Peach
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍑 Yellow peaches 1500g (peach flesh)
🍬 White sugar 150g (approximately 1/10 of peach flesh weight)
🧂 Salt A pinch (for enhancing flavor)
🍋 Lemons 2 (use zest and juice)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife, peeler
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Skimmer (for skimming foam)
🧴 Glass jars or lunch boxes (for storing the canned peaches)

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the yellow peaches under running water, peel off the skin, cut them in half, remove the pits, and cut the peach flesh into even crescent-shaped pieces.
   **Note**: The cut peach pieces can be briefly soaked in lightly salted water to prevent oxidation and discoloration and to remove any astringency.
2. Drain the peach pieces and pour them into a non-stick pan. Add white sugar, salt, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
   **Key Point**: All seasonings must be added before adding water, allowing the peach pieces to initially marinate and release their juices.
3. Pour clean water into the pan, just enough to submerge the peach pieces. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 8 minutes.
   **Doneness Check**: Cook until the liquid in the pan has reduced to about half of its original volume, and the peach pieces can be easily pierced through with chopsticks.
4. During the boiling process, use a skimmer to promptly remove any white foam that rises to the surface, ensuring the resulting syrup is clear.
5. Once cooked, immediately transfer the peach pieces and syrup into sterilized glass jars or lunch boxes while still hot. Serve warm, or let cool and refrigerate for a chilled, refreshing canned yellow peach treat.

## Health Advice
Canned yellow peaches have a moderate sugar content. The addition of lemon juice helps retain more vitamin C, making them suitable for cooling down and relieving greasiness in the summer. Diabetics should control their intake, while children can enjoy them as a healthier alternative to high-sugar snacks.
