# Stir-Fried Mixed Vegetables
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Dried vermicelli 80g
🌱 Chinese chives 200g
🌱 Bean sprouts 100g
🥚 Eggs 4
🧅 Scallion 20g (finely chopped)
🫚 Ginger 5g (julienned)
🧄 Garlic 15g (sliced)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste (cut into sections, adjust according to preference)
🧂 Dark soy sauce 8g
🧂 Light soy sauce 20g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g
🍬 White sugar 3g
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 1.5g
🧂 Salt 1.5g
🫒 Cooking oil as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Bowl
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍳 Wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spatula
🥘 Strainer
🥄 Measuring spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the dried vermicelli in a bowl and pour in freshly boiled water to soak until soft enough to be easily snapped with fingers. Once done, drain with a strainer. If too long, cut shorter with scissors. To prevent sticking, rinse once with cold water and drain again. Return the vermicelli to the bowl, add dark soy sauce, and mix evenly with chopsticks to color.
2. Wash the bean sprouts and drain; wash the Chinese chives, drain, and cut into inch-long sections.
3. Finely chop the scallion, julienne the ginger, slice the garlic, and cut the dried chili peppers into sections. Place all into a small bowl and set aside.
4. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat thoroughly with chopsticks until well mixed. Set aside.
5. Heat the wok over high heat until smoking, add a generous amount of cooking oil, and swirl to coat the sides. When the oil also smokes, slowly pour in the egg mixture with one hand while quickly stirring with chopsticks in the other hand, until the eggs set into fluffy pieces. Once set, immediately remove with chopsticks and set aside.
6. Leave a thin layer of oil in the wok. Keep the heat high and immediately add the scallion, ginger, garlic, and dried chili peppers, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add the bean sprouts and stir-fry quickly with a spatula to prevent the aromatics from burning, until the bean sprouts become slightly tender.
7. Add the colored vermicelli and the cooked egg pieces. Stir-fry quickly until evenly combined.
8. Reduce heat to the lowest setting. Drizzle light soy sauce and oyster sauce along the edge of the wok, then add white sugar, chicken bouillon powder, and salt.
9. Turn the heat back to high and stir-fry the seasonings and ingredients together until well combined. Then add all of the Chinese chives and continue stir-frying quickly over high heat.
10. When the chives become tender and turn a bright green, immediately turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.
    **Key point**: Do not overcook the chives, as this will compromise their texture.

## Health Advice
Stir-fried mixed vegetables are rich in protein and dietary fiber, making them suitable for weight management. However, the vermicelli and oil content are relatively high, so it is recommended to pair this dish with a light soup to balance calories. Chinese chives and bean sprouts provide vitamin C, while eggs supply high-quality protein. For a healthier option, use less salt and oil.
