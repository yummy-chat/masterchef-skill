# Flavored Pork Liver 🐖
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork liver 400g (fresh, sliced into 2mm thin pieces)
🍺 Beer as needed (for washing to remove odor)
🧂 Salt about 0.5g (for marinating)
🌶️ Ground pepper 1g (for marinating) + 1g (for seasoning)
🍶 Light soy sauce 3g (for marinating) + 2g (for seasoning)
🥚 Egg yolk 1
🌾 Dried starch as needed
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed (for coating the liver)
🫒 Rapeseed oil 30g (for stir-frying base oil)
🧄 Ginger about 8g (smashed and minced)
🧄 Garlic as needed (minced)
🌶️ Xiaomi chili peppers as needed (chopped)
🧅 Garlic sprouts 3 stalks (chopped)
🧅 Scallions 2 stalks (chopped)
🧅 Spring onion heads 3 stalks (chopped)
🌶️ Pickled wild chili peppers as needed (chopped)
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste) 5g
🍶 Cooking wine 3g
🍶 Aromatic vinegar 1g
🍬 White sugar a pinch
🧂 Chicken bouillon 1g
✨ Clarified oil a splash

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Cleaver
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔥 Stove

## Instructions
1.  Slice the pork liver into 2mm thin slices. Place in a bowl, add a suitable amount of beer, and rub and wash for 1 minute. Rinse clean with water and squeeze out excess water.
2.  Add 0.5g salt, 1g ground pepper, 3g light soy sauce, and 1 egg yolk to the pork liver. Mix well. Then add a suitable amount of dried starch, stir continuously in one direction for about 2 minutes until sticky. Finally, drizzle in a little vegetable oil, mix well, and set aside to marinate.
**Note**: The dried starch must be fully incorporated to prevent it from falling off during cooking.

3.  Smash the ginger and mince it. Mince the garlic. Chop the Xiaomi chili peppers, garlic sprouts, scallions, spring onion heads, and pickled wild chili peppers.
4.  Heat the wok until hot, pour in 30g of rapeseed oil and heat until it reaches 70% heat. Add all the chopped aromatics and 5g of doubanjiang. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant and the oil turns red.
**Note**: The aromatics and doubanjiang must be stir-fried thoroughly to release their fragrance, forming the flavor base.

5.  Add the marinated pork liver. Turn to medium heat and quickly stir-fry until it is about 80% cooked.
6.  Drizzle 3g of cooking wine along the edge of the wok. Add 2g light soy sauce, 1g aromatic vinegar, a pinch of white sugar, 1g ground pepper, and 1g chicken bouillon. Stir-fry over medium heat until well combined.
7.  Finally, drizzle in a splash of clarified oil, quickly stir-fry to mix evenly, and transfer to a serving plate.
**Note**: The seasoning step must be done quickly. Adding clarified oil enhances shine and aroma.

## Health Advice
Pork liver is rich in iron and Vitamin A, beneficial for blood replenishment and eye health. However, it is high in cholesterol, so individuals with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Quick stir-frying helps retain nutrients, but ensure the liver is cooked thoroughly to eliminate the risk of parasites.
