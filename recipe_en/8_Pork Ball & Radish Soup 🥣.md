# Pork Ball & Radish Soup 🥣  
**Cuisine:** Common Home-style Dish  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Winter  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  

### Main Ingredients  
🐖 Ground pork – about 250g (shoulder or loin, 20% fat, 80% lean)  
🥕 White radish – to taste (peeled and cut into shreds)  
🍜 Vermicelli – to taste (soaked in advance until soft)  
🔴 Goji berries – about 10 pieces  
🧅 Scallion – about half a stalk (minced)  
🫚 Ginger – about 1 small piece (minced)  

### Seasonings  
🧂 Salt – about 1½ teaspoons (used in batches)  
🌿 White pepper – about 1 teaspoon (used in batches)  
🥚 Egg liquid – about 1 egg worth  
💧 Water – about half a bowl  
🫒 Sesame oil – about 2 tablespoons (used in batches)  
🧂 MSG – a pinch (optional)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Large bowl  
🔪 Knife, 🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the meat mixture:** Place ground pork in a large bowl. Add minced scallion and ginger, about 1 teaspoon of salt, and about ½ teaspoon of white pepper. Mix briefly to combine.  
   **Key:** The ideal fat-to-lean ratio is 20% fat to 80% lean for best texture.  
2. **Add water for tenderness:** Gradually pour in about half a bowl of water, stirring vigorously in one direction with chopsticks until the water is fully absorbed and the mixture becomes firm.  
   **Key:** Sufficiently hydrated meat ensures juicy and tender meatballs.  
3. **Incorporate egg liquid:** Add the beaten egg in batches, continuing to stir in the same direction until evenly blended.  
   **Key:** Add egg liquid gradually to prevent the meat from releasing excess moisture.  
4. **Seal with sesame oil:** Add about 1 tablespoon of sesame oil and mix well to lock in moisture. Let rest for a while.  
   **Key:** Adding oil at the end enhances flavor and prevents the meat surface from drying out.  
5. **Shred the radish:** Peel the white radish, slice it, then cut into thin shreds.  
   **Key:** Shreds should not be too thick, as that affects texture and cooking time.  
6. **Blanch the radish shreds:** Bring water to a boil, add the radish shreds, blanch until the water returns to a gentle boil, then immediately remove and drain.  
   **Key:** Blanching effectively removes the raw, pungent taste of the radish.  
7. **Poach the meatballs:** In another pot, bring water to a boil. When small bubbles appear (about 80–90°C), reduce to the lowest heat, maintaining a “barely simmering” state.  
   **Key:** Keep the heat low throughout—this is the core technique for poaching meatballs. High heat will cause the meatballs to break apart or become tough.  
8. **Shape and drop the meatballs:** Use your left hand’s thumb and forefinger to squeeze the meat mixture into a round shape. With a spoon (dipped in water), scoop it off and gently place it into the pot.  
   **Key:** Dip the spoon in water to prevent sticking; work quickly and smoothly.  
9. **Cook the meatballs gently:** After all meatballs are added, continue cooking on low heat. Once they float to the surface and their color changes from pink to grayish-white, they are fully cooked.  
   **Key:** Low-temperature slow cooking ensures the meatballs hold their shape and remain tender.  
10. **Remove the meatballs:** Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked meatballs to a bowl, reserving the broth in the pot.  
    **Key:** Use the original broth to cook the radish for a richer flavor.  
11. **Cook the radish shreds:** Place the drained blanched radish shreds into the reserved broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat.  
12. **Second round of seasoning:** Add the remaining ½ teaspoon of salt, ½ teaspoon of white pepper, and a pinch of MSG. Stir to combine.  
    **Key:** Adjust saltiness and pepper heat according to personal taste.  
13. **Add garnishes:** Add the soaked vermicelli and goji berries. Continue cooking for 1–2 minutes.  
14. **Return the meatballs:** Put the cooked meatballs back into the pot with the radish and vermicelli. Cook until just simmering.  
15. **Finish with oil:** Drizzle in about 1 tablespoon of sesame oil, stir well, then turn off the heat and serve.  
    **Key:** Adding sesame oil right before serving maximizes its aroma.  

## Health Tips  
This dish pairs meat and vegetables—radish aids digestion, and the meatballs are high in protein, making it perfect for warming up in winter. Watch your portion size. Proper hydration of the meat mixture ensures tender meatballs, and keeping the heat low throughout poaching helps them hold their shape without falling apart.
