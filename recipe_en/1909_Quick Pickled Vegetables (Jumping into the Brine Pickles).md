# Quick Pickled Vegetables (Jumping into the Brine Pickles)
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥕 Radish (to taste, peeled and cut into sticks)
🫚 Young ginger (to taste, sliced or cut into sticks)
🧅 Onion (to taste, cut into chunks)
🫑 Large green pepper (to taste, seeded and cut into sticks)
🌶️ Large red pepper (to taste, seeded and cut into sticks)
🥬 Lettuce stem/celtuce (to taste, peeled and cut into sticks)
🥬 Celery (to taste, cut into sections)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili (about 50g, finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic (about 100g, minced)
🫚 Ginger (1 small piece, sliced)
🌿 Scallions (a few, cut into sections)
🌿 Cilantro (a few, cut into sections)
🪵 Cinnamon stick (2 small pieces)
⭐ Star anise (2 pieces)
🍃 Sand ginger/Kaempferia galanga (10g)
🫑 Green Sichuan pepper (3g)

🧂 Table salt (50g for initial pickling + 50g for pickling brine)
🍬 White sugar (50g for initial pickling)
🍬 Rock sugar (150g)
🍶 Red Zhejiang vinegar (150g)
🍶 White vinegar (50g)
🧂 MSG (6g)
🧂 Chicken bouillon (6g)
🍚 Glutinous rice sweet wine (50g)
💧 Purified water (about 1500g)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🔪 Chef's knife
🪚 Cutting board
🫙 Glass pickling jar
🥄 Spoon
🧤 Gloves

## Preparation Steps
1.  Wash the main ingredients such as radish, young ginger, onion, green and red peppers, lettuce stem, and celery. Cut into uniform small sticks.
2.  Add 50g of table salt and 50g of white sugar. Mix well by hand and let marinate for 20 minutes to allow the vegetables to release water and become crisp.
3.  Prepare the aromatics: Finely chop the bird's eye chili, mince the garlic, cut the scallions into sections, slice the ginger, and cut the cilantro into sections.
4.  Place the prepared aromatics into a clean basin along with the cinnamon sticks, star anise, sand ginger, and green Sichuan pepper.
5.  Add 50g of table salt, 150g of rock sugar, 150g of red Zhejiang vinegar, 50g of white vinegar, 6g of MSG, 6g of chicken bouillon, and 50g of glutinous rice sweet wine. Stir thoroughly to combine.
6.  Pour in approximately 1500g of purified water. Stir again to fully combine, creating the pickling brine.
7.  Drain the marinated vegetables thoroughly. Place them into a clean glass pickling jar.
8.  Pour the pickling brine into the jar, ensuring the vegetables are completely submerged.
9.  Close the jar lid and fill the rim channel with fresh water to create an airtight seal.
**Key**: The water in the rim channel must always be kept full to seal out air and prevent contamination by unwanted bacteria.
10. Let the jar sit sealed at room temperature for 6 hours. After this time, it is ready to open and serve.
**Note**: The pickling time should not be too short; 6 hours allows the pickles to become fully flavored while maintaining their crisp texture.

## Health Advice
This quick pickled vegetable dish is rich in dietary fiber and probiotics. It is low in fat, crisp and refreshing, and suitable for stimulating the appetite and aiding digestion. Note that the salt and sugar content is not low; individuals with high blood pressure or those managing blood sugar levels should consume it in moderation.
