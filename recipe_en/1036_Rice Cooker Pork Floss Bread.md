# Rice Cooker Pork Floss Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 400g (100g for starter, 300g for main dough)
💧 Water 100g (for starter)
🥛 Milk 150g
🍄 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerant) 5g (1g for starter, 4g for main dough)
🥚 Egg 1
🫒 Odorless vegetable oil (or butter) 30g
🥓 Pork floss (pork sung) to taste (thick layer)
🧂 Salt 4g
🍬 White sugar 50g
🫘 White sesame seeds to taste
🌿 Chopped scallions to taste
🥗 Mayonnaise to taste

## Cookware & Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker
🫙 Plastic bottle (homemade measuring cup)
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🌡️ Plastic wrap
🔪 Kitchen knife
📄 Parchment paper or plastic bag
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
1. **Make the starter**
   Mix 100g bread flour, 100g water, and 1g instant dry yeast into a dough. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight to ferment until doubled in size.
   **Note**: Keep yeast sealed to prevent loss of activity.

2. **Mix the main dough**
   Remove the starter from the fridge and let it come to room temperature. Add the remaining 4g dry yeast. Then add 300g bread flour, 150g milk, 1 whole egg, 4g salt, and 50g white sugar. First stir into a flaky texture with chopsticks, then knead by hand into a uniform, sticky dough.
   **Note**: The dough has high hydration; stickiness is normal and key to a soft bread.

3. **Develop gluten through folding**
   Cover the dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 20 minutes to rest. Wet your hands with water, fold the edges of the dough toward the center, and tighten it. Refrigerate again for 20 minutes. Repeat the folding-resting process 2–3 times, until the dough can stretch into a uniform thin membrane.
   **Note**: Use folding instead of traditional kneading. Dip hands in water to prevent sticking. Be patient to allow gluten to develop gradually.

4. **Incorporate the fat**
   Pour 30g vegetable oil into the dough. Repeatedly pinch, stretch, and fold the dough until the oil is fully absorbed and the dough surface becomes smooth.
   **Note**: Fat makes the texture more tender and helps delay staling.

5. **First fermentation**
   Shape the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place for about 50 minutes, until doubled in size.

6. **Shape and fill**
   Dust the work surface generously with flour. Turn out the fermented dough and gently press to release air. Roll out into a large sheet. Spread mayonnaise evenly over the surface, then layer with a thick amount of pork floss. Roll up from top to bottom into a long log, brushing the seam with water to seal. Cut the log in half lengthwise, exposing the filling layers. Twist the two strands together like a braid, then coil into a large round spiral.
   **Note**: Use plenty of flour to prevent sticking. Exposing the filling during cutting creates a beautiful spiral pattern.

7. **Second fermentation**
   Line the rice cooker inner pot with parchment paper. Place the shaped dough spiral inside. Cover the lid (do not turn on). Let it ferment for about 35 minutes, until the dough nearly doubles in size and almost fills the pot.
   **Note**: Check doneness—the dough should feel light and airy.

8. **Bake**
   Lightly spray the dough surface with water. Sprinkle with white sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Close the rice cooker lid and press the "Cook" button. Heat for about 50 minutes. Open the lid and gently press the bread surface—if it springs back quickly, it is done. Turn out onto a cooling rack and let cool.
   **Note**: Use the "Cook" button only once during the entire process. Confirm doneness by spring-back test to avoid under- or over-baking.

## Health Advice
This rice cooker pork floss bread uses the starter method to increase moisture, resulting in a soft texture suitable for home baking beginners. Pork floss and mayonnaise provide protein and flavor but are high in calories. It is recommended to pair with milk as breakfast. Be mindful of sugar and oil amounts to avoid excessive intake.
