# Braised Cabbage in Thick Broth  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 1 large baby cabbage (washed and shredded)  
🥚 3 eggs  
🧄 5-6 cloves of garlic (chopped into garlic granules)  
🌾 About half a bowl of flour  
🍜 Vermicelli/glass noodles, as needed (pre-soaked until soft, optional)  
🍤 About 1 small bowl of shrimp (washed and drained)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste  
🌶️ Ground white pepper, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more for fried egg floss)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍲 Clay pot/soup pot  
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife

## Cooking Steps  
1.  Heat a little oil in the wok, add the shredded cabbage and stir-fry until softened. Remove and set aside.  
2.  **Make the egg floss**: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until the temperature is high. Slowly drizzle in the beaten eggs from the edge of the wok, while simultaneously stirring rapidly and continuously with a spatula to fry the eggs into fine, fluffy shreds. When they turn a deep golden brown, turn off the heat. Remove the egg floss with a strainer, reserving the oil at the bottom.  
    **Key point**: High oil temperature and rapid stirring are crucial for forming the egg floss.  
3.  **Cook the broth base**: Pour the reserved egg-frying oil back into the wok. Add the garlic granules and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant. Add the shrimp, turn the heat to medium, and stir-fry until they turn red and are lightly seared on the surface. Add the flour and stir-fry constantly until it turns light yellow and releases a toasty wheat aroma.  
4.  Immediately pour in a sufficient amount of boiling water, stirring quickly to combine, forming a thick, milky white broth.  
5.  Return the stir-fried cabbage and the fried egg floss to the wok. Add the pre-soaked vermicelli if desired. Cover with a lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  
6.  Remove the lid, season with chicken bouillon and salt, and adjust the taste to your liking. Finally, sprinkle in an appropriate amount of ground white pepper, stir to combine, and cook for about 1 more minute before turning off the heat.

## Health Advice  
This dish uses fried egg floss as a substitute for traditional thickening (slurry). It is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for warming the body in winter. Be mindful of the oil quantity; individuals with high blood lipids can reduce the amount of egg floss used.
