# Shaanxi Biangbiang Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g  
🧂 Salt 3g (added to flour)  
💧 Room temperature water about 230-240g  

**Spices (for spiced water)**  
🌿 Cumin 4g  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 3g  
🥜 Cardamom 1 piece (seeds removed)  
⭐ Star anise 1 piece  
🌳 Cinnamon stick 3g  
🌸 Cloves 2 pieces  
🍃 Bay leaf 1g  
💧 Boiling water about 2000g  

**Compound Seasoning Sauce**  
🧄 Minced ginger as needed  
🫘 Soybean oil a little  
🍇 Fragrant vinegar 200g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 30g  
🫘 Bean paste 10g  
🥣 Braising liquid 40g  
🥄 Soy sauce 40g  
🧂 Salt 80g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 15g  
🧂 MSG 6g  
🍬 Rock sugar 4g  

**Bowl Base & Toppings**  
🥄 Compound seasoning sauce about 2 tablespoons  
🧅 Chopped scallions as needed  
🌶️ Chili powder as needed  
🥬 Vegetable toppings as needed  
🧄 Minced garlic as needed  
🫗 Hot oil 1 spoonful  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🫙 Large glass bowl  
⚖️ Small kitchen scale  
🥄 Strainer  
🍽️ Flat plate (tray)  
🔄 Plastic wrap  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Stockpot  
🔪 Cutting board  
🥖 Small rolling pin  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🖌️ Brush  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions

### Preparation Stage
1. **Knead & Rest Dough**
   - Mix flour with 3g salt, gradually add room temperature water while stirring into flaky pieces, then knead into a smooth dough.
- Roll dough into a long log, brush surface with oil, divide into pieces about 70g each.
- Roll each piece into an even, smooth long strip, arrange neatly on a flat plate, brush surface with oil again, cover tightly with plastic wrap.
**Note**: Rest dough for at least 40 minutes. Brush oil evenly to prevent sticking and drying.
2. **Prepare Spiced Water**
   - Rinse all spices, remove cardamom seeds.
- Pour 2000g boiling water over spices, steep for 3-5 minutes until water turns a light tea color, then remove spices.
**Key Point**: Use "steeping" not "boiling" to avoid excessive bitterness and medicinal taste.
3. **Make Compound Seasoning Sauce**
   - Heat a little soybean oil in a wok, stir-fry minced ginger until fragrant, immediately add 200g fragrant vinegar to cook off rawness.
- Pour in steeped spiced water to dilute, bring to a boil over high heat then reduce to medium heat.
- Add soy sauce, braising liquid, oyster sauce, and bean paste in order, stir to combine, then add salt and stir until dissolved. Turn off heat.
- After turning off heat, add chicken bouillon, MSG, and rock sugar, stir until dissolved. Strain through a strainer to remove ginger.
**Note**: Add salt before turning off heat; add chicken bouillon, MSG, and rock sugar after turning off heat. Rock sugar balances flavors.
### Cooking Stage
1. **Roll & Stretch Noodles**
   - Take a rested dough piece, press flat, use a small rolling pin to roll into a long oblong shape that is thinner on edges and thicker in the middle, like a "fish spine".
- Hold both ends of the dough piece, gently stretch, then pull apart sideways while slapping against the cutting board, making a "biang biang" sound as the noodle lengthens and thins.
- Fold and repeat slapping and stretching until desired length and thickness are achieved.
**Key Point**: Slapping motions should be continuous and forceful; this is key to extending without breaking. Final noodle should be thin and even.
2. **Cook & Season Noodles**
   - Bring a large pot of water to a boil, cook the stretched noodles until done.
- While noodles cook, add about 2 tablespoons of compound seasoning sauce to the bowl.
- Remove noodles into the bowl, top with chopped scallions, minced garlic, chili powder, and vegetable toppings in order.
- Heat one spoonful of oil until hot, pour over chili powder and scallions to release aroma.
**Note**: Bow base seasoning should be added generously beforehand. Oil must be hot enough to fully release aromas.

## Health Advice  
Biangbiang noodles are high in carbohydrates and salt, suitable for those with high physical exertion. Pair with vegetables and protein, and reduce seasoning sauce to control sodium intake. Ensure adequate dough resting time, otherwise noodles may break easily.
