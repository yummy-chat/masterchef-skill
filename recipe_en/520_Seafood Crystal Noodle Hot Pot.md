# Seafood Crystal Noodle Hot Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all four seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍝 Dried crystal noodles (vermicelli) as needed (soak in 40–50°C warm water for half an hour, or in cold water for one and a half hours, until soft, then drain)  
🍤 Shrimp as needed (peeled and deveined)  
🦪 Scallops as needed (cleaned)  
🦑 Squid as needed (sliced or scored)  
🥚 1 egg (yolk only)  
🧅 Ginger as needed (minced)  
🧄 Garlic as needed (minced)  
🫑 Green and red bell peppers as needed (sliced into strips or segments)  
🥬 Cabbage as needed (shredded, optional)  
🧅 Scallions as needed (chopped)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶️ Ground white pepper to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste  
🍶 Light soy sauce to taste  
🍶 Dark soy sauce a small amount  
🍶 Rice wine to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (non-stick)  
🫕 Clay pot (sand pot)  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. Drain the softened crystal noodles thoroughly. Add the egg yolk and mix well with your hands, ensuring each strand is coated with the egg mixture.  
**Key point**: The egg yolk enhances fragrance and prevents the noodles from clumping together during cooking.

2. Heat the wok over high heat, add oil, and spread the egg-coated noodles evenly in the wok. Pan-fry for about 3 minutes until the surface turns slightly golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.  
**Key point**: Pan-frying gives the noodles a unique dry and toasted aroma.

3. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Sauté the minced ginger and garlic until fragrant. Add the shrimp and scallops, stir-fry until they change color, then add the squid slices.  
**Key point**: Add the squid later to avoid overcooking.

4. Pour rice wine along the edge of the wok to remove any fishy smell. Stir, then add about half a small bowl of water and bring to a boil.  
5. Season with salt, ground white pepper, oyster sauce, and light soy sauce. Add the bell pepper strips and cabbage shreds (if using), and let them cook briefly in the liquid.  
**Key point**: Water must be added to create a broth, which the noodles will absorb for flavor.

6. Add the pan-fried noodles to the wok. Use chopsticks to toss and mix, allowing the noodles to fully absorb the broth until it is mostly absorbed.  
**Key point**: Absorbing the broth ensures the noodles are infused with seafood flavor throughout, not just on the surface.

7. Add a small amount of dark soy sauce for color and stir-fry evenly. Transfer the mixture to a preheated clay pot, sprinkle with chopped scallions, and serve immediately.  
**Key point**: The clay pot retains heat and releases the signature "pot" aroma.

## Health Advice  
This seafood crystal noodle hot pot is suitable for those seeking a high-protein, low-fat diet. Shrimp and scallops provide quality protein and minerals. Note that the crystal noodles are a source of carbohydrates, so portion control is recommended to avoid excess calorie intake.
