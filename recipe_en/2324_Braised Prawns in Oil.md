# Braised Prawns in Oil

Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Fresh live green prawns, as needed (about one plate, must use green, firm fresh prawns)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (shredded)
🧄 Ginger, as needed (shredded)
🥜 Peanut oil, as needed
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed
💧 Water, as needed (enough to partially submerge the prawns)
🧂 Salt, one small spoon
🍬 Sugar, about three spoons (more than salt, to create a savory-sweet flavor)
🌶️ White pepper powder, a pinch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup spoon (for turning prawns and tapping the heads to extract red oil)
🍳 Lid

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Prawns**: Use scissors to cut off the prawn antennae, whiskers, legs, and tail tips. Cut at the junction of the head and body, squeeze out and remove the sand sac. Cut along the second segment of the back and remove the digestive tract. Ensure thorough cleaning and removal of the digestive tract to avoid affecting the texture.
2. **Pan-fry the Prawns**: Pour peanut oil into the wok and heat to about 40% of its capacity. Add the prepared prawns and pan-fry over medium heat. When the shells instantly turn red and naturally curl, use the back of a spoon to repeatedly tap the prawn heads, pressing the red oil from the brains into the oil until the oil turns bright red. Flip the prawns and continue frying and tapping until the shells are crispy.
3. **Sauté the Aromatics**: Add shredded ginger and scallion to the fried prawns, keeping the heat on medium. Stir-fry until the ginger and scallion release a strong aroma.
4. **Season and Braise**: Immediately turn the heat to high. Add cooking wine, then water, followed by salt, sugar, and a pinch of white pepper powder. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Cover with the lid and braise for 2 minutes.
5. **Reduce the Sauce and Serve**: Remove the lid and continue over medium heat to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, becomes glossy, and evenly coats the prawns, it is ready to serve. The reduction must be thorough, ensuring the sauce is thick and shiny.

## Health Advice
Braised prawns in oil are rich in high-quality protein and astaxanthin, making them suitable for winter nourishment. However, they are high in sugar and oil, so those with high blood lipids should consume them in moderation. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the calorie intake. Tapping the prawn heads with the back of a spoon to extract the red oil is a key technique in the cooking process.
