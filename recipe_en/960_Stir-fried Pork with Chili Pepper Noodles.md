# Stir-fried Pork with Chili Pepper Noodles
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork shoulder (pork neck) 200g (sliced thinly)  
🥓 Fatty pork or pork belly 100g (sliced thinly, fat-to-lean ratio about 3:1)  
🌶️ Spiral chili (or thin-skinned chili) 2 (cut into diagonal strips, seeds removed)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers (optional, to taste) (cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic cloves 5-8 (crushed or sliced)  
🫘 Fermented black beans 20-30 (can substitute with 1 tbsp soybean paste)  
🧂 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp (for marinating meat)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp (for marinating meat) + 1 tbsp (before serving)  
🍾 Dark soy sauce 1/2 tbsp (for marinating meat, add another 1/2 tbsp if color is insufficient)  
🧂 Salt about 2 pinches (for dry-frying chili peppers)  
💧 Water 4-5 tbsp (when stir-frying meat) + 4-5 tbsp (after returning chili peppers to the pan, for noodle sauce)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon or MSG 1 pinch (before serving, optional)  
🧂 White pepper powder 2 pinches (before serving, optional)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (can be operated with one pan)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥢 Spatula  
🥣 Bowl (for marinating meat)  
🥛 Measuring cup (use as needed)  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Chopping and Preparation**: Cut the pork shoulder along the grain to shorten the length, then slice thinly against the grain to break the fibers and prevent getting stuck in teeth. Slice the fatty pork or pork belly thinly as well. Crush or slice the garlic cloves. Cut the spiral chili diagonally into strips, remove the chili seeds; cut the small chili peppers into sections and set aside.
**Note**: When handling chili peppers, you can hold them by the stem to cut, avoiding direct hand contact with capsaicin.

2. **Marinate the Lean Meat**: Place the sliced lean meat in a bowl. Add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Vigorously mix in one direction until the meat slices have fully absorbed the liquid, no residue remains at the bottom of the bowl, and the meat becomes sticky and coated.
**Note**: No need to marinate for a long time; mixing until sticky is sufficient.

3. **Dry-Fry the Chili Peppers**: Heat the pan without oil. Add the spiral chili over medium heat, pressing and frying them with a spatula. You can sprinkle about 2 pinches of salt to accelerate water release. Fry until the chili peppers become soft and develop charred, leopard-spot marks on the surface, then remove and set aside.
**Note**: Control the heat to avoid burning.

4. **Stir-Fry the Fatty Meat**: Add a little oil to the pan. Once the oil is hot, add the sliced fatty meat. Stir-fry over medium heat, adding a few garlic cloves to fry together, until the fatty meat surface is slightly charred, oil is rendered, and the aroma is released.
**Note**: If worried about oil splatter, pat the surface of the fatty meat dry before adding to the pan.

5. **Stir-Fry the Lean Meat**: After frying the fatty meat, add the garlic, fermented black beans, and the marinated lean meat slices. Stir-fry until the lean meat changes color. Before turning to high heat, add 4-5 tbsp of water to prevent sticking and to keep the meat tender. If the color is insufficient, add another 1/2 tbsp of dark soy sauce.
6. **Mix the Sauce**: Return the fried chili peppers to the pan, stirring well with the meat. If serving with noodles, add another 4-5 tbsp of water to create the sauce.
7. **Final Seasoning and Serving**: Just before turning off the heat, add 1 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 pinch chicken bouillon, and 2 pinches white pepper powder. Quickly stir-fry evenly, then remove from the pan.
**Note**: White pepper powder helps remove any gamey smell and enhance flavor; add according to taste.

## Health Advice  
Stir-fried Pork with Chili Pepper Noodles is rich in protein and vitamin C, suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, the fat and sodium content is relatively high. For those on a weight loss diet or with high blood pressure, it is recommended to reduce the amount of fatty meat and salt, and pair with a side of stir-fried vegetables for balanced nutrition.
