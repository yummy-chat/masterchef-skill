# Old Northeast Infused Oil  
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 50g (rinse and soak for about 3 minutes)  
⭐ Star anise 50g (rinse and soak for about 3 minutes)  
🧄 Garlic 150g (crush or slice)  
🥕 Ginger 150g (slice)  
🧅 Spring onions 250g (use both green and white parts, cut into sections)  
🫒 Cooking oil approximately 1.5kg (as the base oil for frying)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  

## Steps  
1. Rinse the Sichuan peppercorns and star anise, soak in water for about 3 minutes, then drain. Crush or slice the garlic, slice the ginger, and cut the spring onion greens and whites into sections.  
2. Pour about 1.5kg of cooking oil into the wok and heat over medium heat until it reaches about 40% hot (approximately 120°C/250°F).  
3. Add the drained Sichuan peppercorns and star anise, then reduce the heat to low and fry for 5–8 minutes, until the color deepens and the aroma is released.  
**Note**: Low heat is crucial to avoid burning; let the fragrance infuse into the oil.  

4. Gradually add the spring onions, ginger, and garlic in batches, continuing to fry over low heat for about 5 minutes, until all aromatics turn a "apricot yellow" color and become dry and shriveled.  
**Safety reminder**: Fresh aromatics contain moisture, so they may cause the oil to foam up when added. Add them in small batches to prevent overflow.  

5. Maintain low heat throughout, frying for a total of about 12 minutes. When the sound of water evaporating diminishes, bubbles decrease, and the oil turns a clear, brownish-yellow, it is ready.  
6. Use a slotted spoon to remove all the fried solids and drain the oil. The resulting liquid is the finished infused oil.  
**Key point**: The solids must be completely removed for clear oil; the fragrance is fully infused into the oil, which can be used for stir-frying, braising meat, steaming fish, cold dishes, and more.  

## Health Advice  
Infused oil is the hidden soul of Northeast Chinese cuisine, adding aroma and richness to everyday dishes. Those with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should control their intake, as its high oil content may be unhealthy if consumed excessively.
