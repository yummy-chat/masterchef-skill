# Braised Beef Short Ribs with Yanbian Perilla Sauce 🥩🌿
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef short ribs 1500g (frozen, thaw in fridge a day ahead, soak for half an hour to remove blood)
🦴 Beef bones (optional, to enrich broth)
🧅 Scallion (for braising) 25g (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 15g (sliced)
🍎 Apple approx. 100g (half a small apple, seeded)
🌶️ Thin-skinned green chili 30g (finely chopped, for sauce)
🧅 Spring onion or purple onion 100g (finely chopped, for sauce)
🧅 Scallion (for sauce) 50g (finely chopped)
🌿 Cilantro 15g (finely chopped, serve separately)

🧂 Coarse chili flakes 30g (3 level spoons)
🧂 Fine chili powder 20g (2 level spoons)
🥣 Beef broth 300g (about one small bowl)
🍬 Sugar 6g (2 teaspoons)
🧂 MSG 5g (1 teaspoon)
🧂 Beef powder 12g (4 level teaspoons, soul seasoning)
🌿 Wild perilla powder 1.5g (1 teaspoon)
🧂 Salt 3g (1 level teaspoon)
🫒 Cooking oil (a bit more than usual)

## Cookware and Equipment
🫕 Soup pot (glass pot)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔥 Gas stove

## Steps
1. Place beef short ribs in cold water and bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off foam as soon as it appears before full boil. Blanch for 3–4 minutes until no pinkish foam rises.  
   **Note**: Use enough water; press meat gently during heating to help release blood. Remove meat from the center boiling area, rinse in broth to clean surface foam, then transfer directly to the braising pot.
2. Put blanched short ribs, ginger slices, scallion sections, and apple into the braising pot. Pour in pre-boiled hot water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and braise for 90 minutes.  
   **Note**: Add enough hot water at once; apple removes odor and cuts richness, leaving no apple taste in final dish. If serving soup, add blanched beef bones and braise together.
3. In a pan, add a bit more cooking oil than usual. Heat over high until slightly smoking. Add all chopped vegetables except cilantro. Stir-fry over high heat, then reduce to medium-low and cook for 2–3 minutes until vegetables soften and wilt.
4. Add coarse chili flakes and fine chili powder. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant, being careful not to burn.
5. Reduce heat to the lowest. Add one small bowl of braising broth, then add sugar, MSG, beef powder, wild perilla powder, and salt. Increase to medium-high heat and cook until the sauce thickens.  
   **Note**: Beef powder and wild perilla powder are key to flavor, try not to skip. Adjust heat promptly to prevent sticking.
6. Add chopped cilantro and stir until wilted, then turn off the heat.
7. Once short ribs are fork-tender, discard apple and other solid seasonings. Remove short ribs, slice or tear into pieces, and serve with perilla sauce.

## Health Tips
Beef short ribs are high in protein and low in fat, suitable for muscle gain or blood sugar control. Perilla sauce is relatively high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should moderate dipping. Braising with apple cuts richness, and the Yanbian-style sauce adds unique flavor to the clear broth. It’s recommended to tear the meat first, then dip in sauce.
