# Homemade Skewers
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork belly, appropriate amount (cut into chunks)
🫑 Green bell pepper, appropriate amount (cut into chunks)
🍍 Pineapple, appropriate amount (cut into chunks, pineapple core can be used)
🍤 Whole shrimp, appropriate amount (with shells)
🥔 Potato, appropriate amount (cut into thick rounds)
🥬 Lettuce, appropriate amount (washed)
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount
🥜 Crushed peanuts, appropriate amount
⚪ White sesame seeds, appropriate amount
⚫ Cumin seeds, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
⚫ Coarsely ground black pepper, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Oven
🍡 Bamboo skewers
🔪 Food processor
🥘 Baking tray/grill grate
🫙 Small glass bowl

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the pork belly into chunks, and cut the green bell pepper and pineapple into chunks. Use bamboo skewers to alternately thread the pork belly chunks, bell pepper chunks, and pineapple chunks into skewers. Thread the whole shrimp onto separate skewers.
2. Cut the potato into thick rounds and lay them flat on the baking tray. Vertically insert the skewers into the potato rounds to keep the skewers upright and stable.
3. Make the dry dipping seasoning: Place the chili flakes, crushed peanuts, white sesame seeds, cumin seeds, salt, and white sugar into the food processor. Grind until coarse particles form, then pour into a bowl and set aside.
4. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Place the baking tray on the lower-middle rack and bake at 180°C for about 15 minutes.
5. Remove from the oven, brush a layer of cooking oil onto the surface of the potato rounds, sprinkle with coarsely ground black pepper, then return to the oven and continue baking until the meat is fully cooked and the shrimp shells turn red.
   **Key point**: The subsequent baking time should be flexibly adjusted according to the size of the meat chunks and the actual oven temperature. Bake until the surface is glossy and slightly charred.
6. Once baked, remove from the oven. Pour the mixed meat oil and seasoned pan juices from the bottom of the baking tray into a small glass bowl.
7. The meat skewers can be dipped in the dry seasoning, or wrapped in lettuce leaves along with a little dry seasoning for eating. The potato rounds can be dipped in the dry seasoning or the pan juices.

## Health Tips
The pairing of pineapple and pork belly helps cut through the richness and enhances freshness. When roasting shrimp alongside meat, pay attention to the heat to avoid overcooking the shrimp, which makes them tough. This recipe suits those who enjoy barbecue but want to avoid smoke and oil splatter. Wrapping the skewers in lettuce adds dietary fiber and reduces greasiness.
