# Spicy Tofu and Pork Blood  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Firm tofu, about 300g (cut into 1.5 cm cubes)  
🩸 Fresh pork blood, about 250g (cut into 1.5 cm cubes)  
🥩 Ground pork belly, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into chopped scallions)  
🧄 Ginger, as needed (minced)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed (cut into sections)  
🫑 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, as needed  
🥄 Pixian broad bean paste, 1 tbsp  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🍾 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, a pinch  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (mixed with water to make a slurry)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥄 Soup spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the firm tofu and fresh pork blood into 1.5 cm cubes of uniform size. Chop the scallions, mince the ginger, mince the garlic, and cut the dried chili peppers into sections. Set aside.  
2. Bring water in a wok to a boil. Add the tofu cubes and pork blood cubes to blanch. Boil vigorously, then remove and drain well.  
   **Key point**: Blanching removes any off-flavors and helps the ingredients hold their shape.  
3. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add the ground pork belly and stir-fry until it changes color.  
4. Add the minced scallions, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Add the dried Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili pepper sections. Stir-fry over low to medium heat until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.  
6. Add one tablespoon of Pixian broad bean paste and stir-fry over low to medium heat until the red oil is released.  
7. Add enough water to cover most of the ingredients.  
8. Season with a pinch of salt, light soy sauce, and white sugar. Stir to combine into a broth base.  
9. Gently slide in the blanched tofu and pork blood cubes. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 5 minutes. During this time, gently push the ingredients with the back of a spatula to avoid breaking them.  
10. Gradually pour in the cornstarch slurry while stirring gently over medium heat until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.  
11. Increase the heat to high and briefly reduce the sauce. Transfer to a serving plate, garnish with chopped scallions, and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and iron. It is suitable for people with anemia or those needing to supplement iron. However, individuals with high blood pressure should control the amount of salt and broad bean paste used.
