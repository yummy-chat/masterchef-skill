# Baked Sea Bass Pot in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 sea bass (fresh, can be filleted into steaks or cut into chunks)  
🧅 Half an onion (cut into large pieces)  
🧄 A handful of garlic (cut into large pieces)  
🫚 1 piece of ginger (cut into large pieces)  
🌿 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections, for final garnish)  
🌶️ Dried chilies, as needed (for final garnish)  
🍋 About 1 wedge of lemon (for final decoration or squeezing juice)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating)  
🤍 White pepper, as needed (for marinating)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed (for marinating)  
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed (for marinating)  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed (for marinating)  
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (for marinating)  
🫒 Vegetable oil, as needed (a little for marinating, oil for the base, and for the final drizzle)  
🍾 A small amount of rice wine or cooking wine (drizzled in during steaming for fragrance)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot or cast iron pot  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥘 Metal tray (for marinating)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
⏲️ Timer  
🥄 Small pot or ladle (for heating oil)  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Prepare aromatics: Cut the onion, ginger, and garlic into large pieces. These are used to line the bottom of the pot to prevent the fish from sticking.
2.  Prepare the fish: Clean the sea bass. You can fillet the fish to remove the bones, or simply cut it into large chunks. Filleted pieces cook faster and have a better texture.
3.  Marinate the fish: Place the fish pieces in a tray. Add salt, white pepper, light soy sauce, cooking wine, oyster sauce, and cornstarch. Massage everything together well with your hands. Finally, add a little vegetable oil and mix well. Marinate for at least 20 minutes to allow the fish to fully absorb the flavors. The seasoning can be slightly heavier than usual.
4.  Sauté aromatics: Add a base layer of oil to the clay pot over low heat. Add the onion, ginger, and garlic pieces, stir-frying until fragrant. Then, spread them evenly across the bottom of the pot.
5.  Arrange the fish: Turn off the heat. Arrange the marinated fish pieces in a single layer on top of the aromatics, avoiding excessive overlapping. Pour the remaining marinade sauce over the fish.
6.  Cover and steam: Cover the pot with its lid and cook over low heat. Time for 12 minutes. Halfway through, you can drizzle a circle of rice wine or cooking wine along the edge of the lid. The alcohol and steam will accelerate the cooking process and add fragrance.
7.  Final aroma boost: After the time is up, uncover the pot. Sprinkle with scallion sections and dried chili pieces. In a separate small pot, heat a small amount of oil until hot. Pour the hot oil over the scallions and chilies to release their fragrance.

## Health Advice  
Sea bass is high in protein and low in fat. Steaming it with ginger and garlic makes for a balanced meal, suitable for those on a weight loss diet or with weak constitutions. Control the amount of salt during marinating to avoid excessive sodium intake. Using onions as a base not only prevents sticking but also adds dietary fiber.
