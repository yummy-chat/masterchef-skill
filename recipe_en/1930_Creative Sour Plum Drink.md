# Creative Sour Plum Drink
Cuisine: Common Home-style
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥤 Sour plum soup 1000g (about 2 jin)
❄️ Ice powder (konjac jelly powder) 40g
🍋 Lemon two slices
🌿 Mint leaves a few pieces
🥤 Sprite as needed
🔵 Blue Gatorade (or similar sports drink) as needed
🧊 Ice cubes as needed
🈚️ No additional seasonings (flavor comes from sour plum soup and subsequently added drinks)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Cooking pot
🥚 Whisk
🍮 Glass baking dish/heat-resistant container
🔪 Knife
🥄 Glass cup
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Pour the sour plum soup into a cooking pot, add the ice powder, and whisk until completely dissolved to avoid lumps.
2. Heat over high heat until boiling. When foam appears on the surface, skim off the foam, then immediately remove from heat.
   **Note**: It must be boiled to activate the setting property of the ice powder.
3. Pour the boiled liquid into a clean, heat-resistant glass baking dish or other flat container. Allow it to cool naturally to room temperature, then transfer to the refrigerator and chill for at least half an hour, until the liquid has completely set into a bouncy sour plum jelly.
4. Remove the sour plum jelly and use a knife to cut it into irregularly sized chunks.
5. Layer a bed of sour plum jelly chunks at the bottom of a glass cup, then place two slices of lemon on top.
6. Fill the cup with ice cubes. The ice helps create layers and maintains a low temperature.
7. Slowly pour Sprite along the wall of the cup until it reaches about half the height of the cup, then slowly pour in the blue sports drink until it almost reaches the rim, creating a blue-and-white gradient layer.
   **Key point**: Pouring slowly results in a clearer and more beautiful layering effect.
8. Garnish with a few mint leaves and serve.

## Health Advice
The sour plum jelly paired with a sports drink both replenishes electrolytes and provides cooling relief, making it suitable for people who sweat heavily in summer. Note that Sprite has a high sugar content; those controlling sugar intake can reduce the amount used.
