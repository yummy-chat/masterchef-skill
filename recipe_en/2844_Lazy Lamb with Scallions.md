# Lazy Lamb with Scallions
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐑 Lamb rolls, about 1 lb (choose fatty lamb rolls that separate into strips after cooking)
🧅 Scallions, about 30–40% of the lamb's weight (cut into chunks, separated as much as possible)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (crush and mince)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (crush and set aside)
🫒 Mature rapeseed oil, to taste (used throughout cooking; some oil should remain on the plate)
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch
🧂 Salt, about 5 g
🍶 Light soy sauce, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/basin
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Wooden cutting board
🔥 Gas stove

## Procedure
1. Fill the wok with enough water and bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Add the lamb rolls, and as soon as the meat changes color, remove immediately. Drain well and set aside.
2. First, heat the wok empty for about 2 minutes. Turn off the heat, coat the wok surface with oil, then add enough mature rapeseed oil.
**Key**: The wok must be fully preheated and coated to prevent sticking.

3. Turn the heat back on and heat until the oil is hot. Add the minced ginger and stir-fry thoroughly for a bit until fragrant.
4. Add the blanched lamb slices. Pan-fry and stir-fry them, flipping both sides, to evaporate moisture until the edges of the lamb are slightly charred.
**Note**: Do not stir too frequently after adding, to avoid drying and toughening the meat.

5. Sprinkle in the white pepper and stir-fry evenly until fragrant. Then remove the lamb and set aside.
6. Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the scallion chunks and stir-fry. Spread the scallions apart to avoid overlapping.
7. Once the scallions are loosened, return the lamb to the wok. Add salt and light soy sauce, and toss to combine for seasoning.
**Key**: Be generous with the salt, as the dish appears large in volume but the actual main ingredient is relatively small.

8. Add the crushed garlic, stir-fry quickly and evenly, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.
**Key**: Garlic must be added at the end to preserve its aroma; if the heat is not intense, do not stir-fry too quickly; scallions should remain crisp and tender, not overcooked until soft.

## Health Advice
This lamb with scallions dish, after blanching to remove gaminess and then quick-stir-fried, reduces oil intake while keeping the meat tender and smooth. It is suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter. White pepper helps warm the body, but lamb rolls are relatively high in fat; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
