# Ice Cream
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs: 3 (yolks only)  
🥛 Whole milk: 60g  
🥛 Whipping cream: 250g  
🍬 White sugar: 70g (divided into 50g and 20g)  
🧂 Salt: 2g  
🍶 White vinegar: a few drops  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Stainless steel pot  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🪣 Spatula  
🧁 Paper cups or small bowls  
🔥 Gas stove  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Instructions  
1. Combine the egg yolks with 50g of white sugar and 2g of salt; stir until uniform. Add 60g of whole milk and a few drops of white vinegar, mixing thoroughly to form a smooth egg-milk mixture.  
   **Note**: Salt enhances sweetness, and white vinegar helps remove the eggy smell.  
2. Pour the egg-milk mixture into a stainless steel pot and heat over the lowest heat, stirring continuously with a wooden spatula until the mixture starts to simmer slightly and thickens. Immediately remove from heat and let cool.  
   **Key**: Stir constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling into lumps; remove from heat as soon as it thickens to avoid overheating.  
3. Combine the whipping cream with 20g of white sugar. Use an electric hand mixer on high speed to whip for about 2 minutes and 30 seconds, until firm peaks form when the beaters are lifted.  
4. Take one-third of the whipped cream and add it to the cooled egg-milk mixture. Fold gently with a spatula until combined.  
5. Pour the mixture back into the remaining whipped cream. Continue folding gently with the spatula until the mixture becomes a uniform, smooth paste.  
   **Note**: Fold gently to prevent deflating the whipped cream.  
6. Divide the ice cream mixture into paper cups or small bowls, smooth the surface, and freeze in the refrigerator for about 3 hours, until fully set and firm. Serve directly.  

## Health Advice  
This homemade egg-milk ice cream uses egg yolks for protein, but the sugar and cream are high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy a small bowl each time and consume in moderation.
