# Willow Leaf Baozi (Liuye Bao)
Cuisine: Common home cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧅 Yellow onion 3 small (finely chopped)
🥩 Ground beef 300g (preferably with some fat)
🧅 Scallions 10 stalks (chopped)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g
🍥 Baking powder 1 tsp
💧 Room temperature water 180g
🍄 Yeast 1 tsp
🫒 Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
🌿 White pepper 1/4 tsp
🌿 Five-spice powder 1/8 tsp
🫚 Ginger paste 1/2 tsp
🫙 Light soy sauce 1.5 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce 1/2 tsp
🫙 Dark soy sauce 1 tsp
🧂 Salt To taste (adjust based on saltiness)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍳 Wok
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Rolling pin
🎋 Bamboo steamer
📜 Non-stick steamer paper

## Instructions
1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok over high heat. Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent. Reduce heat to low and continue cooking until lightly golden. Add white pepper, five-spice powder, ginger paste, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry until fragrant, then turn off the heat. **Let cool completely**.
2. Add the cooled onion mixture to the ground beef. Stir vigorously in one direction until the filling becomes cohesive and stringy. Add chopped scallions and mix well. Taste and adjust salt as needed.
3. Combine all-purpose flour and baking powder in a bowl. Make a well in the center, pour in room temperature water and yeast. Stir into a flaky texture, then knead into a rough dough with no dry flour.
4. Knead the dough for about 3 minutes, cover and rest for 10 minutes. Knead again for about 1 minute until smooth. Cover and rest for another 5 minutes.
5. Roll the dough into a long log and cut into about 16 even pieces. Flatten each piece and roll into a round wrapper with a thicker center and thinner edges.
6. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center of each wrapper. Pinch the top edge and fold alternately left and right to pleat until sealed, forming a willow leaf shape.
7. Place the finished baozi into a steamer lined with non-stick paper. Cover and proof at room temperature for about 1.5 hours, until the baozi have noticeably increased in volume and feel light to the touch.
8. Place the proofed baozi in a cold steamer basket. Once steam rises, reduce to medium-low heat and steam for 15 minutes.
9. After turning off the heat, open the lid slightly to let steam escape slowly. Wait a few minutes before fully opening the lid and removing the baozi.
   **Key point**: Avoid sudden temperature changes to prevent the baozi from collapsing or shrinking.

## Health Tips
Willow Leaf Baozi feature a filling of beef and onion, providing a balanced combination of protein and dietary fiber. Suitable as a breakfast or lunch staple. A small amount of fat in the ground beef enhances texture, but portion control is advised for weight management. Steaming is a low-fat, healthy cooking method.
