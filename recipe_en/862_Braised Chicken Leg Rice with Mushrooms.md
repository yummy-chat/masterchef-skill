# Braised Chicken Leg Rice with Mushrooms
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Boneless chicken thighs, 2 pieces
🥩 Beef, 1 small piece (minced)
🍄 Mixed mushrooms, appropriate amount (enoki, shiitake, etc.)
🥕 Carrot, 1 piece (shredded)
🧅 Purple onion, half (minced)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🍚 Cooked rice, 2 bowls
🍘 Seaweed/laver, appropriate amount
🧂 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar, appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger, a small piece
💧 Water, a small amount

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥄 Spatula
🧀 Shredder
🥣 Strainer/colander

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the chicken thighs and make a few cuts on the surface to help with flavor absorption and even cooking.
2. Add ginger slices, cornstarch, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, sugar, salt, and a little oil. Mix well and marinate for 10–15 minutes.
3. Shred the carrot, mince the onion and beef, wash the mushrooms and drain well.
4. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Place the chicken thighs skin side down and fry until both sides are golden brown and about 80% cooked. Remove and set aside.
5. Leave a little oil in the pan. Add the minced onion and beef, and stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Add the mushrooms and shredded carrot, and stir-fry evenly.
7. Add the cooked rice, season with a pinch of salt and a little water, and stir-fry until evenly mixed.
8. Cut the pan-fried chicken thighs into strips and place them on top of the fried rice. Cover the pan and let it steam until the chicken is fully cooked, allowing the rice to absorb the meat juice.
9. In another pan, fry two runny sunny-side-up eggs.
10. Plate the fried rice, top with the fried eggs, and sprinkle with crushed seaweed.

## Health Tips
Braised chicken leg rice with mushrooms is rich in protein, and the mushrooms provide dietary fiber, making it suitable for muscle building or busy workdays. When marinating the chicken, reduce the salt. Use medium-low heat when frying to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside undercooked.
