# Blueberry Mousse Cake  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍜 Instant noodle cake 1 piece (use only the noodle cake as the cake base; discard the seasoning packet)  
🍪 Oreo cookies About 10 pieces (for making the cake base)  
🫐 Blueberries About two boxes (for making blueberry sauce and top decoration)  
🍮 Gelatin sheets As needed (for setting the blueberry sauce and mousse mixture; soak in ice water until softened in advance)  
🥛 Whipping cream As needed (whipped to make the mousse)  
🍬 Granulated sugar As needed (added to blueberries to season the sauce)  
🌿 Mint leaves 1 small sprig (for final decoration)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl (for washing blueberries and mixing ingredients)  
🍶 Saucepan (for holding blueberries during microwave heating)  
🔁 Microwave (for heating blueberries to make the sauce)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and scooping)  
🥢 Stirring stick/chopsticks (for stirring mixtures)  
🧴 Empty plastic bottle (for shaking and whipping the whipping cream)  
🫙 Plastic wrap (to cover the bowl during microwave heating)  
📦 Instant noodle paper bowl (as the cake mold)  
🔪 Scissors/knife (to cut open the paper bowl for unmolding)  
❄️ Refrigerator (for chilling and setting the mousse cake; requires at least 4 hours)  
🪵 Wooden cutting board (to place the unmolded cake for decoration)  
🍰 Cake knife (for slicing)  
🍽️ Plate (for serving cake slices)  
🍴 Fork (for enjoying)  

## Steps  
1. Take a clean instant noodle paper bowl and place the instant noodle cake inside to serve as the cake base.  
2. Place the blueberries in a bowl, rinse thoroughly with clean water several times, and drain.  
3. Soak the gelatin sheets in ice water until softened and swollen; set aside.  
4. Place the Oreo cookies in the paper bowl, crush them with a tool, and press firmly to form an even cookie crust.  
5. **Make the blueberry sauce**: Combine the washed blueberries with granulated sugar in a saucepan, cover with plastic wrap, and microwave until the blueberries are soft and release juice, resulting in a thick, dark purple liquid.  
   **Note**: Adjust the heating time based on the microwave power to avoid overheating and scorching.  
6. **Incorporate the gelatin**: Add the softened gelatin sheets to the hot blueberry sauce, stirring until completely melted and well combined.  
7. **Whip the whipping cream**: Pour the whipping cream into a clean plastic bottle, screw the lid tightly, and shake vigorously up and down until the cream thickens and reaches a semi-whipped state.  
   **Tip**: If no plastic bottle is available, you can also use an electric mixer on low speed to achieve the same consistency.  
8. **Mix the mousse mixture**: Pour the whipped cream into the blueberry sauce and gently fold until evenly combined, forming a light purple mousse batter.  
9. **Pour mold and chill**: Pour the mousse batter into the paper bowl mold lined with the Oreo crust. Gently tap to level the surface, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours until fully set.  
   **Note**: Ensure sufficient chilling time; otherwise, the mousse may not set properly, affecting unmolding.  
10. **Unmold**: Remove the chilled and set cake from the refrigerator. Carefully tear or cut open the paper bowl to unmold the cake onto a wooden cutting board.  
11. **Decorate**: Drizzle the reserved thick blueberry sauce over the cake surface, allowing it to flow naturally into a drip effect. Then arrange fresh blueberries neatly on top and garnish with a mint sprig.  
12. **Slice and serve**: Use a cake knife to slice the cake and plate it. Enjoy the mousse layer’s smooth and silky texture, the crunchy cookie crust, and the tangy-sweet topping.  
    **Key point**: For cleaner slices, dip the knife in hot water before cutting.  

## Health Advice  
Blueberries are rich in antioxidants. The mousse is made with whipping cream and gelatin, offering a light texture but moderate calories, making it suitable for occasional indulgence. Ensure sufficient chilling time to avoid issues with unmolding.
