# Zibo BBQ Pancake
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g  
🍄 Active dry yeast 5g  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍚 Baking powder 3g  
🧂 Baking soda 2g  
💧 Room temperature water 300ml  
🌶️ Garlic chili sauce to taste (dipping sauce)  
🍂 Five-spice powder to taste (dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili powder to taste (dipping sauce)  
✨ Cumin powder to taste (dipping sauce)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Large mixing bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫓 Rolling pin  
🍳 Flat pan / wok / iron pan  
🔪 Spatula  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (as a mold)  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
⚖️ Kitchen scale

## Preparation Steps
1. Place 500g all-purpose flour, 5g yeast, 3g salt, 3g baking powder, and 2g baking soda into a bowl. Use chopsticks to mix thoroughly until well combined.  
**Note**: All dry ingredients must be evenly mixed to avoid concentrated spots.

2. Slowly pour in 300ml room temperature water while stirring with chopsticks to form flocculent dough, then knead with your hands into a dough.  
**Note**: The dough should be relatively soft; knead until no obvious dry flour lumps remain—no need for smoothness.

3. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 30–50 minutes until the dough is half-fermented.  
**Key Point**: Half-fermented means that when you pull it apart, you can see a honeycomb structure, but the overall volume hasn't significantly increased.

4. Dust the work surface generously with flour. Remove the dough, gently knead for about 1 minute to release air, then roll out into a large, thin sheet using a rolling pin.

5. Sprinkle a layer of dry flour over the large sheet. Use the rim of a stainless steel bowl as a mold to press firmly and cut out multiple round pancake blanks.  
**Note**: Generously dust with flour to prevent sticking; using a bowl mold helps create uniform pancakes with less effort.

6. Gather the scraps, knead them together, and roll out into thinner small round pancakes.

7. Heat a flat pan over medium heat until it feels "scalding" to the touch. Do not add oil; place the pancake blanks directly into the pan.  
**Key Point**: The pan must be hot before adding the pancakes—a cold pan will not produce a fluffy, puffed effect.

8. Keep the heat at medium. After 3–5 seconds, small bubbles will appear on the pancake's surface, then grow larger, and the pancake will puff up. At this point, flip it with a spatula.

9. After flipping, continue cooking for a short while until both sides show light golden patches, then remove from the pan.

10. Stack the cooked pancakes together, letting their residual heat soften them. This keeps them soft and prevents them from drying out. Storage: Wrap any leftovers in a plastic bag and freeze. When ready to eat, thaw them to restore softness.

## Health Advice
Zibo BBQ pancake is rich in carbohydrates, and combined with dipping sauces, it becomes relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume 2–3 pieces per serving, paired with vegetables and lean meat. Suitable for those who enjoy flour-based foods and tend to get hungry easily, but individuals with high blood sugar should be mindful of portion size.
