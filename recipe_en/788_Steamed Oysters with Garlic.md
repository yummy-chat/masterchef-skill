# Steamed Oysters with Garlic

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Oysters, as needed (cleaned and shucked)  
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed  
🫚 Minced ginger, as needed  
🧅 Minced white part of scallion, as needed  
🌶️ Minced red chili pepper, as needed (some for sauce, some for final garnish)  
🌿 Chopped scallion greens, as needed (sprinkle after steaming)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, as needed  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🦪 Oyster sauce, as needed  
🌶️ Chili sauce, as needed  
🌿 Ground white pepper, as needed  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (used in two separate batches, both heated to around 170°C / 340°F)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen knife with a longer tip  
🥣 Large bowl  
🍳 Wok or soup pot (for boiling water and heating oil)  
♨️ Steamer (with a steaming rack)  
🍽️ Heat-resistant plate  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place an oyster with the domed side up. Find the seam along the edge, insert the knife tip to cut the adductor muscle, open the shell, remove the oyster meat, and clean it thoroughly. Arrange the oysters one by one on a heat-resistant plate.
2. In a large bowl, combine minced garlic, minced ginger, minced white scallion parts, and some minced red chili pepper. Heat oil in a pan to around 170°C (340°F), then pour it over the bowl ingredients to release their aroma. Once the oil cools slightly, immediately add light soy sauce, salt, oyster sauce, chili sauce, and ground white pepper. Stir well with chopsticks to combine.
**Note**: Pouring the hot oil when it reaches around 170°C (340°F) gives a richer aroma; add the seasonings while the oil is still warm and stir thoroughly.

3. Place the steamer over heat, add water, and bring to a boil. Put the plate of oysters onto the steaming rack, and cover with a lid. Once a substantial amount of steam escapes from the gaps in the lid, start timing. Steam over high heat for 4 minutes.
**Key Point**: Do not steam for more than 4 minutes, or the oyster meat will shrink and become tough, losing its tender texture.

4. Once the oysters are cooked, remove them immediately. Evenly sprinkle chopped scallion greens and the remaining red chili pepper on top. In another pan, heat oil to around 170°C (340°F). Using a soup ladle, drizzle the hot oil over each oyster to release a layered aroma, then serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
Steamed oysters with garlic are an excellent low-fat, high-protein choice, suitable for those managing calorie intake or needing zinc supplementation. Be careful not to make the garlic sauce too salty, and stick to a steaming time of 4 minutes to prevent the oyster meat from becoming overly firm.
