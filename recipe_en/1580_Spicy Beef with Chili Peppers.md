# Spicy Beef with Chili Peppers
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket/short ribs 1000g (with some fat for tenderness)
🌶️ Spiral chili peppers 400g (about 7 pieces)
🌿 Cilantro with roots 75g (about a bundle as thick as a thumb, keep the roots)
🧅 Scallion 20g (about a 10cm long piece)
🫚 Ginger 10g (slightly larger than the first joint of a thumb)
🧄 Garlic 15g (about 3 cloves, flattened)
🍂 Bay leaves 2 pieces
⭐ Star anise 3g (about 1.5 pieces, use 1 piece if large)
🪵 Cinnamon stick 0.6g (very thin, about the size of the first joint of a thumb, do not use too much)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste (optional)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🍶 High-proof rice wine a small amount (only for boiling beef with strong odor to remove smell, optional)
🧴 Premium soy sauce/light soy sauce 40g (4 tablespoons)
🧴 Dark soy sauce 10g (1 tablespoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce 25g (1 heaping tablespoon)
🧂 Beef powder or chicken powder 6g or 3g (2 teaspoons or 1 tablespoon)
🍬 Rock sugar 10g (about 5 pieces the size of a thumbnail, can substitute with 1 flat tablespoon of white sugar)

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon/soup ladle
♨️ Electric pressure cooker/gas pressure cooker
🥢 Chopsticks, tongs
🪵 Wooden lid (to prevent oil splatter when frying peppers, and to cover when braising)
🪣 Stainless steel bowl

## Instructions
1. Cut the beef into chunks the width of two fingers. Place in cold water and bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off the foam and turn off the heat. Rinse with warm water, squeeze dry, and set aside.
**Note**: If the beef has a strong odor, add a small amount of high-proof rice wine to the boiling water to remove the smell.

2. Cut the scallion into sections, slice the ginger, and flatten the garlic cloves with a knife. Wash the spiral chili peppers, remove the stems, break any overly long ones in half, and drain well. Wash the cilantro with roots and drain, keeping the roots intact.

3. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok, swirl to coat, then reduce to low heat. Add the scallion, ginger, and garlic, and stir-fry over low heat until lightly golden.

4. Add the beef, turn to high heat, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until the surface moisture is dry and the beef is slightly golden.

5. Add the bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon stick, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry until fragrant.
**Key point**: Do not use too much cinnamon stick, or it will overpower the flavor.

6. Reduce to the lowest heat, pour the light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, and quickly stir to combine. Return to high heat and stir-fry until you hear a sizzling sound and the soy sauce aroma develops.

7. Add 500ml of boiling water, then the dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, beef powder, and rock sugar, and stir to combine.

8. Bring to a boil, then turn off the heat. Transfer the beef and liquid to a pressure cooker: For an electric pressure cooker, use the “beef/lamb” function and cook for 35 minutes. For a gas pressure cooker, bring to pressure, then reduce to the lowest heat and cook for about 20 minutes.
**Note**: If you don’t have a pressure cooker, double the amount of water and simmer over low heat for about 2 hours.

9. In a separate wok, add a little oil to coat, then pour out the excess, leaving just enough to coat the bottom. Heat the wok over high heat until very hot, then add the spiral chili peppers. Reduce to low heat and slow-fry.
**Safety tip**: The peppers will splatter oil when frying, so use the lid as a shield for protection.

10. First, fry the peppers in the center of the wok until both sides are golden with charred spots (tiger skin), then push them to the side. Move the peppers from the edges to the center and continue frying. Once all peppers have charred spots and are softened, turn off the heat, cover with a lid, and set aside.

11. Remove the spices from the cooked beef. Pour the beef and liquid back into the wok with the peppers. Turn to high heat, stir to combine, and bring to a boil. Cover with a lid, reduce to the lowest heat, and simmer for 10 minutes.

12. Add the cilantro with roots. Turn to high heat and press the cilantro into the liquid. Bring to a boil, then cover again, reduce to the lowest heat, and simmer for another 10 minutes.

13. Turn to high heat to reduce the sauce until slightly thickened, but do not dry it out completely. Transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Tips
This dish features tender beef and spicy peppers, rich in protein and vitamin C, making it ideal for those with high physical activity levels or craving a satisfying meal. However, peppers can irritate the digestive system, so those with stomach sensitivities or prone to heatiness should consume in moderation.
