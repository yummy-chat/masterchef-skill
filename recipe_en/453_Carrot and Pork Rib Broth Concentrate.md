# Carrot and Pork Rib Broth Concentrate  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦴 Pork ribs, as needed  
🌽 Corn, about 1 section  
🥕 Carrot, about 1 small piece  
🟤 Red dates, about 3-4 (pitted)  
🫚 Ginger slices, about 2-3 slices  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok / soup pot (for blanching)  
♨️ Electric pressure cooker (for simmering ribs and vegetables)  
🧂 Blender / baby food processor (for blending meat puree)  
🧊 Silicone mold (animal shape or similar, for shaping)  
👝 Piping bag (for squeezing puree into molds)  
🛍️ Sealed bag (for storing frozen broth concentrate)  
🍲 Steamer (for reheating before serving)  

## Steps  
1. Clean the pork ribs. Place them in cold water in a pot and blanch. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then drain the ribs.  
   **Key point**: Blanching effectively removes the gamey taste and impurities.  

2. Cut the corn into sections, chop the carrot into chunks, pit the red dates, and slice the ginger. Set aside.  
   **Note**: Red dates must be pitted to prevent choking hazards for babies.  

3. Put the blanched ribs, corn sections, carrot chunks, pitted red dates, and ginger slices into the electric pressure cooker pot. Add enough water to just cover the ingredients.  
   - Select the "soup" function or a similar setting, and cook until the ribs are tender and the broth is flavorful.  

4. Remove the ribs from the electric pressure cooker. Tear the meat off the bones, carefully removing all tiny bones and bone fragments.  
   **Key point**: Thoroughly remove any hard bone debris to ensure food safety for babies.  

5. Place the pure meat into the blender. Add a small amount of the reserved cooking broth and blend until it becomes an extremely smooth, lump-free meat paste.  
   - Texture check: The paste should be smooth, similar to creamy mashed potatoes.  
   **Note**: Do not add too much broth; keep the consistency thick enough for freezing and shaping.  

6. Transfer the meat paste into a piping bag. Snip a small opening at the tip and squeeze it evenly into the silicone mold. Gently smooth the surface with a small spoon if needed.  

7. Place the mold in the freezer and freeze until completely solid.  
   - Storage method: Freezing extends shelf life; take out as needed.  

8. Remove the molds and store the broth concentrate blocks in a sealed bag in the freezer. When serving, take out the required number, place them in a heat-resistant container, and steam or microwave until thoroughly heated through.  

## Health Tips  
This carrot and pork rib broth concentrate is specially designed for babies. It contains no salt and retains its natural flavors, offering balanced nutrition—pork ribs provide high-quality protein and calcium, corn and carrots supplement vitamins and dietary fiber, and red dates add natural sweetness. Suitable for babies aged 6 months and older who have started complementary feeding. Ensure the meat paste is blended finely enough and free of any bone residue.
