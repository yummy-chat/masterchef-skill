# Braised Chicken with Edamame
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 young rooster  
🌱 Edamame, to taste (shelled edamame beans)  
🐔 Chicken intestines, to taste (rub repeatedly with salt to clean)  
🐔 Chicken liver/gizzard and other offal, to taste (optional)  
🫚 Ginger, about 3-4 slices (cut into thick slices)  
🧄 Garlic, about 6-8 cloves (lightly smashed, used in two batches)  
🧅 Scallions, 1-2 stalks (cut into large sections)  
🌶️ Green and red bell peppers, about half each (cut into small pieces)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste  
🥩 A small piece of lard  
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments, one handful  
🌟 1 star anise  
🌿 A few Sichuan peppercorns  
🥄 Light soy sauce, about 1-2 tablespoons  
🍶 A splash of Shaoxing wine  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, about half a tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🧂 A pinch of white pepper  
🍶 A splash of aged vinegar  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Wok (frying pan)  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🧹 Strainer/colander  
🪵 Wooden lid  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  

## Instructions
1.  Cut the young rooster into uniform pieces, rinse repeatedly to remove blood, and drain well. Rub the chicken intestines with salt, rinse clean, and similarly clean any other offal.
2.  Slice the ginger into thick slices, smash the garlic cloves, cut the scallions into large sections, and cut the green and red bell peppers into small pieces. Shell and wash the edamame in advance.
3.  Heat the wok over high heat, add rapeseed oil and a small piece of lard. Once the oil is hot, first add the ginger slices and fry until fragrant. Then add the drained chicken pieces, and stir-fry over medium-high heat until the chicken releases its moisture, the skin tightens, and it becomes lightly golden.
    **Key Point**: Do not blanch fresh chicken; stir-fry it raw to lock in freshness and render the chicken fat.
4.  Add the scallion sections, most of the garlic, dried chili pepper segments, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Continue stir-frying until fragrant.
5.  Drizzle the light soy sauce along the edge of the wok and stir-fry quickly to let the soy sauce develop aroma over high heat. Then add a splash of Shaoxing wine to remove any gamey odor and enhance the flavor.
6.  Pour in boiling water, add dark soy sauce for color, and season with salt, sugar, white pepper, and a splash of aged vinegar. Finally, add the cleaned offal.
    **Key Point**: Always add boiling water to prevent the chicken from shrinking and becoming tough when meeting cold liquid. The seasoned broth should be slightly salty to infuse the chicken during simmering.
7.  Cover with a lid, reduce to medium heat, and braise for 15 minutes.
8.  Remove the lid, add the edamame, stir to combine, cover again, and braise for another 5 minutes.
9.  Remove the lid, increase to high heat, and reduce the sauce until it thickens. When the sauce is thick enough to coat the ingredients, add the green and red bell pepper pieces and the remaining garlic. Stir-fry until the peppers are just cooked through, then remove from the heat.
    **Key Point**: The sauce should be reduced until thick, but some liquid should remain at the bottom of the wok, as it is excellent for mixing with rice.

## Health Tips
The combination of edamame and chicken provides high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for replenishing energy in summer. However, chicken offal is high in cholesterol, so those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Using the method of stir-frying the raw chicken without blanching and braising with boiling water helps lock in its tenderness and freshness.
