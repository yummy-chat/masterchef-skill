# Five Guys Style Double Cheeseburger
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 250g
🍬 Sugar 40g
🧂 Salt 2g
🍄 Yeast 4g
🥛 Milk powder 15g
🥛 Ice-cold milk 60g
💧 Water 55g
🥚 Chilled egg 1
🧈 Softened butter 30g
🥩 Ground beef 200g (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio, makes 2 patties of about 100g each)
🧀 Cheddar cheese slices 2
🧅 Onion, to taste (diced)
🍄 Mushrooms (white or cremini), to taste (sliced)
🥓 Bacon, about 3-4 slices
🍅 Tomato, to taste (sliced)
🥒 Pickles (dill pickles), to taste (sliced)
🥬 Iceberg lettuce, a few leaves
🍅 Ketchup, to taste
🟡 Yellow mustard, to taste
🥚 Mayonnaise, to taste
🧂 Salt, to taste (for seasoning patties)
🌿 Black pepper, to taste
🍷 Sherry vinegar, to taste (optional)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍞 Stand mixer
🥣 Baking bowl
🔪 Baking sheet, wire rack
🔥 Oven
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍳 Non-stick skillet
🥄 Spatula, scraper
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🧴 Squeeze bottle
🫙 Plastic wrap

## Instructions
1. Place all dry and liquid ingredients except butter into the stand mixer. Mix on low speed until combined, then increase to medium-high speed and knead until the dough reaches the windowpane stage.
   **Note**: Ensure the dough has reached the windowpane stage before adding the butter.
2. Add the softened butter and continue kneading until fully incorporated, then knead for another 2-3 minutes until the dough reaches full windowpane stage.
   **Key point**: The dough's consistency is crucial for achieving soft burger buns.
3. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and let it proof at room temperature for about 1 hour, until the dough has doubled in size. It is ready when a finger poke leaves an indentation that does not spring back.
   **Key point**: Accurately judge the proofing stage to avoid over- or under-proofing.
4. Divide the proofed dough into equal portions. Shape each into a ball and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover with plastic wrap and let proof for a second time for about 1 hour, until the dough has doubled again.
5. Brush the proofed dough with whole egg wash and sprinkle with white sesame seeds. Bake in the middle of a preheated oven at 180°C for about 15 minutes, until the surface is golden brown. Remove from the oven and let cool completely on a wire rack.
   **Note**: Baking time and temperature may need adjustment based on your oven; rely on the color of the crust.
6. Prepare crispy bacon:
   - Method 1: Cook bacon slices in a skillet over medium-low heat, turning occasionally to prevent curling and ensure even cooking.
   - Method 2: Place bacon on a wire rack on a baking sheet and bake in a 200°C oven for about 15 minutes, flipping halfway through if needed.
   - Method 3: Add bacon to a skillet with a little water or oil, then cook over medium heat until the water evaporates and the bacon fries in its own fat until crispy.
7. Prepare toppings: Sauté diced onion in a skillet over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes until soft and lightly browned. Adding sherry vinegar is optional for extra aroma. Meanwhile, sauté sliced mushrooms in another pan until both sides are cooked through and browned. Set aside to cool.
8. Take about 100g of ground beef and toss it back and forth between your hands a few times to bind it, then shape into a ball. Place the ball on parchment paper and use the bottom of a skillet to press it into a thin patty.
   **Key point**: Tossing and pressing are the key steps to forming a thin Smash Burger patty.
9. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. No oil is needed, or use a very small amount. Place the patty in the skillet. Season with salt and black pepper. Use a spatula to press down on the patty to ensure even browning on the contact surface. Cook until one side develops a dark brown crust, then flip.
10. Place one cheese slice on top of one cooked patty, then immediately top with the second patty. Use the residual heat to melt the cheese.
    **Key point**: Placing cheese between two patties is the signature element of a double cheeseburger.
11. Slice the cooled burger bun in half. Place the cut side down in the same skillet used for cooking the patties. Heat for about 15 seconds until the cut side is golden brown and slightly crispy.
    **Note**: Toasting enhances the aroma and texture of the bun.
12. Assemble the burger in order: Spread ketchup and yellow mustard on the bottom bun → Add sautéed onions → Add sautéed mushrooms → Add crispy bacon → Place the double cheeseburger patties → Add tomato slices → Add pickle slices → Add iceberg lettuce. Spread mayonnaise on the inside of the top bun, place it on top of all the ingredients, and gently press down.
    **Note**: The assembly order affects the final flavor integration and structural stability.

## Health Advice
This double cheeseburger has about 800 calories. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the meal. The beef patties are high in saturated fat; those with high blood lipid levels are advised to consume half of the portion.
