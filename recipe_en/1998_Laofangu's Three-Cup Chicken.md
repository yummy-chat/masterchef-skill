# Laofangu's Three-Cup Chicken
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients
🍗 Chicken thigh (Sanhuang chicken), about 3 thighs, bone-in preferred for more flavor
🫚 Old ginger, to taste (sliced)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, about 1 whole head (peeled, generous amount)
🌿 Thai basil (holy basil), 1–2 large handfuls, essential aromatic herb
🫑 Green and red bell peppers, to taste (cut into chunks, for color)

## Seasonings
🫒 Sesame oil, about 3 tablespoons (1 cup)
🍶 Rice wine, about 2 tablespoons (1 cup)
🥡 Light soy sauce, about 1 tablespoon (1 cup)
🦪 Oyster sauce, 15–20 g
🌿 Thirteen-spice blend, about 1 teaspoon
🍬 Rock sugar, 10–12 g
🥄 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for color)
🧂 Salt, add as needed
🌶️ Dried chili segments (Erjingtiao), to taste

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Frying pan (non-stick)
🫕 Clay pot
🥄 Spatula, chopsticks

## Steps
1. 🍗 Cut the chicken thighs into evenly sized pieces, bone-in to enhance flavor.
2. 🥣 Soak the chicken pieces in clean water, rinsing for over 1 hour until the water runs clear. Drain well.
3. 🍗 Add thirteen-spice blend, a little rice wine, and a little light soy sauce to the chicken. Mix well with your hands and let marinate for 15–30 minutes.
4. 🫚🧅🧄🌿🫑 Slice the ginger, cut scallions into sections, peel the garlic, cut green and red bell peppers into chunks, wash the basil and drain.
5. 🥡 In a bowl, mix sesame oil, rice wine, and light soy sauce at a ratio of approximately 3:2:1. Add oyster sauce and stir until combined. This is the Three-Cup sauce.
6. 🍳 Heat about half the sesame oil in a frying pan over cold oil. Add the ginger slices and slowly fry over low heat until the edges curl and the surface turns slightly yellow.
**Key point**: Make sure to fully infuse the ginger flavor into the oil.

7. 🧅🧄 Add scallion sections and a generous amount of garlic cloves. Continue stir-frying over low heat until the garlic turns slightly yellow on the surface and is very fragrant.
8. 🍗 Spread the chicken pieces flat in the pan. Pan-fry over medium heat, first on one side, then flip to cook the other side until both sides change color and become partially golden brown, and the chicken shrinks slightly.
9. 🌶️ Add dried chili segments, then immediately pour in the full cup of rice wine to use its aroma to remove gamey smells and enhance flavor.
10. 🥡🫕 Pour in the prepared Three-Cup sauce and rock sugar. Stir-fry until well combined, then transfer all ingredients along with the liquid and solids into a preheated clay pot.
11. 🫕 Cover the clay pot with its lid and simmer over low heat for 10–15 minutes until the sauce thickens, becomes sticky, and coats the chicken pieces.
- During this process, check color and taste: if the color is not deep enough, add a little dark soy sauce; if not salty enough, add salt as needed.

12. 🌿🫑 Add green and red bell pepper chunks and a generous amount of basil leaves. Toss lightly a few times, then immediately turn off the heat. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Advice
The "three cups" in Three-Cup Chicken refer to the golden ratio of sesame oil, rice wine, and light soy sauce. However, be mindful of the actual amounts used to avoid excessive oil intake. It is recommended to pair this dish with a bowl of brown rice to balance the high oil and sodium content. This dish is best enjoyed occasionally rather than as a daily meal.
