# Northeastern Roasted Gluten Skewers
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Vital wheat gluten (gluten flour) 300g  
🧂 Salt 2 tsp (1 tsp for dough, 1 tsp for chili oil)  
💧 Warm water 600ml  
🫒 Cooking oil (for frying aromatics) as needed  
🧅 Scallions (cut into sections) as needed  
🫚 Ginger (sliced) as needed  
🌶️ Chili flakes 1 bowl  
🤍 White sesame seeds 1/2 bowl  
🍂 Ground Sichuan pepper 1 tsp  
🌿 Ground cumin 1 tsp  
🍲 Thirteen-spice powder 1 tsp  
🥩 Beef bouillon powder 1 tsp  
🍬 Sugar 1 tsp  
🥜 Peanut butter 2 tbsp  
🦪 Hoisin sauce 2 tbsp  

## Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl  
📏 Measuring cup  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍲 Pot for boiling  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔥 Oven  
🥘 Baking tray  

## Steps
1. Dissolve 1 tsp salt in 600ml warm water and stir until fully combined to create a saline solution.  
**Note**: The water temperature should not be too high, to avoid affecting gluten formation.

2. While stirring, gradually add 300g vital wheat gluten to the saline bowl. Mix with chopsticks or hands until no dry flour remains and it forms a dough.  
**Key point**: Do not knead; this prevents the gluten from becoming too tough.

3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 1 hour, allowing the gluten network to fully relax.

4. On a cutting board, press and stretch the rested dough into a thin rectangular sheet. Cut it in half, then into strips about 1.5cm wide.

5. Take one strip, clamp one end with a pair of chopsticks, and stretch the strip while wrapping it around the chopsticks to form a cylinder. Then push and pull up and down a few times to tighten the gluten. Remove the chopsticks.  
**Key technique**: Stretch the strip thin while wrapping for a more layered texture in the final product.

6. Bring a pot of water to a boil, then lower to the gentlest simmer. Gently place the raw gluten skewers into the water.  
**Note**: Keep the water at a low simmer throughout. Avoid vigorous boiling, which would create pores on the surface of the gluten.

7. Once all gluten pieces float to the surface and have visibly expanded in size, remove them and immediately soak in cold water to make the texture chewier.

8. In a frying pan, add a suitable amount of oil. Add scallion sections and ginger slices. Fry until golden brown, then remove the solids and turn off the heat.

9. Once the oil temperature has dropped slightly, add chili flakes, white sesame seeds, Sichuan pepper powder, cumin powder, thirteen-spice powder, beef bouillon powder, 1 tsp salt, and sugar. Stir quickly to combine and form a chili oil.

10. In a separate bowl, combine 2 tbsp peanut butter and 2 tbsp hoisin sauce. Thin with a small amount of chili oil and stir until smooth, then thoroughly mix with the remaining chili oil to create a chili oil dip.

11. Thread the cooled gluten onto bamboo skewers. Using a knife, rotate the gluten and cut spiral grooves down to the base, allowing the sauce to penetrate more easily.

12. Completely submerge the gluten skewers in the chili oil dip. Baste repeatedly to ensure they are evenly coated.

13. Preheat the oven to 220°C (top and bottom heat). Arrange the coated gluten skewers on a baking tray and drizzle with a little extra chili oil.

14. Place in the middle of the oven and roast for about 20 minutes, until the outer skin is crispy and the surface is bubbling with oil.  
**Observation tip**: Keep an eye on them during the last few minutes to avoid burning.

## Health Advice
Northeastern roasted gluten skewers are made primarily from vital wheat gluten, which is rich in plant protein and low in fat, making them suitable as a snack for fitness enthusiasts and those watching their oil intake. However, the chili oil and peanut butter are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad to balance nutrition.
