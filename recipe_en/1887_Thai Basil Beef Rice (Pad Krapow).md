# Thai Basil Beef Rice (Pad Krapow)
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice 300g (dry weight)
🥩 Beef patties 3 pieces (about 450g, can substitute with chicken or pork)
🧅 Shallots appropriate amount (sliced, can substitute with onion)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🌶️ Long red chili appropriate amount (finely chopped)
🌿 Holy basil (Thai basil) appropriate amount (leaves picked)
🥚 Eggs depending on serving size (pasteurized eggs recommended)
🧂 Light soy sauce 1 tablespoon
🐟 Fish sauce 1 tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
🟤 Dark soy sauce ½ tablespoon
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (more oil needed for frying eggs)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🗑️ Strainer
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍚 Rice cooker
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Bowls

## Instructions
1. Weigh 300g of rice, rinse in a strainer, and drain.
   **Note**: Weighing is essential for precise rice-to-water ratio.
2. Place the rinsed rice into the rice cooker inner pot and add 300g of water.
   **Note**: Strictly following a rice-to-water ratio of about 1:1.05 to 1:1.15 is the secret to perfect rice.
3. Start the rice cooker's cooking program.
4. Slice shallots thinly, mince garlic finely, and cut bird's eye chili and long red chili into small rings.
5. In a small bowl, combine 1 tablespoon light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon fish sauce, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, ½ tablespoon dark soy sauce, and appropriate amount of sugar. Stir well to make the sauce and set aside.
6. Pick holy basil leaves, wash and drain, set aside.
7. Make a sunny-side-up egg: Pour enough cooking oil into the wok, heat over medium heat until 70% hot. Add the egg white first, fry until it bubbles, the edges are set and turn golden brown.
   **Key point**: For a Thai-style sunny-side-up egg, add the egg white first to make the edges crispy; keep the yolk runny.
8. Gently slide the yolk to the center of the egg white. Continue frying until the yolk surface is set and the edges reach the desired crispiness. Remove and set aside.
9. Leave some oil in the wok. Add sliced shallots, minced garlic, and chopped chilies. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.
10. Add the beef patties, breaking them apart into coarse crumbs with your hands. Stir-fry over high heat until the surface is browned and slightly crispy, allowing the aroma of the aromatics to infuse the beef.
    **Note**: Do not over-mince the beef; keep some texture.
11. Pour in the prepared sauce. Continue to stir-fry for about 2 minutes, until the beef is evenly coated with the sauce.
12. Turn off the heat. Immediately add the holy basil leaves, and toss a few times using residual heat until the leaves wilt and are cooked through.
    **Note**: Must add leaves after turning off the heat to preserve their aroma.
13. Plate the cooked rice, top with the stir-fried beef with basil, and place the fried sunny-side-up egg on top.

## Health Advice
This dish combines beef with a runny egg, providing high-quality protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts. The rice ratio is precise, but portion control is necessary to avoid excessive carbohydrate intake. Lean beef is recommended to reduce fat intake.
