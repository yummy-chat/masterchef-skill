# Braised Pork Knuckle Jelly
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork front knuckle 1.35kg (choose one with more skin and less fat; ask the butcher to singe the hair and remove the bone)  
🧅 Scallions 2-3 stalks (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger 4-5 slices  
🍶 Yellow wine or liquor, as needed (for removing gamey flavor)  
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed (for removing gamey flavor and enhancing aroma)  
🧂 Salt, approx. 8g (added at the final seasoning stage)  
🍾 Light soy sauce, approx. 25g (for enhancing umami)  
🧴 Dark soy sauce, approx. 8g (for coloring, optional if you prefer a lighter color)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for washing and soaking)  
🍲 Soup pot (for blanching)  
♨️ Sealed pressure cooker (for stewing)  
🍳 Wok or electric rice cooker (for reducing and heating the sauce)  
🥣 Iron basin or deep bowl (for setting the jelly)  
🫙 Plastic wrap (for sealing and refrigerating)

## Preparation Steps
1. Scrub the pork knuckle clean with warm water, cut into large pieces about 3 to 4 cm square, and soak in cold water for about 10 minutes to remove blood.
**Key point**: Choose a front knuckle with more skin and less fat to ensure sufficient collagen content for the jelly to set properly.

2. Place the meat pieces in cold water, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and an appropriate amount of yellow wine. Heat slowly; when the water is about to boil, reduce to low heat and maintain a gentle simmer. Blanch for 10 minutes until no blood oozes out when a chopstick is inserted into the meat.
**Note**: Do not boil vigorously over high heat, as this may cause collagen loss. Ensure all blood is thoroughly removed.

3. Remove the meat pieces, rinse clean with cold water, and then cut into smaller pieces.
4. Place the meat pieces into the inner pot of the pressure cooker. Add scallions, ginger, white pepper powder, and yellow wine. Pour in enough cold water to just level with the meat surface.
**Key point**: Use minimal water, just enough to cover the meat. Do not add salt during the entire stewing process.

5. Cover the lid and pressure-cook for about 40 minutes. Allow it to cool naturally before opening the lid.
6. Remove and discard the scallions and ginger. Pour the meat pieces and broth together into a wok. Add salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce for seasoning. Heat again to reduce the broth until it levels with the meat surface.
**Key point**: Season only after the meat is fully cooked to prevent it from becoming dry and tough.

7. Pour the meat and broth into an iron basin or deep bowl. Let it cool naturally, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
**Note**: A layer of white pork fat will solidify on the surface after refrigeration. Scrape it off completely before serving to significantly reduce the calorie content.

8. Once the jelly has set, invert it onto a plate to unmold. Cut into thick slices or large chunks.
**Key point**: Since the jelly is high in lean meat, avoid cutting it into thin slices. Thick chunks offer a better texture.

## Health Advice
This braised pork knuckle jelly is rich in collagen and suitable for an appetizing and refreshing summer dish. However, those with high blood lipids should carefully scrape off the surface layer of pork fat. It is recommended to serve with a vinegar and garlic dipping sauce to enhance the flavor and aid digestion.
