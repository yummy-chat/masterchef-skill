# Braised Beef Tendon with Tofu Skin Sticks
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef tendon 3 pieces  
🥜 Tofu skin sticks appropriate amount (soaked in advance and cut into sections)  
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger a large piece  
🌿 Dahurian angelica root a pinch  
🌿 Round cardamom a pinch  
🌿 Amomum tsao-ko shell a pinch  
🌿 Cinnamon a pinch  
🌿 Bay leaves a pinch  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🧂 Rock sugar appropriate amount (can be substituted with white sugar)  
🧂 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Dark soy sauce appropriate amount  
🧂 Fermented soybean paste two tablespoons  
🍺 Beer one bottle  
💧 Water appropriate amount  
🧂 Sichuan pepper oil appropriate amount (use before serving)  
🫒 Cooking oil a small amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
♨️ Pressure cooker (or rice cooker's tendon cooking mode)  
🥣 Spoon, chopsticks, bowl  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Split the beef tendon lengthwise; soak the tofu skin sticks in advance and cut into sections.
2.  Place the beef tendon in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat and continue blanching for 5 minutes, then remove and rinse under cold water.
    **Key point**: Start in cold water, rinse after blanching.
3.  Transfer the beef tendon to a pressure cooker, add ginger, dahurian angelica root, round cardamom, amomum tsao-ko shell, cinnamon, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers. Then season with salt, rock sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and two tablespoons of fermented soybean paste. Pour in a whole bottle of beer and add enough water to level with the ingredients.
    **Note**: The water should just cover the ingredients.
4.  Cover the pot, bring up to pressure, then reduce to low heat and cook for 30 minutes, until the tendon can be easily pierced with chopsticks.
5.  In a separate wok, add a small amount of cooking oil and stir-fry the scallion sections until soft and fragrant.
6.  Pour the cooked beef tendon along with the cooking liquid into the wok. Remove and discard the spices used, keeping the stir-fried scallion sections.
7.  Turn the heat to high, add the tofu skin stick sections, and continuously shake the wok while stir-frying to reduce the sauce, until the liquid is thickened and evenly coats the ingredients.
    **Key point**: When reducing the sauce over high heat, continuously shake the wok to prevent sticking.
8.  Just before finishing, drizzle in an appropriate amount of Sichuan pepper oil, stir-fry evenly, then plate and serve.

## Health Advice  
Beef tendon is rich in collagen, which benefits joint health and skin elasticity, but is high in calories, so moderate consumption is advised. Tofu skin sticks provide plant-based protein. Cooking with beer helps soften the tendon. Those with weaker digestive function should watch their portion size.
