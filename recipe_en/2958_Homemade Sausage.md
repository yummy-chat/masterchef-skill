# Homemade Sausage
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 3 lbs pork hind leg (cut into small pieces and minced, retaining a grainy texture)
💧 200 ml water (added in batches to help the meat absorb moisture)
🍠 180 g sweet potato starch (for a firmer texture)
🎨 6 g red yeast rice powder (optional, for color, does not affect flavor)
🧂 30 g salt
🍬 30 g sugar
🌟 10 g five-spice powder
🫚 4 g ginger powder
⚪ 3 g white pepper powder
🥚 180 g egg whites

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🫗 Sausage stuffer
🪢 Cotton string
🍲 Pot (for boiling in cold water)
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
### Preparing the Meat Mixture
1. **Mince the meat**: Cut the pork hind leg into small pieces, then mince with a knife, retaining a grainy texture.
   **Key point**: Hand-mincing is better than using a meat grinder, as it gives the meat a chewier texture; avoid mincing into a paste to retain graininess.
2. **Mix seasonings**: Place the minced meat in a large bowl, add salt, sugar, five-spice powder, ginger powder, white pepper powder, and egg whites, and stir in one direction until well combined.
3. **Add water**: Gradually add 200 ml of water in batches, stirring thoroughly in the same direction after each addition until the water is fully absorbed by the meat.
   **Key point**: Thorough stirring prevents the final product from being dry and tough.
4. **Add starch and color**: Add sweet potato starch and mix well; if coloring is desired, add red yeast rice powder at this stage and mix until uniform.
5. **Knead until sticky**: Continue stirring in the same direction without stopping until the meat mixture changes from a loose state to a thick, sticky consistency that forms a gel.
   **Key point**: This is crucial for a bouncy texture; ensure sufficient stirring time.
6. **Test the texture**: Take a small amount of the meat mixture, shape it into a ball, boil in cold water, and taste. If the texture is bouncy, the stirring is sufficient; otherwise, continue stirring.

### Stuffing and Cooking
1. **Prepare the casing**: Take a heat-resistant, food-grade plastic casing, leaving one end unsealed initially.
2. **Stuff**: Load the meat mixture into the sausage stuffer, and steadily squeeze it into the casing, pushing forward as you go to prevent the casing from bursting.
3. **Segment and tie**: Use cotton string to tie the long filled sausage into small sections, squeezing tightly, with lengths similar to store-bought sausages.
4. **Place in cold water**: Put the tied sausages into a pot of cold water and bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Note**: Must start with cold water.
5. **Simmer**: After boiling, reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 40 minutes.
   **Key point**: Do not cover the pot during the entire process to prevent steam pressure from causing the casing to burst.
6. **Cool and set**: Immediately remove the sausages after cooking and place them in ice water or cold water to cool quickly, resulting in a firmer texture.

## Health Tips
Homemade sausages are high in protein and low in additives, making them suitable for health-conscious families. Be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure may reduce the salt by 30%.
