# Tomato Sliced Pork Soup 🍅🥩
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 250g pork shoulder (front leg, lean part, sliced thinly)
🍅 Approximately 640g tomatoes (about 4, peeled and cut into small pieces)
🍄 150g mixed mushrooms (crab mushroom, deer antler mushroom, seafood mushroom)
🫔 1 thin tofu skin (torn into small pieces)
🫙 About 3 tablespoons sweet potato starch (for 250g meat, needs to be mixed with boiling water into a paste)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for scallion-ginger water, sautéing at the start, and final garnish)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for scallion-ginger water and sautéing at the start)
🧂 Salt, as needed (added in two batches: when marinating the meat and when seasoning the tomato soup)
🌶️ White pepper, as needed (added in two batches: when marinating the meat and when seasoning the tomato soup)
🧂 MSG, as needed (added when seasoning the tomato soup)
🍶 Rice vinegar, as needed (added after putting the mushrooms into the tomato soup)
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (drizzled in before serving)
🧈 Lard, as needed (used for sautéing scallions, ginger, and tomatoes at the start)
💧 Water, as needed (for making scallion-ginger water and for the soup base after stir-frying tomatoes)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥣 Small stainless steel bowl (for scallion-ginger water)
🥣 Large glass bowl (for marinating the meat slices)
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for mixing the sweet potato starch paste)
🍳 Iron wok (for stir-frying the tomato base and combined cooking)
🍲 Stainless steel pot (for separately boiling the meat slices)
🥄 Strainer and soup ladle

## Cooking Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Remove the fatty parts from the 250g of pork shoulder (front leg). Slice the lean meat thinly.
   **Note**: The meat should be washed in advance.
2. Crush scallions and ginger, add them to some water, and squeeze with your hands to extract the juice, making scallion-ginger water.
3. Add salt, white pepper, and a little scallion-ginger water to the sliced meat. Using your hand, stir in one direction until the meat slices become sticky on the surface, and there is a sticky texture on the container walls and your hands.
   **Key**: The meat must be stirred until it becomes tacky. This locks in moisture and allows the coating to adhere firmly.
4. Put the sweet potato starch into a basin. First, add a small amount of cold water to moisten it. Rub the coarse lumps apart with your hands. Then, immediately pour in freshly boiled water and stir to dissolve, forming a thick, sticky paste.
   **Key**: Boiling water must be used to make the paste for better stickiness and texture. However, first mix with cold water to prevent clumping.
5. Pour the prepared starch paste into the marinated meat slices and mix well, ensuring each slice is evenly coated.
6. Score a cross on the top of each tomato, blanch them in boiling water, then peel the skin off and cut them into small pieces.

**Actual Cooking**
1. Heat lard in a wok. Add scallions and ginger and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the tomato pieces. Over medium heat, use a spatula to stir and press the tomatoes until they are completely broken down into a thick sauce. Add enough water and bring to a boil.
   **Key**: The tomatoes must be fully stir-fried and broken down to release their flavor.
2. Season the tomato soup with salt, white pepper, and MSG. Once boiling, add the mushrooms. Splash in the rice vinegar. Then, tear the tofu skin into small pieces and add them to the pot.
   **Note**: Add the tofu skin before putting in the meat slices.
3. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Gently place the coated meat slices into the water one by one to prevent sticking. Cook over medium heat until all the meat slices float to the surface (indicating they are fully cooked). Use a strainer to remove them and drain.
   **Key**: The meat must be boiled in a separate pot of clean water to keep the soup color clear and not cloudy. If you are skilled at coating, you can also add the meat directly into the tomato soup to cook.
4. Add the drained meat slices to the tomato soup and continue to cook for 2–3 minutes.
5. Turn off the heat. Drizzle in a little sesame oil. Ladle into a bowl and garnish with chopped scallions. Serve.

## Health Suggestions
This tomato sliced pork soup is rich in high-quality protein and lycopene, making it suitable for people who need nutritional support and immune system enhancement. Note that the sweet potato starch must be mixed with boiling water to achieve a smoother texture. However, diabetic patients should manage their intake of the tomato soup base to control sugar consumption.
