# Braised Fried Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 6  
🥬 Baby bok choy (Shanghai greens) a suitable amount (blanched and used as a bed on the plate)  
🧄 Minced garlic a suitable amount  
🫚 Minced ginger a suitable amount  
🌶️ Chopped chili sauce a little  
🫘 Pixian broad bean paste a little  
🫒 Cooking oil (rapeseed oil) a suitable amount  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🦪 Oyster sauce a suitable amount (can be substituted with hoisin sauce; flavor will vary slightly)  
🧂 Salt a little  
🧂 MSG a little (optional)  
🍶 Vinegar a few drops  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn oil a little  
💧 Water a suitable amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Spatula  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🔥 Induction cooker  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the greens**: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the baby bok choy and blanch until just cooked. Drain and place on the bottom of a bowl as a bed.  
   **Key point**: Do not blanch for too long to ensure the greens maintain a vibrant green color.  

2. **Fry the eggs**: Heat oil in a frying pan. Crack in the eggs and fry over medium heat until the egg whites are set and the edges are golden and slightly crispy, with the yolks remaining runny or semi-runny. Remove and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Avoid breaking the egg yolks during frying to keep the eggs intact. Each egg takes about 2–3 minutes; increase time for multiple eggs.  

3. **Sauté aromatics and prepare the sauce**: Leave a little oil in the pan. Reduce heat to low. Add minced garlic, minced ginger, chopped chili sauce, and Pixian broad bean paste. Stir-fry until fragrant and the oil turns red. Then add dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and water. Stir well, increase heat to medium, and bring to a boil.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry the aromatics over low heat to prevent burning. The Pixian broad bean paste must be cooked until the red oil is released.  

4. **Braise the eggs**: Place the fried eggs into the sauce. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5–10 minutes, allowing the eggs to fully absorb the flavor of the sauce.  
   **Key point**: Simmer over low heat, gently turning the eggs occasionally to prevent breakage, ensuring even flavor absorption.  

5. **Season**: Add a few drops of vinegar and a splash of Sichuan peppercorn oil during the braising process. Stir well.  
   **Key point**: Vinegar and Sichuan peppercorn oil enhance the flavor depth; adjust the amount to personal taste.  

6. **Plate and pour sauce**: Arrange the braised eggs one by one on the bed of greens in the bowl. Evenly pour the remaining sauce from the pan over the eggs.  
   **Key point**: Arrange neatly to ensure every egg is coated with the sauce.  

## Health Advice  
This braised fried egg dish is rich in high-quality protein and vegetable fiber, making it suitable for those needing energy replenishment. However, Pixian broad bean paste and oyster sauce are high in salt; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount. Control the oil temperature when frying the eggs to avoid excessive fat intake.
