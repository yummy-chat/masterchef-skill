# Pickled Chili Beef Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef short ribs 1 piece (preferably fatty, soak in water for half an hour to remove blood)
🌾 Flour 1 bowl
🌽 Cornstarch half a bowl
🥚 Egg 1
🌶️ Pickled sea chili peppers appropriate amount (finely minced with ginger and garlic)
🫚 Pickled ginger appropriate amount (minced as above)
🧄 Old ginger appropriate amount (minced as above)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced as above)
🥬 Pickled vegetables (suan cai) appropriate amount (finely chopped separately)
🌶️ Wild pepper (ye shan jiao) appropriate amount (finely chopped separately)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (some whole for broth, some chopped for garnish)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (some whole for broth, some chopped for garnish)
🧂 Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch
🍶 Rice wine appropriate amount
🧂 Star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, bay leaves a small amount each
🫘 Doubanjiang (fermented bean paste) 1 small spoon
🍚 Fermented rice (lao zao) appropriate amount
🧂 Salt a small amount (for batter)
🥓 Lard 1 spoon (for stir-frying beef)
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Wok or frying pan
♨️ Pressure cooker (or large stockpot)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon, soup spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowls (for soaking beef, storing broth, mixing batter)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Beef pre-treatment**: Cut the beef short ribs into large pieces, soak in water for half an hour to fully remove blood and gamey smell.
2. **Prepare aromatics**: Place pickled sea chili peppers, pickled ginger, old ginger, and garlic on a cutting board and finely mince, so they release aroma quickly when stir-fried.
3. **Prepare vegetables**: Finely chop the pickled vegetables and wild pepper separately and set aside.
4. **Make the batter**: In a bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, and a small amount of salt. Crack in the egg and gradually add water, stirring to form a thick, flowable batter, similar in consistency to concrete.
**Note**: The batter must not be too thin, or it will not hold its shape.

5. **Simmer the pickled chili broth**: Heat oil in a wok. When hot, add a small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns and fry until fragrant. Immediately add the minced pickled chili, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry over medium heat until red oil releases and the aroma is rich. Add water, along with whole scallions and cilantro, and allow to simmer.
**Key point**: Once the broth is bright red and intensely flavored, use a slotted spoon to remove and discard all solids, keeping only the clear red broth.

6. **Blanch the beef**: Place the beef in a pot of cold water, add rice wine, bring to a boil, and skim off any foam. Remove the beef.
**Key point**: After removing, rinse the beef thoroughly with hot water to remove any surface foam. Do not use cold water, or the meat will become tough.

7. **Stir-fry the beef**: In a wok, add vegetable oil and 1 spoon of lard. Once melted, add the blanched beef pieces and stir-fry over low-medium heat until the surface is lightly charred, moisture has evaporated, and the meat is fragrant. Add the chopped pickled vegetables, wild pepper, star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, and bay leaves. Continue stir-frying until the pickled vegetables release water and begin to bubble.
**Key point**: Add 1 small spoon of doubanjiang and stir-fry immediately to release red oil. Use doubanjiang sparingly, as it should not overpower the main pickled chili flavor.

8. **Pressure cook**: Pour the prepared pickled chili broth into the wok, add the fermented rice, and bring to a boil over high heat. Transfer everything to a pressure cooker. Once pressurized, cook over medium heat for about 18 minutes, until the beef is tender and flavorful.
9. **Cook the dough drops**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Tilt the bowl of batter over the edge of the pot and use a spoon to scrape the batter in small amounts into the boiling water. Alternatively, use a chopstick to push the batter along the bowl's edge into strips.
**Doneness indicator**: The dough drops are ready when they float to the surface and turn slightly yellow.
**Key point**: Using a scraping or pushing technique makes the dough drops smoother and chewier.

10. **Assemble the bowl**: Scoop the cooked dough drops into a bowl, ladle over the braised pickled chili beef and red broth, and garnish with chopped scallions and cilantro. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This pickled chili beef noodle soup is rich in protein and carbohydrates. However, due to the high content of pickled chilies, lard, and doubanjiang, it is recommended for moderate consumption, especially suitable for those needing to warm the body and boost energy. Ensure the dough drops are fully cooked, and those with excessive stomach acid should consume with caution.
