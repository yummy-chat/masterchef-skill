# Luxurious Meat Zongzi 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Glutinous rice 5 jin (rinsed, soaked for 1 hour, drained)
🥩 Pork belly/shoulder meat 3 jin (with fat, cut into thick slices)
🟢 Hulled mung beans appropriate amount (pre-soaked until soft)
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks appropriate amount (fresh yolks, add baijiu to remove odor)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms appropriate amount (soaked)
🐚 Small abalone appropriate amount (soaked several days in advance)
🌭 Chinese sausage appropriate amount (sliced)
🦪 Sea cucumber appropriate amount (soaked)
🐚 Dried scallops appropriate amount (soaked)
🍃 Zongzi leaves appropriate amount (choose wide and long leaves)
🧶 Cotton string/grass string appropriate amount

🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon (for marinating meat) + 1 tablespoon (for mixing rice)
🍾 Soy sauce 45 grams (for marinating meat) + 50 milliliters (for mixing rice)
🍯 Oyster sauce 30 grams (for marinating meat)
🍬 White sugar 10 grams (for marinating meat)
🌿 Ground white pepper 5 grams (for marinating meat)
🧂 Five-spice powder 1 tablespoon (for mixing rice)
🍶 Baijiu appropriate amount (for treating salted egg yolks)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥣 Glass bowl/large container
🧺 Colander/sieve
🍽️ Bowls, plates
📦 Plastic food storage container
♨️ Electric pressure cooker
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Cooking Steps
1. **Marinate the meat**: Cut the pork belly or shoulder meat into thick slices, place in a bowl, add 1 tablespoon of salt, 45 grams of soy sauce, 30 grams of oyster sauce, 10 grams of white sugar, and 5 grams of ground white pepper. Mix and massage thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Marinate overnight for more flavor, at least 4 hours; choose meat with fat for a richer taste.

2. **Prepare the ingredients**: Soak the hulled mung beans until soft in advance; spray the salted egg yolks with baijiu to remove odor; soak the dried shiitake mushrooms, small abalone, sea cucumber, and dried scallops separately in advance; slice the Chinese sausage and set aside.

3. **Season the glutinous rice**: Rinse the glutinous rice, soak for 1 hour, then drain. Add 50 milliliters of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of five-spice powder. Toss until the rice is evenly colored.
   **Note**: Be sure to drain the rice well, otherwise it will be difficult to mix evenly and the color will be too dark.

4. **Wrap the zongzi**: Take two zongzi leaves, overlap them, and fold the tips into a cone shape. Layer in one layer of glutinous rice, one layer of mung beans, one layer of marinated meat, then add the salted egg yolk, abalone, Chinese sausage, sea cucumber, dried scallops, and other fillings. Finally, cover with a layer of glutinous rice.
   **Key point**: Layer in a “rice-filling-rice” order; tie tightly to prevent the zongzi from falling apart during cooking.

5. **Cook the zongzi**: Arrange the wrapped zongzi neatly in the electric pressure cooker. Add enough water to fully submerge the zongzi. Select the “Beans/Tendons” function and run the cycle twice.
   **Key point**: After cooking, let the zongzi steep in the pot for 2 hours. This makes the fillings more flavorful and the texture softer and stickier.

## Health Tips
This luxurious meat zongzi is nutritionally balanced but high in calories. It is suitable for those with high energy expenditure or who need to gain weight. People with weak digestive function should control their intake. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables or pu'er tea to cut the richness, and avoid overeating in one sitting.
