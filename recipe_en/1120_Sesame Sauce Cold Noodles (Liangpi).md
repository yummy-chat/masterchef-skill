# Sesame Sauce Cold Noodles (Liangpi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Pure sesame paste 100g
🥜 Peanut butter 20g
🫒 Sesame oil 20g
🫒 Vegetable oil 500g (for frying seasoned oil)
🌿 Cumin 4g
🌟 Star anise 3g
🌳 Cinnamon 2g
🍂 Cardamom 1 piece
🌶 Sichuan peppercorns 2g
🍃 Bay leaf 1g
🧅 Scallion 30g (sliced)
🫚 Ginger 20g (sliced, and some julienned for seasoning water)
🧅 Onion 20g (julienned)
🍝 Cold noodles (Liangpi) 1 portion (store-bought)
🦪 Oyster sauce 30g
🍶 Seasoned soy sauce 15g
🍇 Shaanxi aged vinegar 200g
💧 Seasoning water 800g (made with water, spices, salt, and chicken bouillon)
🧂 Salt 80g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 10g
🧄 Garlic water 1/2 tablespoon (garlic:water = 1:8)
🌶 Chili oil for cold noodles 1 tablespoon
🥒 Cucumber shreds, to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥅 Strainer
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1.  Mix pure sesame paste, peanut butter, oyster sauce, seasoned soy sauce, and sesame oil together until well combined. This enriches the flavor and neutralizes the astringent aftertaste of pure sesame paste.
2.  Pour vegetable oil into a wok. Add cumin, star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaf, sliced scallion, sliced ginger, and julienned onion to the cold oil. Fry over low heat, stirring constantly, until the aromatics are golden and fragrant. Turn off the heat, cover, and let steep in residual heat until cool. Strain out the solids and reserve the clear seasoned oil.
**Note**: The spice flavor should not be too strong, so it doesn't overpower the aroma of the sesame paste.
3.  Gradually add the strained seasoned oil to the sesame paste mixture, stirring continuously, until the sesame paste reaches a pourable, flowing consistency.
4.  Bring about 1000g of water to a boil in a wok. Add the spices and boil for 5 minutes until lightly fragrant. In a separate pot, fry julienned ginger in a little oil until fragrant. Add the Shaanxi aged vinegar, bring to a boil to remove harsh acidity and raw flavor. Then add 800g of the boiled spice water, salt, and chicken bouillon. Bring to a boil again, then strain out the ginger and spices. This is the seasoning water.
5.  Place the cold noodles in a bowl. Add 1/2 tablespoon of seasoning water and 1/2 tablespoon of garlic water. Drizzle with the prepared sesame sauce. Finally, top with a tablespoon of chili oil and cucumber shreds. Mix well and serve.

## Health Advice
Cold noodles are rich in carbohydrates, and pairing them with sesame sauce provides quality fats and protein, making this a good appetizing main dish. Be mindful of the salt content in the seasoning water. Individuals with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of salt used.
