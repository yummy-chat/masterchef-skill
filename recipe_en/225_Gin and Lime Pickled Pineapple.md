# Gin and Lime Pickled Pineapple
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍍 Pineapple, about 1/4 (cut into small pieces)
🍋 Lime, 1 (use yellow zest and juice)
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🍸 Gin, about two bottle caps

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
📦 Rectangular storage container
🥄 Spoon
🧽 Zester
🥃 Measuring cup (or bottle cap)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the pineapple into uniform small pieces and place them in the storage container.
2. Use a zester to grate a thin layer of lime zest, then squeeze in an appropriate amount of lime juice.
   **Key Point**: Only grate the yellow outer skin; the white pith is bitter and should not be included.
3. Sprinkle in white sugar and a pinch of salt, then pour in about two bottle caps of gin.
   **Tip**: Gin can be added according to personal preference, as it enhances the refreshing botanical aroma.
4. Use a spoon to thoroughly mix all ingredients, ensuring the pineapple pieces are evenly coated with the dressing.
5. Cover the storage container and refrigerate for at least two hours to allow the flavors to fully meld and penetrate.
6. Serve chilled. The taste is sweet, crisp, and refreshing, with the lime and gin aromas making the pineapple even more delightful.

## Health Advice
Pineapple is rich in vitamin C and bromelain. Paired with lime and salt, it enhances flavor and aids digestion, making it ideal for a refreshing summer palate cleanser. It is recommended to use less sugar, and diabetics should only have a small taste.
