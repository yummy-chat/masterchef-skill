# Crispy Fried Milk Custard
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Milk Custard Base
🥛 Milk 350g
🍬 Sugar 40g
🌽 Cornstarch 40g
🥛 Condensed milk 15g
🥚 Egg whites 2 (about 60g, add in 2-3 batches)

### Crispy Batter
🌾 Cake flour 100g
🌽 Cornstarch 8g
🥡 Baking powder 6g (must be mixed and used immediately)
🧂 Salt 1g
💧 Water about 130g (adjust as needed)
🥚 Egg yolks 2
🌻 Sunflower oil 10g

### Coating and Frying
🌽 Cornstarch as needed (for coating milk custard strips)
🛢️ Cooking oil as needed (for deep frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Saucepan
🥢 Whisk
📦 Airtight container (lined with plastic wrap)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥘 Deep fryer or wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Spatula
🧻 Paper towels

## Instructions
1. **Make the milk custard mixture**: Add sugar, cornstarch, milk, and condensed milk to the saucepan in that order. Note: Whisk all ingredients together in the cold pan to ensure the cornstarch is fully dissolved before turning on the heat, to prevent lumps. Heat over medium-high heat, stirring continuously. Once the liquid starts to thicken, reduce the heat to low and continue stirring until the mixture is thick and smooth. Remove from heat immediately.
2. **Add the egg whites**: Add the egg whites in 2-3 batches to the still-warm milk mixture, mixing thoroughly after each addition. The egg whites make the custard texture more tender and smooth. Use a spatula to scrape down the sides of the pan and mix evenly.
3. **Set the milk custard**: Line an airtight container with plastic wrap and pour in the milk mixture. Use a spatula to roughly smooth the surface. Cover with plastic wrap, pressing it gently to adhere to the surface of the custard. Freeze for at least 3 hours until completely firm and solid to the touch.
4. **Cut into strips**: Unmold the milk custard and cut it into strips about 1.5cm wide. A slightly longer freezing time allows for cleaner, neater cuts.
5. **Prepare the crispy batter**: Combine cake flour, cornstarch, salt, and baking powder. Add the egg yolks and about 130g of water. Whisk until smooth and lump-free. The batter should be thick enough to flow slowly and coat the ingredients, adjust the water amount as needed. Finally, add the sunflower oil and whisk again until fully incorporated. Note: The batter contains baking powder, which reacts upon contact with water, so it must be made and used immediately.
6. **Coat with dry starch**: Roll the milk custard strips in dry cornstarch, ensuring they are lightly and evenly coated. Gently shake off any excess powder. This helps the moist custard adhere to the batter and prevents it from falling off during frying.
7. **First fry**: Heat oil in a pan to 160-170°C. Gently place the batter-coated milk custard strips into the oil one by one. Fry for about 30 seconds until the outer shell is set and the strips begin to float. Remove and drain on a rack or plate.
8. **Second fry (re-fry)**: Increase the oil temperature to 190°C. Return all the fried milk custard strips to the hot oil and fry for another 15-20 seconds until the shell is golden brown and noticeably crispy. Remove quickly. The second fry helps force out excess oil and makes the outer shell more crispy.
9. **Serve immediately**: Place on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve immediately for the best texture – the inside custard should be soft and moist while the shell is crispy. If left too long, the shell will soften and become soggy, losing the contrast between the crispy exterior and tender interior.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in sugar and oil. It is recommended to control the portion size, especially for individuals with high blood sugar or high cholesterol, who should only consume a small amount as a treat. The exterior is crispy and the filling is tender and smooth, but excessive consumption is not advisable.
