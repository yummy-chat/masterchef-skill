# Caramel Crème Brûlée Macarons
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Butter 15g (for caramel sauce) + 180g (for crème brûlée buttercream, softened in advance)
🥚 Egg yolks 48g
🥚 Egg whites 80g
🥛 Heavy cream 100g (warmed in advance)
🧂 Sea salt 2g
🌿 Vanilla paste appropriate amount (about a few drops)
🍚 Granulated sugar 100g (for caramel sauce) + 29g (for crème brûlée buttercream sugar syrup) + 75g (for macaron shells) + appropriate amount (for caramel crust)
💧 Water 20g (for caramel sauce) + 15g (for crème brûlée buttercream sugar syrup) + appropriate amount (for moistening macaron shells)
🌰 Almond flour 95g (fine flour for macarons)
🍬 Powdered sugar 85g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Small saucepan
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🥄 Electric hand mixer
🍴 Stand mixer
🧺 Metal sieve
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥛 Glass bowl / measuring cup
🔪 Silicone spatula
🌀 Piping bag
📐 Silicone mat with circle pattern
🔥 Oven
📄 Baking sheet / parchment paper
🔥 Blow torch
🫙 Glass jar with lid

## Preparation Steps
1. In a small saucepan, add 20g water and 100g granulated sugar. Heat over medium-low heat. Do not stir. Wait until the sugar syrup turns from clear to a light caramel color.
2. Add 15g butter and stir until melted. After the bubbling subsides, gradually pour in 100g heavy cream that has been warmed in advance, stirring until combined.
3. Add 2g sea salt and mix well. Pour the caramel sauce into a sealed jar and set aside.
4. Heat 15g water and 29g granulated sugar to 110°C. Meanwhile, beat 48g egg yolks until the color lightens and the volume increases.
5. Slowly pour the hot sugar syrup into the egg yolk mixture while continuously beating until thick.
6. Gradually add 180g softened butter, mixing well after each addition. Add a few drops of vanilla paste and continue beating until smooth and paste-like.
7. Place 80g egg whites and 75g granulated sugar in a bowl. Heat over a water bath to about 50°C, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved.
8. Transfer to a stand mixer. Beat from low to high speed until stiff peaks form.
9. Add the sifted 95g almond flour and 85g powdered sugar all at once. Use a spatula to fold and mix until the batter is smooth. When lifting the spatula, the batter should fall in a continuous ribbon-like stream.
10. Transfer the batter to a piping bag and pipe onto a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat with circle patterns, making the sizes uniform.
11. Place in a slightly warm oven to let the shells dry until the surface is no longer tacky to the touch.
12. Preheat the oven to 145°C. Bake for 16 minutes. After baking, let cool completely.
13. Lightly moisten the surface of each macaron shell with water, then evenly coat with granulated sugar.
14. Use a blow torch to melt the surface sugar, forming a caramel-colored crispy crust.
15. Take one macaron shell, pipe a layer of crème brûlée buttercream onto it, add an appropriate amount of caramel sauce in the center, and top with another macaron shell. Gently press together. Refrigerate before serving.

## Health Advice
Caramel Crème Brûlée Macarons are high in sugar and butter. It is recommended to enjoy them occasionally as a dessert, paired with unsweetened tea or black coffee to balance the sweetness. Suitable for those who enjoy challenging baking skills. Those with high blood lipids or who need to control sugar intake should consume in moderation.
