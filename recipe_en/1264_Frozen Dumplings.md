# Frozen Dumplings  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥟 One bag of unexpired frozen dumplings  
🧂 Black vinegar 10-20ml  
🧂 Ginger 50g (a small piece)  
🧂 Sesame oil 2 drops  
🧂 Garlic/garlic paste 3 cloves per person  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Cooking spoon or spatula  
🥘 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Plate or bowl  
🥣 Small bowl (for dipping sauce)  

## Instructions  
1. Fill the pot with an appropriate amount of water and bring to a boil over medium heat. The water level should be about 1–2 times the height of the dumplings.  
2. Add the frozen dumplings directly into the boiling water.  
3. After adding the dumplings, gently stir the water with a cooking spoon, avoiding contact with the dumplings to prevent sticking to the pot or breaking the wrappers.  
4. Stir gently for 3 seconds every 30 seconds. Stop stirring once the dumplings float to the surface.  
5. After the dumplings have floated and the water boils again, take one dumpling to check for doneness. If the wrapper is undercooked, pour in 80ml of cold water to lower the temperature, continue boiling, and repeat the stirring and checking process. Add cold water up to two times at most for fully cooked dumplings.  
6. Once all dumplings have floated, use a slotted spoon or spatula to remove them and place them on a plate or in a bowl. Serve immediately.  
7. After eating, allow the pot to cool, discard the remaining water, and clean the pot promptly with dish soap to prevent flour residue from forming a sticky film.  

## Dipping Sauce Pairings  
* Slice ginger into thin strips and mix with 20ml of black vinegar.  
* Add 1–3 drops of sesame oil to the black vinegar and stir well.  
* Mix garlic paste into the black vinegar (omit or address later if fresh breath is desired).  

## Health Tips  
Frozen dumplings are a convenient fast-food option. It is recommended to pair them with a vegetable salad or clear soup to increase dietary fiber intake. Pay attention to the sodium content in the dipping sauce; those with high blood pressure should reduce the use of soy sauce and chili paste.
