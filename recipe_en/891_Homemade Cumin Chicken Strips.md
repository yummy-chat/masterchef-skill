# Homemade Cumin Chicken Strips
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast, approximately 800g (about 15 pieces, yields approximately 11 servings)
🥚 Eggs, about 2 (beaten into egg wash)
🍞 Bread crumbs, as needed (must be crushed)
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed
🧂 Salt, 15g
🍬 Granulated sugar, 10g
🫚 Ginger powder (or ginger juice), 3g
🌶️ White pepper powder, 5g
🥔 Sweet potato starch, 60g
🌿 Cumin seeds, as needed (key for flavor)
🌶️ Chili powder, as needed (optional)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Glass bowls (multiple)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🫓 Baking tray
🪄 Plastic wrap
🛍️ Ziplock bag (to use as gloves)
📏 Rolling pin

## Preparation Steps
1. **Process the Chicken Breast**: Cut the chicken breast into small pieces, then mince into a fine paste.
   *You can use a meat grinder instead of mincing by hand.*
2. **Season the Mince**: Add salt, sugar, ginger powder, white pepper powder, and sweet potato starch to the minced chicken. Stir continuously in one direction for about 6 minutes, until the mixture is firm, cohesive, and offers noticeable resistance.
   *Thorough stirring is key to a bouncy, Q-like texture.*
3. **Shape and Freeze**: Line a baking tray with plastic wrap, pour the seasoned chicken mixture onto it, press it firmly to compact it and smooth the surface. Freeze for at least 2 hours, or until completely solid.
   *Make sure to press firmly to prevent air pockets from forming when cutting.*
4. **Prepare the Coating**: Use a rolling pin to crush the bread crumbs, then mix well with the cumin seeds. Prepare two additional bowls, one with cornstarch and one with the beaten eggs.
5. **Cut into Strips**: Remove the frozen chicken block. Cut into evenly sized strips, roughly the thickness of store-bought chicken strips.
6. **Three-Step Coating Process**: First, evenly coat each chicken strip with a layer of cornstarch. Then, dip it into the egg wash, ensuring it is completely wet. Finally, coat it with the prepared bread crumb and cumin seed mixture, pressing gently to ensure it adheres well.
   *Ensure the egg wash is sufficient to allow the bread crumbs to stick properly.*
7. **Deep Fry**: Heat oil in a wok to approximately 160°C, then turn the heat to the lowest setting. Gently place the coated chicken strips into the oil. Gently stir to prevent sticking initially. Once they are set, turn them frequently for even cooking. Fry until all strips float to the surface and are a bright, golden color.
8. **Remove from Oil**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried chicken strips. Place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve immediately.
   *If you cannot eat them all at once, the raw, coated strips can be frozen and refried later. Adjust the frying time and temperature as needed for refrying.*

## Health Tips
This cumin chicken strip recipe uses chicken breast as a substitute for high-fat fried meats. It is rich in protein and relatively low in fat, making it suitable for satisfying cravings during a fitness or weight loss phase. Be mindful of controlling the oil temperature and the amount of coating to avoid absorbing too much oil. Reduce the amount of chili powder for children.
