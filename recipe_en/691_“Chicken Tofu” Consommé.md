# “Chicken Tofu” Consommé
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Fresh chicken pieces, as needed (for base stock)
🐷 Ham, as needed (to enhance stock flavor)
🐔 Chicken breast (lean meat), approx. 100g (for clarification, deveined)
🥕 Carrot, approx. 100g (for clarification)
🥚 Eggs, 4 (egg whites only; 2 for clarification, 2 for the “tofu”)
🫘 Green peas, as needed (for garnish, blanched)
🐔 Chicken breast, 250g (for “tofu”, deveined completely)
🌾 Cornstarch, 15g (for “tofu”)
🧅 Ginger-scallion water, as needed (for clarification)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Wok
🪣 Large stockpot
🥄 Strainer / fine-mesh sieve
🌀 Manual food grinder/mincer
🍹 Blender/food processor
🫕 Glass soup pot
🕯️ Ceramic serving bowl (with a recessed base for a candle)

## Instructions
1. Clean the fresh chicken pieces. If not very fresh, blanch them first, then rinse thoroughly to remove scum and impurities.
2. Prepare the clarification mixture: Put ham slices, carrot slices, and the 100g of lean chicken breast into a manual food grinder and grind until granular but not too fine. Transfer to a bowl, add 2 egg whites and some ginger-scallion water, then mix by hand until the mixture becomes loose.
**Key point:** The meat must not contain any fat or the stock will turn cloudy. The mixture should retain a coarse texture to trap impurities.

3. Prepare the “tofu” mixture: Place 250g of deveined chicken breast, 250g of chicken stock, 2 egg whites, 15g of cornstarch, and 1 teaspoon of salt into a blender. Blend on high speed for about 20 seconds until very smooth. Pass through a fine-mesh sieve.
**Key point:** The stock-to-chicken ratio is 1:1. Straining is essential for a silky texture.

4. Make the chicken stock: Put the cleaned chicken pieces into a large stockpot, add enough water, and optionally a small amount of ham for extra flavor. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer until the stock is flavorful.
5. Clarify the stock: Remove the stock from heat. Let it cool slightly or become lukewarm, then stir in the prepared clarification mixture. Return to low heat, keeping the surface still (do not let it boil). The mixture will slowly rise to the top, forming a crust that traps impurities.
**Key point:** Maintain a low heat at all times. Never let the stock boil vigorously, or it will become cloudy.

6. Cook the “tofu”: Bring a separate pot of water to a boil. Stir the water to create a vortex, then gently pour the strained chicken “tofu” mixture into the center. Once the “tofu” rises to the surface, reduce the heat to a bare simmer and cook for about 30 minutes until fully set. Alternatively, you can turn off the heat and let it cook through with residual heat, or steam it.
7. Assembly: Strain the clarified stock through a fine-mesh sieve into the serving bowl, discarding the clarification residue. Carefully lift the cooked “tofu” out, drain it thoroughly, and gently place it into the clear stock. Garnish with goji berries and blanched green peas.
**Note:** The “tofu” must be thoroughly drained. Handle it gently when placing it into the soup to avoid breaking it and clouding the stock.

## Health Tips
This dish features a delicate “tofu” made from chicken stock and egg whites. It is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those seeking a light diet. Note that the ham used to enhance the stock adds sodium; those with high blood pressure should limit their soup intake.
