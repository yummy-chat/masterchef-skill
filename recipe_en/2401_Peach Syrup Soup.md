# Peach Syrup Soup  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍑 2 peaches (choose slightly firmer ones to maintain shape)  
🍄 3 dried white fungus / snow fungus (rehydrated in advance)  
🌿 A small handful of perilla leaves  
🍉 Assorted seasonal fruit cubes (watermelon, grapes, lychee, etc., for garnish)  
🍬 100 g rock sugar  
🍋 Half a lemon (juiced)  
🍎 10 ml apple cider vinegar  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Cooking pot (glass pot)  
🍴 Paring knife  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🫗 Fine mesh sieve/strainer  
🧊 Bowl or glass container (for chilling and soaking)

## Instructions  
1. Place the rehydrated white fungus in the pot, add enough water, and bring to a boil over high heat. Then reduce to low heat and simmer for 45 minutes until the liquid becomes thick and the gelatin is released.  

2. Gently place the peeled whole peaches into the boiling white fungus soup. Keep at a gentle simmer and cook for 5–8 minutes, until the peaches feel soft when lightly pressed with a finger.  
   **Key**: Remove the peaches immediately after cooking and plunge them into ice water to maintain texture and shape.  

3. Add the perilla leaves and peach peels to the soup used to cook the peaches. Continue simmering over low heat for a short while, until the liquid turns a pinkish-purple color.  

4. Strain the soup through a fine mesh sieve, discarding the perilla leaves, peach peels, and white fungus residue to obtain a clear base liquid.  

5. Return the strained liquid to the pot, add the rock sugar, and stir until fully dissolved.  

6. Allow the syrup to cool naturally to 40–50°C, then squeeze in the lemon juice and add the apple cider vinegar. The liquid will turn into a more vibrant purple color.  

7. Place the chilled peaches into the seasoned syrup, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours to let them soak.  

8. Remove the peaches and place them into serving bowls. Spoon some syrup over the top and garnish with assorted seasonal fruit cubes as desired.  

## Health Advice  
This peach syrup soup is rich in gelatin and vitamins, making it ideal for cooling down in summer. White fungus nourishes the lungs, while peach helps replenish qi. Note that the rock sugar content is relatively high, so those with high blood sugar should consume it in moderation.
