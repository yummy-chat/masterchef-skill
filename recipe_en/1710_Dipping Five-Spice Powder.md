# Dipping Five-Spice Powder
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Star anise, to taste
🌿 Cinnamon, to taste
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
🌿 Cloves, to taste
🌿 Fennel seeds, to taste
🧂 Cumin, to taste
🍪 Peach pastry, to taste (crushed)
🫘 Soybeans, to taste (roasted and crushed)
🥜 Peanuts, to taste (roasted and crushed)
🤍 White sesame seeds, to taste (ground into powder)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🌀 Grinder/food processor
🥣 Mixing bowl
🫙 Airtight glass jar
🧂 Table salt (only for bottom of jar to prevent moisture)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place star anise, cinnamon, Sichuan peppercorns, cloves, and fennel seeds into a dry wok. Stir-fry over low heat until the color darkens slightly and the aroma releases. Remove and spread out to cool.
**Key point**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning.
2. Once the cooled spices are ready, grind them into a fine powder using the grinder.
3. Crush the peach pastry; roast the soybeans and peanuts beforehand, then crush them; grind the white sesame seeds into a powder.
4. In a mixing bowl, combine the ground spice powder, cumin, crushed peach pastry, crushed soybeans, crushed peanuts, and white sesame powder. Mix thoroughly until well blended.
5. Evenly sprinkle a thin layer of table salt at the bottom of a dry, airtight glass jar.
**Note**: The salt layer absorbs moisture, preventing the five-spice powder from clumping due to humidity.
6. Pour the mixed dipping powder into the jar, seal it tightly, and use as needed.

## Health Advice
This five-spice powder is enriched with peach pastry and crushed peanuts, reducing salt without sacrificing flavor. It is suitable for dipping fried foods or dressing cold dishes, making it a friendlier option for those needing to control sodium intake. Be careful to use low heat to prevent burning during preparation, and the salt layer at the bottom of the sealed jar helps extend the shelf life.
