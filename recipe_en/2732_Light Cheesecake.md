# Light Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients  
🥚 3 large eggs (use directly from refrigerator, separate yolks and whites)  
🥛 60g milk  
🧀 160g cream cheese (use directly from refrigerator)  
🧈 35g unsalted butter (use directly from refrigerator)  
🌾 25g cake flour  
🍶 3g white vinegar (add to egg whites before whipping)  
🍬 40g sugar (add in batches when whipping egg whites)  

## Tools and Equipment  
📦 Small oval baking pan  
📜 Parchment paper  
♨️ Hot water pot (for melting cheese mixture and water bath baking)  
🌀 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Silicone spatula  
🪝 Sieve  
🫕 Deep baking pan (for water bath)  
🔥 Oven  
🥣 Two mixing bowls (one for egg yolk mixture and one for meringue)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the baking pan**: Line a small baking pan with parchment paper, leaving the sides loose and not sticking to the pan, with the paper slightly higher than the pan's edge. A thin-walled small pan works best.  
2. **Separate eggs**: Separate the egg whites and yolks. Place the egg whites in a clean, grease-free, and water-free bowl, and refrigerate until needed. Use eggs directly from the refrigerator without warming.  
3. **Make the cheese mixture**: Combine milk, cream cheese, and butter in a bowl. Heat over a pot of hot water and stir continuously until fully melted, smooth, and lump-free. Remove from heat and let the residual warmth work.  
4. **Make the egg yolk mixture**: Add the egg yolks to the warm cheese mixture and stir for about 1 minute until well combined. Sift in the cake flour and stir quickly until a smooth batter forms with no dry flour. Stir quickly after adding the flour to prevent the yolks from cooking due to high heat.  
5. **Whip the meringue**: Add white vinegar to the egg whites. Whip on medium-high speed, adding the sugar in batches. After all sugar is added, switch to medium-low speed to stabilize the bubbles. The total whipping time is about 6 minutes. Whip until soft peaks form, with the whisk creating a soft, drooping hook shape. Be careful not to over-whip.  
6. **Combine the batter**: Add the meringue to the egg yolk mixture in three batches. Fold gently using a cutting and folding motion to combine evenly and avoid deflating the meringue. The final batter should be smooth, shiny, and flow easily.  
7. **Pour and water bath**: Pour the batter from a height into the parchment-lined pan, smooth the surface, and do not tap the pan. Place the pan into a larger deep baking pan, then pour boiling water into the larger pan for a water bath.  
8. **Bake**: Preheat the oven in advance. Place the baking pan on the oven's bottom rack and bake at 120°C for 60 minutes. Low-temperature slow baking is key. After 60 minutes, the cake should have doubled in volume and the surface should be set. Turn on the broil function and bake for about 3 more minutes until evenly browned.  
9. **Cool and refrigerate**: Once out of the oven, remove the cake from the pan once it is cool enough to handle. Let it cool completely, then refrigerate for 2-3 hours or overnight. Chilling enhances the flavor and stabilizes the texture.

## Health Advice  
This light cheesecake is moderate in calories and rich in protein and calcium, making it suitable as an afternoon tea treat. For those on a weight-loss plan, the sugar can be reduced to 30g. Those with lactose intolerance can replace the milk with lactose-free milk.
