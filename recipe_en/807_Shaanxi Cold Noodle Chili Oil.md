# Shaanxi Cold Noodle Chili Oil
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫒 Rapeseed oil (or other cooking oil) 1000g  
🤍 White sesame seeds 40g  
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes (Qin pepper, fragrant not spicy) 70g  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 70g  
🧅 Ginger 30g (sliced)  
🧅 Scallion 80g  
🧅 Onion 80g  
🌿 Cilantro 1 stalk (with roots)  
🥬 Celery 1 stalk  
🥕 Carrot 30g  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar 30g  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) 20g  

**Spice Powder Formula** (total approx. 240g, use 30g this time, store remaining in sealed container):  
- Cumin seeds 30g  
- Star anise 20g  
- Cassia cinnamon 7g  
- Sichuan peppercorns 7g  
- Bay leaves 10g  
- Angelica dahurica 1g  
- Black cardamom 7g  
- Amomum villosum 10g  
- Amomum tsao-ko 6g  
- Cloves 2g  
- Nutmeg 5g  
- Galangal 3g  
- Gromwell root 2g (mainly for color, optional)  
- Sophora alopecuroides seeds 5g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (iron pot)  
🌡️ Kitchen thermometer  
🥄 Large slotted spoon  
🕳️ Fine mesh strainer  
🔪 Grinder/spice grinder  
🥣 Metal measuring cup/spoon  
📦 Lidded wooden container  
⚖️ Electronic scale  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix all dry spices together. Crush larger spices first, then grind in two batches in the grinder. After the first grind, mix all together and grind again for a fine, uniform powder. Set aside 30g for use, and seal the remainder for storage.  
**Note**: Avoid grinding too much spice powder at once to prevent flavor loss.

2. Slice the ginger. Cut the scallion, cilantro, and celery into sections. Cut the onion and carrot into chunks or slices. Vegetables have high water content, so be cautious to prevent splattering when adding them to the oil.

3. Heat the rapeseed oil over high heat until it smokes heavily, then turn off the heat. First, add the ginger slices, stirring constantly to help cool the oil. Fry until the ginger turns golden brown.

4. Reduce to medium-low heat and slowly add the remaining vegetables, being careful to avoid hot oil splatters. Fry patiently until all ingredients are golden brown and their fragrance is fully infused into the oil. Do not burn them.

5. Use a slotted spoon and fine mesh strainer to remove all the fried solids, draining the excess oil back into the pot. Turn the heat back to high and bring the oil to a smoke point, then turn off the heat. Let it sit for 2 minutes to allow the temperature to drop to about 200°C.

6. First, add the white sesame seeds and stir for 30 seconds until they float and are not burnt. Evenly sprinkle in 30g of the spice powder and stir for 30 seconds. Then add the coarse chili flakes and stir well. Finally, add the fine chili powder and stir until combined.

7. Immediately pour the fragrant vinegar along the edge of the pot, then pour the baijiu into the center. **Note: Do not stir after adding** to prevent hot oil splatters. Wait until the oil surface calms before stirring evenly.

8. Pour the chili oil into a lidded wooden or ceramic container. Tightly seal the lid and let it sit at room temperature for at least 24 hours to allow the flavors to fully meld. The finished red oil is used for cold noodles, salads, etc. The chili sediment at the bottom contains spice powder and may taste slightly bitter when eaten alone.

## Health Advice  
This chili oil is fragrant but not overly spicy, making it suitable for those who enjoy aroma but avoid heat. Hypertensive patients should be mindful of oil and salt intake.
