# Salt and Pepper Corn
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

🍽️ **Ingredients**
🌽 Corn kernels (bagged) 350g

🧂 **Seasonings**
🧂 Salt and pepper powder 10g
✨ Sesame seeds 10g
🌾 Starch 40-70g (enough to fully coat the corn kernels)
🫒 Oil (as needed)

🔧 **Utensils**
🍳 Wok
🧺 Plastic sieve x2
🧻 Paper towels (several pieces)

## Instructions
1. Thaw the corn kernels, either by soaking in warm water for 15 minutes or boiling in water for 5 minutes.
2. Take one sieve, line it with paper towels, and pour in the thawed corn kernels.
3. Shake the sieve to allow the paper towels to fully absorb the water until completely wet.
4. Take the second sieve, line it with paper towels, and pour all the corn kernels from the first sieve into it.
5. Continue shaking until the paper towels are completely wet.
6. Repeat replacing the paper towels and shaking until there are no visible water droplets on the corn surface, but it remains moist.
7. Add enough starch to fully cover the corn kernels.
8. Shake the sieve to evenly coat each corn kernel with starch.
9. Heat the wok and add an appropriate amount of oil, enough to just cover the bottom of the wok, not too much.
10. When the oil temperature reaches about 80% hot, pour in the starch-coated corn kernels.
11. Fry over medium heat for 30 seconds without stirring to prevent the starch from falling off.
12. Then gently stir-fry for about 3 minutes, and remove from the wok.
13. Finally, sprinkle with 3g of salt and pepper powder and sesame seeds, and serve.

## Health Tips
This salt and pepper corn is coated with starch and fried, making it relatively high in calories. However, corn is rich in dietary fiber, so it can be enjoyed occasionally. Pay attention to controlling the amount of oil and salt, and pair it with light vegetables for a more balanced meal. For those on a weight loss diet, it is recommended to reduce the oil or use an air fryer instead.
