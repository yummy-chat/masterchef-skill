# Improved Spicy Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 Small Bamboo Chicken (about 2-3 catties, cut into 3-4 cm pieces, repeatedly rinsed until water runs clear)
🌶️ 200 g Dried Red Chili Pepper Segments
🫒 30 g Sichuan Peppercorns
🧄 30 g Minced Garlic
🌾 30 g Dried Mung Bean Flour
🌾 20 g All-Purpose Flour
🫘 White Sesame Seeds, to taste
🧂 3 g MSG
🧂 2 g Salt
🌿 0.5 g White Pepper
🍶 5 g Yellow Wine
🥄 Fresh Soy Sauce (or Premium Soy Sauce), to taste (about 3 g for marinating, a little for drizzling before finishing)
🫗 About 18 g Sesame Oil (3 g for marinating, a small amount for handling dried chilies, about 15 g mixed with vegetable oil for stir-frying)
🧂 2 g Five-Spice Powder
🥃 About 10 ml Baijiu (Chinese Liquor)
🫒 About 15 g Vegetable Oil (base oil, mixed with sesame oil for stir-frying)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🔪 Chef’s Knife and Cutting Board
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🔥 Stove

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the Chicken**: Wash the whole chicken, cut into uniform pieces, and rinse thoroughly under running water until the blood is completely removed. Drain well.
- **Key Point**: Thoroughly rinsing off blood is crucial for removing any gamey odor.
2. **Marinate**: Add salt, MSG, white pepper, yellow wine, a small amount of fresh soy sauce, sesame oil, and five-spice powder to the chicken pieces. Mix well with your hands until the liquid is absorbed.
- **Key Point**: Sesame oil helps lock in moisture; five-spice powder adds complex aroma.
3. **Coat and Seal with Oil**: Add the dried mung bean flour and all-purpose flour. Toss until the chicken pieces are evenly coated with a thin batter. Finally, drizzle in a little oil and mix well.
- **Key Point**: Mung bean flour creates extra crispiness; all-purpose flour improves adherence; sealing with oil makes the crust crispier after frying.
4. **Prepare the Dried Chilies**: Place the dried chili segments in a bowl. Spray with baijiu and mix well, then mix in sesame oil. Prepare the minced garlic separately.
- **Key Point**: Baijiu and sesame oil prevent the chilies from burning during stir-frying and add a base flavor.
5. **First Fry for Setting**: Heat oil to 170°C (340°F). Carefully add the chicken pieces one by one. Fry over medium heat for about 3-4 minutes until the chicken floats, the bubbling subsides, and the pieces turn light yellow. Remove.
- **Key Point**: Do not let the oil get too hot; add pieces one by one to prevent sticking; this step is for cooking through and setting.
6. **Second Fry for Crispiness**: Heat the oil to 190°C (375°F). Return all the chicken pieces to the wok. Fry over high heat for 30 seconds to 1 minute until golden brown and the crust is crispy. Remove and drain on a slotted spoon.
- **Key Point**: The second frying makes the crust crispy while keeping the inside tender.
7. **Stir-fry the Aromatics**: Leave a base oil in the wok. Over low heat, first stir-fry the Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Then add the prepared dried chili segments. Stir-fry over medium heat until their color darkens and the aroma is rich.
- **Key Point**: Control the heat throughout to avoid burning, which would make it bitter.
8. **Combine and Season**: Add the fried chicken pieces and minced garlic to the wok. Quickly stir-fry to combine evenly. Drizzle fresh soy sauce along the side of the wok, then stir-fry a few more times.
- **Key Point**: Add the garlic last to preserve its fragrance; pouring soy sauce along the wok edge enhances its aroma.
9. **Finishing Touch**: Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in white sesame seeds. Toss well using the residual heat. Transfer to a serving plate.
- **Key Point**: White sesame seeds add aroma and a visual highlight.

## Health Advice
This spicy chicken dish uses mung bean flour and sesame oil to create a crispy crust, making it suitable for occasional indulgence. However, the deep-frying and large amount of chilies can be heavy on the digestive system, so it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balance. Chicken is rich in high-quality protein, making it suitable for muscle building, but portion control is advised.
