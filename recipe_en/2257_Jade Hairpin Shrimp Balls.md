# Jade Hairpin Shrimp Balls
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Large shrimp 5 (about 500g, live and large-sized preferred)
🥬 Choy sum as needed (use the stem part as the "jade hairpin")
🥩 Yunnan Xuanwei ham/Jinhua ham as needed (for added savory and salty flavor)
🧂 Salt a pinch (for marinating shrimp balls)
🍬 Sugar a pinch (for marinating shrimp balls)
🌽 Corn starch as needed (for coating ham and preparing sauce)
🥚 Egg yolk as needed (for coating ham)
🍶 Shaoxing wine a splash (for aroma)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
✂️ Scissors (for cutting shrimp heads and opening backs)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board (for cutting ham and poking holes in shrimp)
🍳 Wok (for deep-frying ham, stir-frying shrimp balls, and final tossing)
🪆 Plate (for holding ingredients and plating)
🥣 Bowl (for preparing sauce)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the shrimp**: Cut off the shrimp heads, peel the shells leaving the tails on, and make a cut along the back. Use the knife tip to poke a hole about 2 cm from the tail end of the shrimp body. Thread the tail back through this hole, then insert a piece of choy sum stem to form a "jade hairpin" shape.
2. **Marinate the shrimp balls**: Sprinkle a pinch of salt and sugar over the shaped shrimp balls, and gently mix. The shrimp itself is light in flavor, so salt and sugar provide a basic seasoning.
3. **Prepare the ham**: Cut the ham into small cubes. Coat each cube first with egg yolk, then with corn starch.
4. **Deep-fry the ham cubes**: Heat oil to 120°C (250°F), and deep-fry over low heat for about 1 minute until the surface turns golden brown. Remove.
**Key point**: Fry slowly over low heat to set the shape and develop aroma.
5. **Plate**: Slice the fried ham cubes and arrange them in a ring around the edge of a plate.
6. **Stir-fry the shrimp balls**: Heat oil in the wok to 120°C (250°F). Add the shrimp balls and cook for about 1 minute until they change color and set. Remove.
**Key point**: Use the "oil poaching" or "oil bath" method to quickly cook and set the shrimp.
7. **Prepare and cook the sauce**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Pour in the sauce mixture made from Shaoxing wine, salt, sugar, corn starch, and water. Cook until slightly thickened.
8. **Combine and toss**: Add the poached shrimp balls to the sauce in the wok. Stir-fry over high heat quickly to coat the shrimp evenly with the sauce. Finally, drizzle a splash of Shaoxing wine around the edge of the wok, toss well, and remove from heat immediately.
9. **Plate**: Place the cooked shrimp balls in the center of the plate lined with ham slices.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. Shrimp is rich in high-quality protein and zinc, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those managing fat intake. Note that ham is high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
