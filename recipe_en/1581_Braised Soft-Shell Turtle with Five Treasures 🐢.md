Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Instructions  
1. Soak the dried ingredients: Place the red dates, longan, and goji berries in a bowl, add an appropriate amount of water, and soak until softened.  
2. Cook the pigeon eggs: Place the pigeon eggs in cold water, cook over low heat for about 10 minutes. Remove, rinse with cold water, peel, and set aside.  
   **Note**: Starting with cold water and cooking slowly over low heat helps prevent the eggshells from cracking.  
3. Prepare the soft-shell turtle:  
   - Thoroughly remove all yellow fat from the turtle's inner cavity. Farmed turtle fat has a strong, unpleasant smell; thorough removal is the key to reducing the gamey taste.  
   - Cut off the turtle's tail, scrape off the yellow film on the skin, and remove the gills from the head.  
   - Blanch in hot water at about 60°C for roughly one minute. Once the skin turns white and wrinkled, remove. Peel off the thin skin from the skirt, limbs, and underside. If the water is too hot, the skin will become tough and cannot be removed; if too cold, it won't come off easily.  
   - Use steel wool to scrub the turtle's shell vigorously, removing the top layer of skin until it is clean and white. This eliminates any grit and earthy smell, ensuring a clear broth.  
   - Cut the prepared turtle into evenly sized pieces, and remove the tips of the claws.  
   (First, scrape clean the yellow oil inside the turtle's belly; farmed turtle fat is very greasy, it won't do if you don't clean it thoroughly.  
   Chop off the tail, scrape off the yellow film on the skin, and remove the gills.  
   Throw it into 60°C hot water and blanch for a minute. Once the skin turns white and wrinkled, take it out. Peel off the thin skin from the skirt, legs, and belly. If the water is too hot, the skin will be cooked tough and won't peel; if too cold, it won't peel off.  
   Use a steel wool ball to scrub the turtle shell hard, removing that top layer of skin to reveal the white shell. This way, there won't be any sandy or earthy smell, and the soup will be clear.  
   Chop the cleaned turtle into evenly sized chunks, and cut off the tips of the claws.)  
4. Prepare the bamboo shoot: Use a melon baller to scoop the green bamboo shoot into small balls.  
5. Assemble and stew: Place the turtle pieces, red dates, longan, goji berries, and the cooked pigeon eggs into a stewing pot. Add enough water, arrange the ingredients neatly. The goji berries can be added last to preserve their color.  
   **Key point**: Steam over water or simmer slowly over low heat to ensure a clear broth and rich flavor. The total stewing time is usually about 1 to 1.5 hours.  
   (Put the turtle chunks, red dates, longan, goji berries, and cooked pigeon eggs all into the stewing pot. Fill it up with water, arrange everything neatly. The goji berries can be added at the end to prevent their color from fading.  
   It needs to be steamed over water or simmered slowly over low heat so that the soup will be clear and the flavor will be rich. Generally, it needs to be stewed for one to one and a half hours.)  
6. Finish: Stew until the broth is clear, the turtle meat is tender, and the flavors of the herbs and eggs have infused into the soup. The soup is sweet and delicious, and the turtle meat is soft and glutinous.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. The soft-shell turtle is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those needing nourishment or with dry skin. However, individuals with high uric acid or gout should consume it cautiously, as turtle meat has a high purine content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables for balanced nutrition.
