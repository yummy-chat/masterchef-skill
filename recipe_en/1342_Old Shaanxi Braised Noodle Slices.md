# Old Shaanxi Braised Noodle Slices
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Medium or high-gluten flour 2 jin
🧂 Salt to taste (6g for kneading, adjust for seasoning)
💧 Water 460g
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed (for brushing dough pieces and resting)
🍅 Tomatoes as needed (cut into pieces)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms as needed
🥬 Cabbage as needed
🫘 Fried tofu pieces as needed
🥬 Leafy greens (such as bok choy) as needed
🥚 Eggs as needed (pre-scrambled)
🧅 Scallions as needed (minced for sautéing, plus some for garnish)
🫚 Ginger as needed (minced for sautéing)
🧂 Chicken bouillon as needed
🌿 Thirteen-spice blend as needed
🌶️ Oil-poured chili as needed

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel mixing bowl (for kneading dough)
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🥖 Rolling pin
🍽️ Stainless steel tray
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🖌️ Brush
🥄 Ladle

## Steps
1. **Knead the dough**: In a bowl, mix flour with 6g salt. Gradually add water in batches, stirring until it becomes flaky, then knead into a smooth dough.
**Key point**: Add water in batches. Knead until the bowl is clean, hands are clean, and dough is smooth.

2. **Shape the dough pieces**: Roll the dough into a long log, brush the surface with oil, tear into evenly sized small pieces, then roll each piece into a "dough piece" about 15 cm long and of even thickness.
**Key point**: Brush oil evenly to prevent sticking.

3. **Rest the dough**: Arrange the rolled dough pieces neatly in a tray, brush the surface evenly with oil, seal tightly with plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 40 minutes.
**Note**: Must be sealed to prevent the surface from drying out.

4. **Make the noodle slices**: Flatten the rested dough pieces with your palm, then roll out into slices that are slightly thicker in the middle, thinner at the edges, and of even width. Hold both ends with your hands, stretch and shake them up and down to elongate, then quickly tear into evenly sized and evenly thick noodle slices.
**Key point**: Shake with even force so the slices have consistent thickness.

5. **Boil the noodle slices**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the noodle slices, cook until about 80% done, then drain.
**Key point**: Do not cook fully, as they will continue to heat during the braising step.

6. **Stir-fry the vegetables**: Heat oil in a wok, sauté minced scallions and ginger until fragrant, add tomato pieces and stir-fry until soft and juicy. Add shiitake mushrooms, cabbage, and fried tofu pieces, stir-fry quickly over high heat. Once the vegetables release water, season with salt, chicken bouillon, and thirteen-spice blend, and stir-fry evenly.
**Key point**: Let the tomatoes release their red oil for a richer broth.

7. **Braise**: Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok and bring to a boil. Add the boiled noodle slices. Once boiling again, add the leafy greens and the pre-scrambled eggs. Cook until all ingredients are done, then stir in the oil-poured chili.
**Key point**: Add leafy greens and eggs last to preserve texture; add oil-poured chili just before serving to retain its aroma.

8. **Serve**: Ladle into bowls and sprinkle with chopped scallions for garnish.

## Health Advice
This noodle dish is rich in carbohydrates, vegetables, and protein, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing quick energy replenishment. It is recommended to control the amount of noodles and pair with plenty of vegetables; those with high blood pressure should reduce salt and chicken bouillon intake.
