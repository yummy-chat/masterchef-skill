# Gansu-style Spicy Hot Pot (Malatang)
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes, as needed (sliced thick)
🥒 Lotus root, as needed (sliced thick)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms, as needed (stemmed, sliced thick)
🥬 Baby bok choy or choy sum, as needed (roots removed, washed)
🍜 Dingxi potato starch noodles, as needed (soaked for 4 hours in advance)
🍜 Instant noodles, 1 piece per person
🧆 Radish balls, as needed (optional)
🌿 Cilantro, 3 stalks (washed and cut into sections)
🧅 Scallions, 5 stalks (washed and cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, as needed (mashed into a paste)
🌶️ Gangu chili flakes, coarse and fine, about 150g each
🫒 Rapeseed oil, about 300ml
🫘 Crushed peanuts, as needed (made by frying and crushing raw peanuts)
🤍 White sesame seeds, as needed
🧂 Five-spice powder, 2 pinches
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder, 2 pinches (part for the chili oil, part for sprinkling at the end)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍶 Baijiu or rice wine, 2 tablespoons
🍶 Vinegar, 2 tablespoons
🧂 Chicken bouillon or MSG, 1 pinch
🫕 Hot pot base, 1 piece
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste), to taste
🫘 White cardamom, about 8 pods
🌿 Bay leaves, 3-4 pieces
🫘 Cumin seeds, 3 pinches
⭐ Star anise, 3 pieces
🫘 Black cardamom, 3 pieces

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Deep pot or soup pot
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🧄 Food processor or mortar and pestle
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Ladle or spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Trim the tough stems and yellow leaves from the greens, then rinse thoroughly. Peel and wash the potatoes and lotus root. Remove the mushroom stems and wash the caps.
**Safety Tip**: When cutting hard vegetables like lotus root or potatoes, hold them steady with your left hand, keeping your fingers curled inward for safety.

2. Cut the lotus root, potatoes, and mushrooms into thick slices to ensure a soft and tender texture after cooking.
3. Make crushed peanuts: Add a little oil to a wok over medium heat. Add the raw peanuts and shake the wok constantly. Once the peanuts darken in color and their skins begin to crack, turn off the heat and remove them. Let cool, then crush and set aside.
4. Prepare the chili base: In a stainless steel bowl, mix the coarse and fine Gangu chili flakes, white sesame seeds, crushed peanuts, five-spice powder, part of the Sichuan peppercorn powder, and salt. Add the baijiu or rice wine and stir to moisten the chili flakes, preventing them from burning when hot oil is added later.
5. Infuse the oil: Heat about 300ml of rapeseed oil in a wok. Test the oil temperature by adding a piece of vegetable leaf—if it bubbles, add the thoroughly dried cilantro and scallions. Then add the white cardamom, cumin seeds, star anise, and bay leaves. Fry over low heat until the vegetables and spices become dry and withered. Use a slotted spoon to remove and discard them, reserving the infused oil.
**Note**: Ensure the cilantro and scallions are washed and completely dried to prevent oil splatter.

6. Make the chili oil: Let the infused oil cool for 2 minutes. First, ladle 2 spoonfuls of warm oil into the chili flake mixture, stirring constantly. Then, heat the remaining oil over high heat until it smokes. Turn off the heat and pour the hot oil into the chili mixture in batches, stirring quickly. While still hot, add the vinegar and chicken bouillon or MSG, stir well, and set aside.
7. Prepare the soup base: In another pot, heat a little oil and stir-fry the hot pot base and Pixian doubanjiang until the oil turns red. Add about 1500ml of water. Once boiling, add the black cardamom, white cardamom, cumin seeds, bay leaves, and star anise. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 10 minutes. Strain out all the solid spices, leaving the red soup base.
8. Cook the ingredients: Bring the soup base to a boil. Add the ingredients in order, starting with those that take longer to cook. The pre-soaked Dingxi potato starch noodles need about 5 minutes of cooking.
9. Assemble: Remove the cooked ingredients with a slotted spoon and place them into a serving bowl. Sprinkle with Sichuan peppercorn powder, chopped scallions, cilantro, and garlic paste. Finally, generously ladle the prepared "soul chili oil" over the top. Mix well and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish contains starchy ingredients like potatoes, starch noodles, and instant noodles. It is recommended to pair it with more vegetables and to reduce the portion of these staples when serving as a main meal. Be mindful of the high oil and salt content; enjoy in moderation.
