# Seaweed Shrimp Paste Pancake 🥞
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍘 1 large piece of seaweed (used as the pancake base)  
🍤 About 1 pack of shrimp paste  
🥕 Half a carrot (diced)  
🌽 Corn kernels (as needed)  
🥚 1 egg (beaten into egg wash)  
🍅 Dipping sauce (optional): Ketchup, salad dressing, or mayonnaise  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
💧 Spray bottle  
🔗 Tongs  
🔪 Cutting board  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Lay the whole piece of seaweed flat on the countertop, evenly spread a layer of shrimp paste, then sprinkle diced carrots and corn kernels on top.  
   **Key point**: The shrimp paste should cover the entire seaweed, and the vegetable pieces should be evenly distributed for better shaping.  
2. Thoroughly beat the egg into egg wash, then evenly pour it over the seaweed topped with ingredients.  
   **Key point**: The egg wash helps bind the layers together and gives a golden color.  
3. Spray a little oil into the pan and heat over medium-low heat. Hold the seaweed with both hands, gently place it into the pan with the seaweed side facing down.  
   **Note**: Be gentle to prevent the ingredients from shifting.  
4. Fry until the bottom seaweed becomes crispy and the edges are set and firm, then flip using tongs or with the help of a plate.  
   **Doneness check**: The bottom seaweed turns dark, and the edges of the shrimp paste turn white.  
5. Continue frying the other side over medium-low heat until the shrimp paste is fully cooked to a pinkish-white color, the egg wash is set, and the pancake no longer sticks when you shake the pan.  
6. Transfer the cooked pancake to a cutting board and use kitchen scissors to cut it into bite-sized pieces. Plate it.  
   **Key point**: Using scissors to cut keeps the edges neat and prevents crushing the crispy seaweed base.  
7. Serve with ketchup, salad dressing, or mayonnaise for dipping, according to personal preference.  

## Health Tips  
This seaweed shrimp paste pancake is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for children needing nutritional supplements, fitness enthusiasts, or those managing their sugar intake. Use medium-low heat and control the amount of oil when frying to avoid excessive calories. Seaweed has a cooling nature, so those with a weak spleen or stomach should not consume it in excess.
