# Innovative Sweet and Sour Pork Ribs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 1 slab of lean pork ribs (preferably with less meat)
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (sliced)
🍋 1 lemon (sliced and deseeded)
🍂 Dried hawthorn,适量
🧅 2 scallions (cut into sections)
🍬 Rock sugar,适量 (used in two batches: once for caramelizing, once for seasoning during cooking)
🧂 Cooking wine,适量 (for blanching to remove odor)
🫗 5 grams of Baoning vinegar (can be reduced as needed)
🧂 A pinch of salt

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪓 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Large soup spoon
🥣 Strainer spoon
🫙 Strainer net
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
1. Separate the ribs one by one, chop them into evenly sized pieces; slice the ginger; slice the lemon and remove the seeds; set aside the dried hawthorn; cut the scallions into sections; crush the rock sugar with the back of a knife.
2. Fill the wok with water, add the ribs, bring to a boil over high heat, add cooking wine, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium heat and blanch for 10 minutes. Remove and drain with a strainer net.  
   **Purpose**: To loosen the meat texture, making it easier to separate from the bone.
3. Heat the wok, add sufficient cooking oil. When the oil reaches 60% heat, add the ribs and deep-fry over medium heat until the surface turns slightly yellow and tightens. Remove and drain the oil.  
   **Purpose**: To tighten the surface meat and enhance the flavor.
4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, add the crushed rock sugar and a small amount of water. Heat over low heat to melt the sugar. When the syrup turns to a coffee-like color, remove from heat. Use the residual heat of the iron wok to stir-fry the caramel until it reaches a jujube red color. Then pour in prepared boiling water along the edge of the wok and stir well.  
   **Note**: Removing from heat prevents the caramel from burning and turning bitter.
5. Pour the fried ribs into the caramel water. Add ginger slices, lemon slices, and dried hawthorn. Stir well. Add enough water to submerge the ribs. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
6. During the simmering process, add an appropriate amount of rock sugar and 5 grams of Baoning vinegar. After 10 minutes of simmering, add a pinch of salt, then add the scallion sections.  
   **Key Point**: The amount of vinegar can be slightly reduced to avoid conflict with the lemon’s sourness; adding salt aims to enhance the richness of the sweet and sour flavor, not to increase salinity.
7. After 20 minutes of simmering, remove the lemon slices and scallion sections. Reduce to low heat and continue reducing the sauce for about 3 minutes until it thickens. Stir well and remove from the wok.  
   **Key Point**: Always use low heat when reducing the sauce to prevent burning and darkening.

## Health Tips
This innovative sweet and sour pork ribs uses lemon and hawthorn to replace part of the vinegar, resulting in a milder sourness that aids digestion, making it suitable for those who prefer a refreshing taste. The rock sugar is added in two batches, so the total amount should be controlled to avoid excess calories. Those with high blood sugar are advised to consume sparingly.
