# Char Siu (Chinese BBQ Pork) Made in a Rice Cooker  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍖 Pork collar butt (梅花肉) - appropriate amount (this cut is essential, located on the upper shoulder of the pig, with even intramuscular fat distribution)  
🧅 Dried shallots - appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger - appropriate amount  
🧄 Garlic - appropriate amount  
🌿 Scallions - appropriate amount  
🌿 Cilantro - appropriate amount  
🥬 Lettuce - appropriate amount (for plate decoration)  

**Marinade Seasonings:**  
🍶 Hua diao wine (花雕酒) - appropriate amount  
🧂 Dark soy sauce - appropriate amount (for color)  
🧂 Salt - appropriate amount (to balance flavors)  
🧂 Chicken powder - appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar - appropriate amount  
🫘 Red fermented bean curd (红腐乳) - 1 piece (provides flavor and color)  
🦪 Oyster sauce - appropriate amount  
🌿 Sand ginger powder (沙姜粉) - appropriate amount (essential for char siu, do not omit)  
🌿 Five-spice powder - appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce - appropriate amount  
🍯 Honey - appropriate amount (for drizzling after plating)  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing marinade)  
🥣 Glass square dish (for marinating)  
🍚 Rice cooker (core cooking equipment)  
🍽️ Serving bowl  

## Steps  
1. Cut the pork collar butt into slices about 1 cm thick.  
**Key point:** Must use pork collar butt; its even intramuscular fat distribution ensures the texture is not dry or tough.  

2. Rinse the cut meat slices with clean water, draining off blood and impurities.  

3. Prepare the aromatic base: Finely chop the dried shallots, ginger, and garlic; tie the scallions and cilantro into bundles.  

4. Prepare the char siu marinade: In a large bowl, add in order the chopped dried shallots, chopped ginger, chopped garlic, hua diao wine, dark soy sauce, salt, chicken powder, white sugar, red fermented bean curd, oyster sauce, sand ginger powder, five-spice powder, and light soy sauce. Mix well.  
**Key point:** Sand ginger powder is an important source of char siu flavor; do not omit. Red fermented bean curd enhances color and aroma.  

5. Place the cleaned meat slices into the glass square dish, pour in the prepared marinade, and add the tied scallion and cilantro bundles. Use your hands to mix everything evenly, ensuring each slice is coated with the marinade.  

6. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate to marinate.  
**Key point:** Marinating a day in advance is recommended; the longer it marinates, the more flavorful it becomes. The minimum marinating time is 1 hour.  

7. Pour the marinated meat slices along with some of the marinade into the rice cooker inner pot.  
**Key point:** No additional water is needed; use the marinade and the meat's own moisture for cooking.  

8. Start the rice cooker on the "Cook Rice" or "Steam" mode, and cook for about 30 minutes.  
   **Key point:** The meat needs to be cooked twice in total, for about 60 minutes.  

9. After the first 30 minutes, open the lid, turn the meat slices over to ensure even coloring, and let the meat absorb the sauce while the sauce slightly reduces.  

10. Start the second cooking cycle for about 30 minutes, until the sauce becomes thick and evenly coats the surface of the meat slices.  
**Target state:** The sauce should reduce to a thick consistency, fully coating the meat slices.  

11. Remove the cooked char siu, let it cool slightly, and slice it into thick pieces using a diagonal cutting method.  
**Key point:** Diagonal slicing better showcases the meat's grain and enhances visual appeal.  

12. Plating: Place a portion of rice into a bowl, arrange blanched greens on the side, lay out the sliced char siu, and finally drizzle with a little honey.  
**Key point:** The final drizzle of honey adds gloss and a layer of flavor to the char siu.  

## Health Advice  
Pork collar butt provides high-quality protein. When slow-cooked in a rice cooker, it becomes tender and easy to digest. However, the marinade has a relatively high salt content, so portion control is recommended. This dish is suitable for those needing a protein boost and seeking a convenient cooking method.
