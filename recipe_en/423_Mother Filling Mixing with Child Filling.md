# Mother Filling Mixing with Child Filling
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ground pork (or ground beef/lamb) 500g
🥬 Chinese chives (child filling, use as needed)
🧅 Green onion (child filling, use as needed; avoid prolonged storage to prevent water release and off-flavor)
🥬 Celery (child filling, use as needed)
🧂 Salt 7g
🧂 Chicken powder 5g
🌶️ White pepper powder 2g
🍬 Sugar 2g
🥃 Premium soy sauce 30g
🧅 Green onion 15g (sliced, for scallion-ginger water)
🫚 Ginger 15g (sliced, for scallion-ginger water)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 3g
💧 Water 300g (includes spices; about 200g will actually be mixed into the meat filling)
🫒 Spiced oil (fragrant oil) as needed (pre-made in advance, to seal moisture and enhance aroma)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl (for mixing meat filling)
🥣 Small bowl (for holding vegetables, preparing scallion-ginger water, and holding spiced oil)
🧤 Disposable gloves (for mixing the meat filling by hand)

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare the mother filling (ground meat) and the chopped child filling vegetables. The meat filling is the flavor base, while vegetables provide the source of flavor.
2. Prepare scallion-ginger-pepper water: Place sliced green onion, sliced ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns in water. Squeeze and rub with your hands to release aroma, creating a liquid mixture for removing gamey smell and subsequent water addition.
3. Pre-make spiced oil: Slowly fry spices over low heat to create flavored oil. Set aside.
4. Season the mother filling: Add salt, chicken powder, white pepper powder, sugar, and premium soy sauce to the meat filling. Mix evenly by hand to give the meat filling a basic flavor base. **Note**: The flavor should not be too strong; consider the inherent taste of the vegetables to be added later.
5. Add water to the mother filling: Gradually pour the prepared scallion-ginger-pepper water into the meat filling in batches. Stir until the water is fully absorbed each time, continuing until the meat filling is tender, smooth, and elastic.
6. Seal with oil in the mother filling: Drizzle in the pre-made spiced oil and mix evenly. The spiced oil seals in moisture, makes the texture more succulent, and adds layered fragrance.
7. Mix the mother and child fillings: Combine the seasoned mother filling with the vegetable child filling as needed. A common household ratio is 1:1. **Key Point**: Add the vegetables as you wrap the dumplings; do not mix all at once and let it sit for a long time. Especially green onions, which can release water and produce off-flavors when left for too long, affecting the overall taste.

## Health Suggestions
It is recommended to serve with whole wheat dough wrappers to increase dietary fiber and reduce blood sugar spikes. Pay attention to salt control; those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of soy sauce. Chinese chives and celery are rich in vitamins and pair well with lean meat filling to reduce total fat content.
