# Steamed Rice Mooncake
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍚 Rice flour 126g
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 54g
🍬 Powdered sugar 18g
💧 Water 93g (add gradually)
🍓 Hawthorn filling 10g (or low-sugar ready-made filling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Whisk/egg beater
🧽 Coarse sieve
🧁 Mooncake mold (50g)
📄 Baking paper
🍴 Scraper
♨️ Steamer

## Steps
1. Place the rice flour, glutinous rice flour, and powdered sugar into a bowl. Use a whisk to mix thoroughly and evenly.
2. Add water little by little while continuously rubbing the mixture by hand, until the flour can be lightly squeezed into a ball and easily crumbled when pinched.
3. Sift the mixed flour through a coarse sieve to achieve a softer texture.
4. Take a 50g mooncake mold and lightly coat the inner surface with a small amount of rice flour to prevent sticking.
5. First, fill the mold about halfway with the sifted flour. Place the hawthorn filling in the center, then fill the mold to the top with the remaining flour, covering the filling and pressing it down firmly.
6. Place a piece of baking paper under the mold, invert it onto a flat surface, and gently press the handle to release the shaped mooncake.
   **Note**: Do not press too hard to avoid a dense texture; for clearer patterns, pre-coat the mold's inner surface with rice flour.
7. Place the shaped rice mooncakes into a steamer. Steam for 18 minutes after the water starts to boil, until the surface is dry and set without collapsing.

## Health Tips
This steamed rice mooncake uses rice flour to replace a high-oil and high-sugar crust, reducing the calorie load. It is suitable for those who want to satisfy cravings without gaining weight. The hawthorn filling is tangy and appetizing, but be careful not to use too much filling to avoid overflow during steaming.
