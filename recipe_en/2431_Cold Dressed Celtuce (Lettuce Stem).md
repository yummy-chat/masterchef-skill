# Cold Dressed Celtuce (Lettuce Stem)
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 1 celtuce (lettuce stem)
🥕 1 carrot (optional)
🌶️ 2-3 small chili peppers (bird’s eye chili)
🫚 1 small piece of ginger
🧄 3-4 cloves of garlic
🧂 Salt, to taste
🫗 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Strainer or sieve
🔥 Gas stove
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. Peel the celtuce and cut into small strips; cut the carrot into strips as well. Place both in a large bowl, add salt, and mix well. Let sit for 10 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: Peel the celtuce generously to remove tough fibers, ensuring a crunchy texture.

2. After resting, rinse the celtuce 1-2 times with water, then drain off excess moisture.

3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the celtuce strips for 1 minute, then immediately drain and transfer to a large bowl.  
   **Key Point**: Do not blanch for too long, or the crunchy texture will be lost; the carrot can be blanched together or left raw.

4. In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add ginger slices, garlic cloves, and small chili pepper segments, and stir-fry for about 30-45 seconds until fragrant. Pour the oil and aromatics over the celtuce in the bowl.

5. Thoroughly mix the celtuce with the fragrant oil and aromatics, then plate and serve.  
   **Note**: After plating, you may add extra seasonings to taste, such as light soy sauce, white sugar, rice vinegar, or sesame seeds; the dish tastes even better when chilled.

## Health Tips
Cold dressed celtuce is low in fat and high in fiber, rich in vitamins and water, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or to stimulate the appetite in summer. Blanching helps reduce oxalic acid, but avoid prolonged cooking to maintain the crunchy texture. The fragrant chili oil at the end is key to the flavor, but use it in moderation.
