# Secret Yangchun Noodles
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Soybean oil, as needed (for starting the pan)  
🐖 Lard, as needed (generous amount, core of the aroma)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (finely minced)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (finely minced)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (sliced into green onion flowers)  
🧅 Onion, as needed (sliced into shreds)  
🦐 Shrimp roe, as needed (stir-fried to enhance umami, must be fried to remove fishy smell)  
💧 Water, about 4 jin (for making the broth)  
🧄 Garlic sprouts, as needed (finely chopped into garlic sprout flowers, placed at the bottom of the bowl to enhance freshness)  
🍜 Alkaline noodles or thin egg noodles, as needed (Yangzhou local alkaline noodles are preferred)  

🧂 Soy sauce, as needed (drizzled in after stir-frying the base ingredients and before adding water)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed (added after the broth comes to a boil)  
🧂 MSG, as needed (added after the broth comes to a boil)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (added after the broth comes to a boil)  
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed (generous amount, provides the main spicy flavor)  
🌶️ Black pepper powder, as needed (complements white pepper, adds aroma)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Iron ladle  
🥣 Small bowl  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🔥 Stove  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps
1. Prepare all ingredients in advance: mince ginger and garlic, slice scallions into green onion flowers, slice onion into shreds, and chop garlic sprouts into garlic sprout flowers. Set aside.
2. Heat the wok over medium heat, pour in soybean oil and a generous amount of lard, and melt them together.
**Key point**: Use a large amount of lard; it is the core of the aroma.
3. Add the minced ginger, minced garlic, scallion flowers, onion shreds, and shrimp roe. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
**State check**: All ingredients are fragrant, and the fishy smell of the shrimp roe is eliminated.
   **Key point**: The shrimp roe must be stir-fried to remove the fishy smell to release its umami.
4. Drizzle soy sauce along the edge of the wok, stir-fry briefly to enhance color and aroma.
5. Add about 4 jin of water to the wok, turn the heat to high, and bring to a boil.
6. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, add chicken bouillon, MSG, salt, white pepper powder, and black pepper powder. Stir well.
**Key point**: Use a generous amount of white pepper powder; it is the main source of flavor. Combining white and black pepper creates richer layers.
   **Note**: After the broth boils, avoid boiling it over high heat to prevent the broth from becoming cloudy.
7. Turn off the heat, let the broth steep for about half an hour. Then, use a fine mesh strainer to filter out all solid residues from the broth, resulting in a clear broth. Set aside.
**Key point**: Filtering is essential to achieve a clear and refined broth.
8. Prepare serving bowls. Place an appropriate amount of garlic sprout flowers at the bottom of each bowl. Ladle in the hot, filtered broth, ensuring some oil is included.
**Key point**: The garlic sprout flowers are infused with freshness by the hot broth; the broth must contain oil.
9. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook for about 3 minutes until they float to the surface and have no white core. Drain thoroughly.
10. Place the drained noodles into the bowls with the broth and serve.
**Key point**: The noodles must be thoroughly drained before adding to the broth to keep the broth clear.

## Health Tips
It is recommended to serve with a small amount of leafy greens or a poached egg to increase dietary fiber and protein intake. Lard is used generously; people with high blood lipids should control their consumption. Yangchun noodles have a fragrant and savory broth, especially suitable for warming the stomach in cold weather.
