## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the string from the hairy crabs, lift off the shell. Be careful when handling live crabs.
2. Butterfly each Thai white shrimp for better flavor absorption.
3. Heat the clay pot, add oil, stir-fry ginger shreds until fragrant, then add about 20% of the crabs and shrimp, stir-fry until aromatic.  
**Key point**: Fry the seafood with ginger first to release aroma; do not add water directly—this is crucial for a flavorful broth.
4. After stir-frying, pour in about 3 kg of water, add a little peanut butter, and bring to a boil.  
**Key point**: Peanut butter is the secret to enhancing flavor.
5. Once the water boils, add the rice. Keep the heat high. Cover the pot partially to prevent overflow. Cook until the rice grains bloom and the congee thickens, about 20 minutes.
6. When the congee base is nearly done, add the remaining crabs and shrimp to the pot.  
**Key point**: Add seafood in two batches—first for deep broth flavor, second to keep the meat tender.
7. Keep boiling on high heat, season with salt, chicken powder, and freshly ground white pepper. Just before turning off the heat, add cilantro and chopped scallions, drizzle with carrot chicken fat, stir well, and serve.  
**Key point**: Carrot chicken fat gives the congee a golden color and enhances aroma and taste.

## Health Tips
This Chaoshan seafood clay pot congee is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for autumn nourishment. Gout patients should limit seafood intake. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables for a balanced diet, and reduce salt and chicken powder when cooking to preserve the natural freshness of the ingredients.
