# Easy Octopus Balls
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 1 steamed bun (crushed into crumbs)
🥬 Cabbage, to taste (chopped)
🧅 Purple onion, to taste (chopped)
🐙 Octopus legs, to taste (diced)
🥚 1 egg
🧂 Seasoning powder, to taste
🧂 Salt and pepper, to taste
🫒 A small amount of cooking oil
🥄 Mayonnaise, to taste
🍅 Ketchup, to taste
🐟 Bonito flakes, to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🧤 Disposable gloves
🍳 Baking tray and parchment paper
🫗 Oil spray bottle
♨️ Oven

## Preparation Steps
1. Crush the steamed bun into crumbs, place in a large bowl, add a small amount of seasoning powder, and mix well.
2. Chop the cabbage and purple onion into fine pieces, and dice the octopus legs into small cubes.
3. Pour all the chopped vegetables and diced octopus into the bowl with the bun crumbs, crack in one egg, and sprinkle with an appropriate amount of salt and pepper.
4. Put on disposable gloves, use your hands to mix and knead all the ingredients evenly until the filling becomes sticky and can hold together.
5. Take an appropriate amount of filling, roll it into round balls, and place them one by one on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
6. Use an oil spray bottle to evenly spray a thin layer of oil on the surface of the balls.
7. Place the baking tray into a preheated oven and bake until the surface of the balls turns golden brown. Adjust the temperature and time according to your oven's characteristics; it is recommended to bake at 180°C for about 15–20 minutes, until the surface color is satisfactory.
8. Remove the baked balls, first squeeze on mayonnaise and ketchup, then sprinkle with bonito flakes, and serve.

## Health Advice
This steamed bun version of octopus balls replaces batter with leftover steamed bun, resulting in lower calories, but the amount of mayonnaise should be controlled. Suitable for kitchen beginners who want to satisfy their cravings.
