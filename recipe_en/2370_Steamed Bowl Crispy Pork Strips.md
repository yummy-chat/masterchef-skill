# Steamed Bowl Crispy Pork Strips
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork tenderloin, appropriate amount (main ingredient, cut into strips)
🥚 Eggs, 2
🥄 Starch, appropriate amount (for batter and coating)
🌾 Flour, a little
🧂 Baking soda, a little (added to batter for crispier texture)
🌿 Cumin seeds, 3g
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, 3g
⭐ Star anise, 2
🌳 Cinnamon stick, 4g
💮 Cloves, 2
🍃 Bay leaves, 2g
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount
🌶️ Dried chili pepper segments, appropriate amount

🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🍶 Shaoxing wine, appropriate amount
🥢 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount
🍾 Dark soy sauce, a little (for coloring)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Slotted spoon
🍳 Wok
🍲 Steamer
🥟 Steaming rack
🍵 Small bowl (for making spiced water)

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the pork tenderloin**: Slice the pork tenderloin open from the middle, make shallow cuts on both sides, then cut into evenly sized strips.
2. **Make spiced water**: Place cumin seeds, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves, and bay leaves in a small bowl. Pour in about 3 jin (1.5kg) of boiling water, steep for 15 minutes, strain out the spices, and reserve the liquid.
3. **Marinate the meat strips**: Add salt and Shaoxing wine to the meat strips, mix well, and marinate for 15 minutes.
4. **Prepare the whole egg batter**: Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk. Add starch and a little flour, stirring vigorously in one direction until smooth and lump-free. Then add a little baking soda and mix well.
5. **Coat the meat**: First, evenly coat the marinated meat strips with a layer of dry starch. Then dip them into the egg batter, ensuring each strip is fully coated.
6. **First frying**: Heat oil in a wok to 30% of its maximum temperature. Add the battered meat strips one by one to prevent sticking. Stir constantly. Fry until set and floating, then remove.
7. **Prepare the sauce**: In another bowl, combine light soy sauce, salt, white pepper powder, chicken powder, a little dark soy sauce, and the prepared spiced water. Stir until the powders are completely dissolved.
8. **Second frying**: Return the first-fried crispy meat to the wok. Raise the oil temperature to 60-70% of its maximum. Quickly fry again until golden brown and the outer skin is crispy. Remove and drain oil.
9. **Arrange in a bowl**: Place the fried crispy meat into a steaming bowl, gently press down, and pour in the prepared sauce. Top with scallion sections, ginger slices, and dried chili pepper segments.
10. **Steam**: Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the steaming bowl on the rack, cover the bowl with a plate to prevent condensation from dripping in, and steam for 90 minutes. After removing, discard the scallion, ginger, and dried chili pepper segments before serving.

## Health Advice
This steamed bowl crispy pork, soaked in spiced water and steamed for a long time, reduces the greasiness associated with deep-frying, making it easier to digest and suitable for the elderly and children. It is recommended to serve with a vegetable salad or a light soup to balance the oil. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the salt.
