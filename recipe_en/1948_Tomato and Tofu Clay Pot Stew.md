# Tomato and Tofu Clay Pot Stew  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 Silken tofu: Appropriate amount (cut into dice-sized pieces)  
🥚 Eggs: 3 (beaten into egg wash)  
🧅 Purple onion: 1 (sliced into strips, used for lining the bottom of the clay pot)  
🍄 Enoki mushrooms: 1 handful (washed, used for lining the bottom of the clay pot)  
🍅 Tomato: 1 (peeled and diced)  
🧅 Scallions: Appropriate amount (some chopped for stir-frying the base, some sliced into shreds for garnish at the end)  
🧄 Garlic: Appropriate amount (minced)  
🌶️ Red bell pepper: Appropriate amount (diced, used for garnish at the end)  
🧂 Light soy sauce: A little  
🍅 Homemade tomato paste: Appropriate amount  
🧂 Fine salt: 5 grams  
🧂 MSG: A little  
🌿 Ground white pepper: A little  
🫒 Cooking oil: Appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Clay pot  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spoon  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the silken tofu into 1.5 cm cubes. Gently mix them into the beaten egg wash, ensuring each piece of tofu is evenly coated with the egg mixture.  
2. Place the wok on high heat to warm up, then add an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Add the egg-coated tofu cubes into the wok and fry over medium heat. Do not flip them immediately after adding to the pan; wait until the bottom is set and golden brown before flipping. Fry until both sides are golden brown, then remove and set aside.  
   **Key Point**: Be sure to use medium heat to prevent sticking caused by low heat; allow the tofu sufficient time to set before flipping.  
3. Leave an appropriate amount of base oil in the wok, reduce to low heat. Add the chopped scallions and minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant. Add the peeled and diced tomato and the homemade tomato paste. Continue stir-frying over low heat until the tomatoes become soft and release their juices.  
4. Add a splash of light soy sauce and stir-fry until a savory aroma develops. Then pour in an appropriate amount of boiling water. Add fine salt, MSG, and ground white pepper. Stir well and bring the broth to a boil.  
   **Key Point**: Stir-fry over low heat throughout to prevent burning; using boiling water for the broth makes it clearer and more flavorful.  
5. Take the clay pot. First, spread the sliced onion strips at the bottom, then layer the enoki mushrooms on top. Arrange the fried tofu pieces over the mushrooms, then pour in the prepared tomato broth.  
6. Bring the broth in the clay pot to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover with a lid and simmer for 15 minutes, allowing the tofu to fully absorb the broth.  
   **Key Point**: After boiling on high heat, must switch to low heat for slow simmering to infuse flavor; the base vegetables prevent the bottom from burning.  
7. After simmering, remove the lid. Garnish with the shredded scallions and diced red bell pepper. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
Tofu and eggs provide high-quality protein. The combination of tomatoes and mushrooms aids digestion and absorption, making this dish suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or a light diet. Control the amount of oil used when frying the tofu. For individuals with high blood pressure, the use of light soy sauce and salt can be reduced.
