# Pan-Fried Trio with Crispy Lace

Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Instructions
1. Place the frozen dumplings, frozen wontons, and frozen beef balls directly into the non-stick frying pan without thawing.
2. Prepare the starch water: Mix cornstarch with water and stir until completely dissolved.
   **Key point**: Adjust the water amount based on the state of the ingredients. Use more water for frozen ingredients and less for freshly wrapped ones.
3. Drizzle an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the pan, then pour in the prepared starch water.
   **State check**: The water level should just cover the bottom of the ingredients.
4. Cover the pan with a lid and cook over medium heat.
   **Key point**: Use steam to cook the upper portion of the ingredients. Observe the steam through the transparent lid.
5. When the liquid in the pan is almost evaporated, reduce the heat to low and continue frying slowly.
   **Important technique**: Reduce the heat to allow the bottoms to brown evenly and form a crispy lace pattern.
6. Check the color of the bottoms. Turn off the heat once they are golden and crispy.
7. Invert a plate over the frying pan, hold the handle firmly, and quickly flip the pan to transfer the pan-fried trio onto the plate.
   **Key point**: Keep the crispy bottom layer intact.

## Health Advice
Frozen dumplings, wontons, and beef balls are pan-fried with starch water to create a crispy, golden lace bottom. This dish is simple to prepare and suitable for those needing a quick carbohydrate boost. However, frozen ingredients tend to be high in sodium. Pairing with vinegar can help cut the richness, and be mindful of portion control.
