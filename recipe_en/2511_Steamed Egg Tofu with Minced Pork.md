# Steamed Egg Tofu with Minced Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Firm Tofu 1 block
🥚 Eggs 4 pieces
🥩 Ground Pork appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped, for garnish)
💧 Warm Water approximately 1.5 times the volume of egg liquid (for preparing the steaming liquid)
🧂 Salt approximately 2g (for tofu base flavor) + approximately 2g (for steaming liquid) + approximately 1g (for meat sauce seasoning)
🍶 White Vinegar 2 drops
🌶️ Pixian Doubanjiang (Broad Bean Paste) appropriate amount
🍶 Huadiao Wine (or cooking wine) appropriate amount
🧂 Ginger Powder appropriate amount
🌶️ Ground White Pepper approximately 1g
🥄 Light Soy Sauce a small amount
🥄 Dark Soy Sauce a small amount
🧂 MSG a small amount
🫒 Sesame Oil a small amount
🌽 Potato Starch appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🛍️ Plastic Bag (for crushing the tofu)
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🧤 Gloves (for mixing the tofu and egg mixture)
🍽️ Plates 2 pieces (one for steaming, the other to cover and prevent dripping)
🥄 Silicone Spatula
♨️ Steamer (with steaming rack)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Instructions
1.  Place the firm tofu in a food-grade plastic bag and use the palm of your hand to crush it into fine tofu paste.
   **Key Point**: The finer the tofu is crushed, the better.
2.  In a stainless steel bowl, crack 4 eggs, add 2 drops of white vinegar, and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg liquid is uniform.
   **Key Point**: White vinegar helps remove the eggy smell and makes the texture smoother.
3.  Pour half of the beaten egg liquid into the tofu paste, add approximately 2g of salt for base flavor, put on gloves, and mix well by hand until fully combined.
   **Key Point**: Add the egg liquid in batches, first completely mixing the tofu with a portion of the egg liquid.
4.  Pour the mixed tofu and egg batter into a steaming dish and use a silicone spatula to smooth the surface.
5.  To the remaining egg liquid, add approximately 2g of salt and 1g of ground white pepper, then pour in warm water (approximately 1.5 times the volume of the egg liquid) and stir well to make the steaming liquid.
   **Key Point**: The amount of warm water should be about 1.5 times the volume of the egg liquid.
6.  Gently pour the steaming liquid along the edge of the dish onto the leveled tofu batter.
7.  Place the steaming dish on the steamer rack, invert the other plate over the dish, cover the steamer lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and steam for 10 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 2 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Covering with an inverted plate prevents condensation from the lid from dripping onto the egg custard, keeping its surface smooth.
8.  While steaming, prepare the minced pork sauce: Heat oil in a wok over low heat, add the ground pork, and stir-fry until it changes color and releases fragrance.
9.  Add Pixian Doubanjiang, stir-fry over low heat until the red oil releases, then add chopped scallions and ginger powder, continuing to stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
   **Heat Reminder**: Keep the heat low throughout to fully release the flavors of the meat and sauce.
10. Add two ladles of water to the wok, turn the heat to high, and bring to a boil.
11. Once boiling, season with a small amount of light soy sauce, a small amount of dark soy sauce, approximately 1g of salt, and a small amount of MSG. Stir well, then pour in the prepared potato starch water mixture to thicken into a light slurry. When the sauce has thickened, drizzle in a small amount of sesame oil and turn off the heat.
    **Doneness Check**: The sauce should be a light, thin slurry.
12. Remove the steamed tofu and egg custard, evenly pour the prepared minced pork sauce over the surface, and garnish with chopped scallions.

## Health Tips
Tofu and eggs are both high-quality protein sources, suitable for those on a weight loss or muscle-building diet. When steaming, use warm water, and be careful not to exceed a water-to-egg ratio of 1.5:1 to ensure a smooth and tender texture.
