# Galangal Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫚 Galangal, to taste (peeled and cut into small pieces, to be pounded)  
🌿 Lemongrass, a small amount (cut into sections, pounded)  
🍗 Chicken wings, to taste (mid-joint or whole wings, score on both sides)  
🍶 Cooking wine, two tablespoons  
🐟 Fish sauce, one tablespoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, one tablespoon  
🧂 Chicken powder, one tablespoon  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (separately for marinating and making batter)  
🌾 Cornstarch, 100 grams  
🌾 Cake flour, 50 grams  
🧂 Baking powder, 10 grams  
💧 Water, to taste (for making batter)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Mortar and pestle  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok/deep fryer  
🔧 Tongs  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel the galangal and cut into small pieces, cut the lemongrass into sections. Place both in a mortar and pestle and pound until they become fibrous paste.  
**Key point**: The pounding method is essential, as it releases a richer aroma than cutting.

2. Wash the chicken wings, score on both sides, or chop into small pieces for better flavor absorption.  
3. Add cooking wine, fish sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, chicken powder, white sugar, and a little cooking oil to the chicken wings. Mix well with your hands until the seasonings are fully absorbed.  
4. Add the pounded galangal and lemongrass paste to the chicken wings, mix thoroughly again, and marinate for at least 2 hours.  
**Key point**: Marinating overnight in the refrigerator allows the flavors to penetrate more deeply.

5. Make the batter: Mix cornstarch, cake flour, and baking powder evenly. Add water and a little cooking oil, and stir until smooth and lump-free, achieving a batter that flows in a steady stream when lifted.  
**Key point**: Cake flour must be used to ensure a crispy and fluffy texture.

6. Pick out any large pieces of galangal residue from the marinated chicken wings, then coat them evenly in the batter.  
**Key point**: Large galangal pieces must be removed to prevent them from burning and turning bitter during frying.

7. Pour cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 150-160°C. Add the battered chicken wings and fry over medium-low heat for about 5-7 minutes.  
**Doneness check**: The chicken wings float, the coating is set, and the color is light yellow.  
**Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; fry slowly until fully cooked.

8. Remove the chicken wings. Heat the oil to 180-190°C, then return the chicken wings for a second fry of 30 seconds to 1 minute, until the coating is golden and crispy.  
**Key point**: The second fry helps expel excess oil and makes the skin crispier.

9. Using the remaining oil in the wok, fry the previously removed galangal shreds until golden and crispy, then drain the oil.  
**Key point**: The fried galangal shreds are the flavor highlight; fry them separately to avoid burning.

10. Line the bottom of a plate with the crispy galangal shreds, place the fried chicken wings on top, and serve immediately.

## Health Tips  
The herbal aroma of galangal and lemongrass aids digestion and is suitable for those with weak spleen and stomach. However, as the dish is deep-fried, it is relatively high in calories. Pair it with a cold vegetable dish to balance the greasiness. The longer the marination, the deeper the flavor, and be mindful of oil control during the second fry to avoid excessive saturated fat intake.
