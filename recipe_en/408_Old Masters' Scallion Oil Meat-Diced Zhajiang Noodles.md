# Old Masters' Scallion Oil Meat-Diced Zhajiang Noodles  
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥒 Cucumber: appropriate amount (julienned)  
🍎 Sweetheart radish: appropriate amount (julienned)  
🌱 Soybean sprouts: appropriate amount (blanched)  
🍜 Noodles: appropriate amount (hand-pulled or fresh noodles)  
🐖 Pork: appropriate amount (pork belly, diced)  
🧅 Scallion: appropriate amount (for making scallion oil)  
🥣 Mixed sauce (soybean paste, sweet bean paste, dried yellow bean paste mixed in proportion)  
🫒 Scallion oil: appropriate amount (made by simmering cooking oil with scallions)  
🥜 Cooking oil: appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spoon  
🍶 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Prepare toppings: Julienne cucumber and sweetheart radish. Blanch soybean sprouts in boiling water, drain well.  
2. Cook noodles: Boil noodles in a pot of boiling water until cooked. Drain well, keeping them chewy and not mushy.  
3. Prepare the zhajiang sauce:  
   - Add cooking oil to the wok. Add the diced pork and stir-fry over low heat until the color changes and oil renders.  
   - Pour in the mixed sauce. Keep frying over low heat while stirring constantly to prevent sticking.  
   - Continue frying until the sauce is no longer dry and looks glossy and oily, with color not too dark.  
   - Add the pre-made scallion oil, stir-fry evenly to fully incorporate the scallion aroma. The lid-off fragrance should be rich with scallion.  
   - Taste and adjust: The overall saltiness should be moderate, with a sweet aftertaste.  
4. Assemble the bowl: Place the cooked noodles at the bottom of the bowl. Layer cucumber shreds, radish shreds, soybean sprouts in order. Spoon a generous amount of scallion oil meat-diced zhajiang sauce on top.  
5. Mix and serve: Use chopsticks to thoroughly toss the noodles with the sauce and toppings until every strand is evenly coated with sauce.

## Health Tips  
This noodle dish is rich in sauce flavor but high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables for balance. Suitable for people with high physical exertion. Those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should reduce the amount of sauce and meat appropriately.
