# Tiger Vegetable (Laohu Cai)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Peanuts appropriate amount (fried until crispy)  
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections)  
🫑 Green chili peppers 2 (seeded and deveined, cut into thin strips)  
🧅 Scallions 2 (use only the white part, cut into thin strips)  
🫒 Sesame oil appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt 1 tablespoon  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🧂 MSG 1 tablespoon  
🫗 Seafood soy sauce appropriate amount  
🫗 Aged black vinegar appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions
1. **Fry the peanuts**: Add peanuts to cold oil, heat over medium heat. Once the oil temperature rises and the peanuts make a "crackling" sound and deepen in color, immediately remove and spread out to cool.
*Note: Residual heat will further crisp the peanuts; do not fry them completely done in the pan.*

2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Cut cilantro into sections. Halve green chili peppers lengthwise, remove seeds and white inner membranes, then cut into thin strips. For scallions, use only the white part and cut into thin strips.
*Key point: The membranes are the main source of heat; removing them reduces spiciness.*

3. **First, toss with sesame oil**: Place scallion strips, cilantro sections, and chili strips in a bowl. Drizzle with sesame oil and mix thoroughly with chopsticks.
*Purpose: Enhances aroma and seals in vegetable moisture, preventing water release when salt and soy sauce are added later.*

4. **Add powdered seasonings**: Add salt, white sugar, and MSG one at a time, mixing well after each addition, then mix again thoroughly.
*Note: Consider the saltiness of the soy sauce to follow; do not over-salt. White sugar enhances freshness and balances the vinegar.*

5. **Add liquid seasonings**: Pour in seafood soy sauce and aged black vinegar, mixing thoroughly.
*Tip: Seafood soy sauce adds complex umami; aged black vinegar provides a rich, layered acidity.*

6. **Mix in the peanuts**: Finally, add the completely cooled peanuts. Toss gently to combine, then plate.
*Key point: Peanuts must be added last to prevent them from soaking in the dressing and losing their crispness.*

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and vitamin C, making it ideal for cutting through grease and stimulating the appetite. Peanuts are relatively high in calories; those watching their weight should reduce the amount. Soy sauce and salt are high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure are advised to use less salt.
