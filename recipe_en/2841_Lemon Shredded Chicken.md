# Lemon Shredded Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken legs 830g (about 5 pieces, soak in clean water for 2-3 hours in advance to remove blood)
🌶️ Small chili peppers 40g (sliced diagonally into rings)
🌿 Cilantro 15g (two sprigs, cut into inch-long sections)
🧅 Onion 100g (about 1/4, sliced)
🧄 Garlic 25g (5-6 cloves, flattened and minced)
🍋 Lemon 100g (about 2 small or 1 large, scrub the skin with salt, slice, and remove seeds; reserve half for juicing)
🧂 Salt (for scrubbing lemon) as needed

🍶 Donggu Yipinxian (or light soy sauce) 20g
🌶️ Chili fresh sauce 20g
🍬 White sugar 15g
🦪 Oyster sauce 15g
🧂 MSG 2g (optional)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g (optional)
🧂 Salt 5g (for the sauce, adjust to taste)
🍶 White vinegar (5% acidity) 20g
🌿 Sichuan pepper oil 20g (do not omit)
🌰 Sesame oil 5g
🍋 Lemon juice juice from about half a lemon
🌶️ Chili powder 20g
🥜 White sesame seeds 5g
🫒 Hot oil as needed (for pouring over the chili mixture)

🧅 Scallion 15g (for boiling chicken legs)
🧄 Ginger 15g (for boiling chicken legs)
✨ Star anise 1 piece
🌿 Angelica dahurica 2g
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 2g (about twenty-something grains)
💛 Turmeric powder 1g (optional)
🤍 Ground white pepper 1g

## Kitchen Equipment
♨️ Pot with good heat retention (such as a clay pot)
🥄 Strainer
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🥣 Metal bowl/seasoning bowl
🔥 Gas stove
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Steps
1.  Soak the chicken legs in clean water for 2-3 hours, changing the water once during this time. Remove and squeeze from the bottom upward at the blood vessel area of the chicken leg to push out any blood clots, then rinse thoroughly under running water.
   **Key**: Thoroughly removing blood clots is crucial for eliminating any gamey smell.

2.  Add enough clean water to a pot, then add scallion sections, ginger slices, star anise, angelica dahurica, Sichuan peppercorns, turmeric powder, and ground white pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat.

3.  Once the water is boiling, add the chicken legs. Keep the heat high and cook uncovered until it returns to a boil, skimming off any foam. Immediately reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover, and cook for 6 minutes. After 6 minutes, turn off the heat, and without uncovering the lid, let the chicken rest in the residual heat for 30 minutes.
   **Key**: Strictly follow the timing to ensure the chicken is tender and not dry.

4.  Remove the rested chicken legs and immediately cool them down by rinsing repeatedly under cold water until they are cool enough to handle. Put on disposable gloves and tear the chicken into pieces.

5.  Prepare the vegetables: slice the small chili peppers diagonally into rings, cut the cilantro into inch-long sections, slice the onion, flatten and mince the garlic. Scrub the lemon skin with salt, rinse clean, slice thinly, and remove the seeds. Reserve half of the lemon for juicing.

6.  Prepare the hot chili oil: In a bowl, combine the chili powder and white sesame seeds, then pour hot oil over the mixture and stir quickly to combine.

7.  Into the bowl with the chili oil, add in order: light soy sauce, chili fresh sauce, white sugar, oyster sauce, MSG, chicken bouillon, salt, white vinegar, Sichuan pepper oil, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine. Finally, squeeze in the juice from half a lemon and mix evenly.
   **Key**: Sichuan pepper oil is the soul seasoning and should not be omitted. The lemon juice must be squeezed in; lemon slices alone are not sufficient for flavor.

8.  Place the shredded chicken, sliced onion, lemon slices, small chili pepper rings, minced garlic, and cilantro sections into a large bowl. Pour the prepared sauce over the top.

9.  Mix thoroughly by hand, ensuring all ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce. Serve on a plate.

## Health Advice
This Lemon Shredded Chicken is high in protein and low in fat. Combined with lemon and Sichuan pepper oil, it is an appetizing and refreshing dish for summer. Pay attention to controlling the amount of chili and salt. Those with sensitive stomachs should use less.
