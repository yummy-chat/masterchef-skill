# Northeast-style Braised Eggplant with Soybean Paste

Cuisine: Northeast Chinese cuisine  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 Tender eggplant 560g (4 pieces, choose tender ones, score without cutting through)  
🌶️ Hot peppers 260g (4 pieces, including regular green hot peppers, red hot peppers, and long thin peppers)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (about 4-5 cloves, minced and used in batches)  
🧅 Scallion 10g (1 segment, sliced into scallion flowers)  
🌶️ Beauty pepper 15g (1 piece, optional, chopped for garnish and extra spiciness)  
🌿 Cilantro 30g (2 sprigs, cut into inch-long sections)  
🫘 Soybean paste 40g (heaping spoonful, not Pixian broad bean paste)  
🥡 Light soy sauce 15-20g (2 tablespoons)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g (half tablespoon)  
🟤 Dark soy sauce 3g (1/3 tablespoon)  
🍬 White sugar 4g (heaping teaspoon)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g (1 teaspoon)  
🥔 Potato starch 1/3 tablespoon, mixed with 3 tablespoons of water to make starch water  
💧 Water about 2/3 of a rice bowl (for braising) + 3 tablespoons (for starch water)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🪵 Wooden lid (to block oil and prevent splattering)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥢 Tongs  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Small bowl or small pot (for mixing sauce and starch water)  
🍽️ Plate (for holding fried ingredients)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the eggplant**: Wash and remove the stems from the tender eggplants. Make 3-4 cuts along the body, not cutting through, to allow flavor absorption while keeping them intact.  
2. **Prepare the peppers**: Tap the stem ends of the hot peppers with the flat side of the knife to loosen them. Remove stems and seeds. Flatten the whole peppers with a few gentle taps for easier frying.  
3. **Prepare the aromatics**: Crush the garlic cloves, then mince. Slice the scallion into flowers. Chop the beauty pepper. Cut the cilantro into inch-long sections.  
4. **Mix the sauce**: In a bowl, combine soybean paste, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, white sugar, and chicken bouillon. Stir evenly and set aside.  
5. **Heat the oil**: Heat the wok over high heat and add a generous amount of cooking oil. Swirl to coat the sides. Heat until the oil reaches about 40% hot (just warm).  
6. **Fry the eggplant**: Add the whole eggplants, immediately reduce to low-medium heat. Fry slowly for about 90 seconds until one side wrinkles, changes color, and softens. Remove with tongs.  
**Note**: Maintain low-medium heat. Use the lid to shield against oil splatter if needed.  
7. **Fry the peppers**: Using the remaining oil in the wok, add the flattened peppers. Fry over low-medium heat until the skin blisters and they soften. Remove and set aside.  
8. **Sauté aromatics and fry the sauce**: Keep some oil in the wok. Over low-medium heat, sauté the scallion flowers and 1/3 of the minced garlic until fragrant. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry until the sauce is aromatic.  
9. **Braise to infuse flavor**: Add about 2/3 of a rice bowl of water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add the fried eggplants and peppers. Reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer for 5 minutes to fully absorb the sauce.  
10. **Reduce sauce and thicken**: Turn to high heat to reduce the sauce. Gently separate the eggplants and peppers with a spatula. Add the chopped beauty pepper, the remaining minced garlic, and the cilantro. Stir-fry until the cilantro softens. Stir the starch water well, then pour it into the wok. Quickly stir-fry until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.  
**Note**: Stir the starch water thoroughly before adding, and stir-fry immediately to prevent clumps.  
11. **Serve**: Turn off the heat. Transfer to a plate and serve.  

## Health Tips  
This dish absorbs oil, and the sauce has a high salt content. It is recommended to pair it with a light staple like multigrain rice and reduce other salt intake for the day. Suitable for those who enjoy strong flavors but need to manage blood pressure; consume in moderation.
