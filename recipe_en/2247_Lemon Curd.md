# Lemon Curd
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April), Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Egg yolks 6 pieces
🍬 Sugar 100g (can be partially substituted with sugar substitute)
🍋 Lemon juice 200g (strained)
🍋 Lemon zest, approximately the amount from 2 lemons
🧈 Butter 50g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Whisk (hand whisk)
♨️ Hot water pot (for double boiling)
🫙 Sieve
🍶 Glass jar (with airtight seal)
🗜️ Grater

## Preparation Steps
1. Add lemon zest, sugar, strained lemon juice, and egg yolks to a stainless steel bowl. Whisk until well combined.
   **Note**: Sugar can be fully or partially substituted with a sugar substitute.
2. Place the stainless steel bowl over a pot of hot water. Continuously whisk the mixture until it thickens, coats the whisk evenly, and leaves a trail that does not immediately disappear.
   **Key point**: Stir constantly to prevent the egg yolks from curdling or scorching. It is recommended to use a wide-mouthed pot for safer operation.
3. Remove from heat. While the mixture is still warm, add the butter pieces. Stir until the butter is completely melted and the mixture is smooth and glossy.
   **Key point**: Using residual heat to melt the butter results in a silkier curd texture.
4. While still hot, pour the lemon curd through a sieve into a glass jar to remove any fine particles or lemon zest, achieving an ultra-smooth final product.
   **Key point**: Straining enhances the smoothness of the texture.
5. Seal the jar tightly. Allow it to cool naturally to room temperature, then transfer to the refrigerator for storage.

## Health Advice
Lemon curd is rich in Vitamin C and high-quality fats, making it suitable as a breakfast spread or dessert accompaniment. However, it has a high sugar and butter content, so it is recommended to consume 1–2 tablespoons per serving. Diabetic patients may replace some sugar with sugar substitutes and should monitor their intake carefully.
