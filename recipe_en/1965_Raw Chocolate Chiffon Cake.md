# Raw Chocolate Chiffon Cake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Large eggs 10 (about 200g yolks, about 440g egg whites)
🍫 65% dark chocolate 292g (for melting in milk)
🍫 65% dark chocolate 160g (cut into small pieces, folded into batter)
🥛 Milk 234g (heated until warm)
🫒 Vegetable oil 102g (flavorless vegetable oil)
🌾 Cake flour 166g
🍫 Cocoa powder 20g
🍬 Fine sugar 166g
🍋 Lemon juice about 16-20g

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Stand mixer
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🥄 Measuring cup
♨️ Microwave
🥢 Hand whisk
🧂 Flour sifter
🔪 Chopper/press cutter
🥄 Spatula
🍩 8-inch chiffon cake tube pan (2 pieces)
🔥 Oven
🔪 Cake release knife

## Instructions
1. **Prepare chocolate pieces**: Use a chopper to press 160g chocolate into small pieces. Select a chopper with coarser grids to avoid cutting too finely.
2. **Sift dry ingredients**: Combine cake flour and cocoa powder, sift together and set aside. Dry ingredients clump easily and must be sifted.
3. **Heat milk**: Heat milk in the microwave until small bubbles form around the edges.
4. **Separate eggs**: Separate egg yolks and egg whites. Refrigerate egg whites in advance. The bowl for egg whites must be free of grease and water. Do not let any yolk mix into the egg whites.
5. **Make chocolate yolk batter**:
   - Pour hot milk over 292g chocolate, stir until completely melted and smooth.
   - Lightly beat egg yolks, add to the chocolate milk mixture, and stir until combined.
   - Add vegetable oil, stir until smooth.
   - Add the sifted dry ingredients in two batches. Use a whisk for the first addition, then switch to a spatula for the second addition, folding gently to avoid developing gluten or forming lumps. Fold until no dry flour remains. Do not overmix.
6. **Make meringue**:
   - Add lemon juice to the egg whites.
   - Beat on high speed (speed 6-7 on stand mixer), adding sugar gradually, until stiff peaks form—when lifted, the whisk attachment should have short, upright peaks.
   - Adjust speed according to your mixer’s power to ensure the egg whites are fully and evenly whipped.
7. **Combine batters**:
   - Add about 1/3 of the meringue to the chocolate yolk batter, fold quickly with a spatula until evenly combined.
   - Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue.
   - Switch to a larger container, use a handheld scraper to fold and cut until no streaks of meringue remain. Uneven mixing can cause cracking or collapsing.
8. **Add chocolate pieces**: Pour the 160g chocolate pieces into the batter, fold quickly until evenly distributed.
9. **Pan and bake**:
   - Pour the batter from a height into two 8-inch tube pans, filling about 70-80% full. Allow the batter to flow naturally into the pan; do not pour in a circular motion.
   - Gently tap the pans, hold the center tube with your finger to prevent air from getting under the pan and causing a sunken bottom.
   - Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack, bake at 160°C (top and bottom heat) for about 50 minutes.
10. **Release from pan**:
    - Immediately after baking, invert the pan to cool completely.
    - Once fully cooled, run a cake release knife along the inner wall of the pan to help release. The cake is moist, so avoid pressing with your hands to prevent fingerprints or damaging the surface.

## Health Tips
This raw chocolate chiffon cake is rich in antioxidants from dark chocolate, but the egg and sugar content is relatively high. It is suitable for post-workout energy replenishment and not recommended as a daily dessert. Ensure that bowls and utensils used for separating eggs and whipping egg whites are completely free of grease and water to prevent the cake from collapsing.
