# Tri-Color Cold Starch Fish
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍠 Pea starch 1 part  
🍉 Dragon fruit juice about 2 parts (for making the slurry) + about 5 parts (for cooking the slurry)  
💧 Water as needed (for cold bath to set)  
🍋 Lemon 1 slice  
🍇 Raisins as needed  
🥜 Crushed peanuts as needed  
🍬 Brown sugar as needed  
🍯 Honey as needed  
🌼 Dried osmanthus flowers as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing slurry)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for cooking starch paste)  
🥄 Soup spoon (for stirring and scooping paste)  
🧀 Perforated colander or steamer tray (for shaping)  
🥛 Milk pot (for boiling syrup)  
🪣 Large glass bowl (for holding cold water to cool the fish)  
📎 Food tongs (for removing lemon slices)  

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the starch slurry**: Place 1 part pea starch in a bowl, add about 2 parts dragon fruit juice, and stir until smooth with no lumps and an even pink color.
2. **Cook the starch slurry**: Add about 5 parts dragon fruit juice to the pan, bring to a boil, then slowly pour in the slurry while stirring continuously and quickly with a soup spoon over high heat. Cook until the paste is thick, translucent, and glossy, and fully gelatinized.
3. **Make the starch fish**: While hot, scoop the cooked pink paste onto a perforated colander, allowing it to drip through the holes into a large glass bowl filled with cold water to cool and set. Repeat the same method to make white and green starch fish respectively. Drain all the fish and mix together to form tri-color starch fish.
4. **Cook the syrup**: Add water and brown sugar to the milk pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then continue cooking until the syrup deepens in color and thickens, and can be drizzled in a thread-like stream when lifted with a spoon. Turn off the heat, let it cool slightly, add the lemon slice, and once the aroma is released, remove the lemon and pour out the syrup. Stir in honey and sprinkle with dried osmanthus flowers, mixing well.
5. **Assemble the dish**: Place the drained tri-color starch fish into a bowl, pour over the osmanthus-lemon-brown sugar syrup, and sprinkle with raisins and crushed peanuts.

## Health Advice  
This colorful cold starch fish is naturally colored with dragon fruit juice, rich in anthocyanins and vitamins. It is sweet, tangy, and refreshing, especially suitable for cooling down in summer. However, pea starch has a relatively high glycemic index, so diabetic patients should only taste a small amount; pairing with nuts and raisins can supplement protein and dietary fiber.
