# Milk Taro Bun
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Lipu taro 250g (for taro filling)
🍖 Meat floss 5g (to be placed in the taro filling)
⚪ White sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)
🌾 Bread flour 400g
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 58g (for the dough)
🍬 Fine granulated sugar 32g (for the taro filling)
🍼 Milk powder 12g
🥛 Milk 263g (ice-cold milk)
🎂 Whipping cream 32g (for the dough)
🎂 Whipping cream 25g (for the taro filling)
🍞 High-sugar-tolerant fresh yeast 10g (or dry yeast 3.3g)
🍞 Sweet old dough, as needed (optional, pre-prepared milk-based old dough)
🧈 Unsalted butter 40g (for the dough, added later)
🟣 Purple sweet potato powder 2.7g (for coloring the taro filling)
🧂 Salt 5g
🍯 Maltose, as needed (diluted with water for sticking sesame seeds)
🧈 Melted butter, a small amount (for brushing the surface after baking)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Steamer or steamer basket
🤖 Stand mixer (with paddle attachment and dough hook)
🛢️ Stainless steel bowl
🔥 Induction cooktop or gas stove
🍳 Baking sheet and baking mat
🕐 Proofing box
♨️ Oven
🧊 Cooling rack
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🌡️ Thermometer

## Production Steps
1. **Make the Taro Filling**
   Peel the Lipu taro, cut it into pieces, and steam it in a steamer until fully cooked. Place the steamed taro, 32g of fine granulated sugar, 25g of whipping cream, and 2.7g of purple sweet potato powder into the stand mixer. Use the paddle attachment to mix until the color is uniform but with some chunky bits remaining. Avoid overmixing to preserve the sandy texture. Once done, seal and refrigerate for later use.
   **Key Point**: Retaining some chunky bits enhances the texture of the filling.

2. **Knead the Dough**
   Place the bread flour, 58g of fine granulated sugar, milk powder, salt, ice-cold milk, 32g of whipping cream, yeast, and sweet old dough into the mixer bowl. Start by mixing on low speed until combined, then switch to medium speed and knead until a thick membrane forms. At this stage, the dough temperature should ideally be below 22°C. Add the softened unsalted butter, first kneading on low speed until fully incorporated, then switch to high speed and knead until full gluten development is achieved, meaning the windowpane test is thin and strong. The final dough temperature must not exceed 26°C. In summer, an ice pack can be placed around the bowl to keep it cool.
   **Key Point**: Controlling the dough temperature is crucial for a fine bread texture.

3. **First Proofing and Dividing**
   Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a proofing container for the first proof. For small buns, only a half-proof is needed, about 30 minutes, until the dough has slightly expanded. Remove the dough and divide it into 60g pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and let them rest for 25 minutes. A half-proof helps prevent air pockets during shaping.
   **Note**: Ensure adequate resting time; otherwise, the dough will shrink back and be difficult to shape.

4. **Filling and Shaping**
   Take a rested dough piece, smooth side down, and flatten it with your hand into a round disc that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges. Do not use a rolling pin, as this can cause hollow spaces during baking. Place about 40g of the taro filling in the center, then add 5g of meat floss on top. Use the "tiger mouth" closing technique to pinch the edges tightly together, and place the bun seam-side down.
   **Key Point**: Flattening by hand prevents over-stretching the gluten.

5. **Second Proofing**
   Arrange the filled buns evenly spaced on a baking sheet. Place the sheet in a proofing box and let the buns proof at 36–38°C until they double in size.

6. **Decoration and Baking**
   Using a utensil, brush the proofed buns with diluted maltose water, then coat them with white sesame seeds. Preheat the oven. For a convection oven, bake at 185°C for about 12 minutes. For a conventional oven, bake at 210°C top heat and 200°C bottom heat for about 13 minutes. Ensure the total baking time does not exceed 15 minutes to prevent the crust from becoming too thick.
   **Note**: The maltose water should not be too thick; otherwise, the sesame seeds will not stick properly.

7. **Post-Baking Steps**
   As soon as baking is finished, transfer the buns to a cooling rack. Brush the surface with melted butter to keep the crust soft. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

## Health Advice
Taro is rich in dietary fiber. Combined with milk and whipping cream, it offers a more balanced nutritional profile. Use moderation with the meat floss and sugar; this bun is suitable as an occasional breakfast or afternoon tea treat.
