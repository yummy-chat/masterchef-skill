# Sour and Spicy Steamed Potato Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potato (appropriate amount)  
🥕 Carrot (appropriate amount)  
🧅 Chopped green onion (appropriate amount)  
🌾 Potato starch (appropriate amount, add gradually in small batches)  
🫒 Cooking oil (appropriate amount, for mixing and drizzling)  
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)  
🍬 White sesame seeds (appropriate amount)  
🌶️ Chili powder (one tablespoon)  
🌿 Cilantro (appropriate amount, chopped)  
🫑 Red bell pepper (appropriate amount, diced into rice-sized pieces)  
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)  
💧 Light soy sauce (one tablespoon)  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar (one tablespoon)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
♨️ Steamer, steaming rack, steaming cloth  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Shred the potato and carrot into fine strips. Bring water to a boil in a pot. First, blanch the potato strips, then add the carrot strips and blanch for about one minute. Remove and plunge into cold water to cool. Then, thoroughly squeeze out excess moisture with your hands.  
2.  Finely chop the squeezed potato and carrot strips into small pieces and place them in a bowl.  
3.  Add chopped green onion and salt. Gradually add potato starch in small batches, mixing with your hands until each piece is evenly coated with a thin layer of starch and the mixture can be pressed into a ball.  
   **Note**: Do not use too much starch; only add enough for the mixture to hold together.  
4.  Drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix well again to lock in moisture and enhance fragrance.  
5.  Take an appropriate amount of the mixture, roll it into a small ball in your hands, then press it into a small patty. Ensure the patty is firm and does not fall apart.  
6.  Line the steamer rack with a steaming cloth and arrange the potato patties evenly. Steam over high heat until steam rises, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 10 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the patties sit covered for 2–3 minutes before opening the lid to prevent them from shrinking and hardening due to temperature change.  
   **Note**: Resting after turning off the heat prevents the patties from collapsing due to temperature differences.  
7.  Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, white sesame seeds, and chili powder. Heat cooking oil until it reaches 70% heat (just starting to smoke) and pour it over the mixture to release the aroma. Then add light soy sauce and fragrant vinegar, stirring well.  
8.  Plate the steamed potato patties, drizzle with the dipping sauce, and garnish with chopped cilantro and diced red bell pepper.  

## Health Suggestions  
This sour and spicy steamed potato cake uses steaming instead of frying, resulting in lower calories. It is rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss or calorie-controlled diet. Note that potato starch should not be added in excess, or the texture may become too firm.
