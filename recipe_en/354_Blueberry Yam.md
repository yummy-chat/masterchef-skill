# Blueberry Yam  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🫐 Blueberries (some for sauce, some for garnish)  
🥔 Yam (peeled and steamed)  
🫘 Tofu (about half a block, cut into small pieces, for making blueberry sauce)  
🫘 Tofu skin (oily tofu skin, a small amount, for making blueberry sauce)  
🍬 Sugar (to taste)  
🥛 Milk or yogurt (to taste, for blending the blueberry sauce)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍲 Steamer (for steaming the yam)  
📡 Microwave (for heating the blueberry sauce)  
🥣 Large bowl, small bowl (for water bath heating)  
🥄 Spoon, fork (for stirring and mashing the yam)  

## Instructions  
1. Peel the yam and steam it in the steamer until cooked. Remove and mash into a fine paste with a fork, then mound it into a small mountain shape on a plate.  
2. Take a small bowl and add some blueberries, sugar, tofu cubes, and tofu skin. Place the small bowl inside a larger bowl filled with water, then heat both together in the microwave.  
   **Key point**: Use microwave power, heating for about 7 minutes.  
3. Remove the combined blueberry sauce bowl, and stir well to blend into a sauce.  
4. Divide the stirred blueberry sauce into two portions: one portion is poured directly over the mashed yam; for the other portion, add some milk or yogurt and mix well, then top with fresh blueberries to make a dipping sauce or a mousse-like sauce.  
   **Optional**: For a mousse-like texture, refrigerate this sauce for 2 hours before use.  
5. Plate the blended blueberry sauce alongside the mashed yam and serve. The yam paste is smooth and silky, while the blueberry sauce is sweet and tangy.  

## Health Tips  
Yam is rich in dietary fiber. Paired with the anthocyanins in blueberries, it helps promote digestion and provides antioxidant benefits, making it suitable for those who need eye care or blood sugar control. Cooking tofu with blueberries adds plant-based protein and calcium, but be mindful of sugar intake—reduce it during weight loss phases.
