# Microwave Thick Egg Roll
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Egg, about 1 piece (for the egg roll batter)
🥓 Ham slices, as needed (cut into small pieces)
🧀 Mozzarella cheese shreds, a generous handful
🍅 Ketchup, as needed (for decoration)
🥗 Mayonnaise, as needed (for decoration)

## Utensils and Equipment
📄 Disposable paper cup
🍽️ Plate
🫗 Plastic wrap
✂️ Scissors
🥢 Chopsticks
♨️ Microwave oven (with “air fry” function)

## Preparation Steps
1. Crack the egg into a disposable paper cup and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is even.
   **Note**: The egg mixture must be whisked until fully blended.
2. Spread a layer of plastic wrap on a plate, with the edges of the wrap extending beyond the plate so it can be wrapped around.
   **Key point**: The plastic wrap should be laid flat, ready for subsequent rolling.
3. Pour the egg mixture onto the plastic wrap-covered plate, and tilt the plate to spread the mixture evenly across the bottom.
4. Use scissors to cut the ham slices into small pieces, sprinkle them evenly over the egg mixture, then top with a generous handful of mozzarella cheese shreds.
   **Key point**: Fillings can be freely customized; more cheese shreds will produce a better stretch effect.
5. Place the plate in the microwave, select the “air fry” mode, and heat until the egg mixture is fully set and the cheese is melted.
   **Note**: Heating time is not fixed; stop when the egg mixture is set, and adjust flexibly according to the microwave’s power.
6. Remove the plate, and while hot, use the plastic wrap to tightly roll up the egg sheet from one side. Place the seam side down, wrap it tightly with plastic wrap to shape.
   **Key point**: This step must be done while hot; the plastic wrap both insulates and helps set the shape.
7. Squeeze ketchup and mayonnaise on top of the rolled egg roll for decoration.

## Health Advice
This thick egg roll is made using a microwave, making it easy to prepare and rich in protein and calcium. Cheese and ham are high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of sauces used.
