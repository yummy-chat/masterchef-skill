# Stir-Fried Pork Liver with Ginger Shreds
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Preparation Steps
1. Split the pork liver open and remove the connective tissue.
2. Cut along the grain into slices about 0.6 cm thick, ensuring uniform thickness.
3. Place the liver slices in clean water and repeatedly squeeze and wash for 8 to 10 minutes, changing the water 2 to 3 times, until the water runs clear and the liver turns pale.
   **Note**: This step is crucial for removing the gamey smell and ensuring a smooth, tender texture.
4. Drain the washed liver, then add a little rice wine and cornstarch to coat evenly.
5. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the marinated liver, reduce to low heat and keep at a gentle simmer. Poach for 1 minute until the liver is about 50-60% cooked, with a slightly bloody center. Drain thoroughly.
   **Key point**: Simmer on low heat strictly for 1 minute; this directly determines the final texture.
6. Heat the wok with cold oil, then stir-fry the minced garlic and ginger shreds over high heat until fragrant. Add the blanched liver and quickly stir-fry. Season with light soy sauce and white sugar. Pour in the thickening sauce to create a thin glaze. Finally, add the green onion sections and toss rapidly. Keep the heat high throughout, and finish stir-frying within 1.5 minutes.
7. Plate the dish. The finished product should be reddish-brown and glossy, with a strong aroma of ginger and scallions, and the liver should be tender, smooth, and sweet.

## Health Advice
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, suitable for those with anemia or eye strain, but it has a high cholesterol content. It is recommended to consume it 1 to 2 times per month. Pay attention to controlling the poaching and stir-frying time to maintain a tender texture.
