# Iced Sweet and Sour Pork (Gulaorou)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (适量, sliced into approx. 0.2 cm thin pieces)
🥚 Egg (1, yolk only)
🌽 Cornstarch (适量, divided into two portions)
🍍 Pineapple (approx. 1 small bowl, cut into chunks)
🍓 Strawberries (approx. 6, halved)
🧄 Garlic (approx. 1 tbsp, minced)
🧅 Onion (approx. 1/4, cut into chunks)
🫑 Green and red bell peppers (approx. 1/4 each, cut into chunks)
🌱 Scallions (approx. 2, cut into sections)
🧊 Ice cubes (适量)
🧂 Salt (a pinch, for marinating)
🍬 Sugar (a pinch, for marinating)
🌶️ Ground white pepper (a pinch)
🍶 Rice wine (适量)
🍋 White vinegar (2 liang)
💧 Water (5 qian)
🥫 Worcestershire sauce (5 qian)
🍅 Ketchup (5 qian)
🍬 White sugar (1.2 liang)
🧂 Salt (3 fen, for the sweet and sour sauce)
💧 Cornstarch slurry (适量)

## Cookware & Tools
🥣 Glass bowl
🥣 Small bowl
🍳 Deep fryer
🍳 Wok
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Spatula
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧺 Bamboo basket or white plate (for plating)

## Steps
1.  Cut the pork belly into very thin slices, about 0.2 cm thick. The thinner the slices, the crispier they become after frying.
2.  Take one egg yolk and add a little cornstarch, stirring until smooth to create an egg yolk batter.
3.  Prepare another bowl of dry cornstarch for coating later.
4.  Mince the garlic; cut the onion and bell peppers into chunks; cut the scallions into sections; cut the pineapple into chunks; halve the strawberries.
5.  Add a pinch of salt, sugar, ground white pepper, and rice wine to the pork belly slices. Mix well with your hands and set aside to marinate.
6.  Coat the marinated pork slices evenly in the egg yolk batter. Using chopsticks, pick up each slice and roll it in the dry cornstarch until it forms a clump.
**Key Point**: The egg yolk batter must be applied first, followed by the dry cornstarch, to create a layered, textured coating.
7.  Sprinkle a little water over the coated pork clumps to prevent them from turning pale during frying.
8.  First fry: Heat oil to 100–120°C. Fry the pork clumps for 2–3 minutes until they float, then remove.
9.  Second fry: Raise the oil temperature to 160°C. Fry all the pork clumps again for about 1 minute until they are golden and crispy, then remove.
**Key Point**: The second fry forces out excess oil and makes the coating crispier.
10. Prepare the sweet and sour sauce in advance by mixing together white vinegar (2 liang), water (5 qian), Worcestershire sauce (5 qian), ketchup (5 qian), white sugar (1.2 liang), and salt (3 fen). Heat a small amount of oil in a wok, sauté the minced garlic and scallion sections until fragrant. Pour in the mixed sauce and bring it to a boil. Immediately add cornstarch slurry to thicken slightly.
**Note**: Do not cook the sauce for too long, or the sourness will dissipate and the color will darken. Salt must be added for flavor, and the cornstarch slurry should make the sauce quite thick.
11. Add the pineapple chunks, strawberries, onion chunks, and bell pepper chunks to the sauce and stir-fry briefly. Then add the fried sweet and sour pork and toss quickly to coat evenly.
**Note**: Add the sweet and sour pork last and stir-fry quickly to prevent the coating from softening in the hot sauce.
12. Immediately remove the sweet and sour pork from the wok and place it on a bamboo basket or container filled with ice cubes to cool rapidly. The finished dish has a crispy exterior, a fragrant and tender interior, and a refreshing, non-greasy taste.

## Health Advice
This iced sweet and sour pork is tangy and appetizing, ideal for summer. However, the deep-frying and sweet and sour sauce contribute to a higher calorie content. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. People with high blood lipids can consume pork belly in moderation, but should be mindful of portion sizes.
