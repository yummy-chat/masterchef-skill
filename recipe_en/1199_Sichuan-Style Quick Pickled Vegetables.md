# Sichuan-Style Quick Pickled Vegetables  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥕 White radish, about 5 lbs (cut into strips)  
🥕 Carrot, about 2 lbs (cut into strips)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (cut into strips, purple cabbage also works)  
🫑 Bell pepper, to taste (cut into strips, added last)  
🥬 Celtuce stems, about 2 (peeled and cut into strips)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🧂 Salt (for water removal), about 500 g  
🍬 Sugar (for water removal), about 100 g  
💧 Mineral water, about 1500 g  
🍶 White vinegar, about 800 g  
🌶️ Pickled wild chili peppers (with liquid), about 4500 g  
🧄 Garlic, about 300 g (crushed)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, about 20 g  
🍋 Lemon, about 200 g (sliced)  
🌿 Cinnamon stick, about 20 g  
⭐ Star anise, about 30 g  
🍃 Bay leaves, about 20 g  
🍭 Yellow rock sugar, about 1000 g  
🧂 Salt, about 200 g  
🍬 Sugar, about 1500 g  
🧂 MSG or chicken bouillon powder, about 100 g  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, about 20 g  
🍶 High-proof liquor, about 50 g (for sealing the jar)  

## Kitchen Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin  
🥄 Colander/strainer  
🏺 Glass pickling jar (with water-sealed rim)  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Cut the Ingredients**  
   Peel and cut white radish and carrot into strips; cut onion into strips; remove the tough outer skin of celtuce stems and cut into strips; cut bell pepper into strips and set aside; slice the ginger. Ensure all containers and tools are free of oil and raw water. Clean the pickling jar and dry it, then pour a small amount of liquor inside to swirl and sanitize, and discard.  
2. **Draw Out Water**  
   Place the cut white radish, carrot, onion, celtuce strips, and ginger slices into a large basin. Add about 500 g salt and 100 g sugar, mix thoroughly by hand, and let rest for several hours until the vegetables have visibly shrunk and a significant amount of water has released at the bottom of the basin.  
3. **Rinse and Drain**  
   Rinse the dehydrated vegetables thoroughly under running water to remove excess salt and bitter liquid, then drain completely until no surface water remains. The degree of draining directly affects the quality of the pickling brine; ensure no raw water is left behind.  
4. **Prepare the Pickling Brine**  
   In another clean large basin, add mineral water, white vinegar, pickled wild chili peppers (with liquid), crushed garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, lemon slices, cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, yellow rock sugar, salt, sugar, and MSG. Stir until the sugar and salt dissolve, then let rest briefly for the aromatics to meld.  
5. **Pack the Jar**  
   Combine the drained vegetables with the pickling brine. At this point, add the bell pepper strips. Transfer everything into the sanitized pickling jar, filling it 80% full. Ensure the vegetables are completely submerged in the brine.  
6. **Seal the Jar**  
   Pour about 50 g of high-proof liquor over the top layer inside the jar, close the lid, and fill the rim with salt to create an airtight seal. This prevents air from entering and creates an anaerobic fermentation environment.  
7. **Fermentation**  
   Seal and store at room temperature. The pickles will reach their best eating stage in about two days, becoming crisp and flavorful. They can be stored for about a week.  

## Health Tips  
This pickle is low in fat and high in fiber, helping to stimulate the appetite and provide vitamins. However, note that it contains high levels of salt and sugar, so those with high blood pressure or controlling sugar intake should consume in moderation. It is recommended to pair with light main dishes and avoid eating large amounts on an empty stomach to prevent stomach irritation.
