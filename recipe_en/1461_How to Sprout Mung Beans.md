# How to Sprout Mung Beans
Category: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Mung beans 100 g (plump, undamaged kernels)
💧 Water as needed (for soaking and watering)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Steamer (with steaming rack)
🧻 Cheesecloth or muslin (fully soaked, spread on the steaming rack to wrap the beans)
🥣 Bowl or container (for soaking the beans)
🔇 Lid (covered with an opaque cloth to ensure complete darkness)

## Instructions
1. **Soak the mung beans**: Rinse the mung beans clean, add enough water to cover, and soak overnight until the beans are noticeably swollen and some skins begin to split.
2. **Prepare the container**: Place a thoroughly wet piece of cheesecloth on the steaming rack. Spread the soaked beans evenly over the cloth, then fold the edges of the cloth over to wrap the beans completely.
   - Note: Do not spread the beans too thickly; allow for air circulation.
3. **First watering**: Gently pour fresh water over the wrapped cloth to thoroughly wet both the beans and the cloth. Make sure there is no standing water in the bottom of the steamer to prevent the beans from rotting.
4. **Strictly block light**: Cover the steamer with its lid. If the lid is transparent, also cover it with an opaque cloth to block all light.
   - Key point: Any light exposure can cause the sprouts to turn red; blocking light is essential for success.
5. **Daily care**: Over the next 3–4 days, water the beans once in the morning and once in the evening. After each watering, restore the dark cover and drain any excess water.
6. **Harvest**: Around day 5, when the sprout stems are white, thick, and about 5–8 cm long, they are ready to be taken out and eaten. If some redness appears due to light exposure, the sprouts are still edible but will have a slightly inferior taste.

## Health Note
Mung bean sprouts are low in calories, high in fiber, and rich in vitamin C, making them suitable for weight loss or people prone to getting "heaty." Keeping them in the dark prevents them from turning red and bitter.
