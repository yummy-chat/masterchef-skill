# European Bread Cheese Fried Egg Sandwich
Cuisine: Common home cooking  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥖 European bread about 2 slices (taken from the freezer, brought to room temperature)  
🥚 Egg liquid appropriate amount (poured into a pan to form a thin layer)  
🧀 Cheese slice 1 slice/serving (melted using residual heat)  
🌰 Pine nuts appropriate amount (sprinkle a little for added fragrance)  
🧂 No additional seasonings needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🍽️ Plate  
🎒 Sealed bag  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Take the frozen European bread out and bring it to room temperature.  
   **Key point**: Bringing to room temperature makes the bread easier to handle and improves the texture.  
2. Heat the pan, pour in the egg liquid, and wait until the egg liquid begins to set but is not fully cooked.  
3. Place the European bread slices on the egg liquid, flip them with a spatula so that both sides are evenly coated with egg liquid, and fry until the surface is golden brown and slightly crispy.  
   **Key point**: You can fry two slices of bread at the same time to improve efficiency.  
4. Place the cheese slice on the fried European bread, sprinkle with pine nuts, and use the residual heat to melt the cheese.  
   **Key point**: Add the pine nuts at the end to maintain their crunchy texture, and wait until the cheese melts.  

## Health Advice  
This European bread cheese fried egg sandwich uses frozen European bread paired with fried egg and cheese, providing a balanced mix of carbohydrates, protein, and fat, suitable for a quick energy boost at breakfast. Note that the European bread should be fully brought to room temperature to avoid a burnt exterior and raw interior. It is recommended to choose low-fat cheese to reduce calories.
