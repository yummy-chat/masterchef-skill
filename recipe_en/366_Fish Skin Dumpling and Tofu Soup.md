# Fish Skin Dumpling and Tofu Soup

## Cooking Steps
1. Pour an appropriate amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat over medium heat. Add the beaten egg mixture and fry until fully set, golden brown, and slightly charred on the surface, forming slightly overcooked egg pieces.
   **Key Point**: The eggs should be "fried a little longer, with a little more oil" — this is the basis for quickly turning the soup broth milky white later.
2. Immediately pour a sufficient amount of boiling hot water into the wok, turn the heat to high, and keep it boiling until the soup turns a milky white color.
   **Key Point**: You must use freshly boiled hot water; cold or warm water will make it difficult to achieve a white soup in a short time.
3. While the broth is bubbling, add the frozen fish skin dumplings directly into the wok. Continue boiling over high heat for about 2–3 minutes, until all the fish skin dumplings float to the surface.
   **Doneness check**: The dumplings floating indicates they are fully cooked.
4. Add the cubed tofu, shredded wood ear mushrooms, and soaked wakame seaweed. Cook for another 1–2 minutes.
   **Note**: Tofu and mushroom/seaweed ingredients cook quickly, so add them later to maintain their texture.
5. Add salt and umami seasoning powder, stir to mix, and taste to adjust the saltiness.
6. Sprinkle in a few goji berries, then turn off the heat.
7. Ladle the soup into bowls, sprinkle with chopped spring onion on top, and serve hot.

## Health Advice
This soup is high in protein and low in fat. The combination of fish skin dumplings and tofu provides calcium, making it suitable for those who want a warm meal without gaining weight. Note that fish skin dumplings already contain salt, so season accordingly.
