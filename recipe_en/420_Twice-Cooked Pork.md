# Twice-Cooked Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Pork belly (recommended er dao rou/pork rump)  
🧅 Spring onions  
🫚 Ginger  
🌶️ Green and red peppers (not recommended to use thick-fleshed bell peppers)  
🌿 Garlic sprouts  
🍶 Cooking wine  
🌶️ Doubanjiang (broad bean paste)  
🧂 Light soy sauce  
🧂 MSG  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🧽 Steel wool  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Instructions
1.  🍳 Heat the wok over high heat. Press the skin side of the pork belly firmly against the wok bottom to singe the skin, removing sweat glands. Use steel wool to scrub off the charred black parts on the pork skin surface to avoid a bitter taste.
2.  🍖 Place the pork belly in the wok and add cold water until it is completely submerged. Add ginger slices, cooking wine, and tied spring onions. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, and continue boiling for 15 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the lean meat.
    **Key Point**: If using a large piece of pork belly, first cut it into 5cm cubes before boiling. It's done when a chopstick can pierce the skin with slight force.

3.  🥢 Remove the boiled pork belly and submerge it in cold water to cool. Pat dry the surface, then slice into pieces about 2mm thick.
    **Key Point**: The cold water bath makes the meat firmer; patting it dry prevents oil splatter.

4.  🌶️ Cut the green and red peppers into rings, cut the garlic sprouts into sections, and slice the ginger into small, thin pieces. Mix 5ml of doubanjiang with 5ml of light soy sauce in advance.
5.  🍳 Heat the wok, add a thin layer of oil to coat the bottom, then add the pork belly slices. Stir-fry until the fat becomes translucent and the slices are slightly curled.
    **Key Point**: Do not slice the meat too thickly, or it will be greasy; er dao rou yields the best result.

6.  🥄 Pour in the doubanjiang-soy sauce mixture and 5g of MSG. Stir-fry for 15 seconds.
7.  🌶️ Add the green and red pepper rings and ginger slices. Add another 5ml of doubanjiang. Stir-fry for 30 seconds.
8.  🌿 Add the garlic sprouts and stir-fry for 60 seconds. Remove from the wok and plate.
    **Key Point**: Garlic sprouts can be substituted with other vegetables like onion, but they must be stir-fried until just cooked beforehand to avoid being undercooked.

## Health Suggestions
Twice-cooked pork is rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion to replenish energy. However, it is high in fat and salt, so people with cardiovascular disease should consume it in moderation. Skimming off the foam while boiling the meat and firming it with cold water can help reduce the greasy feeling.
