# Sour and Spicy Cold Noodles (Liangfen)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🟡 Pea starch 1 bowl
💧 Water 5 bowls (1:5 volume ratio to pea starch)
🧄 Minced garlic 2 tablespoons
🌶️ Chopped chili 1 teaspoon
🫘 Chili oil 2 tablespoons (or substitute with Laoganma)
🥄 Light soy sauce 2-3 tablespoons
🫗 Aged vinegar to taste
🧂 Edible salt to taste
🌿 Chopped green onions to taste
🌱 Cilantro to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥣 Bowls (multiple, for measuring, cooling, and setting)
🥄 Spoon/silicone spatula
🔪 Liangfen scraper (or knife)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Measure 1 bowl of pea starch and 5 bowls of water at a 1:5 volume ratio, using the same bowl to ensure accurate proportions.
**Key point**: Use the same bowl for measuring to ensure accurate proportions.
2. Pour the starch and water into a non-stick pan. Stir thoroughly without heating until no dry powder remains and the mixture is fully combined.
**Key point**: Must stir to combine before turning on the heat to prevent starch from settling and forming lumps.
3. Turn the heat to high. Stir the starch water continuously and quickly. The liquid will gradually thicken, changing from milky white to a translucent, transparent paste.
**Condition check**: The liquid gradually thickens, changing from milky white to a translucent, transparent paste.
   **Key point**: Must stir continuously to prevent sticking and lumping.
4. Once the starch paste is completely transparent, reduce to medium heat and continue cooking for about 3 minutes. The paste should fall from a spoon in a difficult-to-break, triangular, sheet-like shape.
**Condition check**: When scooped with a spoon, the paste forms a difficult-to-break, triangular, sheet-like shape as it falls.
   **Key point**: This step removes excess moisture, making the liangfen more chewy.
5. Quickly pour the cooked starch paste into a clean bowl. Smooth the surface with a spatula.
**Key point**: Work quickly when removing from the pan.
6. Place the bowl at room temperature and let it cool naturally until fully set. Do not refrigerate, or the liangfen will become grainy and crumbly.
**Key point**: Absolutely do not refrigerate, or the liangfen will become grainy and crumbly, losing its smooth and chewy texture.
7. Once the liangfen is completely cool and set, gently loosen the edges of the bowl and invert to unmold.
8. Use a liangfen scraper to scrape the entire block into long strips, or cut into desired strip or block shapes with a knife.
9. Place the liangfen strips into a bowl. Add minced garlic, chopped chili, light soy sauce, aged vinegar, salt, and chili oil in order. Finally, sprinkle with chopped green onions and cilantro.
10. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks until the liangfen strips are evenly coated with the sauce. Serve.

## Health Advice
This sour and spicy liangfen is a light and refreshing summer dish. The carbohydrates from pea starch are easy to digest, but be mindful of the chili oil and salt in the seasonings. Suitable for occasional enjoyment by those monitoring oil and salt intake.
