# Twice-Cooked Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 400g (even texture, width ≥3 fingers)
🥬 Garlic sprouts 250g (cut garlic heads diagonally into horseshoe-shaped pieces, cut leaves into sections and set aside separately)
🌶️ Thin-skinned peppers 40g (cut diagonally into strips)
🫚 Ginger 10g (part sliced for boiling pork, remainder crushed and minced)
🧄 Garlic 10g (crushed and minced)
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (cut into sections)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns about 2g (about a dozen, used in both boiling and stir-frying)
🍶 High-proof baijiu about 10g (or cooking wine/huangjiu, for deodorizing pork)
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste) about 30g (finely chopped)
🍯 Sweet bean sauce about 10g (half a spoonful, less is better than more)
🫘 Fermented black beans about 10g (one level spoonful)
🍶 Huangjiu or cooking wine about 10g (poured along the edge of the wok)
🍬 White sugar about 8g (2/3 level spoonful, to enhance freshness and balance saltiness)
🧧 MSG about 3g (one spoonful, added just before serving)
🥄 Light soy sauce, to taste (only as a backup to adjust saltiness; not used in actual cooking)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for frying pork slices and finishing the dish)
🥘 Soup pot (for boiling pork belly)
🔥 Kitchen torch or heated wok (for scorching pork skin to remove hair and enhance aroma)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board (for preparing ingredients)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam)
🥣 Bowls and spoons (for holding seasonings and removing excess oil)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. **Process pork skin**: Use a kitchen torch or a heated wok bottom to scorch the pork skin until blackened. Soak in cold water for about 2 minutes, then scrape off the charred parts with a knife and wash clean.
2. **Boil pork belly**: Place the processed pork belly in cold water in a pot. Add high-proof baijiu, ginger slices, scallion knots, and Sichuan peppercorns. Once the water boils, skim off any foam. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for about 25 minutes, until the meat is just cooked through. Remove and let it cool until no longer hot to the touch.
**Key point**: Starting in cold water helps remove the porky smell better. Cook just until the meat is done; do not overcook until mushy.
3. **Prepare aromatics**: Cut garlic sprout heads diagonally into horseshoe-shaped pieces; cut leaves into sections and set aside separately. Cut thin-skinned peppers diagonally into strips. Crush and mince ginger and garlic. Finely chop Pixian doubanjiang with a knife.
4. **Slice meat**: Trim the cooked pork belly of any irregular edges, then slice into thin pieces about the thickness of 1.5 coins.
### Cooking Phase
1. **Fry pork slices**: Heat the wok over high heat, add a small amount of oil, then add the pork slices. Reduce heat to medium-low and slowly stir-fry for about 2 minutes, until the edges curl slightly, fat renders, and the slices become translucent. Pour off excess rendered lard, leaving a small amount in the wok.
**Key point**: Use very little oil initially to prevent splattering. Some sticking to the wok at first is normal; it will release as you continue stirring.
2. **Fry aromatics**: Push the pork slices to one side of the wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili segments to the remaining oil. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, being careful not to burn.
3. **Cook the sauce**: Add fermented black beans and stir-fry over low heat for about ten seconds until aromatic. Then add the chopped Pixian doubanjiang and sweet bean sauce. Continue stir-frying over low heat until well-combined, the oil turns red, and the sauce is richly fragrant.
**Key point**: Slow cooking over low heat is essential for building flavor; do not rush. Not chopping the doubanjiang thoroughly will affect texture.
4. **Combine and burst flavors**: Add minced ginger and garlic, stir-frying with the sauce until fragrant. Then thoroughly mix the pork slices with the sauce.
5. **Add wine and vegetables**: Pour huangjiu or cooking wine along the edge of the wok. Immediately increase heat to high. Add pepper strips and garlic sprout heads, quickly stir-frying to combine.
6. **Season and serve**: Add white sugar and stir-fry evenly. Then add garlic sprout leaves and MSG. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat for about 15-20 seconds, until the leaves are just wilted and bright green. Remove from heat and serve immediately.
**Key point**: White sugar is essential for enhancing freshness and balancing saltiness; do not omit. The final stage is all about high heat and quick stir-frying to keep vegetables crisp and tender, preventing them from releasing water.

## Health Advice
This twice-cooked pork dish is rich in fat, but the garlic sprouts and peppers help cut the richness and add fragrance. It is suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or as an occasional indulgence. Boiling then stir-frying the pork belly to render out fat can reduce some saturated fat, but people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
