# Coconut Cream
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥥 4 fresh mature coconuts (for coconut water and flesh)  
🍬 15g white sugar  

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🌀 Blender/food processor  
🌫️ Cheesecloth/strainer  
🥣 Bowl  
🏺 Plastic wrap  
❄️ Refrigerator  
⚡ Electric hand mixer  
🛁 Basin (for water bath heating)

## Instructions  
1. Find the softest hole on top of the coconut, insert the knife tip, and pour out all the coconut water. Collect and set aside.  
   **Note**: Collect coconut water from all 4 coconuts.  
2. Use the back of the knife to crack the coconut open. Remove the flesh, trim off the outer brown skin, and cut into small pieces.  
3. Divide the coconut flesh and water into two batches. Blend in the blender until a fine coconut paste forms.  
4. Pour all the blended coconut paste into a bowl lined with cheesecloth. Squeeze firmly to extract all liquid, yielding pure coconut milk. The remaining coconut pulp in the cheesecloth can be used for other purposes.  
5. Pour the coconut milk into a large bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 24 hours.  
   **Key point**: Thorough chilling allows the coconut fat to rise and solidify.  
6. Remove the chilled coconut milk. Carefully hold down the solidified white fat layer on top, tilt the bowl, and drain out the clear liquid beneath. Keep the solid fat in the bowl.  
7. Add 15g sugar to the solid coconut fat. First, press the sugar into the fat with the mixer, then beat on low speed.  
8. If the fat is too cold to blend smoothly, place the bowl over warm water to slightly soften it. If overheated and fully melted, return the bowl to the refrigerator for about 10 minutes until it regains a soft-solid state that melts upon touch.  
9. Continue beating the soft-solid fat until the texture is smooth and velvety, with clear lines appearing on the surface. When lifting the mixer, the cream on the whisk should form stable small peaks. Serve in bowls.  
   **Finished product features**: White, airy, rich coconut aroma, melts in the mouth. Ideal for spreading on bread or decorating small cakes.

## Health Advice  
Pure coconut fat cream is high in fat but lactose-free, suitable for lactose-intolerant individuals. However, it is calorie-dense, so use sparingly as a garnish. Refrigerating for 24 hours is key to forming stable coconut fat.
