# Braised Duck Wings
Cuisine: Hubei Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦆 Duck wings 4 jin (cleaned)  
🍺 Beer 1 whole bottle (as the main braising liquid)  
🫚 Ginger a few slices  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste (spiciness adjustable)  
🧂 Cooking wine to taste (for blanching, optional)  
🍯 Sweet bean paste 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 40 g  
🍬 Rock sugar 1/2 tablespoon  
🧂 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons  
🧂 MSG 1/2 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt 1 spoon  
🌿 Mixed spices (soaked in advance): 6 slices of Baizhi, 1 Liangjiang, 1 piece of cinnamon, 3 Caoguo, 6 slices of Shannai, 6 Sharen, 4 star anise, 7 bay leaves, 30 g Sichuan peppercorns  

## Tools and Equipment  
🥘 Enameled cast iron pot (for blanching and braising)  
🥣 Transparent glass bowl (for soaking spices and cleaning duck wings)  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥢 Wooden tongs (for stirring)  

## Steps  
1. Place the duck wings in cold water, add cooking wine if desired, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove and drain.
2. Mix all the dry spices together, soak in water in advance to make spiced water, or directly put them into a spice bag and soak.
3. Return the blanched duck wings to the enameled cast iron pot, pour in a whole bottle of beer, and add a few slices of ginger.
4. Add sweet bean paste, dark soy sauce, rock sugar, light soy sauce, MSG, salt, and the soaked spices.
   **Key**: The liquid must cover the duck wings; if insufficient, add hot water.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and keep it boiling to braise. **Do not cover the pot** throughout the process to allow the alcohol to fully evaporate and avoid residual beer flavor.
6. During the braising process, add dried chili peppers to taste and continue cooking.
7. Observe the sauce thickening: When the liquid in the pot has significantly reduced, the sauce starts to become thick and can coat the duck wings, turn off the heat immediately. Use residual heat to stir and evenly coat the wings with sauce.
   **Note**: The sauce can easily dry out and burn, so watch the heat closely. Turn off the heat as soon as it thickens to prevent scorching.
8. Remove the spice bag, transfer the duck wings along with the sauce to a container. Let cool before serving for better flavor.

## Health Advice  
Duck wings are rich in collagen, which is more easily absorbed after braising, making them a suitable snack. However, note the high salt and sugar content; pair with vegetables to balance sodium intake. Do not consume more than 5 pieces per serving, and those with high uric acid should eat with caution.
