# Braised Fish Head in Clay Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Half of a bighead carp fish head (cleaned and cut into pieces)
🧅 1 stalk of scallion (green parts cut into long sections for lining the bottom)
🫚适量 old ginger (sliced)
🧂 Salt to taste
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste
🧴 Dark soy sauce to taste
🌫️ White pepper powder to taste
🥔 3 teaspoons of potato starch
🫒 Sesame oil to taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫕 Clay pot
🔥 Electric ceramic stove
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Mixing tool
🍽️ Plate

## Steps
1. Check the fish head pieces again, use scissors to remove any remaining gills, abdominal black membrane, and blood attached to the bone, rinse until the water runs clear.
**Note**: The black membrane and blood attached to the bone are the main sources of fishy smell and must be thoroughly removed.
2. Drain the processed fish head pieces, add salt, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, white pepper powder, and three teaspoons of potato starch. Mix well with your hands to marinate and infuse flavor.
3. Evenly spread ginger slices and scallion green sections on the bottom of the clay pot, then drizzle in an appropriate amount of sesame oil.
4. Neatly arrange the marinated fish head pieces on top of the scallions and ginger.
5. Cover the pot and place it on the electric ceramic stove. Braise over medium heat for 15 minutes.
**Key point**: Use the moisture from the scallion greens to prevent burning, maintain medium heat; no water is needed during the braising process.
6. Turn off the heat directly once the time is up, then serve the pot directly to the table. The dish has a rich aroma of scallion and sauce, with tender and smooth fish meat.

## Health Advice
This braised fish head in a clay pot is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it especially suitable for those needing brain nourishment and heart protection. However, fish heads are relatively high in cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids are advised to consume in moderation. Thoroughly removing the fishy smell combined with the clay pot's moisture retention makes the fish tender, smooth, and non-greasy—a healthy and quick delicious dish.
