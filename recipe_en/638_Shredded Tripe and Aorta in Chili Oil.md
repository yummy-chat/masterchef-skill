# Shredded Tripe and Aorta in Chili Oil
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐮 Beef tripe (牛百叶) about 200g, cleaned, coarse outer layer removed, rolled tightly and shredded
🐖 Pork aorta (黄喉) about 150g, cleaned and shredded
🌿 Young ginger about 50g, cut into fine shreds
🌶️ Green bird's eye chilies about 30g, halved lengthwise
🧅 Spring onion about 30g, cut into sections
🧄 Ginger, to taste, minced (for stir-frying)
🧄 Garlic, to taste, minced (for stir-frying)
🌶️ Pickled ginger, to taste
🌶️ Pickled chilies (泡辣椒), to taste
🫘 Red oil broad bean paste about 1 tablespoon
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste
🐖 Lard, one small slice
🌿 Green Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
🍶 Shaoxing wine, to taste (for blanching)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste
🍬 White sugar, to taste
🌿 Ground white pepper, to taste
🍶 Vinegar, a small amount (drizzle in before removing from heat)
🧂 Salt, add as needed (because the broad bean paste and other ingredients are already salty, taste at the end to decide)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large wok or frying pan
🥄 Wok spatula or spatula
🗑 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife
🍽️ Cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔥 Gas stove or range

## Instructions
1.  Rinse the tripe and pork aorta under clean water. Lay the tripe flat, tear off the rough outer layer, roll it tightly into a cylinder, then cut into fine shreds. Shred the pork aorta into fine strips as well.
**Key point**: Removing the rough outer layer improves the texture. Rolling tightly before slicing results in more uniform shreds.

2.  Cut the young ginger into fine shreds. Halve the green bird's eye chilies lengthwise. Cut the spring onion into sections. Mince the ginger and garlic. Prepare the pickled ginger and pickled chilies.
**Key point**: Try to cut all shredded ingredients to a similar thickness to ensure even cooking and consistent texture.

3.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the spring onion sections, ginger slices, and Shaoxing wine. Once the water is boiling vigorously, add the shredded tripe and pork aorta. Blanch for about 10 seconds, just until the ingredients curl slightly and are barely cooked. Immediately remove with a slotted spoon and drain well.
**Note**: The blanching time should be very short to maintain a crisp, tender texture. Do not overcook.

4.  Pour some cooking oil into the wok and add the small slice of lard, letting it melt. When the oil is heated to medium-low heat (about 40-50% hot), add the green Sichuan peppercorns, minced ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry over low-medium heat until fragrant. Then add the pickled ginger and pickled chilies, stir-frying until their sour aroma is released. Finally, add the red oil broad bean paste and stir-fry until the red oil releases and the paste is fragrant.
**Key point**: Do not let the oil get too hot, or the Sichuan peppercorns and garlic will burn.

5.  Add the green bird's eye chilies and shredded young ginger. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry briefly until the chili and ginger aromas are released. Immediately add the drained tripe and pork aorta. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to combine everything evenly.
**Key point**: Work quickly to ensure the aromatics are just cooked and the main ingredients are re-heated evenly.

6.  After quickly stir-frying, season with chicken bouillon, white sugar, and ground white pepper. Drizzle a small amount of vinegar along the edge of the wok. Finally, add the spring onion sections. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat just until the spring onion softens slightly and all ingredients are evenly coated in the sauce. Remove from heat and serve immediately.
**Note**: Stir-fry quickly after seasoning to prevent the ingredients from becoming tough. Adding vinegar before removing from the wok enhances the flavor. Since the broad bean paste, pickled ginger, etc. are already salty, add salt only at the end if needed, after tasting.

## Health Advice
This dish features the crisp, tender textures of tripe and pork aorta, paired with young ginger and pickled chilies to stimulate the appetite and aid digestion, making it suitable for times of poor appetite. However, the dish is high in calories due to the red oil and lard. It is recommended to balance the meal with light vegetables. Be mindful of portion sizes; those with sensitive stomachs should consume with caution.
