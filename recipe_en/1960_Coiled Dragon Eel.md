## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. **Processing the eels**: Place the live small eels in a stainless steel bowl. Sprinkle in a handful of salt, quickly cover with another bowl and press down firmly. Shake vigorously until the eels stop moving and the surface slime is removed.
- **Note**: The bowl must be sealed tightly to prevent eels from jumping out and injuring you; adding salt both stuns and kills the eels and removes the slime.
2. **Chopping garnishes**: Cut scallions, ginger, garlic, bird’s eye chilies, and erjingtiao chilies into granules. Prepare the dried chili segments, Sichuan peppercorns, and perilla.
### Cooking Phase
1. **Frying the eels**: Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok. Once the oil is hot, add the eels and **quickly cover with the lid** to prevent oil splatters from the eels thrashing. Fry over medium heat for 5–6 minutes until the bodies curl into O-rings and are dry and fragrant.
2. **Double frying**: Remove the initially fried eels. Once the oil temperature rises again, add them back into the wok for a second fry until they are 70–80% dry, resulting in a crispier texture.
3. **Stir-frying the base**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns, ginger and garlic granules, and dried chili segments, and stir-fry until fragrant.
4. **Combining and stir-frying**: Add the fried eels and stir-fry. Add the chili powder, then pour in a little beer to remove fishiness.
5. **Seasoning**: Add salt, MSG, white sugar, soy sauce, and vinegar to season.
6. **Adding garnishes**: Add the chopped bird’s eye chilies, erjingtiao chilies, and scallion granules, and stir-fry evenly.
7. **Enhance flavor before finishing**: Drizzle in a little oil, sprinkle with cumin powder, ground Sichuan pepper, chopped scallions, and Sichuan pepper oil. Add a bit more MSG, then finally toss in a handful of perilla and stir-fry quickly until evenly combined. Remove from heat to preserve the aroma of the spices.

## Health Recommendation
It is recommended to pair with a bowl of iced rice porridge or cold cucumber salad to balance the spicy and bold flavors of this dish. Suitable for younger people who enjoy bold tastes, but those with weak digestion should eat sparingly due to the high calorie content from fried eels.
