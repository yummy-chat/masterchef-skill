# Scallion Oil
Cuisine: Common Homestyle Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients
🧅 Oil  
🧅 Scallions (any type, large or small)  
🧅 Ginger  
🧅 Onion  
🧅 Cilantro (optional)  
🧅 Dried Shrimp (optional)

## Seasonings
🧂 Cooking Wine

## Kitchenware
🍳 Pot  
🍳 Knife  
🍳 Cutting Board

## Steps
1. Soak the dried shrimp in 50°C warm water, add cooking wine, let soak for 10 minutes, then remove and drain thoroughly.
2. Wash the scallions and cilantro, cut into segments about 5 cm long, and pat dry the surface moisture.
3. Cut the onion into shreds, boil in water for 5 minutes, then remove and drain well.
4. Peel the ginger and slice it.
5. Pour all the oil into the pot, add the pre-soaked dried shrimp, scallion segments, cilantro segments, onion shreds, and ginger slices. Fry over medium-low heat for 20 minutes.

⚠️ Key Points
- Soak dried shrimp in warm water at around 50°C; adding cooking wine effectively removes the fishy smell.
- After washing scallions, cilantro, and other vegetables, pat dry the surface moisture to prevent oil splatter.
- Boil the onion first before frying to reduce some of its pungent flavor and ensure even heating.
- Maintain medium-low heat throughout to prevent burning and ensure the fragrance fully infuses into the oil.
- The fried scallion and shrimp residue can be eaten; the scallion oil can be strained or kept with the solids as needed.

## Health Advice
Scallion oil is rich in unsaturated fatty acids, suitable for daily flavoring, but it is high in calories, so it is recommended to use just a small spoonful per serving. Take care to fry over medium-low heat to avoid burning; after straining, it can be stored in the refrigerator for longer preservation.
