# Braised Potatoes with Green Beans
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Cooking Steps  
1. Slice the pork belly, cut the potatoes into rolling chunks, snap the green beans into sections, cut the scallions into sections, slice the ginger, and crush the garlic. Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat, add the pork belly slices, and stir-fry until the meat turns white and the edges are slightly crispy.  
   **Key point**: Fully render the fat.  

2. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic, and star anise. Continue stir-frying over medium heat until the aroma is noticeably released.  

3. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, and thirteen-spice powder. Stir well to evenly coat the meat slices.  

4. Add the potato chunks and green bean sections. Stir-fry over medium heat until the vegetables are evenly coated with the sauce.  

5. Add hot water until it roughly covers the ingredients. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise for about 20 minutes, until the potatoes and green beans are soft and tender.  
   **Note**: Must use hot water to prevent the meat from becoming tough.  

6. Remove the lid, increase the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly to reduce the sauce until it thickens and evenly coats the ingredients. Then remove from the wok and serve.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and protein, making it suitable for those needing balanced nutrition. However, pork belly has a high fat content, so it is recommended to pair it with coarse grains. Adding enough hot water during braising keeps the meat tender. Diabetic patients should be mindful of their potato intake.
