# Direct Method Hand-Laminated Croissant
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 175g (Brand: Gold Elephant)
🌾 Cake flour 75g (Brand: Meimei; recommended ratio of bread to cake flour is 7:3)
🍞 Instant dry yeast (high sugar tolerant) 6g (Brand: Swallow; or 12g fresh yeast)
🍬 Caster sugar 25g (caster sugar recommended, do not reduce the amount)
🧂 Salt 5g (do not reduce the amount recommended)
🧈 President fermented butter (for mixing in) 25g (softened state)
🥚 Egg 25g (whole egg liquid)
🥛 Milk 140g
🧈 President fermented butter (for lamination) 140g (pre-shaped into a block and refrigerated)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔀 Stand mixer
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Utility knife / craft knife
🧻 Baking parchment / silicone mat
🛍️ Airtight storage bag / plastic wrap
🌡️ Infrared thermometer (optional)
📏 Ruler
🔪 Rolling cutter / pizza cutter
♨️ Oven
🧺 Cooling rack

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare Ingredients**: Accurately weigh all main dough ingredients, the softened butter for mixing in, and the butter for lamination according to the recipe.
   **Key Point**: The recipe uses milk and whole egg liquid instead of water for a richer flavor; the amount of dry yeast is specific to this recipe and cannot be converted arbitrarily.

2. **Prepare Butter Block for Lamination**: Place the butter for lamination on a sheet of baking parchment. Fold the parchment to enclose it and use a rolling pin to roll it into a uniformly thick rectangle. Refrigerate until firm yet still pliable.

3. **Knead the Dough**: Place all main dough ingredients except the butter for mixing in into the stand mixer. Mix on low speed, then switch to medium-high speed and mix until the dough reaches the windowpane stage (expanded stage). Add the softened butter for mixing in and continue mixing until the dough reaches the full stage (complete stage).
   **Note**: Kneading takes time. You can wrap an ice pack around the mixing bowl to keep it cool. The final dough temperature should be around 24°C.

4. **Dough Resting**: Shape the kneaded dough into a ball. Let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes, until it has increased in volume by about 1.5 times.

5. **Prepare for Lamination**: On a lightly floured surface, roll the rested dough into a rectangle about twice the size of the butter block. Place it in an airtight bag and freeze for about 40 minutes to cool down.
   **Key Point**: Rolling the dough thin helps it cool quickly; at the same time, take the refrigerated butter block out to temper.

6. **Enclose the Butter**: Remove the frozen dough and the tempered butter block. Re-roll the dough slightly if needed. Place the butter block in the center of the dough. Fold the four sides of the dough over the butter to enclose it completely, pinching the seams tightly.
   **Key Point**: Control the temperature difference between the dough and the butter to prevent the butter from being too soft or too hard, which can cause it to break through the dough ("broken butter").

7. **First Fold**: Roll out the dough-butter package along its length into a long rectangle. Brush off any excess flour from the surface, spray lightly with water, and perform a double fold (book fold). Use a craft knife to make a small cut at each of the four connecting corners of the folded dough to release tension in the gluten.

8. **Second Fold**: Rotate the dough from the first fold by 90 degrees. Roll it out again into a long rectangle. Brush off excess flour, spray lightly with water, and perform a single fold (letter fold).

9. **Intermediate Rest**: Place the dough after the two folds into an airtight bag. Freeze for 20 minutes, then transfer to the refrigerator to rest for 40 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Freeze first for quick chilling, then refrigerate for thorough gluten relaxation, making the final roll-out easier.

10. **Final Roll-Out and Shaping**: Remove the rested dough. On a lightly floured surface, roll it out into a final large, thin rectangle. Use a ruler and a rolling cutter/pizza cutter to trim the edges. Cut the dough into isosceles triangles with a base of approximately 10cm.
    **Technique Tip**: If the dough shrinks back or becomes too soft during rolling, pause and refrigerate it for 10 minutes to let it rest.

11. **Shape the Croissants**: Check and brush off any excess flour from the back of the triangles. Lightly mist the surface with water. Starting from the base, roll the triangle towards the tip. Place the tip underneath the rolled croissant to secure it. Optionally, make a small cut (about 1cm) in the center of the base before rolling to prevent a dense center (doughy core).
    **Key Point**: Do not stretch the dough excessively while rolling. Ensure the tip is tucked under the bottom for stability.

12. **Proofing and Baking**: Arrange the shaped croissants on a baking sheet. Brush with a first layer of whole egg wash. Place in a warm environment to proof for about 3 hours, until the croissants have puffed up and feel springy to the touch. Let them sit for 10 minutes after proofing. Gently brush with a second layer of egg wash. Bake in the preheated oven on the lower-middle rack at 180°C for about 20 minutes, until the surface is golden brown.
    **Key Point**: Let the first egg wash dry slightly on the surface before applying the second for a shinier finish. After baking, give the baking sheet a sharp tap on the counter and immediately transfer the croissants to a cooling rack.

## Health Advice
Croissants are high in fat and sugar, thus high in calories. Consuming them occasionally can bring enjoyment. It is recommended to pair them with black coffee to cut the richness, and they taste best when eaten warm. Be mindful to control the frequency of consumption to avoid excessive intake.
