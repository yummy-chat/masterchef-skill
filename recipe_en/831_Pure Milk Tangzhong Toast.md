# Pure Milk Tangzhong Toast
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 22g (for tangzhong) + 500g (for main dough)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 6g (for tangzhong) + 54g (for main dough)  
🥛 Hot milk 42g (barely boiling, for making tangzhong)  
🧊 Cold milk 345g (for main dough; do not use too cold in winter)  
🍯 Condensed milk 40g  
🍞 Instant dry yeast (high-sugar tolerant) 5.2g  
🧂 Salt 8g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 50g (softened)  

## Tools & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Measuring cup/bowl  
🥄 Spatula  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🍞 Stand mixer  
🌡️ Probe thermometer  
🪣 Mixing bowl  
🔥 Proofing box (or oven with proofing function)  
🔥 Oven  
🍞 Toast loaf pan (lidded and lidless)  
🫓 Rolling pin  
🪒 Bench scraper  
⏲️ Timer  

## Steps  
### Preparation  
1. **Make the tangzhong**: Mix 22g bread flour with 6g granulated sugar. Pour in 42g barely boiling hot milk. Stir quickly until a paste forms with no dry flour. Let cool completely.  
2. **Combine basic ingredients**: In the stand mixer bowl, add 345g cold milk, 40g condensed milk, and 5.2g instant dry yeast. Stir briefly. Add 500g bread flour and 8g salt.  
   **Note**: In winter, do not use overly cold milk, as it may hinder fermentation.  

### Kneading & Fermentation  
3. **Knead to initial expansion**: Mix on low speed until a dough forms, then increase to medium-high speed. Knead until a thick membrane forms with jagged edges. Do not add butter or tangzhong yet.  
4. **Add tangzhong and butter**: Add the cooled tangzhong and knead until partially incorporated. When the dough reaches initial expansion, add softened unsalted butter. Knead on low speed until butter is incorporated, then increase to medium-high speed.  
5. **Knead to full expansion**: Continue kneading until the dough is smooth and can be stretched into a very thin, even windowpane with smooth, round edges. Check dough temperature; it should be 26–28°C. If below 26°C, increase the proofing environment temperature or extend fermentation.  
6. **Bulk fermentation**: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a container. Proof at 28°C with 80% humidity for about 1 hour 20 minutes, until doubled in size. A finger dusted with flour poked into the dough should leave an indent that does not spring back or collapse.  

### Shaping & Final Proof  
7. **Divide and rest**: Gently deflate the dough. Divide evenly into 4 portions. Shape each into a smooth oval. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for about 25 minutes, until the dough is soft and does not spring back when rolled out.  
8. **First roll**: Take one rested piece. Smooth side up, gently roll it into a long tongue shape. Flip over. Loosely roll it up from top to bottom, about 4 turns.  
   **Note**: Be gentle; do not tear the gluten. Rolling should be loose.  
9. **Second roll and pan**: After resting, again place the dough smooth side up. Roll into a strip about 30 cm long. Gently pat out large air bubbles on the edges. Flip over. Roll naturally from top to bottom. Place seam side down. The width of the roll should match the width of the toast pan. Place two rolls in each pan.  
10. **Final proof**: Proof at 38°C with 80% humidity for about 1 hour.  
    For lidded toast: Proof until about 1.5 cm below the rim.  
    For dome toast: Proof until level with the rim.  

### Baking  
11. **Baking**:  
    Lidded toast: Bake at about 170°C for 28 minutes.  
    Dome toast: Bake at 165°C top / 210°C bottom for about 25 minutes.  
    Immediately after baking, tap the pan to release steam and unmold. Cool completely on a wire rack.  
    **Note**: Do not open the oven door frequently during baking.  

## Health Tips  
This pure milk tangzhong toast is rich in carbohydrates and quality fats, suitable for quick energy replenishment after exercise. However, it is high in calories. It is recommended to slice thinly and pair with protein. Be mindful of portion size to avoid a sharp spike in blood sugar.
