# Purple Rice Cheese Bun
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Purple rice, appropriate amount (washed and cooked in advance)
🌾 Flour, appropriate amount
🍚 Glutinous rice flour, appropriate amount (approximately 1:1 ratio with flour)
🧈 Butter, a small piece (about 10 grams)
🧀 Cheese block, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes)
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk, appropriate amount
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔌 Rice cooker
🥣 Food processor
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Kitchen knife
🥄 Silicone spatula

## Steps
1. Wash the purple rice thoroughly, add water about 1.5 times the volume of the rice, and cook in a rice cooker until fully done. Set aside.
2. Place the flour, glutinous rice flour, some of the cooked purple rice, butter, and white sugar into a food processor. Pulse for about 8 seconds until it forms a smooth and soft dough.
3. In a separate bowl, take some of the cooked purple rice, add sweetened condensed milk, and mix thoroughly with a spatula until it becomes a sticky and glossy purple rice filling.
4. Roll the dough into a long log and cut it into evenly sized small pieces with a knife. Roll each piece into a round wrapper that is slightly thicker in the center and thinner at the edges.
5. Place a layer of purple rice filling in the center of the wrapper, then add a small piece of cheese. Gather the edges and seal tightly like wrapping a bun. Place the sealed side down and gently press into a round patty.
6. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat without oil or with a very thin layer of oil. Place the patty in the pan and cook until one side is set and golden brown, then flip. Cook until both sides are caramelized and golden brown, and the cheese inside is melted.
   **Note**: Gently press the patty with a spatula or your hand during cooking to ensure even heat distribution and a crispy texture on both sides.

## Health Advice
Purple rice is rich in anthocyanins, and paired with cheese, it provides calcium and protein, making it suitable as an energy-packed breakfast or afternoon snack. Be mindful of the amount of sweetened condensed milk and sugar; those with diabetes can reduce the sweetness as needed.
