# All-Purpose Scallion Oil
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 Onion 100g (sliced)
🫚 Ginger 50g (sliced)
🌿 Cilantro 50g (with roots)
🧅 Scallions 50g
🧅 Leek 400g (white part only)
🫒 Salad oil 500g

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Ladle
🫙 Storage container (with lid)

## Instructions
1. Wash the ginger, cilantro, scallions, leek, and onion separately. Slice the ginger and onion. Cut the white part of the leek into sections. Set aside.
2. Pour the salad oil into the wok, then immediately add all the prepared ingredients.
   **Key point**: Add ingredients to cold oil to allow the aroma to fully infuse into the oil.
3. Heat slowly over low heat, frying until the moisture from the ingredients evaporates and the fragrance is released.
   **Indicator**: The oil temperature gradually rises from cold, and small bubbles appear around the edges of the ingredients.
4. Continuously observe the color of the ingredients. Turn off the heat as soon as the leek turns slightly yellow.
   **Key point**: Avoid frying until burnt, otherwise the oil will taste bitter.
5. After turning off the heat, pour the oil and ingredients together into a heat-resistant container. Cover and let it cool naturally.
   **Key point**: Do not remove the ingredients; allowing them to steep while cooling further enhances the flavor.
6. Once the scallion oil is completely cool, strain out all the solid ingredients using a fine strainer. Collect the clear oil.
   **Indicator**: The resulting scallion oil is golden and translucent.

## Health Advice
Scallion oil is rich in unsaturated fatty acids. It is recommended to use 1-2 tablespoons per serving, suitable for adding fragrance to daily noodle dishes or cold dishes. People with high blood lipids can consume it in moderation but should control their intake.
