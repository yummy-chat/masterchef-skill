# Eggplant Boxes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍆 1 long eggplant (peeled, cut into double slices)  
🐷 Ground pork, as needed  
🥓 Chinese sausage, as needed (finely chopped, mixed with ground pork)  
🧅 White part of scallion, as needed (finely chopped)  
🫚 Ginger water, as needed (for adding moisture)  
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (1:1 ratio with cornstarch)  
🌽 Cornstarch, as needed (1:1 ratio with all-purpose flour)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, a pinch  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🥢 Soy sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🌶️ Ground white pepper, 2 shakes  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed (used twice: for mixing meat filling and mixing scallion)  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount (added to batter)  
🧂 Baking powder, a small amount (added to batter for a fluffy coating)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Vegetable peeler  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for preparing meat filling and batter)  
🍳 Non-stick pan (for deep frying)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing eggplant boxes)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for testing oil temperature)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the long eggplant and cut it into double slices to create pockets for the filling.  
   **Key Point**: Immediately soak the cut eggplant slices in lightly salted water to prevent oxidation and blackening.
2. Add the chopped Chinese sausage to the ground pork. Gradually add ginger water while stirring vigorously in one direction until the water is fully absorbed, making the meat filling tender and juicy. Then add chicken bouillon, sugar, salt, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and white pepper. Continue stirring vigorously in the same direction until the mixture is well combined. Mix the chopped scallion white with sesame oil, then add it to the meat filling and mix evenly.  
   **Key Point**: Adding water and stirring in one direction is the key to a tender and juicy meat filling; coating the scallion with sesame oil locks in moisture and enhances aroma. This meat filling can be used as a "universal meat filling."
3. Mix all-purpose flour and cornstarch in a 1:1 ratio. Add a small amount of baking powder. Gradually add water and a small amount of cooking oil, stirring until the batter reaches a consistency similar to thick yogurt.  
   **Key Point**: The batter should be thick enough to coat the eggplant boxes without dripping; adding oil makes the coating crispier.
4. Remove the eggplant slices from the salted water. Fill each slice with an appropriate amount of the prepared meat filling, then evenly coat with the batter.  
   **Key Point**: Use a moderate amount of filling; too much may cause the boxes to fall apart during frying. Ensure the eggplant boxes are completely covered with batter.
5. Pour a generous amount of oil into the pan and heat over high heat until bubbles form rapidly when chopsticks are inserted. Carefully add the battered eggplant boxes one by one. Fry over high heat until they float and turn a light golden color, then remove them.  
   **Key Point**: The oil must be hot enough; avoid adding too many at once to prevent sticking.
6. Heat the oil over high heat until it begins to smoke slightly. Return the initially fried eggplant boxes to the oil and fry for a second time for about 30 seconds, until they turn a deep golden color and the coating is crispy. Immediately remove and drain on paper towels.  
   **Key Point**: The second fry is essential for making the coating extra crispy and removing excess oil; do not fry for too long.

## Health Advice  
These eggplant boxes are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside with juicy filling. However, as they are deep-fried and relatively high in calories, pairing them with a light, refreshing cold dish is recommended for balance. They are suitable as an occasional snack for gatherings. Those with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume in moderation.
