# Mini Cream Puffs
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Infant formula powder 60g  
💧 Water 350ml  
🌽 Corn oil approx. 30g  
🧂 Salt approx. 1g  
🥚 Eggs as needed  
🌾 Cake flour as needed  
🧈 Butter as needed  
🥛 Heavy cream as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Formula mixer  
🥣 Measuring cup  
🍳 Small saucepan  
🥣 Bowl  
🍴 Fork  
♨️ Microwave  
♨️ Oven  
🎂 Piping bag  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
🟡 Silicone spatula  

## Instructions  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Pour 60g infant formula powder, 350ml water, and approx. 1g salt into the formula mixer. Stir thoroughly until fully dissolved.  
   **Key Point**: Ensure the powder and water are completely blended with no lumps.  
2. Break the butter into pieces and place in a bowl. Add salt and a small amount of water. Microwave on high for about 1 minute until boiling. While still hot, add the beaten eggs in batches, stirring continuously, until a smooth butter-egg mixture forms.  
   **Key Point**: The butter liquid must remain boiling when adding the egg mixture; adjust the amount of eggs based on the final consistency of the batter.  

### Making Phase  
1. Combine the prepared milk mixture and the butter-egg mixture until well blended. Sift in the cake flour and stir until smooth, free of lumps, and the batter forms a stable inverted triangle when lifted.  
   **Key Point**: When lifted, the batter should form a steady inverted triangle shape.  
2. Transfer the batter to a piping bag. Pipe small rounds about 2–3 cm in diameter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Preheat the oven to 190°C and bake for 15–20 minutes until the puffs expand significantly and turn golden brown on the surface.  
   **Key Point**: Avoid opening the oven door frequently during baking to prevent the puffs from collapsing.  
3. Pour the heavy cream into a bowl. Use an electric hand mixer to whip until distinct peaks form and the cream is stiff.  
   **Key Point**: Whip the cream until stiff peaks form to support the filling.  
4. Once the puffs have cooled, pierce a small hole in the bottom of each puff. Use a piping bag to fill the puffs with the whipped cream.

## Health Advice
Using infant formula powder to make puffs can help use up any extra formula powder at home while giving the puffs a mild and nutritious flavor. Be careful not to overwhip the cream, or it may cause the puffs to crack when filling.
