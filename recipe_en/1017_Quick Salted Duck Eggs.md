# Quick Salted Duck Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Fresh duck eggs, 29 pieces  
🧂 Edible salt, 1000 grams (for the curing mixture, must be sufficient)  
🍶 White vinegar, approximately 300 grams (half a bottle, for soaking and cleaning)  
🌿 Star anise, a few pieces  
🌿 Cinnamon, appropriate amount  
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper, appropriate amount  
🍶 High-proof liquor, appropriate amount (for soaking the spices)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin  
🥣 Bowl  
🪵 Cutting board  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🫙 Earthenware jar (for sealed storage)  
🍲 Pot (for boiling)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Clean the duck eggs**: Place the duck eggs in a basin, add water and white vinegar, and soak for 10 minutes to remove impurities from the shells. Remove and drain the moisture.  
2. **Prepare the spices**: Break the star anise into pieces and break the cinnamon into small chunks. Place them together with the green Sichuan pepper into a bowl, rinse with water, and then remove.  
3. **Make the curing mixture**: Add high-proof liquor to the rinsed spices, soak for 30 minutes, then pour in 1000 grams of salt and stir thoroughly to create a dry salt mixture with moist spices.  
   - Key: Soaking in liquor allows the spices to fully release their flavor; the amount of salt should not be reduced, as this is crucial for achieving a sandy texture and flowing oil.  
4. **Wrap the duck eggs**: Spread plastic wrap on a cutting board. First, lay down a layer of the salt mixture, place a duck egg on top, then cover the egg with more salt mixture to ensure even coating. Roll the plastic wrap around the duck egg, twisting and pressing the ends tightly closed.  
   - Suggestion: Wrap in groups of 4 for efficiency; tighten well to prevent salt leakage.  
5. **Place in the jar for sealing**: Put all the wrapped duck eggs into a clean, dry earthenware jar. Close the lid tightly and store in a cool, dark place.  
6. **Wait for curing**: Cure for 30 days, maintaining a cool environment and avoiding direct sunlight.  
7. **Boil and serve**: Remove the cured duck eggs, wash off the salt mixture from the surface, place them in a pot, add water, and boil until cooked. The egg whites will be firm, the yolks will be sandy and flow with oil, offering a savory and delicious taste.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in salt due to the curing process. It is recommended to eat half an egg per serving, paired with plain congee or light vegetables to balance sodium intake. The firm egg whites and oily yolks are the result of 30 days of patient waiting. Individuals with high blood pressure or kidney disease should strictly control their consumption.
