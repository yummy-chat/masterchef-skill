# Milk Bread Toast
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
**Pre-ferment**
🌾 Bread flour 375g
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk 10g
💧 Water 230g (ice water recommended to help control dough temperature)
🍞 Dry yeast 4g

**Main Dough**
🌾 Bread flour 125g
🍬 Granulated sugar 57g
🧂 Salt 8g
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk 25g
🧈 Heavy cream 25g
🍞 High-sugar tolerance dry yeast 1g
🥛 Milk 65g (ice milk recommended to help control dough temperature)
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (softened, added later)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
⚙️ Stand mixer (with kneading and fermentation functions)
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥖 Rolling pin
📦 450g toast mold (2 pieces)
🔥 Convection oven (or home flat oven)
🍞 Cooling rack
🖌️ Brush

## Steps
1. **Prepare the pre-ferment**: Add water, sweetened condensed milk, dry yeast, and bread flour sequentially into the stand mixer bowl.
2. **Knead the pre-ferment**: Mix on the lowest speed for about 3 minutes until the ingredients are combined and no dry flour remains. It does not need to reach a windowpane stage.
3. **Ferment the pre-ferment**: Use the mixer's fermentation function to ferment for 1 hour until the dough expands to about 2.5 times its original size and has a honeycomb structure inside.
4. **Combine the main dough**: Add heavy cream, sweetened condensed milk, and milk to the fermented pre-ferment in sequence, then add high-sugar tolerance dry yeast, granulated sugar, bread flour, and salt.
5. **Knead to initial expansion**: Knead on low speed for about 5 minutes until the dough is smooth, non-sticky, and can form a thicker, easily tearing membrane with jagged edges at the tear. At this point, add the softened unsalted butter. You can manually wrap the butter into the dough to assist in mixing.
6. **Knead to full expansion**: First, knead on low speed for 2 minutes to fully incorporate the butter, then switch to medium speed and knead for about 3 minutes, until the dough can form a thin, strong windowpane with good transparency and smooth edges when torn. After kneading, the dough temperature should be controlled below 26°C; use ice cubes if necessary to cool down.
7. **First fermentation**: Shape the dough into a ball and return it to the mixer bowl. Use the mixer's fermentation function to ferment for 1 hour, until a finger dipped in flour poked into the dough leaves a hole that does not spring back or collapse.
8. **Divide and round**: Gently pat the dough to release gas, then evenly divide it into 4 portions. Round each portion by tightening the bottom so the surface is smooth and taut.
9. **Rest**: Cover with a damp cloth and let rest at room temperature for 25 minutes.
10. **Shape**: Roll the rested dough into a long strip, flip it over, then fold it into thirds lengthwise. Roll it out again lengthwise, then roll it up from top to bottom. After rolling, gently drop the bottom seam onto the work surface to tighten it. Place two dough rolls together as a set into one 450g toast mold.
11. **Second fermentation**: Place in a warm, humid place and ferment until the dough rises to 90% full of the mold.
12. **Bake**: Bake in a convection oven at 165°C for 22 minutes.
13. **Finish**: Immediately unmold after baking. While hot, lightly brush the surface with butter to retain moisture, then cool on a cooling rack.

## Health Advice
This milk bread toast is rich in milk flavor and has a soft, chewy texture. It is suitable as an energy boost for breakfast or afternoon tea, but it is high in calories. It is recommended to limit intake to 2–3 slices per serving to control carbohydrate consumption. Diabetic patients or those needing to control sugar intake should consume cautiously due to the sweetened condensed milk content.
