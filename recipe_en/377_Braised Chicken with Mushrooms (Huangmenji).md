# Braised Chicken with Mushrooms (Huangmenji)
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms (dried shiitake are best)
🫑 Green bell pepper
🧅 Ginger slices
🌶️ Dried chili peppers
🥔 Potato (optional)
🧂 Salt
🍶 Cooking wine
🌿 White pepper powder
🍬 White sugar
🍶 Soy sauce
🧂 MSG

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🍲 Clay pot (optional)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the chicken thighs and chop them into 4 cm square pieces.
2. Slice the ginger and cut dried chili peppers into small rings.
3. Slice the shiitake mushrooms and cut the green bell pepper into slender diamond-shaped pieces; if using dried shiitake, rinse off dust and soak overnight, reserving the soaking water for later use.
4. If adding potatoes, cut them into irregular chunks similar in size to the chicken.
5. Caramelize sugar: Add a base layer of oil to the wok, add white sugar to cold oil, and heat slowly over low heat until the sugar melts and turns dark brown. If you are not proficient, skip this step and substitute with dark soy sauce for color when stir-frying the chicken later.
6. Quickly add the chicken pieces, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry rapidly. Splash in the cooking wine and continue stir-frying for a moment.
7. Add ginger slices and dried chili peppers, stir-frying until fragrant.
8. Add soy sauce and stir well.
9. Pour in the reserved mushroom soaking water or plain water, enough to just cover the chicken.
10. Add the sliced shiitake mushrooms, white pepper powder, salt, and potato chunks, and stir-fry evenly.
11. Cover the wok and simmer over medium-low heat for 15–20 minutes; if using a clay pot, transfer the mixture to the clay pot for simmering.
12. Once the chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened, add the green bell pepper.
13. Add MSG, quickly toss and stir evenly, then turn off the heat. The green bell pepper should be just cooked through; do not overcook.

## Health Advice
Removing the skin and bones from chicken thighs can reduce fat by 30%, but the water used to soak the shiitake mushrooms is rich in natural umami and is healthier than chicken bouillon. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amounts of soy sauce and salt, and it is more balanced to serve with mixed grain rice.
