# Homestyle Sesame Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍝 Noodles – as needed (dried or fresh noodles, medium thickness, straight-cut)
🥒 Cucumber – as needed (finely julienned)
🥬 Zucchini – as needed (finely julienned)
🧄 Garlic cloves – about 8–10 cloves
🥜 Sesame paste – as needed
🫒 Sesame oil – about 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt – as needed (used separately for sesame paste, garlic sauce, and noodle water)
🍬 White sugar – about 1 teaspoon
🧴 Light soy sauce – about 1 tablespoon
🍶 Rice vinegar – 1 tablespoon
💧 Cool boiled water – as needed (to dilute sesame paste and adjust garlic sauce)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🥄 Spoon
🧄 Garlic press (or blender)
🍲 Cooking pot
🥢 Chopsticks
🍝 Strainer
🪣 Large bowl/container (for cold water)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the sesame sauce**: In a large bowl, combine sesame paste with sesame oil, a pinch of salt, white sugar, and light soy sauce. Add cool boiled water in three separate batches, stirring continuously in one direction with a spoon each time, until the sesame paste becomes smooth and pourable. Finally, add rice vinegar and stir well. Set aside.
   **Note**: Adding water gradually and stirring continuously is key to preventing oil-water separation and achieving a smooth, fine texture.
2. **Prepare the garlic sauce**: Place garlic cloves into a garlic press and mash into a paste. Transfer to a small bowl, add a spoonful of salt, and stir and grind repeatedly with a spoon until the garlic paste releases a rich garlic oil. Then add an appropriate amount of cool boiled water to form a drizzle-able sauce.
   **Note**: Adding salt first to "kill" the garlic quickly draws out its aroma and pungency.
3. **Prepare the toppings**: Julienne the cucumber and zucchini separately into fine strips. Set aside.
4. **Cook the noodles**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a spoonful of salt, then add the noodles. Cook over medium heat, maintaining a gentle boil, until the noodles are fully cooked with no hard core.
   **Note**: Adding salt to the noodle water makes the noodles chewier and gives them a savory base. Adjust cooking time based on noodle thickness; taste to confirm doneness.
5. **Quickly cool the noodles**: Use a strainer to lift the cooked noodles and immediately immerse them in prepared cool boiled water. Rapidly cool, then drain well.
   **Note**: Quick cooling prevents the noodles from sticking together and gives them a smoother, chewier texture.
6. **Assemble and mix**: Place the drained noodles into a bowl. Generously drizzle the sesame sauce over the top, then add the garlic sauce. Arrange cucumber and zucchini strips on top. Mix thoroughly before serving.

## Health Tips
Adding salt to the garlic sauce in sesame noodles helps release allicin, which aids in antibacterial effects. Cucumber and zucchini provide dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for a weakened summer digestive system. It is recommended to control the amount of sesame paste to reduce calorie intake, and to mix and serve immediately for the best flavor.
