# Homemade Puff Pastry Egg Tarts 🥧
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🌾
🌾 300g all-purpose flour  
💧 160g water  
🧂 4g salt  
🧈 170g butter  
🍬 35g sugar  
🥛 100g heavy cream  
🥛 80g milk  
🥚 3 egg yolks  
🥚 1 whole egg  

## Kitchenware & Equipment 🥣
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🪷 Rolling pin  
📄 Parchment paper  
🔪 Knife  
🧁 Foil egg tart molds  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Whisk  
🔥 Oven  
📦 Baking tray  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Instructions 📋
1. Combine all-purpose flour, water, and salt. Mix into flocculent pieces, then knead into a rough dough. Cover and let rest for 15 minutes. Knead again until smooth, cover, and let rest for another hour.  
2. Slice the butter. On a piece of parchment paper, fold a square area, place the butter slices inside, and wrap. Use a rolling pin to tap and roll the butter into an even square sheet of uniform thickness. Refrigerate until firm.  
3. Remove the rested dough, roll it into a square, and place the chilled butter sheet in the center. Fold the four corners of the dough toward the center to enclose the butter. Pinch the seams tightly, releasing any trapped air.  
4. Turn the dough package over, dust with a little flour, and roll into a rectangle. Fold the left and right sides toward the center for the first single fold. If the butter hasn't softened, proceed immediately. Roll out again and repeat the fold for a second single fold. Roll and fold a third time, ending with a square dough.  
   - Ensure the butter does not melt throughout the process. If it softens, refrigerate immediately for 10–15 minutes before continuing.  
5. Freeze the folded dough for 10 minutes. Remove and roll into a large, thin rectangular sheet. Roll it tightly from the bottom up into a cylinder. Lightly moisten the surface with water to help seal, wrap in parchment paper, and freeze for 15 minutes.  
6. Remove the chilled dough log and cut it into about 36 even pieces. Place each piece, cut side up, in the center of a foil mold. Using your thumb, press and rotate from the center outward, spreading the dough to cover the mold evenly. Press the bottom thin, bring the edges to the rim or slightly above, and spin the mold to ensure uniform thickness.  
7. Mix sugar, heavy cream, milk, egg yolks, and whole egg. Whisk thoroughly until the sugar is completely dissolved. This amount is enough for approximately 15 egg tarts. Adjust quantities as needed.  
8. Pour the egg tart filling into the shells, filling to about 80% full.  
9. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Place the egg tarts inside and bake for about 20 minutes, until the surface sets, puffs up, and develops caramelized spots.  
   - All types of ovens can be used at 200°C. Be careful to avoid excessively high temperatures that might burn the tarts.  
10. Remove the egg tarts from the oven immediately after baking. Let them cool slightly before enjoying — the pastry will be extra crispy.

## Health Advice 🥗
These egg tarts feature delicate, flaky pastry and a rich, creamy flavor but are high in butter and sugar. It is recommended to enjoy 1–2 pieces per serving, paired with unsweetened tea or black coffee to balance the calories. Ideal for afternoon tea, consume in moderation. They are particularly suitable when you need a quick energy boost.
