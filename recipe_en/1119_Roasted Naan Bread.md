# Roasted Naan Bread
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g
🥛 Warm milk 220g (adjust based on flour absorbency)
🥚 Egg 1
🧅 Purple onion appropriate amount (part for oil, part chopped for filling)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for making onion oil)
🍞 Dry yeast 5g
🧂 Salt 5g (for the dough)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for onion filling seasoning)
🧂 Cumin appropriate amount (for onion filling seasoning)
🌱 White sesame seeds appropriate amount (for decorating plain naan)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🍳 Frying pan
🗑 Strainer, bowls
🥣 Large glass bowl
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥄 Measuring cup, spoons
🪚 Rolling pin
🛡 Plastic wrap
🔲 Dough scraper
📜 Baking tray, parchment paper
🍴 Fork
🖌 Basting brush
🧤 Oven mitts
🔥 Oven

## Preparation Steps
1.  Make onion oil: Finely chop some of the purple onion, fry in hot oil over low heat until golden and shriveled. Strain out the onion residue, and let the oil cool.
- Note: The onion must be thoroughly fried to release its fragrance; the oil temperature should not be too high to avoid burning. The oil must be completely cooled, otherwise it will kill the yeast.

2.  Mix dry ingredients: Place 500g flour, 5g salt, and 5g dry yeast in a large bowl. Make a well in the center.
3.  Mix wet ingredients: Add 220g warm milk and 1 egg to the well in the flour. First, stir the liquids together within the well, then drizzle in 50g of the cooled onion oil.
- Key point: The yeast dissolves more easily when it first contacts the liquid; pouring the onion oil over the flour helps the flour insulate the remaining heat.

4.  Knead and rest: Knead the ingredients into a dough. Once there is no dry flour, cover with plastic wrap and rest for 10 minutes. After resting, continue kneading. If the dough is too tight and difficult to knead, cover and rest again for another 10 minutes.
- Note: If the dough feels very elastic, the liquid amount might be a bit low; you can increase the milk next time. Knead until smooth, no need to over-knead.

5.  Fermentation: Cover the kneaded dough with plastic wrap and let it ferment at room temperature for about 20 minutes, until it has puffed up slightly.
- Note: This is a short fermentation; there is no need to let it double in size.

6.  Prepare onion filling: Finely chop another portion of fresh purple onion. Add an appropriate amount of cumin and salt, and mix well.
7.  Divide and shape: Divide the fermented dough into two portions. Roll each into a ball, then flatten into a large round disc.
- Suggestion: If the dough shrinks back significantly, let the small dough balls rest covered with plastic wrap for 5 minutes before shaping, or work on the two pieces alternately.

8.  Transfer and poke holes: Transfer the dough discs to a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Use a fork to evenly poke holes all over the surface to prevent puffing during baking.
9.  Decorate: For plain naan, brush a layer of egg wash and sprinkle with white sesame seeds. For piyaz (onion) naan, brush with egg wash and then spread the onion-cumin-salt mixture. The egg wash helps the toppings stick.
10. Bake: After preheating the oven, place both trays of naan inside simultaneously. Halfway through baking, swap the positions of the top and bottom trays to ensure even heating and browning. Bake until the surface is golden and the edges are crispy.
- Note: Refer to your home oven's standard settings for specific temperature and time, using browning and doneness as a guide.

## Health Advice
This is a high-carb, fragrant and crispy flatbread, suitable as breakfast or a main dish. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables and protein for a balanced meal. Onions are rich in antioxidants, but naan bread itself is not low in calories, so those watching their weight should be mindful of portion sizes.
