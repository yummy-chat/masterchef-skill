# Ginger Soft Candy  
**Cuisine:** Other  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫚 Ginger 500g (use old ginger if you prefer a spicier taste, young ginger if you prefer a milder taste)  
💧 Water 300g (for making ginger puree)  
🌽 Cornstarch 150g  
💧 Water 150ml (for diluting cornstarch)  
🍬 Brown sugar 60g  
🍯 Maltose 280g  
🍚 White sugar or cooked glutinous rice flour, as needed (to prevent sticking)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥤 Blender/High-speed blender  
🧺 Cheesecloth  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
📄 Parchment paper  
🔪 Knife  
🥣 Glass bowl

## Preparation Steps  
1. Peel and slice the ginger, then place it together with 300g of water into the blender. Blend into a ginger puree, pass through cheesecloth to squeeze out the ginger juice, and set the ginger pulp aside for other uses.  
   **Note:** Squeeze as thoroughly as possible during filtering to extract more ginger juice.

2. Mix cornstarch with 150ml of water and stir until completely dissolved with no dry powder. Set aside as a cornstarch slurry.

3. Pour the ginger juice into a non-stick pan, add brown sugar and maltose, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Note:** Since there is a lot of liquid early on, high heat can be used throughout; when dense bubbles appear, reduce to low heat to prevent scorching.

4. Slowly add the cornstarch slurry in batches, stirring quickly and continuously to prevent lumps.

5. After all the slurry is added, switch to medium heat and continue stirring and cooking for about 10 minutes, until the mixture becomes a thick, glossy dark brown paste, with the moisture reduced enough that it holds together without spreading.  
   **Key:** Stir continuously to prevent sticking to the bottom.

6. Place the cooked candy mass on a board lined with parchment paper. Knead and shape it slightly, wrap it in parchment paper, and refrigerate until completely set and firm.

7. Remove the candy block, cut into strips, then into small cubes. Toss them in white sugar or cooked glutinous rice flour to coat evenly and prevent sticking.

## Health Tips  
Ginger soft candy warms the body in winter. Rich in gingerol, it helps dispel cold and promote blood circulation, making it suitable for people with cold hands and feet. However, diabetic patients should control their intake. Note that maltose and brown sugar are high in sugar, so it is best not to consume too much at one time.
