# Fire-Exploded Double Crisp
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork kidneys, about 2 (halved lengthwise, remove the white core)
🍗 Chicken gizzards, about 4–5 (scrape off the inner yellow membrane)
🧅 Scallions, about 2 (cut into sections)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chilies, about 3–4 (sliced diagonally)
🫚 Young ginger, 1 small piece (julienned)
🌶️ Small spicy chilies, about 5–6 (halved lengthwise)
🥬 Celery, about 2 stalks (cut into sections)
🫙 Pickled ginger, to taste (finely chopped)
🌶️ Pickled chilies, to taste (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon, to taste
🌶️ White pepper, to taste
🌾 Dry starch, to taste
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean chili paste), 1 tablespoon
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🍎 Vinegar, to taste
💧 Slurry (water + starch), to taste
🫒 Vegetable oil, to taste
🥄 Lard, 1 tablespoon
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🥄 Ladle
🍽️ Plate / bowl

## Steps
1. Scrape off the inner yellow membrane of the chicken gizzards. Halve the pork kidneys lengthwise and remove the white core. Rinse both main ingredients clean.
2. Score the chicken gizzards in parallel cuts without cutting through, then rotate and cut through to form chrysanthemum cuts. Score the kidneys in parallel cuts, then cut diagonally into rhombus pieces, severing every three cuts to create phoenix-tail kidney flowers.
3. Soak the cut kidneys and gizzards in water. Rinse thoroughly three times, then squeeze out excess water. Place in a bowl, add salt, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and dry starch; toss to coat evenly.
4. Add the chopped pickled ginger, pickled chilies, sliced garlic, Sichuan peppercorns, and doubanjiang to the marinated main ingredients. Mix well and set aside.
5. In a bowl, combine scallion sections, sliced Erjingtiao chilies, julienned young ginger, small spicy chilies, and celery sections. Add chicken bouillon, sugar, vinegar, and slurry; stir to make a sauce mixture.
6. Heat the wok over high heat. Add vegetable oil and lard, heat until about 70% hot. Quickly pour in the mixed main ingredients and stir-fry over high heat, tossing rapidly until they change color and curl.
7. Immediately add the seasoned vegetables and sauce mixture. Continue stir-frying over high heat for about 10 seconds, until the sauce thickens and turns glossy, and the vegetables are just cooked. Turn off the heat and plate immediately.
**Key point**: Maintain high heat throughout; total stir-fry time should be under 1 minute. Work quickly to keep the kidneys and gizzards extremely tender and crisp.

## Health Advice
This Fire-Exploded Double Crisp is rich in high-quality protein but high in cholesterol. It’s best paired with coarse grains and vegetables to balance nutrition. Enjoy occasionally as a treat; those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume in moderation.
