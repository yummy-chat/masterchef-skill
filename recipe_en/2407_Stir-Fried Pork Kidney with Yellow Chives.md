# Stir-Fried Pork Kidney with Yellow Chives
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Pork kidneys 2 (one pair, cleaned of white membrane and scored)
🥬 Yellow chives A small bunch (about 150g, washed, drained, and cut into inch-long pieces)
🧄 Garlic To taste (crushed and minced)
🫚 Ginger To taste (crushed and minced)
🌶️ Pickled chili peppers/small red chili peppers To taste (sliced or minced)
🥄 Broad bean paste 1 tbsp
🛢️ Rapeseed oil To taste (as base oil, slightly more than usual for stir-frying)
🐖 Lard 1 tbsp (for aroma)
🧂 Salt A pinch
🍬 Sugar A pinch
🧂 MSG A pinch
🌿 White pepper A pinch
🌑 Dark soy sauce A pinch (for color)
🦪 Oyster sauce To taste
🌽 Corn starch A small amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧴 Strainer/filter net
🧻 Kitchen paper
🍳 Wok

## Instructions
1. Halve the pork kidneys lengthwise and carefully remove all white membrane, then rinse thoroughly with clean water. Ensure all white membrane is removed, or the odor will be strong.
2. Place the inner side of the kidney facing up, make a series of parallel diagonal cuts, then make perpendicular cross-cuts, cutting into pieces on the third cut. If you are not skilled at scoring, you can also cut into strips, but scoring ensures more even heating and a crispier texture.
3. Place the scored kidney pieces in a bowl, add crushed scallions and ginger along with water, rub and soak for 5 minutes, then repeatedly rinse with fresh water until the water runs completely clear. Drain well.
4. Squeeze the washed kidney pieces dry, then thoroughly pat them dry with kitchen paper. This step is crucial; any residual moisture will cause the kidneys to release water when cooked, preventing a good wok hei and affecting texture.
5. Add salt, sugar, MSG, white pepper, a little dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and an appropriate amount of corn starch to the kidney pieces. Mix evenly with your hands until the surface is dry and slightly sticky.
6. Directly spread the minced garlic, crushed ginger, pickled chili peppers, and one tablespoon of broad bean paste on top of the marinated kidney pieces. Do not stir; they will go into the wok together.
7. Wash the yellow chives and drain thoroughly, then cut into inch-long pieces.
8. Heat the wok over high heat until very hot, add rapeseed oil and lard, and heat until the oil surface emits large smoke. Quickly pour the entire bowl of kidney pieces along with the surface seasonings into the wok. Use a spatula to quickly break them apart, keep the heat high, and stir-fry for about 10 seconds until the kidney pieces are completely changed in color and the scored patterns curl and open.
9. Immediately add the yellow chive segments and continue stir-frying over high heat for about 10 seconds, just until the chives are cooked through. Do not over-stir, as yellow chives will become soft and watery.
10. Stir evenly and immediately remove from heat and plate. The entire process should be quick and continuous, emphasizing the crispy texture and wok hei from high-heat quick stir-frying.

## Health Advice
Pork kidneys are rich in iron and protein but high in cholesterol; those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Yellow chives are rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. When stir-frying, be sure to drain moisture thoroughly to maintain a crispy texture.
