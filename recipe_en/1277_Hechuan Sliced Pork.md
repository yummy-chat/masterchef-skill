# Hechuan Sliced Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons

🌶️ Pickled red chilies  6-8 (seeded and sliced diagonally)  
🧅 Onion 适量 (sliced diagonally)  
🧄 Ginger 适量 (sliced diagonally)  
🧄 Garlic 适量 (sliced diagonally)  
🍶 Yellow wine 适量 (for marinating to remove odor)  
🧂 Salt  about 2g (for marinating) + a pinch (for sauce mix)  
🌿 White pepper 适量 (for marinating and sauce mix)  
💧 Ginger-scallion water 适量 (for marinating)

🌽 Cornstarch 适量 (for thick batter)  
🍲 Chicken broth 适量 (base for sauce, can substitute with stock or water)  
🍬 Sugar  about 4 tablespoons (for sauce mix)  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar  about 50g (for sauce mix)  
🥄 Soy sauce  about 50g (for sauce mix)  
🧂 MSG  a pinch (for enhancing flavor in sauce mix)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🍳 Wok spatula, iron wok  
🥄 Strainer spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the pork front shoulder, then cut against the grain into slices slightly larger and thicker than standard meat slices.  
- **Key**: The slices should be large and thick, cut against the grain.  
2. Cut winter bamboo shoots into slices of similar size to the meat, soak the wood ear mushrooms, seed and slice the pickled red chilies diagonally, slice ginger, garlic, and onion diagonally, and cut garlic sprouts diagonally into sections.  
- **Note**: All ingredients should be sliced diagonally; use generous portions.  
3. Prepare the sauce mix: In a bowl, combine chicken broth, sugar, fragrant vinegar, soy sauce, white pepper, a pinch of salt, and MSG, then stir until blended and set aside.  
4. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add a little yellow wine and salt. First blanch the winter bamboo shoot slices for about 3 minutes, then add the wood ear mushrooms and cook until done. Remove and drain.  
5. Coat the meat slices: Add yellow wine, salt, white pepper, and ginger-scallion water to the meat slices, mix well. Crack in a whole egg and mix again. Then add cornstarch and toss until each slice is coated with a thick batter. Finally, add a small amount of cooking oil to seal in moisture.  
- **Key**: The batter should be thick, use cornstarch.  
6. First frying: Heat ample oil to 20% of its maximum temperature. Gently add the battered meat slices one by one, using chopsticks to separate them. Fry until set and they float to the surface, then remove and drain.  
- **Note**: Keep the oil temperature low, add slices one at a time to prevent sticking.  
7. Second frying: Raise the oil temperature to 70% of maximum. Quickly add the meat slices and re-fry for about 20 seconds until golden and crispy, then remove.  
8. Stir-frying: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Sauté the sliced pickled chilies, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add the blanched winter bamboo shoots, wood ear mushrooms, and fried meat slices. Stir-fry over high heat quickly.  
9. Re-stir the sauce mix and pour it into the wok in 2-3 batches, stirring rapidly each time, so the sauce evenly coats all ingredients. When the sauce thickens, add one drop of dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok for color adjustment.  
10. Add the garlic sprout sections and stir-fry until just cooked. Before serving, sprinkle in sesame oil and toss well.  
- **Key**: Add the sauce in batches to ensure thorough thickening; add garlic sprouts at the end; sesame oil is the final finishing touch.  

## Health Advice  
Hechuan Sliced Pork is high in oil and calories, making it suitable for after exercise or as an occasional treat. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Winter bamboo shoots and wood ear mushrooms provide dietary fiber, but the batter and frying double the calorie content. Individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume it in moderation.
