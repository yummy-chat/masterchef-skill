# Green Pepper, Potato Slices, and Ham Sausage
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes (appropriate amount, peeled and sliced into pieces about 0.3-0.5cm thick)
🌭 Ham sausage (appropriate amount, sliced to similar thickness as the potato slices)
🫑 Green pepper (appropriate amount, torn into pieces by hand or cut diagonally into sections)
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)
🥄 Light soy sauce 2 tablespoons
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt 1 teaspoon
🌿 Thirteen spices powder 1 teaspoon
🫒 Cooking oil (appropriate amount)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Metal spatula
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing the sauce)
🍽️ Serving plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, and thirteen spices powder together to make a sauce; set aside.
2. Heat cooking oil in the pan over high heat, add the potato slices, and fry over medium-low heat for about 5 minutes, until the edges are slightly yellow and the texture becomes soft.
   **Key point**: Fry slowly over medium-low heat to ensure the potato slices are fully cooked inside and have a caramelized fragrance.
3. Add the ham sausage slices and continue frying over medium-low heat for about 2 minutes, until the surface is slightly browned.
4. Add the green pepper and the prepared sauce, turn to high heat, and quickly stir-fry until the green pepper deepens in color and becomes slightly tender.
   **Key point**: Stir-fry quickly over high heat to cook the green pepper while keeping it crisp, and ensure the sauce evenly coats the ingredients.
5. Finally, sprinkle in the minced garlic, stir-fry quickly over high heat for about 30 seconds to 1 minute, until the garlic aroma is released, then immediately remove from the pan.
   **Key point**: Add the minced garlic at the end to avoid burning it, which would make it bitter.

## Health Tips
Potato slices paired with green pepper and ham sausage provide carbohydrates, vitamin C, and protein, making them suitable for quickly replenishing energy. It is recommended to control the intake of ham sausage, as it is a processed meat product with high sodium content; people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids can substitute it with chicken breast slices.
