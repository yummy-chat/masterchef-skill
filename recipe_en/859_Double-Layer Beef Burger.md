# Double-Layer Beef Burger
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef 1000g (choose a cut with less sinew, minced into a coarse, textured paste)  
🧅 Onion half (finely chopped)  
🥬 Lettuce to taste  
🍅 Tomato to taste (sliced)  
🌾 Flour 260g  
🥚 Eggs 3 (2 for the meat filling, 1 for the dough)  
🥛 Whole milk 120ml  
🍚 Yeast 5g  
🍬 Sugar 15g  
🧂 Salt 8g (for the meat filling) + 2g (for the dough)  
🌶️ Black pepper 6g  
🫒 Cooking oil 45g (15g for the meat filling, 30g for the dough)  
🌿 White sesame seeds to taste (for decoration)  
🥗 Salad dressing to taste  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Cutting board  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🫓 Parchment paper or plastic wrap  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔥 Oven  
⭕ Round burger mold (or baking sheet)  
📏 Scraper or dough cutter  
🧊 Refrigerator (for freezing)  

## Steps  
1. **Prepare the beef filling**: Cut the beef into small pieces and mince into a coarse, textured paste. Add 8g salt, 6g black pepper, 15g cooking oil, and 2 eggs. Stir in one direction for 3–5 minutes until the filling becomes sticky and elastic. Then mix in the chopped onion.
2. **Prepare and freeze the patties**: Take about 100g of the meat filling, press it tightly, and shape it into a round patty. Place the patties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, separating each layer with parchment paper. Seal and freeze in the refrigerator to set.
3. **Make the burger bun dough**: Mix 260g flour, 15g sugar, 2g salt, 5g yeast, 1 egg, and 120ml whole milk. Stir into a shaggy dough, then add 30g cooking oil. Knead into a smooth dough. Transfer to a cutting board and continue kneading until a uniform, thick membrane can be stretched.
4. **Shape and proof**: Divide the dough into 6 equal portions. Roll each into a ball, dip in water, then roll in white sesame seeds to coat. Place into round molds or on a baking sheet. Seal and proof until the dough has visibly expanded and the surface is rounded.
5. **Bake the burger buns**: Place the proofed dough into a preheated oven. Bake until the surface is golden brown and fully cooked. Remove from the oven, let cool slightly until easy to handle, then unmold.
6. **Pan-fry the patties**: Add a small amount of oil to a frying pan. Place the thawed or partially frozen patties in the pan. Cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes per side, until fully cooked and a caramelized crust forms.
7. **Assemble the burgers**: Slice the burger buns horizontally. On the bottom half, layer lettuce, squeeze salad dressing, add a beef patty, and top with tomato slices. Repeat this layering for a double-layer burger, then cover with the top half of the bun.

## Health Tips  
Beef burgers are rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making them suitable for muscle building or post-intense activity consumption. Be mindful of the amount of salad dressing used to avoid excess calories. Homemade patties are healthier than takeout, as they allow for reduced salt and additives.
