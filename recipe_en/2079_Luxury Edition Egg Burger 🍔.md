# Luxury Edition Egg Burger 🍔  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Flour 7 units (approx. measuring cup scale marks)  
💧 Warm water 7 units (equal to flour)  
🦐 Shrimp approx. 3–4 pieces  
🥚 Egg 1  
🧈 Yeast appropriate amount (specific amount not mentioned)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🧂 MSG appropriate amount  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder appropriate amount  
🫒 Cooking oil (or butter) appropriate amount  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl/container  
🫙 Plastic wrap/bag  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🫕 Small silicone pan (for semi-frying and semi-steaming)  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Spoon  
🍽️ Plate  
🔪 Knife  
♨️ Induction cooker (or stovetop)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place a piece of plastic wrap in a bowl. Add flour and warm water in a 1:1 ratio, then add yeast, salt, MSG, and thirteen-spice powder. Mix the ingredients evenly within the plastic wrap, knead into a smooth batter, seal the wrap, and let it rest until it ferments and shows honeycomb-like air holes.  
   **Key point**: The batter should have a honeycomb structure inside after fermentation.  

2. Pour the fermented batter into a preheated non-stick frying pan and spread into a round shape. Before the surface of the batter is fully set, quickly cover it with another pan of the same size, using steam to steam the top layer until it becomes firm but still half-cooked.  
   **Note**: This step ensures even heating on both sides, resulting in a fluffy and soft texture.  

3. Add a little oil to the pan and heat. Pan-fry the shrimp until they change color and are cooked through. Next to the shrimp, crack an egg and fry until the egg white is set and the yolk is runny.  

4. Take the pancake from step 2, which is set on one side, and immediately place it over the fried egg and shrimp. Gently press with a spatula to adhere. Once the bottom egg liquid is fully set and bonded with the pancake, flip the whole piece over.  

5. Take another pancake that has been similarly fried until half-cooked, place it over the already flipped semi-finished product. Brush a thin layer of oil on the burger’s surface. Cook over medium-low heat until both sides are evenly golden brown.  
   **Key point**: Ensure the inside is fully cooked, and the outside is crispy without burning. Turn the burger occasionally during frying, and avoid excessive heat.  

6. The finished egg burger has a golden, crispy outer crust. When cut open, layers of egg and shrimp filling are visible. Serve and enjoy while hot.  

## Health Tips  
This egg burger’s fermented dough is easier to digest. The shrimp and eggs provide high-quality protein, making it suitable for breakfast or a snack. Be careful not to use excessive heat to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside undercooked. Shrimp can be replaced with chicken sausage or vegetable bits to increase dietary fiber.
