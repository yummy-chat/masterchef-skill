# Braised Taro and Mixed Vegetable Casserole  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Taro (appropriate amount, steamed with skin on, then peeled and mashed)  
🍠 Sweet potato starch (appropriate amount, added in batches for kneading)  
🥕 White radish (about 1, cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot (about 1, cut into small pieces)  
🧅 Purple onion (about 1, cut into small pieces)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms (appropriate amount, soaked in hot water and finely chopped)  
🍄 Dried wood ear mushrooms (appropriate amount, soaked in hot water and finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)  
🧅 Chopped green onions (appropriate amount, sprinkled before serving)  
🧂 Salt (a small amount, added when kneading taro mash)  
🌶️ Chili powder (appropriate amount, added when marinating vegetables)  
🍶 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount, drizzled when stir-frying vegetables)  
🫒 Cooking oil (appropriate amount, added when stir-frying base ingredients)  
🦪 Oyster sauce (appropriate amount, added after braising is complete)  
🫘 Bean paste (appropriate amount, added after braising is complete)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Cutting board + kitchen knife  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🫕 Large bowl  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥘 Clay pot  
🛍️ Plastic storage bag  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the taro and steam it with the skin on until fully cooked.  
   **Key point**: Steaming with the skin on helps retain flavor and moisture.  
2. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms and dried wood ear mushrooms in hot water in advance.  
   **Key point**: Hot water speeds up the rehydration process.  
3. Cut the white radish, carrot, and purple onion into small pieces, place them in a bowl, add an appropriate amount of chili powder, mix well, and marinate for 10 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Marinating removes the raw taste of the vegetables and enhances flavor.  
4. Finely chop the rehydrated wood ear mushrooms and shiitake mushrooms; mince the garlic.  
5. Peel the steamed taro, place it in a bowl with a small amount of salt, and mash it into a smooth paste using a rolling pin.  
   **Key point**: The taro paste should be as smooth as possible to ensure uniform dough.  
6. Gradually add sweet potato starch to the taro paste, kneading until the dough is no longer sticky and forms a cohesive ball.  
   **Key point**: Add starch in batches to avoid a tough dough; knead until it no longer sticks to your hands.  
7. Pinch off small pieces of dough, roll them into balls, and press with your thumb to form an indentation. Arrange the shaped pieces on a plate; any extras can be stored in a plastic bag in the freezer.  
   **Key point**: Frozen raw pieces can be directly boiled, stir-fried, or used in hot pot.  
8. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the taro pieces, and cook until they float to the surface, then continue for 2-3 minutes. Remove and rinse with cold water, then drain and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Add to boiling water; rinsing with cold water makes the texture chewier.  
9. Heat the clay pot over low heat, add oil, then add the chopped wood ear mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, and minced garlic. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.  
   **Heat**: Medium-low heat to avoid burning.  
   **Doneness**: The ingredients emit a rich aroma, and the garlic turns slightly golden.  
10. Add the marinated radish and onion pieces, stir-fry over medium heat until just cooked, then drizzle in an appropriate amount of light soy sauce.  
    **Heat**: Medium heat for stir-frying.  
    **Doneness**: The vegetables darken in color and release a fragrant aroma.  
11. Layer the cooked taro pieces on top of the vegetables, cover the clay pot with its lid, and braise over medium-low heat for 5 minutes.  
    **Heat**: Medium-low heat for braising.  
    **Key point**: Utilize the clay pot’s heat retention to fully infuse the flavors into the ingredients.  
12. Remove the lid, add an appropriate amount of oyster sauce and bean paste, and mix well to coat the ingredients evenly with the sauce.  
    **Key point**: Mix thoroughly to ensure even seasoning.  
13. Sprinkle with chopped green onions before serving.  

## Health Tips  
The braised taro and mixed vegetable casserole combines taro pieces with a variety of vegetables, offering balanced nutrition and strong satiety. It is especially suitable for those needing to supplement dietary fiber or control calorie intake. Rinsing the cooked taro pieces in cold water makes the texture chewier, but sweet potato starch is not low in calories, so it is recommended to consume in moderation.
