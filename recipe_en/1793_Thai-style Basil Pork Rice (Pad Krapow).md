# Thai-style Basil Pork Rice (Pad Krapow)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Ground pork 300g  
🌿 Holy basil leaves 20g  
🍚 Cooked rice 300g  
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies 2  
🧄 Garlic 3 cloves  
🥚 Eggs 2  

🫒 Cooking oil as needed  
🍶 Shaoxing wine 15ml  
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g  
🐟 Fish sauce 15g  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 15g  
🍬 Sugar 10g  
🧂 Salt a pinch  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Wooden spoon or wok spatula  
🧄 Garlic press  
🍽️ Plate  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Instructions
1. Prepare garlic and chili: Press garlic into a paste, and slice bird's eye chilies into rings. Set aside.
2. Prepare holy basil: Pick the leaves, wash, and drain well.
3. Separate egg whites and yolks: Crack two eggs, placing the whites and yolks into two separate bowls.
4. Aromatics: Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add garlic and stir-fry until fragrant, then add the chili rings and stir-fry briefly.
5. Cook the pork: Add the ground pork. Stir-fry over medium-high heat continuously until all moisture has evaporated, the oil becomes clear, and the pork is broken into distinct pieces.
6. Season: Add Shaoxing wine, oyster sauce, fish sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar. Stir-fry quickly to coat the pork evenly and develop color and flavor.
7. Add holy basil: Just before turning off the heat, add the holy basil leaves and toss quickly until just wilted. Remove from heat.
8. Make crispy-edged, runny-yolk fried eggs: Heat oil in a pan. First, pour in the egg whites, allowing them to puff up quickly and become crispy and set at the edges. Once the whites are mostly set, gently slide both yolks onto the whites. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Use a spoon to baste the hot oil over the yolks until they are slightly set on the surface but still runny inside.
9. Plate: Serve the rice on a plate. Top with the cooked basil pork. Finally, slide the fried egg on top.

## Health Tips  
This basil pork rice has a notable fat content from the pork. It is recommended to pair it with a serving of stir-fried vegetables to balance the nutrition. Fish sauce and oyster sauce are high in sodium; those with high blood pressure may reduce the amount accordingly.
