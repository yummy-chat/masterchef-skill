# Screw Pepper Appetizer
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫑 Screw pepper, to taste (long, wrinkled green pepper, main ingredient)
🧅 Scallion, about 1/3 stalk (cut into rings)
🍬 White sugar, relatively large amount (to taste)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (mainly for coloring)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste (mainly for seasoning)
🧂 Salt, about 1 gram (a pinch, for final seasoning)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Chinese wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board

## Instructions
1. Wash the screw peppers, cut off the stems and tough stems at the top. Gently tap the peppers with the knife blade to slightly crack them, then cut into evenly sized squares.
   **Key point**: Tap with moderate force, do not break them too finely, keep them in chunks for better flavor absorption.
2. Cut the scallion into rings and set aside.
3. Heat the wok, **without oil**, and add the pepper chunks directly. Stir-fry over high heat first. Once the wok is hot and the peppers start releasing water, reduce to **low heat** and continue dry-frying.
   **Note**: Dry-fry until the peppers become soft overall, with moisture significantly reduced, and **brown spots** appear on the surface. This is the core to achieving the "charred aroma" flavor.
4. Drizzle an appropriate amount of cooking oil over the dry-fried peppers and stir-fry evenly.
5. Add the scallion rings and stir-fry with the peppers over high heat until fragrant.
6. Add a **relatively large amount** of white sugar, and stir-fry until the sugar melts and develops a caramelized aroma.
7. Add dark soy sauce and light soy sauce in sequence, and stir-fry quickly and evenly.
8. **Turn to high heat**, continue stir-frying quickly, allowing the remaining liquid in the wok to be fully absorbed by the peppers, blending the flavors. Finally, sprinkle in a **tiny pinch** of salt, stir-fry evenly, and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish uses a generous amount of white sugar to balance the spiciness of the peppers, making it suitable for those who want to stimulate appetite without excessive heatiness. Note the high sugar content; individuals watching their sugar intake should consume less of the sauce.
