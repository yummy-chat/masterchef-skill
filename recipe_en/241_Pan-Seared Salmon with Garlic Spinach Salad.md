# Pan-Seared Salmon with Garlic Spinach Salad
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Salmon fillet (with skin) 1 piece (about 200-250 grams, preferably thick)  
🥬 Spinach 1 bunch (roots removed)  
🍅 Cherry tomatoes a few (mix of colors)  
🧄 Garlic 2-3 cloves (minced)  
🥬 Homemade pickled vegetables适量 (cut into small pieces)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🌶️ Black pepper to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil (olive oil) to taste  
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil a little (for drizzling at the end)  
🌶️ Tabasco sauce to taste (optional)  
🧂 Coarse black pepper to taste (optional)  
🧂 Flaky sea salt to taste (optional)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Frying pan (non-stick)  
🥢 Tongs  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels  
🥄 Spoon  
🍽️ Plate  
🧀 Grater (optional, for making garlic paste)  

## Steps
1.  Prepare the fish skin: Use the back of a knife to scrape the salmon skin repeatedly to remove excess moisture and slime. This is key for achieving crispy skin.
2.   Marinate: Evenly sprinkle salt and black pepper on both sides of the salmon. Let it sit for 20 minutes to absorb flavor and further release surface moisture.
3.   Prepare the side dishes: Mince the garlic, remove the roots from the spinach, and cut the cherry tomatoes and pickled vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
4.   Dry before searing: After marinating, use kitchen paper towels to thoroughly dry the surface of the salmon.
5.   Heat the pan: Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Add a small amount of cooking oil and swirl to coat the bottom evenly.
6.   Sear the skin: Place the salmon skin-side down in the pan. Gently press down with your hand or tongs to ensure the skin fully contacts the pan bottom. Cook over medium heat until the skin is golden and crispy.
7.   Flip and baste: Flip the salmon. Reduce heat to medium-low. Tilt the pan and use a spoon to continuously baste the hot oil from the pan onto the fish skin to keep it crispy. Cook the other side to your desired doneness.
8.   Make garlic spinach: Remove the salmon and set aside. Without washing the pan, add the minced garlic directly to the remaining oil and fry until fragrant. Then add the spinach, sprinkle with a little salt, and quickly stir-fry until the spinach wilts and shrinks. Turn off the heat immediately.
9.   Plate: Spread the cooked spinach on the bottom of a plate. Place the seared salmon on top. Arrange the cherry tomatoes and pickled vegetables on the side.
10. Final seasoning: Drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the salmon for enhanced aroma. Optionally, sprinkle with coarse black pepper, flaky sea salt, or drizzle with Tabasco sauce according to personal preference.

## Health Tips
This pan-seared salmon is rich in Omega-3 fatty acids and high-quality protein, making it suitable for those who use their brains a lot or need to protect cardiovascular health. Spinach and cherry tomatoes provide vitamins and antioxidants. However, pickled vegetables have a high salt content, so individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the amount accordingly.
