# State Banquet Simmered Broth
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 old hen (about three years old, with hard beak and firm leg meat)  
🐖 1 pork knuckle (front or hind leg, either is fine)  
🍗 Some chicken breast (for clarifying the broth by absorbing impurities)  
🐾 Some chicken feet (for adding gelatin when simmering the base broth)  
🥓 Some chicken fat (taken from the chicken, for extra aroma)  
🧅 2 sections of scallion (smacked)  
🫚 2 pieces of ginger (smacked)  
🍶 3-4 liang of yellow wine (about 150-200 ml)  
🧂 2 tablespoons of salt  
🌿 Some ground white pepper  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🧺 Cheesecloth or muslin bag  
🥩 Food processor or knife (for mincing chicken breast)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the chicken's preen gland, claw tips, and lungs, and rinse thoroughly. Scrape the skin of the pork knuckle to remove any surface grease and impurities.  
   **Note**: The chicken lungs must be completely removed, otherwise the broth will turn dark.
2. Smack the scallion and ginger. Trim the chicken breast of any fat and sinew, then chop it into a coarse, grainy mince. Do not grind it into a fine paste.
3. Place the prepared old hen, pork knuckle, chicken feet, and chicken fat into the soup pot. Add enough cold water to fully submerge them.  
   **Key point**: Always start with cold water to preserve the fat and umami within the meat.
4. Simmer over low heat, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface. Continue for about 6 hours, until the broth turns yellow and most of the foam has been removed.
5. Strain the broth through a fine mesh strainer, discarding the bones and solids. This is your base broth.
6. For a creamy white broth: Return the base broth to the pot and add hot water. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat for 40 minutes, until the broth becomes milky white.  
   **Note**: Do not boil for too long, or the broth will lose its flavor.
7. In a separate pot, pour in some of the base broth. Add the smacked scallion and ginger, yellow wine, salt, and white pepper to season.
8. Add the minced chicken breast into the seasoned broth. Stir gently in a clockwise direction to help the meat form a sponge-like structure.
9. Keep the heat low and maintain the broth just below a simmer. Let it clarify for 40 minutes, allowing the meat to fully absorb impurities and fat.
10. When the chicken breast rises to the surface, add a splash of cold water to lower the temperature and continue the absorption process. Repeat this 2-3 times, until the broth is crystal clear.
11. Strain the broth through cheesecloth. You will obtain a clear state banquet broth that is the color of weak tea or light beer.
12. The final clear broth is perfectly transparent and leaves a lingering aroma on the palate, combining the freshness of chicken with the richness of pork.

## Health Advice  
This broth is made by long simmering to extract maximum umami, concentrating the depth of chicken soup and the gelatin from the pork knuckle. It is suitable for those who are recovering from illness, post-surgery, or seeking an ultimate flavor experience. However, it is high in purines and sodium, so those with gout or high blood pressure should consume it with caution.
