# Raw Simmered Meatball Soup
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Skinless front leg pork (fatty and lean mix, more lean than fat)
🫚 Ginger 50g (minced, part reserved for ginger water)
🧄 Garlic 50g (minced)
🧅 Scallion 50g (chopped)
🌰 Sesame powder 20g
🍜 Vermicelli, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)
🥦 Daylily buds, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, appropriate amount (pre-soaked)
🧅 Spring onion, appropriate amount (chopped)
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (minced)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount
🍶 Baijiu, a small amount
🥚 Egg white, appropriate amount
🫒 Cooked soybean oil, appropriate amount
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount
🛢️ Soybean oil 100g
🐖 Lard 50g
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount
🫗 Special aromatic oil, appropriate amount (made by simmering soybean oil, lard, minced ginger, minced garlic, chopped scallion, and sesame powder)
🍖 Bone broth, appropriate amount (boiling hot)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Large cutting board
🔪🔪 Chef's knives (two)
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🥣 Large bowl
🍵 Small bowl
🧤 Gloves

## Preparation Steps
1. Slice the skinless front leg pork. First, chop with the blade until the fat and lean are well combined. Then repeatedly pound the meat filling with the back of the knife to break the sinews until sticky and glutinous. Finally, finely chop with the blade. This technique gives the meatballs a springy texture.
**Key Point**: Pounding with the back of the knife is crucial for the springy texture; make sure to break all sinews.

2. Mince the ginger. Soak some minced ginger in water to make ginger water; set aside.

3. Add soybean oil and lard to the wok, heat until melted. Add minced ginger, minced garlic, and chopped scallion, stir-fry over low heat until slightly yellow. Then add sesame powder, stir evenly, turn off the heat, remove, and let cool to make the special aromatic oil. Stir-fry slowly over low heat throughout to prevent burning. Use after cooling.
**Key Point**: Stir-fry slowly over low heat to avoid scorching, which would affect the aroma.

4. Add salt, white pepper powder, and a small amount of baijiu to the minced meat. Gradually add ginger water while stirring in one direction until the mixture is firm and sticky. Add egg white, stirring and slapping the mixture until well combined. Then add cooked soybean oil and cornstarch, stirring until the meat filling is thick and elastic.
**Key Point**: Add ginger water in batches; slap the mixture to develop a gelatinous texture.

5. Heat water in the wok until it just starts to simmer. Use hands and a spoon to shape and drop the meatballs into the water. Do not stir immediately after adding; wait until the meatballs set and float, then gently push them. Once all meatballs have floated, reduce the heat to low and cook until fully done, with a springy and tender texture.
**Key Point**: The water must not boil; keep it at a gentle simmer to prevent the meatballs from breaking apart.

6. Place pre-soaked vermicelli, daylily buds, and wood ear mushrooms at the bottom of a bowl. Add the meatballs, and sprinkle with chopped spring onion. Add salt, white pepper powder, chicken powder, and an appropriate amount of special aromatic oil. Pour in the boiling hot bone broth to instantly release the aromas.
**Key Point**: The bone broth must be boiling vigorously; pouring it quickly helps release the fragrances.

7. Sprinkle with minced cilantro and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This raw simmered meatball soup is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, suitable for those needing an energy boost. It is recommended to control the amount of special aromatic oil to reduce fat intake. The meatballs, developed through repeated slapping, have a springy texture and high digestibility. However, ensure daylily buds and wood ear mushrooms are fully soaked to avoid gastrointestinal discomfort.
