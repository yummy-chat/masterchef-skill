# Hot Spring Bean Sauce Fish 🐟

**Cuisine**: Sichuan
**Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐
**Season**: All year round

## Instructions
1.  Clean the fish and place it in a bowl. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, cooking wine, and salt. Rub evenly and marinate for up to 30 minutes.
2.  Rinse the fish thoroughly under water. Trim the fins with scissors and score both sides of the fish with a knife.
3.  Mince the white parts of the scallions and chop the green parts. Mince the ginger and garlic separately.
4.  Combine the Yin bean paste, regular bean paste, and chopped pickled Erjingtiao peppers. Chop the mixture together until well blended.
5.  Bring water to a boil in a deep pot, then reduce to low heat. Add salt and white vinegar. Gradually stir in water starch to create a thin, slightly thickened soup. Gently lower the fish into the soup and cook over low heat, maintaining a gentle simmer, for 5 minutes.
*Key point*: The starchy liquid coats the fish, preventing it from breaking apart and helping it hold its shape.
6.  Heat a wok over high heat and add a base layer of oil. Stir-fry the pork belly slices until they release oil and edges turn golden brown. Add minced scallion whites, ginger, and garlic; stir until fragrant. Reduce heat to low and add the blended bean paste mixture. Stir-fry slowly over low heat until red oil is released.
*Note*: The bean paste must be stir-fried slowly over low heat to prevent burning and bitterness.
7.  Splash light soy sauce and yellow wine along the edge of the wok. Sprinkle in white pepper. Add enough hot water. Season with a small pinch of salt and slightly more sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for 3 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
8.  Remove the cooked fish and transfer it to the red braising liquid in the wok. Maintain medium heat. While cooking, continuously ladle the sauce over the top of the fish for 5 minutes to ensure even flavor.
9.  Carefully transfer the fish to a serving plate. Add dark soy sauce to the remaining sauce in the wok to achieve a red, glossy color. Thicken with water starch, cooking until the sauce coats a spoon. Sprinkle in the chopped scallion greens and finish with a drizzle of aromatic vinegar. As soon as the vinegar aroma rises, turn off the heat immediately. Pour the thickened sauce evenly over the fish.
*Key point*: Add the aromatic vinegar at the very end and remove from heat promptly to retain a delicate vinegar fragrance without excessive sourness.

## Health Tips
The “Hot Spring” cooking method keeps the fish exceptionally tender, making it suitable for those with weak digestion or those aiming to control oil intake. However, the broad bean paste and pork belly are high in salt, so individuals with hypertension should consume the sauce sparingly.
