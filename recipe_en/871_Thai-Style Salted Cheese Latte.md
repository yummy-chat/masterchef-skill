# Thai-Style Salted Cheese Latte
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍡 Brown Sugar Boba 1 spoon
🧀 Cheese Buttermilk 110 grams
🧊 Ice Cubes 55 grams (for cup), 250 grams (to cool tea)
🍂 Ceylon Black Tea (granulated) 25 grams
💧 Hot Water 600 grams (just boiled)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥤 Clear Plastic Cup/Glass
🥄 Spoon (for measuring brown sugar boba, stirring)
🥄 White Scoop (for measuring ice cubes)
🥛 Marked Plastic Measuring Cup
🫗 Glass Measuring Cup/Pitcher
🔍 Strainer
🫖 Electric Kettle

## Instructions
1. Place 25 grams of granulated Ceylon black tea in a container, pour in 600 grams of boiling water, cover and let steep for 8 minutes. At the 4-minute mark during steeping, uncover and stir once to help the tea release its color.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to uncover and stir once at the 4-minute mark during steeping to help the tea release its color.
2. After steeping, strain out the tea leaves with a strainer, add 250 grams of ice cubes to the tea, and quickly cool for later use.
3. Take the serving cup, add 1 spoon of brown sugar boba to the bottom of the cup.
4. Pour in 110 grams of cheese buttermilk.
5. Add approximately 55 grams of ice cubes.
6. Finally, slowly pour 270 grams of the cooled Thai-style black tea along the inner wall of the cup to create a layered effect.
   **Key Point**: The choice of cheese buttermilk and Ceylon black tea directly affects the flavor; strictly adhering to the proportions is key to replicating the taste.

## Health Advice
This beverage is rich in cheese and brown sugar, with relatively high calories, suitable for occasional summer consumption and not recommended in excess. It is advisable to control sugar intake, and those with diabetes should consume with caution.
