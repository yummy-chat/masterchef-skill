# Whole Wheat Steamed Buns
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Whole wheat all-purpose flour 500g
💧 Warm water 250-300g (about 35°C, not hot to the touch)
🍄 Yeast 5g
🍬 White sugar 5g
🧂 Baking soda No more than 10g
🌾 Dry flour (dusting flour) As needed (for preventing sticking)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍞 Dough board/kneading mat
🫙 Plastic wrap
🔪 Dough scraper
🍲 Steamer
🥟 Steaming rack
🧻 Steamer cloth (gauze)
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1.  Add white sugar, baking soda, and yeast in sequence to the warm water, stirring until completely dissolved to create a yeast mixture. The water temperature should be controlled at around 35°C to avoid deactivating the yeast.
2.  Pour the yeast mixture into the whole wheat flour in three batches, stirring with chopsticks as you pour until the mixture becomes flocculent, ensuring the water and yeast are evenly distributed.
3.  Knead the flocculent mixture into a dough. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and place it in a warm environment around 35°C to ferment until the dough doubles in size and has a uniform honeycomb structure inside.
4.  Sprinkle a suitable amount of dry flour on the work surface, take out the fermented dough, and knead it vigorously to release the gas until the surface is smooth.
5.  Roll the dough into a long strip and use a dough scraper to divide it into equal-sized small pieces. Cover them with a damp steamer cloth to prevent the surface from drying out.
6.  Gently knead each piece one by one, skillfully shaping it into a smooth ball, then roll it on the work surface into a tall bun blank to leave room for it to flatten during proofing.
7.  Heat water in the steamer to about 40°C. Line the steaming rack with a steamer cloth that has been wetted and wrung out until it doesn't drip. Place the bun blanks on the rack, cover with the lid, and let them proof for a second time until they are about 1.5 times their original volume. They should spring back slowly when gently pressed.
8.  After the second proofing is complete, turn the heat to high directly. Once steam starts to rise, continue steaming over high heat for 15-20 minutes.
9.  After turning off the heat, let the buns rest in the covered steamer for 3-5 minutes before opening the lid. Open the lid quickly and tilt it to the side to prevent condensation from dripping onto the bun surfaces and causing them to collapse.

## Health Tips
Whole wheat steamed buns retain more dietary fiber and B vitamins than white steamed buns, providing a stronger feeling of fullness. They are suitable for people managing blood sugar or losing weight. Note that the water temperature for the yeast should not exceed 35°C. After steaming, let the buns rest in the steamer for a few minutes before opening the lid to prevent collapsing.
