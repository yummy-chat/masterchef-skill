# Cola Tiger Skin Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 8  
🥤 Cola about 1 can (approx. 330ml)  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce to taste  
🦪 Oyster Sauce to taste  
🍅 Ketchup to taste  
🫒 Cooking Oil as needed (for frying and as base oil)  
🧅 Scallions to taste  
🧄 Ginger to taste  
🧄 Garlic to taste  
💧 Water (for boiling eggs)  
🧊 Ice Water (for cooling)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
⏱️ Timer (or phone)  
🥄 Slotted Spoon  
🥄 Wok Spatula or Ladle  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. **Cook Soft-Boiled Eggs**: Place eggs in cold water, heat over medium-high heat until boiling, then start timing. Continue cooking over medium-high heat for 6 minutes. Immediately remove and transfer to ice water to cool thoroughly, then carefully peel the shells.  
   **Key Point**: Precise timing is crucial for the soft yolk; rapid cooling in ice water keeps the yolk semi-liquid.  

2. **Fry the Tiger Skin**: Pour enough oil into the wok and heat until very hot. Add the peeled eggs, quickly cover the lid to prevent oil splatter, and fry over medium heat until the egg surface turns golden with uniform wrinkled patterns. Remove and drain excess oil. Set aside.  
   **Key Point**: Oil must be hot; covering the lid ensures safety during frying. The tiger skin forms when high heat instantly wrinkles the egg white surface.  

3. **Prepare the Sauce**: Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, heat over medium heat, and sauté the chopped scallions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and ketchup in order, stirring quickly to combine and release the flavors.  

4. **Braise with Cola**: Pour cola into the sauce, turn to high heat, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, add the fried tiger skin eggs, then simmer over medium heat for about 30 seconds to let the eggs fully absorb the sauce.  

5. **Thicken and Serve**: Continue heating until the sauce thickens and evenly coats each egg. Before serving, garnish with chopped scallions or white sesame seeds as desired.  

## Health Advice  
Cola Tiger Skin Eggs feature soft-boiled eggs fried for a crispy exterior and tender interior, but the combination of cola and frying makes them high in calories. It is recommended as an occasional indulgent dish, paired with light vegetables to balance nutrition. This dish suits those who enjoy a sweet-savory flavor, especially younger people. Diabetics and individuals needing to control sugar intake should consume with caution.
