# Cartoon Cookie Base
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧈 Unsalted Butter 100g (softened to a paste-like consistency)
🍚 Powdered Sugar 60g
🥚 Room-Temperature Egg 36g (about 1 egg, beaten into egg liquid)
🌾 Cake Flour 215g
🍫 Cocoa Powder 10g (for chocolate flavor)
🍵 Matcha Powder 5g (for matcha flavor)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl/Ceramic Bowl
🥄 Silicone Spatula
🔍 Sieve
🥢 Rolling Pin
🧁 3D Printed Cookie Cutters or Other Cartoon Molds
♨️ Oven or Air Fryer
📦 Plastic Wrap/Parchment Paper
🍪 Baking Sheet/Air Fryer Basket
🌬️ Perforated Baking Mat (placed on the baking sheet for flatter cookie bottoms)

## Instructions
1. Cut the butter into small pieces and let it soften naturally at room temperature above 20°C until it reaches a paste-like consistency that can be easily pressed with a spatula.
   **Note**: If the room temperature is low, you can assist softening by placing the bowl over warm water, but do not overheat and melt the butter.
2. Sift the powdered sugar into the softened butter and mix thoroughly with a spatula. No need to cream the butter.
3. Add the egg liquid to the butter mixture in 3–4 batches, mixing well after each addition before adding the next, until the mixture is smooth and creamy with no separation of oil and water.
   **Note**: If separation occurs, place the bowl over hot water and stir briefly to help it come together.
4. Sift the cake flour into the mixture and use a spatula to press or fold until combined, forming a soft, smooth dough with no dry flour visible.
   **Note**: Do not over-knead, as this can develop gluten and affect the texture.
5. Place the dough on plastic wrap, shape it roughly into a rectangle, and roll it out into an even sheet about 0.5 cm thick. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for about 15 minutes until firm, making it easier to cut shapes.
6. Remove the dough sheet and lightly dust the surface with a little flour to prevent sticking. Dip the cookie cutter in flour and press out shapes. Arrange the cookie pieces on a baking sheet lined with a perforated mat or parchment paper.
   **Note**: If the dough becomes too soft, return it to the refrigerator before continuing. Leftover scraps can be re-formed, rolled out, frozen, and used again, but avoid repeating this too many times to prevent puffing during baking.
7. Bake in a preheated oven or air fryer.
   **Baking Reference**: Oven in convection mode at 160°C for about 15 minutes; conventional mode at 150°C for about 20 minutes; air fryer at 150°C for about 17 minutes. Bake until the edges are lightly golden and the color is even.
8. Once baked, remove and let cool naturally on the baking sheet. The cookies should become crisp after cooling. Break one open to check that the inside matches the outside color and is completely dry with no soft center. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for about one month.

## Health Tips
Using softened butter instead of creaming it results in a crumblier cookie that holds its shape well, making it ideal for making cartoon shapes with children. Avoid over-kneading the dough, and refrigerating for 15 minutes before cutting helps maintain clean edges.
