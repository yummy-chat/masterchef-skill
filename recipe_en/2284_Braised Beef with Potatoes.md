# Braised Beef with Potatoes
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket/beef short ribs appropriate amount (choose the belly part with sinew and fat for a soft and sticky texture)
🥔 Potatoes appropriate amount (peeled and cut into roll-cut chunks)
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (cut into chunks)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections, used for color before removing from heat)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (sprinkled after removing from heat)

🧂 Rapeseed oil half a tablespoon (heated)
🧈 Lard half a tablespoon
🌶️ Dried chilies one handful (three types: for aroma, for color, for heat, used separately; part for frying seasoning oil, part for making sticky chili)
🫘 Broad bean paste appropriate amount (finely minced)
🌶️ Sticky chili appropriate amount (made by boiling the above dried chilies until soft, then finely mincing)
🫑 Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch
🍬 White sugar a small pinch
🍶 Fermented rice wine appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise a few pieces
🌿 Cinnamon appropriate amount
🌰 Sand ginger appropriate amount
🍂 Black cardamom appropriate amount
🌱 Bay leaves a few pieces
🧂 Soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 MSG appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🌫️ White pepper powder appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking beef)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer spoon
♨️ Pressure cooker (optional, can also simmer slowly in a soup pot)
🥄 Soup spoon/cooking spoon
🫙 Small bowl

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the beef brisket into large chunks, soak in clean water for about thirty minutes, then remove and drain once the blood water seeps out.
2. Pour the three types of dried chilies into a pot of boiling water and cook until softened, then remove and finely mince to make sticky chili; separately, finely mince the broad bean paste and set aside.
3. Slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, crush the garlic, cut the onion into chunks, and peel and cut the potatoes into roll-cut chunks.
4. Place the beef in cold water in a pot, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove and rinse thoroughly with warm water. Be careful not to rinse with cold water to prevent the beef from tightening and becoming tough.
5. Add half a tablespoon of heated rapeseed oil and half a tablespoon of lard to the wok. Add sliced ginger, scallion sections, crushed garlic, and onion chunks. Fry over low heat until the ginger slices shrivel and the scallion sections turn yellow, creating the seasoned oil.
6. Add a handful of dried chilies and a small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns to the seasoned oil. Continue to stir-fry over low heat until the dried chilies develop a smoky aroma and turn a deep reddish-brown. Use a strainer spoon to remove and discard all the solids, retaining the seasoned oil.
7. Use the remaining seasoned oil to stir-fry the drained beef over medium heat until the surface moisture evaporates.
8. Add the finely minced broad bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until fragrant and the red oil emerges. Add a small pinch of white sugar to balance the spiciness, then add the sticky chili. Continue to stir-fry over low heat until a rich aroma and a bright red color develop.
9. Pour enough boiling water into the wok, add the fermented rice wine, then add star anise, cinnamon, sand ginger, black cardamom, and bay leaves. Once the water boils, transfer everything to a pressure cooker. After steam builds up, cook under pressure for 18 minutes.
10. After pressure cooking, remove and discard the spices. Add the potato chunks and continue to cook until the potatoes are fully tender.
11. Pour the beef and potatoes back into the wok. Increase the heat to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, add MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper powder, and soy sauce to season. Just before removing from heat, add the green and red chili pepper sections and stir-fry quickly. Plate and sprinkle with cilantro.

## Health Advice
Braised beef with potatoes is rich in high-quality protein and carbohydrates. However, the Sichuan cooking method involves high oil and sodium content. It is recommended to pair it with stir-fried vegetables to balance the meal. This dish is suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure, but people with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their portion size.
