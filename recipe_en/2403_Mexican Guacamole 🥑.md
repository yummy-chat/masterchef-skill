# Mexican Guacamole 🥑  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Season: All-year-round  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥑 2 ripe avocados (choose ones that yield slightly to gentle pressure)  
🍅 A small amount of tomato (deseeded and diced, about one spoonful, to be mixed in later)  
🧅 Some purple onion (finely minced)  
🧄 A little garlic (finely chopped)  
🌿 Some cilantro (finely chopped)  
🌶️ Some green chili pepper (finely chopped, can substitute with jalapeño)  
🍋 1 lime (juiced, about half to one whole lime)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
⚫ Black pepper, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🫗 Mortar and pestle  
🥄 Spoon  
🍴 Fork  

## Instructions  
1. Finely mince the onion, garlic, cilantro, and green chili pepper. Cut the tomato in half, use a spoon to scoop out the inner seeds and pulp, then dice the flesh into small cubes. Set aside about one spoonful.  
2. Cut the avocados lengthwise around the pit, twist gently to separate, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a mortar.  
3. Immediately squeeze the juice of half to one lime evenly over the avocado flesh.  
   **Key point**: Add the lime juice right away to prevent the avocado from oxidizing and turning brown, while adding a fresh sour note.  
4. Use the pestle to mash the avocado into a chunky paste with some texture.  
5. Add the reserved tomato cubes, minced onion, garlic, cilantro, and chopped chili to the mashed avocado.  
6. Sprinkle with salt and black pepper to taste, and gently mix with a spoon or fork until combined.  
   **Note**: Do not overmix; keep the layers between the ingredients and the avocado paste. The final texture should be a thick, creamy paste dotted with colorful mix-ins.  

## Health Tips  
Avocados are rich in monounsaturated fats, which are beneficial for cardiovascular health. Pairing them with lime juice and tomato adds vitamin C. Suitable for those on a weight-loss or fitness regimen, but consume in moderation—no more than half an avocado per serving, and watch overall calorie intake.
