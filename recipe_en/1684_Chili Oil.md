# Chili Oil
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Dried Tiepihong (Beijing Red) Chili: About 1/6 of total
🌶️ Dried Ghost Pepper: About 1/4 of total
🌶️ Dried Erjingtiao Chili: Remaining portion mixed with Lantern Pepper in a 1:1 ratio
🌶️ Dried Lantern Pepper: Remaining portion mixed with Erjingtiao in a 1:1 ratio
🫘 Beijing Red Chili Powder: To taste
🧂 Green Sichuan Pepper: To taste
🧂 Red Sichuan Pepper: To taste
🧅 Star Anise: 2 segments
🫚 Ginger: To taste (cut into thick slices)
🧄 Garlic: To taste (crushed)
🧅 Scallion: To taste (cut into sections)
🧅 Onion: To taste (shredded)
🥬 Cilantro: To taste
🫒 Rapeseed Oil: 2 times the weight of chili powder
🫒 Flaxseed Oil: To taste
🧂 White Sesame Seeds (roasted): To taste
🧂 Salt: To taste (slightly more)
🍬 White Sugar: To taste
🧂 Songxianxian (or chicken essence/MSG): To taste

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Wok
⚙️ Blender or Grinder
🌡️ Thermometer
🥄 Strainer
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🪣 Sieve
✂️ Kitchen Scissors
🔪 Cutting Board

## Preparation Steps

### Preparation Phase
1. Mix the four types of dried chilies—Tiepihong, Ghost Pepper, Erjingtiao, and Lantern Pepper—according to the specified ratio.
   **Key Point**: A single type of chili cannot simultaneously achieve the ideal color, aroma, and spiciness.
2. Wash the mixed dried chilies, drain them, and cut them into small sections using kitchen scissors.
   **Key Point**: Wash before cutting to avoid losing chili seeds, which would affect the final aroma.
3. Heat the wok, add a little oil, then add the chili sections. Turn off the heat immediately and use residual heat to stir-fry continuously until the chili sections become crispy and break easily when pressed.
   **Key Point**: Avoid continuous heating to prevent burning. An oven or air fryer can be used instead; the goal is to remove moisture for easier grinding and better aroma extraction.
4. Place the dried chili sections into a blender and grind into a moderately fine chili powder.
   **Key Point**: For a brighter red color, add a small amount of Beijing Red Chili Powder to the chili powder.
5. Soak green Sichuan pepper, red Sichuan pepper, and star anise in warm water for 5–10 minutes. Cut ginger into thick slices, crush garlic, cut scallion into sections, shred onion, and wash cilantro; set aside.
   **Key Point**: Soaking dry spices in water makes them release aroma more easily and prevents them from burning during frying.

### Cooking Phase
1. Pour the mixed rapeseed oil and flaxseed oil into the wok. Add ginger slices, garlic cloves, scallion sections, shredded onion, and the soaked and drained Sichuan pepper and star anise to the cold oil. Heat over high heat. When the scallion, onion, and other aromatics are about 70–80% dry, add cilantro. Continue frying until all aromatics turn deep brown and are well colored, then turn off the heat.
   **Key Point**: Turn off the heat when the aromatics are "colored"; do not wait until they start to smoke. Let the aromatics soak in the oil after turning off the heat. Once the oil has cooled naturally, remove all aromatics thoroughly using a strainer.
2. In a stainless steel bowl, combine the mixed chili powder with salt, white sugar, Songxianxian, and roasted white sesame seeds. Stir to blend.
3. Reheat the strained oil to 200°C. Scoop one spoonful of hot oil and pour it into the chili powder, stirring quickly to combine. Repeat this process several times, pouring and stirring until the chili powder and oil reach a moist, evenly saturated state.
   **Key Point**: Use high temperature to release the aroma.
4. Lower the oil temperature to below 160°C, then pour all the remaining oil into the chili powder at once. Stir well.
   **Key Point**: Lowering the oil temperature releases the spiciness and prevents the chili powder from burning.

## Health Advice
This chili oil is suitable for mixing into noodles or as a dip for dumplings, but it has a high salt content. People with high blood pressure should control their intake. The variety of chilies used means vitamin C retention is relatively low; it is recommended to pair with fresh vegetables to balance nutrition.
