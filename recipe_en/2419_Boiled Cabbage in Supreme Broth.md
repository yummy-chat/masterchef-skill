# Boiled Cabbage in Supreme Broth
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork hocks 2 pieces (approximately 4000g, preferably one front hock and one rear hock)
🐔 Black-footed free-range chickens 2 (approximately 3000g, preferably aged over one year, remove innards, lymph, chicken fat, and claws, then split in half)
🦆 Ducks 2 (approximately 3500g, older ducks are more aromatic, clean and chop in half)
🥓 Jinhua ham 1 small piece (approximately 2000g, cut into chunks, wash and soak to remove oil and salt)
🧅 Scallions as needed (cut into long sections)
🫚 Ginger 150g (crushed)
🥬 Napa cabbage 1 head (use only the tender yellow heart)
🥩 Pork loin 800g (for making the meat puree used to clarify the broth)
🐔🦆 Chicken/duck breast meat as needed (removed from the whole chickens and ducks, used for clarifying the broth)
🫐 Goji berries 1 piece (for final garnish)
🧂 Edible salt as needed (for blanching and broth-making; use cautiously as the ham is salty)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chinese chef’s knife
🪵 Round wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowls
🪣 Large stockpot (energy-saving pot)
🪜 Steaming rack or stainless steel stand
🔥 Stove
🥄 Soup ladle, slotted spoon
🧻 Cheesecloth or fine-mesh strainer
🥤 Food processor
♨️ Steamer

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the meats**: Cut the pork hocks open from the middle. Clean the chickens and ducks, removing innards, lymph, chicken fat, and toenails, then split them in half. Before blanching, remove the chicken and duck breast meat and set aside for clarifying the broth.
2. **Prepare the ham**: Chop the Jinhua ham into small chunks. Wash with hot water to remove surface oil, then soak in fresh water for 40 minutes to remove excess salt.
3. **Prepare aromatics**: Cut the scallions into long sections. Crush the ginger with the side of a knife.
4. **Make the clarifying meat puree**: Remove any sinew and fat from the chicken/duck breast meat and pork loin. Cut into small pieces, place in a food processor, add four times their weight in water, and blend until a very fine puree forms. The finer the puree, the better it absorbs impurities when clarifying the broth.
5. **First blanch the meats**: Place the pork hocks, chickens, and ducks in a large pot. Add enough water to cover completely and bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Once boiling, add the ham chunks. Continue boiling, then skim off all the foam. Remove all ingredients and rinse clean with fresh water.
6. **Make the base broth**: Place a steaming rack or stainless steel stand at the bottom of the pot to prevent scorching. Add all the blanched meats. Add 30kg of fresh water, along with the scallion sections and ginger chunks. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to low heat, maintaining a very gentle simmer. Cook for 4 hours. After 1 hour, remove the scallions and skim off the surface fat.
7. **Clarify the broth into a supreme clear soup**: Strain the base broth to remove all solids and let cool to about 60°C. In three separate stages, starting with the darkest meat puree and ending with the lightest, add the puree to the broth. After each addition, gently heat over low flame until it just comes to a simmer. When the meat puree coagulates into a “raft” and floats to the surface, having absorbed impurities from the broth, remove the raft with a slotted spoon and filter the broth through cheesecloth or a fine-mesh strainer. Repeat this process three times until the broth is as clear as water.
8. **Blanch and shape the cabbage**: Bring water to a boil and add salt. Blanch the cabbage heart pieces until just cooked through. Immediately remove and plunge into ice water to cool. Then squeeze out the excess water.
9. **Steam for flavor infusion**: Arrange the cabbage hearts on a plate. Pour a generous amount of the clarified, clear broth over them. Cover tightly with plastic wrap. Place in a steamer and steam over high heat for 5 minutes.
10. **Plating and garnish**: Remove from the steamer. The dish can be served immediately, or you can re-arrange the cabbage into a lotus shape. Pour piping hot, clear broth over the cabbage one more time, and place a single goji berry in the center for garnish. Serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. The clear broth removes any greasiness, making it suitable for people managing their weight or those with weaker digestion. However, the purine content is high, so people with gout should consume it with caution.
