# Shrimp and Edamame Sauce
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 River shrimp 250g (heads removed, squeeze out the mud sac)
🫘 Edamame beans 400g
🥩 Pork belly appropriate amount (skin removed, cut into small dices)
🫘 Dried tofu appropriate amount (choose soft texture, diced, blanched in advance)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers appropriate amount (finely chopped, added at the end for color and fresh spiciness)
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (smashed)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount
🫙 Jin Caidi multi-flavor sauce 250g (or substitute with soybean paste; no other seasonings needed after adding)
🫗 Sesame oil 1 tablespoon (drizzled in before serving)
💧 Water appropriate amount (enough to almost cover the ingredients)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥣 Bowl/container

## Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a pot, blanch the dried tofu dices for about 2 minutes, then remove and drain well to eliminate the bean smell and maintain a tender texture.
2. In another wok, add sufficient rapeseed oil, coat the wok, then add the pork belly dices. Stir-fry over medium heat until the fat renders out fully, leaving the lard in the wok without removing it.
3. Add the smashed ginger and minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant, then immediately pour in the entire box of multi-flavor sauce. Reduce to low heat and stir-fry for about 1 minute to develop a rich sauce aroma.
   **Key point**: After adding this sauce, no additional salt or other seasonings are needed.
4. Add all the prepared edamame beans, headless shrimp, and blanched dried tofu dices into the wok. Stir-fry evenly to coat all ingredients with the sauce.
5. Add water until it almost covers the ingredients. Bring to a boil, then cover the wok with a lid, reduce to low heat, and simmer for 10 minutes.
6. Remove the lid, increase to high heat to reduce the sauce until it thickens and appears glossy. Then sprinkle in the chopped green and red chili peppers, drizzle in one tablespoon of sesame oil, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, suitable for those needing to replenish energy and boost immunity. Note that both edamame beans and shrimp contain purines, so individuals with gout should consume in moderation.
