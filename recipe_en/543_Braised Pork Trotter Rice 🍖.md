# Braised Pork Trotter Rice 🍖
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork trotters (3, ask the butcher to chop into pieces beforehand)
🫚 Ginger (a few slices)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections)
🍚 White rice (appropriate amount)
🥚 Egg (1, hard-boiled for braising)
🍶 High-proof white liquor (a small amount)
🫒 Cooking oil (about 20g)
🍬 White sugar (about 60g)
🌿 Cinnamon stick (half)
⭐ Star anise (2)
🍃 Bay leaves (2)
🌱 Cumin seeds (a small pinch)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (2)
🟤 Sichuan peppercorns (a small pinch)
🍾 Cooking wine (2 tablespoons)
🧂 Soy sauce (2 tablespoons, either light or dark soy sauce can be used)
💧 Water (appropriate amount, for blanching and braising)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🍳 Wok (or use the stockpot as a substitute)
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Tongs
🪵 Spatula

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the pork trotters in a pot of cold water. Add ginger slices and scallion sections. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to medium heat and continue cooking for 15–20 minutes. Remove the trotters and drizzle with a small amount of high-proof white liquor while hot to remove any gamey smell.
   **Note**: Drizzling with high-proof white liquor while hot is more effective at removing the gamey smell.
2. In a separate pan, add the cooking oil and white sugar. Stir continuously over medium-low heat until the sugar melts, forms dense small bubbles, and turns a date-red color. Immediately remove from heat.
   **Key Point**: Removing from heat prevents the caramel from continuing to cook and becoming bitter.
3. Add the drained pork trotters to the pan while they are still hot. Quickly stir-fry using the residual heat to evenly coat them in the caramel color.
4. Add the cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, cumin seeds, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, ginger slices, cooking wine, and soy sauce. Pour in enough water to fully submerge the ingredients.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover with a lid and simmer slowly for about 2 hours.
   **Note**: Add enough water all at once; try to avoid adding more water during the process.
6. Insert chopsticks into the thickest part of a trotter. If they can easily pierce through, the trotters are tender. For a fall-off-the-bone texture, you can extend the simmering time appropriately.
7. Remove the trotters. While hot, debone them and chop into small pieces. The hard-boiled egg can be added to the pot during the second half of the braising process to be cooked together.
8. Serve a bowl of rice. Top with the chopped trotter meat and the braised egg. Pour a spoonful of the braising sauce over the top.

## Health Advice
Pork trotters are rich in collagen, making them suitable for those needing to improve skin elasticity and joint nutrition. However, they are high in calories and fat. It is recommended to eat them with vegetables and control portion sizes. People with high blood lipids or those on a diet should not consume them in large quantities.
