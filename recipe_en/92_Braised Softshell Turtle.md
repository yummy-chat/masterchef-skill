# Braised Softshell Turtle
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐢 Softshell turtle (approx. 1, slaughtered and cleaned, old skin and yellow fat removed, cut into large pieces)
🧄 Old ginger (适量, cut into chunks)
🧄 Garlic (适量, peeled into whole cloves)
🧅 Scallion tails (适量, tied into knots)
🫒 Cooking oil (适量)
🧂 Soy sauce (适量, drizzled along the pan edge after pan-frying the turtle)
🍶 Erguotou (Baijiu, 50g)
🧂 Salt (a pinch)
🍬 Sugar (a pinch)
🦪 Oyster sauce (more, to enhance freshness)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (a few drops, for color)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥢 Spatula/Chopsticks
🥄 Ladle
🥣 Stainless steel bowl

## Step-by-Step Instructions
1. Slaughter and clean the softshell turtle. Scald with 80°C hot water, then peel off the old skin. Remove any yellow fat and other fishy parts, then cut into large pieces.
2. Cut the old ginger into chunks. Peel the garlic into whole cloves. Tie the scallion tails into knots. Set aside.
3. Pour cooking oil into a frying pan and heat over medium heat. First, stir-fry the ginger chunks until fragrant, then add the garlic cloves and stir-fry until the surface turns slightly charred brown. Remove the ginger and garlic, leaving the base oil in the pan.
4. Using the base oil over medium heat, pan-fry the turtle pieces until the skin contracts and turns slightly charred brown. Use the oil infused with the fried ginger and garlic to reduce fishiness and enhance aroma.
5. Turn the heat to high. Drizzle soy sauce along the edge of the pan, allowing the high heat to caramelize the soy sauce. Immediately pour in 50g of Erguotou, and ignite the alcohol to let it burn off, removing the fishy smell while retaining the liquor aroma.
6. Add enough boiling water to cover the ingredients. Return the previously removed ginger chunks, garlic cloves, and scallion knots to the pan.
7. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Add a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar, more oyster sauce, and a few drops of dark soy sauce to adjust the flavor and color.
8. Simmer until the sauce reduces and thickens. Periodically use a ladle to spoon the sauce over the turtle pieces, ensuring they are evenly coated.
9. Before finishing the reduction, remove the scallion knots. Once the sauce is thick, glossy, and evenly coats the turtle pieces, remove from heat and serve.
   *If you prefer spiciness, you can add dried chili peppers during the simmering process.*

## Health Advice
Braised softshell turtle is rich in collagen and high-quality protein, suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing nourishment. Those with high cholesterol should consume in moderation. Softshell turtle is cold in nature; those with a cold spleen and stomach can balance it by pairing with ginger and garlic.
