# Pork Skin Aspic
Cuisine: Dongbei (Northeastern Chinese)  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 Seasonings  
- Salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, white vinegar, cooking wine  

🧄 Spices  
- Star anise, Sichuan pepper, Chinese spice blend, angelica root, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaves, fennel seeds, scallion, ginger  

🥩 Main Ingredients  
- Pork skin  
- Water  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🍳 Pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  
❄️ Refrigerator  

## Steps
1. Cut the pork skin into pieces no larger than 10 cm, and soak in clean water for 12 hours.
**Key point**: Thorough soaking removes blood and reduces gamey smell.

2. Place the pork skin in cold water in a pot, add 10 g ginger and 50 ml cooking wine, blanch for 5–10 minutes, then immediately transfer to cold water.
**Key point**: Starting with cold water fully draws out impurities; the cold water bath firms the skin.

3. Place the blanched pork skin on a cutting board, trim off all the inner fat, cut into strips no thicker than 3 mm, and put them in a bowl.
**Key point**: Remove the white fat completely for a crystal-clear finished product.

4. Add 20 g white vinegar and 5 g salt, vigorously scrub for 3 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with clean water.
**Key point**: Scrubbing with vinegar and salt thoroughly eliminates odors and prevents cloudiness.

5. In a pot, add 4 kg water, the pork skin, 10 g scallion, 10 g ginger slices, 10 g star anise, and 5 g Sichuan pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a low simmer and cook for 90 minutes until the skin is tender.
**Key point**: Slow simmering fully releases collagen.

6. Season with 8 g salt, 10 g MSG, 15 g chicken bouillon, 50 ml light soy sauce, and 20 ml dark soy sauce. Pour into a plate, remove the scallion, ginger, and star anise, and let cool until it sets into a jelly-like consistency.
**Key point**: Remove the spices after seasoning to avoid a bitter aftertaste.

7. Refrigerate. When serving, cut into small cubes or thick slices.

## Health Advice
Pork skin aspic is rich in collagen and suitable for nourishing the skin in winter, but it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume no more than one small bowl per serving. Those with weak digestion should eat sparingly to avoid greasiness and bloating.
