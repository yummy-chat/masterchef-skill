# Shanghai Scallion Pancake
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 240g (200g for dough, 40g for oil pastry)
🐷 Lard 20g (for dough, can substitute with vegetable oil)
💧 Water 100g
🧅 Scallions about 300g (100g for scallion oil, 200g for filling)
🥓 Pork leaf fat 100g (freeze then dice into rice grain-sized pieces)
🫒 Cooking oil 100g (actually use 60g after frying scallions)
🧂 Salt 4g (1g for dough, 3g for oil pastry)
🌿 Five-spice powder 0.5g
🌾 Dry flour as needed (for dusting)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
🧻 Paper towel
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🥖 Rolling pin
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (cast iron)
🥄 Slotted spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Glass bowl
🍴 Dough scraper
🔥 Oven, baking rack
📄 Parchment paper

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**: Mix 200g all-purpose flour with 20g lard. Dissolve 1g salt in 100g water, then gradually add to the flour while stirring until it forms a shaggy dough. Knead into a smooth dough, cover, and let rest for 30 minutes.
**Note**: Different flours absorb water differently, so adjust the water amount as needed. Do not over-knead; the dough will become smooth after resting.

2. **Fry scallion oil**: Cut about 100g scallions into segments. Place in a pan with 100g cooking oil and fry over low heat until the scallions turn golden and crispy. Remove the scallions, and measure out 60g of the scallion oil for later use.
**Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning. The fried scallions absorb oil, so use the specified amount of oil.

3. **Make the oil pastry**: In a bowl, combine 40g all-purpose flour, 3g salt, and 0.5g five-spice powder. Pour in 60g hot scallion oil and stir into a smooth paste.
**Note**: Be careful of splattering when adding the hot oil.

4. **Prepare the filling**: Finely chop the remaining 200g scallions and mix well. Dice the frozen 100g pork leaf fat into rice grain-sized pieces and toss to separate.
**Note**: Freezing the pork fat makes it easier to dice. Toss immediately to prevent sticking.

5. **Roll out the dough**: Sprinkle dry flour on the work surface. Lightly flour both sides of the rested dough and roll it into a large rectangle about 50cm long and 40cm wide, keeping the thickness even.
**Note**: Dust the surface underneath generously with flour to prevent sticking. Be careful not to tear the dough.

6. **Spread filling and roll**: Evenly spread the oil pastry over the dough, sprinkle with the diced pork fat, then cover with chopped scallions. Roll the dough tightly from one end into a long log. Pinch the seam to seal, and gently press to release any trapped air.
**Note**: Distribute the filling evenly and roll tightly to prevent it from falling out.

7. **Divide and shape**: Cut the log into 5 to 6 equal pieces. Take one piece, pinch both ends closed to prevent filling from leaking, place it upright on the work surface, and gently press and rotate with your palm to form a round flat cake.
**Note**: **Do not use a rolling pin**. Pressing and rotating with your hand prevents the dough from tearing. If scallions peek out at the edges, pinch them back in.

8. **Pan-fry**: Add enough oil to a flat-bottomed pan over medium heat, then reduce to medium-low. Place the pancake in the pan and gently press it so the oil contacts it evenly. Fry for about 1 minute, then flip. Continue flipping every 30 seconds to 1 minute, frying for about 4 minutes total, until both sides are golden and crispy.
**Note**: Maintain medium-low heat throughout to avoid burning the outside while leaving the inside undercooked. Use sufficient oil for a crispy texture.

9. **Bake**: Bake the fried pancakes in a preheated 220°C oven for about 5 minutes.
**Note**: This step mimics the “fry then bake” technique, making the exterior extra crispy and the interior softer. If you don’t have an oven, simply extend the frying time by 1–2 minutes.

10. Serve hot for a crispy crust, soft interior, and rich scallion and lard aroma with layered textures.

## Health Advice
The soul of scallion pancakes lies in the combination of lard and scallion aroma, but it is high in calories. Pair with plain tea to cut the richness. This dish is ideal for those who love a crispy texture; those with weak digestion should eat in moderation.
