# Hand-Kneaded Pumpkin Cream Cheese Toast
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍞 Bread flour 250g  
🎃 Old pumpkin 75g pumpkin puree for dough + 100g pumpkin chunks for filling (baked with skin on)  
🥚 Whole egg liquid 30g (plus a little extra for brushing)  
🥚 Egg yolk 8g  
💧 Water 67g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (softened to paste)  
🧀 Cream cheese 40g (softened)  
🧫 High-sugar tolerant fresh yeast 7.5g (or dry yeast 2.5g, must be diluted with water first)  
🍬 Caster sugar 25g  
🥛 Condensed milk 8g for dough + 10g for filling  
🧂 Salt 2.5g

## Tools & Equipment
🔥 Oven  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🍴 Dough scraper  
🥢 Rolling pin  
📦 450g loaf pan  
🧤 Baking gloves  
🕸️ Cooling rack

## Instructions
1. Slice the old pumpkin (with skin on) into thin pieces and roast in the oven until the bottom is lightly charred to enhance aroma; after roasting, mash 75g into pumpkin puree, and set aside the remaining 100g as filling.
2. In a bowl, mix the whole egg liquid, egg yolk, water, 8g condensed milk, pumpkin puree, and caster sugar, stirring until smooth as a liquid mixture.
3. On a work surface, mix the bread flour and salt together, make a well in the center, pour in the liquid mixture, stir with a dough scraper until a shaggy mass forms with no dry flour, then knead by hand into a dough. **Note: Do not add yeast at this stage to prevent premature fermentation in summer conditions.**
4. Round the dough, place it in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow gluten to develop naturally and control dough temperature.
5. Remove the chilled dough and roll it out, pinch the fresh yeast into small pieces and sprinkle over the dough, then add the softened butter, wrap it up, and gently roll it out with a rolling pin. Using a dough scraper, chop, fold, and chop again to fully incorporate the yeast and butter into the dough. **Key: This method is less strenuous than using hands and ensures even distribution.**
6. On the work surface, use a combination of pushing, kneading, and slapping motions for about 5-6 minutes, until the dough is smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky. **Keep dough temperature below 26-28°C.**
7. Round the dough, cover the bowl, and let it rest for 5 minutes. Tear off a small piece and gently stretch it; it should be thin, stretchy, and resistant to tearing (windowpane test).
8. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment at room temperature for about 1 hour, until doubled in size. Press a finger dusted with flour into the dough; the indent should not spring back or collapse.
9. While the dough is fermenting, prepare the pumpkin cream cheese filling: Mix the softened cream cheese, roasted pumpkin chunks, and 10g condensed milk until uniform. **Key: Use roasted pumpkin only; avoid steamed pumpkin, which makes the filling too wet and creates large air pockets in the baked bread.**
10. Remove the dough, gently press to release gases, divide it into two equal portions, round each piece, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for about 25 minutes.
11. Take one rested dough piece and roll it into a tongue shape, pat out bubbles on the edges, flip it over (smooth side down), spread half of the pumpkin cream cheese filling evenly in the center, roll it up naturally from the top, and pinch the seam to seal. Repeat with the other piece.
12. Place the two rolls seam-side down side by side in a 450g loaf pan. Let it ferment in a warm, humid place until it reaches 80-90% full.
13. Brush the surface evenly with whole egg liquid and sprinkle a moderate amount of pumpkin seeds on top for decoration.
14. Preheat the oven, then place the pan inside and bake. Reference temperatures and times: For a San Neng low-sugar loaf pan, top heat 160°C, bottom heat 210°C, bake for 30 minutes; for a Highberg convection oven, 165°C, bake for 24 minutes. Adjust according to your own oven.
15. Immediately unmold after baking and cool completely on a cooling rack. The final texture is as tender as tofu, and pulling it apart reveals strings.

## Health Tips
Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene and dietary fiber. Paired with calcium from cream cheese, it is suitable for nourishing the body and improving complexion in autumn. However, sugar and condensed milk content is relatively high. For those controlling sugar intake, reduce the amount or use zero-calorie sweetener instead. The high-sugar tolerant yeast dough requires careful temperature control; refrigerated resting not only saves effort but also prevents rapid fermentation.
