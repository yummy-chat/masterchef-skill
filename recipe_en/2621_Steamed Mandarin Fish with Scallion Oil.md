# Steamed Mandarin Fish with Scallion Oil  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Ingredients  
- Mandarin fish  
- Scallions  
- Red chili peppers  
- Ginger  

🧂 Seasonings  
- Cooking wine  
- Vegetable oil  
- Salt  
- Steamed fish soy sauce  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen Tools  
- Steamer basket  
- Steamer pot  
- Chopping board  
- Iron wok  
- Plastic plate  
- Plastic basin  
- Disposable gloves  
- Kitchen paper  
- Fish steamer plate  
- Chef's knife  
- Peeler  
- Heat-resistant plate tongs  
- Heat-resistant gloves  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Scrape the scales off the fish skin, and use kitchen paper to wipe away the blood and black membrane inside the belly.  
2. Scrape the fish skin back and forth several times with a chef's knife to remove slime, then rinse both the exterior and interior with running water.  
3. Pat dry the exterior and interior of the fish with kitchen paper. Place the fish with the head facing left, and make a vertical cut every 3 centimeters starting from the gill edge, cutting down to the backbone. Repeat on the other side.  
4. Place the processed fish into a dry basin.  
5. Peel and wash the ginger, then slice it into 3 mm thick pieces.  
6. Wash and remove the stems from the red chili peppers, then cut them into 2 mm thick small rounds or 1 mm wide strips.  
7. Wash the scallions and remove the root ends, then cut them into 3 cm long sections. Split thicker sections lengthwise in half.  
8. Add 8 g of salt and 25 g of cooking wine to the basin. Wearing gloves, massage the fish for 1 minute to ensure the seasonings are evenly applied.  
9. Insert 1 slice of ginger into each cut, place 3 slices of ginger inside the belly, and marinate for 10 minutes.  
10. While marinating, bring 5 liters of water to a boil in the steamer pot and place the steamer basket on top.  
11. Discard the liquid, ginger slices, and cooking wine from the marinating process. Rinse the fish inside and out with running water, then pat dry with kitchen paper.  
12. Lay the fish flat on the fish steamer plate, and reinsert ginger slices into the cuts and belly.  
13. Place the fish steamer plate into the steamer basket, cover with the lid, and steam over medium heat for 20 minutes.  
14. After removing from heat, keep the fish broth in the plate.  
15. Using heat-resistant tools, take out the fish plate and drizzle 10 g of steamed fish soy sauce over it.  
16. Scatter scallion sections and red chili peppers on top.  
17. Pour 15 g of vegetable oil into an iron wok and simmer over medium-low heat for 5 minutes.  
18. Slowly and evenly pour the hot oil over the fish, then serve.  

## Health Tips  
Mandarin fish is low in fat and high in protein. Steaming preserves nutrients, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss diet or needing high-quality protein. Note that marinating time should not exceed 10 minutes to prevent the fish from becoming tough.
