# Old Beijing Meat and Vegetable Noodles in Gravy
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak wood ear mushrooms, daylily buds, and deer horn seaweed in clean water in advance. Wash and slice button mushrooms and shiitake mushrooms. Cut scallions into sections and slice ginger. Beat eggs into egg liquid. Mix starch with water to make a slurry and set aside.
2. Place the whole piece of pork belly in cold water, add yellow wine, and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, maintain high heat and carefully skim off the foam with a ladle until the broth is clear. After skimming, add star anise, ginger slices, and scallion sections. Reduce to medium-low heat and continue cooking for about 20 minutes.
**Judgment**: When a chopstick can easily pierce through the pork without any blood seeping out, it is ready to remove.
   **Key**: The foam must be completely skimmed off, and the original broth should not be discarded.

3. Remove the pork belly, let it cool slightly, and slice it against the grain into pieces about the thickness of a one-yuan coin. Use a slotted spoon to remove the scallions, ginger, and star anise from the broth, retaining the clear liquid.
**Note**: When slicing the meat, cut against the grain for a tender texture.

4. Bring the original broth to a boil over high heat. Add the sliced meat, then gradually add the daylily buds, wood ear mushrooms, button mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, and deer horn seaweed. Add soybean soy sauce, salt, MSG, and light soy sauce. Stir well, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook briefly to allow the flavors of the ingredients to meld into the broth.
5. Keep the broth at a gentle simmer. Slowly pour the starch slurry into the pot while stirring continuously, until the broth thickens and coats the ladle, and the starch is evenly gelatinized.
6. Pour in the beaten egg liquid in a circular motion. Do not stir immediately; let the egg liquid naturally set. Then gently push it along the edge of the pot with the back of a ladle to form large, cloud-like egg clusters.
7. In a separate small pot, heat oil until it reaches about 80% hot. Add Sichuan peppercorns and quickly fry until fragrant, removing from heat as soon as the peppercorns change color.
8. Immediately pour the fried Sichuan peppercorns and hot oil over the gravy. Listen for a sizzling sound, then stir quickly to combine. The old Beijing-style gravy for noodles is now complete.
**Key**: Pouring the hot oil in instantly releases the aroma of the Sichuan peppercorns, which is the soul of this gravy.

9. Place the cooked noodles in a bowl, ladle the freshly made gravy over them, and mix well before serving.

## Health Suggestions
This dish offers balanced nutrition: pork belly provides protein, while wood ear mushrooms and daylily buds are rich in dietary fiber. It is suitable for those needing energy replenishment, such as individuals with high physical exertion. Be mindful of the amounts of pork belly and Sichuan pepper oil used. People with high blood pressure should consume less of the gravy broth.
