# Braised Gluten Balls Stuffed with Pork
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Ground pork 400g (preferably with a balance of fat and lean from pork belly)
🧅 Scallion 30g (chopped)
🫚 Fresh ginger 5g (grated into a paste)
🥚 Egg 1
🍞 Oil gluten balls 1 bag
⭐ Star anise 1 (large) or 2 (small)
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece (about the size of a fingernail)
🍬 Rock sugar 2 pieces (about the size of a thumb)
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder or five-spice powder 1/3 teaspoon
🌶️ White pepper powder a pinch
🧂 Chicken bouillon 1 teaspoon
🍬 Granulated sugar 1/2 teaspoon
🫗 Light soy sauce 4 tablespoons (2 tablespoons for the filling + 2 tablespoons for braising)
🫗 Dark soy sauce 2 tablespoons (1 tablespoon for the filling + 1 tablespoon for braising)
🦪 Oyster sauce about 1 and 2/3 tablespoons (1 tablespoon for the filling + 2/3 tablespoon for braising)
🫒 Sesame oil 1/3 tablespoon
🌽 Cornstarch 6g (mixed with 30g water to make a slurry)
⚪ White sesame seeds, toasted as needed (for garnish)
🧅 Scallion, chopped as needed (for garnish)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🍲 Clay pot or serving bowl
🥢 Spatula or chopsticks
🥄 Small spoon

## Cooking Steps
1.  Finely chop the scallion into tiny pieces. Grate the ginger along its fibers to make a smooth paste. Process both as finely as possible to ensure a smooth texture in the filling without any grittiness.
2.  In a large bowl, combine the ground pork with the chopped scallion, ginger paste, the egg, thirteen-spice or five-spice powder, white pepper powder, chicken bouillon, granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce, and finally drizzle in the sesame oil. Stir vigorously in one direction for about 1 minute, until the meat mixture becomes sticky, stringy, and forms a cohesive ball.
**Note**: Light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce are all salty, so no additional salt is needed for the filling.
3.  Take one oil gluten ball. Use your finger to poke a small hole in it. Using your fingertip, gently scrape the inside to create a pocket by hollowing it out, being careful not to tear the outer skin.
4.  Pour cold oil into a wok or pan. Add 2 slices of ginger, 3 sections of scallion, the star anise, and the cinnamon stick. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry until fragrant, until the scallions are slightly wilted and yellowed, then reduce the heat to medium-low.
5.  Using chopsticks, stuff each hollowed gluten ball with the prepared meat filling until 80-90% full. Place all the stuffed balls, hole-side down, into the pan. Keep the heat at medium-low and pan-fry until the meat filling at the opening is set and sealed, preventing the filling from coming out during subsequent braising.
6.  Once the openings are sealed, reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Add 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce, and 2/3 tablespoon of oyster sauce. Then pour in hot water until it reaches about halfway up the gluten balls. Add the rock sugar for glaze and sweetness, if desired. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and simmer for 15 minutes.
**Key point**: About 7-8 minutes into simmering, use chopsticks to gently turn the gluten balls over for more even flavor absorption.
7.  After braising, turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Use chopsticks to remove and discard the ginger slices, scallion segments, star anise, and cinnamon stick from the pan. Stir the prepared cornstarch slurry again, then while maintaining high heat, drizzle it into the pan while gently pushing the gluten balls with a spatula. Once the sauce becomes thick, glossy, and coats the gluten balls, immediately turn off the heat.
8.  Transfer the stuffed gluten balls to a clay pot or serving plate. Garnish with toasted white sesame seeds and chopped scallion. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Braised gluten balls stuffed with pork are substantial in texture and rich in protein and fat, suitable for those needing muscle gain or energy replenishment. However, they are high in sodium and fat; individuals with high blood pressure or those on a weight loss diet should consume them in moderation.
