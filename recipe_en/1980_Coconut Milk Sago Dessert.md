# Coconut Milk Sago Dessert  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥥 Coconut milk - appropriate amount (store-bought or homemade)  
🫧 Sago pearls - 150g  
🥥 Mango - appropriate amount (diced)  
🐉 Dragon fruit - appropriate amount (diced)  
🍬 White sugar - appropriate amount (optional, add to taste)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Wooden spoon  
🕳️ Strainer  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔥 Induction stove/gas stove  
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps  
1. Boil enough water in a pot, and add the sago pearls once the water reaches a rolling boil.  
2. Cook over high heat for 20 minutes, stirring continuously with a wooden spoon to prevent clumping. Observe the white center of the sago pearls: it will be prominent after about 8 minutes, shrink after 15 minutes, and be very small after 20 minutes.  
3. Once the white center becomes tiny, turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let it sit for 8 minutes to allow residual heat to make the pearls fully transparent.  
4. Drain the sago pearls, rinse them repeatedly under cold water until the water runs clear, and drain thoroughly.  
5. Pour the sago pearls into the coconut milk, add diced mango and dragon fruit, and mix well. Add white sugar to taste if desired. Refrigerate for a better flavor.

## Health Suggestions  
Coconut milk sago dessert is an ideal summer refresher—moderate in calories but requires sugar control. Pairing it with fresh fruit boosts vitamin intake. The white center of the sago must be fully softened through steaming for a chewy texture. Diabetic individuals can reduce sugar or use sugar substitutes.
