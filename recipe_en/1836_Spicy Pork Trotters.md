## Preparation Steps
1.  Burn the pig trotters with a blowtorch until the skin is charred black. Soak in clean water, scrape off the burnt skin with a knife, rinse thoroughly, and chop into pieces.
   **Key Point**: Burning removes hair and reduces gamey odor; scraping must be thorough.
2.  Place the pig trotter pieces in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat to blanch. Skim off any foam, then remove and drain.
3.  Transfer the blanched pig trotters to a pressure cooker. Add ginger slices, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, yellow rice wine, light soy sauce, rock sugar, and water. Then add three tablespoons of salt for a base flavor.
   **Note**: Cover the lid. Once steam is released, reduce to low heat and pressure cook for 18 minutes. If using a regular pot, braise for at least 90 minutes.
4.  While the trotters are cooking, crush the green and red chili peppers with the back of a knife, then chop them. Mince the garlic and cut the garlic leaves into sections.
5.  Heat a little oil in a separate wok. When the oil is slightly warm, add the minced ginger and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add two tablespoons of chopped chili sauce and stir-fry until aromatic.
6.  Pour the pressure-cooked pig trotters along with an appropriate amount of the cooking liquid into the wok. Increase heat to reduce the sauce, and add dark soy sauce for color.
   **Key Point**: Pig trotters are rich in collagen; stir constantly during sauce reduction to prevent sticking.
7.  Taste and adjust seasoning by adding salt, MSG, and white pepper powder as needed. Reduce the sauce until it is thick and evenly coats the pig trotters.
8.  Add the chopped green and red chili peppers. Briefly heat them through; do not cook for long to preserve their fresh, spicy flavor.
9.  Just before serving, sprinkle in the garlic leaves. Quickly stir-fry until well combined, then plate.
