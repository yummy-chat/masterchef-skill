# Dried Squid and Steamed Pork Patty
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (skin removed, moderate fat-to-lean ratio)
🦑 Dried squid (soaked in advance, reserve soaking water)
🥬 Water chestnuts (finely diced)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms (finely diced)
🥚 1 egg
🧅 Scallions (chopped)
🧂 Salt, to taste
🍬 Sugar, to taste
🌶️ White pepper, to taste
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste
🥜 Peanut oil, to taste (used in batches)
🫗 Light soy sauce, to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍽️ Steaming plate
🔥 Steamer or wok
♨️ Stove

## Instructions
1. Tear the dried squid apart, rinse off the white impurities on the surface, and soak in 40–50°C (104–122°F) warm water until softened. After soaking, cut into small dice. Reserve the squid soaking water.
2. Finely dice the water chestnuts and the rehydrated shiitake mushrooms.
3. Remove the skin from the pork belly. First slice it thinly, then cut it into strips, and finally chop into small pieces.
4. Using two knives, mince the pork pieces into a filling, chopping about 42–45 times to achieve a mixture that is both cohesive and retains some texture.
5. Add salt, sugar, and white pepper to the pork filling. Mix well with your hands, then repeatedly slap the mixture against the bowl until it becomes sticky, cohesive, and springy.
6. Gradually add the reserved squid and mushroom soaking water, continuing to slap the mixture so the filling fully absorbs the liquid.
7. Add cornstarch and mix well. Then add the diced water chestnuts, shiitake mushrooms, and squid. Gently combine until evenly distributed.
8. Brush a thin layer of peanut oil onto the base of a steaming plate. Spread the pork filling evenly on the plate, pressing a small indentation in the center.
9. Bring water to a boil in a steamer. Place the plate of meat mixture inside and steam over medium heat for about 4 minutes.
10. After 4 minutes, crack 1 egg into the indentation in the center of the patty. Cover and steam for another minute until the egg is set.
11. Remove the steamed patty from the steamer. Drizzle with a few drops of peanut oil and an appropriate amount of light soy sauce. Sprinkle with chopped scallions before serving.

## Health Advice
Pork belly paired with dried squid provides a dual boost of protein and minerals, but it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with leafy greens to balance nutrition. Suitable for those needing an energy boost; those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their portion size. When steaming, use less oil and salt.
