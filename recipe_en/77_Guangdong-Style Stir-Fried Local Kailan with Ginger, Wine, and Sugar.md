# Guangdong-Style Stir-Fried Local Kailan with Ginger, Wine, and Sugar
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥬 Local kailan (Chinese broccoli) to taste (remove old leaves, trim tough root ends, cut tender stems and leaves into three sections)  
🫚 Old ginger to taste (part crushed and juiced, part sliced or crushed for stir-frying)  
🍶 Shao wine or rice wine a small amount (for making ginger-wine juice)  
🍬 White sugar to适量 (for blanching water)  
🧄 Garlic to taste (crushed)  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🍮 Brown sugar (broken sugar or slab sugar pieces) to taste  
🥃 Ginger-wine juice to taste (mixture of old ginger juice and rice wine)  
🫗 Maggi seasoning sauce a little  
🥓 Lard to taste (for stir-frying base oil)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Small bowl  
🥢 Wok spatula  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the kailan**: Remove excess large leaves, leaving only a few tender ones. Cut off about 0.5–1 cm of the tough root end. Cut the tender stem and top into three evenly sized sections.  
   **Note**: Use only the tender and crisp parts; cut into long sections to maintain texture and presentation.  

2. **Make the ginger-wine juice**: Take an appropriate amount of old ginger, crush it, and place it in a small bowl. Pour in a little shao wine and a small amount of water. Use your hands to squeeze the crushed ginger firmly to extract the juice. Strain to obtain the ginger-wine juice and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Old ginger is strongly flavored; hand-squeezing is essential to fully release the aroma. The ginger-wine juice is the "soul" seasoning of this dish.  

3. **Blanch**: Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add an appropriate amount of white sugar. Add the kailan and blanch over high heat for about 1 minute, until just cooked through. Quickly remove and drain.  
   **Key point**: Adding sugar removes bitterness and enhances crispness and sweetness. Do not blanch for too long.  

4. **Aromatics**: Heat lard in the wok until melted. Add the crushed old ginger and garlic. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until golden brown and fragrant.  
   **Note**: Lard provides more aroma. Make sure to fully release the fragrance of the aromatics.  

5. **Stir-fry**: Turn the heat to high. Add the blanched kailan and stir-fry quickly. Add salt and brown sugar (broken sugar) in sequence, stirring to combine.  
6. **Finish**: Drizzle the ginger-wine juice and a little Maggi seasoning sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly to combine evenly, then immediately remove from the heat and serve.  
   **Key point**: Drizzling along the edge of the wok releases the aromas of the wine and sauce. Stir-fry quickly over high heat to maintain the "wok flavor" (wok hei).  

## Health Tips  
This dish of stir-fried kailan with ginger, wine, and sugar uses lard and ginger-wine juice to enhance flavor, resulting in a crisp, sweet taste with a hint of spiciness. It is suitable for warming the stomach and driving off cold. It is recommended to control the amount of brown sugar and soy sauce to avoid excessive sodium and sugar intake. Although lard adds flavor, people with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
