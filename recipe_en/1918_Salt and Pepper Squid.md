# Salt and Pepper Squid
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦑 Fresh squid 2 pieces
🧅 Onion 1/4 (minced)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili 2-3 (sliced into rings)
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (minced)
🥚 Egg 1/2
🌽 Cornstarch as needed
🍚 Glutinous rice flour as needed
🧂 Salt and pepper powder as needed (used twice)
🍶 Yellow wine as needed (for marinade and cooking)
🫚 White pepper powder as needed
🍬 Sugar 1 pinch
🧂 Salt do not add extra (salt and pepper powder contains salt)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Spatula

## Instructions
1. Rinse the squid under clean water, peel off the outer purple membrane, squeeze out the eyes from the head, split open to remove the inner black membrane and cartilage, and wash clean.
2. Lay the squid flesh flat with the inside facing up. Score diagonally at approximately a 45-degree angle, cutting about 2/3 deep without slicing through. Turn over and score in the opposite direction to create a diamond pattern. Cut into bite-sized pieces. Cut the tentacles and tail into small pieces as well.
3. Mince the garlic and onion, and slice the bird's eye chili into rings.
4. Place the squid pieces in a bowl. Add salt and pepper powder, half an egg, yellow wine, white pepper powder, and a pinch of sugar. Mix well. Then add cornstarch and glutinous rice flour, tossing until each piece is evenly coated. Marinate for 2-3 minutes.
**Note**: Salt and pepper powder already contains salt, so no additional salt is needed; glutinous rice flour is key for crispiness.
5. Heat oil to about 160°C (320°F). Add the squid pieces and fry over high heat for about 30 seconds, until golden brown and set. Remove and drain on a slotted spoon.
**Key point**: Quick frying over high heat locks in moisture and creates a crispy shell.
6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Sauté the minced garlic, minced onion, and sliced bird's eye chili until fragrant. Pour yellow wine along the edge of the wok to enhance aroma, then turn off the heat immediately.
**Note**: Add the squid and seasoning only after turning off the heat, using residual heat to toss, to prevent the squid from becoming tough.
7. Add the fried squid pieces. Sprinkle with an appropriate amount of salt and pepper powder. Toss quickly and evenly to coat the squid with seasoning and aromatics. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Salt and pepper squid is relatively high in calories and sodium. It is recommended for occasional consumption only. Individuals with high blood pressure should consume with caution. Pairing with a vegetable salad can help balance the greasiness. The frying time must be strictly controlled to ensure optimal texture.
