# Soy-Marinated Peppers
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ 3 large green peppers
🌶️ 1 large red pepper
🌶️ A few Erjingtiao peppers (omit if you don't like spicy)
🫚 1 large piece of tender young ginger
🧄 A few cloves of garlic
🌿 A few sprigs of scallions
🌸适量 Sichuan green peppercorns
⭐ 1 star anise
🧂 1 tablespoon (about 4g) of table salt (for first marination) + about 10g (for the sauce)
🍬 A small handful of sugar (about 30g)
🍶 25g Baoning vinegar
🍾 350g light soy sauce
💧 600g purified water
🦪 25g oyster sauce (add after removing from heat)
🫒 A little bit of oil

## Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 2 stainless steel bowls
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spoon
🥘 Slotted spoon (optional)

## Instructions
1. **Prepare peppers and aromatics**: Remove the stems from the green and red peppers. Make one horizontal and one vertical cut in the middle of each pepper, being careful not to cut all the way through. Cut the Erjingtiao peppers in half lengthwise. Slice the young ginger. Slice the garlic. Smash the scallions and cut into sections.
2. **First marination**: Place the prepared peppers, ginger slices, garlic slices, and scallion sections in a bowl. Add 1 tablespoon of salt and a small handful of sugar. Mix thoroughly by hand, then let sit for 2 hours to marinate.
   - **Key point**: This step aims to remove the raw, pungent flavor; use less salt and more sugar.
3. **Prepare the sauce base**: In another bowl, add the Baoning vinegar, light soy sauce, purified water, and about 10g of salt. Stir well with a spoon.
4. **Boil the sauce**: Pour the prepared sauce base into a wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately remove from heat. Add the oyster sauce and stir to combine. Set aside.
   - **Note**: Do not heat the oyster sauce to preserve its freshness.
5. **Fry the infused oil**: Heat the wok over high heat, then add a little bit of oil. When the oil is about 50% hot (150°C/300°F), add the Sichuan green peppercorns and star anise. Reduce heat to low and fry slowly until fragrant. Pour the infused oil into the boiled sauce and mix well.
   - **Note**: Fry the spices just until fragrant; avoid burning them.
6. **Soak and marinate**: Firmly squeeze the excess liquid from the first batch of marinated peppers. Place them in a clean container. Pour the completely cooled sauce over them. Let them soak for 4 hours before serving.

## Health Advice
Green peppers are rich in Vitamin C, but some nutrients are lost during pickling. It is recommended to eat them with fresh vegetables to balance nutrition. This dish has a high salt content, so people with high blood pressure should consume it sparingly.
