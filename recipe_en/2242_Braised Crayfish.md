# Braised Crayfish
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦞 Live crayfish, about 2-3 pounds
🫛 Fresh soybeans (edamame), as needed (used in two batches: once for stewing, once before serving)
🧂 Salt, as needed (for cleaning)
🍶 White vinegar, as needed (for cleaning)
🫚 Ginger slices, about 5-6 slices
🧅 Scallion, about 1 stalk, cut into sections
🌶️ Dried or fresh chili peppers, a handful, chopped
🧄 Garlic, about half a head, minced
🫘 Broad bean paste (Doubanjiang), 1 tablespoon
🍲 Hot pot base, a small piece
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed
🍺 Beer, 1 bottle (about 500ml)
💧 Water, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed (for seasoning during stewing)
🐔 Chicken bouillon, a little (optional)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for garnish before serving)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🪥 Sink or large basin
🧺 Strainer or colander
✂️ Scissors (for cutting off crayfish heads and removing the vein)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Long chopsticks (for stirring during frying)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the live crayfish in a large basin, add enough water to submerge them, sprinkle in a handful of salt and an appropriate amount of white vinegar, and soak for 15-20 minutes to encourage them to expel mud and impurities.
   **Note**: Salt and vinegar help prompt the crayfish to purge dirt. You may change the water once during soaking.
2. Under running water, scrub each crayfish’s belly and body individually, then place them in a colander to drain.
3. Cut off the front third of the crayfish head, remove the black stomach, then cut off the gills on both sides.
   **Key point**: Be sure to remove the gills, as this is where the most grime accumulates.
4. Grasp the middle tail fin, gently twist it left and right, then slowly pull outward to extract the entire digestive tract (vein).
5. Wash the fresh soybeans and set aside. Cut the cilantro into sections.
6. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to about 70% hot (approximately 180°C). Fry the prepared crayfish in batches over high heat.
   **Indicator**: The shells should quickly turn red and crispy; the meat should just set. Remove and drain the oil.
   **Key point**: Quick frying makes the shells crispy and locks moisture into the meat.
7. Leave a moderate amount of base oil in the wok. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, chopped chilies, and minced garlic. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant.
8. Add the broad bean paste and stir-fry until the oil turns red. Then add the hot pot base and stir-fry over low heat until it fully melts and blends with the oil.
   **Note**: The hot pot base scorches easily, so be sure to use low heat.
9. Add the fried crayfish and half of the soybeans. Stir-fry quickly and evenly to coat the ingredients with the sauce.
10. Pour in the entire bottle of beer, then add enough water so the liquid just covers the ingredients. Season with salt, chicken bouillon (if using), and rock sugar.
11. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes. Stir once during cooking to ensure even flavor absorption.
12. Uncover, increase the heat to high, and add the remaining fresh soybeans. Stir-fry quickly to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens and clings to the crayfish, sprinkle in the cilantro sections, toss briefly, and remove from heat.
    **Key point**: Adding the soybeans later keeps them crisp-tender and bright green in color.

## Health Tips
This braised crayfish dish is high in protein and low in fat. Adding fresh soybeans provides extra dietary fiber, making it suitable for summer gatherings. Be mindful of the hot pot base and oil content; those with high blood lipids should enjoy in moderation. Pairing it with beer adds extra calories, so it is recommended to serve with a refreshing cucumber salad to balance the meal.
