# Salt and Pepper Duck Jaws
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck Jaws 8 pieces
🧅 Ginger, Scallions, Green and Red Chili Peppers, Chinese Celery, Cilantro, Small Onions, Garlic (for making vegetable juice)
🥚 Whole Egg 1/2
🌾 Custard Powder 2g
🌾 Glutinous Rice Flour 10g
🌾 Rice Flour 10g
🌾 Cornstarch a pinch
🍷 Red Yeast Rice Water as needed (for coloring)
🧅 Diced Dried Onions, Minced Garlic, Diced Green and Red Chili Peppers a pinch (for stir-frying aromatics)
🧂 Salt to taste
🧂 Sugar to taste
🍷 Brandy about 10g
🌶️ Chili Oil to taste
🍶 Rice Wine as needed
🧂 Salt and Pepper Powder to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🥤 Juicer
🪣 Strainer
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🧻 Kitchen Paper

## Preparation Steps
1. Chop off the hard bone near the head of the duck jaw, remove the trachea, and cut each jaw in half from the center.
**Note**: The hard bone can easily damage the gums; the trachea is meatless and has a strong gamey taste, so both must be removed.

2. Rinse the mucus from the throat area of the duck jaws repeatedly with clean water to remove the gamey odor, then pat dry the surface with kitchen paper.
**Key Point**: The drier the surface, the easier it absorbs the marinade.

3. Prepare vegetable juice: Place ginger, scallions, green and red chili peppers, Chinese celery, cilantro, small onions, and garlic in a juicer. Add an appropriate amount of water and blend into a puree. Strain through a strainer to extract the juice, discarding the solids.
4. Place the prepared duck jaws in a bowl. Add 20g of vegetable juice, an appropriate amount of salt and sugar, 10g of brandy, 2g of custard powder, 10g of glutinous rice flour, 10g of rice flour, and a pinch of cornstarch. Mix well.
5. Add half of the beaten egg and continue mixing evenly. Finally, add an appropriate amount of red yeast rice water and mix to color. Marinate for 15 minutes.
6. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to 140°C (low heat). Add the duck jaws and deep-fry for 3 minutes. If using a home stove with lower heat, you may extend the frying time as needed.
7. After 3 minutes, turn the heat to high and deep-fry for another 1 minute until the surface turns golden and crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.
8. In a separate wok, heat chili oil over high heat. Add diced dried onions, minced garlic, and diced green and red chili peppers. Pour in a splash of rice wine and stir-fry until fragrant.
9. Add the fried duck jaws and sprinkle with an appropriate amount of salt and pepper powder. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until evenly coated and aromatic. Remove from heat and serve immediately.

## Health Suggestion
Duck jaws are rich in collagen but have a relatively high cholesterol content, making them suitable for occasional consumption rather than as a daily protein source. Since deep-frying doubles the calorie count, it is recommended to pair them with light vegetables to balance oil and salt intake.
