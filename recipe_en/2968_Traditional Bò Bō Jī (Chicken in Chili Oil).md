# Traditional Bò Bō Jī (Chicken in Chili Oil)

Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 free-range native rooster
🫚 A few slices of ginger (for cooking the chicken)
🧅 A few sections of scallion (for cooking the chicken)
🌶️ A few dried Sichuan peppercorns (for cooking the chicken)
🧂 Salt, to taste (for cooking the chicken)
🌶️ Mixed dried chilies (Erjingtiao, Zidantou, etc.), about one large bowl (for knife-cut chilies)
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste (for frying chilies and making chili oil)
🌶️ One bowl of medium-coarse chili flakes
🌶️ Half a bowl of fine chili flakes
🔮 About half a bowl of white sesame seeds (with skin, used in batches)
🧂 Salt, to taste (for seasoning the base broth)
🧂 MSG, to taste (for seasoning the base broth)
🍬 A pinch of sugar (for seasoning the base broth)
🌶️ A generous amount of Sichuan peppercorn powder (regular type, for seasoning the base broth)
🥜 Crushed peanuts, to taste (for garnish)
🧅 Chopped scallions, to taste (for garnish)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board and chef's knife
🥄 Metal soup spoon

## Step-by-Step Instructions
1. Thoroughly clean the whole chicken. Repeat a "three-scald, three-shock" process 2-3 times. Add the bruised ginger, scallion sections, a few dried Sichuan peppercorns, and sufficient salt to the cooking water. Place the whole chicken in the pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 40 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it steep for 10 minutes. The chicken is done when a chopstick can easily pierce the thickest part of the thigh without any blood oozing out. Remove the chicken and let it cool naturally. Chop it into pieces and arrange on a plate. **Key Point**: Adding salt is crucial to season the chicken throughout. The broth will be used as the base soup later.
2. Pour rapeseed oil into the wok. Add the dried mixed chili segments and fry slowly over medium-low heat until the chilies turn a deep red, slightly charred, and crispy, and the oil takes on a red hue. Remove the chilies with a strainer and let drain. Finely chop them with a knife to make knife-cut chilies. Place the chopped chilies in a bowl and immediately pour the hot oil used to fry them over the top. Stir well to make knife-cut chili oil. **Note**: Maintain a steady heat; you want a fragrant, slightly burnt aroma without burning the chilies.
3. Combine the medium-coarse chili flakes and fine chili flakes in a bowl. Heat the rapeseed oil until it smokes heavily and the oil no longer appears yellow. Let it cool slightly, then pour it over the chili flakes in batches: First, pour the hotter oil over the medium-coarse chili flakes, stirring quickly to prevent scorching and to cool the oil. Second, after the oil has cooled slightly, pour another portion over the mixture and stir. Finally, add the white sesame seeds and fine chili flakes, stir, then pour in the remaining oil. Mix until well combined. **Key Point**: Adding the oil in batches and stirring constantly is the secret to fragrant, vibrantly colored chili oil. Fine chili flakes are delicate and must be added last.
4. In a bowl, combine the cooled, reserved chicken poaching broth. Season with salt, MSG, a pinch of sugar, and a generous amount of Sichuan peppercorn powder. Stir thoroughly until the seasonings are dissolved. Add two spoonfuls of knife-cut chili oil and a few spoonfuls of the regular chili oil. Stir to form a red chili oil dressing. **Note**: The base broth seasoning should be simple, highlighting the flavors of the Sichuan peppercorn and the two types of chili oil. Be generous with the Sichuan peppercorn powder.
5. Evenly pour the prepared red chili oil dressing over the arranged chicken pieces. The liquid should ideally cover about two-thirds of the chicken to ensure it is well-flavored. Finally, garnish with crushed peanuts, white sesame seeds, and chopped scallions before serving.

## Health Suggestions
For a quicker cooking time (halved) and more tender meat, consider using Sanhuang chicken or chicken thighs instead of a native rooster. This dish is high in fat and sodium. People with high blood pressure are advised to use less salt and oil. Pair with cucumber or lotus root to balance the nutritional profile.
