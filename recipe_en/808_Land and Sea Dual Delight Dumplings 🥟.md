# Land and Sea Dual Delight Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 High-gluten flour 500g
🎃 Pumpkin puree 150g (steamed)
💧 Water 110-120g (added in batches)
🧂 Salt 3g (for the dough)
🥩 Ground pork 500g
🦐 Fresh shrimp, as needed (peeled, with heads and shells reserved for oil)
🥬 Chinese chives, as needed (washed and finely chopped)
🌊 Dried seaweed, as needed (toasted and finely chopped)
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp
🍯 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
⚪ White pepper powder, a pinch
🧂 Chicken powder 2 tbsp
🧂 Salt, a small amount (for the filling)
🌱 Scallion-ginger water, as needed (added in batches while mixing the filling)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn oil, as needed
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp
🔥 Homemade shrimp oil, as needed (made from shrimp heads and shells)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Mixing bowls (for dough and filling)
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1.  Mix the high-gluten flour with salt, add the pumpkin puree, and gradually add the water in small batches. Knead until a smooth dough forms — the bowl, hands, and dough should all be clean.
   **Key point**: Add water gradually in small amounts to avoid adding too much at once.
2.  Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes. Repeat the kneading and resting process three times total to make the dough more elastic.
3.  Peel the fresh shrimp, remove the shells, and lightly smash the shrimp with the back of a knife before chopping into a coarse shrimp paste with some texture. Reserve the shrimp heads and shells for later use.
   **Key point**: The shrimp should be freshly peeled; smashing them with the back of a knife helps retain their gelatinous texture and chunkiness.
4.  Pour cooking oil into a wok, add the shrimp heads and shells, and stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red. Remove and discard the shells, leaving the shrimp oil for later use.
5.  Finely chop the Chinese chives. Place the dried seaweed in a dry wok, toast over low heat until it turns green and releases a dry fragrance, then remove and chop it finely.
6.  Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper powder, chicken powder, and a small amount of salt to the ground pork. Mix well, then gradually add the scallion-ginger water in batches, stirring constantly in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and binds together.
   **Key point**: The scallion-ginger water must be added in small, multiple batches, and always stir in the same direction until the filling becomes firm and sticky.
7.  To the bound filling, add Sichuan peppercorn oil, sesame oil, the prepared shrimp oil, the shrimp paste, chopped Chinese chives, and the chopped toasted seaweed. Mix thoroughly until all ingredients are evenly incorporated.
8.  Roll the rested dough into a long log, cut it into pieces of 12-15g each, roll each piece into a round wrapper, place a portion of filling in the center, fold the wrapper over, pinch pleats to seal, and shape into ginkgo leaf shapes.
9.  Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil, add the dumplings, and gently stir with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. Cook until all dumplings float to the surface, indicating they are done. Remove with a slotted spoon and serve.

## Health Tips
These Land and Sea Dual Delight Dumplings are rich in protein. The shrimp oil and toasted seaweed make them especially suitable for winter calcium supplementation and immune system support. It is recommended to serve with vinegar or ginger juice to aid digestion. However, as Chinese chives and pumpkin puree can easily irritate the digestive tract, those with weaker digestive function should consume them in moderation.
