# Crispy Jujubes
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Red-skinned raw peanuts 100g  
🫘 Dried red jujubes 500g (about 1 pound, do not wash, keep dry)  
🧂 No additional seasonings, relying on the natural flavors of jujubes and peanuts  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Electric griddle  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥤 Yogurt straw (tool for removing pits)  
  Bamboo skewer (for cleaning the pit out of the straw)  
🔗 Baking rack  
♨️ Oven  
🍽️ Plate  
🔒 Sealed bag  

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the red-skinned raw peanuts into the electric griddle, stir-fry over low heat continuously until fragrant and crispy, then remove and let cool completely.
   - Note: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning.
2. Once cooled, gently rub each peanut individually to remove the skin. Avoid grabbing and rubbing a large handful to prevent the peanuts from breaking.
3. Take a dried jujube, use a yogurt straw to pierce from one end to the other, pushing out the pit. If the pit gets stuck in the straw, poke it out with a bamboo skewer.
   - Key: Do not wash or soak the jujubes, otherwise the final product will not be crispy.
4. Insert one peeled peanut into each end of the pitted jujube, allowing the peanut to protrude slightly for a fuller appearance.
5. Arrange the stuffed jujubes evenly on the baking rack, leaving space between each. Preheat the oven to 70°C (158°F), place the baking rack inside, and bake for 1 hour to remove moisture from the jujube flesh.
6. Increase the oven temperature to 100°C (212°F) and continue baking for about 2 hours and 30 minutes, until the jujubes turn a deep purple color and emit a caramelized fragrance.
   - Key: This is a core step for achieving a crispy texture. The baking time may need slight adjustment based on the size of the jujubes and the actual oven temperature.
7. Remove the baking rack. The jujubes will still be soft at this point and must cool completely to become crispy. Once fully cooled, transfer to a plate. Any leftovers should be stored in a sealed bag to prevent absorbing moisture and becoming soft.

## Health Advice  
These crispy jujubes with peanuts are rich in natural sugars and high-quality protein, making them suitable as an energy-boosting snack. However, they are relatively high in calories, so limit consumption to 5-6 pieces per serving. Diabetic patients should control their intake. Do not wash the jujubes during preparation, as this will affect the crispiness of the final product.
