# Braised Tofu with Shrimp and Sea Cucumber in Mapo Sauce
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Silken Tofu 1 box (cut into 2 cm cubes)
🦐 Shrimp About 100g (cut into large cubes)
🦪 Ready-to-eat Sea Cucumber 2 pieces (cut into large cubes)
🥩 Ground Meat To taste
🧅 Scallion White Parts To taste (minced)
🧄 Ginger To taste (minced)
🧄 Garlic To taste (minced)
🌶️ Doubanjiang (Chili Bean Paste) To taste
🫘 Fermented Black Beans A pinch
🧂 Salt To taste
🍬 Sugar About 5g (divided into two uses: one for blanching to remove odor, one for seasoning)
🌶️ Ground Sichuan Pepper About 8g (divided: part for seasoning, part for sprinkling on top)
🧂 White Pepper Powder About 3g
🧂 Light Soy Sauce About 10g
🫕 Chili Oil To taste (drizzled on top before serving)
🌿 Garlic Sprouts / Green Garlic About 10g (minced for garnish)
🧴 Wet Starch Slurry To taste (for thickening)

## Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok Spoon / Ladle
🥘 Strainer / Colander
🔥 Gas Stove
🥣 Bowls, Plates

## Preparation Steps
1.  Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a pinch of salt and sugar. Add the tofu cubes, shrimp cubes, and sea cucumber cubes, blanch for about 1 minute, then remove and drain well.
**Key Point**: Blanching removes the beany taste from the tofu and pre-cooks the shrimp and sea cucumber; the sugar helps reduce any fishy odors.

2.  Heat the wok over high heat, add oil, and stir-fry the ground meat until it separates and becomes fragrant. Add the doubanjiang and fry until the red oil releases. Then add the minced scallion whites, ginger, garlic, and fermented black beans, stir-frying until fragrant.
**Note**: The doubanjiang must be fried thoroughly, and sufficient red oil is key to achieving the dish's bright red color.

3.  Add an appropriate amount of water, bring to a boil, and season with sugar, white pepper powder, ground Sichuan pepper, and light soy sauce.

4.  First, add the blanched sea cucumber and shrimp cubes, then gently add the tofu cubes. Simmer over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes, allowing the ingredients to absorb the flavors fully.
**Key Point**: After adding the tofu, minimize stirring. Gently move the wok by shaking it to prevent the tofu from breaking.

5.  Slowly drizzle in the wet starch slurry, gently pushing it with the bottom of the wok spoon to evenly coat the ingredients. Cook until the sauce becomes thick and glossy, then remove from the wok and plate.

6.  Evenly sprinkle the remaining ground Sichuan pepper over the top, garnish with minced garlic sprouts, and finally drizzle with chili oil that has been heated until bubbling. The aroma is released instantly, and the dish should be served immediately.
**Key Point**: The ground Sichuan pepper and chili oil must be added last to preserve their numbing and spicy aromas to the greatest extent.

## Health Advice
This dish combines the freshness of land and sea. Tofu provides high-quality plant protein, while shrimp and sea cucumber offer low-fat animal protein, making it suitable for those on a high-protein, low-fat diet. Note that doubanjiang and fermented black beans are high in salt; individuals with high blood pressure should consume less of the broth.
