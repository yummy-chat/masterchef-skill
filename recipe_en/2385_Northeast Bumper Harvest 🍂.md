# Northeast Bumper Harvest 🍂
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Suggestion: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork ribs (spareribs) 750g (soak for half an hour in advance, pat dry)  
🥬 Long beans (yardlong beans/cowpeas) 300g (cut into sections)  
🌽 Corn (sweet corn/waxy corn) 280g (about 1 ear, cut into thick slices)  
🎃 Pumpkin (baby pumpkin) 130g (remove seeds, cut into triangular pieces)  
🥔 Potato (yellow-fleshed potato) 260g (peel, cut into rolling chunks)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 125g  
🌽 Cornmeal (fine) 125g  
🧅 Scallion 15g (chopped into green onion flowers)  
🫚 Ginger 10g (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (4 cloves sliced, 3 cloves minced)  
⭐ Star anise 3g (about 1.5 pieces)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers as needed (optional)  
🫙 Yeast 3g  
🍬 Sugar 3g (for the batter)  
🫧 Baking powder 1g (optional)  
💧 Warm water 230g (about body temperature)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for greasing the pan and stir-frying)  
🍶 Yellow rice wine/cooking wine 10g (sprinkle along the edge of the pan)  
🧂 Thirteen-spice/five-spice powder 1g (optional)  
🫘 Soybean paste 40g (a full spoon)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g (about 2 spoons)  
🫗 Boiling water 750ml (enough to cover the ribs)  
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (about 1 spoon)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 10g (about 1 spoon)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g  
🧄 Minced garlic (about 3 cloves’ worth, added before removing from heat)  
🧂 MSG 1 small teaspoon (optional, added before removing from heat)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok  
🥄 Spatula/spoon  
🥚 Whisk  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🧻 Paper towels  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🍽️ Small bowls/dishes  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔥 Stove (open flame)

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the ribs in clean water for half an hour to draw out blood; remove and thoroughly pat dry with paper towels.  
**Key point**: Do not blanch, to preserve the meat’s aroma.

2. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornmeal, yeast, sugar, and baking powder. Stir well, then add warm water. Use a whisk to mix into a smooth, loose batter with no dry flour. Cover with plastic wrap and let rise until doubled in size and honeycombed.  
**Key point**: The batter should be thin and must rise fully.

3. Cut the beans into sections, slice the corn into thick rounds, remove the seeds from the pumpkin and cut into triangular pieces, peel and cut the potato into rolling chunks. Combine all vegetables and set aside.  
4. Chop the scallion into flowers, slice the ginger, slice 4 garlic cloves; flatten and mince 3 garlic cloves and set aside separately. Place scallion, ginger, sliced garlic, star anise, and dried chili peppers in a bowl.  
5. Heat the iron wok until smoking, add a generous amount of oil to coat the pan, pour in all the vegetables, and stir-fry over high heat until the beans turn bright green. Remove and set aside.  
**Key point**: Pre-stir-frying the vegetables removes moisture, making the dish more fragrant.

6. Without washing the pan, add a little more oil and heat until smoking. Place the dried ribs along the edge of the pan, fry over medium heat for about 20 seconds, then stir-fry. Keep on high heat for 2–3 minutes until the surface is lightly golden and the oil at the bottom becomes clear.  
**Key point**: Thoroughly drying the ribs is key to removing any gaminess.

7. Sprinkle the yellow rice wine along the edge of the pan, stir well, then add the scallion, ginger, sliced garlic, star anise, and dried chili peppers. Stir in thirteen-spice powder until fragrant. Turn the heat to the lowest, add soybean paste and light soy sauce. Stir slowly a few times, then increase to high heat and stir-fry until the sauce is aromatic.  
**Key point**: Soybean paste must be fried thoroughly over low heat to release its flavor.

8. Pour in 750ml of boiling water, enough to cover the ribs. Bring to a boil, then add sugar, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and chicken bouillon, stirring to combine. Reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and braise for 40 minutes.  
9. After 40 minutes, add the pre-stir-fried vegetables, turn the heat to high and stir well. Once it returns to a boil, reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and braise for another 10 minutes.  
10. Keep the heat on the lowest setting. Oil your hands or wear oil-sprayed gloves, tear off a piece of batter, roll it into a ball, and press it onto the side of the wok. Hold it in place for a few seconds to ensure it sticks before releasing. The cakes will naturally form a teardrop shape—thicker at the top, thinner at the bottom. Leave some space between each. Once all are attached, cover and continue to braise over the lowest heat for 20 minutes.  
11. Uncover and check the cakes. Gently scrape the bottom of the wok with a spatula to prevent sticking, and remove any residue. Turn the heat to high and reduce the sauce until slightly thickened. Just before removing from heat, sprinkle in the prepared minced garlic and MSG, stir well, and serve.

## Health Notes
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber. The ribs provide energy, while the pumpkin and corn aid digestion, making it suitable for family autumn nourishment. Be mindful of the carbohydrate intake from the potatoes and cakes; diabetic individuals should pair with more vegetables.
