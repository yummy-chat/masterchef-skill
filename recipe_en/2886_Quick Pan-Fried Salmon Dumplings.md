# Quick Pan-Fried Salmon Dumplings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 frozen salmon fillet (no need to thaw)
🥟 8-10 frozen dumplings (no need to thaw)
🌊 Appropriate amount of starch water
🫒 About 1 tablespoon of cooking oil
🧂 Appropriate amount of black pepper salt

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🫗 Lid
🥢 Long chopsticks
🔗 Tongs

## Steps
1. Place the frozen salmon fillet and frozen dumplings directly into the frying pan, no need to thaw.

2. Add an appropriate amount of starch water, enough to just cover the bottom of the ingredients, then drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil.
   **Note**: The salmon will release its own oil, so no need to add too much cooking oil.

3. Cover with the lid and bring to a boil over high heat.

4. Once boiling, reduce to medium heat and continue to cook covered.

5. Observe the liquid level; when the liquid is about to dry up, reduce to the lowest heat.
   **Key point**: Check if the "lace edge" formed by the starch water at the bottom of the pan is bubbling to determine if the bottom has reached a crispy state.

6. Once the liquid is completely dry and the bottom turns golden brown, turn off the heat. Use tongs to remove the dumplings and salmon fillet and plate them.

7. Sprinkle an appropriate amount of black pepper salt on the surface of the salmon for seasoning.

## Health Advice
Salmon is rich in Omega-3, and paired with dumplings, it provides carbohydrates. This dish is nutritionally balanced and quick to prepare, suitable for busy office workers. Note that salmon has its own oil, so use less oil for a healthier result. Using frozen ingredients directly saves thawing time.
