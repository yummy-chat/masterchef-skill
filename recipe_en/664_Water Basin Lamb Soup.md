# Water Basin Lamb Soup
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
### Main Ingredients  
🦴 Lamb Bones 3 lbs (broken, soaked in salted water for over 4 hours, change water midway)  
🐑 Bone-in Lamb 2 lbs (soaked in salted water, then rinsed thoroughly)  
🐏 Lamb Fat (loin area) 1 lb (soaked in salted water for over 4 hours, change water midway)  

### Spices  
🌿 Fennel Seeds 12g  
✨ Star Anise 2g  
✨ Cinnamon Stick 2g  
🍂 Bay Leaves 2g  
🌰 Black Cardamom 1 piece  
🌸 Cloves 1g  
🌼 Angelica Dahurica 3g  
🌱 Round Cardamom 2g  
🌾 Round Cardamom (grass) 3g  
🫚 Dried Ginger 7g  
🫚 Galangal 2g  
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorns (for stewing) 2g  
🌶️ Long Pepper 2g  
🌿 Licorice 2g  
⚪ White Peppercorns 5g  

### Seasonings  
🧂 Salt (for soup) approx. 80g  
🧂 Salt (for seasoning bowls) to taste  
🧂 MSG to taste  
🌶️ White Pepper Powder to taste  
🫒 Spiced Lamb Fat to taste (prepared before use)  
🌿 Sichuan Pepper Water to taste (steeped before use)  

### Side Ingredients  
🧄 Garlic Sprouts to taste (finely chopped)  
🌿 Cilantro to taste (for garnish)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Large Soup Pot  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Fine Mesh Skimmer  
🧤 Cheesecloth Bag (spice bag)  
♨️ Steamer  
🥄 Soup Ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
**Preparation Stage**  
1. Break the lamb bones into pieces, cut the lamb fat into chunks, and cut the bone-in lamb into large pieces. Soak each separately in salted water for over 4 hours, changing the water midway, to fully remove blood and gamey flavor.  
2. Combine all dried spices, place in a cheesecloth bag, and tie securely. Soak the bag in water for 15 minutes, then remove and set aside.  
**Cooking Stage**  
1. Add about 10 quarts (approx. 20 lbs) of cold water to the pot, add the lamb bones, and heat. Once the water temperature rises, reduce to low heat.  
**Key Point**: Bones must be added to cold water; after the water boils, patiently skim off the grayish-white foam using a fine mesh skimmer. Keep the heat low, and continue for about 45 minutes until the broth is clear. **Do not use high heat, or the broth will become cloudy white**.  
2. After skimming the foam, add the lamb fat and continue cooking on low heat to allow the fat to slowly melt into the broth, enhancing richness.  
3. Skim off any new foam that appears, then add the spice bag. Continue simmering on low heat for about 20 minutes to slowly release the aromas.  
4. Add the thoroughly rinsed bone-in lamb, keep on low heat, and cook for about 1.5 hours until the meat easily separates from the bone when pierced with chopsticks.  
**Key Point**: Do not add salt while cooking the meat, as adding it too early will darken the broth.  
5. Once the meat falls off the bone, add about 80g of salt to the broth, stir, and continue simmering on low heat for 30 minutes to infuse the meat and enhance the broth's flavor.  
6. Remove the lamb to cool, then remove the lamb fat and spice bag, yielding a clear, bright broth.  
**Spiced Lamb Fat and Sichuan Pepper Water**  
1. Place the reserved lamb fat and spices in a wok, simmer over low heat until the spice residue turns slightly yellow and aromatic. Turn off the heat, let residual heat finish the process, then strain through a fine mesh skimmer to obtain golden spiced lamb fat.  
2. Place an appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorns in a bowl, pour boiling lamb broth over them, cover, and let steep to make Sichuan pepper water.  
**Assembly**  
1. Tear the cooled lamb into small pieces by hand, place in a bowl, and briefly "scald" it with boiling broth.  
2. Add salt, MSG, and white pepper powder to the bowl to taste. Sprinkle generously with chopped garlic sprouts, then pour in boiling broth to dissolve the seasonings and release the garlic sprouts' aroma.  
3. Drizzle with spiced lamb fat and Sichuan pepper water, garnish with cilantro, and serve. Pairing with crescent-shaped bread, sweet garlic, and pickled vegetables makes it more authentic.  

## Health Tips  
This Water Basin Lamb Soup features a clear broth and tender meat, rich in high-quality protein, iron, and zinc, making it ideal for nourishing the body during cold weather or when feeling weak. However, it is high in lamb fat and salt, so those with high blood pressure or on a weight-loss diet should consume it in moderation.
