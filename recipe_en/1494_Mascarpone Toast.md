# Mascarpone Toast  
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 Bread flour 500g (12% protein content)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 65g  
🧂 Salt 7g  
🥛 Milk 300g (can substitute with water)  
🧀 Mascarpone cheese 120g  
🍞 Active dry yeast 6g  
🧈 Butter 30g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
⚖️ Digital scale  
❄️ Refrigerator  
🌡️ Thermometer  
🍳 Stand mixer  
♨️ Proofing box/oven (proofing function)  
📦 Loaf pan  
🧤 Oven mitts  
🍽️ Cooling rack  
🖌️ Pastry brush  
🍳 Aluminum foil  

## Instructions  
1. Combine bread flour, granulated sugar, salt, mascarpone cheese, and milk in a mixing bowl. Stir until no dry flour remains.  
   **Note**: If the ambient temperature is high, the dough may become too warm. Use subsequent hydration steps to cool it down.  
2. Place the dough in a resealable bag and refrigerate for 1 hour or overnight for hydration.  
   **Note**: Hydration helps cool the dough, preventing it from exceeding 30°C during kneading, which could affect gluten development and fermentation.  
3. Remove the hydrated dough, add active dry yeast, and mix on low speed in a stand mixer until incorporated.  
4. Switch to medium-high speed and knead until the dough reaches the windowpane stage: smooth surface, able to pull a rough, thick membrane with jagged edges.  
5. Add softened butter and mix on low speed until fully absorbed, avoiding separation.  
6. Switch back to medium-high speed and knead until full gluten development: the dough can be stretched into a thin, translucent membrane with smooth edges (the "windowpane test").  
7. Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a container, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rise at around 26°C until doubled in size.  
   **Check**: Dip a finger in flour and poke the dough. If the hole does not spring back or collapse, the first proof is complete.  
8. Remove the dough, gently press to degas, divide it into two equal pieces, shape each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 20–30 minutes.  
   **Note**: Handle gently to retain some air pockets; resting makes shaping easier.  
9. Take one piece, flatten it, roll it into a long oval, flip it so the seam side faces inward, and roll it from top to bottom into a cylinder. Repeat with the other piece.  
10. Place both rolls seam-side down, side by side, into the loaf pan.  
11. Transfer to a proofing box for final proofing until the dough rises to about 90% full in the pan (slightly more).  
    **Note**: Mascarpone cheese can weaken the rise, so do not proof until 100% full.  
12. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Place the pan on the lower-middle rack and bake for about 30 minutes. Check browning after 15–20 minutes, and cover with aluminum foil to prevent burning.  
13. Immediately upon removal, tap the pan on the counter to release steam, and unmold quickly. Optionally, brush the surface with light cream or milk while still hot, then cool on a rack.  
    **Check**: The sides and bottom should be evenly golden brown, and tapping should produce a hollow sound, indicating the bread is fully baked.  

## Health Advice  
This toast uses mascarpone cheese to replace part of the butter, resulting in a denser texture and richer calcium content, making it suitable for those needing a calcium boost.
