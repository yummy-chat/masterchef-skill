# Fried Mini Cream Puffs
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
💧 Water 50g  
🥛 Milk 50g  
🫒 Odorless Vegetable Oil 40g (butter can also be used)  
🌾 Cake Flour 60g  
🥚 Eggs 2 (beaten, add in small batches)  
🧂 Salt 1.5g  
🍫 Chocolate as needed (for decoration, optional)  
🥜 Crushed Nuts as needed (for decoration, optional)  
🍨 Ice Cream 1 serving (for pairing, optional)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Uncoated Iron Pot  
🫗 Mineral Water Bottle (for measuring ingredients + homemade piping tip + piping bag)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for stirring and testing oil temperature)  
🥣 Bowl (for holding egg wash and mixing batter)  
✂️ Homemade Bottle Cap Piping Tip (cut open a mineral water bottle cap and widen it)  
🥄 Spatula  
🗑️ Strainer (for scooping out cream puffs and draining oil)  
📄 Parchment Paper (for lining the tray and preventing sticking)  

## Steps  
1. Use a mineral water bottle to measure 50g water, 50g milk, and 40g vegetable oil; weigh out 60g cake flour; beat 2 eggs and set aside.  
2. Cut open a mineral water bottle cap, widen it to form a piping tip, and attach it to a clean mineral water bottle to use as a piping bag.  
3. Line a tray with parchment paper and set aside.  
4. Pour water, milk, vegetable oil, and salt into an uncoated iron pot. Heat until it boils and forms large bubbles, then immediately turn off the heat.  
5. Add all the cake flour at once and quickly stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains.  
   **Key Point**: Use residual heat for initial gelatinization; add all the flour at once.  
6. Turn the heat to the lowest setting and stir the dough until a thin, light brown film forms on the bottom of the pot, and the dough is evenly heated.  
   **Key Point**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning.  
7. Transfer the cooked dough to a bowl and let it cool naturally.  
8. Add the egg wash in batches, stirring well each time, and check the consistency until the batter forms a smooth, inverted triangle shape when lifted with a spatula—neither too dry nor too runny.  
   **Key Point**: Add the egg wash gradually in small batches; the batter consistency is key to successful cream puffs.  
9. Fill the mineral water bottle with the batter, attach the homemade piping tip, and pipe star-shaped or oblong raw pieces onto the parchment-lined tray. Hold the bottle tip about 1 cm from the tray surface, squeeze to desired size, then lift with a quick stop.  
10. Place the piped cream puff pieces in the freezer for 15 minutes to set until they are no longer sticky and can be handled.  
11. Heat oil in a pot to 170-180°C, then add the frozen cream puff pieces.  
    **Key Point**: Frozen pieces can be placed directly into the oil by hand; controlling the oil temperature is crucial for puffing.  
12. Fry until the cream puffs expand rapidly and turn golden brown. Remove with a strainer and drain the oil.  
13. Once the cream puffs have completely cooled, poke a hole in the bottom to fill with filling, or pipe chocolate sauce on top, sprinkle with crushed nuts, and serve with ice cream.  

## Health Tips  
Although these mini cream puffs are high in calories, their small size makes them great for sharing at parties. Remember to control portions. The fried version is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, best enjoyed hot, and pairing with ice cream can help cut the greasiness.
