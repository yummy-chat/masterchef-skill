# Braised Spare Ribs
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork spare ribs about 2 jin (cut into 6 cm lengths)
🥬 White radish 2 pieces (about 2 jin, cut into large chunks)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced into large pieces)
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧅 Spring onion appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount
🪵 Cinnamon stick appropriate amount
🍬 Rock sugar (or white sugar) 1 spoon
🫒 Cooking oil half spoon (for caramelizing sugar) + appropriate amount (for stir-frying)
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🧂 Salt 2 spoons
🧂 MSG 1 spoon
🍶 Yellow wine (or cooking wine) 1 spoon
💧 Boiling water appropriate amount (for caramelizing sugar and braising)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle, slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl
♨️ Pressure cooker
🧊 Colander (for cooling radish)

## Steps
1. **Preparation**
   - Cut the pork spare ribs into 6 cm lengths; wash the white radish and cut into large chunks; slice ginger into large pieces, cut scallion and spring onion into sections, and prepare cinnamon stick and dried chili peppers.
   - Blanch the radish: Put the radish chunks into boiling water and blanch until cooked through, then remove and cool in cold water, set aside.
   - Blanch the ribs: Place the ribs in a pot with **cold water**, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, continue boiling until no blood water remains, then remove and rinse thoroughly with hot water.

2. **Caramelizing Sugar**
   - In a cold pan without heating, add half a spoon of oil, half a spoon of water, and one spoon of rock sugar.
   - Heat over medium-high heat and quickly stir until the sugar melts, turning a **lemon yellow** color, then immediately reduce to low heat and stir continuously in a clockwise direction.
   - When the color deepens, smoke rises, and **mung bean-sized** dense bubbles appear, immediately add an appropriate amount of boiling water, turn to high heat and boil for 3 minutes to make the caramel sauce, then pour out and set aside.
   **Key point**: Be careful of oil splatter when adding boiling water; this method yields a bright red caramel color that is not easily bitter or burnt.

3. **Stir-frying and Seasoning**
   - Reheat the wok with an appropriate amount of oil, add ginger slices and stir-fry over medium-high heat until fragrant, then add the blanched ribs and stir-fry evenly.
   - Add scallion sections, cinnamon stick, and dried chili peppers, stir-fry briefly, then pour in the prepared caramel sauce and stir-fry until the ribs are evenly coated.
   - Add light soy sauce, 2 spoons of salt, 1 spoon of MSG, and 1 spoon of yellow wine to season, then pour in boiling water until it covers all ingredients.

4. **Pressure Cooking**
   - Transfer all ingredients and liquid from the wok into a pressure cooker, cover the lid, and once steam pressure is reached, cook for **10 minutes**.
   - After the time is up, let the pressure release naturally or cool down by rinsing with cold water, then open the lid.

5. **Adding Radish and Finishing**
   - Open the pressure cooker, add the blanched radish chunks, and stir to combine.
   - Cover the lid, bring to pressure again, and cook for **3 minutes**.
   - After the time is up, use the “intermittent water cooling method” to quickly cool and open the lid. If a softer texture is preferred, let it rest covered for a while after opening.
   **Note**: Since the radish has already been blanched, it should not be cooked too long; 3 minutes is enough to ensure texture and flavor absorption.

## Health Tips
This braised spare ribs dish with white radish is both refreshing and nourishing, suitable for the whole family. Be mindful of salt intake, and those with high blood pressure should consume less of the broth. Ribs are rich in iron and protein, while radish aids digestion. However, the dish is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the meal.
