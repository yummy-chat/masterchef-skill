# Yangchun Noodles
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Thin alkaline noodles (for 1 serving)
🥚 Egg 1
🧅 Small scallions about 10 (finely chopped)
🥬 Baby bok choy or cabbage hearts 2-3
🫗 White vinegar 2-3 drops (for poaching the egg)
🍶 Light soy sauce to taste
🧂 Salt a pinch
🌶️ White pepper to taste
🥓 Lard half a tablespoon

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Cooking pot (non-stick or deep pot)
🥄 Soup ladle or spatula
🥢 Slotted spoon or chopsticks
🍲 Bowl

## Cooking Steps
1.  In a bowl, add light soy sauce, salt, white pepper, and lard. Stir until well combined. Set aside.
2.  Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add 2-3 drops of white vinegar, and quickly stir the water with a ladle to create a whirlpool. Crack the egg into the center of the whirlpool, cover the pot, and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 3 minutes.
**Key point**: Adding vinegar and creating a whirlpool are key to keeping the poached egg intact; simmering rather than boiling makes the egg more tender.
3.  In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add the noodles. Gently separate them with chopsticks.
4.  When the noodles are about 90% cooked, add the baby bok choy and blanch until just done.
**Note**: Do not overcook the noodles; they are best when removed at 90% doneness. Add the greens last to keep them crisp and tender.
5.  Drain the cooked noodles and place them into the prepared bowl with the seasoning base. Pour in an appropriate amount of the noodle-cooking water to dissolve the seasonings.
6.  Top with the poached egg and blanched baby bok choy. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and serve.

## Health Tips
Yangchun noodles are flavored with lard for aroma and paired with greens and a poached egg, offering balanced nutrition and a quick energy boost. Adding vinegar when poaching the egg helps maintain its shape, but be mindful of salt intake; those with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of soy sauce as needed.
