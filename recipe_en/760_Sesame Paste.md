# Sesame Paste
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥜 Walnuts 150g
🥜 Peanuts 50g
🖤 Black Sesame Seeds 400g
🍚 Glutinous Rice Flour 300g
🍬 White Sugar 130g (adjust according to preference)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden Spatula
🔥 Oven
🌀 High-Speed Blender
🪣 Fine Mesh Strainer/Sieve
🫙 Sealed Jar

## Instructions
1.  Place 150g walnuts and 50g peanuts in a wok. Stir-fry over low heat until the skins can be easily rubbed off.
   **Key Point**: Use low heat to prevent burning; walnut skins can be blown off to avoid darkening the finished product. Alternatively, roast in an oven at 150°C for 15 minutes.
2.  Allow to cool, then rub off the red skins of the peanuts and walnuts. Blow off any excess skins.
3.  In another wok, add 400g black sesame seeds. Stir-fry constantly over low heat until fragrant and no longer sticking to the spatula.
   **Key Point**: Must stir frequently over low heat to prevent burning.
4.  Add 300g glutinous rice flour to the wok. Stir-fry over low heat until the color turns slightly yellow and the raw flour smell disappears.
   **Key Point**: Scrape the bottom of the wok with each stir to prevent the flour at the bottom from burning due to high heat.
5.  Place the peeled peanuts, walnuts, and about 1/5 of the stir-fried black sesame seeds into the high-speed blender. Grind in batches, not filling the blender too full each time. If the machine struggles, shake it up and down or stop to stir the mixture, then continue grinding. Repeat until all nuts and sesame seeds are ground into a powder.
   **Note**: Must grind in small batches to avoid overloading the machine.
6.  Add 130g white sugar to the ground powder. Continue to blend vigorously until the mixture forms a dark, oily, "cement-like" thick paste.
7.  Pour the blended "cement-like" oily paste into the stir-fried glutinous rice flour. Use your hands to rub and break apart the oily paste, mixing it thoroughly with the glutinous rice flour until it forms a loose powder.
8.  Sift the mixed powder through a fine mesh strainer for a smooth texture; any coarse particles left in the strainer can be rubbed apart again or re-ground in the blender.
9.  Place 50g of the sesame paste powder into a bowl. Gradually add about 130ml of hot water at around 80°C while stirring continuously, until the mixture is smooth and even. It is ready to enjoy.
   **Note**: Water temperature should be controlled between 80–90°C.
10. Store the finished product in a sealed container in a cool, dry place.

## Health Tips
Sesame paste is rich in high-quality plant fats and vitamin E, which helps nourish hair and protect the brain. However, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to use 50g per serving, and pair it with oatmeal or milk to balance nutrition. It is suitable for students and office workers who engage in heavy mental work, but diabetic patients should reduce the sugar content.
