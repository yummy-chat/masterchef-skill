# Hairy Crab Butter
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦀 Several large hairy crabs (for extracting crab roe and shells)  
🫚 1 piece of ginger (sliced, for crab oil); prepare another slice (for frying crab butter)  
🫒 500-700ml sunflower oil (for crab oil); about 3 tablespoons (as base oil for stir-frying)  
🍶 3 bottle caps of Huadiao wine  
🧂 A pinch of sea salt  
🥄 1 teaspoon soy sauce (optional, add when mixing with rice)  
🫗 1 teaspoon fish sauce (optional, mentioned in the video but not actually added)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Steamer (stainless steel, with steamer rack)  
🍳 Non-stick pan/wok  
🥘 Deep pot/soup pot  
🪣 Strainer, glass bowl  
🫙 Sealed glass jar  
🍴 Spoon, spoon handle, scissors, wooden spatula, food tongs (for crab disassembly, stirring, and handling)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. Select large hairy crabs with full abdomens, and clean them thoroughly.  
2. Tie the crabs securely, place them belly-up on the steamer rack, and steam over high heat for 10-15 minutes until fully cooked.  
   - **Note**: Steaming belly-up prevents the roe from leaking.  
3. Disassemble the crabs: First remove the legs and claws, then separate the shell and body, extracting the roe and meat.  
   - **Note**: Be sure to remove the heart as it is cold-natured; use the spoon handle to efficiently scoop out the roe. The meat can be set aside separately.  
4. Process the shells: Keep all shells, and use scissors to cut off the mouth and eyes.  
   - **Note**: The shells are essential for making crab oil and cannot be discarded.

### Cooking Stage
**Part 1: Making Crab Oil**  
1. Pour an appropriate amount of oil into a pot, add some processed shells, and stir-fry over medium heat until the moisture evaporates and the shells become dry, fragrant, and slightly caramelized.  
   - **Note**: If there are many shells, fry them in batches to ensure dryness; transfer to a deep pot afterward.  
2. Add 500-700ml of sunflower oil to the pot with the shells, along with 1 slice of ginger. Simmer over low heat at 100-110°C for about 1 hour until the oil deepens in color and releases a rich aroma.  
3. Turn off the heat, cover, and let it steep overnight. The next day, gently reheat, strain through a fine mesh, and press the shells to extract any remaining oil, yielding pure crab oil.  
   - **Note**: The oil should be orange-red with intense flavor, a key aromatic medium.

**Part 2: Stir-Frying Hairy Crab Butter**  
1. Take about 3 tablespoons of the prepared crab oil and pour it into a clean pan. Add 1 slice of ginger, and heat over low heat.  
2. Add about 400g of crab roe, and stir-fry over medium-low heat for 6-7 minutes.  
   - **Note**: Avoid crushing the roe; stir gently to preserve its granular texture, which is why hairy crab butter is expensive.  
3. Add 3 bottle caps of Huadiao wine and a pinch of sea salt, stir well. Then pour in 150-200ml of the prepared crab oil, mix, and turn off the heat.  
   - **Note**: Fish sauce can be added at this point; Huadiao wine removes any fishy smell and enhances the aroma.  
4. Remove the ginger slices, and immediately transfer the stir-fried hairy crab butter into a pre-sterilized, dry sealed glass jar for storage.  
   - **Note**: The oil layer on top seals out air, allowing storage at room temperature or in the refrigerator. Classic serving: mix into hot rice, optionally adding a bit of soy sauce and ginger tea to balance the cold nature of the crab.

## Health Advice
Hairy crab butter is rich in high-quality protein and phospholipids, but its fat content reaches 30-40g per 100g. It is suitable for occasional use to enhance rice dishes. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Pair it with ginger tea and rice to neutralize its cold nature and relieve greasiness.
