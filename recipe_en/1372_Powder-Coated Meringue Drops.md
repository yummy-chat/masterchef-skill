# Powder-Coated Meringue Drops
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Egg whites  
🍚 White powder (cooked cornstarch or cooked glutinous rice flour, for layering, anti-stick, and decoration)  
🍬 Granulated sugar (for the syrup)  
💧 Water (for cooking the syrup)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (serves as a shaping mold)  
🥣 Small saucepan (for cooking the syrup)  
🌀 Electric hand mixer  
🥛 Measuring cup or glass bowl (for holding the egg whites)  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Spoon (for pressing pits)  
🎂 Piping bag  
🕸️ Flour sieve  
🥅 Mesh sieve or strainer (for shaking off excess powder during unmolding)  

## Steps
1. **Layering and pressing pits**: Pour a sufficient amount of white powder into a clean, dry flat-bottomed pan, forming a thick, even layer. Use the back of a spoon to press evenly spaced, uniform round pits into the powder, serving as shaping molds for the meringue.  
   **Note**: The powder layer must be thick enough, and the depth and spacing of the pits should be even.

2. **Whipping the egg whites**: Place the egg whites into a grease-free and water-free container. Use an electric hand mixer to whip on high speed until distinct lines form and the mixture is fluffy.  
   **Key**: The container and mixer beaters must be completely clean, otherwise the egg whites will not whip.

3. **Cooking the syrup**: Add granulated sugar and a small amount of water to the small saucepan. Heat over low heat and stir until the sugar is completely dissolved. Continue cooking until the syrup boils with large bubbles and becomes slightly thick.  
   **Heat control**: Do not cook too long; it is ready once it reaches a boiling state.

4. **Combining the meringue**: Slowly pour the freshly cooked hot syrup in a thin, steady stream into the egg whites being whipped at high speed. Continue whipping until the meringue becomes thick, glossy, and has firm lines, forming stiff peaks when the mixer beaters are lifted.  
   **Note**: The syrup must be poured slowly while whipping at high speed to prevent the meringue from deflating.

5. **Piping into shape**: Transfer the meringue into a piping bag, cut a small opening, and pipe it into the powder molds' pits one by one, shaping it into teardrop or small cone shapes with full, firm tops.

6. **Coating with powder**: Using a fine sieve, evenly sift a layer of white powder over the piped meringue, completely covering it.  
   **Note**: Sift gently to avoid distorting the shape.

7. **Unmolding and finishing**: After the meringue surface has slightly set, carefully turn out all the finished pieces along with the powder from the pan. Then, gently shake the mesh sieve to remove excess powder, yielding the powder-coated meringue drops.  
   **Key**: Handle gently during unmolding and sieving to prevent the finished pieces from breaking.

## Health Advice  
These meringue drops are primarily composed of sugar and air, making them high in calories and suitable for occasional consumption. Those with blood sugar sensitivities or those on a weight loss diet should eat them in moderation. During preparation, ensure that containers are free of grease and water, otherwise the egg whites will not whip.
