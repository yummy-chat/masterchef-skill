# Electric Rice Cooker Mixed Rice

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Leftover rice, appropriate amount  
🥬 Water spinach, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🍅 Tomato, 1 (chopped)  
🌭 Sausages, 2 (cut into segments)  
🦑 Jellyfish head, appropriate amount  
🍖 Large meaty bone, 1 (pre-prepared)  
🍄 Mushrooms, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🥦 Broccoli, appropriate amount (broken into small florets)  
🌶️ Laoganma fermented black bean chili sauce, 1 tablespoon

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪🪵 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍲 Electric rice cooker  
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the leftover rice into the electric rice cooker inner pot, add a small amount of water and gently rinse.
   **Note**: Briefly rinsing leftover rice removes excess surface starch, resulting in a cleaner and lighter texture.
2. Add the chopped water spinach, tomato, sausage segments, jellyfish head, mushroom slices, broccoli florets, and raw egg into the inner pot, mixing well with the rice.
3. Add an appropriate amount of water, enough to just submerge most of the ingredients, similar to the amount used for regular rice cooking.
4. Place the inner pot into the rice cooker, press the cook button, and cook using the standard rice cooking program.
   **Key point**: No need to adjust the time; one cooking cycle is sufficient to fully cook all ingredients.
5. Once the cooking program ends and the cooker switches to keep warm mode, open the lid, add one tablespoon of Laoganma chili sauce, and then place the pre-prepared meaty bone into the pot.
6. Stir thoroughly with chopsticks, mixing all ingredients, sauce, and rice evenly until the color is uniform and ingredients are well distributed.
7. Serve directly. The texture is soft and tender, with a savory, slightly spicy flavor, especially suitable for children.

## Health Advice
Using leftover rice paired with jellyfish head and water spinach provides relatively balanced nutrition, but the sausages and meaty bone result in high sodium content. It is recommended to reduce the amount of sausages or replace them with chicken breast to lower sodium intake; when serving to children, halve the amount of Laoganma chili sauce.
