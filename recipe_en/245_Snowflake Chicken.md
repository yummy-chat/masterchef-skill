# Snowflake Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken breast – appropriate amount (remove sinew, score with a crosshatch pattern, then cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot – appropriate amount (diced)  
🥬 Green asparagus lettuce – appropriate amount (diced)  
🫘 Kidney beans – appropriate amount  
🧅 Scallion – appropriate amount (cut into segments)  
🫚 Ginger – appropriate amount (sliced)  
🥚 Egg white – 1 piece  
🥄 Starch – two portions  
🥄 Potato starch – one portion  
🫒 Salad oil – 3 grams  
🧂 Salt – appropriate amount  
🌫️ White pepper – appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon – appropriate amount  
💧 Water starch – appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount (optional, for marinating)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Bowls (multiple)  
🔥 Wok, stove  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍶 Chopsticks  
📦 Plastic wrap  
♨️ Steamer  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Remove the sinew from the chicken breast, score a crosshatch pattern on both sides, then cut into small pieces.  
2. Add salt, white pepper, and cooking wine to the chicken pieces, mix well, and marinate to infuse flavor.  
3. Prepare the egg white batter: In a bowl, combine 1 egg white, two portions of starch, one portion of potato starch, and 3 grams of salad oil, whisk until smooth and batter-like.  
4. First add a little starch to the marinated chicken pieces and mix well, then pour in the egg white batter and mix evenly to coat.  
5. Dice the carrot and green asparagus lettuce, blanch them together with the kidney beans, then remove and drain well. Set aside.  
6. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil over high heat. While the water is vigorously boiling, add the chicken pieces. As soon as they turn white and set, immediately remove them.  
**Key point**: The chicken must be added to boiling water so the starch gelatinizes, sealing in moisture and ensuring the pieces remain tender and smooth.  

7. Place the set chicken pieces into a bowl, pour in enough boiling water to cover, add scallion segments, ginger slices, salt, white pepper, and chicken bouillon, then seal the bowl tightly with plastic wrap.  
8. Once the steamer is producing steam, place the bowl inside and steam over high heat for 10 minutes.  
9. After steaming, remove the bowl, discard the scallion and ginger, then pour the original cooking liquid into a wok and bring to a boil. Thicken with water starch until the liquid is glossy and rich.  
10. Turn off the heat, add the blanched carrot dice, green asparagus lettuce dice, kidney beans, and chicken pieces. Gently toss to combine evenly.  
11. Plate and serve. The chicken pieces are white and silky smooth, with a clear, glossy sauce coating the ingredients.  

## Health Tips  
Chicken breast is low in fat and high in protein; pairing it with carrot and green asparagus lettuce increases dietary fiber, making it suitable for those on a weight-loss or fitness regimen. Ensure the egg white batter is thoroughly mixed, and the boiling-water setting step seals in moisture. Avoid over-steaming, as it may make the meat dry and tough.
