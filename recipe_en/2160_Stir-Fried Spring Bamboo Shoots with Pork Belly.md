# Stir-Fried Spring Bamboo Shoots with Pork Belly
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🎋 Spring bamboo shoots, about 2 pieces  
🥩 Pork belly, about 150g  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for removing astringency and seasoning, added in batches)  
🍬 Sugar, to taste (added during blanching)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for greasing the pan and blanching)  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (added when stir-frying pork belly)  
🧂 Chicken essence, a pinch (for enhancing freshness)  
💧 Starch water, to taste (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Iron wok (frying pan)  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the bamboo shoots**: Cut off the tough root ends, slice lengthwise to peel off the outer skin, use a knife to shave off the harder outer layer, and cut into thin slices of even thickness.  
**Note**: Even thickness ensures uniform cooking; make sure to remove all tough outer skin.

2. **Soak to remove astringency**: Place the bamboo shoot slices in a bowl, add an appropriate amount of salt and water, and soak overnight. Drain and set aside.

3. **Prepare the meat**: Slice the pork belly into thin, even slices and set aside.

4. **Blanch**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add salt, sugar, and a little cooking oil. Add the bamboo shoot slices and blanch until cooked through, then remove and drain.  
**Key Point**: Adding sugar further removes astringency; adding oil gives the slices a brighter color.

5. **Stir-fry the pork belly**: Heat the wok and grease with oil. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the edges are slightly crispy. Splash in cooking wine, sprinkle with a pinch of salt to season, then remove and set aside.  
**Key Point**: Fry until "slightly crispy" to release the meat's aroma; removing them keeps the slices tender.

6. **Stir-fry the bamboo shoots and thicken**: Leave the rendered oil from the pork belly in the wok. Add the blanched bamboo shoot slices and stir-fry over high heat. Season with salt and chicken essence, then drizzle in starch water and stir rapidly to coat the slices evenly with the thickened sauce.

7. **Combine and finish**: Return the stir-fried pork belly to the wok. Stir-fry over high heat for about 30 seconds to blend the flavors of meat and bamboo shoots. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice  
Spring bamboo shoots are high in fiber and low in fat. Paired with pork belly, which provides iron and quality fats, this dish is suitable for cleansing the intestines and nourishing the stomach in spring. For those with high blood lipids, it is recommended to reduce the amount of pork belly. Spring bamboo shoots must be fully blanched to remove oxalic acid and avoid astringency.
