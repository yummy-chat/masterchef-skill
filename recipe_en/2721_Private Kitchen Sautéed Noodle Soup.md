# Private Kitchen Sautéed Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍜 Dried noodles (suitable amount)
🥬 Chinese cabbage (about a handful, cut into thin strips)
🧅 Scallions (suitable amount, minced)
🧄 Ginger (suitable amount, minced)
🧄 Garlic (suitable amount, minced)
🧂 Soy sauce (suitable amount)
🧂 Salt (suitable amount)
🌶️ White pepper powder (a pinch)
🫒 Sesame oil (a few drops)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns (suitable amount)
🛢️ Cooking oil (suitable amount, used in two batches)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (frying pan)
🥛 Saucepan
🥄 Ladle
🥅 Strainer (slotted spoon)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add minced scallions, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat until fragrant.
   **Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to instantly release the aroma of the scallions, ginger, and garlic.
2. Keep the heat high. Pour soy sauce into the wok and stir-fry quickly to release the sauce’s fragrance.
3. Immediately add enough boiling water to the wok, bringing the broth to a rapid boil.
   **Key point**: Boiling water must be used to preserve the broth’s freshness and temperature.
4. Once the water is boiling, add the dried noodles, ensuring they are fully submerged in the broth.
5. When the noodles come to a boil, drizzle a small amount of cold water along the edge of the wok to prevent overflow and ensure even cooking inside and out.
6. Season with salt and white pepper, adjusting to taste.
7. When the noodles are almost done, add the shredded Chinese cabbage. Let it cook briefly in the residual heat until just tender, maintaining a crisp texture.
8. Once the noodles are fully cooked, drizzle in a few drops of sesame oil just before turning off the heat to enhance aroma and shine.
9. In a separate saucepan, heat a suitable amount of cooking oil over high heat. Add Sichuan peppercorns and fry until they darken in color and become aromatic.
   **Note**: Control the heat carefully to release the fragrance without burning the peppercorns.
10. Pour the hot Sichuan peppercorn oil through a strainer directly over the cooked noodles and broth. The high-temperature oil will re-ignite the layered aromas of all ingredients.

## Health Advice
The flavor of sautéed noodle soup comes from three rounds of high-heat aroma activation, which both enhances fragrance and stimulates appetite. It is ideal for warming the body and boosting energy. However, dried noodles have a high glycemic index; people with diabetes are advised to consume less broth and pair with protein.
