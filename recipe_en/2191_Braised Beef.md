# Braised Beef
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
- Beef shank 500g
- Onion half (about 100g)
- Ginger 30g
- Garlic 40g
- Dried chili peppers 10g
- Spice packet 1 pack (about 10g)
- Soybean paste 15ml
- Broad bean paste 15ml
- Oyster sauce 15ml
- Fermented bean curd 15ml
- Light soy sauce 120ml
- Dark soy sauce 60ml
- Salt 10-15g
- White sugar 30g (for caramelizing)
- Beer 1 can (330ml)
- Water enough to cover all meat

## Kitchenware and Equipment
- Pressure cooker
- Strainer

## Steps
1. Soak the beef shank in cold water for more than 3 hours in advance to fully remove blood.
2. Add 30g of white sugar to a pot, heat over low heat until completely melted and turns brown, pour in 150ml of hot water, stir well to make caramel color, set aside.
3. Cut the onion into chunks, crush the ginger and garlic, break the dried chili peppers into sections.
4. Pour enough water into the pot, add the spice packet, onion chunks, ginger, garlic, and dried chili pepper sections, bring to a boil over high heat.
5. Add 15ml each of soybean paste, broad bean paste, oyster sauce, and fermented bean curd, stir well.
6. Pour in the prepared caramel color, mix evenly.
7. Add 120ml of light soy sauce and 60ml of dark soy sauce, stir well.
8. Add 10-15g of salt to season, pour in 1 can of beer, bring to a boil again.
9. Blanch the beef shank in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, remove and rinse off the foam with hot water.
10. Place the blanched beef shank into the braising liquid, ensuring the liquid completely covers the meat, cover the pressure cooker lid, once steam builds up, turn to low heat and cook for 25-30 minutes.
11. Do not open the lid after turning off the heat, let it cool naturally, soak overnight for full flavor.
12. Remove the braised meat, refrigerate to set, then slice and serve.
13. Strain the braising liquid through a strainer to remove all solid residues, bring to a boil again for sterilization, cool, then refrigerate or freeze for storage.

## Notes
- Caramelize the sugar over low heat to avoid burning and bitterness.
- Rinse with hot water after blanching to prevent the meat from shrinking due to cold.
- If braising vegetables, use a portion of the liquid separately and do not reuse.
- Always bring the braising liquid to a boil again for sterilization before storage, and seal it in the refrigerator or freezer.

## Health Suggestions
Braised beef is low in fat and high in protein, making it an excellent source of high-quality protein for fitness or weight loss. The braising liquid is high in salt, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance sodium intake. Using a pressure cooker for short cooking time preserves more nutrients, overnight soaking deepens flavors, and refrigerating before slicing gives a firmer texture.
