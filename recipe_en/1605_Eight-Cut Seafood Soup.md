# Eight-Cut Seafood Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥒 1 cucumber (peeled, julienned)  
🥕 About 1 small piece of carrot (julienned)  
🥩 About 1 small piece of pork loin (julienned)  
🐟 1 freshwater sea bass (flesh only, skin and bones removed, julienned)  
🐚 About 2-3 abalones (shelled, julienned)  
🌊 About 1-2 sea cucumbers (julienned)  
🫚 1 small piece of ginger (julienned)  
🫘 1 small piece of silken tofu (julienned)  

🧂 Salt to taste  
🌽 Cornstarch to taste  
🧂 MSG to taste  
🌶 White pepper to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chinese chef's knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🍳 Non-stick wok/soup pot  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Prepare the eight types of julienned ingredients:  
   - Peel the cucumber, slice thinly using a flat knife technique, then cut into fine julienne strips. Soak in water and set aside.  
   - For the carrot, slice thinly using a flat knife technique, then julienne using a push-pull cutting motion. Soak in water and set aside.  
   - Slice the pork loin thinly using a flat knife technique, stack the slices, and cut into julienne strips using a straight push cut.  
   - Take the sea bass flesh, remove the skin, slice thinly using a flat knife technique into fish strips. Toss with a little salt and cornstarch.  
   - Shell the abalones, slice thinly using a flat knife, then cut into fine julienne strips. Soak in water and set aside.  
   - Slice the sea cucumbers thinly using a flat knife, then cut into fine julienne strips. Soak in water and set aside.  
   - Slice the ginger thinly using a flat knife, then cut into extremely fine ginger julienne strips.  
   - Using a bouncing knife technique, first slice the silken tofu thinly, then cut into extremely fine tofu julienne strips. Soak gently in water and set aside.  
**Key Point**: Choose a flat knife or bouncing knife technique based on the hardness of the ingredients to ensure even julienning. Pre-season the meat and fish strips with salt and cornstarch.

2. Prepare the soup base:  
   Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the ginger strips, season with salt and MSG. Once boiling, drizzle in a cornstarch slurry to thicken, stirring constantly until the soup reaches a thick, chunky consistency.  
**Note**: Stir continuously while thickening to prevent sticking to the bottom.

3. Add the main ingredients:  
   Once the soup is boiling again, add the marinated pork strips, fish strips, abalone strips, and sea cucumber strips in sequence. Then add the carrot strips and cucumber strips.  
4. Season and finish:  
   Sprinkle in white pepper, gently lift the tofu strips from the soaking water into the pot. Stir lightly with a spoon to combine evenly, then bring to a boil.  
**Key Point**: Tofu strips are fragile, so they must be added last and stirred gently to prevent breaking.

## Health Advice  
This Eight-Cut Seafood Soup is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those who need nutritional supplementation but dislike greasy food. However, individuals with poor kidney function should be mindful of the purine content in tofu and sea cucumber.
