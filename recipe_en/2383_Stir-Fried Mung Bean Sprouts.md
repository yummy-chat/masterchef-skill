# Stir-Fried Mung Bean Sprouts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌱 Mung bean sprouts 400g (washed and drained)
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (crushed and minced)
🫚 Ginger a small piece (minced)
🧅 Spring onion bulbs a few (chopped)
🌶️ Dried chilies 10g
🫘 Green Sichuan peppercorns a few
🧈 Lard 20g
🧂 Salt 1.5g
🍬 White sugar a pinch (to enhance flavor)
🍶 White vinegar 1g
💧 Water starch as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Strainer

## Steps
1. Thoroughly wash the mung bean sprouts, drain well, and ensure there is no excess water on the surface to prevent them from releasing water and causing splattering when added to the wok.
   **Key point**: Draining well is the foundation for keeping the sprouts crisp.

2. Crush and mince the garlic, mince the ginger, chop the spring onion bulbs, and place them in a bowl together with the green Sichuan peppercorns and dried chilies.

3. Prepare the sauce in a separate bowl: add salt, white sugar, white vinegar, and water starch in order, and stir well. Preparing the sauce in advance shortens the stir-frying time, ensuring the sprouts remain crisp.
   **Key point**: Mix the sauce in advance to avoid rushing during stir-frying.

4. Place the wok over heat, add the lard, and heat over medium heat until fully melted.

5. When the oil temperature reaches about 60% hot, reduce to low heat, add all the aromatics, and stir-fry until the dried chilies change color and the aroma releases. Be careful not to burn them.
   **Note**: Stir-fry over low heat to avoid a bitter, burnt flavor.

6. Quickly add the drained mung bean sprouts, and immediately turn the heat to high.

7. Stir-fry rapidly over high heat for about 15 seconds, until the sprouts are just cooked. Stir-frying over high heat ensures they stay tender and crisp without releasing water.
   **Key point**: High heat and quick stir-frying are key to crisp results.

8. Once the sprouts are cooked, quickly pour the prepared sauce along the edge of the wok.

9. Continue stir-frying rapidly over high heat to evenly coat the sprouts with the sauce, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Tips
Mung bean sprouts are low in calories and high in fiber. Pairing them with lard and dried chilies enhances flavor, making them suitable for those who want to control calories while satisfying cravings. Stir-frying quickly over high heat preserves their tender and crisp texture, but those with a cold stomach should reduce the amount of Sichuan peppercorns.
