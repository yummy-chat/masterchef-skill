# Stir-Fried Broccoli
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥦 Broccoli 1 head (cut into bite-sized pieces, peel thick stems and slice)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic 2 cloves (minced)
🍲 Chicken broth about 1/2 cup (divided into two equal portions; if unavailable, substitute with water and chicken bouillon)
🌽 Cornstarch 1 teaspoon
🧂 Salt 1/4 teaspoon (omit or reduce if the broth is already salted)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowls (for washing and mixing sauce)
🍳 Wok (with lid)

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the broccoli florets off the head, then into uniform small pieces; peel the thick stems and cut into thick slices. Place the florets and stems together in a bowl, add water, rub clean, and drain.
   **Key Point**: Cut the pieces uniformly to ensure even cooking; use the stems to avoid waste.

2. Prepare the sauces: In the first bowl, pour about 1/4 cup of plain chicken broth. In the second bowl, pour about 1/4 cup of chicken broth, add 1 teaspoon of cornstarch and 1/4 teaspoon of salt, and stir to form a cornstarch slurry.
   **Note**: The two portions have different uses—one for braising and infusing flavor, the other for thickening.

3. Heat the wok over high heat, then add cooking oil. When the oil is at medium-low temperature, add the dried chili pepper sections and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the minced garlic and stir gently. Proceed to the next step immediately before the garlic turns yellow to prevent burning.

4. Turn the heat to high, add the drained broccoli, and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat it with the aromatic oil. Then pour in the first bowl of plain chicken broth, immediately cover the wok with the lid, turn the heat to medium, and braise for about 1.5 minutes until the broccoli turns bright green. Do not stir during this time; keep the lid sealed.

5. Remove the lid, give a brief stir, then turn the heat to high. Pour in the prepared cornstarch slurry, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens, becomes translucent, and evenly coats the broccoli. Turn off the heat. Be careful not to reduce the sauce too much; retain a little liquid.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in fiber and low in calories, making it suitable for weight loss or blood sugar control. Be careful not to burn the dried chili peppers and garlic, as this can create bitterness and affect nutrition. The broccoli stems can be sliced and stir-fried together—they have a crisp texture and can help supplement calcium.
