# Clear Simmered Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Whole chicken 1000g (free-range or three-yellow chicken, free-range requires longer simmering)  
🫚 Ginger about 10g (one piece)  
🫘 Red dates 4 pieces  
🧂 Salt to taste (added to the bowl after cooking)  
🫒 Hot oil to taste (for dipping sauce, about 240°C/eighty percent hot)  
🧅 Scallions to taste (for dipping sauce, chopped)  
🫚 Minced ginger to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🧄 Minced garlic to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Small chili peppers to taste (for dipping sauce, sliced into rounds)  
🍬 Sugar to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🥄 Soy sauce to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🫒 Sichuan peppercorn oil to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili oil to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🧂 MSG to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🍲 Chicken soup to taste (for dipping sauce, ladled from the cooked soup)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife (for preparing chicken)  
🫕 Soup pot (cast iron)  
🥄 Skimmer (to skim foam and fat)  
🍳 Wok (to heat oil for dipping sauce)  
🥣 Bowls, spoons, chopsticks (for mixing dipping sauce and serving soup)  

## Steps  
1. Place the whole chicken on a cutting board. Remove the skin from the chicken neck, the trachea, and the esophagus. Open the neck skin and remove the lymph nodes.  
   - Chop off the chicken head, wing tips, and toenails. Cut off the chicken feet at the joints. Remove the chicken tail.  
2. Remove the innards from the cavity. Keep the chicken heart. Ensure all chicken lungs, kidneys, and blood stuck to the bones are completely removed.  
3. Cut the chicken into large pieces. Discard the bony middle part with little meat and contamination. Rinse the chicken pieces twice under running water and drain well.  
4. Place the chicken pieces in a pot with cold water. Add enough water to fully submerge the chicken.  
   **Key point**: Do not blanch. Start with cold water to preserve the fresh flavor.  
5. Bring to a boil over high heat. Use a skimmer to thoroughly skim off the foam.  
6. Add the ginger and red dates. You may also add other ingredients like chestnuts or mushrooms according to preference.  
7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer with the lid **open**. Free-range chicken takes about 1.5–2 hours or longer; three-yellow chicken requires a shorter time.  
   - Doneness test: The chicken is done when chopsticks can easily pierce through the thickest part. If the broth reduces too much, replenish with hot water.  
   **Note**: Covering the lid while simmering makes the soup cloudy. Keeping the lid open yields a clear broth.  
8. After cooking, use a skimmer to skim off the oil floating on the surface. The skimmed chicken fat can be used for other purposes.  
9. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, combine scallions, minced ginger, minced garlic, and sliced chili peppers. Pour in hot oil at 80% heat to release the aroma. Then add salt, sugar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, Sichuan peppercorn oil, chili oil, and MSG. Finally, ladle in a spoonful of hot chicken soup and stir well.  
10. Once the chicken has cooled to warm, not hot to the touch, tear it into small pieces. Add the shredded chicken to the dipping sauce and mix well.  
    **Key point**: The chicken absorbs flavor better when mixed while still warm.  
11. Ladle the chicken soup into bowls. Serve alongside the hand-torn chicken. The soup is clear and golden. One chicken, two dishes.  

## Health Advice  
Clear simmered chicken soup is mild and nourishing, suitable for people with weak constitutions or those needing a boost in autumn and winter. Skimming off the fat reduces calories. Those with high uric acid should drink the soup sparingly, and those in the acute phase of gout should avoid it.
