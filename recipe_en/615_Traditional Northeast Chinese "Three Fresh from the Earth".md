# Traditional Northeast Chinese "Three Fresh from the Earth"

Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 1 round eggplant (stem removed, cut into rolling wedges)
🥔 1 potato (peeled, cut into rolling wedges)
🫑 1-2 green peppers (seeds removed, cut into diamond or rolling wedges)
🧄 Some garlic (divided into two portions: one for stir-frying, one for sprinkling before serving)
🧅 Some scallion (finely chopped)
🧂 Some salt (for soaking eggplant in salted water and preparing the sauce)
🍬 Some sugar (for preparing the sauce)
🌾 Some starch (for coating eggplant and preparing the sauce)
🥄 Some light soy sauce (for preparing the sauce)
🥄 Some dark soy sauce (for preparing the sauce, mainly for color)
🍶 Some rice vinegar (for preparing the sauce)
💧 Some water (for preparing the sauce)
🫒 Some cooking oil (for frying ingredients and stir-frying, generous amount needed)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for preparing sauce and soaking eggplant)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon
🔥 Gas stove
🪵 Wooden basin (for coating eggplant with starch)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Cut and prepare ingredients**
   - Remove the stem from the eggplant and cut into evenly sized rolling wedges.
   - Peel the potato and cut into rolling wedges.
   - Remove the seeds from the green pepper and cut into diamond or rolling wedges.
   - Finely chop the scallion and mince the garlic. Divide the minced garlic into two equal portions and set aside.
2. **Handle the eggplant**
   - Place the eggplant wedges in clean water, add an appropriate amount of salt, and let them soak until fully hydrated and initially seasoned.
   - Remove the eggplant, drain slightly, and place in a basin. Evenly coat with a thin layer of dry starch.
3. **Prepare the sauce**
   - In a bowl, mix salt, sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice vinegar, water, and starch. Stir until well combined and set aside.
### Cooking Stage
1. **Fry the ingredients**
   - Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat to about 70% hot. Add the potato wedges and fry over medium heat until the surface is golden and the inside is cooked through. Remove and drain the oil.
   - Keep the oil temperature steady, then add the starch-coated eggplant wedges.
     **Note**: Do not stir immediately after adding to the wok. Wait until the eggplant is set, then gently turn it. Fry until the surface is crispy and golden, then remove.
   - Briefly fry the green pepper wedges together with the eggplant, then remove all ingredients and drain the oil.
2. **Stir-fry aromatics and thicken the sauce**
   - Leave a small amount of oil in the wok without washing it. Add the chopped scallion and one portion of the minced garlic, and stir-fry quickly until fragrant.
   - Pour in the prepared sauce, stirring constantly with a spoon, and cook until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy.
3. **Mix and stir-fry, then serve**
   - Quickly add all the fried potatoes, eggplant, and green peppers back into the wok.
   - Turn the heat to maximum and stir-fry and toss rapidly, ensuring the sauce evenly coats every piece.
   - Before serving, sprinkle in the remaining portion of raw minced garlic, stir to combine, and plate.
     **Key**: The addition of raw minced garlic is the soul of the flavor and cannot be omitted.

## Health Tips
Eggplant tends to absorb a lot of oil, so it is recommended to pair it with cold cucumber to balance the calorie intake. For those on a weight-loss diet, only half a portion should be consumed. Both potatoes and eggplant are high-carb vegetables; reduce the corresponding amount of staple food after eating.
