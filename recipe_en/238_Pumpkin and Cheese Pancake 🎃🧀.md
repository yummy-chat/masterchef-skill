# Pumpkin and Cheese Pancake 🎃🧀
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 Pumpkin: about 300g after steaming (mashed into a fine puree)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour: equal amount to the pumpkin puree
🌽 Cornstarch: half a tablespoon
🧀 Mozzarella cheese:适量 (about half the size of a dough piece, used as filling)
🫘 Black sesame seeds:适量
🤍 White sesame seeds:适量
🧂 Salt: 2g
🍬 Sugar: one tablespoon

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🛍️ Plastic bag
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🍳 Electric griddle
🫗 Oil bottle

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the steamed pumpkin in a plastic bag and press it with your palm into a smooth and fine puree, the finer the better.
2. Pour the pumpkin puree into a bowl, add an equal amount of glutinous rice flour, cornstarch, salt, and sugar, and knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
   **Note**: The ratio of pumpkin puree to glutinous rice flour is about 1:1. The dough should be moderately soft and firm, able to form balls without sticking to your hands.
3. The dough does not need to rest. Directly roll it into a long strip on the cutting board and cut it into evenly sized pieces with a knife.
4. Take an appropriate amount of mozzarella cheese and roll it into a ball about half the size of a dough piece.
5. Roll a dough piece into a half-sphere shape, press a dimple in the center with your thumb, place the cheese ball inside, and gradually gather the edges upward with the tiger's mouth to seal the opening. Roll it into a round ball again.
   **Key**: The seal must be tight to prevent the filling from leaking or the pancake from collapsing during frying.
6. Place the filled ball on the cutting board and gently press it with your palm into a pancake about 0.5–1 cm thick. If it is too thick, it will be difficult to cook through.
7. Evenly sprinkle both sides of the pancake with black and white sesame seeds, and gently press them to adhere.
8. Preheat the electric griddle, pour in an appropriate amount of oil, place the pancake, and cook over medium-low heat.
   **Note**: Do not use high heat to avoid a burnt exterior and raw interior.
9. Flip the pancake once the bottom is golden and small bubbles appear in the center. Cook for about 2 minutes on each side until both sides are golden brown and the pancake springs back when pressed. Remove from the griddle.

## Health Advice
Pumpkin is rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene. Combined with glutinous rice flour and cheese, it is easy to digest and suitable for warming the body in autumn and winter. However, it is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat 1–2 pieces per serving and reduce the intake of other staple foods. Diabetic patients should control the amount of sugar used.
