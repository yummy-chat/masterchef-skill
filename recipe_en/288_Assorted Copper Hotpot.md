# Assorted Copper Hotpot
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef brisket, appropriate amount (for making braised beef)
🥩 Ground beef, about 1kg (two jin, for making meatballs)
🧈 Firm tofu, 1-2 pieces (one piece crumbled and mixed into the meat filling, two pieces sliced thick for frying)
🥔 Potatoes, appropriate amount (cut into rolling knife chunks)
🥔 Baby potatoes (small potatoes), appropriate amount
🥚 Eggs, 2 (added to the ground beef filling)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, appropriate amount (for making the broth base; reserve the mushroom soaking water)
🥬 Napa cabbage, appropriate amount (sliced, for layering the bottom and stir-frying)
🌱 Bean sprouts, appropriate amount (for layering the bottom and stir-frying)
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, appropriate amount (for layering during assembly)
🧂 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for blanching beef brisket)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for blanching, braising, flavoring the broth, and making scallion-ginger water)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for blanching, braising, flavoring the broth, and making scallion-ginger water)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for braising beef brisket, seasoning the meat filling, and making the broth)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for braising beef brisket and seasoning the meat filling)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, a small amount (for braising beef brisket and seasoning the meat filling; can also be brushed on the beef brisket surface before frying)
🧂 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount (for seasoning the meat filling and making the broth)
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder, appropriate amount (for seasoning the meat filling)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, appropriate amount (for seasoning the meat filling)
🍯 Honey, appropriate amount (brushed on the beef brisket surface before frying; used as an alternative to dark soy sauce)
🌾 Potato starch, appropriate amount (for seasoning the meat filling to increase stickiness)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount (for flavoring the broth together with scallions and ginger)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (for blanching, braising, frying, and stir-frying vegetable base)
♨️ Stainless steel deep pot (for braising beef brisket and making clear broth)
🥘 Copper hotpot (for assembling the final dish and heating while serving)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming blood foam and removing fried ingredients)
🥄 Soup ladle (for skimming foam, ladling soup, and pouring broth)
🔪 Cutting board and knife (for preparing various ingredients)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the beef brisket in cold water in a pot, add scallion segments, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, and continue boiling for 5 minutes. Then remove the brisket.
2. In another pot, add hot water and the blanched beef brisket. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and braise for at least 40 minutes until the brisket is tender.
3. Prepare the meat filling: To the ground beef, add five-spice powder, thirteen-spice powder, chicken bouillon, eggs, crumbled firm tofu, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and salt. Gradually add scallion-ginger water in batches, stirring vigorously in one direction until the mixture becomes sticky. Finally, add potato starch and mix well. Let it marinate for 10-15 minutes.
4. Pour enough oil into a wok and heat it. When the oil temperature is hot enough that a chopstick inserted causes rapid bubbling, add the formed meatballs, cut potato chunks, and firm tofu pieces that have been pre-steamed for 5 minutes. Fry until the surface is golden brown, then remove.
5. Prepare the braised beef: Remove the braised beef brisket from the pot, let the surface dry, and evenly brush with honey or dark soy sauce. Then place it into the hot oil and fry until the surface turns a dark, charred black. Remove and return to the original braising liquid. Simmer over low heat for another 15 minutes, then remove and slice.
6. Make the broth base: Heat a wok with oil and sauté scallions, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the original braising liquid from the beef brisket, the reserved shiitake mushroom soaking water, five-spice powder, and salt. Bring to a boil, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve to remove all solids, yielding a clear broth.
7. Prepare the vegetable base: Stir-fry the sliced napa cabbage and washed bean sprouts separately in a small amount of oil until fragrant.
8. Assemble the hotpot: In the bottom of the copper hotpot, layer the stir-fried cabbage and bean sprouts. Then arrange the rehydrated dried shiitake mushrooms, fried tofu pieces, fried potato chunks, rehydrated wood ear mushrooms, fried meatballs, and sliced braised beef on top.
9. Gently pour the strained clear broth into the pot, just enough to submerge the ingredients. Finally, place lit charcoal inside the chimney at the center of the copper hotpot. Once heated, it is ready to be served.

## Health Advice
This is a rich, warming dish perfect for winter. The layers of beef brisket, meatballs, and fried tofu provide high protein and calories, making it suitable for manual laborers or for replenishing energy in cold weather. Be mindful of salt intake. Those with high blood lipids should consume fried items and the skin of the braised beef in moderation.
