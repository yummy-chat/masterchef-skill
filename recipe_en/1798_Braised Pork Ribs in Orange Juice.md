# Braised Pork Ribs in Orange Juice  
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 Pork ribs, appropriate amount (cut into pieces)  
🍊 Oranges, appropriate amount (use the zest and about one bottle of juice)  
🫚 Ginger, a few slices  
🧅 Scallions, a bunch (tied into knots)  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, one small teaspoon  
🥡 Light soy sauce, half a bowl  
🥡 Dark soy sauce, a little (for color)  
🌿 Spices (star anise, etc.), appropriate amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Soup spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the pork ribs into pieces, slice the ginger, tie the scallions into knots, and scrape the orange zest from the orange skin with a knife. Set aside.  
2. Place the ribs in cold water in a pot, add ginger slices and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then remove the ribs and rinse them clean with warm water.  
3. Heat the wok without adding oil. Add the blanched ribs and stir-fry over medium heat until the surface turns slightly golden and fragrant.  
   **Key point**: Thorough stir-frying is essential for a dry and aromatic final dish.  
4. Add the scallion knots, ginger slices, spices, and orange zest. Stir-fry evenly. Then pour in the entire bottle of orange juice and one bowl of water. Season with salt, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce.  
   **Note**: Add enough liquid at once; avoid adding water midway to preserve the flavor.  
5. Bring the broth to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for about 60 minutes until the ribs are tender.  
   **Alternative**: If short on time, use the soup or stew function of an electric rice cooker.  
6. After simmering, uncover and discard the softened scallions, ginger, and spices. Turn to high heat to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly to prevent sticking. When the sauce thickens, turns glossy and red, and evenly coats the ribs, turn off the heat.  
7. Plate the ribs and garnish with freshly grated orange zest on top.  

## Health Tips  
Cooking with orange juice adds vitamin C and helps cut through the richness, making it ideal for autumn dryness or when craving a refreshing flavor. The blanching and stir-frying steps effectively reduce greasiness. Be careful not to overuse dark soy sauce to avoid masking the orange aroma with a braised soy sauce taste.
