# Yan Du Xian (Shanghai Spring Bamboo Shoot & Pork Soup)
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 3 spring bamboo shoots (peeled, cut into large rolling knife chunks)
🥩 Half strip of five-layer pork southern wind-dried meat (choose pork belly that is cured, slice thickly, do not use Chinese bacon)
🍖 A few slices of ham
🐷 300g black pork spare ribs (choose ones with rich marbling, cut into small pieces)
🥬 1 asparagus lettuce (peeled, cut into rolling knife chunks)
🫘适量 dried tofu knots
🧅 适量 scallions
🫚 A few slices of ginger
🧂 适量 salt (for blanching bamboo shoots)
🍶 适量 cooking wine (for blanching salted meat and ham)

## Cookware & Equipment
♨️ Clay pot
🍳 Wok
🥘 Stockpot (for blanching)
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife

## Preparation Steps
1.  Cut the five-layer pork southern wind-dried meat into thick slices; chop the black pork spare ribs into small pieces; slice the ham into a few pieces; peel the spring bamboo shoots and cut them into large rolling knife chunks; peel the asparagus lettuce and cut it into rolling knife chunks.
**Note**: The southern wind-dried meat must be cured pork belly; do not substitute with Chinese bacon; bamboo shoots should be cut into large pieces to maintain a crunchy texture.

2.  Bring a pot of water to a boil, add an appropriate amount of salt, blanch the bamboo shoot chunks for about 2 minutes, then remove and drain.
**Key Point**: Adding salt when blanching effectively removes oxalic acid.

3.  In a separate pot, add cold water, then add the sliced salted meat, ham slices, and ginger slices. Pour in cooking wine, bring to a boil, blanch, then remove and drain.
**Key Point**: Blanching in cold water further removes excess salt and any off-flavors.

4.  Heat a wok over high heat, add a little oil, then add the spare rib pieces. Pan-fry until the surface is golden brown and fragrant.
**Key Point**: Fresh spare ribs are not blanched; pan-frying directly better locks in the meat's aroma and helps achieve a rich, white soup color.

5.  Transfer the pan-fried spare ribs to a clay pot. Pour in enough hot water, then add the blanched salted meat slices and ham slices.
**Key Point**: You must use hot water; this makes it easier for the soup to turn a milky white color.

6.  Bring the soup in the clay pot to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and simmer. Once the soup is slightly milky white and the meat is half-tender, add the blanched bamboo shoots, asparagus lettuce chunks, and dried tofu knots.
**Key Point**: Vegetables should not be added too early; prolonged cooking will cause them to lose their crunchy texture and vibrant green color.

7.  Continue simmering until all ingredients are tender and flavorful, and the soup is creamy white and thick. It is then ready to serve.
**Note**: The finished soup should be creamy and rich, with a savory and umami flavor from the salted meat and ham, which typically provides enough saltiness without needing additional salt.

## Health Advice
Yan Du Xian is rich in protein and dietary fiber. The blanching process removes oxalic acid from the bamboo shoots, making them easier to digest, making this soup suitable for spring nourishment. Note that the salted meat and ham contain their own salt; no extra salt is needed in the soup. People with high blood pressure should limit their consumption of the broth.
