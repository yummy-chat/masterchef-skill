# Mayonnaise
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 1 egg yolk
🌿 197g rapeseed oil (added in two parts: 12g + 185g)
🍋 15g lemon juice (or white vinegar)
🧂 2g salt
🌿 4g Dijon mustard

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🥣 Glass mixing bowl
🥄 Measuring spoons/cups
🌀 Hand whisk
🍶 Glass storage jar

## Instructions
1. Place the egg yolk, lemon juice, salt, and Dijon mustard into the glass mixing bowl.
2. Add 12g of rapeseed oil and whisk thoroughly with a hand whisk until the mixture is a thin, flowing consistency.
3. Gradually add the remaining 185g of rapeseed oil in small increments, adding only a very small amount each time and whisking continuously and quickly. Wait until the oil is fully incorporated before adding the next portion.
   **Key point**: Adding oil in small amounts gradually and stirring continuously is crucial for successful emulsification. The egg yolk acts as an emulsifier, dispersing the oil into tiny droplets that are encased by the water content in the lemon juice, forming a stable sauce.
4. Once all the oil has been added, continue whisking until the mayonnaise is semi-solid, displaying distinct lines when stirred, and the mixture clings to the whisk without dripping easily when lifted.
5. Transfer the prepared mayonnaise into a clean glass jar, seal it, and refrigerate. It is recommended to consume within one week.

## Health Advice
Mayonnaise is rich in high-quality egg yolk and fats, making it suitable for those needing a quick energy boost. However, be mindful of its high calorie content; it is recommended to enjoy it in small amounts with vegetable salads or sandwiches. Homemade versions avoid the preservatives found in commercial products, but the amount of oil is substantial. Control the portion size per use to avoid excessive fat intake.
