# Braised Pork Offal with Flatbread (Luzhu Huoshao)
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐖 Pork intestines, appropriate amount (fresh, do not blanch)  
🐖 Pork lung, appropriate amount (rinse repeatedly until snow white)  
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount  
🫓 Flatbread, appropriate amount (made with unleavened dough, kneaded with warm water, rested, baked, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces)  
🫘 Firm tofu, appropriate amount (cut into pieces and fried until golden)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (half stir-fried, half added raw)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (half stir-fried, half added raw)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (divided into three portions: for stir-frying, raw addition, and mashing into paste)  
🌿 Cilantro, appropriate amount (sprinkled at the end)  

🧂 Star anise, appropriate amount  
🍂 Bay leaves, appropriate amount  
🪵 Cinnamon, appropriate amount  
🌸 Cloves, appropriate amount  
🌱 Cumin seeds, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns, appropriate amount  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, appropriate amount  
🫘 Fermented soybean paste (yellow bean paste), appropriate amount (diluted with water)  
🍶 Erguotou (white liquor), appropriate amount (pour along the edge of the wok)  
🫙 Fermented bean curd, appropriate amount (mashed and mixed)  
🫙 Fermented bean curd juice, appropriate amount (for coloring)  
🫘 Fresh fermented soybeans (douchi), appropriate amount (added directly)  
🍾 Soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring)  
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for final seasoning)  
🫒 Sesame oil, a small amount (added at the end for aroma)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🫕 Soup pot or pressure cooker (including inner pot)  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥄 Wok spatula or soup ladle  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Bowls (multiple)  
🫗 Mortar and pestle  
🛍️ Spice bag  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Thoroughly clean the pork intestines. Rinse and squeeze the pork lung repeatedly under running water until it turns snow white. Wash the pork belly and set aside.
2.  Bake the flatbread made with unleavened dough, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces. Cut firm tofu into thick slices and fry until golden and blistered on the surface. Cut cilantro into sections and set aside.
3.  Place the wok over heat without oil. Add star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cumin seeds, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant, then remove and set aside.
4.  Cut scallions into sections, slice ginger, and separate garlic cloves. Divide the scallions, ginger, and garlic into two portions: one for stir-frying and one for raw addition. Reserve some garlic to mash into a paste.
5.  Dilute the fermented soybean paste with a small amount of water. Mash the fermented bean curd and combine with the bean curd juice; add a little water to form a sauce.
6.  Heat oil in the wok. Add half of the scallion sections, ginger slices, and whole garlic cloves. Stir-fry over medium heat until slightly yellow and aromatic. Add the diluted fermented soybean paste and stir-fry until the sauce releases its fragrance.
7.  Pour Erguotou along the edge of the wok, then immediately add enough hot water or broth to submerge all subsequent ingredients.
8.  Once the broth comes to a boil, add the remaining raw scallion sections, ginger slices, and raw garlic cloves. Then add the prepared fermented bean curd mixture and fermented bean curd juice.
9.  Add the whole pork lung, pork intestines, and pork belly to the pot. Add fresh fermented soybeans, soy sauce, and thirteen-spice powder.
**Note**: Fresh fermented soybeans do not need to be stir-fried; add them directly to the pot.

10. Transfer the entire contents to a pressure cooker. Cover, bring to pressure, then reduce to medium-low heat and cook for about 1 hour, until the ingredients are tender and flavorful.
11. After opening the lid, adjust the salt to taste. You can place the fried tofu and flatbread pieces into the braising broth to heat through.
12. Line the bottom of a bowl with the heated tofu and flatbread. Scoop out the intestines, lung, and pork belly, cut them into pieces, and arrange them on top. Ladle the piping hot braising broth over everything. Sprinkle generously with cilantro, drizzle with chili oil, add the mashed garlic paste, and finish with a small drizzle of sesame oil.

## Health Advice  
This dish, Braised Pork Offal with Flatbread, is rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion. However, note that pork intestines and lung are high in cholesterol; it is recommended to pair with vegetables. People with high blood pressure should limit consumption of the broth, as the braising liquid is high in salt.
