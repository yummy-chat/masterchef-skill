# Crystal Rice Dumplings
Cuisine: Others  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍠 Sweet potato 100g  
🟣 Purple sweet potato 100g  
🍚 Sago 120g (used in two portions, **do not wash**)  
🌿 Dried bamboo leaves as needed (soak overnight until soft)  
🍬 White sugar 40g (used in two portions of 20g each)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl  
✂️ Scissors  
♨️ Large pot  
🍳 Lid  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧵 Cotton string

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the sweet potato and purple sweet potato separately. First cut into thick slices, then into strips, and finally into small cubes. The cubes should be uniform in size.
2. Add 120g sago and 20g white sugar to the sweet potato cubes, and mix well with chopsticks.  
   **Key point**: Sago must not be washed; mix directly while dry.
3. Add another 120g sago and 20g white sugar to the purple sweet potato cubes, and mix well in the same way.
4. Soak the dried bamboo leaves in clean water overnight until soft. Before use, trim off the hard stem at the base.
5. Take two soaked bamboo leaves, place them with the fuzzy sides facing each other, overlapping them alternately to increase length.
6. Fold the overlapped bottom of the bamboo leaves upward by half a turn to form a funnel shape. Fill with the sweet potato sago filling or the purple sweet potato sago filling.
7. Use your left hand to pinch the arched edge of the bamboo leaf into a corner shape. With your right hand, fold the upper leaf down, push it forward, and pack it tightly.
8. Following the outline of the bottom corner, press down the excess bamboo leaves on both sides, and fold the remaining leaf to one side.
9. Tie it tightly with cotton string, and trim off any excess bamboo leaf.  
   **Note**: The wrapping and tying must be tight to prevent the filling from leaking.
10. Fill a large pot with enough water. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat, then add the dumplings.
11. Reduce to medium heat and cook for over 4 hours. Occasionally turn the dumplings during cooking to ensure even heating. If the water level drops, add hot water. The dumplings are done when the sago changes from opaque white to translucent.  
    **Key point**: The cooking time must reach a full 4 hours, otherwise the center of the sago may have uncooked white cores.
12. Remove the cooked dumplings. Let them cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for about 1 hour.  
    **Note**: After refrigeration, the sago has a more chewy texture.

## Health Advice
These crystal rice dumplings use sago instead of glutinous rice, resulting in a chewy texture that is gentler on the digestive system. They are suitable for those who want to try a festive treat during the Dragon Boat Festival but are concerned about digesting glutinous rice. The purple sweet potato and sweet potato provide natural sweetness, but attention should be paid to sugar control—40g of white sugar has already been added.
