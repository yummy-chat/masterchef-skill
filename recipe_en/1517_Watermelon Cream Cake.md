# Watermelon Cream Cake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍉 1 watermelon (choose a ripe, sweet one; use half and shape into a hemisphere)  
🥛 About 200g heavy whipping cream  
🫐 Blueberries, as needed  
🍋 1 lemon (sliced thinly or cut into wedges)  
🥥 A pinch of shredded coconut  
🌿 Edible flowers/leaves, as needed (e.g., mint leaves, for garnish)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🌀 Turntable (or a plate as substitute)  
🥄 Spoon (for smoothing and pressing patterns)  
💼 Piping bag  
🍽️ Plate (for final presentation)  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the watermelon and cut it in half. Take half and cut off the bottom to make it stable, then trim it into a regular hemisphere shape with a knife. The remaining half can be cut into a heart shape or other forms, to be used as a backup dessert.  
   **Note**: If there is excess moisture on the watermelon’s surface, gently pat it dry with a paper towel so the cream can adhere better.  
2. Place the trimmed watermelon hemisphere on a turntable or plate. Fill the piping bag with heavy cream.  
3. Starting from the bottom of the watermelon, pipe the cream over the entire surface in a spiral pattern from bottom to top.  
4. Quickly smooth the cream with the back of a spoon to form an even coating. A bit of texture may be left, so complete smoothing is not necessary.  
   **Key point**: Heavy cream softens easily in high temperatures, so work quickly and avoid repeated spreading. In summer, refrigerate the tools and cream beforehand.  
5. Gently press the back of a spoon into the cream to create circular indentations of varying sizes, adding depth.  
6. Arrange washed blueberries, lemon slices, and edible flowers/leaves on top for decoration, then evenly sprinkle shredded coconut over everything.  
7. Decorate the leftover heart-shaped watermelon with remaining cream and fruit, and enjoy them together.  

## Health Advice  
This watermelon cream cake replaces high-sugar cake layers with fruit, making it suitable for summer refreshment and for those watching their calorie intake. Note that heavy whipping cream must be handled at low temperatures throughout; it is recommended to chill the tools in advance to prevent melting.
