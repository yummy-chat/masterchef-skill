# Omelette
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Eggs (3-4, adjust based on pan size)
🧂 A pinch of salt
🧀 Parmesan cheese (for final garnish)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (approximately 20cm)
🥣 2 bowls
🥢 Chopsticks or fork
🔍 Strainer
🔪 Knife and fork
🔥 Stove
🧻 Damp cloth (for shaping assistance)

## Steps
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and whisk thoroughly with a fork until uniform.
2. Strain the egg mixture through a strainer into another bowl to remove air bubbles and impurities, resulting in a finer texture.
3. Heat the pan over medium-low heat. Use chopsticks to pick up a small piece of solid fat and evenly coat the bottom and sides of the pan to ensure nothing sticks.
4. Turn the heat to medium and pour the strained egg mixture into the pan.
5. Immediately use chopsticks to stir the eggs quickly and continuously in a circular motion in the pan until most of the egg mixture forms fine, semi-set curds, with a small amount of runny egg remaining in the pan.
   **Key**: Quick stirring is essential for creating tender, soft curds.
6. Stop stirring and let it sit for about 5 seconds. Tilt the pan to one side, use chopsticks to lift and fold the edge of the omelette toward the pan's side. Combine with a wrist flick to gather the omelette into a dumpling or olive shape. You can briefly remove it from the heat to use residual heat.
7. Use chopsticks to help adjust the shape, aiming for a neat appearance.
8. Carefully flip the omelette so the seam side is down. You can use a damp cloth to gently press and shape it, paying attention to wrist control.
9. Slide the formed omelette onto a plate.
10. Grate or sprinkle Parmesan cheese on top, and serve.

## Health Tips
This omelette is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or as a breakfast option. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to increase dietary fiber. Pay attention to the heat to keep it tender and avoid overcooking, which can cause it to become tough.
