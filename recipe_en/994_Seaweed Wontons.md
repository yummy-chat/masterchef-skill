# Seaweed Wontons  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍖 800g lean pork front leg (finely ground into mince)  
🌿 2–3 sheets dried seaweed (rinsed, squeezed dry, and chopped)  
🍜 Wonton wrappers, as needed (preferably those containing egg and alkaline water, more resistant to boiling)  
🌽 2 tbsp tapioca starch  
💧 150ml water  
🧂 12g table salt  
🧂 2–3g baking soda  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🌶️ A pinch of white pepper  
🧂 A small handful of MSG (referred to as "snowflake MSG" in the video)  
🍯 Fried shallot oil, as needed (for enhancing broth aroma)  
🫒 Cornstarch, as needed (for dusting to prevent sticking)  
🥣 Broth seasonings (adjust to taste): chili oil, garlic chili sauce, light soy sauce, chopped scallions, seaweed, lard, etc.  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🥢 Chopsticks or spatula (for filling)  
📦 Airtight container  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍜 Soup bowl and soup spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the seaweed**: Tear the dried seaweed into small pieces, rinse repeatedly with water, squeeze out excess water thoroughly, then chop finely.  
   - **Note**: Must be washed and squeezed dry to prevent excess moisture from affecting the firmness of the filling.  

2. **Prepare the starch water**: In a measuring cup, mix 150ml water, 2 tbsp tapioca starch, 2–3g baking soda, a pinch of sugar, and a pinch of white pepper. Stir until well combined.  
   - **Note**: This starch water is key to making the filling tender and springy; do not substitute the formula.  

3. **Initial seasoning of the filling**: Place 800g lean ground pork in a bowl, add 12g salt, and vigorously knead with your hands for 2–3 minutes until the mince becomes sticky and clumps together.  
4. **Add liquid and develop stickiness**: Pour the starch water into the meat filling in two batches, continuously kneading, rubbing, or slapping the mixture for 3–5 minutes until all liquid is absorbed, the filling becomes extremely sticky and stringy, and the bowl can be turned upside down without the filling falling out.  
   - **Key point**: The filling must be mixed to this state to ensure a bouncy, Q texture. If the mince is too coarse, ask the butcher to grind it finer.  

5. **Mix the filling**: Add the chopped seaweed into the prepared meat filling and stir evenly. You may also substitute with corn, mushroom, water chestnut, or other side ingredients.  
6. **Assemble the wontons**: Take a wonton wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, and wrap tightly using your preferred method.  
   - **Note**: Since the filling is dense and elastic, use durable alkaline water wrappers to prevent breakage.  

7. **Freeze for storage**: Dust the assembled wontons generously with cornstarch to prevent sticking, place them in an airtight container, and freeze for about 2 hours to set. Then shake them apart and continue freezing in a sealed container.  
8. **Cook and season**: Bring water to a boil, add the wontons, and cook until they float and the wrappers become translucent. In a bowl, prepare seasonings like fried shallot oil, salt, white pepper, and chopped scallions. Pour in boiling noodle soup or broth to dissolve, then add the cooked wontons.  
   - **Optional broths**: Clear broth or spicy-sour broth, or serve dry with sauce.  

## Health Advice  
The key to the wonton filling's bouncy texture lies in tapioca starch and baking soda. The lean pork front leg, high in protein and low in fat, paired with seaweed, makes this suitable for those aiming to build muscle or lose fat, though note the relatively high salt content. Gout patients may reduce the amount of seaweed to avoid excessive purine intake.
