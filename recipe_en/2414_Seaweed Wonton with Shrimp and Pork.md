# Seaweed Wonton with Shrimp and Pork
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine (Min Cuisine)
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork, appropriate amount (ground meat filling)
🦐 Shrimp paste, appropriate amount (mixed with pork)
🧀 Seaweed, appropriate amount (some finely chopped and mixed into the filling, some for the bowl bottom)
🥟 Dumpling wrappers, appropriate amount (for wrapping the filling and making dough pieces)
🥬 Leaf lettuce, about 1 small head (side dish)

🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for seasoning the filling and cold dish)
🧂 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (added to the filling to make it tender and smooth)
🫗 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (used multiple times: for seasoning the filling, bowl base, and dressing the vegetables)
🐷 Lard, one tablespoon (for seasoning the broth base to add aroma)
🐟 Ground dried flounder powder, one tablespoon (key ingredient for Fujian-style umami)
🌶️ White pepper powder, appropriate amount (for seasoning the broth base)
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount (drizzled over the leaf lettuce for aroma)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing the filling)
🛍️ Plastic bag (double-layer or resealable bag recommended, for mixing and piping the filling)
🍳 Pot (for boiling water)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing ingredients)
✂️ Scissors (for cutting seaweed and dumpling wrappers)
🍶 Soup ladle and bowl (for serving soup)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the Filling**: Place the ground pork and shrimp paste in a bowl. Add the finely chopped dried seaweed. Season with salt, light soy sauce, and cornstarch. Mix well.
**Key Point**: The seaweed must be finely chopped to prevent whole pieces from getting stuck in the teeth while eating.

2. **Mix and Bag the Filling**: Transfer the seasoned filling into a double-layer plastic bag. Knead and mix the filling thoroughly from the outside of the bag.
**Key Point**: Use a double-layer bag or a thick resealable bag to prevent the bag from bursting during squeezing.

3. **Assemble the Wontons**: Cut a small corner off the plastic bag to create a piping opening. Squeeze the filling onto the center of a dumpling wrapper. Gather and pinch the wrapper closed to seal.
4. **Cook the Wontons and Dough Pieces**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the wontons and cook until they float. Then, cut the remaining dumpling wrappers into small pieces and add them to the pot. Continue cooking until the dough pieces float and turn translucent.
**Key Point**: The wontons are cooked when they float. The dough pieces are ready when they float and appear translucent. Keep the water at a rolling boil.

5. **Prepare the Bowl Base**: In a serving bowl, place lard, light soy sauce, ground dried flounder powder, white pepper powder, and dried seaweed.
**Regional Flavor Key Point**: Dried flounder powder is a common umami-enhancing seasoning in Fujian-style soups.

6. **Assemble the Bowl**: Ladle the cooked wontons and dough pieces along with the boiling broth into the bowl. Stir immediately to dissolve the bowl base seasonings.

## Health Advice
This dish combines the double umami of seaweed and dried flounder powder, with a tender and smooth filling. It is suitable for individuals needing to supplement protein and iodine. Note that dumpling wrappers and lard are relatively high in calories, so consumption should be moderated during weight loss periods.
