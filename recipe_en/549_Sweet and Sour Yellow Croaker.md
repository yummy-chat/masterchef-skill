# Sweet and Sour Yellow Croaker  
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐟 Yellow croaker 1 (approx. 1.5–2 jin)  
🧅 Scallion 1 segment (for marinating)  
🫚 Ginger 1 piece (for marinating)  
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced for sauce)  
🫘 Green peas appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🥚 Egg 1  
🌾 Sweet potato starch appropriate amount  
🌾 Flour appropriate amount (approx. 1:1 ratio with starch)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount  
🍶 Yellow wine appropriate amount  
🥄 Soy sauce appropriate amount  
🥄 Dark soy sauce a little (optional, for coloring)  
🍎 Vinegar approx. 1 part (added in two batches)  
🍬 Sugar approx. 2 parts  
💧 Water starch appropriate amount  
🫒 Hot oil approx. 1 tablespoon (added at the end)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🥣 Bowls (several)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for removing innards, mixing batter, testing oil temperature, poking holes)  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥘 Skimmer  
🔥 Induction cooker or gas stove  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove innards without cutting belly: Insert two chopsticks through the mouth, twist to pull out gills and innards. Use fingers if necessary to clean thoroughly, then rinse inside with running water.  
**Note**: Keep the belly intact for better presentation.  

2. Score the fish: Lift the fin, make a vertical cut beneath it down to the bone, then slice diagonally toward the head to open the flesh. Repeat every 2–3 cm on both sides. Trim the tail into a swallowtail shape.  
**Key**: Cut vertically first, then diagonally, to ensure the flesh opens up during frying.  

3. Marinate: Slice scallion and ginger, add salt and yellow wine, squeeze firmly to extract juice. Rub the juice evenly over the fish, inside and out, including the cuts. Marinate for about 15 minutes, then discard the solids.  
**Key**: Do not over-marinate, or the fish will release water and affect batter adhesion.  

4. Prep ingredients and make batter: Mince scallion, ginger, and garlic into rice-grain-sized pieces; set aside green peas. Mix sweet potato starch and flour at a 1:1 ratio. Add water first and stir, then beat in an egg to form a smooth, flowing batter that coats the fish.  
**Key**: Batter should be thick enough to coat evenly but not too thick, or it will become hard.  

5. Coat the fish: Hold the marinated fish and pour or brush the batter over every part, especially into the scored cuts.  
**Key**: Coat evenly and completely; exposed areas will become hard and burnt during frying.  

6. Deep-fry for shape: Heat plenty of oil in the wok to 170°C (sixth heat). Hold the tail and slowly lower the fish into the oil. Support the fish with your hand to fry one side until the batter sets, then fully submerge. Fry until golden and crisp, turning once.  
**Note**: Supporting the fish helps create a curved shape; oil temperature must be high enough to prevent batter from falling off.  

7. Poke holes: After removing the fish, use a sharpened chopstick to gently poke a few small holes in the body. This makes the inside crispier and creates a sizzling sound when sauce is poured.  
**Key**: Do not poke too deep to avoid breaking the fish shape; poke while still hot for best effect.  

8. Prepare sweet and sour sauce:  
   a. Leave a little oil in the pan, sauté minced scallion, ginger, and garlic until fragrant.  
   b. Add soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and enough water. Stir and bring to a boil.  
   c. Taste, then add a little more vinegar along the edge of the pan to compensate for evaporated acidity.  
   d. Thicken with water starch until the sauce coats the spatula.  
   e. Pour in a spoonful of hot oil and stir quickly to make the sauce glossy and bubbly.  
**Key**: Add vinegar in two batches—first with sugar for base flavor, second to restore acidity and enhance aroma; hot oil at the end creates shine and bubbles.  

9. Pour sauce and serve: Plate the fish, garnish with green peas, and pour the hot sauce evenly over it. The dish should sizzle audibly when served.  
**Key**: Pour the sauce quickly while the fish is still steaming hot, so the sauce reacts with the heat for a lively effect.  

## Health Tips  
Yellow croaker is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for the whole family. However, the sweet and sour sauce contains a fair amount of sugar, so it’s advisable to control the sauce portion to avoid excessive calorie intake. When frying, keep the oil temperature steady to ensure a crispy exterior and tender interior, which aids digestion.
