# Fermented Rice Cake (Jiu Niang Mi Gao)
Cuisine: Common Homestyle Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 500g Rice (soaked in advance until easily crushed between fingers)
🍚 A small amount of Glutinous Rice (mixed and soaked with rice to balance texture)
💧 180g Water (or 250g as a substitute for fermented rice liquid)
🍶 100g Fermented Rice (Jiu Niang) (including both liquid and rice solids)
🌸 Dried Osmanthus Flowers, to taste (for decoration)
🍬 60~80g White Sugar (60g for mild sweetness, 80g for normal sweetness)
🍞 5g High-Activity Dry Yeast (choose high-sugar or low-sugar type based on sugar content)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥛 Measuring Cup (or a cut mineral water bottle can be used as a substitute)
🌀 Blender or Food Processor
🥣 Stainless Steel Bowl
🥄 Spoon or Spatula
🫖 Heavy-Bottomed Pan
🍳 Steamer
🧻 Plastic Wrap
🥣 Heat-Resistant Bowls (for steaming molds)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Pour the drained soaked rice, fermented rice, and water into the blender. Blend repeatedly until the rice batter is smooth, fine, and can form a ribbon-like consistency, similar in thickness to chiffon cake batter.
2.  Pour the rice batter into a bowl. Add white sugar and yeast, and stir thoroughly until completely dissolved.
    **Note**: The amount of sugar determines the yeast type. If the sugar content exceeds 60g, use high-sugar yeast; otherwise, use low-sugar yeast.
3.  Cover with plastic wrap and place in a warm area for the first fermentation, about 1 hour, until the volume has doubled in size.
    **Key Point**: The fermentation environment temperature should be controlled at around 28℃. Too high a temperature will kill the yeast, while too low a temperature will result in insufficient fermentation.
4.  Stir with a spoon to release gas and break up large air bubbles.
5.  Perform the second fermentation, letting it rise again until the volume has doubled.

   **Steaming Method**
6.  Pour the fermented rice batter into a greased heat-resistant bowl and sprinkle dried osmanthus flowers on the surface.
7.  Place in a steamer after the water has come to a boil. Steam over medium heat for 40 minutes, then remove.

   **Pan-Frying Method**
6.  Grease a heavy-bottomed pan with a thin layer of oil. Pour in the other half of the fermented rice batter and cook slowly over low heat.
7.  Fry until the surface is set and the bottom is golden brown, then flip over. Continue frying until both sides are caramelized.
    **Note**: The pan bottom must be sufficiently thick to prevent burning. Only flip after the surface has set.
8.  Remove from the pan and cut into pieces. The exterior is crispy, while the interior is soft, chewy, and resilient.

## Health Advice
This fermented rice cake is well-fermented and easy to digest. It is suitable for those with weak spleen and stomach or those needing to control sugar intake (the 60g sugar version is recommended). Be sure to choose the correct yeast type based on the amount of sugar used, otherwise fermentation may fail.
