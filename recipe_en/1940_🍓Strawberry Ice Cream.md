## Steps
1. Make strawberry syrup: Wash the strawberries, remove the stems, and cut into 5mm small cubes. Set aside half of the strawberry cubes.
2. Place the other half of the strawberry cubes and white sugar into a saucepan. Heat over medium heat and stir until the strawberries release their juice and form a syrup.
3. Continue heating, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries gradually soften and the syrup thickens slightly. Remove from heat when a line drawn through the syrup holds for 3 seconds without closing.
4. Add 1g of vanilla extract and 1g of salt to the syrup. Stir to combine and let cool.
5. Make the ice cream base: In a bowl, combine sweetened condensed milk, heavy cream, 5g of vanilla extract, and 1g of salt. Whisk with a hand whisk until the mixture is light and fluffy and forms soft peaks when lifted.
6. Gently fold the reserved fresh strawberry cubes into the ice cream base, then transfer to a freezer-safe container.
7. Drizzle the cooled strawberry syrup over the mixture. Use a spatula to lightly swirl it into streaks. Cover the container and freeze for 8 hours before scooping and serving.

## Health Suggestions
Strawberries are rich in vitamin C, but when paired with a large amount of condensed milk and cream, the sugar and fat content is relatively high. Suitable for occasional consumption; those with blood sugar sensitivities should exercise caution. Freezing thoroughly for 8 hours yields the best texture. It is recommended to control your single serving size.
