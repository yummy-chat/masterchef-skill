# Pan-Fried Stuffed Tofu
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🧈 Firm tofu, about 1 block (trim into regular rectangular thick slices)
🥩 Lean pork belly, as needed (finely minced)
🦐 Shelled green shrimp, as needed (minced together with pork belly)
🥚 Eggs, as needed (beaten into egg wash)
🌾 All-purpose flour, as needed (for coating the tofu; do not use starch)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (some cut into shreds, some minced)
🧄 Ginger, as needed (some cut into shreds, some minced)
🍲 Stock, as needed (made by simmering old chicken, pork ribs, pork knuckles, dried scallops, and Jinhua ham, then strained)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for garnish before serving)

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt, as needed (for curing tofu, seasoning filling, and adjusting flavor during braising)
🌶️ White pepper powder, as needed (to remove gamey smell and enhance aroma when seasoning filling)
🍬 Sugar, a pinch (to enhance freshness when seasoning filling)
💧 Water, a small amount (to adjust moisture when seasoning filling)
🥤 Soy sauce, 1-2 drops (for color and freshness during braising)
🫒 Sesame oil, a drizzle (added just before serving)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🍽️ Bowls or plates (for holding egg wash, flour, and filling)
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer (for straining stock)
🥢 Chopsticks (for beating eggs, mixing filling, and removing solid bits from the pan)
🔗 Wok spatula (for assisting flipping and drizzling oil)

## Steps
1. Trim the edges of the firm tofu and cut into rectangular slices of even thickness and size. Trim the shape first, then slice, ensuring all tofu pieces cook evenly.
2. Combine the minced pork belly and minced shrimp. Add salt, white pepper powder, sugar, and a small amount of water. Mix by hand and repeatedly slap the mixture until it becomes sticky and springy.
3. Cure the sliced tofu pieces with a little salt, minced scallion, and minced ginger for a short while. The filling can be prepared simultaneously to improve efficiency.
4. Take one slice of cured tofu, spread an even layer of the prepared filling on it, then cover with another slice of tofu. Gently press down to form a stuffed tofu sandwich. Handle carefully to prevent the tofu from breaking.
5. First, dip the stuffed tofu sandwich into the well-beaten egg wash, ensuring it is evenly coated. Then, place the egg-coated tofu sandwich into the flour, making sure both sides and edges are lightly and evenly dusted with flour. Traditional method uses flour instead of starch.
6. Heat oil in a cold wok, arrange the floured tofu sandwiches neatly in the wok, and pan-fry over medium-low heat until both sides are golden brown. Once the bottom is set and golden, flip the pieces. Mastering the technique of flipping the wok is essential—this is the essence of the "pan-fried" method.
7. Sprinkle shredded scallion and ginger over the pan-fried tofu. Pour in the strained stock, adding enough to roughly submerge the tofu. Adjust seasoning with a little salt and 1-2 drops of soy sauce. Reduce heat to low and simmer. The tofu should be braised for a while to fully absorb the flavors until it has soaked up the stock.
8. When the sauce thickens, use chopsticks to remove the scallion and ginger shreds from the wok for a neat presentation. Add cilantro segments. Increase heat to high to reduce the sauce until thick, drizzle with a little sesame oil, and slide the entire stuffed tofu sandwich onto a plate.

## Health Tips
Tofu paired with shrimp and pork belly provides both plant-based and animal-based protein, making it suitable for those needing calcium and extra energy. It is recommended to reduce the amount of salt in the stock to control sodium intake.
