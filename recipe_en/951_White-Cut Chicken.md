# White-Cut Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October), Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 Qingyuan chicken
🍖 1 piece Jinhua ham (pre-roasted for 30 minutes)
🧅 Ginger, scallions (as needed for ginger-scallion water)
🐾 Chicken feet (several, removed from the Qingyuan chicken, keep some joints when cutting)
🧂 Salt (to taste)
🍶 Huadiao wine (to taste)
🐟 Fish sauce (to taste)
🫚 Minced sand ginger (to taste)
🧄 Minced shallots (to taste)
🥢 Soy sauce (to taste)
🥜 Peanut oil (to taste)
🌿 A few gardenia fruits (for coloring)
🐟 1 dried flathead fish (for umami)
🧂 Dried spices: dried tangerine peel, white cardamom, fennel, dried sand ginger, Solomon’s seal, black cardamom, astragalus root, white peppercorns, Chinese angelica, straight ladybell, codonopsis root, nutmeg (3 small pieces), licorice (a small amount) — a pinch of each

## Cookware & Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker
🍲 Stainless steel soup pot
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
🧤 Spice bag
🥄 Skimmer
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for ice water)

## Preparation Steps
1. Place dried tangerine peel, white cardamom, fennel, dried sand ginger, Solomon’s seal, black cardamom, astragalus root, white peppercorns, Chinese angelica, straight ladybell, codonopsis root, nutmeg, licorice, dried flathead fish, and gardenia fruits into a spice bag. Pre-roast the Jinhua ham for 30 minutes.
**Key point**: Gardenia fruits are used to color the chicken skin; roasting the Jinhua ham enhances its flavor.

2. Remove excess fat from the Qingyuan chicken. Cut off the chicken feet, keeping some joints attached. Reserve the chicken feet for later use.
**Note**: When cutting the joints, keep part of the drumstick to prevent the leg from tightening during cooking, which would affect the appearance.

3. In a stainless steel soup pot, add an appropriate amount of water and ginger and scallions to make ginger-scallion water.
4. Fill the pressure cooker with water, add the spice bag and roasted Jinhua ham. Once steam is released, cook under pressure for 15 minutes.
5. Combine the pressed master stock base with the ginger-scallion water.
**Note**: The spice bag can remain in the stock; remove it when replacing the spices next time.

6. Season the mixed stock with an appropriate amount of salt, Huadiao wine, and fish sauce. The flavor should be relatively light.
7. Submerge the Qingyuan chicken into the stock, then lift it out a few times to tighten the skin. Drain any cold water from the cavity. Repeat the submerge-and-lift process three times.
**Key point**: This step tightens the chicken skin to prevent it from overcooking and ensures even internal temperature.

8. Fully submerge the chicken in the stock, weigh it down with a heavy object, turn off the heat, and steep for 40 minutes.
**Key point**: Use the residual heat of the stock to gently cook the chicken, ensuring tender meat.

9. Remove the chicken and soak it in ice water for about 5 minutes.
**Key point**: The ice water shocks the chicken, making the skin crisp and stopping the cooking process to prevent overcooking.

10. In a bowl, combine minced sand ginger and minced shallots, then drizzle in an appropriate amount of soy sauce and peanut oil to make a dipping sauce. Serve alongside the white-cut chicken.

## Health Tips
White-cut chicken, served deboned, is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for weight-loss periods. Use peanut oil in the dipping sauce in moderation. Those with weak spleen and stomach should eat sparingly to avoid greasiness; using residual heat during steeping makes the meat more tender.
