# East-West Fusion Korean Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh meat, appropriate amount (cut into bite-sized pieces)  
🍚 Rice flour, appropriate amount (1:1 ratio with all-purpose flour)  
🌾 All-purpose flour, appropriate amount (1:1 ratio with rice flour)  
🌽 Cornstarch, appropriate amount (0.5 ratio to the previous two, i.e., 1:1:0.5)  
🧅 Red onion, appropriate amount (cut into pieces, used for stir-frying as the base of the sauce)  
🍋 Lemon, 1 (juiced)  

🌶️ Korean chili powder, appropriate amount (fine texture, mild heat)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (slightly more than usual for marinating, for base flavor)  
🍛 Curry powder, appropriate amount (key seasoning for fusion cuisine, adds complex aroma)  
🌿 Cinnamon powder, appropriate amount (removes gamey smell and enhances flavor)  
⚫ Black pepper powder, appropriate amount  
🍺 Beer, one can (substitutes for rice wine, makes the meat tender)  
🥄 Chicken bouillon, a pinch  
🥛 Milk, appropriate amount (enhances flavor, removes gamey smell, balances acidity)  
🥫 Korean chili paste, appropriate amount (main flavor source for the sauce)  
🥫 Tonkatsu sauce, appropriate amount (key seasoning for fusion cuisine)  
🍯 Honey (or malt syrup), appropriate amount (provides sweetness and shine)  
🍗 Chicken stock, appropriate amount (enhances savory flavor)  
🌶️ Chili flakes, appropriate amount (provides heat and color)  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar, a dash  
🍋 Lemon juice, a few drops (added at the end before finishing the sauce)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Large wok (for simmering the sauce)  
🍳 Non-stick pan or deep frying pan (for frying the chicken)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for marinating the chicken)  
🍽️ Plates or bowls (for holding the coating mix, sauce, etc.)  
🥄 Spoons (for stirring and scooping)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing the chicken)  
🧻 Paper towels (for drying off moisture)  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Cut the chicken thigh meat into bite-sized pieces for even marination and frying. Mix rice flour, all-purpose flour, and cornstarch in a 1:1:0.5 ratio and set aside. Cut the red onion into pieces and juice the lemon; set aside.  
2.  **First Marination**: Add Korean chili powder, salt, curry powder, cinnamon powder, and black pepper powder to the chicken pieces. Mix well, then add scallion sections, ginger slices, and garlic slices. Pour in the entire can of beer and mix again. Refrigerate and marinate for 1 hour.  
3.  **Second Marination**: Remove the chicken from the refrigerator. Pour in an appropriate amount of milk, mix well, and let it sit for 10–20 minutes. Milk is a crucial step in Korean fried chicken for removing gamey smell and enhancing flavor, and it also makes the meat more tender.  
4.  Remove the chicken pieces and gently pat them dry with paper towels to remove surface marinade and moisture. This ensures even coating and prevents oil splatter during frying.  
5.  Place the dried chicken pieces into the mixed coating powder. Coat each piece evenly, then shake off any excess powder.  
6.  **Make the Sauce**: Heat a little oil in the wok over low heat. Stir-fry the red onion pieces until fragrant. Add Korean chili paste, tonkatsu sauce, honey, a suitable amount of water, chicken stock, black pepper powder, curry powder, and chili flakes. Stir and simmer over low heat until the sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon. Just before finishing, drizzle in the apple cider vinegar and a few drops of lemon juice.  
7.  **First Frying**: Heat the oil to 150–160°C (300–320°F). Add the coated chicken pieces one by one. Fry over medium heat until they float and the exterior turns a light golden color. Remove and drain on paper towels.  
8.  **Double-Frying**: Increase the oil temperature to around 170°C (340°F). Add all the once-fried chicken pieces back in. Quickly fry for 5–10 seconds until the color deepens and the exterior becomes golden and crispy. Immediately remove.  
9.  **Coat with Sauce**: Place half of the hot, double-fried chicken pieces into the pan with the sauce. Using the residual heat of the chicken, quickly toss and stir to coat each piece evenly with the sauce. Leave the other half uncoated for a plain version.  
10. Arrange both flavors on separate plates. Garnish the sauced chicken with white sesame seeds if desired.  

## Health Suggestions  
This Korean fried chicken features a fusion of Eastern and Western seasonings. Marinating with beer and milk makes the meat more tender, making it suitable for those who love rich flavors. Be careful to control the frying temperature to prevent excess oil absorption. It is recommended to serve with vegetables to balance the meal.
