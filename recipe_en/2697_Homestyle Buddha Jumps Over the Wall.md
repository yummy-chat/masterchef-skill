# Homestyle Buddha Jumps Over the Wall  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Liaoning sea cucumber 5-6 (thawed in advance)  
🐚 Abalone 5-6  
🥚 Quail eggs to taste  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms to taste (scored)  
🍄 King oyster mushrooms to taste (sliced)  
🦴 Tendons to taste  
🍗 Chicken feet (or pork trotters) to taste (for broth, adds gelatin)  
🦴 Pork leg bone a small amount (for broth base)  
🐔 Old hen a small amount (for broth base)  
🥩 Ham 1 piece (for broth)  
🎃 Pumpkin to taste (mashed cooked pumpkin)  

🧅 Scallions to taste (deodorizing)  
🫚 Ginger to taste (deodorizing)  
🍶 Cooking wine to taste  
🍶 Huadiao wine to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce to taste  
🧂 Light soy sauce to taste  
🧂 Salt to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste  
🧂 MSG to taste  
🥄 Water starch to taste (for thickening before serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Stockpot (for blanching and broth)  
🥄 Strainer/sieve (for mashing pumpkin)  
🥘 Clay pot/soup pot (for final simmering)  
🥄 Ladle (for skimming foam and stirring seasoning)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Thaw sea cucumbers in advance. Blanch shiitake mushrooms, king oyster mushrooms, quail eggs, and tendons in boiling water, then drain and set aside.  
**Key point**: Blanching removes impurities and partially pre-cooks the ingredients.  

2. Place pork leg bone, old hen, and tendons in a pot, add water. Add scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine to remove gamey smell. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off foam. Then add ham, reduce to low heat, and simmer for 3-4 hours. Strain to obtain the broth.  
**Note**: Foam must be skimmed promptly for a clear and flavorful broth.  

3. Steam pumpkin until cooked, then mash it. Press through a strainer to obtain a smooth pumpkin puree.  
4. Pour the strained broth into the pumpkin puree, stirring well. Add Huadiao wine, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, and MSG. Taste and adjust seasoning with more oyster sauce or salt as needed.  
5. Bring the broth to a boil. First add tendons, quail eggs, king oyster mushrooms, and shiitake mushrooms, cooking for about 15 minutes. Then add sea cucumber and abalone, gently mixing.  
6. If the broth is not thick enough, drizzle in water starch to thicken. Turn to high heat and stir gently until the broth becomes glossy and thick, then serve.  
**Key point**: Do not cook long after thickening to prevent sea cucumber and abalone from becoming tough.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in collagen, protein, and trace elements, suitable for autumn and winter nourishment or for those with high physical exertion. Sea cucumber and abalone are low in fat but high in purines; those with gout should consume in moderation and control portion sizes.
