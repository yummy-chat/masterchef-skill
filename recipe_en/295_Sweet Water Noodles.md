# Sweet Water Noodles
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 250g  
🍬 Brown sugar 200g  
💧 Water 120ml (for dough) + as needed (to dilute soy sauce)  
🥜 Roasted peanuts 1 tbsp (chopped)  
🧂 Light soy sauce 150ml  
🧂 Dark soy sauce 100ml  
🧂 Salt 1/2 tsp (for kneading dough)  
🍯 Sesame paste 1 tbsp  
🌶️ Chili oil 1 tsp  
🧄 Minced garlic 1/4 tsp  
🌿 Dried tangerine peel 1 small piece  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🍂 Bay leaves 2 pieces  
🌰 Nutmeg 1 piece  
🌸 Clove 1 piece  
🌱 Fennel seeds 1/2 tsp  
🧅 Sand ginger 2 pieces  
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder optional, a pinch  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for simmering sauce)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for kneading and resting dough)  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🍲 Large pot (for cooking noodles)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫗 Fine mesh strainer (for filtering spices)  
🫙 Glass jar or bottle (for storing sweet soy sauce)  
🥄 Spoons (for measuring seasonings)  
🍜 Bowl (for serving and mixing noodles)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, brown sugar, dried tangerine peel, cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, nutmeg, clove, fennel seeds, sand ginger, and enough water to dilute into a flat-bottomed pan.  
**Note**: Adding water dilutes the mixture to prevent burning during simmering.  
2. Simmer over low heat for 10 to 15 minutes until the brown sugar is fully dissolved, the spices release their aroma, and the soy sauce thickens and forms large bubbles. Turn off the heat when it becomes slightly syrupy when lifted with a spoon.  
3. Let the sweet soy sauce cool completely, then strain out the spice residue using a fine mesh strainer. Pour into a jar and set aside.  
**Key point**: The flavors continue to meld as it cools, resulting in a richer sauce.  
4. Mix all-purpose flour, salt, and water to form a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.  
5. Fold and roll the rested dough repeatedly 3 to 5 times, roll into a large sheet about 8mm thick, dust with dry flour to prevent sticking, fold in half, and cut into strips about 8mm wide. Dust with flour and spread out to prevent sticking.  
**Key point**: Repeated rolling makes the noodles smoother and chewier.  
6. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Gently stretch the noodles and add them to the pot. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes until they float and expand in size, and taste no raw flour flavor when done.  
7. Remove the cooked noodles into a bowl. Add sweet soy sauce, sesame paste, chili oil, minced garlic, and crushed peanuts. Optionally, sprinkle a pinch of Sichuan peppercorn powder to taste.  
8. Mix all seasonings thoroughly with the noodles and serve hot.  

## Health Advice  
Sweet Water Noodles contain a high amount of brown sugar, so they are best enjoyed as an occasional snack, suitable for those with high energy expenditure or needing a quick energy boost. Diabetic patients and those monitoring sugar intake should exercise caution and can reduce the amount of sweet soy sauce. The noodles, repeatedly rolled for a chewy texture, paired with peanuts and sesame paste, can increase protein intake.
