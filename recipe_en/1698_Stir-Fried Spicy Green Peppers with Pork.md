# Stir-Fried Spicy Green Peppers with Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork slices – to taste (specific cut not specified)
🌶️ Spicy green peppers – to taste (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic – a small amount (minced)
🧂 Light soy sauce – to taste (for marinating) + half a tablespoon (for sauce)
🧂 Dark soy sauce – to taste (for marinating) + half a tablespoon (for sauce)
🌽 Cornstarch – to taste (for marinating)
🫒 Cooking oil – to taste (for marinating) + to taste (for stir-frying)
🧂 Salt – to taste (for dry-frying peppers)
🦪 Oyster sauce – half a tablespoon (for sauce)
🍬 White sugar – half a tablespoon (for sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Bowl (for marinating pork slices and mixing sauce)
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Spatula or silicone spatula
🥄 Spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the pork slices with light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and cornstarch in a bowl. Mix evenly with your hands until coated, then drizzle in a small amount of cooking oil and mix again. Marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Marinating removes any off-flavors, enhances taste, and locks in moisture, making the meat slices more tender.
2. In a small bowl, combine half a tablespoon of light soy sauce, half a tablespoon of dark soy sauce, half a tablespoon of oyster sauce, and half a tablespoon of white sugar to make the sauce. Set aside.
3. Without adding oil to the non-stick pan, add the spicy green pepper sections and sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Stir-fry over medium heat until the peppers are cooked through and develop a blister-like skin on the surface. Remove and set aside.
   **Key Point**: Dry-frying removes the raw taste and excess moisture from the peppers, making them more fragrant and easier to absorb flavors.
4. Pour cooking oil into the pan and heat. Add the marinated pork slices and quickly stir-fry over high heat until the meat changes color and is fully cooked.
5. Add the minced garlic, stir-fry until fragrant, then immediately add the dry-fried spicy green peppers and pour in the prepared sauce.
   **Note**: Add the garlic later to avoid burning it from prolonged heating.
6. Turn the heat to high and quickly stir-fry for about 30 seconds, ensuring the sauce evenly coats the pork and peppers. Once the sauce thickens and the ingredients are glistening with oil, remove from heat and serve.

## Health Advice
This dish pairs pork with peppers, offering a complementary balance of protein and vitamin C. However, the marination uses a fair amount of oil and cornstarch, so it is recommended to serve with a portion of stir-fried leafy greens to balance the calories. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy food and manage their body fat—opt for lean cuts of pork.
