# Shredded Crispy Pork Intestine
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Raw pork large intestine 5 jin (or 2 jin regular intestine + 2 jin large intestine head)  
🧅 Scallion 30g  
🫚 Ginger 30g  
⭐ Star anise 4g (about 2 pieces)  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1g  
🍂 Cardamom 1 piece (seeds removed, skin retained)  
🌱 Licorice 2g  
🪵 Galangal 1g  
🍃 Angelica dahurica 1.5g  
🍂 Bay leaves 2 leaves  
🌿 Cumin 1g  
🧂 Table salt (for cleaning, added in batches)  
🌾 Flour (for cleaning, added in batches)  
🫒 Cooking oil (for cleaning, about one swirl)  
🍶 High-proof liquor (for blanching, about one swirl)  
🍶 White vinegar (for blanching, about one swirl)  
💧 Water 2000ml (for the braising liquid)  
🍶 Cooking wine 30g  
🥄 Light soy sauce 60g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 15g  
🍬 White sugar 40g  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (optional)  
🍶 White vinegar 80g (for the crispy skin liquid)  
🍶 Red Zhejiang vinegar 20g  
🍯 Maltose 10g  
🫒 Cooking oil (for spraying before roasting, a thin layer)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin  
🍲 Stainless steel pot/glass pot  
♨️ Air fryer  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥢 Bamboo skewers  
👕 Clothes hangers  
🥘 Metal pan/tray  
🖌️ Brush  
🥄 Small pot (for heating maltose over water)  
🛍️ Sealed bag  
🥣 Bowls, spoons  

## Preparation Steps
1. First cleaning: Place the raw large intestine directly into a basin without water. Add a generous amount of salt and one large spoonful of flour. Vigorously rub and knead to remove the mucus. Rinse thoroughly with clean water. Remove any excess lymph nodes from the intestine head and squeeze out the water.
2. Second cleaning: Add one swirl of cooking oil and mix well, using the oil to absorb fat-soluble impurities. Once mucus is released, add another large spoonful of flour. Continue kneading until sticky. Do not add water. Then rinse clean and squeeze out the water.
3. Turn inside out to remove fat: Turn the intestine inside out to expose the side with fat and lymph. Thoroughly tear off all fat and lymph. While tearing, you can run a thin stream of water. After tearing, sprinkle in table salt and rub until it dissolves. Rinse and squeeze dry.
4. Turn back to the smooth side and perform the third cleaning: Turn each piece of intestine back to the smooth side. Sprinkle in table salt and rub until mucus appears. Add one large spoonful of flour and coat evenly. Let it rest for 15 minutes, then rinse clean.
5. Blanching: Place the intestine in a pot with cold water. Add one swirl of high-proof liquor and one swirl of white vinegar. Bring to a boil over high heat until a large amount of foam rises. Turn off the heat. Remove and rinse the inside and outside thoroughly with warm water.
6. Braising: Place the blanched and squeezed-dry intestine in a pot. Add scallion, ginger, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon stick, cardamom skin, licorice, galangal, angelica dahurica, and cumin. Add 2000ml water, 30g cooking wine, 60g light soy sauce, 15g chicken bouillon, 40g white sugar, and dried chilies (optional). Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Cover and cook for 40 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through.
7. Prepare the crispy skin liquid: In a small bowl, combine 80g white vinegar, 20g red Zhejiang vinegar, and 10g maltose. Heat over water (bain-marie) and stir until the maltose is completely dissolved.
8. Skewer and air-dry: Let the braised intestine cool until it is no longer hot to the touch. Thread onto bamboo skewers. Trim off any excess length. Hang the skewers on a clothes hanger, keeping space between them. Use a brush to evenly coat with the crispy skin liquid. Apply a total of three coats. Then air-dry in a ventilated area for 2-3 hours, or use a fan for about 2 hours, until the skin feels dry to the touch.
9. Air fryer roasting: Cut the air-dried intestine into sections about 15 cm long. Arrange in the air fryer. Spray with a thin layer of oil. For thinner intestine, roast at 200°C for 10 minutes. For thicker large intestine head, roast at 200°C for 10 minutes, then flip and roast at 200°C for another 10 minutes. The skin should be deep red, glossy, bubbly, and crispy. Note that cooking times may vary by about 2 minutes depending on the power of different air fryer models.
10. Portion and plate: Cut the roasted intestine into small pieces. Can be served sprinkled with BBQ seasoning or with Thai sweet chili sauce.

## Health Advice
This shredded crispy pork intestine is rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, people with high cholesterol or sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation. The multiple cleaning steps during cooking help reduce fat and greasiness, and pairing it with a vinegar-based sauce can aid digestion.
