# Homemade Crispy Pork Chop
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork, appropriate amount (mostly lean, with a little fat)  
🍞 Thick-cut white bread slices, 3-4 slices  
🥚 Egg white, 1  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste  
🍂 Five-spice powder, to taste  
🔴 Red yeast rice powder, to taste (for coloring)  
🫚 Ginger juice, to taste  
🌾 Cornstarch, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (some mixed into the meat filling, some for frying)  
🫙 Basting sauce (red sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or barbecue sauce), to taste  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔥 Oven  
🌀 Meat grinder  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍳 Frying pan  
🫙 Glass bowl, stainless steel basin  
📄 Plastic wrap  
🖌️ Basting brush  

## Instructions  
1. Place the thick-cut white bread slices upright on the oven rack and bake until dry, hard, and golden brown. Remove and crush into breadcrumbs. Set aside.  
   **Key point**: The bread slices must be thoroughly dried; after crushing, the texture should be uniform to aid coating later.  

2. Cut the pork into pieces and place into the meat grinder. Add salt, white sugar, white pepper powder, five-spice powder, red yeast rice powder, ginger juice, and cornstarch. Grind until the meat filling is fine and firm.  

3. Take some of the ground meat filling, add 1 egg white and an appropriate amount of cooking oil, and continue grinding until evenly mixed, resulting in a more tender and smooth chop mixture.  

4. Form the chop mixture into a ball and roll it in the breadcrumbs until both sides are evenly coated with a thick layer. Cover with a layer of plastic wrap and gently roll out into a thin slice with a rolling pin.  
   **Key point**: The plastic wrap prevents sticking during rolling; try to keep the chop thickness uniform to ensure even doneness.  

5. Pour a generous amount of cooking oil into a frying pan and heat. Add the chop and pan-fry over medium-low heat, semi-deep frying. Flip as needed, cooking until both sides are golden and crispy and the chop is fully cooked through.  
   **Note**: Do not use too high heat to avoid burning the outside while the inside remains raw.  

6. Remove the cooked chop and, while still hot, use a basting brush to coat with a layer of red sauce. Serve.  

## Health Suggestion  
This crispy pork chop contains both fat and protein, making it suitable for muscle building or as an occasional treat, but due to its high oil and calorie content, portion control is important. Using egg white and ginger juice to tenderize the meat makes it slightly healthier than purely deep-frying.
