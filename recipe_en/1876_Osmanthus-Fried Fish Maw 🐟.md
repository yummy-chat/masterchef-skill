# Osmanthus-Fried Fish Maw 🐟
Cuisine: Cantonese 🥘
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Intermediate)
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons 🗓️

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Freshwater fish maw (fish bladder) – 1 piece (thin type, suitable for oil puffing)
🥚 Eggs – 6 egg yolks + 2 whole eggs
🌱 Mung bean sprouts – to taste
🥬 Celery – to taste (cut into sections)
🥬 Leek – to taste (cut into sections)
🌶️ Red bell pepper shreds – to taste (for color)
🥓 Ham shreds – to taste (sprinkled last to enhance umami)
🫚 Ginger – to taste (sliced or crushed, for simmering fish maw)
🧅 Scallions – to taste (cut into sections, for simmering fish maw)
⚪ White vinegar – to taste (for cleaning and deodorizing)
🧂 Salt – to taste (used multiple times)
🍬 Sugar – to taste (to enhance umami)
🧂 White pepper powder – to taste (to remove fishy smell and add aroma)
🍶 Cooking wine – to taste (to remove fishy smell)
🧴 Soy sauce – to taste (for color and flavor)
🧈 Chicken fat – to taste (to enhance aroma and tenderness)
🫒 Cooking oil – generous amount (for deep frying)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🍚 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. 🫚 Submerge the dried freshwater fish maw into a wok of oil heated to 180°C. Use a slotted spoon to press it down for even heating. Deep-fry for about 3–4 minutes until it fully puffs up and expands.
**Key point**: The oil must reach 180°C; the fish maw will expand immediately upon contact. It needs to be fried until the interior is completely airy; otherwise, it will easily break apart when soaked.

2. 🫚 Remove the deep-fried fish maw and place it in a basin. Soak in water for about half an hour until softened. Squeeze out excess water, then rub and wash with white vinegar to remove oil and impurities. Rinse until the fish maw turns white and has no odor.
**Key point**: Washing with white vinegar effectively removes the oil attached from the puffing process as well as the fishy smell of the fish maw itself.

3. 🫚 Sauté the ginger and scallions in a wok until fragrant. Add water, cooking wine, salt, white pepper powder, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then add the shredded fish maw. Simmer over medium heat for 2 minutes to remove fishy smell, enhance aroma, and infuse flavor.
**Key point**: The simmering step allows the fish maw to absorb a base flavor.

4. 🫚 Heat another wok and stir-fry the mung bean sprouts. Season with a pinch of salt and sugar. Add celery and leek, stir-fry until just cooked, then remove and set aside.
5. 🫚 Crack 6 egg yolks and 2 whole eggs into a bowl. Add salt and white pepper powder. Beat until the egg mixture forms a thin, flowing stream. Finally, add an appropriate amount of chicken fat and mix well.
**Key point**: Adding chicken fat uses its separating property to produce tender, flaky scrambled eggs.

### Cooking Stage
6. 🫚 Heat the wok and add chicken fat. Pour in the prepared egg mixture. When the eggs are half set, quickly add the simmered fish maw shreds.
7. 🫚 Reduce to medium heat and stir-fry slowly. Break the eggs apart, allowing them to form small, osmanthus-like pieces that wrap around the fish maw.
**Key point**: Medium heat, slow stir-frying; patiently work to achieve the “osmanthus” texture.

8. 🫚 Add the previously stir-fried bean sprouts, celery, and leek. Add the red bell pepper shreds and stir-fry evenly. Finally, sprinkle with ham shreds and serve.
**Key point**: The ham shreds greatly enhance the dish’s flavor profile.

## Health Suggestions
This dish features fish maw, rich in collagen, paired with egg yolks and bean sprouts. It is suitable for individuals needing protein supplementation and improved skin elasticity. However, egg yolks are high in cholesterol, so those with high blood pressure should control their portions. Since the fish maw is deep-fried, the calorie content is elevated; during weight loss, the amount of chicken fat used can be reduced accordingly.
