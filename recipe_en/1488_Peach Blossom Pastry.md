# Peach Blossom Pastry
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 120g (for water-oil dough)
🌾 All-purpose flour 50g (for oil dough)
🐖 Lard 50g (for water-oil dough)
🐖 Lard 50g (for oil dough)
💧 Water 50g
🫘 Jujube paste filling appropriate amount (divided into 10 balls, can be substituted with red bean paste)
🍬 White sugar 10g
🌸 Cactus fruit powder 10g (for coloring, can be omitted or replaced with other)
🥚 Egg yolk liquid appropriate amount (for decorating the flower center)
🤍 White sesame seeds appropriate amount (for decorating the flower center)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🍽️ Spatula
🍽️ Silicone mat
🛡️ Plastic wrap
🪔 Rolling pin
🔪 Small knife
🖌️ Small brush (or spoon handle)
🔥 Oven or air fryer
⚖️ Measuring tools (bottle cap, grid estimation)

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix 120 grams of all-purpose flour, 50 grams of lard, 50 grams of water, and 10 grams of white sugar together, stirring into a dough.
**Note**: The dough will not stick to your hands when lard is used; lard does not need to be melted, use it directly.

2. Place the dough on a work surface and repeatedly slap and knead it, or refrigerate the mixed dough overnight to substitute for kneading.
**Key point**: No need to knead to a "windowpane" state; it is acceptable if it can be pulled into a thicker film.

3. Wrap the kneaded dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
4. Mix 50 grams of all-purpose flour, 50 grams of lard, and 10 grams of cactus fruit powder together, stirring until evenly combined.
5. Divide the water-oil dough, oil dough, and jujube paste filling each into 10 equal portions.
6. Take one portion of water-oil dough, roll it flat, wrap in one portion of oil dough, seal the opening using the tiger's mouth technique, and roll it into a ball.
7. Roll the wrapped dough into a tongue shape, roll it up from top to bottom, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for 15 minutes.
**Key point**: First rolling and resting.

8. Take the rested dough roll, roll it out into a long strip again, roll it up, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for 15 minutes.
**Key point**: The second rolling increases the number of pastry layers.

9. Press the ends of the rested dough roll toward the center, roll it into a round wrapper with a slightly thicker center and thinner edges, wrap in the jujube paste filling, seal the opening using the tiger's mouth technique, and roll it into a ball with the seam facing down.
**Condition check**: The wrapper should be soft and not sticky, easy to wrap.

10. Gently flatten the filled ball, make 5 cuts with a small knife, then pinch the edge of each section into a petal shape.
**Key point**: Apply moderate force when pinching the petals to form pointed tips.

11. Use a small knife to score patterns on the flower center, brush with a little egg yolk liquid, and sprinkle with white sesame seeds.
12. Place in a preheated oven and bake at 160 degrees Celsius (top and bottom heat) for 20 minutes; or use an air fryer at 160 degrees Celsius for about 20 minutes.
**Condition check**: Bake until the pastry layers are distinct and the color has slightly darkened.

## Health Advice
The amount of lard used is relatively high, making the calorie content quite concentrated. It is recommended to consume 1-2 pieces per serving, and pairing with green tea can help cut the richness. Although jujube paste filling is relatively healthy, its sugar content is not low; diabetic patients should consume in moderation.
