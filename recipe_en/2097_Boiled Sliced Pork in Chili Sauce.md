# Boiled Sliced Pork in Chili Sauce

Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 Seasonings
- Table salt
- Ground white pepper
- Light soy sauce
- Cooking wine
- Chicken bouillon
- White granulated sugar
- Red chili bean paste (Doubanjiang)
- Rapeseed oil
- Vegetable oil

🥩 Ingredients
- 300g pork tenderloin
- 1 egg white
- 7g potato starch
- 100g mung bean sprouts
- 1 piece of Chinese lettuce (Celtuce tip)
- 3 stalks of Chinese celery
- 2 stalks of garlic sprouts
- 2 scallions
- 20g garlic
- 10g ginger
- 20g red pickled chili peppers
- 20g small dried chili peppers (Thai bird's eye type)
- 5g green Sichuan peppercorns

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🍳 Utensils
- Chef's knife
- Chopping board
- Bowls
- Wok or frying pan
- Spatula

## Cooking Steps
1.  Cut the pork tenderloin into small pieces, then slice into pieces about 2mm thick. Rinse twice with clean water to remove blood and impurities. Drain and squeeze out excess water.
2.  Add 1.5g salt, 1g white pepper, 5g light soy sauce, and 3g cooking wine to the meat slices. Stir continuously in one direction for 2 minutes to fully season the meat.
3.  In a separate bowl, mix 1 egg white and 7g potato starch, stirring in one direction until smooth. Pour this mixture over the meat and stir again in the same direction until the meat is coated with a smooth film. Finally, add 30g vegetable oil and gently mix to prevent sticking.
4.  Wash 100g mung bean sprouts. Cut 1 piece of Chinese lettuce into small strips. Cut 3 stalks of Chinese celery into small sections. Lightly crush 2 stalks of garlic sprouts and cut into small sections.
5.  Mince 20g garlic, 10g ginger, and 20g red pickled chili peppers.
6.  Take 15g small dried chili peppers and 3g green Sichuan peppercorns. Heat a little oil in the wok to coat it, leaving 50g base oil. Add the dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry over low heat until the color darkens and turns slightly black. Remove and chop finely to make "knife-edge chili" (Daokou La Jiao). Set aside.
7.  Heat the wok, add 100g vegetable oil until it reaches 60% heat. Add 2g green Sichuan peppercorns and a small amount of dried chilies, stir-fry until fragrant. Immediately add the bean sprouts, Chinese lettuce, celery, and garlic sprouts. Add 1g salt and stir-fry until just cooked. Transfer to a serving bowl to form the base layer.
8.  Clean the wok. Add 150g vegetable oil and heat to 60% heat. Add the minced ginger, garlic, and red pickled chilies, stir-fry until fragrant. Add 10g red chili bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red.
9.  Add 800ml of water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Season with 2.5g salt, 1.5g chicken bouillon, 1g white sugar, and 1g white pepper. Stir in 5g of water-starch mixture (slurry) to slightly thicken the broth.
10. Keep the broth on low heat. Carefully add the marinated meat slices one by one into the broth. Increase heat to medium and cook until the meat is just done. Gently push the meat with a spatula to prevent sticking. Once the broth boils again and the meat is fully cooked, remove the meat and place it on top of the vegetables in the bowl. Pour in enough of the original broth to just submerge the meat.
11. Evenly sprinkle the knife-edge chili, minced garlic, and chopped scallions over the meat.
12. Clean the wok. Add 200g rapeseed oil and heat to 70% heat. Pour the hot oil evenly over the meat slices in one go to release the aroma. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This Boiled Sliced Pork in Chili Sauce is rich in high-quality protein and vegetable fiber. However, it is high in fat and sodium, making it suitable for occasional indulgence. It is not recommended for those with high blood pressure or those on a weight-loss diet. Pairing it with a bowl of brown rice or a clear soup can help balance the greasiness.
