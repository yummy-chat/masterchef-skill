# Stir-Fried Sliced Conch with Ginger and Scallion  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Sliced conch appropriate amount (pre-cut into thick slices)  
🧅 Dried shallots appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧅 Shredded scallion appropriate amount (added before stir-frying is done)  
🌶️ Shredded red pepper appropriate amount (for garnish)  
🧑‍🍳 Rendered chicken fat from local chicken or capon appropriate amount (for making chicken oil)  
🫚 Local yellow ginger appropriate amount (crushed with skin on, for making chicken oil)  
🧅 Cantonese local red shallots appropriate amount (peeled, for making chicken oil)  
🧅 Local scallion white parts appropriate amount (can partially use spring onion, for making chicken oil)  
🌿 Cilantro stems appropriate amount (for making chicken oil)  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces (for making chicken oil)  
🍃 Bay leaf 1 piece (for making chicken oil)  
🍾 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🍶 Rice wine appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🌫️ White pepper powder appropriate amount  
💧 Water starch appropriate amount  
🫒 Chicken oil appropriate amount (used multiple times)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🔥 Stove  
🥣 Mixing bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place cleaned rendered chicken fat from local chicken or capon into the wok. Add crushed yellow ginger with skin, peeled red shallots, scallion white sections, cilantro stems, star anise, and bay leaf. Simmer over low heat for 1 hour, without adding any water, relying on the moisture from the chicken fat itself. The finished chicken oil can be stored sealed for half a month and is suitable for dishes like rice, noodles, or steamed fish.  
2. Pre-cut the conch into thick slices and place them in a strainer. Evenly cover the surface with shredded ginger to facilitate subsequent oil pouring and draining.  
3. Heat the chicken oil to 200°C. Evenly pour the hot oil over the conch slices covered with shredded ginger until the conch is about 70% cooked. Then drain off excess oil.  
**Note**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high and smoke heavily. Pouring oil over low heat allows precise control of doneness and prevents the conch from becoming tough.  

4. In a bowl, mix oyster sauce, rice wine, white sugar, white pepper powder, and water starch. Stir well to make a pre-mixed sauce for quick flavoring during the stir-fry.  
5. Add a small amount of chicken oil to the wok. Stir-fry the dried shallot slices over low heat until fragrant. Then add the 70% cooked conch slices. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly. Immediately pour in the prepared sauce and stir-fry rapidly to evenly coat the conch slices. Finally, add the shredded scallion and a little chicken oil. Stir-fry until evenly mixed, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.  
**Key**: The entire stir-frying process must be quick to prevent the conch from staying in the wok too long and becoming tough. Adding chicken oil just before removing from the wok enhances aroma and gloss.  

## Health Suggestions  
Conch is rich in protein and low in fat. Although ginger, scallion, and chicken oil add aroma, they are relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair with stir-fried seasonal vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for those needing zinc supplementation and weight management. Individuals with high blood lipids should use chicken oil with caution.
