# Steak and Tomato Pasta
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍝 Pasta: serves two  
🥩 Ground beef: appropriate amount  
🥩 Steak: two pieces  
🧅 Onion: appropriate amount (sliced)  
🍅 Cherry tomatoes: appropriate amount (halved)  
🧈 Butter: appropriate amount (for pasta and steak separately)  
🧀 Grated cheese: appropriate amount (for mixing into pasta and garnish)  
🥫 Tomato paste: appropriate amount  
🥫 Ketchup: appropriate amount  
🥫 Pasta sauce (store-bought): appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt: appropriate amount  
⚫ Black pepper (ground): appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Stockpot (for cooking pasta)  
🍳 Wok or frying pan (for cooking meat sauce and mixing pasta)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for searing steak)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥄 Mixing spoon  

## Steps  
1. Bring a stockpot of water to a boil, add the pasta, cover, and reduce to low heat or turn off the heat to simmer for about 10 minutes until the pasta is tender enough to be easily pinched. Drain and set aside.  
2. In a bowl, combine tomato paste, ketchup, pasta sauce, and an appropriate amount of salt. Add water and stir until a thin paste forms.  
   **Key Point**: Dilute the sauce in advance for easier and even mixing later.  
3. Heat a wok or frying pan, add the ground beef, and stir-fry until it breaks apart and changes color. Add the sliced onion and halved cherry tomatoes, and stir-fry until the onion softens.  
4. Pour the prepared mixed sauce into the pan, stir until well combined, cover, and simmer for 7–8 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  
5. In a separate non-stick frying pan, sear the steak directly (without oil) to your preferred doneness. Remove and cut into strips.  
   **Key Point**: Use the steak's own fat for searing.  
6. Add the drained pasta to the pan with the simmered meat sauce, and toss until evenly coated. Add a small piece of butter and an appropriate amount of grated cheese, and use residual heat to melt and mix them in for enhanced smoothness and flavor.  
7. Using the same pan from searing the steak, add the sliced steak strips, a small piece of butter, and ground black pepper. Toss until the butter coats the steak and adds aroma.  
8. Plate the tomato pasta, arrange the steak strips on top, and sprinkle additional grated cheese as desired.  

## Health Tips  
This dish provides ample protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for muscle building or individuals with high energy expenditure. Tomatoes and onions are rich in vitamin C, but the pasta sauce and cheese are high in sodium, so controlling salt intake is recommended. The overall fat content is not low; during weight loss, consider reducing the amount of butter.
