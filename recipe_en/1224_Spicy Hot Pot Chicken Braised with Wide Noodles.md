# Spicy Hot Pot Chicken Braised with Wide Noodles
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken leg meat (moderate amount, cut into pieces, blanched in advance to remove blood and gamey odor)
🥔 Potatoes (moderate amount, cut into rolling knife chunks)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms (moderate amount, cut into rolling knife chunks)
🍢 Fish balls or meatballs (moderate amount, type and quantity as desired)
🌶️ Green and red chili peppers (moderate amount, cut into sections, added at the end for color and flavor)
🧅 Scallions (moderate amount, for sautéing)
🧄 Ginger (moderate amount, for sautéing)
🧄 Garlic (moderate amount, for sautéing)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (moderate amount, for sautéing, to enhance spicy flavor)
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns (moderate amount, for sautéing, to enhance numbing flavor)
🍲 Hot pot base (1 piece)
🧂 Light soy sauce (3 tablespoons)
🧂 Dark soy sauce (1 tablespoon)
🦪 Oyster sauce (1 tablespoon)
🍯 Sweet bean sauce (1 tablespoon)
🍬 Sugar (half tablespoon)
♨️ Hot water (moderate amount, enough to submerge the ingredients)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥘 Glass lid

## Cooking Steps
1. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, dried chili peppers, and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add the hot pot base and stir-fry over medium-low heat until melted and aromatic.
**Key Point**: Stir-frying until the oil turns red and fragrant forms the flavor base of the entire dish. Use medium-low heat to prevent the spices from burning. Stir-fry until the hot pot base is completely melted, the oil is bright red, and the aroma is rich.
2. Add the blanched chicken leg pieces and stir-fry over high heat until evenly coated.
**Judgment Standard**: The chicken surface is evenly coated with the red oil and sauce color.
3. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sweet bean sauce, and sugar. Continue stir-frying over high heat until evenly mixed.
**Key Point**: Allow the chicken to fully absorb the flavors of the sauces.
4. Add hot water to the wok, ensuring the water level submerges the ingredients.
**Key Point**: Must use hot water to maintain the pot's temperature and help the broth emulsify and meld.
5. Cover with a lid and braise over medium-low heat for 10 minutes.
**Judgment Standard**: The broth remains at a boil, and the chicken starts to become tender and absorb flavor.
6. Remove the lid and add the cut potato chunks, shiitake mushroom chunks, and fish balls or meatballs.
**Key Point**: These ingredients require some time to braise until soft and tender.
7. Cover again and continue braising for 10 minutes.
**Judgment Standard**: Potatoes and shiitake mushrooms become soft, and fish balls or meatballs float to the surface.
8. Finally, add the pre-soaked or pre-boiled wide noodles and green and red chili pepper sections.
**Key Point**: Wide noodles cook quickly and absorb flavors well; they become mushy if cooked too long, so they should be added last.
9. Cover and braise for another 5 minutes, then remove from the wok and serve.
**Judgment Standard**: The broth thickens, the wide noodles become translucent and saturated with broth, and all ingredient flavors have melded together.

## Health Advice
This spicy hot pot chicken braised with wide noodles is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for warming the body in winter. However, it is high in oil and sodium. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance nutrition. People with high blood pressure or those on a weight-loss diet should consume it in moderation. The wide noodles, after absorbing the broth, are relatively high in calories.
