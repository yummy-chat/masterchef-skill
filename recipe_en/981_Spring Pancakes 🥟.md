# Spring Pancakes 🥟
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥟 Wonton wrappers, ten sheets per stack (store-bought wrappers)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (cooked oil, for brushing)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🪚 Cutting board
🖌️ Silicone brush
🥢 Rolling pin

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the wonton wrappers on the cutting board and gently pat them with your hand to remove any excess dry flour from the surface.
   **Note**: Removing dry flour helps the oil coating adhere better, making subsequent steps easier.
2. Dip a silicone brush in cooked oil and evenly coat the surface of each wonton wrapper.
   **Key**: Use enough oil; this is crucial for the layers to separate easily after steaming.
3. Stack the oiled wonton wrappers one by one, about ten sheets per stack, aligning the edges as closely as possible.
4. Apply even pressure with both palms and fingers to press the stack of wonton wrappers until it becomes thinner and larger in area, initially shaping it into a circle.
   **Key**: Apply pressure evenly to ensure each layer is pressed, preventing misalignment during rolling.
5. Use a rolling pin to roll out the pressed stack into a thin round pancake with a diameter of about 15–18 cm.
   **Key**: Roll from the center outward or rotate the stack while rolling to maintain uniform thickness.

## Health Tips
These spring pancakes are made with wonton wrappers, saving time and effort, and are perfect for those who want to enjoy spring flavors without kneading dough. Use enough oil so the layers are distinct after steaming, and they pair well with seasonal vegetables for wrapping. Note that the oil content increases the calorie count, so consume in moderation.
