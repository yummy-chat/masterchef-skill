# Pancakes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥛 Milk Powder 35g (for the crepe skin)
💧 Warm Water 240ml (for the crepe skin)
🍬 White Sugar 20g (for the crepe skin)
🌽 Corn Starch 30g (for the crepe skin)
🌾 Cake Flour 50g
🥚 Eggs 3 (approx. 130g, for the crepe skin)
🫒 Cooking Oil 10g (mild flavor, for the crepe skin)
🍓 Strawberries appropriate amount (diced for filling)

🧈 Egg Yolks 6 (approx. 210g, for the custard)
🍬 White Sugar 30g (for the custard)
🌽 Corn Starch 30g (for the custard)
🥛 Milk Powder 52g (for the custard)
💧 Warm Water 360ml (for the custard)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Large Glass Bowl
🥄 Whisk
🍳 Non-stick Frying Pan
🫗 Strainer
🥘 Saucepan
🫙 Piping Bag
🥛 Measuring Cup
🧊 Large Bowl (for ice bath cooling)

## Preparation Steps
1. Make the crepe skin batter: In a bowl, combine 35g milk powder with 240ml warm water and stir well. Add 20g white sugar, 30g corn starch, and 50g cake flour, stirring until no lumps remain. Beat in 3 eggs, then finally add 10g mild cooking oil and mix well.
   **Key Point**: Strain the batter through a strainer once to achieve a finer texture for the crepe skin; discard any lumps that cannot be strained.

2. Make the custard: Separate 6 egg yolks, add 30g white sugar, and stir until the color lightens and the texture is smooth. Add 30g corn starch and stir until no lumps remain.

3. In a saucepan, combine 52g milk powder with 360ml warm water. Heat over medium heat until just before boiling, then turn off the heat. Slowly and gradually pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolk mixture while whisking quickly to prevent the yolks from cooking and forming lumps.

4. Pour the combined mixture back into the saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly without stopping, until the liquid thickens and distinct lines appear, then turn off the heat.

5. Place the saucepan into a large bowl of ice water and continue stirring until the custard is completely cooled and has a smooth, fine texture. Alternatively, it can be cooled in the refrigerator.

6. Cook the crepe skins: Preheat a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat. Scoop in about one and a half ladles of batter, quickly rotating the pan to spread it evenly. Cook for about 1 minute and 30 seconds, until large bubbles appear on the surface and the crepe is fully cooked. Remove and lay flat to cool.

7. Assemble the pancakes: Take one crepe skin with the smooth side facing down. Fill a piping bag with the cooled custard, pipe a dollop in the center of the crepe skin, and place strawberry dices on top. Gently fold the four sides of the crepe skin inward towards the center to form a square package.
   **Note**: Fold gently to avoid tearing the crepe skin or causing the filling to leak.

## Health Tips
This pancake features a thin crepe skin and fresh strawberries, but the custard made with 6 egg yolks and a large amount of starch is relatively high in calories. It is recommended as an occasional afternoon tea dessert. Suitable for sharing with friends, paired with tea or black coffee to cut through the richness. For those with high blood lipids or during weight loss, the amount of custard can be reduced.
