# Potstickers
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Ground pork, appropriate amount  
🥕 Carrot, about half a small one (finely chopped)  
🌽 Corn kernels, about a small handful  
🥟 Dumpling wrappers, appropriate amount (store-bought)  
🧅 Chopped green onions, appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🌿 Thirteen spices, appropriate amount  
🌾 Flour, 1 tablespoon (for making flour water)  
💧 Water, half a bowl (for making flour water)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for frying)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing filling)  
🥄 Spoon (for adding seasonings, stirring, and filling)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for picking up and assisting in wrapping)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (for frying)  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing flour water)  
🍲 Lid (for steaming while frying)  

## Steps  
1. Place the ground pork in a stainless steel bowl. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen spices, and chopped green onions in order. Then add the finely chopped carrot and corn kernels. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until the meat and vegetables are fully combined with the seasonings.  
2. Take one dumpling wrapper and place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold the wrapper in half and pinch the edges tightly to seal, forming an elongated potsticker with both ends open.  
3. Pour enough cooking oil into a non-stick frying pan. Heat over high heat until the oil is hot. Arrange the wrapped potstickers in the pan in a single layer, leaving some space between each.  
4. Keep frying over high heat. You may check the bottoms of the potstickers; when they turn a light golden brown, mix 1 tablespoon of flour with half a bowl of water to make flour water, and pour it evenly into the pan. The liquid should reach about one-third of the height of the potstickers.  
5. Immediately cover the pan with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and steam-fry for about 7 minutes, until the liquid has completely evaporated. When you hear a light sizzling sound, turn off the heat.  
6. Carefully lift the potstickers out with a spatula and plate them. Serve with dipping sauce of your choice.  

## Health Tips  
Potstickers contain both meat and vegetables. Carrots and corn provide dietary fiber and vitamins, making them suitable for office workers or students who need balanced nutrition. Store-bought dumpling wrappers are not low in sodium, so use soy sauce and oyster sauce sparingly when mixing the filling. People with high blood pressure are advised to use less dipping sauce.
