# Braised Tofu with Minced Pork  
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Silken Tofu (Soft Tofu) 1 box  
🥩 Minced Pork Moderate amount (preferably with some fat)  
🧅 Onion About 1/4 (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic 3-4 cloves (minced)  
🧅 Scallions 3-4 stalks (separate into white parts and chopped greens)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (minced)  
🫒 Cooking Oil Moderate amount  
🍶 Cooking Wine About 2 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster Sauce About 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce About half a tablespoon  
🧂 Chicken Bouillon About 2 teaspoons  
🌶️ White Pepper Powder Moderate amount  
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce About 1/2 teaspoon  
🌾 Cornstarch Slurry Moderate amount (for thickening)  
🫒 Tea Seed Oil (or Scallion Oil) About 1 tablespoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Spatula  
🔪 Chef’s Knife, Cutting Board  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🍲 Clay Pot (or Deep Dish)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the silken tofu from its box and place it on a cutting board. Cut it three times horizontally and three times vertically to create evenly sized dice.  
   **Note**: Using silken tofu ensures a smooth texture. Handle gently to keep the tofu pieces intact.  
2. Carefully transfer the cut tofu into a bowl and set aside. Prepare the minced ginger, minced garlic, chopped onion, white parts of scallions, and chopped scallion greens.  
3. Heat the wok over high heat, add a moderate amount of cooking oil, then add the minced pork. Use a spatula to break it apart and stir-fry over medium heat until the pork is fully cooked and separated into small pieces.  
   **Key Point**: The minced pork must be fully broken apart and fragrant.  
4. Add the minced ginger to the pork and continue stir-frying until aromatic.  
   **Key Point**: This step is crucial for enhancing the flavor.  
5. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and white parts of scallions in order. Continue stir-frying for about 1 minute until the aromatics release their fragrance and the onion becomes translucent.  
   **Note**: Do not use too much minced garlic.  
6. Pour the cooking wine along the edge of the wok and stir-fry a few times.  
7. Add a generous amount of water to the wok, enough to mostly submerge the tofu that will be added later. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Key Point**: Follow the principle of "long-braised tofu" and ensure there is sufficient water.  
8. Once the water is boiling, add the oyster sauce, light soy sauce, chicken bouillon, and white pepper powder. Stir to combine. Taste and add dark soy sauce if needed for a deeper color.  
   **State Check**: The broth should be light brown and taste savory.  
9. Gently slide the tofu cubes from the bowl into the wok along the edge.  
   **Key Point**: Pour along the side to avoid oil splatters and breaking the tofu.  
10. Reduce the heat to medium-low and braise for about 5–8 minutes to allow the tofu to fully absorb the flavors.  
    **Key Point**: Avoid stirring vigorously with the spatula during this time. Gently push the tofu with the back of the spatula to ensure even heating while keeping the pieces intact.  
11. After the tofu is fully flavored, increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. Pour in the cornstarch slurry while gently pushing the tofu with the back of the spatula. Continuously rotate the wok to prevent sticking.  
    **State Check**: The sauce should become thick and cling to the surface of the tofu and minced pork.  
12. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle a generous amount of chopped scallion greens over the dish, then drizzle a spoonful of tea seed oil along the edge of the wok to enhance the shine and aroma.  
13. Carefully transfer the braised tofu from the wok into a preheated clay pot or deep dish.  
    **Key Point**: Move steadily and gently to keep the tofu pieces intact.  

## Health Advice  
Tofu provides high-quality plant protein, which, combined with the animal protein from the minced pork, offers complementary nutrition that is easy to absorb, making it suitable for the whole family. For those with high blood lipids, lean minced meat can be used. The thickening adds a bit of calories, so portion control is advised.
