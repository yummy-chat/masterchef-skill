# Blueberry Scrambled Egg Sandwich
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫐 Frozen blueberries, about one small bowl (thaw in advance)
🍞 2 slices of bread
🥚 1 egg (beaten into egg liquid)

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Toaster
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍽️ Small plate
🥣 Bowl
🍴 Fork

## Instructions
1. Remove the frozen blueberries from the packaging and let them sit in a container to thaw until fully softened.
   **Note**: Frozen blueberries must be thoroughly thawed, otherwise they will be too hard to crush when heated.
2. Prepare 2 slices of bread. Crack the egg into a bowl and whisk thoroughly with a fork until the egg white and yolk are fully combined into a uniform egg liquid.
   **Note**: The egg liquid should be whisked until no visible egg white clumps remain to ensure a smooth and even texture after scrambling.
3. Start the toaster and toast the bread until the surface is light brown and feels crispy to the touch.
   **Note**: Adjust the toasting time based on the toaster's power to avoid burning the bread, which would cause a bitter taste.
4. Place the non-stick frying pan over low heat, pour in the beaten egg liquid, and gently push and stir with a wooden spatula. Once the egg liquid solidifies into tender, fluffy scrambled eggs, remove from the pan.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout and handle gently to prevent the scrambled eggs from breaking apart or becoming overcooked. Doneness indicator: The egg liquid should be just fully set, with a tender, smooth texture and no tough, hard lumps.
5. Pour the thawed blueberries into the frying pan, heat over low heat, and continuously stir and crush the blueberries with a wooden spatula until they release juice and soften, forming a thick sauce. Then remove from the pan.
   **Note**: Stir and crush the blueberries continuously during heating to prevent sticking to the pan. Doneness indicator: The blueberries should be fully softened and mushy, with thick juice and no obvious whole pieces.
6. Take one slice of toasted bread, spread the cooked blueberry sauce evenly on the surface, then top with the scrambled eggs. Cover with the other slice of bread.
   **Note**: Spread each layer evenly. The amounts of blueberry sauce and scrambled eggs can be adjusted to personal taste.
7. Optionally, cut the sandwich in half to show the cross-section before serving. The toast is crispy, the scrambled eggs are tender, and the blueberry sauce is sweet and tangy, creating a rich layering of textures.

## Health Tips
Blueberries are rich in antioxidants, and eggs provide high-quality protein. This sandwich is suitable as a quick energy boost for breakfast. Thawed frozen blueberries should be heated to avoid excessive sourness. Adjust the thickness of the blueberry sauce to prevent a sticky or gummy texture.
