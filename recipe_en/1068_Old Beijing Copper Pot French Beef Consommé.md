# Old Beijing Copper Pot French Beef Consommé  
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Ground beef 750g  
🧅 Onion (purple-skinned) 100g  
🥕 Carrot 100g  
🌿 Celery 100g  
🥚 Eggs 4 (use only egg whites; keep the egg shells intact)  
🧊 Ice cubes 250g  
💧 Ice water, enough  
🌿 Bay leaves, a few pieces  
🌶️ Black pepper, to taste  
🌿 Thyme, a small amount (not too much, to avoid overpowering)  
🍷 Red wine, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🧂 Beef powder, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🫖 Old Beijing copper pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥢 Strainer  
☕ Coffee filter paper  
🍵 Porcelain bowl or soup tureen  

## Instructions  
1. Wash the onion, carrot, and celery, and chop into very fine pieces.  
2. Separate the egg whites from 4 eggs; reserve the egg shells intact. Prepare 250g of ice cubes.  
3. Place the ground beef and all the chopped vegetables in a stainless steel bowl. Add the bay leaves, black pepper, thyme, and red wine. Mix well with your hands.  
4. Add the egg whites and the intact egg shells. Gradually add the ice cubes, crushing them with your hands and mixing thoroughly into the meat mixture to cool it down.  
5. Add the salt and beef powder. Slap the meat mixture repeatedly with your hand until it becomes sticky and elastic.  
6. Fill the copper pot with enough ice water. Pour the rested meat mixture into the ice water and use your hands to completely break it apart in the water, ensuring there are no clumps.  
7. Heat the copper pot over high heat until the surface of the liquid is about to boil, then immediately reduce to low heat and simmer slowly.  
   **Key point**: The meat will gradually coagulate and float to the top, forming a meat raft. Use a spoon to carefully make a small opening in the center of the raft to allow steam to escape, preventing the steam from lifting the raft and making the soup cloudy.  
8. Keep simmering over low heat for about 1.5 hours.  
9. While simmering, heat a separate wok or frying pan without oil. Dry-fry the whole purple-skinned onion until its surface is completely charred black.  
10. Add the charred onion to the soup pot and continue simmering for about 20 minutes to deepen the color.  
11. Turn off the heat. Use a ladle to skim off any foam floating on the surface.  
12. Line a strainer with coffee filter paper. Slowly pour the soup and all its contents from the pot through the strainer to filter, obtaining a clear, oil-free, tea-colored beef consommé.  
13. The filtered consommé can be served directly in bowls, or refrigerated for 3-5 days or frozen for 1 month.

## Health Tips  
This French beef consommé, simmered in a traditional Old Beijing copper pot, combines beef protein with vegetables for a fat-free, low-calorie soup suitable for weight management. Make sure to skim off all foam before filtering to ensure a clear broth. Best enjoyed hot; reheating after refrigeration enhances the flavor.
