# Cold Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken breast, about 250g
🥚 Eggs, 3
🥬 Purple cabbage, about 150g (1/4 head, shredded)
🥕 Carrot, about 100g (half, shredded)
🥒 Cucumber, about 200g (1, ends trimmed, shredded)
🌱 Mung bean sprouts, as needed
🧄 Garlic, about 30g (6 cloves, minced)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (roots set aside separately, stems and leaves cut into sections)
🍜 Noodles, one serving (alkaline noodles, fresh noodles, hand-pulled noodles, etc.)
🌶️ Chili flakes, 2-3 tbsp
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn powder, 1 tsp (about 3g)
🍶 Vinegar, 2 tbsp (about 20g, used in two portions)
🍶 Light soy sauce, 3 tbsp (30-40g)
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tbsp (about 12g)
🍬 Sugar, half tbsp (about 5g)
🌶️ Chili-infused seasoning, 1 tbsp (optional)
🧂 MSG, 1 tsp (1-3g)
🥜 Sesame paste/peanut paste/Erba sauce, as needed (for rich sesame version)
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
💧 Water, as needed (for sauce and thinning sesame paste)
🧅 Scallion segments, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, a little (for poaching chicken breast to remove odor)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Pot/skillet
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🧂 Grater/shredder
🍳 Non-stick flat pan
🌀 Fan
🥣 Bowls, plates
🥄 Spoons, chopsticks

## Steps
1.  Finely mince the garlic, place in a bowl, and pour in a small amount of boiling water to make garlic water. Set aside the cilantro roots separately, and cut the stems and leaves into sections.
2.  Beat the eggs. Heat a non-stick flat pan over high heat, then reduce to low. Lightly spray with oil, pour in the egg liquid, and spread into a thin omelet. Flip and cook briefly, then remove. Once slightly cooled, roll up the omelet and slice into thin strips.
3.  Add plenty of water to a pot, along with scallion segments, ginger slices, and Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add the chicken breast, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until a chopstick can easily pierce through. Remove, rinse under cold water, and shred with your hands.
4.  Shred the purple cabbage and carrot into thin strips, shred the cucumber, and wash the mung bean sprouts. When cutting vegetables, keep your knuckles against the knife to prevent slipping. When using a grater, stop when the ingredient is reduced to a small piece.
5.  In a small bowl, combine the chili flakes, Sichuan peppercorn powder, 1 tbsp of vinegar, cilantro roots, minced garlic, and scallions. Heat oil in a pot until it begins to smoke slightly, then turn off the heat. Pour the oil into the bowl in 2-3 batches, stirring quickly after each pour to release the aroma.
6.  To the oil-infused bowl, add the remaining 1 tbsp of vinegar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, MSG, and chili-infused seasoning (if using). Stir to combine. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water to adjust. This is the basic tangy and spicy sauce.
7.  For a sesame version, place sesame paste/peanut paste/Erba sauce in another small bowl. Add sesame oil or water, and stir continuously in one direction until smooth and pale. Then mix with some of the basic tangy and spicy sauce until well combined.
8.  Bring a separate pot of water to a boil. Cook the noodles until done. Drain well and place in a large dish. Drizzle with a little sesame oil. Use a fan to cool the noodles while lifting and separating with chopsticks until each strand is distinct.
9.  Blanch any vegetables that cannot be eaten raw in the noodle-cooking water for about 1 minute until just cooked. Drain well.
10. Place the cooled noodles in a large bowl. Layer with shredded vegetables, shredded omelet, shredded chicken, mung bean sprouts, and cilantro sections. Pour the prepared sauce over the top and mix well before serving.
    **Note**: The sauce can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for later use. Additions like bird's eye chilies or konjac snacks can be included based on preference.

## Health Tips
This cold noodle dish is rich in protein and vegetables, suitable as a main meal during weight loss periods. However, as sesame paste and chili oil are high in calories, use the sauce in moderation. Shredding the chicken breast after poaching makes it appear lighter, and the combination of purple cabbage and cucumber provides ample dietary fiber and a refreshing texture.
