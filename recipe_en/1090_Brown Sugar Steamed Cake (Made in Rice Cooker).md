# Brown Sugar Steamed Cake (Made in Rice Cooker)
Cuisine Category: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Brown sugar, about 1 tablespoon  
💧 Boiling water, 200 grams  
🍞 Yeast powder, 1 packet (about 3-5 grams)  
🥚 Eggs, 2  
🌾 All-purpose flour, 200 grams  
🌽 Cornstarch, 40 grams  
🧂 Alkaline water (lye water), 1 gram  
🫒 Cooking oil, a small amount  
🫘 Red dates, appropriate amount (for decoration on top)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍥 Silicone spatula  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Kitchen knife  

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the rice cooker’s inner pot in, add about 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, pour in 200 grams of boiling water, and stir with a soup spoon until the brown sugar is completely dissolved.
   **Note**: Using boiling water accelerates the melting of the brown sugar.
2.  Let the sugar water cool to about 35°C, add the yeast powder and 2 eggs, and stir thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Key point**: The temperature must be reduced until it is no longer hot to the touch; otherwise, high heat will kill the yeast and affect the fermentation.
3.  Add 200 grams of all-purpose flour and 40 grams of cornstarch, and stir with chopsticks until a smooth batter without lumps forms.
   **Consistency check**: The batter should be slightly thicker than yogurt, able to drip slowly when lifted with chopsticks.
4.  Cover the rice cooker pot with its lid and let it rest at room temperature to ferment for 40 minutes.
   **Consistency check**: The volume should expand noticeably, with dense, honeycomb-like air holes visible on the surface.
5.  Stir the batter with chopsticks to release the gas, add 1 gram of alkaline water and a small amount of cooking oil, and stir again until evenly incorporated.
   **Note**: Alkaline water neutralizes the sourness produced during fermentation, preventing the final product from becoming too acidic; cooking oil prevents sticking and helps the cake remain soft after cooling.
6.  Level the batter evenly on the bottom of the pot, arrange red dates on the surface for decoration, cover with the lid, and let it rise a second time for 10 minutes.
   **Consistency check**: The volume of the batter should expand again by about double.
7.  Close the lid securely, press the “Rice Cooking” or regular cooking function on the rice cooker to start the heating process.
8.  After the program ends, do not open the lid immediately; let it sit for an additional 3 minutes.
   **Key point**: This resting period prevents the cake from shrinking and hardening suddenly when exposed to cooler air.
9.  Use a silicone spatula to run along the edge of the pot, then invert the cake onto a cutting board to unmold. Slice into pieces and serve.

## Health Advice  
This brown sugar steamed cake is made with yeast fermentation and paired with red dates. It is low in fat and rich in iron, making it suitable as a breakfast or afternoon tea snack. Be mindful of sugar intake; those with diabetes should consume it in moderation. When preparing the batter, avoid making it too thin, or the final product may collapse and lose its shape.
