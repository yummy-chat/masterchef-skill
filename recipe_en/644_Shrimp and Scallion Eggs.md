# Shrimp and Scallion Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Shrimp about a dozen (heads and shells removed, deveined with back slit)  
🥚 Eggs 4 (beaten)  
🌿 Tapioca starch适量 (mixed with water to make starch water)  
🧅 Scallions适量 (finely chopped)  
🧂 Salt适量 (used twice: a pinch in the egg mixture, and a pinch when stir-frying shrimp)  
🧂 Black pepper适量 (added when stir-frying shrimp for deodorizing and enhancing flavor)  
🧈 Butter a small piece (used as base oil for scrambling eggs)  
🧂 White pepper适量 (sprinkled on top after plating, key to flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥣 Bowl (for mixing egg mixture)  
🥄 Strainer (for straining egg mixture)  
🥢 Chopsticks or whisk  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Silicone spatula or spatula  
🍽️ Plate (for temporarily holding shrimp)  
🍽️ Serving plate (for serving the finished dish)  

## Steps
1. **Prepare the shrimp**: Remove heads and shells, slit the backs with a knife, and devein.  
   **Key point**: Slitting the back allows the shrimp to curl naturally when heated, creating an attractive shape and making deveining easier.  
2. **Mix the egg mixture**: In a bowl, combine tapioca starch with some water to make starch water, beat in 4 eggs, whisk thoroughly until well combined, and add a pinch of salt for seasoning.  
   **Key point**: Starch water is the key to making scrambled eggs tender and smooth.  
3. **Strain the egg mixture**: Pour the beaten egg mixture through a strainer into another container to remove egg strands and foam.  
   **Key point**: Straining ensures a fine, smooth final product with no impurities.  
4. **Chop the scallions**: Wash the scallions and finely chop them into small pieces, starting from the white part for a more refined look.  
5. **Stir-fry the shrimp**: Heat a small amount of oil in a pan over high heat, add the prepared shrimp and stir-fry until they change color. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper, stir well, then immediately remove and set aside on a plate.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry over high heat quickly, cooking the shrimp just until done to avoid overcooking and toughening.  
6. **Scramble the eggs**: Clean the pan or use a new one, add a small piece of butter, and heat over medium heat until completely melted and bubbling with small bubbles. Turn the heat to high, pour in the strained egg mixture all at once. Wait briefly until the bottom edges of the egg start to set, then use a silicone spatula to gently push the set parts from the edges toward the center, allowing uncooked egg to flow to the bottom.  
   **Key point**: Bubble formation in the butter signals the right moment to add the eggs; avoid stirring too early, wait until parts of the egg have set before pushing to create large, soft curds.  
7. **Combine with shrimp**: When the egg mixture is semi-set, add the stir-fried shrimp back to the pan and gently fold to combine. Continue lightly folding to let any uncooked egg in the center flow to the sides and heat through.  
8. **Plate**: When the eggs are about 90% cooked, turn off the heat, sprinkle with scallions, and let the residual heat wilt them. Gently slide the entire shrimp and scallion egg onto a serving plate, and sprinkle the surface with white pepper.  
   **Key point**: Plating must be done quickly to maintain the integrity and heat of the eggs; white pepper is the key flavor element of this dish.  

## Health Tips
Shrimp are high in protein and low in fat. Paired with eggs and butter, they provide calcium and enhance flavor. For those on a low-salt diet, reduce the amount of salt. The secret to tender, silky scrambled eggs lies in the starch water and heat control, making this dish suitable for the elderly and children.
