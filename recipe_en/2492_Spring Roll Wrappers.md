# Spring Roll Wrappers
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥖 Bread flour 500g (all-purpose flour works, but bread flour is better)
🧂 Salt 4g
💧 Water 450g (water-to-flour ratio 90%)
🫒 Cooking oil Very small amount (only for greasing the pan to prevent sticking, do not use too much)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🫗 Stainless steel bowl (for mixing dough)
🍴 Spatula (for initial dough mixing)
🍳 Cast iron pan or stainless steel pan (for cooking, **do not use non-stick pan**)
📦 Plastic wrap or lid (for covering dough to retain moisture during resting)
🌡️ Infrared thermometer (optional, for measuring pan temperature)

## Instructions
1. Place bread flour and salt in a bowl, add water, and stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains. The dough will be rough and lack gluten at this stage.
2. Cover with a lid and let rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.
3. Dip your hand in water to prevent sticking, lift the dough from the bottom, fold it inward, and repeat about 20 times until the dough becomes slightly stretchy but still not very strong.
4. Cover again and rest for 15 minutes. Repeat the folding process 20 more times to strengthen the gluten network.
5. Shape the dough, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight. This is a crucial step for achieving optimal elasticity.
6. The next day, remove the dough. It should be smooth and glossy, easily stretchable without breaking.
7. Use a paper towel to dab a very small amount of oil and evenly wipe a thin layer on the pan surface. **Note: Do not use a non-stick pan throughout the entire process**.
8. If using a cast iron pan, heat over high heat until smoking, then turn off the heat and let the pan cool to 120–130°C. Then turn to the lowest heat to keep it warm. For a stainless steel pan, you can judge the temperature by hand feel.
9. Take a large piece of dough and roughly shape it into a ball in your hand. Hold the pan with one hand, and with the other hand, press the dough tightly against the pan bottom. Quickly spread it in a circular motion, then lift the dough immediately, taking away any excess batter. If holes appear, use the dough in your hand to patch them up immediately.
10. Keep the heat on the lowest setting throughout. Cook for about 20 seconds. Once the wrapper is completely white and the edges slightly curl up, gently peel it off with your hand.
11. Stack the cooked wrappers one by one, cover with a lid, and let the residual heat soften them, making them soft and pliable.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in carbohydrates and low in fat, making it suitable for consumption before or after exercise when quick energy is needed. However, as a staple food, it should be paired with protein and vegetables.
