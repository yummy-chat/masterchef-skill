# Molten Lava Custard Mooncake
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Salted egg yolks – as needed (steamed, mashed, and divided: one part for the molten filling, the remainder for the custard filling)
🥛 Milk powder – as needed
🍬 Fine granulated sugar – as needed
🥛 Fresh cream – as needed
🧀 Cheese slices – 2 slices (1 for the molten filling, 1 for the custard filling)
🥚 Eggs – as needed
🌾 Cake flour – as needed
🧈 Cream – as needed
🧈 Butter – as needed (for the custard filling and the dough; dough butter should be softened at room temperature)
🍯 Honey – as needed
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk – as needed
🌽 Cornstarch – as needed
🌾 All-purpose flour – as needed (for dusting)
🥚 Egg yolk – 1 (for glaze)
💧 Water – 1 tablespoon (to mix with the egg yolk for glaze)

## Utensils & Equipment
♨️ Steamer/steaming basket
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Whisk
🪣 Strainer/fine sieve
🍴 Spatula
📦 Microwave
🍳 Non-stick pan
🫙 Plastic wrap
⚖️ Digital scale
🍪 Mooncake mold
🔥 Oven
💨 Spray bottle
🖌️ Brush

## Steps
1. **Prepare salted egg yolks**: Wash and steam the salted egg yolks until cooked. Mash them with a spoon until very smooth. Divide into two portions and set aside.
2. **Make the molten filling**: In a bowl, combine milk powder, fine granulated sugar, and fresh cream. Add one cheese slice and one portion of the mashed salted egg yolks. Microwave for about 45 seconds. Remove and stir until smooth and fully incorporated.  
   **Note**: Do not heat too long to avoid overflowing or burning.
3. **Make the custard filling**:  
   – Beat the eggs, then sift in the cake flour and mix until smooth.  
   – Gently heat cream and butter over low heat until just simmering. Slowly pour into the egg mixture while stirring quickly.  
   – Add the remaining cheese slice, fine granulated sugar, and milk powder. Stir with residual heat until the cheese melts.  
   – Fold in the remaining mashed salted egg yolks. Transfer the mixture to a non-stick pan and stir-fry quickly over low heat until the custard forms a smooth, cohesive dough.  
   – Transfer to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap directly on the surface, and refrigerate until completely chilled.  
   **Key points**: Stir constantly and scrape the bottom during the entire stir-frying to prevent burning; cover the surface with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming.
4. **Make the dough**: Beat the softened butter until smooth. Add honey and sweetened condensed milk, and mix well. Sift in cornstarch and cake flour, and combine into a soft, moist dough. Wrap with plastic wrap and let rest.
5. **Divide the fillings and freeze**: Put the molten filling into a small freezer bag, shape into a square, and freeze until hard. Cut into pieces of about 8 g each, and return immediately to the freezer. Divide the chilled custard filling into portions of about 28 g each, roll into balls, and refrigerate.
6. **Wrap the molten core**: Flatten a custard ball, place one frozen molten filling piece in the center, and use the “tiger mouth” technique to seal and roll it into a ball. Freeze all wrapped balls until hard.
7. **Wrap with dough**: Divide the rested dough into portions of about 15 g each. Flatten into a thin round, wrap around a frozen filling ball, and gently push the dough up with the “tiger mouth” to seal completely.
8. **Press into molds**: Roll the mooncake ball in a thin layer of flour. Shape it into a slightly elongated oval, place vertically into the mooncake mold, and press evenly. Remove from the mold and freeze again until hard.  
   **Note**: Dust the mold with flour to prevent sticking; press evenly to avoid lopsided shapes.
9. **Baking**: Preheat the oven to 220–230°C. Lightly spray the frozen mooncakes with water, then bake for 3–4 minutes. Remove and brush a thin layer of egg yolk glaze. Continue baking for about 8 minutes until the surface is golden brown.  
   **Key points**: Baking straight from frozen helps keep the pattern from collapsing; spraying water prevents the surface from drying; apply the glaze very thinly.
10. **Rest and return to oil**: The crust may be dry and uneven in color right out of the oven – this is normal. Seal the mooncakes and let them rest for one day to allow the skin to soften and achieve the best texture. Reheating before serving enhances the molten center.

## Health Advice
Molten lava custard mooncakes are high in calories. It is recommended to enjoy one piece at a time, paired with plain tea to cut the richness. Those with high blood sugar or on a weight-loss plan should taste in moderation. The mooncakes are at their best after the “return to oil” rest.
