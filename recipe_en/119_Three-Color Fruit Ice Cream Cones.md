# Three-Color Fruit Ice Cream Cones
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
**Basic Cones**  
🍦 Ice cream cones, as needed  
🍫 Chocolate sauce, as needed (for inner coating)  

**Mango Flavor**  
🥭 Mango, about 200g (peeled, diced, and frozen solid)  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed (about 1:1 ratio with frozen fruit by volume)  
🍇 Raisins, a small amount (optional, mixed in for added flavor)  

**Strawberry Flavor**  
🍓 Strawberries, about 100g (hulled, whole or halved and frozen)  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed  

**Green Grape Flavor**  
🍇 Green grapes/seedless grapes, about 150g (washed and frozen)  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed  

**(Optional) Blueberry Flavor**  
🫐 Blueberries, about 50g (washed and frozen)  
🥛 Heavy cream, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Fruit peeler  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🔀 Blender (food processor)  
👝 Piping bag  
🧊 Refrigerator (freezer)  
🥣 Bowl  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Instructions  
**Preparation Phase**  
1. Peel and dice the mango. Wash the green grapes, strawberries, and blueberries. Divide them into separate portions and place in the freezer until completely frozen solid.  
   **Key Point**: The fruit must be fully frozen solid; this is the core to making ice cream without ice crystals.  

2. **Taking mango flavor as an example to make the ice cream base**: Place the frozen mango pieces and an appropriate amount of heavy cream into a blender. Blend until fully combined into a smooth and thick paste.  
   **Key Point**: Pause blending midway to ensure an even texture. Follow the same process for other flavors.  

**Assembly and Setting**  
3. Transfer each flavor of ice cream into separate piping bags.  
4. Take an ice cream cone and evenly spread a layer of chocolate sauce on the inner wall.  
5. Pipe the different colored ice creams into the cone in sequence, creating a layered effect.  
6. Once finished, place in the freezer to set for at least 1 hour before serving.  

## Health Advice  
This ice cream uses frozen fruit to replace large amounts of sugar and cream, resulting in lower calories compared to store-bought products, making it suitable for satisfying cravings in spring and summer. Mango, strawberry, and green grapes are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. For those with lactose intolerance, it is recommended to substitute heavy cream with coconut milk.
