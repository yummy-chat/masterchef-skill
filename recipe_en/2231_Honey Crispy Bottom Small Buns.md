# Honey Crispy Bottom Small Buns
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥖 Bread flour 430g
🍬 Fine sugar 75g (for dough) + appropriate amount (mixed 1:1 with white sesame seeds for crispy bottom)
🥛 Milk powder 8g
🧂 Salt 4g
🥛 Milk 251g (reserve about 10g for dissolving yeast)
🥚 Whole egg liquid 27g
🧈 Unsalted butter 42g (softened and added to dough) + appropriate amount (brushed on surface after baking)
🍞 Fresh yeast 13g (or high-sugar tolerant dry yeast 4.5g)
🌱 White sesame seeds appropriate amount (for crispy bottom)
🌱 Black sesame seeds a pinch (for decorating crispy bottom)
🌽 Corn oil appropriate amount (for brushing the baking pan)
🍯 Honey 8g (mixed with 5~8g water to make honey water)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Scraper
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🍱 Plastic wrap/plastic bag
📏 Rolling pin
🔪 Knife
🍽️ 28×28cm square baking pan
🔥 Oven
🌡️ Food thermometer
🥣 Small bowl
🥢 Whisk or chopsticks
🖌️ Basting brush

## Steps
1.  Place bread flour, fine sugar, milk powder, and salt into the mixing bowl. Use a scraper to mix evenly.
2.  Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients. Pour in most of the milk and the whole egg liquid. Use the scraper to mix until it forms a shaggy mass with no dry flour. Knead into a rough dough. Lightly brush the surface with a little softened butter, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
3.  After resting, flatten the dough. Evenly sprinkle the crumbled fresh yeast over it. Pour in the remaining milk and add the remaining softened butter. Use the scraper to repeatedly cut and mix until the butter is fully incorporated. Then, knead and throw the dough using the heel of your hand until the surface is smooth and firm.
    **Note**: Measure the dough temperature. If it exceeds 24°C, flatten the dough, seal it, and place it in the freezer for 5~10 minutes to cool down to about 22°C.
4.  Remove the dough from the bowl. Divide it into 13 portions. Roll each portion into a ball. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest at room temperature for 25 minutes.
5.  First roll: Take one portion of the rested dough. Flatten it and gently roll it out into a long oval. Flip it over and roll it up from the top down. After all portions are rolled, cover with plastic wrap and let rest again at room temperature for 25 minutes.
6.  Prepare the honey water. Mix the crispy bottom ingredients: Combine white sesame seeds and fine sugar in a 1:1 ratio. Sprinkle in a pinch of black sesame seeds. Pour an appropriate amount of corn oil into the baking pan and spread it evenly with a basting brush.
7.  Final shaping: Take one rested dough roll. Flatten it and roll it out into a narrow, long strip. Flip it over and thin out the bottom edge. Loosely roll it up from the top down, pinching the seam to seal. Cut the roll in half with a knife.
8.  Dip the cut side of each small bun roll first into the honey water, then generously coat it in the sesame seed sugar mixture. Place the coated side (the bottom) facing down into the prepared baking pan. Arrange them evenly.
9.  Let the buns ferment in a warm, humid place until doubled in size.
10. Preheat the oven to 155°C top heat / 230°C bottom heat. Place the baking pan in the oven and bake for about 25 minutes, until the surface is evenly golden brown.
11. Immediately after removing from the oven, brush the bun surfaces with melted butter. Then, unmold and invert the entire pan onto a cooling rack so the crispy bottoms face upward to cool.

## Health Advice
These honey crispy bottom small buns are sweet and crispy, but they contain a significant amount of sugar and butter. It is recommended to enjoy just 1-2 pieces at a time. Pairing them with unsweetened tea or black coffee can help cut the richness for a healthier option. They are suitable as a breakfast or afternoon tea snack; be mindful of your total daily sugar intake.
