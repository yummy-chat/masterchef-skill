# Stir-Fried Prawn Balls with Celery and Lily Bulb  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 150g fresh prawns (peeled, deveined, and butterflied into prawn balls)  
🥬 3 stalks celery (sliced diagonally into diamond shapes)  
🫑 1 piece red bell pepper (cut into diamond shapes, for color)  
🥕 1 section carrot (carved into butterfly or decorative shapes, for color and garnish)  
🧅 1 lily bulb (separated into scales)  
🥜 Approximately 1 small bowl cashew nuts (pre-fried until crispy)  
🥚 1 egg (egg white used for marinating)  
🫚 Approximately 1 small piece ginger (for making ginger juice wine; some sliced for stir-frying)  
🧄 Approximately 3 cloves garlic (sliced for stir-frying)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (used three times: for marinating prawns, blanching, and sauce)  
🧂 MSG, to taste (for marinating prawns and sauce)  
🌿 Ground white pepper, to taste (for marinating prawns and sauce, to remove fishiness and enhance aroma)  
🍶 Ginger juice wine, to taste (ginger juice + water + rose dew wine, for marinating prawns to remove fishiness and enhance aroma)  
⚪️ 15g edible alkali (for making alkaline water to soak prawns, making them tender and crunchy)  
💧 Approximately 400g water (for making alkaline water; additional water needed for blanching)  
🌽 Approximately 15g cornstarch (for marinating and making sauce)  
🍬 A pinch of sugar (for sauce, to enhance freshness)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for stir-frying prawns, frying cashews, and cooking)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Round thick wooden cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🌀 Blender/food processor (for making ginger juice)  
🧵 Fine-mesh strainer (for filtering ginger juice wine)  
🥄 Spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Peel and devein the fresh prawns, then butterfly them by slicing open along the back to form prawn balls.  
2. Mix 15g edible alkali with 400g water to create alkaline water. Soak the prawns in it for about 20 minutes, until their color changes from green to pink.  
3. Rinse the soaked prawns under running water for 1 to 2 hours, until they naturally curl and expand in volume. Ensure thorough rinsing to remove any alkaline taste and allow the prawn meat to fully absorb water—this is key to a crunchy texture. For home cooking, an alternative is to vigorously wash the prawns with a generous amount of salt, then rinse clean.  
4. Slice the celery diagonally into diamond shapes; cut the red bell pepper into diamond shapes; trim and carve the carrot into butterfly or other decorative shapes. Cantonese stir-fries emphasize attractive shapes and knife skills for ingredients.  
5. To the rinsed and drained prawns, add salt, MSG, ground white pepper, ginger juice wine, and egg white in that order. Mix well to season. Finally, add cornstarch and continue mixing until the prawns are evenly coated with a thin layer of starch slurry. Ginger juice wine, made from ginger juice, water, and rose dew wine, is a signature Cantonese seasoning for removing fishiness and enhancing aroma. The starch slurry should be thin and even.  
6. Briefly blanch the cashew nuts in boiling water, then drain. Heat oil to about 170°C (340°F) and deep-fry the cashews slowly until light golden, then remove and drain. Blanching before frying makes the cashews cleaner and less prone to over-browning; residual heat prevents burning.  
7. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a pinch of salt and a dash of oil to preserve color. Quickly blanch the sliced celery and lily bulb scales, then immediately remove and refresh under cold water or drain. Keep the blanching time short to maintain crispness and vibrant color.  
8. Heat a generous amount of oil in the wok to about 180°C (355°F). Add the marinated prawns and gently push them apart with a spatula to stir-fry. Once the prawns change color and curl, immediately remove and drain oil. If the oil is too hot, the starch coating on the prawns may burn and darken, affecting the dish’s appearance.  
9. In a small bowl, combine seasonings in a ratio of approximately 3:2:1 salt, MSG, and sugar. Add a pinch of ground white pepper and some water, then stir in water starch to form a sauce. This is the “bowl sauce” commonly used in Cantonese clear stir-fries, allowing for quick thickening and even coating of ingredients.  
10. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. First, stir-fry the sliced ginger, garlic, and red bell pepper pieces until fragrant. Add the blanched celery and lily bulb, and stir-fry over medium heat to evaporate excess surface moisture, ensuring a clean, non-soggy final dish. Add the stir-fried prawns and carved carrot flowers, and quickly toss to combine. Pour the prepared bowl sauce along the edge of the wok while stirring rapidly, so the sauce evenly coats all ingredients. The sauce should be thick enough to “cling” to the ingredients. Finally, add the fried cashew nuts, toss a few times, and immediately remove from heat to keep the cashews crispy.  
11. Plate the dish. The finished dish should have a fresh, bright appearance: translucent pink prawns, vibrant green celery, white lily bulb scales, accented by carrot flowers and red bell pepper. The sauce should be glossy and evenly coat the ingredients. The prawns are crunchy and springy, the vegetables crisp, and the cashews fragrant and crispy. The flavor is savory and light, with a hint of ginger wine aroma.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. The prawns and lily bulb provide quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for weight loss diets. Note that after soaking prawns in alkaline water, they must be thoroughly rinsed; otherwise, the sodium content may be high.
