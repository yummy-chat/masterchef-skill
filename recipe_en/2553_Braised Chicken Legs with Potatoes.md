# Braised Chicken Legs with Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken legs, as needed (cut into pieces, blanch in cold water to remove gamey smell)  
🥔 Potato, 1 piece (peeled and cut into irregular chunks)  
🧅 Scallion, as needed (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (whole cloves)  
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed (for caramelizing)  
🧂 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1.5 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, as needed  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Measuring spoon (small gold spoon)  
🥣 Bowl (for serving the finished dish)  

## Steps  
1. Place the chicken leg pieces in a pot with cold water and blanch. Skim off any foam, then remove and drain.  
   **Note**: Blanching helps remove the gamey smell and some of the blood.  
2. Peel the potato and cut into irregular chunks. Cut the scallion into sections, slice the ginger, peel the garlic cloves, and prepare the dried chili peppers.  
   **Key point**: Soak the potato chunks in water after cutting to prevent them from turning black due to oxidation.  
3. Pour oil into the wok and heat until warm. Add the rock sugar and stir-fry over **low heat** until the sugar melts, turns a jujube red color, and forms small bubbles.  
   **Note**: Be sure to stir-fry slowly over low heat to avoid burning the sugar, which would make it bitter.  
4. Immediately add the blanched chicken leg pieces and stir-fry quickly to coat each piece evenly with the caramelized sugar color.  
5. Add the scallion sections, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, garlic, as well as the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Continue stir-frying until fragrant.  
6. Add **hot water**, ensuring the water fully covers the ingredients.  
   **Key point**: Using hot water for braising helps the meat become tender more easily and makes the broth more flavorful.  
7. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce heat to **medium-low**, and braise for 5 minutes.  
8. Uncover the lid, add the potato chunks and a suitable amount of salt, and gently stir to combine.  
   **Key point**: Add the potatoes later to prevent them from becoming too soft and mushy from overcooking.  
9. Cover the lid again and continue braising over medium-low heat for 15 minutes, until the potatoes are tender.  
10. Turn the heat to **high** to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly with the spatula, until the sauce thickens and clings to the ingredients. Then remove from heat.  
    **Note**: Stir continuously while reducing the sauce to prevent it from burning and sticking to the wok.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost. However, be mindful of the amounts of potatoes and rock sugar used to avoid blood sugar fluctuations. It is recommended to serve with a green leafy vegetable to balance the dietary fiber.
