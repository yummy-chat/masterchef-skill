# Smooth and Fragrant Fish Head with Ginger, Scallion, and Fermented Tofu
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 large fish head (cut into about 6 large pieces, preferably reservoir fish head/male fish head, choose by checking elasticity, eyes, and gills)  
🫚 About 15-20 slices of ginger (thick slices, for lining the bottom)  
🧄 About 10-15 cloves of garlic (whole or cut into large pieces, for lining the bottom)  
🧅 About 10-15 shallots (peeled and cut into pieces or halved, for lining the bottom)  
🌿 1 small handful of green Sichuan pepper (for adding fragrance to the bottom)  
🌶️ 1-2 Thai chili peppers (sliced into rings, for final garnish)  
🍊 1 small piece of dried tangerine peel (cut into strips, sprinkled in before serving)  

🧂 About 3-4 pieces of fermented tofu (mashed into a paste, used to make the sauce, as the main flavor)  
🐔 About 2 tablespoons of chicken fat (for making the sauce and coating the clay pot, can be substituted with cooking oil)  
🧂 About 1/2 teaspoon of salt (added when marinating the fish pieces, adjust based on the saltiness of the fermented tofu and the size of the fish head)  
🍬 About 1 teaspoon of sugar (added when marinating the fish pieces, **key: neutralizes the saltiness of the fermented tofu and enhances umami**)  
🍾 About 1 tablespoon of soy sauce (light soy sauce, added when marinating the fish pieces, to enhance the sauce flavor)  
🌽 About 1 tablespoon of cornstarch (added when marinating the fish pieces, to lock in moisture and make the fish tender)  
🍶 About 2-3 tablespoons of Guangdong rice wine (add 1 tablespoon when marinating, and drizzle 1-2 tablespoons before serving, to remove fishiness and enhance fragrance)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board (for processing and cutting the fish head)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for holding and marinating the fish pieces)  
🍲 Clay pot (main cooking vessel, for braising the fish head)  
🔥 Gas stove (provides heat source)  

## Cooking Steps
1. Clean the large fish head, remove any fishy parts and remaining gills, pat dry with kitchen paper, and cut into about 6 large pieces.
2. In a small bowl, mash the fermented tofu into a paste, add about 2 tablespoons of chicken fat, and stir until smooth and thick. Set aside.
3. Place the fish pieces in a stainless steel bowl, add salt, sugar, soy sauce, cornstarch, and the prepared fermented tofu paste in order, and mix well to marinate.
**Note**: Marinate for 10-15 minutes to ensure sufficient flavor absorption.

4. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of Guangdong rice wine over the marinated fish pieces, then add about 1 tablespoon of cooking oil and mix well.
**Key**: The oil seal helps keep the fish tender and allows the flavors to better penetrate.

5. Heat the clay pot over medium-high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil, first add the ginger slices and garlic cloves and stir-fry until fragrant, then add the shallots and stir-fry until the edges are slightly charred, and finally sprinkle in the green Sichuan pepper.
**Key**: The aromatics must be fully stir-fried to provide a complex base flavor for the dish.

6. Lay the marinated fish pieces flat on top of the aromatics, avoiding overlap as much as possible, and evenly pour the remaining marinade from the bowl over the fish pieces.
7. Cover the clay pot with its lid, reduce the heat to medium, and braise for 5-6 minutes.
**Indication**: You can see steam continuously rising through the gap in the lid and hear a sizzling sound.

8. Open the lid, drizzle 1 tablespoon of Guangdong rice wine along the edge of the clay pot.
**Key**: It is normal for flames to flare up briefly, which greatly enhances the aroma.

9. Sprinkle the Thai chili pepper rings over the top, replace the lid, and let it braise with residual heat for about half a minute to 1 minute.
10. Finally, open the lid, sprinkle with dried tangerine peel strips and chopped green onions, and serve.
**Note**: The final addition of rice wine is the finishing touch, instantly releasing a rich aroma. The fish is smooth and tender, with no fishy taste.

## Health Advice
The combination of fermented tofu and fish head provides calcium and enhances flavor, making it suitable for spring nourishment. However, fermented tofu is high in sodium, so people with high blood pressure should control their intake. Fish is rich in high-quality protein, suitable for all ages, and should be paired with vegetables for a balanced diet.
