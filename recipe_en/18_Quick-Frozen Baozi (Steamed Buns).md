# Quick-Frozen Baozi (Steamed Buns)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonality: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g
🍞 Yeast 10g
🧂 Aluminum-free baking powder 8g
🍬 White sugar 4g
🐖 Lard 20g
🫘 Soybean oil 40g
💧 Warm water 480g (approx. 40℃)
🥩 Filling for baozi, as needed (e.g., pork and scallion filling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍚 Ceramic bowl
🍳 Steamer basket and steamer pot
🔪 Cutting board
📦 Plastic wrap
🥘 Tray
🥶 Refrigerator

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix flour, yeast, baking powder, and white sugar evenly. Add lard and soybean oil, and rub until no dry flour remains.
   **Key Point**: The high oil content in the dough ensures the buns stay fluffy and not dry after freezing.
2. Gradually add warm water and knead into a firm dough to prevent collapsing during steaming.
3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place until it expands in volume and develops a honeycomb-like interior.
4. Take out the dough and knead it thoroughly to release air, until the surface is smooth and fine. A smooth dough is essential for producing white, glossy bun wrappers.
5. Roll the dough into a long log, divide into evenly sized pieces, press into wrappers, fill with the filling, and pleat the edges to seal, forming raw baozi buns.
6. Arrange the raw buns on a tray, cover with plastic wrap, and let them proof at room temperature for about 20 minutes, until they visibly increase in size.
   **Note**: A second proofing is essential to achieve optimal steaming condition; otherwise, the finished buns will not be plump enough.
7. Place the proofed buns, still on the tray, directly into the freezer and freeze until solid.
8. No need to thaw when steaming: Bring water to a boil in the steamer pot, place the frozen buns directly into the steamer basket lined with a damp cloth. Once the water is boiling, steam over high heat for 15 minutes.
   **Key Point**: Maintain high heat throughout; low heat will cause the buns to collapse and lose their appealing appearance.
9. Turn off the heat and remove the buns. The finished buns have a smooth, white, glossy skin and are soft and springy inside, with a texture comparable to freshly made buns.

## Health Advice
Quick-frozen baozi contain a relatively high amount of oil. It is recommended to eat no more than 3 at a time, paired with plain congee or vegetable soup for better digestion. Suitable for busy office workers to make in batches, but ensure steaming over high heat throughout to achieve fluffy and plump buns.
