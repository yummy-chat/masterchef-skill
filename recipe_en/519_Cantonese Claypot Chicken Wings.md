# Cantonese Claypot Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken wings 8-10 pieces (thawed, cleaned, scored on both sides)  
🥩 Pork belly To taste (diced)  
🫚 Ginger About one small piece (cut into chunks)  
🧄 Garlic 8-10 cloves (whole)  
🧅 Small shallots 5-6 (halved)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili 2-3 (sliced into rings)  
🌿 Cilantro A small bunch  
🧂 Salt To taste  
🍬 White sugar To taste  
🍶 Rice wine To taste (for marinating and simmering)  
🧂 Soy sauce (light soy sauce) To taste  
🍾 Worcestershire sauce To taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍲 Claypot  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Knife  
🥄 Spatula  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Score both sides of each chicken wing twice, squeeze out excess moisture, and place in a bowl.
**Key point**: Scoring helps remove the freezer taste from frozen chicken wings, allowing them to absorb flavor faster and cook through more easily.
2.  Add salt, white sugar, rice wine, soy sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Mix thoroughly until well combined, then refrigerate to marinate for 15 minutes.
**Note**: Worcestershire sauce is essential for enhancing the flavor layers and should not be omitted.
3.  Heat the claypot over high heat, then add oil. Add the diced pork belly and stir-fry over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant. The fried pork cracklings can be removed if desired; retain the rendered lard for added aroma.
4.  Turn the heat to high, add the ginger chunks, garlic cloves, and shallots. Stir-fry until they are golden on the surface and intensely fragrant.
5.  Lay the marinated chicken wings flat on top of the aromatics, trying to avoid pouring in excess marinade. Use the high heat of the claypot to sear the surface of the chicken wings until golden and slightly charred.
**Key point**: Use the high heat of the claypot to sear the outside and lock in the meat juices.
6.  Cover the claypot with its lid, reduce the heat to medium, and simmer for about 10 minutes, until the chicken wings are fully cooked.
7.  During the simmering process, pour a circle of rice wine along the edge of the lid. The steam from the alcohol will further remove any odor and enhance the fragrance.
8.  Remove the lid, scatter the bird's eye chili rings and cilantro over the chicken wings. Re-cover and simmer for about 30 seconds, using the residual heat to release the aroma of the fresh garnishes.
9.  Mix everything well and serve immediately while hot. The sauce is rich, the chicken is tender and juicy, with a delicate smoky char.

## Health Advice  
This Cantonese claypot chicken wing dish is rich in protein with moderate fat content, making it suitable for muscle building or daily energy replenishment. However, the pork belly and lard are relatively high in calories, so consumption should be moderated. When marinating, reduce the amount of salt to avoid excessive sodium intake.
