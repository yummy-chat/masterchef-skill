# Braised Ribs in Black Bean Sauce

Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable all year round

---

🥩 **Ingredients**  
- Pork ribs  
- Yangjiang fermented black beans  
- Minced garlic  
- Minced ginger  
- Chopped green onion (optional)  
- White sesame seeds (optional)  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Light soy sauce  
- Dark soy sauce  
- Oyster sauce  
- White sugar  
- Cornstarch  
- Cooking oil  
- Water  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- Steamer  
- Kitchen paper  

---

📖 **Instructions**  
1. Soak the ribs in cold water for 10 minutes to let the blood seep out. Change the water and repeat twice. Then pat dry with kitchen paper.  
   (Soak the ribs in cold water for 10 minutes to let the blood seep out. Change the water twice, then use kitchen paper to pat dry the surface.)  
2. Soak the fermented black beans in clean water for 5 minutes. Remove and coarsely chop.  
   (Soak the fermented black beans in clean water for 5 minutes, remove and chop slightly.)  
3. Mix all ingredients (add only 10 ml of cooking oil first) evenly. Marinate for 8–30 minutes.  
   (Combine all ingredients (only add 10 ml of cooking oil for now) and mix well. Marinate for 8–30 minutes.)  
4. Once the steamer is producing steam, place the marinated ribs on the steaming rack. Steam over high heat for 18 minutes.  
   (After the steamer starts producing steam, put the marinated ribs on the steaming rack and steam over high heat for 18 minutes.)  
5. Turn off the heat and let the ribs rest inside the steamer for 2 minutes before removing.  
   (Turn off the heat and let the ribs rest in the steamer for 2 minutes before taking them out.)  
6. Pour the remaining 10 ml of cooking oil into a wok. Heat to 180–200°C (when the oil surface begins to lightly smoke).  
   (Pour the remaining 10 ml of oil into the wok and heat to 180–200°C (when the oil surface begins to lightly smoke).)  
7. Evenly sprinkle the chopped green onion and white sesame seeds over the surface of the ribs. Pour the hot oil over the toppings to release their aroma.  
   (Sprinkle the chopped green onion and white sesame seeds evenly over the ribs. Pour the hot oil over them to release the aroma.)  

⚠️ **Key Points**  
- Chopping the fermented black beans after soaking helps release their flavor more easily.  
- Adjust the steaming time based on the size of the ribs. They are done when a chopstick can easily pierce the cartilage.  
- The traditional method does not include the toppings and hot oil step.  
- The remaining sauce can be used to mix with rice or noodles.  

## Health Advice  
Braised ribs in black bean sauce are rich in protein and calcium, suitable for muscle building or recovery. However, fermented black beans have a high sodium content. People with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of sauce used. Removing the blood and controlling the oil before steaming can lower the fat and purine content. Gout patients should consume in moderation.
