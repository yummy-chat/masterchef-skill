# Red Bean Bread
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 180g (must use bread-specific high-gluten flour)
🍚 Granulated sugar 29g
🍞 High-sugar-tolerant dry yeast 1.8g (must use high-sugar-tolerant type)
🥛 Milk 101g
🥚 Whole egg liquid 36g (for dough)
🧈 Unsalted butter 11g (softened in advance)
🫘 Sweetened red beans 260g
🍶 Heavy cream 50g (mix with sweetened red beans for filling)
🖤 Black sesame seeds about 1 tbsp (for surface decoration)
🥚 Egg wash a small amount (for brushing before baking)
🧂 Salt 2.7g (add after spreading butter)

## Tools & Equipment
🥛 Glass measuring cup (for mixing liquid ingredients)
🥄 Hand whisk (for stirring liquid ingredients)
🔪 Scraper (for mixing dry and wet ingredients, cutting and kneading dough, dividing dough)
📦 Plastic wrap (for covering dough and filling)
🍳 Flat-bottom pan (for heating and making red bean filling)
⚖️ Kitchen scale (for weighing all ingredients)
♨️ Oven/proofer (for fermentation and baking)
🍞 Baking tray
🗼 Cooling rack (for cooling bread)
🖌️ Brush (for brushing egg wash on surface)

## Steps
### Prepare Red Bean Filling
1. Pour sweetened red beans and heavy cream into a flat-bottom pan. Heat over low heat, stirring and mashing until the mixture combines into a ball.  
   **Note**: The purpose of heating is to mash the red beans and combine with the cream, not to dry them out.
2. Turn off the heat and transfer to a container. Cover with plastic wrap directly on the surface and refrigerate to cool.

### Mix Liquid Ingredients
3. Pour milk, whole egg liquid, granulated sugar, and high-sugar-tolerant dry yeast into a container. Stir with a hand whisk until uniform.

### Initial Kneading
4. Place bread flour on the work surface and form a well in the center. Pour the mixed liquid into the well. Use a scraper to gradually pull the flour from the edges into the liquid, mixing until no dry flour remains and a very sticky dough forms.  
   **Key point**: The dough has about 67% hydration and is extremely sticky; initial handling should be gentle to prevent liquid from spilling.
5. Transfer the dough to the work surface. Use the heel of your palm to push the dough forward, then use your fingers to pull it back. Repeat this kneading motion like washing clothes on a board.  
   **Texture check**: Knead until the dough holds together, is sticky on the surface but has some elasticity, and can be stretched into a thick film that tears easily.  
   **Key point**: Keep your palms as clean as possible; the dough sticking to your hands is normal—continue working.

### Rest and Add Butter
6. Place the kneaded dough in a container, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 20–30 minutes to rest.
7. Remove the dough and press it flat into a large piece with your hands. Spread the softened unsalted butter evenly over the dough surface, then sprinkle salt evenly on top.
8. Use a scraper to repeatedly cut, chop, and knead the dough until the butter is fully incorporated.  
   **Texture check**: Initially, the dough becomes slippery; continue until the dough is uniform, no longer greasy, and sticky again.

### Slap and Fold for Gluten Development
9. Pick up the dough and repeatedly slap it onto the work surface, folding it over each time. Continue for about 5 minutes.  
   **Texture check**: The dough becomes very smooth, elastic, highly extensible, and no longer sticky.
10. Cut off a small piece of dough and slowly stretch it outward in all directions. It should form an extremely thin, translucent, and tough windowpane membrane with smooth, round edges when torn.

### First Fermentation
11. Shape the dough into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and ferment at room temperature for about 40 minutes, until it doubles in size.  
    **Check method**: Dip a finger in flour and poke a hole in the dough; the hole should not spring back or collapse. Avoid over-fermentation.

### Divide and Fill
12. Remove the dough, gently pat it to release gas. Use a scraper to divide it into 6 portions. Shape each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 25 minutes. After shaping, the surface of the dough balls should be smooth and taut.
13. Take one dough ball, with the seam side facing out, press it flat with your palm to release gas. Place about 50g of chilled red bean filling in the center of the dough piece.
14. Use your thumb and index finger to push the edges of the dough upward, wrapping the filling like a dumpling. Pinch the seams tightly to seal, then place seam-side down.  
    **Key point**: Press the filling firmly and release gas thoroughly to avoid hollow tops after baking.

### Final Fermentation and Decoration
15. Place the shaped dough on a baking tray. Ferment in a warm, humid environment for 40 minutes, until it doubles in size.
16. Remove and let sit at room temperature briefly to allow the surface to dry and form a thin skin.
17. Brush a thin, even layer of egg wash onto the surface of the bread. Dip the bottom of a bottle cap in water, then in black sesame seeds, and press it onto the top of the bread to form a round sesame stamp.

### Baking
18. Place the decorated bread into a preheated oven and bake at high heat quickly.  
    **Reference temperature**: Convection oven 185°C for 8 minutes, or conventional oven top heat 210°C/bottom heat 180°C for 8–10 minutes, removing within 10 minutes.  
    **Key point**: Quick baking at high heat allows the bread to expand and set quickly, resulting in a softer texture. Adjust temperature and time according to your oven.

### Cooling
19. Immediately after baking, transfer the bread from the baking tray to a cooling rack to prevent moisture accumulation on the bottom.

## Health Advice
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron. Paired with the creamy filling, they offer a smooth texture, but the calorie density is relatively high. It is recommended to eat 1–2 pieces at a time, suitable for breakfast or as a post-workout snack. Those requiring strict sugar control should not consume in excess. Use high-sugar-tolerant yeast during preparation to ensure successful fermentation.
