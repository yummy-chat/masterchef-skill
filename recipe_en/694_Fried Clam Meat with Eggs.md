# Fried Clam Meat with Eggs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Live clams 400g (blanched and shelled)  
🥬 Chinese chives 150g (finely chopped)  
🥚 Egg 1 (beaten and set aside)  
🫚 Ginger适量 (minced)  
🌾 Sweet potato starch / sweet potato flour 适量  
🧂 Salt 适量  
🥄 Light soy sauce 适量  
🦪 Oyster sauce 适量  
🌿 Ground white pepper A pinch  
💧 Water 1 small bowl  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spatula / ladle  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the chopped chives, blanched clam meat, minced ginger, salt, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground white pepper, and sweet potato starch together in a bowl. Add the small bowl of water and stir into a thick, paste-like batter.
2. Heat the wok over high heat, then pour in a suitable amount of cooking oil. Pour the chive batter into the wok, using a spatula to continuously stir and ensure even heating. Fry over medium-low heat until the bottom is golden brown and the batter has set.
3. Once the bottom is golden, flip the cake. Drizzle the beaten egg along the edge of the wok. After the egg has set, flip again and continue frying until both sides are golden brown and the inside is fully cooked, then remove from the wok.
   **Key Points**: During frying, continuously move the cake to prevent localized burning and ensure the inside cooks through; adding the beaten egg enhances the golden color and aroma.

## Health Tips
Clam meat is a good source of zinc and iron. Chinese chives support yang energy and aid digestion. This dish is especially suitable for nourishing yang in spring or when you have a poor appetite. Note that clams are cold in nature; those with a weak and cold spleen or stomach should not eat too much. It is recommended to balance this with ginger.
