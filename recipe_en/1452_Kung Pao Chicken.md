# Kung Pao Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs (preferred, for tender texture)
🥜 Peanuts (to be fried)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (Erjingtiao) (bright red, moderate heat, aromatic)
🍂 Sichuan peppercorns (added in two batches)
🧅 Scallions (diced)
🫚 Ginger (diced)
🧄 Garlic (diced)
💧 Ginger-scallion water (added in batches for marinating)
🍶 Cooking wine (1 tbsp, used twice: for marinating and Kung Pao sauce)
⚫ White pepper (for marinating)
🍾 Soy sauce (for marinating)
🧂 Salt (for marinating and Kung Pao sauce)
🥚 Egg white (1/3)
🌽 Cornstarch (for marinating)
🫒 Vegetable oil (for sealing, and mixed 1:1 with chili oil as base oil)
🧂 MSG (for Kung Pao sauce)
🍾 Dark soy sauce (2 tbsp, for Kung Pao sauce)
🍬 Sugar (3 tbsp, for Kung Pao sauce)
🍋 Rice vinegar (4 tbsp, for Kung Pao sauce)
💧 Water starch (2 tbsp, for Kung Pao sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil (a few drops, for Kung Pao sauce)
🍯 Honey (for Kung Pao sauce, to enhance color, shine, and flavor)
🍶 Baijiu (to mix with fried peanuts for longer crispness)
🌶️ Chili oil (1 part, mixed 1:1 with vegetable oil as base oil)
🫒 Oil for drizzling (3-5 drops, added before serving)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🪵 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🥣 Glass bowl
🍳 Frying pan/wok
🥄 Strainer
🍽️ Serving plate
🥄 Spoon

## Steps
1.  Tenderize the chicken thighs by pounding, then score crosshatch patterns and cut into cubes.  
**Key point**: Chicken thighs are more tender; scoring helps with flavor absorption and quick cooking.

2.  Marinate the chicken: Add cooking wine, white pepper, and soy sauce to the chicken cubes. Gradually add ginger-scallion water in three batches, mixing until fully absorbed. Then add salt, 1/3 egg white, and cornstarch, mixing well. Finally, seal with vegetable oil.  
**Note**: Add ginger-scallion water in batches to let the chicken absorb moisture fully; sealing with oil locks in moisture for a tender result.

3.  Prepare the Kung Pao sauce: In a bowl, combine salt, MSG, 1 tbsp cooking wine, 2 tbsp dark soy sauce, 3 tbsp sugar, 4 tbsp rice vinegar, 2 tbsp water starch, and a few drops of sesame oil. Stir well, then add honey and mix again.  
**Note**: Honey enhances color, shine, and complexity of flavor; stir the sauce thoroughly before adding to the wok.

4.  Fry the peanuts: Add peanuts to cold oil and fry over medium heat until they sound crispy and are cooked through. Remove and immediately mix with baijiu, then store in an airtight container.  
**Note**: Starting in cold oil prevents the peanuts from browning too quickly without cooking through; baijiu helps maintain crispness longer.

5.  Prepare the dried chili peppers: Wipe the Erjingtiao dried chilies clean and cut into segments.  
**Note**: Erjingtiao dried chilies are bright red, moderately spicy, and very aromatic.

6.  Cook the aromatics: Add the mixed base oil to the wok. First, add the first batch of Sichuan peppercorns and fry over medium heat until blackened, then remove. Next, stir-fry the dried chili segments until slightly charred, then add the second batch of Sichuan peppercorns and stir until fragrant.  
**Note**: Add Sichuan peppercorns in two batches; do not add chilies and peppercorns at the same time, or the peppercorns will burn before the chilies color.

7.  Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry quickly until fully changed color.  
**Note**: Stir-fry the chicken rapidly to ensure even heating.

8.  When the chicken is about 60% cooked, add the diced scallions, ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
**Note**: Adding aromatics at 60% doneness allows their fragrance to release while the chicken finishes cooking without becoming tough.

9.  Stir the Kung Pao sauce again, pour it into the wok, and once the starch thickens, stir-fry until the sauce evenly coats the ingredients.  
**Note**: The sauce should be thick and fully coat the chicken.

10. Add the fried peanuts, drizzle 3-5 drops of oil, and stir-fry quickly to combine. Plate and serve.  
**Note**: Adding peanuts last keeps them crispy; the oil drizzle enhances the dish's shine and aroma.

## Health Advice
While Kung Pao Chicken features tender thighs and crispy peanuts, the sauce is high in oil and sugar, making it calorie-dense. It is suitable for occasional enjoyment rather than frequent consumption. Moderate the amount of dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns to avoid irritating the stomach.
