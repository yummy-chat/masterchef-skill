# Braised Fish
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧂 Salt  
🧂 Cooking wine  
🧂 Vinegar  
🧂 Soy sauce (dark soy sauce)  
🧂 White granulated sugar  
🧂 Oyster sauce (optional)  
🧂 MSG or chicken bouillon (optional)  
🐟 Fish (medium-sized crucian carp recommended, score the skin beforehand)  
🐟 Ginger  
🐟 Garlic cloves  
🐟 Dried chili peppers  
🐟 Scallions (optional)  
🐟 Cilantro (optional)  
🐟 Bird’s eye chili peppers (optional)  

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (lid recommended)  
🔪 Chef's knife (⚠️ Handle with care)  
🔪 Cutting board  

## Steps
1. **Prep Ingredients**
   Julienne the ginger, smash or slice the garlic cloves, chop the dried chili peppers, and chop the bird's eye chili peppers. Wash the fish and thoroughly dry its surface with a kitchen towel or cloth.

2. **Fry the Fish**
   Heat the wok, then pour in 30-50 ml of oil, and reduce to low heat. Place the dried fish in the wok, gently shake the wok so the hot oil evenly contacts the fish’s surface, and fry slowly until one side is browned. Flip the fish and repeat the frying process.

3. **Aromatics**
   Add the julienned ginger, smashed garlic, and chopped dried chili peppers, gently stir-frying until fragrant.

4. **Season and Braise**
   Drizzle cooking wine along the edge of the wok. ⚠️ There will be significant smoke at this point, so be cautious. Add vinegar, then white granulated sugar and soy sauce. Pour in cold water, just enough to cover the fish. Turn to medium heat and cover with a lid. After about 1 minute, uncover and flip the fish, then re-cover.

5. **Reduce and Enhance Flavor**
   After braising for 3-4 minutes, add salt, bird's eye chili peppers, oyster sauce, and MSG. Cover and continue braising, flipping the fish once more if needed. When the sauce has reduced to just below the fish's dorsal fin line, or when there’s less liquid left, reduce to low heat.

6. **Add Fragrance and Serve**
   Sprinkle in chopped cilantro and scallions, cover for 20 seconds, then turn off the heat. Carefully plate the fish and serve.

## Health Tips
Braised fish is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3 fatty acids, which help support brain function and recovery. However, improper oil temperature control during frying may lead to excess calories. It’s recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Diabetic individuals should be mindful of the sugar content.
