# Shanghai-Style Sizzling Eel Shreds
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 Fresh eel shreds 300g (freshly cut, sliced into 6-7 cm pieces)
🧅 Ginger, to taste (finely shredded)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)
🌿 Scallions, to taste (white and green parts minced separately)
🫒 Vegetable oil, to taste (more than usual for stir-frying)
🥓 Lard, a small piece
🍶 Shaoxing wine, to taste (about one splash)
🧂 Light soy sauce, to taste
🧂 Dark soy sauce, to taste (more than light soy sauce)
💧 Water, to taste
🍬 White sugar, generous amount (key to Shanghai flavor)
🌶️ White pepper powder, to taste (used twice)
🥣 Cornstarch slurry (small bowl of water + 2 tablespoons cornstarch, mixed in advance)
🫗 Sesame oil, a dash
🔥 Boiling hot oil, one ladle (for final sizzle)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🫕 Small clay pot (preheated in advance)
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1.  Rinse the eel shreds, cut into 6-7 cm pieces, not too short. Finely shred the ginger, mince the garlic, and separately mince the white and green parts of the scallions. Set aside.
2.  Heat the wok over high heat, add vegetable oil and a small piece of lard. The amount of oil should be more than usual for stir-frying.
3.  Reduce heat to low, add the shredded ginger, minced garlic, and minced scallion whites. Stir-fry slowly until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
4.  Turn the heat to high, add all the eel shreds. Stir-fry quickly until the eel flesh begins to turn white.
5.  Pour a splash of Shaoxing wine along the edge of the wok, stir-fry to remove any fishy smell and enhance the aroma.
6.  Add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce (more dark than light). Stir-fry over high heat to release the savory sauce aroma.
7.  Add an appropriate amount of water, a generous amount of white sugar, and some white pepper powder. Stir to combine.
8.  Turn the heat to medium. Gently push the eel shreds while gradually reducing the sauce until it thickens.
9.  During the sauce reduction, add the pre-mixed cornstarch slurry in three separate batches. After each addition, bring to a boil and stir well to ensure the sauce is thick and evenly coats the eel shreds.
10. Before removing from the heat, drizzle in a dash of sesame oil. Stir to combine, then transfer to the preheated small clay pot.
11. Sprinkle white pepper powder over the surface of the eel shreds. Make a small well in the center and place the prepared minced garlic and minced scallion greens inside.
12. Splash one ladle of boiling hot oil precisely over the garlic and scallion mixture, creating a sizzling sound that releases all the aromas. This is the signature "sizzling oil" finishing touch. Mix the flavorful oil and toppings on top with the eel shreds at the bottom before serving.

## Health Tips
This sizzling eel dish is a classic example of Shanghai cuisine's "thick oil and rich sauce". The eel is high in protein and low in fat, making it a good choice for summer nourishment. However, the sugar and oil content are not low. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables to help manage overall calorie intake.
