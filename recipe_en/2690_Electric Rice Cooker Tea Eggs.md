# Electric Rice Cooker Tea Eggs  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs: about 15-20  
🍵 Loose tea leaves: a small handful  
⭐ Star anise: 2-3 pieces  
🌿 Cinnamon stick: a small piece  
🍃 Bay leaves: 2-3 leaves  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers: 2-3  
✨ Sichuan peppercorns: a small pinch  
🧂 Salt: to taste  
🧴 Light soy sauce/soy sauce: to taste  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🚰 Vegetable washing basin/sink  
🍳 Cooking pot  
🥄 Soup spoon  
♨️ Electric rice cooker  
🍽️ Plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Rinse the eggs under running water until clean, ensuring no dirt remains on the eggshells.  
2. Place the cleaned eggs in a cooking pot, add enough water, bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for about 8-10 minutes until the yolks are fully set.  
   **Note**: The eggs must be cooked through, with the yolks completely solidified, so the shells won't crack easily during the next step.  
3. Gently tap the eggshells with the back of a soup spoon to create fine, even cracks, keeping the shells intact and not falling off.  
   **Key**: The finer the cracks, the more evenly the marinade will penetrate, and the more beautiful the final pattern.  
4. Add star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and tea leaves to the cooking pot. Then, add salt and light soy sauce/soy sauce to taste, and stir to create a marinade. The broth should be a dark brown color.  
   **Note**: Add all spices at once; the base flavor of the marinade should be slightly salty for better penetration later.  
5. Pour the cracked eggs along with the marinade and all spices into the inner pot of the electric rice cooker.  
6. Close the lid of the electric rice cooker, select the "Cook Rice" mode, and let the automatic program complete the marination process without supervision. After the program finishes, you can keep the eggs warm and let them steep for 2-3 hours for deeper flavor.  
   **Key**: The sealed, heat-retaining feature of the electric rice cooker allows the tea eggs to continue absorbing flavor at a gentle simmer, resulting in a softer, more layered texture.  

## Health Advice  
Tea eggs are rich in protein and low in fat, making them suitable as a snack or breakfast energy booster. However, the marinade contains high levels of salt and spices. Those with high blood pressure or sensitive stomachs are advised to consume 2-3 eggs per week.
