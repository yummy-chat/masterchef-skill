# Braised Common Carp 🐟

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 1 common carp (about 1250g)
🫚 Ginger: 10g for marinating (smashed), 30g for cooking (cut into large pieces)
🧅 Scallion: 15g for marinating (smashed), 40g for cooking (sliced diagonally)
🧄 Garlic: 30g (about 9 cloves, flattened)
🌿 Cilantro: 1 stalk (with root, washed)
🫑 Green chili pepper: 1 (stemmed, seeded, split open)
⭐ Star anise: 2 pieces
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns: about 3g
🌶️ Dried chili peppers: to taste (cut into segments, adjust according to preference)
🍶 Cooking wine: 30g (3 tablespoons)
🧂 Salt: 3g for marinating + 3g for stewing
🍾 Light soy sauce: 40g (4 tablespoons, low-sodium)
🍾 Aged vinegar: 10g (1 tablespoon)
🍬 White sugar: 6g (2 teaspoons)
🧂 Chicken bouillon: 3g (1 teaspoon)
🍾 Dark soy sauce: 7g (2/3 tablespoon)
🍾 Oyster sauce: 20g (1 tablespoon)
🥜 Cooking oil: as needed
🥓 Lard: 1 tablespoon (about 15g)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
✂️ Scissors
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Cast iron pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Silicone spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Cooking Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Clean the fish**
   Scale the fish, use scissors to trim the fins, and shape the tail into a swallowtail shape. Remove the internal organs, thoroughly clean the black membrane and blood from the abdominal cavity, and pat the fish completely dry inside and out with a kitchen towel.
   **Key point**: Leaving the dorsal fin untrimmed will affect the appearance when frying; the black abdominal membrane must be completely removed to eliminate fishy odor; drying the fish thoroughly is essential to prevent the skin from breaking during frying.

2. **Score the fish**
   With the fish’s back facing you, make cuts at one-finger intervals down to the bone, gently slicing without cutting through the belly or tail. Score both sides similarly, and make a diagonal cross-hatch cut on the tail.
   **Key point**: Do not cut all the way to the belly or the connection with the backbone, to prevent the fish from breaking apart during stewing.

3. **Marinate the fish**
   Rub the smashed scallion and ginger with cooking wine and 3g of salt to extract the juice. Evenly coat both sides of the fish and the inside of the cavity. Set aside.

4. **Prepare the aromatics**
   Cut the cooking ginger into large pieces, slice the scallion diagonally, flatten the garlic cloves; wash the cilantro, and split the green chili pepper after removing the seeds.

### Cooking Stage
1. **Heat the pan and fry the fish**
   Heat the iron pot over high heat until it smokes and turns white. Add a generous amount of cooking oil, swirl to coat the entire surface, then pour out the excess oil, leaving only a thin layer. Hold the fish by the head and first lower the tail into the hot oil to set, then gently slide the whole fish in. Fry over medium-high heat for 30-40 seconds per side. Shake the pot occasionally to ensure the head and tail are also cooked. Use a spatula to lift the belly and flip the fish. Fry until both sides are golden brown and set, then remove the fish and discard the frying oil.
   **Key point**: Do not move the fish immediately after placing it in the pan; wait about ten seconds for it to set before shaking the pot. The frying oil must be discarded to avoid a fishy flavor in the final dish.

2. **Sauté the aromatics**
   Add a normal amount of cooking oil for stir-frying and 1 tablespoon of lard to the pot. Melt the lard over medium heat, then add the scallion, ginger, garlic, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili pepper segments. Stir-fry until fragrant.
   **Key point**: The pot is hot, so do not use too high a heat to prevent the aromatics from burning.

3. **Stew for flavor**
   Return the fried fish to the pot. Pour light soy sauce and aged vinegar along the edge of the pot and let it simmer briefly to release the aroma. Add enough boiling water or hot water to cover, then add sugar, salt, chicken bouillon, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Finally, add the cilantro and green chili pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover and reduce to medium-low heat. Simmer for 20 minutes.
   **Key point**: Using hot water helps keep the broth milky white; the stewing time must be sufficient—"a thousand stews for tofu, ten thousand for fish."

4. **Reduce the sauce and plate**
   After 20 minutes, turn the heat to the lowest setting. Use a slotted spoon and chopsticks to remove all solid aromatics from the broth. Increase the heat to high and reduce the sauce, continuously spooning the sauce over the fish. When the sauce becomes slightly thickened, gently push the fish onto a plate using a spatula. Finally, pour the remaining sauce from the pot over the fish.
   **Key point**: Remove all solid aromatics thoroughly for a clean sauce; do not reduce the sauce too much—it is excellent for mixing with rice.

## Health Advice
This braised common carp is rich in high-quality protein and Omega-3, making it suitable for the whole family. However, the frying process uses a fair amount of oil, so be mindful of overall calorie intake. Those with weak digestive systems should reduce the amount of chili pepper and Sichuan peppercorn to avoid irritating the stomach.
