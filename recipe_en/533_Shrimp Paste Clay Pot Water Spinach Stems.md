# Shrimp Paste Clay Pot Water Spinach Stems
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

## Ingredients  
🌱 Water spinach stems (break into small sections by hand, stems only, preferably "wild water spinach" with thin stalks)  
🥓 Pork belly (a few slices, sliced)  
🧄 Garlic cloves (6–8, peeled and kept whole)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chilies (2–3, cut into sections)  

## Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp paste (adjust to taste, requires pre-treatment)  
🍬 White sugar (adjust to balance shrimp paste)  
🥄 Lard (adjust to taste, used twice: once mixed with shrimp paste, once as base oil)  
🍶 High-proof liquor (adjust to taste, poured along the edge of the pot)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot or heavy-bottomed wok  
🥢 Wok spatula or chopsticks  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  

## Steps  
1. In a small bowl, combine shrimp paste with white sugar and lard. Stir until well blended. Set aside.  
- Pre-mixing balances the saltiness, enhances umami and sweetness, and prevents uneven seasoning when added to the pot.  

2. Slice the pork belly. Peel the garlic cloves, keeping them whole. Cut the bird's eye chilies into sections.  
3. Place the clay pot over high heat until smoking, ensuring it is thoroughly heated.  
- The soul of clay pot cooking lies in high heat for instant cooking; insufficient heat will result in loss of crisp texture.  

4. Melt lard in the clay pot. Add the pork belly slices and stir-fry until the edges are lightly charred and oil renders. Then add the garlic cloves and stir-fry until fragrant.  
- Use the fat from lard and pork belly to build a fragrant base for the dish.  

5. Add the water spinach stems to the pot. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until they begin to soften and absorb the wok hei (breath of the wok).  
6. Splash a small amount of high-proof liquor along the edge of the pot to enhance aroma. Immediately add the prepared shrimp paste mixture. Stir-fry quickly over high heat until evenly coated, ensuring every stem is covered with the sauce.  
7. Add the bird's eye chili sections and toss briefly for a few stirs.  
- Keep the total stir-frying time **within 2 minutes** to maintain the crispness of the water spinach stems.  

8. Turn off the heat and serve immediately, bringing the clay pot directly to the table. Enjoy while hot. The stems should be bright green and glossy, with the sauce just reduced and clinging to the ingredients.  

## Health Advice  
Shrimp paste is high in salt, so control the amount used. Water spinach stems are rich in dietary fiber, suitable for cooling and relieving summer heat. Pork belly provides a small amount of fat, which aids absorption of fat-soluble vitamins, but should be reduced for those with high blood lipids.
