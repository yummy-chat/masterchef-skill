# Egg Tarts
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧊 All-purpose flour 300g
🍬 White sugar 35g (15g for dough, 20g for custard)
🥛 Whole milk 280ml (160ml for dough, 120ml for custard)
🧈 Butter 200g (40g kneaded into dough, 160g for lamination)
🥚 Eggs 2

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Air fryer
🥣 Glass bowl
🥛 Measuring cup
🍴 Silicone spatula
🥖 Rolling pin
Plastic wrap
🔪 Knife
Cutting board
🥄 Hand whisk or mixer
Strainer
Egg tart molds

## Instructions
1. **Make the basic dough**: Mix all-purpose flour, 15g white sugar, and 160ml milk until no dry flour remains. Add 40g butter and knead into a medium-firm dough. Seal and refrigerate for 30 minutes to rest.
2. **Prepare the butter for lamination**: Wrap 160g butter in plastic wrap and roll it into an 18cm x 18cm square sheet. Refrigerate until ready to use.
3. **Make the egg custard**: Whisk together 120ml milk, 20g white sugar, and 2 eggs until well combined. Strain through a sieve to obtain a smooth custard. Set aside.
4. **Wrap the butter**: Roll out the rested dough into a square sheet. Place the chilled butter sheet in the center. Fold the four edges over the butter, pinching all seams to seal.
5. **First fold**: Gently press and roll out the butter-wrapped dough from both sides. Fold it like a blanket (letter fold). Wrap in plastic wrap and freeze for 15 minutes to set.
6. **Second fold**: Remove the dough and roll it out again. Fold it in the same blanket style, wrap, and freeze for 15 minutes.
7. **Third fold and roll up**: Remove the dough. While pressing and rolling, make one end of the sheet slightly thinner. Starting from the other end, tightly roll it into a long cylindrical log. Wrap and freeze for 15 minutes.
   - Note: Freezing after each fold prevents the butter from softening and ensures distinct layers.
8. **Cut and shape**: Slice the frozen log into evenly sized small rounds. Place one round into an egg tart mold. Using your thumb, press from the center outward to thin the center and push the edges flush with the mold. Smooth any excess dough. Refrigerate the shaped shells for at least 20 minutes to rest.
9. **Fill with custard**: Remove the rested tart shells. Pour the egg custard into each, filling to 80% full to prevent overflow during baking.
10. **Bake**: Place the tarts in the air fryer. Bake at 180°C for 20 minutes, checking the color during baking and adjusting time as needed. Bake until the surface and edges turn golden brown.

## Health Suggestions
Egg tarts are high in fat and sugar, with relatively high calories. They are suitable for occasional consumption and not recommended as a daily afternoon snack. For those with lactose intolerance or who are monitoring sugar intake, consider reducing the sugar or using low-fat milk.
