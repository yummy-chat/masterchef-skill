# Waterless Seafood Boil  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦀 3-4 flower crabs (cleaned and prepared in advance, ready to cook)  
🦐适量 fresh sea shrimp (washed and ready to use)  
🧂 None (traditional coastal method, no salt, ginger, or any seasonings added throughout)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Stainless steel pot (with lid)  
🔥 Gas stove  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍽️ Serving plate  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the prepared flower crabs upside down (shell side down) in the waterless stainless steel pot, then add the washed sea shrimp. Cover with the lid.  
2. Heat over medium heat and cook for 6-7 minutes, until all the moisture in the pot has evaporated and the bottom of the pot begins to dry and slightly char.  
**Indicator**: The crabs are fully red, the meat is thoroughly cooked, and you can smell a faint charred aroma.  
3. Turn off the heat. Use chopsticks or a slotted spoon to remove the seafood and arrange it on a serving plate.  
**Key Points**:  
   - Do not add a single drop of water throughout the process; the seafood is steamed using its own natural moisture.  
   - The crabs must be placed upside down to prevent the roe from leaking out and to ensure even heat distribution.  
   - No seasonings are added to preserve the original flavor. The charred bottom of the pot imparts a unique smoky taste to the seafood—do not scrape it off.  

## Health Tips  
This waterless seafood boil perfectly preserves the natural flavors of flower crabs and sea shrimp. It is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for those on a low-fat diet. Use fresh seafood, and those with gout should consume in moderation.
