# Northeast China Iron Pot Braised Pork Ribs

Cuisine: Northeast Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork shoulder blade bones (Haraba) about one pot full  
🥔 Dried potato slices, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (partially sliced into scallion flowers)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (partially minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, 2-3 pieces  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed  
⭐ Star anise, 1-2 pieces  
🫚 Ginger, as needed  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more)  
🧂 Salt, two tablespoons  
🥄 Light soy sauce, as needed (about one tablespoon)  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, a small amount  
💧 Boiling water, sufficient amount  

## Tools and Equipment  
♨️ Wok  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the pork shoulder blade bones into pieces. Place in cold water and slowly blanch over medium heat. During this time, continuously skim off foam with plenty of water, and turn the pieces to ensure even heating. Once fully blanched, remove and rinse with warm water, then drain well.  
**Note**: Must blanch slowly over medium heat to allow blood foam to fully release, ensuring tender meat; if the raw ingredients are not very fresh, add scallions, ginger, cooking wine, or Sichuan peppercorns and star anise during blanching.  

2. Soak the dried potato slices in boiling water for about 5 minutes, rinse off surface dust, soak until the surface softens slightly, then remove and drain well. Set aside.  
**Note**: Soaking time should be short to allow them to absorb the meat broth; do not soak them through.  

3. Heat the wok over high heat until hot, pour in slightly more cooking oil to coat the wok. Once the oil is hot, add the drained meat pieces and stir-fry over high heat, using the oil to sear the meat surface and seal in the flavor.  
4. Add scallions, ginger, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, and dried chili peppers. Then add two tablespoons of salt and stir-fry evenly over high heat.  
**Key Point**: Salt should be added all at once to establish a base flavor for the meat and to first solidify the protein.  

5. Drizzle in light soy sauce and a small amount of dark soy sauce. Continue stir-frying over high heat to fully release the sauce's aroma, being careful to avoid burning.  
6. Add the soaked dried potato slices and stir-fry together with the meat over high heat, allowing the potato slices to evenly absorb the sauce and meat flavors.  
7. Add sufficient boiling water to cover all ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat. Cover with a lid and braise for about 1 hour, until bubbles persistently appear in every corner of the pot and the broth thickens.  
**Key Point**: Must add boiling water; otherwise, the meat becomes tough and easily develops a fishy smell. Maintain the heat so the broth keeps boiling.  

8. Remove the lid, stir, and turn up the heat to reduce the broth, allowing it to naturally thicken until only oil remains at the bottom of the pot with no visible broth.  
**Key Point**: Reducing the broth is the key to the iron pot braise’s aroma; the flavors of the ingredients are concentrated in the oil.  

9. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in a generous amount of scallion flowers and minced garlic. Toss a few times using residual heat to release the aroma of the fresh scallions and garlic, then serve.  

## Health Suggestions  
This iron pot braised pork ribs paired with dried potato slices offers balanced nutrition: pork provides high-quality protein and B vitamins, while dried potato slices are rich in dietary fiber and potassium. It is suitable for those with high physical exertion or for warming the body in winter. Note the longer braising time; it is recommended to control total salt intake. People with high blood pressure can reduce the amount of light soy sauce.
