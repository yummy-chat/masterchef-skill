# Qian County Tofu Pudding  
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌶️ Fine chili powder 80g (for color)  
🌶️ Coarse chili powder 40g (for aroma)  
🥜 Peeled raw white sesame seeds 20g  
🧅 Scallion segments, to taste  
🧄 Ginger slices, to taste  
🧅 Onion shreds, to taste  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste  
🍶 Aromatic vinegar, to taste  
💧 Water, 1 bowl  
🍬 Rock sugar, 2 pieces  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🌿 Black cardamom, to taste  
🌿 Bay leaves, to taste  
⭐ Star anise, to taste  
🌿 Cinnamon stick, to taste  
🌿 Galangal, to taste  
🧂 Spice powder (star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, black cardamom, Sichuan peppercorns, fennel seeds, white pepper, long pepper, cloves) Use 8g per 500g oil  
🍲 Spice bag for spiced water (white pepper, star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, amomum villosum, dried ginger, cardamom, white cardamom) 1 bag  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🫘 Tofu pudding, to taste  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, 500g (1 catty)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🥣 Soy sauce, 30g  
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder, 20g  
🧂 MSG, 8g  
🍶 High-proof baijiu, a dash  
🫒 Cooked soybean oil, a small amount  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥘 Stockpot  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Stone mortar and pestle  
🥢 Strainer spoon  
🍶 Ceramic bowl  
🫙 Lidded ceramic container  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix fine chili powder, coarse chili powder, and peeled raw white sesame seeds together. Set aside as the dry chili oil mixture.  
2. Pour rapeseed oil into a wok, heat until the oil is cooked through, then turn off the heat. When the oil temperature drops below 200°C, add scallion segments, ginger slices, onion shreds, and cilantro. Turn the heat to low and fry slowly until the solid ingredients turn scorched yellow. Remove and discard them.  
3. Make chili oil:  
   - When the oil temperature drops to 200°C, add white sesame seeds and fry until they float slightly and turn yellow.  
   - Add the spice powder.  
   - When the oil temperature drops to 170°C, first add the coarse chili powder and mix well, then add the fine chili powder and stir thoroughly to release the color.  
   - Finally, drizzle in a small amount of high-proof baijiu to release the fragrance.  
**Key Point**: Strictly follow the order of adding sesame seeds at 200°C and chili powder at 170°C; add the fine powder last to prevent burning.  

4. Pour the chili oil into a lidded ceramic container and immediately cover it. Let it simmer using residual heat to fully release the aroma.  
5. Prepare the vinegar water:  
   - Heat a small amount of oil in a pan, stir-fry ginger slices until fragrant, then pour in aromatic vinegar to flash-fry.  
   - Add 1 bowl of water, Sichuan peppercorns, black cardamom, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon stick, galangal, and 2 pieces of rock sugar.  
   - Simmer over low heat until the sharp sourness evaporates and the spice aroma is released.  
**Key Point**: Rock sugar softens the sourness; adding water dilutes the vinegar to avoid an overly harsh acidity.  

6. Prepare the spiced water:  
   - Bring 2 catties of water to a boil. Add the spice bag and simmer until the liquid turns a pale green color with a subtle spice fragrance, then remove the spice bag immediately.  
   - Add 30g soy sauce for color, then add 20g chicken bouillon powder and 8g MSG for umami. Keep warm for later use.  
7. Prepare the garlic water:  
   - Place garlic in a stone mortar, add a small amount of cooked soybean oil, and pound into a paste.  
   - Mix the garlic paste with water in a ratio of 1 part garlic paste to 2 parts water, stirring until it forms a thick, milky-white garlic liquid.  
**Note**: The cooked soybean oil prevents the garlic from oxidizing and turning dark; the garlic water should be slightly thick.  

8. Assemble the tofu pudding:  
   - Scoop several spoonfuls of tofu pudding into a bowl. Add a small amount of salt, a small amount of garlic water, and a small amount of vinegar water in sequence.  
   - Pour half a spoonful of hot spiced water over the top.  
   - Finally, drizzle one spoonful of the simmered chili oil.  
**Note**: Do not add too much seasoning; the focus should be on the chili and spice aromas. Mix well before eating.  

## Health Advice  
This tofu pudding layers chili oil, vinegar water, and spiced water, delivering a sour, spicy, and aromatic experience that suits those wanting to taste Shaanxi flavor. However, it is high in oil and salt, so consumption should be controlled.
