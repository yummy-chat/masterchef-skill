# Shredded Potatoes with Hot and Sour Flavor
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potatoes Approximately 650g with skin (peeled and shredded to medium thickness)  
🌶️ Thin-skinned Green Chili Peppers 65g (stemmed, seeded, and sliced diagonally into strips)  
🌶️ Bird's Eye Chili Peppers 40g (roots removed, split lengthwise in half)  
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers To taste (cut into segments)  
🧄 Garlic 20g (about 4 cloves, sliced)  
🌿 Cilantro Stems 15g (leaves removed, cut into small sections; can substitute with scallion sections)  
🌿 Sichuan Peppercorns 2g  
🫒 Cooking Oil To taste  
🧈 Lard 1 tablespoon (optional)  
🧂 White Vinegar 10g (for soaking shredded potatoes)  
🧂 Light Soy Sauce 20g  
🧂 Dark Soy Sauce 3g  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🧂 MSG 3g  
🍬 White Sugar 3g  
🧂 Black Vinegar (or White Vinegar) 20g (added just before removing from heat)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥒 Box Grater  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Colander/Strainer  
🥄 Fine Mesh Skimmer (for frying Sichuan peppercorns)  
🔪 Chef’s Knife, Cutting Board  

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash and peel the potatoes, shred them into medium-thick strips using a box grater, then place in water and repeatedly wash and rub until the water runs completely clear.
   **Note**: Rinse off excess starch to prevent sticking during stir-frying.
2. Add enough water to cover the shredded potatoes and 10g white vinegar, mix well, and soak for 10 minutes. Remove and drain thoroughly.
   **Key**: Soaking in vinegar makes the shredded potatoes crisper; drain as much water as possible to avoid affecting the wok heat.
3. Pour oil into the wok, place the fine mesh skimmer with Sichuan peppercorns in the oil, and fry over medium-low heat until the peppercorns turn deep purple and release their aroma. Immediately remove and discard the peppercorns.
   **Key**: Do not burn the Sichuan peppercorns until they turn black, as this will make them bitter.
4. Immediately turn off the heat after removing the peppercorns. When the oil temperature drops slightly, add the garlic slices, bird's eye chili peppers, and dried chili pepper segments. Stir-fry briefly using residual heat to release their fragrance.
5. Turn the heat to high, drizzle light soy sauce and dark soy sauce along the edge of the wok, and stir-fry quickly until the sauce bubbles and releases a fragrant aroma.
6. Immediately add the drained shredded potatoes. Stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute, until the potatoes soften and are evenly coated with the sauce.
7. Add the green chili pepper strips and stir-fry until well combined. Then add salt, MSG, and white sugar, continuing to stir-fry quickly.
8. Add the cilantro stem sections and stir-fry until combined. Then, drizzle 20g black vinegar along the hot edge of the wok, stir-fry rapidly a few times, and immediately turn off the heat and remove from the wok.
   **Key**: Add the vinegar along the edge of the wok; the high heat will release the vinegar's aroma. Keep the stir-frying time short to maintain the sour fragrance.

## Health Advice  
This shredded potatoes with hot and sour flavor dish is rich in carbohydrates, but soaking in vinegar before cooking reduces the starch content and lowers the glycemic response, making it suitable for weight management. Bird's eye chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns can stimulate appetite, but those with excessive stomach acid should consume less vinegar.
