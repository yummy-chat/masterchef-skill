# Rice Broth Fish Slices  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍚 Rice, as needed (used in two separate stages; Thai jasmine rice recommended if available)  
🐟 Freshwater fish, 1 (about 1 lb; options include sea bass, mandarin fish, or ya fish)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed  
🧅 Scallion, as needed  
🥚 Egg, half (use the egg white)  
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (use only the stems)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🌶️ White pepper, a pinch  
🧂 MSG, a pinch  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🌾 Cornstarch or bean starch, as needed  
🧈 Lard, a small amount  

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok or frying pan  
🫖 Soup pot or soup pitcher  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥄 Strainer  
🧽 Cheesecloth  
🥣 Bowl or deep dish  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Soak the rice**: Soak the rice in freshly boiled water, stir, and let sit for at least half an hour. Drain and set aside.  
2. **Prepare the fish**: Remove the head and tail. Fillet the fish along the backbone, remove the large belly bones to get clean fillets. Chop the head and bones into pieces. Slice the fillets diagonally as thinly as possible.  
3. **Rinse and marinate the fish slices**: Rinse the fish slices repeatedly under running cold water until the water runs completely clear. Squeeze out excess moisture. Add salt, white pepper, MSG, chicken bouillon, and ginger-scallion water. Mix until sticky, then add half an egg white and mix well. Finally, add cornstarch and mix to seal in moisture.  
**Key point**: Rinsing until the water is clear is essential for the fish to be white and tender; the marinade must create a sticky, gelatinous texture.  

4. **Toast the rice**: Drain the soaked rice. Dry-fry in a wok over low heat, stirring constantly, until the grains turn a light yellow and become crispy. Set aside.  
**Note**: Stir patiently to avoid burning.  

5. **Fry the fish bones**: Heat plenty of oil in a wok to 60% hot. Fry the fish bones until golden and crispy, then remove and drain.  
6. **Make the fish broth**: Leave a base of oil in the wok, add a little lard, and stir-fry ginger slices and scallion sections until slightly yellow. Add the fried fish bones and stir-fry, crushing them with the spatula as you go to fully release the aroma. Pour in enough **boiling water**, bring to a boil over high heat, add a small handful of raw rice, and keep boiling vigorously for about 5 minutes until the broth turns rich, milky white, and creamy.  
**Key point**: Boiling water must be used to achieve a rich, white broth; the fish bones must be crushed and stir-fried well.  

7. **Season and strain**: Season the broth with salt, a pinch of MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, and sugar. Mix well, then strain through cheesecloth to remove all bones and residue, leaving only the smooth, rich broth. Transfer to a soup pitcher and keep piping hot.  
8. **Assemble the dish**: Spread the toasted rice in an even layer at the bottom of a deep bowl. Arrange the marinated fish slices evenly on top, and sprinkle with minced cilantro stems and chopped scallion. Pour the scalding hot fish broth evenly over the bowl, blanching the fish slices until they turn white and are cooked through.  
**Key point**: The broth must be boiling hot to instantly cook the raw fish slices, ensuring a tender, smooth texture.  

## Health Tips  
This dish poaches raw fish slices with a hot broth, preserving the nutrients well. It is suitable for people who need a high-protein, low-fat diet. Toasting the rice adds dietary fiber, but the overall calorie content is not low, so portion control is recommended.
