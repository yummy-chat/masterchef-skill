# Fried Spring Chicken
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
### Ingredients  
🐔 8 dressed chickens (about 500g each)  
🌾 About 5 kg (10 jin) of scale-coated flour  
💧 Water, as needed (for soaking; 1120g used for marinade)  
🫒 25 jin shortening  
🫒 Soybean oil, as needed (mix with shortening at a 3:2 ratio by volume in winter)  

### Seasonings (mixed into a dry marinade powder)  
🧂 100g salt  
🌶️ 25g extra-fine chili powder  
🌿 6g Sichuan peppercorn powder  
🧄 25g garlic powder  
🍬 75g white sugar  
🧂 20g MSG  
🌰 20g white pepper powder  
🟤 3g five-spice powder  
🟤 3g black pepper powder  
🟫 2g cinnamon powder  
💊 8g chicken flavor essence  
💊 12g compound meat water-retaining agent  
🧂 15g baking soda  
🍗 75g New Flavor Orleans seasoning  
💊 2.5g disodium 5'-ribonucleotide  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🛡️ Large resealable bag  
🪣 Large plastic basin/sink  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🍳 Commercial electric deep fryer  
🧺 Oil filter basket  
📦 Rectangular plastic breading box  
🥢 Kitchen tongs  
🎞️ Plastic wrap  
🧤 Disposable gloves  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare the dry marinade powder**  
   Weigh all seasonings according to the recipe, mix evenly, and shake thoroughly in a sealed bag.

2. **Process the whole chicken**  
   - Soak the dressed chicken in clean water for 4 hours to thaw and remove blood, changing the water once during this time.  
   - Use kitchen scissors to remove the chicken fat, lymph nodes, the "waste sac" at the tail, and any internal organ residue under the ribs.  
   - Rinse the chicken thoroughly on both sides under running water, and pluck any remaining feathers.

3. **Marinate**  
   Mix the marinade using a ratio of 8 jin chicken, 240g dry marinade powder, and 1120g water. Place the whole chicken in the marinade and massage by hand for 20 minutes. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 12 hours.

4. **Heat the oil**  
   First heat the shortening to 100°C to melt it, then raise the temperature to 170°C and maintain it.

5. **Breading to form scales**  
   - **First breading**: Place the marinated chicken in the breading box, press firmly to coat, ensuring all surfaces are evenly covered with dry flour.  
   - **Water dip**: Place the chicken in the filter basket, soak in clean water for about 2 seconds, then lift quickly.  
   - **Second breading**: Return the chicken to the flour box and use a "press, rub, and shake" technique for a second coating to create scale-like patterns on the surface.  
   - Repeat the water dip and second breading steps twice, then shake off any excess dry flour.

6. **Fry**  
   When the oil temperature reaches 170°C, add the whole chicken and fry for about 12 minutes. Flip once halfway through to ensure both sides are evenly golden brown.

7. **Remove from the oil**  
   Use kitchen tongs to take out the fried chicken and place it on a filter or paper towel to drain excess oil.

## Health Advice  
This fried chicken contains water-retaining agents and flavor essence, so it is recommended for occasional consumption only, not as a regular meal. Be mindful of sodium and trans fat intake. Suitable as a "cheat meal" for fitness enthusiasts or for sharing at gatherings; those with high blood lipids should exercise caution.
