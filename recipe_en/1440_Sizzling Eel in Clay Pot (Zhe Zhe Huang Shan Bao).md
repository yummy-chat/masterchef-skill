# Sizzling Eel in Clay Pot (Zhe Zhe Huang Shan Bao)
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐍 Eel: appropriate amount (cleaned, cut into 6cm pieces)
🫚 Ginger: appropriate amount (sliced or smashed)
🧄 Garlic: appropriate amount (whole cloves or smashed)
🧅 Scallion: appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🌶️ Red bell pepper: appropriate amount (cut into chunks)
🌿 Cilantro: appropriate amount (for garnish and aroma)
🥜 Peanut butter: 1 tbsp
🫘 Broad bean paste: 1 tbsp
🍛 Chu hou paste: 1 tbsp
🦪 Hoisin sauce: 1 tbsp
🧂 Salt: a small amount
🍬 Sugar: a small amount
🦪 Oyster sauce: 2 tbsp
🫗 Light soy sauce: 2 tbsp
🍊 Dried tangerine peel: a small amount (key for removing fishy smell and enhancing aroma)
🌶️ White pepper: a small amount
🌾 Cornstarch: a small amount (to lock in moisture)
🐷 Lard: half a tbsp
🫒 Cooking oil: appropriate amount
🍶 Rice wine: appropriate amount

## Kitchenware & Equipment
♨️ Clay pot
🥣 Large bowl
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Cooking Steps
1.  **Prepare the eel:** Rub the surface of the eel with salt and cornstarch to remove the slimy coating. Slit the belly open and clean out the internal organs. Rinse thoroughly and cut into pieces about 6cm long. **Be sure to pat the surface completely dry with kitchen paper towels.**
2.  **Make the marinade:** In a large bowl, combine the peanut butter, broad bean paste, chu hou paste, and hoisin sauce. Add salt, sugar, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dried tangerine peel, white pepper, and cornstarch. Mix until well combined. Finally, add half a tablespoon of lard and mix thoroughly.
3.  **Marinate the eel:** Add the dried eel pieces to the marinade bowl. Mix well, ensuring every piece of eel is coated with the sauce.
4.  Heat the clay pot over high heat. When it feels noticeably hot to your hand held a few inches above the pot, add an appropriate amount of oil. Add the ginger pieces and garlic cloves, stir-frying until they release their fragrance and their surfaces turn a golden-brown color.
5.  Pour the marinated eel, along with all the sauce, into the clay pot. Stir-fry quickly to mix well, then spread the eel in an even layer. Cook the eel by using the high heat and steam inside the covered pot. **Typically, cook covered over high heat for about 2 minutes.** The exact time depends on the size of the eel pieces and your stove's heat level.
6.  Cover the clay pot with its lid. Pour a ring of rice wine along the edge of the lid. This will create a sizzling "zhe zhe" sound and flames.
7.  Open the lid. Add the scallion sections and red bell pepper chunks. Give everything a few quick stirs. Finally, sprinkle with cilantro, then turn off the heat and serve immediately from the pot.

## Health Advice
Eel is rich in high-quality protein and DHA, making it ideal for replenishing energy in the summer. However, its calorie content is not low, and the combination of various sauces results in a relatively high sodium content. Those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
