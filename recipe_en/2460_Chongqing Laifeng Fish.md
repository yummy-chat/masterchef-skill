# Chongqing Laifeng Fish
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Year Round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 1 Silver Carp (can be substituted with other fish)  
🌶️ Pickled Chili Peppers, to taste (mix red and green, finely chopped)  
🫚 Pickled Ginger, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (minced)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (some cut into sections, some into short pieces)  
🌿 Green Sichuan Peppercorns, a pinch  
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers, to taste (for making knife-cut chilies, frying base, and oil splashing)  
🧅 Chopped Scallions, to taste (for garnishing and fragrance)  
🧄 Minced Garlic, to taste (for garnishing and fragrance)  
🌾 Sweet Potato Starch, a small amount (for thickening)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌶️ White Pepper, to taste  
🍺 Beer, to taste  
🫘 Broad Bean Paste (Doubanjiang), to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🧂 Chicken Bouillon Powder, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🧂 Soy Sauce, to taste  
🍶 Vinegar, to taste  
🧂 Ground Sichuan Pepper, to taste  
🫒 Rapeseed Oil, to taste  
🧈 Lard, to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's Knife  
🪵 Cutting Board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wok Spatula  
🥄 Strainer/Skimmer  
🥣 Seasoning Bowls  
🍽️ Serving Plate  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Preparing the Fish**
   Select 1 silver carp, clean and gut it. Remove the two belly fillets, chop the backbone and head into pieces, and cut the belly fillets into finger-width strips.
   **Note**: Do not slice into fillets; use a hand-pulling technique with the knife for smoother cutting. If the fishmonger does the cutting, clearly specify the cutting method.

2. **Marinating and Cleaning the Fish**
   Place the fish pieces in a bowl. Add crushed ginger, crushed scallion sections, generous salt, white pepper, and beer. Mix well and marinate for about 10 minutes. After marinating, rinse thoroughly with plenty of water to remove the salt. Drain and discard the ginger and scallions.
   **Note**: Beer is more effective than cooking wine for removing fishy odor; rinsing makes the fish meat whiter and more tender.

3. **Preparing the Aromatics**
   - Finely chop the pickled chili peppers (both red and green) and place in a separate bowl.
   - Finely chop the pickled ginger and garlic, and place them together in another bowl.
   - Cut scallions into short sections and place in a separate bowl.
   - Fry a small amount of dried chili peppers and green Sichuan peppercorns in a little oil until dark red. Remove and press them with a knife to create "knife-cut chilies."
   **Note**: Using both red and green pickled chilies creates a richer flavor; the knife-cut chilies must be fried to change color before chopping to avoid bitterness. This is the soul seasoning of the dish.

4. **Frying the Base**
   Heat a mix of rapeseed and lard in a wok. Once hot, fry the green Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant. Add the pickled chilies and stir-fry. Then, add the pickled ginger and garlic, stir-frying over low heat until fragrant. Add the broad bean paste and fry until it releases its red oil and becomes fragrant. Toss in a few dried chili peppers and stir-fry briefly. Finally, add the knife-cut chilies and stir-fry briefly.
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning; a mix of oils is more aromatic than a single oil; knife-cut chilies are already cooked, so only brief stir-frying is needed.

5. **Preparing the Broth**
   After stir-frying evenly, add water, then add the scallion sections. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon powder, sugar, white pepper, soy sauce, and vinegar. Adjust the taste.
   **Note**: The water ratio must be precise to avoid a broth that is too thin or too thick; ensure even seasoning.

6. **Cooking the Fish Pieces**
   Once seasoned, add the fish pieces. First, boil over high heat until the fish skin tightens, then reduce to low heat and cook until flavored, about 4 minutes, just until the fish is cooked through.
   **Note**: "Soft cooking" is not exclusively low heat; start with high heat, then finish with low heat to prevent the fish from breaking apart. The fish should be just cooked through and remain intact.

7. **Thickening the Sauce**
   Once the fish is cooked, gradually add a slurry made from sweet potato starch and water in small batches, stirring until the liquid in the wok bubbles and the fish surface becomes glossy.
   **Note**: Add the starch slurry in small batches to prevent lumps.

8. **Plating and Splashing Oil**
   Pour the cooked fish and sauce onto a serving plate. Sprinkle generously with chopped scallions, minced garlic, and ground Sichuan pepper. Heat oil in a separate wok, fry the dried chili peppers until fragrant. When the oil is hot and the chili peppers are about to change color, add half a spoonful of vinegar and immediately pour the hot oil evenly over the fish.
   **Note**: Adding vinegar during the oil splash instantly releases a rich aroma; ensure the oil covers the fish surface evenly.

## Health Advice  
This Chongqing Laifeng Fish is rich in high-quality protein and a small amount of unsaturated fatty acids. The inclusion of pickled chilies and Sichuan peppercorns can stimulate appetite and blood circulation, making it suitable for times of high physical exertion or cold weather. However, due to its heavy oil and salt content, those with high blood lipids or sensitive stomachs should control their portions to avoid excessive intake.
