# Rich White Lamb Soup
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦴 Lamb Bones 3 jin (need to be cleaned, soaked, and blanched)
🧈 Lamb Fat 1 jin (need to be cleaned and soaked, boiled together with bone broth)
🥩 Lamb Meat 2 jin (need to be cleaned and soaked, added later to the soup for stewing)
🥬 White Radish appropriate amount (sliced, cooked as bottom garnish in bowl)
🍲 Vermicelli appropriate amount (as bottom garnish in bowl)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for seasoning upon serving)
🌿 Ground White Pepper appropriate amount (for seasoning upon serving)
🌱 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped, sprinkled upon serving)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (for garnish upon serving)

**Spice Pouch**
🌶️ White Peppercorns 4 grams
🌿 Angelica Dahurica 2 grams
🫚 Dried Ginger 4 grams
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorns 2 grams
(Place the above ingredients in a spice pouch, wash, then add to the pot)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass Bowl (for soaking and cleaning ingredients)
🍲 Large Soup Pot (for blanching and making broth)
🥄 Slotted Spoon (for skimming foam)
🔪 Knife, Cutting Board (for slicing radish and lamb)
🍜 Ceramic Bowl (for serving the finished dish)
🥄 Soup Ladle (for serving and pouring soup)

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the lamb bones, lamb meat, and lamb fat separately in lightly salted water to draw out blood.
2. Place the lamb bones in a pot with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Skim off the foam and blanch for about 5–6 minutes, then remove.
**Note**: Use ample water for blanching; a gentle simmer helps fully extract impurities from the bones.
3. Rinse the blanched lamb bones in warm water 2–3 times to thoroughly clean off any blood residue and impurities on the surface.
4. In a fresh pot, bring a sufficient amount of boiling water to a rapid boil. Add the lamb bones, keeping the heat on high throughout.
**Key Point**: Adding bones to boiling water and maintaining high heat is essential for achieving a rich white broth; do not reduce the heat midway.
5. At the same time, add the cleaned whole lamb fat. Boil for about 40 minutes, until the lamb fat is completely melted and the broth becomes rich and fragrant.
6. Once the broth has turned a rich white color, reduce to low heat. Add the washed spice pouch and the soaked lamb meat. Simmer gently over low heat, keeping the pot uncovered the entire time.
**Key Point**: Simmering over low heat without a lid helps release gamey odors; stew until the lamb is fully cooked and tender.
7. Line the bottom of a bowl with cooked white radish slices and vermicelli. Remove the cooked lamb, slice it, and arrange it on top.
8. Ladle the piping hot soup over the meat slices repeatedly. Add salt and ground white pepper, and sprinkle with chopped scallions.
9. Ladle more boiling hot soup into the bowl, ensuring a generous layer of oil on the surface. Finally, garnish with cilantro and serve.

## Health Advice
Lamb soup is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. Drinking it in winter can help dispel cold and warm the body. Lamb fat is high in calories, so people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should consume it in moderation. White radish and vermicelli add dietary fiber, aiding digestion.
