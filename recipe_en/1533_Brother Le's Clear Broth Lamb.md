# Brother Le's Clear Broth Lamb
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐑 Black-skinned lamb belly from Lianshan, about 22 lbs of whole lamb, use a portion (must be scorched with a blowtorch and scrubbed clean)
🦴 Lamb bones, appropriate amount (blanched in advance)
🎋 Sugarcane, 3-4 segments (cracked and cut into sections)
🌰 Water chestnuts, 6-8 pieces (with skin on, cut off ends and washed clean)
🥕 White radish, 1 piece (peeled and cut into chunks)
🫚 Old ginger, 3-4 slices (smashed flat)
🍈 Dried longan, a small handful
🌿 Angelica root, a small amount
🫘 Amomum villosum, about 10 pieces
🌶 Peppercorns, appropriate amount
🍊 Dried tangerine peel, 2-3 small pieces
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for final seasoning)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Blowtorch
🪣 Large stockpot
♨️ Portable gas stove
🔗 Tongs
🥄 Soup ladle
🔪 Chef's knife
🍽 Plate

## Cooking Steps
1. Use a blowtorch to scorch the lamb skin until golden brown, then scrape it clean and rinse thoroughly with water.
**Note**: Black-skinned lamb must be used; scorch the skin evenly and scrub thoroughly to remove any gamey or strong smell.

2. Place the lamb bones in cold water, heat to blanch, then remove and rinse clean, set aside.
3. Crack the sugarcane and cut into segments; cut off the ends of the water chestnuts and wash with skin on; smash the old ginger flat; peel and cut the white radish into chunks.
4. Fill the stockpot with enough water and bring to a rolling boil over high heat.
**Key**: Ingredients must be added only after the water boils to prevent them from sticking to the bottom.

5. Add the smashed ginger slices, blanched lamb bones, dried tangerine peel, water chestnuts, sugarcane segments, amomum villosum, dried longan, processed lamb chunks, angelica root, and peppercorns in sequence. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat.
6. Simmer over medium heat for about 1 hour, until the broth starts to turn milky white and the lamb is initially infused with flavor.
7. Add the white radish chunks and continue simmering over medium heat for about half an hour, until the radish is tender, the broth is milky white, and the lamb can be easily pierced with chopsticks.
**Note**: Do not add the radish too early, or it will become mushy and lose its sweet, crisp texture.

8. Before turning off the heat, add salt to taste; salt must be added at the very end.
9. Remove the lamb, let it cool slightly, then chop into small pieces and plate. It can be served with a dipping sauce made from fermented bean curd and shredded lemon leaves.
10. Ladle out the lamb broth, serve with the cooked radish, and enjoy.

## Health Tips
This clear broth lamb uses sugarcane and water chestnuts to balance the warming nature of the lamb, while the white radish adds sweetness and cuts the richness. It is suitable for warming the body and nourishing qi in winter, but those with high uric acid should drink the broth sparingly. Note: Scorching the lamb skin to remove the gamey smell is essential, otherwise, residual mutton odor may persist.
