# Fujian Seafood Stir-Fried Noodles
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine (Min Cai)
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍜 Noodles appropriate amount (alkaline noodles or egg noodles, cook until 70-80% done and drain)
🐚 Clams appropriate amount (some used for broth, some added at the end)
🦑 Squid appropriate amount (cut into rings or scored)
🦐 Shrimp appropriate amount (shelled or with shells on)
🦪 Oysters appropriate amount
🥩 Pork appropriate amount (thinly sliced)
🥚 Eggs 1-2 (beaten and set aside)
🧅 Onion 1/4 (julienned)
🫚 Ginger a little (julienned)
🥬 Baby cabbage appropriate amount (julienned)
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (julienned, possibly red carrot)
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms appropriate amount (soaked until soft, then julienned)
🌿 Celery appropriate amount (cut into small sections)

🧂 Chicken bouillon powder 1 tbsp
🌶 White pepper powder 1/2 tbsp
🥄 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1 tbsp
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount
🧅 Fried shallot oil appropriate amount (drizzled in just before serving)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥘 Pot
🥄 Spoon and wooden spatula
🥣 Bowls (for beating eggs, mixing sauce, and holding clam broth)
🥢 Strainer or slotted spoon

## Cooking Steps
1.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook until they turn slightly white and translucent, then immediately drain and set aside, keeping them dry and springy.
2.  In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the clams and cook until they open. Remove the clam meat and set aside. Strain the clam cooking liquid to use as broth.
3.  Slice the pork into thin pieces. Beat the eggs and set aside. Julienne or cut the onion, ginger, baby cabbage, carrot, shiitake mushrooms, and celery into strips or sections. Clean the squid, shrimp, and oysters and set aside.
4.  Mix the chicken bouillon powder, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce together in a bowl to form the sauce. Set aside.
5.  Heat the wok over high heat, add cold oil, then add the sliced pork and stir-fry until it changes color and releases its aroma. Pour in the beaten eggs and quickly scramble them until cooked.
6.  Immediately add the raw squid and shrimp. Stir-fry over high heat quickly to preserve their freshness, avoiding pre-blanching.
7.  Add the julienned onion and ginger, and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the julienned baby cabbage and carrot, and stir-fry until slightly softened. Add the julienned shiitake mushrooms and stir-fry everything together.
8.  Pour in the strained clam broth. Cover and simmer over medium heat briefly, allowing the vegetables to absorb the liquid.
9.  Add the drained noodles, the oysters, and the cooked clam meat. Pour the prepared sauce over everything.
10. Turn the heat to high and stir-fry quickly until the sauce is absorbed, the noodles are evenly colored and flavorful. Add the celery sections.
11. Just before turning off the heat, drizzle in the fried shallot oil. Toss everything together evenly, then transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
This seafood stir-fried noodle dish is rich in high-quality protein and various vitamins, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, be mindful of the amount of noodles and oil used to avoid excessive calories. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or a vegetable salad for a balanced meal.
