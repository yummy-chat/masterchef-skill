# Irritant Oil Cold Mixed Vegetables
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 120g (boiled before use)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 30g (needs pretreatment with liquor)
🫒 Rapeseed oil 500g
🧅 Onion 50g (cut into rings)
🥬 Scallion 30g (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced)
🥬 Celery 20g (cut into sections)
🌿 Cilantro 20g (cut into sections)
🌰 Sesame seeds, as needed
🥗 Cold mixed vegetable ingredients (lotus root slices, green beans, tofu shreds, etc.), as needed (blanched and drained in advance)
🍶 Liquor 1 bottle cap
🧂 Dipping sauce, as needed
🧄 Ginger and garlic juice, as needed
🌶️ Chili oil 2 tablespoons

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Wooden spatula
🪵 Wooden bowl lid

## Preparation Steps
1. Place dried chili peppers into a pot of boiling water and cook until fully done, then remove and drain.
**Note**: Boiling prevents the peppers from burning during subsequent frying and reduces spiciness; cook until the peppers become soft in texture.

2. Rinse Sichuan peppercorns with clean water to remove impurities, then add 1 bottle cap of liquor and mix well.
**Note**: Liquor helps promote the release of numbing aroma from the peppercorns when heated.

3. Cut onion into rings, scallion into sections, ginger into slices, celery into sections, and cilantro into sections; set aside as aromatics.
**Note**: Using a variety of aromatics thoroughly enhances the aroma of the flavored oil and removes any unpleasant odors.

4. Blanch cold mixed vegetable ingredients such as lotus root slices, green beans, and tofu shreds separately until cooked, then drain; wash cilantro leaves and set aside.
5. Pour 500g of rapeseed oil into a wok, add onion, scallion, ginger, celery, and cilantro to the cold oil, and fry slowly over low heat until the aromatics turn dry and yellow, and the aroma is fully released, then remove the aromatics.
**Note**: Adding ingredients to cold oil and frying slowly over low heat fully extracts the aroma and removes the rawness of the rapeseed oil.

6. Maintain the oil temperature, add the drained boiled chili peppers, and fry slowly over low heat until they become dry, crispy, and slightly charred in color.
**Note**: Maintain low heat throughout, constantly monitor the color to avoid burning; aim for a charred aroma without bitterness.

7. Add an appropriate amount of sesame seeds and mix well, then quickly remove from heat and pour the hot oil and chili peppers together into a bowl containing the Sichuan peppercorns.
**Note**: Pour over the Sichuan peppercorns after removing from heat, using residual heat to release the numbing aroma, avoiding high temperatures that could cause the numbing aroma to evaporate.

8. Stir the ingredients in the bowl, quickly cover, and allow to cool naturally.
**Note**: Covering and steeping allows the flavors of the irritant oil to fully meld while also releasing any residual flavors and alcohol smell.

9. Place the blanched lotus root slices, green beans, and tofu shreds into a container, add 1 tablespoon of dipping sauce, 1 tablespoon of ginger and garlic juice, 2 tablespoons of chili oil, and 2 tablespoons of irritant oil, mix well, and plate.
**Note**: Irritant oil is key to enhancing the aroma; mix thoroughly to ensure the ingredients are well-coated.

## Health Advice
This dish uses frying aromatics to remove unwanted flavors and enhance aroma, but it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light staple foods. Suitable for those who enjoy spicy and numbing flavors, but those with sensitive digestive systems should control their intake.
