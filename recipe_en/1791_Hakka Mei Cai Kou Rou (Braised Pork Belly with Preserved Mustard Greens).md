# Hakka Mei Cai Kou Rou (Braised Pork Belly with Preserved Mustard Greens)
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly with skin, about 2–3 large pieces
🥬 Dried preserved mustard greens (preserved vegetable), appropriate amount (soak in boiling water for 40 minutes in advance, squeeze dry and chop finely)
🥦 Broccoli, appropriate amount (blanched for plating)
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (for boiling pork to remove gamey flavor)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for boiling pork to remove gamey flavor)
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount (for boiling pork to remove gamey flavor)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for coloring and stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🍯 Char siu sauce, about 1 tablespoon (for coloring pork skin and stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount (for stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount (for seasoning preserved mustard greens)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (for stir-frying preserved mustard greens and blanching broccoli)
🍬 White sugar, a pinch (to enhance flavor when stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, a few (cut into segments, for stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
⭐ Star anise, 1–2 pieces (for stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (minced, for stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for frying pork, stir-frying preserved mustard greens, and blanching broccoli)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok/deep pot (for boiling pork, frying pork, and stir-frying preserved mustard greens)
🥘 Stockpot (for blanching broccoli)
🥢 Slotted spoon (for removing ingredients and broccoli)
🪥 Brush (for brushing sauce onto pork skin)
🪥 Toothpicks (for poking holes in pork skin)
🔪 Knife and cutting board (for cutting meat and vegetables)
🥣 Deep bowl (for arranging and steaming)
🍽️ Plate (to cover the bowl during steaming and for final plating)
♨️ Steamer (for steaming for 90 minutes)

## Instructions
### Preparation
1. **Boil the pork to set shape**: Place the pork belly pieces in cold water in a pot. Add scallions, ginger, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then cover and reduce to medium heat. Cook for about 15 minutes until the meat is about 70–80% done. Remove and set aside.
2. **Prepare the coloring sauce**: In a bowl, mix dark soy sauce and char siu sauce, stirring well.
3. **Treat the pork skin**: Use toothpicks to poke dense holes into the pork skin. Then brush the prepared sauce evenly over the skin.
4. **Fry the pork skin**: Pour enough oil into a wok and heat until it just begins to smoke. Place the pork pieces skin-side down into the oil. **Immediately cover with a lid** to prevent splattering. Fry for about 3–5 minutes until the skin turns golden and blistered. Turn off the heat and wait for the pot to cool before removing the lid.
5. **Soak to create crackling texture**: Immediately transfer the fried pork pieces into cold water and soak for at least 20 minutes, until the skin develops a clear wrinkled pattern.
6. **Slice the meat**: Trim the edges of the soaked pork, then cut into slices about 0.5 cm thick.
7. **Prepare preserved mustard greens and broccoli**: Soak the dried preserved mustard greens in boiling water for 40 minutes. Rinse, squeeze dry, and chop finely. Blanch the broccoli until just cooked, then drain and set aside.
### Stir-frying and Steaming
1. **Aromatics and stir-fry preserved mustard greens**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add minced garlic, dried chili segments, and star anise, and stir-fry until fragrant. Add the trimmed pork scraps and stir-fry until they release oil. Then add the chopped preserved mustard greens and stir-fry over medium heat until dry and crackling sounds are heard.
2. **Season the preserved mustard greens**: Add oyster sauce, the remaining dark soy sauce and char siu sauce mixture, light soy sauce, salt, and a pinch of sugar. Stir well. **The seasoning should be slightly saltier than usual**, as the saltiness will mellow during steaming.
3. **Infuse the pork slices**: Add the sliced pork into the preserved mustard greens and pour in a small amount of hot water. Gently cook for 2 minutes to allow the meat to absorb the flavors, while keeping the slices intact.
4. **Arrange in a steaming bowl**: Arrange the pork slices skin-side down neatly in a deep bowl. Then pour the preserved mustard greens and their liquid over the top, spreading evenly and pressing down firmly.
5. **Steam**: **Cover the bowl with a plate** to prevent condensation from dripping in. Place in a steamer, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and steam for 90 minutes until the meat is tender and soft.
6. **Invert and plate**: Once steamed, remove the bowl. Place a plate upside down over the bowl, then quickly flip it over so the meat is perfectly inverted onto the plate. Garnish with blanched broccoli around the edges.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in protein and iron, but pork belly is high in fat. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables. Those with cardiovascular conditions should consume in moderation. The preserved mustard greens become savory and help cut through the richness after long steaming, but individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of reducing salt intake.
