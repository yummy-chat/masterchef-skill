# Ultimate Pork Rib Rice Cake
Cuisine: Shanghai Benbang Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 1 pork spare rib (meat removed from bone, firm texture)
🍚 Appropriate amount of rice cake (cut into long strips)
🧅 Appropriate amount of ginger (sliced, for blanching)
🍂 Appropriate amount of star anise
🪵 Appropriate amount of cinnamon
🌿 Appropriate amount of bay leaves
🍒 A few preserved plums (to enhance flavor layers)
🥤 1 can of cola (about 330ml, used as braising liquid)
🧂 Appropriate amount of light soy sauce
🧂 Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce (for coloring)
🍶 Appropriate amount of cooking wine (for deodorizing)
🫒 A little cooking oil (for pan-frying)
🧊 Appropriate amount of ice cubes (to soak the ribs after blanching, making the meat springy and tender)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
♨️ Wok/deep pot
🥢 Slotted spoon
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🧊 Ice cubes (made from refrigerator ice)

## Cooking Steps
1. Wash the whole spare rib thoroughly. Place in cold water, add ginger slices and cooking wine, and blanch to remove scum.
**Note**: The ribs must be rinsed repeatedly until the water runs clear to ensure deodorization.
2. Remove the blanched ribs and immediately soak in ice water to tighten the meat, making it easier to debone and giving it a more elastic and chewy texture.
3. While still warm, remove the bone from the rib, keeping the whole slab of pork flat and set aside.
4. Insert the rice cake strips into the center of the pork rib meat, shaping it into a "rib-wrapped rice cake" form. Gently press to secure.
5. Add a little oil to the pot. Place the assembled rib rice cake in the pot and pan-fry over medium-low heat until both sides are lightly golden and set.
6. Add star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, and preserved plums. Pour in the cola, then add light soy sauce and dark soy sauce for seasoning and coloring.
7. Cover the pot, reduce heat to low, and simmer slowly until the ribs are tender and the rice cake has fully absorbed the broth.
8. Remove the lid, increase heat to high to reduce the sauce. Once the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients, remove from heat.
**Note**: Rice cake tends to stick to the bottom of the pot when cooked for a long time. Stir carefully during the sauce reduction to prevent burning.

## Health Advice
Pork rib rice cake is slowly braised with cola and preserved plums, skillfully blending sweet and savory flavors. It is rich in collagen and can be consumed occasionally. Rice cake is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables and control portion sizes.
