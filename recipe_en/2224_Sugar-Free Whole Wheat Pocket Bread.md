# Sugar-Free Whole Wheat Pocket Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Whole Wheat Flour 100g
🍞 Bread Flour 200g
💧 Boiling Water 120g (for scalding whole wheat flour)
💧 Water 100g (for main dough)
🧂 Salt 2g
🍚 Active Dry Yeast 3g
🫒 Olive Oil 6g (or other vegetable oil)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Digital Scale
🥣 Glass Bowl
🍴 Silicone Spatula
🫙 Plastic Wrap
🔪 Dough Scraper
🥖 Rolling Pin
🍳 Baking Sheet
🔥 Electric Oven (with independent upper and lower temperature control)

## Instructions
1. **Make Whole Wheat Scald Dough**
   - Place whole wheat flour in a bowl, pour in boiling water, and stir quickly until no dry flour remains.
     **Key Point**: Scalding the flour with boiling water softens the wheat bran, ensuring a tender bread.
   - Cover the scald dough with plastic wrap and set aside to cool.

2. **Mix Dry Ingredients for Main Dough**
   - Combine bread flour and salt on the work surface, then make a well in the center to form a "flour wall."

3. **Combine Liquid and Yeast**
   - Pour water into the center of the "flour wall," add active dry yeast, and gently stir to dissolve.

4. **Add Scald Dough and Oil**
   - Tear the cooled scald dough into small pieces and add them to the water, then add olive oil.
     **Key Point**: Adding scald dough and oil is essential to keep the bread soft and intact the next day.

5. **Knead the Dough**
   - First, mix the liquid, scald dough, and oil in the center, then gradually incorporate the surrounding flour.
   - Use the "spread and gather" technique: spread the sticky dough on the work surface with your palm, then use a scraper to gather it back into a ball. Repeat 6-10 times.
     **How to Check**: Knead until the dough surface is smooth and no loose flour pieces are visible.

6. **First Rest**
   - Shape the dough into a ball, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
     **Note**: If room temperature is below 20°C, extend to 60 minutes; if too high, reduce yeast amount slightly.

7. **Fold and Divide**
   - After resting, flatten the dough and fold it twice.
   - Flatten the folded dough and use a scraper to divide it into 6 equal pieces, about 80g each.

8. **Shape into Balls and Second Rest**
   - Roll each piece into a ball, cover with a damp cloth, and rest for another 30 minutes.
     **Key Point**: This rest must be sufficient; otherwise, the dough will shrink excessively when rolling.

9. **Shape**
   - Take a rested dough piece and roll it into a thin, elongated oval (tongue shape) with a rolling pin.
     **Key Points**:
     - Roll as thin as possible.
     - Ensure even thickness across the middle and edges, or the bread won't puff evenly.
     - If the dough shrinks noticeably, it means it hasn't rested enough; stop and let it rest further.

10. **Place on Sheet and Final Proof**
    - Transfer the rolled dough to a baking sheet, gently stretch it into a longer shape, and deliberately press down the edges with your finger.
      **Note**: If the baking sheet is small, cover any unshaped dough to prevent drying; bake immediately after shaping without additional proofing.

11. **Bake**
    - Preheat the oven to 200°C top heat and 230°C bottom heat.
    - Place the baking sheet on the middle rack and bake for about 6 minutes.
      **How to Check**: Around the 4th minute of baking, the pocket bread should fully puff up.
      **Key Point**: High-temperature baking is essential for rapid expansion and cavity formation.

## Health Tips
The whole wheat scald dough ensures a soft crust. This recipe is sugar-free and suitable for those who need to control sugar intake as a staple food. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables and protein. Do not bake for more than 6 minutes to prevent the bread from becoming hard.
