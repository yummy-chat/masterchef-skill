# Deep-Fried Crucian Carp
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients  
🐟 Small crucian carp 8-10 pieces (cleaned, with cross-hatch cuts on both sides, belly left intact)  
🧅 Scallion about 2 stalks (use the white part, cut into diagonally sliced horse-ear shapes)  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (thinly sliced)  
🌾 Flour as needed (for coating the fish, all-purpose flour)  

## Seasonings  
🧂 Salt 2g (add to scallion and ginger, rub to extract juice)  
🧂 MSG a pinch  
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder / five-spice powder / thirteen-spice powder as needed (choose one or a combination)  
🥢 Light soy sauce as needed  
🥢 Regular soy sauce as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Glass bowl or basin  
🍳 Stainless steel wok  
🥄 Large slotted spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Steps  
1. Cut the white part of the scallion diagonally into horse-ear slices, thinly slice the ginger, place them in a bowl, add salt and MSG, rub firmly with your hands to extract the scallion-ginger juice.  
2. Make cross-hatch cuts on both sides of the crucian carp, keeping the belly intact without any cuts.  
3. Place the prepared crucian carp into the bowl with the scallion-ginger juice, add Sichuan peppercorn powder, light soy sauce, and regular soy sauce. Gently mix and rub evenly, then marinate for 15-20 minutes.  
4. Remove the marinated crucian carp and place it in flour, coating the inside and outside of the fish evenly with a thin layer of dry flour. Shake off any excess flour.  
5. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat it. When a few drops of water flicked in with chopsticks sizzle and quickly float to the surface, add the crucian carp one by one.  
**Note**: Do not stir after adding; let them fry for about 2 minutes to set.  
6. Once the fish are set, gently push them with a slotted spoon and continue frying for about 3 minutes until the fish turn golden brown, become firm, and produce a crisp sound when tapped.  
7. Remove and drain the oil thoroughly, then plate. For a crispier texture, deep-fry a second time.  

## Health Tips  
This deep-fried crucian carp is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for weight loss phases or for those needing calcium supplementation. Be mindful of the bones when eating; it is recommended that the elderly and children choose larger crucian carp or chew slowly and carefully.
