# Bean Paste Flower Decorated Cream Puffs
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Lima beans/white kidney beans 630g (pre-soaked)  
🥐 Frozen cream puff batter as needed (divided into small portions)  
🥮 Frozen puff pastry sheets as needed  
🌿 Fresh mint leaves as needed  
🍬 Powdered sugar 150g (added in batches)  
🍯 Corn syrup 95g  
🧂 Salt 3.7g  
🧈 White butter or light cream as needed (to adjust bean paste consistency)  
⚪ White food coloring as needed  
🎨 Food coloring (red, yellow, blue, etc.) as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
⚖️ Digital scale  
🥣 Glass bowl/plastic basin  
🍲 Cooking pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon/soup ladle  
🔪 Immersion blender  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🪄 Silicone spatula  
💼 Piping bag  
🌸 Piping tip  
📌 Piping nail (DIY base can be made with an electric drill)  
🔥 Oven  
🧈 Baking sheet  
📜 Silicone mat  
🔧 Electric drill (for DIY drilling)  
✂️ Kitchen scissors  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions
1.  Soak the beans in clean water overnight in advance until fully expanded, then wash and drain. Set aside.
2.  Remove the skin from each soaked bean by pinching, place them in a pot with enough clean water, bring to a boil, and skim off the foam. Change the water midway to remove the beany smell, and simmer over medium-low heat for about 80 minutes until the beans are soft and mushy, resembling porridge.
3.  Pour the cooked beans along with a small amount of the cooking liquid into a container, and blend with an immersion blender until a very smooth bean paste forms.
    **Note**: The smoother the bean paste, the easier it will be to pipe.
4.  Pour the bean paste into a non-stick pan. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring continuously without stopping. Cook and stir for about 40+ minutes until the moisture evaporates, the paste thickens and clumps together, and no longer sticks to the spatula.
    **Key**: When adding powdered sugar, do so off the heat. First, add the corn syrup and salt, then add the powdered sugar in batches, stirring thoroughly using arm strength.
5.  Strain the finished bean paste through a sieve and let it cool. Add an appropriate amount of white butter or light cream to adjust the consistency. Add white food coloring to achieve a white base color, and finally, adjust to different colors as needed.
6.  Place the colored bean paste into a piping bag. Choose a suitable piping tip and pipe flower shapes onto a piping nail. Decorate with fine stamens.
7.  Divide the frozen cream puff batter into evenly sized small balls. Place them on a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat. Cover each ball of batter with a piece of puff pastry. Transfer to a preheated oven and bake at the appropriate temperature for about 40 minutes until the cream puffs are fully puffed, the pastry is set, and the surface is golden brown.
    **Note**: Immediately after baking, turn off the oven, crack the oven door open slightly, and let the puffs rest for about half an hour. This effectively prevents them from collapsing.
8.  Whip the hojicha ganache cream and vanilla diplomat cream separately, and prepare the peach jam in advance.
9.  Once the cream puffs have cooled, poke a hole in the bottom of each with chopsticks. Pipe in the hojicha cream first, then the peach jam, and finally seal the top with vanilla cream.
10. Pipe a ring of heavy cream onto the top of each cream puff as a base. Carefully use scissors to transfer the bean paste flowers onto the cream. Arrange them to form a composition, and finally, garnish with mint leaves.
    **Recommendation**: Serve chilled for a colder, smoother layered texture.

## Health Advice
This dessert features fine bean paste and crispy cream puffs, but due to the large amount of powdered sugar and corn syrup added, it can cause a rapid spike in blood sugar. It is recommended to be enjoyed only occasionally as a party dessert, not as a daily snack. For those watching their weight or managing blood sugar levels, it is advisable to pair it with unsweetened tea and reduce the serving size per sitting.
