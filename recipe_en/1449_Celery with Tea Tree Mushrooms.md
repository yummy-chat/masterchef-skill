# Celery with Tea Tree Mushrooms
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

---
🥬 **Ingredients**
- 1 jar of Minxing Tea Tree Mushrooms
- 250-300g celery (recommend 30cm tall celery seedlings; if using mountain celery or regular celery, blanch until just cooked)

🧂 **Seasonings**
- 5ml sesame oil
- About 7ml oyster sauce
- 3ml premium soy sauce
- About 2g salt

🍳 **Utensils**
- Hot water kettle (for blanching regular celery)
- Pot
- Bowl
- Knife

---

**Instructions**
1. Prepare the celery: Remove any yellow leaves from fresh celery seedlings or regular celery, and wash clean. If using regular celery, set aside the celery leaves separately, and cut the celery stalks lengthwise into 2–3mm wide strips; if using celery seedlings, directly cut into 4cm long sections.
2. If using regular celery: Boil a kettle of water and bring it to a boil in a pot. Blanch the celery strips in the boiling water for about 20 seconds, then add the celery leaves and blanch for another 5 seconds. Remove immediately and rinse under cold water, then drain well. If using celery seedlings, skip the blanching step.
   **Key point**: The water level during blanching must cover the celery; if the celery leaves are too old, discard them.
3. Place the blanched celery (or celery seedlings) into a bowl. Add salt, sesame oil, oyster sauce, and premium soy sauce in order, and mix well.
4. Pour the tea tree mushrooms into the bowl and mix thoroughly with the celery.
5. Plate and serve.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and mushroom polysaccharides, making it suitable for weight loss and blood sugar control periods; blanched celery is crisp and tender, easier to digest. Note that oyster sauce contains sodium, so people with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of salt used.
