# Cola Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potato, about 1 (medium-sized, cut into bite-sized chunks)
🥤 Cola, 330 ml (1 can)
🧂 Salt, about 2 teaspoons (for blanching) + 1 pinch (for stewing)
🍾 Light soy sauce, to taste
🫚 Ginger, 2–3 slices (for blanching)
🧅 Scallions, about 2 stalks (for blanching) + some chopped scallions (for garnish)
🌶️ Red chili pepper rings, to taste (for garnish)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🍲 Pot for boiling
🥣 Soup bowl (to reserve the blanching liquid)
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon, ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the potato and cut it into evenly sized bite-sized chunks.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add the potato chunks, 2 teaspoons of salt, ginger slices, and scallions. Once boiling again, cook for about 3 minutes. After cooking, scoop out 2–3 ladles of the blanching liquid and set aside. Remove the potatoes and drain well.
   **Note**: Reserving some blanching liquid helps blend flavors during the subsequent stewing. The ginger and scallions used for blanching do not need to be removed.
3. Heat oil in a wok or frying pan. Add the potato chunks and stir-fry over medium heat until a slightly charred crust forms on the surface.
   **Key Point**: Creating a charred crust adds a fragrant, smoky flavor and improves the texture of the finished dish.
4. Pour the cola into the pan, then add the reserved blanching liquid. Sprinkle in a pinch of salt and season with light soy sauce to taste.
5. Bring the liquid to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer until the sauce gradually thickens and evenly coats the potato chunks.
   **Key Point**: Reducing the sauce is crucial; a thick, rich sauce is the key to the flavor.
6. Just before serving, sprinkle red chili pepper rings and chopped scallions over the top for garnish. Plate and serve.

## Health Advice
The sugar content in cola potatoes mainly comes from the cola. It is recommended to enjoy this dish occasionally as a side dish for a treat, rather than frequently. It is suitable for replenishing carbohydrates after exercise or as a snack-like dish for picky children. Diabetic patients should be cautious with portion sizes.
