# Shaanxi-Style Braised Pork in a Bun (Rou Jia Mo)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 1000g pork front leg (with alternating lean and fatty portions, including skin and sinew)  
🌾 600g all-purpose flour  
💧 Water (for soaking meat) – enough to submerge the pork  
💧 2000g water (for braising meat)  
💧 280g water (for kneading dough)  
💧 200g water (for caramelizing sugar)  
🧅 30g scallions  
🧄 30g ginger  
🫒 15g vegetable oil  
🐖 15g lard  
🍞 4g yeast  
🧂 1g baking soda  

### Seasonings  
🍬 50g rock sugar or white sugar (for caramelizing sugar)  
🧂 30g light soy sauce  
🧂 20g dark soy sauce  
🍶 20g cooking wine  
🍬 20g rock sugar (for braising meat)  
🧂 20g salt  
🌿 3g cinnamon  
🌿 3g cardamom (seeds removed)  
🍊 3g dried tangerine peel  
🌿 2g Sichuan peppercorns  
🌸 3 cloves  
🌿 2g galangal  
🌿 3 star anise  
🌿 2g amomum villosum  
🌿 2g long pepper  
🌿 3g fennel seeds  
🍃 2 bay leaves  
🌶️ 3g dried chili peppers  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Large pot (e.g., cast iron pot)  
🧽 Cheesecloth bag (spice bag)  
🟫 Silicone kneading mat  
🥢 Rolling pin  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔥 Oven  
🔌 Electric griddle (optional)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Soak the pork front leg in water for 2 hours to remove blood.  
2. Place all spices into a cheesecloth bag, tie it tightly, and rinse briefly. Make sure to remove the seeds from the cardamom, or the broth will become bitter.  
3. **Caramelize the sugar**:  
   - Add rock sugar to a wok over low heat and stir constantly.  
   - When the sugar is fully melted and turns a deep jujube red color with yellow smoke rising, quickly add 200g water.  
   - Stir well over high heat and set aside. The finished caramel should be jujube-red and have almost no sweetness.  
### Braising the Meat  
1. Place the soaked pork into a large pot, add 2000g water, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
2. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 5 minutes to allow impurities to surface. Skim off the foam using a slotted spoon.  
3. Add the caramelized sugar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, cooking wine, rock sugar, salt, and the spice bag containing scallions, ginger, and spices.  
4. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 3 hours until the meat is tender and easily pierced with chopsticks.  
   **Note**: Since the meat is already soaked, you can keep the original broth after skimming to retain the meat’s flavor. The simmering time can be flexibly adjusted between 2-4 hours, with corresponding adjustments to water and salt.  
### Making the White Sesame Bun (Bai Ji Mo)  
1. **Knead the dough**: Combine all-purpose flour, yeast, and baking soda with water, mix into flocculent pieces, then knead into a ball. Add vegetable oil and lard, knead thoroughly until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 20 minutes.  
2. **Shape the buns**:  
   - Divide the dough into portions of about 82g each and knead smooth.  
   - Roll each portion into a spindle shape.  
   - Use a rolling pin to flatten from the center outward, forming a piece that is thinner on top and thicker at the bottom.  
   - Holding one end, stretch and roll it up into a cylinder, sealing the seam at the bottom.  
   - Stand the dough upright, flatten it into a round disc, then press an indent in the center with your finger.  
3. **Bake the buns**:  
   - Heat a frying pan without oil, place the dough disc in the pan, and cook over low heat for 2 minutes.  
   - Flip it over, cook until a golden ring appears, then flatten it slightly. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then flip and cook until both sides are golden brown.  
   - Place in a preheated oven at 200°C (392°F) and bake for about 10 minutes.  
   - **Doneness test**: Press the edge of the bun with your finger; it should spring back quickly when done.  
   - **Alternatives**:  
     - **Frying pan only**: Cover and cook over low heat, flipping every 2 minutes for a total of 6-8 minutes.  
     - **Electric griddle**: Preheat, place the bun, cover and cook for 2 minutes, then flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes.  
### Assembly  
1. Remove the braised meat and chop it, including fat, lean, skin, and sinew, without mashing it into a paste—keep some texture.  
2. Cut the bun open from the middle without cutting all the way through, and stuff it with the chopped meat.  
3. Spoon a bit of the braising broth over the meat.  

## Health Suggestions  
Shaanxi Rou Jia Mo is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive salt and fat intake. People with high blood lipids may reduce the amount of fatty meat.
