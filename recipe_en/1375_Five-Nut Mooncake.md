# Five-Nut Mooncake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫒 Peanut Oil 90g (for the crust)
🍯 Honey 130g
🧪 Alkaline Water 2g
🥛 Milk Powder 15g (optional)
🌾 Cake Flour 250g (for the crust) + 50g (for the filling's cooked flour)
🌾 Bread Flour 40-50g
🥜 Nuts (cashews, pistachios, macadamia nuts, pecans, etc.) 250g (pre-roasted or stir-fried and chopped)
🍚 Glutinous Rice Flour 40g (for the filling's cooked flour)
🖤 Black Sesame Seeds, as needed
🥓 Pork Floss, as needed (optional, to enhance texture and flavor)
🍬 White Sugar 35g
🍯 Maltose 45g
💧 Water 60g
🍶 Baijiu (White Liquor) 5g
🥚 Egg Yolk 1 (for brushing the surface)
🌽 Cornstarch, a small amount (to prevent sticking in the mold)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥄 Hand Whisk
🔧 Silicone Spatula
🔪 Chef's Knife and Cutting Board
🍳 Non-Stick Pan
🔥 Electric Griddle
🧁 Mooncake Mold (75g size)
📄 Parchment Paper
🖌️ Pastry Brush
🧤 Disposable Gloves

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. **Make the Crust Dough**
   Pour 90g peanut oil, 130g honey, and 2g alkaline water into a glass bowl. Whisk with a hand whisk for 3-5 minutes until fully emulsified. Sift in 15g milk powder and 250g cake flour, stirring until it becomes a sticky consistency. Then add 40-50g bread flour, and mix evenly to form a dough.
   **Key Point**: The flours must be added in stages; add the cake flour first until the dough is sticky, then add the bread flour to adjust the dough's softness.
   Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and rest at room temperature for 2-3 hours.

2. **Make the Five-Nut Filling**
   Place the 250g of roasted and chopped nuts into a large bowl and set aside. In a non-stick pan, stir-fry 50g cake flour and 40g glutinous rice flour over low heat until lightly yellow. Turn off the heat, let it cool, then pour it into the bowl with the nuts. In another bowl, whisk together 35g white sugar, 45g peanut oil, 45g maltose, and 60g water until emulsified and free of lumps. Pour this mixture into the nut bowl. Add an appropriate amount of black sesame seeds, 5g baijiu, and an appropriate amount of pork floss. Wearing disposable gloves, mix and squeeze the filling until it clumps together.
   **Key Point**: The cooked flour must be stir-fried to remove the raw taste; the sugar syrup must be fully emulsified; the filling needs to be mixed until it is cohesive and can be formed into balls.
   Divide the filling into portions of 45g each, roll them into balls, and set aside.

3. **Prepare the Egg Wash for Brushing**
   Take one egg yolk, add a little water, and whisk with a pastry brush until you have a smooth, thin egg yolk wash.

### Production Phase
1. **Wrap the Mooncakes**
   Divide the rested crust dough into portions of 30g each, roll them into balls, then flatten them. Place one filling ball in the center of a crust disc. Using the tiger's mouth of your hand, gently push the crust upward until it completely encloses the filling, then roll it into a round ball.
   **Key Point**: The crust must be of even thickness. Be patient when closing the opening to prevent the filling from leaking out.

2. **Mold the Mooncakes**
   Sprinkle a small amount of cornstarch inside the mooncake mold to prevent sticking, then tap out any excess. Place the wrapped mooncake ball into the mold. Press the handle vertically 2-3 times, then push it out to release the mooncake.
   **Key Point**: Use a very small amount of cornstarch in the mold to avoid blurring the pattern's clarity; apply even pressure when pressing.

3. **Pan-Fry to Set in the Electric Griddle**
   Preheat the electric griddle to medium heat. Line the bottom with a piece of parchment paper and place the mooncake blanks evenly on it. Cover with the lid and cook for 5 minutes to set the shape.
   **State Check**: The bottom of the mooncake should be set and lightly yellow in color.

4. **Brush with Egg Wash**
   Open the lid. Using a pastry brush, gently apply a thin, even layer of the egg yolk wash onto the surface of the mooncakes. Cover the lid again and cook for about 5 minutes until the egg wash has set.
   **Key Point**: Apply the egg wash thinly and evenly to avoid obscuring the pattern.

5. **Flip and Cook Through**
   Carefully flip the mooncakes over. Cover the lid and continue cooking for another 5-6 minutes. Repeat the flipping and cooking process until the crust on the sides of the mooncake changes from a translucent, raw dough color to an opaque, cooked white, and the overall color is satisfactory.
   **Heat and Time**: Medium heat, total cooking time is about 25-30 minutes.
   **Key Point**: The side crust turning completely white is the main indicator that the mooncake is thoroughly cooked.

6. **Removal from Pan and "Return of Oil"**
   Remove the cooked mooncakes from the griddle and place them on a cooling rack or a baking tray to cool completely. Once fully cooled, store them in an airtight bag or container at room temperature for 1-2 days.
   **Key Point**: The crust is quite hard when first taken out of the pan. It needs a process called "return of oil," where the crust absorbs oil from the filling, becoming soft and achieving a better texture.

## Health Tips
Five-nut mooncakes are pastries high in sugar and oil. It is recommended to consume a quarter of a piece at a time, paired with unsweetened tea to cut the richness. Suitable for festive indulgence, but individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume in moderation.
