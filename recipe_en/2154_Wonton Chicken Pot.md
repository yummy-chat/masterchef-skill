# Wonton Chicken Pot
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐷 Pork shank (moderate amount, for making soup base)  
🐷 Spare ribs (fresh, for making soup base)  
🦪 Conpoy (large, for making soup base)  
🐔 Chicken feet (for making soup base)  
🐔 Old hen (for making soup base)  
🐷 Pork skin (for making soup base)  
🦐 Fresh shrimp (for making wonton filling)  
🐔 Whole chicken (raw, about 1, for poaching as the main ingredient)  
🥬 Cabbage (slow-cooking variety, such as napa cabbage, poached with the chicken)  
🥟 Wonton wrappers (for wrapping wontons)  
🫚 Ginger (to remove gamey smell)  
🌿 Sand ginger (for dipping sauce)  
🧄 Garlic (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili pepper (for dipping sauce)  
🧅 Scallion (for dipping sauce)  
🧂 Salt  
🍬 Sugar  
⚪ White pepper powder  
🐷 Lard  
🐟 Dried flounder powder  
🐔 Chicken powder  
🐔 Chicken fat (needs to be heated until smoking)  
🥄 Soy sauce  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍲 Large pot (for making soup base and poaching)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok (for heating chicken fat until smoking)  
🥣 Small bowl (for preparing dipping sauce)  
🫙 Glass pot (for serving the finished dish)  

## Cooking Steps  
### Preparation  
1. **Make wonton filling**: Shell the fresh shrimp, devein them, wash, pat dry, and cut into small pieces.  
2. **Season the shrimp filling**: Add salt, sugar, white pepper powder, lard, and dried flounder powder to the shrimp pieces. Mix well. Lard and dried flounder powder are key to the authentic flavor.  
3. **Wrap wontons**: Place a moderate amount of filling in the center of a wonton wrapper. Fold it diagonally into a triangle shape. Use your thumb and index finger to push the two bottom corners inward and pinch them tightly together, forming a goldfish tail shape. Do not overfill.  
4. **Prepare the whole chicken**: Wash the fresh whole chicken thoroughly. The giblets can be retained. Do not blanch.  
### Making the Soup Base & Poaching  
5. **Make the soup base**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add ginger slices. Then add the pork shank, spare ribs, conpoy, chicken feet, old hen, and pork skin. Cook over medium heat for 30 minutes until the soup turns milky white, then continue boiling for a full 2 hours. None of the meat has been blanched.  
6. **Poach the whole chicken and cabbage**: After 2 hours, remove and discard the solid ingredients from the soup. Bring the soup back to a boil. Place the whole chicken in the pot, lifting and lowering it with a soup ladle a few times to ensure even heating. Then add the cabbage. Reduce the heat to medium and poach for 20 minutes.  
7. **Retrieve the main ingredients**: First, remove the cabbage. Then remove the whole chicken. Let the chicken cool slightly, then debone it and tear the meat into pieces. Set aside.  
### Boiling Wontons & Preparing Dipping Sauce  
8. **Boil wontons**: Using the flavorful broth used for poaching the chicken, add the wontons and boil for about 5 minutes until they all float to the surface, indicating they are cooked.  
9. **Prepare dipping sauce**: In a bowl, combine minced sand ginger, minced garlic, chili pepper rings, and chopped scallions. Heat chicken fat in a wok until smoking. Pour the hot fat over the ingredients in the bowl to release their fragrance. Finally, add soy sauce and stir to combine.  
### Assembly & Plating  
10. **Assemble**: Line the bottom of a glass pot with the poached cabbage. Arrange the torn chicken pieces on top. Place the cooked wontons in the pot. Ladle the boiling hot broth over everything.  
11. **Final seasoning**: Season with a little salt and chicken powder if desired. For extra gloss and aroma, you can drizzle a bit more hot oil over the top.

## Health Advice  
This Wonton Chicken Pot combines multiple high-protein ingredients. The broth is rich and nutritious, making it suitable for those needing to replenish energy or during a recovery period. Note that the whole chicken is not blanched; individuals with gout should control their intake. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the flavor.
