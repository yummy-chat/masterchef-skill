# Hand-Grabbed Lamb Ribs
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Skin-on young lamb ribs, about 2.35 jin (approx. 2.8 lbs / 1.3 kg)
🫚 Ginger, large piece (for stewing)
🌿 Chinese angelica (Bai Zhi), 1 slice
⚪ White peppercorns, a small handful
🧄 Garlic, about half a head (minced, for dipping sauce)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (some chopped for dipping sauce, some cut into sections for soup base)
🧅 Green garlic leaves, to taste (chopped, for soup base)
🧄 Garlic chives, to taste (chopped, for soup base)
🧂 Salt, to taste (for stewing and dipping sauce)
🧂 MSG, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🍬 White sugar, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🧴 Light soy sauce, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🧴 Aromatic vinegar, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste (for dipping sauce)
🌶 Water-ground chili paste, to taste (for dipping sauce)

## Cookware & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🍲 Stewing pot (clay pot)
🥄 Ladle
🥣 Small bowl (for dipping sauce)

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut lamb ribs into large pieces along the bone gaps.
   **Note**: Use skin-on young lamb ribs for tender meat and mild gamey flavor. Cut into large pieces to retain juices.
2. Soak the cut lamb ribs in water for 30 minutes to release blood, then rinse clean.
   **Key Point**: Soaking and rinsing are essential steps to remove gamey smell; do not skip.
3. Place the cleaned lamb ribs in the pot with **cold water**.
   **Key Point**: Always start with cold water to allow blood to slowly leach out, keeping the broth clear.
4. Heat over high heat until boiling, then continue boiling for 6–7 minutes, skimming off any foam with a ladle until the broth is clear.
   **Note**: Foam must be completely skimmed. No need to change water afterward to preserve the broth’s flavor.
5. Add large ginger pieces, 1 slice of Chinese angelica, and a small handful of white peppercorns to the pot. Add enough hot water to replenish the broth volume.
6. Cover the pot, reduce heat to **low**, and simmer for 1 hour.
7. While stewing, prepare the dipping sauce: Place minced garlic in a bowl and pour hot oil over it to release fragrance. Then add salt, MSG, sugar, light soy sauce, aromatic vinegar, sesame oil, water-ground chili paste, scallions, and chopped cilantro. Stir well.
8. Once the lamb ribs are cooked, season the broth with salt to taste. Remove and discard the ginger pieces and Chinese angelica from the broth.
9. Remove the lamb ribs and arrange on a plate. Serve with the dipping sauce.
10. Make lamb soup: Place green garlic leaves, cilantro sections, and garlic chives in a bowl. Ladle hot lamb broth over them, letting the heat release the aroma of the greens.

## Health Advice
This hand-grabbed lamb rib dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium. It is warming and helps ward off cold in winter, especially suitable for people with cold hands and feet. Note that lamb is warming in nature, so those with a hot constitution should consume it in moderation. The vinegar and chili in the dipping sauce can aid digestion.
