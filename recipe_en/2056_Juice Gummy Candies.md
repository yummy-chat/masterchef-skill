# Juice Gummy Candies
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍊 Fresh fruit (choose from oranges, strawberries, blueberries, melon, etc., juiced and strained to yield 500g pure juice)
🍬 White sugar 150g
🍯 Corn syrup (water怡) 400g
🌽 Corn starch 85g
💧 Water 85g (to mix with corn starch)
🧈 Butter 60g
🍋 Lemon juice 20g
🧂 Sugar to taste (for final coating to prevent sticking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
🧃 Juicer/blender
🫗 Strainer
🥛 Measuring cup with markings
📦 Square container (lined with parchment paper)
🔪 Knife, cutting board

## Steps
1. Prepare juice and starch water: Wash and process the selected fruit, blend in a juicer until smooth, strain out the pulp using a strainer, and set aside 500g of pure juice. Mix 85g corn starch with 85g water, stir until smooth to make starch water, and set aside.
   **Note**: For fruits prone to oxidation and discoloration, such as strawberries and blueberries, use the juice immediately after juicing and do not store for long.
2. In a non-stick pan, pour in 500g juice, 150g white sugar, and 400g corn syrup. Heat and stir until the sugar is completely dissolved, then bring to a boil.
3. Once boiling, add 60g butter and 20g lemon juice. Stir until the butter is completely melted.
   **Key**: Lemon juice helps stabilize color, add acidity, and reduce greasiness. For oxidation-prone fruits like blueberries and strawberries, it is recommended to add lemon juice in the first step when mixing ingredients to better prevent discoloration; for fruits less prone to oxidation, such as oranges, add it at this step.
4. Stir the starch water again, then slowly pour it into the boiling syrup while stirring quickly and continuously with a silicone spatula. Reduce heat to low and continue cooking for about 20 minutes, until the syrup becomes very thick, forms a cohesive mass, and does not stick to the bottom of the pan, resembling a dough-like consistency.
   **Note**: This process requires continuous stirring to prevent burning and clumping.
5. Drop a small amount of the sugar mass into cold water. If it quickly solidifies into shape and does not break apart, remove from heat.
6. Pour the cooked sugar mass into a parchment-lined square container, smooth the surface with a spatula, and refrigerate until completely set.
7. Once set, remove from the container and peel off the parchment paper. Cut into uniform small squares with a knife.
8. Place the cut candy pieces into a plate filled with sugar, and coat evenly with sugar granules to prevent sticking.

## Health Tips
This gummy candy is rich in sugar and corn syrup, suitable for occasional indulgence, but it should be consumed in small amounts by diabetics, those on a weight loss diet, or those who need to control sugar intake. It is recommended to pair with unsweetened tea to balance the sweetness and help moderate blood sugar fluctuations.
