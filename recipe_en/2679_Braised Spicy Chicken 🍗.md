# Braised Spicy Chicken 🍗

Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
### Main Ingredients
🍗 Chicken 1500g (whole chicken or chicken thighs, cut into pieces)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 30g (adjust spiciness to taste)
🫚 Green Sichuan pepper 5g
🫚 Red Sichuan pepper 5g (use only one type if that is all you have)
🍃 Bay leaves 3 pieces
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces (about 3g, medium size)
🌿 Cinnamon stick about 0.5g (no more than 1g)
🌰 Cardamom 5 pieces
🤍 Angelica dahurica about 3g (size of three thumbnails)
🌿 Amomum tsaoko 1 piece (use only the skin, discard the seeds)
🧅 Scallion 40g (about the length of an index finger, cut into large sections)
🧄 Garlic 50g (9 cloves, trimmed top and bottom)
🫚 Ginger 30g (5 slices, slightly thick)
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for garnish)

### Seasonings
🫘 Pixian broad bean paste 40g (one heaping spoonful)
🫘 Soybean paste 30g (one spoonful)
🧂 Light soy sauce 20g (two spoonfuls)
🧂 Dark soy sauce 5g (half a spoonful)
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (one spoonful)
🍬 White sugar 9g (3 teaspoons)
🧂 White pepper powder 1g (two or three shakes)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 8g (3 teaspoons)
🧂 MSG 5g (2 teaspoons)
🧂 Salt 1-2g (adjust to taste before finishing)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (slightly more than usual for stir-frying)
🐖 Lard, as needed (optional, enhances aroma)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok or frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧻 Paper towels
🥣 Bowl
🥄 Spoon
🫕 Lid

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the chicken into pieces, rinse thoroughly under running water, remove any remaining feathers, and pat completely dry with paper towels.
**Key point**: The surface of the chicken pieces must be thoroughly dried.
2. Place the dried chili peppers, green Sichuan pepper, and red Sichuan pepper in a large bowl. Add warm water and soak for about 5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
**Key point**: Soaking removes dust and the bitter taste of the Sichuan pepper.
3. Place the bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon stick, cardamom, angelica dahurica, and the skin of the amomum tsaoko in a bowl. Cut the scallion into sections, trim the garlic cloves, and slice the ginger into thick pieces. Set aside.
4. Prepare the "fried chicken sauce": In a bowl, mix the Pixian broad bean paste, soybean paste, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Stir until well combined.
5. Heat oil in a wok. You can add a small piece of lard to enhance the aroma. Heat the oil over high heat, then add the ginger slices. Fry over high heat until the edges of the ginger turn golden brown.
**Key point**: Frying the ginger until golden helps preheat the wok to prevent sticking and removes the pungent taste of the ginger.
6. Carefully slide the drained chicken pieces into the wok along the edge. Do not stir immediately. Spread the chicken flat and sear over high heat for about 20 seconds until the bottom is set, then start stir-frying. Continue stir-frying over high heat until the chicken surface turns golden and the oil in the wok becomes clear.
7. Add the drained dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppers. Stir-fry over high heat until fragrant. Then add all the dry spices and continue stir-frying until aromatic.
8. Pour in the prepared "fried chicken sauce". Stir-fry over high heat for about 2 minutes until the sauce releases its red oil and aroma.
9. Spread the chicken pieces flat and add boiling water. The water should completely cover the chicken and be roughly level with it.
10. Once the water boils, add the white sugar, white pepper powder, chicken bouillon, and MSG. Stir well. Cover with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 25 minutes if using a whole chicken, or 15 minutes if using chicken thighs (reduce the amount of water slightly for thighs).
11. After simmering, increase the heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, add the scallion sections and garlic cloves. Stir-fry until fragrant. Taste the sauce and adjust saltiness by adding salt as needed. Stir well, then turn off the heat and remove from the wok.
**Key point**: Salt must be added last, as the saltiness of different sauces varies.
12. Transfer to a serving plate, garnish with cilantro, and serve.

## Health Advice
Chicken stewed with a variety of spices is rich in nutrients but relatively high in calories. It is suitable for those building muscle or with high physical energy expenditure. Pay attention to controlling the amount of dried chili peppers and salt. Those with sensitive stomachs should consume in moderation.
