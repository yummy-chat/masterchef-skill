# Thin Pancake  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g  
💧 Boiling water 150g  
🍞 Yeast 1/4 tsp (dissolved in 1 tbsp water)  
🧈 Unsalted butter 40g (melted)  
🧂 Salt 1/4 tsp  
🌾 Dry flour as needed (for anti-stick)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for brushing to prevent sticking)  
🥚 Egg 1 (for making egg pancake)  
🧅 Scallions as needed (for making egg pancake)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🫗 Measuring cup / tablespoon  
🍳 Stainless steel pot  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks / scraper  
🪵 Wooden lid / bowl (to cover dough and prevent drying)  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🫙 Dry flour bowl  
🖌️ Brush  
🪘 Rolling pin  
🍳 Nonstick pan / cast iron pan  
🔗 Spatula  
🧺 Basket / bowl + towel (for keeping warm and preventing drying)  
🪬 Lid (for softening)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the all-purpose flour in a large bowl. Gradually pour in boiling water while stirring with chopsticks until the mixture becomes flaky.  
   **Note**: Using boiling water to scald the flour makes the pancake skin soft and easier to roll out.  
2. Let the scalded dough flakes cool for 15 minutes to avoid burning your hands.  
3. Mix 1 tablespoon of water with yeast until dissolved. Add to the dough flakes, then add the melted unsalted butter and salt.  
   **Note**: A small amount of yeast improves the texture, making the pancake skin fluffier.  
4. Knead all ingredients into a dough. The dough will be rough on the surface. Cover and let it rest for 15 minutes. Take it out and knead again until smooth, then cover and let it rest for another 45 minutes.  
   **Note**: Resting in stages helps achieve a smooth dough.  
5. Take the rested dough, poke a hole in the center, cut it, and roll into a long strip. First divide into larger sections, then into 20 even small pieces. Press each piece flat.  
6. Prepare a bowl of dry flour. Take one small piece, roll it out slightly, brush the surface evenly with cooking oil, then press the oiled side into the dry flour to coat. Repeat this process, stacking the prepared dough pieces with the oiled side facing up.  
   **Key point**: Brushing oil and dipping in dry flour is essential for creating layers.  
7. Cover the stacked dough pieces and let them rest for 10 minutes to prevent shrinking.  
8. Press the rested stack flat with your hand, flip over, and press again. Roll it out with a rolling pin, flipping every few rolls, until it forms a round thin pancake slightly larger than your palm. The pancake should be thin with intact layers.  
   **Note**: Repeated flipping while rolling ensures even thickness and prevents shrinking.  
9. Preheat a flat pan over medium-low heat. Do not add oil. Place the rolled-out pancake dough into the pan, turning it by hand to ensure even heating.  
   **Note**: Maintain medium-low heat throughout.  
10. When one side changes color and sets, flip it over. After flipping, the top cooked layer will naturally lift. Use your hand or a spatula to peel it off and set aside. Continue cooking the remaining layers, peeling off each cooked layer until all are done.  
    **Key point**: Insufficient oil or flour coating will cause the layers to stick and prevent separation.  
11. Stack all cooked thin pancakes in a container lined with a towel, cover, and let them soften with residual heat, making them more pliable.  
12. To make egg pancake: Beat 1 egg, add scallions and salt, and mix well. Heat a pan with a little oil, pour in the egg mixture, and quickly cover with a thin pancake. When the egg sets, flip and cook until lightly browned, then roll it up.  

## Health Advice  
This thin pancake uses the scalding method and oil layering technique, resulting in a soft texture that is easy to digest, making it suitable for the elderly and children. However, each pancake layer is brushed with oil and dipped in flour, so it is relatively high in calories. During weight loss, it is recommended to pair it with light side dishes.
