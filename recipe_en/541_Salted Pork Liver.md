# Salted Pork Liver
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Season: All-year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐷 Pork liver 1000g
🧅 Scallion 10g (for marinating) + 10g (for cooking)
🫚 Ginger 20g (for marinating) + 10g (for cooking)
🧂 Salt 20g (for marinating) + 7.5g (for cooking)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 3g (for marinating) + 3g (for cooking)
⭐ Star anise 2g (for marinating) + 2g (for cooking)
🍶 Cooking wine 30g
🌶️ Dried chili peppers 8g (optional)
🍃 Bay leaf 1 piece
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g (optional)
🥄 Light soy sauce 20g
🫒 Sesame oil 5g
🧂 Black vinegar 10g
🧅 Chopped scallion 5g
🧄 Garlic 20g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating)
🍲 Pot (for blanching and cooking)
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Small bowl (for dipping sauce)
🛠️ Tweezers/kitchen shears (for pressing garlic)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the pork liver, cut along the grain, and split large pieces in half.
2. Crush the scallion and ginger, cut into sections, and add to the liver along with salt, Sichuan peppercorns, broken star anise pieces, and cooking wine. Use your hands to squeeze the scallion, ginger, and spices to release their aroma, then thoroughly rub and mix with the liver.
**Key point**: Must crush the scallion and ginger, and massage until the salt grains are completely dissolved. Cover with plastic wrap and marinate for at least 3 hours.

3. Remove the marinade from the marinated liver. Place the liver in cold water and heat over medium heat to blanch. During blanching, press the liver with chopsticks to help release blood foam, and turn occasionally. Once the water boils and a large amount of foam appears, immediately turn off the heat.
4. Rinse the foam from the surface of the liver carefully with warm water.
5. Add about 1500g of water to the pot, along with 7.5g salt, 3g Sichuan peppercorns, 2g star anise, 10g scallion, 10g ginger slices, dried chili peppers, bay leaf, and chicken bouillon to create the cooking broth.
**Key point**: The ratio of water to salt is approximately 5g salt per 1kg of water.

6. Place the liver into the broth, bring to a boil over high heat, then cover and turn to the lowest heat to cook. Turn the liver once during cooking if it is not fully submerged. Once cooked, turn off the heat and let the liver cool naturally in the broth. Do not remove it early, otherwise the edges will become hard.
**Key point**: Keep the heat at the lowest level throughout; otherwise, the texture will not be smooth. Adjust cooking time based on the size of the liver, ensuring it is fully cooked through.

7. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a bowl, mix light soy sauce, sesame oil, black vinegar, chopped scallion, and crushed garlic. Stir well.
8. Remove the completely cooled liver, trim off irregular edges and thinner parts. Slice the neat portions into thin pieces, and arrange them in a circular pattern on a plate.
**Key point**: Slices should be thin to avoid a choking sensation when eating.

9. Wrap the remaining liver tightly in plastic wrap and store in the refrigerator. Do not expose it to air, or it will dry out.

## Health Tips
Pork liver is rich in iron, making it effective for blood nourishment and suitable for those with anemia. However, it is high in cholesterol, so it is recommended to consume 2-3 slices per serving. Adequate marinating time and slow cooking over low heat ensure a smooth texture without any fishy smell.
