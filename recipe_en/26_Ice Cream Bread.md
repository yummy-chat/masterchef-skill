# Ice Cream Bread
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 300g (bread base)
🍚 Fine granulated sugar 18g (bread base); 30g (custard sauce); 10g (cream filling)
🧂 Salt 4.8g
🥛 Milk powder 9g
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk 24g
💧 Water 180g (added as ice water + ice cubes)
🦠 Fresh yeast 9g (or dry yeast 3g)
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g (bread base); 9g (custard sauce)
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (custard sauce)
🥛 Milk 160g (custard sauce)
🌾 Cake flour 6g (custard sauce); additionally prepare a small amount for surface decoration
🌽 Cornstarch 4g (custard sauce)
🥛 Whipping cream 310g (ice cream filling)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍞 Stand mixer
🌡️ Probe thermometer
🍴 Dough scraper
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Small saucepan
🥄 Hand whisk
🧹 Fine-mesh sieve
🥢 Bamboo skewer
🍦 Piping bag and piping tip
🕸️ Cooling rack
🔥 Oven (convection or conventional)
📦 Plastic wrap/plastic bag

## Instructions
1. Prepare and weigh all ingredients.
   **Note**: In summer, it is recommended to replace some liquid with ice cubes; controlling liquid temperature is key to kneading dough in summer.
2. Make the custard sauce: In order, mix the egg yolks, 30g fine granulated sugar, milk, cake flour, and cornstarch until smooth. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until thickened. Remove from heat, add 9g butter, and stir until smooth. Cover with plastic wrap directly on the surface and refrigerate until completely cooled.
   **Note**: Stir constantly while cooking to prevent lumps; must be thoroughly chilled before mixing with whipped cream.
3. Place all bread base ingredients except butter and yeast into the stand mixer bowl. Mix on speed 3 for 4 minutes until combined, then switch to speed 6 for 10 minutes, until the dough comes together, the surface is relatively smooth, and a thick membrane can be pulled, but the structure remains rough.
   **Note**: If the dough temperature is too high at this point, flatten the dough and freeze it to cool down.
4. Return the cooled dough to the stand mixer. Add the yeast and softened 30g butter. Mix on speed 4 for 3 minutes until the butter is absorbed, then switch to speed 6 for 2-3 minutes, mixing until full gluten development is achieved.
   **Note**: After adding butter, do not knead for too long; monitor the dough's condition. The final dough temperature should ideally not exceed 28°C.
5. Round the dough and perform the first proof. Proof at room temperature for about 30 minutes, until the dough has expanded and increased in volume, but do not let it proof for too long.
6. Remove the proofed dough, gently press to degas, divide into 9 equal portions, and round each portion.
   **Note**: Degassing only needs to remove most of the large air bubbles; retain some gas. Rounding helps create a finer crumb structure.
7. Place the rounded small dough pieces onto a baking tray for the second proof. Proof at room temperature, covering the surface with plastic wrap to prevent drying out.
8. After proofing, sift a layer of cake flour onto the surface of the dough pieces.
   **Note**: Cake flour browns less, giving the finished product a softer appearance.
9. Bake in a preheated oven. For a convection oven: bake at 160°C for 8 minutes. For a conventional oven: bake at 170°C top heat / 190°C bottom heat for 8 minutes. Bake until the surface is golden brown. The bread is done when a probe thermometer inserted into the center comes out clean without any raw dough.
   **Note**: Do not bake for too long, otherwise the crust will become thick and hard.
10. Remove the thoroughly chilled custard sauce and gently stir to smooth it out.
11. Whip the whipping cream and 10g fine granulated sugar using a stand mixer or hand mixer until it reaches 60-70% stiffness, showing streaks but still having strong fluidity.
    **Note**: Do not overwhip, otherwise the texture will be dry when mixed with the custard sauce.
12. Add the custard sauce to the whipped cream and gently fold until evenly combined, resulting in a smooth, flowing ice cream batter consistency.
13. After the bread has completely cooled, use a bamboo skewer or piping tip to poke a hole in the bottom of a bread piece and swirl it inside to create a cavity.
    **Note**: Wait until the bread is completely cool before operating.
14. Fill a piping bag with the ice cream filling. Pipe the filling into the bread through the hole created. Fill each bread piece with about 50g of filling, not less than 35g.
15. After filling, seal the hole with a small piece of baking paper. Then place all bread pieces into a sealed plastic bag.
16. Freeze in the freezer until firm, then serve.
    **Note**: Before eating, remove from the freezer and let it soften slightly for a better texture.

## Health Advice
This ice cream bread is high in sugar and fat, suitable as an occasional summer dessert. It is not recommended for frequent consumption during weight loss or by diabetics. After freezing, allow it to soften slightly before eating to reduce the cold stimulus to the stomach and intestines.
