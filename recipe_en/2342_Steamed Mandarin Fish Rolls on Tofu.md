# Steamed Mandarin Fish Rolls on Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐟 1 mandarin fish (head and tail removed, butterflied into slices; fish head split open to stand upright)
🫘 1 block silken or soft tofu (cut into rectangular slices about 1 cm thick, approximately 8 slices)
🥒 Cucumber, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🥕 Carrot, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🧅 Scallion white part, as needed (cut into thin strips)
🧅 Shredded scallion, as needed (for final garnish)
🫚 Shredded ginger, as needed (for final garnish)
🌶️ Shredded red bell pepper, as needed (for final garnish)
🧂 Salt, as needed (used twice: to marinate fish slices and to season tofu base)
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed
🥚 1 egg white
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed
🍾 Steamed fish soy sauce, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (heated for drizzling)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Red round cutting board
🍽️ White rectangular deep dish (for steaming and plating)
🍽️ White square plate (for rolling fish slices)
🥣 Bowl (for marinating fish slices)
♨️ Steamer or steaming oven
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Remove the head and tail of the mandarin fish. Fillet both sides of the fish along the backbone, then remove the rib bones. Using a diagonal cut, slice the fillets into connected butterfly slices. Split the fish head open from the lower jaw so it can stand steadily.
2. Cut the tofu into rectangular slices about 1 cm thick. Cut the cucumber, carrot, and scallion white into thin strips.
3. Place the fish slices in a bowl. Add salt, cooking wine, egg white, and cornstarch. Mix thoroughly so each slice is evenly coated with a thin batter.
4. Arrange a layer of tofu slices on the bottom of a rectangular deep dish and sprinkle with a little salt. Take one fish slice, place cucumber strips, carrot strips, and scallion white strips at one end, and gently roll into a fish roll. Arrange all fish rolls together with the fish head and tail neatly on the tofu to form a complete fish shape.
   **Note**: Try to keep the fish rolls uniform in size for an attractive presentation.
5. Once the steamer or steaming oven is producing steam, place the fish dish inside and steam over high heat for about 5 minutes, until the fish is fully white and the rolls are set and cooked through.
   **Note**: Do not steam for too long to prevent the fish from becoming tough. Adjust the time slightly based on the size and thickness of the rolls.
6. Remove the fish dish from the steamer. Evenly drizzle steamed fish soy sauce over the surface. Sprinkle with shredded scallion, shredded ginger, and shredded red bell pepper. In a separate pan, heat cooking oil until it begins to smoke, then immediately pour the hot oil over the shredded toppings, creating a sizzling sound and releasing a rich aroma.
   **Note**: The oil drizzle is the finishing touch. The oil temperature must be high enough to fully release the fragrance.

## Health Tips
This dish uses tofu as a base, with mandarin fish shaped into butterfly rolls. It is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those on a weight loss diet or needing a brain boost. Be careful not to steam too long, as overcooking will make the fish tough and affect the texture.
