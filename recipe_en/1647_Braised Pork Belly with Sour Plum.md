# Braised Pork Belly with Sour Plum
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly (three-layer pork) - to taste (cut into large cubes)  
🍐 Snow pear - 1 (diced)  
🫒 Sour plums - about one small bowl  
🧅 Scallions - about 2 (tied into knots)  
🫚 Ginger - to taste (sliced)  
🌿 Star anise - to taste  
🌿 Bay leaves - to taste  
🌿 Cinnamon stick - to taste  
🍬 White sugar - to taste (for caramelizing)  
🧂 Dark soy sauce (chicken rice dark soy sauce) - to taste  
🧂 Light soy sauce - two tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce - one tablespoon  
🐔 Chicken powder - one tablespoon  
🍶 Vinegar - to taste  
🍬 Rock sugar - to taste  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥘 Enameled cast iron pot  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🥄 Wooden spatula  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🥅 Strainer  
🍳 Lid  

## Steps  
1. Cut the pork belly into cubes approximately the size of mahjong tiles.  
2. Peel and dice the snow pear, blanch in boiling water for about 1 minute, then drain and set aside.  
3. In a bowl, mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, chicken powder, and vinegar to create the sauce. Set aside.  
4. Place the enameled pot over low heat, add a small amount of oil and white sugar. Slowly heat while stirring, until the sugar melts into a deep brown syrup with fine bubbles.  
   **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout to prevent the sugar from burning and becoming bitter.  
5. Add the pork cubes and stir-fry quickly to evenly coat each piece with the caramelized sugar.  
6. Add the ginger slices and scallion knots, drizzle in dark soy sauce, and continue stir-frying until evenly colored.  
7. Pour in the prepared sauce and stir to combine.  
8. Add the sour plums and stir-fry evenly.  
9. Add enough boiling water to cover, then add rock sugar, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick.  
   **Key Point**: It is best to add all the boiling water at once to avoid needing to top up later, which could affect the texture.  
10. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and braise for about 45 minutes.  
    **Key Point**: Using an enameled pot helps lock in moisture, keeping the meat tender and soft.  
11. After braising, uncover the pot and remove the scallions, ginger, star anise, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick.  
12. Add the blanched snow pear cubes to the pot and stir-fry together with the braised pork, allowing the pear to absorb the sauce.  
13. Increase the heat to medium-high to reduce the sauce. Once the sauce thickens and the pear is infused with flavor, remove from heat and serve.  

## Health Suggestions  
The sweet and sour flavor of sour plums helps cut the richness of the pork belly, while the refreshing snow pear adds another layer of texture. This dish is ideal for warming up in autumn and winter or for serving guests. Be mindful of the amount of sugar and dark soy sauce used; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation.
