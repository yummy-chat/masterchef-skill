# Sesame Red Bean Pastry

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥡 All-purpose flour 140g (for water dough) + 80g (for oil dough)
🫗 Boiling water 60g
💧 Room temperature water 40g
🫒 Vegetable oil 40g
🫘 Red bean paste 320g (divided into 8 portions)
🧂 White sesame seeds, as needed (for surface decoration)
🧂 Baking powder 1/2 tsp (added to oil dough)
🥣 Flour 1 tsp (for adhesive)
💧 Water 1 tbsp (for adhesive)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl or large bowl (for kneading dough)
🍳 Flat frying pan
🔪 Knife
🪤 Cutting board
🥄 Spatula
🥛 Measuring cups/measuring spoons
🖌️ Brush
🫖 Pot (for boiling water)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make water dough**: Place 140g all-purpose flour in a bowl. First, scald the flour with 60g boiling water and stir, then add 40g room temperature water and knead into a smooth dough.
   **Note**: Scalding the dough makes the pastry softer. After kneading, cover and let rest for 15 minutes, then knead again until smooth, and let rest for 1 hour.

2. **Make oil dough**: In another bowl, mix 80g all-purpose flour with 1/2 tsp baking powder, add 40g vegetable oil, and knead into a smooth dough. Divide into 4 equal portions and roll into balls. Set aside.

3. **Divide filling**: Press 320g red bean paste flat, divide into 8 equal portions, and roll each into a ball.

4. **Wrap the oil dough**: Take one portion of water dough, flatten it, wrap one portion of oil dough inside, and seal the opening tightly.

5. **First rolling**: Roll the wrapped dough into a long tongue-shaped thin sheet, then roll it up from one end into a cylinder. Repeat for all portions and let rest for a moment.
   **Note**: Apply even pressure when rolling to avoid tearing the oil dough.

6. **Divide into pieces**: Cut each cylinder in half with a knife, yielding 8 pieces with spiral cross-sections.

7. **Second rolling and filling**: Take one piece, place the cut side facing up, and flatten it. First, gather the two ends of the cut side toward the center, then flatten again and roll into a round thin sheet. Wrap the red bean paste inside, seal the opening tightly, and gently press the seam side down to form a pastry disc.
   **Key point**: Working with the cut side facing up helps create distinct layers.

8. **Coat with sesame seeds**: Mix 1 tbsp water with 1 tsp flour to make a flour-water mixture. Brush this onto the surface of the pastry disc, sprinkle with white sesame seeds, and press gently. Repeat this process on both sides.
   **Note**: The flour-water mixture helps the sesame seeds adhere during pan-frying.

9. **Pan-fry**: Add an appropriate amount of oil to a flat frying pan. Place the pastry discs in the pan before the oil gets too hot. Cook over medium-low heat until the bottom is set and golden brown, then flip and continue cooking until both sides are golden and the pastry puffs up.
   **Note**: Use medium-low heat throughout to ensure the inside is fully cooked and the outside is not burnt.

## Health Advice
Sesame red bean pastry has a high oil and sugar content. It is recommended to eat 1-2 pieces per serving, paired with unsweetened tea to cut the richness. The red bean paste provides some dietary fiber, but the scalding and rolling techniques cause the crust to absorb more oil. Individuals sensitive to blood sugar should be mindful of their intake.
