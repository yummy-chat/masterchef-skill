# Homemade Focaccia
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌾 Bread flour 250g
💧 Water 200ml
🍞 Yeast 3g
🍬 Caster sugar 2g
🧂 Salt 5g
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil about 10g (kneaded into dough)
🫒 Olive oil enough (for greasing the baking pan and drizzling)
🍅 Cherry tomatoes to taste
🌿 Rosemary to taste
🧄 Oil-packed garlic to taste
🧂 Coarse salt to taste (optional for decoration)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🥣 Mixing bowl
🥄 Wooden spoon or spatula
🫙 Plastic wrap
🍳 Baking pan (cast iron or ceramic)
🔥 Oven

## Instructions
1.  Add yeast and caster sugar to water, stir to combine, and let sit for a moment to activate the yeast.
2.  In a large bowl, mix bread flour and salt until well combined.
3.  Pour the yeast water into the flour, and stir with a wooden spoon or spatula until no dry flour remains. No need to knead by hand.
4.  Add extra virgin olive oil and continue stirring until the oil is fully incorporated into the dough.
5.  Cover with plastic wrap and let it ferment at room temperature for 30 minutes to allow the gluten to develop naturally.
6.  Dip your fingers in water, lift the edges of the dough and fold them towards the center. Repeat this folding process twice.
    - **Key Point**: This step helps build gluten and strengthens the dough's structure.
7.  Cover again with plastic wrap and refrigerate for an overnight cold fermentation.
    - **Key Point**: Long cold fermentation makes the interior of the bread airier and softer.
8.  Grease the bottom of the baking pan generously with olive oil. Pour the fermented dough into the pan and gently spread it out with your hands.
9.  Place the pan in an oven preheated to about 35℃, add a cup of boiling water, cover with plastic wrap, and let it undergo the final proof for 1 hour, until the volume has visibly increased.
10. Meanwhile, prepare the toppings: cherry tomatoes, rosemary, oil-packed garlic, etc.
11. Grease your fingers with oil to prevent sticking, poke dimples evenly into the surface of the proofed dough. Then arrange the cherry tomatoes, rosemary, and oil-packed garlic on top.
12. Drizzle another spoonful of olive oil over the surface. Sprinkle with a little coarse salt if desired.
13. Preheat the oven to 230℃. Place the pan in the oven and bake for about 20 minutes.
    - **Key Point**: It is better for the temperature to be slightly too high than too low. Watch the color carefully. Bake until the surface is puffed, caramelized, and the crust is fragrant and crisp.

## Health Advice
Focaccia is rich in quality olive oil and is a fermented bread, making it suitable as a main dish paired with salads or soups. However, it is relatively high in calories, so fitness-conscious individuals should control their portion size. Cold fermentation can help lower the bread's glycemic index, making it a more friendly option for blood sugar management.
