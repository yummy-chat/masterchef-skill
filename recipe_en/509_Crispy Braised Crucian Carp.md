# Crispy Braised Crucian Carp
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Crucian Carp 2 pieces (scales left on, no score cuts, cleaned and fins and tail trimmed)
🧅 Scallion 1 piece (cut into rolling chunks)
🫚 Ginger a few slices
🧄 Garlic a few cloves
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers a few pieces
⭐ Star Anise a few pieces
🌸 Sichuan Peppercorns to taste
🍃 Bay Leaves a few pieces
🍬 Rock Sugar 1 bowl
🧂 Whole Black Peppercorns to taste
🍶 Cooking Wine a generous amount
🍶 White Vinegar to taste
🧂 Soy Sauce to taste
🦪 Oyster Sauce to taste
🍅 Tomato Paste 1 tablespoon
🧂 Salt about 3 tablespoons
🍬 White Sugar to taste

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🔪 Chef's Knife and Cutting Board
🍳 Wok (Frying Pan)
♨️ Pressure Cooker
🥄 Slotted Spoon
🍽️ Bowls and Plates

## Cooking Steps
1.  **Prepare the fish:** Use scissors to trim the fins and tail of the crucian carp. Clean the surface of the fish. Note: Do not scale the fish and do not make score cuts. Keep the fish whole.
    **Key Point:** Keeping the fish scales adds a crispy texture; not scoring prevents the fish from breaking apart during braising.

2.  **Deep-fry the fish to set the shape:** Pour about half a wok of cooking oil and heat until a faint wisp of blue smoke appears. Gently slide the fish into the oil. Do not move the fish immediately. Let it naturally set its shape and until the bottom side turns golden brown, then carefully flip it. Deep-fry until the entire fish is golden brown. Remove and drain on a slotted spoon.
    **Key Point:** Do not flip the fish right after adding it to the oil, or the skin will break. Frying to a golden brown color makes the fish bones crispy.

3.  **Sauté the spices and make the sauce:** Heat the wok over medium heat. Add scallion segments, ginger slices, garlic cloves, dried chili peppers, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, and whole black peppercorns. Sauté until fragrant. Then, sequentially add a generous amount of cooking wine, white vinegar, soy sauce, and oyster sauce to deglaze and release the aroma. Finally, add the tomato paste and enough water, and then add the rock sugar.
    **Note:** The cooking wine and white vinegar must be added to deglaze first, to remove any fishy smell and enhance the flavor.

4.  **Adjust the seasoning:** Bring the sauce to a boil over high heat. Add about 3 tablespoons of salt and an appropriate amount of white sugar. Taste and adjust until the sauce has a distinct flavor profile of sourness, sweetness, and saltiness.
    **Key Point:** The three flavors of sour, sweet, and salty need to be balanced. The ratio of rock sugar to vinegar significantly impacts the final flavor.

5.  **Pressure cook to tenderize the bones:** Transfer the deep-fried fish and the seasoned sauce into a pressure cooker, ensuring the sauce covers the fish. Seal the lid and pressure cook until the fish bones are completely tender and soft.
    **Note:** The pressure cooking time must be sufficient to ensure the fish bones are soft enough to eat.

6.  **Finished dish:** Upon serving, the sauce is rich, the fish remains whole, and the bones and fins are all edible, offering a flavor profile of sweet, sour, salty, and savory.

## Health Advice
This crispy braised crucian carp allows the bones and fins to be chewed and eaten. It is rich in calcium, making it suitable for people who need calcium supplementation. The elderly and children can safely consume this dish, but individuals with high blood pressure should limit their intake of the sauce.
