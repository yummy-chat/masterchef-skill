# Glutinous Rice Rolls with Soybean Flour
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍚 Water mill glutinous rice flour 1000g
💧 Water 1000ml (can be increased appropriately in winter)
🫘 Soybeans 200-300g (for homemade soybean flour)
🍬 Soft white sugar about 20g (for adding to rice batter)
🍬 White sugar 1-2 tablespoons (for adding to soybean flour)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for brushing the pan and preventing sticking, preferably odorless cooked oil)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🍲 Steamer/Steaming basket
🥘 Stainless steel flat-bottomed pan
🥣 Glass bowl
🥡 Blender/Food processor
🧺 Sieve
🔪 Scraper
🥟 Rolling pin
🔨 Metal meat mallet
🛡️ Plastic wrap
🧤 Food gloves

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make soybean flour**: Wash and drain the soybeans. Use a dry, oil-free flat-bottomed pan. First, stir-fry over high heat to dry the surface moisture, then reduce to the lowest heat and continue stir-frying for 10-20 minutes until the soybeans are cooked through and the skins crack open.
   **Note**: The fried soybeans must be completely cooled before putting them together with white sugar into the blender to grind into fine powder. If the powder is not fine enough, sift it through a sieve once.

2. **Mix batter and steam**: In a glass bowl, add 1000g water mill glutinous rice flour, 1000ml water, and about 20g soft white sugar. Stir until smooth with no lumps.
   **Note**: In winter, the amount of water can be increased appropriately, keeping the water-to-flour ratio approximately 1:1.
   Evenly brush a layer of cooking oil inside the stainless steel flat-bottomed pan, pour in the rice batter, and place in the steamer over high heat for 30-40 minutes.
   Use a scraper to dig into the center to check; if there is no raw white batter, it can be removed.

3. **Pound the rice cake**: Spread plastic wrap on the work surface. While hot, scrape the steamed glutinous rice cake onto the plastic wrap. Brush a layer of cooked oil on the surface to prevent sticking. Use a metal meat mallet to repeatedly pound the glutinous rice ball for about 10 minutes until the texture is softer, stickier, and more elastic.

4. **Shape and roll**: Move the pounded glutinous rice ball to a work surface dusted with soybean flour. Open it up on both sides and evenly coat with soybean flour. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a large, thin rectangular sheet. Sprinkle another layer of soybean flour on the sheet. Starting from one end, roll it up tightly and shape it into a long log.

5. **Cut and plate**: Use a scraper to cut the log into evenly sized small pieces. Place them in a plate with soybean flour and coat the cut surfaces with the flour.
   **Key**: It tastes best when eaten hot, with a soft, sticky, and stretchy texture. If it hardens after cooling, reheat it to soften before eating.

## Health Advice
This glutinous rice roll with soybean flour is rich in carbohydrates, suitable for replenishing energy before or after exercise, but diabetic patients should control their intake. Homemade soybean flour is more fragrant than store-bought. Note that the fried soybeans must be completely cooled before grinding into powder, otherwise, they will clump.
