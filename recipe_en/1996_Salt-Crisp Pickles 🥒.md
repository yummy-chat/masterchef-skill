# Salt-Crisp Pickles 🥒
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Year Round

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Chinese cabbage (cabbage), appropriate amount (cut into large pieces)
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (cut into thick slices)
🫛 Cowpea (long beans), appropriate amount (used whole)
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (split in half)
🌶️ Fresh red chili peppers, appropriate amount (used whole)
🧂 Non-iodized salt, appropriate amount (for pickling brine)
🧊 Rock sugar, a few pieces
🍶 High-proof liquor, appropriate amount (for sterilization and sealing the jar)
🌿 Spices (star anise, Sichuan peppercorn, bay leaves, cinnamon, etc.), appropriate amount

## Tools & Equipment
🏺 Clay pickling jar (with water trough, inner and outer lids)
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🍳 Iron wok
🥣 Strainer
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1.  Wash the Chinese cabbage, carrot, cowpea, ginger, and fresh red chili peppers thoroughly. Allow them to air-dry completely on the surface.
**Note**: The vegetables must be completely dry of raw water (tap water) to prevent contamination and spoilage in the jar.

2.  Prepare the vegetables: Cut the carrot into thick slices, split the ginger in half, cut the Chinese cabbage into large pieces, and keep the cowpea and chili peppers whole.
3.  Sterilize the pickling jar: Pour an appropriate amount of high-proof liquor into the empty jar and shake it so the liquid coats the entire inner surface. Discard the liquor.
**Note**: Thorough sterilization is important to ensure no unwanted bacteria remain in the jar.

4.  Layer the prepared vegetables into the jar one by one, pressing down gently as you go. Do not pack the jar too full; leave space for the pickling brine.
5.  Prepare the pickling brine: Bring clean water to a boil in a wok. Add the spices and let them infuse. Add the non-iodized salt, stirring until fully dissolved. Turn off the heat and let the brine cool completely to room temperature.
**Key Point**: All utensils used must be free of oil; the brine must be completely cool – never pour hot water directly into the jar; boiling the water sterilizes it and removes raw water.

6.  Once the pickling brine has completely cooled, strain out the spice solids using a strainer. Pour the brine into the jar, ensuring all vegetables are fully submerged.
7.  Add a few pieces of rock sugar to the jar to provide nutrients for the lactic acid bacteria.
8.  Seal the jar: Pour some high-proof liquor along the rim of the jar and ignite it. While the flame is burning, quickly place the inner lid on, followed immediately by the outer lid. Then, fill the water trough (rim) with water to create an airlock.
**Key Point**: The fire and venting step is crucial for creating an oxygen-free environment; seal the lids quickly. The water seal must be tight; check regularly and replenish water to keep the trough from drying out.

9.  Place the pickling jar in a room-temperature environment and ferment for about 20 days.
**Note**: Do not open the jar lid during fermentation to avoid disrupting the anaerobic environment.

10. After fermentation is complete, the vegetables will turn a translucent pale yellow. The brine will be clear without mold. The pickles will have a crisp, refreshing texture with a mild sourness and are ready to eat. When removing pickles, wear disposable gloves and use clean, oil-free, and dry utensils to avoid contaminating the jar’s environment.

## Health Advice
These pickles rely on lactic acid bacteria fermentation and are rich in probiotics and dietary fiber, which can aid digestion. However, they are high in salt, so people with high blood pressure should consume them in moderation. Ensure all equipment and ingredients are free of oil and bacteria throughout the process to guarantee successful fermentation.
