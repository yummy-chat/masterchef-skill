# Jade Vegetable Bag 🥬
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Ham 300g
🥬 Pointed cabbage 2 pieces (must use pointed head cabbage; regular round cabbage leaves are easy to tear)
🌰 Lotus seeds as needed
🫑 Red bell pepper 1 piece
🫑 Yellow bell pepper 1 piece
🌿 Chinese chives a small amount (for tying, can substitute with scallion leaves)
🧂 Salt to taste
🧂 Ground white pepper to taste
🍲 Broth as needed (1 tablespoon per vegetable bag)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Food processor (meat grinder)
🍳 Cooking pot
🥢 Slotted spoon/tongs
♨️ Steamer
🥄 Soup spoon

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the filling**: Put ham, deseeded red and yellow bell peppers, and lotus seeds into a food processor. Blend into a smooth mixed filling. Transfer to a bowl, add salt and ground white pepper to taste, and mix well.
   **Key point**: All ingredients go directly into the food processor for grinding; no pre-dicing is needed.

2. **Prepare the cabbage**: Use pointed cabbage. If peeling whole leaves is difficult, cut the cabbage in half first. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add a small spoonful of baking soda, then blanch the cabbage leaves for about 60 seconds until soft and bright green. Remove and immediately plunge into cold water to cool. Drain and set aside.
   **Key point**: Must use pointed cabbage; adding baking soda during blanching helps keep the leaves bright green.

3. **Assemble the jade vegetable bags**: Lay a blanched cabbage leaf flat. Place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Gather the leaf edges toward the center, like wrapping a bun. Tie the gathered opening tightly with a softened Chinese chive to form a small bag shape.

4. **Steam**: Arrange the wrapped vegetable bags neatly on a plate. Place in a steamer and steam over high heat for about 15 minutes.

5. **Prepare the broth**: While steaming, use a small saucepan to make broth from bouillon or homemade stock. Bring to a boil and keep warm.

6. **Finish with broth**: Remove the steamed vegetable bags. While hot, pour one tablespoon of hot broth over each bag.
   **Key point**: Pouring broth over the bags significantly enhances flavor and moisture.

## Health Tips
This dish wraps ham and bell peppers in cabbage leaves, retaining protein and vitamins while reducing oil intake. Suitable for those watching calories but still wanting meat. Soak lotus seeds until soft before blending to avoid a hard texture. Do not tie the chives too tightly, to prevent bursting during steaming.
