# Braised Tofu with Shrimp and Sea Cucumber in Sichuan Style
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Silken tofu 1 box (cut into 2cm cubes)
🦐 Shrimp about 100g (cut into large cubes)
🦪 Ready-to-eat sea cucumber 2 pieces (cut into large cubes)
🥩 Ground meat appropriate amount
🧅 Scallion white appropriate amount (minced)
🧄 Ginger appropriate amount (minced)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (minced)
🌶️ Chili bean paste (Doubanjiang) appropriate amount
🫘 Fermented black beans (Douchi) a pinch
🧂 Salt appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar about 5g (used twice: once for blanching to remove odor, once for seasoning)
🌶️ Ground Sichuan peppercorn about 8g (part for seasoning, part for sprinkling on top)
🧂 Ground white pepper about 3g
🧂 Light soy sauce about 10g
🫕 Chili oil appropriate amount (drizzle on top before serving)
🌿 Garlic sprouts/leeks about 10g (minced for garnish)
🧴 Wet starch slurry appropriate amount (for thickening)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🥘 Slotted spoon/strainer
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Bowls, plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Add a pinch of salt and sugar. Blanch the tofu cubes, shrimp cubes, and sea cucumber cubes together for about 1 minute. Remove and drain well.
**Key point**: Blanching removes the beany flavor from tofu and pre-cooks the shrimp and sea cucumber while adding sugar helps eliminate any fishy odor.

2. Heat oil in another wok over high heat. Add the ground meat and stir-fry until it separates and releases its aroma. Add chili bean paste and stir-fry until red oil is released. Then add the minced scallion, ginger, garlic, and fermented black beans, stir-frying until fragrant.
**Note**: The chili bean paste must be stir-fried thoroughly to ensure sufficient red oil, which is essential for the dish’s bright red color.

3. Add an appropriate amount of water. Bring to a boil, then season with sugar, ground white pepper, ground Sichuan peppercorn, and light soy sauce.

4. First add the blanched sea cucumber and shrimp cubes. Then gently add the tofu cubes. Simmer over medium heat for about 2–3 minutes to allow the ingredients to absorb the flavors fully.
**Key point**: After adding the tofu, minimize stirring. Use a gentle shaking motion of the wok to move the ingredients, preventing the tofu from breaking apart.

5. Gradually drizzle in the wet starch slurry while gently pushing the mixture with the bottom of the spatula to evenly coat all ingredients. Cook until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, then remove from heat and plate.

6. Evenly sprinkle the remaining ground Sichuan peppercorn over the surface. Garnish with minced garlic sprouts. Finally, drizzle with hot chili oil to release the aromatic fragrance, and serve immediately.
**Key point**: Both the ground Sichuan peppercorn and the chili oil must be added at the very end to best preserve the numbing sensation and spicy aroma.
