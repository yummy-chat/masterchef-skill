# Snowflake Chicken Floss
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken breast 150g
🥚 Egg whites (from 4 eggs)
🌾 Pea starch, as needed
🍄 Truffle, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🫚 Scallion and ginger water, as needed (scallion and ginger soaked in water, then strained)
🤍 White pepper, a pinch
🍲 Chicken broth, as needed
🫒 Olive oil, as needed (lard can be used as a substitute for a whiter result)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Large bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🧀 Grater (for shaving truffle slices)

## Preparation Steps
1. Remove the sinew from the chicken breast, and repeatedly pound it with the back of a knife for about 40 minutes until it becomes a fine paste.
**Key point**: Pounding must be done in an orderly and rhythmic manner to loosen the meat, making it easier to absorb flavor and achieve a tender texture.

2. Gradually add the scallion and ginger water to the chicken paste in small amounts, always mixing in one direction to allow the chicken to fully absorb the liquid; add salt and continue mixing quickly until the paste becomes firm and elastic.
**Key point**: Adding water in stages is crucial for "water absorption." Mixing after adding salt enhances elasticity and tenderness.

3. Separate the egg whites from 4 eggs, and repeatedly knead the egg whites by hand to "break the sinews," disrupting the structure of the egg whites.
**Key point**: This step ensures a smoother final texture with no graininess.

4. Place the well-kneaded chicken paste, the sinew-broken egg whites, pea starch, white pepper, and chicken broth into a large bowl. Mix thoroughly until a thin batter forms.

5. Heat the wok until smoke rises, pour out the hot oil to season the wok, then add cold olive oil. Keep the heat on low throughout. Pour in the chicken batter, and gently but continuously push it in one direction using the back of a spoon.
**Key point**: The pushing motion should be slow and steady to prevent sticking, until the chicken floss has fully set into a white, fluffy, flocculent texture.

6. Plate the stir-fried snowflake chicken floss, and shave fresh truffle slices on top. Done.

## Health Advice
This dish primarily uses chicken breast and egg whites, making it rich in high-quality protein and low in fat. It is suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose weight. Cook over low heat and push slowly throughout the process to avoid clumping, which could affect texture.
