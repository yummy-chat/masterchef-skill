# Sweet and Sour Eggs with Rice
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 6 (serves 2, adjust as needed)
🧄 Garlic 4 cloves (finely minced)
🧅 Scallion 1 stalk (chopped)
🫑 Green bell pepper to taste (sliced; use only this if avoiding spice)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili to taste (sliced; add if you like heat)
🍚 Cooked white rice 1 large bowl

🍾 Premium soy sauce (or light soy sauce) 3 tbsp
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp
🍬 White sugar 3 tbsp
🍎 Vinegar (rice vinegar/white vinegar/black vinegar/aromatic vinegar all work) 3 tbsp
🍅 Ketchup 1 tbsp (if using thin tomato sauce, double the amount)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2 pinches
🧂 Beef bouillon powder ½ tbsp (optional, can omit if unavailable)
💧 Water 8 tbsp
🧈 Corn or potato starch ½ tbsp

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🔥 Gas stove (or induction cooktop)
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥣 Bowl (2)
🥄 Soup spoon
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife

## Steps
1.  Add premium soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, vinegar, ketchup, chicken bouillon, beef bouillon powder, starch, and water to a bowl in order. Stir until well combined into a thin, runny consistency.
   **Note**: The sauce should be a thin liquid; the starch will thicken it upon heating.

2.  Pour cooking oil into the pan and heat over high heat until slight ripples appear on the surface. Add the minced garlic, reduce to medium heat, and stir-fry for about 15 seconds until it bubbles and becomes fragrant.

3.  Pour in the prepared sweet and sour sauce. Stir quickly over medium heat until the sauce thickens and bubbles, then immediately turn off the heat. Add the sliced green bell pepper and bird's eye chili, toss a couple of times, and remove from the pan to set aside.
   **Note**: Turn off the heat immediately once bubbling to prevent the sauce from becoming too thick or burning.

4.  In another pan, pour cooking oil and heat over high heat, then reduce to medium heat. Crack in the eggs. Once all eggs are in the pan, reduce the heat to the lowest setting and fry slowly until the egg whites are set and the bottom reaches the desired level of crispness.

5.  Evenly pour the prepared sweet and sour sauce over the fried eggs. Keep the heat on the lowest setting, cover, and simmer for 3 to 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Simmer for 3 minutes for runny yolks, 5 minutes for fully set yolks. Be sure to use the lowest heat to prevent burning.

6.  Once the eggs reach the desired doneness, sprinkle with chopped scallions and place the cooked white rice in the pan. Using a wooden spatula, mix the rice, sauce, and eggs together over low heat until the rice is well coated and has absorbed the sauce.
   **Note**: Mixing in the pan allows the rice to absorb the sauce while gently heating everything together.

## Health Advice
This dish provides a good balance of protein and carbohydrates, but the sweet and sour sauce is high in sugar. It is recommended to halve the white sugar or use a sugar substitute. Suitable for those in a muscle-building phase or heavy physical laborers; those on a weight loss diet should control the rice portion. When frying eggs, always use the lowest heat to minimize the formation of burnt compounds.
