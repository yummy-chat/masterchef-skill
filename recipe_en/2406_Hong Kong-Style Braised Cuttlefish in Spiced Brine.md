## Instructions
1. **Prepare the spice pouch**: Put all spices into a spice bag, tie it tightly, place it in a pressure cooker, add water, and press for 15 minutes. Process red yeast rice separately, not with the other spices.
- Note: The spice pouch must be tied tightly to prevent spices from escaping; red yeast rice can be processed later by soaking in water to extract color, as in standard brining methods.

2. **Prepare the chicken fat**: If the chicken fat is not fresh, bring water to a boil in a pot, blanch the chicken fat, add high-proof baijiu to remove odor, then rinse clean with water.
- Key point: If the chicken fat is fresh, this step can be skipped; proceed directly to rendering the oil.

3. **Fry aromatics to enhance flavor**: Add water to a wok, place the blanched chicken fat in, and render the oil using the water method. Once the whole piece of chicken fat starts releasing oil, add ginger, shallots, and garlic to fry. After the aroma releases, add cilantro and scallions. Fry until the ingredients turn golden, then remove all the fried solids and set aside.
- Key point: The water-rendering method prevents the chicken fat from turning bitter; all fried solids are reserved and will be added to the brine pot later.

4. **Fry the pork ribs**: Add the pork ribs to the oil used for frying the aromatics, fry until the surface is slightly charred, then remove.
5. **Assemble the brine base**: Prepare clean water in a brining pot, add the fried aromatics and fried ribs, then pour all the rendered chicken fat back into the pot.
- Key point: Chicken fat is crucial for keeping the brine warm; it must be returned to the pot. Bones or bone-in ingredients must be added to ensure the brine has meaty flavor.

6. **Combine the spice water**: Pour the spice water (along with the spice pouch) from the pressure cooker into the brining pot, mixing with the base.
7. **Caramelize sugar for color**: Add water and half the rock sugar to a wok, cook over medium-low heat until it turns a jujube-red color, then add an appropriate amount of boiling water. Pour the caramelized sugar into the brining pot.
- Note: Use medium-low heat throughout; cooking to a jujube-red color is the standard. Adding boiling water prevents clumping; beginners can substitute with dark soy sauce.

8. **Simmer the brine**: Bring the brine to a boil over high heat, then continue boiling vigorously for 1.5 hours. Add the brandy, then boil for another 10 minutes.
- Key point: Prolonged simmering allows the flavors to meld; brandy is added at the end to retain its aroma.

9. **Season the brine and braise the cuttlefish**: Remove all solids from the brine pot, returning only the spice pouch. Add the remaining rock sugar, fish sauce, and about 80g of salt, then taste and adjust seasoning.
- Add the cleaned cuttlefish, weigh it down with a heavy object to prevent floating. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and braise for 15 minutes.
- Key point: Braising over low heat keeps the cuttlefish meat firm and springy; the heavy object ensures even heat distribution.

10. After braising, turn off the heat and let the cuttlefish soak in the brine for a while to absorb more flavor. Remove and slice before serving.

## Health Tips  
This braised cuttlefish is rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making it suitable for those monitoring calorie intake. However, note that the brine is high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should consume the broth sparingly. Cuttlefish is cool in nature by traditional Chinese medicine; those with a cold spleen and stomach should pair it with ginger and vinegar.
