# Salt Water Prawns
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Fresh prawns, appropriate amount (cleaned)
🧅 Scallion, about 3-4 sections (cut diagonally)
🫚 Ginger, about 4-5 slices
🍶 Yellow wine, a small amount (for blanching) + appropriate amount (for seasoning)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, about one small handful
🍂 Bay leaves, 2 pieces
🌫️ White pepper powder, 1 teaspoon
🧂 Salt, 4 tablespoons

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥘 Pot (for blanching and making seasoning liquid)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Glass bowl (for cooling and soaking)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the fresh prawns thoroughly and drain excess water.
   **Key point**: The prawns must be fresh, as this is the foundation for a tender texture.
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add the prawns and a small amount of yellow wine. Once boiling again, cook for 30 seconds, then immediately remove and transfer to cold water to cool.
   **Key point**: Keep the blanching time short, and rapidly cool the prawns after removal to maintain their bounciness.
3. In another pot, add an appropriate amount of water, then add Sichuan peppercorns, bay leaves, scallion sections, ginger slices, 1 teaspoon white pepper powder, 4 tablespoons salt, and an appropriate amount of yellow wine. Bring to a boil to create the seasoning liquid.
   **Key point**: The seasoning liquid must be brought to a boil to fully release the aroma of the spices.
4. Pour the boiling seasoning liquid directly over the drained prawns, and let them soak for about 1 hour.
   **Key point**: Use the boiling seasoning liquid for soaking; do not change the water during soaking, or the prawn heads may turn black. The combination of salt and heat helps the prawn meat become sweeter, more tender, and crisp.
5. After soaking, remove the prawns and arrange them on a plate.

## Health Advice
This salt water prawn dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it an excellent choice for fat loss and muscle gain. It suits those controlling calorie intake and those needing calcium supplementation. Note that the seasoning liquid has a high salt content, so the soaking time should not exceed 1 hour to avoid excessive sodium intake.
