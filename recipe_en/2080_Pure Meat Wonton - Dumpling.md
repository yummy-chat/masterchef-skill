# Pure Meat Wonton / Dumpling
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork leg meat 2 jin (skin removed)  
🫚 Ginger 50g (for making crispy oil)  
🧅 Scallion white 50g (for making crispy oil)  
🧄 Garlic 80g (for making crispy oil)  
🧅 Onion 150g (for making crispy oil)  
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (finely chopped, needs blanching)  
🍄 Shiitake mushrooms appropriate amount (finely chopped, needs blanching)  
🌽 Corn kernels appropriate amount (needs blanching)  
🥬 Greens appropriate amount (finely chopped, needs blanching)  
🦐 Shelled shrimp appropriate amount (fresh shelled shrimp do not need blanching)  
🦀 Crab roe appropriate amount  
🥟 Wonton wrappers appropriate amount (or dumpling wrappers)  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for making water starch and preventing sticking)  
🧂 Table salt one large spoon  
🌶️ White pepper a small amount  
🧂 Chicken bouillon a small amount  
🧂 Baking soda 2-3g  
💧 Water 3 spoons  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Meat grinder  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥄 Small bowl  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the pork leg meat, cut into large chunks, and grind in a meat grinder for about 1.5 minutes until the filling forms small granular pieces.  
   **Key point**: Do not grind for too long; maintain a granular texture in the filling.  

2. Grind the ginger, scallion white, garlic, and onion into fine bits in the meat grinder. Add to cold oil in a wok, and cook over medium-low heat until the ingredients’ moisture evaporates and they turn golden and crispy. Turn off the heat, remove, and let cool to make crispy oil.  
   **Doneness check**: All moisture in the ingredients has evaporated, and they appear golden and crispy.  

3. In a small bowl, mix 2 spoons of cornstarch, 3 spoons of water, and 2 spoons of cooled crispy oil. Stir until well combined to form a water starch oil mixture, and set aside.  

4. Place the ground meat filling in a large bowl, add table salt, white pepper, and chicken bouillon. Without adding any liquid first, stir in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and noticeable resistance is felt.  
   **Doneness check**: The filling has developed stickiness, and stirring resistance has increased.  

5. Add baking soda to the filling, and continue stirring in the same direction for several minutes until the filling becomes extremely thick and stringy.  
   **Key point**: Continuous stirring is the essential step for building the filling’s elasticity.  

6. Pour the prepared water starch oil mixture into the filling, and continue stirring until the liquid is completely absorbed by the filling.  

7. Add the blanched and drained vegetable bits, or fresh shelled shrimp and crab roe, and gently mix until evenly combined.  
   **Key point**: Vegetables need blanching to remove excess moisture; fresh shelled shrimp do not need blanching.  

8. Take one wonton or dumpling wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, and pinch tightly to seal.  
   **Key point**: The wrapping method can follow personal preference; ensure the seal is tight and no filling leaks out.  

9. For frozen storage, coat the wontons with a layer of cornstarch to prevent sticking, then place them in a container and freeze. When cooking, do not thaw; add directly to boiling water and cook until the wontons float to the surface.  
   **Doneness check**: Wontons floating on the surface indicates they are fully cooked.  

## Health Advice  
Pure meat wontons are rich in protein, making them suitable for fitness enthusiasts looking to build muscle and those needing an energy boost. Pay attention to controlling the amounts of salt and chicken bouillon. People with high blood lipids can appropriately reduce the proportion of fatty meat.
