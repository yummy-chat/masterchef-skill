# Indian Spiced Chai (Masala Chai)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍵 Ingredients
- Black tea (CTC tea is best, or Assam black tea)
- Whole milk
- Ginger
- Green cardamom pods
- Cinnamon stick
- Cloves
- Black peppercorns
- Sugar

🧂 Seasonings
- Sugar

## Cookware
🍳 Small saucepan
- Strainer
- Teacup

## Instructions
1. Smash the ginger with the back of a knife to crack it and release the juice, without chopping.
2. Lightly crush the green cardamom pods with the back of a knife to crack the shells and expose the seeds.
3. Crush the black peppercorns with the back of a knife.
4. In a small saucepan, pour 120 ml of water.
5. Add the smashed ginger, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, and crushed black peppercorns.
6. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 2 minutes to fully release the spice flavors.
7. Add 8 grams of black tea and continue to simmer over low heat for 2 minutes until the tea darkens.
   **Key point**: CTC tea leaves are finely granulated and release a strong tea flavor quickly; if unavailable, Keemun black tea or Yunnan black tea can be used as substitutes.
8. Pour in 120 ml of whole milk.
9. Heat over medium heat and watch closely. Once the surface of the chai begins to bubble and rise, immediately reduce to low heat.
10. Continue to simmer over low heat for 2-3 minutes, repeatedly lifting the saucepan and pouring the chai back in to fully blend the tea and milk.
11. Add sugar and stir until completely dissolved.
12. Turn off the heat.
13. Strain through a fine mesh strainer into a teacup, removing the tea leaves and spice remnants.
14. Serve hot.

## Health Advice
Indian spiced chai is rich in antioxidants from the black tea and warming spices, making it suitable for cold seasons, helping to warm the body and aid digestion. However, the whole milk and sugar content should be controlled; it is best enjoyed as an afternoon beverage and not consumed in large quantities daily.
