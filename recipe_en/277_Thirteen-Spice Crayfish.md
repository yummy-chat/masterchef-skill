# Thirteen-Spice Crayfish
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients
🦞 Crayfish 1000g (scrub and rinse 6+ times until water runs clear; remove heads, gills, and vein; reserve the roe from the heads)
🦞 Crayfish roe as needed (taken from the crayfish heads)
🧄 Garlic 25g (finely diced)
🫚 Fresh ginger 25g (diced)
🫚 Fresh ginger 25g (sliced, for frying scallion oil)
🧅 Onion 50g (julienned)
🌶 Fresh red chili peppers (xiaomila) 20g (finely chopped; omit if you don’t eat spicy)
🍺 Beer 500g
🌿 Cilantro as needed (for garnish)

## Seasonings
🫒 Vegetable oil generous amount (for frying the crayfish) + about 40g (for stir-frying the base)
🫘 Broad bean paste (doubanjiang) 20g
🧂 Thirteen-spice powder (shisan xiang) as needed (added in two batches: during braising and 30 seconds before removing from heat)
🧂 Salt 2g
🧂 Ground white pepper 1g
🧴 Light soy sauce 10g (drizzled along the side of the wok)
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance freshness before removing from heat)

## Tools & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok (wok for stir-frying)
🥄 Wok spatula
🥄 Strainer/skimmer
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🔥 Gas stove
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowls

## Cooking Steps
1. Scrub and rinse the crayfish under running water at least 6 times, until the wash water runs completely clear.
2. Cut off and discard the heads; remove the gills (the feathery “lungs”) on both sides; pinch the middle flipper of the tail, twist half a turn, and pull out the vein; extract the roe from the heads and set it aside separately.
3. Finely dice the garlic and ginger; julienne the onion; slice the remaining ginger; finely chop the fresh chili peppers.
4. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok, heat to about 70% hot, then reduce to low heat. Add the prepared crayfish. First fry over high heat for 30 seconds to set the shape, then reduce to medium-low heat and fry for another 50 seconds until the shells are crispy. Remove and drain the oil.
   **Note**: Frying over high heat initially locks in the moisture of the meat; medium-low heat makes the shells crisper.
5. In a separate wok, add about 40g vegetable oil. When the oil reaches 50% hot (moderate), add the onion shreds and ginger slices. Fry over medium-low heat for about 90 seconds until golden and dry. Remove the solids and keep the flavored oil in the wok.
   **Note**: This step extracts aroma; do not burn the aromatics.
6. When the oil temperature drops to about 40% hot, add the diced ginger, diced garlic, and chopped chili. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Add 20g broad bean paste, turn to low heat, and stir-fry slowly until red oil releases and the paste smells rich.
   **Note**: The bean paste must be cooked slowly over low heat to avoid burning, which would produce bitterness.
7. Keep the heat low, add the crayfish roe and stir-fry briefly until fragrant. Immediately add about 200g water and 500g beer. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil.
8. Once the liquid is boiling, add the fried crayfish. Bring to a boil again, then reduce to low heat. Simmer with or without the lid for 10 minutes so the meat fully absorbs the flavor.
9. During simmering, add 2g salt, 1g ground white pepper, drizzle 10g light soy sauce along the side of the wok, and sprinkle in the first batch of thirteen-spice powder. Gently turn the crayfish.
10. After 10 minutes, turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce. When the sauce thickens, 30 seconds before removing from heat, add a pinch of sugar and the second batch of thirteen-spice powder. Quickly stir-fry to combine evenly.
    **Note**: Adding the thirteen-spice in two batches gives a more layered and lasting aroma; the sugar enhances freshness and balances flavors.
11. Turn off the heat and transfer the crayfish to a serving plate. Garnish with cilantro leaves and serve immediately.

## Health Advice
Crayfish are high in protein and low in fat, but be careful not to consume too much of the braising liquid, as it is high in oil and salt. Suitable for group meals; gout patients should limit intake of the cooking liquid.
