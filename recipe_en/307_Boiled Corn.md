# Boiled Corn
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ★★  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)  

🌽 Ingredients  
- Fresh corn (with husk)  

🧂 Seasonings  
- Salt  
- Sugar (optional)  

🍳 Utensils  
- Pot  
- Water  

📝 Steps  
1. Peel off the outer tough husks of the fresh corn, keeping the inner thin husks, and place them in a pot.  
2. Add water until it covers the corn by about half a finger joint, then add salt and sugar (optional).  
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and continue cooking for 15–20 minutes (corn holds up well to longer cooking, so extending the time is fine).  
4. Once cooked, remove the corn and drain the water, then let it cool before eating.  

⚠️ Notes  
- Keeping some inner thin husks while boiling adds extra fragrance.  
- The ratio of salt and sugar can be adjusted to personal taste; a light saltwater solution is recommended at about 2 grams of salt per 50 milliliters of water.  
- Slow cooking over low heat helps maintain the corn's tender texture.  

## Health Tips  
Boiled corn is a low-fat, high-fiber healthy option, rich in dietary fiber and B vitamins, making it suitable for reducing swelling and promoting digestion in summer. Pay attention to controlling salt and sugar intake; diabetic patients are advised to use less sugar.
