# Macau-Style Minced Beef Rice
Cuisine: Guangdong Hakka Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Minced beef  
🧅 Onion  
🥚 Egg  
🌿 Spring onion (optional)  
🍚 Rice  
🧂 Light soy sauce  
🧂 Dark soy sauce  
🍅 Ketchup  
🍬 White sugar  
🍶 Cooking wine  
🌶️ Black pepper  
🌾 Cornstarch  
🧈 Butter (can be replaced with regular cooking oil)

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🍲 Saucepan  
🥣 Bowl  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the minced beef in a bowl, add light soy sauce, white sugar, and black pepper, mix well, and marinate for 10 minutes.  
2. Dice the onion into small pieces and set aside.  
3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, ketchup, cooking wine, cornstarch, dark soy sauce, and water, mix well, and set aside.  
4. In a separate pot, make a poached egg or a sunny-side-up egg, and set aside.  
5. Heat the wok, add butter, and once melted, add the diced onion. Sauté over low heat until fragrant.  
**Note**: If butter is unavailable, regular cooking oil can be used as a substitute, but butter adds a unique creamy aroma.  

6. Add the marinated minced beef to the wok and stir-fry with the onion until the beef changes color.  
7. Pour in the prepared sauce, sprinkle black pepper, and continue stir-frying until the sauce thickens, then turn off the heat.  
8. Spoon the cooked minced beef over hot rice, and top with the previously prepared poached egg or sunny-side-up egg.  
**Key Point**: When eating, pierce the egg yolk and mix it with the beef and rice for the best flavor.  

9. Garnish the surface with chopped spring onions for decoration.  

## Health Tips  
Beef paired with onion provides high-quality protein and iron, making it suitable for those needing an iron boost, but watch the salt intake. The poached egg adds nutrition, though those with high blood lipids are advised to reduce egg yolk consumption.
