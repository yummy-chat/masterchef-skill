# Braised Chicken with Chestnuts  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Free-range chicken, about 1 whole (cut into pieces)  
🌰 Chestnuts, about 200g (shelled)  
🫚 Ginger, 1 small piece (cut into chunks)  
🧄 Garlic, 5-6 cloves (smashed)  
🧅 Shallots, 1-2 pieces (cut into chunks)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (cut into sections, for garnish)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections, for garnish)  
🎃 Pumpkin, to taste (for pumpkin puree)  
🥕 Carrot, to taste (for pumpkin puree)  
🌰 Chestnuts, a few (for pumpkin puree)  
🌽 Cornstarch, a little (for pumpkin puree)  
🍶 Cooking wine, to taste (for marinating and braising)  
🥢 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🧂 Chicken bouillon/chicken powder, to taste  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🍯 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for coloring)  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste (for stir-frying chicken)  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (for frying chestnuts and sautéing aromatics)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🫕 Clay pot  
🥄 Spoon  
🥣 Blender  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Prepare aromatics: Cut shallots into chunks, smash garlic cloves, cut ginger into chunks, and cut scallions and cilantro into sections. Set aside.  
2. Marinate the chicken: Place chicken pieces in a bowl. Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, chicken bouillon, salt, oyster sauce, and dark soy sauce. Mix well and marinate for 15 minutes.  
3. Make pumpkin puree: Steam pumpkin, carrot, a few chestnuts, and cornstarch together until cooked. Then blend into a smooth puree using a blender. Set aside.  
4. Shell the chestnuts: Heat cooking oil in a wok to 180°C (350°F). Place raw chestnuts in a slotted spoon and submerge them briefly in the hot oil to fry. When the chestnut shells crack open, remove and peel while hot. Briefly boil the fried chestnuts in water to remove excess oil.  
   **Key Point**: Use the principle of thermal expansion and contraction for easier shelling.  
5. Stir-fry the chicken: Heat a wok, add rapeseed oil, and stir-fry the marinated chicken pieces over high heat.  
   **Note**: Using rapeseed oil enhances the aroma of the chicken.  
6. First, add ginger chunks and stir-fry until fragrant. Then add garlic and shallots, and continue stir-frying until the chicken surface is lightly browned.  
7. Add the shelled chestnuts and stir-fry evenly.  
8. Pour in about one bowl of cooking wine, then add light soy sauce, salt, chicken powder, and white pepper to taste. Stir briefly, then add hot water until the ingredients are just submerged. Cover and braise over medium-low heat for 40 minutes.  
9. Heat a separate clay pot, add a little cooking oil, and sauté garlic and shallots until fragrant. Transfer the braised chicken and chestnuts with the liquid into the clay pot. Add the prepared pumpkin puree, stir to combine, and simmer over medium heat until the sauce thickens.  
   **Key Point**: The pumpkin puree gives the sauce a golden, glossy color and adds a subtle sweetness.  
10. Before serving, sprinkle with scallion and cilantro sections. Serve immediately. The retained heat of the clay pot will continue to release aromas.  

## Health Advice  
Braised Chicken with Chestnuts is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter. However, chestnuts are high in starch, so diabetics should limit their intake. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables for balanced nutrition.
