# Pork and Sauerkraut Dumplings 🥟
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Ground pork 400g (30% fat, 70% lean)
🧅 Scallion 20g (for scallion-ginger water)
🫚 Ginger 15g (for scallion-ginger water)
💧 Water 200g (for scallion-ginger water, yields about 160g after straining)
🥬 Sauerkraut 400g (roughly 1:1 ratio with pork)
🥟 Dumpling wrappers, as needed (store-bought; roll slightly larger before use)
🧂 Salt 4g
🍬 Sugar 4g
🌶️ White pepper powder 0.5g
🍾 Light soy sauce 15g
🫗 Dark soy sauce 5g
🦪 Oyster sauce 10g
🫒 Aromatic oil about 70g (30g for filling, 40g for sauerkraut)

## Tools & Equipment
🔪 Blender/food processor
🔍 Strainer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🫙 Glass bowl
🔪🍴 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🥢 Rolling pin
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Slotted spoon

## Instructions
1. **Make scallion-ginger water**: Cut 20g scallion and 15g ginger into chunks, place in a blender with 200g water, and blend. Strain through a fine mesh, reserving about 160g liquid.
**Key point**: Blending extracts scallion and ginger flavor more efficiently than hand-mixing. You may substitute with stock for extra umami.

2. **Prepare the sauerkraut**: Chop sauerkraut into small pieces, rinse once in clean water, then drain. Squeeze out excess liquid by hand, but do not press too dry.
**Key point**: Do not chop too fine to preserve texture; rinsing reduces excessive sourness. Retaining some moisture prevents a dry final texture.

3. **Prepare dumpling wrappers**: If using store-bought wrappers, roll each one slightly larger with a rolling pin to allow more filling, achieving a thin-skinned, generously filled dumpling.
**Key point**: —

4. **Hydrate the meat filling**: Place 400g ground pork in a bowl. Gradually add scallion-ginger water in batches, stirring and slapping the meat in one direction with your hand until each batch is fully absorbed before adding the next.
**Indicator**: The filling becomes moist, increases in volume, and turns sticky and stringy with a gelatinous texture.
**Core principle**: This is the key step for a juicy, tender filling, using protein hydration to absorb water before denaturation.

5. **Season and bind the meat**: Once the meat has absorbed the water, add 4g salt, 4g sugar, 0.5g white pepper, 15g light soy sauce, 5g dark soy sauce, and 10g oyster sauce. Continue mixing by hand until the filling becomes sticky again.
**Key point**: At this stage, salt begins to denature the protein, locking in the previously incorporated water. No need for MSG or chicken bouillon, as sauerkraut will be added later.

6. **Seal in moisture with oil**: Add about 30g aromatic oil to the seasoned meat filling and mix gently. The oil coats the surface, further sealing in moisture and flavor.
**Key point**: —

7. **Combine with sauerkraut**: In a separate bowl, toss the prepared 400g sauerkraut with 40g aromatic oil, then mix it into the meat filling until evenly combined.
**Key point**: Pre-oiling the sauerkraut prevents it from releasing water, and ample oil is needed to bring out its aroma.

8. **Chill the filling to rest**: Cover the mixed filling with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes. This allows flavors to meld and the texture to firm up, making it easier to wrap.
**Check**: After chilling, the filling should not have any water leakage; if it does, the hydration step was insufficient.

9. **Wrap and cook the dumplings**: Place an appropriate amount of filling on each wrapper, seal tightly using your preferred method. Ensure the seal is secure to prevent bursting. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add dumplings, and cook until they float and the skins puff up. Cook for about half a minute more, then remove with a slotted spoon.
**Key point**: —

10. **Enjoy**: The finished dumplings have smooth, puffed wrappers, a tender and juicy filling, and crisp, tangy sauerkraut. The rich pork and refreshing sourness complement each other perfectly—a classic Northeastern flavor.
**Tip**: While there is a formula for good cooking, the most unique taste always comes from your attention to detail and passion.

## Health Notes
When the meat filling is properly hydrated, the dumplings are tender and juicy; the sauerkraut cuts the richness and aids digestion, making them suitable for the whole family. Control salt intake—those with high blood pressure may reduce the light soy sauce.
