# Stir-Fried Squid
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦑 Fresh squid, 2 pieces  
🧅 Small red shallots (or onion), appropriate amount, minced  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount, minced  
🧅 White part of scallions, appropriate amount, minced  
🌿 Coriander roots, appropriate amount, minced  
🌿 Coriander leaves, a little, for garnish  
🌶️ Chili pepper, optional, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🥄 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little  
🍶 Rice wine (or cooking wine), appropriate amount  
🌶️ White pepper powder, a little  
🫒 Sesame oil, a little  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for blanching, stir-frying, and preparing the sauce)  

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon or strainer  
🥣 Spatula  
🔥 Stove  
🍵 Small bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Prepare the squid: Clean the squid thoroughly, separate the head from the body, remove the transparent cartilage from the squid tube, and discard the internal organs. Squeeze out the ink sac and eyes from the squid head while holding it in water. Cut the squid tube into rings.  
**Note**: Handle the squid in water to prevent ink from splattering. Do not score the squid to reduce water release during stir-frying.  

2. Prepare the aromatics: Mince the small red shallots, garlic, white scallion parts, and coriander roots separately as the stir-fry aroma base.  

3. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, mix together salt, sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice wine, white pepper powder, sesame oil, and a little cooking oil. Stir well and set aside.  
**Key Point**: Preparing the sauce in advance shortens the stir-frying time and ensures even seasoning.  

4. Blanch the squid: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the squid rings and blanch for about 10 seconds, until they curl up and change color. Immediately remove and drain well.  
**Note**: Blanching removes about 80% of the moisture from the squid, preventing excessive water release during stir-frying and resulting in a drier, more aromatic dish.  

5. Flash-fry in oil: Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok and heat until a slight white smoke appears. Add the squid rings and quickly stir to separate them, then immediately remove and drain on paper towels.  
**Key Point**: Flash-frying in oil locks in the squid's internal moisture, making the texture more tender and juicy.  

6. Stir-fry: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add the minced shallots, garlic, white scallion parts, and coriander roots. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Stir the prepared sauce again, pour it into the wok, and mix briefly. Immediately add the flash-fried squid rings, turn the heat to high, and stir-fry quickly until the sauce evenly coats the squid. Remove from heat and serve.  
**Note**: Keep the heat on high throughout after adding the squid to prevent overcooking and toughening.  

7. Garnish with coriander leaves after plating. Serve hot for a crisp, fresh flavor and tender texture.  

## Health Tips  
This squid dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it a satisfying option for those on a weight-loss diet. However, the double process of blanching and flash-frying in oil increases the calorie count significantly, so it is best balanced with light vegetables. Note that squid is not low in cholesterol, so individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
