# Hong Kong-Style White Cut Chicken
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 Qingyuan chicken  
🫚 About 1 large piece of ginger (for poaching water, marinating, and dipping sauce)  
🧅 5-6 spring onions (for poaching water and dipping sauce)  
🟡 3-4 gardenia fruits (crushed, for coloring the poaching water)  
🧊 Ice cubes, as needed (for ice water)  
🧂 Sand ginger powder, as needed (for marinating the chicken)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (for marinating, poaching water, and dipping sauce)  
🧂 Chicken powder, as needed (for poaching water)  
🧂 A pinch of MSG (for poaching water and dipping sauce)  
🍬 1 tablespoon white sugar (about 15g, for Shanghai-style dipping sauce)  
🍗 Chicken broth concentrate, as needed (liquid chicken broth, for both dipping sauces)  
🥢 3 parts light soy sauce (southern Chinese style) (soy sauce liquid for Shanghai-style dipping sauce)  
🥢 1 part dark soy sauce (for coloring)  
💧 6 parts water (to dilute the soy sauce)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🍲 Large stockpot (for poaching the chicken)  
🪣 Deep pot or bucket (for holding ice water)  
🪵 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Small bowls (for preparing dipping sauces)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the Whole Chicken**: Cut off the chicken feet below the joint and discard them. Rinse the whole chicken thoroughly.  
   - Key point: The cutting point for the feet must be accurate to keep the chicken body intact.  

2. **Marinate for Flavor**: Evenly rub the chicken inside and out with sand ginger powder and salt. Massage firmly for a moment to let the flavors penetrate, then refrigerate for 2 hours to marinate.  
   - Key point: Marinating provides the chicken with a base flavor; it is essential.  

3. **Prepare Poaching Water**: Fill a large pot with water and bring to a boil. Add flattened ginger slices, tied spring onions, an appropriate amount of salt, chicken powder, and a pinch of MSG.  
   - Key point: The water needs a basic savory flavor to infuse the chicken.  

4. **Prepare Ice Water and Coloring Agent**: Prepare another bucket of cold water and add plenty of ice cubes. Crush the gardenia fruits with the back of a knife and set aside.  

5. **Prepare Dipping Sauces**:  
   - Ginger and Scallion Oil: Mix minced ginger and minced scallion in a 1:1 ratio. Add salt, chicken broth concentrate, and a little MSG. Stir to combine.  
   - Shanghai Soy Sauce: Based on the Ginger and Scallion Oil, add white sugar. Then, mix in soy sauce liquid in the ratio of dark soy sauce : light soy sauce : water = 1:3:6. Stir well.  
   - Key point: Ginger should be smashed before mincing. The Shanghai-style sauce must include sugar and use southern Chinese light soy sauce.  

6. **Scald the Skin Three Times**: Once the poaching water is boiling, hold the chicken by the head and submerge it completely in the boiling water for 7-8 seconds. Quickly lift it out and immerse it in the ice water for a moment. Repeat this process for a total of three times.  
   - State judgment: The chicken skin tightens upon heating; the alternation of hot and cold makes it firm.  
   - Key point: This "hot-cold denaturation" process is the core step for achieving crispy chicken skin.  

7. **Poach**: Fully submerge the scalded chicken in the poaching water. Add the crushed gardenia fruits. Cover the pot and cook over the lowest heat, maintaining a state where the water is "barely simmering," for about 25 minutes.  
   - Key point: The heat must not be too high, otherwise the chicken skin may crack and the meat will become tough.  

8. **Cool and Set**: Remove the cooked chicken and immediately immerse it in ice water until completely cool.  
   - Key point: Rapid cooling firms the chicken meat, makes the skin smooth, and stops the residual heat from continuing to cook the chicken.  

9. **Chop and Plate**: Drain the cooled chicken. Chop it into pieces and arrange them on a plate to resemble the original shape of the chicken.  

10. **Drizzle with Oil for Aroma**: Drizzle a little sesame oil or cooked oil over the chicken pieces to add luster and fragrance. Serve with the two dipping sauces.  

## Health Advice  
This white cut chicken is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for people aiming to reduce fat or build muscle. However, the soy sauce and sugar in the dipping sauces should be consumed in moderation. Pay attention to the poaching heat to avoid overcooking the meat; the ice water bath is key for achieving crispy skin and tender meat.
