# Sesame Tangyuan

Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🖤 Black sesame seeds 80g  
🥜 Pecans or other nuts 30g  
🍬 White sugar 80g  
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 250g  
🧈 Melted unsalted butter 60g  
💧 Water 30g (for mixing the filling)  
💧 Boiling water 140g (for scalding the dough)  
💧 Room temperature water 70g (for kneading the dough)  
💧 Water 800g (for cooking the tangyuan)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
⚙️ Blender  
🍳 Small pot (for melting butter)  
🥣 Bowl  
🛍️ Piping bag or plastic bag  
📦 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Knife  
🍽️ Cutting board  
👐 Hands (for kneading and wrapping)  
🥘 Pot (for cooking tangyuan)  
🔥 Stove  
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Place black sesame seeds, nuts, and white sugar into a blender and grind into a powder. Add melted butter and 30g of water to the powder, stirring until it forms a thick paste.  
   **Key Point**: The butter and water must be fully incorporated into the powder, resulting in a paste that can be formed into a lump.
2. Transfer the filling into a piping bag or plastic bag, shape it into a flat block, and refrigerate until firm for easier cutting later.
3. Make the tangyuan skin: Put glutinous rice flour into a large bowl, pour in 140g of boiling water, and quickly stir with chopsticks until it becomes flaky. Gradually add 70g of room temperature water and knead with your hands into a smooth and soft dough.  
   **Key Point**: Boiling water must be used to scald the flour to enhance the dough's elasticity, preventing it from cracking during wrapping.
4. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
5. Remove the chilled filling block and cut it into evenly sized small cubes. Divide the rested dough into small portions, press each into a round wrapper that is thicker in the center and thinner at the edges, then wrap one piece of filling.
6. Use the tiger's mouth (area between thumb and index finger) to slowly push and pinch the wrapper upward until the filling is completely enclosed and the seal is tight. Gently roll it into a ball, ensuring a smooth surface and a tight seal.
7. Pour 800g of water into a pot and bring to a rolling boil over high heat. Gently add the tangyuan and lightly push them with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking to the bottom.
8. Keep boiling over medium-high heat until all tangyuan float to the surface and visibly expand in size, indicating the filling has melted and the wrapper is fully cooked. Remove them.

## Health Advice
Black sesame seeds are rich in calcium and vitamin E. Combined with nuts and butter, this dish has a high energy density, making it suitable for cold seasons or for people with high physical exertion. Diabetics should reduce the amount of white sugar. Glutinous rice is difficult to digest, so those with weak gastrointestinal function are advised to eat only 3-5 tangyuan per serving.
