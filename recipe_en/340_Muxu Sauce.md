# Muxu Sauce
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs (fully beaten into egg liquid)
🧅 Appropriate amount of scallion (cut into chopped green onions)
🌶️ Appropriate amount of red and green peppers (cut into small cubes)
🌶️ 1 dried red chili pepper (cut into segments, optional)
🥒 1 raw cucumber (for dipping as a side dish, optional)
🫘 Appropriate amount of soybean paste (broad bean paste)
🌾 About 1 tablespoon of white flour
🫒 Appropriate amount of cooking oil
🧂 About 1 tablespoon of MSG (optional)
🍬 About 1 tablespoon of white sugar

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Spoon
🥣 Bowl
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg liquid is even. Crack the eggs on the flat edge of the bowl to avoid shell fragments falling in.
2. Cut the scallion into chopped green onions, dice the red and green peppers, and cut the dried chili pepper into segments for later use.
3. Prepare the soybean paste, white flour, MSG, and white sugar in separate bowls.
4. Pour an adequate amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat until 70% hot. Pour in the egg liquid while quickly stirring with chopsticks over medium-high heat for about 1 minute. Stir-fry until the egg liquid forms thin shreds, then remove and set aside.
**Key**: The eggs must be stir-fried into shreds to better coat with the sauce.

5. Leave some base oil in the wok. Once heated, add the dried chili pepper segments and fry over medium-low heat until fragrant. Then add the chopped green onions and diced red and green peppers, stir-frying until aromatic.
6. Add one tablespoon of flour to the fragrant ingredients, turn to low heat, and stir quickly until the flour turns slightly yellow, becomes granular, and releases aroma.
**Key**: This is the secret step to making the sauce thick and rich in flavor.

7. Pour in the soybean paste and stir evenly. Add half a bowl of water, turn to low heat, and simmer for about 3 minutes until the sauce becomes thick, glossy red, and richly aromatic.
**Key**: Must simmer over low heat to remove the raw taste of the paste and enhance its fragrance.

8. Add MSG and white sugar to the simmered sauce and stir well.
9. Return the stir-fried egg shreds to the wok, turn to high heat, and quickly stir-fry for about 1 minute until the egg shreds are evenly coated with the sauce. Then remove from heat.
**Key**: Do not stir-fry the eggs for too long after returning them to the wok to prevent them from becoming tough and hard.

## Health Advice
This Muxu sauce features eggs as the main ingredient and has a rich, savory flavor. It pairs well with coarse grain staples to balance sodium intake. For weight loss, reduce the amount of soybean paste and add more chili for flavor. Ensure the eggs are stir-fried into thin shreds and do not exceed 1 minute when stir-frying them back in the wok to avoid a tough texture.
