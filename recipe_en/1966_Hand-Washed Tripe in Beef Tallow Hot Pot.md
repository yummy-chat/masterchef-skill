# Hand-Washed Tripe in Beef Tallow Hot Pot
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫕 Fresh tripe, as needed (thoroughly washed, hand-torn into pieces)
🧈 Beef tallow hot pot base, 1 piece
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
🫘 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed
🧪 Edible alkali, a little (for soaking tripe)
🫒 Cooking oil, generous amount (plenty of oil)
🥬 Self-selected side dishes: Assorted ham, beef meatballs, tofu, corn, sweet potato vermicelli/wide noodles, tomatoes, leafy greens, etc., as needed

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🥘 Stainless steel soup pot
🫗 Large basin
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥢 Bowls, chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the tripe**: Rinse the whole piece of tripe thoroughly under running water, especially the folds. After washing, tear it along the grain into pieces of moderate size.
**Note**: Hand-tearing is better than cutting with a knife for flavor absorption and texture.
2. **Soak the tripe**: Place the torn tripe in a basin, sprinkle with a little edible alkali, and add clean water to soak, making the texture more tender and crunchy.
3. **Prepare the side dishes**: Slice the ham, cut the corn into sections, cube the tofu, wash the vegetables, and arrange on plates with other hot pot ingredients.
4. **Stir-fry the hot pot base**: Heat a large iron wok over high heat and add a generous amount of oil. Once the oil is hot, add the scallions and ginger slices, stir-frying until fragrant. Add the whole piece of beef tallow hot pot base, dried Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers, and stir-fry over medium heat until the base is completely melted, the red oil is released, and the fragrance is intense.
**Key point**: Use plenty of oil and avoid overheating to prevent burning.
5. **Make the soup base**: Add enough water to the wok, stir well, and bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt and chicken bouillon to taste, then reduce to medium heat and simmer for a few minutes to let the spicy, numbing flavors fully meld, until the soup base is bright red and covered with chili and peppercorns.
6. **Cook the ingredients**: Pour the soup base into a stainless steel hot pot and keep it boiling on the stove. Start with harder-to-cook ingredients, then pick up the tripe and quickly blanch it using the "seven up, eight down" method—dip it in and out of the boiling broth until it curls and changes color, then remove and eat immediately.
**Key point**: The cooking time for tripe must be very short to preserve its tender, crunchy texture.

## Health Suggestions
Hand-washed tripe in beef tallow hot pot provides high protein but is also high in fat and sodium, making it suitable for occasional gatherings. It is recommended to pair with more vegetables and tofu to balance nutrition. When blanching tripe, use the "seven up, eight down" method for no more than 10 seconds to retain the crunchy texture and reduce oil intake.
