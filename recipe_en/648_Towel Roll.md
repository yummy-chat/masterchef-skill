# Towel Roll
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 12 pieces (approx. 480g)
🧂 Granulated Sugar 150g (added to egg mixture)
🥛 Whole Milk 850ml
🌾 Flour 300g (all-purpose or cake flour, both are fine)
🫒 Cooking Oil 80g
🍵 Matcha Powder 8g
🍫 Cocoa Powder 8g
🧈 Whipping Cream 600g
🧂 Granulated Sugar 60g (added to whipping cream)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Glass Bowl
🥄 Hand Whisk
🕸️ Strainer
🍳 Flat-bottomed Pan (non-stick)
🍦 Electric Mixer
🍴 Silicone Spatula
🔪 Knife
📦 Plastic Wrap / Airtight Container

## Directions
1. Take a large bowl, crack in the eggs, add the granulated sugar, and mix thoroughly using a hand whisk.
   **Key Point**: The eggs and sugar must be whisked until fully combined.

2. Pour in the whole milk and stir until the mixture is an even color.
   **Key Point**: Stir into a liquid of uniform color.

3. Add the flour and stir until a smooth, lump-free batter forms.
   **Key Point**: Either all-purpose or cake flour can be used; stir until no lumps remain.

4. Add the cooking oil and stir again until well combined.
   **Key Point**: Stirring thoroughly after adding oil makes the crêpes softer.

5. Strain the prepared batter through a fine-mesh strainer to obtain a smoother batter.
   **Key Point**: Straining results in a finer texture; if you don't have a strainer, stir the batter until smooth and fine.

6. Make plain crêpes: Set aside one-third of the strained batter. Heat a flat-bottomed pan over the lowest heat without adding oil. When the pan is just warm, pour in one and a half ladles of batter and quickly tilt the pan to spread the batter evenly across the bottom.
   **Heat and Timing**: Lowest heat, approximately 20 seconds per crêpe.
   **Doneness Check**: The crêpe is done when the batter is fully set and small bubbles appear on the surface.
   **Key Point**: The pan should not be too hot, or the batter will set before it can spread evenly.

7. Make matcha crêpes: Take half of the remaining batter, add the matcha powder, stir evenly, and strain. Cook using the same method.

8. Make cocoa crêpes: Take the last portion of remaining batter, add the cocoa powder, stir evenly, and strain. Cook using the same method.

9. Whip the cream: Pour the whipping cream and granulated sugar into a clean bowl. Use an electric mixer to whip on high speed for about 3 minutes until stiff peaks form when the beaters are lifted.
   **Key Point**: The cream must be whipped properly, or the roll will collapse when wrapped.

10. Assemble the towel roll: Take 4 cooked crêpes and overlap them in a staggered row. Spread an even layer of whipped cream over the center portion of the crêpes. Fold the top and bottom edges of the crêpes inward to cover part of the cream, then roll tightly from one end.
    **Key Point**: The crêpes must be staggered; apply cream only to the center area, fold the edges, and then roll.

11. Decorate and chill: Use a strainer to sift the corresponding flavored powder over the surface of the rolled towel roll. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
    **Doneness Check**: After chilling, the cream is set, providing a better texture and less risk of melting.

12. Remove the chilled towel roll from the refrigerator, slice into pieces with a knife, and serve.
    **Key Point**: Chilling before serving provides the best texture.

## Health Advice
This recipe uses 12 eggs and 850ml of milk, making the crêpes relatively high in nutritional density, rich in protein and calcium, suitable for dessert lovers needing added nutrition. However, be mindful of the calories from cream and sugar; it is recommended to serve as an afternoon tea shared among several people.
