# Stir-Fried Meat Topping (Sao Zi)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork front leg (fat-to-lean ratio 4:6, fatty part with skin sliced thin, lean part sliced slightly thicker; keep fatty and lean parts separate)  
🌶️ Dried chili segments 10g  
🫚 Ginger, minced 30g  
🍂 Bay leaves, to taste (crushed)  
🪵 Cinnamon stick, to taste  
🪵 Galangal, to taste  
⭐ Star anise, to taste  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste  
🫘 Cardamom, to taste (cracked open)  
🫒 Rapeseed oil, to taste (enough to submerge all meat)  
🍶 Fragrant vinegar 30g  
🥄 Soy sauce 10g  
🍶 Baijiu (white liquor), a small amount  
🧂 Salt 10g (per 500g of meat)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder, to taste (added last for color)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🍳 Iron wok  
🥄 Hand ladle  
🫗 Stainless steel bowl/container  

## Instructions  
1. Pour rapeseed oil into an iron wok, heat over high heat until blue smoke rises to remove the raw oil flavor, then turn off the heat and let it cool to 30-40% hot.  
2. With the heat off, add all the fatty pork slices and separate them, then turn on low heat and stir-fry slowly until the fat renders fully, becoming dry and fragrant without greasiness.  
3. Keeping the heat low, add the lean pork slices and stir to separate them, then splash a small amount of baijiu along the edge of the wok to remove any gamey smell.  
4. Add dried chili segments, minced ginger, and all spices, stir-frying over low heat until fragrant.  
5. Immediately pour in the fragrant vinegar, then add soy sauce and salt, and mix well. The vinegar must be added first to tenderize the meat and highlight the tangy flavor.  
6. Maintain the lowest possible heat and slow-simmer (man lu) for 40 minutes to 1 hour, stirring 2-3 times during the process, until the meat is fully cooked and most of the moisture has evaporated.  
   **Key Point**: Slow-simmering is the core technique, ensuring the meat is tender and not dry. Normally no extra water is needed, unless high heat causes the liquid to evaporate too quickly.  
7. Once the meat is cooked, increase the heat slightly, add fine chili powder and stir-fry to color the mixture until the oil turns a bright red.  
   **Note**: The chili powder must be added last and should be fine; adding it too early will cause it to burn.  
8. Remove from heat and plate. The finished dish has a bright red oil color, with the meat topping being tender, tangy, and fragrant. Perfect for mixing with noodles, stuffing into flatbreads, and more.  

## Health Advice  
This stir-fried meat topping is high in fat. It is recommended to pair it with whole-wheat noodles or plenty of vegetables to balance the oil intake. Suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing an energy boost; individuals with high blood lipids should control their portions. Note that the heat must be kept very low during slow-simmering to prevent the moisture from evaporating too quickly and the meat from becoming tough.
