# Seaweed Egg Fried Rice
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Instructions
1. Place the leftover rice in a rice cooker or large bowl, crack the raw eggs into it, and thoroughly mix with your hands until each grain of rice is evenly coated with the egg mixture, forming egg-coated rice.  
   **Key point**: The rice and egg mixture must be evenly combined into a paste-like consistency; this is the key to achieving golden fried rice grains.  

2. Take one large sheet of whole seaweed and place it in a dry wok without oil or water. Dry over low heat until crispy. Tear the dried seaweed into small pieces by hand, add white sesame seeds, sugar, salt, and sesame oil to the wok, stir-fry evenly, then remove and set aside.  
   **Key point**: The seaweed must be dried until crispy and torn into pieces to mix evenly; the seasoned seaweed crumble is a "rice-deception tool" that enhances the overall flavor.  

3. Dice the ham or luncheon meat into small cubes, and thaw the shrimp in advance for later use.  

4. Heat oil in the wok over medium heat. Add the ham cubes and shrimp, stir-fry until the ham is slightly browned on the surface and the shrimp turns red and curls. Remove or push to the side of the wok.  

5. Pour the egg-coated rice into the wok and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the rice grains are distinct and evenly golden yellow.  

6. Add the prepared seaweed crumble, chopped scallions, and BBQ seasoning powder, then quickly stir-fry until evenly combined.  
   **Key point**: The seaweed crumble must be added last and stir-fried quickly to maintain its crispy texture.  

7. Once evenly stir-fried, plate and serve.  

## Health Tips
This dish uses the egg-coated rice technique, ensuring each grain of rice is evenly covered with egg liquid, providing a balanced ratio of protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for quick energy replenishment after workouts or overtime work. Seaweed and shrimp provide iodine and high-quality protein, but ham and sesame oil slightly increase sodium and fat content, so control the amount of salt accordingly.
