# Slap Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Old tofu 1 piece (crush and break apart by hand)
🥩 Pork belly appropriate amount (cut into diamond-shaped pieces)
🧅 Chinese green onions a handful (white parts)
🌿 Chinese scallion greens appropriate amount (sprinkle in before serving)
🧴 Cooking oil appropriate amount (hot pan, cold oil method)
🍶 Cooking wine a splash (to remove odor)
🫙 Soybean paste one spoonful
🍾 Light soy sauce a drizzle
🍾 Dark soy sauce a little (adjust according to color)
🧂 Chicken bouillon powder appropriate amount
🌶️ White pepper powder appropriate amount
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount (to enhance freshness)
🌾 Cornstarch slurry appropriate amount (for thickening)
💧 Boiling water appropriate amount (to cover the tofu)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🫕 Clay pot or deep dish (for serving the finished dish)

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the old tofu in a plate. Directly crush and break it apart into irregular pieces using your palm. Avoid using a knife.
2. Heat the pan thoroughly, then pour in cold oil. Use the hot pan, cold oil method to prevent sticking.
3. Add the crushed tofu pieces and the cut diamond-shaped pork belly slices.
4. Cook over medium-high heat. Fry until one side turns golden, then flip and continue frying until both sides are browned and crisp, and the pork belly becomes crackling-like.
5. Add the white parts of the Chinese green onions. Stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Splash a little cooking wine along the edge of the pan to remove any odor.
7. Pour in boiling water, enough to cover the tofu. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil.
8. Add the soybean paste, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce in sequence to color and season.
   **Note**: Adjust the amount of dark soy sauce based on personal preference for color.
9. Then add the chicken bouillon powder, white pepper powder, and sugar for seasoning.
10. Keep the heat on high and simmer for 3 minutes, allowing the tofu and pork belly to fully absorb the broth.
11. Reduce the heat to medium, pour in the cornstarch slurry to thicken. Stir until the sauce becomes thick.
12. Once the sauce is thick, sprinkle in the scallion greens. Stir-fry quickly until well combined, then remove from heat. Serve in a clay pot or deep dish.

## Health Advice
Slap tofu is rich in protein. Pairing it with pork belly can supplement iron and energy, making it suitable for people who need to build muscle or have high physical exertion. Due to the pan-frying step and the high sodium content in the sauces, individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake.
