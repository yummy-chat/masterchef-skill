# Dormitory Instant Mochi  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫙 Lotus root starch 4 spoons (approx. 30g) or tapioca starch 4 spoons (approx. 30g)  
🥛 Milk powder 2 spoons (approx. 16g)  
💧 Room temperature water 1 spoon (approx. 8g)  
♨️ Boiling water, as needed (adjust based on desired consistency)  
🍫 Cocoa powder 1 spoon (approx. 8g, used when making chocolate flavor)  
🍪 Crushed biscuits, as needed (e.g., Oreo crumbs or other biscuits, for coating or bottom layer)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl (ceramic bowl)  
🥄 Spoon (ceramic spoon)  
🔌 Electric kettle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. In a bowl, add 4 spoons of lotus root starch and 2 spoons of milk powder.  
   **Note**: If making the chocolate flavor, add 1 spoon of cocoa powder at this point.  
2. Add 1 spoon of room temperature water and stir thoroughly with a spoon until no dry powder remains, forming a smooth, lump-free paste.  
   **Key point**: Room temperature water must be added and stirred first to prevent lumps when boiling water is added later.  
3. Slowly pour freshly boiled water into the paste in two batches while stirring quickly.  
   **Key point**: Pour in batches and keep stirring to prevent local overheating and clumping; stir until the mixture becomes translucent, glossy, and stretchy in consistency.  
4. Adjust consistency by adding a small amount of boiling water as needed and mix well.  
   **Note**: When using tapioca starch, the final product will be stickier and more stretchy.  
5. Place some biscuits in a bag and crush them, then spread on the bottom of a plate. Pour the prepared mochi paste over the biscuit crumbs, then sprinkle another layer of biscuit crumbs on top.  

## Health Tips  
This mochi uses lotus root starch or tapioca starch instead of glutinous rice flour, making it lower in calories and easier to digest. It is ideal for dormitory dwellers craving sweets while wanting to control carbohydrate intake. Note that boiling water should be added in batches and stirred quickly to avoid lumps that affect the stretchy texture.
