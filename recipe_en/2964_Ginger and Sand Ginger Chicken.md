# Ginger and Sand Ginger Chicken  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole chicken (about 1.5-2 catties, choose a smaller "Hang ji" female chicken, cut into evenly sized pieces)  
🥚 1 raw egg  
🌿 Sand ginger, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧅 Shallots, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (finely chopped or flattened)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced or flattened)  
🫑 Green bell pepper, to taste (cut into pieces)  
🌶 Red bell pepper, to taste (cut into pieces for color)  
🧅 Spring onions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🌶 Ground white pepper, a pinch  
🥄 Light soy sauce, a splash  
🍶 Wine (cooking wine or rice wine), a splash  
🦪 Oyster sauce, about half a tablespoon  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Spatula  
🥄 Spoon  
🍽 Plate  
🥣 Bowl  
🔥 Stove  

## Steps  
1. Prepare the chicken: Rinse the whole chicken and drain, then cut into evenly sized pieces.  
2. Marinate the chicken: Place chicken pieces in a bowl. Add a pinch of salt, sugar, ground white pepper, and a splash of light soy sauce. Mix well with your hands. Crack in one raw egg and continue stirring until the chicken is coated with the egg mixture. Finally, add a splash of wine. Marinate for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: Do not use too much light soy sauce to avoid darkening the chicken; the egg is key for making the chicken tender and smooth.  
3. Pan-fry the chicken: Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat and add an appropriate amount of oil. Lay the marinated chicken pieces flat in the pan in a single layer. Cook over medium-low heat. After about 1-2 minutes, when the bottom is set and golden, flip and continue frying until the other side is golden. The total process takes about 6-7 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and the skin is crispy.  
**Key point**: Do not use high heat; patiently fry over low heat until both sides are golden, which is the source of aroma.  
4. Sauté the aromatics: Push the fried chicken to the side of the pan or remove it. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the chopped sand ginger, shallots, and garlic. Sauté over medium-low heat until fragrant.  
5. Season: Combine the chicken with the aromatics. Add half a tablespoon of oyster sauce and quickly stir-fry evenly. Splash wine along the edge of the pan ("praise the wine") to let the alcohol evaporate and remove any gamey smell, enhancing the aroma. Turn to medium-high heat and continue stir-frying.  
**Key point**: "Praising the wine" is a common Cantonese technique to remove odor and enhance flavor.  
6. Add vegetables: Add green and red bell pepper pieces. Stir-fry for about 1 minute until they are just cooked and vibrant in color.  
7. Finish and serve: Finally, add the spring onion sections. Quickly stir-fry for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Turn off the heat and plate.  
**Key point**: Add the spring onions last to maintain their bright green color and fresh, fragrant texture.  

## Health Tips  
This dish uses pan-frying instead of deep-frying, and the chicken is marinated with sand ginger and egg, resulting in tender, smooth meat that is not greasy. It is suitable for those needing protein while managing calorie intake. Ensure the chicken is fully cooked through. The spicy and aromatic sand ginger is particularly beneficial for consuming when feeling damp or having a poor appetite.
