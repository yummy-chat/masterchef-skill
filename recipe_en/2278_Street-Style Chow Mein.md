# Street-Style Chow Mein
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥬 Cabbage 125g (about ¼ head, thinly shredded)  
🥕 Carrot 100g (about half, grated into shreds)  
🌱 Mung bean sprouts A handful  
🥬 Baby bok choy 2 (Shanghai bok choy, separated and washed)  
🥚 Eggs 2  
🌭 Ham sausages 2 (or use grilled sausages; simple ham sausage recommended, sliced)  
🍜 Noodle cakes 2 (fresh noodles, egg noodles, or alkaline noodles recommended; video uses instant noodle cakes)  
🧅 Onion A moderate amount (sliced crosswise to prevent clumping)  
🧅 Scallions, cilantro A moderate amount (for flavor, chopped and set aside)  

### Sauce (for 2 servings)  
🥄 Light soy sauce 3 tablespoons  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon  
🧂 Instant noodle seasoning powder 1 packet (if not using, add 1 extra tablespoon of light soy sauce and a pinch of salt)  
🍶 Vinegar 1.5 tablespoons  
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon  
🧄 Garlic powder 1 teaspoon (or substitute with minced garlic)  
🧂 Cumin powder 2-3 teaspoons (key for flavor; can substitute with BBQ seasoning)  
🧂 MSG A pinch  
💧 Water 3 tablespoons  
🫒 Cooking oil As needed (for greasing the pan, scrambling eggs, and stir-frying)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick pan or wok  
🔥 Gas stove or induction cooktop  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥣 Strainer  
🥣 Bowls (for mixing sauce and beating eggs)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧤 Grater  

## Instructions
1. Place the cabbage flat-side down and thinly shred it. Slice the onion crosswise into shreds, grate the carrot into shreds, wash and separate the baby bok choy, slice the ham sausages, and chop the scallions and cilantro. Set aside.
2. In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, oyster sauce, instant noodle seasoning powder, vinegar, sugar, garlic powder, cumin powder, MSG, and water. Mix thoroughly to make the sauce. Set aside.
3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the noodle cakes and cook over medium heat until the noodles just begin to loosen. Immediately remove and rinse under cold water, then drain well. This step makes the noodles firmer and prevents them from clumping or sticking during stir-frying.
4. Heat a little oil in the pan until it reaches about 70% hot. Pour in the beaten eggs and gently stir until set. For a crispy texture, cook a little longer. Once the eggs are done, remove and set aside.
5. Using the remaining oil in the pan, add the onion shreds and sliced ham sausages. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the onions become soft and translucent with slightly charred edges, and the sausages release their oil and aroma.
6. First, add the grated carrot and stir-fry until softened. Then add the cabbage shreds and stir-fry over medium heat until softened.
7. Pour the prepared sauce into the pan and stir-fry quickly to combine. If it seems like it might burn, immediately add a small amount of water to deglaze. Reduce to medium heat.
8. Add the drained noodles, mung bean sprouts, and baby bok choy. Increase heat to high and stir-fry vigorously, ensuring the noodles are evenly coated with the sauce and the vegetables are just cooked.
9. Return the scrambled eggs to the pan. Sprinkle in the chopped scallions and cilantro. Using chopsticks and a spatula, continue stir-frying over high heat until the noodles are dry and evenly colored, and the wok hei (breath of the wok) is fully developed. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips  
This chow mein is rich in vegetables and has moderate protein, making it ideal for a quick lunch or dinner. Rinsing the noodles in cold water makes them firmer, but instant noodles are high in sodium, so reducing the seasoning packet amount is recommended.
