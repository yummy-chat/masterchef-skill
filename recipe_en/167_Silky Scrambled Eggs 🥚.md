# Silky Scrambled Eggs 🥚
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 4 eggs
🥛 A little milk (to adjust egg mixture tenderness)
🫒 A little cooking oil (to grease the pan and prevent sticking)
🧂 Black pepper, to taste (freshly ground, sprinkle after plating)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Measuring cup (for beating and mixing eggs)
🍳 Tamagoyaki pan or square non-stick pan
🥄 Silicone spatula
🧂 Pepper grinder (for black pepper)

## Steps
1. Crack the 4 eggs into a measuring cup, add a little milk, and whisk thoroughly until the egg mixture is smooth.
2. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat, brush with a thin layer of oil, and pour in the egg mixture.
3. Immediately use a silicone spatula to continuously stir the egg mixture in one direction, ensuring even heating.
   **Note**: Maintain medium-low heat throughout to prevent the bottom from setting too quickly.
4. When the eggs are half-set and still moist, immediately turn off the heat and transfer the silky scrambled eggs to a plate.
   **Key point**: Do not wait until the eggs are fully set before removing them from the pan; the residual heat from the plate will continue to cook them. Overcooking will result in a tougher texture.
5. After plating, sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper to taste, and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
The secret to silky scrambled eggs lies in adding milk to the egg mixture, making them ideal for elderly people and children who need high protein and easy digestion. Be sure to stir over medium-low heat throughout, and do not wait until fully cooked before removing from the pan—let the residual heat from the plate finish the cooking process.
