# Old-Style Large Spicy Slices
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Condiments
🫘 Dried tofu skin 500 grams (cut open for easier flavor absorption)
🌱 White sesame seeds 50 grams
🌿 Cumin powder 30 grams
🌿 Cumin seeds 30 grams
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for frying spice oil)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for frying spice oil)
🌶️ Chili flakes 200 grams (fine powder form)
🧂 Salt 15 grams
🌿 Five-spice powder 5 grams
💧 Water 70 milliliters (for making syrup)
🍬 White sugar 150 grams (for making syrup)
🫒 Cooking oil 400 grams (for frying spice oil)
⭐ Star anise 5 grams
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorns 10 grams
🪵 Cinnamon stick 4 grams
🍃 Bay leaves 1 gram
🌿 Arnebia root 5 grams (optional, for enhancing color and shine)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
✂️ Scissors
🍲 Large pot (for soaking tofu skin)
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing dry ingredients and chili oil)
🧺 Drying rack (for air-drying tofu skin)
🍳 Wok (for making syrup and frying spice oil)
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥢 Strainer (for removing spice residue)
🧤 Disposable gloves
📦 Vacuum sealing bags and vacuum sealer

## Preparation Steps
1. Use scissors to cut the dried tofu skin into three equal parts, and cut each part in half for better flavor absorption.
2. Place the cut tofu skin into a large pot, add enough cold water to fully submerge, soak for 2 minutes, then immediately remove. Spread it out on a ventilated drying rack and let it air-dry until the surface is moisture-free.
   **Note**: Do not extend the soaking time, otherwise the texture will become too soft; avoid direct sunlight or prolonged drying, as this may make the tofu skin hard.
3. In a glass bowl, mix the chili flakes, white sesame seeds, cumin powder, cumin seeds, salt, and five-spice powder to create the dry mixture. Set aside.
   **Key**: Cumin seeds are essential; they are the soul of the flavor.
4. Add water and white sugar to a wok, heat over low heat while stirring continuously, until the sugar is completely dissolved and the syrup becomes thick and sticky.
5. Pour the hot syrup into the dry mixture, and quickly stir with chopsticks to evenly combine, enhancing the freshness and flavor.
6. In a separate wok, pour in the cooking oil and heat over high heat. Add scallions, ginger, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and arnebia root. Reduce to low heat and fry slowly until the spices turn yellow and release a rich aroma. Then, use a strainer to remove all spice residue, retaining the spice oil.
   **Note**: Arnebia root can be added according to preference, used to brighten the color.
7. Gradually pour the hot spice oil into the chili flake mixture, stirring quickly after each addition to prevent the chili from burning due to adding it all at once.
8. Once the chili oil has cooled to room temperature, put on disposable gloves. Take the air-dried tofu skin, layer it in a bowl, and flip and mix repeatedly, ensuring each piece is evenly coated with the oil mixture.
   **Tip**: You can work with several pieces at a time, stacking them to allow the oil mixture to be sealed between layers; if there is insufficient oil, heat additional oil to supplement.
9. Place the mixed spicy slices into vacuum sealing bags, seal with a vacuum sealer to extend the storage time.

## Health Advice
These spicy slices are high in oil, sugar, and calories. It is recommended to enjoy them occasionally as a snack and not consume too much at once. For those prone to getting overheated from spicy food, pair with yogurt or herbal tea to avoid discomfort.
