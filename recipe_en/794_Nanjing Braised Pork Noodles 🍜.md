# Nanjing Braised Pork Noodles 🍜
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥩
- 1 piece of pork belly (choose a whole piece with cartilage)
- Appropriate amount of green vegetables (such as Shanghai bok choy) 🥬
- Appropriate amount of noodles (one serving) 🍜
- Appropriate amount of scallions (for blanching and stir-frying) 🧅
- Appropriate amount of ginger (for blanching and stir-frying) 🫚
- A large amount of garlic (a whole bowl of garlic cloves, for stir-frying) 🧄
- Appropriate amount of yellow wine (1 tbsp for blanching, 1 tbsp for braising) 🍶
- Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce (for coloring the pork skin and adjusting color during braising) 🧂
- Approximately 12 ml of camellia oil (for stir-frying) 🫒
- Appropriate amount of dried spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, dried tangerine peel, cardamom, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, etc.) 🌿
- 1 tbsp of soy sauce (for braising, plus a little extra for seasoning the noodle bowl) 🧂
- Premade caramel color (optional) 🍯
- 1 tsp of white sugar (for braising, plus a little extra for seasoning) 🍬
- 1 tsp of chicken powder (for braising, plus a little extra for seasoning) 🧂
- 1 tsp of white pepper (for braising, plus a little extra for seasoning) 🌶️
- Appropriate amount of salt (added at the end of braising, plus a little extra for seasoning) 🧂
- Appropriate amount of lard (from the braising broth, used for assembly) 🐷

## Kitchenware & Equipment 🍳
- Wok or shallow pan
- Stove
- Slotted spoon 🥄
- Bowl or basin 🥣
- Strainer 🫗
- Chopsticks 🥢
- Fork 🍴
- Paper towels 🧻
- Knife and cutting board 🔪
- Lid 🫙
- Ladle 🥄
- Stainless steel container 🫙

## Cooking Steps
1. Cut the pork belly into pieces, place in cold water, add scallions, ginger, and yellow wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, cover, and reduce to low heat; cook for 40 minutes until a chopstick can be easily inserted.  
**Key Point**: Cook until a chopstick can be easily inserted, indicating the meat is initially tender.

2. Remove the meat and drain. Strain the pork cooking liquid through a strainer and reserve. Use a fork to poke dense holes into the pork skin, then wipe off the surface oil and moisture with paper towels. Evenly spread dark soy sauce on the pork skin and let it sit to absorb color.  
**Key Point**: Poking holes helps release fat; after spreading dark soy sauce, let it sit for a moment to let the color penetrate.

3. Trim the edges of the colored pork belly and cut into slices about 1 cm thick.
4. Heat the wok, add camellia oil. Over medium heat, stir-fry ginger and garlic cloves until fragrant, then add scallion segments and fry until the surface is slightly charred. Finally, add all the dried spices and stir-fry until aromatic.
5. Heat the oil until small bubbles form around a chopstick when inserted. Use two chopsticks, crossed, to hold the whole pork belly piece and lower it into the oil. Immediately cover with a lid. Fry over high heat until the crackling sound weakens and a slight caramelized sugar scent is detected, then reduce to medium heat. When the pork skin is uniformly reddish-brown, turn off the heat. Carefully flip the meat, use paper towels to blot the liquid from the other side, cover, and turn on the heat again to fry the other side until evenly colored. Remove and drain.  
**Safety Key**: Cover immediately after placing the meat in the oil to prevent hot oil splatters and burns.

6. Place the fried meat slices into the wok with the stir-fried aromatics. Add soy sauce, yellow wine, the strained pork cooking liquid, dark soy sauce, caramel color, white sugar, chicken powder, and white pepper. Bring to a boil over high heat, cover, and reduce to the lowest heat; simmer for at least 40 minutes. Uncover, taste, and adjust seasoning with salt or sugar. Then increase to high heat and boil vigorously for 5 minutes to meld the flavors.
7. Cook the noodles in boiling water until done, and blanch the green vegetables at the same time. In each noodle bowl, add a little salt, sugar, soy sauce, white pepper, and chicken powder. Pour in one ladle of the noodle cooking water to dissolve, then add one ladle of the braising broth with lard. Place the noodles in the bowl, arrange the greens and a large meat slice on top, and finally drizzle with one more ladle of braising broth. Serve.

## Health Advice
This braised pork noodle dish is rich in protein and energy, making it suitable for those with high physical exertion or a craving for a hearty meal. However, pork belly is high in fat, so pairing it with green vegetables is recommended for balanced nutrition. People with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
