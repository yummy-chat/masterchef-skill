# Perfect Thousand-Layer Cake
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍠 Water chestnut flour 200g (100g for yellow batter, 100g for white batter)
💧 Water 830g (550g for yellow batter, 280g for white batter)
🥛 Milk 135g
🥥 Coconut milk 135g
🟣 Purple sweet potato powder 20g
🟢 Pandan powder 20g
⚫ Black sesame paste 60g
🍓 Strawberry puree, as needed
🌸 Osmanthus honey 60g
🍯 Yellow rock sugar 120g
🍬 White granulated sugar 80g

## Utensils and Equipment
🥤 Disposable plastic cups
🥣 Large bowl
🫗 Sieve
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan/wok
♨️ Steamer
📦 Stainless steel square mold
🥄 Spoon/chopsticks
🔥 Gas stove

## Procedure
1. Prepare yellow raw batter: Combine 100g water chestnut flour with approximately 275g water in a bowl. Stir well, then strain through a sieve to obtain a smooth raw batter.
2. Prepare yellow cooked batter: Add the remaining 275g water and 120g yellow rock sugar to a pot. Bring to a boil until the sugar completely dissolves. While boiling, quickly scoop one ladle of raw batter into the pot, stirring rapidly to form a thin paste-like cooked batter. Immediately pour this cooked batter back into the remaining raw batter and stir quickly until well combined.
**Note**: The sugar water must be boiling; the scalding process should be fast to prevent lumps. The final batter should have a slightly thick, rice-soup-like consistency but still be pourable.
3. Prepare white raw batter: Combine the other 100g water chestnut flour, 135g milk, and 135g coconut milk in a bowl. Stir well, then strain through a sieve.
4. Prepare white cooked batter: Add 280g water and 80g white granulated sugar to a pot. Bring to a boil, then similarly scald one ladle of white raw batter, and pour it back into the remaining raw batter, stirring quickly.
5. Mix colors and flavors: Take appropriate amounts of the base white batter and separately add purple sweet potato powder, pandan powder, black sesame paste, strawberry puree, and osmanthus honey. Stir each into different colored batters.
6. Preheat steamer and mold: Bring water in the steamer to a boil. Place the stainless steel square mold inside to preheat briefly.
7. Steam in layers: First, pour one ladle of colored batter into the bottom of the mold and spread it evenly. Steam over high heat for about 2 minutes until the surface sets and changes color. Then pour one ladle of white coconut batter, gently shake to level, and continue steaming until set.
**Key point**: Ensure the previous layer is completely set before adding the next, otherwise the layers will become cloudy and unclear.
8. Alternate and repeat: Maintain the order of "one colored layer + one white layer," steaming each layer until the mold is nearly full.
9. Cool and unmold: Once steaming is complete, remove and let it cool completely. Gently loosen the edges of the mold with your fingers, then invert to unmold.
**Note**: If not fully cooled, the cake will be sticky and difficult to unmold cleanly.
10. Cut into pieces: Use a knife to cut into bite-sized pieces or slices. Enjoy the distinct layers, which are springy, soft, and chewy.

## Health Advice
This thousand-layer cake has a high sugar and carbohydrate content, making it suitable as a dessert rather than a staple. Natural ingredients like purple sweet potato and pandan are rich in antioxidants, but portion control is necessary. It is recommended to pair it with unsweetened tea to balance the sweetness. Those with weak digestion should consume in moderation to avoid bloating.
