# Bean Curd Skin with Beef Tripe Skewers

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare spice water**: Place cumin seeds, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon bark, Sichuan peppercorns, and black cardamom (seeds removed) into a bowl. Pour in 500g of boiling water and let steep for 15 minutes. Then, strain out the spice water using a strainer and allow it to cool completely.
2. **Prepare ingredients**: Cut the bean curd skin and beef tripe separately, and thread them onto bamboo skewers.
3. **Mix the sesame sauce**: In a bowl, combine 6 parts sesame paste, 1 part light soy sauce, 2 parts sesame oil, 1 part oyster sauce, and an适量 amount of salt. Stir vigorously with a spoon, **always in one direction**. Gradually add the cooled spice water in small amounts, continuing to stir in the same direction until the sauce can **coat the spoon and drip in a steady stream**. Finally, mix in the minced garlic and oil-poured chili flakes to create a glossy, rich sauce.
4. **Blanch**: Fill a pot with water, add scallion segments and ginger slices, and bring to a boil. Add the bean curd skin and beef tripe skewers, and blanch quickly over high heat until just cooked through. Remove and drain.
5. **Coat with sauce**: Arrange the blanched skewers flat on a plate. While still hot, pour the prepared sesame sauce over them. Gently turn the skewers with a spoon or chopsticks to ensure each is evenly coated with the sauce.
6. **Garnish**: Sprinkle with a small amount of chopped scallions and serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Beef tripe is low in fat and high in protein, while bean curd skin is rich in calcium. However, sesame paste is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to control the portion size. This dish is suitable for those needing iron and protein supplementation. Individuals with weaker digestive systems should ensure the beef tripe is thoroughly blanched.
