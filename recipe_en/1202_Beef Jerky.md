# Beef Jerky
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 1000 grams beef (soak in water for 2 hours in advance to draw out blood, then rinse clean)  
🫚 Ginger slices, to taste  
⭐ 3 star anise  
🍃 A few bay leaves  
🌸 A handful of Sichuan peppercorns  
🌿 A handful of cumin seeds  
🧂 1 tablespoon ground white pepper  
🧂 1 tablespoon five-spice powder  
🧂 2 tablespoons salt  
🍬 1 tablespoon sugar  
🥄 3 tablespoons light soy sauce  
🍶 3 tablespoons cooking wine  
💧 1200 milliliters water (enough to submerge the beef)  
🌿 1 large tablespoon cumin powder (adjust to taste)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl (for marinating)  
🔪 Chef’s knife + cutting board  
🍳 Stewing pot  
🔥 Oven + baking rack  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫙 Plastic wrap  

## Steps
1. Cut the beef into long strips along the muscle fiber grain, then cut into smaller pieces and place in a glass bowl.
   **Key Point**: Cut along the grain to prevent the jerky from becoming loose and breaking apart after baking.

2. Add ginger slices, ground white pepper, five-spice powder, salt, sugar, light soy sauce, and cooking wine. Mix thoroughly with chopsticks.
   **Note**: Use a slightly generous amount of seasoning; otherwise, the finished product will be bland.

3. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours to marinate.
   **Key Point**: Thorough marinating is essential for the beef to become fully infused with flavor.

4. Transfer the marinated beef to a stew pot, add water, and then star anise, bay leaves, Sichuan peppercorns, and cumin seeds. Stir to combine.

5. Cover with a lid, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 40 minutes.
   **Note**: Cooking time can be adjusted based on the size of the meat pieces; the beef is done when fully cooked through.

6. After cooking, remove the beef, trim off any surface connective tissue, place in a bowl, add cumin powder, and toss to coat evenly.
   **Key Point**: Connective tissue affects texture and must be removed; the amount of cumin powder can be increased or decreased according to personal taste.

7. Arrange the seasoned beef evenly on a baking rack, place in the oven, and bake at 140°C for 80 minutes.
   **Note**: Baking time can be adjusted based on the size of the meat pieces and your desired level of dryness.

8. Once baked, remove from the oven and let cool to room temperature before serving.

## Health Tips  
This beef jerky is a high-protein, low-fat fitness snack suitable for post-workout energy replenishment. However, it has a relatively high salt content, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Homemade beef jerky contains fewer additives than store-bought versions, but be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive sodium intake.
