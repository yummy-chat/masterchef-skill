# Hong Kong-Style Wonton Soup Base
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Chicken carcass – appropriate amount (fresh, with fat, lymph nodes, lungs, kidneys, and trachea removed)
🦴 Pork leg bone – 2 jin (fresh)
🐟 Dried flounder – 200g (scorched lightly using a blowtorch)
🦐 Dried shrimp – 200g (use seasonal dried shrimp to avoid mustiness; shrimp roe can be used as a substitute)
🫚 Ginger – 50g (crushed)
🍶 Cooking wine – 80g
🧂 Salt – to taste
🐟 Fish sauce – to taste
🍬 Rock sugar – about 50g

## Cookware & Equipment
🪣 Stainless steel basin (for cleaning ingredients)
🥘 Stockpot (aluminum or stainless steel, for simmering stock)
🔥 Gas stove
🔫 Blowtorch (for scorching dried flounder)
🍳 Iron wok (frying pan, for dry-frying dried shrimp)
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer (for filtering out solids)

## Preparation Steps
1. Rinse the chicken carcass thoroughly under running water. Carefully remove any fat, lymph nodes from the neck, as well as the lungs, kidneys, and any remaining trachea from the interior.
**Key point**: The chicken carcass must be thoroughly cleaned to avoid any off-flavors or fishiness in the stock.

2. Place the cleaned chicken carcass together with 2 jin of fresh pork leg bones in clean water and wash them thoroughly.
3. Put the washed chicken carcass and pork leg bones into a pot and add cold water.
**Note**: Always start with cold water.

4. Add 50g of crushed ginger and 80g of cooking wine.
5. Bring the pot to a boil over high heat.
6. Prepare the dried flounder: Place 200g of dried flounder on a tray and use a blowtorch to scorch the surface until it has a few burnt brown spots.
**Key point**: If you don’t have a blowtorch, an oven or charcoal fire can be used instead. Scorching the flounder enhances the aroma of the stock.

7. Add the scorched dried flounder into the boiling stockpot.
8. In a separate dry iron wok (without oil or water), add 200g of dried shrimp and dry-fry over low heat for about 8 minutes, until the shrimp are crispy and fragrant.
**Note**: You must use seasonal dried shrimp. Any mustiness will severely affect the quality of the stock base.

9. Add the fried dried shrimp to the stockpot.
10. Reduce the heat to low and continue to simmer for approximately 4 hours.
11. After 4 hours of simmering, add salt to taste, fish sauce to taste, and about 50g of rock sugar. Stir until the rock sugar is completely dissolved to season and enhance the flavor.
12. Strain the stock through a fine mesh strainer to remove all solids, obtaining a clear stock.

## Health Advice
This stock is rich in collagen and astaxanthin, suitable for those needing calcium supplementation or flavor enhancement. Note that the chicken carcass must be thoroughly defatted. Gout sufferers should consume in moderation.
