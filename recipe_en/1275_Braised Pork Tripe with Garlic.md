# Braised Pork Tripe with Garlic
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐷 1 pork tripe
🧄 About half a bowl of whole garlic cloves (large ones cut in half)
🥬 Some asparagus lettuce (cut into strips)
🫚 Some ginger (sliced)
🧅 Some scallions (cut into sections)
🧂 Some salt
🍶 Some cooking wine
🧂 Some MSG
🌾 Some water starch (for thickening)
🫒 Some scallion oil (drizzle before serving)
🐖 Some lard (for stir-frying)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🪣 Enamel basin
🔪 Chef's knife, scissors
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Spoon
🍲 Clay pot
🚰 Faucet

## Cooking Steps
1. Place the pork tripe in a basin. Add salt and flour and rub it vigorously, paying special attention to the folds. Use the flour to absorb the slime and grease. Then rinse clean under running water. Repeat 2-3 times until the tripe feels smooth and odorless.
2. Blanch the cleaned tripe in boiling water briefly. Remove and use a knife to scrape off the white membrane on the surface. Use scissors to trim off any excess fat.
3. Place the processed tripe in a pot of cold water. Add ginger, scallions, and cooking wine. Cover and simmer for 1.5 hours. Once cooked, remove and immediately submerge in ice water to cool and prevent discoloration. Then cut into even strips.
4. Cut large garlic cloves in half. Slice the ginger. Cut the scallions into sections. Cut the asparagus lettuce into strips.
5. Heat lard in a wok. Add the sliced ginger, scallion sections, and whole garlic cloves. Stir-fry until fragrant. Then add the pork tripe strips and stir-fry until the aroma releases.
**Key Point**: Using lard makes the dish whiter in color and richer in fragrance.
6. Pour in the original broth used to cook the tripe. Add the asparagus lettuce strips. Season with salt and MSG. Cook over medium heat for 3-5 minutes until the asparagus lettuce is tender yet still slightly crisp.
**Key Point**: The original broth retains the tripe's umami. Keep the seasoning light to highlight the "clean, white, and bright" flavor profile.
7. Thicken with water starch, stirring until the sauce coats the ingredients evenly. Finally, drizzle with scallion oil, and transfer to a clay pot before serving.
**Note**: Drizzle the scallion oil quickly after thickening to enhance the aroma and sheen, resulting in a white, rich sauce.

## Health Advice
Pork tripe is rich in collagen and protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. However, individuals with high cholesterol should consume it in moderation. Using flour to rub the tripe during preparation helps effectively remove any unpleasant odor. After braising, the texture becomes soft and tender, making it easier to digest.
