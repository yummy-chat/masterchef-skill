# Convenience Store-Style Oden
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Dried baby shrimp 20g (5 spoonfuls, not too salty)
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms 20g (8 small ones; reduce quantity if large)
🥬 Napa cabbage stalks 350g (about half a head, can include a few leaves)
🍎 Apple 250g (about 1 medium apple, halved and deseeded)
🧅 Onion 250g (about 2/3 of a medium onion, halved)
🌿 Thick kelp (kombu) 40g (about 3 palm-sized pieces, wipe clean only, do not wash)
💧 Water 4500ml (about 15 household bowls)
🥕 Daikon radish, as needed (cut into thick rounds, trim edges, optional crosshatch score)
🪢 Kelp knots, as needed
🍢 Arctic wings (fish cake), as needed
🍢 Tempura fish cake, as needed
🍢 Meatballs (e.g., fish balls), as needed (blanched beforehand)
🍶 Sake 30ml (3 spoonfuls, can substitute with 1 spoonful of high-proof liquor)
🍘 Bonito flakes 10g (2 large pinches)
🐟 Bonito powder (katsuobushi powder) 15g (1.5 level spoonfuls)
🥢 Light soy sauce 20g (2 spoonfuls)
🍶 Mirin 20g (2 spoonfuls, optional if unavailable)
♨️ Boiling water, as needed (for rinsing dried shrimp and shiitake mushrooms)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🫗 Large glass cooking pot (capacity > 4500ml)
🥢 Strainer
🪣 Stainless steel small basin
🥣 Household rice bowl (for measuring water, 1 bowl ≈ 300ml)
🔪 Kitchen knife + cutting board
🔥 Gas stove / induction cooker
🥄 Measuring spoon / small bowl

## Steps
1. Place dried shrimp and dried shiitake mushrooms in a container. Pour in boiling water to soak and rinse for about 1 minute until mushrooms soften. Drain and set aside.
2. Wipe the thick kelp gently with a damp cloth to clean surface dust. Cut into large pieces and set aside.
3. Prepare napa cabbage stalks, apple, and onion as per quantities. Cut daikon radish into thick rounds and trim edges; optional crosshatch score on the surface. Blanch meatballs to remove surface oil. Set aside all skewered ingredients.
4. Add water to a large pot. Add napa cabbage stalks, apple, onion, kelp, dried shrimp, and shiitake mushrooms. Pour in sake. Bring to a boil over high heat.
   **Note**: If sake is unavailable, substitute with 1 spoonful of high-proof liquor; alcohol will evaporate during cooking.
5. Reduce to low heat, keeping the pot uncovered, and simmer slowly for 90 minutes.
   **Key point**: Initial fishy aroma from shiitake mushrooms and kelp is normal; it will dissipate later. Final soup volume should be about 2500ml.
6. Turn off the heat. Add light soy sauce, mirin, and bonito powder, stirring well. Then add bonito flakes, gently pressing them into the soup with a spoon. Cover with a lid and let steep for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Do not boil bonito flakes. Steeping after turning off the heat releases their umami and smoky flavor.
7. Strain the broth using a strainer. Gently press the vegetables to extract liquid, but do not crush them forcefully to avoid cloudy soup.
8. Bring strained broth to a boil again. Add daikon radish first. Reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and cook for 20 minutes.
   **Note**: Cooking daikon radish first ensures it becomes tender and flavorful.
9. Add all skewered ingredients. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes. Ready to serve.
   **Tip**: Leftover broth can be portioned and frozen for reuse, such as cooking udon noodles.

## Health Advice
This oden broth achieves natural sweetness from apples and vegetables, with reduced salt and low fat. Suitable for those managing weight or seeking a light diet. It is recommended to cook daikon radish before adding skewers to prevent them from disintegrating during prolonged simmering. Leftover broth can be frozen and reused as a versatile soup base.
