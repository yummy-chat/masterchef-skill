# Crispy Pork Cutlet with Soft Scrambled Eggs over Rice
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork shoulder (blade steak) 1 piece (thick-cut)
🧅 Yellow onion half (sliced)
🥚 Eggs about 5 (2 for coating the pork, 3 for pouring into the pan)
🍚 Rice as needed (cooked for serving over rice)
🌾 Flour as needed
🍞 Breadcrumbs as needed
🧂 Salt generous amount
🌶️ Black pepper generous amount
🍶 Mirin 3 parts
🥄 Soy sauce 2 parts
🍶 Sake 1 part
🍬 White sugar 0.5 part

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan or small saucepan
🔪 Knife
🪓 Cutting board
🫗 Grinder
🥢 Tongs
🥄 Slotted spoon or spatula
🧺 Oil strainer or frying basket

## Instructions
1. Cut the pork shoulder into thick slices, and slice the yellow onion. In advance, mix 3 parts mirin, 2 parts soy sauce, 1 part sake, and 0.5 part white sugar to make the sauce and set aside. Also prepare flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs.

2. Season both sides of the pork cutlets generously with salt and black pepper. Let marinate for 15 minutes.

3. Coat the marinated pork cutlets in flour, then beaten eggs, and finally breadcrumbs, ensuring an even coating.

4. Heat oil in a pan to 150°C. Fry the pork cutlets for about 7 minutes until the surface is golden brown and the inside is cooked through. When cut, the center should be about 70–80% done. Remove and drain on an oil strainer or frying basket.

5. In another pan, add the sliced yellow onion and the prepared sauce. Bring to a boil and continue cooking for 2–3 minutes.

6. Cut the fried pork cutlets into pieces and add them to the pan. Evenly pour in 2–3 beaten eggs, and sprinkle with chopped scallions.

7. Cover with a lid and simmer until the egg surface is just slightly set. Remove from heat. The eggs should remain soft and half-cooked, not fully set.

8. Serve the finished pork cutlet with soft scrambled eggs and its broth over steamed rice.

## Health Advice
This crispy pork cutlet with soft scrambled eggs over rice is rich in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure or those looking to build muscle. However, the fried portion and sugar content are relatively high, so it is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad to balance the calories. When frying the pork cutlet, be careful to control the oil temperature to avoid excessive oil absorption. Keeping the eggs half-cooked enhances their smooth and tender texture.
