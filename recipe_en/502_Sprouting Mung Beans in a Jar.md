# Sprouting Mung Beans in a Jar
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Mung Beans 1/3 cup (for sprouting)
🧂 Seasonings None

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍶 Glass jar (with sprouting lid) or stainless steel cup
🩹 Gauze/Kitchen paper
🔗 Rubber band (to secure a homemade lid)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for rinsing and draining)
🔲 Baking tray (to catch excess water)
🥢 Chopsticks (to elevate the container)
🛍️ Kraft paper bag (to block out light)
🥄 Strainer
📦 Airtight container (for storage)
🧻 Kitchen paper (to line the bottom for storage)

## Steps
1.  Prepare the sprouting container. You can use a dedicated sprouting jar with a mesh lid, or cover the jar mouth with gauze or kitchen paper and secure it tightly with a rubber band to ensure breathability and drainage. If using a light-proof stainless steel cup, it is more conducive to sprouting in darkness.
2.  Place 1/3 cup of mung beans into the container and add clean water for a gentle rinse.
3.  Add enough clean water to soak the beans for 8 hours or overnight, until the mung beans have visibly absorbed water and expanded.
4.  Discard all the soaking water. Tilt or invert the container over a baking tray, elevating it with chopsticks to allow excess water to drain out and maintain air circulation.
5.  Completely cover the container with a kraft paper bag, or place it in a dark environment, to simulate soil conditions and encourage whiter, more tender sprouts.
6.  Rinse the beans twice a day (morning and evening). After pouring off the water, be sure to drain thoroughly again. Keep them moist but ensure there is absolutely no standing water. When rinsing, smell the sprouts; they should only have a fresh scent, no musty odor.
7.  Around days 5-6, if you want the mung bean sprouts to develop green leaves, move the container near a window to receive indirect light for photosynthesis.
8.  After about 6 days, when the sprouts have reached your desired length, remove them. Rinse in clean water to remove any loose seed coats, and drain well.
9.  Line the bottom of an airtight container with a layer of kitchen paper, place the sprouts inside, cover, and refrigerate. They can be stored for about one week.
   **Key Points**: Throughout the process, avoid grease and stuffy heat. Use clean water for rinsing, and ensure thorough drainage when inverting the container, as standing water will easily cause rot.

## Health Advice
These mung bean sprouts are a prime example of "zero-calorie cooking," completed only with water and containing no oil or salt. They are rich in Vitamin C and dietary fiber, making them especially suitable for weight loss periods or for regulating the digestive system.
