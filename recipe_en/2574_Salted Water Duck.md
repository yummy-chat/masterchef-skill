# Salted Water Duck
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck legs 5 pieces
🌿 Bay leaves appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount
🧂 Salt about 150 grams
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
🌟 Star anise 2 pieces
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) appropriate amount

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🍳 Wok
🍲 Stainless steel stockpot
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥄 Soup ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the duck legs in cold water to fully draw out blood, then remove and drain.
**Key point**: Thoroughly releasing blood effectively reduces gamey odor.
2. Place a wok on the stove (cold), add salt, Sichuan peppercorns, and star anise. Stir-fry over low heat for about 10 minutes until the salt grains turn slightly yellow and release fragrance, then turn off the heat and let cool.
**Note**: Keep on low heat throughout to avoid burning the spices.
3. Pour the cooled roasted salt into the basin with the duck legs. Add ginger slices, scallion segments, and baijiu. Rub the duck legs repeatedly with your hands to evenly distribute the seasonings. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight to marinate.
**Key point**: Rubbing helps seasonings penetrate; adequate marinating time is essential.
4. Add enough water to the stockpot, then add ginger slices, scallion knots, star anise, bay leaves, and Sichuan peppercorns. Heat to about 80°C (176°F), then immediately reduce to the lowest heat.
**Key point**: The water temperature must not boil, or the duck meat will become loose.
5. Gently place the marinated duck legs into the pot. Cook over the lowest heat for about 40 minutes, maintaining a very gentle simmer on the surface.
**Note**: Do not cover the pot during cooking.
6. Remove the duck legs and place them on a cutting board to cool naturally until no longer hot to the touch.
7. Chop the duck legs into small pieces, arrange neatly on a plate, drizzle a spoonful of the original poaching liquid over the top, and garnish with cilantro.
**Key point**: Adding the poaching liquid enhances moisture and flavor of the duck meat.

## Health Advice
Salted water duck is low in fat and rich in protein, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those managing calorie intake. However, since the marinade is high in salt, it is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance sodium consumption. Maintaining a gentle simmer during cooking locks in the tenderness of the meat, and letting it cool before chopping helps it hold its shape better.
