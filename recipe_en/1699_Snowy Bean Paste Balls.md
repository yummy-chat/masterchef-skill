# Snowy Bean Paste Balls  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Year  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 7 eggs (use 255g egg whites, refrigerated)  
🌽 80g cornstarch (used in two batches: once for egg white batter, once for coating bean paste balls to prevent sticking)  
🫘 Red bean paste filling, about 25g each (store-bought bagged red bean paste)  
🍋 About 4g lemon juice (added to egg whites to stabilize the protein)  
🍬 Powdered sugar, to taste (for garnish after cooking, original flavor base)  
🍵 Matcha powder, to taste (optional flavor)  
🍫 Cocoa powder, to taste (optional flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
♨️ Induction cooktop (can precisely control power)  
🌡️ Infrared thermometer  
🥣 Large bowl (oil-free and water-free)  
🥄 Soup spoon (for shaping, scooping batter, lowering into oil, and drizzling oil to flip)  
🥢 Slotted spoon  
🫙 Sieve  
🍽️ Plate (for sprinkling cornstarch)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Separate egg whites: Crack the refrigerated eggs and separate out about 255g of egg whites. **Note: All containers that touch the egg whites must be completely free of oil, water, and raw water.**  
2. Prepare bean paste balls: Divide the bagged red bean paste into small balls of about 25g each. Spread a layer of cornstarch on a plate, place the bean paste balls on it, then sprinkle another layer of cornstarch on top. Shake the plate to coat the balls evenly with cornstarch, then firmly and evenly roll each ball by hand. **Key: This step prevents sticking and prepares the balls for coating with batter.**  
3. Whip egg whites: Add about 4g lemon juice to the egg whites. Use the electric hand mixer on the highest speed to whip for about 2 minutes, moving the mixer continuously. Whip until the egg whites are very fine, with no visible bubbles, and a pair of chopsticks inserted can stand upright without falling—this indicates success.  
4. Prepare the batter: Add all 80g of cornstarch at once to the whipped egg whites. Use the hand mixer on the lowest speed to mix until combined. **Judgment standard: The batter should remain stable and show no significant changes whether folded upward or downward.**  
5. Prepare the oil pan: Pour enough oil into the pan. Use the induction cooktop at high power to heat the oil to 100°C (212°F), then immediately reduce to 200W low power, maintaining the oil temperature between 80–100°C (176–212°F).  
6. Shape and lower into oil: Take one bean paste ball. First place a layer of egg white batter on a soup spoon, put the bean paste ball on it, then completely cover it with more batter, smoothing it out and shaping it as round as possible. **Note: Keep the bean paste ball centered in the batter—do not press it down, to avoid it being off-center while frying.** Gently slide the shaped raw ball into the low-temperature oil using the spoon, repeating for each ball.  
7. Low-temperature frying: After the raw balls enter the oil, use the spoon to drizzle oil over them and gently flip them every 2 minutes. Maintain 200W power throughout, frying for 10–15 minutes, until they puff up, float, and remain white in color. **Key: The first flip must be very gentle—use oil drizzling to turn them, preventing breakage; after they set, flipping can be slightly bolder.**  
8. Remove and garnish: Remove using a slotted spoon. If needed, pat dry with paper towels to absorb surface oil. Plate, then sieve powdered sugar over the original flavor version. Alternatively, first sieve powdered sugar, then add matcha powder or cocoa powder.

## Health Advice  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for muscle gain or calorie control. However, the deep-frying process and the sugar content of the bean paste mean it is not low in calories. It is recommended to consume no more than 3 pieces per serving to avoid exceeding caloric limits.
