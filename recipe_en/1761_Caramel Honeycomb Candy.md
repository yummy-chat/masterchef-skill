# Caramel Honeycomb Candy
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🍬 White sugar 100g
💧 Water 50g

## Seasonings
🧂 Baking soda 3g

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Small milk pot
✂️ Plastic water bottle (cut open to use as a measuring cup)
🥢 Chopsticks
📜 Baking sheet or flat pan (lined with parchment paper)
🫙 Glass jar (for airtight storage)

## Instructions
1. Cut a plastic bottle in half, use the bottom half, and use the groove on the bottle as a measurement reference to create a simple measuring cup.
2. Use the homemade measuring cup to measure 100g of white sugar and 50g of water, and set aside 3g of baking soda.
3. Pour the white sugar and water into a small milk pot, and heat over the lowest heat.
   **Note**: Do not stir throughout the process to prevent the syrup from crystallizing. Turn off the heat immediately when the syrup turns yellow to avoid it turning reddish-brown.
4. Immediately after turning off the heat, add the baking soda and quickly stir with chopsticks for about 5 seconds. The syrup will expand rapidly.
5. Stop stirring as soon as the mixture begins to expand, and let it sit naturally in the pot to expand to its maximum volume.
6. When the expansion nears the rim of the pot, quickly pour the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and let it sit for about 20 minutes until completely cool and hard.
7. Cleaning the pot: Add hot water to the pot and bring it to a boil. Once the caramel dissolves, pour it out, and the pot can be washed clean.
8. Once cooled, the caramel honeycomb candy will be golden yellow with a honeycomb texture, crispy and delicious. Break into pieces and eat directly or pair with coffee or milk to make a drink. Store any leftovers in an airtight glass jar.

## Health Advice
This caramel honeycomb candy is extremely high in sugar and contains almost no other nutrients, making it suitable only for occasional indulgence. People with diabetes or those controlling their sugar intake should avoid it. It is high in calorie density, so it is recommended to take only a small piece at a time, paired with unsweetened coffee or milk, to prevent blood sugar fluctuations.
