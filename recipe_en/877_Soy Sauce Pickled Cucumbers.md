# Soy Sauce Pickled Cucumbers
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥒 Cucumbers, 2 jin (about 1 kg), seeded
🌿 Cilantro roots, to taste
🌶️ Red chili peppers (Thai chili), to taste, sliced
🧄 Garlic, to taste, sliced
🫚 Ginger, to taste, sliced
🌼 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste
⭐ Star anise, to taste
🧂 Salt, 15 g
🍬 Sugar, 15 g (10 g for first pickling, 5 g for seasoning liquid)
🥄 Soy sauce, 6 liang (about 300 ml)
🧂 MSG, 3 g (optional)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, to taste (add before serving)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Soup ladle
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥣 Strainer
🪣 Stainless steel basin
🍶 Weight for pressing (e.g., wine bottle, oil container)
🍽️ Stainless steel tray
🥢 Chopsticks
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1. Quickly blanch whole cucumbers in boiling water to remove surface pesticide residue, then immediately remove and cool under cold running water.
**Key point**: Blanching time should be very short; do not boil for long.

2. After cooling, cut cucumbers lengthwise into four strips, remove the central seeded section, then cut crosswise into segments about 1 cun (approx. 3 cm) long.
3. Place cucumber segments in a basin, add 15 g salt, 10 g sugar, and an appropriate amount of cilantro roots. Toss to combine evenly, then let sit for 30 minutes.
**Indicator**: Pickled cucumbers should be pliable enough to bend without breaking.

4. Slice red chili peppers, garlic, and ginger; set aside. Rinse Sichuan peppercorns and star anise with warm water, then drain thoroughly.
5. Pour cold oil into the wok, add the rinsed and drained Sichuan peppercorns and star anise, and fry over low heat slowly until fragrant.
**Key point**: Rinse spices before frying to prevent burning and achieve a purer aroma.

6. After the spices become fragrant, pour in 6 liang (300 ml) soy sauce. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil. Add 5 g sugar and 3 g MSG, stir to dissolve, then immediately turn off the heat.
**Key point**: The seasoning liquid must be completely cooled before use. Do not add vinegar at this stage.

7. Squeeze excess salted water from the pickled cucumber segments, then transfer them to a bag. Place a cutting board on top and weigh it down with a heavy object. Press for about 2 hours to thoroughly remove moisture.

8. Transfer the pressed cucumbers to a large tray. Add sliced red chili peppers, garlic, and ginger, then pour in the completely cooled seasoning liquid. Stir thoroughly to combine.

9. Let the cucumbers marinate at room temperature for 2 hours before serving. For a crisper texture, refrigerate overnight.

10. Before serving, add an appropriate amount of fragrant vinegar according to personal taste.

## Health Tips
This dish is low in calories and high in fiber, making it suitable as a summer appetizer or a side dish during weight loss. Due to its relatively high salt content, individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. Vinegar should be added at the end to preserve its flavor.
