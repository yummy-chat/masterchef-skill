# Old Sugar
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍬 White sugar 200g
💧 Water 100g
🍯 Maltose 50g
🧂 Baking soda 10g

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Stainless steel pot
🥄 Silicone spatula
🍽️ Baking tray
📄 Parchment paper
🔪 Chef’s knife

## Steps
1. Line the baking tray with parchment paper, ensuring all tools and containers are clean and oil-free.
2. Place white sugar, water, and maltose in the pot, heat over medium heat, stirring moderately until the sugar is completely dissolved and the liquid boils.
   **Note**: Do not stir excessively before the sugar is fully dissolved.
3. Continue boiling the syrup, reducing stirring. Use a thermometer; when the syrup reaches 160°C, immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key**: Precise temperature control is crucial; if the color becomes too dark, it will taste bitter.
4. Quickly add the baking soda, stir rapidly and briefly with the spatula until the syrup expands quickly and turns a light caramel color.
   **Note**: Work quickly; stir just until combined, do not overmix.
5. Immediately pour the expanded syrup into the parchment-lined baking tray. Do not touch or stir after pouring.
6. Let it sit undisturbed until completely cooled and set, which takes several hours. Once cooled, the sugar becomes hard, with a dry, non-sticky surface and slight shrinkage.
7. Remove the sugar block from the tray, peel off the parchment paper. Use a knife tip or hands to break or cut into pieces of suitable size.

## Health Tips
This caramel is made of sugar and air, suitable for occasional indulgence, but should be avoided by people with diabetes or those on a weight-loss diet. The baking soda gives it a fluffy texture; rinse your mouth after eating to prevent sugar from sticking to teeth.
