# Yunnan-style Oil-braised Chicken
Cuisine: Yunnan cuisine
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍗 Chicken thighs 2 (boneless, cut into large pieces, retain skin, tendons, and meat)
🥔 Potatoes appropriate amount (cut into large pieces)
🍅 Tomatoes appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (chopped, for garnish at the end)
🫚 Ginger about half a piece (for spice paste)
🧄 Garlic about 8 cloves (for spice paste)
🌶️ Xiaomila (Thai bird's eye chilies) about 15 (for spice paste)
🌿 Lemongrass a small amount (for spice paste)
🌰 Black cardamom 1 (seeds removed, for spice paste; seeds have an off-flavor)
🥛 Milk about 1 can (for simmering)
🧂 Light soy sauce a small amount (for marinating, to avoid too dark color)
🧂 Chicken powder 1 tbsp (for marinating) + 1 tbsp (for seasoning during simmering)
🦪 Oyster sauce 2 tbsp (for marinating)
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp (for marinating)
🫒 Salad oil 1 tbsp (for marinating)
🍬 White sugar half a small spoon (for seasoning during simmering)
🧂 Salt to taste (for seasoning during simmering)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥘 Pot (for boiling potatoes)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🌀 Blender or food processor (for making spice paste)
🍳 Wok
🔥 Clay pot or deep dish (for final serving)
🥢 Spatula

## Steps
**Preparation Stage**
1. Cut potatoes into large pieces, boil or steam until cooked in water used for washing rice.
2. Blend lemongrass, black cardamom, ginger, garlic, and xiaomila in a blender to make spice paste.
3. Debone chicken thighs, cut into large pieces, retain skin, tendons, and meat.
4. Marinate chicken pieces with light soy sauce, chicken powder, oyster sauce, cooking wine, and salad oil. Mix well and let sit for 15–20 minutes.
**Cooking Stage**
1. Pour a generous amount of oil into the wok. Heat the oil to high temperature. Add the marinated chicken pieces and quickly stir-fry until the surface tightens instantly.
**Key point**: Oil-braised chicken requires sufficient oil. High-heat quick-frying seals in the juices of the chicken.

2. When the chicken is about 30–40% cooked, pour in the spice paste. Stir-fry together until fragrant.
**Key point**: Stir-fry until the spice paste releases its aroma and fully integrates with the chicken.

3. Add tomato pieces and cooked potato pieces. Then pour in the entire can of milk.
**Note**: Milk is a special feature of this version. Simmering it with chicken and potatoes creates a rich milky aroma.

4. Add 1 tbsp chicken powder, half a small spoon of white sugar, and salt to taste. Stir to combine.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, until the sauce thickens and dries up, clinging to the potatoes and chicken.
**Key point**: Slow simmering over low heat concentrates the sauce, coating the ingredients with a thick gravy.

6. Turn off the heat. Transfer to a clay pot or deep dish. Sprinkle chopped scallions on top.

## Health Advice
This dish offers a balanced combination of protein and carbohydrates. However, the combination of milk and oil-braising is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a refreshing vegetable dish for balance. The spice paste made from xiaomila and black cardamom can boost metabolism and is suitable for people with cold hands and feet. However, those with weak stomachs should control the spiciness.
