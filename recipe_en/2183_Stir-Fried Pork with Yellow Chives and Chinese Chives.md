# Stir-Fried Pork with Yellow Chives and Chinese Chives
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Yellow chives, as needed (cut into sections)
🥬 Chinese chives, as needed (cut into sections, mixed with yellow chives)
🥩 Pork tenderloin, about 200g (cut into shreds)
🧄 Garlic, half a head (minced)
🥚 Egg, half (for marinating the pork shreds)
🧂 Salt, half a tablespoon
🧂 Chicken powder, one tablespoon
🌿 White pepper powder, as needed
🍶 Fragrant vinegar, as needed
🥄 Light soy sauce, one tablespoon
🥄 Dark soy sauce, one tablespoon
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🍾 Huadiao wine, as needed
🌾 Cornstarch, as needed (used twice, for marinating and thickening)
🛢️ Cooking oil, as needed (for sliding, sealing, and stir-frying)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥣 Small bowl and spoon
🍳 Wok
🥢 Slotted spoon
🥄 Spatula
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1.  **Prepare the sauce**: In a small bowl, add salt, chicken powder, white pepper powder, fragrant vinegar, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sesame oil, and Huadiao wine in that order. Stir to combine and set aside.
    **Note**: This sauce forms the main flavor of the dish. Preparing it in advance prevents scrambling during stir-frying.

2.  **Process the pork shreds**: Cut the pork tenderloin into even, thin shreds. Add half of the beaten egg liquid, one tablespoon of the prepared sauce, and an appropriate amount of cornstarch. Mix well with your hands until the shreds are coated. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and mix again.
    **Key point**: Coating ensures the pork shreds remain tender; sealing with oil prevents them from sticking together when cooking.

3.  **Process the vegetables**: Wash and thoroughly clean the yellow chives and Chinese chives. Cut them into inch-long sections.

4.  **Slide the pork in oil**: Pour a generous amount of cold oil into the wok. Heat over medium-low heat until slightly warm. Add the marinated pork shreds and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Once the shreds change color and are distinctly separated, immediately remove them with a slotted spoon and drain the oil.
    **Note**: Use a hot wok and cold oil. If the oil temperature is too high, the pork shreds will clump together and become tough.

5.  **Stir-fry aromatics**: Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Over high heat, stir-fry the minced garlic until fragrant. First, add the thicker stem parts of the yellow chives and Chinese chives and stir-fry a few times. Then, add the leafy parts and stir-fry until they are just cooked and fragrant.
    **Note**: Add the vegetables in stages to ensure even cooking.

6.  **Combine and thicken**: Return the slid pork shreds to the wok. Quickly stir-fry everything together with the vegetables. Pour in the remaining sauce and drizzle in a little cornstarch slurry. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat for about 10 seconds until the sauce evenly coats the ingredients, achieving an "oil-enveloped sauce" state.
    **Note**: Act quickly to avoid the sauce separating or the dish releasing water.

7.  **Plate and serve**: Turn off the heat and transfer to a plate. Ensure there is no excess liquid pooling at the bottom of the plate.

## Health Suggestions
This dish combines yellow chives with Chinese chives for a rich texture. The pork tenderloin remains tender through marinating and sliding in oil, making it suitable for supplementing protein and vitamins in winter. Pay attention to controlling the amount of salt and soy sauce; people with high blood pressure are advised to reduce the chicken powder. Yellow chives and Chinese chives are pungent and warm in nature, so those with sensitive stomachs should avoid overconsumption.
