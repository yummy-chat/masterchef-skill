# Taro Balls, Red Beans, and Purple Rice in Coconut Milk
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Red beans, as needed (pre-soaked)  
🥔 Taro, as needed (peeled and cut into pieces)  
🍡 Raw taro balls, as needed  
🍚 Purple glutinous rice, as needed (pre-soaked overnight)  
🥛 Sweetened condensed milk, as needed  
🥥 Coconut milk, 1 box (or substitute with fresh milk)  
🍦 Small pudding ice cream bar, 1 piece (optional)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
♨️ Microwave  
🥣 Microwave-safe bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🛡️ Microwave-safe lid  

## Steps  
1. Place the pre-soaked red beans in a microwave-safe bowl, add a small amount of water, and heat on high for 8 minutes until the red beans are soft and tender.
2. Remove the bowl of red beans, add the taro pieces and raw taro balls, then pour in boiling water until it just covers all the ingredients. Place in the microwave and heat for about 6 minutes.  
   **Note**: The heating time is not fixed; check the status midway. If the water evaporates, add a moderate amount of boiling water. Heat until the taro is soft and tender.
3. In another bowl, place the pre-soaked purple glutinous rice with a little water, and heat on high for about 6 minutes until the purple rice absorbs water and becomes rice-like.
4. Add sweetened condensed milk to the steamed purple rice and mix well.
5. Combine the cooked red beans, taro, taro balls, and the mixed purple rice in a large bowl.
6. Pour in the entire box of coconut milk and stir evenly.
7. Before serving, add one small pudding ice cream bar to the bowl to enhance the cool and sweet flavor.

## Health Advice  
This dessert has a relatively high carbohydrate content, making it suitable for replenishing energy after exercise or as an afternoon snack, but diabetics should carefully control portions. Purple rice and red beans are rich in dietary fiber, and when paired with taro balls, they provide a long-lasting feeling of fullness.
