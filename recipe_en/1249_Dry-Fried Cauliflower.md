# Dry-Fried Cauliflower
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥦 Cauliflower (appropriate amount, broken into small florets)
🥓 Pork belly (appropriate amount, sliced)
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic (appropriate amount, minced)
🧂 Light soy sauce (appropriate amount)
🧂 Salt (appropriate amount)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
📡 Microwave oven
🔪 Chef's knife
🍽️ Bowls and plates

## Cooking Steps
1. Break cauliflower into small florets, microwave until softened, remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Microwave pre-treatment can simulate the dry-frying effect while saving oil.
2. Slice pork belly thinly, cut scallions into sections, mince garlic.
3. Heat the wok with cold oil, add pork belly slices and stir-fry, rendering out the lard until the slices are slightly charred and curled.
4. Add minced garlic and stir until fragrant, then add the microwaved cauliflower and quickly stir-fry over high heat.
5. Drizzle light soy sauce along the edge of the wok, continue stir-frying until the edges of the cauliflower show slight char marks and a rich soy aroma develops.
6. Before removing from heat, add scallion sections and salt, sprinkle in a little water, quickly stir-fry evenly and serve.
**Note**: Adding a splash of water just before finishing allows the seasonings to blend instantly, creating a more harmonious texture.

## Health Tips
Pre-microwaving the cauliflower before stir-frying saves oil while keeping it crisp and tender. The lard rendered from the pork belly is the soul of this dish. This dish is high in fiber and low in calories, but the pork belly is relatively high in fat, so it is recommended to share between two or three people to avoid excessive calorie intake.
