# Handmade Lemon Tea
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April to August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍋 3 slices of fragrant lemon (per serving)
🍋 4 slices of yellow lemon (per serving)
🍃 40g Thai green tea leaves (steep in 1000ml 80℃ water for 10 minutes)
🍃 40g Phoenix Dan Cong tea (steep in 1000ml 80℃ water for 10 minutes)
🍃 30g Jasmine Green Yan (steep in 800ml 80℃ water for 8 minutes)
🧊 50g ice cubes (for mashing the lemons)
🧊 500g ice cubes (for rapid cooling of Thai green tea broth)
🧊 Approx. 600g ice cubes (for rapid cooling of Phoenix Dan Cong tea broth)
🧊 500g ice cubes (for rapid cooling of jasmine green tea broth)
🧊 Ice cubes as needed (for filling the glass to about 70% in the final serving)
🍯 60g rock sugar syrup

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🥢 Muddlestick
🍹 Shaker
🥃 Glass
🫖 Teapot
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Tea strainer
⏱️ Timer
🍶 Large-capacity kettle

## Preparation Steps
### Preparing the Tea Base
1. **Thai Green Tea Broth**: Place 40g Thai green tea leaves into a teapot, add 1000ml of 80℃ hot water, cover, and steep for 10 minutes. Strain the tea broth into a container with 500g of ice cubes and cool rapidly for later use.
2. **Phoenix Dan Cong Tea Broth**: Place 40g Phoenix Dan Cong tea into a teapot, add 1000ml of 80℃ hot water, cover, and steep for 10 minutes. Strain the tea broth into a container with approx. 600g of ice cubes and cool rapidly for later use.
3. **Jasmine Green Tea Broth**: Place 30g Jasmine Green Yan into a teapot, add 800ml of 80℃ hot water, cover, and steep for 8 minutes. Strain the tea broth into a container with 500g of ice cubes and cool rapidly for later use.
   **Key Points**: Use 80℃ hot water; steeping times vary slightly for different teas; rapidly cool the tea broth with ice immediately after steeping to preserve optimal flavor.

### Preparing the Lemons
- Slice the fragrant lemon and yellow lemon, then ensure all seeds are removed to prevent the final product from becoming bitter.

### Making a Single Serving of Handmade Lemon Tea
1. In a shaker or measuring cup, place 3 slices of fragrant lemon, 4 slices of yellow lemon, and 50g of ice cubes. Use a muddlestick to vigorously press and break up the lemons until they are crushed and the juice is released.
2. Add ice cubes up to about 70% full. Pour in 60g of rock sugar syrup, then add approximately 300ml of any one of the tea broths.
3. Close the shaker lid and shake vigorously for 10 to 15 seconds to thoroughly mix all ingredients.
4. Pour the shaken lemon tea into a glass and serve.
   **Tip**: Fragrant lemon provides a strong aroma, yellow lemon adds acidity; the combination creates a richer flavor profile.

## Health Advice
This handmade lemon tea uses three types of tea bases and two types of lemons, making it rich in vitamin C. However, the syrup content is relatively high; it is recommended to reduce the amount of syrup or use a sugar substitute. Suitable for relieving summer heat, but those with excessive stomach acid should consume in moderation.
