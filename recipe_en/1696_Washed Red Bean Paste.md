# Washed Red Bean Paste
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Red beans 500g (soak for more than 2 hours in advance)
💧 Water as needed (for soaking, cooking beans, and washing the bean paste)
💧 Cold water as needed (for cooling after cooking)
🍬 Rock sugar 300g
🫒 Mild-flavored cooking oil 120g (such as corn oil or sunflower oil)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Stainless steel soup pot
🥣 Cooking pot or milk pot (optional, for secondary rinsing of bean skins)
🔍 Fine mesh strainer
🍳 Non-stick wok or frying pan
🥄 Wooden spoon or heat-resistant spatula
🥣 Large glass bowl
🛍️ Airtight storage bags

## Steps
1. Soaking: Rinse the red beans clean, add enough water to soak for more than 2 hours to fully absorb water.
2. Cooking: Wash the soaked beans again, place them in a large pot with enough water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for about 1 hour until the beans can be easily mashed with fingers.
   **Note**: Stir occasionally during cooking to prevent sticking to the bottom of the pot.
3. Cooling and washing: After removing from heat, add cold water to the pot to cool until it is no longer hot to the touch. Using your hands directly in the pot, repeatedly squeeze, rub, and knead until all the beans are mashed, allowing the bean paste to dissolve fully into the water and the bean skins to separate.
4. Straining: Use a fine mesh strainer to scoop out the bean skins, filtering out the bean paste water.
   **Suggestion**: Place the scooped-out bean skins in another container, add a small amount of water to rinse them again, then pour the rinsed water back into the bean paste water to minimize waste.
5. Settling: Let the bean paste water sit for more than 30 minutes, allowing the bean paste to naturally settle to the bottom of the pot.
6. Separating the bean paste: Gently pour off the clear water from the top; the thick portion at the bottom of the pot is the fine bean paste.
7. Seasoning and boiling: Transfer the bean paste to a wok, add 300g of rock sugar, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer slowly.
   **Key**: Stir continuously throughout the process to prevent burning.
8. Adding oil for richness: When the bean paste thickens and clear lines can be drawn on its surface, add 120g of cooking oil in batches. Stir thoroughly after each addition to fully incorporate the oil into the bean paste.
9. Stir-frying to completion: Continue to stir-fry until the bean paste significantly reduces in moisture and forms a complete dough, and it no longer flows when moved with a spatula. Then, remove from heat.
10. Cooling and storing: Transfer the bean paste to a large bowl, let it cool completely, then portion it into airtight storage bags as needed. Refrigerate and consume as soon as possible.

## Health Advice
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron, making them suitable for individuals with anemia. However, the amounts of rock sugar and oil are high; those controlling sugar intake or losing weight should consider reducing the quantities by half. Homemade bean paste contains fewer additives compared to store-bought versions, making it more suitable for family consumption.
