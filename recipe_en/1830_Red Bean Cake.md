# Red Bean Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 100g red beans (soaked for 2 hours in advance, drained)  
🌾 100g water chestnut flour  
💧 Approximately 1200ml + 140ml water (1200ml for cooking beans, 140ml for mixing batter)  
🍬 100g white sugar  

## Cookware and Equipment
🫙 Glass bowl (for soaking and rinsing red beans)  
🍲 Stainless steel pot (for cooking red beans and boiling red bean water)  
🥣 Whisk/mixer (for stirring batter until smooth)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for quick stirring when pouring hot liquid)  
🫗 Strainer (for filtering red beans and red bean water)  
🫕 Square heat-resistant container (for holding the batter)  
♨️ Stainless steel steamer  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🪵 Cutting board  

## Steps
1. Place the soaked red beans in a pot, add approximately 1200ml of water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 40 minutes until the beans are soft enough to be easily mashed with fingers.  
   **Note**: Do not add sugar while cooking the beans, otherwise they will not soften properly.  
2. Strain the cooked red beans to separate the beans from the red bean water. Accurately weigh out 300g of red bean water and set aside. Set the cooked red beans aside separately.  
3. In another container, add 100g water chestnut flour and 140ml cold water. Use a whisk to stir thoroughly until a smooth white raw batter forms with no lumps.  
4. Pour the prepared 300g red bean water into a pot, add 100g white sugar, and heat over high heat until it reaches a full boil.  
5. Slowly pour the boiling red bean water into the raw batter while continuously stirring quickly with chopsticks, resulting in a translucent, sticky paste.  
   **Key**: The water must be boiling when pouring into the batter, stir while pouring to prevent starch clumping.  
6. Add the cooked red beans to the mixed batter and stir evenly.  
7. Pour the red bean batter into the heat-resistant container and smooth the surface with a scraper.  
8. Bring water in the steamer to a boil, place the container inside, cover with a lid, and steam over high heat for 16 minutes until the batter is fully set, with a darker and translucent color.  
9. After steaming, remove and let cool until no longer hot to the touch. Press around the inner wall of the container to loosen the cake, then invert to unmold.  
   **Note**: Unmolding is easier while still slightly warm.  
10. Place the unmolded red bean cake on a cutting board and cut into small pieces with a knife dipped in water to prevent sticking.

## Health Tips
Red beans are rich in dietary fiber and iron, making them suitable for those needing blood nourishment and improved intestinal motility. However, the sugar content is high, so individuals with diabetes or those controlling sugar intake should consume in moderation. Water chestnut flour provides a chewy texture and is low in fat, but overall calories are concentrated in the sugar. It is recommended to enjoy with unsweetened tea.
