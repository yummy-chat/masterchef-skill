# Homemade Cream Cheese Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Whole pasteurized milk, 3 liters  
🍋 Lemons, about 4 (approximately 120 ml juice for curdling; an additional 2 teaspoons for mascarpone)  
🧈 Unsalted butter, 25 grams  
🍪 Digestive biscuits, about 60 grams  
🥚 Egg, 1  
🍬 Granulated sugar, 80 grams  
🌽 Cornstarch, 2 tablespoons  
🍦 Heavy cream, 150 grams (for making mascarpone)  
🌿 Vanilla extract, a few drops  
🧂 Sea salt, 1 teaspoon  
🍓 Fresh raspberries, as needed  
🍓 Strawberry jam, as needed  
🍚 Powdered sugar, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
♨️ Saucepan  
🌡️ Food thermometer  
🧽 Sieve and fine cheesecloth/muslin  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🪨 Weight (such as a sealed bag of water)  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
📐 6-inch springform cake pan  
♨️ Microwave or double boiler  
🛍️ Sealed bag  
📏 Rolling pin  
🔥 Oven  
🥄 Spatula  

## Instructions  
### Making Ricotta Cheese  
1. Pour the whole milk into a saucepan and heat over low heat to about 70°C, monitoring with a food thermometer. Avoid boiling.  
2. Remove from heat, slowly add the lemon juice, and gently stir a few times with a spatula. Let rest for 15 minutes until the milk is fully curdled into fluffy solids and the whey is relatively clear with a slightly greenish tint.  
3. Line a sieve with fine cheesecloth, pour in the curds to drain. Then wrap the cheese in the cheesecloth, place a weight on top, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours to press out excess whey.  
4. Remove the cheese from the refrigerator, add sea salt, and beat with an electric hand mixer until smooth and creamy. Set aside.  

### Making Mascarpone Cheese  
1. Pour the heavy cream into a saucepan and heat over low heat to about 70°C.  
2. Remove from heat, add the lemon juice, and stir. The cream will thicken immediately but should not form lumps.  
3. Filter through cheesecloth, wrap, and press with a weight. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight before use.  

### Making the Crust  
1. Place the digestive biscuits in a sealed bag and crush them into fine crumbs using a rolling pin.  
2. Melt the butter and mix it thoroughly with the biscuit crumbs until the mixture can be pressed into a ball.  
3. Pour the mixture into a 6-inch springform pan, press it flat and firm with a spoon, and refrigerate for 15 minutes to set.  
4. Grease the inside of the pan with a layer of butter and dust with a thin layer of all-purpose flour to prevent sticking.  

### Making the Batter & Baking  
1. Take the homemade Ricotta cheese, add the granulated sugar, and beat with an electric hand mixer until smooth.  
2. Add the egg and beat again until well combined.  
3. Add the homemade Mascarpone cheese and beat until smooth.  
4. Add the cornstarch and vanilla extract, mixing into a smooth batter.  
5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smooth the surface, and tap the pan gently a few times to release large air bubbles.  
6. Preheat the oven to 160°C. Place the pan in the oven and bake for about 60 minutes, until the surface is golden and the center still jiggles slightly when shaken.  

### Cooling & Decorating  
1. Allow the baked cake to cool completely before removing it from the pan.  
2. Spread a layer of strawberry jam in the natural depression in the center of the cake, arrange fresh raspberries on top, and finish by sifting powdered sugar over the cake as decoration.  

## Health Advice  
Homemade cream cheese cheesecake is rich in calcium and protein, suitable as an occasional dessert treat. However, it is high in calories and fat, so portion control is recommended. Be cautious for those with lactose intolerance, and diabetics should reduce the amount of sugar used.
