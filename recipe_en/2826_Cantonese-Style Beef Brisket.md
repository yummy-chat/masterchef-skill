# Cantonese-Style Beef Brisket
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Beef tendon (beef brisket) – appropriate amount (freshly trimmed, pieces with fascia and trimmings taste better)  
🫚 Ginger – 2-3 slices  
🧄 Garlic – 2-3 cloves  
🥦 Broccoli – appropriate amount  
🍜 Noodles – 1 serving  
🍶 Cooking wine – appropriate amount (for blanching)  
🥫 Chu hou paste – appropriate amount  
🥜 Peanut butter – appropriate amount  
🍬 Rock sugar – a few pieces  
🥢 Light soy sauce – a little  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥄 Ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the beef brisket into bite-sized pieces. Place in a pot with cold water, add ginger slices and cooking wine. Bring to a boil, skim off the foam, then drain.
**Key**: Must start with cold water to fully remove blood and odor.
2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat, sauté ginger slices and garlic cloves until fragrant. Add the blanched beef brisket and stir-fry until the surface slightly changes color.
3. Add chu hou paste and peanut butter, stir-fry quickly so each piece of beef brisket is evenly coated, and the aroma of the sauce is released.
**Key**: This step establishes the base Cantonese flavor; the sauce must be stir-fried until fragrant.
4. Pour in enough hot water, add rock sugar and light soy sauce, and add the spice pack. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for about 40 minutes until the sauce thickens and the beef brisket is tender.
**Note**: Add enough water at once; preferably use hot water. Transferring to a clay pot for simmering yields better results. Adjust the simmering time according to the cut of beef brisket.
5. In another pot, cook the noodles and broccoli until done, then drain and place in a bowl. Pour the stewed beef brisket and plenty of sauce over the noodles, mix well, and serve.

## Health Advice  
Beef brisket is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for those needing to replenish blood or build muscle. However, chu hou paste and peanut butter are high in sodium and calories, so it is recommended to control the amount used. Broccoli adds dietary fiber, balancing the meat and vegetable combination.
