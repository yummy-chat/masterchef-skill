# Tomato and Egg Lazy Noodle Soup
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍞 Flour 300g (for dough)  
🍅 Tomato 1 (peeled and chopped)  
🥚 Eggs 2 (beaten and set aside)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped into scallion flowers)  
🧂 Salt 1 small spoon (for dough) + to taste (for stir-frying)  
🍾 Light Soy Sauce, to taste  
🍬 White Sugar 1 small spoon  
🫒 Cooking Oil, to taste  
💧 Cold Water, approx. 150ml (for dough)  
💧 Cold Water, to taste (for soaking dough)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting Board  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Wooden Spatula  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the dough**: Add 1 small spoon of salt to the flour. Gradually add about 150ml of cold water, stirring with chopsticks until it forms a flaky texture, then knead into a smooth dough.
   *Note: Adding salt increases the dough's gluten strength; using cold water makes the texture more chewy.*
2. **Knead and cut the dough**: Continue kneading the dough for about 5 minutes until the surface is smooth and springy when pressed. Roll it into a long strip, fold it, and cut into small dough pieces about 1cm square.
   *Key point: Ensure the dough is kneaded until smooth and elastic; try to make the dough pieces uniform in size.*
3. **Soak the dough pieces**: Place the cut dough pieces in cold water and soak for about 5 minutes.
   *Key point: Soaking in cold water relaxes the dough pieces, making them less likely to shrink or stick when stretched.*
4. **Prepare ingredients**: Peel the tomato and chop it finely. Beat the eggs into a liquid. Chop the scallions into scallion flowers and set aside.
5. **Stir-fry the tomato broth**: Heat oil in a wok over medium-low heat. Add the chopped tomato and stir-fry until soft and the juices release. Add salt, light soy sauce, and 1 small spoon of white sugar. Continue stir-frying until it becomes a thick, red sauce.
   *Key point: Fully cooking out the tomato juice is key to a bright red broth; the sugar balances the acidity and enhances the flavor.*
6. **Stretch the dough sheets and cook**: Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok and bring to a boil. Remove the soaked dough pieces. Hold each piece by both ends and evenly stretch it into a thin, uniform sheet. Immediately drop it into the boiling broth.
   *Key point: Stretch the dough sheets as evenly as possible. Cook until they float completely and become translucent, avoiding undercooking.*
7. **Add the egg flower and serve**: Slowly pour the beaten egg into the wok in a circular motion. Once the egg flower sets, sprinkle in the scallion flowers. Gently stir to combine, then turn off the heat and serve.
   *Key point: Adding the egg at the end keeps it tender; sprinkling scallions just before serving preserves their aroma.*

## Health Advice  
Tomato and egg noodle soup is rich in lycopene and high-quality protein, making it ideal for office workers needing a quick energy boost. It is recommended to use less oil when stir-frying the tomatoes, and you can add leafy greens to increase dietary fiber.
