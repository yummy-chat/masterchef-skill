# Spinach Little Steamed Buns 🥬
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 500g spinach (washed)
🥕 About a handful of carrots (julienned)
🌾 All-purpose wheat flour (add gradually in small amounts, knead until no longer sticky)
🧂 2g salt (for the filling)
🥄 1g baking soda (for the filling, to keep green)
🧄 A few cloves of garlic
🫘 1 tbsp black and white sesame seeds
🌶️ 1 tbsp chili flakes
🫒 Appropriate amount of hot oil (to infuse aroma)
🍶 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce
🍶 1/3 tbsp fragrant vinegar

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥘 Pot of boiling water (for blanching)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪣 Bowl of cold water
🔪 Cutting board & knife
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
♨️ Steamer, steaming basket, steaming liner
🧂 Mortar & pestle (to mash garlic)
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Stir-fry ladle
🫙 Small dipping sauce bowl

## Preparation Steps
1.  Blanch the washed spinach in boiling water with a little cooking oil, then immediately add the julienned carrots and blanch together for about 5 seconds. Remove and immediately plunge into cold water to cool.
**Key Point**: Blanching removes pesticide residue and oxalic acid; adding oil enhances the vegetable’s color.
2.  Remove the vegetables, squeeze out all the water firmly, and chop the spinach and carrots into very fine mince.
3.  Place the minced vegetables in a bowl, add 2g salt and 1g baking soda, and mix well. Gradually add flour in small amounts, stir into a flaky dough with chopsticks, then knead by hand into a smooth, non-sticky green dough.
**Key Point**: Ensure water is thoroughly squeezed out; flour must be added gradually in small amounts to avoid a dry, hard dough; baking soda maintains the bright green color and improves texture.
4.  Lightly dust your hands with flour, take a piece of dough, roll it into a ball, press a dimple in the center with your thumb, and shape it into a hollow little nest-like bun.
**Key Point**: The hollow shape ensures even heat distribution and thorough cooking.
5.  Place the shaped buns spaced apart on a steamer basket lined with a steaming liner. Cover, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high heat and steam for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let them rest, covered, for 3 minutes before removing the lid.
**Key Point**: The 3-minute rest after steaming prevents the buns from shrinking, collapsing, or hardening due to sudden temperature change.
6.  Prepare the dipping sauce: Mash the garlic in a mortar into a paste, add black and white sesame seeds and chili flakes, pour hot oil over them to release the aroma, then stir in 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce and 1/3 tbsp fragrant vinegar. Mix well.
**Key Point**: The hot oil must be hot enough to fully release the aroma of the seasonings.

## Health Advice
Spinach and carrots are rich in vitamins and dietary fiber. Paired with a low-oil steaming method, this dish is suitable for those with digestive issues or those managing their weight. The dipping sauce is relatively high in calories, so use it in moderation.
