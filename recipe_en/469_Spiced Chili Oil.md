# Spiced Chili Oil

Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫒 Rapeseed oil 500g  
🌶️ Dried chili segments 60g (choose a variety with high aroma and low heat)  
🌸 Dried Sichuan peppercorns 20g  
⚪ White sesame seeds 20g  
🧅 Scallion 30g (cut into sections)  
🧅 Onion 20g (shredded)  
🫚 Ginger 10g (shredded)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥢 Mixing spoon  
🫙 Container with lid (bowl or jar)  

## Steps
1. Blanch the Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili segments separately in boiling water briefly, then remove and drain.
   **Note**: Blanching prevents scorching later and helps release aroma.
2. Pour the rapeseed oil into the wok, heat until the oil is cooked, then turn off the heat and let it cool slightly.
3. When the oil is no longer splattering violently, add the scallion sections, shredded onion, and shredded ginger.
4. Fry over low heat until the ingredients turn dry and yellow and the aroma is released, then remove and discard them with a slotted spoon.
5. When the oil temperature drops below 120°C, add the drained Sichuan peppercorns.
   **Note**: Adding them at a low temperature fully extracts the numbing aroma.
6. Keep frying over low heat at 100–120°C until the numbing aroma is pronounced and the color of the Sichuan peppercorns deepens, then turn off the heat and remove the peppercorns.
   **Note**: Do not fry for too long, otherwise the oil will become bitter.
7. Pour the drained chili segments into the wok and fry over low heat.
8. When the chili segments are gradually drying and feel slightly dry to the touch, sprinkle in the white sesame seeds, stir quickly to combine, and immediately turn off the heat.
   **Key point**: The timing of turning off the heat determines success; if delayed, the chili can burn.
9. Quickly pour all contents from the wok into a clean container, cover with the lid to seal, and let cool naturally.
   **Note**: The cooling process while sealed is key for infusing the chili aroma into the oil, using residual heat to drive out flavor.
10. Once completely cooled, it is ready to use for dressing various cold dishes.

## Health Advice  
This spiced chili oil is rich in quality fats and the numbing aroma of Sichuan peppercorns, ideal for enhancing cold dishes. Due to its high calorie content, it is recommended to use only one tablespoon per serving. People with high blood pressure should reduce salt pairing, and pregnant women should use Sichuan peppercorns with caution.
