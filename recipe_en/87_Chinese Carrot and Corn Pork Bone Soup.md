# Chinese Carrot and Corn Pork Bone Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork spine 1000g (recommended backbone)
🌽 Sweet corn 1 piece
🥕 Carrot 1 piece
🎋 Sugarcane 50g
🌰 Water chestnuts 50g
🫚 Ginger slices 10g
🧂 Salt to taste (add after cooking)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🫕 Clay pot (narrow-mouthed clay pot better retains moisture)
🍳 Wok/soup pot (for blanching)
🔥 Stove

## Steps
1. **Blanch the pork bones**: Place the pork spine in cold water and heat until boiling. Once foam rises to the surface, remove the bones and rinse with warm water.
**Key point**: It is recommended to blanch frozen meat; fresh meat can skip this step.
2. **Prepare the vegetables**: Cut the corn into sections, peel and cut the carrot into rolling chunks; split the sugarcane in half, peel and wash the water chestnuts.
**Key point**: Water chestnuts and sugarcane make the broth clearer and sweeter; vegetable sizes can be adjusted according to preference.
3. **Add all ingredients to the pot**: Place the blanched pork bones, corn, carrot, water chestnuts, sugarcane, and ginger slices into the clay pot, and add enough water.
**Key point**: Add enough water at once; do not add water midway.
4. **Slowly simmer the soup**: Bring to a boil over high heat, then immediately turn to low heat, keeping the surface gently simmering. Cook slowly for about 2 hours.
**Key point**: Use a clay pot with good heat retention. Slow simmering allows umami substances to be released gradually, resulting in a clear soup.
5. **Season and serve**: After turning off the heat, add salt to taste according to personal preference.
**Key point**: Do not add salt during the entire simmering process to prevent the meat from becoming tough and to preserve the original flavors of the ingredients.

## Health Tips
This soup is rich in collagen and dietary fiber, suitable for clearing heat and moisturizing dryness in summer. However, pork bones are high in fat, so it is recommended to skim off the floating oil before drinking. People with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control their portions. The natural sweetness from sugarcane and water chestnuts makes the soup guilt-free, with no need for extra sugar.
