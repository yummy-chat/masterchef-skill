# Baked Dungeness Crab with Cheese
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦀 1 Dungeness Crab (about 1 jin 2 liang, commercially available are all male crabs)  
🍝 Appropriate amount of Cantonese Yi Noodles (for lining the plate)  
🧄 Appropriate amount of minced garlic  
🧅 Appropriate amount of small shallots (finely diced)  
🧈 A small piece of butter  
🧀 2 slices of cheese (must use cheese suitable for baking, such as pasta baking cheese; pizza cheese is not suitable)  
🌽 Appropriate amount of cornstarch (for dusting)  
🍶 Appropriate amount of rice wine (can be substituted with white wine)  
🧂 A pinch of salt  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🐔 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon (for umami, optional)  
🥜 A little peanut oil (drizzled after thickening to add sheen)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪚 Round thick wooden cutting board  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥄 Skimmer  
🥄 Large ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
🍽️ Plate  

## Cooking Steps
1. Insert the knife into the crab's mouth, pry open the shell, and reserve the crab roe. Cut off the binding string, chop off the crab claws; remove the gills and mouth, clean and cut the body into four pieces, and split the legs in half. Clean the shell and discard the stomach.  
**Note**: The claws must be chopped off before untying the string to avoid pinching; the stomach must be removed, or it will affect the texture.  

2. Prepare minced garlic and diced shallots; place cheese slices and butter in a bowl. Cook Cantonese Yi Noodles in cold water until soft, drain, and spread evenly on the plate.  
**Note**: Ensure the cheese slices are suitable for baking; do not use pizza cheese.  

3. Evenly dust cornstarch on all cut surfaces of the crab pieces.  
**Key Point**: Dusting locks in crab meat moisture, keeping it tender.  

4. Pour enough oil into the wok and heat to high temperature. First, fry the thicker crab meat pieces for about 10 seconds, then add the legs and shell, and finally the top shell. Fry until aromatic, then remove all and drain oil.  
**Key Point**: Fry parts in order for consistent doneness; at home, pan-frying can replace deep-frying.  

5. Leave some base oil in the wok, stir-fry minced garlic and diced shallots over medium heat until fragrant. Add butter to melt, then introduce the fried crab. Splash rice wine along the wok edge for aroma, turn to high heat, and stir-fry quickly to combine.  
**Note**: Add butter later to avoid burning; high-heat stir-frying melds flavors.  

6. Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok, then add cheese slices, chicken bouillon, salt, and sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens and the cheese fully melts.  
**Key Point**: Do not stir-fry cheese slices directly; they must be cooked with the liquid.  

7. Remove the cooked crab pieces and set aside. Let the sauce sit for a moment, then add a water-starch slurry to thicken lightly. Finally, drizzle in a little peanut oil and stir to make the sauce glossy.  
**Note**: Let the sauce rest before adding the slurry to prevent lumps.  

8. On the plate, first lay the cooked Yi Noodles, then arrange the crab meat on top, and drizzle with some sauce. Place the top shell over the crab, and evenly pour the remaining sauce over the shell and the entire dish.  
**Note**: Ensure the crab meat and noodles are thoroughly coated with the sauce.  

## Health Advice  
This baked Dungeness crab with cheese is high in protein and low in fat; crab meat is rich in zinc and selenium, paired with Yi Noodles for carbohydrates, making it suitable for autumnal nourishment. Control the amounts of cheese and butter; those with high cholesterol should consume in moderation.
