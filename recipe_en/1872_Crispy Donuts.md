# Crispy Donuts
Cuisine: Common Home-style  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍞 Bread flour 200g  
🥛 Milk powder 20g (for dough)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 16g  
🧂 Salt 3g  
💧 Water 125g  
🍄 Active dry yeast 3g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 10g  
🥮 Cake flour 60g (for crispy batter)  
🧁 Baking powder 3g  
🧊 Ice water 80g  
🌽 Corn oil 10g  
🍚 Powdered sugar 25g (for final decoration)  
🥛 Milk powder 25g (for final decoration, mix 1:1 with powdered sugar)  
📄 Parchment paper as needed (to prevent sticking)  
🍳 Frying oil enough (for deep frying)  

## Utensils and Equipment  
🪵 Mixing bowl or large bowl  
🔪 Dough scraper  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🧀 Proofing cloth or plastic wrap  
📦 Baking sheet  
🌡️ Proofer or warm, humid environment  
🌡️ Thermometer  
🍳 Pan or deep fryer  
🥢 Slotted spoon or strainer  
🏗️ Cooling rack  
🥣 Metal bowl  
🥚 Whisk  
🥣 Small bowl and spoon  
🫙 Sifter  

## Instructions  
1. Mix bread flour, milk powder, granulated sugar, and salt together, stirring with a dough scraper until combined.  
2. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients, pour in water and active dry yeast. Stir the liquid first with the scraper, then gradually mix in the dry ingredients to form a shaggy dough.  
3. Add unsalted butter, knead and slap the dough repeatedly until the surface is smooth, no longer sticky, and the bowl is clean.  
   **Key point**: There is no need to develop a windowpane membrane; just knead until smooth.  
4. Cover the dough with a proofing cloth and let it rest at room temperature for 25 minutes.  
5. After resting, remove the dough, pat it flat, and divide it into 8 equal pieces, cutting them into roughly triangular shapes.  
6. Pat each piece flat, fold the edges toward the center, roll into a ball, and pinch the seam to seal.  
7. Dust the surface with a little flour, poke a hole in the center with a finger, and gently stretch it into a ring shape. Place a piece of parchment paper underneath.  
   **Key point**: The paper prevents sticking and makes it easier to transfer later.  
8. Arrange the donut rings on a baking sheet and place them in a warm, humid environment for the second proofing until they spring back slightly when gently pressed.  
9. Prepare the crispy batter: Mix cake flour and baking powder together, add ice water and whisk with a whisk until smooth, then add corn oil and stir into a smooth batter.  
   **Key point**: Ice water inhibits the baking powder from reacting prematurely.  
10. Fully dip the proofed donut rings into the crispy batter, coating them evenly. Use your hand to support the bottom when lifting them out, and return them to the baking sheet, taking care not to distort the center hole.  
11. Heat oil in a pan to about 170°C. Place the donut rings, still on their parchment paper, into the oil. Quickly turn them over to cook evenly on both sides, which helps them puff up.  
    **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too low or too high; too low causes oil absorption, too high causes burning.  
12. Once the donuts float and are set, remove the parchment paper. Continue frying until both sides are golden brown, about 12 minutes total.  
    **Key point**: You can fry them to a deeper color for longer-lasting crispness.  
13. Remove and place on a cooling rack to drain oil. When they are just warm, mix powdered sugar and milk powder in a 1:1 ratio, and sift evenly over the surface.  

## Health Advice  
This crispy donut is high in oil and sugar, suitable for occasional consumption as an energy boost or afternoon tea, not for frequent eating. Those who are fitness-conscious or controlling sugar intake may enjoy half a donut, paired with black coffee to cut the grease.
