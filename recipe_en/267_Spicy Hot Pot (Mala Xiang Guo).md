# Spicy Hot Pot (Mala Xiang Guo)
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 **Ingredients**  
- Leafy greens (bok choy, romaine lettuce, spinach): total 455 g  
- Boneless meat (pork, beef, chicken, fish balls, ham sausage): total 430 g  
- Dried tofu skin: 152 g  
- Beijing-style spicy instant noodles: 1 pack  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Mala Xiang Guo seasoning mix: 110 g  
- Dried chili peppers: 5 g  
- Cooking oil: 105 g  

🍳 **Utensils**  
- Pot for boiling  
- Wok  
- Chopsticks  

## Instructions
1. Fill a pot with water and bring to a boil. Use enough water to submerge all ingredients.
2. Add the leafy greens to the boiling water and blanch for 5 minutes. Remove and drain.
3. Add the boneless meat to the same boiling water and blanch for 4 minutes. Remove and drain.
4. Add the Beijing-style spicy instant noodles to the boiling water and cook for 2 minutes. Remove and drain.
5. Discard the hot water, dry the pot, and pour in 105 g of cooking oil. Heat over high heat for 30 seconds.
6. Add the Mala Xiang Guo seasoning mix and stir-fry for 20 seconds.
7. Add the dried chili peppers and stir-fry for 10 seconds.
8. Add the blanched leafy greens, reduce the heat to medium, and stir-fry for 3 minutes.
9. Add the blanched boneless meat and stir-fry for 3 minutes.
10. Add the cooked Beijing-style spicy instant noodles and toss with chopsticks for 1 minute.
11. Turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
This Mala Xiang Guo offers a balanced combination of vegetables and protein, but the seasoning and oil content are relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or reduce the amount of rice. This dish is best enjoyed as an occasional treat rather than a daily meal. Be mindful of sodium intake from dried chili peppers and seasoning mix; those with high blood pressure should consume with caution.
