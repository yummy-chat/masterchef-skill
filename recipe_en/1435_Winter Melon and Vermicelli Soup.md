# Winter Melon and Vermicelli Soup
Category: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Winter melon 1000g (peeled and seeded, cut into 2mm thin slices)
🍜 Vermicelli appropriate amount (soaked in warm water for 20 minutes)
🍤 Dried shrimp appropriate amount (soaked in warm water for 20 minutes, squeezed dry)
🍤 Conpoy 3 pieces (soaked in warm water for 20 minutes, shredded)
🧅 Spring onion appropriate amount (use the white part, chopped)
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (pounded and minced)
🥄 Lard appropriate amount
🧂 Edible salt approximately 5g
🧂 Ground white pepper a pinch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
✂️ Scissors
🥄 Wok spatula and strainer
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Peel and seed the winter melon, then cut into approximately 2mm thin slices. Set aside.
   **Note**: Cutting into thin slices can shorten the subsequent cooking time.
2. Soak the vermicelli, dried shrimp, and conpoy separately in warm water for 20 minutes. After soaking, shred the conpoy and remove the dried shrimp, squeezing dry.
3. Chop the spring onion and pound and mince the ginger. Set aside as the aromatic base.
4. Heat the iron wok over high heat, add an appropriate amount of lard to coat the wok, ensuring an even oil film to prevent sticking.
5. When the oil is at 50% heat, add the shredded conpoy, dried shrimp, chopped spring onion, and minced ginger. Stir-fry over medium heat until the aroma is released.
   **Key**: Fully sautéing the aromatic base is the core of the soup's umami flavor.
6. Add the winter melon slices. Stir-fry a few times over medium heat to evenly coat the winter melon with the oil and the aroma of the aromatics, removing any raw taste.
7. Add enough water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for approximately 6 minutes, until the winter melon slices become translucent and soft.
8. Add the soaked vermicelli. Continue cooking over medium heat for approximately 1 minute.
   **Note**: The vermicelli is already soft after soaking, so the cooking time should not be too long to avoid it becoming mushy.
9. Add approximately 5g of edible salt and a pinch of ground white pepper. Stir evenly, then turn off the heat and serve.
   **Key**: Adding the ground white pepper at the end helps better retain its aroma.

## Health Advice
This soup is low in fat and high in water content. Winter melon is rich in potassium and water, making it suitable for rehydration and cooling in summer, as well as weight control. Dried shrimp and conpoy provide umami flavor and trace amounts of iodine. Individuals with high blood pressure should pay attention to controlling the amount of salt used.
