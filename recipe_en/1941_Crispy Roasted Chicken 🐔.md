# Crispy Roasted Chicken 🐔
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🛒
🐔 1 whole chicken (about 1.5 kg, plump, with intact skin and chicken fat)
🧂 Chicken salt (mixed seasoning) — about 2 tablespoons (contains five-spice powder, sugar, salt, pepper salt, and chicken powder, used for marinating)
🫗 Cold brine — enough to fully submerge the chicken (prepared with store-bought spice pack plus salt, sugar, and chicken powder, then cooled before use)
🍶 White vinegar — about 2 tablespoons
🥃 Chinese liquor (qujiu) —适量
🍷 Red Zhejiang vinegar —适量 (for coloring)
🍯 Maltose — about 1 tablespoon (dissolved in hot water in advance)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment 🛠️
🪣 Large basin
🥄 Large ladle
🪝 Butcher's hook
🌀 Fan
🍳 Large iron wok
🥢 Slotted spoon
✂️ Scissors

## Instructions 👩‍🍳
1.  Fill the chicken cavity with about 2 tablespoons of chicken salt, rub evenly inside, and marinate for 1 hour to allow the flavors to penetrate.
**Key point**: The marinating time must be sufficient for the flavor to infuse the meat.

2.  After marinating, rinse the chicken thoroughly inside and out with clean water to remove surface salt.
3.  Fully submerge the chicken in cold brine and soak for 3 hours, allowing the meat to absorb flavor and moisture. This is crucial for smooth skin and juicy, tender meat.
**Key point**: The brine must be completely cold; hot brine will cause the skin to shrink and dry out.

4.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Holding the chicken by the head, use a ladle to repeatedly pour boiling water over the skin. This shrinks and raises the pores, making it easier to remove any remaining fine feathers and ensuring the skin is clean.
**Note**: Pour the water quickly and evenly to avoid partially cooking the skin.

5.  Mix the white vinegar, Chinese liquor, Red Zhejiang vinegar, and pre-dissolved maltose together until well combined. The maltose is key for coloring and crispy skin.
6.  Once the chicken has cooled, place it in the prepared 'skin water' mixture, applying it evenly and massaging it all over the body. Then hang it using a butcher's hook.
**Key point**: Application must be thorough, especially in areas like the armpits and leg creases, which should not be missed.

7.  Hang the chicken in front of a fan for 3-4 hours, until the skin is completely dry, taut, and shiny, and feels no longer moist to the touch. This is the foundation for crispy skin.
**Key point**: Insufficient drying time will result in skin that isn't crispy when fried. The drying time can be extended if needed.

8.  Cut off the chicken head and place it into hot oil at about 80°C (176°F). If it turns red quickly, the oil temperature is suitable.
9.  Place the whole chicken into the oil. Using a ladle, continuously pour hot oil over the chicken's body and cavity. Deep-fry over medium-low heat for about 15 minutes, until the chicken is an even golden yellow. Do not let the chicken touch the bottom of the wok directly, as this can cause burning and uneven coloring.
**Key point**: Maintain a stable oil temperature during deep-frying. Turn the chicken to ensure even heating of all parts.

10. Remove the chicken. Increase the oil temperature and fry again briefly. Once the skin rapidly turns a deep jujube red and becomes crispier, remove it immediately. Drain the oil and serve.
**Key point**: The second frying at high temperature is very short. Act quickly and keep a close watch to prevent burning.

## Health Advice 💡
This Crispy Roasted Chicken is marinated in cold brine and slowly fried, resulting in tender meat rich in protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. It is recommended to control the oil temperature to reduce the formation of carcinogens. Individuals with high blood lipids may remove the skin before eating.
