# Cucumber and Pig Ear Salad
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Cucumber 2 pieces (smash and cut into chunks)
🐷👂 Braised pig ears 2 pieces (cooked)
🥜 Fried peanuts to taste
🧄 Garlic to taste (divided into two parts: one part minced for chili oil; the other part whole cloves for frying peanuts)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste (choose aromatic, not spicy variety)
🧂 Premium soy sauce 20 grams
🫒 Cooking oil to taste (peanut oil recommended; for frying chili oil and peanuts)
🧂 Salt 3 grams
🍬 Sugar 18 grams
🍶 Zhenjiang balsamic vinegar 20 grams

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🌡️ Infrared thermometer
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🫗 Small bowl, spoon
🧤 Disposable gloves

## Instructions
1. Smash the cucumber, remove seeds, and cut into small chunks. Place in a bowl, sprinkle with 3 grams of salt, mix well, and let marinate for a moment.
   **Note**: Smashed cucumber has a crisper texture; salting in advance gives it a base flavor.
2. In a small bowl, first add the minced garlic, then the dried chili pepper segments. Pour 20 grams of premium soy sauce along the edge of the bowl. In a separate pan, heat cooking oil until smoking, then pour the hot oil into the bowl in batches, stirring continuously, to make an aromatic but not spicy chili oil.
   **Note**: High oil temperature neutralizes spiciness and enhances aroma; soy sauce should be poured along the bowl's edge to avoid direct contact with the chili peppers.
3. Pour cold oil into the pan, add the peanuts and half a clove of garlic. Fry over low heat throughout. When the garlic turns golden brown and the peanuts are crispy, use a slotted spoon to remove and drain.
   **Note**: Use garlic as a doneness indicator; keep the heat low throughout to prevent burning.
4. Trim the braised pig ears to remove any thick or hairy root sections. Slice thinly against the cartilage grain, cutting the fatty parts as thin as possible.
   **Note**: Slicing against the grain yields a crisper texture; thin slices of fat reduce greasiness.
5. Place the marinated cucumber, sliced pig ears, and fried peanuts into a large bowl. Add the prepared chili oil, 18 grams of sugar, and 20 grams of Zhenjiang balsamic vinegar. Put on disposable gloves and mix thoroughly by hand until evenly coated.
   **Note**: Maintain a 1:1 ratio of sugar to vinegar; mix until each ingredient is evenly coated with the sauce.

## Health Advice
Cucumber and Pig Ear Salad is crisp and refreshing, ideal for cutting through grease and pairing with drinks. As pig ears and peanuts are relatively high in calories, portion control is recommended during weight loss. Opt for low-fat braised pig ears and increase the proportion of cucumber to reduce overall calorie intake.
