# Microwaved Black Cod with Scallion and Ginger
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🛒 **Ingredients**
🐟 Black cod (skin-on) 450g

🧂 **Seasonings**
🧅 Scallion 35g (white part 25g, green part 10g)
🫚 Ginger 13g
🍶 Cooking wine 5mL
🥢 Soy sauce 25mL
🫗 Sesame oil 2mL
🥜 Peanut oil 50mL

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🛍️ Sealing bag
🔥 Microwave oven
🍳 Small pot

## Cooking Steps
1. Place black cod fillets separately into sealing bags, laying them skin-side down flat on a plate.
2. Take 25g of scallion white part and cut into fine strips. Peel 10g of ginger and cut into fine strips. Mix them together, divide evenly into two portions, and spread each portion over the fish fillets inside the bags.
3. Drizzle 2.5mL of cooking wine into each bag, evenly moistening the fish fillets.
4. Seal the bags and place them in the microwave. Heat on medium power until the fish fillets are opaque and the flesh easily flakes, about 3.5 to 5 minutes. Remove the fish fillets.
   **Key point**: Microwave time varies depending on fish density. Refer to the appendix for times for sea bass, tilapia, etc. for adjustment.
5. Remove the scallion strips and ginger strips from the surface of the fish fillets.
6. Mix 25mL of soy sauce with 2mL of sesame oil until well combined. Evenly drizzle the mixture over both fish fillets.
7. Cut 10g of scallion green part into fine strips. Peel the remaining 3g of ginger and cut into fine strips. Mix them together, divide evenly into two portions, and sprinkle over the fish fillets.
8. Pour 50mL of peanut oil into a small pot and heat to 190°C. Immediately drizzle the hot oil evenly over the scallion and ginger strips on the fish. Serve right away.
   **Key point**: For a richer aroma, rub the scallion, ginger, and cooking wine onto both sides of the fish fillets to marinate briefly before microwaving.

## Health Tips
This microwaved black cod uses scallion and ginger to remove fishy odors, requires little oil, and cooks quickly, making it suitable for those who need to control their oil intake. However, the final addition of 50mL of peanut oil significantly increases calories; it is recommended to reduce the oil amount as needed or use a spray oil instead. Overall, black cod is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, and with light seasoning, this dish is a nutritious and convenient choice.
