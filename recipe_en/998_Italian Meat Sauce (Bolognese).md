# Italian Meat Sauce (Bolognese)
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Ground beef 2 lbs (fat-to-lean ratio approx. 3:7)  
🧅 Onion 1 (finely diced)  
🥕 Carrot approx. 1 (finely diced)  
🥬 Celery approx. 1 stalk (finely diced)  
🧄 Garlic 6 cloves (minced)  
🌿 Dried thyme to taste  
🌿 Dried oregano to taste  
🌿 Dried marjoram to taste  
🧈 Butter to taste  
🍷 Dry red wine to taste  
🍅 Pure tomato paste approx. 1.5 cans (large can, not ketchup)  
🍖 Beef or chicken bouillon powder to taste  
🍬 White sugar 6 tbsp  
🧂 Salt 5 tbsp  
🧂 Coarsely ground black pepper to taste (added before serving)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍳 Iron pot  
🥄 Wok spatula/metal ladle  
🪵 Wooden spatula  
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board  
🍲 Stockpot/colander  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Instructions  
1. Finely dice the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic into small pieces. The finer they are, the better they will blend into the sauce and release their nutrients.  
2. Heat the iron pot over low heat, then add butter until fully melted. **Note**: Keep the heat low throughout this step to prevent the butter from burning.  
3. Add the ground beef, increase to medium-high heat, and stir-fry until the meat is broken apart, cooked through, and the moisture has evaporated, until the surface is slightly browned. **Key**: Ensure the meat is thoroughly dry.  
4. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant, then add the diced onion and cook until soft, translucent, and aromatic.  
5. Add the diced carrot and celery, stir-fry until just cooked and the aromas have combined.  
6. Splash in a small amount of dry red wine and stir-fry to remove any off-flavors and enhance the aroma, similar to using cooking wine in Chinese cuisine.  
7. Add the pure tomato paste, increase to high heat, and stir-fry until the meat sauce turns a bright red and the tomato paste fully coats the ingredients. **Be sure to use pure tomato paste, not ketchup**.  
8. Sprinkle in the dried thyme, oregano, and marjoram in sequence, and stir-fry evenly.  
9. Add the bouillon powder, 6 tbsp of white sugar, and 5 tbsp of salt, and mix well. **Note**: The sauce should be seasoned on the salty side, as it will be mixed with unsalted pasta later.  
10. Pour in enough hot water to cover the ingredients, stir to combine, and bring to a boil.  
11. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot (or leave a small gap), and simmer for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Slow simmering over low heat is essential for developing the flavor.  
12. With about 5–6 minutes left, grind in some black pepper and stir well to preserve its aroma.  
13. In a separate pot, cook the spaghetti for 10–15 minutes until no white center remains, then drain.  
14. Ladle the meat sauce over the pasta, toss to combine, and serve. You can make a large batch of the sauce at once; it can be portioned and frozen for 1–2 months, or stored in the refrigerator and used within 3 days.  

## Health Suggestion  
This meat sauce is rich in protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for fitness or muscle-building individuals. However, the saturated fat from 2 lbs of ground beef and the 6 tbsp of sugar should be noted. It is recommended to pair with whole-wheat pasta to help balance blood sugar.
