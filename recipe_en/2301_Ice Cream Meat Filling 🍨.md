# Ice Cream Meat Filling 🍨  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork hind leg ~500g (remove fascia, cut into small pieces; fat-to-lean ratio 2:8 or 1:9)  
🌽 Sweet corn kernels, as needed  
🥚 Century egg, as needed (finely chopped)  
🥚 Salted duck egg yolk, as needed  
🍤 Shrimp, as needed  
🌿 Scallion, as needed (finely chopped)  
🍥 Dried seaweed, as needed  
🫓 Wonton wrappers, as needed  

🧂 White pepper powder, a pinch  
🧂 Ginger powder, a pinch (can substitute with ginger-scallion juice)  
🧂 Salt, 1.5 dessert spoons  
🧂 MSG, 1 dessert spoon  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, 1 dessert spoon  
🧂 Tapioca starch, 3 dessert spoons  
🧂 Baking soda, 0.5 dessert spoon  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🌀 Meat grinder  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥛 Measuring cup  
🥄 Dessert spoon  
🧊 Refrigerator (freezer)  
🥟 Tray  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the meat grinder bowl and approximately 300ml of water into the refrigerator to freeze for about 1 hour until thoroughly chilled.  
   **Note**: The bowl and water must remain sufficiently cold to prevent the meat filling from warming during mixing, which can affect texture and color.  
2. Into the chilled water, add white pepper powder, ginger powder, salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, baking soda, and tapioca starch. Stir until fully dissolved to create the ice-seasoning water.  
   **Key**: Do not add light soy sauce or soy sauce, as this may discolor the filling and produce a sour taste.  
3. Place the fresh pork pieces into the frozen meat grinder bowl. Run at high speed continuously for 15-20 seconds, pause for 2 seconds, and repeat 3 times until a coarse meat paste forms.  
4. Pour in half of the ice-seasoning water. Again run at high speed for 15-20 seconds, pause for 2 seconds, and repeat 3 times to allow the meat filling to fully absorb the liquid and become sticky.  
5. Pour in the remaining ice-seasoning water. Repeat the mixing operation 3 more times until the filling is smooth, elastic, and reaches an ice-cream-like consistency with a pinkish color.  
6. Take the desired amount of base filling and mix in any combination of sweet corn kernels, chopped century egg, salted egg yolk, shrimp, seaweed, scallion, etc., to vary the flavor.  
7. Wrap the filling in wonton wrappers, seal the edges tightly, and arrange neatly on a tray. Cover with plastic wrap and freeze.  
   **Storage tip**: When the wontons are half-frozen, separate them individually to prevent them from sticking together in a clump.  
8. To serve, cook directly in boiling water until done.  

## Health Advice  
This filling uses baking soda and ice water for mixing, resulting in a texture as delicate as ice cream. However, it is relatively high in calories, making it suitable for those aiming to build muscle or requiring a high-protein diet. Control the portion size to avoid overconsumption.
