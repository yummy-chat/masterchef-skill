# Good Morning One-Bowl Noodles
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧅 Scallion, to taste (finely chopped)  
🧄 Garlic, to taste (finely chopped)  
🥩 Beef rolls, to taste  
🥚 1 egg  
🥬 Leafy greens, to taste  
🫑 1 green chili pepper (sliced into rings)  
🍜 Noodles, to taste (dried noodles, hand-pulled noodles, etc.)  
🌶️ 1 tbsp chili flakes  
🧂 1 tsp salt  
🥄 1 tbsp light soy sauce  
🍬 A pinch of sugar  
🍶 1 tsp vinegar  
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste (a few drops for blanching + oil for drizzling)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Knife + cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Finely chop the scallion and garlic, slice the green chili pepper into rings, wash and drain the leafy greens, and beat the egg. Set aside.  
2. Prepare the bowl sauce: In a heat-resistant bowl, add the chopped scallion and garlic, chili flakes, salt, light soy sauce, sugar, and vinegar.  
3. In a separate pan, heat an appropriate amount of oil, fry one egg, and set aside.  
4. Fill the pan with water, bring to a boil over high heat, and add a few drops of cooking oil. First, blanch the leafy greens until the color deepens and they soften, then remove. Next, blanch the beef rolls until they change color, then drain.  
   **Note**: Always blanch the vegetables before the beef to prevent the meaty smell from affecting the greens; the same pot of water can be reused.  
5. Using the same pot of water, cook the noodles until no hard core remains, then drain and place them into the bowl with the prepared sauce.  
6. Ladle several spoonfuls of the hot noodle-cooking water into the bowl to release the aromas, and stir well.  
7. Top the noodles with the blanched vegetables, beef rolls, and fried egg, then garnish with the green chili pepper rings.  

## Health Advice  
This noodle dish pairs vegetables, beef rolls, and eggs, providing carbohydrates, protein, and various vitamins. It is suitable as a quick breakfast or lunch. Note that beef rolls and chili flakes are relatively high in sodium; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce the seasoning amounts accordingly.
