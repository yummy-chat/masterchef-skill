# Old-Fashioned Sweet and Sour Fish
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Carp 1 piece (scaled, gills removed, gutted, skin mucus scraped clean)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (for marinating fish and stir-frying)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (for marinating fish)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (for stir-frying)
🌽 Cornstarch 3 parts (for making batter)
🌾 Flour 1 part (for making batter)
🌿 Cilantro a little (for garnish)
🧂 Salt a little (for marinating fish and seasoning the sweet and sour sauce)
🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for marinating fish)
🌶️ Ground white pepper appropriate amount (for marinating fish)
🟤 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount (for marinating fish)
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount (for making batter, deep-frying fish, and brightening the sauce)
🍎 Rice vinegar appropriate amount (for making sweet and sour sauce)
🥢 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (for making sweet and sour sauce)
🟤 Dark soy sauce a little (for making sweet and sour sauce and adjusting color in the sauce)
🍬 White sugar 1 large tablespoon (for making sweet and sour sauce)
🧊 Cornstarch powder appropriate amount (for making sweet and sour sauce, used for thickening)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle
🪓 Cutting board
🔪 Scaling knife/Chef's knife
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl
🍚 Bowl
🧻 Kitchen paper towels
🪣 Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1.  Clean the carp thoroughly. Starting about two finger-widths below the head, make a straight cut down to the bone. Then, at a 45° angle, slice the knife toward the head to create a pocket. Make two shallow cuts on the flesh of the fish body and a cross-shaped cut on the tail section.
**Note**: These cuts help the fish absorb flavor, hold the batter, and set its shape during frying.

2.  Place scallion segments, ginger slices, salt, cooking wine, ground white pepper, and Sichuan peppercorns in a bowl. Pinch and rub the aromatics to release their juices. Add the fish and rub the marinade evenly all over the inside and outside of the fish, including inside the cuts. Marinate for about thirty minutes.
**Note**: Ensure every part of the fish is evenly coated with the marinade for full flavor.

3.  In a bowl, combine cornstarch and flour in a 3:1 ratio. Add water and mix until a smooth batter forms. Finally, add a little vegetable oil and mix well.
**Note**: The batter should be thick enough to coat your hand. Adding oil makes the fried surface smoother and crispier.

4.  In a small bowl, combine rice vinegar, light soy sauce, a touch of dark soy sauce, salt, one large tablespoon of white sugar, and cornstarch powder. Stir well to make the sweet and sour sauce. Set aside.
5.  Remove the marinated fish from the aromatics. Pat the surface dry with kitchen paper towels. Place the fish in the batter and use your hands to evenly coat the entire fish, inside and out.
**Note**: Patting the fish dry helps the batter adhere firmly.

6.  Heat enough oil in a wok to 70% of its smoking point (approx. 350°F/180°C). Holding the fish by the tail, carefully lower it into the oil with the help of a spatula. Hold it in place for about twenty seconds to let the surface set and harden. Then, gently release the fish completely into the oil. Use chopsticks to gently move and press the fish. Fry until the fish feels light and no steam/water sounds are heard. Remove and drain.
**Note**: Maintain the oil temperature throughout frying. The fish is fully cooked when it feels very light, indicating a crispy exterior and tender interior.

7.  In a clean wok, heat a small amount of oil. Sauté the minced garlic and scallions until fragrant. Pour in the prepared sweet and sour sauce. Cook over high heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon. If the color is too light, add a small amount of dark soy sauce. Remove the wok from the heat and quickly stir in one ladleful of hot oil to brighten the sauce.
**Note**: Cook the sauce over high heat quickly to prevent it from losing its flavor.

8.  Place the fried fish on a serving plate. Immediately pour the hot sweet and sour sauce evenly over the fish. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.
**Note**: Pour the sauce while the fish is still hot to maintain its crispy texture.

## Health Tips
Sweet and sour fish is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. It is rich in high-quality protein and minerals, making it suitable for people needing nutritional supplementation. However, due to the frying process and the sugar content in the sauce, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume in moderation and pair with light vegetables to balance the fat intake.
