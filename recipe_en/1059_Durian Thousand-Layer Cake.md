# Durian Thousand-Layer Cake
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season dish

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 225g eggs
🥛 340g milk
🌾 108g cake flour
🥛 750g heavy cream
🍈 750g fresh durian flesh (pitted, recommended: Monthong variety)
🧈 20g butter
🧂 54g granulated sugar (for the batter)
🍬 37g powdered sugar (for whipping cream)
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (approx. 5g, for greasing the pan)

## Kitchen Utensils & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Spatula/scraper
🥄 Hand whisk
🌀 Electric mixer
🧊 Strainer (flour sifter)
🥔 Potato masher
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🎂 Turntable
🖌️ Oil brush
📦 Plastic wrap

## Steps
1.  Heat the butter over hot water until fully melted into liquid.
2.  Beat the eggs, then add milk and granulated sugar, stirring until the sugar has completely dissolved. If the ingredients are refrigerated, you may place the bowl over warm water while stirring, but ensure the liquid temperature does not exceed 36°C (97°F).
3.  Pour in the melted butter and stir well. Sift in the cake flour, and mix until no dry flour is visible.
4.  Strain the batter through a sieve 3 times to obtain a smooth and fine batter.
5.  Pit the durian flesh and remove any hard stems. Use a potato masher to mash the durian into a puree; a few fibers may remain.
6.  Add powdered sugar to the heavy cream, and using an electric mixer over ice water, whip until stiff peaks form—when the whisk is lifted, the cream forms firm, short peaks.
7.  Preheat a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat for about ten seconds. Lightly brush the pan with a thin layer of oil.
8.  Pour one ladleful of batter into the center of the pan, quickly rotate the pan to spread the batter evenly across the bottom, and pour any excess batter back into the bowl. If the pan is properly preheated, you should hear a faint sizzle when the batter hits the pan.
9.  Keep cooking over medium-low heat. When the surface of the crepe is covered with fine bubbles and the edges start to curl slightly, use a spatula to help lift the edge and invert the pan to remove the crepe. If there are any bits stuck to the pan during cooking, clear them promptly using chopsticks or wipe clean with a damp paper towel.
10. Place one crepe on the turntable. Spread approximately 101g of durian cream filling evenly over the crepe with an offset spatula, leaving about 1cm (0.4 inches) around the edge unfilled.
11. Cover with another crepe, gently press it flat, and adjust thickness by rotating. Repeat the "one crepe, one layer of filling" process until all the crepes and filling are used.
12. Wrap the assembled cake in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2–4 hours. Insufficient chilling will result in dry, firm crepes.
13. Remove from the fridge, slice, and enjoy. The crepes are tender and melt in your mouth, while the filling is rich and smooth like ice cream.

## Health Suggestion
Durian thousand-layer cake is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to cut it into small slices and enjoy in portions, paired with black coffee or green tea to reduce the cloying sweetness. Perfect for sharing with friends to avoid consuming too many calories at once.
