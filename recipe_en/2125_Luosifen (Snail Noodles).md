# Luosifen (Snail Noodles)
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐚 River snails 300g (tails cut off, cleaned, blanched)
🦴 Pork bone 1 piece (broken, blanched)
🥔 Potato starch/sweet potato starch 80g
🍚 Rice flour 80g
🥬 Leafy greens, as needed
🥒 Pickled long beans, a handful
🥜 Peanuts, a handful
🫘 Fried tofu skin 1 piece (broken into small pieces)
🫘 Gluten chunks 3 pieces
🎋 Pickled bamboo shoots, as needed
🧂 Salt 1.5 tbsp (for broth), 2g (for rice noodles)
🧂 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp
🍶 Cooking wine 2 tbsp
🌿 Fennel seeds, a handful
🫒 Cooking oil 15g
🌶️ Chili oil, to taste
🫚 Ginger, as needed (sliced)
⭐️ Star anise, as needed
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed
🌳 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🍃 Bay leaves, as needed

## Tools & Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🍳 Wok
🥘 Stockpot/soup pot
🔧 Noodle press/饸饹 press
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Glass bowl
🔲 Cutting board

## Instructions
1. **Prepare snails and pork bone**
   - Use kitchen scissors to cut off the tails of the snails. Rinse repeatedly until the water runs clear.
   - Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the snails for 3 minutes, then remove and rinse again.
   - Bring another pot of water to a boil. Blanch the broken pork bone for 3 minutes, then remove and rinse clean. Set aside.

2. **Stir-fry the base aromatics**
   - Heat oil in a wok over low heat. Add ginger slices, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves. Stir-fry until fragrant.
   - Add the prepared snails and continue stir-frying for about 3 minutes, until the snail aroma is released.

3. **Simmer the broth**
   - Transfer the stir-fried snails and blanched pork bone to a large pot. Add about 2500ml of water.
   - Add 1.5 tbsp salt, 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 2 tbsp cooking wine, and a handful of fennel seeds.
   - Cover with a lid. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for 2 hours. The longer the simmer, the richer the broth.

4. **Make the rice noodles**
   - In a glass bowl, combine 80g starch, 80g rice flour, and 2g salt.
   - Add 130ml of freshly boiled water and stir quickly until no dry flour remains. Knead into a dough.
   - Transfer the dough to a cutting board and knead until slightly sticky. Gradually add 15g of cooking oil, continuing to knead until the dough is smooth and elastic.
   - Bring a pot of water to a boil. Place the dough into a noodle press and press the noodles directly into the boiling water.
   - Once the noodles are cooked, turn off the heat. Remove and rinse under cold water to make them chewy and non-sticky.

5. **Assemble the dish**
   - Place the rinsed noodles in a bowl. Top with blanched greens, pickled long beans, peanuts, fried tofu skin, gluten chunks, and pickled bamboo shoots.
   - Drizzle with chili oil to taste.
   - Ladle the hot broth over the noodles. Mix well and serve.

## Health Advice
The broth of Luosifen is made by slow-simmering snails and pork bone for two hours, rich in calcium and umami, but also high in sodium and fat. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of leafy greens and use peanuts and fried tofu skin in moderation. Suitable for those who enjoy bold flavors and need calcium supplements. Individuals with high blood pressure should limit their intake of the broth.
