# Qinzhen Rice Noodles
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🛢️ Rapeseed oil, appropriate amount (based on 500g oil as base ratio)  
⚪ Hulled white sesame seeds, 30g per 500g oil  
🌿 Spice powder, 25g per 500g oil (ground mixture of fennel, star anise, cinnamon, Sichuan pepper, cardamom, sand ginger, amomum, bay leaf, thyme, nutmeg, vanilla, clove, bitter beans, gromwell, etc.)  
🌶️ Fine chili powder, 75g per 500g oil (Shaanxi Qinchuan pepper, ground very fine)  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (cut into chunks, for removing fishy smell from oil)  
🥬 Scallion, appropriate amount (cut into sections, for removing fishy smell from oil)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced, for frying oil; additionally, minced ginger for searing vinegar)  
🍶 Aromatic vinegar (Shaanxi aromatic vinegar), one bag (about 500g)  
🌿 Spices for spice water, 1 portion (star anise, cinnamon, bay leaf, Sichuan pepper, long pepper, cardamom, fennel, amomum, clove, cassia twigs, etc.)  
🌱 Cooked bean sprouts, appropriate amount (as base layer in bowl)  
🥬 Cooked celery, appropriate amount (chopped, as final garnish)  
🍜 Rice noodles, appropriate amount (prepared in advance)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for seasoning rice noodles when mixing)  
🧂 MSG, a pinch (for seasoning rice noodles when mixing)  
🍶 Baijiu, a splash (added to chili oil at the end)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥘 Cooking pot  
🥄 Wooden spatula or wooden spoon  
🫗 Strainer  
🍚 Lidded ceramic bowl (for chili oil)  
🍚 Ceramic bowl (for holding ingredients and mixing rice noodles)  
🥄 Spoon  

## Steps
### Preparation
1. Grind various spices into a fine powder according to the recipe, mix well to make the spice powder.
2. Cut onion into chunks, scallion into sections, and ginger into slices; set aside as aromatics for deodorizing.
3. Prepare the dried spices for making the spice water.
4. Prepare the garnishes: cooked bean sprouts and chopped cooked celery.
### Making the Seasoned Oil
1. Pour rapeseed oil into the wok and heat over high heat until it emits heavy smoke. Turn off the heat to remove the raw oil flavor.
2. Once the oil temperature drops slightly, add the onion chunks, scallion sections, and ginger slices.
3. Turn the heat back to low and fry the vegetable aromatics until they turn dry and yellow and release aroma. Turn off the heat and strain out all residues with a strainer to obtain the seasoned oil.
### Making the Chili Oil
1. Remove the seasoned oil from heat. When the oil temperature drops to about 200°C, add the hulled white sesame seeds and stir until the seeds turn slightly yellow and float.
2. When the oil temperature drops to 185°C–190°C, evenly sprinkle in the spice powder, using the residual heat to release the aroma.
3. When the oil temperature drops to 160°C–170°C, evenly sprinkle in the fine chili powder to fully extract the color and fragrance.
4. Drizzle a small amount of aromatic vinegar and baijiu along the edge of the wok to quickly lower the oil temperature, preventing the chili from burning while enhancing color and aroma.
5. Pour the finished chili oil into a large lidded bowl, cover, and let it steep overnight to thicken the oil and intensify the flavor.
### Making the Vinegar Water
1. Bring 1.5 kg of water to a boil in a pot. Add the dried spices for the spice water and boil for about 1 minute until the water turns a faint tea color. Immediately strain. The spice residue can be reused several times.
2. Heat a small amount of oil in another pot. Sauté the minced ginger until fragrant, then pour in the aromatic vinegar and "sear" it to release the aroma and remove the raw, astringent taste.
3. Pour 4–5 times the amount of spice water into the seared vinegar to dilute the sourness. Bring to a boil and remove from heat.
4. Strain out the minced ginger, and let the vinegar water cool before use.
### Mixing the Rice Noodles
1. Place cooked bean sprouts at the bottom of a bowl, then add the cut rice noodles.
2. Season with salt and MSG, then add half a spoonful of vinegar water.
3. Take a small bundle of rice noodles, dip and swirl it in the thick, red chili oil to evenly coat the noodles.
4. Place the chili-oil-coated rice noodles into the bowl, and garnish with chopped cooked celery.

## Health Tips
This rice noodle dish features rich, aromatic chili oil and crisp, tangy vinegar water. Bean sprouts and celery add a refreshing touch, making it ideal for stimulating the appetite in summer or cutting through greasiness year-round. Those with sensitive stomachs should control the amount of vinegar water, and due to the relatively high calorie content of the chili oil, it is recommended to consume in moderation.
