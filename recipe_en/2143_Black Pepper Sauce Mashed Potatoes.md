# Black Pepper Sauce Mashed Potatoes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Potatoes 2 (peeled and sliced into thin pieces)  
🧅 Onion appropriate amount (shredded, used for aroma then removed)  
🥛 Milk appropriate amount (to adjust consistency)  
🍯 Condensed milk appropriate amount (to enhance flavor and sweetness)  
🧈 Small butter appropriate amount (added while hot to enhance creaminess and smoothness)  
🧂 Salt a small amount  
🌿 Black pepper appropriate amount (provides main flavor)  
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount  
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (about half a bowl of cornstarch slurry for thickening)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Bowl (for pressing mashed potatoes and shaping)  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Pan/saucepan (for making sauce)  
🍽️ Plate  
📄 Plastic wrap  

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the potatoes and cut into evenly thin slices. Arrange them flat on the steamer rack, cover, and steam until fully cooked.  
   **Key point**: Slices should be thin and even, easy to mash with a tool when cooked.  
2. Place the steamed potato slices in a bowl and use a tool to crush them into a fine, sandy mashed potato with no lumps.  
3. While hot, add butter, milk, condensed milk, and a small amount of salt. Stir thoroughly until well combined and the mashed potatoes are smooth.  
   **Key point**: Work while hot to help butter melt and ingredients blend.  
4. Line a bowl with plastic wrap, fill it with the seasoned mashed potatoes, press firmly.  
   **Key point**: Press firmly for easy unmolding and shaping.  
5. Add a little oil to the pan, add shredded onion, and stir-fry over low heat until slightly browned and fragrant, with edges turning light golden.  
6. Turn off the heat, add black pepper, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white sugar. Stir briefly.  
   **Key point**: Add seasonings after turning off heat, use residual heat to mix, avoiding high heat that burns.  
7. Turn on the heat, add hot water, and bring the sauce to a boil.  
8. Remove and discard the shredded onion.  
   **Key point**: Only retain the onion aroma, discard the solids.  
9. Pour the prepared cornstarch slurry into the sauce while stirring continuously, cook until thick and smooth.  
   **Key point**: Stir quickly to prevent lumps.  
10. Invert the bowl with mashed potatoes onto a plate, lift the bowl to form a hemisphere of mashed potatoes.  
11. Evenly pour the cooked black pepper sauce over the mashed potatoes, garnish with a sprinkle of chopped green onions.  
    **State judgment**: The sauce should be fluid and glossy.  

## Health Advice
Potatoes provide high-quality complex carbohydrates, milk and condensed milk supply calcium, making this suitable for those needing energy replenishment. The addition of butter increases calorie content, so portion control is advised.
