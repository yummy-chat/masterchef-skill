# Anhui Ban Mian (Anhui-Style Pulled Noodles)
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🐂 Beef brisket 500g (with sinew and fat, cut into large chunks)
🥚 Eggs Approximately 6 (hard-boiled and peeled)
🌭 Sausages/Hot dogs Approximately 3
🍢 Tofu skin Appropriate amount (cut into long strips and tied into knots)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (Red Sun variety) 50g (soaked in water for 30 minutes and drained)
🧅 Onion 50g (cut into pieces)
🥕 Carrot 50g (cut into pieces)
🧅 Scallion 50g (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger 30g (cut into pieces)
🧄 Garlic 50g (cut into pieces)
🌿 Cilantro roots 20g (optional)
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g

## Seasonings
🫒 Vegetable oil 250g (for making spiced oil, rapeseed or soybean oil preferred)
🫒 Vegetable oil 20g (for kneading dough)
🍲 Beef tallow hot pot base 1 piece (approximately 90g)
🫘 Broad bean paste 25g
🍅 Ketchup 50g
🧂 Soy sauce 20g
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (optional)
🍬 Rock sugar 20g
🧂 Chicken essence/MSG/Beef powder Appropriate amount (optional, for umami)
🧂 Salt Appropriate amount (season after 30 minutes of simmering, season boiling water for noodles, and adjust before serving)
⭐ Star anise 1 piece
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn 2g
🌿 Cassia bark 2g
🌰 Black cardamom 1 piece
🌱 Cumin seeds 2g
🍃 Bay leaves 2 pieces
⚪ White pepper 2g
🌽 Cornstarch Appropriate amount (to prevent sticking during rolling and stretching)

## Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for soaking ingredients)
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying base and simmering)
🥄 Strainer/Skimmer (for removing fried spices)
🧻 Paper towels (for drying ingredient surfaces)
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥖 Rolling pin
🍲 Stockpot
🥄 Slotted spoon (for picking up noodles and eggs)
🧵 Spice bag (for holding dried spices)

## Steps
1.  Soak the beef brisket in clean water for about 1 hour, remove and pat dry with paper towels. Cut into relatively large pieces.
    **Key Point**: Do not cut the pieces too small for a better texture; soaking and drying helps remove blood and reduce oil splatter when frying.
2.  Boil the eggs in cold water for 7 minutes, remove and cool in cold water, then peel and set aside. Cut the tofu skin into long strips and tie into knots. Thaw the sausages and set aside.
3.  Soak the dried chili peppers in water for 30 minutes, drain and pat dry with paper towels. Soak the dried spices in water for about 10 minutes, drain and pat dry, then place them in a spice bag.
    **Key Point**: Soaking and drying prevents the spices from burning and oil splatter when frying.
4.  Cut the onion, carrot, scallion, ginger, garlic, and cilantro roots into pieces or sections. Weigh and prepare the broad bean paste, ketchup, soy sauce, oyster sauce, rock sugar, and other seasonings separately.
5.  Dissolve salt in clean water, then gradually pour it into the flour while stirring to form flocculent pieces. Add 20g of vegetable oil and knead into a dough. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 10 minutes, then knead until the surface is smooth. Cover again with a damp cloth and rest for another 20 minutes or more.
    **Key Point**: The dough needs sufficient resting time for better extensibility.
6.  Pour 250g of vegetable oil into a wok and heat to approximately 100°C. Add all the vegetable aromatics and fry over low heat until they turn golden brown and withered. Remove and drain the oil thoroughly.
7.  Reheat the oil used for frying the vegetables. Add the dried chili peppers and beef pieces. First, increase to high heat to bring the oil temperature back up, then reduce to low heat and fry slowly for about 10 minutes, until some spots on the chili peppers darken.
8.  Add the hot pot base, broad bean paste, and ketchup to the wok. Stir-fry over low heat for about 2 minutes, until the hot pot base is completely melted and red oil and savory aromas are released.
9.  Add enough clean water and bring to a boil over high heat. Add the spice bag, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and rock sugar. Then, add the boiled eggs, sausages, and tofu skin knots. Reduce to low heat and simmer for 1-2 hours, until the beef is fork-tender and the side ingredients are flavorful.
    **Key Point**: Add salt to season after 30 minutes of simmering. For a flavor closer to commercial versions, you can add chicken essence, MSG, or beef powder. If the liquid level gets low, replenish with boiling water.
10. Place the rested dough on a surface dusted with cornstarch. Roll it into a thick, rectangular sheet. Cut into strips of moderate width. Dust with cornstarch to prevent sticking. Stack a few strips together, use a rolling pin to gently lengthen and shape them. Hold both ends of a strip, and slap and stretch it on the surface to the desired length and thickness.
    **Key Point**: Slapping is key to achieving a chewy and smooth noodle texture. You can first slap into thick noodles, then pull each strand individually into a thin noodle.
11. Bring a separate pot of water to a boil, add a small amount of salt. Add the pulled noodles and cook, boiling them 2-3 times until just done. You can also blanch a few green vegetables at the same time. Ladle the cooked noodles and vegetables into bowls.
12. Arrange the braised eggs, sausages, tofu skin knots, and beef on top of the noodles. Ladle the rich, red braising liquid over the top to soak the noodles. Adjust the saltiness of the final bowl as needed.

## Health Advice
Anhui Ban Mian is high in protein and carbohydrates, suitable for manual laborers or those who enjoy strong flavors. However, it is also high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the diet. People with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should be cautious with the spicy oil and hot pot base.
