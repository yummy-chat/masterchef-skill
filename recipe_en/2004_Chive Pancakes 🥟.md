# Chive Pancakes 🥟  
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs: 4 (for scrambling) + 1 (raw, mixed into filling)  
🧈 Firm Tofu: 250g (diced)  
🥬 Chives: 240g (washed and finely chopped)  
🌾 All-Purpose Flour: 200g  
💧 Boiling Water: 65g  
💧 Room-Temperature Water: 65g  
🌿 Ginger: 1/2 tsp (grated into paste)  
🧂 Salt: to taste  
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorn Powder: to taste  
🦪 Oyster Sauce: 1 tbsp  
🍂 Five-Spice Powder: 1/8 tsp  
🌶️ White Pepper Powder: 1/8 tsp  
🌿 Vegetable Oil: as needed (used in batches for frying tofu, scrambling eggs, and pan-frying pancakes)

## Cookware & Equipment  
🪵 Wooden Bowl (for kneading and resting dough)  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🥣 Mixing Bowl / Chopsticks  
🔪 Cutting Board / Kitchen Knife  
🍳 Non-Stick Pan  
🥄 Spatula / Turn Spatula  
🥖 Rolling Pin  
🥣 Small Bowl (to assist with shaping)  
🪵 Wooden Cutting Board (for placing finished pancakes)

## Procedure  

1. **Knead the Dough**: Add 200g flour to a bowl. First, pour in 65g boiling water and stir, then add 65g room-temperature water and stir until no dry flour remains. Knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Key Point**: The hot-water dough method makes the finished wrapper softer.

2. **First Resting**: Cover and let the dough rest for 15 minutes.

3. **Second Kneading & Resting**: After resting, knead the dough for 1–2 minutes until smooth, shape into a ball, and cover again to rest for 1 hour.  
   **Key Point**: Adequate resting improves dough elasticity, preventing tearing during assembly.

4. **Prepare the Filling**:  
   - Beat 4 eggs with 1/4 tsp salt. Heat oil in a pan over low heat, pour in the eggs, and stir quickly with chopsticks to create small curds. Remove and let cool.  
   - Dice the firm tofu into small cubes. Heat oil in the pan over medium heat, pan-fry until golden brown on the surface. Add salt and Sichuan peppercorn powder, stir evenly, then remove to cool.  
   - Wash the chives, pat dry, and chop finely.  
   - In a large bowl, combine the chopped chives, tofu cubes, and scrambled eggs. Add oyster sauce, five-spice powder, white pepper powder, and grated ginger, then crack in one raw egg. Mix thoroughly until well combined.  
   **Key Point**: Tofu and eggs must be cooked and fully cooled before mixing with chives; the raw egg adds binding; season lightly to highlight the natural flavors; do not add extra salt when mixing the chive filling.

5. **Divide & Roll Out Wrappers**: Dust the work surface with dry flour. Take out the dough, knead it into a ball, poke a hole in the center, stretch it into a long log, and cut into 9 equal pieces. Flatten a piece and roll it into a round wrapper, slightly thicker in the center and very thin at the edges.

6. **Assemble the Pancakes**:  
   - **Method 1**: Drape the wrapper over a small bowl, fill generously with filling, press down firmly, gather the edges toward the center to seal tightly, pinch off any excess dough, then invert the bowl to release the wrapped pancake.  
   - **Method 2**: Hold the wrapper in your palm, add filling, and use a baozi-style pleating technique to seal the top tightly, pinching off any excess dough.  
   **Key Point**: The goal is to pack in plenty of filling and seal tightly, ensuring a thin wrapper and generous filling.

7. **Pan-Fry the Pancakes**:  
   - Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Place the assembled pancakes sealed-side down into the pan.  
   - Gently press and spread the pancake into a round shape with your hand, pushing from thick areas toward the thin edges.  
   - Fry until the bottom is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side until golden and the pancake puffs up slightly. Remove from the pan.  
   **Key Point**: Starting sealed-side down helps set the shape; avoid tearing the wrapper when spreading; since the filling is already cooked, only the wrapper needs to be fully cooked and browned—chives cook quickly, so no prolonged frying is needed.

## Health Tips  
These chive pancakes feature a thin wrapper with generous filling. Tofu and eggs provide high-quality protein, while chives are rich in fiber, making this a suitable dish for nourishing the liver in spring. Be aware that chives release liquid easily, so prepare the filling and assemble just before cooking. For a healthier result, pan-fry over medium heat and control the oil quantity.
