# Chongqing-Style Fish-Flavored Shredded Pork
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork shoulder, appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, about 2 stalks
🌶️ Chongqing local pickled chilies, 5-6 pieces (stems and seeds removed, finely chopped)
🫚 Chongqing local pickled ginger, small piece (finely chopped)
🧄 Garlic, 3-4 cloves (minced)
🥚 Egg yolk, 1
🧂 Salt, a pinch
🧂 White pepper, appropriate amount
🫒 Sesame oil, a dash
🌾 Bean starch (or sweet potato starch, cornstarch), appropriate amount
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for marinating seal, stir-frying, and finishing drizzle)
🍬 Granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons
🍇 Fragrant vinegar, 3 tablespoons
🧂 Light soy sauce, half a tablespoon
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount (for color adjustment)
💧 Wet bean starch (water starch), appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowls
🥄 Spoons
🥢 Chopsticks
❄️ Refrigerator

## Instructions
1.  Cut pork shoulder into thin shreds. Add a pinch of salt, pepper, and sesame oil, mixing well. Add small amounts of water gradually, working until the shreds are fully hydrated. Then add egg yolk and mix thoroughly. Add bean starch to coat, and finally drizzle cooking oil to seal in moisture. Refrigerate for 20 minutes to marinate.
**Note:** Shreds must fully absorb water; refrigeration helps set, ensuring a tender texture.

2.  Cut scallions into small sections. Finely chop pickled chilies and pickled ginger, and mince garlic. In a separate bowl, combine a pinch of salt, 2 tablespoons sugar, 3 tablespoons fragrant vinegar, appropriate pepper, half a tablespoon light soy sauce, and appropriate dark soy sauce. Stir until sugar dissolves. Add wet bean starch and mix well.
**Key Point:** The pre-mixed sauce is crucial for the fish-flavor profile; sweet, sour, and salty need balance. Stir thoroughly again before use.

3.  Heat ample oil in a wok over medium heat. Add marinated pork shreds and quickly separate them with chopsticks. Stir-fry until shreds are completely white and cooked through, then remove and drain oil.
**Note:** Oil temperature should not be too high; quickly separate to prevent sticking. Remove as soon as color changes to avoid overcooking.

4.  Leave a little base oil in the wok. Over high heat, add chopped pickled chilies, pickled ginger, and garlic. Stir-fry rapidly until fragrant and red oil is released.
5.  Add the drained pork shreds and stir quickly to combine. Add scallion sections. Stir the pre-mixed sauce thoroughly again, then pour it along the wok edge. Stir-fry over high heat for about 30 seconds, letting the sauce fully coat the ingredients. Before plating, drizzle a little reserved cooking oil from the meat for shine. Serve immediately.
**Key Point:** Add scallions last to keep them crisp; the sauce must be fully stirred before adding to prevent starch from settling.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein, but pickled chilies and pickled ginger are high in sodium. Pair with light vegetables to balance the meal. Its sweet-sour-spicy flavor stimulates appetite, ideal for when you lack appetite, but control sugar and oil intake.
