# Thousand-Layer Meat Pie
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍞 All-purpose flour 250g  
💧 Room temperature water 150g (for dough)  
🥩 Ground pork belly 250g (recommended 30% fat, 70% lean)  
🫚 Ginger about 5g  
🧅 Scallion white part 10g (for scallion-ginger water) + one palm-length section (chopped for filling)  
🫒 Cooking oil 10g (for dough) + 20g (for filling)  
🫒 Sesame oil 10g  
🌾 Dry flour as needed (for dusting to prevent sticking)  
💧 Water 85g (for scallion-ginger water, use 75g after straining)  
🧂 Salt 3g (for dough) + half teaspoon about 1–2g (for filling)  
🍬 White sugar 1 teaspoon  
🧂 Chicken bouillon or MSG 1 teaspoon  
🍂 Five-spice powder or thirteen-spice powder 1/3 teaspoon  
🥄 Light soy sauce 2/3 tablespoon  
🥄 Dark soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🥄 Oyster sauce half tablespoon  

## Tools and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍽️ Cutting board or silicone mat  
♨️ Electric griddle (or frying pan)  
💨 Oil spray bottle  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🫗 Strainer  
🫙 Small bowl (to cover dough while resting to prevent drying)  

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**: Mix 250g flour with 3g salt evenly. Gradually add 150g water, stirring into flakes. Add 10g cooking oil and knead until the surface is relatively smooth.  
   **Key point**: The dough has high hydration and may be sticky; wear gloves if needed. Adding oil enhances crispness and prevents sticking.  
2. **Rest the dough**: Cover and let rest for 10 minutes. Knead briefly until the surface is smooth, divide into two equal portions, shape into balls, cover again, and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Resting improves dough elasticity for easier rolling.  
3. **Prepare the filling**: In 250g ground meat, add salt, sugar, chicken bouillon/MSG, five-spice powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and oyster sauce in order.  
   - **Scallion-ginger water**: Crush 10g scallion white and ginger, add 85g water, squeeze to release flavors, strain, and use 75g.  
   - Add the scallion-ginger water to the meat in three portions, stirring in the same direction each time until the meat becomes sticky, forms a paste, and shows stringy texture.  
   - Finally, add 20g cooking oil and 10g sesame oil, mixing until fully absorbed.  
   **Key point**: Adding water in portions and stirring in one direction is key to a tender, juicy filling. Adding oil afterward locks in moisture.  
4. **Mix in scallions**: Chop the palm-length scallion section. Mix into the filling just before wrapping to avoid off-flavors.  
5. **Roll the dough**: Dust the work surface with dry flour. Take one dough ball, flatten it, and roll into a thin sheet about 28cm square.  
   **Key point**: The dough is soft; dust with flour as needed to prevent sticking. Do not roll too large or thin.  
6. **Divide and spread the filling**: Use a scraper to score the dough into a tic-tac-toe pattern, creating 9 sections. The top row should be widest, the middle row medium, and the bottom row narrowest. Spread the filling evenly over all sections except the bottom row, leaving a small border at the edges.  
   **Key point**: Do not spread filling on the scored cuts, to prevent sticking during folding.  
7. **Fold and wrap**: Start from the bottom blank section, fold upward and press gently to release air. Fold the lower left corner to the right, then the upper left corner downward. Fold the right side of the dough to the left, and finally fold the wide top section downward to cover the entire pie. Pinch all openings tightly.  
   **Key point**: After each fold, press lightly to release air. Use the dough's elasticity to gently stretch it when covering.  
8. **Second rest and shaping**: Place the wrapped dough seam-side down on a floured surface, cover with a bowl, and let rest for about 15 minutes. After resting, gently press the rolling pin from the center outward, rolling into a square pie about 2cm thick and 18cm on each side.  
   **Key point**: Apply gentle pressure to avoid tearing and leaking filling.  
9. **Cook**: Preheat the electric griddle with top and bottom heat. Once hot, spray oil, place the pie, and spray oil on top. Cover and cook for about 8 minutes, flipping every 2 minutes, until both sides are golden brown, the pie puffs up, and pressing feels springy—indicating it is cooked through.  
   **Key point**: If using a frying pan, cook on the lowest heat. When flipping, insert the spatula under the center of the pie to avoid breaking.  
10. Remove from heat, cut into pieces. Layers should be distinct, crispy outside, tender inside, and the filling juicy.  

## Health Tips
Thousand-layer meat pie uses ground pork belly with scallion-ginger water to achieve a tender, juicy texture, but it is relatively high in calories. Pair it with a light vegetable soup to balance nutrition. Suitable for those needing energy replenishment, such as manual laborers, or for family gatherings. People with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should control their portions.
