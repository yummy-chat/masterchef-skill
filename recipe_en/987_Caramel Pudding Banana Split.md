# Caramel Pudding Banana Split
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
💧 Water 30g
🍬 Granulated Sugar 55g (for caramel) + 25g (for pudding mixture)
🚰 Hot Water 30g
🥚 Eggs 2 large
🥛 Milk 250g
🍶 Heavy Cream 240g
🍌 Banana 1 (halved lengthwise)
🥝 Kiwi appropriate amount (sliced)
🍊 Orange appropriate amount (cut into pieces)
🍎 Apple appropriate amount (sliced)
🍓 Strawberry appropriate amount (sliced)
🍦 Vanilla Ice Cream appropriate amount
🍫 Chocolate Sauce appropriate amount
🌈 Colorful Sprinkles appropriate amount
🍒 Candied Cherry 1

## Cookware & Equipment
♨️ Small Saucepan
🔥 Induction Cooktop/Stove
⚖️ Kitchen Scale
🥄 Whisk
🥣 Glass Bowl
🍶 Measuring Cup
🔍 Strainer
📦 Baking Mold (small rectangular or round)
♨️ Deep Baking Pan
🔆 Aluminum Foil
🔥 Oven
🧊 Refrigerator
🍨 Boat-Shaped Glass Bowl
🥄 Ice Cream Scoop

## Instructions
1. Make the caramel: Pour 30g water and 55g granulated sugar into a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat without stirring throughout. Cook until the syrup turns amber. Then carefully add 30g hot water, stir to combine, and immediately remove from heat. Pour into the bottom of the mold to form an even layer.
2. Make the pudding mixture: Beat the eggs, add 25g granulated sugar, and stir until well combined. In a separate pan, combine milk and heavy cream. Heat until small bubbles appear around the edges and it is about to boil, then remove from heat. Slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the egg mixture in small amounts while whisking quickly to avoid cooking the eggs. Then strain the mixture through a strainer into a measuring cup to remove impurities and bubbles.
3. Prepare the fruit: Wash and peel the banana, kiwi, orange, apple, and strawberry. Cut into appropriate slices or pieces and set aside.
4. Bake the pudding: Pour the caramel and pudding mixture into the mold in order. Cover tightly with aluminum foil. Place the mold in a deep baking pan and pour hot water into the pan, ensuring the water level is roughly level with the pudding mixture. Place in a preheated oven at 150°C and bake using a water bath for about 40 minutes, until the pudding is fully set and no longer moves in the center.
5. Chill and set: After removing the pudding from the oven, let it cool to room temperature, then transfer to the refrigerator to chill for at least 4 hours, allowing it to fully set for a smoother texture.
6. Assemble the banana split: Unmold the pudding onto the center of a boat-shaped glass bowl. Arrange the sliced banana, kiwi, orange, apple, and strawberry around it. Scoop a ball of vanilla ice cream onto the side. Pipe whipped cream onto the pudding and top with a candied cherry. Finally, drizzle chocolate sauce over the ice cream and sprinkle with colorful sprinkles.
   **Note**: Place the pudding first before arranging other ingredients to avoid insufficient working space. Use a rich variety of fruits for a more vibrant color.

## Health Tips
This dessert is high in calories, so it is recommended to share it with friends. Pairing with fresh fruit can increase dietary fiber intake. Suitable for occasional afternoon tea enjoyment, be mindful of total sugar intake.
