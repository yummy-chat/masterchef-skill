# Oyster Omelette 🦪
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦪 Small oysters - to taste (thumb-sized, rinse 1-2 times and drain)
🥚 Eggs - 2 (traditionally use sea duck eggs)
🧅 Garlic sprouts - to taste (finely chopped; substitute with scallions if preferred)
🍠 Sweet potato starch - 100g
🐟 Fish sauce - to taste (used for seasoning both egg mixture and batter)
🧂 White pepper powder - to taste (to remove fishy smell)
🧂 Salt - a pinch
🐖 Lard - to taste (for frying)
🍅 Ketchup - to taste (for dipping)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Spatula
🥣 Glass bowls (multiple)
🥄 Stainless steel strainer
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Lid (to assist with flipping)

## Instructions
1. Gently rinse the oysters with water 1-2 times, then drain thoroughly.
*Use thumb-sized oysters; larger ones will affect texture.*
2. Crack eggs into a bowl, add some fish sauce, and whisk until well combined.
*Fish sauce enhances umami.*
3. Finely chop garlic sprouts and set aside.
4. Place oysters in a bowl, add chopped garlic sprouts, 100g sweet potato starch, some white pepper, a little fish sauce, and salt. Mix by hand until no dry starch remains.
*The starch will become fluid as it absorbs the moisture released by the oysters.*
5. Heat a frying pan over medium heat, then add lard and melt completely.
6. Pour in the oyster batter, quickly spread it evenly with a spatula, and fry over medium heat until the bottom is set and fragrant.
*Spread to an even thickness for consistent texture.*
7. Evenly pour the egg mixture over the oyster omelette, and continue frying until the egg is partially set.
8. Use the lid to press down on the pan and assist in flipping the omelette in one piece to avoid breaking. Fry the other side until golden.
9. Once both sides are golden and crispy, remove from the pan and plate.
10. Serve with fish sauce and ketchup as dipping sauces.

## Health Advice
This oyster omelette is fried in lard and is rich in protein and zinc, making it suitable for adults needing zinc supplementation. However, those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Pairing with ketchup and fish sauce helps cut the greasiness.
