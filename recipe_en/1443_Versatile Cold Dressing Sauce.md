# Versatile Cold Dressing Sauce
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌶️ Qin chili powder 50g
🌶️ Millet chili powder 25g
🧅 Scallion appropriate amount
🧅 Onion appropriate amount
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount
🫒 Rapeseed oil approximately 500g
🫘 White sesame seeds one handful
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount
🌶️ Millet chili appropriate amount

## Seasonings
🌰 Spice blend (star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, cardamom, cumin, Sichuan peppercorns, white cardamom) appropriate amounts each
🍾 Fragrant vinegar a little (for aroma)
🥃 Baijiu a little (for aroma)
🥫 Donggu soy sauce 15g
🍾 Fragrant vinegar 40g
🌶️ Spicy fresh sauce 30g
🥫 Chicken bouillon 15g
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn oil 30g
🧂 Salt 10g
🍬 White sugar 6g
🧂 MSG 2g
🫙 Prepared chili oil 200g
🧅 Minced scallion, minced ginger, minced garlic, millet chili rings, chopped cilantro appropriate amount

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Metal strainer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl/large bowl
🥄 Spoon
🔪 Knife, cutting board

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the Qin chili powder and millet chili powder evenly, divide into three equal portions and set aside.
2. Soak the spices in water in advance to moisten, then drain thoroughly.
3. Cut the scallion, onion, ginger slices, and cilantro for frying; separately prepare minced garlic, minced ginger, millet chili rings, minced scallion, and chopped cilantro and set aside.
4. Pour rapeseed oil into the wok, heat over high heat until smoking, then turn off the heat.
**Note**: Soaking spices in water beforehand prevents them from burning when added to the oil; fry using residual heat after turning off the heat.
5. Immediately add scallion, onion, ginger slices, and cilantro, fry until slightly yellow, then add the soaked spices and fry until withered.
6. Remove all solids, retaining only the clear oil.
7. Prepare the chili oil: Heat the flavored oil, and when the temperature drops to about 60-70% heat, turn off the heat.
- First, add white sesame seeds; once they float, add the first portion of chili powder.
   - When the oil temperature drops to about 50% heat, add the second portion of chili powder.
   - When the oil temperature drops to about 40% heat, add the remaining chili powder and stir well.
   - Drizzle a little fragrant vinegar and a splash of baijiu along the edge of the wok.
   **Key point**: Adding chili powder in batches creates layered flavors; vinegar enhances color and baijiu releases aroma.
8. Pour the chili oil into a large bowl, cover, and let it cool completely.
9. Prepare the sauce base: In a bowl, sequentially add Donggu soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, spicy fresh sauce, chicken bouillon, and Sichuan peppercorn oil, then add salt, white sugar, and MSG. Stir until dissolved.
10. Add 200g of cooled chili oil and the prepared minced scallion, ginger, garlic, millet chili, and chopped cilantro. Mix thoroughly to complete the versatile cold dressing sauce.

## Health Advice
This cold dressing sauce is rich in vitamin E and capsaicin, which can stimulate appetite and metabolism. It is suitable for those who prefer strong flavors but need to limit oil intake, used in small amounts. It is recommended to pair with high-fiber vegetables to neutralize greasiness, while being mindful of salt intake.
