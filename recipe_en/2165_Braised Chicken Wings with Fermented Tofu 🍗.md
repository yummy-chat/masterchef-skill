## Instructions
1.  Make several cuts on the surface of the chicken wings. Add cooking wine, light soy sauce, and white pepper powder. Mix well and marinate for 20 minutes. Shred the dried tofu. In a small bowl, mash 2 pieces of fermented tofu with a little fermented tofu sauce. Prepare ginger slices, scallion segments, dried chili peppers, rock sugar, and star anise.
2.  Heat the pan over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Without adding oil, place the marinated chicken wings skin-side down in the pan. Slowly fry over low heat until all sides are golden brown and slightly crispy, and the chicken fat is fully rendered.
    **Note**: Maintain low heat throughout. Use the fat from the chicken skin; no additional oil is needed to prevent burning.
3.  Pour a splash of cooking wine along the edge of the pan. Add the mashed fermented tofu mixture and stir-fry until evenly combined.
4.  Pour in enough boiling water to just submerge the ingredients. Add ginger slices, scallion segments, dried chili peppers, rock sugar, and star anise. Lay the shredded dried tofu on top. Drizzle a little dark soy sauce for color.
    **Key Point**: Boiling water must be used to prevent the meat from becoming tough due to temperature shock.
5.  Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat. Cover with a lid and braise for 30 minutes.
6.  Remove the lid and increase to high heat to reduce the sauce. Stir constantly until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients. Turn off the heat and transfer to a plate.
    **Note**: Monitor the heat carefully while reducing the sauce to prevent scorching. Stop once the sauce has thickened.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. Fermented tofu and soy sauce are high in sodium; people with high blood pressure should consume less of the sauce. Braising utilizes the fat from the chicken skin, reducing additional fat intake, but portion control is still recommended.
