# Korean Sweet & Spicy Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken feet, appropriate amount
🧄 Garlic, 1.5 heads (minced)
🫚 Ginger, a few slices (for blanching to remove odor)
🧅 Scallions, a few sections (for blanching to remove odor)
🌿 Bay leaf, 1 piece
⭐ Star anise, 2 pieces
🌶️ Korean chili paste (gochujang), 3 tbsp
🍅 Ketchup, 3 tbsp
🍬 Rice syrup (mulyeot), 2 tbsp (or substitute with 2 tbsp white sugar)
🧂 Dasida beef stock powder, 1.5 tbsp (or substitute with 2 tsp MSG)
🧂 Soy sauce, 3 tbsp
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp
🌶️ Chili powder (gochugaru), 1 tbsp
🤍 Toasted white sesame seeds, 3 tbsp
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount
💧 Water, about 1/2 bowl

## Kitchenware & Equipment
✂️ Kitchen scissors
🧄 Garlic press
🍳 Pot (stockpot or skillet)
🥅 Strainer
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Use kitchen scissors to cut off the chicken feet nails. Remove any small sac-like parts on the pads that may contain mucus. Squeeze out the mucus and rinse thoroughly.
   **Note**: Clean completely to ensure the texture and hygiene of the final dish.
2. Add enough cold water to a pot. Place the chicken feet, scallion sections, and ginger slices into the pot.
   **Key Point**: Starting with cold water helps the blood drain out fully, reducing the gamey smell.
3. Put 1.5 heads of garlic into a garlic press and crush into coarse granules. Set aside.
4. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine the Korean chili paste, ketchup, rice syrup, beef stock powder, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, chili powder, and toasted white sesame seeds. Stir well and set aside.
5. Boil the chicken feet with the scallions and ginger over cold water on the stove. Use a strainer to skim off the foam from the surface. After skimming, add the bay leaf and star anise.
   **Key Point**: The foam is the main source of the gamey smell; make sure to skim it clean.
6. After boiling and skimming over high heat, reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
7. Remove the cooked chicken feet from the pot and drain well.
8. In a separate pan, add an appropriate amount of vegetable oil and heat it. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry over high heat until fragrant.
9. Pour in the cooked chicken feet and the prepared sauce. Quickly stir-fry to coat each chicken foot evenly with the sauce.
10. Add about half a bowl of water to the bowl that held the sauce. Swish to rinse off any remaining sauce, then pour this mixture into the pan.
11. Bring to a boil over high heat, then cover the pan and reduce to low heat. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    **Key Point**: This step allows the chicken feet to fully absorb the flavor of the sauce.
12. Uncover the pan, increase to high heat, and reduce the sauce until thickened. Stir frequently with a spatula during this process to prevent sticking.
    **Key Point**: The sauce should become thick and evenly cling to the chicken feet; do not reduce it until it is too dry. Stir continuously throughout the reduction process.
13. Plate the chicken feet and drizzle the remaining sauce from the pan over the top.

## Health Advice
This Korean sweet and spicy chicken feet dish is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those looking to improve skin or increase protein intake. However, it is high in sodium, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables or rice to balance the flavors and reduce the seasoning burden.
