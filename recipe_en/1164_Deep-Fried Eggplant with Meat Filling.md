# Deep-Fried Eggplant with Meat Filling
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 1 long eggplant (about 250g, peeled)
🐖 300g ground pork
🧄 Minced scallion and ginger, to taste
🌿 Five-spice powder (or Sichuan pepper powder/Chinese thirteen-spice), to taste
🍶 Soy sauce blend, to taste
🧂 MSG, to taste
🧂 Salt (season meat filling to 60% saltiness, batter to 30% saltiness)
🥔 Small amount of water starch (added to meat filling)
🍶 Small amount of cooking wine
🥚 1 egg (for batter)
🫒 About 25g cooking oil (for batter), plus more for frying
💧 About 150g water (6 tablespoons)
🥔 Starch, as needed (small amount for meat filling, plus for batter and dusting)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Vegetable peeler
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Tablespoon
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Flat plate

## Steps
1. **Prepare the eggplant**: Use a vegetable peeler to peel the long eggplant. Cut it into linked slices, each slice about 2mm thick, with a 1cm connection between each pair.
– **Note**: Slices should be evenly thick, and the connection should not be too short to prevent the filling from falling apart.

2. **Prepare the meat filling**: In 300g of ground pork, add minced scallion and ginger, five-spice powder, soy sauce blend, MSG, salt to 60% saltiness, a small amount of water starch, and a small amount of cooking wine. Stir in one direction until the filling becomes firm.
– **Key point**: Adding water starch locks in moisture; stirring until firm makes the filling tight and juicy.

3. **Assemble the eggplant pockets**: Take a pair of linked eggplant slices and open them like the character “八“. Fill generously with meat filling, press the slices together, and smooth the filling at the edges to form an eggplant pocket.
– **Note**: The filling should be fully packed, with no exposed edges, and the shape should remain neat.

4. **Prepare the crispy batter**: In a bowl, beat 1 egg. Add about 25g cooking oil, 150g water, and salt to 30% saltiness, and stir well. Gradually add starch and mix by hand until the batter sticks to your hand. Adjust thickness by adding a small amount of water if needed.
– **Key point**: The batter should be thick enough to coat evenly, neither too thin nor too thick.

5. **Dust and coat**: First, evenly dust each eggplant pocket with dry starch. Then dip it into the batter, coating thoroughly. Lift it out and scrape off excess batter against the edge of the bowl.
– **Note**: Dusting with starch enhances adhesion; scraping off excess batter prevents “batter whiskers” during frying.

6. **First frying (set shape)**: Heat oil to about 50% hot. Add the eggplant pockets one by one. Fry over medium-low heat slowly. Once you hear a distinct “sizzling” sound from the pockets, remove and drain.
– **Judgment**: The pockets should be set and light golden; the meat inside should be fully cooked.

7. **Second frying (crisp and color)**: Raise oil temperature to 70% hot. Add all the first-fried eggplant pockets and fry for about 2 minutes, until the exterior is golden and crispy, and you hear the sound of moisture escaping from the meat filling.
– **Note**: The second frying makes the exterior crispier and prevents oil absorption. Do not over-fry.

8. **Remove and serve**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the eggplant pockets, drain excess oil thoroughly, and arrange on a plate. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This dish is oil-absorbing and rich in meat filling. It is suitable for occasional indulgence rather than regular consumption. Pair it with a light vegetable soup or cold dish to reduce overall fat intake. Those with high blood lipids or on a weight-loss diet should control portion sizes.
