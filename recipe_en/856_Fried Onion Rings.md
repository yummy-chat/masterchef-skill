# Fried Onion Rings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🧅 Sweet onions 2 (white onions can also be used)
🌾 All-purpose flour 100 g (for vinegar-water batter) + 120 g (for crispy coating mix) + about 30–50 g (for initial base dusting)
🌽 Cornstarch 120 g
💧 Water 200 g
🧂 Salt 1/2 tsp
🍶 White vinegar 60 g
🍞 Baking powder 2 tsp
🍞 Baking soda 1/4 tsp

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, knife
🥣 Wooden bowl
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🥄 Whisk
🍽️ Plate/bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍳 Deep pot/fryer
🕸️ Oil strainer/slotted spoon
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Instructions
1. **Prepare onions**: Peel onions and cut horizontally into rounds about 1 cm thick. Separate into rings.
   - Leftover trimmings can be saved for stir-frying.
2. **Marinate onions**: Place onion rings in a bowl, sprinkle with 1/2 tsp salt, gently mix well, and let sit for 15 minutes.
   - **Key point**: Marinating causes the onions to release moisture and soften, helping the coating adhere later.
3. **Make vinegar-water batter**: Mix 200 g water with 60 g white vinegar and 100 g flour. Stir until smooth with no lumps.
   - **Key point**: This batter increases stickiness on the onion surface, making it easier for the dry coating to adhere.
4. **Make crispy coating mix**: Combine 120 g flour, 120 g cornstarch, 2 tsp baking powder, and 1/4 tsp baking soda. Mix well.
   - **Key point**: This is the dry mix used for the frying coating.
5. **Initial base dusting**: Sprinkle about 30–50 g of the crispy coating mix over the marinated onion rings. Gently toss so each ring is thinly coated.
   - **Key point**: Using the onion’s own moisture for this first “base” dusting is crucial for the success of subsequent steps.
6. **Coat with wet batter**: Using chopsticks, pick up a base-dusted onion ring, fully submerge it in the vinegar-water batter, coat evenly, then remove and scrape off excess batter against the bowl edge.
   - **Key point**: Using chopsticks is more convenient and avoids using your hands.
7. **Coat with dry mix**: Place the batter-coated onion ring into the remaining crispy coating mix. Turn and gently press to evenly coat with a thick layer of dry mix.
   - **Key point**: White vinegar and baking soda react during frying to create bubbles, making the crust crispier.
8. **First frying**: Heat oil to 165°C. Add the coated onion rings one by one. Fry over medium-low heat until they float and turn light golden.
   - **Key point**: This step cooks the onions through and sets the batter coating.
9. **Remove and clean oil**: Using a strainer, remove all onion rings and place on a plate lined with kitchen paper towels to drain. Also remove any residue from the oil.
   - **Key point**: Cleaning out oil residues prevents them from burning and affecting flavor during the next frying.
10. **Second frying**: Increase oil temperature to 190°C. Return all onion rings to the oil and fry over high heat for about 30 seconds to 1 minute, until deep golden and the crust is hard and crispy.
    - **Key point**: High-temperature refrying quickly forces out excess oil, achieving an extremely crispy texture.
11. **Remove and plate**: Quickly remove the refried onion rings, drain oil, and serve on a plate.

## Health Tips
Fried onion rings are rich in carbohydrates and contain a small amount of dietary fiber. They are suitable as an occasional party snack. However, high-temperature frying increases calories. It is recommended to serve with a low-fat dipping sauce and control portion sizes. Use oil-absorbing paper to remove excess grease to avoid excessive intake.
