# Seaweed and Shrimp Egg Pancake
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Fresh shrimp 10 pieces (deheaded, peeled, deveined, and cut into small pieces)
🍘 Dried seaweed 1/5 sheet (part of a large sheet, soaked until softened and squeezed dry)
🥚 Eggs 4 pieces
🧅 Scallion a small amount (chopped into scallion flowers)
🧂 Salt a small amount (for marinating shrimp)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for seasoning egg mixture)
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for greasing the pan and frying in batches)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Ice bowl (or regular bowl)
🔪 Cutting board and kitchen knife
🥄 Glass bowl (for mixing egg mixture)
🥣 Stainless steel basin or small bowl (for holding ingredients separately)
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥘 Spatula
🔥 Gas stove
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the shrimp**: Soak fresh shrimp in ice water for a moment. The thermal expansion and contraction will make the shells easier to peel. Dehead, peel, and make a shallow cut along the back to remove the vein. Rinse clean and cut into small pieces. Add a pinch of salt, mix well, and marinate for 2 minutes.
2. **Prepare the seaweed**: Soak dried seaweed in room-temperature water for about 2 minutes. Remove and squeeze out the water forcefully. ⚠️ Be sure to squeeze it completely dry to prevent the pancake from releasing moisture.
3. **Prepare the egg mixture**: Crack 4 eggs into a bowl, add an appropriate amount of salt, and whisk thoroughly until well combined. Add the chopped scallion, the squeezed-dry seaweed, and the marinated shrimp. Stir to mix.
4. **Scramble the egg mixture halfway**: Heat the pan over medium heat, add an appropriate amount of oil to coat the surface. Pour in half of the egg mixture and quickly stir-fry until about 70% cooked. Remove and pour it back into the bowl with the remaining raw egg mixture.
5. **Combine raw and cooked egg mixtures**: Quickly stir the half-cooked eggs and the raw egg mixture together until the raw egg mixture evenly coats the cooked egg pieces.
6. **Fry the egg pancake**: Add oil to the pan again, reduce the heat to low, and pour in all of the combined raw and cooked egg mixture. Use a spatula to spread it flat and shape it into a round pancake. Increase the heat to medium and fry for about 3 minutes, until the bottom is set and turns a golden brown. Cover the pancake with a lid, invert the pan to flip the pancake, and slide it back into the pan. Continue frying the other side until golden brown and fragrant.
7. **Cut and plate**: Transfer the fried egg pancake to a cutting board. Trim off the uneven edges, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces. Place the trimmings on the bottom of the plate, and arrange the intact diamond pieces into a flower shape.

## Health Advice
The seaweed and shrimp egg pancake is rich in high-quality protein and iodine. It is suitable for children who need calcium supplementation and adults seeking antioxidant benefits. Shrimp and seaweed are low in fat and high in fiber. It is recommended to use a small amount of oil and fry slowly over low heat to prevent the pancake from absorbing too much grease.
