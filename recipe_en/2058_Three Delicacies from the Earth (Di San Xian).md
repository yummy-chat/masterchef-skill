# Three Delicacies from the Earth (Di San Xian)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Eggplant
🥔 Potato
🌶️ Green pepper
🧅 Scallion
🧄 Ginger
🧄 Garlic
🥫 Doubanjiang (fermented bean paste)
🧂 Light soy sauce
🍬 Sugar
🌾 Starch

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥣 Bowl

## Steps
1. Peel the potato and cut it into irregular small pieces, about 15 grams each, along with the eggplant. Tear the green pepper along its grain into pieces roughly 25% larger than the potato and eggplant pieces, and pat dry the surface moisture with a kitchen towel. Cut the scallion into 0.5 cm sections, mince the garlic into a paste, and mince the ginger. In a bowl, combine 10 ml light soy sauce, 10 grams sugar, 8 grams starch, and 80 ml water. Stir thoroughly to create a sauce mixture and set aside.
   **Key point**: Removing moisture from the green pepper prevents oil splatter when frying. The sauce mixture should be prepared in advance to avoid clumping when added to the wok.

2. Heat the wok and add 150 ml oil. Fry the potato pieces for about 3–4 minutes until the surface is golden brown and a chopstick can easily pierce through, then remove and drain the oil. In the same wok, fry the eggplant pieces for about 2–3 minutes until soft, with slightly charred edges and oil releasing, then remove. If there is insufficient oil in the wok, add 15 ml more. Fry the green pepper pieces for about 15 seconds until the edges show blistering, then immediately remove and pour out the oil from the wok.
   **Note**: Moisture from the green pepper may cause oil splatter when added; use a lid for protection.

3. Reheat the wok, add 15 ml fresh oil. Sauté the minced scallion, ginger, and 7.5 grams of garlic paste over medium-low heat until fragrant. Add 15 ml doubanjiang, and stir-fry over low heat until the oil turns red and the sauce is aromatic.
   **Key point**: Doubanjiang must be stir-fried over low heat to prevent burning.

4. Pour in the prepared sauce mixture, immediately turn to high heat and stir vigorously. After about 10 seconds, when the sauce bubbles and thickens, it is ready.

5. Quickly add all the fried potato, eggplant, and green pepper pieces. Stir-fry over high heat for 20 seconds to evenly coat the ingredients with the sauce.

6. Turn off the heat, sprinkle in the remaining 7.5 grams of garlic paste, toss to combine, and plate.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, but it is relatively high in calories due to frying. It is recommended to pair it with a light soup or reduce the amount of staple food. Eggplant is highly absorbent to oil, and potatoes are high in starch; this dish is suitable for occasional consumption by those with high physical exertion, while individuals with high blood lipids should eat it in small amounts.
