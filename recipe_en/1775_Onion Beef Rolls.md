# Onion Beef Rolls
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧅 1 onion (sliced)  
🥩 About 200g beef rolls  
🫚 Some ginger slices (for blanching)  
🧅 Some scallions (chopped)  
🧂 About 2 tbsp light soy sauce  
🧂 Some dark soy sauce  
🧂 Some oyster sauce  
🍬 About 1 tbsp sugar  
🧂 About 1 tbsp salt  
🌾 Some starch  
🍶 Some cooking wine  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🍳 Frying pan  
🥄 Measuring spoon  
🥄 Spatula  
🥣 Strainer  

## Steps  
1. Slice the onion into even thin slices; chop the scallions and set aside.  
2. Prepare the sauce: Mix light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, salt, starch, and a small amount of water, stirring until well combined.  
3. Bring water to a boil in a pot, add ginger slices and cooking wine, then add the beef rolls. Remove immediately once they change color, and drain using a strainer to discard excess water and foam.  
   **Note**: Blanch the beef rolls briefly; remove as soon as the color changes to prevent them from becoming tough.  
4. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat, add the onion slices, and stir-fry until they soften and release their aroma.  
5. Add the blanched beef rolls and stir-fry quickly to combine.  
6. Evenly pour in the prepared sauce, and continue stir-frying until the sauce evenly coats the beef and onions.  
7. Before removing from heat, sprinkle in the chopped scallions, mix well, and plate.  
   **Key point**: Stir-fry quickly after adding the sauce to prevent the starch from settling and clumping.  

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for replenishing blood and quickly restoring energy. However, beef rolls are high in fat, so it is recommended to serve with leafy green vegetables. Be mindful of the amount of salt and soy sauce used; those with high blood pressure can reduce the seasoning by half.
