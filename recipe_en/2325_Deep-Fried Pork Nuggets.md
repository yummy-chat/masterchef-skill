# Deep-Fried Pork Nuggets

Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 One pound of pork hind leg (choose lean meat, cut into dice-sized pieces)  
🥔 Potato starch, about 1/3 of the weight of the pork (potato starch is essential for a crispy texture)  
🧅 Scallion, to taste  
🧄 Ginger, to taste  
🧄 Garlic, to taste  
🧂 7 grams of salt  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
💁 Appropriate amount of cold water (for soaking starch and making scallion-ginger-garlic water)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🪣 Stainless steel basin  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🍳 Iron wok  
🌡️ Oil thermometer  
🥢 Strainer  
🪓 Wooden cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the potato starch in a basin, add cold water without stirring, and let it sit for about 10 minutes to settle. Pour off the clear water on top and use the settled wet starch from the bottom.  
**Key Point**: This step prevents unsoaked starch from causing oil splattering during frying.  

2. Cut the pork hind leg into uniform dice-sized pieces. If washed, drain thoroughly.  
3. Crush the scallion, ginger, and garlic, add 7 grams of salt, MSG to taste, and water. Squeeze with your hands until the liquid becomes sticky, then strain out the solids and reserve the liquid.  
4. Place the pork pieces in a basin, gradually add the scallion-ginger-garlic water, and mix well with your hands to allow the meat to fully absorb the liquid.  
**Key Point**: Adjust the amount of water based on the freshness of the meat.  

5. Add the settled wet starch and mix until the meat is coated with a thin batter, with the meat color faintly visible. If too dry, add a small amount of water.  
6. Heat oil to 170–200°C. Individually scatter the pork pieces into the wok; you should see dense bubbles forming around them. Fry until the coating hardens and sets, then remove. Work in batches to maintain the oil temperature.  
7. Maintain the same oil temperature and fry all the pork pieces a second time. The meat color will initially show through; continue frying for about 2 minutes until the exterior is golden and crispy, and the inside is fully cooked.  
**Note**: Do not let the oil temperature get too high during the second fry, or the potato starch will expand rapidly.  

8. When the pork pieces turn golden, the coating has natural bumps and wrinkles, and a rich meat aroma is released, remove them and drain the oil.  

## Health Tips  
Pork hind leg lean meat is rich in protein, suitable for those needing energy replenishment or fitness, but deep-frying is high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with a vegetable salad to balance the fat. Control the oil temperature during the second fry to prevent the potato starch from over-expanding and affecting the texture.
