# Honey Date Zeng Cake
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍚 Long glutinous rice (Jiangmi) 500g (long-grain, softer and stickier than round-grain)  
💧 Water 500g (same amount as dry rice, for steaming the rice)  
🫒 Honey dates 300g (Shanxi honey dates, dry or wet, preferably pitted)  
🫒 Red dates 250g (Xinjiang or northern Shaanxi variety, preferably seedless)  
🫘 Red kidney beans 150g (large grains, purple kidney beans also work)  
🍬 White sugar 50g (optional, used during re-steaming)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
♨️ Electric pressure cooker (for steaming glutinous rice and pressing beans)  
🍲 Steamer with a perforated rack (ensures steam circulation)  
🥣 Regular pot (for boiling beans and steaming dates)  
🧽 Stainless steel basin (for washing and soaking)  
🥄 Strainer (to drain glutinous rice)  
🪵 Wooden spatula (for mashing dates)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for poking holes)  
🫗 Large bowl (for softening lotus leaves)  

## Instructions  
1. Soak the long glutinous rice for at least 12 hours, drain, then place in an electric pressure cooker. Add 500g water and cook on high pressure for 25–30 minutes.  
**Key point**: Use long-grain glutinous rice; water amount should equal the dry rice.

2. Wash the honey dates and red dates, then steam for 30 minutes. Choose pitted varieties if possible; when tearing the honey dates, check and remove any pits.

3. Wash and soak the red kidney beans for at least 12 hours, then cook until extremely soft but with the skins intact.  
**Heat control**: About 30 minutes in an electric pressure cooker, or 40–50 minutes in a regular pot.

4. On a perforated steamer rack, create the first layer: spread half of the cooked kidney beans, then tear half of the honey dates and evenly cover the beans. **Do not add red dates to this layer.**

5. Spread the steamed glutinous rice over the honey date layer. Use your hand or a spoon to flatten and lightly press it down. Wet your hands slightly to prevent sticking.

6. Add the remaining kidney beans on top, then cover with the remaining torn honey dates.

7. Place all the steamed red dates as the top layer. Use chopsticks to evenly poke holes all the way to the bottom.  
**Key point**: Poking holes allows steam to circulate evenly, ensuring consistent texture throughout the cake.

8. Cover the surface with softened lotus leaves and tightly close the steamer lid. Optionally, place a weight on the lid to improve the seal and prevent condensation from dripping onto the cake.

9. Steam over high heat for 1 hour, add water if needed to prevent drying, then reduce to low heat and steam for another 2 hours.

10. After turning off the heat, keep the lid closed and let it rest in the residual heat for at least one night to allow flavors to fully meld.

11. The next day, remove and sprinkle 50g white sugar on top. Re-steam over high heat until thoroughly heated through.

12. Use a wooden spatula to mash the surface red dates into a fine paste. Lift and fold to mix evenly with the glutinous rice and kidney beans below. Serve.

## Health Advice  
This Zeng cake is rich in carbohydrates. The glutinous rice, honey dates, and kidney beans together provide long-lasting energy, making it suitable for those who need sustained stamina or have a sweet tooth. Be mindful of portion size; it is recommended to consume in moderation for those with high blood sugar or on a weight-loss diet.
