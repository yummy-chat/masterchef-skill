# Five-Cup Chicken
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Whole chicken, about 1250g (choose a chicken with small beak, small feet, thin legs, large tail, and plenty of yellow fat)
🫚 Ginger, 50g (sliced)
🧅 Pearl onions (shallots), 50g (whole)
🫑 Red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, as needed (cut into chunks)
🧅 Scallions, as needed (cut into segments)
🌿 Thai basil (holy basil / "gold no change"), 50g (fresh leaves)
🧂 Salt, about 5g (for marinating)
🥜 Peanut oil (or cooking oil), as needed (for marinating)
🧈 Chicken fat, 75g (for starting the pan)
🥫 Light soy sauce (生抽), 25g
🍾 Rice vinegar (white vinegar), 25g
🍬 White sugar, 25g
🌶️ Worcestershire sauce (喼汁 / American Tabasco-style pepper sauce), 25g
🍶 Rice wine (米酒), 25g

## Cooking Tools & Equipment
🫕 Clay pot (sand pot)
🔪 Kitchen scissors
🍳 Non-stick pan
🍳 Wok
🔥 Gas stove
🥄 Large soup ladle
🥣 Glass bowl

## Steps
1. Clean the whole chicken and chop it into pieces about 3 cm square ("sun-shaped" pieces).
2. Add 5g of salt to the chicken pieces and mix well, then drizzle in some cooking oil and mix again. Marinate for a short while.
3. Heat the clay pot, then add 75g chicken fat and melt it.
4. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry over high heat for about 1 minute, until the surface of the chicken shrinks, some moisture evaporates, and the aroma of chicken fat is released.
5. Add the ginger slices and pearl onions, continue to stir-fry over medium-high heat for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
6. Add 25g each of light soy sauce, rice vinegar, white sugar, Worcestershire sauce, and rice wine in sequence. Stir well to let the alcohol evaporate and coat the chicken pieces evenly with the sauce.
7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the clay pot with its lid, and braise for about 15 minutes, until the sugar has cooked into a glaze and the chicken pieces are glossy and red.
8. Remove the lid, add the Thai basil, bell pepper chunks, and scallion segments. Stir-fry briefly and let the sauce reduce slightly.
   **Note**: Thai basil is the soul spice; it must be added at the very end to preserve its aroma.
9. Use a large soup ladle to transfer the dish to a serving plate. Serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This Five-Cup Chicken uses chicken fat as a base, blending the five flavors of sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, wine, and Worcestershire sauce. It provides quality protein, but is relatively high in sugar and fat. It is recommended to serve with stir-fried vegetables and control portion sizes.
