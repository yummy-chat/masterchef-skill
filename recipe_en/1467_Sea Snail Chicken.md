# Sea Snail Chicken  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 Free-range chicken, half (chopped into small pieces)  
🐚 Sea snails, as needed  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (use the white part, cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Thai bird's eye chilies, as needed (chopped, optional)  
🧂 Salt, as needed  
🌿 Ground white pepper, as needed  
🥄 Light soy sauce, two small spoonfuls  
🦪 Oyster sauce, a little  
🍶 Rice wine, as needed  
🌾 Cornstarch, a small amount  
🥜 Peanut oil, a little  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🪓 Wooden cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥣 Large bowl  
📦 Aluminum foil (two sheets)  
🍳 Wok (or frying pan)  
🔥 Gas stove  

## Instructions  
1. Rinse the free-range chicken and chop into evenly sized pieces. Place them in a large bowl.  
2. Add minced ginger, white scallion sections, chopped Thai bird's eye chilies (if using), along with salt, ground white pepper, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice wine, and cornstarch. Finally, drizzle in a little peanut oil. Mix thoroughly with your hands until the seasonings are fully coated on the chicken pieces. Set aside to marinate.  
   **Note**: Be cautious with the salt, as sea snails have a natural saltiness; avoid making the final dish too salty.  
3. Stack two sheets of aluminum foil and lay them flat. Spread the rinsed sea snails evenly in the center as a base layer.  
4. Place the marinated chicken, along with any leftover marinade liquid, on top of the sea snails. Lift the four edges of the foil, fold them together, and press firmly to seal completely.  
   **Key Point**: Always use two sheets of aluminum foil to prevent tearing. The sea snail base helps distribute heat evenly and prevents the chicken from burning.  
5. Place the sealed foil packet into the wok. Gently press it down to ensure good contact with the wok bottom. Cover with a lid and cook over low heat.  
6. Once steam begins to escape from the wok, start timing. Cook over low heat for 10 minutes.  
   **Observation**: The foil packet will gradually puff up and expand.  
7. After turning off the heat, remove the foil packet. Open it and transfer the contents to a serving plate. The juices from the chicken and seafood are fully retained, resulting in tender chicken and a beautifully blended, savory flavor.

## Health Advice  
Sea snails are rich in zinc and protein, while chicken is low in fat and high in protein. This dish is an ideal choice for muscle building and brain health. Be mindful to use less salt, as sea snails naturally provide a salty and savory taste, helping to control sodium intake and support healthy blood pressure.
