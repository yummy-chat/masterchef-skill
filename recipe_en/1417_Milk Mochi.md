# Milk Mochi
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Milk 250ml
🍬 White sugar 50g
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 160g
🌽 Cornstarch 40g
🧈 Butter 40g
🌾 Soybean flour, as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🥢 Whisk or chopsticks
Strainer
🍽️ Rectangular steaming pan or deep bowl
Plastic wrap
🦷 Toothpick
♨️ Microwave or steamer
🍚 Rice paddle
🧤 Food-grade disposable gloves
🥏 Plate

## Instructions
1. Pour the milk into a large bowl. Add white sugar, glutinous rice flour, and cornstarch. Stir until the batter is smooth and free of lumps. Then strain the batter through a strainer into the steaming pan.
   **Key point**: Straining removes lumps and ensures the batter is fine and smooth.

2. Cover the surface of the steaming pan with plastic wrap. Use a toothpick to poke several small holes in the plastic wrap.
   **Note**: Covering with plastic wrap prevents steam from dripping into the batter during steaming, and poking holes prevents the plastic wrap from expanding excessively due to heat.

3. Microwave on high heat for 4 minutes, or steam over high heat for 15 minutes, until the inside is completely set and no liquid flows.
   **Key point**: The heating time can be adjusted according to the power of your appliance; ensure the batter is fully cooked.

4. While still hot, add the butter to the steamed dough. After the temperature drops slightly, put on disposable gloves and knead repeatedly until the dough becomes smooth and can be stretched into a thin film.
   **Key point**: The butter must be fully kneaded into the dough. If the dough is too dry and breaks when pulled, add a little milk and butter and continue kneading.

5. Divide the kneaded dough into small portions, roll them into balls, and coat them evenly in soybean flour. Arrange on a plate for serving.

## Health Advice
This milk mochi is rich in carbohydrates and a moderate amount of fat, making it suitable as an energy-boosting snack. However, it contains a relatively high amount of sugar and butter, so it is recommended to control the portion size per serving. It is especially suitable for consumption in small amounts after exercise or during afternoon tea. Glutinous rice flour is difficult to digest, so those with weak digestive systems should avoid overeating.
