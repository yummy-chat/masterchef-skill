# Classic Soufflé Cheesecake
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧀 Cream cheese 100g  
🥚 Egg yolks 2 (room temperature)  
🥚 Egg whites 5 (about 150g, room temperature)  
🌾 Cake flour 50g (sifted)  
🥛 Milk 60g (room temperature)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 20g (for cheese batter)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 20g (for meringue)  
🍋 Lemon juice 5g

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowls (at least two)  
🍴 Spatula  
🕸️ Sieve  
🔌 Electric hand mixer  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🎂 Removable-bottom chiffon cake pan (line with parchment paper and wrap with aluminum foil)  
🔥 Oven  
🍽️ Deep baking pan

## Steps
1. Preparation: Bring all liquid ingredients to room temperature. Line the inside and bottom of the cake pan with parchment paper. Wrap the outside of the removable-bottom pan with three layers of aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in during baking.  
   **Key point**: All liquid ingredients should be brought to room temperature in advance to avoid batter separation due to temperature differences.

2. Make the cheese batter: Place cream cheese over a hot water bath and stir until smooth and softened. Add 20g granulated sugar, lemon juice, milk, and egg yolks one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Sift in the cake flour and mix until combined. Pass the batter through a sieve once, cover with plastic wrap, and set aside to keep warm.

3. Whip the meringue: Place all egg whites and 20g granulated sugar in a grease-free and water-free mixing bowl. Use an electric hand mixer on high speed until streaks appear, then switch to low speed to refine, whipping until soft peaks form.

4. Combine the batter: Add one-third of the meringue to the cheese batter and fold gently with a spatula until combined. Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue and fold quickly until fully incorporated. Work swiftly to avoid deflating.

5. Pour into the pan and bake: Pour the batter into the pan, filling it to 70–90% full. Tap the pan lightly to release large air bubbles. Preheat the oven. Place the pan on a rack in the middle-lower position, set the pan in a deep baking pan, and pour hot water into the baking pan. Bake at 190°C for about 25 minutes, until the surface is golden brown.  
   - If the surface browns too quickly, lower the top heat by 10°C or cover the surface with aluminum foil.

6. After baking, remove the cake from the oven, pour out the water from the baking pan, and open the oven door to let the steam escape. Allow the cake to cool slightly, then unmold and remove the parchment paper. Optionally, dust the surface with powdered sugar for decoration.

## Health Advice
This cheesecake is rich in protein but high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with black coffee to balance the sweetness, making it perfect for an afternoon treat.
