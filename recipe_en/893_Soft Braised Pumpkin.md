# Soft Braised Pumpkin
Cuisine Type: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Suggestion: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 800g old pumpkin (peeled, seeded, and cut into even small cubes)
🧄 20g garlic (lightly crushed, can be omitted if you dislike garlic flavor)
🥣 Appropriate amount of water starch (made from potato starch, used for thickening)
🫒 A little clarified oil (added before removing from heat to enhance shine)
🫒 A little vegetable oil (for sautéing and stir-frying)
🧂 About 3g table salt (added after the broth comes to a boil)

## Cookware and Equipment
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🛡️ Lid
🥣 Ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Peel the pumpkin, remove the seeds, and cut it into even small cubes. Lightly crush the garlic and set aside.
2. Heat the wok over heat, add a little vegetable oil. When the oil reaches about 50% heat, add the crushed garlic and sauté until fragrant.
   **Key point**: The oil temperature should not be too high; the garlic should make a slight sizzling sound when added and release its aroma.
3. Add the pumpkin cubes and stir-fry over medium-low heat until the edges of the pumpkin become slightly translucent and release yellow pigment.
   **Key point**: The goal is to draw out the pumpkin's natural pigment, giving the broth a beautiful golden color.
4. Add enough water to just cover the pumpkin. Bring the broth to a boil, then add the table salt.
5. Cover the wok with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes until the pumpkin is soft and tender.
   **Key point**: It is advisable to cut the pumpkin into slightly larger pieces; otherwise, it may dissolve and lose its shape.
6. Remove the lid, pour in the water starch, and stir the broth until it thickens and evenly coats the pumpkin cubes.
7. Once the broth has thickened, drizzle in the clarified oil, stir to combine, and then remove from heat and plate.

## Health Suggestions
Pumpkin is rich in dietary fiber and beta-carotene. It is soft and easy to digest, making it suitable for the elderly and those with sensitive stomachs. It is recommended to use less oil and salt. Diabetics can consume it in moderation, but should pair it with protein.
