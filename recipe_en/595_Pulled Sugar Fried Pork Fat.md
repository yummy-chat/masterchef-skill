# Pulled Sugar Fried Pork Fat

**Cuisine:** Dongbei (Northeastern Chinese)
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐
**Season:** Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐖 Pork fatback, appropriate amount (sliced to the thickness of a coin)
🌾 All-purpose wheat flour, one tablespoon
🌽 Cornstarch, one tablespoon
🥚 Egg, one (use the yolk)
💧 Water, as needed
🫒 Cooking oil, about 10 grams (for the batter) + oil for frying, as needed
🫘 White sesame seeds, as needed
🧅 Scallion, as needed
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🍶 Huadiao wine (or cooking wine), as needed
🧂 Salt, one teaspoon
🍬 Soft white sugar, one tablespoon

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Strainer / bowl
🫗 Stainless steel bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🧪 Measuring cup
🥄 Ladle
🔥 Stove
🍽️ Tray

## Preparation Steps
1.  Slice the pork fatback into pieces about the thickness of a coin.
2.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the pork slices, scallion, ginger, and Huadiao wine. Blanch for 2 minutes, then remove. Drain well, discard the scallion and ginger, and pat the surface dry with paper towels.
3.  Prepare the batter: In a small bowl, combine one tablespoon of flour, one tablespoon of cornstarch, one egg yolk, and a pinch of salt. Add water a little at a time, stirring until the batter is slightly thicker than yogurt and forms a thread when lifted with chopsticks. Finally, add about 10 grams of cooking oil and mix thoroughly.
4.  Add the drained pork slices to the batter and toss to coat each piece evenly.
5.  Frying: When the oil in the wok reaches medium heat (about 50% hot), add the pork slices one by one. Fry for about 10 seconds to set the coating, then gently push them apart. Maintain medium heat and fry for about 2-3 minutes until the pieces float and turn golden brown. Remove and drain the oil.
    **Note:** Do not stir immediately after adding the pieces to the oil; wait until the coating is set. Maintain medium heat throughout to prevent the fat from absorbing too much oil.
6.  Making the syrup: In a separate wok, add one tablespoon of water and one heaping tablespoon of soft white sugar. Drizzle a little oil along the edge of the wok. Cook over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes. The syrup will first turn a milky white with dense small bubbles. When it reaches a golden color, turn off the heat immediately.
    **Key point:** Adding oil along the edge of the wok prevents the syrup from burning. The heat *must* be turned off before adding the fried pork pieces.
7.  Coating: With the heat off, quickly add the fried pork fat pieces and sprinkle with white sesame seeds. Stir-fry rapidly and evenly so each piece is coated with syrup and sesame seeds.
8.  Immediately transfer to a tray and spread flat to cool quickly. The outside will become crispier once cooled.

## Health Advice
This dish uses pork fatback as its main ingredient, which is fried and coated in sugar, resulting in high fat content. It is suitable for an occasional indulgence but is not recommended for regular consumption by those with high blood lipids or on a weight-loss diet. Pairing it with light vegetables or green tea can help balance the greasiness.
