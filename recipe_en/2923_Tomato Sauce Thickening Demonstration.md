# Tomato Sauce Thickening Demonstration  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 2 tomatoes (cut into small pieces)  
🥔 Approx. one small bowl of potato starch  
🌽 Approx. one small bowl of cornstarch  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
💧 Water, as needed (for slurry and soup base)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Iron ladle (for stirring slurry, stir-frying, and drizzling thickening mixture)  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for preparing slurry)  
🍽️ White plate (for plating and presentation)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for presenting the finished dish)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the tomatoes into small pieces and set aside. Place potato starch and cornstarch into separate stainless steel bowls, add water to dilute into a slurry. The cornstarch slurry should be relatively thick, while the potato starch slurry should be relatively thin.  

2. Add cooking oil to the wok and heat over medium heat. Add the tomato pieces and stir-fry until fragrant and slightly softened.  

3. Add a small amount of water to form a soup base.  

4. **Cornstarch thickening demonstration**: Keep the soup base in the wok boiling. Pour the thick cornstarch slurry into the wok all at once. Stir-fry quickly until the sauce thickens and most of the water is absorbed. Transfer to a plate. The finished sauce is relatively dry with moderate coating ability.  

5. **Potato starch thickening demonstration**: In a separate wok, repeat steps 2–3. Keep the soup base boiling. Drizzle the thin potato starch slurry in small batches while stirring quickly. Once the sauce rapidly thickens, turns glossy, and evenly coats the ingredients, remove from the wok.  
   - Note: Potato starch has strong water absorption; dilute it well and add in batches to avoid clumping or over-thickening. The finished sauce has a glossy appearance and excellent coating ability.  

## Health Tips  
Tomatoes are rich in lycopene and vitamin C, and their nutrients are more easily absorbed after heating. They are suitable for those seeking antioxidant benefits. After thickening, control the amount of oil to avoid excess calories. Additionally, due to its strong water absorption, potato starch should be diluted and added in batches to prevent clumping and ensure a smooth sauce texture.
