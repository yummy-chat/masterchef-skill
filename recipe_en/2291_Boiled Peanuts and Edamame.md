# Boiled Peanuts and Edamame
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Clean the Peanuts**: Place the muddy peanuts in a basin, add water to soak for about one hour, then rub them with your hands until the water runs clear.
**Key Point**: Soaking before scrubbing makes the mud easier to remove.

2. **Prepare the Edamame**: Use scissors to cut off both ends of the edamame pods, then wash off any surface dust.
**Key Point**: Trimming the ends allows for better flavor absorption and also enhances appearance.

3. **Dry-fry Spices and Make Spice Water**:
   1. In the pan, add 50 grams of salt and all the dried spices. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
   2. Gradually add a total of about 5.5 jin (approx. 2.75 kg or 5.5 pounds of water). Bring to a boil after each addition before adding the next.
   3. Add several large slices of ginger. **Do not add spring onions**.
   4. After all the water has been added, boil for about 30 seconds, then turn off the heat.
   5. Pour the prepared spice water into a basin containing the spring onion segments. Let it **cool completely** before use.
**Key Point**: Avoid using an iron wok to prevent a reaction with tannins in the peanuts, which can cause darkening. Use spices in moderate amounts; more is not always better. Do not boil spring onions with the spices to avoid off-flavors.

4. **Boil the Peanuts**: Place the cleaned peanuts in a pot with cold water and add enough water to cover generously. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 15-30 minutes. Once done, drain and rinse under cold water until completely cool. Drain well.
**Time Reference**: 15 minutes yields a crispier texture, 30 minutes yields a softer texture.
   **Key Point**: Start with cold water, use plenty of water, and avoid using an iron wok.

5. **Blanch and Process the Edamame**:
   1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add enough salt, one small teaspoon of baking soda, and a suitable amount of cooking oil.
   2. Once the water is fully boiling, add the prepared edamame. Keep the heat on high and cook for 6-10 minutes.
   3. Immediately remove and plunge into a large bowl of ice-cold water (with plenty of ice cubes). Cool rapidly until completely cold.
**Time Reference**: 6 minutes yields a crunchy texture, 10 minutes yields a softer texture.
   **Key Point**: Adding salt, baking soda, oil, and dropping into boiling water all help preserve chlorophyll and maintain a vibrant green color. Do not blanch for too long. Must use ice-cold water for rapid cooling.

6. **Mix and Soak**: Combine the completely cooled spice water with the cooled boiled peanuts and the drained, iced edamame in an airtight container. Refrigerate and let soak overnight.
**Key Point**: All ingredients and liquids must be completely cooled before mixing to ensure proper flavor absorption and prevent spoilage. Sealing the container prevents flavor mixing and moisture evaporation.

## Health Advice
Peanuts and edamame together provide plant-based protein and dietary fiber, making them an excellent summer evening snack to accompany drinks. Note the high salt content; those with high blood pressure should consume in moderation. After soaking in the refrigerator overnight, the peanuts become soft and the edamame stays crunchy, offering an even better texture.
