# Bread Floss Meatballs
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Ground pork 500g (fresh ground meat)  
🧅 Scallion 30g  
🫚 Ginger 20g  
💧 Water 60g  
🍞 Cold steamed buns 3 (use only the inner crumb)  
🥚 Eggs 2  
🌾 Cornstarch 30g  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  
🧂 Salt 8g  
🌿 Ground white pepper 2g  
🌿 Five-spice powder 2g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥤 Blender (food processor)  
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🍳 Wok (for deep frying)  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧺 Bamboo basket/plate  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the scallion sections, sliced ginger, and water into a blender. Blend into a smooth scallion-ginger juice.  
**Note**: The finer the juice, the better. Use only the liquid to avoid charred ginger and scallion bits during frying.  

2. Split the cold steamed buns in half. Rub the inner crumb between your palms to create fine bread floss. Discard the crust and use only the soft interior.  

3. Gradually add the scallion-ginger juice to the ground pork, stirring in one direction until the mixture becomes tacky. Then add salt, ground white pepper, and five-spice powder. Mix evenly.  
**Key**: Add the juice in batches to allow the meat to fully absorb moisture and flavor.  

4. Pour the bread floss into the seasoned meat mixture. Add the eggs and mix by hand. Then add the cornstarch and mix again. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil and fold until evenly combined.  
**Note**: The eggs counteract the dryness from the bread floss; the cornstarch makes the exterior crispier; the oil moistens the filling.  

5. Heat cooking oil in a wok to 350°F (180°C). Wet your hands, shape the mixture into meatballs (either by rolling or squeezing through your fist), and use a spoon to drop them into the oil. Fry over medium heat.  
**Check**: If the meatballs float to the surface within 1–2 seconds, the oil temperature is correct.  

6. Fry until the meatballs are golden brown and set. Remove and drain excess oil.  
**Note**: Do not let the oil temperature drop too low or become too high.  

7. Raise the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Return the partially fried meatballs for a quick second fry until evenly deep golden and fully cooked. Remove immediately and drain. You should hear a crisp crackling sound as they are lifted.  
**Purpose**: The second fry forces out excess oil and creates an extra-crispy exterior.  

## Health Advice  
Combining ground pork with bread crumb provides an ideal mix of protein and carbohydrates, suitable for muscle building or those with high physical exertion. Control frying temperature to avoid excessive oil intake. Individuals with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should consume in moderation.
