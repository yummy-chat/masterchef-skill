# Lamb and Cabbage Dumplings
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Lamb leg meat, about 500g (minced into filling)
🥬 Chinese cabbage, as needed (use only the leaves, finely chopped)
🧅 Scallion, as needed (finely minced)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (finely minced, not too much)
🌾 All-purpose flour, 500g
💧 Water, about 250g (adjust based on flour's water absorption)
🧂 Salt, as needed (1 tsp for dough; appropriate amount for filling)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (soak in hot water to make Sichuan peppercorn water, removes gamey smell)
🥄 Soy sauce, as needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce, as needed
⚪ White pepper powder, about 2g
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed
🫒 Seasoned oil, as needed (oil fried with scallion, ginger, star anise, etc.)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🫗 Large stainless steel bowl
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🧂 Small strainer
🥄 Spoon
🍲 Cooking pot

## Instructions
1. **Make the dough**: Add one small spoonful of salt to the all-purpose flour. Gradually add about 250g of water, stirring as you go to form flocculent pieces, then knead into a smooth dough until your hands, the dough, and the bowl are clean.
2. **Rest the dough**: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest.
3. **Prepare Sichuan peppercorn water**: Steep Sichuan peppercorns in hot water, let cool, then strain out the peppercorns and set the liquid aside.
4. **Prepare the lamb filling**: Place the minced lamb in a bowl. Gradually add the strained Sichuan peppercorn water while stirring continuously in one direction by hand until the meat becomes firm and sticky.  
**Note**: Must be stirred by hand to reach the right consistency; Sichuan peppercorn water is key to removing the gamey smell. Avoid using cooking wine, as its flavor will not dissipate during cooking.
5. **Season the filling**: Add salt, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper powder to the prepared meat filling. Continue to stir evenly.
6. **Mix in vegetables**: Add the chopped cabbage leaves, scallion, and ginger. Mix thoroughly by hand until the vegetables soften, reduce in volume, and fully combine with the meat.  
**Note**: Do not pre-squeeze water from the cabbage; add it directly.
7. **Seal with oil**: Finally, drizzle in the sesame oil and seasoned oil, then mix well to lock in moisture and make the filling richer.
8. **Wrap the dumplings**: Knead the rested dough again, roll it into a log, cut into small pieces, roll out into wrappers, and fill with the filling.
9. **Boil the dumplings**: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and add a small spoonful of salt to prevent sticking. Gently drop in the dumplings and push them along the edge of the pot with the back of a spoon. Once the water returns to a boil, cover and simmer. When the dumplings float to the surface, uncover and add one splash of cold water. Repeat the process of boiling and adding cold water until all dumplings float and appear plump and translucent.  
**How to check doneness**: The dumpling skin becomes semi-translucent, and the filling feels firm when pressed.

## Health Tips
Lamb and cabbage dumplings are rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making them ideal for warming the body in autumn and winter. However, lamb is warming in nature, so those with a hot constitution or prone to internal heat should consume in moderation. Use Sichuan peppercorn water to remove the gamey smell and avoid cooking wine. It is recommended to serve with vinegar and minced garlic to cut the richness and aid digestion.
