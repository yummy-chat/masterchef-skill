# Braised Pork Hock Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 1 pork front hock (ask the butcher to burn the skin; front hock must be used)
🥚 Quail eggs, as needed
🌶️ Pickled ginger, as needed
🍚 Rice, as needed
🧅 Scallions, as needed (for blanching)
🫚 Ginger, as needed (for blanching)
🧂 Cooking wine, as needed (for blanching)
🍬 Rock sugar, as needed (for caramelizing)
🫒 Cooking oil, a little
💧 Boiling water, as needed
🍺 500ml beer
🥘 Sweet bean paste, two tablespoons
🥘 Fermented bean curd liquid, two tablespoons
🧴 Light soy sauce, two circles (about two tablespoons)
🧴 Dark soy sauce, one tablespoon
🦪 Oyster sauce, one tablespoon
🥄 Spare rib sauce, one tablespoon (can substitute with soybean paste)
🌿 Spice mix, as needed (Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves)
💧 Pickled ginger original juice (aged brine), as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed (final seasoning)
🍗 Chicken powder, as needed (to enhance flavor)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🍲 Electric pressure cooker
🥣 Soup ladle

## Cooking Steps
1. Scrub the charred pork front hock clean with a steel wool pad, thoroughly removing the burnt skin.
   **Key point**: Burning the skin removes gaminess, eliminates fine hairs, and improves skin texture; be sure to use a front hock.
2. Place the hock in cold water, add scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off the foam, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and cook for 30 minutes. Remove and drain.
3. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok, add rock sugar, and cook over medium-low heat until the sugar is completely melted and turns a jujube red color with dense small bubbles. Quickly add boiling water, bring to a boil, then remove and set aside as caramel color.
   **Key point**: Add water precisely when dense small bubbles appear to avoid burning the sugar bitter or black.
4. Place the hock in an electric pressure cooker. Pour in the caramel color and 500ml of beer. Add sweet bean paste, fermented bean curd liquid, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, and spare rib sauce. Add the spice pack. Strain and pour in the original broth from step 2 until the liquid submerges the hock. Then add the pickled ginger and its original juice.
5. Select the “tendon” setting and pressure cook until tender; if using a regular pressure cooker, cook over medium heat for 30 minutes after reaching pressure; if using a regular pot, simmer over low heat for at least 2 hours.
6. After opening the lid, remove the hock. Strain the sauce through a strainer to remove solids and return it to the wok. Season with salt and chicken powder as needed. Add the shelled quail eggs, cover, and simmer over the lowest heat for another 30 minutes to fully infuse the hock and eggs with flavor and reduce the sauce.
7. Place rice in a bowl, top with the braised hock, and ladle the concentrated sauce and quail eggs over the top.

## Health Advice
This braised pork hock rice bowl is rich in collagen and suitable for occasional consumption, but it is high in calories and sodium. Those with high blood pressure or on a weight-loss diet should control portions. The front hock is braised until soft and flavorful; when served with rice, pair it with a light vegetable side for a balanced meal.
