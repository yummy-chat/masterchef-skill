# Easy Salt-Baked Shrimp  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp (appropriate amount, cleaned and drained)  
🧑‍🍳 Ginger (a few slices)  
🌿 Scallions (a few segments)  
🧂 Salt (a small handful, to highlight the savory original flavor)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick flat pan (for baking the shrimp)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (to drain the shrimp)  
🔥 Stove  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Clean the shrimp and drain them well. Place them directly into the flat pan, sprinkle with a small handful of salt, and arrange the ginger slices and scallion segments.  
2. Cover the pan with a lid, turn on the heat, and bake over medium heat.  
**Key point**: No water is needed throughout the process. The shrimp are cooked using their own moisture and the osmotic effect of the salt, maximizing the retention of the natural flavor.  
3. Continue baking until the shrimp shells change completely from greyish-blue to orange-red, the bodies curl, and the meat becomes firm. Then turn off the heat.  
4. Remove the baked shrimp, arrange them on a plate, give them a quick tidy, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Shrimp are rich in high-quality protein and low in fat, making them suitable for supplementing nutrition during weight loss. People with high cholesterol can consume them in moderation. The dry-baking method preserves the natural flavor, but the amount of salt should be controlled; those with high blood pressure should use less salt.
