# Braised Pork Belly with Tofu Skin
Cuisine: Shandong Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly (cut into chunks, not too fatty)
🧈 Tofu skin (slice in half)
🫚 Ginger (sliced for sautéing)
🧅 Scallions (cut into sections for sautéing)
⭐ Star anise
🍃 Bay leaves
🫒 Vegetable oil
🧴 Light soy sauce
🧴 Dark soy sauce (for color)
🧂 Salt
🍬 Sugar (for umami)
🍶 Cooking wine (to remove gameyness and enhance aroma)
🫖 Boiling water (enough to cover ingredients)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Large iron wok
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥄 Large spoon (ladle)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Heat the wok over high heat, pour in vegetable oil, add ginger slices and stir-fry until fragrant. Add pork belly chunks and slowly stir-fry over medium-low heat until the meat turns golden brown and fat renders out.
**Key Point**: Slow stir-fry over medium-low heat to fully render the fat from the pork belly, ensuring a non-greasy texture.

2.  Add star anise and bay leaves, stir-fry until fragrant. Drizzle in light soy sauce for base seasoning and dark soy sauce for color, stirring well to evenly coat the meat chunks.
**Key Point**: Do not overuse dark soy sauce to avoid an overly dark or black color.

3.  Pour in enough boiling water to cover the ingredients. Add salt, sugar, and cooking wine for seasoning. Cover the wok, reduce to medium-low heat, and braise for 40 minutes.
**Note**: Always use boiling water; cold water will cause the meat to tighten, affecting the texture.

4.  After uncovering, add the sliced tofu skin and stir well. Continue braising over medium heat for 8 minutes until the tofu skin absorbs the flavors.
**Key Point**: Tofu skin has inherent saltiness, so taste and adjust salt as needed later.

5.  Increase to high heat and reduce the sauce slightly. Once the sauce thickens a bit, remove from heat and serve.
**Note**: Do not reduce the sauce too much; leaving a little liquid results in a moister texture.

## Health Advice
This dish of braised pork belly with tofu skin is rich in high-quality protein and collagen but high in calories. It is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance nutrition. Suitable for manual laborers or as an occasional indulgence. Those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids should control portions.
