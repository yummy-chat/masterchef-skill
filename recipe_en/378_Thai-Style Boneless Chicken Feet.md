# Thai-Style Boneless Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 Chicken feet 4 jin (2 kg, deboned)  
🌶️ Wild pepper 160 g (sliced, use both the peppers and the pickling liquid)  
🍬 White sugar 80 g  
🧂 MSG 40 g  
🍶 Donggu Yipinxian soy sauce 80 g  
🌶️ Thai sweet chili sauce 400 g  
🍎 Apple cider vinegar 280 g  
🍾 Dahong Zhejiang vinegar 160 g (if unavailable, substitute with 80 g regular vinegar)  
💧 Wild pepper brine 240 g  
🥣 Spicy fresh sauce 160 g (can substitute with Sichuan-style spicy fresh sauce)  
💦 Cool boiled water 160 g  
🫒 Sesame oil 80 g  
🌶️ Chili oil 120 g  
🧄 Garlic cloves, sliced 160 g  
🌶️ Thai bird's eye chili, sliced 160 g  
🫑 Fresh Sichuan green pepper 160 g  
✨ Sichuan peppercorn a small pinch  
⭐ Star anise 3 pieces  
🍃 Bay leaves 3-4 pieces  
🌿 White angelica root 1 piece  
🧅 Scallion 2 stalks  
🫚 Ginger 2 slices  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or frying pan  
🪵 Wooden lid  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🧊 Refrigerator  
🥢 Slotted spoon or chopsticks  

## Instructions
1.  Place chicken feet in cold water in a pot. Add Sichuan peppercorn, star anise, bay leaves, white angelica root, scallion, and ginger.
   **Key**: After the water comes to a boil, reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and cook for 7 minutes. Turn off the heat and let steep, covered, for 7 minutes. This prevents the skin from breaking.
2.  Remove the chicken feet and immediately transfer to ice water. Rinse repeatedly until completely cooled. Then refrigerate for 30 minutes to enhance the chewy, bouncy texture.
3.  Prepare the sauce: In a large bowl, add white sugar, MSG, Donggu Yipinxian soy sauce, Thai sweet chili sauce, apple cider vinegar, Dahong Zhejiang vinegar, wild pepper brine, and sliced wild peppers, and spicy fresh sauce. Stir until the sugar and MSG are fully dissolved.
4.  Add cool boiled water, sesame oil, chili oil, sliced garlic, sliced Thai bird's eye chili, and fresh Sichuan green pepper. Stir to combine.
5.  Pour the chilled chicken feet into the sauce and mix thoroughly by hand.
6.  Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 12 hours to allow the flavors to fully penetrate.

## Health Advice
This Thai-style boneless chicken feet dish is rich in collagen and ideal for stimulating the appetite in summer. However, the sauce is high in sugar and sodium. It is recommended to balance the meal with a vegetable salad. People with high blood pressure should be mindful of their portion size.
