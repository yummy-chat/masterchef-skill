# Sour Soup Beef

Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients
🎃 Old pumpkin 100g (peeled and sliced, for making golden broth)
🥩 Beef slices 300g (thawed in advance)
🍄 Enoki mushrooms 100g (torn into small strands)
🥬 Baby cabbage 100g (sliced vertically into strips)
🧅 Onion 20g (diced)
🫚 Ginger 6g (minced)
🧄 Garlic 15g (mashed and minced)
🌶️ Pickled wild peppers 15g (roots removed, sliced into rings)
🌶️ Small red chili peppers 10g (sliced diagonally into rings)
🌶️ Green chili peppers 10g (sliced diagonally into rings)
🧅 Scallion 10g (white part only, cut into shreds)

## Seasonings
🫙 Pickled pepper brine 4 tablespoons + additional as needed (liquid from pickled wild pepper bag, added in two batches to adjust broth acidity)
🫙 Yellow lantern chili paste about 50g (2 heaping tablespoons, key to dish flavor; reduce if sensitive to high spiciness)
🧂 Salt 2-3g
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g
🍬 Sugar 2g (omit if pumpkin is sweet or if you don't prefer sweetness)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (normal amount for stir-frying base; small amount for final aromatic oil)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥒 Vegetable peeler
♨️ Steamer, lid
🔄 Blender/food processor
🍳 Wok
🥣 Large bowl, plates
🥄 Measuring spoons, ladle
🥢 Strainer, chopsticks

## Steps
1. Peel old pumpkin and slice into thick pieces. Arrange flat in steamer, steam over boiling water for 10 minutes until a chopstick can easily pierce through. Transfer steamed pumpkin to blender, add 50g water and blend into smooth puree. Pour pumpkin puree into a large bowl, add about 700g water and stir well to make golden broth. **Note**: Pumpkin must be fully steamed; finer puree yields smoother broth.

2. Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add thawed beef slices, quickly separate with chopsticks. Remove as soon as beef changes color, rinse with warm water to remove surface foam, drain and set aside. **Note**: Blanch briefly, remove immediately once color changes to avoid tough texture; must rinse with warm water.

3. Dice onion, mince ginger, mash and mince garlic, remove roots from pickled wild peppers and slice into rings. Place all four ingredients in one bowl, add 4 tablespoons pickled pepper brine and mix. **Note**: Pickled wild peppers and their brine are essential for flavor.

4. Tear enoki mushrooms into small strands, slice baby cabbage vertically into strips. Slice small red chili peppers and green chili peppers diagonally into rings, cut scallion white part into shreds. **Note**: Scallion shreds and chili rings are used for final aromatic oil infusion; do not use too much.

5. Add normal cooking oil amount to wok. When oil is slightly warm, add mixed ginger, garlic, onion, and pickled wild peppers. Stir-fry over high heat until fragrant. Reduce to lowest heat, add yellow lantern chili paste, stir-fry briefly over low heat, then turn to high heat and stir-fry until paste aroma develops. **Note**: Yellow lantern chili paste must be stir-fried in oil to release flavor.

6. Pour prepared pumpkin golden broth into wok, bring to a boil over high heat. Add salt, chicken bouillon, sugar, and additional pickled pepper brine to taste, stir well. **Note**: Adjust sugar according to pumpkin sweetness and personal preference.

7. Once broth boils, add baby cabbage and enoki mushrooms. Boil over high heat for about 2 minutes until vegetables are cooked. Remove and place at bottom of a large serving bowl. **Note**: —

8. Add blanched beef slices to broth. As soon as broth returns to a boil, immediately remove beef and arrange on top of vegetables. **Note**: Do not overcook beef in broth; just heat through before removing.

9. Pour the sour broth from wok along the edge of the bowl over the ingredients. Place scallion shreds and green-red chili rings on top of beef. Heat a small amount of oil in another pot until smoking, then quickly pour over scallion shreds and chili rings to release aroma. **Note**: Oil must be heated until smoking to fully release aroma.

## Health Advice
This sour soup beef is rich in high-quality protein and vitamin A, suitable for those needing an energy boost or appetite stimulation. However, sodium content is relatively high; pairing with light vegetables is recommended. Those sensitive to spiciness may reduce the amount of yellow lantern chili paste.
