# Salted Duck
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦆 Duck legs, 2
🧅 Scallion, 1
🫚 Ginger, 4-5 slices
🧂 Salt, half a bowl
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns, a handful
⭐ Star anise, about 2
🌳 Cinnamon stick, about 1 small piece
🍂 Bay leaves, about 3
🌼 Angelica dahurica, about 1 slice
🍶 Cooking wine, as needed
💧 Water, as needed

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥘 Large pot (stockpot)
🛍️ Ziplock bag
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon / soup ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. Place the salt and Sichuan peppercorns in a wok. Stir-fry over low heat until the salt turns slightly yellow and the peppercorns become fragrant. Remove and set aside.
2. Wash the duck legs. Thoroughly pat dry the surface with a kitchen paper towel or a dry cloth.
3. Evenly rub the Sichuan peppercorn salt all over both sides of the duck legs, massaging the thicker parts more. Place them in a ziplock bag, expel all the air, seal tightly, and refrigerate for 24 hours to marinate.
4. Add enough water to a large pot. Add the scallion segments, ginger slices, star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and angelica dahurica. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 5 minutes to fully release the aroma of the spices.
5. Scrape off the surface Sichuan peppercorns from the marinated duck legs. Place them into the boiling spice broth. Add cooking wine and use a spoon to skim off any foam. Boil vigorously for 3 minutes, then reduce to the lowest heat, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
**Key Point**: The duck legs must be added only after the water is boiling. Control the heat and timing throughout the process.
6. Turn off the heat. Remove the duck legs and place them on a cutting board to cool completely. Once fully cooled, slice into thick pieces and arrange on a plate for serving.

## Health Suggestions
This salted duck is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for people managing their weight. Due to the high salt content during marination, it is recommended that those with high blood pressure consume it in small amounts. Duck meat is cool in nature; pairing it with ginger slices can neutralize this coolness, making it especially good for summer consumption.
