# Salted Egg Yolk and Shrimp Tofu Stew
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-season meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧈 Silken Tofu 1 block (soft or silken tofu)
🥚 Salted Egg Yolks 4 pieces (preferably oily salted egg yolks)
🦐 Shell-on Shrimp appropriate amount (for making shrimp stock and providing shrimp meat)
🍄 Shimeji Mushrooms appropriate amount (can be substituted with other mushrooms)
🧄 Garlic a few cloves (minced)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (sliced, for garnish and aroma)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for blanching tofu and final seasoning)
🍬 Sugar a pinch (to enhance umami and balance flavors)
🌿 White Pepper Powder a pinch (to remove fishiness and enhance aroma)
🫒 Cooking Oil appropriate amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife
🪓 Cutting Board
🍳 Pot
🥄 Soup Spoon/Wooden Spatula
🥛 Measuring Cup
🕸️ Strainer
🦷 Toothpick

## Steps
1. **Prepare the Shrimp**: Peel the fresh shrimp, use a toothpick to remove the intestinal vein, set aside the shrimp heads, and cut the shrimp meat into small pieces.
2. **Make Shrimp Stock**: Inject a little oil into the pot, add the shrimp heads and stir-fry over low heat until the red shrimp oil is released, pour in boiling water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 10 minutes. Filter out the residue with a strainer, and set the shrimp stock aside.
   *Key point: Fry the shrimp heads first before adding water for a more vibrant and flavorful broth; if short on time, plain water can be used as a substitute.*
3. **Prepare the Tofu**: Cut the silken tofu into even small cubes, bring water to a boil in a pot, add a little salt, blanch the tofu for 3 minutes, then remove.
   *Key point: Blanching removes the beany flavor and makes the tofu firmer and less likely to break.*
4. **Prepare the Salted Egg Yolks**: Finely chop the salted egg yolks, or steam them for 5 minutes and then crush them with a spoon for easier disintegration later.
5. **Prepare the Aromatics**: Cut the shimeji mushrooms into sections, mince the garlic, and slice the scallions.
6. **Stir-fry the Salted Egg Yolks**: Heat oil in a pan, lower the heat, add the chopped salted egg yolks and minced garlic, and stir continuously until the salted egg yolks are completely melted, forming dense small bubbles with a sandy texture and a rich aroma.
   *Key point: This is the most critical step of the entire dish. Must be stir-fried slowly over low heat to fully release the fragrance and sandy texture of the salted egg yolks.*
7. **Combine and Cook**: Once the salted egg yolks have turned sandy, pour in the shrimp stock, add the blanched tofu, shrimp meat pieces, and shimeji mushrooms, and cook until the broth turns golden and the ingredients are cooked through.
8. **Season**: Adjust the flavor to taste, optionally adding a little salt, sugar, and white pepper powder, and stir to combine.
9. **Serve**: Garnish with sliced scallions, plate, and serve. The broth is rich and perfect for mixing with rice.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and calcium, suitable for growing children or recovering individuals needing nutritional supplementation. However, salted egg yolks are high in sodium; it is recommended that individuals with high blood pressure use little to no salt.
