# Homemade New-Style Pickled Fish

Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Bighead carp 1 (take the fillet and slice thinly; keep the head and bones for broth)  
🫚 Young ginger, as needed (shredded, for garnish)  
🌶️ Bird’s eye chili, as needed (sliced into rings, for garnish)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed  
🫚 Old ginger, as needed  
🫚 Young ginger, as needed  
🫘 Pickled cowpea, as needed  
🥕 Pickled radish, as needed  
🥬 Pickled mustard greens, as needed  
🫚 Pickled ginger, as needed (minced)  
🌶️ Pickled chili, as needed (pickled pepper, minced)  
🌿 Celery, a small amount (for garnish)  
🧂 Salt, a large amount (for the first washing) + as needed (for broth and second marination)  
⚪️ White pepper, as needed  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🍺 Beer, as needed (1 can for broth, a small amount for second marination)  
🍠 Sweet potato starch, as needed (for coating)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, as needed  
🍶 Vinegar, a small amount  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🐖 Lard, one ladle  
🌸 Dried Sichuan pepper, as needed  
🌶️ Dried chili, as needed  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef’s knife  
🥣 Large stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Iron wok (two)  
🥄 Strainer, soup ladle  
🚰 Sink  
🥛 Small saucepan  
🍽️ Bowls, plates  

## Preparation Steps
1. Take the fillet of the bighead carp and slice thinly; keep the head and bones.
**Note**: The fish slices should be as thin as possible for a tender texture.

2. Place the fish slices in a bowl, add a large amount of salt, and vigorously rub and wash with your hands until the slices become sticky and gelatinous.
**Note**: Use a generous amount of salt; this step removes the fishy smell and changes the texture, making the slices tender.

3. Rinse the rubbed fish slices under running water repeatedly, changing the water several times. Finally, let the slices float in the bowl under a slow stream of water, then squeeze out excess water.
**Note**: Thoroughly wash off excess salt to avoid overly salty results; floating indicates proper cleaning.

4. Prepare the side ingredients: shred young ginger, slice bird’s eye chili into rings, and chop or cut pickled mustard greens, pickled radish, pickled cowpea, pickled ginger, and pickled chili as needed.
5. Cook the base: Heat cooking oil and lard in a wok. Add dried Sichuan pepper and dried chili, stir-fry until fragrant. Then add pickled mustard greens, pickled radish, and pickled cowpea, stir-frying until aromatic. Finally, add minced pickled ginger and pickled chili, and continue stir-frying.
**Note**: Using a mix of oils enhances the broth’s richness; stir-fry until the oil turns bright red and the fragrance is fully released.

6. Add enough water to the wok, then pour in one can of beer. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and keep at a gentle simmer for the broth.
7. Second marination of the fish slices: Add a little salt, white pepper, a small amount of beer, and sweet potato starch to the drained fish slices. Gently mix until the slices are evenly coated with a thin layer.
**Note**: Squeeze out excess water from the slices beforehand; sweet potato starch is key for a smooth and tender texture, and the coating should not be too thick or too thin.

8. Heat oil in another wok. The oil temperature should not be too high. Add the coated fish slices one by one, quickly separate them, and remove immediately once they change color and set.
**Note**: Keep the oil-frying time short; this is a key innovation of this recipe for a more tender texture.

9. Quickly transfer the oil-fried fish slices to the simmering pickled broth. Sprinkle bird’s eye chili rings and fresh young ginger shreds on top. Add chicken bouillon and a small amount of vinegar. Gently push with the back of a ladle.
**Note**: Add fresh ingredients and vinegar just before serving to preserve their aroma; handle gently to avoid breaking the fish slices.

10. Plate the dish. Optionally, drizzle a small amount of hot oil over the fresh chili and young ginger on top. Garnish with celery segments.

## Health Advice  
This dish is rich in protein and probiotics from fermented vegetables, making it suitable for muscle building or when appetite is low. However, it is high in salt and oil, so portion control is advised. People with high blood pressure should consume with caution. Coating the fish slices in starch and frying in oil is the secret to tenderness, but the use of lard increases calorie content.
