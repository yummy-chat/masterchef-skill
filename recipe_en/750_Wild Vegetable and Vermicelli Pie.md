# Wild Vegetable and Vermicelli Pie
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January–April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g  
🧂 Salt 3g (for the dough)  
🍬 Sugar 3g (for the dough)  
💧 Boiling water 105g  
💧 Cold water 105g  
🐷 Lard (or vegetable oil) 9g  
🍄 Dried wild vegetables (地软) 25g  
🍜 Dried vermicelli 50g  
🥬 Chinese chives 200g  
🥚 Eggs 4  
🧂 Salt 5g (for the filling)  
🍬 Sugar 3g (for the filling)  
🥡 Soy sauce 20g  
🥡 Dark soy sauce 5g  
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan pepper 5g  
🌶️ Chili powder 5g  
🫒 Aromatic oil (or sesame oil) 20g  
🫒 Rapeseed oil 80g (for oil splashing and pan-frying)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🥄 Measuring spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧤 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Strainer  
🍴 Silicone spatula  
🥢 Wooden spatula

## Cooking Steps
1. **Knead and Rest the Dough**: Mix the all-purpose flour with 3g salt and 3g sugar. First, pour in 105g boiling water and stir into flocculent pieces. Then add 105g cold water and mix. Finally, add 9g lard and knead into a rough dough.  
   **Note**: The half-scalded dough method prevents the finished product from hardening after cooling; the fat makes the crust crispier.  
   Cover the dough with plastic wrap, rest for 10 minutes, then knead briefly. Rest another 10 minutes, repeat 1–2 times. Afterward, you can rest for 1 hour or prepare in advance.
2. **Prepare the Ingredients**: Soak the dried wild vegetables in clean water for 20 minutes, rinse repeatedly, and squeeze out excess water. Put the dried vermicelli into a pot of boiling water, cover, turn off the heat, and let steep for 20 minutes. Drain and cut into small sections. Beat the eggs, scramble them in a hot pan with cold oil, and break into small pieces.
3. **Make the Filling**: In a bowl, combine the wild vegetables, scrambled eggs, and vermicelli pieces. Add 5g salt, 3g sugar, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, ground Sichuan pepper, and chili powder. In a separate pan, heat 80g rapeseed oil to 180°C (356°F), pour it over the chili powder and ground Sichuan pepper, and mix with chopsticks.  
   Toss the Chinese chives with 20g aromatic oil in advance to seal in moisture. Just before wrapping, mix the chives into the wild vegetable filling.  
   **Key Point**: Add the chives at the very end to prevent them from releasing water.
4. **Divide and Roll Out the Dough**: Divide the rested dough into 10 equal pieces. Roll each into a ball, cover with plastic wrap, and rest for 10 minutes. Lightly oil the work surface. Take one piece and roll it into a large, thin circle.
5. **Wrap and Shape**: Place about 101g of filling in the center of each wrapper. Use one of the following methods to seal:
   - **Baozi method**: Pleat the edges like making baozi, then fold and flatten the dough knot at the closure.
   - **Two-fold method**: Pinch the left and right sides together, then fold and pinch the top and bottom ends closed.
   - **Fold-over method**: Fold the wrapper over the filling, then fold and pinch the left and right sides toward the center to seal.
6. **Pan-fry**: Preheat a flat-bottomed pan to 200°C (392°F). Add a little rapeseed oil. Place the pie seam-side down into the pan. Cook over low or medium-low heat.  
   Fry until the bottom is golden brown and set, then flip. Continue flipping back and forth until both sides are evenly golden brown.  
   **Key Point**: Maintain low heat throughout so the oil evenly coats the crust, making it crispy; frying seam-side down first helps the pie set.

## Health Tips
This wild vegetable and vermicelli pie is rich in dietary fiber and plant protein, making it suitable for nourishing the spleen and stomach in spring. The vermicelli and oil are relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to eat 1–2 pies per serving, paired with vegetables for a more balanced meal.
