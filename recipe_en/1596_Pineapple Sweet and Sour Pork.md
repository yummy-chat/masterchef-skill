# Pineapple Sweet and Sour Pork
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

---

🥩 **Ingredients**  
- Pork shoulder 100g  
- Green bell pepper 25g  
- 🍍 Canned pineapple slices 75g  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Salt 1/4 teaspoon  
- Ketchup 4 tablespoons  
- White vinegar 2 teaspoons  
- Minced garlic 1 tablespoon  
- Light soy sauce 1/2 teaspoon  
- Cornstarch 2 1/2 teaspoons + 6 tablespoons (for coating meat)  
- White sugar 2 tablespoons  
- Water 200 ml  
- Cooking oil 500 ml (for frying meat) + 1 teaspoon (for stir-frying sauce)  

🥄 **Utensils**  
- Kitchen paper  
- Bowl  
- Pot  
- Stove  

---

🍳 **Cooking Steps**  
1. Wash the pork shoulder, pat dry with kitchen paper, and cut into pieces slightly smaller than the final serving size.  
   (Wash the pork shoulder, pat dry with kitchen paper, and cut into pieces slightly smaller than the final serving size.)  
   **Key point**: Cutting the pork into small pieces makes it easier to cook through and ensures even texture.  

2. Marinate the pork shoulder with 1/2 teaspoon of salt for 20 minutes.  
   (Marinate the pork shoulder with half a teaspoon of salt for 20 minutes.)  

3. Chop the green bell pepper.  
   (Chop the green bell pepper.)  

4. Cut the pineapple slices into pieces.  
   (Cut the pineapple slices into pieces.)  

5. In a bowl, mix together ketchup, white vinegar, minced garlic, light soy sauce, 2½ teaspoons of cornstarch, white sugar, ¼ teaspoon of salt, and water to make the sauce. Set aside.  
   (In a bowl, mix together ketchup, white vinegar, minced garlic, light soy sauce, two and a half teaspoons of cornstarch, white sugar, a quarter teaspoon of salt, and water to make the sauce. Set aside.)  

6. Evenly coat the marinated pork pieces with 6 tablespoons of cornstarch.  
   (Evenly coat the marinated pork pieces with six tablespoons of cornstarch.)  
   **Key point**: Coat evenly with cornstarch to avoid sticking. For a crispier texture, you can add beaten egg to the meat before coating with cornstarch for extra crunch.  

7. Pour 500 ml of cooking oil into a pot and heat over medium heat.  
   (Pour 500 ml of cooking oil into a pot and heat over medium heat.)  

8. Place the coated pork pieces into the pot and fry over medium heat for 5 minutes. Remove.  
   (Place the coated pork pieces into the pot and fry over medium heat for 5 minutes. Remove.)  

9. Increase the oil temperature, add the pork pieces, and fry again over high heat for 1 minute. Remove and drain the oil.  
   (Increase the oil temperature, add the pork pieces, and fry again over high heat for 1 minute. Remove and drain the oil.)  
   **Key point**: The double-frying step ensures the meat is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Do not skip it.  

10. In a separate pot, add 1 teaspoon of oil and the prepared sauce. Heat over medium heat for 3 minutes.  
    (In a separate pot, add a teaspoon of oil and the prepared sauce. Cook over medium heat for 3 minutes.)  

11. Add the green bell pepper and pineapple, then increase the heat to high and cook for 2 minutes.  
    (Add the green bell pepper and pineapple, then increase the heat to high and cook for 2 minutes.)  

12. Place the fried pork pieces into the pot and quickly toss with the sauce until evenly coated. Serve immediately.  
    (Place the fried pork pieces into the pot and quickly toss with the sauce until evenly coated. That's it.)  
    **Key point**: Toss the sauce quickly to keep the meat crispy.  

## Health Advice
Pineapple sweet and sour pork is tangy and appetizing, but due to frying and high sugar content, it is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables to balance the meal. Pork is rich in protein and suitable for those needing iron supplementation. Diabetic patients should control sugar intake and avoid excessive consumption.
