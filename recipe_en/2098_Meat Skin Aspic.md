# Meat Skin Aspic
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork skin About 1 jin (with a little fat attached for easier oil removal)
🐖 Hind leg meat About 3 jin (choose leaner cuts, remove sinew and excess fat)
💧 Water About 2.5 liters

🧂 Cooking wine As needed (for blanching to remove odor)
🧅 Scallion sections 3-4 sections
🫚 Ginger slices About 5-6 slices
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece (do not use too much)
⭐ Star anise 3-4 pieces
🧂 Salt As needed
🥄 Light soy sauce As needed
🥄 Dark soy sauce A small amount (only if the color is not deep enough)

## Cookware and Equipment
♨️ Pressure cooker
🍳 Wok or pot (for blanching)
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board
🪥 Steel wool or scraper
🔥 Blowtorch
🥄 Measuring spoons
🪣 Large basin
🧽 Strainer or slotted spoon
🥣 Storage container (for setting)
🌡️ Thermometer (optional)

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Stage
1. **Prepare the pork skin**
   - Use a knife to thoroughly scrape off all the white fat layer from the inside of the pork skin.
- Use a blowtorch to evenly char the surface of the pork skin until it turns black, then soak it in warm water and scrub off the charred layer with steel wool or a knife.
- Place the pork skin in cold water, add cooking wine, and turn off the heat immediately when small bubbles begin to appear at the bottom of the pot. Cover and let it soak using residual heat.
- Remove the pork skin while hot, scrape off any remaining fat, and cut into thin strips.
- Add edible lye or baking soda to the pork skin strips, pour in boiling water, and vigorously rub and wash for a few minutes. Then rinse repeatedly with clean water until the water runs clear. The prepared pork skin strips can be portioned and frozen for storage.
2. **Prepare the pork**
   - Remove sinew and obvious fat from the hind leg meat, then cut into large chunks.
- Once the water is boiling, add the meat chunks and cooking wine. Boil over high heat, continuously skimming off any foam, until no new foam appears and the water surface is clear.
- Remove the meat chunks, rinse off any remaining foam with warm water, and set aside.
### Cooking Stage
1. **Braise the pork skin**
   - Place the pork skin strips, scallion sections, ginger slices, star anise, and cinnamon stick into the pressure cooker. Add enough water.
- Cover the pot, bring to pressure, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 30 minutes.
- Turn off the heat. After the pressure has naturally released, open the lid and remove and discard the softened scallion, ginger, and spices.
2. **Add the pork and season**
   - Add the blanched pork chunks to the pressure cooker, along with salt and light soy sauce. Add a small amount of dark soy sauce if the color needs deepening.
- Cover the pot again, bring to pressure, then reduce to medium heat and cook for another 30 minutes.
- After the pressure has naturally released, open the lid. The pork skin should be very tender and the broth thick.
3. **Set and remedy**
   - Pour the mixture of aspic and meat from the pot into a container.
- **Key point**: Do not cover with plastic wrap. Let it cool naturally at room temperature until it is no longer hot to the touch, then refrigerate overnight. Covering directly with wrap will cause condensation to drip.
- If the aspic does not set after refrigeration, pour it back into a non-iron pot, heat over low heat until boiling, and evaporate excess water. Then pour it back into a container and refrigerate again. There is no need to worry about fat during reheating, as it has been thoroughly removed in the earlier steps.

## Health Advice
This meat skin aspic is rich in collagen, making it suitable for people with dry skin or joint discomfort. However, it is high in calories and cholesterol, so those with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Be sure to thoroughly remove all fat during preparation to avoid excessive fat intake.
