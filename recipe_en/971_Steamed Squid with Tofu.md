# Steamed Squid with Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐙 Fresh squid, about 3 small ones
🧈 Soft tofu, appropriate amount (inner-bean curd or soft Southern tofu)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount
🌶️ Red bell pepper, appropriate amount
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount
🥢 Steamed fish soy sauce or light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🫒 Vegetable oil, about 2 tablespoons (for the final hot oil pour)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting board
🔪 Chef's knife
🍳 Steamer
🍽️ Plate
🥄 Soup spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the fresh squid thoroughly and slice the squid bodies into even rings.
   **Key point**: Ensure the squid is fresh and cut the rings into uniform sizes.

2. Slice the tofu into thick pieces and arrange them neatly on the bottom of the steaming plate.

3. Cut the scallion into sections and slice the ginger. Separate some scallion whites, ginger, and red bell pepper, then julienne them for later use.

4. Sprinkle a layer of salt over the tofu on the plate, then lay the squid rings flat over the tofu. Finally, top with the scallion sections and ginger slices.
   **Key point**: Sprinkle the salt directly onto the tofu.

5. Once the steamer water is boiling, place the assembled plate inside, cover with the lid, and steam over high heat for 5 minutes.
   **Key point**: Timing is crucial to prevent the squid from becoming tough; the squid will turn white and curl slightly when done.

6. After steaming, remove the plate. Discard the scallion sections and ginger slices on top, and drizzle with an appropriate amount of steamed fish soy sauce or light soy sauce.

7. Pile the julienned scallion white, ginger, and red bell pepper in the center of the dish. Heat the vegetable oil to about 60% hot (approx. 180°C), then quickly pour it over the julienned toppings to release their aroma.
   **Key point**: The oil must be hot enough to fully release the fragrance.

## Health Advice
This dish is low in fat and high in protein. The combination of tofu and squid is light and delicious, suitable for those controlling fat intake or supplementing calcium. Steaming for 5 minutes ensures the squid remains tender and smooth.
