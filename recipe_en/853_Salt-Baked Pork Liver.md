# Salt-Baked Pork Liver
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫁 Pork liver 2500g (fresh, whole, remove fascia and cut into large pieces)
🌿 Star anise, as needed
🌿 Three nai (dried sand ginger), as needed
🌿 Green Sichuan pepper, as needed
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed
🌿 Cinnamon stick, about 6g
🌿 Fennel seeds, as needed
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (only for final garnish)
🧂 Edible salt 4000g (for making the salt-baking layer)
🍶 White vinegar, as needed (add to clean water for washing the pork liver)

## Cookware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Wooden cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Metal spoon
🍲 Wok lid
🧺 Bamboo winnowing basket
🍽️ White ceramic plate
🧻 Clean kitchen towel

## Preparation Steps
1. Evenly cut the pork liver into several large pieces, and use a knife to remove the white fascia in the middle.
   - Removing the fascia improves texture.
2. Place the pork liver in a bowl, add clean water and an appropriate amount of white vinegar, and scrub thoroughly.
   - White vinegar effectively suppresses the gamey smell.
3. Remove the pork liver and thoroughly dry the surface with a clean kitchen towel.
   - Must be dried before going into the pan, otherwise it will affect the aroma of the finished dish.
4. Pour 4000g of edible salt into a wok, heat and stir-fry until it reaches about 60°C.
5. Add star anise, three nai, green Sichuan pepper, dried chili peppers, cinnamon stick, and fennel seeds to the salt. Continue stir-frying until the salt temperature rises to about 120°C and the aroma of the spices is released.
6. Scoop out a little more than half of the hot salt and set aside. Leave a base layer of salt spread flat on the bottom of the wok, and place the dried pork liver flat on this salt base.
   - The salt base prevents the bottom of the pork liver from burning.
7. Evenly pour the reserved hot salt over the pork liver, ensuring the liver is completely and tightly covered by the salt.
8. Cover with the wok lid, reduce to very low heat, and salt-bake for 55 minutes.
9. Once the time is up, remove the lid, use chopsticks to push aside the salt block, and take out the pork liver.
10. Use a clean kitchen towel to carefully wipe off all salt grains and spices from the surface of the pork liver.
    - Must be wiped clean, otherwise it will affect the taste.
11. Slice the pork liver into thin slices about 2mm thick, arrange evenly on a plate, and garnish with cilantro.

## Health Advice
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, beneficial for blood nourishment and eye health, but it is high in cholesterol. People with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation. Salt-baking removes the gamey smell and enhances flavor, but be mindful of salt intake. Wiping off the surface salt grains before eating is healthier.
