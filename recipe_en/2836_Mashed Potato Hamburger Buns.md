# Mashed Potato Hamburger Buns
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Russet potatoes - as needed (to make about 105g of mashed potatoes)  
🌾 Bread flour 160g  
🌾 All-purpose flour 310g  
🥛 Milk 230g  
🧈 Unsalted butter 30g  
🥚 Egg 1 (for the dough; reserve a little for brushing the surface)  
🧂 Salt 5g  
🍬 Sugar 15g  
🍄 Dry yeast 5g  
🌿 Sesame seeds - as needed (for decorating the surface)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Peeler, chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Cooking pot, strainer, glass bowl  
🌀 Hand blender  
🍞 Stand mixer  
📏 Dough scraper  
⚖️ Digital scale  
🖌️ Silicone brush  
📦 Baking sheet  
🔥 Oven  

## Instructions  
1. Make the mashed potatoes: Peel the potatoes and cut them into evenly sized small pieces. Place them in a cooking pot, add water to cover, and cook until easily pierced with a chopstick. Drain thoroughly, then use a hand blender to blend into a smooth, lump-free paste.  
   **Key point**: The water content of the mashed potatoes directly affects the dough's consistency; be sure to drain thoroughly before blending.  
2. Mix the dough: Place the bread flour, all-purpose flour, 105g mashed potatoes, milk, egg, salt, sugar, and dry yeast into the stand mixer bowl. Start the machine and knead for about 7 minutes, until the dough is smooth and can form a thick membrane.  
3. Add the butter: Add the softened unsalted butter and continue kneading until fully incorporated and the dough can form a thin, elastic membrane.  
4. First fermentation: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a container. Cover with plastic wrap and let it ferment in a warm place until doubled in size.  
   **How to check**: Dust your finger with flour and poke a hole in the dough; the hole should not immediately spring back or collapse.  
5. Divide and shape: Remove the dough and gently press to release gas. Use a dough scraper to divide it into 8 equal pieces. Dust your work surface and hands with a little flour to prevent sticking. Roll each piece into a ball and place them spaced apart on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.  
6. Second fermentation: Let the dough rise in a warm, humid place for about 30-40 minutes, until it expands noticeably and springs back gently when pressed.  
7. Preheat the oven: Toward the end of the second fermentation, preheat the oven to 180°C.  
8. Brush with egg wash and decorate: Brush the surface of the risen dough with beaten whole egg and sprinkle with an appropriate amount of sesame seeds.  
9. Bake: Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack and bake at 180°C for about 25 minutes, until the surface is a glossy golden brown. Remove from the oven and transfer to a wire rack to cool.  
   **Key point**: Oven temperatures may vary; keep an eye on the color during the last few minutes.  
10. Finished product: The buns have a thin, soft crust and a moist, tender interior with a subtle potato aroma. They can be eaten as is or sliced to make hamburgers.  

## Health Advice  
Replacing part of the flour with mashed potatoes makes the hamburger buns more moist and soft while increasing dietary fiber and potassium content. This is suitable for those needing to control blood sugar or seeking a feeling of fullness. Be sure to drain the mashed potatoes well; otherwise, the dough will be too sticky.
