## Cooking Steps
1. Remove the internal organs and gills from the carp, clean thoroughly, especially scrape the inner belly walls clean – this is key to removing the fishy smell.
2. Make diagonal slashes ("one-flower cuts") on the thicker parts of the fish's back to allow flavors to penetrate.
3. Rub the fish inside and out evenly with scallion sections, ginger slices, and cooking wine. Marinate for a while to initially remove the fishy smell.
4. Prepare the aromatics: Crush and mince the ginger, cut the garlic into slightly larger pieces, cut some scallion into sections, and chop the remaining scallion together with spring onions. Prepare the Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, and cassia bark.
5. Heat the wok, add oil to coat the surface, then pour out the oil. Add enough fresh cold oil. When the oil is hot, sprinkle in a little salt. Gently place the fish into the wok and fry over medium heat.
**Key Point**: "Hot wok, cold oil", sprinkle salt, and minimize turning during frying to ensure the fish skin doesn't break. Once one side is golden brown and set, carefully flip the fish. Fry the other side until golden brown. Remove the fish and set aside.
6. Add a piece of lard to the wok and melt it. Sauté the scallion sections, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add the broad bean paste and stir-fry until the oil turns red. Then add the Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, and cassia bark, and stir-fry until fragrant.
**Key Point**: Lard enhances aroma; the broad bean paste must be stir-fried sufficiently to release the red oil.
7. Pour enough clean water into the wok. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, salt, chicken bouillon, white sugar, and ground white pepper. Reduce the heat to medium-low and adjust the seasoning.
8. Gently place the fried fish into the sauce. Since the sauce may not completely cover the fish, constantly use a ladle to scoop the sauce and pour it over the fish. Braise over medium-low heat.
**Key Point**: Basting the fish ensures the top part is also thoroughly flavored.
9. Cover the wok with a lid and braise over medium-low heat for about 6 minutes, until the fish is cooked through and has absorbed the flavors.
10. Carefully lift the fish out and place it onto a serving plate, keeping the fish intact.
11. Turn the heat to high to reduce the sauce in the wok. Use a slotted spoon to remove the solid seasonings (the dregs) for a cleaner sauce.
12. Once the sauce is thick and glossy, drizzle in a little bright oil and sprinkle in the chopped scallions. Stir to combine.
13. Pour the reduced sauce evenly over the fish on the plate. Serve.

## Health Tips
Braised carp is rich in high-quality protein and unsaturated fatty acids, making it suitable for people who need nutritional supplementation. It is recommended to serve with vegetables to balance the high salt and oil content. Those with high blood pressure should be mindful of the amount of broad bean paste used.
