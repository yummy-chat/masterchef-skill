# Sichuan Home-Style Pork Elbow

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍖 1 pork elbow (skin burned and cleaned)
🫚 1 large piece of ginger (divided in two: large piece for boiling elbow, minced for sauce)
🧅 3-4 scallions (divided in two: whole for boiling elbow, chopped for garnish)
⭐ 2-3 star anise
🌿 1 small piece of cinnamon
🍃 2-3 bay leaves
🌶️ 1 handful dried chili peppers
🌿 1 small handful Sichuan peppercorns
🍾 Appropriate amount of cooking wine
🍬 Appropriate amount of rock sugar (for caramelizing)
🧂 Appropriate amount of salt
🧂 Appropriate amount of MSG
🧂 Appropriate amount of chicken bouillon
🧂 Appropriate amount of white pepper
🥢 Appropriate amount of soy sauce (light/dark)
🫘 Appropriate amount of Pixian broad bean paste
🧄 Garlic (optional, minced)
🍬 A pinch of white sugar
🍶 Appropriate amount of fragrant vinegar
🥄 Appropriate amount of water starch
💧 Appropriate amount of water

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok (also used as stew pot)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon, wok spatula
🥣 Large bowl (for holding fried elbow)
🍲 Lid

## Preparation Steps
1. Scrape and clean the pork elbow. Place it in a pot of cold water, add the ginger piece, whole scallions, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, then skim off the foam.
**Note**: Must thoroughly remove the blackened skin, otherwise there will be a hair-like odor.

2. Reduce heat to low, cover with a lid, and simmer for about 1 hour until the elbow is initially cooked through.
3. In a separate pan, add a little oil and rock sugar. Stir-fry over low heat slowly. When the sugar melts to an oil-like color and bubbles, forming dense fish-eye bubbles, immediately add boiling water. Cook for 1-2 minutes to create caramel color.
**Key**: Must use low heat. Fish-eye bubbles are the precise moment to add boiling water.

4. Remove the boiled elbow. While still hot, evenly coat the skin with the caramel color.
5. Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok to 160°C (320°F). Add the elbow, immediately cover with a lid to prevent splattering, and fry until the skin turns a date-red color. Remove.
6. Return the fried elbow to the broth used for boiling. Add water, star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, salt, MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, caramel color, and soy sauce.
7. Cover with a lid and simmer over low heat for 2-3 hours until the elbow is tender enough to be easily pierced with chopsticks.
8. In a separate pan, heat oil. Add Pixian broad bean paste and stir-fry over low heat until red oil and fragrance release. Then add minced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant.
9. Add an appropriate amount of water. Season with MSG, chicken bouillon, white pepper, white sugar, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then thicken with water starch until it becomes dense and forms large bubbles.
10. Before turning off the heat, add a generous amount of chopped scallions and fragrant vinegar. Bring back to a boil until bubbling.
11. Plate the stewed elbow. While hot, pour the prepared ginger sauce over it.

## Health Advice
Pork elbow is rich in collagen, making it suitable for those needing skin elasticity or post-surgery recovery. However, due to its high fat and calorie content, those with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or high blood sugar should consume it in small amounts. It is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables to cut the richness. Reduce salt and sugar when stewing.
