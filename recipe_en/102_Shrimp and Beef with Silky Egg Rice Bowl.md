# Shrimp and Beef with Silky Egg Rice Bowl
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦐 Shrimp, about 8-10 (peeled and deveined)
🥩 Beef, about one small bowl (sliced and pre-marinated)
🥚 Eggs, 2 (beaten)
🥦 Broccoli, about 4-5 small florets (blanched)
🍘 Seaweed, to taste (for garnish)
🧅 Scallions, to taste (chopped)
🍚 Rice, one bowl
🧂 Salt, to taste
🥛 Milk, to taste (about 1-2 tablespoons)
🫒 Cooking oil, to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan
🥄 Wooden spatula
🥣 Small saucepan
🔗 Food tongs
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Bowl/plate

## Instructions
1. Crack the eggs into a small saucepan and beat. Add milk and salt, then stir to combine to create the egg mixture; set aside.
2. Heat a frying pan over high heat, add an appropriate amount of oil. Pan-fry the shrimp until both sides turn red and they curl, then remove and set aside.
**Key point**: Pan-fried shrimp are more aromatic than boiled ones.
3. Add oil to the pan again and heat until a faint wisp of smoke rises from the oil surface. Add the marinated beef slices and quickly stir-fry.
**Heat**: Stir-fry over high heat.
   **Key point**: The pan must be fully preheated; this is crucial for tender beef.
   **State check**: Remove the beef as soon as the surface changes color.
4. Return the cooked beef and pan-fried shrimp to the pan together. Sprinkle with chopped scallions and stir-fry evenly.
5. Reduce the heat to medium-low. Evenly pour the prepared egg mixture into the pan, covering the surface of the beef and shrimp.
6. When the bottom of the egg mixture begins to set slightly, use a wooden spatula to gently push the egg mixture from the edges toward the center, allowing the uncooked egg liquid to flow to the bottom of the pan.
**Key point**: Gently push with a wooden spatula to avoid breaking the egg mixture, maintaining a silky and tender texture.
   **State check**: Turn off the heat when most of the egg is set but still moist. Use residual heat to finish cooking the egg.
7. Spoon the silky egg, shrimp, and beef over the rice in a bowl. Sprinkle with crumbled seaweed and serve with blanched broccoli florets.

## Health Advice
This dish is high in protein and low in carbohydrates, suitable for those looking to build muscle or lose fat. Although the dual protein from beef and shrimp is nutritious, it may increase digestive burden; it is recommended to pair with plenty of vegetables for balance. Be careful not to overcook the egg mixture to avoid making the silky egg tough.
