# Sour and Spicy Chicken Feet
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 Chicken feet, about 2 pounds (trimmed of toenails, cut in half lengthwise)
🌶️ Bird's eye chili peppers, as needed
🧄 Garlic, a large bowl of cloves
🫚 Ginger, 1 small piece
🌿 Cilantro, as needed (for garnish)
🧂 Salt, 2 tablespoons
🍬 White sugar, 1 tablespoon
🥣 Light soy sauce, 3 tablespoons
🍶 Rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons
🥄 Dark soy sauce, a little
🍋 Lemon, half (juiced)
🥃 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), a small half tablespoon (for the sauce)
🫒 Sesame oil or chili oil, as needed
🥃 Cooking wine or Baijiu, as needed (for blanching)
🌿 Bay leaves, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, a little (for making spiced water; use only the water)
🌶️ Chili powder, a handful (optional, adjust according to personal spice preference)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Cooking pot
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧂 Food processor/meat grinder
🥣 Bowls, basin
🧤 Disposable gloves
🥡 Plastic wrap

## Preparation Steps
1.  Clean the chicken feet thoroughly, trim off the toenails, and cut them in half lengthwise using a knife.
    **Note**: Choose chicken feet without blemishes and with plump meat; cutting them in half allows for better absorption of flavors.
2.  Put the ginger, garlic cloves, and bird's eye chili peppers together into a food processor and grind into a fine paste.
    **Note**: Use a generous amount of garlic; the ratio of ginger, garlic, and chili can be adjusted according to personal taste.
3.  Prepare the sauce: In a large bowl, combine the ground ginger, garlic, and chili paste. Add salt, white sugar, light soy sauce, rice vinegar, dark soy sauce, lemon juice, and baijiu. Stir until well blended. Finally, drizzle in the sesame oil or chili oil and mix again.
    **Note**: Taste the sauce first and adjust the saltiness, sourness, and spiciness according to your preference.
4.  Make the spiced water: Add a small amount of water to a pot. Add bay leaves, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove the spices, strain the water, and allow it to cool completely before use.
    **Note**: The spiced water is used to remove gamey odors and enhance flavor. It must be completely cooled before using.
5.  Blanch the chicken feet: Add cold water to a pot, along with ginger slices and cooking wine. Add the chicken feet. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium heat and cook for 8-10 minutes, until the chicken feet are cooked through.
    **Note**: Be sure to start with cold water. Do not overcook, to maintain the chewy, Q-like texture of the chicken feet.
6.  Cool and rinse: Remove the cooked chicken feet with a slotted spoon and immediately immerse them in cold water or ice water. Then, wash them repeatedly under running water for 3-5 times, scrubbing with your hands, until the water runs clear and all fat and foam are removed.
    **Note**: Cooling in cold water is crucial for the crispy and springy texture of the chicken feet. Thorough washing ensures the final dish is refreshing, not greasy, and has no unpleasant smell.
7.  Combine: Place the drained chicken feet in a large basin. First, pour in the cooled spiced water and mix well. Then, pour in the prepared sauce. Wearing disposable gloves, mix everything thoroughly by hand. You can add chili powder at this stage and mix again if desired.
    **Note**: The total amount of sauce and spiced water should roughly cover the chicken feet.
8.  Marinate in the refrigerator: Seal the basin tightly with plastic wrap and place it in the refrigerator to marinate overnight.
    **Note**: You may take it out and stir it 1-2 times during the marinating period for more even flavor distribution.
9.  Serve: Take out the marinated chicken feet, sprinkle with chopped cilantro, mix well, and plate.
    **Note**: Cilantro adds flavor and color but is optional; you can omit it according to preference.

## Health Advice
Sour and spicy chicken feet are rich in collagen but also high in fat and sodium. They are suitable for occasional consumption and should not be eaten in excess. People with high blood lipids or high blood pressure are advised to reduce the amount of salt and chili oil and pair this dish with light vegetables to balance nutrition.
