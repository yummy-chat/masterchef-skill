# Butter Rice Cake
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 Whole egg liquid 50g (room temperature recommended)
🥛 Milk 200g (room temperature recommended)
🧈 Butter 30g (melted)
🧈 Softened butter as needed (for greasing the mold)
🍬 Fine sugar 50g
🧂 Salt 1.5g
🍼 Milk powder 20g
🍚 Three Elephants glutinous rice flour 160g
🍠 Lipu taro 500g (steamed, for taro filling)
🍬 Fine sugar for taro filling 75g
🧈 Butter for taro filling 25g
🍈 Durian filling as needed (sautéed durian pastry filling recommended)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel bowls 2-3
🥄 Whisk
⚖️ Digital scale
🫗 Strainer/flour sieve
🍮 12-cavity chestnut/butter rice cake mold
🍴 Silicone spatula
🔥 Oven
🫖 Bain-marie equipment (small pot + hot water)
🌡️ Infrared thermometer (optional)
🧽 Cooling rack
🥣 Food processor
🍲 Steamer

## Instructions
### Preparation Stage
1. Peel and cut the Lipu taro into chunks, steam in a steamer for 30 minutes until fully cooked.
2. Place the steamed taro into a food processor, add 75g fine sugar and 25g butter, and blend until smooth and silky.
   **Note**: Adding milk is not recommended, as it may dilute the flavor and make the filling too thin; if too dry, add a small amount as needed.

### Making the Batter
1. In a bowl, add room temperature milk, whole egg liquid, melted butter, fine sugar, and salt in order. Use a whisk to stir until the sugar and salt are completely dissolved and well combined.
   **Key point**: If using refrigerated milk and eggs, they may solidify again after adding melted butter. Use a bain-marie to warm the batter to 28–30°C until the butter re-melts.
2. Sift in the milk powder and glutinous rice flour, stir until the batter is smooth and lump-free.
3. Strain the batter through a sieve at least twice to obtain a smooth, pourable batter.
   **Note**: Straining is key to a fine texture and cannot be omitted.

### Molding and Baking
1. Evenly coat each cavity of the mold with a layer of softened butter.
2. First pour the batter into the mold until 80–90% full, then evenly distribute any remaining batter into each cavity.
   **Note**: Generously butter the mold to ensure easy unmolding.
3. Preheat the oven. Place the baking pan on the middle-lower rack and bake at an actual temperature of 180°C for about 35 minutes.
   **Check for doneness**: The surface should be an even golden or deep brown; adjust time according to your oven.

### Removal from Oven and Unmolding
1. Immediately after removing from the oven, gently tap the baking pan on the counter a few times to release steam.
2. Quickly invert the baking pan onto a cooling rack; the rice cakes should release automatically.
   **Key point**: Unmold immediately while hot, otherwise they will stick after cooling.

### Storage and Reheating
- Once fully cooled, the outer shell may lose its crispness. Reheat in the oven for a few minutes to restore the crispy texture.
- Refrigerating or freezing will harden the rice cakes; reheating restores their soft and chewy texture.

## Health Advice
This rice cake is relatively high in calories, with the taro and butter providing rich energy. Pair it with unsweetened tea or black coffee to cut the richness, and control portion sizes to avoid excessive calorie intake.
