# Northeast Chinese Jar Pork
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork belly 4 jin (with thick skin, can mix with front shoulder meat or pork collar, lean meat is not recommended)
🌿 Spice bag (14 types): Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, white nutmeg, long pepper, magnolia flower, cloves, galangal, bay leaves, dried tangerine peel, gardenia, cumin, black cardamom
🧄 Scallions,适量 (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger,适量 (sliced)
🫙 Rock sugar, about 2 liang (used in two batches: 1 liang for caramelizing and 1 liang for seasoning)
🫙 Braising sauce, about 25 grams
🧂 Salt, about 30 grams
🫙 Seafood soy sauce, about 50 grams
🧂 Fermented bean curd liquid (red variety), about 40 grams
🍶 Baijiu, about 25-30 grams
🫙 Red yeast rice powder, a little more than half a spoon
🍬 White sugar, about 20 grams

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔥 Gas stove
🍳 Large iron wok
🏺 White ceramic jar
🥄 Spatula / large ladle
🥣 Strainer / slotted spoon
🧻 White gauze (for making the spice bag)
🪣 Stainless steel basin / glass bowl
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the spice bag**: Wrap the 14 dried spices in gauze and tie tightly to form a spice bag. Cut scallions into sections and slice the ginger.
2. **Treat the pork skin**: Place the whole piece of skin-on pork belly skin-side down over an open flame and burn until the skin is charred yellow. Wash in clean water using a steel wool scrubber until the surface is clean.
**Note**: Burning the skin removes residual hairs and imparts a smoky aroma. Ensure all charred parts are thoroughly cleaned off.
3. **Cut into pieces**: Cut the cleaned pork belly into uniform cubes of 3–4 centimeters.
4. **Blanch**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the pork pieces, and boil over high heat for 2–3 minutes. Skim off any foam, then remove and drain.
**Key point**: There is no significant difference between adding meat to boiling water versus cold water. The blanching time must be sufficient to remove blood.
5. **Caramelize sugar**: Add a small amount of oil to the wok, then add about 1 liang of rock sugar. Stir-fry over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar completely melts, forms dense small bubbles, and turns a jujube red color.
**Note**: The caramelized sugar is the basis for the meat’s glossy red appearance. Precise heat control is needed to avoid burning.
6. **Color the meat**: Immediately after the caramel is ready, add the blanched pork pieces and stir-fry quickly to coat each piece evenly.
7. **Season and braise**: Add enough hot water to the wok. Add the braising sauce, salt, seafood soy sauce, fermented bean curd liquid, remaining rock sugar, baijiu, red yeast rice powder, white sugar, the spice bag, and scallions and ginger. Bring to a boil over high heat.
**Key point**: The fermented bean curd liquid is an important source of flavor for this dish. Ensure it is fully incorporated into the broth.
8. **Transfer to the jar**: Transfer all contents from the wok into the ceramic jar. Cover with the lid and simmer over low heat for 40 minutes.
**Note**: Using a jar is the characteristic of "jar pork." It makes the meat more tender and flavorful. Do not open the lid frequently during simmering.
9. **Steep for flavor**: After simmering for 40 minutes, turn off the heat. Do not open the lid. Let the jar cool naturally and rest for 8–10 hours.
**Key point**: The long steeping time is crucial for the meat to fully absorb flavor and achieve a "melt-in-your-mouth" texture. This step cannot be omitted. At this point, the meat is about 90% cooked, tender but not falling apart, with a slight elasticity.
10. **Reheat and serve**: Before serving, reheat the jar until it comes to a boil.
**Note**: Reheating melts any solidified fat, enhancing the flavor.

## Health Advice
This dish is made with pork belly and 14 kinds of spices. It is nutritious but high in calories, suitable for occasional indulgence or for those with high physical exertion. Control portion sizes when eating to avoid excessive intake of saturated fat. It is recommended to pair with vegetables for a balanced meal.
