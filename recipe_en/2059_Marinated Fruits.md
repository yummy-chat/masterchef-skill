# Marinated Fruits
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Fruit knife/Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Milk pot or stock pot  
🔥 Induction cooker/Electric stove  
🥣 Large bowl (for holding fruit)  
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and scooping)  
🧊 Plastic wrap (for sealing when refrigerating)  
🥢 Toothpicks (for serving)  

## Steps  
1. Cut the fruit into uniformly sized pieces and place them in a large bowl, mixing evenly.  
   **Note**: You can freely combine fruits available on hand, no need to adhere strictly to the listed types.  
2. Make the plum juice: Add preserved plums, water, and rock sugar to a pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then turn off the heat.  
3. Remove the cooked plum juice and let it cool naturally to room temperature.  
   **Key point**: **The plum juice must be completely cooled down**, otherwise pouring it over the fruit will soften the flesh and affect the texture.  
4. Pour the cooled plum juice into the bowl of fruit and gently stir to ensure the fruit is mostly submerged in the liquid.  
5. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate to marinate.  
   **Time suggestion**: Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes; **overnight refrigeration yields better flavor**, allowing the fruit to fully absorb the taste and the plum juice to meld with the composite fruit aroma.  
6. When serving, use toothpicks or a small spoon to take the fruit. The plum juice left from marinating the fruit can also be drunk directly, providing a refreshing thirst-quenching experience.  

## Health Suggestions  
This marinated fruit dish is rich in vitamin C and dietary fiber, making it suitable for cooling off in summer or as a low-calorie snack. However, diabetic patients should be mindful of the rock sugar dosage. It is recommended to use seasonal fruits for the best results when marinating overnight, but note that the marinating time should not be too long to avoid the fruit becoming overly soft and losing its texture.
