# Salted Egg Yolk Pumpkin

Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Autumn (September–October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 Old pumpkin 300g (peeled and seeded, cut into finger-thick strips)
🥚 Salted duck egg yolks 50g (about 4–5 cooked yolks, mashed into a fine paste, hard centers removed)
🧂 Salt 3g (for marinating the pumpkin)
🌽 Corn starch (as needed, for coating the pumpkin strips)
🍬 Sugar (to taste, for enhancing flavor, optional)
🫒 Cooking oil (as needed, for deep frying, enough to submerge ingredients)
🫒 Cooking oil (a small amount, for pan-frying the egg yolks)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🕳 Strainer or colander
🍳 Deep frying pot or wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🌡 Kitchen thermometer
🥚 Whisk or egg beater
🥄 Spatula
🍽 Plate

## Instructions
1. Peel and seed the old pumpkin, cut into finger-thick strips, place in a bowl, add 3g salt, mix well, and marinate for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: Marinating helps the pumpkin release excess moisture, making it easier to coat with starch. This amount of salt will not make the pumpkin too salty.

2. Drain the marinated pumpkin strips, place them in a container, sprinkle with enough corn starch, and toss thoroughly by hand to ensure each strip is evenly coated with a thick layer of starch. After coating, pick out the pumpkin strips individually and discard any excess starch left at the bottom of the container.
   **Key point**: The coating must be thick, even, and firm—this is crucial for a crispy outer layer.

3. Place the salted duck egg yolks in a bowl, mash them into a fine paste with a spoon, and remove any hard centers. Set aside.
   **Note**: The finer the yolks, the easier they will foam and form a sandy texture when stir-fried.

4. Pour enough oil into the pot and heat to 180°C. Reduce to low heat and add the coated pumpkin strips in batches.
   **Caution**: Do not stir immediately after adding; wait until the coating sets, then gently loosen with a slotted spoon to prevent the starch from falling off. Fry for about 5 minutes, until the surface is golden and firm. You can break one open to check if it is cooked through.

5. Reheat the oil to 180°C, add the first-fried pumpkin strips for a second fry of about 30 seconds. When the strips make a crisp sound as they knock together, they are crispy. Quickly remove and drain the oil.
   **Key point**: The second fry ensures a crispy texture—do not fry for too long.

6. In a separate clean pot over low heat, add the mashed salted egg yolks and quickly whisk and stir with a whisk.
   **Caution**: Stir until the yolks are completely melted and produce fine bubbles, then immediately turn off the heat to avoid burning.

7. Immediately after turning off the heat, add the second-fried pumpkin strips and toss with a spatula to evenly coat each strip with the egg yolk paste. At this point, add a little sugar to taste to enhance flavor. If not salty enough, add a pinch of salt as needed.
   **Key point**: Use residual heat to mix; continued heating may burn the mixture.

## Health Advice
Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene, which pairs well with egg yolks for better absorption, making it suitable for eye health in autumn and winter. However, frying and salted egg yolks are high in calories; consider using an air fryer as a substitute for deep frying and reduce the amount of egg yolk used.
