# Lazy Peanuts
Cuisine: Common home-style dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥜 Peanuts 1000g  
⭐ Star anise a few pieces (divided into two portions)  
🍃 Bay leaves a few pieces (divided into two portions)  
🪵 Cinnamon stick some (divided into two portions)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns some (divided into two portions)  
🌶️ Dried chilies a few pieces (divided into two portions)  
🧂 Table salt 700g (used for heat conduction during salt-roasting; sifted out before eating)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥣 Strainer  
🥘 Stainless steel bowl

## Instructions
1. Prepare the peanuts. Divide the star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chilies into two equal portions.
2. Add an appropriate amount of water to the wok. Place the peanuts in cold water and add one portion of the spices. Bring to a boil over medium-low heat and cook for 5 minutes to remove the raw taste. Drain the peanuts.
3. Heat another wok over high heat, then add the table salt and the remaining portion of spices. Reduce to low heat and stir-fry until the salt reaches about 100°C (212°F).
4. Add the drained peanuts to the hot salt and stir-fry continuously.
   **Note**: Keep the heat on low throughout to prevent burning. When the peanuts are first added, the salt will stick heavily to their surface; do not taste them at this point.
5. Continue stir-frying until the salt grains gradually fall off and dry out, and the peanuts release their aroma and feel slightly dry.
6. Stir-fry until the salt grains begin to turn slightly yellow and the peanuts become crispy and fragrant. The total stir-frying time is about 10 minutes.
7. Turn off the heat, and continue to stir-fry using the residual heat of the wok for another 5 minutes to make the peanuts even crispier.
8. Use a strainer to separate the peanuts from the hot salt, transfer them to a clean bowl, and let them cool completely before serving.

## Health Advice
Peanuts are rich in unsaturated fatty acids and protein, making them a healthy snack. However, salt-roasted peanuts have a high sodium content, so it is recommended to eat only a small handful (about 30g) at a time to avoid excess intake. Moderate consumption can benefit cardiovascular health in middle-aged and elderly people, but those with high blood pressure should carefully control their salt intake.
