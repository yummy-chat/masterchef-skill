# Braised Pork Belly with Tofu Puffs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Preparation Steps
1.  Pork belly with skin on: Use a blowtorch or open flame to singe any remaining hairs on the skin. Soak in warm water, then scrape and clean thoroughly. First cut into thick slices, then cut into small pieces. Set aside.
2.  Cut a small slit into each tofu puff. Once water is boiling, blanch the tofu puffs for about 2 minutes. Remove and use a slotted spoon to press out excess moisture, eliminating the oily smell and aiding in later sauce absorption.
3.  Heat the wok over high heat, add a little oil. Add the pork belly pieces and stir-fry over high heat until the fat renders. Reduce to medium heat and stir-fry until the surface is golden brown and the fat has rendered out. Push the meat to the side of the wok.
4.  Using the remaining oil in the wok, sauté scallion sections, ginger slices, garlic cloves, broken star anise, and dried chilies over medium heat until fragrant.
5.  Pour 1 tablespoon of yellow wine along the side of the wok, stir-fry quickly to let the alcohol evaporate. Add light soy sauce and stir-fry until a savory aroma develops. Then add the soybean paste, reduce heat to low, and stir-fry to release the paste's fragrance.
    **Key**: The yellow wine must be added along the hot side of the wok over high heat. After adding the paste, do not use high heat to prevent burning.
6.  Add enough boiling water to cover. Add rock sugar, dark soy sauce, and aged vinegar. Stir to combine, cover, and simmer over low heat for 20 minutes.
    **Key**: Do not add salt at this point; allow the meat to braise and become tender while absorbing flavor.
7.  Remove the lid. Discard the aromatics (scallions, ginger, etc.) from the wok. Season with salt, white pepper powder, and MSG. Stir to combine.
8.  Add the prepared tofu puffs. Toss to combine evenly. Increase heat to high to reduce the sauce, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients evenly. Do not reduce the sauce until completely dry.
9.  Turn off the heat. Transfer to a preheated clay pot, garnish with chopped scallions, and serve. The tofu puffs, having absorbed the rich meat broth, are bursting with juice; the pork belly is tender and soft without being greasy.

## Health Tips
Pork belly is rich in high-quality protein and B vitamins, but it is also high in saturated fat. It is recommended to serve alongside stir-fried vegetables to balance nutrition. While the tofu puffs are delicious after absorbing the meaty sauce, their calorie content is doubled, so portion control is advisable for a healthier meal.
