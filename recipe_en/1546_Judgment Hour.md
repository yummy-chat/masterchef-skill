# Judgment Hour
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍠 Lotus root, appropriate amount (sliced and shaped by carving holes)  
🌾 Lotus root starch, appropriate amount (used to make chewy balls)  
🌿 Butterfly pea flowers, appropriate amount (for coloring)  
🌼 Dried osmanthus flowers, appropriate amount (sprinkled last for fragrance)  
🌾 Flour, appropriate amount (used to make butter roux)  
🧈 Butter, appropriate amount (for stir-frying the roux)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (to enhance sweetness)  
🍋 Lemon juice, appropriate amount (to turn the broth from blue to purple)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board (for cutting ingredients)  
🗡️ Small knife (for shaping lotus root slices)  
🥄 Soup spoon (for stirring and serving)  
🍳 Frying pan, wooden spatula (for dry-frying flour and making butter roux)  
🫖 Clay pot (for boiling the main broth)  
🥣 Glass bowl (for mixing lotus root starch and boiling water)  
🥢 Strainer (for removing butterfly pea flowers)  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Phase  
1. Slice the lotus root, and use a small knife to carve small holes in the slices to create a “skeleton lotus root” shape.  
2. Soak the shaped lotus root slices in water with lemon juice to prevent oxidation and discoloration.  
3. Pour lotus root starch into a bowl, add boiling water and stir quickly until it forms a thick, translucent paste. Let it cool slightly, then cut into strips and dice, and roll into small balls.  
   **Note**: Store-bought small glutinous rice balls can be used instead of lotus root starch balls.  
4. Heat a frying pan, add flour and dry-fry until fragrant. Add butter and stir-fry over low to medium heat until the butter is completely melted and mixed with the flour into a smooth paste. Remove and set aside.  

### Cooking Phase  
1. Add water to a clay pot, place in the butterfly pea flowers and bring to a boil. Simmer until the water turns deep blue, then strain out the butterfly pea flowers with a strainer.  
2. Add the soaked “skeleton lotus root” slices and the rolled lotus root starch balls into the blue broth.  
3. Once the broth comes to a boil, add a pinch of salt, then squeeze in the lemon juice. The broth will quickly change from blue to purple-pink.  
   **Key Point**: “To enhance sweetness, add a bit of salt”; a small amount of salt enhances the overall flavor.  
4. Add the prepared butter roux, stir well to slightly thicken the broth.  
5. Turn off the heat, sprinkle with dried osmanthus flowers for garnish, and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Lotus root is rich in dietary fiber and aids digestion; lotus root starch balls are gentle on the stomach and suitable as a light dessert or afternoon snack. Note that the coloring from butterfly pea flowers comes from natural anthocyanins, and the color change with lemon juice is an acid-base reaction, which can be used to teach children about natural phenomena.
