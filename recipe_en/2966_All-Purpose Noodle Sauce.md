# All-Purpose Noodle Sauce
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Cinnamon 2g
🌿 Star Anise 2g
🌿 Black Cardamom 1 piece
🌿 Long Pepper 2 pieces
🌿 Curry Leaf 3g
🌿 Amomum 2g
🌿 Angelica Dahurica 4g
🍃 Bay Leaf 1g
🌸 Cloves 2 pieces
🪵 Licorice Root 2g
🌶️ Sichuan Peppercorns 6g
🌿 Fennel Seeds 4g
🌿 Kaempferia Galanga 2g
🍄 Dried Shiitake Mushrooms 30g
🥬 Celery 20g
🌱 Cilantro 15g
🫑 Green Pepper 20g
🥕 Radish Slices 20g
🧅 Scallion 40g
🫚 Ginger 20g
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers appropriate amount (for stir-frying)
🧂 Salt 30g
🧂 MSG 12g (add after turning off heat)
🍶 Soy Sauce 350g
🍶 Premium Light Soy Sauce 150g
🍎 Chinese Black Vinegar 1500g (3 jin)
🍬 Rock Sugar 10g
💧 Water 750g
🛢️ Cooking Oil appropriate amount (quantity unspecified)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Wooden Spatula
🕸️ Strainer
🥣 Soup Bowl

## Instructions
1.  Rinse all dried spices with clean water and soak until moist.
2.  Prepare the shiitake mushrooms, celery, cilantro, green pepper, radish slices, scallion, and ginger by cutting them appropriately and setting aside.
3.  Heat oil in the wok, then add the dried chili peppers and stir-fry until fragrant.
4.  Drain the soaked, moist spices, add them to the wok, and stir-fry. As the temperature rises, allow the spices to fully release their aromatics and flavors.
5.  Once the spices are fragrant and the temperature is high, quickly pour in the Chinese black vinegar to "wok-fry" (deglaze).
**Key Point**: High heat quickly evaporates the harsh acidity in the vinegar, removing its raw, astringent taste.

6.  Immediately after the vinegar comes to a boil, add 750g of water.
**Key Point**: Do not boil the vinegar excessively to avoid completely evaporating its sourness; adding water "breaks" the sourness and increases the total volume of the sauce, ensuring the noodles remain smooth and not sticky.

7.  Once the water boils again, add all the prepared vegetables. Reduce the heat to low and simmer slowly to infuse the sauce with the aroma of the spices and vegetables.
**Key Point**: The fresh fragrance of celery and green pepper is a hallmark of this flavor profile.

8.  After the vegetable aromas have been released, add the soy sauce, premium light soy sauce, salt, and rock sugar. Simmer until the rock sugar is completely dissolved.
**Key Point**: Do not simmer for too long after adding the soy sauce, to prevent the overall flavor from dissipating.

9.  Turn off the heat, sprinkle in the MSG, and let it dissolve using the residual heat.
10. Strain the finished sauce through a strainer into a bowl, removing all solid residues to obtain a clear noodle sauce.

## Health Advice
This noodle sauce is rich in spices with complex aromatic layers, but has a high sodium content. It is recommended that individuals with high blood pressure use it in moderation and pair it with vegetables and protein for balanced nutrition. It has a high vinegar content; those with excessive stomach acid or stomach ulcers should consume with caution.
