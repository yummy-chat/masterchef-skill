# Sam’s Club Beef Roll Replica
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef ribeye 300g  
🌾 High-gluten flour 200g  
🌾 Low-gluten flour 50g  
💧 Warm water 100g  
🥛 Milk 55g  
🫗 Yeast 2g  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🍬 Sugar 20g  
🧈 Butter 20g  
🫑 Bell peppers 180g  
🧅 Onion 120g  
🧀 Mozzarella cheese 300g  
🧀 Parmesan cheese powder, to taste  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🧂 Daesang bulgogi marinade 75g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🪣 Sieve  
🥄 Whisk  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🫙 Plastic wrap  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🫗 Glass bowl  
🍳 Frying pan  
🔥 Oven  
📏 Rolling pin  
🟫 Silicone kneading mat  
🧻 Baking parchment paper  
🖌️ Pastry brush  
🥄 Measuring spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
### Preparation Stage  
1. Sift and mix high-gluten flour with low-gluten flour. In another container, combine warm water, milk, yeast, salt, and sugar, stirring until the salt and sugar are mostly dissolved. Gradually pour the liquid into the flour and knead into a dough. Add softened butter and knead until the butter is fully absorbed and the dough surface is smooth.  
   **Note**: The dough will be sticky initially; sprinkle a small amount of high-gluten flour to prevent sticking. This is a semi-leavened dough, so no need for full fermentation.  
2. Cover the kneaded dough with plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.  
   **Note**: The purpose of resting is to relax the gluten, making it easier to roll out later.  
3. Trim the ribeye of connective tissue, slice it thinly, add 45g of Daesang bulgogi marinade, mix well, and marinate for 15 minutes.  
   **Note**: Slice the beef as thin as possible to ensure a tender texture.  
4. Cut bell peppers and onion into thin strips. Divide the mozzarella cheese into two portions of 150g each and bring them to room temperature.  
   **Note**: Pre-warming the cheese is crucial for even melting.  
5. Preheat the oven to 250°C.  
   **Note**: High-temperature quick baking is needed to set the crust swiftly while keeping the interior moist.  

### Cooking Stage  
1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onion strips until fragrant. Add the marinated beef slices and stir-fry over medium-high heat until about half cooked. Add bell pepper strips, then add 30g of Daesang bulgogi marinade, stir-fry quickly to combine, and turn off the heat.  
   **Note**: Do not fully cook the beef; otherwise, it will become tough during baking. Let the filling cool completely before use.  
2. Mix the cooled filling with 150g of mozzarella cheese until well combined.  
3. Divide the rested dough into 3 equal portions, roll them into balls, and let them rest for 10 minutes. Take one piece of dough, sprinkle a little dry flour, and roll it into a large thin sheet about 30 cm square.  
   **Note**: If the dough is sticky while rolling, sprinkle a small amount of high-gluten flour. Some slight shrinking of the dough sheet is normal.  
4. Spread the mixed filling on one-third of the dough sheet, shaping it into a long strip. Top the filling with the remaining 150g of mozzarella cheese. Fold the left and right sides of the dough inward to cover the ends of the filling. Roll from the side closest to you, tightening as you go, and place the roll seam-side down.  
   **Note**: Seal the edges tightly when wrapping to prevent leaking during baking.  
5. Place the rolled beef rolls seam-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the surface with a layer of cooking oil and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese powder.  
   **Note**: Oiling helps the cheese powder adhere.  
6. Place in the preheated oven on the middle rack and bake at 250°C for 6–9 minutes, until the surface is golden with browned spots. If not sufficiently colored, move to the top rack for another 1–2 minutes.  
   **Note**: Adjust baking time based on your oven’s actual temperature; high-temperature quick baking is key to avoid drying and hardening the crust.  

## Health Tips  
This beef roll offers a balanced combination of protein and carbohydrates, with bell peppers providing vitamin C, making it suitable for post-workout energy replenishment. Avoid fully cooking the beef beforehand, as it will become tough after baking. Pair with a salad to control calorie intake, given the generous amount of cheese used.
