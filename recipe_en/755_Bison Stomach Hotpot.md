# Bison Stomach Hotpot
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐂 Bison stomach contents (undigested grass from the bison stomach, the core of the broth) about the amount from one whole bison stomach  
🥩 Beef appropriate amount (sliced thinly against the grain)  
🍡 Beef balls appropriate amount  
🐟 Fish balls appropriate amount  
🦀 Crab sticks appropriate amount  
🐄 Tripe appropriate amount  
🥬 Green vegetables (referred to as "scallion segments" in the video) appropriate amount (washed and cut into segments)  
🫒 Vegetable oil appropriate amount (slightly more than usual for stir-frying)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (sliced or crushed)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount  
🍲 Hotpot base about 200-300 grams  
🌶️ Dried chilies a large handful  
🫘 Dried Sichuan peppercorns a large handful  
🧂 Bison stomach broth all of it  
🧂 Salt/other seasonings optional (the hotpot base already contains salt)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍲 Large iron wok  
🥄 Strainers (one coarse mesh, one fine mesh)  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥣 Large bowl  

## Preparation Steps  
### Prep Stage  
1. Wash the surface of the fresh bison stomach clean, cut it open along the middle, and remove the undigested grass contents inside, placing them in a bowl.  
**Note**: Carefully separate the grass from the stomach lining, ensuring the grass is not spoiled or rotten.  
2. Add clean water to the grass, stir vigorously, first strain through a coarse mesh strainer to remove large residue, then strain through a fine mesh strainer to obtain a smooth, dark green broth.  
**Note**: Straining must be thorough to avoid residue affecting the texture of the broth.  
3. Pour the strained bison stomach broth into a clean large pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and set aside.  
**Note**: Boiling the bison stomach broth first helps remove some of the raw grassy smell.  
4. Slice the beef thinly against the grain; prepare beef balls, fish balls, crab sticks, and other ingredients for dipping; wash the green vegetables and cut into segments.  
**Note**: The beef slices should be thin for quick cooking.  

### Cooking Stage  
1. Heat oil in a wok, add ginger and scallions, and stir-fry until fragrant.  
2. Add the whole block of hotpot base, stir-fry over low heat until melted and the red oil has released.  
3. Add dried chilies and dried Sichuan peppercorns, continue stir-frying until fragrant.  
**Note**: Keep the heat on low to medium throughout to prevent the spices from burning; the hotpot base must be fully melted and aromatic.  
4. Pour all of the pre-boiled bison stomach broth into the pot with the stir-fried base, the broth will turn a dark green with red.  
**Note**: Bring to a boil again after mixing, avoid prolonged simmering to prevent the flavor from becoming too bitter.  
5. Bring the mixed hotpot broth to a boil over high heat until it is rolling and bubbling.  
6. Add the beef slices, tripe, meatballs, and other ingredients in sequence for dipping and cooking.  
**Key Points**:  
   - Quick-cooking ingredients like beef slices and tripe should be blanched briefly to maintain a tender texture.  
   - Meatballs can be cooked longer until they float and expand.  
   - For serving, you can dip in sesame oil or other condiments.  

## Health Advice  
Bison stomach hotpot is rich in plant fiber and fermented products from the grass, but the broth is high in oil and salt, so watch your sodium intake. Suitable for adventurous eaters, but those with sensitive stomachs should be cautious.
