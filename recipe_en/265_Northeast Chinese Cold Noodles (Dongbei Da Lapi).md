# Northeast Chinese Cold Noodles (Dongbei Da Lapi)
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥔 Sweet potato starch 300g
💧 Water 600g
🥜 Peanuts 50-60g
🥒 Cucumber appropriate amount (shredded)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic 5 cloves (mashed into garlic paste)
🌶️ Chili flakes 20g
🫘 White sesame seeds 5g
🥢 Soy sauce 30g
🍶 White vinegar 20g
🍬 Sugar 15g
🫗 Sesame oil 7g
🧂 MSG 3g
🧂 Salt 3g
🥜 Sesame paste 20g
🫒 Hot oil appropriate amount
🫒 Peanut oil a small amount

## Cookware and Equipment
🥣 Large bowl
🍽️ Stainless steel flat plate (2 pieces)
🍲 Large pot
🪣 Large basin
♨️ Air fryer
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥄 Spoon, bowl
🥢 Food tongs

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the starch slurry**: Pour sweet potato starch and water into a large bowl, mix by hand until no visible lumps remain.
**Note**: The ratio of starch to water is 1:2. Use your hands to feel if the lumps are completely dissolved.

2. **Fry the peanuts**: Place peanuts in the air fryer, spray a light layer of peanut oil, and cook at 180°C for 6 minutes.
**Note**: Peanuts are light; do not line with parchment paper to prevent them from being blown up by hot air and touching the heating element.

3. **Prepare for steaming**: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Prepare another large basin of cold water. Use two flat plates in rotation.

4. **Steam the noodles**: Stir the slurry well each time before scooping about 100g. Pour it onto a flat plate and spread it evenly to cover the bottom. Place the plate on top of the boiling water, let it sit for a few seconds, then gently rotate to ensure even thickness. Cover with a lid and steam over high heat for 2 minutes, until the noodle sheet puffs up with large bubbles and is fully cooked.
**Key**: Rotating the plate prevents uneven thickness; maintain high heat throughout.

5. **Cool and remove from mold**: Use tongs to remove the steamed plate and immediately place it into the cold water basin to cool. Once fully cooled, gently peel off the noodle sheet from the edge while submerged in water.
**Key**: Cooling is essential for easy removal; keep the finished noodles submerged in cold water to prevent sticking.

6. **Cut into strips**: Transfer the noodles to a fresh basin of water. Fold them in a Z-shape and cut into wide strips. Continue to keep them soaked in water until ready to use.

7. **Prepare the sesame paste**: Gradually add water to the sesame paste, stirring until it reaches a slightly thinner consistency than yogurt.

8. **Prepare the sauce**: In a bowl, combine chili flakes and white sesame seeds. Pour hot oil over them to release the aroma and mix well. Then add soy sauce, white vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, MSG, and salt, and stir to combine.

9. **Assemble the dish**: Drain the soaked noodles and place them in a large bowl. First, mix in the diluted sesame paste. Then add cucumber shreds, cilantro sections, and garlic paste. Pour the sauce over the top. Finally, add the fried peanuts and mix everything together by hand until well combined.

## Health Advice
Homemade cold noodles made from pure sweet potato starch are low in fat and high in fiber, making them ideal for summer appetizers or to help cut the richness of heavy main dishes. Peanuts and sesame paste provide healthy fats, but they are calorie-dense; during weight loss, consider controlling the amount of peanuts. These noodles should be made and eaten fresh, as they will harden over time.
