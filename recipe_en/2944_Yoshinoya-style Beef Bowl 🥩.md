# Yoshinoya-style Beef Bowl 🥩
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable all year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🧅 1 onion
🥩 500g thinly sliced beef rolls (beef belly)
🧄 4 cloves garlic (minced)
🥚 Eggs (optional)
🍚 Rice (as needed)
🌾 White sesame seeds (to taste)

🍯 3 tbsp light soy sauce
🍯 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
🍶 2 tbsp mirin
🍶 1 tbsp rice wine, sake, or fermented rice juice
🍬 1 tbsp sugar
🌽 1/2 tbsp cornstarch
🧂 A pinch of chicken bouillon
💧 About 6 tbsp water
🫒 Sesame oil (to taste)
🧂 Salt (to taste)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🥘 Saucepan
🔪 Cutting board and knife
🥣 Bowl and spoon
🥛 Water cup
🫗 Lid
🥢 Slotted spoon or chopsticks

## Instructions
1. Cut off the root and tip of the onion, peel off the dry skin starting from the second-to-last layer, rinse, halve, and slice into strips.
   **Note**: Cutting off the root reveals the layers, making it easier to peel. To avoid stinging eyes, hold a sip of water in your mouth without swallowing.
2. Press the garlic cloves flat with the side of a knife, then chop into mince.
   **Note**: Pressing prevents the garlic from rolling while cutting, reducing the risk of injury.
3. In a bowl, combine light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, mirin, sugar, cornstarch, chicken bouillon, and water. Stir until no lumps remain to make the sauce.
   **Note**: Pay attention to the ratio of light soy sauce to dark soy sauce; adjust sugar based on whether mirin is used; add a little extra water if needed, and supplement during cooking if the sauce is insufficient.
4. Bring water to a boil in a saucepan. Add the beef rolls and blanch over high heat for about 30 seconds until they change color. Remove and drain.
   **Note**: This step removes impurities and speeds up later cooking. Skip if you prefer, and stir-fry directly instead.
5. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Add all the sliced onion and minced garlic. Stir-fry for about 1 minute until the onion is noticeably softened.
   **Note**: If the vegetable surface is dry (no visible water droplets), the oil is less likely to splatter.
6. Pour the prepared sauce into the softened onion and mix well. Add extra water if needed to adjust the sauce volume. Then add the blanched beef rolls, mix thoroughly, and simmer until the sauce is gently bubbling.
   **Note**: The sauce contains cornstarch and will thicken upon stirring. Keep enough sauce for mixing with rice. If using raw beef, add more water and cook thoroughly.
7. In another pot, bring 3 cups of water to a boil, then turn off the heat. Immediately add 1 cup of cold water, gently place room-temperature eggs into the water, cover, and let sit for 10 minutes.
   **Note**: It is best to use eggs that are safe to eat raw.
8. Serve the stir-fried beef and onion over rice, sprinkle with white sesame seeds, and top with a soft-boiled (onsen) egg—crack the egg into a bowl first, then transfer to the rice to keep it intact.
   **Note**: Pre-cracking the egg into a bowl prevents breakage when placing it on the rice.

## Health Tips
This beef bowl paired with vegetables and a soft-boiled egg is nutritionally balanced but relatively high in calories. It is recommended to serve with a side of lightly stir-fried vegetables or a salad. Suitable for those needing a quick energy boost, but those who are obese or have high blood lipids should control their portion size.
