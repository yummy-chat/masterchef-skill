# Restaurant-Style Tomato Cheese Pasta
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍅 Tomato approx. 1.5kg (recommend using a mix of around 5 varieties, such as cherry tomatoes, grape tomatoes, regular tomatoes, and two types of canned tomatoes)
🧅 Onion 1 (yellow/white onion, finely diced)
🥕 Carrot 1 (finely diced)
🥬 Celery approx. 1/4 stalk (finely diced)
🧄 Garlic to taste (minced)
🍝 Pasta to taste (choose shapes with texture or rough edges, such as No. 37, No. 33, or No. 210)
🌿 Fresh basil leaves to taste (for final seasoning and garnish)
🧂 Salt to taste (a small amount when sautéing vegetables; a generous amount, enough to make the water noticeably salty, for boiling pasta)
🥫 Tomato paste 15g (must be sautéed to remove acidity)
⚫ Black pepper to taste (added after mixing sauce with pasta)
🧀 Mixed cheese generous amount (15g each of Gruyère, Parmesan, and Comté, or choose any one, or other hard cheeses)
🧈 Butter approx. 15g (to smooth and enrich the sauce)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Sauté pan/saucepan (for sautéing vegetables and simmering sauce)
🌀 Blender (to purée the cooked tomatoes and vegetables)
🕸️ Fine-mesh strainer (to strain the tomato purée, removing seeds and skins)
🥣 Pasta pot (for boiling pasta)
🍳 Non-stick pan (for combining pasta and sauce at home)
🥄 Spoon (for stirring and tasting)
🔪 Knife, cutting board (for prepping ingredients)

## Steps
1. Cut the onion, carrot, and celery into small, uniform dice; mince the garlic; seed and chop large tomatoes, wash small tomatoes, and open the canned tomatoes.
   **Key Point**: The smaller and more uniform the vegetable dice, the more evenly they heat and the faster the aroma develops.

2. Heat oil in a pan, add the diced onion, carrot, and celery, sprinkle with a little salt, and sauté over medium-low heat until the vegetables soften and become fragrant.
   **Key Point**: Adding salt helps the vegetables release water, drawing out their flavors.

3. Add 15g of tomato paste and continue sautéing until the color deepens, the sour, pungent smell disappears, and a rich aroma emerges.

4. Add all the fresh tomatoes and canned tomatoes to the pot and stir to combine.
   **Key Point**: Do not add water at this point; rely on the tomatoes' own juices for braising.

5. Cover the pot and heat over medium-low heat for about 30 minutes, until the tomatoes are fully broken down and the sauce is thick.

6. Let the cooked sauce cool, then transfer it to a blender and purée until smooth and even.
   **Key Point**: Be sure to let the sauce cool before blending to prevent hot liquid from splashing and causing injury.

7. Pour the tomato purée through a fine-mesh strainer, pressing with a spoon to remove seeds and skins, resulting in a smooth sauce.
   **Key Point**: This step greatly improves the sauce's smoothness; it can be optionally omitted for home cooking.

8. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of water to a boil, add a generous amount of salt, add the pasta, and cook according to the package directions until al dente.

9. Drain the cooked pasta and add it directly to the pot with the tomato sauce. Add salt, black pepper, a generous amount of mixed grated cheese, and a pat of butter.
   **Key Point**: Use the residual heat from the pasta and the warmth of the sauce to melt the cheese and butter; emulsify them by stirring or tossing the pan so they evenly coat each strand of pasta.

10. Add torn fresh basil leaves, gently toss to combine, and serve immediately.
    **Key Point**: Add the basil at the very end to preserve its fresh aroma.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in lycopene and dietary fiber, making it suitable for most people. However, the cheese and butter are high in calories, so portion control is recommended. Pairing it with a green salad can help balance the nutritional density.
