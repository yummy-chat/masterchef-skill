# Lotus Seed Porridge
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🪷 Lotus Root 10 jin (for homemade lotus root starch)  
⚪️ Dried Lotus Root Starch 30 g (finished product, for brewing)  
🍬 White Sugar 1 tbsp  
⚫️ Cooked Black Sesame Seeds 1 tbsp  
⚪️ Cooked White Sesame Seeds 1 tbsp  
🍎 Crushed Hawthorn Cake 1 tbsp  
🌻 Melon Seeds 1 tbsp  
🍇 Crushed Raisins 1 tbsp  
🥜 Crushed Peanuts 1 tbsp  
💧 Cool Boiled Water 30 g (for mixing lotus root starch paste)  
🫖 Boiling Water 520 ml (for brewing)

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Kitchen Knife, Cutting Board  
🥒 Vegetable Peeler  
🌀 High-Speed Blender (or Blender)  
🩹 Gauze (or Filter Cloth)  
🫕 Large Pot (for settling)  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🥢 Whisk  
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. **Preprocess the Lotus Root**: Wash 10 jin of lotus root thoroughly, cut off the knots, peel the skin with a vegetable peeler, first cut into small sections, then cut into small pieces.
   **Note**: Be careful when peeling to avoid injuring your fingers.
2. **Make Lotus Root Puree**: Place the lotus root pieces into the high-speed blender, add an appropriate amount of water, and blend until a smooth, fine puree with no visible particles.
   **Key**: The amount of water added should be just enough to allow the blender to operate smoothly; the puree should have no obvious coarse particles.
3. **Strain the Lotus Root Puree**: Line a large bowl with gauze, pour in the lotus root puree in batches, and squeeze hard to filter the white starchy water into the large pot; set the lotus root pulp aside. Add water to the pulp again, knead and rinse, then perform a second filtration. Repeat this process until the drained liquid becomes clear.
   **Key**: The filtration must be thorough to extract as much starch as possible. The leftover lotus root pulp can be used for other purposes.
4. **Settle and Extract Starch**: Let the collected starchy water sit for at least 3 hours. Pour off the clear water on top, retaining the white wet lotus root starch at the bottom. Spread the wet starch out and let it dry completely to obtain dried lotus root starch.
5. **Prepare the Raw Starch Paste**: Place 30 g of dried lotus root starch into a large bowl, add 30 g of cool boiled water, and whisk until smooth with no lumps.
   **Key**: Must use cool water to mix; adding hot water directly will cause clumping.
6. **Brew into a Thick Soup**: Pour 520 ml of freshly boiled water all at once into the lotus root starch paste, while whisking continuously and rapidly.
   **Key**: The water must be at a rolling boil; whisk immediately and quickly after pouring to allow the starch to gelatinize evenly, resulting in a thick, translucent consistency.
7. **Add Toppings**: While hot, add white sesame seeds, black sesame seeds, crushed hawthorn cake, melon seeds, crushed raisins, crushed peanuts, and white sugar to the lotus root starch soup, and stir well to combine.
8. **Serve in Bowls**: Ladle the mixed lotus seed porridge into small bowls and enjoy.

## Health Advice
Lotus seed porridge is rich in starch and dietary fiber, making it suitable for people with poor digestion or those needing gentle nourishment. Although the process of making homemade lotus root starch is slightly tedious, it ensures no additives; it is recommended to make small batches each time. Note that boiling water must be used during brewing, and stir quickly to prevent clumping.
