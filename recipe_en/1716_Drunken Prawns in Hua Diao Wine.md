# Drunken Prawns in Hua Diao Wine
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🦐 Giant river prawn 600g (choose large prawns with dark head roe)  
🧂 Salt to taste (for blanching)  
🍋 Lemon 1-2 slices (or ginger, for blanching to remove odor and enhance flavor)  
❄️ Ice/ice water to taste (to cool prawns after blanching)  
🌿 Cilantro a little (for garnish)  
🍶 Light soy sauce (Premium) 150g  
🍬 Rock sugar 120g  
🥃 Erguotou 20g (add after sauce has cooled)  
🍷 Hua Diao wine 250g (recommend 5-10 year aged, add after sauce has cooled)  
🥃 White spirit about 10g (optional, add after sauce has cooled)  
🍑 Preserved plums 3 pieces (place in sauce basin)  
⭐ Star anise 2 pieces  
🍃 Bay leaf 1 piece  
🍊 Dried tangerine peel 1 piece  
🪵 Cinnamon stick 1/4 piece  
🌶️ Peppercorns 5 grains  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Soup pot (for blanching)  
🥘 Saucepan (for simmering spice water)  
✂️ Scissors (for trimming prawn claws, antennae, and butterflying)  
🫗 Metal basin/bowl (for preparing soaking sauce)  
🍱 Airtight container (for refrigerated soaking)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for retrieving prawns)  
🍽️ Serving plate (for plating)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. **Prepare live prawns**: Wash the giant river prawns. Use scissors to trim the claws and long antennae, then cut a slit along the back of each prawn to allow flavor to penetrate.
2. **Blanch**: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add salt and lemon slices. Once boiling, add the prepared prawns and cook over high heat for about 1 minute. Remove immediately.  
**Note**: Do not add cooking wine when blanching live prawns; lemon or ginger is sufficient to remove odor.
3. **Ice bath**: Immediately transfer the cooked prawns to ice water and soak, allowing the meat to contract for a firmer, springier texture. If ice water is unavailable, this step can be skipped, but it will affect the crispness.
4. **Toast spices**: In a separate dry pot (without turning on the heat), add star anise, bay leaf, dried tangerine peel, cinnamon stick, and peppercorns. Turn the heat to medium-low and dry-roast briefly to release the aroma.
5. **Prepare sauce base**: Add water, 150g light soy sauce, and 120g rock sugar to the pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 5 minutes, creating a spiced soy sauce base.
6. **Cool and add wine**: Strain the cooked spice water or pour it directly into a large basin containing 3 preserved plums. Let it cool to room temperature. Once the sauce has cooled, add 20g Erguotou, 250g Hua Diao wine, and about 10g white spirit. Stir to combine.  
**Key**: Make sure the sauce has cooled to room temperature before adding the wine; otherwise, the heat will volatilize the wine's aroma and affect the flavor.
7. **Soak for flavor**: Place all the iced, butterflied prawns into the prepared sauce, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover the container and refrigerate overnight.
8. **Plate**: Remove the marinated prawns and arrange on a serving plate. Garnish with cilantro if desired, and drizzle a little of the chilled original sauce over the prawns before serving.

## Health Notes  
This dish is high in protein and low in fat. The prawn meat is tender and bouncy, making it an ideal summer appetizer. However, the sauce contains higher levels of sugar and salt, so those with high blood pressure or managing sugar intake should consume in moderation.
