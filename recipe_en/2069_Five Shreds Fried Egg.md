# Five Shreds Fried Egg
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Season: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Five Shreds (pickled vegetables, made from 5 types of fruits and vegetables preserved with sugar) 50g
🥚 Eggs 5
🍅 Ketchup 80g
💧 Water 100g
🍬 White sugar 30g
🍶 White vinegar 50g
🧂 Salt a pinch
🫒 Cooking oil a large amount (for deep frying)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🕸️ Strainer (for filtering hot oil)

## Instructions
1. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, mix together ketchup, water, white sugar, white vinegar, and a pinch of salt. Stir well and set aside.
**Key point**: The balance of sweet and sour in the sauce is crucial to the flavor.

2. Pour a sufficient amount of cooking oil into the wok and heat to about 150°C (high oil temperature).
**Key point**: High oil temperature is essential for the eggs to puff up.

3. Crack the eggs into the hot oil one at a time, maintaining high heat. The eggs will quickly expand and develop large bubbles.
**Check for doneness**: The eggs should puff up and form large bubbles upon entering the oil.

4. Deep fry over high heat for about 2-3 minutes. Gently flip the eggs halfway through until the edges turn golden brown and the inside is cooked through.
**Check for doneness**: The eggs should be fully set, puffed, and have browned edges.

5. Remove the eggs with a slotted spoon and drain off excess oil. Set aside.

6. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok, add the Five Shreds, and stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.
**Key point**: If Five Shreds are unavailable, this step can be omitted.

7. Pour the prepared sauce into the wok, turn the heat to high, and bring to a boil. Cook until the sauce thickens to a syrup-like consistency that coats the spatula.
**Key point**: The sauce must be thickened to ensure it clings to the eggs.

8. Return the fried eggs to the wok, reduce the heat to low, and let both sides soak in the sauce. Cook for about 2 minutes to allow the eggs to fully absorb the sauce.
**Key point**: Simmer over low heat to infuse the eggs with flavor and coat them evenly.

9. Carefully transfer the sauced eggs to a serving plate.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in protein but, due to deep frying and the generous amount of sweet and sour sauce, is relatively high in calories. It is recommended to consume in moderation. People with high blood lipids should be cautious. Pair with light vegetables to balance the richness and control staple food intake.
