# Tips for Steaming Buns (Mantou)
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Flour, appropriate amount  
🍊 Orange peel, appropriate amount (for enhancing fragrance)  
🐖 Lard, appropriate amount (add when kneading dough to make buns whiter and softer)  
🍶 White vinegar, appropriate amount (for remedying yellow discoloration)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment
♨️ Steamer  
🥣 Bowl/Plate  

## Steps
1. Mix the flour with an appropriate amount of lard and knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Key Point**: Lard helps the buns achieve a white color and soft, fluffy texture after steaming.  
2. Follow the standard method for proofing and steaming.  
   **Fragrance Tip**: Place an appropriate amount of orange peel in the steamer while steaming to give the buns a fresh, aromatic scent.  
   **Note**: Use a moderate amount of alkali to avoid excessive alkalinity, which can cause the buns to turn yellow.  
3. If after steaming the buns turn yellow due to too much alkali, do not remove them. Instead, add an appropriate amount of white vinegar directly to the original steamer.  
4. Cover the steamer lid and continue steaming for 3–5 minutes. The buns will then change from yellow to white in color.  

## Health Advice
These buns incorporate lard and orange peel, resulting in a white, soft, and fragrant texture suitable as a staple food for the whole family. Be careful not to overuse alkali; if yellowing occurs, it can be remedied by adding white vinegar and steaming for a few more minutes.
