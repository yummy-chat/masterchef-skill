# Compound Soy Sauce
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥬 Celery 70g (cracked and cut into sections)
🫚 Old Ginger 30g (sliced into large pieces)
🥕 Carrot 30g (sliced)
🧅 Onion 120g (sliced)
🌿 Cilantro 40g (with roots or whole)
🍶 Soy Sauce 500g
💧 Water 250g
🧊 Rock Sugar 20g

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife
🪓 Cutting Board
🍳 Wok/Cooking Pot
🥄 Ladle
🥢 Strainer/Skimmer

## Instructions
1. Crack the celery with the knife blade, then cut into sections. Slice the old ginger. Slice the carrot and onion. Set the cilantro aside whole.
   **Note**: Cracking the celery helps release its aroma.
2. Pour 500g of soy sauce into the pot and bring to a boil over high heat.
3. Add 250g of room-temperature water to the boiling soy sauce and stir to combine.
4. Add 20g of rock sugar. Once the sugar is fully dissolved, add all the vegetables.
5. Continue simmering until the aroma of the vegetables is fully infused into the soy sauce.
   **Key Point**: Rock sugar enhances umami, adds sheen, and creates a smoother taste. The simmering time can be adjusted based on the release of aromas.
6. Strain out the solids using a strainer, and retain the soy sauce liquid.

## Health Tips
This compound soy sauce uses vegetables to enhance freshness and rock sugar to add sheen. It is suitable as a dipping sauce or for cold dishes. Those on a light diet can use it with confidence, but individuals with high blood pressure should control their intake. The vegetables release natural flavors during simmering, making it more nutritious than plain soy sauce, though consumption should still be moderate.
