# Meat Dragon (Chinese Steamed Meat Roll)
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 All-purpose flour 500g (protein content 9-12g)
🥩 Ground pork 250-300g (30% fat, 70% lean or 40% fat, 60% lean)
🧅 Scallion half (about 75-90g, divided into two parts)
🫚 Ginger 2-4 slices (about 10g, for scallion-ginger water)
🥄 Yeast 5g
💧 Water 280g (for kneading dough)
💧 Water 75-100ml (for soaking scallions and ginger)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns a small pinch
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🧂 Light soy sauce as needed
🧂 Dark soy sauce as needed
🦪 Oyster sauce as needed
🌿 Thirteen-spice powder as needed
🍬 White sugar about two pinches (for seasoning)
🧂 Salt 2 pinches (for kneading dough)
🍬 White sugar 2 pinches (for kneading dough)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔄 Stand mixer (with meat grinding and dough kneading functions) or manual substitution
🍳 Wok
♨️ Steamer (electric or gas steamer)
🥛 Measuring cup/graduated bottle (can use a water bottle for reference)
🫙 Plastic wrap
🥢 Rolling pin
🥄 Scraper or spoon
⚖️ Kitchen scale (optional)

## Preparation Steps
### Prep Phase
1. **Make scallion-ginger water**
   - Take part of the scallion and ginger, slice or crush them, and place in a bowl. Add a small pinch of Sichuan peppercorns.
   - Pour in boiling water and steep for at least 10 minutes. Strain and reserve the scallion-ginger water for later use.
   *Note: The finer the scallions and ginger are cut, the easier they release flavor. The steeping time must be sufficient.*

2. **Prepare the meat filling**
   - Keep the ground pork with some texture; avoid blending it into a fine paste.
   - Add the cooled scallion-ginger water to the meat in three batches, stirring in the same direction each time until the water is fully absorbed, until the filling becomes sticky, offers resistance, and takes on a gelatinous consistency.
   *Note: Thoroughly incorporating the water is key to a juicy filling.*

3. **Cook the sauce**
   - Heat oil in a wok, then add most of the scallion and stir-fry until fragrant.
   - Turn off the heat or reduce to the lowest setting. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, thirteen-spice powder, and white sugar, stirring to combine.
   - Turn the heat back to medium and stir-fry for about 10 seconds until bubbling, then immediately turn off the heat and let cool.
   *Note: The sauce must be completely cooled to a temperature that won't burn your hands before mixing with the meat filling.*

4. **Mix the filling**
   - Pour the cooled sauce into the hydrated meat filling, stirring in the same direction until well combined and the mixture firms up.
   - Add the reserved raw scallion, gently mix to combine.
   - Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to use.
   *Note: Raw scallions should be added just before assembling to prevent off-flavors. Chilling prevents the filling from releasing liquid and helps it absorb flavors more deeply.*

5. **Knead and ferment the dough**
   - Combine flour, salt, white sugar, and yeast, mixing well. Gradually add water while stirring with chopsticks to form shaggy dough clumps.
   - Knead into a smooth dough.
   - Place the dough in a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it ferment in a warm place until doubled in size.
   *State check: Dough has expanded to twice its size; when poked with a finger, the indentation does not spring back or collapse; the interior is honeycombed.*
   *Note: If the room temperature is low, you can use the oven's proofing function. The dough does not need to be extremely smooth; proper fermentation is sufficient.*

### Assembly Phase
1. **Roll out the dough and spread filling**
   - Dust the work surface with dry flour. Remove the dough and gently press to release air, avoiding excessive kneading.
   - Roll out into a large, thin rectangle or circle.
   - Spread the meat filling evenly over the dough, leaving a border around the edges.
   *Note: The thickness of the dough can be adjusted according to personal preference. Leave the edges empty to prevent filling from leaking out when rolling.*

2. **Shape into a roll**
   - Starting from one end, tightly roll into a long log. Pinch the two ends and the seam to seal.
   - You may coil it into a circle or keep it as a long shape, with the seam side down.
   *Note: Be sure to pinch the seam tightly to prevent the filling from leaking.*

3. **Proof and steam**
   - Place the shaped meat roll on a steaming rack lined with a damp cloth. Let it proof for a second time for 15–20 minutes.
   - Wait until the water in the steamer is boiling and steaming, then place the rack in the steamer. Cover tightly and steam over medium heat for 20 minutes.
   *Note: The water must be boiling before placing the roll in the steamer.*

4. **Remove from steamer**
   - After turning off the heat, let it rest for 3–5 minutes before lifting the lid.
   *Note: Resting the steamed bread for a few minutes prevents it from shrinking and becoming hard.*

## Health Advice
This Meat Roll skillfully wraps juicy meat filling into a soft, fluffy dough. Steaming ensures a balanced meal suitable for the whole family. It is recommended to control the proportion of fat and pair with a vegetable salad for a healthier meal. Those with high blood lipids should eat in moderation.
