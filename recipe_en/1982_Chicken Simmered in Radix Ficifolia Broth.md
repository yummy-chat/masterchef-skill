# Chicken Simmered in Radix Ficifolia Broth
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐔 1 chicken (choose a free-range chicken, following the rule of "three small, one large": small beak, small wings, small feet, large skin, and ample tail fat)
🌿 1 bowl of Radix Ficifolia (washed in advance)
🌱 1 bunch of Codonopsis Pilosula
🟡 3 Gardenia Fruits (for color and fragrance)
🦪 1 small handful of dried scallops (conpoy)
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (crushed)
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧂 About 100g of salt (for seasoning 6 jin of water to create a savory broth base)
🍬 2 tablespoons of sugar
🍶 About 1 liang of rice wine
🫕 A small amount of chicken fat

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🍲 Large soup pot
🥄 Soup ladle
🥢 Chopsticks
🥄 Slotted spoon or large ladle
🪣 Large basin (for ice water)

## Cooking Steps
1.  Clean the chicken. Wash the Radix Ficifolia, slice the ginger, crush the garlic, and cut the scallion into sections.
2.  Heat the wok, add a small amount of chicken fat. Add the ginger slices and garlic cloves, and stir-fry over low heat until the edges of the ginger slices are slightly browned, releasing their fragrance.
3.  Add the dried scallops, Radix Ficifolia, and scallion sections. Stir-fry over medium heat for a short time, then drizzle in the rice wine to release a rich aroma.
4.  Add 6 jin of clean water to the wok and bring to a boil over high heat. Add the Codonopsis Pilosula, Gardenia Fruits, salt, and sugar, and stir well to create a savory broth base.
5.  Pour all the broth into the large soup pot and heat until it reaches a gentle simmer at about 90°C.
6.  Hold the chicken by the head and immerse it in the hot broth, then lift it out. Repeat this three times to ensure even heating inside and outside the chicken cavity.
7.  Fully submerge the chicken in the broth. Do not cover the pot, and maintain a low simmer. First, cook for 10 minutes, then carefully turn the chicken with chopsticks and continue to simmer for about 15 minutes. The total cooking time is about 25-30 minutes.
**Note**: Not covering the pot allows you to monitor the heat and prevent the water temperature from getting too high, which would make the chicken tough.

8.  Once cooked, quickly remove the chicken and immediately immerse it in pre-prepared ice water for a short time to tighten the skin and make it crispier. Do not soak for too long.
9.  Insert chopsticks into the thickest part of the chicken thigh. If clear juice runs out, it indicates the chicken is cooked through.
10. Remove the chicken, drain it, and chop it into pieces for serving. The chicken meat will be tender and juicy, with the coconut-like fragrance of Radix Ficifolia and the delicate aroma of the medicinal herbs.

## Health Advice
This dish of Chicken Simmered in Radix Ficifolia Broth combines the technique of white-cut chicken with a medicinal broth base. The chicken is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts or those on a fat-control diet. The Radix Ficifolia and Codonopsis Pilosula in the broth help strengthen the spleen and eliminate dampness. However, the salt content is relatively high, so those sensitive to blood pressure are advised to drink less of the broth.
