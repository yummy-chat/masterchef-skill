# Corn and Shrimp Roll
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦐 Shrimp paste, appropriate amount (minced shrimp)  
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (julienned)  
🌽 Sweet corn kernels, about one bowl  
🥟 Wonton wrappers, several (store-bought)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🌿 Black pepper, a pinch  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Steamer (with steaming mat)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Place the shrimp paste, julienned carrot, sweet corn kernels, salt, and black pepper into a bowl. Stir thoroughly with a spoon until evenly combined to form the filling.  
   **Note**: Stir until the ingredients are evenly blended to avoid uneven seasoning.  
2. Take one wonton wrapper and place an appropriate amount of filling in the center. Fold it in half and pinch the edges tightly to seal.  
   **Key Point**: Ensure the seal is firmly pinched to prevent the filling from leaking during steaming.  
3. After wrapping all the shrimp rolls one by one, arrange them evenly on a steaming mat placed in the steamer, leaving suitable gaps between each.  
4. Bring water in the pot to a boil. Once the water is boiling, place the steamer in, cover the lid tightly, and steam over medium heat for 10 minutes.  
   **Key Point**: Wait for the water to boil before placing the steamer in to ensure the texture.  
5. Once steaming is complete, remove the rolls. The wrappers will appear translucent. Serve with your preferred dipping sauce.

## Health Advice  
Shrimp paste paired with corn and carrots provides high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for those on a weight loss regimen or needing to manage blood sugar levels. Although wonton wrappers are thin, consuming many still leads to high calorie intake; steaming is healthier than frying, yet moderation is still recommended.
