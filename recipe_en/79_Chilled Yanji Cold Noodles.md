# Chilled Yanji Cold Noodles
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients 🛒
🍜 Cold noodles – to taste
🥚 Marinated eggs – to taste
🥩 Saucy beef – to taste
🍎 Apple – to taste
🥒 Cucumber – to taste
🥬 Kimchi – to taste
🥜 Sesame seeds & peanuts – to taste
🧊 Ice cubes – to taste
🥤 Sprite – to taste
💧 Cool boiled water – to taste
🧂 Light soy sauce – to taste
🍶 White vinegar – to taste
🧂 Salt – to taste
🌶️ Spicy sauce – to taste (optional)

## Cookware & Equipment 🥣
🥣 Transparent glass bowl
🍲 Cooking pot
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Instructions 📝
1. Prep all ingredients in advance: cold noodles, marinated eggs, saucy beef, apple, cucumber, kimchi, and the ingredients for the cold noodle broth (ice cubes, Sprite, cool boiled water, light soy sauce, white vinegar, salt).
   **Note**: Have everything ready to ensure a smooth cooking process.

2. In a transparent glass bowl, add ice cubes, Sprite, cool boiled water, light soy sauce, white vinegar, and salt in order. Stir thoroughly with a spoon to create the cold noodle broth.
   **Note**: Follow the order and stir well to evenly blend the flavors.

3. Fill a cooking pot with enough water and bring to a boil over high heat. Add the cold noodles, then reduce to medium–low heat and cook until the noodles have no hard center. Turn off the heat.
   **Note**: Do not overcook; avoid making the noodles too soft and losing their chewy texture.

4. Use a strainer to remove the cooked noodles, then immediately rinse them under cold water. Drain well.
   **Note**: Quick rinsing locks in the chewy texture; drain thoroughly to prevent diluting the broth.

5. Place the drained noodles into the cold noodle broth. Arrange the marinated eggs, saucy beef, apple, cucumber, and kimchi on top. Finally, sprinkle with sesame seeds and peanuts, and drizzle with spicy sauce to taste.
   **Note**: Adjust the toppings according to personal preference; spicy sauce can add an extra layer of flavor.

## Health Advisory 🍏
The sweet and tangy broth made with apple and Sprite helps stimulate appetite and is rich in vitamins, making it ideal for cooling down in summer. To reduce sodium intake, use less spicy sauce and salt, and control the portion of saucy beef—especially for those with high blood pressure.
