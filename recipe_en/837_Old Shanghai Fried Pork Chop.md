# Old Shanghai Fried Pork Chop
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Pork loin or pork collar 200-300g (about 0.5cm thick, with a little fat)
🌾 All-purpose flour适量 (for coating)
🥚 Egg 1 (half the egg white for marinating, remaining egg beaten for coating)
🍞 Large-grain breadcrumbs适量 (must use large-grain)
🧂 Salt 1g
🍶 Cooking wine 10g
🌿 White pepper 0.5g
🍯 Worcestershire sauce (spicy soy sauce)适量 (for dipping)

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Cutting board, meat knife
🔨 Meat mallet (or knife back, rolling pin)
🍳 Deep frying pan
🌡️ Oil thermometer
🥄 Slotted spoon
🧺 Wire mesh rack

## Steps
1. Prepare the pork: Cut a few slits at the junction of lean and fat meat to sever the connective tissue, preventing the pork from curling during frying. Slice the meat into pork chop pieces about 0.5cm thick.
2. Tenderize: Use a meat mallet or the back of a knife to evenly pound both sides of the pork chop, flattening it and loosening the muscle fibers for a more tender texture.
3. Marinate: Add salt, white pepper, cooking wine, and half the egg white to the pork chop. Mix and rub with your hands until the liquid is fully absorbed by the meat. Marinate for 20 minutes.
4. Coat: Coat the marinated pork chop evenly with all-purpose flour, then beaten whole egg, and finally large-grain breadcrumbs. When applying the breadcrumbs, gently press them onto the pork chop to ensure they stick firmly. Let it rest for 10 minutes to allow the breadcrumbs to fully absorb moisture.
5. First fry for setting: Pour oil into a pan and heat to 150℃, then reduce to low heat. Slide one end of the pork chop closest to you into the oil first. Once fully submerged, do not flip initially. Fry over low heat for about 2 minutes, flipping once, until set and lightly colored.
6. Second fry for color: Increase heat to high to raise the oil temperature, then fry for about 30 seconds more until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on a wire mesh rack.
7. Serve with dipping: Cut into pieces and enjoy with Worcestershire sauce.

## Health Advice
This fried pork chop is best paired with a refreshing salad or pickled cucumbers to balance the oiliness. It is suitable for those needing high protein and energy. Pay attention to controlling the oil temperature to prevent the breadcrumbs from absorbing too much oil, and for the best texture, consume immediately after the second fry.
