# Three Fresh Vegetables (Di San Xian)
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍆 Long eggplant 350g (remove root, cut into rolling chunks)
🥔 Potato 250g (peel, cut into thin rolling chunks)
🌶️ Spiral pepper 100g (cut into rolling chunks, use thick sweet bell pepper if avoiding heat)
🧄 Garlic cloves 40g (about 8 cloves, minced)
🧂 Light soy sauce 30g (about 3 tablespoons)
🦪 Oyster sauce 20g (about 2 tablespoons)
🍯 Dark soy sauce 3g (small amount, for color)
🍬 White sugar 6g (2/3 level tablespoon)
🧂 MSG 3g (1 teaspoon)
🌾 Cornstarch 5g (half level tablespoon, for the sauce, do not exceed)
💧 Water 100g (about 1/3 bowl, using a rice bowl)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (must submerge ingredients, actual consumption about 30g)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Small bowl (for mixing sauce)
🔥 Gas stove

## Preparation Steps
### Preparation Phase
1. Sprinkle a little water on the eggplant surface to moisten it. Add dry cornstarch in small batches, shaking the bowl as you go, until the surface is evenly coated with a thin layer of powder.
**Note**: Coating reduces oil absorption by the eggplant.
2. Peel the potato and cut into thin rolling chunks. Cut the pepper into rolling chunks.
**Note**: Potato chunks should not be too large, otherwise they won't cook through when fried.
3. Mince the 8 garlic cloves.
4. In a small bowl, mix light soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, MSG, cornstarch, and water. Stir well to form the sauce and set aside.
**Note**: Control the amount of cornstarch to prevent the sauce from becoming too thick.

### Cooking Phase
1. Pour oil into the wok and heat until you feel obvious heat when holding your hand 5cm above the oil surface. Add the potato chunks, gently pushing with the back of a spoon to prevent sticking. Fry until dense "large fish-eye bubbles" appear in the oil, then reduce to medium-low heat. Continue frying for about 4 minutes until the potatoes are golden and cooked through.
**Key Point**: After large bubbles appear, you must reduce to medium-low heat to avoid the outside burning while the inside remains raw.
2. Remove the potatoes. Turn the heat to high and increase the oil temperature. When the oil surface just begins to smoke, add the eggplant chunks. Let them sit for about ten seconds without stirring until the outer shell sets, then gently separate them. Fry over high heat for about 3 minutes until each chunk is golden brown.
**Key Point**: Fry eggplant at high oil temperature and do not stir immediately after adding.
3. Remove the eggplant and turn off the heat. Use the residual heat of the oil to quickly blanch the pepper chunks, then immediately remove them.
**Note**: The pepper must be blanched for a very short time to maintain texture and color.
4. Leave a small amount of base oil in the wok. Add 1/3 of the minced garlic. Do not turn on the heat; use the residual heat of the wok to sauté the garlic until fragrant. Then pour in the prepared sauce, turn the heat to high, and cook until the sauce bubbles.
**Key Point**: Use residual heat to first release some garlic aroma.
5. Once the sauce bubbles, quickly add the fried potatoes, eggplant, and peppers. Stir-fry rapidly until evenly coated.
6. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle in the remaining 2/3 of raw minced garlic. Toss a few times and then serve.
**Key Point**: Add raw garlic after turning off the heat, using the residual heat of the dish to release a strong garlic aroma. This is the soul of the Three Fresh Vegetables flavor.

## Health Suggestions
Eggplant absorbs a lot of oil, so it's recommended to pair it with mixed grain rice or a light soup to balance the calories. Potatoes and peppers are rich in vitamin C and potassium, making this dish suitable for manual laborers needing energy replenishment. Pay attention to frying time to avoid excessive acrylamide formation.
