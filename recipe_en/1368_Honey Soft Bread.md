# Honey Soft Bread
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
💧 Room temperature water 320g (microwave to about 30°C)
🥖 Dry yeast 4g
🌾 Bread flour 400g
🍯 Honey 30g
🧂 Salt 2g
🧈 Unsalted butter 60g (melted, then added to dough)
🧈 Additional melted butter, as needed (for brushing after baking)

## Tools & Equipment
🥛 Measuring cup
🥣 Wooden bowl
🥄 Silicone spatula
⌛ Microwave
🔥 Oven
🍞 Baking sheet
🧻 Silicone baking mat
🗼 Cooling rack

## Instructions
1. Prepare yeast water: Microwave 320g room temperature water for 30 seconds to about 30°C, add 4g dry yeast, stir well, and let stand briefly.
2. Mix the base dough: In a wooden bowl, place bread flour, honey, and salt. Pour in the yeast water and stir with a silicone spatula until no dry flour remains. The dough will be quite wet and sticky at this stage; avoid overmixing.
3. Rest dough, then add butter: Cover and let the dough rest for 15 minutes. Then add the melted unsalted butter, knead and press with your hands until the butter is fully incorporated into the dough. The dough will become soft, wet, and oily—this is normal; just knead until uniform.
4. Shape the dough: Cover and let rest at room temperature for another 10 minutes. Lightly wet your hands with water to prevent sticking, fold the edges of the dough toward the center several times, and shape it into a smooth ball.
5. First fermentation: Cover and ferment at room temperature for about 1.5 hours, until the dough has doubled in size. Dust your finger with flour and poke a hole; if the hole does not spring back or collapse, fermentation is complete.
6. Deflate and divide: Remove the dough, gently pat it to release gas, and dust the work surface lightly with flour. Divide the dough into approximately 13 equal portions. Place each portion seam-side down, smooth-side up, and use the edge of your palm and thumb to round and shape into smooth balls.
7. Second fermentation: Arrange the shaped dough balls evenly on a baking sheet lined with a silicone baking mat. Place the sheet in the oven with a pan of hot water on the lower rack. Ferment for 30-45 minutes, until the dough balls have visibly expanded and are nearly touching each other. Lightly press the surface; it should slowly spring back.
8. Bake: Preheat the oven to 190°C. Remove the pan of hot water and the proofed dough. Place the baking sheet on the middle rack and bake for 25 minutes, until the bread surface is an even golden brown.
9. Finish: Immediately transfer the bread to a cooling rack. While still hot, brush the surface with melted butter to create a soft, glossy crust.

## Health Advice
This honey soft bread is rich in carbohydrates and contains a natural sweetness from a small amount of honey, making it suitable as an energy supplement for breakfast or an afternoon snack. Due to the relatively high butter and honey content, it is recommended to control the serving size per consumption, especially suitable for those needing a quick energy boost. Note that the fermentation temperature should not exceed 40°C to avoid deactivating the yeast.
