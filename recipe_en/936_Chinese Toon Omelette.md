# Chinese Toon Omelette
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January-April)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🌿 Chinese toon 150g
🥚 Eggs 6
🧂 Salt approx. 2.5g (for blanching) + approx. 2.5g (for egg mixture seasoning)
🫒 Vegetable oil as needed (for blanching, coating the pan, and frying)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥢 Bamboo strainer
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife
🥄 Wok spatula
🥢 Chopsticks

## Steps
1. Wash the Chinese toon. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add 1 spoonful of salt and an appropriate amount of vegetable oil. Add the Chinese toon and blanch for about 30 seconds, then remove and immediately immerse in cold water to cool.
   **Key Point**: Blanching removes bitterness and preserves the vibrant green color; adding salt and oil helps set the color.
2. Squeeze out excess water from the cooled Chinese toon. Cut off the tougher root ends, and finely chop the remainder as finely as possible.
   **Key Point**: The finer the chop, the more uniform the thickness of the omelette center, resulting in better texture and doneness.
3. Place the chopped Chinese toon in a bowl, add the eggs, and add 1 spoonful of salt. Beat thoroughly with chopsticks until well combined.
   **Key Point**: The egg mixture must be beaten until uniform.
4. Heat the iron wok until scorching hot. Add cold oil to coat the wok, swirl to coat evenly, then pour out the hot oil. Add a small amount of fresh cold oil.
   **Key Point**: This step effectively prevents sticking.
5. Pour about half of the egg mixture into the wok. Cook over medium-low heat, adding a little oil as needed for lubrication. When the bottom begins to set, spread it flat and gather it together.
6. Evenly pour the remaining egg mixture along the edge of the wok. Continue cooking over low heat until the newly added egg mixture gradually sets.
   **Key Point**: Adding the egg mixture in batches helps with shaping and ensures the edges cook through.
7. During cooking, carefully use the wok spatula to scoop out any uncooked egg mixture from the center of the omelette.
   **Key Point**: Avoid a center that is too thick, which affects even cooking and texture.
8. Once the omelette has fully set, carefully flip it. When cooking the other side, drizzle a small amount of oil around the edge in several additions to make the edges crispier. Maintain low heat and continuously rotate the wok to ensure even heating on all sides, until both sides are golden brown and fragrant.
9. Remove from the wok and plate.

## Health Advice
Chinese toon is rich in a unique aroma and vitamins. Blanching reduces nitrite levels. Paired with eggs, it provides high-quality protein, making it suitable for a spring seasonal dish. However, this dish uses a significant amount of oil, so those managing weight or with high blood lipids should consume it in moderation.
