# Homestyle Fresh Pork Wontons
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork (front leg meat, 30% fat and 70% lean)
🥟 Wonton wrappers
🧅 Scallions (for scallion-ginger water, scallion oil, and garnish)
🫚 Ginger (for scallion-ginger water)
🧅 Onion (for frying scallion oil)
🧄 Garlic (for frying scallion oil)
🌿 Small scallion blossoms (bowl-bottom seasoning and garnish)
🫘 Dried seaweed (bowl-bottom seasoning)
🍤 Dried shrimp (bowl-bottom seasoning)
🦐 Dried baby shrimp (finely chopped and added to filling)
🧂 Salt
🌶 White pepper powder
🥢 Light soy sauce
🫒 Sesame oil
🧈 Lard
🫗 Scallion oil (made from frying onion, garlic, and scallions)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥣 Bowls (multiple)
🍳 Wok (for frying scallion oil and cooking wontons)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup ladle

## Preparation Steps
1. Finely chop the pork front leg meat by hand into a mince, then place it in a bowl.
   **Key point**: Hand-chopped filling has a better texture than machine-ground meat.
2. Lightly crush the scallions and ginger, place them in a bowl, add water, and knead to extract the juice. Strain out the scallion-ginger water and set aside.
   **Key point**: Use only the scallion-ginger water, not the solid scallion and ginger pieces, to preserve the meat's freshness.
3. Add salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, sesame oil, and finely chopped dried baby shrimp to the minced meat. Mix well.
4. Add the scallion-ginger water to the meat mixture in three batches, stirring in the same direction each time until fully absorbed. Continue stirring until the filling becomes sticky and firm.
   **Key point**: Use about 150g of scallion-ginger water per 100g of meat filling; stirring by hand gives better results than using chopsticks.
5. Take a wonton wrapper, place an appropriate amount of filling in the center, fold it in half, and pinch the edges tightly to seal, ensuring no filling leaks out.
6. Heat cooking oil in a wok. When it is slightly warm, add the chopped onion, garlic, and scallions. Fry over low heat until the moisture has evaporated and the mixture turns a deep brown color. Strain out the scallion oil and let it cool.
7. In a bowl, add salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, dried shrimp, dried seaweed, small scallion blossoms, one spoonful of lard, and an appropriate amount of scallion oil.
8. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the wontons and cook over high heat for about 2 minutes, until the wontons float to the surface and the wrappers become translucent.
9. First, ladle a spoonful of hot broth into the bowl and stir to combine. Then, add the cooked wontons.
   **Key point**: Adding the broth before the wontons allows the flavors to meld more fully.

## Health Advice
These wontons have a filling with ample moisture from the added water, combined with dried baby shrimp and scallion oil, providing a rich source of protein and flavor, making them suitable for people in need of energy. However, the broth base is relatively high in fat. For those with weak digestion or on a weight loss plan, the amounts of lard and scallion oil can be reduced as needed.
