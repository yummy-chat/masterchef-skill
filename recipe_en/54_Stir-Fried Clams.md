# Stir-Fried Clams
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐚 Clams appropriate amount (pre-soaked to expel sand, cleaned)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (cut into sections, seeds removed, not washed)
🫑 Sichuan peppercorns appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (sliced, slightly larger proportion)
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (julienned)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (crushed and minced, slightly larger proportion)
🧴 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (add enough for base flavor all at once)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections, sprinkle in before serving)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick pan or iron wok (for pre-cooking clams)
🍳 Large iron wok (for stir-frying)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for retrieving clams)
🥢 Chopsticks (assist in retrieving clams)
🥄 Soup spoon (for adding soy sauce)
🔥 Gas stove

## Instructions
1. Rinse the clams that have been soaked to expel sand repeatedly under running water, scrubbing the shells, and drain thoroughly.
2. Pre-cook the clams using one of the following two methods:
   Method 1: Place the cleaned clams in a dry iron wok, cover with lid, and heat over medium-low heat. As the clams gradually open, immediately use chopsticks to pick out the opened ones.
   Method 2: Add enough water to the pot, place the clams in cold water, and heat over medium heat. As soon as the first clam opens, immediately reduce to low heat and quickly scoop out the opened clams with a slotted spoon or chopsticks.
   **Note**: Regardless of the method, promptly remove the opened clams to prevent the meat from becoming tough.
3. Place the retrieved clams in a slotted spoon to drain thoroughly, to avoid affecting the formation of wok hei.
4. Heat the large iron wok until very hot, then pour in an appropriate amount of cooking oil. Once the oil is hot, reduce to low heat. First, add scallions, ginger, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-frying until fragrant. Then add the dried chili pepper sections and stir-fry until the spiciness is released.
   **Note**: If you prefer a charred aroma from the chili peppers, first stir-fry the dried chili pepper sections until they turn brownish-black, then add scallions, ginger, and garlic. Keep the heat at medium throughout the aroma-infusing process to fully release the flavors.
5. After the aromatics are fragrant, increase to high heat, add the drained clams and quickly stir-fry. If the heat is insufficient, do not stir-fry continuously. Stir a few times to coat the clams with the aromatics and prevent burning, then let the wok sit still over high heat for a moment until a slight charred aroma emerges from the wok bottom, creating “wok hei.”
   **Key Operation**: Utilize high heat and stillness to develop wok hei, avoiding frequent stirring that lowers the temperature.
6. Once wok hei appears, pour in enough light soy sauce along the edge of the wok. Do not stir immediately after adding the soy sauce; let it sit over high heat for a few seconds to release the soy sauce’s aroma.
   **Key Operation**: High heat releases the soy sauce’s aroma; let it sit briefly before stirring.
7. Continue stir-frying until the soy sauce evenly coats the clams, cooking until the sauce reduces and thickens.
8. Sprinkle in the cilantro sections, quickly stir-fry until the cilantro is just wilted and well mixed, then serve immediately.

## Health Advice
This stir-fried clam dish is high in protein and low in fat, making it suitable for consumption during weight loss. Light soy sauce has a high sodium content; individuals with high blood pressure should reduce or omit additional salt. Ensure clams have expelled all sand thoroughly for a clean texture.
