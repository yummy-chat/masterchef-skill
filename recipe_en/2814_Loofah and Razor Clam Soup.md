# Loofah and Razor Clam Soup  
Cuisine: Fujian Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🦪 Razor clams, about one box (large ones recommended; splurge on king razor clams if budget allows)  
🥒 Loofah, 1-2 pieces  
🫚 Ginger, one small piece (cut into thin strips, divided into two portions)  
🧂 Salt, 1 spoon (for purging sand) + to taste (for seasoning)  
🍶 Cooking wine (or rice wine), one small cup  
💧 Water, one small cup (to supplement soup; water amount should not exceed loofah if possible)  
🌶️ White pepper powder, one small spoon  
🫒 Sesame oil, to taste  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍲 Cooking pot (for blanching)  
🍳 Flat-bottomed wok  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥒 Vegetable peeler  
🥄 Strainer/slotted rack (to drain clams and collect original broth)  
🥢 Tongs  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Place the razor clams in clean water, add one spoon of salt, and soak for about 30 minutes to allow them to fully purge sand.  
2. Bring water to a boil, add the sand-purged clams, and immediately remove them with tongs once the shells open. Place on a slotted rack to drain, with a container underneath to collect the dripping original broth. Let cool, then remove the meat from the shells and slowly peel off the black thread along the edge of the clam.  
3. Peel the ginger and slice into thin strips; lightly scrape the outermost rough skin off the loofah with a peeler, retaining some green color. Trim both ends, then cut into triangular chunks or half-round slices using a rolling cut.  
4. Heat a wok over medium heat with a little oil. Add half of the ginger strips and stir-fry until the ginger aroma is distinct.  
5. Add the loofah and stir-fry until the surface becomes slightly translucent.  
6. Add the clam meat, all the collected original broth, and one small cup of cooking wine. If the liquid seems insufficient, supplement with one small cup of water. Maintain medium-low heat, letting the soup simmer with small bubbles, and cook for 7 minutes.  
**Key point**: 7 minutes will make the loofah soft on the outside and crisp on the inside, retaining some texture.  

7. Season with salt to taste, then sprinkle in one small spoon of white pepper powder and drizzle a little sesame oil.  
8. Transfer to a bowl and top with the remaining ginger strips.  

## Health Advice  
Loofah and razor clam soup is low in fat and high in protein, making it suitable for moistening dryness in autumn. Razor clams are rich in zinc and iron, while loofah helps clear heat. However, gout patients should limit soup consumption. Ensure the clams are thoroughly purged of sand to avoid a gritty texture.
