# Stuffed Green Peppers with Meat Filling
Cuisine: Hakka Cuisine, Guangdong  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly, appropriate amount (with alternating fat and lean, minced into filling)  
🫒 Water chestnuts, appropriate amount (finely chopped)  
🌶️ Green peppers, 4-5 (choose straight ones)  
🧅 Scallion, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Ginger, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🧄 Garlic, appropriate amount (sliced)  
🌾 Cornstarch, appropriate amount  
🧂 Five-spice powder, appropriate amount  
💧 Scallion-ginger water, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🧂 White pepper powder, appropriate amount  
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 Dark soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🫒 Sesame oil, appropriate amount  
🫘 Fermented black beans, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🍬 White sugar, appropriate amount  
🍶 Vinegar, appropriate amount  
💧 Water starch, appropriate amount  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Large bowl  
🎒 Piping bag (or ziplock bag)  
🍲 Lid  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Prepare the meat filling**: Mince the pork belly. Add the chopped water chestnuts, then season with five-spice powder, salt, white pepper powder, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sesame oil. Gradually add the scallion-ginger water while stirring vigorously in one direction until the filling becomes sticky and firm.  
**Key point**: Stir vigorously until the filling is well combined to ensure it is tender and does not fall apart.  
2. **Prepare the green peppers**: Use a small knife to cut a circle around the stem of each pepper. Remove the stem and core intact, creating a clean cavity. Try to keep the pepper whole.  
3. **Stuff the peppers**: Put the meat filling into a piping bag. Lightly dust the inside of each pepper with cornstarch, shaking to coat evenly. Then pipe the meat filling into the cavity, filling it tightly.  
**Key point**: Dusting the inside with cornstarch prevents the filling from falling out during frying.  
4. **Pan-fry the peppers**: Heat enough oil in a wok to 70% hot. Add the stuffed peppers and fry over high heat for about 15 seconds until the skin wrinkles and turns leopard-spotted. Remove and drain.  
5. **Sauté the aromatics**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the fermented black beans, scallion slices, ginger slices, and garlic slices. Stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.  
6. **Braise for flavor**: Return the fried peppers to the wok. Pour in a sauce made from light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, white sugar, vinegar, salt, and water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low heat and simmer covered for about 10 minutes.  
7. **Thicken the sauce**: Uncover and turn to high heat. Drizzle in water starch to create a light glaze, shaking the wok gently to coat the peppers evenly. Drizzle a little sesame oil for shine before serving.  

## Health Advice  
Green peppers and water chestnuts provide dietary fiber and a refreshing texture. However, the pork belly and frying step make this dish relatively high in calories. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or mixed grain rice for a balanced meal. Suitable for those who enjoy rich flavors; people with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high cholesterol should control their portion size.
