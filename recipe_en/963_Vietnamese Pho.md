# Vietnamese Pho
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦴 Beef bones 500g (for broth)
🐂 Oxtail, as needed
🥩 Beef shank, as needed
🧆 Beef meatballs, 2 pieces
🥩 Raw beef slices (Wagyu or similar), 2 slices
🌿 Lemongrass, 2 stalks (bruised and cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger, 1 small piece (bruised)
🧅 Shallots, 5 (dry-roasted whole)
🍜 Vietnamese rice noodles (pho), as needed
🌱 Bean sprouts, as needed
🌿 Herb garnishes, as needed (Thai basil, mint, scallions, cilantro, bird’s eye chili, onion slices)
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🌰 Black cardamom, as needed
🪵 Cinnamon stick, as needed
🌸 Cloves, as needed
🌿 Coriander seeds, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🍬 Rock sugar (or crystal sugar), 30g
🥫 Fish sauce, 70ml
🍋 Lime, 1/4 piece

## Cookware & Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🍳 Skillet or frying pan
🧤 Muslin bag or spice sachet
🥄 Strainer or skimmer
🥣 Colander
🥄 Ladle
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🔗 Tongs
📦 Plastic wrap
🍱 Airtight container

## Instructions
1. **Blanch the meat**: Place beef bones, oxtail, and beef shank in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then remove and rinse thoroughly with clean water.
2. **Dry-roast the spices**: In a dry skillet (no oil), add the bruised lemongrass sections, bruised ginger, and whole shallots. Dry-roast over medium heat until charred marks appear. Then add star anise, black cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and coriander seeds. Continue dry-roasting until fragrant. Transfer all spices into a muslin bag and tie it closed.
3. **Simmer the broth**: Return the rinsed meat pieces to the pot. Add enough fresh water to cover, then add the spice sachet, salt, and rock sugar. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat and simmer for at least 4 hours. Maintain only tiny bubbles on the surface (do not let it boil vigorously) to keep the broth clear.
4. **Prepare the beef shank**: After about 1 hour of simmering, pierce the beef shank with a fork. If it goes through easily, remove the shank. Immediately wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate until completely cold. Then slice it thinly and set aside.
5. **Finish the broth**: Continue simmering until the oxtail meat easily falls off the bone. Remove the spice sachet and all bones. The broth should now be clear. Add fish sauce and adjust salt to taste.
6. **Cook the noodles and meatballs**: In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the rice noodles and cook until tender, then drain and rinse under cold water (or ice water) to stop cooking. Use the noodle water or some broth to cook the beef meatballs until done.
7. **Assemble and pour broth**: Place the cooked noodles in a bowl. Top with sliced beef shank, beef meatballs, raw beef slices, bean sprouts, and all the herb garnishes. Bring the broth to a rolling boil and pour it directly over the contents in the bowl. The hot broth will cook the raw beef slices instantly.
8. **Season before eating**: Squeeze lime juice over the pho and mix well before enjoying.

## Health Advice
Beef bone broth is rich in collagen and calcium, making it suitable for those needing calcium or recovering energy. Raw beef slices provide iron and protein, but ensure the beef is fresh and hygienic. Bean sprouts and herbs supply vitamin C, which aids iron absorption.
