# Cake Roll
Cuisine: Other
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥚 5 eggs (separate egg whites and yolks)
🌾 90g cake flour
🫒 70g vegetable oil
🥛 60g milk
🍬 55g sugar (for meringue)
🧁 185g heavy cream (35% fat)
🍬 35g sugar (for cream filling)
🍋 5g white vinegar (for whipping egg whites)

## Tools & Equipment
📦 Rectangular baking pan (approximately 11.5×13 inches, modified with aluminum foil)
📜 Parchment paper (baking paper)
🔪 Spatula/offset spatula
🌀 Electric hand mixer
🥄 Whisk
🥣 Mixing bowls
🪜 Cooling rack
🔪 Long knife/bread knife
🥖 Rolling pin
🔥 Oven

## Steps
1. Prepare the baking pan: Use aluminum foil to create a baking area of approximately 11×13 inches. Separate the egg whites and yolks from 5 eggs, placing them in separate clean, dry, grease-free containers. Preheat the oven to 160°C.
2. Make the egg yolk batter: Add the vegetable oil and milk to the egg yolks, and mix with a whisk until well combined. Sift in the cake flour, and mix using a "Z" motion or folding technique until smooth and no dry flour remains.
3. Whip the meringue: Add the white vinegar to the egg whites. Using an electric hand mixer on high speed, whip until coarse bubbles appear. Add the sugar in three batches, continuing to whip until soft peaks form.
4. Combine the batters: Add 1/3 of the meringue to the egg yolk batter, folding until just combined. Pour the mixture back into the remaining meringue, and continue folding until you have a smooth and glossy cake batter.
5. Pour and bake: Pour the cake batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the surface with a spatula. Gently tap the pan on the counter to release large air bubbles. Bake on the middle rack of the oven at 160°C for about 30 minutes, until the surface is golden brown and springs back when pressed.
6. Cool and set: Remove the cake sheet from the pan and place it on a cooling rack to cool for 15–20 minutes until warm. Cover with a new piece of parchment paper, flip it over, and peel off the old parchment paper from the bottom. Trim the slightly dry and firm edges. Using the new parchment paper, gently start rolling the cake sheet from the beginning end, forming a natural curve, then let it cool completely.
7. Make the cream filling: Place the heavy cream and the whisk attachment in the freezer for about 10 minutes. Remove 185g of cold cream and whip on medium speed until lines appear. Add 35g of sugar, and continue whipping until the cream is firm, smooth, and spreadable.
8. Roll and assemble: Unroll the cooled and set cake sheet. Evenly spread the cream over the surface. Using the parchment paper, roll the cake sheet up. Tighten and shape the cake roll using the rolling pin and parchment paper, ensuring it forms a firm, compact cylinder with the seam facing down.
9. Chill and slice: Wrap the cake roll in parchment paper and refrigerate for at least 3–4 hours, until the shape is set and the cream is firm. Remove from the fridge, trim off the uneven ends, and slice into uniform pieces to serve.
   **Note**: The meringue must be whipped to soft peaks; over-whipping will cause the cake to become dry and crack. The cream must be well-chilled before whipping to help maintain its firm shape. Insufficient refrigeration time will affect the neat appearance of the slices.

## Health Advice
For this cake roll, precise control of the meringue to soft peaks is necessary to avoid over-whipping, which may cause the cake to dry out and crack. Suitable for moderate enjoyment during afternoon tea. Be mindful of the high calorie content from the sugar and cream; pairing with black coffee is recommended to balance the flavor.
