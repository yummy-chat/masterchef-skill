# Stir-Fried Noodles in Soup (Vegetarian)

Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍅 1 tomato (score a cross on the top, blanch in boiling water, peel, and finely dice)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (finely chopped, a generous amount)  
🦐 Dried shrimp, as needed (rinsed and set aside)  
🌿 Dried seaweed, about 1 bowl (added at the end for freshness)  
🍜 Hand-pulled noodles, one portion  

🫒 Peanut oil, as needed  
🧂 Light soy sauce, a small amount  
🧂 Salt, two teaspoons  
🍬 Sugar, one teaspoon  
🧂 Chicken essence, as needed  
🌶️ White pepper powder, one teaspoon  
🫒 Sesame oil, as needed  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Wok or iron pot  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Knife and cutting board  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Score a cross on the top of the tomato, blanch in boiling water for about 1 minute, then remove and peel off the skin. Finely dice the tomato.  
**Key point**: The peeled tomato must be finely diced to facilitate mashing into a paste later.  

2. Finely chop the scallions. Rinse and drain the dried shrimp. Tear the dried seaweed into small pieces and set aside.  
3. Heat the wok over high heat, add peanut oil, and stir-fry the scallions until fragrant.  
4. Add the diced tomatoes and stir-fry continuously for at least 2 minutes, until the tomatoes are completely broken down into a thick paste.  
**Key point**: This step is critical for a rich broth—stir-fry until the oil turns red and the tomato flavor is intense.  

5. Add the rinsed dried shrimp to the wok and stir-fry together with the tomato paste until fragrant.  
6. Splash a small amount of light soy sauce along the edge of the wok to release the aroma.  
**Key point**: This is one of the key steps for “stir-frying” (炝锅) to enhance flavor.  

7. Add enough hot water to the wok and bring to a boil over high heat.  
8. Once the water is boiling, season with salt, sugar, and chicken essence, and stir to combine.  
**Key point**: Sugar balances the acidity of the tomatoes, and chicken essence enhances the umami.  

9. Add the hand-pulled noodles and gently separate them with chopsticks. Keep the broth at a rolling boil.  
10. When the noodles soften and become pliable, sprinkle in the white pepper powder.  
**Doneness check**: The noodles should transition from firm to soft and pliable.  

11. Add the dried seaweed, drizzle in the sesame oil, stir to combine, then remove from heat and serve.  
**Key point**: The seaweed and sesame oil are added at the end to enhance freshness and aroma.  

## Health Tips  
This is a vegetarian noodle soup where the umami comes from tomatoes, dried shrimp, and seaweed. Rich in flavor, it is suitable for people who need more iron or calcium. Be sure to stir-fry the tomatoes until the oil turns red before adding water for a richer broth. Since the dried shrimp are naturally salty, reduce the salt to taste.
