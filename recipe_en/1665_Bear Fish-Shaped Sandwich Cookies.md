# Bear Fish-Shaped Sandwich Cookies
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 Unsalted Butter 100g (softened at room temperature, for cookie dough)  
🍬 Powdered Sugar 76g  
🧂 Salt 1g  
🥚 Whole Egg Liquid 30g (must be at room temperature)  
🌾 Cake Flour 168g  
🌰 Almond Flour 40g  
🍫 White Chocolate Chips 100g (filling)  
🧈 Salted Butter 11g (filling, adds savory flavor)  
🍫 Dark Chocolate Approximately 10g (for decoration)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🥣 Glass Bowl  
🥄 Hand Whisk  
🍴 Silicone Spatula  
📄 Parchment Paper  
🥖 Rolling Pin  
⚖️ Kitchen Scale  
🔥 Oven  
🧽 Perforated Silicone Baking Mat  
🌐 Cooling Rack  
🍪 Bear Fish-Shaped Cookie Cutter (stamp and outline)  
🧁 Piping Tip (for cutting round holes)  
🖊️ Fine Decoration Tool (toothpick/fine pen)  
🍶 Piping Bag  
🥣 Small Metal Bowl (for melting dark chocolate)  
🍯 Sieve  

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut the unsalted butter into small pieces and soften at room temperature until it can be easily pressed with a finger but shows no signs of melting. Weigh the cake flour, almond flour, powdered sugar, salt, and whole egg liquid separately, and ensure the egg liquid is at room temperature.
   **Key Point**: The softening degree of butter is the foundation for success. Ensure it is fully softened but not melted into liquid.
2. Place the softened butter in a bowl, add powdered sugar and salt, and stir slowly with a hand whisk until the color lightens slightly and the texture is smooth.
   **Note**: Do not use an electric mixer, and do not over-whip the butter; otherwise, the cookies may expand and deform during baking.
3. Add the room-temperature whole egg liquid in two batches, mixing thoroughly after each addition until fully incorporated to prevent oil-water separation.
   **Note**: The egg liquid must be at room temperature; otherwise, it may cause oil-water separation.
4. Mix the cake flour and almond flour together, then sift them into the bowl. Use a silicone spatula with a cutting and folding motion until no dry flour remains and a smooth dough forms.
   **Note**: Do not over-knead, as this may develop gluten in the dough and affect the crispness of the cookies.
5. Place the dough between two sheets of parchment paper and roll it into a uniform sheet about 0.5 cm thick. Freeze for about 30 minutes until the sheet becomes firm.
6. Remove the hardened dough sheet. First, press patterns with the stamp part of the mold, then cut out cookie shapes with the outline mold. For the other half of the dough, directly cut out solid fish shapes to serve as the bottom. Use a piping tip to press round holes in the center of some cookies to create windows.
   **Key Point**: Dust the dough surface with a little flour to prevent sticking before pressing the mold. Leftover dough can be re-kneaded, chilled, and reused.
7. Place the cookie pieces on a baking sheet lined with a perforated silicone baking mat. Bake in the preheated oven at 150°C (302°F) in the middle rack for about 10-14 minutes until the edges are slightly golden. If baking two trays at once, swap their positions halfway through or remove the top tray early.
   **Key Point**: A perforated silicone baking mat helps the cookie bottoms bake more evenly. The exact baking time depends on cookie size and oven performance.
8. Place the white chocolate chips and salted butter in a bowl, melt over hot water, and stir until smooth to form the filling. Transfer to a piping bag and keep warm until use.
   **Note**: Do not let any water enter the bowl while melting. Use the filling while warm, as it may solidify otherwise.
9. Once the cookies are completely cool, take a solid bottom cookie, pipe a layer of white chocolate filling in the center, leaving a small gap around the edges. Then place the top cookie with the round hole over it, aligning the hole, and gently press.
10. After the white chocolate filling has slightly set, use a toothpick or fine pen dipped in melted dark chocolate to dot the eyes and nose of the bear on the filling within the round hole.
    **Key Point**: Ensure cookies are completely cool before assembling. Pipe the filling and cover quickly for the best adhesion.

## Health Advice  
These cookies replace some flour with almond flour for a crisper texture. The white chocolate filling includes salted butter for a balanced sweet-salty flavor, reducing greasiness. Suitable as an afternoon tea snack, but high in calories; it is recommended to eat 2-3 pieces per serving. During preparation, avoid over-whipping the butter, or the cookies may expand and deform after baking.
