# Traditional Sichuan Boiled Fish (Shui Zhu Yu)
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐟 Fish fillet (appropriate amount, e.g., black fish or grass carp, sliced into thin pieces)  
🥬 Green asparagus lettuce (appropriate amount, as a base vegetable)  
🥬 Celery (appropriate amount, as a base vegetable)  
🥬 Green garlic (appropriate amount, as a base vegetable)  
🍶 Cooking wine (appropriate amount, for marinating and stir-frying the base)  
🌶️ White pepper powder (appropriate amount, for marinating and cooking the broth)  
🥚 Egg white (appropriate amount, for marinating to make the fish tender and smooth)  
🧂 Salt (1g, for marinating)  
🫘 Doubanjiang (broad bean chili paste, appropriate amount, for marinating and stir-frying the base)  
🌾 Cornstarch (appropriate amount, for coating the fish)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers (appropriate amount, for making knife-cut chili)  
🫑 Sichuan peppercorns (appropriate amount, for stir-frying the base and making knife-cut chili)  
🫘 Fermented black beans (appropriate amount, for stir-frying the base)  
🌶️ Chili powder (appropriate amount, for stir-frying the base)  
🧄 Sliced garlic (appropriate amount, for stir-frying the base)  
🧅 Scallions (appropriate amount, cut into sections for stir-frying the base)  
🫚 Ginger (appropriate amount, sliced for stir-frying the base)  
🥄 Soy sauce (appropriate amount, for color and umami)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon (appropriate amount, for seasoning the broth)  
🫒 Vegetable oil (appropriate amount, for the final hot oil pour)  
🫒 Sesame oil (appropriate amount, for the final hot oil pour to add complex aroma)  
🌶️ Ground Sichuan peppercorns (appropriate amount, for the broth to increase numbing flavor)  
⚪ Sesame seeds (appropriate amount, for garnish and aroma)  
🌿 Chopped scallions (appropriate amount, for garnish and aroma)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🔪 Chef’s knife  
🪓 Cutting board  
🥣 Soup bowl  
🥄 Strainer (slotted spoon)  
🥄 Ladle (soup spoon)  
🥣 Small bowl  

## Instructions  
1. Place the fish fillets in a bowl. Add cooking wine, white pepper powder, egg white, 1g salt, doubanjiang, and cornstarch. Mix thoroughly with your hands until well coated and slightly sticky. Set aside to marinate.  
2. Prepare knife-cut chili: Heat a wok over heat without oil. Add dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns. Dry-fry over low heat until the aroma releases and the chili skin turns tiger-skin colored. Remove and chop finely on a cutting board with a knife.  
3. Cut the green asparagus lettuce, celery, and green garlic into bite-sized sections. Lightly stir-fry them, then spread them at the bottom of a soup bowl.  
4. Heat oil in the wok. Add Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, and fermented black beans. Stir-fry until fragrant. Then add doubanjiang and stir-fry until the red oil releases. Finally, add chili powder, sliced garlic, scallion sections, and sliced ginger. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
5. Add cooking wine and soy sauce. Pour in an appropriate amount of water. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
6. Use a strainer to remove all solid residue from the broth, leaving a clear soup base.  
7. Add white pepper powder, ground Sichuan peppercorns, and chicken bouillon to the strained broth. Stir well.  
8. Reduce heat to low. Gently slide the marinated fish fillets one by one into the barely simmering broth. Keep the heat low and cook slowly until the fish fillets change color and are fully cooked. Avoid vigorous boiling to prevent the fish from breaking apart.  
**Note**: Prevent the broth from vigorously boiling to avoid breaking the fish fillets.  
9. Pour the cooked fish fillets and broth into the soup bowl containing the base vegetables.  
10. Evenly sprinkle the knife-cut chili over the fish fillets. In a separate pot, heat vegetable oil and sesame oil until 70% hot. Quickly and evenly pour the hot oil over the chili to release a rich aroma.  
11. Finally, garnish with chopped scallions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately.  

## Health Advice  
This Sichuan boiled fish is high in protein and low in fat, but the amount of oil and salt used is significant. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables and mixed-grain rice to balance nutrition. It is suitable for those who enjoy spicy food and are not prone to getting overheated. People with gout should avoid drinking the broth.
