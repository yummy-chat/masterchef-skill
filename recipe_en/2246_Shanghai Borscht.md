# Shanghai Borscht
Cuisine: Shanghai Local Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧅 Onion, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🥕 Carrot, appropriate amount (diced)  
🥔 Potato, appropriate amount (diced)  
🍅 Tomato, about 1 (diced)  
🥬 Cabbage, appropriate amount (sliced, can be a bit more)  
🧈 Butter, about 10g (for making roux)  
🌾 Flour, about 10g (for making roux)  
🫒 Cooking oil, appropriate amount (for sautéing onion)  
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar, a pinch  
🥫 Tomato paste or ketchup, appropriate amount (for color and flavor)  
🧂 Beef bouillon cube, 1 (optional, to enhance meaty flavor)  
🌶️ Black pepper, appropriate amount (freshly ground before serving)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef’s knife, cutting board  
🍲 Soup pot (e.g., cast iron pot)  
🥘 Small pot  
🥄 Wooden spoon/spatula  
🥣 Ladle  

## Steps  
1.  Chop the onion, dice the carrot and potato, slice the cabbage, and dice the tomato.  
2.  Pour cooking oil into the soup pot, add the chopped onion, and sauté until soft and translucent, releasing fragrance.  
3.  Add the diced carrot, and continue sautéing until the edges are slightly browned.  
**Key Point**: Carotene is fat-soluble; thorough sautéing helps release nutrients.  
4.  Add the diced potato and tomato, and stir-fry evenly.  
5.  Add the tomato paste, and continue stir-frying until all ingredients are evenly coated in the red sauce, with oil and aroma released.  
6.  Add enough water, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
7.  Season with salt and a pinch of sugar, and stir evenly.  
8.  Add one beef bouillon cube, and stir until dissolved.  
9.  Add the sliced cabbage, stir to submerge it in the broth, cover with a lid, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 15 minutes until vegetables are tender and the broth is rich.  
10. Meanwhile, in a separate small pot, melt the butter, add the flour, and stir-fry over low heat for about 2 minutes until the roux turns light yellow and gives off a nutty aroma.  
**Key Point**: Keep the heat low and stir constantly.  
11.  Pour the roux into the soup, stir well, and continue cooking until the broth thickens and becomes smooth.  
12.  Before serving, sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper to enhance the aroma, then enjoy.  
**Key Point**: Adding black pepper at the end yields the best flavor.  

## Health Tips  
This borscht is rich in lycopene and carotene, with tender vegetables that are easy to digest. It’s ideal for warming the body in autumn and winter, suitable for the whole family. Note that the roux is relatively high in calories; you may reduce it during weight loss periods.
