# Japanese-Style Salmon Cream Stew
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐟 Salmon 200g (pre-marinated with salt, sugar, sake, scallion, and ginger)
🧅 Onion 1/4 (cut into chunks)
🍄 Button mushrooms 2-3 (cut into chunks)
🥔 Potato 1/2 (cut into chunks)
🥕 Carrot 1/4 (cut into chunks)
🥦 Broccoli, as needed (cut into small florets)
🍥 Shirataki noodles, as needed
🧂 Salt, as needed
🌿 White pepper, as needed

### Basic Cream White Sauce
🧈 Butter 30g
🌾 Flour 3 tablespoons
🥛 Milk 400ml

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan (for searing salmon and making white sauce)
🍲 Cooking pot (for stewing)
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife
📦 Sealed bag (for portioning and marinating)
🥣 Bowl

## Instructions

### Advance Preparation
- Marinate the salmon: Mix the salmon pieces with salt, sugar, sake, scallion segments, and ginger slices. Rub evenly and marinate for about 20 minutes to remove any fishy smell and infuse flavor. You can marinate a larger batch at once, portion it into sealed bags, and freeze for later use.
- Make the basic white sauce: In a frying pan over low heat, melt the butter. Add the flour and stir-fry until slightly yellow and fragrant. Pour in the cold milk in three additions, stirring each time until smooth and lump-free. Cook until thickened, then strain through a sieve. Once cooled, you can portion and freeze it, then take it out and bring it to room temperature before using.

### Making the Stew
1. Over medium heat, heat a little oil in a frying pan. Cut the marinated salmon into bite-sized pieces and pan-sear until golden brown on the outside and cooked through on the inside. Remove and set aside.
2. Add oil to the cooking pot over medium heat. First, stir-fry the onion chunks until fragrant. Then add the carrot chunks and potato chunks, and stir-fry for about 2 minutes.
3. Add enough water to the pot, and add the shirataki noodles. Cover with a lid and simmer over medium-low heat for about 8–10 minutes, until the potatoes and carrots are tender.
4. Once the vegetables are soft, add the pre-made basic cream white sauce. Gently stir to combine, then add the button mushroom chunks and continue to cook for about 3 minutes.
5. Finally, add the broccoli and the previously seared salmon pieces. Gently fold them in, being careful not to break the fish apart.
6. Season with salt and white pepper. Stir until evenly mixed, then turn off the heat and serve.
   **Note**: Broccoli cooks quickly; adding it last helps maintain its vibrant green color and crisp-tender texture. Adding the salmon at the end keeps it tender and prevents it from becoming tough from overcooking.

## Health Tips
Salmon is rich in omega-3 fatty acids. Paired with vegetables and shirataki noodles, it increases dietary fiber, making this dish suitable for those needing heart health support or weight management. However, the cream white sauce is relatively high in calories, so it's recommended to control the portion size or substitute with low-fat milk.
