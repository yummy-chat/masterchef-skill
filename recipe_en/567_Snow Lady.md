# Snow Lady

Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Four Seasons Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥛 Whole milk 350 ml
🍬 White sugar 80 g (for batter)
🍬 White sugar 50 g (for whipping cream)
🌽 Cornstarch 50 g
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 280 g (for batter)
🧈 Butter 70 g
🥛 Whipping cream 500 ml
🥭 Mango (as needed)
🍓 Strawberries (as needed)
🥝 Kiwi (as needed)
🍪 Oreo cookies (as needed)
🍚 Glutinous rice flour 80 g (toasted for dusting)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl
🥄 Spatula
🔍 Sieve
♨️ Steamer
🧤 Disposable gloves
🍶 Plate (to cover the bowl while steaming)
🔌 Electric hand mixer
🎒 Piping bag
🍳 Non-stick pan
🥖 Rolling pin
🥣 Molds or small bowls (for shaping)
✂️ Scissors
🧻 Plastic wrap
🧁 Paper cupcake liners

## Instructions
1.  Make the glutinous rice batter: Pour 350 ml whole milk, 80 g white sugar, 50 g cornstarch, and 280 g glutinous rice flour into a bowl. Stir with a spatula until no dry flour remains.
2.  Strain the batter through a sieve to remove lumps, resulting in a smooth and fine texture.
3.  Pour the strained batter into a bowl, cover with a plate, and steam over high heat for 30 minutes. If the center still appears white, continue steaming for another 10 minutes until fully cooked.
4.  While the cooked dough is still hot, add 70 g of butter. First, stir with a spatula until the butter melts and incorporates. Once cool enough to handle, put on disposable gloves and knead the dough on a work surface for 5–8 minutes until it becomes smooth and can be stretched into a thin film.
5.  Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
6.  Whip the cream: Place 500 ml of chilled whipping cream and 50 g of white sugar into a pre-chilled bowl. Use an electric hand mixer on high speed to whip for about 3 minutes until stiff peaks form and the cream shows clear, firm lines.
7.  Prepare fillings: Cut mango, strawberries, and kiwi into small pieces. Remove the cream filling from the Oreo cookies and crush the cookies into fine crumbs.
8.  Make dusting flour: Pour 80 g glutinous rice flour into a dry non-stick pan. Stir constantly over medium-low heat until the flour turns slightly yellow and no raw flour smell remains. Remove from heat and let cool.
9.  Divide the dough: Take out the chilled glutinous rice dough. Dust the work surface with some of the toasted flour. Knead briefly, then roll into a long log. Cut into small pieces, each weighing 20–30 g.
10. Roll out the wrappers: Flatten each small piece. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a round wrapper, making the center slightly thicker than the edges.
11. Assemble: Place a wrapper into a mold or small bowl. First, pipe a layer of whipped cream as the base. Add fruit pieces or Oreo crumbs on top, then pipe another layer of cream to cover the filling.
12. Seal: Gather the edges of the wrapper towards the center, like pleating a dumpling or bun, and pinch the seams firmly to seal. Use scissors to trim off any excess dough from the sealed top. Dust the sealed area with some toasted flour to prevent sticking.
13. Unmold the assembled Snow Lady from the mold and place it into a paper liner. If paper liners are unavailable, wrap each one individually in plastic wrap.
14. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set, or enjoy directly after freezing.

## Health Advice
Snow Lady is a high-sugar, high-fat dessert. It is recommended to consume no more than 1–2 pieces per serving, and it can be paired with green tea to cut the richness. Fresh fruits like mango and strawberries provide vitamins, but the cream and glutinous rice skin are high in calories. Those with high blood sugar or on a weight loss plan should control portions. The texture is best after chilling, but avoid eating too much cold food to prevent stomach discomfort.
