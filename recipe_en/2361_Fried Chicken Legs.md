# Fried Chicken Legs  
Cuisine: Common Home-Style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken legs – to taste  
🍗 Chicken mid-joint wings – to taste  
🧅 Scallions – a few stalks (rub before use)  
🫚 Ginger – a small piece (sliced)  
🌾 All-purpose flour – 300g  
🥔 Potato starch – 200g  
🍺 Beer – 100g for marinating + enough for batter  
🧂 Salt – about 3g  
🌶 Ground pepper – 1 tbsp  
🥄 Light soy sauce – about 5g  
🥄 Dark soy sauce – about 5g  
🫒 Cooking oil – enough for frying  
🍅 Ketchup – to taste (for dipping, optional)  
🌶️ Chili powder – to taste (for dipping, optional)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board  
🪡 Meat tenderizer needle (or bamboo skewers / toothpicks)  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Stove  
🥄 Slotted spoon (large holes)  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🥄 Wok spatula  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Use a meat tenderizer needle to poke holes evenly over the surface of the chicken legs and mid-joint wings, allowing blood to seep out and enhancing flavor absorption.  
2. Place the poked chicken legs and wings into a bowl. Add water, scrub, and soak for 10 minutes. Remove and squeeze out moisture thoroughly.  
3. Add ginger slices, rubbed scallion sections, salt, ground pepper, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and 100g beer. Mix well and marinate for 20 minutes.  
4. In a separate bowl, combine all-purpose flour and potato starch. Gradually add beer in small amounts while stirring, until a batter that is slightly thick and flows slowly forms.  
5. Remove the scallions and ginger from the marinade. Pour the chicken into the batter and toss to coat evenly, ensuring each piece is covered.  
6. Pour enough cooking oil into a wok and heat to 170°C (340°F). Reduce to low heat. Add the battered chicken pieces one by one, gently stirring with a slotted spoon to prevent sticking. After all pieces are added, reduce heat to very low and deep-fry for 9 minutes.  
   **Note**: Splash a small amount of cold water into the oil to help lower the temperature, ensuring the inside is cooked through without burning the outside.  
7. After 9 minutes of deep-frying, remove the fully cooked chicken mid-joint wings. Continue frying the chicken legs over very low heat for 5 more minutes, then remove.  
8. Use a fine mesh strainer to remove any batter residue from the oil.  
9. Heat the oil again to 180°C (356°F). Add all the fried chicken pieces back in and fry for about 10 seconds, until the outer shell is golden and crispy. Quickly remove and drain excess oil.  
10. Serve on a plate. Enjoy as is, or sprinkle with chili powder and serve with ketchup for dipping.  

## Health Advice  
Using beer batter for frying gives the chicken legs a crispy exterior and tender interior. However, note that fried food is high in calories; pairing it with a vegetable salad can help balance nutrition. Best reserved for occasional indulgence; those with high blood lipids should control their intake.
