# Beef Pulled Noodles
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🦴 Beef leg bone 1000g (cut open, soaked in water for 1 hour in advance)
🥩 Beef 500g (beef leg or shank, soaked in water for 1 hour in advance)
🥓 Beef fat 30g
🌿 Cilantro 2 stalks (about 30g, keep roots, wash and pat dry)
🧅 Onion 35g (shredded)
🧄 Garlic 35g (sliced or smashed)
🥔 Ginger 35g (thinly sliced)
🥒 White radish, appropriate amount (thinly sliced, halved)
🍜 Fresh alkaline noodles, appropriate amount

🌶️ Chili flakes 100g
🫘 White sesame seeds 10g
🧂 Salt 4g (for chili oil) + 10g (for broth base, adjustable)
🫒 Cooking oil 500g (for frying spice oil to pour over chili)
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns 3g
🌱 Cumin seeds 3g
🍂 Black cardamom 2g (1 piece, cut open)
⭐ Star anise 1g (1 piece, broken into segments)
🫘 Nutmeg 0.3g
🫚 Sand ginger 1g (2 slices)
🌶️ Long pepper 0.5g
🧂 Dried ginger 1.5g (for spice bag) + 3g (added later)
🫙 Black pepper 2g
🍥 Beef powder (Daebak) 12g

🧂 MSG 1g (per bowl)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2g (per bowl)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔥 Wok
🥣 Large stockpot
🔨 Rolling pin
🥄 Slotted spoon
🪢 Gauze bag
📦 Plastic wrap
🕸️ Strainer
🍲 Noodle pot

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare chili oil**: Mix chili flakes, white sesame seeds, and 4g salt in a heatproof bowl. Pour 500g cooking oil into a wok, add the washed and dried whole cilantro, ginger slices, onion shreds, garlic slices, black cardamom, star anise, and Sichuan peppercorns. Heat over high heat until bubbling, then turn to the lowest heat and fry slowly. When the vegetables and spices are shriveled and golden, remove them all. Continue heating the oil to about 200°C, then pour it in batches into the chili flake mixture, stirring quickly while pouring. Immediately cover and let cool. It is recommended to prepare this a day in advance for better flavor.
   **Note**: All vegetables must be thoroughly dried to prevent hot oil splatter; the spice oil must be fried slowly over low heat to fully release the aroma.

2. **Prepare broth spice bag**: Mix Sichuan peppercorns, cumin seeds, cut-open black cardamom, broken star anise, nutmeg, sand ginger, long pepper, 1.5g dried ginger, and black pepper. Use a rolling pin to crush into slightly coarse granules, place into a gauze bag, and tie tightly.

3. **Cook beef broth base**: Add 8 jin (about 4 kg) of water to a large stockpot. Add the soaked beef leg bones, beef, and beef fat. Heat over high heat with the lid off. Before the soup comes to a full boil, repeatedly skim off all the foam with a slotted spoon. After the foam is completely removed, reduce to the lowest heat, keeping the soup surface at a gentle simmer, and cook for 90 minutes.
   **Judgment standard**: The beef should be easily pierced with chopsticks, with slight resistance when pulling out.

4. **Season and continue cooking**: After 90 minutes of cooking, remove the beef and immediately wrap tightly in plastic wrap to prevent oxidation and darkening. Leave the bones in the pot. Add the spice bag, 3g dried ginger, beef powder, and 10g salt to the broth. Cover the pot and continue cooking over the lowest heat for 30 minutes.
   **Key point**: The spice bag should not cook for more than 30 minutes, or it will become bitter; the broth saltiness can be slightly mild at this point, as it can be adjusted later to taste.

5. **Prepare broth base and side dishes**: Remove and discard the spice bag. Strain the broth through a strainer and set aside. The white radish slices can be blanched in boiling water, or cooked in the beef broth and then removed. Place 1g MSG and 2g chicken bouillon per serving in the noodle bowls.

6. **Cook noodles and assemble**: Bring a separate pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles. After the noodles are cooked, remove and place into the prepared bowls. Bring an appropriate amount of beef broth to a boil again, add the white radish slices and blanch briefly, then pour the broth and radish over the noodles. Slice the cooked beef and arrange on top, sprinkle with chopped cilantro, and finally drizzle with the prepared chili oil.
   **Note**: Noodles must be cooked in plain water, not in the beef broth; bring the broth to a rolling boil before pouring over the noodles for better texture.

## Health Advice
Beef broth is rich in collagen and calcium, suitable for replenishing energy after physical exertion, but the broth has a high fat content. It is recommended to limit broth intake to control calories. Chili oil can stimulate appetite, but those with sensitive stomachs should consume it in moderation.
