# Sweet and Sour & Braised Two-Flavor Fried Eggs
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Fried sunny-side-up eggs, as needed

**Base Sauce** (shared by both flavors)
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🦪 Oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon
🌽 Cornstarch, half a tablespoon

**Additions for Sweet and Sour Flavor**
🍬 White sugar, 2 tablespoons
🍶 Vinegar, 2 tablespoons
💧 Water, half a bowl

**Additions for Braised Flavor**
🧂 Salt, a pinch
💧 Water, half a bowl

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Non-stick frying pan, 2
🥄 Soup spoon
🥣 Bowl, 2

## Steps
1. **Prepare the two sauces**: Prepare the sweet and sour flavor and the braised flavor sauces separately. Take two portions of the base sauce and place them in separate bowls. For the sweet and sour flavor: add 2 tablespoons of white sugar, 2 tablespoons of vinegar, and half a bowl of water to the base sauce, and stir well. For the braised flavor: add a pinch of salt and half a bowl of water to the base sauce, and stir well.
2. **Prepare the fried eggs**: Set aside the pre-fried sunny-side-up eggs.
3. **Heat both pans at the same time**: Place two non-stick frying pans over heat and warm them up. Pour the prepared sweet and sour sauce and braised sauce into the respective pans.
4. **Bring to a boil over high heat**: Keep the heat high and bring the sauces to a full boil.
5. **Add the fried eggs**: Place the sunny-side-up eggs into the boiling sauces separately. Keep the heat high and let them simmer to absorb the flavors.
6. **Reduce the sauce**: Continue cooking over high heat until the sauce becomes noticeably thicker, with rich bubbles and a fragrant aroma.
7. **Plate and serve**: Turn off the heat. Remove the two-flavor fried eggs from the pans, plate them, and enjoy.

## Health Advice
This is a high-protein breakfast dish. However, the sweet and sour version has a high sugar content, so it is recommended to pair it with vegetables and whole wheat bread to balance blood sugar. The braised version is moderately salty and savory; those with high blood pressure should minimize consumption of the sauce.
