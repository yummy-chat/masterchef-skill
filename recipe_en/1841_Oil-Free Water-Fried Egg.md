# Oil-Free Water-Fried Egg
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥚 Eggs 2  
🧂 Black Pepper Sea Salt to taste  
🥄 Sauce to taste (specific ingredients not mentioned)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan  
🥛 Small milk pot (for adding water)  
🪟 Glass lid  
🪵 Wooden spatula  
🌶️ Grinder bottle (black pepper sea salt)  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps
1. Heat a non-stick frying pan over low heat, crack in two eggs, and keep the heat low throughout.  
   **Key point**: Do not use high heat, otherwise the egg white will develop large air bubbles and become tough.  
2. Once the egg white slightly sets and turns white, use a small milk pot to pour a small amount of water along the edge of the pan.  
   **Key point**: Adding water creates steam, which will steam the upper part of the egg until cooked.  
3. Immediately cover with the lid and steam for 20-30 seconds.  
   **Note**: The time can be adjusted according to preference. Steaming a little longer will make the surface of the fried egg smoother and flatter.  
4. After turning off the heat, gently press the yolk area with a wooden spatula to check doneness.  
   **Judgment standard**: If the yolk feels slightly soft and runny, it is soft-boiled; if there is no runniness, it is fully cooked.  
5. Transfer the fried egg to a plate, sprinkle with black pepper sea salt to taste, and drizzle with an appropriate amount of sauce.  

## Health Tips  
This oil-free water-fried egg uses steam instead of hot oil, preserving the tender texture of the egg while reducing fat intake, making it suitable for those controlling oil and losing weight. Note to keep the heat low throughout to prevent the egg white from bubbling and becoming tough; soft-boiled or fully cooked can be controlled according to personal preference.
