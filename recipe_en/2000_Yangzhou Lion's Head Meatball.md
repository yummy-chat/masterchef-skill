# Yangzhou Lion's Head Meatball
Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐖 Pork butt (fat and lean separated, final fat-to-lean ratio 3:7)  
🥕 Water chestnuts, to taste (finely diced)  
🦀 Live river crabs, to taste (for crab roe and meat)  
🥬 Chinese cabbage leaves, to taste (washed)  
🫚 Old ginger, to taste (for steaming crabs and making ginger-scallion water)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (for steaming crabs and making ginger-scallion water)  
🍶 Huadiao wine, to taste (for steaming crabs)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🥚 Egg whites, to taste  
🍤 Shrimp roe, to taste  
🧊 Ice water, to taste (for soaking scallions and ginger)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board, chef's knife  
🥟 Steamer basket  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥣 Large bowl  

## Cooking Steps
1. Separate the fat and lean parts of the pork butt, then cut each into small pieces. Using a method of coarse chopping and fine mincing, maintain a fat-to-lean ratio of 3:7.
2. Peel the water chestnuts and dice them into small pieces. Truss the live river crabs, place them in a steamer basket lined with ginger slices and scallion segments, drizzle with Huadiao wine, and steam over cold water. Once steam rises, cook for 15 minutes. Remove and extract the crab roe and crab meat for later use.
3. Soak scallion segments and ginger slices in ice water to make ginger-scallion ice water.
4. Place the lean meat pieces in a large bowl. Add the ginger-scallion ice water in batches, stirring continuously in one direction by hand, until the meat mixture becomes firm, sticky, and springy.
- **Key**: Stir by hand to gauge the texture, adding water in batches to ensure the meat fully absorbs it.

5. Add the fat pieces, crab meat, water chestnut pieces, shrimp roe, salt, and egg whites, and mix well.
6. Take a small spoonful of the meat mixture and place it in a bowl of plain water. If it floats on the surface, the stirring is successful.
7. Wet both hands with water, take an appropriate amount of the meat mixture, and toss it back and forth between your palms to shape into a smooth, large meatball.
8. Add cold water to a clay pot, gently place the meatballs in, then gradually heat the pot over fire. Skim off any foam. Reduce the heat to low, cover with the washed Chinese cabbage leaves, put the lid on, and simmer slowly for 240 minutes.
- **Key**: Start with cold water and slow simmering over low heat; the cabbage leaves prevent the surface from drying out and add aroma.

## Health Advice
This Lion's Head Meatball uses a 3:7 fat-to-lean ratio, is rich in high-quality protein and crab roe and meat, and is suitable for autumn nourishment. However, pay attention to total calorie intake. It is recommended to serve with light vegetables. Individuals with high blood pressure may reduce the amount of salt as needed.
