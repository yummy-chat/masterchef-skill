# Crispy Cucumber Strips
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥒 Cucumber About 3 jin (core removed, cut into strips)
🫚 Ginger To taste (sliced)
🧄 Garlic To taste (sliced)
🌶️ Small Chili Peppers To taste (sliced into rings)
🧂 Salt About 1 tael (for draining water from 3 jin cucumbers)
🌿 Star Anise 4 pieces
🌸 Sichuan Peppercorns 5 grams
🪵 Cinnamon Stick 10 grams
🍃 Bay Leaves 5 pieces
🌶️ Dried Chili Peppers 5 pieces
🥣 Soy Sauce 1 jin
💧 Water 1 jin
🍬 Rock Sugar Half a jin
🍶 Rice Wine (or Baijiu) One bottle cap

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's Knife
🪓 Cutting Board
🥘 Wok
🥄 Spatula
🥢 Skimmer
🍽️ Stainless Steel Tray (for marinating)
🥣 Glass Bowl (for rinsing)
🫙 Glass Jar (oil-free and water-free, airtight)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Wash the cucumbers, trim off both ends, split them lengthwise into quarters, and use a knife to remove the cores.
   **Key Point**: Removing the cores prevents the finished product from releasing water, maintaining a crisp texture.
2.  Place the cucumber strips in a basin, sprinkle salt according to the ratio of 1 tael of salt per 3 jin of cucumber, mix well, and let sit for about 2 hours until the cucumber strips can be bent without breaking and have a crisp-tender texture.
   **Note**: The amount of salt can be appropriately increased to fully draw out the water.
3.  Rinse the marinated cucumber strips once with clean water to remove excess surface salt, then remove and thoroughly drain and air-dry until there is no raw water on the surface.
   **Note**: If raw water is not dry, it can easily lead to fermentation and spoilage.
4.  Take an oil-free and water-free glass jar, scald it with boiling water, then wipe it dry for later use.
5.  Place a wok over heat without adding oil. Add the star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry over low heat until the spices release their aroma, the dried chili peppers deepen in color, and they show slight signs of browning.
   **Note**: Keep the heat on low throughout to prevent burning.
6.  Pour in the soy sauce and water, add the rock sugar, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 3-4 minutes until the rock sugar is completely dissolved.
7.  Turn off the heat, immediately add one bottle cap of rice wine, stir well, and let it cool completely until cold.
   **Key Point**: The rice wine is added after turning off the heat for sterilization purposes. The marinade must be completely cooled before use.
8.  Place the air-dried cucumber strips, ginger slices, garlic slices, and small chili pepper rings into the glass jar. Pour in the cooled marinade, seal the jar tightly, and store in a cool, dark place to marinate overnight before serving.
   **Note**: When removing portions, use clean, oil-free and water-free utensils to prevent contamination.

## Health Advice
Rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, suitable for stimulating the appetite and relieving summer heat. However, the soy sauce and rock sugar content is relatively high, so individuals with high blood pressure or those controlling blood sugar should consume in moderation. Ensure no raw water is present during the pickling process to avoid spoilage. It is recommended to pair with a light staple food.
