# Tender Beef Slices
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef (top round/knuckle/tenderloin) 1000g (trimmed of sinew)
🫚 Ginger 40g (for ginger-scallion water)
🧅 Scallion 40g (for ginger-scallion water)
💧 Water 600g (for ginger-scallion water; yields about 500g juice after straining)
🌿 Cilantro 2 sprigs (for dressing)
🧄 Garlic 4 cloves (for dressing)
🧂 Salt 14g
🧂 Baking soda 10g
🫗 Ginger-scallion water 500g (added in batches)
🌿 Cumin seeds (or ground) 20g (2 tablespoons, optional)
🌶️ Fine chili powder 10g (1 tablespoon, optional)
🌶️ Coarse chili flakes 10g (1 tablespoon, optional)
🌶️ Sichuan peppercorn powder to taste (optional)
🍬 Sugar about 3g (1 teaspoon)
🫗 Light soy sauce 10g (1 tablespoon)
🫒 Sesame oil 5g (1/2 tablespoon)
🧂 MSG 3g (optional)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef’s knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Large bowl/basin
🫗 Strainer
⚖️ Kitchen scale
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍲 Soup pot
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer

## Instructions
1. Make ginger-scallion water: Smash ginger and cut into small pieces, cut scallion into small sections. Place in a bowl with 600g water. Squeeze and rub with your hand for about 1 minute. Strain through a filter to remove solids, yielding about 500g ginger-scallion juice. Set aside.
2. Prepare beef: Choose lean, sinew-free beef. Carefully trim any visible sinew from the surface. Slice against the grain into 2–3 mm thin pieces.
*Note*: Do not wash the beef after purchase, as this affects its ability to absorb liquid later. If washing is necessary, rinse quickly and pat thoroughly dry with paper towels immediately.
3. Marinate and hydrate: Place beef slices in a bowl. Add salt and baking soda, then mix well. Gradually add the ginger-scallion water in 5–7 batches, adding only a small amount each time. Quickly stir in one consistent direction until the water is fully absorbed and the slices become sticky again before adding the next batch. Toward the end, you can pick up the meat and slap it against the bowl several times, then continue stirring until the surface of the slices develops a glue-like coating. The meat should appear plump, sticky, and gelatinous, with visible mucus on the bowl walls and no free liquid pooling.
*Success criteria*: Slices are plump and cling together; when lifted, they appear glossy and feel slippery and tender.
4. Flavor seasoning: After hydration, for cumin flavor, mix in cumin seeds and toss well. For spicy or numbing-spicy flavor, add fine chili powder, coarse chili flakes, and Sichuan peppercorn powder on top of the cumin base, and mix again. The beef will become slightly drier.
5. Cooking: Bring water to a rolling boil in a pot. Add the beef slices. Cook until the water returns to a boil and the slices have completely changed color. Remove immediately; do not overcook to maintain tenderness.
6. Dress: Place cooked beef in a bowl. Add cilantro segments and minced garlic. Add sugar, light soy sauce, sesame oil, and MSG. Toss well to combine.

## Health Advice
The key to tender beef slices lies in baking soda and gradual water hydration. This dish is suitable for fitness enthusiasts as a high-protein, low-fat meal, but those with kidney conditions should control their intake.
