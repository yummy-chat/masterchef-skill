# Fennel Seed Small Fried Flatbread
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 1000g (divided into two halves for use)  
💧 Boiling water as needed (for scalding dough)  
💧 Warm water 320g (for yeasted dough)  
🧂 Salt 10g  
🍞 Dry yeast 10g  
🌿 Fennel seeds approx. 30g (must be toasted first)  
🫒 Cooked soybean oil 40g  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🍳 Wok (iron wok, for toasting fennel seeds)  
🥖 Rolling pin  
🍲 Large pot/fryer  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Slotted spoon/spider strainer  
📦 Plastic wrap  

## Instructions  
**Preparation Stage**  
1. Divide 1000g of flour into two equal portions. For one half, gradually add boiling water while stirring to scald the dough completely.  
**Note**: Use freshly boiled water to ensure the flour is fully gelatinized.  
2. For the other half: Add 10g salt and 10g yeast to 320g warm water, stirring until fully dissolved. Gradually add the yeast water to the flour, stirring as you go, and knead into a soft yeasted dough.  
3. Place fennel seeds in a dry wok and toast over low heat until fragrant. Remove and set aside.  
**Key point**: Keep the heat low throughout to avoid burning; remove as soon as the aroma is strong.  
4. Sprinkle the toasted fennel seeds over both the scalded dough and the yeasted dough, then add 40g cooked soybean oil. Fully combine and repeatedly knead the two doughs together until completely uniform.  
**Key technique**: Adding cooked soybean oil at the end ensures the flatbread is crispy on the outside without becoming greasy.  
5. Roll the combined dough into a long log and cut into small pieces of about 30g each. Roll each piece into a ball.  
6. Arrange all dough balls and cover with plastic wrap. Let rest for 3-5 minutes.  
**Purpose**: This allows the gluten to relax, preventing shrinkage during rolling, and is not intended for the dough to rise.  

**Frying Stage**  
1. Using a rolling pin, roll each rested dough ball into a round, evenly thick flatbread. Then poke a hole in the center with your finger.  
2. When the oil reaches 60% heat (around 170-180°C), carefully lower the flatbread into the oil. Fry over medium-low heat.  
**How to check**: The flatbread will sink first, then float to the surface. Once floating, use chopsticks to flip it quickly so both sides cook evenly.  
3. Keep flipping until the flatbread turns a uniform golden brown and puffs up evenly throughout. Remove and drain.  
**Key point**: Do not let the oil get too hot; flip frequently to ensure even color and thorough cooking. The finished product will stay crisp and not harden or weep oil as it cools.  

## Health Advice  
This fennel seed flatbread combines a unique texture—crispy outside and soft inside—thanks to its half-scalded, half-yeasted dough. However, since it is deep-fried and calorie-dense, it is best enjoyed occasionally as a breakfast or afternoon snack, paired with light tea to cut the grease. Limit yourself to 2-3 pieces per serving to avoid excessive oil intake.
