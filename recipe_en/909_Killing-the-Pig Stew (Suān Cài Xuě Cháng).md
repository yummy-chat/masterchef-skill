# Killing-the-Pig Stew (Suān Cài Xuě Cháng)
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥬 Ingredients
- Blood sausage
- Pickled Chinese cabbage (suān cài)
- Pork ribs
- Goji berries

🧂 Seasonings
- Cooking wine
- Garlic cloves
- Ginger powder
- Dried chilies
- Bay leaves
- Star anise
- Scallion knot
- Sesame oil
- Rapeseed oil
- Salt
- Dipping sauce: chili oil, light soy sauce, minced garlic, sesame oil

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Cookware
- Toothpicks
- Wok
- Electric pressure cooker

## Steps
1. Use a toothpick to prick small holes densely over the surface of the blood sausage. Place it in warm water at about 80°C (176°F) and simmer on low heat for 10 minutes, keeping the water just below boiling to prevent the sausage from bursting.
**Key point**: Pricking holes releases internal gases; cooking at a low temperature prevents the sausage from expanding and splitting.

2. Remove the cooked blood sausage and cut it into pieces. Set aside.

3. Place the pork ribs in a pot of cold water, add cooking wine, and blanch. Skim off any foam, then drain and set aside.
**Key point**: Blanching removes blood residue and reduces any gamey smell.

4. Heat rapeseed oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add garlic cloves, dried chilies, and ginger powder, and stir-fry until fragrant.
5. Add the pork ribs and stir-fry until the surfaces turn golden brown.
6. Rinse the pickled cabbage, squeeze out excess water, and add it to the wok. Drizzle with sesame oil and stir-fry over high heat for 2 minutes.
7. Pour in 600 ml of hot water and bring to a boil.
8. Transfer all ingredients from the wok into an electric pressure cooker. Add bay leaves, star anise, scallion knot, and salt.
9. Select the "rich flavor" mode and pressure cook for 40 minutes.
10. After the pressure releases naturally, open the lid. Add the blood sausage pieces and goji berries, cover, and let sit for 2 minutes.
11. Ladle the stew into a large bowl. To prepare the dipping sauce, mix chili oil, light soy sauce, minced garlic, and sesame oil according to your taste. Serve the dipping sauce alongside the stew.

## Health Tips
Blood sausage is rich in iron and protein, making it ideal for warming the body and replenishing blood in winter. However, it is high in cholesterol, so those with elevated cholesterol levels should consume it in moderation. The pickled cabbage is fermented and rich in probiotics, which aid digestion, but it is also high in sodium, so be mindful of salt intake.
