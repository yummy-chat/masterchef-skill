# Mung Bean Cake
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌱 Dried mung beans 500g (need to soak for 36 hours in advance, refrigerate if soaking in summer)
🍵 Matcha powder 5g (for 1/3 of the mung bean paste)
🍫 Cocoa powder 5g (for half of the remaining mung bean paste)
🍬 White sugar 100g
🫒 Corn oil or butter 140g

## Kitchen Utensils and Equipment
🫕 Steamer
🩹 Cheesecloth
🪥 Strainer
🍳 Non-stick pan
🪵 Wooden spatula
🍡 Molds (with patterns)
🥣 Bowl

## Instructions
1. Rinse the dried mung beans and add enough water to soak for 36 hours; refrigerate in summer. Soak until the beans are fully expanded and the skins can be easily rubbed off. If there is a hard center, extend the soaking time.
2. Rub the soaked beans repeatedly with both hands to loosen the skins, then pour off the floating skins; rinse with water several times, rubbing off any remaining skins to ensure they are completely removed.
3. Line a steamer with cheesecloth, spread the skinned beans evenly, steam over high heat until boiling, then reduce to medium heat and steam for 1 hour, until the beans can be easily mashed into a paste when pressed between fingers.
4. Place the steamed beans in a strainer in batches, press through with a spoon, and repeat until all beans are processed into a fine mung bean paste.
5. Heat 140g of oil in a non-stick pan, add the mung bean paste, and stir-fry over low heat for about 5 minutes until the oil is fully absorbed. Add 100g of white sugar and continue stir-frying for about 5 minutes until the sugar is dissolved and the paste forms a ball that does not stick to the spatula. If the paste is too wet, extend the stir-frying time.
6. Divide the stir-fried mung bean paste into three portions: take 1/3 and knead in 5g of matcha powder; take half of the remaining portion and knead in 5g of cocoa powder; leave the other half plain. Knead each until the color is even and the texture is firm.
7. Divide each portion of mung bean paste into 30g balls, repeatedly squeeze and roll into rounds, then press evenly into molds to form patterns. If no molds are available, simply flatten them. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
Mung beans require long soaking and peeling before steaming. Combined with butter for stir-frying, they make a delicate summer dessert to clear heat and relieve heatstroke. Suitable for occasional consumption by those with high blood sugar, but pay attention to total oil and sugar intake to avoid exceeding calorie limits.
