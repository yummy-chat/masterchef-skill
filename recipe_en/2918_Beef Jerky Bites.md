# Beef Jerky Bites
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Beef 1000g  
🧂 Salt 6g  
🍬 White Sugar 80g  
🌶️ Sichuan Pepper Powder 3g  
🌶️ Chili Powder 8g  
🧄 Ginger Powder 3g  
🥄 Light Soy Sauce 80g  
🥄 Dark Soy Sauce 10g  
🦪 Oyster Sauce 20g  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Kitchen Knife  
🪓 Cutting Board  
🥣 Large Glass Bowl  
🫓 Baking Tray  
📜 Baking Parchment Paper  
Plastic Wrap  
🥢 Rolling Pin  
♨️ Oven  
Baking Rack  
🥣 Bowl  
🍬 Aluminum Foil  

## Instructions
1. Trim the beef of any sinew, cut into small pieces, then chop into coarse crumbles, keeping some texture—do not mince too fine.
2. Place the beef crumbles in a large bowl, add all seasonings, and wearing gloves, stir continuously in one direction for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes sticky and forms a cohesive mass.
3. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate to marinate for 2 hours.
4. Line a baking tray with parchment paper, place the beef mixture on top, cover with plastic wrap, and use your hands and a rolling pin to press it into a firm, even sheet about 0.5 cm thick, ensuring there are no cracks or gaps.
5. Remove the plastic wrap, place the tray in the middle of a preheated oven, and bake at 120°C for 1 hour, until the beef is set and firm and obvious moisture has separated on the bottom.
6. Remove the tray, pour off the liquid, place fresh parchment paper on top of the beef sheet, then invert a baking rack over it, flipping the sheet onto the rack.
7. Place the rack in the middle of the oven and slow-bake at 65°C, flipping the sheet every hour for about 6 flips total, until the beef sheet is dry, firm, and chewy.
8. Remove the beef sheet, place it on a cutting board, and cut into 1 cm cubes.
9. Wrap each beef cube individually in aluminum foil, sealing tightly for easy eating and storage.

## Health Advice  
Beef jerky bites are high in protein and low in fat, making them suitable as a snack during fitness or calorie control, but the sugar and soy sauce contain high sodium, so consumption quantity should be monitored. Homemade versions are healthier, as they avoid the additives found in commercial beef jerky.
