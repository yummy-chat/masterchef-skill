# Yogurt Coconut Mousse
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍍 Fruit (pineapple) Approximately 160g (pureed, can substitute with seasonal fruit)
🥛 Thick Yogurt 400g (approximately 2 boxes)
🥛 Heavy Cream 280g (for whipping)
🥥 Shredded Coconut 50g
🍫 White Chocolate 150g (for glaze)
🧊 Gelatin Sheets 25g (5g for filling, 10g for mousse, 10g for glaze, soak separately in ice water until soft)
🍬 White Sugar 240g (20g for filling, 70g for mousse, 150g for glaze)
🥃 Coconut Liqueur 25g
🍯 Sweetened Condensed Milk 100g (for glaze)
🍯 Glucose Syrup 150g (for glaze)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Metal Pot (for cooking fruit puree, yogurt mixture, syrup)
🥣 Bowls/Containers (for soaking gelatin, holding ingredients, whipping cream over ice water)
🌀 Handheld Electric Mixer (for whipping heavy cream)
❄️ Refrigerator/Freezer (for chilling and freezing to set)
🔥 Microwave or Hot Water Bath (for melting chocolate and reheating glaze)
🛡️ Plastic Wrap (for lining molds, covering glaze)
🥄 Strainer/Sieve (for straining glaze)
🍮 Molds (bowls/containers for setting the mousse)
🔗 Cooling Rack and Drip Tray (for glazing operation)

## Preparation Steps
1.  Place the 5g gelatin for the filling, 10g for the mousse, and 10g for the glaze into separate bowls of ice water. Soak until softened.
2.  Blend the fruit into a puree and place it in a pot with 20g sugar. Bring to a boil to deactivate the bromelain enzyme, otherwise it will affect setting. Remove from heat and cool to 50-60°C. Add the softened 5g gelatin for the filling and stir to combine. Pour into a small bowl lined with plastic wrap. Freeze until completely solid.
3.  In a pot, pour in approximately 100g of the yogurt and 70g sugar. Heat over low heat, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved (no need to boil). Remove from heat. Add the softened 10g gelatin for the mousse and stir until dissolved. Pour in the remaining approximately 300g yogurt and mix to form a yogurt mixture. In a separate bowl, pour 280g heavy cream and whip over an ice bath using an electric mixer until it reaches a thick yogurt-like consistency - when you lift the beater, the cream should flow slowly and leave a trail. Gently fold the whipped cream into the yogurt mixture until just combined. Add 50g shredded coconut and 25g coconut liqueur and mix well. This is the yogurt coconut mousse mixture.
4.  Pour half of the mousse mixture into a mold lined with plastic wrap. Freeze for about 8 minutes until the surface is set and not sticky. Unmold the frozen solid fruit filling and invert it into the center of the semi-set mousse. Pour the remaining mousse mixture to completely cover the filling and fill the mold. Freeze for at least 4 hours or until completely frozen solid, which makes unmolding and glazing easier.
5.  In a pot, combine 75g water, 150g sugar, 150g glucose syrup, and 100g sweetened condensed milk. Heat to a boil until the sugar is completely dissolved. Pour the boiling sugar mixture into a bowl containing 150g white chocolate. Add the softened 10g gelatin for the glaze and stir until completely melted and smooth. Use an immersion blender or strain through a sieve to make the glaze shiny and smooth. Cover the surface directly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 12 hours until it reaches a paste-like consistency.
6.  Remove the refrigerated glaze and take the needed amount. Melt it in a hot water bath or microwave until it reaches approximately 32°C. Color can be added as desired. Pour different colored glazes into the same measuring cup. Unmold the frozen solid mousse cake and place it on a cooling rack. Pour the glaze evenly over the entire surface. Use a spatula to scrape off excess glaze from the bottom. Transfer to a cake board, refrigerate until serving.

## Health Advice
This mousse is high in sugar and fat, suitable for an occasional treat. Those with diabetes or monitoring their sugar intake should only have a small taste.
