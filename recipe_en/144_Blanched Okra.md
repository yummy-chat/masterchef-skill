# Blanched Okra
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌿 Okra, about 8-10 pieces  
🧄 Minced garlic, as needed  
🧅 White part of scallion, shredded, as needed  
🌶️ Red bell pepper, shredded, as needed  
🧂 Steamed fish soy sauce, two tablespoons  
🍶 Aged vinegar, one tablespoon  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (a little for blanching, an appropriate amount for drizzling to release aroma)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥄 Spoon  
🍳 Wok  
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Steps
1. Use a knife to cut off the stem end of the okra, without cutting through, to maintain its overall shape.
- Key point: Only remove the inedible stem, keep the okra body intact.
2. Place the minced garlic, shredded scallion whites, and shredded red bell pepper into a bowl.
3. Pour oil heated to about 60% hot over the aromatics to release their fragrance, then add two tablespoons of steamed fish soy sauce and one tablespoon of aged vinegar, and stir well to make a sauce.
- Key point: The hot oil temperature must be high enough to fully release the aroma of the garlic and scallions.
4. Add water to the wok, put in a little oil and a pinch of salt, and bring to a rolling boil.
- Key point: Adding oil and salt to the water helps the okra maintain a brighter green color and gives it a light base flavor.
5. Once the water is fully boiling, add the okra, maintain high heat, and cook until the water boils again and the okra turns a bright, vibrant green, then remove immediately.
- Key point: Add the okra after the water is boiling to shorten the blanching time and preserve the texture; remove promptly to avoid it becoming soft and turning yellow.
6. Arrange the drained okra neatly on a plate, then drizzle the prepared sauce over it and serve.

## Health Advice
The blanching technique preserves the okra's crisp texture and mucilage, which is rich in dietary fiber and vitamins, making it suitable for cooling and moistening the intestines in summer. The blanching time should be short to prevent nutrient loss; okra is relatively cooling in nature, so those with a weak or cold spleen and stomach are advised to eat it with some shredded ginger.
