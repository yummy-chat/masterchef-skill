Difficulty Level: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Preparation Steps
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the fresh shrimp, and cook over high heat for about 10 seconds. Once the shrimp turn red, immediately reduce to low heat or turn off the heat.
- The cooking time over high heat should be short to prevent the shrimp meat from becoming tough.
2. After turning off the heat, add sugar water to the pot and let the shrimp soak in the residual heat for 30 seconds to make the meat sweeter.
- The entire process from adding the shrimp to removing them takes about 1 minute and 30 seconds. After soaking, the shrimp meat is tender and juicy.
3. Remove the shrimp, drain the water, and plate them for later use.
4. Prepare three types of dipping sauce ingredients separately: minced garlic, chili rings, and diced shallots. Divide them into three small bowls.
5. Add chicken fat to a wok spoon and heat to about 60% hot. Pour the hot chicken fat equally into the three bowls of ingredients. Listen for the "sizzling" sound to release the aroma.
6. Immediately after drizzling the oil, add light soy sauce to each bowl, with an oil-to-soy sauce ratio of about 1:3. Stir well to use as a dipping sauce.
7. Serve the boiled shrimp with the three dipping sauces on the table. The minced garlic dipping sauce, due to the sticky garlic paste, adheres better to the shrimp meat, enhancing the flavor.

## Health Advice
Boiled shrimp are low in fat and high in protein, rich in zinc and selenium, making them suitable for weight loss and fitness enthusiasts. Note that shrimp should be removed after 1 minute and 30 seconds of cooking to avoid tough meat; although chicken fat in the dipping sauce is fragrant, it is high in calories, so the amount of oil can be reduced appropriately.
