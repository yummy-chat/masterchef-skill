# Chive and Pork Baozi  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork front leg meat, about 500g (recommended fat-to-lean ratio: 30% fat, 70% lean)  
🌾 All-purpose flour, 500g  
🫧 Dry yeast, 5g  
💧 Room-temperature water, 260ml  
🫚 Fresh ginger,适量 (for making ginger paste and ginger juice)  
🧅 Chives, about 50g (finely chopped)  
🐖 Lard (or cooking oil), 1 tablespoon (for kneading dough)  
🧂 Salt (for filling), to taste  
🍬 White sugar, to taste  
🧂 MSG, to taste  
🌶️ White pepper, to taste  
🦪 Oyster sauce, to taste  
🥢 Light soy sauce, to taste  
🥢 Dark soy sauce, to taste  
🥜 Peanut oil or sesame oil, to taste  
🧂 Salt (for dough), 1 small teaspoon  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Meat grinder  
🧽 Ginger grater  
🥣 Large bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍞 Cutting board  
🔪 Kitchen knife  
🍰 Dough scraper  
🥟 Steamer basket  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Remove the skin from the pork front leg meat, cut it into small pieces, and grind it into minced meat using a meat grinder.  
2. Add ginger paste, ginger juice, salt, sugar, MSG, white pepper, oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and peanut oil to the minced meat. Stir continuously in one direction for 3-5 minutes until the filling is well-incorporated and firm. Finally, mix in the finely chopped chives.  
   **Note**: Firmly mixed filling can be frozen until slightly firm for easier wrapping.  
3. Make a well in the flour, add the 5g dry yeast and 260ml room-temperature water, and stir with chopsticks until flaky. Add 1 tablespoon of lard and 1 small teaspoon of salt, then knead into a smooth dough.  
   **Note**: Adding lard makes the dough softer and less sticky; knead thoroughly until the dough is smooth.  
4. Roll the dough into a long log, cut it into evenly sized pieces, flatten each piece, and roll it into a round wrapper with thin edges and a thicker center.  
5. Place an appropriate amount of filling on a wrapper, pinch the edges to seal tightly, and remove any excess dough at the top to ensure a secure closure with no leakage.  
6. Shape the baozi using one of the following two methods:  
   - Use a dough scraper or the back of a fruit knife to press radial patterns on the surface of the baozi;  
   - Place the baozi at an angle, rotate it with your left hand, and continuously pleat forward with your right index finger while keeping your thumb stationary.  
7. Arrange the baozi with spacing on a steamer basket lined with a cloth, and let them rest at room temperature for 20-35 minutes.  
   **Indicator**: The baozi should visibly increase in size, feel light in hand, and slowly spring back when gently pressed.  
8. Once the water in the steamer is boiling, place the steamer basket on top and steam over high heat for 15-20 minutes. Turn off the heat and let them sit for a few minutes before uncovering and removing.  

## Health Tips  
Chive and pork baozi provide high-quality protein and carbohydrates, making them suitable for breakfast or lunch. However, the fatty meat filling is relatively high in calories, so it is recommended to pair with light vegetables. Diabetics should control portion sizes, and elderly individuals can reduce seasonings to lower sodium intake.
