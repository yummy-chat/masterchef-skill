# Lazy Person's Cold Noodles (Liang Pi)
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥡 Plain flour 300g
💧 Water 550-600ml
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for brushing the pan and preventing sticking)
🧄 Garlic appropriate amount (mashed into a paste for garlic water)
🥕 Carrot appropriate amount (shredded)
🥒 Cucumber appropriate amount (shredded)
🌿 Cilantro appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🧂 Salt one teaspoon (for the batter); a little extra for garlic water
🍲 Spice water one tablespoon
  (Contains: Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, bay leaves, cumin seeds. Boil in water for 10 minutes, strain, and let cool.)
🧄 Garlic water one tablespoon
  (Garlic paste + a little salt + water)
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount
🍶 Fragrant vinegar appropriate amount
🧂 Chicken bouillon appropriate amount
🥜 Sesame paste appropriate amount
🌶️ Chili oil one tablespoon

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Large glass bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🍲 Small saucepan
🍕 Round flat baking pan (pizza pan)
🖌️ Silicone brush
🍳 Wok / Steamer
🔘 Lid
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥗 Large mixing bowl
🥛 Small bowl and strainer

## Preparation Steps
1. Mix the batter: Add one teaspoon of salt to 300g of flour. Gradually add 550-600ml of water, stirring continuously until smooth and lump-free. Let it rest for 20 minutes.
2. Prepare sauces and side dishes: Make spice water - bring Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, bay leaves, and cumin seeds to a boil in water, then simmer for 10 minutes. Strain and let cool. Make garlic water - mix garlic paste with a little salt and water. Prepare side dishes - shred carrot and cucumber, cut cilantro into sections.
3. Steam the cold noodles: Lightly brush oil onto the flat baking pan. Pour an appropriate amount of batter into the pan, swirling to spread evenly. Once the water in the steamer is boiling, place the pan inside, cover with the lid, and steam over medium heat for 3 minutes.
**Key Indicator**: The noodles are fully cooked when large bubbles appear on the surface.

4. Cool and unmold: Remove the pan and immediately place it into a basin of cold water to cool. Once completely cool, brush a layer of oil on the surface of the noodle sheet. Slowly peel it off from the edge to get a complete sheet of cold noodles.
5. Cut and mix: Cut the cold noodle sheets into wide strips and place them in a large mixing bowl. Add salt, light soy sauce, fragrant vinegar, chicken bouillon, sesame paste, one large tablespoon of spice water, and one large tablespoon of garlic water. Then add the shredded carrot, cucumber, and cilantro. Finally, drizzle with one large tablespoon of chili oil. Mix thoroughly and serve.

## Health Advice
This cold noodle dish is light and refreshing, making it ideal for summer to stimulate the appetite or control calorie intake. Sesame paste and chili oil are high in fat, so use them in moderation. Resting the batter for 20 minutes improves texture. The noodles are fully cooked when large bubbles appear on the surface during steaming. Homemade cold noodles are healthier than store-bought ones.
