# Shredded Potato Stir-fry with Pork
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare the potatoes**: Peel the potatoes and shred them using a shredder. Immediately rinse repeatedly with clean water to remove excess surface starch, then drain well and set aside.
   **Note**: Thorough rinsing and draining are key to keeping the shredded potatoes crispy.
2. **Prepare the pork**: Separate the fat and lean parts of the pork front leg. Slice the lean meat first, then cut into shreds; slice the fat meat into thin pieces.
3. **Marinate the lean pork shreds**: Add salt, light soy sauce, and scallion-ginger water to the lean meat shreds. Mix by hand until sticky and tacky, then finally add a little cornstarch and mix well to coat.
   **Note**: Mixing until sticky helps seal in moisture, making the meat shreds tender and smooth.
4. **Prepare the aromatics and garnishes**: Shred the green and red chili peppers, smash and mince the garlic, cut the scallion into sections, and smash the ginger. Soak the smashed scallion and ginger in water to make scallion-ginger water.
5. **Render the fat and stir-fry the meat shreds**: Heat the wok, add a little oil to coat, then add the smashed ginger and fat meat slices. Stir-fry over medium heat until the fat renders and the edges are slightly browned. Add the marinated lean meat shreds, keeping the heat at medium, and stir-fry until the meat is fully cooked and has changed color. Remove and set aside. Keep the mixed oil from cooking the meat in the wok.
6. **Sauté aromatics and stir-fry the shredded potatoes**: Using the residual oil in the wok, add the minced garlic and quickly sauté over high heat until fragrant. Immediately add the drained shredded potatoes and chili pepper shreds. Continue stir-frying over high heat until the potatoes are slightly wilted and softened.
   **Note**: Stir-frying over high heat throughout is key to maintaining the potato shreds’ crispness.
7. **Season and combine**: Add salt, MSG, and light soy sauce, and stir-fry evenly. Return the previously cooked meat shreds to the wok. If the color is too light, add a little dark soy sauce. Stir-fry quickly to evenly coat and blend the ingredients.
8. **Finish and enhance aroma**: Splash aged vinegar along the hot edge of the wok to release a tangy aroma, then immediately scatter the scallion sections over the dish. Stir-fry a few more times, then remove from the wok and plate.
   **Note**: Splashing vinegar along the wok edge maximizes the “wok hei” (breath of the wok).

## Health Advice
Soaking the shredded potatoes to remove starch is the secret to a crispy texture. Pairing with pork front leg provides high-quality protein, making this dish suitable for those needing to control oil intake and build muscle. Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid excessive carbohydrate consumption.
