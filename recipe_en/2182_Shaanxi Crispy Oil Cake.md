# Shaanxi Crispy Oil Cake
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 1000g medium or high gluten flour (for scalding dough)
💧 1500g drinking water (for scalding dough)
🐖 100g lard
🧂 5g baking soda (dissolved in 50g cooled boiled water)
🧊 50g cooled boiled water (for dissolving baking soda)
🍂 Brown sugar, as needed (filling)
🫘 Cooked sesame seeds, as needed (filling)
🌾 Steamed flour, as needed (filling)
🌾 50g dry flour (for kneading)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍲 Stainless steel pot
🥢 Wooden rolling pin/stirring stick
🔪 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel basin
🫗 Small bowl
🥄 Spoon
🍳 Deep fryer (iron pot)
🕳️ Slotted spoon
🥢 Chopsticks

## Preparation Steps
1. Add lard to the pot, heat until melted, then pour in water and bring to a boil. Once boiling, add 1000g flour all at once, stirring continuously with a stick until the flour is scalded and cooked through. Turn off the heat, cover, and let it sit for 5 minutes.
2. While the scalded dough cools, add 5g baking soda to 50g cooled boiled water, stirring until completely dissolved. Set aside.
3. Place brown sugar, cooked sesame seeds, and steamed flour in a basin, mix well to form the filling.
4. Transfer the scalded dough onto a pre-oiled cutting board, quickly separate it with chopsticks to release heat, and allow it to cool completely.
5. Evenly sprinkle the baking soda water over the completely cooled dough.
6. Gather the dough, and repeatedly press and fold it using the heel of your palm. Note: Do not knead it like bread; use only pressing and folding techniques to avoid damaging the structure. Once evenly pressed, sprinkle in 50g dry flour, continuing to press and fold until the dry flour is fully incorporated and the dough becomes smooth.
7. Cover the kneaded dough with a basin and let it rest for 20 minutes.
8. Take a piece of rested dough, roll it into a smooth ball. Press down from the center with your thumb and rotate to form a cavity in the middle, making the edges slightly thinner. Fill with an appropriate amount of sugar filling. Seal the opening tightly like wrapping a bun, pinch off any excess dough, and gently press into a flat round shape, ensuring the surface is smooth and crack-free.
9. Heat oil in a pot to around 60-70% hot. Gently lower each raw cake into the oil, using a spoon to lightly push and prevent sticking. Fry until the oil cakes float to the surface, develop dense small bubbles on the skin, and turn golden brown. Then remove with a slotted spoon.
   - Judgment criteria: Upon entry into the oil, the raw cake will immediately be surrounded by dense small bubbles and float to the surface.
   - Finished product requirements: Golden surface, covered with small bubbles, forming a distinct crispy skin, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

## Health Advice
The combination of scalded dough and baking soda can make the skin crispy, but the pairing of lard and brown sugar is high in calories. It is recommended to pair with green tea to cut the grease, or consume in portions to control sugar intake. Suitable for those needing a quick energy boost, but those with weak digestive function should chew thoroughly and eat slowly.
