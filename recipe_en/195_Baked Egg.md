# Baked Egg
Cuisine: Sichuan
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs 4
🌾 Cornstarch to taste
🌾 Flour to taste
💧 Water to taste

## Seasonings
🧂 Salt to taste
🌶️ White pepper powder to taste
🌶️ Chopped pickled chili to taste
🫚 Minced ginger to taste
🧄 Minced garlic to taste
🧂 MSG to taste
🧂 Chicken bouillon to taste
🍬 Sugar to taste
🫗 Soy sauce to taste
💧 Water starch to taste
🧅 Chopped scallions to taste
🍶 Vinegar to taste

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Ladle
🥄 Skimmer
🥣 Soup bowl
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🔥 Gas stove

## Steps
1.  **Make the egg batter**: Beat 4 eggs in a bowl until smooth. In a separate basin, combine cornstarch and flour with water, salt, and white pepper powder, stirring until a smooth, lump-free batter forms. Pour the beaten eggs into the batter and mix thoroughly.  
**Key point**: The cornstarch and flour must first be dissolved in water until lump-free before combining with the eggs. This step determines the texture and stability of the baked egg.

2.  **Heat the wok and spread the batter**: Thoroughly heat the wok to prevent sticking, leaving a thin layer of oil. Pour in about four-fifths of the egg batter, gently rotating the wok so the batter spreads evenly into a large, thin sheet.  
**Heat**: Medium-low heat.  
**Key point**: Do not pour all the batter at once; reserve about one-fifth for later sealing. Handle gently and spread as thinly as possible. If there is uncooked batter in the center, gently stir it with a ladle to ensure even heating.

3.  **Fold into shape**: When the bottom of the egg sheet is set but the surface is still slightly runny, turn off the heat. Fold the edges of the sheet toward the center, shaping it into a square. Use the reserved raw batter to brush along the folded seams to help seal them.  
**Key point**: Work while the batter is just about to set but still soft. Folding must bond the surface with the still-runny interior to prevent separation during heating.

4.  **Pan-fry to set**: Place the folded square egg block back into the wok, add a little oil, and fry slowly over low heat, turning occasionally, until both sides are set and fully cooked.  
**Heat**: Low heat, slow frying.  
**Key point**: This step cooks the interior thoroughly and fixes the shape. The heat must be low to prevent burning the outside while leaving the inside raw.

5.  **Cut and deep-fry**: Remove the pan-fried egg block and place it on a cutting board. Cut it into pieces about 3–4 cm square. Heat plenty of oil in the wok to a low temperature, then gently add the egg pieces and fry slowly.  
**Heat**: Add at low oil temperature, maintain medium-low heat for slow frying.  
**Doneness check**: Fry until the pieces puff up, float to the surface, and turn golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain oil.  
**Key point**: The pieces must be added at low oil temperature and fried slowly to ensure even heating inside and out, achieving a crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior.

6.  **Make the fish-flavored sauce**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Sauté the chopped pickled chili, minced ginger, and minced garlic until fragrant. Add water, then season with MSG, chicken bouillon, sugar, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, thicken with water starch to a thin consistency. Finally, stir in chopped scallions and vinegar, and mix well.  
**Key point**: Vinegar must be added last to preserve its aroma.

7.  **Plate and serve**: Neatly arrange the fried egg pieces on a plate. Pour the prepared fish-flavored sauce into a small bowl and serve alongside the egg pieces, allowing diners to dip.  
**Key point**: Traditionally, the baked egg and fish-flavored sauce are served separately.

## Health Advice
Eggs are rich in high-quality protein, but coating and deep-frying increases calorie content, making this dish suitable for those needing muscle gain or energy replenishment. The fish-flavored sauce contains high sugar and salt; those with high blood pressure should avoid dipping excessively.
