# Private Kitchen Beef Meatballs
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef front leg meat 500g (remove some connective tissue, retain chewiness)
🧊 Ice cubes approx. 10g (for first grinding) + 100g (for mixer beating)
🥚 Egg 1 (separate egg white and yolk)
💧 Ice water 100-120g
🌿 Sesame oil 5g
🌾 Potato starch or tapioca starch 30g
🧂 Salt 6g
🧂 Baking soda 3g
🍬 Sugar 3-5g
🌽 Corn oil 25g
🦪 Oyster sauce 5-10g
🧄 Garlic paste (optional) half clove
🌶 Black pepper (optional) to taste
🫚 Ginger a few slices

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🌀 Food processor (meat grinder)
🥣 Stand mixer (with paddle attachment)
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Pot, stove
🌡 Food thermometer
🥄 Spoon
🥢 Slotted spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Clean the beef, cut into small pieces, and place in the food processor together with about 10g of ice cubes. Grind until a fine paste forms.
2. Prepare compound seasoning oil: In a bowl, mix corn oil, sesame oil, and oyster sauce. Optionally add garlic paste, black pepper, and the separated egg yolk. Stir until well combined and set aside.
3. Transfer the meat paste to the stand mixer bowl. Gradually add ice water, salt, baking soda, and 100g of ice cubes.
   **Key point**: Start at low speed to prevent ice splashing. Once the ice gradually melts, increase to medium speed and beat for about 10 minutes until the ice is completely melted and the meat paste has fully absorbed the liquid. Check consistency: When lifted with a spatula, the paste should cling without falling off, showing elasticity and stickiness.
4. Add sugar and starch to the meat paste. First mix at low speed until evenly combined, then beat at medium speed for 3-5 minutes.
5. Pour in the compound seasoning oil. Mix at low speed until the paste fully absorbs the oil. Stop immediately to avoid overmixing, which may cause the mixture to become loose.
6. Place the beaten meat paste in the refrigerator to chill briefly.
7. Fill a pot with water, add a few slices of ginger, and heat to about 80°C. Then **turn off the heat**.
8. Wearing gloves, take an appropriate amount of meat paste. Squeeze into round balls from the gap between thumb and index finger, cut off with a wet spoon, and gently place into the hot water. Keep the heat off the entire time, allowing the meatballs to set in the residual heat.
9. After all meatballs are formed, the water temperature will drop to about 40°C. **Turn on low heat** and slowly heat up. Do not stir the meatballs initially. Once the water temperature rises above 75°C and the meatballs float to the surface, having set and changed color, you can gently move them.
10. Continue to cook slowly on low heat until the water temperature exceeds 80°C. Cut one meatball open to check; it is fully cooked when the inside is completely white and no longer pink.
11. Remove the meatballs with a slotted spoon and immediately place them into cold mineral or purified water to cool. Drain well, then seal and freeze for storage.

## Health Tips
Beef is rich in heme iron, making it suitable for people with anemia. The use of baking soda and ice cubes during beating creates a springy texture, but individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of salt intake.
