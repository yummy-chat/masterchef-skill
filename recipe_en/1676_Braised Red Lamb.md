# Braised Red Lamb

Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November–December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Bone-in lamb 1.2 kg (cut into chunks, bone-in for better flavor)  
🧅 Scallions (white parts) 2 stalks  
🫚 Ginger 1 small piece (sliced)  
🍤 Dried shrimp 1 tsp (for umami, optional)  
🌿 Cilantro 1 bunch (for garnish and aroma)  
🫒 Cooking oil 3 tbsp  
🍬 White sugar 2 tbsp (for caramelizing)  
🧂 White pepper to taste  
🌱 Coriander seeds to taste  
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns to taste  
⭐ Star anise 1  
🌳 Cinnamon stick 1  
🌶️ Dried chilies 1 handful  
🥣 Doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 tbsp  
🫗 Light soy sauce 1 tbsp  
🧂 Salt to taste (adjust before serving)

## Tools & Equipment
🍲 Stainless steel deep pot (for blanching, caramelizing, stir-frying, and braising)  
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing lamb and spice residues)  
🥄 Wooden spatula (for stirring)  
🍳 Flat frying pan (for serving)  
🔒 Pot with tight-fitting lid (for braising)  
🥣 Small bowl (for holding dried spices)  
🧺 Colander or strainer basket (for draining lamb)

## Cooking Steps
1. Place lamb in cold water and bring to a boil. Skim off foam, then increase heat to a rolling boil. Remove lamb from the boiling area with a slotted spoon and transfer to a colander to drain. Do not rinse; let residual heat evaporate surface moisture so the lamb is dry, which helps with browning and frying.  
   **Key point**: Do not rinse after blanching; keeping the surface dry aids in coloring and stir-frying.

2. Place white pepper, coriander seeds, Sichuan peppercorns, star anise, cinnamon stick, and dried chilies in a small bowl and set aside.

3. Add sugar to cold oil and heat over high heat. When the edges of the sugar begin to melt and change color, immediately reduce to the lowest heat and stir constantly. When the caramel turns from white to jujube red and small bubbles appear, proceed to the next step quickly to avoid burning.  
   **Key point**: Caramelize over low heat throughout; when it turns jujube red and bubbles, add the meat promptly to prevent bitterness.

4. Add the drained lamb to the caramel and stir-fry over medium heat quickly, coating each piece evenly. Cook until the surface turns reddish-brown.

5. Add scallion segments and ginger slices, then add all the dried spices. Stir-fry over medium heat until fragrant.

6. Add 1 tbsp of doubanjiang and continue stir-frying over medium heat until the oil turns red and the lamb deepens in color and smells rich with the bean paste.

7. Add hot water, just enough to almost cover the lamb. Add 1 tsp of dried shrimp for umami and 1 tbsp of light soy sauce for seasoning. Stir to combine, taste, and add salt as needed. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and braise for 1.5 hours, keeping the broth at a gentle simmer.  
   **Key point**: Braise over low heat the entire time, maintaining a light simmer, so the lamb becomes tender and flavorful.

8. After braising, uncover and remove all scallions, ginger, and spice residues. Increase heat to high and reduce the broth until about half remains, thick and glossy red. Turn off the heat. Transfer the lamb and broth to a flat frying pan, sprinkle with cilantro, and serve.

## Health Advice
Braised red lamb is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for winter nourishment. However, doubanjiang and caramelized sugar contain significant sodium and sugar, so those with high blood pressure or blood sugar concerns should consume in moderation. Braising for 1.5 hours makes the meat tender and easy to digest.
