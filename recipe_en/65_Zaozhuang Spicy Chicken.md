# Zaozhuang Spicy Chicken
Cuisine: Lu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🐔 1 free-range rooster (about 1300g, ideally over six months old)  
🌶️ 300g Zaozhuang thin-skinned peppers (can substitute with regular green peppers)  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of bird's eye chilies  
🧅 1 scallion  
🧄 1 piece of ginger  
🧄 Several garlic cloves  
🌿 Appropriate amount of cilantro  
🥚 Some chicken testicles (for garnish)  
🫒 250g vegetable oil  
⭐ 5 star anise  
🌿 Appropriate amount of green Sichuan peppercorns  
🌶️ Appropriate amount of dried chili segments  
🍾 Appropriate amount of cooking wine  
🥫 About 8g doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste)  
🥫 About 5g soybean paste  
🍶 6g rice vinegar  
🥄 20g light soy sauce  
🥄 Appropriate amount of dark soy sauce  
🧂 2g salt  
🧂 3g chicken bouillon (optional)  
🧂 3g MSG (optional)  
🥄 A little fresh soy sauce  
🫒 Appropriate amount of sesame oil  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🔪 Cutting board  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🍳 Wok (Chinese iron wok)  
🥄 Wok spatula  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🪣 Stainless steel bowl  
🫓 Electric griddle (for making accompanying flatbread)  
🥖 Rolling pin (for making flatbread)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut the rooster into small cubes. First cut the tendons of the chicken feet and crack the bones. Rinse in clean water to remove blood, then drain thoroughly.
2. Cut the scallion into segments, slice the ginger, cut the bird's eye chilies into small strips, chunk the thin-skinned peppers, smash the garlic cloves, and cut the cilantro into sections. Prepare a small bowl with star anise, green Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili segments.
3. Place the wok over heat and add 250g vegetable oil. When the oil reaches about 40% heat, add the star anise and fry until fragrant, then add the ginger slices and scallion segments, stir-frying until aromatic.
4. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry over medium heat for about two minutes until the surface moisture is mostly evaporated.  
**Key point**: Use a wok spatula to push and stir the chicken, maintaining a continuous rise in wok temperature.
5. Add the dried chili segments and green Sichuan peppercorns, stir-frying until fragrant. Drizzle cooking wine along the edge of the wok to remove any gamey smell. Once the wine has evaporated, add the doubanjiang and soybean paste, stir-frying until aromatic and the color turns red and glossy.
6. Drizzle 6g rice vinegar along the wok edge, stir-fry, then add 20g light soy sauce. Stir-fry until the sauce is fragrant. Add enough water to submerge the chicken, then add dark soy sauce for color.
7. Bring to a full boil over high heat, skim off any foam, cover with a lid, and reduce to medium-low heat. Simmer for 15 minutes. Stir the chicken occasionally during simmering to ensure even heating.
8. Remove the lid, add the bird's eye chilies, and season with 2g salt, 3g chicken bouillon, and 3g MSG. Turn to medium heat and reduce the sauce for about 1 minute.
9. Add the thin-skinned pepper chunks and smashed garlic cloves. Stir-fry until the green peppers are just cooked but still crisp. You may remove the chicken testicles beforehand to prevent them from breaking apart.
10. Add a little fresh soy sauce and sesame oil, sprinkle in the cilantro sections, and stir-fry quickly until evenly mixed. Serve immediately, garnishing with the reserved chicken testicles on top.

## Health Suggestions
This Zaozhuang spicy chicken is rich in protein and fat, making it suitable for replenishing energy for those with high physical exertion. People with high blood pressure or those on a weight-loss diet should control their portions. When cooking, add more green peppers and use less salt and oil to balance flavor and health. It is recommended to drink plenty of water after eating to alleviate the spiciness.
