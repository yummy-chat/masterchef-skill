# Fresh Milk Mochi
Cuisine: Taiwanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-year-round meal

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🫘 Dried soybeans 100g (for making soybean powder)
🍠 Tapioca starch 20g
🥛 Whole milk 250g
🍬 White sugar 25g

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Frying pan
🌀 Blender (or rolling pin as substitute)
🪝 Strainer
🥄 Non-stick pan (saucepan)
🥄 Silicone spatula
🥢 Chopsticks
🍽️ Plate

## Instructions
### Preparing soybean powder
1. Pour the dried soybeans into the frying pan, turn the heat to the lowest setting, and stir continuously for about 20 minutes, until the soybeans darken in color, release a rich bean aroma, and no raw bean smell remains, then remove from heat.
**Key point**: Stir continuously to prevent burning.
2. Pour the roasted soybeans into the blender and grind into a fine powder.
3. Sift the soybean powder through the strainer, set aside the fine powder, and discard the coarse residue.
### Making the mochi
1. In the non-stick pan, add the tapioca starch, white sugar, and whole milk in order, then turn the heat to the lowest setting.
**Key point**: Stir continuously with the silicone spatula to prevent sticking to the bottom.
2. Keep stirring and heating, observing the changes in the mixture:
   - About 4 minutes: starts to become sticky
   - About 5 minutes: becomes stickier
   - About 7 minutes: takes on a semi-liquid pasty consistency
   - About 10 minutes: the mochi paste is very thick, smooth, and can be pulled into strands, then remove from heat.
3. Keep stirring the cooked mochi paste in the pan or transfer it to another container to help it cool down, until it cools and becomes more chewy in texture.
4. Spread a layer of soybean powder on a plate, then pour the mochi dough, which has cooled to lukewarm, onto the soybean powder.
5. Use chopsticks to turn the mochi dough so that its surface is evenly coated with the soybean powder.
6. Divide into small pieces and enjoy.

## Health Advice
Homemade mochi with soybean powder is low in sugar and high in calcium, making it a suitable snack to satisfy cravings. However, tapioca starch raises blood sugar quickly, so those monitoring their sugar intake are advised to consume half the portion.
