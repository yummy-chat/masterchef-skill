# Universal Garlic Spinach Pesto 🌿
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal

## Ingredients & Seasonings 🥬
🥬 Spinach 500g
🌰 Walnuts 6 pieces
🧄 Garlic a few cloves
🧀 Parmesan cheese 50g
🧂 Baking soda 1 tsp
🫒 Extra virgin olive oil as needed
🍋 Lemon juice a little
🧂 Salt 1 tbsp (for blanching) + as needed (for seasoning)
🧂 Black pepper as needed

## Cookware & Equipment 🍳
🍳 Wok/saucepan
🪣 Stainless steel bowl (for ice water)
🥄 Blender/food processor
🔪 Knife, cutting board
🥢 Tongs/slotted spoon
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Wash the spinach and trim off the roots; peel the garlic; grate the Parmesan cheese; shell the walnuts and set aside.

2. Fill a pot with enough water, add 1 tbsp of salt and 1 tbsp of baking soda, and bring to a boil.

3. Prepare a separate bowl of ice water.

4. Blanch the spinach in the boiling water for about 1 minute.
   **Key point**: Adding baking soda helps maintain the spinach's vibrant green color.

5. Use a slotted spoon to remove the spinach and immediately immerse it in the ice water to cool.
   **Key point**: Rapid cooling prevents the spinach from oxidizing and turning black.

6. Remove the cooled spinach and squeeze out excess water with your hands as much as possible, forming a tight, dry clump.

7. Place the squeezed spinach, garlic, grated Parmesan cheese, and walnuts into a blender.

8. Squeeze in a little lemon juice, and add salt and black pepper to taste.

9. Start the blender and pulse the ingredients into a coarse paste.

10. With the blender running, slowly and continuously pour in olive oil through the top opening.
    **Key point**: Adding oil while blending is crucial for emulsification; stop once the sauce becomes smooth, evenly light green, glossy, and silky.

11. Cook homemade or store-bought pasta, drain, and gently toss over low heat with an appropriate amount of the pesto.
    **Note**: Avoid high heat to prevent the sauce from discoloring.

## Health Tips
Spinach is rich in iron and folic acid. Combined with walnuts and olive oil, this is a green choice for eye and brain health. Suitable for vegetarians and those on a weight-loss diet. However, as cheese is high in sodium, those with high blood pressure should control the amount used.
