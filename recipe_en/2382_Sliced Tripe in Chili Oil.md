# Sliced Tripe in Chili Oil  
Cuisine: Sichuan  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: All-year round  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫕 Beef tripe 1  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)  
🥒 Cucumber, about half (shredded for the base)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍶 Baijiu (Chinese liquor), to taste  
🌶️ Dried chilies, to taste (soaked in baijiu)  
🫘 Dried Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (soaked in baijiu)  
🍂 Mixed spices, to taste (soaked in baijiu, containing a variety of spices)  
🫗 Chili oil sauce, to taste (pre-made seasoning sauce)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🪣 Large stainless steel basin  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔪 Chef's knife and wooden cutting board  
🥄 Large slotted spoon  
🥄 Ladle  
🥣 Bowl

## Preparation Steps  
1. Rub the beef tripe in clean water twice to wash the surface.  
2. Lay the tripe flat on the cutting board, evenly pour hot water (about 80°C / 176°F) over the skin, and use a small knife to scrape off the black membrane completely.  
**Note**: The water temperature should not be too high, and the membrane must be thoroughly scraped off.  
3. Rinse the scraped tripe repeatedly in a basin of clean water until it turns white and has no slippery membrane residue.  
4. In a bowl, place the dried Sichuan peppercorns, dried chilies, and mixed spices, then pour in baijiu to soak, releasing the aroma.  
5. Place the tripe in cold water in a pot, add enough clean water to cover, then add scallion sections, ginger slices, and a little baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat.  
**Key point**: The tripe must be added to cold water; skim off any foam promptly once the water boils.  
6. After skimming off the foam, add the soaked spices and an appropriate amount of salt. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to medium-low, and braise for 1 hour.  
7. Once braised, turn off the heat and let the tripe cool. Meanwhile, shred the cucumber and spread it on a plate as the base.  
8. Once the tripe has cooled, slice it thinly on a diagonal angle and arrange it neatly on top of the shredded cucumber.  
**Note**: Only slice the tripe after it has fully cooled for easier shaping.  
9. Evenly pour the pre-prepared chili oil sauce over the sliced tripe.  
**Flavor core**: The chili oil sauce should fully cover the tripe to enhance the flavor and aroma.

## Health Tips  
Beef tripe is rich in protein but high in cholesterol, making it suitable for occasional consumption. Scraping off the membrane is key to removing the gamey smell. After braising, let it cool completely before slicing for better shape. Chili oil sauce is relatively high in calories, so pairing it with refreshing shredded cucumber helps cut the richness.
