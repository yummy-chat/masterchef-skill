# Garlic Crispy Oysters
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🦪 Oysters 10 pieces (shucked, meat only)  
🧄 Garlic 50g (crushed and minced)  
🥚 Egg 1 piece  
🌾 Cake flour 80g  
🌾 Cornstarch 15g  
🌾 Potato starch or similar about 20g (for coating oysters)  
🧂 Baking powder 3-5g  
🧂 Salt a pinch  
🍬 Sugar a pinch  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (a little for batter + plenty for deep frying)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🔪 Kitchen knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl, glass bowl  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🧽 Gauze bag or cloth  
🍳 Wok  
🔥 Stove  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make garlic water**: Soak 50g minced garlic in 50g room-temperature water, then squeeze through a gauze bag to collect the garlic water. Set aside the garlic pulp for other use.
2. **Prepare oysters**: Rinse oyster meat thoroughly under clean water to remove any shell fragments and impurities. Drain well.
3. **Blanch**: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add oysters and blanch for about 20 seconds. Remove immediately.  
*Note*: Blanching only tightens the meat and releases excess moisture; do not overdo the time.
4. **Pat dry**: Place oysters on paper towels, then gently press with another towel to absorb all surface moisture.  
*Key*: This step determines the success of coating and battering; must pat completely dry.
5. **Marinate**: Add a little garlic water to the oysters, gently toss to coat, and let rest briefly.
6. **Make crispy batter**: In a bowl, mix cake flour, cornstarch, baking powder, a pinch of salt and sugar. Add the egg and remaining garlic water, and stir until smooth. If too thick, add a little plain water. Finally, add a small amount of cooking oil and stir into a thin, smooth-flowing batter.
7. **Coat with starch**: Evenly coat the marinated oysters with a layer of potato starch or similar.  
*Key*: The starch increases friction and prevents the batter from sliding off later.
8. **Apply crispy batter**: Dip the starch-coated oysters into the crispy batter, ensuring full coverage.
9. **First frying**: Heat plenty of oil in a wok to about 30% hot (low temperature), then reduce to low heat. Add oysters one by one.  
*Note*: Gently push with a spoon immediately after adding to prevent sticking. Fry slowly on low heat until set.
10. **Clean oil**: Use a slotted spoon to remove any scattered bits of batter from the oil, keeping it clean.
11. **Second frying**: Turn to high heat to raise oil temperature. Continue frying until golden brown and the shell is crispy.  
*Tip*: With home stoves having lower heat, fry in batches; total frying time is about 10 minutes.
12. **Remove and serve**: Once fried, use a slotted spoon to lift out, drain excess oil, and plate.

## Health Advice
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and zinc, suitable for winter nourishment. However, it is high in calories due to frying, so it is recommended to pair with a vegetable salad. Those with poor digestion or gout should limit consumption.
