# Bandit Pork Liver
Cuisine: Hunan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork liver 300g
🥓 Pork belly 100g (fatty)
🫚 Ginger 40g
🌶️ Red chili peppers 100g (red hanging peppers, beauty peppers, etc.)
🌶️ Xiaomi chili peppers 30g
🧄 Garlic 25g (about 5 cloves)
🧅 Scallions 25g (about 2 stalks)

🧂 Salt 3g (for cleaning pork liver)
🧂 Salt 2g (for marinating pork liver)
🧂 Salt 1g (for stir-frying ingredients)
🍶 White vinegar 20g (about 2 spoons, for cleaning)
🧂 Chicken bouillon 3g (about 1 teaspoon, for marinating)
🥡 Light soy sauce 5g (half a spoon, for marinating)
🥫 Dark soy sauce 7g (2/3 spoon, for marinating)
🍷 Yellow wine 5g (half a spoon, for marinating)
🌾 Potato starch 8g (1 level spoon, for making water starch)
💧 Water 6–8g (half a spoon, for making water starch)
🍬 White sugar 3g
🌿 White pepper powder 1g (half a spoon)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍳 Iron wok
🥄 Wok spatula
🔪 Kitchen knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for cleaning and marinating)
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥄 Soup spoon
⚖️ Electronic scale
🧻 Kitchen paper
🥢 Chopsticks
🥣 Small bowl (for making starch slurry)

## Steps
1. **Process the pork liver**
   - Remove the white membranes on the surface of the liver. Slice the liver at a 45-degree angle into thin slices about the thickness of a coin.
- **Note**: The membranes must be completely removed. Angled slicing helps the starch adhere better.

2. **Clean and remove odor**
   - Place the liver slices in a bowl, add 3g salt and 20g white vinegar. Gently toss until the salt dissolves.
- Rinse repeatedly with a gentle stream of water 6–7 times until the water runs clear.
- Drain in a slotted spoon, then use kitchen paper to pat off excess surface moisture.
- **Note**: Drain thoroughly; otherwise, the starch coating will slide off, and the liver will release water during high-heat stir-frying.

3. **Make water starch**
   - Mix 8g potato starch with 6–8g water, stir with your fingers into a non-Newtonian fluid consistency that can form threads.
- **Note**: The water starch should be thick enough to coat the liver evenly.

4. **Prepare the aromatics**
   - Smash the ginger, then finely mince it.
- Cut the red chili peppers and Xiaomi chili peppers into rings, then lightly chop them.
- Slice or chop the garlic.
- Scallions: Cut the white part into diagonal horse-ear pieces, and the green part into small sections.
- Slice the pork belly into thin pieces.
- **Note**: Ginger, garlic, scallion whites, and chili peppers can be set aside together; scallion greens separately.

5. **Season and starch the liver**
   - Place the drained liver slices in a bowl. Add 2g salt, 3g chicken bouillon, 5g light soy sauce, 7g dark soy sauce, and 5g yellow wine. Mix evenly with your hands.
- Pour in the prepared water starch, mix again until each slice is coated.
- **Key**: Let the coated liver rest for 3–5 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate.

6. **Render the pork belly**
   - Heat the wok over high heat, add slightly more oil than usual for stir-frying.
- Add the pork belly slices, lower the heat to low, and slowly fry until the slices are dry and crispy, and the fat has rendered out.
- **Note**: Render slowly over low heat to fully release the fat and enhance the aroma.

7. **Bloom aromatics and season**
   - After frying the pork belly, first add the minced ginger, then turn the heat to high and stir-fry until fragrant.
- Add the red chili peppers, Xiaomi chili peppers, scallion whites, and garlic. Stir-fry evenly.
- Add 1g salt, 3g white sugar, and 1g white pepper powder. Continue to stir-fry until well combined.
- **Note**: Adding ginger first makes it easier to release its fragrance; sugar helps balance any bitterness from the liver.

8. **High-heat stir-fry the liver**
   - Keep the heat on maximum. Quickly slide the starched liver slices into the wok, and use a wok spatula to quickly separate them.
- Stir-fry rapidly for about 1 minute until the liver color changes from deep red to a grayish-brown, with each slice separated.
- **Key**: Use maximum heat and quick stir-frying; keep the time short so the liver remains crisp, tender, and springy.

9. **Add scallion greens and serve**
   - Once the liver has changed color and separated, immediately toss in the scallion greens. Quickly stir-fry until just wilted, then turn off the heat immediately.
- Plate and serve.
- **Note**: Add the scallion greens at the very end to maintain their bright green color and fresh aroma.

## Health Tips
Pork liver is rich in iron and vitamin A, but its cholesterol content is relatively high. It is recommended to pair it with vegetables. Suitable for people with anemia, but those with high blood lipids should consume in moderation. Pay attention to the heat when stir-frying to avoid overcooking, which affects the texture.
