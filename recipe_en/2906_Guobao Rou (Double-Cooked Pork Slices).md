# Guobao Rou (Double-Cooked Pork Slices)

## Steps
1. **Prepare the meat slices**: Remove the white connective tissue from both sides of the pork tenderloin and cut into large slices about 0.8cm thick.
2. **Marinate**: Add salt, white pepper, and Huangjiu to the meat slices. Rub evenly with your hands to ensure a sufficient base flavor.
3. **Prepare the sweet and sour sauce**: In a bowl, combine 5g salt, 3g MSG, 50-60g white sugar, 30-35g white vinegar, and a little rice vinegar. Stir until well mixed; this is the traditional "lying sauce".
4. **Prepare the batter**: Soak the potato starch in water for at least 30 minutes in advance. After settling, pour off the clear water on top and take the thick, paste-like wet starch from the bottom. Place the meat slices in the wet starch, add a little cooking oil, and mix well, ensuring each slice is coated with the starch batter and remains smooth and flat.
5. **First fry for setting**: Heat the oil to about 60% of its maximum temperature (around 180°C/350°F). Fry the battered meat slices one by one until set and cooked through. Remove and drain on paper towels.
6. **Second fry to expel oil**: Lower the oil temperature slightly, then fry the meat slices again to force out excess oil and make the exterior crispier. Remove and set aside.
7. **Third fry for color**: Heat the oil to about 80% of its maximum temperature (around 200°C/390°F). Quickly fry the meat slices again until they turn a golden, tiger-skin color and the surface is very crispy. Immediately remove and drain.
8. **Prepare the sauce**: Leave a little oil in the wok. Add the sliced garlic and julienned ginger, stir-fry until fragrant. Pour in the prepared sweet and sour sauce and boil over high heat, stirring constantly with a spoon until the sauce thickens and large bubbles form.
9. **Toss and plate**: Quickly add the fried meat slices, julienned carrot, julienned scallion, and cilantro sections into the wok. Stir-fry a few times to evenly coat everything with the sauce, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.
- **Note**: Stir-fry quickly to prevent the meat from becoming soft. The final dish should be crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and intensely sour and sweet.

## Health Advice
This dish of Guobao Rou is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, but it is high in oil and sugar. It is recommended to pair it with blanched or stir-fried green vegetables to balance the calories, and it is best enjoyed occasionally as a treat. The triple-frying process results in a relatively high fat content; individuals with high blood pressure, high blood sugar, or high blood lipids should only taste a small amount.
