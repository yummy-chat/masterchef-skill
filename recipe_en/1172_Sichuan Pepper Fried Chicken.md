# Sichuan Pepper Fried Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🐔 1 whole chicken (Sanhuang chicken or chicken thighs, cut into pieces)  
🫚 Ginger, to taste (slice generously)  
🧅 Scallions, to taste (cut into large sections)  
🌱 Garlic sprouts, to taste (slice diagonally; can substitute with garlic)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste  
🫑 Green Sichuan pepper (numbing pepper), to taste  

🫒 Peanut oil, to taste (use generously)  
🌿 3 slices of baizhi (angelica dahurica, preferably with yellow spots)  
🪵 2 pieces of cinnamon (use green-skinned smokecinnamon, which is sweeter than regular cinnamon)  
🍶 30g yellow wine (or cooking wine)  
🧂 25g light soy sauce  
🧂 5g salt  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok  
🔫 Infrared thermometer  
🥄 Wooden spatula/wok spatula  
🔪 Chef's knife and cutting board  
🥣 Strainer  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Remove the chicken's feet, innards, blood vessels, lymph nodes, and tail. Divide the chicken at the cartilage joints and chop into pieces.  
2. Rinse the chicken pieces repeatedly with clean water until the water runs clear. Drain thoroughly and ensure the chicken is completely dry, otherwise it will release water during cooking and affect the texture.  
3. Prepare the aromatics: slice the ginger, cut the scallions into large sections, slice the garlic sprouts diagonally. Set aside the baizhi, cinnamon, dried chili peppers, and green Sichuan pepper.  
4. Heat the iron wok over high heat to 400°C or above, then add the peanut oil to coat the wok. Use enough oil, as it serves as the medium for transferring aroma and heat. Insufficient wok temperature may cause sticking.  
5. First, add the ginger slices and stir-fry over medium-high heat until the edges are golden brown, fully releasing the ginger's fragrance.  
6. Add the scallion sections, baizhi, cinnamon, green Sichuan pepper, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry until some of the dried chili peppers turn black, adjusting based on their color change. If controlling the heat is difficult, reduce to medium heat.  
7. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry over high heat the entire time. Do not stir too frequently; instead, toss gently. Cook until the moisture in the wok has evaporated and the free water from the chicken is completely driven off.  
8. When most of the moisture in the wok is gone, add 30g of yellow wine, 25g of light soy sauce, and 5g of salt. Stir well to combine. Add all the salt at once—salt helps quickly coagulate proteins, locking in the non-free water within the meat for a tender, juicy texture.  
9. If you are unsure whether the chicken is fully cooked, add a small amount of water and simmer briefly.  
10. Once the sauce has thickened, add the garlic sprout sections, stir-fry evenly, and then remove from the wok and serve.  

## Health Tips  
Sichuan Pepper Fried Chicken is prepared using a high-oil stir-fry method. It is recommended to pair it with light vegetables or cold dishes to balance the greasiness. This dish is suitable for those who prefer bold flavors. Ensure the chicken is thoroughly drained before adding it to the wok; otherwise, excess moisture will affect the crispy texture.
