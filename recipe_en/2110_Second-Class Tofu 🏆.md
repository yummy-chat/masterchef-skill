# Second-Class Tofu 🏆

Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken Breast 1 piece (remove membrane, finely mince, then pass through a fine sieve)  
🫘 Brined Tofu Equal amount to chicken breast (mash, then pass through a fine sieve)  
🧅 Scallion-Ginger Water Appropriate amount (pre-brew with boiling water, let cool)  
🧂 Salt A small amount  
🍬 White Sugar A small amount  
🌶️ White Pepper Powder To taste  
🥚 Egg White Appropriate amount  
🐷 Lard Appropriate amount (grease the bottom of the plate to prevent sticking)  
🍄 Rehydrated Shiitake Mushrooms Appropriate amount (trimmed to resemble plum tree branches)  
🥬 Pea Sprouts Appropriate amount (blanched before use)  
🥕 Carrot A small amount (cut into small flower shapes)  
🍒 Goji Berries A few (for garnish)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's Knife, Cutting Board  
🥣 Bowls (multiple)  
🌀 Fine Sieve  
🍽️ Round Plate  
♨️ Steamer  
🍲 Pot  
✂️ Scissors  
🥄 Spoon  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Completely remove all membranes from the chicken breast. Cut into strips, then mince into a paste. Pass through a fine sieve to obtain an extremely fine meat paste.  
**Note**: Removing the membrane is essential for a smooth, tender, and non-dry texture.  

2.  Brew scallion and ginger with boiling water to make scallion-ginger water; let cool and set aside. Mash the brined tofu, then press it through a fine sieve to get a smooth tofu paste.  
3.  Mix the chicken paste and tofu paste in a 1:1 ratio. Season with salt, white sugar, and white pepper powder. Stir vigorously until the mixture is smooth, sticky, and forms a fine paste. Add egg white and continue stirring until evenly combined.  
**Key Point**: Thorough stirring enhances binding power, resulting in a texture that melts in the mouth.  

4.  Evenly spread a layer of lard on the bottom of a round plate. Spread the mixed chicken-tofu paste onto the plate. Use the trimmed shiitake mushrooms, small carrot flower slices, and a few pea sprouts to arrange a plum tree design.  
5.  Place the plate in a steamer. Steam over low heat for about 5 minutes, then remove.  
**Note**: Low heat steaming prevents the meat from becoming tough; lard helps with easy unmolding.  

6.  Quickly blanch the pea sprouts in boiling water to remove the raw bean flavor, then drain. Transfer the steamed "Second-Class Tofu" into a soup bowl. Pour in seasoned clear chicken broth. Place the blanched pea sprouts on top and garnish with goji berries.  

## Health Suggestions  
"Second-Class Tofu" processes chicken breast and tofu through a fine sieve, resulting in a silky smooth texture. It is suitable for the elderly, children, or those with weaker digestive systems. Use low heat during steaming to prevent the meat from becoming tough. The chicken broth seasoning should be light to highlight the natural flavors of the ingredients.
