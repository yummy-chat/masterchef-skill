# Imperial Crispy Beef Pie
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🌾 Flour 2 jin (high-gluten or all-purpose, for dough and oil paste)  
🧂 Alkaline water 2 grams (added to dough)  
🥩 Ground beef approximately 200 grams (main filling ingredient)  
🫚 Ginger appropriate amount (minced for seasoning)  
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (for filling)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn water appropriate amount (mixed into ground beef)  
🧂 Salt 6 grams (for kneading dough)  
🧂 Salt 20 grams (for oil paste)  
🌶️ Ground pepper 6 grams (for oil paste)  
🌿 Five-spice powder 6 grams (for oil paste)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for filling seasoning)  
🌶️ Ground pepper appropriate amount (for filling seasoning)  
🫚 Minced ginger appropriate amount (for filling seasoning)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorn water appropriate amount (for filling seasoning)  
🫒 Sesame oil a little (to seal moisture in meat filling)  
🍂 Spice powder appropriate amount (mix of Sichuan peppercorn, cumin, star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, angelica, galangal, sprinkled on filling)  
🫒 Cooking oil appropriate amount (for brushing dough pieces, making oil paste, spreading on dough, and frying)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🥣 Mixing bowl  
🔪 Cutting board  
🧹 Rolling pin  
🖌️ Brush  
Plastic wrap  
🥣 Small bowl  
🧇 Electric griddle  
🥄 Spatula, chopsticks, tongs  

## Preparation Steps  
**Kneading and Resting the Dough**  
1. In 2 jin of flour, mix in 6 grams of salt and 2 grams of alkaline water. Gradually add 560 grams of warm water, stirring until no dry flour remains, then knead into a dough.  
2. Knead the dough until smooth on the surface, roll into a long strip, brush with oil, then divide into evenly sized dough pieces. Roll each piece into a long strip again. Brush all strips with oil, place them on a tray, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and let rest for 40 minutes for better elasticity.  

**Making the Oil Paste**  
3. In 100 grams of flour, mix in 20 grams of salt, 6 grams of ground pepper, and 6 grams of five-spice powder. Heat 200 grams of oil to about 200°C, pour it hot over the flour mixture while stirring to form a thin paste-like oil paste.  

**Preparing the Filling**  
4. Add minced ginger, salt, ground pepper, and an appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorn water to the ground beef. Stir vigorously in one direction until the liquid is fully absorbed, then repeatedly slap the mixture to firm it up. Finally, mix in a little sesame oil.  

**Assembling and Rolling**  
5. Take a rested dough strip, flatten it, and roll it into a thin, long sheet. Spread an even layer of oil paste over it, then spread a layer of beef filling. Sprinkle with scallions and spice powder.  
6. Lift one side of the dough sheet, fold it over to enclose the filling, then roll it forward while gently stretching both ends to make the dough thinner and longer. Finally, coil it into a spiral shape, tuck the end underneath, and gently press flat.  

**Second Resting and Frying**  
7. Brush the surface of the pie blank with a little oil and let it rest for 2-3 minutes.  
8. Preheat the electric griddle to about 200°C and pour in enough oil. Press the pie blank into a round shape, place it on the griddle, and rotate it a few times for even heating. Cover and fry for about 3 minutes until the bottom is golden, then flip and continue frying with the lid on until the other side is golden, the pie puffs up, and layers are distinct. Total time: about 6-8 minutes. Remove from heat.  

## Health Tips  
Beef is rich in protein, and combined with flour as a carbohydrate source, this dish suits those with high physical energy expenditure. However, the oil paste and frying oil content are high; pairing with cold mixed vegetables is recommended to balance calories. Control daily intake to avoid excess calories.
