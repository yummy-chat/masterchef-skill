# Secret Spicy Braised Beef Leg
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Beef leg 1 (whole, large)
🫚 Old ginger 1 large piece (smashed)
🧅 Scallion 2 stalks (cut into sections)
🌿 Spices (bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, etc.) to taste
🌶️ Dried chili peppers to taste
🌶️ Green and red Sichuan peppercorns to taste
🫒 Cooking oil to taste
🍬 White sugar to taste (for caramelizing)
🫘 Pixian doubanjiang (broad bean paste) 1 large spoon
🫘 Yellow soybean paste 1 spoon
🥫 Light soy sauce to taste
🥫 Dark soy sauce to taste
🧂 Salt 1 small pinch
🍶 Cooking wine to taste
🌶️ Red oil (chili oil) to taste (for drizzling at the end)

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🍲 Large stockpot
🍳 Large iron wok (frying pan)
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🍽️ Large tray
🥄 Ladle

## Preparation Steps
1.  Clean the whole beef leg thoroughly. Remove any obvious sinew and fat from the surface. Make several cuts into the thicker parts of the meat to allow flavor to penetrate and ensure even cooking.
2.  Smash the old ginger. Cut the scallions into sections. Rinse the spices, dried chili peppers, and green and red Sichuan peppercorns once in clean water. Drain and set aside.
3.  Fill the large stockpot with enough cold water. Add the beef leg, scallion sections, ginger pieces, and cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface to keep the broth clear. Note: Reserve this blanching liquid; do not discard it.
4.  In a separate wok, heat oil. Add the white sugar. Stir constantly over low heat. When the sugar syrup changes from large bubbles to dense small bubbles and turns a brownish-red color, quickly pour in hot water. Stir well to combine, then set aside.
5.  Heat oil again in the wok. Sauté the scallions and ginger until fragrant. Add the Pixian doubanjiang and yellow soybean paste. Stir-fry over medium heat until red oil is released and the aroma is strong. Add the pre-rinsed spices, Sichuan peppercorns, and dried chili peppers. Stir-fry until their fragrance is fully released. Key Point: Rinsing the spices in water before frying enhances their flavor and prevents them from burning and becoming bitter.
6.  Pour the prepared caramel color and the entire stir-fried spice mixture into the stockpot containing the reserved beef leg blanching liquid. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and a pinch of salt to season. Bring to a boil over high heat, then add the beef leg. Cover the pot and reduce the heat to medium-low. Braise for 1 hour. Turn off the heat. Do not uncover the lid. Let the beef leg continue to steep in the hot broth for another 1 hour. Key Point: This steeping step is crucial for infusing flavor. Do not skip it.
7.  Remove the beef leg and place it on a tray. Make a few cuts into the thicker parts. Drizzle with red oil and sprinkle with chopped scallions. It can be eaten as is, or served with dried chili flakes or a dipping sauce for extra flavor.

## Health Advice
Beef leg is rich in protein and iron, making it suitable for fitness enthusiasts and those looking to supplement their iron intake. However, the braising liquid contains doubanjiang and sugar, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
