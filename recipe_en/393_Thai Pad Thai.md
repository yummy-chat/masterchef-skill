# Thai Pad Thai
Cuisine: Other  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥜 Tamarind 1 small handful (for making the sauce)  
🍚 Dried rice noodles 1 serving (about 100-150g, requires pre-soaking for 6-10 hours)  
🧅 Shallots 3-4 (julienned)  
🧈 Firm tofu 1 small piece (diced)  
🍤 Shrimp about 8 (peeled and deveined)  
🥬 Bean sprouts appropriate amount  
🥚 Eggs 1-2 (made into a thin omelet and sliced into strips)  
🥜 Peanuts appropriate amount (crushed, for final topping)  
🐟 Fish sauce appropriate amount (for tamarind sauce and final seasoning)  
🍬 Sugar appropriate amount (to balance the sourness of tamarind)  
🥄 Light soy sauce appropriate amount (for final seasoning to enhance flavor)  
🍋 Lime juice appropriate amount (for final seasoning and squeezing before eating)  
🌶️ Chili powder appropriate amount (to add spiciness at the end)  
🫒 Oil appropriate amount (for stir-frying shallots, pan-frying tofu and shrimp)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🥣 Bowl (for soaking tamarind and mixing sauce)  
🥄 Strainer (for filtering the tamarind paste)  
🍳 Wok (for stir-frying shallots, sauce, and noodles)  
🍳 Frying pan (for pan-frying tofu and shrimp)  
🔌 Induction cooker (heat source)  
🔪 Cutting board, knife (for preparing all ingredients)  
🥢 Spatula or chopsticks (for tossing)  
🧂 Measuring spoons (for assisting seasoning, not mandatory but recommended)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Prepare tamarind sauce: Soak dried tamarind in hot water until soft. Mash it with hands or a tool. Strain through a sieve to remove coarse fibers and seeds, resulting in a smooth tamarind paste. Add fish sauce and sugar to the paste, stir well to combine, and set the sauce aside.
   **Key Point**: The tamarind must be fully softened to make it easy to handle; straining is a crucial step for obtaining a smooth sauce.

2. Soak the rice noodles: Place the dried rice noodles in a tray and completely submerge them in hot water. Soak for 6-10 hours according to the package instructions until fully softened, then drain well.
   **Note**: These noodles require a long soaking time; be sure to prepare them in advance, otherwise the texture will be too firm.

3. Prepare the ingredients: Julienne the shallots. Dice the firm tofu into small cubes. Peel and devein the shrimp. Wash and drain the bean sprouts. Beat the eggs and make a thin omelet, then slice it into strips and set aside.

4. Cook the sauce base: Pour oil into a wok. Add the julienned shallots and stir-fry over medium-low heat until golden brown and fragrant. Pour in the prepared tamarind sauce, stir-fry evenly, then remove from the wok and set aside.
   **Note**: The shallots must be stir-fried until fragrant and slightly caramelized; this is the core of the flavor. Use medium-low heat and stir-fry slowly to avoid burning.

5. Pan-fry the tofu and shrimp: Pour oil into a frying pan and heat over medium heat. First, add the diced tofu and pan-fry until golden brown on all sides. Then, add the shrimp and stir-fry until they curl and change color.
   **Note**: Pan-frying the tofu first helps it hold its shape, making it less likely to break apart during subsequent stir-frying.

6. Stir-fry the rice noodles: Place the drained rice noodles into the wok. Add half of the tamarind sauce. Stir-fry quickly over medium-high heat, ensuring the noodles are evenly coated with the sauce and become soft.
   **Note**: Rice noodles tend to stick to the pan; keep the heat at medium-high and stir-fry quickly to prevent burning.

7. Add the quick-cooking vegetables and final seasonings: Add the bean sprouts and stir-fry a few times. Drizzle in light soy sauce and lime juice, and sprinkle in chili powder. Stir-fry vigorously over high heat until everything is well combined, then remove from the wok immediately.
   **Note**: Add the bean sprouts at the very end to keep them crunchy. All seasonings should be added just before removing from the wok to avoid losing their fresh flavor through prolonged cooking.

8. Plate and garnish: Plate the stir-fried noodles. Sprinkle with crushed peanuts and the sliced omelet. Squeeze a little extra lime juice over the top before serving for a flavor boost.
   **Note**: Crushed peanuts are the soul of the dish for adding aroma and texture; sprinkling them fresh yields the best flavor.

## Health Advice  
This Thai Pad Thai offers a balanced nutritional profile, with tofu and shrimp providing protein, and bean sprouts and lime adding vitamins and a refreshing taste. It suits those who enjoy sweet, sour, and spicy flavors. However, the tamarind sauce contains a moderate amount of sugar, so those watching their weight should consume it in moderation.
