# Grilled Meat on an Iron Plate 
Cuisine: Beijing Cuisine 
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons 

## Ingredients & Seasonings 
🥩 Beef top blade 500g (sliced thinly against the grain) 
🧅 Large green onion (white part cut into fine "eyebrow shreds", green part cut into large pieces for oil) 
🌿 Cilantro a handful (cut into sections, added before finishing) 

🧂 **Meat Marinade** 
- Salt 2g 
- Sugar 5g 
- White pepper 0.5g 
- Soy sauce 30ml 

🧂 **Spiced Oil** 
- Cooking oil to taste (added to a cold pan) 
- Star anise 4-5 pieces 
- Cinnamon sticks 2 pieces 
- Ginger a large piece 
- Sichuan peppercorns 30+ pieces 
- Cumin seeds to taste 
- Bay leaves 3 pieces (added after turning off the heat) 
- Sesame oil (roasted sesame oil) about 1/3 the amount of the finished spiced oil 

## Cookware & Equipment 
🍳 Wok or small saucepan (for frying spiced oil) 
🥢 Mesh strainer 
🥣 Glass bowl (for holding oil, marinating meat, mixing scallions) 
🍳 Flat-bottomed pan (for final stir-frying, must be heat-resistant) 
🔪 Cutting board and knife 
🥄 Chopsticks, spoon 

## Preparation Steps 
1. **Slice meat**: Slice beef top blade thinly against the grain to ensure a tender and smooth texture without getting stuck in the teeth. 
2. **Fry spiced oil**: 
   - Add cooking oil to a cold pan, add star anise and cinnamon stick, fry over low heat until fragrant. 
   - Add large pieces of green onion (green part) and ginger, fry until the scallion sections turn golden brown. 
   - Add Sichuan peppercorns and cumin seeds, fry until fragrant, then turn off the heat. 
   - Immediately add bay leaves after turning off the heat, steep using the residual heat. 
   - When the oil temperature drops slightly, strain out all spice solids using a mesh strainer, keep the clear oil. 
   - Add about 1/3 amount of sesame oil to the clear oil, mix well to form the spiced oil. 
   > Note: Add spices in batches according to their heat tolerance; strained spice solids can be saved for stewing meat. 
**Note**: Add spices in batches according to their heat tolerance; strained spice solids can be saved for stewing meat. 
3. **Marinate meat**: Add salt, sugar, white pepper, and soy sauce to the beef slices. Mix and knead until the meat becomes sticky. Add about 1/3 of the spiced oil, mix well. 
   > Key: Absolutely do not add water. This is a prerequisite for creating the Maillard reaction; do not use too many seasonings. 
**Key Point**: Do not add water. This is a prerequisite for creating the Maillard reaction; do not use too many seasonings. 
4. **Mix scallion shreds**: Put the fine shreds of white scallion into a bowl. Pour in half of the remaining spiced oil. Mix with chopsticks to lock in the spiciness and enhance the aroma. 
5. **Stir-fry**: 
   - Heat the flat-bottomed pan over high heat until extremely hot. Pour in all the remaining spiced oil. 
   - Quickly slide in the marinated beef slices. You should hear a loud sizzling sound. 
   - Use chopsticks to quickly break apart the slices. Wait until the meat is just cooked through and has browned edges. 
   - Spread all the oil-mixed scallion shreds over the meat. Stir-fry quickly until the scallion shreds soften and wilt. 
   - Sprinkle in a large handful of cilantro sections. Turn off the heat. Toss and mix well using the residual heat. 
   > Stir-fry over high heat the entire time. The pan must be hot enough to create a charred aroma. Mix and eat immediately after turning off the heat. 
**Key Point**: Stir-fry over high heat the entire time. The pan must be hot enough to create a charred aroma. Mix and eat immediately after turning off the heat. 

## Health Advice 
Beef is a high-protein, low-fat, high-quality ingredient. Paired with spiced oil and scallion shreds, it is suitable for muscle building or blood sugar control groups. Note that no water should be added during marination to achieve a charred aroma when stir-frying. Although spiced oil enhances flavor, it is relatively high in calories, so moderate use is recommended.
