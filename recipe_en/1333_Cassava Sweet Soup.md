# Cassava Sweet Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Yellow cassava 500g (peeled net weight, be sure to use improved edible varieties)  
🟡 Yellow slab sugar 100g  
🔸 Yellow rock sugar 20g  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🍲 Non-stick pot (for stewing)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Pressure cooker (optional, for reducing cooking time)  

## Instructions
1. Prepare the cassava: Wash the cassava, then use a knife to cut open the outer skin. Lift it with the knife tip, then use your thumb to peel off the outermost brown skin along with the white or light yellow hard "skin" layer tightly attached to the inner flesh, retaining only the cassava flesh.  
**Note**: Both layers of skin must be completely removed, otherwise it will affect the texture of the finished product.
2. Cut the peeled cassava into evenly sized chunks.
3. Place the cassava chunks in a pot with cold water, and add the yellow slab sugar and yellow rock sugar at the same time.
4. Add enough water to completely submerge the cassava.
5. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer slowly for about 1.5 hours, until the soup is thick and the cassava is translucent and golden yellow.  
**Key point**: Using a pressure cooker can save time; after turning off the heat, cover the pot and let it rest for a while longer, and the soup will become thicker and stickier, giving the cassava a more chewy and resilient texture.

## Health Advice  
Cassava sweet soup is rich in dietary fiber and carbohydrates, making it suitable for replenishing energy. However, it has a high sugar content, so diabetics or those controlling their sugar intake should only have a small taste. Cassava must be thoroughly peeled and fully cooked, otherwise traces of toxins may remain.
