# Geleshan Spicy Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Whole chicken 1 (chopped into fingertip-sized pieces)
🌶️ Dried chili peppers appropriate amount (mixed Erjingtiao, Zidantou, and Xinyidai varieties)
🧄 Scallion sections appropriate amount (for marinating and making spiced oil)
🫚 Ginger shreds appropriate amount (for marinating)
🧅 Ginger slices, garlic appropriate amount (for making spiced oil)
🌿 Spices appropriate amount (Angelica dahurica, star anise, white cardamom, cassia bark, bay leaves)
🧅 Scallions appropriate amount (finely chopped, sprinkled in before serving)
🫘 White sesame seeds appropriate amount (sprinkled in before serving)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for marinating)
🍾 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for marinating)
🌾 Cornstarch appropriate amount (for marinating)
🫒 Rapeseed oil appropriate amount (generous amount, for frying spices and stir-frying)
🥃 Baijiu (Chinese liquor) a small amount (added when soaking chili peppers)
🥫 Soy sauce a small amount (for seasoning and coloring in the later stage of cooking)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🥄 Slotted spoon
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board
🔥 Stove

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare the chicken**: Wash the whole chicken, chop it into fingertip-sized pieces, allowing for quick flavor absorption and the formation of a crispy texture.
2. **Marinate the chicken**: Add salt, cooking wine, scallion sections, and ginger shreds to the chicken pieces and mix well. Then add cornstarch and mix evenly. Finally, drizzle in a little cooking oil, mix to seal in moisture, and let it marinate.
**Key point**: Sealing with oil locks in moisture, making the chicken more tender.
3. **Prepare the chili peppers**: Mix three types of dried chili peppers together, soak in clean water for a short while, drain, and set aside.
**Key point**: Soaking prevents them from scorching too quickly during frying and helps release their aroma. Add a small amount of Baijiu when soaking the peppers to stimulate aroma.
4. **Fry the spiced oil**: Pour a generous amount of rapeseed oil into the wok. Add scallions, ginger, garlic, as well as Angelica dahurica, star anise, white cardamom, cassia bark, and bay leaves. Fry over medium-low heat slowly until the spices darken in color and release a rich aroma. Remove and discard the spices.
**Key point**: This provides the base aroma for the dish. The heat should not be too high to avoid burning.
5. **Stir-fry the chicken**: Pour the marinated chicken pieces into the spiced oil. Stir-fry over medium-high heat, continuously turning, until the chicken is cooked through, the surface is golden, and the texture is dry and fragrant.
**Key point**: The moisture in the chicken must be fully released to achieve the "dry-fried" texture, which is the core of spicy chicken.
6. **Add the chili peppers and stir-fry together**: Pour in the drained soaked chili peppers and stir-fry together with the chicken. Allow the peppers to slowly dehydrate and become crispy in the wok, with their aroma fully infusing the chicken.
**Key point**: With the limited heat of a home stove, this method allows the peppers and chicken to cook evenly, preventing the peppers from burning or the chicken from being undercooked.
7. **Season**: Drizzle in a little soy sauce and stir-fry quickly to evenly distribute, adding depth of flavor and color.
**Note**: Do not use too much soy sauce to prevent the color from becoming too dark or the dish from being too salty.
8. **Serve**: When the chili peppers become crispy, sprinkle in white sesame seeds and chopped scallions. Stir-fry quickly to combine, then immediately remove from the wok and plate.
**Key point**: Sesame seeds and scallions are added last to preserve their aroma and bright color.

## Health Advice
Spicy chicken is rich in protein but has a high oil content; it is recommended to pair with light vegetables to balance the calorie intake. Suitable for those needing protein supplementation and those who prefer rich flavors. Cutting the chicken into small pieces makes it easier for the flavor to penetrate, but attention should be paid to controlling the oil temperature to avoid the outside being burnt while the inside remains raw.
