# Quick-Scored Dry Tofu Strips  
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Dried tofu skin, about 2 sheets (cut into slightly thick and long strips)  
🥕 Carrot, about half a root (julienned, for color)  
🧅 Purple onion, about half (remove core and julienne)  
🌶️ Green chili pepper, about 1-2 (remove veins and julienne, optional)  
🌿 Cilantro, to taste (cut into sections)  
🧄 Garlic, 4-5 cloves (smash into minced garlic)  
🧂 Haitian premium soy sauce, 15g  
🫒 Sesame oil, 5g  
🌶️ Dried red chili peppers, about a small handful  
🥜 Peanut oil, about 2-3 tablespoons  
🧂 Salt, 4g  
🍬 White sugar, 18g  
🍶 Hengshun 5.5-degree fragrant vinegar, 11g  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Iron wok/stir-fry pan (for splashing hot oil)  
🍳 Nonstick skillet (for blanching dried tofu strips)  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (for cooling, squeezing dry, and mixing)  
🥣 Small bowl (for holding prepared sauce)  
🥄 Spatula, ladle, chopsticks  
🧤 Disposable gloves (for mixing by hand)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Cut the dried tofu skin into slightly thick and long strips.  
**Key point**: Strips should not be too thin to avoid breaking during blanching and mixing.  

2. Julienne the carrot; remove the core of the purple onion and julienne; remove the veins of the green chili pepper and julienne finely; cut the cilantro into sections; smash the garlic into minced garlic.  
**Key point**: Choosing onions and chili peppers with natural pungent flavors enhances the dish's flavor profile.  

3. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, place the minced garlic at the bottom, add 15g of premium soy sauce and 5g of sesame oil, then lay the dried red chili peppers on top.  
**Key point**: Minced garlic at the bottom, dried chili peppers on top, so they are fragrant when splashed with hot oil.  

4. Add enough cold water to a pot, put in the dried tofu strips and carrot strips, and boil until the dried tofu strips soften.  
**Key point**: Start with cold water; ensure plenty of water; **absolutely do not add alkaline water**.  

5. Remove the blanched dried tofu strips and carrot strips, place them in cold water to cool, then take them out and gently squeeze out excess water by hand.  
**Key point**: Cooling maintains texture; squeeze gently to avoid crushing the dried tofu strips.  

6. Heat peanut oil in a pan to about 200°C or above, until it just starts to smoke, then pour the hot oil over the dried chili peppers in the bowl.  
**State check**: The dried chili peppers sizzle when hit by hot oil, turn brownish-red, and release their fragrance.  

7. Put the squeezed-dry dried tofu strips into a large bowl, add 4g of salt, 18g of white sugar, and 11g of fragrant vinegar. Then pour in the prepared seasoned oil. Finally, add the julienned onion, green chili pepper, and cilantro sections.  
8. Wear disposable gloves and mix all ingredients and seasonings evenly by hand.  
**Key point**: Use your fingers to lift and toss, ensuring even distribution of seasonings and that every dried tofu strip is coated with the combined flavors.  

## Health Tips  
This dish is rich in plant-based protein and dietary fiber, suitable for satisfying cravings during weight loss. Onion and green chili pepper are warming and appetizing, but those with sensitive stomachs should reduce the chili peppers. Note that dried tofu strips should not be cut too thin, and after blanching, squeeze gently to avoid breaking.
