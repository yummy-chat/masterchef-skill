# Lard
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork leaf fat 1500g (cut into small pieces)  
💧 Water 300g  
🧅 Scallion 30g (cut into sections)  
🫚 Ginger 30g (sliced)  
🫘 Soybeans appropriate amount (dry-fried before use, optional)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (add based on intended use)  
🍬 White sugar appropriate amount (optional, for specific seasoning)

## Cookware and Equipment
🍲 Soup pot  
🍳 Wok or frying pan (non-stick or iron)  
🥣 Large bowl or enamel basin  
🥄 Slotted spoon or fine mesh strainer  
🕸️ Finer high-end sieve  
🥄 Spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🍳 Cast iron pan (for stir-frying soybeans)

## Steps
1. Place the pork leaf fat in cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and cook for about 2 minutes. Remove and rinse thoroughly under running water, then drain well.  
   **Key point**: Blanching completely removes blood and impurities, which is the basis for lard that is not fishy.

2. Put the cleaned pork leaf fat into the wok, add 300g of water, and bring to a boil over high heat.  
   **Key point**: The water-rendering method prevents the oil temperature from getting too high and causing discoloration.

3. Once boiling, reduce to medium-low heat and continue rendering while stirring constantly. When the liquid in the pot changes from milky white to clear and transparent, and most of the water has evaporated, the oil will begin to release.

4. As soon as the oil becomes clear, immediately turn the heat to the lowest setting. Add the scallion sections and ginger slices. Continue rendering while using a spoon to scoop out the clear oil, passing it through a slotted spoon and strainer into a container.  
   **Key point**: Skim the oil continuously during rendering to achieve snow-white lard; the scallion and ginger remove odor and enhance aroma.

5. When the cracklings in the pot turn a light golden yellow and slightly browned, immediately turn off the heat to avoid over-rendering, which would make the lard yellow and bitter.

6. Pour out the remaining cracklings, use chopsticks to remove and discard the dried scallions and ginger. The cracklings can be reserved for other uses.

7. For greater purity, strain the collected lard a second time through a finer sieve.

8. Dry-fry the soybeans in a dry pan until small black spots appear on the surface, then pour them into the hot lard. This is said to remove any off-flavors. Depending on the final use, add salt or sugar as desired, or leave plain.

9. Let cool and rest. Once solidified, the lard will be snow-white and smooth in texture, ready for use.

## Health Tips
Although lard adds wonderful flavor, it is high in saturated fat. It is recommended to consume in moderation alongside vegetables, using it occasionally to enhance taste rather than as a daily staple. Controlling the temperature and removing odors are key during preparation; the snow-white version is ideal for home-style dishes where appearance matters.
