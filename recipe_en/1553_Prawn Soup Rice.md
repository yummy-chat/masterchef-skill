# Prawn Soup Rice
Cuisine: Cantonese
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All year round

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍤 Fresh prawns (some for making soup, some reserved as prawn meat)
🥩 Beef (diced into small pieces)
🍄 Mushrooms (cut into small pieces)
🍚 Crispy rice (pre-fried or fry immediately before use)
🍚 Cooked rice
🌿 Coriander/celery (finely chopped)
🧂 Salt
🫒 Cooking oil (for stir-frying and frying crispy rice)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🍳 Wok
🍲 Soup pot
🥄 Strainer
🔪 Knife and cutting board
🥄 Slotted spoon
🥘 Frying pan or small saucepan (for frying crispy rice)
🍽️ Bowls and plates

## Preparation Steps
1. Make prawn soup: Peel the fresh prawns and set the meat aside. Blanch some prawns in boiling water, then crush them with the back of a knife. Put the crushed prawns into a soup pot, add boiling water, and simmer. After cooking, strain through a strainer to obtain a pure prawn soup. Set aside.
2. Prepare ingredients: Dice the beef into small pieces, cut the mushrooms into small pieces, finely chop the coriander and celery, and have the crispy rice ready.
3. Stir-fry the ingredients: Heat oil in a wok, add the mushroom pieces, prawn meat, and beef pieces. Stir-fry until all ingredients are cooked and the beef is fully done. Remove and set aside.
4. Cook the rice: Using the remaining oil in the wok, pour in the prawn soup and bring to a boil. Season with salt. Add the stir-fried ingredients and cooked rice, and heat through until hot.
**Key point**: Only heat the rice through; do not boil for too long, otherwise it will turn into congee.

5. Enhance aroma: Add the finely chopped coriander and celery. Turn off the heat as soon as the soup comes to a boil.
6. Fry the crispy rice: In a separate small pan, heat oil and add the crispy rice. Fry until golden and puffed up. When the rice grains “pop,” quickly remove them to prevent burning.
7. Serve: Ladle the cooked rice soup into bowls. Just before serving, sprinkle the fried crispy rice on top. Eat while hot to experience the layering of softness and crunch as the crispy rice absorbs the soup.

## Health Advice
This prawn soup rice is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for people who need an energy boost. Be mindful of the amount of oil used for the crispy rice to avoid excess calories. The prawn soup is low in fat and easy to digest, but its sodium content may be relatively high, so those with high blood pressure should consume it in moderation.
