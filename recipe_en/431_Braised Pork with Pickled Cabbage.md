# Braised Pork with Pickled Cabbage  
Cuisine: Northeast Chinese Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All-Season Meal  

## Essential Ingredients and Tools  
🥩 **Ingredients**  
- Pork belly or pork ribs  
- Northeast pickled cabbage (sauerkraut)  
- Scallions  
- Ginger  
- Garlic  

🧂 **Seasonings**  
- Salt  
- Light soy sauce  
- Five-spice powder  
- Cooking wine  
- Star anise  

🍳 **Kitchen Tools**  
- Pot  
- Spatula  
- Knife  
- Cutting board  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Cut scallions into sections; take 50g ginger and cut into sections, mince the remaining 50g; mince garlic, set aside.  
2. Peel pickled cabbage layer by layer, cut into thin strips, rinse with clean water 2–3 times, drain and set aside.  
**Note**: Adjust rinsing times based on personal taste—rinse twice if you prefer sour, three to four times if you prefer less sour. Do not skip rinsing, or it will be too sour.  
3. Place ribs and pork belly in a pot, add cold water until fully submerged. Add all scallion sections, 50g ginger sections, and 20ml cooking wine. Bring to a boil over high heat, keep boiling for 5 minutes, then turn off the heat. Remove ribs and pork belly, rinse off surface foam with cold water, drain and set aside.  
4. Cut the cooked pork belly into thick slices or chunks, set aside.  
5. Wash and dry the previously used pot to prevent oil splatter when heating.  
6. Pour oil into the pot, heat over medium heat. Add minced ginger and garlic, stir-fry until fragrant. Add pork belly slices and ribs, fry until golden brown. Sprinkle in 10g five-spice powder and 15g light soy sauce, stir-fry with a spatula for 1–2 minutes.  
7. Add the rinsed pickled cabbage strips to the pot, continue stir-frying for 3 minutes.  
8. Pour in purified water until it just covers all ingredients. Add 2 star anise, turn to high heat and bring to a boil. Then reduce to medium heat, cover with a lid, and simmer.  
9. Simmer for 1.5–2 hours until the pork belly is tender.  
10. Remove the lid, turn to high heat to reduce the sauce. Continuously stir the ingredients in the pot until only a thin layer of sauce remains at the bottom. Reduce to low heat for seasoning.  
**Note**: To prevent sticking, stir occasionally and check water levels.  
11. Season: Add 10g salt, stir well.  
**Tip**: Add salt gradually in small amounts, tasting each time until the saltiness is right.  
12. Turn off the heat, serve and plate.  

## Health Advice  
Braised pork with pickled cabbage is rich in protein and dietary fiber, suitable for times of high physical exertion or cold weather. However, pork belly has high fat content; it is recommended to pair with whole grains or vegetables to balance nutrition. Blanching the pickled cabbage can reduce salt content and acidity; those with high blood pressure should use less salt.
