# Microwave Steamed Egg Custard
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥚 Eggs
💧 Water
🦐 Dried shrimp (optional)
🧅 Scallion (optional)
🧂 Salt
🍶 Soy sauce (optional)
🛢️ Sesame oil (optional)

## Utensils and Equipment
🥣 Microwave-safe ceramic bowl
🥢 Chopsticks
🔪 Knife
🍽️ Ceramic plate or plastic wrap
🧤 Heat-resistant gloves or towel

## Steps
1.  Crack the eggs into a microwave-safe ceramic bowl and whisk thoroughly with chopsticks until the egg mixture is even. **Key point**: Make sure to whisk evenly, otherwise localized boiling may occur during heating.
2.  Add water and salt, and stir until the salt is completely dissolved. Note that the total volume of egg mixture and water should not exceed two-thirds of the bowl's capacity to prevent overflowing. **Key point**: A single egg weighs about 50 grams; adjust the water amount according to this ratio.
3.  If adding dried shrimp, place them in the bowl and mix well, ensuring they are evenly distributed.
4.  If adding scallion, finely chop the scallion, then add to the bowl and stir evenly.
5.  Cover the bowl mouth with plastic wrap or place a ceramic plate on top, then put it in the microwave and heat at 500 watts for 2 minutes.
6.  Carefully remove the cover, then continue heating for another 2 minutes.
7.  If the microwave does not have a turntable, rotate the bowl 180 degrees horizontally during the heating interval to ensure even heating. **Note**: If making multiple portions, it is recommended to do them in batches, with no more than 3 portions at a time.
8.  Drizzle with sesame oil.
9.  Carefully remove the bowl using heat-resistant gloves.
10. If adding soy sauce, gently drizzle it over the surface to cover evenly.
11. Serve and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Steamed egg custard is an excellent source of high-quality protein. When steamed with water, it is low in calories and easy to digest, making it especially suitable for elderly people and children to supplement nutrition. When using plastic wrap, leave some gaps to prevent splattering; since dried shrimp are naturally salty, the amount of salt should be reduced accordingly.
