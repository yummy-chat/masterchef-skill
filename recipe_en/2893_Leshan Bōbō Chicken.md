# Leshan Bōbō Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Skill Level: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 1 year-old chicken (boiled, air-dried, cut into finger-wide strips)
🦶 Chicken feet, as needed (deboned)
🍢 Other skewer ingredients (chicken skin, tripe, vegetables, etc., as desired)
🌶️ Erjingtiao chili flakes (fine, medium, coarse mixed)
🌶️ Bullethead chili flakes (about twice the amount of Erjingtiao)
🌿 Bay leaves, as needed
🍂 White cardamom, as needed
⭐ Star anise, as needed
🧈 Rapeseed oil (double the amount of chili flakes)
🧅 2 scallions
🫚 Ginger, as needed
🌰 Sand ginger, as needed
🍷 Huadiao wine, as needed
🧂 6 tablespoons salt
🍬 5 tablespoons sugar
🦪 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
🥫 5 tablespoons light soy sauce
🍲 6 tablespoons fresh broth

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🍲 Stockpot
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef's knife
🪓 Cutting board
🥄 Ladle
🥢 Mixing spoon
🥣 Large soup bowl
🪣 Stainless steel basin

## Cooking Steps
1. **Boil the chicken**: Place the whole chicken in a pot of cold water. Add scallions, ginger, sand ginger, star anise, and Huadiao wine. Bring to a boil over high heat and keep boiling for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat, cover, and let steep for 20 minutes. Remove the chicken and air-dry in a ventilated area until cool, then cut into finger-wide strips.
**Key point**: Use a year-old chicken for better flavor; be sure to air-dry after boiling for crispier skin.

2. **Prepare chili oil**: Mix Erjingtiao and Bullethead chili flakes in a 1:2 ratio. Add bay leaves, white cardamom, and star anise. Heat rapeseed oil to 210°C until fragrant, then turn off the heat. Let it sit for 3–5 minutes to cool to about 120°C. Pour the oil into the chili mixture in batches, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
**Note**: For best flavor, let the chili oil sit overnight before use.

3. **Prepare other ingredients**: Debone chicken feet using a "tendon-separating" technique. Clean tripe, vegetables, and other ingredients, then skewer them.
4. **Marinate**: Submerge all skewered ingredients in the chili oil broth. Let them soak for about ten minutes before serving.
**Key point**: The broth should have "more oil, less chili"; ensure ingredients are fully soaked for flavor—crispy chicken skin and odorless innards.

## Health Tips
Bōbō chicken is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for fitness and calorie-conscious individuals. However, the chili oil is high in calories, so it is recommended to control the amount of dipping broth. Crispy chicken skin and odorless innards require fresh ingredients; use high-salt sauces sparingly.
