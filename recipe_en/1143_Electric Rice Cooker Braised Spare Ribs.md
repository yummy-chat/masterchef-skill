# Electric Rice Cooker Braised Spare Ribs
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🥩 Pork spare ribs, appropriate amount (about one bowl)
🫚 Ginger, 5–6 slices (enough to cover the bottom of the pot)
🌿 Scallion sections, 2–3 pieces
⭐ Star anise, 2 pieces
🍶 Fermented bean curd (red variety), 1 piece
🧂 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
🧂 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon
🍶 Vinegar, 3 tablespoons
🍬 White sugar, 4 tablespoons
🍺 Beer, 1 can (about 330ml)
🧂 Salt, appropriate amount (adjust to taste after reducing the sauce)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🥣 Stainless steel basin (for soaking and washing the ribs)
🍚 Electric rice cooker/electric pressure cooker
🍳 Wok/frying pan
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer
🥄 Spoon

## Preparation Steps
1. Soak the spare ribs in clean water for 1 hour to allow them to fully release blood water, then rinse clean and squeeze out the excess water.
2. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, combine the fermented bean curd, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, vinegar, and white sugar. Pour in the beer, then crush the fermented bean curd and stir until well blended.
3. Line the bottom of the electric rice cooker’s inner pot with ginger slices. Place the prepared ribs inside, pour in the prepared sauce so it mostly covers the ribs, then add the scallion sections and star anise.
4. Start the rice cooking program on the electric rice cooker and simmer until the ribs are tender and fully cooked.
5. After the program ends, pour the ribs together with the sauce into a slotted spoon and strain into a wok. Remove and discard the ginger slices, scallion sections, and star anise.
6. Cook over medium heat to reduce the sauce, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. When the sauce gradually thickens and evenly coats the ribs, turning a deep red and glossy color, taste and add an appropriate amount of salt as needed.
7. Transfer the ribs to a serving plate and drizzle the remaining sauce from the wok on top.

## Health Advice
This electric rice cooker braised spare rib recipe uses beer and fermented bean curd as substitutes for traditional braising methods, resulting in tender and flavorful meat that is convenient and delicious for those seeking ease. Soaking to remove blood water helps reduce purine content, but individuals with high uric acid levels are advised to consume less of the sauce.
