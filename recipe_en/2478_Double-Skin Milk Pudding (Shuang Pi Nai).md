# Double-Skin Milk Pudding (Shuang Pi Nai)
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥛 Pure milk 500g (whole milk recommended)  
🥚 Eggs 3 (egg whites only, discard yolks)  
🫘 Red beans 200g (for making sweetened red bean paste)  
🍓 Strawberries appropriate amount (for surface decoration)  
🥭 Mango appropriate amount (for surface decoration)  
🍬 White sugar approx. 70g (to mix into egg-milk mixture)  
🍬 Rock sugar appropriate amount (to sweeten when cooking red beans)  

## Cookware & Equipment  
🍲 Stainless steel wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🥣 Small white porcelain bowls  
🥣 Large glass bowl  
🫗 Fine mesh strainer/sieve  
♨️ Steamer and steaming rack  
🥄 Soup spoon  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Instructions
**Preparation Phase**
1. Pour 500g pure milk into a pot and heat until boiling. Maintain a boil for 1–2 minutes, allowing moisture to evaporate and protein concentration to increase, which helps form a thick milk skin later.
2. Divide the boiled milk into small bowls and let it cool until a complete milk skin forms on the surface.
3. Using chopsticks, poke a small hole at the edge of the milk skin. Slowly pour the milk from the bowl into a separate container, leaving a small amount in the bowl to prevent the skin from sticking.
4. Prepare sweetened red bean paste: Place 200g red beans, an appropriate amount of rock sugar, and about 500g water into the pressure cooker. After it reaches pressure, cook for 10 minutes. Allow to naturally cool, then open the lid. Return to heat and reduce the liquid until thick. Set aside.
5. Place 3 egg whites into a large bowl. Add approx. 70g white sugar to the reserved milk and stir until dissolved. Gently pour the sweetened milk into the egg whites and stir lightly to combine, minimizing air bubbles.
**Steaming Phase**
1. Strain the egg-milk mixture through a fine mesh sieve 1–2 times to remove any unwhipped egg white strands and foam, ensuring a smooth texture.
2. Slowly pour the strained egg-milk mixture back into the small bowls along the edge until 80% full, allowing the first layer of milk skin to float intact.
3. Once the steamer water has come to a boil, place the small bowls on the steaming rack. Reduce to low heat and steam for approximately 15 minutes. Keep the heat low throughout; no need to cover the bowls with plastic wrap.
4. After steaming, remove and let cool slightly. Top with the sweetened red bean paste, or with chopped strawberries and mango pieces.

## Health Advice  
It is recommended to use whole milk to achieve a thicker milk skin. When mixing the egg whites, stir gently to avoid bubbles that may compromise the smooth texture. This dessert is suitable for all seasons and especially beneficial for those needing extra calcium and protein. Diabetic patients should monitor sugar intake.
