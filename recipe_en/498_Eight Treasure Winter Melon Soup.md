# Eight Treasure Winter Melon Soup
Cuisine: Cantonese
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🍈 1 mini winter melon (serves as container and main ingredient)  
🥩 Pork shank meat, to taste (part cut into cubes, part for initial steaming broth)  
🐚 Conpoy (dried scallops), to taste (part for initial steaming broth, part for garnish)  
🫚 2 slices ginger (for removing fishy smell during initial steaming)  
🥓 Ham, to taste (minced, for final garnish)  
🍄 Fresh shiitake mushrooms, to taste (diced)  
🥒 Loofah, to taste (diced)  
🦆 Roast goose, to taste (diced)  
🐔 Chicken gizzards, to taste (diced)  
🍤 Shrimp, to taste  
🪷 Lotus seeds, to taste (dried, hulled, pre-steamed until soft)  
🧂 Salt, a pinch (for seasoning during initial steaming)  
🧂 Chicken bouillon, a pinch (optional, for final seasoning)

## Kitchenware and Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife  
🥄 Spoon  
♨️ Steamer  
🍳 Wok  
🥣 Strainer  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍽️ Plate

## Preparation Steps
1. Cut off the top of the mini winter melon, remove all seeds and pulp to create a hollow melon cup. You may carve decorative patterns on the rim. Reserve the cut-off top for use as a base.
**Key Point**: Keeping the melon top ensures the cup sits steadily.

2. Place 2 slices of pork shank meat, 2 conpoy, and 2 slices of ginger inside the melon cup. Add a small amount of water and a pinch of salt as the initial steaming broth.
3. Place the melon cup into the steamer and steam for approximately **45 minutes**, until the melon flesh softens and the broth releases its savory flavor.
**Doneness Check**: The melon flesh should be noticeably softened upon completion of initial steaming.

4. Prepare the remaining ingredients: Dice the pork shank meat, chicken gizzards, shrimp, roast goose, fresh shiitake mushrooms, and loofah into uniform small cubes. Pre-steam the lotus seeds with water for **40 minutes** until soft.
**Key Point**: Roast goose is cooked, **do not blanch**, to preserve its roasted flavor.

5. Bring water to a boil in a wok. Blanch the cubed pork, chicken gizzards, and shrimp for 1–2 minutes, then drain.
**Purpose**: To remove raw blood and off-flavors.

6. Strain the steamed broth from the melon cup through a sieve into the wok, reserving it. Keep the melon cup intact for later use.
7. Add the diced shiitake mushrooms, loofah, blanched pork cubes, chicken gizzards, shrimp, roast goose cubes, and steamed lotus seeds to the reserved broth. Bring to a boil over medium heat and cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
**Seasoning**: Taste and add a pinch of chicken bouillon if desired; it can be omitted to highlight the natural freshness of the ingredients.

8. Carefully ladle all the cooked soup and ingredients back into the melon cup. Evenly sprinkle a small amount of minced ham on top.
**Key Point**: Assemble while keeping the melon cup's shape intact; do not overfill with soup ingredients.

## Health Advice
This Eight Treasure Winter Melon Soup helps clear heat and relieve summer heat, making it suitable for summer consumption. Roast goose and conpoy are high in sodium, so individuals with high blood pressure should consume the broth in moderation. The mini winter melon cup is low-calorie and eye-catching, but be mindful of portion sizes to avoid overconsumption.
