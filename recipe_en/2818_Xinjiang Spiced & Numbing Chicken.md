# Xinjiang Spiced & Numbing Chicken
Cuisine: Xinjiang Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh 1 piece (main ingredient)  
🧅 Onion, to taste (shredded)  
🌶️ Long green chili peppers, to taste (cut into small sections)  
🫘 Tofu skin, to taste (cut into strips)  
🫘 Dried bean curd sticks, to taste (cut into sections)  
🧂 Sichuan peppercorn powder, to taste (for marinating)  
🧂 Salt, to taste (for marinating and seasoning)  
🫚 Sichuan peppercorns, to taste (for spice water)  
🫚 Numbing peppercorns, to taste (for spice water)  
🫚 Cinnamon stick, to taste (for spice water)  
⭐ Star anise, to taste (for spice water)  
🍃 Bay leaves, to taste (for spice water)  
🧄 Scallion segments, to taste (for spice water)  
🫚 Ginger slices, to taste (for spice water)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, to taste (for spice water)  
🫘 Red dates, to taste (for spice water)  
🫒 Sesame oil, generous amount (for final seasoning, key ingredient)  

## Kitchenware & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍲 Stockpot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  

## Instructions
1. **Marinate the chicken**: Wash the chicken thigh. Evenly sprinkle with Sichuan peppercorn powder and salt. Massage thoroughly, then marinate for 30 minutes to absorb flavor.
2. **Prepare the side ingredients**: Shred the onion. Cut the long green chili peppers into small sections. Cut the tofu skin and dried bean curd sticks into strips or sections for easy eating.

3. **Simmer the spiced broth**: Add water to a pot. Add the Sichuan peppercorns, numbing peppercorns, cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, scallion segments, ginger slices, dried chili peppers, and red dates. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 5 minutes until fragrant.
- Key point: The spices must be simmered first to release their aroma, making the broth rich and flavorful.

4. **Cook the chicken**: Place the marinated chicken thigh into the boiling spiced broth. Cover with a lid and cook over medium heat for 20 minutes.
- Doneness check: The chicken is fully cooked when a chopstick can easily pierce through the thickest part and no blood oozes out.

5. **Prepare the broth and side ingredients**: Remove the chicken thigh and set aside to cool. Strain all the spice residue from the broth. Use the hot, clear broth to quickly blanch the tofu skin and dried bean curd sticks. Remove them and lay them at the bottom of a serving bowl.
- Key point: The blanching time should be short to maintain the texture of the soy products; the broth must be completely free of spice residue.

6. **Assemble and season**: Place the shredded onion and long green chili pepper sections on top of the tofu skin and dried bean curd sticks in the bowl. Immediately pour the piping hot spiced chicken broth over the ingredients to soak them. Then, generously pour in a sufficient amount of sesame oil. Finally, sprinkle a little salt to taste.
- Key point: The broth must be poured while boiling hot to release the aroma of the vegetables; the amount of sesame oil is the soul of the flavor and must be ample.

7. **Final combination**: Tear the cooled chicken thigh meat from the bone. Place the shredded meat into the bowl. Use your hands to thoroughly mix and massage the chicken with all the ingredients and broth, ensuring the meat is fully coated and has absorbed the flavors. Serve immediately.

## Health Tips
This dish is rich in high-quality protein and dietary fiber, making it suitable for fitness and weight loss diets. However, the amounts of sesame oil and salt should be controlled; people with high blood pressure should be cautious. Tearing the chicken after cooking and then mixing it can reduce oil intake and make it easier for the meat to absorb flavor.
