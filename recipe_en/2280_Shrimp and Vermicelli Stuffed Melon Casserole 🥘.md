# Shrimp and Vermicelli Stuffed Melon Casserole 🥘  
**Cuisine:** Cantonese  
**Cooking Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐  
**Seasonal Recommendation:** Suitable for all seasons 🌿  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥒 1 angled luffa (about 400g, cut into thick strips)  
🍤 30g dried shrimp (rinsed and soaked, water reserved)  
🍜 1 pack Longkou vermicelli (about 100g, soaked in cold or warm water until soft)  
🫑 20g red bell pepper (julienned for color)  
🧅 2 shallots (minced)  
🧄 3 cloves garlic (minced)  
🫚 1 small piece ginger (minced)  
🥩 30g fatty pork, julienned  
🧅 1 scallion (chopped for garnish)  
🫒 2 tbsp lard  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🍬 Sugar, to taste  
🥄 2 tbsp light soy sauce  
🥄 1 tbsp oyster sauce  
🌶️ A pinch of white pepper  
🥄 A dash of dark soy sauce  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🍲 Clay pot (砂锅)  
🥄 Soup ladle  
🍳 Wok spatula  
🔥 Lighter (for testing vermicelli quality, optional)  

## Cooking Steps  
1. Rinse dried shrimp and soak in water for about 1 hour. Reserve the soaking water.  
   **Note:** Vermicelli should be soaked in cold or warm water in advance until soft, then drained. Peel the angled luffa and cut into thick strips about the thickness of chopsticks to prevent overcooking.  

2. Heat the clay pot over high heat, add lard, then add the julienned fatty pork. Stir-fry over medium-low heat until the fat renders.  
3. Add minced shallots, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry until fragrant, then add the soaked dried shrimp. Continue stir-frying until the aroma is released.  
4. Add the angled luffa strips and stir-fry briefly. Pour in all the reserved shrimp soaking water. If the liquid does not cover the ingredients, add enough water to submerge them. Turn the heat to high and bring to a boil.  
   **Key Point:** Add enough liquid at once, as the vermicelli will absorb a lot of broth later.  

5. Once the broth is boiling, season with salt, sugar, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white pepper. Stir well, cover the pot, and reduce the heat to medium. Simmer for 6–7 minutes.  
   **Cooking Check:** The angled luffa strips should be tender but still hold their shape.  

6. Uncover and add the soaked and drained vermicelli. Quickly toss with chopsticks or a spatula to fully submerge the vermicelli in the broth. Keep the heat at medium.  
7. Drizzle in a little dark soy sauce for color. Continue tossing and cooking for about 2–3 minutes, until the vermicelli has absorbed the broth, the liquid is thickened, and the color is even and glossy.  
8. Sprinkle with julienned red bell pepper and chopped scallion. Toss briefly using residual heat until just wilted. Turn off the heat and serve directly from the clay pot.  

## Health Tips  
This dish features dried shrimp and angled luffa as a low-fat, high-fiber combination. However, the vermicelli, once soaked in the broth, becomes a calorie bomb, so it’s wise to control your rice intake. Suitable for those needing calcium or flavor enhancement, but people with high blood pressure should use oyster sauce and soy sauce sparingly. 🥗
