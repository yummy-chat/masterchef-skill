# Curry Pork Cutlet Rice
Cuisine: Common home-style dish  
Cooking difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potato 250g (cut into small pieces)  
🥕 Carrot 200g (cut into small pieces)  
🧅 Onion 150g (about half, cut into small pieces)  
🥩 Pork loin 750g (slice against the grain into about 1cm thick pieces)  
🥚 Eggs as needed (whole egg liquid, for coating pork cutlets)  
🍞 Breadcrumbs as needed  
🌾 Flour as needed  
🧅 Scallion half a palm length (shredded, for scallion-ginger water)  
🫚 Ginger 4-5 slices (for scallion-ginger water)  
🍚 Rice as needed  

**Curry Seasoning**  
🍯 Light soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🍅 Tomato paste 2 tbsp (can substitute with tomato sauce or ketchup)  
🍬 White sugar 2 pinches  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 1 pinch  
🍛 Curry block for 2 servings  

**Pork Cutlet Marinade**  
🍯 Light soy sauce 4 tbsp  
🍬 White sugar half tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1-2 tbsp  
🧂 Salt 2 pinches  
⚫ Black pepper 2 pinches  
⚪ White pepper 1 pinch  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2 pinches  
🌿 Scallion-ginger-Sichuan pepper-baizhi water about 100-200ml (use after cooling)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed (for pan-frying/deep-frying)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🍳 Frying pan (two)  
🔥 Gas stove/induction cooker  
🔪 Cutting board, kitchen knife  
🥄 Spatula, tongs, strainer  
⚖️ Measuring spoon, measuring cup  
🥣 Bowls, plates, basins  
🔨 Meat tenderizer or heavy knife back  
🍴 Utensils  

## Cooking Steps  
### Preparation  
1. Cut the potato, carrot, and onion into cubes about 1-2 cm on each side.  
2. Slice the pork loin against the grain into pieces about 1 cm thick. Make a few cuts on the edge of the fascia. Pound both sides thoroughly with a meat tenderizer or the back of a knife to flatten and loosen the meat.  
3. Shred the scallion and slice the ginger. Place them in a bowl with a small pinch of Sichuan pepper and 2 slices of baizhi. Pour about 100-200ml of boiling water over them and let cool to make scallion-ginger water.  
4. Prepare three containers, each filled with flour, beaten whole egg liquid, and breadcrumbs.  

### Making the Curry Sauce  
1. Heat a pan over medium heat, add an appropriate amount of oil, and stir-fry the onion until fragrant.  
2. Add the potato and carrot cubes, and stir-fry for about 1 minute.  
3. Reduce to low heat, add 2 tbsp light soy sauce, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 2 pinches white sugar, and 1 pinch chicken bouillon. Stir well to combine.  
4. Pour in enough water to just cover the ingredients, add the curry block, and gently stir to prevent sticking.  
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the sauce thickens.  

### Making the Fried Pork Cutlet  
1. **Marinate**: Add all the marinade seasonings to the pork cutlets, pour in the cooled scallion-ginger-Sichuan pepper-baizhi water, and knead thoroughly to incorporate. Marinate for 20-30 minutes.  
2. **Coating**:  
   - First, evenly coat each pork cutlet with a layer of flour, shaking off any excess.  
   - Dip into the egg liquid, making sure the surface is fully covered.  
   - Place into the breadcrumbs, pressing firmly to ensure the crumbs adhere well. For a thicker, crispier crust, let rest for 3-5 minutes, then repeat the "egg liquid + breadcrumbs" step once.  
3. **Pan-frying**: Add oil about 2-3 times the amount used for stir-frying to a pan. Heat over high heat until about 5-6 hot, then reduce to low heat. Add the pork cutlets. Do not move them for the first 30 seconds, then flip every 30-40 seconds. Total frying time is about 7-8 minutes, until both sides are golden and crispy.  
   **Note**: If deep-frying, heat a generous amount of oil, add the pork cutlets, reduce to low heat, and gently stir with chopsticks to prevent sticking. Fry for about 7 minutes.  
4. **Double-fry**: Remove the pork cutlets. Increase the heat to high and heat the oil for about 40 seconds. Return the pork cutlets for a second fry of about ten seconds to make the crust even crispier.  
5. **Plating**: Cut the fried pork cutlets into strips, arrange beside the rice, and top with the curry sauce.  

## Health Advice  
This curry pork cutlet rice is rich in protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for consumption after exercise or for those with high physical exertion. However, the vegetable content is relatively low, so it is recommended to pair it with a portion of stir-fried seasonal vegetables to balance nutrition. The pork cutlets, after repeated pounding and double coating, have a crispy and chewy texture.
