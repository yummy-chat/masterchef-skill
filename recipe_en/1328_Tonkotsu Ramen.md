# Tonkotsu Ramen
Cuisine: Japanese
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Season: All Seasons

## Preparation Steps

### Prep Phase
1. **Tonkotsu Broth Ingredient Prep**
   Clean all bones and the whole chicken. Soak in clean water for 4 hours, changing the water twice to fully remove blood.
   **Key Point**: Must soak thoroughly to remove blood.

2. **Blanching & Cleaning Ingredients**
   Place soaked ingredients in a pot. Add scallion, ginger, and cold water. Bring to a boil over cold water. Skim off foam. Remove and rinse each piece under running water to remove any remaining foam and impurities.
   **Key Point**: Must clean thoroughly, otherwise it affects broth quality.

3. **Dashi Soaking**
   Soak kelp, dried shiitake mushrooms, and dried sardines in 1000g clean water for 12 hours.

4. **Bamboo Shoot Prep**
   Soak dried winter bamboo shoots in clean water for 24 hours, until fully rehydrated.

### Making Tonkotsu Broth
1. **Adding Ingredients & Marking**
   Place cleaned ingredients in the stockpot. Add 20400g of clean water. Mark the initial water level on the pot.
   **Key Point**: Strictly follow the water ratio and mark the level.

2. **Phase 1: Separating Meat from Bones**
   Maintain medium-high heat throughout. Stir every 30 minutes to prevent sticking, leave lid slightly ajar to slow evaporation. Check water level every 2 hours, top up to the mark with **hot water**, and skim off foam. Continue until meat starts to fall off the bones.

3. **Phase 2: Accelerating Emulsification**
   Switch to continuous high heat. Every 30 minutes, remove bones and hammer them with a spoon to speed up marrow and collagen extraction. **Leave lid off**. Stir frequently to prevent burning. Again, top up to the mark with **hot water** every 2 hours. The broth will gradually turn white and become thicker.

4. **Phase 3: Enhancing Flavor & Reducing**
   At the 9-hour mark, add crushed ginger, white onion, dried shiitake mushrooms, carrot, garlic, and cabbage all at once. Turn heat to **maximum** and boil for 1 hour. At the 10-hour mark, use a density meter to test. When the reading reaches **5 degrees**, turn off the heat.
   **Key Point**: Judge by the density meter reading; time is only a reference.

5. **Straining & Storing**
   After turning off the heat, use a strainer to remove all solids. Strain the broth into another pot. Transfer a portion of the broth to a small pot and keep warm for later use. Cool the remaining broth quickly in an ice bath to below 30℃, then portion and seal. Refrigerate or freeze.

### Making Chashu Pork
1. **Preparing the Sauce**
   Make dashi: Heat the soaked kelp, mushroom, and sardine water to 85℃. Hold at a constant 85℃ for 1 hour. Skim off foam. Turn off heat and pour the hot liquid through a strainer lined with bonito flakes to get the base dashi. Add dark soy sauce, sashimi soy sauce, light soy sauce, sake, mirin, and sushi vinegar to the base dashi. Mix well and set aside.

2. **Prepping & Marinating**
   Roll the pork belly tightly and tie securely with kitchen twine. Place in a vacuum bag, pour in the base seasoning sauce, vacuum seal, and refrigerate for 2 hours to marinate.
   **Key Point**: Tie securely to prevent unrolling during cooking.

3. **Sous Vide**
   Place the vacuum bag in a sous vide machine set to **70℃**. Cook for 4 hours.

4. **Cooling & Setting**
   Remove the cooked chashu. Immediately plunge into ice water to cool quickly. Remove from bag, pat dry, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate to set.

5. **Sauce Upgrade**
   Pour the cooking liquid from the chashu bag back into the sauce pot. Add sea salt and sugar. Bring to a boil and reduce. Strain to get the final seasoning sauce. Usage ratio: 30g of this sauce per 450g of broth.

### Making Soft-Boiled Eggs
1. **Egg Prep**
   Remove pasteurized eggs from the fridge and bring to room temperature. Use an egg puncher to poke a hole at the air cell end.
   **Key Point**: Room temperature eggs prevent cracking during cooking; poking a hole helps peeling.

2. **Cooking & Cooling**
   Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil. Gently lower the eggs in. Start timing immediately. Once the time is up, quickly remove and plunge into ice water until completely cool.
   **Key Point**: Must add eggs to boiling water and strictly control timing.

3. **Peeling & Marinating**
   Peel eggs in water starting from the poked hole. Place peeled eggs in the prepared marinade. Seal and refrigerate for at least 12 hours.

### Making Aromatic Oil
1. **Frying Aromatics**
   Combine canola oil and lard in a pot. Heat to 160℃. Add chopped onion and crushed garlic. Fry over medium-low heat until deep caramelized. Remove and discard the solids.

2. **Extracting Seafood Flavor**
   Lower oil temperature to 100℃. Add dried sardines, non-spicy chili powder, and crushed white peppercorns. **Maintain oil temperature at 100℃**. Infuse for 30 minutes to fully extract flavor.
   **Key Point**: Low-temperature, slow infusion to avoid burning.

3. **Straining**
   Strain through a fine mesh strainer to remove solids. Store the aromatic oil at room temperature.

### Making Braised Bamboo Shoots
1. **Blanching to Remove Bitterness**
   Place rehydrated bamboo shoot slices in cold water. Bring to a boil. Drain and rinse under cold water. Repeat this process 3 times.

2. **Cutting & Braising**
   Cut the processed bamboo shoots into approx. 3cm strips. In a pot, combine water, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and mirin. Bring to a boil. Add bamboo shoot strips. Reduce heat to low and braise for 30 minutes.

3. **Infusing Flavor**
   Turn off heat. Let the bamboo shoots soak in the braising liquid for 1 hour to absorb flavor. Remove and toss with a little olive oil to prevent drying.

### Making Back Fat
1. **Prepping & Blanching**
   Cut pork back fat into large pieces. Place in a pot with cold water, scallion segments, and ginger slices. Bring to a boil and skim off foam.

2. **Slow Cooking**
   Reduce heat to the **lowest setting**. Cover the pot but leave a small crack. Cook for 2 hours.
   **Key Point**: Must use the lowest heat to prevent water from evaporating too quickly and turning the fat into lard.

3. **Preparing**
   Remove the cooked, softened back fat. Mash it into a paste using the back of a spoon. Set aside. Before use, it needs to be melted with hot broth.

### Making Ramen Noodles
1. **Mixing Alkali Water**
   Dissolve salt and edible alkali in clean water. Add the strained egg liquid. Mix well.

2. **Kneading & Sheeting**
   Make a well in the high-gluten flour. Pour in the alkali-egg mixture. Mix until it forms a shaggy dough. Use an electric pasta machine to repeatedly press the shaggy dough into sheets to develop gluten.

3. **Cutting & Aging**
   Cut the sheeted dough into thin noodles using a 2mm cutting die. Portion the noodles into nests. Place in an airtight container and refrigerate for 24 hours to age before use.

### Final Assembly
1. **Seasoning the Bowl**
   In a warmed ramen bowl: add 30g of the seasoning sauce and 2g of aromatic oil. Place a suitable amount of back fat paste into a ladle, pour some boiling hot broth over it to melt it, then drizzle into the bowl.

2. **Adding Broth**
   Ladle in approx. 450g of boiling hot tonkotsu broth. Stir to mix the seasonings at the bottom.

3. **Adding Noodles**
   Drain the cooked noodles well. Place them in the broth. Use chopsticks to arrange them neatly.

4. **Plating**
   Arranged in order: seared chashu slices, braised bamboo shoots, shredded wood ear mushrooms, halved soft-boiled egg. Sprinkle with chopped scallions. Finally, insert a sheet of nori.

## Health Suggestions
Tonkotsu ramen is a high-protein, high-collagen "energy bomb," suitable for those with high physical exertion or needing calcium. However, those with high cholesterol, blood pressure, or lipids are advised to only drink the broth and not eat the meat. Be mindful of salt intake; the chashu and bamboo shoots use a significant amount of soy sauce, so it is not recommended to consume large quantities of the broth.
