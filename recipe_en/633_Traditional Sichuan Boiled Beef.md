# Traditional Sichuan Boiled Beef

Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥩 Beef tenderloin, approx. 450g (sliced thinly against the grain)
🥬 Chinese lettuce or asparagus lettuce, 1 stalk (peeled and sliced, or substitute with youmai vegetable)
🌿 Chinese celery, 1 small bunch (cut into sections)
🌱 Garlic sprouts, a few stalks (crushed and cut into sections)
🧄 Garlic, to taste (sliced)
🫚 Ginger, to taste (sliced)
🌶️ Hanyuan red Sichuan peppercorns, approx. 3g
🌶️ Seven-star chilies, a few pieces
🌶️ New generation dried chilies, approx. 40g
🧂 Salt, 5g (for marinating) + 8g (for broth base)
🍾 Light soy sauce, approx. 5g
🎨 Dark soy sauce, to taste
🥫 Broad bean paste (doubanjiang), approx. 20g
🫒 Rapeseed oil, approx. 50g (for stir-frying broth base) + to taste (for final oil splash)
🧂 Chicken bouillon, approx. 2g + 1 tablespoon
🍂 White pepper powder, a pinch
🧂 MSG, 2 tablespoons
🥔 Sweet potato starch, to taste
🥚 Egg white, a bit more than half

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment
🔪 Chef's knife
🪵 Cutting board
🥣 Stainless steel bowl
🍳 Wok
🥄 Strainer
🍲 Bowl
🥄 Spoon
🌀 Blender (optional)
🥢 Chopsticks

## Cooking Steps
1. **Slice the beef**: Rinse the beef tenderloin and slice it against the grain into thin, even pieces.
   - Key: Always slice against the grain to cut through the muscle fibers, ensuring tenderness.

2. **Marinate the beef**: In a bowl, add the beef slices. Add 5g salt, light soy sauce, egg white, and dark soy sauce successively, mixing well. Gradually add a small amount of water while stirring until the water is fully absorbed. Add an appropriate amount of sweet potato starch and mix well. Finally, drizzle with vegetable oil to seal in moisture.
   - Key: Add water in small batches to allow the beef to absorb it fully; sealing with oil locks in moisture.

3. **Prepare vegetables and aromatics**: Peel and slice the Chinese lettuce, cut the celery into sections, crush and cut the garlic sprouts into sections. Slice the garlic and ginger separately.

4. **Make chopped chili**: Add a little oil to a cold wok. Add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns, stir-fry over low heat for about 10 minutes until fragrant and crispy. Remove and let cool, then chop finely.
   - Key: Fry over low heat slowly to prevent the chilies from burning and turning bitter.

5. **Prepare the broth**: Heat the wok, add approx. 50g rapeseed oil, and stir-fry ginger slices, garlic slices, and broad bean paste until fragrant and red oil is released. Add half of the chopped chili and continue stir-frying. Add water and bring to a boil. Season with 8g salt, approx. 2g chicken bouillon, and a pinch of white pepper.
   - Key: Fully stir-fry the broad bean paste until red oil is released; use only half of the chopped chili, the other half is reserved for final plating.

6. **Cook the vegetables**: Add the Chinese lettuce, celery, and garlic sprouts into the boiling broth. Cook over high heat until just done, then remove and spread at the bottom of a serving bowl.
   - Judging: The vegetables darken in color but remain slightly crisp.

7. **Cook the beef**: Remove the wok from heat. Add the marinated beef slices one by one or gently separate them. After all slices are added, return to low heat. Add 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon and 2 tablespoons MSG, gently stirring. Cook for about 1 minute until the beef is fully cooked.
   - Key: Keep the broth at a gentle simmer while adding the beef to avoid washing off the batter; cook for no more than 1 minute to keep the beef tender.

8. **Plate**: Pour the beef and broth into the bowl with the vegetables at the bottom.

9. **Splash hot oil**: Evenly sprinkle the remaining half of the chopped chili over the beef. Heat an appropriate amount of rapeseed oil in a separate pot until it reaches about 70% heat, then pour the hot oil over the chili.
   - Key: The oil should not be too hot, or the chili will burn and become bitter.

10. **Garnish and serve**: Sprinkle with chopped green onions or cilantro.

## Health Tips
Beef tenderloin is high in protein and low in fat, suitable for muscle building and weight loss; however, broad bean paste and the oil splash are high in calories, so it’s recommended to pair with light vegetables for balance. Slicing thinly against the grain and marinating with water in small batches ensures tender beef that is not chewy.
