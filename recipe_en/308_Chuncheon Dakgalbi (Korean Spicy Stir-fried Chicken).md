# Chuncheon Dakgalbi (Korean Spicy Stir-fried Chicken)
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken thigh 500g (boneless, cut into large chunks)  
🧅 Onion 300g (cut into large chunks)  
🥔 Potato 250g (cut into thick slices)  
🥬 Cabbage 350g (cut into large chunks)  
🧄 Garlic cloves 4 (minced)  
🌿 Perilla leaves to taste  
🍡 Rice cakes to taste (optional)  
🍜 Instant noodle block 1  

## Seasonings  
🌶️ Korean chili paste 3 tbsp  
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tbsp  
🧂 Soy sauce 2 tbsp  
🍶 Yellow wine 2 tbsp  
🍬 Sugar 2 tbsp  
🧂 White pepper powder 1 pinch  
🌶️ Chili flakes 2 tbsp  
🧂 Chicken bouillon 2 pinches  
🍛 Curry powder 2 tbsp (or curry block 25g)  
🫒 Cooking oil as needed  

## Utensils & Equipment  
🔥 Gas stove or induction cooktop  
🍳 Frying pan or wok  
🔪 Kitchen knife and cutting board  
🥣 Bowl  
🥘 Lid  

## Preparation Steps
1. **Prepare chicken**: Rinse chicken thighs, remove any residual blood vessels and excess skin, and cut into large chunks.
   **Key point**: Chicken shrinks during cooking, so do not cut it too small.

2. **Marinate chicken**: Place chicken in a bowl, add Korean chili paste, oyster sauce, soy sauce, yellow wine, sugar, white pepper powder, chili flakes, chicken bouillon, and curry powder. Mix vigorously with your hands until evenly combined, and marinate for about 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: Mix forcefully, repeatedly kneading to ensure the marinade fully penetrates the chicken.

3. **Prepare vegetables**: Peel and cut onion into large chunks, remove tough cores from cabbage and cut into large chunks, slice potato into thick pieces, mince garlic, and rinse perilla leaves.
   **Key point**: Onion and cabbage shrink significantly when heated, so cut them into large chunks; only rinse the outer leaves of the cabbage.

4. **Sauté aromatics**: Pour an appropriate amount of oil into the pan, heat over medium heat until hot, add minced garlic and onion chunks, and stir-fry until fragrant.
   **Key point**: The oil temperature is right when garlic sizzles immediately upon contact.

5. **Stir-fry chicken**: Add the marinated chicken along with all the marinade from the bowl into the pan, stir-fry over medium heat for about half a minute until the chicken is evenly coated with the sauce.
   **Key point**: Scrape out any remaining marinade from the bowl to avoid waste.

6. **Add vegetables**: Add potato slices, cabbage, and all other vegetables, continue stir-frying over medium heat for about 3–4 minutes until the vegetables soften and release moisture.

7. **Add water and curry block**: If cooking instant noodles, add an appropriate amount of water at this point. If curry powder was not used earlier, add the curry block, stirring until it melts, then reduce the heat to low.

8. **Simmer**: Cover the pan with a lid, and simmer over the lowest heat for 10 minutes.
   **Key point**: Be sure to use the lowest heat to prevent scorching.

9. **Add noodles and finish**: Uncover, place the instant noodle block into the pan, press it into the sauce with chopsticks, and cook until the noodles are tender. Turn off the heat, sprinkle with perilla leaves, mix well, and serve.
   **Key point**: The noodles only need to cook in the remaining sauce; adding ingredients at the end best preserves their flavor and texture.

## Health Advice  
This dish offers a balanced mix of chicken and vegetables, but Korean chili paste, instant noodles, and rice cakes are high in carbohydrates or sodium. It is recommended to reduce rice intake to avoid excessive carbs and sodium. Suitable as a post-workout cheat meal, but not for frequent consumption.
