# Taiwan-Style Braised Pork Rice (Lu Rou Fan)
Cuisine: Taiwanese  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Season: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥩 Pork belly with skin 1kg+ (alternating fat and lean, skin-on, cut into strips)  
🧅 Purple onion, to taste (traditionally shallots, substitute with purple onion)  
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms, to taste (soaked in advance, do not use fresh mushrooms)  
🥚 Eggs, several (hard-boiled and peeled, score a few cuts on the egg whites)  
🦐 Dried shrimp 5g (soaked in advance, for umami, do not use too much)  
🦐 Golden hook dried shrimp, a small amount (similar function to dried shrimp)  
🍬 Rock sugar, about 15g  
🌿 Five-spice powder, about 1.5g  
🍶 Taiwanese sorghum liquor (baijiu), to taste  
🥡 Light soy sauce, about 75g  
🥡 Dark soy sauce, to taste (for color)  
🍷 Aged yellow rice wine (cooking wine), to taste  
🌿 Spice pouch (bay leaves, angelica, cassia bark, star anise, aged tangerine peel)  

## Tools & Equipment  
🍳 Wok/frying pan  
🔥 Kitchen torch  
🍲 Clay pot  
🥄 Large metal spoon  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  

## Instructions
1. Use a kitchen torch to scorch the pork belly skin until golden brown, scrape clean to remove hair and skin odor, then cut into thin strips.
2. Soak dried shiitake mushrooms, dried shrimp, and golden hook dried shrimp in advance; hard-boil eggs, peel, and score a few cuts on the egg whites; place spices in a spice pouch.
3. Heat cooking oil in a wok over medium heat, stir-fry chopped purple onion until fragrant, creating the flavor base.
4. Add the pork belly strips, stir-fry over medium heat, continuously turning, until the meat is golden with slightly crispy edges and a large amount of fat has rendered out.
5. Add rock sugar and five-spice powder, drizzle in sorghum liquor, then add the fried onion mixture and soaked dried shiitake mushrooms.
6. Add light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and aged yellow rice wine, pour in the mushroom soaking liquid and enough boiling water, then add the spice pouch.
7. Transfer all ingredients to a clay pot, bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.
8. Skim off foam and excess fat, add the hard-boiled eggs, and continue simmering over low heat for about 20 minutes.
9. Finally, increase heat to reduce the sauce until it thickens and coats the meat and eggs.

## Health Advice
This braised pork rice is rich in protein and collagen, but high in fat and sodium. It is recommended to serve with plenty of vegetables or a clear soup. Suitable for those with high physical energy expenditure; those with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, or on a weight loss diet should control portions.
