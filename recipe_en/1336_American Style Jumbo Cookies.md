# American Style Jumbo Cookies
Cuisine: Other  
Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons  

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🧈 Unsalted butter 300g (fully softened at room temperature)  
🌾 All-purpose flour 450g (sifted)  
🥚 Whole eggs 165g (about 3, must be at room temperature)  
🟤 Brown sugar 120g (mixed with eggs until dissolved)  
🍬 Granulated sugar 180g  
🍫 Dark chocolate (pure cocoa butter) 225g (chopped, for dark chocolate flavor)  
🥜 Walnuts (toasted) 195g (finely chopped)  
⚪ White chocolate 150g (for white chocolate flavor)  
🌰 Macadamia nuts 150g (cut into quarters)  
🍇 Raisins 120g (recommended to chop)  
🧂 Salt 3g  
🥄 Baking soda 4.5g  
🥄 Baking powder 1-10g (adjust according to brand instructions)  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  
⚖️ Kitchen scale  
🥣 Measuring cup  
🌀 Electric hand mixer  
🥄 Spatula  
🪄 Sifter  
🫗 Mixing bowl (stainless steel/glass)  
🛡️ Plastic wrap  
♨️ Baking sheet  
🔥 Oven (convection or conventional)  
🕊️ Cooling rack  

## Instructions  
1. Cut the unsalted butter into small pieces and let it soften at room temperature until it leaves an indentation when lightly pressed with a finger, but do not melt it into liquid.  
2. Whisk the room temperature whole eggs, add brown sugar, and stir with a whisk until the brown sugar granules are mostly dissolved; set aside.  
   - **Note**: The whole eggs must be at room temperature, otherwise the brown sugar will be difficult to dissolve.  
3. Combine all-purpose flour, baking soda, and baking powder, sift 2-3 times; set aside.  
4. Prepare the add-ins: Chop the dark chocolate into small pieces; finely chop the walnuts; cut the macadamia nuts into quarters; chop the raisins to prevent bursting during baking.  
5. Place the softened butter, granulated sugar, and salt in a mixing bowl. Beat on low speed until evenly combined and slightly lighter in color; do not beat until fluffy and feathery.  
6. Add the brown sugar egg mixture in 3-4 batches, mixing thoroughly after each addition until fully incorporated, emulsified, and smooth. The final mixture should be smooth, fluffy, and free of oil-water separation.  
7. Add the sifted dry ingredients to the butter-egg mixture in two batches. Use a spatula to fold or cut until no dry flour remains. Do not stir in circles to avoid developing gluten.  
8. Divide the dough evenly into two portions:  
   - For the first portion: Add dark chocolate chunks and walnut pieces, fold until evenly distributed.  
   - For the second portion: Add white chocolate, macadamia nuts, and raisins, fold until evenly distributed.  
9. Cover both dough portions with plastic wrap and refrigerate to rest for at least 1 hour.  
   - **Note**: After refrigeration, the dough will be firm and non-sticky, making it easier to shape later.  
10. Remove the dough from the refrigerator. Divide into portions of about 90g each. Gently roll into balls, place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and lightly flatten with the palm of your hand.  
11. Preheat the oven in advance.  
    - Convection oven: 160°C, bake for about 15 minutes.  
    - Conventional oven: 170°C (top and bottom heat), bake for about 15-17 minutes.  
    - **Doneness check**: The edges of the cookies should be browned while the centers are still soft; remove from oven at this point.  
    - **When baking multiple trays**: Swap the positions of the trays halfway through to ensure even browning.  
12. After removing from the oven, let the cookies rest on the baking sheet for a few minutes until they firm up slightly. Then carefully transfer them to a cooling rack using a spatula to cool completely. Cookies are very soft when freshly baked and may break easily if moved immediately.  

## Health Advice  
Refrigerating the dough for 1 hour helps achieve a crispy exterior and soft interior. These cookies are high in sugar, fat, and calories, so consume in moderation. They are suitable as an occasional dessert and should not be overindulged.
