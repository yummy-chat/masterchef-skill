# Purple Rolls
Cuisine: Shaanxi Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Spring (January to April)

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌾 All-purpose flour 300g  
💧 Water 135g (for kneading)  
🫒 Cooking oil 10g (for kneading)  
🦐 Shrimp 150g  
🥚 Eggs 4  
🥬 Chinese chives 150g  
🧂 Baking soda 1/8 tsp (added to chives to retain color)  
🧂 Salt 1/8 tsp (for dough), 1/4 tsp (for eggs), 1/4 tsp (for dipping sauce)  
🌶️ White pepper 1/4 tsp (for marinating shrimp)  
🫚 Ginger paste 1/2 tsp (for marinating shrimp)  
🧴 Light soy sauce 1 tsp (for marinating shrimp), 1 tbsp (for dipping sauce)  
🌽 Cornstarch 1/2 tsp (for marinating shrimp)  
🫒 Sesame oil 1 tbsp (for marinating shrimp), 1 tsp (for dipping sauce)  
🫒 Cooking oil 1 tbsp (for mixing chives), 2 tbsp (for scrambling eggs), 1 tbsp (for pouring over dipping sauce)  
🌶️ Chili powder to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🫘 Sesame seeds to taste (for dipping sauce)  
🍚 Fragrant vinegar 2 tbsp (for dipping sauce)  
🍬 Sugar 1/2 tsp (for dipping sauce)  
💧 Water about 3 tbsp (for thinning dipping sauce)  
🧅 Chopped green onions, minced garlic to taste (for dipping sauce)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🪵 Wooden basin (for kneading and resting dough)  
🔪 Cutting board, knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥣 Bowls (multiple)  
🫗 Measuring cup  
🍳 Wok  
🪷 Rolling pin  
♨️ Steamer (with steamer basket and cloth)  
🧂 Small bowl (for dipping sauce)  

## Preparation Steps  
1. Mix 300g all-purpose flour, 10g cooking oil, and 1/8 tsp salt evenly. Gradually add 135g water, stirring with chopsticks until flaky, then knead into a relatively stiff dough.  
2. Cover with plastic wrap and rest for 20 minutes. Remove and knead for about 1 minute until the surface is smooth. Divide the dough into two equal portions, roll each into a ball, cover again with plastic wrap, and let relax for 10 minutes.  
**Note**: After relaxing, the small dough balls must remain covered with plastic wrap for a final 2-hour rest to fully relax the gluten.  
3. Slice the shrimp in half lengthwise, then cut into small dice. Add 1/4 tsp white pepper, 1/2 tsp ginger paste, and 1 tsp light soy sauce, mixing well to marinate. Add 1/2 tsp cornstarch and mix evenly, then drizzle 1 tbsp sesame oil to seal the surface. Set aside to marinate.  
4. Beat the eggs with 1/4 tsp salt. Heat the wok, add oil over low heat, pour in the egg mixture, and stir constantly over low heat until scrambled into fine pieces. Remove and let cool.  
5. Wash the chives, drain, and chop finely. Add 1/8 tsp baking soda and 1 tbsp cooking oil, gently mixing to maintain a vibrant green color and prevent water release.  
6. In a small bowl, combine chopped green onions, minced garlic, chili powder, sesame seeds, and 1/4 tsp salt. Heat 2 tbsp oil until smoking, then pour over the seasonings. Add 2 tbsp fragrant vinegar, 1 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1/2 tsp sugar, and about 3 tbsp water. Stir well to make the dipping sauce.  
7. Flatten the rested small dough balls and use a rolling pin to roll them into very thin, large round wrappers. Focus on rolling the edges to ensure even thickness, achieving a “thin as silk” state that is translucent but not torn.  
8. Spread the wrapper flat. Lay the marinated shrimp, scrambled eggs, and prepared chives evenly in a line. Gently roll from one end into a long log.  
**Key**: Roll gently to avoid tearing the wrapper.  
9. Carefully coil the rolled log into a steamer basket lined with a cloth. Once the steamer water is boiling, place the basket on top, reduce to low heat, and steam for 15 minutes.  
**Note**: Be sure to use low heat to prevent the wrapper from over-expanding and breaking.  
10. After steaming, let cool slightly, remove the roll, and cut into bite-sized pieces. Serve with the spicy-sour dipping sauce.

## Health Tips  
This dish provides a balanced mix of carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables, making it suitable for a spring tonic. Shrimp and eggs offer high-quality protein, while chives help stimulate appetite. However, the wrapper is relatively firm, so those with weak digestion should chew slowly. For a lower-calorie option, reduce the oil and salt in the dipping sauce.
