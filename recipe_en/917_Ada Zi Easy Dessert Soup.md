# Ada Zi Easy Dessert Soup  
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April–August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings  
🥔 Tapioca starch 100g  
🍬 White sugar 20g  
💧 Water 80g (for kneading, must be freshly boiled water)  
💧 Water 100g (for cooking)  
🍓 Strawberries,适量  
🥥 Coconut milk,适量 (chilled)  
🫘 Mung bean soup / mung bean paste,适量  

## Kitchen Tools & Equipment  
🥣 Bowl  
🫗 Measuring cup / kettle  
🥄 Spatula / chopsticks  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🍞 Cutting board / kneading mat  
🔪 Dough scraper  
🍳 Pot  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Knife, cutting board  

## Steps  
1. Mix 100g tapioca starch with 20g white sugar evenly in a bowl.  
2. Pour in 80g freshly boiled water all at once, and quickly stir with a spatula.  
**Key point**: Use boiling water to gelatinize the starch, ensuring the dough forms and has a chewy texture.  
3. Let the mixture cool slightly until no longer hot to the touch, then put on disposable gloves and knead into a smooth, non-sticky dough.  
4. Roll the dough into an even long strip on the cutting board.  
5. Use a dough scraper to cut the strip into small pieces about 1 cm in size, as raw Ada Zi.  
**Note**: Cut them smaller for easier cooking and better texture.  
6. Add about 100g water to the pot and bring to a boil.  
7. Once the water boils, add the raw Ada Zi and keep at a rolling boil for about 1 minute.  
**Doneness check**: All Ada Zi float to the surface, indicating they are cooked.  
8. Immediately remove with a slotted spoon and place into prepared cold water to cool.  
**Key point**: Cooling in cold water removes surface stickiness, making the texture firmer and chewier.  
9. **Strawberry Coconut Milk Dessert Soup**: Place cut strawberries and cooled Ada Zi in a bowl, then pour in chilled coconut milk.  
10. **Mung Bean Dessert Soup**: Mix Ada Zi with pre-cooked mung bean soup or mung bean paste and serve.  

## Health Advice  
This Ada Zi dessert soup features a chewy texture from tapioca starch, paired with coconut milk or mung bean paste, making it a low-fat, refreshing summer choice. Suitable for those needing a quick energy boost or preferring a light mouthfeel, but diabetics should reduce sugar content.
