# Wensi Tofu

Cuisine: Jiangsu Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🧈 1 box of silken tofu  
🐔 About half or one whole old hen (for making clear broth)  
🐔 Chicken feet, chicken heads, etc., as needed (to enhance richness and aroma of the broth)  
🍄 Wood ear mushrooms, as needed (soaked and cut into shreds)  
🥬 Rape leaves, as needed (cut into shreds)  
🧂 Salt, to taste  
🌽 Cornstarch (starch), as needed (for thickening)  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Cutting board  
🍲 Stockpot  
🍳 Wok  
♨️ Pressure cooker  
🕸️ Fine mesh strainer  
🥄 Large ladle  

## Cooking Steps  
1. **Make clear broth**: Place the old hen, chicken feet, chicken heads, etc., in cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then thoroughly skim off any foam. Reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for an extended period, or use a pressure cooker for 20 minutes. Strain the cooked broth through a fine mesh strainer to obtain a clear base.  
**Key point**: The chicken must be added to cold water, and the blood foam must be skimmed off completely.  

2. **Prepare the tofu**: Remove the silken tofu from the box whole and trim it into a rectangular block. Slice it horizontally into extremely thin sheets, then cut into hair-thin shreds. Gently place the tofu shreds into clean water, allowing them to naturally separate.  
**Note**: The knife work must be steady, with thin slices and even shreds, and no pauses in between. After cutting, the shreds should immediately separate into strands in the water.  

3. **Prepare the garnishes**: Cut the wood ear mushrooms and rape leaves into shreds of the same thickness as the tofu shreds.  

4. **Season and thicken**: Pour the clear broth into a wok and bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt to taste. Slowly drizzle in the cornstarch slurry while gently stirring in one direction with a large ladle. Cook until the broth reaches a "rice soup" consistency, thick enough to support the tofu shreds.  
**Key point**: The thickening must be even to prevent starch lumps. The consistency of the slurry is crucial—too thin and it won't hold the tofu, too thick and it affects the texture.  

5. **Add the main ingredients**: Gently place the prepared garnishes and tofu shreds into the thickened broth.  
**Note**: Tofu shreds are extremely fragile—do not stir them after adding.  

6. **Combine and serve**: Using the back of a ladle, gently push the broth along the bottom of the wok to evenly distribute and heat the tofu shreds and garnishes. Remove from heat once the broth just begins to simmer.  
**Key point**: Only "push" the broth, never "stir," to prevent the tofu shreds from breaking apart.  

## Health Suggestions  
This dish is a true test of knife skills, with the tofu cut into hair-thin shreds, but the nutrition lies almost entirely in the broth. Chicken broth is rich in collagen and amino acids, making it suitable for those who are weak or have poor digestion. Tofu itself is low in fat and high in calcium, but the thickening increases the glycemic index, so those managing blood sugar should consume the broth sparingly.
