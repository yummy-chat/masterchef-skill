# Spiced Tossed Shredded Tofu Skin  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🫘 Tofu skin (dried bean curd sheets) A few pieces  
🧅 Scallion Three sections (white part, remove the core and slice diagonally into thin strips)  
🥕 Carrot A small amount (cut into thin strips)  
🌿 Cilantro A small amount (cut into segments)  
🧄 Garlic To taste (minced)  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers To taste (cut into thin strips, remove seeds)  
🧂 Salt To taste  
🍬 White sugar To taste  
🧂 MSG To taste  
🥄 Light soy sauce To taste  
🫒 Sesame oil To taste  
🌶️ Spicy chili oil To taste (optional)  
🫒 Cooking oil To taste (for frying Sichuan pepper oil and drizzling hot oil)  
🌿 Sichuan peppercorns A small handful  
🧂 Baking soda or edible alkali About one tablespoon (a small amount, for blanching tofu skin)  

## Kitchenware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon  
🥣 Stainless steel mixing bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🧤 Disposable gloves  
🍽️ Plate  

## Steps  
1. Cut the tofu skin into large pieces, stack them, roll up, and then cut into thin strips.  
2. Take three sections of scallion white, remove the core, flatten, and slice diagonally into thin strips; cut carrot into thin strips; cut cilantro into segments; mince garlic; cut dried chili peppers into thin strips and remove seeds; set aside.  
3. Bring water to a boil in a wok, add a small spoonful of baking soda or edible alkali, add the tofu skin strips, and cook on high heat for about half a minute.  
   **Key Point**: Adding alkali softens the tofu skin for a tender and smooth texture.  
4. After blanching, immediately remove and rinse multiple times with clean water to remove the alkali taste, then squeeze out the water by hand and place in a mixing bowl.  
5. Spread minced garlic and dried chili pepper strips on top of the tofu skin strips.  
6. Heat oil in a wok until it begins to smoke slightly, add a small handful of Sichuan peppercorns, and fry until the aroma is released.  
   **Indicator**: The color of the peppercorns deepens when fried.  
   **Key Point**: Use a slotted spoon to remove the peppercorns, retaining only the Sichuan pepper oil.  
7. Heat the Sichuan pepper oil in the wok on high heat until it smokes, then quickly pour it over the dried chili peppers and garlic on the tofu skin.  
   **Key Point**: The oil must be very hot to release the fragrance.  
8. Immediately cover the bowl with a plate and let it steam for two minutes.  
   **Key Point**: This is a key step in the "spiced toss" technique, allowing the fragrance to infuse into the tofu skin.  
9. Remove the cover, add salt, white sugar, MSG, light soy sauce, and sesame oil for seasoning.  
10. Put on disposable gloves, gently mix and press the tofu skin by hand to evenly absorb the seasonings and aromas.  
    **Indicator**: There should be no excess liquid at the bottom of the bowl after mixing.  
11. Add the prepared scallion strips, carrot strips, and cilantro segments, and gently mix.  
    **Key Point**: Add the side ingredients last to prevent them from releasing water.  
12. Optionally, drizzle spicy chili oil to enhance color and flavor, and mix well again.  

## Health Advice  
Using alkaline water to blanch the tofu skin gives it a softer texture, but rinse thoroughly to remove the alkali taste. This dish is rich in plant protein and dietary fiber, suitable for weight loss or vegetarian diets. Those with high blood pressure should reduce the amount of salt used.
