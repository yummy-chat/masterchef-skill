# Banana Oat Soy Milk Cup
Cuisine: Other
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🥣 Instant oatmeal, as needed (about 1/4 of the cup's capacity)
🍌 1 ripe banana (sliced into thin rounds)
🥛 Plain thick yogurt, as needed
🌾 Plain unsweetened soy milk powder, as needed (about 1-2 tablespoons)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥃 Glass cup (or any cup)
🥄 Spoon
🧂 Fine mesh strainer/flour sifter

## Steps
1. Take a glass cup and evenly spread a layer of instant oatmeal on the bottom.
2. Pour in a layer of plain thick yogurt and smooth it out with a spoon.
3. Place the strainer over the cup and evenly sift a layer of plain unsweetened soy milk powder on top.
   **Key point**: The flavor of the soy milk powder blends more easily with the yogurt upon contact.
4. Arrange a layer of banana slices.
5. Repeat the layering order of oatmeal, yogurt, soy milk powder, and banana slices until the cup is nearly full.
   **Key point**: The thickness of each layer can be adjusted according to preference; ensure the layers are even.
6. Sprinkle a little instant oatmeal and plain unsweetened soy milk powder on the top layer, and place a banana slice as a garnish.

## Health Advice
This is a high-fiber, high-protein, low-fat dessert, suitable for weight control or as a post-workout snack. Bananas provide natural sweetness and potassium, while the soy milk powder and yogurt supplement calcium and increase satiety. Note: Oats and bananas are not low in carbohydrates, so they should not be consumed in large amounts as a main meal during a weight loss phase.
