# Braised Pork Ribs Stuffed with Rice Cakes
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🐖 Pork spare ribs, appropriate amount
🍚 Rice cakes (small cylindrical segments), appropriate amount
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (cut into sections)
🫚 Ginger slices, appropriate amount
🧅 Small spring onion knots, appropriate amount
🍾 Huadiao wine, appropriate amount
🌿 Angelica dahurica, appropriate amount
🌿 Cinnamon stick, appropriate amount
⭐ Star anise, appropriate amount
🧂 Light soy sauce, appropriate amount
🥄 Caramel color (prepared in advance), appropriate amount
🍬 Rock sugar, appropriate amount
🌱 White sesame seeds, appropriate amount (for garnish)
🌿 Chopped scallions, appropriate amount (for garnish)

## Cookware and Equipment
🥘 Soup pot (for blanching and braising ribs)
🥄 Strainer (for filtering the broth)
🍳 Wok (for pan-frying and reducing sauce)
🍳 Frying pan (for pan-frying)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for removing ribs)

## Preparation Steps
1. **Blanch the ribs**: Place the pork spare ribs in a pot with cold water, bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then drain and set aside.
**Key point**: Starting with cold water helps draw out the blood more thoroughly.

2. **Slow-simmer the broth**: Transfer the blanched ribs to a soup pot. Add ginger slices, scallion sections, star anise, spring onion knots, angelica dahurica, and cinnamon stick. Drizzle in Huadiao wine, then add enough hot water to cover. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
**Key point**: Using the original broth for braising enhances the base flavor.

3. **Prepare the ribs**: Take a piece of well-braised, tender rib. Gently twist and pull out the bone, then stuff a cylindrical rice cake into the cavity left by the bone.
**Key point**: The ribs must be braised until tender for this step to work smoothly; this technique is called "replacing the beam with a pillar."

4. **Pan-fry the ribs**: Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Place the stuffed ribs meat-side down and fry until golden brown. Flip them, then add ginger slices, scallion sections, and star anise. Continue frying for about 1 minute.
**Key point**: Pan-frying enhances the meaty aroma and gives the surface a caramelized, crispy texture.

5. **Season and color**: Drizzle in light soy sauce and the pre-prepared caramel color. Stir-fry evenly to coat the ribs thoroughly with the sauce.
**Key point**: Ensure the soy sauce and caramel color fully coat the ribs before adding the broth.

6. **Add broth and braise**: Pour in the filtered original broth from braising the ribs, enough to almost cover them. Add rock sugar.
**Note**: Using the original broth preserves the rich, deep flavor.

7. **Reduce the sauce over medium heat**: Continue simmering over medium heat. As the sauce thickens, stir frequently to ensure the glaze coats the ribs evenly.
**Doneness check**: The sauce should be bright red, glossy, and thick enough to cling to the ingredients.
   **Key point**: The final sauce reduction determines the ultimate flavor and appearance; patience is essential.

8. **Plate and serve**: Remove the ribs and arrange them on a plate. Garnish with white sesame seeds and chopped scallions.

## Health Advice
This dish of braised pork ribs stuffed with rice cakes offers a balanced combination of protein and carbohydrates, making it suitable for those in need of an energy boost. Rice cakes are digested more slowly, so those with weaker digestive function are advised to consume in moderation.
