# Pumpkin and Silken Tofu
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Difficulty: ⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October)

## Ingredients and Seasonings
🎃 Pumpkin, about half (peeled, then cut into thick slices and small cubes)  
🫘 Silken Tofu, 1 box  
🐖 Pork, appropriate amount (cut into small cubes or minced)  
🫛 Green Peas, appropriate amount (sweet pea kernels)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (minced)  
🥢 Cooking Oil, appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking Wine, about 2 tablespoons  
💧 Water, appropriate amount  
🧂 Salt, 1 tablespoon  
🍗 Chicken Bouillon Powder, 1 tablespoon  
🤍 White Pepper Powder, a pinch  
🌾 Cornstarch (water slurry), appropriate amount  

## Kitchen Tools and Equipment
🔪 Cutting Board, Chef's Knife  
🥘 Steamer, Plate  
🥣 Bowl  
🍲 Clay Pot  
🥄 Soup Spoon, Silicone Spatula  
🗡️ Small Knife  

## Preparation Steps
1. Peel the pumpkin. Cut part of it into thick slices and part into small cubes.
2. Steam the thick pumpkin slices in a steamer over high heat for 7–8 minutes until soft and tender. Steam the pumpkin cubes for 3 minutes until just cooked. Remove and set aside.
3. Use the back of a spoon to mash the steamed thick pumpkin slices into a fine pumpkin puree.
4. Cut the pork into small cubes or mince it. Mince the ginger. Prepare the green peas by shelling them. Keep the silken tofu in whole pieces.
5. Heat the clay pot, add cooking oil, then stir-fry the minced pork until it changes color and breaks apart. Add the minced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant.
6. Splash the cooking wine along the edge of the pot and stir-fry to remove any unpleasant odor. Add an appropriate amount of water and bring to a boil.
7. Once the water is boiling, add salt, chicken bouillon powder, and white pepper powder. Stir to mix and season.
8. First, add the steamed pumpkin cubes and bring to a boil. Then, pour in the pumpkin puree and stir until the soup turns a golden, fragrant color.
9. Gently slide the whole piece of silken tofu into the pot. Use a small knife to cut it into small pieces directly in the pot. Simmer over low heat to let the flavors infuse.
   **Note**: The tofu is extremely soft; handle it gently to avoid breaking it.
10. Pour in the cornstarch slurry. Gently push with a silicone spatula to thicken the soup and coat the ingredients.
11. Finally, sprinkle in the green peas. Cook until the soup comes to a boil again, then turn off the heat and serve.
    **Key Point**: Adding the green peas at the end helps maintain their bright green color and crisp texture.

## Health Suggestions
Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene, and silken tofu provides high-quality plant protein. This dish is suitable for moistening dryness and nourishing the stomach in autumn. It is recommended to control the amount of oil and salt used. For those with high blood pressure, blood sugar, or blood lipids, the pork can be replaced with shrimp for a lighter flavor.
