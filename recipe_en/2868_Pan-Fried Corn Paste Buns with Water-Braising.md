# Pan-Fried Corn Paste Buns with Water-Braising
Cuisine: Northeastern Chinese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🌽 Frozen corn paste buns, about 4 pieces (frozen, soft and sticky texture)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (pre-chopped)  
🧂 Salt, as needed (sprinkle on surface before serving)  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed (for greasing the pan and frying)  
💧 Water, as needed (for steaming, enough to cover the bottom of the ingredients)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Regular iron frying pan (achieve non-stick effect through proper pan greasing)  
✂️ Kitchen scissors (to cut open individual packaging)  
🥄 Wooden spatula (for flipping and moving ingredients)  
🔮 Glass lid (for covering and steaming)  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl (to hold greasing oil and water)  
🔥 Gas stove (heat source)  

## Cooking Steps
1.  Remove the frozen corn paste buns and cut open the packaging for later use; chop the scallions and prepare the cooking oil, salt, and water.  
**Key Point**: The corn paste buns are frozen raw ingredients. Frying them directly will not heat them through; they must be prepared using the water-braising method.

2.  Thoroughly scrub the regular iron pan clean, heat it over high heat, pour in an appropriate amount of oil to grease the pan, and once fully greased, pour out all the oil from the pan.  
**Key Point**: The pan must be thoroughly cleaned; otherwise, even multiple rounds of greasing will still cause sticking. All the greasing oil must be poured out completely to avoid uneven heating in the second round.

3.  Heat the iron pan over high heat again, turn off the heat, pour in oil to grease the pan, and once fully greased, pour out the oil.  
**Key Point**: Before the second round of greasing, the heat must be turned off to prevent the pan temperature from being too high and igniting the greasing oil. The greasing oil must also be poured out completely.

4.  Place the corn paste buns into the prepared iron pan. Once the ingredients can slide freely in the pan without sticking, turn on the heat, add a small amount of oil, and begin frying.  
**Key Point**: Wait until the ingredients are set and no longer stick to the pan before turning on the heat to prevent the ingredients from sticking and breaking.

5.  Add an appropriate amount of water to the pan, enough to cover the bottom of the ingredients, cover with a lid, and steam.  
**State Check**: After bringing to a boil over high heat, the water in the pan should be continuously boiling, producing sufficient steam.  
**Key Point**: Occasionally shake the pan during this process to allow the ingredients to slide and prevent sticking. The amount of water should be adjusted based on the size of the ingredients: larger ingredients require more water.

6.  When the water in the pan is almost dry, adjust the heat according to the remaining water: if there is more water, use high heat to quickly evaporate it; if there is less water, reduce to low heat to maintain steam in the pan, avoid burning the bottom, and ensure the ingredients are fully cooked through.  
**State Check**: A small amount of water remains in the pan, nearly dry.

7.  Once the water is completely dry, sprinkle salt and scallions on the surface of the corn paste buns. Flip them as desired to fry until the other side is golden brown, then remove from the pan and plate.  
**Key Point**: If slight sticking occurs at the bottom, since the pan has been well-greased, gently loosen it with a wooden spatula. The bottom of the properly cooked product should be evenly golden, not sticking or burnt.

## Health Advice  
This pan-fried corn paste bun with water-braising is high in fiber and low in fat, making it suitable for blood sugar control. However, ensure that the frozen ingredients are fully steamed through to avoid being burnt on the outside while raw inside. Pairing it with eggs and vegetables for breakfast provides more balanced nutrition.
