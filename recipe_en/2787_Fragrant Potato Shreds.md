# Fragrant Potato Shreds  
Cuisine: Sichuan Cuisine  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥔 Potato, about 2 pieces  
🌶️ Dried chili peppers, as needed (cut into shreds)  
🌸 Sichuan peppercorns and green Sichuan peppercorns, as needed (mixed, pre-soaked in warm water)  
🧅 Scallions, as needed (some finely chopped, some cut into long sections)  
🧄 Garlic, as needed (finely chopped)  
🫑 Green bell pepper, as needed (cut into shreds)  
🌶️ Bird's eye chili, as needed (chopped fine, omit if you don't eat spicy)  
🍶 White vinegar, 2 to 3 tablespoons (for blanching) + 1 tablespoon (for finishing)  
🥄 Light soy sauce, 2 tablespoons  
🥄 Dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon  
🍶 Cooking wine, 1 tablespoon  
🫒 Cooking oil, as needed  
🍬 White sugar, a pinch  
🧂 Salt, a pinch  
🧂 Chicken powder, a pinch  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🔪 Chef's knife, cutting board  
🍲 Pot (saucepan)  
🥢 Colander or strainer basket  
🍳 Wok (iron wok)  
🥣 Bowl  
🥢 Chopsticks  

## Preparation Steps  
1.  Peel the potatoes. First, trim a small slice from the bottom so they sit flat on the cutting board. Then, slice them thinly, and cut into fine shreds. Immediately soak the shreds in water after cutting.  
**Note**: If your knife skills are not up to par, you can use a mandoline slicer instead.  

2.  Cut the dried chili peppers into fine shreds. Soak the Sichuan peppercorns and green Sichuan peppercorns in warm water, then drain and set aside.  
3.  Prepare the sauce: In one bowl, mix 2 tablespoons light soy sauce with 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce to make the soy sauce mixture. In another bowl, mix 1 tablespoon vinegar with 1 tablespoon cooking wine to make the cooking-wok sauce.  
4.  Finely chop some scallions and cut others into sections. Finely chop the garlic. Cut the green bell pepper into shreds. If you like it spicy, chop the bird's eye chili finely and set aside.  
5.  Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add 2 tablespoons of white vinegar, then add the potato shreds and blanch for about 20 seconds. Immediately drain and rinse with cold water.  
**Note**: Adding white vinegar helps keep the potato shreds crisp. The blanching time must be strictly controlled to under 20 seconds; do not exceed it.  

6.  Place the wok over heat. Use the "hot wok, cold oil" method to coat the wok. When the oil is hot, add the drained dried chili shreds, Sichuan peppercorns, chopped scallions, and minced garlic. Stir-fry until fragrant.  
7.  Pour in the prepared soy sauce mixture, and fry it briefly with the aromatics to release the sauce's fragrance.  
**Key Point**: The soy sauce mixture must be fried and released its fragrance before the potato shreds are added to fully enhance the sauce's aroma.  

8.  Immediately add the blanched potato shreds. Stir-fry quickly for about 30 seconds to remove excess moisture.  
9.  Add a pinch of white sugar, salt, and chicken powder to season.  
10.  Add the green bell pepper shreds, scallion sections, and bird's eye chili (if using). Continue stir-frying for about ten to fifteen seconds until a distinct aroma is released.  
11.  Pour the cooking-wok sauce along the edge of the wok. Stir-fry quickly over high heat a few times to cook off the alcohol and vinegar. Turn off the heat and serve immediately.  
**Note**: Pouring the sauce along the wok edge helps release its fragrance more effectively. Stir-fry only a few times; do not overcook.  

## Health Advice  
This potato shred dish uses vinegar blanching to maintain a crisp texture and is paired with Sichuan peppercorns and dried chili for a fragrant and spicy flavor. It is suitable for those who enjoy a sour, spicy, and crunchy taste. However, be mindful to control the amount of oil and salt used to avoid excessive calories.
