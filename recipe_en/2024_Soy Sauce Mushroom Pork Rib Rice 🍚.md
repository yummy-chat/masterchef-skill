# Soy Sauce Mushroom Pork Rib Rice 🍚
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍄 Dried shiitake mushrooms 5-6 pieces (pre-soaked, diced)
🥩 Pork ribs about 500g (chopped into small pieces)
🫚 Ginger 3-4 slices
🧅 Scallion 3-4 sections (white part)
🍚 Rice 1 large bowl (about 300g, pre-cooked, preferably day-old)
🫘 Kidney beans about 50g (pre-cooked)
🫑 Bell peppers about 1/4 each of red and yellow (diced)
🧅 Chives 2 stalks (chopped)
🫒 Cooking oil as needed
🫘 Broad bean paste 1 tablespoon
🍾 Dark soy sauce about 2 tablespoons
🌿 Five-spice powder a pinch
🦪 Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Wok
🔪 Chef’s knife and cutting board
🥄 Strainer
🥄 Spatula or ladle
🥣 Bowl (for holding ingredients)

## Cooking Steps
1. **Prepare ingredients**: Dice the soaked shiitake mushrooms, slice the ginger, cut the scallion into sections, dice the bell peppers, and chop the chives. Cut the pork ribs into small pieces.
   **Key point**: Mushrooms need to be fully soaked in advance; ribs should be cut into uniform pieces for even flavor and cooking.
2. **Prepare rice**: Cook the rice in advance and let it cool. Day-old rice works best.
3. **Blanch the ribs**: Add cold water and the ribs to a pot, bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any foam, then continue boiling for 5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
   **Note**: Starting with cold water helps draw out blood; boiling for 5 minutes partially cooks the ribs, reducing later simmering time.
4. **Sauté aromatics**: Heat a little oil in a wok, add ginger slices and scallion sections, and stir-fry over low heat until lightly golden and fragrant.
   **Key point**: Use low heat to avoid burning.
5. **Stir-fry the ribs**: Add the blanched ribs and turn to medium heat, stir-fry until the surface is lightly browned and golden.
6. **Add mushrooms and sauces**: Add the diced mushrooms and stir-fry quickly, then add broad bean paste and dark soy sauce, stirring rapidly to coat evenly.
   **Note**: Stir quickly after adding sauces to prevent sticking.
7. **Braise and season**: Add enough boiling water to just cover or slightly below the ingredients. Bring to a boil, then add a pinch of five-spice powder and oyster sauce to taste.
   **Key point**: Use boiling water to prevent the meat from contracting and becoming tough.
8. **Simmer the ribs**: Keep boiling over high heat for about 5 minutes, until the ribs are about 80% done and the sauce thickens slightly.
9. **Add rice and braise**: Gently pour the rice into the wok, spread it flat with a spatula to evenly soak in the sauce. Cover and cook over medium-low heat for 5 minutes.
   **Note**: Spreading the rice flat ensures even absorption; medium-low heat prevents burning at the bottom.
10. **Add beans halfway**: After about 3 minutes of braising, uncover, sprinkle in the cooked kidney beans, and quickly cover again to continue braising.
11. **Reduce and stir-fry**: After 5 minutes, uncover and check if the sauce is fully absorbed by the rice. Gently toss all ingredients together with a spatula.
    **Indicator**: Rice should be dry and grains separated.
    **Note**: Stir gently to avoid breaking the rice.
12. **Finish and serve**: Before turning off the heat, add the diced bell peppers and chopped chives, quickly toss a few times, then serve.
    **Key point**: Add bell peppers and chives at the end to maintain bright color and crunchy texture.

## Health Tips
This dish offers a balanced mix of protein and carbs; shiitake mushrooms are rich in dietary fiber, making it suitable for those needing an energy boost. Use less salt, as soy sauce and broad bean paste already provide saltiness; blanching ribs in cold water helps remove any gamey flavor.
