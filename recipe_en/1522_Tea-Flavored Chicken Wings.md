# Tea-Flavored Chicken Wings
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🍗 Chicken wings (about one plate, adjustable in quantity, main ingredient)  
🍃 Green tea leaves (about one cup, divided for making tea broth and frying)  
🫚 Ginger (3-4 slices, for blanching to remove odor)  
🧅 Scallions (2-3 sections, for blanching to remove odor)  
🍶 Baijiu (a small amount, for blanching to remove odor)  
🥄 Light soy sauce (to taste, for seasoning after frying, provides saltiness and color)  
🧂 Salt (to taste, for seasoning)  
🍬 Rock sugar (about 7-8 pieces, adds sweetness and shine)  
🍵 Tea broth (strained from brewed tea, core flavor base, enough to submerge chicken wings)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok (black, for blanching)  
🍳 Non-stick frying pan (white, for frying and simmering)  
🍵 Gaiwan, strainer, glass bowl (for brewing and straining tea)  
🍽️ Flat plate (for holding tea leaves)  
🧻 Kitchen paper towels (for drying tea leaves and chicken wings)  
🥢 Chopsticks (for flipping chicken wings)  
🧲 Slotted spoon (for removing chicken wings and fried tea leaves)  

## Steps
1. **Brew tea and prepare tea leaves**  
   Brew green tea with hot water, strain and set aside the tea broth; spread the brewed tea leaves on kitchen paper towels, press to absorb excess moisture, and set aside.  
   **Key point**: Tea leaves must be thoroughly dried, otherwise they will splatter oil when fried.

2. **Blanch chicken wings**  
   Place chicken wings in a pot with cold water, add ginger slices, scallion sections, and a small amount of baijiu. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then remove and drain.  
   **Key point**: Starting with cold water helps thoroughly remove blood and odor.

3. **Fry chicken wings**  
   Add a small amount of oil to a non-stick frying pan. Place the drained chicken wings in the cold oil, and fry over medium heat until both sides are golden brown.  
   **Key point**: Adding to cold oil prevents sticking; frying until golden enhances flavor and color.

4. **Season and simmer**  
   Add light soy sauce, salt, and rock sugar to the fried chicken wings. Pour in the tea broth until it almost submerges the wings. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for about 20 minutes.  
   **Key point**: Tea broth is the core flavor; slow simmering over low heat ensures infusion.

5. **Fry crispy tea leaves**  
   In a separate wok, heat oil to about 140°C (284°F). Add the dried tea leaves and fry over medium-low heat until all moisture has evaporated, the leaves darken and become crispy. Remove the tea leaves; the strained oil is "tea oil," set aside.  
   **Key point**: Oil temperature should not be too high to prevent burning and bitterness; the leaves are done when no more dense bubbles appear.

6. **Reduce sauce and serve**  
   Once the chicken wings are done simmering, remove the lid and increase heat to reduce the sauce. Just before turning off the heat, drizzle in the fried tea oil, mix well, and plate. Garnish with the crispy fried tea leaves.  
   **Key point**: Adding the hot tea oil at the end is the finishing touch, instantly enhancing the overall tea aroma.

## Health Advice  
This tea-flavored chicken wing dish uses green tea in cooking, is low in fat and high in protein, and is suitable for weight loss periods. Tea leaves are rich in antioxidants, but fried tea leaves are higher in calories, so it is recommended to use only a small amount as a garnish.
