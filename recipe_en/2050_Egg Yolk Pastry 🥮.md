# Egg Yolk Pastry 🥮
Cuisine: Common Home Cooking
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for All Seasons

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🫘 Red beans 300g (soak for 6-8 hours in advance)
🥚 Salted egg yolks 12 (extracted from fresh salted duck eggs)
🌾 All-purpose flour (medium gluten) 150g
🌾 Cake flour (low gluten) 90g
🐖 Lard 90g (50g for the water-oil dough, 40g for the oil dough)
🍬 White sugar 110g (30g for the water-oil dough, 80g for the red bean filling)
🌽 Corn oil 90g
🍯 Maltose 40g
💧 Water 70g
🖤 Black sesame seeds, as needed
🍶 Cooking wine (Baijiu), a small amount
🥚 Egg yolk wash, as needed (for brushing the surface)

## Cookware & Equipment
🍚 Rice cooker
🌀 High-speed blender
🍳 Non-stick pan
🔥 Oven
🥣 Glass bowl
🥖 Rolling pin
🔪 Spatula
🖌️ Pastry brush
🎞️ Plastic wrap
⚖️ Kitchen scale

## Preparation Steps
1. **Make the red bean paste filling**: Place the soaked (6-8 hours) red beans into a rice cooker, press the cook button and simmer until tender, to the point where they can be easily crushed between fingers. Pour the cooked beans into a high-speed blender, add an appropriate amount of water, and blend into a smooth, fine paste.
   **Note**: Do not add too much water, otherwise the subsequent stir-frying time will be significantly extended.
2. Pour the bean paste into a non-stick pan. Add 80g white sugar, 90g corn oil, and 40g maltose. Stir-fry over low heat, continuously mixing with a spatula, until the bean paste forms a cohesive ball and no longer sticks to the pan or spatula. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.
3. **Prepare the salted egg yolks**: Clean the salted egg yolks and place them on a baking tray. Sprinkle with a little cooking wine (Baijiu). Place in a preheated oven and bake at 180°C (top and bottom heat) for 5 minutes, until oil appears on the surface and the color changes. Remove.
4. **Make the water-oil dough**: Mix 150g all-purpose flour, 30g white sugar, 50g lard, and 70g water. Knead into a smooth dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
5. **Make the oil dough**: Mix 90g cake flour with 40g lard. Knead into a dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
6. **Assemble the filling**: Divide the red bean paste into portions of about 20g each. Flatten a portion, wrap it around one baked salted egg yolk, and use the tiger's mouth technique to gather and seal the edges. Shape into a ball. After wrapping all, refrigerate for a short while to firm up slightly, making subsequent steps easier.
7. **Laminate the dough (Kai Su)**: Divide the water-oil dough and the oil dough into equal, small portions. Take one water-oil dough portion, flatten it, wrap it around an oil dough portion, and seal the opening tightly.
   **Note**: Place the sealed side up. Flatten with your hand, then use a rolling pin to roll it into an oval (ox-tongue shape). Roll it up from the bottom. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 minutes.
   After resting, flatten it again, roll it into a longer strip, roll it up again, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rest for another 15 minutes.
8. **Shape the pastries**: Take one rested puff pastry roll. Pinch the two ends towards the center, flatten it, and roll it into a round wrapper with thinner edges and a thicker center. Wrap one refrigerated bean paste and egg yolk filling inside. Use the tiger's mouth technique to rotate and seal the opening tightly, ensuring a firm seal to prevent leaking during baking.
9. **Bake**: Arrange the assembled pastries on a baking tray. Brush the surface evenly with a layer of egg yolk wash. Sprinkle with an appropriate amount of black sesame seeds. Preheat the oven. Bake at 180°C (top and bottom heat) for 25 minutes, until the surface is golden brown.

## Health Tips
Egg yolk pastries are relatively high in lard and sugar. It is recommended to limit consumption to 1-2 pieces to satisfy cravings, paired with unsweetened tea to balance the flavor. Homemade red bean paste allows you to control the sweetness, making it suitable for those who enjoy desserts but are mindful of excessive sugar intake.
