# XO Sauce Crispy Pork Intestines
Cuisine: Cantonese  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork intestines (intestinal head) appropriate amount  
🫚 Ginger several pieces (for blanching to remove odor and stir-frying for aroma)  
🧅 Scallions several stalks (for blanching to remove odor and stir-frying for aroma, some cut into shreds for garnish)  
🧄 Garlic several cloves (minced, for stir-frying aromatics)  
🧅 Dried shallots several (minced, for stir-frying aromatics)  
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (for cleaning and blanching)  
🌾 Cornstarch (bean starch) appropriate amount (for cleaning)  
🍶 Hua Diao wine appropriate amount (added after blanching and stir-frying, and for making sauce)  
⭐ Star anise about 2 pieces  
🌿 Cinnamon stick 1 small piece  
🍃 Bay leaves about 2 pieces  
⚫ Black peppercorns appropriate amount  
🍬 Rock sugar appropriate amount (for blanching and making sauce)  
🥄 Soy sauce appropriate amount (for blanching, seasoning, and coloring)  
🥄 Premium soy sauce 1 tablespoon  
🦐 XO sauce appropriate amount (core seasoning)  
🥩 Char siu sauce appropriate amount  
⚫ Black pepper appropriate amount  
🍬 Sugar a pinch  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🍳 Wok  
🥄 Slotted spoon/strainer  
🥣 Stainless steel bowl  
🔪 Cutting board and knife  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🔩 Metal skewer (or iron rod)  
💨 Hair dryer  
🍶 Sauce bowls (multiple)  

## Preparation Steps
1. Turn the raw intestines inside out, remove most of the internal fat and impurities, leaving a thin layer of fat membrane, then turn them back to the original side.
2. Add salt and cornstarch to the intestines, vigorously rub to remove mucus and off-flavors, then rinse thoroughly with clean water.
3. Heat oil in a wok, stir-fry ginger pieces until slightly charred and fragrant, then add scallion segments and stir-fry until fragrant. Splash in Hua Diao wine along the edge of the wok, then add water. Add star anise, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, black peppercorns, rock sugar, soy sauce, and a pinch of salt for seasoning.
4. Place the cleaned intestines into the wok, cover and cook over medium heat for about 30 minutes, until a chopstick can easily pierce through the intestines. Remove and drain.
**Key Point**: Black peppercorns are essential for removing odor and enhancing flavor, especially for pork intestines and tripe.

5. Thread the intestines onto a metal skewer and hang them. Use a hair dryer to thoroughly dry the surface of the intestines, about 30 minutes.
**Key Point**: Thoroughly drying the surface is crucial for achieving a crispy texture during subsequent frying.

6. Prepare the XO sauce: In a bowl, mix XO sauce, Hua Diao wine, 1 tablespoon premium soy sauce, char siu sauce, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Stir well and set aside.
7. Cut the dried intestines diagonally into bite-sized pieces for better flavor absorption and presentation.
8. First frying: Heat oil in the wok to about 100°C (212°F). Fry the intestines for about 1 minute until the surface wrinkles and becomes fluffy. Remove.
**Key Point**: Oil temperature should not be too high to prevent the outside from burning while the inside remains raw. Also, be careful of oil splatter.

9. Second frying: Raise the oil temperature to about 160°C (320°F). Fry the intestines again for about 30 seconds until the skin is golden and crispy. Remove and drain excess oil.
**Key Point**: High-temperature quick frying forces out excess fat, achieving the best crispiness on the surface.

10. Leave a small amount of oil in the wok. Add minced garlic and minced dried shallots, stir-fry over low heat until fragrant.
11. Add the double-fried intestines, immediately pour in the pre-mixed XO sauce, and stir-fry quickly to coat the intestines evenly with the sauce. Remove from heat immediately.
12. Plate and garnish with scallion shreds on top.

## Health Advice
This crispy pork intestine dish has a high fat content and is suitable for occasional indulgence. It is recommended to pair it with plenty of vegetables to balance the greasiness. Individuals with high blood pressure should be mindful of the sodium content in the sauce and may reduce the amount of soy sauce and XO sauce used.
