# Colorful Lace Skirt Potstickers  
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish  
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐  
Seasonal Recommendation: Suitable for all seasons  

## Ingredients and Seasonings  
🥩 Pork filling, appropriate amount  
🥬 Pickled vegetables (sour cabbage), appropriate amount (need to be soaked in advance, squeezed dry, and chopped)  
🧅 Scallions, appropriate amount (chopped)  
🫚 Ginger, appropriate amount (for making ginger and scallion water)  
🌾 Flour, appropriate amount (for white dough and potsticker water)  
🫑 Bell pepper juice, appropriate amount (for orange dough)  
🥬 Spinach juice, appropriate amount (for green dough)  
🦑 Squid ink, appropriate amount (for black dough)  
🌽 Cornstarch, one tablespoon  
🫒 Vegetable oil, appropriate amount (for pan-frying)  
💧 Water, appropriate amount (for kneading dough, ginger and scallion water, and potsticker water)  

🧂 Salt, appropriate amount  
🧂 Chicken powder, appropriate amount  
🍶 Cooking wine, appropriate amount  
🧂 Soy sauce, appropriate amount  
🦪 Oyster sauce, appropriate amount  
🧂 White pepper powder, appropriate amount  
💧 Ginger and scallion water, appropriate amount (add in batches)  

## Cookware and Equipment  
🥣 Glass bowl  
🔪 Chef's knife  
🪵 Wooden cutting board  
🧈 Silicone kneading mat  
🪱 Rolling pin  
🍳 Non-stick pan  
🔥 Induction cooktop  
🍽️ Large plate (for flipping)  
🥢 Chopsticks  
🥄 Spatula / ladle  

## Steps  
1. **Make colored dough**: Mix flour with bell pepper juice, spinach juice, and squid ink respectively. Knead into smooth orange, green, and black dough until the hands, dough, and bowl are all clean. Cover with plastic wrap and let the dough rest.  
   **Key point**: Resting relaxes the gluten, making the dough easier to roll out.  

2. **Prepare the filling**: Add chopped pickled vegetables, scallions, salt, chicken powder, cooking wine, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and white pepper powder to the pork filling. Gradually add the ginger and scallion water while stirring vigorously in one direction until the mixture becomes stiff. Finally, add one tablespoon of cornstarch, mix evenly, and repeatedly slap the filling until it becomes sticky and bouncy.  
   **Note**: Pickled vegetables must be soaked, squeezed dry, and chopped in advance to prevent them from being too salty; stirring and slapping will make the filling firm and springy.  

3. **Roll out wrappers**: Shape the rested dough into a long log, cut it into small pieces, flatten each piece, and roll them into round wrappers with a thicker center and thinner edges.  
   **Key point**: Dust the work surface generously with flour to prevent sticking.  

4. **Assemble**: Place an appropriate amount of filling on a wrapper. Pinch the center together first, then seal the two sides while pleating.  
   **Note**: The filling should not be too wet, as this will affect the wrapping and frying processes.  

5. **Prepare potsticker water**: In a bowl, mix one tablespoon of flour, one bowl of water, and half a bowl of oil until well combined.  
   **Key point**: This is the key to forming the lace skirt.  

6. **Initial sear**: Heat oil in a non-stick pan over high heat, then reduce to the lowest heat. Place the potstickers in the pan one by one and fry until the bottoms are lightly golden.  

7. **Steam**: Pour the flour-water mixture evenly along the edge of the pan, ensuring it reaches just above the bottoms of the potstickers. Immediately cover with a lid and steam the potstickers until cooked through.  

8. **Reduce liquid to form the lace skirt**: Continue frying over medium-low heat. When the liquid is almost evaporated, turn the heat to high and fry until the bottoms are golden and crispy, forming a web-like lace skirt.  
   **Judgment criteria**: The liquid has evaporated, and the bottoms show a distinct golden, net-like structure.  

9. **Remove from pan**: Turn off the heat. Slightly tilt the pan to drain excess oil. Place a large plate upside down over the pan, then quickly invert the pan so the potstickers are neatly transferred onto the plate.  
   **Note**: The motion must be quick to keep the lace skirt intact.  

## Health Tips  
This colorful lace skirt potsticker uses natural fruit and vegetable juices for coloring, making it more nutritious and ideal for adding color to family meals or gatherings. Be sure to soak the pickled vegetables to remove excess salt, and slap the filling until firm for a springier texture.
