# Cold Shredded Chicken
Cuisine: Sichuan
Difficulty: ⭐⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Summer (April-August)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🍗 Chicken legs (drumsticks with thigh) about 2-3
🧅 Onion half (sliced into strips)
🌿 Cilantro one bunch (cut into sections)
🍋 Lemon one (sliced, seeds removed)
🥜 Fried peanuts one small bowl
🧂 **Seasonings for boiling chicken legs**:
　　🍶 Shaoxing wine as needed
　　🌶️ Dried chili peppers as needed
　　🧄 Sichuan peppercorns as needed
　　🧅 Scallions in large pieces as needed
　　🫚 Ginger in large pieces
　　🟡 Gardenia fruit 2-3 (for color)
🧂 **Sauce**:
　　🌶️ Bird’s eye chili 1 part
　　🧄 Minced garlic 1 part
　　🟢 Scallions 1 part
　　🔴 Fine chili powder 1 part
　　⚪ White sesame seeds 1 part
　　🫒 Cooking oil as needed (heated to 70% hot)
　　🥄 Light soy sauce as needed
　　🧂 Salt as needed
　　🍬 Sugar as needed
　　🍶 Vinegar as needed
　　🦪 Oyster sauce as needed
　　🫒 Sesame oil as needed

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🍳 Pot (for boiling chicken legs)
🔥 Gas stove
🪥 Toothpicks (for poking holes in chicken legs)
🥄 Slotted spoon (for skimming foam)
🥣 Bowls (for sauce mixing, combining, and ice water)
🧻 Paper towels
🔪 Knife and cutting board

## Steps
1. Use toothpicks to poke dense holes in the chicken legs, to allow blood to seep out during boiling and remove any unpleasant smell.
2. In a bowl, place bird’s eye chili, minced garlic, scallions, fine chili powder, and white sesame seeds, one part each.
**Note**: Fine chili powder more easily releases a bright red color.

3. Heat the cooking oil until 70% hot, pour it over the base ingredients to release fragrance, then add light soy sauce, salt, sugar, vinegar, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Stir thoroughly to combine into a sauce and set aside.
4. Slice the onion into strips, cut the cilantro into sections, and slice the lemon, then remove the seeds.
**Key**: Remove lemon seeds, otherwise the taste will be bitter.

5. Place the chicken legs in cold water in a pot, adding enough water to fully submerge them. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off any foam, then add Shaoxing wine, Sichuan peppercorns, dried chili peppers, scallions, ginger, and gardenia fruit. Reduce to low heat and keep at a gentle boil, cooking uncovered for about 20 minutes.
**Key**: Keep the pot uncovered throughout to allow odors to dissipate; insert a chopstick into the thickest part of the meat—if no blood oozes out, it is done.

6. Remove the chicken legs and immediately immerse them in ice water until completely cooled. This makes the meat firm and tender.
7. Take out the chicken legs, pat dry the surface with paper towels, then tear the meat by hand into bite-sized pieces.
**Note**: Be sure to dry thoroughly, otherwise it will dilute the sauce.

8. Place the shredded chicken in a large bowl, add the onion strips, cilantro sections, lemon slices, and fried peanuts. Pour in the sauce, and toss well by hand, then plate.

## Health Tips
Removing the skin from the chicken leg before cold mixing can reduce calories. Lemon and onion are rich in vitamin C, making this a satisfying option during calorie-restricted periods. When shredding the chicken, be sure to dry the meat thoroughly to avoid diluting the sauce and affecting the flavor.
