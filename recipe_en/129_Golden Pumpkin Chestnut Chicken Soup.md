# Golden Pumpkin Chestnut Chicken Soup
Cuisine: Common Home-style Dish
Cooking Difficulty: ⭐
Seasonal Recommendation: Autumn (September-October), Winter (November-December)

## Ingredients & Seasonings
🐔 Free-range chicken 2 jin (cut into pieces)
🌰 Chestnuts 0.5 jin (use fresh autumn/winter chestnuts)
🎃 Pumpkin 100g (to make pumpkin puree, can use Kabocha squash or old pumpkin, steamed and mashed)
🫘 Red dates appropriate amount (pre-soaked)
🍒 Goji berries appropriate amount (pre-soaked)
🫚 Ginger about 5-6 slices

🍶 Cooking wine appropriate amount (for blanching)
🧂 Salt appropriate amount (added in two batches)

## Kitchenware & Equipment
🥣 Glass bowl (for soaking and holding ingredients)
🍳 Wok (for blanching chicken)
🫕 Clay pot (for slow simmering)
🥄 Wooden spoon or soup ladle (for stirring and serving soup)

## Cooking Steps
1. Soak the red dates and goji berries in clean water until soft, set aside.
2. Steam the pumpkin until cooked, then mash into puree. Take 100g and set aside.
3. Cut the free-range chicken into pieces, place in a pot with cold water, add a few slices of ginger, an appropriate amount of cooking wine, and a little salt.
4. Bring to a boil over high heat and continue boiling for about 2 minutes. Skim off the foam, remove the chicken pieces, and rinse clean with water.
5. Place the blanched chicken pieces and ginger slices into the clay pot. Add enough clean water at once.
   **Key Point**: Water must be added all at once; do not add water during cooking.
6. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low heat and simmer for 45 minutes.
7. Add the chestnuts.
   **Key Point**: Chestnuts should not be added too early, otherwise they may dissolve and lose their flavor.
8. Add 100g pumpkin puree, stir well, the soup will turn golden.
9. Add the soaked red dates and goji berries, continue to simmer over low heat for about 20 minutes.
10. Before serving, add an appropriate amount of salt to taste, stir well, then serve and enjoy.

## Health Tips
Chestnuts nourish the spleen and strengthen the kidneys. Pumpkin is rich in beta-carotene. This soup is suitable for nourishing the body in autumn and winter, for those with weak constitution or who are prone to feeling cold. Removing the skin from the chicken before simmering can reduce fat. Pumpkin puree serves as a natural thickener, making it healthier without the need for starch slurry.
